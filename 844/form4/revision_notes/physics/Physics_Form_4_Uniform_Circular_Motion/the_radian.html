The Radian

One radian is the angle subtended at the centre of the circle by an arc of length equal to the radius of the circle. Since one circle = 360o and has 2π radians therefore 1 radian = 360o/2πr= 57.296o or 57.3o.
Example
A wheel of radius 50 cm is rolled through a quarter turn. Calculate

  1. The angle rotated in radians
  2. The distance moved by a point on the circumference.

    Solution
    (i) A quarter turn = 360o/4= 90o . Since 360o = 2π radians. Alternately since 1 radian = 57.3o hence 90o = 1.57 radii.
    (ii) A point on the circumference moves through an arc, Arc = radius × θ(θ in radians) = 50 cm × 1.57 = 78.5 cm.
