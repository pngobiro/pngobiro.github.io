2010

Certificate in Early Childhood Development and Education
December 2010

Instructions to Candidates:

This question paper consists of TWO sections: A and B. Answer ALL the questions in section A. Answer any FOUR questions from section B. Answers to both sections A and B must be written in the spaces provided in this booklet.

SECTION A (40 marks)

Answer ALL the questions in this section.

Question 1

(a) Explain the meaning of the following terms:

(i) Child rights;

Sample Answer:

Child rights refer to the fundamental freedoms and entitlements that every child should have, including the right to education, health, protection from harm, and participation in decisions affecting their lives.

(ii) Child labour.

Sample Answer:

Child labour refers to the exploitation of children through any form of work that deprives them of their childhood, interferes with their ability to attend school, or is harmful to their physical, mental, or social development.

(b) State two types of transition in Early Childhood Development and Education in Kenya.

Sample Answer:

  • Transition from home to pre-school.
  • Transition from pre-school to primary school.

(c) Identify four ways in which a teacher would motivate pre-schoolers to learn.

Sample Answer:

  • Using positive reinforcement and praise.
  • Incorporating play-based learning activities.
  • Creating a safe and supportive learning environment.
  • Providing engaging and age-appropriate learning materials.

Question 2

(a) State five measures a community should consider on food taboos to positively provide for the development of young children.

Sample Answer:

  • Educating the community on the nutritional needs of young children.
  • Encouraging the consumption of a balanced diet.
  • Challenging harmful food taboos that restrict access to essential nutrients.
  • Promoting the inclusion of locally available nutritious foods.
  • Involving community leaders in advocating for positive dietary practices.

(b) Explain three conditions that are necessary for normal fertilization to take place in human beings.

Sample Answer:

  • The presence of healthy sperm and egg cells.
  • Proper timing of intercourse during the woman's ovulation period.
  • A healthy reproductive system in both the male and female.

(c) Explain two changes in society that may negatively affect child-rearing in Kenya.

Sample Answer:

  • Urbanization leading to the breakdown of extended family support systems.
  • Increased economic pressures forcing parents to work long hours, reducing time spent with children.

Question 3

(a) Give the meaning of Female Genital Mutilation (FGM).

Sample Answer:

Female Genital Mutilation (FGM) refers to the partial or total removal of external female genitalia or other injury to the female genital organs for non-medical reasons.

(b) Outline three negative effects of Female Genital Mutilation on a girl child.

Sample Answer:

  • Severe pain and risk of infection during the procedure.
  • Long-term complications such as childbirth difficulties and chronic pain.
  • Psychological trauma and emotional distress.

(c) Give five ways in which a teacher would manage misbehavior in a pre-school class.

Sample Answer:

  • Using positive reinforcement to encourage good behavior.
  • Setting clear and consistent rules and expectations.
  • Redirecting the child's attention to a positive activity.
  • Providing a calm and structured environment.
  • Communicating with parents to address underlying issues.

Question 4

(a) Outline four prenatal causes of cerebral palsy.

Sample Answer:

  • Infections during pregnancy, such as rubella or toxoplasmosis.
  • Maternal health conditions like high blood pressure or diabetes.
  • Genetic factors or chromosomal abnormalities.
  • Exposure to toxins or harmful substances during pregnancy.

(b) Identify four characteristics of cerebral palsy.

Sample Answer:

  • Muscle stiffness or spasticity.
  • Difficulty with coordination and balance.
  • Delayed motor development milestones.
  • Speech and communication challenges.

(c) Suggest two ways in which a teacher would prevent children from developing cerebral palsy in an ECDE center.

Sample Answer:

  • Educating parents on the importance of prenatal care and healthy pregnancy practices.
  • Ensuring a safe and hazard-free environment to prevent accidents that could lead to brain injury.

SECTION B (60 marks)

Answer any FOUR questions from this section.

Question 5

(a) (i) State three physical changes that occur in boys during adolescence.

Sample Answer:

  • Growth of facial and body hair.
  • Deepening of the voice.
  • Increase in muscle mass and height.

(ii) Identify seven problems associated with physical changes in girls during adolescence.

Sample Answer:

  • Menstrual cramps and discomfort.
  • Body image issues and self-esteem concerns.
  • Acne and skin problems.
  • Weight gain and changes in body shape.
  • Mood swings and emotional instability.
  • Breast tenderness and development.
  • Increased risk of eating disorders.

(b) Propose five ways in which parents would assist children to overcome challenges of the adolescence stage.

Sample Answer:

  • Providing emotional support and understanding.
  • Encouraging open communication about physical and emotional changes.
  • Promoting a healthy lifestyle with proper nutrition and exercise.
  • Helping children build self-esteem and confidence.
  • Seeking professional help if needed, such as counseling or medical advice.

Question 6

(a) (i) Define the term bed-wetting in children.

Sample Answer:

Bed-wetting, or nocturnal enuresis, refers to the involuntary passage of urine during sleep in children who are old enough to have bladder control.

(ii) Identify seven causes of bed-wetting in children.

Sample Answer:

  • Delayed development of the bladder.
  • Genetic predisposition.
  • Stress or emotional disturbances.
  • Urinary tract infections.
  • Diabetes or other medical conditions.
  • Deep sleep patterns.
  • Excessive fluid intake before bedtime.

(b) State six ways in which a caregiver would manage bed-wetting in children.

Sample Answer:

  • Limiting fluid intake before bedtime.
  • Encouraging regular toilet use before sleep.
  • Using bed-wetting alarms to train the child.
  • Providing emotional support and reassurance.
  • Rewarding the child for dry nights.
  • Consulting a healthcare professional for medical advice.

Question 7

(a) Identify six roles of children's rescue centers in Kenya.

Sample Answer:

  • Providing shelter and protection for abused or neglected children.
  • Offering counseling and psychological support.
  • Facilitating access to education and vocational training.
  • Reuniting children with their families when possible.
  • Advocating for children's rights and welfare.
  • Providing medical care and rehabilitation services.

(b) (i) Give six reasons for referring children to rehabilitation centers.

Sample Answer:

  • Severe physical or emotional abuse.
  • Neglect or abandonment by caregivers.
  • Exposure to substance abuse or addiction.
  • Involvement in child labor or trafficking.
  • Mental health issues requiring specialized care.
  • Homelessness or lack of family support.

(ii) Highlight three challenges facing child rehabilitation centers in Kenya.

Sample Answer:

  • Limited funding and resources.
  • Overcrowding and lack of adequate facilities.
  • Difficulty in reintegrating children into society.

Question 8

(a) Define the following terms as used in special needs education:

(i) Referral;

Sample Answer:

Referral is the process of directing a child with special needs to appropriate services or professionals for further assessment and support.

(ii) Placement.

Sample Answer:

Placement refers to the assignment of a child with special needs to a specific educational setting or program that meets their individual needs.

(b) Outline five services offered by sheltered workshops to children with special needs in Kenya.

Sample Answer:

  • Vocational training and skill development.
  • Employment opportunities in a supportive environment.
  • Counseling and psychological support.
  • Life skills training for independent living.
  • Social integration and community participation programs.

(c) Identify six gaps at the community level in the promotion of child rights and protection in Kenya.

Sample Answer:

  • Lack of awareness about child rights and protection laws.
  • Inadequate reporting mechanisms for child abuse cases.
  • Limited access to legal and social services for children.
  • Cultural practices that violate child rights, such as FGM.
  • Poverty and economic challenges affecting child welfare.
  • Insufficient community-based child protection programs.

Question 9

(a) (i) Define an assessment tool.

Sample Answer:

An assessment tool is a method or instrument used to evaluate a child's development, learning progress, or specific skills in a structured and systematic way.

(ii) Give six reasons that would make a teacher use an assessment tool in pre-school.

Sample Answer:

  • To identify a child's strengths and areas for improvement.
  • To monitor progress and development over time.
  • To plan individualized learning activities.
  • To communicate with parents about their child's progress.
  • To identify children who may need additional support or intervention.
  • To evaluate the effectiveness of teaching methods and curriculum.

(b) List seven characteristics in young children determined by heredity.

Sample Answer:

  • Eye color.
  • Hair color and texture.
  • Height and body structure.
  • Skin tone.
  • Predisposition to certain health conditions.
  • Temperament and personality traits.
  • Intellectual potential.
