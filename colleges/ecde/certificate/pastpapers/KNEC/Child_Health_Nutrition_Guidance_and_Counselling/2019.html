2019

Certificate in Early Childhood Development and Education
December 2017

Instructions to Candidates:

This question paper consists of TWO sections: A and B. Answer ALL the questions in section A and FOUR questions from section B. Answers to both sections A and B must be written in the spaces provided in this booklet.

SECTION A (40 marks)

Answer ALL the questions in this section.

Question 1

(a) Highlight three challenges of using the calendar method in family planning. (3 marks)

Sample Answer:

  • Irregular menstrual cycles can make it difficult to predict fertile days.
  • Requires strict adherence to tracking and abstaining during fertile periods.
  • Not effective for women with hormonal imbalances or irregular ovulation.

(b) Give three signs and symptoms of measles. (3 marks)

Sample Answer:

  • High fever and cough.
  • Runny nose and red, watery eyes.
  • Rash that starts on the face and spreads to the rest of the body.

(c) Identify four ways in which maternal and child health care services can be made more accessible in rural areas of Kenya. (4 marks)

Sample Answer:

  • Establishing mobile clinics to reach remote areas.
  • Training community health workers to provide basic services.
  • Providing free or subsidized health care services.
  • Improving infrastructure such as roads and communication networks.

Question 2

(a) List four food nutrients that make up a balanced diet. (2 marks)

Sample Answer:

  • Carbohydrates.
  • Proteins.
  • Fats.
  • Vitamins and minerals.

(b) State four food taboos that may affect the nutritional status of mothers and children. (4 marks)

Sample Answer:

  • Prohibiting pregnant women from eating certain nutritious foods.
  • Restricting children from consuming eggs or milk.
  • Beliefs that certain foods are harmful during specific life stages.
  • Cultural practices that limit the consumption of animal proteins.

(c) Identify four eating habits a teacher would emphasize to children during meal times in an ECDE centre. (4 marks)

Sample Answer:

  • Eating a variety of foods to ensure a balanced diet.
  • Chewing food properly to aid digestion.
  • Washing hands before and after meals.
  • Avoiding wastage of food.

Question 3

(a) (i) Give the meaning of first aid. (1 mark)

Sample Answer:

First aid is the immediate assistance given to a person who has been injured or suddenly taken ill before professional medical help is available.

(ii) Identify three qualities of a good first aider. (3 marks)

Sample Answer:

  • Calmness under pressure.
  • Good communication skills.
  • Knowledge of basic first aid techniques.

(b) Identify four health services supported by UNICEF that promote Early Childhood Development Education in Kenya. (4 marks)

Sample Answer:

  • Immunization programs to prevent childhood diseases.
  • Nutrition programs to combat malnutrition.
  • Provision of clean water and sanitation facilities.
  • Health education campaigns for parents and caregivers.

Question 4

(a) Give the meaning of:

(i) Career guidance. (1 mark)

Sample Answer:

Career guidance is the process of helping individuals make informed decisions about their career paths and educational choices.

(ii) Conflict resolution. (1 mark)

Sample Answer:

Conflict resolution is the process of resolving disputes or disagreements between parties in a peaceful and constructive manner.

(b) State four issues that may require a counsellor to refer a learner in an ECDE centre for further assistance. (4 marks)

Sample Answer:

  • Severe emotional or behavioral problems.
  • Learning disabilities or developmental delays.
  • Signs of abuse or neglect.
  • Mental health issues such as anxiety or depression.

(c) Identify four behavioral signs that will alert an ECDE teacher that a child requires counselling. (4 marks)

Sample Answer:

  • Withdrawal from social interactions.
  • Aggressive or disruptive behavior.
  • Frequent crying or emotional outbursts.
  • Difficulty concentrating or completing tasks.

SECTION B (60 marks)

Answer FOUR questions from this section.

Question 5

(a) (i) Define the term weaning. (1 mark)

Sample Answer:

Weaning is the process of gradually introducing an infant to solid foods while reducing their dependence on breast milk or formula.

(ii) Explain three problems a baby is likely to experience during weaning. (6 marks)

Sample Answer:

  • Digestive issues such as constipation or diarrhea.
  • Allergic reactions to new foods.
  • Refusal to eat solid foods due to unfamiliar textures or tastes.

(b) State four ways of enriching maize meal porridge for children in an ECDE centre. (4 marks)

Sample Answer:

  • Adding milk or milk powder for protein and calcium.
  • Mixing in mashed fruits for vitamins and natural sweetness.
  • Including groundnuts or peanut butter for healthy fats.
  • Adding eggs for additional protein and nutrients.

(c) Explain the roles of two minerals whose intake is required in higher proportions during pregnancy. (4 marks)

Sample Answer:

  • Iron: Essential for the production of hemoglobin to prevent anemia and support fetal growth.
  • Calcium: Important for the development of the baby's bones and teeth, and to maintain the mother's bone health.

Question 6

(a) (i) Apart from the teacher, name four other caregivers who provide safety and care for children. (2 marks)

Sample Answer:

  • Parents or guardians.
  • Nannies or babysitters.
  • Relatives or extended family members.
  • Community health workers.

(ii) Explain three roles of a teacher as a caregiver in early childhood development. (6 marks)

Sample Answer:

  • Providing a safe and nurturing environment for children.
  • Supporting children's emotional and social development.
  • Encouraging learning through play and structured activities.

(b) Outline the procedure an ECDE teacher would follow to remove an insect from a child’s ear. (4 marks)

Sample Answer:

  1. Stay calm and reassure the child.
  2. Tilt the child's head to the side to allow the insect to crawl out.
  3. If the insect does not come out, use a small amount of oil (e.g., olive oil) to suffocate the insect.
  4. Seek medical assistance if the insect remains lodged in the ear.

(c) Highlight three ways that may not transmit HIV/AIDS. (3 marks)

Sample Answer:

  • Sharing utensils or eating food prepared by someone with HIV.
  • Hugging or shaking hands with an infected person.
  • Using the same toilet or bathroom facilities.

Question 7

(a) Outline the first four stages of the counselling process. (4 marks)

Sample Answer:

  1. Establishing rapport and building trust with the client.
  2. Identifying the client's issues and concerns.
  3. Exploring the client's feelings and emotions.
  4. Setting goals and developing a plan of action.

(b) Describe five non-verbal behaviors a counsellor should observe when attending to a client. (5 marks)

Sample Answer:

  • Facial expressions, such as smiles or frowns.
  • Body posture, such as slouching or leaning forward.
  • Eye contact, or lack thereof.
  • Gestures, such as hand movements or nodding.
  • Tone of voice and speech patterns.

(c) Explain three ways in which prescription drugs may be abused. (6 marks)

Sample Answer:

  • Taking higher doses than prescribed.
  • Using someone else's prescription medication.
  • Using drugs for non-medical purposes, such as to get high.

Question 8

(a) (i) Give the meaning of Primary Health Care (PHC). (2 marks)

Sample Answer:

Primary Health Care (PHC) is the first level of contact individuals have with the health care system, providing essential health services that are accessible, affordable, and community-based.

(ii) Describe five principles of Primary Health Care (PHC). (5 marks)

Sample Answer:

  • Equitable distribution of health services.
  • Community participation in health care decisions.
  • Focus on prevention and health promotion.
  • Use of appropriate technology and resources.
  • Multi-sectoral collaboration to address health determinants.

(b) Complete the immunization table below. (4 marks)

Sample Answer:

DPT Injection on outer right thigh Diphtheria, Pertussis, Tetanus
OPV Oral/through the mouth Polio
Measles Injection on the upper arm Measles

(c) Explain two measures to take to prevent malaria in the community. (4 marks)

Sample Answer:

  • Use of insecticide-treated bed nets to prevent mosquito bites.
  • Elimination of stagnant water to reduce mosquito breeding sites.

Question 9

(a) State four danger signs that may require an expectant mother to seek medical attention. (4 marks)

Sample Answer:

  • Severe abdominal pain or cramping.
  • Vaginal bleeding or discharge.
  • Severe headaches or blurred vision.
  • Swelling of the hands, feet, or face.

(b) Identify four cultural practices that promote the spread of HIV infection. (4 marks)

Sample Answer:

  • Female Genital Mutilation (FGM) using unsterilized tools.
  • Wife inheritance, where a widow is inherited by a relative of her deceased husband.
  • Polygamous marriages with multiple sexual partners.
  • Traditional practices involving shared cutting instruments, such as circumcision rituals.

(c) (i) Give three reasons why bottle feeding is discouraged. (3 marks)

Sample Answer:

  • Increased risk of infections due to improper sterilization of bottles.
  • Lack of essential antibodies found in breast milk, which protect the baby from diseases.
  • Higher cost and inconvenience compared to breastfeeding.

(ii) Explain two economic causes of malnutrition in Kenya. (4 marks)

Sample Answer:

  • Poverty: Many families cannot afford nutritious food due to low income.
  • High food prices: The cost of healthy food options is often too high for low-income households.
