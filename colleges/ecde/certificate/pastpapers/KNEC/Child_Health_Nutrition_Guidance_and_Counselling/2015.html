2015

Certificate in Early Childhood Development and Education
December 2015

Instructions to Candidates:

This question paper consists of TWO sections: A and B. Answer ALL the questions in section A and FOUR questions in section B. Answers to both sections A and B must be written in the spaces provided in this booklet.

SECTION A (40 marks)

Answer ALL the questions in this section.

Question 1

(a) State four factors to consider in order to preserve nutrients when preparing vegetables. (4 marks)

Sample Answer:

  • Use minimal water when cooking vegetables to prevent nutrient loss.
  • Avoid overcooking vegetables to retain their nutritional value.
  • Store vegetables in a cool, dry place to preserve their nutrients.
  • Use fresh vegetables whenever possible to maximize nutrient content.

(b) Highlight four ways of maintaining good health of a child at an ECDE centre. (4 marks)

Sample Answer:

  • Ensure regular handwashing to prevent the spread of germs.
  • Provide a balanced diet to support the child's growth and development.
  • Encourage regular physical activity to promote overall health.
  • Maintain a clean and safe environment to prevent accidents and infections.

Question 2

(a) State the uses of three improvised items of a First Aid kit in an ECDE centre. (3 marks)

Sample Answer:

  • A clean cloth can be used to stop bleeding or as a bandage.
  • A plastic bag can be used as an ice pack when filled with ice.
  • A piece of string can be used as a tourniquet in emergencies.

(b) State four measures a teacher may take to prevent the spread of respiratory tract infections among children in an ECDE centre. (4 marks)

Sample Answer:

  • Encourage children to cover their mouths when coughing or sneezing.
  • Ensure proper ventilation in the classroom.
  • Promote regular handwashing with soap and water.
  • Isolate children showing symptoms of respiratory infections.

Question 3

(a) Identify three factors that influence growth in children. (3 marks)

Sample Answer:

  • Genetic factors inherited from parents.
  • Nutritional intake and diet.
  • Environmental factors such as access to healthcare and living conditions.

Question 4

(a) Define the term client as used in counselling. (1 mark)

Sample Answer:

A client is an individual who seeks professional help or guidance from a counsellor to address personal, emotional, or psychological issues.

(b) State four characteristics of congruence as a quality of a good counsellor. (4 marks)

Sample Answer:

  • Being genuine and authentic in interactions with clients.
  • Maintaining consistency between words and actions.
  • Being transparent and honest in communication.
  • Demonstrating self-awareness and emotional stability.

(c) Give five factors that may cause examination fever. (5 marks)

Sample Answer:

  • Fear of failure or poor performance.
  • High expectations from parents or teachers.
  • Lack of adequate preparation for the exam.
  • Pressure to perform well in competitive environments.
  • Anxiety about the consequences of poor results.

SECTION B (60 marks)

Answer FOUR questions from this section.

Question 5

(a) Outline seven categories of people who should seek VCT services. (7 marks)

Sample Answer:

  • Individuals engaging in unprotected sex.
  • People who have multiple sexual partners.
  • Individuals who share needles or syringes.
  • Pregnant women to prevent mother-to-child transmission.
  • Healthcare workers exposed to blood or bodily fluids.
  • People who have been sexually assaulted.
  • Individuals with symptoms of HIV/AIDS.

(b) Highlight four social factors that put women at risk of contracting HIV. (4 marks)

Sample Answer:

  • Gender inequality and lack of empowerment.
  • Cultural practices that promote early marriage.
  • Economic dependence on male partners.
  • Lack of access to education and healthcare.

(c) Explain two relationships between STIs and AIDS. (4 marks)

Sample Answer:

  • STIs can increase the risk of HIV transmission due to open sores or inflammation.
  • People with STIs are more likely to engage in risky sexual behaviors, increasing the likelihood of HIV infection.

Question 6

(a) (i) State four symptoms of diarrhoea and vomiting among children. (4 marks)

Sample Answer:

  • Frequent loose or watery stools.
  • Nausea and vomiting.
  • Abdominal pain or cramps.
  • Dehydration, indicated by dry mouth or reduced urination.

(ii) Identify three ways of preventing diarrhoea and vomiting among children. (3 marks)

Sample Answer:

  • Ensuring proper handwashing with soap and water.
  • Providing safe and clean drinking water.
  • Promoting breastfeeding for infants to boost immunity.

(b) Describe the procedure for treating a child who has been stung by a bee. (8 marks)

Sample Answer:

  1. Remove the stinger by scraping it off with a flat object.
  2. Wash the affected area with soap and water.
  3. Apply a cold compress to reduce swelling and pain.
  4. Monitor the child for signs of an allergic reaction, such as difficulty breathing or swelling of the face.
  5. Seek medical attention if an allergic reaction occurs.

Question 7

(a) State two strategies that can be used to promote community health. (2 marks)

Sample Answer:

  • Health education campaigns to raise awareness about preventive measures.
  • Providing access to clean water and sanitation facilities.

(b) Give five reasons why counselling and testing for HIV and AIDS is important. (5 marks)

Sample Answer:

  • Early detection allows for timely medical intervention.
  • Reduces the risk of transmitting the virus to others.
  • Provides emotional and psychological support to those affected.
  • Encourages behavior change to prevent further infections.
  • Helps in planning for future health and lifestyle adjustments.

(c) Explain four different ways through which poisoning may occur to a child at home. (8 marks)

Sample Answer:

  • Ingestion of household chemicals or medications.
  • Inhalation of toxic fumes from cleaning products.
  • Skin contact with harmful substances like pesticides.
  • Accidental consumption of poisonous plants or berries.

Question 8

(a) Outline six factors that may affect normal development of a foetus. (6 marks)

Sample Answer:

  • Maternal nutrition and diet during pregnancy.
  • Exposure to harmful substances like alcohol or tobacco.
  • Maternal health conditions such as diabetes or hypertension.
  • Infections during pregnancy, such as rubella or toxoplasmosis.
  • Genetic or chromosomal abnormalities.
  • Stress and emotional well-being of the mother.

(b) Identify five methods of modern food preservation that can be used in the home. (5 marks)

Sample Answer:

  • Refrigeration to slow down bacterial growth.
  • Freezing to preserve food for longer periods.
  • Canning to seal food in airtight containers.
  • Drying or dehydrating to remove moisture.
  • Using preservatives like salt or sugar to inhibit microbial growth.

(c) State four ways of ensuring good hygiene when preparing food in the kitchen at an ECDE centre. (4 marks)

Sample Answer:

  • Washing hands thoroughly before handling food.
  • Keeping kitchen surfaces clean and sanitized.
  • Storing food at the correct temperature to prevent spoilage.
  • Using separate utensils for raw and cooked foods to avoid cross-contamination.

Question 9

(a) (i) Explain the meaning of educational guidance. (2 marks)

Sample Answer:

Educational guidance refers to the process of helping students make informed decisions about their education, career paths, and personal development.

(ii) Identify four advantages of educational guidance. (4 marks)

Sample Answer:

  • Helps students identify their strengths and interests.
  • Provides support in making career and academic choices.
  • Encourages personal and social development.
  • Reduces dropout rates by addressing students' needs and challenges.

(b) (i) Define substance abuse. (2 marks)

Sample Answer:

Substance abuse refers to the harmful or excessive use of drugs or alcohol, leading to physical, psychological, or social problems.

(ii) Give three reasons why the youth use drugs. (3 marks)

Sample Answer:

  • Peer pressure and the desire to fit in with friends.
  • Curiosity and experimentation with new experiences.
  • Stress or emotional problems leading to self-medication.

(c) State four issues a counsellor would assist a client to establish during the exploration stage of counselling. (4 marks)

Sample Answer:

  • Identifying the client's main concerns or problems.
  • Exploring the client's feelings and emotions related to the issue.
  • Understanding the client's goals and expectations from counselling.
  • Assessing the client's readiness for change and commitment to the process.
