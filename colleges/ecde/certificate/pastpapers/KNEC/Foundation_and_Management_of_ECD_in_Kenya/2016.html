CERTIFICATE IN EARLY CHILDHOOD DEVELOPMENT AND EDUCATION
FOUNDATIONS AND MANAGEMENT OF ECDE, 2016

Instructions to Candidates:

Write your name and index number in the spaces provided above.

Sign and write the date of the examination in the spaces provided above.

This question paper consists of TWO sections; A and B.

Answer ALL the questions in section A and any FOUR questions from section B.

Answers to both section A and B must be written in the spaces provided in this booklet.

Do NOT remove any pages from this booklet.

Candidates should answer the questions in English.

Question 1

(a) Define the term data as used in Research. (2 marks)

Sample Answer:

Data refers to raw facts, figures, or information collected for analysis in research.

(b) Give four advantages of using frequency tables for data presentation in Research. (4 marks)

Sample Answer:

  1. Simplifies complex data.
  2. Facilitates easy comparison of data.
  3. Helps in identifying patterns and trends.
  4. Provides a clear and organized summary of data.

(c) Outline four benefits to a trainee who uses observation method when collecting data in an Early Childhood Development and Education (ECDE) centre. (4 marks)

Sample Answer:

  1. Provides firsthand information.
  2. Enhances understanding of children's behavior.
  3. Allows for real-time data collection.
  4. Helps in identifying non-verbal cues.

Question 2

(a) Distinguish between capacity building and community empowerment. (4 marks)

Sample Answer:

Capacity building focuses on enhancing the skills, knowledge, and abilities of individuals or groups, while community empowerment involves enabling communities to take control of their own development and decision-making processes.

(b) State four elements of a communication process between an ECDE teacher and the community. (4 marks)

Sample Answer:

  1. Sender (ECDE teacher).
  2. Message (information to be communicated).
  3. Medium (channel of communication).
  4. Receiver (community members).

Question 3

(a) Explain the term General Knowledge. (2 marks)

Sample Answer:

General Knowledge refers to a broad understanding of various subjects and topics that are not specialized or technical.

(b) Give five reasons that make integrity an important value for a pre-school teacher in the society. (5 marks)

Sample Answer:

  1. Builds trust with parents and the community.
  2. Sets a good example for children.
  3. Promotes ethical behavior.
  4. Enhances professional reputation.
  5. Encourages fairness and honesty in teaching.

(c) State three characteristics of a socialism type of Government. (3 marks)

Sample Answer:

  1. Collective ownership of resources.
  2. Equitable distribution of wealth.
  3. Government control of key industries.

Question 4

(a) Give four reasons for maintaining permanent store records in an ECDE centre. (4 marks)

Sample Answer:

  1. Ensures accountability of resources.
  2. Facilitates inventory management.
  3. Helps in budgeting and planning.
  4. Provides a reference for audits.

(b) Identify the type of information that a Sub-County Early Childhood Coordinator would find in each of the following records kept in an ECDE centre:

(i) log book; (3 marks)

Sample Answer:

A log book contains records of daily activities, incidents, and maintenance activities in the ECDE centre.

(ii) visitors book. (3 marks)

Sample Answer:

A visitors book contains records of visitors, including their names, purpose of visit, and time of visit.

Question 6

(a) Identify seven ways in which the County Governments would effectively participate in community capacity building and mobilization of ECDE programmes in Kenya. (7 marks)

Sample Answer:

  1. Providing funding for ECDE programs.
  2. Training community members on ECDE practices.
  3. Establishing ECDE centers in underserved areas.
  4. Collaborating with NGOs and other stakeholders.
  5. Conducting awareness campaigns on the importance of ECDE.
  6. Monitoring and evaluating ECDE programs.
  7. Providing resources and materials for ECDE centers.

(b) Examine four disadvantages of using indirect mode of communication when mobilizing the community to support an ECDE project. (8 marks)

Sample Answer:

  1. Delayed feedback.
  2. Misinterpretation of the message.
  3. Limited interaction with the audience.
  4. Reduced personal connection with the community.

Question 7

  2. Respect for others' opinions.
  3. Transparency.
  4. Accountability.
  5. Fairness.
  6. Encourages participation.
  7. Open to feedback.

  7. Geographical challenges.
  8. Low awareness of ECDE importance.

Question 8

Question 9

(a) Identify five ethical considerations that an ECDE teacher trainee should ensure when making preparations for data collection for research. (5 marks)

Sample Answer:

  1. Informed consent from participants.
  2. Confidentiality of data.
  3. Avoiding harm to participants.
  4. Transparency in research methods.
  5. Respect for participants' rights.

(b) Explain five ways in which research data would be useful in ECDE. (10 marks)

Sample Answer:

  1. Informing policy decisions.
  2. Improving teaching methods.
  3. Enhancing curriculum development.
  4. Identifying areas for improvement.
  5. Providing evidence-based practices.
