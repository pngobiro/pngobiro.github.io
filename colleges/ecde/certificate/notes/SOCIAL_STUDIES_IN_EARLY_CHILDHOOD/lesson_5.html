Lesson 5

Social Studies Resources for Teaching

Objectives

By the end of the lesson, students should be able to:

  • Describe the criteria for selection of instructional media for social studies in ECE.
  • Discuss types of instructional media for social studies in ECE.
  • Discuss methods of sourcing instructional media for social studies in ECE.

Introduction

Children should interact with materials to acquire skills, ask questions, and receive answers. Teachers should facilitate social interaction and curiosity. Well-prepared materials encourage children to practice familiar skills and tackle new challenges.

Selection Criteria for Social Studies Materials

Planning for social studies lessons should include identifying activities, collecting materials, and conducting activities to achieve lesson objectives.

Guidelines for Selecting Materials

  • Safety: Avoid materials with rough edges, sharp corners, or small parts that children may ingest. Ensure cleanliness.
  • Durability: Choose long-lasting materials like plastic or hard wood to reduce costs.
  • Function: Ensure materials are in working condition and can be manipulated by children.
  • Relevance: Materials should match the concepts or skills to be taught and be age-appropriate.
  • Appearance: Use attractive materials with different colors, shapes, and textures to sustain interest.
  • Variety: Provide various materials to allow freedom of choice and cater to different learning needs.
  • Versatility: Use multipurpose materials that develop multiple skills, like language, math, or social studies concepts.

Care of Materials

To ensure materials last, teachers should model responsibility. Some care methods include:

  • Storing materials according to type, such as washable, dustable, or laminated items.
  • Using stain repellents or stitching to preserve materials like dolls or puppets.
  • Sanding rough edges and painting wooden items for durability and ease of cleaning.
  • Repairing damaged items promptly to avoid further wear and costs.

Sourcing Materials for Social Studies

Teachers should use real materials, or realia, which are effective in teaching. Materials can be gathered by teachers and students and displayed in various learning centers.

Examples of Social Studies Materials

  • Charts: Large sheets displaying information, typically made from durable materials like manila or polythene sheets.
  • Flash Cards: Small, durable cards for matching activities, made with visible text and durable materials like old plastics.
  • Picture Cards: Pictures glued onto cardboard, covered with clear polythene for protection.
  • Thematic Picture Books: Collections of topic-related images organized in a book, useful for storytelling.
  • Chalkboard: An essential teaching tool, used for drawings and student engagement in class illustrations.
  • Puppets: Sock or string puppets that stimulate creativity and storytelling.
  • Models: Physical representations of objects, created with materials like clay or papier-mâché.

Types of Instructional Resources for Pre-School Social Studies

Instructional resources are vital for helping children understand social studies concepts, fostering engagement, and making abstract ideas concrete. These resources include:

1. Reading Materials

Reading materials help children learn various skills and concepts. Examples include:

  • Books: Must cater to individual differences, including reading disabilities and visual impairments.
  • Newspapers: Provide awareness of current events in the physical and social environment.
  • Magazines: Broaden children's knowledge with topics on environment, animals, and plants.

2. Audio-Visual Resources

Audio-visual materials enhance learning through sensory engagement, including:

  • Realia: Real specimens from the environment.
  • Artifacts: Cultural objects like gourds or sculptures.
  • Photographs and Pictures: Visual aids that help explain societal and environmental concepts.
  • Cartoons: Simple, humorous illustrations that convey ideas effectively.
  • Globes and Maps: Tools for teaching location, direction, and distance.
  • Media Programs: Educational programs on radio, television, and videos.

3. Community Resources

Community resources can be integrated into the classroom through:

  • Creating a social studies corner with materials from the local environment.
  • Field trips to community sites.
  • Inviting guest speakers.
  • Using audio-visual materials that depict community experiences.

The Social Studies Corner

This is a designated classroom area where relevant materials for social studies are displayed. It is the teacher's responsibility to ensure these materials are useful and educational.

Activity

  1. Identify locally available materials for teaching social studies in your school.
  2. Choose a social studies theme and list materials that would support instruction for that theme.
