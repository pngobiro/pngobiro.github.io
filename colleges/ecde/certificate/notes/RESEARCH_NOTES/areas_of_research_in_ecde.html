AREAS OF RESEARCH IN ECDE

a) Child growth and development

Aspects researched include: mental, language, social, emotional, psychomotor, moral, and spiritual developments.

b) Child rearing practices

Research generates appropriate skills and describes attributes significant in holistic child development. Research locations may include children's homes, orphanages, and rehabilitation centers.

c) Children learning

Research focuses on how children acquire knowledge, informing teacher and caregiver strategies. Methodologies can be child-centered, teacher-centered, or participatory; teaching approaches include thematic, Montessori, and play-based.

d) Transition in ECDE

Research areas include the transition of children from home to ECDE centers and from ECDE centers to primary school.

e) Community involvement

Focuses on community participation in ECDE, including school establishment, feeding programs, funding, resource provision, and teacher payment.

f) Health, nutrition, and care

Research areas include feeding programs, immunization effects, facilitating child health during learning, and types of care provided at different ages.
