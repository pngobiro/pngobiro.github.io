2016

Diploma in Early Childhood Development and Education
December 2016

Instructions to Candidates:

Answer any FIVE questions. Use clear and concise language.

Question 1

(a) Distinguish between the philosophies of Johann H. Pestalozzi and Friedrich W. Froebel in Early Childhood Development and Education. (4 marks)

Sample Answer:

  • Johann H. Pestalozzi emphasized the importance of sensory learning and believed that education should be based on the natural development of the child.
  • Friedrich W. Froebel, on the other hand, is known for developing the concept of kindergarten and believed in the importance of play and self-activity in early childhood education.

(b) Explain the following provisions as outlined in the Children’s Act, 2001:

(i) Right to parental care; (3 marks)

Sample Answer:

  • The Children’s Act, 2001, guarantees every child the right to parental care, which includes the right to live with and be cared for by their parents or guardians.

(ii) Protection from child labour and armed conflict. (3 marks)

Sample Answer:

  • The Act protects children from engaging in child labour and from being recruited or used in armed conflict, ensuring their safety and well-being.

(c) Discuss five reasons that make philosophy relevant to ECDE teachers. (10 marks)

Sample Answer:

  1. Philosophy helps teachers understand the purpose and goals of education.
  2. It provides a framework for making decisions about curriculum and teaching methods.
  3. Philosophy encourages critical thinking and reflection on teaching practices.
  4. It helps teachers develop a personal teaching philosophy that guides their interactions with children.
  5. Philosophy promotes a deeper understanding of child development and learning theories.

Question 2

(a) State two differences between The African Charter on the Rights and Welfare of the Child, 1990 and the United Nations Convention on the Rights of the Child, 1989. (4 marks)

Sample Answer:

  • The African Charter specifically addresses issues relevant to African children, such as cultural practices and armed conflicts, while the UN Convention has a more global perspective.
  • The African Charter emphasizes the role of the community in child upbringing, whereas the UN Convention focuses more on the role of the state and parents.

(b) Explain four positive ways in which children were perceived in African Traditional Societies. (8 marks)

Sample Answer:

  1. Children were seen as a blessing and a continuation of the family lineage.
  2. They were valued for their potential to contribute to the community’s future.
  3. Children were often involved in communal activities, fostering a sense of belonging.
  4. They were respected and cared for by the entire community, not just their immediate family.

(c) Outline four negative effects of illiterate parents on ECDE learners. (8 marks)

Sample Answer:

  1. Illiterate parents may struggle to support their children’s learning at home.
  2. They may not understand the importance of early childhood education, leading to low enrollment rates.
  3. Illiterate parents may have limited communication with teachers, affecting the child’s progress.
  4. They may not be able to provide a stimulating learning environment at home.

Question 3

(a) Identify two similarities of ECDE objectives in Kenya and Ghana. (2 marks)

Sample Answer:

  • Both countries aim to provide a strong foundation for lifelong learning and development.
  • Both emphasize the importance of holistic development, including physical, emotional, and social growth.

(b) State four achievements of the pre-school education project co-founded by the Ministry of Education, Science and Technology and Bernard Van Leer Foundation. (8 marks)

Sample Answer:

  1. Increased access to pre-school education for children in underserved areas.
  2. Improved quality of early childhood education through teacher training programs.
  3. Development of culturally relevant teaching materials.
  4. Enhanced community involvement in early childhood education initiatives.

(c) Explain five ways in which ECDE learners may benefit from mobile schools as a measure to increase access to basic education. (10 marks)

Sample Answer:

  1. Mobile schools can reach remote and marginalized communities, providing access to education for children who would otherwise be excluded.
  2. They offer flexible learning schedules, accommodating the needs of children who may have other responsibilities.
  3. Mobile schools can provide a safe and supportive learning environment, especially in areas affected by conflict or natural disasters.
  4. They can deliver tailored educational programs that meet the specific needs of the community.
  5. Mobile schools can help bridge the gap in education during emergencies, ensuring continuity of learning.

Question 4

(a) Explain the term syllabus. (2 marks)

Sample Answer:

  • A syllabus is a document that outlines the topics, objectives, and content to be covered in a particular course or subject.

(b) State four approaches of sustaining learners attention in an ECDE class. (8 marks)

Sample Answer:

  1. Use of interactive and engaging teaching methods, such as storytelling and games.
  2. Incorporating visual aids and hands-on activities to make learning more interesting.
  3. Maintaining a positive and supportive classroom environment.
  4. Providing regular breaks and opportunities for physical activity to keep children focused.

(c) Identify five factors considered when including topics of a subject in the syllabus. (10 marks)

Sample Answer:

  1. Relevance to the learners’ age and developmental stage.
  2. Alignment with the overall educational goals and objectives.
  3. Cultural and social relevance to the learners’ context.
  4. Availability of resources and teaching materials.
  5. Feedback from educators and stakeholders on the importance of the topics.

Question 5

(a) Give two benefits of devolving ECDE services to the county governments. (4 marks)

Sample Answer:

  • Devolving ECDE services allows for more localized decision-making, ensuring that programs are tailored to the specific needs of the community.
  • It can lead to more efficient use of resources, as county governments are better positioned to identify and address local challenges.

(b) Describe three challenges the Kindergarten Headmistresses’ Association (KHA) may face in performing its duties. (6 marks)

Sample Answer:

  1. Limited funding and resources to support their initiatives.
  2. Difficulty in coordinating efforts across different regions and schools.
  3. Resistance to change from some educators and stakeholders.

(c) Explain five functions of Education Standard and Quality Assurance Council in ECDE curriculum development. (10 marks)

Sample Answer:

  1. Setting and maintaining standards for early childhood education.
  2. Monitoring and evaluating the implementation of the curriculum.
  3. Providing guidance and support to educators on curriculum delivery.
  4. Conducting research to inform curriculum development and improvement.
  5. Ensuring that the curriculum is inclusive and meets the needs of all learners.

Question 6

(a) Identify four ways in which national unity can be nurtured in an ECDE centre. (4 marks)

Sample Answer:

  1. Promoting cultural diversity and inclusion through activities and celebrations.
  2. Teaching children about the importance of unity and cooperation.
  3. Encouraging collaborative projects and group activities.
  4. Providing opportunities for children to interact with peers from different backgrounds.

(b) Name and explain the three levels of presentation that children go through according to Jerome Brunner. (6 marks)

Sample Answer:

  1. Enactive Level: Children learn through direct manipulation of objects and physical actions.
  2. Iconic Level: Children use images and visual representations to understand concepts.
  3. Symbolic Level: Children use symbols, such as language and numbers, to represent ideas and solve problems.

(c) Describe five contributions of Jean Piaget to Early Childhood Development and Education. (10 marks)

Sample Answer:

  1. Piaget’s theory of cognitive development provides a framework for understanding how children learn and think.
  2. He emphasized the importance of play in children’s learning and development.
  3. Piaget introduced the concept of stages of development, which helps educators tailor their teaching methods to the child’s developmental level.
  4. He highlighted the role of active learning, where children construct knowledge through exploration and experimentation.
  5. Piaget’s work has influenced the development of child-centered approaches to education.
