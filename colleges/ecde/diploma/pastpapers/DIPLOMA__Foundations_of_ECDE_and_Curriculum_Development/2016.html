Foundations of ECDE and Curriculum Development

Questions and Sample Answers

  1. (a) Distinguish between the philosophies of Johann H. Pestalozzi and Friedrich W. Froebel in Early Childhood Development and Education. (4 marks)

    Answer:

    • Johann H. Pestalozzi: Emphasized the importance of hands-on learning and the role of the teacher as a guide. He believed in educating the "head, heart, and hands" to develop a child holistically.
    • Friedrich W. Froebel: Known as the father of the kindergarten system, Froebel focused on play-based learning and the use of toys (gifts) to stimulate creativity and imagination in children.

    (b) Explain the following provisions as outlined in the Children’s Act, 2001:

    (i) Right to parental care; (3 marks)

    Answer:

    The Children’s Act, 2001, guarantees every child the right to parental care, which includes the provision of basic needs such as food, shelter, education, and emotional support. Parents or guardians are legally obligated to ensure the well-being and development of their children.

    (ii) Protection from child labour and armed conflict. (3 marks)

    Answer:

    The Act prohibits the engagement of children in labor that is harmful to their health, education, or development. It also protects children from being recruited or used in armed conflicts, ensuring their safety and well-being.

    (c) Discuss five reasons that make philosophy relevant to ECDE teachers. (10 marks)

    Answer:

    1. Helps teachers understand the purpose and goals of education.
    2. Guides teachers in developing child-centered teaching methods.
    3. Encourages critical thinking and reflection on teaching practices.
    4. Promotes the holistic development of children (physical, emotional, social, and cognitive).
    5. Supports the creation of inclusive and equitable learning environments.

  2. (a) State two differences between The African Charter on the Rights and Welfare of the Child, 1990 and the United Nations Convention on the Rights of the Child, 1989. (4 marks)

    Answer:

    1. African Charter: Focuses on the specific cultural and social context of African children. UN Convention: Provides a universal framework applicable to all children globally.
    2. African Charter: Emphasizes the role of the extended family and community in child-rearing. UN Convention: Focuses more on the rights of the child within the nuclear family.

    (b) Explain four positive ways in which children were perceived in African Traditional Societies. (8 marks)

    Answer:

    1. Children were seen as a blessing and a sign of prosperity.
    2. They were valued as future leaders and contributors to the community.
    3. Children were considered a link between the past (ancestors) and the future.
    4. They were seen as a source of joy and pride for the family and community.

    (c) Outline four negative effects of illiterate parents on ECDE learners. (8 marks)

    Answer:

    1. Limited ability to support children’s learning at home.
    2. Difficulty in understanding the importance of early childhood education.
    3. Inability to assist with homework or school-related activities.
    4. Lower likelihood of advocating for their children’s educational needs.

  3. (a) Identify two similarities of ECDE objectives in Kenya and Ghana. (2 marks)

    Answer:

    1. Both aim to promote holistic development (physical, emotional, social, and cognitive).
    2. Both emphasize the importance of preparing children for primary education.

    (b) State four achievements of the pre-school education project co-founded by the Ministry of Education, Science and Technology and Bernard Van Leer Foundation. (8 marks)

    Answer:

    1. Improved access to pre-school education in underserved areas.
    2. Enhanced teacher training and professional development.
    3. Development of child-centered teaching materials and resources.
    4. Increased community involvement in early childhood education.

    (c) Explain five ways in which ECDE learners may benefit from mobile schools as a measure to increase access to basic education. (10 marks)

    Answer:

    1. Mobile schools bring education to remote and marginalized areas.
    2. They provide flexible learning schedules for children who cannot attend regular schools.
    3. Mobile schools offer tailored curricula to meet the needs of specific communities.
    4. They promote inclusivity by reaching out-of-school children.
    5. Mobile schools often provide additional support services, such as meals and healthcare.

  4. (a) Explain the term syllabus. (2 marks)

    Answer:

    A syllabus is a document that outlines the topics, content, and learning objectives for a specific subject or course. It serves as a guide for teachers and students in the teaching and learning process.

    (b) State four approaches of sustaining learners' attention in an ECDE class. (8 marks)

    Answer:

    1. Use of interactive and hands-on activities.
    2. Incorporating games and play into lessons.
    3. Using visual aids and multimedia resources.
    4. Maintaining a positive and engaging classroom environment.

    (c) Identify five factors considered when including topics of a subject in the syllabus. (10 marks)

    Answer:

    1. Relevance to the learners' age and developmental stage.
    2. Alignment with the overall goals of the curriculum.
    3. Availability of teaching resources and materials.
    4. Cultural and social relevance to the learners' context.
    5. Potential to promote critical thinking and problem-solving skills.

  5. (a) Give two benefits of devolving ECDE services to the county governments. (4 marks)

    Answer:

    1. Improved access to ECDE services at the local level.
    2. Enhanced community involvement and ownership of ECDE programs.

    (b) Describe three challenges the Kindergarten Headmistresses’ Association (KHA) may face in performing its duties. (6 marks)

    Answer:

    1. Limited funding and resources to support ECDE programs.
    2. Difficulty in coordinating activities across different regions.
    3. Lack of adequate training and professional development for members.

    (c) Explain five functions of Education Standard and Quality Assurance Council in ECDE curriculum development. (10 marks)

    Answer:

    1. Setting and maintaining standards for ECDE curriculum and teaching practices.
    2. Monitoring and evaluating the implementation of the curriculum.
    3. Providing guidelines for teacher training and professional development.
    4. Ensuring the quality of teaching and learning materials.
    5. Conducting research to inform curriculum improvements.

  6. (a) Identify four ways in which national unity can be nurtured in an ECDE centre. (4 marks)

    Answer:

    1. Promoting cultural diversity and inclusivity in the classroom.
    2. Teaching children about the importance of national unity and cohesion.
    3. Encouraging teamwork and collaboration among learners.
    4. Celebrating national holidays and cultural events.

    (b) Name and explain the three levels of presentation that children go through according to Jerome Brunner. (6 marks)

    Answer:

    1. Enactive Level: Children learn through physical actions and hands-on experiences.
    2. Iconic Level: Children use visual representations, such as pictures and diagrams, to understand concepts.
    3. Symbolic Level: Children use abstract symbols, such as language and numbers, to represent ideas.

    (c) Describe five contributions of Jean Piaget to Early Childhood Development and Education. (10 marks)

    Answer:

    1. Piaget’s theory of cognitive development emphasizes the importance of stages in children’s learning.
    2. He highlighted the role of play in cognitive development.
    3. Piaget’s work influenced the development of child-centered learning approaches.
    4. He emphasized the importance of hands-on and experiential learning.
    5. Piaget’s research provided a foundation for understanding how children construct knowledge.
