DIPLOMA IN EARLY CHILDHOOD DEVELOPMENT AND EDUCATION
FOUNDATIONS OF ECDE AND CURRICULUM DEVELOPMENT, 2012

Instructions to Candidates:

Write your name and index number in the spaces provided above.

Sign and write the date of examination in the spaces provided above.

This paper consists of SIX questions.

Answer any FIVE questions in the spaces provided after question 6.

Each question carries a total of 20 marks.

Do NOT remove any pages from this booklet.

Question 1

(a) Explain the term, ‘sociology’. (2 marks)

Sample Answer:

Sociology is the study of human social relationships, institutions, and societies. It examines how individuals interact within social structures and how these structures influence behavior.

(b) Explain five rights of a parent as outlined in the Children’s Act 2001. (10 marks)

Sample Answer:

  1. Right to care and protection of the child.
  2. Right to provide guidance and discipline.
  3. Right to make decisions regarding the child's education.
  4. Right to access information about the child's welfare.
  5. Right to participate in the child's upbringing and development.

(c) Suggest four measures that an ECDE centre can take to reduce incidences of child abuse. (8 marks)

Sample Answer:

  1. Implementing strict child protection policies.
  2. Training staff on recognizing and reporting abuse.
  3. Creating a safe and supportive environment for children.
  4. Encouraging open communication with parents and guardians.

Question 2

(a) Outline four challenges facing ECDE after the introduction of Free Primary Education (F.P.E.). (4 marks)

Sample Answer:

  1. Increased enrollment leading to overcrowding.
  2. Inadequate funding for ECDE programs.
  3. Shortage of trained ECDE teachers.
  4. Lack of adequate infrastructure and resources.

(b) Explain five similarities between Maria Montessori’s and Froebel’s Philosophies of Early Childhood learning. (10 marks)

Sample Answer:

  1. Both emphasize the importance of play in learning.
  2. Both advocate for child-centered education.
  3. Both stress the importance of the environment in learning.
  4. Both believe in the natural development of the child.
  5. Both promote hands-on learning experiences.

(c) Explain three measures that an ECDE centre in Kenya can take to mitigate hunger. (6 marks)

Sample Answer:

  1. Providing school feeding programs.
  2. Partnering with NGOs for food donations.
  3. Educating parents on nutrition and food security.

Question 3

(a) Describe four ways through which the African traditional society imparted skills in young children. (8 marks)

Sample Answer:

  1. Through storytelling and oral traditions.
  2. By involving children in daily chores and activities.
  3. Through apprenticeship and mentorship.
  4. By participating in cultural ceremonies and rituals.

(b) Highlight three principles advocated by Jean Jacques Rousseau that are in practice today in ECDE centres in Kenya. (6 marks)

Sample Answer:

  1. Child-centered learning.
  2. Learning through experience and exploration.
  3. Respect for the natural development of the child.

(c) Discuss three similarities between the provision of ECDE in Kenya and Ghana. (6 marks)

Sample Answer:

  1. Both countries emphasize the importance of early childhood education.
  2. Both face challenges related to funding and resources.
  3. Both have government policies supporting ECDE programs.

Question 4

(a) Explain the term, ‘hidden curriculum.’ (2 marks)

Sample Answer:

The hidden curriculum refers to the unwritten, unofficial, and often unintended lessons, values, and perspectives that students learn in school, such as social norms and cultural expectations.

(b) Highlight four goals of education in Kenya and give relevant examples of how the ECDE curriculum is helping attain these goals. (8 marks)

Sample Answer:

  1. Promoting national unity: ECDE curriculum includes activities that encourage cooperation and understanding among children from different backgrounds.
  2. Fostering individual development: The curriculum focuses on holistic development, including cognitive, emotional, and social skills.
  3. Promoting social equality: ECDE programs aim to provide equal opportunities for all children, regardless of their socio-economic status.
  4. Preparing children for future education: The curriculum lays a strong foundation for primary education by developing basic literacy and numeracy skills.

(c) Explain five instruments that the Kenya Institute of Education (K.I.E.) may use to evaluate the ECDE curriculum. (10 marks)

Sample Answer:

  1. Surveys and questionnaires.
  2. Classroom observations.
  3. Interviews with teachers and parents.
  4. Standardized tests.
  5. Focus group discussions.

Question 5

(a) List the seven steps of curriculum development as indicated in Gilbert P. Olnoch’s theory. (7 marks)

Sample Answer:

  1. Needs assessment.
  2. Setting objectives.
  3. Content selection.
  4. Organization of content.
  5. Selection of learning experiences.
  6. Organization of learning experiences.
  7. Evaluation.

(b) Explain four categories of physical and outdoor activities in which learners may be involved. (8 marks)

Sample Answer:

  1. Sports and games: Activities like football, athletics, and relay races.
  2. Nature exploration: Activities like gardening, nature walks, and bird watching.
  3. Creative play: Activities like building sandcastles, playing with water, and role-playing.
  4. Adventure activities: Activities like obstacle courses, camping, and hiking.

(c) State five measures that can be taken to reduce negative effect of commercializing ECDE teacher training in Kenya. (5 marks)

Sample Answer:

  1. Regulating private training institutions.
  2. Providing government subsidies for teacher training.
  3. Enhancing quality assurance mechanisms.
  4. Encouraging public-private partnerships.
  5. Promoting continuous professional development for teachers.

Question 6

(a) Explain four principles that should govern ECDE curriculum development according to Jerome Bruner. (8 marks)

Sample Answer:

  1. Spiral organization: Revisiting key concepts at increasing levels of complexity.
  2. Readiness: Ensuring that the curriculum is appropriate for the child's developmental stage.
  3. Intuitive thinking: Encouraging children to explore and discover knowledge on their own.
  4. Motivation: Creating a curriculum that engages and motivates children to learn.

(b) State four ways in which an ECDE centre can promote health care needs of the learners. (4 marks)

Sample Answer:

  1. Providing regular health check-ups.
  2. Ensuring a clean and safe environment.
  3. Educating children on hygiene practices.
  4. Offering nutritious meals and snacks.

(c) Discuss the role of the Teachers Service Commission (TSC) in the development and implementation of curriculum in Kenya. (8 marks)

Sample Answer:

The Teachers Service Commission (TSC) plays a crucial role in curriculum development and implementation by recruiting, training, and deploying qualified teachers. It also ensures that teachers are continuously trained on new curriculum changes and provides guidelines for effective curriculum delivery. Additionally, TSC monitors and evaluates the performance of teachers to ensure that the curriculum is effectively implemented in schools.
