Question 1

(a) Outline two positive effects of the Gachathi Report (1976) to ECDE. (4 marks)

1. The Gachathi Report led to the establishment of more Early Childhood Development and Education (ECDE) centers, increasing access to early education.

2. It emphasized the importance of teacher training, leading to improved quality of education in ECDE centers.

(b) Explain three factors of urbanization that have impacted positively on Early Childhood Development and Education. (6 marks)

1. Urbanization has led to better infrastructure, such as schools and healthcare facilities, which support ECDE.

2. Increased access to information and communication technologies (ICT) in urban areas has enhanced learning opportunities for young children.

3. Urban areas often have more diverse populations, which can enrich the cultural experiences of children in ECDE centers.

(c) “Education in Kenya should foster nationalism, patriotism and promote national unity”. State five learning activities through which the above national goal of education can be implemented in ECDE. (10 marks)

1. Celebrating national holidays and teaching children about their significance.

2. Singing the national anthem and other patriotic songs.

3. Organizing cultural days where children learn about different ethnic groups in Kenya.

4. Teaching children about national heroes and their contributions to the country.

5. Encouraging community service projects that promote unity and cooperation.

Question 2

(a) State two ways in which home-based care centres have enhanced early childhood learning. (4 marks)

1. Home-based care centers provide a familiar and comfortable environment for young children, which can enhance their learning experience.

2. They offer personalized attention to children, allowing for tailored learning experiences that meet individual needs.

(b) Highlight three positive skills acquired by children from watching television. (6 marks)

1. Language and communication skills through educational programs and storytelling.

2. Cognitive skills such as problem-solving and critical thinking from interactive shows.

3. Social skills by observing and learning from characters' interactions and behaviors.

(c) Identify five protective rights as indicated in the Children’s Act, 2001. (10 marks)

1. The right to protection from abuse, neglect, and exploitation.

2. The right to education and access to educational materials.

3. The right to healthcare and access to medical services.

4. The right to protection from harmful cultural practices.

5. The right to be protected from child labor and hazardous work.

Question 3

(a) Highlight two challenges experienced by ECDE teachers working with pastoral communities. (4 marks)

1. Limited access to educational resources and materials due to the remote locations of pastoral communities.

2. High mobility of pastoral families, which can lead to inconsistent attendance of children in ECDE centers.

(b) Outline four characteristics of ECDE centres established by the missionaries at pre-independence. (8 marks)

1. They were often attached to churches and had a strong religious influence on the curriculum.

2. The centers focused on basic literacy and numeracy skills.

3. They provided education primarily to the children of converts and local elites.

4. The centers were often underfunded and relied on donations and support from missionary organizations.

(c) “The learners were busy and enjoying the free-choice activities; The teacher sung a familiar song for them to stop and change over”. Highlight four advantages of involving learners in the clearing up activity. (8 marks)

1. It teaches children responsibility and the importance of taking care of their environment.

2. It helps develop fine motor skills as children handle and organize materials.

3. It encourages teamwork and cooperation among children.

4. It provides a smooth transition between activities, reducing chaos and confusion.

Question 4

(a) State the meaning of the term education. (2 marks)

Education is the process of facilitating learning, acquiring knowledge, skills, values, beliefs, and habits through various methods such as teaching, training, or research.

(b) Explain the sources of curriculum objectives according to Ralph Tyler. (6 marks)

1. Studies of the learners themselves: Understanding the needs, interests, and abilities of the students.

2. Studies of contemporary life: Identifying the skills and knowledge needed in modern society.

3. Suggestions from subject matter specialists: Input from experts in various fields to determine essential content.

(c) Using an illustration, explain Hilda Taba’s model of curriculum development. (12 marks)

Hilda Taba’s model of curriculum development is a grassroots approach that involves teachers in the process. The model consists of the following steps:

  1. Diagnosis of needs: Identifying the needs of the learners and the community.
  2. Formulation of objectives: Defining clear and specific learning objectives based on the diagnosed needs.
  3. Selection of content: Choosing appropriate content that aligns with the objectives.
  4. Organization of content: Structuring the content in a logical and meaningful sequence.
  5. Selection of learning experiences: Designing activities and experiences that facilitate learning.
  6. Organization of learning experiences: Arranging the learning experiences in a coherent manner.
  7. Evaluation: Assessing the effectiveness of the curriculum and making necessary adjustments.

This model emphasizes the importance of teacher involvement and a bottom-up approach to curriculum development.

Question 5

(a) State two roles of District Centres for Early Childhood Education in the implementation of Early Childhood Development and Education curriculum. (2 marks)

1. Providing training and professional development for ECDE teachers.

2. Monitoring and evaluating the implementation of the ECDE curriculum in local centers.

(b) Describe four ways of imparting social skills to ECDE learners. (8 marks)

1. Encouraging group activities and cooperative play to foster teamwork and communication.

2. Teaching children to share and take turns through structured games and activities.

3. Modeling positive social behaviors and interactions for children to emulate.

4. Using role-playing and storytelling to teach empathy and understanding of others' feelings.

(c) Explain five activities each from the sub theme: Sun, that a preschool teacher can organise for five-year old’s, in Creative arts and Science lessons. (10 marks)

Creative Arts:

  1. Drawing and painting pictures of the sun.
  2. Creating sun-shaped crafts using paper plates and yellow paint.
  3. Singing songs about the sun and its importance.
  4. Making sun catchers using colored tissue paper and clear contact paper.
  5. Role-playing as the sun and other celestial bodies in a simple play.

Science:

  1. Observing and discussing the effects of sunlight on plants.
  2. Conducting simple experiments to show how the sun affects temperature.
  3. Creating a sundial to understand how the sun's position changes throughout the day.
  4. Discussing the importance of the sun for life on Earth.
  5. Exploring the concept of day and night using a globe and a flashlight to represent the sun.

Question 6

(a) Highlight two measures an ECDE teacher may use to discourage early marriages. (4 marks)

1. Educating children and their parents about the negative consequences of early marriage, such as health risks and limited educational opportunities.

2. Promoting gender equality and empowering girls through education and awareness programs.

(b) Outline three measures that can be undertaken to improve health, safety and nutrition within ECDE centres. (6 marks)

1. Implementing regular health check-ups and vaccinations for children.

2. Ensuring that the ECDE centers have safe and clean environments, including proper sanitation and hygiene practices.

3. Providing balanced and nutritious meals to children, and educating parents about the importance of good nutrition.

(c) (i) State two challenges the government may face in implementing ICT in ECDE. (4 marks)

1. Limited infrastructure and access to technology in rural areas.

2. Lack of trained teachers who can effectively integrate ICT into the curriculum.

(ii) Identify three ways in which ICT can enhance the learning environment in Early Childhood Development Education Centres. (6 marks)

1. Providing interactive and engaging learning materials, such as educational games and videos.

2. Facilitating communication and collaboration between teachers, parents, and students through digital platforms.

3. Offering access to a wide range of online resources and information that can supplement traditional teaching methods.
