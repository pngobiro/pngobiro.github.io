2019

Diploma in Early Childhood Development and Education
December 2017

Instructions to Candidates:

Answer any FIVE questions. Use clear and concise language.

Question 1

(a) (i) State two characteristics of Early Childhood Development and Education. (2 marks)

Sample Answer:

  • Focus on holistic development, including physical, emotional, social, and cognitive growth.
  • Emphasis on play-based learning to foster creativity and exploration.

(ii) Outline two practices adopted from Maria Montessori in Early Childhood Development and Education. (4 marks)

Sample Answer:

  • Use of self-directed learning activities that allow children to choose their tasks.
  • Incorporation of sensory-based materials to enhance learning through hands-on experiences.

(b) Highlight three differences between the traditional and contemporary concept of the African child. (6 marks)

Sample Answer:

  1. Traditional views often saw children as communal property, while contemporary views emphasize individual rights and needs.
  2. In traditional societies, children were expected to contribute to household chores early, whereas contemporary views advocate for age-appropriate responsibilities.
  3. Traditional education was informal and community-based, while contemporary education is formal and structured.

(c) Explain four challenges of integrating pre-primary into the primary school system. (8 marks)

Sample Answer:

  1. Lack of adequate infrastructure to accommodate younger children.
  2. Insufficient training for primary school teachers to handle pre-primary learners.
  3. Differences in curriculum requirements and teaching methodologies.
  4. Potential neglect of the unique developmental needs of pre-primary children in a primary school setting.

Question 2

(a) State four ways in which needs assessment is important in the curriculum development process. (4 marks)

Sample Answer:

  1. Identifies the specific needs and gaps in the current curriculum.
  2. Helps in setting clear and achievable educational goals.
  3. Ensures that the curriculum is relevant to the learners’ needs and societal demands.
  4. Provides a basis for resource allocation and prioritization.

(b) Describe three contributions of Jean Piaget to ECDE. (6 marks)

Sample Answer:

  1. Piaget’s theory of cognitive development provides a framework for understanding how children learn and think.
  2. He emphasized the importance of play in children’s learning and development.
  3. Piaget introduced the concept of stages of development, which helps educators tailor their teaching methods to the child’s developmental level.

(c) (i) State two main activities a teacher would use to instil obedience in ECDE learners. (2 marks)

Sample Answer:

  • Use of positive reinforcement to reward good behavior.
  • Setting clear and consistent rules and expectations.

(ii) Identify four reasons for considering chronological age of a child before admission to pre-school. (8 marks)

Sample Answer:

  1. To ensure the child is developmentally ready for the activities and learning experiences.
  2. To align with the cognitive and social-emotional milestones expected at that age.
  3. To maintain a consistent learning environment for all children in the class.
  4. To comply with legal and regulatory requirements for school admission.

Question 3

(a) Explain the term subject as used in curriculum development. (2 marks)

Sample Answer:

  • A subject refers to a specific area of knowledge or study that is taught as part of the curriculum, such as mathematics, science, or language arts.

(b) (i) State four features of a day care centre. (4 marks)

Sample Answer:

  1. Provides care and supervision for children during the day.
  2. Offers structured activities and learning opportunities.
  3. Ensures a safe and nurturing environment for children.
  4. May provide meals and snacks for the children.

(ii) Highlight three possible challenges the county governments may face in managing day care centres. (6 marks)

Sample Answer:

  1. Limited funding and resources to maintain quality standards.
  2. Difficulty in recruiting and retaining qualified staff.
  3. Ensuring compliance with health and safety regulations.

(c) Describe four activities that can enhance the development of reading skills in preschoolers. (8 marks)

Sample Answer:

  1. Reading aloud to children to model fluent reading.
  2. Encouraging children to retell stories in their own words.
  3. Using picture books to help children associate words with images.
  4. Providing opportunities for children to practice writing and recognizing letters.

Question 4

(a) Identify four effects of poverty on the development of ECDE infrastructure. (4 marks)

Sample Answer:

  1. Limited funding for building and maintaining school facilities.
  2. Inadequate supply of learning materials and resources.
  3. Poor sanitation and hygiene conditions in schools.
  4. Overcrowded classrooms due to lack of space and resources.

(b) Describe four economic factors that lead to curriculum change. (8 marks)

Sample Answer:

  1. Changes in labor market demands requiring new skills and knowledge.
  2. Economic downturns leading to budget cuts and resource constraints.
  3. Globalization and the need for a competitive workforce.
  4. Technological advancements requiring updated curriculum content.

(c) Discuss four negative socio-economic effects of single parenthood on an ECDE learner. (8 marks)

Sample Answer:

  1. Financial strain leading to limited access to educational resources.
  2. Reduced parental involvement due to work commitments.
  3. Emotional stress affecting the child’s learning and behavior.
  4. Social stigma and isolation from peers.

Question 5

(a) Identify four differences in the functions of DICECE and NACECE. (4 marks)

Sample Answer:

  1. DICECE focuses on district-level implementation of ECDE programs, while NACECE oversees national policies and standards.
  2. DICECE provides direct support to local ECDE centers, whereas NACECE conducts research and development.
  3. DICECE is responsible for training and capacity building at the district level, while NACECE sets national training guidelines.
  4. DICECE monitors and evaluates ECDE programs within its district, while NACECE ensures compliance with national standards.

(b) (i) State four roles of the university in ECDE curriculum development. (4 marks)

Sample Answer:

  1. Conducting research to inform curriculum content and teaching methods.
  2. Providing training and professional development for ECDE educators.
  3. Developing and updating curriculum frameworks and guidelines.
  4. Collaborating with other stakeholders to ensure the curriculum meets societal needs.

(ii) Discuss two effects of breakdown of extended families on a child's upbringing. (4 marks)

Sample Answer:

  1. Reduced emotional and social support for the child, leading to potential behavioral issues.
  2. Increased financial burden on the nuclear family, affecting the child’s access to resources and opportunities.

(c) Explain four measures an ECDE teacher can adopt to enhance participatory rights of a learner. (8 marks)

Sample Answer:

  1. Encouraging children to express their opinions and ideas during class discussions.
  2. Providing opportunities for children to make choices in their learning activities.
  3. Creating a classroom environment that respects and values each child’s contributions.
  4. Involving children in decision-making processes, such as setting classroom rules.

Question 6

(a) Highlight three negative effects of African resistance movement to colonial rule on ECDE development in Kenya. (6 marks)

Sample Answer:

  1. Disruption of traditional education systems, leading to a loss of cultural knowledge.
  2. Destruction of community structures that supported early childhood care and education.
  3. Diverted resources from education to fund resistance efforts, delaying the development of formal ECDE systems.

(b) Explain three measures an ECDE teacher can put in place to curb bullying amongst the learners. (6 marks)

Sample Answer:

  1. Establishing clear anti-bullying policies and consequences.
  2. Promoting a culture of respect and inclusion through classroom activities.
  3. Providing support and counseling for both victims and perpetrators of bullying.

(c) Outline four practices an ECDE teacher can inculcate in the learners to enhance environmental hygiene. (8 marks)

Sample Answer:

  1. Teaching children the importance of handwashing and proper hygiene practices.
  2. Encouraging children to keep their classroom and play areas clean.
  3. Organizing regular clean-up activities to instill a sense of responsibility.
  4. Using visual aids and stories to explain the impact of hygiene on health.
