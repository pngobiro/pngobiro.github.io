Kenya National Examinations Council

Diploma in Early Childhood Development and Education

Foundations of ECDE and Curriculum Development

April 2019 - Time: 3 hours

Questions and Sample Answers

  1. (a) Describe two differences between Philosophy and Sociology in Early Childhood Development and Education. (4 marks)

    Answer:

    • Philosophy: Focuses on the purpose and meaning of education, while Sociology examines how society and culture influence education.
    • Philosophy: Deals with abstract ideas like morality and ethics, whereas Sociology studies social structures and their impact on learning.

    (b) Explain three curriculum objectives of Early Childhood Development and Education. (6 marks)

    Answer:

    1. To promote holistic development (physical, emotional, social, and cognitive).
    2. To prepare children for primary education by developing foundational skills.
    3. To foster a love for learning and curiosity in children.

    (c) In the African Traditional Society, the child does not belong to the family. Discuss five communal responsibilities in child rearing. (10 marks)

    Answer:

    1. Shared Caregiving: Extended family members help raise the child.
    2. Moral Education: The community teaches values and cultural norms.
    3. Discipline: Elders correct and guide the child’s behavior.
    4. Economic Support: The community contributes resources for the child’s upbringing.
    5. Socialization: The child learns social skills through interaction with the community.

  2. (a) Highlight four perceptions of pregnancy in African Traditional Society. (4 marks)

    Answer:

    1. Pregnancy is seen as a blessing and a sign of fertility.
    2. It is viewed as a communal event, with the entire community celebrating and supporting the expectant mother.
    3. Certain taboos and rituals are observed to protect the mother and unborn child.
    4. Pregnancy is often associated with spiritual significance, such as ancestral blessings.

    (b) Explain four negative effects of Information Communication Technology (ICT) on ECDE learners. (8 marks)

    Answer:

    1. Reduced Physical Activity: Excessive screen time can lead to sedentary lifestyles.
    2. Social Isolation: Overuse of ICT can reduce face-to-face interactions.
    3. Distraction: ICT devices can divert attention from learning activities.
    4. Exposure to Inappropriate Content: Young children may access unsuitable material online.

    (c) Discuss four ways in which an ECDE teacher may adopt to promote social equity in class. (8 marks)

    Answer:

    1. Use inclusive teaching materials that reflect diverse cultures and backgrounds.
    2. Encourage group activities to foster collaboration and understanding among learners.
    3. Address biases and stereotypes in the classroom.
    4. Provide equal opportunities for all learners to participate and succeed.

  3. (a) Identify two similarities and two differences between Kenya and Uganda ECDE organizational structures. (4 marks)

    Answer:

    • Similarities:
      1. Both countries have government bodies overseeing ECDE (e.g., Ministry of Education).
      2. Both emphasize community involvement in ECDE programs.
    • Differences:
      1. Kenya has a more centralized ECDE system, while Uganda’s system is more decentralized.
      2. Kenya’s ECDE curriculum is more standardized compared to Uganda’s.

    (b) Discuss four roles of the Teachers Service Commission in implementing ECDE curriculum. (8 marks)

    Answer:

    1. Recruiting and deploying qualified ECDE teachers.
    2. Providing professional development and training for teachers.
    3. Ensuring teachers adhere to ethical standards and curriculum guidelines.
    4. Monitoring and evaluating teacher performance.

    (c) Explain four negative effects of gender imbalance in the ECDE teaching profession. (8 marks)

    Answer:

    1. Lack of male role models for young boys.
    2. Reinforcement of gender stereotypes (e.g., teaching as a “female” profession).
    3. Limited diversity in teaching approaches and perspectives.
    4. Potential for gender bias in classroom management.

  4. (a) State four reasons why a teacher trainee should study history of ECDE. (4 marks)

    Answer:

    1. To understand the evolution of ECDE policies and practices.
    2. To learn from past successes and failures in ECDE.
    3. To appreciate the cultural and social context of early childhood education.
    4. To inform current and future ECDE curriculum development.

    (b) Discuss four factors which may influence ECDE learners into drug and substance abuse. (8 marks)

    Answer:

    1. Peer pressure from older children or adults.
    2. Exposure to drug use in the family or community.
    3. Lack of parental supervision and guidance.
    4. Stress or trauma from challenging home environments.

    (c) Highlight four ways in which evaluation is important in Early Childhood Development and Education curriculum. (8 marks)

    Answer:

    1. To assess the effectiveness of teaching methods and materials.
    2. To identify areas where learners need additional support.
    3. To ensure the curriculum meets its objectives.
    4. To provide feedback for continuous improvement of the curriculum.

  5. (a) Explain the term curriculum as used in Early Childhood Development and Education. (2 marks)

    Answer:

    The curriculum refers to the planned learning experiences and activities designed to achieve specific educational goals for young children.

    (b) Describe four factors that an ECDE teacher should consider when developing curriculum support materials. (8 marks)

    Answer:

    1. Age-appropriateness of the materials.
    2. Cultural relevance and inclusivity.
    3. Availability of resources.
    4. Alignment with curriculum objectives.

    (c) Discuss five ways a teacher can enhance creativity skills to an ECDE learner. (10 marks)

    Answer:

    1. Provide open-ended activities (e.g., drawing, storytelling).
    2. Encourage exploration and experimentation.
    3. Use play-based learning to stimulate imagination.
    4. Praise and reward creative efforts.
    5. Integrate arts and crafts into daily activities.

  6. (a) Highlight two dimensions of G. P. Oltoch’s model of school curriculum. (4 marks)

    Answer:

    1. Content: What is taught (knowledge, skills, values).
    2. Process: How it is taught (teaching methods, interactions).

    (b) Outline four ways an ECDE teacher can promote individual development and self-fulfillment among learners. (8 marks)

    Answer:

    1. Provide personalized learning experiences.
    2. Encourage self-expression through art, music, and drama.
    3. Set achievable goals to build confidence.
    4. Foster a supportive and inclusive classroom environment.

    (c) Describe four ways in which insecurity affects ECDE development in Kenya. (8 marks)

    Answer:

    1. Disruption of learning due to conflicts or violence.
    2. Limited access to ECDE centers in insecure areas.
    3. Trauma and emotional stress affecting children’s development.
    4. Reduced funding for ECDE programs in favor of security measures.
