2014

Diploma in Early Childhood Development and Education
Foundation of ECDE and Curriculum Development - December 2014

Instructions to Candidates:

  • Write your name and index number in the spaces provided above.
  • Sign and write the date of the examination in the spaces provided above.
  • This question paper consists of SIX questions.
  • Answer any FIVE questions in the spaces provided after question 6.
  • Each question carries a total of 20 marks.
  • Do not remove any pages from this booklet.
  • Candidates should answer the questions in English.

Question 1

(a) Outline six benefits of children to parents in the Traditional African context. (6 marks)

  • Children provide labor for family chores and agricultural activities.
  • They ensure the continuation of the family lineage.
  • Children offer social security to parents in old age.
  • They enhance the social status of parents within the community.
  • Children contribute to the family's economic well-being through their future earnings.
  • They bring joy and emotional fulfillment to parents.

(b) Examine six negative effects of HIV and AIDS on the ECDE teacher. (6 marks)

  • Increased absenteeism due to illness.
  • Reduced productivity and performance.
  • Emotional and psychological stress.
  • Financial strain due to medical expenses.
  • Stigmatization and discrimination from colleagues and the community.
  • Increased workload for healthy teachers due to staff shortages.

(c) Explain four benefits of a sociology lesson to an ECDE teacher. (8 marks)

  • Enhances understanding of social dynamics and cultural diversity.
  • Improves ability to address social issues affecting children.
  • Promotes effective communication with parents and the community.
  • Encourages the development of inclusive teaching practices.

Question 2

(a) Identify four objectives of ECDE in Uganda. (4 marks)

  • To promote holistic development of children.
  • To prepare children for primary education.
  • To enhance children's social and emotional skills.
  • To provide a foundation for lifelong learning.

(b) Discuss four contributions of Maria Montessori in the development of ECDE curriculum. (8 marks)

  • Emphasized child-centered learning.
  • Introduced the use of sensory-based learning materials.
  • Advocated for individualized learning plans.
  • Promoted the importance of a prepared learning environment.

(c) Suggest four measures the Government of Kenya should put in place to ensure provision of quality education in early childhood in Arid and Semi-arid Lands. (8 marks)

  • Increase funding for ECDE programs in these regions.
  • Provide training and support for ECDE teachers.
  • Improve infrastructure and learning materials.
  • Implement community awareness programs on the importance of early childhood education.

Question 3

(a) Identify four forms of child abuse that can take place in an ECDE centre. (4 marks)

  • Physical abuse.
  • Emotional abuse.
  • Sexual abuse.
  • Neglect.

(b) Describe four characteristics of Traditional African Education. (8 marks)

  • It was community-oriented and focused on communal values.
  • It emphasized practical skills and vocational training.
  • It was oral-based, with knowledge passed down through storytelling.
  • It included initiation rites as a form of transition to adulthood.

(c) Explain four ways in which an ECDE teacher can use language activities to promote hard work in the learners. (8 marks)

  • Encourage storytelling that highlights the value of hard work.
  • Use songs and rhymes that promote diligence and perseverance.
  • Incorporate role-playing activities that model hard work.
  • Provide positive reinforcement and praise for effort and achievement.

Question 4

(a) Differentiate between curriculum and syllabus. (4 marks)

  • Curriculum refers to the overall content, structure, and objectives of an educational program.
  • Syllabus is a detailed outline of topics to be covered in a specific course or subject.

(b) Describe three elements of school curriculum according to Gilbert P. Oltoch. (6 marks)

  • Objectives: The goals and outcomes the curriculum aims to achieve.
  • Content: The subject matter and topics included in the curriculum.
  • Evaluation: The methods used to assess student learning and curriculum effectiveness.

(c) Explain five functions of NACECE in curriculum development. (10 marks)

  • Developing and reviewing ECDE curriculum.
  • Providing training and support for ECDE teachers.
  • Conducting research on early childhood education.
  • Monitoring and evaluating the implementation of ECDE programs.
  • Advising the government on ECDE policies and practices.

Question 5

(a) Identify four categories of children who may not easily access regular ECDE services. (4 marks)

  • Children with disabilities.
  • Children from marginalized communities.
  • Orphans and vulnerable children.
  • Children in remote or conflict-affected areas.

(b) Highlight three negative effects of liberalization of ECDE Teacher training in the country. (6 marks)

  • Decline in the quality of teacher training programs.
  • Increased commercialization of teacher training.
  • Lack of standardization and regulation in training institutions.

(c) Explain five achievements of sessional paper No.1 of 2005 in the ECDE sector. (10 marks)

  • Increased enrollment in ECDE programs.
  • Improved infrastructure and learning materials.
  • Enhanced teacher training and professional development.
  • Strengthened policy framework for early childhood education.
  • Increased community involvement in ECDE programs.

Question 6

(a) Explain the roles of the following in curriculum development process. (i) course panel (3 marks) (ii) academic board (3 marks)

  • Course Panel: Responsible for designing and reviewing the curriculum, ensuring it meets educational standards and objectives.
  • Academic Board: Oversees the overall academic policies and approves the curriculum developed by the course panel.

(b) Outline six roles of the Directorate of Quality Assurance and Standards in the implementation of ECDE curriculum in Kenya. (6 marks)

  • Monitoring and evaluating the implementation of the curriculum.
  • Providing guidelines and standards for curriculum delivery.
  • Conducting inspections and assessments of ECDE centers.
  • Offering training and support to ECDE teachers.
  • Ensuring compliance with national education policies.
  • Collecting and analyzing data on curriculum effectiveness.

(c) Describe four merits of evaluation in ECDE curriculum. (8 marks)

  • Identifies strengths and weaknesses in the curriculum.
  • Provides feedback for continuous improvement.
  • Ensures accountability and quality assurance.
  • Supports decision-making for curriculum adjustments.
