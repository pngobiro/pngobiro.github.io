2008

The Kenya National Examinations Council
Diploma in Early Childhood Development and Education
December 2008

Instructions to Candidates:

This paper consists of SIX questions. Answer any FIVE questions in the answer booklet provided. Each question carries a total of 20 marks.

Question 1

(a) State four effects of the recommendations of the Gachathi Commission on the Development of Early Childhood Development and Education in Kenya.

Sample Answer:

  • Increased funding for ECDE programs.
  • Expansion of ECDE centers across the country.
  • Improved teacher training and certification.
  • Integration of ECDE into the national education system.

Question 2

(b) Explain nine roles of the community in the implementation of Early Childhood Development and Education programme, according to the Early Childhood Development and Education policy framework.

Sample Answer:

  1. Providing a conducive learning environment.
  2. Participating in the management of ECDE centers.
  3. Supporting the construction and maintenance of facilities.
  4. Encouraging parental involvement in children's education.
  5. Contributing to the development of local curricula.
  6. Providing cultural and traditional knowledge.
  7. Ensuring child protection and welfare.
  8. Organizing community events that promote education.
  9. Monitoring and evaluating the ECDE program's effectiveness.

Question 3

(c) State three ways in which each of the following emerging issues affect child growth and development: (i) urbanization; (ii) changing family structure.

Sample Answer:

(i) Urbanization:

  • Increased exposure to diverse cultures and technologies.
  • Potential for higher stress due to crowded living conditions.
  • Access to better educational resources and healthcare.

(ii) Changing family structure:

  • Impact of single-parent households on child emotional development.
  • Increased reliance on extended family or caregivers.
  • Effects of blended families on social relationships.

Question 4

(a) Describe the four stages of Ralph Tyler’s theory of the curriculum development process.

Sample Answer:

  1. Determining educational objectives.
  2. Selecting learning experiences.
  3. Organizing learning experiences.
  4. Evaluating the curriculum's effectiveness.

Question 5

(c) Explain five ways you would use to develop social equality when handling children in the Early Childhood Development and Education curriculum.

Sample Answer:

  • Ensuring inclusive educational activities that accommodate all children.
  • Providing equal access to resources and learning materials.
  • Encouraging interactions among children from diverse backgrounds.
  • Implementing anti-bias education to promote equality.
  • Training teachers to recognize and address social inequalities.

Question 6

(b) Give five reasons why the stage of evaluation in curriculum development is important.

Sample Answer:

  1. To assess whether educational objectives are being met.
  2. To identify areas that need improvement.
  3. To ensure the curriculum remains relevant and effective.
  4. To provide feedback for curriculum revision.
  5. To measure student learning outcomes and progress.
