2010

Diploma in Early Childhood Development and Education
December 2010

Instructions to Candidates:

This paper consists of SIX questions. Answer any FIVE questions in the answer booklet provided. Each question carries a total of 20 marks.

Question 1

(a) Describe four components of philosophy. (8 marks)

Sample Answer:

  • Metaphysics: The study of reality and existence.
  • Epistemology: The study of knowledge and how it is acquired.
  • Ethics: The study of moral values and principles.
  • Aesthetics: The study of beauty and art.

(b) Identify four ways in which a teacher would use the concept of philosophy in Early Childhood Development and Education (ECDE). (4 marks)

Sample Answer:

  • To guide curriculum development and implementation.
  • To inform teaching methods and strategies.
  • To create a conducive learning environment.
  • To promote critical thinking and problem-solving skills in children.

(c) Explain four methods through which values were imparted to young children in Traditional African Society. (8 marks)

Sample Answer:

  • Storytelling: Elders used stories to teach moral lessons and cultural values.
  • Modeling Behavior: Children learned by observing and imitating adults.
  • Community Involvement: Children participated in communal activities to learn social values.
  • Ceremonial Practices: Rituals and ceremonies reinforced cultural and spiritual values.

Question 2

(a) Outline five roles played by missionaries in the establishment of ECDE in Kenya. (5 marks)

Sample Answer:

  1. Establishing the first formal schools for young children.
  2. Introducing Western education systems and curricula.
  3. Providing resources such as books and teaching materials.
  4. Training local teachers to work in ECDE centers.
  5. Promoting the importance of early childhood education in communities.

(b) Give eight negative effects of Free Primary Education (FPE) on ECDE in Kenya. (8 marks)

Sample Answer:

  • Reduced funding for ECDE programs as resources were diverted to FPE.
  • Overcrowding in primary schools, leading to neglect of ECDE.
  • Decline in the quality of ECDE due to lack of trained teachers.
  • Increased dropout rates in ECDE as parents focused on free primary education.
  • Lack of infrastructure development for ECDE centers.
  • Reduced parental involvement in ECDE programs.
  • Shift in government priorities away from ECDE.
  • Increased pressure on ECDE teachers to meet primary school standards.

(c) Examine seven benefits of government policies on ECDE in Kenya. (7 marks)

Sample Answer:

  • Increased access to ECDE programs for children across the country.
  • Standardization of the ECDE curriculum to ensure quality education.
  • Provision of funding and resources for ECDE centers.
  • Training and professional development opportunities for ECDE teachers.
  • Improved infrastructure and facilities for ECDE centers.
  • Enhanced community involvement and support for ECDE programs.
  • Promotion of inclusive education for children with special needs.

Question 3

(a) Highlight five ways in which a pre-school teacher would use the philosophy of John Pestalozzi in the classroom situation. (5 marks)

Sample Answer:

  • Emphasizing hands-on learning and experiential activities.
  • Focusing on the holistic development of the child (physical, emotional, and intellectual).
  • Using child-centered teaching methods to cater to individual needs.
  • Encouraging active participation and interaction among children.
  • Integrating moral and ethical education into the curriculum.

(b) (i) Identify five ways of naming children in Traditional African Society. (5 marks)

Sample Answer:

  • Based on the circumstances of birth (e.g., time, season, or events).
  • Named after ancestors or relatives to honor them.
  • Named after significant cultural or spiritual beliefs.
  • Named based on the child's physical characteristics or behavior.
  • Named after important community leaders or heroes.

(ii) Explain five reasons for naming children in Traditional African Society. (10 marks)

Sample Answer:

  • To preserve family and cultural heritage.
  • To honor ancestors and maintain a connection with the past.
  • To reflect the circumstances or events surrounding the child's birth.
  • To instill a sense of identity and belonging in the child.
  • To convey hopes and aspirations for the child's future.

Question 4

(a) Differentiate between collage and mosaic as used in creative activities. (2 marks)

Sample Answer:

  • Collage: A technique where various materials (e.g., paper, fabric, photographs) are glued onto a surface to create a composition.
  • Mosaic: A technique where small pieces of materials (e.g., tiles, glass, stones) are arranged to form a pattern or image.

(b) Outline six roles played by the National Centre for Early Childhood Education (NACECE) in ECDE in Kenya. (6 marks)

Sample Answer:

  • Developing and revising the ECDE curriculum.
  • Training and capacity building for ECDE teachers.
  • Conducting research on ECDE practices and policies.
  • Providing technical support and guidance to ECDE centers.
  • Promoting community awareness and involvement in ECDE.
  • Monitoring and evaluating the implementation of ECDE programs.

(c) Explain six challenges that affect the implementation of the ECDE curriculum in Kenya. (12 marks)

Sample Answer:

  • Inadequate funding and resources for ECDE programs.
  • Lack of trained and qualified ECDE teachers.
  • Poor infrastructure and facilities in ECDE centers.
  • Low parental and community involvement in ECDE activities.
  • Inconsistent implementation of ECDE policies across regions.
  • High teacher-to-child ratios, leading to overcrowded classrooms.

Question 5

(a) Define the term curriculum. (3 marks)

Sample Answer:

A curriculum refers to the planned and structured set of learning experiences and content designed to achieve specific educational goals and objectives.

(b) Discuss six steps that take place during the process of development of ECDE curriculum support materials by the Kenya Institute of Education (KIE). (12 marks)

Sample Answer:

  1. Conducting a needs assessment to identify gaps in the current curriculum.
  2. Setting clear objectives and goals for the new curriculum materials.
  3. Designing and drafting the curriculum content and materials.
  4. Reviewing and revising the draft materials based on feedback.
  5. Piloting the materials in selected ECDE centers to test their effectiveness.
  6. Finalizing and distributing the materials to all ECDE centers.

(c) Give five reasons for the involvement of Directorate of Quality Assurance and Standards in the Ministry of Education in ECDE curriculum development in Kenya. (5 marks)

Sample Answer:

  • To ensure the curriculum meets national education standards.
  • To monitor and evaluate the implementation of the curriculum.
  • To provide technical support and guidance to curriculum developers.
  • To ensure the curriculum is relevant and responsive to the needs of learners.
  • To promote quality assurance and continuous improvement in ECDE programs.

Question 6

(a) Give five ways in which the Teachers Service Commission (TSC) is involved in the development of curriculum in ECDE in Kenya. (5 marks)

Sample Answer:

  • Recruiting and training qualified ECDE teachers.
  • Providing professional development opportunities for ECDE teachers.
  • Ensuring teachers adhere to the national curriculum guidelines.
  • Monitoring and evaluating the performance of ECDE teachers.
  • Collaborating with other stakeholders to improve ECDE curriculum implementation.

(b) Highlight five considerations a teacher would make when planning for teaching-learning activities in the development of the ECDE curriculum. (5 marks)

Sample Answer:

  • The age and developmental level of the children.
  • The learning objectives and outcomes of the curriculum.
  • The availability of teaching resources and materials.
  • The cultural and social background of the children.
  • The need for inclusive and differentiated instruction.

(c) Assess five challenges of rural-urban migration that may affect ECDE in Kenya. (10 marks)

Sample Answer:

  • Overcrowding in urban ECDE centers due to increased population.
  • Strain on resources and infrastructure in urban areas.
  • Decline in the quality of ECDE in rural areas due to reduced population.
  • Increased demand for ECDE services in urban areas, leading to higher costs.
  • Disruption of family structures, affecting children's emotional and social development.
