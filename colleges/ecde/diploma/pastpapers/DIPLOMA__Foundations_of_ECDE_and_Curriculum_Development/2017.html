Kenya National Examinations Council

Diploma in Early Childhood Development and Education

Foundations of ECDE and Curriculum Development

November 2017 - Time: 3 hours

Questions and Sample Answers

  1. (a) (i) State two characteristics of Early Childhood Development and Education. (2 marks)

    Answer:

    1. Focuses on holistic development (physical, emotional, social, and cognitive).
    2. Emphasizes play-based learning to foster creativity and curiosity.

    (a) (ii) Outline two practices adopted from Maria Montessori in Early Childhood Development and Education. (4 marks)

    Answer:

    1. Use of child-centered learning materials that encourage self-directed learning.
    2. Emphasis on hands-on activities and sensory-based learning.

    (b) Highlight three differences between the traditional and contemporary concept of the African child. (6 marks)

    Answer:

    1. Traditional: The child is seen as a communal responsibility. Contemporary: The child is primarily the responsibility of the nuclear family.
    2. Traditional: Education is informal and based on cultural practices. Contemporary: Education is formal and structured.
    3. Traditional: Children are expected to conform to societal norms. Contemporary: Children are encouraged to express individuality.

    (c) Explain four challenges of integrating pre-primary into the primary school system. (8 marks)

    Answer:

    1. Lack of adequate infrastructure for young children in primary schools.
    2. Insufficient training for primary school teachers to handle pre-primary learners.
    3. Mismatch between the developmental needs of pre-primary children and the primary school curriculum.
    4. Limited resources for play-based learning in primary schools.

  2. (a) State four ways in which needs assessment is important in the curriculum development process. (4 marks)

    Answer:

    1. Identifies the specific needs of learners.
    2. Helps in setting clear and achievable curriculum objectives.
    3. Ensures the curriculum is relevant to the learners' context.
    4. Guides the allocation of resources effectively.

    (b) Describe three contributions of Jean Piaget to ECDE. (6 marks)

    Answer:

    1. Piaget’s theory of cognitive development emphasizes the importance of stages in children’s learning.
    2. He highlighted the role of play in cognitive development.
    3. Piaget’s work influenced the development of child-centered learning approaches.

    (c) (i) State two main activities a teacher would use to instil obedience in ECDE learners. (2 marks)

    Answer:

    1. Setting clear rules and expectations for behavior.
    2. Using positive reinforcement to reward good behavior.

    (c) (ii) Identify four reasons for considering chronological age of a child before admission to pre-school. (8 marks)

    Answer:

    1. To ensure the child is developmentally ready for structured learning.
    2. To align the child’s cognitive abilities with the curriculum.
    3. To promote social interaction with peers of similar age.
    4. To avoid overwhelming the child with tasks beyond their developmental stage.

  3. (a) Explain the term subject as used in curriculum development. (2 marks)

    Answer:

    A subject refers to a specific area of knowledge or study within the curriculum, such as mathematics, science, or language arts.

    (b) (i) State four features of a day care centre. (4 marks)

    Answer:

    1. Provides care and supervision for young children.
    2. Offers structured activities to promote learning and development.
    3. Has trained caregivers or teachers.
    4. Includes facilities for play, rest, and meals.

    (b) (ii) Highlight three possible challenges the county governments may face in managing day care centres. (6 marks)

    Answer:

    1. Lack of adequate funding to maintain and improve facilities.
    2. Insufficient trained staff to meet the needs of children.
    3. Difficulty in ensuring consistent quality across different centres.

    (c) Describe four activities that can enhance the development of reading skills in preschoolers. (8 marks)

    Answer:

    1. Reading aloud to children to build vocabulary and comprehension.
    2. Using picture books to encourage storytelling and imagination.
    3. Introducing phonics games to develop letter-sound recognition.
    4. Encouraging children to retell stories in their own words.

  4. (a) Identify four effects of poverty on the development of ECDE infrastructure. (4 marks)

    Answer:

    1. Lack of funds to build and maintain ECDE facilities.
    2. Inadequate learning materials and resources.
    3. Poor sanitation and hygiene facilities.
    4. Overcrowded classrooms due to limited infrastructure.

    (b) Describe four economic factors that lead to curriculum change. (8 marks)

    Answer:

    1. Changes in labor market demands requiring new skills.
    2. Economic growth leading to increased investment in education.
    3. Globalization influencing the need for a more competitive workforce.
    4. Government policies aimed at reducing unemployment through education.

    (c) Discuss four negative socio-economic effects of single parenthood on an ECDE learner. (8 marks)

    Answer:

    1. Financial strain leading to limited access to educational resources.
    2. Emotional stress affecting the child’s ability to concentrate in school.
    3. Lack of parental supervision due to work commitments.
    4. Social stigma affecting the child’s self-esteem and social interactions.

  5. (a) Identify four differences in the functions of DICECE and NACECE. (4 marks)

    Answer:

    1. DICECE: Focuses on implementing ECDE programs at the district level. NACECE: Provides national policy guidance and coordination.
    2. DICECE: Conducts training for ECDE teachers at the local level. NACECE: Develops national training curricula and standards.
    3. DICECE: Monitors and evaluates ECDE programs within the district. NACECE: Conducts research and evaluation at the national level.
    4. DICECE: Works closely with local communities. NACECE: Collaborates with international organizations and stakeholders.

    (b) (i) State four roles of the university in ECDE curriculum development. (4 marks)

    Answer:

    1. Conducting research to inform curriculum development.
    2. Training ECDE professionals and curriculum developers.
    3. Providing expertise in curriculum design and evaluation.
    4. Collaborating with government and NGOs to implement curriculum changes.

    (b) (ii) Discuss two effects of breakdown of extended families on a child's upbringing. (4 marks)

    Answer:

    1. Loss of communal support in child-rearing, leading to increased pressure on nuclear families.
    2. Reduced exposure to cultural values and traditions traditionally passed down by extended family members.

    (c) Explain four measures an ECDE teacher can adopt to enhance participatory rights of a learner. (8 marks)

    Answer:

    1. Encouraging children to express their opinions and ideas in class.
    2. Involving learners in decision-making processes, such as choosing classroom activities.
    3. Creating a safe and inclusive environment where all children feel valued.
    4. Teaching children about their rights and responsibilities in a child-friendly manner.

  6. (a) Highlight three negative effects of African resistance movement to colonial rule on ECDE development in Kenya. (6 marks)

    Answer:

    1. Disruption of traditional education systems and cultural practices.
    2. Destruction of infrastructure, including schools, during conflicts.
    3. Loss of skilled educators who were involved in the resistance movement.

    (b) Explain three measures an ECDE teacher can put in place to curb bullying amongst the learners. (6 marks)

    Answer:

    1. Establishing clear anti-bullying policies and consequences.
    2. Teaching children about empathy and respect for others.
    3. Encouraging open communication and reporting of bullying incidents.

    (c) Outline four practices an ECDE teacher can inculcate in the learners to enhance environmental hygiene. (8 marks)

    Answer:

    1. Teaching children to wash their hands regularly.
    2. Encouraging proper waste disposal and recycling.
    3. Promoting cleanliness in the classroom and playground.
    4. Educating children about the importance of clean water and sanitation.
