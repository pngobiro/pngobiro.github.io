2010

Certificate in Early Childhood Development and Education
FOUNDATIONS AND MANAGEMENT OF ECDE, December 2010

Instructions to Candidates:

Answer ALL questions in section A and any FOUR questions from section B. Use clear and concise language.

Question 1

(a) Differentiate between a goal and an objective. (6 marks)

Sample Answer:

  • A goal is a broad, long-term aim that provides overall direction, while an objective is a specific, measurable, and time-bound target that helps achieve the goal.
  • Goals are general and abstract, whereas objectives are concrete and detailed.

(b) Define the term policy. (2 marks)

Sample Answer:

  • A policy is a set of guidelines or principles that guide decision-making and actions within an organization or institution.

(c) State two reasons that are considered important in having an Early Childhood Development and Education (ECDE) policy in Kenya. (2 marks)

Sample Answer:

  • To ensure uniformity and standardization in the delivery of ECDE services across the country.
  • To provide a framework for the allocation of resources and support for ECDE programs.

Question 2

(a) Give the meaning of Madrassa. (2 marks)

Sample Answer:

  • A Madrassa is an Islamic educational institution where children learn about the Quran, Islamic teachings, and Arabic language.

(b) Identify three functions of Madrassa in ECDE in Kenya. (3 marks)

Sample Answer:

  1. Providing religious education and moral guidance to young children.
  2. Teaching Arabic language and Quranic studies.
  3. Promoting cultural and religious identity among Muslim children.

(c) Outline five reasons that may make it necessary to establish mobile schools in Arid and Semi-Arid Lands (ASAL) in Kenya. (5 marks)

Sample Answer:

  1. To reach children in remote and inaccessible areas.
  2. To provide education to nomadic communities who move frequently.
  3. To address the lack of permanent infrastructure in ASAL regions.
  4. To ensure continuity of education during droughts and other emergencies.
  5. To promote equity in access to education for all children.

Question 3

(a) Define the following terms:

(i) Democracy; (2 marks)

Sample Answer:

  • Democracy is a system of government where power is vested in the people, who exercise it directly or through elected representatives.

(ii) Entertainment. (2 marks)

Sample Answer:

  • Entertainment refers to activities or performances that provide enjoyment, amusement, or relaxation to an audience.

(b) Outline four reasons for the importance of entertainment to young children. (4 marks)

Sample Answer:

  1. Entertainment helps in the cognitive and emotional development of children.
  2. It provides a break from routine activities, reducing stress and boredom.
  3. Entertainment can be educational, teaching children new skills and concepts.
  4. It fosters creativity and imagination in young children.

(c) Identify two ways in which pre-schoolers can exercise democracy in a classroom. (2 marks)

Sample Answer:

  • By participating in class discussions and decision-making processes.
  • By voting on simple classroom choices, such as selecting a storybook or game.

Question 4

(a) (i) Identify a suitable source of data that was used in a study on the challenges facing transition in an ECDE centre attached to a primary school in Kenya. (1 mark)

Sample Answer:

  • Interviews with teachers, parents, and children.

(ii) Outline six possible negative findings of the study in ECDE. (6 marks)

Sample Answer:

  1. Lack of adequate preparation for children transitioning to primary school.
  2. Insufficient communication between ECDE and primary school teachers.
  3. Inadequate resources and facilities to support the transition process.
  4. High dropout rates among children during the transition period.
  5. Lack of parental involvement in the transition process.
  6. Emotional and social challenges faced by children during the transition.

(b) Identify three advantages of a role model in mobilising the community in ECDE programmes. (3 marks)

Sample Answer:

  1. Role models inspire and motivate community members to support ECDE initiatives.
  2. They provide a positive example of the benefits of early childhood education.
  3. Role models can help bridge the gap between the community and ECDE programs, fostering trust and collaboration.

Question 5

(a) Identify seven services provided by the local government to promote ECDE programmes in Kenya. (7 marks)

Sample Answer:

  1. Providing funding and resources for ECDE centers.
  2. Training and capacity building for ECDE teachers.
  3. Developing and implementing ECDE policies and guidelines.
  4. Monitoring and evaluating ECDE programs to ensure quality.
  5. Constructing and maintaining ECDE infrastructure.
  6. Promoting community awareness and involvement in ECDE.
  7. Providing health and nutrition services to ECDE learners.

(b) Discuss four challenges facing service delivery by the local government in Kenya. (8 marks)

Sample Answer:

  1. Limited financial resources to support ECDE programs.
  2. Inadequate infrastructure and facilities for ECDE centers.
  3. Lack of trained and qualified ECDE teachers.
  4. Poor coordination and communication between local government and other stakeholders.

Question 6

(a) (i) Write EFA in full. (1 mark)

Sample Answer:

  • Education for All.

(ii) Outline six achievements of the Kenyan government in expanding ECDE towards realisation of EFA. (6 marks)

Sample Answer:

  1. Increased enrollment rates in ECDE programs.
  2. Improved access to ECDE services in rural and marginalized areas.
  3. Development of a national ECDE policy framework.
  4. Training and capacity building for ECDE teachers.
  5. Provision of learning materials and resources to ECDE centers.
  6. Enhanced community involvement in ECDE programs.

(b) Propose eight ways of overcoming challenges in provision of free and compulsory primary education in Kenya. (8 marks)

Sample Answer:

  1. Increasing government funding for primary education.
  2. Improving infrastructure and facilities in primary schools.
  3. Recruiting and training more qualified teachers.
  4. Providing free learning materials and uniforms to students.
  5. Promoting community awareness and involvement in education.
  6. Implementing policies to reduce dropout rates.
  7. Addressing gender disparities in access to education.
  8. Enhancing monitoring and evaluation of primary education programs.

Question 7

(a) Describe five elements of the communication process in community capacity building and mobilization in ECDE programmes. (10 marks)

Sample Answer:

  1. Sender: The person or organization initiating the communication, such as an ECDE teacher or program coordinator.
  2. Message: The information or content being communicated, such as the benefits of ECDE programs.
  3. Channel: The medium through which the message is transmitted, such as meetings, flyers, or social media.
  4. Receiver: The target audience, such as parents, community leaders, or local government officials.
  5. Feedback: The response from the receiver, which helps in assessing the effectiveness of the communication and making necessary adjustments.

(b) Outline five tips on public speaking a teacher would use to effectively communicate with parents in an ECDE centre. (5 marks)

Sample Answer:

  1. Prepare and organize the content in advance to ensure clarity and coherence.
  2. Use simple and clear language that is easily understood by all parents.
  3. Maintain eye contact and use positive body language to engage the audience.
  4. Speak with confidence and enthusiasm to convey the importance of the message.
  5. Encourage questions and feedback to foster interaction and understanding.

Question 8

(a) (i) State four types of information that should be included in a title of an ECDE research project. (4 marks)

Sample Answer:

  1. The main topic or subject of the research.
  2. The specific focus or area of study within the topic.
  3. The target population or group being studied.
  4. The geographical location or context of the research.

(ii) Give five reasons that would make it necessary for an ECDE trainee to report research findings. (5 marks)

Sample Answer:

  1. To share knowledge and insights gained from the research with the academic community.
  2. To contribute to the body of knowledge in the field of ECDE.
  3. To inform policy and practice in early childhood education.
  4. To fulfill academic requirements and demonstrate research competence.
  5. To provide evidence-based recommendations for improving ECDE programs.

(b) Give six reasons for reviewing literature in research in ECDE. (6 marks)

Sample Answer:

  1. To identify gaps in existing knowledge and research.
  2. To build a theoretical framework for the study.
  3. To understand the historical context and evolution of the topic.
  4. To avoid duplication of previous research efforts.
  5. To gain insights into methodologies and approaches used in similar studies.
  6. To support the validity and reliability of the research findings.

Question 9

(a) (i) Describe documentary analysis method of collecting data. (2 marks)

Sample Answer:

  • Documentary analysis involves the systematic review and interpretation of existing documents, such as reports, records, and publications, to gather relevant data for research.

(ii) Identify five advantages of using observation method in collecting data in ECDE research. (5 marks)

Sample Answer:

  1. It allows researchers to gather data in natural settings, providing authentic insights into children's behavior and interactions.
  2. Observation is a non-intrusive method that does not disrupt the normal activities of the children.
  3. It provides detailed and rich qualitative data that can capture the complexity of children's experiences.
  4. Observation can be used to validate or complement other data collection methods, such as interviews or surveys.
  5. It allows for real-time data collection, enabling researchers to observe changes and developments over time.

(b) Explain four leadership skills a teacher should possess to succeed in the development of ECDE programmes in the community. (8 marks)

Sample Answer:

  1. Communication Skills: Effective communication is essential for conveying the vision and goals of ECDE programs to stakeholders, including parents, community leaders, and government officials.
  2. Collaboration Skills: Building partnerships and working collaboratively with various stakeholders is crucial for the success of ECDE programs.
  3. Problem-Solving Skills: Teachers need to be able to identify challenges and develop practical solutions to address them, ensuring the smooth implementation of ECDE programs.
  4. Motivational Skills: Inspiring and motivating others, including colleagues, parents, and community members, is key to gaining support and commitment for ECDE initiatives.
