  • ‚Ä¢ The CXR findings should confirm your physical findings rather than be an aid to make your physical findings later

  1. 1. Technical quality.
  2. 2. Chest wall and diaphragm.
  3. 3. Heart and mediastinum.
  4. 4. Lungs.
  5. 5. Review areas.

üìÑ Standard Views and Techniques

  • ‚Ä¢ Chest radiographs are done in a postero-anterior (PA) and left lateral positions.
  • ‚Ä¢ The PA view is done to minimize cardiac magnification.
  • ‚Ä¢ Lateral view is done to allow 3-D interpretation of the chest film findings (see alter).
  • ‚Ä¢ In sick patients, an AP chest film is done either supine or upright depending on how mobile the patient is.
  • ‚Ä¢ On AP view, it is sometimes hard to assess cardiac enlargement, pleural effusion and lung parenchyma, especially if the patient did not take good inspiratory effort
  • ‚Ä¢ When taking a PA X ray, the patient is facing the X-ray film cassette and the X-ray beam enters the patient's back.

PA VIEW TECHNIQUES

  1. 1. Ensure the film belongs to the patient, the date is correct, the side marker is present
  2. 2. Adequate penetration of the mediastinum
    • ‚Ä¢ Is the thoracic spine seen?
  3. 3. Has the patient taken good inspiratory effort
    • ‚Ä¢ About 10 posterior thoracic ribs (7 anterior) should be seen through the lungs.
  4. 4. Is there any rotation of the chest
    • ‚Ä¢ Assess by checking the upper thoracic spinous process (oval) in relation to the medial ends of the clavicles (lines 'a') this CR is rotated to the left.

EXPOSURE

  • ‚Ä¢ Overexposure masks opacities in the lung field
  • ‚Ä¢ Poor inspiration apparent cardiomegaly
