üîä 1. ULTRASOUND

  • ‚Ä¢ investigation of choice in children
  • ‚Ä¢ In adults, depending on indication Ultrasound or IVU/CT pyelogram maybe considered.

Indications of ultrasound

  1. 1. Assess the kidneys in suspected renal pathology; location, size, echotexture, masses, cysts, hydronephrosis, calcifications
  2. 2. Vascularity of the kidney, renal masses or suspected vascular lesions
  3. 3. Resistive index of renal arteries in suspected renal artery stenosis
  4. 4. Ultrasound guided procedures e.g. Percutaneous kidney biopsies, drainage catheter placement
  5. 5. Assess renal transplants; size, structure, vascularity, fluid collections
  6. 6. Obstructive uropathy; level of obstruction and sometimes the cause of the obstruction
  7. 7. Bladder outline, bladder volume, residual volume
  8. 8. Assess the surrounding structures in the retroperitoneum/extraperitoneum for fluid
  1. 1. it is cheap and readily available
  2. 2. it is safe - no danger of contrast reactions, can still assess kidneys in renal failure
  3. 3. no radiation
  4. 4. Can be used in point-of-care or emergency settings

‚ùå Ultrasound; Disadvantages

  1. 1. the ureters cannot be seen unless dilated.
  2. 2. calyceal detail cannot be seen which is necessary for assessment of chronic pyelonephritis, papillary necrosis and tuberculosis
  3. 3. Uro-endothelial (transitional cell) tumours of the renal pelvis/calyces are not visualised until large.

üíâ 3. INTRAVENOUS PYELOGRAM (IVP)

  1. 1. Intravenous Urogram is indicated in suspected urinary tract pathology when ultrasound has failed to make a diagnosis.
  2. 2. Is NOT sensitive in hypertension
  3. 3. Iodinated water-soluble contrast medium is given intravenously and series of films taken

Indications for IVU

  1. 1. Haematuria
  2. 2. dilated ureter on ultrasound
  3. 3. inconclusive ultrasound scan

Less common indications:

  1. 1. to show the position of ureters for planning surgery or X-ray therapy
  2. 2. in suspected calyceal deformity in tuberculosis, papillary necrosis and chronic pyelonephritis
  3. 3. Trauma - if ultrasound is inconclusive and CT not available

üö´ Contra-indications: IVU

Intravenous pyelography is not indicated in:

  1. 1. Advanced renal failure - use ultrasound
  2. 2. Prostatism
  3. 3. Polycystic renal disease - better seen on ultrasound
  4. 4. Vague abdominal pain
  5. 5. Acute urinary tract infection
  6. 6. Renal artery stenosis
  7. 7. Previous reaction to iodinated contrast

Relative contraindications: IVU

Intravenous pyelography should be performed with caution in:

  1. 1. diabetic patients
  2. 2. very small children
  3. 3. myelomatosis
  4. 4. patients with sickle cell disease

‚ò¢Ô∏è 8. RADIONUCLIDESTUDIES

  1. 1. Static scan:
    • - Radiopharmaceutical attaches to normal functioning renal tissue.
    • - Demonstrates the size, outline and amount of normal renal parenchyma (Dimercaptosuccinate-99mTc).
    • 1. Indication- renal parenchymal disease
  2. 2. Dynamic scan:
    • - Measures the excretion the radiopharmaceutical by the kidneys primarily by GFR and its clearance down the ureters
    • - Functional study (99mTc-DTPA).
    • 1. Indications- hypertension, renal tubular acidosis, renal transplant

üíâ 9. ANGIOGRAPHY

Main indications:

  1. 1. prior to interventional techniques e.g. angioplasty

  4. 4. anatomical detail prior to renal transplant or suspected vascular occlusion following surgery
