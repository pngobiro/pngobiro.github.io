Imaging of the Surgical Abdomen - The Acute Abdomen.

Dr Alfred Odhiambo. Lecturer ; University of Nairobi.

üó£Ô∏è Definition of an Acute Abdomen.

A group of conditions characterized by severe abdominal pain in which prompt surgical treatment must be considered to treat perforation, peritonitis, vascular or other intra-abdominal catastrophe

üí° Principles of Imaging.

  • - The objective of imaging in the whole of the human body is to demonstrate structures not usually visible to the eye.
  • - Unlike in the chest where natural contrast is maximal abdominal contrast is limited.
  • - A soft issue opacity of 2 mm in the lung fields would stand out against air.
  • - In the abdomen a polyp 20 times that would easily be missed.

General Principles

  • - Role of the radiologist - help the surgeon decide if surgery is indicated
  • - If immediate surgery indicated on clinical findings, negative radiology should be ignored

The Plain Abdominal Radiograph (AXR)

Often the most useful initial investigation

Interpretation depends on the gas patterns

Specific diagnosis is possible in many cases

Objectivity is improved by viewing r/g initially in the absence of any clinical info.

Choice of Radiograph

Influenced by clinical suspicion & patient's ability to stand

Supine abdomen = most useful single r/g. In some conditions, e.g. Renal colic, it may be adequate alone.

Erect CXR = best film for free intra-abdominal gas

üü¢ Normal Appearances

  • - Amount of gas in SB is small. Only short lengths of it are seen
  • - Valvulae conniv. Are rarely seen
  • - 3 - 5 fluid levels <2.5 cm long are normal
  • - 3 or more fluid levels >2.5 cm are abnormal (ileus/obstruction)
  • - Colon contains gas - haustra are readily identified
  • - Colonic fluid levels of several cm in left colon are normal

S/bowel Rule of 3:

  • - Wall thickness < 3 mm
  • - Valvulae conn. < 3 mm wide
  • - Caliber < 3 cm
  • - Air fluid levels < 3

Bowel Calibre Limits; Rule of 3:

  • - Small bowel 3 cm
  • - TRV colon 6 cm
  • - Caecum 9 cm

ü§í Acute Colitis
