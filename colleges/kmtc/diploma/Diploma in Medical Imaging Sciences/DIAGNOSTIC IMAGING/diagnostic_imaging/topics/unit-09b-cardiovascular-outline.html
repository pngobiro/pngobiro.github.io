üéØ INTRODUCTION

  • ‚Ä¢ The fetal heart develops from a series of complex and rapid changes which occur between the 2nd and 8th week of intrauterine life.
  • ‚Ä¢ During the 3rd to 5th weeks cardiac structures develop most actively and are most susceptible to adverse external influence e.g. rubella virus or drugs such as thalidomide resulting in congenital heart diseases (CHD).

üß¨ EMBRYOLOGY

  • ‚Ä¢ The heart originates from the splanchnic mesoderm.
  • ‚Ä¢ The endocardial tube is the initial basic structure.
  • ‚Ä¢ The development is characterized by rapid and complex changes
  • ‚Ä¢ 5 dilatations develop in an initially straight longitudinal midline tube. These include:
    1. 1. Common truncus arteriosus
    2. 2. Bulbous cordis
    3. 3. Common primitive ventricle
    4. 4. Common primitive atrium

üîÑ NORMAL FETAL CIRCULATION

  • ‚Ä¢ Oxygenated blood is carried by the umbilical vein (UV) through the ductus venosus (DV) and IVC into the RA through the foramen ovale.
  • ‚Ä¢ SVC & IVC blood is passed into RA, RV & PA. Most of the RV output passes through the PDA into the descending aorta to supply lower parts of fetus and the umbilical arteries which supply the fetal placental plexus.

üíî VSDs.

  • ‚Ä¢ One of the commonest anomaly (second only to bicuspid aortic valves). Incidence 3570/1m live births.
  • ‚Ä¢ 50% of patients with VSD have associated cardiac anomaly e.g. coarctation of the aorta.
  • ‚Ä¢ 80% are perimembranous & the vast majority(75%) of small ones close spontaneously.

VSD Anatomy

Lets us first understand the anatomy.

The Interventricular septum has two parts, the larger muscular and the smaller membranous part.

The membranous part is the posterosuperior region.

Membranous VSDs account for 80% of all VSDs.

Muscular VSDs tend to close spontaneously.

Its possible to have multiple VSDs at any part of the interventricular septum.

üíô Congenital Cyanotic Heart Disease

  • ‚Ä¢ For clinically detectable central cyanosis, there must be at least 5 g of reduced Hb per 100 ml of aortic blood. Occurs in 3 ways:
    1. 1. Direct right to left shunt eg. TOF
    2. 2. Transposition of great arteries.
    3. 3. Common chambers eg. Common atria, common ventricle, truncus arteriosus.

üíô TOF

TOF Pathology:

  • - Most common cyanotic heart lesion.
  • - Incidence: 3-5 per 10,000 live births.
  • - Primary hypoplasia of infundibular septum secondary to unequal partitioning of the conotruncus.
  • - Conotruncal septum displaced anteriorly, and its lowermost component, the Infundibular septum fails to fuse with the top of the IV septum.
  • - Features include:
    1. 1. Infundibular right ventricular outflow tract stenosis
    2. 2. subaortic VSD
    3. 3. Over-riding aorta
    4. 4. Right ventricular hypertrophy

üìÑ CONT

  • ‚Ä¢ Desaturated blood enters RA, flows through RVOT into lungs. Some blood shunts right to left through VSD into ascending aorta.
