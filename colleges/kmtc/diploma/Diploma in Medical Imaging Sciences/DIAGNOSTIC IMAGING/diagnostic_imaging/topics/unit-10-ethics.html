üí° Values in Medical Ethics

  • ‚Ä¢ There are 6 main values
  • 1. Autonomy- The patient has the right to refuse or choose their treatment
  • 2. Beneficence- The doctor must act in the best interest of the patient

Values in Medical Ethics: Cont.

  1. 3. Non-maleficence- Actions including those should not harm or bring harm to the patient or others. "First, do no harm"
  2. 4. Justice- Being fair or equitable to the community, including sharing resources fairly. Distribution of scarce health resources, e.g. beds and availability

Values in Medical Ethics; Cont:

  1. 5. Dignity; The patient (and the person treating the patient) have a right to be treated with dignity
  2. 6. Truthfulness/Honesty (In terms of results, management, prognosis, outcome etc.) Information and informed consent
