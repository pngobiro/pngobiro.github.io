CARDIOVASCULAR IMAGING

INTRODUCTION

üéØ INTRODUCTION

The fetal hert develops from a series of complex and rapid changes which occur between the second and 8th week of intrauterine life

During the 3rd and 5th weeks cardiac structures fevelop most actively ‚Üí most damage

üß¨ EMBRYOLOGY

  • - Splanchnic mesoderm
  • - Endocardial tube is the initial basic structure
  • - Development characterized by rapid and complex changes
  • - 5 dilatations
    • - Common truncus arteriosus
    • - Bulbous cordis
    • - Common primitive ventricle
    • - Pcommon primitive atrium
    • - Sinus venosus

CONT.

  • - Truncus arteriosus ‚Üí aorta and pulmonary trunk
  • - Bulbus cordis ‚Üí smooth part of RV (conus arteriosus) and smooth part of LV (aortic vestibule)
  • - Primitve ventricle ‚Üí trabeculated part of LV and RV
  • - Pimitive atrium ‚Üí trabeculated part of atria
  • - Sinus venosus ‚Üí smooth parts of RA; coronary sinus; oblique vein of LA

üîÑ NORMAL FETAL CIRCULATION

  • - Oxygenated blood is carried by the umbilical vein through the ductus venosus and IVC ‚Üí RA ‚Üí LA through the foramen ovale ‚Üí LV ‚Üí systemic circulation through the aorta (bypassing lungs)
  • - SVC & IVC ‚Üí RA, RV an PA
  • - Most of RV output passess through the PDA into the descending aorta to supply the lower parts of the fetus and umbilical arteries which supply the fetal placental plexus

üìä CHD IMAGING MODALITIES

  • - Plain radiography
  • - Echocardiography ‚Üí 1st line to assess anatomy and function esp. in congenital cardiac anomalies
  • - Cardiac catheterization ‚Üí definition of vascular anatomy
    • - Preserved for complex anomalies that cannot be resolved by echocardiography
    • - This is an invasive procedure, contrast is used.
  • - CT ‚Üí description of cardiac and vascular anatomy in relation to other structures of the chest
  • - MRI ‚Üí quantification of cardiac function and vascular flow in vivo

„É¨„É≥„Éà„Ç≤„É≥ CHEST RADIOGRAPH INDICATIONS

  • - Suspected congenital heart disease
  • - Cyanosis
  • - Cardiac failure
  • - Failure to thrive
  • - Difficult in feeding in the neonatal period
  • - Fainting spells

üìÑ CONT.

  • - Standard views: erect PA or AP (children) chest; Left lateral
  • - Role
    • - Provides clues of underlying congenital heart disease
    • - Instrumental in planin further investigations
    • - Limited use in neonated and young children
    • - May show related skeletal anomalies e.g. in trisomy 21
  • - Assessment:
    • - Cardiac and visceral situs ‚Üí situs solitus, inversus, ambiguous (2/3 of the heart should be on the left side and 1/3 of the hear should be on the right, if vice versa dextrocardia)
    • - Cardiac size and shape.
    • - Mediastinum- Aortic arch, PA, trachea indentation
    • - Pulmonary vasculature-normal, plethora, oligemia, congestion
    • - Skelefal features - Rib notching: coarctation of aorta
    • - post surgical stigmata eg. sternal sutures, implants

CARDIAC SIZE MEASURMENT

Measure the transverse diameter of the heart and compare that of the chest - 50-55% in adults upto 60% in children
