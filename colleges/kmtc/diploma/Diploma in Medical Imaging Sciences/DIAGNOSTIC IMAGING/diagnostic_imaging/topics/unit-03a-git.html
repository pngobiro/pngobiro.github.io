‚ö™‚ö´ Contrast Studies

Contrast material is introduced into the system. Ba sulphate, Air, Ionic water soluble as well as non-ionic water soluble contrast materials used.

  • ‚Ä¢ Choice of contrast depends on suspected pathology.

Single Contrast.

  • ‚Ä¢ The gastrointestinal lumen is filled with positive contrast material usually (Barium sulfate).
  • ‚Ä¢ This then makes easy visualization of pathological conditions such as stenosis, dilatations, intraluminal filling defects, organ contour deformity and external compression.
  • ‚Ä¢ The examination can also verify function of the system i.e. emptying and peristalsis.

Double contrast studies

This examination applies use of two contrast materials a positive one (Barium sulfate or gastrografin) and a negative one (usually air).

  • ‚Ä¢ The wall of the organ is relaxed by smooth muscle relaxant. Membrane is coated by contrast material and lumen distended with gas.
  • ‚Ä¢ This provides more information than single contrast examination as detailed visualization of gut mucosa is achieved, the method however does not afford a functional information.
