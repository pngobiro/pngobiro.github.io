2.2.2 Applied Research

  • ‚úì Applied research is research that is designed to produce findings that can be used to remediate or modify a given situation
  • ‚úì The term refers to those that have their purpose an identified practical use or application
  • ‚úì It provides data to support theory, guide theory revision
  • ‚úì A problem is investigated and some resolution is sought by way of research findings

2.3 CLASSIFICATION BY METHOD OF ANALYSIS

2.3.1 Descriptive research

  • ‚úì This is the process of collecting data in order to test hypotheses or to answer questions concerning the current status in the study
  • ‚úì It determines and reports things the way they are at a particular time
  • ‚úì It gives accurate profile of the research group

2.3.2 Cause comparative research

  • ‚úì This is used to explore relationships between variables. This is used to determine the reasons or causes for the current status of a phenomenon
  • ‚úì This is used where experimental research is not possible
  • ‚úì This is different from experimental in that the researcher is not able to manipulate the variables to determine the effect of one on the other. E.g. study weight gain among infants breastfed

2.4 CLASSIFICATION BY TYPE OF RESEARCH

2.4.1 Survey research

  • ‚úì A survey is an attempt to collect data from members of a population in order to determine the current status of the population with one or more variables
  • ‚úì It is therefore a self report study which requires the collection of quantifiable information from the sample.
  • ‚úì Survey study could be descriptive, explanatory e.t.c
  • ‚úì Survey generally ask subjects to report their attitude, opinions, perceptions or bahaviour

2.4.2 Historical research

  • ‚úì This requires collecting data from the past
  • ‚úì Research instruments are not administered to individuals
  • ‚úì Sources of data/information include;
    • Eye witness reports, original documents as examples of primary sources
    • Second hand information i.e. other people's account (secondary sources)

2.4.3 Retrospective studies

  • ‚úì This begins with a phenomenon of interest (dependable variable) in the present and examines the relationship to other variables in the past. E.g. case studies, causes of heart attack and the investigating the associated factors e.g. contraceptive use, activity, childhood infections, atherosclerosis

Experimental designs are divided into;

2.4.4.1 True or classical
  • ‚úì This is where the researcher has a great deal of control over the research situation. Examples include
  • ‚úì One group pre-test-post-test design. i.e first measure the dependent variable give experimental treatment or independent variable then do a post test 0x0
  • ‚úì Pre-test post test control group this is where there are two groups one experimental (given Rx ) and another control group (no Rx given ). A pre-test is done to both groups, treatment is given only to experiment group and then a post test to both groups
  • ‚úì One short case study. This is where there is only the experimental group which is given treatment and then a post-test is done to test the effect of the treatment
2.4.4.2 Quasi-experimental design
  • ‚úì This is where full experimental control is not possible randomization not possible. These involve implementing a specific treatment and later examining the effects of this treatment

Examples

A. Non-equivalent control group design

This is similar to pre-test -post test control group design except that there is no random assignment of subject to the experimental and comparison group.

B. Time-series design

In this study, the researcher periodically observes or measures the subjects. The experimental is administered between two of the observations e.g. researcher assesses the level of pain in patients with low back pain weekly and then gives treatment after the third week. He then continues weekly assessment for another 3 weeks to see the effect of the treatment.

2.5 CLASSIFICATION BASED ON TIME DIMENTION IN RESEARCH

Research types under this classification include;

2.5.1 Cross-sectional research

  • ‚úì Used when all the information on a specific topic is collected at the same time
  • ‚úì It is non-recurrent in nature and done at specific point in time
  • ‚úì It concentrates only on here and now
  • ‚úì It is cost effective than longitudinal

2.5.2 Longitudinal research

  • ‚úì Two or more studies are done on a specific topic or phenomenon over a longer period of time
  • ‚úì The instruments used must be standardized as far as possible. Research determines if there are changes over a given period of time
  • ‚úì It is predominantly descriptive and explanatory in nature
  • ‚úì It is more complex and more expensive
2.5.2.1 Sub types of longitudinal Research
  1. Panel study-the same people, group or organization is investigated over various periods of time
  2. Cohort -cohorts is a group of individuals with similar characteristics and are treated as one for the purposes of research
  3. Time series- the research is on what happened to people over a period of time. Information is collected at a specific point in time.

2.5.3 Case study

  • ‚úì It entails an in depth investigation into various characteristics of a small number of cases over a specific period of time.
  • ‚úì Data collected is more detailed, comprehensive and varied in nature
  • ‚úì A phenomenon is studied in entirely
  • ‚úì It contains an in depth investigation into interaction among factors influencing explanations or change which are then analyzed
