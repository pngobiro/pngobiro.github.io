Major Cranial Bones

ü¶¥ The Parietal Bones

The two parietal bones are roughly square shaped, found on both sides of the skull they are connected and make up the roof and part of sides of the skull.

ü¶¥ The Occipital Bone

The occipital bone is located at the rear of the cranium. This bone protects the brain and supports the back of the head.

The occipital bone contains the foramen magnum through which passes the spinal cord. The foramen magnum allows the cranial cavity to communicate with the vertebral column.

ü¶¥ The Temporal Bones

The temporal bones are situated on the sides and bases of the skull. The functions of the temporal bones are protection of the brain and support of the face.

Parts of Temporal Bone:

  • ‚Üí The Squamous part: The largest part, located superiorly. It is flat and plate-like, with its outer surface convex in shape.
  • ‚Üí The Tympanic part: Surrounds the external ear, lies inferior to the squamous part, and anterior to the mastoid part.
  • ‚Üí The Mastoid part: An inferior bony projection, lies just behind the external ear. It is a site of attachment for many muscles.
  • ‚Üí The Petrous part: Pyramidal shaped, lies superior to the mastoid part, it contains the inner ear.
  • ‚Üí The Styloid process: Located immediately underneath the tympanic part; acts as an attachment point for muscles and ligaments.
