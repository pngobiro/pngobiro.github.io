Chapter 3: Epithelial Tissues - Functions and Types

üî¨ Functions of Epithelial Tissues

  • Protection from physical, chemical, and biological wear and tear
  • Control of substance entry into the body through epithelial tissues
  • Control of permeability and selective transfer of materials
  • Secretion of mucous and chemical compounds (e.g., digestive enzymes in small intestine)
  • Movement of substances (e.g., ciliated epithelium in bronchioles)
  • Ability to stretch and expand organ capacity (e.g., urinary bladder)

üìã Types of Epithelial Tissues

Classification Categories

  1. Based on the shape of epithelial cells
  2. Based on the number of cell layers

Shape-Based Classification

  • Squamous epithelial tissue: Extremely thin and flattened cells resembling fish scales
  • Cuboidal epithelial tissue: Square-shaped cells, as wide as tall
  • Columnar epithelial tissue: Elongated cells of rectangular shape

Layer-Based Classification

  • Simple epithelia: Single layer of cells
  • Stratified epithelia: Two or more layers of cells

Combined Classification Types

Simple Epithelium:

  • Simple squamous epithelium
  • Simple cuboidal epithelium
  • Simple columnar epithelium

Stratified Epithelium:

  • Stratified squamous epithelium
  • Stratified cuboidal epithelium
  • Stratified columnar epithelium
