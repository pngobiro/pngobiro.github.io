Chapter 3: Specialized Tissues

üî¨ Connective Tissues

Connective tissues are essential parts of the body. Their main function is to connect tissues and organs.

Characteristic Components

  • Cells
  • Ground substance
  • Protein fibers

Connective tissue is mainly composed of ground substance in which cells are dispersed, and crisscrossed by protein fibers.

Ground Substance

The ground substance is the main component of connective tissue. It is mainly composed of extracellular material and can be:

  • Fluid in nature (e.g., blood)
  • Mineralized and solid (e.g., bones)

Protein Fibers

There are three types of fibers in the intercellular matrix of connective tissue:

  1. Collagen fibers
  2. Reticular fibers
  3. Elastic fibers

üìä Classification of Connective Tissues

Connective tissues are classified according to the characteristics of their ground substance and types of fibers into three main types:

1. Connective Tissues Proper

  • Tendons - connect muscles to bones
  • Ligaments - connect bones to other bones at joints
  • Adipose tissue - forms loose connective tissues

2. Supportive Connective Tissues

  • Bone: Most rigid type with hard ground substance of calcium salts
  • Cartilage: Flexible tissue with chondrocytes in gelatinous ground substance (chondrin)

3. Fluid Connective Tissues

  • Blood: Cells suspended in liquid matrix (plasma)
  • Lymph: Contains liquid matrix and white blood cells
