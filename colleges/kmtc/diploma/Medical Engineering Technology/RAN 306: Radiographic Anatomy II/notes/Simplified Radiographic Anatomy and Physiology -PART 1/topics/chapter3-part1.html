üî¨ Introduction to Tissues

Tissue is an assembly of similar cells with extracellular matrix; they are both from the same embryonic origin that arranged in an orderly pattern to carry out a specific function. The functional grouping of multiple tissues leads to the formation of different body organs.

Main Categories of Tissues

There are four broad categories of tissues:

  • Epithelial tissue
  • Connective tissue
  • Muscular tissue
  • Nervous tissue

üîç Epithelial Tissues

Epithelial tissues are essentially large sheets of cells covering all the surfaces of the body. They cover the external surfaces of the body such as skin, and form the inner lining of the internal organs that are exposed to the external environment, such as the inner lining of the digestive tract, respiratory tract, urinary tract and reproductive organs.

Key Characteristics

  • Highly cellular, with little or no extracellular material present between cells
  • Cells are closely bound together through specialized structures called tight junctions
  • Free from blood vessels and nerves
  • Supported by a connective tissue called the basement membrane
