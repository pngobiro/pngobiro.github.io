Chapter 11: The Pelvic Girdle

The Pelvic Girdle

The pelvic girdle (also known as pelvic bone) is formed by three bones:

  1. The ilium
  2. The ischium
  3. The pubis

These three bones fuse together during development to form a single large irregular bone known as the hip bone (os coxae). The two hip bones join anteriorly at the pubic symphysis and posteriorly with the sacrum to form the pelvis.

The Ilium

The ilium is the largest of the three bones forming the hip bone. It forms the superior and lateral walls of the pelvis. The ilium consists of:

  • A body that forms part of the acetabulum
  • An expanded wing (ala)

The wing of the ilium has several notable features:

  • The iliac crest: the superior border of the ilium
  • Anterior superior iliac spine: the anterior end of the iliac crest
  • Posterior superior iliac spine: the posterior end of the iliac crest
  • Anterior inferior iliac spine: located below the anterior superior spine
  • Posterior inferior iliac spine: located below the posterior superior spine
  • Greater sciatic notch: a deep notch on the posterior border

The Ischium

The ischium forms the posteroinferior part of the hip bone and consists of:

  • A body that forms part of the acetabulum
  • A ramus that extends from the body
  • The ischial tuberosity: a rough area on the posterior surface that bears weight when sitting
  • The ischial spine: a sharp projection on the posterior border

The Pubis

The pubis forms the anterior part of the hip bone and consists of:

  • A body that forms part of the acetabulum
  • Superior ramus extending from the body to join the ilium
  • Inferior ramus extending from the body to join the ischium
  • Pubic crest: a ridge on the superior surface of the body
  • Pubic tubercle: a projection at the lateral end of the pubic crest

Sex Differences in the Pelvis

There are several significant differences between male and female pelves:

Male Pelvis

  • Heavier and more robust
  • Higher and narrower pelvic inlet
  • Heart-shaped pelvic inlet
  • Narrower pubic arch (less than 90 degrees)
  • Smaller sciatic notch

Female Pelvis

  • Lighter and smoother
  • Wider and more oval pelvic inlet
  • More circular pelvic inlet
  • Wider pubic arch (greater than 90 degrees)
  • Larger sciatic notch
