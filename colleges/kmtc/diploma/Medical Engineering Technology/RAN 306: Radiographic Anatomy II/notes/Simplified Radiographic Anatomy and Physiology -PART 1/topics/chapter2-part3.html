The Nine Abdominal Regions

The nine regions are two hypochondrial, two lumbar, two inguinal, one epigastric, one umbilical and one hypogastric.

  1. Hypochondriac regions: are the two upper right and left regions below the cartilage of the ribs that extend over the abdomen.
  2. Epigastric region: is located above the stomach.
  3. Lumbar regions: are the two middle right and left regions near the waist.
  4. Umbilical region: is located on middle of distance between xiphoid process and pubic symphysis.
  5. Hypogastric region: is located below the stomach
  6. Iliac regions: are located on either side of the hypogastric regions, and below the lumbar regions.

Contents of Nine Abdominal Regions

Left hypochondriac region:

  1. The stomach
  2. The dome of the left lobe of the liver
  3. The left kidney
  4. The spleen
  5. The tail of the pancreas
  6. Parts of the small intestine
  7. The transverse colon
  8. The descending colon

Right hypochondriac region

  1. The liver
  2. The gallbladder
  3. The small intestine
  4. The ascending colon
  5. The transverse colon
  6. The right kidney

Directional Terms

These terms are essential for describing the relative locations of different body structures:

  1. Anterior (or ventral): describes the front or direction toward the front of the body.
  2. Posterior (or dorsal): describes the back or direction toward the back of the body.
  3. Superior (or cranial): describes a position above or higher than another part of the body
  4. Inferior (or caudal): describes a position below or lower than another part of the body.
  5. Lateral: describes the side or direction toward the side of the body.
  6. Medial: describes the middle or direction toward the middle of the body.
  7. Proximal: describes a position in a limb that is nearer to the point of attachment or the trunk of the body.
  8. Distal: describes a position in a limb that is farther from the point of attachment or the trunk of the body.
  9. Superficial: describes a position closer to the surface of the body.
  10. Deep: describes a position farther from the surface of the body.

Anatomical Planes

A plane is an imaginary two-dimensional surface that passes through the body. There are three planes commonly referred to in anatomy and medicine:

  1. The sagittal plane
  2. The coronal plane
  3. The transverse plane

The Sagittal Plane

The sagittal plane divides the body or an organ vertically into right and left sides. It is known as midsagittal plane when it divides the body into two equal right and left sides, and its known parasagittal plane when it divides the body into unequal right and left sides.

The Coronal Plane

The coronal plane divides the body or an organ into an anterior (front) portion and a posterior (rear) portion. The coronal plane is often referred to as frontal plane.

The Transverse plane

Also known as axial plane, the transverse plane divides the body or organ horizontally into upper and lower portions. Transverse planes produce images referred to as cross sections.
