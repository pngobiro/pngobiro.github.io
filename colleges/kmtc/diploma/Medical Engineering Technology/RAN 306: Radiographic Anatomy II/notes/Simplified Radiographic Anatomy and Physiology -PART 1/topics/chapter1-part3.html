🔊 Ultrasound

It is an imaging method that uses sound waves with frequencies higher than the upper audible limit of human hearing to produce sonographic images of structures within human body. The images can provide valuable information for diagnosing and treating a variety of diseases and conditions.

Basic Principle of Ultrasound

Ultrasound uses high frequency sounds, generally range between $2-15 \mathrm{MHz}$. Ultrasound is produced and detected using an ultrasound transducer, composed mainly of piezoelectric crystal that converts:

  • •Electrical energy to mechanical energy (producing ultrasound)
  • •Mechanical energy back to electrical energy (detecting echoes)

Ultrasound Image Display Modes

A-mode (Amplitude mode)

The oldest and simplest ultrasound technique where:

  • •Single transducer sends ultrasound pulses
  • •Displays echo spikes in one-dimensional image
  • •Shows series of vertical peaks

B-mode

A two-dimensional image where:

  • •Echo amplitude converted to brightness levels
  • •Horizontal and vertical directions represent real distances
  • •Gray scale indicates echo strength

M-mode (Motion mode)

Displays time motion of ultrasound wave, ideal for:

  • •Moving structures like cardiac valves
  • •High time resolution display
  • •Measurement of rapidly moving structures

Doppler Mode

Based on frequency changes from relative motion:

  • •Positive Doppler shift: source moving toward receiver
  • •Negative Doppler shift: source moving away from receiver
  • •Color Doppler shows direction of blood flow

Echogenicity

Echogenicity is the ability of a tissue to reflect ultrasound waves, displayed as brightness levels:

  • • Anechoic structures: Appear completely black (e.g., cysts, bladder)
  • • Hypoechoic structures: Appear dark (e.g., lymph nodes, tumors)
  • • Hyperechoic structures: Appear bright (e.g., bones, fibrous tissue)
  • • Isoechoic structures: Appear gray (e.g., liver, spleen)

☢️ Nuclear Medicine (Isotope Scan)

Nuclear medicine imaging is based on injecting or introducing radioactive material (isotope) that:

  • •Concentrates in specific organs or tissues
  • •Emits gamma rays (similar to X-rays)
  • •Is detected by special cameras to build images

Isotope Bone Scanning

An excellent screening method for bone metastases:

  • •Uses technetium-99m-labeled diphosphonates
  • •About 50% of injected dose reaches skeletal system within 2-6 hours
  • •Uptake depends on blood flow and new bone formation rate

Bone metastases typically appear as:

  • •Multiple foci of increased activity
  • •Random distribution
  • •Varying size, shape, and intensity
  • •Occasionally as areas of decreased uptake
