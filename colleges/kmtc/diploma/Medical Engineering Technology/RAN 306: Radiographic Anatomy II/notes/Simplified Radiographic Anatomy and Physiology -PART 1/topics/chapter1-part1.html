üìö Anatomy & Physiology

Anatomy is the study of the normal structure and components of body parts.

Physiology is the study of normal mechanisms, which works within different structures and organs of the human body, and their interactions together to support the functions of life.

Radiographic anatomy and physiology involves the study of anatomical structure of human body and its basic physiological mechanisms through the application of medical imaging.

Medical imaging is the technique and process of creating visual representation of the internal components of a human body for clinical analysis and medical intervention.

üì∑ X-ray Radiography

It is an imaging technique using X-rays to view the internal structure of an object. X-rays are high energy, nonvisible and ionizing electromagnetic radiation with short wavelength that can penetrate body parts, during exposure, to produce a radiographic image.

Radiographic Image: is a two dimensional representation of a three dimensional structure, therefore it is made up of multiple overlying structures.

Types of Structures in X-ray Images:

  • ‚Ä¢ Radio-opaque structures: are internal structures of high density. They will absorb the X-ray beam more than other structures, leading to high attenuation of X-ray beam, (e.g. bones and metals).
  • ‚Ä¢ Radio-lucent structures: are internal structures of low density. They will absorb the X-ray beam less than other structures, leading to low attenuation of X-ray beam, (e.g. soft tissues and air).

On X-ray images:

  • ‚Ä¢ Bones will appear as white structures because they highly attenuate X-ray beam.
  • ‚Ä¢ Soft tissues will appear as grey structures because they minimally attenuate X-ray beam.
  • ‚Ä¢ Air or gas will appear as black structures because they do not attenuate any X-ray beam.
