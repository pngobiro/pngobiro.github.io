Chapter 12: Lower Limb Bones

Lower Limb Divisions and Bones

The lower limb is divided into three main regions:

  1. The thigh: extends from the hip joint to the knee joint
  2. The leg: extends from the knee joint to the ankle joint
  3. The foot: extends from the ankle joint to the toes

The bones of the lower limb can be grouped as follows:

  • Thigh: Femur
  • Knee: Patella
  • Leg: Tibia and Fibula
  • Foot:
    • 7 Tarsals
    • 5 Metatarsals
    • 14 Phalanges

The Femur

The femur is the longest and strongest bone in the human body. It extends from the hip to the knee and consists of:

  • A proximal end
  • A shaft
  • A distal end

The proximal end features:

  • Head: spherical articular surface that fits into the acetabulum
  • Neck: connects the head to the shaft
  • Greater trochanter: large, irregular projection laterally
  • Lesser trochanter: conical projection medially
  • Intertrochanteric line: ridge connecting trochanters anteriorly
  • Intertrochanteric crest: ridge connecting trochanters posteriorly

The shaft of the femur:

  • Is cylindrical and slightly curved forward
  • Has a prominent posterior ridge called the linea aspera
  • Features the gluteal tuberosity laterally

The distal end includes:

  • Medial and lateral condyles for articulation with the tibia
  • Intercondylar fossa separating the condyles posteriorly
  • Patellar surface anteriorly for articulation with the patella

The Patella

The patella (kneecap) is the largest sesamoid bone in the body. It is roughly triangular in shape and features:

  • An anterior surface that is convex and rough
  • A posterior surface with a smooth articular area
  • A thick superior border (base)
  • A pointed inferior border (apex)

Tibia and Fibula

The tibia is the main weight-bearing bone of the leg and features:

  • A proximal end with medial and lateral condyles
  • An anterior tibial tuberosity
  • A triangular shaft
  • A distal end with the medial malleolus

The fibula is the slender lateral bone of the leg and includes:

  • A proximal head that articulates with the tibia
  • A long, thin shaft
  • A distal end forming the lateral malleolus

Bones of the Foot

The foot contains 26 bones arranged in three groups:

Tarsal Bones

Seven tarsal bones form the posterior part of the foot:

  1. Calcaneus (heel bone)
  2. Talus (ankle bone)
  3. Navicular
  4. Cuboid
  5. Three cuneiform bones (medial, intermediate, and lateral)

Metatarsal Bones

Five metatarsal bones form the middle part of the foot, numbered from medial to lateral (1-5). Each has:

  • A base proximally
  • A shaft
  • A head distally

Phalanges

Fourteen phalanges form the toes:

  • The great toe (hallux) has two phalanges (proximal and distal)
  • Each other toe has three phalanges (proximal, middle, and distal)
