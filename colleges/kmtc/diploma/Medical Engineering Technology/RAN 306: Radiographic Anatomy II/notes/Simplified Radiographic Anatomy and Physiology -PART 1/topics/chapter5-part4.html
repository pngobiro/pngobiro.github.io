Complex Cranial Bones and Foramina

ü¶¥ The Sphenoid Bone

The sphenoid bone is 'butterfly-shaped' bone located at the base of the skull. It is one of several bones that form the eye socket (orbit), located posterior to the eye socket and anterior to the temporal bone.

Components of sphenoid bone:

The sphenoid bone consists of:

  • ‚Üí A body
  • ‚Üí Two greater wings
  • ‚Üí Two lesser wings
  • ‚Üí Two pterygoid processes (each with medial and lateral plates)

ü¶¥ The Ethmoid Bone

The Ethmoid bone is located in the midline of the anterior part of the skull between the two orbits. It contributes to:

  • ‚Üí Formation of the medial wall of the orbits
  • ‚Üí Formation of the roof and lateral walls of the nasal cavity
  • ‚Üí Formation of the upper portion of the nasal septum
  • ‚Üí Formation of the floor of the anterior cranial fossa

Components of Ethmoid Bone:

  • ‚Üí Crista galli: An upward bony projection located at the midline of the superior surface
  • ‚Üí Cribriform plate: Flattened horizontal areas with numerous small openings
  • ‚Üí Perpendicular plate: A thin, vertical lamina forming part of the nasal septum
  • ‚Üí Superior nasal concha: A curled shelf-like structure in the superior nasal cavity
  • ‚Üí Middle nasal concha: A curled shelf-like structure in the middle nasal cavity

üîç Foramina of the Cranial Base

The cranial base contains several important foramina (openings) through which various structures pass:

  • ‚Üí Foramen Caecum: Contains emissary vein to superior sagittal sinus
  • ‚Üí Foramina of Cribriform Plate: Contain olfactory nerve bundles
  • ‚Üí Optic Canal: Contains optic nerve [II] and ophthalmic artery
  • ‚Üí Superior Orbital fissure: Contains multiple cranial nerves and vessels
  • ‚Üí Foramen Rotundum: Contains maxillary nerve [V2]
  • ‚Üí Foramen Ovale: Contains mandibular nerve [V3] and associated vessels
  • ‚Üí Foramen Spinosum: Contains middle meningeal vessels
  • ‚Üí Foramen Lacerum: Contains internal carotid artery and associated structures
