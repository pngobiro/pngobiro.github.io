üî¨ Vulnerability analysis:

  • ‚Ä¢A process which results in an understanding of the types and levels of exposure of persons, property, and the environment to the effects of identified hazards at a particular time.
  • ‚Ä¢Vulnerability $=$ People + Condition + Place + Time + Event
  • ‚Ä¢vulnerability also differs according to intensity of the hazard

Types of Vulnerability:

  • ‚Ä¢Physical Vulnerability
  • ‚Ä¢Economic
  • ‚Ä¢Social
  • ‚Ä¢environmental

Physical Vulnerability:

  • ‚Ä¢pertaining to matters of location, structural and infrastructural conditions etc
  • ‚Ä¢Includes
  • ‚Ä¢Buildings at risk
  • ‚Ä¢Unsafe infrastructure: eg. Transportation system, sanitation system etc.
  • ‚Ä¢Unsafe critical facilities
  • ‚Ä¢Rapid urbanization
  • ‚Ä¢Agricultural-primarily considering physical assets-related, but opportunity loss potential of essential natural resources, crops, trees, livestock, fisheries, also should be recognized.

Economic Vulnerability:

  • ‚Ä¢Determined by evaluating
  • ‚Ä¢the direct loss potential of economic assets
  • ‚Ä¢indirect loss potential
  • ‚Ä¢secondary effects
  • ‚Ä¢Direct Loss Potential
  • ‚Ä¢destruction of buildings, plants, facilities, raw material, products,
  • ‚Ä¢Replacement costs,
  • ‚Ä¢Loss of employment,
  • ‚Ä¢Crop losses, and
  • ‚Ä¢Damage to means of production.
  • ‚Ä¢Indirect Loss Potential
  • ‚Ä¢Impact of lost production,
  • ‚Ä¢Impact of lost employment,
  • ‚Ä¢Loss of markets,
  • ‚Ä¢Loss of opportunity,
  • ‚Ä¢Loss to consequential income-earning activities.
  • ‚Ä¢Secondary Effects
  • ‚Ä¢Inflation,
  • ‚Ä¢Indebtedness,
  • ‚Ä¢Labour migration,

Social Vulnerability:

  • ‚Ä¢determined by the perception of risk and the ability of people to take measures to reduce that risk
  • ‚Ä¢more difficult to measure than either physical or economic vulnerability
  • ‚Ä¢Critical Indicators of Perceived Risk, Ability of Response:
  • ‚Ä¢Poverty, limitation of resources, reserves or options,
  • ‚Ä¢Degree of public awareness about the immediate social and physical environments
  • ‚Ä¢Prior personal experience of specific risk, consequence.
  • ‚Ä¢Demographic Considerations:
  • ‚Ä¢Magnitudes, total population,
  • ‚Ä¢Concentration densities,
  • ‚Ä¢Demographic distinctions, vulnerable groups of socially disadvantaged people or those requiring special attention. These include children, disabled, elderly people, women

Capabilities:

When conducting vulnerability analysis, special note should be taken of positive attributes able to be identified that may contribute to an enhanced ability to prevent or mitigate the effects of a disaster, or which may strengthen a community's ability to respond effectively to the hazard

Technocratic view for managing the risk

  • ‚Ä¢Problem-Physical vulnerability
  • ‚Ä¢Symptoms as perceived- High death toll and damages of infrastructure attribute to severity of hazard, extensive and recurrent rehabilitation needs.
  • ‚Ä¢The causes- uncontrolled characteristics of hazard events, physiography of the prone areas, lack of inadequacy of protective infrastructure, failure of forecasting and warning.

üõ°Ô∏è Risk Management Process

Risk management has three components namely:

  • ‚Ä¢Risk identification
  • ‚Ä¢Risk reduction
  • ‚Ä¢Risk transfer

Risk identification has to be done through mapping and using other available technological options

It is usual to allocate risk management to special body at national level. Usually, it is a national disaster management organization at national level. Others support should be obtained within a National Action Plan for disaster management.

At local, it may be the responsibility of a disaster mitigation committee, which administers risk management.

Risk reduction:

Effective risk reduction involves mitigation measures in hazard prone areas. It may also involve overcoming the socio-economic, institutional and political barriers to the adoption of effective risk reduction strategies and measures in developing countries.

Risk Management:

This is the process by which assessed risks are mitigated, minimized or controlled through engineering, management of land use prances or other operational means. This involves the optimal allocation of available resources in support of group goals

Risk Transfer:

Effective risk transfer involves different tools such as insurances; tax polices special measures focused on land management. Organizational structures, policies, legislation etc is required for effective implementation of risk transfer strategies for a country or local government areas.

Planning

Planning is making decisions about which courses of action to follow. It is the root to objective where it involves conceptualizing and anticipating future action

It includes the following activities:

  • ‚Ä¢Establishing objectives to determine the end result;
  • ‚Ä¢Developing strategies to determine how to reach objectives, by when, and by whom;
  • ‚Ä¢Preparing budgets to determine the cost of using resources;
  • ‚Ä¢Establishing policies to have standing decisions on recurring situations;
  • ‚Ä¢Establishing standards to ensure continued quality of services and products.

Importance of planning

  • ‚Ä¢Gives a sense of direction.
  • ‚Ä¢Focuses attention on objectives.
  • ‚Ä¢Involve others -participation, teamwork.
  • ‚Ä¢Helps anticipate problems and cope with any change in an organization.
  • ‚Ä¢Enhances appropriate allocation of resources.
  • ‚Ä¢Helps in coordination
  • ‚Ä¢Leads to more effectiveness and efficiency of services

Key elements of planning

  • ‚Ä¢Knowing where we are (Situation)
  • ‚Ä¢Deciding where we want to go (Vision/Objectives)
  • ‚Ä¢What do we need to reach where we want to go (Resources)
  • ‚Ä¢How we are going there (Method/process/how)
  • ‚Ä¢Deciding how far we can get towards your target in a period of time (Time line/When)
  • ‚Ä¢How will we know when we get there?

This requires the development of measurable indicators for monitoring progress and evaluating results

  • ‚Ä¢How far we have gone
  • ‚Ä¢Where we are not on target
  • ‚Ä¢How we can do better in future
  • ‚Ä¢Amending the implementation plan (RE-planning

Situation analysis

Improving the understanding of the current situation in order to:

  • ‚Ä¢Provide a common reference point for the planning process
  • ‚Ä¢Allow the selection of priority areas of concern for planning

SA outlines and assesses 3 broad areas

  • ‚Ä¢Demographic situation and health needs
  • ‚Ä¢Services and resources
  • *Socioeconomic Status
  • *Health related services of other sectors
  • *Geographic and topographic pattern
  • *Present infrastructure and service pattern
  • *Existing service resources
  • *Effectiveness and efficiency of current services
  • ‚Ä¢Policy and political environment (General, Govt. Policies)

Setting priority

  • ‚Ä¢Making choices among major needs so as to allocate resources efficiently.
  • ‚Ä¢Consider the following in setting priority
  • ‚Ä¢Magnitude of the issue/Problem
  • ‚Ä¢Who is determining the needs
  • ‚Ä¢Target group
  • ‚Ä¢Cost, ease and effectiveness of intervention
  • ‚Ä¢Political context

Options appraisal

  • ‚Ä¢Process of deciding between alternative approaches to achieve an objective
  • ‚Ä¢Determine the objectives of the activity being appraised
  • ‚Ä¢Identify alternative options (exclude unfeasible and uneconomical option)
  • ‚Ä¢Identify resources required to achieve each option and the benefit of each option
  • ‚Ä¢Cost the resources required
  • ‚Ä¢A good option appraisal should;
  • ‚Ä¢Present quantifiable and non-quantifiable costs and benefits
  • ‚Ä¢Explicitly recognize and incorporate the need for other perspective
  • ‚Ä¢Recognize the resource and financial implication
  • ‚Ä¢Examine the technical feasibility
  • ‚Ä¢Consider the process effects
  • ‚Ä¢Set out information on the methodology used
  • ‚Ä¢Perform where necessary sensitivity analysis
  • ‚Ä¢Set out key assumptions
  • ‚Ä¢Use of sustainability Grid

Programming

  • ‚Ä¢Budgeting and Developing of a plan of action
  • ‚Ä¢This process is carried out for each priority area and its associated set of targets.
  • ‚Ä¢It translates the results of the earlier decisions into a series of programs each with a budget and resources
