LECTURE 4: TIME MANAGEMENT

‚è±Ô∏è Definitions:

  1. 1.It is an enabling technique fundamental to job performance, an important need for managerial effectiveness and efficiency based on time, allocation, and utilization of resources by scheduling and prioritizing factors to accomplish better results, goals, and objectives of the individual and the organization.
  2. 2.It is about making time and using it productively.
  3. 3.Taking time to think more clearly about the job, analyze it, and set priorities.
  4. 4.It is a unique resource that you cannot hire, rent, buy, or otherwise obtain.

‚öôÔ∏è Factors Affecting Time Management

  1. 1.Organization culture - the way workers are used to doing their jobs.
  2. 2.Nature of the job.
  3. 3.Demands made by own staff.
  4. 4.Management style of the superior.
  5. 5.Influence of colleagues.
  6. 6.Personality and skills of the job holder.

üí° Concepts of Time Management

  • ‚Ä¢The purpose is to improve the quality of your life while improving your productivity.
  • ‚Ä¢The principle of time management relies on the concepts of efficiency and effectiveness, which requires knowing your personal and work goals, setting the priorities needed to reach them, and having the discipline to stick to those priorities.
  • ‚Ä¢Key aspects of time management: Time utilization and reduction of time wasted.

Principle issues are:

  • ‚Ä¢Related to the nature of the job.
  • ‚Ä¢Related to the personality and attributes of the job holder.
  • ‚Ä¢Related to the people who make up the job holder's role set.

üîë Principle Issues Details

1. Nature of the Job

  • ‚Ä¢This is fundamental to the amount of control over time.
  • ‚Ä¢Should identify priorities in the job:
    • a)The tasks they are responsible for.
    • b)Tasks that require the greatest effort or produce the greatest return.

2. Personal Attributes

Good time management depends on personality and inclinations, e.g.:

  • ‚Ä¢Some people work well early in the day while others work best later.
  • ‚Ä¢Some spread out their work, while others prefer short, intensive periods.
  • ‚Ä¢Some only deal with one issue at a time, while others juggle several.
  • ‚Ä¢Some like to delegate, while others keep tasks to themselves.
  • ‚Ä¢Some are tidy and methodical; others are untidy and disorganized.
  • ‚Ä¢Some are more skilled and experienced than others.

3. Job Context

It is related to:

  • ‚Ä¢Role set, i.e., boss, own staff, colleagues, etc.
  • ‚Ä¢Physical surroundings - office, location of others.
  • ‚Ä¢Culture of the organization.

üìà How Can Managers Improve Use of Time

Personal Planning

  • ‚Ä¢Personal priority and action planning: Develop personal priorities and means for achieving them.
  • ‚Ä¢Identify time-wasting activities.
  • ‚Ä¢Set targets for self and staff to focus on the parts of the job that matter and what needs to be delegated.

Delegation

  • ‚Ä¢Manager transfers legitimate authority to subordinates or team members without giving up ultimate responsibility.

Assertiveness

  • ‚Ä¢The capacity to express ideas, opinions, or feelings openly and directly without putting down ourselves or others.

Personal Communication Skills

  • ‚Ä¢Faster reading skills.
  • ‚Ä¢Report writing.
  • ‚Ä¢Handling meetings.

Personal Effectiveness

  • ‚Ä¢Be proactive.
  • ‚Ä¢Begin with the end in mind.
  • ‚Ä¢Put first things first.
  • ‚Ä¢Think of win-win situations, i.e., all can benefit.
  • ‚Ä¢Understand first before being understood.
  • ‚Ä¢Personal renewal and reflection.
  • ‚Ä¢Synergize.

  13. 13.Inability to say "no."
  14. 14.Poor delegation.
  15. 15.Traveling between jobs and departments.

üìú Principles & Guidelines

Principles of time management.

Efficiency.

Operating in such a way that resources are not being wasted.

Effectiveness.

Doing the right thing in the right way. Achieving the desired results, while efficiency is achieving the targets with the least possible use of resources.

Guidelines on time management.

  1. 1.Know how you spend your time.
  2. 2.Learn why your time goes where it goes.
  3. 3.Delegate.
  4. 4.Sort out what needs to be done now from what can wait until later.
  5. 5.Cut down on time-wasting activities.
  6. 6.Be ruthless about distractions or interruptions.
  7. 7.Locate information fast.
  8. 8.Build a time control plan that fits your job and your unique personality.
  9. 9.Beat the procrastination habit.
  10. 10.Use other people's time to your advantage.
  11. 11.Be creative with the use of your time.
  12. 12.Work smarter not harder.
