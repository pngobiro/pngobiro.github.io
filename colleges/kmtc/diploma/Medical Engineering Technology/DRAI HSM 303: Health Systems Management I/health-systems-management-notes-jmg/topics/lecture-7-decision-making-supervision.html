LECTURE 7: DECISION MAKING & SUPERVISION

ü§î DECISION MAKING

Decision making is an everyday activity that may be on the spur of the moment or may involve much thought and consultation. It may be emotional or reasoned.

Definition

It is the process through which a course of action is selected as a solution to a specific problem.

Or

A choice of option or alternatives.

Steps in decision making

  1. i.Define the problem.
  2. ii.Collect relevant data.
  3. iii.Develop alternative solutions.
  4. iv.Assess the consequences.
  5. v.Select the best solution.
  6. vi.Implement solution.
  7. vii.Measure results.

Problem finding.

  • ‚Ä¢This refers to a process of identifying problems and making decisions and attempts to solve them.
  • ‚Ä¢It also involves activities with existing problems.
  • ‚Ä¢A problem exists when there is a gap between a given situation and objective.

Why make decisions.

We make decisions in order to solve problems and initiate action which will hopefully solve the problem.

When is it required.

  1. 1.When choosing a particular remedy for solving the problem, e.g., outbreak of cholera, serious accidents, etc.
  2. 2.When sorting out objectives, e.g., the policies of the ministry of health.

Stages or requirements in decision making.

  1. 1.Facts - All facts that have an influence on the decision should be known.
  2. 2.Analysis - Analyze the facts to weigh the pros and cons.
  3. 3.Authority - Formal authority permitting decisions at that level.
  4. 4.Action - Takes the decision and implements it.
  5. 5.Follow-up - Ensure the decisions are carried out.

Types of decisions.

  1. 1.Emergency decisions
    In a crisis such as accidents, or an issue that calls for immediate action.
  2. 2.Routine decisions
    Everyday decisions, e.g., reporting time for duty, budget speech, etc.
  3. 3.Debatable decisions
    These are tough decisions that involve more thought and discussion. They usually bring changes, e.g., constitutional review, salary review, etc.

(Decisions can also be categorized into strategic, operating, and administrative decisions.)

Kinds of action when making a decision.

  1. 1.Interim action
    When one buys time on finding the cause of the problem.
  2. 2.Adaptive action
    You adapt to the problem so that you live with it, e.g., a disabled man using a wooden leg.
  3. 3.Corrective action
    When one gets rid of the known cause of the problem, e.g., repairing a leak, performing an operation, etc.
  4. 4.Preventive action
    When the action taken makes the problem less likely to occur, e.g., putting up a strong roof.
  5. 5.Contingency action
    Provides standby arrangements to minimize the effects of a serious problem, e.g., generator to cater for power failures or gas.
  • ‚Ä¢Monitor the results of your decision.
  • ‚Ä¢Your decision may be wrong; it is better to admit the fact and change the decision. You will be respected more if you change than to persist in carrying out something that is wrong.

üëÄ SUPERVISION

DEF:

It is the art of working with people through the management functions (i.e., planning, directing, coordinating, controlling, and communicating) effectively to achieve the objectives of the organization.

Objectives of supervision.

  • ‚Ä¢Build and maintain an efficient organization.
  • ‚Ä¢Create and maintain an effective workforce.
  • ‚Ä¢Control and add value toward performance and communicate this value to subordinates.

Responsibilities of a supervisor.

  1. a)Handle grievances.
  2. b)Handle indiscipline cases.
  3. c)Responsible for giving instructions.
  4. d)Coordinates activities.
  5. e)Training and development of the people he supervises.
  6. f)Effectiveness and efficiency.

Principles of effective supervision.

  1. 1.An effective supervisor makes sure the people under him understand:
    • -What is expected of them and how the organization is expected to operate.
    • -How his job relates to others and other departments.
    • -Know where to get resources.
    • -Know where to communicate information.
    • -Know how the work he is doing will be measured.
  2. 2.The workers should have guidance in doing their work by providing them with information in terms of content, techniques, e.g., how to live, organize, schedule, and conduct meetings, personality improvement, etc.
  3. 3.Show appreciation for good work by giving certificates of merit, writing to tell the worker the good work he or she has done.
  4. 4.Correct poor work through constructive criticism:
    • -Tell the individual on the spot.
    • -Tell the individual why it is poor work.
    • -Tell the individual what he can do to improve the work.
  5. 5.Give workers the opportunity to show that they can accept greater responsibilities.
  6. 6.Encourage people to improve themselves and progress (avoid sitting on your workers' improvement).
  7. 7.Ensure people work in a safe and enabling environment.

Qualifications of a Supervisor

  • ‚Ä¢Knowledge of the job - know the operational aspect of the job.
  • ‚Ä¢Knowledge of the people - knows and understands the people in order to achieve your goals through them.
  • ‚Ä¢Knowledge of leadership.
  • ‚Ä¢Knowledge of self.

Tools to Apply for Supervision

  1. 1.Planning
    • -What is to be done
    • -Why is it to be done
    • -Where should it be done
    • -Who should do it
  2. 2.Directing
    - Direct others to ensure plans are executed and achieved according to the set objectives.
  3. 3.Coordination
    - Coordinate individual activities so that the job is done in a proper manner.
  4. 4.Controlling
    - Inspect to see plans are being carried out.
  5. 5.Have the "Skills of Personnel"
    • -Communicate and relate well
    • -Select people to perform specific tasks
    • -Appraisal
    • -Discipline - self-discipline
    • -Personnel development
    • -Custodian of morale - increase morale and motivate
    • -Interviewing and counseling skills
    • -Orientation skills
    • -Skills in teamwork
    • -Encourage self-development.
