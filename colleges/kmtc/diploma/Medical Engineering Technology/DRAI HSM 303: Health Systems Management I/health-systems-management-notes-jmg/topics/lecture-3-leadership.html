LECTURE 3: LEADERSHIP

üéØ Leadership Definition

Leadership is a dynamic process within a group where one individual influences others to voluntarily contribute to the achievement of group tasks in a given situation. While managers engage in various activities, their most critical function as leaders is to motivate employees to produce their best work, ultimately improving performance.

A leader:

  • ‚Ä¢Plans
  • ‚Ä¢Makes policies
  • ‚Ä¢Organizes
  • ‚Ä¢Controls
  • ‚Ä¢Coordinates

üë§ TYPES OF LEADERS

  1. 1. Charismatic Leader
    This type has influence due to the strength of their personality, exemplified by figures like Napoleon, Hitler, and Churchill.
  2. 2. Traditional Leader
    Leadership is based on birthright or inheritance.
  3. 3. Situational Leader
    Provides temporary leadership by being in the right place at the right time.
  4. 4. Appointed Leader
    Their influence arises from their position in the organizational hierarchy, such as managers and supervisors.
  5. 5. Functional Leader
    Gains leadership through their actions, determined by expertise or skills.

üëî Formal and Informal Leaders

Leaders can also be categorized into formal and informal types:

  • ‚Ä¢Informal Leaders: Chosen by the group, such as in social groups, churches, or political parties.
  • ‚Ä¢Formal Leaders: Appointed or selected based on organizational roles.

1. The Quality Approach

Qualities suggested are:

  • ‚Ä¢Good personality.
  • ‚Ä¢Intelligent.
  • ‚Ä¢Self-confident.
  • ‚Ä¢Courage.
  • ‚Ä¢Initiative.
  • ‚Ä¢Imaginative.

2. The Situational Approach

This suggests that authority flows from one who knows. A person with appropriate ability for the group's task is likely to make a better leader.

3. The Functional Approach

The leadership process is a function of the leader, the followers, and the situations. Therefore, the basic elements of leadership are:

  1. i.The leader - skills, knowledge, personality
  2. ii.Tasks or goals of the organization.
  3. iii.Group members, i.e., subordinate - skills and motivation.
  4. iv.The environment or situation.

‚ö° AUTHORITY AND POWER.

Leadership in an organization requires authority and power to influence the thoughts and actions of other people.

  • ‚Ä¢Authority is the legitimate right to give commands, to act in the interest of an organization; it can be delegated.
  • ‚Ä¢Power is the ability to impose the will of one person or group to bring about certain behaviors in other persons or groups.
  • ‚Ä¢In organizations, power is the capacity to influence another through the control over needed resources.
  • ‚Ä¢Power can originate from a variety of sources.

Responsibility is an obligation to perform certain functions on behalf of the organization. Commonly called accountability, it can be delegated.

There are five kinds of power bases, which generally make an effective leader:

1. Legitimate power

Power given to the manager by the organization because of the manager's position in the hierarchy, e.g., director.

2. Expert power

Most professionals rely on this power. It is derived from special ability, skill, or knowledge demonstrated by an individual. It makes the leader to be believed because of superior knowledge and skills.

3. Reward power

A leader gives rewards such as money, praise, and promotion to subordinates.

4. Referent power

It is based on:

  1. i.A certain attractiveness or appeal of one person to another, e.g., one who is able to influence others easily to agree with his or her views.
  2. ii.Also based on a person's connection or relationship with another powerful individual.

5. Coercive power

Gives the leader the ability to discipline or reprimand subordinates or punish for non-compliance with management.

üé≠ LEADERSHIP STYLES.

STYLES OF LEADERSHIP

A style is a way in which something is said or done.

It is a particular form of behavior directly associated with an individual.

There are three types:

  1. I.Authoritarian/Autocratic
  2. II.Democratic or Consultative
  3. III.Laissez-Faire or Free Reign

1. AUTHORITARIAN OR AUTOCRATIC

The autocratic leader is a boss, and others are there to follow his instructions. He can have any of the following:

  1. i.Coercive autocracy: He orders or threatens.
  2. ii.Benevolent autocracy: He tells and explains, utilizing positive reinforcement if behavior is forthcoming.
  3. iii.Manipulative autocracy: He cons subordinates into thinking they are significantly participating while he pulls strings behind the scenes.
  • ‚Ä¢The autocratic leader has no trust in subordinates' ability. Everything is centralized, and there is no delegation.

2. DEMOCRATIC OR CONSULTATIVE

  • ‚Ä¢Employees feel and believe that their inputs are desired and can influence a decision.
  • ‚Ä¢The subordinates are encouraged to participate in decision-making, but the leader knows he is accountable for whatever is produced.

3. LAISSEZ-FAIRE OR FREE REIGN

  • ‚Ä¢The leader joins as a fellow participant.
  • ‚Ä¢He delegates work to subordinates, and all that he wants at the end are results.
  • ‚Ä¢He only assists where there are major problems.
