Chapter 3: Plasma Membrane & Membrane Potential

üî¨ Membrane Basics

  • Selectively permeable - controls what gets in and out of cell

üß¨ Membrane Structure

Basic Structure:

  1. Phospholipid Bilayer
    • Hydrophilic (water loving) heads are polar, face outward toward water
    • Hydrophobic (water fearing) tails are nonpolar, face inward away from water
    • Functions:
      • Barrier to diffusion - stops water soluble molecules
      • Water itself is small enough to get through
      • Responsible for membrane fluidity
  2. Cholesterol
    • Located between phospholipids
    • Contributes to fluidity and stability
  3. Proteins
    • Spanning the membrane:
      • Selective channels for transport
      • Carrier proteins for specific molecules
    • On one side of membrane:
      • Receptors - bind with molecules and initiate changes
      • Membrane-bound enzymes - chemical reactions
      • Filamentous meshwork - maintain cell shape
      • Cell adhesion molecules (CAMs) - secure cells together
  4. Carbohydrates
    • On outer surface, bound to proteins and lipids
    • Important in cell recognition and tissue organization
    • Involved in tissue growth regulation

This organization is known as the fluid mosaic model
