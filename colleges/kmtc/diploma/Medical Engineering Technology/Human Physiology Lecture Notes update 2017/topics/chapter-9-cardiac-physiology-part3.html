Chapter 9: Cardiac Physiology (continued)

üîÑ Cardiac Cycle

Major Phases:

  1. Systole (Contraction)
    • Atrial Systole:
      • AV valves open
      • Blood flows to ventricles
      • Duration ~0.1 sec
    • Ventricular Systole:
      • AV valves close
      • Semilunar valves open
      • Blood ejected to arteries
      • Duration ~0.3 sec
  2. Diastole (Relaxation)
    • Atrial Diastole:
      • Atria fill from veins
      • Passive process
    • Ventricular Diastole:
      • Semilunar valves close
      • Ventricles relax and fill
      • Duration ~0.4 sec

üìä Pressure-Volume Relationships

Events During Cardiac Cycle:

  1. Isovolumetric Contraction
    • All valves closed
    • Pressure rises, volume constant
  2. Ejection
    • Semilunar valves open
    • Volume decreases, pressure high
    • Stroke volume = end-diastolic - end-systolic volume
  3. Isovolumetric Relaxation
    • All valves closed
    • Pressure falls, volume constant
  4. Rapid Filling
    • AV valves open
    • Volume increases, pressure low
