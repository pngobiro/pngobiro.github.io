‚ö° Cardiac Action Potentials

Ventricular Action Potential Phases:

  1. Phase 0: Rapid Depolarization
    • Fast $\mathrm{Na}^{+}$ channels open
    • Quick $\mathrm{Na}^{+}$ influx
  2. Phase 1: Early Repolarization
    • $\mathrm{K}^{+}$ channels open briefly
    • Small $\mathrm{K}^{+}$ efflux
  3. Phase 2: Plateau
    • Slow $\mathrm{Ca}^{2+}$ channels open
    • $\mathrm{Ca}^{2+}$ influx balances $\mathrm{K}^{+}$ efflux
    • Maintains depolarization ~200-300 ms
  4. Phase 3: Repolarization
    • $\mathrm{Ca}^{2+}$ channels close
    • $\mathrm{K}^{+}$ channels remain open
    • Return to resting potential
  5. Phase 4: Resting Potential
    • Maintain -90 mV until next AP

üîÑ Cardiac Conduction System

Components and Functions:

  1. SA Node (Pacemaker)
    • Located in right atrial wall
    • Spontaneous depolarization
    • Sets heart rate (60-100 beats/min)
  2. Internodal Pathways
    • Conduct impulses through atria
    • Lead to AV node
  3. AV Node
    • Delays conduction (~0.1 sec)
    • Allows atrial contraction to complete
    • Filters excessive impulses
  4. Bundle of His
    • Conducts impulses to ventricles
    • Divides into right and left bundle branches
  5. Purkinje Fibers
    • Rapid conduction through ventricles
    • Ensures coordinated contraction
