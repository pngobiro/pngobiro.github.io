Chapter 4: Neuronal Physiology

üìö Key Terms

Excitable Tissues
Capable of producing electrical signals (transient, rapid changes in membrane potential), nerve & muscle
Resting Membrane Potential
The membrane potential that exists when no net changes in potential are occurring
Graded Potentials
Local changes in membrane potential that vary in magnitude (flow of ions)
Action Potentials
Brief, rapid reversals in membrane potential, which can spread throughout the membrane (flow of ions)
Voltage-gated Channels
Membrane channels that open or close in response to changes in potential

‚ö° Membrane States

States and Changes:

  • Polarization
    • A membrane that has potential is polarized
  • Depolarization
    • A decrease in membrane potential (inside becomes more positive)
    • Triggered by events like:
      • Light
      • Touch
      • Chemical messengers
  • Hyperpolarization
    • An increase in membrane potential (inside becomes more negative)
  • Repolarization
    • Return to resting potential after a depolarization
