Chapter 6: PNS Afferent Division (continued)

üì° Types of Receptors

  1. Photoreceptors
    • Respond to light
    • Found in retina of eye
  2. Mechanoreceptors
    • Respond to mechanical stimuli
    • Examples: touch, pressure, stretch
  3. Thermoreceptors
    • Respond to temperature changes
    • Different receptors for heat and cold
  4. Osmoreceptors
    • Monitor concentration of body fluids
  5. Chemoreceptors
    • Respond to chemical stimuli
    • Examples: taste, smell, blood chemistry
  6. Nociceptors (Pain Receptors)
    • Respond to tissue damage or distortion
    • Note: intense stimulation of any receptor perceived as pain

‚è±Ô∏è Receptor Adaptation

Types of Adaptation:

  1. Tonic Receptors
    • Do not adapt or adapt slowly
    • Important for continuous information:
      • Posture and balance (proprioceptors)
      • Pain (nociceptors)
  2. Phasic Receptors
    • Adapt quickly
    • Useful when continuous information not necessary (touch)
    • Show off response - respond again when stimulus removed

Adaptation mechanisms not well known, may involve inactivation of $\mathrm{Na}^{+}$ channels
