Chapter 5: Central Nervous System (Final Part)

üéØ Functional Areas

Types of Areas:

  1. Motor Areas
    • Control voluntary motor functions
  2. Sensory Areas
    • Conscious awareness of sensation
  3. Association Areas
    • Integrate diverse information

Selected Functional Areas:

  1. Primary Visual Cortex
    • Receives visual information
    • Surrounded by higher-order visual cortex for interpretation
  2. Primary Auditory Cortex
    • Receives information on sound
    • Surrounded by higher-order auditory cortex for interpretation
  3. Somatosensory Cortex
    • Receives sensory input (touch, temperature, proprioception)
    • Features sensory homunculus - specific body parts map to specific regions
  4. Motor Areas
    • Primary Motor Cortex - voluntary control of skeletal muscle
    • Supplementary Motor Area - prepares complex movement patterns
    • Premotor Cortex - plans movement based on body orientation
  5. Language Areas
    • Broca's Area - speech production
    • Wernicke's Area - language comprehension
    • Usually in left hemisphere only

üîÑ Brain Plasticity

  • Many areas can change based on need:
    • Other areas may take over for damaged areas
    • Increased use of body parts can expand their cortical representation
  • Areas constantly interact and adapt
