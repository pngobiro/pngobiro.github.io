Basic Components:

  1. Systolic Pressure
    • Peak pressure during ventricular contraction
    • Normal ‚âà 120 mmHg
    • Reflects:
      • Stroke volume
      • Arterial compliance
  2. Diastolic Pressure
    • Lowest pressure during ventricular relaxation
    • Normal ‚âà 80 mmHg
    • Reflects:
      • Peripheral resistance
      • Arterial elastic recoil
  3. Pulse Pressure
    • Systolic - Diastolic pressure
    • Normal ‚âà 40 mmHg
  4. Mean Arterial Pressure (MAP)
    • Diastolic + ‚Öì Pulse Pressure
    • Average pressure during cardiac cycle
    • Normal ‚âà 93 mmHg

üîç Blood Pressure Measurement

Methods:

  1. Auscultation (Korotkoff Sounds)
    • Phase 1: First clear sound (systolic)
    • Phase 2: Soft, longer sounds
    • Phase 3: Loud, crisp sounds
    • Phase 4: Muffled sounds
    • Phase 5: Sounds disappear (diastolic)
  2. Direct Measurement
    • Arterial catheterization
    • Most accurate method
    • Used in critical care
  3. Automated Devices
    • Oscillometric method
    • Measures oscillations in cuff pressure

Blood pressure varies throughout day and with activity level
