Three Major Steps in Forming ATP:

1. Glycolysis

  • Occurs in cytosol
  • Glucose $\rightarrow$ 2 pyruvic acid (a series of steps)
  • Yields 2 ATP/glucose (not very efficient by itself)
  • Attaches H to carrier molecules

2. Citric Acid Cycle (Krebs Cycle/Tricarboxylic Acid Cycle)

  • Pyruvic acid is transported to mitochondrial matrix
  • Pyruvic acid $\rightarrow$ acetyl CoA
  • Acetyl CoA enters citric acid cycle (series of reactions)
  • $\mathrm{CO}_{2}$ is produced
  • Hydrogen atoms attach to carrier molecules:
    • $\mathrm{NAD}^{+}$ (nicotinamide adenine dinucleotide)
    • FAD (flavine adenine dinucleotide)
    • Become NADH and $\mathrm{FADH}_{2}$
  • 2 more ATP/original glucose

‚ö° Electron Transport Chain & Oxidative Phosphorylation

  • Occurs on inner mitochondrial membrane containing electron carrier molecules
  • NADH and $\mathrm{FADH}_{2}$ from previous steps enter chain
  • Electron removed from each H and passed through carriers
  • Final electron acceptor is $\mathrm{O}_{2}$
  • Energy from electrons used to transport $\mathrm{H}^{+}$ across inner membrane
    • Creates concentration gradient
    • $\mathrm{H}^{+}$ flows back through ATP synthase channels
    • Flow activates ATP synthase enzyme
  • Yields 28 ATP/original glucose
  • $\mathrm{H}_{2}\mathrm{O}$ formed from $\mathrm{O}_{2}$ and $\mathrm{H}^{+}$

Energy released gradually to prevent heat damage to cell

Not all energy in glucose used to make ATP (no energy transfer is 100% efficient)

üí™ ATP Usage

  • Building blocks of fats and proteins can be used to make ATP
    • Can be converted to intermediate molecules in citric acid cycle
  • ATP is used for all cell's energy needs:
    • Making and transporting substances
    • Mechanical work like muscle contraction
