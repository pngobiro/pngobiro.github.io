Chapter 6: PNS Afferent Division (Final Part)

üî• Pain

Protective function: signals tissue damage is occurring or about to occur

Nociceptor Categories:

  1. Mechanical
  2. Thermal
  3. Polymodal

Pain Pathways:

  1. Fast Pain Pathway
    • From mechanical and thermal nociceptors
    • Large, myelinated fibers
    • Localized, sharp sensation
  2. Slow Pain Pathway
    • From polymodal nociceptors
    • Small, unmyelinated C fibers
    • Dull, aching, poorly localized sensation
    • Activated by chemicals from damaged tissue (e.g., bradykinin)

CNS Processing:

  • First-order fibers release substance P in spinal cord
  • Second-order fibers project to:
    • Reticular formation (increases alertness)
    • Somatosensory cortex via thalamus (localizes stimulus)
    • Hypothalamus and limbic system (emotional responses)

üéØ Sensory Acuity

Factors Affecting Discriminative Ability:

  1. Receptive Fields
    • Each sensory neuron has specific area it responds to
    • Greater density of receptors results in greater acuity
  2. Lateral Inhibition
    • Areas nearest stimulus stimulated more strongly
    • Areas farther away stimulated less
    • Strong pathways inhibit weaker ones via CNS interneurons
