Chapter 2: Cell Physiology (Final Part)

üíß Cytosol

  • Makes up 55% of cell volume
  • Composition varies in different areas of cell
  • Cell Activities:
    1. Enzymatic regulation of intermediary metabolism
      • Chemical reactions involving synthesizing and breaking down small organic molecules (sugars, amino acids, fatty acids)
      • Provides raw material for structure and function
    2. Ribosome protein synthesis
      • Free ribosomes make enzymes for use in cytosol
    3. Storage of fat and glycogen
      • Inclusions - masses of stored nutrients
      • Can be broken down when needed to make ATP

üî® Cytoskeleton

Complex protein network running through cytosol

  • Supports and organizes parts of cell
  • Controls movements of cell and within cell

Components:

  1. Microtubules
    • Largest of cytoskeletal parts
    • Made mostly of protein tubulin
    • Functions:
      • Maintain cell shape
      • Transport of vesicles
      • Movement of cilia and flagella
      • Formation of mitotic spindle
  2. Microfilaments
    • Smaller elements, many made of protein actin
    • Functions:
      • Cellular contractile systems
      • Mechanical support
      • Support microvilli
  3. Intermediate Filaments
    • Medium sized
    • Made of different proteins depending on cell
    • Functions:
      • Strengthen and stabilize cells
      • Hold together contractile units in muscle cells
