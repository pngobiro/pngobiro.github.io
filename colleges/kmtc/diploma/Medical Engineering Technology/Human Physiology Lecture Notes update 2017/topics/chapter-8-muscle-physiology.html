Chapter 8: Muscle Physiology

üí™ Basic Muscle Types

Three Types of Muscle:

  1. Skeletal Muscle
    • Striated appearance
    • Voluntary control
    • Multinucleate cells
    • Function: Movement of body parts
  2. Cardiac Muscle
    • Striated appearance
    • Involuntary control
    • Single nucleus per cell
    • Function: Pump blood
  3. Smooth Muscle
    • Unstriated appearance
    • Involuntary control
    • Single nucleus per cell
    • Function: Movement within hollow organs

üîÑ Skeletal Muscle Organization

Hierarchical Structure:

  1. Whole Muscle
    • Made of long cells bundled with connective tissue
    • Connective tissue extends to form tendons
    • Tendons attach muscle to bone
  2. Fascicles
    • Bundles of muscle fibers
    • Surrounded by perimysium
  3. Muscle Fibers (Cells)
    • Individual muscle cells
    • Surrounded by endomysium
    • Contains myofibrils
  4. Myofibrils
    • Specialized contractile organelles
    • Made of protein filaments (myofilaments)
    • Organized into sarcomeres
