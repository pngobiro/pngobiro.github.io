Chapter 9: Cardiac Physiology

‚ù§Ô∏è Cardiac Muscle Characteristics

Unique Features:

  1. Cellular Organization
    • Branched, interconnected cells
    • Gap junctions between cells (intercalated discs)
    • Electrical coupling through gap junctions
    • Functions as syncytium (coordinated unit)
  2. Metabolic Properties
    • High density of mitochondria
    • Extensive capillary network
    • Primarily aerobic metabolism
    • Large glycogen stores
  3. Contractile Properties
    • Long refractory period
    • Cannot develop tetanus
    • Resistant to fatigue
    • Rhythmic self-excitation (automaticity)

üîÑ Basic Heart Organization

Functional Anatomy:

  • Four chambers:
    • Right atrium - receives systemic venous blood
    • Right ventricle - pumps to pulmonary circulation
    • Left atrium - receives oxygenated blood
    • Left ventricle - pumps to systemic circulation
  • Valves:
    • AV valves (between atria and ventricles)
      • Tricuspid (right)
      • Mitral (left)
    • Semilunar valves
      • Pulmonary valve (right ventricle)
      • Aortic valve (left ventricle)
