  1. Dura Mater - outer layer
    • Forms dural sinuses and venous sinuses
    • Blood and CSF pool here before returning to circulation
  2. Arachnoid Mater - middle layer
    • Subarachnoid space contains CSF
    • Arachnoid villi reabsorb CSF back into blood in sinuses
  3. Pia Mater - inner layer
    • Well vascularized
    • Important in forming CSF

Physical Protection:

  • Cranium (skull) - protects brain
  • Vertebral column - protects spinal cord

üß† Brain Structure

Cerebrum:

  • Structure
    • Cortex - outer layer of gray matter
      • Contains neuron cell bodies and dendrites
      • Contains glial cells
    • White matter underneath
      • Tracts of myelinated fibers
      • Transmits signals between cortical areas
      • Connects to other CNS locations
  • Organization
    • Two hemispheres connected by corpus callosum
    • Most functional areas in both hemispheres
    • Hemispheric specialization:
      • Left - logical, analytical tasks, language, math, fine motor control
      • Right - nonlanguage skills, spatial perception, art/music
    • Generally controls opposite side of body (contralateral)
  • Lobes
    • Paired lobes: occipital, temporal, parietal, frontal
    • Functional areas often contained within specific lobes
