üìà Graded Potentials

Key Characteristics:

  • Magnitude Relationship
    • Magnitude of graded potential related to magnitude of triggering event
    • Stronger trigger ‚Üí greater magnitude of change in potential

Current Flow Process:

  1. Initial Response
    • A piece of membrane (active area) has different potential than rest of membrane (inactive area)
  2. Current Flow
    • Current flows between active and adjacent inactive areas
    • Opposite charges attract
    • Previously inactive areas become active
  3. Signal Spread
    • Spreads decrementally (decreases as it moves)
    • Current leaks into extracellular fluid
    • Functions as signals over short distances

üîç Important Features

  • Usually not an actual reversal of charges
    • Just a reduction in potential
    • Inside becomes less negative than before
    • Small depolarization occurs
  • Importance in Neural Function
    • Critical for nerve and muscle cells
    • Example: postsynaptic potentials

Graded potentials are localized responses that serve as important signaling mechanisms in neural communication, particularly at synapses.
