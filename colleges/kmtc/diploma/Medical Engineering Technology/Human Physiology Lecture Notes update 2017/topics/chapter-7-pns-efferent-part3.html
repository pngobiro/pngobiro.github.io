‚öñÔ∏è Dual Innervation

Basic Principles:

  • Most visceral organs innervated by both divisions
  • Generally have opposite effects
  • Can be excitatory or inhibitory depending on organ
  • Both systems usually partially active (tonic activity)
  • System dominance occurs when:
    • One increases firing rate
    • Other decreases firing rate

Exceptions to Dual Innervation:

  • Most blood vessels:
    • Only sympathetic fibers
    • Regulated by increasing/decreasing from tonic level
  • Most sweat glands:
    • Only sympathetic fibers
    • Release ACh instead of NE

üéØ System Effects

Sympathetic Dominance ("Fight or Flight"):

  • Increases oxygen/nutrient-rich blood flow to skeletal muscles:
    • Vessel dilation in muscles
    • Heart beats faster and more forcefully
    • Blood pressure increases
    • Respiratory airways dilate
  • Metabolic changes:
    • Glycogen and fat stores broken down
    • Digestive and urinary activities inhibited
  • Other effects:
    • Pupils dilate
    • Sweating increases

Parasympathetic Dominance ("Rest and Digest"):

  • Increases normal resting functions:
    • Digestive activity enhanced
    • Urinary function increased
  • Inhibits sympathetic activities:
    • Slows heart rate
    • Reduces blood pressure

This dual control system allows precise regulation of body functions
