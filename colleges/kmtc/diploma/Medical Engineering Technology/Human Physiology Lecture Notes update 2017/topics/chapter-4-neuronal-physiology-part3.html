‚ö° Action Potentials (AP)

Can be transmitted over long distances without losing strength

Depolarization Process:

  1. Initial Phase
    • Triggering event causes slow depolarization
    • Continues until threshold potential reached (-50 to -55 mV)
  2. Threshold Response
    • At threshold, membrane quickly depolarizes to +30 mV
    • Voltage-gated $\mathrm{Na}^{+}$ channels open
    • $\mathrm{Na}^{+}$ flows into cell
  3. Channel Dynamics
    • Further depolarization opens more $\mathrm{Na}^{+}$ channels
    • At threshold, all $\mathrm{Na}^{+}$ channels open
    • Explosive increase in $\mathrm{Na}^{+}$ permeability
  4. Peak and Closure
    • At peak depolarization, $\mathrm{Na}^{+}$ channels close
    • Same depolarization that opens channels also closes them

üîÑ Repolarization

  1. Channel Activity
    • As $\mathrm{Na}^{+}$ channels close, $\mathrm{K}^{+}$ channels open
    • Due to delayed voltage-gated response to depolarization
    • $\mathrm{K}^{+}$ flows out of cell
  2. Return to Resting State
    • $\mathrm{Na}^{+}$ channels resume original conformation
    • Newly opened $\mathrm{K}^{+}$ channels close
  3. Hyperpolarization
    • Occurs before channels close completely
    • Membrane becomes even more negative than resting potential
    • Eventually returns to resting potential

The entire action potential lasts about 1 millisecond

Ion gradients are restored by the $\mathrm{Na}^{+}\text{-}\mathrm{K}^{+}$ pump
