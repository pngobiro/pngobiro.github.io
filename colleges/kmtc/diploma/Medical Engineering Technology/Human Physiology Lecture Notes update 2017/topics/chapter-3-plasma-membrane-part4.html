Chapter 3: Plasma Membrane (Final Part)

‚ö° Membrane Potential

Separation of charges across a membrane

Basic Concepts:

  • Separated charges have potential to do work
  • Measured in millivolts (mV)
  • All plasma membranes have potential
  • Due to unequal distribution of key ions

Key Ions Involved:

  • $\mathrm{Na}^{+}$
  • $\mathrm{K}^{+}$
  • $A^{-}$ (large anionic intracellular proteins)

üîÑ $\mathrm{Na}^{+}\text{-}\mathrm{K}^{+}$ Pump

Direct Effects (20% of potential):

  • Actively transports 3 $\mathrm{Na}^{+}$ out for every 2 $\mathrm{K}^{+}$ in
  • Results:
    • Cell slightly negative inside
    • Establishes concentration gradients
    • $\mathrm{Na}^{+}$ high outside, $\mathrm{K}^{+}$ high inside

Indirect Effects (80% of potential):

  • Membrane more permeable to $\mathrm{K}^{+}$ than $\mathrm{Na}^{+}$
  • $\mathrm{K}^{+}$ flows out passively
  • Flow continues until:
    • Concentration gradient balanced by electrical gradient
    • Negative charges inside attract $\mathrm{K}^{+}$
  • Very little $\mathrm{Na}^{+}$ leaks back in (closed channels)

Results in resting membrane potential of -70 mV in typical nerve cell (negative inside)

üîã Other Effects on Membrane Potential

  • $A^{-}$ cannot leave cell (too large)
    • Contributes to negative charges balancing $\mathrm{K}^{+}$ leakage
  • $\mathrm{Cl}^{-}$ distribution influenced by membrane potential
    • High outside
    • Negative charge inside drives $\mathrm{Cl}^{-}$ out
