üî¨ Glial Cells (Neuroglia)

  1. Astrocytes
    • Hold neurons together
    • Repair injury and form scar tissue
    • Induce and participate in blood-brain barrier
    • Take up and break down neurotransmitters (glutamate, GABA)
    • Take up excess $\mathrm{K}^{+}$ in ECF
    • Enhance synapse formation and modify function
    • Communicate via gap junctions and chemicals
  2. Oligodendrocytes
    • Forms myelin sheaths
  3. Ependymal Cells
    • Line internal cavities of CNS
    • Help form cerebrospinal fluid (CSF)
    • Serve as stem cells in some brain areas
  4. Microglia
    • Defense cells, can perform phagocytosis
    • Secrete nerve cell growth factor

Blood-Brain Barrier:

  • Capillaries in brain have tight junctions joining cells
  • Selective substance passage:
    • Lipid soluble substances (e.g., $\mathrm{O}_{2}$, $\mathrm{CO}_{2}$, alcohol, steroid hormones)
    • Substances with specific carriers (e.g., glucose, amino acids, ions)
  • Functions:
    • Protects brain from harmful substances
    • Keeps out circulating hormones that act like neurotransmitters

Cerebrospinal Fluid (CSF):

  • Formed by choroid plexuses
  • Surrounds brain and spinal cord
  • Functions:
    • Cushions CNS
    • Serves as interstitial fluid of CNS
    • Enables exchanges with CNS cells
  • Composition:
    • Similar to plasma
    • Lower in $\mathrm{K}^{+}$ and higher in $\mathrm{Na}^{+}$
