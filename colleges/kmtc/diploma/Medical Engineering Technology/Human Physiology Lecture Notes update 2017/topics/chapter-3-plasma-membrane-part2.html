üîó Cell to Cell Adhesions

Carbohydrates on membrane surface help arrange cells into groups, which are held together in various ways

Types of Adhesion:

  1. Cell Adhesion Molecules (CAMs)
  2. Extracellular Matrix
    • Cells embedded in matrix of carbohydrates and protein fibers
    • Components:
      • Collagen - resists tension (e.g., skin)
      • Elastin - stretch and recoil (e.g., skin, lungs)
      • Fibronectin - holds cells in position
    • Functions:
      • Substances diffuse through between blood and tissues
      • Important in normal cell functioning
  3. Specialized Junctions
    • Desmosomes - join cells at particular spots, found where stretch occurs
    • Tight junctions - form impermeable barrier in epithelial sheets
    • Gap junctions - protein tunnels allowing small molecules to pass between cells

Factors Influencing Transport:

  1. Solubility of the substance in lipid
  2. Size of substance

Transport Categories:

  • Small, uncharged or nonpolar molecules
    • Move through lipid bilayer
    • Examples: $\mathrm{O}_{2}$, $\mathrm{CO}_{2}$, fatty acids
  • Ions and small polar molecules
    • Move through channels or carrier proteins
    • Example: glucose
  • Large molecules
    • Require special transport mechanisms
