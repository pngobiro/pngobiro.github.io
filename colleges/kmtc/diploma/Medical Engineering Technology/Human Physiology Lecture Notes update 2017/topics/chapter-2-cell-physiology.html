Chapter 2: Cell Physiology

üî¨ Cell Basics

Typical human cell is 10-20 $\mu \mathrm{m}$ in diameter

($\mu \mathrm{m} = $ micrometer, $1/1000 \mathrm{~mm}$, $1/1,000,000 \mathrm{~m}$)

Three Major Subdivisions:

  1. Plasma membrane (cell membrane)
    • Defines inside/outside
    • Intracellular fluid (ICF) - inside cell
    • Extracellular fluid (ECF) - outside cell
    • Selectively permeable - controls movement of molecules between ICF and ECF
  2. Nucleus
    • Usually near cell center
    • Double layered membrane
    • Contains DNA, "genetic blueprint," directs protein synthesis, control center of cell
  3. Cytoplasm
    • Area between nucleus and plasma membrane
    • Contains organelles for separation of chemical reactions
    • Cytosol is semiliquid, site of chemical reactions

üß¨ Endoplasmic Reticulum (ER)

Interconnected fluid-filled membranous system

Two Types:

  1. Rough ER
    • Interconnected flattened sacs
    • Has ribosomes which help in protein synthesis
    • Functions:
      • Synthesizes proteins and lipids
      • Releases products to ER lumen
      • Some products secreted from cell (hormones, enzymes)
      • Some become new membrane for cell or organelles
      • Proteins can be modified in lumen (pieces removed, sugars added)
  2. Smooth ER
    • Interconnected tubules
    • Functions:
      • Packages and transports products of rough ER
      • Lipid synthesis in steroid hormone secreting cells
      • Detoxifies harmful substances in liver cells
      • Stores calcium in muscle cells
