Chapter 3: Plasma Membrane (continued)

⬇️ Passive Transport

No ATP used

Types of Passive Transport:

  1. Diffusion
    • Molecules move down their concentration gradient (greater → lesser concentration)
    • Charged particles move down electrochemical gradients
    • Types:
      • Simple diffusion - through lipid bilayer or protein channels (e.g., $\mathrm{O}_{2}$, $\mathrm{CO}_{2}$, some ions)
      • Osmosis - water moves down its concentration gradient
      • Facilitated diffusion - uses carrier protein that binds to molecule (e.g., glucose)
  2. Filtration
    • Water and solutes forced through membrane by pressure
    • Example: in kidneys

⬆️ Active Transport

ATP used

Types of Active Transport:

  1. Carrier Protein Transport
    • Transport substance against concentration gradient
    • Needs ATP to change conformation
    • Types:
      • Primary active transport - ATP used directly (e.g., $\mathrm{Na}^{+}\text{-}\mathrm{K}^{+}$ pump)
      • Secondary active transport - driven by gradients from primary transport
  2. Vesicular Transport (Bulk Transport)
    • For large molecules or multimolecular substances
    • Types:
      • Endocytosis (into cell)
        • Fuse with lysosomes to break down substances
        • Transport across cells (transcytosis)
      • Exocytosis (out of cell)
        • Secretion of large molecules
        • Adding membrane components
