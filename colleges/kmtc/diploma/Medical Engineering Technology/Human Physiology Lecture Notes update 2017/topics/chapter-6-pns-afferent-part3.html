‚ö° Graded Receptor Potentials

Signal Generation Process:

  1. Initial Response
    • Stimulus alters membrane permeability of receptor
    • Ion channels open, mainly $\mathrm{Na}^{+}$ flows in
    • Depolarization occurs
  2. Action Potential Generation
    • If summation of depolarizations reaches threshold:
      • Generator potential triggers AP in receptor
      • Receptor potential triggers AP in nearby neuron
  3. Signal Coding
    • Frequency code: stronger stimuli ‚Üí greater frequency of APs
    • Population code: stronger stimuli activate more receptors

üîÄ Somatosensory Pathways

Signal Processing Chain:

  1. First-Order Sensory Neuron
    • Afferent neuron with receptor
    • Synapses with second-order neuron
  2. Second-Order Sensory Neuron
    • Located in spinal cord or medulla
    • Synapses with third-order neuron
  3. Third-Order Sensory Neuron
    • Located in thalamus
    • Projects to specific areas of cortex

Information Processing:

  • Projection through specific pathways provides information about:
    • Type of stimulus
    • Location of stimulus
    • Intensity of stimulus
