Chapter 10: Blood Vessels and Blood Pressure

ü©∏ Types of Blood Vessels

Major Categories:

  1. Arteries
    • Thick, elastic walls
    • Carry blood away from heart
    • High pressure system
    • Types:
      • Elastic arteries (large)
      • Muscular arteries (medium)
      • Arterioles (small)
  2. Capillaries
    • Single cell thick walls
    • Site of exchange with tissues
    • Types:
      • Continuous (most tissues)
      • Fenestrated (kidneys, intestines)
      • Sinusoids (liver, bone marrow)
  3. Veins
    • Thinner, less elastic walls
    • Carry blood toward heart
    • Low pressure system
    • Contains valves
    • Types:
      • Venules (smallest)
      • Medium veins
      • Large veins

üî¨ Vessel Wall Structure

Three Layers (Tunics):

  1. Tunica Intima
    • Endothelium
    • Basement membrane
    • Internal elastic lamina
  2. Tunica Media
    • Smooth muscle
    • Elastic fibers
    • Controls vessel diameter
  3. Tunica Externa
    • Connective tissue
    • Collagen fibers
    • Anchors vessel
