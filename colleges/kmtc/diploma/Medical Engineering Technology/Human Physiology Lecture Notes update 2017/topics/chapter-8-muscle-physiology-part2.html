Chapter 8: Muscle Physiology (continued)

üî¨ Muscle Fiber Structure

Components:

  1. Sarcolemma (Plasma Membrane)
    • Forms T tubules:
      • Project into cell
      • Continuous with surface membrane
      • Allow electrical activity transmission throughout cell
  2. Sarcoplasmic Reticulum (SR)
    • Modified smooth ER
    • Network of tubules surrounding myofibrils
    • Terminal cisternae:
      • Expanded ends of SR
      • Store calcium ions ($\mathrm{Ca}^{2+}$)

üß¨ Myofilament Structure

Types of Filaments:

  1. Thick Filaments
    • Made of myosin molecules
    • Each myosin has:
      • Head region (forms cross bridges)
      • Tail region
      • Actin binding site
      • ATPase site
  2. Thin Filaments
    • Made mostly of actin molecules
    • Each actin has myosin binding site
    • Additional proteins:
      • Tropomyosin - blocks binding sites at rest
      • Troponin - holds tropomyosin, has $\mathrm{Ca}^{2+}$ binding site

Sarcomere Organization:

  • Functional units that contract
  • Regular arrangement creates striations:
    • A bands - thick filaments
    • I bands - thin filaments only
    • Z lines - anchor thin filaments
    • H zone - thick filaments only
    • M line - holds thick filaments
