Chapter 2: Cell Physiology (continued)

üîÑ Golgi Complex

  • Layers of flattened membranous sacs (cisternae)
  • Processes ER products into final form
  • Sorts and sends products to appropriate place:
    • Transport vesicles take products to locations in or outside cell
    • Coating made up of proteins that recognize the product and its destination
    • Secretory vesicles transport products out of specialized cells

üß™ Lysosomes and Peroxisomes

Lysosomes:

  • Membranous sacs containing hydrolytic enzymes
  • Digest cellular debris and other substances (old organelles, bacteria)
  • Material from outside cell can be brought in to be digested:
    • Pinocytosis - fluids, "cell drinking"
    • Phagocytosis - large particles, "cell eating"
  • Cell can use digested material

Peroxisomes:

  • Membranous sacs containing oxidative enzymes
  • Use oxygen to remove hydrogen from molecules
  • Detoxify wastes and other chemicals like alcohol

‚ö° Mitochondria

Structure:

  • Double membrane
    • Inner membrane has folds called cristae (increase surface area)
    • Matrix is gel inside

Functions:

  • Play a role in apoptosis (programmed cell death)
  • Converts energy from food into usable energy for the cell - ATP
    • Energy in chemical bonds of food molecules can't be used directly
    • ATP $\rightarrow$ ADP $+\mathrm{P}_{\mathrm{i}}+$ useful energy
