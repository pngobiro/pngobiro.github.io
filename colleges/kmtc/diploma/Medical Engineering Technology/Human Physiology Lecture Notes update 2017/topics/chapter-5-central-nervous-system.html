Chapter 5: Central Nervous System

üß† System Overview

A "wired" system with specific pathways for transmission of signals between areas of body

Primary Functions:

  • Coordinates rapid, precise responses
  • Interacts with endocrine system ("wireless")

Organization:

  1. Central Nervous System (CNS)
    • Brain
    • Spinal cord
  2. Peripheral Nervous System (PNS)
    • Nerve fibers carrying information between CNS and body
    • Divisions:
      • Afferent (sensory) - carries information toward CNS
      • Efferent (motor) - carries information away from CNS to effector organs

üîÑ Classes of Neurons

  1. Afferent Neurons
    • In afferent division of PNS
    • Peripheral end has sensory receptor
    • Generates APs in response to stimulus
    • Cell body near spinal cord
    • Synapses with other neurons in spinal cord
  2. Efferent Neurons
    • In efferent division of PNS
    • Cell body in CNS
    • Terminates at muscle or gland
  3. Interneurons
    • Most numerous type of neuron
    • Located in CNS
    • Between afferent and efferent neurons
    • Interconnect with one another
