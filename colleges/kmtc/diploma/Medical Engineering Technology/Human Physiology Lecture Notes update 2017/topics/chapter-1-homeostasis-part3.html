Chapter 1: Homeostasis (Final Part)

üéÆ Control Mechanisms

Body controlled mainly by nervous and endocrine systems

Parts of a Control System:

  1. Sensor
    • Monitors variable (factor being regulated)
    • Responds to changes (stimuli) by sending input to integrator
  2. Integrator
    • Determines set point (appropriate level of variable)
    • Compares set point to input
    • Sends response to effector
  3. Effector
    • Responds to changes

Feedback Systems:

  • Negative Feedback
    • Decreases or shuts off original stimulus
    • Resists change
  • Positive Feedback
    • Enhances original stimulus
    • Examples:
      • Uterine contractions during childbirth
      • Blood clotting

üîÑ Major Organ Systems

Levels of Organization:

  • Organs
    • Group of two or more tissues designed to perform specific functions
  • Body System (organ system)
    • Group of organs designed to perform particular functions
  • Whole Organism
    • Group of organ systems
