Chapter 8: Muscle Physiology (continued)

‚ö° Excitation-Contraction Coupling

Process Steps:

  1. End-plate potential at motor end plate results in AP
  2. AP spreads throughout cell surface and T tubules
  3. Triggers release of $\mathrm{Ca}^{2+}$ from terminal cisternae
  4. $\mathrm{Ca}^{2+}$ binds to troponin, changing its shape
  5. Tropomyosin moves away from binding sites
  6. Allows cross bridge attachment between myosin and actin

üîÑ Sliding Filament Mechanism

Cross Bridge Cycle:

  1. Cross Bridge Attachment
    • Myosin heads bind to actin
    • Requires $\mathrm{Ca}^{2+}$ bound to troponin
  2. Power Stroke
    • Myosin head pivots
    • Uses energy of ATP
    • Pulls thin filaments toward center of sarcomere
  3. Cross Bridge Detachment
    • New ATP molecule binds to myosin head
    • Cross bridge releases
  4. "Cocking" of Myosin Head
    • ATP ‚Üí ADP + $\mathrm{P}_i$ by ATPase
    • Myosin head returns to original position
    • High energy conformation established

Important Notes:

  • During contraction:
    • Only some cross bridges attached at any time
    • As some release, others attach
    • Prevents thin filaments from sliding backward
  • Timing:
    • Latent period between AP and contraction
    • Contraction and relaxation last about 100 msec
  • Response:
    • Individual cell contraction is all-or-none
