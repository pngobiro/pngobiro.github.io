Chapter 6: PNS Afferent Division

üì° Basic Concepts

Information Flow:

  • Information carried toward CNS from:
    • Viscera (internal organs)
    • Surface areas
  • Types of Sensation:
    • Somatic sensation
      • Somesthetic sensation (skin)
      • Proprioception (muscles, joints, inner ear)
    • Special senses
      • Vision
      • Hearing
      • Taste
      • Smell

Perception: Our conscious interpretation of the world created by the brain from a pattern of nerve impulses from sensory receptors

üîç Receptor Physiology

General Characteristics:

  • Located at peripheral ends of afferent neurons
  • Detect stimuli (changes in internal or external environment)
  • Convert stimulus to action potential (transduction)
  • Specificity:
    • Each receptor specialized for one type of stimulus (adequate stimulus)
    • May respond to other stimuli, but leads to sensation usually detected by that receptor
  • Structure:
    • May be special cell associated with peripheral ending of neuron
