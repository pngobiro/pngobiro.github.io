üî¨ Tissues

Group of cells with similar structure and function, plus extracellular material

Four Major Types:

  1. Muscle
    • Specialized for contraction and force generation
    • Skeletal - movement of body or body parts
    • Cardiac - pump blood
    • Smooth - movement of organs
  2. Epithelial
    • Specialized for exchange between cell and environment
    • Two general types - sheets and secretory glands
    • Sheets are tightly joined cells covering or lining parts of the body
    • Glands secrete products (exocrine glands have ducts leading to a body surface, e.g. sweat glands; endocrine glands release products to interstitial fluid and it goes into blood, e.g. adrenal glands)
  3. Nervous
    • Specialized for initiating and transmitting electrical impulses
    • Brain, spinal cord, nerves
  4. Connective
    • Specialized for connecting and supporting
    • Found all over body

‚öñÔ∏è Homeostasis

A dynamic equilibrium where body conditions are maintained within narrow limits

  • Necessary for each cell to survive
  • Each cell contributes
  • All cells are in contact with the aqueous internal environment

Major Factors Maintained:

  • Concentration of nutrient molecules
    • Cells need energy and building blocks
  • Concentration of $\mathrm{O}_{2}$ and $\mathrm{CO}_{2}$
    • $\mathrm{O}_{2}$ used to make usable energy (ATP)
    • $\mathrm{CO}_{2}$ made must be removed
  • Concentration of waste products
    • Become toxic at high levels
  • pH
    • Acidity affects enzyme reactions and nerve cell impulses
  • Water, salt and electrolyte concentration
    • Maintaining cell volume
    • Various functions of electrolytes
  • Temperature
    • Too cold or too much heat harmful to cells
  • Volume and pressure
    • Blood must be at appropriate volume and pressure to be transported around the body
