Introduction

  • ‚Ä¢Death does occur at any stage
  • ‚Ä¢However, old people are more aware of its eminence
  • ‚Ä¢They are less afraid of death than others
  • ‚Ä¢Cultural beliefs influence the way people perceive death and react to dying & death Elizabeth Kubler-Ross (1969) organized her theory after interviewing dying patientsAccording to her, the dying process consists of five interrelated stages

NB: some people may skip a stage & other may not go through a given order while others may get stack at a particular stage. For others, the five stages may overlap

Defination

  • ‚Ä¢Loss
    This is when the other person, possession, body part, familiar environment or sense of self is no longer present?
  • ‚Ä¢Grief
    It is when emotional response to a loss manifest in ways unique to an individual, based on personal experiences, cultural expectation and spiritual beliefs.
  • ‚Ä¢Mourning
    It is the social expression of grief and the behaviors associate with loss.
  • ‚Ä¢Bereavement
    It captures both grief and mourning and includes the emotional response and outward behavior of a person experiencing loss.
  • ‚Ä¢Death is loss of life.

Stages of death or grieving

There are five stages that are in an orderly manner but some people do not experience them in that order or length of time. They sometimes move back and forth between the stages.

Denial

  • ‚Ä¢Acceptance of facing death is difficult.
  • ‚Ä¢People may react with shock and disbelief
  • ‚Ä¢A person acts as if nothing has happened and refuses to accept the reality
  • ‚Ä¢Shows no understanding of what is happening
  • ‚Ä¢Denial permits hope to exist and it is short lived.

Anger

  • ‚Ä¢When denial is no longer successful, the patients experience feelings of anger, rage and envy or resentment
  • ‚Ä¢The person asks the "Why me" question, expresses resistance and sometimes feels intense anger at God, other people or the situation.
  • ‚Ä¢The person is hostile to people assisting him and anything done does not please him.

Bargaining

  • ‚Ä¢The patient hopes that death can be delayed
  • ‚Ä¢He may promise God to do many things if allowed to live
  • ‚Ä¢The dying person attempts to negotiate not to die at that time
  • ‚Ä¢This cushions and postpones awareness of the loss by trying to prevent it from happening.
  • ‚Ä¢It involves a deal with God, doctors, nurses.
  • ‚Ä¢The dying or grieving person make promises to self, god or loved ones that they will live or believe differently if they are spared.

Depression

  • ‚Ä¢This happen when the person realizes the full impact of the loss
  • ‚Ä¢The defensive mechanism are no longer effective
  • ‚Ä¢The person expresses sadness and anguish and some individuals are overwhelmingly sad, hopeless and lonely.
  • ‚Ä¢Crying elicit support of the loved ones and the mental health caregiver, so they should be available for the patient
  • ‚Ä¢They sometimes withdraw from relationships and life and may have suicide attempts. Resolution leads to acceptance.

Acceptance

  • ‚Ä¢This stage allows the dying to express their feelings, settle and wait for their death
  • ‚Ä¢The person incorporates the loss into life and finds ways to move forward
  • ‚Ä¢This time the person finds peace and contentment.
  • ‚Ä¢The patient may desire time alone with his thoughts NB: these stages may also apply to the bereaved Some patients may ask to get outside the room This time the patient reviews his past and thinks of the unknown future. Patient need quietness with minimal verbalization. The patient may now talk about his funeral plans. Writing of the will.
