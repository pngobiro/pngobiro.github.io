Graphical Methods

Graphs are useful for presenting quantitative data. They give a quick impression of the important features of the data.

Commonly used graphs include:

  • ‚Ä¢Bar chart
  • ‚Ä¢Pie chart
  • ‚Ä¢Histogram
  • ‚Ä¢Frequency polygon
  • ‚Ä¢Cumulative frequency curve (Ogive)
  • ‚Ä¢Scatter plot

Bar Chart

Used for presenting qualitative data (nominal or ordinal) or discrete quantitative data.

The frequency (or relative frequency) of each category is represented by a bar.

The bars are drawn separated from each other.

Types of Bar Charts:

  • a)Simple Bar Chart: Used when the data has only one variable.

Example: Immunization status of children in Adami Tulu woreda, Feb. 1995.

Pie Chart

Used for presenting qualitative data (nominal or ordinal).

A circle is divided into sectors, where each sector represents a category.

The angle of each sector is proportional to the frequency (or relative frequency) of the category: Angle = (Frequency / Total Frequency) * 360¬∞.

Histogram

Used for presenting quantitative data (usually continuous) that is grouped into classes.

The frequencies (or relative frequencies) are represented by rectangles (bars).

The bars are drawn adjacent to each other (no gaps).

The horizontal axis represents the class boundaries.

The vertical axis represents the frequency (or relative frequency).

The area of each bar is proportional to the frequency of the corresponding class.
