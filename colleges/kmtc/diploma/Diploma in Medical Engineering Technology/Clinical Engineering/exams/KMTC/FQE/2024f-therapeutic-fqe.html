16.
How does jet nebulizers deliver medication?
A. By ultrasonic waves
B. By pushing jets of compressed air through the liquid drug to aerosolize medication
C. By dissolving the drugs in liquid propellant
D. By spraying a jet of water through the drug
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B. By pushing jets of compressed air through the liquid drug to aerosolize medication

Explanation: Jet nebulizers use the Bernoulli principle. Compressed air passing through a venturi creates low pressure that draws liquid medicine up a tube, where it is blasted into a fine mist (aerosol) by the air jet.

46.

a) State three types of Retinoscopy. (3 marks)

b) Describe Focault's principle as applied in Retinoscopy. (2 marks)

Show Answer

a) Types:

  1. Static Retinoscopy
  2. Dynamic Retinoscopy
  3. Radical Retinoscopy
  4. Cycloplegic Retinoscopy

b) Foucault's Principle (Knife-edge test):

This principle explains the movement of shadows. If light is intercepted coming from a point source, the shadow moves in a direction depending on the focus relative to the interception point. In retinoscopy, the movement of the reflex ("with" or "against" motion) indicates whether the retina is focused in front of, at, or behind the peephole (observer), allowing determination of the refractive error.

49.

a) Explain the need for countering humidity in operation theatre. (6 marks)

b) Explain four essential requirements for operation theatre lamps in operation theatre. (4 marks)

c) Draw a labelled diagram of a modern operating theatre table and indicate the below components; (10 marks)
i. Leg rest down position
ii. Lateral rotation arm
iii. Back rest elevation arm
iv. Warm gears

Show Answer

a) Countering Humidity in OT

Maintaining optimal humidity (typically 50-60%) is crucial for:

  1. Infection Control: High humidity promotes bacterial and fungal growth. Low humidity can dry tissues and increase airborne dust/bacteria.
  2. Static Electricity: Low humidity increases the risk of electrostatic discharge (ESD), which can ignite flammable anesthetic gases (historically) or damage sensitive electronic medical equipment.
  3. Comfort: High humidity is uncomfortable for the surgical team wearing sterile gowns.
  4. Equipment Protection: High humidity can cause corrosion or condensation on equipment.

b) Requirements for OT Lamps

  1. Shadowless Illumination: Must minimize shadows from the surgeon's head/hands (multi-point light source).
  2. Cool Light (Heat Reduction): Must not generate excessive heat (infrared) that could dry tissues or discomfort the surgeon (Dichroic filters/LEDs).
  3. Color Rendering (CRI): Must provide natural color (high CRI) to distinguish tissue types and pathology accurately.
  4. Adjustability/Depth of Field: Must be easily positioned and focusable into deep cavities.
