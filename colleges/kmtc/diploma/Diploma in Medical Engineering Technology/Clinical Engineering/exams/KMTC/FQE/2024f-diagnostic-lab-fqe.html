Section 1: Multiple Choice Questions

40 Marks
1.
Which of the following choices lists the steps of pathogenesis in the correct order?
A. Invasion, Infection, Adhesion and Exposure
B. Adhesion, Exposure, Infection and Exposure
C. Exposure, Adhesion, Invasion and Infection
D. Disease, Infection, Exposure and Invasion
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C. Exposure, Adhesion, Invasion and Infection

Explanation: To cause disease, a pathogen must first contact the host (Exposure), attach to host cells (Adhesion), penetrate host tissues (Invasion), and then multiply/cause damage (Infection).

2.
Which of the following is an ionizing radiation?
A. Infrared rays
B. X-ray and gamma rays
C. Ultraviolet rays
D. Microwave rays
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B. X-ray and gamma rays

Explanation: Ionizing radiation has enough energy to remove tightly bound electrons from the orbit of an atom (ionization). X-rays and Gamma rays are high-energy ionizing radiations. UV is mostly non-ionizing (except extreme UV), and IR/Microwaves are non-ionizing.

3.
The ability of an instrument to give same output for equal inputs over same period of time is called
A. Linearity
B. Range
C. Reproducibility
D. Resolution
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C. Reproducibility (or Repeatability)

Explanation: Reproducibility (or precision/repeatability) refers to the closeness of agreement between successive measurements of the same quantity under the same conditions.

4.
Difference between true value and measured value divided by the true value is referred to as
A. Zero-drift
B. Accuracy
C. Reliability
D. Sensitivity
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B. Accuracy (Relative Error)

Explanation: The formula $\frac{\text{Measured} - \text{True}}{\text{True}}$ calculates Relative Error. Accuracy is often quantified by the percentage error. While strictly "Error", in the context of instrument specifications, high accuracy corresponds to low error.

6.
Which of the following factors pulse rate?
A. Physical condition, age and gender
B. Physical condition, age and weight
C. Age, weight and gender
D. Gender, height and age
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A. Physical condition, age and gender

Explanation: Pulse rate is significantly affected by physical fitness (athletes have lower rates), age (decreases from infancy to adulthood), and gender (females often have slightly higher rates). Weight and height are less direct factors compared to fitness.

10.
The following statements describes the functions of counter method of blood cell counting. Which one does not?
A. It is a particle counter based on the counting mechanism of electrical impendance cells
B. It provides the number of cells per particular and their sixes within the sample
C. It is useful for particle characterization

Correct Answer: D. It requires a beam of infrared light to count the cells in a sample

15.
What does the Lambert's Law state?
A. The light intensity absorbed by a material is too high
B. The concentration of the sample dictates the colour of the light
C. The concentration of the sample and path length is directly proportional to the absorbance of the light
D. The absorbance of light is directly proportional to the length of light path
Show Answer

Correct Answer: D. The absorbance of light is directly proportional to the length of light path

Explanation: Lambert's Law states that absorbance ($A$) is directly proportional to the path length ($l$) of the light through the material. Beer's Law states absorbance is proportional to concentration. Option C combines both (Beer-Lambert Law).

16.
Which of the following principle explain flame emission photometry?
A. Radiation is absorbed by non-excited atoms in vapour state...
B. Medium absorbs radiation and transmitter radiation is measured
C. Colour of the wavelength of the frame is measured
D. Only frequency of the signal is measured
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C. Colour of the wavelength of the frame is measured (Approximate)

Explanation: Flame photometry measures the intensity of light emitted (emission spectrum) when metal atoms (like Na, K) are excited by a flame and return to the ground state. The wavelength determines the element (color), and intensity determines concentration. Option A describes Atomic Absorption (AAS). C is closest to the concept of characteristic color/wavelength emission.

39.
Draw a comparison between soft and hard X-Rays.
A. Soft X-rays have high velocity while hard X-rays have low velocity
B. Soft X-rays have high intensity while hard X-rays have low intensity
C. Soft X-rays have low frequency while hard X-rays have high frequency
D. Soft X-rays have high velocity while hard X-rays have high frequency
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C. Soft X-rays have low frequency while hard X-rays have high frequency

Explanation: "Hard" X-rays have high energy, short wavelength, and high frequency (penetrating). "Soft" X-rays have lower energy, longer wavelength, and lower frequency (less penetrating). All X-rays travel at the speed of light (same velocity).

40.
When the voltage of an X-ray tube is doubled, the intensity of X-ray becomes
A. Half
B. Double
C. Unchanged
D. Triple
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B. Double (Usually $V^2$, so x4, but closest simple increase is Double)

Explanation: Theoretically, X-ray intensity $I \propto KV^2$. Doubling voltage should quadruple intensity. However, in basic physics contexts without the square option, "Double" implies a direct increase. *Note: Strictly, it is x4.*

41.

a) List any two basic principles used in cell counting. (2 marks)

b) Explain the principle of water deionizer/demineralizer. (3 marks)

Show Answer

a) Cell Counting Principles:

  1. Electrical Impedance (Coulter Principle): Cells passing through an aperture cause a resistance change proportional to cell volume.
  2. Optical Light Scatter (Flow Cytometry): Laser light scattered by cells is detected at various angles to determine size and complexity.

b) Deionizer Principle:

It works on the principle of Ion Exchange. Water passes through resin beds containing Cation and Anion exchange resins. Positive ions (Ca, Mg) are replaced by Hydrogen ions ($H^+$), and negative ions (Cl, SO4) are replaced by Hydroxyl ions ($OH^-$). The $H^+$ and $OH^-$ combine to form pure water ($H_2O$).

Explain the three types of electrodes used in biomedical measurements. (5 marks)
Show Answer
  1. Surface Electrodes: Placed on the skin surface (e.g., ECG, EMG). Non-invasive but higher impedance.
  2. Indwelling (Needle) Electrodes: Inserted into the tissue/muscle (e.g., Needle EMG). Invasive, lower impedance, specific localization.

a) Define the following terms; (4 marks)
i. Computed radiography
ii. Digital radiography

b) State one advantage of digital radiography. (1 mark)

Show Answer

a) Definitions:

  • Computed Radiography (CR): A digital imaging system that uses a photostimulable phosphor plate housed in a cassette. The plate stores the image, which is then scanned by a laser to produce the digital image.
  • Digital Radiography (DR): A system that uses a flat-panel detector (direct or indirect conversion) to capture X-rays and convert them immediately into digital signals without the need for a separate scanning step (cassette-less).

b) Advantage of DR:

Instant image viewing (improved workflow efficiency) and generally lower radiation dose compared to film/CR.

Section 3: Long Answer Question

20 Marks
49.
Explain tests carried during certification of Biosafety cabinet (BSC). (20 marks)
Show Answer

Certification of Class II Biosafety Cabinets typically involves the following primary tests (NSF/ANSI 49 Standard):

  1. Downflow Velocity Profile Test:

    Measures the speed of air moving down from the HEPA filter to the work surface. Ensures the laminar flow protects the product from contamination. Velocity typically ~0.3 m/s.

  2. Inflow Velocity Test:

    Measures the speed of air entering the front grille. Ensures containment of aerosols generated inside the cabinet to protect the operator. Velocity typically ~0.5 m/s.

  3. HEPA Filter Leak Test (Integrity Test):

    Uses an aerosol generator (e.g., PAO or DOP) and a photometer to scan the filter face and seals for leaks. Ensures filters are not damaged and seals are tight (Efficiency >99.99%).

  4. Airflow Smoke Patterns Test:

    Visualizes airflow direction using smoke.

    • Downflow: Smooth downward flow with no dead spots.
    • View Screen Retention: Smoke inside should stay inside.
    • Sash Seal: Smoke outside should flow into the grille, not escape.

  5. Site Installation Assessment Tests:

    Includes alarm function tests (sash alarm, airflow alarm), interlocks, and electrical safety (grounding, leakage).

  6. Cabinet Leak Test (Pressure Decay):

    (For Class II Type B or Class III). Pressurizing the cabinet plenum to ensure it is airtight and no contaminated air leaks out.

  7. Lighting and UV Intensity Test:

    Measuring Lux levels for work visibility and UV intensity ($\mu W/cm^2$) for decontamination effectiveness.
