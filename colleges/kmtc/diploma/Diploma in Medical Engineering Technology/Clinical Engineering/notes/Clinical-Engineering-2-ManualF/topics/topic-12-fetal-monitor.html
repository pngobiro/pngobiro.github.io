üí° Overview & Methods

A fetal monitor is a device used to evaluate the well-being of the fetus by assessing the rate and rhythm of the fetal heartbeat.

Methods of fetal heart rate monitoring

External Monitoring

Uses a device to listen to or record the fetal heartbeat through the mother's abdomen.

  • Fetoscope: Basic stethoscope (Pinard horn).
  • Doppler: Hand-held electronic ultrasound device.
  • Ultrasound Transducer: Placed on abdomen, conducts sounds to a computer/monitor.

Internal Monitoring

Uses an electronic transducer connected directly to the fetal skin.

  • Spiral/Scalp Electrode: Wire attached to fetal scalp through cervical opening.
  • Benefits: More accurate/consistent; not affected by movement.
ü©∫

Basic Monitoring Tools

Fetoscope (Pinard Horn): A hollow horn-shaped stethoscope pressed against the patient's stomach. It must be placed precisely close to the heart to hear the beat.

Fetal Doppler: A hand-held ultrasound transducer that uses sound waves (Doppler effect) to detect the heartbeat. It provides an audible simulation of the heartbeat.

üè• Clinical Applications

Need for fetal heart monitoring

  • High-risk pregnancy: Especially when the mother has diabetes or high blood pressure.
  • Preterm labor medicines: To check how medicines used to keep labor from starting too early are affecting the baby.

Specific Tests

Test Name Description
Non-stress test Measures the fetal heart rate as the baby moves.
Contraction stress test Measures fetal heart rate along with uterine contractions (started with medicine or other methods).
Biophysical profile (BPP) Combines a non-stress test with ultrasound.

Factors Affecting Accuracy

Factors affecting rate during labor

  • Uterine contractions
  • Pain-reliever medicines or anesthesia
  • Tests done during labor
  • Pushing during the second stage

Contributors to less accurate monitoring

  • Obesity of the mother
  • Position of the baby or mother
  • Too much amniotic fluid (poly-hydramnios)
  • Cervix not dilated / amniotic sac not broken (prevents internal monitoring)

Critical Contraindication

One should not have internal fetal heart rate monitoring if she is HIV positive. This is because she may pass on the infection to the baby.

üîß Maintenance & Inspection

Visual Inspection (Daily)

Prior to using the monitor, perform the following inspections:

  • Check monitor and accessories for visible damage (especially seams on transducers).
  • Check all outer cables, power socket, and power cables.
  • Check monitor function; stop use if damage is detected.
  • Ensure exposed screws are tight.
  • Check external cables for splits, cracks, or twisting. Replace if damaged.

Routine Inspection

Every 6 to 12 months: Perform safety check and function check by qualified personnel. Includes leakage current measurement and insulation testing.

Cleaning & Care Guidelines

Monitor Unit

  • Keep exterior clean, free of dust/dirt.
  • Unplug and remove battery before cleaning.
  • Recommended solutions: Mild soapy water, Ten sides, Ethylate, Acetaldehyde.
  • Clean screen with dry soft cloth; avoid scratching.

Transducers

  • Keep in dry environment (temp < $45^{\circ}\mathrm{C}$ / $113^{\circ}\mathrm{F}$).
  • Wipe gel immediately after use.
  • Avoid hard/sharp objects (protect piezoelectric crystals).
  • Do not excessively flex cables.
  • Wipe with soft cloth dampened in cleaning solution/water, then dry.
