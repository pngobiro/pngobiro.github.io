üìä Introduction & Types

Measuring patient‚Äôs weight is an important part of monitoring health as well as calculating drug and radiation doses. It is therefore vital that scales continue to operate accurately.

Weighing scales can be used for all ages of patients and therefore vary in the range of weights that they measure. These scales can be arranged for patients to stand on, or can be set up for weighing wheelchair bound patients (adults).

For infants, the patient can be suspended in a sling below the scale (spring type) or placed in a weighing cot on top of the scale (electronic type) during the weight measurement.

  • Mechanical scales: Have a spring that is deflected by patient‚Äôs (baby) weight. The spring pushes a pointer along a display or rotates a disc to indicate weight.
  • Electronic weighing scales: Have a sensor that bends under the patient‚Äôs weight and the circuitry converts this to displayed digits.

‚öôÔ∏è Principles of Operation

How Mechanical Weighing Scales Work

A hook/spring or hanging scale operates on a principle based on Hooke's Law. This law states that "the force exerted on a spring is directly proportional to the distance it moves as a result of the force."

$$ \mathbf{F} = \mathbf{k}\mathbf{X} $$

Where:

  • $\mathbf{F}$ is the amount of force applied to the spring
  • $\mathbf{X}$ is the distance traveled
  • $\mathbf{k}$ is the constant stiffness of the particular spring

The spring pushes a pointer along a display or rotates a disc to indicate weight of the patient due to the force/weight exerted by the patient (baby). Beam balances use an application of the lever principle which states that "the further away the counterweight is from the fulcrum, the more downward force it exerts."

Modern Weighing Scales (Electronic)

Majority of modern scales work by use of devices known as load cells to measure weights of objects, whether being used in medical, industrial or retail set ups.

Load Cell Principle

A load cell is a force gauge that is made up of a transducer that is used to create an electrical signal whose magnitude is in direct proportion with the force being measured.

Essentially, when any weight is placed on the scale, the load cell bends slightly; this causes the electrical signal that runs through the load cell to change. The signal change is due to the amount of electrical resistance that the bending causes to the strain gauge inside the load cell.

The signal is then read by an electronic device, often a digital weight indicator, and transformed into a digital weight value. The value is then displayed for reading in terms of weight.

Calibration Warning

Since a load cell essentially measures mass by converting the deformation caused by the load to an electronic signal, it is sensitive to accidental deformation, such as shocks or temperature changes. That's why it's essential to treat weighing instruments with care and to calibrate them frequently.

üë∂ Baby Weighing Scales

A baby weighing scale is specifically-designed to monitor the progress of a baby‚Äôs physical development. If a baby is born prematurely, it is often important to closely monitor their weight gain with a baby scale, more often than for on-time babies.

A major difference with the adult scale is that the baby cannot stand on his/her own, special constructions have been created for this. For children who can stand independently, an adult scale is used. Because babies have a lower weight and weight is an important indicator of growth (and malnutrition) baby scales must be able to measure more accurately.

Why Baby‚Äôs Weight is Measured

  • To control feeding of the baby
  • To control the weight of the baby
  • To determine growth and development of the baby
  • To monitor health conditions of the baby

Types of Baby Weighing Scales

  1. Spring Balance
  2. Beam balance
  3. Electronic baby weighing scale (Most preferred)

Requirements

  • Should be of table top type
  • Should be light and portable
  • Should be easy to clean
  • Should have zero weight adjustment facility
  • Should be of non-metallic tray
  • Its measurement of weight should not change with position of the pan

Main Parts

  • Digital display
  • Load cell (strain gauge)
  • Counter
  • Gradual scale
  • Pan

Setting of Baby Weighing Scales

  1. Should be on leveled ground/flat surface
  2. The pointer should be indicating zero before weight measurement. This is achieved by zero setting

Factors that can Cause Errors

  • Temperature
  • Moisture (humidity)
  • Electrostatic effects
  • Magnetism
  • Vibration
  • Gravitational force
  • Air

‚öñÔ∏è Balance vs. Weighing Scale

In basic terms, balances measure mass while scales measure weight.

Balance

Determines mass by balancing an unknown mass against a known mass. In modern weighing instruments, balances use a force restoration mechanism that creates a force to balance that exerted by the unknown mass.

Weighing Scale

Displays weight by measuring a deflection; force needed to deform the springs is measured and converted into weight.

üîß Maintenance & Care

General Care

  • Never apply sudden weight on the scales or sudden off load from scales.
  • Weight on the scale should not exceed its maximum weight.
  • Cleaning/removing dirt from the scales.
  • Avoid unnecessary oiling of moving parts.
  • Check all control knobs and balance positioning.
