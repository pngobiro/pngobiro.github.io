üí° Overview & Purpose

A resuscitaire is a medical device used by midwives and other health care professionals when the baby requires some additional support with their breathing after birth. In some cases, it is used for clinical emergencies such as resuscitation when the baby requires much more support than just facial oxygen.

Why help is needed

ü´Å

The First Breath

When the baby is in the womb, their lungs are filled with fluid as they don't need to use lungs for breathing (oxygen comes via the umbilical cord). When born, this fluid is pushed out. When they take their first breaths, air and oxygen inflate their lungs. Most babies need very little stimulation, but the resuscitaire assists those who struggle to make this transition.

02

‚öôÔ∏è Working Principle

A resuscitaire creates a controlled environment to give intensive care to new-born children who may experience difficulties (e.g., inadequate oxygenation). It combines an overhead heater for warming with a face mask for delivering facial oxygen, helping the baby expand their lungs and clear fluid.

Controlled Factors

  • Temperature
  • Humidity
  • Oxygen
  • Light

Included Equipment

  • Suction unit
  • Ventilator
  • Gas flow meter
  • Storage drawer
03

üõ†Ô∏è Key Functions

i) Thermo-regulation

Uses a Radiant warmer/heater positioned ~1 meter above the infant, combined with an insulating mattress.

  • Baby Mode (Servo): Uses a temperature sensor connected to the baby (liver location). Automatically maintains set temperature range.
  • Manual Mode: Heater operates with constant output.
‚ö†Ô∏è

Safety Warning

Manual mode is used if the temperature sensor is broken or lost. This mode is more dangerous as the baby can be overheated if not monitored closely.

ii) Ventilation

Delivers positive pressure ventilation to the baby's lungs. The ventilator ensures air pressure is as low as possible to get the required volume into the lungs, adapting to the baby's continuously changing lung compliance.

iii) Lighting

Built-in exam lights (incandescent, mini-spot, or Halogen) provide illumination for procedures.

04

üß© Main Parts & Inputs/Outputs

Main Parts

  1. Controller (Control panel): Interface to adjust settings and monitor vital signs.
  2. Gas supply module: Provides oxygen supply.
  3. Ventilation Unit (Blender): Delivers positive pressure ventilation.
  4. Temperature control Unit (Warmer head): Maintains body temperature.
  5. Monitoring Unit: Displays vital signs (heart rate, O2 saturation, respiratory rate).
  6. Bassinet/Mattress: Platform for the infant.
  7. Storage: For resuscitation and suction equipment.

Inputs

  • Power
  • Oxygen
  • Air
  • Water

Outputs

  • Oxygen under pressure (Ventilation)
  • Heat (Temperature control)
  • Suctions
  • Alarms
  • Light

üîß Common Faults & Maintenance

Troubleshooting

Fault Effect Remedy
Oxygen supply issues Blender fails to deliver correct oxygen amount/pressure. Check oxygen source and ensure proper connection.
Suction blockage Inability to clear airway effectively. Check suction catheter for blockage; clear if necessary.
Malfunctioning warmers Failure to maintain body temperature. Check temperature sensor and ensure proper functioning.
Phototherapy issues Ineffective jaundice treatment. Check light source function.

Maintenance Checklist

  • Check the oxygen supply and make sure it is connected properly.
  • Check the suction catheter for blockage and clear them if necessary.
  • Check the temperature sensor and ensure its functioning properly.
  • Check the light source of the phototherapy unit and examination light making sure that they function properly.
