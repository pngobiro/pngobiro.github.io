Section 1: Multiple Choice Questions

20 Marks
1.
Transistor as a switch operates at
a) Cut off region only
b) Saturation region only
c) Between cut off and saturation region
d) Active region only
Show Answer

Correct Answer: Cut-off and Saturation regions

Note: The options provided in the exam text are slightly ambiguous. Ideally, a switch toggles between two states.

When used as a switch, a transistor operates in the Cut-off region (Open switch/OFF state) and the Saturation region (Closed switch/ON state). It avoids the active region to minimize power dissipation.

2.
An SCR is turned off by
a) Interrupting its anode current
b) Reversing polarity of its anode current
c) Reducing anode current
d) All of the above
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) All of the above

To turn off an SCR (Commutation), the anode current must be reduced below the Holding Current ($I_H$). This can be achieved by interrupting the current, reversing the polarity (AC line commutation), or diverting the current.

3.
An LED is basically a ____ PN Junction
a) Forward biased
b) Reversed biased
c) Lightly doped
d) Heavily doped
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Forward biased

Light Emitting Diodes emit light via electroluminescence when electrons recombine with holes, which occurs when the junction is Forward Biased.

4.
In a photoconductive cell the resistance of the semiconductor materials varies ____ with the intensity of incident current.
a) Directly
b) Inversely
c) Exponentially
d) Logarithmically
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Inversely

As light intensity increases, the number of free charge carriers increases, causing conductivity to increase and resistance to decrease. Thus, resistance is inversely proportional to light intensity.

5.
The following are causes of signal loss in optical fiber except
a) Bend loss
b) Absorption loss
c) Reflection loss
d) Rayleigh scatter
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Reflection loss (in context of propagation mechanism)

Rayleigh scattering, Absorption, and Bend loss are the three intrinsic mechanisms of attenuation in fibers. While Fresnel reflection loss occurs at connectors, "Reflection" (Total Internal Reflection) is the mechanism of propagation, not a loss mechanism. However, if referring to Fresnel reflection, it is a loss. Typically, in this context, the exam often considers standard attenuation factors vs mechanisms.

6.
Multimode step index fiber has
a) Large core diameter and large numerical aperture
b) Large core diameter and small numerical aperture
c) Small core diameter and large numerical aperture
d) Small core diameter and small numerical aperture
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Large core diameter and large numerical aperture

Multimode step index fibers typically have a large core (50-200 microns) to facilitate light coupling and a relatively large Numerical Aperture (NA) to accept light from a wider angle.

8.
In LEDs, light is emitted because
a) Recombination of charge carriers takes place
b) Diode gets heated up
c) Light falling on the diodes gets amplified
d) Light gets reflected due to lens action
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Recombination of charge carriers takes place

When an electron (from n-type) recombines with a hole (from p-type), energy is released in the form of photons (light) in direct bandgap semiconductors.

9.
The di/dt effect in an SCR leads to the formation of
a) Local hot spots
b) Conduction zone
c) Charge spreading zone
d) None of the above
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Local hot spots

A high rate of rise of anode current (di/dt) during turn-on can cause current to concentrate in a small area near the gate connection before the conduction spreads across the entire junction. This high current density creates local hot spots that can damage the device.

10.
The frequency of oscillation of an elementary LC oscillatory circuit depends on
a) Coil resistance
b) Coil inductance
c) Coil inductance and resistance
d) Coil inductance and capacitance
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Coil inductance and capacitance

The resonant frequency is determined by $f = \frac{1}{2\pi\sqrt{LC}}$.

11.
The light is propagated within the fiber core by the phenomenon of

14.
A step index fiber has specified parameters for refractive index of fiber core and cladding as 1.50 and 1.46 respectively. Its numerical aperture is
a) 0.344
b) 0.04
c) 0.486
d) 0.244
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) 0.344

$NA = \sqrt{1.50^2 - 1.46^2} = \sqrt{2.25 - 2.1316} = \sqrt{0.1184} \approx 0.344$.

In spontaneous emission, the light source in an excited state undergoes the transition to a state with
a) Higher energy
b) Moderated energy
c) Lower energy
d) All of the above
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Lower energy

An atom releases energy in the form of a photon when it decays from an excited (high energy) state to a lower energy state (ground state).

16.
When a UJT is turned ON, the resistance between emitter terminal and lower base terminal
a) Remains the same
b) Is decreased
c) Is increased
d) Is fluctuating
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Is decreased

When the UJT fires, charge carriers are injected into the base region, significantly increasing conductivity (Negative Resistance phenomenon), which means resistance $R_{B1}$ decreases drastically.

a) By magnetic induction
b) By a tickler coil
c) From the center of split capacitors

18.
The current produced by the photovoltaic cell can be given by
a) $I_L - I_D + I_{sh}$
b) $I_L - I_D - I_{sh}$
c) $I_L + I_D + I_{sh}$
d) $I_L - I_D \cdot I_{sh}$
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) $I_L - I_D - I_{sh}$

The output current $I$ is the light-generated current ($I_L$ or $I_{ph}$) minus the diode dark current ($I_D$) and the shunt leakage current ($I_{sh}$).

Formula: $I = I_{ph} - I_0(e^{V/nV_T} - 1) - \frac{V+IR_s}{R_{sh}}$.

19.
A triac is equivalent to two SCRs
a) In parallel
b) In series
c) In inverse parallel
d) In inverse series
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) In inverse parallel

A TRIAC functions as two thyristors (SCRs) connected back-to-back (inverse parallel) with a common gate, allowing it to conduct in both directions.

20.
The two output waves of astable multivibrator are phase ____ degrees out of
a) 90°
b) 180°
c) 45°
d) 270°
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) 180°

An astable multivibrator has two outputs (Q and $\bar{Q}$) taken from the collectors of the two transistors. When one is High (cut-off), the other is Low (saturation), making them 180 degrees out of phase.

Section 2: Short Answer Questions

40 Marks
1.
State two advantages of electronic switches and three disadvantages of mechanical switches.
Show Answer

Answer:

Advantages of Electronic Switches:

  • Faster switching speed (microseconds/nanoseconds vs milliseconds).
  • Longer lifespan (no moving parts to wear out).
  • No contact bounce.
  • Small size and silent operation.

Disadvantages of Mechanical Switches:

  • Contact bounce (creates noise).
  • Mechanical wear and tear (limited life cycles).
  • Slower switching speed.
  • Susceptible to oxidation and dirt on contacts.
2.
Explain briefly the following terminologies used in transistor switch response time: (i) Time delay, (ii) Rise time, (iii) Turn on time.
Show Answer

Answer:

  • (i) Time Delay ($t_d$): The time interval between the application of the input pulse and the time when the collector current rises to 10% of its maximum value. It represents the time required to charge the base-emitter junction capacitance.
  • (ii) Rise Time ($t_r$): The time taken for the collector current to rise from 10% to 90% of its maximum saturation value.
  • (iii) Turn-on Time ($t_{on}$): The sum of the delay time and the rise time ($t_{on} = t_d + t_r$). It is the total time required for the transistor to switch from the OFF state to the ON state.
3.
In a Colpitts oscillator, $L=40 \text{mH}$, $R_1=R_2=10k\Omega$, $C_1=C_2=0.01\mu F$. Calculate the frequency of oscillation of the circuit.
Show Answer

Answer:

For a Colpitts oscillator, the equivalent capacitance $C_{eq}$ is the series combination of $C_1$ and $C_2$.

$$C_{eq} = \frac{C_1 C_2}{C_1 + C_2} = \frac{0.01 \times 0.01}{0.01 + 0.01} = \frac{0.0001}{0.02} = 0.005 \mu F$$

Frequency formula: $$f = \frac{1}{2\pi\sqrt{LC_{eq}}}$$

Substitute values ($L = 40 \times 10^{-3} H$, $C = 5 \times 10^{-9} F$):

$$f = \frac{1}{2\pi\sqrt{40 \times 10^{-3} \times 5 \times 10^{-9}}}$$

$$f = \frac{1}{2\pi\sqrt{200 \times 10^{-12}}} = \frac{1}{2\pi\sqrt{2 \times 10^{-10}}}$$

$$f = \frac{1}{2\pi \times 1.414 \times 10^{-5}} \approx \frac{1}{8.88 \times 10^{-5}} \approx 11.25 \text{ kHz}$$

4.
If $\eta=0.8$, $V_{BB}=10.3 V$ and $R_{B2}=5 k\Omega$. Determine $R_{B1}$ for Unijunction transistor. Assume $V_D=0.7V$.
Show Answer

Answer:

The intrinsic standoff ratio $\eta$ is given by:

$$\eta = \frac{R_{B1}}{R_{B1} + R_{B2}}$$

Given $\eta = 0.8$ and $R_{B2} = 5 k\Omega$:

$$0.8 = \frac{R_{B1}}{R_{B1} + 5}$$

$$0.8(R_{B1} + 5) = R_{B1}$$

$$0.8 R_{B1} + 4 = R_{B1}$$

$$4 = R_{B1} - 0.8 R_{B1}$$

$$4 = 0.2 R_{B1}$$

$$R_{B1} = \frac{4}{0.2} = 20 k\Omega$$

5.

a) State three factors which causes losses in a fiberoptic.

b) State two methods used for turning the silicon controlled rectifier off.

Show Answer

Answer:

(a) Causes of Fiber Losses (Attenuation):

  • Absorption: Caused by impurities (like water ions/OH-) in the glass absorbing light energy.
  • Scattering (Rayleigh Scattering): Caused by microscopic density fluctuations in the glass material.
  • Bending Losses: Macrobending (tight loops) and microbending causing light to escape the core.

(b) SCR Turn-off Methods (Commutation):

  • Natural Commutation: In AC circuits, the SCR turns off when the AC source voltage reverses polarity (zero crossing).
  • Forced Commutation: In DC circuits, using external circuitry (capacitors/inductors) to momentarily force the anode current to zero or reverse the voltage across the SCR.
6.

a) Highlight three main components of any laser device.

b) Distinguish between latching and holding currents as used in SCR.

Show Answer

Answer:

(a) Main Components of a Laser:

  1. Active Medium (Gain Medium): Material (gas, crystal, semiconductor) that amplifies light via stimulated emission.
  2. Energy Source (Pump): Provides energy to excite atoms in the active medium (e.g., flash lamp, electrical current).
  3. Optical Resonator (Cavity): Pair of mirrors (one fully reflective, one partially reflective) to reflect light back and forth for amplification.

(b) Latching vs Holding Current:

  • Latching Current ($I_L$): The minimum anode current required to turn on and maintain the SCR in conduction immediately after triggering when the gate signal is removed. It is associated with the turn-on process.
  • Holding Current ($I_H$): The minimum anode current required to keep the SCR conducting. If current drops below $I_H$, the SCR turns off. It is associated with the turn-off process. Generally, $I_L > I_H$.

Answer:

A photovoltaic cell (solar cell) typically consists of:

  • Top Layer (n-type silicon): Thin and heavily doped to allow light penetration.
  • P-N Junction: Where the electric field separates electron-hole pairs generated by light.
  • Bottom Layer (p-type silicon): Thicker base layer.

8.
Draw an opto-coupler that combines a photodiode and an LED and explain how it functions.
Show Answer

Answer:

Construction: An opto-coupler consists of an LED (Light Emitting Diode) as the transmitter and a Photodiode (or Phototransistor) as the receiver, housed in a light-tight package.

Operation:

  1. When an electrical signal is applied to the input (LED), it emits infrared light.
  2. The light travels across a small gap (transparent insulation) and strikes the photodiode.
  3. The photodiode generates a photocurrent (or conducts) in proportion to the light intensity.
  4. This converts the optical signal back into an electrical signal at the output.

Function: It allows signal transmission while maintaining electrical isolation (no direct electrical connection) between the input and output circuits, protecting the low-voltage input side from high voltages on the output side.

Section 3: Long Answer Questions

40 Marks
1.

a) Draw a circuit diagram of a relaxation oscillator utilizing a UJT and describe its operation.

b) A ray of light is travelling in a transparent material of refractive index 1.51 and approaches a second material of refractive index 1.46. Calculate the critical angle.

c) State three advantages and two disadvantages of using optical fibers.

Show Answer

Answer:

(a) UJT Relaxation Oscillator

Circuit: Requires a UJT, a capacitor $C_E$ connected to the Emitter, a resistor $R_E$ charging the capacitor from $V_{BB}$, and base resistors $R_{B1}$ and $R_{B2}$.

Operation:

  1. When power is applied, capacitor $C_E$ charges through resistor $R_E$. The voltage across the capacitor ($V_E$) increases exponentially.
  2. The UJT is initially OFF (high resistance).
  3. When $V_E$ reaches the Peak Point Voltage ($V_P$), the UJT fires (turns ON). The resistance between Emitter and Base 1 drops drastically.
  4. The capacitor discharges rapidly through the UJT and $R_{B1}$, creating a sharp pulse across $R_{B1}$.
  5. When $V_E$ drops below the Valley Point ($V_V$), the UJT turns OFF.
  6. The cycle repeats, generating a sawtooth waveform at the emitter and pulses at Base 1.

(b) Critical Angle Calculation

Formula: $\sin(\theta_c) = \frac{n_2}{n_1}$ where $n_1 > n_2$.

Given: $n_1 = 1.51$ (core/denser), $n_2 = 1.46$ (cladding/less dense).

$\sin(\theta_c) = \frac{1.46}{1.51} = 0.9669$

$\theta_c = \sin^{-1}(0.9669) \approx 75.2^\circ$.

(c) Optical Fiber Pros and Cons

Advantages:

  1. Huge Bandwidth: Can carry vast amounts of data.
  2. Immunity to EMI/RFI: Not affected by electromagnetic interference.
  3. Low Attenuation: Signals travel long distances without regeneration.
  4. Security: Difficult to tap into without detection.

Disadvantages:

  1. Fragility: Glass fibers are brittle and can break if bent too sharply.
  2. Cost of Installation: Splicing and connecting requires specialized equipment and skilled personnel.
2.

a) Draw and explain the I-V characteristics of a unijunction transistor.

b) With the aid of a circuit diagram of a Colpitts oscillator, explain its operation.

c) Given that $C_1=0.018\mu F$, $C_2=0.16\mu F$ and $L=15.9 mH$ (Assumed mH) of a Colpitts oscillator, determine the frequency of oscillation.

Show Answer

Answer:

(a) UJT I-V Characteristics

The curve plots Emitter Current ($I_E$) vs Emitter Voltage ($V_E$).

  • Cut-off Region: $V_E < V_P$. Only small leakage current flows. UJT is OFF.
  • Peak Point ($V_P$): The voltage where the UJT fires.
  • Negative Resistance Region: As $I_E$ increases, $V_E$ decreases drastically. This is the unstable region used for oscillation.
  • Valley Point ($V_V$): Minimum voltage/current to sustain conduction.
  • Saturation Region: Beyond $V_V$, $V_E$ increases slowly with $I_E$ (like a standard diode).

(b) Colpitts Oscillator Operation

Circuit: Uses a common-emitter amplifier with a tank circuit consisting of an inductor $L$ in parallel with two series capacitors $C_1$ and $C_2$. Feedback is taken from the junction of $C_1$ and $C_2$.

Operation:

  1. When power is ON, capacitors charge and discharge through the inductor, setting up oscillations in the tank circuit.
  2. A portion of the voltage across $C_2$ is fed back to the emitter (or base) of the transistor.
  3. The capacitors provide a 180° phase shift (voltage divider action relative to ground). The transistor amplifier provides another 180° phase shift.
  4. Total phase shift = 360° ($0^\circ$), satisfying the Barkhausen criterion for sustained oscillations.

(c) Frequency Calculation

Assumption: L is likely 15.9 mH. 15.9 H is unrealistically large for this context.

Calculate $C_{eq}$:

$$C_{eq} = \frac{C_1 C_2}{C_1 + C_2} = \frac{0.018 \times 0.16}{0.018 + 0.16} = \frac{0.00288}{0.178} \approx 0.01618 \mu F$$

Convert units: $L = 15.9 \times 10^{-3} H$, $C = 16.18 \times 10^{-9} F$.

$$f = \frac{1}{2\pi\sqrt{LC}} = \frac{1}{2\pi\sqrt{15.9 \times 10^{-3} \times 16.18 \times 10^{-9}}}$$

$$f = \frac{1}{2\pi\sqrt{257.26 \times 10^{-12}}} = \frac{1}{2\pi \times 16.04 \times 10^{-6}}$$

$$f \approx \frac{1}{100.7 \times 10^{-6}} \approx 9930 \text{ Hz} \approx 9.93 \text{ kHz}$$
