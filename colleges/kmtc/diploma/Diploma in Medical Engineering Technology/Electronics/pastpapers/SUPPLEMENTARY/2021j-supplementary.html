Section 1: Multiple Choice Questions

20 Marks
1.
Cathode ray oscilloscope is
a) An ohmmeter
b) An ammeter
c) A voltmeter
d) A multimeter
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) A voltmeter

While a CRO displays waveforms (Voltage vs Time), its primary measurement capability regarding the vertical axis is Voltage. It acts as a high-speed voltmeter.

2.
Electron beam is deflected in
a) 1 direction
b) 4 directions
c) 3 directions
d) 2 directions
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) 2 directions

The electron beam in a CRO is deflected in two orthogonal directions: Horizontally (X-axis, usually Time base) and Vertically (Y-axis, Signal amplitude).

3.
In an unregulated power supply, if input ac voltage increases, the output voltage
a) Increases
b) Decreases
c) Remains the same
d) Depends on the amount of current
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Increases

In an unregulated supply, the output DC voltage is directly proportional to the input AC peak voltage. If input rises, output rises.

4.
A zener diode is used as
a) Series voltage regulating device
b) Shunt voltage regulating device
c) Series-shunt voltage regulating device
d) Shunt-series voltage regulating device
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Shunt voltage regulating device

A Zener diode is connected in parallel (shunt) with the load to maintain a constant voltage across it.

5.
In amplifier circuit, biasing of transistor is necessary to
a) Fix the value of current amplification
b) Establish suitable D.C working conditions
c) Ensure the transmitter is saturated
d) Ensure the transistor is cut-off
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Establish suitable D.C working conditions

Biasing sets the Q-point (Operating point) in the active region to ensure faithful amplification without distortion.

6.
The configuration in which current gain of transistor is lowest is
a) Common collector
b) Common emitter
c) Common base
d) Common collector and common base
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Common base

In Common Base configuration, the current gain ($\alpha$) is less than 1 (typically 0.95-0.99), which is the lowest among the three configurations.

7.
[Reconstructed from fragment] The inputs of an Op-Amp are known as?
a) High and Low
b) Positive and Negative
c) Inverting and Non-inverting
d) Active and Passive
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Inverting and Non-inverting

Based on standard electronics questions matching the OCR fragment "Inverting and Non-inverting". An Op-Amp has two inputs: Inverting (-) and Non-inverting (+).

8.
[Reconstructed] An oscillator produces?
a) Damped oscillations
b) Undamped oscillations
c) Damped and undamped
d) None
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Undamped oscillations

A practical oscillator circuit is designed to produce sustained (undamped) oscillations by using positive feedback to overcome losses.
