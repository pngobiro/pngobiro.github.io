Section I: Multiple Choice Questions

1.
The light sources used in fibre optic communication are.
A. LEDs
B. semiconductor lasers
C. phototransistors
D. both a and b
Show Answer

Correct Answer: D. both a and b

Fiber optic transmitters typically use LEDs (Light Emitting Diodes) for short distances and lower speeds, and Semiconductor Lasers (Laser Diodes) for long distances and high speeds.

2.
Acceptance angle is defined as the ____ angle of incidence at the end face of an optical fibre, for which the ray can be propagated in the optical fibre is.
A. maximum
B. minimum
C. Either a or b
D. none of the above
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A. maximum

The acceptance angle is the maximum angle relative to the fiber axis at which light can enter the fiber and still be guided by total internal reflection.

3.
In multimode graded index fibre, light rays travel ____ in different parts of the fibre.
A. at different speeds
B. with same speed
C. both a and b
D. none of the above
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A. at different speeds

In graded-index fiber, the refractive index decreases from the center to the cladding. Light travels faster in the lower-index outer regions and slower in the higher-index center. This varying speed compensates for different path lengths, reducing modal dispersion.

4.
The working of Laser is based on the principle of
A. induced absorption
B. spontaneous emission
C. ionization
D. stimulated emission
Show Answer

Correct Answer: D. stimulated emission

LASER stands for Light Amplification by Stimulated Emission of Radiation.

5.
The purpose of the optical resonator in a laser is
A. to make it monochromatic
B. to enhance energy density of radiation
C. to make it coherent
D. to achieve population inversion
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C. to make it coherent

The optical resonator (cavity mirrors) provides feedback, reflecting photons back and forth through the gain medium to stimulate more emission in the same phase and direction, establishing spatial and temporal coherence.

6.
An SCR behaves as a ____ switch
A. Unidirectional
B. Bidirectional
C. Mechanical
D. None of the above
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A. Unidirectional

An SCR (Silicon Controlled Rectifier) allows current to flow in only one direction (Anode to Cathode), acting as a unidirectional switch.

Correct Answer: C. Appropriate gate current

Applying a positive gate current pulse is the standard and controlled method to trigger an SCR into conduction.

Section II: Theory Questions

1.
Explain how a voltage doubler works aided with a diagram.
Show Answer

Answer:

Half-Wave Voltage Doubler Operation:

  • Components: Transformer, Capacitor C1, Diode D1, Capacitor C2, Diode D2.
  • Negative Half Cycle: D1 is forward biased and charges C1 to the peak voltage $V_m$ with polarity + on the right. D2 is reverse biased.
  • Positive Half Cycle: Input voltage is $+V_m$. Since C1 is charged to $V_m$, the total voltage appearing across D2 and C2 is $V_{in} + V_{C1} = V_m + V_m = 2V_m$. D2 conducts and charges C2 to $2V_m$.
  • Output: Taken across C2, providing a DC voltage roughly equal to $2V_{peak}$.
2.

A relay is controlled by a photo-conductive cell which has resistance of $100 \mathrm{k} \Omega$ (corrected from text inconsistency) when in the dark and $1 \mathrm{k} \Omega$ when is illuminated. The relay is supplied with 10 mA from a 30 V supply when the cell is illuminated and is required to be de-energized when the cell is in the dark. Sketch a suitable circuit and calculate the required series resistance and value of dark current.

Show Answer

Answer:

Circuit: Series circuit with Voltage Source (30V), Series Resistor (R), Relay, and LDR.

Given:

  • $V_S = 30V$
  • $I_{light} = 10 \text{ mA} = 0.01 \text{ A}$
  • $R_{LDR(light)} = 1 k\Omega = 1000 \Omega$
  • $R_{LDR(dark)} = 100 k\Omega$ (or $400 k\Omega$ as per different text versions, let's use 100k for calculation).

Calculate Series Resistance (R):

Assuming the relay resistance is negligible or part of the calculation, but typically we limit current. If the 10mA flows through the total circuit:

$I = \frac{V}{R_{total}} \Rightarrow R_{total} = \frac{30V}{0.01A} = 3000 \Omega$

$R_{total} = R + R_{relay} + R_{LDR(light)}$

Assuming Relay is ideal/load is just current requirement:

$R + 1000 = 3000 \Rightarrow R = 2000 \Omega = 2 k\Omega$.

Calculate Dark Current ($I_{dark}$):

$R_{total(dark)} = R + R_{LDR(dark)} = 2000 + 100,000 = 102,000 \Omega$.

$I_{dark} = \frac{30V}{102,000 \Omega} \approx 0.29 \text{ mA}$.

This is much less than 10mA, so the relay de-energizes.

3.
State any five advantages of optical fiber technology.
Show Answer

Answer:

  1. Extremely High Bandwidth: Can carry vast amounts of data.
  2. Low Attenuation: Signals travel long distances without regeneration.
  3. Electromagnetic Immunity: Immune to EMI, RFI, and EMP pulses.
  4. Electrical Isolation: No ground loops or sparking hazards (safe in explosive environments).
  5. Security: Difficult to tap without detection; no radiated signals.
  6. Lightweight and Small Size: Compared to copper cables of equivalent capacity.
4.
With aid of a diagram explain the construction and working of a Monostable multivibrator.
Show Answer

Answer:

Construction: Two transistors (Q1, Q2). Q2 is coupled to Q1 via a resistor (DC coupling), while Q1 is coupled to Q2 via a capacitor (AC coupling). Q2 is normally biased ON, Q1 is normally OFF.

Working:

  • Stable State: Q2 is ON (saturated), holding Q1 OFF via common emitter or feedback. Capacitor charges.
  • Triggering: A trigger pulse (negative to Q2 base or positive to Q1 base) turns Q1 ON and Q2 OFF. This is the Quasi-stable state.
  • Timing: The capacitor discharges through the base resistor of Q2. Q2 remains OFF until the capacitor voltage rises enough to turn Q2 back ON.
  • Return: Once Q2 turns ON, it quickly turns Q1 OFF, returning the circuit to the stable state.
  • Result: Produces a single output pulse of fixed duration ($T \approx 0.69 RC$) for each trigger.
5.
Define the following terms: (I) Time delay, (II) Rise time, (III) Holding current, (IV) Forward break over voltage.
Show Answer

Answer:

  • (I) Time Delay ($t_d$): The time interval between the application of the input pulse and the output signal reaching 10% of its final value.
  • (II) Rise Time ($t_r$): The time taken for the signal to rise from 10% to 90% of its maximum amplitude.
  • (III) Holding Current ($I_H$): The minimum anode current required to maintain an SCR in the conducting (ON) state. If current falls below this, the SCR turns OFF.
  • (IV) Forward Breakover Voltage ($V_{BO}$): The voltage at which an SCR enters conduction (breaks down) with the gate open ($I_G=0$).
