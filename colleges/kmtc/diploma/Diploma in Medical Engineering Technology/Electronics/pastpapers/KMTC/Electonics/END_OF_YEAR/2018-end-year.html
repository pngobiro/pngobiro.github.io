Section A: Multiple Choice Questions

20 Marks
1.
When a transistor is driven to cut-off by an input, the collector voltage is equal to
a) zero
b) $V_{CC}$
c) The input of the transistor
d) The output current of the transistor
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) $V_{CC}$

In the cut-off region, the transistor is OFF (acts as an open switch). Collector current $I_C \approx 0$. Therefore, voltage drop across the collector resistor is 0, making $V_C = V_{CC} - I_C R_C \approx V_{CC}$.

2.
The flip flop is also called
a) Binary circuits
b) Multi vibrator
c) Memory device
d) Non sinusoidal oscillator
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Multi vibrator (Bistable)

A flip-flop is formally known as a Bistable Multivibrator. It is also a memory device (1-bit), but "Multivibrator" is the circuit class.

3.
The intrinsic voltage stand- off ratio of unijunction transistor equals to
a) $RB_{1} / (RB_{1}+RB_{2})$
b) $RB_{2} / (RB_{1}+RB_{2})$
c) $RB_{1}(RB_{1}+RB_{2})$
d) $RB_{2}(RB_{1}+RB_{2})$
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) $RB_{1} / (RB_{1}+RB_{2})$

The intrinsic standoff ratio ($\eta$) is the ratio of the lower base resistance ($R_{B1}$) to the total interbase resistance ($R_{BB} = R_{B1} + R_{B2}$).

4.
The maximum conduction angle of silicon controlled rectifier is
a) 45
b) 90
c) 180
d) 360
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) 180

An SCR acts as a rectifier (diode). In a simple AC circuit, it can only conduct during the positive half-cycle. Therefore, the maximum conduction angle is 180 degrees.

5.
One method of switching off a triac is to
a) Increase supply voltage
b) Reduce gate current
c) Change voltage polarity
d) Reduce current impedance
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Change voltage polarity (or Reduce Main Terminal Current)

Like an SCR, a TRIAC turns off when the main current drops below the holding current. In AC circuits, this happens naturally at the zero-crossing when the voltage polarity changes.

6.
In photo conductive cell the resistance of the semiconductor materials varies .... ____
a) Directly
b) Inversely
c) Exponentially
d) logarithmically
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Inversely

Higher light intensity creates more charge carriers, increasing conductivity and thus decreasing resistance.

7.
A LED is basically a ................... PN junction
a) Forward biased
b) Reverse biased
c) Lightly doped
d) Heavily doped
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Forward biased

LEDs emit light due to recombination of electrons and holes, which occurs when current flows in forward bias.

8.
The following are causes of signal loss in optic fibre except
a) Bend loss
b) Absorption loss
c) Reflection loss
d) Rayceigh scatter (Rayleigh)
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Reflection loss (Contextual)

While Fresnel reflection at connectors is a loss, "Reflection" (Total Internal Reflection) is the mechanism of propagation. Absorption, Bending, and Rayleigh scattering are the three primary intrinsic loss mechanisms.

9.
The minimum value of current required to maintain conduction in SCR is called ____ current
a) commutation
b) Holding
c) Gate trigger
d) Break over
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Holding

Holding current ($I_H$) is the minimum anode current to maintain the ON state.

10.
The unique characteristics of laser light are that it is
a) Coherent
b) Monochromatic
c) Collimated
d) All of the above
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) All of the above

Laser light is coherent, monochromatic, and collimated (directional).

Section B: Essay Questions

1.

a) Draw and explain the I-V characteristics of a unijunction transistor (UJT).

b) If $\eta=0.8$, $V_P=10.3 \text{ V}$ and $R_{B2}=5 \text{ k}\Omega$, determine $R_{B1}$ (denoted as $R_1$) and $V_{BB}$ for UJT.

c) State any four applications of multivibrators.

Show Answer

Answer:

(a) UJT I-V Characteristics:

The graph plots Emitter Voltage ($V_E$) versus Emitter Current ($I_E$).

  • Cut-off Region: $V_E < V_P$ (Peak Voltage). The emitter junction is reverse biased. Only leakage current flows. UJT is OFF.
  • Negative Resistance Region: When $V_E > V_P$, the emitter junction becomes forward biased. Holes are injected into the N-region, reducing the resistance of $R_{B1}$. As $I_E$ increases, $V_E$ decreases. This is the switching region.
  • Saturation Region: Beyond the Valley Point ($V_V$), the device behaves like a saturated diode. $V_E$ increases slowly with $I_E$.

(b) Calculations:

Given: $\eta=0.8, V_P=10.3V, R_{B2}=5k\Omega$. Assume $V_D \approx 0.7V$.

Step 1: Find $V_{BB}$

$$V_P = \eta V_{BB} + V_D$$

$$10.3 = 0.8 V_{BB} + 0.7$$

$$9.6 = 0.8 V_{BB} \Rightarrow V_{BB} = 12 \text{ V}$$

Step 2: Find $R_{B1}$

$$\eta = \frac{R_{B1}}{R_{B1} + R_{B2}}$$

$$0.8 = \frac{R_{B1}}{R_{B1} + 5}$$

$$0.8 R_{B1} + 4 = R_{B1}$$

$$4 = 0.2 R_{B1} \Rightarrow R_{B1} = 20 \text{ k}\Omega$$

(c) Applications of Multivibrators:

  1. Square wave generation (Astable).
  2. Frequency Division (Bistable/Flip-flops).
  3. Memory/Storage Elements (Bistable/Registers).
  4. Pulse Width Modulation / Timing (Monostable).
  5. Counting pulses (Counters).
