Section 1: Multiple Choice Questions

20 Marks
1.
A photodiode is normally
a) forward biased
b) reverse biased
c) neither forward biased nor reverse biased
d) emitting light
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) reverse biased

Photodiodes are designed to operate in reverse bias. This increases the width of the depletion region, making the device more sensitive to light and reducing junction capacitance for higher speed.

2.
____ is the angle within which the light is launched into the core of the fibre cable
a) incident angle
b) refraction angle
c) critical angle
d) acceptance angle
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) acceptance angle

The acceptance angle is the maximum angle of incidence (relative to the fiber axis) at the input face of the fiber for which light will be guided/propagated through the core by total internal reflection.

3.
An astable multivibrator has
a) no stable state
b) one stable state
c) two stable states
d) none of the above
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) no stable state

An astable multivibrator has two quasi-stable states and switches continuously between them without any external trigger, producing an oscillating output.

4.
For total internal reflection to happen in a fibre-optic cable, the critical angle should be
a) less than right angle
b) equal to right angle
c) greater than the right angle
d) none of the above
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) less than right angle (Interpretation required)

Clarification: For TIR to occur, the angle of incidence must be greater than the critical angle. The critical angle itself is determined by refractive indices ($\theta_c = \arcsin(n_2/n_1)$) and is always less than 90 degrees (right angle). The question phrasing is slightly ambiguous, but typically asks about the incidence angle relation. Assuming it asks about the value of the critical angle itself, it is less than 90.

5.
The multivibrator which generates square wave of its own is the ____ multivibrator
a) monostable
b) bistable
c) Astable
d) none of the above
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Astable

The Astable multivibrator is also known as a free-running multivibrator because it generates a continuous square wave output without external triggers.

6.
An SCR is turned off by ____
a) reducing gate voltage to zero
b) reducing anode voltage to zero
c) reverse biasing the gate
d) none of the above
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) reducing anode voltage to zero

To turn off an SCR (Commutation), the anode current must drop below the holding current. This is achieved by reducing the anode voltage to zero or making it negative (reverse biasing the anode-cathode).

7.
A UJT has how many pn junctions?
a) two
b) one
c) three
d) four
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) one

A Unijunction Transistor (UJT) consists of a bar of N-type silicon with a P-type emitter doped into it, creating a single PN junction.

8.
To turn on UJT, the forward bias on the emitter diode should be voltage ____ the peak point
a) less than
b) equal to
c) more than
d) none of the above
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) more than

The UJT fires (turns on) when the Emitter voltage ($V_E$) exceeds the Peak Point Voltage ($V_P$).

9.
When the light increases, the reverse current in a photo-diode ____
a) increases
b) decreases
c) is unaffected
d) none of the above
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) increases

The reverse current (photocurrent) in a photodiode is directly proportional to the intensity of incident light. More light creates more electron-hole pairs, increasing the current.

10.
Diacs are primarily used as
a) pulse generators
b) triggering devices
c) surge protection devices
d) power thyristors
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) triggering devices

A DIAC is mainly used to trigger TRIACs in AC control circuits (like dimmers) because it breaks down symmetrically in both directions, providing a sharp trigger pulse.

A UJT has $R_{BB}=10K$ and $R_{B2}=4K$. Its intrinsic stand-off ratio is ____________.

Section 3: Long Answer Questions

60 Marks
1.

A 555 timer is connected as shown below (refer to circuit diagram).

a) Explain the operation of the circuit (begin with PIN 3 output being LOW).

b) Determine the time duration the LED remains ON.

c) Determine the time duration the LED remains OFF.

d) Calculate the frequency of the 555-timer output waveform.

e) Duty cycle of the 555-timer output waveform.

f) State what should be done to make the duty cycle 50%.

Show Answer

Answer:

Assumptions for generic Astable 555: $R_A, R_B, C$. Charging through $R_A+R_B$, discharging through $R_B$. LED connected to output.

(a) Operation:

  • Start (Output Low): The discharge transistor (Pin 7) is ON. Capacitor $C$ discharges through $R_B$ to pin 7. Voltage across $C$ drops.
  • Trigger: When capacitor voltage drops to $1/3 V_{CC}$, the Trigger comparator (Pin 2) sets the internal Flip-Flop.
  • Charging (Output High): Output (Pin 3) goes High. Pin 7 goes OFF (Open). Capacitor $C$ charges through $R_A + R_B$.
  • Reset: When capacitor voltage reaches $2/3 V_{CC}$, the Threshold comparator (Pin 6) resets the Flip-Flop. Output goes Low, and discharge begins again.

(b) Time ON ($T_{HIGH}$):

Capacitor charges through $R_A + R_B$.

$T_{ON} = 0.693 (R_A + R_B) C$

(c) Time OFF ($T_{LOW}$):

Capacitor discharges through $R_B$.

$T_{OFF} = 0.693 (R_B) C$

(d) Frequency:

$T_{total} = T_{ON} + T_{OFF} = 0.693 (R_A + 2R_B) C$

$f = \frac{1}{T} = \frac{1.44}{(R_A + 2R_B)C}$

(e) Duty Cycle ($D$):

$D = \frac{T_{ON}}{T_{total}} = \frac{R_A + R_B}{R_A + 2R_B}$

(f) 50% Duty Cycle:

To achieve 50% duty cycle with a standard 555 astable, $T_{ON}$ must equal $T_{OFF}$. This requires $R_A$ to be effectively 0 (which shorts supply during discharge) or modifying the circuit. A common method is to place a diode across $R_B$ so charging bypasses $R_B$ (charging via $R_A$, discharging via $R_B$). Then make $R_A = R_B$.

2.

a) Explain any THREE properties of laser light.

b) The diagram below shows the equivalent circuit for unijunction transistor.

i. Draw and explain the I-V characteristics of a unijunction transistor. (8 marks)

ii. If $\eta=0.8, V_P=10.3V$, and $R_{B2}=5K\Omega$, determine $R_{B1}$ and $V_{BB}$ for the UJT. (6 marks)

Show Answer

Answer:

(a) Properties of Laser:

  1. Monochromatic: Consists of a single wavelength (color).
  2. Coherent: All waves are in phase with one another.
  3. Directional (Collimated): The beam has very low divergence and travels in a straight line.
  4. High Intensity: High energy concentrated in a small area.

(b) i. UJT I-V Characteristics:

Curve of Emitter Voltage ($V_E$) vs Emitter Current ($I_E$).

  • Cutoff: $V_E < V_P$. UJT is off.
  • Negative Resistance Region: Between Peak Point ($V_P$) and Valley Point ($V_V$). As current increases, voltage decreases. This is the switching region.
  • Saturation: Beyond $V_V$. $V_E$ increases slowly with current.

(b) ii. Calculations:

Given: $\eta=0.8, V_P=10.3V, R_{B2}=5K$.

Assuming $V_D = 0.7V$ (standard diode drop).

1. Find $V_{BB}$:

Formula: $V_P = \eta V_{BB} + V_D$

$10.3 = 0.8 V_{BB} + 0.7$

$0.8 V_{BB} = 9.6$

$V_{BB} = 9.6 / 0.8 = \mathbf{12V}$.

2. Find $R_{B1}$:

Formula: $\eta = \frac{R_{B1}}{R_{B1} + R_{B2}}$

$0.8 = \frac{R_{B1}}{R_{B1} + 5000}$

$0.8 R_{B1} + 4000 = R_{B1}$

$0.2 R_{B1} = 4000$

$R_{B1} = 4000 / 0.2 = \mathbf{20K\Omega}$.

3.

a) Define the following term as used in pulse descriptions:

i. Rise time (2 marks)

ii. Storage time (2 marks)

b) A communication system uses 10 km of fibre that has a 2.5 dB/km loss characteristic. Find the output power if the input power is 400 mW.

c) Draw an optocoupler that combines a photodiode and an LED and explain how it functions.

d) i. Define the term multiplexing as used in optical fibre telecommunication systems. (1 mark)

ii. Explain TWO multiplexing methods commonly used. (6 marks)

Show Answer

Answer:

(a) Definitions:

  • i. Rise Time ($t_r$): The time taken for the pulse (voltage or current) to rise from 10% to 90% of its maximum amplitude.
  • ii. Storage Time ($t_s$): The time interval between the removal of the input drive (base current) and the start of the decrease in output current (when transistor comes out of saturation). It is due to stored minority charge carriers in the base region.

(b) Fiber Loss Calculation:

Total Loss (dB) = Length $\times$ Loss/km = $10 \text{ km} \times 2.5 \text{ dB/km} = 25 \text{ dB}$.

Formula: $Loss(dB) = 10 \log_{10}(\frac{P_{in}}{P_{out}})$

$25 = 10 \log_{10}(\frac{400mW}{P_{out}})$

$2.5 = \log_{10}(\frac{400}{P_{out}})$

$10^{2.5} = \frac{400}{P_{out}}$

$316.22 = \frac{400}{P_{out}}$

$P_{out} = 400 / 316.22 \approx \mathbf{1.265 \text{ mW}}$.

(c) Optocoupler:

(Diagram should show LED on left facing a Photodiode on right inside a box). Function: Input signal drives LED. LED emits light. Photodiode detects light and conducts current. Provides electrical isolation.

(d) Multiplexing:

i. Definition: The technology of combining multiple signals or data streams into a single composite signal for transmission over a shared medium (fiber).

ii. Methods:

  • Wavelength Division Multiplexing (WDM): Different signals are assigned different wavelengths (colors) of laser light and transmitted simultaneously through the fiber.
  • Time Division Multiplexing (TDM): Different signals are assigned fixed time slots. Bits from each signal are interleaved sequentially.
