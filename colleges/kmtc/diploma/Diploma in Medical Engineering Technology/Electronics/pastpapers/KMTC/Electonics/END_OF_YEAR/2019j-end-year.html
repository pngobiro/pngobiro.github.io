Section A: Multiple Choice Questions

20 Marks
1.
In LEDs light is emitted because
a. Recombination of charge carrier takes place
b. Diodes get heated up
c. Light falling on the diode gets amplified
d. Light gets reflected due to lens action
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a. Recombination of charge carrier takes place

When forward biased, electrons from the N-region recombine with holes in the P-region at the junction, releasing energy in the form of photons (electroluminescence).

2.
After firing the SCR, the gating pulse is removed. The current in the SCR will
a. Remains the same
b. Immediately falls to zero
c. Rise up
d. Rise a little then fall to zero
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a. Remains the same

Once the SCR is latched (fired), the gate loses control. The anode current continues to flow as long as it is above the holding current.

3.
A unijunction transistor has
a. Anode, Cathode and gate
b. Two bases and one emitter
c. Two anodes and one gate
d. Anode, Cathode and two gates
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b. Two bases and one emitter

A UJT has three terminals: Base 1 (B1), Base 2 (B2), and Emitter (E).

4.
A monostable multivibrator circuit
a. Has two unstable states
b. Has one energy - storage element
c. Switches between its two states automatically
d. It's not an oscillator
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b. Has one energy - storage element (Best fit contextually)

Standard Definition: A monostable multivibrator has one stable state and one quasi-stable state. While it typically uses one timing capacitor (energy storage), the defining feature is the single stable state. Given the options, 'b' or 'd' might be selected, but 'b' refers to the single capacitor typically used for timing the unstable state duration.

5.
The unique characteristics of LASER light are that it is
a. Coherent
b. Monochromatic
c. Collimated
d. All of the above
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d. All of the above

Laser light is Coherent (in phase), Monochromatic (single wavelength), and Collimated (non-divergent).

6.
A solar cell operates on the principle of
a. Diffusion
b. Recombination
c. Photo voltaic action
d. Carrier flow
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c. Photo voltaic action

The photovoltaic effect is the generation of voltage and current in a material upon exposure to light.

7.
A UJT has $R_{BB} = 10K$ and $R_{B2} = 4K$. Its intrinsic stand off ratio is
a. 0.6
b. 0.4
c. 2.5
d. 5/3
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a. 0.6

Calculations: $R_{B1} = R_{BB} - R_{B2} = 10 - 4 = 6K$.

$\eta = \frac{R_{B1}}{R_{BB}} = \frac{6}{10} = 0.6$.

8.
The device possessing the highest sensitivity is a
a. Photoconductive cell
b. Photovoltaic cell
c. Photodiode
d. Phototransistor
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a. Photoconductive cell (or d depending on context)

Photoconductive cells (LDRs) have very high sensitivity to light (large resistance change). Phototransistors have higher sensitivity than photodiodes due to gain. In general electronics questions, LDRs are often cited for high sensitivity.

9.
In an optical fibre communication system, which among the following is not a typical transmitter function
a. Coding for protection
b. Decoding of input data
c. Electrical to optical conversion
d. Recording to march output standard
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b. Decoding of input data

Decoding is typically a receiver function. The transmitter encodes data and converts electrical signals to optical signals.

10.
When a transistor is driven to cut off by an output, the collector voltage is equal to
a. Zero
b. VCC
c. The input of the transistor
d. The output current of the transistor
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b. VCC

In cut-off, the transistor acts as an open switch. No current flows through the collector resistor, so there is no voltage drop across it. Therefore, $V_C = V_{CC}$.

Section C: Essay Questions

60 Marks
1.

a) Differentiate between single mode optical fibre and multimode fibre. (6 marks)

b) State any four advantages of optical fibre. (8 marks)

c) If a UJT has been supplied with power and the following data has been obtained: $\eta=0.8$, $V_p=10.3V$, $V_D=0.7V$ and $R_{B1}=50k\Omega$. Determine:

i) $V_{BB}$

ii) $R_{B2}$

iii) $R_{BB}$ (8 marks)

d) State two applications of bistable multivibrator. (4 marks)

Show Answer

Answer:

(a) Single Mode vs Multimode:

  • Core Diameter: Single mode has a very small core (~9$\mu$m), Multimode has a large core (50-62.5$\mu$m).
  • Propagation: Single mode supports only one mode of light propagation; Multimode supports hundreds of modes.
  • Light Source: Single mode typically uses Laser; Multimode uses LEDs.
  • Distance/Bandwidth: Single mode is for long distance/high bandwidth (low dispersion); Multimode is for shorter distances (higher modal dispersion).

(b) Advantages of Optical Fiber:

  1. High Bandwidth.
  2. Immunity to EMI/RFI.
  3. Low signal attenuation.
  4. Electrical isolation (safety).
  5. Security (difficult to tap).

(c) UJT Calculation:

Given: $\eta=0.8, V_P=10.3V, V_D=0.7V, R_{B1}=50k\Omega$.

  • i) $V_{BB}$:
    $V_P = \eta V_{BB} + V_D$
    $10.3 = 0.8 V_{BB} + 0.7$
    $9.6 = 0.8 V_{BB}$
    $V_{BB} = 9.6 / 0.8 = \mathbf{12V}$.
  • ii) $R_{B2}$:
    $\eta = \frac{R_{B1}}{R_{B1} + R_{B2}}$
    $0.8 = \frac{50k}{50k + R_{B2}}$
    $0.8(50k + R_{B2}) = 50k$
    $40k + 0.8 R_{B2} = 50k$
    $0.8 R_{B2} = 10k$
    $R_{B2} = 10k / 0.8 = \mathbf{12.5 k\Omega}$.
  • iii) $R_{BB}$:
    $R_{BB} = R_{B1} + R_{B2} = 50k + 12.5k = \mathbf{62.5 k\Omega}$.

(d) Bistable Multivibrator Applications:

  • Digital memory elements (Flip-flops).
  • Frequency dividers.
  • Counters.
  • Registers.
2.

a) Define the term waveshaping as applied to electronics.

b) State and explain three reasons for waveshaping.

c) Using a suitable circuit diagram, explain the operation of a unijunction transistor oscillator.

d) State four factors that causes loss in fibre optic.

Show Answer

Answer:

(a) Waveshaping: The process of changing the shape of a waveform (voltage or current) from one form to another (e.g., sine to square) or altering its characteristics (clipping, clamping).

(b) Reasons for Waveshaping:

  • Generation: To create specific waveforms required for timing or digital logic (e.g., square waves for clocks).
  • Protection: To limit voltage amplitudes to protect sensitive components (Clipping).
  • Restoration: To fix the DC level of a signal (Clamping).

(c) UJT Oscillator: (Standard Relaxation Oscillator diagram required). Operation involves charging a capacitor $C_E$ through $R_E$ until $V_P$ is reached, UJT fires, capacitor discharges through $R_{B1}$, UJT turns off, cycle repeats.

(d) Fiber Losses: Absorption, Rayleigh Scattering, Macrobending, Microbending.

4.

a) An optical fibre has a core of refractive index 1.51 and cladding of refractive index 1.5. Calculate:

i) The velocity of propagation

ii) The time taken to travel over a 100 M length of the cable (6 marks)

b) Briefly explain the following transistor time responses:

i) Risetime

ii) Turn ON time

iii) Fall time (6 marks)

Show Answer

Answer:

(a) Calculations:

i) Velocity ($v$): Speed of light in core.

$v = c / n_{core} = (3 \times 10^8) / 1.51 = 1.986 \times 10^8 \text{ m/s}$.

ii) Time ($t$):

$t = \text{Distance} / \text{Velocity} = 100 / (1.986 \times 10^8) = 5.03 \times 10^{-7} \text{ s} = \mathbf{503 \text{ ns}}$.

(b) Transistor Responses:

  • i) Rise Time ($t_r$): Time for collector current to rise from 10% to 90% of max value during turn-on.
  • ii) Turn ON Time ($t_{on}$): Sum of Delay time + Rise time. Total time to switch from OFF to ON.
  • iii) Fall Time ($t_f$): Time for collector current to fall from 90% to 10% of max value during turn-off.
