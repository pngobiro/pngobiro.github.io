Section 1: Multiple Choice Questions

20 Marks
1.
What is the full form of LASER?
a Light Absorbent and Stimulated Emission of Radiations
b Light Amplification by Stimulated Emission of Radiations
c Light Absorbing Solar Energy Resource
d Light Amplification of Singular Emission of Radiations
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Light Amplification by Stimulated Emission of Radiations

LASER is an acronym describing the physical principle of generating coherent light via stimulated emission.

2.
In Stimulated Absorption, what is the lifetime of atoms ground state?
a 1 second
b 1 minute
c 1 hour
d Infinity
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Infinity

An atom in its ground state is in its lowest energy configuration and stable. It will remain there indefinitely (infinite lifetime) unless disturbed by external energy.

3.
A triac is a ............. switch
a Bidirectional
b Unidirectional
c Mechanical
d None of the above
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Bidirectional

A TRIAC can conduct current in both directions between its main terminals (MT1 and MT2), making it a bidirectional AC switch.

4.
Multimode step index fiber has ____
a Large core diameter & large numerical aperture
b Large core diameter and small numerical aperture
c Small core diameter and large numerical aperture
d Small core diameter & small numerical aperture
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Large core diameter & large numerical aperture

Multimode step index fibers are characterized by a large core (e.g., 50-100 $\mu m$) to support many modes and a large numerical aperture to facilitate easy coupling of light.

5.
Multimode step index fiber has a large core diameter of range is
a 100 to 300 $\mu m$
b 100 to 300 nm
c 200 to 500 $\mu m$
d 200 to 500 nm
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) 100 to 300 $\mu m$ (Approximate)

Standard data fibers are 50 or 62.5 $\mu m$, but large-core POF (Plastic Optical Fibers) or PCS (Polymer Clad Silica) can range from 100 $\mu m$ to 1000 $\mu m$. Among options, A is the most realistic range for "large core".

6.
The fibers mostly not used nowadays for optical fiber communication system are ____
a Single mode fibers
b Multimode step fibers
c Coaxial cables
d Multimode graded index fibers
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Multimode step fibers

Multimode Step Index fibers suffer from severe intermodal dispersion, which drastically limits bandwidth and distance. They are rarely used for modern telecommunications compared to Graded Index or Single Mode.

7.
If a light travels in a certain medium and it gets reflected off an optically denser medium with high refractive index, then it is regarded as ____
a External Reflection
b Internal Reflection
c Both a and b
d None of the above
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) External Reflection

Internal Reflection occurs when light tries to move from dense to rare (e.g., glass to air). External Reflection occurs when light hits a denser medium from a rarer medium (e.g., air to glass) and reflects.

8.
In spontaneous emission, the light source in an excited state undergoes the transition to a state with ____
a Higher energy
b Moderate energy
c Lower energy
d All of the above
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Lower energy

Emission occurs when an electron drops from a high energy state to a lower energy state, releasing the energy difference as a photon.

9.
RL integrator circuits are used to produce ____ pulses at output.
a sharp
b narrow
c linear
d all are correct
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) linear (Triangular/Ramp)

An integrator circuit converts a square wave input into a triangular (linear ramp) wave. Sharp/narrow pulses are produced by differentiator circuits.

10.
An RC series circuit can be used as an integrator when the output is taken across;
a Resistor
b capacitor
c either of the above
d none of the above
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) capacitor

In an RC integrator, the output is taken across the capacitor ($V_C \propto \int i dt$). If taken across the resistor, it acts as a differentiator.

Section 3: Long Answer Questions

60 Marks
1.

a) What is meant by clipper circuit? (3 Marks)

b) Define Photovoltaic effect. (3 Marks)

c) Differentiate photodiode and phototransistor. (4 marks)

d) Explain how Forward Voltage Triggering is done to trigger an SCR. (7 marks)

e) State three Reasons why wave-shaping is necessary in electronic devices. (3 marks)

Show Answer

Answer:

(a) Clipper Circuit: This is an electronic circuit used to clip off (remove) unwanted portions of a waveform (positive or negative peaks) above or below a specified reference level, without distorting the remaining part of the waveform.

(b) Photovoltaic Effect: The generation of a potential difference (voltage) and electric current in a material (typically a semiconductor P-N junction) upon exposure to light (photons). It is the operating principle of solar cells.

(c) Photodiode vs Phototransistor:

  • Photodiode: A 2-terminal device (Anode, Cathode) that converts light to current. Faster response time, lower sensitivity (no gain). Used in high-speed data comms.
  • Phototransistor: A 2 or 3-terminal device (Collector, Emitter, opt. Base). It effectively combines a photodiode and a transistor. It amplifies the light-generated current by factor $\beta$ ($I_C = \beta I_{ph}$). Slower response time, higher sensitivity.

(d) Forward Voltage Triggering:

This method involves increasing the anode-to-cathode voltage ($V_{AK}$) while the gate is open ($I_G=0$).
1. In the forward blocking state, junctions J1 and J3 are forward biased, but inner junction J2 is reverse biased.
2. As voltage increases, the depletion layer at J2 widens.
3. When $V_{AK}$ exceeds a critical value called the Forward Breakover Voltage ($V_{BO}$), an avalanche breakdown occurs at J2 due to high electric field accelerating carriers.
4. This flood of carriers closes the junction, and the SCR switches rapidly to the conduction state.
Note: This is generally a destructive or uncontrolled method and is rarely used for normal operation.

(e) Reasons for Wave Shaping:

  • To generate specific waveforms required for digital circuits (e.g., creating square waves from sine waves).
  • To limit voltage amplitudes to protect sensitive components (Clipping).
  • To restore or shift DC levels of a signal (Clamping).
  • To generate trigger pulses for timing circuits (Differentiation).

b) Explain any four applications of lasers. (14 marks)

c) Explain in detail how mobile phone works when you make a call. (6 marks)

Show Answer

Answer:

(a) Symbols:

  • SCR: Unidirectional diode with Gate.
  • TRIAC: Bidirectional (two diodes back-to-back) with Gate.
  • DIAC: Bidirectional diode without Gate.

(b) Applications of Lasers:

  1. Medical: Laser surgery (Lasik eye surgery, tumor removal), cauterizing blood vessels, dental procedures. Precision cutting minimizes bleeding.
  2. Communications: Fiber optic data transmission. Lasers provide the high-intensity, monochromatic light source required to send data over long distances with minimal loss.
  3. Industrial: Cutting, welding, and drilling metals and other materials. High intensity allows precise melting/vaporization.
  4. Consumer Electronics: Barcode scanners, Laser printers, CD/DVD/Blu-ray players (reading microscopic pits on discs).
  5. Military: Range finding, target designation, missile guidance.

(c) Mobile Phone Operation:

  1. Microphone: Converts voice sound waves into analog electrical signals.
  2. Processing: The phone converts analog signals to digital data.
  3. Transmission: The antenna transmits this digital data as radio waves (electromagnetic waves) to the nearest Cell Tower (Base Station).
  4. Routing: The tower sends the signal to the Mobile Switching Center (MSC). The MSC locates the recipient's phone.
  5. Connection: If the recipient is on the same network, it routes to their nearest tower. If different, it routes to the Public Switched Telephone Network (PSTN) or other network.
  6. Reception: The recipient's phone receives the radio waves, demodulates them back to digital, then analog, and the speaker produces sound.
