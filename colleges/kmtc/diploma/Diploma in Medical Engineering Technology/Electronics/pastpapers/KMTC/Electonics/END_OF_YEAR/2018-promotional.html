Section A: Multiple Choice Questions

20 Marks
1.
When a transistor is driven to cut-off by an input, the collector voltage is equal to
a) zero
b) $V_{CC}$
c) The input of the transistor
d) The output current of the transistor
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) $V_{CC}$

In cut-off, the transistor is OFF (acts as an open switch). No collector current flows ($I_C \approx 0$). Therefore, there is no voltage drop across the collector resistor ($I_C R_C = 0$), so $V_C = V_{CC} - 0 = V_{CC}$.

2.
The flip flop is also called
a) Binary circuits
b) Multi vibrator
c) Memory device
d) Non sinusoidal oscillator
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Multi vibrator (Bistable)

A flip-flop is technically defined as a Bistable Multivibrator. While it acts as a binary memory device, "Multivibrator" is the circuit classification.

3.
The intrinsic voltage stand- off ratio of unijunction transistor equals to
a) $RB_{1} / (RB_{1}+RB_{2})$
b) $RB_{2} / (RB_{1}+RB_{2})$
c) $RB_{1}(RB_{1}+RB_{2})$
d) $RB_{2}(RB_{1}+RB_{2})$
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) $RB_{1} / (RB_{1}+RB_{2})$

The Intrinsic Stand-off Ratio ($\eta$) is defined as the ratio of the lower base resistance ($RB_1$) to the total interbase resistance ($R_{BB} = RB_1 + RB_2$).

4.
The maximum conduction angle of silicon controlled rectifier is
a) 45
b) 90
c) 180
d) 360
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) 180

In a standard AC circuit, an SCR (which is a rectifier) can only conduct during the positive half-cycle. It blocks the negative half-cycle. Therefore, the maximum theoretical conduction angle is 180 degrees.

5.
One method of switching off a triac is to
a) Increase supply voltage
b) Reduce gate current
c) Change voltage polarity
d) Reduce current impedance
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Change voltage polarity (Natural Commutation)

A TRIAC turns off when the main current drops below the holding current. In AC circuits, this happens naturally near the zero-crossing point where the voltage polarity reverses.

6.
In photo conductive cell the resistance of the semiconductor materials varies .... ____
a) Directly
b) Inversely
c) Exponentially
d) logarithmically
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Inversely

In a photoconductive cell (LDR), light energy creates electron-hole pairs, increasing conductivity. Since Resistance is the inverse of Conductivity ($R \propto 1/\sigma$), the resistance decreases as light intensity increases.

7.
A LED is basically a ................... PN junction
a) Forward biased
b) Reverse biased
c) Lightly doped
d) Heavily doped
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Forward biased

Light Emitting Diodes (LEDs) emit light via electroluminescence, which only occurs when current flows through the junction in the forward direction (recombination of carriers).

8.
The following are causes of signal loss in optic fibre except
a) Bend loss
b) Absorption loss
c) Reflection loss
d) Rayceigh scatter
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Reflection loss (Contextual)

Absorption, Bending, and Rayleigh Scattering are the three fundamental intrinsic loss mechanisms. "Reflection" (specifically Total Internal Reflection) is the mechanism that keeps light inside the fiber. While Fresnel reflection at ends causes loss, within the context of propagation mechanisms vs loss mechanisms, Reflection is usually the odd one out here.

9.
The minimum value of current required to maintain conduction in SCR is called ____ current
a) commutation
b) Holding
c) Gate trigger
d) Break over
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Holding

Holding current is the minimum anode current required to keep the SCR in the ON state after the gate signal is removed.

10.
The unique characteristics of laser light are that it is
a) Coherent
b) Monochromatic
c) Collimated
d) All of the above
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) All of the above

Laser light is Coherent (in-phase), Monochromatic (single wavelength), and Collimated (non-divergent/directional).

Section B: Essay Questions

1.

a) Draw and explain the I-V characteristics of a unijunction transistor (UJT).

b) If $\eta=0.8$, $V_P=10.3 \text{ V}$ and $R_{B2}=5 \text{ k}\Omega$, determine $R_{B1}$ (denoted as $R_1$) and $V_{BB}$ for UJT.

c) State any four applications of multivibrators.

Show Answer

Answer:

(a) UJT I-V Characteristics:

The curve represents Emitter Voltage ($V_E$) vs Emitter Current ($I_E$).

  • Cut-off Region: $V_E < V_P$. The emitter diode is reverse-biased. Current is negligible (leakage). UJT is OFF.
  • Negative Resistance Region: When $V_E$ reaches $V_P$, the diode conducts. Holes injected into the base reduce the resistance of $R_{B1}$. As current increases, voltage drops. This region allows the UJT to function as an oscillator.
  • Saturation Region: Beyond the Valley Point ($V_V$), the device saturates. Voltage increases slowly with current.

(b) Calculations:

Given: $\eta=0.8, V_P=10.3V, R_{B2}=5k\Omega$. Assume $V_D \approx 0.7V$.

Step 1: Calculate $V_{BB}$

Formula: $V_P = \eta V_{BB} + V_D$

$10.3 = 0.8 V_{BB} + 0.7$

$9.6 = 0.8 V_{BB}$

$V_{BB} = \frac{9.6}{0.8} = 12 \text{ V}$.

Step 2: Calculate $R_{B1}$

Formula: $\eta = \frac{R_{B1}}{R_{B1} + R_{B2}}$

$0.8 = \frac{R_{B1}}{R_{B1} + 5}$

$0.8(R_{B1} + 5) = R_{B1}$

$0.8 R_{B1} + 4 = R_{B1}$

$4 = R_{B1} - 0.8 R_{B1}$

$4 = 0.2 R_{B1}$

$R_{B1} = \frac{4}{0.2} = 20 \text{ k}\Omega$.

(c) Applications of Multivibrators:

  1. Clock Pulse Generation: Astable multivibrators are used as clock sources for digital circuits.
  2. Memory Elements: Bistable multivibrators (Flip-flops) store binary data (1-bit memory).
  3. Frequency Division: Flip-flops can divide input frequency by 2.
  4. Timing/Delay Circuits: Monostable multivibrators generate precise time delays or pulse widths.
