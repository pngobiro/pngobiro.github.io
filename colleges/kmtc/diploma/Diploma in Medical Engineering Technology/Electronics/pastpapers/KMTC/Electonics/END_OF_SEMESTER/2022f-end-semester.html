Section 1: Multiple Choice Questions

20 Marks
1.
An Astable multivibrator produces
A. Sine wave
B. Triangular wave
C. Sawtooth wave
D. Square wave
Show Answer

Correct Answer: D. Square wave

An astable multivibrator is a regenerative circuit that switches continuously between two unstable states, producing a square wave output.

2.
The charging path and discharging path of a capacitor are different so the ON time and OFF time of the output signal are not equal. What is the name of the ratio of the ON time pulse to the time period?
A. OFF cycle
B. Duty cycle
C. Time cycle
D. On cycle
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B. Duty cycle

Duty Cycle is defined as the ratio of the pulse width ($T_{ON}$) to the total period ($T$) of the waveform, usually expressed as a percentage: $D = \frac{T_{ON}}{T_{ON} + T_{OFF}} \times 100\%$.

3.
An RC circuit that changes the shape of a triangular wave into a rectangular wave is termed as a
A. Integrating circuit
B. Differentiating circuit
C. Clamper circuit
D. Clipper circuit
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B. Differentiating circuit

Ideally, the derivative of a triangular wave (linear ramp) is a square wave (constant step). The slope is constant positive (giving +V) then constant negative (giving -V). Therefore, a differentiator performs this conversion.

4.
RL integrator circuits are used to produce ____ pulses at output.
A. sharp
B. narrow
C. linear
D. all are correct
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C. linear

An integrator circuit (whether RC or RL) produces a linear ramp output when the input is a step or square wave. Sharp/narrow pulses are typically produced by differentiator circuits.

5.
What are the basic circuits required for a clipping circuit?
A. Diode and resistor
B. Transistor and diode
C. Diode and capacitor
D. Capacitor and resistor
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A. Diode and resistor

Simple clipper circuits are constructed using a diode (to block/conduct signal portions) and a resistor (to limit current/drop voltage). Capacitors are added for *clampers*.

6.
TRIAC is a semiconductor power electronic device which contains
A. Two SCR's connected in reverse parallel
B. Two SCR's connected in parallel
C. Two SCR's connected in series
D. Two BJT's connected in series
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A. Two SCR's connected in reverse parallel

A TRIAC is effectively two Thyristors (SCRs) connected in inverse parallel (back-to-back) with a common gate, allowing bidirectional conduction.

7.
Silicon controlled rectifier is turned off only when current becomes zero
A. turned off only when current becomes zero
B. And turn off by applying gate pulse
C. By applying a gate pulse and turned off by removing the gate pulse
D. By making current non zero and turned off by making current zero
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A/D. (Context: Commutation)

An SCR can only be turned off (commutated) by reducing the anode current below the holding current (effectively zero). Gate control is lost once the device is latched ON.

8.
Which of the following is a bidirectional semiconductor device?
A. Diode
B. BJT
C. SCR
D. TRIAC
Show Answer

Correct Answer: D. TRIAC

Diode, BJT, and SCR are unidirectional. The TRIAC is designed to conduct current in both directions (AC).

9.
The conductivity value of a semiconductor device is ____.
A. Infinity
B. Below conductors conductivity value
C. Above insulator conductivity value
D. Between conductor and insulator conductivity value
Show Answer

Correct Answer: D. Between conductor and insulator conductivity value

Semiconductors have conductivity levels between those of good conductors (metals) and insulators.

10.
The majority charge carriers in a P-type semiconductor are
A. Electrons
B. Holes
C. Both a and b
D. Partial electrons and partial holes
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B. Holes

In P-type material (Positive type), holes are the majority charge carriers and electrons are the minority carriers.

11.
Photodiodes operate at
A. Forward bias
B. Breakdown region
C. Reverse bias
D. Saturation region
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C. Reverse bias

Photodiodes are connected in reverse bias to widen the depletion region and increase sensitivity to incident light (measuring leakage/dark current modulation).

12.
An LED in reverse bias condition emits
A. Brighter light
B. Dimmer light
C. Same as in forward biasing
D. No light
Show Answer

Correct Answer: D. No light

LEDs only emit light when forward biased (recombination of carriers). In reverse bias, no current flows (until breakdown), so no light is emitted.

13.
A DIAC has ____ terminals
A. Two
B. Three
C. Four
D. One
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A. Two

A DIAC (Diode for Alternating Current) is a two-terminal device (MT1 and MT2). It has no gate.

14.
To turn on the UJT, the forward bias on the emitter diode should be voltage ____ the peak point
A. Less than
B. Equal to
C. More than
D. It does not matter
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C. More than

The UJT fires (enters negative resistance region) only when the Emitter voltage ($V_E$) exceeds the Peak Point voltage ($V_P$).

15.
The UJT may be used as ____
A. An amplifier
B. A sawtooth generator
C. A rectifier
D. A multiplier
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B. A sawtooth generator

UJTs are commonly used in relaxation oscillators to generate sawtooth or trigger waveforms.

16.
After the peak point, the UJT operates in the
A. Negative resistance region
B. Saturation
C. Cut-off
D. Ohmic
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A. Negative resistance region

Once the UJT fires (past $V_P$), the emitter voltage drops as the current increases, characteristic of negative resistance.

17.
Which of the following does not have a gate terminal?
A. TRIAC
B. DIAC
C. SCR
D. FET
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B. DIAC

TRIAC, SCR, and FET all have Gate terminals. The DIAC is a 2-terminal device triggered by voltage breakdown.

18.
What is the principle of fibre optical communication?
A. Frequency modulation
B. Population inversion
C. Total internal reflection
D. Doppler Effect
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C. Total internal reflection

Light is guided through the fiber core because it reflects off the core-cladding boundary due to Total Internal Reflection (TIR).

19.
Which of the following loss occurs inside the fibre?
A. Radiative loss
B. Scattering
C. Absorption
D. Attenuation
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C. Absorption (also Scattering)

Intrinsic losses *inside* the material are Absorption (by impurities) and Rayleigh Scattering (by structure). Absorption is a primary material loss.

20.
Which of the following is a unique property of laser?
A. Directionality
B. Speed
C. Coherence
D. Wavelength
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C. Coherence

While lasers are directional and monochromatic, Coherence (waves are in phase in time and space) is the most defining and unique property compared to other light sources.

5.

There are two ways to turn on an SCR.

a. State the two ways to turn on an SCR.

b. Explain the forward blocking mode of the SCR.

Show Answer

Answer:

(a) Turn-on Methods:

  1. Gate Triggering: Applying a positive pulse to the gate.
  2. Forward Voltage Triggering (Breakover): Increasing anode-cathode voltage beyond the breakover voltage ($V_{BO}$).
  3. (Others include dv/dt triggering, thermal triggering).

(b) Forward Blocking Mode: In this state, the Anode is positive relative to the Cathode, but the Gate is open (0V). Junctions J1 and J3 are forward biased, but the central junction J2 is reverse biased. Only a small leakage current flows, and the device acts as an open switch (OFF).

6.

Define the following terms;

a. Breakover voltage

b. Holding current

c. TRIAC

Show Answer

Answer:

  • (a) Breakover Voltage ($V_{BO}$): The minimum forward voltage across the anode and cathode at which the SCR starts conducting without any gate current.
  • (b) Holding Current ($I_H$): The minimum anode current required to maintain the SCR in the conducting (ON) state after the gate signal is removed.
  • (c) TRIAC: A three-terminal bidirectional semiconductor device that functions like two SCRs connected in inverse parallel, used for controlling AC power.

8.

Wave shaping is important in electronics;

a. Define the term wave shaping circuit.

b. State three reasons why we need wave shaping circuits.

Show Answer

Answer:

(a) Definition: A circuit used to change the shape or characteristics (amplitude, DC level) of a voltage or current waveform.

(b) Reasons:

Section 3: Long Answer Questions

1.

a. The optical fiber has three main parts. Draw a diagram with the three parts labelled. (3 marks)

b. State one function of each part.

c. Define the term total internal reflection.

d. State two conditions that are necessary for total internal reflection to occur in an optical fiber.

e. State Snell's law.

f. A beam of flashlight traveling in air incident on a surface of a thin glass at an angle of $38^\circ$ with the normal. The index of refraction of the glass is 1.56. What is the angle of refraction? (6 marks)

Show Answer

Answer:

(a) Diagram:

Concentric circles: Center = Core, Middle ring = Cladding, Outer ring = Buffer/Jacket.

(b) Functions:

  • Core: Carries the light signal.
  • Cladding: Keeps light in the core via TIR (lower refractive index) and provides mechanical strength.
  • Buffer/Jacket: Protects the fiber from moisture and physical damage.

(c) Total Internal Reflection (TIR): The phenomenon where a light ray traveling from a denser medium to a less dense medium is completely reflected back into the denser medium when the angle of incidence exceeds the critical angle.

(d) Conditions for TIR:

  1. Light must travel from a medium of higher refractive index (core) to a medium of lower refractive index (cladding). ($n_1 > n_2$).
  2. The angle of incidence must be greater than the critical angle ($\theta_i > \theta_c$).

(e) Snell's Law: relates the indices of refraction of two media to the directions of propagation in terms of the angles to the normal. Formula: $n_1 \sin\theta_1 = n_2 \sin\theta_2$.

(f) Calculation:

  • $n_1 = 1.00$ (Air)
  • $\theta_1 = 38^\circ$
  • $n_2 = 1.56$ (Glass)
  • Find $\theta_2$.

$$1.00 \times \sin(38^\circ) = 1.56 \times \sin(\theta_2)$$

$$0.6157 = 1.56 \times \sin(\theta_2)$$

$$\sin(\theta_2) = \frac{0.6157}{1.56} = 0.3947$$

$$\theta_2 = \sin^{-1}(0.3947) \approx 23.25^\circ$$

2.

a. Draw a shunt positive clipper circuit.

b. Explain how the shunt positive clipper works.

c. Draw the input and output waveforms.

d. Explain the construction of a silicon controlled rectifier.

Show Answer

Answer:

(a) Shunt Positive Clipper: Resistor in series with signal. Diode in parallel (shunt) with output. Diode Anode connected to signal line, Cathode to ground.

(b) Operation:

  • Positive Half Cycle: Input voltage is positive. The diode is Forward Biased (acts as a short circuit). Current flows to ground. Output voltage is 0V (or 0.7V diode drop). Ideally, the positive cycle is clipped off.
  • Negative Half Cycle: Input voltage is negative. The diode is Reverse Biased (acts as open circuit). Current flows to the load. Output follows the input.

(c) Waveforms:

  • Input: Sine Wave.
  • Output: Only negative half cycles are present. Flat line at 0V during positive times.

(d) SCR Construction:

  • A 4-layer (P-N-P-N) semiconductor device.
  • It has 3 terminals: Anode (A) attached to outer P-layer, Cathode (K) attached to outer N-layer, and Gate (G) attached to the inner P-layer (near cathode).
  • It contains 3 junctions (J1, J2, J3).
