Common Base Characteristics

üìö Basic Electronics - Unit 2
‚è±Ô∏è 8 min read
01

üìä Experimental Setup

Transistor characteristics represent relationship between different dc currents and voltages of a transistor. The three important characteristics called static characteristics of a transistor are :

  1. Input characteristic
  2. Output characteristic
  3. Constant-current transfer characteristic.

The static characteristics of an NPN transistor connected in common-base configuration can be determined by using circuit as shown in Fig. 2.16. Milliammeters are included in series with the emitter and collector circuits to measure $\mathrm{I_E}$ and $\mathrm{I_C}$. Similarly, voltmeters are connected across E and B to measure voltage $\mathrm{V_{BE}}$ and across C and B to measure $\mathrm{V_{CB}}$. The two potentiometer resistors $\mathbf{R}_1$ and $\mathbf{R}_2$ supply variable voltage from the collector and emitter dc supplies.

02

üì• Input Characteristics

It is a graph between $\mathrm{I_E}$ and $\mathrm{V_{BE}}$ when voltage $\mathrm{V_{CB}}$ is kept constant. The method of determining this characteristic is as follows:

  1. Voltage $\mathrm{V_{CB}}$ is adjusted to a suitable value with the help of $\mathrm{R_1}$ (Fig. 2.16).
  2. Voltage $\mathrm{V_{BE}}$ is increased in steps and corresponding values of $\mathrm{I_E}$ are noted from the milliammeter.
  3. Plot $\mathrm{I_E}$ verses $\mathrm{V_{BE}}$. We get the input characteristics as shown in Fig. 2.17 one for Ge transistor and the other for Si transistor. Both curves are exactly similar to the forward characteristic of a P-N diode.
üìê

Input Resistance

This characteristic may be used to find the input resistance of the transistor. Its value is given by the reciprocal of its slope.

$$R_{in} = \frac{\Delta V_{BE}}{\Delta I_E} \quad \text{when } V_{CB} \text{ is constant}$$
03

üì§ Output Characteristics

It is a graph between $\mathrm{I_C}$ and $\mathrm{V_{CB}}$ when $\mathrm{I_E}$ is kept constant. The method of determining this characteristic is as follows:

  1. Emitter current $\mathrm{I_E}$ is kept suitable constant value by adjusting $\mathrm{R_2}$.
  2. $\mathrm{V}_{\mathrm{CB}}$ is increased from zero in a number of steps and the corresponding collector current $\mathrm{I}_{\mathrm{C}}$ that flows is noted.
  3. Next, $\mathrm{V}_{\mathrm{CB}}$ is reduced back to zero, $\mathrm{I}_{\mathrm{E}}$ is increased to a value a little higher than before and the whole procedure is repeated. In this way, whole family of curves is obtained, A typical family of such curves is shown in Fig. 2.18.

Key Conclusions from Output Characteristics:

  • AC Current Gain ($\alpha_{\infty}$): The ac current gain of the transistor is expressed as:
    $$\alpha_{\infty} = \frac{\Delta I_C}{\Delta I_E} = \frac{DE}{BC}$$
  • Independence from $V_{CB}$: $\mathrm{I}_{\mathrm{C}}$ is practically independent of $\mathrm{V}_{\mathrm{CB}}$ over the working range (active region) of the transistor. However, if $\mathrm{V}_{\mathrm{CB}}$ is permitted to increase beyond a certain value, $\mathrm{I}_{\mathrm{C}}$ eventually increases rapidly due to avalanche breakdown.
  • Leakage Current: It is seen that $\mathrm{I}_{\mathrm{C}}$ flows even when $\mathrm{V}_{\mathrm{CB}} = 0$. It is due to the fact that electrons are being injected into the base under the action of forward-biased E/B junction and are being collected by the collector due the action of the internal junction voltage at the C/B junction. A small amount of collector current flows even when emitter current $\mathrm{I_E} = 0$, due to collector leakage current $\mathrm{I_{CBO}}$.
  • High Output Resistance: The output resistance $(\mathbb{R}_{\mathrm{O}})$ of the Transistor in CB configuration is very high due to horizontal nature of the graph in active region.

üîÑ Current Transfer Characteristic

It shows how $I_{C}$ varies with changes in $I_{E}$ when $V_{CB}$ is kept constant. For drawing this characteristic, first $V_{CB}$ is set to a convenient value and then $I_{E}$ is increased in steps and corresponding values of $I_{C}$ is noted.

Practical Application Note

It may be noted that CB connection is rarely used for audio-frequency circuits because:

  1. Its current gain is less than unity.
  2. Its input and output resistances are not suitable.

Key Points

  • ‚úì

    Input characteristics show relationship between $\mathrm{I_E}$ and $\mathrm{V_{BE}}$ with $\mathrm{V_{CB}}$ constant.

  • ‚úì

    Output characteristics show relationship between $\mathrm{I_C}$ and $\mathrm{V_{CB}}$ with $\mathrm{I_E}$ constant.

  • ‚úì

    In CB configuration, output resistance is very high while input resistance is low.

  • ‚úì

    Current gain ($\alpha$) is always less than unity (typically 0.95 to 0.99).
