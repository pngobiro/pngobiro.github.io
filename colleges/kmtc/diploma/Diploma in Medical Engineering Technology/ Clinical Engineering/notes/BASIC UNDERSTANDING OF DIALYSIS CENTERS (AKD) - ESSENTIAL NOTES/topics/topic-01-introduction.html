üí° What is AKD

Artificial kidney department (AKD) is where ESRD (End Stage Renal failure) patient are given Haemodialysis with continuous monitoring and handling of intradialytic complications with maximum safety and ease to the patient. AKD has skilled staff with the necessary knowledge and experience to conduct dialysis to such patients.

02

ü©∏ What is Dialysis?

Dialysis is a medical process through which a person's blood is cleansed of the 'Toxins', the kidney normally would flush out. It is generally used when a person's kidney no longer function properly. This can be a result of congenital kidney disease, long term diabetes, HTN or other condition.

Global Context: Need of AKD

About 1.7 million patients worldwide depend on long-term renal replacement therapies. The no. of such patients worldwide is rising about 6% annually. Asia, Latin, America, Africa and Middle East are experiencing even higher proliferative rates up to 10%.

Haemodialysis being the most widely used form of therapy with an incidence of 89%.

03

üèÜ IDEAL DEPARTMENT

Objective

To provide hemodialysis with meticulous care and aseptic precautions during initiation, monitoring and termination.

Scope

The scope of an ideal AKD includes:

  • Initial assessment, diagnosis and commencement of treatment of OPD and admitted patients.
  • Continuous monitoring and evaluation of patients undergoing hemodialysis.
  • Handling intradialytic complications.
  • Pre, intra and post dialysis assessment and recording their findings.
  • Administration of treatment and drugs prescribed by the consultant.
  • Continuous communication and co-ordination with consultant to control the complicated studies.
  • Training of AKD staff for continuous quality improvement.
