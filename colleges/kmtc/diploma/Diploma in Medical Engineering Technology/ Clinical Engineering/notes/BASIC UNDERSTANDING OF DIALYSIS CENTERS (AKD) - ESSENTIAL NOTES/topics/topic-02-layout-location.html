üíß Reverse Osmosis Plant (Water Treatment System)

  • The hemodialysis patient is exposed to a very large quantity of water across a thin, non-selective dialyser membrane. T
  • Patient gets exposed to the organic and inorganic contaminants in the water used to prepare dialysate. Bacterial and endo-toxins contamination of the water also poses a significant threat to the safety and well being of the hemodialysis patient.
  • contaminant like aluminum, chloramines, fluorides, copper, zinc, nitrates, sulphates, calcium, magnesium and sodium to safe levels. Microbial and endotoxin contamination of water used for hemodialysis also needs to be controlled.

Treatment options available for rendering the feed water suitable for dialysis includes

  • Charcoal filter
  • Sand filter Activated carbon filter
  • Softner
  • Reverse osmosis membrane
  • Ultraviolet irradiation

üè• Support Facilities

Preparation Room/ Wash Room

  • Wash room for dialysis and tubing should be separate
  • Separate room is must for positive patient
  • Wash area should have 2 washing sinks with multiple tapering tapes/ racks for soap and disinfectant
  • Wash room should have provision of pigeon and tubing which should ideally be of non-corrosive material/wood or high grade stainless steel.
  • This area require a heavy duty exhaust fan
  • Also requires cupboards for storing all the costly consumables viz., dialyzers, tubing etc. and a separate cupboard for storing liven.

Patients Waiting Area

  • Should be planned with 2-6 beds for holding a dialysis patient for a temporary period.
  • Pre/post dialysis for observation and monitor in case require.
  • This will enable the dialysis bed to free as soon as dialysis is terminated so that the next shift is not delayed

Nursing and Monitoring Station

  • Centrally situated so that every patient can easily monitored.
  • Well illuminated
  • Having all the furniture like desk, table, chairs, all stationary
  • With one phone to contact with other department

Nephrology OPD Room

  • It can be situated adjacent to the department or can be located at the other OPD‚Äôs premises.
  • It should have all the necessary equipment for examination

Levatory facility

  • Toilet facility should provide separate for male and female.
  • Separate facility should be provided for staff.
  • Cleanliness and hygiene must be maintained.
