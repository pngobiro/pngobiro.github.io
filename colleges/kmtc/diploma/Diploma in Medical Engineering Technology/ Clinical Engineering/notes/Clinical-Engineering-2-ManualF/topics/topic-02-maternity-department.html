üí° Definition & Functions

Maternity is the department that takes care of expectant mothers before and during delivery.

Functions and need for maternity department in a hospital facility

  1. Admits expectant mothers who are about to deliver
  2. Keeps records showing the number of newborn babies and other necessary records.
  3. Cares for expectant mothers together with monitoring their progress.
  4. Operational services to mothers who develops compliations during child birth.
  5. Has a nursery section where prematurely babies are admitted and taken care of.
  6. A child is able to get a legal birth certificate easily.

üó∫Ô∏è Layout of Maternity Department

Sections and their functions

  1. Reception; Opening files, keeping records, discharge and enquiries.
  2. Examination room; Determination of mother's condition during pregnancy, blood pressure measurements (systolic and diastolic) and any other condition.
  3. Post-natal; where recovery is done to mothers after delivery and their health status monitored. Mothers who have delivered are also advised accordingly.
  4. Labour Room; It is a room where expectant mothers who are almost delivering stay i.e. waiting room.
  5. Wash room; where toilets and bathrooms are located.
  6. Sterilizing room; where equipment are sterilized to avoid infection.
  7. General Store: Storage for cloth and linen
  8. Theatre: Mothers who undergo caesarian or assisted deliveries are operated.
  9. Recovery room: Mothers from theatre stays and medical care taken.
  10. Matron‚Äôs office: Place where matron in charge keeps her records.
  11. General Staff office: Resting points for doctors and nurses assigned in the ward.
  12. Acute room: Expectant mothers who undergo caesarian section operation are prepared.
  13. Changing room: Where doctors and nurses change.
  14. Nursery: Where premature born babies are monitored to desirable conditions.
  15. Prenatal/Antenatal room: Expectant mothers come for checkups to find out on their health conditions.
