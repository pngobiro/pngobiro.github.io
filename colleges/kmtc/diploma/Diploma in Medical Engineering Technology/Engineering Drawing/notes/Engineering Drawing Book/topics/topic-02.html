üìä Overview & Engineering Scales

The types of scale normally used are:

  • Plain scales
  • Diagonal scales

Engineering Scales

Size of objects may be extremely small, medium or large.

  • Full scale: 1:1
  • Reduced scale: 1:2, 1:2.5, 1:5, 1:10, 1:20, 1:50, 1:100, 1:200
  • Enlarged scale: 10:1, 5:1, 2:1
02

üßÆ Representative Fraction (R.F.)

The ratio of the dimension of the element in the drawing to the dimension of the same element in the object is called the representative fraction.

$$\frac {10 \mathrm{mm}}{1 \mathrm{metre}} = \frac {10 \mathrm{mm}}{1000 \mathrm{mm}} = \frac {1}{100} \quad \text{It is also represented by 1:100}$$

Suppose a line of $10 \mathrm{mm}$ length in drawing represent $1 \mathrm{mm}$ length of object:

$$\mathrm{R.F.} = \frac {10 \mathrm{mm}}{1 \mathrm{mm}} = \frac {10}{1}$$
03

‚ûñ Plain Scales

A plain scale is simply a line which is divided into a suitable number of equal parts, the first of which part is further sub-divided into small parts. It is used to represent either two units.

‚úçÔ∏è

Example Problem

On a survey map the distance between two places 1 km apart is 5 cm. Construct the scale to read 4.6 km.

‚ñ∂ View Calculation & Construction Steps

1. Calculate Representative Fraction (R.F.):

$$R.F. = \frac{5 \text{ cm}}{1 \times 1000 \times 100 \text{ cm}} = \frac{1}{20000}$$

2. Calculate Drawing Size:

If X is the drawing size required:

$$X = 5 \times 1000 \times 100 \times \frac{1}{20000}$$

Therefore, $X = 25 \text{ cm}$

3. Construction Steps:

  1. Draw a line of length 25 cm.
  2. Divide this into 5 equal parts. Now each part is 1 km.
  3. Divide the first part into 10 equal divisions. Each division is 0.1 km.
  4. Mark on the scale the required distance 4.6 km.
04

üìê Diagonal Scales

Plain Scale Usage

Used to read lengths in two units such as meters and decimeters, centimeter and millimeters etc., or to read to the accuracy correct to first decimal.

Diagonal Scale Usage

Used to represent either three units of measurement such as metres, decimeters, centimeters or to read to the accuracy correct to two decimals.

Principle of Diagonal Scale

  • Draw a line AB and erect a perpendicular at B.
  • Mark 10 equal distant points (1, 2, 3 etc.) of any suitable length along this perpendicular and mark C.
  • Complete the rectangle ABCD.
  • Draw a diagonal BD.
  • Draw a horizontal through the division points to meet BD at 1', 2', 3, etc.
üí°

Key Concept

The line 1-1', 2-2' etc. measure 0.1CD, 0.2CD, etc. respectively. The line CD is divided into 1/10 the divisions by the diagonal BD, i.e. each horizontal lines is a multiple of 1/10CD.

Diagonal Scale Problem

Problem: On a plan, at line of 22 cm long represents a distance of 440 metres. Draw a diagonal scale for the plan to read up to a single metre. Measure and mark a distance of 187 m on the scale.

‚ñ∂ View Solution Steps

1. Calculate R.F.:

$$R.F. = \frac{22}{440 \times 100} = \frac{1}{2000}$$

2. Determine Drawing Size:

As 187 m are required, consider 200 m (next round figure suitable for division).

$$\text{Drawing size} = R.F. \times \text{actual size} = \frac{1}{2000} \times 200 \times 100 = 10 \text{ cm}$$

3. Construction:

  • When a length of 10 cm representing 200 m is divided into 5 equal parts, each part represents 40 m as marked in the figure.
  • The first part is sub-divided into 4 divisions, so that each division is 10 m.
  • On the diagonal portion 10 divisions are taken to get 1 m.
