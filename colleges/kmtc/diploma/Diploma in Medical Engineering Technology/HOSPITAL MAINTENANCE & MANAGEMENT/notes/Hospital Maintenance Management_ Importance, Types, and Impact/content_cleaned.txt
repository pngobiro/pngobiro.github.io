<div data-testid="stHeadingWithActionElements" class="st-emotion-cache-1p9ibxm e1icttdg2"><h1 level="1" id="hospital-maintenance-management">HOSPITAL MAINTENANCE MANAGEMENT<span data-testid="stHeaderActionElements" class="st-emotion-cache-gi0tri e1icttdg3"><a href="#hospital-maintenance-management" class="st-emotion-cache-yinll1 e1icttdg1"><svg xmlns="http://www.w3.org/2000/svg" width="16" height="16" viewBox="0 0 24 24" fill="none" stroke="currentColor" stroke-width="2" stroke-linecap="round" stroke-linejoin="round"><path d="M15 7h3a5 5 0 0 1 5 5 5 5 0 0 1-5 5h-3m-6 0H6a5 5 0 0 1-5-5 5 5 0 0 1 5-5h3"></path><line x1="8" y1="12" x2="16" y2="12"></line></svg></a></span></h1></div>
<div data-testid="stHeadingWithActionElements" class="st-emotion-cache-1p9ibxm e1icttdg2"><h2 level="2" id="definition-of-maintenance">Definition of maintenance<span data-testid="stHeaderActionElements" class="st-emotion-cache-gi0tri e1icttdg3"><a href="#definition-of-maintenance" class="st-emotion-cache-yinll1 e1icttdg1"><svg xmlns="http://www.w3.org/2000/svg" width="16" height="16" viewBox="0 0 24 24" fill="none" stroke="currentColor" stroke-width="2" stroke-linecap="round" stroke-linejoin="round"><path d="M15 7h3a5 5 0 0 1 5 5 5 5 0 0 1-5 5h-3m-6 0H6a5 5 0 0 1-5-5 5 5 0 0 1 5-5h3"></path><line x1="8" y1="12" x2="16" y2="12"></line></svg></a></span></h2></div>
<p>The following are some definitions of maintenance:</p>
<p>a) Maintenance is a set of activities which help keep the plant, machinery and other facilities in a good working condition.</p>
<p>b) Maintenance is a combination of actions carried out to retain an item/machine equipment/plant in order to restore it to an acceptable working condition.</p>
<div data-testid="stHeadingWithActionElements" class="st-emotion-cache-1p9ibxm e1icttdg2"><h2 level="2" id="scope-of-plant-maintenance">Scope of plant maintenance<span data-testid="stHeaderActionElements" class="st-emotion-cache-gi0tri e1icttdg3"><a href="#scope-of-plant-maintenance" class="st-emotion-cache-yinll1 e1icttdg1"><svg xmlns="http://www.w3.org/2000/svg" width="16" height="16" viewBox="0 0 24 24" fill="none" stroke="currentColor" stroke-width="2" stroke-linecap="round" stroke-linejoin="round"><path d="M15 7h3a5 5 0 0 1 5 5 5 5 0 0 1-5 5h-3m-6 0H6a5 5 0 0 1-5-5 5 5 0 0 1 5-5h3"></path><line x1="8" y1="12" x2="16" y2="12"></line></svg></a></span></h2></div>
<p>a) The maintenance of machines and equipment due to their frequent use.</p>
<p>b) Constant and continuous services such as cleaning, lubrication, repair and replacement of equipment of mechanical devices of machines so that their operational efficiency can be maintained.</p>
<p>c) New installation of equipment and building.</p>
<p>d) Generation and distribution of utilities.</p>
<p>e) Any other services concerning maintenance as delegated by management.</p>
<div data-testid="stHeadingWithActionElements" class="st-emotion-cache-1p9ibxm e1icttdg2"><h2 level="2" id="importance-of-maintenance">Importance of maintenance<span data-testid="stHeaderActionElements" class="st-emotion-cache-gi0tri e1icttdg3"><a href="#importance-of-maintenance" class="st-emotion-cache-yinll1 e1icttdg1"><svg xmlns="http://www.w3.org/2000/svg" width="16" height="16" viewBox="0 0 24 24" fill="none" stroke="currentColor" stroke-width="2" stroke-linecap="round" stroke-linejoin="round"><path d="M15 7h3a5 5 0 0 1 5 5 5 5 0 0 1-5 5h-3m-6 0H6a5 5 0 0 1-5-5 5 5 0 0 1 5-5h3"></path><line x1="8" y1="12" x2="16" y2="12"></line></svg></a></span></h2></div>
<p>a) It is responsible for the smooth and efficient working of the industrial plant and helps in improving the productivity.</p>
<p>b) It helps to keep the machines/equipment in their optimum operating condition.</p>
<p>c) It helps in maintaining and improving the operational efficiency of the plant facilities and hence contributes towards revenue by decreasing the operating cost.</p>
<p>d) Absence of plant maintenance may lead to frequent machine break down and failure of certain productive centres which in turn results to stoppage in production.</p>
<div data-testid="stHeadingWithActionElements" class="st-emotion-cache-1p9ibxm e1icttdg2"><h2 level="2" id="objective-of-maintenance">Objective of maintenance<span data-testid="stHeaderActionElements" class="st-emotion-cache-gi0tri e1icttdg3"><a href="#objective-of-maintenance" class="st-emotion-cache-yinll1 e1icttdg1"><svg xmlns="http://www.w3.org/2000/svg" width="16" height="16" viewBox="0 0 24 24" fill="none" stroke="currentColor" stroke-width="2" stroke-linecap="round" stroke-linejoin="round"><path d="M15 7h3a5 5 0 0 1 5 5 5 5 0 0 1-5 5h-3m-6 0H6a5 5 0 0 1-5-5 5 5 0 0 1 5-5h3"></path><line x1="8" y1="12" x2="16" y2="12"></line></svg></a></span></h2></div>
<p>a) Minimizing the loss of productive time because of equipment failure by maximizing the availability of plant/equipment/machine.</p>
<p>b) To extend the useful life of the plant/machinery by minimizing their wear and tear.</p>
<p>c) Minimizing the loss due to production stoppage.</p>
<p>d) To ensure operational readiness of all equipment needed for emergency purposes at all times such as fire fighting equipment.</p>
<p>e) To ensure safety of personnel and patients through regular inspection and maintenance of facilities e.g., boilers, compressors, material handling equipment etc.</p>
<p>f) To maximize efficiency and economy in production, by utilization of available facilities.</p>

<p>g) To improve the quality of products and to improve the productivity of the plant.
h) To minimize the total maintenance cost which may consist of the cost of repairs, cost of preventive maintenance and inventory costs of spares/material required for maintenance.</p>
<div data-testid="stHeadingWithActionElements" class="st-emotion-cache-1p9ibxm e1icttdg2"><h2 level="2" id="impact-of-poor-maintenance">Impact of poor maintenance<span data-testid="stHeaderActionElements" class="st-emotion-cache-gi0tri e1icttdg3"><a href="#impact-of-poor-maintenance" class="st-emotion-cache-yinll1 e1icttdg1"><svg xmlns="http://www.w3.org/2000/svg" width="16" height="16" viewBox="0 0 24 24" fill="none" stroke="currentColor" stroke-width="2" stroke-linecap="round" stroke-linejoin="round"><path d="M15 7h3a5 5 0 0 1 5 5 5 5 0 0 1-5 5h-3m-6 0H6a5 5 0 0 1-5-5 5 5 0 0 1 5-5h3"></path><line x1="8" y1="12" x2="16" y2="12"></line></svg></a></span></h2></div>
<p>Failure or breakdowns of machines and equipment have a direct impact on:</p>
<p>a) Production capacity of the system is reduced by machines idled by failures or breakdowns.
b) Production cost: poor maintenance leads to increase in production cost. Machine breakdowns increase the labour cost per unit due to idle labour. When machines breakdowns frequently, cost of maintenance which includes costs such as providing repair facilities, maintenance crew, and spare parts also increase.
c) Product and service quality: poor maintenance of equipment leads to production of low-quality products. Such equipment cannot provide satisfactory services to customers.
d) Workers or customer safety: poorly maintained equipment or worn-out equipment is likely to fail at any moment and such failure causes injuries to operators working on those machines.
e) Customer satisfaction: products often cannot be produced as per master production schedules when production equipment break down and work stoppage occur. This result in delayed delivery of products to customers hence decrease customer satisfaction.</p>
<div data-testid="stHeadingWithActionElements" class="st-emotion-cache-1p9ibxm e1icttdg2"><h2 level="2" id="function-of-maintenance">Function of maintenance<span data-testid="stHeaderActionElements" class="st-emotion-cache-gi0tri e1icttdg3"><a href="#function-of-maintenance" class="st-emotion-cache-yinll1 e1icttdg1"><svg xmlns="http://www.w3.org/2000/svg" width="16" height="16" viewBox="0 0 24 24" fill="none" stroke="currentColor" stroke-width="2" stroke-linecap="round" stroke-linejoin="round"><path d="M15 7h3a5 5 0 0 1 5 5 5 5 0 0 1-5 5h-3m-6 0H6a5 5 0 0 1-5-5 5 5 0 0 1 5-5h3"></path><line x1="8" y1="12" x2="16" y2="12"></line></svg></a></span></h2></div>
<p>a) The basic function of maintenance is to keep plant, machinery, equipment, system in a condition that will meet normal operating requirements.
b) To develop maintenance policies, procedures and standards for the plant maintenance system.
c) To schedule the maintenance work after due consultation with the concerned production department.
d) To carry out repairs and rectify or overhaul planned equipment/facilities for achieving the required level of availability and optimum operational efficiency.
e) To ensure scheduled inspection, lubrication, oil checking, and adjustment of plant machinery and equipment.</p>

<p>f) To document and maintain record of each maintenance activity (i.e., repairs, replacement, overhauls, modification and lubrication etc.)
g) To maintain and carry out repairs of building, utilities, material handling equipment and other service facilities such as electrical installations, sewers, central stores etc.
h) To carry out and facilitate periodic inspections of equipment and facilities to know their conditions related to their failure and stoppage.
i) To prepare inventory list of spare parts and materials required for maintenance.
j) To forecast the maintenance expenditure and prepare a budget and to ensure that maintenance expenditure is as per planned budget.
k) To recruit and train personnel to prepare the maintenance workforce for effective and efficient plant maintenance.
l) To implement safety standards as required for the use of specific equipment or certain categories of equipment such as boilers, overhead cranes, chemical plant etc.
m) To develop management information systems, to provide information to top management regarding the maintenance activities.
n) To monitor the equipment condition at regular intervals.</p>
<div data-testid="stHeadingWithActionElements" class="st-emotion-cache-1p9ibxm e1icttdg2"><h2 level="2" id="terminologies-used-in-maintenance">Terminologies used in maintenance<span data-testid="stHeaderActionElements" class="st-emotion-cache-gi0tri e1icttdg3"><a href="#terminologies-used-in-maintenance" class="st-emotion-cache-yinll1 e1icttdg1"><svg xmlns="http://www.w3.org/2000/svg" width="16" height="16" viewBox="0 0 24 24" fill="none" stroke="currentColor" stroke-width="2" stroke-linecap="round" stroke-linejoin="round"><path d="M15 7h3a5 5 0 0 1 5 5 5 5 0 0 1-5 5h-3m-6 0H6a5 5 0 0 1-5-5 5 5 0 0 1 5-5h3"></path><line x1="8" y1="12" x2="16" y2="12"></line></svg></a></span></h2></div>
<p>i. <strong>Maintenance schedule</strong>: A detailed/comprehensive list of all maintenance actions/operations and their incidence (when, where, who).
ii. <strong>Availability</strong>: Period for which a facility is in usable state.
iii. <strong>Down time</strong>: time for which facility is not available for use.
iv. <strong>Maintenance planning</strong>: establishing in advance the work to be done and the methods, tools labour, resources and time necessary.
v. <strong>History card</strong>: a document providing details regarding maintenance action to be taken on a particular item.
vi. <strong>Scheduling of maintenance work</strong>: timing and sequence of operation.
vii. <strong>Maintenance crew</strong>: inspectors, technicians, mechanics, electricians, cleaners, plumbers, carpenters, painters, welders, masons, etc.
viii. <strong>Facility</strong>: a complex of buildings, structures, roads and associated equipment, such as a hospital, school, shopping centre, etc., which represents a single management unit for financial, operational, maintenance or other purposes.</p>

<p>Maintenance policy of a productive system provides specific answer to problems concerned with:</p>
<p>a) The selection of specific components parts of a system for maintenance.
b) Decision regarding the specific forms of maintenance to be used.
c) A choice between internal and external maintenance, and
d) A further choice between centralized and decentralized maintenance in the case of internal maintenance.</p>
<p>Preventive maintenance policy is appropriate for items that wear out with time due to its use i.e., for items that show a normal failure mode. Primarily, maintenance policy must answer the questions of the extent of activities and the size of the maintenance department.</p>
<p>As far as the extent of activities is concerned, practices vary across companies. Small enterprises, for example, use the maintenance department for simple repair and replacement.</p>
<p>With regard to equipment maintenance two practices are commonly followed. One practice is to have a well planned and organized maintenance programme formulated to secure maximum life and utilization of machinery. The second practice is to adopt a policy of minimum maintenance and maximum wear. This practice is more economical in view of the fact that the equipment is usually superseded before it wears out.</p>
<p>Other issues which deserve consideration while framing an appropriate maintenance policy are:</p>
<p>(i) Construct out some maintenance work during peak periods to avoid excessive delays in future maintenance but avoid hiring temporary extra help.
(ii) Defer some maintenance work until slack periods so as to utilize work force during such periods.
(iii) Replace machine and equipment at the optimum time.</p>

<div data-testid="stHeadingWithActionElements" class="st-emotion-cache-1p9ibxm e1icttdg2"><h2 level="2" id="plant-maintenance-methods">PLANT MAINTENANCE METHODS<span data-testid="stHeaderActionElements" class="st-emotion-cache-gi0tri e1icttdg3"><a href="#plant-maintenance-methods" class="st-emotion-cache-yinll1 e1icttdg1"><svg xmlns="http://www.w3.org/2000/svg" width="16" height="16" viewBox="0 0 24 24" fill="none" stroke="currentColor" stroke-width="2" stroke-linecap="round" stroke-linejoin="round"><path d="M15 7h3a5 5 0 0 1 5 5 5 5 0 0 1-5 5h-3m-6 0H6a5 5 0 0 1-5-5 5 5 0 0 1 5-5h3"></path><line x1="8" y1="12" x2="16" y2="12"></line></svg></a></span></h2></div>
<p>1- Damage-based plant maintenance: The damage-based method of plant maintenance (also known as the breakdown or 'runt-to-failure' method) is characterized by the fact that no preventive maintenance is carried out. System components are installed with no PM outlay, worn out, and replaced completely in the event of a malfunction. It is carried out only under the following conditions:</p>
<ul>
<li>If the system components involved cost considerably less to acquire than to maintain.</li>
<li>If system components involved present no danger to either production or safety in the event of a breakdown, and can be repaired without significant outlay (e.g., without shutting down the system).</li>
<li>If the system components involved have a limited service life that it is neither feasible nor desirable to extend (no refurbishment).</li>
</ul>
<p>2- Time-based plant maintenance: With time-based (or periodic) plant maintenance, preventive maintenance of a system component is carried out regularly after a specific period of use. One advantage of this method is that regular preventive maintenance increases the service life of a system component. In some cases, statutory or safety regulations require proof of regular preventive maintenance (for example, fire extinguishers and automobiles are subject to inspection at regular intervals).
