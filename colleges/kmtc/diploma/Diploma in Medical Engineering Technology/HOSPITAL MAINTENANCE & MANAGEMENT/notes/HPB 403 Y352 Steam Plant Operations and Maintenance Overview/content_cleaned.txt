<p>3.) Grate - It is a platform, in the combustion chamber, upon which fuel (coal or wood) is burnt. The grate, generally, consists of cast iron bars which are spaced apart so that air (required combustion) can pass through them. The surface area of the grate, over which fire types place is called grate surface.</p>
<p>4.) Furnace - It is the space, above the grate and below the boiler-shell, in which the fuel is actually burnt.</p>
<p>5.) Heating Surface - It is the part of boiler surface, which is exposed to the fire (or hot laser from the fire).</p>
<p>6.) Mountings - These are fittings which are mounted on the boiler for its proper functioning. They include water level indicators, pressure gauge, safety valve etc. It may be noted that a boiler, can not function safely without the mountings.</p>
<p>7.) Accessories - These are devices, which form an integral part of a boiler, but are not mounted on it. They include super heater economizer, feed pump etc. It may be noted that the accessories help in controlling and running the boiler efficiently.</p>

<p>→ The purpose of installing steam traps is to obtain a fast heating of the product and equipment by keeping the steam lines and equipment free of condensate, air and other non-condensible gases.</p>
<p>→ A steam trap is a valve device that discharges condensate and air from the steam line without discharging steam (while trapping steam).</p>

<p>BOILER DRAUGHT → is the pressure difference which causes flaws of the boiler to take place.</p>
<p>→ It may be noted that the rate of steam generation, in a boiler depends upon the rate at which the fuel is burnt.
→ The rate of fuel burning depends upon the availability of oxygen or in other words availability of fresh air.
→ The fresh air will enter the fuel bed, if the gases of combustion are exhausted from the combustion chamber of the boiler.
→ This is possible only if a difference of pressure is maintained above and below the fire grate. This difference of pressure is known as draught.</p>
<p>The main objects of producing draught in a boiler are:
i) To provide an adequate supply of air for the fuel combustion.
ii) To exhaust the gases of combustion from the combustion chamber.
iii) To discharge these gases to the atmosphere through the chimney.</p>
<div data-testid="stHeadingWithActionElements" class="st-emotion-cache-1p9ibxm e1icttdg2"><h2 level="2" id="classifications-of-draughts">Classifications of draughts.<span data-testid="stHeaderActionElements" class="st-emotion-cache-gi0tri e1icttdg3"><a href="#classifications-of-draughts" class="st-emotion-cache-yinll1 e1icttdg1"><svg xmlns="http://www.w3.org/2000/svg" width="16" height="16" viewBox="0 0 24 24" fill="none" stroke="currentColor" stroke-width="2" stroke-linecap="round" stroke-linejoin="round"><path d="M15 7h3a5 5 0 0 1 5 5 5 5 0 0 1-5 5h-3m-6 0H6a5 5 0 0 1-5-5 5 5 0 0 1 5-5h3"></path><line x1="8" y1="12" x2="16" y2="12"></line></svg></a></span></h2></div>
<p>→ They are classified into two types:
i) Natural draught.
→ It is the draught produced by a chimney due to the difference of densities between the hot gases inside the chimney and cold atmospheric air outside it.</p>
<p>ii) Artificial draught.
→ May be mechanical draught or a steam jet draught.
→ The draught produced by a fan or blower is known as mechanical or fan draught where as the draught produced by a steam jet is called jet draught.
→ It is provided, when natural draught is not sufficient, it may be induced or forced.</p>
<div data-testid="stHeadingWithActionElements" class="st-emotion-cache-1p9ibxm e1icttdg2"><h2 level="2" id="types-of-draughts">Types of Draughts.<span data-testid="stHeaderActionElements" class="st-emotion-cache-gi0tri e1icttdg3"><a href="#types-of-draughts" class="st-emotion-cache-yinll1 e1icttdg1"><svg xmlns="http://www.w3.org/2000/svg" width="16" height="16" viewBox="0 0 24 24" fill="none" stroke="currentColor" stroke-width="2" stroke-linecap="round" stroke-linejoin="round"><path d="M15 7h3a5 5 0 0 1 5 5 5 5 0 0 1-5 5h-3m-6 0H6a5 5 0 0 1-5-5 5 5 0 0 1 5-5h3"></path><line x1="8" y1="12" x2="16" y2="12"></line></svg></a></span></h2></div>
<p>→ Draughts are of 3 types.
i) Chimney draught.
→ The draught produced by means of a chimney alone.</p>
<p>Downloaded by Patrick Ngobiro (<a href="mailto:patrick.ngobiro@student.moringaschool.com" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">patrick.ngobiro@student.moringaschool.com</a>)

<p>It is natural draught and has induced effect.
Since the atmospheric air is heavier than the hot gases, the outside air will flow through the furnace into the chimney.
It will push the hot gases to pass through the chimney.
The chimney draught varies with climatic conditions, temp of the furnace gases and height of chimney.</p>
<p>ii) Mechanical or fan draught.
The draught, produced by means of a fan or blower.
The fan is, generally, of centrifugal type and is driven by an electric motor.
In an induced fan draught, a centrifugal fan is placed in the path of the flue gases before they enter the chimney.
It draws the flue gases from the surface and forces them up through the chimney.
The action of this type of draught is similar to that of the natural draught.
In case of forced fan draught, the fan is placed before the gate, and air is forced into the gate through the closed ash pit.</p>
<p>iii) Steam jet draught
It is simple and cheap method of producing artificial draught.
The exhaust steam, from a non-condensing steam engine, is used for producing draught.
It is mostly used in locomotive boilers, where the exhaust steam from the engine cylinder is discharged through a blast pipe placed at the smoke box and below the chimney.
In an induced steam jet draught, the steam jet issuing from a nozzle, is placed in the chimney.
But in a forced steam jet draught, the steam jet issuing from a nozzle is placed in the ash pit under the fire grate of the furnace.</p>

<ol>
<li>It is more economical</li>
<li>It is better in control</li>
<li>The flow of air through the grate and furnace is uniform</li>
