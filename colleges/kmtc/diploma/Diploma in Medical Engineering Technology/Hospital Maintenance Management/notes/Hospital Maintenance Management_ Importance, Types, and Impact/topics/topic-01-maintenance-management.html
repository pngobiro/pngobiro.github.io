üìñ Definition of maintenance

The following are some definitions of maintenance:

  • Maintenance is a set of activities which help keep the plant, machinery and other facilities in a good working condition.
  • Maintenance is a combination of actions carried out to retain an item/machine equipment/plant in order to restore it to an acceptable working condition.
02

üî≠ Scope of plant maintenance

  • The maintenance of machines and equipment due to their frequent use.
  • Constant and continuous services such as cleaning, lubrication, repair and replacement of equipment of mechanical devices of machines so that their operational efficiency can be maintained.
  • New installation of equipment and building.
  • Generation and distribution of utilities.
  • Any other services concerning maintenance as delegated by management.
03

‚≠ê Importance of maintenance

  • It is responsible for the smooth and efficient working of the industrial plant and helps in improving the productivity.
  • It helps to keep the machines/equipment in their optimum operating condition.
  • It helps in maintaining and improving the operational efficiency of the plant facilities and hence contributes towards revenue by decreasing the operating cost.
  • Absence of plant maintenance may lead to frequent machine break down and failure of certain productive centres which in turn results to stoppage in production.
04

üéØ Objective of maintenance

  • Minimizing the loss of productive time because of equipment failure by maximizing the availability of plant/equipment/machine.
  • To extend the useful life of the plant/machinery by minimizing their wear and tear.
  • Minimizing the loss due to production stoppage.
  • To ensure operational readiness of all equipment needed for emergency purposes at all times such as fire fighting equipment.
  • To ensure safety of personnel and patients through regular inspection and maintenance of facilities e.g., boilers, compressors, material handling equipment etc.
  • To maximize efficiency and economy in production, by utilization of available facilities.
  • To improve the quality of products and to improve the productivity of the plant.
  • To minimize the total maintenance cost which may consist of the cost of repairs, cost of preventive maintenance and inventory costs of spares/material required for maintenance.
05

üìâ Impact of poor maintenance

Failure or breakdowns of machines and equipment have a direct impact on:

  • Production capacity of the system is reduced by machines idled by failures or breakdowns.
  • Production cost: poor maintenance leads to increase in production cost. Machine breakdowns increase the labour cost per unit due to idle labour. When machines breakdowns frequently, cost of maintenance which includes costs such as providing repair facilities, maintenance crew, and spare parts also increase.
  • Product and service quality: poor maintenance of equipment leads to production of low-quality products. Such equipment cannot provide satisfactory services to customers.
  • Workers or customer safety: poorly maintained equipment or worn-out equipment is likely to fail at any moment and such failure causes injuries to operators working on those machines.
  • Customer satisfaction: products often cannot be produced as per master production schedules when production equipment break down and work stoppage occur. This result in delayed delivery of products to customers hence decrease customer satisfaction.
06

‚öôÔ∏è Function of maintenance

  • The basic function of maintenance is to keep plant, machinery, equipment, system in a condition that will meet normal operating requirements.
  • To develop maintenance policies, procedures and standards for the plant maintenance system.
  • To schedule the maintenance work after due consultation with the concerned production department.
  • To carry out repairs and rectify or overhaul planned equipment/facilities for achieving the required level of availability and optimum operational efficiency.
  • To ensure scheduled inspection, lubrication, oil checking, and adjustment of plant machinery and equipment.
  • To document and maintain record of each maintenance activity (i.e., repairs, replacement, overhauls, modification and lubrication etc.)
  • To maintain and carry out repairs of building, utilities, material handling equipment and other service facilities such as electrical installations, sewers, central stores etc.
  • To carry out and facilitate periodic inspections of equipment and facilities to know their conditions related to their failure and stoppage.
  • To prepare inventory list of spare parts and materials required for maintenance.
  • To forecast the maintenance expenditure and prepare a budget and to ensure that maintenance expenditure is as per planned budget.
  • To recruit and train personnel to prepare the maintenance workforce for effective and efficient plant maintenance.
  • To implement safety standards as required for the use of specific equipment or certain categories of equipment such as boilers, overhead cranes, chemical plant etc.
  • To develop management information systems, to provide information to top management regarding the maintenance activities.
  • To monitor the equipment condition at regular intervals.
07

üó£Ô∏è Terminologies used in maintenance

  • Maintenance schedule: A detailed/comprehensive list of all maintenance actions/operations and their incidence (when, where, who).
  • Availability: Period for which a facility is in usable state.
  • Down time: time for which facility is not available for use.
  • Maintenance planning: establishing in advance the work to be done and the methods, tools labour, resources and time necessary.
  • History card: a document providing details regarding maintenance action to be taken on a particular item.
  • Scheduling of maintenance work: timing and sequence of operation.
  • Maintenance crew: inspectors, technicians, mechanics, electricians, cleaners, plumbers, carpenters, painters, welders, masons, etc.
  • Facility: a complex of buildings, structures, roads and associated equipment, such as a hospital, school, shopping centre, etc., which represents a single management unit for financial, operational, maintenance or other purposes.
08

üîß Maintenance actions

  • Replace: to remove an unserviceable (worn out) item and install a serviceable counterpart in its place.
  • Repair: application of maintenance actions to identify troubles and restore an item by correcting specific damage, fault, malfunction, or failure in a part.
  • Overhaul: That maintenance effort (service/action) prescribed to restore an item to a completely serviceable/operational condition as required by maintenance standards in appropriate technical publications.
  • Rebuild: It consists of those services/actions necessary for the restoration of unserviceable equipment to a like new condition in accordance with original manufacturing standards. Recondition and renovation are also used for rebuild.
  • Service: operations required periodically to keep an item in proper operating condition i.e., to clean (includes decontaminate, when required), to preserve, to drain, to paint, or to replenish fuel, lubricants, chemical fluids, or gases.
  • Inspect: To determine the serviceability of an item by comparing its physical, mechanical, and/or electrical characteristics with established standards through examinations.
  • Test: To verify serviceability by measuring the mechanical or electrical characteristics of an item and compare those characteristics with prescribed standards.
  • Adjust: To maintain, within prescribed limits, by bringing into proper or exact or desired performance.
  • Calibrate: To determine and cause corrections to be made or to be adjusted on instruments or test, measuring, and diagnostic equipment used in precision measurement.
  • Install: The act of emplacing, seating or fixing into position an item, part, or module (component or assembly) in a manner to allow the proper functioning of an equipment or system.

üí∞ ECONOMIC ASPECTS OF MAINTENANCE

The main goal of maintenance department is to make available the plant, equipment and machinery for production with minimum possible wastage and with minimum total cost involved. Total cost includes maintenance labour cost, material cost etc.

Figure 9.2 above illustrates the total maintenance cost which has been optimized by equating various direct and indirect maintenance costs.

There are various mathematical relations to evaluate maintenance in performance in numerical terms, either as a single overall factor or as a series of factors.

A simple and important efficiency index may be expressed by:

$$E = \frac{K}{mT_m + nt + CW}$$

Where

  • E = maintenance efficiency index
  • K = a constant such that value of expression is 100 for base year
  • m = total cost of maintenance in the base year
  • n = total cost of lost time due to maintenance in the base year
  • C = total cost of waste materials (scrap) in the base year.
  • Tm = total cost of maintenance expressed as percentage of the replacement value of the plant and equipment.
  • t = down time due to maintenance operation expressed as a percentage of the scheduled production hours.
  • W = materials wastage due to maintenance operation expressed as percentage of total output at that stage of the process.
