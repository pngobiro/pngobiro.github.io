üî© Boiler Mountings

These are the fittings, which are mounted on the boiler for its proper and safe functioning. Though there are many types of boiler mountings, yet the following are important from the subject point of view: they are:

  1. Water Level Indicators.
    It is an important fitting, which indicates water level inside the boiler to an observer. It is a safety device, upon which the correct working of the boiler depends. This fitting may be seen in front of the boiler and are generally [two] in number.
  2. Pressure gauge.
    A pressure gauge is used to measure of the steam inside the steam boiler. It is fixed in front of the steam boiler. The pressure gauge generally used are of Bourdon type.
  3. Safety Valve.
    Pressure, relative to water, pressure reducing valve → These are devices attached to the steam chest for preventing explosions due to excessive internal pressure of steam. A steam boiler is usually provided with [two] safety valves. These are directly placed on the boiler. In brief, the function of a safety valve is to blow off the steam when the pressure of steam inside the boiler exceeds the working pressure.
  4. Stop Valve.
    The principal functions of a stop valve are:-
    • To control the flow of steam from the boiler to the main steam pipe.
    • To shut off the steam completely when required. (during maintenance of the boiler)
  5. Blow off cock.
    The principal functions of a blow off cock are:-
    • To empty the boiler whenever required.
    • To discharge the mud, scale or sediment which are accumulated at the bottom of the boiler.
  6. Feed check valve. (one directional flow valve)
    It is a non-return valve, fitted to a screwed spindle to regulate the lift. It's function is to regulate the supply of water, which is pumped into the boiler, by the feed pump. This valve must have its spindle, lifted before the pump is started. It is fitted to the shell slightly below the normal water level of the boiler.
  7. Fusible plug.
    It is fitted to the crown plate of the furnace or the fire. It's function is to put off the fire in the furnace of the boiler, when the level of water in the boiler falls to an unsafe limit, and thus avoids the explosion which may take place due to overheating of the furnace plate.

üöÄ Boiler Accessories

These are devices which are used as integral parts of a boiler, and help in running efficiently. Though these are many types of boiler accessories, yet the following are important from the point of view.

  1. Feed Pump.
    • Natural draught → Water gets freely in the boiler.
    • Forced draught → Water is free inside the boiler + it is

    We know that water in a boiler is continuously converted into steam which is used by the engine. Thus we need a feed pump to deliver water to the boiler.

    The pressure of steam inside the boiler is high. So the pressure of feed water has to be increased proportionately before it is made to enter the boiler. Generally, the pressure of feed water is 20% more than that in the boiler.

    A feed pump may be of centrifugal type or reciprocating type. But a double acting reciprocating pump is commonly used as a feed pump these days. The reciprocating pumps are run by the steam from the same boiler in which water is to be fed.
  2. Super heater.
    • Is an important device of a steam generating unit. Its purpose is to increase the temp of saturated steam without raising in pressure.

    It is generally an integral part of a boiler and is placed in the path of hot flue gases from the furnace. The heat given up by these flue gases, is used in superheating the steam. Such super heaters, which are installed within the boiler, are known as integral super heaters.
  3. Economizer
    → Is a device used to meet feed water by utilizing the heat in the exhaust flue gases before leaving through the chimney.
    → As the name indicates, the device improves the economy of a steam boiler.
  4. Air Pre-heater
    → Is used to recover heat from the exhaust flue gases. It is installed from the economizer and the chimney. The air required for the purpose of combustion is drawn through the pre-heater - where its temp is raised.
    → It is then passed through ducts to the furnace. The air is passed through the tubes of the heater internally while the hot flue gases are passed over the outside of the tubes.
  5. Injector
    → Is a device used to feed the water into the boiler.
  6. Soot blower
    → Pressure reducing valve (pressure regulator) internal.
