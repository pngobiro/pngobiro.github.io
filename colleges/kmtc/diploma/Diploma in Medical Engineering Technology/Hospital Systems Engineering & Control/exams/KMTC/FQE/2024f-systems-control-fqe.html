3.
Which of the following represents an illustration of a closed-loop system?
A. Automatic electric iron
B. Automatic washing machine
C. Bread toaster
D. Electric hand drier
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A. Automatic electric iron

Explanation: An automatic electric iron uses a thermostat to sense temperature and switch the heating element on/off to maintain the set temperature. This feedback mechanism makes it a closed-loop system. Washing machines and toasters typically run on preset timers (Open Loop).

4.
Which of the following basic control circuit components initiates another device to open or close?
A. Contactor
B. Relay
C. Circuit-breaker
D. Motor
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A. Contactor (or B. Relay)

Explanation: Both relays and contactors are used to switch circuits. However, contactors are specifically designed to switch high power loads (like motors/heaters) and are "initiated" by a control signal to open/close power contacts.

5.
The output of the feedback control system must be a function of:
A. Feedback signal and the input
B. Feedback signal and the output
C. Reference input signal and input signal
D. Reference input signal and output signal
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A. Feedback signal and the input (Reference)

Explanation: In a closed-loop system, the actuating error signal (which drives the output) is the difference between the Reference Input and the Feedback Signal.

8.
The transfer function of two blocks in parallel is obtained by
A. Sum of individual gain
B. Addition of individual gain
C. Subtraction of individual gain
D. Multiplication of individual gain
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A. Sum of individual gain (or B)

10.
Which of the following occurs without change in the internal energy?
A. Isochoric process
B. Isenthalpic process
C. Steady state process
D. Isentropic process
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C. Steady state process (or Isothermal for ideal gas)

Explanation: For an ideal gas, Internal Energy ($U$) depends only on temperature. An Isothermal process ($\Delta T=0$) implies $\Delta U=0$. Usually, "Steady state" implies properties don't change with time, but Isothermal is the specific thermodynamic process. Given options, Isenthalpic is constant enthalpy. Isentropic is constant entropy. If the system is cyclic, $\Delta U=0$. In many texts, free expansion or isothermal processes are the key examples. Here, C is the most general "no change" state.

11.
What is specific heat capacity of a substance?
A. Heat required to convert a solid in to a liquid
B. Act or science of measuring the change in state variable of a body
C. Heat quantity of a sample of a substance divided by the mass of the sample
D. Heat gained by a substance is equal to the heat lost by a substance
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C. Heat quantity... (Approximation of definition)

Explanation: Specific Heat Capacity is the heat energy required to raise the temperature of a unit mass of a substance by one degree Kelvin ($Q = mc\Delta T$). Option C implies Heat per Mass ($Q/m$), which relates to the specific nature, though it misses the "per degree temp change" part.

12.
Pressure volume temperature relationship for isobaric process is
A. $T_2/T_1 = P_2/P_1$
B. $T_2/T_1 = V_2/V_1$
C. $P_2/P_1 = V_1/V_2$
D. $P_1/V_1 = P_2/V_2$
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B. $T_2/T_1 = V_2/V_1$ (Charles's Law)

Explanation: In an Isobaric process, Pressure is constant ($P=C$). From ideal gas law $PV/T = C$, if $P$ is constant, then $V/T = C$, so $V_1/T_1 = V_2/T_2$ or $T_2/T_1 = V_2/V_1$.

13.
Which of the following happens in adiabatic process?
A. Heat is exchanged between system and surrounding and temperature remains constant
B. Volume remains constant and system does not do any work
C. No heat is exchanged between system and there is work done
D. Volume is maintained constant as temperature changes
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C. No heat is exchanged between system and there is work done

Explanation: An adiabatic process is one where there is no heat transfer into or out of the system ($dQ = 0$). Work is done at the expense of internal energy.

14.
What is thermal efficiency as applied in second law of thermodynamics?
A. Ratio of required input to the desired result
B. Ratio of desired result to required input
C. Difference between required input and desired result
D. Ratio of desired output to the desired results
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B. Ratio of desired result to required input

Explanation: Thermal efficiency ($\eta$) = Desired Output (Work Done) / Required Input (Heat Supplied). $\eta = W_{out} / Q_{in}$.

15.
A steam power plant produces 50mw of network while burning fuel to produce 150mw of heat energy at the high temperature. Determine the cycle thermal efficiency and the heat ejected by the cycle to the surroundings.
A. 33% and 150mw
B. 33% and 100mw
C. 25% and 150mw
D. 25% and 100mw
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B. 33% and 100mw

Explanation:
Efficiency $\eta = \frac{\text{Work Output}}{\text{Heat Input}} = \frac{50}{150} = 0.333$ or $33.3\%$.
Heat Ejected ($Q_{out}$) = Heat Input ($Q_{in}$) - Work ($W$) = $150 - 50 = 100\text{ MW}$.

16.
What is the ideal gas law at standard pressure?
A. $P_1V_1 = P_2V_2$
B. $V = RT$
C. $PV/nT = R$
D. $P = KT$
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C. $PV/nT = R$ (Rearrangement of $PV=nRT$)

Explanation: The Ideal Gas Law is $PV = nRT$. Rearranging gives $R = PV/nT$. Option B is specific volume equation if $m=1$. Option A is Boyle's Law.

17.
The measure of energy dispersal in a thermodynamic system is?
A. Entropy

C. Working substance

Correct Answer: A. Entropy

Explanation: Entropy is often described as a measure of the disorder or randomness of a system, or the dispersal of energy.

41.

a) Explain the following contacts as used in basic control circuits (3 marks)
i. Normally open contacts
ii. Normally closed contact
iii. Change-over contacts

b) Which principle of magnetism does a relay work (2 marks)

Show Answer

a) Contacts:

  • Normally Open (NO): Contacts that are open (no current flow) when the device is de-energized, and close when energized.
  • Normally Closed (NC): Contacts that are closed (current flows) when the device is de-energized, and open when energized.
  • Change-over (SPDT): A combination having a common terminal that switches connection from an NC contact to an NO contact upon actuation.

b) Principle:

Relays work on the principle of Electromagnetism. When current flows through a coil, it generates a magnetic field that attracts a ferrous armature, mechanically moving the contacts.

42.
Discuss the function of the following as used in steam generators (5 marks)
i. Safety valve
ii. Fusible gauge (plug)
iii. Pressure gauge
iv. Blow off cock
v. Feed pump
Show Answer
  • Safety Valve: Releases excess steam pressure to prevent boiler explosion if pressure exceeds safe limits.
  • Fusible Plug: Melts to allow steam into the furnace to extinguish the fire if the water level drops dangerously low (preventing dry firing).
  • Pressure Gauge: Indicates the internal steam pressure of the boiler.
  • Blow Off Cock: Drains water and sludge/sediments from the bottom of the boiler.
  • Feed Pump: Pumps water into the boiler to maintain the working water level against the boiler pressure.
43.

a) Distinguish between a sensor and a transducer (3 marks)

b) Explain load cell as a primary transducer (2 marks)

Show Answer

a) Difference:

A Sensor detects a physical change (e.g., temperature, light) and produces a signal (often non-electrical initially, like mercury expansion). A Transducer converts one form of energy into another (typically physical to electrical). All electrical sensors are transducers, but not all transducers are sensors (e.g., actuators). *Simple distinction: Sensor senses; Transducer converts.*

b) Load Cell:

A load cell is a transducer that converts force (weight) into an electrical signal. As a primary transducer (elastic member), it deforms under load (strain), which is then detected by secondary transducers (strain gauges) to change resistance.

45.
Describe the following terms as applied in steam system (5 marks)
i. Inducer draft
ii. Water hammer
iii. Thermocompressor
iv. Economizer
v. Draft
Show Answer
  • i. Induced Draft: A fan located at the chimney base that sucks flue gases out of the furnace, creating negative pressure to draw air in.
  • ii. Water Hammer: A pressure surge caused by steam coming into contact with condensate, causing rapid condensation and a vacuum that slams water into pipes/fittings.
  • iii. Thermocompressor: A steam jet ejector that compresses low-pressure steam to a higher pressure for reuse using high-pressure motive steam.
  • iv. Economizer: A heat exchanger that preheats feed water using waste heat from flue gases to improve efficiency.
  • v. Draft: The pressure difference between the furnace and the atmosphere that causes air/flue gases to flow through the boiler.

Using block diagram reduction, find the transfer function of the system whose block diagram is shown in the figure below. (5 marks)
Show Answer

Analysis:

The diagram typically contains a forward path $G_1, G_3$ and feedback loops $H_1, H_2$.

  1. Identify Inner Loop: Block $G_3$ usually has a feedback loop $H_2$. Transfer function $T_1 = \frac{G_3}{1 \pm G_3H_2}$.
  2. Combine with Series Block: If $G_1$ is in series with $T_1$, then $T_2 = G_1 \cdot T_1$.
  3. Identify Outer Loop: Feedback $H_1$ around the whole system.
  4. Final Transfer Function: $TF = \frac{T_2}{1 + T_2H_1}$.

a) Highlight six faults in steam boilers equipment indicating the cause and corrective action as appropriate (12 marks)

b) Indicate the elements of a steam trap by aid of neat sketch and indicate the direction of condensate flow (8 marks)

Show Answer

a) Steam Boiler Faults

Fault Cause Corrective Action
Priming/Foaming High dissolved solids, oil/grease in water. Blowdown boiler, check water treatment.
Scale Formation Hard water (Ca/Mg salts). Use water softener, descale boiler.
Low Water Level Feed pump failure, leakages. Check pump, fix leaks, test level controls.
Corrosion Dissolved Oxygen or CO2 (Acidity). Deaerate feed water, add chemical scavengers.
Flame Failure Blocked nozzle, faulty photo-cell. Clean nozzle/filters, replace sensor.
Black Smoke Insufficient air, dirty burner. Adjust air/fuel ratio, clean burner.
