Normal saline:

0.85 gms of sodium chloride in 100 mls of distilled water.

Free from contamination of bacteria, metal, detergents and silicones
It must not be stored for long periods in glass /metal containers /non- -sterilized containers. It may get moulds and pH changes.
Storage containers should be washed frequently.

-It should always be used according to manufacturer's direction.
-Final concentration for albumin during testing should be about $12 \%$

‚úÇÔ∏è Enzymes

There are many proteolytic enzymes which have been used in serological tests.
Enzymes are organic compounds that catalyze the digestion of the R.B.C membrane. It is said to be effective because they lower the negative surface charge and thus the Zeta potential on the R.B.C by digesting the red cell membrane and removing the Sialic acid residues. Thus there is altering of the structure of the cell agglutins such that they are exposed.
NB. Receptors (antigens) were originally inside the cell membrane
There are different types of enzymes used in artificial serological reactions

  • Papain $\_\_\_\_$ Pawpaw
  • Trypsin $\_\_\_\_$ Pancrease /stomach of the pig
  • Bromeline $\_\_\_\_$ pineapples
  • Ficin $\_\_\_\_$ fig tree

Properties of enzymes

  • Speeds up the Ag - Ab reaction
  • Increase the specificity of antigen
  • Increase the sensitivity of a reaction
  • Increase the avidity of antigen (power of agglutination rapidly and strongly)

Disadvantages:

Some sera cause pan - agglutination of enzyme treated cells due to cells containing auto antibodies against enzymes treated cells which causes agglutination of all the cells Some enzymes cause damage to the mucous membrane e.g. Ficin.
Some antigens are destroyed by enzymes

Sorensen's buffer:

Composition
- $\mathrm{KH}_{2} \mathrm{PO}_{4}$ (anhydrous) (potassium dihydrogen phosphate)
- $\mathrm{Na}_{2} \mathrm{HPO}_{4}$ (Disodium Hydrogen Phosphate)
- $\mathrm{D} \mathrm{H}_{2} \mathrm{O}$ Distilled water

Preparation

Solution A: Take 9.08 g of $\mathrm{KH}_{2} \mathrm{PHO}_{4}$. Make it to one litre using D . $\mathrm{H}_{2} \mathrm{O}$
Solution B: Take 9.47 g of $\mathrm{Na}_{2} \mathrm{HPO}_{4}$ and make it to one litre using D. $\mathrm{H}_{2} \mathrm{O}$
-If not to be used put in refrigerator at $4^{\circ}\mathrm{C}$
- -When needed for use, warm to room temperature
- -Add in a ratio of $96 \mathrm{mls}: 4 \mathrm{mls}$ Solution A & Solution B respectively (24:1)
- -The pH is 5.5
- -Prepare PBS (Phosphate Buffered Saline) by taking 10 mls of Buffer and 90 mls saline. Final pH should be 5.5

Hendry's buffer

Composition
- $\mathrm{NaH}_{2} \mathrm{PO}_{4}$ Sodium dihydrogen phosphate
- $\mathrm{Na}_{2} \mathrm{HPO}_{4}$ Disodium hydrogen phosphate

Preparation
-Dilute 0.514 g of $\mathrm{NaH}_{2} \mathrm{PO}_{4}$ to 100 mls D.H. 20
- -Take 0.445 g of $\mathrm{Na}_{2} \mathrm{HPO}_{4}$ and make it to 100 mls with D.H20
- -Mix in a ratio of $20 \mathrm{mls}: 80 \mathrm{mls} \mathrm{NaH}_{2} \mathrm{PO}_{4}$ & $\mathrm{Na}_{2} \mathrm{HPO}_{4}$ (1:4)
- -The pH is 7.3-7.5
- Prepare PBS by mixing 1:9 with normal saline
- Final ph 7.3-7.5

‚ñ∂ BROMELIN Preparation & Method

Preparation

Add 90 mls of saline to 10 mls of Sorensen's M/15 Buffer ( pH 5.5 ) to 0.5 g of bromeline powder. If pH is not 5.5 add 0.1 ml of HCL 0.5 N to adjust it
-Add 0.1 of Sodium azide /preservative against bacteria and fungi.
-Dispense into small bottles the solution can also be stable at $4^{\circ}\mathrm{C}$ for one month it can
Can be stable for 4-5 months if put at $-20^{\circ}\mathrm{C}$ and below

Method

Label two test tubes test/control
Add two drops of patient's serum in the two test tubes
Add two drops of saline to donor's unwashed cells 2-4%
Add one drop of bromeline solution
Add one drop of saline donor's unwashed bromelinised cells to the two test tubes
Mix and incubate at room temp for 15minutes centrifuge and examine for agglutination or haemolysis
If there is no reaction, incubate at $37^{\circ}\mathrm{C}$ for 15 min centrifuge and examine for agglutination or haemolysis
If still there's no reaction wash the cells three times, add anti human globulin centrifuge and examine
(This test is known as one stage technique)

‚ñ∂ FICIN Method

Method

Label two test tubes test / Auto control
Add 9 drops of 2% saline suspension of donor's cells to the tube labelled Test

Add 9 drops of 2% saline suspension of recipient's cells to the tube labelled control
Add one drop of 1% Ficin solution to each tube -mix and incubate at $37^{\circ}\mathrm{C}$ for 15 min
After incubation wash the cells once with saline and decant
Re-suspend with saline to 2% cell suspension
Place two drops of recipient's serum to two labelled tubes
Add one drop of ficinised cells to each tube (corresponding)
Mix and incubate at $37^{\circ}\mathrm{C}$ for 15 minutes and examine for agglutination
If no agglutination change to A.H.G Test
The test is known as 2 stage technique

‚ñ∂ TRYPSIN (2 Stages Technique)

-This method incorporates use of Buffered saline
-Take one volume of Sorensen's buffer and add to 9 volumes of isotonic saline
-Prepare 2% cell suspension of both donors and recipient washed cells
-Add one drop of 1% buffered Trypsin solution to 10 drops of 2% cell suspension. Mix and incubate at $37^{\circ}\mathrm{C}$ for 10 min Take back the trypsinised cells and re-suspend

Method

-Label test /control
-Add two drops of patient's recipient serum to each tube
-Add two drops of donor's trypsinised cells suspension to tube labelled test
-Add two drops of recipient trypsinised cells to tube labelled control
-Incubate at $37^{\circ}\mathrm{C}$ for 10 min / after mixing
- Centrifuge, examine for agglutination / /haemolysis
-If no reaction convert to A.H.G

‚ñ∂ PAPAIN Method

-The technique for Papain is excellent for detection of Rh antibodies. It does however detect cold antibodies e.g. Anti P, Lewis, Anti H.
-It can also detect auto antibodies it can both react R.T and $37^{\circ}\mathrm{C}$

Method

-Prepare 4% both donors and recipient cells
-Label two test tubes Test and control
-Add 2 drops of 4% saline suspension of donor's cells to tube labelled test/and 2 drops of recipient's 4% saline suspension cells to tube labelled control
-Add a drop of Papain solution in each test-tube
-Mix and incubate at $37^{\circ}\mathrm{C}$ for 15 min
-Add two drops of recipient sera to two test tubes
-Incubate at R.T /$37^{\circ}\mathrm{C}$ for 15 min
-Centrifuge and examine for agglutination or haemolysis
-If negative converts to A.H.G test

‚ö° Albumin & Ionic Cloud

ALBUMIN:

Def: Protein used as diluent in blood typing and a potentiator solution in serologic testing to enhance ag/ab reaction.
Albumin is produced from healthy cattle (bovine). It is then standardized for avidity / concentration. Normally the concentration is 20% bovine albumin in ab/ag reaction
-The albumen is usually supplied as 30% thus it has to be diluted in a ratio of 1:2 It can be diluted by use of human AB serum or Normal Saline
-Part of AB serum is added to 2 parts of bovine albumen
which gives 20% of bovine albumen concentration required for ab/ag reaction
30% conc. is not used. If we use 20% it is easy to make -ve reactions. If conc. was high 30% it would be hard to know whether the reaction was -ve

Uses (characteristics)

Minimizes tendency of Rouleaux formation
Composition of commercial bovine albumen
Sodium Azide -preservative
Caprylate: - it prevents over depolymerization of the albumin
NB. Albumin is a protein -protein is amino acid
Two amino acids make a polymer /peptide
Peptides form proteins: depolymerization is breaking up of peptide bonds thus hard for reaction.
The albumin is a colloidal medium for the ag/ab reaction. It is used in blood transfusion to increase the di-electric constant. It thus reduces the Zeta potential for ag-ab reaction. If Zeta potential is reduced the distance between cells in minimized and thus agglutination.
The force of repulsion between two cells can be reduced in two ways
Altering the media in which the cells are suspended during testing e.g. by using albumin. Always follow the manufacturer's direction while using product like albumin

IONIC CLOUD:

-It is a cloud of ions that surrounds each cell. If r.b.c's are suspended in saline and looked in a microscope, they appear to touch each other when viewed but they are not. This is because they are surrounded by ionic cloud.
(DIAGRAMME)
-Red cells have -ve electrostatic charges on their surface. The negative charge is due to the ionization of carboxyl group (-COOH) of the cyalic acid present on the surface (membrane of R.B.C) - which attracts positive ions from the surrounding media - e.g. Sodium from N. Saline

The positive layer attracted by the -ve layer subsequently attracts negatively charged ions to its surface e.g. Chloride ions. This process continues in layers until there is no more sufficient force to attract more ions to the cell-thus equilibrium is reached. The outer edge is then known as the surface of shear or slipping plane after reaching equilibrium
This is the effective surface of the cell in relation to the approach of another cell. When the slipping plane of one cell approaches the S.P of another the likely charges repel because they have the same conditions /they oppose any motion towards each other. Unless these forces are counteracted the cells won't come close together even in the presence of an antibody. Since the IgM molecules are large enough they will be able to strangle the cloud and cause reaction.
Since the IgG molecules are small they are unable to join the two receptors (reacting sites) and they just block them (sensitize the red blood cells). Thus the cells are suspended in bovine albumen to / decrease Zeta potential.

‚ö†Ô∏è Sources of Error in Grouping

  • Failure to add necessary reagents
  • Use of expired reagents (potency lost -false negative)
  • Use of dirty glass ware (pipettes - T.T Slides )
  • Failure to incubate at $37^{\circ}\mathrm{C}$ when using AHG
  • Use of low cell suspension -More anti-sera used: Prozone
  • Use of high cell suspension (false -ve)
  • Clerical error in reporting
  • Contamination of blood e.g. Bacteria (cloudy serum)
  • Patient with Kalaazar and Multiple myeloma don't have factors. It is difficult to use the serum for typing
  • Patients administered with Dextran 40 / Rouleaux formation -(False positive)
  • Excessive centrifugation (False positive) cells pack tightly can also cause haemolysis thus when shaken they don't disintegrate
  • Use of haemolysed blood specimen (false negative)
  • Failure to follow manufactures instructions.
