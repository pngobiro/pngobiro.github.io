üíâ TYPES OF IMMUNIZATION

Immunization is a process by which antibodies are produced in response to antigenic stimulus. It involves the immune mechanism. There are three types of immunization

Iso-immunization

It is the production of antibodies after the antigenic simulation of a member of the same species e.g. A person who is blood group A-negative is transfused with person with A-positive he's going to be stimulated and produces antibodies against D antigens. Antibodies involved in Isoimmunization are iso-agglutinins. They are also referred to as Allo-agglutinins.

Hetero-immunization

It is the production of antibodies after antigenic stimulation from different species like in Coombs reagent production. Hybrid-rabbits are injected with human serum (Immunoglobulins) and the rabbit produces anti human globulin (antibodies) otherwise called AHG or Coomb's reagent. Antibody involved in hetero immunization is hetero-agglutinin.

Auto Immunization

It is the production of antibodies against ones cells auto haemolytic reaction. This is due to:
Malfunction of the thymus glands
Patients with auto haemolytic anaemia
They can cause problem during blood transfusion. The anti body which reacts with the individual's antigen is referred to as auto-antibody

üêá ANTI HUMAN GLOBULIN (COOMBS) SERA

Preparation

There are several animals used for preparation of A.H.G. The rabbit is the most commonly used.
It can also be prepared from goats and sheep. Not all strains of rabbits are good for A.H.G preparation. Brown rabbit (Hybrid-type) are mostly used
This is achieved by use of purified human sera e.g. -Gamma globulins
-Beta globulin
Adjuvants are the compounds used to increase the production of A.H.G.

Examples of adjuvants -

  • Ammonium sulphate (saturated or $40 \%$)-
  • Freud's Adjuvant,
  • Sodium alignate.

Human group O serum pooled from several donors is used. The rabbit is injected with purified gamma globulin or human Beta globulin in either one of the adjuvants. The rabbit is normally injected in the peritoneal area (cavity area). Afterward Calcium chloride ( $\mathrm{Cacl}_{2}$ ) is injected and the area is massaged. Massaging is done to prevent swelling. It also helps adjuvant mixed to flow properly.
The A.H.G can be prepared from separate colonies. Beta globulin or globulin can be used. One colony is injected with human Gamma globulin. The other colony is injected with human Beta globulin. It produces antibodies specific for B-globulin e.g. compliment-component (reacts with compliment component). The two are blended to come up with poly-specific reagent
-When a reagent is prepared separately it is known as mono-specific. If combined together they are referred to as poly-specific or broad-spectrum A.H.G. Broad spectrum A.H.G detects both immunoglobulins and compliment-components.

Principle behind A.H.G.

When the anti-sera is added to the sensitized cells (cells attached with antibodies) the rabbits an-ti-IgG molecules attach their antigen binding pieces to the F.C.P's (fragment crystallization pieces) of the coated cells resulting to agglutination.
In a similar manner the rabbit anti-compliment attaches to the compliment-component and since the compliment is attached to the red cell haemolysis occurs.
Anti human globulin doesn't react with antigen it just forms bridges for agglutination to the sensitized red blood cells.

üî¨ COOMBS TESTS

Any method using Coomb's (AHG) reagent is Coomb's test.
They are of two major types.

  • Direct Coomb's test
  • Indirect Coomb's test

DIRECT COOMB'S TEST

It is a test used for detection of sensitized cells in vivo. Under normal circumstances there are no sensitized cells found in the body.
Sensitized red blood cells are fond in;

  • Children born with H.D.N.B (Haemolytic disease of the new born).
  • Patients with Haemolytic transfusion reactions (HTRs) Also
  • Auto immune haemolytic anaemia AIHA
  • Acquired haemolytic anaemia:

Procedure

Label test tube Direct Coomb's
Put two drops of test cells in a clean grouping tube $8 \mathrm{mmx} 75 \mathrm{~mm}$
Wash the test cells with large amounts of normal saline for about three times.
Discard the last wash of N. saline completely
Add two drops of A.H.G.
Mix and centrifuge at 1000 rpm for 1 min (3000 for 20 seconds)
Examine for agglutination/haemolysis
Record results:
If agglutination record Direct Coomb's test positive
If no agglutination record Direct Coomb's test -ve
Set positive and -ve control
In positive control we use coated (sensitized red cells). Prepared by diluting AntiD 1:20 with Normal-saline and making 5% cell suspension using this solution.

INDIRECT COOMB'S TEST:

The test is used to detect the presence of sensitized cells in vitro. Therefore it is used to detect antibodies in serum using known antigens e.g. O-positive pooled cells or panel cells or reagent cells.

Significance

Cross -match to detect incompatibility
Detection and identification of irregular antibodies (immune antibodies)

Indirect Coomb's test has three stages

  • Sensitization
  • Washing cells to free from all globulin
  • Mixing washed cells with A.H.G

Procedure

Label test tube as I.C. (Indirect Coomb's)
Make a 4% cell suspension of O positive pooled cells
Add two drops of cell suspension into a test tube
Add 2 drop of patient's serum
Mix and incubate at $37^{\circ}\mathrm{C}$ for 30-60 minutes (sensitization)
Wash contents with large volumes of normal saline and discard the last wash
Add two drops of A.H.G. to re-suspend the cell.
Centrifuge at 1000 rpm for one minute
Examine for agglutination. Both macro and microscopically
Record results

If agglutination is present record Indirect Coomb's test= positive
If no agglutination is present record Indirect Coomb's Test = negative.

üìù COMMENT

Positive test is due to presence of immune antibodies
Negative test = no immune antibodies in the test serum

üïµÔ∏è ANTI BODY SCREENING TEST

Test done to detect irregular antibodies in vitro. (Immune antibodies) the test is done on women suspected to be immunized and would like to get pregnant, or patients who have undergone multiple transfusions and is suspected to be immunized and requires blood transfusion.
Conditions indicating presence of irregular antibodies where antibody screening is requested are;

  • Jaundice in new born
  • Incompatible cross match
  • blood transfusion reaction

Antibody screening test has six phases

  • $4^{\circ}\mathrm{C}$
  • RT
  • A $37^{\circ}\mathrm{C}$
  • C $37^{\circ}\mathrm{C}$
  • S $37^{\circ}\mathrm{C}$
  • Enzyme. (Apply Enzyme technique)

$4^{\circ}\mathrm{C}$ PHASE; Detects cold antibodies which react at $4^{\circ}\mathrm{C}$ Anti-I, Anti-i, anti-M, N etc.
RT PHASE also detects cold antibodies Anti I and Anti $\mathrm{P}_{1}$ react strongly at low temp. $4^{\circ}\mathrm{C}$. Anti A anti B, (complete Anti -A and B) react well at RT.
Most of the immune antibodies are detected at $37^{\circ}\mathrm{C}$.

Technique used in screening test

Requirements / Reagents
- Test Serum
- $\mathrm{O}^{+}$ pooled cells, or panel cells or Selectogen I & II
- Albumen
- Coomb's serum
- Saline

Procedure

Label 6 test tubes- $4^{\circ}\mathrm{C}, \mathrm{RT}, \mathrm{S} 37^{\circ}\mathrm{C}, \mathrm{A} 37^{\circ}\mathrm{C}, \mathrm{C} 37^{\circ}\mathrm{C}$, and Enzyme $37^{\circ}\mathrm{C}$
Add two drops of patients / donors serum to all test tubes
Add two drops of panel cells / O+ pooled cells/reagent cells to other except Enzyme tube.
Add two drops of enzyme treated cells to test-tube labelled enzyme
To $\mathrm{A} 37^{\circ}\mathrm{C}$ add two drops of $20 \%$ albumin & mix

Incubate the tubes at respective temperatures $4^{\circ}\mathrm{C}, 37^{\circ}\mathrm{C}, \mathrm{RT}$ for 1 hr. - this is to allow reaction to take place at optimum temp.
Wash the Coombs test tube in large volumes of normal saline X3, Add A.H.G (two drops) Centrifuge the test tubes and look for agglutination.

This is to identify the particular antibody present in the serum / encountered during screening test. The identification of an antibody is based on the characteristic of the particular antibody. It is also based on the media at which it reacted and the temp e.g. C$37^{\circ}\mathrm{C}$. (Coombs @ $37^{\circ}\mathrm{C}$) In identification panel cells are used e.g. panel cells in Rh system: They include
1. $\mathrm{Rh}_{0}$, (2) $\mathrm{Rh}_{1}$, (3) $\mathrm{Rh}_{2}$,
(4) $\mathrm{Rh}_{\mathrm{z}}$, (5) rh, (6) $\mathrm{rh}^{1}$, (7) $\mathrm{rh}^{\mathrm{ii}}$, (8) $\mathrm{rh}^{\mathrm{y}}$
