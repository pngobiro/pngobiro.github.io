Haemolytic Disease of the New Born (H.D.N.B.)

üî¨ Erythroblastosis Foetalis
01

üìò Definition & Symptoms

It is also called erythroblastosis foetalis. This is a condition in which the life span of the infant's red cells is shortened by the action of specific antibodies derived from the mother by placement transfer.

Symptoms of an infected infant

  • aAnaemia (severe)
  • bJaundice
  • cLiver enlargement-hepatomegally (blood forming organs)
  • dHydrops foetalis (oedomatosis foetalis)
  • eSpleenomegary

The foetus erythropoetic system is affected and becomes hyperactive causing the presence of erythroblasts in the blood circulation. This is what led to this condition being called erythroblastosis foetalis.
The most common cause of HDNB is blood group incompatibility whereby antibodies from the mother cross the placenta and attach to the red cells of the foetus, which therefore get destroyed (haemolysed).
The foetal bone marrow is then forced to produce excess red cells to replace the destroyed R.B.C's. The liver spleen and the lymph nodes are also called upon to produce excessive R.B.C.S

Congestive cardiac failure will also occur due to lack of blood cells circulating resulting from severe anaemia. After the breakdown of the R.B.C.S

üß¨ Origin & Transmission

THE ORIGIN OF THE MATERNAL ANTIBODY Anti -D

Blood group antibodies are gamma globulins produced by an individual after an exposure to red blood cells having antigens, which are leaking in the host.

The common cause of HDNB is anti D (anti Rh). This is produced in females who are Rh-ve carrying Rhesus positive foetus.
Antibody productions depend on the amount of the antigen released into the maternal circulation and the routine of administration.
Prenatal antibodies are formed due to blood transfusion, incompatible foetus, during delivery the mothers immune system is triggered by the crossing of D antigen infant to produce anti-D, the antibody production may continue for years without stimulation and the introduction of any other same antigen there after may act as a booster.
The production of the antibody quantities will just increase.

THE PLACENTAL BARRIER

The placenta plays a major role in the development of the foetus. These roles include the following:

  1. iTransfer nutrients from mother to foetus
  2. iiProtection of foetus
  3. iiiExcretory organ for the foetus

The placenta plays a vital role in iso-immunization.
The placenta consists of blood vessels' vesicular spaces and supportive tissue.
The foetus and the mother's circulation are separated there's an extensive surface contact.
The placental vessel villi and connective tissue rapture during birth allowing the foetal red blood cells to escape into the maternal circulation.

‚ö†Ô∏è Causes of HDNB

Other Causes of HDNB:

  1. 1ABO blood group incompatibility cause 2/3 of the cases, however they are so mild that they can go undetected.
  2. 2Rhesus incompatibility causes 1/3 of the cases but is the most severe.
  3. 3Other blood groups e.g. Kell, Duffy, Kidd, MNSs is however very rare.

HDNB DUE TO RHESUS INCOMPATIBILITY:

Rhesus antigens of the foetal red cells are well developed early in foetal life and if released into maternal circulation may stimulate ab formation. Only incomplete anti Rh antigens (antibodies) (IgG) are able to cross the placenta and sensitise the foetal red cells. HDNB due to rhesus incompatibility is common with infants who are $\mathrm{Rh}^{+}$ ve while the mother is Rh -ve. Usually the first born (Para 1) is not very much affected because the sensitisation before is minimal. Sensitization during pregnancy follows the release of foetal red cells into the maternal circulation. This release is maximum during caesarean section and abortion. After the release and transfer of the D antigen the mother produces anti D thus during the second pregnancy the antibody crosses the placenta sensitising the foetal red cells and thus destroying them (haemolyses).
After birth the antibody action continues destroying the foetal red cells haemolysing them and thus bilirubin is increased (due to Hb release). The poorly developed hepatic enzymes glucoronyl transferase. (Conjugates free bilirubin ready for excretion). Doesn't convert the free bilirubin and so there is accumulation of the toxic (free bilirubin) in the tissues causing jaundice - yellowing of tissues - (eyes) if it accumulates in the brain it causes Kernicterus. (Irreversible damage of brain cells)

SYMPTOMS IN ORDER OF SEVERITY

  1. iKernicterus (brain damage)
  2. iiCardiac arrest. (Due to severe anaemia)
  3. iiiJaundice
  4. ivSevere anaemia
  5. vHaemoglobinuria
  6. viihepato spleenomegally
  7. viiiHydrops foetalis (still birth)

ü©∫ Diagnosis & Treatment

HAEMATOLOGICAL FINDINGS OF HDNB

Hb estimation is reduced
Red cell count is reduced on the P.B. F. (peripheral blood film)

  • Anisocytosis - with macrocytes and microcytes
  • Poikilocytosis -schistocytes (fragmented cells)
  • Polychromasia
  • Nucleated r.b.c. increased = up to 6%

Bilirubin level of the cord blood is increased especially indirect bilirubin. This is a biochemical test.
Serological test - Direct coomb's test antibody screening test should be positive.

THE ROLE OF RHOGAM

Rhogam is a purified form of anti-D. It is given to Rhesus $^{-}$ ve mothers who have given birth to a Rhesus $^{+}$ ve positive baby. Or within 72 hrs of delivery. The reason why it is given is to remove of all Rh+ve cells before they get a chance to sensitise the mother or cause stimulation of antibodies by the mother.

RX OF HDNB

1. Exchange transfusion

This is a process whereby the baby's blood is removed and a similar amount of blood is transfused.

2. Intra Uterine transfusion

This is the transfusion of blood into the foetus when it still is in uterus. This is done when the foetus is in distress from severe anaemia or jaundice and is still too young to be delivered.

3. Early induction of Labour

This means inducing labour-in the mother b4 the normal time.

Criteria for exchange Transfusion

  1. 1Laboratory findings e.g. haematological finding, jaundice etc.
  2. 2The doctor examines the physical condition of the infant and the history of the family.

SELECTION OF BLOOD FOR BLOOD TRANSFUSION

  • Blood should be fresh preferably not more than three days old.
  • Should not contain gas corresponding to be antibodies in the mother's circulation
  • The safer blood to give is O-ve because it has no antigens.

‚ö†Ô∏è OTHER CAUSES OF NEONATAL JAUNDICE

OTHER THAN BLOOD GROUP INCOMPATIBILITY

Physiological

  • Hereditary spherocytosis
  • Congenital disorders

Metabolic

  • Diabetic mothers
  • Galactosemia
  • G-6-PD deficiency
  • Pyruvate kinase deficiency

Infection:

  • Hepatitis
  • Congenital syphilis
  • Rubella (measles)

Drugs

  • Overdose of Vit K
  • Naphthalene

It's the process by which the amniotic sac is entered with a needle and some amount of amniotic fluid is drawn. It is done after 28 weeks of conceiving and the titre is done of antibody. If the titre goes beyond 1:64 then further analysis - spectrophotometry is done to check the bilirubin.

Other tests done:

  • ABO and rhesus grouping - if D -ve confirm by performing $\mathrm{D}^{\mathrm{u}}$ test.
  • Indirect Coombs test ICT.
  • Rhesus grouping.
  • Antibody screening test to detect any irregular ab.
  • Antibody identification test.
  • Antibody titration.
  • Antibody exclusion.
  • Father's cells against mother's serum.
  • Need for induced labour could arise.
