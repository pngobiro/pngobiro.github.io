‚öóÔ∏è Preparation of Antisera

Normally preparation of antiserum is done in specialized reference laboratories. There are four points to consider:

  1. iPotency
  2. iiAvidity
  3. iiiSpecific
  4. ivFree from Rouleaux
  5. vFree from contamination
  6. viNot turbid

The antisera should be tested for the above four things it must contain a powerful antibody that reacts only with the red cells carrying the corresponding antigen.

02

‚úÖ Criteria for Determining Serum Suitability

1. POTENCY

Titre

This is the strength of the antisera. It should have a titre of at least 1:256

2. Avidity

This is a measure of avidity, the speed at which antisera agglutinates red cells. The avidity is determined by adding one drop of $10 \%$ saline suspension of washed red cells to an equal volume of antiserum to a microscope slide or a glass tile. Record the time it takes for the cells to agglutinate. Repeat this three times and then take an average. Should be less than 10 seconds

3. Specificity

The antisera should react with the corresponding antigen. A-cells B-cells and O-cells are used. Anti-A should react with only A-cells etc

4. Rouleaux formation

Check under a microscope x4 to ensure it is true agglutination

The antisera should be free from fat since it gives antisera a turbid appearance falsely suggesting presence of bacteria.

03

ü©∏ Plasma Processing

-If plasma is used to prepare antisera:

  1. iRecalcification
  2. iiAdsorption of fibrinogen (Kaolin and Benfonile)

Recalcification

  • place the separated plasma in an incubator @ $37^{\circ}\mathrm{C}$ for 15 mins .
  • Add 2 mls of calcium chloride for each 100 mls of plasma
  • The mixture is left for half hour $\frac{1}{2} \mathrm{hr}$ to allow clot to form.
  • The clot broken by shaking and left in an incubator for $1 \frac{1}{2} \mathrm{hr}$.
  • The fluid is stored at $4^{\circ}\mathrm{C}$ centigrade overnight.
  • Sterilize by passing through muslin.
04

üìù Steps Involved in Preparation of Antisera

  1. iIdentify the source of antisera (sheep/horse/rabbit)
  2. iiTitrate to test the strength of antiserum by steps likely to be used routinely.
  3. iiiSelect suitable antisera according to criteria laid down.
  4. ivConfirm the specificity of antiserum by testing against a panel of standard cells.
  5. vRemoval of unwanted antibodies can be done in
    a. Adsorption with appropriate red cells.
    b. Fractionation
    c. Dilution
  6. viiiSterilize the antisera by Seitz filters
  7. ixpreservative is added / Sodium-azide-0.1%
  8. xSuitable dyes are added.
    Anti A- blue - Trypan blue
    Anti B yellow -Acriflavine orange
    Anti D No colour is added
  9. xiDispense in suitable quantities ($5 \mathrm{~ml}$ dropper bottles) in sterile containers.
  10. xiiStore at $4^{\circ}\mathrm{C}$ if it is to be used immediately or at $-20^{\circ}\mathrm{C}$ if not to be used immediately.

üåø LECTINS

Lectins are extracts from certain plants normally from the seeds of particular plants. These extracts are capable of causing agglutination of the red bld cells. The agglutination is not specific in most cases (can react c any antigens present). Not all the extracts, some show blood group specificity. It is important to note that the extracts are not antibodies but they have a similar effect as antibodies they are thus referred to as phytoagglutins. There are many types of phytoagglutins. But the two most commonly used are:

  1. 1Dolichos biflorous (Indian cattle bean) house gram/grain. Its specificity shows a reaction similar to antibody $\mathrm{A}_{1}$-Aggregates $\mathrm{A}_{1}$ cells.
  2. 2Ulex europaeus (common gorse) it has Anti H specificity can agglutinate O cells but not Bombay cells.

ü•£ Lectin Preparation Protocols

Preparation of Anti A1 from Dolichos biflorous

  • Take the seeds and grid with pestle and mortal or madrine until they are in powder form weigh about 7 g of the powder D. biflorous / put in a test-tube.
  • Add 10 mls of N . saline
  • Using a shaking machine mix the mixture
  • Centrifuge at 3000 rpm for 5 mins
  • Discard the sediment / by pipeting the supernatant
  • Refine the supernatant fluid by filtration using Whatman No 1 filter paper
  • Standardize the antiserum / perform titration
  • Titration titre 1:512
  • Check specificity using $\mathrm{A}_{1}$ cells
  • Check the avidity $\mathrm{A}_{1}$-10 seconds
  • Sterilize the antiserum / use filters ( Seitz)

Dispense the antiserum in small bijou bottles
If to be used after certain time store at $4^{\circ}\mathrm{C}$ or $-20^{\circ}\mathrm{C}$ if not to be used immediately.

Preparation of AntiH from Ulex europaeus.

  • Grind to make into powder form
  • To each gram of powder 10 mls of N. saline is added
  • Using a shaking machine mix the mixture
  • Centrifuge at 3000 rpm for 5 mins
  • Transfer the supernatant fluid to another test tube v
  • Refine using Whatman no 1 filter paper
  • Standardize the antiserum
  • Dispense in bijou bottles store at an appropriate temp $^{-4}$ degrees centigrade if to be used quickly and $^{-} 20$ degrees centigrade if to be used after a long time
  • Blood group O can be used for specificity and avidity and also for titration
