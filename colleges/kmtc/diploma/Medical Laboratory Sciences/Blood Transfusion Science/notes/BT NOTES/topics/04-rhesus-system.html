üìñ Discovery and Theories

It was discovered by Landsteiner and Weiner in 1940. It was discovered through a Rhesus monkey (genus).
He took Rhesus monkey's r.b.c's and injected them into rabbits and guinea pigs
The rabbit produced antibodies against Rhesus monkey's r.b.c's (foreign bodies)
Immune antibodies produced by rabbit after injection reacted with rhesus monkey's r.b.c's, and about $85\%$ human r.b.c's.
-Anti serum from rabbit was detecting and unknown human blood group antigen and used it to type -Rh positive (those which reacted $=85\%$ )
Rh negative (didn't react $=15\%$ )
Antigen involved is RhD antigen or antigen D
Rh-blood group system is governed by two theories

  • Fisher - Race theory
  • Weiner theory

FISHER - RACE THEORY:

States that antigens of Rh system are determined by three pairs of genes which occupy closely linked loci.
Either two or three alleles on a chromosome

The gene d appears to be a silent gene because there is no demonstrable product of d. thus d (gene) is assumed to be an amorph. Thus the order of loci is D-C-E- but it's only the D antigen that determines whether you are RhD positive or RhD negative.
The three loci that carry the Rh gene are so closely linked on the chromosome that they never separate but they are passed from generation to generation as a unit or as a gene complex.
In the fisher-Race notation the gene and the red cell antigen it produces are given the same symbol gene E and antigen E, however the letter is italicized for the antigen. Each of the alleles gene mentioned controls the presence of respective antigens on the r.b.c's except d which is a morph.

WEINERS THEORY:

Postulates that two genes are on each chromosome of the pair controls the entire expression of the Rh system in one individual.
The pairs on each genes might be alike homozygous or different heterozygous.
There are eight major alleles which can be produced.
According to Weiner's hypothesis each gene produces a structure on the red cells called agglutinogen and each agglutinogen can be identified by its parts or factors, but react with specific antibodies e.g. gene $R^{1}$ produces agglutinogen $\mathrm{Rh}^{1}$, on r.b.c
The agglutinogen has three factors

  • Rh
  • rh
  • hr

-gene r determines agglutinogen rh. Agglutinogen rh has two factors

  • -hr'
  • -hr"

‚ö†Ô∏è $\mathbf{RH}_{\text {NULL }}$ PHENOTYPE

This is an individual lacking all the representation of Rhesus antigens on the R.B.C. The person is devoid of any Rhesus antigens. It was discovered among the Australian Aborigines due to consanguineous marriages. $\mathrm{Rh}_{\text {null }}$ individuals are of phenotype and genotype ---/--- (minus minus minus) The common allele $\mathrm{X}^{1} \mathrm{r}$ is responsible for converting precursor substance I to precursor substance II which is then converted by the Rh genes DCE into Rh antigen DCE/Dce depending on genes inherited.
The more common regulator type of $\mathrm{Rh}_{\text {null }}$ mutations occur in the RHAG gene that result in complete absence of core Rh complex (Rh polypeptides and RhAg ) that is necessary for the expression of Rh antigens. The other form of $\mathrm{Rh}_{\text {null }}$ the amorph type has a normal RHAG gene; however, there is a mutation in each RHCE gene, together with the common deletion of RHD.
The $\mathrm{Rh}_{\text {null }}$ individuals lack the common allele and they instead have the modifier / regulator gene $\mathrm{x}^{\circ} \mathrm{r} / \mathrm{x}^{\circ} \mathrm{r}$ which does not convert precursor substance I to precursor substance II. Thus there is no production of Rh antigens. When x is heterozygous $\mathrm{x}^{\circ} \mathrm{r} / \mathrm{x}^{1} \mathrm{r}$, partial expression of Rh - antigens results. When $\mathrm{x}^{\circ} \mathrm{r}$ is homozygous, there is complete suppression of Rh gene. $\mathrm{Rh}_{\text {null }}$ individuals have inherited homozygous $\mathrm{x}^{\circ} \mathrm{r}$ gene which suppresses the expression of Rh genes.
$\mathrm{Rh}_{\text {null }}$ phenotype when tested with any or all Rh antibodies CDEce the cells fail to react and they appear to be devoid of Rhesus antigens However the Rh genes are normal as shown by the fact that $\mathrm{Rh}_{\text {null }}$ individuals transmit to their offspring functional Rh genes which they inherited from their parents. An individual who is $\mathrm{Rh}_{\text {null }}$ is considered to be Rh negative because of lack of D antigen
A person with $\mathrm{Rh}_{\text {null }}$ cannot be transfused with Rh negative blood to avoid contamination of CE antigens.
$\mathrm{Rh}_{\text {null }}$ individuals present with stomatocytosis, with mild haemolytic process.

üíâ RH - TYPING

This is a test donor to ascertain whether an individual is Rh-positive or Rhnegative and normally in Rh typing it's normally the Rh cell grouping This is the detection of presence or absence of antigen D . When present the patient is classified rhesus-D positive and when negative he is classified rhesus-D negative
D antigen and D antibody account for over $90\%$ of all haemolytic reactions caused by Rh misgrouping There are two kinds of anti D

  • Complete anti D saline (monoclonal)
  • Incomplete anti D (albumen anti D)

Slide method:

  • Make a 4% cell suspension (2-5%)
  • Label the slide accordingly
  • Add 1 drop of the cell suspension onto the slide
  • Add 1 drop of complete (monoclonal) saline anti D
  • By use of an applicator stick, mix to enhance reaction (agglutination) Examine for agglutination both macroscopically and microscopically If the test is positive (presence of agglutination), record the results Rh-D positive If negative, carry out the tube technique.

After doing tube technique and results are still negative, perform $\mathrm{D}^{u}$ test. If all is negative, the donor / patient is Rhesus negative

Tube Method:

  • Make 4% cell suspension
  • Label test tubes according to the tests
  • Add one drop of cell suspension
  • Add one drop of complete (monoclonal) saline anti-D
  • Mix and allow to stand for at least 2 minutes
  • Centrifuge the tubes at 1000 r.p.m for one min OR 3000 rpm for 20 seconds Examine for agglutination macroscopically

Albumen addition Method:

It uses incomplete (polyclonal) anti -D

Procedure

  • Make 4% cell suspension
  • Label tubes according to the number of specimens
  • Add one drop of 4% cell suspension to the tubes
  • Add one drop of incomplete anti -D
  • Add one drop of 20% bovine albumen
  • Mix the tubes gently
  • Incubate the tubes at $37^{\circ} \mathrm{C}$ in water bath for 30-60 min
  • Remove the tubes and examine for agglutination
  • If the test is negative, centrifuge the tubes at about 1000 r.p.m. for one min
  • If still the test is negative perform $\mathrm{D}^{\mathrm{u}}$ test
  • Record results
  • Wash cells three times in large volumes of normal saline (the more times the better) remove excess globulin /proteins
  • Add A.H.G. (Coombs reagent) Centrifuge and read the reaction

REPORT
Rhesus-D positive (agglutination)
Rhesus-D negative (no agglutination)

Chown capillary tube method (Rapid method)

Requirements

  • Chown capillary tubes (10-15 cm) length 0.5 mm (diameter)
  • Chown rack
  • Plasticine (Petroleum)
  • Patients / donors cells -30% cell suspension
  • Hand lens
  • 30% known washed O-positive cells
  • 30% known washed O-negative cells
  • Potent saline anti-D (monoclonal)

Procedure

  • Dip the end of the capillary tube into the anti D serum. Allow the serum (AntiD) to run up to 2 cm -in height / by capillary
  • Repeat for each of the tubes / positive, negative, auto-control (Auto-control use patient's serum)
  • Using the same technique, allow the patients 30% cell suspension to run up the tube
  • Invert the tubes so that cells are above and serum at the lower end
  • seal with Plasticine / petroleum jelly
  • place tubes on chowns rack at an angle of $45^{\circ}$
  • Incubate the tubes the tubes at $37^{\circ \mathrm{C}}$ for 30 min
  • Read the results and record them
  • when positive there is a bead-like appearance
  • When -ve, the cells are homogenous (smooth line)

üß¨ $\mathbf{D}^{\mathbf{u}}$ (Weak D)

These are weakly reacting form of antigen D. The normal D antigen has been found to be more complex than the other antigens. 4 fractions have been detected with the number of reactions and components present. D antigen with all the four fractions is the complete Rh-D antigen.
D antigen has three variations (grades):- High

grade $\mathbf{D}^{\mathbf{u}}$
The cell lacks one fraction. It also reacts with commercial anti D. both polyclonal and monoclonal.

Medium or $\mathbf{D}^{\mathbf{u}}$
It lacks two components. It gives positive reaction with monoclonal commercial anti D. but not polyclonal.

Low grade $\mathbf{D}^{\mathbf{u}}$
It lacks three fractions
It doesn't give positive reaction with commercial anti D . $\mathrm{D}^{\mathrm{u}}$ test is performed to detect low grade $\mathrm{D}^{\mathrm{u}}$.
$\mathrm{D}^{\mathrm{u}}$ test utilizes anti human globulin (AHG Test)

It has been established that the $\mathrm{D}^{\mathrm{u}}$ is inherited. A parent who has the $\mathrm{D}^{\mathrm{u}}$ will pass to a child $\mathrm{D}^{\mathrm{u}}$ antigen which will not react with anti D sera.

$\mathrm{D}^{\mathrm{u}}$ presents a problem (anomalies) when typing red cells using Anti-D serum If $\mathrm{D}^{\mathrm{u}}$ antigen is injected to persons who lacks D antigen ( dcE, dCe ) the $\mathrm{D}^{\mathrm{u}}$ will immunize causing anti-D. All D negative individuals should be considered as Rh-positive and a $\mathrm{D}^{\mathrm{u}}$ test should be conducted to confirm that they are negative.

$\mathbf{D}^{\mathbf{u}}$ Test Method:

  • Make a 4% cell suspension of the specimen.
  • Label the test tubes, test and control.
  • Add two drops of 4% cell suspension to all the test tubes.
  • Add one drop of complete anti D to the test tube labelled test.
  • Add one drop of Bovine albumen to the test tube labelled control.
  • Incubate both tubes at $37^{\circ} \mathrm{C}$ in water bath for 30 minutes- 60 minutes
  • Remove the tubes from the water bath and centrifuge for about 1 min @ 1000 r.p.m.
  • Examine for agglutination in both tubes
  • If the test is negative after centrifugation wash three times in large volumes of normal saline / to remove excess globulin / to avoid neutralizing anti human globulin
  • Add at least two drops of antihuman globulin or (Coomb's serum) A.H.G
  • Centrifuge for 1 min and examine for agglutination. Record your findings

Result Interpretation:

  • Agglutination $\mathrm{D}^{\mathrm{u}}$ positive - Rh positive
  • No agglutination $\mathrm{D}^{\mathrm{u}}$ negative - Rh negative
