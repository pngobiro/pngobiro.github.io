OTHER BLOOD GROUP SYSTEMS

üìÑ Kell, Lewis, Duffy, Kidd & More

These are Blood groups other than ABO and Rh. They are rare and are not routinely determined in routine serological studies e.g. blood bank. There major clinical significance is when they cause problems such as transfusion reaction and HDNB. They also help in disputed parentage and forensic medicine.

Examples They are:

  1. Lutheran
  2. Kell
  3. Lewis
  4. P
  5. MNSs.

01

üß¨ LUTHERAN

It was first in 1946 when an unknown antibody appeared in the blood of a pt that had received multiple transfusions. The ab was immune and the stimulating ag was present in one of the donors whose name was Lutheran.
Lutheran gene is independent of all other blood groups otherwise linkage exists between the secretor genes. Lutheran a (Lu a ) is a weak antigen and possibly plays a minor role in transfusion reactions.
Anti-Lu a has not been found to cause HDNB.
Lutheran b (Lu b ) is a weak antigen. It rarely forms Anti-Lu b. Anti Lu b when formed can cause transfusion reactions.

02

üíâ KELL

Mouran and Race discovered this blood group system in 1946. These antibodies were discovered after they had caused H.D.N.B. they are represented as Anti-Kell Anti-K and Anti ' (cellano)

It is the third blood group of importance from ABO & Rhesus. The genotypes of Kell + ve are KK and Kell -ve are". (Or cellano). Anti-K and Anti-' (cellano) exists. They are immune been encountered. No linkage exists between this blood group and others.

SEROLOGY.

Both antibodies fail to agglutinate cells suspended in saline. They are best detected by A.H.G. method.
Agglutination best occurs when the cells are suspended on the human serum.
Agglutination is inhibited by bovine albumin.
Immune anti-K best reacts at 37¬∞C.
Kell antibodies are labile so fresh specimens are required every time for reliable results. Anti-K exhibits dosage effect (reacts more strongly in monozygous than heterozygous).

03

üîó LEWIS BLOOD GROUP SYSTEM

The system was called Lewis after one of the two individuals after whom it was first found.
The system is related to the secretor status (A.B.H.) in such a way that all the Le a+ ve individuals are always non-secretors of A.B.H. specific substances, but secretors of Le s substances.
Lewis system plays no part in paternity disputes. This is because both parents will have Le a +ve and still have babies who are Le a -ve. Le a antigens are not inherited. They can be found in the plasma.

ANTI Le a

It mostly occurs as a natural cold complete antibody. It reacts in saline and also in the albumin medium. It is a common cause of difficulties in compatibility tests. Anti-Le a has got lytic properties. It can lyse cells in the presence of complements and enzymes (Ficin, Papain). Anti-Le a precipitating and agglutinating sera has been prepared in animals. The reaction of Anti Le a appears like a necklace.

04

üß¨ THE P BLOOD GROUP SYSTEM

Landsteiner and Levine discovered this blood group in 1927. They were doing research by immunising rabbits with human R.B.C's. They discovered antibodies, which they named anti-P, then later called anti-P1. 75% of the people were found to be P1+ve and 25% were found to be P1-ve. Those who were found to be P1-ve were termed as P2.

CHARACTERISTICS OF ANTI P1:

  1. A naturally occurring antibody (very rare)
  2. It belongs to IgM class
  3. It is the antibody that most commonly causes problems in grouping and compatibility tests.
  4. It binds complement in enzyme treated cells.
  5. It is inhibited by Hydatid cyst fluid.
05

üî¨ MNSs Blood Group System

The antibodies most commonly encountered are directed at the M, N, S and s antigens MN and Ss are allelomorphic. They are inherited together. Landsteiner and Levine discovered it in 1927.

CHARACTERISTICS OF ANTI M

  • Cold ab belong to 1 g M class
  • Reacts in saline at RT
  • It binds compliments
  • Doesn't cause H.D.N.B but can cause transfusion reaction. Best detected by indirect antiglobulin method.
  • It is destroyed by enzymes (M & N antigens)
