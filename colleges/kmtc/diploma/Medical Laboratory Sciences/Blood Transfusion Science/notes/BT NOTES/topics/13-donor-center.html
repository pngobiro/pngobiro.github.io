PLANING AND SETTING OF A BLOOD DONOR CENTRE

üìÑ Operations, Selection & Products
01

üìã Roles and Setup

Roles of donor centre;

  1. Donor recruitment
  2. Phlebotomy.
  3. Blood screening e.g. blood grouping
  4. Preparing of blood products e.g. Fresh Frozen Plasma
  5. Advisory centre or Research centre.

STAFF

  1. The centre should be headed by a qualified staff either a medical technologist or pathologist. The in charge serves as the administrator, public relation adviser and a research worker.
  2. Technical staff worker in the centre must have a thorough knowledge of blood donor recruitment. They should make sure that they perform phlebotomy to donors who are fit medically to avoid any undue legal implications.
  3. Only people in experience in vene-puncture should perform phlebotomy to donors using aseptic technique. It is important that donors have confidence in the staff.
  4. Clerical officers are also essential for record keeping and read other supporting officers for cleaning the place and equipment that does no require any skills.

‚úÖ Donor Selection

DONOR CRITERIA:

A prospective donor is only allowed to donate if she/he fulfils the following criteria.

  1. Age: Females - 16 - 45 years; Males - 16 - 65 years
  2. Physical appearance: A donor should not have rushes on the body - or many scars especially on the arms.
  3. Lactating, pregnant or women in their menstrual periods.
  4. Period of last donation: Not less than 4 months for Female and 3 months for male.
  5. Anyone who has suffered from hepatitis and/or HIV.
  6. Anyone who is alcoholic or drug addicted should be permanently deferred.
  7. People with bleeding disorders.
  8. Hb level: Female 12.5-16.5 gm/dl; Male 13.5-18 g/dl
  9. People suffering from syphilis, Aids, hypertension, Diabetes should not donate blood.
  10. Low risk group.
  11. Should NOT have taken a heavy meal prior to the procedure

üì¶ Blood Products

1. PACKED RED CELLS;

These are cells from which virtually all plasma has been removed.
Uses: For treating anaemic pts particularly those requiring many pints: For pts who had formed mild reactions to some none - red cell components of the blood.

2. WASHED RED CELLS:

Also called white cells poor blood.
Washing - is to remove all traces of plasma and some white cells and platelets. Washed cells are used in cases of ;
(i) Paroxysmal Nocturnal Haemoglobinuria - PNH
(ii) Pts who have transfusion reactions due to antibodies against platelets, WBC & protein components.

3. PLATELETS RICH PLASMA:

Uses: To treat pts in thrombocytopenia, to stop spontaneous bleeding; in pts with platelet dysfunction; hypoplastic anaemia; or bone marrow failure due to replacement or to the effects of chemotherapy.

4. FRESH FROZEN PLASMA (F.F.P.)

Is prepared from fresh blood less than 4 hrs old. Is prepared by centrifuging whole blood a 2000-3000 rpm for 20-30 minutes and then plasma extracted to the satellite bag at Room temperature.
F.F.P is immediately stored @ -20¬∞c or less.
Uses: Pts with burns, Pts in shock, Surgery (heart surgery)

5. CRYOPRECIPITATE:

This precipitate is rich in factor VIII and Fibrinogen and is what is known as cryoprecipitate and does not dissolve back in plasma.
Uses: Treatment of haemophilic patients.
