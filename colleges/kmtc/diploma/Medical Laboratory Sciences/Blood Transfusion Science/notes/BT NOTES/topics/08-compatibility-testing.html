COMPATIBILITY TESTING & TRANSFUSION REACTIONS

üìÑ Cross-Match Methods & Safety
01

üéØ Purpose of Cross Match

Compatibility test is a laboratory procedure to determine ahead of time that there is serology compatibility between a blood donor and the intended recipient. This test is one of the most important tests done in a transfusion service.

  • To detect irregular antibodies in the recipient's serums that are directed against donor's cells. This is known as the major crossmatch To detect donor's antibodies directed against the recipients r.b.c's. This is minor x-match.
  • To detect errors in ABO grouping
  • To detect errors in labelling, recording or identifying donors or recipients
  • Assurance of maximum benefit for the recipient receiving the blood. This is done by (i) Preventing transfusion reaction by giving compatible blood to recipient (ii) Prevent allo-immunization

Types of Cross-Match

Major X-match
To check whether serum has antibodies, which will react with donor's cells. Pts serum is used & donors cells.

Minor X-match
To check whether donor's serum has irregular antibodies which would react with pts cells hence causing transfusion ration. Donor's serum & pts cells are used.

02

üå°Ô∏è Phases of X-match

There are four phases in X-match
(i) S.R.T
(ii) $\mathrm{S} 37^{\circ} \mathrm{C}$
(iii) $\mathrm{A} 37^{\circ} \mathrm{C}$ (iv) $\mathrm{C} 37^{\circ} \mathrm{C}$
(v) Enzymes (not commonly used)

Importance of the Phases

(i) R.T (saline)
(i) To detect ABO incompatibility
(ii) To detect incompatibility caused by other naturally occurring antibodies
(iii) To detect incompatibility caused by the few immune antibodies that react in saline at R.T

(II) $\mathbf{S 3 7}^{\circ} \mathbf{C}$
(i) To detect saline reacting antibodies e.g. of Rh system-warm agglutinins (antibodies)
(ii) To distinguish between warm and cold antibodies

(III) A $37^{\circ} \mathbf{C}$
Detects most of Rh incomplete antibodies

(IV) $\mathbf{C} 37^{\circ} \mathbf{C}$
Best techniques for the detection of most irregular antibodies
Detects almost all Rh antibodies
Detects most antibodies associated c haemolytic anaemia e.g. acquired haemolytic anaemia
The only means to detect antibodies found in rare blood groups particularly antibodies found in- Duffy, Kidd, Kell blood grp system

üß™ Methods

4 TUBE METHOD:

Procedure label test tubes S.R.T, S37, A37, C37

  • Make 4% cell suspension of donors cells and add two drops of cells to the four test tubes
  • Add two drops of pts serum to each tube
  • To A $37^{\circ} \mathrm{C}$ add two drops of $20\%$ Albumin
  • Incubate the test tube to respective temperature for $\frac{1}{2} \mathrm{hr}-1 \mathrm{hr}$
  • Wash the Coombs cells with saline X3
  • Add 1 drop of AHG and mix to resuspend the cells
  • Centrifuge all test tubes and observe for agglutination record results

If negative, label blood/pint=COMPATIBLE and put your initials/sign

3 TUBE METHOD:

Procedure:

  • Label RT, $\mathrm{S} 37^{\circ} \mathrm{C}$ and $\mathrm{A} 37^{\circ} \mathrm{C}$
  • Add two drops of 4% donors cell suspension to the three tubes
  • Add two drops of pts serum to the three tubes
  • Add two drops of bovine albumin 20% to t.t. labelled $\mathrm{A} 37^{\circ} \mathrm{C}$
  • Incubate tubes at respective temps for $30-60$ minutes
  • Centrifuge the tubes and examine for agglutination (centrifugation 1000 r.p.m for $1 \mathrm{~min}$ )

If negative, convert A $37^{\circ} \mathrm{C}$ to Coombs $37^{\circ} \mathrm{C}$

üö® EMERGENCY X.MATCH

In cases of emergency the pts physician must weigh the risks of transfusing un-xmatched blood against the hazards of waiting for a compatibility test.

‚ö†Ô∏è Protocol

If the physician/Doctor believes the urgency of situation warrants releasing of blood without x-match then it may justifiably be done. But the physician/Doctor must accept any responsibility of any incompatibility to the compatibility test (Ab / Ag reaction) and should put it in writing.

In urgent situation:

  • Give the un x-matched blood to physician if he demands it. (He puts it in writing)
  • Make sure the physician signs the release of the blood.
  • Begin the routine compatibility testing procedures.
  • Compare the tests as soon as possible and if incompatibility is noticed at any stage of testing, notify the physician so that he stops the transfusion.

In cases of extreme emergencies blood group O-ve with low titre is given although transfusions of such blood is not without a risk.

ü©∏ UNIVERSAL DONORS

U.D is an individual whose blood can be safely transfused to any other blood group. Group O dangerous blood donor is in existence. It's an individual blood group O of high titre. Group O individual with low titre is a safe blood donor (Below 100).

SCREENING FOR A UNIVERSAL DONORS:

There are three methods

  • Titre for saline methods
  • Test for haemolysis antibodies A and B
  • Test for haemolysis (immune anti A anti B)
  • Neutralization Titre for saline antibodies

Procedure (Titre):
Make a dilution of $1: 100$ of donors fresh serum with N. saline e.g. 0.1 mls of O serum -9.9 mls N . saline.
Label two test tubes as A and B.
Add two drops of donor's diluted serum to both test tubes.
Add two drops of known / washed A, cells to test tube labelled A and washed B cells to test tubes labelled B.
Mix the tubes gently. Let stand at R.T for about $15 \mathrm{mins}$ .
Examine for agglutination both macro and microscopically.

Results
No agglutination-the individual is a U.D and thus blood can be used for transfusion.
Agglutination -dangerous U.D and thus not good for transfusion.
