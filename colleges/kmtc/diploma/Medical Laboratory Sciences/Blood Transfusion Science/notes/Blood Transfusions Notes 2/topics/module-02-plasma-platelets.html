02

Fresh Frozen Plasma (FFP)

FFP... it contains plasma. Let's talk a little bit more about what this plasma is per se. So it contains a bunch of different types of proteins that are relevant here. One is it contains a little bit of fibrinogen. It contains some von Willebrand factor. And here is the key thing. And I'm going to actually write this in red here. It contains ALL clotting factors.

  • Fibrinogen
  • von Willebrand factor
  • ALL clotting factors

So all the clotting factors you can possibly think of that are in our plasma is present in the FFP. Factors 2, 7, 9, 10, 1, 3, etc. The list goes on. All of those clotting factors are in FFP.

Now, FFP is usually in a frozen bag. It's about 200 to 300 milliliters. Now when we give Fresh Frozen Plasma, we're giving it to patients who maybe have a deficiency in fibrinogen or von Willebrand's factor. But more specifically, we're giving it to patients who have some type of coagulopathy.

04

Cryoprecipitate

Let's take the Fresh Frozen Plasma and do a little bit more to it though. Because we got all these clotting factors. Maybe I can fractionate it a little bit. So what I'm going to do here is I'm going to fractionate out the Fresh Frozen Plasma. One of the things that I'm going to do is I'm going to kind of fractionate it as I said, so I'll fractionate the clotting factors... the proteins that are present in the Fresh Frozen Plasma. And the other thing that I'm going to do is I'm going to de-thaw. So I'm going to unfreeze the Fresh Frozen Plasma.

What happens in these two processes when you fractionate out the proteins or you de-thaw is you get two new products.

So we're going to thaw the FFP. So when I thaw out the FFP what happens is some of the proteins in the FFP precipitates out of solution. So it precipitates out of solution. So what I'm going to do is... those proteins that precipitate out of solution, I'm going to put that in one bag and then I'm going to pull or decant all of the other stuff that didn't precipitate. The stuff that precipitates is going to be called Cryoprecipitate. Kind of a cool name here.

Now Cryoprecipitate is a great kind of blood product. When you cause this thing to precipitate out of solution, it only contains certain proteins that do precipitate. And guess what it is? Fibrinogen, von Willebrand factor, and only a couple, like two clotting factors. And so what you're left with here is you're left with a very high concentration of:

  • Fibrinogen
  • von Willebrand factor
  • Factor 8
  • Factor 13

We can see when you give a patient Cryoprecipitate, you should really give it when they have some type of fibrinogen deficiency, or von Willebrand's factor deficiency, or Factor 8 deficiency.
