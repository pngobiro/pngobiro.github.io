<p>Entrepreneurship Education module is designed to equip the learner with knowledge, skills and attitude to enable them come up with a business plan and participate in nutrition and health related studies.</p>
<p>The module is offered in the first semester of the third year of training. The module takes 60 contact hours: 34 hours for theory and 26 hours for practical. <div data-testid="stHeadingWithActionElements" class="st-emotion-cache-1p9ibxm e1icttdg2"><h2 level="2" id="1-1-definition-of-terms">1.1 Definition of Terms<span data-testid="stHeaderActionElements" class="st-emotion-cache-gi0tri e1icttdg3"><a href="#1-1-definition-of-terms" class="st-emotion-cache-yinll1 e1icttdg1"><svg xmlns="http://www.w3.org/2000/svg" width="16" height="16" viewBox="0 0 24 24" fill="none" stroke="currentColor" stroke-width="2" stroke-linecap="round" stroke-linejoin="round"><path d="M15 7h3a5 5 0 0 1 5 5 5 5 0 0 1-5 5h-3m-6 0H6a5 5 0 0 1-5-5 5 5 0 0 1 5-5h3"></path><line x1="8" y1="12" x2="16" y2="12"></line></svg></a></span></h2></div>
<p><strong>Entrepreneur</strong>: A person who has possession of an enterprise, or venture, and assumes significant accountability for the inherent risks and the outcome.</p>
<p>He is an ambitious leader who combines land, labour and capital to create and market new goods or services.</p>
<p><strong>Entrepreneurship</strong>: is a process of establishing an enterprise. It is the function performed by an entrepreneur in establishing an enterprise.</p>
<p>Entrepreneurship creates value through recognition of a business opportunity, managing risk in the opportunity and mobilizing human, financial and material resources necessary to bring an enterprise into operation.</p>
<div data-testid="stHeadingWithActionElements" class="st-emotion-cache-1p9ibxm e1icttdg2"><h2 level="2" id="1-2-difference-between-self-employment-and-formal-employment">1.2 Difference between Self-Employment and Formal Employment<span data-testid="stHeaderActionElements" class="st-emotion-cache-gi0tri e1icttdg3"><a href="#1-2-difference-between-self-employment-and-formal-employment" class="st-emotion-cache-yinll1 e1icttdg1"><svg xmlns="http://www.w3.org/2000/svg" width="16" height="16" viewBox="0 0 24 24" fill="none" stroke="currentColor" stroke-width="2" stroke-linecap="round" stroke-linejoin="round"><path d="M15 7h3a5 5 0 0 1 5 5 5 5 0 0 1-5 5h-3m-6 0H6a5 5 0 0 1-5-5 5 5 0 0 1 5-5h3"></path><line x1="8" y1="12" x2="16" y2="12"></line></svg></a></span></h2></div>
<p>Entrepreneurs differ from other business owners in that, entrepreneurs take higher risk, create more wealth within a short time and involve substantial innovation.</p>
<p>Entrepreneurship is inclusive of one or more of the following elements</p>
<ul>
<li>Starting a business.</li>
<li>Being creative and innovative in developing new products or services.</li>
<li>Managing an existing venture in such a way that it grows rapidly and consistently.</li>
<li>Seeking significant financing and other resources for a potentially high-growth venture.</li>
<li>Accepting risk in the development of a new or growing venture.</li>
</ul>
<p>These elements are important aspects of the entrepreneurial process. It is not necessary, however, to have all elements present in every entrepreneurial situation.</p>
<p>Entrepreneurship involves innovation which can take any of the following form.</p>
<ul>
<li>New product</li>
<li>New production method</li>
<li>New market</li>
<li>New forms of organisation</li>
</ul>
<p>Wealth is created when such innovation results in new demand. The function of an entrepreneur can therefore be defined as one of combining various input factors in an innovative manner to generate value to the customer with the hope that this venture will exceed the cost of the input factor, thus generating superior returns that results in the creation of wealth.</p>

<li>It provides large scale employment</li>
<li>It promotes balanced regional development</li>
<li>It helps reduce the concentration of economic power</li>
<li>It stimulates the equitable redistribution of wealth, income and even political power in the interest of the country.</li>

<li>Income received from entrepreneurship will be used to improve the standard of living and quality of life</li>

<li>It induces backward and forward linkages which stimulates the process of economic development in the country.</li>
<li>It promotes country's export trade i.e. an important ingredient to economic development.</li>

<p>Early references to the entrepreneur in the 14th century spoke about tax contractors – individuals who paid a fixed sum of money to a government for the license to collect taxes in their region. Entrepreneurship was a common topic in economic essays for much of the 18th and 19th centuries. Notable early French, British and Austrian economists wrote enthusiastically about entrepreneurs as the ‘change agents’ of progressive economies.</p>
<p>The concept of entrepreneurship evolved naturally over the years.</p>
<p>☐ The first economist to have used the term entrepreneurship was Richard Cantillion, a French economist of Irish descent. He is credited with giving the concept of entrepreneurship a central role in economics.</p>
<p>Cantillion described an entrepreneur as a person who pays a certain price for a product to resale it at an uncertain price, thereby making decisions about obtaining and using resources while consequently assuming the risk of enterprise. Thus, according to Cantillion, “an entrepreneur is a person who undertakes to do a job” or is a bearer of risk which is non-insurable; engages in market exchange in order to make profit.</p>
<p>Cantillion’s work was published posthumously during 1755 in his book ‘Essai sur la nature du commerce en general’. He recognized three classes of economic agents – land owners, entrepreneurs and labourers.</p>
<p>☐ A little later, Adam Smith in his famous book ‘Wealth of Nations’ (1776) introduced the word ‘enterpriser’ as an individual who undertook the formation of an</p>
<p>organization for commercial purposes. He thereby ascribed to the entrepreneur the role of industrialist and also described his quality of having unusual foresight to recognize potential demand for goods and services. Adam Smith viewed entrepreneurs as instrumental to economic change, thereby becoming the economic agents who transformed demand into supply. Thus, according to Smith, - an entrepreneur is a person who acts as agent in transforming demand into supply.</p>
<p>Jean Baptiste Say (1845), the French economist described an entrepreneur as one who possessed the arts and skills of creating new economic enterprises, a person who had exceptional insight into society's needs and was able to fulfill them. Therefore, according to Say, an entrepreneur is someone who consciously moves economic resources from an area of lower into an area of higher productivity. Say therefore combined the 'economic risk taker' of Cantillion and the 'industrial manager' of Adam Smith in a unique manner. Say's entrepreneur influenced society by creating new enterprises and at the same time was influenced by society to recognize needs and fulfill them through efficient management of resources.</p>
<p>By 1848, John Stuart Mill, the British economist recognized entrepreneurship as central to economic theory. Mill emphasized on the necessity of entrepreneurship in private enterprise. The term entrepreneur subsequently became common as a description of business founders and the fourth factor of economic endeavor that was essential for ownership of a commercial enterprise. Thus, according to Mill, an entrepreneur is a prime mover in the private enterprise</p>
<p>Carl Menger, the Austrian economist, made a revolutionary contribution in 1871 through his book 'Principles of Economics' where he introduced the model of value – added transformation of resources. Menger expressed economic change does not arise from circumstances but from an individual's awareness and understanding of those circumstances. The entrepreneur is thus the change agent who transforms resources into useful goods and services –</p>
<ul>
<li>Entrepreneur acts as an economic agent who transforms resources into products and services (transforms and gives added value)</li>
</ul>

<p>Joseph Aloysius Schumpeter (1934), the notable Austrian economist revived the concept of entrepreneurship in his famous works between 1911 and 1950, while serving at the Harvard University. Schumpeter described entrepreneurship as a force of "creative destruction" whereby established ways of doing things are destroyed by the creation of new and better ways. Schumpeter described entrepreneurship as a process and entrepreneurs as innovators who use the process to shatter the status quo through new combinations of resources and new methods of commerce. Schumpeter thus was undoubtedly more responsible than any other economist of his time for the revival of the interest on the functions and characteristics of the entrepreneur. He saw entrepreneur as innovator—the individual who introduces new production functions. To quote him, "the essence of entrepreneurship lies in the perception and exploration of new opportunities in the realm of business. It always has to do with bringing about a different use of national resources in that they are withdrawn from their traditional employ and subjected to new combinations". Thus, Schumpeter brought into sharp focus the role of innovation as an essential entrepreneurial function. He argued that the entrepreneur changes the conditions of supply, combines resources in new ways and thereby sets a new production function. According to him, innovations that produce upswings in economic activity include one or more of the following:</p>

<li>Introduction of new goods</li>

<li>The opening of a new market</li>

<p>6.1 Entrepreneurship and Self - employment</p>
<p>Someone who is self-employed is the owner of a business, an individual who earns a living by working for himself/herself and not as an employee of someone else. You are self-employed if you have one of the following types of businesses: sole proprietorship, limited liability company (LLC).</p>
<p>Some ways to Make Self-Employment Work</p>
<p>Many of us have dreams of owning our own business. Considering the fact that doing so often allows owners to make their own hours, pick their own staff, and do what they love, why wouldn't we dream big? Of American adults, 53 percent have actually considered starting their own business, many of whom consider doing so in order to make more money. Unfortunately, according to Bloomberg, 80 percent of businesses fail in the first 18 months. There is clearly a large discrepancy between those who want to start a business and those who actually succeed at being self-employed. There are several things that will help increase the chance that self -employment will work for an individual. The following is an important checklist:</p>
<ol>
<li>Come up with a good idea and a business plan</li>
</ol>
<p>Not anyone can start a business. In addition to financial backing, you will need a good idea. If you have a unique talent that can be turned into an idea, then you might already be on your way. If you have a passion that would make a good business, then you are also set. However, if you simply know that you want to be your own boss, but you don't have any great ideas yet, you might need to think about this and do some research.</p>

If your city of choice already has five pizza restaurants for 20,000 people, you might want to rethink your choice of restaurant. In addition, if possible, find out what businesses have failed before. The more you know about your potential business venture, and the spot you are choosing to create it, the better start you will have. Some areas really need new businesses, especially neighbourhoods that are up and coming, and close to bigger cities.</p>

<p>In order to own your own business, you must be motivated and on-task. You also will probably need to have strong communication skills, excellent interpersonal skills, and be extremely organized. If you are starting without a lot of capital, you may need to do both the business side and the people side mostly by yourself for a while. If you are starting a business that will require you to serve or interact with customers face-to-face daily, you will probably need to hire a staff. If you are uncomfortable interviewing, managing, or simply interacting with other people, you will need to hire someone who is comfortable overseeing those aspects of your job. You also might want to consider a business partner who has the skills that you lack.</p>

Having enough money to really help your business grow will increase your chance of success. <p>The process of identifying business opportunity</p>
<p>An entrepreneur should follow the following processes in order to identify an appropriate business opportunity.</p>
<p>a) Identify your strength – strength can be a talent that has been in a person since earlier days and has always manifested itself. One needs to identify these traits and look at their business aspects.</p>
<p>b) Assess the surrounding – the entrepreneur needs to study the current political, social and economic environment to see how they are likely to affect his business in the long run. He or she should also analyse the competitors and identify the sources of his business supplies.</p>
<p>c) Identification of gaps – after serious study and analysis, the entrepreneur can identify opportunities that can give rise to a business if exploited. An opportunity can be a market demand which has not been satisfied.</p>
<p>d) Selection of gaps to fill – the opportunities may be very many but the entrepreneur needs to identify only those opportunities which are related to his/her strengths as identified earlier on.</p>
<p>e) Identification of risks and constraints – the entrepreneur will look at potential weaknesses that would exist in the business. Each business venture has pitfalls which if not well planned for, can ruin the business. The risks and constraints may include; change in legislation, natural disasters, burglary etc. he/she may mitigate against these risks by insuring the business against losses that may result from these events.</p>
<p>f) Lay out an execution plan – the entrepreneur should put all his ideas and plans writing. This involves coming up with a business plan that clearly sets out the objectives and strategies of the business.</p>

<p>Motivation has the following characteristics:</p>
<p>i. Motivation is a personal and internal feeling – it is a psychological phenomenon that arises in the mind of an individual when his needs and wants are satisfied.
ii. Motivation is a continuous process. Human needs are unlimited. Satisfaction of one need leads to feeling of another and the process goes on.
iii. Human needs are interrelated and influence human behaviour in a different way.
iv. Motivation causes goal-directed behaviour. A person works for the goal to obtain rewards that satisfy his needs.</p>
<div data-testid="stHeadingWithActionElements" class="st-emotion-cache-1p9ibxm e1icttdg2"><h2 level="2" id="motivational-process">Motivational process<span data-testid="stHeaderActionElements" class="st-emotion-cache-gi0tri e1icttdg3"><a href="#motivational-process" class="st-emotion-cache-yinll1 e1icttdg1"><svg xmlns="http://www.w3.org/2000/svg" width="16" height="16" viewBox="0 0 24 24" fill="none" stroke="currentColor" stroke-width="2" stroke-linecap="round" stroke-linejoin="round"><path d="M15 7h3a5 5 0 0 1 5 5 5 5 0 0 1-5 5h-3m-6 0H6a5 5 0 0 1-5-5 5 5 0 0 1 5-5h3"></path><line x1="8" y1="12" x2="16" y2="12"></line></svg></a></span></h2></div>
<p>Motivation is a cycle which does not end. It is always there as long as human needs are there. The various steps in the process of motivation are as follows.</p>
<p>i. Awareness of needs. A person becomes aware of an unsatisfied need. This creates tension in the mind of that person.</p>
<p>ii. Search for action. In order to relieve his tension and satisfy his needs, the individual looks for a suitable action. He develops certain goals and makes an attempt to achieve them.</p>
<p>iii. Fulfillment of need. In case the individual is successful in his attempt, his need is satisfied and he feels motivated. If the attempt is unsuccessful the need remains unsatisfied and the individual engages himself in search for a new action.</p>
<p>iv. Discovery of new need. Once one need is fulfilled, some other need will emerge and the individual will set a new goal. This process continues to work within an individual because human needs are unlimited.</p>

<ul>
<li>Identification of the business idea or the business opportunity. This is the first step of starting a business. The business opportunity describes the type of business a person desires to do. The opportunity is identified through market research, consultation with others, observation etc. sometimes more than one opportunities may be identified.</li>
<li>Evaluation of opportunities—where more than one opportunity exists, the entrepreneur measures and compares each opportunity with the others. The opportunity is measured in terms of the cost of implementing it, the income expected from it and the availability of technical skills and equipments to implement it.</li>
<li>Selection of the best opportunity - after evaluating the opportunities, the best opportunity is selected. The best idea would be the one that would provide maximum possible returns at minimum possible costs, not only in the short run but also from a long-term point of view.</li>
<li>Decision on location – the entrepreneur then decides where the business should be located. A good location is one which is close to the source of raw materials and customers, with good infrastructures, social amenities and security.</li>

<p>Business is concerned with the production and distribution of goods and services for the satisfaction of needs of society. For carrying out various activities, business requires money. Finance, therefore, is called the life blood of any business. The requirement of funds by a business to carry out its various activities is called business</p>
<p>finance.</p>
<p>A business cannot function unless adequate funds are made available to it. The initial capital contributed by the entrepreneur is not always sufficient to take care of all financial requirements of the business. A business person, therefore, has to look for different other sources from where the need for funds can be met. A clear assessment of the financial needs and the identification of various sources of finance, therefore, is a significant aspect of running a business organization.</p>
<p>The need for funds arises from the stage when an entrepreneur makes a decision to start a business. Some funds are needed immediately say for the purchase of plant and machinery, furniture, and other fixed assets. Similarly, some funds are required for day-to-day operations, say to purchase raw materials, pay salaries to employees, etc. Also when the business expands, it needs funds.</p>
<p>The financial needs of a business can be categorised as follows:</p>
<p><strong>Fixed capital requirements:</strong> In order to start business, funds are required to purchase fixed assets like land and building, plant and machinery, and furniture and fixtures. This is known as fixed capital requirements of the enterprise. The funds required in fixed assets remain invested in the business for a long period of time.</p>
<p><strong>Working Capital requirements:</strong> The financial requirements of an enterprise do not end with the procurement of fixed assets. No matter how small or large a business is, it needs funds for its day-to-day operations. This is known as working capital of an enterprise, which is used for holding current assets such as stock of material, bills receivables and for meeting current expenses like salaries, wages, taxes, and rent. The requirement for fixed and working capital increases with the growth and expansion of business.</p>

<p>Equity financing means exchanging a portion of the ownership of the business for a financial investment in the business.</p>
<p>It involves no direct obligation to repay any funds; but involves selling a partial interest in your business. <div data-testid="stHeadingWithActionElements" class="st-emotion-cache-1p9ibxm e1icttdg2"><h2 level="2" id="debt-financing">Debt Financing<span data-testid="stHeaderActionElements" class="st-emotion-cache-gi0tri e1icttdg3"><a href="#debt-financing" class="st-emotion-cache-yinll1 e1icttdg1"><svg xmlns="http://www.w3.org/2000/svg" width="16" height="16" viewBox="0 0 24 24" fill="none" stroke="currentColor" stroke-width="2" stroke-linecap="round" stroke-linejoin="round"><path d="M15 7h3a5 5 0 0 1 5 5 5 5 0 0 1-5 5h-3m-6 0H6a5 5 0 0 1-5-5 5 5 0 0 1 5-5h3"></path><line x1="8" y1="12" x2="16" y2="12"></line></svg></a></span></h2></div>
<p>(e.g. banks and commercial lenders, Government Programs, lease)</p>
<p>Debt is a direct obligation to pay something (cash) to someone (an investor or lender).</p>
<p>Debt financing involves borrowing funds from creditors with the stipulation of repaying the borrowed funds plus interest at a specified future time. For the creditors (those lending the funds to the business), the reward for providing the debt financing is the interest on the amount lent to the borrower.</p>
<p>Debt financing may be secured or unsecured. Secured debt has collateral (a valuable asset which the lender can attach to satisfy the loan in case of default by the borrower).</p>
<p>Conversely, unsecured debt does not have collateral and places the lender in a less secure position relative to repayment in case of default.</p>
<p>Debt financing (loans) may be short term or long term in their repayment schedules. Generally, short-term debt is used to financing current activities such as operations while long-term debt is used to finance assets such as buildings and equipment.</p>
<p>What do debt lenders look for? A debt lender will evaluate your loan request by considering answers to several questions: can you offer reasonable evidence of repayment ability either established earnings (for an existing business) or income (profit &amp; loss) projections (for a new business)? Do you have sufficient management experience to operate the business? Do you have enough equity in the business? Equity provides what lenders call a cushion for creditors. For example, a manager in a corporation is supposed to ensure that the company is profitable so that its stockholder-owners receive a return on their investment. In other words, the manager has a responsibility to investors. If she instead makes decisions that give her more power or money but do not help the company, then she has a conflict of interest—she is acting to benefit herself at the expense of her company and is not fulfilling her responsibilities. To avoid conflicts</p>

<p>It is considered improper to give or accept bribes—payments, gifts, or special favours intended to influence the outcome of a decision. A bribe is a conflict of interest because it benefits an individual at the expense of an organization or society.</p>
