üë• Types of Entrepreneurs

üõ†Ô∏è Craft Entrepreneurs

Exploits and utilizes personal skills to start a business without thinking of its growth or the expansion of a business. Often, most than not in this type of entrepreneur, there is no expanding even after a long time, it is not a business expansion oriented. This skills can be technical skills, professional skill, etc.

üöÄ Opportunistic Entrepreneurs

This is a person who starts a business, acts as a manager and with a view to expand the business to maximum. He sees beyond and has abilities to initiate and venture into business that will expand and grow. He is innovative, i.e. somebody able to delegate activities to others, ready and able to see, scan the environment.

ü§ù Social Entrepreneur

Recognizes a social problem and uses entrepreneurial principles to organize, create and manage a venture to achieve a social change.

üèõÔ∏è Political Entrepreneur

Is a business person who utilizes political systems or seeks support from political bodies in order to promote, expand and profit from their own commercial ventures.

Other Classifications

Type Description
Concept Multipliers Someone who identifies a successful concept that can be duplicated by others.
Acquirer Those who take over a business started by others and use their own idea to make it successful.
Buy/Sell Artists Those who buy a company for the purpose of improving it before selling it for a profit.
Economy of Scale Exploiters Those who benefit from large volume of sales by offering discount prices and operating with low overheads.
Inventors Those with particular inventive abilities who design a better product and then create a company to develop, produce and sell them.

‚ú® Characteristics/Traits of a Potential Entrepreneur

  • Initiative and risk taking:
    • Doing things before being asked or forced by events
    • Acts to extend business into new areas products etc
    • Sees and acts on opportunity
  • Persistence and patience:
    • Taking repeated action to overcome obstacles
    • Taking action in the face of significant obstacles
  • Information and property seeking:
    • Takes action on his own to get information to help reach business objectives
    • Does personal research on how to provide a product or service
    • Consultation of experts on business and technical advice
  • Concern for high quality work:
    • Acting to do things that meet or beat existing standards
    • A desire to produce and sell top and better quality products or services
  • Commitment to work contract:
    • Placing the highest priority on getting the job completed
    • Accepts full responsibility for problems that may arise in getting the job done
  • Efficiency orientation:
    • Finding ways of doing things faster and cost effectively
    • Uses information to improve efficiency
  • Systematic planning:
    • Developing and using logical plans to meet goals
    • Breaking tasks down to sub-tasks
    • Developing plans which anticipate obstacles
    • Evaluates alternatives
  • Self-confidence:
    • Has a strong belief in self and own abilities
    • Expresses confidence in own ability to complete task or meet challenges
    • Sticks with own judgment in the face of opposition or early lack of success
  • Persuasion:
    • Convinces people to buy the products or service
    • Convinces people on providing funds
    • Asserts own competence reliability and the company product
  • Uses strategic influence and networking:
    • To develop business contact
    • Uses influential people as agent to accomplish objectives

üìã Factors to Consider in Starting a Business

  1. Personal Factors
    1. Personal abilities: One must have personality traits that will enable him/her to start, continue and succeed in business (innovative, creative, optimistic).
    2. Skills: One needs to have the training and experience necessary in the proposed line of business.
    3. Interests / hobbies: An interest or hobby can be a basis for a business.
    4. Family: Small businesses and family are inseparable; consider possible effects of the business on the family and vice versa.
    5. Health: Running a business involves a lot of physical and mental energies.
  2. Resource Factors
    1. Financial Resources: Money saved by the entrepreneur or borrowed from individuals/institutions to start and run a business.
    2. Labor Resources: Availability and suitability of labor should be assessed.
    3. Equipment/Machinery: Tools of the trade essential in any business (furniture, machines, etc.).
    4. Raw Materials: Items that are to be converted to states that can be readily used by consumers.
  3. Environmental Factors
    1. Location: Factors such as community profile, communication, road, water, safety, source of raw materials and competitions.
    2. Government Policies / regulations: Policies such as taxation, pricing, licensing, loans and policy documents.
    3. Infrastructure: Availability of roads, power, water and telephones.
    4. The economy of the society: Average income and employment trends determine the demand.
    5. Competition: Assess the number of competitors in the environment.
    6. Market: Assess whether there is a steady market for the product or services.
    7. Community and Culture: Consider general customer behavior, politics, taboos/beliefs (e.g., Muslims cannot operate any business dealing with pork products), and traditions.
