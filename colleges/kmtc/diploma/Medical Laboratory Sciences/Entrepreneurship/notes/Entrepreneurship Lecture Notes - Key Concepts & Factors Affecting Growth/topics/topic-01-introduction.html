The Entrepreneur

An entrepreneur is a person who identifies a business opportunity, harnesses and obtains the resources necessary to initiate a successful business activity. The entrepreneur implements the idea and undertakes to operate the business.

Entrepreneurship refers to the means of stimulating innovative and creative undertakings for a better business community or world. It is the act or process of identifying business opportunities and gathering the necessary resources to initiate a successful business activity.

It is a French word meaning to undertake and focuses on a business enterprise. Entrepreneurship encompasses:

  • Identifying a business opportunity of a particular demand.
  • Looking at the opportunity as a process of creating something that did not exist.
  • Constantly searching/harnessing one's environment and resources to implement the activities.
  • Creating a totally new product and using it in a new way.

Enterprise: A business undertaking by an individual for the purpose of making a profit.

üìâ Factors Impeding the Growth of Entrepreneurship

Several factors can hinder the development and growth of entrepreneurial ventures:

  • High Taxation Levels: Reduces profits earned and raises production costs.
  • Corruption and Official Harassment: Forced bribes to officials and arbitrary raids.
  • Unregulated competition from outside countries: Liberalization opening imports competing with locally produced goods.
  • Decline in personal incomes: Due to increasing cost of living and rise in unemployment.
  • The high cost of finance: High cost of borrowing and inability to repay loans.
  • Lack of entrepreneurial culture.
  • Poor transport and communication networks: High energy costs and inconveniences.
  • Lack of skills and knowledge: Due to lack of training opportunities and high education costs.

üåç Contribution to National Development

Entrepreneurship contributes to economic growth, improved quality of life, equitable distribution of resources, and the capacity of the economy to deal with economic, social, and environmental development.

üéØ

Main Objectives of National Development

  • Provide basic needs to every citizen: food, shelter, health, security, education, clothing.
  • Elimination / Reduction of poverty: reduce rural-urban migration.
  • Provision of opportunities for employment and personal advancement.
  • Trying to narrow the income differences between the rich and the poor.

Specific Contributions:

  1. Employment Creation
  2. Promotion of National Productivity: Through production of goods and services within the country and therefore the growth of the Gross Domestic Product (GDP).
  3. Raising Standards of Living: Measured by the capacity of people of a country to buy goods and services. Entrepreneurs contribute by employing people and giving themselves or wages to buy their necessities.
  4. Conservation of foreign Exchange: Reduction in importing goods hence the governments' save foreign exchange.
  5. Export Promotion: Entrepreneurs produce goods and services for exporters.
  6. Conservation of Resources: Entrepreneurs can contribute to conservation and utilization of resources (e.g., manufacturing a Jiko that use sawdust instead of wood charcoal).
  7. Rural development: Promotes change to the formal sector from informal sector, equitable development, and reduced rural-urban migrations.
  8. Development of technology: Through establishment of research institutions and education systems.
  9. Government revenue: Through taxes and from domestic borrowing (TBs).
  10. Facilitating community development: Through establishment of small businesses and participation in community dev. Projects.
  11. Providing a positive role model: Facilitating competition between domestic entrepreneurship and imported goods.
  12. Reducing dependence on imported goods and services.
  13. Stimulates competition: Quality production methods are adopted, quality products are produced, and variety of goods and services are produced.
  14. Facilitated development of the financial sector: Capital accumulation is possible through savings, loaning is facilitated, and development of the capital market.

üè¢ Intrapreneurship

Entrepreneurship looks at particular individuals in a business set-up. It operates in large business or organization which is business minded to make profit. Entrepreneurs operate autonomously for the welfare of the organization.

The term Intra refers to within. Therefore, Intrapreneurship is a process whereby an individual or group within a large organization creates something new or different to maximize on the available opportunities to that organization.

üí°

Concept

Intra can therefore be equated to entra within the context of a large organization where the workers are given a leeway to be creative or innovative on their own. They become competitive socially and economically; the idea is to allow individuals within the organization to act and think independently.
