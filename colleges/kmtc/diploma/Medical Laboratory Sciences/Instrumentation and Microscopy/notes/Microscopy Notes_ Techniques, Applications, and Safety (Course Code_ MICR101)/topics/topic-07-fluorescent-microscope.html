Part Function
Light Source (High-intensity lamp, e.g., mercury or LED lamp) Emits intense light for exciting fluorescent dyes.
Excitation Filter Selects specific wavelengths to excite the fluorophores in the specimen.
Dichroic Mirror (Beam Splitter) Reflects the excitation light down to the specimen and allows emitted light to pass through to the eyepiece or camera.

Emission Filter Blocks any remaining excitation light and allows only fluorescence to pass through.
Eyepiece/Camera Used to view or capture the fluorescent image.
Specimen Stage Holds the slide in position. Often motorized in advanced models.
Focus Adjustment Knobs Used for coarse and fine focusing of the image.
Power Supply Unit Powers the high-intensity light source and other electronic parts.

üí° Principle of Operation

Fluorescence microscopy relies on the ability of certain dyes (fluorophores) to absorb high-energy light (usually UV or blue) and emit lower-energy light (visible wavelengths).

The process involves:

  1. Excitation:
    Light from the lamp passes through the excitation filter, allowing only specific wavelengths to reach the specimen.
  2. Emission:
    The fluorescent molecules in the specimen absorb the excitation light and emit fluorescence.
  3. Detection:
    The emitted light passes through the dichroic mirror and emission filter to reach the eyepiece or camera for viewing or imaging.

üîß Common Faults, Causes, and Remedies

Fault Possible Cause Remedy
No illumination Blown lamp or power failure Replace lamp or check power supply
Weak fluorescence signal Faded fluorophore or misaligned filters Use fresh dye, realign filters
Image not visible or blurry Poor focusing, dirty lenses or filters Adjust focus, clean lenses/filters
Uneven illumination Faulty light source or misaligned optics Realign light source, service optics
Excessive background fluorescence Non-specific staining or dirty slide Prepare slide properly, clean thoroughly
Flickering light Loose electrical connection or faulty bulb Tighten connection or replace bulb

üßπ Maintenance Procedures

1. Daily Checks

  • Ensure proper lamp functioning before use.
  • Clean lenses and filters using lens paper and appropriate solution.

2. Weekly

  • Check alignment of the excitation and emission filters.
  • Inspect the dichroic mirror for dust or damage.

3. Monthly

  • Test and calibrate the focus and stage movement.
  • Inspect electrical cords, switches, and connections.

4. As Needed

  • Replace the excitation lamp (mercury/halogen) as per manufacturer‚Äôs lifespan.
  • Clean or replace dirty filters and damaged parts.

General Tips

  • Always allow the lamp to cool before turning off.
  • Avoid touching optical components with bare fingers.

ü¶∫ Safety Precautions

Handling and Operation

  • Use UV Protection: Some light sources emit UV rays; wear protective eyewear.
  • Avoid Direct Exposure: Never look directly at the light source.
  • Follow Manufacturer Guidelines: Especially when replacing mercury lamps (they contain toxic materials).

Cleaning

  • Use Appropriate Materials: Only use lens paper and approved cleaning agents.
  • Turn Off and Unplug: Before cleaning or performing maintenance.

Storage

  • Cover the Microscope: Use a dust cover when not in use.
  • Cool Down Before Storing: Allow the light source to cool before storage.
  • Store in a Dry, Dust-Free Environment: Prevent moisture and fungal growth.
