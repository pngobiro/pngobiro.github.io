üí°

Definition

Microscopy is the technique of using a microscope to view objects and areas of objects that cannot be seen with the naked eye. It is essential in medical diagnostics, research, and quality control in healthcare settings.

02

üéØ Purpose and Application

In Medical Engineering and healthcare, microscopy serves several critical functions:

  • To examine cells, tissues, and microorganisms (e.g., in pathology labs).
  • To aid in diagnosing infections, cancers, and blood disorders.
  • To assess material surfaces in medical equipment (e.g., wear or contamination).
  • To support research in drug development and biomedical sciences
03

üî¨ Types of Microscopes

üìä Classification of Microscopes
Type Description Application
Light Microscope Uses visible light to magnify samples. Viewing stained tissue, bacteria, and blood smears.
Electron Microscope (EM) Uses electron beams for very high magnification. Imaging viruses, cell ultrastructure.
Fluorescence Microscope Uses UV light and fluorescent dyes. Detecting specific proteins or cells using tags.
Digital Microscope Captures and displays images on a screen. Teaching, image documentation, telepathology.
