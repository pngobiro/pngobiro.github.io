Section 1: Multiple Choice Questions (20 Marks)

1.
Jean Piaget is known for?
A. Cognitive psychology
B. Multiple intelligence theory
C. Triarchic intelligence
D. Psychosocial theory
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A. Cognitive psychology

Explanation: Jean Piaget is a pioneer in the study of child development and is most famous for his theory of cognitive development, which describes how children construct a mental model of the world.

Why others are incorrect:

  • B. Associated with Howard Gardner.
  • C. Associated with Robert Sternberg.
  • D. Associated with Erik Erikson.
2.
The proponent of multiple intelligence theory is?
A. Erick Erickson
B. James bond
C. Jean Piaget
D. Howard Gardener
Show Answer

Correct Answer: D. Howard Gardener

Explanation: Howard Gardner proposed the theory of multiple intelligences in 1983, suggesting that intelligence is not a single general ability but consists of distinct modalities (e.g., linguistic, musical, spatial).

3.
The proponent of structuralism was:
A. William James
B. Edward Tichna (Titchener)
C. Wilhelm Wundt
D. G. Stanley
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B. Edward Tichna (Titchener)

Explanation: While Wilhelm Wundt is often called the father of psychology, it was his student, Edward B. Titchener, who formally established the school of thought known as Structuralism in the United States. Structuralism sought to analyze the adult mind in terms of the simplest definable components.

Note: William James is associated with Functionalism.

4.
Which of the following are proponents of Humanistic as the contemporary school of thought?
A. Sigmund Freud and Erickson Neo Freudian
B. John Watson and Ivan Pavlov
C. Carl Rogers and Abraham Maslow
D. G. Stanley and Wilhelm Wundt
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C. Carl Rogers and Abraham Maslow

Explanation: Humanistic psychology, often called the "third force" in psychology, emphasizes individual potential and self-actualization. Its key figures are Carl Rogers (Client-Centered Therapy) and Abraham Maslow (Hierarchy of Needs).

5.
Sigmund Freud and Erickson Neo Freudian are proponents of modern approaches to psychology called?
A. Psychodynamic
B. Cognitive
C. Humanistic
D. Behavioral
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A. Psychodynamic

Explanation: The psychodynamic approach includes all theories in psychology that see human functioning based upon the interaction of drives and forces within the person, particularly unconscious, and between the different structures of the personality. It stems from Freud's Psychoanalysis.

6.
The method of studying psychology that allow the researcher to collect data of an individual behavior and study for some time is called?
A. Test method
B. IQ Test
C. Experimental method
D. Case studies and Clinical studies
Show Answer

Correct Answer: D. Case studies and Clinical studies

Explanation: A case study involves an in-depth investigation of a single individual, group, or event over a period of time. It allows researchers to collect detailed data on behavior that might be rare or require long-term observation.

7.
Which of the following branches of psychology, study the relationship between physiological processes and behavior?
A. Counseling psychology
B. Social psychology
C. Biopsychology
D. Clinical psychology
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C. Biopsychology

Explanation: Biopsychology (also known as biological psychology or physiological psychology) explores how biological processes, such as the brain, neurotransmitters, and genetics, influence behavior and mental processes.

8.
The domain of basic human psychology that refers to interactions and relationships with others is called?
A. Emotional
B. Social
C. Mental
D. physical
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B. Social

Explanation: The social domain of development focuses on how individuals interact with others, form relationships, and understand the social world around them.

9.
Which of the following is a factor influencing human growth and development?
A. socioeconomic
B. Sociocultural
C. social life
D. physiology
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A. socioeconomic (or D. physiology)

Explanation: Both socioeconomic status and physiology are factors. However, in the context of external variables that cause differences in development, socioeconomic status is often highlighted as a major environmental influence affecting nutrition, healthcare access, and education. Physiology is an internal biological factor. Without a strict marking key, socioeconomic is a strong candidate for an environmental influence.

Study Note: Factors are generally categorized into Heredity (Nature/Physiology) and Environment (Nurture/Socioeconomic/Cultural).

10.
Abraham Maslow is known for?
A. moral development theory of human growth and development
B. psychosocial theory of human growth and development
C. Growth and development needs
D. Hierarchy of human needs

Correct Answer: D. Hierarchy of human needs

Explanation: Maslow created the pyramid of human needs, starting from physiological needs at the base to self-actualization at the top.

11.
The proponent of moral development as a theory of human growth and development is?
A. John Bowlby's
B. Erick Erickson
C. Gilligan
D. Mary Ainsworth
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C. Gilligan

Explanation: Lawrence Kohlberg is the primary figure for Moral Development, but he is not an option. Carol Gilligan proposed a theory of moral development (Ethics of Care) in response to Kohlberg's theory, arguing that women's morality is focused on relationships and care rather than abstract justice.

Other options: Bowlby and Ainsworth are Attachment Theory; Erikson is Psychosocial Development.

12.
The following factors affect our ability to memorize things?
A. Encoding
B. Storage
C. retrieval
D. Interference
Show Answer

Correct Answer: D. Interference

Explanation: Encoding, Storage, and Retrieval are the stages of memory processing. Interference (Proactive and Retroactive) is a phenomenon/factor that negatively affects the ability to retain or recall information, thus affecting memory performance.

13.
Fluid and crystallized intelligence are two components of general intelligence according to?
A. Raymond Cattell
B. Robert Sternberg
C. Jean Piaget
D. Howard Gardner
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A. Raymond Cattell

Explanation: Raymond Cattell proposed the distinction between fluid intelligence (problem-solving in novel situations) and crystallized intelligence (ability to use learned knowledge and experience).

14.
Motivation coming from within an individual is called?
A. Intrinsic
B. Extrinsic
C. General
D. Personality
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A. Intrinsic

Explanation: Intrinsic motivation refers to behavior that is driven by internal rewards (e.g., satisfaction, curiosity). Extrinsic motivation is driven by external rewards (e.g., money, grades).

15.
Hearing of voices that are not really there would be called?
A. Hallucination
B. Delusion
C. Auditory regression
D. Depressive psychosis
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A. Hallucination

Explanation: A hallucination is a sensory perception in the absence of an external stimulus. Hearing voices is specifically an auditory hallucination. A delusion (Option B) is a false belief.

16.
Major processes involved in memory in their order are?
A. Retrieval, encoding, and storage
B. Encoding, storage, and retrieval
C. Retrieval, storage, and encoding
D. Storage, retrieval, and encoding
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B. Encoding, storage, and retrieval

Explanation: The standard information processing model of memory follows this sequence:
1. Encoding: Getting info in.
2. Storage: Retaining info.
3. Retrieval: Getting info out.

17.
Continuing storage of information refers to?
A. Long-term memory
B. Short-term memory
C. Sensory memory
D. General memory
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A. Long-term memory

Explanation: Long-term memory is the stage of the memory model where informative knowledge is held indefinitely. Sensory and Short-term memories are transient/temporary.

18.
Which of the following is not a reason why forgetting occurs?
A. Failure to store
B. Interference
C. Motivated forgetting
D. Retrieval ability
Show Answer

Correct Answer: D. Retrieval ability

Explanation: Options A (Encoding failure), B (Interference), and C (Repression/Suppression) are all accepted theories/reasons for why we forget. "Retrieval ability" describes the capacity to recall, not a cause of forgetting. "Retrieval failure" (or cue-dependent forgetting) would be a reason, but "ability" is not.

19.
People who are always competing against time, belong to personality type?
A. Type A
B. Type B
C. Type C
D. Type D
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A. Type A

Explanation: Type A personalities are characterized as competitive, high-achieving, impatient, and often have a sense of time urgency ("hurry sickness"). Type B is relaxed; Type C is detail-oriented/suppressive; Type D is distressed.

20.
A goal of psychology that can foresee what might happen in the future is?
A. Describe
B. Explain
C. Predict
D. Control
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C. Predict

Explanation: The four goals of psychology are Describe, Explain, Predict, and Control. Prediction specifically involves forecasting future behavior based on current knowledge and data.

Section 2: Short Answer Questions (40 Marks)

21.
Outline five (5) branches of basic human psychology (5 marks)
Show Answer

Answer:

  1. Biopsychology (Physiological Psychology): Studies the biological basis of behavior (brain, genetics).
  2. Cognitive Psychology: Studies mental processes like memory, thinking, and problem-solving.
  3. Developmental Psychology: Studies changes in human behavior across the lifespan.
  4. Social Psychology: Studies how individuals interact with and are influenced by others.
  5. Clinical Psychology: Focuses on the diagnosis and treatment of mental disorders.
  6. Personality Psychology: Studies individual differences in characteristic patterns of thinking, feeling, and behaving.

26.
List five (5) theories of emotion (5 marks)
Show Answer

Answer:

  1. James-Lange Theory: Physiological arousal comes before emotion (We feel sad because we cry).
  2. Cannon-Bard Theory: Physiological arousal and emotional experience occur simultaneously.
  3. Schachter-Singer (Two-Factor) Theory: Emotion equals physiological arousal plus cognitive labeling of that arousal.
  4. Lazarus (Cognitive Appraisal) Theory: Thought comes before emotion/arousal.
  5. Facial Feedback Hypothesis: Facial expressions influence emotional experience.
  6. Evolutionary Theory (Darwin): Emotions exist because they serve an adaptive role.
27.
Briefly describe two (2) types of motivation (5 marks)
Show Answer

Answer:

  1. Intrinsic Motivation: The drive to perform an action because the act itself is rewarding, interesting, or satisfying (e.g., reading a book for pleasure).
  2. Extrinsic Motivation: The drive to perform an action to receive an external reward or avoid punishment (e.g., working to earn money, studying to avoid failing).

Section 3: Long Answer Questions (40 Marks)

29.
Briefly discuss five (5) theories of motivation (20 marks)
Show Answer

Answer:

  1. Maslow’s Hierarchy of Needs:

    Proposed by Abraham Maslow, this theory suggests human needs are arranged in a pyramid. We must satisfy lower-level needs (Physiological: food, water) before moving to higher levels (Safety, Love/Belonging, Esteem) and finally Self-Actualization (realizing one's potential).

  2. Drive-Reduction Theory (Hull):

    Suggests that physiological needs (like hunger or thirst) create an aroused tension state (a drive) that motivates an organism to satisfy the need (eat/drink) to return to homeostasis (balance).

  3. Incentive Theory (Skinner/Behavioral):

    Posits that behavior is motivated by the desire for external rewards (incentives). Positive reinforcement strengthens behavior (studying to get an A), while punishment discourages it.

  4. Herzberg’s Two-Factor Theory:

    Focuses on workplace motivation. Identify "Hygiene Factors" (salary, conditions) that prevent dissatisfaction but don't motivate, and "Motivators" (recognition, achievement) that actually drive high performance.

  5. Expectancy Theory (Vroom):

    Motivation depends on three factors:
    - Expectancy: Belief that effort leads to performance.
    - Instrumentality: Belief that performance leads to a reward.
    - Valence: The value placed on the reward.
    Formula: Motivation = E × I × V.

30.
Human growth and development begins with conception

a) Define growth and development (4 marks)

Show Answer
  • Growth: Refers to the physical increase in size, height, weight, and body dimensions. It is quantitative and measurable (e.g., growing 2 inches).
  • Development: Refers to the qualitative changes in functional ability, skills, and complexity. It involves the maturation of systems and acquisition of skills (e.g., learning to speak or solve problems).

b) Outline six (6) factors influencing growth and development (12 marks)

Show Answer
  1. Heredity/Genetics: Sets the biological potential (e.g., height cap, predisposition to diseases).
  2. Nutrition: Adequate nutrients are essential for cell growth and brain development; malnutrition causes stunting.
  3. Hormones: Growth hormone, thyroxine, and sex hormones regulate physical growth rates.
  4. Environment (Physical): Pollution, climate, and living conditions affect health and growth.
  5. Socioeconomic Status: Determines access to quality food, medical care, and education.
  6. Emotional Factors/Love: Children in loving, supportive environments develop better socially and cognitively than those in neglected ones (psychosocial dwarfism).

c) List four domains of human growth and development (4 marks)

Show Answer
  1. Physical Domain: Body size, brain development, motor skills.
  2. Cognitive Domain: Thought processes, memory, language.
  3. Psychosocial/Emotional Domain: Interactions, feelings, personality.
  4. Moral/Spiritual Domain: Understanding of right/wrong and purpose.
