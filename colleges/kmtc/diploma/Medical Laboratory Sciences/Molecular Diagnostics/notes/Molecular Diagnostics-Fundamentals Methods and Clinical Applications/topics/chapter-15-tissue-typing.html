DNA-Based Tissue Typing

üìö Techniques in the Clinical Lab
‚è±Ô∏è 40 min read

The Major Histocompatibility Complex (MHC) is a group of genes located on the short arm of chromosome 6. In humans, the MHC gene products are called Human Leukocyte Antigens (HLA). These molecules play a critical role in the immune system's ability to distinguish self from non-self. HLA typing is essential for organ and bone marrow transplantation to minimize rejection and Graft-Versus-Host Disease (GVHD).

01

üìç The MHC Locus

The MHC is the most polymorphic region in the human genome. It is divided into three regions: Class I, Class II, and Class III.

üìä Table 15.1 MHC Gene Classes
Class Key Loci Expression Function
Class I HLA-A, HLA-B, HLA-C All nucleated cells Present endogenous antigens to CD8+ Cytotoxic T cells.
Class II HLA-DR, HLA-DQ, HLA-DP B cells, Monocytes, Dendritic cells (APCs) Present exogenous antigens to CD4+ Helper T cells.
Class III C2, C4, TNF Plasma proteins Complement system and inflammation (not used for typing).
02

üìù HLA Nomenclature

Standardized nomenclature is maintained by the WHO Nomenclature Committee for Factors of the HLA System. The system has evolved from serological designations to precise DNA-based allele names.

üí°

Understanding the Name: HLA-A*02:101

  • HLA: Human Leukocyte Antigen region.
  • A: The gene locus (Gene A).
  • *: Separator indicating DNA-based typing.
  • 02: Allele group (corresponds to serological type, e.g., A2).
  • 101: Specific allele protein sequence (synonymous substitutions or non-coding changes may add more digits).

Resolution Levels:

  • Low Resolution: Identifies the allele group (2 digits), e.g., HLA-A*02. Equivalent to serology.
  • High Resolution: Identifies specific alleles (4 digits or more), e.g., HLA-A*02:01. Essential for bone marrow transplantation.
03

üß™ Molecular Analysis Methods

Serological Analysis (Traditional)

The Lymphocytotoxicity Test (CDC) uses complement and specific antibodies to kill cells expressing specific HLA antigens. Dead cells take up dye (eosin or trypan blue). While fast, it requires viable cells and has low resolution.

DNA-Based Typing

Molecular methods amplify the polymorphic regions (Exon 2 and 3 for Class I; Exon 2 for Class II). They offer higher resolution and do not require viable cells.

1. Sequence-Specific Oligonucleotide Probe (SSOP)

PCR is performed to amplify the gene of interest. The product is hybridized to a panel of probes immobilized on a membrane (Reverse Dot Blot) or microbeads (Luminex). The pattern of binding determines the allele.

  • Throughput: High (good for screening large batches).
  • Resolution: Low to Intermediate.

2. Sequence-Specific Primers (SSP-PCR)

Uses primers designed to bind only to specific alleles. If the allele is present, amplification occurs; if not, no product is formed. Detection is by agarose gel electrophoresis. An internal control is included in every well to verify PCR worked.

  • Throughput: Low (labor intensive).
  • Resolution: Low to High (depending on primer mix).
  • Speed: Fast (good for STAT cadaveric donor typing).

3. Sequence-Based Typing (SBT)

The gold standard for high-resolution typing. The polymorphic regions are amplified by PCR and then sequenced using Sanger sequencing. The resulting sequence is compared to a database of known HLA alleles.

  • Throughput: High (with capillary sequencers).
  • Resolution: Highest.
  • Use: Stem cell transplantation, confirming new alleles.
04

üõ°Ô∏è Additional Recognition Factors

Minor Histocompatibility Antigens (mHags)

Peptides derived from cellular proteins (other than MHC) that can be presented by MHC molecules. Mismatches can cause GVHD even in HLA-identical transplants.

Killer Cell Immunoglobulin-like Receptors (KIR)

Receptors on Natural Killer (NK) cells that recognize Class I MHC molecules. KIR-HLA mismatch can influence the outcome of hematopoietic stem cell transplantation (HSCT) through the "missing self" recognition mechanism, potentially aiding in the Graft-versus-Leukemia effect.

Key Points

  • ‚úì

    The MHC is located on chromosome 6 and contains the most polymorphic genes in the human genome.

  • ‚úì

    Class I (A, B, C) presents to CD8+ T cells; Class II (DR, DQ, DP) presents to CD4+ T cells.

  • ‚úì

    Molecular methods (SSOP, SSP, SBT) have largely replaced serology due to higher resolution and accuracy.

  • ‚úì

    High resolution typing is required for unrelated bone marrow transplantation to prevent rejection and GVHD.

  • ‚úì

    HLA-B27 is a classic marker for Ankylosing Spondylitis; HLA-B*57:01 screening prevents severe drug reactions to Abacavir.
