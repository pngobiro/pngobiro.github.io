01

üß¨ General Characteristics

The family Enterobacteriaceae consists of enterobacteria which inhabit the large intestines of man and animals.

Characteristics

  • Gram negative rods, facultatively anaerobic, non-sporing, non-acid fast.
  • Grow over a wide range of temperature on ordinary nutrient agar.
  • Generally motile (few non-motile).
  • Ferment glucose and other carbohydrates, reduce nitrate to nitrite.
  • Catalase positive and oxidase negative.

Antigenic Structure

Most genera possess the following antigens:

  1. O (somatic) antigens: Found on the cell wall. Heat stable. Used for subgrouping.
  2. K (surface, envelope) antigens: Cover the cell wall (e.g., Vi antigen of S. typhi). Heat labile.
  3. H (flagellar) antigens: Present in flagella. Useful for serotyping.

Endotoxin: All members possess an endotoxin responsible for endotoxic shock.

02

üî¨ Genus Escherichia

Escherichia coli

Characteristics: Gram negative straight rods ($1-3 \times 0.4-0.7 \mu\mathrm{m}$). Motile (peritrichous flagella). Facultatively anaerobic. Ferments lactose and glucose (gas +). Indole +, MR +, VP -, Citrate -.

Pathogenicity

  1. Urinary tract infections: Cystitis, pyelonephritis.
  2. Wound infection, peritonitis, gall bladder infection.
  3. Septicaemia and meningitis (neonates).
  4. Diarrhoeal Diseases:
    • EPEC (Enteropathogenic): Infantile diarrhoea.
    • ETEC (Enterotoxigenic): Traveller's diarrhoea. Produces Heat Stable (ST) and Heat Labile (LT) toxins.
    • EIEC (Enteroinvasive): Dysentery-like diarrhoea (blood/mucus).
    • EHEC (Enterohaemorrhagic): Haemorrhagic colitis (Serotype O157:H7).

üíä Genus Salmonella

Motile (except S. gallinarum/pullorum). Non-lactose fermenter. $H_2S$ positive. Urease negative.

Pathogenicity

  • Enteric Fever (Typhoid): Caused by S. typhi and S. paratyphi A, B, C. Systemic infection, rose spots, intestinal perforation.
  • Septicaemia: S. paratyphi C, S. cholerae-suis.
  • Food Poisoning: S. typhimurium, S. enteritidis. Zoonotic.
  • Osteomyelitis: In sickle-cell patients.

Widal Test

Tube agglutination test for enteric fever antibodies.

Interpretation

  • Significant Titre: Usually >80 (O or H).
  • Diagnostic: 4-fold rise in titre between acute and convalescent sera.
  • H antibodies persist longer than O antibodies.
  • Factors affecting results: Vaccination (TAB), previous infection, chronic liver disease.

üß´ Klebsiella & Related Genera

Genus Klebsiella

Non-motile, capsulated, lactose fermenter. Mucoid colonies.

  • K. pneumoniae: Pneumonia, UTI, septicaemia.
  • K. ozaenae: Ozaena (foul smelling nasal discharge).
  • K. rhinoscleromatis: Rhinoscleroma (granuloma of nose/pharynx).

Genus Enterobacter

Motile, lactose fermenter. Indole -, MR -, VP +, Citrate +. Opportunistic pathogen.

Genus Citrobacter

Motile. Late lactose fermenter. Citrate +, $H_2S$ variable. Confused with Salmonella/E. coli.

Genus Serratia

Motile. Non-lactose fermenter. DNAse +, Gelatinase +. Some strains produce red pigment (prodigiosin). Multi-drug resistant opportunistic pathogen.

06

üå´Ô∏è The Proteus Group

Genera: Proteus, Morganella, Providencia.

Common features: PPA positive, Urease positive (mostly), Non-lactose fermenters.

Genus Proteus

Swarming motility on non-inhibitory media. Fishy odour. $H_2S$ positive.

  • P. mirabilis: Indole negative. Commonest cause of infection.
  • P. vulgaris: Indole positive.

Weil-Felix Reaction: Certain Proteus strains (OX19, OX2, OXK) agglutinate with sera from Rickettsial patients.

üí°

Preventing Swarming

Use CLED agar (electrolyte deficient), MacConkey (bile salts), or increase agar concentration to 6%.

üê≠ Genus Yersinia

Gram negative coccobacilli, bipolar staining ("safety pin").

Yersinia enterocolitica

Pathogenicity: Gastroenteritis (mimics appendicitis - mesenteric lymphadenitis). Grows at 4¬∞C (cold enrichment).

Characteristics: Motile at 22¬∞C, non-motile at 37¬∞C. Urease positive.

Yersinia pestis

(Note: Often grouped separately, but described in context of Yersinia). Causes Plague (Bubonic, Pneumonic, Septicaemic). Transmitted by rat fleas (Xenopsylla cheopis). Non-motile.

üåä Other Related Enteric Bacteria

Genus Vibrio

Curved Gram negative rods, motile (polar flagellum). Oxidase positive. TCBS selective medium (Yellow colonies for V. cholerae).

  • V. cholerae: Cholera (Rice water stool). Serogroups O1 (Classical/El Tor) and O139 cause epidemics.
  • V. parahaemolyticus: Food poisoning (seafood). Halophilic (requires salt). Green colonies on TCBS.

Genus Pseudomonas

Strictly aerobic, motile. Oxidase positive. Non-fermenter (Oxidative).

Ps. aeruginosa: Produces pigments (Pyocyanin - blue/green, Fluorescein). "Grape-like" odour. Opportunistic pathogen (Burns, CF, UTI). Resistant to many antibiotics.

Genus Campylobacter

Curved/S-shaped/Gull-wing rods. Microaerophilic. Thermophilic (42¬∞C). C. jejuni causes gastroenteritis.

Genus Helicobacter

H. pylori: Spiral shaped. Urease positive (Rapid Urease Test). Associated with gastritis and peptic ulcers.

Genus Alcaligenes

A. faecalis: Oxidase positive, non-fermenter. Commensal/Opportunist.
