üëÅÔ∏è Genus Moraxella

Moraxella organisms are short stout Gram negative rods or coccobacilli, typically appearing in pairs which makes them look like gonococci. They are aerobic, non-motile and inactive against carbohydrates. They are oxidase positive.

  • Type species: M. lacunata. Causes purulent conjunctivitis (angular blepharoconjunctivitis).
  • Culture: Requires protein enriched medium (serum agar/Loeffler's). Produces liquefaction (pitting).
  • Sensitivity: Sensitive to penicillin (distinguishes from Acinetobacter).
02

üê∂ Genus Pasteurella

Pasteurella multocida

Habitat: Respiratory tract of animals (dogs, cats, rats). Human infection follows animal bites or trauma.

Characteristics: Small Gram negative coccobacillus, non-motile. Bipolar staining. Oxidase (+), Indole (+), Catalase (+).

Pathogenicity: Wound infections, septicaemia, osteomyelitis after animal bites.

Antibiotics: Sensitive to penicillin.

ü©∏ Genus Haemophilus

Small, non-motile, pleomorphic Gram negative rods. Characterised by requirement for growth factors X and V.

‚ÑπÔ∏è

Growth Factors

  • X Factor: Heat stable iron-porphyrin haematin (in blood agar).
  • V Factor: Heat labile NAD/NADP. Found inside RBCs or synthesized by S. aureus.

Haemophilus influenzae

Major pathogen (Meningitis, Epiglottitis, Pneumonia). Type b (Hib) is most virulent (capsulated).

Satellitism

On blood agar, H. influenzae grows as large colonies around Staph. aureus colonies because Staph provides the V factor (NAD) which diffuses into the medium, while blood agar provides the X factor.

Haemophilus ducreyi

Causes Chancroid (Soft sore). Gram stain shows "School of fish" appearance. Difficult to culture (requires Vancomycin + Isovitalex).

ü¶† Genus Gardnerella

Gardnerella vaginalis

Causes bacterial vaginosis (fishy odour, pH > 4.5). Gram variable coccobacilli.

Diagnosis

Clue Cells: Epithelial cells covered with small Gram negative rods seen in wet prep or Gram stain.

Culture: Columbia CNA medium or human blood agar (beta haemolysis). Sensitive to Metronidazole.

Bordetella pertussis

Causes Whooping Cough (Pertussis). Small Gram negative coccobacillus. "Mercury drop" colonies.

Laboratory Diagnosis

  • Specimen: Pernasal swab or Cough plate.
  • Culture: Bordet-Gengou medium (Potato-blood-glycerol) or Charcoal Cephalexin Blood Agar (CCBA).

üêÑ Genus Brucella

Causes Brucellosis (Undulant fever). Zoonotic infection (Goats, Cattle, Pigs). Small Gram negative coccobacilli. Strict aerobe (B. abortus needs $CO_2$).

Diagnosis

  • Culture: Blood culture (Castaneda diphasic medium). Prolonged incubation (4-6 weeks).
  • Serology: Standard Agglutination Test (SAT). Titre > 1:80 significant. Prozone phenomenon may occur. 2-mercaptoethanol test differentiates IgG/IgM.
  • Milk Ring Test: Screening of milk for antibodies. Blue ring at top = Positive.
