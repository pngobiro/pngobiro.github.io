üîç Overview

Actinomycetes are Gram positive, non-motile, non-capsulated and non-sporing filamentous structures that break up into bacillary and coccoid forms. They bear a superficial resemblance to fungi because they form a mycelium of branching filaments.

The four medically important genera are:

  • Actinomyces
  • Nocardia
  • Streptomyces
  • Actinomadura
02

ü¶† Genus Actinomyces

Characteristics: Grow as mycelium breaking up into rods of unequal lengths. Non-motile, non-acid fast, microaerophilic or anaerobic. Filaments may have club-shaped ends.

Habitat: Normally present in the mouth and female genital tract.

Pathogenicity

  • A. israelii: Causes Actinomycosis‚Äîa chronic granulomatous and suppurative disease (jaws, abdomen, thorax). In tissues, the organism grows as mycelium appearing as granules (0.3‚Äì1 mm) known as Sulphur granules.
  • A. bovis: Causes actinomycosis in cattle.
  • A. naeslundii, A. viscosus: Associated with dental caries and gingivitis.

Clinical Note

Women wearing intrauterine devices are prone to actinomyces infections such as cervicitis and endometritis.

Laboratory Diagnosis

Specimens: Pus, sputum, infected tissue.

Microscopy

Gram positive. Branches appear as fine intertwining threads.

Treatment of Sulphur Granules

  1. 1Wash granules free of pus with sterile water. (If not visible, shake pus with saline and collect settled granules).
  2. 2For microscopy: Crush between two slides.
  3. 3For culture: Crush in sterile water and inoculate.

03

üß¨ Genus Nocardia

Similar morphologically to Actinomyces but are aerobic and weakly acid fast. Produce aerial hyphae. Saprophytes in soil.

Pathogenicity

  • N. asteroides: Pulmonary infections (immunosuppressed), brain abscess.
  • N. brasiliensis: Mycetoma, pulmonary infection.

Laboratory Diagnosis

Microscopy: Gram positive branching filaments. Weakly acid fast (decolourise with 1% $H_2SO_4$).

Culture: Grow on ordinary media. Aerobic. Slow growing (3-14 days). Colonies dry, granular, wrinkled, often pigmented (yellow/red).

üíä Genus Streptomyces

Type species: Streptomyces somaliensis. Saprophyte in soil.

Pathogenicity

Causes Mycetoma (Actinomycetoma). Common in Sudan, Somalia, Central Africa, South America.

Diagnosis

  • Specimen: Pus from draining sinuses containing yellow-brown granules.
  • Microscopy: Branches Gram positive, non-acid fast. Spores weakly acid fast.
  • Culture: Grows on Sabouraud agar. Creamy, heaped colonies.
  • ID: Urease negative, casein hydrolysis positive.
05

üåæ Genus Actinomadura

Formerly included in Nocardia. Soil saprophytes.

Pathogenicity

Causes Mycetoma.

Species Differentiation

  • A. madurae: Produces whitish pink granules (soft).
  • A. pelletieri: Produces small dark red granules.

Microscopy: Gram positive. Non-acid fast.

Culture: Grow on Sabouraud agar (creamy colonies). Hydrolyse casein, urease negative.
