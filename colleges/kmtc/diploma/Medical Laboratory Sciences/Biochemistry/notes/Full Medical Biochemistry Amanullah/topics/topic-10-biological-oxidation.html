üîã Bioenergetics and Redox Potential

This chapter explains how the food we take in and the oxygen we respire produce energy to continue the process of life. The food is oxidized by enzymes, producing reducing equivalents like NADH and FADH$_2$. These donate electrons to oxygen, releasing energy to produce Adenosine Triphosphate (ATP).

Basic Terminology

  • Oxidation: Addition of oxygen, removal of hydrogen, or removal of electrons.
  • Reduction: Removal of oxygen, addition of hydrogen, or addition of electrons.
  • Redox Potential ($E_0$): The relative tendency of a reductant to donate electrons as compared to hydrogen. The redox potential of hydrogen is taken as zero ($pH=0$).
üí°

Electron Flow Rule

Electrons flow from compounds with negative redox potential (better electron donors) to those with positive redox potential (poor electron donors). This flow releases energy.

High Energy Compounds

Any bond which on hydrolysis gives a minimum free energy of 7.4 Kcal/mol is known as an energy rich bond ($\sim$).

Examples: ATP, Pyrophosphate, 1,3-Diphosphoglycerate, Phosphoenolpyruvate, Creatine phosphate, Acetyl-CoA.

Adenosine Triphosphate (ATP)

ATP is the "energy currency" of the cell. It has two high-energy pyrophosphate bonds. Hydrolysis of the terminal bonds yields >7.4 Kcal/mol.

02

‚õìÔ∏è Electron Transport Chain (ETC)

The transfer of electrons from substrate to molecular oxygen through a chain of electron carriers is called the electron transport chain or respiratory chain. It is located in the inner mitochondrial membrane.

Electrons flow from the most electronegative system (NADH or FADH$_2$, -0.32V) to the most electropositive system (Oxygen, +0.82V) in a stepwise manner.

‚ö°

Sites of ATP Formation

ATP is generated at three specific sites where sufficient energy (>7.4 Kcal) is released:

  1. Between NADH dehydrogenase and Ubiquinone (CoQ).
  2. Between Cytochrome b and Cytochrome c$_1$.
  3. Between Cytochrome a and Cytochrome a$_3$ (Cytochrome oxidase).

Sequence of Carriers

Substrate $\rightarrow$ NAD $\rightarrow$ FMN $\rightarrow$ CoQ $\rightarrow$ Cyt b $\rightarrow$ Cyt c$_1$ $\rightarrow$ Cyt c $\rightarrow$ Cyt a $\rightarrow$ Cyt a$_3$ $\rightarrow$ O$_2$

P/O Ratio: The number of inorganic phosphates esterified per atom of oxygen consumed.

  • For NADH: 3 ATPs are produced.
  • For FADH$_2$: 2 ATPs are produced (enters chain at CoQ, bypassing site 1).
03

‚öôÔ∏è Oxidative Phosphorylation

The enzymatic phosphorylation of ADP to ATP coupled with electron transport from a substrate to molecular oxygen is known as oxidative phosphorylation.

Mechanisms

  1. 1 Chemical Coupling Hypothesis: Suggests a high-energy intermediate compound is formed which transfers energy to ADP.
  2. 2 Conformational Coupling Hypothesis: Energy from ETC causes a conformational change in membrane proteins ($F_0F_1$ ATPase), which then drives ATP synthesis.
  3. 3 Chemi-osmotic Hypothesis (Mitchell): Electron transport pumps protons ($H^+$) from the matrix to the intermembrane space. The resulting proton gradient drives $H^+$ back into the matrix through the $F_0F_1$ ATPase pore, synthesizing ATP.

Formation and Detoxification of $H_2O_2$

If $O_2$ accepts only two electrons, hydrogen peroxide ($H_2O_2$) is formed. One electron reduction creates superoxide ($O_2^-$). These are toxic and damage membranes.

$$2O_2^- + 2H^+ \xrightarrow{\text{Superoxide Dismutase}} H_2O_2 + O_2$$
$$2H_2O_2 \xrightarrow{\text{Catalase}} 2H_2O + O_2$$
04

üõë Inhibitors and Uncouplers

Inhibitors of Electron Transport

These substances block the flow of electrons through the chain.

Site Inhibitor
Site I (NADH to CoQ) Rotenone, Amytal, Barbital
Site II (Cyt b to Cyt c$_1$) Antimycin A
Site III (Cyt a$_3$ to O$_2$) Cyanide ($CN^-$), Carbon Monoxide (CO), $H_2S$

Uncouplers of Oxidative Phosphorylation

Uncouplers allow electron transport to continue but prevent the formation of ATP. The energy is dissipated as heat.

Chemical Uncouplers

  • 2,4-Dinitrophenol (DNP): Transports protons across membrane, dissipating the gradient.
  • Valinomycin: Ionophore for $K^+$.
  • Gramicidin: Ionophore for $Na^+$.

Physiological Uncouplers

Thermogenin: A protein in Brown Adipose Tissue. It uncouples phosphorylation to generate heat for maintaining body temperature in newborns and hibernating animals.

Inhibitors of ATPase

Oligomycin: Inhibits $F_0F_1$ ATPase, blocking ATP synthesis.

Atractyloside: Inhibits the ATP/ADP transport across the mitochondrial membrane.

‚ò†Ô∏è Chemical Warfare

Chemical warfare involves using toxic properties of chemical substances to kill or incapacitate. Many act by interfering with biological oxidation or neurotransmission.

Agent Class Examples Mechanism Symptoms
Nerve Agents Sarin, Soman, Tabun, VX Inhibit Acetylcholinesterase. Prevents breakdown of acetylcholine. Pinpoint pupils, seizures, foaming, muscle twitching, death.
Blood Agents Hydrogen Cyanide, Cyanogen Chloride Inhibit Cytochrome Oxidase (Site III of ETC). prevent $O_2$ utilization. Gasping, cherry-red skin, metabolic acidosis, rapid death.
Blister Agents Mustard Gas, Lewisite Alkylate DNA and proteins. Vesicants. Severe burns, blisters on skin/lungs, blindness.
Choking Agents Chlorine, Phosgene Damage respiratory tract. Pulmonary edema, suffocation.

Cytotoxic Proteins

Ricin and Abrin are non-living biological proteins that inhibit protein synthesis, leading to cell death and organ failure.
