üîé Nucleic Acid Probes

Nucleic acid probes are pieces of ssDNA or RNA labeled with radioactive isotopes ($^{32}\mathrm{P}$) or fluorescent compounds. They bind (hybridize) to complementary base sequences in a sample.

  • Applications: Identifying pathogens (e.g., Plasmodium falciparum), detecting genetic diseases (Sickle cell anemia), and screening gene libraries.
02

üß¨ Gene Sequencing

Methods to determine the nucleotide sequence of DNA.

  1. 1 Chemical Cleavage (Maxam-Gilbert): Uses chemicals (hydrazine, piperidine) to cleave DNA at specific bases.
  2. 2 Enzymatic Method (Sanger Dideoxy): Uses DNA Polymerase and 2',3'-dideoxynucleotides (ddNTPs). Incorporation of a ddNTP terminates the chain because it lacks the $3'$-OH group needed for the next phosphodiester bond.
03

‚ö° Polymerase Chain Reaction (PCR)

In vitro enzymatic gene amplification ("DNA Xeroxing").

Components

  • Template DNA
  • Primers: Short DNA fragments complementary to $3'$ ends.
  • Taq Polymerase: Thermostable enzyme from Thermus aquaticus.
  • dNTPs and $Mg^{2+}$.

Steps (Thermal Cycling)

Step Temperature Action
Denaturation $94^{\circ}\mathrm{C}$ Strand separation
Annealing $55^{\circ}\mathrm{C}$ Primers attach to template
Extension $72^{\circ}\mathrm{C}$ Taq polymerase adds nucleotides

Clinical Utility: Detection of HIV, Tuberculosis (RT-PCR), genetic defects, and forensics.

04

‚úÇÔ∏è Recombinant DNA Technology

Also known as Genetic Engineering. Involves joining DNA from two different species.

Key Tools

  • Restriction Endonucleases: Molecular scissors. Cut DNA at specific palindromic sequences (e.g., EcoRI, BamHI). Can produce "Sticky ends" or "Blunt ends".
  • Vectors: Vehicles to carry foreign DNA.
    • Plasmids: Small circular DNA (e.g., pBR322) with antibiotic resistance genes.
    • Bacteriophages: e.g., Lambda phage.
    • Cosmids: Hybrid of plasmid and phage cos site (for large DNA).
  • Ligase: Joins DNA fragments.

Applications

Production of Human Insulin (Humulin), Growth Hormone, Interferons, and recombinant vaccines (Hepatitis B).

06

üêÅ Hybridoma & Vaccines

Hybridoma Technique

Production of Monoclonal Antibodies (MAb). Fusion of a specific B-lymphocyte (antibody producing) with a Myeloma cell (cancerous/immortal). Fused cells are selected using HAT Medium (Hypoxanthine-Aminopterin-Thymidine).

Uses: Diagnostic kits, Cancer therapy, Immunopurification.

Subunit Vaccines

Use specific antigens (proteins/polysaccharides) rather than whole organisms. Safer. Example: Hepatitis B Vaccine (HBsAg produced in Yeast).

07

üç∫ Fermentation Technology

Use of organisms to produce food, drugs, and chemicals in a Bioreactor.

  • Primary Metabolites: Essential for growth (Ethanol, Citric acid, Amino acids).
  • Secondary Metabolites: Not essential for growth (Antibiotics, Alkaloids).

Downstream Processing: Recovery and purification of the product (Filtration, Centrifugation, Drying).

02

üß™ Sperm Processing Techniques

Separation of X and Y Spermatozoa

Used for sex selection. Based on physical differences:

  • X-Sperm: More DNA, larger, heavier, slower, negative charge.
  • Y-Sperm: Less DNA, lighter, faster, fluorescent (with quinacrine), H-Y antigen positive.

Methods: Albumin column separation (Y-sperms swim faster), Sephadex filtration, Density gradient centrifugation.

Sperm Washing

Removal of seminal plasma, debris, and dead sperm. Essential for intrauterine insemination (IUI) and IVF.

  • Swim-up Technique: Live, active sperm swim up into a culture medium layer, leaving debris behind. Superior to simple centrifugation.

Capacitation

Physiological change sperms undergo in the female tract to become capable of fertilization. In vitro, induced by centrifugation or high ionic strength media.

03

‚ùÑÔ∏è Cryopreservation

Preservation of semen in liquid nitrogen ($-196^{\circ}\mathrm{C}$) using Semen Extenders.

Components of Extender:

  • Nutrients: Glucose/Fructose (Energy).
  • Cryoprotectant: Glycerol (Protects against cold shock/freezing damage).
  • Buffer: Citrate/Phosphate (Maintains pH).
  • Antibiotics: Penicillin/Streptomycin.
  • Cold Shock Protectant: Egg yolk.
04

üß´ In Vitro Fertilization (IVF)

Fertilization of an oocyte by sperm outside the body (test tube baby). Used for blocked fallopian tubes, immunologic infertility, etc.

Steps

  1. 1 Ovarian Stimulation: Using hormones (FSH, hMG) to produce multiple eggs.
  2. 2 Egg Retrieval: Transvaginal ultrasound-guided aspiration.
  3. 3 Fertilization: Capacitated sperm mixed with eggs in culture dish.
  4. 4 Embryo Culture: Growth to cleavage stage (2-8 cells).
  5. 5 Embryo Transfer: Transfer of pre-embryos into the uterus.
‚≠ê

Key Points

  • ‚úì

    Glycerol is the key cryoprotective agent for freezing semen.

  • ‚úì

    Sperm washing removes seminal plasma inhibitors and debris, essential for ART.

  • ‚úì

    X-spermatozoa are heavier and contain more DNA than Y-spermatozoa.
