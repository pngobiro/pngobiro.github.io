üß¨ Introduction and Classification

Vitamins are organic substances required in minute quantities in the diet to maintain the normal physiological functions of the body.

Classification

Vitamins are classified into two main groups based on solubility:

  1. 1 Water Soluble Vitamins: B-complex group and Vitamin C.
  2. 2 Fat Soluble Vitamins: Vitamins A, D, E, and K.
02

üíß Water Soluble Vitamins (B-Complex)

9.1 Thiamine (Vitamin $\mathbf{B}_1$)

Also known as Anti-Beri Beri vitamin and Anti-Neuritic vitamin.

Chemistry and Coenzyme

Thiamine contains a pyrimidine ring and a thiazole ring linked by a methylene bridge. The active coenzyme form is Thiamine Pyrophosphate (TPP), formed by esterification with two phosphoric acid molecules.

Functions

TPP acts as a coenzyme for:

  • Oxidative decarboxylation: E.g., Pyruvate to Acetyl-CoA.
  • Transketolation: In HMP pathway.
  • Essential for carbohydrate utilization and normal brain metabolism.
‚ö†Ô∏è

Deficiency Disease: Beri-Beri

Characterized by polyneuritis, edema, cardiovascular changes, and weakness.

  • Dry Beri-Beri: Nervous symptoms predominate.
  • Wet Beri-Beri: Edema and serous effusions predominate.
  • Acute Pernicious Beri-Beri: Heart involvement.
  • Infantile Beri-Beri: Seen in breast-fed children of deficient mothers.
  • Wernicke's Encephalopathy: Seen in alcoholics.

Daily Requirement: 1.5 - 2.0 mg/day (Adults). Dependent on carbohydrate intake.

9.2 Riboflavin (Vitamin $\mathbf{B}_2$)

Chemistry and Coenzyme

Contains substituted isoalloxazine ring attached to ribityl alcohol. Active forms are FMN (Flavin Mononucleotide) and FAD (Flavin Adenine Dinucleotide).

Functions

FMN and FAD act as coenzymes for flavoproteins involved in oxidation-reduction reactions (e.g., Succinate dehydrogenase, Xanthine oxidase). Involved in ETC.

Deficiency

Cheilosis (fissures at angles of mouth), glossitis (magenta tongue), dermatitis, and vascularization of cornea.

Daily Requirement: 1.5 - 2.0 mg/day.

9.3 Niacin (Vitamin $\mathbf{B}_3$)

Chemistry and Coenzyme

Chemical name: Nicotinic acid (pyridine derivative). Amide form: Nicotinamide. Active coenzymes: NAD$^+$ and NADP$^+$.

Source note: Can be synthesized in the body from Tryptophan (60 mg Tryptophan $\rightarrow$ 1 mg Niacin).

Functions

NAD$^+$ and NADP$^+$ are coenzymes for oxidoreductases (dehydrogenases). NAD$^+$ is generally involved in catabolic oxidative reactions (e.g., Glycolysis, TCA cycle), while NADPH is involved in anabolic reductive synthesis (e.g., Fatty acid synthesis).

ü§í

Deficiency Disease: Pellagra

Characterized by the 3 Ds:

  1. Dermatitis
  2. Diarrhoea
  3. Dementia

If untreated, leads to the 4th D: Death.

Daily Requirement: 15 - 20 mg/day.

9.4 Pyridoxine (Vitamin $\mathbf{B}_6$)

Chemistry and Coenzyme

Exists as Pyridoxine, Pyridoxal, and Pyridoxamine. Active coenzyme form is Pyridoxal Phosphate (PLP).

Functions

PLP acts as a coenzyme for:

  • Transamination: Transfer of amino groups.
  • Decarboxylation: Formation of biogenic amines (e.g., Histamine, GABA, Dopamine).
  • Heme Synthesis: ALA synthase reaction.
  • Niacin Synthesis: Kynureninase reaction.

Deficiency & Antagonists

Deficiency is rare but can cause convulsions, depression, and peripheral neuropathy. INH (Isoniazid), a TB drug, is an antagonist to Vitamin B6.

Daily Requirement: 1.6 - 2.0 mg/day.

9.5 Pantothenic Acid (Vitamin $\mathbf{B}_5$)

Chemistry and Coenzyme

Beta-alanine + Pantoic acid. Active form is Coenzyme A (CoA).

Functions

Carrier of acyl groups (e.g., Acetyl-CoA, Succinyl-CoA). Essential for metabolism of carbohydrates, fats, and proteins.

Deficiency

Rare. "Burning feet syndrome" (gopalan).

9.6 Biotin (Vitamin $\mathbf{B}_7$)

Sulfur-containing vitamin. Active form is Biocytin (bound to lysine).

Functions

Coenzyme for Carboxylation reactions (CO$_2$ fixation). Examples: Pyruvate carboxylase (Gluconeogenesis), Acetyl-CoA carboxylase (Fatty acid synthesis).

Deficiency

Rare. Can be induced by eating raw egg white which contains Avidin (antagonist).

Daily Requirement: 100 - 300 $\mu$g/day.

ü©∏ Folic Acid & Vitamin B12

9.7 Folic Acid (Vitamin $\mathbf{B}_9$)

Chemistry

Composed of Pteridine, PABA (Para amino benzoic acid), and Glutamic acid. Active form is Tetrahydrofolate (THF).

Functions

Transfer of One Carbon Moieties (methyl, methylene, formyl, etc.). Essential for DNA synthesis (purines and thymidylate) and maturation of RBCs.

ü©∏

Deficiency: Megaloblastic Anemia

Characterized by large immature RBCs (megaloblasts) due to impaired DNA synthesis. Sulfonamides act as antibiotics by inhibiting folate synthesis in bacteria (competitive inhibition with PABA).

Daily Requirement: 300 - 400 $\mu$g/day.

9.8 Vitamin $\mathbf{B}_{12}$ (Cobalamine)

Chemistry

Contains a Corrin ring with a central Cobalt atom. Known as the "Castle's Extrinsic Factor".

Absorption

Requires Intrinsic Factor secreted by gastric parietal cells for absorption in the ileum.

Functions

Active coenzyme is Cobamide coenzyme (5'-Deoxyadenosyl cobalamine). Functions include:

  • 1,2-hydrogen shift (Methylmalonyl-CoA $\rightarrow$ Succinyl-CoA).
  • Methyl transfer (Homocysteine $\rightarrow$ Methionine), recycling Folate (Folate Trap).
ü©∏

Deficiency: Pernicious Anemia

A type of megaloblastic anemia caused by lack of Intrinsic Factor. Also causes neurological lesions (demyelination).

Daily Requirement: 5 $\mu$g/day.

üß¨ Other Important Factors

9.9 Lipoic Acid

Sulfur-containing fatty acid. Essential cofactor for Pyruvate Dehydrogenase and $\alpha$-ketoglutarate dehydrogenase complexes (oxidative decarboxylation). Also acts as an antioxidant and chelating agent.

9.10 Vitamin C (Ascorbic Acid)

Strong reducing agent. Structure resembles hexose sugar.

Functions

  • Collagen Synthesis: Hydroxylation of proline and lysine.
  • Iron Absorption: Reduces $Fe^{3+}$ to $Fe^{2+}$.
  • Antioxidant.
üçã

Deficiency Disease: Scurvy

Symptoms include swollen/bleeding gums, loose teeth, fragile capillaries, delayed wound healing, and weak bones.

Daily Requirement: 70 mg/day.

üßà Fat Soluble Vitamins

9.11 Vitamin A

Exists as Retinol (alcohol), Retinal (aldehyde), Retinoic acid (acid). Derived from carotenes ($\beta$-carotene) in plants.

Functions

  • Vision: Component of Rhodopsin (Visual purple) in rod cells (dim light vision). Wald's Visual Cycle involves interconversion of cis/trans retinal.
  • Growth and Differentiation: Essential for epithelial tissue integrity and bone growth.
  • Reproduction.
üëÅÔ∏è

Deficiency

  • Nyctalopia: Night blindness.
  • Xerophthalmia: Dryness of conjunctiva/cornea.
  • Keratomalacia: Softening/ulceration of cornea (Blindness).

9.12 Vitamin D (Calciferol)

Considered a hormone. Derived from cholesterol. Active form is 1,25-Dihydroxycholecalciferol (Calcitriol).

Functions (Calcium Homeostasis)

  • Increases Ca$^{2+}$ absorption from intestine.
  • Promotes Ca$^{2+}$ reabsorption in kidneys.
  • Mobilizes bone calcium.
ü¶¥

Deficiency

  • Rickets: In children (bowed legs, bone deformities).
  • Osteomalacia: In adults (soft bones).

9.13 Vitamin E (Tocopherol)

Chemically $\alpha$-tocopherol. Known as Anti-sterility factor.

Functions

Potent Antioxidant. Protects PUFA in cell membranes from peroxidation. Prevents hemolysis of RBCs.

Deficiency

Sterility (in rats), hemolysis, muscular dystrophy.

9.14 Vitamin K

Anti-hemorrhagic vitamin. Forms: K1 (Phylloquinone), K2 (Menaquinone - intestinal bacteria), K3 (Menadione - synthetic).

Functions

Essential for formation of clotting factors II (Prothrombin), VII, IX, and X. Coenzyme for $\gamma$-carboxylation of glutamic acid residues.

Deficiency

Delayed blood clotting, hemorrhage.
