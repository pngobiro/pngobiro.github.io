üß± Epithelial Tissue

Epithelium is a tissue composed of layers of cells that line the cavities and surfaces of structures throughout the body. It is also the type of tissue from which many glands are formed.

Classification

Epithelial cells are classified by three factors:

  1. 1 Shape:
    • Squamous: Flat cells (e.g., alveoli, capillaries). Diffusion.
    • Cuboidal: Cube-shaped (e.g., kidney tubules, glands). Secretion/Absorption.
    • Columnar: Taller than wide (e.g., intestine). Absorption.
    • Transitional: Can stretch (e.g., bladder).
  2. 2 Stratification: Simple (single layer) or Stratified (multiple layers).
  3. 3 Specializations: Keratinized (skin), Ciliated (respiratory tract).

ü¶¥ Connective Tissue and Bone

Connective tissue holds everything together. It is characterized by the separation of the cells by non-living material, called extracellular matrix (ECM).

Biochemistry of Bone

Bone is a mineralized connective tissue.

  • Inorganic (65%): Mainly crystalline Hydroxyapatite, $\mathrm{Ca_{10}(PO_4)_6(OH)_2}$.
  • Organic (35%): Mainly proteins (Type I Collagen).

Bone Cells

  • Osteoblasts: Synthesize bone matrix (osteoid) and control mineralization. Contain alkaline phosphatase.
  • Osteocytes: Mature bone cells trapped in matrix. Maintain bone tissue.
  • Osteoclasts: Multinucleated cells responsible for bone resorption (dissolution) via acid and lysosomal enzymes.
ü©∫

Bone Disorders

  • Osteoporosis: Reduction in bone density causing weakness and fractures.
  • Osteopetrosis: Marble bone disease. Increased bone density due to defective resorption (Carbonic anhydrase II deficiency).
  • Rickets/Osteomalacia: Defective mineralization due to Vitamin D deficiency.
  • Osteogenesis Imperfecta: Brittle bone disease due to defective Collagen Type I synthesis.

üï∏Ô∏è Extracellular Matrix (ECM) Proteins

1. Collagen

Major component of ECM (25% of total protein). Triple helical structure (three $\alpha$ chains). Repeating sequence Gly-X-Y (X often Proline, Y often Hydroxyproline). Requires Vitamin C for hydroxylation.

Clinical: Scurvy (Vit C deficiency), Ehlers-Danlos syndrome (Collagen deficiency).

2. Elastin

Rubber-like protein in lungs, arteries, ligaments. Rich in glycine, alanine, valine. Cross-links via Desmosine.

Clinical: Emphysema is caused by destruction of elastin in lung alveoli due to $\alpha_1$-antitrypsin deficiency.

3. Proteoglycans & GAGs

Proteins linked to Glycosaminoglycans (GAGs). Form hydrated gels.

  • Hyaluronic acid: Synovial fluid, cartilage.
  • Heparin: Anticoagulant.
  • Chondroitin sulfate: Cartilage, bone.

Mucopolysaccharidoses: Hereditary disorders accumulating GAGs (e.g., Hurler syndrome).

üí™ Muscle Tissue

Contractile tissue derived from mesoderm. Contains contractile filaments (Actin and Myosin).

Types of Muscle

  1. 1 Skeletal Muscle: Striated, Voluntary. Multinucleated. Used for locomotion.
  2. 2 Cardiac Muscle: Striated, Involuntary. Branched fibers with intercalated discs. Found in heart.
  3. 3 Smooth Muscle: Non-striated, Involuntary. Found in walls of organs (stomach, blood vessels).

Muscle Fiber Types

  • Type I (Slow Twitch): Red muscle. High myoglobin/mitochondria. Aerobic. Sustained contraction.
  • Type II (Fast Twitch): White muscle. Low myoglobin. Anaerobic/Glycolytic. Rapid fatigue.

üß† Nervous Tissue

Specialized for communication. Composed of:

  • Neurons: Conduct impulses. Consist of Cell body (Soma), Dendrites (receive), and Axon (transmit).
  • Neuroglia (Glial cells): Support cells.
    • Astrocytes: Metabolic support, Blood-Brain Barrier.
    • Oligodendrocytes: Myelin sheath in CNS.
    • Schwann cells: Myelin sheath in PNS.
    • Microglia: Immune defense (phagocytosis).

üèõÔ∏è Organ Biochemistry

1. Liver

The metabolic hub of the body.

  • Carbohydrate Metabolism: Glycogenesis, Glycogenolysis, Gluconeogenesis.
  • Protein Metabolism: Synthesis of plasma proteins (Albumin, Clotting factors), Urea cycle (Ammonia detoxification).
  • Lipid Metabolism: Cholesterol synthesis, Lipoprotein synthesis (VLDL), Bile production.
  • Detoxification: Drug metabolism (Cytochrome P450).

2. Kidney

Maintains homeostasis.

  • Excretion: Urea, Uric acid, Creatinine, Toxins.
  • Regulation: Water balance (ADH), Electrolyte balance (Aldosterone), Acid-base balance.
  • Endocrine Function: Erythropoietin (RBC production), Renin (BP regulation), Calcitriol (Active Vit D).

3. Brain

High energy demand (20% of total $O_2$ consumption). Strictly aerobic.

  • Fuel: Primarily Glucose. During starvation, adapts to use Ketone Bodies. Fatty acids cannot cross Blood-Brain Barrier (BBB).
  • Neurotransmitters: Synthesized from amino acids (e.g., Glutamate, GABA, Dopamine, Serotonin).
