üî¨ Semen and Analysis

Reproduction is the sexual union of sperm and ova to produce offspring. Biotechnology has developed methods to improve reproductive success in infertile couples.

Semen

Semen is a semi-gelatinous suspension containing spermatozoa (male gametes) and seminal plasma (secretions from testis, epididymis, and accessory glands like the prostate). Sperms are haploid (23 chromosomes), carrying either an X or Y chromosome, determining the sex of the offspring.

Composition of Human Semen

Component Average Value / Note
Volume 2.30 - 4.99 ml (Avg: 3.65 ml)
pH 7.19 - 8.47
Sperm Count > 40 million per ejaculate
Motility > 50% with forward progression
Fructose Energy source (136 - 628 mg/dl)
Acid Phosphatase Prostate function marker
Citric Acid 304 - 751 mg/dl

Collection: Mechanical massage or Electro-ejaculation. Donors should abstain from sex for 3 days prior. Good quality semen is viscous and cream-white.

üß™ Sperm Processing Techniques

Separation of X and Y Spermatozoa

Used for sex selection. Separation is based on physical differences between X-bearing and Y-bearing sperm.

Property X-Spermatozoa Y-Spermatozoa
DNA Content More DNA (Larger chromosome) Less DNA
Weight/Density Heavier, Slower motility Lighter, Faster motility
Fluorescence Non-fluorescent Fluorescent (with quinacrine)
Surface Charge Positively charged (moves to cathode) No surface charge
H-Y Antigen Absent Present

Separation Techniques:

  • Albumin Columns: Y-sperms swim faster through albumin. Yields 75-80% Y-sperms.
  • Sephadex Filtration: X-sperms (larger) pass faster. Yields 70-75% X-sperms.
  • Velocity Sedimentation: Y-sperms float on top.

Sperm Washing

Removal of seminal plasma, dead sperm, and debris is essential for artificial insemination (IUI) and IVF because seminal fluid contains inhibitors.

Swim-up Technique

Superior to simple centrifugation. Semen is placed under a layer of culture medium. Healthy, motile sperm swim up into the medium, leaving debris and dead sperm behind. The supernatant containing active sperm is collected.

Capacitation

Physiological changes sperm undergo in the female tract to become capable of fertilization. In vitro, this is induced by high speed centrifugation or high ionic strength media.

‚ùÑÔ∏è Cryopreservation of Semen

Preservation of semen in a state of suspended animation at deep freezing temperatures ($-196^{\circ}\mathrm{C}$) using liquid nitrogen. Requires Semen Extenders.

üõ°Ô∏è

Components of Semen Extender

  • Nutrients: Glucose/Fructose (Energy source).
  • Cryoprotectant: Glycerol (3-10%). Essential to protect cells from freezing damage/ice crystals. Added at $5^{\circ}\mathrm{C}$.
  • Cold Shock Protectant: Egg yolk or Milk.
  • Buffers: TRIS, Citrate, Phosphate (Maintain pH).
  • Antibiotics: Penicillin, Streptomycin.

Process:

  1. 1 Dilution with extender at $30^{\circ}\mathrm{C}$.
  2. 2 Cooling to $5^{\circ}\mathrm{C}$ over 1 hour.
  3. 3 Equilibration with glycerol (4-6 hours).
  4. 4 Packing into straws.
  5. 5 Freezing in nitrogen vapour (rapid cooling).
  6. 6 Storage in Liquid Nitrogen ($-196^{\circ}\mathrm{C}$).

Thawing: Rapid thawing in ice water/warm water to $37^{\circ}\mathrm{C}$ is required to prevent cell death.

üß´ In Vitro Fertilization (IVF)

Fertilization of an oocyte by a sperm in a test tube (glass) outside the human body. Used for blocked fallopian tubes, immunologic infertility, or unexplained infertility.

Procedure Steps

  1. 1 Ovarian Stimulation: Hormonal therapy (FSH, hMG, hCG) to induce development of multiple follicles.
  2. 2 Oocyte Retrieval: Transvaginal ultrasound-guided aspiration of eggs from ovary.
  3. 3 Sperm Preparation: Semen collection and capacitation (washing/centrifugation).
  4. 4 Fertilization: Sperm and eggs mixed in culture dishes. Sperm penetrate Zona Pellucida.
  5. 5 Embryo Culture: Zygotes grow to cleavage stage (2-8 cells) or blastocyst stage.
  6. 6 Embryo Transfer: Pre-embryos are transferred into the uterus via a catheter.
