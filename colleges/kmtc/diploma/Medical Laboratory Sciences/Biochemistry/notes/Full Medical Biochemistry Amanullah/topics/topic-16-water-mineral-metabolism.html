üåä Water Metabolism

Water makes up about 50-70% of adult body weight. It is distributed as:

  • Intracellular Fluid (ICF): 50% of body weight. Major cation: $K^+$.
  • Extracellular Fluid (ECF): 20% of body weight (Plasma 5%, Interstitial 15%). Major cation: $Na^+$.

Dehydration

Loss of water exceeding intake. Can be primary (water depletion) or secondary (electrolyte depletion).

  • Causes: Vomiting, diarrhea, excessive sweating, diabetes insipidus.
  • Effects: Hemoconcentration, rise in body temperature, circulatory failure.
  • Water Intoxication: Excessive retention causing headache, nausea, muscular weakness.

üíé Mineral Metabolism Overview

Minerals are inorganic elements essential for vital processes.

Classification

  1. 1 Macro Elements: Required > 100 mg/day. Includes Ca, P, Mg, Na, K, Cl, S.
  2. 2 Micro (Trace) Elements: Required in trace amounts. Includes Fe, Cu, Zn, Co, Mn, I, F.
03

ü¶¥ Calcium (Ca)

Most abundant mineral. 99% in bones/teeth as hydroxyapatite crystals.

Normal Serum Calcium: 9.5 - 10.5 mg/dl.

Absorption Factors

  • Promoters: Vitamin D, Acidic pH, PTH, Lysine/Arginine.
  • Inhibitors: Phytates, Oxalates, High Phosphate, Alkaline pH.

Functions

  • Bone/Teeth formation.
  • Blood Coagulation (Factor IV).
  • Muscle Contraction.
  • Nerve transmission.
  • Second messenger.

Regulation of Blood Calcium

  1. Parathormone (PTH): Increases blood Ca (Bone resorption, Renal reabsorption).
  2. Calcitonin: Decreases blood Ca (Bone deposition).
  3. Vitamin D (Calcitriol): Increases Ca absorption from intestine.
ü©∫

Clinical Disorders

  • Tetany: Low serum calcium (< 7 mg/dl). Increased neuromuscular excitability.
  • Rickets/Osteomalacia: Defective mineralization due to Vit D/Calcium deficiency.
  • Osteoporosis: Decalcification of bone (common in post-menopausal women).
  • Hypercalcemia: > 11 mg/dl. Seen in hyperparathyroidism. Causes renal stones.
04

‚ö° Phosphorus & Magnesium

Phosphorus (P)

Serum level: 3.0 - 4.5 mg/dl.

  • Functions: Bone formation, High energy compounds (ATP), Phospholipids, Nucleic acids (DNA/RNA), Buffering ($\mathrm{HPO}_4^{2-}/\mathrm{H}_2\mathrm{PO}_4^-$).
  • Relation to Ca: Inverse relationship (Ca $\times$ P product is constant).

Magnesium (Mg)

Serum level: 2 - 4 mg/dl.

  • Functions: Cofactor for kinases (ATP dependent enzymes), constituent of bone/teeth, neuromuscular irritability (depressant).
  • Deficiency: Tremors, convulsions (similar to tetany).

Regulation

Aldosterone (Mineralocorticoid): Increases renal reabsorption of $Na^+$ (and water) and excretion of $K^+$.

ü©∫

Clinical Conditions

  • Hyponatremia: Addison's disease, diarrhea, renal failure.
  • Hypernatremia: Cushing's syndrome, dehydration.
  • Hypokalemia: Cardiac arrhythmia, muscle weakness.
  • Hyperkalemia: Renal failure, Addison's disease (Cardiac arrest risk).
06

üî© Iron Metabolism

Total body iron: 4-5 g. Essential for Oxygen transport (Hb, Myoglobin) and Cellular respiration (Cytochromes).

Absorption

Occurs in duodenum. Absorbed in Ferrous ($Fe^{2+}$) state.

  • Promoters: Acidic pH (HCl), Vitamin C (reduces $Fe^{3+}$ to $Fe^{2+}$).
  • Inhibitors: Phytates, Phosphates, Oxalates (form insoluble complexes).

Transport & Storage

  • Transport: In plasma as $Fe^{3+}$ bound to Transferrin.
  • Storage: Stored as Ferritin (soluble) and Hemosiderin (insoluble) in liver, spleen, bone marrow.
ü©∏

Disorders

Iron Deficiency Anemia: Hypochromic microcytic anemia. Common in nutritional deficiency, blood loss.

Hemosiderosis/Hemochromatosis: Iron overload. Deposition of iron in liver (cirrhosis), pancreas (bronze diabetes), and skin.

07

üî¨ Trace Elements

Copper (Cu)

  • Functions: Cofactor for enzymes (Cytochrome oxidase, Tyrosinase, Lysyl oxidase). Essential for Hb synthesis and collagen formation.
  • Plasma transport: Bound to Ceruloplasmin (Ferroxidase activity).
  • Wilson's Disease: Hepatolenticular degeneration. Defect in copper excretion/ceruloplasmin synthesis. Copper accumulates in liver and brain.

Iodine (I)

  • Functions: Synthesis of Thyroid hormones ($T_3, T_4$).
  • Deficiency: Goiter (Enlargement of thyroid gland), Cretinism (in children).

Selenium (Se)

  • Functions: Component of Glutathione Peroxidase (Antioxidant). Synergistic with Vitamin E.

Sulfur (S)

  • Present in amino acids (Cysteine, Methionine) and vitamins (Thiamine, Biotin).
  • Used for detoxification (Ethereal sulfates) and synthesis of connective tissue (Chondroitin sulfate).
