üíâ Introduction to Blood

Blood is the major fluid present in the body that flows through blood vessels.

  • Total Volume: About 5 litres in an adult male.
  • pH: 7.35 to 7.45.
  • Specific Gravity: 1.055 to 1.065.

Composition of Blood:

  1. 1 Blood Cells: Erythrocytes (RBC), Leukocytes (WBC), Platelets.
  2. 2 Blood Plasma: Fluid remaining after removal of cells. (Plasma - Fibrinogen = Serum). Contains 90% water, proteins, organic and inorganic constituents.
02

üß™ Plasma Proteins

Total protein concentration: 6.5 - 7.5 g/dl. Separated by electrophoresis into Albumin, $\alpha_1, \alpha_2, \beta$, and $\gamma$ globulins.

Albumin

Highest concentration (3.5-5.5 g/dl). Synthesized in liver. Soluble in water.

  • Functions:
    • Maintains Colloidal Osmotic Pressure (Oncotic Pressure). Prevents edema.
    • Transport carrier for fatty acids, bilirubin, calcium, steroid hormones, drugs.
    • Acts as a buffer.
  • Clinical: Decreased in liver cirrhosis, nephrotic syndrome, malnutrition (Kwashiorkor). Albumin/Globulin (A/G) ratio (Normal 1.2:1 to 1.8:1) is reversed in liver disease.

Bence Jones Proteins

Abnormal light chains of immunoglobulins excreted in urine in Multiple Myeloma. They coagulate at 60¬∞C and dissolve on further heating.

03

üî¥ Hemoglobin

Conjugated chromoprotein found in RBCs. Function: Transport $O_2$ and $CO_2$.

Structure: Heme (Prosthetic group) + Globin (Protein).

  • Heme: Iron-porphyrin complex. Iron is in Ferrous ($Fe^{2+}$) state. Linked to histidine residues of globin.
  • Globin: Tetramer of 4 polypeptide chains.
    • HbA (Adult): $2\alpha, 2\beta$ ($\alpha_2\beta_2$).
    • HbA2 (Minor): $2\alpha, 2\delta$ ($\alpha_2\delta_2$).
    • HbF (Fetal): $2\alpha, 2\gamma$ ($\alpha_2\gamma_2$). Higher affinity for $O_2$.

Biosynthesis of Heme

Synthesized from Glycine and Succinyl-CoA. Key regulatory enzyme: ALA Synthase (requires PLP). Inhibited by Heme (feedback).

üö®

Porphyrias

Disorders of heme synthesis leading to accumulation of porphyrins. Characterized by photosensitivity, abdominal pain, and neurological symptoms. (e.g., Acute Intermittent Porphyria).

04

üß¨ Derivatives and Abnormal Hemoglobins

Hemoglobin Derivatives

  • Oxyhemoglobin ($HbO_2$): $O_2$ bound to $Fe^{2+}$. Relaxed (R) state.
  • Carbaminohemoglobin: $CO_2$ bound to amino terminal of globin chains.
  • Methemoglobin (MetHb): Iron oxidized to Ferric ($Fe^{3+}$) state. Cannot bind $O_2$. Caused by nitrates, sulfonamides. Treated with Methylene Blue.
  • Carboxyhemoglobin (HbCO): CO bound to Heme iron. CO has 200x higher affinity than $O_2$. Causes cherry-red skin, hypoxia.

Abnormal Hemoglobins (Hemoglobinopathies)

1. Sickle Cell Anemia (HbS)

Genetic defect in $\beta$-chain. Glutamic acid at 6th position is replaced by Valine (Glu6Val). Deoxy-HbS polymerizes forming fibers that distort RBC into sickle shape. Leads to hemolysis and vascular occlusion.

2. Thalassemias

Decreased rate of synthesis of $\alpha$ or $\beta$ chains.

  • $\alpha$-Thalassemia: Defect in $\alpha$-chain synthesis.
  • $\beta$-Thalassemia: Defect in $\beta$-chain synthesis. Excess $\alpha$-chains precipitate.
05

üõ°Ô∏è Immunoglobulins (Antibodies)

Produced by B-lymphocytes (Plasma cells). Glycoproteins consisting of 2 Heavy (H) chains and 2 Light (L) chains linked by disulfide bonds ($H_2L_2$).

Regions: Variable (V) region (Antigen binding) and Constant (C) region.

Class Heavy Chain Key Features
IgG Gamma ($\gamma$) Most abundant (80%). Crosses placenta. Secondary immune response.
IgA Alpha ($\alpha$) Secretory antibody (Saliva, Tears, Colostrum). Protects mucosa. Exists as Dimer.
IgM Mu ($\mu$) Macroglobulin. Pentamer. Primary immune response (first to appear).
IgE Epsilon ($\epsilon$) Allergic reactions (Type I hypersensitivity). Binds to Mast cells/Basophils.
IgD Delta ($\delta$) B-cell receptor.
06

üß™ Other Body Fluids

1. Cerebrospinal Fluid (CSF)

Clear fluid circulating in ventricles and subarachnoid space. Produced by choroid plexus.

  • Functions: Mechanical cushion/buffer, nutrient distribution.
  • Composition: Protein (15-40 mg/dl), Glucose (50-85 mg/dl), Chloride (higher than plasma).
ü¶†

Diagnostic Significance

Meningitis:
- Bacterial: Low Glucose, High Protein, High Neutrophils.
- Viral: Normal Glucose, Normal/High Protein, Lymphocytes.

Multiple Sclerosis: Oligoclonal IgG bands on electrophoresis.

2. Amniotic Fluid

Fluid surrounding the fetus. Used for antenatal diagnosis (Amniocentesis). Contains fetal cells. Elevated Alpha-Fetoprotein (AFP) indicates Neural Tube Defects.

3. Interstitial Fluid

Fluid bathing cells. Similar to plasma but with very low protein content (ultrafiltrate of plasma).
