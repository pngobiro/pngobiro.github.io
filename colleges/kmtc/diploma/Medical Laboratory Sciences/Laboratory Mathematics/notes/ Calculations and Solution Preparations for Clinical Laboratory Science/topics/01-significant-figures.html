Significant Figures

First, what are significant figures?

Significant figures, we also call them as significant digits, they are very important part of scientific and mathematical calculations because they deal with accuracy and precision of numbers.

üí° Uncertainty

So like for example, you are uncertain of your final answer. So you have to take note of the number of the significant figures in order to determine what significant figures you are going to place in your final answer. So that is why significant figures become very important in this case.

When making calculations, the final answer is rounded off to reflect the value with the least certainty (the fewest significant figures).

03

Rules in Identifying Significant Figures

  • All non zero digits are significant. That means all numbers except zero, they are all significant.
  • Leading zeros are not significant. So when we say leading zeros, they are all zeros before the non zero digit.
  • Trailing zeros are significant (decimal point). When we say trailing zeros, these are zeros after a non zero digit. But you have to take note of this one. Trailing zeros are significant only if the digits have a decimal point. So if no decimal point, then that trailing zero is not a significant digit.
  • Captive zeros are significant. When we say captive zeros, those are zeros that are in between of the non zero digits.

Mathematical Operations

Let's now apply the rules of significant figures to mathematical operations.

Multiplication and Division

For multiplication and division, the answer must have the same number of significant figures as the starting value with the least number of significant figures.

‚ú® Rule

So let's say you are multiplying or dividing numbers with different amount of significant figures. So the amount of significant figures on your answer must be based on the starting value with the lowest amount of significant figures.

Example 
2.5 √ó 0.5250 = 1.3125

So you have to remember the rules of significant figures when we are multiplying or dividing numbers. So based on our rule, we need to take note of the number of significant figures.

So your answer must be rounded off to a value with the same number of significant figures to the starting value with the lowest or fewest number of significant figures.

So the starting value with the fewest significant figures in this example is 2.5 because it has two significant figures. Whereas your 0.5250 has four significant figures. So our answer will be 1.3.

Addition and Subtraction

For addition and subtraction, the answer must have the same number of decimal places as the starting value with the fewest number of decimal places.

So this time, when you add and when you subtract, you have to take note of the decimal places.

Example 
15.5 mL + 25.92 mL = 41.42 mL

So what does a rule in addition and subtraction say? So in addition and in subtraction, what we are looking for are the number of decimal places.

So our rule states that decimal places should be the same with the decimal place of the starting value with the lowest or fewest decimal place.

So in this case, the 15.5 has the fewest decimal place. As compared to your 25.92. So our final answer must be 41.4 mL. Because again your final answer should follow the decimal place of a starting value with the fewest number of decimal place.

Let's have now our exponential notation. So sometimes, we are dealing with very high and very low numbers in which if we have to write them as a whole figure, it will be very lengthy. That is why we have exponential notation for these instances.

Examples in Mathematical Operations

  • In multiplication, the exponents are added.

    $$(3 \times 10^4)(5 \times 10^5) = 15 \times 10^9$$

    Because the main numbers, they are multiplied. So 3 times 5 would give us 15. And then we have to add the exponents. So that would give us 4 plus 5 is 9. So that will be our answer.

  • In division, the exponents are subtracted.

    $$\frac{(4 \times 10^3)}{(2 \times 10^5)} = 2 \times 10^{-2}$$

    So we have first to divide the main number, so 4 divided by 2 would give us 2. And then of course we have to subtract the exponent. So 3 minus 5 would give us -2.
