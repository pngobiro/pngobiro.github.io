<li>Communication is the act of exchanging information. It is the process for creating understanding where two or more parties are involved, social interaction through messages. It can be used to inform, command, instruct, assess, influence and persuade other people. Good managers develop effective communication skills to absorb information, motivate employees, and deal effectively with customers and co-workers. <p>Good communication is central to effective healthcare. Healthcare providers may want to achieve several goals namely:</p>
<ul>
<li>Providing information</li>
<li>Instruction or assurance</li>
<li>Influencing opinions and attitudes</li>
<li>Changing behavior.</li>
</ul>
<p>There are six core issues that need to be taken to account when planning any health communication:</p>
<ul>
<li>The purpose of the message</li>
<li>The state of mind of the intended recipient(s), including their cognitive abilities and emotional state</li>
<li>The general context or climate in which the message will be received</li>
<li>The medium of communication to be used</li>
<li>Feedback mechanisms to assess the effects of the message.</li>
<li>Monitoring and evaluation</li>
</ul>

<div data-testid="stHeadingWithActionElements" class="st-emotion-cache-1p9ibxm e1icttdg2"><h1 level="1" id="purpose-of-communication">PURPOSE OF COMMUNICATION<span data-testid="stHeaderActionElements" class="st-emotion-cache-gi0tri e1icttdg3"><a href="#purpose-of-communication" class="st-emotion-cache-yinll1 e1icttdg1"><svg xmlns="http://www.w3.org/2000/svg" width="16" height="16" viewBox="0 0 24 24" fill="none" stroke="currentColor" stroke-width="2" stroke-linecap="round" stroke-linejoin="round"><path d="M15 7h3a5 5 0 0 1 5 5 5 5 0 0 1-5 5h-3m-6 0H6a5 5 0 0 1-5-5 5 5 0 0 1 5-5h3"></path><line x1="8" y1="12" x2="16" y2="12"></line></svg></a></span></h1></div>
<ul>
<li>For instruction – The communicator transmits with necessary directives and guidance to the next level, so as to enable them to accomplish particular tasks. Flows from top to the lower level</li>
<li>For integration – involves bringing about inter-relationship among the various functions of the business organization. It helps in unification of different management functions.</li>
<li>For information – inform individuals or group about particular task or company policies and procedures. Top management informs policies to lower levels through middle level. Information can flow vertically, horizontally or diagonally across the organization.</li>
<li>For evaluation – examination of activities to inform an idea or judgment of the worth of task is achieved through communication. Communication is a tool to appraise the individual or team’s contribution to the organization. Evaluating one’s own input or other’s outputs or some ideological scheme demands an adequate and effective communication process.</li>
<li>For direction – communication is necessary for issuing directions by the top management or manager to lower level. Employee can perform better when he is directed by his senior. Direction can be communicated either orally or through writing. An order may be common order, request order, or implied order.</li>
<li>For teaching – the importance of personal safety on the job has been greatly recognized. A complete communication process is required to teach and educate workers about personal safety on their jobs. This communication helps workers to avert accidents, risks thus avoiding costs and procedures that are risky at work place.</li>
<li>For influencing – it is necessary for leaders at workplace to persuade others of the need to accomplish tasks and goals at the organization. Communication thus provides a feedback which tells the effect of communication.</li>
<li>For image building – a business enterprise cannot isolate from the rest of the society. There is interrelationship and interdependence between the society and the enterprise operating in the society. Goodwill and confidence are necessarily created among the public. It can be done by the communication with the different media, which has to project the image of the enterprise in a positive manner.</li>

<div data-testid="stHeadingWithActionElements" class="st-emotion-cache-1p9ibxm e1icttdg2"><h1 level="1" id="importance-of-internal-communication">Importance of internal Communication<span data-testid="stHeaderActionElements" class="st-emotion-cache-gi0tri e1icttdg3"><a href="#importance-of-internal-communication" class="st-emotion-cache-yinll1 e1icttdg1"><svg xmlns="http://www.w3.org/2000/svg" width="16" height="16" viewBox="0 0 24 24" fill="none" stroke="currentColor" stroke-width="2" stroke-linecap="round" stroke-linejoin="round"><path d="M15 7h3a5 5 0 0 1 5 5 5 5 0 0 1-5 5h-3m-6 0H6a5 5 0 0 1-5-5 5 5 0 0 1 5-5h3"></path><line x1="8" y1="12" x2="16" y2="12"></line></svg></a></span></h1></div>
<ul>
<li>Internal communication refers to exchange of information or message between persons and departments of the same organization. Its importance includes:</li>
<li>Better understanding – good communication enhances better understanding between the employer and the employee. If the complaints of staff are communicated to the employers in time and in right way then the chances of any misunderstanding become minimal</li>
<li>Greater efficiency – sound communication system enables the management to instruct the supervisors and subordinate staff about any change of policy, hence increasing the efficiency of workers</li>
<li>Effective coordination – only under good communication system can employees coordinate effectively</li>
<li>Avoid losses – proper communication system enables the employees to inform the management about any breakdown of machinery or other shortages. The management can action in time and avoid any possible losses</li>
<li>Saleemi N.A. (2010). Principles and Practice of Management Simplified</li>
<li>Saleemi N.A. (2010). Business English Simplified</li>

<div data-testid="stHeadingWithActionElements" class="st-emotion-cache-1p9ibxm e1icttdg2"><h1 level="1" id="importance-of-external-communication">Importance of external Communication<span data-testid="stHeaderActionElements" class="st-emotion-cache-gi0tri e1icttdg3"><a href="#importance-of-external-communication" class="st-emotion-cache-yinll1 e1icttdg1"><svg xmlns="http://www.w3.org/2000/svg" width="16" height="16" viewBox="0 0 24 24" fill="none" stroke="currentColor" stroke-width="2" stroke-linecap="round" stroke-linejoin="round"><path d="M15 7h3a5 5 0 0 1 5 5 5 5 0 0 1-5 5h-3m-6 0H6a5 5 0 0 1-5-5 5 5 0 0 1 5-5h3"></path><line x1="8" y1="12" x2="16" y2="12"></line></svg></a></span></h1></div>
<ul>
<li>External communication refers to exchange of information or messages between a particular organization or office and outside persons and organizations. Its importance includes:</li>
<li>Good reputation – effective communication with customers and other business enterprises enables a company to establish good reputation which increases company's prestige</li>
<li>Improvement in public relations – when a company communicates with the general public to keep them informed about its activities and services then more people become interested to deal with the company</li>
<li>Better business prospects – good communication attracts more customers which in return can increase sales in the organization</li>
<li>Choice of customers – a company can get the information about the liking and disliking of customers. This information helps the company to produce goods according to customers' choice</li>
<li>Government departments – business organizations are required to deal with licensing authorities, foreign trade offices, customs authorities, banks and other financial institutions, income tax and VAT offices, post office authorities where at times negotiations are needful</li>
<li>Job requirements – some areas like personnel, public relations, marketing, sales, labour relations call for exceptional communication skills which has become an important requirement.</li>
<li>Cole A. G (2004). Management Theory and Practice 6th Edition</li>
</ul>

<ul>
<li>The story of human communication begins with language some 500m years ago with small group of prehistory hunters who lived in caves. It is believed that during this period people received and exchanged information about the environment through their senses: smell, sight, taste, touch and hearing. They also communicated with each other through gestures, posture and facial expressions, and expressed a limited number of sounds such as grunts and cries. The inability to speak put limits on the ability of these early people to think and be creative. Over time, climatic changes caused people to move out of the caves and settle in small communities. The need to communicate played an increasingly important role in their ability to participate in community life. The development of speech and language was the first major revolution in the means of communication available to human beings.</li>
