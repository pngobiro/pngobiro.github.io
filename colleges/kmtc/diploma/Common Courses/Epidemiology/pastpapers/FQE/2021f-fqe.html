Section 1: Multiple Choice Questions

40 Marks
1.
The following statements are true about an epidemiologist except:
a) One who studies the etiology, diagnosis and prevention of disease in a community
b) One who uses a thermometer and stethoscope to draw inferences and conclusion about an individual's disease
c) Measures the frequency of disease in a specified population
d) Counts cases of disease in relation to census of the population at risk
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) One who uses a thermometer and stethoscope to draw inferences and conclusion about an individual's disease

This describes a Clinician. Epidemiologists focus on populations ("The Community is the Patient"), not individual diagnosis using physical exam tools.

2.
Which of the following information is NOT included when calculating prevalence?
a) New cases occurring within a time period are included
b) All persons with the disease that have carried over previous time period
c) All people who recovered from a particular disease in the previous period
d) Recurrences during a succeeding time period (one year) are included
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) All people who recovered from a particular disease in the previous period

Prevalence measures existing cases. If someone recovered, they are no longer a case and are excluded from the prevalence count.

3.
Which of the following is not a property of proportions in epidemiology?
a) Proportions are the most descriptive measures used in epidemiology
b) They are used to describe the amount of disease that can be attributed to a particular exposure
c) When calculating proportion, the numerator is included to the denominator
d) Proportion cannot be expressed in a fraction or decimal
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Proportion cannot be expressed in a fraction or decimal

This is false. Proportions are expressed as fractions (e.g., 1/10), decimals (0.1), or percentages (10%).

4.
The following are examples of individual based descriptive study, which one is not?
a) Ecological/correlational studies
b) Case series
c) Cross-sectional studies
d) Longitudinal studies
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Ecological/correlational studies

Ecological studies use populations or groups as the unit of analysis, not individuals.

5.
Which of the following statements is not true about cohort studies?
a) They are suitable for common diseases
b) They are suitable for rare diseases
c) In cohort studies differential misclassification of exposure is less likely to occur
d) Can be used to evaluate multiple health effects of one or more exposures
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) They are suitable for rare diseases

Cohort studies are inefficient for rare diseases because you would need an incredibly large sample size to find enough cases. Case-control studies are better for rare diseases.

6.
Which of the following is not a determinant of prevalence?
a) Cure rate
b) Period
c) Fatality rate
d) Population growth rate
Show Answer

Answer: This is tricky as all options can affect prevalence mathematically. However, in standard determinants ($P \approx I \times D$), "Period" is part of the definition (Point vs Period) rather than a biological determinant like Cure or Fatality (which affect Duration). Or, population growth adds to the denominator. Usually, "Period" is the least likely "determinant" in this context.

7.
The following statements are true about relative risk except:
a) It shows ones risk of developing a disease over a time period
b) It measures the strength of association between a risk of exposure and an outcome
c) It requires knowledge of all those exposed and un-exposed
d) It measures the risk of developing a disease relative to the exposure
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) It shows ones risk of developing a disease over a time period

Option (a) describes Incidence (Absolute Risk). Relative Risk compares two risks (ratio), it doesn't just show "one's risk" in isolation.

8.
Which of the following is not an ingredient of a good disease surveillance system?
a) Good feedback and rapid response
b) Basic and sound knowledge of epidemiology
c) Availability of clear case definition
d) An effective process of identifying the diagnosis
Show Answer

Answer: All options seem positive. However, "effective process of identifying the diagnosis" might imply advanced lab capacity which isn't strictly required for syndromic surveillance. Usually, a lack of Simplicity is a flaw. In this context, all are good, but option (d) is the most variable requirement (syndromic surveillance doesn't always need confirmed diagnosis initially).

9.
The following are sources of surveillance data except:
a) Laboratory reports of infections
b) Reports from investigation of outbreaks
c) Active surveillance for all diseases
d) Mortality reports from health care professionals describing individual cases
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Active surveillance for all diseases

"Active surveillance for all diseases" is not a data source; it is a method, and practically impossible/too expensive to do for all diseases.

10.
Which of the following cannot be a route disease infection?
a) Congenital
b) Urogenital
c) Skin
d) Gastro-intestinal
Show Answer

Answer: This question is poorly phrased as all options represent portals of entry/transmission routes. "Congenital" (Transplacental) is a route. "Urogenital", "Skin", and "GI" are portals. If forced, maybe distinction between Portal vs Route.

11.
All of the following are true about odds ratio except:
a) It is an estimate of relative risk
b) It is only a measure of risk and can be obtained directly from a case control study
c) It can be calculated without data on the rates (as in a case control study)
d) It is a ratio of incidence in exposed divided by incidence in unexposed
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) It is a ratio of incidence in exposed divided by incidence in unexposed

Option (d) is the definition of Relative Risk. Odds Ratio compares odds, not incidence rates.

12.
What do we call the mode of transport of an infectious agent through an environment to a susceptible host?
a) Carrier
b) Reservoir
c) Vector
d) Vehicle
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Vehicle (or possibly Vector depending on context)

Generally, "transport through environment" implies an inanimate object (Vehicle) like water or air. A Vector is a living transporter.

17.
Prevalence rate is useful to health care planners because it measures the following except:
a) The need for patients
b) The need for treatment
c) Health care manpower needs
d) Information important for planning health care services
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) The need for patients

Planners need to know the need for beds/facilities (based on patient load), not "need for patients". The phrasing is slightly awkward, but options B, C, and D are valid uses of prevalence (burden of disease).

18.
Which one of the following in not a factor for secular changes of disease?
a) Economic changes
b) Successes of eradication programmes
c) Rapid urbanization
d) None of the above
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) None of the above

Secular trends are long-term changes. Economics, eradication programs (e.g., polio), and urbanization all drive long-term secular changes.

19.
The common outbreak of covid-19 disease can be prevented through the following except:
a) Water treatment
b) Health education
c) Case treatment
d) Quarantine
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Water treatment

COVID-19 is respiratory, not water-borne. Water treatment prevents Cholera/Typhoid.

20.
Which of the following is not among the principles of screening?
a) It should be acceptable
b) The cost of screening should balance the benefits
c) Knowledge on natural history of disease is not important
d) The interval for repeating the test should be determined
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Knowledge on natural history of disease is not important

Understanding the natural history (specifically the latent period) is crucial for screening.

21.
In epidemiology the "BEINGS" model is useful paradigm for:

BEINGS: Biological, Environmental, Immunological, Nutritional, Genetic, Social factors (Risk factors/Etiology).

State the difference between the following terms (5 Marks)

(ii) Confounding factors and association
(iii) Portal of entry and portal of exit
(iv) Risk factor and incubation period
(v) Disease distribution and burden of disease
Show Answer

In a community the number of individuals who ate various floods led to further discoveries that 21 of the 26 persons who ate fish in 1000 household became ill. What is the attack rate for those who ate fish? Is this secondary or primary attack rate. (5 Marks)
Show Answer

Section 3: Long Answer Question

20 Marks
1.

Covid-19 disease has become a common outbreak in the world. The disease is a respiratory disease transmitted by corona virus and it is prevalent in China, France and USA among others. Assuming Kenya is a covid-19 free country and unfortunately James Koyiet who travelled from China to Narok County two weeks ago developed signs similar to those of a person suffering from covid-19 and upon being taken to Narok County hospital he passed on. Explain the steps you would take to investigate the outbreak assuming that you have been provided with all the required resources.

Show Answer

Steps for Outbreak Investigation:

1. PREPARATION (Team, PPE, Transport) | 2. VERIFY DIAGNOSIS (Review records, Lab tests) | 3. CONFIRM OUTBREAK (Compare current vs expected) | 4. CASE DEFINITION (Time, Place, Person, Clinical) | 5. FIND CASES (Contact tracing, Active search) | 6. DESCRIPTIVE EPI (Epidemic Curve, Maps) | 7. HYPOTHESIS (Source: Travel/China) | 8. ANALYTIC STUDY (Test hypothesis if needed) | 9. CONTROL (Isolation, Quarantine) | 10. REPORT (Communication, Prevention)
  1. Prepare for field work: Assemble a Rapid Response Team (RRT) with epidemiologists, clinicians, and lab techs. Ensure PPE, sample collection kits, and transport are ready.
  2. Establish the existence of an outbreak: One case of a new disease (COVID-19) in a free country constitutes an outbreak.
  3. Verify the diagnosis: Review James Koyiet's clinical history (travel to China, respiratory symptoms). Collect post-mortem samples for PCR testing to confirm COVID-19.
  4. Construct a working case definition:
    • Suspect: Fever/Cough + Travel to China in last 14 days.
    • Confirmed: Lab positive.
  5. Find cases systematically and record information:
    • Contact Tracing: Identify everyone James interacted with since arrival (family, taxi driver, plane passengers).
    • Quarantine these contacts.
  6. Perform Descriptive Epidemiology: Map his movements (Time/Place). Describe contacts by Age/Sex (Person). Draw an epidemic curve (though it's currently 1 case).
  7. Develop Hypotheses: Source is likely imported infection from China. Transmission may have occurred to close contacts.
  8. Evaluate Hypotheses: Test contacts. If contacts are positive, local transmission is confirmed.
  9. Implement Control and Prevention Measures:
    • Isolation of any new symptomatic cases.
    • Quarantine of asymptomatic contacts (14 days).
    • Safe burial for the deceased.
    • Public education on hygiene and symptoms.
  10. Communicate Findings: Report to Ministry of Health and WHO. Issue situation reports.
