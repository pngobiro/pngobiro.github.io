Which of the following is not a type of analytic studies?
a) Cross sectional surveys
b) Observational studies
c) Case control studies

Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Cross sectional surveys

While cross-sectional studies can be analytic, they are primarily descriptive (prevalence studies). "Observational studies" is a category that includes analytic types (Cohort, Case Control). Given the options, Cross-sectional is the best candidate for "not analytic" in many basic classifications.

41.
List and explain five advantages of passive surveillance. (5 marks)
Show Answer

Answer:

  1. Cost-effective: Requires fewer dedicated resources and staff compared to active surveillance.
  2. Wide Coverage: Can cover large geographic areas and entire populations.
  3. Baseline Data: Provides data on disease trends over long periods.
  4. Sustainability: Easier to maintain over time as it's integrated into routine health services.
  5. Standardization: Uses standard reporting forms suitable for international comparison.
42.
State and explain three types of cases in the case definition of COVID-19. (5 marks)
Show Answer

Answer:

  1. Suspect Case: A person meeting clinical criteria (fever, cough) AND epidemiological link (travel history or contact).
  2. Probable Case: A suspect case with inconclusive testing or a chest X-ray suggestive of COVID-19.
  3. Confirmed Case: A person with laboratory confirmation of COVID-19 infection (PCR positive), irrespective of clinical signs and symptoms.
43.
Briefly explain the concept of confounding. (5 marks)
Show Answer

Answer:

Confounding is a distortion of the association between an exposure and an outcome by a third variable (the confounder). For a variable to be a confounder, it must:

  1. Be associated with the exposure.
  2. Be an independent risk factor for the outcome.
  3. Not be in the causal pathway between exposure and outcome.

Example: Age confounding the relationship between grey hair and cancer.

44.
Explain the concept of herd immunity. (5 marks)
Show Answer

Answer:

Herd immunity is the resistance of a group to an attack by a disease to which a large proportion of the members of the group are immune. When a high percentage of the population is vaccinated or immune (threshold), it disrupts transmission, indirectly protecting those who are not immune (e.g., newborns, immunocompromised) because the pathogen cannot find enough susceptible hosts to spread.

45.
An area Y has a population of 100,000. 20 persons become sick of malaria in a year. 18 of the 20 persons die from malaria. (5 marks)
i. Calculate the mortality rate from malaria
ii. Calculate the case fatality rate from malaria
Show Answer

i. Cause Specific Mortality Rate:

$\frac{\text{Deaths from Malaria}}{\text{Total Population}} \times 100,000$

$\frac{18}{100,000} \times 100,000 = 18$ deaths per 100,000 population.

ii. Case Fatality Rate (CFR):

$\frac{\text{Deaths from Malaria}}{\text{Cases of Malaria}} \times 100$

$\frac{18}{20} \times 100 = 90\%$

46.
List and explain five guidelines for judging whether an observed association is causal. (5 marks)
Show Answer

Answer (Hill's Criteria):

  1. Temporality: Cause must precede effect (Essential).
  2. Strength of Association: Stronger associations (high RR) are more likely causal.
  3. Consistency: Repeatedly observed by different persons, in different places.
  4. Biological Gradient (Dose-response): Increased exposure leads to increased risk.
  5. Plausibility: It makes biological sense according to current knowledge.
47.
List and explain five different types of epidemiology. (5 marks)
Show Answer
  1. Descriptive Epidemiology: Studies distribution (Time, Place, Person).
  2. Analytic Epidemiology: Studies determinants (Causes) using comparison groups.
  3. Experimental Epidemiology: Involves active intervention (Trials).
  4. Clinical Epidemiology: Application of principles to patient care.
  5. Social Epidemiology: Studies social determinants of health.
48.
Write short notes on incidence density. (5 marks)
Show Answer

Answer:

Incidence Density (or Incidence Rate) measures the rate at which new cases occur in a population. Unlike cumulative incidence, it uses Person-Time in the denominator (e.g., person-years). It handles dynamic populations where people enter/leave the study at different times. It reflects the "speed" of disease occurrence.

49.
Using a well labeled diagram, explain cohort studies with use of coronary heart disease and smoking as an example. (10 marks)
Show Answer

Answer:

A Cohort Study starts with a disease-free population, classifies them by exposure (Smoking), and follows them forward in time to see who develops the outcome (CHD).

TIME (Follow-up Period) ----------------------> /-----------------> Developed CHD (a) / (Smokers) Study /-------------------> No CHD (b) Population / (Disease Free) < \ \ (Non-Smokers) \------------------> Developed CHD (c) \ \----------------> No CHD (d) Calculate Relative Risk (RR) = (a / a+b) / (c / c+d)

Explanation:

  1. Select Population: Identify a group free of CHD.
  2. Classify Exposure: Determine who smokes (Exposed) and who doesn't (Unexposed).
  3. Follow-up: Monitor both groups over time (e.g., 10 years).
  4. Outcome: Count how many develop CHD in each group.
  5. Analysis: Compare incidence in smokers vs non-smokers. If incidence is higher in smokers, smoking is a risk factor.
50.

The following table presents the results of a screening test. Calculate: i. Sensitivity, ii. Specificity, iii. Positive predictive value, iv. Negative predictive value.

Results Disease No disease Total
Positive 80 (a) 100 (b) 180
Negative 20 (c) 800 (d) 820
Total 100 900 1000
Show Answer

i. Sensitivity:

$\frac{\text{True Positives}}{\text{Total Diseased}} = \frac{80}{100} \times 100 = 80\%$

ii. Specificity:

$\frac{\text{True Negatives}}{\text{Total Non-Diseased}} = \frac{800}{900} \times 100 = 88.9\%$

iii. Positive Predictive Value (PPV):

$\frac{\text{True Positives}}{\text{Total Positives}} = \frac{80}{180} \times 100 = 44.4\%$

iv. Negative Predictive Value (NPV):

$\frac{\text{True Negatives}}{\text{Total Negatives}} = \frac{800}{820} \times 100 = 97.6\%$
