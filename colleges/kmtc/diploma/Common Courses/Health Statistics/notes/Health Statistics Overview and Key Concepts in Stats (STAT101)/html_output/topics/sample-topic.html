üìä Introduction to Health Statistics

Health statistics is the science of collecting, organizing, summarizing/analyzing, presenting and interpreting health data and using them for decision making.

Key Definitions

  • Health: A state of complete physical, mental and social well-being and not merely the absence of diseases or infirmity.
  • Statistics: The study of how to collect, organize, analyze and interpret numerical information from data.

Types of Statistics

Statistics has 3 distinct parts:

  1. Descriptive Statistics: Summarizes population data numerically or graphically
  2. Inferential Statistics: Infers from a sample and generalizes to the population
  3. Experimental Statistics: Relates to the design of experiments

Sample Formula

The sampling interval for systematic random sampling is calculated as:

$$K = \frac{N}{n}$$

Where:

  • $K$ = interval length
  • $N$ = total population
  • $n$ = sample size
