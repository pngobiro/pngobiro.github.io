Section 1: Multiple Choice Questions

40 Marks
1.
The following are probability distributions except
a) Binomial distribution
b) Frequency distribution
c) Poisson distribution
d) Normal distribution
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Frequency distribution

Binomial, Poisson, and Normal are theoretical probability distributions. A Frequency distribution is a descriptive summary of actual observed data.

2.
Another name for chain referral sampling method is
a) Snowball
b) Purposive
c) Convenience
d) Quota
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Snowball

Snowball sampling (or chain-referral sampling) is a non-probability sampling technique where existing study subjects recruit future subjects from among their acquaintances.

3.
The size of a sample depends on the following except
a) The indicators chosen
b) The baseline value of the indicators
c) The amount of change you would want to measure accurately
d) The geographical area
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) The geographical area

Statistical sample size calculations depend on the variability of the population (indicators/baseline) and the desired precision (amount of change/confidence level), not directly on the physical size of the area.

4.
Which of the following is not a feature of a standard normal curve
a) Its mean is 1
b) Its standard deviation is 1
c) Its total area is 1
d) Its symmetrical and bell shaped
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Its mean is 1

A Standard Normal Curve (Z-distribution) has a Mean of 0 and a Standard Deviation of 1.

5.
The following are examples of errors during sampling except
a) Error of origin
b) Biased error
c) Sampling error
d) Non sampling error

Correct Answer: a) Error of origin

"Error of origin" is not a standard statistical term for survey errors (unlike biased error, sampling error, and non-sampling error).

6.
Which one of the following is NOT true about primary sources of data?
a) It gives very reliable data
b) It is very cheap to conduct
c) There is a possibility of sampling errors
d) It has a short-term value

Correct Answer: b) It is very cheap to conduct

Primary data collection (surveys, experiments) is typically expensive and time-consuming compared to using secondary data.

7.
Which of the following is NOT TRUE?

Degrees of freedom usually involve \( n-1 \) or \( (rows-1)(cols-1) \), not just the raw number of categories.

Section 2: Short Answer Questions

40 Marks
1.
State five types of events in probability (5 marks)
Show Answer

Answer:

  1. Simple Event: An event with a single outcome.
  2. Compound Event: An event with more than one outcome.
  3. Independent Events: The occurrence of one event does not affect the other.
  4. Dependent Events: The occurrence of one event affects the probability of the other.
  5. Mutually Exclusive Events: Events that cannot happen at the same time.
2.
List five properties of normal distribution (5 marks)
Show Answer

Answer:

  1. The curve is bell-shaped and symmetrical.
  2. The Mean, Median, and Mode are equal and located at the center.
  3. The total area under the curve is equal to 1 (or 100%).
  4. It is asymptotic (the tails get closer to the x-axis but never touch it).
  5. It is defined by two parameters: Mean (\(\mu\)) and Standard Deviation (\(\sigma\)).
3.
Calculate the number of classes required for the following data:
9, 5, 6, 12, 18, 25, 32, 46, 8, 7, 10, 12, 16, 17, 21, 24, 26, 29, 32, 35, 38 (5 marks)
Show Answer

Answer:

First, count the number of observations (N):

Data points: 9, 5, 6, 12, 18, 25, 32, 46, 8, 7, 10, 12, 16, 17, 21, 24, 26, 29, 32, 35, 38.

Total count \( N = 21 \).

Using Sturges' Rule: \( k = 1 + 3.322 \log_{10} N \)

\( k = 1 + 3.322 \log_{10} (21) \)

\( \log_{10}(21) \approx 1.322 \)

\( k = 1 + (3.322 \times 1.322) \)

\( k = 1 + 4.39 = 5.39 \)

Rounding to the nearest integer, the number of classes required is 5 (or 6).

4.
Briefly describe the two types of numerical data (5 marks)
Show Answer

Answer:

  1. Discrete Data: Numerical data that can only take specific, distinct values (usually integers). There are gaps between possible values. Examples: Number of children, number of beds.
  2. Continuous Data: Numerical data that can take any value within a range. It can be measured to infinite precision (decimals). Examples: Height, weight, temperature, time.
5.
Outline 5 important points to consider while constructing a histogram (5 marks)
Show Answer

Answer:

  1. Continuous Data: Ensure data is continuous; bars must touch each other (gap width = 0).
  2. Class Intervals: Classes should generally be of equal width. If unequal, frequency density must be used.
  3. Axes Labeling: Clearly label the X-axis (class boundaries/midpoints) and Y-axis (frequency).
  4. Zero Origin: The Y-axis should start at zero. The X-axis can use a break mark if values start high.
  5. Title: Provide a descriptive title explaining what the graph represents.

Section 3: Long Answer Questions

20 Marks
1a.
In a study to determine the adoption of EMR systems in Maragua Sub county hospital, 150 heath professionals were studied. Determine the sample size if the proportion of the characteristic being measured was 30%. Use the fishers et al formula at 95% confidence level. (10 marks)
Show Answer

Answer:

Step 1: Calculate sample size for infinite population (\(n_0\))

Fisher's Formula: \( n_0 = \frac{Z^2 p q}{d^2} \)

  • \( Z = 1.96 \) (for 95% confidence)
  • \( p = 0.30 \) (proportion)
  • \( q = 1 - p = 0.70 \)
  • \( d = 0.05 \) (margin of error/precision)

\( n_0 = \frac{1.96^2 \times 0.3 \times 0.7}{0.05^2} \)

\( n_0 = \frac{3.8416 \times 0.21}{0.0025} \)

\( n_0 = \frac{0.806736}{0.0025} \approx 322.7 \)

\( n_0 \approx 323 \)

Step 2: Adjust for Finite Population (Correction)

Population size \( N = 150 \).

Since \( n_0 > N \), we must apply the correction formula:

\( n = \frac{n_0}{1 + \frac{n_0 - 1}{N}} \)

\( n = \frac{323}{1 + \frac{322}{150}} \)

\( n = \frac{323}{1 + 2.146} = \frac{323}{3.146} \)

\( n \approx 102.6 \)

Final Sample Size: 103 Health Professionals.

1b.
Pona hospital had a bed complement of 150 and an average length of stay of 7 days. The hospital had a daily occupancy of 119 with bed turn over of 57 patients. At discharge each patient paid 250 shillings per day as fees.

Calculate:
i. Percentage occupancy (3 marks)
ii. Total discharges and death (3 marks)
iii. Turn over interval (2 marks)
iv. Total fees collected in the year (2 marks)
Show Answer

Answer:

Note: There is an inconsistency in the question data ("turn over of 57 patients" usually implies count, but at 150 beds that is too low; ALOS of 7 implies higher throughput. We will calculate based on standard relationships derived from ALOS and Daily Occupancy).

i. Percentage Occupancy:

\( \% \text{Occupancy} = \frac{\text{Average Daily Census}}{\text{Bed Count}} \times 100 \)

\( = \frac{119}{150} \times 100 = \textbf{79.33\%} \)

ii. Total Discharges and Death (DD):

Using ALOS Formula: \( \text{ALOS} = \frac{\text{Total Inpatient Days}}{\text{DD}} \)

Total Inpatient Days = Daily Occupancy 365 = \( 119 \times 365 = 43,435 \).

\( 7 = \frac{43,435}{\text{DD}} \)

\( \text{DD} = \frac{43,435}{7} = \textbf{6,205 patients} \)

iii. Turnover Interval (TOI):

TOI is the average time a bed remains empty between patients.

\( \text{TOI} = \frac{\text{Available Bed Days} - \text{Patient Days}}{\text{Discharges (DD)}} \)

Available Bed Days = \( 150 \times 365 = 54,750 \).

\( \text{TOI} = \frac{54,750 - 43,435}{6,205} = \frac{11,315}{6,205} \approx \textbf{1.82 days} \)

iv. Total Fees Collected:

Fee is per patient per day (Total Inpatient Days).

Total Fees = Total Patient Days Rate

\( = 43,435 \times 250 = \textbf{10,858,750 Shillings} \)
