10.
In a moderately skewed distribution the value of mode and median are 8 and 6 respectively. The value of mean in such a situation is approximated equal to
a) 1
b) 5
c) -5
d) -1
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) 5

Using the empirical relationship: \( \text{Mean} - \text{Mode} = 3(\text{Mean} - \text{Median}) \).
\( \text{Mean} - 8 = 3(\text{Mean} - 6) \)
\( \text{Mean} - 8 = 3\text{Mean} - 18 \)
\( 10 = 2\text{Mean} \rightarrow \text{Mean} = 5 \).

Section 2: Short Answer Questions

1.
Define the following terms as used in probability (5 marks)
i. Event
ii. Compound event
iii. Mutually exclusive events
iv. Experiment
v. Trial
Show Answer

Answer:

  • i. Event: A set of outcomes of an experiment (a subset of the sample space).
  • ii. Compound event: An event that consists of two or more simple events or outcomes.
  • iii. Mutually exclusive events: Events that cannot occur at the same time; if one happens, the other cannot.
  • iv. Experiment: A process or procedure that leads to one of several possible outcomes (e.g., rolling a die).
  • v. Trial: A single performance of an experiment.
2.
A binomial distribution has n=20 and P=0.3. Find the mean and standard deviation in this distribution (5 marks)
Show Answer

Answer:

Given: \( n = 20 \), \( p = 0.3 \). Then \( q = 1 - 0.3 = 0.7 \).

Mean (\(\mu\)): \(\mu = np = 20 \times 0.3 = \textbf{6}\)

Standard Deviation (\(\sigma\)): \(\sigma = \sqrt{npq}\)

\(\sigma = \sqrt{20 \times 0.3 \times 0.7} = \sqrt{4.2} \approx \textbf{2.05}\)

3.
Briefly discuss five (5) disadvantages of interview in data collection.
Show Answer

Answer:

  1. Time Consuming: It takes a lot of time to interview subjects individually.
  2. Expensive: Requires trained interviewers, travel costs, etc.
  3. Interviewer Bias: The interviewer's presence, tone, or appearance may influence responses.
  4. Small Sample Size: Due to cost/time, sample sizes are usually smaller than questionnaires.
  5. Lack of Anonymity: Respondents may not feel comfortable answering sensitive questions face-to-face.

5.
In the past three months, you have noted that the average length of stay in the facility you are working is 20 days. Advice the management on various actions to be taken to inform their routine decisions (5 marks)
Show Answer

Answer:

An ALOS of 20 days is generally high for acute care. Management should consider:

  1. Discharge Planning: Investigate if delays in discharge processes are keeping patients longer than medically necessary.
  2. Case Mix Analysis: Determine if the facility is treating more chronic/complex cases (e.g., orthopedic, psychiatric) which naturally have longer stays.
  3. Efficiency Review: Audit clinical pathways to ensure timely diagnostics and treatments.
  4. Bed Utilization: Assess if long stays are causing bed blockages for new admissions.
  5. Resource Allocation: Ensure staffing and resources align with long-term patient care needs.
6.
In a class of 50 students, 10 have failed and their average marks is 25. The total marks scored by the entire class were 2810. Find the average mark of those who have passed. (5 marks)
Show Answer

Answer:

Total Students = 50. Total Marks = 2810.

Number of Failed Students = 10. Mean mark of Failed = 25.

Total marks of Failed = \( 10 \times 25 = 250 \).

Number of Passed Students = \( 50 - 10 = 40 \).

Total marks of Passed = \( \text{Total Class Marks} - \text{Total Failed Marks} = 2810 - 250 = 2560 \).

Average mark of Passed = \( \frac{2560}{40} = \textbf{64} \).

7.
Before embarking on a data collection exercise, it is imperative to do a pilot survey. Briefly explain how useful the pilot survey is. (5 marks)
Show Answer

Answer:

  1. Testing Instruments: Helps identify ambiguous or confusing questions in the questionnaire.
  2. Estimating Time/Cost: Provides a realistic estimate of the time and budget required for the main survey.
  3. Training Staff: Allows field enumerators to practice and standardize data collection procedures.
  4. Checking Feasibility: Tests the logistics (transport, technology) to ensure the study is practical.
  5. Refining Methodology: Highlights potential problems in the sampling frame or selection process.
8.
Briefly describe the four levels of measurement (5 marks)
Show Answer

Answer:

  1. Nominal: Data categorised by names/labels with no intrinsic order (e.g., Gender, Blood Type).
  2. Ordinal: Data with a meaningful order or rank, but intervals between ranks are unknown (e.g., Satisfaction scale, Severity of pain).
  3. Interval: Numerical data with equal intervals between values but no true zero point (e.g., Temperature in Celsius).
  4. Ratio: Numerical data with equal intervals and a true absolute zero, allowing for ratios (e.g., Weight, Height, Age).

Section 3: Long Answer Questions

20 Marks
1a.
Assuming that a hospital has two departments, past records shows that department 1 collects 30% of the total revenue and department 2 collects 70% of the revenue. Further information indicates that 5% of the revenue collected by department 1 were used for salaries and only 1% collected by department 2 were used for salaries. If a salary is drawn at random, what is the probability that the salary was produced by department 1 or department 2.
Show Answer

Answer:

Let \( D1 \) be revenue from Dept 1, \( D2 \) be revenue from Dept 2.

\( P(D1) = 0.30 \), \( P(D2) = 0.70 \).

Let \( S \) be "revenue used for salary".

\( P(S|D1) = 0.05 \), \( P(S|D2) = 0.01 \).

Step 1: Calculate Total Probability of finding a Salary revenue (P(S)):

\( P(S) = P(D1) \cdot P(S|D1) + P(D2) \cdot P(S|D2) \)

\( P(S) = (0.30 \times 0.05) + (0.70 \times 0.01) \)

\( P(S) = 0.015 + 0.007 = 0.022 \)

The question asks: "If a salary is drawn at random, what is the probability that the salary was produced by department 1 or department 2."

Assuming this asks for the probability that a salary unit came from Dept 1 (Posterior Probability):

\( P(D1|S) = \frac{P(D1) \cdot P(S|D1)}{P(S)} = \frac{0.015}{0.022} \approx \textbf{0.68} \)

Probability it came from Dept 2:

\( P(D2|S) = \frac{P(D2) \cdot P(S|D2)}{P(S)} = \frac{0.007}{0.022} \approx \textbf{0.32} \)

1b.
In an experiment on drug testing, Thomas obtained the following frequencies of results: 315 positives, 101 false positives, 108 negatives and 32 false negatives. According to his theory of testing the numbers should be in proportion 9:3:3:1. Is there evidence to doubt the theory at [implied significance level, usually 0.05]?
Show Answer

Answer:

1. Hypotheses:

\( H_0 \): The data follows the 9:3:3:1 ratio (Good fit).

\( H_1 \): The data does not follow the ratio (Bad fit).

2. Calculations:

Total Observation (N) = \( 315 + 101 + 108 + 32 = 556 \).

Ratio Total Parts = \( 9 + 3 + 3 + 1 = 16 \).

Expected Frequencies (E):

  • Positives: \( \frac{9}{16} \times 556 = 312.75 \)
  • False Positives: \( \frac{3}{16} \times 556 = 104.25 \)
  • Negatives: \( \frac{3}{16} \times 556 = 104.25 \)
  • False Negatives: \( \frac{1}{16} \times 556 = 34.75 \)

3. Chi-Square Calculation \( \sum \frac{(O-E)^2}{E} \):

  • \( \frac{(315 - 312.75)^2}{312.75} = \frac{5.06}{312.75} = 0.016 \)
  • \( \frac{(101 - 104.25)^2}{104.25} = \frac{10.56}{104.25} = 0.101 \)
  • \( \frac{(108 - 104.25)^2}{104.25} = \frac{14.06}{104.25} = 0.135 \)
  • \( \frac{(32 - 34.75)^2}{34.75} = \frac{7.56}{34.75} = 0.218 \)

Calculated \( \chi^2 \) = 0.47

4. Critical Value:

Degrees of freedom (df) = \( 4 - 1 = 3 \).

From table at 0.05 significance level, Critical Value = 7.81.

5. Conclusion:

Since \( 0.47 < 7.81 \), we fail to reject the null hypothesis. There is no evidence to doubt the theory; the observed data fits the 9:3:3:1 ratio well.
