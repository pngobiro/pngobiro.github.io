42.
Describe the stratified sampling method approach (5 marks)
Show Answer

Answer:

Stratified random sampling involves the following steps:

  1. Identify Strata: Divide the entire population into distinct, non-overlapping subgroups (strata) that share similar characteristics (e.g., age groups, gender).
  2. Determine Sample Size: Decide the total sample size and how many to sample from each stratum (can be proportionate or disproportionate to the stratum size).
  3. Random Selection: Perform simple random sampling within each stratum to select the subjects.
  4. Combine: Pool the selected subjects to form the final sample. This ensures representation of all key subgroups.

46.
In the year 2018, Kocholia sub-county hospital had 800 beds permanently allocated for inpatient use during the same period the percentage occupancy was 110% and 1500 patients were discharged home alive while 20 died. Calculate:
(i) Turn over per bed (3 marks)
(ii) Available bed days ABD (2 marks)
Show Answer

Answer:

Given: Beds = 800, Year = 2018 (365 days), Occupancy = 110%, Discharges = 1500, Deaths = 20.

(i) Turnover Per Bed (TOB)

Total Discharges & Deaths (DD) = 1500 + 20 = 1520.

\( \text{TOB} = \frac{\text{Total Deaths & Discharges}}{\text{Average Bed Count}} \)

\( \text{TOB} = \frac{1520}{800} = \textbf{1.9 patients per bed} \)

(ii) Available Bed Days (ABD)

\( \text{ABD} = \text{Bed Count} \times \text{Days in Year} \)

\( \text{ABD} = 800 \times 365 = \textbf{292,000} \)

49.
A random of 50 male employees is taken at the end of a year and the mean number of hours of absenteeism for the year is found to be 63 hours. A similar sample of 50 female employees has a mean of 66 hours. Could these samples be drawn from a population with the same mean and standard deviation of 10 hours?
Show Answer

Answer:

Hypothesis Testing (Z-test for two means)

Given:

  • \( n_1 = 50, \bar{x}_1 = 63 \) (Males)
  • \( n_2 = 50, \bar{x}_2 = 66 \) (Females)
  • Population SD (\( \sigma \)) = 10 (assumed same for both)

1. Stating Hypothesis:

\( H_0: \mu_1 = \mu_2 \) (There is no significant difference)

\( H_1: \mu_1 \neq \mu_2 \) (There is a significant difference)

2. Calculate Standard Error (SE):

\( SE = \sqrt{\frac{\sigma^2}{n_1} + \frac{\sigma^2}{n_2}} = \sigma \sqrt{\frac{1}{n_1} + \frac{1}{n_2}} \)

\( SE = 10 \sqrt{\frac{1}{50} + \frac{1}{50}} = 10 \sqrt{0.02 + 0.02} = 10 \sqrt{0.04} \)

\( SE = 10 \times 0.2 = \textbf{2} \)

3. Calculate Z-statistic:

\( Z = \frac{\bar{x}_1 - \bar{x}_2}{SE} = \frac{63 - 66}{2} = \frac{-3}{2} = \textbf{-1.5} \)

4. Conclusion (at 95% confidence level, \(\alpha=0.05\)):

Critical Z values are \(\pm 1.96\).

Since \( |-1.5| < 1.96 \), calculated Z falls within the acceptance region.

Conclusion: We fail to reject the null hypothesis. There is no significant evidence to suggest the means are different. Yes, these samples could be drawn from the same population.

50.
In 2001, Moding Health Centre recorded 11,676 inpatients days, 130 discharges and death and the health facility has a capacity of 30 beds. Calculate (10 marks)
(i) ABD (1 mark)
(ii) EVBD (1 mark)
(iii) Percentage occupancy (1 mark)
(iv) Average length of stay (ALOS) (2 marks)
(v) Supposing this unit represent 25% of the entire health facility bed and inpatients days. Calculate the entire health facility statistics. (5 marks)
Show Answer

Answer:

(i) ABD (Available Bed Days):

Days in 2001 = 365.

\( ABD = 30 \times 365 = \textbf{10,950} \)

(ii) EVBD (Excess/Empty Bed Days):

Inpatient Days (11,676) > ABD (10,950).

Wait, technically EVBD usually means "Empty". Here we have overcrowding.

Difference = \( 10,950 - 11,676 = -726 \).

This represents 726 Excess Bed Days (overcrowding).

(iii) Percentage Occupancy:

\( \frac{11,676}{10,950} \times 100 = \textbf{106.6\%} \)

(iv) Average Length of Stay (ALOS):

\( ALOS = \frac{\text{Inpatient Days}}{\text{Discharges & Deaths}} = \frac{11,676}{130} \approx \textbf{89.8 days} \)

(v) Entire Facility Statistics (Extrapolation):

If Moding HC represents 25% of the total:

  • Total Beds: \( 30 / 0.25 = \textbf{120 beds} \)
  • Total Inpatient Days: \( 11,676 / 0.25 = \textbf{46,704 days} \)
  • Total ABD: \( 120 \times 365 = 43,800 \)
  • Occupancy: Remains \(\textbf{106.6\%}\) (assuming uniform distribution)
