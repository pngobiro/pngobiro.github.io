Section 2: Short Answer Questions

1.
Differentiate between the two types of hypothesis and give an example in each case (5 marks)
Show Answer

Answer:

  • Null Hypothesis (\(H_0\)): A statement that there is no significant difference or relationship between variables. It assumes any observed difference is due to chance.
    Example: "There is no difference in blood pressure between patients taking Drug A and Drug B."
  • Alternative Hypothesis (\(H_1\)): A statement that opposes the null hypothesis, suggesting a significant difference or relationship exists.
    Example: "Patients taking Drug A have lower blood pressure than those taking Drug B."
2.
Use the data below for a certain sample to calculate the given measures of central tendency and dispersion (5 marks)
Data: 8, 12, 23, 28, 22, 26, 30, 35, 42
a) Mean (2 marks)
b) Standard Deviation (3 marks)
Show Answer

Answer:

Data: 8, 12, 23, 28, 22, 26, 30, 35, 42 (n=9)

a) Mean (\(\bar{x}\)):

Sum = \( 8+12+23+28+22+26+30+35+42 = 226 \)

Mean = \( 226 / 9 \approx \textbf{25.11} \)

b) Standard Deviation (s):

Sum of squares (\(\sum x^2\)) = \( 64 + 144 + 529 + 784 + 484 + 676 + 900 + 1225 + 1764 = 6570 \)

Variance formula: \( s^2 = \frac{\sum x^2 - \frac{(\sum x)^2}{n}}{n-1} \)

\( s^2 = \frac{6570 - \frac{226^2}{9}}{8} = \frac{6570 - 5675.11}{8} = \frac{894.89}{8} \approx 111.86 \)

Standard Deviation = \( \sqrt{111.86} \approx \textbf{10.58} \)

7.
Using a period of 25 days, allocated bed of 35 and OBD of 900. Average length of stay of 20 days. Calculate (10 marks)
a. Percentage occupancy (2 marks)
b. Discharges and Deaths (2 marks)
c. Turn over per Bed (2 marks)
d. Explain your results in a and c above (4 marks)
Show Answer

Answer:

a) Percentage Occupancy:

Available Bed Days (ABD) = 35 beds × 25 days = 875

% Occupancy = (OBD / ABD) × 100 = (900 / 875) × 100 = 102.86%

b) Discharges and Deaths (DD):

Formula: ALOS = OBD / DD => DD = OBD / ALOS

DD = 900 / 20 = 45 patients

c) Turnover per Bed (TOB):

TOB = DD / Allocated Beds

TOB = 45 / 35 = 1.29 patients per bed

d) Explanation:

  • Occupancy (102.9%): The hospital is overcrowded. The number of patients exceeds the allocated bed capacity, likely utilizing temporary beds or stretchers.
  • Turnover (1.29): In this 25-day period, each bed treated approximately 1.3 patients. This reflects a relatively slow turnover, consistent with the long ALOS of 20 days.

Section 3: Long Answer Questions

20 Marks
1.
Test whether there is a significant association between smoking and Lung cancer at 95% confidence level. (10 marks)
Lung Cancer
Smoking YES NO
YES 39 21
NO 29 19
Show Answer

Answer:

1. Hypothesis:

\(H_0\): Smoking and Lung Cancer are independent (No association).

\(H_1\): Smoking and Lung Cancer are associated.

2. Totals:

Row Totals: Smokers = 60, Non-Smokers = 48 (Total N = 108)

Col Totals: Cancer = 68, No Cancer = 40

3. Expected Values (E): \( E = \frac{Row Total \times Col Total}{Grand Total} \)

  • E(Yes, Yes) = (60 × 68)/108 = 37.78
  • E(Yes, No) = (60 × 40)/108 = 22.22
  • E(No, Yes) = (48 × 68)/108 = 30.22
  • E(No, No) = (48 × 40)/108 = 17.78

4. Chi-square Statistic (\( \chi^2 \)): \( \sum \frac{(O-E)^2}{E} \)

  • (39 - 37.78)² / 37.78 = 1.488 / 37.78 = 0.039
  • (21 - 22.22)² / 22.22 = 1.488 / 22.22 = 0.067
  • (29 - 30.22)² / 30.22 = 1.488 / 30.22 = 0.049
  • (19 - 17.78)² / 17.78 = 1.488 / 17.78 = 0.084

\( \chi^2_{calc} = 0.239 \)

5. Critical Value:

df = (2-1)(2-1) = 1. At 0.05 level, Critical Value = 3.841.

6. Conclusion:

Since \( 0.239 < 3.841 \), we fail to reject the null hypothesis. There is no statistically significant association between smoking and lung cancer based on this specific dataset.

2.
The finance division of a large company is investigating its procedures for the selection of new accountancy trainees. The independent rankings of written test, interview assessment and job performance for the eight trainees are given below.
TraineeABCDEFGH
Written Test62741538
Interview14236587
Job Performance12345678
Calculate a rank correlation coefficient between:
a) Job performance and written test (4 marks)
b) Job performance and interview assessment (4 marks)
c) Interpret your results of a and b above (2 marks)
Show Answer

Answer:

Formula: \( R = 1 - \frac{6 \sum d^2}{n(n^2 - 1)} \), \(n=8\), Denominator = \( 8(63) = 504 \).

a) Job vs Written:

Differences (d): (1-6)=-5, (2-2)=0, (3-7)=-4, (4-4)=0, (5-1)=4, (6-5)=1, (7-3)=4, (8-8)=0

\(d^2\): 25, 0, 16, 0, 16, 1, 16, 0. Sum = 74.

\( R = 1 - \frac{6(74)}{504} = 1 - \frac{444}{504} = 1 - 0.88 = \textbf{+0.12} \)

b) Job vs Interview:

Differences (d): (1-1)=0, (2-4)=-2, (3-2)=1, (4-3)=1, (5-6)=-1, (6-5)=1, (7-8)=-1, (8-7)=1

\(d^2\): 0, 4, 1, 1, 1, 1, 1, 1. Sum = 10.

\( R = 1 - \frac{6(10)}{504} = 1 - \frac{60}{504} = 1 - 0.12 = \textbf{+0.88} \)

c) Interpretation:

There is a very weak positive correlation (+0.12) between the written test and job performance, meaning the test is not a good predictor. However, there is a strong positive correlation (+0.88) between the interview and job performance, suggesting the interview is a much better selection tool.
