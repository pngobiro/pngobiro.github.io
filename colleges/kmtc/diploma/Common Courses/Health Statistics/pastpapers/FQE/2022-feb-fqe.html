41.
A student sat for exams in five papers and scored the following: 64, 56, 70, 67, 73. Calculate the variance and standard deviation of the scores. (5 marks)
Show Answer

Answer:

Step 1: Calculate the Mean (\(\bar{x}\)):

\(\bar{x} = \frac{64 + 56 + 70 + 67 + 73}{5} = \frac{330}{5} = 66\)

Step 2: Calculate Deviations and Squared Deviations:

  • \( (64 - 66)^2 = (-2)^2 = 4 \)
  • \( (56 - 66)^2 = (-10)^2 = 100 \)
  • \( (70 - 66)^2 = (4)^2 = 16 \)
  • \( (67 - 66)^2 = (1)^2 = 1 \)
  • \( (73 - 66)^2 = (7)^2 = 49 \)

Sum of squared deviations \(\Sigma(x-\bar{x})^2 = 170\)

Step 3: Calculate Variance (\(S^2\)):

Assuming sample data: \(S^2 = \frac{170}{5-1} = \frac{170}{4} = \textbf{42.5}\)

Step 4: Calculate Standard Deviation (\(S\)):

\(S = \sqrt{42.5} \approx \textbf{6.52}\)

42.

In the year 2018, Mwangaza hospital which had 45 beds allocated throughout the year recorded 11,680 in-patient days. A total of 98 patients died while 2,238 were discharged during the periods. From the above information, calculate:

  1. Available bed days (1 mark)
  2. % occupancy (1 mark)
  3. Average daily vacant beds (1 mark)
  4. ALOS (1 mark)
  5. TOB (1 mark)
Show Answer

Answer:

(a) Available Bed Days (ABD)

ABD = Beds × Days = \(45 \times 365 = \textbf{16,425}\)

(b) Percentage Occupancy

Occupancy = \(\frac{\text{Total Inpatient Days}}{\text{Available Bed Days}} \times 100\)

\(= \frac{11,680}{16,425} \times 100 = \textbf{71.11\%}\)

(c) Average Daily Vacant Beds

Average Daily Census = \(\frac{11,680}{365} = 32\) beds occupied.

Vacant Beds = Total Beds - Daily Census = \(45 - 32 = \textbf{13 beds}\)

(d) Average Length of Stay (ALOS)

Total Discharges & Deaths = \(2,238 + 98 = 2,336\)

ALOS = \(\frac{\text{Total Inpatient Days}}{\text{Total Discharges & Deaths}}\)

\(= \frac{11,680}{2,336} = \textbf{5.0 days}\)

(e) Turnover Per Bed (TOB)

TOB = \(\frac{\text{Total Discharges & Deaths}}{\text{Average Bed Count}}\)

\(= \frac{2,336}{45} = \textbf{51.9 patients per bed}\)

47.
Calculate the geometric mean for the following data: 10, 12, 12, 13, 15, 19. (5 marks)
Show Answer

Answer:

Geometric Mean (GM) formula: \( GM = \sqrt[n]{x_1 \cdot x_2 \cdot \dots \cdot x_n} \)

\(n = 6\)

Product = \(10 \times 12 \times 12 \times 13 \times 15 \times 19\)

\(= 10 \times 144 \times 195 \times 19\)

\(= 5,335,200\)

\(GM = \sqrt[6]{5,335,200}\)

Using logs: \(\frac{\log(10) + \log(12) + \dots}{6}\)

\(\log(GM) \approx 1.123\)

\(GM \approx \textbf{13.22}\)

Answer:

Data Table with Calculations:

Class Midpoint (x) Frequency (f) fx Cum. Freq (cf)

(a) Mean Height:

\(\text{Mean} = \frac{\Sigma fx}{\Sigma f} = \frac{8520}{50} = \textbf{170.4 cm}\)

(b) Median Height:

Location of Median = \(N/2 = 50/2 = 25^{th}\) observation.

The 25th observation lies in the class 170-174.

  • Lower Boundary (L) = 169.5
  • Cumulative frequency before median class (cf) = 21
  • Frequency of median class (f) = 10
  • Class Width (w) = 5

Median Formula: \( \text{Median} = L + \left(\frac{\frac{N}{2} - cf}{f}\right) \times w \)

\(= 169.5 + \left(\frac{25 - 21}{10}\right) \times 5\)

\(= 169.5 + \left(\frac{4}{10}\right) \times 5\)

\(= 169.5 + 2 = \textbf{171.5 cm}\)

(c) Estimate Mode (Graphical Method):

To estimate the mode graphically, a Histogram is required.

  1. Identify the modal class (highest frequency): 175-179 (Frequency 11).
  2. Draw the bar for the modal class and the adjacent bars (170-174 and 180-184).
  3. Draw a diagonal line from the top left corner of the modal bar to the top left corner of the succeeding bar (180-184).
  4. Draw a diagonal line from the top right corner of the modal bar to the top right corner of the preceding bar (170-174).
  5. Drop a perpendicular line from the intersection of these diagonals to the x-axis. This point is the mode.

By calculation check: \( \text{Mode} = L + \left(\frac{f_1 - f_0}{2f_1 - f_0 - f_2}\right) \times w \)

\( L=174.5, f_1=11, f_0=10, f_2=5 \)

\( \text{Mode} = 174.5 + \left(\frac{11 - 10}{22 - 10 - 5}\right) \times 5 \)

\( = 174.5 + \left(\frac{1}{7}\right) \times 5 \approx 174.5 + 0.71 = \textbf{175.21 cm} \)
