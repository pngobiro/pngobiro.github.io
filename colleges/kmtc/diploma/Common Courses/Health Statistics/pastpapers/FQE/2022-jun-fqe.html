42.
Estimate the population mean at 95% and 99% confidence level where the sample data is: mean = 950 kg, s = 15 kg and n = 25
Show Answer

Answer:

Standard Error (SE) = \( s / \sqrt{n} = 15 / \sqrt{25} = 3 \).

Degrees of freedom = \( n-1 = 24 \).

95% Confidence Interval:

\( t_{0.025, 24} \approx 2.064 \)

\( CI = 950 \pm (2.064 \times 3) = 950 \pm 6.192 \)

Range: 943.81 to 956.19 kg

99% Confidence Interval:

\( t_{0.005, 24} \approx 2.797 \)

\( CI = 950 \pm (2.797 \times 3) = 950 \pm 8.391 \)

Range: 941.61 to 958.39 kg

43.
A die having 4 red and 2 yellow faces is thrown twice. For each red face showing, the thrower is paid ksh 90 and for each yellow face ksh 270 is paid. What is the throwers expectation?
Show Answer

Answer:

Probability of Red \( P(R) = 4/6 = 2/3 \). Payoff = 90.

Probability of Yellow \( P(Y) = 2/6 = 1/3 \). Payoff = 270.

Expected Value for one throw:

\( E(X) = (2/3 \times 90) + (1/3 \times 270) = 60 + 90 = 150 \).

Since the die is thrown twice, the total expectation is:

\( 150 \times 2 = \textbf{Ksh 300} \).

44.
What is the probability of having 2 girls and 1 boy in a three-child family if the probability of having a boy is 0.5?
Show Answer

Answer:

Using Binomial Distribution: \( n=3 \), \( p=0.5 \) (girl), \( q=0.5 \) (boy).

We want \( k=2 \) girls.

\( P(X=2) = \binom{n}{k} p^k q^{n-k} \)

\( P(2G, 1B) = \binom{3}{2} (0.5)^2 (0.5)^1 \)

\( = 3 \times 0.25 \times 0.5 = 3 \times 0.125 = \textbf{0.375} \) (or 3/8).

Compute the median of the following set of data:
Minutes LateFrequency
0-411
4-813
8-127

16-204

Answer:

Median position = \( N/2 = 44/2 = 22^{nd} \) value.

Cumulative Frequencies: 11, 24 (11+13)...

The 22nd value falls in the 4-8 class.

  • \( L = 4 \) (Lower boundary)
  • \( cf = 11 \) (Cum freq before)

\( = 4 + \left(\frac{22 - 11}{13}\right) \times 4 \)

\( = 4 + \left(\frac{11}{13}\right) \times 4 \approx 4 + 3.38 = \textbf{7.38 minutes} \)

48.
In the year 2004, Hospital B recorded the following data: 280 allocated beds, 24,800 Inpatient [Days], deaths and discharges 3248. 120 beds were closed for four months with effect from 1st September 2004 to 31st December 2004. On 1st January 2005 they were opened. Calculate: a) Daily average occupancy b) Average available beds.
Show Answer

Answer:

Note: 2004 is a leap year (366 days).

(a) Daily Average Occupancy

\( = \frac{\text{Total Inpatient Service Days}}{\text{Days in Year}} \)

\( = \frac{24,800}{366} \approx \textbf{67.76 patients} \)

(b) Average Available Beds

Period 1 (Jan 1 - Aug 31): 244 days × 280 beds = 68,320 bed-days.

Period 2 (Sep 1 - Dec 31): 122 days × (280 - 120 = 160 beds) = 19,520 bed-days.

Total Available Bed Days = 68,320 + 19,520 = 87,840.

Average Available Beds = \( \frac{87,840}{366} \approx \textbf{240 beds} \).

(a) Define the following terms as used in correlation:

  1. Correlation
  2. Coefficient
  3. Spurious correlation
  4. Covariance
  5. Rank correlation

(b) The finance division of a large company is investigating its procedures for the selection of new accountancy trainees. Answer:

(a) Definitions:

  1. Correlation: A statistical measure that indicates the extent to which two or more variables fluctuate together.
  2. Coefficient: A numerical measure that quantifies the strength and direction of the relationship (e.g., Pearson's r).
  3. Spurious Correlation: A relationship between two variables that appears causal but is actually due to a third unseen factor (confounding variable).
  4. Covariance: A measure of how much two random variables change together.
  5. Rank Correlation: A method of correlation (like Spearman's) that uses the rankings of data rather than raw values, useful for non-linear relationships.

(b) Calculations (Spearman's Rank Correlation):

Formula: \( R = 1 - \frac{6 \sum d^2}{n(n^2 - 1)} \) where \(n=8\).
