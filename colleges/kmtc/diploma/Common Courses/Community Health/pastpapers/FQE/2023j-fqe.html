Section 1: Multiple Choice Questions

40 Marks
1.
You are invited to give a health talk on child weaning. Which of the following would form part of your information?
A) It is advisable to mix weaning foods with high energy concentration into the basic staple food
B) Your children should be fed more than four times a day
C) Weaning mixtures should be avoided
D) Breast feeding should be stopped during weaning to avoid food interactions
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A

Weaning foods (complementary foods) should be energy and nutrient-dense to meet the growing infant's needs. Mixing staples (like porridge) with milk, oil, or groundnut paste is a recommended practice to increase energy density.

2.
A Clinical Officer who does blood pressure screening and related health education is conducting activities in the level of:
A) Primary prevention
B) Secondary prevention
C) Tertiary prevention
D) Focused prevention
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B

Secondary prevention involves early diagnosis and prompt treatment to prevent disease progression. Screening (like checking BP) identifies asymptomatic individuals with risk factors or early disease.

3.
The habitual presence of a disease or infections agent with a given geographical area is:
A) Epidemic
B) Pandemic
C) Sporadic
D) Endemic
Show Answer

Correct Answer: D

Endemic refers to the constant presence of a disease or infectious agent within a given geographic area or population group.

4.
The health care activities that are representing primary prevention include:
A) Getting immunized and practicing safe sex
B) Quitting smoking
C) Avoiding alcohol and using drugs
D) Starting an exercise and height reduction program
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A

Primary prevention aims to prevent the onset of disease. Immunization (preventing infection) and safe sex (preventing HIV/STIs) are classic primary prevention strategies.

5.
Determine the correct response regarding health education:
A) Helps people attain their health through the nurses' role efforts
B) Should not be flexible
C) Is a fast and mushroom like process
D) Is a slow and continuous process
Show Answer

Correct Answer: D

Health education aims to change behaviors and attitudes, which is inherently a slow and continuous process requiring reinforcement over time.

6.
For primary health care to improve health, people must first have:
A) Access to medical services
B) Their basic needs met
C) A health promotion programme
D) Electronic medical records
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B

PHC recognizes that health is determined by socio-economic factors. Meeting basic needs (food, shelter, clean water, sanitation) is a prerequisite for improving community health.

7.
Understanding of health determination is essential for:
A) Primary health care interventions and assessment
B) Effective programs to enable people to maintain good health
C) Organization of health services in regions or cities
D) Acute care service provision
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B

Understanding the social determinants of health allows for the design of effective programs that address the root causes of ill health, enabling people to maintain good health.

8.
Choose the vitamin deficiency commonly found in Chronic alcoholics.
A) Vitamin B1
B) Vitamin B2
C) Vitamin B12
D) Vitamin B6
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A

Chronic alcoholism leads to Thiamine (Vitamin B1) deficiency, which can cause Wernicke-Korsakoff syndrome.

9.
Determine the vitamin with anticancer properties.
A) Vitamin A
B) Vitamin B12
C) Vitamin B17
D) Vitamin C
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A

Vitamin A (and C and E) are antioxidants. Retinoids (Vitamin A derivatives) play a role in cell differentiation and have been studied for anticancer properties. (Note: "Vitamin B17" / Laetrile is not a recognized vitamin and its anticancer claims are scientifically disproven).

10.
Which one of the following is a result of high exposure of radiation?
A) Loss of white blood cells
B) Hair loss
C) Tooth decay
D) Leg fractures
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A

High exposure to radiation affects rapidly dividing cells. Loss of white blood cells (leukopenia) is a common acute effect due to bone marrow suppression.

11.
Chose water-washed disease in the following responses.
A) Scabies
B) Cholera
C) Typhoid
D) Malaria
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A

Water-washed diseases are caused by the lack of adequate water for personal hygiene. Scabies and Trachoma are classic examples. Cholera and Typhoid are water-borne (fecal-oral via water).

12.
Select borne water borne disease.
A) Cholera trachoma, schistosomiasis and yellow fever
B) Cholera, typhoid, amoebic and bacillary dysentery
C) Filariasis schistosomiasis, scabies and trachoma
D) Scabies trachoma, schistosomiasis and yellow fever
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B

Water-borne diseases are transmitted by ingesting contaminated water. Cholera, Typhoid, Amoebic and Bacillary Dysentery fit this classification.

13.
The most appropriate age for implementation of primordial measures for prevention of cervical cancer is:
A) Below 9 year
B) 9-14 years
C) Over 30 years
D) 25-45 years
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B

Primordial prevention involves preventing risk factors. For cervical cancer, HPV vaccination is key. The WHO recommends HPV vaccination for girls aged 9-14 years before sexual debut.

14.
Determine the correct response that implies communicable diseases.
A) Host who can transmit the pathogens before showing symptoms are described as convalescent carriers
B) Host who can transmit the pathogen beyond the period of illness are described as incubating carriers
C) The line between the onset and end of communicability is described as the latent period
D) The period of communicability is the state where the host is infectious and shedding pathogens into the environment
Show Answer

Correct Answer: D

The period of communicability is the time during which an infectious agent may be transferred from an infected person to another. (A describes incubating carriers, B describes convalescent/chronic carriers).

15.
Select the appropriate characteristic of non-communicable disease;
A) Contagious origin of limited latency
B) Long latency and aetiology
C) Short latency with functional impairment
D) Sudden onset and curability
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B

Non-communicable diseases (NCDs) typically have a long latency period (long time between exposure to risk factors and disease onset) and complex aetiology.

16.
Categorize zoonotic diseases from responses below.
A) Covid-19, rabies, trachoma and scabies
B) Rabies, plague, salmonellosis and brucellosis
C) Scabies, pediculosis, chicken pox and herpes
D) Trachoma, rabies, plague and Leishmaniosis
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B

Zoonotic diseases are transmitted from animals to humans. Rabies (dogs), Plague (rats/fleas), Salmonellosis (poultry/animals), and Brucellosis (cattle/goats) are all zoonoses.

17.
Mechanisms of disease transmission in communicable disease include;
A) Biological droplet and airborne
B) Droplet airborne and water borne
C) Droplet, trans placental and physical contact
D) Vector borne, mechanical transfer and vehicle borne
Show Answer

Correct Answer: D

This option covers indirect transmission mechanisms: Vector-borne (biological), Mechanical (e.g., houseflies), and Vehicle-borne (fomites, water, food). (Note: All options list valid mechanisms, but D is a distinct grouping of indirect methods).

18.
The major cardiovascular disease is:
A) cerebrovascular accident
B) Hypertension ischemia
C) Arthrosclerosis
D) Rheumatic heart disease
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B

Hypertension is a major risk factor and the most common cardiovascular condition globally. Ischemic heart disease is the leading cause of death.

19.
Determine advantage of the population strategy in disease prevention.
A) Behavioral appropriateness
B) Heath care provider motivation
C) Limited effect
D) Motivation of subjects
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A

The population strategy aims to shift the distribution of risk factors in the whole population. It is often behaviorally appropriate because it establishes new social norms (e.g., smoking bans, seatbelt laws).

20.
Select factors that influence the severity of post-crash outcomes in road traffic accidents in Kenya.
A) Inappropriate pre-hospital care, delayed rescue and demographic factors such as age and sex
B) Inappropriate pre-hospital care, delayed rescue and inappropriate care at the emergency centers at the hospital
C) Inappropriate pre-hospital care, delayed rescue and presence of road side objects
D) Inappropriate pre-hospital care, fatigue and road designs
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B

Post-crash outcomes depend heavily on the trauma care system: Pre-hospital care (ambulance, first aid), rescue time, and the quality of emergency care at the hospital.

21.
Choose the correct statement regarding hospital waste management.
A) Clinical waste such as used gloves are stored in yellow jerry cans
B) Clinical waster such as used needles are stored in black line bags

Section 2: Short Answer Questions

40 Marks
41.
Determine factors that influence development of a disease
Show Answer

Answer:

Disease development depends on the interaction of the Epidemiological Triad:

  1. Agent: The pathogen (virus, bacteria), chemical, or physical force.
  2. Host: The individual's genetics, age, immune status, and lifestyle.
  3. Environment: External factors like climate, sanitation, housing, and vectors.
42.
Compile characteristics of non-communicable diseases.
Show Answer

Answer:

  • Non-infectious: Not transmitted from person to person.
  • Chronic course: Long duration and slow progression.
  • Complex aetiology: Multiple risk factors (genetic + environmental).
  • Long latency period: Long time between exposure and symptom onset.
  • Functional impairment: Often lead to disability.
  • Incurability: Often managed rather than cured.
43.
What are the 5 factors that determine health care delivery
Show Answer

Answer:

  1. Availability: Adequate health facilities and resources.
  2. Accessibility: Physical reach (distance/transport).
  3. Affordability: Cost of services relative to income.
  4. Acceptability: Cultural appropriateness and patient satisfaction.
  5. Adequacy/Quality: Standard of care provided.
44.
Using relevant examples construct the concept of breaking the chain of infection.
Show Answer

Answer:

  • Reservoir: Treatment of TB patients (cure reservoir).
  • Portal of Exit: Safe stool disposal in latrines (Cholera).
  • Transmission: Washing hands (fecal-oral), Sleeping under nets (Malaria vector).
  • Portal of Entry: Wearing masks (COVID-19/TB).
  • Susceptible Host: Vaccination (Measles).

48.
Elaborate on five (5) causes of food spoilage you would advise in your talk.
Show Answer

Answer:

  1. Microorganisms: Bacteria, yeast, and molds causing fermentation or putrefaction.
  2. Enzymes: Natural enzymes in food causing ripening and eventual decay (autolysis).
  3. Insects/Pests: Weevils, rats, cockroaches contaminating food.
  4. Physical changes: Bruising, drying out, or moisture absorption.
  5. Chemical reactions: Oxidation (rancidity of fats) or interaction with containers.

Section 3: Long Answer Question

20 Marks
49.
You are the team leader of a sub-county disease surveillance team in sub county Y. It has been brought to the attention of the sub-county health management team of an outbreak of cases of diarrhoea and vomiting of sudden onset from one of the rural health facilities. The diarrhoea is rice water type. Vomiting is watery and non-projectile. Design an approach you would use to handle this situation.
Show Answer

Answer:

This scenario presents a classic Cholera Outbreak (Rice water stool). The management approach involves:

1. Immediate Verification & Notification:

  • Deploy a Rapid Response Team (RRT) to the facility.
  • Verify the diagnosis clinically and collect stool (rectal swabs) for lab confirmation.
  • Notify County and National authorities immediately (Notifiable disease).

2. Case Management:

  • Establish a Cholera Treatment Centre (CTC) to isolate patients.
  • Rehydration: Primary goal. ORS for mild cases, IV Ringer's Lactate for severe dehydration.
  • Antibiotics: Administer Doxycycline or Ciprofloxacin for severe cases to reduce shedding.
  • Infection Prevention Control (IPC) within the facility (chlorine disinfection).

3. Epidemiological Investigation:

  • Establish a Case Definition.
  • Conduct Active Case Search in the community.
  • Map cases to identify the source (e.g., contaminated well, funeral feast).
  • Collect water samples for testing.

4. Prevention & Control Measures:

  • Water Treatment: Distribute chlorine tablets (Aqua tabs), chlorinate wells, promote boiling.
  • Sanitation: Ensure proper use of latrines, safe disposal of cholera stools (disinfection).
  • Food Safety: Ban food hawking in affected areas, public education on food hygiene.
  • Health Education: Mobilize CHVs to teach handwashing and hygiene.
  • Safe Burials: Supervise burials of cholera victims.

5. Surveillance & Monitoring:

  • Daily line listing of cases and deaths.
  • Monitor the epidemic curve to assess control effectiveness.
