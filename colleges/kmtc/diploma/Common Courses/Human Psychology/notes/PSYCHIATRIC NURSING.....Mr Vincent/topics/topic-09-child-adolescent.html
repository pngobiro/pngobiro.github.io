üß© Autism

A disorder characterized by impairment in communication skills, or the presence of stereotyped behavior, interests and activities with associated impairment in social interactions. Usually diagnosed at age 2 and is more common among boys.

Common Signs and Symptoms

  • Resist normal teaching methods
  • Silly laughing or giggling
  • Echolalia (repetition of words)
  • Acts as deaf
  • No fear of danger
  • Insensitive to pain
  • Crying tantrums
  • Loves to spin objects
  • Resists change in routine
  • No eye contact
  • Attachment to inanimate objects
üí°

Management Strategies

  • Tantrums/Risk for injury: Involves head-banging; place a helmet on the head.
  • Communication: Use short sentences when talking to the child.
  • Routines: Provide consistency.

üß† Mental Retardation (Intellectual Disability)

It is not a mental illness; the main problem is inadequate mental functioning. Essential feature is below-average functioning (IQ less than 70) accompanied by significant limitations in adaptive functioning.

Levels of Mental Retardation

Level IQ Range Functional Capability
Mild 50/55 to 70 Educable
Moderate 35/40 to 50/55 Trainable
Severe 20/25 to 35/40 Needs Close Supervision
Profound Below 20/25 Needs Custodial Care

Nursing Care Principles

  • Help parents accept the diagnosis.
  • Consider the developmental/functional age, not the chronological age.
  • Repetition, Role Modeling, and Restructuring the Environment are key.
  • Teach one thing at a time; demonstrate whenever possible.
  • Make the child as independent as possible.

‚ö° Attention Deficit Hyperactivity Disorder (ADHD)

A disorder characterized by Inattentiveness, Over-activity, and Impulsiveness. Occurs before the age of 7 and is more common among boys.

Inattentive Behaviors

  • Misses details/careless mistakes
  • Difficulty sustaining attention
  • Does not seem to listen
  • Difficulty organizing tasks
  • Loses necessary things
  • Easily distracted

Hyperactive/Impulsive Behaviors

  • Fidgets, leaves seat
  • Runs or climbs excessively
  • Always on the go; driven
  • Talks excessively
  • Blurts out answers/Interrupts
  • Can't wait for turn
üíä

Psychopharmacology for ADHD

Stimulant drugs are commonly used:

  • Methylphenidate (Ritalin) ‚Äì drug of choice
  • Dextroamphetamine (Dexedrine)
  • Amphetamine (Adderall)

Nursing Care Priorities

  • Safety: Priority diagnosis is RISK FOR INJURY. Provide close supervision.
  • Structure: Establish a structured daily routine/schedule. Minimize changes.
  • Communication: Get child's full attention. Break complex tasks into small steps. Simplify instructions.
  • Environment: Manage environment (e.g., quiet place for tasks) to reduce distractions.
