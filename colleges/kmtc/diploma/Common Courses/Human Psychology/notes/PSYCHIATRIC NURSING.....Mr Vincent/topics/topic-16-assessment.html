üé§ Interview Guidelines

History taking should include a thorough psychological assessment followed by a Mental Status Exam (MSE).

üí°

Setting the Scene

  • Maintain privacy for the client.
  • Establish rapport with the client.
  • Introduce yourself and give the patient an estimate of the time the interview will take.
  • If the patient is anxious, CALM him/her.
üõ°Ô∏è

Safety Precaution

Make sure you and the patient are seated near the door. This ensures an exit path is available if the patient becomes agitated.

02

üìã Components of Assessment

A complete psychiatric assessment consists of four main pillars:

  1. Psychiatric History
  2. Mental State Examination (MSE)
  3. Physical Examination
  4. Formulation
03

üìú Psychiatric History

A comprehensive history includes the following elements:

  • Identifying information
  • Presenting complaint
  • History of presenting complaint
  • Past psychiatric history
  • Current medication
  • Past medical history
  • Family history
  • Alcohol and substance use
  • Social circumstances
  • Forensic history
  • Personal history
  • Premorbid personality
04

üß† Mental State Examination (MSE)

The MSE is a snapshot of the patient's current psychological functioning.

Domain Assessment Focus
General Appearance Grooming, dress, hygiene, posture.
Behaviour Psychomotor function, restlessness, agitation, retardation.
Rapport Quality of interaction with the interviewer.
Speech Rate, tone, volume, quantity.
Mood & Affect Subjective feeling (mood) and observed emotional expression (affect).
Anxiety Presence of anxiety or obsessive-compulsive phenomena.
Perception Hallucinations, illusions.
Thinking Content (delusions) and Form (flight of ideas, etc.).
Cognition Orientation, memory, attention, concentration.
Insight Awareness of illness and need for treatment.
Risk Assessment Self-harm, self-neglect, risk to others, children, and property.
05

üß© Formulation

The formulation summarizes the case and guides treatment. It includes:

  • Description of the patient
  • Differential diagnosis
  • Aetiology (Predisposing, Precipitating, Perpetuating factors)
  • Management plan
  • Prognosis
