üìñ Overview & Etiology

Definition

Personality is defined as an ingrained, enduring pattern of behaving and relating to self, others, and the environment; personality includes perceptions, attitudes, and emotions. These behaviors are consistent across a broad range of situations and do not change easily.

Personality Disorders are personality styles that are rigid and maladaptive, causing significant personal distress and impair social functioning. These are diagnosed when personality traits become inflexible and maladaptive.

Etiological Factors

  • Genetic Factors: Due to inherited traits
  • Temperamental Factors: Due to emotional climate at home
  • Biological Factors: Due to imbalance in hormones and neurotransmitters
  • Psychoanalytic Factors: Due to fixation at certain psychosexual stage of development

Categories (Clusters)

Cluster A

Individuals whose behavior appears odd or eccentric.

  • Paranoid
  • Schizoid
  • Schizotypal
Cluster B

Individuals who appear dramatic, emotional, or erratic.

  • Antisocial
  • Borderline
  • Histrionic
  • Narcissistic
Cluster C

Individuals who appear anxious or fearful.

  • Avoidant
  • Dependent
  • Obsessive-Compulsive

üëΩ Cluster A: Odd or Eccentric

‚ñ∂ Paranoid Personality Disorder

Symptoms/Characteristics:

  • Mistrust and suspicion of others.
  • Guarded or hypervigilant and generally appear alert to any impending danger.
  • Restricted affect; labile mood, quickly changing from quietly suspicious to angry or hostile.
  • Uses the defense mechanism of projection (blaming others for their own difficulties).

Nursing Interventions:

  • The nurse must be serious and straightforward with patient.
  • Involve the client in treatment planning (gives them a sense of control).
‚ñ∂ Schizoid Personality Disorder

Symptoms/Characteristics:

  • Detached from social relationships.
  • Display a constricted affect (aloof, indifferent, emotionally cold).
  • Pervasive lack of desire for involvement with others; involve themselves more with things than people.

Nursing Interventions:

  • Focus on improved functioning in the community.
  • Assist client to obtain essential services.
‚ñ∂ Schizotypal Personality Disorder

Symptoms/Characteristics:

  • Social and interpersonal deficits marked by acute discomfort with close relationships.
  • Cognitive or perceptual distortions.
  • Possesses eccentric behavior.

Nursing Interventions:

  • Development of self-care skills (hygiene and grooming routine).
  • Provide social skills training.
03

üé≠ Cluster B: Dramatic, Emotional, Erratic

‚ñ∂ Antisocial Personality Disorder

Symptoms/Characteristics:

  • Violation of the rights of others; Lack of remorse.
  • Shallow emotions, lying, rationalization of own behavior.
  • Poor judgment, impulsivity, irritability, aggressiveness.

Nursing Interventions:

  • Promote responsible behavior; remind of expectations.
  • Consistent adherence to rules and treatment plan.
  • Confront the patient.
‚ñ∂ Borderline Personality Disorder

Symptoms/Characteristics:

  • Fear of abandonment; Unstable and intense relationships.
  • Unstable self-image; Impulsivity or recklessness.
  • Recurrent self-mutilating behavior or suicidal threats.
  • Chronic feelings of emptiness; Labile mood.
üìù

Nursing Interventions

  • Promote safety: Seriously consider suicidal ideation. Encourage a no self-harm contract (a promise to the self to be safe).
  • Cope with emotions: Help identify feelings. Use journaling to gain awareness.
  • Cognitive Restructuring: Decatastrophizing; Stop & positive self talk.
‚ñ∂ Histrionic Personality Disorder

Symptoms/Characteristics:

  • Excessive emotionality and attention-seeking.
  • Overly concerned with appearance; flirtatious behavior.
  • Rapid shifts in mood (may seem phony).
  • Uncomfortable when not the center of attention.

Nursing Interventions:

  • Give feedback about social interactions.
  • Teach social skills and role-play.
  • Encourage assertive communication ("I" statements).
‚ñ∂ Narcissistic Personality Disorder

Symptoms/Characteristics:

  • Pervasive pattern of grandiosity, need for admiration, and lack of empathy.
  • Arrogant attitude; view problems as the fault of others.
  • Underlying fragile self-esteem; hypersensitive to criticism.

Nursing Interventions:

  • Use self-awareness skills to avoid anger/frustration.
  • Do not internalize criticism.
  • Goal is to gain cooperation with treatment.
04

üò® Cluster C: Anxious or Fearful

‚ñ∂ Avoidant Personality Disorder

Symptoms/Characteristics:

  • Social discomfort and reticence; low self-esteem.
  • Hypersensitivity to negative evaluation.
  • Fear rejection, criticism, shame or disapproval.

Nursing Interventions:

  • Require support and reassurance.
  • Explore positive self-aspects.
  • Practice self-affirmations.
‚ñ∂ Dependent Personality Disorder

Symptoms/Characteristics:

  • Excessive need to be taken care of; submissive/clinging behavior.
  • Fears of separation; lacks self-confidence.
  • Need excessive reassurance.

Nursing Interventions:

  • Foster autonomy and self-reliance.
  • Assist in daily functioning (planning, budgeting) but encourage independence.
  • Teach problem-solving skills.
‚ñ∂ Obsessive-Compulsive Personality Disorder

Symptoms/Characteristics:

  • Preoccupation with perfectionism, control, and orderliness.
  • Inflexible, formal, serious.
  • Problems with decision-making due to checking details.
  • Cannot tolerate lack of control.

Nursing Interventions:

  • Help view decision-making from a different perspective.
  • Cognitive restructuring (decatastrophizing).
  • Encourage risk-taking (e.g., letting someone else plan an activity).

Points to Consider

  • Talk to colleagues about feelings of frustration to deal with emotional responses (manage countertransference).
  • Clear, frequent communication with other health care providers helps diminish manipulation.
  • Do not take undue flattery or harsh criticism personally.
  • Set realistic goals; behavior change is slow.
