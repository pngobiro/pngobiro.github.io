üß¨ Organic Mental Disorders

Organic mental disorders are defined as a mental or behavioral abnormality related to the dysfunction of the brain. The two primary categories discussed here are Delirium and Alzheimer's Disease (Dementia).

02

‚ö° Delirium

A disturbance in consciousness and a change in cognition that develops over a short period of time. If identified early, it is usually reversible.

üö©

Clinical Hallmarks

  • Decreased attention span.
  • On and off confusion.
  • Disturbance of consciousness.
  • Altered diurnal patterns.

Common Causes

üíä Medications

üå´Ô∏è Hypoxia

üç¨ Hypoglycemia

üå°Ô∏è Hyperthermia

üç∫ Alcohol withdrawal

ü¶† Infections

Nursing Management

  1. Identify and Eliminate the Cause: This is the primary intervention.
  2. Physical Safety: Protect patient from injury (use low beds, guard rails). Maintain a hazard-free environment.
  3. Environment: Maintain a well-lit environment to prevent misperception. Remove stimuli that cause misperception.
  4. Cognitive Support: Reorient the patient.

üë¥ Alzheimer's Disease (Dementia)

A chronic, progressive condition that is the major cause of degenerative dementia seen in the elderly. The main pathology is the presence of senile plaques that destroys neurons leading to decreased acetylcholine.

The 4 A's of Alzheimer's

üó£Ô∏è Aphasia

Loss of language ability.

ü§≤ Apraxia

Loss of purposeful movement without loss of muscle power or coordination.

üëÅÔ∏è Agnosia

Loss of sensory ability to recognize objects.

üß† Amnesia

Mnemonic disturbances or memory loss.

Predisposing Factors

  • Genetics: 10% to 20% runs in the family.
  • Biological: Aluminum toxicity, Vitamin B12 deficiency, metabolic abnormalities.
  • Other: Viral, Down's syndrome connection, Immune system defect.

üè• Nursing Care for Alzheimer's

‚ñ∂ Safety & Environment
  • Promote normal motor behavior.
  • Living areas must be well lit; leave furniture in the same place.
  • Install safety bars near toilets/showers.
  • Structure environment to support cognition (clocks, orientation boards).
‚ñ∂ Self-Care & Daily Living
  • Allow client to do as much as possible unassisted.
  • Remind client about grooming and hygiene.
  • Maintain toileting routine to promote elimination.
  • Promote optimal nutritional status.
‚ñ∂ Sleep & Rest
  • Allow sleepless clients to wander in a confined area until tired.
  • Ensure room is lighted and without shadows.
‚ñ∂ Communication & Cognition
  • Call client by name, approach in clear view.
  • Give simple, non-demanding directions.
  • Do not argue about verbal discrepancies; redirect to familiar/pleasurable areas.
  • Do not quiz the client.
  • Gently orient the client.
