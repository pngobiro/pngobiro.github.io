üìâ The Concept of Anxiety

Anxiety is a stage of uneasiness or discomfort experienced to varying degrees (terror/dread). It is frequently coupled with doubts, fears, and obsessions. According to Hildegard Peplau, it is an "initial response to a psychic threat."

Hildegard Peplau's Four Levels of Anxiety

Level Characteristics
Mild Anxiety A positive state of heightened awareness.
Moderate Anxiety Focus on immediate task only.
Severe Anxiety Involves feelings of dread or terror.
Panic Anxiety Involves loss of rational thought, delusions, hallucinations, and complete physical immobility and muteness.

Ego Defense Mechanisms

The self or ego uses defense mechanisms to protect the self and cope with basic drives or emotionally painful thoughts. Most operate at the unconscious level of awareness.

Principles of Nursing Care

  • Calm the patient.
  • Administer medications.
  • Listen to the patient's concerns.
  • Minimize environmental stimuli.

Priority Nursing Diagnoses: Ineffective individual coping; Anxiety.

üìã Anxiety Disorders Overview

These are emotional illnesses characterized by fear, autonomic nervous system symptoms, and avoidance behavior. While manifestations vary, anxiety is the key feature of each.

Types of Anxiety Disorders

  • Agoraphobia
  • Panic Disorder
  • Specific Phobia
  • Social Phobia
  • Obsessive-Compulsive Disorder (OCD)
  • Generalized Anxiety Disorder (GAD)
  • Acute Stress Disorder
  • Post-Traumatic Stress Disorder (PTSD)
03

üò® Phobias & Panic Disorders

‚ñ∂ Agoraphobia

Anxiety about or avoidance of places or situations from which escape might be difficult or help might be unavailable. Essentially, a fear of being alone in public places.

Symptoms: Avoids being outside alone, traveling in vehicles, impaired ability to work, difficulty meeting daily responsibilities (e.g., grocery shopping).

Management: Anti-anxiety medications, Social skills training (ask questions, give compliments, maintain eye contact).

‚ñ∂ Panic Disorder

Recurrent, unexpected panic attacks that cause constant concern. A panic attack is a sudden onset of intense apprehension, fearfulness, or terror associated with feelings of impending doom.

Symptoms (Episodes last 15-30 mins):

  • Palpitations, Sweating, Trembling
  • Shortness of breath, Choking sensation
  • Chest pain, Nausea
  • Derealization or Depersonalization
  • Fear of dying or going crazy

Management: Anti-anxiety meds, Relaxation/Deep breathing, CBT (Positive Reframing, Decatastrophizing).

‚ñ∂ Specific Phobia

Significant anxiety provoked by a specific feared object or situation, often leading to avoidance behavior.

Management: Anti-anxiety medications, Systematic Desensitization.

‚ñ∂ Social Phobia

Anxiety provoked by certain types of social or performance situations.

Symptoms: Fear of embarrassment or inability to perform; belief that others are judging him or her negatively.

Management: Anti-anxiety medications, Social skills training.

üîÑ Obsessive-Compulsive Disorder (OCD)

Involves obsessions (thoughts, impulses, or images) that cause marked anxiety and/or compulsions (repetitive behaviors or mental acts) that attempt to neutralize anxiety.

Obsessions (Thoughts) Compulsions (Behaviors)
Fear of dirt and germs Excessive hand washing
Fear of burglary or robbery Repeated checking of door and window locks
Worries about discarding something important Counting and recounting of objects in everyday life
Worries that things must be symmetrical or matching Excessive straightening, ordering, or arranging of things

Management

  • Anti-anxiety medications
  • Response prevention: Delaying or avoiding performance of the rituals.

üò´ Generalized Anxiety & Stress Disorders

Generalized Anxiety Disorder (GAD)

Characterized by at least six months of persistent and excessive worry/anxiety that interferes with life.

Symptoms: Restlessness, fatigue, difficulty concentrating, irritability, muscle tension, sleep disturbance.

Management: Anti-anxiety/Anti-depressant medications, Psychotherapy.

Acute Stress Disorder

Development of anxiety and dissociative symptoms within 1 month of exposure to an extremely traumatic stressor. Lasts 2 days to 4 weeks.

Post-Traumatic Stress Disorder (PTSD)

Re-experiencing of an extremely traumatic event, avoidance of stimuli, numbing, and arousal. Begins within 3 months to years after event.

Symptoms

  • Re-experiencing: Flashbacks, nightmares.
  • Avoidance: Avoiding thoughts/places related to trauma.
  • Numbing: Detachment, restricted affect.

Management

  • Anti-anxiety/Anti-depressant meds
  • Group therapy
  • Cognitive Behavioral Therapy
