üìñ Definitions & Concepts

  • Drug: Any chemical agent that once taken in the body is capable of causing physiological and psychological changes.
  • Psychoactive substance: A chemical compound that produces emotional, cognitive or behavioral changes which may be pleasurable or desirable to the user, with adverse medical consequences and is socially unsanctioned.
  • Use: Taking psychoactive substance with no subsequent harm to health, social and occupational function.
  • Abuse: Pathological pattern of use where one experiences loss of control and begins to suffer health, social and occupational effects.

Tolerance

The need for more of the drug in order to achieve a similar effect realized before at a lower dose.

Dependence

Compulsion to take the drug on a continuous basis in order to feel its effect and to further avoid the discomfort of its absence.

Intoxication

Clinically significant maladaptive behavioural or psychological changes due to the effect of the substance on the CNS developing shortly after use.

Withdrawal

Development of a substance specific syndrome due to the cessation (or reduction) of substance use that has been heavy and prolonged.

üå± Etiological Factors

Involves an interaction of three main factors:

1

Agent (Substance)

Substances have euphoric and calming effects, leading to repeated use, tolerance, and avoidance of withdrawal discomfort.

2

Host (Individual)

  • Genetic factors: Predisposition if parents/siblings are dependent.
  • Family drug use: Children copy behavior of elders.
  • Peer pressure: Conforming to group norms.
  • Mental state: Emotional attitude (negative/positive) regarding use.
3

Environment

Availability/supply, poverty, unemployment, dysfunctional families, migration, and rapid urbanization.

üç∫ Alcohol

The most widely abused substance globally with serious health and social effects.

Alcohol Intoxication

Signs: Slurred speech, incoordination, unsteady gait, nystagmus, impairment in attention/memory, stupor, coma.

Behavioral changes: Inappropriate sexual/aggressive behavior, mood lability, impaired judgment.

Chronic Effects

  • Digestive: Esophagitis, cancer, ulcers, liver cirrhosis, pancreatitis.
  • CVS: Hypertension, cardiomyopathy.
  • Reproductive: Loss of libido, erectile dysfunction, fetal damage.
  • Neuropsychiatric: Wernicke‚Äôs encephalopathy, Korsakoff‚Äôs psychosis, alcoholic dementia.

Withdrawal Syndromes

Standard Withdrawal

Develops within hours/days of cessation.

  • Autonomic hyperactivity (pulse > 100, sweating)
  • Tremor (shakes)
  • Insomnia, Nausea/Vomiting
  • Transient hallucinations
  • Anxiety, Agitation, Seizures

Delirium Tremens (DTs)

Serious condition manifesting 3-4 days after cessation.

  • Clouding of consciousness
  • Disorientation
  • Profound confusion
  • Vivid hallucinations
  • Worse at night

Diagnosis Tools

CAGE Questionnaire:

  1. Have you ever tried to Cut down your drinking?
  2. Do you get Annoyed when people talk about your drinking?
  3. Do you ever feel Guilty about your drinking?
  4. Do you ever take an Eye-opener (early morning drink)?

üíä Other Substances

‚ñ∂ Cannabis Sativa (Bhang)

Forms: Smoked (dry leaves), Boiled (tea), Chewed.

Signs of Intoxication: Conjunctival injection (red eyes), increased appetite, dry mouth, tachycardia within 2 hours.

Effects: Euphoria, slowed time sensation, relaxation, impaired coordination. Chronic use: Lung disease, amotivational syndrome.

‚ñ∂ Catha Edulis (Khat/Miraa)

Effects: Euphoria, suppresses appetite/hunger, alertness. Used for relaxation or suppressing fatigue.

Health Issues: Oral-dental complications, gastritis, constipation, spermatorrhoea, erectile dysfunction, psychosis.

‚ñ∂ Amphetamines

Intoxication: Tachycardia, pupillary dilatation, elevated BP, perspiration, nausea, agitation, confusion.

Withdrawal: Dysphoric mood, fatigue, vivid unpleasant dreams, increased appetite, psychomotor retardation.

‚ñ∂ Heroin (Opiates)

Intoxication: Pupillary constriction, drowsiness/coma, slurred speech, impairment of attention.

Withdrawal: Dysphoric mood, nausea/vomiting, muscle aches, lacrimation/rhinorrhea, pupillary dilatation, sweating, diarrhea, yawning, fever, insomnia.

‚ñ∂ Cocaine

Intoxication: Tachycardia, pupillary dilatation, elevated BP, perspiration, nausea, weight loss, agitation, chest pain.

Withdrawal: Fatigue, vivid unpleasant dreams, increased appetite, retardation.

‚ñ∂ Solvents (Inhalants)

Glue, petrol, varnish. Sniffed from cloth or container.

Intoxication: Dizziness, nystagmus, incoordination, slurred speech, lethargy, tremor, blurred vision, stupor.

‚ñ∂ Nicotine

Withdrawal: Dysphoric mood, insomnia, irritability, anxiety, difficulty concentrating, restlessness, decreased heart rate, increased appetite.

ü©∫ Principles of Management

Management involves a multi-disciplinary programme comprising:

1. Detoxification

Helping individual stop using substance without painful effects (3-7 days).

Medical: Substitutes (Alcohol ‚Üí Chlordiazepoxide, Heroin ‚Üí Methadone).

2. Rehabilitation

Focuses on full recovery, social re-integration, skill development, and job training.

3. Relapse Prevention

Identifying triggers (people, places, feelings) and warning signs. Teaching life skills and coping mechanisms.

4. After Care

Follow up treatment and participation in self-help or support groups.
