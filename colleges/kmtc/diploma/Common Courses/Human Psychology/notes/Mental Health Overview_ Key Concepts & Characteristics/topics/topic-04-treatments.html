Side Effects of Antipsychotics

‚ñ∂ 1. Extrapyramidal Symptoms (EPS)
  • Neuroleptic induced parkinsonism: Rigidity, tremors, bradykinesia, stooped posture, drooling, akinesia, ataxia. Treated with anticholinergic drugs.
  • Acute dystonia: Slow sustained muscular spasm (neck, jaw, tongue). Can involve entire body (opisthotonus) or eyes (oculogyric crisis). Prevented with anticholinergics/antihistamines.
  • Akathisia: Subjective feeling of muscular discomfort, agitation, restlessness. Treated with propranolol, benzodiazepines.
  • Tardive dyskinesia: Delayed adverse effect. Abnormal, irregular choreoathetoid movements (chewing, sucking, grimacing).
  • Neuroleptic malignant syndrome: Rare but serious. Onset usually in first 10 days.
‚ñ∂ Other Side Effects

Autonomic: Dry mouth, constipation, cyclopedia, mydriasis, urinary retention, orthostatic hypotension, impotence.

Others: Seizures, sedation, agranulocytosis, sialorrhoea (increased salivation), weight gain, jaundice, dermatological effects.

üìã

Nurse's Responsibility

  • Instruct patient to take sips of water for dry mouth.
  • Advise slow position changes to minimize orthostatic hypotension (check BP before/after).
  • Differentiate between akathisia and agitation.
  • Observe for abnormal movements and seizure precautions.

MAOI Warning: Hypertensive Crisis

Characterized by severe headache, palpitation, neck stiffness, nausea, vomiting, increased BP, chest pain and collapse. Occurs after eating food containing tyramine (cheese, wine, beer, sour cream, liver, chocolate, bananas, avocados, soy sauce, beans).

C. Anxiolytics (Minor Tranquilizers)

Used for generalized anxiety disorder and acute anxiety states.

  • Benzodiazepines: Chlordiazepoxide (Librium), Diazepam (Valium), Clonazepam, Flurazepam, Nitrazepam.
  • Side Effects: Dizziness, headache, nervousness, insomnia, dependence/withdrawal if used >4 months.
  • Contraindications: Do not use with other CNS depressants (alcohol). Caution in elderly, depressed, or suicidal patients.

D. Antiparkinsonian Drugs

Given to counteract side effects of major tranquilizers (EPS).

  • Anticholinergics: Trihexyphenidyl (Artane), Benztropine (Cogentin), Biperiden.
  • Dopaminergic agents: Bromocriptine, Carbidopa/levodopa.

E. Mood Stabilizers

  • Lithium: 900-2100 mg/day (Acute mania).
  • Carbamazepine: 600-1800 mg/day.
  • Sodium Valproate: 15mg/kg/day.

üß† Psychological Treatments

I. Psychotherapy

Treatment involving communication between patient and therapist to modify/alleviate illness.

  • Psychodynamic: Insight into early relationships/experiences.
  • Humanistic: Centers on patient's view, unconditional positive regard.
  • Cognitive: Correcting distorted thinking patterns.
  • Supportive: Relieve distress without probing past (Ventilation, Environmental modification, Persuasion, Re-education, Reassurance).

II. Behaviour Therapy

Attempts to change behavior directly rather than correct basic causes. Based on Classical and Operant conditioning.

‚ñ∂ Techniques of Behaviour Therapy
  1. Systematic Desensitization: Relaxation training, Hierarchy construction, Desensitization. (For phobias).
  2. Flooding: Direct exposure to phobic stimulus without escape.
  3. Aversion Therapy: Pairing pleasant stimulus with unpleasant response (e.g., for alcohol).
  4. Positive Reinforcement: Rewarding desired behavior.
  5. Token Economy: Token rewards for desired targets.
  6. Modeling: Teaching by demonstration.
  7. Chaining: Breaking complex tasks into small steps.
  8. Extinction/Ignoring: Removal of attention following problem behavior.
  9. Punishment: Presented contingent upon undesirable response.
  10. Timeout: Removing patient from reward/setting.
  11. Restitution: Restoring disturbed situation to better state (e.g., cleaning up mess).

III. Cognitive Therapy Techniques

  • Stopping intrusive cognitions: Distraction.
  • Counterbalancing faulty cognitions: Replacing with other thoughts.
  • Altering cognitions: Identifying logical errors (faulty inference, overgeneralization, magnification).
  • Resolving problems directly: Defining problem, dividing into sub-problems, finding alternatives.

IV. Group Therapy

Carefully selected people meet in a group guided by a therapist. Optimal size 8-10 members.

Therapeutic Factors: Sharing experience, Support, Socialization, Imitation, Interpersonal learning.

V. Family Therapy

Focuses on the family unit rather than individual.

  • Individual family therapy: Each member has a therapist.
  • Conjoint family therapy: Nuclear family seen together.
  • Couples therapy: For marital difficulties.
  • Multiple family group therapy: 4-5 families meet.

VI. Other Therapies

Play Therapy

Uses play as natural mode of expression for children. Can be individual/group, free/controlled.

Psychodrama

Dramatic approach where patients become actors in life situation scenarios.

Creative Arts

Music Therapy: Facilitates emotional expression.
Dance Therapy: Uses movement for integration.

Relaxation

Progressive muscular relaxation, mental imagery, yoga, meditation, deep breathing exercises.

‚ö° Electroconvulsive Therapy (ECT)

Artificial induction of a grand mal seizure through application of electrical current to the brain.

Parameters

Voltage: 70‚Äì120 volts.
Duration: 0.7‚Äì1.5 seconds.
Seizure: Tonic (10-15s), Clonic (30-60s).

Types

Direct: No anaesthesia/relaxation.
Modified: With muscle relaxant & general anaesthesia.
Unilateral vs Bilateral.

Indications

  • Major depression with suicidal risk/stupor.
  • Severe catatonia.
  • Severe psychosis (Schizophrenia/Mania) unresponsive to drugs.

Contraindications

Contraindications

Absolute: Raised Intracranial Pressure (ICP).

Relative: Cerebral aneurysm, cerebral hemorrhage, brain tumor, acute MI, CHF, pneumonia, aortic aneurysm, retinal detachment.

Role of the Nurse

‚ñ∂ Pre-treatment Evaluation
  • Detailed history, informed consent.
  • NPO for 6-8 hours.
  • Withhold night doses of anticonvulsants/BZ.
  • Remove jewellery, dentures, contact lenses.
  • Empty bladder/bowel.
  • Administer atropine (0.6mg) 30 mins before.
‚ñ∂ Intra-procedure Care
  • Place patient supine.
  • Ensure patent airway/ventilator support.
  • Insert mouth gag.
  • Monitor seizure activity (cuff method).
  • Provide 100% oxygen.
‚ñ∂ Post-procedure Care
  • Monitor vital signs.
  • Assess for post-ictal confusion/restlessness.
  • Safety precautions (side-lying, suctioning, side rails).
  • Reorient patient.
