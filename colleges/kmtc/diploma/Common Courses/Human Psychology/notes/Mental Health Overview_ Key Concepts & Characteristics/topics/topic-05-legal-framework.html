üèõÔ∏è Overview & The Board

The Mental Health Act (Cap 248) is an act of parliament to amend and consolidate the law relating to the care of persons who are suffering from mental disorders, the custody of persons and management of their properties, and the management and control of mental hospitals.

‚ÑπÔ∏è

Legal Requirement

No person shall be received or detained for treatment in a mental hospital unless they have been received and detained under this act or under the criminal procedure code.

Kenyan Board of Mental Health

Established for the purposes of administering the Act. Members include:

  • Chairman (Director or Deputy Director of Medical Services).
  • Psychiatrist (medical practitioner).
  • Clinical Officer with mental health training.
  • Psychiatric Nurse with mental health experience.
  • Commissioner of Social Services (or nominee).
  • Director of Education (or nominee).
  • A representative from each province (resident).

Functions of the Board

Under the control of the Minister for Health:

  • Coordinate mental health activities in Kenya.
  • Advise the government on the state of mental health.
  • Inspect mental health hospitals (standards compliance).
  • Approve establishment of mental health hospitals.
  • Receive and investigate complaints from patients/relatives.
  • Initiate community or family-based care programs.

‚ñ∂ Part V: Voluntary Patients (Section 10)

Age 16+: Any person who desires to voluntarily submit to treatment makes a written application to the 'person in charge'.

üìù

Form MOH 613

Filled in duplicate by the patient indicating admission is at their own request.

Under 16: Parent or guardian makes the application.

üìù

Form MOH 637

Filled in duplicate and signed by the guardian or parent.

‚ñ∂ Part VI: Involuntary Patients (Section 14)

Patients incapable of expressing willingness or unwillingness to receive treatment.

üìù

Required Forms

  • MOH 614: Filled in duplicate by husband, wife, or relative (or another person explaining why a relative could not).
  • MOH 615: Filled by the doctor indicating why the patient can benefit from treatment (signed with qualifications).

Note: Forms must reach the hospital within 14 days of signing or they become invalid.

Duration: Not more than 6 months initially. Can be prolonged by 6 months (Total max 12 months).

‚ñ∂ Part VII: Emergency Admissions

A police officer, chief, or assistant chief can arrest any person found to be dangerous to themselves or others.

  • Take to mental hospital within 24 hours.
  • Review: Within 72 hours.
  • Form MOH 638: Filled by police or administrative officer.

üéñÔ∏è Special Admissions

Part VIII: Armed Forces (Section 17)

Admitted for observation if a medical officer of the armed forces certifies by letter that:

  • Member examined within 48 hours before issuing letter.
  • Member is a proper person for admission.

Duration: Initial 28 days. Extension requires recommendation by two practitioners (one psychiatrist). Discharge requires certification within 72 hours of exam.

‚ÑπÔ∏è

Note

If a member is admitted, they cease to be a member of the armed forces and become an involuntary patient (Part VI) from the date information is received.

Part IX: Patients from Foreign Countries (Section 18)

Requires written approval from the Mental Health Board. Application must indicate the person has been legally detained in the foreign country for a period not exceeding two months.

Procedure:

  • 'Person in charge' examines patient within 72 hours.
  • Report forwarded to the Board.
  • Detention not longer than 2 months unless Board approves.
04

üì§ Discharge & Transfer (Part X)

Section 21: 'Person in charge' may order discharge upon recommendation of the medical practitioner in charge of treatment (except for criminal procedure detainees).

Section 15: The Board may at any time order the discharge of any person received under Part V (Voluntary) or Part VI (Involuntary).

Section 22: Delivery to Relative/Friend

Relative/friend may apply to take custody. The 'person in charge' may order delivery upon terms and conditions, after consultation with the medical practitioner and the Board.
