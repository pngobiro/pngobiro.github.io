üìñ Overview & Definitions

Neuroses are less severe forms of psychiatric disorders where patients show either excessive or prolonged emotional reaction to any given stress. These disorders are not caused by organic disease of the brain and, however severe, do not involve hallucinations and delusions.

Anxiety Definition

Anxiety is a state of tension and apprehension with hyperactivity of the autonomic nervous system as a natural response to perceived threat. The frequency and intensity of anxiety responses are out of proportion when compared to situations that trigger them.

Components of Anxiety Disorders

  • Cognitive components: Subjective feeling of apprehension, a sense of impending danger, and a feeling of inability to cope.
  • Physiological response: Increased heart rate, BP, muscle tension, rapid breathing, dry mouth, diarrhea, and frequent urination.
  • Behavioural responses: Avoidance of certain situations and impaired task performance.
02

üò∞ Generalized Anxiety Disorder (GAD)

This is a disorder in which there is persistent and excessive feelings of anxiety not attached to any particular specific situations, but rather caused by a general tendency to worry excessively. Anxiety may last for months. There is a sense of impending disaster, though not specific.

Common Symptoms of GAD

Psychological: Fearful anticipation, irritability, sensitivity to noise, restlessness, poor concentration, worrying thoughts, and apprehension.

Physical Symptoms

Gastrointestinal

Dry mouth, difficulty in swallowing, epigastric discomfort, frequent or loose motions.

Respiratory

Constriction in the chest, difficulty inhaling, over-breathing.

Genitourinary

Frequency or urgent micturition, failure of erection, menstrual discomfort, amenorrhea.

Neuromuscular

Tremor, prickling sensations, tinnitus, dizziness, headache, aching muscles.

Sleep disturbances: Insomnia, night terrors.

Management

  • Pharmacotherapy: Antianxiety drugs, Antidepressants, SSRIs, Beta blockers.
  • Behavioural therapy: Teach patient to become aware of and consciously control body functions (Relaxation techniques e.g., Yoga, meditation).
  • Cognitive therapy: Teaching the patient how to restructure thoughts, view worries realistically, and avoid procrastination.
03

‚ö° Panic Disorder

Characterized by anxiety which is intermittent and unrelated to particular circumstances. It is a discrete period of intense fear or discomfort, in which four or more symptoms develop abruptly and reach a peak within 10 minutes.

Physical
  • Palpitations/pounding heart
  • Sweating
  • Trembling/shaking
  • Shortness of breath
  • Chest pain
Sensory/GI
  • Nausea/abdominal distress
  • Dizziness/faintness
  • Paresthesias (numbness)
  • Chills or hot flushes
Psychological
  • Derealization/Depersonalization
  • Fear of losing control
  • Fear of going crazy
  • Fear of dying

Management

  • Pharmacotherapy: Benzodiazepines, antidepressants, beta blockers.
  • Behavioural therapy: Relaxation techniques, deep breathing, positive verbalization, guided imagery.
  • Cognitive therapy: Identify triggers and restructure negative thoughts.
04

üï∑Ô∏è Phobic Anxiety Disorder

A phobia is an unreasonable fear of a specific object, activity, or situation. This fear is disproportionate to the circumstances, cannot be dealt with by reasoning, and leads to avoidance.

Types of Phobia

‚ñ∂ 1. Simple (Specific) Phobia

Irrational fear of a specific object or stimulus. Examples include:

  • Acrophobia: Fear of heights
  • Hematophobia: Fear of blood
  • Claustrophobia: Fear of closed spaces
  • Gamaphobia: Fear of marriage
  • Insectophobia: Fear of insects
  • Zoophobia: Fear of animals
  • Microphobia: Fear of germs
  • Brontophobia: Fear of thunder
  • Algophobia: Fear of pain
‚ñ∂ 2. Social Phobia

Irrational fear of performing activities in the presence of others or interacting with others. The patient fears embarrassment or humiliation.

Symptoms: Hyperventilation, sweating, blushing, palpitations, trembling, urinary urgency, anticipatory anxiety.

‚ñ∂ 3. Agoraphobia

Irrational fear of being in places away from familiar setting (home), in crowds, or situations difficult to leave. Leads to avoidance of public places and confinement to home.

Management

  • Pharmacotherapy: Benzodiazepines and antidepressants.
  • Behavioral therapy: Desensitization therapy (gradual reintroduction), Role playing, Guided imagery.
05

üîÑ Obsessive Compulsive Disorder (OCD)

Obsessions (Thoughts)

Repetitive, persistent thoughts, impulses, or images experienced as intrusive and inappropriate. They cause marked anxiety. The person attempts to ignore or suppress them but they are difficult to control.

Example: Thoughts of being infected by germs.

Compulsions (Acts)

Repetitive, persistent behavioural urges (hand-washing, checking, ordering) or mental acts (praying, counting) performed in response to an obsession. Failure to perform leads to anxiety.

Example: Washing and cleaning rituals.

Management

  • Pharmacotherapy: Antidepressants, anxiolytics.
  • Exposure and Response Prevention: E.g., Compulsive hand washers touch contaminated objects and refrain from washing.
  • Thought Stoppage: Patient concentrates on unwanted thought, then gives command "Stop" followed by diversion to pleasant thought.
  • Relaxation techniques.
06

üöë Post Traumatic Stress Disorder (PTSD)

A long-lasting anxiety response following a traumatic or catastrophic event (e.g., violent assault, kidnapping, war, natural disasters). Usually develops within 3-6 months of the event.

Major Symptoms

  • Severe anxiety and arousal on exposure to trauma cues.
  • Reliving the event ("flashbacks", dreams, images).
  • Nightmares and disturbed sleep.
  • Emotional numbness and avoidance of stimuli.
  • Being easily startled.
  • Amnesia about aspects of the event.
  • Survivor's guilt.

Management

  • Provide emotional support and establish trusting relationship.
  • Encourage recall of traumatic events and expression of grief.
  • Encourage move from physical to verbal expressions of anger.
  • Benzodiazepines may be needed to reduce anxiety.
