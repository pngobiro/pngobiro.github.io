üìö Terbutaline sulfate: Selective Beta2-Adrenergic Agonist

Terbutaline sulfate (Bricanyl, Monovent) is a selective beta-2 adrenergic receptor agonist, primarily used for its bronchodilator effects in respiratory conditions and as a tocolytic agent to delay preterm labor. Key features of terbutaline sulfate include:

  • Generic name: terbutaline sulfate.
  • Trade name: Bricanyl, Bricanyl, Monovent.
  • Pharmacologic class: Selective beta2-adrenergic receptor agonist, sympathomimetic.
  • Therapeutic class: Bronchodilator, tocolytic.
  • Pregnancy risk category B: Generally considered acceptable for use during pregnancy when indicated, particularly for tocolysis to manage preterm labor.
  • Action: Terbutaline works by selectively stimulating beta-2 (Œ≤2) adrenergic receptors, leading to:
    • Bronchial Smooth Muscle Relaxation: It relaxes bronchial smooth muscle, causing bronchodilation, which reduces airway resistance and improves airflow. This bronchodilator effect is particularly useful in asthma and COPD.
    • Inhibition of Hypersensitivity Mediators Release: It inhibits the release of hypersensitivity mediators, especially from mast cells. This action can reduce inflammation and allergic responses in the airways.

Indications and dosages

  1. Bronchospasm in reversible obstructive airway disease: To relieve bronchospasm associated with asthma, bronchitis, emphysema, and other conditions causing reversible airway obstruction.
    • Aerosol for inhalation: Metered-dose inhaler (MDI) delivering 0.18 ‚Äì 0.2 mg per inhalation (puff). Typically 1-2 inhalations every 4 ‚Äì 8 hours as needed for asthma symptoms.
    • Solution for inhalation: Solution for nebulization, 1.25 mg in 2 ‚Äì 5 ml of saline, administered via nebulizer over 5-15 minutes, often for more acute or severe exacerbations.
    • Oral syrup and tablets: Oral formulations are also available but less common, especially for acute relief. Oral syrup or tablets at doses of 2 ‚Äì 6 mg two to three times daily (tid ‚Äì qid) may be used for maintenance therapy or in children.
  2. Tocolytic in preterm labor: To delay premature labor by relaxing uterine smooth muscle contractions.
    • Subcutaneous injection or IV infusion: For tocolysis, administered via subcutaneous injection or continuous intravenous infusion in a hospital setting to manage preterm labor contractions. Dosage is carefully titrated and monitored.

üìö Contraindications and Precautions of Terbutaline sulfate

Contraindications

  • Hypersensitivity to drug or adrenergic amines: Patients with known allergy to terbutaline or other sympathomimetic amines.

Precautions

Use cautiously in patients with:

  • Cardiovascular disorders: Including hypertension, coronary artery disease, arrhythmias, as beta-agonists can exacerbate these conditions due to cardiovascular stimulation.
  • Diabetes mellitus: May cause hyperglycemia, requiring closer monitoring of blood glucose levels and adjustment of diabetic medications.
  • Seizure disorders: May lower seizure threshold, increasing risk of seizures in susceptible individuals.
  • Elderly patients: Elderly patients may be more sensitive to the adrenergic side effects.
  • Breastfeeding patients: Terbutaline passes into breast milk; use should be cautious due to potential effects on the infant.

üìö Side effects and Patient monitoring of Terbutaline sulfate

Side effects:

  • Central Nervous System (CNS): Tremors (most common), anxiety, nervousness, restlessness, insomnia, headache, dizziness, drowsiness. CNS stimulation is due to adrenergic effects.
  • Cardiovascular (CV): Palpitations, tachycardia (rapid heart rate), angina pain (chest pain), arrhythmias (irregular heartbeat), hypertension. Cardiovascular effects are due to Œ≤1-adrenergic receptor stimulation, although terbutaline is Œ≤2-selective, some Œ≤1 stimulation can occur, especially at higher doses.
  • Gastrointestinal (GI): Nausea, vomiting.
  • Other: Skin diaphoresis (sweating), flushing.

Patient monitoring

  • Monitor vital signs: Regularly monitor vital signs, particularly heart rate and blood pressure, for cardiovascular effects.
  • Assess neurologic status: Assess for CNS side effects such as tremors, anxiety, and dizziness, especially when initiating therapy or adjusting dosage.

  • Increased Intracellular cAMP: Activation of Œ≤2 receptors stimulates intracellular adenylate cyclase, increasing the production of cyclic adenosine monophosphate (cAMP).
  • Bronchial Smooth Muscle Relaxation: Increased cAMP levels lead to relaxation of bronchial smooth muscle. This relaxation causes bronchodilation, which widens the airways, reduces airway resistance, and improves airflow to and from the lungs.
  • Reduced Airway Resistance: By relaxing bronchial smooth muscles, terbutaline effectively reverses bronchospasm, a key component of asthma, thereby alleviating symptoms like wheezing, shortness of breath, and chest tightness.
