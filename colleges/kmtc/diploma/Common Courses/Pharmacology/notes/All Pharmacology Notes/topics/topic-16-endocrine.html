Classifications of Insulin:

1- Rapid- acting insulin.

Action:

  2. 2Increases synthesis of glycogen in the liver.
  3. 3Stimulates protein synthesis & lipogenesis.
  4. 4Inhibits lipolysis & release of free fatty acids from fat cells.
  5. 5Causes intracellular shifts of potassium.

N.B. : Since insulin is a protein , it is destroyed in the GIT thus it must be administered parenterally. It is metabolized mainly in the liver.

Adrenocorticosteroids and analogs

Action:

They are a group of natural hormones produced by the adrenal cortex. These hormones influence many metabolic pathways & all organ systems & are essential for survival.

Corticosteroids have the following effect:

  1. 1CHO metabolism : Deposition of glucose as glycogen in the liver & conversion of glycogen to glucose when needed.(Gluconeogenesis).
  2. 2Protein metabolism: The stimulation of protein loss from many organs.
  3. 3Fat metabolism: The deposition of fatty tissue in facial, abdominal & shoulder regions.
  4. 4Water & electrolyte balance: Alteration of glomerular filtration rate, increase sodium & fluid retention, also affect the excretion of potassium, calcium & phosphorus.
  5. 5Have anti-inflammatory effect: they decrease prostaglandin synthesis.
  6. 6The immunosuppresant effect : they decrease number of Tlymphocyte, monocytes, and eosinophils.

‚û¢Prolonged therapy may cause cushing-like syndrome & atrophy of the adrenal cortex & subsequent adrenocortical insuficiency.

N.B: steroid withdrawal syndrome may lead to: anorexia, nausea, vomiting, weight loss, headache myalgia & hypotension.

Side effects include: Edema, alkalosis, hypokalemia, hypertension, CHF muscle wasting, weakness, osteoporosis, nausea & vomiting. Headache, hypercholesterolemaea , hirsutism, amenorrhea, depression, increase heart rate, hyperglycemia, and peptic ulcer.

Posterior Pituitary Hormones

1-Methylergonovine Maleate:

Trade name: Methergine.

Class: Oxytocic agent.

Action: Is a synthetic agent stimulates the rate, tone & amplitude of uterine contractions.

Uses: Management & prevention of postpartum hemorrhage by producing firm cervical contractions & decrease uterine bleeding.

N.B.: use of this substance during labor may result in uterine tetany with rupture, cervical laceration, embolism of amniotic fluid & intracranial hemorrhage in infant.

2-Oxytocin:

Trade name: Pitocin

Class: oxytocic agent.

Action: It has uterine stimulant, vasopressive & antidiuretic properties. Mimics uterine contractions of normal labor.

Uses:

  • Antepartum induction or stimulation of labor.
  • Uterine inertia (hypotonic contractions).
  • To hasten uterine involution .

N.B.: Oxytocin should never be given I.V. undiluted in high concentration.

Diuretics

Action of diuretics: It increase the urinary output of water and sodium ‚Äïprevention or correction of edema‚Äñ through one of the following mechanisms:

  1. 1Increasing the glomerular filtration rate.
  2. 2Decreasing the rate at which sodium is reabsorbed from the glomerular filtrate by the renal tubules, therefore water is excreted along with sodium.
  3. 3Promoting the excretion of sodium & therefore water by the kidney.

Furosemide

Trade name : Fused , Lasix

Class: Loop diuretic.

Action: It inhibits the reabsorption of sodium and chloride in the ascending loop of Henle resulting in the excretion of sodium, chloride & to a lesser degree potassium & bicarbonate ions.

Uses: Edema associated with Congestive heart failure, Liver cirrhosis, Nephrotic syndrome, Acute pulmonary edema, Hypertension.

Side effects: Dehydration, hypovlemia. Hypokalemia ,hyperglycemia, Hyponatremia.

N.B.: Because the drug potentates the effects of muscle relaxants, it is recommended to discontinue oral medication 1 week before surgery & the I.V. 2 days before surgery

Thyroid & Antithyroid Drugs

Thyroid hormone preparations:

  • Levothyroxine sodium (T4) (synthroid)
  • Liothyronine sodium (T3) (cytomel)
  • Liotrix (Mixture of T4+ T3 ) (Euthroid, thyrolar).

Action of thyroid hormones:

  1. Essential for normal physical & mental development of the fetus & infants.
  2. Increase the BMR & blood sugar level, increase synthesis of fatty acids, and decrease plasma cholesterol & triglycerides.
  3. Increase H.R. & peripheral resistance.

Antithyroid Drugs

Anti-thyroid drugs include thiourcil derivatives & large doses of iodide.

Action: Inhibit partially or completely the production of thyroid hormones by the thyroid gland.

Examples:

  2. Propylthiouracil (PTU).

Oral Contraceptives

Estrogen- Progesterone Combinations: The most effective form of birth control available.

There are 3 types of combinations:

  1. 1Monophasic: contain the same amount of estrogen & progesterone in each tablet.
  2. 2Biphasic: contain the same amount of estrogen in each tablet but the progestin content is lower for the first 10 days of the cycle & higher in the last 11 days.
  3. 3Triphasic: The estrogen content may be the same or may vary throughout the medication cycle. The progestin content varies.

Other Reproductive & Miscellaneous Agents

medroxyprogesterone acetate

Trade name: Depo-Provera, Provera

Classification: hormone

Action: Suppresses ovulation, possibly by inhibiting pituitary gonadotropin secretion, thus preventing follicular maturation and causing endometrial thinning.

Rho(D) Immune Globulin

Trade name: RhoGAM

Indications: Administered to Rh-negative women who have been exposed to Rh-positive blood by: Delivering an Rho(D)-positive infant, Aborting an Rho(D)-positive fetus, etc.

Action: Prevents production of anti Rho(D) antibodies in Rho(D)-negative patients.

misoprostol

Therapeutic class: Antiulcerative, cytotec agent

Action: Reduces gastric acid secretion and increases gastric mucus and bicarbonate production. It works on uterus to induce uterine contractions.

dinoprostone (prostaglandin E2, PGE2)

class: Oxytocic, prostaglandin

Action: Initiates strong contractions of uterine smooth muscle by stimulating myometrium and promoting cervical softening, effacement, and dilation

Tetanus Toxoid

Class: Tetanus Prophylaxis/Vaccine

Action: Active immunization against tetanus.

Omega-3

Class: Fish Oil Supplements (Polyunsaturated Fatty Acid)

Uses: CAD, hypercholesterolemia, hypertriglyceridemia, type 2 DM, arthritis

terbutaline sulfate

Trade name: Bricanyl, Bricanyl , Monovent

Therapeutic class: Bronchodilator

Indications and dosages: Bronchospasm in reversible obstructive airway disease. Tocolytic in preterm labor
