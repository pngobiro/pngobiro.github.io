Section I: Multiple Choice Questions (20 Marks)

1.
The following clinical features denotes cardiac disease grade III?
A. Patient is comfortable even at rest
B. Patient is asymptomatic initially
C. Marked limitation of physical activity
D. Absolute limitation of physical activity
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C. Marked limitation of physical activity

Explanation: New York Heart Association (NYHA) Classification:
Class I: No limitation of physical activity.
Class II: Slight limitation of physical activity. Comfortable at rest.
Class III: Marked limitation of physical activity. Comfortable at rest, but less than ordinary activity causes fatigue, palpitation, or dyspnea.
Class IV: Unable to carry out any physical activity without discomfort. Symptoms of heart failure at rest.

2.
Wernickle’s encephalopathy and Mallory-Weiss syndrome are the complications of;
A. Placenta praevia
B. Hypertensive disorders
C. Vitamin B deficiency
D. Hyperemesis gravidarum
Show Answer

Correct Answer: D. Hyperemesis gravidarum

Explanation: Hyperemesis gravidarum (severe vomiting in pregnancy) causes nutritional deficiencies (Thiamine/B1 deficiency leading to Wernicke's encephalopathy) and mechanical stress on the esophagus due to retching (Mallory-Weiss tears).

3.
The diagnostic factor of Polyhydramnios, based on percussion is;
A. Muffled fetal heart sounds
B. Presence of a dull sound
C. Excessive fetal movements
D. Presence of a fluid thrill
Show Answer

Correct Answer: D. Presence of a fluid thrill

Explanation: In Polyhydramnios (excess amniotic fluid), a fluid thrill can often be elicited because the uterus is tense and fluid-filled. Muffled heart sounds are also a sign but are found on auscultation, not percussion.

4.
A Mother who needs Anti-D to prevent isoimmunization is;
A. RH positive mother who delivered RH negative fetus within 72 hours
B. RH Negative mother who delivered RH positive fetus within 72 hours
C. RH negative mother who delivered RH positive fetus after 72hours
D. RH negative mother who delivered RH negative fetus within 72 hours
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B. RH Negative mother who delivered RH positive fetus within 72 hours

Explanation: Anti-D immunoglobulin is given to an Rh-negative mother who has delivered an Rh-positive baby to prevent the formation of maternal antibodies against Rh-positive blood cells (isoimmunization). It must be given within 72 hours of delivery or sensitizing event.

5.
Excessive bleeding from the genital tract after the first 24 hours, but within puerperium period is referred to as
A. Primary postpartum haemorrhage
B. Tertiary postpartum haemorrhage
C. Secondary postpartum haemorrhage
D. Incidental postpartum haemorrhage
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C. Secondary postpartum haemorrhage

Explanation:
Primary PPH: Bleeding >500ml within the first 24 hours.
Secondary PPH: Excessive bleeding occurring between 24 hours and 6 weeks (puerperium) postpartum.

6.
On vaginal examination, the Nurse felt the chin. The presenting diameter in this presentation is;
A. Submentobregmatic
B. Suboccipitofrontal
C. Occipitalfrontal
D. Mentovertical
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A. Submentobregmatic

Explanation: Feeling the chin indicates a **Face Presentation**. In a fully extended face presentation, the engaging diameter is the **Submentobregmatic** (9.5 cm).

7.
A mother in labour has taken 3 hours in second stage. On vaginal examination, the midwife diagnosed obstructed labour and the following observations were made;
A. Dehydration, Hot and dry vagina, Diluted urine
B. Plenty amniotic fluid, Hot and dry vagina, meconium stained liquor
C. Hot and dry vagina and Oedema of the cervix
D. Caput succedaneum, Oedema of the cervix, plenty amniotic fluid
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C. Hot and dry vagina and Oedema of the cervix

Explanation: Signs of obstructed labour include: Maternal distress (dehydration, ketosis, concentrated urine), Bandl's ring, and vaginal signs: **Hot, dry vagina** (due to friction/infection), **Oedema of the cervix/vulva**, large caput succedaneum, and severe moulding. Liquor would be drained (not plenty) or meconium stained.

8.
Delivery of breech with an extended head is accomplished through;
A. Burns marshall maneuver
B. Lovset maneuver
C. Mauriceau-smellie-veit
D. Mc Robert’s maneuver
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C. Mauriceau-smellie-veit

Explanation:
Burns Marshall: For delivery of the trunk/lower body (allowing gravity to flex the head).
Lovset: For delivery of extended arms.
Mauriceau-Smellie-Veit: Specific maneuver to deliver the after-coming head (especially if extended) by flexing it manually.
McRobert's: For shoulder dystocia.

9.
State whether the following statements are TRUE (T) or FALSE (F):

A. Zavanelli maneuver is a non-invasive technique commonly used to manage shoulder dystocia.

Show Answer

Answer: False. Zavanelli is an invasive, last-resort maneuver involving replacement of the fetal head into the uterus followed by C-section. It is not "commonly used" or "non-invasive".

B. Post-partum haemorrhage is a common outcome of shoulder dystocia due to difficult delivery.

Show Answer

Answer: True. The manipulation required to resolve shoulder dystocia often causes genital tract trauma (lacerations) and uterine atony, leading to PPH.

10.
The earliest onset of cephalohaematoma is;
A. Six hours postnatally
B. Twelve hours postnatally
C. Eighteen hours postnatally
D. Immediately after birth
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B. Twelve hours postnatally

Explanation: Unlike Caput Succedaneum (present at birth), a Cephalohaematoma (bleeding under the periosteum) usually appears 12-24 hours after birth as the blood accumulates slowly. It increases in size over the first few days.

11.
The following is a characteristic of fetal post-maturity syndrome;
A. Closed eyes, unusually alert
B. Long, frail finger nails
C. Wrinkled, peeling skin
D. Excess vernix caseosa
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C. Wrinkled, peeling skin

Explanation: Post-term infants often exhibit the "old man" look: dry, cracked, peeling, wrinkled skin; long nails; absence of vernix caseosa and lanugo; and an alert, wide-eyed appearance (not closed eyes).

12.
Sign of intrauterine fetal death include
A. The uterus increases in the size
B. Brownish discharge per vagina
C. Decreased fetal heart sounds
D. Reduced Fetal movements
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B. Brownish discharge per vagina

Explanation:
A: Uterus size decreases or stops growing (fundal height < dates).
B: Dark brownish discharge is a classic sign.
C: Fetal heart sounds are ABSENT, not decreased.
D: Fetal movements stop completely (Cessation). Reduced movement is a symptom of distress, not necessarily death.

13.
A Mother delivers at 38 weeks and newborn scores 6/1, 5/5 and 4/10. The baby has chest indrawing, nasal flaring and blue extremities. The following diagnosis was made;
A. Mild Asphyxia
B. Moderate Asphyxia
C. Respiratory Distress Syndrome
D. Severe Asphyxia
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C. Respiratory Distress Syndrome

Explanation: The clinical picture of chest indrawing, nasal flaring, and cyanosis (blue extremities) specifically points to respiratory difficulty (RDS/HMD). While low APGAR scores (deteriorating from 6 to 4) indicate asphyxia/depression, the specific signs listed are the hallmark of Respiratory Distress Syndrome.

14.
Pathological jaundice in newborns appears
A. Within the 1st 34 hours of life
B. Within the 1st 24 hours of life
C. Within the 1st 14 hours of life
D. Within the 1st 4 hours of life
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B. Within the 1st 24 hours of life

Explanation: Jaundice appearing within the first 24 hours of life is always considered pathological (e.g., due to Rh/ABO incompatibility or sepsis) until proven otherwise.

15.
The mother with severe Eclampsia was managed with magnesium sulphate. A Nurse observed respiration less than 16b/m, reduced urine output and absence of Tendon reflex. Which drug can be used to manage these effects;
A. Calcium gluconate
B. Calcium chloride
C. Calcium sulphate
D. Calcium hydrochloride
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A. Calcium gluconate

Explanation: These are signs of Magnesium Sulphate toxicity (respiratory depression, oliguria, loss of reflexes). The specific antidote is 10% Calcium Gluconate IV.

16.
A 20-year primigravida mother is taken to postnatal ward 3 hours after being managed for post-partum Haemorrhage. You are assigned to provide primary nursing care for her, your priority nursing diagnosis is?
A. Acute pain related to uterine cramping
B. Risk of impaired parenting related to lack of experience as a mother
C. Risk of deficient fluid volume
D. Anxiety related parental role
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C. Risk of deficient fluid volume

Explanation: After managing PPH (significant blood loss), the immediate physiological priority is maintaining hemodynamic stability. The patient is at high risk for hypovolemia/deficient fluid volume due to the blood loss.

17.
Sixth-day into puerperium the mother’s lochia serosa is profuse and foul-smelling and has chills, on abdominal palpation there is tenderness. You suspect;
A. Puerperal mastitis
B. Puerperal infection
C. Poor hygiene
D. Sub-involution
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B. Puerperal infection

Explanation: Foul-smelling lochia, fever (chills), and abdominal/uterine tenderness are classic triad signs of Puerperal Sepsis (Endometritis/Genital tract infection).

18.
A 48 hour old infant who is being breastfed is diagnosed with physiological jaundice and phototherapy is prescribed. Which measure, taken by the nurse would enhance bilirubin excretion?
A. Keeping the infant slightly wrapped.
B. Nursing the infant in a quiet, dark room.
C. Providing the infant with additional fluids.
D. Encouraging the mother to temporarily stop breastfeeding.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C. Providing the infant with additional fluids.

Explanation: Bilirubin is excreted in stool and urine. Phototherapy can cause dehydration (insensible water loss). Adequate hydration (breastfeeding frequently or additional fluids if necessary) promotes excretion and prevents dehydration. The baby should NOT be wrapped (skin must be exposed to light) and breastfeeding should continue (unless contraindicated).

19.
When the membranes rupture at cervical dilatation of 8 centimetres, a vaginal examination performed should rule out:
A. Obstructed labour, cord prolapse & foetal compromise.
B. Cord prolapse, cord presentation & foetal distress.
C. Obstructed labour, cord prolapse & prolonged labour.
D. Maternal distress, prolonged labour & cord presentation.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B. Cord prolapse, cord presentation & foetal distress.

Explanation: The immediate risk upon rupture of membranes (especially if the head is not fully engaged) is Cord Prolapse. A VE is done to check for the cord (prolapse/presentation) and assess the fetal condition (distress due to compression).

20.
A mother is admitted in postnatal and has been diagnosed of deep venous thrombosis, which of the following management should she receive
A. Reassurance
B. Analgesics, then exercises
C. Ant-coagulants, restriction of movements
D. Application of crape bandages, warm compression
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C. Ant-coagulants, restriction of movements

Explanation: Standard management for acute DVT includes Anticoagulation (Heparin/Warfarin) to prevent clot extension/PE and Rest/restriction of movement initially to prevent the clot from dislodging. (Note: Early ambulation is now encouraged once anticoagulation is therapeutic, but acute phase often involves rest. Massage is contra-indicated).

Section II: Short Answer Questions (40 Marks)

21.
Draw and label a diagram indicating the regions of the Gynaecological pelvis. (6 marks)
Show Answer

Regions of the Pelvis:

  1. False Pelvis: Above the pelvic brim.
  2. True Pelvis: Below the pelvic brim, consisting of:
    • Pelvic Brim (Inlet): Bounded by the sacral promontory, alae of sacrum, sacroiliac joints, iliopectineal line, and upper border of pubic symphysis.
    • Pelvic Cavity: Circular canal between the inlet and outlet.
    • Pelvic Outlet: The lower boundary, consisting of the coccyx, ischial tuberosities, and pubic arch.

(Diagram should label: Iliac crest, Sacrum, Pelvic Brim/Inlet, Ischial spines, Pubic symphysis, Coccyx).

22.
State the four congenital heart conditions found in tetralogy of fallot. (4 marks)
Show Answer
  1. Ventricular Septal Defect (VSD): A hole between the right and left ventricles.
  2. Pulmonary Stenosis: Narrowing of the pulmonary valve/outflow tract.
  3. Overriding Aorta: The aorta is shifted to the right and lies directly above the VSD.
  4. Right Ventricular Hypertrophy: Thickening of the right ventricular wall due to pumping against high pressure.
23.
State five effects of malaria on pregnancy. (5 marks)
Show Answer
  • Maternal Anaemia: Severe hemolysis of red blood cells.
  • Spontaneous Abortion/Miscarriage: In early pregnancy.
  • Intrauterine Growth Restriction (IUGR): Leading to low birth weight.
  • Preterm Labour: Due to fever and placental infection.
  • Congenital Malaria: Transmission of parasites to the fetus.
  • Stillbirth: In severe cases.
24.
Outline five clinical manifestation of a low birth weight. (5 marks)
Show Answer
  1. Small Size: Weight less than 2,500g.
  2. Thin Skin: Skin may appear translucent or gelatinous (in preterms).
  3. Scanty Subcutaneous Fat: Looking wasted or thin.
  4. Large Head: Head appears disproportionately large compared to the body.
  5. Weak Reflexes: Poor sucking and swallowing reflexes.
  6. Lanugo: Excessive fine hair on the body (if preterm).
25.
Outline five health messages you will share on the care of engorged breasts. (5 marks)
Show Answer
  1. Frequent Feeding: Breastfeed frequently and on demand to empty the breasts.
  2. Warm Compresses: Apply warm cloths before feeding to stimulate milk flow (let-down reflex).
  3. Cold Compresses: Apply cold packs/cabbage leaves after feeding to reduce swelling and pain.
  4. Express Milk: Hand express a little milk before latching to soften the areola if too hard for the baby to grasp.
  5. Supportive Bra: Wear a well-fitting, supportive bra that is not too tight.
26.
Differentiate between placenta Abruptio and placenta praevia. (6 marks)
Show Answer
Feature Placenta Praevia Placenta Abruptio
Bleeding Painless, Bright Red, Recurrent. Painful, Dark Red (or concealed).

27.
Outline four (4) predisposing factors to preterm labour. (4 marks)
Show Answer
  1. Infections: Urinary tract infections (UTI), Bacterial Vaginosis, STIs.
  2. Uterine Overdistension: Multiple gestation (twins/triplets), Polyhydramnios.
  3. Cervical Incompetence: Weak cervix that opens too early.
  4. Maternal Conditions: Pre-eclampsia, Diabetes, severe anaemia.
  5. History: Previous history of preterm birth.
28.
Outline five (5) causes of foetal distress. (5 marks)
Show Answer
  1. Cord Compression: Prolapsed cord or nuchal cord reducing blood flow.
  2. Placental Insufficiency: Placenta praevia, abruptio placentae, or post-maturity.
  3. Maternal Hypoxia/Hypotension: Severe anaemia, seizures (eclampsia), or supine hypotension syndrome.
  4. Uterine Hyperstimulation: Excessive oxytocin use leading to frequent contractions without relaxation.
  5. Fetal Infection: Chorioamnionitis or sepsis.

Section III: Long Answer Questions (40 Marks)

29.
Miss Z. a multigravida is admitted to labour ward in first stage of labour. She gives a history of rupturing membranes on her way to the hospital. On vaginal examination, a soft pulsating mass is felt in front of the presenting part. Discuss specific management of Miss Z. from admission to delivery. (20 marks)
Show Answer

Diagnosis: Cord Prolapse (Pulsating mass = umbilical cord). This is an Obstetric Emergency.

Immediate Management (Relieving Pressure):

  • Call for Help: Shout for assistance immediately but do not leave the patient.
  • Positioning: Place Miss Z in Knee-Chest position or Trendelenburg position (hips elevated) to use gravity to pull the fetus away from the pelvis.
  • Manual Elevation: Insert a gloved hand into the vagina and gently push the presenting part (fetal head) UP and AWAY from the cord. DO NOT HANDLE THE CORD (to prevent vasospasm). Keep hand in place until C-section is underway.
  • Bladder Filling: If manual elevation is difficult or delay is expected, fill the bladder with 500ml saline via catheter to lift the presenting part.

Resuscitation & Monitoring:

  • Oxygen: Administer O2 to the mother (8-10L/min by mask) to improve fetal oxygenation.
  • Fetal Heart Rate: Monitor FHR continuously to assess distress.
  • Tocolysis: Administer a tocolytic (e.g., Terbutaline) if prescribed to stop contractions and relieve pressure.

Definitive Management:

  • Immediate Caesarean Section: Prepare for emergency C-section (Category 1). This is the standard delivery method for cord prolapse with a living fetus.
  • Consent & Prep: Obtain informed consent quickly, establish IV access, take blood for grouping/cross-match.
  • Transport: Transfer to theatre maintaining the relieving position/manual elevation.

Post-Procedure:

  • Monitor mother and baby closely in recovery.
  • Explain events to the mother (debriefing).
30.
Baby J has been born at 38 weeks of gestation with a birth weight of 2500g and been transferred to the new-born unit (NBU) for admission due deep jaundice involving palms and soles. Discuss management of the baby in the first 24 hours. (20 marks)
Show Answer

Diagnosis: Severe Pathological Jaundice (involving palms/soles implies high bilirubin levels >15mg/dL).

1. Assessment & Investigation:

  • History: Check maternal blood group (Rh/ABO incompatibility), family history of jaundice, birth trauma (cephalohaematoma).
  • Labs: Urgent Serum Bilirubin (Total/Direct), Blood Group & Coombs test, Full Blood Count (infection/hemolysis), Reticulocyte count.

2. Phototherapy (Primary Treatment):

  • Initiation: Start intensive phototherapy immediately.
  • Exposure: Expose maximum skin surface area (baby naked except for diaper).
  • Protection: Cover baby's eyes with opaque eye patches to prevent retinal damage. Cover genitals.
  • Distance: Ensure lights are at the correct distance (usually 30-45cm).
  • Turning: Turn the baby every 2 hours to expose different skin areas.

3. Hydration & Feeding:

  • Breastfeeding: Encourage frequent breastfeeding (every 2-3 hours) to promote bowel movements and bilirubin excretion.
  • Fluids: If dehydrated or bilirubin is very high, IV fluids may be needed to flush out bilirubin and prevent dehydration from phototherapy heat.

4. Monitoring:

  • Temperature: Monitor temp every 2-4 hours (risk of hyperthermia from lights or hypothermia if naked).
  • Hydration Status: Check skin turgor, sunken fontanelle, urine output.
  • Bilirubin Levels: Re-check serum bilirubin every 4-6 hours to assess effectiveness.

5. Exchange Transfusion (If indicated):

  • Prepare for exchange transfusion if bilirubin rises to toxic levels (risk of Kernicterus) or fails to drop with phototherapy.

6. Infection Control:

  • Observe strict asepsis. Administer antibiotics if sepsis is suspected as a cause.
