Section 1: Multiple Choice Questions (40 Marks)

1.
The aims of psychology include:
a) Maintenance of behavior
b) Camouflaging behavior
c) controlling behavior
d) Maintenance of behavior
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) controlling behavior

Explanation: The four main goals/aims of psychology are to Describe, Explain, Predict, and Control (or Influence) behavior.

2.
A person with avoidant personality disorder has pervasive pattern of:
a) Attention-seeking behaviour
b) Need for admiration
c) Social inhibition and inadequacy.
d) Disregard for social norms.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Social inhibition and inadequacy.

Explanation: Avoidant Personality Disorder is characterized by a long-standing pattern of feelings of inadequacy, extreme sensitivity to negative evaluation, and social inhibition.

Why others are wrong: A (Attention-seeking) is Histrionic. B (Need for admiration) is Narcissistic. D (Disregard for norms) is Antisocial.

3.
Early signs of increased intracranial pressure in an infant include:
a) Crying when held, distended scalp veins, coma.
b) High pitched cry, headache, and changes in feeding.
c) Bulging fontanelles, crying when held, increased occipital frontal circumference.
D) Increased occipital frontal circumference, drowsiness, coma.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Bulging fontanelles, crying when held, increased occipital frontal circumference.

Explanation: In infants with open fontanelles, raised ICP typically presents with a tense/bulging fontanelle, increasing head size (circumference), and irritability (crying when held/disturbed). Coma (in A and D) is a late sign.

4.
Feeding recommendation for a child 2 years and above is
a) Given enriched family foods 3 times a day with a least two snacks in between
b) Given enriched family foods 3 times a day avoid snacks in between
c) Given enriched family foods 2 times a day with a least three snacks in between
D) Given enriched family foods 3 times a day start introducing complementary feeds
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Given enriched family foods 3 times a day with a least two snacks in between

Explanation: For children aged 2 years and older, WHO/IMCI recommends 3 main meals of family foods and 2 nutritious snacks per day.

5.
After initiating treatment for a child with classification of pneumonia the guardian is counseled to
a) Give treatment and wait for return date
b) Come back to the facility immediately if the child become worse
c) Seek senior review at a higher facility
d) Reduce amount of clothes and give prescribed medication
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Come back to the facility immediately if the child become worse

Explanation: "When to return immediately" is a critical counseling component in IMCI. Parents must bring the child back if breathing becomes difficult, fast breathing develops, or the child cannot drink.

6.
Which disease is related with total energy depletion where individuals are alert, ravenous with very high appetite and very irritable
a) Kwashiorkor
b) Marasmus
c) Stunting
d) Underweight
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Marasmus

Explanation: Marasmus is caused by severe deficiency of nearly all nutrients (total calorie depletion). Children are typically wasted ("old man appearance") but remain alert and often have a voracious appetite (ravenous), unlike the apathy and poor appetite seen in Kwashiorkor.

7.
In nutritional assessment of children, acute malnutrition is indicated by,
a) Height for age and MUAC
b) Height for weight Z score and MUAC
c) Weight for age Z score and MUAC
d) Weight for height Z score and MUAC
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Weight for height Z score and MUAC

Explanation: Acute malnutrition (Wasting) is assessed by Low Weight-for-Height (WHZ < -2 SD) or Low Mid-Upper Arm Circumference (MUAC < 125mm). Height-for-age indicates Stunting (Chronic). Weight-for-age indicates Underweight.

8.
In order to identify and minimize trauma to a child, the nurse should
a) Avoid separation the child and mother, explain the procedure to the patient and caretaker, give child and care givers chance to express their feelings about procedure
b) Avoid separation the child and mother, explain the procedure to the patient and caretaker, avoid any procedure that is unpleasant to child
c) Separate the child and mother during painful procedure, explain the procedure to the patient and caretaker, give child and care givers chance to express their feelings about procedure
d) Separate the child and mother to reduce panic, explain the procedure to the patient and caretaker, give child and care givers chance to express their feelings about procedure
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Avoid separation the child and mother, explain the procedure to the patient and caretaker...

Explanation: Atraumatic care principles emphasize preventing family separation, promoting a sense of control (explanation), and minimizing bodily injury/pain. Options C and D (separating mother and child) increase trauma.

9.
The commonest type of hallucination in an alcoholic patient is:
a) Tactile
b) Visual
c) Gustatory
d) Olfactory
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Visual

Explanation: Visual hallucinations (e.g., seeing bugs, small animals) are the hallmark of alcohol withdrawal delirium (delirium tremens). In schizophrenia, auditory hallucinations are more common.

10.
Which of the following actions indicates a need for further health messages to parents of a child with congenital heart defect?
a) Counting the number of wet diapers to monitor fluid levels
b) Listening to pulse for one minute while giving digoxin at home
c) Protecting the child from people with upper respiratory infections
d) Waking up the child for feeding every 2 hours to help gain weight
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Waking up the child for feeding every 2 hours to help gain weight

Explanation: Children with CHD tire easily. While nutrition is important, they need uninterrupted rest to conserve energy and reduce cardiac workload. Frequent waking can cause fatigue. A, B, and C are correct care actions.

11.
In curriculum development, the must learn indicates:
a) Tasks that broaden the profession in a work set up.
b) Tasks the learner performs in an educational program
c) Tasks which the learner has to know and perform.
d) Tasks to be learnt in the syllabus
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Tasks which the learner has to know and perform.

Explanation: "Must learn" refers to the core competencies or essential skills that are mandatory for professional practice and safety.

12.
Which of the following statements is most accurate regarding audio-visual learners
a) Benefit from using charts, maps, notes, and flash cards when studying
b) Do not prefer to see information such as pictures, diagrams, cartoons, demonstrations
c) Learn best when intense visuals accompanied by lecture
d) Not easily distracted in lecture with no visual aids
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Learn best when intense visuals accompanied by lecture

Explanation: Audio-visual learners process information best when they can both see it (visuals, diagrams) and hear it (lecture/audio) simultaneously.

13.
In an objective, a description of the subject reference which could be knowledge, attitude or skills is
a) Action verb
b) Content reference
c) Performance criteria
d) Performance reference
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Content reference

Explanation: The content reference (or subject matter) specifies *what* is being learned (the K/S/A), while the action verb specifies the behavior.

14.
A neurotransmitter that mediates sleepiness and wakefulness is:
a) GABA
b) Dopamine
c) Epinephrine
d) Serotonin
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Serotonin

Explanation: Serotonin plays a key role in regulating sleep-wake cycles (and mood). It is a precursor to melatonin. GABA (A) is inhibitory/calming but Serotonin is the primary mediator of the cycle.

15.
In research, and in-depth investigation of an individual, group, institution or phenomena is called
a) Action research
b) Case study
c) Descriptive study
d) Retrospective study
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Case study

Explanation: A case study is a research methodology that involves an intensive, in-depth investigation of a single unit (person, group, or event).

16.
Attitudes are best learnt through:
a) role play, experience, role modeling
b) role modeling, experiments, lecturing
c) experience, experiments, lecturing
d) lecturing, demonstration, experiments
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) role play, experience, role modeling

Explanation: Attitudes (Affective domain) are best acquired through social learning methods like observing others (modeling), experiencing situations, and simulation/role play. Lecturing is poor for changing attitudes.

17.
The main difference between experimental and non-experimental studies in research is
a) The researcher is a passive observer in non-experimental study
b) The researcher manipulates the independent variable in non-experimental study
c) The researcher is an active agent in experimental study
d) The researcher manipulates the dependent variable in experimental study
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) The researcher is an active agent in experimental study

Explanation: In experimental research, the researcher actively manipulates the independent variable (intervention). In non-experimental research (A), the researcher observes without manipulation.

18.
In order to minimize a type II error in research the researcher should
a) Increase the sample size
b) Decrease the sample size
c) Avoid developing a null hypothesis
d) Decrease the level of significance
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Increase the sample size

Explanation: Type II error (False Negative) occurs when you fail to detect a real effect. Increasing sample size increases statistical Power (1 - beta), reducing the chance of Type II error.

19.
Which of the following is not a factor to consider when choosing a research design
a) Available time
b) Type of hypothesis
c) Statistical significance
d) Type of data
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Statistical significance

Explanation: Statistical significance is a result of data analysis, not a factor in choosing the *design* itself. Time, hypothesis type, and data type all influence the choice of design (e.g., qualitative vs quantitative).

20.
Which part of the research process helps the researcher to narrow the research topic and determine new approaches
a) Literature review
b) Methodology
c) Data analysis
d) Problem statement
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Literature review

Explanation: Reviewing existing literature helps the researcher see what has already been done, identify gaps, refine the topic, and find appropriate methods (approaches).

21.
An instructional objective is stated in terms of
a) Teacher performance
b) Learner performance
c) Institutional performance
d) Process performance
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Learner performance

Explanation: Objectives must be learner-centered, describing what the *student* will be able to do, not what the teacher does.

22.
In cognitive domain of learning, the learner is expected to gain,
a) Knowledge
b) Skills
c) Attitudes
d) Manual dexterity
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Knowledge

Explanation: Cognitive domain = Knowledge/Intellectual skills. Psychomotor = Skills/Manual dexterity. Affective = Attitudes.

23.
In order to overcome her/his own anxieties in dealing with the patient, the nurse should
a) Go through their record, Talk to the clinical supervisor or other nurses about their fears
b) Set her goals very clearly, Plan a long interaction with the patient
c) Go through their record, avoid talking to the clinical supervisor or other nurses about their fears
d) Avoid talking to the clinical supervisor or other nurses about her fears, plan a brief interaction with the patient
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Go through their record, Talk to the clinical supervisor or other nurses about their fears

Explanation: Self-awareness is key in psychiatric nursing. Discussing fears with a supervisor/mentor and being prepared (record review) helps manage anxiety effectively.

24.
A symptom where the patient has lengthy and tedious verbalizations and because of numerous details they never reach the intended point is referred to as
a) Tangentiality
b) Thought echo
c) Circumstantiality
d) Telepathy
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Circumstantiality

Explanation: Circumstantiality involves excessive detail but *eventually* reaching the point. Tangentiality (A) involves going off-topic and *never* reaching the point. The question says "never reach the intended point", which technically describes Tangentiality, but often in exam contexts, "lengthy/tedious details" cues Circumstantiality. However, strictly "never reach" = Tangentiality. Let's look at the options. A is valid. C is valid. Given the "numerous details" emphasis, C is often the intended answer in nursing exams, but if they *never* reach it, it's A. *Correction*: The definition provided "never reach the intended point" is the hallmark of Tangentiality. Circumstantiality takes a long time but gets there.

25.
Which of the following is a negative symptom in schizophrenia
a) Flat affect: separation from external world
b) Increase of behavior (increase motor movements)
c) Excessive or additional to normal thoughts, emotions, or behaviors
d) Disorganized thoughts and speech
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Flat affect: separation from external world

Explanation: Negative symptoms represent a loss of normal function (Flat affect, Avolition, Alogia). Positive symptoms add to normal function (Hallucinations, Delusions, Disorganized speech/behavior).

26.
In treatment of depression, tricyclic antidepressants
a) Block the reuptake of norepinephrine at the post-synaptic terminals
b) Block the reuptake of norepinephrine at the pre-synaptic terminals
c) Increase the reuptake of norepinephrine at the pre-synaptic terminals
d) Increase the reuptake of norepinephrine at the post-synaptic terminals
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Block the reuptake of norepinephrine at the pre-synaptic terminals

Explanation: TCAs work by inhibiting (blocking) the reuptake of neurotransmitters (serotonin and norepinephrine) into the pre-synaptic nerve terminal, thus increasing their concentration in the synapse.

27.
Motivation associated with activity that are their own reward is:
a) Automatic
b) Extrinsic
c) Intrinsic
d) Self
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Intrinsic

Explanation: Intrinsic motivation comes from within; the activity itself is enjoyable or rewarding. Extrinsic (B) is driven by external rewards (grades, money).

28.
In behaviorism, an attractive object or event supplied as a consequence of behavior is:
a) Participation
b) Motivation
c) Incentive
d) A reward
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) A reward

Explanation: In operant conditioning, a reward (positive reinforcer) increases the likelihood of a behavior being repeated. An incentive (C) is an object that *encourages* behavior before it happens.

29.
A goal to improve and to learn is
a) Mastery goal
b) Motivational goal
c) Incentive
d) Performance goal
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Mastery goal

Explanation: Mastery goals focus on acquiring new skills or improving competence. Performance goals (D) focus on demonstrating competence relative to others (winning/grades).

30.
A learner who prefers to see information such as pictures, diagrams, cartoons demonstrations is:
a) Visual learner
b) Auditory learner
c) Tactile learner
d) Artistic learner
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Visual learner

Explanation: Visual learners learn best by seeing (diagrams, pictures). Auditory (B) by hearing. Tactile (C) by touching/doing.

31.
Which of the following is not a nursing diagnosis for a patient who is suicidal
a) Risk for injury related to a recent suicide attempt and the verbalization, “next time I won’t fail”
b) Risk for injury to others related to stated desire to end it all and recent purchase of a handgun
c) Risk for violence: Self-directed related to multiple losses secondary to retirement
d) Hopelessness related to diagnosis of terminal cancer as evidence by the statement, “I’d rather be dead”
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Risk for injury to others related to stated desire to end it all and recent purchase of a handgun

Explanation: "Risk for injury to OTHERS" is a diagnosis for homicidal/aggressive patients, not specifically for a patient who is suicidal (self-harm). Suicidal intent focuses on self.

32.
In tertiary prevention of mental illness, the focus is on
a) Management of individuals with severe and persistent mental illness
b) Management of populations at risk
c) Dealing with stressful life events
d) Stress management

Correct Answer: a) Management of individuals with severe and persistent mental illness

33.
The fear of places or situations that might cause embarrassment is
a) Agoraphobia
b) Antisocial phobia
c) Claustrophobia
d) Homophobia

35.
Which of the following will help reduce bias in research?
a) Use of multiple people to code the data, not allowing participants review your results.
b) Use of single data source, Check for alternative explanations.

36.
Which type of bias would occur when samples under-represent certain people or group, and over-represent others
a) Recall bias
b) Selection bias
c) Observation bias
d) Hawthorne effect
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Selection bias

Explanation: Selection bias occurs when the sample chosen is not representative of the population intended to be analyzed (e.g., only interviewing people with landlines).

37.
In nephrotic syndrome, proteinuria can be explained to be as a result of
a) Changes in permeability of the glomerular membrane
b) Changes in protein metabolism

Correct Answer: a) Changes in permeability of the glomerular membrane

Explanation: Nephrotic syndrome is caused by damage to the glomeruli, increasing their permeability and allowing large proteins (albumin) to leak from the blood into the urine.

38.
Secondary or existing data may include which of the following
a) Questionnaire
b) Personal documents
c) Literature
d) Focus group notes
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Personal documents

Explanation: Secondary data is data collected by someone else for another purpose (e.g., census data, medical records, diaries/personal documents). Questionnaires and Focus Groups (A/D) are primary data collection methods.

39.
A child with disorientation of time, place and person is said to be
a) In a coma
b) In stupor
c) Lethargic
d) Disorientated
Show Answer

Explanation: By definition, lack of orientation to time, place, and person is disorientation/confusion.

40.
In children with poisoning:
a) Induced emesis is contraindicated in hydrocarbon poisoning
b) Induced emesis is contraindicated in all poisons
c) Gastric lavage is contraindicated in all poisons
d) Gastric lavage can be performed at home

Correct Answer: a) Induced emesis is contraindicated in hydrocarbon poisoning

Section 2: Short Answer Questions (40 Marks)

41.
State one (1) advantage and one (1) disadvantage of the following teaching method:
a) Simulation
b) Lecture (4 marks)
Show Answer

Answer:

  • a) Simulation:
    • Advantage: Allows practice of skills in a safe, risk-free environment without harming patients.
    • Disadvantage: Can be expensive to set up (equipment/mannequins) and time-consuming.
  • b) Lecture:
    • Advantage: Efficient for covering large amounts of content to large groups in short time.
    • Disadvantage: Passive learning; minimal student engagement and difficult to assess understanding in real-time.

  2. To avoid duplication of work that has already been done.
  3. To identify appropriate research methodologies and tools used in similar studies.
43.
State four (4) theoretical foundations of learning (4 marks)
Show Answer

Answer:

  1. Behaviorism: Learning as a change in observable behavior (stimulus-response).
  2. Cognitivism: Learning as an internal mental process (information processing).
  3. Constructivism: Learners build new knowledge upon previous experience.
  4. Humanism: Learning focused on self-actualization and personal growth.
  5. Social Learning Theory: Learning through observation and modeling.

48.
State two (2) methods of stimulus variation (2 marks)
Show Answer

Answer:

  1. Teacher Movement: Moving around the class rather than standing still.
  2. Gestures: Using hand and body movements to emphasize points.
  3. Change in Speech Pattern: Varying tone, pitch, and speed (pausing).
  4. Change in Interaction Style: Teacher-student, student-student interaction.
  5. Shifting Sensory Channels: Switching from listening (lecture) to seeing (visual aids).
49.
State five nursing care management of a patient with depression (5 marks)
Show Answer

Answer:

  1. Safety: Assess for suicide risk and maintain safe environment (1:1 observation if needed).
  2. Therapeutic Communication: Use silence, active listening, and open-ended questions to encourage expression of feelings.
  3. Self-Care: Assist with ADLs (hygiene, nutrition) if the patient lacks energy/motivation.
  4. Activity: Encourage participation in simple, non-competitive activities to build self-esteem.
  5. Medication Management: Administer antidepressants as prescribed and monitor for side effects.
50.
Outline four purposes of research. (4 marks)
Show Answer

Answer:

  1. Exploration: To investigate a new topic or phenomenon.
  2. Description: To accurately characterize a situation or population.
  3. Explanation: To understand "why" and "how" variables relate (causality).
  4. Prediction: To forecast future outcomes based on current data.
  5. Control: To manipulate variables to produce desired outcomes.
51.
Distinguish between qualitative and quantitative research. (2 marks)
Show Answer

Answer:

  • Quantitative Research: Deals with numbers and statistical analysis; focuses on testing hypotheses and generalizing findings (Objective).
  • Qualitative Research: Deals with words, meanings, and descriptions; focuses on exploring depth, experiences, and context (Subjective).

Section 3: Long Answer Question (20 Marks)

52.
Convulsions are common in children during infancy and second year of life.
a) Define infantile convulsions (1 marks)
b) State four (4) main causes (4 marks)
c) State four (4) complications of convulsions (4 marks)
d) Describe the specific nursing management of a child during convulsions (11 marks)
Show Answer

Answer Guide:

a) Definition (1 Mark)

Infantile convulsions (seizures) are involuntary muscular contractions and relaxations, often accompanied by loss of consciousness, occurring in infants due to abnormal electrical discharges in the brain.

b) Causes (4 Marks)

  1. Febrile Convulsions: High fever (malaria, pneumonia, otitis media).
  2. Infections: Meningitis, Cerebral Malaria, Encephalitis.
  3. Metabolic Disorders: Hypoglycemia, Hypocalcemia.
  4. Trauma: Birth asphyxia, head injury.
  5. Epilepsy: Idiopathic causes.

c) Complications (4 Marks)

  1. Aspiration Pneumonia: Inhaling secretions/vomit.
  2. Brain Damage/Hypoxia: Due to prolonged lack of oxygen (Status Epilepticus).
  3. Physical Injury: Fractures, tongue biting, head trauma from falls.
  4. Death: From asphyxia or underlying cause.

d) Nursing Management During Convulsions (11 Marks)

  • Safety First (Protect from Injury):
    • Do not restrain the child.
    • Move sharp/hard objects away.
    • Place on a soft surface (floor/bed with rails up).
  • Airway Management:
    • Turn child to the side (recovery position) to drain secretions and prevent aspiration.
    • Do not put anything in the mouth (no tongue depressors).
    • Suction secretions if possible/available.
    • Administer oxygen if cyanosed.
  • Pharmacological Management:
    • Administer Diazepam (Rectal or IV) as per protocol to stop the seizure.
    • Administer Phenobarbitone if seizures persist (loading dose).
  • Assessment/Monitoring:
    • Monitor vital signs (Temperature, Pulse, Respiration).
    • Check blood sugar (treat hypoglycemia if present).
    • Time the seizure duration.
  • Post-Ictal Care:
    • Allow child to rest/sleep.
    • Reassure parents.
    • Document the event (type, duration, interventions).
