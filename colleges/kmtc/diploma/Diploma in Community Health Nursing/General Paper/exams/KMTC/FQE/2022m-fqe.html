Section 1: Multiple Choice Questions (40 Marks)

1.
In assessing developmental milestones, a nurse should be concerned if the child does not hold head steady unsupported at
A) Birth
B) 2 months
C) 4 months
D) 2 weeks
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C) 4 months

Explanation: Infants typically achieve good head control by 3-4 months. Lack of head control (head lag) by 4 months is a developmental red flag indicating possible motor delay or hypotonia.

2.
In order to 'pass' the appetite test, the child 10-14.9 kgs should eat Ready to use therapeutic foods (RUTF)
A) 1 packet in 30 minutes
B) 2 packets in 1 hour
C) 1 packet in 1 hour
D) ½ packet in 30 minutes
Show Answer

Correct Answer: D) ½ packet in 30 minutes

Explanation: According to IMAM/WHO guidelines for the appetite test, a child weighing 10-14 kg should be able to consume at least half to three-quarters of a sachet of RUTF within 30 minutes to demonstrate they can be treated as an outpatient.

3.
A child with a Middle Upper Arm Circumference (MUAC) of 112 mm is
A) Suffering from severe acute malnutrition
B) Having a normal weight
C) Suffering from chronic malnutrition
D) Having a poor skin turgor
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A) Suffering from severe acute malnutrition

Explanation: A MUAC measurement of less than 115mm (11.5 cm) indicates Severe Acute Malnutrition (SAM) in children 6-59 months. 112mm is 11.2cm, falling into the red zone.

4.
In management of poisoning in children
A) Emesis is done as a standard procedure to reduce amount of poison ingested
B) Catharsis has immense benefits in improving the outcome
C) Excretion of certain substances can be hastened by urinary alkalinization or dialysis
D) Patient with Marked hypothermia or hyperthermia not responding to therapy should not be put on dialysis
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C) Excretion of certain substances can be hastened by urinary alkalinization or dialysis

Explanation: Urinary alkalinization is effective for salicylates and barbiturates. Emesis (A) is generally contraindicated due to aspiration risk. Catharsis (B) has limited proven benefit.

5.
In routine prevention of helminthiasis in children,
A) Avoid offering universal treatment to all individuals in an area of high endemicity, offering individual treatment based on intensity of current or past infection
B) Avoid offering treatment targeted to groups with high frequency of infection
C) Offering treatment targeted to groups with high frequency of infection, such as children attending primary school, Improving sanitary conditions and sewage facilities
D) Improving sanitary conditions and sewage facilities and using human feces as fertilizer, offering individual treatment based on intensity of current or past infection
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C) Offering treatment targeted to groups with high frequency of infection...

Explanation: WHO recommends periodic deworming (preventive chemotherapy) for high-risk groups like school-age children in endemic areas, combined with sanitation improvements.

6.
The part of the personality that is called the rational self is
A) Ego
B) Ego ideal
C) ID
D) Super ego
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A) Ego

Explanation: In Freud's theory, the Ego operates on the reality principle and acts as the rational decision-maker, mediating between the Id (impulses) and Superego (morality).

7.
In order to reduce stress for a child admitted in hospital
A) Encourage parental/caregiver presence, don't allow choices
B) Explain procedure to child, discourage parental/caregiver presence
C) Allow choices where possible, encourage parental/caregiver presence
D) Discourage choices, explain procedure to parent/caregiver
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C) Allow choices where possible, encourage parental/caregiver presence

Explanation: Giving children control (choices) and maintaining their support system (parents) are key principles of atraumatic care to reduce hospitalization anxiety.

8.
A person who collects a wallet on the road and decides to keep the money is operating on
A) Ego
B) Conscience
C) ID
D) Super ego
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C) ID

Explanation: The ID operates on the pleasure principle ("I want it now"), disregarding moral consequences. Keeping the money satisfies immediate desire without regard for the owner (Superego) or social rules.

9.
A child should turn towards the source of sound at
A) 1 month
B) Birth
C) 6 months
D) 2-3 months
Show Answer

Correct Answer: D) 2-3 months

Explanation: Infants typically start turning their head/eyes toward a sound source (localization) around 3-4 months. 2-3 months is the start of this developmental milestone.

10.
For children born of HIV positive mothers, Cotrimoxazole prophylaxis should be
A) Delayed until PCR test is done
B) Initiated immediately after birth
C) Initiated after antibody test results
D) Initiated at 6 weeks after delivery
Show Answer

Correct Answer: D) Initiated at 6 weeks after delivery

Explanation: WHO and Kenya guidelines recommend starting Cotrimoxazole preventive therapy at 6 weeks of age for all HIV-exposed infants until HIV infection is excluded.

11.
In the learning theorist that suggests that learning is for self-improvement is
A) Abraham Maslow
B) Ivan Pavlov
C) B. F. Skinner
D) Sigmund Freud
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A) Abraham Maslow

Explanation: Maslow's Humanistic theory focuses on self-actualization and learning as a means of personal growth and self-improvement.

12.
Tricyclic antidepressants include: -
A) Mellaril, Stelazine
B) Lithium carbonate, Haloperidol
C) Imipramine, Amitriptyline
D) Chlorpromazine, Clopixol
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C) Imipramine, Amitriptyline

Explanation: These are classic TCAs. Option A/D are antipsychotics (Phenothiazines). Option B lists a Mood stabilizer and an antipsychotic.

13.
Learning is encouraged in an environment that;
A) Encourages the cooperate rather than the personal nature of learning
B) Tolerates perfection
C) Puts emphasis on teacher evaluation
D) Recognizes the people's right to make mistakes
Show Answer

Correct Answer: D) Recognizes the people's right to make mistakes

Explanation: A safe learning environment allows learners to make mistakes and learn from them without fear, fostering experimentation and growth.

14.
When a teacher sets projects or problems, they are most likely;
A) Challenging the quiet members
B) Encouraging active participation
C) Motivating learning
D) Slowing down the pace of fast leaners
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C) Motivating learning

Explanation: Projects and problem-based learning are active strategies designed to motivate students by engaging them in relevant, real-world tasks. Option B is also true, but C is the overarching goal.

15.
In the psychopathology of mental illness an increase in dopamine levels will;
A) Cause increase in psychomotor activity
B) Cause emotional blunting
C) Cause psychomotor retardation
D) Cause decreased appetite and libido
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A) Cause increase in psychomotor activity

Explanation: High dopamine activity is associated with positive symptoms of schizophrenia (hallucinations, delusions) and mania, which includes increased psychomotor activity/agitation. Low dopamine (Parkinsonism) causes retardation (C).

16.
The purpose of evaluation in learning is to
A) Motivate the teacher
B) Enhance retention
C) Measure content coverage
D) Protect the learner
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C) Measure content coverage

Explanation: Evaluation determines the extent to which objectives have been achieved and content covered/mastered.

17.
A longitudinal study is a classification based on
A) Type of data collected
B) Aim of the study
C) Based on time
D) Based on respondents
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C) Based on time

Explanation: Research designs classified by time dimension are Cross-sectional (one point in time) and Longitudinal (over a period of time).

18.
A type of research that aims at building knowledge but not of immediate application by practitioners is
A) Action research
B) Basic research
C) Qualitative research
D) Quantitative research study
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B) Basic research

Explanation: Basic (or Pure) research is conducted to increase the base of knowledge for the sake of knowledge itself, without immediate practical application. Applied research seeks to solve immediate problems.

19.
A hypothesis that states that 'There is no difference in performance of Nursing Council Examination between students sitting a written examination and online examination' is an example of
A) Non-directional hypothesis
B) Null hypothesis
C) Associative or casual hypothesis
D) Directional hypothesis
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B) Null hypothesis

Explanation: A Null Hypothesis (H0) states that there is no relationship or difference between variables. It is the hypothesis that the researcher tries to disprove.

20.
Which of the following is a source of research topic
A) People only individuals
B) Lack of existence of an issue or problem relating to lives
C) Lack of evaluation of the effectiveness of an intervention
D) Phenomena to establish patterns
Show Answer

Correct Answer: D) Phenomena to establish patterns

Explanation: Research topics often arise from observing phenomena to understand patterns, curiosity, social problems, or gaps in existing theory. "Lack of evaluation..." (C) is also a valid source (gap in knowledge), but D describes the broader source.

21.
Which of the following statement is true about research questions?
A) Should be broader than the objectives
B) Should be extracted from the literature
C) Should relate to the variables of the study
D) Should go beyond the population of study
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C) Should relate to the variables of the study

Explanation: Research questions must directly address the variables (independent and dependent) being investigated to ensure the study remains focused.

22.
An entire group of individuals, events or objectives having a common observable characteristic is;
A) Sample population
B) Study population
C) Accessible population
D) Population
Show Answer

Correct Answer: D) Population

Explanation: In research, the "Population" (or Target Population) refers to the entire aggregate of cases that meet the designated criteria. The sample is drawn from this.

23.
In the Psychomotor domain of learning, the learner is expected to gain,
A) Attitudes
B) Knowledge
C) Manual dexterity
D) Reasoning
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C) Manual dexterity

Explanation: The Psychomotor domain deals with physical skills, coordination, and manual dexterity. Cognitive deals with knowledge/reasoning. Affective deals with attitudes.

24.
A psychiatric patient who displaces affection from a previous relationship to the nurse is said to be having
A) Transference
B) Countertransference
C) Role confusion
D) Tangentiality
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A) Transference

Explanation: Transference is the unconscious redirection of feelings from significant others in the patient's past onto the therapist/nurse. Countertransference is the nurse's feelings toward the patient.

25.
Which of the following is an absolute contra-indication of electro-convulsive therapy
A) Fever
B) History of suicide
C) Catatonia
D) Increased intracranial pressure
Show Answer

Correct Answer: D) Increased intracranial pressure

Explanation: Raised ICP is the only absolute contraindication because the seizure induced by ECT causes a transient but significant rise in blood pressure and ICP, which could lead to herniation.

26.
In bipolar disorder, the patient with manic attack/phase presents with,
A) Flat affect: separation from external world, depression
B) Indiscretion, grandiosity, flight of ideas
C) Excessive or additional to normal thoughts, emotions, or behaviors
D) Lack of sleep, loss of interest and reduced energy
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B) Indiscretion, grandiosity, flight of ideas

Explanation: These are classic symptoms of Mania: Grandiosity (inflated self-esteem), Flight of ideas (racing thoughts), and sexual/spending indiscretions. Option A describes negative symptoms of Schizophrenia or depression.

27.
The main difference between bipolar I and Bipolar II is
A) Bipolar I has one or more manic episodes accompanied by major depressive episode while bipolar II has only depressive episodes
B) Bipolar I has one or more manic episodes accompanied by major depressive episode while bipolar II has only depressive episodes accompanied by hypomanic episode
C) Bipolar I has one or more depressive episodes accompanied by hypomanic episode while bipolar II has only depressive episodes accompanied by manic episodes
D) Bipolar I has one or more manic episodes accompanied by major depressive episode while bipolar II has depressive episodes accompanied by hypomanic episode
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B) Bipolar I has one or more manic episodes... while bipolar II has ... hypomanic episode

Explanation: The defining feature of Bipolar I is the presence of at least one full Manic Episode (with or without depression). Bipolar II requires a Major Depressive Episode + Hypomania (never full Mania). Options B and D are very similar, but D phrasing is slightly less standard.

28.
In preparation of a handout,
A) Ensure statements are consistent with other texts that the learner uses
B) Do not use major and minor headings
C) Avoid summarizing major ideas
D) Avoid questions and exercises within the text
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A) Ensure statements are consistent with other texts that the learner uses

Explanation: Consistency prevents confusion. Handouts should use headings (B is wrong), summarize ideas (C is wrong), and can include exercises (D is wrong).

29.
In problem based learning, the learner should;
A) Actively listen but not contribute to avoid interruptions
B) Carry out analysis and synthesis of the whole learning process
C) Avoid redefining explanations to avoid confusion
D) Act as a solution receiver and change agent
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B) Carry out analysis and synthesis of the whole learning process

Explanation: PBL is student-centered. Learners must actively analyze the problem, synthesize information, and direct their own learning.

30.
A sampling design that involves identifying initial subjects with the desired characteristics then they are asked to name others that they know who have those required characteristics is
A) Quota
B) Accidental
C) Snowball
D) Purposeful
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C) Snowball

Explanation: Snowball sampling (chain referral) relies on subjects referring the researcher to other potential subjects, useful for hard-to-reach populations.

31.
A disorder characterized by the expression of an emotional conflict through the development of physical symptoms is called
A) Conversion disorder
B) Psychomotor retardation
C) Displacement
D) Anxiety disorder
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A) Conversion disorder

Explanation: Conversion disorder (Functional Neurological Symptom Disorder) involves motor or sensory symptoms that cannot be explained by a medical condition and are often linked to psychological stress.

32.
In a child with a chronic disorder, the family should;

34.
Atypical antipsychotics
A) Have more extrapyramidal side effects
B) Are more likely to cause hypotension
C) Are more likely to cause weight gain

A) Voluntary

36.
A situation where an experimenter assigns participants to a control or experimental group on a random basis is called
A) Randomization
B) Control
C) Manipulation
D) Pretest
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A) Randomization

Explanation: Randomization ensures that every participant has an equal chance of being assigned to any group, eliminating selection bias.

37.
A situation where the researcher ratings are influenced by his or her initial impression of the subject or by his/her knowledge of the subjects' previous performance on another variable is
A) Recall bias
B) Selection bias
C) Halo effect
D) Hawthorne effect
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C) Halo effect

Explanation: The Halo effect is a cognitive bias where the perception of one positive trait influences the overall opinion of a person (e.g., assuming a good student is good at everything).

38.
An infant who presents with poor weight gain, tachypnoea, tachycardia and cyanosis is likely to be suffering from
A) Acute malnutrition
B) Fever
C) Congenital heart disease
D) Dehydration
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C) Congenital heart disease

Explanation: This triad (poor growth, fast breathing, fast heart rate, cyanosis) specifically points to cardiac failure or cyanotic heart disease. Malnutrition (A) doesn't typically cause acute cyanosis/tachycardia at rest unless severe shock/infection.

39.
Qualitative data can be

D) Can be analyzed into average
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A) In form of words and text

Explanation: Qualitative research deals with non-numerical data like descriptions, interview transcripts, and observations. B, C, and D describe Quantitative data.

40.

A) Judgement
B) Insight

Section 2: Short Answer Questions (40 Marks)

41.
Explain (3) three purposes of literature review in research (6 marks)
Show Answer

Answer:

  1. Identify Knowledge Gaps: To determine what is already known about the topic and what is yet to be discovered, justifying the need for the current study.
  2. Methodological Guidance: To identify research methods, designs, and tools that have been successful (or unsuccessful) in previous similar studies.
  3. Theoretical Framework: To identify theories and concepts that can serve as a foundation for the current study and help in interpreting findings.
42.
Explain two differences between qualitative and quantitative studies (4 marks)
Show Answer

Answer:

  • Data Type: Quantitative studies collect numerical data (measurable), whereas Qualitative studies collect narrative/textual data (words, images).
  • Objective vs Subjective: Quantitative research seeks objectivity and generalization of findings (deductive), while Qualitative research acknowledges subjectivity and seeks in-depth understanding of specific contexts (inductive).
43.
Outline five (5) psychological effects of excessive alcohol consumption (5 marks)
Show Answer

Answer:

  1. Depression: Alcohol is a depressant and long-term use lowers mood.
  2. Anxiety: Withdrawal and chronic use increase anxiety levels.
  3. Psychosis: Can cause hallucinations and delusions (e.g., alcohol-induced psychosis).
  4. Memory Impairment: Blackouts and long-term cognitive decline (dementia).
  5. Personality Changes: Increased aggression, irritability, or emotional instability.

45.
Explain the three (3) domains of learning as per Benjamin Bloom's taxonomy (6 marks)
Show Answer

Answer:

  1. Cognitive Domain: Deals with mental skills, knowledge acquisition, and intellectual processing (Thinking).
  2. Affective Domain: Deals with feelings, emotions, attitudes, and values (Feeling).
  3. Psychomotor Domain: Deals with physical skills, manual dexterity, and coordination (Doing).

  1. Inability to drink or breastfeed: The child is too weak to suckle or swallow.
  2. Vomiting everything: The child cannot keep any food or fluid down.

47.
Explain three (3) components of a lesson plan (6 marks)
Show Answer

Answer:

  1. Specific Objectives: Statements describing what the learner should be able to do by the end of the lesson.
  2. Content/Subject Matter: The specific topics, facts, and skills to be taught.
  3. Learning Activities/Methods: The strategies used by the teacher and students to achieve the objectives (e.g., lecture, discussion).
  4. (Also: Evaluation methods, Resources/Teaching aids).
48.
State four (4) ethical issues in research that concern the respondents (4 marks)
Show Answer

Answer:

  1. Informed Consent: Participation must be voluntary with full knowledge of risks/benefits.
  2. Confidentiality/Anonymity: Protecting the participant's identity and data.
  3. Beneficence (Do No Harm): Protecting participants from physical or psychological harm.
  4. Justice: Fair selection of participants and fair distribution of benefits/burdens.

Section 3: Long Answer Question (20 Marks)

49.
Master A 10 years old is admitted in the paediatric ward with a diagnosis of nephrotic syndrome.
a) Draw and label a diagram of the longitudinal section of the right kidney. (6 marks)
b) List eight (8) clinical manifestations Master A will present with. (4 marks)
c) Using the Nursing process, describe the specific immediate Nursing management of the patient for the first 24 hours. (10 marks)
Show Answer

Answer Guide:

a) Diagram of Kidney (6 Marks)

(Student should draw and label: Cortex, Medulla, Pyramids, Renal Pelvis, Ureter, Renal Artery, Renal Vein, Capsule).

b) Clinical Manifestations (4 Marks) - 0.5 marks each

  1. Generalized Edema (Anasarca): Especially periorbital edema in the morning.
  2. Proteinuria: Frothy urine (massive protein loss).
  3. Hypoalbuminemia: Low blood protein.
  4. Hyperlipidemia: High cholesterol.
  5. Oliguria: Decreased urine output.
  6. Weight Gain: Due to fluid retention.
  7. Fatigue/Lethargy.
  8. Pallor.

c) Nursing Management (10 Marks)

  • Assessment: Assess fluid status (weigh daily, strict input/output chart), measure abdominal girth (ascites), monitor vital signs (BP).
  • Diagnosis 1: Fluid Volume Excess related to compromised regulatory mechanism (renal failure/low albumin).
    • Intervention: Administer diuretics (e.g., Furosemide) as prescribed. Restrict fluid intake as per order. Maintain low salt diet. Elevate edematous parts. Monitor skin turgor.
  • Diagnosis 2: Risk for Infection related to immunosuppression (corticosteroid therapy) and loss of immunoglobulins.
    • Intervention: Isolate from infectious cases. Strict aseptic technique. Monitor temperature. Teach hand hygiene.
  • Diagnosis 3: Imbalanced Nutrition: Less than body requirements related to protein loss.
    • Intervention: Provide high calorie, moderate protein (depending on renal function), low sodium diet. Small frequent meals.
  • Skin Care: Turn every 2 hours to prevent pressure sores on edematous skin. Keep skin clean and dry.
