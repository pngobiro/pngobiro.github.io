Section 1: Multiple Choice Questions (40 Marks)

1.
In teaching and learning, curriculum plays a key role in achievement of program/course goals. As a community health nurse when developing content, what would you consider 'core content' to include:
A. Content that must be included for achievement of competences
B. Knowledge, skills and professional qualities that learners would find relevant to their learning needs
C. Nice to know content which includes subject matter that facilitates performance in different working environments
D. Content that can be accessed in textbooks by lecturers only and disseminated to students
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A. Content that must be included for achievement of competences

Explanation: Core content refers to the essential, "must-know" material that is strictly necessary for a student to achieve the required competencies of the profession. It forms the foundation of the curriculum.

Why others are wrong:

  • B: This describes "useful to know" or supplementary content tailored to interest, not necessarily the mandatory core.
  • C: "Nice to know" content is peripheral and not critical for basic competency.
  • D: Core content is for students to learn, not just for lecturers to access.
2.
Match the items in column A with statements in column B:

Column A:

  • A. Formative evaluation
  • B. Assessment

Column B:

  • i. Is assigning grades for students' achievement at the end of a course.
  • ii. Is measurement of student abilities during and after participation in a course.
  • iii. Is on-going assessment of students while the course is in progress.
  • iv. Is quantifying data and assigning values.
Show Answer

Correct Match:

  • A (Formative evaluation) matches with iii: Is on-going assessment of students while the course is in progress. Its purpose is to provide feedback to improve learning.
  • B (Assessment) matches with ii: Is measurement of student abilities during and after participation in a course. It is the broader process of gathering data.

Note: Summative evaluation would match with (i) (end of course).

3.
The cardinal triggers that would lead you to suspect Sickle cell crisis in a child include:
A. Haemorrhage, malnutrition, tiredness
B. Infection, dehydration, haemolysis
C. Infection, dehydration, malnutrition
D. Malnutrition, tiredness, dehydration
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B. Infection, dehydration, haemolysis

Explanation: The primary triggers for a vaso-occlusive crisis are conditions that cause hypoxia, acidosis, or dehydration. Infection increases metabolic demand and oxygen use. Dehydration increases blood viscosity. Haemolysis is the underlying mechanism but acute exacerbation (hemolytic crisis) is also a type of crisis.

Why others are wrong: Malnutrition is a long-term complication, not an acute trigger for a crisis. Haemorrhage is not a standard trigger for vaso-occlusion, though sequestration crisis involves blood pooling.

4.
While assessing children with chest infection, you would suspect a child has severe croup (LTB) if they present with:
A. A stridor only when at rest
B. Hoarse voice
C. Low grade fever
D. Rapid breathing and chest in drawing
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A. A stridor only when at rest

Explanation: Stridor at rest indicates significant upper airway obstruction and is a sign of severe croup. Stridor only upon agitation is considered mild/moderate.

Clinical Note: Chest indrawing (D) is a sign of lower respiratory infection (pneumonia) or severe respiratory distress, but "stridor at rest" is the specific hallmark distinguishing severe laryngotracheobronchitis.

5.
Congenital malformations can be detected and corrected early. As a nurse you would suspect Oesophageal atresia or trachea-oesophageal fistula when there is:
A. Absence of sucking reflex
B. Excessive amount of frothing saliva in the mouth
C. Hyper-active bowel movement
D. Jaundice
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B. Excessive amount of frothing saliva in the mouth

Explanation: Infants with esophageal atresia cannot swallow saliva, leading to accumulation and "frothing" at the mouth. The "3 Cs" of TEF are Coughing, Choking, and Cyanosis upon feeding.

6.
In neonatal resuscitation, the sequence of chest compressions to ventilation you would apply is:
A. 2:1
B. 3:1
C. 15:2
D. 30:2
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B. 3:1

Explanation: The standard ratio for neonatal resuscitation is 3 compressions to 1 breath (3:1), delivering approximately 90 compressions and 30 breaths per minute.

Why others are wrong: 15:2 is for child/infant 2-rescuer CPR. 30:2 is for adult/child single-rescuer CPR.

7.
Bronchiectasis is defined as:
A. Collection of serous fluid in the pleural cavity
B. A permanent dilatation of the bronchi and bronchioles resulting from obstruction or infection
C. A serious illness characterized by inflammation of the bronchioles causing severe dyspnoea
D. Infection of the lung parenchyma which may be primary or secondary to acute bronchitis or bronchiolitis
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B. A permanent dilatation of the bronchi and bronchioles resulting from obstruction or infection

Explanation: Bronchiectasis is defined by the irreversible widening (dilation) of the bronchial airways due to damage to the airway wall.

Why others are wrong: A is Pleural Effusion. C describes Bronchiolitis. D describes Pneumonia.

8.
A congenital abnormality which presents with pulmonary stenosis, ventricular septal defect, right ventricular hypertrophy and overriding aorta is referred to as:
A. Patent ductus arteriosus
B. Transposition of the great arteries
C. Tetralogy of Fallot
D. Hypoplastic left heart syndrome
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C. Tetralogy of Fallot

Explanation: These are the four (tetra) classic defects of TOF: 1. Pulmonary Stenosis, 2. Right Ventricular Hypertrophy, 3. Overriding Aorta, 4. Ventricular Septal Defect (VSD).

9.
Different types of vitamins provide protection to children. When a child is diagnosed with deficiency of vitamin D the likely condition thy would likely develop is:
A. Night blindness
B. Scurvy
C. Rickets
D. Osteomalacia
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C. Rickets

Explanation: Vitamin D deficiency in growing children leads to Rickets (soft bones and skeletal deformities). In adults, it causes Osteomalacia (D).

Why others are wrong: A is Vitamin A deficiency. B is Vitamin C deficiency.

10.
Acute diarrhea in children is a common cause of death. The common bacteria you would take care to eliminate and ensure they don't affect the child would include:
A. Candida albicans, antibiotics, food allergy
B. Rota virus, enterovirus, measles virus
C. Staphylococcus, clostridium difficile, influenza virus
D. Escherichia coli, shigella, salmonella
Show Answer

Correct Answer: D. Escherichia coli, shigella, salmonella

Explanation: These are the primary bacterial causes of acute gastroenteritis/dysentery in children. Rotavirus (B) is a viral cause, not bacterial. Candida (A) is fungal.

11.
Hepatitis A can be prevented:
A. By improving food, water and personal hygiene and environmental sanitation
B. Through administration of hepatitis B vaccine
C. By avoiding contamination with infected blood or its products
D. Limiting the use of potentially dangerous blood derivatives
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A. By improving food, water and personal hygiene and environmental sanitation

Explanation: Hepatitis A is transmitted via the fecal-oral route. Prevention focuses on sanitation, clean water, and hygiene. Options C and D refer to blood-borne hepatitises like Hep B and C.

12.
A child with sickle cell disease in vaso-occlusive crisis will present with:
A. Obstruction of micro-circulation in the lungs from venous occlusion by sickle cells
B. Transient and self-limiting bone marrow failure following a severe infection
C. Obstruction of splenic outflow by sickled cells
D. Painful bones such as the femur, tibia and lower vertebrae
Show Answer

Correct Answer: D. Painful bones such as the femur, tibia and lower vertebrae

Explanation: Vaso-occlusive crisis typically presents as severe pain (pain crisis) in the long bones, chest, or abdomen due to ischemia. Option B describes Aplastic Crisis. Option C describes Sequestration Crisis.

13.
Indicate whether the following statements are true or false:

A. A sampling frame is a list, directory or index of cases from which a sample can be selected

B. The discrepancy between sample characteristics and the population characteristics is referred to as sampling error

Show Answer

Answers:

  • A: True - A sampling frame is indeed the source material/list from which the sample is drawn.
  • B: True - Sampling error is the statistical difference between the sample mean and the true population mean.
14.
Sampling in research increases confidence in evidence based practice. To sample the population in a Purposive sampling method the researcher:
A. Sampling where every Kth case in the population frame is selected for inclusion in the sample
B. Placing numbers in a container and allowing members of the accessible population to pick any number at random
C. When it is not possible to obtain a sampling frame because the population is either very large or scattered over a large geographical area
D. Where cases or subjects are handpicked because they are informative or they possess the required characteristics
Show Answer

Correct Answer: D. Where cases or subjects are handpicked because they are informative or they possess the required characteristics

Explanation: Purposive (judgmental) sampling involves the researcher consciously selecting participants based on specific qualities needed for the study. A is Systematic sampling. B is Simple Random sampling. C describes situations for Cluster sampling.

15.
You have decided to sample a population and selected non-probability sampling methods. The main ones would include:
A. Snowball, quota, simple random
B. Purposive, convenient, snowball
C. Accidental, stratified, simple random
D. Systematic, cluster, stratified
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B. Purposive, convenient, snowball

Explanation: All three listed in B are non-probability methods (do not involve random selection). "Simple random" (in A and C) and "Stratified/Cluster" (in D) are probability sampling methods.

16.
A dependent variable is one that:
A. Affects outcome of a research study either because the researcher is not aware of its existence or does not have control over it
B. Attempts to indicate the total influence arising from the effects of an independent variable
C. A researcher manipulates in order to determine its effect or influence on another variable
D. Converts what was thought of as a positive relationship into a negative relationship and vice versa
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B. Attempts to indicate the total influence arising from the effects of an independent variable

Explanation: The dependent variable is the outcome or "effect" variable that changes in response to the independent variable. Option C describes the Independent variable (manipulated). Option A describes an Extraneous/Confounding variable.

17.
Ratio scale is a measurement scale that:
A. Groups subjects into categories and ranks them into some order
B. Assigns numerals to each measure and ranks them in an order with intervals between numerals equally
C. Has all the characteristics of the other scales with an additional true zero point hence all mathematical operations can be applied to yield meaningful values
D. Groups subjects or cases from the sample into categories into common sets of characteristics e.g. sex, race etc.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C. Has all the characteristics of the other scales with an additional true zero point...

Explanation: The defining feature of a ratio scale (like weight, height, pulse) is the existence of an "absolute zero," which allows for meaningful ratios (e.g., 10kg is twice as heavy as 5kg). A is Ordinal. B is Interval. D is Nominal.

18.
Construct validity refers to:
A. A measure of the degree to which a research instrument yields consistent results after repeated trials
B. A measure of the degree to which a research instrument meaningfully and accurately reflects or represents a theoretical concept
C. A measure of degree to which data collected using a particular instrument represents a specific domain of indicators or content of a particular concept
D. The use of a measure in assessing subjects' behavior in specific situations
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B. A measure of the degree to which a research instrument meaningfully and accurately reflects or represents a theoretical concept

Explanation: Construct validity ensures the test actually measures the theoretical construct (e.g., intelligence, anxiety) it claims to measure. A is Reliability. C is Content Validity.

19.
The Hawthorne effect refers to a situation where:
A. Subjects' awareness of being in an experimental group motivates them to perform better
B. A chemically inactive substance with the same colour and taste as the true drug are given to half the patients to determine significant difference between the two groups
C. The subjects perform much better because of the researcher's expectation
D. The researcher ratings are influenced by his/her initial impression of the subject
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A. Subjects' awareness of being in an experimental group motivates them to perform better

Explanation: The Hawthorne effect is the alteration of behavior by the subjects of a study due to their awareness of being observed. B is Placebo. C is Pygmalion/Rosenthal effect. D is Halo effect.

20.
You wish to analyze data using measures of central tendencies. To determine the median in data you would consider:
A. The most commonly attained measurement or value
B. The 50th percentile in a group of scores
C. The average of a set of scores or measurements
D. The distribution of scores around a particular central score or value
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B. The 50th percentile in a group of scores

Explanation: The median is the middle value when data is ordered, effectively splitting the data in half (50th percentile). A is Mode. C is Mean. D refers to Dispersion.

21.
Variance refers to:
A. The difference between the highest and the lowest score in a distribution
B. The extent to which scores in a distribution deviate from their mean or average
C. The sum of each squared deviation divide by the degrees of freedom
D. The dispersion of scores around the mean of the distribution
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C. The sum of each squared deviation divide by the degrees of freedom

Explanation: Mathematically, Variance (s²) is calculated by summing the squared differences from the mean and dividing by n-1 (degrees of freedom). A is Range. B and D are general descriptions of dispersion/deviation.

22.
You have been assigned as a research supervisor to some students attached to your area of work. They seek to understand what should be contained in the research methodology chapter. How would you advise them?
A. Details of the gap or gaps that exist in the area of study and presents the rationale for the study
B. Researcher's critique of findings from other studies done in related areas
C. Details regarding the procedures used in conducting the study
D. Method of the systematic data analysis during the study
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C. Details regarding the procedures used in conducting the study

Explanation: The methodology chapter outlines *how* the research was done (design, population, sampling, data collection procedures). A is Background/Problem Statement. B is Literature Review. D is part of methodology but C is the comprehensive definition.

23.
Serial seven tests provide a general summary of
A. Judgement
B. Cognitive domain
C. Cognitive function
D. Memory
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C. Cognitive function

Explanation: Specifically, "Serial Sevens" (counting back from 100 by 7s) tests concentration and attention. While this is part of the general "Cognitive function" (or sensorium) assessment in the Mental State Exam, it is the best fit among the broad options provided if "Concentration" is not listed.

24.
A condition in which a patient invents new words/phrases and gives new meaning to the standard words is called:
A. Clang association
B. Word salad
C. Echolalia
D. Neologism
Show Answer

Correct Answer: D. Neologism

Explanation: Neologisms are newly coined words or expressions that have meaning only to the person using them, common in schizophrenia. A is rhyming. B is incoherent mixture of words. C is repeating others' words.

25.
A motor activity disturbance where a patient can maintain an odd position for a long period is called:
A. Negativism
B. Stupor
C. Echopraxia
D. Waxy flexibility
Show Answer

Correct Answer: D. Waxy flexibility

Explanation: Waxy flexibility (cerea flexibilitas) is a psychomotor symptom of catatonia where a patient's limbs retain any position into which they are manipulated by another person.

26.
Mental illness that manifests itself with physical symptoms is called:
A. Anxiety
B. Somatic disorder
C. Fictitious disorder
D. Psychosomatic disorder
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B. Somatic disorder

Explanation: Somatic symptom disorders are characterized by an extreme focus on physical symptoms — such as pain or fatigue — that causes major emotional distress and problems functioning. "Psychosomatic" is an older term often used interchangeably, but "Somatic disorder" is the current DSM terminology.

27.
A misinterpretation of a real stimuli is referred to as:
A. An illusion
B. Delusion
C. Hallucination
D. Somatoform disorder
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A. An illusion

Explanation: An illusion is a distortion of the senses, revealing how the brain normally organizes and interprets sensory stimulation (misinterpreting a real external stimulus, e.g., seeing a rope as a snake). A Hallucination (C) is perception *without* external stimulus.

28.
A patient is said to lack insight when he/she:
A. Is disorientated
B. Does not know that he/she is sick and needs help
C. Knows his problem and does not need help
D. Knows his problem and needs help
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B. Does not know that he/she is sick and needs help

Explanation: Insight in psychiatry refers to the patient's awareness of their condition. Lack of insight means they do not recognize they are ill or require treatment.

30.
You have prepared a lesson for new nursing students. For your teaching to be effective you should organize the content from:
A. Complex to simple
B. Unknown to known
C. Concrete to abstract

Correct Answer: C. Concrete to abstract

31.
You are teaching students on human resource concepts. In your definition of job description, you would tell them it refers to:
A. tasks or duties a student is required to perform in their daily clinical placement
B. An outline that focuses on the minor responsibilities of a professional
C. duties or tasks professionals are expected to perform in future
D. the roles the trainee is expected to perform in future
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A. tasks or duties a student is required to perform in their daily clinical placement

Explanation: A job description is a written statement describing the duties, responsibilities, and tasks of a specific job. In the context of students, it refers to their current required tasks (A). C and D refer to "future", which is speculative.

32.
In the cognitive domain objectives are ordered as follows:
A. Application, comprehension, analysis, evaluation, simple knowledge, synthesis
B. Simple knowledge, comprehension, application, analysis, synthesis, evaluation
C. Simple knowledge, application, comprehension, analysis, synthesis, evaluation
D. Evaluation, synthesis, analysis, application, comprehension, simple knowledge
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B. Simple knowledge, comprehension, application, analysis, synthesis, evaluation

Explanation: This follows Bloom's Taxonomy (original 1956) from lowest to highest order: Knowledge → Comprehension → Application → Analysis → Synthesis → Evaluation.

33.
The criterion or standard in an educational objective that:
A. Describes what the learner is expected to do
B. Describes the activity to be undertaken
C. Indicates the condition under which the activity is to be undertaken
D. Refers to the level of performance or accuracy of doing the activity
Show Answer

Correct Answer: D. Refers to the level of performance or accuracy of doing the activity

Explanation: A behavioral objective has three components: Behavior (what they do), Condition (circumstances), and Criterion (standard/level of performance, e.g., "correctly," "within 5 minutes").

34.
As a clinical instructor, you need to teach nurse students on how to draw and administer medication. Which appropriate teaching method would you go for?
A. Small group discussion.
B. Role play
C. Lecture
D. Demonstration

Correct Answer: D. Demonstration

Explanation: Demonstration is the best method for teaching psychomotor skills (like drawing medication) as it allows students to observe the technique before practicing it (return demonstration).

35.

A. Case conference, clinical rounds, demonstration.
B. Lecture, ward reports, simulation.

Correct Answer: A. Case conference, clinical rounds, demonstration.

Explanation: These are classic bedside/clinical teaching methods. Lectures (B) and Role play (C/D) are typically classroom methods, though role play can be used clinically. Case conferences and rounds are distinct to the clinical environment.

36.
Multiple choice question stem must:
A. Be made complex not precise.

38.

43.
Explain three (3) preventive measures you would utilize to prevent and control neonatal tetanus. (6 marks)
Show Answer

Answer:

  1. Maternal Immunization: Administering Tetanus Toxoid (TT) vaccine to pregnant women (at least 2 doses) to transfer passive immunity to the fetus.
  2. Clean Delivery Practices (5 Cleans): Ensuring clean hands, clean delivery surface, clean cord cutting instrument, clean cord tie, and clean cord care.
  3. Hygienic Cord Care: Avoiding application of harmful substances (e.g., cow dung, ash) to the umbilical stump; using chlorhexidine if recommended or keeping it dry and clean.
44.
As a nursing student outline four (4) key clinical evaluations for a child to be undertaken that would indicate ingestion of a poisonous substance (4 marks)
Show Answer

Answer:

  1. History: Sudden onset of symptoms in a previously healthy child, history of unsupervised play, empty containers found nearby.
  2. Neurological Status: Assessing for altered level of consciousness (drowsiness, coma), seizures, or ataxia.
  3. Respiratory Status: Checking for respiratory distress, unusual odors on breath (e.g., kerosene, almonds), or aspiration pneumonia signs.
  4. Gastrointestinal Signs: Vomiting, diarrhea, abdominal pain, or burns around the mouth/lips (corrosive substances).
  5. Vital Signs: Tachycardia, bradycardia, hypotension, or hyperthermia depending on the toxin.

47.
Mental health illness can present in many different ways. Explain three (3) classifications of Schizophrenia (6 marks)
Show Answer

Answer:

  1. Paranoid Schizophrenia: Characterized prominently by delusions of persecution or grandeur and auditory hallucinations, with relatively preserved cognitive function.
  2. Catatonic Schizophrenia: Characterized by psychomotor disturbances, which may include immobility (waxy flexibility), stupor, excessive motor activity, or echolalia/echopraxia.
  3. Disorganized (Hebephrenic) Schizophrenia: Characterized by disorganized speech, disorganized behavior, and flat or inappropriate affect.
  4. Undifferentiated Schizophrenia: Symptoms are present but do not fit clearly into the other specific subtypes.
48.
A young female has been reported to have attempted suicide and is brought to you for health assessment. Formulate five (5) questions that you may use to evaluate her as a means of suicide risk assessment. (5 marks)
Show Answer

Answer:

  1. "Do you currently have thoughts of harming yourself or ending your life?" (Ideation)
  2. "Do you have a specific plan on how you would do it?" (Plan)
  3. "Do you have access to the means (e.g., pills, weapon) to carry out this plan?" (Means)
  4. "Have you ever attempted suicide before?" (History)

Section 3: Long Answer Question (20 Marks)

49.
Mr. G a seventeen (17) year old has been brought to the outpatient department by the police. The Police allege that he sat in the middle of the road shouting and threatening vehicle owners that he was the police and dared them go past him. He is very excited and keeps interrupting the police as they hand him over to you. The police report that this is the second time he has been found with similar behaviour and parents indicate they are unable to manage him. On examination you have made a diagnosis of mood disorder. Detailing any special considerations or concerns of this case describe his care from admission to discharge. (20 marks)
Show Answer

Answer Guide:

Diagnosis: Bipolar Disorder (Manic Episode)

1. Admission & Immediate Safety (Acute Phase)

  • Admission Mode: Involuntary admission (Sectioning) due to risk to self (traffic) and others (threatening behavior), brought by police.
  • Safety: Search for harmful objects. Nurse in a quiet, low-stimulus environment to reduce manic excitation.
  • Pharmacology: Administer antipsychotics (e.g., Olanzapine, Haloperidol) or Benzodiazepines for rapid tranquilization if aggressive. Mood stabilizers (Lithium/Valproate) initiated.

2. Nursing Management (Physical & Psychological)

  • Communication: Use firm, calm, short sentences. Do not argue with delusions (grandeur - being police) but present reality gently.
  • Nutrition: Provide high-calorie finger foods and fluids as the patient is too hyperactive to sit for meals.
  • Sleep: Encourage rest periods, reduce stimuli at night, administer sedatives if prescribed.
  • Hygiene: Assist with ADLs as the patient may be too distracted.

3. Special Considerations (Adolescent/Legal)

  • Age: He is a minor (17). Consent for treatment usually involves parents/guardians.
  • Police Involvement: Document police report clearly. Address legal aspects if charges are pending (forensic consideration).
  • Family: Parents stated they are "unable to manage him." Family therapy and education are critical before discharge.

4. Discharge Planning & Rehabilitation

  • Education: Teach patient/family about the chronic nature of Bipolar disorder, importance of medication adherence (Lithium monitoring).
  • Warning Signs: Educate on signs of relapse (lack of sleep, racing thoughts).
  • Follow-up: Link with community mental health team or outpatient clinic.
