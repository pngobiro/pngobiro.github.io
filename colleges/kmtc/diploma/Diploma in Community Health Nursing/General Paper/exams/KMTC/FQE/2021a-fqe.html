Section 1: Multiple Choice Questions (40 Marks)

1.
In assessing developmental milestones, a nurse should be concerned if the child does not hold head steady unsupported at
a) Birth
b) 2 months
c) 4 months
d) 2 weeks
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) 4 months

Explanation: Head control (holding head steady without support) is typically achieved by 4 months. If a child cannot do this by 4 months, it is a developmental warning sign (red flag) suggesting possible motor delay or hypotonia.

2.
In IMCI, when a child has a pink classification:
a) The child is given treatment and the first doses to be taken immediately they reach home to feed and then continue with treatment
b) The child is given treatment and the first doses are administered at the facility and then sent home but to be coming for treatment
c) The child is given treatment and the first doses are administered at the refer facility and then to the next facility
d) The child is given treatment and the first doses are administered at the facility and then refer urgently to hospital
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) The child is given treatment and the first doses are administered at the facility and then refer urgently to hospital

Explanation: In IMCI (Integrated Management of Childhood Illness), a Pink classification indicates a severe condition requiring urgent referral. The protocol is to administer the first dose of appropriate treatment (e.g., antibiotic, antimalarial) at the facility and then refer urgently.

3.
Feeding recommendations for a child 2 years and above is
a) Give enriched family foods 3 times a day with a least two snacks in between
b) Give enriched family foods 3 times a day avoid snacks in between
c) Give enriched family foods 2 times a day with a least three snacks in between
d) Give enriched family foods 3 times a day start introducing complementary feeds
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Give enriched family foods 3 times a day with a least two snacks in between

Explanation: For children aged 2 years and older, the recommendation is 3 meals of family foods and 2 extra snacks per day to meet energy requirements.

4.
After initiating treatment for a child with classification of pneumonia the guardian is counseled to
a) Give treatment and wait for return date
b) Come back to the facility immediately if the child becomes worse
c) Seek senior review at a higher facility
d) Reduce amount of clothes and give prescribed medication
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Come back to the facility immediately if the child becomes worse

Explanation: A critical part of IMCI management is counseling the mother on "When to return immediately." Signs include the child becoming sicker, unable to drink/breastfeed, or developing fast breathing/difficulty breathing.

5.
For any pink classification in dehydration in the child and no other pink classification
a) A first dose of a broad spectrum antibiotic orally, fluids as per plan C refer urgently to the hospital
b) An antimalarial or both, treat for low blood sugar, fluids as per plan B refer urgently to the hospital
c) A first dose of a broad spectrum antibiotic orally, fluids as per plan A refer urgently to the hospital
d) Give fluids as per plan C.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Give fluids as per plan C.

Explanation: A pink classification for dehydration is "Severe Dehydration". The treatment is Plan C (Rapid IV rehydration). If there are *no other* severe classifications (pink), you start Plan C immediately. If referral is needed for *other* reasons, you refer. The question implies "no other pink classification", so the priority is treating the dehydration with Plan C.

6.
A patient who seeks to organize others in the outpatient department is likely to have a personality type called
a) Melancholic
b) Phlegmatic
c) Choleric
d) Sanguine
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Choleric

Explanation: The Choleric temperament is associated with being a leader, dominant, organized, and taking charge (the "driver"). Sanguine (D) is talkative/social. Melancholic (A) is analytical/quiet. Phlegmatic (B) is peaceful/relaxed.

7.
In nutritional assessment of children, acute malnutrition is indicated by,
a) Height for age and MUAC
b) Height for weight Z score and MUAC
c) Weight for age Z score and MUAC
d) Weight for height Z-score and MUAC
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Weight for height Z-score and MUAC

Explanation: Acute malnutrition (wasting) is measured by Weight-for-Height (WHZ < -2 SD) or Mid-Upper Arm Circumference (MUAC). Height-for-age measures stunting (chronic). Weight-for-age measures underweight (general).

8.
In order to identify and minimize trauma to a child, the nurse should
a) Avoid separation the child and mother, explain the procedure to the patient and caretaker, give child and care givers chance to express their feelings about procedure
b) Avoid separation the child and mother, explain the procedure to the patient and caretaker, avoid any procedure that is unpleasant to child
c) Separate the child and mother during painful procedure, explain the procedure to the patient and caretaker...
d) Separate the child and mother to reduce panic...
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Avoid separation the child and mother, explain the procedure to the patient and caretaker...

Explanation: Atraumatic care involves preventing separation of the child from the family, promoting a sense of control (explanation), and minimizing bodily injury/pain.

9.
Which is the most accurate statement concerning haemophilia
a) It's an X-linked recessive disorder
b) It's equally distributed in males and females
c) It's an autosomal recessive disorder
d) It's a Y-linked dominant disorder
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) It's an X-linked recessive disorder

Explanation: Haemophilia is caused by a gene mutation on the X chromosome. It predominantly affects males (who have only one X chromosome). Females are typically carriers.

10.
Which of the following actions indicates a need for further health messages to parents of a child with congenital heart defect?
a) Counting the number of wet diapers to monitor fluid levels
b) Listening to pulse for one minute while giving digoxin at home
c) Protecting the child from people with upper respiratory infections
d) Waking up the child for feeding every 2 hours to help gain weight
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Waking up the child for feeding every 2 hours to help gain weight

Explanation: Children with CHD tire easily. Frequent waking for feeding disrupts rest, increases fatigue, and increases cardiac workload. They need uninterrupted rest periods. A, B, and C are correct actions.

11.
In the learning process, when the learner checks the meaning and the inferences derived with other learners it is called
a) Analysis
b) Validation
c) Observation
d) Description
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Validation

Explanation: Validation involves checking understanding and inferences with others (peers or teachers) to ensure correctness and shared meaning.

12.
Which of the following statements most accurate about visual learners?
a) Benefit from using charts, maps, notes, and flash cards when studying
b) Do not prefer to see information such as pictures, diagrams, cartoons, demonstrations
c) Learn best when intense visuals accompanied by lecture
d) Not easily distracted in lecture with no visual aids
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Benefit from using charts, maps, notes, and flash cards when studying

Explanation: Visual learners learn best by seeing. They process information using charts, graphs, and written notes.

13.
In an objective, a description of the subject reference which could be knowledge, attitude or skill is
a) Action verb
b) Content reference
c) Performance criteria
d) Performance reference
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Content reference

Explanation: The content reference (or subject matter) specifies *what* is being learned (the content), whereas the action verb specifies the behavior.

14.
A neurotransmitter that inhibits sleep, arousal and appetite is
a) GABA
b) Dopamine
c) Epinephrine
d) Serotonin
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Serotonin

Explanation: Serotonin plays a key role in the regulation of sleep, appetite, and mood. High levels are often associated with satiety (appetite inhibition) and wakefulness/sleep modulation. While GABA is the main inhibitory neurotransmitter, Serotonin is the classic regulator for "sleep, arousal, and appetite" as a group in this context.

15.
In research, an in-depth investigation of an individual, group, institution or phenomena is called
a) Action research
b) Case study
c) Descriptive study
d) Retrospective study
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Case study

Explanation: A case study is a research methodology that involves an intensive, in-depth, and detailed examination of a single case (person, group, or event).

16.
In a focused group discussion,
a) The moderator does not need competence in the topic of discussion
b) Extroverts should be allowed to contribute more as they are more talkative
c) The moderator can probe for more in-depth or additional information
d) The group does not provide a complete picture as compared to individuals
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) The moderator can probe for more in-depth or additional information

Explanation: The moderator's role is to facilitate the discussion, ensure all voices are heard (managing extroverts/introverts), and probe deeply into topics to get rich qualitative data.

17.
The main difference between experimental and non-experimental studies in research is
a) The researcher is a passive observer in non-experimental study
b) The researcher manipulates the independent variable
c) The researcher is an active agent in experimental study
d) The researcher manipulates the dependent variable in experimental study
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) The researcher manipulates the independent variable

Explanation: The defining characteristic of experimental research is the manipulation of the independent variable (intervention) by the researcher. In non-experimental studies, variables are observed as they exist naturally.

18.
In order to minimize a type II error in research the researcher should
a) Increase the sample size
b) Decrease the sample size
c) Avoid developing a null hypothesis
d) Decrease the level of significance
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Increase the sample size

Explanation: Type II error is failing to detect an effect that actually exists (False Negative). Increasing sample size increases statistical power, reducing the risk of Type II error.

19.
Which of the following is not a factor to consider when choosing a research design?
a) Available time
b) Type of hypothesis
c) Statistical significance
d) Type of data
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Statistical significance

Explanation: Statistical significance is an *outcome* of data analysis, not a factor used to choose the design beforehand. Time, data type, and hypothesis determine the design.

23.
In order to overcome her/his own anxieties in dealing with the patient, the nurse should

b) Can set her goals very clearly, Plan a long interaction with the patient
c) Can go through their record, avoid talking to the clinical supervisor...

24.
A symptom where the patient has lengthy and tedious verbalizations and because of numerous details they never reach the intended point is referred to as
a) Tangentiality
b) Thought echo
c) Circumstantiality
d) Echolalia
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Tangentiality

Explanation: Tangentiality is wandering from the topic and *never* returning to the original point. Circumstantiality involves excessive detail but *eventually* reaches the point. The key phrase "never reach the intended point" defines Tangentiality.

25.
Which of the following is a negative symptom in schizophrenia
a) Flat affect: separation from external world
b) Increase of behavior (increase motor movements)
c) Excessive or additional to normal thoughts, emotions, or behaviors
d) Disorganized thoughts and speech
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Flat affect: separation from external world

Explanation: Negative symptoms are "deficits" or absence of normal function (Flat affect, Alogia, Avolition). B, C, and D describe Positive symptoms (excesses/additions).

26.
In treatment of depression, tricyclic antidepressants
a) Block the reuptake of norepinephrine at the post-synaptic terminals
b) Block the reuptake of norepinephrine at the pre-synaptic terminals
c) Increase the reuptake of norepinephrine at the pre-synaptic terminals
d) Increase the reuptake of norepinephrine at the post-synaptic terminals
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Block the reuptake of norepinephrine at the pre-synaptic terminals

Explanation: TCAs inhibit (block) the reuptake pumps on the pre-synaptic neuron, keeping more neurotransmitter in the synapse.

41.
List the ten (10) steps in the WHO guidelines for inpatient treatment of severe malnutrition in children (5 marks)
Show Answer

Answer:

  1. Treat/prevent Hypoglycaemia.
  2. Treat/prevent Hypothermia.
  3. Treat/prevent Dehydration.
  4. Correct Electrolyte Imbalance.
  5. Treat/prevent Infection.
  6. Correct Micronutrient Deficiencies.
  7. Start Cautious Feeding (F75).
  8. Achieve Catch-up Growth (F100).
  9. Provide Sensory Stimulation and Emotional Support.
  10. Prepare for Follow-up after Recovery.

47.
Explain six levels of the cognitive domain of learning in bloom's taxonomy (6 marks)
Show Answer

Answer: (Lowest to Highest)

Section 3: Long Answer Question (20 Marks)

49.
P, a 29-year-old police officer is brought to the hospital by her husband with major depressive disorder. She admits to attempting suicide through drug overdose.
a) Explain two possible modes of admission of this patient. (2 marks)
b) List diagnostic criteria of major depression as per the DSM 5. (8 marks)
c) State five possible nursing diagnosis for this patient and an intervention for each. (10 marks)
Show Answer

Answer Guide:

a) Modes of Admission (2 Marks)

  1. Emergency Admission: Due to immediate risk of harm (suicide attempt).
  2. Involuntary Admission: If she refuses care but poses danger to self.
  3. (Voluntary admission is possible if she agrees, but less likely given the police/attempt context).

b) DSM-5 Diagnostic Criteria (8 Marks)

Must have 5+ symptoms for 2 weeks; one must be (1) or (2):

  1. Depressed mood most of the day.
  2. Markedly diminished interest or pleasure (Anhedonia).
  3. Significant weight loss/gain or appetite change.
  4. Insomnia or Hypersomnia.
  5. Psychomotor agitation or retardation.
  6. Fatigue or loss of energy.
  7. Feelings of worthlessness or excessive guilt.
  8. Diminished ability to think/concentrate.
  9. Recurrent thoughts of death/suicidal ideation.

c) Nursing Diagnoses & Interventions (10 Marks)

  • 1. Risk for Suicide related to past attempt/hopelessness.
    • Intervention: Maintain 1:1 observation; remove harmful objects.
  • 2. Hopelessness related to long-term stress.
    • Intervention: Encourage expression of feelings; identify sources of hope/support.
  • 3. Ineffective Coping related to high-stress job.
    • Intervention: Teach adaptive coping strategies (relaxation, talking).
  • 4. Self-Care Deficit related to lack of energy/motivation.
    • Intervention: Assist with ADLs; encourage independence gradually.
  • 5. Social Isolation related to depressed mood.
    • Intervention: Encourage participation in group activities; spend time with patient.
