Section 1: Multiple Choice Questions (40 Marks)

1.
In research, the term rigor refers to:
A. The probability that the study estimations are correct in predicting the phenomenon.
B. Carefulness, scrupulousness and the degree of exactitude in research investigation.
C. The achievement of the objectives, purpose and reliability of the research findings.
D. Ability to replicate study findings in a different setting using the same methodology.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B. Carefulness, scrupulousness and the degree of exactitude in research investigation.

Explanation: Rigor in research refers to the strict adherence to the rules and procedures of the scientific method to ensure accuracy and reliability. It implies thoroughness and exactitude.

2.
Independent variable in research:
A. Constitute the links between variables under investigation in a study.
B. Comprises the presenting problem under investigation.
C. Are the conditions that cause a particular outcome.
D. Refers to the researcher's anticipated opinion with regard to study outcome.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C. Are the conditions that cause a particular outcome.

Explanation: The independent variable is the "cause" or the variable that is manipulated or naturally varies to observe its effect on the outcome (dependent variable).

3.

B. Hypothesis
C. Limitations
D. Objectives
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A. Assumptions

Explanation: Assumptions are basic principles that are accepted as being true on the basis of logic or reason without proof or verification.

5.
In randomized controlled study designs:
A. Results from different studies are compared to identify consistent patterns and disagreements.
B. The results projected in the research should be free from bias but remain neutral.
C. The study subjects are purposively assigned to "treatment" and "comparison" groups.
D. Research subjects are randomly assigned to "treatment" and "comparison" study groups.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: D. Research subjects are randomly assigned to "treatment" and "comparison" study groups.

Explanation: The defining characteristic of an RCT is randomization, where subjects are assigned to groups by chance to minimize selection bias. Purposive assignment (C) is not randomization.

6.
Purposiveness as characteristic of research refers to:

7.
You have been recruited in a study group that is researching student performance in a given assessment. One of the describes the research approaches that you may utilize:
A. Mixed, quantitative, observational
B. Quantitative, quasi-experimental, observational
C. Analytical, quantitative, experimental
D. Qualitative, quantitative, mixed
Show Answer

Correct Answer: D. Qualitative, quantitative, mixed

Explanation: These are the three broad approaches/paradigms to research. Quantitative deals with numbers, Qualitative with words/meanings, and Mixed methods combine both. The other options list specific designs (experimental, observational) mixed with broad approaches.

8.

A. Exploration, description, community diagnosis
B. Cohort study, needs assessment, survey
C. Case control, description, exploration
D. Correlation study, prediction, historical research
Show Answer

Correct Answer: D. Correlation study, prediction, historical research

Explanation: While the options are slightly mixed, historical research is strictly a time dimension (past). Most standard texts classify by time as Cross-sectional (one point) vs Longitudinal (over time). However, Historical (Past), Descriptive (Present), Prediction (Future) is a valid time-based classification.

11.
You have a number of clients with mental illness and you are therefore required to establish perpetuating factors to mental illness. These will include:
A. Family history of mental illness, gender
B. Imbalance of neurotransmitters, substance abuse
C. Environmental stress, substance abuse
D. Inadequate family support, poor access to health care

Correct Answer: D. Inadequate family support, poor access to health care

Explanation: Perpetuating factors are those that maintain the illness or prevent recovery. Lack of support and poor healthcare access keep the patient sick. A is Predisposing. C is Precipitating.

12.
The ego defense mechanism displayed by a woman who bears an unwanted child and unconsciously dislikes the child while consciously overprotective of the child is:
A. Sublimation
B. Displacement
C. Rationalization
D. Reaction formation
Show Answer

Correct Answer: D. Reaction formation

Explanation: Reaction formation involves converting unwanted or dangerous thoughts/impulses into their opposites. Here, unconscious hate is converted into conscious overprotection.

13.
You have received a suicidal patient in the clinic. What would be your main action?
A. Nurse the patient in a secluded room
B. Discourage the patient from expressing negative feelings
C. Maintain suicide caution and observe client regularly
D. Keep the patient under sedation at all times to avoid suicidal ideation.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C. Maintain suicide caution and observe client regularly

Explanation: Safety is the priority. Continuous observation (suicide watch) prevents the act. Seclusion (A) increases risk by isolation. Discouraging feelings (B) is therapeutic error. Constant sedation (D) is not a long-term solution.

15.

16.

Section 2: Short Answer Questions (40 Marks)

41.
Explain "FINER" as a criteria used in the selection of the research problem. (5 Marks)
Show Answer

Answer:

  • F - Feasible: Can it be done? (Resources, time, expertise available).
  • I - Interesting: Is the topic intriguing to the researcher and peers?
  • N - Novel: Does it provide new information or confirm/refute previous findings?
  • E - Ethical: Can the study be conducted without harming subjects and with board approval?
  • R - Relevant: Does it matter to scientific knowledge, clinical practice, or future research?

43.
IMNCI is a program that improves care of children and new-borns. Explain the five (5) step process of case management in IMNCI. (5 Marks)
Show Answer

Answer:

  1. Assess: Check for danger signs, ask about main symptoms, examine the child.
  2. Classify: Use the color-coded triage system (Pink, Yellow, Green) based on assessment.
  3. Identify Treatment: Determine specific treatments based on classification.
  4. Treat: Administer first dose, teach mother to give drugs/fluids at home.

44.
You have been attached to the Orthopaedic section and you note that fractures in children are on the increase. As a team, you decide to conduct a health talk on family planning. Outline five (5) specific nursing interventions on children with fracture of the tibia that would be part of the discussion? (5 Marks)
Show Answer

Answer: (Note: The question prompt mentions "health talk on family planning" but asks for "interventions on children with fracture". The answer addresses the fracture interventions).

  1. Immobilization: Maintain cast/splint care, ensure limb is elevated to reduce swelling.
  2. Pain Management: Administer analgesics and use non-pharmacological methods (distraction).

  5. Nutrition: Encourage high protein and calcium diet for bone healing.

Section 3: Long Answer Question (20 Marks)

49.
Mr. DK aged 35 years is brough to the psychiatric ward accompanied by his elder brother with allegations of being violent, wandering and inability to sleep. A diagnosis of Bipolar I disorder (manic phase) is made on admission. Outline the mode of admission that would be used to admit him then describe his specific management and care within 72 hours of admission. (20 marks)
Show Answer

Answer Guide:

1. Mode of Admission (2 Marks)

  • Involuntary Admission (Emergency): Since Mr. DK is reported to be violent, wandering, and likely lacks insight (typical of mania), he poses a danger to himself or others. Admission is likely done under the Mental Health Act (involuntary) by a relative or police/medical officer.

2. Immediate Management (0-24 Hours) - Safety & Stabilization

  • Safety/Security: Search patient for harmful objects. Nurse in a low-stimulus environment (seclusion if extremely violent) to reduce agitation.
  • Chemical Restraint (Rapid Tranquillization): Administer prescribed antipsychotics (e.g., Haloperidol, Olanzapine) and Benzodiazepines (e.g., Diazepam) to control aggression and induce sleep.
  • Physical Health: Monitor vital signs. Ensure hydration and nutrition (finger foods if too hyperactive to sit).

3. Ongoing Management (24-72 Hours)

  • Pharmacotherapy: Initiate Mood Stabilizers (e.g., Lithium Carbonate or Sodium Valproate) once acute agitation settles. Continue antipsychotics.
  • Nursing Care:
    • Communication: Use firm, calm, consistent approach. Do not argue with delusions. Keep directions simple.
    • Hygiene: Supervise ADLs; manic patients often neglect self-care.
    • Sleep: Reduce evening stimuli, give warm milk, ensure quiet environment to restore sleep pattern.
    • Elimination: Monitor for constipation (common with dehydration/meds).

4. Monitoring & Assessment

  • Observe for side effects of medication (EPS).
  • Monitor response to treatment (decrease in hyperactivity/aggression).
  • Begin engaging in therapeutic nurse-patient relationship as patient calms.
