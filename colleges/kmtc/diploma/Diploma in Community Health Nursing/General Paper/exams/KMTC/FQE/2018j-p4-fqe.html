Part I: Multiple Choice Questions (20 Marks)

1.
Appendices in a research report consist of:
a) Data collection instruments, copy of research permit, letters of approval
b) Copy of research permit, references, histograms
c) Letters of approval, budget, data collection tool
d) References, budget, copy of research permit
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Data collection instruments, copy of research permit, letters of approval

Explanation: Appendices contain supplementary material that is too detailed for the main text but supports the study. This includes the tools (questionnaires), official permits, approval letters, and maps. References form their own section preceding the appendices, not inside them.

2.
The scale measurement with an absolute zero is:
a) Ratio
b) Internal (Interval)
c) Norminal (Nominal)
d) Ordinal
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Ratio

Explanation: A Ratio scale has all the properties of interval scales plus a true, absolute zero point (e.g., weight, height, pulse rate), meaning "zero" indicates the total absence of the variable.

3.
Graphic methods of predisposing (presenting) data includes:
a) Pie charts, frequency tables, polygons
b) Bar charts, linear graphs, histograms
c) Venn diagrams, maps, curvilinear graphs
d) Curves, pictograms, scatter diagrams
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Bar charts, linear graphs, histograms

Explanation: These are standard graphic methods for presenting quantitative research data. Option A includes "frequency tables," which are tabular, not graphic. Option C/D include logic/schematic diagrams (Venn) or less common statistical plots.

4.
The main sources of hypothesis are:
a) Scientific theory, analogies, problem statement
b) Scientific theory, general culture, problem statement
c) Analogies, literature review, personal experience
d) Analogies, general culture, scientific theory
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Analogies, literature review, personal experience

Explanation: Hypotheses are derived from theories, personal observation/experience, previous studies (literature), and analogies. The problem statement is what the hypothesis aims to address, not necessarily the "source" material itself.

5.
The following are ethical issues pertaining to research subjects which the researcher is supposed to observe:
a) Freedom from harm, plagiarism, informed and voluntary consent
b) Fraud, freedom from harm, self-respect and dignity
c) Confidentiality, plagiarism, fraud
d) Freedom from harm, informed and voluntary consent, confidentiality
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Freedom from harm, informed and voluntary consent, confidentiality

Explanation: These are the core ethical principles (Beneficence, Autonomy, Privacy). Plagiarism and Fraud (in A, B, C) are examples of *unethical* misconduct that must be avoided, not "observed" as a duty to the subject.

6.
Indicate whether the following statements are True or False:

a) Speaking loudly, writing big neat handwriting and use of visual aids make teaching meaningful

b) Projected teaching aids may not enhance learning and retention of knowledge

Show Answer
  • a) True: These are principles of effective communication and stimulus variation that enhance clarity and engagement.
  • b) False: Projected aids (visuals) are proven to significantly *enhance* retention and understanding compared to verbal instruction alone.
7.
An assessment that is generally carried out at the end of the course to assign students a course grade is called:
a) Diagnostic
b) Formative
c) Summative
d) Contemporary
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Summative

Explanation: Summative assessment evaluates student learning at the end of an instructional unit by comparing it against some standard or benchmark (e.g., Final Exam). Formative (B) is ongoing.

8.
In teaching, stimulus variation is done in order to:
a) Maintain the attention of the learners, give an opportunity for praising...
b) Provide a review of the points of presentation...
c) Maintain interest of the learners, maintain the attention of the learners, avoid repetitive presentation style...
d) Avoid repetitive presentation, promote thinking...
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Maintain interest of the learners, maintain the attention of the learners, avoid repetitive presentation style...

Explanation: Stimulus variation (changing voice, movement, gestures, aids) is primarily used to capture and sustain student attention and interest, preventing boredom.

9.
The components of the body of a lesson plan entails the following:
a) Topic, level of learners, broad objective...
b) Topic, duration of the lesson, level of the learners...
c) Specific objectives, date, duration of the lesson...
d) Specific objectives, teaching method, evaluation, content, learners activity
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Specific objectives, teaching method, evaluation, content, learners activity

Explanation: The "body" (matrix) of the lesson plan typically contains the columns for Time, Objectives, Content, Learning Activities, Resources, and Evaluation. Options A, B, C list administrative details (header info).

10.
Criterion-referenced assessment is described as:
a) An assessment in which an individual's score is compared with the scores of a group...
b) The assessment carried out at the end of the term...
c) The extent to which an assessment performance is related to another valued measure
d) An assessment intended to measure how well an individual has learned a specific subject/topic or skill
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) An assessment intended to measure how well an individual has learned a specific subject/topic or skill

Explanation: Criterion-referenced tests measure performance against a fixed set of standards (criteria), not against other students (Norm-referenced - Option A).

11.
Local bacterial infection in a sick young infant aged 1 week up to 2 months is characterized by the following:
a) Red umbilicus, convulsions
b) Red umbilicus, skin pustules
c) Pus draining from the ear, fever
d) Skin pustules, fever
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Red umbilicus, skin pustules

Explanation: In IMCI for young infants (0-2 months), "Local Bacterial Infection" is classified by: Red umbilicus draining pus (or redness extending to skin), or Skin pustules. Convulsions and Fever indicate "Very Severe Disease".

12.
A child with mumps is best managed by:
a) Cold compression, isolation, antibiotics
b) Anti-inflammatory drugs, fluids, bed rest
c) Analgesics, antibiotics, bed rest
d) Fluids, bed rest, analgesics
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Fluids, bed rest, analgesics

Explanation: Mumps is viral, so antibiotics (A, C) are ineffective. Management is supportive: isolation, rest, hydration, and pain relief (analgesics) for parotitis.

13.
Follow-up of a child treated for pneumonia is done after:
a) 1 day
b) 2 days
c) 3 days
d) 4 days
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) 2 days

Explanation: IMCI guidelines require a follow-up visit in 2 days for pneumonia to assess breathing and response to antibiotics.

14.
An infant with confirmed meningitis may be treated with:
a) Chloramphenicol 50mg/kg every six hours
b) Ampicillin 25mg/kg every six hours
c) Benzyl penicillin 100,000 units every six hours
d) Ceftriaxone 50 mg/kg I.V every 12 hours
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Ceftriaxone 50 mg/kg I.V every 12 hours

Explanation: Ceftriaxone is a third-generation cephalosporin with excellent CSF penetration, making it a first-line choice for bacterial meningitis. It usually has a once or twice daily dosing schedule.

15.
Hirschsprung's disease is caused by:
a) Abdominal wall defect
b) Absence of ganglion cells precursors in the distal bowel
c) Imperforate anus due to embryologic defects
d) Out pouching of the distal ileum
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Absence of ganglion cells precursors in the distal bowel

Explanation: Hirschsprung's (Congenital Aganglionic Megacolon) is the absence of parasympathetic ganglion cells in the myenteric plexus of the distal colon, preventing peristalsis.

16.
The state in which a mental patient experiences persecutory delusion and excessive grandeur is:
a) Catatonic schizophrenia
b) Paranoid schizophrenia
c) Manic state
d) Depressive state
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Paranoid schizophrenia

Explanation: The paranoid subtype of schizophrenia is characterized by the presence of prominent delusions (persecution or grandeur) and auditory hallucinations, with relative preservation of cognitive functioning/affect.

17.
Speech disorder include:
a) Presence of speech, flight of ideas, illusion.
b) Circumstantiality, amnesia, confabulation.
c) Pressure of speech, flight of ideas, retardation of speech.
d) Perseveration, flight of ideas, paranoid ideas
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Pressure of speech, flight of ideas, retardation of speech.

Explanation: These terms specifically describe the *rate, flow, and quantity* of speech (or thought process manifested as speech). Amnesia/Illusion/Paranoia are disorders of memory, perception, or thought content.

18.
Displacement is an ego defense mechanism where the person:
a) Directs aggression towards subordinate.
b) Replaces unacceptable feelings with their exact opposite
c) Directs aggressiveness unto competitive business
d) Covers his or her weakness by emphasizing a desirable trait
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Directs aggression towards subordinate.

Explanation: Displacement involves shifting impulses (like anger) from a threatening target to a safer, less threatening one (e.g., kicking the dog instead of yelling at the boss).

19.
The principles of psychiatric nursing include:
a) Empathy, accepting the patient as he is, spontaneity, accountability
b) Awareness of basic human needs, permissiveness and firmness, creativity, active listening
c) Shared activities, sympathy, empathy, therapeutic relationship
d) Self-understanding, sympathy, awareness of basic human needs, strictness
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Empathy, accepting the patient as he is, spontaneity, accountability

Explanation: Key principles include Acceptance (non-judgmental), Empathy (not sympathy), Self-awareness, and Consistency. Option A lists the core therapeutic attitudes.

20.
The following are the principles of management of substance abuse:
a) Detoxification, empathy, awareness of basic human needs, social re-integration, communication skills
b) Detoxification, behavioral therapy, recreational therapy, family therapy, vocational rehabilitation
c) Detoxification, rehabilitation, relapse prevention, after care, social rehabilitation
d) Detoxification, excursions, giving minor responsibilities, social rehabilitation, relapse prevention
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Detoxification, rehabilitation, relapse prevention, after care, social rehabilitation

Explanation: Management follows a logical sequence: Detox (medical stabilization) -> Rehabilitation (therapy/skills) -> Relapse Prevention -> Aftercare/Reintegration.

Part II: Short Answer Questions (40 Marks)

1.
State five (5) specific nursing management you would give to an infant with trachea-oesophageal atresia (5 marks)
Show Answer

Answer:

  1. Maintain Airway: Keep infant in a supine position with head elevated (30-45 degrees) to prevent reflux and aspiration.
  2. Suctioning: Continuous or frequent suctioning of the upper pouch (mouth/nose) to clear excessive secretions.
  3. NPO Status: Withhold all oral feedings immediately to prevent aspiration pneumonia.
  4. Hydration: Establish IV access and administer maintenance fluids/nutrition as ordered.
  5. Comfort/Temp Control: Maintain thermoregulation in an incubator and minimize crying to reduce air swallowing.
2.
Explain three (3) major phases of research proposal (6 marks)
Show Answer

Answer:

  1. Conceptual Phase (Planning): Identifying the problem, reviewing literature, formulating hypothesis, and defining variables.
  2. Design and Planning Phase: Selecting research design, identifying population/sample, developing data collection tools, and planning data analysis.
  3. Empirical Phase (Pilot): Obtaining ethical approval and conducting a pilot study to test tools before full data collection. (Note: A proposal typically covers Chapters 1-3: Introduction, Literature Review, Methodology).
3.
State four (4) components of an effective research abstract. (4 marks)
Show Answer

Answer:

  1. Background/Purpose: Brief statement of the problem and study objectives.
  2. Methods: Summary of design, sample, and data collection procedures.
  3. Results: Key findings and statistical significance.
  4. Conclusion/Recommendations: Main implications of the findings.
4.
Explain three (3) ethical issues in research concerning the subject (6 marks)
Show Answer

Answer:

  1. Informed Consent: Subjects must have full knowledge of the study's purpose, risks, and benefits and voluntarily agree to participate without coercion.
  2. Confidentiality/Anonymity: The researcher must protect the subject's identity and personal data from unauthorized disclosure.
  3. Beneficence (Do No Harm): The study must maximize benefits and minimize physical, psychological, or social harm to the subject.
5.
Outline four (4) qualities of an educational objective (4 marks)
Show Answer

Answer: (SMART)

  1. Specific: Clearly states the behavior to be learned.
  2. Measurable: The outcome can be observed or quantified.
  3. Attainable/Achievable: Realistic for the learner's level.
  4. Relevant: Aligned with the curriculum goals.
  5. (Bonus: Time-bound).
6.
Outline five (5) disadvantages of simulation as a teaching method (5 marks)
Show Answer

Answer:

  1. Expensive to set up (mannequins, equipment, labs).
  2. Time-consuming to prepare and conduct.
  3. Requires specialized training for instructors.
  4. May not perfectly replicate real-life unpredictability (artificiality).
  5. Can be stressful for students if not briefed/debriefed properly.
7.
State five (5) principles of learning (5 marks)
Show Answer

Answer:

  1. Readiness: Learners must be physically and mentally prepared.
  2. Motivation: Learning is more effective when the learner desires to learn.
  3. Active Involvement: Learning is an active process (doing).
  4. Individual Differences: People learn at different rates and ways.
  5. Reinforcement/Feedback: Positive feedback strengthens learning.
  6. (Bonus: Repetition/Practice).
8.
Using the acronym "CAGE", outline how it is used in screening and diagnosing alcoholism. (5 marks)
Show Answer

Answer: The CAGE questionnaire asks:

  1. C - Cut down: Have you ever felt you needed to Cut down on your drinking?
  2. A - Annoyed: Have people Annoyed you by criticizing your drinking?
  3. G - Guilty: Have you ever felt Guilty about drinking?
  4. E - Eye-opener: Have you ever felt you needed a drink first thing in the morning (Eye-opener) to steady your nerves or get rid of a hangover?
  5. (Scoring: Two or more "Yes" answers indicate clinically significant alcohol problems).

Part III: Long Answer Questions (40 Marks)

1.
Mr. Z a college student aged 20 years is brought to your outpatient clinic having survived a grenade terror attack whilst aboard a bus three months ago. A diagnosis of post-traumatic stress disorder is made:
a) State other four (4) possible causes of this condition other than the above. (4 marks)
b) State four (4) clinical features he may present with. (4 marks)
c) Describe his management for the first 72 hours. (12 marks)
Show Answer

Answer Guide:

a) Causes of PTSD (4 Marks)

  • Sexual assault/Rape.
  • Natural disasters (floods, earthquakes).
  • Serious accidents (car crashes).
  • Physical assault/Torture/Kidnapping.
  • Combat exposure/War.

b) Clinical Features (4 Marks)

  • Intrusion symptoms: Flashbacks, nightmares, intrusive thoughts of the event.
  • Avoidance: Avoiding places, people, or thoughts related to the trauma.
  • Negative alterations in cognition/mood: Guilt, detachment, inability to feel happiness.
  • Hyperarousal: Hypervigilance, exaggerated startle response, insomnia, irritability.

c) Management in First 72 Hours (12 Marks)

  • Immediate Safety: Ensure Mr. Z is in a safe environment. Assessing for suicide risk is priority #1.
  • Crisis Intervention (Psychological First Aid):
    • Listen actively and allow him to vent feelings (catharsis).
    • Validate his feelings ("It's okay to feel this way").
    • Reassure him of his safety now.
  • Pharmacology: Administer prescribed anxiolytics (e.g., Benzodiazepines) or sleep aids if severe insomnia/anxiety is present, to provide rest.
  • Support System: Involve family/friends. Educate them on PTSD.
  • Basic Needs: Ensure nutrition and hydration.
  • Referral: Arrange for specialized trauma counseling (CBT/EMDR) once stabilized.
2.
Master x, aged 3 years has been brought to the outpatient department and has been triaged according to ETAT + guidelines:
a) Define triage. (2 marks)
b) State six (6) signs that can be used to categorize the child as a priority case. (6 marks)
c) Describe how to asses for the Emergency signs using the "ABCD" approach. (12 marks)
Show Answer

Answer Guide:

a) Define Triage (2 Marks)

Triage is the process of rapidly screening sick children upon arrival at the hospital to identify those with emergency signs (requiring immediate treatment), priority signs (requiring urgent assessment), and non-urgent cases (can wait).

b) Priority Signs (3Ps) (6 Marks)

(Think: "Tiny baby, Temperature, Trauma, Pallor, Poisoning, Pain, Respiratory distress, Restless, Referral, Malnutrition, Oedema, Burns")

  1. Tiny baby (< 2 months).
  2. Temperature (Very high fever).
  3. Trauma/Urgent Referral.
  4. Pallor (Severe).
  5. Poisoning.
  6. Severe Pain.
  7. Respiratory Distress (not severe).
  8. Restless, Irritable, or Lethargic.
  9. Malnutrition (Visible severe wasting).
  10. Oedema of both feet.
  11. Burns.

c) Assessment of Emergency Signs (ABCD) (12 Marks)

  • A - Airway & Breathing:
    • Look for obstructed breathing.
    • Look for central cyanosis.
    • Look for severe respiratory distress (head nodding, severe indrawing).
  • B - Breathing (continued above): Is the child breathing? If not, manage airway.
  • C - Circulation:
    • Check for cold hands with:
    • Capillary Refill Time (CRT) > 3 seconds.
    • Weak and fast pulse.
  • C - Coma/Convulsion:
    • Is the child convulsing now?
    • Is the child unconscious (AVPU score - only responds to Pain or Unresponsive)?
  • D - Dehydration (Severe):
    • Lethargic or unconscious?
    • Sunken eyes?
    • Skin pinch goes back very slowly (>2 secs)?
