The
Diploma in Kenya Registered Community Health Nursing (KRCHN) is a three-year program that integrates three major areas of training: General Nursing, Community Health Nursing, and Midwifery. 
The curriculum is divided into modules or trimesters covering the following key subject areas: 
1. Basic Sciences & Pre-Clinical Subjects

    Anatomy and Physiology: Study of the human body structure and functions.
    Microbiology & Parasitology: Study of microorganisms and parasites that cause disease.
    Pharmacology: Introduction to drugs, their administration, and effects.
    Nutrition: Basics of human nutrition and therapeutic diets.
    Behavioral Sciences: Sociology and psychology as applied to healthcare. 

2. Clinical Nursing Subjects

    Medical-Surgical Nursing: Care of patients with various medical conditions and those undergoing surgery.
    Pediatric Nursing: Healthcare and nursing management for infants and children.
    Psychiatry/Mental Health Nursing: Nursing care for individuals with mental health disorders.
    Theatre Nursing: Skills for perioperative care and assisting in operating rooms.
    Casualty and Emergency Nursing: Managing acute injuries and medical emergencies. 

3. Community Health & Midwifery

    Community Health Nursing: Focus on preventive, promotive, and rehabilitative healthcare at the community level.
    Midwifery/Reproductive Health: Maternal and child health, including antenatal, labor, and postnatal care.
    Epidemiology: Study of disease patterns and control within populations.
    Primary Health Care (PHC): Principles of accessible healthcare services. 

4. Research and Management

    Nursing Research: Methods and application of evidence-based practice.
    Health Services Management (HSM): Leadership, management, and health system principles.
    Communication Skills & IT: Basic professional communication and computer literacy.
