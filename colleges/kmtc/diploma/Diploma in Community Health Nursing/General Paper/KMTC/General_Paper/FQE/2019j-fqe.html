Section 1: Multiple Choice Questions (40 Marks)

1.
Anonymity in research is aimed at:
a) Keeping names of respondents known to the research supervisor only
b) Protecting disclosure of privileged information
c) Ensuring protection of the source of information
d) Selectively disclosing information to specific individuals
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Ensuring protection of the source of information

Explanation: Anonymity means the data cannot be linked to the respondent by anyone, including the researcher. This ensures the source is protected. Option A describes confidentiality (where the researcher knows but keeps it secret).

2.
The aim of identifying learners' needs in the process of teaching and learning is to:
a) Enhance sequence in presentation
b) Promote time management
c) Enhance learners' motivation
d) Induce interaction
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Enhance learners' motivation

Explanation: When teaching addresses the specific needs and gaps of the learner, the content becomes relevant, which significantly boosts motivation to learn (Adult learning principle).

3.
The sum total of all learning activities and experiences a learner undertakes in a learning institution from admission till qualifying is a:
a) Curriculum
b) Syllabus
c) Lesson plan
d) Scheme of work
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Curriculum

Explanation: The curriculum encompasses the totality of student experiences (academic, social, practical) planned by the institution. A syllabus is just the content list.

4.
Restrictions in the study that may decrease credibility of the findings are:
a) Study limitations
b) Study Outcomes
c) Study variables
d) Study inferences
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Study limitations

Explanation: Limitations are potential weaknesses in the study (e.g., small sample size, bias) that are out of the researcher's control and may affect the generalizability or credibility of results.

5.
Variables are best described as:
a) Concepts and relational statements that present view of phenomena
b) The abstract and theoretical bases for a study
c) Concepts and relational statements with a theoretical meaning
d) Concepts at various levels that are measured, manipulated or controlled
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Concepts at various levels that are measured, manipulated or controlled

Explanation: A variable is a characteristic or quality that can take on different values (vary) and can be measured (e.g., age) or manipulated (e.g., drug dosage).

6.
An in-depth investigation in research of an individual, group, institution or phenomena is called:
a) Case study
b) Action research
c) Descriptive study
d) Retrospective study
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Case study

Explanation: Case studies involve a detailed, intensive analysis of a single unit (person, group, event) to understand complex issues in a real-life context.

7.
The main difference between experimental and non-experimental studies in research is:
a) The researcher manipulates the independent variable in an experimental study
b) The researcher is an active agent in experimental study
c) The researcher manipulates the dependent variable in experimental study
d) The researcher manipulates the dependent variable in non-experimental study
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) The researcher manipulates the independent variable in an experimental study

Explanation: In experiments, the researcher intervenes (manipulates the independent variable) to observe the effect. In non-experimental studies, they observe naturally occurring variables without intervention.

8.
In the treatment of diarrhoea at home as per the IMCI guidelines, the mother is advised to:
a) Reduce the number of times of breastfeeding to give room for ORS
b) Continue giving fluids until the diarrhoea stops
c) Stop giving fluids if the child vomits
d) Stop giving fluids if the child looks well hydrated
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Continue giving fluids until the diarrhoea stops

Explanation: IMCI Plan A (Home management) rules: 1. Give extra fluid (as much as the child will take), 2. Continue feeding (do not reduce breastfeeding), 3. Give Zinc, 4. When to return. Fluids should continue until diarrhea stops to prevent dehydration. If vomiting occurs, wait 10 mins and give slower.

9.
An instructional objective is stated in terms of:
a) Process performance
b) Institutional performance
c) Learner performance
d) Teacher performance
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Learner performance

Explanation: Behavioral objectives must state what the *learner* will be able to do at the end of instruction, not what the teacher does.

10.
The following are examples of quantitative research:
a) Descriptive, Historical, Phenomenological
b) Historical, Outcomes, Quasi-experimental
c) Correlational, Experimental, Historical
d) Descriptive, Correlational, Experimental
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Descriptive, Correlational, Experimental

Explanation: These are standard quantitative designs dealing with numbers/statistics. Historical and Phenomenological (in A, B, C) are qualitative methods.

11.
The health message you will share with a patient who is discharged on Haloperidol 10mg orally twice daily, will be:
a) Take the medication 1 hour before a meal
b) Decrease the dosage if signs of illness decrease
c) Apply a sunscreen before exposure to the sun
d) Increase the dosage up to 50mg twice per day if signs of illness don't decrease
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Apply a sunscreen before exposure to the sun

Explanation: Haloperidol (and other phenothiazines/antipsychotics) can cause photosensitivity, making the skin prone to severe sunburn.

12.
The goal of crisis intervention is:
a) To solve the clients problems for him
b) Psychological resolution of the immediate crisis
c) To establish a means of long term therapy
d) To provide a means for admission to an acute care facility
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Psychological resolution of the immediate crisis

Explanation: Crisis intervention aims to restore the individual to their pre-crisis level of functioning. It is short-term and focused on the immediate problem.

13.
The advice a nurse to an alcoholic who wants to deal with his alcohol addiction, is tell the client that the only effective treatment for alcoholism is:
a) Psychotherapy
b) Total abstinence
c) Alcoholic Anonymous
d) Aversion therapy
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Total abstinence

Explanation: For true addiction/dependence, controlled drinking is rarely successful. Total abstinence is widely regarded as the cornerstone of effective recovery.

14.
The best intervention for a client having panic attack is:
a) Involve the client in a physical activity
b) Offer a distraction such as music
c) Remain with the client
d) Teach the client relaxation technique
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Remain with the client

Explanation: During a panic attack, the client is terrified and may feel they are dying. The priority is safety and security. Staying with the client provides reassurance. Teaching (D) cannot happen during high anxiety.

15.
The overall goal of psychiatric rehabilitation is for the client to gain:
a) Control of symptoms
b) Freedom from hospitalization
c) Management of anxiety
d) Recovery from the illness

Correct Answer: d) Recovery from the illness

Explanation: The modern "Recovery Model" focuses on helping the patient achieve their optimal level of functioning, independence, and quality of life, despite the illness.

18.
The client who is involuntarily committed to an inpatient psychiatric unit loses the following rights:

22.
Priority signs in management of paediatric emergencies are:
a) Convulsions, severe dehydration, high temperature
b) Central cyanosis, Convulsions, severe pain
c) Small baby less than two months, convulsions, high temperature
d) Severe pain, signs of dehydration, convulsions
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Small baby less than two months, convulsions, high temperature

Explanation: According to ETAT guidelines, "Priority signs" (3Ps) include: Tiny baby (<2mo), Temperature (hot), Trauma, Pallor, Poisoning, Pain, Respiratory distress, Restless/Irritable, Referral, Malnutrition, Oedema, Burns. While Convulsions is technically an *Emergency* sign (ABCD), this specific grouping in option C fits the typical exam pattern for "conditions needing urgent attention" often grouped together in older texts. *Correction*: Strictly speaking, Convulsions is Emergency (E). High Temp is Priority (P). Small Baby is Priority (P). This question mixes them. However, based on KMTC patterns, C is the likely intended answer due to the specific inclusion of "Small baby".

23.
The percentage of burns in a child aged 2 years whose lower limbs are burnt is:
a) 12%
b) 14%
c) 15%
d) 16%
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) 14% (per leg)

Explanation: Using the Lund and Browder chart or modified Rule of Nines for children: An infant's leg is ~14%. (Adult leg is 18%). As the child grows, the head size percentage decreases and legs increase. At 2 years, the head is still relatively large, so legs are smaller than adults. 13-14% per leg is the standard estimate.

24.
Measles contributes to malnutrition because it causes:
a) Malabsorption, vomiting, pneumonia
b) Stomatitis, conjunctivitis, stridor
c) Ear infection, high fever, dysentery
d) Diarrhoea, high fever, mouth ulcers
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Diarrhoea, high fever, mouth ulcers

Explanation: Measles causes mouth ulcers (stomatitis) making eating painful, high fever (increased metabolic rate/catabolism), and often diarrhea (nutrient loss), creating a perfect storm for rapid malnutrition.

25.
An intravenous fluid suitable for treatment of severe dehydration in children is:
a) Normal saline
b) Half strength darrows solution
c) Ringers lactate solution
d) 10% dextrose solution
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Ringers lactate solution

Explanation: Ringer's Lactate (Hartmann's Solution) is the preferred isotonic crystalloid for resuscitation in severe dehydration as it corrects acidosis and replaces electrolytes. Normal Saline is an alternative.

26.
An elevation of body temperature in a child with congestive cardiac failure is as a result of:
a) Insulating effect of edema and electrolyte imbalance
b) Increased heat production and respiratory acidosis
c) Heat dissipation as a result of diminished cardiac output
d) Increased interstitial fluid and muscular activity
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Heat dissipation as a result of diminished cardiac output (impaired heat dissipation)

Explanation: Diminished cardiac output causes peripheral vasoconstriction (to save blood for vital organs). This vasoconstriction prevents the skin from dissipating heat effectively, leading to a rise in core temperature. Also, increased work of breathing generates heat.

Section 3: Long Answer Question (20 Marks)

1.
Master Y, 3 years old, is admitted suffering from Laryngo-Tracheo Bronchitis (LTB)
a) Explain the pathophysiology of LTB (5 Marks)
b) Describe the management of Master Y till discharge (10 Marks)
c) Explain the preventive measures of LTB (5 Marks)
Show Answer

Answer Guide:

a) Pathophysiology of LTB (5 Marks)

LTB (Croup) is usually caused by viral infection (Parainfluenza virus). The virus infects the upper airway mucosa, spreading to the larynx, trachea, and bronchi. This causes inflammation and edema of the mucosa. Because the subglottic area in children is narrow and encased in the cricoid cartilage, swelling leads to significant airway narrowing/obstruction. This results in the characteristic barking cough, inspiratory stridor, and respiratory distress.

b) Management (10 Marks)

  • Airway/Breathing: Maintain patent airway. Nurse in upright position (high fowlers) to ease breathing. Administer humidified oxygen if saturations low.
  • Medication:
    • Nebulized Adrenaline (Racemic epinephrine) to reduce mucosal edema rapidly.
    • Corticosteroids (Dexamethasone) to reduce inflammation.
    • Antipyretics for fever.
  • Hydration: IV fluids if unable to drink due to respiratory distress. Encourage oral fluids if stable to thin secretions.
  • Environment: Keep the child calm (crying worsens obstruction). Minimal handling.
  • Monitoring: Continuous pulse oximetry, respiratory rate, checking for signs of worsening obstruction (cyanosis, severe retractions).

c) Preventive Measures (5 Marks)

  1. Immunization: Ensure vaccines (like Influenza, Measles, Hib) are up to date, as these can cause croup-like illnesses.
  2. Hygiene: Frequent hand washing to prevent viral spread.
  3. Isolation: Avoid contact with people who have Upper Respiratory Tract Infections (URTI).
  4. Nutrition: Proper nutrition (Exclusive breastfeeding <6mo, balanced diet) to boost immunity.
  5. Crowding: Avoid overcrowded/poorly ventilated areas.
