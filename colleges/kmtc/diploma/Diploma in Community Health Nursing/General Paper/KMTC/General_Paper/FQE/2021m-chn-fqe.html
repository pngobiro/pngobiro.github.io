Section 1: Multiple Choice Questions (40 Marks)

1.
The correct sequence of learning levels in affective domain according to Bloom's theory is
a) Organization, receiving, valuing, responding, characterization
b) Responding, receiving, organization, valuing, characterization
c) Valuing, responding, receiving, organization, characterization
d) Receiving, responding, valuing, organization, characterization
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Receiving, responding, valuing, organization, characterization

Explanation: This is the correct hierarchy of the Affective Domain (Krathwohl, Bloom, Masia, 1964) moving from simplest to most complex: Receiving (awareness), Responding (participation), Valuing (committing), Organization (structuring values), Characterization (internalizing values).

2.
The affective domain of learning
a) Embraces the development of motor skills and attitudes
b) Embraces the intellectual abilities of remembering and reasoning
c) Emphasizes the emotional qualities and feelings
d) Emphasizes the employment of attitudes and values
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Emphasizes the emotional qualities and feelings

Explanation: The affective domain deals with emotions, feelings, values, appreciation, motivations, and attitudes. Option B describes the Cognitive domain. Option A mixes Psychomotor (motor skills) with Affective.

3.
Indicate whether the following statements are true or false:

a) Guidance has no role in evaluation in teaching

b) Demonstration is best method in teaching attitude

Show Answer

Answer:

  • a) False: Guidance plays a role in formative evaluation by helping students identify gaps and improve.
  • b) False: Demonstration is best for psychomotor skills. Attitudes are best taught through role modeling, role play, or discussion.
4.
As a principle of teaching, individualization means
a) Engaging the learners individually in tuition to accommodate slow learners
b) Varying teaching methods to accommodate each learner's speed and compatibility
c) The teacher should teach the subject alone
d) Giving feedback to and individual learner for confidentiality
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Varying teaching methods to accommodate each learner's speed and compatibility

Explanation: Individualization in teaching means catering to individual differences (learning styles, pace, needs) by using diverse methods and strategies, not necessarily teaching students one-on-one (A).

5.
Reinforcement of positive behavior should be done
a) In 30 minutes
b) In 40 minutes
c) Immediately
d) When the person who did well asks
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Immediately

Explanation: In behaviorism, immediate reinforcement connects the behavior to the reward most effectively, strengthening the association.

6.
The sum total of all learning activities and experiences a learner undertakes in a learning institution from admission till qualifying is referred to as
a) Syllabus
b) Curriculum
c) Scheme of work
d) Course plan
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Curriculum

Explanation: Curriculum represents the totality of student experiences (planned and unplanned) in the educational process. A syllabus (A) is a list of topics. A scheme of work (C) is a teacher's plan.

7.
Methods of teaching attitude include
a) Role play, discussion, lectures
b) Role play, discussion, practice
c) Symposium, lectures, discussion
d) Lectures, symposium, providing information
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Role play, discussion, practice

Explanation: Attitudes are best taught via experiential and social methods. Role play allows experiencing others' perspectives. Discussion allows exploring values. Practice reinforces behavior. Lectures (in A, C, D) are generally ineffective for changing attitudes.

8.
The appropriate approach a teacher should use when teaching adults is
a) Pedagogy
b) Andragogy
c) Practice
d) Demonstration
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Andragogy

Explanation: Andragogy (Knowles) is the art and science of helping adults learn, focusing on self-direction and experience. Pedagogy (A) refers to teaching children.

9.
A lesson plan is useful because
a) Students are able to use it during classes
b) It enables the teacher to be systematic in his/her presentation
c) It is a blue print of teaching problems identified by the teacher
d) It provides guidelines on curriculum issues
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) It enables the teacher to be systematic in his/her presentation

Explanation: A lesson plan serves as a roadmap for the instructor, ensuring a logical flow, covering objectives, and managing time effectively.

10.
In relation to assessment of skills, OSCE stands for
a) Objective Structured Clinical Examination
b) Objective Simplified Clinical Examination
c) Objective Skillslab Clinical Examination
d) Objective Simulated Clinical Examination
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Objective Structured Clinical Examination

Explanation: OSCE is the standard acronym for this type of practical assessment in health education.

11.
Hospitals established by Dorothea Dix were designed to provide which of the following
a) Asylum
b) Confinement
c) Therapeutic Milieu
d) Public Safety
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Asylum

Explanation: Dorothea Dix was a reformer who advocated for the humane treatment of the mentally ill, leading to the establishment of state psychiatric hospitals, which were then known as "Asylums" (places of safety/refuge), replacing prisons and poorhouses.

12.
Psychiatric rehabilitation focuses on
a) Clients strength
b) Medication compliance
c) Social skills deficits
d) Symptom reduction
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Clients strength

Explanation: The recovery model and psychiatric rehabilitation emphasize building upon the client's existing strengths and abilities to achieve their goals, rather than just focusing on deficits or symptoms.

13.
The client belief that news broadcast has special meaning for him or her is an example of
a) Abstract thinking
b) Flight of ideas
c) Ideas of reference
d) Thought broadcast
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Ideas of reference

Explanation: Ideas of reference involve the false belief that irrelevant occurrences or details in the world (like TV news) relate directly to oneself. Thought broadcast (D) is the belief others can hear one's thoughts.

14.
The state in which the patient repeats words or phrases spoken by another is
a) Echopraxia
b) Dejavu
c) Echolalia
d) Confabulation
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Echolalia

Explanation: Echolalia is the parrot-like repetition of words spoken by others. Echopraxia (A) is repetition of movements. Confabulation (D) is filling memory gaps with fantasy.

15.
Misinterpretation of an external stimuli is referred to as
a) Delusion
b) Hallucination
c) Disorientation
d) Illusion
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Illusion

Explanation: An illusion is a false perception or misinterpretation of a real external stimulus (e.g., seeing a coat rack as a person). A hallucination (B) has no external stimulus.

16.
Causes of organic mental illness include
a) Genetic, Alcohol, Stress
b) Trauma, Meningitis, Alcohol
c) Genetic, Cultural, Malaria
d) Genetic, Alcohol, Malaria
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Trauma, Meningitis, Alcohol

Explanation: Organic mental disorders have a demonstrable physical/physiological cause (e.g., Head trauma, Infection like Meningitis, Substance toxicity like Alcohol). Stress and Culture (in A, C) are functional/environmental factors.

17.
The legal document /form filled during emergency admission of a mental patient is
a) MOH 312
b) MOH 614
c) MOH 613
d) MOH 638
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) MOH 613

Explanation: In the Kenyan context, MOH 613 is typically used for involuntary/emergency psychiatric admissions (Mental Health Act forms). MOH 205 is outpatient.

18.
Atypical conventional antipsychotic drugs include
a) Chlorpromazine, Risperidone
b) Chlorpromazine, Clozapine
c) Chlorpromazine, Haloperidol
d) Haloperidol, Risperidone
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Chlorpromazine, Risperidone

Correction/Note: The question asks for "Atypical conventional" which is a contradiction. "Atypical" means 2nd generation; "Conventional" means 1st generation.
A contains Conventional (Chlorpromazine) + Atypical (Risperidone).
B contains Conventional (Chlorpromazine) + Atypical (Clozapine).
C contains Conventional (Chlorpromazine) + Conventional (Haloperidol).
D contains Conventional (Haloperidol) + Atypical (Risperidone).
If the question meant "Examples of antipsychotics include", any option works. If it meant "Atypical AND Conventional", A, B, or D work. Given typical exam patterns, it might simply be asking to identify the group containing an atypical. Risperidone/Clozapine are atypicals. C contains ONLY conventionals. This question is poorly phrased.

19.
The nurse is teaching a psychiatric client about her prescribed drugs chlorpromazine and Benztropine. Benztropine is administered
a) To reduce psychotic symptoms
b) To reduce extrapyramidal symptoms
c) To control nausea and vomiting
d) To relief anxiety
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) To reduce extrapyramidal symptoms

Explanation: Benztropine (Cogentin) is an anticholinergic used to treat medication-induced Extrapyramidal Side Effects (EPS) like tremors and rigidity caused by antipsychotics like Chlorpromazine.

20.
A state that is progressive to permanent intellectual impairment due to organic change in the brain is
a) Depression
b) Dementia
c) Delirium
d) Mania
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Dementia

Explanation: Dementia is characterized by chronic, progressive, and often permanent cognitive decline due to brain disease. Delirium (C) is acute and reversible.

21.
Classification of severe dehydration in a child include
a) Lethargy, sunken eyes, inability to drink
b) Restlessness, eager to drink, sunken eyes
c) Skin pinch goes back slowly, irritable, eager to drink water
d) Sunken eyes, ability to drink, irritable
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Lethargy, sunken eyes, inability to drink

Explanation: Severe Dehydration signs (IMCI): Lethargic/Unconscious, Sunken eyes, Unable to drink/drinking poorly, Skin pinch goes back very slowly. B and C describe "Some Dehydration".

22.
The most appropriate management of a child with severe malnutrition who is in a state of shock includes
a) Vitamin A, Intravenous fluids, Antipyretics
b) Prevention of hypoglycemia, Antibiotics, Antipyretics
c) High protein diet, Intravenous fluids, Warmth
d) Intravenous fluids, high calorie diet, Antibiotic
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Intravenous fluids, high calorie diet, Antibiotic

Explanation: Caution: Standard IMCI/WHO guidelines advise AGAINST IV fluids for severe malnutrition unless there is shock with lethargy/unconsciousness, because the heart is weak and fluid overload is fatal. However, if shock is present, careful IV fluids (half-strength Darrow's/Ringer's with glucose) are indicated alongside antibiotics and hypoglycemia prevention. Option D is the best fit for "Shock management" context among choices, but normally oral/NG ReSoMal is preferred if no shock.

23.
Cleft palate in an infant born at term is best repaired at the age of
a) 3 months
b) 24 months
c) 3 -6 months
d) 18 months
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) 18 months

Explanation: Cleft palate is typically repaired between 9-18 months (before speech develops significantly). Cleft lip is repaired earlier (3-6 months). Option D is the best fit for palate.

24.
The conditions associated with cataract in children are
a) Diabetes insipidus, Downs syndrome
b) Congenital rubella, Trachoma
c) Trachoma, Galactocemia
d) Downs syndrome, Trachoma
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Congenital rubella, Trachoma

Explanation: Congenital Rubella Syndrome is a classic cause of congenital cataracts. Trachoma causes corneal scarring/blindness but not typically cataracts, but in the context of eye pathology options, Rubella is the strong key here.

25.
The nurse will question an order of lumbar puncture of a child with
a) Leukemia
b) Encephalitis
c) Increased intracranial pressure
d) Meningitis
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Increased intracranial pressure

Explanation: Lumbar puncture is contraindicated in patients with raised ICP (e.g., from a mass lesion) because the sudden release of pressure can cause brain herniation (coning), which is fatal.

26.
The priority nursing intervention for a 4 year old brought to casualty convulsing is
a) Check for signs of shock
b) Confirm whether the child is breathing and give oxygen
c) Give per rectal diazepam 0.5mg/kg
d) Check for airway obstruction and place in lateral position
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Check for airway obstruction and place in lateral position

Explanation: Priority in convulsions is Airway and Safety. Placing the child in a lateral (recovery) position prevents aspiration of saliva/vomit and maintains a patent airway. Drugs (C) come after airway is secured.

27.
The nursing care for a child with advanced cystic fibrosis will include
a) Providing postural drainage after meals at bedtime
b) Administering high levels of oxygen
c) Providing frequent skin care
d) Administering pancreatic enzymes between meals
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Providing frequent skin care

Correction: The classic intervention for CF is Pancreatic Enzymes, but they must be given *with* meals (not between, D is wrong). Postural drainage is done *before* meals (not after, A is wrong). High oxygen can be dangerous (CO2 retention). Skin care (C) is generally good but less specific. However, given the flaws in A/B/D, C is a safe general nursing care option, or the question implies "Enzymes with meals" and D is the intended (but slightly misphrased) answer. Actually, chest physiotherapy is key. *Best available answer:* Likely C or D (if interpreted loosely).

28.
When performing physical examination on a 2-year-old, the nurse will
a) Carefully explain all procedures at this time
b) Begin with the least intrusive procedure
c) Proceed in a head to toe examination
d) Ask the parent to leave the room so that the child can give full attention to the nurse
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Begin with the least intrusive procedure

Explanation: For toddlers, exam sequence should be modified to reduce anxiety. Start with non-threatening areas (hands, feet) and do intrusive checks (ears, mouth) last. Head-to-toe (C) is for older children.

29.
In emergency triage and treatment (ETAT) of children, emergency signs are
a) Central cyanosis
b) Tiny child below 3 months, referral case
c) Convulsions, vomiting everything, central cyanosis
d) Severe dehydration, vomiting, lower limb oedema
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Convulsions, vomiting everything, central cyanosis

Explanation: ETAT Emergency signs (ABCD): Airway/Breathing (Cyanosis), Circulation, Coma/Convulsions, Dehydration (Severe). Option C includes two clear emergency signs (Convulsions, Cyanosis).

30.
Indicate whether the following statements are true or false

a) In qualitative research, information is mainly collected without formal structured instruments

b) In quantitative research, the investigators participants in the event under study

Show Answer

Answer:

  • a) True: Qualitative research uses flexible, semi-structured, or unstructured tools (interview guides).

31.
The degree of consistence with which an instrument yields similar results is
a) Validity
b) Reliability
c) Objectivity
d) Sensitivity
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Reliability

Explanation: Reliability refers to the consistency or stability of a measure (getting the same result on repeated trials). Validity (A) is accuracy.

32.
The main difference between experimental and non-experimental studies in research is that
a) The researcher is a passive observer in an experimental study
b) The researcher manipulates the independent variable in non-experimental study
c) The researcher is an active participant in experimental study
d) The researcher manipulates the dependent variable in experimental study
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) The researcher is an active participant in experimental study

Correction: The standard difference is that in Experimental studies, the researcher manipulates the Independent Variable. Option B says "manipulates in non-experimental" (False). Option D says "manipulates dependent" (False - dependent is outcome). Option C is the closest valid statement if interpreted as "active agent of change/intervention", though technically they manipulate variables, not necessarily "participate".

33.
The term "editing" in data processing refers to
a) Assigning numerals or other symbols to answers

d) Arranging assembled data into concise and logical order
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Examining the collected raw data to detect errors and omissions

Explanation: Editing involves cleaning the data: checking for completeness, consistency, and accuracy before coding (A) or classification (B).

34.
The main purpose of publishing research findings is to
a) Meet the requirement of the sponsor
b) Solicit for more funds for future research
c) Get recognition in the profession
d) Contribute to scientific literature

Correct Answer: d) Contribute to scientific literature

35.
The type of research that focusses on finding a solution to an immediate clinical problem is termed as
a) Basic research
b) Applied research
c) Explotory research
d) Descriptive research
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Applied research

Explanation: Applied research is designed to solve practical problems of the modern world, rather than to acquire knowledge for knowledge's sake (Basic research).

36.
Hawthorn effect in research means
a) People behave differently when they know they are being observed
b) The researchers getting involved in the activities they are observing
c) The first impression created by the researcher towards the subjects
d) The ability to produce same results using same circumstances
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) People behave differently when they know they are being observed

Explanation: The Hawthorne effect is a type of reactivity in which individuals modify an aspect of their behavior in response to their awareness of being observed.

37.
In research, probability sampling involves selection of:
a) Subjects for convenience sake
b) Individual at regular intervals from sampling frame
c) Categories of study units such that a certain number appears in the sample
d) Procedures to ensure that each unit of the sample is chosen on the basic of chance
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Procedures to ensure that each unit of the sample is chosen on the basic of chance

Explanation: Probability sampling requires that every member of the population has a known, non-zero chance (random selection) of being included.

38.
Calculate the mean in the following sample values: 3, 8, 6, 14, 0, -4, 0, 12, -7, 0, -10
a) 2
b) 2.5
c) 3.3
d) 4.0
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) 2

Explanation: Sum: 3 + 8 + 6 + 14 + 0 + (-4) + 0 + 12 + (-7) + 0 + (-10) = 22.
Count (n): 11 items.
Mean = Sum / n = 22 / 11 = 2.

39.
The demerits of longitudinal research include
a) It is more effective, more information will be collected
b) It is more expensive, more time is required
c) Large sample can be covered, less dropout rate
d) More time is required, more information will be collected
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) It is more expensive, more time is required

Explanation: Longitudinal studies follow subjects over a long period, making them costly, time-consuming, and prone to high attrition (dropout) rates.

a) Demonological period

45.
Outline FIVE features of obsessive compulsory disorder (5 marks)
Show Answer

Answer:

  1. Obsessions: Recurrent, intrusive, unwanted thoughts.
  2. Compulsions: Repetitive behaviors or mental acts performed to reduce anxiety.
  3. Anxiety/Distress: Significant distress if rituals are prevented.
  4. Time-consuming: Rituals take up >1 hour/day.
  5. Insight: Patient often recognizes thoughts are irrational (unlike psychosis).

48.

Section 3: Long Answer Question (20 Marks)

49.
Masha a 9-year-old is admitted in a paediatric ward with a diagnosis of acute glomerulonephritis
a) Draw and label a diagram illustrating structure of the nephron (5 marks)
b) Outline FIVE signs and symptoms Masha may present with (5 marks)
c) Describe the management of Masha in the first 48 hours (10 marks)
Show Answer

Answer Guide:

a) Diagram of Nephron (5 Marks)

(Student should draw and label: Glomerulus, Bowman's Capsule, Proximal Convoluted Tubule, Loop of Henle, Distal Convoluted Tubule, Collecting Duct).

b) Signs and Symptoms (5 Marks)

  1. Haematuria: Tea-colored or cola-colored urine.
  2. Edema: Especially periorbital (puffy eyes) in the morning.
  3. Hypertension: High blood pressure.
  4. Oliguria: Decreased urine output.
  5. Proteinuria: Foamy urine (mild to moderate).
  6. Fatigue/Lethargy.

c) Management in First 48 Hours (10 Marks)

  • Rest: Strict bed rest during the acute phase to conserve energy and reduce metabolic waste.
  • Fluid Management: Strict Input/Output monitoring. Fluid restriction may be ordered (Output + insensible loss) if oliguric/edematous. Daily weighing.
  • Diet: Low sodium diet (to control edema/BP). Low protein if azotemia (high BUN) is present. High calorie carbohydrates.
  • Medication:
    • Antihypertensives: Diuretics (Furosemide) or vasodilators to control BP.
    • Antibiotics: Penicillin if evidence of active Streptococcal infection exists.
  • Monitoring: Vital signs (BP every 4 hours), observe for complications (Hypertensive encephalopathy, heart failure).
