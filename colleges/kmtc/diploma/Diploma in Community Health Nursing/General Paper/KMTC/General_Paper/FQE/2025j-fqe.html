Section 1: Multiple Choice Questions (40 Marks)

1.
While assessing a mentally ill patient in the psychiatry ward, a student nurse was asked to assess for delusions. Which response from the student shows that she understands the instruction?
A. A false visual perception of a real stimuli
B. A false perception affecting the five common senses
C. A false belief inconsistent with a patient's culture
D. An abnormal thought process affecting the patient's speech
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C. A false belief inconsistent with a patient's culture

Explanation: A delusion is defined as a fixed, false belief that is not consistent with the patient's cultural or educational background and cannot be corrected by reasoning. A and B describe illusions or hallucinations.

2.
A client is diagnosed with a bipolar disorder. In order to treat the manic episodes associated with this disorder, the drug of choice is:
A. Chlorpromazine
B. Phenobarbitone
C. Levodopa
D. Lithium carbonate
Show Answer

Correct Answer: D. Lithium carbonate

Explanation: Lithium Carbonate is the classic "Mood Stabilizer" and is the first-line drug of choice for treating acute mania and preventing recurrence in Bipolar Disorder.

3.
The drug of choice in the management of an alcoholic with Korsakoff syndrome is
A. Thiamine
B. Disulfram
C. Benzodiazepines
D. Choloroliazeopoxide (Librium)
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A. Thiamine

Explanation: Korsakoff's syndrome (and Wernicke's encephalopathy) is caused by severe Vitamin B1 (Thiamine) deficiency due to alcoholism. High-dose parenteral Thiamine is the specific treatment.

4.
Paranoid schizophrenia is characterized by
A. Excessive negativism
B. Stupor
C. Delusions of persecution
D. Manic episodes
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C. Delusions of persecution

Explanation: The hallmark of paranoid schizophrenia is the presence of prominent delusions (often persecutory or grandiose) and auditory hallucinations, while cognitive function remains relatively preserved. A and B are features of Catatonic schizophrenia.

5.
During your shift in the psychiatric unit, a newly admitted patient starts to fight others without any provocation. The preferred drugs for this patient are:
A. Imipramine, Clopixol
B. Haloperidol, Amitriptyline
C. Clonazepam, Imipramine
D. Lithium carbonate, Chlorpromazine
Show Answer

Correct Answer: D. Lithium carbonate, Chlorpromazine

Explanation: The patient exhibits unprovoked aggression and likely mania. Chlorpromazine (a typical antipsychotic) provides rapid sedation and control of psychosis. Lithium is the mood stabilizer of choice for mania. Option B contains Amitriptyline (antidepressant), which could worsen mania. Option A contains Imipramine (antidepressant).

6.
The student asked the nurse why the patient was receiving a dose of Artane yet is not anti-psychotic drug. The correct response from the nurse was;
A. To prevent orthostatic hypotension.
B. To prevent extrapyramidal symptoms.
C. To prevent concurrent seizures.
D. To prevent hypertensive crisis.

Correct Answer: B. To prevent extrapyramidal symptoms.

Explanation: Artane (Trihexyphenidyl) is an anticholinergic drug prescribed to prevent or treat the Extrapyramidal Side Effects (EPS) such as stiffness and tremors caused by antipsychotic medications.

A. Analytic research
B. Quantitate research
C. Experimental research
D. Qualitative research

Correct Answer: D. Qualitative research

Explanation: Qualitative research focuses on non-numerical data such as words, experiences, and descriptions, rather than discrete numbers/statistics.

9.
The choice of hypothesis method to be used largely depends on:
A. The study area
B. The sample size
C. The problem statement
D. The purpose of study
Show Answer

Correct Answer: D. The purpose of study

Explanation: The hypothesis is a prediction derived from the study's purpose and problem statement. The specific method or type of hypothesis depends on what the study aims to achieve (the purpose).

10.
A research lecturer is having a lesson with 2nd year students on sampling methods. The students understood that the sampling methods that make use of probability theory are:
A. Quota, cluster
B. Snowball, cluster
C. Convenience, systematic random
D. Cluster, simple random
Show Answer

Correct Answer: D. Cluster, simple random

Explanation: Probability sampling requires random selection where every member has a chance of being picked. Cluster and Simple Random are probability methods. Quota, Snowball, and Convenience are Non-probability methods.

11.
In nursing research, the following is most important for ensuring the validity of a study;
A. Random sampling
B. Control groups
C. Large sample size
D. Conventional sampling
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A. Random sampling

Explanation: Random sampling ensures the sample is representative of the population, thereby ensuring External Validity (generalizability). Control groups (B) help with Internal Validity in experiments.

Explanation: The primary goal of critiquing research is to determine if the findings are valid, reliable, and applicable to clinical practice (Evidence-Based Practice).

17.
Active participation is achieved by;
A. Students giving feedback to the teacher after demonstration
B. Students performing various activities
C. Setting projects for students
D. Telling learners how they could have done better
Show Answer

Correct Answer: D. Telling learners how they could have done better

Correction: Active participation is best achieved by "B. Students performing various activities". However, in teaching methodology exams, giving feedback (D) is a form of reinforcement that *encourages* participation. But logically, B is the definition of active participation. If the question implies "How does a teacher ENSURE active participation", setting projects (C) or making them perform (B) is correct. Given KMTC patterns, B is the strongest distractor, but D (Feedback) is often cited as a key teacher action to *sustain* it.

19.
A 7-year-old child is brought to the emergency department with complaints of difficulty breathing and stridor. The child's parents report that the symptoms started suddenly and have been worsening over the past hour. Upon examination, you note inspiratory stridor and retractions. The diagnosis is:
A. Croup
B. Asthma exacerbation
C. Bronchiolitis
D. Epiglottitis
Show Answer

Correct Answer: D. Epiglottitis

Explanation: Sudden onset, rapid progression (within hours), stridor, and respiratory distress in a 7-year-old are classic signs of Epiglottitis (medical emergency). Croup usually has a slower onset and affects younger children. Asthma presents with wheezing, not stridor.

41.
Differentiate between hallucinations and delusions, providing examples of each. (5 marks)
Show Answer

Answer:

  • Hallucinations: False sensory perceptions occurring in the absence of any external stimulus. They can affect any sense.
    • Example: Hearing voices commanding to do something (Auditory hallucination).
  • Delusions: Fixed, false beliefs that are not consistent with the patient's cultural background and cannot be corrected by reasoning.
    • Example: Believing one is the President or God (Delusion of Grandeur).

Section 3: Long Answer Question (20 Marks)

49.
Mr. D 32-year-old, a manager is admitted in a psychiatry ward and history reveals that he has been abusive, had pressure of speech, euphoria, and irritable for the last one week. During history taking he starts driving imaginary vehicle calling it 'the Beast' while pretending to be happy to meet American president elect. The significant other says that he is always restlessness rarely sleeps. Describe the management from admission until the patient is integrated back to the community. (20 marks)
Show Answer

Answer Guide:

Diagnosis: Bipolar I Disorder (Acute Manic Episode). Symptoms: Euphoria, pressure of speech, irritability, delusions of grandeur (meeting president), restlessness, lack of sleep.

1. Admission Phase

  • Mode of Admission: Likely involuntary due to lack of insight and disruptive behavior.
  • Assessment: Mental Status Exam (MSE), physical exam to rule out organic causes (drug screen).
  • Safety: Search for harmful items. Place in a quiet, low-stimulus room to reduce agitation.

2. Pharmacological Management

  • Mood Stabilizers: Start Lithium Carbonate (gold standard) or Valproate/Carbamazepine.
  • Antipsychotics: Administer Olanzapine or Haloperidol (chemical restraint) to control acute agitation and delusions ("the Beast").
  • Benzodiazepines: Lorazepam/Diazepam for sedation and sleep promotion.

3. Nursing Management

  • Communication: Use firm, calm, short sentences. Do not argue with delusions but present reality gently. Set limits on abusive/intrusive behavior.
  • Nutrition: Provide high-calorie, high-protein finger foods (patient is too restless to sit). Ensure hydration.
  • Sleep/Rest: Reduce environmental stimuli. Encourage rest periods. Administer sedatives as ordered.
  • Hygiene: Supervise ADLs (manic patients often neglect grooming or dress inappropriately).

4. Psychological/Therapeutic Phase

  • Psychotherapy: Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (CBT) once stabilized to identify triggers.
  • Group Therapy: To improve social skills and reality testing.

5. Rehabilitation & Reintegration (Discharge Planning)

  • Family Therapy: Educate family on the illness, medication compliance, and signs of relapse (lack of sleep).
  • Occupational Therapy: Assess ability to return to work (he is a manager); stress management.
  • Follow-up: Link with outpatient clinic and community mental health team.
