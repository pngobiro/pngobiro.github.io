Part 1: Multiple Choice Questions (20 Marks)

1.
The second step in clotting mechanism is:
a) Coagulation
b) Platelet pluggin
c) Fibrin formation
d) Vessel constriction
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Platelet plugging

Explanation: Hemostasis involves three main steps: 1. Vascular Spasm (Vessel constriction), 2. Platelet Plug Formation, 3. Coagulation (Fibrin formation).

2.
Indicate whether the following statements are True or False

a) Arthrology is the study of joints

b) Kinesiology is the study of body movement

Show Answer
  • a) True: Arthrology (from Greek arthron, joint) is the anatomy/study of joints.
  • b) True: Kinesiology is the scientific study of human or non-human body movement.
3.
The risk factors for cataract formation include:
a) Age, Trauma, Metabolic Disturbances, Systemic Diseases
b) Eye Strain, Trauma, Metabolic Disturbances, Hypertension
c) Diabetes Mellitus, Systemic Diseases, Eye Strain, Hypertension
d) Eye Strain, Age, Metabolic Disturbances, Trauma
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Age, Trauma, Metabolic Disturbances, Systemic Diseases

Explanation: Ageing is the most common cause. Trauma, metabolic issues (Diabetes/Hypocalcemia), and systemic diseases (or steroid use) are major risk factors. Eye strain does not cause cataracts.

4.
Multiple sclerosis is a progressive disease of the nervous system due to:
a) An autoimmune disorder of the myelin sheath in central nervous system.
b) An autoimmune disorder affecting the myo-neural junction
c) Is an autoimmune attack of the peripheral nerve myelin
d) Degeneration of the nerves in the peripheral nerves system.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) An autoimmune disorder of the myelin sheath in central nervous system.

Explanation: MS is characterized by demyelination in the CNS (Brain/Spinal Cord). Option B describes Myasthenia Gravis. Option C/D describe Guillain-Barre or Peripheral Neuropathy.

5.
The holding instruments used in a general set include:-
a) Artery forceps, needle holder, armynavy
b) Alice, Littlewood, artery forceps
c) Armynavy, probe, artery forceps
d) Probe, Littlewood, artery forceps
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Alice, Littlewood, artery forceps

Explanation: "Holding" or "Grasping" instruments include Allis (Alice) tissue forceps, Littlewood (tissue forceps), and Artery forceps (hemostats). "Armynavy" is a retractor.

6.
The purpose for abdominal Paracentesis is :
a) Diagnostic examination of kidney , relieve of pressure
b) Diagnostic examination of ascitic fluid , relieve of pressure
c) Diagnostic examination of gall bladder , relieve of pressure
d) Diagnostic examination of liver, relieve of pressure
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Diagnostic examination of ascitic fluid , relieve of pressure

Explanation: Abdominal paracentesis involves draining fluid (ascites) from the peritoneal cavity for analysis (diagnostic) or to relieve abdominal pressure/dyspnea (therapeutic).

7.
Acne vulgaris is associated with :
a) Blockage of the sweat glands
b) Poor hygiene, and malnutrition
c) Fungi infection that arises within the hair follicles
d) Occlusion of pilosebaceous ducts
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Occlusion of pilosebaceous ducts

Explanation: Acne is a chronic inflammatory disease of the pilosebaceous units (hair follicles + sebaceous glands), characterized by blockage (comedones) and inflammation.

8.
Organs that can be palpated on the left hypochondriac region include:-
a) Stomach, gallbladder, Kidney
b) Gall bladder, kidney, spleen
c) Kidney, spleen, stomach
d) Kidney , liver, stomach
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Kidney, spleen, stomach

Explanation: The Left Hypochondriac region contains the Spleen, part of the Stomach, the left Kidney (upper pole), and the Splenic flexure of the colon. The Gallbladder/Liver are on the Right.

9.
Effective traction is achieved by:-
a) Avoiding the line of the pull on the traction to relieve pain
b) Resting weights on the bed or floor for proper alignment
c) Resultant line of pull being in the opposite direction with long axis of the bone
d) Allowing weights to hang freely at all times
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Allowing weights to hang freely at all times

Explanation: For traction to be effective, the weights must hang freely and not touch the bed or floor. Counter-traction is also essential.

10.
Common sites of endometriosis include:
a. Rectal vaginal septum, ovary, Broad ligament
b. Ovary, ureter, urinary bladder
c. Broad ligament, appendix, uterosacral ligaments
d. Rectal vaginal septum, utero sacro ligament, ureters
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Rectal vaginal septum, ovary, Broad ligament (or C, depending on frequency)

Explanation: The most common sites are the Ovaries (chocolate cysts), Uterosacral ligaments, Pouch of Douglas, and Broad Ligaments. Option A and C both list common pelvic sites.

11.
Haemophilia A results from deficiency of factor:
a. VIII
b. IX
c. III
d. V
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a. VIII

Explanation: Hemophilia A is Factor VIII deficiency. Hemophilia B (Christmas Disease) is Factor IX deficiency.

12.
The nursing diagnosis that best describes a patient with psoriaisis who has quit her job as a receptionist because she feels her appearance is disgusting to customers is:
a) Ineffective coping related to loss of skin integrity
b) Social isolation related to loss of skin integrity
c) Impaired skin integrity related to presence of lesions
d) Anxiety related to lack of knowledge of the disease process
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Ineffective coping related to loss of skin integrity (or b)

Explanation: Quitting a job is a maladaptive behavior indicating an inability to cope with the psychosocial impact of the disease. "Social isolation" (B) is also valid, but quitting a job is an active (though negative) coping mechanism/withdrawal.

13.
Non absorbable sutures include:
a) vicryl, silk
b) chromic catgut, monofilament nylon
c) vicryl, chromic catgut
d) silk, monofilament nylon
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) silk, monofilament nylon

Explanation: Silk and Nylon (Polyamide) are non-absorbable. Vicryl (Polyglactin) and Catgut are absorbable.

14.
The accumulation of thick, purulent fluid within the pleural space with fibrin development is characteristic of :-
a) Empyema
b) Effusion
c) Pleurisy
d) Emphysema
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Empyema

Explanation: Empyema is the collection of pus in the pleural cavity. Effusion is fluid (can be serous). Pleurisy is inflammation.

15.
The Nursing Council of Kenya (NCK) is established under The Nurses Act Cap.
a) 275
b) 248
c) 257
d) 256
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) 257

Explanation: The Nurses Act Cap 257 of the Laws of Kenya establishes the NCK.

16.
The etiology of the nursing diagnosis, “impaired skin integrity related to surgical incision, evidenced by incision wound”, is:
a) Situational
b) Maturational
c) Treatment
d) Patho-physiologic
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Treatment (or a) Situational)

Explanation: The etiology (cause) is the "surgical incision". Surgery is a treatment modality. Therefore, the impairment is treatment-related (Mechanical factor). It is not maturational (age) or pathophysiologic (disease). "Situational" implies external factors.

17.
The restricted zone in theatre refers to area:
a) In the operating room where scrub attire and surgical masks are required
b) In the operating room that interfaces with other theatre
c) Where only the surgical team can access
d) Where patients who have undergone special surgery are confined
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) In the operating room where scrub attire and surgical masks are required

Explanation: The restricted zone includes the OR itself and scrub sinks. Full surgical attire (scrubs, head cover, mask) is mandatory.

18.
An allergic reaction is:
a) A delayed or cellular reaction that occurs 1 to 3 days after exposure to an antigen.
b) A Clinical response to an immediate immunologic reaction between a specific antigen and antibody.
c) An abnormal heightened reaction to a stimulus of any kind.
d) Inappropriate and often harmful response of the immune system to normally harmless substances.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Inappropriate and often harmful response of the immune system to normally harmless substances. (Or C)

Explanation: Allergy (Hypersensitivity) is defined as an exaggerated or inappropriate immune response to an antigen (allergen) that is usually harmless.

19.
Complications of tension pneumothorax include:
a) Pleurisy due to friction of the two membranes
b) Accumulation of blood and air in the pleural space
c) Paradoxical chest movement which leads to severe distress
d) Shifting of the heart and other mediastinal structures to the opposite side
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Shifting of the heart and other mediastinal structures to the opposite side

Explanation: The hallmark of tension pneumothorax is the buildup of pressure that pushes the mediastinum (trachea, heart) away from the affected side, compressing the healthy lung and reducing venous return.

20.
During resuscitation, appropriate chest compression in adults are:
a) 2 breaths to 30 compressions
b) 2 breaths to 20 compressions
c) 1 breaths to 30 compressions
d) 1 breaths to 20 compressions
Show Answer

Correct Answer: 30 compressions to 2 breaths (Standard Protocol). Option A says "2 breaths to 30 compressions" (Sequence is C-A-B, usually 30:2). The question asks for ratio. Standard is 30:2.

Note: Option A is the correct *ratio* values, even if stated "Breaths to Compressions". The standard is 30 compressions then 2 breaths.

6.
Explain three (3) nursing interventions for a patient with penetrating chest injury (6marks)
Show Answer

Answer:

  1. Cover the wound: Immediately apply an occlusive dressing (taped on 3 sides) to prevent air entering the pleural space (sucking chest wound).
  2. Positioning: Place patient in Semi-Fowler's or high Fowler's position to facilitate breathing (if not contraindicated by spine injury).
  3. Oxygen Therapy: Administer high-flow oxygen to maintain saturation.
  4. Chest Tube Management: Assist with insertion and monitor underwater seal drainage if pneumothorax/hemothorax develops.

8.
Describe artesunate injection using the following headlines:
i. Classification (1mark)
ii. Indication (1mark)
iii. Adult dose (1mark)
iv. side effects (2mark)
v. 2 nursing responsibilities (2marks)
Show Answer

Answer:

  1. Classification: Antimalarial (Artemisinin derivative).
  2. Indication: Severe Malaria.
  3. Adult dose: 2.4 mg/kg body weight IV or IM (at 0, 12, 24 hours).
  4. Side effects: Dizziness, neutropenia, elevated liver enzymes, hemolysis.
  5. Nursing responsibilities:
    • Reconstitute with the correct solvent (bicarbonate) then saline/dextrose.
    • Administer slowly IV push (over 2-3 mins).
    • Monitor for signs of hypersensitivity.

Part Three-Long Answer Questions-40 marks

1.
Mrs. Magigukok 65 years old has been suffering from osteoporosis and developed lumbar vertebral damage. She is for follow up in the orthopedic clinic.
a) State five (5) individual risk factors that may have predisposed her to osteoporosis. (5 marks)
b) Explain three (3) clinical features she may present with (6 marks)
c) Describe her specific outpatient management until she has stabilized (9 marks)
Show Answer

Answer Guide:

a) Risk Factors (5 Marks)

  1. Post-menopausal status: Lack of estrogen increases bone loss.
  2. Gender: Females are at higher risk.
  3. Age: >65 years.
  4. Low Calcium/Vitamin D intake: Dietary deficiency.
  5. Sedentary Lifestyle: Lack of weight-bearing exercise.
  6. (Others: Smoking, Alcohol, Family history, Thin body build).

b) Clinical Features (6 Marks)

  • Back Pain: Caused by collapsed vertebrae/compression fractures.
  • Loss of Height: Progressive vertebral collapse over time.
  • Kyphosis (Dowager's Hump): Curvature of the upper spine due to vertebral fractures.

c) Outpatient Management (9 Marks)

  • Pharmacology: Administer Bisphosphonates (e.g., Alendronate) to inhibit bone resorption. Calcium (1200mg) and Vitamin D (800IU) supplementation.
  • Pain Management: Analgesics (NSAIDs/Acetaminophen) and heat therapy for back pain.
  • Diet: Counsel on diet rich in calcium (dairy, greens) and Vitamin D.
  • Exercise: Encourage weight-bearing exercises (walking) to strengthen bone, but avoid high-impact/twisting.
  • Fall Prevention: Educate on removing home hazards (rugs), using rails, good lighting to prevent fractures.
  • Support: Use of back brace/corset for support if needed.
2.
Mr. K is admitted in a ward with cancer of the larynx and is for tracheostomy
a) Outline three (3) risk factors of laryngeal cancer (3marks)
b) State three (3) indications for tracheostomy (3marks)
c) Describe the specific management of Mr. K for the first 72 hours (10marks)
d) Explain two (2) complications of tracheostomy surgery (4marks)
Show Answer

Answer Guide:

a) Risk Factors (3 Marks)

  1. Tobacco Smoking.
  2. Excessive Alcohol consumption.
  3. HPV infection.
  4. Occupational exposure (asbestos, wood dust).

b) Indications for Tracheostomy (3 Marks)

  1. Upper airway obstruction (Tumor/Cancer).
  2. Prolonged mechanical ventilation.
  3. Inability to clear secretions (Bulbar palsy).

c) Management First 72 Hours (10 Marks)

  • Airway Patency: Frequent suctioning (sterile technique) as secretions will be copious. Clean inner cannula regularly.
  • Humidification: Use humidified oxygen or HME filter to prevent crusting (mucus plugs).
  • Communication: Provide a magic slate/pen and paper as patient cannot speak. Establish bell/signal system.
  • Wound Care: Clean stoma site, change dressing if soiled to prevent infection. Check for subcutaneous emphysema.
  • Monitoring: Watch for bleeding (hemorrhage) or tube displacement. Keep obturator and spare tube at bedside.
  • Positioning: Semi-Fowlers to reduce edema and ease breathing.

d) Complications (4 Marks)

  • Hemorrhage: Bleeding from the site.
  • Tube Obstruction: By mucus plugs.
  • Accidental Decannulation: Tube falling out before tract forms.
  • Subcutaneous Emphysema: Air trapped under skin.
  • Infection: Tracheitis or stoma infection.
