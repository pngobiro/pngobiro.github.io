SECTION 1: MULTIPLE CHOICE QUESTIONS (40 MARKS)

1.
The predominant ion in extracellular fluid is:
a) Sodium
b) Potassium
c) Calcium
d) Phosphate
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Sodium

Explanation: Sodium (Na+) is the major cation in the extracellular fluid (ECF). Potassium (K+) is the major intracellular cation.

2.
The avascular body structures include:
a) Teeth, Bone, Cornea
b) Pupil, Cartilage, Epidermis
c) Bone, Cornea, Nail bed
d) Hair, Teeth, Bone
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Pupil, Cartilage, Epidermis

Explanation:
- Epidermis and Cartilage are definitely avascular.
- The Pupil is an aperture (hole) in the iris, not a structure itself, but the lens behind it and the cornea in front of it are avascular.
- Options A, C, and D all contain "Bone" or "Nail bed", which are vascular (contain blood vessels). Therefore, B is the only set where the tissues mentioned are not vascularized.

5.
The characteristic of a microorganism to enter tissues and cause disease is referred to as:
a) Virulence
b) Pathogenesis
c) Invasiveness
d) Colonization
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Invasiveness

Explanation: Invasiveness specifically refers to the ability of a pathogen to enter, survive, and spread within host tissues. Pathogenicity is the general ability to cause disease. Virulence is the *degree* of pathology caused.

8.
When administering drugs, the route a nurse should consider safest is:
a) Oral route
b) Intravenous route
c) Intramuscular injection
d) Intrathecal injection
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Oral route

Explanation: The oral route is generally considered the safest because it is non-invasive, avoids the risks of infection/embolism associated with injections, and allows time for retrieval (vomiting/charcoal) in case of overdose before full absorption.

9.
Expiratory Reserve volume is reduced in patients with:
a) Ascites
b) Atelectasis
c) Paralysis
d) Pneumonia
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Ascites

Explanation: Ascites (fluid in the abdomen) pushes the diaphragm upward, restricting lung expansion and significantly reducing the Expiratory Reserve Volume (ERV) and Functional Residual Capacity (FRC). Obesity and pregnancy have similar effects.

10.
Soft, high pitched, discontinuous popping sounds that occur during inspiration in patient with lung infection are known as:
a) crackles
b) Wheezes
c) Friction rubs
d) Stridor
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) crackles

Explanation: Crackles (rales) are described as discontinuous, popping sounds (like Velcro or hair rubbing together) heard on inspiration, often indicating fluid in the alveoli (pneumonia, heart failure).

11.
An adolescent who experiences painful menses in the absence of any other pelvic pathology is likely to be suffering from:
a) Metrorrhagia
b) Primary dysmenorrhea
c) Secondary amenorrhea
d) Dysfunctional uterus
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Primary dysmenorrhea

Explanation: Primary dysmenorrhea is defined as painful menstruation without identifiable pelvic pathology, caused by excess prostaglandins. Secondary dysmenorrhea is pain with pathology (e.g., endometriosis).

12.
The common causes of ectopic pregnancy include:
a) Uterine fibroids, Cervical cancer
b) Ovarian cysts, endometriosis
c) Salpingitis, Peritubal adhesions
d) Gonorrhea, ovarian cancer
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Salpingitis, Peritubal adhesions

Explanation: Damage to the fallopian tubes is the main risk factor. Salpingitis (inflammation of tubes, often from PID) and adhesions (scarring) prevent the fertilized egg from traveling to the uterus.

13.
Patients with total stomach resection are likely to suffer from deficiency of:
a) Vitamin B6
b) Folic acid
c) Vitamin K
d) Vitamin B12
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Vitamin B12

Explanation: The stomach parietal cells secrete Intrinsic Factor, which is essential for the absorption of Vitamin B12 in the ileum. Total gastrectomy removes the source of Intrinsic Factor, leading to B12 deficiency (Pernicious Anemia).

14.

16.
Nephrotic syndrome is a primary glomerular disease characterized by:
a) Edema, Hematuria, Proteinuria, Leukocytosis
b) Hyperlipidemia, Proteinuria, Hypoalbuminemia, Hematuria
c) Proteinuria, Hypoalbuminemia, Hematuria, Leukocytosis
d) Proteinuria, Hypoalbuminemia, Hyperlipidemia, Edema
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Proteinuria, Hypoalbuminemia, Hyperlipidemia, Edema

Explanation: The classic tetrad of Nephrotic Syndrome is massive Proteinuria (>3.5g/day), Hypoalbuminemia (from protein loss), Edema (from low oncotic pressure), and Hyperlipidemia (liver compensates for protein loss). Hematuria is more characteristic of Nephritic syndrome.

32.
During Weber test, a patient who reports the sound as better in the right ear is likely to be suffering from:
a) Sensorineural hearing loss in the right hear
b) Conductive hearing loss in the right hear
c) Conductive hearing loss in the left ear
d) Vestibular damage of the right year
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Conductive hearing loss in the right hear

Explanation: In the Weber test, sound lateralizes to the affected ear in conductive loss (bone conduction > air) and to the unaffected ear in sensorineural loss. If heard better in Right, it's either Conductive Loss Right or Sensorineural Loss Left.

38.

SECTION 2: SHORT ANSWER QUESTIONS (40 MARKS)

1.
Outline five (5) predisposing factors to cerebral vascular accidents (5 Marks)
Show Answer
  1. Hypertension: The single most important risk factor.
  2. Smoking: Damages vessels and increases clotting.
  3. Diabetes Mellitus: Accelerates atherosclerosis.
  4. Atrial Fibrillation: Causes clots that embolize to the brain.
  5. Hyperlipidemia: High cholesterol leads to plaque buildup.
  6. (Alternative) Obesity / Sedentary lifestyle.
2.
Explain three (3) types of Fractures (6 Marks)
Show Answer
  1. Simple (Closed) Fracture: The bone is broken but the skin remains intact; no communication with the external environment.
  2. Compound (Open) Fracture: The broken bone penetrates the skin, creating an open wound and increasing infection risk.
  3. Comminuted Fracture: The bone is shattered into three or more fragments.
  4. (Alternative) Greenstick Fracture: Incomplete break common in children; bone bends and cracks.
3.
Outline five (5) measures you would institute as a nurse to reduce incidences of adverse drug reactions in the ward (5 Marks)
Show Answer
  1. Assessment of Allergy History: Always ask about allergies before administration.
  2. Adherence to "Rights" of Administration: Right patient, drug, dose, route, time.
  3. Monitoring: Observe patient closely after administration, especially for new drugs.
  4. Patient Education: Teach patients about side effects to report.
  5. Reconciliation: Check for drug-drug interactions.
4.
Formulate five (5) nursing diagnosis for a patient with pneumonia (5 Marks)
Show Answer
  1. Ineffective Airway Clearance r/t excessive mucus.
  2. Impaired Gas Exchange r/t alveolar fluid consolidation.
  3. Ineffective Breathing Pattern r/t chest pain/fatigue.
  4. Acute Pain (Pleuritic) r/t inflammation.
  5. Activity Intolerance r/t imbalance between O2 supply and demand.
5.
Outline five (5) clinical manifestations of thyrotoxicosis (5 Marks)
Show Answer
  1. Tachycardia/Palpitations: Rapid heart rate at rest.
  2. Heat Intolerance: Sweating and preference for cold.
  3. Weight Loss: Despite increased appetite.
  4. Exophthalmos: Bulging eyes.
  5. Tremors: Fine tremors of hands.
  6. (Alternative) Anxiety/Restlessness.
6.
Explain three (3) types of sickle cell crisis (6 Marks)
Show Answer
  1. Vaso-occlusive (Painful) Crisis: Sickled cells block blood flow to tissues causing ischemia and severe pain.
  2. Sequestration Crisis: Pooling of blood in the spleen/liver, leading to shock and anemia.
  3. Aplastic Crisis: Temporary cessation of RBC production usually due to infection (Parvovirus), leading to severe anemia.
7.
Outline four (4) practices a nurse can adopt to reduce risk of infections in operating theatre (4 Marks)
Show Answer
  1. Strict Aseptic Technique: Maintain sterile field.
  2. Surgical Hand Scrubbing: Proper duration and technique.
  3. Proper Attire: Wearing sterile gowns, gloves, masks, and caps correctly.
  4. Traffic Control: Limiting movement and number of people in the OR.
  5. Environmental Cleaning: Disinfecting surfaces between cases.
8.
List eight (8) risk factors for developing cancer of the lungs (4 Marks)
Show Answer
  1. Cigarette Smoking (Active).
  2. Passive Smoking (Second-hand smoke).
  3. Asbestos exposure.
  4. Radon gas exposure.

SECTION 3: LONG ANSWER QUESTION (20 MARKS)

1.
Mr. J is admitted in the burns unit with 60% burns following fire outbreak at Sinai slum
a) Draw and label a diagram of the skin (5 Marks)
b) Outline four (4) types of burns (4 Marks)
c) Discuss the management of Mr. J for the first 72 hours following admission (11 Marks)
Show Answer

a) Diagram of Skin:

(Student should draw and label):
- Epidermis (Stratum corneum, basal layer)
- Dermis (Hair follicles, sweat glands, blood vessels, nerve endings)
- Subcutaneous tissue (Fat)

b) Types of Burns:

  1. Thermal Burns: Caused by flame, steam, hot liquids.
  2. Chemical Burns: Caused by acids or alkalis.
  3. Electrical Burns: Caused by electrical current.
  4. Radiation Burns: Caused by UV light or X-rays.
  5. (Alternative classification by depth): Superficial, Partial Thickness, Full Thickness.

c) Management (First 72 Hours - Emergent/Resuscitative Phase):

  • Airway & Breathing: Assess for inhalation injury (soot, hoarseness). Administer 100% humidified O2. Intubate if necessary.
  • Fluid Resuscitation (Crucial): Initiate IV fluids using Parkland Formula (4ml x kg x %TBSA). Give half in first 8 hours, rest in next 16 hours. Monitor urine output (maintain 30-50ml/hr).
  • Pain Management: IV Opioids (Morphine) - avoid IM/SC due to poor absorption in shock.
  • Thermoregulation: Keep patient warm to prevent hypothermia (loss of skin barrier).
  • Wound Care: Cover with sterile sheets. Administer Tetanus prophylaxis. Escharotomy if circumferential burns compromise circulation.
  • Infection Control: Sterile technique. Prophylactic antibiotics if indicated (topical usually).
  • Nutrition: NPO initially (risk of ileus). Start Enteral feeding early to support high metabolic demand.
