SECTION 1: MULTIPLE CHOICE QUESTIONS (40 MARKS)

1.
Hypotension in acute pancreatitis is mainly caused by;
A) Profuse vomiting
B) Loss of large amounts of protein rich fluid into tissues and peritoneal cavity.
C) Bleeding into the tissues and peritoneal cavity.
D) Profuse sweating and anxiety
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B) Loss of large amounts of protein rich fluid into tissues and peritoneal cavity.

Explanation: In acute pancreatitis, inflammation causes increased capillary permeability. This leads to massive third-spacing of fluid, enzymes, and proteins into the retroperitoneal space and abdominal cavity, causing hypovolemic shock (hypotension).

2.
Clinical manifestations of neurogenic shock include;
A) Tachycardia, hypotension and dysrhythmias.
B) Hypotension, hyperthermia and tachypnea.
C) Warm dry skin, bradycardia and hypotension.
D) Hypothermia, warm dry skin and tachycardia.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C) Warm dry skin, bradycardia and hypotension.

Explanation: Neurogenic shock is characterized by the loss of sympathetic tone. This leads to massive vasodilation (causing hypotension and warm/dry skin due to blood pooling) and unopposed vagal/parasympathetic tone (causing bradycardia). This triad distinguishes it from hypovolemic shock (which has tachycardia and cool skin).

3.
Hysteroscopy is indicated in;
A) Cases of infertility and cervical carcinoma.
B) Endometrial carcinoma & infertility.
C) Unexplained per vaginal bleeding & recurrent early pregnancy loss.
D) Retained intra-uterine device & acute pelvic inflammation.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C) Unexplained per vaginal bleeding & recurrent early pregnancy loss.

Explanation: Hysteroscopy allows direct visualization of the uterine cavity to diagnose and treat structural causes of bleeding (polyps, fibroids) and uterine anomalies (septum) causing recurrent miscarriage. It is contraindicated in acute infection (PID), making D incorrect.

4.
Abnormal findings on Breast examination includes;
A) Longstanding nipple inversion, breast tenderness before menses.
B) Unilateral venous prominence, skin dimpling.
C) Lumpiness before menstruation, skin dimpling.
D) Longstanding nipple inversion, edema
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B) Unilateral venous prominence, skin dimpling.

Explanation: Unilateral increased venous pattern can indicate a fast-growing tumor requiring more blood. Skin dimpling (retraction) suggests a tumor pulling on Cooper's ligaments. Longstanding inversion is often normal; recent inversion is concerning.

C) Pelvic pain, unusual vaginal discharge and increased urinary frequency.
D) Increased urinary frequency, dyspareunia and bleeding between menstrual periods.

Correct Answer: A) Bleeding between menstrual periods, dyspareunia and prolonged bleeding.

7.
Fracture immobilization is meant to achieve the following;
A) Pain relieve, reduced movement and correction of malunion
B) Prevention of displacement, pain relieve and reduced deformities.
C) Prevent muscle spasms, bone alignment and increased circulation.
D) Reduce malformations, restore circulation and relieve pain
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B) Prevention of displacement, pain relieve and reduced deformities.

Explanation: Immobilization prevents bone fragments from moving (displacement), which prevents further soft tissue injury and significantly reduces pain (muscle spasm). It maintains alignment to prevent deformity during healing.

10.
The stratified epithelium lines the following;
A) Kidney tubules, buccal cavity and the throat.
B) Lungs, trachea and inner lining of blood vessels.
C) Eye conjunctiva, vagina and oesophagus
D) Inner lining of blood vessels, buccal cavity and lungs.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C) Eye conjunctiva, vagina and oesophagus

11.
A third degree burn involves;
A) The epidermis and a portion of the dermis.
B) Epidermis, upper dermis & a portion of deeper dermis.
C) Epidermis, entire dermis, subcutaneous & connective tissue.
D) Muscles and the bones only.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C) Epidermis, entire dermis, subcutaneous & connective tissue.

Explanation: Third-degree (full-thickness) burns destroy the entire epidermis and dermis and extend into the subcutaneous tissue. (Note: A/B are partial thickness/2nd degree; D is 4th degree).

13.

14.
While caring for a patient in the recovery room after an operation under general anaesthesia, the nurse should notify the physician if the;
A) Patient pushes out the airway.
B) Respiration is regular but shallow.
C) Systolic pressure drops from 130mmhg to 100mmhg.
D) Patient has snoring respirations.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C) Systolic pressure drops from 130mmhg to 100mmhg.

Explanation: A drop of 30 mmHg in systolic BP indicates significant hypotension/shock or hemorrhage and requires immediate notification. Pushing out airway (A) is a normal sign of waking up. Snoring (D) requires nursing intervention (airway positioning) first.

B) 3 minutes

Show Answer

Correct Answer: B) 3 minutes (or 5 minutes per quadrant depending on text)

44.
Explain three (3) nursing management for a patient with severe head injury. (6 marks)
Show Answer
  1. Airway Management: Ensure patent airway, suctioning as needed, and maintaining oxygenation (prevent hypoxia which increases ICP).

45.
Describe the pathophysiology of Asthma (5marks)
Show Answer

Answer:

  1. Exposure to Trigger: Allergen binds to IgE on mast cells.
  2. Mediator Release: Mast cells degranulate, releasing histamine, leukotrienes, prostaglandins.
  3. Inflammation: Causes mucosal edema and increased mucus production.
  4. Bronchospasm: Smooth muscles of bronchi constrict.
  5. Airway Obstruction: The combination of edema, mucus, and spasm narrows airways, trapping air (hyperinflation) and causing wheezing/dyspnea.
46.
Describe the pathophysiology of rheumatic fever (5marks)
Show Answer
  1. Infection: Starts with Group A Beta-hemolytic Streptococcus (GABHS) pharyngitis ("Strep throat").
  2. Immune Response: Body produces antibodies against Strep bacteria.
  3. Molecular Mimicry: Strep antigens resemble human tissue (heart valves, joints, brain).
  4. Autoimmune Attack: Antibodies cross-react and attack the host's own tissues.
  5. Inflammation: Leads to carditis (heart), polyarthritis (joints), chorea (brain), and skin nodules.
47.
State five (5) specific nursing responsibilities for a patient with vesicle-vaginal fistula post operatively (5 marks)
Show Answer
  1. Catheter Care: Ensure continuous drainage (free flow) to prevent bladder distension which could stress the repair line.
  2. Fluid Intake: Encourage high fluid intake (3-4L/day) to flush the bladder.
  3. Hygiene: Perineal care to prevent infection.
  4. Activity: Bed rest as prescribed, avoiding straining/heavy lifting.
  5. Monitoring: Check for hematuria or blockage of catheter.
48.
Outline five (5) factors that affect blood pressure. (5marks)
Show Answer
  1. Cardiac Output: Volume of blood pumped per minute.
  2. Peripheral Vascular Resistance: Diameter of blood vessels (constriction vs dilation).
  3. Blood Volume: Total amount of blood (hypovolemia lowers BP).
  4. Blood Viscosity: Thickness of blood (polycythemia raises BP).
  5. Elasticity of Arteries: Stiff arteries ( arteriosclerosis) increase BP.

SECTION 3: LONG ANSWER QUESTION (20 MARKS)

49.
Mr. Nininit has undergone an explorative laparotomy of the abdomen.
a) Explain the concept of peri-operative care (2 marks)
b) State four (4) types of wounds (4 marks)
c) Describe the specific care Mr. Nininit will receive post operatively for the first 24 hours (11 marks)
d) Outline three (3) complications Mr. Nininit is likely to develop. (3 marks)
Show Answer

a) Peri-operative Care:

It is the comprehensive nursing care provided to a patient throughout the entire surgical experience, encompassing three phases: Pre-operative (before surgery), Intra-operative (during surgery), and Post-operative (after surgery).

b) Types of Wounds:

  • Clean wound: Uninfected, no inflammation (e.g., surgical incision).
  • Clean-contaminated wound: Surgical incision entering GI, respiratory, or GU tracts.
  • Contaminated wound: Open, traumatic, or surgical with break in asepsis.
  • Dirty/Infected wound: Old traumatic wounds, clinical infection present.
  • (Alternative classification): Incised, Lacerated, Puncture, Abrasion.

c) Post-operative Care (First 24 Hours):

  • Airway/Breathing: Maintain patent airway, monitor O2 sat, encourage deep breathing.
  • Circulation: Monitor Vital Signs (BP, Pulse) q15min -> q30min -> q1hr to detect shock/hemorrhage. Check nail beds.
  • Fluid Management: Monitor IV fluids rate. Record Intake/Output strictly (Target urine >30ml/hr).
  • Wound Care: Check dressing for soakage/bleeding. Reinforce if needed (do not remove first dressing usually).
  • Pain Management: Administer analgesics as prescribed. Assess pain score.
  • Positioning: Semi-Fowler's (after recovery from anesthesia) to ease breathing and reduce abdominal tension.
  • GI Function: Keep NPO until bowel sounds return. Monitor for nausea/vomiting.
  • Safety: Raise bed rails.

d) Three Complications:

  1. Hemorrhage / Hypovolemic Shock.
  2. Paralytic Ileus (due to bowel handling).
  3. Wound Infection / Dehiscence.
  4. (Alternative) Deep Vein Thrombosis (DVT).
