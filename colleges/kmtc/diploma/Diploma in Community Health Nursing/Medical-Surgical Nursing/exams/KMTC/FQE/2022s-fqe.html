SECTION 1: MULTIPLE CHOICE QUESTIONS (40 MARKS)

1.
When applying the abdominal examination technique, the correct sequence is;
A) Palpation, Inspection, Percussion, Auscultation
B) Percussion, Palpation, Auscultation, Inspection
C) Inspection, Auscultation, Percussion, Palpation
D) Inspection, Percussion, Palpation, Auscultation
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C) Inspection, Auscultation, Percussion, Palpation

Explanation: For the abdomen, Auscultation is performed before Percussion and Palpation to avoid altering bowel sounds or causing discomfort that might distort the findings. The sequence IAPP (Inspection, Auscultation, Percussion, Palpation) is the standard exception for abdominal assessments (IPPA is used for other systems).

2.
The Synthesis of proteins in a bacterium cell occur in;
A) Mitochondria
B) Ribosomes
C) Nucleus
D) Capsule
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B) Ribosomes

Explanation: Ribosomes are the sites of protein synthesis (translation) in all cells, including bacteria. Bacteria do not have mitochondria or a true nucleus (they are prokaryotes).

3.
The size of the pupils of the eye influence accommodation by;
A) Dilating when the light is bright
B) Bending light rays from a distant object
C) Constricting when the light is bright
D) Bending light from near object
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C) Constricting when the light is bright

Explanation: The pupillary light reflex causes pupils to constrict in bright light to limit the amount of light entering the eye and improve focus (depth of field). (Note: Accommodation specifically refers to lens shape change, but in the context of this question's options, pupillary constriction is the correct physiological response to light).

4.
Adult cardiopulmonary resuscitations (CPR) by two rescuers is achieved by performing;
A) 30 chest compressions, 2 breaths
B) 10 chest compressions, 3 breaths
C) 15 chest compressions, 2 breaths
D) 3 breaths, 15 chest compressions
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A) 30 chest compressions, 2 breaths

Explanation: Current BLS guidelines recommend a compression-to-ventilation ratio of 30:2 for adults, regardless of whether there are one or two rescuers. (15:2 is used for children/infants with 2 rescuers).

5.
Potential complications following appendectomy includes;
A) Gastritis, pelvic abscess, inguinal hernia
B) Peritonitis, peptic ulcer, acute retention of urine
C) Intestinal obstruction, mal-absorption, Gastritis
D) Peritonitis, pelvic abscess, paralytic ileus
Show Answer

Correct Answer: D) Peritonitis, pelvic abscess, paralytic ileus

Explanation: Common post-appendectomy complications include infection (pelvic abscess, peritonitis if rupture occurred) and paralytic ileus (temporary paralysis of bowel). Gastritis and ulcers are not direct surgical complications of appendectomy.

6.
A non- infectious skin conditions characterized by inflammatory lesions and well defined large adherent silvery scale is;
A) Atopic eczema
B) Psoriasis
C) Seborrhoeic eczema
D) Erythrodermic psoriasis
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B) Psoriasis

Explanation: Plaque Psoriasis is classically described as well-demarcated erythematous plaques covered with silvery scales.

7.
Myasthenia gravis is a condition that occurs due to;
A) Loss of pigmentation of neurons
B) Myelination of the peripheral nerves
C) Lack of transmission of nerve impulses to muscle fibers
D) Increased transmission of nerve impulses the basal ganglia
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C) Lack of transmission of nerve impulses to muscle fibers

Explanation: Myasthenia Gravis is an autoimmune disorder where antibodies attack Acetylcholine receptors at the neuromuscular junction, preventing nerve impulses from triggering muscle contraction.

8.
After retrograde pyelogram a patient should take plenty of fluids in-order to;
A) Flush the bladder and prevent urinary stasis
B) Obtain specimen of urine
C) Hydrate the patient
D) Facilitate the examination of the urinary bladder
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A) Flush the bladder and prevent urinary stasis

Explanation: Fluids help flush the contrast dye (which can be nephrotoxic) out of the kidneys and urinary tract, preventing stasis and potential infection or kidney injury.

9.
Write True or False in the space provided as the correct response of the following statements.
a) The first two heart sounds 'lub dup' are most easily distinguished through the stethoscope. 'dup' occurs due to the closure of atrioventricular valves...
b) The heart is able to generate its own electrical impulses and beats independently of nervous or hormonal control...
Show Answer

a) FALSE: 'Dup' (S2) is caused by the closure of the Semilunar valves (Aortic/Pulmonary). 'Lub' (S1) is the closure of AV valves.

b) TRUE: The heart has automaticity (SA node generates impulses), though the rate is modulated by the nervous system.

10.
One of the common causes of sensorineural hearing impairment is;
A) Ototoxic drugs
B) Acute otitis media
C) Osteoclerosis
D) Tympanic membrane injury
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A) Ototoxic drugs

Explanation: Sensorineural hearing loss involves damage to the inner ear (cochlea) or auditory nerve. Ototoxic drugs (e.g., Gentamycin) damage hair cells. Options B, C, and D typically cause Conductive hearing loss.

11.
The clinical manifestation of Parkinson's disease include;
A) Fatigue, jaundice, numbness, difficulties in coordinating and loss of balance
B) Cholera, intellectual decline, emotional disturbance, fatigue and loss of postural reflexes
C) Numbness, muscular rigidity, loss of balance, fatigue, tremors and loss of vision
D) Impaired movement, muscular rigidity, tremors, muscle weakness and loss of postural reflex
Show Answer

Correct Answer: D) Impaired movement, muscular rigidity, tremors, muscle weakness and loss of postural reflex

Explanation: The classic triad of Parkinson's is Tremor (resting), Rigidity, and Bradykinesia (impaired movement), along with postural instability.

12.
A common complication of tension pneumothorax is;
A) Pleurisy due friction of the two membranes
B) Accumulation of blood and air in the pleural space
C) Paradoxical chest movement which leads to severe distress
D) Shifting of the heat and other mediastinal structures to the opposite side
Show Answer

Correct Answer: D) Shifting of the heat (heart) and other mediastinal structures to the opposite side

Explanation: Tension pneumothorax creates high pressure in the pleural space that pushes the mediastinum (heart, trachea, great vessels) away from the affected side, compressing the healthy lung and decreasing cardiac output. (Note: "heat" is a typo for "heart").

13.
The inflammation of the iris, cilliary body and the choroid plexus is known as;
A) Lephesitis
B) Keratitis
C) Uveitis
D) Choroiditis
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C) Uveitis

Explanation: The uveal tract consists of the iris, ciliary body, and choroid. Inflammation of these structures is collectively called Uveitis.

14.
Adequate intake of vitamin K in diet will prevent'
A) Anemia
B) Thrombocytopenia
C) Prothrombin deficiency
D) Leukemia
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C) Prothrombin deficiency

Explanation: Vitamin K is essential for the synthesis of clotting factors in the liver, including Prothrombin (Factor II). Deficiency leads to bleeding disorders.

15.
One of the complications of excess aldosterone in circulation is;
A) Hypotension
B) Hyponatraemia
C) Hyperkalaemia
D) Hypertension
Show Answer

Correct Answer: D) Hypertension

Explanation: Aldosterone causes kidneys to retain Sodium and Water and excrete Potassium. Excess aldosterone leads to fluid volume overload (Hypertension) and Hypokalemia. Options A, B, and C are opposite to the effects of aldosterone.

16.
Complicated wound healing presents with the following characteristics;
A) Slough, increase in wound size, pus discharge
B) Oedema, slough, blood oozing
C) Slough, fistula, oedema
D) Increase in wound size, slough, oedema
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A) Slough, increase in wound size, pus discharge

Explanation: Signs of a chronic or complicated wound (often infected) include purulent discharge (pus), necrotic tissue (slough), and failure to heal or increasing size.

17.
The non- opioid drug administered for pain management as per WHO pain management ladder are;
A) Paracetamol, brufen
B) Codeine, Morphine
C) Tramadol, paracetamol
D) DF 118, morphine
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A) Paracetamol, brufen

Explanation: Step 1 of the WHO ladder uses Non-Opioids (Paracetamol, NSAIDs like Ibuprofen/Brufen) +/- Adjuvants. Codeine and Tramadol are weak opioids (Step 2). Morphine is a strong opioid (Step 3).

18.
Wallace scoring of an adult who has sustained burns on one leg and the genitalia is;
A) 9% burn
B) 10% burn
C) 18% burns
D) 27% burns
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B) 10% burn (Assuming "anterior" leg or specific interpretation, otherwise closer to 19%)

Explanation:
Rule of Nines:
- Genitalia = 1%
- One entire Leg = 18%
Total = 19%. This option is not available.
However, if the question implies "Anterior Leg" (9%) + Genitalia (1%) = 10%, then B is the intended answer. In many local exam contexts, inconsistencies occur; 10% is the most logical sum of component parts available in the options.

19.
Primary dysmenorrhea occurs due to;
A) Endometriosis
B) Menorrhagia
C) Higher concentration of prostaglandin
D) Pelvic inflammatory diseases
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C) Higher concentration of prostaglandin

Explanation: Primary dysmenorrhea (painful menstruation without pathology) is caused by the excessive production of uterine prostaglandins (specifically PGF2a), leading to strong uterine contractions and ischemia. Options A and D are causes of Secondary dysmenorrhea.

20.
A nursing diagnosis development for a patient immediately post- operatively under general anesthesia is;
A) Risk for urinary retention related to catheterization
B) Acute pain related to surgical intervention
C) Anxiety related to complications of general anesthesia
D) Ineffective airway clearance related to depressed respiratory function
Show Answer

Correct Answer: D) Ineffective airway clearance related to depressed respiratory function

Explanation: The priority (ABC) immediately post-op is Airway and Breathing. General anesthesia depresses the respiratory drive and cough reflex, making airway clearance the most critical immediate concern.

22.

23.
Drug that is commonly associated with ototoxicity and nephrotoxicity is:-
A) Kanamycin
B) Gentamycin
C) Streptomycin
D) Vancomycin
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B) Gentamycin (and others listed)

Explanation: All Aminoglycosides (Gentamycin, Streptomycin, Kanamycin) and Vancomycin can cause ototoxicity and nephrotoxicity. However, Gentamycin is the classic and most frequently cited example in nursing exams for these specific dual toxicities.

24.
The most accurate indicator for fluid loss in an acutely sick patient is: -
A) Body weight
B) Facial expression
C) Muscle cramps
D) Lethargy
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A) Body weight

Explanation: Daily weight is the most reliable and accurate indicator of fluid volume status. 1 kg of weight gain/loss equals approximately 1 Liter of fluid.

25.
Menorrhagia of unknown origin normally affects: -
A) Calcium levels
B) Hemoglobin levels
C) Urea levels
D) Blood urea nitrogen levels
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B) Hemoglobin levels

Explanation: Heavy menstrual bleeding (menorrhagia) leads to significant blood loss, causing iron deficiency anemia and lowered hemoglobin levels.

26.
After cataract surgery, the patient is instructed to -
A) Lie in the prone position while sleeping
B) Strengthen eye muscles by chewing hard food
C) Maintain bed rest for two weeks
D) Avoid bending head below the waist.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: D) Avoid bending head below the waist.

Explanation: Bending below the waist increases intraocular pressure (IOP), which can damage the surgical site. Prone position (A) also increases IOP. Chewing hard food (B) causes jarring.

27.
Tetany is a classic characteristic manifestation of: -
A) Hyperglycemia
B) Hypocalcemia
C) Hypokalemia
D) Hypernatremia
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B) Hypocalcemia

Explanation: Low calcium levels increase neuromuscular excitability, leading to tetany (muscle spasms, twitching, Chvostek's/Trousseau's signs).

43.
Explain three (3) types of sickle cell crisis (6 marks)
Show Answer
  1. Vaso-occlusive Crisis (Painful Crisis): Sickled cells block blood flow to tissues, causing ischemia and severe pain (most common).
  2. Sequestration Crisis: Sickled cells get trapped in the spleen (or liver), causing enlargement and sudden drop in hemoglobin (shock).
  3. Aplastic Crisis: Temporary cessation of red blood cell production, often triggered by Parvovirus B19 infection, leading to severe anemia.

Show Answer
  1. Alopecia: Hair loss.
  2. Nausea and Vomiting: GI irritation and CTZ stimulation.
  3. Bone Marrow Suppression: Anemia, Leukopenia (infection risk), Thrombocytopenia (bleeding).
  4. Stomatitis/Mucositis: Inflammation of mouth/gut lining.
  5. Fatigue.

46.
Describe the pathophysiology of asthma (5 marks)
Show Answer

Answer:

  1. Trigger exposure: Allergen/trigger activates mast cells.
  2. Inflammation: Release of mediators (histamine, leukotrienes) causes edema of bronchial mucosa.
  3. Bronchospasm: Constriction of smooth muscle surrounding bronchioles.
  4. Mucus production: Increased secretion of thick mucus.
  5. Airway Narrowing: The combination of swelling, spasm, and mucus narrows airways, trapping air and causing wheezing/dyspnea.
47.
State five (5) components of the planning phase in the nursing process (5 marks)
Show Answer
  1. Prioritization: Determining which problems to address first (Maslow's).
  2. Setting Goals/Outcomes: Establishing SMART goals (Specific, Measurable, Achievable, Realistic, Timely).
  3. Selecting Nursing Interventions: Choosing evidence-based actions.
  4. Writing the Nursing Care Plan: Documenting the plan.
  5. Consulting: Coordinating with other healthcare members.
48.
State five (5) indications for hemodialysis (5 marks)
Show Answer
  1. Uremia: Symptomatic accumulation of wastes (BUN/Creatinine).
  2. Hyperkalemia: Persistent high potassium unresponsive to medical management.
  3. Fluid Overload: Pulmonary edema unresponsive to diuretics.
  4. Severe Acidosis: Metabolic acidosis (pH < 7.1).
  5. Drug Overdose/Toxicity: Removal of dialyzable toxins (e.g., aspirin, lithium).

SECTION 3: LONG ANSWER QUESTION (20 MARKS)

49.
Mrs. Ace, 24 years old is admitted to the ward due to a ruptured ectopic pregnancy
a) Define the term 'ectopic pregnancy' (1 mark)
b) State two (2) causes of ectopic pregnancy (2 marks)
c) List four (4) clinical features of ectopic pregnancy (2 marks)
d) Describe the management of Mrs. Ace till discharge (15 marks)
Show Answer

a) Definition: Implantation of a fertilized ovum outside the uterine cavity, most commonly in the fallopian tube.

b) Causes:

  • Pelvic Inflammatory Disease (PID) causing scarring.
  • Previous ectopic pregnancy.
  • Tubal surgery or sterilization reversal.
  • Endometriosis.

c) Clinical Features:

  • Abdominal pain (unilateral, sharp).
  • Amenorrhea (missed period).
  • Vaginal bleeding (spotting).
  • Signs of shock (if ruptured): Hypotension, tachycardia, shoulder tip pain (Kehr's sign).

d) Management (Ruptured = Emergency):

  • Immediate Resuscitation:
    • ABC management.
    • Insert 2 large-bore IV cannulas.
    • Rapid fluid replacement (Crystalloids/Colloids) and Blood Transfusion (Cross-match urgent).
    • Positioning (Trendelenburg/flat) to improve cerebral perfusion.
  • Surgical Management (Salpingectomy):
    • Prepare for emergency laparotomy to stop bleeding and remove the affected tube.
    • Obtain consent (if patient condition allows, or next of kin/emergency doctrine).
  • Post-Operative Care:
    • Monitor vital signs q15min until stable.
    • Manage pain.
    • Monitor incision/drainage.
    • Administer Anti-D immunoglobulin if patient is Rh Negative.
  • Psychological Support:
    • Support for pregnancy loss (grief counseling).
    • Education on future fertility.
