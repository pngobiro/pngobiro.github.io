SECTION 1: MULTIPLE CHOICE QUESTIONS (40 MARKS)

1.
The laboratory test that evaluates the blood PH is:
A. Hemoglobin level
B. Red blood cell count
C. Arterial blood gases (ABG) analysis
D. Blood culture
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C. Arterial blood gases (ABG) analysis

Explanation: ABG analysis measures the pH, oxygen (PaO2), and carbon dioxide (PaCO2) levels in arterial blood, providing a direct evaluation of the body's acid-base balance.

2.
The cause of circulatory insufficiency that can lead to shock as a result of decreased plasma volume is:
A. Penetrating trauma with blood loss.
B. Gastrointestinal bleeding.
C. Anaphylaxis because of medications.
D. All of the above
Show Answer

Correct Answer: D. All of the above

Explanation:
- A & B: Cause absolute hypovolemia (loss of whole blood).
- C (Anaphylaxis): Causes relative hypovolemia due to massive vasodilation and increased capillary permeability, leading to a loss of plasma volume from the vessels into the interstitial space.
Therefore, all listed causes lead to circulatory insufficiency related to volume issues.

3.
The mitral valve in the heart is located in:
A. Between right ventricle and pulmonary artery
B. Where left ventricle meets the aorta
C. Between left atrium and left ventricle
D. Between right atrium and right ventricle
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C. Between left atrium and left ventricle

Explanation: The mitral (bicuspid) valve separates the left atrium from the left ventricle.
- A: Pulmonary valve.
- B: Aortic valve.
- D: Tricuspid valve.

4.
The blood test most indicative of cardiac damage is:
A. Complete blood count
B. Haemoglobin A1C
C. Lactate dehydrogenase
D. Creatinine kinase
Show Answer

Correct Answer: D. Creatinine kinase

Explanation: Specifically the CK-MB isoenzyme, is a cardiac marker. While Troponin is the gold standard (not listed), among these options, Creatinine Kinase (CK) is the enzyme released during muscle damage, including heart muscle.

5.
The condition caused by long term exposure to high levels of cortisol is:
A. Addison's disease
B. Crohn's disease
C. Adrenal insufficiency
D. Cushing's syndrome
Show Answer

Correct Answer: D. Cushing's syndrome

Explanation: Cushing's syndrome results from prolonged exposure to cortisol (hypercortisolism). Addison's disease (A) and Adrenal Insufficiency (C) are caused by low levels of cortisol.

11.
The nurse is monitoring the patient who is 24 hours post-operation from surgery. Which findings requires immediate intervention:
A. BP of 100/80 mmHg
B. 24 hours urine output of 300 ml
C. Pain rating of 4 on 1-10 scale
D. Temperature of 99.3Â° F
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B. 24 hours urine output of 300 ml

Explanation: Normal urine output should be at least 0.5 ml/kg/hr or roughly 30ml/hr. 300ml in 24 hours averages to 12.5ml/hr, indicating severe oliguria and potential acute kidney injury or severe dehydration requiring immediate intervention.

12.
A potential post-operative concern regarding a patient who has already resumed solid diet is:
A. Failure to pass stool within 12 hours of eating solid food
B. Failure to pass stool within 48 hours of eating solid food
C. Passage of excessive flatus
D. Patient reports a decreased diet
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B. Failure to pass stool within 48 hours of eating solid food

Explanation: Post-operative ileus or constipation is a concern. While bowel habits vary, absence of stool for 48 hours after resuming solids (which stimulate peristalsis) warrants assessment.

13.

A. Urinary tract infections
B. History of premature ventricle beats

15.
The Valve that prevents the backflow of blood into the atria when the ventricles contract is the:
A. Aortic
B. Semilunar
C. Atrio- ventricular
D. Pulmonary

Explanation: The Atrioventricular (AV) valves (Tricuspid and Mitral) close during ventricular contraction (systole) to prevent blood from flowing back into the atria.

16.
Withholding treatment: For example not carrying out a surgery that will extend life of the patient for a short time is:
A. Active euthanasia
B. Passive euthanasia
C. Indirect euthanasia
D. Direct euthanasia
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B. Passive euthanasia

Explanation: Passive euthanasia involves letting a patient die by withholding or withdrawing life-sustaining treatment (allowing the underlying disease to take its course), as opposed to active euthanasia which involves doing something to cause death.

SECTION 2: SHORT ANSWER QUESTIONS (40 MARKS)

41.
Explain five (5) non-pharmacological ways of managing hypertension. (5 marks)
Show Answer
  1. Dietary Modifications (DASH Diet): Reducing sodium intake and increasing fruits/vegetables.
  2. Weight Reduction: Maintaining a healthy BMI reduces cardiovascular strain.
  3. Regular Physical Exercise: Aerobic activity helps lower blood pressure.
  4. Smoking Cessation: Nicotine is a vasoconstrictor; quitting improves vascular health.
  5. Stress Management: Techniques like meditation or therapy to reduce sympathetic nervous system arousal.
  6. (Alternative) Limiting Alcohol Consumption.
42.
Explain five (5) nursing interventions used in prevention of pressure sores in a bed ridden patient. (5 marks)
Show Answer
  1. Repositioning: Turning the patient every 2 hours to relieve pressure.
  2. Skin Assessment: Daily inspection of skin, especially bony prominences, for redness.
  3. Skin Care: Keeping skin clean and dry; managing incontinence immediately.
  4. Use of Pressure-Relieving Devices: Air mattresses, gel cushions, or heel protectors.
  5. Nutrition and Hydration: Ensuring adequate protein and fluid intake to maintain tissue integrity.
43.
State five (5) signs and symptoms suggestive of wound infection. (5 marks)
Show Answer
  1. Erythema: Redness around the wound edges.
  2. Edema: Swelling at the site.
  3. Increased Pain: Or tenderness at the site.
  4. Purulent Exudate: Pus or foul-smelling discharge.
  5. Local Heat: Warmth around the wound.
  6. (Alternative) Fever/Systemic signs.
44.
Outline five (5) important relevant health messages you would share with a diabetic patient. (5 marks)
Show Answer
  1. Adherence to Medication: Importance of taking insulin/oral hypoglycemics as prescribed.
  2. Dietary Management: Importance of a balanced diet, carbohydrate counting, and meal timing.
  3. Foot Care: Daily inspection, wearing proper shoes, and avoiding barefoot walking.
  4. Blood Glucose Monitoring: How and when to check sugar levels.
  5. Recognition of Hypo/Hyperglycemia: Knowing signs (dizziness, sweating vs thirst, polyuria) and how to manage them.
45.
Explain five (5) nursing consideration before a nasogastric feeding of a patient. (5marks)
Show Answer
  1. Verify Placement: Confirm tube is in stomach via pH test of aspirate or checking marking.
  2. Check Residual Volume: Aspirate stomach contents to ensure digestion is occurring (prevent aspiration).
  3. Patient Positioning: Elevate head of bed (Semi-Fowler's, 30-45 degrees).
  4. Assess Bowel Sounds: Ensure peristalsis is present.
  5. Check Formula: Verify correct feed, expiration date, and ensure it is at room temperature.
46.
Briefly state the process of bone healing. (5 marks)
Show Answer
  1. Hematoma Formation: Bleeding creates a clot at the fracture site (Inflammation).
  2. Soft Callus Formation: Fibrocartilage fills the gap.
  3. Hard Callus Formation: Osteoblasts convert soft callus to spongy bone.
  4. Bone Remodeling: Osteoclasts remove excess bone; compact bone replaces spongy bone to restore shape.
47.
Explain the complications of Pneumonia. (5 marks)
Show Answer
  1. Pleural Effusion: Fluid accumulation in the pleural space.
  2. Empyema: Pus in the pleural cavity.
  3. Bacteremia/Sepsis: Infection spreads to the blood.
  4. Respiratory Failure: Inability to maintain oxygenation (ARDS).
  5. Lung Abscess: Formation of a cavity containing necrosis and pus.
48.
State the responsibilities of a receiving nurse in theatre. (5 marks)
Show Answer
  1. Patient Identification: Verify patient name, file number, and wristband.
  2. Consent Verification: Ensure informed consent is signed and valid.
  3. Pre-operative Checklist: Confirm NPO status, removal of jewelry/dentures, and pre-medication.
  4. Site Verification: Ensure the surgical site is marked.
  5. Reassurance: Provide psychological support to reduce anxiety.

49.
Mr. Zee is admitted in the ward with a diagnosis of chronic renal failure. The doctor orders for immediate hemodialysis.
a) Draw an illustrated diagram showing a kidney nephron. (5 marks)
b) Describe the role of a nurse during hemodialysis. (5 marks)
c) State any five (5) complications of a patient with chronic renal failure. (10 marks)
Show Answer

a) Diagram of Kidney Nephron:

(Student should draw and label):
- Afferent/Efferent Arterioles
- Glomerulus (Bowman's Capsule)
- Proximal Convoluted Tubule (PCT)
- Loop of Henle (Descending and Ascending limbs)
- Distal Convoluted Tubule (DCT)
- Collecting Duct

b) Role of a Nurse During Hemodialysis:

  • Monitoring Vitals: Frequent BP checks (q30min) to detect hypotension.
  • Access Care: Ensure needle placement is secure and monitor for bleeding or infiltration.
  • Patient Comfort: Manage symptoms like cramping or nausea (adjust flow rate/saline bolus).
  • Anticoagulation: Administer heparin as ordered to prevent clotting in the circuit.
  • Monitoring Equipment: Check machine pressures and alarms.

c) Five Complications of Chronic Renal Failure:

  1. Hyperkalemia: Dangerous accumulation of potassium leading to arrhythmias.
  2. Hypertension: Due to sodium/water retention and RAAS activation.
  3. Anemia: Due to decreased erythropoietin production.
  4. Renal Osteodystrophy: Bone disease due to calcium/phosphate imbalance (hypocalcemia/hyperphosphatemia).
  5. Pulmonary Edema / Fluid Overload: Accumulation of fluid in lungs due to inability to excrete water.
  6. (Alternative) Uremic Pericarditis.
