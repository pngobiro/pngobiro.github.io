SECTION 1: MULTIPLE CHOICE QUESTIONS (40 MARKS)

1.
The ethical principal in Nursing that implies to the duty to do good and active promotion of benevolence is
a) Autonomy
b) Beneficence
c) Non maleficence
d) Fidelity
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Beneficence

Explanation: Beneficence is the obligation to act in ways that benefit the patient ("do good"). Non-maleficence is "do no harm". Autonomy is self-determination. Fidelity is keeping promises.

2.
Determination of the patient's response to nursing intervention is done during
a) Planning
b) Evaluation
c) Diagnosing
d) Implementation
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Evaluation

Explanation: Evaluation is the final step of the nursing process where the nurse determines if the goals were met and the patient's response to interventions.

3.
During physical assessment of a patient pain may be elicited by
a) Client verbalization, elevated pulse rate
b) Guarding behavior, elevated blood pressure
c) Guarding behavior, client verbalization
d) Elevated pulse rate, client verbalization
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Guarding behavior, client verbalization

Explanation: Pain assessment relies on both subjective data (verbalization - the gold standard) and objective behavioral signs (guarding, grimacing). Vital signs (pulse/BP) may change but are not reliable indicators on their own as they can normalize in chronic pain.

4.
Factors associated to potential nutritional deficit in a patient with appendicitis awaiting to go to surgery include:
a) Nausea and vomiting, medications
b) Pain, nausea and vomiting
c) Tube feeding, medications
d) Anorexia, wound drainage
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Pain, nausea and vomiting

Explanation: Appendicitis typically presents with severe pain, anorexia, nausea, and vomiting, which directly prevent oral intake, leading to nutritional deficit. Also, the patient is kept NPO for surgery.

5.
After removal of a plaster cast in a limb, the patient is likely to have
a) Atrophy
b) Hyperplasia
c) Dysplasia
d) Hypertrophy
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Atrophy

Explanation: Disuse atrophy occurs when a muscle is not used for a prolonged period (as when immobilized in a cast), causing it to waste away and become smaller.

6.
A disorder where there is history of multiple generations with similar disorder is
a) Autosomal dominant inheritance
b) Autosomal close inheritance
c) Autosomal recessive inheritance
d) Autosomal genetic inheritance
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Autosomal dominant inheritance

Explanation: Autosomal dominant disorders typically appear in every generation (vertical transmission) because only one affected gene is needed for the trait to be expressed.

7.
To identify valvular and ventricular functions and structures, the most commonly used investigation is
a) E.E.G (Electro- encephalogram)
b) E.C.T (Electro-convulsive therapy)
c) E.C. G (Electro cardiogram)
d) Echocardiogram
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Echocardiogram

Explanation: An echocardiogram uses ultrasound waves to visualize the heart's structure (valves, ventricles) and function (wall motion, ejection fraction).

8.
Which of the following factors can increase serum osmolarity
a) Renal failure
b) Diuretic use
c) Adrenal insufficiency
d) Uraemia
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Diuretic use

Explanation: Serum osmolarity increases when there is a water deficit relative to solutes. Diuretics (especially loop or osmotic) cause water loss, which can lead to dehydration and hemoconcentration, increasing osmolarity. (Note: Renal failure with Uremia can also increase osmolarity due to retained BUN/solutes, but Diuretics causing dehydration is a classic cause of hyperosmolarity).

9.
A patient re-admitted to the ward with preventable complications of diabetes mellitus has the following nursing diagnosis
a) Pain related to trauma
b) Risk for altered skin integrity
c) Nutrition imbalance, less than body requirements
d) Knowledge deficient
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Knowledge deficient

Explanation: If complications are "preventable" and the patient is re-admitted, it strongly suggests a lack of understanding or knowledge on how to manage the condition at home (diet, insulin, foot care), making Knowledge Deficit the priority diagnosis.

10.
In choosing a site for venipuncture, the nurse will consider
a) Conditions of vein, duration of therapy
b) Patients age and size, condition of canula
c) Skill of person performing, time of the day
d) Whether patient is right or left handed, skill of patient
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Conditions of vein, duration of therapy

Explanation: The primary considerations are the vein's condition (avoid sclerosis, infection) and how long the IV needs to be in (duration determines location, e.g., hand vs forearm). Handedness (D) is also a factor but vein condition is paramount.

11.
Advantages of crystalloids in treatment of shock is
a) Cannot cause pulmonary oedema
b) Cannot cause hypernatremia
c) Area cheap and widely available
d) Rapidly expand plasma volume
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Area cheap and widely available

Explanation: Crystalloids (like Normal Saline, Ringer's Lactate) are inexpensive, readily available, and easy to store compared to colloids or blood. They CAN cause edema (A) and hypernatremia (B) if given in excess. Colloids expand volume more rapidly per unit (D).

12.
Cardiac tamponade is the
a) Accumulation of fluid in the pericardium
b) Compression of the heart by fluid in the pericardial sac
c) Accumulation of fluid in the endocardium
d) Compression of the heart by fluid in the pleural cavity
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Compression of the heart by fluid in the pericardial sac

Explanation: While A is true (accumulation), B describes the pathophysiology (compression) that defines tamponade (restricted filling), distinguishing it from a simple effusion.

13.
Risk factors for chronic obstructive air disease include
a) Tobacco smoking, genetic, nutritional deficiencies
b) Passive smoking, ambient air pollution, nutritional deficiency
c) Occupational exposure, ambient air pollution, passive smoking
d) Passive smoking, alpha protease deficiency, ambient air pollution
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Passive smoking, alpha protease deficiency, ambient air pollution

Explanation: This option includes the major causes: Smoking (Passive/Active), Genetics (Alpha-1 Antitrypsin deficiency), and Environmental pollutants. Option A is also strong but "Nutritional deficiencies" is less direct than Alpha-1 deficiency.

14.
In measurement of blood pressure
a) Cuff bladder is centred on radial artery
b) Caliberation of blood pressure machine should be done monthly
c) Palpation of systolic pressure is done by hand
d) Cuff size must be appropriate for patient
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Cuff size must be appropriate for patient

Explanation: Using the wrong cuff size is a common source of error (too small = false high, too large = false low). The bladder is centered on the brachial artery (not radial, A).

15.
Lidocaine used intravenously is indicated for:
a) Local anaelgesic
b) Antirrhythmic
c) Muscle relaxant
d) Severe pain
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Antirrhythmic

Explanation: While Lidocaine is a local anesthetic (A), when given intravenously, it acts as a class IB antiarrhythmic agent used to treat ventricular arrhythmias.

16.
Streptococcal infections can complicate to
a) Sore throat
b) Endocarditis
c) Sepsis
d) Septicaemia
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Endocarditis (specifically Rheumatic Endocarditis)

Explanation: Group A Streptococcus infections can lead to Rheumatic Fever, which causes inflammation of the heart valves (Endocarditis).

17.
Digoxin toxicity may be indicated by
a) Fatique, depression
b) Nausea, fever
c) Tachycardia, fever
d) Restlessness, anorexia
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Fatique, depression (Also Anorexia, Nausea, Vomiting)

Explanation: Early signs of toxicity are GI (Anorexia, Nausea) and Neuro (Fatigue, Visual changes). However, usually Anorexia/Nausea/Vomiting are the classic first signs. Option D has Anorexia but "Restlessness" is less common (usually lethargy). Let's look closely. Nausea is correct, Fever is not. Tachycardia is usually wrong (Bradycardia is key). Fatigue/Depression are neuro signs. Usually, Nausea/Vomiting is the answer. Between A and D, D contains Anorexia (classic) but Restlessness is odd. A contains Fatigue (classic). Nausea (B) is classic but Fever is wrong.
Clinical Note: Classic signs = Anorexia, Nausea, Vomiting, Bradycardia, Yellow/Green halos.
In this list, none are perfect. Fatigue/depression fits the neuro profile.

18.
Cardiopulmonary resuscitation is done using
a) Oxygen, Adrenalin, 50% Dextrose
b) Atropine, Adrenalin, 50% Dextrose
c) Oxygen, Adrenalin, Atropine
d) Vasopressin, 50% Dextrose, Adrenalin
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Oxygen, Adrenalin, Atropine

Explanation: These are standard resuscitation drugs (though Atropine is no longer routine for all cardiac arrests in newer ACLS, it is still in many curricula for bradycardia-induced arrest). Adrenaline (Epinephrine) is key. Dextrose 50% is for hypoglycemia.

19.
Risk factors for deep venous thrombosis include
a) Endothelial Damage, Venous Stasis, Pregnancy
b) Age Below 50 Years, Obesity, Venous Stasis
c) Exercise, Surgery, Venous Stasis
d) Low Body Weight, Venous Stasis, Pregnancy
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Endothelial Damage, Venous Stasis, Pregnancy

Explanation: This refers to Virchow's Triad: Venous Stasis, Endothelial Injury, and Hypercoagulability. Pregnancy causes both stasis (pressure) and hypercoagulability.

20.
Modifiable factors that may lead to hypertension include
a) Obesity, Age, Diet
b) Smoking, Diet, Genetics
c) Sodium Intake, Genetics, Obesity
d) Diet, Obesity, Smoking
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Diet, Obesity, Smoking

Explanation: Modifiable factors are things you can change. Diet, Weight (Obesity), and Smoking can be changed. Age and Genetics (in A, B, C) are non-modifiable.

21.
A patient with sickle cell anaemia will have the following nursing diagnosis
a) Acute pain related to tissue damage
b) Risk for infection
c) Anxiety related to low haemoglobin
d) Non-compliance related to pain
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Acute pain related to tissue damage (or ischemia)

Explanation: The hallmark of sickle cell crisis is severe pain caused by vaso-occlusion and tissue ischemia.

22.
The digestive enzyme ptyalin is produced by
a) Pancreas
b) Intestinal mucosa
c) Liver
d) Salivary glands
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Salivary glands

Explanation: Ptyalin is another name for Salivary Amylase.

23.
Referred pain on the right shoulder is likely to be
a) Liver or duodenal problem
b) Rectal lesions and cardiac problem
c) Pancreatis, appendicitis
d) Biliary colic, cardiac problem
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Liver or duodenal problem (specifically Gallbladder/Liver)

Explanation: Pain from the liver and gallbladder (biliary colic) refers to the right shoulder due to phrenic nerve irritation. (Note: Option D says Biliary colic + Cardiac; Cardiac usually refers to LEFT shoulder).

24.
A nursing diagnosis of Impaired urinary elimination may be evidenced by
a) Recurrent urinary tract infections
b) Request for a urinal or bed pan
c) Taking a lot of fluids
d) No drinking enough fluids
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Recurrent urinary tract infections (or Dysuria/Frequency/Hesitancy)

Explanation: Evidence (Defining Characteristics) of impaired elimination includes urgency, frequency, dysuria, and infection. Requesting a urinal is a normal behavior. Fluids relate to intake, not elimination.

25.
In order to confirm if a nasal-gastric tube is in the stomach, the nurse will
a) Use a litmus paper to test the PH of mouth contents
b) Use a stethoscope and listen for heart sounds
c) Aspirate the contents before feeding and check if they contain food remains
d) Put the tube end in a bowl of water and check for bubble
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Aspirate the contents before feeding and check if they contain food remains (and check pH)

Explanation: Aspiration of gastric contents (checking pH < 5.5) is the most reliable bedside method. The bubbling method (D) is dangerous and obsolete (risk of aspiration). Heart sounds (B) are irrelevant.

26.
While nursing a patient with a colostomy, the nurse will maintain skin integrity by
a) Applying skin barrier on the colostomy bag
b) Changing the pouch only when full
c) Teaching the patient to identify signs of irritation
d) Dressing the pressure sore asceptically
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Applying skin barrier on the colostomy bag (around the stoma)

Explanation: A skin barrier (wafer/paste) protects the peristomal skin from irritating fecal enzymes.

27.
Which of the following are functions of the liver?
a) Vitamin metabolism, bile formation, fat metabolism
b) Protein metabolism, glucose metabolism, vitamin storage
c) Ammonia conversion, bile formation, fat metabolism
d) Bile storage, fat metabolism, protein metabolism
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Ammonia conversion, bile formation, fat metabolism

Explanation: The liver forms bile (stored in gallbladder), converts toxic ammonia to urea, and metabolizes fats/proteins/carbs.

28.
The possible complications after cholecystogram include
a) Pain at the injection site
b) Exposure to radiation
c) Vomiting as tube is inserted
d) Bleeding
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Pain at the injection site (if IV) or Allergic reaction

Explanation: Cholecystogram involves contrast dye. Risks include allergy or injection site issues.

29.
A patient with hyperparathyroidism will present with
a) Kidney stones, muscle weakness, bulging eyes
b) Pancreatitis, vomiting, flushed skin
c) Rapid pulse, pancreatitis, kidney stones
d) Kidney stones, muscle weakness, pancreatitis
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Kidney stones, muscle weakness, pancreatitis

Explanation: "Stones, Bones, Groans, and Moans". Hypercalcemia from Hyperparathyroidism causes renal stones, muscle weakness (hypotonicity), and GI issues like pancreatitis.

30.
Excessive use of corticosteroid medications is likely to cause
a) Down's syndrome
b) Korsakoff's syndrome
c) Cushing's syndrome
d) Nephrotic syndrome
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Cushing's syndrome

Explanation: Exogenous Cushing's syndrome is caused by prolonged use of corticosteroids.

31.

a) Kidney disease
b) Urethral discharge

32.
Urine that is colourless or pale yellow could indicate
a) Excess fluid intake, diabetes insipidus
b) Dehydration, diabetes mellitus
c) Overhydration, infection
d) Pyuria, dehydration

Correct Answer: a) Excess fluid intake, diabetes insipidus

33.

36.
Risk factors for development of cataract include

37.

SECTION 2: SHORT ANSWER QUESTIONS (40 MARKS)

41.
State FIVE local factors at the site of fracture that affect bone healing (5 marks)
Show Answer
  1. Degree of Trauma: Extensive damage to bone/soft tissue delays healing.
  2. Vascular Injury: Poor blood supply leads to delayed union or non-union.
  3. Infection: Osteomyelitis at the site stops healing.
  4. Immobilization: Inadequate fixation (movement) prevents callus formation.
  5. Interposition of Soft Tissue: Muscle/tissue between fragments prevents union.
42.
On gentamicin, state:
a) Mode of action (2 marks)
b) Contra indications (1 mark)
c) Drug interaction (2 marks)
Show Answer

a) Mode of Action: Gentamicin is an aminoglycoside that inhibits bacterial protein synthesis by binding irreversibly to the 30S ribosomal subunit.

b) Contraindications: Hypersensitivity to aminoglycosides, Myasthenia Gravis, Pregnancy (unless necessary).

c) Drug Interactions:
- Diuretics (Furosemide): Increases risk of ototoxicity.
- Neuromuscular Blockers: Enhances blockade (respiratory paralysis).

43.
Explain the THREE stages in the pathophysiology of chronic renal disease (6 marks)
Show Answer
  1. Reduced Renal Reserve: GFR drops to ~50%. No clinical symptoms. BUN/Creatinine normal.
  2. Renal Insufficiency: GFR 20-50%. Azotemia (raised BUN/Cr) appears. Polyuria/Nocturia. Mild anemia.
  3. Renal Failure (ESRD): GFR < 20%. Uremia affects all systems. Oliguria/Anuria. Requires dialysis/transplant.

45.
Outline THREE functions of insulin in the body (3 marks)
Show Answer
  1. Glucose Uptake: Facilitates entry of glucose into cells (muscle/adipose) for energy.
  2. Glycogenesis: Promotes storage of glucose as glycogen in the liver.
  3. Inhibits Gluconeogenesis: Stops the liver from making new glucose.
46.
State FOUR goals in the nursing management of a patient in sickle cell crisis (4 marks)
Show Answer
  1. Relieve Pain: Administer analgesics (opioids).
  2. Promote Hydration: IV fluids to reduce blood viscosity and sickling.
  3. Improve Oxygenation: O2 therapy to reverse hypoxia.
  4. Prevent/Treat Infection: Antibiotics if indicated (infection triggers crisis).
47.
Outline FIVE factors that can lead to disseminated intravascular coagulopathy (5 marks)
Show Answer
  1. Sepsis: Severe bacterial infection (Gram-negative).
  2. Obstetric Complications: Abruptio placentae, amniotic fluid embolism.
  3. Trauma: Severe burns, crush injuries.
  4. Malignancy: Cancer (Leukemia).
  5. Transfusion Reactions: ABO incompatibility.
48.
State FOUR actions to prevent anaphylaxis (6 marks?? usually 4)
Show Answer
  1. Assessment: Always ask about allergies before administering drugs.
  2. Test Dose: Perform sensitivity testing for risky drugs (e.g., Penicillin/ATG).
  3. Avoidance: Ensure known allergens are clearly flagged (red bracelet/chart).
  4. Observation: Monitor patient closely for 15-30 mins after injection.

SECTION 3: LONG ANSWER QUESTION (20 MARKS)

49.
A 48 year old male patient is admitted in the surgical ward with cancer of the colon.
a) Outline FIVE agents or factors relating to carcinogenesis (5 marks)
b) State THREE imaging tests used to detect cancer (3 marks)
c) Describe the management if this patient until discharge (12 marks)
Show Answer

a) Carcinogenic Factors:

  1. Chemicals: Tobacco, asbestos, pesticides.
  2. Viral Agents: HPV, Hepatitis B/C, Epstein-Barr.
  3. Radiation: UV light, X-rays.
  4. Dietary Factors: High fat, low fiber, red meat, alcohol.
  5. Genetics: Familial polyposis, BRCA genes.

b) Imaging Tests:

  1. Colonoscopy (Visualization).
  2. CT Scan (Computed Tomography).
  3. MRI (Magnetic Resonance Imaging).
  4. PET Scan (Positron Emission Tomography).

c) Management (Pre-op, Post-op, Discharge):

  • Pre-operative: Bowel preparation (cleansing), antibiotics, consent, nutritional support (TPN if needed), stoma siting/education.
  • Post-operative:
    • Immediate: Vitals monitoring, pain management, wound care, NGT suction.
    • Stoma Care: Assess stoma color (should be pink/red), size, and output. Teach appliance change.
    • DVT Prophylaxis: Early ambulation, heparin.
  • Adjuvant Therapy: Chemotherapy or Radiation if indicated.
  • Diet: Gradual reintroduction. If stoma, avoid gas-forming foods.
  • Psychological Support: Body image, coping with cancer diagnosis.
  • Discharge: Home care education, follow-up appointments.
