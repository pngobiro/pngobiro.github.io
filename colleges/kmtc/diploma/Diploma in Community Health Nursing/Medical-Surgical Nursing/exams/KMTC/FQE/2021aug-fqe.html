SECTION 1: MULTIPLE CHOICE QUESTIONS (40 MARKS)

1.
C6H12O6 is the chemical formula for a ____________
a) Polymer of carbohydrate
b) Pentose monosaccharide
c) Hexose monosaccharide
d) All of the above
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Hexose monosaccharide

Explanation: C6H12O6 is the formula for Glucose (and its isomers Fructose and Galactose). It contains 6 carbon atoms, making it a Hexose (Hex = 6).

a) Thyroid
b) Liver
c) Kidneys
d) Hypothalamus
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Kidneys

Explanation: The adrenal glands (suprarenal glands) sit directly on top (superior) of the kidneys.

3.
Which is an example of a normal physiologic process that uses a positive feedback loop?
a) Blood pressure regulation
b) Childbirth
c) Regulation of fluid balance
d) Temperature regulation
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Childbirth

Explanation: Positive feedback loops amplify a response. In childbirth, uterine contractions stimulate the release of oxytocin, which causes stronger contractions, leading to more oxytocin, until the baby is born. The others are negative feedback loops (maintaining homeostasis).

d) Person, environment, nursing, health

7.
People can be accidentally exposed to HIV through healthcare work or due to exposures outside the healthcare setting. PEP is recommended under one of the following conditions:
a) Even if rapid HIV blood test results are unavailable
b) Only if the source of body fluids is confirmed to be HIV positive
c) The reported exposure represents no risk of HIV transmission
d) When care is sought more than 72 hours after potential exposure
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Even if rapid HIV blood test results are unavailable

Explanation: PEP (Post-Exposure Prophylaxis) should be started as soon as possible (within 72 hours) if the risk is significant, even if the source's status is unknown or results are pending, as delay reduces efficacy. It is not indicated if there is no risk (C) or after 72 hours (D).

11.
A client with iron deficiency anemia is scheduled for discharge. Which instruction about prescribed ferrous gluconate therapy should the nurse include in the teaching plan?
a) Take the medications with an antacid
b) Take the medication with a glass of milk
c) Take the medication together with calcium supplement
d) Take the medication on an empty stomach
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Take the medication on an empty stomach

Explanation: Iron is best absorbed in an acidic environment (empty stomach). Antacids, milk, and calcium (Options A, B, C) interfere with iron absorption and should be avoided at the same time.

12.
Which findings do you expect to find in a patient with acute blood loss?
a) Weakness, Lethargy, warm and dry skin
b) Restlessness, hyperthermia and bradycardia
c) Tachycardia, hypotension, cool and clammy skin
d) Widened pulse pressure, anxiety and hypoventilation
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Tachycardia, hypotension, cool and clammy skin

Explanation: These are the classic signs of hypovolemic shock. The body compensates for volume loss by increasing heart rate (tachycardia) and vasoconstricting peripheral vessels (cool/clammy skin). BP drops (hypotension) as volume loss continues.

16.
Mechanism of action of digitalis(digoxin)include:
a) Reduces the force of myocardial contraction and increases conduction through the atrioventricular node.
b) Increases the force of myocardial contraction and slows conduction through the atrioventricular node.
c) Increases conduction through the Sino-atrio node thereby reducing myocardial contraction.

Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Increases the force of myocardial contraction and slows conduction through the atrioventricular node.

20.

c) "The blood culture determines the most appropriate antibiotics."

43.
Explain three (3) characteristics of adaptive immunity (6 Marks)
Show Answer
  1. Specificity: It targets specific antigens (pathogens) precisely using antibodies or T-cell receptors.
  2. Memory: After initial exposure, the immune system "remembers" the antigen, allowing for a faster and stronger response upon re-exposure (Basis of vaccination).
  3. Self/Non-Self Recognition (Tolerance): It distinguishes between the body's own cells (self) and foreign invaders (non-self), normally attacking only the latter.

46.
Outline the pathophysiology of myocardial infarction (5 Marks)
Show Answer
  1. Atherosclerosis: Plaque buildup narrows coronary arteries.
  2. Plaque Rupture: An unstable plaque ruptures, exposing the sub-endothelium.
  3. Thrombus Formation: Platelets aggregate and a clot forms, occluding the artery.
  4. Ischemia: Blood flow/Oxygen supply to the myocardium is blocked.
  5. Necrosis: Prolonged ischemia (>20 mins) leads to irreversible death of heart muscle cells (Infarction).

SECTION 3: LONG ANSWER QUESTION (20 MARKS)

49.
Patient X comes into your casualty department accompanied by relatives, presenting with the following symptoms: Slow heart rate of below 60 beat /min, low BP below 90mmhg, Rapid and shallow respirations, crackles due to fluid in the lungs, unconscious state, signs of acute renal failure, raised bilirubin levels, low pH, jaundice and tissue edema.
i. Identify the condition of patient X above. (1 marks)
ii. Giving the 4 classifications and brief description. (8 marks)
iii. Describe the nursing management of patient X from admission to discharge (11 marks)
Show Answer

i. Condition:

Shock (Specifically Cardiogenic Shock or Multiple Organ Dysfunction Syndrome (MODS)). Given the combination of slow heart rate (bradycardia), low BP (hypotension), and multi-organ failure (Lung crackles/edema, Kidney failure, Liver jaundice), this presents as Decompensated Shock or End-Stage Heart Failure leading to shock.

ii. Four Classifications of Shock (and description):

  1. Hypovolemic Shock: Caused by loss of blood volume (hemorrhage or dehydration).
  2. Cardiogenic Shock: Caused by failure of the heart to pump effectively (e.g., MI, Heart Failure).
  3. Distributive Shock: Caused by abnormal distribution of blood flow due to massive vasodilation (Includes Septic, Anaphylactic, Neurogenic).
  4. Obstructive Shock: Caused by physical obstruction to blood flow (e.g., Pulmonary Embolism, Tamponade).

iii. Nursing Management:

  • Airway & Breathing: Administer high-flow oxygen. Position in semi-Fowler's (if BP allows, though shock usually requires flat/trendelenburg, pulmonary edema requires head up - balance is key, likely intubation required). Monitor O2 sats and ABGs.
  • Circulation (Hemodynamics): Establish IV access. Administer fluids cautiously (due to crackles/edema). Administer Inotropes (Dopamine/Dobutamine) to strengthen heart beat and Vasopressors to raise BP as ordered. Monitor Vital signs q15mins.
  • Renal Management: Insert Foley catheter to monitor strict output. Prepare for Dialysis if renal failure persists.
  • Positioning: Frequent turning to prevent sores (tissue edema increases risk).
  • Monitoring: Continuous ECG (for bradycardia). Monitor LOC.
  • Safety: Side rails up for unconscious patient.
