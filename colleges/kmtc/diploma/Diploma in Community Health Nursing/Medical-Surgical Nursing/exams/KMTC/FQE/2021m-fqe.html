SECTION 1: MULTIPLE CHOICE QUESTIONS (40 MARKS)

1.
Yellow to milky white urine signifies:
a) Extremely concentrated urine
b) Infection
c) Dilute urine
d) Gross blood
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Infection

Explanation: Milky white urine (pyuria) typically indicates the presence of pus, white blood cells, or bacteria, which are signs of a urinary tract infection. It can also indicate chyle (chyluria).

2.
One of the following endocrine hormone is produced by the anterior pituitary gland:
a) Growth Hormone
b) Thyroid releasing hormone
c) Insulin
d) Corticosteroids
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Growth Hormone

Explanation: The Anterior Pituitary produces Growth Hormone (GH), TSH, ACTH, FSH, LH, and Prolactin. Thyroid Releasing Hormone (b) is from the Hypothalamus. Insulin (c) is from the Pancreas. Corticosteroids (d) are from the Adrenal Cortex.

3.
Parietal cells in the stomach produce:
a) Pepsinogen
b) Mucus
c) Hydrochloric acid
d) Salivary amylase
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Hydrochloric acid

Explanation: Parietal cells secrete Hydrochloric Acid (HCl) and Intrinsic Factor. Pepsinogen is secreted by Chief cells.

4.
One of the following occurs in mitral valve prolapse:
a) Stretching of the valve leaflet in to the atrium during systile
b) Valve tissue thickens and narrows the valve opening
c) Margins of the mitral valve cannot close during systole
d) There is back flow from the aorta during systole
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Margins of the mitral valve cannot close during systole

Explanation: In Mitral Valve Prolapse, the valve leaflets bulge (prolapse) upward into the left atrium during contraction (systole), which can prevent the margins from closing tightly, leading to regurgitation (backflow) into the atrium. (Note: Option A is also descriptive, but C describes the functional failure).

5.
A fusiform aneurysm is characterized by:
a) Projection from one side of the vessel only
b) Entire arterial segment becomes dilated
c) Edema that splits the layers of the arterial wall
d) Rectangular shape
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Entire arterial segment becomes dilated

Explanation: A fusiform aneurysm involves dilation of the entire circumference of the artery (spindle-shaped). A saccular aneurysm (A) involves a projection from one side.

6.
The urgent management of a patient with hypertensive emergency include:
a) Administer beta brokers orally
b) Administer intravenous vasodilators
c) Monitor deep venous thrombosis
d) Weight reduction
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Administer intravenous vasodilators

Explanation: Hypertensive emergency (BP >180/120 with end-organ damage) requires immediate, controlled reduction of BP using IV medications (like Nitroprusside, Labetalol, or Hydralazine) to prevent stroke or MI.

7.
The following structures are located superior to the stomach:
a) Small intestines transverse colon
b) Diaphragm and oesophagus
c) Spleen and pancreas
d) Kidneys and gall bladder
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Diaphragm and oesophagus

Explanation: The diaphragm sits above (superior to) the stomach. The esophagus enters the stomach superiorly. Small intestines are inferior. Spleen/Pancreas are posterior/lateral.

8.
The bone forming cells that secrete collagen and other constituents of bone tissue are called:
a) Osteoblasts
b) Osteoclasts
c) Trabeculae
d) La cona
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Osteoblasts

Explanation: Osteoblasts are responsible for bone formation (ossification). Osteoclasts (b) break down bone.

9.
A definitive management of chronic obstructive pulmonary disease include:
a) Giving 100% concentration of oxygen
b) Weight reduction
c) Facilitation of removal of secretions
d) Giving vasodilators to reduce peripheral resistance
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Facilitation of removal of secretions

Explanation: COPD patients struggle with airway obstruction due to mucus. Clearing secretions (physiotherapy, hydration, coughing) improves ventilation. High concentration O2 (A) is dangerous as it may suppress the respiratory drive.

10.
Bronchoconstriction in asthma can be caused by:
a) Stimulation of beta adrenergic receptors
b) Inhibition of mast cells
c) Release of acetylcholine
d) Excess administration of oxygen
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Release of acetylcholine

Explanation: Parasympathetic stimulation releases Acetylcholine, which acts on muscarinic receptors in bronchial smooth muscle to cause constriction. (Note: Stimulation of beta-adrenergic receptors causes bronchodilation).

11.
An example of potassium sparing diuretic is:
a) Benzylthiazide
b) Furosemide
c) Acetazolamide
d) Spironolactone
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Spironolactone

Explanation: Spironolactone is an aldosterone antagonist and a potassium-sparing diuretic. Furosemide is a Loop diuretic. Thiazides are potassium-wasting.

12.
Synergistic effect in drug interaction refers to:
a) Two drugs with similar effects are taken for a doubled effect
b) One drug interferes with the action of another drug
c) The combined effect of two drugs is greater than the sum of the effect of each drug given alone
d) The first drug inhibits the metabolism of the second drug causing increased activity of the second drug
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) The combined effect of two drugs is greater than the sum of the effect of each drug given alone

Explanation: Synergism implies that 1 + 1 > 2. (Option A describes an additive effect).

13.
Sulphonamides acts by:
a) Inhibiting cell wall synthesis
b) Inhibiting DNA synthesis
c) Inhibiting protein synthesis
d) It is bacteriocidal
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Inhibiting DNA synthesis (Indirectly)

Explanation: Sulphonamides are bacteriostatic agents that competitively inhibit dihydropteroate synthase, an enzyme required for folic acid synthesis. Since bacteria need folic acid to synthesize DNA and RNA, blocking this pathway inhibits DNA synthesis and bacterial growth.

14.
Antidote of phenothiazines is:
a) Lithium
b) Artane
c) Dlazepam
d) Amitriptyline
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Artane

Explanation: Phenothiazines (antipsychotics) can cause Extrapyramidal Side Effects (EPS) like rigidity and tremors. Artane (Trihexyphenidyl) is an anticholinergic used to treat these side effects.

15.
Primary prevention of cancer include:
a) Surgical operations to remove cancer
b) Health education to the community on ways to prevent cancer
c) Screening for cervical cancer and treatment
d) Doing radiotherapy
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Health education to the community on ways to prevent cancer

Explanation: Primary prevention aims to prevent the disease from occurring (e.g., vaccination, education, avoiding risks). Screening (C) is Secondary prevention.

16.
The following is a cardiovascular complication for someone with burns:
a) Inflammation of epiglottis
b) Decreased urinary output
c) Hypovolemia
d) Reduced body temperature
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Hypovolemia

Explanation: Severe burns cause massive fluid shift from blood vessels to tissues (edema), leading to decreased circulating blood volume (Hypovolemic shock).

17.
Hypokalaemia can be caused by:
a) Use of potassium sparing diuretics
b) Vomiting and gastric suctioning
c) Decreased renal excretion of potassium
d) Misuse of potassium suppliments
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Vomiting and gastric suctioning

Explanation: Gastric fluids are rich in potassium. Loss of these fluids via vomiting or suction is a direct cause of hypokalemia.

18.
Tetanus is caused by toxins produced by:
a) Vibrio cholerae
b) Staphylococcus aureus
c) Pneumococcal bacillus
d) Closmidium tetani
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Closmidium tetani (Clostridium tetani)

Explanation: Clostridium tetani produces the neurotoxin tetanospasmin which causes the symptoms of tetanus.

19.
An example of a synthetic absorbable suture is:
a) Plain catgut
b) Vicryl
c) Nylon
d) Silk
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Vicryl

Explanation: Vicryl (Polyglactin) is synthetic and absorbable. Catgut is natural absorbable. Nylon and Silk are non-absorbable.

20.
Brachial plexus nerve block regional anaesthesia involves one of the following areas:
a) Chest and abdominal wall
b) Lower limbs
c) Upper arms
d) Perineum
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Upper arms

Explanation: The brachial plexus supplies the upper limb. A block here provides anesthesia for the arm/shoulder.

21.
The following is a sign of benign prostatic hypertrophy:
a) Prostate gland hard and tender
b) Presence of proteinuria
c) Terminal dribbling of urine
d) Urethral discharge
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Terminal dribbling of urine

Explanation: BPH obstructs the urethra, causing hesitancy, weak stream, and terminal dribbling.

22.
Superficial partial thickness burn is characterized by:
a) Wound appearing reddened with possible blisters
b) Has mottled broken epidermis
c) Painless burned area
d) Leathery pale white wound
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Wound appearing reddened with possible blisters

Explanation: Partial thickness burns (2nd degree) are painful, red, and blistered.

23.
An example of extrinsic muscle of the tongue is:
a) Longitudinalis superior
b) Genioglossus
c) Trasversus linguae
d) Verticalis linguae
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Genioglossus

Explanation: Genioglossus is an extrinsic muscle (moves tongue position). The others are intrinsic muscles (change tongue shape).

24.
A scrubbed sterile member of an operating team:
a) Primary surgeon
b) Anaesthetist
c) Receiving nurse
d) Sluice nurse
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Primary surgeon

Explanation: The surgeon and scrub nurse are sterile. The anesthetist and circulating/receiving nurses are non-sterile.

25.
Metabolic disturbances in chronic renal failure include:
a) Hyperglycaemia
b) Hypernatraemia
c) Anaemia
d) Stomatitis
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Hyperglycaemia

Explanation: CRF causes insulin resistance and impaired insulin clearance, leading to metabolic disturbances like hyperglycemia. While anemia is common, it is hematologic.

26.
The mucosa of the urinary bladder is made up of:
a) Simple columnar epithelium
b) Transitional epithelium
c) Stratified squamous epithelium
d) Keratinized epitrichium
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Transitional epithelium

Explanation: Transitional epithelium (urothelium) allows the bladder to stretch as it fills.

27.
Which of the following are functions of the liver?
a) Vitamin metabolism, bile formation, fat metabolism
b) Protein metabolism, glucose metabolism, vitamin storage
c) Ammonia conversion, bile formation, fat metabolism
d) Bile storage, fat metabolism, protein metabolism
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Ammonia conversion, bile formation, fat metabolism

Explanation: The liver converts ammonia to urea, produces bile (storage is in gallbladder, making D wrong), and metabolizes fats.

28.
One of the following is a cause of neurogenic shock:
a) Acute myocardial infarction
b) Traumatic blood loss
c) Spinal cord injury
d) Severe allergic reaction
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Spinal cord injury

Explanation: Spinal cord injury (especially above T6) disrupts the sympathetic nervous system, causing loss of vascular tone (neurogenic shock).

29.
A condition in which the right ventricle of the heart enlarges as a result of disease that affects the structure or function of the lung is referred to as:
a) Acute respiratory distress syndrome
b) Cor pulmonale
c) Empyema
d) Heart failure
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Cor pulmonale

Explanation: Cor pulmonale is right-sided heart failure caused by pulmonary hypertension resulting from lung disease.

30.
Non modifiable risk factors for atherosclerosis include:
a) Age, gender
b) Nicotine use, hypertension
c) Obesity, stress
d) Diabetes mellitus, diet
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Age, gender

Explanation: You cannot change your age or genetics/gender (Non-modifiable). Lifestyle factors like diet, smoking, and weight are modifiable.

31.
Characteristics of gastric ulcers include:
a) Weight loss
b) Vomiting is common
c) Rarely cause gastric carcinoma
d) Constitutes 80% of peptic ulcers
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Weight loss

Explanation: Eating often exacerbates pain in gastric ulcers, leading to food avoidance and weight loss. Duodenal ulcers are relieved by food (weight gain). Duodenal ulcers are 80% of cases (making D wrong).

32.
As per Dukes classification in staging of colorectal cancer Class C is when:
a) Tumor extends to the mucosa
b) Tumor extends the entire bowel wall and no nodal involvement
c) Tumor extends the entire bowel wall and has nodal involvement
d) Tumor has metastasized to other structures like lung and bone marrow
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Tumor extends the entire bowel wall and has nodal involvement

Explanation: Duke's C implies lymph node involvement. A/B are localized. D is Duke's D (Metastasis).

33.
A characteristic of Hepatitis A is:
a) It's transmitted through blood
b) Has carriers state
c) It's transmitted through fecal oral
d) It can progress to hepatic cancer
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) It's transmitted through fecal oral

Explanation: Hepatitis A is spread by contaminated food/water (fecal-oral). Hep B/C are blood-borne.

34.
A patient with pheochromocytoma will present with:
a) Hypercalcemia
b) Increased levels of catecholamines plasma
c) Increased levels of blood urea nitrogen and creatine levels
d) Hyperkalemia
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Increased levels of catecholamines plasma

Explanation: Pheochromocytoma is a tumor of the adrenal medulla that secretes excess catecholamines (adrenaline/noradrenaline).

35.
Effects of parasympathetic nervous system in the body include:
a) Increased heart rate
b) Increased secretions in GIT and peristalsis
c) Broncho dilatation
d) Vasodilatation of blood vessels
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Increased secretions in GIT and peristalsis

Explanation: Parasympathetic is "Rest and Digest" - it stimulates digestion (peristalsis/secretions). It slows heart rate and constricts bronchi.

36.
A patient with indigestion is said to be suffering from:
a) Dysplasia
b) Dyspnea
c) Dysphagia
d) Dyspepsia
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Dyspepsia

Explanation: Dyspepsia is the medical term for indigestion. Dysphagia is difficulty swallowing. Dyspnea is difficulty breathing.

37.
The priority nursing intervention for the unconscious patient is:
a) Administer oxygen
b) Assess hydration status
c) Establish and maintain airway
d) Administer intravenous fluid
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Establish and maintain airway

Explanation: ABCs: Airway is always the first priority in an unconscious patient to prevent hypoxia and aspiration.

38.
Clinical features of hyperthyroidism include:
a) Nervousness, exophthalmos
b) Tetany, carpopedal spasms
c) Dark pigmentation of mucous membrane and skin, shock
d) Buffalo hump, kyphosis
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Nervousness, exophthalmos

Explanation: Exophthalmos (bulging eyes) and nervousness/anxiety are classic signs of Graves' disease (Hyperthyroidism). Tetany is hypocalcemia. Buffalo hump is Cushing's.

39.
Needles are necessary in surgery for:
a) Approximating tissue together
b) Stitching tissues
c) Placing of sutures into tissues
d) Minimizing trauma to tissues
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Placing of sutures into tissues

Explanation: The needle is the vehicle that carries the suture material through the tissue.

40.
One of the following is a part of periodontium:
a) Alveolar bone
b) Tooth
c) Tongue
d) Visceral peritoneum
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Alveolar bone

Explanation: The periodontium (supporting structures of teeth) includes the gingiva, periodontal ligament, cementum, and alveolar bone.

SECTION 2: SHORT ANSWER QUESTIONS (40 MARKS)

41.
State any FIVE complications of liver cirrhosis (5 marks)
Show Answer
  1. Ascites: Fluid accumulation in the abdomen.
  2. Esophageal Varices: Dilated veins causing bleeding.
  3. Hepatic Encephalopathy: Confusion due to ammonia buildup.
  4. Coagulopathy: Bleeding disorders due to lack of clotting factors.
  5. Jaundice: Yellowing due to bilirubin buildup.
42.
State any FIVE risk factors to laryngeal obstruction (5 marks)
Show Answer
  1. Anaphylaxis: Severe allergic reaction causing laryngeal edema.
  2. Foreign Body Aspiration: Choking on food or objects.
  3. Infection: Epiglottitis or severe Laryngitis (Croup).
  4. Tumors: Laryngeal cancer.
  5. Trauma: Injury to the neck/throat.
43.
Outline FIVE clinical features of a patient with cerebrovascular accident (CVA) (5 marks)
Show Answer
  1. Hemiplegia/Hemiparesis: Paralysis or weakness on one side.
  2. Aphasia/Dysphasia: Difficulty speaking or understanding speech.
  3. Visual Disturbances: Blurred vision or loss of vision in one eye.
  4. Severe Headache: Sudden onset (hemorrhagic).
  5. Facial Droop: Asymmetry of the face.
44.
Describe the pathophysiology of head injury (5 marks)
Show Answer
  1. Primary Injury: Direct trauma causes contusion, laceration, or shearing of brain tissue.
  2. Cerebral Edema: Inflammation leads to swelling of brain tissue.
  3. Increased Intracranial Pressure (ICP): Swelling in the rigid skull raises pressure.
  4. Decreased Perfusion: High ICP reduces Cerebral Perfusion Pressure (CPP), causing ischemia.
  5. Secondary Injury: Hypoxia and ischemia lead to further neuronal death.
45.
Explain any FIVE complications of a patient with chronic renal failure (5 marks)
Show Answer
  1. Hyperkalemia: High potassium levels leading to arrhythmias.
  2. Hypertension: Due to fluid overload and RAAS activation.
  3. Anemia: Due to lack of Erythropoietin.
  4. Renal Osteodystrophy: Bone weakness due to calcium/phosphate imbalance.
  5. Pulmonary Edema: Fluid accumulation in lungs.
46.
Explain THREE complications of Asthma (6 marks)
Show Answer
  1. Status Asthmaticus: Severe, life-threatening attack unresponsive to treatment.
  2. Respiratory Failure: Fatigue and obstruction lead to hypoxia and CO2 retention.
  3. Atelectasis: Lung collapse due to mucous plugging.
  4. (Alternative) Pneumothorax: Bursting of alveoli due to air trapping.
47.
State TWO risk factors to bladder cancer (4 marks)
Show Answer
  1. Cigarette Smoking: Major risk factor.
  2. Occupational Exposure: Chemicals like dyes, rubber, leather.
  3. (Alternative) Chronic Bladder Infections or Schistosomiasis.
48.
Outline steps in nursing process (5 marks)
Show Answer
  1. Assessment: Data collection.
  2. Diagnosis: Identifying the problem.
  3. Planning: Setting goals.
  4. Implementation: Carrying out interventions.
  5. Evaluation: Checking outcomes.

SECTION 3: LONG ANSWER QUESTION (20 MARKS)

49.
Madam X was brought to the surgical ward with severe acute abdominal pain and a diagnosis of intestinal obstruction was made.
a) Draw an illustrated diagram showing the organs of gastro-intestinal tract (6 marks)
b) State any FOUR causes of mechanical intestinal obstruction (4 marks)
c) Describe the management you would give to Madam X following emergency laparotomy (10 marks)
Show Answer

a) Diagram of GIT:

(Diagram should label: Mouth, Oesophagus, Stomach, Small Intestine (Duodenum, Jejunum, Ileum), Large Intestine (Colon), Rectum, Anus).

b) Causes of Mechanical Obstruction:

  1. Adhesions: Scar tissue from previous surgery.
  2. Hernias: Strangulation of bowel.
  3. Volvulus: Twisting of bowel.
  4. Intussusception: Telescoping of bowel.
  5. Tumors: Colorectal cancer blocking the lumen.

c) Post-operative Management (Laparotomy):

  • Immediate Care: Airway maintenance, O2 therapy, Monitor Vitals q15min.
  • Pain Management: Administer analgesics (e.g., Morphine) as prescribed.
  • Fluid/Electrolytes: Maintain IV fluids, monitor I/O strictly.
  • Gastrointestinal Decompression: Maintain Nasogastric Tube (NGT) suction until bowel sounds return (NPO status).
  • Wound Care: Monitor incision for bleeding/infection. Splint wound when coughing.
  • Mobilization: Encourage early ambulation to prevent DVT and ileus.
  • Education: Discharge planning and diet advice.
