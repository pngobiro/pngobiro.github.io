Part A: Human Nutrition

Section I: Multiple Choice Questions (5 Marks)

1.
Consumption of foods rich in dietary fiber and antioxidants is associated with:
A. Reduced risk of diarrhea
B. Reduced risk of certain cancers
C. Reduced risk of cardiovascular disorders
D. Reduced risk of fetal anomalies.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C. Reduced risk of cardiovascular disorders

Explanation: Dietary fiber helps lower cholesterol levels, and antioxidants reduce oxidative stress, both significantly reducing the risk of cardiovascular diseases. (Note: Option B is also true as fiber/antioxidants reduce cancer risk, but C is the most direct and broadly cited physiological benefit in this context alongside cancer).

2.
Indicate whether each of the following statement is either True or False;

A. Vitamin E reduces the risk of cardiovascular diseases and cancer.

Show Answer

Answer: True (Vitamin E is a potent antioxidant that protects cells from damage).

B. Vitamin supplements contain Calories and can replace a healthy diet.

Show Answer

Answer: False (Vitamins are micronutrients and do not provide energy/calories; they cannot replace the complex benefits of whole foods).

3.
The following statements describes enteral feeding
A. It is used to deliver nutrients directly to the blood stream
B. Feeding by passes the GIT
C. Indicated when the GIT is functional.
D. Metabolic complications are most common when using this method of feeding.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C. Indicated when the GIT is functional.

Explanation: Enteral feeding uses the gastrointestinal tract (via tube). It is preferred whenever the gut is working ("If the gut works, use it"). Option A and B describe Parenteral feeding.

4.
The caloric value of a patient with diabetes should be:
A. Increased above normal requirements to meet the increased metabolic demand
B. Decreased below normal requirements to prevent glucose formation
C. The individual's normal energy requirement to maintain ideal weight.
D. Contributed mainly by fat to spare carbohydrate
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C. The individual's normal energy requirement to maintain ideal weight.

Explanation: The goal of medical nutrition therapy in diabetes is to maintain a healthy body weight and control blood glucose. Caloric intake is matched to the individual's needs for their ideal weight, not arbitrarily increased or decreased.

5.
Dietary modifications in the management of hepatitis includes:
A. A gradual decrease in energy intake to below 20% less than the normal diet
B. Provide normal protein but of high biological value to ensure maximum utilization
C. Most of the energy should be provided by fats
D. Limit the amounts of vitamins B and C due to malabsorption
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B. Provide normal protein but of high biological value to ensure maximum utilization

Explanation: In hepatitis, the liver needs protein for regeneration. Unless there is hepatic encephalopathy, normal to slightly high protein of high biological value is recommended. Energy should come from carbohydrates; fats are often restricted if they cause nausea.

Section II: Short Answer Questions (Nutrition)

1.
Explain three (3) methods used in nutritional status assessment. (6 marks)
Show Answer
  1. Anthropometric Methods: Measurement of physical dimensions and gross composition of the body (e.g., Height, Weight, Mid-Upper Arm Circumference - MUAC, BMI).
  2. Biochemical Methods: Laboratory tests on body fluids (blood, urine) to detect sub-clinical deficiencies (e.g., Hemoglobin for anemia, Serum Albumin for protein status).
  3. Clinical Methods: Physical examination for visible signs of malnutrition (e.g., Bitot's spots for Vit A, Pallor for Iron, Edema for Kwashiorkor).
  4. Dietary Methods: Assessment of food consumption patterns (e.g., 24-hour recall, Food Frequency Questionnaire).

(Any 3 points explained)

2.
State four (4) functions of protein. (4 marks)
Show Answer
  1. Growth and Maintenance: Building and repairing body tissues (muscles, skin, organs).
  2. Enzymatic Activity: Functioning as enzymes to catalyze metabolic reactions.
  3. Immunity: Formation of antibodies (immunoglobulins) to fight infection.
  4. Fluid Balance: Maintaining oncotic pressure (Albumin) to prevent edema.
  5. Energy Source: Providing 4 kcal/g of energy when carbohydrates and fats are insufficient.
3.
Explain three (3) pillars of food security. (6 marks)
Show Answer
  1. Availability: The existence of sufficient quantities of food of appropriate quality, supplied through domestic production or imports.
  2. Accessibility: Access by individuals to adequate resources (entitlements) for acquiring appropriate foods for a nutritious diet.
  3. Utilization: Utilization of food through adequate diet, clean water, sanitation, and health care to reach a state of nutritional well-being where all physiological needs are met.
  4. Stability: To be food secure, a population, household or individual must have access to adequate food at all times (no risk of losing access due to sudden shocks).

(Any 3 explained)

4.
State six (6) Nutritional interventions in prevention of malnutrition in the community. (6 marks)
Show Answer
  1. Promotion of Exclusive Breastfeeding: For the first 6 months of life.
  2. Vitamin A Supplementation: Routine dosing for children under 5 years.
  3. Deworming: Regular deworming to prevent nutrient loss through parasites.
  4. Growth Monitoring and Promotion: Regular weighing of children to detect faltering growth early.
  5. Food Fortification: Adding micronutrients to staple foods (e.g., Iodine in salt, Folate in flour).
  6. Nutrition Education: Teaching mothers about balanced diets and complementary feeding.
  7. Therapeutic Feeding Programs: OTP (Outpatient Therapeutic Program) and SFP (Supplementary Feeding Program) for acute malnutrition.

Part B: Health Promotion

Section I: Multiple Choice Questions (15 Marks)

1.
Behavior change approach in health promotion;
A. Involves medical intervention to prevent or ameliorate ill-health
B. Aim is to change people’s individual attitudes and habits so that they adopt a healthy lifestyle
C. Help in carrying out those decisions and adopting new health practices may also be offered
D. Aim is to work with clients in order to help them to identify what they want to know about and take action on and make their own decisions and choices according to their own interest and values
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B. Aim is to change people’s individual attitudes and habits so that they adopt a healthy lifestyle

Explanation: The behavior change approach focuses on encouraging individuals to adopt healthier behaviors (e.g., stop smoking, exercise) by changing attitudes and knowledge. Option D describes the client-centered or empowerment approach.

2.
The approach to health promotion that is based on the assumption that humans are rational decision-makers refers to;
A. Behavior change approach
B. Community development approach
C. Biomedical approach
D. Humanitarian model
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A. Behavior change approach

Explanation: This approach assumes that if people are given the correct information and understand the risks, they will rationally choose to change their behavior to improve their health.

3.
The combination of learning experiences designed to help individuals and communities improve their health, by increasing their knowledge or influencing their attitudes is referred to:
A. Health promotion
B. Health education
C. Health literacy
D. Health policy
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B. Health education

Explanation: Health Education is specifically defined as the learning experiences (communication of information) designed to facilitate voluntary adaptation of behavior conducive to health. Health Promotion is a broader concept that includes education plus environmental/political support.

4.
The nurse educator planning a smoking cessation program understands that the most basic type of health promotion program is
A. Utilizing a variety of media for information dissemination
B. Conducting health risk surveys
C. Providing counseling for lifestyle and behavior change
D. Facilitating environmental control programs
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A. Utilizing a variety of media for information dissemination

Explanation: Information dissemination (awareness raising) is considered the most basic level of health promotion intervention, often the first step before more intensive counseling or environmental changes.

5.
Indicate whether it’s true or false in the following criticism of the behavior change approach to health promotion

A. It is unable to target the major causes of ill health.

Show Answer

Answer: True. (It focuses on individual behavior and often ignores the underlying social determinants of health like poverty or environment).

B. The choice of which behavior to change lies with ‘experts’ whose task it is to communicate and justify this choice to the public.

Show Answer

Answer: True. (This is a valid criticism; the agenda is often set by professionals rather than the community).

6.
Distributing stress management pamphlets and presenting a poster exhibit at a health fair is an example of;
A. Information dissemination
B. Health risk appraisal and wellness assessment
C. Lifestyle and behavior change
D. Environmental control program
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A. Information dissemination

Explanation: Pamphlets and posters are one-way communication tools used to spread information to a broad audience.

7.
The modifiable determinants of health include;
A. Hereditary, change of lifestyle, cigarette smoking
B. Biological, unsafe sex, eating habits
C. Genetics, alcohol consumption, safe sex
D. Cigarette smoking, alcohol consumption, unsafe sex
Show Answer

Correct Answer: D. Cigarette smoking, alcohol consumption, unsafe sex

Explanation: Modifiable determinants are those that an individual can change (behaviors). Hereditary, Genetics, and Biology (in options A, B, C) are generally non-modifiable.

8.
It’s important to do community dialogue since it;
A. Builds a culture that supports healthy life choices and a high quality of life
B. Reinforce government policies on matters of health
C. Is a way of boosting the morale of health care workers in the community?
D. Is a first step for health workers to partner with community members?
Show Answer

Correct Answer: D. Is a first step for health workers to partner with community members?

Explanation: Community dialogue is a participatory process that initiates the partnership between the health system and the community, allowing for problem identification and ownership.

9.
Social health is;
A. Ability to make and maintain acceptable interaction with other people
B. Ability to think clearly
C. A state where people are unambiguously in control and self determining
D. Behavior motivated by the desire to increase wellbeing and actualize human health potential
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A. Ability to make and maintain acceptable interaction with other people

Explanation: Social health refers to the ability to form satisfying interpersonal relationships and adapt to social situations.

10.
Which of the following is a priority action area for health promotion?
A. Setting for health practical opportunities
B. Sustaining efforts of community members
C. Strengthening community action for health
D. Tracking strategies on implementation
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C. Strengthening community action for health

Explanation: "Strengthening Community Action" is one of the 5 key action areas defined in the Ottawa Charter for Health Promotion.

11.
Educational approach in health promotion:

B. Aim is to change people’s individual attitudes and habits so that they adopt a healthy lifestyle
C. Help in carrying out those decisions and adopting new health practices may also be offered
D. Aim is to work with clients in order to help them to identify what they want to know about and take action on and make their own decisions and choices according to their own interest and values
Show Answer

Correct Answer: D. Aim is to work with clients in order to help them to identify what they want to know about and take action on and make their own decisions and choices according to their own interest and values

Explanation: The educational approach focuses on providing knowledge and developing skills so that individuals can make informed, voluntary choices about their health.

12.
Client – centre approach in health promotion:
A. Involves medical intervention to prevent or ameliorate ill-health
B. Aim is to change people’s individual attitudes and habits so that they adopt a healthy lifestyle
C. Help in carrying out those decisions and adopting new health practices may also be offered
D. Aim is to work with clients in order to help them to identify what they want to know about and take action on and make their own decisions and choices according to their own interest and values

Correct Answer: D. Aim is to work with clients in order to help them to identify what they want to know about and take action on and make their own decisions and choices according to their own interest and values

13.

14.

15.

Section II: Short Answer Questions (Health Promotion)

16.
Describe five (5) key levels of health promotion as suggested by Ottawa Charter and published in 1986. (5 marks)
Show Answer
  1. Building Healthy Public Policy: Putting health on the agenda of policy makers in all sectors (legislation, fiscal measures, taxation).
  2. Creating Supportive Environments: Generating living and working conditions that are safe, stimulating, satisfying, and enjoyable.
  3. Strengthening Community Action: Empowering communities to set priorities, make decisions, and plan strategies for better health.
  4. Developing Personal Skills: Providing information and education for health to enhance life skills and decision-making.
  5. Reorienting Health Services: Moving beyond clinical/curative services to a focus on health promotion and prevention.
17.
As a member of Non-governmental organization that is in the process of starting a feeding programme in a hunger striken community. Explain how you can use the principles of health promotion to ensure success of the programme. (5 marks)
Show Answer
  1. Participation: Involve community members in the planning and distribution of food to ensure ownership and relevance.
  2. Empowerment: Train local community health volunteers to manage the program, reducing dependency.
  3. Equity: Ensure the most vulnerable (children, elderly, pregnant women) are prioritized in distribution.
  4. Sustainability: Combine immediate feeding with long-term agricultural education to prevent future hunger.
  5. Inter-sectoral Collaboration: Work with local government, agriculture officers, and water department to address root causes.
18.
State five (5) aims of health education. (5 marks)
Show Answer
  1. To encourage people to adopt and sustain health-promoting lifestyles and practices.
  2. To promote the proper use of health services available to the community.
  3. To arouse interest, provide new knowledge, improve skills, and change attitudes in making rational decisions.
  4. To stimulate individual and community self-reliance and participation in health development.
  5. To help people understand that health is a valuable community asset.
19.
Outline five (5) principles of health education. (5 marks)
Show Answer
  1. Credibility: The message should be trustworthy and based on facts.
  2. Interest: Education should relate to the felt needs and interests of the people.
  3. Participation: Active involvement of the learners leads to better retention and acceptance.
  4. Motivation: People change behavior when they have a strong desire/motive to do so.
  5. Comprehension: The level of teaching must be within the mental capacity and literacy level of the audience.
  6. Reinforcement: Repetition is necessary for learning to stick.

Section III: Long Answer Questions (40 Marks)

20.
Discuss the steps of organizing community dialogue. (20 marks)
Show Answer

Community dialogue is a forum that draws participants from the community to exchange information and share views.

  1. Preparation and Planning (Entry):
    • Identify the issue to be discussed (e.g., poor sanitation).
    • Identify key stakeholders and opinion leaders.
    • Select a suitable venue (neutral, accessible) and time.
    • Mobilize the community and send invitations.
  2. Setting the Climate (Introduction):
    • Welcome participants and introduce the facilitators.
    • Explain the purpose and objectives of the dialogue.
    • Set ground rules (respect, one person speaks at a time).
    • Create a relaxed, non-threatening atmosphere.
  3. Problem Identification and Analysis:
    • Facilitate discussion to identify the core problem.
    • Use participatory methods (e.g., problem tree) to analyze root causes and effects.
    • Allow everyone to air their views (ensure gender balance).
  4. Generating Solutions:
    • Brainstorm possible solutions to the identified problems.
    • Discuss the feasibility of each solution using available local resources.
    • Reach a consensus on the best way forward.
  5. Action Planning:
    • Develop an action plan: What will be done? Who will do it? When? With what resources?
    • Form a committee or assign roles for implementation.
  6. Closing and Way Forward:
    • Summarize the key points and decisions made.
    • Agree on a date for the next meeting or follow-up.
    • Thank the participants.
21.
You are a team leader in a Rural facility and you are to carry out a health education on Malaria. What would be your roles as Community Health Nurse? (20 marks)
Show Answer

1. Assessment and Diagnosis (Pre-planning):

  • Conduct a community needs assessment to determine the prevalence of Malaria and gaps in knowledge/practice.
  • Identify the target audience (e.g., pregnant women, mothers of under-5s).
  • Assess available resources (IEC materials, staff, time).

2. Planning:

  • Formulate clear objectives for the session (e.g., "Mothers will be able to demonstrate correct ITN use").
  • Develop lesson content (Causes, transmission, signs/symptoms, prevention, treatment).
  • Select appropriate teaching methods (Demonstration, Discussion, Role-play).
  • Prepare teaching aids (Posters, ITN nets for demo).
  • Schedule the venue and time convenient for the community.

3. Implementation (Teaching):

  • Organizer: Arrange the physical environment (seating, lighting).
  • Communicator: Deliver the message clearly, using local language and simple terms.
  • Facilitator: Encourage participation and questions from the audience.
  • Demonstrator: Show practical skills (e.g., how to hang a net, how to mix anti-malarials).
  • Clarifier: Correct myths and misconceptions about Malaria.

4. Evaluation:

  • Assess understanding during the session (ask questions, return demonstration).
  • Evaluate the outcome (e.g., increased uptake of ITNs, early health seeking behavior).
  • Get feedback from the community on the session's effectiveness.

5. Coordination and Networking:

  • Collaborate with community leaders and CHVs to mobilize the community.
  • Link with other sectors (e.g., environment for drainage) for holistic malaria control.
