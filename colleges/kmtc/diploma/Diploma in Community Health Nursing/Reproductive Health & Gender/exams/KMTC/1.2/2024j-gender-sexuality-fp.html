Module A: Gender & Sexuality in Reproductive Health

Section I: Multiple Choice Questions (10 Marks)

1.
Social construct characteristics specific to either male or female is known as;
A. Gender rights.
B. Gender blind.
C. Gender equality.
D. Gender.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: D. Gender.

Explanation: Gender refers to the socially constructed characteristics of women and men, such as norms, roles, and relationships. "Sex" refers to biological characteristics.

2.
A person's internal identification as male or female is referred to as;
A. Gender identity
B. Sexual background
C. Sex
D. Sexuality
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A. Gender identity

Explanation: Gender identity is a person's deeply felt internal and individual experience of gender, which may or may not correspond with the sex assigned at birth.

3.
One of the following best describes 'human sexuality'
A. The way people experience and express themselves sexually
B. The way humans use sexuality to reproduce
C. The science of gender and sex
D. The human sexual activities
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A. The way people experience and express themselves sexually

Explanation: Human sexuality encompasses how people experience and express themselves as sexual beings, including biological, psychological, social, and cultural aspects.

4.
Ms. K was told by her mother that a woman's place is in the kitchen. This is an example of;
A. Gender analysis
B. Community stereotype
C. Gender discrimination
D. Marginalization of women
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B. Community stereotype

Explanation: This is a gender stereotype—a generalized view or preconception about attributes or characteristics, or the roles that are or ought to be possessed by, or performed by, women and men.

5.
Freedom over sexual activity and reproduction, in particular with reference to sexual orientation and access to contraception and healthcare is called
A. Conjugal right
B. Sexual right
C. Sex right
D. Human right
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B. Sexual right

Explanation: Sexual rights embrace human rights that are already recognized in national laws, international human rights documents and other consensus statements. They include the right of all persons, free of coercion, discrimination and violence, to the highest attainable standard of sexual health.

6.
In some cultures women stay outside their homes during monthly period Indicating;
A. Gender discrimination.
B. Gender violence.
C. Gender trend.
D. Gender sensitivity
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A. Gender discrimination.

Explanation: Restricting women's movement or access to the home based on biological functions (menstruation) is a form of discrimination and a harmful cultural practice (taboo).

7.
An aspect of sexuality that indicates how we feel and appear to others is;
A. Gender role.
B. Relationship.
C. Sexual preference.
D. Body image
Show Answer

Correct Answer: D. Body image

Explanation: Body image involves a person's perception of the aesthetics or sexual attractiveness of their own body.

8.
The following statements is true about women empowerment.
A. It benefits not only themselves but the society as whole.
B. Enhances independence and physical structure
C. It postpones marriage and child bearing.
D. Initiates women to have higher levels of knowledge
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A. It benefits not only themselves but the society as whole.

Explanation: Women's empowerment is widely recognized as essential for sustainable development, benefiting families, communities, and nations through improved health, education, and economic growth.

9.
The strategies that are used to empower women include;
A. Encourage women to play multiple roles in the community.
B. Implement policies that uphold women rights.
C. Tightening male domination in work force.
D. Reducing Labour input of women.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B. Implement policies that uphold women rights.

Explanation: Legislative and policy frameworks protecting women's rights (land ownership, protection from violence, equal pay) are fundamental strategies for empowerment.

10.
The complications of female genital mutilation in sexual health of the youth include;
A. Failure to reach orgasm, STI
B. Multiple Fistula during birth, STI
C. Early marriage, STI
D. Early marriage, multiple fistulas during birth
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A. Failure to reach orgasm, STI

Explanation: The question specifically asks for "sexual health" complications. FGM involves removal of the clitoris (essential for sexual pleasure/orgasm). While fistula (B/D) is a complication, it is obstetric. Early marriage (C/D) is a social consequence.

Section II: Short Answer Questions (20 Marks)

1.
Explain three tools used in Gender Analysis. (6 marks)
Show Answer
  1. Activity Profile (Harvard Framework): Identifies who does what (productive, reproductive, and community roles) by gender and age.
  2. Access and Control Profile: Analyses who has access to resources (land, credit, education) and who controls the benefits derived from them.
  3. Daily Activity Clock (24-hour schedule): Maps out how men and women spend their time throughout the day to identify workloads and leisure time.
  4. Influencing Factors: Identifies external factors (culture, economics, politics) that shape gender relations.
2.
Outline four determinants of sexual health among the adolescents. (4 marks)
Show Answer
  1. Access to Information and Services: Availability of Youth Friendly Services and comprehensive sexuality education.
  2. Socio-Cultural Norms: Peer pressure, religious beliefs, and cultural practices (e.g., FGM, early marriage).
  3. Economic Factors: Poverty leading to transactional sex or lack of access to commodities.
  4. Biological Factors: Puberty, hormonal changes, and physical maturation.
  5. Psychological Factors: Self-esteem, body image, and mental health.
3.
Explain three factors associated with sexual orientation. (6 marks)
Show Answer
  1. Biological Factors: Genetic predisposition and hormonal exposure during prenatal development are believed to play a role.
  2. Environmental Factors: While less defined, some theories suggest early childhood experiences and social interactions may contribute, though no single factor has been isolated.
  3. Psychological Factors: Individual developmental processes and identity formation.
  4. (Note: Scientific consensus is that sexual orientation is likely caused by a complex interplay of genetic, hormonal, and environmental influences, and is not a "choice".)
4.
Explain two characteristics of youth friendly services. (4 marks)
Show Answer
  1. Privacy and Confidentiality: Services must ensure that information shared by youth is kept private and consultations are confidential to build trust.
  2. Non-judgmental Staff: Healthcare providers should be trained to be respectful, empathetic, and supportive, avoiding moral judgment of adolescent behaviors.
  3. Accessibility: Convenient hours (e.g., after school/weekends), affordable (or free), and location should be easily reachable.

Section III: Long Answer Questions (20 Marks)

1.
A 16-year-old girl comes to the youth friendly clinic for counselling after gender-based violence. Discuss in detail the counselling needs of adolescents and describe the benefits of Gender equality to an individual, community and the nation as a whole. (20 marks)
Show Answer

Part 1: Counselling Needs of Adolescents (10 Marks)

  • Crisis Intervention: Immediate psychological first aid to stabilize emotions, ensure safety, and address shock or trauma from the GBV incident.
  • Confidentiality and Trust: Assurance that her story is safe (except where mandatory reporting laws apply), creating a safe space for disclosure.
  • Information on Rights and Options: Guidance on legal rights, reporting mechanisms (Police/Gender desk), and medical options (PEP, ECP, STI treatment).
  • Empowerment and Self-Esteem: Rebuilding confidence and self-worth, which are often damaged by abuse. Helping her realize she is not to blame.
  • Sexual and Reproductive Health Education: Providing accurate information on safe sex, consent, anatomy, and prevention of pregnancy/STIs.
  • Safety Planning: Developing a plan to stay safe from future harm, identifying safe contacts and shelters.

Part 2: Benefits of Gender Equality (10 Marks)

To the Individual:

  • Autonomy: Freedom to make life choices regarding career, marriage, and health.
  • Health: Better access to healthcare and reduced risk of GBV/HIV.
  • Economic Independence: Equal pay and opportunity lead to financial stability.

To the Community:

  • Reduced Poverty: When women work and earn, they invest 90% of income back into families, lifting communities out of poverty.
  • Improved Health Indicators: Educated and empowered mothers raise healthier, better-educated children.
  • Social Cohesion: Reduced violence and discrimination creates a more peaceful society.

To the Nation:

  • Economic Growth (GDP): Full utilization of the workforce (men and women) boosts national productivity.
  • Sustainable Development: Gender equality is a prerequisite for achieving other SDGs (health, education, zero hunger).
  • Good Governance: Diverse leadership leads to more inclusive and effective policies.

Module B: Family Planning & Reproductive Health

Section I: Multiple Choice Questions (10 Marks)

1.
When performing a preconception visit, the following assessment finding by the nurse would necessitate a recommendation for genetic counseling;
A. History of heavy alcohol use 2 years prior
B. Family history of genetic disorders
C. Client is 33 years old
D. Exposure to secondhand smoke
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B. Family history of genetic disorders

Explanation: Genetic counseling is specifically indicated when there is a known family history of hereditary conditions (e.g., Sickle Cell, Cystic Fibrosis) to assess risk to offspring.

2.
A client has just been fitted for a diaphragm, the following information about the diaphragm should be included by nurse in the client teaching;
A. It should be replaced every 3 years
B. It must be left in place for 12 hours after intercourse.
C. It must be refitted after childbirth.
D. It can be use during a menstrual period.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C. It must be refitted after childbirth.

Explanation: The cervix and vaginal size change after pregnancy/birth (and significant weight change), requiring re-fitting of the diaphragm to ensure effectiveness. It should be left in for 6 hours (not 12 minimum) and avoided during menses (TSS risk).

3.
A client in the family clinic asks the nurse about the most likely time for her to conceive. The nurse explains that conception is most likely to occur when:
A. Estrogen levels are low.
B. Luteinizing hormone is high
C. The endometrial lining is thin
D. The progesterone level is low
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B. Luteinizing hormone is high

Explanation: The "LH Surge" triggers ovulation (release of the egg). This is the most fertile window for conception. Estrogen is high just before this, and progesterone rises after (luteal phase).

4.
The following instructions about activities during menstruation would the nurse include when counseling an adolescent who has just begun to menstruate;
A. Take mild analgesics if needed for menstrual pain.
B. Stop exercise while menstruating
C. Avoid sexual intercourse during menstruation
D. Avoid cold foods if menstrual pain persists.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A. Take mild analgesics if needed for menstrual pain.

Explanation: Dysmenorrhea is common in adolescents. Mild analgesics (NSAIDS) are appropriate. Exercise is actually beneficial for cramps. Avoiding cold foods is a cultural myth.

5.
A client with diabetes asks the nurse for advice regarding methods of birth control. The following method of birth control is most suitable;
A. Intrauterine device
B. Oral contraceptives
C. Diaphragm
D. Contraceptive sponge
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A. Intrauterine device

Explanation: Copper IUDs have no hormonal effect on glucose metabolism or cardiovascular risk, making them ideal for diabetics (WHO MEC Category 1). Hormonal methods (like COCs) may have risks depending on severity of diabetes (vascular involvement).

6.
Before advising a 25-year-old client desiring oral contraceptives for family planning, the nurse would assess the client for signs and symptoms of the following;
A. Anemia
B. Hypertension
C. Dysmenorrhea
D. Acne vulgaris
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B. Hypertension

Explanation: Hypertension is a contraindication for Combined Oral Contraceptives (COCs) due to increased risk of stroke and heart disease. BP must be checked before prescription. COCs often improve acne, anemia, and dysmenorrhea.

7.
A 22-year-old nulligravid client tells the nurse that she and her husband have been considering using condoms for family planning. The following instructions are included about the use of condoms;
A. Using spermicide with the condom offers added protection against pregnancy.
B. Natural skin condoms protect against sexually transmitted diseases
C. The typical failure rate for couples using condoms is about 25%
D. Condom users frequently report penile gland sensitivity
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A. Using spermicide with the condom offers added protection against pregnancy.

Explanation: Combining barriers enhances efficacy. Note: Natural skin (lambskin) condoms do NOT protect against viruses (HIV/Hep B) due to large pores.

8.
Mode of action of copper IUDs in preventing pregnancy;
A. Inhibition of ovulation
B. Thickening of cervical mucus
C. Disruption of implantation of fertilized egg
D. Modulation of hormones levels
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C. Disruption of implantation of fertilized egg

Explanation: Copper ions are spermicidal (primary mechanism - killing sperm before fertilization). However, among the options provided, the inflammatory reaction preventing implantation is a secondary mechanism. Options A, B, and D are mechanisms of hormonal contraceptives.

9.
Estrogen, one of the hormones regulating cyclic activities in female reproductive system is responsible for;
A. Increases the quantity and pH of cervical mucus, causing it to become thin and watery and can be stretched to a distance of 10-13 cm.
B. Inhibits the production of LH
C. Increases endometrial tortuosity
D. Increase the production of LH
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A. Increases the quantity and pH of cervical mucus, causing it to become thin and watery and can be stretched to a distance of 10-13 cm.

Explanation: This describes the Spinnbarkeit effect caused by high estrogen levels just before ovulation, facilitating sperm transport.

10.
Assessment of a 16-year-old nulligravid client who visits the clinic... for information on... ovulation and fertility management. The following would be important to include in the teaching plan for the client;
A. The ovum survives for 96 hours after ovulation...
B. The basal body temperature falls at least 0.2° F after ovulation has occurred.
C. Ovulation usually occurs on day 14, plus or minus 2 days, before the onset of the next menstrual cycle.
D. Most women can tell they have ovulated because of severe pain and thick, scant cervical mucus.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C. Ovulation usually occurs on day 14, plus or minus 2 days, before the onset of the next menstrual cycle.

Explanation: The luteal phase (post-ovulation) is relatively constant at 14 days. Ovum survival is 12-24 hours. BBT rises (not falls) after ovulation.

Section II: Short Answer Questions (20 Marks)

11.
State five social benefits of using family planning. (5 marks)
Show Answer
  1. Education: Allows parents to invest more in the education of each child (quality over quantity).
  2. Economic Stability: Smaller families generally have fewer expenses and more savings potential.
  3. Empowerment: Enables women to pursue careers, education, and participate in community life.
  4. Reduced Poverty: Helps break the cycle of poverty by balancing resources with family size.
  5. Better Living Conditions: Reduced overcrowding in homes and less strain on community resources.
12.
Explain how you would use acronym SOLER to make the session effective. (5 marks)
Show Answer
  • S - Sit squarely: Facing the client to show you are involved.
  • O - Open posture: Avoid crossing arms/legs to show openness and non-defensiveness.
  • L - Lean forward: Slightly towards the client to demonstrate interest and engagement.
  • E - Eye contact: Maintain appropriate eye contact to show you are listening (cultural sensitivity needed).
  • R - Relax: Maintain a relaxed demeanor to put the client at ease.
13.
State five danger signs/ serious side effects of using combined oral contraception. (5 marks)
Show Answer

Mnemonic: ACHES

  1. A - Abdominal pain (severe): Could indicate liver problems or gallbladder disease.
  2. C - Chest pain (severe), shortness of breath: Possible Pulmonary Embolism or Myocardial Infarction.
  3. H - Headaches (severe): Possible stroke, hypertension, or migraine.
  4. E - Eye problems (blurred vision, flashing lights): Possible stroke or hypertension.
  5. S - Severe leg pain (calf/thigh): Possible Deep Vein Thrombosis (DVT).
14.
State five non contraceptive benefits of using Progestin only pills. (5 marks)
Show Answer
  1. Reduced menstrual cramps (Dysmenorrhea).
  2. Reduced menstrual bleeding (less anemia).
  3. Decreased risk of Endometrial cancer.
  4. Decreased risk of Ovarian cancer.
  5. Management of Endometriosis symptoms.
  6. Reduced risk of Pelvic Inflammatory Disease (PID) due to thick cervical mucus.

Section III: Long Answer Questions (20 Marks)

15.
Sophia is a 30-year-old woman who is considering using combined oral contraceptive pills as method of family planning. She has come to you, as a nurse, for guidance and information.

i. Explain the mechanism of action of combined oral contraceptive pills in preventing pregnancy and how they affect the menstrual cycle. (10 marks)

Show Answer

Mechanism of Action:

  1. Inhibition of Ovulation (Primary Mechanism):
    • Estrogen: Suppresses Follicle Stimulating Hormone (FSH), preventing the development and maturation of the dominant follicle.
    • Progestin: Suppresses the Luteinizing Hormone (LH) surge, preventing the release of the egg (ovulation).
  2. Thickening of Cervical Mucus:
    • Progestin thickens the mucus, creating a barrier that is difficult for sperm to penetrate, thus preventing fertilization.
  3. Endometrial Atrophy:
    • The combination renders the endometrium thin and hostile, making it unsuitable for implantation should fertilization occur.
  4. Tubal Motility:
    • May alter fallopian tube peristalsis, affecting sperm/egg transport.

Effect on Menstrual Cycle:

  • Regularity: Creates a predictable "withdrawal bleed" during the hormone-free interval (placebo week), mimicking a regular 28-day cycle.
  • Volume/Pain: Reduces the amount of bleeding and menstrual cramps (prostaglandin inhibition) due to thinner endometrium.

ii. Discuss the potential side effects and risks associated with using combined oral contraceptive pills, and provide recommendations on how to manage or minimize these side effects. (10 marks)

Show Answer
Side Effect/Risk Management/Minimization
Nausea Take pill with food or at bedtime. Usually resolves in 3 months.
Breast Tenderness Wear supportive bra; apply warm compresses. Reassure it's temporary.
Breakthrough Bleeding Reassure. Ensure consistent timing of daily dose. If persistent >3 months, may need dose adjustment.
Headaches Mild analgesics. If severe/migraine with aura, STOP immediately (stroke risk).
Weight Gain Diet and exercise counseling. Often due to fluid retention (temporary).
Thrombosis (DVT/PE) Screen for history of clots/smoking. Advise to stop smoking if >35. Teach warning signs (ACHES).
Hypertension Monitor BP regularly. If BP rises significantly, switch to progestin-only or non-hormonal method.
