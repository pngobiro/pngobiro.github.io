Module I: Gender & Sexual Health

Section I: Multiple Choice Questions (12 Marks)

1.
In African setup, hierarchical system of governance in which cultural, political and economic structures was dominated by males. This system is referred to;
A. Gendered division of labour
B. Pluralist Model
C. Elite Model
D. Patriarchy
Show Answer

Correct Answer: D. Patriarchy

Explanation: Patriarchy is a social system in which men hold primary power and predominate in roles of political leadership, moral authority, social privilege, and control of property.

2.
According to text, the terms masculinity and femininity are linked to.
A. Gender
B. Sex
C. Sexism
D. Patriarchy
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A. Gender

Explanation: Masculinity and femininity are sets of qualities, characteristics, or roles generally considered typical of, or appropriate for, men and women respectively. They are social constructs (Gender), whereas male and female are biological categories (Sex).

3.
Male and female have come together to work in various aspect of life, where the rights, responsibilities, expectations, and relationships of women and men. Is refers to:
A. Gender roles
B. Gender Diversity
C. Gender Equality
D. Gender Equity
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C. Gender Equality

Explanation: Gender Equality refers to the equal rights, responsibilities, and opportunities of women and men and girls and boys. It implies that the interests, needs, and priorities of both women and men are taken into consideration.

5.
It is a norm in some cultures to have the aspect of ranking a certain gender in terms of its superiority. This refers to: -
A. Gender bias
B. Gender blind
C. Gender stereotype
D. Gender stratification
Show Answer

Correct Answer: D. Gender stratification

Explanation: Gender stratification refers to the social ranking, where men typically have higher status, power, and access to resources than women. Gender bias is a prejudice, but stratification is the structural ranking system.

A. Mechanisms for upholding cultural norms

Correct Answer: D. Guidelines on how to address emerging gender issues

Explanation: A gender policy provides a framework and guidelines for achieving gender equality and addressing issues like GBV, discrimination, and emerging concerns.

8.
When there is fair and equitable representation of both men and women in all community activities is referred to as-
A. Gender equity
B. Gender equality

11.
A mother comes in labour and on Examination, the nurse was unable to do vaginal examination due to a visible scar. This type of Female genital mutilation is:
A. Clitoridectomy
B. Unclassified
C. Excision
D. Infibulation
Show Answer

Correct Answer: D. Infibulation

Explanation: Infibulation (Type III FGM) involves narrowing of the vaginal orifice with creation of a covering seal. The "visible scar" obstructing the vaginal opening prevents examination, requiring de-infibulation for delivery.

12.
State five (5) rites of passage in African Set up (5 marks)
Show Answer
  1. Birth and Naming: Initiation into the community of the living.
  2. Initiation (Circumcision): Transition from childhood to adulthood.

  4. Eldership: Recognition of wisdom and leadership status.

13.
Outline five (5) complications of female genital mutilation. (5 marks)
Show Answer

Immediate Complications:

  1. Severe pain and shock.
  2. Haemorrhage (excessive bleeding).
  3. Acute urine retention.
  4. Infection (sepsis, tetanus).
  5. Injury to adjacent tissues (urethra, anus).

Long-term Complications:

  • Keloid scar formation.
  • Dyspareunia (painful intercourse).
  • Obstructed labour/Fistula formation.
  • Psychological trauma.
  • Recurrent UTIs.
14.
State five (5) consequences of early marriage (5 marks)
Show Answer
  1. Early Pregnancy/Health Risks: Increased risk of obstructed labour, fistula, and maternal mortality due to physical immaturity.
  2. School Drop-out: Termination of education, limiting future economic opportunities.
  3. Poverty: Lack of skills/education leads to economic dependence and the cycle of poverty.
  4. Gender-Based Violence: Young brides are often more vulnerable to domestic violence and have less bargaining power.
  5. Psychological Trauma: Loss of childhood, depression, and social isolation.
15.
State five (5) services offered in a youth friendly centre. (5 marks)
Show Answer
  1. Counseling Services: Mental health, relationship, and career guidance.
  2. Sexual and Reproductive Health Services: STI screening/treatment, HIV testing (VCT), contraception.
  3. Recreational Activities: Indoor games, sports, and arts to attract youth.
  4. Life Skills Training: Decision making, negotiation skills, leadership training.
  5. Information and Education: Library services, internet access, health talks.

Section III: Long Answer Questions (20 Marks)

16.
Gender Based Violence is at high rise in Kenya today, where Male and female faces killing. With examples, discuss the forms of Gender Based Violence. (20 marks)
Show Answer

Gender-Based Violence (GBV) manifests in various forms. Five key forms include:

1. Physical Violence (4 Marks)

  • Description: The intentional use of physical force with the potential for causing death, injury, or harm.
  • Examples: Slapping, kicking, beating, burning, use of weapons, murder (femicide/homicide).

2. Sexual Violence (4 Marks)

  • Description: Any sexual act, attempt to obtain a sexual act, or unwanted sexual comments or advances directed against a person's sexuality using coercion.
  • Examples: Rape, defilement (sex with a minor), sexual harassment, forced prostitution, marital rape.

3. Emotional/Psychological Violence (4 Marks)

  • Description: Acts or omissions that cause or are likely to cause mental or emotional suffering or injury.
  • Examples: Verbal abuse, humiliation, intimidation, threats of harm, confinement/isolation from family/friends.

4. Socio-Economic Violence (4 Marks)

  • Description: Denial of funds, refusal to contribute financially, denial of food and basic needs, and controlling access to health care or employment.
  • Examples: Taking away a partner's earnings, refusing to pay school fees, prohibiting a partner from working (Economic sabotage).

5. Harmful Traditional Practices (4 Marks)

  • Description: Cultural or traditional practices that are harmful to the health and well-being of individuals, mostly women and children.
  • Examples: Female Genital Mutilation (FGM), Early/Forced Marriage, Wife Inheritance, "Beading" of young girls.

Module II: Family Planning & Reproductive Health

Section I: Multiple Choice Questions (8 Marks)

1.
During a counselling session, a client mentions religious concerns about contraception. What should a counsellor do?
A. Encourage the client to ignore these concerns
B. Explain that religion should not affect contraceptive use
C. Respectfully discuss options that may align with the client’s beliefs
D. Refer the client to a different counsellor
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C. Respectfully discuss options that may align with the client’s beliefs

Explanation: Counseling must be client-centered and respectful of their values. Exploring methods like Natural Family Planning (NFP) or discussing the health benefits of spacing within their religious context is appropriate.

2.
For individuals with a Category three condition, the WHO recommends:
A. The method is generally not recommended unless other methods are unavailable or unacceptable
B. Method can be used without restriction
C. Method should never be used
D. It requires regular follow-ups
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A. The method is generally not recommended unless other methods are unavailable or unacceptable

Explanation: WHO MEC Categories:
1: No restriction.
2: Advantages outweigh risks.
3: Risks usually outweigh advantages (Not recommended unless no alternative).
4: Unacceptable health risk (Do not use).

3.
Evaluation in a family planning program primarily aims to:
A. Measure the program’s outcomes and impact on the target population
B. Ensure every activity is completed on schedule
C. Track daily attendance of program staff
D. Calculate the program’s budget utilization rate
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A. Measure the program’s outcomes and impact on the target population

Explanation: Evaluation focuses on the effectiveness, outcomes (e.g., increased contraceptive prevalence rate), and impact (e.g., reduced maternal mortality), whereas monitoring (B, C, D) tracks daily operations and inputs.

4.
After delivery a mother desires to start family planning before she can be discharged due to some social economic reasons, One of the following methods of family planning can be commenced within the 1st 48 hours after delivery:
A. Contraceptive implants
B. Combined oral contraceptives
C. Injectable contraceptive
D. Intrauterine contraceptive device
Show Answer

Correct Answer: D. Intrauterine contraceptive device

Explanation: Post-partum IUCD (PPIUCD) can be inserted within 48 hours of delivery (post-placental or immediate postpartum). Implants can also be inserted, but IUCD is the classic answer for immediate post-partum methods emphasized in recent guidelines as "PPIUCD". *Note: Implants (A) are also WHO MEC 1 for breastfeeding women <48h, but COCs (B) are contraindicated due to DVT risk. PPIUCD is a specific program intervention.*

Section II: Short Answer Questions (20 Marks)

9.
State five (5) reasons for integrating Family Planning (FP) and other Reproductive Health (RH) services, including STIs, HIV and reproductive cancer services. (5 marks)
Show Answer
  1. Increased Access/Uptake: Clients coming for one service (e.g., HIV care) can access FP without making a separate visit.
  2. Cost-Effectiveness: Sharing resources (staff, infrastructure) reduces overall health system costs.
  3. Client Convenience: Saves clients time and transport costs ("One-stop shop").
  4. Reduced Stigma: Integrated clinics reduce the stigma associated with visiting specific clinics like HIV or STI clinics.
  5. Improved Health Outcomes: Comprehensive care addresses multiple needs (e.g., preventing unintended pregnancy in HIV+ women reduces PMTCT needs).
10.
State five (5) common side effects of Progestin Only Pills (POPs) and their management. (5 marks)
Show Answer
  1. Irregular Bleeding/Spotting: Reassure client it is common and usually resolves; rule out other causes (pregnancy, STI).
  2. Amenorrhea (No menses): Reassure client this is normal and harmful blood is not building up; rule out pregnancy.
  3. Headaches: Mild analgesics (Paracetamol); evaluate if severe.
  4. Breast Tenderness: Reassure; recommend supportive bra; mild analgesics.
  5. Nausea/Dizziness: Take pill with food or at bedtime.
11.
Outline the two (2) different types of IUCDS and their mode of action. (4 marks)
Show Answer
  1. Copper-bearing IUCD (e.g., Cu T 380A):
    • Mode of Action: Copper ions released are toxic to sperm and ova, preventing fertilization (spermicidal). They also cause a sterile inflammatory reaction in the endometrium preventing implantation.
  2. Hormonal IUCD (Levonorgestrel-releasing intra-uterine system / Mirena):
    • Mode of Action: Thickens cervical mucus (blocking sperm entry), thins the endometrium (preventing implantation), and may suppress ovulation in some women.
12.
Explain three (3) methods under fertility awareness. (6 marks)
Show Answer
  1. Basal Body Temperature (BBT) Method: The woman takes her temperature every morning before getting out of bed. A slight rise (0.2-0.5°C) indicates ovulation has occurred. She avoids sex from the end of menses until 3 days after the temperature rise.
  2. Cervical Mucus (Billings) Method: Monitoring changes in cervical mucus. Fertile days are indicated by "wet, slippery, stretchy" (egg-white) mucus. Dry days are considered infertile.
  3. Calendar (Rhythm) Method: Tracking menstrual cycle lengths for 6-12 months. Calculate the fertile window by subtracting 18 from the shortest cycle (first fertile day) and 11 from the longest cycle (last fertile day).

Section III: Long Answer Question (20 Marks)

13.
A client comes to family planning at 6 weeks postnatally and she is not sure of the family planning methods to use. The nurse counsel the client on various family planning methods. Discuss the process she will use during this counselling. (20 marks)
Show Answer

The nurse should use the GATHER approach for comprehensive counseling:

G - Greet the Client (2 Marks)

  • Welcome the client warmly and politely.
  • Offer a seat and ensure privacy.
  • Introduce yourself.
  • Ask the client how you can help her.

A - Ask the Client (4 Marks)

  • Ask about her reproductive goals (spacing or limiting?).
  • Take a history: Medical, Obstetric, and Sexual history.
  • Ask about her breastfeeding status (crucial at 6 weeks postpartum).
  • Ask about her preferences and previous experiences with FP methods.
  • Assess her knowledge and dispel any myths.

T - Tell the Client (4 Marks)

  • Tell her about the available methods suitable for a breastfeeding mother at 6 weeks (e.g., POPs, Implants, IUCD, Injectables, LAM, Condoms).
  • Explain how each method works, its effectiveness, advantages, and disadvantages/side effects.
  • Contraindications: Avoid Combined Oral Contraceptives (COCs) if breastfeeding < 6 months as estrogen reduces milk supply.

H - Help the Client Choose (4 Marks)

  • Help her match the method characteristics with her lifestyle and needs.
  • Support her decision-making without imposing your own views.
  • Ensure the chosen method is medically safe for her (using WHO MEC wheel).

E - Explain How to Use (4 Marks)

  • Once a method is chosen (e.g., POPs), explain exactly how to use it (take daily at same time).
  • Demonstrate use (e.g., show pill pack, condom demonstration).
  • Explain what to do if a dose is missed or problems arise.
  • Discuss warning signs requiring immediate medical attention.

R - Return Visit (2 Marks)

  • Schedule a follow-up visit.
  • Advise her to come back anytime if she experiences side effects or wants to change the method.
  • Give the client the method (supply) and a reminder card.
