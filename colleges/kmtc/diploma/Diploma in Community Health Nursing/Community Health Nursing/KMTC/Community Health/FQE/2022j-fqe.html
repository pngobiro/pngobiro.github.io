1.
According to Abraham Maslow the most elevated type of need is:
A. Social
B. Self esteem
C. Safety
D. Self-actualization
Show Answer

Correct Answer: D. Self-actualization

Explanation: In Maslow's Hierarchy of Needs, Self-actualization (the realization of one's full potential) is at the very top (pinnacle) of the pyramid, above physiological, safety, social, and esteem needs.

22.
The incidence rate of malaria in community X with a population of 50,000 people where new cases were 3,000 and old cases 1,000 is;
A. 8%
B. 6%
C. 33%
D. 2%
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B. 6%

Explanation: $$ \text{Incidence Rate} = \frac{\text{New Cases}}{\text{Total Population}} \times 100 $$ $$ \text{Incidence Rate} = \frac{3,000}{50,000} \times 100 = 6\% $$ (Old cases are included in Prevalence, not Incidence).

24.

A. Salmonella food poisoning
B. Clostridium botulinum food poisoning
C. Staphylococcus aureus food poisoning
D. Bacillus cereus food poisoning
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C. Staphylococcus aureus food poisoning

Explanation: The key clue is the short incubation period (2 hours). Staph aureus produces pre-formed toxins in food, causing symptoms very rapidly (1-6 hours). Salmonella typically takes 12-72 hours. Botulism presents with neurological symptoms.

49.
You are a nurse posted to a level five hospital as the director of nursing services. During orientation, you notice that provision of the nursing services is not going on well and many clients/patients have been reported to have deserted the hospital facility in the recent past. You have decided to make decisions that will target the staff related issues to rectify the situation
(a) List four (4) steps in the decision-making processs (2 marks)
(b) Explain three (3) staff related issues that can lead to desertion of clients/patients from the unit. (6 marks)
(c) Describe how you would apply the decision-making process to improve provision of the nursing care services in the hospital (12 marks)
Show Comprehensive Answer

(a) Steps in Decision-Making Process

  1. Defining the problem.
  2. Analyzing the problem/Gathering data.
  3. Developing alternative solutions.
  4. Selecting the best alternative.
  5. Implementing the decision.
  6. Evaluating the results.

(b) Staff Related Issues Leading to Desertion

  1. Negative Attitude/Rudeness: Nurses who are harsh, abusive, or indifferent to patients create a hostile environment, causing patients to leave.
  2. Absenteeism/Lateness: When staff are not available on duty, patients wait for long hours without care, leading to frustration and desertion.
  3. Lack of Professional Competence: If staff engage in malpractice or show lack of skill (e.g., poor injection technique, wrong medication), patients lose trust in the safety of care.

(c) Application of Decision-Making Process

1. Define the Problem:
I would clearly articulate that "Quality of nursing care has declined due to staff issues, leading to patient desertion." I would acknowledge the severity of the reputation loss.

2. Gather Data/Analyze:
I would conduct an assessment to understand the root causes.

  • Review patient complaint registers and exit interviews.
  • Observe staff conduct during shifts.
  • Check duty rosters against actual attendance.
  • Hold staff meetings to hear their grievances (e.g., burnout, lack of equipment).

3. Develop Alternatives:
Based on findings, I would generate options:

  • A: Punitive measures (warnings/dismissals) for rude staff.
  • B: Training on Customer Care and Ethics.
  • C: Improving staff welfare and motivation (recognition).
  • D: Restructuring the duty roster for better coverage.

4. Select Best Alternative:
I would choose a hybrid approach: Immediate Training on Customer Care (B) combined with Restructuring Rosters (D) and clear communication of standards (A). This addresses both skill/attitude and system issues.

5. Implement Decision:

  • Organize a mandatory workshop on "Patient-Centered Care".
  • Release a new, fair duty roster ensuring senior coverage on all shifts.
  • Establish a "Patient Feedback Desk" to monitor satisfaction.

6. Evaluate:
After 3 months, I would:

  • Check patient attendance statistics (is desertion reducing?).
  • Review feedback forms for reduced complaints.
  • Observe staff-patient interactions to ensure behavior change.
