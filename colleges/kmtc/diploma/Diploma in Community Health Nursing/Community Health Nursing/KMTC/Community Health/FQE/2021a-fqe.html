1.
Contraceptive methods used by breastfeeding mothers include
a) Implant and combined oral contraceptives
b) Condoms and spermicides
c) Oestrogen free pills and copper T IUCD
d) Combined oral contraceptives and hormonal IUD
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Oestrogen free pills and copper T IUCD

Explanation: Breastfeeding mothers should avoid estrogen-containing contraceptives (like Combined Oral Contraceptives) as they can diminish milk supply. Progestin-only pills (POPs/Mini-pills) and non-hormonal methods like the Copper T IUCD are safe and recommended. Option B is also safe but C includes highly effective long-acting methods often prioritized.

2.
The steps followed in community dialogue are
a) Problem identification, problem analysis, identification of best options, prioritization of options
b) Prioritization of options, identification of the best options, problem identification, problem analysis
c) Problem identification, identification of the best options, problem analysis, prioritization of options
d) Problem identification, problem analysis, prioritization of options, identification of best options
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Problem identification, problem analysis, identification of best options, prioritization of options

Explanation: The logical flow of a problem-solving process (dialogue) is to first identify the issue, analyze its root causes, brainstorm possible solutions (best options), and then select/prioritize the most feasible action.

3.
Ground water includes
a) Ponds, rivers, shallow wells
b) Deep wells, springs, rivers
c) Springs, rivers, ponds
d) Shallow wells, springs, deep wells
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Shallow wells, springs, deep wells

Explanation: Ground water is water found underground in the cracks and spaces in soil, sand, and rock. Wells (shallow and deep) and springs tap into this aquifer. Rivers and ponds are classified as surface water.

18.

19.

b) Calcium sulphate and magnesium nitrite
c) Calcium bicarbonate and magnesium bicarbonate
d) Calcium chloride and magnesium chloride
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Calcium bicarbonate and magnesium bicarbonate

Explanation: Temporary hardness is caused by dissolved bicarbonate minerals (calcium and magnesium bicarbonates) which can be removed by boiling. Permanent hardness is caused by sulfates and chlorides (Option B and D).

20.
The type of immunity which occurs after an infection is
a) Natural passive immunity
b) Natural active immunity
c) Artificial induced immunity
d) Natural passive immunity
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Natural active immunity

Explanation: When the body fights off a wild infection, it produces its own antibodies and memory cells. This is "Natural" (happens in nature) and "Active" (immune system works). Passive is receiving antibodies (breastmilk).

34.
In 2011, 199 confirmed cased of meningococcal meningitis occurred in Nyanza, yielding an incidence rate of 22 cases per 100,000 persons. There were 20 deaths and 160 severe non-fatal cases. In this situation, the case fatality rate for meningococcal meningitis was roughly
a) 0.02%
b) 80%
c) 6%
d) 10%
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) 10%

Explanation: Case Fatality Rate (CFR) = (Number of deaths / Number of confirmed cases) × 100. CFR = (20 / 199) × 100 ≈ 10.05%.

49.
In order to get a comprehensive picture of family's health status, as a community health nurse you will need to make a home visit:
a) Explain 5 principles of home visiting (5 marks)
b) Describe the process of home visiting (10 marks)
c) Explain 5 advantages of home visiting (5 marks)
Show Comprehensive Answer

a) Principles of Home Visiting

  1. Planning: Visits should be planned with a clear purpose and objective.
  2. Flexibility: The nurse must be adaptable to the family's situation and schedule.
  3. Respect: Respect the family's culture, privacy, and home environment.
  4. Professionalism: Maintain professional boundaries while building rapport.
  5. Scientific Basis: Care provided should be based on nursing process and evidence.

b) Process of Home Visiting

1. Preparatory Phase (Pre-visit):

  • Select the family to visit based on priority needs.
  • Review family records/data.
  • Contact family to set appointment (if possible).
  • Prepare the home visiting bag with necessary equipment and supplies.

2. Introduction Phase (Entry):

  • Knock and wait for permission to enter.
  • Greet the family culturally appropriately.
  • Introduce self and state the purpose of the visit.
  • Establish rapport.

3. Assessment/Working Phase:

  • Observe the home environment (sanitation, housing).
  • Assess family members' health status.
  • Identify health problems/needs.
  • Plan and implement care (health education, demonstration, treatment) together with the family.

4. Termination Phase:

  • Summarize the visit and points discussed.
  • Set goals for the next visit.
  • Book the next appointment.
  • Thank the family and leave.

5. Post-visit Phase:

  • Clean equipment.
  • Document findings and care given in records.
  • Evaluate the visit.

c) Advantages of Home Visiting

  1. Accurate Assessment: Allows direct observation of the home environment and family dynamics.
  2. Identification of Hidden Problems: Uncovers issues not mentioned in the clinic.
  3. Family-Centered Care: Care is tailored to the specific family context and resources.
  4. Convenience: Beneficial for immobile, elderly, or sick patients who cannot travel.
  5. Health Promotion: Provides opportunity for health education of the entire family unit.
