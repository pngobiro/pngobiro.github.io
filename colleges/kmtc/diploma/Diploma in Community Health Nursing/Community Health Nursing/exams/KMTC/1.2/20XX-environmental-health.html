B. Cholera
C. Dracunculiasis
D. Malaria
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A. Soil transmitted helminths

STH: Transmitted via soil (geo-helminths), not water based.

20.

A. River, ponds, borehole
B. Spring, rivers, wells
C. Wells, Springs and boreholes

Show Answer

25.
What is food safety? (5 Marks)
Show Answer

i. Definition:

Food safety is the protection of consumers from the risk of food poisoning and food-borne diseases. It covers all methods for destroying physical, chemical, and biological hazards in food to prevent illness, especially in vulnerable groups like children and the elderly.

ii. Definitions of terms:

  • Sterilization: Any process that eliminates, removes, kills, or deactivates all forms of life (microorganisms like fungi, bacteria, viruses) and other biological agents present in a specified region/surface/fluid.
  • Contamination: The presence of harmful chemicals and microorganisms in food which can cause consumer illness. It can occur directly or indirectly through cross-contamination.

Section C: Long Answer Questions (40 Marks)

29.
Discuss the various aspects of human wastes that is important in the study of Environmental Health.
Show Answer

Introduction:

Human waste comprises primarily of feces and urine. Its proper management is critical in environmental health to prevent disease transmission and environmental degradation.

1. Health Risks Associated with Human Feces:

  • Pathogen Transmission: Feces contain bacteria (Salmonella, Shigella), viruses (Polio, Hepatitis A), and parasites (Hookworm, Ascaris). Improper disposal leads to fecal-oral diseases.
  • Contamination of Water Sources: Runoff from open defecation sites can pollute rivers and groundwater, causing outbreaks of Cholera and Typhoid.
  • Food Contamination: Use of night soil (untreated feces) as fertilizer transmits helminths and pathogens to crops.

2. Environmental Impact:

  • Aesthetics: Open defecation makes the environment unsightly and produces offensive odours.
  • Vector Breeding: Feces attract flies which mechanically transfer pathogens to food (The 5 Fs: Faeces, Fluids, Fingers, Flies, Food).

3. Control Measures (Barriers):

  • Primary Barrier: Safe disposal of feces (Sanitation). Use of latrines/toilets isolates feces from the environment.
  • Secondary Barriers: Hand washing with soap, clean water supply, and washing fruits/vegetables.

4. Socio-Cultural Aspects:

  • Taboos regarding sharing latrines (e.g., in-laws) can hinder sanitation usage.
  • Privacy and dignity issues, especially for women and girls.
30.
Describe the HACCP principle as used in food hygiene and safety. (20 marks)
Show Answer

Hazard Analysis and Critical Control Point (HACCP) is a systematic, pro-active approach to food safety that focuses on preventing hazards rather than inspecting finished products.

The Seven Principles of HACCP:

  1. Conduct a Hazard Analysis: Identify potential biological, chemical, or physical hazards at every stage of the process (growing to serving). Determine likelihood and severity.
  2. Determine Critical Control Points (CCPs): Identify the points in the process where control can be applied to prevent, eliminate, or reduce the hazard to an acceptable level (e.g., cooking temperature).
  3. Establish Critical Limits: Set maximum or minimum values (limits) for parameters like temperature or time at each CCP to distinguish safe from unsafe conditions (e.g., Cook chicken to 74°C).
  4. Establish Monitoring Procedures: Define how to measure the critical limits (Who, What, When, How). E.g., Using a thermometer to check soup temperature every 2 hours.
  5. Establish Corrective Actions: Decide what must be done if monitoring shows a critical limit has been reached or exceeded (e.g., Discard food, re-cook).
  6. Establish Verification Procedures: Activities (audits, testing) to confirm that the HACCP system is working effectively and is scientifically valid.
  7. Establish Record-Keeping and Documentation: Maintain proof of all procedures, monitoring logs, deviations, and corrective actions taken for traceability and legal defence.
