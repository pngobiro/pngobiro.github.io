Part I: Multiple Choice Questions (20 Marks)

1.
Conditions screened in school health programme include:
a) Visual disturbance, Anaemia
b) Anaemia, Typhoid
c) Typhoid, Malaria
d) Visual disturbance, diabetes mellitus
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Visual disturbance, Anaemia

Explanation: School health screening typically targets common, detectable conditions that affect learning. Visual acuity tests and checking for signs of anaemia (pallor) are standard. Typhoid and Malaria are acute infections usually managed curatively, not via routine mass screening in asymptomatic children. Diabetes is less common in this demographic compared to vision issues and anaemia.

2.
The statement that communicates the reason for existence of an organisation is a:
a) Vision
b) Mission
c) Philosophy
d) Organisational structure
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Mission

Explanation: A Mission Statement defines the organization's purpose and primary objectives ("why we exist"). A Vision describes the future desired state ("where we want to be").

3.
Artificial immunity is achieved through:
a) Infection
b) Immunisation
c) Breastfeeding
d) High immunisation coverage
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Immunisation

Explanation: Artificial immunity is induced by medical intervention. Immunization (vaccination) provides Artificial Active Immunity. Infection provides Natural Active Immunity. Breastfeeding provides Natural Passive Immunity.

4.
The following is a competitive procurement method:
a) Low value procurement
b) Open tender
c) Risk purchase
d) Direct procurement
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Open tender

Explanation: Open tendering is the preferred competitive method where opportunities are advertised publicly, allowing any qualified supplier to bid. Direct procurement is non-competitive.

5.
The side effects of streptomycin include:
a) Hepatitis, deafness, skin rash, tingling in the ear
b) Pellagra, nausea, red colouration of body fluids, ataxia
c) Dizziness, diarrhoea, vomiting, liver damage
d) Hearing strange voices, dizziness, ataxic gait, deafness
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Hearing strange voices, dizziness, ataxic gait, deafness

Explanation: Streptomycin is an aminoglycoside antibiotic known for Ototoxicity (damage to the 8th cranial nerve). Symptoms include tinnitus (ringing in ears/strange voices), dizziness/vertigo (vestibular damage), ataxia (balance issues), and sensorineural deafness.

6.
A contraceptive method recommended for a client with a history of blood clotting disorders is:
a) combined oral pill
b) copper T 380A
c) Bilateral tubal ligation
d) Implants
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) copper T 380A

Explanation: Estrogen-containing contraceptives (Combined Oral Pills) increase the risk of thrombosis and are contraindicated in clotting disorders. Progestin-only methods (Implants) are generally safer but caution is sometimes advised. The Copper T IUD is non-hormonal and has no effect on coagulation, making it the safest reversible option listed.

7.
The ability of the organism to spread rapidly from one host to another is called:
a) Invasiness
b) Virulence
c) Infectivity
d) Opportunistic
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Infectivity

Explanation: Infectivity is the ability of a pathogen to invade and multiply in the host. High infectivity often correlates with rapid spread (communicability). Virulence refers to the severity of disease caused. Invasiveness is the ability to spread within the host's tissues.

8.
Disposal of biomedical wastes segregated into containers/bags with a yellow coding include:
a) Autoclaving, deep burial
b) Chemical treatment, shredding
c) Microwaving, incineration
d) Incineration, deep burial
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Incineration, deep burial

Explanation: Yellow bags typically contain infectious waste (human tissues, soiled dressings, swabs). The standard disposal method for pathological and infectious waste is incineration or deep burial to ensure complete destruction of pathogens.

9.
Manmade disasters include:
a) Terrorists attacks, pollution, structural collapse
b) Fires, civil unrest, communicable disease epidemics
c) Toxic materials, avalanches accidents, conventional warfare
d) Transportation accidents, non-conventional warfare, blizzards
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Terrorists attacks, pollution, structural collapse

Explanation: Manmade disasters are caused by human action, negligence, or error. Option A lists exclusively human-caused events. Other options mix in natural disasters (avalanches, blizzards) or biological events (epidemics) which can be natural.

10.
Learning refers to:
a) Practice of teaching in an institution
b) Process of acquiring knowledge, skills and dispositions
c) Essential tool of communication
d) Making informal decisions
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Process of acquiring knowledge, skills and dispositions

Explanation: Learning is defined as a relatively permanent change in behavior or knowledge potential resulting from experience or practice.

11.
The following is are ways of interrupting the route of transmission:
a) Chemoprophylaxis, personal hygiene
b) Isolation of cases, treatment of carriers
c) Personal hygiene, vector control
d) Environmental hygiene, isolation of cases
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Personal hygiene, vector control

Explanation: The chain of infection includes: Agent -> Reservoir -> Portal of Exit -> Mode of Transmission -> Portal of Entry -> Host.
Vector control (killing mosquitoes) directly blocks the mode of transmission. Personal hygiene (handwashing) blocks contact transmission.
Isolation (b/d) primarily blocks the Portal of Exit from the reservoir. Chemoprophylaxis (a) protects the Susceptible Host.

12.
Occupation health hazard can be prevented by:
a) Separating workers from dangerous machinery or poison
b) Health educating the community on protective devices
c) Special medical checkups in case of over exposure
d) Early retirement of worker
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Separating workers from dangerous machinery or poison

Explanation: Hierarchy of controls: Engineering controls (like isolation/separation of hazards) are more effective than administrative controls or PPE. Keeping the worker away from the hazard is a primary prevention strategy.

13.
Brucellosis is transmitted through:
a) Ingestion of contaminated vegetables and poultry
b) Ingestion of half cooked poultry and meat for dead animals
c) Ingestion of untreated milk and milk products
d) Inhalation of Bacilli from sick and dead animals
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Ingestion of untreated milk and milk products

Explanation: Brucellosis (Malta fever) is a zoonotic disease primarily transmitted by consuming unpasteurized milk or dairy products from infected animals (cows, goats).

14.
While calculating infant mortality rate, the correct formula is;
a) Number of deaths of individuals in a year/number of live births in the same year X 1,000
b) Number of death of infants in a year/number of live births in the previous year X 1,000
c) Number of deaths of infants in a year/number of live births in the same year X 1,000
d) Number of deaths of infants in a month/number of births in the same year X 1,000.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Number of deaths of infants in a year/number of live births in the same year X 1,000

Explanation: Infant Mortality Rate (IMR) is the number of deaths of children under one year of age per 1,000 live births in a given year.

15.
A client with, burning sensation, itchy and foamy greenish yellow discharge in the vagina is diagnosed to have;
a) Gonorrhea
b) Trichomonas
c) Candidiasis
d) Bacterial vaginosis
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Trichomonas

Explanation:
Trichomoniasis: Frothy (foamy), greenish-yellow, foul-smelling discharge with itching/burning and "strawberry cervix".
Candidiasis: Thick, white, curdy (cheese-like) discharge with intense itching.
Bacterial Vaginosis: Thin, grey/white discharge with "fishy" odor.
Gonorrhea: Often purulent or asymptomatic.

16.
The following group of people are classified as socially handicapped;
a) Internally displaced, street children, sexually abused
b) Epileptic, psychiatric cases, widowed
c) Drug addicts, elderly, mongolism
d) Obese, blind, deaf
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Internally displaced, street children, sexually abused

Explanation: Social handicap refers to disadvantages arising from social circumstances rather than physical or mental impairment. IDPs and street children face barriers due to their social situation. Options B, C, and D contain medical/physical conditions (epilepsy, blindness, mongolism/Down syndrome).

17.
The management of poliomyelitis during pre-paralytic stage includes:
a) Administration of mild sedative, avoiding all injections, passive movements of the affected limb
b) Absolute bed rest, administration of mild sedatives, passive movements of the affected limbs
c) Immobilization of the affected limbs, absolute bed rest, avoidance of all then injections
d) Passive movements of the affected limbs, absolute bed rest, isolation of the case
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Immobilization of the affected limbs, absolute bed rest, avoidance of all then injections

Explanation: During the acute/pre-paralytic phase, the goal is to prevent further damage. Absolute bed rest is crucial as exercise increases paralysis risk. Injections (IM) can precipitate paralysis ("provocation polio") and must be avoided. Passive movements are generally avoided in the acute painful stage to prevent spasm and pain; gentle positioning/splinting (immobilization) is preferred to prevent contractures until pain subsides.

18.
State whether the following is True or False

i. For entry to clients' homes during home visiting, the nurse requires to go through the community opinion leader.

Show Answer

Answer: True. Entering a community usually requires permission/blessing from local leaders (gatekeepers) to ensure acceptance and safety.

ii. One of the roles of the County Chief Nurse is to employ and deploy nurses within the county.

Show Answer

Answer: False. Employment is typically a function of the County Public Service Board. The Chief Nurse may advise on deployment but does not inherently have the power to employ.

19.
Priority in management of cholera patients during epidemic is:
a) Administration of tetracycline
b) Referral of patients to hospital
c) Administration of fluids and electrolytes
d) Administration of analgesics
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Administration of fluids and electrolytes

Explanation: Death in cholera is caused by severe dehydration and electrolyte imbalance. Immediate rehydration (Oral Rehydration Therapy or IV fluids) is the lifesaving priority. Antibiotics are secondary.

20.
At the age of 12 months the baby's weight is ;
a) Double birth weight
b) Three times birth weight
c) Four times birth weight
d) Five times birth weight
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Three times birth weight

Explanation: A healthy infant typically doubles their birth weight by 5-6 months and triples it by 1 year (12 months).

Part II: Short Answer Questions (40 Marks)

1.
Draw and label a diagram showing the life cycle of Leishmania. (5 Marks)
Show Answer

Life Cycle Stages:

  1. Sandfly Stage:
    • Sandfly takes a blood meal (ingests macrophages infected with Amastigotes).
    • Amastigotes transform into Promastigotes in the midgut.
    • Promastigotes divide and migrate to the proboscis.
  2. Human Stage:
    • Infected Sandfly bites human, injecting Promastigotes.
    • Promastigotes are phagocytized by macrophages.
    • Inside macrophages, they transform into Amastigotes.
    • Amastigotes multiply in cells of various tissues (spleen, liver, bone marrow for visceral type).

(Diagram should depict the circular transmission between the Phlebotomus sandfly vector and the human host, labeling Promastigotes and Amastigotes).

2.
State four (4) characteristics of a good community based health information system. (4 Marks)
Show Answer
  1. Simplicity: Easy to understand and use by community members/CHVs.
  2. Acceptability: Culturally appropriate and accepted by the community.
  3. Accuracy/Validity: Data collected should reflect the true situation.
  4. Timeliness: Information should be available when needed for decision making.
  5. Relevance: Collects data that is actually needed and used.
3.
Outline four (4) strategic objectives of Community strategy. (4 Marks)
Show Answer
  1. To provide Level 1 services for all cohorts and socio-economic groups.
  2. To build the capacity of the community health extension workers (CHEWs) and community health volunteers (CHVs).
  3. To strengthen health facility-community linkages.
  4. To strengthen the community to progressively realize their rights for accessible and quality care.
4.
Explain two (2) types of empowerment used in Health Promotion. (4 Marks)
Show Answer
  1. Psychological (Self) Empowerment: Enabling individuals to believe in their capabilities (self-efficacy), improving their confidence to make healthy choices and control their own health.
  2. Community Empowerment: The process of enabling communities to increase control over their lives. It involves collective action to improve the quality of life and advocate for policy changes.
5.
State five (5) challenges faced by the factory inspectorate. (5 Marks)
Show Answer
  1. Shortage of Staff: Few inspectors to cover many industries.
  2. Lack of Transport/Resources: Difficulty reaching remote factories.
  3. Corruption: Compromising standards for bribes.
  4. Ignorance of Laws: Workers and sometimes employers are unaware of OHS laws.
  5. Poor Legislation Enforcement: Weak penalties for non-compliance.
  6. Technological Changes: New hazards emerging faster than regulations.
6.
State Four (4) advantages of community participation in Primary Health Care (PHC). (4 Marks)
Show Answer
  1. Sustainability: Projects are more likely to continue if the community owns them.
  2. Resource Mobilization: Communities contribute local resources (labor, materials).
  3. Appropriateness: Services are tailored to actual community needs and culture.
  4. Cost-effectiveness: Using local volunteers reduces costs.
  5. Empowerment: Builds community confidence and self-reliance.
7.
Explain five (5) procurement methods used according to the government procurement and Disposal Act. (5 Marks)
Show Answer
  1. Open Tender: Public advertisement for anyone to bid (most preferred).
  2. Restricted Tender: Invitation limited to a few pre-qualified suppliers (e.g., highly specialized goods).
  3. Direct Procurement: Buying directly from one source without competition (emergencies or monopoly).
  4. Request for Quotations: For low-value standard goods, obtaining quotes from at least 3 suppliers.
  5. Request for Proposals: Used for consultancy services where skill/quality is paramount.
8.
State four (4) limitations of natural family planning methods. (5 Marks)
Show Answer
  1. High failure rate: If not practiced perfectly (typical use failure is high).
  2. Requires high cooperation: Both partners must agree to abstinence during fertile periods.
  3. No STI protection: Does not prevent HIV or other infections.
  4. Difficult with irregular cycles: Hard to predict ovulation in women with irregular menses or during breastfeeding.
  5. Detailed record keeping: Requires daily monitoring (temperature, mucus) which can be tedious.
9.
State four (4) sources of health care financing. (4 Marks)
Show Answer
  1. Government Revenue (Taxation): Budget allocation from the exchequer.
  2. Social Health Insurance: Contributions to NHIF/SHIF.
  3. Out-of-Pocket Expenditure: User fees paid by patients at facilities.
  4. Donor Funding: Grants and loans from international partners (e.g., World Bank, USAID).
  5. Private Health Insurance: Corporate or individual private schemes.

Part III: Long Answer Question (40 Marks)

1.
Among the roles of a Kenya Registered Community Health Nurse (KRCHN) is to carry out management functions.

a) Explain Three (3) functions of a manager. (6 Marks)

Show Answer
  1. Planning: Determining in advance what to do, how to do it, when to do it, and who is to do it. It involves setting goals and objectives.
  2. Organizing: Arranging resources (people, materials, finance) and tasks to achieve objectives. It involves creating structure and assigning roles.
  3. Staffing: Recruiting, selecting, training, and developing personnel to fill the roles in the organization.
  4. Directing/Leading: Guiding, motivating, and supervising staff to work towards organizational goals.
  5. Controlling: Monitoring performance against standards and taking corrective action when necessary.

(Any 3 well-explained points)

b) Describe the scientific approach in management. (10 Marks)

Show Answer

The scientific approach (Frederick Taylor) focuses on efficiency and systematic processes:

  1. Observation and Analysis: Studying tasks to find the "one best way" to perform them (Time and Motion studies).
  2. Selection: Scientifically selecting workers with the right skills for the specific task.
  3. Training: Scientifically training and developing workers to perform the task exactly as defined.
  4. Cooperation: Close cooperation between management and workers to ensure work is done according to scientific principles.
  5. Division of Labor: Clear separation of planning (management) and execution (workers).

c) Outline two (4) Challenges experienced by a manager. (4 Marks)

Show Answer
  1. Resource Constraints: Inadequate budget, staff shortage, or lack of equipment.
  2. Resistance to Change: Staff resisting new policies or technologies.
  3. Conflict Resolution: Managing interpersonal or interdepartmental conflicts.
  4. Time Management: Balancing administrative duties with clinical or supervisory roles.
2.
Master Gay was brought to the health facility with generalized severe itching on the skin folds and a diagnosis of scabies was made:

a) Explain the epidemiology of scabies. (6 Marks)

Show Answer
  • Agent: Sarcoptes scabiei var. hominis (mite).
  • Host: Humans are the only reservoir. Affects all ages but common in children.
  • Transmission:
    • Direct prolonged skin-to-skin contact with an infested person.
    • Indirect contact via infested bedding, clothes, or towels (fomites).
  • Environment: Associated with overcrowding, poor hygiene, poverty, and institutions (schools, prisons).
  • Incubation Period: 2-6 weeks for primary infestation; 1-4 days for re-infestation.

b) State Four (4) clinical picture that he may present with. (4 Marks)

Show Answer
  1. Intense Pruritus (Itching): Typically worse at night.
  2. Rash: Papular, vesicular, or pustular rash found in specific distribution.
  3. Burrows: Thin, greyish/white lines on skin (path of the mite), often on wrists or between fingers.
  4. Distribution: Lesions commonly found in web spaces of fingers, wrists, elbows, axillae, waist, buttocks, and genitalia. (In infants: palms, soles, and face).
  5. Secondary Infection: Signs of bacterial superinfection (impetigo) due to scratching (pustules, crusts).

c) Discuss the treatment, prevention and control of scabies. (10 Marks)

Show Answer

Treatment:

  • Scabicides: Apply Benzyl Benzoate Emulsion (BBE) 25% (diluted for children) or Permethrin 5% cream. Apply to the whole body from neck down (include head in infants). Leave for 8-12 hours (overnight) then wash off. Repeat after 1 week.
  • Treat Contacts: Treat ALL household members and close contacts simultaneously, even if asymptomatic.
  • Symptomatic Relief: Antihistamines (e.g., Chlorpheniramine) for itching; Antibiotics for secondary bacterial infection.

Prevention & Control:

  • Hygiene: Wash all clothes, bed sheets, and towels used in the last 3 days in hot water and dry in the sun or iron them to kill mites.
  • Isolation: Avoid skin-to-skin contact with infected persons until treated.
  • Education: Educate family on personal hygiene and avoiding overcrowding.
  • Environment: Keep items that cannot be washed in a sealed plastic bag for 3 days (mites die without a host).
