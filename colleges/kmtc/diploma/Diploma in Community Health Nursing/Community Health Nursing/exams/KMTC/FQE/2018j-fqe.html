Section 1: Multiple Choice Questions

20 Marks
1.
One of the following is a characteristic of a good team:
a) Every member has all the required skills, knowledge, or role that contributes to the work of the team
b) The members regards themselves as a group of competing team
c) The team member cannot cope with the various personalities and relationships in the team
d) The members regard themselves as one and not as a group of individuals.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) The members regard themselves as one and not as a group of individuals.

Explanation: Cohesion and a shared identity ("we" instead of "I") are hallmarks of a good team. They work towards a common goal rather than competing (Option B).

2.
Controlling is a managerial function which involves:
a) Integration and coordination of activities
b) Reducing cost and directing and directing efforts towards desired results
c) Measuring and correcting the performance of employee
d) Contributing to purposeful organization of work
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Measuring and correcting the performance of employee

Explanation: Controlling involves monitoring performance against standards, measuring deviations, and taking corrective action to ensure organizational goals are met.

3.
The following vaccines are stored at -15 degrees and -25 degrees at the National level:
a) Yellow fever, Tetanus toxoid, Pentavalent
b) BCG, Pentavalent, Tetanus toxoid
c) Measles, Polio, Pneumoccocal
d) Polio, Yellow fever, Measles
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Polio, Yellow fever, Measles

Explanation: These are the heat-sensitive vaccines that are kept frozen (-15°C to -25°C) at the national and regional stores to maintain potency. Tetanus and Pentavalent (Option A, B) are freeze-sensitive and must never be frozen.

4.
One of the "Reaching every district" (RED) is:
a) Community participation
b) Community involvement
c) Opening up new service delivery points
d) Re-orientation of health staff
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Opening up new service delivery points

Explanation: The 5 components of the RED strategy are: 1. Planning and management of resources, 2. Reaching the target population (which involves re-establishing outreach and opening new static sites), 3. Community links, 4. Supportive supervision, 5. Monitoring for action. Option C describes the operational action of reaching underserved areas.

5.
BCG vaccine requirement for a health centre inclusive of wastage whose target population is 1500 children per year is:
a) 750 doses
b) 1500 doses
c) 2000 doses
d) 2250 doses
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) 2250 doses

Explanation: This calculation uses a wastage factor of 1.5 (implying 33% wastage rate).
Calculation: Target Population (1500) × Wastage Factor (1.5) = 2250 doses.
Note: While BCG often has a higher wastage factor (2.0 or 50% wastage) leading to 3000 doses, 2250 is the only calculated option higher than the target among the choices provided.

8.
The number of new cases in a specific population within a defined time interval is:
a) Prevalence
b) Ratio
c) Incidence
d) Rate
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Incidence

Explanation: Incidence specifically refers to new cases arising over a period. Prevalence refers to all (new + old) existing cases at a point in time.

Incubation period of shigellosis is usually:
a) 1-4 day
b) 5-7 days

12.
The spread of hepatitis A can be reduced by:
a) Avoiding over-crowding in houses
b) Proper disposal of sewage waste within communities
c) Draining all stagnant water in the fields
d) Vaccination of all stray and domestic animals
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Proper disposal of sewage waste within communities

Explanation: Hepatitis A is transmitted via the fecal-oral route. Proper sanitation (sewage disposal) breaks the chain of transmission. Overcrowding (A) relates to airborne diseases. Stagnant water (C) relates to vectors (malaria).

13.
Random sampling methods used in community diagnosis include:
a) Stratified sampling, quota sampling, snowball sampling
b) Simple random sampling, cluster sampling, purposive
c) Cluster sampling, systemic sampling, stratified sampling
d) Snowball sampling, simple random sampling, stratified sampling
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Cluster sampling, systemic sampling, stratified sampling

Explanation: "Random" implies probability sampling where every unit has a chance of selection. Cluster, Systematic (Systemic), and Stratified are probability methods. Quota, Snowball, and Purposive are non-probability methods.

17.
Indicate whether the following statements are TRUE or FALSE:
a) Human immunodeficiency virus can be transmitted during the window stage (TRUE)
b) Acquired immunodeficiency syndrome can be prevented through intensive health education (TRUE)
Show Explanation

a) TRUE: The viral load is often very high during the acute infection (window period), making the person highly infectious even though antibodies are not yet detectable.

b) TRUE: Health education promotes behavior change (e.g., condom use, abstinence) which is the primary method of preventing HIV transmission.

18.
Menopause is a stage in life:
a) When menstration does not occur any more
b) When ovulation and reproductive capacity ceases
c) Where production of oestrogen ceases
d) When grey hair appears and hair line recedes
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) When ovulation and reproductive capacity ceases

Explanation: Menopause is biologically defined by the cessation of ovarian function (ovulation) and the end of reproductive ability, marked clinically by the cessation of menstruation (amenorrhea) for 12 months.

19.
The mechanism of action of implants is :
a) Thickening of cervical mucus, suppressing ovulation, thinning of the endometrium
b) Thinning of cervical mucus, suppressing ovulation, thinning of endometrium
c) Thickening of cervical mucus, suppressing ovulation, thickening of endometrium
d) Suppressing the endometrium, encouraging ovulation, thickening cervical mucus
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Thickening of cervical mucus, suppressing ovulation, thinning of the endometrium

Explanation: Progestin implants work by 1. Thickening cervical mucus (barrier to sperm), 2. Suppressing ovulation (in most cycles), and 3. Thinning the endometrial lining (preventing implantation).

20.
Which of the following is true about natural family planning:
a) Women with irregular menstrual cycle can use
b) Highly effective
c) All clients of reproductive age can use
d) Women with regular /established menstrual cycles can use
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Women with regular /established menstrual cycles can use

Explanation: Most natural methods (like Calendar or Standard Days Method) rely on predictable fertile windows based on regular cycles (usually 26-32 days). Irregular cycles make these methods unreliable.

Section 3: Long Answer Questions

40 Marks
1.
You are the nurse incharge of Que health facility, cholera outbreak has been reported in the neighboring facility:
a) Define cholera (1 mark)
b) Explain the three (3) stages of cholera (6 marks)
c) Describe the control and preventive measures of cholera (11 marks)
d) List four (4) complications of cholera (2 marks)
Show Comprehensive Answer

a) Definition

Cholera is an acute diarrheal infection caused by ingestion of food or water contaminated with the bacterium Vibrio cholerae, characterized by profuse watery diarrhea.

b) Stages of Cholera

  1. Stage of Evacuation: Sudden onset of profuse, painless watery diarrhea ("rice-water stools") and vomiting.
  2. Stage of Collapse (Algid Stage): Signs of severe dehydration appear - sunken eyes, washerwoman's hands, loss of skin elasticity, hypotension, cold clammy skin, and potential shock.
  3. Stage of Recovery (Reaction): If treated, patient improves, vital signs stabilize. If untreated, may progress to renal failure or death.

c) Control and Prevention Measures

1. Case Management:

  • Rehydration: Prompt administration of ORS for moderate dehydration and IV fluids (Ringer's Lactate) for severe dehydration to restore fluid balance.
  • Antibiotics: Administration of Doxycycline or Ciprofloxacin to shorten the duration of illness and reduce shedding.
  • Isolation: Treating patients in a designated Cholera Treatment Centre (CTC) to prevent spread.

2. Water, Sanitation and Hygiene (WASH):

  • Safe Water: Chlorinating water sources, boiling drinking water, and distributing water treatment products (WaterGuard).
  • Sanitation: Proper disposal of human waste (latrines), disinfection of patient's vomit/stool with chlorine.
  • Hygiene Promotion: Handwashing with soap and water, food hygiene (cook it, peel it, or leave it).

3. Surveillance and Education:

  • Notification: Reporting cases immediately to public health authorities.
  • Contact Tracing: Following up contacts and providing chemoprophylaxis if indicated.
  • Burial Practices: Supervising burials to ensure they are safe and dignified.

d) Complications

  1. Hypovolemic Shock
  2. Acute Renal Failure
  3. Severe Electrolyte Imbalance (Hypokalemia)
  4. Hypoglycemia (especially in children)
2.
A community health nurse utilizes the function of delegation to achieve organizational goals:
a) Outline five(5) factors you would consider when choosing the officer to delegate to (5 marks)
b) Describe your responsibility during the implementation of the task you have delegated (5 marks)
c) Explain two(2) reasons why delegation is beneficial to the organization (8 marks)
d) State three (3) reasons why managers fail to delegate (3 marks)
Show Comprehensive Answer

a) Factors for Choosing Delegatee

  1. Competence/Skills: Does the person have the necessary skills and knowledge to perform the task?
  2. Workload: Does the person have the time to take on extra work, or are they already overwhelmed?
  3. Attitude/Willingness: Is the person motivated and willing to learn/take responsibility?
  4. Experience: Have they done similar tasks before?
  5. Job Description: Is the task within their scope of practice or legal limits?

b) Manager's Responsibility During Implementation

  1. Monitoring/Supervision: Checking progress periodically to ensure the task is on track without micromanaging.
  2. Support/Resourcing: Providing necessary resources (supplies, information) needed to do the job.
  3. Consultation: Being available to answer questions or give guidance if problems arise.
  4. Feedback: Providing constructive feedback on performance.
  5. Retaining Accountability: Acknowledging that the ultimate responsibility for the outcome remains with the manager.

c) Benefits of Delegation (4 marks each)

1. Efficiency and Continuity: Delegation allows routine tasks to be handled by subordinates, freeing up the manager to focus on strategic, high-level issues. This ensures work continues smoothly even in the manager's absence, preventing bottlenecks.

2. Personnel Development and Motivation: It acts as a training ground for subordinates, developing their skills and confidence. Giving staff responsibility increases their job satisfaction, sense of worth, and motivation, leading to better organizational performance.

d) Why Managers Fail to Delegate

  1. Fear of Loss of Control: Fear that the job won't be done right ("I can do it better myself").
  2. Insecurity: Fear that the subordinate might outshine the manager.
  3. Lack of Trust: Lack of confidence in the subordinates' abilities.
