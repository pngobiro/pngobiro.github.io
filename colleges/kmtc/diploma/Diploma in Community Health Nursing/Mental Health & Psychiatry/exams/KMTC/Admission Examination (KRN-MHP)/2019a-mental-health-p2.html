Part I: Multiple Choice Questions (20 Marks)

1.
Increased intra-ocular pressure in the eye will result in a condition known as:
a) Leucoma
b) Glaucoma
c) Trachoma
d) Cataract
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Glaucoma

Explanation: Glaucoma is a group of eye conditions that damage the optic nerve, often caused by abnormally high pressure in the eye (intraocular pressure).

2.
The following are vascular structures of the eye:
a) Cornea, lens, sclera
b) Iris, sclera, lens
c) Sclera, choroid, cornea
d) Lens, iris, cornea
Show Answer

Correct Answer: None of the options are strictly correct.

Explanation: The vascular coat of the eye is the Uvea, which consists of the Iris, Ciliary Body, and Choroid.
- The Cornea and Lens are avascular (no blood vessels).
- The Sclera is largely avascular but has a vascular episcleral layer.
In the context of the options provided, the presence of avascular structures (Cornea, Lens) in all choices makes them technically incorrect. However, the Choroid (in option C) and Iris (in option B/D) are vascular.

3.
Pulse oximetry is used to measure:
a) Haemoglobin level
b) Glucose level
c) Heart conductivity
d) Oxygen saturation
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Oxygen saturation

Explanation: Pulse oximetry is a non-invasive method for monitoring a person's oxygen saturation (SpO2).

4.
In triage steps, if a child has no emergency signs:
a) Check for priority signs
b) Check for non-urgent signs
c) Give pre-referral treatment
d) Discharge and advice when to return
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Check for priority signs

Explanation: According to ETAT (Emergency Triage Assessment and Treatment) guidelines, after checking for Emergency signs (ABCD), the next step is to check for Priority signs (3TPMOB).

8.
The most reliable sites to check for cyanosis in an infant are:
a) Nail beds, lips
b) Conjunctiva, big toe
c) Under the tongue, gums
d) Sclera, palms
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Nail beds, lips

Explanation: Central cyanosis is best observed in the tongue and lips. Peripheral cyanosis is seen in nail beds. In general assessment, lips and nail beds are standard check points.

9.
If a mother with HIV has a positive antibody test, then the baby is classified as:
a) HIV exposed
b) HIV infected
c) HIV infection likely
d) Symptomatic HIV infection
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) HIV exposed

Explanation: An infant born to an HIV-positive mother carries maternal antibodies and tests positive on antibody tests, but this does not confirm infection. The infant is "HIV Exposed" until confirmed infected by PCR (DNA/RNA) test.

10.
Fast breathing in a child aged 24 months is:
a) 30 breaths per minute or more
b) 40 breaths per minute or more
c) 50 breaths per minute or more
d) 60 breaths per minute or more
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) 40 breaths per minute or more

Explanation: WHO IMCI Guidelines:
- 2 months to 12 months: Fast breathing is 50 breaths/min or more.
- 12 months to 5 years: Fast breathing is 40 breaths/min or more.

15.
In the management of impetigo, antibiotics are used to:
a) Treat deep seated infection
b) Prevent re-infection
c) Reduce contagious spread
d) Reduce sensitivity
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Treat deep seated infection

Explanation: Antibiotics (topical or systemic) are used to treat the bacterial infection (Staph or Strep). Systemic antibiotics are specifically used for widespread or deep infection.

16.
Modifiable risk factors for ischemic stroke include:
a) Obesity, old age
b) Diabetes, male gender
c) Smoking, high cholesterol levels
D) Female gender, stress
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Smoking, high cholesterol levels

Explanation: Modifiable risk factors are those that can be changed or treated. Smoking and Hyperlipidemia (cholesterol) are modifiable. Age and Gender are non-modifiable.

17.
The location of the sub mucous uterine fibroids is:
a) Outside of the uterus
b) In the peri-metrium
c) In the wall of the uterus
d) Underneath the uterine lining
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Underneath the uterine lining

Explanation:
- Submucous: Just under the endometrium (uterine lining).
- Intramural: Within the uterine wall (myometrium).
- Subserous: Just under the outer serosa (perimetrium).

18.
The following are Braxton Hicks contractions, EXCEPT:
a) Lightening
b) Effacement of cervix, dilatation of cervix
c) Lightening, Braxton Hicks, contractions
d) Irregular, painless contractions
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Effacement of cervix, dilatation of cervix

Explanation: Braxton Hicks contractions are irregular, usually painless "practice" contractions that do not cause cervical dilation or effacement. True labor contractions cause cervical changes.

19.
The grimace response in a newborn is termed as:
a) Stimulative response
b) Facial expressiveness
c) Vocalization
d) Reflex irritability
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Reflex irritability

Explanation: In the APGAR score, "Grimace" refers to Reflex Irritability - the response to stimulation (e.g., suctioning or drying).

20.
Signs of intracranial injury in the newborn include:
a) High pitched cry, bulging anterior fontanelle, convulsions
b) High pitched cry, bulging anterior fontanelle, jaundice
c) Muscular twitching, convulsions, sunken fontanelle
d) Convulsions, sunken fontanelle, high pitched cry
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) High pitched cry, bulging anterior fontanelle, convulsions

Explanation: Intracranial injury leads to increased intracranial pressure (ICP), manifested by a bulging fontanelle, high-pitched cry, and seizures. Sunken fontanelle indicates dehydration.

Part II: Short Answer Questions (40 Marks)

1.
List four (4) functions of fat in the body. (2 marks)
Show Answer
  1. Provide a concentrated source of energy.
  2. Insulate the body against heat loss.
  3. Protect vital organs (cushioning).
  4. Facilitate absorption of fat-soluble vitamins (A, D, E, K).
  5. Component of cell membranes.
2.
Outline five (5) types of abortions. (5 marks)
Show Answer
  1. Threatened Abortion.
  2. Inevitable Abortion.
  3. Incomplete Abortion.
  4. Complete Abortion.
  5. Missed Abortion.
  6. Septic Abortion.
3.
State five (5) indications of haemodialysis. (5 marks)
Show Answer
  1. Severe Hyperkalemia (refractory to medical management).
  2. Fluid Overload (Pulmonary Edema) unresponsive to diuretics.
  3. Severe Metabolic Acidosis.
  4. Uremic symptoms (Encephalopathy, Pericarditis).
  5. Drug Overdose/Toxicity (dialyzable toxins).
4.
Outline five (5) medico-legal aspects in theatre nursing. (5 marks)
Show Answer
  1. Informed Consent: Ensuring valid consent is obtained before surgery.
  2. Surgical Count: Accountability for swabs, needles, and instruments to prevent retained foreign bodies.
  3. Patient Identification: Correct patient, correct site, correct procedure (Time Out).
  4. Confidentiality: Protecting patient privacy.
  5. Negligence/Malpractice: Ensuring standard of care to prevent injury (e.g., burns from diathermy, falls).
5.
Outline five components of a partograph. (5 marks)
Show Answer
  1. Patient Identification: Name, Gravida, Parity, Date/Time.
  2. Fetal Condition: Fetal Heart Rate, Liquor, Moulding.
  3. Progress of Labour: Cervical Dilation, Descent of Head, Contractions.
  4. Maternal Condition: BP, Pulse, Temperature, Urine (protein/ketones/volume).
  5. Drugs and Fluids: Oxytocin, IV fluids, medications given.
6.
State six (6) entry signs of sick young infant with very severe disease. (6 marks)
Show Answer
  1. Convulsions.
  2. Fast breathing (60 breaths/min or more).
  3. Severe chest indrawing.
  4. Nasal flaring.
  5. Grunting.
  6. Fever (>37.5°C) or Low body temperature (<35.5°C).
  7. Movement only when stimulated or no movement at all.
  8. Not feeding well.
7.
Outline three (3) adverse effects of malnutrition in children. (3 marks)
Show Answer
  1. Stunted Growth: Physical and developmental delay.
  2. Impaired Immunity: Increased susceptibility to infections.
  3. Cognitive Impairment: Learning difficulties and poor school performance.
  4. Increased Mortality: Higher risk of death.
8.
State four (4) characteristics of a preterm baby. (4 marks)
Show Answer
  1. Small Size: Low birth weight.
  2. Skin: Thin, translucent, red skin with visible veins; abundant lanugo.
  3. Posture: Extended/frog-like posture (hypotonia).
  4. Genitalia: Undescended testes (males) or prominent clitoris/labia minora (females).
  5. Sole Creases: Few or absent creases on feet.
  6. Ears: Soft cartilage, slow recoil.
9.
Using rule of nines, describe how burns are estimated. (5 marks)
Show Answer

The Rule of Nines estimates Total Body Surface Area (TBSA) burned:

  • Head and Neck: 9%
  • Anterior Trunk: 18%
  • Posterior Trunk: 18%
  • Each Arm: 9% (Total 18%)
  • Each Leg: 18% (Total 36%)
  • Perineum: 1%

Part III: Essay/Long Answer Questions (40 Marks)

1.
Master X age three and a half (3 1/2) yrs is admitted in a pediatric ward with a diagnosis of acute otitis media (AOM).

a) Draw and label a diagram of the ear. (5 marks)

Show Answer

(Diagram should include):
- Outer Ear: Pinna, External Auditory Canal.
- Middle Ear: Tympanic Membrane, Ossicles (Malleus, Incus, Stapes), Eustachian Tube.
- Inner Ear: Cochlea, Semicircular Canals, Vestibulocochlear Nerve.

b) Describe four (4) cardinal clinical features of AOM. (4 marks)

Show Answer
  1. Otalgia (Ear Pain): Child may pull or rub the ear, cry excessively.
  2. Fever: Often high grade.
  3. Otorrhea: Discharge from the ear (if tympanic membrane ruptures).
  4. Hearing Loss: Conductive hearing loss due to fluid.
  5. Irritability/Restlessness: Due to pain.

c) List four (4) complications of otitis media. (2 marks)

Show Answer
  1. Mastoiditis.
  2. Meningitis.
  3. Chronic Suppurative Otitis Media (CSOM).
  4. Permanent Hearing Loss.
  5. Brain Abscess.
  6. Facial Nerve Paralysis.

d) Describe the specific management of Master X until discharge. (9 marks)

Show Answer
  • Antibiotic Therapy: Administer prescribed antibiotics (e.g., Amoxicillin) to treat bacterial infection. Ensure full course.
  • Pain Management: Administer analgesics (Paracetamol/Ibuprofen) to relieve pain and fever. Apply warm compress to the ear.
  • Ear Care: Keep the ear dry. If discharging, gently wick out discharge with dry cotton wool (do not insert deep).
  • Antipyretics: Manage fever.
  • Hydration/Nutrition: Encourage fluids and soft diet.
  • Monitoring: Monitor temperature and response to treatment.
  • Health Education: Teach mother to complete medication, keep ear dry, and follow up.
2.
Mr. Y is admitted into a surgical ward with severe head injury following a road traffic accident.

a) State three (3) functions of cerebrum. (3 marks)

Show Answer
  1. Sensory Perception: Interpreting sensory impulses (touch, vision, hearing).
  2. Motor Control: Initiating voluntary muscle movements.
  3. Higher Mental Functions: Memory, reasoning, intelligence, personality, and emotions.

b) Outline five (5) nursing diagnosis for head injury. (5 marks)

Show Answer
  1. Ineffective Tissue Perfusion (Cerebral) related to increased ICP.
  2. Ineffective Airway Clearance related to unconsciousness.
  3. Risk for Injury related to seizures or confusion.
  4. Acute Pain related to trauma.
  5. Impaired Physical Mobility.

c) Describe the specific management of Mr. Y for the first 72 hours. (12 marks)

Show Answer
  • Airway & Breathing: Maintain patent airway (suctioning), oxygenation to prevent hypoxia (which increases ICP). Keep head elevated 30 degrees to promote venous drainage.
  • Neurological Monitoring: Frequent GCS assessment (q15-30 mins), pupil reaction, and limb movement checks.
  • ICP Management: Administer Mannitol or hypertonic saline as ordered to reduce cerebral edema. Avoid activities that increase ICP (straining, coughing).
  • Fluid Balance: Strict input/output monitoring. Avoid fluid overload.
  • Seizure Prophylaxis: Administer anticonvulsants (e.g., Phenytoin) as prescribed.
  • Temperature Control: Prevent hyperthermia (increases metabolic demand) with antipyretics/cooling.
  • Sedation/Analgesia: Manage pain and agitation to lower metabolic demand.
  • Vitals: Monitor for Cushing's Triad (High BP, Low Pulse, Irregular Resp).
