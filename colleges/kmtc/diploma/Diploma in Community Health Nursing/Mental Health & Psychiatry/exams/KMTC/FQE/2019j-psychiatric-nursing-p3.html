Section I: Multiple Choice Questions (40 Marks)

1.
The benefits of psychotherapy include:
A. Socialization, re-education, improved adjustment
B. Socialization, enhanced physical and psychosocial well-being, improved adjustment
C. Improved adjustment, re-education, increased awareness of body and ego strength
D. Improved adjustment, socialization, increased awareness of body and ego strength
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A. Socialization, re-education, improved adjustment

Explanation: Psychotherapy focuses on psychological treatment. Key benefits include helping the patient adjust to life (improved adjustment), learning new coping skills (re-education), and improving interpersonal skills (socialization). Options including "physical well-being" or "body awareness" are often associated with other therapies (like somatic or occupational therapy).

2.
Any person believed to be dangerous to himself or others or likely to act in a manner offensive to public decency may be admitted as:
A. Voluntary admission
B. Emergency admission
C. Involuntary admission
D. Criminal admission
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C. Involuntary admission

Explanation: Involuntary admission is legally indicated when a person poses a danger to themselves or others and refuses voluntary treatment.

3.
An individual who wants to block unwanted painful experiences would be using the defence mechanism:
A. Projection
B. Dissociation
C. Repression
D. Sublimation
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C. Repression

Explanation: Repression is the unconscious blocking of unpleasant emotions, impulses, memories, and thoughts from the conscious mind.

4.
The term premorbid describes:
A. Past psychiatric history
B. Person's functioning prior to the onset of any disturbance
C. Any acute mental disturbance
D. Any emotional disturbance
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B. Person's functioning prior to the onset of any disturbance

Explanation: "Pre-morbid" literally means "before the disease". It refers to the personality and functional state of an individual before the onset of illness.

5.
Which of the following is a key component of the therapeutic nurse-patient relationship?
A. Sympathy
B. Social friendship
C. Empathy
D. Controlling behavior
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C. Empathy

Explanation: Empathy—the ability to understand and share the feelings of another—is the cornerstone of a therapeutic relationship, distinguishing it from a social friendship.

6.
In psychiatric nursing, primary prevention focuses on:
A. Treating existing mental illness
B. Reducing the incidence of mental disorders
C. Reducing the residual defects of mental illness
D. Rehabilitation
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B. Reducing the incidence of mental disorders

Explanation: Primary prevention aims to prevent disease from occurring (reducing incidence) through education, stress management, and reducing risk factors.

7.
The commonly used antipsychotic drugs include:

B. Chlorpromazine, Librium
C. Haloperidol, amitriptyline
D. Chlorpromazine, amitriptyline

Correct Answer: A. Haloperidol, chlorpromazine

The person who recommends admission of a member of the armed forces is:
A. Court martial of armed forces

D. Commander in the armed forces

Correct Answer: C. Medical officer of the armed forces

9.
Main advantage of the newer antipsychotic medications is:
A. Increased photosensitivity and sedation
B. Lower incidence of extrapyramidal side effects
C. No known extrapyramidal side effects
D. Abolishes hallucinations within 24 hours
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B. Lower incidence of extrapyramidal side effects

Explanation: Second-generation (atypical) antipsychotics are preferred largely because they carry a significantly lower risk of EPS (like tardive dyskinesia) compared to first-generation drugs, although they carry metabolic risks.

10.
People who suffer from cyclothymia experience:
A. Recurrent acute episodes of mania
B. Recurrent acute episodes of depression
C. Chronic unrelenting hypomania
D. Chronic variation in mood ranging from dysphoria to hypomania
Show Answer

Correct Answer: D. Chronic variation in mood ranging from dysphoria to hypomania

Explanation: Cyclothymia is a chronic mood disorder (lasting at least 2 years) characterized by numerous periods of hypomanic symptoms and depressive symptoms that do not meet the full criteria for a Major Depressive Episode.

11.
Autistic disorder is characterized by:
A. Marked chromosomal effects
B. Qualitative impairment in communication
C. Marked impairment in physical growth
D. Social-cultural mental retardation
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B. Qualitative impairment in communication

Explanation: Autism Spectrum Disorder is characterized by persistent deficits in social communication and social interaction, along with restricted, repetitive patterns of behavior.

12.
Antidepressants act by:
A. Enhancing activity of GABA
B. Blocking dopamine receptors in the brain
C. Increasing concentration of noradrenaline and serotonin in the brain
D. Depressing the central nervous system
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C. Increasing concentration of noradrenaline and serotonin in the brain

Explanation: Most antidepressants (SSRIs, SNRIs, TCAs) work by inhibiting the reuptake of neurotransmitters like serotonin and norepinephrine, thereby increasing their availability in the synapse.

13.
The role of a nurse in primary prevention of mental illness include:
A. Early identification of mental health problems and initiating prompt effective treatment
B. Referring the mentally sick for rehabilitation
C. Administering the atypical antipsychotics to the mentally sick
D. Ensuring many psychiatric hospitals are opened
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A. Early identification of mental health problems and initiating prompt effective treatment

Explanation: Primary prevention focuses on preventing onset, but in the context of options where others are clearly Tertiary (Rehab) or Curative (Administering drugs/Hospitals), Early ID/Prompt treatment is technically Secondary prevention but often the "best" answer for prevention roles in these exam contexts compared to pure treatment. Note: Strictly, Primary Prevention is health education/stress reduction. Secondary is Early Diagnosis. However, given the options, A is the closest to a preventative/early intervention role compared to pure chronic care.

14.
A child presenting with hostility defiant behaviour and violation of rights of others is described as:
A. Autistic
B. Deceitful child
C. Attention deficient hyperactive disorder
D. Conduct disorder
Show Answer

Correct Answer: D. Conduct disorder

Explanation: Conduct Disorder is characterized by a repetitive and persistent pattern of behavior in which the basic rights of others or major age-appropriate societal norms or rules are violated (aggression, destruction of property, deceitfulness, violation of rules).

15.
The following are positive symptoms of schizophrenia:
A. Hallucination, delusions, disorganized thinking
B. Waxy flexibility, alogic, apathy
C. Flat affect, avolition, anhedonia
D. Somatic delusions, echolalia, flat affect
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A. Hallucination, delusions, disorganized thinking

Explanation: Positive symptoms are "additions" to normal behavior (e.g., hallucinations, delusions). Negative symptoms are "deficits" (e.g., flat affect, avolition, apathy).

16.
The following are cognitive factors that would determine someone’s behaviour:
A. Knowledge, beliefs, memories
B. Knowledge, ethnicity, socio-economic status
C. Environment, ethnicity, politics
D. Religion, socio-economic status, beliefs
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A. Knowledge, beliefs, memories

Explanation: Cognitive factors relate to mental processes like thinking, knowing, remembering, and believing. Ethnicity, politics, and socio-economic status are environmental or social factors.

19.
The symptom of catatonic schizophrenia include:
A. Flat affect, auditory hallucinations, suspicion
B. Incongruous giggling, facial grimaces, delusions of persecution
C. Mutism, negativism, waxy flexibility
D. Delusions of grandeur, argumentative, suspicious

20.
The autonomic nervous system side effects of tricyclic antidepressants are:
A. Muscle twitching, fine tremors, peripheral neuropathy
B. Agranulocytosis, jaundice, epileptic seizures
C. Constipation, increased sweating, difficulty micturition
D. Tachycardia, epileptic fits, muscle twitching

Correct Answer: C. Constipation, increased sweating, difficulty micturition

Explanation: TCAs have strong Anticholinergic effects (blocking the parasympathetic nervous system), leading to dry mouth, blurred vision, constipation, and urinary retention.

Section II: Short Answer Questions (40 Marks)

1.
State the five (5) classification of schizophrenia. (5 Marks)
Show Answer
  1. Paranoid Schizophrenia: Preoccupation with delusions (persecutory/grandiose) and hallucinations.
  2. Disorganized (Hebephrenic) Schizophrenia: Disorganized speech, behavior, and flat/inappropriate affect.
  3. Catatonic Schizophrenia: Psychomotor disturbance (immobility, waxy flexibility, or excessive motor activity).
  4. Undifferentiated Schizophrenia: Symptoms present but do not fit clearly into the above categories.
  5. Residual Schizophrenia: History of at least one episode, but current absence of prominent positive symptoms (negative symptoms persist).
2.
Outline five (5) clinical features of brief psychotic disorder. (5 Marks)
Show Answer
  1. Sudden Onset: Psychotic symptoms appear rapidly (within 2 weeks).
  2. Duration: Symptoms last at least 1 day but less than 1 month.
  3. Positive Symptoms: Presence of delusions, hallucinations, or disorganized speech.
  4. Emotional Turmoil: Rapid shifts in affect, confusion, or severe distress.
  5. Full Recovery: Complete return to premorbid level of functioning after the episode.
3.
State five (5) classification of antidepressant agents. (5 Marks)
Show Answer
  1. Tricyclic Antidepressants (TCAs): e.g., Amitriptyline, Imipramine.
  2. Selective Serotonin Reuptake Inhibitors (SSRIs): e.g., Fluoxetine, Sertraline.
  3. Monoamine Oxidase Inhibitors (MAOIs): e.g., Phenelzine.
  4. Serotonin-Norepinephrine Reuptake Inhibitors (SNRIs): e.g., Venlafaxine.
  5. Atypical Antidepressants: e.g., Bupropion, Mirtazapine.
4.
State five (5) extrapyramidal side effects of antipsychotics. (5 Marks)
Show Answer
  1. Acute Dystonia: Muscle spasms of tongue, face, neck (e.g., oculogyric crisis).
  2. Parkinsonism (Pseudoparkinsonism): Tremor, rigidity, bradykinesia, shuffling gait.
  3. Akathisia: Motor restlessness, inability to sit still.
  4. Tardive Dyskinesia: Involuntary movements of mouth, tongue, trunk (long-term effect).
  5. Neuroleptic Malignant Syndrome (NMS): (Often grouped here though distinct) Severe rigidity, fever, instability.
5.
State five (5) components of the communication process. (5 Marks)
Show Answer
  1. Sender (Source): The person initiating the message.
  2. Message: The content/information being conveyed.
  3. Channel: The medium used to transmit the message (speech, visual, tactile).
  4. Receiver: The person who receives and interprets the message.
  5. Feedback: The response from the receiver ensuring the message was understood.
6.
Outline four (4) types of social action as distinguished by particular motivations or goals of actors according to Max Weber’s theory of social action. (4 Marks)
Show Answer
  1. Instrumentally Rational (Zweckrational): Action determined by expectations of behavior of objects/people to attain rational goals (calculating means to ends).
  2. Value Rational (Wertrational): Action determined by conscious belief in the intrinsic value (ethical/religious) of behavior, regardless of outcome.
  3. Affective (Emotional): Action determined by the actor's specific affects and feeling states (emotion-driven).
  4. Traditional: Action determined by ingrained habituation and custom.
7.
Explain the process of emergency admission as per the Mental Health Act Section 1b. (6 Marks)
Show Answer

Emergency admission is used when a person is dangerous to self or others and requires urgent care:

  1. Application: Often made by a police officer or relative who brings the person to the facility.
  2. Assessment: An authorized mental health professional/medical officer assesses the patient immediately upon arrival.
  3. Duration: The patient can be detained for a limited period (typically 72 hours) for observation and safety.
  4. Conversion: Within this period, the status must be converted to Voluntary (if patient agrees) or Involuntary (if danger persists and patient refuses), or the patient must be discharged.
8.
Outline five (5) characteristics of self-actualized persons according to Abraham Maslow’s humanistic theory. (5 Marks)
Show Answer
  1. Acceptance: They accept themselves, others, and nature as they are.
  2. Realism: Efficient perception of reality; comfortable with the unknown.
  3. Autonomy: Independent, self-reliant, and resistant to social pressure.
  4. Peak Experiences: Frequent experiences of profound happiness, wonder, and connection.
  5. Creativity: Highly creative and inventive in their approach to life.
  6. Problem-centering: Focused on solving problems outside themselves (mission-oriented).

Part III: Long Answer Question (20 Marks)

1.
Mr X with a diagnosis of major depressive disorder is scheduled for electro-convulsive therapy.

a) State four (4) other indications for electro-convulsive therapy (4 Marks)

Show Answer
  1. Severe Mania: Not responsive to medication or when rapid control is needed.
  2. Catatonia: Especially life-threatening catatonia.
  3. Schizophrenia: Especially if catatonic or with prominent affective symptoms.
  4. Suicidality: Severe, acute suicidal ideation where rapid response is critical.
  5. Treatment-Resistant Depression: When medication trials have failed.

b) State three (3) contra-indications for electro-convulsive therapy (3 Marks)

Show Answer
  1. Increased Intracranial Pressure (ICP): Absolute contraindication (e.g., Brain tumor).
  2. Recent Myocardial Infarction: Within the last 3 months (Relative contraindication).
  3. Severe Hypertension: Uncontrolled high blood pressure.
  4. Retinal Detachment: Risk of further damage.

c) Outline three (3) side effects of electro-convulsive therapy (3 Marks)

Show Answer
  1. Memory Loss: Temporary retrograde or anterograde amnesia.
  2. Confusion/Disorientation: Immediately post-procedure.
  3. Headache/Muscle Aches: Common physical complaints post-seizure.
  4. Nausea: Post-anesthesia effect.

d) Describe the procedure for electro-convulsive therapy (10 Marks)

Show Answer

1. Pre-Procedure Preparation:

  • Consent: Ensure informed consent is signed.
  • Assessment: Check physical status (cardiac/resp clearance), baseline vitals, and ensure NPO status (6-8 hours).
  • Preparation: Remove dentures, jewelry, glasses. Empty bladder. Administer pre-medication (e.g., Atropine) if ordered to reduce secretions/bradycardia.

2. Intra-Procedure:

  • Positioning: Place patient supine. Establish IV access.
  • Monitoring: Attach ECG, pulse oximetry, and BP cuff.
  • Anesthesia: Administer short-acting anesthetic (e.g., Propofol) and muscle relaxant (e.g., Succinylcholine) to prevent fractures.
  • Protection: Insert bite block to protect tongue/teeth. Ventilate with 100% Oxygen.
  • Stimulation: Apply electrodes (unilateral or bilateral). Deliver electrical stimulus to induce a controlled seizure (lasting >25 seconds). Monitor seizure activity (EEG/motor).

3. Post-Procedure Care:

  • Recovery: Position on side to prevent aspiration. Suction if needed. Monitor vitals and oxygen saturation until stable/awake.
  • Reorientation: Reorient patient to time/place as confusion clears.
  • Safety: Assist with ambulation initially (fall risk). Offer analgesia for headache.
