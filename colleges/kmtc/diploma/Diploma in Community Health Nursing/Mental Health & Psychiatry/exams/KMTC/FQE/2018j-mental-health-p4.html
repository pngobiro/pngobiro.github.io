Part 1: Multiple Choice Questions (20 Marks)

1.
Learning refers to:
a) Practice of teaching in an institution.
b) Process of acquiring knowledge, skills and dispositions.
c) Essential tool of communication.
d) Making informal decisions.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Process of acquiring knowledge, skills and dispositions.

Explanation: Learning is the transformative process of taking in information that – when internalized and mixed with what we have experienced – changes what we know and builds on what we do.

2.
The function of a manager that ensures effective and efficient use of resources is:
a) Organizing
b) Planning
c) Controlling
d) Making informal decisions
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Controlling

Explanation: Controlling is the management function that involves monitoring performance, comparing it with goals, and taking corrective action to ensure resources are being used effectively and efficiently to achieve objectives. (Organizing arranges resources, but Controlling ensures their *effective use* against standards).

3.
The Non-contraceptive benefits of Depo-Provera include:
a) Prevention against endometrial cancer, reduction of menstrual flow
b) Protection against sexually transmitted infections, prevention of endometrial cancer
c) Protection against sexually transmitted infections, prevention of endometriosis
D) Increased menstrual flow, protection against anemia
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Prevention against endometrial cancer, reduction of menstrual flow

Explanation: Depo-Provera (DMPA) thins the endometrium, reducing menstrual flow (often leading to amenorrhea) which treats anemia and reduces the risk of endometrial cancer and endometriosis. It does NOT protect against STIs.

4.
Complications of kwashiorkor include:
a) Infections, anemia, Overweight
b) Hypothermia, alopecia, muscle wasting
c) Hypoglycemia, diarrhea, muscle wasting
d) Anemia, hypothermia, infections
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Anemia, hypothermia, infections

Explanation: Children with Kwashiorkor (severe protein deficiency) have weakened immune systems (Infections), poor thermoregulation (Hypothermia), and lack of globin synthesis (Anemia). Muscle wasting is more characteristic of Marasmus (though present but masked by edema in Kwashiorkor). Hypoglycemia is also a major complication.

5.
Small group teaching skills include:
a) Active participation, reinforcement, questioning
b) Reinforcement, structure, set induction
c) Set induction, stimulus variation, use of example
d) Use of teaching aid, stimulus demonstration
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Active participation, reinforcement, questioning

Explanation: Small groups rely on high engagement. Skills like encouraging participation, using reinforcement, and questioning are vital to facilitate interaction.

6.
Characteristics of nursing process include:
a) Increased client participation
b) Permits creativity and rational decision making
c) Systematic, dynamic, interpersonal
d) Use of teaching aid, stimulus demonstration
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Systematic, dynamic, interpersonal

Explanation: The nursing process is defined as systematic (ordered steps), dynamic (changing with patient needs), interpersonal (patient-centered), and goal-directed.

7.
Ethical aspects considered during research include:
a) Informed and voluntary consent on part of the researcher
b) Confidentiality of research report
c) Confidentiality of data collected
D) Protection of the individual from understanding
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Confidentiality of data collected

Explanation: Key ethical principles include informed consent (from the *participant*, not researcher), confidentiality/anonymity of data, and protection from harm (beneficence).

8.
The two stages of evaluation in a learning program are:
a) Clinical and Summative evaluation
b) Written and Clinical evaluation
c) Formative and Written evaluation
d) Formative and Summative evaluation
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Formative and Summative evaluation

Explanation: Evaluation is broadly classified into Formative (ongoing, during the course) and Summative (final, at the end of the course).

9.
The purpose of communication is to motivate people to:
a) Acquire new information
b) Change behavior
c) React and adopt desired results
d) Pass health messages to others
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Change behavior

Explanation: In health contexts, the ultimate goal of communication (like IEC/BCC) is to influence and motivate a positive change in behavior.

10.
The environmental host factors for Tetanus are:
a) Use of unsterile instruments, high altitude
b) Traditional unhygienic habits, stimulation of sympathetic nervous system
c) High altitude stimulation of sympathetic nervous system
d) Traditional unhygienic habits, Use of unsterile instruments
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Traditional unhygienic habits, Use of unsterile instruments

Explanation: Tetanus risk is increased by practices that introduce spores into wounds, such as applying cow dung/mud to umbilical cords (traditional habits) or using unsterile blades for cutting cords/circumcision.

11.
In research, data editing is done:
a) Before analyzing the data
b) After analyzing the data
c) During research
d) Immediately the data is received
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Before analyzing the data

Explanation: Editing involves checking raw data for errors, omissions, and consistency to ensure quality *before* it is coded and analyzed.

12.
Priority in management of cholera patients during epidemic is:
a) Administration of tetracycline
b) Referral of patients to hospital
c) Administration of fluids and electrolytes
d) Administration of analgesics
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Administration of fluids and electrolytes

Explanation: The immediate life-threatening issue in cholera is severe dehydration and electrolyte imbalance. Rehydration (Oral or IV) is the priority intervention.

13.
Life skills development in the context of home-based care means:
a) Provision of material assistance
b) Harnessing positive living skills of care givers and patient for self-care
c) Advocating for living skills care and support
d) Facilitating outreach program to create community awareness
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Harnessing positive living skills of care givers and patient for self-care

Explanation: Life skills in HBC focus on empowering patients and families with the psychological and practical skills to cope with illness and manage daily life.

14.
Victor Vroom emphasizes on motivation variables as:
a) valence, success, expectancy
b) Expectancy, valence, instrumentality
c) Resource, valence, expectancy
d) Attitude, success, instrumentality
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Expectancy, valence, instrumentality

Explanation: Vroom's Expectancy Theory of Motivation posits that Motivation = Expectancy × Instrumentality × Valence.

15.
Genital herpes can be treated through topical application or ingestion of:
a) Acyclovir
b) Podophylin
c) Ceftriaxone
d) Tetracycline
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Acyclovir

Explanation: Acyclovir is the standard antiviral medication for Herpes Simplex Virus. Podophyllin is for warts; Ceftriaxone/Tetracycline are for bacterial STIs.

16.
Shaping learners attitudes involves:
a) Exposure to new information.
b) Encouraging discussion amongst students.
c) Giving a lecture followed by a handout.
d) Giving assignments.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Encouraging discussion amongst students.

Explanation: Affective learning (attitudes) is best achieved through interactive methods like discussion, role-play, and simulation where learners can explore values and feelings.

17.
Which of the following is a type of probability sampling:
a) Purpose
b) Convenience
c) Cluster
d) Quota
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Cluster

Explanation: Cluster sampling is a probability method. Purposive, Convenience, and Quota sampling are non-probability methods.

18.
Planning in management is a process of:
a) Determining in advance what is to be accomplished.
b) Determining alternative modes of action.
c) Selecting the rules, regulation, and procedures to be followed.
d) Making decisions on the tasks which are going to be carried out.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Determining in advance what is to be accomplished.

Explanation: Planning is the primary management function that involves defining goals for future organizational performance and deciding on the tasks and use of resources needed to attain them.

19.
Koplik's spots are seen in:
a) Rubella
b) Chicken pox
c) Measles
d) Typhoid
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Measles

Explanation: Koplik's spots (small white spots on the buccal mucosa) are a pathognomonic sign of Measles (Rubeola).

20.
Most of HIV virus transmission to the infant occurs during:
a) Early pregnancy
b) Labor and delivery
c) Breastfeeding
d) Late pregnancy
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Labor and delivery

Explanation: Without intervention, the highest risk of MTCT (about 50-65% of all transmissions) occurs during labor and delivery due to exposure to maternal blood and fluids. Intrauterine transmission is less common, and breastfeeding poses a cumulative postnatal risk.

Part II: Short Answer Questions (40 Marks)

1.
State five (5) roles of behavior change communication. (5 marks)
Show Answer
  1. Increase Knowledge: Providing information to raise awareness about health issues.
  2. Stimulate Community Dialogue: Encouraging discussions about social norms and practices.
  3. Promote Attitude Change: Reducing stigma and misconceptions.
  4. Improve Skills: Teaching people how to perform healthy behaviors (e.g., condom use).
  5. Create Demand for Services: Encouraging people to seek health services.
2.
State five (5) purposes of literature review. (5 marks)
Show Answer
  1. To identify gaps in current knowledge.
  2. To avoid duplication of previous studies.
  3. To identify appropriate research methodologies and tools used by others.
  4. To provide a theoretical framework/context for the study.
  5. To justify the need for the current study.
3.
Explain three (3) teaching traits in an effective teacher. (6 marks)
Show Answer
  1. Subject Mastery: Deep knowledge of the subject matter to explain concepts clearly and answer questions.
  2. Communication Skills: Ability to convey information effectively, listen actively, and use non-verbal cues.
  3. Empathy and Patience: Understanding learners' needs and difficulties, and creating a supportive learning environment.
4.
Explain three (3) uses of demographic data in health care system. (3 marks)
Show Answer
  1. Resource Planning: Allocating health facilities and staff based on population density and age structure.
  2. Epidemiology: Understanding disease patterns (incidence/prevalence) in specific demographic groups.
  3. Policy Formulation: Developing health policies tailored to the needs of the population (e.g., MCH services for a young population).
5.
State three (3) measures applicable in preventing constipation. (3 marks)
Show Answer
  1. High Fiber Diet: Increasing intake of fruits, vegetables, and whole grains.
  2. Adequate Hydration: Drinking plenty of water (6-8 glasses/day).
  3. Regular Exercise: Physical activity stimulates bowel motility.
6.
State four (4) ways of orientating new staff in an organization. (4 marks)
Show Answer
  1. Tour of the Facility: Familiarizing them with the physical layout and key departments.
  2. Introduction to Policies: Providing manuals and explaining organizational rules, vision, and mission.
  3. Job Description Review: Clarifying roles, responsibilities, and reporting lines.
  4. Mentorship/Preceptorship: Pairing with an experienced staff member for guidance.
7.
State four (4) phases of disaster management cycle. (4 marks)
Show Answer
  1. Mitigation/Prevention: Measures to reduce the severity or likelihood of a disaster.
  2. Preparedness: Planning and training before a disaster occurs.
  3. Response: Immediate actions taken during or immediately after the disaster.
  4. Recovery: Restoring the community to normal (Rehabilitation/Reconstruction).
8.
Explain three (3) clinical features of secondary stage of syphilis. (6 marks)
Show Answer
  1. Skin Rash: Non-itchy, reddish-brown rash, typically on the palms of hands and soles of feet.
  2. Condylomata Lata: Moist, warty lesions in genital or anal areas.
  3. Systemic Symptoms: Fever, swollen lymph nodes (lymphadenopathy), sore throat, and muscle aches.
9.
State five (5) advantages of problem based learning. (5 marks)
Show Answer
  1. Promotes critical thinking and problem-solving skills.
  2. Encourages self-directed learning.
  3. Increases retention of knowledge through active engagement.
  4. Enhances teamwork and communication skills.
  5. Connects learning to real-life situations (relevance).

Part III: Essay/Long Answer Questions (40 Marks)

1.
Research process starts with formulating a research problem that can be investigated.

a) Define the term research (2 marks)

Show Answer

Research is a systematic, scientific process of inquiry or investigation to discover new knowledge, validate existing knowledge, or solve a specific problem.

b) State four (4) sources of identifying a research problem (4 marks)

Show Answer
  1. Clinical Practice: Observations or issues encountered in daily work.
  2. Literature Review: Gaps or recommendations for further study in existing journals.
  3. Social Issues: Current events or community health problems.
  4. Theory: Testing propositions of nursing or health theories.

c) Outline the components of a research report (14 marks)

Show Answer
  1. Title Page: Topic, author, date.
  2. Abstract: Summary of the entire study.
  3. Introduction: Background, problem statement, objectives, justification.
  4. Literature Review: Summary of existing knowledge.
  5. Methodology: Design, population, sampling, data collection tools, ethical considerations.
  6. Results/Findings: Presentation of data (tables, graphs).
  7. Discussion: Interpretation of results.
  8. Conclusion and Recommendations: Summary and suggestions.
  9. References: List of sources cited.
  10. Appendices: Tools, permission letters.
2.
YOU are posted to UTESI dispensary in a village where floods occur every rainy season and assigned to lead others on disaster preparedness campaign.

a) Identify four (4) man-made disasters that are likely to occur (2 marks)

Show Answer
  1. Fires (structural).
  2. Road Traffic Accidents.
  3. Chemical Spills/Pollution.
  4. Civil Unrest/Conflict.
  5. (Floods are natural, but collapsed dams/structural failure is man-made).

b) State two (2) effects of disaster on each of the following (4 marks)

Show Answer

i. Individual:

  • Physical injury or death.
  • Psychological trauma (PTSD, anxiety).
  • Loss of property/livelihood.

ii. Community:

  • Destruction of infrastructure (roads, hospitals).
  • Disruption of essential services (water, power).
  • Disease outbreaks (epidemics).

c) State four (4) phases of disaster management (4 marks)

Show Answer
  1. Mitigation.
  2. Preparedness.
  3. Response.
  4. Recovery.

d) State six (6) roles of a nurse in disaster management (12 marks)

Show Answer
  1. Triage: Sorting victims based on severity of injury for treatment priority.
  2. Direct Care: Providing first aid and emergency medical care to victims.
  3. Coordination: Organizing resources, volunteers, and logistics.
  4. Education: Training community members on preparedness and first aid.
  5. Psychological Support: Providing counseling and support to victims and families.
  6. Disease Surveillance: Monitoring for potential outbreaks following the disaster.
  7. Advocacy: Identifying vulnerabilities and advocating for safety measures.
