Part I: Multiple Choice Questions (20 Marks)

1.
Major risk factors for stroke (CVA) include:
a) Arterial hypertension, trauma, cigarette smoking.
b) Arterial hypertension, head injury, severe anaemia.
c) Arterial hypertension, head injury, thrombo-embolism.
d) Trauma, cigarette smoking severe anaemia.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Arterial hypertension, trauma, cigarette smoking.

Explanation: Hypertension is the single most significant risk factor for both ischemic and hemorrhagic stroke. Smoking damages blood vessels. Trauma can lead to hemorrhagic stroke. (Note: Thromboembolism is a cause or mechanism, but Hypertension and Smoking are primary risk factors).

2.
The most common symptom of spinal cord tumour is:
a) Paralysis below the level of involvement
b) Impaired sensation of all the extremities
c) Back pain that worsens with activity
d) Urinary incontinence.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Back pain that worsens with activity

Explanation: Pain is the earliest and most common symptom of spinal cord tumors, often aggravated by movement or coughing and worse at night.

3.
Weber test assesses:
a) Both air and bone conduction of sound
b) Bone conduction of sound
c) Air conduction of sound
d) Sensori neural hearing loss.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Bone conduction of sound

Explanation: The Weber test involves placing a vibrating tuning fork on the midline of the head to test for lateralization of sound via bone conduction.

4.
Advantages of ultrasound include:
a) Studies air filled structure, inexpensive, painless
b) Painless, non-invasive, studies air filled structures
c) In expensive, non-invasive, little preparation of patient.
d) Studies air filled structures, little preparation of patient, inexpensive.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) In expensive, non-invasive, little preparation of patient.

Explanation: Ultrasound is cost-effective, safe (non-invasive/no radiation), and requires little preparation compared to other modalities. It is poor at studying air-filled structures (like lungs or bowel gas).

5.
After lumber puncture the patient is asked to lie in:
a) Left lateral position for 30 minutes.
b) Right lateral position for 1 hour
c) Supine position during the period of bed rest.
d) Prone position during the period of bed rest.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Supine position during the period of bed rest.

Explanation: Lying flat (supine) for several hours after a lumbar puncture helps prevent post-dural puncture headache (PDPH) by minimizing CSF leakage.

6.
The mechanism of normal labor in normal sequence include:
a) Descent, flexion of the head, internal rotation of the head, crowning, extension of the head, restitution.
b) Descent, internal rotation of the head, flexion of the head crowning, extension of the head.
c) Internal rotation of the head, flexion of the head crowning, external rotation of the head, descent extension of the head.
d) Flexion of the head, internal rotation of the head, descent extension of the head, restitution, crowning, descent.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Descent, flexion of the head, internal rotation of the head, crowning, extension of the head, restitution.

Explanation: The cardinal movements are: Engagement, Descent, Flexion, Internal Rotation, Extension, Restitution (External Rotation), and Expulsion.

7.
During the 4th antenatal visit at 36 weeks the nurse:
a) Administers tetanus toxoid, assess for pelvic adequacy, give iron and folate.
b) Assess for anaemia, assess for pelvic adequacy, give iron and folate.
c) Give tetanus toxoid, assess for anaemia give antimalarials
d) Check for fetal presentation, assess for anaemia, give antimalarials.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Check for fetal presentation, assess for anaemia, give antimalarials.

Explanation: At 36 weeks, confirming fetal presentation (vertex vs breech) is critical for birth planning. Iron/folate and malaria prophylaxis (if indicated) continue.

8.
Risk of developing significant neonatal jaundice is increased in:
a) Low birth weight, visible jaundice in the first 24 hours
b) Over weight babies, neonates of mothers with diabetes mellitus
c) Visible jaundice in the first 72 hrs, prematurity
d) Small for dates neonate, visible jaundice in the first 72 hrs.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Visible jaundice in the first 72 hrs, prematurity

Explanation: Prematurity is a major risk factor due to immature liver function. Jaundice appearing in the first 24 hours is pathological; appearing within 72 hours usually peaks around then and can be significant if associated with other factors like prematurity.

9.
The cardinal rule in management of Kerosene poisoning is:
a) Give plenty of oral fluids and induce vomiting.
b) Do gastric or stomach lavage immediately
c) Give alkali or bicarbonate solution
d) Do not induce vomiting.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Do not induce vomiting.

Explanation: Inducing vomiting with hydrocarbons (like kerosene) increases the risk of aspiration into the lungs, leading to chemical pneumonitis, which is often more fatal than the toxicity of absorption.

10.
In all victims who are unconscious:
a) Stay near them and by their side
b) Never give anything orally
c) Administer humidified oxygen.
d) Cover them with light clothing
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Stay near them and by their side

Explanation: Ensuring safety and monitoring the airway (staying by their side) is the first priority. Option B is also a strict rule, but A encompasses the duty of care.

11.
The causes of hydrocephalus can be prevented by early treatment of;
a) Congenital malformation of the brain
b) Excessive production of cerebral spinal fluid
c) Infections like meningitis
d) Adhesion in the cerebral space
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Infections like meningitis

Explanation: Acquired hydrocephalus is often caused by adhesions/obstruction following infections like meningitis. Treating the infection early reduces this risk.

12.
In the pathophysiology of tetanus tetarolysin toxins actions in the body is to:
a) Damage leukocytes
b) Enhance infection of tetanus
c) Cause local tissue necrosis
d) Cause the infection in the nervous system
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Damage leukocytes

Explanation: Clostridium tetani produces two toxins: Tetanospasmin (causes spasms/neuro symptoms) and Tetanolysin (damages tissues and blood cells, including leukocytes, at the site of infection).

13.
80% of urinary tract infection is caused by:
a) Escherichia coli
b) Proteus
c) Pseudomonas
d) Klebsiella
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Escherichia coli

Explanation: E. coli is responsible for the vast majority (approx 80%) of uncomplicated UTIs.

An investigation done for assessment of gall bladder and the biliary tract is called:

15.

The theoretical effectiveness of billing ovulation method is:
a) 98%
b) 94%

18.
Communication as a tool in communication and counseling addresses the following aspects.
a) Establishes commonness, inform and persuade
b) Encourage, persuade and guide
c) Guide, advice and inform
d) Advise, persuade and establish commonness
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Establishes commonness, inform and persuade

Explanation: Effective communication establishes common understanding (rapport), informs (educates), and persuades (motivates behavior change).

19.
In theatre design, the restricted zone includes:
a) Recovery room, changing room for staff, transfer room
b) Scrub-up room, recovery room, transfer room
c) Operating room, disposal room, anesthetic room.
d) Anesthetic room, operating room, scrub-up room.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Operating room, disposal room, anesthetic room.

Explanation: The restricted zone includes areas where surgical procedures are performed and sterile items are exposed. Proper OR attire including masks is required.

20.
The role of the scrub nurse during operation of a patient includes:
a) Administration of oxygen through nasal prongs
b) Assisting the anesthetist during the insertion of endotracheal tube
c) Ensuring that no swabs or instruments are left in the patient body
d) Writing down the number of swabs to be used on a visible board/chart
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Ensuring that no swabs or instruments are left in the patient body

Explanation: The scrub nurse (working within the sterile field) is directly responsible, along with the circulating nurse, for counting swabs, sharps, and instruments to prevent retained foreign objects.

Part II: Short Answer Questions (40 Marks)

1.
State five (5) clinical features of increased intracranial pressure. (5 Marks)
Show Answer
  1. Severe Headache (worse in the morning).
  2. Vomiting (often projectile, without nausea).
  3. Papilledema (swelling of the optic disc).
  4. Altered Level of Consciousness (drowsiness to coma).
  5. Cushing's Triad (Increased BP, Bradycardia, Irregular respirations).
2.
Outline six (6) causes of seizures in children. (6 Marks)
Show Answer
  1. Febrile Seizures: High fever associated with infections.
  2. Infections: Meningitis, Encephalitis, Malaria.
  3. Head Trauma: Accidental injury or birth trauma.
  4. Metabolic Disturbances: Hypoglycemia, Hypocalcemia.
  5. Epilepsy: Idiopathic or genetic conditions.
  6. Toxins/Poisoning: Ingestion of lead, drugs, or pesticides.
3.
State the four (4) rules of home treatment according to integrated management of childhood illness (IMCI). (4 Marks)
Show Answer
  1. Give extra fluid.
  2. Give Zinc supplements (specifically for diarrhea).
  3. Continue feeding.
  4. Advise mother when to return (Danger signs).
4.
State four (4) responsibilities of a first aid giver. (4 Marks)
Show Answer
  1. Assess the situation for safety (Danger).
  2. Protect the casualty from further harm.
  3. Provide life-saving treatment (ABC).
  4. Arrange for transport to medical care.
5.
Explain three (3) conditions where bronchoscopy is used as a diagnostic tool. (6 Marks)
Show Answer
  1. Suspected Lung Cancer: To visualize tumors and take biopsies.
  2. Hemoptysis: To identify the source of bleeding.
  3. Chronic Cough/Infection: To collect secretions for culture (e.g., in TB or pneumonia) or investigate unexplained cough.
6.
State three (3) characteristics of normal labour. (3 Marks)
Show Answer
  1. Spontaneous onset at term (37-42 weeks).
  2. Vertex presentation.
  3. Progression without complications, resulting in vaginal delivery of a live baby within 18-24 hours.
7.
Outline four (4) natural methods used in labour induction. (4 Marks)
Show Answer
  1. Nipple Stimulation: Releases endogenous oxytocin.
  2. Sexual Intercourse: Semen contains prostaglandins; orgasm causes uterine contractions.
  3. Membrane Sweeping: Releases local prostaglandins.
  4. Physical Activity/Walking: Gravity and pressure on the cervix.
8.
State any four (4) complications of gestational diabetes on the neonate. (4 Marks)
Show Answer
  1. Macrosomia: Large for gestational age due to high maternal glucose.
  2. Hypoglycemia: After birth due to hyperinsulinemia.
  3. Respiratory Distress Syndrome: Delayed lung maturity.
  4. Jaundice (Hyperbilirubinemia).
9.
Outline the psychosocial stages of development theory by Erick Erickson. (5 Marks)
Show Answer
  1. Trust vs. Mistrust (Infancy).
  2. Autonomy vs. Shame and Doubt (Early Childhood).
  3. Initiative vs. Guilt (Preschool).
  4. Industry vs. Inferiority (School Age).
  5. Identity vs. Role Confusion (Adolescence).
  6. Intimacy vs. Isolation (Young Adulthood).
  7. Generativity vs. Stagnation (Middle Adulthood).
  8. Integrity vs. Despair (Maturity).

Part III: Long Answer Questions (40 Marks)

1.
Mr. John is admitted in the intensive care unit after a fatal road traffic accident, which left him with acute spinal injury and head injury.

a) Briefly describe how to assess neurologic status using the Glasgow coma scale. (10 Marks)

Show Answer

The GCS assesses consciousness based on three responses (Total Score 3-15):

  • Eye Opening (E) - Max 4: 4=Spontaneous, 3=To Speech, 2=To Pain, 1=None.
  • Verbal Response (V) - Max 5: 5=Oriented, 4=Confused, 3=Inappropriate Words, 2=Incomprehensible Sounds, 1=None.
  • Motor Response (M) - Max 6: 6=Obeys Commands, 5=Localizes Pain, 4=Withdraws from Pain, 3=Abnormal Flexion (Decorticate), 2=Extension (Decerebrate), 1=None.

b) Briefly describe the general management of a patient with spinal cord injury. (10 Marks)

Show Answer
  • Immobilization: Use cervical collar and spine board to prevent further injury. Log roll for all movements.
  • Airway & Breathing: Maintain patent airway (jaw thrust). Monitor for respiratory distress (C-spine injuries affect diaphragm).
  • Circulation: Maintain BP (spinal shock causes hypotension). IV fluids/Vasopressors.
  • Bladder/Bowel Care: Catheterize to prevent retention. Bowel regimen.
  • Skin Care: Prevent pressure ulcers by regular turning (log roll).
  • Steroids: Administer Methylprednisolone (if within 8 hours) to reduce inflammation.
  • VTE Prophylaxis: SCDs or anticoagulants to prevent DVT.
2.
Master X age 4 Â½ years is admitted in the pediatric ward with a diagnosis of rheumatic fever.

a) State (4) investigation that can be used to diagnose rheumatic fever. (4 Marks)

Show Answer
  1. Throat Culture: For Group A Beta-hemolytic Streptococcus.
  2. ASO Titer: Elevated Antistreptolysin O antibodies.
  3. ESR/CRP: Elevated inflammatory markers.
  4. Echocardiogram/ECG: To detect carditis or prolonged PR interval.

b) Outline four (4) clinical features that Master X will present with. (4 Marks)

Show Answer
  1. Polyarthritis: Migratory pain and swelling in large joints.
  2. Carditis: Murmurs, tachycardia, chest pain.
  3. Chorea: Involuntary jerky movements (St. Vitus Dance).
  4. Erythema Marginatum: Ring-like rash on trunk/limbs.
  5. Subcutaneous Nodules: Painless lumps over bony prominences.

c) Describe the specific management for Master X form admission till discharge. (10 Marks)

Show Answer
  • Antibiotics: Administer Penicillin (Benzathine Penicillin G) to eradicate Streptococcal infection.
  • Anti-inflammatory: High dose Aspirin for arthritis/fever. Corticosteroids (Prednisone) if severe carditis is present.
  • Bed Rest: Strict bed rest during the acute phase to reduce cardiac workload (especially if carditis exists).
  • Symptomatic Care: Manage fever, pain, and heart failure symptoms (Digoxin/Diuretics if needed).
  • Chorea Management: Safety precautions (padded rails), sedatives (Haloperidol/Valproate) if severe.
  • Nutrition: Nutritious diet.
  • Secondary Prophylaxis: Plan for long-term Penicillin injections to prevent recurrence.

d) List four (4) complication of rheumatic fever. (2 Marks)

Show Answer
  1. Rheumatic Heart Disease: Permanent valve damage (Mitral Stenosis/Regurgitation).
  2. Heart Failure.
  3. Infective Endocarditis: Risk due to damaged valves.
  4. Atrial Fibrillation.
