Part I: Multiple Choice Questions (20 Marks)

1.
Objective questions:
a) Measure student's proficiency in performing procedures
b) Activates several senses in a learner
c) Improves on student's reflective thinking and skills on patient care
d) Measures student's achievement in relation to knowledge, skills and attitudes
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Measures student's achievement in relation to knowledge, skills and attitudes

Explanation: Objective questions (like MCQs) are designed to measure learning outcomes (knowledge, comprehension) across cognitive domains efficiently. While "proficiency in performing procedures" is usually measured by checklists/OSCEs, objective tests measure the underlying knowledge.

2.
The problem statement in a research proposal should include:
a) The background of the study
b) An outline of the study objectives
c) The magnitude of the issue under study
d) The benefits of the study shall contribute against challenges
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) The magnitude of the issue under study

Explanation: A problem statement must clearly define the gap or issue, its magnitude (prevalence/impact), and why it requires study. Background and objectives are separate sections.

3.
The Hawthorne effect is as a result of:
a) A researcher lacking expertise to analyze data
b) Study subjects being aware they are participating in a study
c) Study tools being biased
d) Respondents not completing the interview schedule
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Study subjects being aware they are participating in a study

Explanation: The Hawthorne effect refers to the alteration of behavior by the subjects of a study due to their awareness of being observed.

4.
Mode refers to:
a) The sum of all values divided by the total number
b) The value which divides the given data in equal parts
c) The middle most value in a given set of numbers
d) The frequently occurring value in a given set of numbers
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) The frequently occurring value in a given set of numbers

Explanation: Mode is the most frequent value. (a) is Mean, (c) is Median.

5.
Brain storming as a teaching strategy:
a) Ensures all group members have their ideas
b) Encourages discovery of knowledge
c) Yields into convincing solutions to most of the cases
d) Usually ends in useful conclusions and solutions to problems
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Encourages discovery of knowledge

Explanation: Brainstorming is an active learning strategy that encourages participants to generate a wide range of ideas freely, fostering discovery and creativity.

6.
The most appropriate method of studying characteristics of wound healing would be:
a) Use of a questionnaire
b) Participants observation
c) Pure observation
d) Context analysis
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Pure observation

Explanation: Wound healing is a physiological process. Pure observation (direct clinical observation/checklist) is the most objective method to assess characteristics like size, color, and drainage.

7.
Projected teaching Aids/ instructional media include:
a) Flip chart, flannel board, simulator
b) 35mm slide, transparency, film
c) Overhead projector, flip chart, pictures
d) Film, simulator, whiteboard
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) 35mm slide, transparency, film

Explanation: Projected aids require a light source and lens to project images onto a screen. Slides and films fit this category. Flip charts and boards are non-projected.

8.
The following is an obstetric factor for prevention of mother to child transmission:
a) Preterm delivery
b) Placental disruption
c) Low maternal CD4 count
d) Substance abuse
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Placental disruption (Note: Question likely refers to risk factors)

Explanation: The question framing is ambiguous. Preterm delivery, Placental disruption, and Low CD4 are risk factors that increase transmission. If "factor for prevention" implies something to manage, avoiding placental disruption (e.g., careful delivery) is key. However, in exam contexts, identifying the obstetric *risk* like placental disruption is often the intended answer to "factors influencing transmission".

9.
During breastfeeding an HIV exposed client / patient should receive:
a) Lamivudine
b) Nevirapine
c) Lopinavir/ritonavir
d) Zidovudine
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Nevirapine

Explanation: According to standard PMTCT guidelines (during the time of this paper), infants born to HIV-positive mothers received daily Nevirapine (NVP) prophylaxis during the breastfeeding period (usually for 6 weeks or until 1 week after cessation of breastfeeding depending on risk profile).

10.
In environmental health, a reservoir host is defined as:
a) A person diagnosed with the disease
b) An organism capable of producing infectious disease in human
c) Any healthy human being, animal or inanimate matter which cannot habitate an infectious agent
d) Any human being, animal or inanimate matter in which an infectious agent habitates and multiplies
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Any human being, animal or inanimate matter in which an infectious agent habitates and multiplies

Explanation: This is the standard definition of a reservoir: the habitat where the agent normally lives, grows, and multiplies.

11.
Fleas in the environment can be controlled by:
a) Clearing water channels
b) Use of traps
c) Avoiding contact with animals
d) Washing and laundering clothes once a week
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Washing and laundering clothes once a week

Explanation: Fleas often live in clothing and bedding (human fleas) or animal fur. Hygiene (washing clothes/bedding, cleaning floors) is a primary control method for environmental infestation. Clearing water is for mosquitoes; traps are for rodents.

12.
The live attenuated vaccines include:
a) Pneumococcal, Haemophilus influenzae, Hepatitis B
b) Human Papilloma Virus, Measles, Rotavirus
c) Inactivated Polio Vaccine, Oral Polio Vaccine, BCG
d) Measles, yellow fever, BCG
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Measles, yellow fever, BCG

Explanation: Measles, Yellow Fever, and BCG (Bacillus Calmette-Guérin) are all classical live attenuated vaccines. IPV (Option C) is inactivated. Pneumococcal/Hib (Option A) are conjugate/subunit.

13.
The order of sensitivity to freezing of vaccines from the most sensitive to the least sensitive:
a) PCV 10, Pentavalent, Rotavirus, BCG, OPV
b) OPV, BCG, Rotavirus, Pentavalent, PCV 10
c) Rotavirus, Tetanus Toxoid, IPV, Yellow Fever
d) Pentavalent, Yellow fever, Tetanus Toxoid, PCV 10
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Pentavalent, Yellow fever, Tetanus Toxoid, PCV 10 (Wait - Hep B/TT/DPT are most freeze sensitive). Let's re-evaluate based on KEPI.
Freeze Sensitive: Hep B (in Penta), DPT (in Penta), TT, PCV-10, IPV.
Heat Sensitive: OPV, Measles, Yellow Fever, BCG.
Option A correctly lists freeze-sensitive vaccines first (PCV10, Penta) but ends with OPV (not freeze sensitive).
Option B lists heat sensitive ones first.
Strictly, Tetanus Toxoid is extremely freeze sensitive. The best answer matching the "Freeze Sensitive" group is likely a variation containing T-series vaccines and Hepatitis B (Pentavalent).

14.
In de facto census:
a) A representative sample of the population is registered
b) People are registered where they usually reside
c) People are registered based on customary attachment to an area
d) People are registered where they are usually staying
Show Answer

Correct Answer: (Closest is typically "Where they are found on census night").

Explanation:
De Facto: Counts people where they are physically present on census night.
De Jure: Counts people where they usually reside (Option B/D).
Since De Facto implies "in fact" or "in reality" at that moment, it usually means counting people where they slept.

15.
The relationship between the economically active population and the non-working population is known as the:
a) Employment ratio
b) Economic ratio
c) Dependency ratio
d) Critical ratio
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Dependency ratio

Explanation: The dependency ratio compares the working-age population (economically active) to the non-working-age population (children and elderly).

16.
Incidence rate:
a) Is used to record protracted diseases
b) Shows the magnitude/ burden of disease in the community
c) Is useful for planning and administration of health care services
d) Shows the rate of development of disease in the community
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Shows the rate of development of disease in the community

Explanation: Incidence measures the number of new cases arising in a population over a period, indicating the rate at which the disease is developing (risk).

17.
A typical case of severe cholera presents with:
a) Decreased urine production
b) High fever with severe abdominal cramps
c) Vomiting preceding watery stool
d) Profuse watery stool preceding vomiting
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Profuse watery stool preceding vomiting

Explanation: The hallmark of cholera is sudden onset of painless, profuse watery diarrhea ("rice water stool") which typically starts before vomiting occurs. Fever and abdominal cramps are usually absent.

18.
The first step in the process of staffing is:
a) Job analysis
b) Recruitment strategy
c) Orientation
d) Planning
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Planning (Human Resource Planning)

Explanation: Staffing begins with planning (determining needs), followed by job analysis/description, recruitment, selection, and orientation. (Note: Some texts list Job Analysis as part of the planning/prep phase).

19.
The vision of an organization outlines:
a) The power of imagination to see something that is not actually visible
b) The worth, usefulness, or importance of something
c) The reason that an organization exists
d) The achievements of an organization
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) The achievements of an organization (Future state)

Explanation: A vision statement describes the future aspirations and desired position of the organization ("where we want to be"). Option C describes the Mission.

20.
The theory that states that ‘leaders are born, not made’ is:
a) Trait
b) Situational
c) Charismatic
d) Great man
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Trait (or Great Man Theory)

Explanation: The Trait Theory (often associated with the Great Man Theory) posits that leadership qualities are inherent/innate characteristics (e.g., intelligence, confidence) that people are born with.

Part II: Short Answer Questions (40 Marks)

1.
Explain the disease transmission cycle. (6 marks)
Show Answer

The chain of infection includes 6 links:

  1. Infectious Agent: Pathogen (bacteria, virus, etc.).
  2. Reservoir: Where the agent lives and multiplies (human, animal, soil).
  3. Portal of Exit: Path by which the pathogen leaves the reservoir (respiratory tract, blood).
  4. Mode of Transmission: Method of transport to a new host (contact, airborne, vector).
  5. Portal of Entry: Path by which pathogen enters new host (broken skin, mucous membranes).
  6. Susceptible Host: Individual with low immunity who gets sick.
2.
State four (4) importance of demographic information. (4 marks)
Show Answer
  1. Health Planning: Determining resource needs based on population size/age structure.
  2. Identifying Trends: Tracking disease patterns, fertility, and mortality rates.
  3. Policy Formulation: Developing housing, education, and employment policies.
  4. Resource Allocation: Distributing funds and facilities equitably.
3.
State five characteristics of an effective performance appraisal. (5 marks)
Show Answer
  1. Objective: Based on observable behavior/results, not bias.
  2. Valid/Relevant: Measures what it is supposed to measure (job-related).
  3. Reliable: Consistent across different evaluators.
  4. Continuous/Regular: Done periodically, not just once a year.
  5. Participatory: Involves feedback and discussion with the employee.
4.
Outline six (6) qualities of a good questionnaire. (6 marks)
Show Answer
  1. Clarity: Questions should be unambiguous and easy to understand.
  2. Brevity: Short and to the point.
  3. Relevance: Questions must relate directly to the study objectives.
  4. Unbiased: Avoid leading questions.
  5. Logical Sequence: Flow from simple to complex/sensitive topics.
  6. Objective: Questions should be capable of being answered definitively.
5.
Outline five (5) purposes of evaluating learners. (5 marks)
Show Answer
  1. To assess achievement of learning objectives (Grading/Certification).
  2. To identify learning difficulties and provide feedback (Diagnostic).
  3. To evaluate teaching effectiveness.
  4. To motivate learners (encouragement).
  5. To select learners for higher courses/placement.
6.
State four (4) purposes of record keeping in a health facility. (4 marks)
Show Answer
  1. Continuity of Care: Ensuring consistent treatment across shifts/providers.
  2. Legal Protection: Documenting care provided in case of litigation.
  3. Research/Statistics: Data source for health trends and audits.
  4. Communication: Sharing information between healthcare team members.
7.
State six ways to prevent mother to child transmission during labor, delivery and breastfeeding. (6 marks)
Show Answer
  1. Antiretroviral Therapy (ART): Maternal adherence to ART to suppress viral load.
  2. Infant Prophylaxis: Administering Nevirapine/Zidovudine to the newborn.
  3. Safe Delivery: Minimizing invasive procedures (e.g., episiotomy, vacuum) and prolonged labor; Elective C-section where indicated.
  4. Exclusive Breastfeeding: For first 6 months (avoiding mixed feeding which damages gut lining) OR Exclusive Replacement Feeding if AFASS.
  5. Routine Monitoring: Regular viral load checks for mother.
  6. Early Diagnosis: PCR testing for infant at 6 weeks.
8.
State four (4) types of solid waste. (4 marks)
Show Answer
  1. Organic Waste: Food scraps, garden waste (biodegradable).
  2. Recyclable Waste: Paper, glass, metals, plastics.
  3. Hazardous/Biomedical Waste: Medical waste, chemicals, paints.
  4. Inert/Construction Waste: Debris, sand, rocks.
  5. Electronic Waste (E-waste).

Part III: Long Answer Questions (40 Marks)

1.
Since the emergence of Ebola outbreak in West Africa the Kenyan government through the Ministry of Health has considered it a necessary public health priority warranting adequate preparedness.

i) Name the micro-organisms which causes Ebola. (1 mark)

Show Answer

Ebolavirus (Genus), Family Filoviridae.

ii) Describe the pathogenesis of Ebola. (5 marks)

Show Answer
  1. Entry: Virus enters via mucous membranes or broken skin.
  2. Infection: Infects macrophages and dendritic cells.
  3. Dissemination: Spreads to lymph nodes, liver, and spleen; replicates rapidly.
  4. Cytokine Storm: Triggers massive release of pro-inflammatory cytokines, causing vascular leakage and shock.
  5. Coagulopathy: Depletes clotting factors causing disseminated intravascular coagulation (DIC) and hemorrhage.

iii) List four (4) typical features seen in patients with Ebola infection. (2 marks)

Show Answer
  1. Sudden high fever.
  2. Severe muscle/joint pain and weakness.
  3. Vomiting and Diarrhea.
  4. Unexplained bleeding (gums, nose, stool).

iv) Explain how the government of Kenya through the Ministry of Health has enhanced its preparedness and response strategies against the outbreak. (12 marks)

Show Answer
  • Surveillance: Strengthening screening at points of entry (airports/borders) with thermal scanners.
  • Isolation Facilities: Designating and equipping isolation units (e.g., KNH, Mbagathi) for case management.
  • Training: Training healthcare workers on Infection Prevention and Control (IPC) and case management.
  • Laboratory Capacity: Enhancing KEMRI labs for rapid viral testing.
  • Public Awareness: Conducting mass media campaigns on prevention and reporting.
  • Coordination: Establishing a National Task Force for outbreak coordination.
  • Logistics: Stockpiling PPEs and medical supplies.
2.
Your county has appointed you as the Head of Division of Family Health Services in the County. Due to personnel shortage, the management further temporarily appoints you as the chairperson of the staffing committee in order to carry out quality recruitment exercise to manage the human resource shortage.

i) Outline four (4) objectives of staffing that you will apply in the recruitment exercise. (4 marks)

Show Answer
  1. To ensure optimum utilization of human resources.
  2. To recruit competent and qualified personnel for specific roles.
  3. To improve organizational productivity and quality of care.
  4. To facilitate employee development and high morale.

ii) Explain six (6) human resource management functions that you will apply in personnel management. (12 marks)

Show Answer
  1. HR Planning: Assessing current and future staffing needs.
  2. Recruitment and Selection: Attracting and choosing the best candidates.
  3. Training and Development: Orientation and continuous education for staff.
  4. Performance Appraisal: Evaluating staff performance against set standards.
  5. Compensation/Benefits: Managing salaries and incentives.
  6. Employee Relations: Handling grievances, discipline, and ensuring a safe work environment.

iii) State four (4) standard operating procedures (SOPS) that will enable you achieve fair treatment of your personnel. (4 marks)

Show Answer
  1. Equal Opportunity Policy: Non-discrimination in hiring/promotion.
  2. Clear Job Descriptions: Defined roles and responsibilities.
  3. Transparent Grievance Mechanism: Formal process for complaints.
  4. Standardized Disciplinary Procedures: Fair hearing and due process.
