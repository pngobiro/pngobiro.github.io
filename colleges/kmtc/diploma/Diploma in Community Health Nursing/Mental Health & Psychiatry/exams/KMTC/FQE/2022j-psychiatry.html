Section I: Multiple Choice Questions (40 Marks)

1.
The following therapeutic communication skill should be used by a nurse when communicating with a client who has a cognitive impairment:
A) Complete explanations with multiple details
B) Picture or gestures instead of words
C) Stimulating words and phrases to capture the client's attention
D) Short words and simple sentences
Show Answer

Correct Answer: D) Short words and simple sentences

Explanation: Patients with cognitive impairment process information slowly. Short, simple sentences reduce cognitive load and improve understanding.

2.
The client outcome most appropriately achieved in a community approach setting in psychiatric nursing is:
A) The client performs activities of daily living and learns about crafts.
B) The client prevents aggressive behavior and monitors his use of medications.
C) The client demonstrates self-reliance and social adaptation.
D) The client experience anxiety relief and learns about his symptoms.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C) The client demonstrates self-reliance and social adaptation.

Explanation: The primary goal of community-based psychiatric care is integration, independence (self-reliance), and functioning within society (social adaptation).

3.
Benzodiazepines are used in the treatment of:
A) Depression.
B) Schizophrenia.
C) Mood disorders.
D) Anxiety.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: D) Anxiety.

Explanation: Benzodiazepines (e.g., Diazepam, Alprazolam) act on GABA receptors to produce anxiolytic (anti-anxiety), sedative, and muscle-relaxant effects.

4.
Second-generation antipsychotics include:
A) Risperidone, Quetiapine, Olanzapine, Brexpiprazole.
B) Aripiprazole, Asenapine, Cariprazine, Haloperidol.
C) Paliperidone, Llperidone, Haloperidol.
D) Clozapine, Llperidone, Lurasidone, Haloperidol.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A) Risperidone, Quetiapine, Olanzapine, Brexpiprazole.

Explanation: This list contains only second-generation (atypical) antipsychotics. Haloperidol (in options B, C, D) is a first-generation (typical) antipsychotic.

5.
Monoamine oxidase inhibitors (MAOIs) are effective drugs for the treatment of:
A) Schizophrenia
B) Obsessive compulsive disorder
C) Major depression
D) Generalized anxiety disorder
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C) Major depression

Explanation: MAOIs are antidepressants used for major depression, especially atypical depression, though they are often second-line due to dietary restrictions (tyramine).

6.
Second-generation medications are generally preferred because they:
A) Are easily available
B) Pose a lower risk of serious side effects
C) Less expensive
D) Long acting
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B) Pose a lower risk of serious side effects

Explanation: Atypical antipsychotics have a lower risk of Extrapyramidal Side Effects (EPS) like tardive dyskinesia compared to first-generation drugs.

7.
Drugs used in bipolar 1 prophylaxis are:
A) carbamazepine, Reserpine, Propranolol
B) lithium, carbamazepine, sodium valproate
C) Diazepam, Clonazepam, Chlordane
D) lamotrigine, Reserpine, Fluoxetine
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B) lithium, carbamazepine, sodium valproate

Explanation: These are the standard Mood Stabilizers used for maintenance treatment (prophylaxis) in Bipolar 1 Disorder. Lithium is the gold standard; Valproate and Carbamazepine are anticonvulsants used as mood stabilizers.

8.
The behavior therapy that involves exposing the patient to the feared object in a real-life context is:
A) Systematic desensitization
B) Reinforcement
C) Aversion therapy
D) Flooding
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A) Systematic desensitization

Explanation: Systematic Desensitization involves gradual exposure to the feared stimulus (often in imagination first, then in vivo) while practicing relaxation. Flooding (D) involves immediate, intense exposure. Given the context of "therapy involves exposing...", systematic desensitization is the classic behavioral treatment for phobias involving exposure hierarchies.

9.
The pleasure principle is associated with
A) The super Ego
B) The Ego
C) The Id
D) The ego and Id
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C) The Id

Explanation: In Freud's psychoanalytic theory, the Id operates on the pleasure principle (immediate gratification of needs). The Ego operates on the reality principle.

10.
Information first gets meaning in the
A) Sensory Memory
B) Short term memory
C) Long term memory
D) Sub conscious
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B) Short term memory

Explanation: Sensory memory holds raw data. Short-term (working) memory is where processing occurs and meaning is assigned before encoding into long-term memory.

11.
The later childhood stage is characterized by
A) Generativity vs stagnation
B) Ego-integrity vs despair
C) Intimacy vs isolation
D) Self-esteem vs Self sufficiency
Show Answer

Correct Answer: D) Self-esteem vs Self sufficiency

Explanation: Options A, B, and C correspond to Adulthood and Old Age stages in Erikson's theory. "Later childhood" (School Age) is typically Industry vs. Inferiority, which relates closely to developing competence (self-sufficiency) and confidence (self-esteem).

12.
Encoding involves
A) processing and combining of received information
B) maintaining the coded information
C) Storing the coded information
D) bringing information stored into awareness
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A) processing and combining of received information

Explanation: Encoding is the initial process of transforming sensory input into a form that can be processed and stored by the memory system.

13.
Reinforcement is delivered after successive approximations of the desired response
A) Shaping
B) Primary reinforcement
C) Secondary response
D) Punishment
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A) Shaping

Explanation: Shaping is an operant conditioning procedure in which reinforcers guide behavior toward closer and closer approximations of the desired behavior.

14.
A patient who believes that his neighbor wants to kill him since he keeps on stalking him could be suffering from:
A) Illusion
B) Delusion
C) Hallucination.
D) Idea of reference
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B) Delusion

Explanation: This is a fixed false belief of persecution (Persecutory Delusion), where the patient believes they are being targeted or harmed.

15.
As a nurse you will know if a patient is suffering from mania if he has:
A) Flight of ideas
B) Flat affect
C) Delusions of worthlessness
D) Clouding of consciousness
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A) Flight of ideas

Explanation: Flight of ideas (rapid, continuous speech with abrupt changes from topic to topic) is a hallmark symptom of Mania. Flat affect is seen in Schizophrenia; Delusions of worthlessness in Depression; Clouding of consciousness in Delirium.

16.
The most appropriate nursing action when trying to diffuse a client's impending violent behavior is:
A) Placing the client in seclusion
B) Leaving the client alone until he can talk about his feelings
C) Involving the client in a quiet activity to divert attention
D) Helping the client identify and express feelings of anxiety and anger
Show Answer

Correct Answer: D) Helping the client identify and express feelings of anxiety and anger

Explanation: Verbal de-escalation is the first line of intervention. Helping the client express feelings helps lower anxiety and prevents acting out.

23.

24.
In antagonistic acculturation:
A) Both cultural parties give and borrow cultural traits from each other.

Correct Answer: B) One of the cultural groups borrows cultural elements for the purpose of protection against the group they are borrowing from.

Section II: Short Answer Questions (40 Marks)

41.
Outline five (5) advantages of Electroconvulsive therapy (ECT) (5 Marks)
Show Answer
  1. Rapid Onset: Works faster than antidepressants, crucial for suicidal patients.
  2. Effectiveness: Highly effective for severe/treatment-resistant depression.
  3. Safety: Safer than medications for certain groups (e.g., pregnant women, elderly with polypharmacy).
  4. No Drug Side Effects: Avoids systemic side effects of psychotropics (weight gain, sexual dysfunction).
  5. Life-saving: Can be life-saving in catatonia or severe mania.
42.
State three (3) communication skills that are used in nurse - patient therapeutic relationships. (3 Marks)
Show Answer
  1. Active Listening.
  2. Empathy.
  3. Clarifying/Validating.
  4. (Also: Open-ended questions, Silence, Reflection).
43.
State five (5) barriers to effective communication. (5 Marks)
Show Answer
  1. Physical Barriers: Noise, distance, poor lighting.
  2. Psychological Barriers: Anxiety, fear, prejudice, bias.
  3. Language Barriers: Different languages, jargon, slang.
  4. Physiological Barriers: Pain, deafness, illness, fatigue.
  5. Cultural Barriers: Differences in norms, beliefs, and non-verbal cues.
44.
State five (5) principles of classical conditioning. (5 Marks)
Show Answer
  1. Acquisition: Learning the association between the Neutral Stimulus and Unconditioned Stimulus.
  2. Extinction: Decreasing response when the Conditioned Stimulus is presented without the Unconditioned Stimulus.
  3. Spontaneous Recovery: Reappearance of a weakened conditioned response after a pause.
  4. Stimulus Generalization: Responding to stimuli similar to the Conditioned Stimulus.
  5. Stimulus Discrimination: Distinguishing between the Conditioned Stimulus and other stimuli.
45.
Explain any two (2) preventive measures of mental retardation (4 marks)
Show Answer
  1. Prenatal Care: Proper maternal nutrition (Folic acid/Iodine), avoiding teratogens (alcohol, drugs), and treating infections (Rubella, Syphilis) to prevent congenital brain damage.
  2. Genetic Counseling: Screening for genetic disorders (Down Syndrome, PKU) in at-risk parents to make informed decisions and manage early.
  3. Perinatal Care: Safe delivery practices to prevent birth asphyxia and trauma.
  4. Postnatal Care: Early treatment of infections (Meningitis), avoiding lead poisoning, and proper nutrition.
46.
Explain any two (2) offences under mental health act (6 marks)
Show Answer
  1. Ill-treatment of patients: Any person employed in a mental hospital who ill-treats or willfully neglects a patient is guilty of an offence.
  2. Sexual intercourse with female patient: Any male person employed in a mental hospital who has sexual intercourse with a female patient is guilty of an offence.
  3. Assisting escape: Assisting a patient to escape from lawful custody in a mental hospital.
  4. False entries: Making false entries in statutory books or records.

Section III: Long Answer Question (20 Marks)

49.
Mr. P is admitted with alcohol use disorder:

a) State any four (4) clinical features he may present with (4 marks)

Show Answer
  • Withdrawal symptoms: Tremors, sweating, nausea, anxiety (if alcohol is stopped).
  • Cravings: Strong urge to drink.
  • Tolerance: Needing more alcohol to get the same effect.
  • Loss of Control: Inability to stop drinking once started.
  • Neglect of Responsibilities: Failure to fulfill work/home duties.

b) Explain three (3) risk factors that may have contributed to his illness (6 marks)

Show Answer
  1. Genetic/Family History: Alcoholism runs in families; genetic predisposition affects metabolism and reward pathways.
  2. Environmental/Social Factors: Peer pressure, easy availability of alcohol, cultural norms encouraging drinking, and stressful life events (unemployment, loss).
  3. Psychological Factors: Comorbid mental health issues (Depression, Anxiety), low self-esteem, or poor coping skills leading to self-medication.

c) Describe the specific nursing care of Mr. P until he is ready for discharge (10 marks)

Show Answer

1. Detoxification & Safety (Acute Phase):

  • Monitor for withdrawal signs (CIWA score). Administer benzodiazepines (e.g., Chlordiazepoxide) as ordered to prevent seizures/DTs.
  • Ensure safety: Seizure precautions, low stimulation environment, fall prevention.
  • Hydration and Nutrition: IV fluids if needed, high-protein diet, and Thiamine (Vitamin B1) supplementation to prevent Wernicke-Korsakoff syndrome.

2. Therapeutic Relationship & Counseling:

  • Establish a non-judgmental, supportive relationship.
  • Encourage expression of feelings (guilt, shame).
  • Educate on the disease process of addiction (it's a disease, not a moral failure).

3. Rehabilitation & Coping Skills:

  • Teach healthy coping mechanisms for stress (exercise, relaxation).
  • Identify triggers and high-risk situations for relapse.
  • Involve family in therapy to address codependency and build support.

4. Discharge Planning:

  • Refer to support groups (Alcoholics Anonymous - AA).
  • Prescribe anti-craving medications (e.g., Naltrexone, Disulfiram) if ordered and educate on their use.
  • Plan follow-up appointments.
