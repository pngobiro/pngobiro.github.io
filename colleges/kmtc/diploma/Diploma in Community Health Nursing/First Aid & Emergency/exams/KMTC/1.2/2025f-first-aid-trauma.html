Module I: First Aid

Section I: Multiple Choice Questions (13 Marks)

1.
In You encounter a casualty with a burn on the right leg that contains small blisters and is extremely pink-red and moist. Which category will you assign it?
A. First degree
B. Second degree
C. Third degree
D. Fourth degree
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B. Second degree

Explanation: Second-degree burns (Partial Thickness) are characterized by blisters (fluid-filled vesicles), moist/weeping surface, and pink/red coloration. They are also typically very painful. First degree is dry/red without blisters. Third degree is dry, leathery, and white/charred.

2.
If a victim had a snake bite on the finger. The following part of the upper limb should the first aider apply a bandage;
A. Forearm
B. Wrist
C. Elbow
D. Palm
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A. Forearm (Implying the whole limb)

Explanation: The standard First Aid for snakebite is the Pressure Immobilization Technique. This involves bandaging the bite site and extending the bandage up the entire limb (forearm and arm) to restrict lymphatic flow. Bandaging just the wrist or elbow is insufficient.

3.
The priority of care for a casualty with an asthmatic attack;
A. Assess the vital signs
B. Help the victim use their inhaler
C. Wrap a blanket around them
D. Remove extra clothing
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B. Help the victim use their inhaler

Explanation: In an acute asthma attack, the priority is to relieve airway bronchoconstriction. Assisting the patient with their prescribed bronchodilator (inhaler) is the most effective immediate intervention.

4.
The principle of first aid include;
A. Control bleeding
B. Care for the dead
C. Care for multi-organ failure
D. Perform chest compressions when the pulse is between 60-100 beats per minute
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A. Control bleeding

Explanation: The main aims of First Aid are: Preserve Life (Control bleeding/CPR), Prevent Worsening, and Promote Recovery. Care for the dead or managing multi-organ failure are not First Aid principles.

5.
The best maneuver to open the airway for a patient with shortness of breath is;
A. Flexion
B. Extension
C. Sniffing position
D. Head tilt-chin lift
Show Answer

Correct Answer: D. Head tilt-chin lift

Explanation: For a non-trauma patient, the Head Tilt-Chin Lift is the standard basic life support maneuver to open the airway by lifting the tongue off the pharynx.

6.
Rescue breathing is used for a victim who is unconscious, but has a pulse. The correct rate for rescue breathing in a child or infant is;
A. 1 breath every 3 to 5 seconds
B. 1 breath every 5 to 6 seconds
C. 2 breaths every 3 to 5 seconds
D. 2 breaths every 5 to 6 seconds
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A. 1 breath every 3 to 5 seconds

Explanation: Current guidelines (AHA 2020) actually recommend 1 breath every 2-3 seconds for children/infants. However, older guidelines (often tested) cited 1 breath every 3-5 seconds (12-20 breaths/min). Option B is for adults.

7.
The aim of primary assessment (ABCD) for casualties is to;
A. Transport the person to the hospital
B. Check for any life-threatening conditions
C. Check the body for identification
D. Find any other injuries or signs
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B. Check for any life-threatening conditions

Explanation: The Primary Survey (DRABC/ABCDE) is designed specifically to identify and immediately treat life-threatening conditions (Airway, Breathing, Circulation).

8.
Correct procedure when treating with an automated electric defibrillator (AED) is;
A. Remove clothing, dry the casualty's skin (when necessary) attach electrodes and turn on the AED
B. Call for assistance, turn on the AED and follow the instructions provided by the AED
C. Dry the casualty's skin (when necessary), attach electrodes, start rescue breathing, call for assistance and turn on the AED
D. Remove the clothing, call for assistance and turn on the AED
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A. Remove clothing, dry the casualty's skin (when necessary) attach electrodes and turn on the AED

Explanation: The key steps for AED use involve exposing the chest, ensuring the skin is dry for adhesion and conduction, attaching pads, and turning it on (order can vary slightly, but pads must be on dry skin).

9.
The following levels of consciousness indicates a victim who is quiet, seems to be sleeping, involuntarily urinates or defecates and can be awakened by voice;
A. Fully conscious
B. Stuporous
C. Restless
D. Coma
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B. Stuporous

Explanation: While the definition of "awakened by voice" typically points to Lethargy/Obtundation, among the given options, "Stuporous" represents the altered state between consciousness and coma. Incontinence suggests a significant depression of consciousness.

10.
A gardener has been bitten by a snake on the ankle. The first aider should;
A. Wrap the entire extremity with an elastic bandage
B. Apply a tourniquet above the ankle
C. Reassure him and apply a compression bandage at the ankle
D. Splint the limb and apply a compression bandage on the whole limb
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A. Wrap the entire extremity with an elastic bandage

Explanation: The Pressure Immobilization Technique involves wrapping a pressure bandage starting from the bite site upwards to cover the entire limb, then immobilizing the limb (splinting). This slows lymphatic spread of venom. Tourniquets (B) are dangerous.

11.
In a chocking victim, the five abdominal thrusts are referred to as;
A. Heimlich maneuver
B. Abdominal draw in maneuver
C. Valsalva maneuver
D. Intra-abdominal maneuver
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A. Heimlich maneuver

Explanation: Abdominal thrusts used to clear an upper airway obstruction are famously known as the Heimlich maneuver.

12.
An unconscious patient who is breathing adequately is placed in a recovery position to prevent;
A. Drainage of secretions out of the mouth
B. Aspirations of secretions and vomitus into the airway
C. More injury to the spine
D. Application of pressure on carotid arteries
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B. Aspirations of secretions and vomitus into the airway

Explanation: The Recovery Position (lateral recumbent) uses gravity to drain fluids (saliva, vomit) out of the mouth, preventing them from entering the trachea (aspiration).

13.
A passenger has been stung by a honey bee. The stinger is still under the skin and needs to be removed to prevent anaphylactic shock. The next course of action is to;
A. Remove the stinger using a pair of sterile tweezers
B. Remove the stinger using a credit card or a butter knife to scrap it out
C. Apply ointment in the area and rub gently
D. Monitor for 24 hours then remove it
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B. Remove the stinger using a credit card or a butter knife to scrap it out

Explanation: The stinger usually has a venom sac attached. Squeezing it with tweezers (A) can inject more venom. Scraping it sideways removes the stinger without compressing the sac.

Section II: Short Answer Questions (25 Marks)

14.
State five (5) signs and symptoms of drug poisoning. (5 marks)
Show Answer
  1. Nausea and Vomiting: GI irritation.
  2. Abdominal Pain/Cramps: Severe gastric distress.
  3. Altered Level of Consciousness: Drowsiness, confusion, or coma.
  4. Respiratory Distress: Slow or shallow breathing (especially with opioids).
  5. Seizures/Convulsions: CNS excitation.
  6. Changes in Pupil Size: Pinpoint (opioids) or dilated (stimulants).
15.
Explain three (3) first aid management for unconscious chocking casualty. (6 marks)
Show Answer
  1. Call for Help: Shout for help or send someone to call EMS/Ambulance immediately.
  2. Start CPR: Begin chest compressions immediately (do not check for pulse). Compressions help increase intrathoracic pressure to dislodge the object.
  3. Check Airway: Before each set of ventilations, open the mouth and check for the object. Only perform a finger sweep if the object is visible. If not seen, attempt ventilation.
16.
State four (4) roles of a triage nurse. (4 marks)
Show Answer
  1. Rapid Assessment: Quickly assessing patients to determine severity of condition.
  2. Prioritization: Categorizing patients based on urgency (e.g., Red, Yellow, Green) to allocate resources effectively.
  3. First Aid/Initial Care: Initiating life-saving measures while waiting for definitive care.
  4. Traffic Control/Organization: Directing patients to appropriate areas and maintaining order in the emergency area.
17.
Explain the first aid management of a casualty who has convulsions. (5 marks)
Show Answer
  1. Safety: Clear the area of sharp or hard objects to prevent injury. Protect the head with something soft (e.g., pillow/jacket).
  2. Do Not Restrain: Allow the seizure to run its course; do not hold the patient down.
  3. Airway: Do not put anything in the mouth. Loosen tight clothing around the neck.
  4. Recovery Position: Once the seizure stops, place the patient in the recovery position to maintain airway and drain secretions.
  5. Monitor: Time the seizure and monitor breathing/pulse. Call for help if it lasts >5 mins or repeats.
18.
Compare and contrast burns based on the depth of skin damage. (5 marks)
Show Answer
  • 1st Degree (Superficial): Affects epidermis only. Red, dry, painful, no blisters (e.g., sunburn). Heals in 3-7 days.
  • 2nd Degree (Partial Thickness): Affects epidermis and part of dermis. Red/pink, moist/weeping, blistered, extremely painful. Heals in 2-3 weeks.
  • 3rd Degree (Full Thickness): Destroys epidermis and dermis. Skin appears white, charred, or leathery. Dry. No pain (nerve endings destroyed). Requires grafting.
  • 4th Degree: Extends to muscle, tendon, and bone. Charred appearance.

Section III: Long Answer Question (20 Marks)

19.
You are called upon to attend to a victim of a stub wound on the chest with a knife in situ at Emergency department. Discuss the management of the casualty. (20 Marks)
Show Answer

1. Immediate Safety & Assessment (DRABC):

  • Danger: Ensure scene is safe (in ER, ensure staff safety).
  • Airway & Breathing: Assess patency. Administer high-flow oxygen. If breathing is compromised (tension pneumothorax), prepare for needle decompression (away from the object).
  • Circulation: Check pulse and BP. Assess for shock.

2. Management of the Impaled Object (Knife):

  • DO NOT REMOVE: The knife may be tamponading (plugging) a severed blood vessel. Removal could cause massive uncontrollable hemorrhage.
  • Stabilization: Secure the knife in place to prevent movement which could cause further internal damage. Use bulky dressings (gauze/pads) around the base of the knife and tape securely.

3. Wound Care:

  • Control bleeding around the object with direct pressure (carefully) without pressing on the object itself.
  • Cover the surrounding wound with a sterile dressing.

4. Resuscitation:

  • IV Access: Establish two large-bore IV lines (14G/16G).
  • Fluids: Initiate fluid resuscitation with crystalloids (RL/NS) or blood products if signs of shock are present.

5. Position & Monitoring:

  • Keep patient in a position of comfort (usually semi-sitting if conscious to aid breathing, or supine if in shock).
  • Monitor Vital signs (BP, Pulse, RR, SpO2) continuously.
  • Monitor for signs of Pneumothorax/Hemothorax.

6. Definitive Care:

  • Prepare for urgent transfer to the Operating Theatre for surgical removal and repair under controlled conditions.
  • Administer Tetanus toxoid and antibiotics as ordered.

Module II: Trauma & Emergency/BLS

Section I: Multiple Choice Questions (7 Marks)

1.
The immediate nursing intervention of 2nd second degree burn;
A. Fluid administration
B. Applyingv of ice cubes
C. Application of silver sulfur diazine
D. Debride the wound
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A. Fluid administration

Explanation: In a trauma/emergency setting, preventing burn shock via fluid resuscitation (Parkland formula) is the critical systemic nursing intervention for significant burns. Locally, cooling with water is first, but ice (B) is contraindicated (causes vasoconstriction/damage). Silver (C) and debridement (D) are secondary wound care steps.

2.
The main indication for Helmlich maneuver?
A. To treat the absence of a victims breathing
B. To treat insufficient breathing.
C. To relocate/move the patient
D. To remove a blockage in the victims airway.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: D. To remove a blockage in the victims airway.

Explanation: The Heimlich maneuver (abdominal thrusts) uses residual air in the lungs to forcefully expel a foreign body obstructing the airway.

3.
Among the victims, who would only need rescue breaths?
A. Agonal gasping with no pulse
B. Breathing with a weak pulse
C. No breathing and a pulse
D. No breathing and no pulse
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C. No breathing and a pulse

Explanation: This is the definition of Respiratory Arrest. Since there is a pulse, chest compressions are not needed yet, only ventilation (rescue breathing). A, D require CPR. B requires monitoring.

4.
The rate of rescue breaths given to infants and children when a pulse is present;
A. 1 breath every 2 to 3 seconds
B. 1 breath every 3 to 5 seconds
C. 1 breath every 5 to 6 seconds
D. 1 breath every 8 to 10 seconds
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A. 1 breath every 2 to 3 seconds

Explanation: Note: AHA Guidelines 2020 updated the rate for children/infants to 1 breath every 2-3 seconds (20-30 breaths/min). Older guidelines stated 3-5 seconds. Option A reflects the current standard.

5.
The action you should take when the Automated External Defibrillated(AED) is analysing the heart rhythm;
A. Check the pulse
B. Continue chest compressions
C. Give rescue breaths only
D. Stand clear of the victim.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: D. Stand clear of the victim.

Explanation: Movement or touching the patient interferes with the AED's ability to analyze the rhythm correctly. Everyone must stand clear.

6.
Which of the following is the highest priority in the initial assessment of a trauma patient?
A. Establishing a patent airway
B. Assessing circulation
C. Stabilizing the cervical spine
D. Assessing level of consciousness
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A. Establishing a patent airway

Explanation: According to ATLS (Advanced Trauma Life Support), Airway (with C-spine protection) is the first priority (A) in the primary survey. Breathing and Circulation follow.

7.
A trauma patient arrives in the emergency department with signs of internal bleeding. The nurse suspects a ruptured spleen. The intervention to prioritize will be.
A. Administering pain medication
B. Preparing for an immediate blood transfusion
C. Administering IV fluids rapidly
D. Preparing the patient for surgery
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C. Administering IV fluids rapidly

Explanation: In hemorrhagic shock, the immediate priority is resuscitation to restore circulating volume (C) while simultaneously preparing for definitive treatment (Surgery - D). Rapid IV fluids (and blood if available/unresponsive) is the first intervention to maintain perfusion.

Section II: Short Answer Questions (15 Marks)

8.
State five components of basic life support (5 Marks)
Show Answer
  1. Recognition: Immediate recognition of cardiac arrest (unresponsiveness, no breathing/no pulse).
  2. Activation: Activation of the Emergency Response System (Call for help).
  3. Circulation: Early CPR with an emphasis on chest compressions.
  4. Defibrillation: Rapid defibrillation (AED use).
  5. Airway/Breathing: Delivering effective rescue breaths (if trained/equipped).
9.
State the four categories of patients using color coding system (4 marks)
Show Answer
  1. Red (Immediate): Life-threatening injuries but treatable (e.g., airway obstruction, shock).
  2. Yellow (Urgent/Delayed): Serious but not immediately life-threatening; can wait a short time (e.g., fractures).
  3. Green (Minor/Walking Wounded): Minor injuries, ambulatory (e.g., abrasions).
  4. Black (Expectant/Deceased): Dead or unsalvageable injuries (e.g., massive head trauma, cardiac arrest in MCI).
10.
Explain three (3) benefits of triaging (6 marks)
Show Answer
  1. Resource Allocation: Ensures limited resources (staff, beds, equipment) are used on patients who need them most and are most likely to survive.
  2. Prioritization of Care: Identifies the most critically ill patients so they receive immediate life-saving treatment, improving survival rates.
  3. Organization/Efficiency: Brings order to chaos during mass casualty incidents or busy ER shifts, facilitating patient flow and reducing confusion.

Section III: Long Answer Questions (20 Marks)

11.
You are an Emergency Nurse at the Casualty Department and you have been called to respond to a motor vehicle head on collision accident victims and one of the victim is unconscious with bruises. Discuss how you will ensure Universal precaution is observed and manage this patient for the first 24 hours. (20 Marks)
Show Answer

Part 1: Universal Precautions (Infection Prevention):

  • Hand Hygiene: Wash hands or use sanitizer before and after patient contact.
  • PPE: Wear gloves (exam or sterile), gown/apron (to prevent blood splash), mask and eye protection/goggles (if risk of splashing fluids/intubation).
  • Safe Disposal: Dispose of sharps in safety boxes and soiled items in appropriate biohazard bins.

Part 2: Management (Primary Survey - ABCDE):

  1. A - Airway with C-Spine Control:
    • Assume spinal injury. Apply cervical collar.
    • Check patency. Suction secretions. Insert Oropharyngeal Airway (OPA) if unconscious.
  2. B - Breathing:
    • Administer 100% Oxygen.
    • Assess for chest injuries (pneumothorax). Assist ventilation if inadequate.
  3. C - Circulation:
    • Check pulse and BP. Stop external bleeding.
    • Insert 2 large-bore IVs. Start IV Fluids (Ringer's Lactate) to treat potential shock.
  4. D - Disability:
    • Assess GCS (Glasgow Coma Scale). Check pupils.
  5. E - Exposure:
    • Remove clothing to inspect for other injuries (bruises mentioned). Keep warm to prevent hypothermia.

Part 3: Secondary Survey & Ongoing Care (First 24 Hours):

  • Head-to-Toe Exam: Assess "bruises" for underlying fractures or internal bleeding.
  • Imaging: CT Head (for unconsciousness/head injury), Chest X-ray, Abdominal Ultrasound (FAST).
  • Monitoring: Continuous vital signs, GCS monitoring (neuro obs), Urine Output (catheterize).
  • Medication: Tetanus toxoid, Analgesics (caution with head injury), Antibiotics if open wounds.
  • Documentation: Record all findings and treatments.
  • Admission: Admit to ICU/High Dependency Unit for close observation.
