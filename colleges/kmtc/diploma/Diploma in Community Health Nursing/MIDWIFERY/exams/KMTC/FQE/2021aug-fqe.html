Section 1: Multiple Choice Questions (40 Marks)

1.
The functional part of the breast is the:
A. Nipple area.
B. Myoepithelial cells.
C. Acini cells.
D. Lactiferous tubules.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C. Acini cells.

Explanation: The Acini cells (alveolar cells) are the milk-producing cells of the breast. Myoepithelial cells contract to eject milk, and tubules transport it.

2.
During embryo development, oral and nasal structures begin to form at:
A. 3 weeks.
B. 5 weeks.
C. 4 weeks.
D. 6 weeks.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C. 4 weeks.

Explanation: Facial development (including the stomodeum or primitive mouth) begins around the 4th week of gestation.

3.
Mrs. Kiwi is in second stage of labour with an Occipito-posterior position. On vaginal examination, the midwife feels the fetal sagittal suture in the transverse diameter of the pelvic cavity and both fontanelles are palpable. This is diagnostic of:
A. Cephalopelvic disproportion.
B. Abnormal fetal lie.
C. Persistent Occipito-posterior position.
D. Deep transverse arrest.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: D. Deep transverse arrest.

Explanation: In Deep Transverse Arrest, the head engages but fails to rotate anteriorly from the OT position. The sagittal suture remains transverse deep in the pelvis, often with deflexion (making both fontanelles palpable).

4.
The congenital cardiac defects that present with cyanosis include:
A. Patent ductus arteriosus and tricuspid atresia.
B. Fallot’s tetrallogy and ventricular septal defect.
C. Hypoplastic left heart syndrome and aortic stenosis.
D. Great vessels transposition and trancus arteriosus.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: D. Great vessels transposition and trancus arteriosus.

Explanation: Cyanotic heart defects (Right-to-Left shunts) include the "5 Ts": Tetralogy of Fallot, Transposition of Great Arteries, Truncus Arteriosus, Tricuspid Atresia, and TAPVR.
A: PDA is acyanotic.
B: VSD is acyanotic.
C: Aortic stenosis is acyanotic.
D: Both are cyanotic.

5.
The 1st and 2nd home visits following spontaneous vertex delivery should be accomplished within:
A. 2 – 4 weeks.
B. 0 – 4 weeks.
C. 4 – 6 weeks.
D. 6 – 8 weeks.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B. 0 – 4 weeks. (Ideally 0-2 weeks).

Explanation: First visit is within 48 hours. Second visit is within 1-2 weeks. Therefore, both are completed within the first 4 weeks (Option B).

6.
On placenta examination, the characteristics of the chorion membrane include:
A. It’s opaque and tough to tear.
B. It’s whitish and very smooth.
C. It’s opaque and easy to tear.
D. It’s transparent and smooth.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C. It’s opaque and easy to tear. (Friable)

Explanation: The Chorion is the outer membrane, rough/opaque, and friable (tears easily). The Amnion is inner, smooth, transparent, and tough.

7.
In development of the zygote, amnion membrane develops from the:
A. Inner cell mass.
B. Outer cell mass.
C. Embryo.
D. Fetus.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A. Inner cell mass.

Explanation: The Inner Cell Mass (embryoblast) differentiates into the embryo and the amnion. The Outer Cell Mass (trophoblast) becomes the chorion and placenta.

8.
During labour, the PRIORITY action for the midwife to take upon noticing variable deceleration of the fetal heart rate is to:
A. Change the mother’s current position.
B. Prepare for emergency caesarean section.
C. Rule out the presence of placenta praevia.
D. Administer oxygen to the mother immediately.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A. Change the mother’s current position.

Explanation: Variable decelerations are typically caused by cord compression. Changing the mother's position (e.g., to left lateral or knee-chest) is the first-line intervention to relieve pressure on the cord.

9.
The CORRECT statement about foetal circulation is:
A. Ductus venosus conveys deoxygenated blood to the right atrium.
B. Ductus arteriosus conveys oxygenated blood to the right atrium.
C. The foetal haemoglobin is produced by the yolk sack between the 5th - 36th weeks.
D. Most of the blood in the right atrium is diverted to the left atrium via foramen ovale.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: D. Most of the blood in the right atrium is diverted to the left atrium via foramen ovale.

Explanation: Highly oxygenated blood from the IVC is directed across the right atrium through the foramen ovale into the left atrium to supply the brain and heart.

10.
Nurse Clara is performing routine postnatal care for a client who delivered a few days ago. The statement which, if uttered by the client is suggestive of post-partum depression is:
A. “I feel like crying for no apparent reason”
B. “I have been frequently getting a headache”
C. “I find myself becoming teary-eyed at times”
D. “I have been feeling restless a lot lately”
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A. “I feel like crying for no apparent reason”

Explanation: While A and C can be signs of "Baby Blues", unprovoked crying, restlessness, and persistent low mood are early indicators that it may be developing into depression. Severe headaches (B) suggest pre-eclampsia.

11.
An important landmark of the pelvis that determines the distance of the descent of the presenting part is the:
A. Linea terminalis.
B. Sacral promontory.
C. Ischial spines.
D. Ischial tuberosities.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C. Ischial spines.

Explanation: Station 0 is defined as the level of the Ischial Spines. Descent is measured in cm above (-) or below (+) this landmark.

12.
The PRIORITY action to take for a client with hypotonic uterine contractions intrapartumly is:
A. Obtain an ultrasonic result.
B. Augment with oxytocin infusion.
C. Administer opioid analgesia.
D. Prepare her for amniotomy.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B. Augment with oxytocin infusion. (After ruling out CPD)

Explanation: Hypotonic inertia (weak contractions) is primarily treated by augmentation with Oxytocin (Syntocinon) to strengthen contractions, provided there is no obstruction.

13.
In the management of the second stage of labour, when the face sweeps the perineum, the midwife should first:
A. Clean the face using a soft gauze.
B. Deliver the anterior shoulder gently.
C. Clean the nose gently.
D. Facilitate delivery of the body.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A. Clean the face using a soft gauze. (And checking for cord)

Explanation: Once the head is born, the immediate action is usually to check for a nuchal cord and wipe the face/mouth to clear airways before the next contraction delivers the shoulders.

14.
The analysis of the foetal deaths from 22 weeks gestation up to the first 7 days post-partum is an accurate measure of the:
A. Neonatal death rate.
B. Still birth rate.
C. Perinatal death rate.
D. Post-neonatal death rate.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C. Perinatal death rate.

Explanation: Perinatal mortality rate includes stillbirths (after 22/28 weeks) and early neonatal deaths (0-7 days).

15.
Cord presentation is where the cord lies in front of the presenting part:
A. After the head has engaged.
B. After the membranes have ruptured.
C. Before the membranes have ruptured.
D. Before the head has engaged.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C. Before the membranes have ruptured.

Explanation:

  • Cord Presentation: Cord is in front of the presenting part, membranes are intact.
  • Cord Prolapse: Cord is in front/below presenting part, membranes are ruptured.

16.
The reflex response that is assessed by gently touching the corner of the baby’s mouth is:
A. Rooting reflex.
B. Sucking reflex.
C. Moro reflex.
D. Grasp reflex.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A. Rooting reflex.

Explanation: The baby turns the head towards the stimulus and opens the mouth to find the nipple.

17.
Following normal delivery, the assistant is instructed to keep the baby warm, score at 5 minutes & at 10 minutes then,
A. Take birth weight, administer vitamin K.
B. Take neonate to NBU, take birth weight.
C. Take neonate to NBU, give tetracycline eye ointment.
D. Bring neonate for breastfeeding, take neonate to NBU.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A. Take birth weight, administer vitamin K. (Or D depending on protocol emphasis)

Explanation: Immediate care includes warmth and bonding (breastfeeding). Once stable (after APGARs), routine care like weighing, Vit K, and eye care are performed. "Take to NBU" implies separation which is discouraged unless sick. Option A represents standard ward care procedures.

18.
Icterus gravis and hydrops fetalis are conditions highly associated with:
A. Rhesus D incompatibility.
B. ABO incompatibility.
C. Physiological jaundice.
D. Instant perinatal death.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A. Rhesus D incompatibility.

Explanation: Severe Rh isoimmunization causes severe hemolysis, leading to severe jaundice (Icterus Gravis) and severe edema/heart failure (Hydrops Fetalis).

19.
Vitamin K prevents haemorrhagic disease of the neonate by:
A. Activating the white blood cells.
B. Altering the red blood cell count.
C. Activating the dormant clotting factors.
D. Adopting a fibrinogen sparing mechanism.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C. Activating the dormant clotting factors. (Synthesis of factors)

Explanation: Vitamin K is a cofactor required for the hepatic synthesis/activation of clotting factors II, VII, IX, and X.

20.
Delivery of breech with extended arms is accomplished through:
A. Burns- Marshall maneuver.
B. Lovset maneuver.
C. Wood’s maneuver.
D. Zavanelli maneuver.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B. Lovset maneuver.

Explanation: Lovset's maneuver involves rotation of the body to bring the posterior arm anteriorly for delivery, then rotating back to deliver the other arm.

21.
The first cluster of cells formed after fertilization is known as:
A. Zygote.
B. Morula.
C. Blastula.
D. Foetus.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B. Morula.

Explanation: Zygote divides (cleavage) to form a solid ball of cells called the Morula (mulberry shape), which then becomes a Blastocyst.

22.
The presenting diameter in brow presentation is:
A. Mentovertical.
B. Occipitofrontal.
C. Sub-mentobregmatic.
D. Sub-occipitofrontal.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A. Mentovertical.

Explanation: The Mento-vertical diameter (13.5 cm) is the largest diameter of the fetal head and presents in Brow presentation, usually causing obstruction.

23.
Clinical features of intracranial injury include:
A. Limpness, bruises and hypothermia.
B. Irritability, hypoxia and paralysis.
C. Hypothermia, limpness and irritability.
D. Hypocalcaemia, paralysis and hypoxia.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B. Irritability, hypoxia and paralysis. (Or high pitched cry, tense fontanelle)

24.
In focused antenatal care, the 2nd visit occurs between:
A. 28th – 32nd week.

25.
The correct management approach, for a patient with severe hyperemesis gravidarum is:
A. Hospitalization, reassure & correct fluid imbalance.
B. Outpatient care, normal diet & specific drugs.
C. Hospitalization, reassure & special liquid diet.
D. Outpatient care, correct fluid imbalance & normal diet.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A. Hospitalization, reassure & correct fluid imbalance.

26.
During the second stage of labour, the breathing technique that the mother should be instructed to use as the fetal head is crowning is:
A. Blowing technique.
B. Slow chest breathing.
C. Shallow breathing.
D. Accelerated- decelerated.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A. Blowing technique. (Panting)

Explanation: To prevent rapid expulsion and perineal tearing during crowning, the mother is told to stop pushing and "pant" or "blow" to allow slow extension of the head.

27.
During the first stage of labour, the frequency of uterine contractions is determined by timing from the beginning of one contraction:
A. To the beginning of the next contraction.
B. Until the time it is completely over.
C. To the end of a second contraction.
D. Until the time the uterus becomes firm.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A. To the beginning of the next contraction.

Explanation: Frequency is the interval from the onset of one contraction to the onset of the next.

28.
While providing care to the neonate after birth, which one of the following interventions has the HIGHEST PRIORITY?
A. Administer vitamin K.
B. Administer IV dextrose.
C. Cover the neonate’s head with a cap.
D. Perform top-tailing of the neonate.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C. Cover the neonate’s head with a cap. (Thermoregulation)

Explanation: Preventing heat loss (hypothermia) is a critical immediate priority (Warm Chain). The head has a large surface area for heat loss. Vitamin K and cleaning (top-tailing) are later routine care.

29.
Persistent occipitoposterior position is always associated with:
A. Deep transverse arrest.
B. Face to pubis.
C. Face presentation.
D. Shoulder dystocia.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B. Face to pubis. (Outcome)

Explanation: If the occiput rotates posteriorly (short rotation) and persists, the baby is born looking up (Face to Pubis). If it arrests in transverse, it's DTA (Option A), but POP specifically implies posterior orientation.

30.
The PRIORITY management of uncomplicated malaria during the FIRST TRIMESTER includes administration of:
A. Sulphadoxine pyrimethamine.
B. Parenteral quinine.
C. Oral quinine.
D. Artemether lumefantrine.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C. Oral quinine. (For uncomplicated)

Explanation: In the 1st trimester, Quinine is the drug of choice to avoid potential teratogenicity of AL. For uncomplicated malaria, Oral Quinine is used. Parenteral is for severe malaria.

The method of heat loss prevented by wiping the neonate dry at birth is:

33.
To ensure that the baby will breathe as soon as the head is delivered, the nurse’s PRIORITY action is to:
A. Slap the baby’s buttocks to make the baby cry.
B. Suction the nose and mouth to remove mucous secretions.
C. Clamp the cord about 6 inches from the base.

34.
The normal umbilical cord is composed of:
A. 2 arteries and 1 vein.
B. 2 veins and 1 artery.
C. 2 arteries and 2 veins.
D. 3 arteries and 1 vein.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A. 2 arteries and 1 vein.

Explanation: The cord contains two umbilical arteries (carrying deoxygenated blood) and one umbilical vein (carrying oxygenated blood).

35.
At what stage of labour and delivery does a primigravida differ mainly from a multigravida?
A. Stage 1
B. Stage 2
C. Stage 3
D. Stage 4
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A. Stage 1

Explanation: The duration of the first stage (dilation) is significantly longer in primigravidae (12-18 hours) compared to multigravidae (6-12 hours). The second stage is also longer, but the absolute time difference is greatest in Stage 1.

36.

40.
A client with pregnancy induced hypertension is likely to exhibit the following symptoms:
A. Proteinuria, headaches and severe vomiting.
B. Headaches, double vision & vaginal bleeding.
C. Proteinuria, headaches & double vision.

42.
State five (5) benefits of breastfeeding to the mother. (5 marks)
Show Answer

Answer:

  1. Involution: Oxytocin release promotes uterine contraction and involution.
  2. Reduces Bleeding: Contractions reduce risk of PPH.
  3. Weight Loss: Burns calories (approx 500 kcal/day).
  4. Contraception: Lactational Amenorrhea Method (LAM) delays ovulation.
  5. Cancer Protection: Reduces risk of breast and ovarian cancer.
  6. Bonding: Promotes emotional bonding with baby.
43.
Explain five (5) causes of asphyxia neonatorum. (5 marks)
Show Answer

Answer:

  1. Cord Accidents: Cord prolapse, nuchal cord, true knot (interrupts blood flow).
  2. Placental Issues: Abruptio placentae, Placenta previa (reduced perfusion).
  3. Maternal Factors: Severe Pre-eclampsia, Hypotension, Anemia (reduced oxygen delivery).
  4. Fetal Factors: Prematurity (immature lungs), Post-maturity (placental aging).
  5. Prolonged/Obstructed Labor: Continuous compression prevents reperfusion.
44.
State two (2) physiological changes prenatally on each of the following body systems:
a) Cardiovascular system.
b) Renal system.
c) The skin. (6 marks)
Show Answer

Answer:

a) Cardiovascular System:

  • Increased blood volume (Plasma > RBCs) leading to physiological anemia.
  • Increased Cardiac Output (Stroke volume + Heart rate).

b) Renal System:

  • Increased Glomerular Filtration Rate (GFR).

c) The Skin:

  • Striae Gravidarum: Stretch marks on abdomen/breasts.
45.
Outline four (4) objectives of post-natal home visits. (4 marks)
Show Answer

Answer:

  1. To assess the general health of the mother (involution, lochia, breast care).
  2. To assess the health of the newborn (cord, feeding, jaundice, weight).
  3. To detect and treat complications early (e.g., sepsis, mastitis).
  4. To provide health education on hygiene, nutrition, and family planning.
  5. To promote bonding and breastfeeding.
46.
Explain the evaluation scale which is done on neonates one minute after birth. (5 marks)
Show Answer

Answer: This refers to the APGAR Score:

  • A - Appearance (Color): 0=Blue/Pale, 1=Body pink/extremities blue, 2=All Pink.
  • P - Pulse (Heart Rate): 0=Absent, 1=<100 bpm, 2=>100 bpm.
  • G - Grimace (Reflex Irritability): 0=None, 1=Grimace, 2=Cry/Sneeze.
  • A - Activity (Muscle Tone): 0=Limp, 1=Some flexion, 2=Active motion.
  • R - Respiration: 0=Absent, 1=Slow/Irregular, 2=Good cry.
47.
Explain three (3) elements in Bishop’s score leading to successful Induction of labour. (6 marks)
Show Answer

Answer: (Any 3 elements)

  1. Cervical Dilation: Measures the opening of the cervix (0, 1-2, 3-4, 5+ cm).
  2. Cervical Effacement: Measures the thinning/shortening of the cervix (0-30%, 40-50%, 60-70%, 80%+).

Section 3: Long Answer Question (20 Marks)

49.
Baby Kim, born 36 hours ago at term via caesarean section with a birth weight 3000 grams is admitted in the newborn unit with neonatal jaundice. Phototherapy is prescribed.
a) State four (4) causes of physiological jaundice. (4 marks)
b) Explain the process of bilirubin conjugation. (5 marks)
c) Describe baby Kim’s specific management until the jaundice clears. (11 marks)
Show Answer

Answer:

a) Causes of Physiological Jaundice (4 marks)

  • Polycythemia: High red blood cell count at birth.
  • Short RBC Lifespan: Fetal RBCs last only ~80 days (vs 120 in adults), leading to rapid breakdown.
  • Immature Liver: Low levels of Glucuronyl Transferase enzyme limit conjugation capacity.
  • Increased Enterohepatic Circulation: Lack of gut flora allows beta-glucuronidase to deconjugate bilirubin, reabsorbing it.

b) Process of Bilirubin Conjugation (5 marks)

1. Breakdown: RBCs break down in the reticuloendothelial system (spleen), releasing Heme. Heme is converted to Biliverdin, then to Unconjugated Bilirubin (Fat soluble).

2. Transport: Unconjugated bilirubin binds to albumin in the blood and is transported to the liver.

3. Uptake: Liver hepatocytes take up the bilirubin.

4. Conjugation: In the smooth endoplasmic reticulum, the enzyme Uridine Diphosphoglucuronyl Transferase (UDPGT) conjugates bilirubin with glucuronic acid to form Conjugated Bilirubin (Water soluble).

5. Excretion: Conjugated bilirubin is excreted into bile and enters the intestine.

c) Specific Management of Baby Kim (11 marks)

  1. Phototherapy Care:
    • Eye Protection: Cover eyes with opaque patches to prevent retinal damage.
    • Skin Exposure: Maximize skin exposure (only diaper on).
    • Distance: Ensure light source is at appropriate distance (approx 45cm).
    • Position Change: Turn baby every 2-3 hours to expose all skin areas.
  2. Hydration/Feeding:
    • Encourage frequent breastfeeding (every 2-3 hours) to promote stooling (bilirubin excretion) and prevent dehydration from phototherapy heat.
    • Calculate fluid requirements (Day 2: ~80ml/kg) and supplement if necessary.
  3. Thermoregulation:
    • Monitor temperature frequently (risk of overheating under lights or cooling if undressed). Adjust incubator/room temp.
  4. Monitoring:
    • Monitor Serum Bilirubin levels regularly (e.g., every 12-24 hours).
    • Observe skin color (though unreliable under lights).
    • Monitor for side effects: Loose stools, skin rash, dehydration.
  5. Parental Support:
    • Explain the condition and treatment to reduce anxiety.
    • Allow mother to breastfeed and bond during breaks (if lights allow).
