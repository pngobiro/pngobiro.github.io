Section 1: Multiple Choice Questions (40 Marks)

1.
Pregnancy test for presence of;
A. Oestrogen
B. Human Chorionic Gonadotropin
C. Human placental lactogen
D. Progesterone
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B. Human Chorionic Gonadotropin

Explanation: Pregnancy tests detect the presence of Human Chorionic Gonadotropin (hCG) in urine or blood. This hormone is produced by the syncytiotrophoblast cells of the placenta shortly after implantation.

2.
In early developmental stages, the trophoblast become the;
A. Placenta
B. Embryo
C. Blastocyst
D. Umbilical cord
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A. Placenta

Explanation: The blastocyst differentiates into two layers: the inner cell mass (which becomes the embryo/fetus) and the outer layer called the trophoblast, which develops into the placenta and chorion.

3.
The four temporary structures in fetal circulation include;
A. Ductus venosus, ductus arteriosus, foramen ovale, hypogastric arteries
B. Ductus venosus, ductus arteriosus, superior venacava, inferior venacava
C. Pulmonary vein, pulmonary artery, superior venacava, inferior venacava
D. Foramen ovale, Hypogastric arteries, ductus arteriosus, pulmonary artery
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A. Ductus venosus, ductus arteriosus, foramen ovale, hypogastric arteries

Explanation: The temporary fetal structures are:

  1. Ductus Venosus: Bypasses liver.
  2. Foramen Ovale: Shunt between atria.
  3. Ductus Arteriosus: Shunt between pulmonary artery and aorta.
  4. Hypogastric Arteries (Umbilical Arteries): Return blood to placenta.

4.
The three bones fused together to make the innominate bones are the;
A. Ilium, Ischium, sacrum
B. Ilium, ischium, pubis
C. Pubis, sacrum, ischium
D. Ischium, coccyx, sacrum

Correct Answer: B. Ilium, ischium, pubis

Explanation: Each innominate (hip) bone is formed by the fusion of three bones: the Ilium (superior), Ischium (inferior/posterior), and Pubis (anterior). They meet at the acetabulum.

6.
After 36 weeks of gestation, the lie of the fetus in the uterus is termed as normal if it is;
A. Transverse
B. Longitudinal
C. Oblique
D. Vertex
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B. Longitudinal

Explanation: "Lie" refers to the relationship of the long axis of the fetus to the long axis of the mother. A Longitudinal lie is the only normal lie for vaginal delivery. Vertex (Option D) is a presentation, not a lie.

7.
Physiological changes that take place in the uterus during first stage of labour include;
A. Braxton hicks contractions, effacement, rupture of membranes
B. Lightening, contraction and retraction of the uterine muscles, increased leucorrhoea
C. Contraction and retraction of the uterine muscles, effacement, rupture of membranes
D. Rupture of membranes, show, Braxton hicks contraction
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C. Contraction and retraction of the uterine muscles, effacement, rupture of membranes

Explanation: The key physiological events in the 1st stage are true labor contractions, retraction (shortening of muscle fibers to open cervix), effacement (thinning of cervix), and dilation. Rupture of membranes often occurs during this stage. Braxton hicks and lightening (Options A, B) are pre-labour events.

8.
Premonitory signs of labour include;
A. Painful, regular, rhythmic uterine contractions, dilatation of the cervix, presence of show
B. Braxton hicks contractions, dilatation of the cervix, presence of show
C. Lightening, frequency of micturition, effacement
D. Dilatation of the cervix, effacement, increased leucorrhoea
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C. Lightening, frequency of micturition, effacement

Explanation: Premonitory signs occur before the onset of true labor. They include lightening (baby dropping), increased frequency of micturition (due to head pressure), weight loss, energy spurt ("nesting"), and "taking up" (effacement) of the cervix.

b) During pregnancy, cardiac output increases by 30 – 50% reaching its peak during the second trimester

10.

11.
Antepartum haemorrhage due to placenta praevia is characterized by;
A. Abdomen, visible per vaginal bleeding
B. Abdomen woody hard, visible per vaginal bleeding
C. Shock corresponds to amount of blood loss, per vaginal bleeding absent
D. Shock does not correspond to amount of blood loss, abdomen woody hard
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A. Abdomen [Soft], visible per vaginal bleeding (Option A is incomplete but implies 'Soft' abdomen vs 'Woody' in abruption)

Explanation:

  • Placenta Praevia: Painless, causeless, recurrent bright red vaginal bleeding. Uterus is soft and non-tender. Shock corresponds to visible blood loss.
  • Abruptio Placentae: Painful, woody hard uterus. Shock may be out of proportion to visible bleeding (concealed).
Note: Option A is the best fit by elimination of "Woody hard" and "Absent bleeding".

14.

15.

16.
The hormones responsible for fetal growth and development to maturity include;
A. Estrogen and prolactin
B. Progesterone and oxytocin
C. Oestrogen and progesterone
D. Prolactin and oxytocin
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C. Oestrogen and progesterone

17.
A mother with cardiac disease grade four presents with signs which include;
A. No significant sign of ailment, can perform her normal duties
B. Is exhausted even when at rest, requires total nursing care for all activities
C. Can attempt to climb stairs, breathlessness
D. Cough, mild fever, exhaustion that wears off easily
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B. Is exhausted even when at rest, requires total nursing care for all activities

Explanation: NYHA Classification of Heart Failure:

  • Grade I: No limitation.
  • Grade II: Slight limitation (symptoms on ordinary activity).
  • Grade III: Marked limitation (symptoms on less than ordinary activity).
  • Grade IV: Inability to carry on any physical activity without discomfort. Symptoms of heart failure at rest.

19.

A. Atonic uterus, tears, hypo-frinogenaemia
B. Clots, fragments of placenta and membranes, lacerations of the cervix

Correct Answer: A. Atonic uterus, tears, hypo-frinogenaemia

20.

B. CPD, delivery of a premature baby, fetal distress
C. Cardiac disease in pregnancy, delivery of a premature baby, CPD

43.
Explain three (3) causes of cord prolapse. (6 marks)
Show Answer

Answer:

  1. Malpresentations (e.g., Breech, Transverse Lie): The presenting part does not fit snugly into the lower uterine segment, leaving space for the cord to slip past.
  2. Polyhydramnios: Sudden rupture of membranes causes a rapid gush of fluid that can wash the cord down before the head engages.
  3. Prematurity: Small fetal size relative to the pelvis allows space for the cord to descend.
  4. High Head (Non-engaged) at Rupture of Membranes: If membranes rupture when the head is still floating (e.g., CPD, Multiparity), the cord can prolapse.
44.
Outline four (4) causes of Anaemia in pregnancy. (4 marks)
Show Answer

Answer:

  1. Nutritional Deficiency: Lack of Iron (Iron Deficiency Anemia) or Folic Acid (Megaloblastic Anemia).
  2. Parasitic Infections: Malaria (destroys RBCs) and Hookworm infestation (causes chronic blood loss).
  3. Haemorrhage: Antepartum haemorrhage (Placenta praevia/Abruption) or bleeding from other sites.
  4. Chronic Diseases: HIV/AIDS, Tuberculosis, Renal failure.
  5. Haemoglobinopathies: Sickle Cell Disease.
45.
Explain the physiological changes that take place in the uterus during 2nd stage of labour (5 marks)
Show Answer

Answer:

  1. Contraction: Stronger, longer, and more frequent uterine contractions (expulsive nature).
  2. Retraction: Permanent shortening of muscle fibers in the upper uterine segment, reducing uterine cavity size and pushing the fetus down.
  3. Polarity: Coordination between the upper segment (contracting/retracting) and lower segment (dilating/passive).
  4. Distension of Lower Segment: The lower segment stretches and thins out to accommodate the fetus.
  5. Formation of Retraction Ring: A distinct physiological boundary forms between the thick upper segment and thin lower segment.
46.
Describe the physiology of lactation. (5 marks)
Show Answer

Answer:

  1. Mammogenesis: Preparation of breasts during pregnancy (Estrogen grows ducts, Progesterone grows alveoli).
  2. Lactogenesis I (Mid-pregnancy): Colostrum production begins but is inhibited by high progesterone.
  3. Lactogenesis II (Onset of Milk): After placental delivery, estrogen/progesterone drop. Prolactin (from Anterior Pituitary) stimulates milk synthesis in alveoli.
  4. Galactokinesis (Milk Ejection): Suckling stimulates Oxytocin (from Posterior Pituitary), causing contraction of myoepithelial cells to eject milk ("Let-down reflex").
  5. Galactopoiesis (Maintenance): Continuous milk production driven by frequent emptying (Supply and Demand) and Prolactin.
47.
State four (4) preventive measures of Ophthalmia Neonatorum. (4 marks)
Show Answer

Answer:

  1. Eye Prophylaxis: Routine application of Tetracycline eye ointment (1%) within 1 hour of birth.
  2. Antenatal Screening/Treatment: Screening mothers for Gonorrhea/Chlamydia and treating infections before delivery.
  3. Wiping Eyes: Wiping the baby's eyes immediately after head is born (from inner to outer canthus) with sterile wet swabs.
  4. Hygiene: Observing aseptic technique during delivery.
48.
Explain three (3) causes of fetal distress. (6 marks)
Show Answer

Answer:

  1. Cord Compression: (e.g., Cord prolapse, nuchal cord) interrupts blood flow to the fetus.
  2. Placental Insufficiency: (e.g., Post-maturity, Pre-eclampsia, Abruption) reduces oxygen transfer to the fetus.
  3. Maternal Hypotension: (e.g., Supine hypotension, shock, epidural) reduces placental perfusion.
  4. Uterine Hyperstimulation: (e.g., Oxytocin overdose) prevents placental reperfusion between contractions.

a) Indications for First Examination (3 marks)

  • To detect any congenital abnormalities (e.g., cleft lip/palate, spina bifida).
  • To determine the general condition of the baby (APGAR scoring).
  • To confirm gender and identity of the baby.
  • To determine maturity (Gestational age assessment).

b) Preventive Measures of Ophthalmia Neonatorum (5 marks)

  • Eye Care at Birth: Wipe eyes with sterile cotton swabs (one for each eye) from inner to outer canthus immediately after the head is born.
  • Prophylaxis: Apply Tetracycline Eye Ointment (1%) to both eyes within the first hour.
  • Maternal Screening: Screen and treat maternal genital infections (Gonorrhea/Chlamydia) during ANC.
  • Hygiene: Maintain asepsis during delivery to minimize contamination.
  • Prompt Treatment: Treat any eye discharge immediately to prevent spread.

c) Specific Management in First 24 Hours (12 marks)

  1. Maintenance of Thermoregulation:
    • Keep baby warm using skin-to-skin contact (KMC).
    • Dress in warm clothing (vest, wrapper, hat, socks).
    • Keep room warm and free of drafts.
    • Delay bathing for at least 24 hours.
  2. Establishment of Breastfeeding:
    • Initiate breastfeeding within the first hour.
    • Assist mother with attachment and positioning.
    • Encourage exclusive breastfeeding on demand (at least every 2-3 hours).
  3. Infection Prevention:
    • Cord Care: Keep stump clean and dry (or use Chlorhexidine digluconate 7.1% if protocol allows). Do not cover with diaper.
    • Eye Care: Monitor for discharge.
    • Hand washing before handling baby.
  4. Monitoring and Observation:
    • Monitor vital signs (Temp 36.5-37.5°C, HR 120-160, RR 40-60).
    • Observe for passage of meconium (within 24h) and urine.
    • Check for danger signs (jaundice, bleeding, convulsions, refusal to feed).
  5. Immunization:
    • Administer birth vaccines: BCG (for TB) and OPV 0 (Polio).
  6. Bonding:
    • Encourage rooming-in (mother and baby together 24/7).
