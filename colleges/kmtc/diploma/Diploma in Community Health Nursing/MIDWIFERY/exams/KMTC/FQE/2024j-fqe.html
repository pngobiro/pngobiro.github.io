Section 1: Multiple Choice Questions (40 Marks)

1.
During the pre-historic age midwifery was practiced by: -
A. Trained midwives
B. Ordinary trained nurses
C. Uneducated but experienced women
D. Midwives and doctors.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C. Uneducated but experienced women

Explanation: Historically, before the formalization of midwifery education, childbirth was attended by "Traditional Birth Attendants" (TBAs) or community women who gained skills through experience rather than formal training.

2.
You are teaching anatomy and physiology to newly admitted nursing students. The features that are true about the android pelvis that you would share with them include:
A. Rounded brim, narrow fore pelvis, Ischial spines blunt.
B. Fore pelvis wide, sciatic notch wide, sub-pubic angle more than 90°.
C. Sciatic notch rounded, brim rounded, ischial spines
D. Rounded brim, side walls are straight, fore pelvis generous.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A. Rounded brim, narrow fore pelvis, Ischial spines blunt.

Note: The Android pelvis is typically heart-shaped (not rounded), with a narrow fore pelvis and prominent (not blunt) spines. However, compared to other options: B, C, D describe Gynaecoid features (Wide fore pelvis, wide notch, rounded brim). Option A contains "Narrow fore pelvis" which is a key Android feature, even if other descriptors vary by text. In multiple choice elimination, Android = Narrow/Funnel.

3.
You are recording your findings on the partograph. An indicator of good progress in established labor would be:
A. Cervical dilatation to the Right of the Alert Line
B. Cervical dilatation to the Right of the Action Line
C. Foetal descend of 5/5 at 8 cm dilatation
D. Two uterine contractions every 10 min lasting 10 sec
Show Answer

Correct Answer: (None of the above strictly "Good", likely meant Left of Alert Line). However, in context of *poor* progress indicators, A/B are signs of delay.

Correction: Good progress is plotted to the Left of the Alert line.

  • Right of Alert Line = Delay.
  • Right of Action Line = Critical Delay.
  • 5/5 descent at 8cm = Obstruction (Head high).
  • 2 contractions/10 mins = Uterine inertia (Poor).
If the question meant "Indicator of POOR progress", A/B/C/D all apply. Assuming typo in question asking for "Alert" status.

5.

8.
During fetal development, the organs formed from the ectoderm layer include:
A. Mucous membranes and glands
B. Bones, muscles, the heart and blood vessels
C. Skin and the nervous system
D. Nervous system and mucous membranes
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C. Skin and the nervous system

Explanation:

  • Ectoderm: Epidermis (skin), Nervous system (CNS/PNS), Hair, Nails.
  • Mesoderm: Bones, Muscles, Heart, Circulatory system (Option B).
  • Endoderm: Mucous membranes, glands, gut lining (Option A).

9.
The membranous space on the fetal skull that separates the frontal bones from the two parietal bones is called the:
A. Frontal
B. Coronal
C. Sagittal
D. Lambdoidal
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B. Coronal

Explanation: The Coronal suture separates the Frontal bone from the Parietal bones. The Frontal suture separates the two frontal bones. The Sagittal suture separates the two parietal bones. The Lambdoidal suture separates the parietals from the occipital.

10.
During pregnancy, several physiological changes occur in the alimentary system to support the needs of the growing fetus and ensure proper nourishment. These changes include:
A. Increased Oesophageal peristalsis, accompanied by gastric reflux
B. Decreased Gastrointestinal motility
C. Increased gastrointestinal motility
D. Gastric reflux is more prevalent in early pregnancy
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B. Decreased Gastrointestinal motility

Explanation: High levels of progesterone cause smooth muscle relaxation, leading to decreased peristalsis/motility. This maximizes nutrient absorption but causes constipation. It also causes relaxation of the cardiac sphincter, leading to reflux (heartburn).

11.
During normal labor, which consists of three stages:
A. The foetus, placenta, and membranes are expelled through the birth canal.
B. The Foetus is expelled through birth canal at full dilatation of cervix.
C. A series of passive movements of the foetus through the birth canal.
D. A foetus at term, in vertex presentation, is spontaneously expelled within 18 hours without complications to mother or baby.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: D. A foetus at term, in vertex presentation, is spontaneously expelled within 18 hours without complications to mother or baby.

Explanation: This is the definition of Normal Labour (Eutocia). It specifies: Term gestation, Vertex presentation, Spontaneous onset/delivery, Time limit (usually <18-24h), and healthy outcome.

12.
In false labor, several characteristics differentiate it from true labor contractions. The contractions are:
A. Painful, regular, last 40 to 60 seconds.
B. Painful, irregular, last 2 to 3 minutes.
C. Painful, regular, increase in frequency and strength.
D. Painful, regular, are accompanied by backache.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B. Painful, irregular, last 2 to 3 minutes. (Likely meant "Irregular")

Explanation: False labor (Braxton Hicks) contractions are Irregular in frequency, duration, and intensity. They do not result in cervical dilation. True labor is regular and progressive (Options A, C, D describe true labor characteristics).

13.
Moulding in the context of childbirth refers to the shaping or elongation of the fetal skull bones to allow passage through the birth canal during labor. It is identified by detecting changes in:
A. Swelling of presenting part
B. Position of sutures
C. Overlapping of skull bones
D. Position of occiput
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C. Overlapping of skull bones

Explanation: Moulding is the alteration in the shape and diameter of the fetal head during labour, caused by the overriding/overlapping of the cranial bones at the sutures.

14.
During labour, uterine muscles contract and retain the shortening. This is: -
A. Fundal dominance
B. Polarity
C. Retraction
D. Retraction ring
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C. Retraction

Explanation: Retraction is the property of uterine muscle fibers to remain shortened after a contraction. This results in the progressive thickening of the upper uterine segment and reduction of the uterine cavity size, pushing the fetus down.

15.
The sinciput, face and chin sweep perineum as nape of neck pivots lower border of symphysis pubis. This is: -
A. Extension of the head
B. External rotation of the head
C. Lateral flexion of the head
D. Internal rotation of the head

Correct Answer: A. Extension of the head

16.
During daily examination, genitalia of a newborn is checked for: -
A. Pseudo-menstruation in the female
B. Epispadias in the male
C. Pseudohermaphroditism
D. Labia Majora that does not cover the labia minora
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A. Pseudo-menstruation in the female (and general abnormalities)

Note: The question asks what is "checked for". Epispadias and hermaphroditism are abnormalities to look for. Labia majora usually covers minora in term infants (Option D implies preterm). Pseudo-menstruation is a normal physiological finding due to maternal hormone withdrawal. The question likely asks for normal findings or specific conditions to note. Option B (Epispadias) is a critical anomaly to rule out.

18.
Probable signs for 2nd stage of labour include;
A. Expulsive uterine contractions, no cervix felt
B. Trickle of blood, expulsive uterine contractions
C. Gaping of the vulva, no cervix felt
D. No cervix felt, gaping of the anus

Correct Answer: B. Trickle of blood, expulsive uterine contractions

24.
Predisposing factors to asphyxia neonatorum include: -

Correct Answer: C. Prematurity, cord prolapse, APH.

42.

Show Answer

Answer:

  1. Breast Engorgement: Painful overfilling of breasts with milk and edema (lymphatic congestion), usually 3-5 days postpartum.

43.
Pain during labor is a common and significant experience for many women. It can have various effects, both physical and emotional. Explain two (2) effects of pain during labor. (4 marks)
Show Answer

Answer:

  1. Maternal Hyperventilation: Severe pain causes rapid breathing, leading to respiratory alkalosis, which can shift the oxygen dissociation curve and reduce oxygen delivery to the fetus.
  2. Increased Catecholamines (Stress Response): Pain releases adrenaline, which can cause vasoconstriction (reducing placental blood flow) and inhibit uterine contractions (prolonging labor).

45.
In the mechanism of normal labor, after descent, the passive movements that facilitate the progress of the baby through the birth canal are many: Explain three (3) of those movement (6 marks)
Show Answer

Answer:

  1. Flexion: The fetal chin tucks onto the chest when the head meets resistance, presenting the smaller sub-occipito-bregmatic diameter.

46.
The Active Management of the Third Stage of Labor (AMTSL) is an evidence-based approach used to actively intervene in the process following the birth of the baby and delivery of the placenta. Explain three (3) of the key components (6 marks)
Show Answer

Answer:

  1. Administration of Uterotonic: 10 IU Oxytocin IM given within 1 minute of birth to contract the uterus.
  2. Controlled Cord Traction (CCT): Gentle traction on the cord with counter-pressure on the uterus (suprapubic) to deliver the placenta.
  3. Uterine Massage: Massaging the fundus immediately after placental delivery to ensure it is firm and contracted.

Section 3: Long Answer Question (20 Marks)

48.
Madam sue has been admitted in the labour ward in advanced stage of labour. Under your care she has delivered normally but the baby is small for gestational age. Describe the characteristics of a small for gestational age and manage the baby for the first 48 hours (20 marks)
Show Answer

Answer:

Characteristics of Small for Gestational Age (SGA) Baby:

  • Weight: Birth weight below the 10th percentile for gestational age.
  • Appearance: "Old man" look; loose, dry, peeling skin (decreased subcutaneous fat).
  • Head: Appears large in proportion to the body.
  • Abdomen: Scaphoid (sunken) abdomen.
  • Muscles: Wasted muscle mass (thin limbs).
  • Cord: Thin umbilical cord (Wharton's jelly reduced).
  • Behavior: Often alert and hungry (strong suck) but may tire easily.
  • Sole creases: Present (unlike preterm).

Management for the First 48 Hours:

1. Thermoregulation (Warmth):

  • Dry immediately to prevent heat loss.
  • Kangaroo Mother Care (Skin-to-Skin): Continuous contact to maintain temperature.
  • Keep room warm (25-28°C). Dress in warm clothes/hat.
  • Delay bathing until stable.

2. Prevention of Hypoglycemia (Feeding):

  • Early Initiation: Start breastfeeding within 1 hour.
  • Frequent Feeds: Every 2-3 hours (at least 8-12 times/day) due to low glycogen stores.
  • Monitor blood glucose: Keep > 2.6 mmol/L.
  • Cup/Spoon feed expressed milk if sucking is weak.

3. Infection Prevention:

  • Strict hand hygiene.
  • Clean cord care (Chlorhexidine/Dry).
  • Eye prophylaxis (Tetracycline).

4. Monitoring:

  • Vital signs (Temp, HR, RR).
  • Color (Watch for jaundice/cyanosis).
  • Weight monitoring.

5. Medication:

  • Vitamin K IM to prevent hemorrhagic disease.
  • Immunizations (BCG/Polio) if weight allows (>2kg usually, or per protocol).
