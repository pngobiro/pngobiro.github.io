Module 1: Introduction and Importance of Histopathology

🎯 Learning Objectives

  • Define histology, histopathology, and pathology.
  • Explain the importance of histology in research, teaching, and experimental work.
  • Describe the importance of histopathology in understanding disease and diagnosis.

Definitions and Importance of Histology and Histopathology

📚 Histology is the microscopic study of the normal tissues of the body.

📚 Histopathology is the microscopic study of the tissues of the body affected by disease.

📚 Pathology is the study of disease.

Importance of Histology

Histology plays a crucial role in various fields:

  1. As a method of examination, histology is used in research work for the discovery of new facts.
  2. It is used extensively in teaching centers.
  3. It shows the functions of the tissue cells by providing information about the minute organization of cells and tissues to supplement the sciences of biochemistry and physiology.
  4. Histology is used in much of experimental work which includes; testing of new drugs, cosmetic and food additives before being declared fit for human consumption, e.g., food preservation to assess changes in stored meat.
  5. Histologic techniques are also essential in immunopathology.

Importance of Histopathology

Histopathology is vital for several reasons:

  1. Knowledge of the cellular structures of different organs of the body thus improving understanding of their functions.
  2. It helps to understand the changes due to inflammation.
  3. Helps in diagnosis of nature of disease:
    • Diagnosis of benign and malignant tumors (including classification, grading and staging of cancers.
    • Diagnosis of parasitic diseases affecting tissues, e.g., Trichinella spiralis and S. mansoni.
    • Diagnosis of fungal infections, especially deep fungi, e.g., actinomyces and Cryptococci.
    • Diagnosis of viral infections, e.g., herpes and negri bodies.
    • Changes due to bacterial infection in tissue, e.g., leprosy and TB, Spirocheates e.g., T. pallidum.

Types of Material Obtained and Basic Histological Techniques

Advantages of Histopathology

  1. Microscopic examination usually is much less demanding.
  2. Ability to evaluate architecture.
  3. Ability to cut additional sections for special stains.

Disadvantages of Histopathology

  1. Time required to create sections.
  2. Identification of certain types of cells - small cell carcinoma vs lymphoma.

Types of Material Obtained in the Laboratory

The human tissue comes from the surgery and autopsy room. In surgery, two types of tissue are obtained:

  • Biopsy: a small piece of lesions or tumor which is sent for diagnosis before final removal of the lesion or the tumor (incisional biopsy).
  • Excisional biopsy: the whole tumor or lesion that is sent to the lab for examination and diagnosis.
  • Autopsy: tissues from autopsy are sent for the study of disease and its course, for the advancement of medicine.

Basic Histological Techniques/Principles

Several fundamental techniques are employed in histological studies:

  1. Tissue preservation (fixation)
  2. Tissue processing
  3. Tissue embedding
  4. Tissue sectioning
  5. Methods for visualizing tissues for light microscopy:
    • Staining (H & E method)
    • Histochemistry and immunohistochemistry

💡 Pro Tip

Understanding the basic histological techniques is crucial for accurate interpretation of tissue samples.

✍️ Practice Exercise

1. Differentiate between histology and histopathology.

2. List three advantages and two disadvantages of histopathology.

View Solution

1. Histology is the study of normal tissues, while histopathology is the study of diseased tissues.

2. Advantages: less demanding examination, evaluation of architecture, ability to cut additional sections. Disadvantages: time-consuming, difficulty in identifying certain cell types.

📚 Key Takeaways

  • Histology is the study of normal tissues, while histopathology focuses on diseased tissues.
  • Histology is essential in research, teaching, and experimental work.
  • Histopathology plays a crucial role in disease diagnosis and understanding.
