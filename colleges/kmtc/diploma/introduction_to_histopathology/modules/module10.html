Module 11: Post Chroming and Removal of Fixation Pigments

🎯 Learning Objectives

  • Describe the purpose and principle of post-chroming and post-mordanting.
  • Explain how to remove formalin pigments using saturated absolute alcohol with picric acid or 70% alcohol with ammonium hydroxide.
  • Describe the procedure for removing mercury pigments using Gram or Lugol's iodine and sodium thiosulfate.
  • Explain how to remove melanin pigments using potassium permanganate and oxalic acid.

Post Chroming and Post Mordanting

Are methods designed to facilitate the subsequent preservation and staining of a particular tissue component and entail the treatment of tissue blocks (after fixation) or sections with a chrome salt. This method is often made use of as an aid to the demonstration of myelin and mitochondria, e.g. WEIGERT was the originator of the haematoxylin method for myelin using chrome-mordanted material.

The principle of the method is the reduction of the chrome salt to chromium dioxide by the lipids in the myelin. The chromium dioxide acts as a mordant and forms a lake with the subsequent haematoxylin stain. The chromium, myelin complex being largely insoluble in processing reagents.

Removal of Fixation Pigments

Formalin Pigments

These can be removed by immersing the sections in saturated absolute alcohol with picric acid for 10 minutes to three hours. Then wash sections well with water. Also, a solution of 70% alcohol containing 3 mL of ammonium hydroxide for 30 minutes to 3 hours. After treatment wash sections well in 1% acetic acid.

Mercury Pigments

These can be removed by immersing the sections in Gram or Lugol's Iodine for 10 minutes, and then place the section in a 5% solution of Sodium thiosulfate for 3 minutes. Wash slides well in running water for 10 minutes.

Melanin Pigments

This is removed by placing the section in a solution of Potassium permanganate for approximately 20 minutes, followed by a solution of Oxalic acid to remove the excess of Potassium permanganate.

💡 Pro Tip

Proper removal of fixation pigments is essential for accurate staining and interpretation of histological sections.

✍️ Practice Exercise

1. What is the purpose of post-chroming?

2. Describe the procedure for removing formalin pigments.

3. How are mercury pigments removed from tissue sections?

View Solution

1. To facilitate the subsequent preservation and staining of particular tissue components, such as myelin and mitochondria.

2. Immerse sections in saturated absolute alcohol with picric acid for 10 minutes to 3 hours, then wash well with water. Alternatively, use 70% alcohol with ammonium hydroxide for 30 minutes to 3 hours, then wash in 1% acetic acid.

3. Immerse sections in Gram or Lugol's iodine for 10 minutes, then in 5% sodium thiosulfate for 3 minutes, and wash well in running water for 10 minutes.

📚 Key Takeaways

  • Post-chroming and post-mordanting are used to enhance the preservation and staining of specific tissue components.
  • Fixation pigments, such as formalin, mercury, and melanin pigments, can interfere with staining and must be removed.
  • Specific procedures are used to remove each type of fixation pigment, involving different reagents and incubation times.
