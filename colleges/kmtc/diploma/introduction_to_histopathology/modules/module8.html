Module 8: Classification and Types of Fixatives

🎯 Learning Objectives

  • Classify fixatives as simple or compound.
  • Further classify fixatives based on their action on cell and tissue constituents (micro-anatomical and cytological).
  • Describe various simple fixatives, including formaldehyde, mercuric chloride, osmium tetroxide, picric acid, chromic acid, potassium dichromate, glacial acetic acid, trichloroacetic acid, ethyl alcohol, glutaraldehyde and acetone.
  • Understand the composition and uses of each simple fixative.

Classification of Fixatives

Fixatives can be classified as:

  1. Simple: Contain one chemical substance.
  2. Compound: Mixture of two or more simple fixatives.

Fixatives can be further classified according to their action upon cell and tissue constituents:

  • Micro-anatomical fixatives: Preserve the tissue to allow the general microscopical study of the tissue and allow various layers of the tissues and cells to retain their former relationships with each other.
  • Cytological fixatives: Used for their specific action on the cell structures. They can be further subdivided into:
    • Nuclear: This has effects on the nucleus and its contents.
    • Cytoplasmic: This has effects on the cytoplasm and its contents.

Simple Fixatives

Contain one chemical substance in solution or vapor form.

They include:

  • Formaldehyde
  • Mercuric chloride
  • Osmium tetroxide
  • Picric acid
  • Chromic acid
  • Potassium dichromate
  • Glacial acetic acid
  • Trichloroacetic acid
  • Ethyl alcohol
  • Glutaraldehyde
  • Acetone

💡 Pro Tip

Understanding the classification and types of fixatives is essential for selecting the appropriate fixative for a specific application.

✍️ Practice Exercise

1. What is the difference between a simple and a compound fixative?

2. Explain the difference between micro-anatomical and cytological fixatives.

3. List five simple fixatives.

View Solution

1. A simple fixative contains one chemical substance, while a compound fixative contains a mixture of two or more simple fixatives.

2. Micro-anatomical fixatives preserve the general tissue structure, while cytological fixatives preserve specific cell structures.

3. Formaldehyde, mercuric chloride, osmium tetroxide, picric acid, chromic acid, potassium dichromate, glacial acetic acid, trichloroacetic acid, ethyl alcohol, glutaraldehyde and acetone.

📚 Key Takeaways

  • Fixatives are classified as simple or compound based on their composition.
  • Fixatives are further classified as micro-anatomical or cytological based on their action.
  • Simple fixatives contain one chemical substance and include a variety of compounds with different properties and uses.
