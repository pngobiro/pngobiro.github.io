Barrier Nursing Requirements (Part 2)

üéØ Learning Objectives

  • Describe the specifications for caps and masks used in barrier nursing.
  • Explain the features of appropriate clean and heavy duty gloves and boots for barrier nursing.
  • Describe the ideal type of eye wear required during barrier nursing and the contents of an observation tray.

üìö Specification for Caps and Masks

CAPS

  1. Cotton material that can laundered.
  2. Be disposable.

MASKS

  1. 3–ú HEPA or N series.

üìö Specification for Gloves and Boots

CLEAN GLOVES

  1. Must reach well above the wrist, preferably 10 to 15cm (4" to 6") long measure from the wrist up along the arm.
  2. Should be tested for pin holes.
  3. May be powdered or non powdered.

HEAVY DUTY GLOVES

  1. Should be made from neoprene or other thick rubber material.
  2. Must reach well above the wrist, preferably about 30cm (12") measuring from wrist up along the arm.

BOOTS

  1. Should be 30cm (12") high and have textured soles.

üìö Specification for Eye Wear and Observation Tray

PROTECTIVE EYE WEAR (GOGGLES)

  1. Should be non fogging goggles that are vented at the sides.
  2. Local clear glasses can also be used.
  3. Should have ties extending from ear holders that can be tied around the back of the head so glasses will not fall off when health care worker leans over the patient.

OBSERVATION TRAY

The basic tray for barrier nursing care includes the following:

  • Kidney dish
  • Cotton swabs
  • Gauze swabs
  • Surgical masks
  • Gloves
  • Alcohol disinfectant

üìö Key Takeaways

  • Caps should be made from cotton or be disposable, masks should be 3M HEPA or N series.
  • Clean gloves should reach above the wrist, heavy duty gloves should be thicker and reach further up the arm. Boots should be textured and reach up the leg.
  • Protective eye wear should be non fogging and well secured and an observation tray should have basic equipment.
