Barrier Nursing Essentials

üéØ Learning Objectives

  • Understand the procedures for equipment and linen management in barrier nursing.
  • Describe the safe handling of sharps during barrier nursing.
  • Explain procedures for environmental control, food, and visitor guidelines in barrier nursing.

üìö Equipment and Linen

4. Equipments and linen

  • Decontaminate and clean equipment after use.
  • Disinfect used linen before laundering.
  • Use protective gloves while handling used equipment and linen.

üìö Safe Handling of Used Sharps

5. Safe handling of used sharps.

  • Do not disassemble needles from syringes or other device; discard as a single unit.
  • Do not recap needles.
  • Do not carry used sharps by hand or pass to another person
  • Discard sharps immediately after use into a sharp safety container
  • Ensure sharps containers are placed at point of use.
  • Ensure sharps containers are securely closed when three quarters full and stored in designated areas ready for transportation to final disposal sites.

üìö Environment, Food, and Visitors

6. Environment

  • Keep environment clean and free from dust.
  • Disinfect spills of body fluid with recommended disinfectant before cleaning.
  • Use colour coded leak proof waste containers and liners to segregate generated wastes.

7. Food

  • The patient should be provided with appropriate and properly cooked food to ensure proper nutrition and quick recovery.

8. Visitors

  • Number of visitors to isolated patients should be limited to the most significant person.
  • Use of protective clothing should be ensured during the visit.
  • Hand washing before and after visiting the isolated patient emphasized.

  • Used equipment and linen should be disinfected before laundering or reuse.
