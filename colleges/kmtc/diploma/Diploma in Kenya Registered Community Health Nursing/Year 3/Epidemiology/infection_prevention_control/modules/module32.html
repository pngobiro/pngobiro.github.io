Barrier Nursing Practices

üéØ Learning Objectives

  • Explain the importance of hand washing in barrier nursing.
  • Describe key steps in patient direct care during barrier nursing.
  • Identify appropriate protective clothing for barrier nursing.

üìö Hand Washing

1. Hand washing

  • Hand washing has been identified as the single most effective in reduction of micro-organisms. It is documented that effective hand washing eliminates 95% of transient micro-organisms
  • Hands should be washed thoroughly with soap and running water or alcohol hand rub before and after contact with patient to prevent transfer of micro-organism from one patient to another or self.
  • Hand washing should be done after contact with body fluids in order to prevent transmission of infectious diseases.
  • Hands should be washed when in contact with any dirt plus before and after gloving

üìö Patient Direct Care

2. Patient direct care.

  • Detailed assessment should underpin the principles of standard of care.
  • Assessment of the risk of transmitting infection should form part of nursing activity.
  • Limit patient's movement to prevent spread of infection.
  • Use of person toiletries; a self contained room is most ideal, where such facilities are not available, a bedpan can serve this purpose and should be individualized, ensure proper disinfection is done after use.
  • Emphasis hand washing after attending toilets and before taking meals.
  • Disposable gloves, goggles and aprons should be used when in contact with blood, body fluids and secretions.
  • Staff with upper respiratory symptoms should be excluded from direct contact with the patients.

üìö Protective Clothing

3. Protective clothing

  • Use of protective clothing such as gloves, masks, goggles, apron, boots, and caps should be worn by all doctors, nurses and other health care workers who provide care to patients having barrier nursing.
  • All supportive staff that cleans the barrier area, handles contaminated supplies and equipment, or collects and disposes off waste from patients plus Laboratory staff who handle specimen, support staff who clean and disinfect the laboratory equipment, should also wear protective clothing.
  • Protective clothes prevent micro-organisms transmission from patients having barrier nursing in the ward and from staff providing care.

üìö Key Takeaways

  • Hand washing is the single most effective method in reducing the transmission of micro-organisms in barrier nursing.
  • Patients should have individualized personal items and their movement should be limited.
  • Protective clothing prevents the transmission of micro-organisms from patients to healthcare workers.
