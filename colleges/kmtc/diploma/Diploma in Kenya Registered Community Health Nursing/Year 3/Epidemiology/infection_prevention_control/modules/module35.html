üìö Community Education

Most outbreaks of diseases occur in the community therefore it is important to educate the community on;

  • Standard case definition and detection of common infectious diseases
  • Action to be taken once a case is identified including reporting
  • Mode of transmission, prevention and treatment
  • General hygiene and hand washing
  • Maintenance of social distance during out breaks
  • Limiting number of contacts with the infected

üìö Standard Layout of Barrier Area or Room

Basic standards for barrier nursing layout must have a designated area or a cubicle in a ward. The following should be observed in the layout for barrier nursing:

  1. There must be a demarcated area in a ward with curtains or screens or a cubicle with a notice indicating the barrier area with words ‚ÄúKEEP OFF‚Äù or BARRIER NURSING AREA.
  2. There should be reasonable space in the area and round the bed to facilitate barrier nursing and flow of air.
  3. Infection preventions practices should observed strictly and the area should have color coded bins and liners for segregation of generated wastes
  4. Where applicable the area or room should have running tap water nearby, and if not available then running water is improvised for hand washing as this is A MUST
