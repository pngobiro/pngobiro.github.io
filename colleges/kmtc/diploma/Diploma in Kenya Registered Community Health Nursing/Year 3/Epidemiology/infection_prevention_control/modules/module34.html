Health Education in Barrier Nursing

üéØ Learning Objectives

  • Understand the importance of training and education of health care staff in infection control
  • Describe the key aspects of patient and family education in barrier nursing.
  • Explain the impact of health education on barrier nursing and patient outcomes

üìö Health Education - Staff

HEALTH EDUCATION

Staff

  • The adoption and training of a routine standards of infection control done on regular basis e training to ensure that all health care workers understand the procedures and what standard are expected.
  • Written policies should be developed to clarify the local arrangement for minimizing identified hazards.
  • The guideline should be used to form the basis for staff education and training and are subject of regular monitoring and evaluation.

üìö Health Education - Patient and Family

Patient and Family

Separation from others affects patients in different ways, the combination of being moved away from others and fear of being infectious can be very stressing for the patients. Therefore;

  • Explain to the patient the reason for barrier care, evidence indicate that this reduces level of anxiety and depression.
  • Stop the precaution once symptoms have disappeared or in some infections after a short cause of appropriate treatment.

The family member will usually be anxious when the patient is not being taken care of with others; it is important that they are explained the reason for barrier care:

  • Explain about the risk of infection and the importance of protective clothing's while attending the patient.
  • In case of large out breaks a liaison person is identified to provide information between health facility staff, patient and family members.
  • Explain the reason for limiting number of visitors.
  • Importance of hand washing before and after visiting the patient.

The involvement of both patient and family allow the nurse to in-cooperate the knowledge, values, beliefs and cultural background into planning for barrier care. The patient and family should be fully informed and involved from the stage of planning to evaluation of barrier nursing in order to optimize support and cooperation.

  • Regular training of healthcare staff improves infection control.
  • Written policies guide actions in barrier nursing.
  • Involving and informing patients and families is important to obtain cooperation and improve patient outcomes.
