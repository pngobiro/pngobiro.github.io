üè• Overview

Preventive healthcare (alternately preventive medicine or prophylaxis) consists of measures taken for disease prevention, as opposed to disease treatment.

Just as health encompasses a variety of physical and mental states, so do disease and disability, which are affected by environmental factors, genetic predisposition, disease agents, and lifestyle choices.

The Dynamic Process

Health, disease, and disability are dynamic processes which begin before individuals realize they are affected. Disease prevention relies on anticipatory actions.

02

üìä Levels of Disease Prevention

Preventive healthcare strategies are typically described as taking place at three main levels: Primary, Secondary, and Tertiary. However, a fourth level, Primordial prevention, is also recognized.

Level Definition Examples
Primordial Prevention of risk factors themselves, beginning with change in social and environmental conditions. Improving socioeconomic status, reducing air pollution, discouraging smoking habits in youth.
Primary Methods to avoid occurrence of disease either through eliminating disease agents or increasing resistance. Immunization, healthy diet, exercise, condoms, sanitation.
Secondary Methods to detect and address an existing disease prior to the appearance of symptoms (Early diagnosis & prompt treatment). Cancer screenings (Pap smear, Mammography), hypertension checks, HIV testing.
Tertiary Methods to reduce negative impact of symptomatic disease, such as disability or death (Rehabilitation). Physical therapy after stroke, insulin for diabetes management, support groups.

Deep Dive: Primary Prevention

Primary prevention consists of two main components:

  • Health Promotion: Non-clinical life choices (e.g., eating nutritious meals, exercising) that create a sense of overall well-being. These do not target a specific disease but promote general health.
  • Specific Protection: Targets a type or group of diseases (e.g., immunizations, using condoms to prevent STIs).

Deep Dive: Secondary Prevention

Secondary prevention deals with latent diseases and attempts to prevent an asymptomatic disease from progressing to symptomatic disease.

‚ö°

Goal: Disability Limitation

Secondary prevention consists of "early diagnosis and prompt treatment" to contain the disease and prevent its spread, and "disability limitation" to prevent potential future complications.

Deep Dive: Tertiary Prevention

Unlike secondary prevention, which aims to prevent disability, the objective of tertiary prevention is to maximize the remaining capabilities and functions of an already disabled patient.

üéØ Prevention Strategies

The strategy provides evidence-based recommendations fundamental to improving health through active engagement. It identifies four broad strategic directions:

  1. 1
    Building Healthy and Safe Community Environments: Prevention starts in communities and at home. (e.g., clean air, safe roads, parks).
  2. 2
    Expanding Quality Preventive Services: Better access to clinical and community prevention efforts (e.g., immunizations, cancer screenings).
  3. 3
    Empowering People to Make Healthy Choices: Providing actionable information and resources to make healthy options the easy choice.
  4. 4
    Eliminating Health Disparities: Addressing inequities to improve quality of life for all population groups.
