üëÅÔ∏è Definition & Purpose

Public health surveillance is the ongoing systematic collection, analysis, interpretation, and dissemination of health data to help guide public health decision making and action.

üí°

Information for Action

Surveillance is sometimes called "information for action." Its purpose is to portray ongoing patterns of disease occurrence and potential so that investigation, control, and prevention measures can be applied efficiently.

Surveillance is equivalent to monitoring the pulse of the community.

02

üîÑ The Surveillance Cycle

Effective surveillance involves a continuous cycle of activities, from data collection to public health action.

üîç Click to zoom

Fig. 4.1: The Surveillance Cycle

Core Activities

  • 1
    Data Collection: Gathering reports from health facilities, laboratories, and communities.
  • 2
    Analysis: Organizing data by time, place, and person to identify trends.
  • 3
    Interpretation: Determining the significance of the findings (e.g., Is this an outbreak?).
  • 4
    Dissemination: Sharing findings with decision-makers and feedback to data providers.
  • 5
    Action: Implementing control measures, planning programs, or policy changes.

Uses of Surveillance Data

  • Estimate Importance: Identify health needs and rank priorities.
  • Monitor Trends: Detect changes in disease frequency or distribution.
  • Evaluate Programs: Assess the effectiveness of control measures (e.g., vaccination coverage).
  • Detect Outbreaks: Identify clusters of disease for immediate investigation.
  • Research: Generate hypotheses for analytical studies.
