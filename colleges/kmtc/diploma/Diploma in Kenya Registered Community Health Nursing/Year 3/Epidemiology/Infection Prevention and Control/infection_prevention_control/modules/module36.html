Barrier Nursing Requirements (Part 1)

  • List the quantity of personal protective equipment required for one patient in barrier nursing.
  • Describe the specifications for protective gowns.
  • Explain the specifications for aprons to be worn during barrier nursing.

üìö Requirement for one patient

(1) Requirement for one patient

ITEM QUANTITY
Gowns 2
Apron 2
Caps 2
Masks disposable
Boot or Over boots 2 pairs
Examination gloves 2 pairs
Protective eye wear 2

üìö Specification for protective wear

(2) Specification for protective wear required for barrier nursing

GOWNS

  1. Made from cotton cloth, cotton blend, or disposable fabric. These requirements are the same for both disposable and reusable gowns.
  2. Open at the back with ties at the neck, waist and middle of the back.
  3. Ribbed or elasticized cuffs. If elasticized or ribbed cuff is not available then attach thumb hooks at the end of the sleeve which can then be covered with gloves.
  4. Be long enough to reach the knees.

üìö Specification for Aprons

APRON

  1. Should be of rubber or plastic with hooks or ties around the neck and with ties at the back.
  2. Made from disposable plastic or heavy plastic which can be disinfected for reuse.
  3. Able to fit over the gown.

üìö Key Takeaways

  • Specific quantities of PPE are required for one patient in barrier nursing.
  • Gowns should be made from specific materials, open at the back, and have appropriate cuffs.
  • Aprons must be waterproof and fit over the gown.
