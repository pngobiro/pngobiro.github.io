  • Compare the advantages and disadvantages of hexachlorophene and iodine.

üìö Hexachlorophene

Hexachlorophene (Phisohex)

Antimicrobial Spectrum Advantages Disadvantages Comments
Poor effectiveness against most micro-organisms. Has a good, persistent effect with repeated use which remains effective for at least six hours after being applied. Potentially toxic to the nervous system. Occasional use is not effective in reducing the number of micro-organisms on the hands. Contraindicated for routine use on irritated or broken skin or mucous membranes. Not recommended for use in surgical hand scrub or skin preparation due to its limited capacity to kill micro-organisms. If use of hexachlorophene is discontinued after long-term use, rebound increase of growth of bacteria will occur (bacterial whose growth was being inhibited by its use will grow and multiply causing large scale contamination).

üìö Iodine

Iodine, including tincture of iodine (iodine and alcohol)

Antimicrobial Spectrum Advantages Disadvantages Comments
Effective against a broad range of micro-organisms. Fast acting. Can cause irritation. Effectiveness is markedly reduced by blood or other organic material. Too irritating for routine use in skin surgical hand scrub or for use on mucous membranes. Because of the potential to cause skin irritation, when used for preparation, iodine must be allowed to dry and then remove from the skin with alcohol.

Why is Hexachlorophene contraindicated for routine use and why is iodine only used for skin preparation in some circumstances?

View Solution

Hexachlorophene is toxic and can lead to rebound growth of bacteria, iodine is irritant to skin.

üìö Key Takeaways

  • Hexachlorophene is not recommended for routine use due to its toxicity and potential for rebound bacterial growth.
  • Iodine is fast-acting but can cause skin irritation and is inactivated by organic material.
  • The choice of antiseptic depends on its safety profile and the intended use.
