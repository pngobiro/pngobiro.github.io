Transmission-based Precautions

These are used for protection against infections which spread by contact, droplets or the airborne route. They are always used in addition to Standard Precautions.

üìö Contact Precautions

1. Contact Precautions - Use for protection against infections which spread by contact. In addition to Standard Precautions:

  1. Use non-sterile, clean, disposable gloves, gown, apron (only if gown is not impermeable)
  2. Use disposable or dedicated reusable equipment (which must be cleaned and disinfected before use on other patients)
  3. Limit patient contact with non-infected persons
  4. Place patient in a single room or cohort with similar patients

üìö Droplet and Airborne Precautions

2. Droplet Precautions Used for protection against respiratory pathogens transmitted by large droplets. In addition to Standard Precautions:

  1. Use a surgical/medical mask
  2. Maintain a distance ‚â• 1 meter between infectious patient and others.
  3. Place patient in a single room or cohort with similar patients.
  4. Limit patient movement.

3. Airborne Precautions - Use for protection against inhalation of tiny infectious droplet nuclei. In addition to Standard Precautions:

  1. Use particulate respirator /N 95 mask
  2. Place the patient in adequately ventilated room (‚â• 12 air changes per hour)
  3. Limit patient movement
  4. Use airborne precautions during performance of any aerosol-generating procedures associated with risk pathogen transmission like bone cutting, dental procedures.

‚úçÔ∏è Practice Exercise

When are contact precautions used? What is the difference between droplet and airborne precautions?

View Solution

Contact precautions are used for infections spread by direct contact, droplet precautions are for infections spread by large droplets, and airborne precautions are for tiny airborne particles.

üìö Key Takeaways

  • Transmission-based precautions are used in addition to standard precautions to stop the spread of infection.
  • Contact precautions are used to prevent infections spread through direct or indirect contact.
  • Droplet precautions are used for large droplets, while airborne precautions are for tiny particles that can be inhaled.
