Storage of Autoclaved Items

üéØ Learning Objectives

  • Explain how to store wrapped autoclaved items correctly.
  • Understand the importance of shelf life for sterile items.
  • Describe the proper storage for unwrapped autoclaved items.

üìö Storage of Autoclaved Items

i. Wrapped Items

Shelf life is the length of time a wrapped sterile item is considered sterile. Shelf life depends on whether or not a contaminating event occurs. Shelf life is affected by a number of factors which include:

  • Type of packaging material used
  • Cleanliness of the store
  • Humidity in the store
  • Temperature of the storage area
  • Storing the packs on open or closed shelves
  • Usage of dust proof covers

Storage time and handling of sterile packs should be kept to a minimum, since the likelihood of handling and contamination increases over time. Place sterile packs in closed cabinets in areas that are not heavily trafficked, have moderate temperature and are dry.

üìö Storage of Autoclaved Items (cont.)

ii. Unwrapped Items

Unwrapped items should be used immediately after removal from the autoclave. They can also be kept in a covered, sterile container for up to one week.

What factors affect the shelf life of wrapped sterile items? How long can unwrapped autoclaved items be stored?

View Solution

Packaging material, cleanliness of store, humidity, temperature, type of shelf, dust proof covers. Unwrapped autoclaved items can be stored for up to one week in a covered sterile container.

üìö Key Takeaways

  • Shelf life of wrapped sterile items depends on packaging, storage conditions, and handling.
  • Sterile packs should be stored in clean, dry, and enclosed areas with low traffic.
  • Unwrapped autoclaved items should be used immediately or stored in a sterile container for up to one week.
