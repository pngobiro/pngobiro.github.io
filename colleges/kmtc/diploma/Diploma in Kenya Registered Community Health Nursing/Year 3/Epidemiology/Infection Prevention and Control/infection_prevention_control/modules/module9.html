üìö Common Antiseptics and Disinfectants

You are by now aware that the use of antiseptics and disinfectants may vary from hospital to hospital.

Alcohol (60-90% ethyl or isopropyl)

Antimicrobial Spectrum Advantages Disadvantages Comments
Effective against a broad range of micro-organisms. Kills micro-organisams most rapidly. Most effective in reducing micro-organisms. Has a drying effect on skin. Cannot be used on mucous membranes. Effectiveness is only moderately reduced by blood or other organic material. Cannot be used when skin is dirty. Wash the area before applying. Must dry skin completely to be effective. The 60-70% strength is most effective because alcohol must be diluted for optimal killing of micro-organisms, it is less drying to skin, and it is less expensive.

üìö Chlorhexidine

Chlorhexidine gluconate (Hibitane, Hibiclens, Hibiscrub)/Chlorhexidine gluconate with cetrimide (Savlon)

Antimicrobial Spectrum Advantages Disadvantages Comments
Effective against a broad range of micro-organisms, remains effective for at least 6 hours after being applied. Effectiveness is not reduced by blood or other organic material. Has a good persistent effect. On rare occasions products containing chlorhexidine have been reported to cause irritation, especially when used in the genital area. Effectiveness can be reduced by hard water, hand creams and natural soaps. Recommended antiseptic for surgical hand scrub and skin preparation. Preparations without cetrimide are preferable to those with cetrimide. While products containing chlorhexidine are ideal for surgical hand scrub and skin preparation in general, they may not be the best antiseptics to use in the genital area, vagina and cervix because of the small potential for irritating these areas. Iodophors are a better choice for use in these areas. If iodophor is not available, a product containing chlorhoxidine is the best alternative.

‚úçÔ∏è Practice Exercise

List three advantages and two disadvantages of using alcohol as an antiseptic and chlorhexidine as an antiseptic.

View Solution

Alcohol Advantages: fast acting, effective, inexpensive. Disadvantages: drying, ineffective when skin dirty. Chlorhexidine Advantages: persistent action, effective in the presence of blood. Disadvantages: irritation, inactivation by soaps.

üìö Key Takeaways

  • Alcohol is a fast-acting antiseptic, but its effectiveness is reduced by organic material.
  • Chlorhexidine provides a longer-lasting antiseptic effect and is less affected by organic material.
  • The choice of antiseptic depends on the specific situation and desired effect.
