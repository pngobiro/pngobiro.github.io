üå°Ô∏è Meningitis

Meningitis is an inflammation of the meninges, the protective membranes surrounding the brain and spinal cord.

Type Description Common Causes
Septic Caused by bacteria. Neisseria meningitidis, Haemophilus influenzae, Streptococcus pneumoniae.
Aseptic Non-bacterial inflammation. Viral, or secondary to lymphoma, leukemia, brain abscess.

Pathophysiology

Infections originate via the bloodstream or by direct extension (trauma/surgery). The causative organism enters the bloodstream, crosses the blood-brain barrier, and causes inflammation in the subarachnoid space and pia mater. This leads to increased ICP and accumulation of inflammatory material in the CSF.

Clinical Manifestations

  • Headache & Fever: Often initial symptoms; fever remains high.
  • Nuchal Rigidity: Stiff neck; flexion is difficult and painful.
  • Photophobia: Extreme sensitivity to light.
  • Rash: Striking feature of N. meningitidis.
  • Mental Status: Disorientation, memory impairment, seizures.

Medical Management

  • Antibiotics: Early administration that crosses BBB (Penicillins, Cephalosporins, Vancomycin).
  • Corticosteroids: Dexamethasone to reduce inflammation.
  • Supportive: Fluid volume expanders for shock/dehydration; Phenytoin for seizures.

Nursing Management

  • Isolation: Droplet precautions until 24 hours after antibiotic initiation.
  • Monitoring: Neurologic status, vital signs, pulse oximetry, ABGs.
  • Support: Manage fever (antipyretics), protect from injury (seizures), prevent immobility complications.

üî¥ Brain Abscess

A collection of infectious material within the brain tissue. It may occur by direct invasion (trauma/surgery) or spread from nearby sites (sinuses, ears, teeth).

Clinical Manifestations

  • Headache: Usually worse in the morning; most prevailing symptom.
  • Neurologic: Vomiting, focal signs (weakness, vision loss, seizures).
  • Mental Status: Lethargy, confusion, irritability.
  • Fever: May or may not be present.

Management

Diagnosis: CT scan or MRI.

Treatment:

  • Medical: Antimicrobial therapy (Penicillin G, Chloramphenicol), Corticosteroids (for edema), Antiseizure meds.
  • Surgical: Incision or aspiration.
  • Nursing: Ongoing neurologic assessment (ICP), patient safety (falls/seizures), family support.

ü¶† Encephalitis

Acute inflammatory process of the brain tissue. Herpes Simplex Virus (HSV) is the most common cause.

Symptoms Fever, headache, confusion, behavioral abnormalities. Focal signs: seizures, dysphasia, hemiparesis.
Diagnosis Neuroimaging, EEG, CSF examination.
Treatment Acyclovir (antiviral agent) is the medication of choice for HSV.
Other Causes Fungal infections, Arthropod-borne viruses.
