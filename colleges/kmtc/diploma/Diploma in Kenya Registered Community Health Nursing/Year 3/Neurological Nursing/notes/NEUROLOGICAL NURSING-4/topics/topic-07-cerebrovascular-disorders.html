üìñ Definition & Classification

Cerebrovascular Disorders (CVA) is an umbrella term referring to any functional abnormality of the central nervous system (CNS) occurring when the normal blood supply to the brain is disrupted. Also referred to as stroke.

Category Frequency Pathology
Ischemic Stroke 85% Vascular occlusion and significant hypoperfusion of brain tissue.
Hemorrhagic Stroke 15% Extravasation of blood into brain tissue or subarachnoid space.
03

üõë Ischemic Stroke

Etiology & Categories

  1. 1
    Large Artery Thrombotic: Atherosclerotic plaques in large vessels leading to ischemia/infarction.
  2. 2
    Small Penetrating Artery Thrombosis: Affects one or more vessels; most common type.
  3. 3
    Cardiogenic Embolic: Associated with dysrhythmias (usually atrial fibrillation). Emboli travel from heart to brain (commonly left middle cerebral artery).
  4. 4
    Cryptogenic: No known cause.
  5. 5
    Other: Drugs (cocaine), coagulopathies, migraine, etc.

Pathophysiology: The Ischemic Cascade

Disruption of blood flow initiates a complex series of cellular metabolic events:

Blood flow falls < 25 mL/100 g/min → Neurons lose aerobic respiration → Switch to anaerobic respiration → Lactic acid generation → Change in pH → Insufficient energy → Cell death (Infarction).

Risk Factors

Non-Modifiable Modifiable
Advanced Age Hypertension (HTN)
Gender Cardiovascular Disease
Race High Cholesterol
Obesity & Diabetes
Smoking, Drug abuse (cocaine), Alcohol
Oral Contraceptives (high estrogen)

Clinical Manifestations

Sudden numbness/weakness (face, arm, leg), confusion, speech trouble, visual disturbances, dizziness, balance loss, sudden severe headache.

Specific Deficits

  • Visual Field: Hemianopsia (loss of half visual field), Diplopia.
  • Motor: Hemiparesis, Hemiplegia, Ataxia, Dysphagia.
  • Sensory: Paresthesia (occurs on side opposite lesion).
  • Verbal: Expressive aphasia, Receptive aphasia.
  • Cognitive: Memory loss, decreased attention span, poor abstract reasoning.
  • Emotional: Loss of self-control, emotional lability, depression, withdrawal.

Medical Management

Diagnosis: CT scan (ischemic vs hemorrhagic), Cerebral angiography, Transcranial Doppler.

Thrombolytic Therapy (t-PA)

Recombinant t-PA binds to fibrin and converts plasminogen to plasmin, stimulating fibrinolysis.

t-PA Administration Protocols

  • Dose: 0.9 mg/kg (Maximum dose: 90 mg).
  • Monitoring: ICU admission, continuous cardiac monitoring.
  • Vital Signs: q15min for 2 hrs → q30min for 6 hrs → q1h for 16 hrs.

Non-t-PA Therapy

  • Anticoagulants: Heparin / Low-molecular-weight heparin.
  • Hemodynamics: Maintain cerebral perfusion. Reduce ICP (Mannitol).
  • BP Control: Maintain Systolic < 180 mmHg, Diastolic < 100 mmHg.
  • Positioning: Elevate HOB to promote venous drainage.
04

ü©∏ Hemorrhagic Stroke

Etiology

Caused by bleeding into brain tissue, ventricles, or subarachnoid space.

  • Primary Intracerebral Hemorrhage (80%): Spontaneous rupture of small vessels (uncontrolled HTN).
  • Secondary: AVMs, Intracranial Aneurysms, Medications (anticoagulants).

Intracranial Aneurysm

Dilation of cerebral artery wall due to weakness (atherosclerosis, congenital defect, HTN, trauma).

Clinical Manifestations

  • Sudden, unusually severe headache.
  • Loss of consciousness.
  • Nuchal rigidity (pain/rigidity of neck/spine).
  • Visual disturbances (if aneurysm adjacent to oculomotor nerve).
  • Tinnitus, dizziness, hemiparesis.

Complications

  • Rebleeding
  • Cerebral Vasospasm (ischemia)
  • Acute Hydrocephalus
  • Seizures

Management

Goal: Allow brain recovery, prevent rebleeding, treat complications.

  • Bed rest with sedation (prevent agitation).
  • Analgesics (Codeine, Acetaminophen) for pain.
  • Elastic compression stockings (DVT prevention).
  • Surgical: Isolate aneurysm from circulation or strengthen arterial wall.
üõë

Aneurysm Precautions

  • Immediate & Absolute Bed Rest: Quiet, non-stressful environment.
  • Positioning: HOB elevated 15-30 degrees (venous drainage).
  • No Exertion: Nurse administers all personal care (feeding, bathing).
  • Activity: Avoid any activity that raises BP.
  • DVT Prophylaxis: Thigh-high elastic compression stockings.
05

üìù Nursing Process

Assessment

Focus on airway patency, respiratory function, and cardiovascular status. Complete neurologic exam (LOC, pupils, motor/sensory, cranial nerves).

Nursing Diagnoses

  • Ineffective cerebral tissue perfusion related to bleeding/edema.
  • Impaired physical mobility related to hemiparesis.
  • Acute pain (headache or painful shoulder).
  • Self-care deficits (hygiene, toileting, grooming, feeding).
  • Disturbed sensory perception (aneurysm precautions).
  • Anxiety related to illness/restrictions.
