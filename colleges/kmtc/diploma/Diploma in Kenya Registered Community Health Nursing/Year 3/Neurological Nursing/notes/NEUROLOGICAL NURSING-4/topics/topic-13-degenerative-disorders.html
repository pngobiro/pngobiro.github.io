üß¨ Overview

Neurologic disorders of the central and peripheral nervous system that are degenerative in nature include:

  • Parkinson's disease
  • Huntington's disease
  • Alzheimer's disease
  • Amyotrophic lateral sclerosis (ALS)
  • Muscular dystrophies
  • Degenerative disc disease
02

üë¥ Parkinson's Disease

Parkinson's disease is a slowly progressing neurologic movement disorder that eventually leads to disability. The degenerative or idiopathic form is the most common.

‚ÑπÔ∏è

Etiology

Research suggests several causative factors, including genetics, atherosclerosis, excessive accumulation of oxygen free radicals, viral infections, head trauma, chronic antipsychotic medication use, and some environmental exposures.

Symptoms usually first appear in the fifth decade of life, though cases have been diagnosed as early as 30.

Clinical Manifestations

Onset is gradual and progression is slow. The three cardinal signs are:

  1. 1
    Tremor
  2. 2
    Rigidity (muscle stiffness)
  3. 3
    Bradykinesia (abnormally slow movements)

Other features include hypokinesia, gait disturbances, and postural instability. Psychiatric changes (depression, dementia, sleep disturbances, hallucinations) are also common.

Medical Management

Treatment aims to control symptoms and maintain independence.

Medication Class Examples Action/Notes
Levodopa Levodopa (Dopar), Carbidopa-Levodopa (Sinemet) Mainstay of treatment. Converted to dopamine in the basal ganglia. Carbidopa prevents peripheral breakdown.
Anticholinergics Trihexyphenidyl, Benztropine mesylate Control tremor and rigidity by counteracting acetylcholine.
Dopamine Agonists Bromocriptine, Pergolide Useful in postponing Levodopa therapy or when effectiveness wanes.
Antidepressants Tricyclic antidepressants Alleviate depression.

Surgical Management

Stereotactic surgery and transplantation approaches are considered when medication is limited.

Nursing Process

Assessment: Focus on ADLs and functional abilities. Observe for degree of disability and responses to medication (on-off phenomenon).

Nursing Diagnoses

  • Impaired Physical Mobility: Related to rigidity and bradykinesia.
  • Self-Care Deficits: Feeding, dressing, hygiene, toileting.
  • Constipation: Related to medication and reduced activity.
  • Imbalanced Nutrition: Related to tremor, slow eating, difficulty chewing/swallowing.

üß¨ Huntington's Disease

A chronic, progressive, hereditary disease resulting in progressive involuntary choreiform movement and dementia.

Pathology

Involves premature death of cells in the striatum (caudate and putamen) of the basal ganglia (control of movement) and cells in the cortex (thinking, memory) and cerebellum.

Clinical Manifestations

  • Chorea: Abnormal involuntary movements. Constant writhing, twisting, uncontrollable movements involving the entire body as disease progresses.
  • Intellectual Decline: Dementia.
  • Emotional Disturbance.

Management is palliative; no treatment halts the underlying process.

04

üß† Alzheimer's Disease

Chronic, progressive, degenerative brain disorder accompanied by profound effects on memory, cognition, and self-care ability.

üìä

Prevalence

Affects ~10% of the population over 65, reaching 47% by age 85. Management primarily consists of supportive care.
