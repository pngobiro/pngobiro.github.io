Heat-Related Emergencies

Hyperthermia

  • Acute Medical Emergency
  • Failure of heat regulating mechanisms
  • Elderly and young at risk
  • Exceptional heat exhaustion

Treatment

Lower temp as quickly as possible (102 and lower) How can this be done?

  • Mortality =duration of hyperthermia
  • Give O2, Start large bore IV

Heat Exhaustion Stroke

Heat Exhaustion

  • Caused by dehydration
  • Stems from heavy perspiration
  • Poor electrolyte consumption

Signs/Symptoms

  • Normal mental status
  • Flu like
  • Headache
  • Weakness
  • N/V
  • Orthostatic hypotension
  • Tachycardia

Heat Exhaustion

Treatment

Outside hospital

  • Stop activity
  • Move to cool place
  • Cold packs
  • Remove constrictive clothing
  • Re-hydrate (water, sports drinks)
  • If remains call 911

In hospital

  • IV 0.9% saline
  • Frequent vitals
  • Draw serum electrolyte level
