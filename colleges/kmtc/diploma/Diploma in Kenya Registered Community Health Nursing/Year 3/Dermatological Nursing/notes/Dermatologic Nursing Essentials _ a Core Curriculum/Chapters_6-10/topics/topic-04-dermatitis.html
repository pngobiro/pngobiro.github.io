01

Overview & Types

Dermatitis and eczema describe a particular type of inflammatory response in the skin characterized by pruritus, lesions with indistinct borders, and epidermal changes. The process can be acute, subacute, or chronic.

Types of Dermatitis (Eczema)

Atopic Dermatitis

Chronic, itchy, inflamed skin associated with the "atopic triad" (asthma, allergies, eczema).

Contact Dermatitis

Allergic: Immune reaction (e.g., poison ivy).
Irritant: Direct damage (e.g., chemicals).

Seborrheic Dermatitis

Yellowish, oily, scaly patches on scalp (cradle cap) and face.

Nummular Eczema

Coin-shaped patches, often on extremities. Crusted and scaly.

Dyshidrotic Eczema

Clear, deep blisters on palms and soles that itch and burn.

Stasis Dermatitis

Lower leg irritation related to circulatory problems.

General Therapeutic Modalities

"Soak and Seal" Protocol

Intact and healthy skin is the body's first line of defense. Hydration is key.

Figure 9-6. Soak in warm water for 15 to 20 minutes.

Figure 9-7. Seal with topicals immediately after bathing.

  1. 1 Soak: Bath/shower at least daily. Warm water, 10-15 mins. Use gentle cleansers.
  2. 2 Seal: Pat dry gently. Immediately (within 3 mins) apply moisturizer or medication to damp skin.
  3. 3 Moisturize: Use liberally throughout the day. Ointments/creams are better than lotions.
üí°

Tips to Reduce Irritation

  • Use fragrance-free, dye-free products.
  • Double rinse laundry.
  • Wear cotton-blend, loose-fitting clothing.
  • Keep fingernails short and smooth.

Atopic Dermatitis (AD)

The most common chronic, relapsing inflammatory skin disease of children. Associated with the "Atopic Triad": Asthma, Allergic Rhinitis, and AD.

Atopic Dermatitis Action Plan

Severity Action Steps
Basic / Daily "Soak and Seal". Avoid irritants. Moisturize liberally.
Mild-to-Moderate Bathe 1-2x daily. Moisturize. Apply low-mid potency steroid or calcineurin inhibitor to affected areas twice daily.
Moderate-to-Severe Bathe 2x daily (possibly bleach baths). High potency topicals. Wet Wrap Therapy. Oral antihistamines for itch.

Wet Wrap Therapy

Procedure for Flares

Used to relieve inflammation, remove scale, and increase medication penetration. Not for maintenance.

  1. 1Soak in warm water (15-20 min). Pat dry.
  2. 2Apply topical medication to affected areas; moisturizer to unaffected.
  3. 3Apply wet layer (damp gauze, pajamas, socks).
  4. 4Apply dry layer on top.
  5. 5Leave for 2-4 hours or overnight.

Seborrheic Dermatitis

Chronic dermatitis in areas of high sebaceous gland concentration. Linked to Malassezia yeast and sebum production.

Figure 9-9. Infant "Cradle Cap".

Figure 9-10. Adult Seborrheic Dermatitis.

Management

  • Infants: Baby oil to soften scale, gentle brushing, mild shampoo.
  • Adults: Antiseborrheic shampoos (selenium sulfide, zinc pyrithione, ketoconazole).
  • Topical: Low-potency steroids or antifungals for inflamed skin.

Contact Dermatitis

Divided into two types based on etiology.

Type Cause Prevalence Example
Irritant (ICD) Direct damage/irritation (non-immunologic) ~80% Soap, water, chemicals
Allergic (ACD) Type IV delayed hypersensitivity (immunologic) ~20% Poison ivy, nickel, fragrances

Diagnostics

Patch Testing is the gold standard for Allergic Contact Dermatitis. It reproduces the eczema in a controlled manner.

Diaper Dermatitis

Most commonly Irritant Contact Dermatitis caused by wetness, friction, and fecal enzymes. Can be complicated by Candida (yeast).

Management: The ABCDE Acronym

  • AAir: Go diaper-free.
  • BBarrier: Paste/ointment (Zinc oxide, petrolatum).
  • CClean: Gentle cleansing.
  • DDisposable: Superabsorbent diapers.
  • EEducate: Prevention strategies.
07

Nummular Dermatitis

Also called Discoid Eczema. Characterized by coin-shaped, scaly lesions, usually on the lower extremities. Can be "dry" or "wet" (exudative).
