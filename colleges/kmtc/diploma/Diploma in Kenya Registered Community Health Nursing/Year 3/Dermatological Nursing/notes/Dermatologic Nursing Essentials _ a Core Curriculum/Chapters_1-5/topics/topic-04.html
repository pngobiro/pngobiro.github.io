01

Overview & Topical Agents

The skin regulates body temperature, protects underlying skin cells from environmental toxins and harmful ultraviolet radiation, and acts as a barrier. The outermost layer of the epidermis, the stratum corneum, provides these functions. The goal of topical and systemic preparations is to maintain and restore the functions of the skin barrier. Normal skin pH is 5.4 to 5.9.

Vehicles

Choice of the appropriate vehicle determines the rate of absorption. Fat-soluble vehicles are more rapidly absorbed than water-soluble vehicles.

  • Ointment: Consists primarily of greases (petroleum jelly). Increases lubrication, greater penetration, preservative-free. Don't use in infected/hairy areas.
  • Creams: Oil and water emulsion. Lubricating, easily applied, cosmetically acceptable. Best for acute exudative inflammation.
  • Solutions/Lotions: Powder suspended in liquid. Good for hairy areas/scalp. Drying.
  • Gel: Greaseless mixture of propylene glycol/water/alcohol. Drying and cooling. Good for acute inflammation (poison ivy).
  • Powder: Finely ground solids. Absorptive, decreases friction. Good for intertriginous areas.
  • Paste: Powder in ointment (>50% powder). Protective, decreases absorption.
  • Foam: Useful for scalp dermatoses; spreads between hair strands.

Application & Occlusion

Occlusion produces a barrier (usually plastic film) that enhances absorption by 10 to 100 times by preventing evaporation and increasing hydration.

Topical Medications

A. Topical Steroids

Used to reduce inflammation via vasoconstriction, relieve pruritus, and induce remission. Ranked by potency groups 1-7.

Group Generic Name Brand Examples
I (Highest) Clobetasol propionate 0.05% Cormax, Olux
Halobetasol propionate 0.05% Ultravate
II Fluocinonide 0.05% Lidex
III-V Triamcinolone acetonide 0.1% Kenalog
VII (Lowest) Hydrocortisone 1.0-2.5% Hytone

Side Effects: Atrophy, striae (irreversible), telangiectasia, acneiform eruptions, hypopigmentation, HPA axis suppression (with potent use >50g/week).

B. Topical Antifungals

Treats dermatophyte infections (tinea). Combinations with steroids may mask infection.

C. Other Topicals

  • Antipruritics: Contain camphor, menthol, phenol (e.g., Sarna, Calamine).
  • Antibacterials: Bacitracin, Mupirocin (impetigo/MRSA), Silver sulfadiazine (burns), Clindamycin/Erythromycin (acne).
  • Antivirals: Acyclovir, penciclovir (Denavir). Reduce viral shedding.
  • Keratolytics: Salicylic acid, Coal tar, Anthralin. Dissolve stratum corneum.
  • Retinoids (Vitamin A): Tretinoin. For acne, photoaging. Increases cell turnover.

Patient Education: Retinoids

  • Avoid eyes/mouth/mucous membranes.
  • Increases risk of sunburn; use SPF 30+.
  • Use caution in pregnant women.
  • Avoid abrasive cleansers or alcohol-based products.
03

Intralesional Therapy

Injected directly into or just beneath the lesion. Provides a reservoir of medication.

  • Steroids (Triamcinolone): For psoriasis, alopecia areata, cystic acne, keloids. Risk of atrophy.
  • Antineoplastics:
    • Bleomycin: Warts.
    • Vinblastine: Kaposi sarcoma.
    • Interferon: Genital warts, BCC/SCC.
04

Systemic Medications

Antibiotics

  • Macrolides (Erythromycin, Azithromycin): Bacteriostatic. Safe. GI side effects common.
  • Penicillins: Bactericidal. Hypersensitivity (anaphylaxis) is major risk.
    • Dicloxacillin: Staph infections.
    • Amoxicillin: Broad spectrum.
  • Cephalosporins: 1st-5th generations. Cross-reactivity with penicillin. Very safe generally.
  • Tetracyclines: Acne, Lyme disease. Photosensitivity, teeth discoloration (don't use <8 yrs or pregnant).

Systemic Retinoids (Isotretinoin)

For recalcitrant cystic acne. iPLEDGE program required due to severe teratogenicity.

Isotretinoin Warning

Females must have negative pregnancy test before and during treatment. Two forms of birth control required. Side effects include cheilitis, dry skin, hypertriglyceridemia, and hepatotoxicity.

Biologics & Immunosuppressants

  • Methotrexate: Psoriasis. Hepatic fibrosis risk.
  • Cyclosporine: Psoriasis, Pyoderma gangrenosum. Nephrotoxicity risk.
  • Acitretin (Soriatane): Psoriasis. Teratogenic (no pregnancy for 3 years post-treatment).

05

Local Anesthetics

Block sodium ions to stop nerve conduction.

  • Amide linked (Lidocaine): Metabolized in liver. Low allergy risk.
  • Ester linked (Procaine/Novocain): Metabolized by pseudocholinesterase. Higher allergy risk (PABA).
  • Vasoconstrictors (Epinephrine): Prolongs duration, reduces bleeding, reduces systemic toxicity.
    • Avoid in: Compromised digital circulation, catecholamine sensitivity.
  • Toxicity: CNS (tinnitus, numbness, seizures) and CV (hypotension, arrhythmias).

Advanced Procedures

Lasers

Light Amplification by Stimulated Emission of Radiation. Targets chromophores (hemoglobin, melanin, water).

  • Vascular: Pulse Dye Laser (PWS, hemangiomas).
  • Pigmented: Q-switched Ruby/Nd:YAG (Tattoos, lentigines).
  • Resurfacing: CO2, Erbium:YAG.

Unna's Boot

Compression dressing for venous ulcers (zinc oxide paste). Wraps from metatarsals to knee with 50% overlap. Reduces edema, aids healing.
