Key Points

  • Incidence of bites and stings can be reduced by avoidance behaviors.
  • Prompt emergency treatment is indicated for anaphylactic reactions.
  • All cases of very pruritic rashes with intense nighttime itching should raise suspicion of scabies.
  • Parent education must include methods of eradicating pests from the home environment.

ü¶Ç Bites and Stings

Overview

Humans come in frequent contact with insects. While many cause only fleeting annoyance, some inject venom or use humans for a blood meal. Approximately 1% of children and 3% of adults experience allergic reactions.

I. Arachnida (Eight-legged)

1. Scorpions

Stings cause sharp burning sensation, edema, and discoloration. The Bark Scorpion (SW USA) can cause severe symptoms: hypersalivation, tachycardia, and agitation.

2. Black Widow Spider

Glossy black with red hourglass. Venom contains α-latrotoxin (neurotoxin). Causes severe muscle spasms (abdominal rigidity) within 1 hour, mimicking MI or acute abdomen.

Rocky Mountain Spotted Fever (RMSF)

Figure 12-8: RMSF rash (petechial).

Vector: Deer or Dog tick (Rickettsia rickettsii).
Presentation: Severe headache, fever, morbilliform rash on palms/soles progressing to petechiae on trunk.
Treatment: Doxycycline immediately (even in children). Chloramphenicol for pregnant women.

üéØ

Lyme Disease

Figure 12-9: Erythema migrans (Bull's eye rash).

Vector: Deer tick (Borrelia burgdorferi).
Presentation: Erythema Migrans (Bull's eye rash) in 60% of patients. Chronic disease can affect joints, heart (AV block), and CNS.
Treatment: Doxycycline or Amoxicillin.

II. Insecta (Six-legged)

Hymenoptera (Bees, Wasps, Ants)

  • Bees/Wasps: Venom contains histamines. Honeybees leave a barbed stinger.
  • Fire Ants: Stings create erythematous sterile pustules due to piperidine alkaloids.

Figure 12-11: Fire ant bite (sterile pustule).

Hypersensitivity Reactions

Type 1 (Immediate): IgE mediated. Wheal, pruritus, edema. Anaphylaxis involves respiratory distress and hypotension.
Treatment: Epinephrine for severe reactions. Venom immunotherapy (VIT) for prevention.

Other Insects

  • Fleas: Bites often around ankles. Erythematous papules.
  • Sandflies: Vector for Leishmaniasis (ulcerated lesions, Fig 12-13).
  • Bedbugs: Cimex lectularius. Feeds at night. Bites in linear pattern ("breakfast, lunch, dinner").
üîç Click to zoom

Figure 12-14: Bedbug (Cimex hemipterus).

Treatment Modalities

General Measures

  • Cold compresses.
  • Topical/Oral antihistamines.
  • Topical corticosteroids (contraindicated if open wound).
  • DEET: 10-50% concentration for prevention.

Specific Protocols

  • Black Widow: IV benzodiazepines (muscle spasms), narcotics, Antivenom if severe.
  • Brown Recluse: Dapsone for necrosis. Avoid surgery for 6-8 weeks until stable.
  • Lyme/RMSF: Doxycycline is first line.

üêõ Ectoparasite Infestations

I. Scabies

üîç Click to zoom

Figure 12-15: Sarcoptes scabiei mite.

Organism: Sarcoptes scabiei. Female mite burrows into stratum granulosum.

Presentation: Intense itching (worse at night). Linear/serpentine burrows in web spaces, wrists, axillae, groin. Norwegian (Crusted) Scabies occurs in immunocompromised hosts.

Diagnosis

Mineral Oil Prep: Scrape burrow, identify mite, ova, or feces (scybala). Burrow Ink Test.

Treatment

  • Permethrin 5% Cream: Apply neck down (head-to-toe for infants), leave 8-14 hours. Repeat in 1 week.
  • Ivermectin (Oral): 200 ¬µg/kg, repeat in 7-10 days.
  • Environmental: Wash clothes/bedding in hot water. Seal non-washables in plastic for 3 days. Treat all household contacts.

II. Pediculosis (Lice)

üîç Click to zoom

Figure 12-17: Nit on hair shaft.

Types

  • Head Lice: Pediculus humanus capitis. Nits attach to hair shaft.
  • Body Lice: Pediculus humanus corporis. Nits in clothing seams.
  • Pubic Lice: Phthirus pubis (Crabs).

Treatment

  • Permethrin 1%: Cream rinse. Repeat in 1 week.
  • Malathion 0.5%: Apply to dry hair for 8-12 hours. Flammable.
  • Spinosad 0.9%: Apply 10 mins. Ovidical.
  • Nit Removal: Mechanical removal with nit comb is essential.
