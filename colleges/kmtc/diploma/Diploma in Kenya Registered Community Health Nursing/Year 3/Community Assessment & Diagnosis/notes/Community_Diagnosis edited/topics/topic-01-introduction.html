Community Diagnosis

The process through which health workers, together with members of the community, identify the community‚Äôs priority health problems and together make plans of action and implement them.

It involves the identification and quantification of health problems in a community as a whole in terms of:

  • Mortality and morbidity rates and ratios.
  • Identification of their correlates for the purpose of defining those at risk or those in need of health care.

üß† Core Concepts

The Four Pillars of Participation

A community's full participation relies on four key concepts of primary health care:

1. Acceptability
2. Accessibility
3. Affordability
4. Availability

Community Involvement

The concept emphasizes that:

  • The community must identify its own problems.
  • The community must prioritize identified problems and draw a plan of action.
  • Total community involvement is crucial because members know their problems better and can prioritize more effectively than health workers alone.

üéØ Objectives & Purpose

Key Objectives

  • Analyze the health status of the community.
  • Evaluate health resources, services, and systems of care.
  • Assess attitudes toward community health services and issues.
  • Identify priorities, establish goals, and determine courses of action.
  • Establish an epidemiologic baseline for measuring improvement over time.

Purpose: Information Collection

Community diagnosis aims to collect information on the following specific areas:

Category Details
Demography Population statistics, age and gender distribution.
Disease Burden Causes of morbidity and mortality.
Services Use of group services (especially MCH/FP).
Nutrition Diet, weaning patterns, and growth monitoring of children.
Social Factors Societal culture, socio-economic stratification, leadership, and communication patterns.
Environment Water, housing, disease vectors, and general state of the environment.
Behavior Knowledge, Attitude, and Practices (KAP) related to health activities.
Epidemiology Details of endemic diseases.

üìä Analysis & Indicators

Community Analysis

This is the process of examining data to define needs, strengths, barriers, opportunities, readiness, and resources. The final product of this analysis is the "Community Profile".

Data Needed for Analysis

Demographic, Environmental, Socioeconomic, Health resources/services, Health policies, and study of target groups.

Health Indicators

Health indicators are variables used for the assessment of community health. They allow for the quantification of health status.

  • Mortality & Morbidity rates
  • Disability rates
  • Nutritional status
  • Health care delivery & Utilization
  • Social and mental health
  • Environmental factors
  • Socio-economic factors
  • Health policy
  • Quality of life

Ethical Considerations

üõ°Ô∏è

Do No Harm

The community diagnosis process should not cause any physical, emotional, spiritual, or cultural harm to the community.

Steps to Ensure Ethical Practice:

  1. Obtaining permission to enter into the community boundaries.
  2. Obtaining informed consent before interviewing clients, families, or groups.
  3. Establishing rapport before exploring sensitive areas.
  4. Ensuring confidentiality of data collected.
  5. Selecting good interviewers.
  6. Training interviewers thoroughly.
