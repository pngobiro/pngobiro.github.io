üìã Execution of the Survey

This phase involves going out to the field to collect information from the sample population. It is the practical implementation of the planning phase.

Protocol for Interviewers

ü§ù

Field Guidelines

  • Greet the respondents.
  • Introduce yourself by name and show identity cards/permission letters.
  • Explain carefully why you have come and the purpose of the survey.
  • Ask Permission: Check if it is convenient to interview the family at that time.
  • Confidentiality: Emphasize that all information recorded is confidential.
  • Clarify: Give them a chance to ask questions.

Data Handling

Once data is collected, it must be organized systematically:

  • Check all forms for completeness.
  • Ensure questionnaires are numbered.
  • Identify one person responsible for secure storage.
  • Sequence and label record forms clearly.

üî¢ Data Analysis Process

Data analysis is the separation and categorization of numerical data into groups to interpret its meaning. It transforms raw data into usable information.

1. Data Cleaning

Finding missing data, correcting mistakes (after verification), and excluding inconsistent information.

2. Sorting & Tallying

Arranging raw data into an array or groups. Tallying involves setting up classes using vertical strokes (four vertical, one diagonal) to create frequency tables.

3. Coding

Converting data into numerical codes. A code book should be prepared to describe codes assigned to each response category.

Types of Analysis

Qualitative Analysis Quantitative Analysis
Applied to data that can be counted but not measured (e.g., opinions, attitudes). Applied to data that has a numerical basis or can be measured (e.g., age, weight).

üìä Data Presentation

Data can be presented using Tabular methods (tables) or Graphical methods:

Histogram
Frequency Polygon
Bar Graph
Pie Chart

Qualities of a Good Graph

  • Title: Clear, concise, and unambiguous.
  • Units: Clear statement of units used on vertical/horizontal axes.
  • Scales: Correct vertical and horizontal scaling.
  • Key/Legend: Explains features of the graph if needed.

üì¢ Feedback & Reporting

Types of Reports

‚ñ∂ 1. Individual Report

Feedback given to survey participants or those who provided specimens.

  • Inform them of abnormal findings that require treatment.
  • Refer to health facilities for follow-up care.
  • Caution: Do not arouse anxiety about harmless conditions.
  • Report notifiable diseases to authorities.
‚ñ∂ 2. Preliminary Report

Given to the community/leaders soon after fieldwork to thank them.

  • Covers general impressions of health status.
  • Avoid specific data (analysis not yet ready).
  • Promise a detailed report later.
‚ñ∂ 3. Non-Medical & Scientific Reports

Non-medical Report: Less scientific, produced for leaders/politicians to influence policy and resource allocation.

Medical/Scientific Report: Detailed account including methodology and analysis.

Structure of the Scientific Report

  1. Title
  2. Table of Content
  3. List of figures/tables
  4. Abbreviations
  5. Acknowledgements
  6. Introduction
  7. Aims & Objectives
  8. Methodology
  9. Limitations
  10. Results/Findings
  11. Discussions
  12. Conclusions & Recommendations
  13. References
  14. Appendices

‚ö° Community Action

The ultimate goal of community diagnosis is to identify problems and mobilize the community to take action.

Common Interventions

  • Awareness: Making the community aware of their problems and prompting Primary Health Care (PHC).
  • Health Education: Targeted education based on identified gaps.
  • Immunization: Campaigns to address coverage gaps.
  • Environmental Improvement: Sanitation and hygiene initiatives.
