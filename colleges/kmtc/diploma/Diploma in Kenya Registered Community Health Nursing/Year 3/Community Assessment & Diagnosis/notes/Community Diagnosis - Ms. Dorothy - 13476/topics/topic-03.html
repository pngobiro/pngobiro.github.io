üß∞ Data Collection Tools

To measure a community's health status, various tools can be employed. The primary tools include:

Questionnaires

Standardised sets of questions for gathering specific information.

Focus Group Discussions

Group interviews to gather qualitative insights from similar backgrounds.

Physical Exams & Tests

Measurements, physical examinations, and laboratory tests.

Key Informant Interviews

In-depth interviews with knowledgeable community members.

üìù Questionnaires

A questionnaire is a set of standardised questions designed to collect information about a specific aspect or issue in the community.

üó£Ô∏è Focus Group Discussions (FGDs)

This is a group discussion that gathers together people from similar backgrounds or experiences to discuss a specific topic of interest to the researcher.

The group of participants are guided by a moderator (or group facilitator), who introduces topics for discussion and helps the group to participate in a lively and natural discussion amongst themselves.

üß™ Pre-testing Instruments

It is very important to pre-test all the instruments you intend to use before they are finally administered.

Why Pre-test?

  • It enables the interviewing team to discern, alter or delete questions which are being misinterpreted.
  • It helps identify questions that are too sensitive to be asked without offending people.
  • It gives you the opportunity to discover if the various parts of the questionnaire flow in a logical order.
