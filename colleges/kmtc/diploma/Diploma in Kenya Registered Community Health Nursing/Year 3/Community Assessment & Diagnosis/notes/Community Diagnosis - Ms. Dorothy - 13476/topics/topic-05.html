üìù Feedback and Report Writing

After a survey is done, those in the community who cooperated with you are entitled to receive some form of feedback. They want to know what you found.

Feedback means giving comments about how well or badly a person is doing in order to help them do better. It is a form of communication.

All individuals in the community who are concerned with the health of the people are entitled to feedback.

Types of Feedback

1. Individual Results

The individuals who formed part of your survey sample as well as those who provided you with specimens and tests deserve to be given feedback on your findings.

2. Preliminary Report

Covers general impressions of the community‚Äôs health status and preliminary findings.

  • Target: The community and leaders (to thank them).
  • Timing: Soon after field work is completed.
  • Content: Obvious points like disease outbreaks, hygiene, sanitation, attitudes, nutrition.

3. Non-Medical Reports

Less scientific reports produced for non-medical people.

  • Timing: After full analysis.
  • Purpose: A social or political tool to shed light on improvements needed.
  • Note: Make recommendations practical.

4. The Medical or Community Diagnosis Report

A detailed scientific report providing an account of the planning, execution, and results of the survey.

03

üìë Medical Report Structure

A well written community diagnosis report is made up of distinct sections or components:

  1. Title
  2. Table of contents
  3. Lists of tables and figures
  4. List of abbreviations and acronyms
  5. Acknowledgements
  6. Introduction
  7. Aims and objectives of the study
  8. Materials and Methods
  9. Limitations of study
  10. Results/ Findings
  11. Discussions
  12. Recommendations and Conclusions
  13. References
  14. Appendices

ü§ù Community Health Action

Sit down with the community to prioritize and plan what you going to do about the identified health problems. You need to mobilize them to take action.

Mobilizing Community Action

You can mobilize the community through a number of interventions:

  • Making them aware of their problems and promoting primary health care
  • Health education
  • Immunisation
  • Environmental improvement

Critical Note
