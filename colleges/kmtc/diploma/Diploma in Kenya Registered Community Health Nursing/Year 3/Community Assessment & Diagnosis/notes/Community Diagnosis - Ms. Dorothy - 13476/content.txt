<div data-testid="stHeadingWithActionElements" class="st-emotion-cache-1p9ibxm e1icttdg2"><h1 level="1" id="the-concept-of-community-diagnosis">The Concept of Community Diagnosis<span data-testid="stHeaderActionElements" class="st-emotion-cache-gi0tri e1icttdg3"><a href="#the-concept-of-community-diagnosis" class="st-emotion-cache-yinll1 e1icttdg1"><svg xmlns="http://www.w3.org/2000/svg" width="16" height="16" viewBox="0 0 24 24" fill="none" stroke="currentColor" stroke-width="2" stroke-linecap="round" stroke-linejoin="round"><path d="M15 7h3a5 5 0 0 1 5 5 5 5 0 0 1-5 5h-3m-6 0H6a5 5 0 0 1-5-5 5 5 0 0 1 5-5h3"></path><line x1="8" y1="12" x2="16" y2="12"></line></svg></a></span></h1></div>
<p>Community diagnosis is a process through which health workers together with members of the community identify the community's priority health problems, and together make plans of action and implement them.</p>
<p>It points out where the health services should put their main efforts and resources. You learnt in unit three on Primary Health Care (PHC), a community's full participation depends on the four concepts of PHC or the 4As.</p>
<p>The 4 A’s are acceptability, accessibility, affordability and availability using appropriate and local technologies</p>
<p>People have the ability to help themselves if they are given some facilitation or guidance. People want to be independent and self-reliant.</p>
<p>It is also true that people tend to cherish and care for what they have acquired through a struggle. Such involvement empowers individuals and makes them resourceful and self-reliant.</p>
<p>The community diagnosis concept therefore stresses that the community must identify its problems, prioritise them and draw a plan of action to address the identified problems.</p>
<p>The community then implements this plan to resolve the problems. It emphasises total community involvement. This is because the community knows its problems and priorities better than the health worker.</p>
<p>When they actively participate in solving these issues they become bound by the decisions they make and feel motivated to see the plans through.</p>
<div data-testid="stHeadingWithActionElements" class="st-emotion-cache-1p9ibxm e1icttdg2"><h1 level="1" id="patient-diagnosis-versus-community-diagnosis">Patient Diagnosis versus Community Diagnosis<span data-testid="stHeaderActionElements" class="st-emotion-cache-gi0tri e1icttdg3"><a href="#patient-diagnosis-versus-community-diagnosis" class="st-emotion-cache-yinll1 e1icttdg1"><svg xmlns="http://www.w3.org/2000/svg" width="16" height="16" viewBox="0 0 24 24" fill="none" stroke="currentColor" stroke-width="2" stroke-linecap="round" stroke-linejoin="round"><path d="M15 7h3a5 5 0 0 1 5 5 5 5 0 0 1-5 5h-3m-6 0H6a5 5 0 0 1-5-5 5 5 0 0 1 5-5h3"></path><line x1="8" y1="12" x2="16" y2="12"></line></svg></a></span></h1></div>
<ul>
<li>Collecting basic information or history taking</li>
<li>Planning a programme or diagnosis</li>
<li>Implementation or treatment</li>
<li>Follow up and evaluation</li>
</ul>
<p>In community diagnosis, you follow the same basic steps as the ones you have seen in patient diagnosis. The only difference is that the amount of data is much greater and requires more lengthy analysis and processing.</p>
<p>In community diagnosis you start by collecting basic information. You collect information about the following:</p>
<ol>
<li>Local people and their environment</li>
<li>The number of people and their distribution</li>
<li>The diseases the local people suffer from</li>
<li>The organisation of local health services</li>
</ol>
<p>You then make a community diagnosis by identifying the main health problems and the reasons for them.</p>
<p>Identify priority health problems and plan a community health programme or treatment to solve these problems.</p>
<p>Remember:</p>
<p>It is important to only select priority health problems</p>
<ul>
<li>This is because health centres often have limited resources and many demands on those resources. There are simply not enough resources to solve all the health problems in the community. Therefore, the health care worker together with the community must select priorities for health action.</li>
<li>Remember, it is important to choose only those problems that the community can do something practical about with the help of the local health team (health centre or dispensary staff).</li>
<li>The final step is follow up to evaluate the programme and see if it has made the community healthier.</li>
</ul>
<p>The tools you use in patient diagnosis are, for example:</p>
<p>☐ Sphygmomanometer (BP machine)
☐ Stethoscope
☐ Weighing scales
☐ Thermometer
☐ Chairs
☐ Record books</p>
<p>In community diagnosis, you use survey tools for example:</p>
<p>☐ Maps
☐ Weighing scale
☐ Specimen bottles
☐ Questionnaires</p>
<div data-testid="stHeadingWithActionElements" class="st-emotion-cache-1p9ibxm e1icttdg2"><h1 level="1" id="purpose-of-community-diagnosis">Purpose of Community Diagnosis<span data-testid="stHeaderActionElements" class="st-emotion-cache-gi0tri e1icttdg3"><a href="#purpose-of-community-diagnosis" class="st-emotion-cache-yinll1 e1icttdg1"><svg xmlns="http://www.w3.org/2000/svg" width="16" height="16" viewBox="0 0 24 24" fill="none" stroke="currentColor" stroke-width="2" stroke-linecap="round" stroke-linejoin="round"><path d="M15 7h3a5 5 0 0 1 5 5 5 5 0 0 1-5 5h-3m-6 0H6a5 5 0 0 1-5-5 5 5 0 0 1 5-5h3"></path><line x1="8" y1="12" x2="16" y2="12"></line></svg></a></span></h1></div>
<p>The main purpose of carrying out a community diagnosis is to collect information on the following:</p>
<ul>
<li>Demographic data plus all the vital health statistics</li>
<li>Utilisation of health services especially of maternal and child health clinics</li>
<li>The causes of morbidity and mortality (by age and sex)</li>
<li>State of nutrition, diet, weaning patterns and the growth of preschool and school-going children</li>
<li>Patterns of leadership and communication within the community</li>
<li>State of mental health and common causes of stress</li>
<li>State of the environment including water, housing and disease vectors</li>
</ul>
<p>o The community's knowledge, attitudes and practices (KAP) in relation to health-related activities</p>
<p>o Epidemiological details of endemic diseases</p>
<p>o Available resources and services for overall development of health-related activities for example, education, agriculture, veterinary and social services</p>
<p>o Socio-cultural and socio-economic class divisions within the community (social stratification)</p>
<p>Although a community diagnosis can yield a lot of data, it is not possible to collect it all in detail.</p>
<p>Therefore, you need to tailor your survey to suit the specific information you want to collect.</p>

<p>Read and make short notes on the terminologies used in community diagnosis</p>
<div data-testid="stHeadingWithActionElements" class="st-emotion-cache-1p9ibxm e1icttdg2"><h1 level="1" id="ethical-considerations-in-community-diagnosis">Ethical Considerations in Community Diagnosis<span data-testid="stHeaderActionElements" class="st-emotion-cache-gi0tri e1icttdg3"><a href="#ethical-considerations-in-community-diagnosis" class="st-emotion-cache-yinll1 e1icttdg1"><svg xmlns="http://www.w3.org/2000/svg" width="16" height="16" viewBox="0 0 24 24" fill="none" stroke="currentColor" stroke-width="2" stroke-linecap="round" stroke-linejoin="round"><path d="M15 7h3a5 5 0 0 1 5 5 5 5 0 0 1-5 5h-3m-6 0H6a5 5 0 0 1-5-5 5 5 0 0 1 5-5h3"></path><line x1="8" y1="12" x2="16" y2="12"></line></svg></a></span></h1></div>
<p>When conducting community diagnosis, it is very important to avoid taking any action that may be considered offensive by the community.</p>
<p>You need to make sure that the tools you use to collect information are not in any way offensive to the community.</p>
<p>They should NOT cause any physical, emotional, spiritual or cultural harm to that community.</p>
<p>You should consider some of the following:</p>
<ul>
<li>Obtaining permission to enter into the community boundaries</li>
<li>Obtaining informed consent before interviewing patients, families or groups</li>
<li>Establishing rapport before exploring sensitive areas</li>
<li>Ensuring confidentiality of the data collected</li>
<li>Selecting good interviewers</li>
<li>Training interviewers</li>
</ul>
<div data-testid="stHeadingWithActionElements" class="st-emotion-cache-1p9ibxm e1icttdg2"><h1 level="1" id="section-2-planning-a-community-diagnosis">SECTION 2: PLANNING A COMMUNITY DIAGNOSIS<span data-testid="stHeaderActionElements" class="st-emotion-cache-gi0tri e1icttdg3"><a href="#section-2-planning-a-community-diagnosis" class="st-emotion-cache-yinll1 e1icttdg1"><svg xmlns="http://www.w3.org/2000/svg" width="16" height="16" viewBox="0 0 24 24" fill="none" stroke="currentColor" stroke-width="2" stroke-linecap="round" stroke-linejoin="round"><path d="M15 7h3a5 5 0 0 1 5 5 5 5 0 0 1-5 5h-3m-6 0H6a5 5 0 0 1-5-5 5 5 0 0 1 5-5h3"></path><line x1="8" y1="12" x2="16" y2="12"></line></svg></a></span></h1></div>
<div data-testid="stHeadingWithActionElements" class="st-emotion-cache-1p9ibxm e1icttdg2"><h2 level="2" id="objectives">Objectives<span data-testid="stHeaderActionElements" class="st-emotion-cache-gi0tri e1icttdg3"><a href="#objectives" class="st-emotion-cache-yinll1 e1icttdg1"><svg xmlns="http://www.w3.org/2000/svg" width="16" height="16" viewBox="0 0 24 24" fill="none" stroke="currentColor" stroke-width="2" stroke-linecap="round" stroke-linejoin="round"><path d="M15 7h3a5 5 0 0 1 5 5 5 5 0 0 1-5 5h-3m-6 0H6a5 5 0 0 1-5-5 5 5 0 0 1 5-5h3"></path><line x1="8" y1="12" x2="16" y2="12"></line></svg></a></span></h2></div>
<p>By the end of this section you will be able to:</p>
<ul>
<li>Describe the process of community diagnosis</li>
<li>Explain how to conduct initial exploration and interaction with the community</li>
<li>Explain how to plan a community diagnosis survey</li>
<li>Describe how to select a representative sample for the survey</li>
</ul>
<p>The process of community diagnosis requires <strong>careful planning</strong> right from the beginning.</p>
<p>It involves initial exploration and interaction with the community, planning of the survey, developing and pre-testing the survey tools and methods, and executing the survey and analysing the results.</p>
<p>Once all the information has been gathered it must be documented and its conclusions disseminated to the community.</p>
<p>The process of community diagnosis is made up of the following steps:</p>
<ol>
<li>
<p>Exploration</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Planning of the survey</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Developing and pre-testing survey tools</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Execution of the survey and data analysis</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Report writing, dissemination and community action</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>EXPLORING THE COMMUNITY (COMMUNITY INVENTORY)</p>
</li>
</ol>
<p>Exploration simply means mapping out of a community in order to learn or discover about it. It is also known as community inventory.</p>
<p>Ideally, you should only carry out a community diagnosis after a request by the community or the people involved in providing health care.</p>
<p>The exploration phase is made up of three main activities:</p>
<ol>
<li>Seeking permission and informing the various leaders</li>
<li>Seeking reactions of members of the community</li>
<li>Gathering background data about the community</li>
</ol>
<div data-testid="stHeadingWithActionElements" class="st-emotion-cache-1p9ibxm e1icttdg2"><h1 level="1" id="seeking-permission-and-informing-authorities-in-the-community">Seeking Permission and Informing Authorities in the Community<span data-testid="stHeaderActionElements" class="st-emotion-cache-gi0tri e1icttdg3"><a href="#seeking-permission-and-informing-authorities-in-the-community" class="st-emotion-cache-yinll1 e1icttdg1"><svg xmlns="http://www.w3.org/2000/svg" width="16" height="16" viewBox="0 0 24 24" fill="none" stroke="currentColor" stroke-width="2" stroke-linecap="round" stroke-linejoin="round"><path d="M15 7h3a5 5 0 0 1 5 5 5 5 0 0 1-5 5h-3m-6 0H6a5 5 0 0 1-5-5 5 5 0 0 1 5-5h3"></path><line x1="8" y1="12" x2="16" y2="12"></line></svg></a></span></h1></div>
<p>For the survey to succeed you must seek permission from community leaders.</p>
<p>☐ Start by channelling your request through the official hierarchy of administrative leaders in that community:
☐ Health personnel such as Medical Officer in Charge
☐ Governmental officials such as Chiefs or District Officers
☐ Community leaders through, for example, the village health committee
☐ You should approach all these people, introduce yourself and clearly state the objectives of the survey and your plan of action</p>
<div data-testid="stHeadingWithActionElements" class="st-emotion-cache-1p9ibxm e1icttdg2"><h1 level="1" id="seeking-reactions-of-members-of-the-community">Seeking Reactions of Members of the Community<span data-testid="stHeaderActionElements" class="st-emotion-cache-gi0tri e1icttdg3"><a href="#seeking-reactions-of-members-of-the-community" class="st-emotion-cache-yinll1 e1icttdg1"><svg xmlns="http://www.w3.org/2000/svg" width="16" height="16" viewBox="0 0 24 24" fill="none" stroke="currentColor" stroke-width="2" stroke-linecap="round" stroke-linejoin="round"><path d="M15 7h3a5 5 0 0 1 5 5 5 5 0 0 1-5 5h-3m-6 0H6a5 5 0 0 1-5-5 5 5 0 0 1 5-5h3"></path><line x1="8" y1="12" x2="16" y2="12"></line></svg></a></span></h1></div>
<p>This can be done by talking to people informally in market places and eating places.</p>
<p>This way, you will easily find their opinions or problems in the community and their likely solutions.</p>
<div data-testid="stHeadingWithActionElements" class="st-emotion-cache-1p9ibxm e1icttdg2"><h1 level="1" id="gathering-background-data">Gathering Background Data<span data-testid="stHeaderActionElements" class="st-emotion-cache-gi0tri e1icttdg3"><a href="#gathering-background-data" class="st-emotion-cache-yinll1 e1icttdg1"><svg xmlns="http://www.w3.org/2000/svg" width="16" height="16" viewBox="0 0 24 24" fill="none" stroke="currentColor" stroke-width="2" stroke-linecap="round" stroke-linejoin="round"><path d="M15 7h3a5 5 0 0 1 5 5 5 5 0 0 1-5 5h-3m-6 0H6a5 5 0 0 1-5-5 5 5 0 0 1 5-5h3"></path><line x1="8" y1="12" x2="16" y2="12"></line></svg></a></span></h1></div>
<ul>
<li>The period of exploration also presents you with the opportunity to gather background data about the community.</li>
<li>For instance, the Medical Officer of Health in charge of the district will give you an overview of the health profile of the district.</li>
<li>The District Commissioner will give you boundaries, the population and maps of the area.</li>
<li>The District Education Officer will give information about educational activities and literacy levels of the community members. Other district heads will give information related to their areas of jurisdiction.</li>
</ul>
<div data-testid="stHeadingWithActionElements" class="st-emotion-cache-1p9ibxm e1icttdg2"><h1 level="1" id="gather-information-as-you-move-around-the-community-by">Gather information as you move around the community by:<span data-testid="stHeaderActionElements" class="st-emotion-cache-gi0tri e1icttdg3"><a href="#gather-information-as-you-move-around-the-community-by" class="st-emotion-cache-yinll1 e1icttdg1"><svg xmlns="http://www.w3.org/2000/svg" width="16" height="16" viewBox="0 0 24 24" fill="none" stroke="currentColor" stroke-width="2" stroke-linecap="round" stroke-linejoin="round"><path d="M15 7h3a5 5 0 0 1 5 5 5 5 0 0 1-5 5h-3m-6 0H6a5 5 0 0 1-5-5 5 5 0 0 1 5-5h3"></path><line x1="8" y1="12" x2="16" y2="12"></line></svg></a></span></h1></div>
<ul>
<li>Questioning</li>
<li>Observing</li>
<li>Smelling</li>
<li>Listening</li>
</ul>
<p>You should also look at findings of previous surveys so that you can adequately address any new problems.</p>
<ol start="2">
<li>PLANNING THE SURVEY</li>
</ol>
<p>This is a very important part of the process of community diagnosis.</p>
<p>There is a popular adage which says 'If you fail to plan you plan to fail'.</p>
<p>So if you fail to plan the survey carefully and properly, your study will be unsuccessful or will give you unreliable results.</p>
<p>Why is the Survey Being Done?</p>
<p>Where Will it Take Place?</p>
<p>Who Will be Interviewed?</p>
<p>When Will the Survey Take Place?</p>
<p>What Will be Covered in the Survey?</p>
<p>Why are certain variables included and others left out?</p>
<p>What instruments will be used to measure the community’s health status?</p>
<p>How will data be collected and with what resources?
How do we select and train the official interviewers?</p>
<p>Remember:</p>
<p>It is advisable to overestimate the time needed for data collection to allow for unseen delays.</p>
<p>Interviewers should conduct themselves in an appropriate manner when approaching respondents. The points below should be followed:</p>
<p>a. Establish rapport by greeting the respondents and introducing themselves
b. Explain carefully why they have come and what is the purpose of the survey
c. Ask if they are welcome to interview the family and if it is convenient for them at that time
d. Explain that they will be recording the information they collect
e. Emphasise that all information collected is confidential
f. Give them a chance to ask questions for clarification</p>
<div data-testid="stHeadingWithActionElements" class="st-emotion-cache-1p9ibxm e1icttdg2"><h1 level="1" id="reading-assignment">READING ASSIGNMENT<span data-testid="stHeaderActionElements" class="st-emotion-cache-gi0tri e1icttdg3"><a href="#reading-assignment" class="st-emotion-cache-yinll1 e1icttdg1"><svg xmlns="http://www.w3.org/2000/svg" width="16" height="16" viewBox="0 0 24 24" fill="none" stroke="currentColor" stroke-width="2" stroke-linecap="round" stroke-linejoin="round"><path d="M15 7h3a5 5 0 0 1 5 5 5 5 0 0 1-5 5h-3m-6 0H6a5 5 0 0 1-5-5 5 5 0 0 1 5-5h3"></path><line x1="8" y1="12" x2="16" y2="12"></line></svg></a></span></h1></div>

<div data-testid="stHeadingWithActionElements" class="st-emotion-cache-1p9ibxm e1icttdg2"><h1 level="1" id="sampling-for-a-survey">Sampling for a Survey<span data-testid="stHeaderActionElements" class="st-emotion-cache-gi0tri e1icttdg3"><a href="#sampling-for-a-survey" class="st-emotion-cache-yinll1 e1icttdg1"><svg xmlns="http://www.w3.org/2000/svg" width="16" height="16" viewBox="0 0 24 24" fill="none" stroke="currentColor" stroke-width="2" stroke-linecap="round" stroke-linejoin="round"><path d="M15 7h3a5 5 0 0 1 5 5 5 5 0 0 1-5 5h-3m-6 0H6a5 5 0 0 1-5-5 5 5 0 0 1 5-5h3"></path><line x1="8" y1="12" x2="16" y2="12"></line></svg></a></span></h1></div>
<p>Sampling is the process of selecting a number of individuals or units of the study population in such a way that the individuals selected represent the larger groups from which they are selected.</p>
<p>That is why the part of the population studied is called <strong>a sample</strong> and you must make sure that it is representative of the whole population.</p>
<div data-testid="stHeadingWithActionElements" class="st-emotion-cache-1p9ibxm e1icttdg2"><h1 level="1" id="a-study-population">A Study Population<span data-testid="stHeaderActionElements" class="st-emotion-cache-gi0tri e1icttdg3"><a href="#a-study-population" class="st-emotion-cache-yinll1 e1icttdg1"><svg xmlns="http://www.w3.org/2000/svg" width="16" height="16" viewBox="0 0 24 24" fill="none" stroke="currentColor" stroke-width="2" stroke-linecap="round" stroke-linejoin="round"><path d="M15 7h3a5 5 0 0 1 5 5 5 5 0 0 1-5 5h-3m-6 0H6a5 5 0 0 1-5-5 5 5 0 0 1 5-5h3"></path><line x1="8" y1="12" x2="16" y2="12"></line></svg></a></span></h1></div>
<p>A study population is the entire group of individuals, events or objects that have common observable characteristics.</p>
<p>For example:</p>
<ul>
<li>All first years in nursing</li>
<li>All under fives in a given community</li>
<li>All qualified nurses with mental health qualifications</li>
</ul>
<p>It must be clearly defined for example, according to age, sex, or residence.</p>
<p>A study population can be divided into two main groups, namely, accessible population and the representative sample.</p>
<div data-testid="stHeadingWithActionElements" class="st-emotion-cache-1p9ibxm e1icttdg2"><h1 level="1" id="a-study-population">A study population<span data-testid="stHeaderActionElements" class="st-emotion-cache-gi0tri e1icttdg3"><a href="#a-study-population" class="st-emotion-cache-yinll1 e1icttdg1"><svg xmlns="http://www.w3.org/2000/svg" width="16" height="16" viewBox="0 0 24 24" fill="none" stroke="currentColor" stroke-width="2" stroke-linecap="round" stroke-linejoin="round"><path d="M15 7h3a5 5 0 0 1 5 5 5 5 0 0 1-5 5h-3m-6 0H6a5 5 0 0 1-5-5 5 5 0 0 1 5-5h3"></path><line x1="8" y1="12" x2="16" y2="12"></line></svg></a></span></h1></div>
<p>The accessible population is a group of individuals, objects and events with characteristics comparable to the target population and relevant to the study.</p>
<p>The representative sample is a group from the study population, which has all the important/relevant characteristics of the total population.</p>
<div data-testid="stHeadingWithActionElements" class="st-emotion-cache-1p9ibxm e1icttdg2"><h1 level="1" id="sampling-sampling-methods">Sampling &amp; Sampling Methods<span data-testid="stHeaderActionElements" class="st-emotion-cache-gi0tri e1icttdg3"><a href="#sampling-sampling-methods" class="st-emotion-cache-yinll1 e1icttdg1"><svg xmlns="http://www.w3.org/2000/svg" width="16" height="16" viewBox="0 0 24 24" fill="none" stroke="currentColor" stroke-width="2" stroke-linecap="round" stroke-linejoin="round"><path d="M15 7h3a5 5 0 0 1 5 5 5 5 0 0 1-5 5h-3m-6 0H6a5 5 0 0 1-5-5 5 5 0 0 1 5-5h3"></path><line x1="8" y1="12" x2="16" y2="12"></line></svg></a></span></h1></div>
<p>Sampling techniques fall under two main groups:</p>
<ul>
<li>☑ Probability Sampling</li>
<li>☑ Non probability Sampling</li>
</ul>
<div data-testid="stHeadingWithActionElements" class="st-emotion-cache-1p9ibxm e1icttdg2"><h1 level="1" id="probability-sampling">Probability Sampling<span data-testid="stHeaderActionElements" class="st-emotion-cache-gi0tri e1icttdg3"><a href="#probability-sampling" class="st-emotion-cache-yinll1 e1icttdg1"><svg xmlns="http://www.w3.org/2000/svg" width="16" height="16" viewBox="0 0 24 24" fill="none" stroke="currentColor" stroke-width="2" stroke-linecap="round" stroke-linejoin="round"><path d="M15 7h3a5 5 0 0 1 5 5 5 5 0 0 1-5 5h-3m-6 0H6a5 5 0 0 1-5-5 5 5 0 0 1 5-5h3"></path><line x1="8" y1="12" x2="16" y2="12"></line></svg></a></span></h1></div>
<ul>
<li>Probability sampling looks at the entire group of individuals, events or objects that have common observable characteristics.</li>
<li>It has been found to give accurate results when one is studying groups that are too large to study in their entity.</li>
</ul>
<p>The following are the most commonly used methods in probability sampling:</p>
<ol>
<li>Simple random sampling</li>
<li>Systematic sampling</li>
<li>Stratified sampling</li>
<li>Cluster sampling</li>
<li>Multi-stage sampling</li>
</ol>
<div data-testid="stHeadingWithActionElements" class="st-emotion-cache-1p9ibxm e1icttdg2"><h1 level="1" id="non-probability-sampling">Non probability Sampling<span data-testid="stHeaderActionElements" class="st-emotion-cache-gi0tri e1icttdg3"><a href="#non-probability-sampling" class="st-emotion-cache-yinll1 e1icttdg1"><svg xmlns="http://www.w3.org/2000/svg" width="16" height="16" viewBox="0 0 24 24" fill="none" stroke="currentColor" stroke-width="2" stroke-linecap="round" stroke-linejoin="round"><path d="M15 7h3a5 5 0 0 1 5 5 5 5 0 0 1-5 5h-3m-6 0H6a5 5 0 0 1-5-5 5 5 0 0 1 5-5h3"></path><line x1="8" y1="12" x2="16" y2="12"></line></svg></a></span></h1></div>
<ul>
<li>
<p>Is used when a researcher is not interested in selecting a sample that is representative of the population.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Mainly used in qualitative studies where the focus is on in-depth information rather than making generalisations.</p>
</li>
</ul>

<ol>
<li>Convenient sampling,</li>
<li>Quota sampling,</li>
<li>Purposive sampling</li>

<div data-testid="stHeadingWithActionElements" class="st-emotion-cache-1p9ibxm e1icttdg2"><h1 level="1" id="section-3-developing-and-pre-testing-tools-for-data-collection">SECTION 3: DEVELOPING AND PRE-TESTING TOOLS FOR DATA COLLECTION<span data-testid="stHeaderActionElements" class="st-emotion-cache-gi0tri e1icttdg3"><a href="#section-3-developing-and-pre-testing-tools-for-data-collection" class="st-emotion-cache-yinll1 e1icttdg1"><svg xmlns="http://www.w3.org/2000/svg" width="16" height="16" viewBox="0 0 24 24" fill="none" stroke="currentColor" stroke-width="2" stroke-linecap="round" stroke-linejoin="round"><path d="M15 7h3a5 5 0 0 1 5 5 5 5 0 0 1-5 5h-3m-6 0H6a5 5 0 0 1-5-5 5 5 0 0 1 5-5h3"></path><line x1="8" y1="12" x2="16" y2="12"></line></svg></a></span></h1></div>
<div data-testid="stHeadingWithActionElements" class="st-emotion-cache-1p9ibxm e1icttdg2"><h1 level="1" id="objectives">Objectives<span data-testid="stHeaderActionElements" class="st-emotion-cache-gi0tri e1icttdg3"><a href="#objectives" class="st-emotion-cache-yinll1 e1icttdg1"><svg xmlns="http://www.w3.org/2000/svg" width="16" height="16" viewBox="0 0 24 24" fill="none" stroke="currentColor" stroke-width="2" stroke-linecap="round" stroke-linejoin="round"><path d="M15 7h3a5 5 0 0 1 5 5 5 5 0 0 1-5 5h-3m-6 0H6a5 5 0 0 1-5-5 5 5 0 0 1 5-5h3"></path><line x1="8" y1="12" x2="16" y2="12"></line></svg></a></span></h1></div>

<div data-testid="stHeadingWithActionElements" class="st-emotion-cache-1p9ibxm e1icttdg2"><h1 level="1" id="tools">TOOLS<span data-testid="stHeaderActionElements" class="st-emotion-cache-gi0tri e1icttdg3"><a href="#tools" class="st-emotion-cache-yinll1 e1icttdg1"><svg xmlns="http://www.w3.org/2000/svg" width="16" height="16" viewBox="0 0 24 24" fill="none" stroke="currentColor" stroke-width="2" stroke-linecap="round" stroke-linejoin="round"><path d="M15 7h3a5 5 0 0 1 5 5 5 5 0 0 1-5 5h-3m-6 0H6a5 5 0 0 1-5-5 5 5 0 0 1 5-5h3"></path><line x1="8" y1="12" x2="16" y2="12"></line></svg></a></span></h1></div>
<p>The tools used to measure a community’s health status are:</p>
<ul>
<li>Questionnaires</li>
<li>Focus group discussions (FGDS)</li>
<li>Measurements, physical examination and laboratory tests</li>

<div data-testid="stHeadingWithActionElements" class="st-emotion-cache-1p9ibxm e1icttdg2"><h1 level="1" id="questionnaire">Questionnaire<span data-testid="stHeaderActionElements" class="st-emotion-cache-gi0tri e1icttdg3"><a href="#questionnaire" class="st-emotion-cache-yinll1 e1icttdg1"><svg xmlns="http://www.w3.org/2000/svg" width="16" height="16" viewBox="0 0 24 24" fill="none" stroke="currentColor" stroke-width="2" stroke-linecap="round" stroke-linejoin="round"><path d="M15 7h3a5 5 0 0 1 5 5 5 5 0 0 1-5 5h-3m-6 0H6a5 5 0 0 1-5-5 5 5 0 0 1 5-5h3"></path><line x1="8" y1="12" x2="16" y2="12"></line></svg></a></span></h1></div>
<ul>
<li>A questionnaire is a set of standardised questions designed to collect information about a specific aspect or issue in the community.</li>
<li>It is therefore a tool for collecting information. Keep it short and simple (KISS).</li>
<li>Information from a questionnaire helps you to make plans for your health services and to evaluate them.</li>
</ul>

<div data-testid="stHeadingWithActionElements" class="st-emotion-cache-1p9ibxm e1icttdg2"><h1 level="1" id="execution-of-the-survey">Execution of the Survey<span data-testid="stHeaderActionElements" class="st-emotion-cache-gi0tri e1icttdg3"><a href="#execution-of-the-survey" class="st-emotion-cache-yinll1 e1icttdg1"><svg xmlns="http://www.w3.org/2000/svg" width="16" height="16" viewBox="0 0 24 24" fill="none" stroke="currentColor" stroke-width="2" stroke-linecap="round" stroke-linejoin="round"><path d="M15 7h3a5 5 0 0 1 5 5 5 5 0 0 1-5 5h-3m-6 0H6a5 5 0 0 1-5-5 5 5 0 0 1 5-5h3"></path><line x1="8" y1="12" x2="16" y2="12"></line></svg></a></span></h1></div>
<ul>
<li>This is another important step in the process of community diagnosis.</li>
<li>It requires just as much care as the planning stage.</li>
<li>It involves going out to the field to collect information from the sample population you have selected.</li>
</ul>
<p>There are three stages in data collection:</p>
<ol>
<li>Interviewing the respondents</li>
<li>Data collection</li>
<li>Data handling</li>
</ol>
<p>When the interviewers approach a respondent, they should:</p>
<ul>
<li>Introduce themselves by name</li>
<li>Show their identity cards for the activity</li>
<li>Show their letter of permission to carry out the exercise</li>
<li>Explain why they have come and the purpose of the survey</li>
<li>Establish rapport with the respondent/s so that they can feel at ease with each other</li>
<li>Ask if it is convenient to interview the person at that time</li>
<li>Should the person refuse to cooperate, the interviewers should do their best to persuade such a person to agree</li>
</ul>
<div data-testid="stHeadingWithActionElements" class="st-emotion-cache-1p9ibxm e1icttdg2"><h1 level="1" id="data-analysis">Data Analysis<span data-testid="stHeaderActionElements" class="st-emotion-cache-gi0tri e1icttdg3"><a href="#data-analysis" class="st-emotion-cache-yinll1 e1icttdg1"><svg xmlns="http://www.w3.org/2000/svg" width="16" height="16" viewBox="0 0 24 24" fill="none" stroke="currentColor" stroke-width="2" stroke-linecap="round" stroke-linejoin="round"><path d="M15 7h3a5 5 0 0 1 5 5 5 5 0 0 1-5 5h-3m-6 0H6a5 5 0 0 1-5-5 5 5 0 0 1 5-5h3"></path><line x1="8" y1="12" x2="16" y2="12"></line></svg></a></span></h1></div>
<p>☐ The data you obtain from the field is known as 'raw data'.</p>
<p>☐ In this state, it does not give much information and is therefore difficult to interpret. That is why it needs further work known as data analysis.</p>
<div data-testid="stHeadingWithActionElements" class="st-emotion-cache-1p9ibxm e1icttdg2"><h1 level="1" id="defin">Defin.<span data-testid="stHeaderActionElements" class="st-emotion-cache-gi0tri e1icttdg3"><a href="#defin" class="st-emotion-cache-yinll1 e1icttdg1"><svg xmlns="http://www.w3.org/2000/svg" width="16" height="16" viewBox="0 0 24 24" fill="none" stroke="currentColor" stroke-width="2" stroke-linecap="round" stroke-linejoin="round"><path d="M15 7h3a5 5 0 0 1 5 5 5 5 0 0 1-5 5h-3m-6 0H6a5 5 0 0 1-5-5 5 5 0 0 1 5-5h3"></path><line x1="8" y1="12" x2="16" y2="12"></line></svg></a></span></h1></div>
<p>Data analysis is the separation and categorisation of numerical data into groups in order to understand its meaning.</p>
<p>Statistical methods are used to do this</p>
<p>The process of data analysis involves the following steps:</p>
<ol>
<li>Data cleaning</li>
<li>Sorting or tallying</li>
<li>Coding and entering data</li>
<li>Analysis of results</li>
</ol>
<p>There are two types of analysis that are carried out on data.</p>
<ol>
<li>Qualitative analysis</li>
<li>Quantitative analysis</li>
</ol>
<div data-testid="stHeadingWithActionElements" class="st-emotion-cache-1p9ibxm e1icttdg2"><h1 level="1" id="qualitative-analysis-vs-quantitative-analysis">Qualitative analysis vs Quantitative Analysis<span data-testid="stHeaderActionElements" class="st-emotion-cache-gi0tri e1icttdg3"><a href="#qualitative-analysis-vs-quantitative-analysis" class="st-emotion-cache-yinll1 e1icttdg1"><svg xmlns="http://www.w3.org/2000/svg" width="16" height="16" viewBox="0 0 24 24" fill="none" stroke="currentColor" stroke-width="2" stroke-linecap="round" stroke-linejoin="round"><path d="M15 7h3a5 5 0 0 1 5 5 5 5 0 0 1-5 5h-3m-6 0H6a5 5 0 0 1-5-5 5 5 0 0 1 5-5h3"></path><line x1="8" y1="12" x2="16" y2="12"></line></svg></a></span></h1></div>
<p>Qualitative analysis is usually applied on data which can be counted but cannot be measured, such as, colour. It allows you to analyse the information in a systematic way in order to reach some useful conclusions and recommendations.</p>
<p>Quantitative analysis on the other hand, is usually applied to data that can be given a numerical basis or can be measured, for example, age in years, weight in kilograms.</p>
<div data-testid="stHeadingWithActionElements" class="st-emotion-cache-1p9ibxm e1icttdg2"><h1 level="1" id="data-presentation">Data Presentation<span data-testid="stHeaderActionElements" class="st-emotion-cache-gi0tri e1icttdg3"><a href="#data-presentation" class="st-emotion-cache-yinll1 e1icttdg1"><svg xmlns="http://www.w3.org/2000/svg" width="16" height="16" viewBox="0 0 24 24" fill="none" stroke="currentColor" stroke-width="2" stroke-linecap="round" stroke-linejoin="round"><path d="M15 7h3a5 5 0 0 1 5 5 5 5 0 0 1-5 5h-3m-6 0H6a5 5 0 0 1-5-5 5 5 0 0 1 5-5h3"></path><line x1="8" y1="12" x2="16" y2="12"></line></svg></a></span></h1></div>
<p>The other ways of presenting data are:</p>
<ul>
<li>Tabular presentation- use of tables</li>
<li>Graphical presentation- use of graphs</li>
</ul>
<div data-testid="stHeadingWithActionElements" class="st-emotion-cache-1p9ibxm e1icttdg2"><h1 level="1" id="tabular-presentation">Tabular Presentation<span data-testid="stHeaderActionElements" class="st-emotion-cache-gi0tri e1icttdg3"><a href="#tabular-presentation" class="st-emotion-cache-yinll1 e1icttdg1"><svg xmlns="http://www.w3.org/2000/svg" width="16" height="16" viewBox="0 0 24 24" fill="none" stroke="currentColor" stroke-width="2" stroke-linecap="round" stroke-linejoin="round"><path d="M15 7h3a5 5 0 0 1 5 5 5 5 0 0 1-5 5h-3m-6 0H6a5 5 0 0 1-5-5 5 5 0 0 1 5-5h3"></path><line x1="8" y1="12" x2="16" y2="12"></line></svg></a></span></h1></div>
<p>Various tables that are used to present data, for example;</p>
<p>Frequency distribution table and a contingency table</p>
<p>There are various types of graphical presentations, these include:</p>
<ul>
<li>Histogram</li>
<li>Frequency polygon</li>
<li>Bar graph</li>
<li>Pie chart</li>
<li>Maps</li>
</ul>

<div data-testid="stHeadingWithActionElements" class="st-emotion-cache-1p9ibxm e1icttdg2"><h1 level="1" id="feedback-and-report-writing">Feedback and Report Writing<span data-testid="stHeaderActionElements" class="st-emotion-cache-gi0tri e1icttdg3"><a href="#feedback-and-report-writing" class="st-emotion-cache-yinll1 e1icttdg1"><svg xmlns="http://www.w3.org/2000/svg" width="16" height="16" viewBox="0 0 24 24" fill="none" stroke="currentColor" stroke-width="2" stroke-linecap="round" stroke-linejoin="round"><path d="M15 7h3a5 5 0 0 1 5 5 5 5 0 0 1-5 5h-3m-6 0H6a5 5 0 0 1-5-5 5 5 0 0 1 5-5h3"></path><line x1="8" y1="12" x2="16" y2="12"></line></svg></a></span></h1></div>
<ul>
<li>After survey is done, those in the community who cooperated with you are entitled to receive some form of feedback.</li>
<li>They want to know what you found.</li>
<li>Feedback means giving comments about how well or badly a person is doing in order to help them do better. It is a form of communication.</li>
</ul>
<p>All individuals in the community who are concerned with the health of the people are entitled to feedback.</p>
<div data-testid="stHeadingWithActionElements" class="st-emotion-cache-1p9ibxm e1icttdg2"><h1 level="1" id="types-of-feedback">Types of Feedback<span data-testid="stHeaderActionElements" class="st-emotion-cache-gi0tri e1icttdg3"><a href="#types-of-feedback" class="st-emotion-cache-yinll1 e1icttdg1"><svg xmlns="http://www.w3.org/2000/svg" width="16" height="16" viewBox="0 0 24 24" fill="none" stroke="currentColor" stroke-width="2" stroke-linecap="round" stroke-linejoin="round"><path d="M15 7h3a5 5 0 0 1 5 5 5 5 0 0 1-5 5h-3m-6 0H6a5 5 0 0 1-5-5 5 5 0 0 1 5-5h3"></path><line x1="8" y1="12" x2="16" y2="12"></line></svg></a></span></h1></div>
<div data-testid="stHeadingWithActionElements" class="st-emotion-cache-1p9ibxm e1icttdg2"><h2 level="2" id="1-individual-results">1. Individual Results<span data-testid="stHeaderActionElements" class="st-emotion-cache-gi0tri e1icttdg3"><a href="#1-individual-results" class="st-emotion-cache-yinll1 e1icttdg1"><svg xmlns="http://www.w3.org/2000/svg" width="16" height="16" viewBox="0 0 24 24" fill="none" stroke="currentColor" stroke-width="2" stroke-linecap="round" stroke-linejoin="round"><path d="M15 7h3a5 5 0 0 1 5 5 5 5 0 0 1-5 5h-3m-6 0H6a5 5 0 0 1-5-5 5 5 0 0 1 5-5h3"></path><line x1="8" y1="12" x2="16" y2="12"></line></svg></a></span></h2></div>
<p>The individuals who formed part of your survey sample as well as those who provided you with specimens and tests deserve to be given feedback on your findings.</p>
<ol start="2">
<li>Preliminary Report</li>
</ol>
<ul>
<li>This report covers the general impressions of the community’s health status as well as preliminary survey findings.</li>
<li>This goes to the community in which the diagnosis was conducted in order to thank them for their cooperation.</li>
<li>It is given soon after the field work is completed by arranging meetings for the people and their leaders.</li>
</ul>
<p>Give them information on obvious points like disease outbreak, hygiene, sanitation, attitudes, nutrition, practices and utilisation of health services.</p>
<p>You should promise them a more detailed report later.</p>
<p>By giving them the findings of the report, you increase their awareness of their real health needs and how these can be solved.</p>
<ol start="3">
<li>Non-Medical Reports</li>
</ol>
<ul>
<li>This report is less scientific and is usually produced for non-medical people.</li>
<li>It comes out <strong>after</strong> full analysis has been done and conclusions and recommendations have been formulated.</li>
</ul>
<p>The contents of this report should cover the following aspects of the survey:</p>
<ul>
<li>The reasons for doing the community diagnosis</li>
<li>The findings which help to define the situation.</li>
</ul>
<p>This report is a social or political tool to shed light on the improvements that need to be done in the community's health services.</p>
<p>So make your recommendations practical.</p>
<ol start="4">
<li>The Medical or Community Diagnosis Report</li>
</ol>
<p>Is a detailed scientific report which provides an account of the planning and execution of the survey as well as the results.</p>
<p>It should present the data collected fully and adequately and give accurate interpretations of the analyses.</p>
<p>A well written community diagnosis report is made up of distinct sections or components which fall under the following headings:</p>
<p>o Title
o Table of contents
o Lists of tables and figures
o List of abbreviations and acronyms
o Acknowledgements
o Introduction
o Aims and objectives of the study</p>
<p>Good objectives should be ‘SMART’:
-Specific -Realistic
-Timely -Measurable
-Attainable</p>

<div data-testid="stHeadingWithActionElements" class="st-emotion-cache-1p9ibxm e1icttdg2"><h1 level="1" id="community-health-action">Community Health Action<span data-testid="stHeaderActionElements" class="st-emotion-cache-gi0tri e1icttdg3"><a href="#community-health-action" class="st-emotion-cache-yinll1 e1icttdg1"><svg xmlns="http://www.w3.org/2000/svg" width="16" height="16" viewBox="0 0 24 24" fill="none" stroke="currentColor" stroke-width="2" stroke-linecap="round" stroke-linejoin="round"><path d="M15 7h3a5 5 0 0 1 5 5 5 5 0 0 1-5 5h-3m-6 0H6a5 5 0 0 1-5-5 5 5 0 0 1 5-5h3"></path><line x1="8" y1="12" x2="16" y2="12"></line></svg></a></span></h1></div>
<p>Sit down with the community to prioritize and plan what you going to do about the identified health problems i.e. you need to mobilize them to take action.</p>
<div data-testid="stHeadingWithActionElements" class="st-emotion-cache-1p9ibxm e1icttdg2"><h1 level="1" id="mobilse-community-action">Mobilse Community Action<span data-testid="stHeaderActionElements" class="st-emotion-cache-gi0tri e1icttdg3"><a href="#mobilse-community-action" class="st-emotion-cache-yinll1 e1icttdg1"><svg xmlns="http://www.w3.org/2000/svg" width="16" height="16" viewBox="0 0 24 24" fill="none" stroke="currentColor" stroke-width="2" stroke-linecap="round" stroke-linejoin="round"><path d="M15 7h3a5 5 0 0 1 5 5 5 5 0 0 1-5 5h-3m-6 0H6a5 5 0 0 1-5-5 5 5 0 0 1 5-5h3"></path><line x1="8" y1="12" x2="16" y2="12"></line></svg></a></span></h1></div>
<p>You can mobilise the community through a number of interventions, namely:</p>
<ol>
<li>Making them aware of their problems and promoting primary health care</li>
<li>Health education</li>
<li>Immunisation</li>
<li>Environmental improvement</li>
</ol>
