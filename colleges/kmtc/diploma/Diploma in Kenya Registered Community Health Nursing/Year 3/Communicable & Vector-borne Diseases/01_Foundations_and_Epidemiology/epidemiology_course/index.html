Epidemiology Course

Duration: 15 hours Level: Intermediate

Course Overview

This course provides a comprehensive introduction to epidemiology, covering its fundamental concepts, historical development, disease causation models, transmission dynamics, and key measures of morbidity and mortality. It is designed for students at the Kenya Medical Training College and others interested in public health.

  • Analyze the natural history of disease and its stages.

  • Describe vital statistics and demographics and how they are used.

Course Modules

  1. Epidemiology: Concept, Types, and History

    Introduces the fundamental concepts, types, and historical evolution of epidemiology.

    2 hours

  2. Disease Causation, Models, and Hill's Criteria

    Explores disease causation, models of causation, and Bradford Hill's criteria for causal inference.

    2 hours

  3. Disease Transmission and Natural History

    Covers the dynamics of disease transmission, the natural history of disease, and general disease processes.

    2 hours

  4. Epidemic Occurrence and Epidemiologic Approach

    Examines chain of infection, epidemic disease occurrence and the epidemiological approach to disease investigation.

    2 hours

  5. Uses of Epidemiology and Measures of Morbidity

    Explores the various uses of epidemiology, vital statistics, and introduces measures of morbidity.

    3 hours

  6. Prevalence and Incidence

    Discusses about measures of morbidity which include prevalence and incidence of disease.

    2 hours

  7. Measures of Mortality and Fertility

    Discusses measures of mortality and measures of fertility.

    2 hours

Prerequisites

  • Basic understanding of medical terminology.
  • Interest in public health and disease prevention.
