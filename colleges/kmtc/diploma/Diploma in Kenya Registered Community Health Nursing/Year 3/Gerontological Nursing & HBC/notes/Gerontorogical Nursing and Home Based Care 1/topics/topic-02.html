Definition of Home Based Care

üìñ

Core Definition

Home based care is the care of persons with chronic condition or terminally ill extended from the health facility to patient home through family and community participation with available resources and collaboration with health care professionals in order to enhance quality of life of the patient and the family.

HBC concerns those with advanced disease and cannot take care of themselves and those with chronic conditions and are able to take care of themselves.

Chronic Disease Characteristics

An illness is said to be chronic if it meets the following criteria:

  • It is permanent.
  • Leaves a residual disability.
  • Caused by non-reversible pathological conditions.
  • Requires long-term supervision.
  • Requires special rehabilitative training of the patient.
ü¶†

Examples of Chronic Diseases

Diabetes, Hypertension, Sickle cell disease, Renal disease, Heart disease, Terminal carcinoma, HIV.

üß© Components & Objectives

Components of HBC

‚ñ∂ 1. Nursing Care

Extend nursing care services up to the family level by training family and community members. Services include:

  • Personal hygiene (including environmental hygiene).
  • Infection prevention techniques.
  • Physical therapy.
  • Pain management.
  • Drug administration.
  • Nutrition.
  • Taking patient to hospital when need arise.
  • Reassuring clients at all times.
‚ñ∂ 2. Nutrition

Metabolism changes in elderly persons, chronic conditions, degenerative changes, and medications interfere with nutrition. Good nutrition maintains body strength and stimulates the immune system.

  • Involve nutritionist in care of the chronically ill.
  • Inform patient and family that balanced diet improves body functioning.
  • Counsel client/family on meeting nutritional demand as per social status.
  • Address factors that hinder food intake (e.g., mouth ulcers, difficulty chewing).
‚ñ∂ 3. Clinical Care

Involves continuation of medical care at home. The family can take patient to clinic for review or look for a doctor visit. It involves collaboration of health care provider, family, and community.

  • HBC follows proper diagnosis.
  • Involves earlier detection and treatment of complications/opportunistic infections.
  • Involves drug adherence.
‚ñ∂ 4. Counseling & Psycho-spiritual Care

Help patient to forgive others, allow religious leaders to offer support. It reduces stress and anxiety to both client and family members. Helps client to plan for future and deal with problems.

‚ñ∂ 5. Social Support & Rehabilitation

Social Support: Enables patient to meet physiologic needs, sense of love and belonging, and personal security.

Rehabilitative Services: Help a person regain physical and mental abilities lost due to disease/injury.

Objectives of HBC Program

  1. To facilitate the continuity of care of the client/patient from facility to the home and community.
  2. To reduce burden of getting co-morbidities.
  3. Help patient accept and prepare for death.
  4. To mobilize for the resources necessary to sustain care of the patient.
  5. To facilitate quality community care.
  6. To streamline patient referral to and from hospitals, social facilities and community organizations.
  7. To raise acceptability of the terminally ill patient by the community hence reducing stigma.
  8. To empower client/family/community with knowledge to ensure long term care and support.

Principles of HBC

  • Take multi-sector approach in care.
  • Ensure respect for basic human rights (right of patient to retain control over own body).
  • Ensure appropriate cost effective access to quality health care.
  • Encourage active participation of the client and family members.
  • Minimize the physical and psychological exhaustion of the care taker.
  • Motivate active participation of influential community people.

üåü Advantages of Home Based Care

‚ñ∂ A) To the Patient/Client
  • Close to family, relatives and friends thus promotes love and belonging.
  • More privacy at home.
  • Culture, values and beliefs are better taken care of (e.g., dress, food).
  • Close contact with family enhances acceptance of condition.
  • Cost effective (reduces hospital bills).
  • Reduces risks of co-morbidities (hospital acquired infections).
  • Home is quiet and more peaceful.
  • Retain control over their own life.
  • Familiar environment reduces stress and anxiety.
‚ñ∂ B) To the Family
  • Less expensive compared to hospital care.
  • Family learns about the condition, improving care.
  • Improves family bonding.
  • Reduces cost of regular trips to hospital.
  • Family can spend last days with loved ones.
  • Promotes holistic care approach.
  • Family can attend to other responsibilities at home.
‚ñ∂ C) To the Community & Health System

Community:

  • Awareness of conditions promotes community togetherness.
  • Reduces cost of visiting sick person in hospital.
  • Community gets involved in caring for their own.

Health Care System:

  • Best alternative where there is no access to hospital.
  • Reduces pressure on hospital resources.
  • Reduces financial pressure.
  • Reduces hospital acquired infections.

üë• Providers & Roles

Providers of HBC

The Patient, Family members/care givers, Health care workers, Home care team, Community organizations, Government.

Roles of Providers

Provider Key Responsibilities
1. The Patient Main player. Provides contact info. Provides safe home/security. Takes care of self when able. Identifies caregiver. Participates in decision making.
2. Family / Caregiver Accept patient condition. Collaborate with care providers. Provide security. Consult with patient. Encourage will writing. Being present as support.
3. Home Care Team Provide home nursing care. Supportive counseling. Refer patient. Arrange spiritual care. Mobilize resources. Train patient/caregiver. Supervise caregiver.
4. Health Care Workers Make initial diagnosis. Identify needs. Care for terminally ill. Initiate referral. Market HBC services. Prepare/educate family.
5. The Community Accept patient condition. Collaborate. Prepare memory book. Identify spiritual needs. Initiative to prevent disease spread.
6. The Government Develop policies. Provide training. Provide drugs/commodities. Help form support groups.

üì¢ Community Mobilization

Definition: Process of involving and motivating people to take action in order to accomplish a task. Involves community members forming groups and working towards problem solving.

Importance

  • Prepare community for participation.
  • Create awareness about chronic infections.
  • Identify problems together and seek solutions.
  • Ownership and sustainability.
  • Identifying local resources.
‚ö†Ô∏è

Hindrances to Mobilization

Lack of information, lack of involvement in identification, lack of resources, lack of knowledge of partners, social differences, poor infrastructure, lack of social structures, poor leadership.

Steps in Community Mobilization

  1. Planning and organizing self: Understand HBC, know mobilizers, confirm appointments.
  2. Enter the community: Show respect to members.
  3. Conduct mobilization session: Greet per culture, find out knowledge, give correct info, allow expression of fears.
  4. Monitor response: Acknowledge positive/negative response, give feedback to authority.

üéí Resources & Networking

Community Resources (The 4 Ms)

1. Money

Financial security is essential. Sources: savings, family, contributions (harambees), Government, insurance.

2. Materials

Food, water, supportive devices (wheelchair, walker). Provided by community, hospital, NGOs.

3. Minutes (Time)

Caring is time consuming. Relatives/friends must spare time to help and stay together.

4. Manpower

Volunteers who spare time. Health workers, family, relatives, leaders, community volunteers.

Networking & Referral

Networking: Group of individuals working together to strengthen and achieve a goal. Promotes unity, learning, problem solving, and reduces duplication.

Referral: Two way process of linking a client from one caring service to another.

  • Reasons for referral: When resources are insufficient, caregiver burnout, need for competent management, specialized care, or patient wish.
