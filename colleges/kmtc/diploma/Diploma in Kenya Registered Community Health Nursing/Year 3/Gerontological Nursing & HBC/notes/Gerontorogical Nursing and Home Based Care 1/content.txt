<div data-testid="stHeadingWithActionElements" class="st-emotion-cache-1p9ibxm e1icttdg2"><h1 level="1" id="theories-of-aging">THEORIES OF AGING<span data-testid="stHeaderActionElements" class="st-emotion-cache-gi0tri e1icttdg3"><a href="#theories-of-aging" class="st-emotion-cache-yinll1 e1icttdg1"><svg xmlns="http://www.w3.org/2000/svg" width="16" height="16" viewBox="0 0 24 24" fill="none" stroke="currentColor" stroke-width="2" stroke-linecap="round" stroke-linejoin="round"><path d="M15 7h3a5 5 0 0 1 5 5 5 5 0 0 1-5 5h-3m-6 0H6a5 5 0 0 1-5-5 5 5 0 0 1 5-5h3"></path><line x1="8" y1="12" x2="16" y2="12"></line></svg></a></span></h1></div>

<div data-testid="stHeadingWithActionElements" class="st-emotion-cache-1p9ibxm e1icttdg2"><h1 level="1" id="1-disengagement-theory">1. DISENGAGEMENT THEORY<span data-testid="stHeaderActionElements" class="st-emotion-cache-gi0tri e1icttdg3"><a href="#1-disengagement-theory" class="st-emotion-cache-yinll1 e1icttdg1"><svg xmlns="http://www.w3.org/2000/svg" width="16" height="16" viewBox="0 0 24 24" fill="none" stroke="currentColor" stroke-width="2" stroke-linecap="round" stroke-linejoin="round"><path d="M15 7h3a5 5 0 0 1 5 5 5 5 0 0 1-5 5h-3m-6 0H6a5 5 0 0 1-5-5 5 5 0 0 1 5-5h3"></path><line x1="8" y1="12" x2="16" y2="12"></line></svg></a></span></h1></div>
<ul>
<li>Was established by ( Elaine, 1961)</li>
<li>It states that over time elderly people withdraw from the social roles and relationships that were central to their life in adulthood.</li>
<li>They become inactive and withdrawn.</li>
<li>Transfer of power and responsibility to younger persons is done to replace the old from work place.</li>
</ul>
<p>cont</p>
<ul>
<li>
<p>The theory claims that it is natural and acceptable for older adult to withdraw from societal roles and the society eases away from them.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>It says elderly people do not need to be active.</p>
</li>
<li></li>
</ul>

<ul>
<li>Was developed as a reaction to</li>
<li>disengagement theory.</li>
<li>It says that persons who are active will be more satisfied and better adjusted than those who have disengaged.</li>
<li>Optimal ageing is achieved when individuals engage in activities.</li>
</ul>

<div data-testid="stHeadingWithActionElements" class="st-emotion-cache-1p9ibxm e1icttdg2"><h1 level="1" id="community-mobilization-for-hbc">COMMUNITY MOBILIZATION FOR HBC<span data-testid="stHeaderActionElements" class="st-emotion-cache-gi0tri e1icttdg3"><a href="#community-mobilization-for-hbc" class="st-emotion-cache-yinll1 e1icttdg1"><svg xmlns="http://www.w3.org/2000/svg" width="16" height="16" viewBox="0 0 24 24" fill="none" stroke="currentColor" stroke-width="2" stroke-linecap="round" stroke-linejoin="round"><path d="M15 7h3a5 5 0 0 1 5 5 5 5 0 0 1-5 5h-3m-6 0H6a5 5 0 0 1-5-5 5 5 0 0 1 5-5h3"></path><line x1="8" y1="12" x2="16" y2="12"></line></svg></a></span></h1></div>
<ul>
<li>
<p>Definition</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Community mobilization is the process of involving and motivating people to take action in order to accomplish a task.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>It involves members of the community forming groups and working towards problem solving.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Communities are required to initiate and sustain activities which will support HBC services to members with chronic or terminally ill disease</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>They must take part in planning, organizing, implementing and monitoring activities associated with home based care</p>
</li>
</ul>

<li>Prepare the community for participation</li>
<li>Create awareness about the problem; chronic infections.</li>
<li>Identify problems together with the community and seek means of solving them.</li>
<li>Ownership and sustainability of the program</li>
</ul>

<div data-testid="stHeadingWithActionElements" class="st-emotion-cache-1p9ibxm e1icttdg2"><h1 level="1" id="community-resources">COMMUNITY RESOURCES<span data-testid="stHeaderActionElements" class="st-emotion-cache-gi0tri e1icttdg3"><a href="#community-resources" class="st-emotion-cache-yinll1 e1icttdg1"><svg xmlns="http://www.w3.org/2000/svg" width="16" height="16" viewBox="0 0 24 24" fill="none" stroke="currentColor" stroke-width="2" stroke-linecap="round" stroke-linejoin="round"><path d="M15 7h3a5 5 0 0 1 5 5 5 5 0 0 1-5 5h-3m-6 0H6a5 5 0 0 1-5-5 5 5 0 0 1 5-5h3"></path><line x1="8" y1="12" x2="16" y2="12"></line></svg></a></span></h1></div>
<ul>
<li>To effectively provide HBC activities, you need the following resources;</li>
<li>Money</li>
<li>Materials</li>
<li>Minutes(time)</li>
<li>Man power</li>
</ul>
<ul>
<li>
<p>a)Money</p>
<ul>
<li>
<p>Financial security is essential in HBC.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Sources of finance will include; individual savings, family, community contributions in form of harambees, Government, insurance</p>
</li>
</ul>
</li>
</ul>
<ul>
<li>b)Materials</li>
<li>Can be in form of food, water, supportive devices such as wheelchair, walker</li>
<li>Can be provided by community members,hospital,NGO</li>
</ul>
<p>c)Minutes</p>
<ul>
<li>
<p>Caring for terminally or critically ill patient for long time is time consuming and needs patience as it is stressful.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Relatives, friends should understand the importance of sparing time not only to help patient and family members but also to stay together.</p>
</li>
<li></li>
</ul>
<p>d)Man power</p>
<ul>
<li>
<p>It involves individuals who spare their time to voluntarily help the patient and family.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>They include; health workers, family members, relatives and friends, community leaders, community volunteers</p>
</li>
<li></li>
</ul>
