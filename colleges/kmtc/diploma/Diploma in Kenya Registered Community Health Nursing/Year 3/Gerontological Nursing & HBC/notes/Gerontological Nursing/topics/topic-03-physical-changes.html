‚ù§Ô∏è Cardiovascular Changes

Structural & Functional Changes

  • The Heart: Muscles become rigid (loss of elasticity), contracting poorly. This affects output, especially during activity.
  • Apex Position: Misplacement of the heart's apex occurs due to kyphoscoliosis (bent back). The apex beat may be missed at the normal anatomical position.
  • Murmurs: Diastolic murmurs at the base of the heart are common due to aortic valve hardening.
  • Heart Rate: Does not change significantly with age.
ü©∏ Circulatory Changes

Arterial Circulation: Arteries become rigid, increasing resistance to blood flow, which causes a rise in blood pressure.

Peripheral Vessels: Easy to palpate due to arterial narrowing and loss of connective tissue. They feel tortuous and rigid. Extremities may be cold and discolored.

Common Conditions: Hypertension, Congestive Cardiac Failure (CCF), Myocardial Infarction, Arteriosclerosis, Thrombo-phlebitis.

ü´Å Respiratory System Changes

Aging affects lung capacity and efficiency significantly:

  • Pulmonary Flow: Decreased blood flow leads to poor gaseous exchange and diffusion.
  • Volumes: Increased residual lung volume; reduced vital capacity.
  • Structure: Increased anterior-posterior diameter of the chest.
  • Muscles: Degeneration of accessory muscles and increased chest wall rigidity reduce breathing efficiency.
  • Reflexes: Decreased cough reflex, increasing aspiration risk.

üôå Integumentary Changes

Component Change
Texture Loss of elasticity, wrinkling, dryness. Cooler to touch with reduced sweating.
Fat Distribution Less subcutaneous fat on extremities; more accumulation on the trunk.
Hair Greying/whitening. Thinning on scalp. Women may develop chin/lip hair.
Nails Decreased growth rate.

üöª Genito-Urinary & Reproductive

Genito-Urinary System

  • Kidney Function: Reduced glomerular filtration rate (GFR) due to decreased cardiac output. Risk of protein loss.
  • Micturition (Men): Frequency may increase due to prostatic enlargement.
  • Micturition (Women): Urgency and stress incontinence due to changes in perineal muscle tone.
  • General: Nocturia increases for both sexes. Incontinence increases with advanced age.

Reproductive System

‚ñ∂ Female Changes

Reduction in estrogen causes atrophy of breasts, ovaries, uterus, and vaginal epithelial lining.

  • Vaginal canal narrows and shortens.
  • Reduced secretions and increased alkalinity lead to higher rates of vaginitis.
‚ñ∂ Male Changes

Decrease in testosterone production leads to decreased sexual drive.

  • Testes decrease in size.
  • Sperm count decreases.
  • Viscosity of seminal fluid diminishes.
05

ü¶¥ Musculoskeletal Changes

  • Muscles: Decreased mass leading to reduced physical strength.
  • Bones: Reduction in calcium and phosphorus causes porosity (osteoporosis) and weakness. Height shortens due to intervertebral space narrowing.
  • Joints: Become less flexible and mobile with reduced range of motion.
06

üçΩÔ∏è Digestive System

  • Oral Cavity: Partial/total tooth loss and malocclusion impair mastication.
  • Swallowing: Difficult due to diminished salivary secretions.
  • Digestion: Decreased production of digestive enzymes.
  • Motility: Sluggish peristalsis leads to constipation. Decreased esophageal peristalsis increases risk of hiatus hernia.
07

üß† Nervous & Sensory Systems

Neurological Function

  • General decrease in response to stimuli.
  • Slowed reflexes.
  • Decline in ability to learn new skills.
  • Memory: Recent memory difficulty is common; past memory is often preserved.
  • Sleep: Stay in bed longer but sleep less.

Sensory Changes

Vision Decreased accommodation (presbyopia), lens opacity/cataracts, reduced lacrimation, poor dark adaptation, diminished color perception.
Hearing Progressive decrease in auditory threshold, especially for high frequencies (presbycusis). May lead to suspiciousness.
Smell & Taste Decline in sensitivity to smells. Decrease in taste buds may lead to complaints about food.
Touch & Proprioception Decreased sensitivity (but sense does not diminish entirely). Increased pain threshold. Impaired position sense affects balance.
üìå Psychosocial Tasks

Despite physical changes, older people face specific tasks:

  • Getting along with children/grandchildren.
  • Dealing with own death.
  • Desire to leave a legacy.
  • Maintaining attachment to familiar objects.
