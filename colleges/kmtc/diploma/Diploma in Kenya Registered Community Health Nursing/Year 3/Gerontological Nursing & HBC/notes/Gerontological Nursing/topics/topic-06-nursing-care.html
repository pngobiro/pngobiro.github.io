üè† Care in the Community

The Family as Caregivers

Planning of care is primarily done with the family. Adult children often assume responsibilities, providing financial, social, and psychological support.

‚ö†Ô∏è Caregiver Strain

Despite support, prolonged caregiving can lead to strain. A declining child-parent relationship, especially when the parent is in poor health, can lead to elder abuse (physical violence, neglect, financial exploitation, or denial of healthcare).

Home Environment

Safety and Comfort

  • Fall Prevention: Falls are caused by vision loss, balance issues, and cardiovascular changes.
  • Lighting: Adequate lighting with minimal glare is essential.
  • Adaptations: Install supportive bars in bathrooms.

Personal Space

Older persons need private space to keep life treasures. Even if moved, they should be allowed to hold, touch, and enjoy familiar objects to maintain comfort.

ü•ó Nutritional & Elimination Needs

Nutritional Needs

Requirement: At least 1 gm of protein per kg body weight (increased if sick).
  • Challenges: Reduced hunger (poor taste/smell), slowed absorption, loneliness reducing motivation to cook.
  • Diet: Rich in protein, carbohydrates, minerals, vitamins, and adequate fluids.
  • Texture: Soft food is preferred due to dentures/chewing difficulties and swallowing problems.

Elimination Needs

‚ñ∂ Bowel Function

Constipation: Common issue. Reassure that daily movements may not be necessary.

Management: Encourage exercise, fiber (fruits/vegetables), and water. Avoid dependency on laxatives/stool softeners.

‚ñ∂ Bladder Function

Incontinence: Can be managed by bladder retraining schedules (short intervals gradually lengthened). Avoid catheters to prevent infection.

Retention: Common in men due to prostatic enlargement. Requires assessment/surgery.

üõÅ Hygiene & Physical Care

Skin Care
Skin is delicate. Use oils/lotions to keep soft and promote circulation. Bathe less often with mild soap (sweat/oil glands are less active). Keep incontinent patients clean and dry to prevent breakdown.

Nails
Finger nails become hard and brittle. Soak in warm water or use lotion before cutting. Keep short.

Oral Hygiene
Encourage care of mouth and dentures.

Safety & Activity
Use restraints (side rails) cautiously. Use adaptive devices for baths/toilets. Encourage activity to maintain circulation and muscle tone.

üß† Mental Health Problems

Common Conditions

‚ñ∂ Anxiety

Major problem due to threats to self-esteem, health, and independence. Can manifest as withdrawal, isolation, confusion, or combativeness.

‚ñ∂ Depression

Affects ~50% of elderly; high suicide risk. Causes include chemical imbalance, poverty, loss of spouse, chronic illness, and lack of stimulation.

Note: Antidepressants have adverse side effects in the elderly; monitor closely and reduce dosage.

‚ñ∂ Chemical Dependency

Alcohol: Often due to loneliness or boredom. Manage via gradual withdrawal.

Prescription Drugs: Misuse via self-medication or multiple doctors. Manage via education on proper use and side effects.

Therapeutic Approaches

  • Re-motivation technique
  • Recreation & Social activities
  • Pet therapy
  • Religious support
  • Continued learning
