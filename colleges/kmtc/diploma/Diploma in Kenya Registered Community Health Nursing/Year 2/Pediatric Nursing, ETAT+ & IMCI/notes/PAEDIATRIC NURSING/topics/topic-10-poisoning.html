üõ°Ô∏è General Principles

Poisoning can be accidental (common in young children) or suicidal (older children/adolescents). Management focuses on stabilization, decontamination, and supportive care.

Initial Stabilization

  • ABC: Maintain Airway, Breathing, and Circulation.
  • Fluids: IV fluids and vasopressors for hypotension.
  • Dextrose: IV dextrose for altered consciousness (hypoglycemia).
  • Naloxone: For apnea/opiod overdose.
  • Thiamine: Before dextrose if thiamine deficiency suspected.

Decontamination & Treatment

Method Indication/Action
Topical Flush body surface/eyes with large amounts of water. Remove contaminated clothing.
Activated Charcoal For serious oral poisonings (binds toxin in GI tract).
Gastric Lavage Rarely used (only if life-threatening and recent ingestion).
Whole Bowel Irrigation For heavy metals, drug packets (body packers).
Dialysis For Salicylates, Theophylline.

üíä Common Poisons

Acetaminophen (Paracetamol)

Causes gastroenteritis initially, followed by hepatotoxicity 1-3 days later.

  • Symptoms: Nausea, vomiting, RUQ pain, jaundice, confusion, stupor.
  • Diagnosis: Serum levels plotted on Rumack-Matthew nomogram.
  • Treatment: N-acetylcysteine (Oral or IV). Activated charcoal if recent ingestion.

Carbon Monoxide (CO)

  • Symptoms: Headache, nausea, weakness, angina, dyspnea, cherry-red skin (rare), coma.
  • Diagnosis: Carboxyhemoglobin levels.
  • Treatment: High-flow supplemental Oxygen.

Aspirin (Salicylates)

  • Symptoms: Vomiting, tinnitus (ringing in ears), confusion, hyperthermia, respiratory alkalosis followed by metabolic acidosis.
  • Treatment: Activated charcoal, Alkaline diuresis (bicarbonate), Hemodialysis for severe cases.

üêú Organophosphates & Carbamates

Common insecticides that inhibit cholinesterase, leading to acetylcholine accumulation.

Cholinergic Toxidrome (Muscarinic)

SLUDGE: Salivation, Lacrimation, Urination, Diarrhea, GI distress, Emesis.
Also: Bronchorrhea, bronchospasm, bradycardia, miosis (pinpoint pupils).

Management

  • Atropine: High doses titrated to dry secretions (relieves bronchospasm/bronchorrhea).
  • Pralidoxime (2-PAM): Reactivates cholinesterase (treats neuromuscular symptoms like weakness/fasciculations).

‚õΩ Hydrocarbons & Lead

Hydrocarbons

Sources: Gasoline, Kerosene, Turpentine, Paint thinner.

Major Risk: Aspiration pneumonia (Chemical pneumonia). Symptoms include choking, gagging, coughing, tachypnea, cyanosis, retractions.

üö´

Contraindication

DO NOT INDUCE VOMITING. Emesis increases risk of aspiration. Gastric lavage is generally contraindicated unless airway is protected (cuffed ET tube).

Treatment: Symptomatic. High humidity oxygen, hydration, antibiotics for secondary infection.

Lead Poisoning (Plumbism)

Can cause acute encephalopathy or irreversible organ damage. Cognitive deficits are a common long-term complication in children.

  • Diagnosis: Whole blood lead level.
  • Treatment: Stop exposure. Chelation therapy for high levels.

üö∏ Prevention Strategies

  • Clearly label household products and prescription drugs.
  • Store drugs/toxins in locked cabinets inaccessible to children.
  • Promptly dispose of expired drugs.
  • Use carbon monoxide detectors.
  • Use safety caps and child-proof containers.
