üìä WHO Clinical Staging

Clinical staging helps in assessing the severity of HIV infection in infants and children.

‚ñ∂ Stage I (Asymptomatic)
  • Asymptomatic
  • Persistent generalized lymphadenopathy (PGL)
  • Unexplained, asymptomatic Hepatosplenomegaly
‚ñ∂ Stage II (Mild Symptoms)
  • Papular pruritic eruptions (PPE)
  • Seborrheic dermatitis
  • Fungal nail infections
  • Angular cheilitis / Linear gingival erythema
  • Extensive HPV or molluscum (>5% body area)
  • Recurrent oral ulcerations (>2 in 6 months)
  • Parotid enlargement
  • Herpes zoster (>1 episode/12 months)
  • Recurrent/chronic URI (otitis media, sinusitis)
‚ñ∂ Stage III (Advanced Symptoms)
  • Unexplained moderate malnutrition (-2SD)
  • Unexplained persistent diarrhea (>14 days)
  • Unexplained persistent fever (>1 month)
  • Oral candidiasis (outside neonatal period)
  • Oral hairy Leucoplakia
  • Pulmonary tuberculosis
  • Severe recurrent bacterial pneumonia (>2/12 months)
  • Lymphoid interstitial pneumonitis (LIP)
  • Unexplained anaemia, neutropenia, or thrombocytopenia
‚ñ∂ Stage IV (Severe Symptoms / AIDS)
  • Unexplained severe wasting (-3SD)
  • Pneumocystis pneumonia (PCP)
  • Recurrent severe bacterial infections (excluding pneumonia)
  • Chronic orolabial/cutaneous HSV (>1 month)
  • Extra-pulmonary tuberculosis
  • Kaposi‚Äôs sarcoma
  • Esophageal candidiasis
  • CNS toxoplasmosis / Cryptococcal meningitis
  • HIV encephalopathy

Birth Testing Protocol

HIV DNA PCR should be done at birth or first contact (within 2 weeks) for infants born to HIV-positive mothers.

Priority Groups for Birth Testing

  • Mother on ART < 6 months.
  • Mother‚Äôs viral load (VL) ≥ 1,000 copies/mL before delivery.
  • No maternal VL available within past 6 months.
  • Suspected ART failure.
  • Pre-term or Low birth-weight infants.

Algorithm

PCR Result Action
Negative Continue infant ARV prophylaxis.
Repeat HIV DNA PCR at 6 weeks.
Follow HEI (HIV Exposed Infant) guidelines.
Positive Start ART immediately.
Take confirmatory PCR & baseline VL.
If 2nd PCR is positive: Confirm infection.
If 2nd PCR is negative (discordant): Continue ART, send 3rd sample.

üó£Ô∏è Counseling & Testing Services

Age-Appropriate Counseling

  • < 6 years: Focus on engaging caregivers.
  • 6-12 years: Involve both child and caregiver. Use drawing/play for younger children.
  • Disclosure: Commence by 5 years; complete by 10-12 years.

HIV Testing Services (HTS) Package

  1. Pre-test Session: Risk assessment, consent, introduction.
  2. Test: Using approved rapid HIV antibody test kit.
  3. Post-test Session:
    • Negative: Risk reduction plan, linkage to prevention services.
    • Positive: Enrollment in care, partner/family testing, positive living counseling.

üíä Management & Prophylaxis

PMTCT Nevirapine (NVP) Prophylaxis

Duration: Initiate immediately for 12 weeks.

Age Dose (Once Daily)
0 - 6 weeks 10 mg (1ml) if < 2.5kg
15 mg (1.5ml) if > 2.5kg
6 - 14 weeks 20 mg (2mls)
14 weeks - 6 months 25 mg (2.5mls)
6 - 9 months 30 mg (3mls)

CTX Prophylaxis: All HIV exposed/infected infants start Co-trimoxazole at 6 weeks.

ART Regimens

Age / Weight Regimen
< 2 weeks AZT + 3TC + NVP
2 weeks - 4 weeks ABC + 3TC + LPV/r
4 weeks - 3 years ABC + 3TC + LPV/r
3 - 15 years (< 35 kg) ABC + 3TC + EFV
3 - 15 years (≥ 35 kg) TDF + 3TC + EFV
> 15 years TDF + 3TC + EFV

Note: PWID > 15 years: TDF + 3TC + ATV/r
