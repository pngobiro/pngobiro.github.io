ü§í Upper Respiratory Infections

Common Cold (Coryza)

The commonest infection in children, primarily caused by viruses (Rhinovirus, Coronavirus, RSV).

  • Manifestations: Clear or mucopurulent nasal discharge, nasal blockage.
  • Management: Self-limiting. Pain/fever treated with paracetamol/ibuprofen. Saline nasal drops. Antibiotics are NOT beneficial.

Streptococcal Sore Throat (Pharyngitis)

Inflammation of the pharynx and soft palate. Etiology is 90% viral, but can be bacterial (Group A Beta hemolytic streptococci).

Management

Treat with oral penicillin/Amoxicillin or IM penicillin to prevent Rheumatic fever. Erythromycin for allergic children. Warm saline gargles for older children.

Tonsillitis

Extensive inflammation of the palatine tonsils, often with purulent inflammation.

‚ñ∂ Tonsillectomy Indications & Care

Indications for Surgery

  • Recurrent streptococcal infection
  • Enlarged tonsils affecting breathing/eating
  • Child should be 3 years or older

Post-Operative Nursing Care

  • Positioning: Place on abdomen or side until fully awake to facilitate drainage.
  • Hemorrhage Watch: Observe for frequent swallowing, tachycardia, pallor, or vomiting bright red blood.
  • Diet: Restrict fluids until alert. Avoid red/brown fluids (confused with blood). No straws. Soft foods (gelatin, mashed potatoes) after 1-2 days.
  • Comfort: Ice collar, analgesics, antiemetics.
ü©∏

Hemorrhage Risk

Hemorrhage may occur up to 10 days after surgery due to tissue sloughing.

üëÇ Otitis Media

Inflammation of the mucous membrane lining the middle ear space. Primarily caused by a dysfunctional Eustachian tube, common in children <5 years.

Clinical Manifestations

  • Infants: Irritable, holding/pulling ears, rolling head.
  • Older children: Complaint of pain.
  • Fever (up to 40¬∞C), diarrhea, vomiting.
  • Otoscopy: Red, bulging tympanic membrane.

Management

Treat with antibiotics (Amoxicillin, cefaclor, co-trimoxazole) for 5-10 days. Tympanoplasty (myringotomy) may be indicated for chronic effusion lasting >3-4 months with hearing loss.

üó£Ô∏è Acute Laryngotracheobronchitis (Croup)

Viral inflammation (Parainfluenza, RSV) of the larynx, trachea, and bronchi causing airway narrowing.

üìã

Classic Symptoms

Hoarseness, "barking" or "brassy" cough, inspiratory stridor, and varying degrees of respiratory distress.

Management

  • Maintain airway and give oxygen.
  • Nebulized Epinephrine: Causes mucosal vasoconstriction to decrease subglottic edema.
  • Corticosteroids for anti-inflammatory effects.

ü¶† Pneumonia

Inflammation of the lung parenchyma. Classification by morphology (Lobar, Bronchopneumonia, Interstitial) or agent (Viral, Bacterial, Aspiration).

Kenya Paediatric Guidelines (2016)

Classification Signs / Symptoms Management
SEVERE PNEUMONIA
  • O2 saturation <90% or Cyanosis
  • Inability to drink/breastfeed
  • AVPU = 'V', 'P' or 'U'
  • Grunting
  • Admit & Give Oxygen
  • Benzyl Penicillin (50,000 iu/kg 6hrly)
  • Gentamicin (7.5 mg/kg/24hr)
PNEUMONIA
  • Lower chest wall indrawing OR
  • Fast breathing:
    • RR ≥50/min (2-11 mo)
    • RR ≥40/min (12-59 mo)
  • Oral Amoxicillin
  • Review within 48 hrs
  • Counsel on danger signs
NO PNEUMONIA Probably URTI (Cold/Cough) Home care, soothe throat

üå¨Ô∏è Asthma

Chronic inflammatory disorder characterized by bronchoconstriction, airway inflammation, and hyper-responsiveness.
Classic Triad: Cough, Dyspnea, Wheezing.

Management Guidelines (Kenya 2016)

‚ñ∂ Severe Asthma Management
üö®

Danger Signs

O2 sat <90%, Central cyanosis, Inability to drink/talk, Pulse >200 bpm (0-3yrs).

  • ADMIT and give Oxygen.
  • Salbutamol: Nebulize 2.5mg or 6 puffs (spacer) every 20 mins (up to 3 doses).
  • Prednisolone: 2mg/kg.
  • Consider Ipratropium bromide if poor response.
‚ñ∂ Mild/Moderate Asthma Management

Signs: Wheeze + Lower chest indrawing OR Fast breathing.

  • Salbutamol: 2 puffs (or 2.5mg neb) every 20 mins up to 3 doses.
  • Monitor closely for 1-2 hours.
  • If mild symptoms persist, discharge on Salbutamol MDI (2 puffs q6h).
  • Prednisolone: 20-30mg/day for 3-5 days.
