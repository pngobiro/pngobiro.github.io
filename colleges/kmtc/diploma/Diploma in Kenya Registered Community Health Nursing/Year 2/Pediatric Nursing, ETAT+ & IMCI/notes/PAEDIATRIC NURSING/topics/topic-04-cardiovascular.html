üå°Ô∏è Rheumatic Fever

Rheumatic fever is a poorly understood inflammatory disease that occurs after pharyngitis caused by Group A beta hemolytic streptococci. It mostly affects children aged 5-15 years.

Etiology & Pathophysiology

This is an immunological disorder caused by damage to the heart by antibodies, which are produced in response to a primary infection of the throat or skin by Group A Beta hemolytic streptococci.

The exact pathogenesis is not known. Following untreated streptococcal sore throat there is a latent period that may last from 10 days to several weeks. During this period antibodies form from the streptococcal antigen. These antibodies have the capacity to react with human connective tissue, specifically the cardiac muscle (Antigen-Antibody reaction).

Clinical Manifestations (Major)

‚ñ∂ Major Cardiac Manifestations
  1. Carditis: Involves endocardium, myocardium, and pericardium.
    • Pericarditis: Chest pains and pericardial rub.
    • Myocarditis: Tachycardia, edema, tachypnea, cardiomegaly.
    • Endocarditis: Thrill and precordial murmurs (Aortic and mitral valves commonly affected).
  2. Polyarthritis: Edema, inflammation and effusions in joint tissue. Reversible and migratory; favors large joints (knees, elbow, hips). Joints are swollen, hot, red and painful for 1-2 days.
  3. Erythema Marginatum: Rash that is reddish, macular, non-itching, predominantly on trunk.
  4. Subcutaneous Nodules: Small, non-tender swellings on bony prominences (feet, hands, elbows, scalp, scapulae, vertebrae).
  5. Chorea (St. Vitus Dance): Sudden aimless, irregular movements, involuntary facial grimaces, speech disturbances, emotional lability, and muscle weakness. Exaggerated by anxiety, relieved by rest.

Management

  • Antibiotics: Oral penicillin or single IM penicillin to eradicate organism. Erythromycin if allergic.
  • Anti-inflammatory: Aspirin (start high dose 100mg/kg/day, then decrease to 70mg/kg/day). Give with antacids.
  • Bed Rest: Until inflammation resolves. Restrict activities if aortic insufficiency/mitral regurgitation present.

Secondary Prophylaxis

Oral penicillin 250mg BID or a monthly IM injection of penicillin.

This is done for at least 10 years or more. Throat infections should be treated timely to avoid under-treatment.

Rheumatic Heart Disease (RHD)

RHD is a chronic heart condition caused by RF. Acute rheumatic fever causes fibrosis of heart valves, leading to crippling valvular heart disease, heart failure, and death. Surgery is often required to repair heart valves.

ü©∏ Anaemia

Anaemia is a condition marked by a deficiency of red blood cells or of hemoglobin in the blood, resulting in pallor and weakness. It is not a disease but a manifestation of an underlying pathologic process.

Causes

  • Inadequate production of RBC or RBC components
  • Increased destruction of RBC
  • Excessive loss of RBC through hemorrhage

Management

Objective is to reverse the anaemia by treating the underlying cause.

  • Treat Specific Deficiency: For nutritional anaemias.
  • Transfusion: Red blood cell transfusion for acute hemorrhage.
  • Supportive Care: Oxygen therapy, bed rest, IV fluids for volume replacement.
  • Activity Tolerance: Assess tolerance for ADLs and play; adjust to allow self-care without undue exertion.
  • Prevention of Complications: Protect from infection (hand washing, isolation), monitor for heart failure.
