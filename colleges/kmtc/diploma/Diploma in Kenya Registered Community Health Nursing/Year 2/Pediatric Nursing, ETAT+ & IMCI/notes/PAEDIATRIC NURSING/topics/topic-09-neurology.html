üß† Convulsive Disorders

A seizure is an abnormal, unregulated electrical discharge that occurs within the brain‚Äôs cortical gray matter and transiently interrupts normal brain function.

Etiology by Age

  • Before Age 2: Fever, birth/developmental defects, birth injuries, metabolic disorders.
  • Ages 2 to 14: Idiopathic seizure disorders.
  • Adults: Cerebral trauma, alcohol withdrawal, tumors, strokes.

Key Definitions

Epilepsy
Recurrent, unprovoked seizures.
Febrile Seizures
Seizures associated with high fever in young children.

üìã Classification

‚ñ∂ 1. Generalized Seizures

Abnormal electrical discharge diffusely involves the entire cortex of both hemispheres. Consciousness is usually lost.

  • Tonic-Clonic: Major convulsions with stiffness and jerking.
  • Absence: Sudden onset, brief loss of consciousness, blank stare, automatisms.
  • Atonic: Sudden, momentary loss of muscle tone ("drop attacks").
  • Myoclonic: Sudden, brief contractions of a muscle group.
  • Infantile Spasms
‚ñ∂ 2. Partial Seizures

Discharge occurs in one cerebral cortex area. May evolve into generalized seizures (secondary generalization).

  • Simple Partial: Simple symptoms, NO alteration of consciousness.
  • Complex Partial: Complex symptoms, impairment of consciousness.

üëÅÔ∏è Clinical Manifestations

Generalized Tonic-Clonic Seizures

Occurs without warning.

  • 1
    Tonic Phase (10-30 sec)

    Eyes roll up, immediate loss of consciousness. Generalized stiffening. Arms flex, legs extend. Mouth snaps shut (tongue biting). Apnea/Cyanosis possible.

  • 2
    Clonic Phase (30-50 sec)

    Intense jerking movements (contraction/relaxation). Incontinence of urine/feces. Increased salivation (foaming).

  • 3
    Post-ictal Phase (1-15 min)

    Semiconscious, difficult to arouse, confused, sleeps.

üíä Management

üö®

Status Epilepticus

Continuous seizure lasting >30 minutes or a series of seizures without regaining consciousness. Medical Emergency!

Acute Management:

  • Maintain airway (ABC), give Oxygen.
  • Drugs: Diazepam, Lorazepam, or Midazolam to terminate seizure.
  • Endotracheal intubation if airway compromised.

General Management

  • Safety: Protect from injury during seizure (do not restrain, do not put objects in mouth). Place in recovery position post-seizure.
  • Medication: Anticonvulsants (Phenobarbitone, Phenytoin, Sodium Valproate, Carbamazepine).
  • Surgery: Considered if seizures are uncontrolled by ≥ 2 drugs in therapeutic doses.
  • Diagnosis: Neuroimaging (CT/MRI) and EEG.
