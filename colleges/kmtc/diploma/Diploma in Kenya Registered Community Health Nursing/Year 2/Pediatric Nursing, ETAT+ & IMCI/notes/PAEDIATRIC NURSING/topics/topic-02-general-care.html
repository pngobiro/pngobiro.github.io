üë™ Impact of Sickness and Admission

Hospitalization and sickness have significant impacts on the child, parents, and the family unit. These impacts can be both immediate and long-term.

Impact on the Child

  • Emotional Distress: Immediate and long term emotional distress to the child. Children below the age of 5 years are more vulnerable to emotional distress from hospitalization.
  • Anxiety: Due to lack of understanding and minimal experience with illness, hospitalization, and hospital procedures.
  • Sensory Deprivation: A lack of sensory stimulation in the hospital environment produces listlessness, indifference, unhappiness, and even appetite changes.
  • Motor Restriction: When a child‚Äôs motor activity is restricted, anger and hyperactivity may result.

üòü Impact on Parents

Parents have anxiety and concern about their child‚Äôs health and this contributes to stress to the child. Parents watching children in pain is difficult and some may feel guilty of not bringing these children earlier to hospital.

Common Parental Reactions

Parents may exhibit feelings such as denial, anger, depression, and confusion.

  • Denial: Parents may deny that their child is ill.
  • Anger: May be exhibited due to lack of control of caring for their child and may be directed to the nursing staff, family members, or a higher power.
  • Depression: May occur due to exhaustion, psychological and physiological requirements of spending long hours in hospital caring for the child.
  • Confusion: May arise due to dealing with an unfamiliar environment or the loss of parental roles, long separation, and increased stress.
  • Helplessness: At times parents feel helpless when they play a passive role in their child‚Äôs care, such as when a medical procedure is required that hurts or traumatizes the child.

üè† Siblings and Family Impact

Siblings

Siblings may experience jealousy, insecurity, resentment, confusion, and anxiety. They may have difficulty understanding why their sibling is ill or getting all the attention and leaving little for them. Due to changes in roles in the family, children may feel insecure or anxious and they develop change in behavior or school performance. During this time siblings may feel that they caused the illness.

Family

Whether planned or unplanned, hospitalization increases the family‚Äôs stress and anxiety level and this affects all the members of the family. The family‚Äôs normal routines are disrupted and may alter family roles. Parents and siblings have their own reactions to this experience.

üõ°Ô∏è Management Strategies

To mitigate the negative impacts of hospitalization on the child and family, the following management strategies should be employed:

  • Liberal visitation: Encouraging frequent visits by family members.
  • Rooming-in: Implementing hospital policies that allow parents to stay with the child.
  • Shorter hospital stays: Minimizes separation anxiety.
  • Play and recreation: Educational opportunities provide an outlet to distract children from illness.
  • Parental involvement: Parents attempt to understand by becoming informed and understanding procedures. They deal with fear of uncertainty and attempt to promote a sense of comfort by interacting with hospital staff. They seek reassurance from care givers.
