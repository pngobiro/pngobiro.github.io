üìâ Overview & Etiology

Malnutrition is a major health condition affecting children under 5 years of age. It is caused by not having enough to eat, not eating enough of the right things, or being unable to use the food that one does eat. The most extreme forms of Protein-Energy Malnutrition (PEM) are Kwashiorkor and Marasmus.

Etiology

  • Diarrhea (Gastroenteritis): A major factor.
  • Bottle feeding: Especially in poor sanitary conditions.
  • Socio-economic factors: Poverty, lack of adequate food, parental illiteracy.
  • Medical: Chronic health problems.

‚öñÔ∏è Clinical Forms of PEM

Feature Kwashiorkor ("Sickness of weaning") Marasmus ("Withering/Wasting")
Deficiency Protein deficiency (adequate calories) General malnutrition (Protein + Calories)
Appearance Edema (moon face, ascites), thin wasted extremities masked by swelling Severe emaciation, "Old man" look, loose wrinkled skin
Skin/Hair Scaly/dry skin (dermatosis), Depigmentation, hair changes (red/thin) No dermatosis, hair usually normal
Behavior Apathetic, miserable, irritable Fretful, hungry (initially), alert
Liver Fatty liver (Hepatomegaly) No fatty liver

Marasmic Kwashiorkor: A form of PEM in which clinical findings of both conditions are evident.

Admission Criteria

Admit if: Acute illness AND (MUAC < 11.5cm OR WHZ < -3 OR Edema/Kwashiorkor signs).

Phase 1: Stabilization (Days 1-7)

  • 1
    Treat Hypoglycemia
    Check blood glucose. If < 3 mmol/l: Give 5 mls/kg 10% dextrose. Feed immediately (Oral/NG).
  • 2
    Treat Hypothermia
    If axillary temp < 35¬∞C: Warm with blankets/heater. Keep dry.
  • 3
    Treat Dehydration

    Shock? (AVPU < A, weak pulse, cold hands).
    YES → IV Fluids (Ringer's with 5% Dextrose, 20mls/kg).
    NO → ReSoMal (Oral Rehydration Solution for Malnutrition).

  • 4
    Correct Electrolytes
    Use F75 (contains minerals). Add Potassium (4 mmol/kg/day) if using local mix.
    NEVER use Frusemide for nutritional edema!
  • 5
    Treat Infection
    Give IV Penicillin/Ampicillin AND Gentamicin to all ill children.
  • 6
    Micronutrients
    • Vitamin A (if eye signs).
    • Folic Acid (2.5mg).
    • Iron: Give ONLY when child is gaining weight (Rehab phase) - Iron can worsen infection in acute phase.
  • 7
    Cautious Feeding (F75)
    Start with F75 (75 kcal/100ml). Small, frequent feeds (e.g., every 2-3 hours).
    • Severe Edema: 100mls/kg/day.
    • No/Moderate Edema: 130mls/kg/day.

Phase 2: Rehabilitation (Week 2-6)

  • 8
    Catch-up Growth
    Transition from F75 to F100 or RUTF (Ready to Use Therapeutic Food).
    Transition Phase: Replace F75 with F100 for 2 days.
    Rehab Phase: Increase volume. Monitor for heart failure (rapid pulse/breathing).
  • 9
    Sensory Stimulation
    Provide a caring and stimulating environment. Play therapy.
  • 10
    Prepare for Discharge
    Education on home feeding, hygiene, and follow-up.
