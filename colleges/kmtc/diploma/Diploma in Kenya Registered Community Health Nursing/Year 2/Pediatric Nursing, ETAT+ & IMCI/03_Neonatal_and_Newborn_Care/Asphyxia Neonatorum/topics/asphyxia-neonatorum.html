ASPHYXIA NEONATORUM AND NEONATAL CONVULSIONS

DR. MWENDWA

OBJECTIVES

  1. 1.Define Asphyxia neonatorum / birth asphyxia
  2. 2.Review etiology
  3. 3.Review the APGAR Score and interpretation
  4. 4.Review the clinical presentation and differential diagnosis
  5. 5.Evaluation and treatment of a neonate with asphyxia neonatorum
  6. 6.Review complications

DEFINITION

Asphyxia neonatorum, also known as perinatal asphyxia, occurs when a newborn doesn't get enough oxygen before, during, or just after birth. It is defined as the failure to initiate and maintain spontaneous respiration within one minute of birth, which then results in the absence of a pulse. This lack of oxygen and blood flow can damage the baby's brain and other organs.

AETIOLOGY

There are two main types of neonatal asphyxia:

  • •Acute asphyxia which was caused by intranatal factors only.
  • •Asphyxia, which was developed due to prolonged fetal hypoxia associated with placental insufficiency.

The high risk factors of fetal (antenatal) hypoxia development:

  • •Maternal age of less than 16 years old or over 40 years old.
  • •Postmaturity.
  • •Multiple pregnancy.
  • •Threatened preterm labor.
  • •Diabetes mellitus in pregnant women.
  • •Bleedings and infectious diseases in II/III trimester of pregnancy.
  • •Smoking or drug addiction in pregnant women.
  • •Intrauterine growth restriction or another diseases revealed in fetus in ultrasound examination.

The high risk factors of acute (intranatal) asphyxia development:

  1. 1.Cesarean operation (planned or urgent).
  2. 2.Malpresentation (breech, transverse/oblique presentation).
  3. 3.Premature or SGA.
  4. 4.Placental abruption.
  5. 5.Obstetrical forceps or vacuum-extractor use.
  6. 6.Birth trauma.
  7. 7.Congenital malformations of fetus.
  8. 8.Acute labor hypoxia in mother (shock, amniotic fluid embolism, poisonings, decompensated diseases).
  9. 9.Maternal anesthesia (both the intravenous drugs and the anesthetic gases cross the placenta and may sedate the fetus).

At birth, symptoms may include:

  • •Physiological newborns reflexes are depressed.
  • •Meconium in the amniotic fluid.
  • •Arterial hypotension.
  • •rales over the lungs.
  • •Hepatomegaly.
  • •Fluid, electrolyte and metabolic abnormalities including hyperkalaemia, hypoglycaemia, and acidosis.

Interpretation of the APGAR SCORE

The Apgar score is used to quickly assess a newborn's health at 1 and 5 minutes after birth.

  • •A score of 7-10 is normal (no depression).
  • •4-6 indicates moderate CNS depression.
  • •0-3 indicates severe CNS depression.

MANAGEMENT

Management is divided into three categories:

  • •Prophylactic
  • •Supportive
  • •Definitive

PROPHYLACTIC

  1. 1.Antenatal detection of high risk patients.
  2. 2.Careful fetal monitoring, particularly in high risk pregnancy.
  3. 3.Judicious administration of anaesthetic agents and depressant drugs during labor.

BABIES WITH APGAR SCORE 7-9 (PINK, BREATHING REGULAR HR > 100).

  • •O₂ administration if cyanosed on peripheries.
  • •Re-assess the condition at 5 minutes.

BABIES WITH APGAR SCORE 4-6 (PERIPHERAL CYANOSIS, BREATHING IRREGULAR HR ≥ 100)

  • •Place under a radiant heater and dry the baby.
  • •Baby is put flat or airway in neutral position.
  • •Immediate suction.
  • •O₂ administration.

BABIES WITH APGAR SCORE BELOW 4 (CENTRAL CYANOSIS, NO BREATHING HR <100)

  • □Tracheal intubation and ventilatory support must be started immediately.
  • □If mother received pethidine or morphine within 3 hrs of delivery give Naloxone 10 µg/kg i/v it may be repeated every 2-3 mts.

Supportive

  • •IV Fluids: 10% dextrose 2/3 of the volume for the day.
  • •Convulsions chart.
  • •Input/output chart.
  • •Trophic feeds.
  • •Oxygen if in distress.
  • •Counsel the parents.

Complications

Birth asphyxia can lead to a wide range of serious complications, affecting multiple organ systems. The damage can be immediate or delayed.

IMMEDIATE

  • •Cardiovascular - HTN, cardiac failure.
  • •Renal - acute cortical necrosis, renal failure.
  • •Liver function - compromised.
  • •GI - Ulcers and necrotising enterocolitis.
  • •Lungs - Persistent pulmonary HTN.
  • •Brain - Cerebral edema, seizures.

DELAYED

Severe cases of birth asphyxia can cause permanent damage to the brain and other organs. Long-term complications can include:

  • •Retarded mental and physical growth.
  • •Epilepsy - up to 30% in severe asphyxia.
  • •Minimal brain dysfunction, which can lead to conditions like attention deficit hyperactivity disorder (ADHD).
