Metabolic & Temperature Disorders

Metabolic and Temperature Disorders

Hypoglycemia

This is a metabolic disorder where the blood glucose level falls below 2.6 mmol/L. It is common in infants of diabetic mothers, preterm babies, and those with asphyxia or sepsis. Prolonged hypoglycemia can lead to permanent neurological damage.

Clinical Features: Poor feeding, high-pitched cry, lethargy, irritability, hypotonia, hypothermia, and apnoea.

Management:

  • •Give 10% dextrose infusion until normal glucose levels are achieved.
  • •Encourage breastfeeding or feed EBM via NG tube or cup.
  • •In severe cases, a bolus dose of 25% dextrose may be given.
  • •Monitor glucose levels hourly until stable.

Prevention: Early and frequent feeding (within 1 hour of life), preventing hypothermia, and regular glucose monitoring for at-risk infants.

Neonatal Hypothermia

This is a condition where the baby's body temperature falls below 36°C. Newborns lose heat rapidly through radiation, conduction, convection, and evaporation.

Predisposing Factors: Prematurity, asphyxia, and cold environment.

Clinical Features: Baby feels cold to the touch, paleness, weak cry, low respiration rate, and poor feeding.

Management:

  • •Rewarm the baby in a resuscitaire, incubator, or using KMC.
  • •Feed with EBM and provide extra glucose (dextrose).
  • •Observe for signs of hypoglycemia.

Prevention (The Warm Chain): Ensure a warm delivery room, dry the baby immediately, initiate skin-to-skin contact, encourage early breastfeeding, postpone bathing, and use appropriate clothing.
