Oxygen Therapy

Hypoxeamia ( $\mathrm{SpO}_{2}<90 \%$ ) can be assessed through clinical signs, pulse oximetry and blood gas analysis. It often presents in sick children and is a major risk factor for death regardless of the diagnosis.

Give Oxygen if Any Child Presents With:

  • Central cyanosis, head nodding, nasal flaring, grunting, severe lower chest-wall in-drawing, respiratory rate >70bpm
  • Oxygen saturation $\left(\mathrm{SpO}_{2}\right)<90 \%$
  • Convulsions
  • Post resuscitation

Steps:

  1. Start giving oxygen at accurate and safe levels (Check flow rate, delivery method and oxygen prescription instructions on page 29).
  2. Target saturation: 91-95% for neonates, 90-95% for older children and 94-98% post resuscitation.
  3. Titrate every $15-30 \mathrm{mins}$ by $0.5 \mathrm{~L} / \mathrm{min}$ until the target saturation is achieved.
  4. Target Saturation Achieved?
    • NO: Increase flow rates to achieve targets $\mathrm{SpO}_{2}$. Change the oxygen delivery methods as appropriate. Monitor the $\mathrm{SpO}_{2}$ and work of breathing. Treat any underlying medical problem. If target is still not achieved with highest flow rate, refer patient for advanced care (high flow nasal cannula, CPAP or mechanical ventilation).
    • YES: Maintain the oxygen flow rate. Monitor the $\mathrm{SpO}_{2}$ and work of breathing. Treat any underlying medical problem.
  5. Patient Stable? ($\mathrm{SpO}_{2}>90 \%$; No increased WoB; No emergency signs)
    • NO: Continue current treatment and monitoring.
    • YES: Start weaning off oxygen by $0.5 \mathrm{~L} / \mathrm{min}$ every 30 mins. Monitor the $\mathrm{SpO}_{2}$ and work of breathing. Recheck $\mathrm{SpO}_{2} 1$ hour after stopping oxygen as late desaturation can occur.
  6. Discharge only if child has been stable with $\mathrm{SpO}_{2} \geq 90 \%$ on room air and no increased work of breathing for at least 24 hrs.

Use of Intra-osseous Lines

If IV access fails in a child in circulatory compromise IO line is a rapid, safe & reliable route for obtaining blood samples and administration of drugs, fluids & blood.

  • Size - use IO or bone marrow needle 15-18G if available or $16-21 \mathrm{G}$ hypodermic needle if not available
  • Sterility - Clean after identifying landmarks then use sterile gloves and sterilize site
  • Site - Middle of the antero-medial (flat) surface of tibia at junction of upper and middle thirds
  • - bevel to toes and introduce vertically $\left(90^{\circ}\right)$
  • - advance slowly with rotating movement
  • - Stop advancing when there is a 'sudden give' - then aspirate with 5 mls syringe
  • - Slowly inject 3 mls Normal Saline looking for any leakage under the skin - if OK attach IV fluid giving set and apply dressings and strap down
  • - Give fluids as needed - a $20 \mathrm{mls} / 50 \mathrm{mls}$ syringe will be needed for boluses
  • - Watch for leg / calf muscle swelling
  • - Replace IO access with IV within 8 hours

Treatment of Convulsions

Age > 1 month. For convulsions in the first month, refer to page 65.

  • DO NOT give more than 2 doses of diazepam in 24 hrs once phenobarbitone is used
  • DO NOT give a phenobarbitone-loading dose to an epileptic on maintenance phenobarbitone
  • Phenytoin, levetiracetam and IV sodium valproate (see doses in the formulary) are alternatives to phenobarbitone.
