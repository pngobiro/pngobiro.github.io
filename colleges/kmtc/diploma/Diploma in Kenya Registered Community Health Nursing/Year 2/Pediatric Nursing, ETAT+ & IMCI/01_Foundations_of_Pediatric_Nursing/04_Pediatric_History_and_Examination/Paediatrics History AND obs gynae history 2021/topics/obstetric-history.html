Obstetric History

A. Past Obstetric history

  1. Menarche
  2. Menstrual cycle - duration. Flow
  3. Gravidity
  4. Abortions/miscarriage
  5. Live children - number and sate of health
  6. Still births
  7. Child deaths
  8. Previous pregnancies - get detailed information

B. Current pregnancy

  1. Contraceptives - type, duration of use and date of discontinuation
  2. Last menstrual period (LMP)
  3. Previous menstrual history (PMP) to establish whether the menses were regular
  4. Symptoms since LMP
    1. Nausea, vomiting, indigestion
    2. Constipation
    3. Vaginal discharge
    4. Vaginal bleeding
    5. Abdominal pain
    6. Infections
    7. Radiological examination
    8. Medications being taken presently and any other time during the current pregnancy
  5. Others

C. Past medical history

  1. Vascular disorders
  2. Viral infections
  3. Heart disease & Rheumatic heart disease
  4. Hypertension
  5. Diabetes
  6. Jaundice
  7. Ant blood transfusions
  8. Bladder and kidney disease
  9. Thyroid disease
  10. STI's - syphilis, HIV/AIDS
  11. Accidents and any surgical operations e.g. #pelvis
  12. Others

D. Family history

  1. Diabetes
  2. Hypertension
  3. Cancer
  4. Health of infant's father
  5. Inherited abnormalities
  6. Twins/multiple pregnancies
  7. Sickle cell disease
  8. Others

E. Personal habits

  1. Smoking
  2. Alcohol intake
  3. Drug abuse
  4. Drug addiction

F. History of previous pregnancies

  1. The year/date when the child was delivered
  2. Abortion/miscarriage
    1. Date
    2. Trimester
    3. Any medical intervention e.g. evacuation
    4. Elective or spontaneous abortion
  3. Complications during pregnancy
    1. Blood pressure
    2. Blood sugar
    3. Vaginal bleeding
    4. Infections
    5. Others
  4. Delivery
    1. Where child was born - home or hospital
    2. Hours in labour
    3. Type of delivery
      1. Vaginal
        1. Spontaneous
        2. Induced (state reason for induction)
        3. Assisted (state reason for assistance)
      2. Caesarean section (state reason for the operation)
        1. Elective
        2. Emergency
    4. Anaesthesia given
      1. Epidural
      2. General
      3. Local
      4. Inhaled
    5. Maternal complications
      1. Sepsis
      2. Psychosis
      3. Bleeding
        1. Antepartum heamorrhage (APH)
        2. Postpartum heamorrhage (PPH)
    6. The baby
      1. Birth weight
      2. Sex
      3. Estimated weeks of gestation
      4. Neonatal complications
        1. Hypoglycaemia
        2. Asphyxia
        3. Neonatal sepsis
        4. Opthalmia neonatoram
      5. Was the baby breast-fed or bottle-fed?
      6. The child's present age
      7. Child's present health
      8. Associated problem such as: -
        1. Anaemia
        2. Allergy
        3. Infections
        4. Deficiency disorders
        5. Others
  5. Parity
  6. Problems during pregnancy
    1. Diseases
    2. Complications during labour
    3. Puerperal period - complications and infections
  7. Abortions
    1. Stage or gestation
    2. Any underlying factor
      1. Infection
      2. Stress
      3. Trauma
    3. Any termination of pregnancy (method and reason)
    4. Infertility (primary or secondary)
    5. Family planning methods
  8. Menstrual cycle
    1. Menarche (10-16 years)
    2. How long it took to become regular
    3. Regularity
    4. Duration of the cycle (21-35 days in Britain and 21-30 days in America)
    5. Duration of flow (3-7 days)
  9. Past Medical History - Previous disease, operations and dates
  10. Family History
    1. Related disease
    2. Chronic diseases
      1. Tuberculosis
      2. Cardiac disease
      3. Allergies
      4. Psychiatric history
  11. Chief complains
  12. History of presenting illness
    1. Pain
    2. Bleeding pattern
      1. Flow
      2. Watery
      3. Clotted
      4. Duration
    3. Per vaginal discharge (PV)
      1. Timing
      2. Duration
      3. Colour
        1. Yellow purulent - vaginitis and cervicitis
        2. Whitish, cream (curdy) - candida infection)
        3. Blood stained-CaCx, Ca vulva
        4. Greenish - trichomoniasis
