Paediatrics and Obstetrics & Gynaecology History (Overview)

Paediatrics History

History taking is an important aspect in Paediatrics as it posses an excellent challenge to learners in terms of collection，correlation and corroboration of information，which is from a legion list of sources． History forms the most important single factor in making proper assessment．

General Consideration

  • Interpretation of history
    • Take special interpretation of the complains given
    • Guide the process to the productive phase of the interview
  • Source of history
    • Think of important and informative sources
  • Direction of questioning
    • Should be methodical and exhaustive
    • Be three dimensional
    • Should not be prying
  • Recorded history
    • Be detailed
    • Clear
    • Chronological
    • Contain significant information
    • Include the parents＇interpretation of the situation
  • Psychotherapeutic effects

General Outline of History

  1. Identification data
  2. Presenting complain
  3. Present illness
  4. Previous health
    • Antenatal
    • Natal
    • Neonatal
  5. Development - milestones
  6. Nutrition
    • Breast feeding
    • Weaning
    • Solid foods
    • Supplements
  7. Immunization
  8. Past medical history
    • Illnesses
    • Operations
    • Accidents/injuries
  9. Habits
    • Eating
    • Sleeping
    • Exercise and play
    • Urinary
    • Bowel
    • Disturbances
  10. Family history
    • Health of parents
    • Marital relationships
    • Siblings
    • Stillbirths
    • Diseases
    • Health of contacts
  11. Personality history
    • Relations with other children
    • School progress
  12. Social history
    • Family
    • School

Obstetric History

A. Past Obstetric history

  • Menarche
  • Menstrual cycle - duration. Flow
  • Gravidity
  • Abortions/miscarriage
  • Live children - number and sate of health
  • Still births
  • Child deaths
  • Previous pregnancies - get detailed information

B. Current pregnancy

  • Contraceptives - type, duration of use and date of discontinuation
  • Last menstrual period (LMP)
  • Previous menstrual history (PMP) to establish whether the menses were regular
  • Symptoms since LMP
    1. Nausea, vomiting, indigestion
    2. Constipation
    3. Vaginal discharge
    4. Vaginal bleeding
    5. Abdominal pain
    6. Infections
    7. Radiological examination
    8. Medications being taken presently and any other time during the current pregnancy
    9. Others

C. Past medical history

  • Vascular disorders
  • Viral infections
  • Heart disease & Rheumatic heart disease
  • Hypertension
  • Diabetes
  • Jaundice
  • Ant blood transfusions
  • Bladder and kidney disease
  • Thyroid disease
  • STI's - syphilis, HIV/AIDS
  • Accidents and any surgical operations e.g. #pelvis
  • Others

D. Family history

  • Diabetes
  • Hypertension
  • Cancer
  • Health of infant's father
  • Inherited abnormalities
  • Twins/multiple pregnancies
  • Sickle cell disease
  • Others

E. Personal habits

  • Smoking
  • Alcohol intake
  • Drug abuse
  • Drug addiction

F. History of previous pregnancies

  • The year/date when the child was delivered
  • Abortion/miscarriage
    1. Date
    2. Trimester
    3. Any medical intervention e.g. evacuation
    4. Elective or spontaneous abortion
  • Complications during pregnancy
    1. Blood pressure
    2. Blood sugar
    3. Vaginal bleeding
    4. Infections
    5. Others
  • Delivery
    1. Where child was born - home or hospital
    2. Hours in labour
    3. Type of delivery
      • Vaginal
        • Spontaneous
        • Induced (state reason for induction)
        • Assisted (state reason for assistance)
      • Caesarean section (state reason for the operation)
        • Elective
        • Emergency
    4. Anaesthesia given
      • Epidural
      • General
      • Local
      • Inhaled
    5. Maternal complications
      • Sepsis
      • Psychosis
      • Bleeding
        • Antepartum heamorrhage (APH)
        • Postpartum heamorrhage (PPH)
    6. The baby
      • Birth weight
      • Sex
      • Estimated weeks of gestation
      • Neonatal complications
        • Hypoglycaemia
        • Asphyxia
        • Neonatal sepsis
        • Opthalmia neonatoram
      • Was the baby breast-fed or bottle-fed?
      • The child's present age
      • Child's present health
      • Associated problem such as: -
        • Anaemia
        • Allergy
        • Infections
        • Deficiency disorders
        • Others

Further Gynaecological History

  1. Parity
  2. Problems during pregnancy
    • Diseases
    • Complications during labour
    • Puerperal period - complications and infections
  3. Abortions
    • Stage or gestation
    • Any underlying factor
      • Infection
      • Stress
      • Trauma
    • Any termination of pregnancy (method and reason)
    • Infertility (primary or secondary)
    • Family planning methods
  4. Menstrual cycle
    • Menarche (10-16 years)
    • How long it took to become regular
    • Regularity
    • Duration of the cycle (21-35 days in Britain and 21-30 days in America)
    • Duration of flow (3-7 days)
  5. Past Medical History - Previous disease, operations and dates
  6. Family History
    • Related disease
    • Chronic diseases
      • Tuberculosis
      • Cardiac disease
      • Allergies
      • Psychiatric history
  7. Chief complains
  8. History of presenting illness
    • Pain
    • Bleeding pattern
      • Flow
      • Watery
      • Clotted
      • Duration
    • Per vaginal discharge (PV)
      • Timing
      • Duration
      • Colour
        • Yellow purulent - vaginitis and cervicitis
        • Whitish, cream (curdy) - candida infection)
        • Blood stained-CaCx, Ca vulva
        • Greenish - trichomoniasis
