üëÑ The Mouth & Salivary Glands

Oral Cavity Structure

  • Oral cavity: Lined with mucous membranes (cheeks and lips).
  • Tongue: Voluntary muscular structure involving the sense of taste. Plays a part in chewing, deglutition, and speech.
  • Teeth: Responsible for Cutting, Biting, and Chewing.

Saliva Composition

Secretions are produced from salivary glands and mucus. Approximately 1.5 litres are produced daily.

üíß

Components of Saliva

  • Water
  • Mineral salts
  • Digestive enzyme ‚Äì Salivary amylase
  • Mucus
  • Immunoglobulins
  • Blood clotting factors

Functions of Saliva

  1. Cleaning effect: Washing away food debris.
  2. Swallowing: Makes swallowing easier.
  3. Antibacterial effect: Fighting off bacteria entering the mouth.
  4. Lubricating effect: Protects mucous membranes.
  5. pH buffering effect: Prevents dental caries.
  6. Remineralisation: Promotes remineralisation of teeth.

ü•£ Oesophagus & Stomach

The Oesophagus

Passes through the diaphragm at the diaphragmatic hiatus. Its primary function is to transport masticated and swallowed materials (bolus). It contains upper and lower oesophageal sphincters.

The Stomach

A hollow muscular organ that stores food, secretes digestive fluids, and propels chyme into the small intestines.

Gastric Function

  • Produces fluid: 2.4 litres daily.
  • Can have a pH of 1 (high acidity) from Parietal/Oxyntic cells (glands of stomach).
  • Breaks down food into more absorbable components.
  • Aids in destruction of ingested bacteria.

The Ileum terminates at the ileal-caecal valve. Its functions are:
