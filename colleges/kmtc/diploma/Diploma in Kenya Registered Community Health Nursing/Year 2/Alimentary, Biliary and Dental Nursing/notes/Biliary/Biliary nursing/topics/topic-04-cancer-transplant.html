ü¶Ä Cancer of the Liver

Primary cancer of the liver is rare. Metastatic cancer is more common.

  • Liver is a common site for metastases because of increased rate of blood flow and capillary network.
  • Metastases are found in the liver in approximately one-half of all clients with late-stage cancer.
  • Prognosis is poor.

Assessment

  • Risk factors/etiology: malignancy elsewhere in the body.
  • Clinical manifestations: Anorexia, weight loss, fatigue, anemia; Right upper quadrant pain, ascites, jaundice.

Treatment

Treatment is primarily palliative.

  1. Surgical excision of tumor, if it is localized.
  2. Chemotherapy: very poor response.
  3. Radiofrequency (RF) ablation: uses heat to burn tumor (percutaneous approach).
  4. Cryosurgery (cryoablation): uses liquid nitrogen to freeze liver tissue; not used for metastatic disease.
  5. Percutaneous ethanol injection (PEI) or percutaneous acetic acid injection (PAI) used to treat unresectable liver cancer.

Nursing Interventions

Focused on maintaining comfort; nursing care is the same as that for the client with advanced cirrhosis.

02

üè• Liver Transplantation

Therapeutic option for clients with end-stage liver disease; not recommended for widespread malignant disease.

Assessment

  • Rigorous prescreening process.
  • Rejection less common than with kidney transplants.

Treatment Types

  • Live liver transplant: portion of liver is donated.
  • Split liver transplant: donor liver is divided and given to two recipients.

Nursing Interventions

Goal: To monitor for postoperative complications

  • Assess neuro status.
  • Monitor for hemorrhage.
  • Monitor for common respiratory problems of pneumonia, atelectasis, and pleural effusion.
  • Monitor drainage systems: IV fluids, nasogastric tube drainage, Jackson-Pratt drain, and T-tube drainage.
  • Administer antibiotics and analgesics.
ü¶† CRITICAL ALERT

Critical to monitor for infection the first 2 months after surgery; fever may be the only sign.

Goal: To provide nursing care of the immunocompromised client

Strict infection control measures must be maintained due to immunosuppressive therapy required to prevent rejection.
