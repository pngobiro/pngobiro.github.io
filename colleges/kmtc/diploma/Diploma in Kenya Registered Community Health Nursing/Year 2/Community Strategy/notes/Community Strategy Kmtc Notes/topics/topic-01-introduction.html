Key Innovation

One of the key innovations of KEPH was the recognition and introduction of Level 1 service, which aimed at empowering Kenyan households and communities to take charge of improving their own health.

02

üë• Life Cycle Cohorts

KEPH targets everybody in all age groups in the community through the life cycle focus instead of limiting the services and activities to specific groups like mothers and children. To tackle the health concerns of everybody in the community, KEPH divides the community by age groups because each age group has different health needs.

These age groups are referred to as "Age Cohorts" in the Community Health Strategy.

Key Changes to Cohorts

  • Reduction: From 6 to 5 cohorts.
  • Cohort 1 Extension: From 14‚Äì28 days to cover the full neonatal period.
  • Consolidation: Early and late childhood cohorts combined into one cohort (Children and Youth) due to similar health challenges.

03

üèóÔ∏è Service Delivery Tiers

Key changes were made to align the structure with the County system, reducing the levels from 6 to 4. Notably, the Provincial level is no longer in existence.

  • Tier 1: Community Level

    This is a Community unit as defined in the previous KEPH Level 1 unit.

  • Tier 2: Primary Care Level

    Provision of basic outpatient health services. Combines Dispensaries and Health Centres (previous KEPH levels 2 and 3).

  • Tier 3: County Level

    Provision of primary referral services (previous KEPH level 4).

  • Tier 4: National Level

    Provision of secondary and specialized services (previous KEPH levels 5 and 6).
