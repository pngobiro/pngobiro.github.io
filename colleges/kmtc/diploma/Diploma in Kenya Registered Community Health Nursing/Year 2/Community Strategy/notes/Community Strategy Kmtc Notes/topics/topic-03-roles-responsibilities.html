üßë‚Äçü§ù‚Äçüßë Key Actors

A Community Unit consists of three types of key actors, each with distinct roles:

  • 1. Community Health Volunteer (CHV)

    Volunteer workers who provide Level 1 services and support the community in their initiatives to improve health status.

  • 2. Community Health Committee (CHC)

    The governance body for the CU. Consists of representatives from different groups and villages who provide leadership for managing Level 1 services and build partnerships with stakeholders.

  • 3. Community Health Extension Worker (CHEW)

    Health or development workers (government employees) who support CHVs and CHCs technically through supervision and mentoring. They strengthen the linkage between the CU and higher health systems.

üëî Roles of CHC

The Community Health Committee (CHC) provides oversight and leadership.

  • Governance: Provide leadership in health and related matters in the community.
  • Planning: Prepare and present the Community Unit's Annual Work Plan (AWP) to the Link Health Facility Committee.
  • Networking: Network with other players (Ministries of Water, Agriculture, Education) to improve health status.
  • Resource Mobilization: Look for resources (including money) to implement the work plan and ensure accountability.
  • Management: Manage workers and finances at the community level.
  • Mobilization: Mobilize the community to participate in dialogue and health action days.
  • Conflict Resolution: Help solve problems among stakeholders.

Important Note on Linkage

Effective referral is dependent on effective linkage. Each CHW must be linked to a constituent health facility. Community health work requires the facility to be effective, and community participation is key to sustainability.
