üìã Preparation & Awareness

Launching the Community Strategy involves a structured preparatory phase to ensure alignment and buy-in from all stakeholders.

1. Define Implementation Guidelines

This includes the development of comprehensive message materials, the consolidation of the commodity kit, and the definition of training needs, curricula, and manuals for both CORPs and CHEWs.

2. Create Awareness

Awareness must be created among county leaders, including the County Commissioner (CC), the County Development Committee (CDC), and relevant line ministries.

üö™ Community Entry Process

Effective community entry is based on a process of engagement that recognizes the need for the health system to negotiate its way into community agendas.

  • 1. Exploration

    A relatively low-key fact-finding mission. Service providers gain knowledge and understanding of the community‚Äôs situation. Findings should be written up and shared with the community.

  • 2. Protocol (Gatekeepers)

    Identifying formal and informal leaders (gatekeepers) to formalize the process and gain authority. Facilitators introduce the Level One Services idea to these leaders to involve them.

  • 3. Clarification

    Clarifying objectives and identifying target groups to ensure inclusion. Leads to the identification of task groups to spearhead detailed assessment and planning.

  • 4. Participatory Assessment

    Discussions with key individuals at every level down to the household. The new idea must be negotiated through gatekeepers down to the individuals concerned.

üìä Scale Up & Monitoring

Follow Up and Monitoring

Once training is completed, CHEWs act as coaches. They follow up to monitor activities, provide supportive supervision, assess progress, and solve problems.

üìà

Scaling Up

Scaling up is assured through the multi-sector working group, building on existing programs. This is strengthened by iterative rapid assessment, planning, and action reinforced by regular health days.
