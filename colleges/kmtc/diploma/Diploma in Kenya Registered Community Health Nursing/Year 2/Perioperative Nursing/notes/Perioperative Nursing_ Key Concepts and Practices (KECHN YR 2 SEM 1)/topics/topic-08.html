üßµ Sutures & Ligatures

Suture: A medical device used to hold skin, internal organs, blood vessels, or other tissues together after injury or surgery until healing takes place.

Ligature: A suture used for tying off blood vessels or hollow organs to shut them off.

Classification of Sutures

‚ñ∂ 1. Absorbable Sutures

Broken down by body tissues over time. Do not require removal.

  • Natural:
    • Plain Catgut: Untreated.
    • Chromic Catgut: Treated with chromium salts to persist longer (10-21 days tensile strength).
  • Synthetic:
    • Polyglycolic acid (DEXON)
    • Polyglatin (VICRYL)
    • Polydioxanone (PDS)
    • Poliglecaprone (Monocryl)
‚ñ∂ 2. Non-Absorbable Sutures

Resistant to degradation by living tissue. Permanent unless removed.

  • Natural: Silk, Cotton, Linen, Stainless steel.
  • Synthetic: Nylon, Polypropylene, Polyester.

Uses: Skin closure (removable), internal tissues requiring long-term support (heart valves, blood vessels).

Key Characteristics

  • ‚û§ Tensile Strength: Force needed to break the suture. Weakest at the knot (knots reduce strength by 30-40%).
  • ‚û§ Memory: Capability to return to packaging shape. High memory makes handling difficult.
  • ‚û§ Plasticity: Capacity to retain length/strength after stretching (accommodates edema).
  • ‚û§ Capillarity: Wicking of fluids. Multifilament sutures have higher capillarity (risk of transporting bacteria).
  • ‚û§ Elasticity: Ability to stretch and return to original length.

Suture Sizes (USP System)

Classified by diameter. Larger numbers indicate larger diameter (e.g., 1 > 0 > 2/0 > 6/0).

  • 12/0 ‚Äì 7/0: Microsurgery (smallest)
  • 6/0 ‚Äì 5/0: Face, neck, blood vessels
  • 4/0 ‚Äì 3/0: Mucosa, tendon, limbs, intestine

üìç Surgical Needles

Needles place suture material in the wound. They must be sterile, corrosion-resistant, high-quality stainless steel, and rigid enough to resist bending yet flexible enough to bend before breaking.

Needle Anatomy

  • The Point: Sharpest portion, penetrates tissue.
  • The Body: Mid-portion.
  • The Swage: Thickest portion where suture material is attached.

Types of Needles

Type Characteristics Usage
Round Bodied Round and smooth, makes a puncture rather than a cut. Delicate tissues/organs (uterus, intestines).
Cutting Sharp edge, cuts a crack as it passes. Strong tissues (skin, tendon, muscles).
Atraumatic Suture and needle joined together (eyeless). Minimal tissue trauma.

üî¢ Swabs & Surgical Counts

Swabs are porous materials used to absorb fluids, protect tissues, or dissect. All surgical swabs must contain a radio-opaque marker for X-ray detection.

Types of Swabs

  • Gauze Swabs: 3"x3" or 4"x4" (Raytec). Used for small incisions.
  • Abdominal Swabs: Large, with attached 25cm tapes.
  • Peanut/Lahey Swabs: Small round gauze for blunt dissection (e.g., thyroidectomy).
  • Tonsil Swabs: Cotton filled gauze with thread.
  • Neuro-patties: Compressed rayon/cotton for delicate structures (brain/spine).
üö® Critical Safety Rule

All swabs, instruments, and needles MUST be accounted for at ALL times during an invasive procedure to prevent foreign body retention (Retained Surgical Items).

Counting Procedure

1. Pre-operative Count

  • Scrub nurse and circulating nurse count audibly together.
  • Check each swab separately. Do not count one bundle on top of another.
  • Standard bundles contain 5 swabs.
  • If a bundle is incorrect, remove the entire bundle from the theatre immediately.

2. Intra-operative Count Rules

  • Minimum of three (3) counts:
    1. Before peritoneum closure.
    2. Before fascia closure.
    3. Before skin suturing.
  • Report correct count to the surgeon, who must acknowledge it.

3. Procedure for Lost Swabs/Items

  1. Inform surgeon immediately. Do a recount.
  2. Search surgical cavity and theatre environment.
  3. If not found, inform Nurse In-Charge.
  4. X-Ray must be taken.
  5. If visible on X-ray, cavity is re-opened.
  6. If not visible, surgeon records "missing article does not show on X-Ray" in patient file.
  7. Scrub nurse endorses missing item in RED in the register.
  8. Incident report/statement written by scrub and circulating nurses.
