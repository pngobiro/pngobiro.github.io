ü•º Personal Protective Equipment (PPE)

Definition: PPE is a device worn by a worker to prevent contact with hazards present at the workplace. It is designed to protect employees from serious injuries or illness resulting from contact with chemical, radiological, physical, electrical, infective agents, or other workplace hazards.

üìã

General Principles

  • ‚û§

    Perform hand hygiene before handling and putting on PPE.

  • ‚û§

    Immediately remove PPE after completing the procedure to avoid contaminating other surfaces.

  • ‚û§

    Properly discard all single-use PPE immediately after use.

  • ‚û§

    Perform hand hygiene immediately after removing and discarding any item of PPE.

Factors Influencing Selection

  • Type of exposure anticipated (splash/spray vs. touch)
  • Category of isolation precautions
  • Durability and appropriateness for the task
  • Fit

üß§ Types of PPE

Category Items Usage Notes
Hand Protection Gloves Additional measure, not a substitute for hand washing. Not required for routine care on intact skin.
Body Protection Gowns, Aprons, Overalls, Dust coats Protect against workplace hazards. Launder reusable gowns after every use.
Face/Eye Protection Masks, Goggles, Face shields Masks cover nose/mouth against airborne pathogens. Goggles protect eyes from splashes. Do not touch front when removing.
Head & Foot Caps, Helmets, Boots, Shoe covers Surgical caps (cotton). Waterproof footwear for specific hazards.
‚ö†Ô∏è Limitations of PPE
  • They do not remove the contaminants.
  • Protection is limited (efficacy and time factor).
  • Can cause extra physical load or discomfort.
  • Proper fit can be difficult due to anatomical features.

üîÑ Donning & Doffing Sequence

Sequence for Donning (Putting On)

Perform before patient contact/entering room.

  1. 1 Gown first
  2. 2 Mask or respirator
  3. 3 Goggles or face shield
  4. 4 Gloves

Sequence for Doffing (Removing)

Remove at doorway or outside room.

  1. 1 Gloves
  2. 2 Face shield or goggles
  3. 3 Gown
  4. 4 Mask or respirator
üí° Glove Removal Technique
  1. Grasp outside edge near wrist.
  2. Peel away from hand, turning glove inside-out.
  3. Hold in opposite gloved hand.
  4. Slide un-gloved finger under the wrist of the remaining glove.
  5. Peel off from inside, creating a bag for both gloves.
  6. Discard.

üëï Theatre Attire

Theatre attire provides a barrier protecting the patient from microorganisms shed by personnel and protecting personnel from theatre contaminants.

Attire Standards

  • Material: Close-knit, antistatic, flame resistant, lint-free, and resistant to bacterial strike-through.
  • Headwear: Must cover all head and facial hair completely. Donned prior to scrub suit to avoid shedding hair.
  • Jewellery: Must be removed as it increases bacterial counts. Fingernails should be short, clean, and free of varnish. No false nails.
  • Footwear: Antistatic, well-fitting, supportive, and protective against spillages. Should be auto-clavable or suitable for decontamination.
üö´ Laundering Policy

Home laundering of theatre attire is not recommended. It must be done in an approved hospital facility to prevent cross-contamination into the home environment and ensure standards are met.

üßº Scrubbing, Gowning & Gloving

Scrubbing

Removal of microorganisms from the forearm and hands by mechanical washing and chemical disinfection. Prevents contamination of the patient.

Preparation Checklist

  • Theatre suit tucked in, sleeves rolled 3 inches above elbow.
  • Cap covering all hair.
  • Mask worn correctly (short side nose, long side chin).
  • Remove all jewelry.
  • Nails short, clean, no varnish.
  • No septic wounds or respiratory infections.

Drying Procedure

  1. Pick up towel and step back.
  2. Start with one hand: blot dry fingers, webs, palm, then back of hand.
  3. Move to forearm using circular movement up to elbow.
  4. Change towel orientation (keep wet part away) and repeat for other arm.
  5. Discard towel when finished.

Gowning Technique

Pick gown, step back, hold neck-band allowing hem to drop. Slide hands into armholes without touching the outside. A runner nurse ties the back.

‚ú® Maintaining a Sterile Field

Strict adherence to aseptic principles is critical for infection prevention.

  • ‚úì

    Only sterile items are used within a sterile field.

  • ‚úì

    Sterile personnel must not reach across unsterile areas.

  • ‚úì

    Unsterile personnel must not reach across the sterile field.

  • ‚úì

    Sterile items out of vision or below waist level are considered unsterile.

  • ‚úì

    Edges of a sterile field are considered unsterile.

  • ‚úì

    If in doubt about sterility, consider it unsterile.

  • ‚úì

    Sterile drapes: Only the top surface is sterile.
