ğŸ¥ Team Composition

Successful surgery relies on the seamless collaboration of a multidisciplinary team. The core members include:

  • The Surgeon: Performs the procedure.
  • The Anaesthesiologist: Manages anesthesia and vital signs.
  • The Nurse Manager: Oversees theatre operations.
  • The Scrub Nurse: Maintains the sterile field.
  • The Circulating Nurse: Coordinates activities outside the sterile field.
  • Receiving Area Nurse: Manages patient admission to the suite.

ğŸ§¼ Role of the Scrub Nurse

The scrub nurse works directly within the sterile field. Their primary duties involve maintaining asepsis and anticipating the surgeon's needs.

ğŸ’¡ Key Responsibility

The scrub nurse must remain sterile until the patient leaves the operating room and is responsible for the accuracy of swab and instrument counts.

Key Duties

  • Preparation: Checks the next day's list, orders sterile packs, and prepares the theatre (cleanliness, machine checks).
  • Sterile Field: Scrubs, gowns, and gloves. Sets up the sterile trolley, checking instruments for correctness and function.
  • Counts: Counts instruments, swabs, and sharps audibly with the circulating nurse.
  • Intra-operative: Anticipates surgeon's needs, passes instruments, receives specimens (hands off to circulating nurse), and assists with wound closure.
  • Post-operative: Checks patient for diathermy burns, clears theatre, hands over instruments for cleaning, and ensures notes are written.
03

ğŸ”„ Role of the Circulating Nurse

The circulating nurse manages the nursing care within the operating room outside the sterile field, acting as a link between the sterile team and the external environment.

Key Duties

  • Patient Care: Admits patient to the OR, assists in positioning, induction, catheterization, and skin preparation.
  • Team Support: Assists the surgical team with gowning (tying backs), connects machines, and anticipates needs (supplies).
  • Documentation & Safety: Maintains records, labels specimens received from the scrub nurse, and continuously monitors aseptic technique.
  • Counts: Records the counts of swabs and instruments performed with the scrub nurse.
  • Post-operative: Removes diathermy plate (checks for burns), assists in transfer to recovery, and prepares theatre for the next case.

  • Preparation: Checks emergency equipment (suction, defibrillator), drugs, and receives handover reports.
  • Assessment: Performs initial assessment upon admission (airway, breathing, circulation, consciousness).
