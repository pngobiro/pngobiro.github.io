üìê Design Considerations

Proper design of the operating theatre is crucial for infection control, safety, and efficiency. The design must allow a one-way flow of traffic to prevent the return flow of contaminants into the clean area.

Key Factors Influencing Design

  • Number, type, and length of surgical procedures.
  • Type and distribution of surgical specialties and their equipment needs.
  • Safety of staff, patients, and other personnel during construction and renovation.
  • Scheduling policies (hours per day/week in use).
  • Patient volumes and hospital size.
  • Planned technology and future expansion plans.
  • Systems for efficient patient, staff, and supplies flow.
‚ö†Ô∏è Traffic Principle

The cardinal rule of theatre design: Operated patients should not meet with un-operated patients.

üß± Physical Environment Standards

Size & Dimensions

  • Theatres should ideally be of equal sizes.
  • Standard measurement: 20ft x 20ft x 10ft (Length x Width x Height) or 400 sq.ft.
  • Larger rooms are required for complicated procedures like cardiac surgery.

Surface Materials

Component Requirements
Walls Solid without windows. Pastel colour (e.g., baby blue) to reduce glare. Stainless cuffs at collision corners. Materials must be hard, non-porous, fire resistant, and waterproof.
Ceiling Minimum 10ft high, seamless construction. White colour to reflect at least 90% of light.
Floors Antistatic material (Terrazzo or seamless PVC). Slip-proof when wet. Curved skirting (6 inches up wall). Easy to clean via flooding/wet vacuum.
Doors ~4ft wide sliding doors (swung open if necessary). Should not remain open during procedures.

Lighting

  • Fluorescent Lighting: Best for general illumination with emergency backup. White light preferred in patient areas (blue light may mimic cyanosis).
  • Overhead Operating Light:
    • Shadowless (tungsten/incandescent with heat filters).
    • Adjustable intensity (dimmer knob).
    • Autoclavable handle covers to allow surgeon adjustment.

Ventilation

  • Air flow directed from clean to less clean areas.
  • Relative humidity maintained at 50-55%.
  • Crucial to prevent heat/water loss in small babies and maintain comfort.

üö¶ Traffic Control & Zoning

Proper zoning minimizes infection risk by restricting access based on attire and sterility.

‚ñ∂ 1. Unrestricted Area

Traffic is not limited. Personnel may wear street clothes. Examples: Staff lounge, locker rooms.

‚ñ∂ 2. Semi-Restricted Area

Includes processing/storage areas and corridors leading to restricted zones.

  • Personnel: Must wear surgical attire (scrubs) and caps.
  • Patients: Must wear gowns and hair coverings.
  • Only authorized personnel allowed.
‚ñ∂ 3. Restricted Area

Includes operating rooms, clean core, and scrub sink areas.

  • Requirements: Surgical attire, masks, and caps are mandatory.
  • Contains open sterile supplies and scrubbed personnel.
  • Sterile procedures performed here.

Location Strategy

The theatre should be a terminal location to prevent unrelated traffic from passing through. It should be accessible to:

  • Critical Care Unit
  • Sterile Supplies Area
  • Laboratory Department
  • Radiology Department
