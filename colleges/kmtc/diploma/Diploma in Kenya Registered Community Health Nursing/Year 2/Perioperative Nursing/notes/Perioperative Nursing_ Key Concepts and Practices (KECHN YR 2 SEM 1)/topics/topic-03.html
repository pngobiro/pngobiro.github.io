üåü Core Competency Domains

Competence is defined as the proven ability to use knowledge, skills, and personal and/or methodological abilities in work or study situations.

The core competencies for perioperative nursing are based on the following 5 domains:

‚ñ∂ Domain 1: Professional, Legal, Ethical Practice

The perioperative nurse will analyse situations and events, understand and make professional clinical judgment, uphold ethical practice, and maintain respect for the patient.

Required Knowledge: Legislation, Perioperative care, Professional ethics, Professional regulations, Policies/guidelines, Perioperative philosophy, Concept of perioperative practice.

‚ñ∂ Domain 2: Perioperative Care and Practice

The nurse provides quality care by adopting a holistic and individual approach in accordance with relevant legislation. This covers assessment, planning, implementation, and evaluation of care.

Key Areas: Anatomy/Physiology, Pharmacology, Surgical/Anaesthetic techniques, Infection prevention, Pain management, Technology use, Risk management.

‚ñ∂ Domain 3: Interpersonal Relationships & Communication

Establish efficient communication with the patient/family and the multidisciplinary team. This involves non-technical skills, human factors, and nurturing respectful relationships.

Key Areas: Communication technique, Health education, Problem solving, Inter-professional pedagogy.

‚ñ∂ Domain 4: Organisational, Management & Leadership

Lead and manage groups of equals and other professionals, considering organizational purposes and results.

Key Areas: Principles of organisation, Emotional intelligence, Financial implications, Quality assurance, Material management, Conflict management.

‚ñ∂ Domain 5: Education, Professional Development & Research

Develop an enquiring mind regarding work and profession, using research to build a professional portfolio and meet evolving requirements.

Key Areas: Self-learning, Personal growth, Applied research, Evidence-based practice.

üîÑ The Peri-operative Phases

The term peri-operative encompasses the entire surgical experience. It begins when the patient is informed of the need for surgery and continues until the patient resumes their usual activities.

üéØ Primary Goal

To prepare the client mentally and physically for surgery and to assist in full recovery in the shortest time possible with the least discomfort.

  1. 1
    Pre-operative Phase

    Begins when a decision is made to perform a surgical procedure and ends when the client enters the operating room.

  2. 2
    Intra-operative Phase

    Begins when the client arrives in the surgical area and lasts until admission to the Post Anaesthesia Care Unit (PACU).

  3. 3
    Post-operative Phase

    Begins upon admission to the PACU and continues through the entire recovery phase until discharge/resumption of usual activities.

üè• Pre-operative Nursing Care

Nursing Goals

  • Assessing for risk factors
  • Emotional support of client
  • Client teaching
  • Physical preparation of client

Nursing Assessment

History Component Key Considerations
Patient Profile Age, Weight, Height
Medical History Chronic conditions, Allergies, Prior surgeries
Medication Current drugs, Drug history
Anaesthesia Personal & familial history, complications
Lifestyle Alcohol, smoking, exercise habits

Common Nursing Diagnoses

  • Anxiety: Related to situational crisis, change in health status, fear of unknown/pain/disfigurement.
  • Knowledge Deficit: Related to pre/post operative procedures.
  • Disturbed Sleep: Related to anxiety about upcoming surgery.

ü©∫ Nursing Interventions & Teaching

Emotional Support

  • Utilize positive communication techniques (touch, eye contact, validating statements).
  • Active listening and encouraging verbalization of fears.
  • Avoid false reassurance or judgmental statements.

Client Teaching

Crucial for preventing postoperative complications:

ü´Å Pulmonary Exercises

Diaphragmatic Breathing: Hands on ribs, inhale through nose allowing abdomen to expand, hold 3-5 sec, exhale through pursed lips. Repeat 10x/hr while awake.

  • Leg Exercises: Dorsi/Plantar flexion, ankle rotation, knee/hip flexion. Perform 5x each leg/hr while awake to promote circulation.
  • Ambulation: Discuss importance of early ambulation and safe method for getting out of bed.
  • Turning & Positioning: Use of side rails for assistance.
  • Use of Devices: Explanation of Sequential Compression Devices (SCDs) or external pneumatic compression to prevent DVT (Deep Vein Thrombosis).
