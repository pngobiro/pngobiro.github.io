Causes of Renal Trauma

  • Pelvic fractures
  • Road traffic accidents (RTA)
  • Small internal lacerations of the kidney
  • Penetrating or perforating wounds
  • Pelvic surgery
  • Blunt crushing injuries
  • Radiation therapy

Signs of Injury

  • Hematuria: Blood in the urine.
  • Oliguria: Decreased urinary output.
  • Hypotension: Low blood pressure if severe shock is present.
  • Pain: Renal colic due to clots or fragmentations obstructing the collecting system.

ü©∫ Diagnosis & Investigations

  • History Taking: Mechanism of injury (blunt vs. penetrating).
  • Physical Examination: Palpation for masses or tenderness.
  • Urinalysis: To note hematuria.
  • Blood Work: Hemoglobin (Hb) and Hematocrit (Hct) levels to assess blood loss.
  • Intravenous Pyelogram (IVP): To assess kidney function and structural integrity.

üè• Management

üéØ

Aims of Management

  • To control hemorrhage, pain, and infection.
  • To preserve and restore renal function.
  • To maintain urinary drainage.
  • To prevent loss of electrolyte balance.

1. Active Resuscitative Management

  • Treatment of shock if present.
  • Control bleeding.
  • Maintain blood pressure and establish urinary flow.
  • Observe vital signs 4-hourly.
  • Save all urine and send to laboratory for investigations.
  • Record time of voiding and volume.
  • Monitor for signs of oliguria or hemorrhage.
  • Detect presence of hematuria.
  • Palpate areas around lower ribs, upper lumbar vertebrae, flank, and abdomen for palpable mass and tenderness.
  • Outline the area of original mass with a marking pencil to monitor expansion.

2. Conservative Management

Indicated in minor injuries.

  • Complete bed rest.
  • Administer intravenous fluids.
  • Antimicrobial drugs to prevent infection.
  • Meticulous observation and evaluation of patient for the first few days.
  • Monitor vital signs to detect evidence of deterioration.

3. Surgical Intervention

  • Removal or repair of the affected kidney, urethra, or bladder (Nephrectomy/Repair).
  • Pre- and post-operative care.
  • Educate the patient on home health care.
  • Ensure follow-up.
