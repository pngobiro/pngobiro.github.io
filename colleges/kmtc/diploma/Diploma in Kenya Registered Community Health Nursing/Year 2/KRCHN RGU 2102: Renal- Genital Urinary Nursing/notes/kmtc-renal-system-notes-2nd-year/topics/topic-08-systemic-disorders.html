Good Pasture Syndrome

A combination of alveolar hemorrhage and glomerular hemorrhage leading to anti-basement membrane antibodies. Common in young men.

Clinical Presentation

  • Haemoptysis: Coughing up blood (lung involvement).
  • Anemia: Iron deficiency due to bleeding.
  • Kidney Disease: Interstitial nephritis and renal vein thrombosis may lead to nephrotic syndrome.

Characterized by non-thrombocytopenic purpura.

  • Arthralgia (joint pain).
  • Abdominal pain.
  • Glomerulonephritis (Proteinuria & Haematuria).
  • Serum IgA is raised.

Alport's Syndrome

Consists of sensory neurodeafness associated with hereditary nephritis.

  • Manifests at early age.
  • Men are more affected.
  • Presents with recurrent haematuria.
  • May show thrombocytopenia.

üë™ Polycystic Kidney Disease (PKD)

Genetically determined abnormality of structure, often associated with cystic liver.

Infantile Type

Rare. Inherited as Autosomal Recessive. Usually fatal within the first year of life.

Adult Type

Autosomal Dominant. May be discovered in infancy but symptoms develop later. Both kidneys affected, enlarged with cortical cysts, fibrosis, and atherosclerosis.

Clinical Features

  • Pain in renal angles.
  • Haematuria.
  • Frequent upper urinary tract infections.
  • Slow development of Hypertension.
  • Signs of renal failure.
