üîç Overview & Definitions

Renal failure results when the kidneys cannot remove the body‚Äôs metabolic wastes or perform their regulatory functions. The substances normally excreted in urine accumulate in the body fluids as a result of impaired renal excretion, affecting endocrine and metabolic functions as well as fluid, electrolyte, and acid‚Äìbase disturbances.

Renal failure is a systemic disease and is a final common pathway of many different kidney and urinary tract diseases.

Acute Renal Failure (ARF) / Acute Kidney Injury (AKI)

Is a rapid loss of renal function due to damage to the kidneys. Potentially life-threatening metabolic complications can occur, including metabolic acidosis & fluid and electrolyte imbalances.

Diagnostic Criteria & Urine Volume

A widely accepted criterion for ARF is a 50% or greater increase in serum creatinine above baseline (normal creatinine is less than 1.0 mg/dL).

Urine volume changes:

  • Oliguria: Less than 500 mL/day
  • Non-oliguria: Greater than 800 mL/day
  • Anuria: Less than 50 mL/day

üóÇÔ∏è Categories of Acute Renal Failure

Conditions that reduce blood flow to the kidney and impair kidney function include hypovolemia, hypotension, reduced cardiac output, and obstruction. If these conditions are treated and corrected before the kidneys are permanently damaged, the signs may be reversed.

Acute Tubular Necrosis (ATN)

Characteristics of ATN include intratubular obstruction, tubular back leak (abnormal reabsorption of filtrate), vasoconstriction, and changes in glomerular permeability. These result in decreased GFR, progressive azotemia, and fluid/electrolyte imbalances.

03

‚è±Ô∏è Phases of Acute Renal Failure

  • 1
    Initiation Phase: Begins with the initial insult and ends when oliguria develops.
  • 2
    Oliguria Phase: Accompanied by an increase in serum concentration of substances usually excreted by the kidneys (urea, creatinine, uric acid, potassium, magnesium).
    Note: The minimum amount of urine needed to rid the body of normal metabolic waste is 400 ml. In this phase, uremic symptoms appear and life-threatening hyperkalemia develops.
  • 3
    Diuresis Phase: Marked by a gradual increase in urine output, signaling that glomerular filtration has started to recover. Lab values stabilize and decrease.
    Warning: The patient must be observed closely for dehydration; if dehydration occurs, uremic symptoms may increase.
  • 4
    Recovery Phase: Signals the improvement of renal function and may take 3 to 12 months. Lab values return to normal. A permanent 1% to 3% reduction in GFR is common but not clinically significant.

ü©∫ Assessment & Diagnostics

Almost every system of the body is affected. The patient may appear critically ill and lethargic. CNS symptoms include drowsiness, headache, muscle twitching, and seizures.

Laboratory Findings

  • Urine Specific Gravity: Low (fixed) compared to normal 1.010‚Äì1.025. Indicates inability to concentrate urine.
  • Sodium:
    Pre-renal: Decreased urine sodium (< 20 mEq/L).
    Intra-renal: Increased urine sodium (> 40 mEq/L) with casts.
  • BUN & Creatinine: BUN increases steadily based on catabolism; Creatinine increases with glomerular damage.
  • Hyperkalemia: High serum potassium due to protein catabolism. May lead to ventricular tachycardia and cardiac arrest.
  • Metabolic Acidosis: Decrease in blood pH and CO2-combining power due to inability to eliminate acid-type substances.
  • Calcium/Phosphate: Increased blood phosphate, decreased calcium.
  • Anemia: Due to reduced erythropoietin, uremic GI lesions, and reduced RBC lifespan.

üíä Medical Management & Prevention

Prevention Strategy

Factors influencing mortality include increased age, comorbid conditions, and pre-existing diseases. Prevention steps include:

  • Monitor Nephrotoxic Agents: (Gentamicin, tobramycin, contrast agents, NSAIDs). Check BUN/Creatinine at baseline and twice weekly.
  • Hydration: Adequate hydration before/during/after surgery and diagnostic studies.
  • Shock Treatment: Treat hypotension and shock promptly with blood/fluids.
  • Infection Control: Prevent infections (indwelling catheters) and treat promptly.

Management Objectives

Objectives are to restore normal chemical balance and prevent complications until renal function is restored.

  • Pre-renal: Optimize renal perfusion (IV fluids, blood products, albumin).
  • Post-renal: Relieve the obstruction.
  • Intra-renal: Supportive therapy, removal of causative agents.

Pharmacologic Therapy: Hyperkalemia

Hyperkalemia is the most life-threatening imbalance (K+ > 5.0 mEq/L). ECG changes include tall, tented, or peaked T waves.

Treatment:

  • Kayexalate (Sodium polystyrene sulfonate): Cation-exchange resin (oral or enema). Exchanges Na+ for K+ in the intestine. Sorbitol may be used to induce diarrhea.
  • Emergency Measures (Hemodynamically unstable): IV Dextrose 50% + Insulin, Calcium replacement (to shift K+ into cells).
  • Albuterol: Can lower plasma K+ by 0.5 to 1.5 mEq/L.

Note: The shift of potassium into cells is temporary; dialysis may be needed.

Nutritional Therapy

ARF causes severe nutritional imbalances due to nausea, vomiting, and increased tissue catabolism.

  • Diet: High-carbohydrate meals (protein-sparing effect). Protein is restricted but individualized.
  • Restrictions: Foods containing potassium (bananas, citrus, coffee) or phosphorus are restricted.
  • Monitoring: Weigh daily. Patient loses 0.2 to 0.5 kg daily if nitrogen balance is negative. Weight gain implies fluid retention.

üë©‚Äç‚öïÔ∏è Nursing Management

Monitoring Fluid & Electrolyte Balance

Hyperkalemia: Monitor serum electrolytes and physical signs (irritability, muscle weakness, paresthesia, ECG changes). Screen all oral/IV intake for hidden potassium.

Fluid Status:

  • Monitor IV fluid intake meticulously (use smallest volumes for meds).
  • Monitor Urine Output, Edema, Distended Neck Veins.
  • Auscultate lungs for crackles (Pulmonary Edema).
  • Accurate daily weights and I&O records are essential.

Psychological Support: Provide support to patient and family, explaining treatments and condition status.
