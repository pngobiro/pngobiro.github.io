üî¢ Types of Amputation

1. Guillotine or Open Amputation

Used when infection is present or probable. The wound is left open to drain.

  • Secondary closure
  • Plastic repair
  • Revision of the stump
  • Re-amputation

2. Closed Amputation

Here the skin is closed primarily. This is performed when there is no evidence of infection and the skin flaps have good blood supply.

‚ö†Ô∏è Complications

Potential complications following amputation include:

  • Haematoma
  • Infections
  • Skin flap necrosis
  • Deformities of joints
  • Neuroma (Painful growth of nerve cells at the nerve ending)
  • Phantom sensations (Sensation that the amputated limb is still present)
04

üéØ Goals of Management

Management for a client who has undergone amputation focuses on:

  • Pain management
  • Psychological counseling
  • Dressing (Proper stump wrapping/dressing)
  • Skin care
  • Prevention of deformity, maintain ROM (Range of Motion), strengthening
