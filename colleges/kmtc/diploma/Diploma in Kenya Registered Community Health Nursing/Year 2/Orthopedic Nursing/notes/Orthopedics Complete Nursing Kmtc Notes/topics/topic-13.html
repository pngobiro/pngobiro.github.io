Rehabilitation is a crucial phase in orthopedic care. The core principles can be remembered using the mnemonic "ATC IS IT".

üí°

ATC IS IT Mnemonic

  • A Avoid Aggravation: Do not aggravate the injury during rehabilitation. Therapeutic exercise, if administered incorrectly, can exacerbate the injury. Advance gradually.
  • T Timing: Begin as soon as possible without causing aggravation. Rest is necessary, but too much rest can be detrimental.
  • C Compliance: Success depends on a compliant patient. Inform the patient of the content and expected course to ensure cooperation.
  • I Individualization: Each person responds differently. Physiological and chemical differences affect responses to injury.
  • S Specific Sequencing: Follow a specific sequence determined by the body's physiological healing response.
  • I Intensity: Must challenge the patient without overtaxing the injury. Requires observation of the patient's response.
  • T Total Patient: Treat the whole person, not just the injury. Maintain cardiovascular system, ROM, and strength of uninjured areas.
02

üìà 5 Stages of Rehabilitation

  1. Phase 1: Control Pain and Swelling.
  2. Phase 2: Improve Range of Motion and/or Flexibility.
  3. Phase 3: Improve Strength & Begin Proprioception/Balance Training.
  4. Phase 4: Proprioception/Balance Training & Sport-Specific Training.
  5. Phase 5: Gradual Return to Full Activity.

‚öñÔ∏è Rights of Persons with Disabilities

Based on Article 54 of the Kenyan Constitution, persons with disabilities (PWDs) are entitled to specific rights:

‚ñ∂ 1. Dignity and Respect

Section 1(a): Requires that PWDs be addressed and referred to in a manner that is not demeaning. Advocates for non-discrimination on any basis and requires all persons not to prejudice PWDs because of their disability.

‚ñ∂ 2. Access to Educational Institutions

Section 1(b): Educational facilities must be made comfortable for PWDs. Advocates for accessibility, reasonable accommodation, and universal design (ramps, elevators, handrails) so structures can be accessed without strain.

‚ñ∂ 3. Reasonable Access to Places/Information

Section 1(c): Government must ensure public infrastructures (buildings, roads) and transport are friendly/accessible. Advocates for Universal Design: products and environments usable by all people to the greatest extent possible without adaptation.

‚ñ∂ 4. Sign Language & Braille

Ensures PWDs with visual/hearing impairments can communicate. Article 7 promotes the use of indigenous languages, Kenyan Sign Language (KSL), Braille, and other formats. KSL could become the 3rd official language (KSL Bill 2021).

‚ñ∂ 5. Accessibility of Materials/Devices

Article 54(1)(e): Entitles PWDs to assistive devices to become as independent as possible (e.g., crutches, wheelchairs, hearing aids).
