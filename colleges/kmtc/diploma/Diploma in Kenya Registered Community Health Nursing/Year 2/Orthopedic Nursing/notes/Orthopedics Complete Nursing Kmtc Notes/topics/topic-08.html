ü¶¥ Arthritis Overview

Arthritis is the swelling and tenderness of one or more joints.

Types of Arthritis

Inflammatory Arthritis

Occurs as a defense against viruses/bacteria or response to injuries. Examples: RA, reactive arthritis, ankylosing spondylitis, psoriatic arthritis.

Degenerative/Mechanical

Mainly involves damage to the cartilage that covers the ends of the bones (e.g., Osteoarthritis).

Connective Tissue Disease (CTD)

Examples: SLE (Lupus), scleroderma, dermatomyositis, Sjogren‚Äôs.

Infectious Arthritis

Caused by bacterium, virus, or fungus entering a joint (e.g., salmonella, shigella, chlamydia, gonorrhea, hepatitis C).

Metabolic Arthritis

Caused by abnormal metabolism, such as uric acid buildup in Gout.

Childhood Arthritis

Juvenile idiopathic arthritis (JIA) / Juvenile rheumatoid arthritis (JRA). Can cause permanent damage.

General Management

  • Medication: Analgesics (Tylenol), NSAIDs (Ibuprofen), Counterirritants (menthol/capsaicin), DMARDs (Methotrexate), Biologics (Enbrel), Corticosteroids.
  • Physical Therapy: Specific exercises, heat/ice packs, massage.
  • Diet: Mediterranean diet (fish, nuts, fruits, olive oil) may help reduce inflammation.
  • Surgery: Synovectomy, Total Joint Replacement (TJR), Osteotomy, Joint fusion.

üñêÔ∏è Rheumatoid Arthritis (RA)

Rheumatoid arthritis (RA) is a chronic autoimmune disease that causes joints to swell and can result in pain, stiffness, and progressive loss of function. It often affects pairs of joints (symmetric) and can lead to permanent joint damage and deformity.

üß¨

Pathophysiology

  1. Antigen Presenting Cell (APC) (usually a macrophage in synovium) engulfs the antigen.
  2. Enzymes break down antigen; processed antigens are transported to APC surface.
  3. Antigen presented to T-cells (CD4/CD8).
  4. APC secretes cytokines: Interleukin-1 (IL-1), Interferon-alpha/gamma, Tumor necrosis factor (TNF).
  5. APC secretes enzymes (Lysozymes, Elastases, Collagenases) causing cartilage breakdown.
  6. Synoviocytes proliferate and produce IL-6, Prostaglandins (pain), and Matrix Metalloproteases.

Symptoms

Joint pain & tenderness
Joint swelling & redness
Joint warmth
Joint stiffness
Polyarthritis (many joints)
Symmetric involvement

Diagnosis & Treatment

Diagnosis Treatment
  • Physical examination
  • Blood test (Antibody test, ESR)
  • X-Ray / MRI
  • NSAIDs, Steroids
  • DMARDs (Disease-modifying antirheumatic drugs)
  • Surgery (Synovectomy, Tendon repair, Total joint replacement)

üö∂ Ankylosing Spondylitis (AS)

A form of arthritis which mainly affects the spine and also affects other joints, leading to severe chronic pain and discomfort.

  • Local AS: Affects any one joint.
  • Generalised AS: Affects more than one joint.

Clinical Presentation

  • Stiffness and pain in lower back, buttocks, hips after inactivity.
  • Back pain relieved by movement.
  • Difficulty in bending.
  • Straightening of normal curvature of spine ("Poker spine").
  • Fatigue, weight loss, difficulty taking breath.

Complications

Uveitis, Pulmonary fibrosis, Cardiac arrhythmias, Inflammatory Bowel Disease, Amyloidosis, Osteoporosis, Cauda equina syndrome.

ü¶∂ Gout

Gout is a metabolic disorder characterized by elevated serum uric acid levels (hyperuricemia) and deposits of urate crystals in synovial fluids and surrounding tissues.

Derived from Latin gutta ("a drop").

Etiology

  • Primary Gout: Hereditary.
  • Secondary Gout: Internal/external factors (e.g., diet, kidney problems, obesity). High dietary intake of purines, alcohol.

Clinical Manifestation

  • Severe pain at the joint
  • Redness and Inflammation
  • Fever
  • Tophi (chalky nodules)

Medical Treatment

Drug Action/Usage Side Effects
Colchicine (Colcrys) Tablet Mild nausea, stomach pain, mild diarrhea
Allopurinol Tablet Upset stomach, diarrhea, drowsiness
Probenecid Tablet (2x daily) Headache, vomiting, loss of appetite, dizziness
Corticosteroid (Prednisone) Tablet Fluid retention, increased BP, mood swings

Nursing Care Plan

  • Acute Pain: Assess pain (1-10), cold packs, rest/relaxation.
  • Activity Intolerance: Monitor inflammation, assist with ADLs, passive ROM.
  • Hyperthermia: Monitor vital signs, tepid sponge bath.

ü©∫ Pelvic Inflammatory Disease (PID)

PID is an infection of one or more of the upper reproductive organs (uterus, fallopian tubes, ovaries).

  • Causes: Sexually transmitted bacteria (Gonorrhea, Chlamydia) spreading from vagina to upper organs. Can also occur after childbirth, miscarriage, or abortion.
  • Symptoms: Lower abdominal/pelvic pain, unusual discharge, bleeding, pain during sex, fever, painful urination.
  • Complications: Ectopic pregnancy, Infertility, Chronic pelvic pain, Tubo-ovarian abscess.
