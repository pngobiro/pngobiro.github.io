ü©∫ Cancer of the Larynx

It is a malignant tumor in and around the larynx, mostly a squamous cell carcinoma.

Predisposing Factors

  • Carcinogens: Tobacco, alcohol, asbestos, tar products, cement dust.
  • Straining the voice.
  • Chronic laryngitis.
  • Nutritional deficiencies.
  • Age (> 60 years) and Family history.
  • History of alcohol abuse.

Early Signs & Symptoms

  • Hoarseness: Harsh, raspy, low pitched voice > 2 weeks.
  • Persistent cough.
  • Sore throat.
  • Pain/burning in throat (esp. with hot liquids/citrus).
  • Lump felt in throat.

Later Symptoms

  • Dysphagia (difficulty swallowing).
  • Dyspnea.
  • Unilateral nasal obstruction/discharge.
  • Foul breath.
  • Metastasis: Weight loss, cervical lymphadenopathy, ear pain.

Categories & Diagnosis

Tumor Categories
  • Supraglottic: Involves false vocal cords.
  • Glottic: Involves true vocal cords.
  • Subglottic: Downward extension from vocal cords.
Diagnostic Tests History and physical exam, Laryngoscopy, Histology of tumor sample.

üî™ Medical & Surgical Management

Treatment involves Radiation (early stages or combination) and Surgery (Laryngectomy).

Surgical Options

‚ñ∂ Partial Laryngectomy

Removal of part of the larynx, one vocal cord, and tumor. The airway remains intact.

‚ñ∂ Supraglottic Laryngectomy

Removal of hyoid bone, glottis, and false vocal cords.

  • Tracheostomy tube left in place until glottic airway is established.
  • Enteral feeds given until healing occurs.
  • Risk: Aspiration is a potential complication.
  • Voice is preserved but may change.
‚ñ∂ Hemilaryngectomy

Removal of one true cord, one false cord, and the tumor.

  • Patient has NGT and tracheostomy for several days post-op.
  • High risk of aspiration.
  • Voice changes but airway/swallowing remain intact.
‚ñ∂ Total Laryngectomy

Removal of all laryngeal structures ‚Äì hyoid bone, epiglottis, cricoid cartilage, and 2-3 tracheal rings.

Permanent Changes

Voice is lost completely. A permanent tracheostomy (stoma) is created.

üë©‚Äç‚öïÔ∏è Nursing Care (Laryngectomy)

Assessment

  • Health History: Hoarseness, sore throat, dyspnea, dysphagia, pain.
  • Physical Exam: Head/neck exam, airway patency, palpate for swelling/adenopathy.
  • General: Nutritional status (BMI), Psychosocial readiness.

Key Nursing Diagnoses

  • Ineffective airway clearance
  • Impaired verbal communication
  • Imbalanced nutrition: less than body requirements
  • Disturbed body image
  • Anxiety/Depression

Interventions

1. Pre-operative Care

  • Clarify misconceptions (e.g., effect on speech).
  • Communication: Establish methods for post-op communication (writing, hand signals, call bell).
  • Teaching: Coughing/deep breathing exercises.

2. Post-operative Care

‚ñ∂ Airway Management
  • Position: Fowler's or Semi-Fowler's to reduce edema and promote lung expansion.
  • Monitor: Signs of restlessness, dyspnea, lung sounds.
  • Suctioning: To remove excess secretions. Encourage turn, cough, deep breathe.
  • Tube Care: Clean stoma daily. Humidify air to prevent crusting/excess mucus.
‚ñ∂ Nutrition & Hydration
  • NPO initially. IV fluids/TPN.
  • Enteral feeding via NGT or Gastrostomy.
  • Oral Feeds: Start with thick liquids (easier to swallow). Keep suction nearby.
‚ñ∂ Communication & Body Image
  • Use established non-verbal methods. Be patient.
  • Encourage expression of feelings regarding disfigurement and voice loss.
  • Refer to support groups.

Managing Complications

  • Respiratory Distress/Hypoxia: Restlessness, agitation, tachypnea, confusion. Cyanosis is a late sign. Check patency, reposition, administer O2.
  • Hemorrhage: Monitor surgical site/drains/trachea. Watch for tachycardia, hypotension, cold clammy skin.
  • Wound Breakdown: Danger of carotid artery rupture. Observe stoma site carefully.
  • Aspiration: Keep suction near. Elevate head of bed >30¬∞ during and 30-45 mins after feedings. Check tube placement.

Discharge Teaching

  • Tracheostomy/Stoma care.
  • Importance of humidification (avoid air conditioning).
  • Safety: Protect stoma during shower (prevent water entry). No swimming.
  • Oral care to prevent infection/halitosis.
