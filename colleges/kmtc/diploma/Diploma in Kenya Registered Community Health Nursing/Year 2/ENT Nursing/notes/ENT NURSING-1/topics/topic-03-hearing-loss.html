üîç Definition & Types

Hearing loss ranges from minor difficulty in understanding words or hearing certain sounds to total deafness.

Types of Hearing Loss

‚ñ∂ A) Conductive Hearing Loss

Also called transmission hearing loss. Conduction of sound waves to the organs of hearing is disrupted.

  • Air Conduction Loss: Due to a defect in the external auditory canal.
  • Bone Conduction Loss: Due to a defect in the bones of the middle ear.

Causes:

  • Obstruction: Impacted cerumen, foreign body, marked swelling of canal walls, otomycosis.
  • Tympanic Membrane Abnormalities: Perforation, thickening, retraction, scarring.
  • Middle Ear Pathology: Otosclerosis (ossicle fixation), secretions, granulations, otitis media.
‚ñ∂ B) Sensorineural (Perceptive) Hearing Loss

Involves a disturbance of the organs of the inner ear or the transmitting nerve.

  • Sensory: Due to damage in the cochlea.
  • Neural: Due to damage of the eighth cranial nerve.

Causes:

  • Toxic Neuritis: Mumps (common cause of unilateral deafness), influenza, diphtheria, rubella.
  • Trauma: Blows or falls causing labyrinth concussion or base of skull fractures; industrial noise, gunfire.
  • Drugs/Poisons (Ototoxicity): Quinine, salicylates, aminoglycosides, arsenic, mercury, lead.
  • Other: Meningitis, Old age (Presbycusis), Acoustic Neuroma, Meniere's disease, Congenital predisposition.
‚ñ∂ Other Types
  • C) Central Hearing Loss: The brain is unable to interpret sound waves after transmission. Deficit is in the auditory nucleus of the cerebral cortex. May occur in atherosclerosis or after CVA.
  • D) Functional Hearing Loss: No organic cause found; no damage to auditory nerve. Believed to arise from psychological or emotional conditions.
  • E) Mixed Hearing Loss: Patient has both conductive and sensorineural hearing loss.

üö© Clinical Manifestations

Early/Common Signs

  • Tinnitus
  • Inability to hear in groups
  • Answering questions inappropriately
  • Cupping hand around ear
  • Showing irritability with others
  • Increased volume of TV or radio

Later Manifestations

  • Speech deterioration: Slurring, flat tone, loud or soft voice.
  • Fatigue: From straining to listen.
  • Social withdrawal: Indifference, loneliness.
  • Insecurity & Indecision: Lack of self-confidence.
  • Suspiciousness: Paranoia about conversations.
  • False pride: Concealing hearing loss.

Risk Factors

Family history of sensorineural hearing loss, congenital malformations, low birth weight (< 1.5 kg), use of ototoxic medication, viral infections (mumps, measles), age, and exposure to loud noise.

üíä Management

Management aims at restoring or assisting hearing, aural rehabilitation, and managing tinnitus.

1. Restore Hearing

  • Antibiotics for bacterial infections.
  • Acyclovir or oral corticosteroids for herpes virus infection.
  • Stop any ototoxic medications to lessen progress or reverse loss.

2. Assist Hearing

Hearing Aids

Amplify sound. Components include:

  • Microphone: Receives sound waves, converts to electrical signals.
  • Amplifier: Increases signal strength.
  • Receiver/Loudspeaker: Converts signals back to sound waves.
  • Battery: Power source.

3. Implantable Hearing Devices

  • Cochlear Implant: Contains a computer chip changing spoken word to electrical impulses transmitted via auditory nerve endings in the cochlea to the brain.
  • Temporal Bone Stimulation: For conductive hearing loss. External part transmits sound to inner device implanted in skull, conducting sound via bone to inner ear.

4. Aural Rehabilitation

Aims at maximizing communication skills.

  • Speech/Lip Reading: Integration of lip movements, gestures, clues.
  • Sign Language: Using hands to represent words.
  • Non-verbal aids: Hand movements, avoiding careless expressions.
  • Verbal aids: Speak normally/slowly, don't exaggerate, simple sentences, avoid shouting, move closer to better ear.

Prevention of Hearing Loss

Primary: Minimize risks (noise, trauma, ototoxic drugs, infection).

Secondary: Early detection (screening) and referral.

Tertiary: Rehabilitation programs to optimize functioning.
