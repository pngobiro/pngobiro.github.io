üìò Definition & Types

Definition: Infertility is the inability of a couple to conceive or to get a child after two years of regular coitus without having used any form of contraception.

Most normal couples achieve a pregnancy within a few months of trying. Failure to conceive after 2 years may be arbitrarily defined as infertility provided the normal intercourse is taking place less than 4 times a week.

Primary Infertility

Is the term used for a couple who have never achieved a pregnancy at any time after 1 year of unprotected sex.

Secondary Infertility

Refers to a couple who have previously succeeded in achieving at least one pregnancy even if this ended in spontaneous abortion being unable to conceive again.

üìä

Statistics

Statistics show that at least 10% of all couples have an infertility problem. 30% may be the male partners' contribution, 40% the woman's contribution and 30% the responsibility of both partners.

üë© Causes of Infertility in Female

  • Defective Implantation
    • Major cause is tubal blockage due to PID (in Uganda especially). This contributes to 60 ‚Äì 70%.
    • Abnormalities of the uterus (e.g., bicornuate uterus, Didelphys uterus).
    • Uterine fibroids (gross) causing an irregular implantation surface.
    • Endometritis, Severe inflammation of the endometrium also called synache.
    • Stenosed Cervix due to trauma or injury due to dilatation and curretage.
    • Endometriosis.
  • Endocrine Disorders
    • Alteration of hypothalamic function resulting from stress, drugs, or weight issues.
    • Pituitary tumour leading to production of excessive prolactin hormone (hyperprolactinaemia) hence causing Anovulation.
    • Changes in thyroid function (hyper or hypothyroidism) or in adrenal function.
    • Age ‚Äì menopause. Fertility declines with age.
  • Ovarian Causes
    • Resistant ovary syndrome (Absence of FSH receptors).
    • Polycystic ovarian syndrome (PCOS).
    • Premature menopause.
    • Surgery i.e. removal of the whole ovary mistakenly.
    • Infection such as mumps can destroy the ovary substance.
    • Chronic or excessive exposure to radioactive substances or xray.
  • Defective Transport
    • Allergy to the man‚Äôs sperms/cervical hostility.
    • Vaginal pH (acidic destroying the motility of the sperm).

üë® Causes of Infertility in Male

Male fertility depends on production of adequate numbers of healthy spermatozoa by the semineferous tubules and their subsequent delivery.

  • Defective Spermatogenesis
    • Poor sperm quality due to varicocele associated with high temperatures (cryptoorchism, febrile illness, tight underclothing).
    • Congenital lack of sperms.
    • Testicular abnormality due to infection.
    • Exposure to environmental toxins (pesticides, herbicides) or radioactive substances (xrays).
    • Previous vasectomy for sterilisation.
  • Defective Transport
    • Blockage of the vas deferens due to infection (gonorrhea, tuberculosis) and trauma.
    • Infection of the seminal glands by gonorrhoea and trichomonas vaginalis.
  • Ineffective Delivery
    • Premature ejaculation or Lack of ejaculation.
    • Retrograde ejaculation.
    • Impotence.
    • Hypospadiasis and Epispadiasis.
    • Hydrocele.
üß¨

Genetic Factors

  • Klinefelter's Syndrome: Consisting of gynaecomastia, testicular atrophy, azoospermia and infertility. Patients have 47 chromosomes (XXY instead of XY).
  • Turner's Syndrome: Caused by absence of one X chromosome (45XO). They are females but have ovarian failure, widely spaced nipples, and infertility.

üî¨ Investigations

General Investigations in Female

  • Menstrual history and previous gynecological history.
  • Previous obstetric history.
  • General state of health and nutrition (weight).
  • Check for excessive prolactin and hair distribution (virilism).
  • Hormonal Investigations (Progesterone on day 21, FSH, LH).

Special Tests

‚ñ∂ Basal Body Temperature & Cervical Mucus

Basal Body Temperature: A rise in the basal body temperature of about $0.5^{\circ}_{\mathrm{C}}$ in the last 14 days of the cycle indicates that ovulation has occurred.

Cervical Mucus: Ovulatory mucus is clear copious and can be drawn out into a fine thread (spinnbarkeit). On drying it crystallizes out into the characteristic fern pattern.

‚ñ∂ Tubal Patency Tests

Laparoscopy: A premenstrual laparoscopic exam combined with injection of methylene blue. Distal block is recognized if there's no spill.

Hysterosalpingogram: An opaque radio aqueous solution is passed through the cervix to the uterus and tubes under radiographic control. Free spill of dye from the distal ends of the tubes proves patency.

Tubal Insufflation: An unreliable method where CO2 is used passed via the vagina to the uterus.

üíä Treatment

Chemotherapy (Female)

Clomiphene Citrate (Clomid): 50mgs daily for 5 days of onset of menstruation preferably on the second day. It induces ovulation by stimulating the Hypothalamic pituitary system.

‚ö†Ô∏è

Clomiphene Side Effects

Visual disturbance, Abdominal discomfort, Headache, Insomnia, Ovarian hyperstimulation, Hair loss, Breast tenderness, Depression, Hot flushes, Abnormal uterine bleeding, Multiple pregnancy.

Bromocriptine: Used if patient has hyperprolactanaemic infertility. Inhibits synthesis of release of prolactin.

Tamoxifen: 20mgs daily on days 2, 3, 4 and 5 of the menstrual cycles.

Surgical Measures

Tubal Blockage: Salpingolysis (dividing adhesions), Salpingostomy (new opening), Tubal Anastomosis and Repair.

Male Surgery: Hernia repair, Varicocele ligation.

Assisted Reproductive Technology

In Vitro Fertilization (IVF): An ovum is obtained from the woman, mixed with sperms in a test tube, and the embryo is implanted in the uterus. Developed in 1978 (Robert Edwards).

Additional Techniques:

  • Ovarian hyper stimulation by GnRH agonist and FSH.
  • Ultra sound guided transvaginal oocyte retrieval.
  • ICSI (Intra cytoplasmic sperm injection).

Other Options: Surrogate Parents, Adoption, Artificial Insemination by a Sperm Donor (AID).
