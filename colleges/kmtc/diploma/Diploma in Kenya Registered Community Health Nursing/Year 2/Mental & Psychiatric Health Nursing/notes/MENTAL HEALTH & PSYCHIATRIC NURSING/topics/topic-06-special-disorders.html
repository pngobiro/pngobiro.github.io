Definition

An enduring pattern of inner experience and behavior that deviates markedly from the expectations of the individual‚Äôs culture (cognition, affect, interpersonal functioning, impulse control). It is pervasive, inflexible, stable over time, and leads to distress or impairment.

üìã Specific Disorders & Management

Cluster A: Odd or Eccentric

‚ñ∂ Paranoid Personality Disorder

Characteristics: Long-standing suspiciousness, mistrust, hostile, irritable. Interprets actions of others as demeaning or malevolent.

Management:

  • Be open, honest, and strictly professional (do not be overly friendly).
  • Acknowledge mistakes immediately.
  • Do not confront delusions directly but present reality.
  • Psychotherapy is treatment of choice.
‚ñ∂ Schizoid Personality Disorder

Characteristics: Lifelong social withdrawal, discomfort with interaction, bland/constricted affect, solitary interests.

Management:

  • Understand need for isolation; minimize intrusions.
  • Do not insist on reciprocal emotional responses.
‚ñ∂ Schizotypal Personality Disorder

Characteristics: Strikingly odd/strange behavior, magical thinking, ideas of reference, illusions, derealization.

Management: Respect need for privacy, do not ridicule oddities, correct misperceptions gently.

Cluster B: Dramatic, Emotional, Erratic

‚ñ∂ Antisocial Personality Disorder

Characteristics: Inability to conform to social norms, lack of remorse/conscience, lying, truancy, illegal activities. Onset before age 15.

Management:

  • Set firm, consistent limits.
  • Avoid manipulation; maintain skepticism.
  • Group therapy (self-help groups) often more effective than individual.
‚ñ∂ Borderline Personality Disorder

Characteristics: Instability in mood, relationships, and self-image. Crisis state is common. Impulsivity, splitting (all good/all bad).

Management:

  • Open, continuous communication to prevent splitting of staff.
  • Set fair and consistent limits on acting out.
  • Maintain calm reaction to crises.
‚ñ∂ Histrionic Personality Disorder

Characteristics: Excitable, emotional, dramatic, attention-seeking, seductive behavior. Temper tantrums if not center of attention.

‚ñ∂ Narcissistic Personality Disorder

Characteristics: Heightened self-importance, grandiosity, lack of empathy, sense of entitlement, exploits others.

Management: Help handle criticism, set limits on demanding behavior, reinforce positive traits.

Cluster C: Anxious or Fearful

  • Avoidant: Extreme sensitivity to rejection, social withdrawal despite desire for companionship, timidity.
    Mgmt: Patience, reassurance, do not criticize.
  • Dependent: Pervasive submissive behavior, inability to make decisions without reassurance, tolerates abuse to maintain attachment.
    Mgmt: Respect feelings, encourage independence gradually.
  • Obsessive-Compulsive (OCPD): Perfectionism, inflexibility, preoccupation with rules/lists/order, emotional constriction.
    Mgmt: Rational explanations, allow patient control where possible, avoid power struggles.

Therapeutic Intervention

Masters and Johnson Approach:

  • Joint therapy (couple treated together).
  • Sensate Focus: Exploration of bodies to give/receive pleasure without demand for intercourse ("ban on intercourse" initially).
  • "Give to Get": Partners encouraged to give pleasure to receive it.
  • Communication: Verbal and non-verbal communication about sexual needs.

Other Treatments:

  • Biological: Sildenafil (Viagra), Cialis, hormones.
  • Mechanical: Vacuum pumps, implants, dilators (for vaginismus).
  • Techniques: "Squeeze technique" for premature ejaculation.

üö´ Paraphilias

Recurrent and intense sexual urges or sexually arousing fantasies involving non-human objects, children, or nonconsenting adults.

  • Frotteurism: Urges to touch/rub against non-consenting persons.
  • Voyeurism: Observing unsuspecting people naked/disrobing ("Peeping Tom").
  • Exhibitionism: Exposing genitals to unsuspecting strangers.
  • Fetishism: Use of non-living objects (e.g., shoes, underwear) for arousal.
  • Pedophilia: Sexual activity with prepubescent children.
  • Sexual Masochism: Arousal from being humiliated, beaten, bound, or made to suffer.
  • Sexual Sadism: Arousal from inflicting pain/suffering on others.
