Antianemic Drugs

‚û¢Anemia: Refers to many clinical conditions in which there is a deficiency in the number of RBCs or in the hemoglobin level within those cells.

  • ‚Ä¢Iron deficiency anemia (hypochromic microcytic)
  • ‚Ä¢Vitamin C (citrus fluids) enhances absorption of iron.
  • ‚Ä¢Megaloblastic anemia (vitamin B12 and folic acid deficiency).

Iron Preparations:

‚û¢A complex of iron and other substances are normally taken orally.

‚û¢Sometimes administered parenterally when:

  • Some disorders limiting the amount of drug absorbed by GIT.
  • Patient is unable to tolerate oral iron.

‚û¢Iron deficiency is common in infants (low iron in diet), and during pregnancy (increased requirements).

‚û¢Action: Iron (diet or drug) absorbed from GIT and transport to bone marrow after combining with protein transferrin, to incorporate with hemoglobin.

‚û¢Uses: prophylaxis and treatment of iron deficiency anemia.

‚û¢Contraindications: Hemosiderosis, peptic ulcer, enteritis, ulcerative colitis and liver cirrhosis.

‚û¢Drug interactions:

  • Antacids: decrease absorption of iron.
  • Tetracycline: iron diminishes affect of tetracycline (decrease its absorption).
üö´Side Effects & Toxicity

‚û¢Side effects:

  • Constipation, gastric irritation, and abdominal cramps, change color of stool to black.
  • Toxic reaction: (parenteral): causes nausea, vomiting, peripheral vascular collapse. Occurs within 60 seconds of toxic dose. Symptoms may disappear then reappear after 6-24 hours.

‚û¢Treatment of iron toxicity:

  • Symptomatic treatment.
  • Induce vomiting, then give egg and milk.
  • Gastric lavage may be done with IV NaHco3 solution to counteract acidosis.
  • Give antidote (chelliating agent); IV infusion of deferoxamine (desferal).
üë©‚Äç‚öïÔ∏èNursing considerations:
  • Take a complete history, including use of antacids.
  • Ask about any evidence of GI bleeding.
  • Advise client to take iron with meals to reduce gastric irritation.
  • Take iron with citrus juices to enhance absorption.
  • Advise client not to take iron with milk, tea, or antacids (they decrease iron absorption).
  • Encourage client to eat a well balanced diet.
  • Keep drug out of reach of children (extremely toxic).
  • When administering iron to child, dilute it with water or fruit juice & use a straw to minimize teeth staining.
  • Discuss with client the possibility of indigestion, changes in stool color (black) and constipation.

Folic acid (Generic Name: Folvite)

Action
Stimulates production of red and white blood cells and platelets in some megaloblastic anemias.

Indications and dosages

  • Recommended dietary allowance
  • Megaloblastic anemia related to folic acid deficiency in nutritional deficiency, pregnancy, childhood, or infancy.
  • Folate deficiency

Dose: 150 to 400 mcg, up to 1 mg/day

Contraindications and precautions

  • Contraindicated in pernicious, aplastic, or normocytic anemia
  • Use cautiously in breastfeeding patients.

Adverse reactions
Altered sleep pattern, malaise, poor concentration, impaired judgment, hyperactivity, anorexia, nausea, flatulence, bitter taste, allergic reaction (including rash, pruritus, erythema), Bronchospasm.

Nursing Considerations:

  1. Teach woman about importance of taking daily dose 3 months before pregnancy and in first trimester as it decreases fetal neural tube defects by 50%.
  2. It is not effective in normocytic anemias

1) Warfarin sodium: coumadin

‚û¢Class: anticoagulant.

‚û¢Action: prevent the formation of factors II, VII, IX and X in the liver.

‚û¢Uses:

  • Prophylaxis and treatment of deep venous thrombosis.
  • Thromboembolison - Thrombophlebitis.
  • Prophylaxis from myocardial infarction.

‚û¢Contraindications:

  • Hemorrhagic tendencies
  • Blood disorders.
  • Ulcerative lesion of GIT.
  • Impaired renal and hepatic function.
  • Severe hypertension.
  • Thrombocytopnia.

‚û¢Drug interaction:

  • Antacids: decrease effect of anticoagulants (by decreasing its absorption)
  • Salicylate: effect of anticoagulants.

‚û¢Side effects: hemorrhagic accidents.

‚û¢Antidote: vitamin K.

üë©‚Äç‚öïÔ∏èNursing considerations:
  • Daily monitoring of prothrombin time is recommended.
  • Instruct clients to take the drug before meal.
  • Remind clients to wear identification band that states that they are on anticoagulant therapy.
  • Advise client to avoid activities that may cause injury.
  • Vitamin K should be available
  • Food rich in vitamin K should be avoided.

2) Heparin:

‚û¢Class: anticoagulant

‚û¢Naturally occurring substance isolated from porcine intestinal mucosa or bovine lung tissue.

‚û¢Must be given parentally.

‚û¢Doesn‚Äôt interfere with wound healing.

‚û¢Action: Potentiate the inhibitory action of antithrombin III on various coagulation factors. Inactivate thrombin and prevent the conversion of fibrinogen to fibrin.

‚û¢Uses:

  • To prevent extension of clots.
  • To prevent thrombi and emboli from recurring.
  • Prophylactic from thromboembolic diseases.
  • After some types of surgery (cardiac, orthopedic & vascular).
  • Prevent clotting during hemodialysis.
  • Treatment of DIC (disseminated intravasculor coagulation)
  • Coronary occlusion after MI.

‚û¢Contraindications:

  • Blood disorders with bleeding tendencies (hemophilia).
  • Suspected intracranial hemorrhage.
  • Open wounds.
  • During surgery of the eyes, brain and spinal cord.
  • Menstruation.
  • Abortion.
  • Any drugs or conditions affecting blood coagulation.

‚û¢Side effects: Hemorrhage.

‚û¢Overdose: nose bleeding, hematouria, petechiae, tarry stool.

‚û¢Antidote: protamin sulfate.

‚û¢Dose: IV or S.C measured in units according to bleeding & clotting time.

üë©‚Äç‚öïÔ∏èNursing considerations:
  • Should not be administered IM, administer by deep sc to minimize local irritation and to prolong the action of drug.
  • Don‚Äôt massage before and after injection.
  • Change site of administration to avoid ecchymosis.
  • Instruct and stress the importance of reporting any signs of active bleeding.
  • Use electric razor for shaving, soft bristle tooth brush to decrease gum irritation.
  • Patient should be hospitalized for IV therapy.
  • Clotting time, PTT should be done before the start of therapy each dose of drug then daily.

3) Enoxaparin sodium

Trade name: Clexane , Lovenox 3

Class: Anticoagulant, low-molecularweight heparin.

‚û¢Pregnancy risk category B

Action: Inhibits thrombus and clot formation by blocking factor Xa and factor IIa. This inhibition accelerates formation of antithrombin III-thrombin complex (a coagulation inhibitor), thereby deactivating thrombin and preventing conversion of fibrinogen to fibrin.

Indications and dosages

  • Patients at risk for thromboembolic complications (pulmonary embolism and deep-vein thrombosis) due to severely restricted mobility during acute illness or post a major surgery.
  • Prevent blood clots.
  • In some cases of pregnancy (sometime, a blood clot is formed in the umbilical cord due to antiphispholid syndrome, which leads to miscourage).

‚û¢Adults: 40 mg subcutaneously daily

Contraindications

  • Hypersensitivity to clexane or heparin.
  • Thrombocytopenia
  • Active major bleeding
  • Major blood disorders
  • Certain types of stroke
  • Do not give Clexane to a child.

Adverse reactions

  • CNS: dizziness, headache, insomnia, confusion, cerebrovascular accident
  • CV: edema, chest pain, atrial fibrillation, heart failure
  • GI: nausea, vomiting, constipation
  • GU: urinary retention
  • Hematologic: anemia, bleeding tendency, thrombocytopenia, hemorrhage
  • Skin: bruising, pruritus, rash, urticaria, irritation, or erythema at injection site
üë©‚Äç‚öïÔ∏èNursing considerations:
  • I.M. injection may be painful. Monitor sites for evidence of sterile abscess.
  • Rotate injection sites to prevent muscle atrophy.
  • Monitor patient for pain and swelling, warmth, or redness in calves; sudden, severe headaches; visual disturbances; numbness in extremities; signs of depression; signs of liver dysfunction (abdominal pain, dark urine, jaundice).
  • Advise patient to take medication with food if GI upset occurs.
  • Alert: Tell patient to report unusual symptoms immediately and to stop drug and notify doctor about visual disturbances or migraine.
  • Teach woman how to perform routine breast self-examination.
  • Advice patient to immediately report to doctor any breast abnormalities, vaginal bleeding, swelling, yellowish skin or eyes, dark urine, shortness of breath, chest pain, or pregnancy.
  • Advise patient that injection must be given every 3 months to maintain adequate contraceptive effects.

Thrombolytic Agents

‚ñ™ Agents used to promote the dissolution(lysis ) of the insoluble fibrin trapped in intravascular emboli and thrombi.

  • The most serious complication is hemorrhage.
  • Heparin therapy usually follows treatment with these agents.

1- Streptokinase:

Class: Thrombolytic agent.

Action: Acts with plasminogen to produce an activator complex which enhance the conversion of plasminogen to plasmin which breaks down fibrinogen, fibrin clot & other plasma proteins.

Uses: Deep venous thrombosis (DVT), Myocardial infarction (MI), To clear occluded arteriovenous or IV canula.

Contraindication: Hemorrhage.

Side effects: Bleeding, nausea, and headache.

Antifibrinolytic agents

Tranexamic acid

Trade Name: Hexacaprone

Classification: Antifibrinolytic

Uses: Prevention of heavy bleeding such as heavy periods, nose bleeds. Prevention of bleeding during tooth extraction in haemophiliacs (people who lack a clotting factor)

Contraindications: pregnancy, breast feeding, history of thromboembolic disease, kidney problems, bleeding disorders, and menstrual bleeding.

Side-effects: Nausea, vomiting, Diarrhea

üë©‚Äç‚öïÔ∏èNursing considerations:
  1. To overcome GI problems, instruct patient to eat little and often, and eat simple foods such as dry toast.
  2. Teach patient to drink plenty of fluids.
  3. Watch for thrombus or embolus formation
  4. Teach patient to report sudden slurred speech, loss of co-ordination, pain in the chest or groin or leg you should contact a doctor as soon as possible. If any of these occur, stop taking the medication and see your doctor.
