‚ö° Immediate Care

Last offices refers to the care given to the body after death. It involves respectful preparation for transfer to the mortuary.

Initial Steps

  • Valuables: Remove all things (money, bangles, rings, necklaces, earrings, artificial dentures). Put them in a container, label it, and keep in a safe place to be given to a close family member.
  • Medical Equipment: Remove pillows, I.V. fluids, and blood transfusion lines.
  • Positioning: Straighten the legs and put them together. Close the eyes and the mouth.
üíç

Wedding Ring Protocol

The wedding ring is typically not removed. Strap it securely with tape and record this action in the nursing notes (kardex).

Timing

After completing care, wait approximately 1 hour before preparing to take the body to the mortuary.

üß∞ Equipment Required

Hygiene & Care

  • Soap and bowl with warm water
  • Towel
  • Brush and comb
  • Cotton wool
  • Dissecting/dressing forceps
  • Bandages

Wrapping & Transport

  • Mortuary sheet
  • Identification bands
  • Container for soiled dressings
  • Bin for dirty linen

Documentation (Mortuary Card)

The mortuary card must contain:

Name of Patient IP Number Ward Date of Death Time of Death
03

üìù Procedure

  1. 1 Clean: Clean the body, rinse, and dry. Cut and clean the nails. Arrange hair smartly and leave it to lie flat.
  2. 2 Dressings: If any dressing is clean, leave it. If dirty, change it.
  3. 3 Orifices: Close all orifices (ears, nose, vagina) with cotton wool to stop any discharge that may ooze out. If the mouth isn't closing, use strapping.
  4. 4 Positioning: Put lower limbs together. Put arms at the sides. Tie the body appropriately (often ankles and thumbs/arms).
  5. 5 Identification: Double identification of the body is required (e.g., ankle or waist). Wrap the body in the mortuary sheet, then attach the mortuary card to the outside of the sheet using cellotape.
  6. 6 Environment: Leave the body covered with the sheet. Screen the bed or move the body to the sluice room. Clear the area and clean all used equipment thoroughly.
04

üìã Post-Procedure

  • Ring the mortuary attendant to collect the body.
  • The doctor should write the Death Certificate.
üö®

Sudden Death / Medico-Legal Cases

If the death is sudden or unexpected:

  • A Summary Request Form must accompany the body.
  • Relatives must sign.
  • A Post-mortem is typically required.
