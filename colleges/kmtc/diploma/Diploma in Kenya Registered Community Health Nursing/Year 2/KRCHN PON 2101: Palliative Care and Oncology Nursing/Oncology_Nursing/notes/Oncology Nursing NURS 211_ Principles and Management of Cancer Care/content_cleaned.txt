<div data-testid="stHeadingWithActionElements" class="st-emotion-cache-1p9ibxm e1icttdg2"><h1 level="1" id="answer-c">Answer: C<span data-testid="stHeaderActionElements" class="st-emotion-cache-gi0tri e1icttdg3"><a href="#answer-c" class="st-emotion-cache-yinll1 e1icttdg1"><svg xmlns="http://www.w3.org/2000/svg" width="16" height="16" viewBox="0 0 24 24" fill="none" stroke="currentColor" stroke-width="2" stroke-linecap="round" stroke-linejoin="round"><path d="M15 7h3a5 5 0 0 1 5 5 5 5 0 0 1-5 5h-3m-6 0H6a5 5 0 0 1-5-5 5 5 0 0 1 5-5h3"></path><line x1="8" y1="12" x2="16" y2="12"></line></svg></a></span></h1></div>
<p>A is incorrect. Placing cool washcloths on the client's head is not the priority, there is a better answer. This would only need to be done if the client was at risk for seizures due to an incredibly high body temperature. The temperature of 38.7C does not warrant cooling measures, and the nurse has another immediate priority given the clients immunosuppression and their suspicion of an infection.</p>
<p>B is incorrect. It is not appropriate for the nurse to simply continue with their assessment. they suspects an infection in their client who is immunocompromised. Another answer has an immediate priority that the nurse must do.</p>
<p>C is correct. It is the priority action to establish intravenous access on this client. This client has a diagnosis of ALL, so the nurse knows that he is immunocompromised. He is very susceptible to infections, and with a fever of 38.7C they has a high index of suspicion for an infection. Broad spectrum IV antibiotics will need to be started right away, therefore it is the priority for the nurse to start an IV.</p>
<p>D is incorrect. Assessing the client's perfusion has nothing to do with the nurses suspicion of an infection. they should immediately establish IV access for the administration of antibiotics.</p>
