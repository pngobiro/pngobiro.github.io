üóÇÔ∏è Cancer Classification

Cancers are generally classified into two broad categories:

  • Solid Tumors: Masses of tissue (e.g., sarcomas, carcinomas).
  • Hematological Cancers: Cancers of blood-forming tissues (e.g., leukemia, lymphoma, myeloma).
02

üî¨ Cancer Grading

Grading evaluates the amount of differentiation (how much the tumor cells resemble normal cells) and estimates the rate of growth.

  • Grade X: Grade cannot be determined.
  • Grade I: Cells differ slightly from normal cells and are well differentiated (Mild Dysplasia).
  • Grade II: Cells are abnormal and are moderately differentiated (Moderate Dysplasia).
  • Grade III: Cells are very abnormal and are poorly differentiated (Severe Dysplasia).
  • Grade IV: Cells are immature (Anaplasia) and undifferentiated; cell of origin is difficult to determine.

üìê Cancer Staging (TNM System)

The TNM system is the most widely used method to stage cancer.

TNM Key Components

  • T: The extent of the Primary Tumor.
  • N: The absence or presence of Regional Lymph Node metastasis.
  • M: The absence or presence of Distant Metastasis.

Primary Tumor (T)

  • TX: Primary tumor cannot be assessed.
  • T0: No evidence of primary tumor.
  • Tis: Carcinoma in situ.
  • T1, T2, T3, T4: Increasing size and/or local extent of the primary tumor.

Regional Lymph Nodes (N)

  • NX: Regional lymph nodes cannot be assessed.
  • N0: No regional lymph node metastasis.
  • N1, N2, N3: Increasing involvement of regional lymph nodes.

Distant Metastasis (M)

  • MX: Distant metastasis cannot be assessed.
  • M0: No distant metastasis.
  • M1: Distant metastasis present.

üß™ Tumor Markers

Tumor markers are substances produced by cancer cells or by other cells of the body in response to cancer or certain benign (noncancerous) conditions.

Oncofetal Antigens

  • Carcinoembryonic Antigen (CEA)
  • Alpha-feto Protein (AFP)

Hormones

  • ADH (Antidiuretic Hormone)
  • Calcitonin
  • Catecholamines
  • HCG (Human Chorionic Gonadotropin)
  • PTH (Parathyroid Hormone)

Isoenzymes

  • Neurospecific enolase (NSE)
  • Prostatic acid phosphatase (PAP)

Tissue-Specific Antigens

  • Prostate-specific Antigen (PSA)
