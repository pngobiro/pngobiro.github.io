Radiation therapy can be classified into two main types based on the source location:

  • Internal Radiation Therapy (Brachytherapy)
  • External Radiation Therapy (Teletherapy)

üíâ Brachytherapy (Internal)

Sources & Administration

  • Implanted into the affected tissue or body cavity
  • Ingested as a solution
  • Injected as a solution into the bloodstream or body cavity
  • Introduced through a catheter into the tumor

Client Education

  • Avoid close contact with others until the treatment is completed
  • Maintain daily activities unless contraindicated
  • Rest
  • Maintain a balanced diet and fluid intake
  • If implant is temporary, the client should be on bed rest
  • Excreted body fluids may be radioactive; double flush toilets after use

Nursing Management

Safety Precautions

  • Minimize time spent in close proximity to the radiation sources
  • Minimum distance should be 6 feet
  • Use lead shields
  • Place the client in a private room
  • Ensure proper handling and disposal of body fluids
  • Pregnant women and children are not allowed inside the client's room

üì° Teletherapy (External)

Side Effects

  • Tissue damage to target area
  • Ulcerations of oral mucous membranes
  • Nausea, vomiting, and diarrhea
  • Radiation pneumonia
  • Fatigue
  • Alopecia
  • Immunosuppression

Client Education: Skin Care

üß¥

Protecting the Skin

  • Wash marked area of the skin with plain water only and pat dry. Do not wash off the treatment site marks.
  • Avoid rubbing, scratching, or scrubbing the treatment site.
  • Do not apply extreme temperatures to the treatment site.
  • If shaving is necessary, use an electric razor.
  • Wear soft, loose-fitting clothing over the treatment area.
  • Protect skin from sun exposure during the treatment and for at least 1 year after the treatment is completed.

Additional education includes maintaining proper rest, diet, and fluid intake. Be aware that hair loss (alopecia) may occur.

04

üî™ Surgical Therapy

Surgery can be used for various purposes in cancer management:

  • Primary treatment: Removal of the tumor.
  • Prophylactic: Removing tissue at risk of becoming cancerous.
  • Palliative: Relieving symptoms without curing the cancer.
  • Reconstructive: Restoring function or appearance after cancer surgery.
