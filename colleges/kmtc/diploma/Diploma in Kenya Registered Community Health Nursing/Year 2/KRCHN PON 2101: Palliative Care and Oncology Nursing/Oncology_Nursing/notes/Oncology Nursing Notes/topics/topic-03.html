üß™ Diagnostic Tests of Cancer

1. Biopsy / Cytology

Histologic and cytologic examination of specimens are performed by the pathologist on tissues collected by needle aspiration of solid tumors, exfoliation from epithelial surface, and aspiration of fluid from blood or body cavities.

Tissues may be obtained by excisional biopsy, incisional biopsy, and needle biopsy. By examination of these tissues, the name, grade, and stage of the tumor can be identified.

üè∑Ô∏è Tumor Markers

Tumor markers are protein substances found in the blood or body fluids. They are released either by the tumor itself, or by the body as a defense in response to the tumor (called host response).

Markers derived from the tumor itself:

  • Oncofetal antigens: present normally in fetal tissue, may indicate an anaplastic process in tumor cells.
    Examples: carcinoembryonic antigen (CEA) and alpha-fetoprotein (AFP).
  • Hormones: present in large quantities in the human body; however, high levels may indicate a hormone-secreting malignancy.
    Examples: antidiuretic hormone (ADH), calcitonin, catecholamines, human chorionic gonadotropin (HCG), and parathyroid hormone (PTH).
  • Isoenzymes: normally present in a particular tissue; may be released into bloodstream if the tissue is experiencing rapid, excessive growth.
    Examples: neuron-specific enolase (NSE) and prostatic acid phosphatase (PAP).
  • Tissue-specific proteins: identify the type of tissue affected by malignancy.
    Example: prostatic-specific antigen (PSA) utilized to identify prostate cancer.

üìë Classification of Neoplasms

Classification According to Tissue of Origin

  1. 1
    Carcinoma: refers to a tumor that arises from epithelial tissue; the name of the cancer identifies location (e.g., basal cell carcinoma).
  2. 2
    Sarcoma: refers to a tumor arising from supportive tissues; the name of the cancer identifies the specific tissue affected (e.g., osteosarcoma).
  3. 3
    Adenocarcinoma: from glandular tissue (e.g., lungs, ovaries).

TNM System Breakdown

T - Tumor Size
  • T0: no evidence of tumor
  • Tis: tumor in situ
  • T1, T2, T3, T4: indicate progressive degrees of tumor size and involvement
N - Lymph Node Involvement
  • N0: no abnormal lymph nodes detected
  • N1a, N2a: regional nodes involved with increasing degree from N1a to N2a, no metastases detected
  • N1b, N2b, N3b: regional lymph nodes involvement with increasing Degree from N1b to N3b, metastasis suspected
  • Nx: inability to assess regional nodes
M - Distant Metastasis
  • M0: no evidence of distant metastasis
  • M1, M2, M3: indicate ascending degrees of distant metastasis and includes distant lymph nodes
