  • Directly:-

PTH acts directly on bone, where it induces calcium resorption, and on the kidney, where it enhances calcium reabsorption and synthesis of 1,25-dihydroxyvitamin D [1,25(OH)‚ÇÇD], a hormone that increases gastrointestinal calcium absorption. Serum PTH levels are tightly regulated by a negative feedback loop.

‚¨ÜÔ∏è Hyperparathyroidism

  • Increased PTH secretion leading to hypercalcemia and hypophosphatemia.
  • Incidence 27/100,000
  • Prevalence women >60 years more than 1%
  • characterised by high blood calcium levels (hypercalcaemia) and is usually caused by a benign parathyroid tumour which secretes high levels of parathormone (PTH).
  • This results in release of calcium from bones, raising blood calcium levels. The effects may include:
    • polyuria and polydipsia
    • formation of renal calculi
    • anorexia and constipation
    • muscle weakness

Aetiology

  1. Solitary adenoma
  2. Associated with hereditary syndromes
    • MEN 1 (hyperparathyroidism, pituitary tumors, pancreatic tumors)
    • MEN 2 ‚Äì hyperparathyroidism, pheochromocytoma, medullary carcinoma of thyroid.

Clinical features

  • >1/2 asymptomatic
  • Symptomatic:-
    • ‚òë Peripheral nerve/muscle- proximal mm weakness, easy fatigability,
    • ‚òë GIT ‚Äì vague abdominal complains
    • ‚òë kidney-Recurrent nephrolithiasis, Urinary obstruction,
    • ‚òë bones-Osteitis fibrosa cystica:
    • ‚òë Cardiac- arrhythmias & arrest.

Diagnosis

  1. Phosphate usually low and calcium( high).
  2. Assess albumin level- R/o false hypercalcemia
  3. Immunoassays :Elevated immunoreactive PTH level
  4. Dual-energy x-ray absorptiometry(DEXA) of the spine- assess bone density

Treatment

  • Hydration
  • Hydration with forced diuresis with loop diuretics
  • bisphosphonates - powerful inhibitors of bone resorption
  • Glucocorticoids have utility - increase urinary calcium excretion and decrease intestinal calcium absorption
  • Surgical excision of the adenoma

‚¨áÔ∏è Hypoparathyroidism

  • Parathyroid hormone (PTH) deficiency causes hypocalcaemia, i.e. abnormally low blood calcium levels, and is much rarer than hyperparathyroidism.
  • There is reduced absorption of calcium from the small intestine and less reabsorption from bones and glomerular filtrate.
  • Low blood calcium causes:
    • tetany
    • anxiety
    • paraesthesia
    • grand mal seizures
    • in some cases, cataracts and brittle nails.
  • Causes of hypoparathyroidism include damage to or removal of the glands during thyroidectomy, ionizing radiation, development of autoantibodies to PTH and parathyroid cells, and congenital abnormalities.
