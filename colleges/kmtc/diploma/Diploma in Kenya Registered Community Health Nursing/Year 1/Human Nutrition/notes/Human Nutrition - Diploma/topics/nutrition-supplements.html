VITAMIN AND MINERAL SUPPLEMENTS

Terms

Supplements

A supplement is a product that provides nutrients in synthetic or isolated form. The vitamin or mineral is extracted from a food source. The body does not recognize easily, synthetic or isolated nutrients.

Complement

A complement is a product that provides nutrients as they exist in nature from their original food source. There is a mix of vitamins and minerals in their natural form.

Purpose/Function of supplements

  1. 1.Correct overt deficiencies. Adults rarely suffer nutrient deficiencies such as scurvy, pellagra, beri-beri but they still occur.
  2. 2.Improve nutrition status - For habitual dieters, elderly, vegetarians etc.
  3. 3.Reduce disease risks i.e. menopausal women who are intolerant to lactose may not receive enough calcium. A lack of oestrogen prevents absorption and utilization of calcium.
  4. 4.Support increased nutrient needs - Women who lose a lot of blood during menstruation may need an Iron supplement. Pregnant women may need folate supplements to reduce the risk of neural tube defects.
  5. 5.Improve body's defenses. Health care workers may provide supplementation to people being treated for addictions like alcohol or other drugs and people with prolonged illnesses, injuries, surgery, malabsorption etc.

FOOD SUPPLEMENTS

These are different type's feeds which are added to other foods given to patients to complement or make addition (dietary) so that the patients may get good nourishment. Some of the feeds are nutritionally complete while others may be having fewer nutrients or even miss some nutrients. Following are some of the major supplemental feeds used.

  1. 1.Forceval- A protein powder for oral and naso-gastric feeding, vitamins and minerals are added.
  2. 2.Pediasure- A high calorie and protein feed for malnourished children.
  3. 3.Ensure- A high calorie, high protein feed used for malnourished adults.
  4. 4.Fresubin original- Fibre free, gluten free, clinically free from lactose for those at risk of malnutrition
  5. 5.Fresubin Isofibre- Contains fibre, gluten free and lactose free for nasogastric tube feeding
  6. 6.Naurish- For renal patients, 496 and 249,92 kcals per 25 gms ,essential amino acids fortified, low protein and electrolytes, cholesterol free, gluten free.
  7. 7.Fresubin protein- Energy drink- A high protein, energy drink for patients with poor appetite.
  8. 8.Diaben- For nasogastric and oral feeding for diabetic patients.
  9. 9.Cow and gate I- For malnourished babies below 6 months.
  10. 10.Cow and gate II: fig- malnourished babies, over 6months.
  11. 11.Lactose free milk- Lactose free, given to patients who have lactose Intolerance
  12. 12.Infant formula normal- Given to babies who do not breast feed and have normal weight
  13. 13.Pre-term infant formula- Given to preterm babies who does not breast feed
  14. 14.SNA. Infant formula- Used to replace breast milk when mothers do not breast feed, e.g. children born of HIVIAIDS infected mothers as feeding option.
  15. 15.Nutren- Used for diabetics, enteral (tube), or oral feeding of Patients with hyperglycemia.
  16. 16.Peptamen- Used for different conditions .e.g. HIV/AIDS, mal-absorption syndrome, pancreatitis insufficiency etc.
  17. 17.Isomil powder- For babies who are lactose intolerance.
  18. 18.F75 and F100- Used for malnourished children.
  19. 19.Plumpy nuts- for malnourished children. can be given to children while still on F100.

Vulnerable groups

  1. 1.People with nutrient deficiencies
  2. 2.People with low food intakes < 1200 kilocalories per day.
  3. 3.People who eat all-plant diets (vegans)
  4. 4.Women who bleed excessively during menstruation.
  5. 5.People whose calcium intake is too low, they are given calcium to forestall extensive bone loss.
  6. 6.People in certain stages of the life cycle who have increased nutrient needs (women of child bearing age, pregnant women, LBW infants).
  7. 7.People who have diseases, infections, injuries, malabsorption, metabolic disorders.
  8. 8.People taking medicines that interfere with the body's use of specific nutrients.
