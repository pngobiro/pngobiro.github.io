First Aid Notes

Duration: 10 hours Level: Beginner

Course Overview

This course provides an introduction to first aid principles and practices for health records and information technology students at Kenya Medical Training College. It covers essential skills for handling emergency situations and providing immediate care.

  • Know how to arrange for the appropriate kind of help.

Course Modules

  1. Introduction to First Aid

    This module introduces the basics of first aid, its principles, aims, and the qualities of a first aider.

    90 minutes

  2. Assessing a Casualty

    This module explains how to assess a casualty in an emergency situation and introduces the primary survey.

    120 minutes

  3. Secondary Survey and Medical History

    This module covers the secondary survey, and collecting the history of the casualty.

    100 minutes

  4. Physical Examination

    This module covers a head to toe physical examination.

    100 minutes

  5. Difficult First Aid Situations

    This module covers how to handle difficult first aid situations such as traffic, fire, electrical and water incidents, and breathing problems.

    120 minutes

  6. Specific First Aid Procedures Part 1

    This module covers first aid for choking, croup, drowning, hyperventilation, heart conditions and head and eye injuries.

    120 minutes

  7. Specific First Aid Procedures Part 2

    This module covers first aid for bleeding, cuts, grazes, nose bleeds and shock.

    100 minutes

  8. Specific First Aid Procedures Part 3

    This module covers first aid for alcohol, drug, and food poisoning, and carbon monoxide poisoning and swallowed poisons.

    120 minutes

  9. Specific First Aid Procedures Part 4

    This module covers first aid for animal bites, blisters, bumps, bruises, burns, frostbite, fainting and fever.

    120 minutes

  10. Specific First Aid Procedures Part 5

    This module covers first aid for meningitis, hypothermia, bone and muscle injuries and includes revision questions.

    100 minutes

Prerequisites

  • No prior knowledge of first aid is required.
