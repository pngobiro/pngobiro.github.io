Essentials, Tools, & Review

ACLS ESSENTIALS

  • Prompt recognition and intervention with high-quality CPR is critical in any arrest situation.
  • Mentally prepare for resuscitation as you approach the scene and the individual.
  • Scene safety is critical; do not get injured yourself.
  • BLS focus is early CPR and early defibrillation.
  • Do not attempt to place an oropharyngeal airway in an awake individual.
  • Pull the jaw up into the mask; do not push the mask onto the face as it may close the airway.
  • IV or IO is the preferred routes for drug delivery; ET route is discouraged and unpredictable.
  • The dose of amiodarone is different for VF and VT with a pulse.
  • Resume chest compressions immediately after delivering a shock.
  • Targeted temperature management is utilized after return of spontaneous circulation.
  • Learn specific cardiac rhythms: sinus tachycardia, SVT, atrial fibrillation/flutter, VF, VT, torsades de pointes, and asystole.
  • Confirm asystole in two separate leads.
  • VF and pulseless VT are treated the same: deliver a shock.
  • Remember the reversible causes of cardiac arrest: the H's and the T's.
  • Capnography is a valuable tool in resuscitation. If PETCO2 is lower than 10 mmHg, attempt to improve CPR quality and investigate the advanced airway placement.
  • If capnography is still less than 10 mmHg, consider termination of efforts.
  • Use nitroglycerin with caution in individuals with inferior myocardial infarction; avoid if systolic blood pressure (SBP) is less than 90 mmHg, or if taking erectile dysfunction medications (phosphodiesterase inhibitors) within 24 hours.
  • Confusion may be a presenting sign of a stroke.

ACLS REVIEW QUESTIONS

  1. The following are included in the ACLS Survey:
    1. Airway, Breathing, Circulation, Differential Diagnosis
    2. Airway, Breathing, Circulation, Defibrillation
    3. Assessment, Breathing, Circulation, Defibrillation
    4. Airway, Breathing, CPR, Differential Diagnosis
  2. The primary focus of treating cardiac arrest is:
    1. Effective CPR
    2. Early defibrillation
    3. Drug administration
    4. Both A and B
  3. Which of the following is not an example of an advanced airway?
    1. Oropharyngeal airway
    2. Esophageal-tracheal tube
    3. Laryngeal mask airway
    4. Combitube
  4. The following are possible effects of hyperventilation:
    1. Increased intrathoracic pressure
    2. Decreased venous return to the heart
    3. Both A and B
    4. None of the above
  5. The normal sinus rhythm of the heart starts in the:
    1. Left ventricle
    2. Atrioventricular node
    3. Sinoatrial node
    4. Right ventricle
  6. What is high-quality CPR?
    1. 80 compressions per minute at a depth of at least one-inch (2.5 cm)
    2. 100 to 120 compressions per minute at a depth of 2 to 2.4 inches (5 to 6 cm)
    3. 80 compressions per minute at a depth of at least two inches (5 cm)
    4. 100 compressions per minute at a depth of at least one inch (2.5 cm)
  7. Before placement of an advanced airway, the compression to ventilation ratio during adult CPR is:
    1. 30:1
    2. 30:2
    3. 15:1
    4. 20:2
  8. You should _____ in an individual with ventricular fibrillation immediately following a shock.
    1. Resume CPR
    2. Check heart rate
    3. Analyze rhythm
    4. Give amiodarone
  9. _____ joules (J) are delivered per shock when using a monophasic defibrillator.
    1. 380
    2. 320
    3. 340
    4. 360
  10. The following medication(s) can be used to treat hypotension during the post-cardiac arrest phase:
    1. Dopamine
    2. Milrinone
    3. Amiodarone
    4. Both A and B
  11. The following antiarrhythmic drug(s) can be used for persistent ventricular fibrillation or pulseless ventricular tachycardia, except:
    1. Amiodarone
    2. Lidocaine
    3. Atropine
    4. Epinephrine
  12. Which of the following is not a potential cause of PEA?
    1. Toxins
    2. Hyperkalemia
    3. Hyperventilation
    4. Trauma
  13. Which of the following is a shockable rhythm?
    1. Ventricular fibrillation
    2. Ventricular tachycardia (pulseless)
    3. Torsades de Pointes
    4. All of the above
  14. The following drug(s) may be used in an ACS individual for cardiac reperfusion:
    1. Fibrinolytic therapy
    2. Epinephrine
    3. Atropine
    4. Both A and C
  15. All of the following are bradycardic rhythms except:
    1. Atrial fibrillation
    2. First-degree heart block
    3. Mobitz Type I
    4. Third-degree heart block
  16. _____ access is preferred in arrest due to easy access and no interruption in CPR.
    1. Central
    2. Peripheral
    3. Intraosseous
    4. Endotracheal
  17. Which of the following is first-line treatment for ACS?
    1. Morphine
    2. Aspirin
    3. Nitroglycerin
    4. All of the above
  18. The following are classic signs of an acute stroke except:
    1. Facial droop
    2. Arm drift
    3. Abnormal speech
    4. Seizure
  19. Which of the following is not found within the 8 D's of stroke care?
    1. Detection
    2. Dispatch
    3. Delivery
    4. Defibrillate

1. A: Airway, Breathing, Circulation, Differential Diagnosis

2. D: Both A and B

3. A: Oropharyngeal airway

4. C: Both A and B

5. C: Sinoatrial node

6. B: 100 to 120 compressions per minute at a depth of 2 to 2.4 inches (5 to 6 cm)

7. B: 30:2

8. A: Resume CPR

9. D: 360

10. A: Dopamine

11. C: Atropine

12. C: Hyperventilation

13. D: All of the above

14. A: Fibrinolytic therapy

15. A: Atrial fibrillation
