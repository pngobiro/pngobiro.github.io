üåä Introduction & Types of Water

Water is a substance composed of the chemical elements hydrogen and oxygen and existing in gaseous, liquid, and solid states. It is one of the most plentiful and essential of compounds.

Different Types of Water

‚ñ∂ Common Water Classifications
  • 1
    Tap Water: Supplied through pipelines from dams/rivers. Suitable for household purposes but may require purification for drinking.
  • 2
    Spring Water: Comes from natural underground springs. Generally safe as it is not recycled.
  • 3
    Mineral Water: From underground sources, naturally rich in calcium, magnesium, and manganese.
  • 4
    Well Water: Main source in rural areas. Taps directly into the water table via drilled wells.
  • 5
    Distilled Water: Subjected to treatment to remove minerals and salts. One of the purest forms, but lacks essential minerals.
  • 6
    Sparkling Water: Undergone carbonation (frizzy).
  • 7
    Hard Water: High mineral content (calcium/magnesium). Causes skin issues and plumbing corrosion.
  • 8
    Alkaline Water: Higher pH level than normal tap water. Neutralizes excessive acid in the body.

Sources of Water

Primary Sources

  • Surface water: Collects on the ground or in a stream, river, lake, reservoir, or ocean.
  • Ground water: Located below the surface of the earth in spaces between rock and soil (aquifers).

Causes of Water Contamination

Source Details
Industrial Waste Contains pollutants like mercury, lead, asbestos, sulphur, and nitrates. Causes eutrophication and temperature changes.
Wastewater & Sewage Carries harmful bacteria, chemicals, and pathogens. Breeding ground for disease carriers.
Marine Dumping Household garbage (plastics, glass, rubber) taking up to 200 years to decompose.
Oil Spills Forms layers preventing oxygen absorption; harmful to marine life and humans.
Pesticides & Fertilizers Runoff from farms introduces chemicals into water bodies.

Prevention Strategies

  • ‚úì
    Sewage Treatment: Effective sewer treatment processes before discharge.
  • ‚úì
    Protect Water Bodies: Prevent littering and direct waste discharge.
  • ‚úì
    Reduce Coolant Discharge: Reuse water used as coolant in industries to prevent thermal pollution.
  • ‚úì
    Public Awareness: Education on dangers of pollution.

‚öôÔ∏è Water Purification

Public drinking water systems use a series of water treatment steps to ensure safety.

  1. 1
    Coagulation: Chemicals with a positive charge (aluminum/iron salts) are added to neutralize negative dirt particles, binding them together.
  2. 2
    Flocculation: Gentle mixing to form larger, heavier particles called "flocs".
  3. 3
    Sedimentation: Flocs settle to the bottom because they are heavier than water.
  4. 4
    Filtration: Clear water passes through filters (sand, gravel, charcoal) to remove dissolved particles, parasites, bacteria, and viruses. Reverse osmosis or ultrafiltration may also be used.
  5. 5
    Disinfection: Addition of chemical disinfectants (chlorine, chloramine) or use of UV light/ozone to kill remaining germs. pH adjustment and fluoridation often follow.

ü¶† Water-Associated Diseases

‚ñ∂ Classification of Diseases
Category Description Examples
Water-borne Transmitted by ingesting contaminated water. Cholera, Typhoid, Dysentery, Hepatitis A
Water-washed "Water-scarce" diseases caused by lack of water for hygiene. Scabies, Trachoma, Ringworm, Conjunctivitis
Water-related Spread by insects/vectors that breed in water. Malaria, Yellow Fever, Filariasis

Common Waterborne Diseases

‚ñ∂ Cholera

Cause: Vibrio cholerae bacterium.

Symptoms: Profuse diarrhea ("rice water" stool), vomiting, rapid dehydration, muscle cramps. Can be fatal within hours.

Prevention: Safe water, cooked food, hand washing.

‚ñ∂ Typhoid Fever

Cause: Salmonella Typhi.

Symptoms: Gradual fever (up to 104¬∞F), muscle aches, fatigue, sweating, rash ("rose spots").

Prevention: Vaccination, safe water/food.

‚ñ∂ Dysentery

Definition: Severe diarrhea with blood or mucus.

Symptoms: Stomach cramps, fever, nausea, vomiting, dehydration.

Prevention: High hygiene standards, avoid street food/ice.

‚ñ∂ Hepatitis A

Type: Viral liver infection.

Symptoms: Jaundice (yellow skin/eyes), clay-colored stool, fatigue, nausea, abdominal pain.

Prevention: Vaccine, thorough cooking of food.

‚ñ∂ Chemical Poisoning Diseases
  • Fluorosis: Teeth/bone damage from high fluoride.
  • Lead Poisoning: Anemia, reproductive issues, kidney/nervous system damage.
  • Arsenicosis: Bladder, kidney, lung, skin problems; cancer risk.
  • Cancer: Linked to MTBE and chlorinated solvents.
