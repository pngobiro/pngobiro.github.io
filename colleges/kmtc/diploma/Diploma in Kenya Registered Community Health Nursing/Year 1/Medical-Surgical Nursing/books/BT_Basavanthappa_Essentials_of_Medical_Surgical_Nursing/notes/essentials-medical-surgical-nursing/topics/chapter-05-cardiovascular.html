Chapter Overview

Comprehensive coverage of cardiovascular assessment and management of heart diseases, including coronary artery disease, heart failure, hypertension, and arrhythmias.

Cardiovascular Assessment

Health History

  • Chest pain characteristics (PQRST)
  • Dyspnea, orthopnea, PND
  • Palpitations, syncope
  • Risk factors: smoking, diabetes, hypertension, family history

Physical Examination

  • Vital signs (BP both arms, pulse rate/rhythm)
  • Inspection: JVD, edema, cyanosis
  • Palpation: PMI, peripheral pulses, edema
  • Auscultation: Heart sounds (S1, S2, murmurs, S3, S4)

Diagnostic Tests

  • ECG (12-lead, Holter monitor)
  • Cardiac enzymes (troponin, CK-MB)
  • Echocardiography
  • Stress testing
  • Cardiac catheterization
  • Lipid profile

Coronary Artery Disease & Myocardial Infarction

Coronary Artery Disease (CAD)

  • Pathophysiology: Atherosclerosis causing narrowing of coronary arteries
  • Risk Factors:
    • Non-modifiable: Age, gender, family history
    • Modifiable: Smoking, hypertension, diabetes, obesity, sedentary lifestyle, stress
  • Angina Pectoris:
    • Stable angina: Predictable, relieved by rest/nitroglycerin
    • Unstable angina: Unpredictable, increasing frequency/severity
    • Prinzmetal's angina: Coronary artery spasm

Myocardial Infarction (MI)

  • Definition: Death of myocardial tissue due to prolonged ischemia
  • Types: STEMI (ST-elevation MI), NSTEMI (Non-ST-elevation MI)
  • Signs & Symptoms:
    • Severe crushing chest pain (>20 minutes)
    • Radiation to left arm, jaw, back
    • Diaphoresis, nausea, vomiting
    • Dyspnea, anxiety
    • Women/elderly: Atypical symptoms (fatigue, indigestion)
  • Diagnosis:
    • ECG changes (ST elevation/depression, Q waves)
    • Elevated cardiac enzymes (troponin peaks 12-24 hrs)
  • Immediate Management (MONA):
    • Morphine (pain relief)
    • Oxygen (if SpO2 <90%)
    • Nitroglycerin (vasodilation)
    • Aspirin (antiplatelet)
  • Reperfusion Therapy:
    • Thrombolytics (within 12 hours)
    • PCI (Percutaneous Coronary Intervention) - preferred
    • CABG (Coronary Artery Bypass Graft)
  • Nursing Care:
    • Continuous cardiac monitoring
    • Bed rest initially, gradual mobilization
    • Monitor for complications (arrhythmias, heart failure, cardiogenic shock)
    • Administer medications: anticoagulants, beta-blockers, ACE inhibitors, statins
    • Cardiac rehabilitation education

Heart Failure

  • Definition: Inability of heart to pump adequate blood to meet body's needs
  • Types:
    • Left-sided failure: Pulmonary congestion
    • Right-sided failure: Systemic congestion
    • Systolic dysfunction: Reduced ejection fraction
    • Diastolic dysfunction: Impaired filling
  • Causes: CAD, hypertension, cardiomyopathy, valvular disease
  • Signs & Symptoms:
    • Left-sided: Dyspnea, orthopnea, PND, cough, crackles, S3 gallop
    • Right-sided: JVD, hepatomegaly, ascites, peripheral edema
  • Management:
    • Medications: Diuretics, ACE inhibitors, beta-blockers, digoxin
    • Lifestyle: Low-sodium diet, fluid restriction, daily weights
    • Oxygen therapy
    • Monitor I&O, daily weights
    • Position in high Fowler's

Hypertension

  • Definition: BP ≥140/90 mmHg on multiple readings
  • Classification:
    • Normal: <120/80
    • Elevated: 120-129/<80
    • Stage 1: 130-139/80-89
    • Stage 2: ≥140/90
    • Hypertensive crisis: >180/120
  • Types:
    • Primary (essential): Unknown cause (90-95%)
    • Secondary: Identifiable cause (renal disease, endocrine disorders)
  • Complications: Stroke, MI, heart failure, kidney disease, retinopathy
  • Management:
    • Lifestyle: DASH diet, weight loss, exercise, limit alcohol/sodium
    • Medications: Diuretics, ACE inhibitors, ARBs, calcium channel blockers, beta-blockers
    • Regular BP monitoring
    • Medication compliance

Arrhythmias

  • Atrial Fibrillation: Irregular, rapid atrial rate; risk of stroke
  • Ventricular Tachycardia: Life-threatening; requires immediate treatment
  • Ventricular Fibrillation: Cardiac arrest; requires defibrillation
  • Heart Blocks: Impaired conduction; may need pacemaker

Key Points

  • CAD is leading cause of death; prevention through risk factor modification is crucial
  • MI requires immediate intervention - time is muscle
  • Heart failure management includes medications, diet, and lifestyle modifications
  • Hypertension is "silent killer" - regular screening important
  • Cardiac rehabilitation improves outcomes post-MI
