Chapter Overview

Comprehensive study of cancer pathophysiology, treatment modalities, and nursing management of oncology patients.

Cancer Basics

Pathophysiology

  • Definition: Uncontrolled growth and spread of abnormal cells
  • Characteristics:
    • Uncontrolled proliferation
    • Loss of differentiation
    • Invasion of local tissues
    • Metastasis to distant sites
  • Benign vs Malignant:
    • Benign: Localized, encapsulated, slow-growing
    • Malignant: Invasive, metastatic, rapid growth

Risk Factors

  • Modifiable: Tobacco, alcohol, diet, obesity, sun exposure, infections (HPV, HBV)
  • Non-modifiable: Age, genetics, family history

Warning Signs (CAUTION)

  • Change in bowel or bladder habits
  • A sore that does not heal
  • Unusual bleeding or discharge
  • Thickening or lump
  • Indigestion or difficulty swallowing
  • Obvious change in wart or mole
  • Nagging cough or hoarseness

Diagnosis and Staging

  • Diagnostic Tests: Biopsy (definitive), imaging (CT, MRI, PET), tumor markers
  • TNM Staging:
    • T: Tumor size
    • N: Lymph node involvement
    • M: Metastasis
  • Grading: Degree of differentiation (Grade 1-4)

Cancer Treatment

Surgery

  • Types: Curative, palliative, reconstructive
  • Nursing Care: Pre/postoperative care, wound management, pain control

Radiation Therapy

  • Types: External beam, brachytherapy (internal)
  • Side Effects:
    • Skin reactions (erythema, desquamation)
    • Fatigue
    • Site-specific effects (mucositis, diarrhea)
  • Nursing Care:
    • Skin care (gentle cleansing, avoid irritants)
    • No lotions/creams on treatment area
    • Protect from sun exposure
    • Adequate rest and nutrition

Chemotherapy

  • Mechanism: Kills rapidly dividing cells
  • Administration: IV, oral, intrathecal, intraperitoneal
  • Side Effects:
    • Bone marrow suppression (neutropenia, anemia, thrombocytopenia)
    • Nausea and vomiting
    • Alopecia
    • Mucositis
    • Diarrhea or constipation
    • Peripheral neuropathy
  • Nursing Care:
    • Safe handling (PPE, spill kits)
    • Monitor for extravasation
    • Antiemetics before chemotherapy
    • Infection prevention (neutropenic precautions)
    • Monitor CBC
    • Hydration

Immunotherapy

  • Mechanism: Enhances immune system to fight cancer
  • Types: Checkpoint inhibitors, CAR T-cell therapy, monoclonal antibodies
  • Side Effects: Immune-related adverse events (colitis, pneumonitis, hepatitis)

Targeted Therapy

  • Mechanism: Targets specific molecular pathways
  • Examples: Tyrosine kinase inhibitors, EGFR inhibitors
  • Advantages: More selective, fewer side effects than traditional chemotherapy

Hormone Therapy

  • Used for: Hormone-sensitive cancers (breast, prostate)
  • Examples: Tamoxifen, aromatase inhibitors, androgen deprivation

Oncology Nursing Care

Bone Marrow Suppression

  • Neutropenia (ANC <1500):
    • Infection risk - implement neutropenic precautions
    • Avoid crowds, fresh flowers, raw foods
    • Hand hygiene
    • Monitor for fever (>38°C) - medical emergency
  • Thrombocytopenia (Platelets <50,000):
    • Bleeding precautions
    • Avoid IM injections, rectal procedures
    • Soft toothbrush, electric razor
    • Monitor for bleeding
  • Anemia:
    • Fatigue management
    • Erythropoietin or transfusions if severe

Nausea and Vomiting

  • Antiemetics (ondansetron, metoclopramide, dexamethasone)
  • Small, frequent meals
  • Avoid strong odors
  • Ginger, acupressure

Mucositis

  • Oral care every 2-4 hours
  • Soft, bland diet
  • Avoid alcohol-based mouthwashes
  • Topical anesthetics (magic mouthwash)

Alopecia

  • Prepare patient (usually temporary)
  • Scalp cooling (may reduce hair loss)
  • Wigs, scarves, hats
  • Gentle hair care

Pain Management

  • WHO analgesic ladder
  • Around-the-clock dosing
  • Adjuvant medications
  • Non-pharmacological methods

Nutritional Support

  • High-protein, high-calorie diet
  • Small, frequent meals
  • Nutritional supplements
  • Enteral or parenteral nutrition if needed

Psychosocial Support

  • Emotional support for patient and family
  • Support groups
  • Counseling services
  • Address body image concerns
  • End-of-life care discussions

Palliative and End-of-Life Care

  • Palliative Care: Symptom management and quality of life at any stage
  • Hospice Care: End-of-life care when curative treatment stopped
  • Focus: Comfort, dignity, family support
  • Symptom Management: Pain, dyspnea, nausea, anxiety

Key Points

  • Early detection improves outcomes - know warning signs
  • Chemotherapy affects rapidly dividing cells (cancer and normal)
  • Neutropenic fever is medical emergency
  • Safe handling of chemotherapy agents is essential
  • Multimodal treatment often most effective
  • Supportive care crucial for quality of life
  • Palliative care appropriate at any stage
