Chapter Overview

Comprehensive study of musculoskeletal assessment, fractures, arthritis, osteoporosis, and orthopedic nursing care.

Fractures

Types of Fractures

  • Complete: Bone completely broken
  • Incomplete: Partial break (greenstick)
  • Closed: Skin intact
  • Open (Compound): Bone protrudes through skin
  • Comminuted: Multiple fragments
  • Spiral: Twisting injury
  • Compression: Bone crushed (vertebrae)

Signs & Symptoms

  • Pain, swelling
  • Deformity, abnormal movement
  • Crepitus
  • Loss of function
  • Ecchymosis

Complications

  • Fat Embolism: Petechiae, dyspnea, confusion (long bone fractures)
  • Compartment Syndrome: 6 P's - Pain, Pressure, Pallor, Paresthesia, Pulselessness, Paralysis
  • DVT/PE: Immobility risk
  • Infection: Open fractures
  • Delayed Union/Non-union

Management

  • Reduction: Closed (manipulation) or Open (surgery)
  • Immobilization: Cast, splint, traction, external fixation
  • Cast Care:
    • Assess neurovascular status (5 P's)
    • Keep cast dry and clean
    • Elevate to reduce swelling
    • Do not insert objects inside cast
    • Report: Increased pain, numbness, foul odor
  • Traction Care:
    • Maintain proper alignment
    • Keep weights hanging freely
    • Assess pin sites for infection
    • Prevent complications of immobility

Hip Fracture

  • Common in elderly (osteoporosis, falls)
  • Signs: Shortened, externally rotated leg; severe pain
  • Treatment: Surgical repair (ORIF, hip replacement)
  • Nursing Care:
    • Prevent dislocation (avoid hip flexion >90°, adduction, internal rotation)
    • Use abduction pillow
    • Early mobilization
    • DVT prophylaxis

Arthritis

Osteoarthritis (OA)

  • Definition: Degenerative joint disease
  • Risk Factors: Age, obesity, joint injury, genetics
  • Pathophysiology: Cartilage breakdown
  • Signs & Symptoms:
    • Joint pain (worse with activity)
    • Stiffness (morning <30 min)
    • Crepitus
    • Limited ROM
    • Heberden's and Bouchard's nodes
  • Management:
    • Weight loss
    • Exercise (low-impact)
    • NSAIDs, acetaminophen
    • Heat/cold therapy
    • Joint replacement (severe cases)

Rheumatoid Arthritis (RA)

  • Definition: Autoimmune inflammatory disease
  • Pathophysiology: Synovial inflammation, joint destruction
  • Signs & Symptoms:
    • Bilateral, symmetrical joint involvement
    • Morning stiffness (>1 hour)
    • Warm, swollen joints
    • Fatigue, low-grade fever
    • Rheumatoid nodules
    • Elevated ESR, RF, anti-CCP
  • Management:
    • DMARDs (methotrexate)
    • Biologics (TNF inhibitors)
    • NSAIDs, corticosteroids
    • Rest during flares
    • Joint protection techniques
    • PT/OT

Gout

  • Definition: Uric acid crystal deposition in joints
  • Risk Factors: Diet high in purines, alcohol, obesity, diuretics
  • Signs: Sudden severe pain, redness, swelling (often big toe)
  • Management:
    • Acute: NSAIDs, colchicine, corticosteroids
    • Chronic: Allopurinol, febuxostat
    • Low-purine diet
    • Increase fluid intake

Osteoporosis

  • Definition: Decreased bone density and strength
  • Risk Factors:
    • Postmenopausal women
    • Age, family history
    • Low calcium/vitamin D intake
    • Sedentary lifestyle
    • Smoking, alcohol
    • Corticosteroid use
  • Complications: Fractures (hip, spine, wrist)
  • Diagnosis: DEXA scan (bone density)
  • Prevention & Management:
    • Adequate calcium (1200 mg/day) and vitamin D
    • Weight-bearing exercise
    • Fall prevention
    • Medications: Bisphosphonates, denosumab, teriparatide
    • Avoid smoking and excessive alcohol

Other Conditions

Osteomyelitis

  • Definition: Bone infection
  • Causes: Bacteria (S. aureus), trauma, surgery
  • Signs: Bone pain, fever, swelling, drainage
  • Management: Long-term IV antibiotics, surgery

Amputation

  • Causes: Trauma, PVD, diabetes, cancer
  • Nursing Care:
    • Stump care (keep clean, dry)
    • Prevent contractures (positioning)
    • Phantom limb pain management
    • Prosthesis training
    • Psychological support

Key Points

  • Compartment syndrome is emergency - requires fasciotomy
  • Hip fracture precautions: No flexion >90°, adduction, internal rotation
  • OA: Pain with activity; RA: Morning stiffness >1 hour
  • Osteoporosis prevention: Calcium, vitamin D, weight-bearing exercise
  • Cast care: Assess neurovascular status regularly
