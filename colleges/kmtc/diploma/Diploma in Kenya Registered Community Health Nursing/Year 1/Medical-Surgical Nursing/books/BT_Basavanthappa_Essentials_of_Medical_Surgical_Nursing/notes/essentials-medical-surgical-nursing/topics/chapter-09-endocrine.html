Chapter Overview

Comprehensive study of endocrine system disorders including diabetes mellitus, thyroid disorders, and adrenal gland dysfunction.

Diabetes Mellitus

Types

  • Type 1: Autoimmune destruction of beta cells; absolute insulin deficiency
  • Type 2: Insulin resistance and relative insulin deficiency (90-95% of cases)
  • Gestational: Develops during pregnancy

Diagnosis

  • Fasting blood glucose ≥126 mg/dL
  • Random blood glucose ≥200 mg/dL with symptoms
  • HbA1c ≥6.5%
  • Oral glucose tolerance test ≥200 mg/dL

Signs & Symptoms

  • Classic 3 P's: Polyuria, Polydipsia, Polyphagia
  • Weight loss (Type 1)
  • Fatigue, blurred vision
  • Slow wound healing
  • Recurrent infections

Management

  • Type 1:
    • Insulin therapy (basal-bolus regimen)
    • Blood glucose monitoring
    • Carbohydrate counting
  • Type 2:
    • Lifestyle: Diet, exercise, weight loss
    • Oral medications: Metformin (first-line), sulfonylureas, DPP-4 inhibitors
    • Injectable: GLP-1 agonists, insulin (if needed)
  • Monitoring:
    • Self-monitoring blood glucose (SMBG)
    • HbA1c every 3 months (target <7%)
    • Annual eye, foot, kidney exams

Acute Complications

  • Hypoglycemia (<70 mg/dL):
    • Signs: Shakiness, sweating, confusion, tachycardia
    • Treatment: 15-15 rule (15g carbs, recheck in 15 min)
    • Severe: Glucagon injection or IV dextrose
  • Diabetic Ketoacidosis (DKA) - Type 1:
    • Signs: Hyperglycemia, ketones, acidosis, Kussmaul respirations, fruity breath
    • Treatment: IV fluids, insulin, electrolyte replacement
  • Hyperosmolar Hyperglycemic State (HHS) - Type 2:
    • Severe hyperglycemia (>600 mg/dL), dehydration, altered mental status
    • Treatment: Aggressive fluid replacement, insulin

Chronic Complications

  • Microvascular: Retinopathy, nephropathy, neuropathy
  • Macrovascular: CAD, stroke, PVD
  • Diabetic Foot: Ulcers, infections, amputation risk

Patient Education

  • Medication administration and timing
  • Blood glucose monitoring
  • Meal planning and carb counting
  • Exercise guidelines
  • Foot care (daily inspection, proper footwear)
  • Sick day management
  • Recognition and treatment of hypo/hyperglycemia

Thyroid Disorders

Hyperthyroidism (Graves' Disease)

  • Pathophysiology: Excessive thyroid hormone production
  • Signs & Symptoms:
    • Weight loss despite increased appetite
    • Heat intolerance, diaphoresis
    • Tachycardia, palpitations
    • Nervousness, tremor
    • Exophthalmos (bulging eyes)
    • Goiter
  • Lab Values: Low TSH, high T3/T4
  • Management:
    • Antithyroid drugs (methimazole, PTU)
    • Radioactive iodine therapy
    • Beta-blockers for symptoms
    • Thyroidectomy (if indicated)
  • Thyroid Storm: Life-threatening emergency
    • Extreme hyperthyroidism: High fever, tachycardia, agitation
    • Treatment: Antithyroid drugs, beta-blockers, cooling measures

Hypothyroidism

  • Pathophysiology: Insufficient thyroid hormone
  • Causes: Hashimoto's thyroiditis, iodine deficiency, post-thyroidectomy
  • Signs & Symptoms:
    • Weight gain
    • Cold intolerance
    • Fatigue, lethargy
    • Bradycardia
    • Constipation
    • Dry skin, hair loss
    • Depression
  • Lab Values: High TSH, low T3/T4
  • Management:
    • Levothyroxine (synthetic T4)
    • Take on empty stomach
    • Lifelong therapy
    • Monitor TSH levels
  • Myxedema Coma: Severe hypothyroidism emergency
    • Hypothermia, hypotension, altered mental status
    • Treatment: IV levothyroxine, supportive care

Adrenal Disorders

Cushing's Syndrome

  • Definition: Excess cortisol
  • Causes: Prolonged corticosteroid use, pituitary tumor, adrenal tumor
  • Signs & Symptoms:
    • Moon face, buffalo hump
    • Central obesity, thin extremities
    • Purple striae
    • Hyperglycemia, hypertension
    • Osteoporosis
    • Easy bruising
  • Management:
    • Treat underlying cause
    • Gradual steroid withdrawal
    • Surgery if tumor

Addison's Disease

  • Definition: Adrenal insufficiency (low cortisol, aldosterone)
  • Signs & Symptoms:
    • Hyperpigmentation
    • Weakness, fatigue
    • Weight loss
    • Hypotension
    • Hyponatremia, hyperkalemia
    • Hypoglycemia
  • Management:
    • Hormone replacement (hydrocortisone, fludrocortisone)
    • Increase dose during stress/illness
    • Medical alert bracelet
  • Addisonian Crisis: Life-threatening emergency
    • Severe hypotension, shock, confusion
    • Treatment: IV hydrocortisone, fluids, electrolytes

Key Points

  • Diabetes: Monitor blood glucose, HbA1c, prevent complications
  • Hypoglycemia: 15-15 rule for treatment
  • DKA vs HHS: DKA has ketones and acidosis; HHS has extreme hyperglycemia
  • Hyperthyroidism: Increased metabolism; Hypothyroidism: Decreased metabolism
  • Levothyroxine: Take on empty stomach, lifelong therapy
  • Cushing's: Excess cortisol; Addison's: Cortisol deficiency
  • Endocrine emergencies require immediate treatment
