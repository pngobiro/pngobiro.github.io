Classification of Diseases

üîç Classification by Etiology (Cause)

a) Infectious Diseases

Caused by pathogenic microorganisms such as bacteria, viruses, fungi, or parasites. Examples include influenza, tuberculosis, and malaria.

  • Bacterial: Diseases caused by bacteria (e.g., Streptococcus pneumoniae causing pneumonia).
  • Viral: Diseases caused by viruses (e.g., HIV causing AIDS).
  • Fungal: Diseases caused by fungi (e.g., Candida causing yeast infections).
  • Parasitic: Diseases caused by parasites (e.g., Plasmodium causing malaria).

b) Non-Infectious Diseases

Not caused by pathogens but by genetic, environmental, or lifestyle factors.

  • Genetic: Diseases resulting from genetic mutations or abnormalities (e.g., cystic fibrosis, Down syndrome).
  • Autoimmune: Conditions where the immune system attacks the body‚Äôs own tissues (e.g., rheumatoid arthritis, lupus).
  • Environmental: Diseases caused by exposure to environmental factors (e.g., lung cancer from smoking, asbestosis from asbestos exposure).
  • Lifestyle-related: Diseases linked to lifestyle choices (e.g., type 2 diabetes due to poor diet and lack of exercise).
02

‚è±Ô∏è Classification by Duration and Severity

  • Acute Diseases: Diseases with a rapid onset and a short duration, typically requiring immediate medical attention. Examples: Acute myocardial infarction (heart attack), acute appendicitis.
  • Chronic Diseases: Diseases that develop slowly and persist over a long period, often for the rest of the person's life. Examples: Hypertension, chronic obstructive pulmonary disease (COPD)
  • Subacute Diseases: Diseases that fall between acute and chronic in terms of onset and duration. Examples: Subacute bacterial endocarditis, subacute thyroiditis
03

ü´Ä Classification by System Affected

  • Cardiovascular Diseases: Diseases affecting the heart and blood vessels. Examples: Coronary artery disease, heart failure, hypertension.
  • Respiratory Diseases: Diseases affecting the lungs and respiratory system. Examples: Asthma, chronic bronchitis, pneumonia.
  • Gastrointestinal Diseases: Diseases affecting the digestive tract. Examples: Gastroesophageal reflux disease (GERD), ulcerative colitis, liver cirrhosis.
  • Renal Diseases: Diseases affecting the kidneys. Examples: Chronic kidney disease, acute kidney injury, nephritis.
  • Endocrine Diseases: Diseases affecting the hormonal glands. Examples: Diabetes mellitus, Addison‚Äôs disease, hyperthyroidism.
  • Neurological Diseases: Diseases affecting the nervous system. Examples: Multiple sclerosis, epilepsy, stroke.
  • Musculoskeletal Diseases: Diseases affecting muscles and bones. Examples: Rheumatoid arthritis, osteoporosis, muscular dystrophy
04

ü§ù Classification by Transmission

  • Communicable Diseases: Diseases that can be transmitted from one person to another. Examples: Tuberculosis, influenza, sexually transmitted infections (STIs).
  • Non-Communicable Diseases: Diseases that are not spread from person to person. Examples: Cancer, cardiovascular diseases, diabetes.
05

üî¨ Classification by Pathophysiology (Disease Mechanism)

a) Inflammatory Diseases

Diseases characterized by inflammation in the affected tissues.

  • Acute Inflammatory Diseases: Short-term inflammation often caused by infection or injury (e.g., appendicitis, acute bronchitis).
  • Chronic Inflammatory Diseases: Long-term inflammation that may cause tissue damage and fibrosis (e.g., Crohn's disease, chronic sinusitis).

b) Degenerative Diseases

Conditions that involve the progressive degeneration of tissues or organs.

  • Neurodegenerative Diseases: Diseases affecting the nervous system (e.g., Alzheimer's disease, Parkinson's disease).
  • Osteoarticular Degeneration: Diseases affecting the bones and joints (e.g., osteoarthritis, osteoporosis).

c) Metabolic Diseases

Disorders resulting from abnormalities in metabolic processes.

  • Endocrine Disorders: Diseases related to hormone imbalances (e.g., hypothyroidism, diabetes mellitus).
  • Inborn Errors of Metabolism: Genetic conditions that result in metabolic dysfunction (e.g., phenylketonuria, galactosemia).

d) Neoplastic Diseases

Diseases characterized by abnormal cell growth leading to tumors.

  • Benign Neoplasms: Non-cancerous tumors that do not spread (e.g., lipomas, benign prostatic hyperplasia).
  • Malignant Neoplasms: Cancerous tumors that invade surrounding tissues and may metastasize (e.g., breast cancer, lung cancer).
