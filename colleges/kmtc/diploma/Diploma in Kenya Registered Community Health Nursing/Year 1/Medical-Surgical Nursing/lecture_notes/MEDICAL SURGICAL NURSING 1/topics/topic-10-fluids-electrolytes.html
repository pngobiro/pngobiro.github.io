üíß Fluid Balance Overview

(60% of body wt is made up of fluids)

Fluid compartments

  • Extra cellular
  • Intravascular
  • interstitial
  • Intracellular (within the cells)
  • Transcellular ‚Äì (cerebrospinal, pericardial, synovial, intraocular, and Pleural spaces)
  • Third space ‚Äì abnormal amount of fluid trapped in peritoneal, plueral or other tissues.

To maintain homeostasis the body regulates the output to match the input by the renal system, adrenal glands, pituitary glands (renin, angiotensin II, aldosterone and ADH)

‚ö†Ô∏è Fluid Imbalances

Fluid volume deficit

Caused by:

  • Trauma- burns, excessive bleeding
  • Diseases such as diarrheal, systemic diseases leading to third spacing. Eg. Ascites, pleural effusion, edema, internal heamorrhage etc
  • Insufficient intake
  • Renal disease
  • Congestive cardiac failure
  • Diabetis mellitus,
  • Diuretic overuse
üìâ

Effects of imbalances

  • Low fluid volume - poor cardiac output - poor tissue perfusion - shock
  • Poor cell metabolism
  • Reduced skin targor, muscle clamps, dizziness, hypotension, peripheral constriction, reduced renal output, sunken eyes, nausea, lethargy, confusion, acute brain failure and ureamia

Management of Deficit

  • Fluid replacement ‚Äì oral or intravenous
  • Transfusion ‚Äì whole or plasma depending with what was lost
  • Over a litre ‚Äì give colloids e.g dextran hemacel etc
  • Treat underlying conditions

Fluid volume excess

Caused by:

  • Administration of Excessive amounts of hypo-osmolar fluids- e.g 0.45% Saline/ 5% Dextrose in water.
  • Excessive intake of fluid
  • Inability to excrete excess water (Renal)
  • Poor sodium intake
  • Use of diuretics
  • Loss of sodium and water & replaced only by water
  • Fluid overload i.e., water and sodium retention.
  • Stressful conditions cause increase in release of ADH and aldosterone which increases water reabsorption from renal tubules.

Clinical manifestations

Cerebral & Behavioral
  • Behavioral changes
  • Headache
  • Inc. ICP & pupillary changes
  • Confusion, Drowsiness
  • Disorientation
  • Convulsions
Vital Signs & Physical
  • Bradycardia
  • Increased systolic B.P.
  • Increased respiration
  • Nausea, Projectile vomiting
  • Increase in weight
  • Peripheral or generalized oedema

Circulatory overload causes:

  • Bounding pulse
  • Distended neck and peripheral vein
  • Cough, dyspnoea, orthopenea
  • Crackles in lungs
  • Increased urine output
  • Ascites

Management of Excess

  1. Medication: Diuretics- commonly used to treat fluid volume excess.
    • LOOP DIURETICS: Furosemide [Lasix].
    • THIAZIDELIKE DIURETICS: Chlorothiazide [Diuril].
    • POTASSIUM SPARING DIURETICS: Spironolactone [Aldactone]
  2. Fluid Management: Fluid intake may be restricted to client having fluid volume excess.
  3. Dietary management: Because sodium retention is a primary cause of fluid volume excess, so sodium restriction diet is often prescribed.

Nursing Diagnosis & Care

Excess fluid volume:

  • Assess vital signs, heart sounds and BP.
  • Assess for the presence of edema.
  • Obtain weight daily at same time of day.
  • Administer diuretics as prescribed.

üß™ Electrolyte Imbalances

Electrolyte functions: Transmission of neural impulses, Muscle contraction (especially myocardium), Body fluid movement & retention, Acid-base balance.

Most important electrolytes

Electrolyte Location Normal Range (mmol/L)
Sodium (Na) Extra cellular $135 - 142$
Potassium (K) Intracellular $3.5 - 5.5$
Calcium (Ca) Extra cellular $2.15 - 2.5$
Magnesium (Mg) Intracellular $0.65 - 1.25$
Chloride (Cl) Extra cellular $97 - 107$
Phosphorus Intracellular $0.87 - 1.45$
Hydrogen carbonate Extra cellular $110 - 124$

Sodium Imbalances

Hyponatremia ($< 135\text{ mmol/l}$)

Causes
  • Diarrhea, Vomiting
  • Excessive sweating
  • Impaired kidneys
  • Addison's disease
  • Overuse of diuretics
  • Excess water retention
Effects
  • Hypotension
  • Abdominal muscle cramps
  • Nausea, Fatigue
  • Confusion, convulsions (severe)

Management: Normal diet with salt, IV N/S (if not due to retention), salt tablets. Close monitoring.

Hypernatremia

Causes: Reduced water intake, Cushings syndrome, Fasting, Vomiting, Burns, Hypertonic solutions.

Effects: Thirst, Nausea/vomiting, Confusion, Lethargy, Hallucination, Edema, Postural hypotension.

Management: Give water or hypotonic fluids carefully (avoid brain edema), treat cause, monitor levels.

Potassium Imbalances

Hyperkalemia ($> 5.5\text{ mmol/l}$)

  • Causes: Hemolysis, Renal failure, Acidosis, Iatrogenic (KCl, ACE inhibitors, K-sparing diuretics).
  • Effects: Muscle weakness, paralysis, ECG changes (narrow T waves, shortened QT), Nausea, Diarrhea.
  • Management: Monitor ECG, restrict dietary K, Calcium gluconate IV (antagonizes K effects).

Hypokalemia ($< 3.5\text{ mmol/l}$)

  • Causes: Diarrhea, Vomiting, Ileostomies, Metabolic alkalosis, Suctioning, Hyperaldosteronism, Diuretics.
  • Signs: Fatigue, Anorexia, Muscle weakness, Leg cramps, Paresthesias, ECG changes (flat T wave).
  • Management: Dietary measures, IV infusion (max $10-20\text{ mmol/hr}$), ECG monitoring.

‚öñÔ∏è Acid-Base Imbalance

Normal serum pH is $7.35 - 7.45$.

  • Acidosis: pH below 7.35 (acidic reserve used up).
  • Alkalosis: pH above 7.45 (acidic reserve used up).

Metabolic Acidosis

Low pH, Low Bicarbonate.

  • Causes: Ketoacidosis, Lactic acidosis, Uremia, Methanol toxicity.
  • Signs: Headache, Confusion, Kussmaul respirations (increased rate/depth), Nausea.
  • Management: Sodium bicarbonate (if pH < 7), treat underlying cause.

Metabolic Alkalosis

High pH, High Bicarbonate.

  • Causes: Vomiting, Suctioning, Diuretics, Antacids, Cushing's.
  • Signs: Tingling fingers, Depressed respiration, Hypertonic muscles, Hypokalemia.
  • Management: Restore fluid volume, NaCl (helps excrete bicarb).

Respiratory Acidosis

pH < 7.35, CO2 retention.

  • Causes: Pulmonary edema, Obstruction, Pneumothorax, Pneumonia, Sedative overdose.
  • Signs: High pulse/BP, Mental cloudiness, Fullness in head, Increased ICP.
  • Management: Improve ventilation, Oxygen, Bronchodilators.

Respiratory Alkalosis

pH > 7.45.

  • Causes: Extreme anxiety, Hypoxemia, Early salicylate intoxication, Mechanical ventilation settings.
  • Signs: Lightheadedness, Numbness/tingling, Tinnitus, Loss of consciousness.
  • Management: Relieve anxiety (reassurance/sedation), Treat cause.

  • ‚úì
