üè• Immunization Service Delivery Strategies

1. Static / Fixed

Non mobile SDP (Service Delivery Point) offering day-day immunization services eg H/facilities.

2. Outreach Services

Taking MCH services and curative services from a health facility to the community within the catchment area and returning back to the health facility the same day.

3. Mobile Clinic

Taking MCH services to a community, lasting for more than a day without returning to the health facility.

Scheduling Outreach Sessions

At least once in a month; the multi-dose vaccines DPT-HepB+Hib, OPV and Tetanus Toxoid require an interval of at least a month between doses.

‚ñ∂ Activities involved in outreach/mobile clinic
  • Determine the need for outreach clinics in terms of access and utilization.
  • Determining the size of target population and the number of children and women that you can immunize in one session.
  • For the best results, consult with community leaders and clients about dates and time, as they will help mobilize the community.
  • Make sure you tell mothers which days to expect you, and the time session will start. Be reliable and punctual.
  • Make sure that you keep vaccines cold (+2¬∞C to +8¬∞C degrees Centigrade).
  • When you arrive, arrange your mobile or outreach clinics similar to that of your static H/facility.

Completing an outreach session

  1. Repacking the vaccine carrier
    • Note the temperature inside the vaccine carrier at the end of the session. If the temperature is >+8¬∞C, Vaccines be discarded unless VVM shows that it is still safe for use.
    • Pack unopened vaccines and open vials for which multi-dose vial policy is applicable.
    • Put empty vials to be discarded in a separate container be carried back to facility for proper disposal.
  2. Leave outreach site tidy
    • Do not leave behind anything that might be a health risk to the community.
    • Collect safety boxes containing sharps and take the safety boxes back to the facility. Do not leave any syringes and needles at the site.
    • The health worker should supervise the burning of other wastes on site.
  3. At the facility: Return vaccines to the refrigerator
    • If the ice packs in your vaccine carrier have melted during your trip back to the health facility and temperature is above +8¬∞C, and VVM has not reached discard point. Return the vaccines in the refrigerator and indicate use first.
    • Put ice ‚Äì packs into the freezer, check and record the temperature.

üöÄ Innovative Approaches

Reach Every District (RED) Approach

WHO & other partners strategy to increase immunization coverage by reaching every child in the district mostly in Africa. It focuses on planning, managing & monitoring H/services to improve immunization coverage.

RED approach operational components

  • Effective planning strategies for better resource mx; human, finance & material to achieve immunization targets.
  • Reaching all target popns eg <1yr, WRA (pregnant) plus ;underimmunized, unimmunized, unreached or hard to reach by outreach/mobile clinics.
  • Provide supportive supervision to identify gaps including OJT/ training needs.
  • Community linkages to increase immunization coverage by social mobilization-comm/dialogue.
  • Monitoring & Evaluation for action by utilization of data generated to identify and solve problems.

New challenges to immunization

Unimmunized

Eligible child for immunization but not received any antigen.

Under immunized

Eligible child for immunization who hasn't completed vaccines (drop out).

Unreached

Has never been immunized due to lack of social mobilization.

Hard to reach

Geographically unable to access immunization services. Compassable roads.

üíâ Injection Safety

Definitions

Safe injection: One that does not harm the recipient, health worker & the community to any risk.

Unsafe injection: One that can result in transmission from one patient to another such infectious Complications as HIV/AIDS, Hepatitis B and C and D.

üö´

Practices that Cause Harm

To Recipient: Re-using a syringe or needle, Loading syringe with multiple antigens, Leaving needle on the vial, Applying pressure to bleeding site with used materials.

To Health Worker: Recapping, Placing used needles on surfaces, Sorting out mixed health care wastes.

To Community: Leaving used syringes and needle in unprotected areas accessible to children and animals.

Steps aimed at promoting injection safety

  • Minimize the risk of needle-stick injuries
  • Pre-filled AD injection devices
  • One syringe one child injection
  • Swabbing site of injection
  • Proper needle/syringe disposal-safety box
  • Avoid recapping of needles

Handling Syringes

You have to hold a syringe to give an injection, but should not touch part that comes into contact with the vaccine or the child: The shaft of the needle, The bevel of the needle, The adaptor of the needle, The adaptor of the syringe, The plunger seal.

Safety Box

  • A leak and puncture proof container that carries 100 syringes and needles when $3/4$ full.
  • Should NOT be full level to avoid spillage, needle stick injuries and to easy the sealing of safety box.
  • Do not open used safety boxes or emptied for Re-use.
  • Do not squeeze or force syringes inside the safety box.

Safe waste disposal methods

  1. Incineration: completely destroys needles and syringes by burning at temperatures above $800^{\circ}\mathrm{C}$.
  2. Burning in a metal drum (container burning) if no incinerator is available.
  3. Burying of wastes eg sharps deep into the soil.
