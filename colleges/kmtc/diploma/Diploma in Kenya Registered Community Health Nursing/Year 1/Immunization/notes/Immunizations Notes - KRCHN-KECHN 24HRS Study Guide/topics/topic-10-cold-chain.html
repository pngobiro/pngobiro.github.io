Learning Objectives

  • Define the cold chain system.
  • List the cold chain equipment used in the country.
  • Demonstrate packing of vaccines in the cold chain equipment.
  • Monitor cold chain temperature.
  • Carry out preventive maintenance activities.

Cold Chain System: Process of maintaining vaccines potency & safety from the manufacturer to the recipient at SDP (child and woman of child bearing age). Vaccines lose their potency when exposed to; high temperature, sunlight or freezing conditions depending on type.

Components of Cold Chain System

  • People/personnel- to manage and distribute vaccines/cold chain equipment
  • Equipments-for storing and transporting vaccines
  • Maintaining of equipments-handling
  • Monitoring of equipments-if operational
  • Logistics-transport system-airroplane, vehicles

Principles of Cold Chain

  • Keep vaccines cold and out of sunlight
  • Effective distribution of vaccines.
  • Maintain functional equipments

‚ñ∂ Refrigerators & Freezers Details

Cold Rooms and Freezer Rooms

Large rooms specially constructed for storage of large quantities of vaccines. They have two cooling units; one running while the other is standby, a 24-hour temperature monitoring system with an alarm, and a backup generator.

Ice-lined Refrigerator (ILR)

Used at Sub-county stores. Example: TCW3000AC.

Gas Electric Refrigerators (Absorption Type)

Example: RCW 42 EG or RCW 50 EG. Uses a heating unit (gas or electricity). Quiet operation. Can be converted into either a refrigerator or a freezer based on need.

üîå Principles of Refrigeration

Types of Refrigeration Systems

Compression Type

Uses a compressor to pump refrigerant. Hums when in operation. Requires electricity.

Example: TCW 1152

Absorption Type

Uses a heating unit (gas or electricity). Refrigerant boils, evaporates, and circulates. Quiet operation.

Example: RCW 42 EG

Transport Equipment

Cold Box

Used for transportation of vaccines or temporary storage during breakdown.

Vaccine Carriers

Used to transport vaccines from SCounty stores to service delivery points (fixed, outreach / mobile-campaigns) and during immunisation sessions. The cold life is approximately 8 hours.

Icepacks

Flat rectangular plastic containers filled with water or gel. Always have at least an extra set of icepacks as a reserve.

üõ†Ô∏è Monitoring & Maintenance

Temperature Monitoring

Thermometers: Indicate safe operating ranges ($+2^{\circ}\text{C}$ to $+8^{\circ}\text{C}$ for fridges & $-15^{\circ}\text{C}$ to $-25^{\circ}\text{C}$ for freezers).

  • Dial Thermometer
  • Alcohol Thermometer
  • Fridge Tag: Data logger showing daily min/max temps over 30 days and current temp.

Preventive Maintenance Activities

‚ñ∂ Daily Activities
  • Check temperature twice (morning and evening) including holidays. Chart on monitoring chart.
  • Check that refrigerator is operating and burner flame is blue (for gas).
  • Ensure enough gas in cylinder. Keep a spare.
  • Ensure vaccines are well arranged. DO NOT keep other items.
‚ñ∂ Weekly & Monthly Activities

Weekly

  • Check ice formation on evaporator. If thicker than 6-10mm, defrost.
  • Check that refrigerator is level.

Monthly

  • Clean condenser and cooling unit with soft brush.
  • Clean inside/outside with damp cloth.
  • Clean door gasket and powder with talcum.
  • Check gas connections for leaks.
  • Solar: Wash panels, check battery acid/terminals.

Troubleshooting

Checking Door Sealing: Place a thin paper (foolscap) strip in between the door and body of refrigerator.

Checking Gas Supply: Use soapy water to check for leaks. If leak in tube, replace it.

Defrosting: Move vaccines to cold box. Turn off fridge. Let ice melt. Wipe dry. Turn on and wait for $+2^{\circ}\text{C}$ before reloading.

Cold Chain Emergencies

Common emergencies that can interrupt services:

  • Equipment breakdown
  • Electric power failure (black out)
  • Shortage of gas
  • Shortage of spare parts

Storage & Transportation Rules

  • Transport vaccines with a cold chain monitor. Maintain temp between $+2^{\circ}\text{C}$ and $+8^{\circ}\text{C}$.
  • Diluents do not need refrigeration during storage/transport (unless for outreach) BUT must be cooled to $+2^{\circ}\text{C}$ to $+8^{\circ}\text{C}$ before reconstitution to prevent thermal shock.
  • Preparation for Transport:
    • Vials with YF, OPV and measles, BCG (LAV) and diluents placed at the bottom.
    • PCV10, Pentavalent, TT & IPV vials placed at the top (to avoid freezing against icepacks).
