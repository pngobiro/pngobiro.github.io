üè∑Ô∏è Vaccine Classification

There are three main types of vaccine used in the Kenya UVIS:

‚ñ∂ 1. Live Attenuated Vaccines

Derived from disease-causing viruses or bacteria that have been weakened under laboratory conditions.

  • They will multiply in a vaccinated individual by causing disease in a mild form.
  • Only one dose of this type of vaccine provides life-long immunity, except OPV, which requires multiple doses.
  • LAVs divided into 2 types:
    • Virus: oral polio vaccine (OPV), measles, yellow fever
    • Bacteria: BCG, oral typhoid (Salmonella typhi) and oral cholera
üìù

Note

BCG, OPV, Measles & Yellow fever are EPI Live Attenuated Vaccines.

‚ñ∂ 2. Inactivated or Killed Vaccines

Produced by growing viruses or bacteria then inactivating them with heat or chemicals.

  • Cannot grow in a vaccinated individual (killed) and therefore cannot cause the disease.
  • Multiple doses are required for full protection (killed non-effective).
  • Booster doses are needed to maintain immunity because protection by these vaccines diminishes over time.

Whole-cell vaccines

Made of an entire bacterial or viral cell taken from their surface capsules.

  • Whole inactivated viral vaccines: e.g. Smallpox, Injectable Polio Vaccine (IPV) (Salk), hepatitis A, Influenza, antirabies.
  • Whole inactivated bacterial vaccines: e.g. whole-cell pertussis, inactivated cholera, anthrax.

Subunit and fractional vaccine

These vaccines are composed of parts (i.e. subunits or fractions) of the pathogen, instead of the whole pathogen.

  • Fractional: Diphtheria and tetanus toxoids, Haemophilus influenzae type b conjugate vaccine (Hib), pneumococcal conjugate vaccine (PCV).
‚ñ∂ 3. Recombinant or Conjugate Vaccines
  • Vaccines are produced by inserting genetic material from a disease-causing organism into a harmless cell, which replicates the proteins of the disease-causing organism.
  • The proteins are then purified and used as vaccine.

Examples: pentavalent; Hepatitis B, HIB, TT, Diphtheria, pertussis.

üî¢ Valency & Preparation Types

Sub types of Recombinant or conjugate vaccines

Monovalent vaccine

Single antigen protecting against one disease or MOs.

Bivalent vaccine

Antigen against two strains of disease causing mos. e.g. Bopv-type 1&3 strains of polio viruses, two strains of HPV.

Trivalent vaccine

Antigen against 3 strains of disease causing otgs e.g. OPV, influenza A, B&C.

Polyvalent

Antigen against two or strains of disease causing organisms e.g. penta, MMR, 10 valent pneumococcal conjugate vaccine (PCV10).

üìã Summary of Vaccines

EPI vaccines currently in use

  • BCG
  • Oral Polio/inactivated polio vaccine (IPV)
  • DPT/HepB-Hib (Pentavalent)
  • PCV10
  • Measles
  • Tetanus Toxoid
  • Rota virus
  • Yellow Fever

Characteristics/qualities of an ideal vaccine

  • Safety - free from any harm
  • Cost effective - affordable to the government & local mwananchi.
  • Thermal stability - able to tolerate heat to maintain potency.
  • Efficacy - ability to induce or confer long term immunity.
  • Easy to administer - eg orally
  • Multivalency - able to prevent many or multiple diseases.
  • Herd immunity - able to confer protection to a large proportion of popn ie 80-90%.
