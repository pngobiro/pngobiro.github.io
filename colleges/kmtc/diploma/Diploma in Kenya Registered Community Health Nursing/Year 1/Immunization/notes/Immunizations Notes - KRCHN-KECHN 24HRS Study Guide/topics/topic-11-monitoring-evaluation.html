üìù Concepts & Definitions

Monitoring

Day to day activities within a program with the aim of identifying problems and developing solutions (inputs). Data is collected, analyzed, reported & used routinely.

Evaluation

Aims to measure the program performance against its objective at specific times (Outputs).

Performance: level of fulfillment of operational capacity of a person or a program.

Activity: a task or a set of interrelated tasks aimed at generating a product or a result.

Indicators: this is a variable used to compare program performance against its stated objective(s).

Targets (Denominators)

To conduct M&E, one must set targets. Targets are set for:

  • 0-11 months old (population <1 year old) for primary infant vaccination.
  • Pregnant women (expected deliveries) for vaccination with tetanus toxoid.
  • Women of Child Bearing Age (for districts considered at high risk of neonatal tetanus) for vaccination with tetanus toxoid.

üë• Supervision & Communication

Supportive Supervision

Definition: Facilitative approach promoting mentorship in joint problem solving & communication btn supervisors and surpervisee. Aimed at improving routine program immunization by close M&E by ensuring staff personal & CPD.

Supervision Styles

  • Autocratic/Authoritarian: Decision is by the supervisor.
  • Laissez-faire: Do what you want.
  • Democratic: Both supervisor and supervisee decide what to do.

Communication & Community Participation

Importance: Creates awareness, acceptance, trust, and utilization of services. Reduces missed opportunities and dropouts. Minimizes mistrust and myths (e.g. AEFIs).

Missed Opportunities

Occurs when any eligible child or woman comes to a health facility and does not receive any or all of the vaccine doses for which he or she is eligible.

Common Causes: False contraindications (fever, mild respiratory infections), Vaccine stock-outs, Scheduling vaccines, Failure to screen status.

üìà Performance Indicators & Analysis

1. Immunization Coverage

Proportion of vaccinated individuals among the target population.

$$Coverage = \frac{\text{Number of individuals vaccinated}}{\text{Target Population}} \times 100$$

2. Drop-out Rate (DOR)

Measures utilization. High dropout (>10%) indicates a problem with service utilization.

$$DOR = \frac{\text{Penta 1} - \text{Penta 3}}{\text{Penta 1}} \times 100$$

3. Vaccine Wastage

Wastage Rate: The percentage of vaccine doses wasted.

$$Wastage Rate = \frac{\text{Doses Issued} - \text{Doses Administered}}{\text{Doses Issued}} \times 100$$

Wastage Factor: A multiplier used for ordering vaccines.

$$Wastage Factor = \frac{100}{100 - \text{Wastage Rate}}$$
üßÆ

Calculation Example

Scenario: 200 doses issued, 150 children immunized.

Wastage Rate: $\frac{200 - 150}{200} \times 100 = 25\%$

Wastage Factor: $\frac{100}{100 - 25} = 1.33$

Problem Analysis: Access vs. Utilization

Using Penta 1 coverage (Access) and Drop-out Rate (Utilization).

Category Access (Penta 1 Coverage) Utilization (Drop-out Rate) Interpretation
No Problem High (>80%) Low (<10%) Good Access, Good Utilization
Category 1 High (>80%) High (>10%) Good Access, Poor Utilization
Category 2 Low (<80%) Low (<10%) Poor Access, Good Utilization
Category 3 Low (<80%) High (>10%) Poor Access, Poor Utilization

üõ†Ô∏è Tools & Evaluation Methods

Routine Data Tools

  • Mother and Child Health Booklet: Individual record kept by caregiver.
  • Immunization Tally Sheet: Daily record of immunizations given.
  • Permanent Register: Facility record. Each child appears once. Used for tracking defaulters.
  • Summary Sheet: Monthly summary sent to district (timeliness/completeness).
  • Immunization Monitor Chart: Graphical presentation of performance (Cumulative totals).

Evaluation Methods

Immunization Coverage Survey

Examines a small number of individuals (e.g. 30x7 cluster survey) to determine true immunization status, reasons for failure, and validity of routine reports.

Data Quality Self-Assessment (DQS)

Flexible method to evaluate accuracy of reported figures and quality of the monitoring system at district/facility levels.
