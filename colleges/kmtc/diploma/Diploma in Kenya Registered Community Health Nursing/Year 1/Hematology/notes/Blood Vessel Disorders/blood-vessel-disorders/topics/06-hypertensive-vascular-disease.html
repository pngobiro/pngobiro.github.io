Hypertensive Vascular Disease

Definition and Overview

Hypertensive vascular disease is characterized by thickening of the arteriolar walls and luminal narrowing. There is an increased susceptibility to aneurysm formation as well as occlusive vascular disease elsewhere.

This condition forms in response to the most important risk factor for atherosclerosis - hypertension. The vascular changes affect primarily the small arteries and arterioles throughout the body.

Types of Hypertensive Vascular Disease

1. Hyaline Arteriolosclerosis

The most common form characterized by:

  • Homogeneous pink hyaline material in arteriolar walls
  • Loss of underlying cellular detail
  • Smooth muscle cell death
  • Increased collagen deposition
  • Most common in elderly patients and diabetics

2. Hyperplastic Arteriolosclerosis

Also known as "onion-skinning" characterized by:

  • Smooth muscle cell proliferation in arteriolar walls
  • Concentric layers of smooth muscle cells
  • Associated with acute hypertension
  • May cause acute arterial necrosis
  • Can lead to malignant hypertension

Pathogenesis

The pathogenesis of hypertensive vascular disease involves:

Mechanical Effects

  • Increased blood pressure causes stress on vessel walls
  • Compensatory smooth muscle cell hypertrophy
  • Eventually leads to hyperplasia

Metabolic Effects

  • Increased glucose and lipid deposition
  • Protein leakage through damaged endothelium
  • Basement membrane thickening

Hemodynamic Effects

  • Altered blood flow patterns
  • Increased turbulence
  • Endothelial dysfunction

Clinical Significance

Target Organs

Hypertensive vascular disease primarily affects:

  • Heart: Left ventricular hypertrophy, coronary artery disease
  • Brain: Stroke, lacunar infarcts, hypertensive encephalopathy
  • Kidneys: Nephrosclerosis, chronic kidney disease
  • Eyes: Hypertensive retinopathy, papilledema
  • Peripheral vessels: Accelerated atherosclerosis

Complications

  • Increased risk of myocardial infarction
  • Cerebrovascular accidents (strokes)
  • Renal failure
  • Aneurysm formation and rupture
  • Dissecting aortic aneurysm
