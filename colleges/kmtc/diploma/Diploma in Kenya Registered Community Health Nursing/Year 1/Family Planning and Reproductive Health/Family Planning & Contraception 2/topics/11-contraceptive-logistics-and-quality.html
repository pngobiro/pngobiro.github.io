Contraceptive Logistics and Quality Assurance

Introduction to Contraceptive Logistics

Managing family planning contraceptive logistics is the process of ensuring that a consistent, quality supply of contraceptives is available at all service delivery points. It involves ordering, storing, reporting, and follow-up to meet client needs effectively.

Purpose of a Contraceptive Logistics System

To obtain and move materials and supplies in a timely manner to the place where they are needed, ensuring they are the:

  • Right item
  • Right quantity
  • Right condition (not expired or damaged)
  • At the right time and right place
  • For the right cost

Supply Distribution Systems

Information and supplies move through the distribution system. In Kenya, supplies typically move from the central stores (Kenya Medical Supplies Agency - KEMSA) to county stores, and then to service delivery points (health facilities). Information flows in both directions to aid in planning.

Push vs. Pull Systems

System TypeDescriptionWhen to Use
Push System The quantity of contraceptives to be distributed is determined by a higher-level authority (e.g., central stores).
  • When information about consumption is unavailable or unreliable.
  • When demand is greater than supply and rationing is needed.
  • When staff at the service delivery point lack logistics management skills.
Pull System The service delivery point places an order for the contraceptives they need based on their consumption data.
  • When data processing at the central level is limited.
  • When staff at the service delivery point have management skills.
  • When there is good supervision.

Inventory and Stock Management

Effective stock management prevents stockouts and overstocking, ensuring clients have a choice of methods.

Conducting a Physical Inventory

A physical inventory is the process of counting all contraceptives on hand by name and brand. It should be conducted regularly (e.g., before writing a quarterly report, or when receiving new supplies).

Purpose of Physical Inventory:

  • •

    To identify differences between actual supplies and what is recorded on stock cards (bin cards).

  • •

    To detect supplies that are about to expire or have already expired.

  • •

    To determine the stock on hand before placing a new order.

During inventory, arrange contraceptives according to the FEFO (First Expiry, First Out) system.

Calculating Stock Levels

Before placing an order, a service provider must determine their consumption rate and required stock levels.

  • 1

    Average Monthly Consumption Rate (AMCR): Calculate the average quantity of each contraceptive brand used per month.
    Formula: (Total consumption of the last 3 months) / 3 = AMCR

  • 2

    Minimum Stock Level: The amount of a contraceptive brand that the service delivery stock should not go below. This is typically a 3-month supply.
    Formula: AMCR x 3 months = Minimum Stock

  • 3

    Maximum Stock Level: The amount of a contraceptive brand that the service delivery stock should not go beyond. This is typically a 6-month supply.
    Formula: AMCR x 6 months = Maximum Stock

Storage of Contraceptives

Proper storage is essential to maintain the shelf life, safety, and potency of contraceptives. The shelf life for most contraceptives is 5 years if stored correctly.

Storage Guidelines

  • ✓

    Store in a dry, well-lit, and ventilated room, out of direct sunlight.

  • ✓

    Do not store cartons directly on the floor. Keep them at least 10 cm from the walls.

  • ✓

    Arrange cartons so that identification labels and expiry dates are clearly visible.

  • ✓

    Store condoms away from electric motors and direct light.

  • ✓

    Store contraceptives away from other chemicals like insecticides.

  • ✓

    Separate and dispose of damaged or expired contraceptives without delay.

Factors that Shorten Shelf Life

The quality of contraceptives can be negatively affected by:

  • Direct Sunlight
  • Extreme Temperatures (heat or cold)
  • Rain and Humidity/Dampness
  • Chemicals
  • Inadequate ventilation

Quality Assurance and Provider Roles

Quality assurance determines that contraceptives are in good condition for clients to use.

How to Determine Quality (Signs of Deterioration)

  • •

    Visual Inspection: Check for changes in color, hardness, or consistency.

  • •

    Package Integrity: Do not use if the inner package is broken, incomplete, or if the seal on injectables is broken.

  • •

    Condoms: Do not use if they are sticky, brittle, or discolored.

  • •

    Expiry Date: Always check the expiry date before dispensing.

  • •

    Client Feedback: Listen to feedback from clients about the quality of the products.

Role and Responsibility of a Service Provider

Service providers are crucial to the success of the logistics management system. Their roles include:

  • Maintaining accurate family planning records (daily activity registers, client files).
  • Calculating AMCR and placing orders for contraceptives correctly.
  • Ensuring good storage of contraceptives following proper guidelines (FEFO).
  • Conducting regular physical inventories.
  • Filling and submitting quarterly reports on time.
  • Training other staff on logistics management.
  • Providing quality services and information to clients.
