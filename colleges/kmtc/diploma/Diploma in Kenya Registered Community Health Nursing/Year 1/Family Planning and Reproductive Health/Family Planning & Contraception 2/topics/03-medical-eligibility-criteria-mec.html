WHO Medical Eligibility Criteria (MEC)

Introduction to the MEC

The World Health Organization (WHO) Medical Eligibility Criteria for Contraceptive Use (MEC) provides recommendations on the safety of using given contraceptive methods in the presence of specific medical conditions or disorders. These guidelines are based on the best available scientific evidence, which is reviewed periodically by expert working groups.

Purpose of the MEC

  • To base guidelines for family planning practices on the best available evidence.
  • To address misconceptions regarding who can and cannot safely use a method.
  • To reduce medical barriers to accessing family planning.
  • To improve access and quality of care in family planning.

The WHO groups medical conditions into four categories to help providers make safe and effective recommendations.

The Four WHO Categories

The MEC categorizes conditions to balance the benefits of a contraceptive method against its potential risks for a client with a specific health condition.

WHO Medical Eligibility Criteria Categories
Category Description Recommendation
Category 1 A condition for which there is no restriction on the use of the contraceptive method. Use the method under any/all circumstances.
Category 2 A condition for which the advantages of using the method generally outweigh the theoretical or proven risks. Generally use the method, though careful follow-up may be required.
Category 3 A condition for which the theoretical or proven risks generally outweigh the advantages of using the method. Do not use the method unless other more appropriate alternatives are not available or acceptable. The method can be used with extreme care and requires access to continuous monitoring.
Category 4 A condition that presents an unacceptable health risk if the contraceptive method is used. Method not to be used under any circumstances.

Application of MEC in Kenya

The national guidelines in Kenya adapt the WHO MEC framework to guide clinical judgment at different levels of the healthcare system.

MEC Application Framework in Kenya
Category Where Clinical Judgement is Adequate Where Clinical Judgement is NOT Adequate
Category 1 Use the method. Use the method.
Category 2 Use the method with care; close follow-up needed in some cases. Initiate method and refer the client for evaluation as soon as possible.
Category 3 Help client to choose another method or use method with extreme care - ensure access to continuous monitoring. Do not use method. Refer client or help her choose an alternative method.
Category 4 Do not use method. Do not use method.

Examples of MEC in Practice

Medical Condition / Characteristic Contraceptive Method Category
Uterine fibroids Combined Oral Contraceptives (COCs) 1
Anemia Copper IUD 2
Breastfeeding, less than 6 weeks postpartum DMPA (Depo-Provera) 3
Current breast cancer Hormonal Implants 4

MEC Guidelines for Clients with HIV and AIDS

Special considerations apply for clients with HIV and AIDS, particularly concerning interactions between hormonal methods and antiretroviral (ARV) therapy. The need for dual protection (preventing both pregnancy and STI/HIV transmission) is a critical factor.

Other Methods:

  • •

    Condoms: No restrictions. Use is encouraged to prevent STI/HIV transmission (dual protection).

  • •

    Emergency Contraceptive Pills (ECPs): No restrictions.

  • •

    Sterilization: No reason to deny. Delay in case of acute HIV-related infection.

  • •

    Lactational Amenorrhea Method (LAM): May be used, but client must be advised on the risk of HIV transmission through breastfeeding.

  • •

    Spermicides and Diaphragm: Not recommended as they may increase the risk of HIV transmission or super-infection.
