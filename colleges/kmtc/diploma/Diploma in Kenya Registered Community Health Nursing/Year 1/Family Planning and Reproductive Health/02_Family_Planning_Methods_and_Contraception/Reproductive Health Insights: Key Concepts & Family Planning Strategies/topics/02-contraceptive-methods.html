Modern Contraceptive Methods

Hormonal Contraceptive Methods

Hormonal contraceptives are amongst the most widely used FP methods worldwide. According to KDHS nearly 44% of women using modern contraceptives choose hormonal method with 26% choosing injectable contraceptives.

Hormonal contraceptives contain synthetic hormones that is combination of estrogen and progestin. Which work primarily by preventing ovulation and making the cervical mucus too thick for sperm movement.

They can be taken in form of:

  1. 1.Oral pills
  2. 2.Injectables (intramuscular or subcutaneous)
  3. 3.Implants
  4. 4.Skin patches
  5. 5.Hormone releasing intrauterine systems or vaginal rings

Examples of hormonal methods commonly available in Kenya

  1. 1.Combined oral contraceptives (COCs)
  2. 2.Progestin only contraceptive pills (POPs)
  3. 3.Progestin only injectables contraceptives
  4. 4.Progestine only contraceptive implants e.g jadelle, implanon,zarin
  5. 5.Hormone-releasing intrauterine system (LNG-IUS)
  6. 6.Dedicated products for emergency contraception
  7. 7.Vaginal contraceptive rings included combined hormonal e.g nuvaring

1. Combined Oral Contraceptive Pills (COCs)

Combined oral contraceptive are pills that contain synthetic estrogen and progesterone (progestin) which are similar to the natural hormones produced in a woman body.

Key messages

  • Take one pill everyday
  • Take any missed pill as soon as possible
  • Use COCs helps protect from ovarian and endometrical cancers

NB: effectiveness depends on the user. its 97% effective in preventing pregnancy if correctly and consistently.

Advantages

  • Highly effective if used correctly and consistently
  • Are effective immediately if given within the first 5 days of the cycle
  • Easy to use, easy to obtain can be provided by trained non clinical service providers
  • COCs are safe for the majority of women
  • Reduction of menstrual flow (lighter,shorter periods)
  • Decrease in dysmenorrhea (painful periods)
  • Reduction of symptoms of endometriosis and polycystic ovarian syndrome (PCOS)
  • Improvement and prevention of iron deficiency anemia
  • Protection against ovarian and endometrial cancer
  • Treatment for acne and hirsutism

Limitations of COCs

  1. COCs must be taken daily to be effective, preferably at the same time each day
  2. Effectiveness may be lowered if client is on TB drugs, anti epilepsy treatment
  3. Contraceptive effectiveness, could also be lowered in the presence of gastroenteritis ,severe vomiting and diarrhea
  4. COCS do not offer protection against STI including Hepatitis B and HIV. therefore, at risk individual should use condoms to ensure protection against STI
  5. Reduce milk production in breastfeeding women

Side Effects of COCs

Minor Side Effects: Nausea, spotting or bleeding, mild headaches, breast tenderness, mood changes.

Major Side Effects: Major side effects or complications are rare but possible include Stroke and Venous thrombosis.

2. Progestin Only Pills (POPs)

The progestin only pills (POPS) also called the mini pill are oral hormonal contraceptive that contain progesterone only in smaller doses.

Effectiveness

Pops are 99.5% effective if used correctly and consistently during exclusive breastfeeding. They are most effective when taken at the same time everyday.

Advantages of POPs

  • Effective and safe
  • Does not affect breast milk production and can be used during breastfeeding starting 6 weeks after child birth
  • Pelvic exam is not required to initiate use
  • Suitable for women with risk factors such as heart attack, stroke and thrombosis
  • Return to fertility is immediate upon discontinuation

Limitations

  1. They provide a slightly lower level of contraceptive protection than COCs
  2. They require strict daily pill taking, preferably at the same time
  3. They do not protect against STI, including hepatitis B and HIV/AIDS. Therefore at risk individual should use barrier method to ensure protection against STI and HIV/AID
  4. Effectiveness may decrease if the client are also taking some other medications such as anti TB drugs, anti convulsant and antiretroviral

Side Effects

Irregular spotting or bleeding, frequent or infrequent bleeding, prolonged bleeding, bleeding changes are common but not harmful. Headache, dizziness, nausea, mood changes, breast tenderness.

Who can use POPs

It is suitable for all ages, women of any parity, postpartum (non-breastfeeding mothers immediately and breastfeeding women at 6 weeks), post-abortion, and for those with risk factors like smoking or obesity.

3. Emergency Hormonal Contraceptive Pills (EC)

Emergency contraception (EC) refers to the use of certain contraceptive methods by women to prevent pregnancy after unprotected sexual intercourse. EC provides emergency protection (prevents pregnancy) for about 75-95% of those at risk.

Mode of action for the EC

Prevention or delaying ovulation; Inhibiting or slowing down transportation of the egg and sperm through the fallopian tubes, which prevents fertilization and implantation.
NB: ECs do not work once a woman is pregnant.

Effectiveness

EC are 98% effective if used correctly i.e. if taking ECS within 120 hours. The earlier the ECS is used after unprotected sexual intercourse the more effective they are.

Advantages of ECs

  1. Safe, effective and easy to use
  2. Runs protection after unprotected sexual intercourse
  3. Can be used in emergency situations without having to see a clinician
  4. Accessible and has less serious side effects
  5. Can be used as a backup method, can be used anytime in the menstrual cycle

Limitation and side effects of ECs

  • They are only effective if used within 120 hours of unprotected intercourse
  • They are not used as regular method of contraception
  • Do not protect against STIs, HIV or AIDS
  • They can cause nausea

4. Injectable Contraceptives

Injectable contraceptives contain one or two contraceptive hormones and provide protection from pregnancy for one, two or three months depending on the type. The most widely used injectable method contain only progestin (e.g., Depot-medroxyprogesterone acetate) given at three monthly intervals.

Mode of action

Progestin-only injectables prevent pregnancy by preventing the release of eggs from the ovaries (suppressing ovulation).

Effectiveness

Effectiveness depends on receiving injections on time. It is 99% effective if used correctly and consistently.

Advantages of POIs (Progestin-only injection)

  • Highly effective and safe
  • Convenient as it doesn't require daily action
  • They do not contain estrogen thus do not have cardiac and blood clotting side effects
  • Do not affect breastmilk production hence can be used during breastfeeding

Side effects/ disadvantages

Changes in menstrual bleeding patterns, weight changes, headaches, dizziness, mood swings, abdominal bloating, acne, breast tenderness. Return of fertility may be delayed four months or longer after discontinuation.

5. Contraceptive Implants

Contraceptive implants also sub dermal implants are small hormone progesterone bearing capsules or rods which when inserted under the skin of woman upper arm, release the hormone slowly over a period of time to prevent pregnancy.

Mode of action

Contraceptive implants prevent pregnancy primarily by making cervical mucus too thick for sperm to penetrate. They also suppress ovulation in many cycles.

Effectiveness of implants

Implants provide 99.9% effective protection against pregnancy. They are effective 24 hrs post insertion.

Types of Contraception Implants

  • Jadelle - 2 rods - 5 years
  • Implanon and Implanon NXT - 1 rod - 3 years
  • Sino implant (Zarin) - 2 rods - 4 years
  • Indoplant - 2 rods - 4 years

Advantages of implants

  • Highly effective and offers long term protection against pregnancy
  • Does not interfere with act of sexual intercourse
  • Effective within 24 hrs after insertion
  • No frequent clinic visit required
  • Fertility returns immediately after implants are removed
  • May reduce menstrual flow and protect against iron deficiency anemia
  • They help protect against endometrial cancer

Side effects of using implants

Change in menstrual patterns, headache, dizziness, nausea, breast tenderness, mood changes, weight changes, mild abdominal pain.

Non-Hormonal & Permanent Methods

Intrauterine Contraceptive Devices (IUCD)

An intrauterine device is a small flexible plastic device inserted into the uterine cavity to prevent pregnancy. It provides long term protection against pregnancy.

Types of IUCD

  • Copper based devices: Release copper and work mainly by preventing fertilization.
  • Hormone releasing devices: Release a progestin over a period of five years.

Effectiveness

IUCD is 99% effective if used correctly and consistently.

Advantages of IUCDs

  • High effectiveness and safety
  • Provides immediate protection after insertion
  • Long acting protection (copper based 12 years, hormone releasing 5 years)
  • Immediate return to fertility upon removal of the device
  • Copper IUCD is effective as an emergency contraceptive if inserted within 5 days after unprotected sex.
  • Do not interfere with breastfeeding

Limitation and side effects

Do not offer protection against STI/HIV transmission. Require trained service provider for insertion and removal. Copper IUCDs might increase menstrual bleeding and cause cramping more commonly during the first few months.

Voluntary Surgical Contraception (VSC)

Voluntary surgical contraception (VSC) includes surgical procedure that are intended to provide permanent contraception these include Bilateral tubal ligation (female) and Vasectomy (male).

Key messages

  • Permanent and irreversible method
  • Very effective protection against pregnancy
  • Has no effect on sex drive
  • After vasectomy the couple must use a backup method for at least 3 months

Female Voluntary Surgical Contraception (Bilateral Tubal Ligation)

This is a minor surgical operation that involves cutting and tying the fallopian tubes in order to prevent the sperm from fertilizing the ovum. BTL is a permanent contraception method for women not wanting any more children.

Advantages of BTL

  • Highly effective and safe
  • Efficacy does not depend on the client action
  • It is permanent
  • Has no effect on breastfeeding
  • Does not affect a woman sexual desire, ability and performance
  • It is cost effective after the initial procedures
  • No significant long term side effects

Key Points

  • •

    Hormonal contraceptives (pills, injectables, implants) work by preventing ovulation and thickening cervical mucus, with effectiveness rates ranging from 97% to 99.9%.

  • •

    Long-acting reversible contraceptives (LARCs) like implants and IUCDs offer highly effective, long-term protection (3-12 years) and immediate return to fertility upon removal.

  • •

    Emergency Contraception (EC) can prevent pregnancy if used within 120 hours of unprotected sex but is not intended for regular use.

  • •

    Voluntary Surgical Contraception (Bilateral Tubal Ligation and Vasectomy) is a permanent, highly effective method for individuals or couples who have completed their families.

  • •

    No contraceptive method, other than condoms, offers protection against Sexually Transmitted Infections (STIs), including HIV.
