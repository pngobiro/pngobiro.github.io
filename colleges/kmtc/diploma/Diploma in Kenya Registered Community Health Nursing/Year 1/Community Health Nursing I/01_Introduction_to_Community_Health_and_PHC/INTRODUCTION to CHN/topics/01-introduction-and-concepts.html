Learning Objectives

By the end of this session, the learner should be able to;

  1. Define Community Health
  2. State the components of a community
  3. Explain functions of a community
  4. Outline the sub-systems of a community
  5. Describe goals of community health
  6. Describe features and principles of community health nursing

Definitions

Health

A state of physical, mental and social wellbeing not merely the absence of disease or infirmity(WHO)

What is nursing?

  • Assisting individuals - sick and well to attain, maintain and recover optimum health and wellness

Community

A group of people living in a certain geographical area and having a common interest or goal.

Elements of a Community

  • A group of people
  • Interact with each other
  • Having shared;
    • Territory & Administrative unit
    • Resources
    • Language
    • Religion
    • Culture
    • Occupation
    • Disadvantages (e.g. shortages)
  • Share a sense of belonging

Examples of Community

  • Occupants of a town/sub county/village (e.g. slum community)
  • Group of farmers (e.g. fishing community)
  • Institutions' community (e.g. KMTC student community, prisons community)
  • Persons living with disability (PLWD community)
  • Caregivers of mentally ill/terminally ill

Population and Community Health

Population

Definition: The whole number of people occupying a country or region. These People do not necessarily interact with one another and do not necessarily share a sense of belonging to that group.

Community Health

Involves identification of needs, promotion of health and prevention of disease and disability within a geographically defined area or within a group of people with common interest through organized community participation. Also referred to as Community medicine.

Components of a community

The community is made up of:

  • Individual persons - Each individual is unique. Each has a mixture of characteristics, some of which are part of a particular culture (e.g. language, beliefs, customs)
  • Families - each of the individuals belong to a family. The smallest recognized group of individuals in a community. An individual can only attain full health if they have wholesome growth in a family.

Functions of a Community

  • Transmitting and sharing information, ideas and beliefs
  • Educating its children about their culture, norms and behavior (socializing)
  • Welcoming newcomers into the group's culture;
  • Introducing the community's values, beliefs, language, customs and mannerisms to the newcomers (acculturation)
  • Producing and distributing goods & services
  • Providing companionship and support to individual members and smaller groups
  • Sharing and utilizing space for living, schools, health facilities, fields, roads etc.
  • Protecting individual and group rights and welfare

Community sub systems

  • Socio-cultural subsystem - customs, beliefs, family ways
  • Political subsystem - governance and policies
  • Educational subsystem - systems of passing knowledge & information
  • Religious subsystem - structures that impart values through religion
  • Environmental subsystems - safe water, waste disposal, sanitation
  • Health subsystem - structures for preventive, curative and rehabilitative health services
  • Economic subsystem - structures for income generation

NOTE: The Sub-systems determine whether a community will be healthy or problematic

Goals of Community Health

  • Increasing the lifespan of every individual
  • Increasing the total well being (physical, mental & social) of every individual
  • Increasing the quality of life for all people
  • Decreasing mortality rates particularly of mothers & children
  • Decreasing morbidity rates (the state of being unneathy for a particular disease or situation) from all causes

Community Health goals are achieved by:

  1. Promotion of health - Empower individuals & communities to choose healthy behavior and reduce risk of developing diseases. The most important component of community health.
  2. Prevention of health problems
  3. Treatment of disorders
  4. Rehabilitation
  5. Evaluation
  6. Research

Community Health Activities

  • Health education, counseling and training
  • Community health assessment and diagnosis
  • Environmental sanitation & Supply of clean water
  • Personal hygiene
  • Food hygiene and security
  • Vector and pest control
  • Control of communicable and non communicable diseases
  • Maternal and child health - Antenatal services, immunization services, Family planning services
  • Community Health Worker services
  • School health services
  • Home visiting
  • Home Based Nursing
  • Occupational nursing - Care of disabled, elderly, disadvantaged & chronically ill
  • Early diagnosis and prevention of disease
  • Disease surveillance - Case/contact tracing and treatment
  • Outreach and mobile services
