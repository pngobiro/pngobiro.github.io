ENVIRONMENTAL HEALTH

UNIT OBJECTIVES

This unit will cover the following key areas:

  • Types of environment (biological, physical, political, economic)
  • Food safety and hygiene
  • Water and sanitation
  • Housing
  • Waste and excreta disposal
  • Air and ventilation
  • Pollution
  • Public health act
  • Control of vector and pests
  • Social and cultural factors that affect health

DEFINITION OF ENVIRONMENTAL HEALTH

Environmental health is the branch of public health that is concerned with all aspects of the natural and built environment that may affect human health. It focuses on preventing disease and creating health-supportive environments by assessing and controlling environmental factors.

Public Health Officers

Also known as Public Health officers, these professionals are responsible for carrying out measures for protecting public health, including administering and enforcing legislation related to environmental health and providing support to minimize health and safety hazards.

ENVIRONMENT TYPES

  1. Natural environment - is not man-made, such as Earth and all of its natural component including ground, water, flora and fauna. It is full of beauty and hazards.
  2. Induced environment - are those been affected by human action e.g. highly polluted air that results from exhaust emissions of automobiles in heavily populated cities.
  3. Controlled environment - is a natural or induced environment that has been changed in some way to reduce or eliminate potential environmental hazards e.g. a home or workplace that is cooled to reduce potential hazards associated with heat.
  4. Artificial environment - one that is fully created to prevent definite hazardous conditions from affecting people or material e.g. environment within the space shuttle.
  5. Closed environment - one that is completely or almost completely shut off from the natural environment. Both controlled and artificial environments must be closed.
  6. Free environment - one that does not interfere with the free movement of air.

PUBLIC HEALTH ACT

The guiding legislation is CAP 242 (revised 2012 from 1986), with a commencement date of September 6th, 1921. This Act of parliament makes provisions for securing and maintaining health in Kenya. It covers rules related to food hygiene, protection of water supplies, animal keeping, and the control of nuisances and disease vectors like mosquitos.

You can find the full text at www.kenyalaw.org or by searching for the Public Health Act.

Student Task

Students are expected to read and familiarize themselves with the Public Health Act (CAP 242).
