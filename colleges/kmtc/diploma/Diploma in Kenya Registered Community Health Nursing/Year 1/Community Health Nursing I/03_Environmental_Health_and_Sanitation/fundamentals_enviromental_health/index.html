Basic Health Computer Course

Duration: 10 hours Level: Beginner

Course Overview

This course provides a comprehensive introduction to environmental health, covering its basic principles, historical development, key figures, and practical applications in public health. It explores the role of environmental factors in health and disease, and offers strategies for prevention and control. It also includes details on the Environmental Health and Sanitation department.

Course Modules

  1. Introduction to Environmental Health

    Basic definitions, terminologies, and early practices.

    15 minutes

  2. Historical Context of Environmental Health

    History of public health from early religions to the 19th century.

    15 minutes

  3. Key Figures in Environmental Health

    Contributions of Edwin Chadwick and the Mosaic Law.

    15 minutes

  4. Public Health in Kenya

    Early medical work by missionaries to 1920s and training of health workers.

    15 minutes

  5. Appointments, Training and Qualifications

    Health worker training, rules and regulations.

    15 minutes

  6. DEHS and Its Functions

    Structure of DEHS, its vision, mission, and the divisions.

    15 minutes

  7. DEHS Core Functions and Challenges

    Rationale, core functions, and challenges faced by DEHS.

    15 minutes

  8. Principles of Disease: Stages and Prevention

    Stages of disease, primary, secondary, and tertiary prevention.

    15 minutes

  9. Risk Assessment in Environmental Health

    Qualitative and quantitative risk assessment

    15 minutes

  10. Pillars of Environmental Health

    Community health, air quality, pollution control, food quality and built environment.

    15 minutes

  11. Understanding Disease Transmission

    Communicable diseases, source of infection, routes of transmission, and susceptible hosts.

    15 minutes
