Module 1: Concepts and Principles of Health Promotion

Question 1

Define health promotion according to the Ottawa Charter (1986). (2 marks)

Sample Answer:

Health promotion is the process of enabling people to increase control over, and to improve, their health. It moves beyond a focus on individual behaviour towards a wide range of social and environmental interventions.

Question 2

List and briefly describe three core values of health promotion. (6 marks)

Sample Answer:

  1. Empowerment: Enabling people to gain greater control over decisions and actions affecting their health.
  2. Participative: Ensuring people take an active part in decision-making processes related to health promotion initiatives.
  3. Holistic: Taking into account the separate influences on health and the interaction of these dimensions (physical, mental, social, environmental).

Question 3

Explain the significance of the Health Promotion Logo established in 1986. (4 marks)

Sample Answer:

The Health Promotion Logo symbolizes a multi-pronged approach to health promotion. It represents:

  • Five action areas: building healthy public policy, creating supportive environments, strengthening community action, developing personal skills, and reorienting health services.
  • Three fundamental strategies: enabling, mediating, and advocacy.
  • The interconnectedness of policy, environment, community, skills and services in promoting health.

Question 4

Describe two historical periods that significantly shaped the field of health promotion. (4 marks)

Sample Answer:

  1. The Early Public Health Movement in Europe (18th-19th Century): Characterized by responses to industrialization and urbanization, focusing on environmental sanitation and public health reforms, driven by concerns over infectious diseases.
  2. The New Public Health Movement (Post-1974 Lalonde Report): Shifted focus to broader determinants of health beyond medical care, emphasizing lifestyle, environment, and human biology. Highlighted the need for health promotion strategies beyond the medical model.

Question 5

What are the main aims of health promotion? List any four. (4 marks)

Sample Answer:

  1. To give people appropriate education and information to make choices about their health.
  2. To enable people to examine those health choices.
  3. To help people develop the skills to analyze and recognize alternative health choices.
  4. To empower people to be able to make informed decisions about their health.

Module 2: Processes of Health Promotion

Question 6

Outline the six key steps in planning a health promotion program. (12 marks)

Sample Answer:

  1. Manage the Planning Process: Develop a plan to manage stakeholder participation, timelines, resources, and decision-making methods.
  2. Conduct a Situational Assessment: Learn about the population of interest, their needs, assets, and issues affecting implementation.
  3. Identify Goals, Populations of Interest, Outcomes and Outcome Objectives: Determine overall goals, specific populations to target, and desired outcomes.
  4. Identify Strategies, Activities, Outputs, Process Objectives, and Resources: Select feasible strategies and activities to achieve objectives, considering available resources.
  5. Develop Indicators: Create measurable variables to track the extent to which objectives are met.
  6. Review the Program Plan: Ensure clarity, consistency, and adequacy of resources, aligning with situational assessment findings.

Explain how socioeconomic conditions can act as determinants of health. Provide three examples. (6 marks)

Socioeconomic conditions significantly influence health by shaping living and working conditions, access to resources, and opportunities. Examples:

Module 4: Theories and Models of Health Promotion

Question 16

Define what is meant by a ‘theory’ and a ‘model’ in health promotion. (4 marks)

Sample Answer:

  • Theory: A plausible or scientifically acceptable general principle or body of principles offered to explain phenomena. It's a system of ideas intended to explain something, based on general principles.
  • Model: A simplified description that can be used to predict something. In health promotion, models are often used as frameworks to understand and address health behaviors.

Question 17

Explain the key concepts of the Health Belief Model (HBM). (6 marks)

Sample Answer:

The Health Belief Model (HBM) proposes that health behavior is influenced by:

  1. Perceived Susceptibility: Belief about the chances of getting a condition.
  2. Perceived Severity: Belief about the seriousness of a condition and its consequences.
  3. Perceived Benefits: Belief about the effectiveness of taking action to reduce risk.
  4. Perceived Barriers: Beliefs about the material and psychological costs of taking action.
  5. Cues to Action: Factors that activate "readiness to change".
  6. Self-Efficacy: Confidence in one's ability to take action.

Question 18

Describe three stages of change in the Transtheoretical Model (Stages of Change model). (6 marks)

Sample Answer:

  1. Pre-contemplation: Individual is not intending to take action in the foreseeable future (usually defined as within the next 6 months). They may be unaware or in denial about the problem.
  2. Contemplation: Individual is intending to change in the next 6 months. They are aware of the pros of changing but also acutely aware of the cons.
  3. Preparation: Individual is intending to take action in the immediate future, usually defined as in the next month. They have a plan of action and may have taken some behavioral steps in this direction.

Question 19

How does Social Cognitive Theory (SCT) explain behavior change through reciprocal determinism and self-efficacy? (6 marks)

Sample Answer:

Social Cognitive Theory (SCT) explains behavior change through:

  • Reciprocal Determinism: Behavior is a result of dynamic interaction between person, behavior, and environment. Change strategies consider multiple ways to promote behavior change by adjusting the environment or influencing personal attitudes.
  • Self-Efficacy: Confidence in one's ability to take action. SCT emphasizes that behavior change is more likely when individuals believe in their capability to succeed. Interventions should boost self-efficacy through mastery experiences, modeling, social persuasion, and reducing stress.

Question 20

Outline the five diagnostic steps of the PRECEDE-PROCEED model. (10 marks)

Sample Answer:

  1. Social Assessment (Phase 1): Assess the community's views of their own needs and quality of life.
  2. Epidemiological Assessment (Phase 2): Identify the health problems that are most important for specific groups in a community and their behavioral and environmental causes.
  3. Behavioral and Environmental Assessment (Phase 3): Identify the behavioral and environmental factors that contribute to the health problems of interest.
  4. Educational and Ecological Assessment (Phase 4): Identify the predisposing, enabling, and reinforcing factors that must be in place to initiate and sustain change.
  5. Administrative and Policy Assessment (Phase 5): Analyze the policies, resources, and organizational circumstances in the program's context that may help or hinder implementation.

Module 5: Health Promotion Approaches

Question 21

Describe the medical approach to health promotion and its levels of prevention. (5 marks)

Sample Answer:

The medical or preventive approach aims to reduce premature death by targeting populations at higher risk of disease. It operates at three levels:

  1. Primary Prevention: Preventing the onset of disease (e.g., immunization, safe nutrition).
  2. Secondary Prevention: Preventing progression through early detection and diagnosis (e.g., screening programs).
  3. Tertiary Prevention: Preventing further disability and suffering in those already ill (e.g., rehabilitation, palliative care).

Question 22

Explain the behavior change approach to health promotion, including its purpose and criticisms. (6 marks)

Sample Answer:

The behavior change approach assumes healthy lifestyles are crucial for maintaining good health. It aims to encourage people to adopt healthy behaviors through:

  • Purpose: To inform individuals about unhealthy behaviors and motivate them to change through communication, education, and persuasion.
  • Criticisms: Overlooks complex health decisions, socio-economic factors, and operates top-down. Assumes a direct link between knowledge, attitudes, and behavior, and homogeneity among receivers, which is often not the case.

Question 23

What are the key principles of the Peer to Peer approach in health promotion? (4 marks)

Sample Answer:

Key principles of the Peer to Peer approach:

  • Recognizes peer groups' strong influence on behavior, especially among young people.
  • Utilizes peer educators who are credible and relatable to their target group.
  • Empowers young people to provide advice and support to their peers on health issues.
  • Promotes positive health behaviors through social influence and interaction.

Question 24

Describe the Child-to-Child (CtC) approach and its two main pillars. (5 marks)

Sample Answer:

The Child-to-Child (CtC) approach empowers children to contribute to their own and others' health and well-being. It's developed for school-aged children to learn and pass on health messages. Its pillars are:

  1. Popular Education: Learning from one another in a non-expert environment, based on learners' concerns and experiences.
  2. Child Participation: Children playing a meaningful role in the world around them, in line with the UN Convention on the Rights of the Child, recognizing their right to participate in decisions affecting them.

Question 25

Explain the educational approach to health promotion, including its focus and main aspects. (4 marks)

Sample Answer:

The educational approach assumes increasing knowledge leads to healthier behavior. It focuses on:

  • Providing people with knowledge and information about health.
  • Learning and comprises three aspects: Cognitive (understanding), Affective (feelings and attitudes), and Behavioral (skills).
  • Aiming to improve health literacy, enabling individuals to access, understand, and use health information.

Module 6: Health Promotion Strategies

Question 26

What is advocacy in health promotion, and list four roles of advocacy. (6 marks)

Sample Answer:

Advocacy is a combination of social actions designed to gain political commitment, policy support, social acceptance, and systems support for a particular goal or program.

Roles of advocacy:

  1. Influence changes in policies, programs, and environments.
  2. Mobilize resources and opinions to support changes.
  3. Increase the power of people and groups to make institutions more responsive.
  4. Widen the range of choices for people in defining problems and solutions.

Question 27

Describe networking as a health promotion strategy and its importance. (4 marks)

Sample Answer:

Networking is working collaboratively across disciplines, sectors, and partners to enhance the impact and sustainability of health promotion programs and policies.

Importance:

  • Useful for coordination, avoiding duplication, and sharing information.
  • Essential in planning, implementing, and evaluating health promotion activities.
  • Builds allies and leverages resources for greater impact.

Question 28

Explain social marketing as a health promotion strategy, detailing its four Ps. (8 marks)

Sample Answer:

Social Marketing applies marketing concepts to promote socially beneficial ideas and behaviors. Its four Ps are:

  1. Product: The socially desirable goal, not necessarily a physical product, e.g., behavioral or attitudinal change.
  2. Price: What the "buyer" must give up to adopt the product, including monetary costs, time, effort, or psychological costs.
  3. Place: How and where the "product" is made available, ensuring convenient and compatible distribution channels.
  4. Promotion: Communication and persuasion strategies to make the "product" familiar, acceptable, and desirable, emphasizing benefits over telling.

Question 29

Discuss the importance of communication in health promotion. List three types of communication. (6 marks)

Sample Answer:

Communication is crucial in health promotion for exchanging information, influencing attitudes, and changing behaviors. It helps in:

  • Saving lives and preventing disease spread.
  • Encouraging improvements in health services.
  • Increasing social involvement and empowerment.

Three types of communication:

  1. Verbal Communication: Using sounds, words, language, and speech for direct interaction and emotional expression.
  2. Nonverbal Communication: Conveying messages without words, using physical cues like tone of voice, touch, and expressions.
  3. Written Communication: Using written words in letters, emails, reports, etc., allowing for editing and formal documentation.

Module 7: Social Mobilization and Capacity Building

Question 31

Define social mobilization and its primary aim in community development. (3 marks)

Social Mobilization is a process where people are organized to collectively think and act upon their development. Its primary aim in community development is to enable communities to recover from conflicts and disasters by thinking, understanding, organizing, and initiating action with their own creativity.

Question 32

List and briefly describe the first three steps involved in developing an Advocacy Communication and Social Mobilization Campaign (ACSM). (9 marks)

Sample Answer:

  1. STEP 1: Situation Analysis: Analyze in detail the causes/reasons for a program challenge, such as poor case detection, identifying possible reasons like lack of awareness or poor knowledge.
  2. STEP 2: Audience Segmentation, Prioritizing, and Profiling: Divide populations into smaller groups with similar communication-related needs, prioritize which audiences to focus on, and profile them to personalize communication.
  3. STEP 3: Developing ACSM Objectives: Define communication objectives that provide direction by answering questions like what audience/target group to change and which barriers to address through communication.

Question 33

Explain the concept of capacity building in health promotion. (3 marks)

Sample Answer:

Capacity building is about enhancing the abilities of individuals, communities, organizations, or systems to increase involvement, decision-making, and ownership of health issues. It aims to make health promotion efforts more effective and sustainable by extending beyond traditional health sector boundaries.

Question 34

How does social mobilization contribute to advancing sustainable development policy? (3 marks)

Sample Answer:

Social mobilization advances sustainable development policy by making it more responsive and accountable to people's needs and demands. It ensures policies are grounded in community needs and that development is participatory and inclusive.

Question 35

What are the final four steps (Step 4-7) in developing an Advocacy Communication and Social Mobilization Campaign (ACSM)? (8 marks)

Sample Answer:

  1. STEP 4: Defining the Strategic Approach: Decide on the appropriate approach to reach the intended audience and address program challenges effectively, choosing between advocacy, mass media, or community mobilization.
  2. STEP 5: Selection of Channels: Select the most appropriate and cost-effective media/channels of communication to reach the target audiences with the communication messages.
  3. STEP 6: Creating and Messaging: Employ a scientific approach in creating and designing communication messages, ensuring they are developed with initial concept testing, message designing, and pre-testing.
  4. STEP 7: ACSM Implementation: Focus on quality implementation of ACSM activities, requiring preparedness and attention to all aspects and arrangements, with every planned activity requiring preparation.

How can the Transtheoretical Model be applied to design interventions for smoking cessation? (4 marks)

The Transtheoretical Model informs smoking cessation interventions by:

  • Tailoring interventions to individuals' stage of change (e.g., pre-contemplation, contemplation, preparation).

  • Recognizing relapse as a normal part of the change process and providing support for maintenance.

Question 83

What is the difference between 'health' and 'absence of disease' from a health promotion perspective? (3 marks)

Sample Answer:

From a health promotion perspective:

  • Health is seen as a positive concept emphasizing social and personal resources, and physical capacities, encompassing physical, mental, and social well-being.
  • Absence of disease is a narrower, negative definition focusing only on not being ill. Health promotion goes beyond merely avoiding disease to actively enhancing well-being and quality of life.

  • Highlighted the need to address the growing global inequities and challenges to health.

Module 3: Determinants of Health

Question 91

How does "Food insecurity" act as a social determinant of health? (3 marks)

Sample Answer:

Food insecurity acts as a social determinant of health by:

  • Limiting availability of nutritious and safe foods, leading to poor nutrition and hunger.
  • Increasing the risk of negative health outcomes across all ages, especially for children's physical and mental development.
  • Being caused by socioeconomic factors like low income and joblessness, creating a cycle of poor health and poverty.

Question 92

Explain the impact of "Poor quality and inadequate housing" on health. (3 marks)

Sample Answer:

Poor quality and inadequate housing impacts health by:

  • Contributing to infectious and chronic diseases, injuries, and poor child development.
  • Increasing exposure to hazards like dampness, contaminants, and lack of safety devices.
  • Affecting mental health due to lack of privacy, security, and stability.

Question 93

How does "Occupational health" contribute to overall determinants of health framework? (3 marks)

Sample Answer:

Occupational health contributes to the determinants of health framework by:

  • Focusing on the protection and promotion of workers' health.
  • Preventing and controlling occupational diseases and accidents.
  • Eliminating occupational factors and conditions hazardous to health and safety at work.
  • Recognizing workplace as a key setting that influences health and well-being.

Question 94

What are the "Lifestyle Factors" that significantly contribute to poor health outcomes? (4 marks)

Sample Answer:

Lifestyle factors that significantly contribute to poor health outcomes include:

  • Smoking: Leading to lung cancer, cardiovascular diseases, and respiratory illnesses.
  • Alcohol Abuse: Causing liver cirrhosis, violence, and accidents.
  • Poor Diet: Contributing to obesity, heart disease, and diabetes.
  • Lack of Physical Exercise: Increasing risk of obesity and related chronic conditions.

Question 95

Explain how "Political determinants of health" operate to create health inequities? (3 marks)

Sample Answer:

Political determinants of health create health inequities by:

  • Structuring relationships and distributing resources unequally through systematic processes.
  • Operating through voting, government actions, and policies that favor certain groups over others.
  • Leading to unequal opportunities and resources, resulting in disparities in health outcomes among different populations.

Module 5: Health Promotion Approaches

Question 96

What are the limitations of the Medical Approach in addressing complex health issues? (3 marks)

Sample Answer:

Limitations of the Medical Approach include:

  • Focus on disease prevention and treatment, often neglecting broader determinants of health.
  • Primarily individual-centered, with less emphasis on community and environmental factors.
  • Can be costly and resource-intensive without addressing root causes of health problems.

Question 97

In Behavior change approach, what is 'positive deviance' approach? (3 marks)

Sample Answer:

Positive deviance approach in behavior change:

  • Focuses on identifying individuals or groups within a community who exhibit exceptionally positive health behaviors despite facing similar challenges.
  • Learns from these 'positive deviants' to understand successful strategies.
  • Promotes these strategies within the broader community as locally relevant and effective solutions.

Question 98

How does 'Popular education' pillar enhance Child-to-Child (CtC) approach? (3 marks)

'Popular education' enhances CtC approach by:

  • Creating a learner-centered environment where children learn from each other.
  • Valuing children's experiences and knowledge as central to the learning process.
  • Fostering collaborative and participatory learning where everyone teaches and learns, breaking down traditional expert-learner hierarchies.

Question 99

What are the three aspects of learning emphasized in 'Educational approach' to health promotion? (3 marks)

Sample Answer:

Three aspects of learning emphasized in 'Educational approach' are:

  1. Cognitive: Addressing people's understanding and knowledge about health topics.
  2. Affective: Considering individual's feelings, attitudes, and values towards health.
  3. Behavioral: Focusing on developing people's skills and abilities to adopt healthy behaviors.

Question 100

How does 'Empowerment or client centered approach' promote patient autonomy in healthcare settings? (3 marks)

Sample Answer:

'Empowerment or client centered approach' promotes patient autonomy by:
