Principles & Practice of Health Promotion I

Duration: 80 hours Level: Intermediate

Course Overview

This course is designed to equip learners with an understanding of the core principles and practices of health promotion. It covers key concepts, theories, approaches, and strategies for effective health promotion interventions.

Course Modules

  1. Module 1: Concepts and Principles of Health Promotion

    Introduction to health promotion, key terms, aims, values, and historical background.

    20 hours

  2. Module 2: Processes of Health Promotion

    Explores the steps and strategies involved in planning and implementing health promotion programs.

    10 hours

  3. Module 3: Determinants of Health

    Focuses on the various factors that influence health, including social, economic, and political determinants.

    10 hours

  4. Module 4: Theories and Models of Health Promotion

    Overview of key theories and models used to understand and facilitate behavior change.

    20 hours

  5. Module 5: Health Promotion Approaches

    Examines different approaches to health promotion, including medical, behavioral, and educational approaches.

    10 hours

  6. Module 6: Health Promotion Strategies

    Overview of essential strategies such as advocacy, networking, communication, and health education.

    8 hours

  7. Module 7: Social Mobilization and Capacity Building

    Explores how to mobilize communities and build capacity for health initiatives.

    2 hours

  8. Module 8: Policy Development, Community Mobilization, and Participation

    Discusses the role of policy and community engagement in health promotion.

    2 hours

Prerequisites

  • Basic understanding of public health concepts.
