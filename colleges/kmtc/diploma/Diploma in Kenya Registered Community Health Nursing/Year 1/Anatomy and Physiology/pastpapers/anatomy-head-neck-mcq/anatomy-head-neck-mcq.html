Question 1

Which of the following statements is INCORRECT? The thyroid cartilage:

  1. Is hyaline
  2. May ossify
  3. Is attached to the epiglottis
  4. Articulates with the cricoid cartilage
  5. Gives origin to part of the middle constrictor

Answer

The correct answer is E.

Explanation: The statement that the thyroid cartilage "Gives origin to part of the middle constrictor" is incorrect. The middle constrictor muscle arises from the hyoid bone and the stylohyoid ligament. It is the inferior pharyngeal constrictor that takes partial origin from the oblique line of the thyroid cartilage. The other statements are all correct.

Question 2

Which of the following statements is INCORRECT ? Deep to hyoglossus is the:

  1. Middle constrictor
  2. Lingual artery
  3. Intermediate tendon of digastric
  4. Genioglossus
  5. Insertion of geniohyoid

Answer

The correct answer is C.

Explanation: The intermediate tendon of the digastric muscle is incorrect as it lies superficial to the hyoglossus muscle, not deep to it. The lingual artery, genioglossus, insertion of geniohyoid, and the middle constrictor muscle are all found deep to the hyoglossus muscle.

Question 3

Helping to bound the laryngeal aditus is ALL BUT ONE of the following:

  1. Corniculate cartilage
  2. Cuneiform cartilage
  3. Epiglottis
  4. Vestibular ligament
  5. Aryepiglotticus

Answer

The correct answer is D.

Explanation: The vestibular ligament (false vocal cord) is located within the larynx, inferior to the aditus (inlet), and does not form its boundary. The laryngeal aditus is bounded superiorly by the epiglottis, laterally by the aryepiglottic folds (which contain the cuneiform cartilages), and posteriorly by the notch between the corniculate cartilages.

Question 4

  2. Subclavian vein
  3. Phrenic nerve
  4. Transverse cervical artery
  5. Ascending cervical artery

Answer

The correct answer is B.

Explanation: The subclavian vein passes anterior to the scalenus anterior muscle, but it is not directly on its surface; it is separated by some distance. The phrenic nerve, transverse cervical artery, and ascending cervical artery all run directly across the anterior surface of the muscle, deep to the prevertebral fascia which covers it.

Question 6

Question 7

Question 10

  1. Internal carotid

Question 11

The upper group of deep cervical lymph nodes receives lymph directly from ALL BUT ONE of the following:

  1. nasopharynx
  2. scalp
  3. supratentorial part of the brain
  4. eyelids
  5. tongue

Answer

The correct answer is C.

Explanation: The brain does not have a conventional lymphatic drainage system. Cerebrospinal fluid drains into the dural venous sinuses. The other structures listed (nasopharynx, scalp, eyelids, tongue) all have lymphatic drainage pathways that lead to the deep cervical lymph nodes.

Question 12

Which of the following statements is INCORRECT? The tentorium cerebelli:

  1. contains branches of the ophthalmic nerve
  2. is related to the lateral sinus
  3. lies close to the posterior inferior cerebellar artery
  4. contains part of the trochlear nerve
  5. is close to part of the posterior cerebral artery

Answer

The correct answer is C.

Explanation: The posterior inferior cerebellar artery (PICA) is typically located inferior to the tentorium cerebelli, in the posterior cranial fossa with the cerebellum. The tentorium cerebelli separates the cerebellum from the cerebrum, and is more closely related to the superior cerebellar and posterior cerebral arteries. Branches of the ophthalmic nerve (tentorial branches), the lateral sinus, and the trochlear nerve are all correctly associated with it.

Question 13

Question 14

Which of the following statements is INCORRECT? Flat bones of the skull vault:

  1. contain red bone marrow
  2. have a deep surface line by the endosteal layer of dura
  3. develop by endochondral ossification
  4. have diploic veins which are devoid of valves
  5. have small apertures for emissary veins in which blood normally flows in a direction away from the brain

Answer

The correct answer is C.

Explanation: The flat bones of the skull vault develop by intramembranous ossification, not endochondral ossification. Endochondral ossification is the process where bone replaces a cartilage model, which occurs in long bones and the base of the skull.

Question 15

  4. Is grooved by the middle meningeal vessels

Question 19

The cavernous sinus communicates directly with ALL BUT ONE of the following:

  1. Sphenoparietal sinus
  2. Veins in the orbit
  3. Sigmoid sinus
  4. Inferior petrosal sinus
  5. Pterygoid venous plexus

Answer

The correct answer is C.

Explanation: The cavernous sinus drains posteriorly into the superior and inferior petrosal sinuses, which in turn drain into the sigmoid sinus. Question 33

  1. Bifid spinous processes
  2. No vertebrarterial foremen
  3. No body
  4. No transverse process
  5. No posterior tubercle

BECAUSE

(R) the abducent nerve supplies lateral rectus.

The correct answer is D.

Question 39

(S) Damaging the ansa subclavia may give slight miosis

BECAUSE

(R) dilator pupillae is supplied by sympathetic fibres running from the superior cervical ganglion.

Answer

The correct answer is B.

Explanation: Statement (S) is correct. The ansa subclavia is a nerve cord that connects the middle and inferior cervical sympathetic ganglia. Damage can interrupt sympathetic pathways, potentially causing slight miosis (pupil constriction) as part of Horner's syndrome. Reason (R) is also correct. The dilator pupillae muscle is indeed supplied by postganglionic sympathetic fibers that originate in the superior cervical ganglion. However, (R) is not a direct explanation for (S). The fibers supplying the dilator pupillae ascend from the superior ganglion, while the ansa subclavia is located lower down. Both are related to the cervical sympathetic chain, but one doesn't directly explain the other's specific function.

The occipitofrontalis muscle is supplied by the temporal and posterior auricular branches of the facial nerve (CN VII), not the greater occipital nerve (which is sensory).

Question 45

(S) Corneal ulceration may accompany herpes zoster lesions on the tip of the nose

BECAUSE

(R) afferent fibres from both the cornea and and the tip of the nose run in the maxillary nerve.

Answer

The correct answer is C.

Explanation: Statement (S) is correct. This is known as Hutchinson's sign. The nasociliary nerve (a branch of the ophthalmic nerve) supplies both the tip of the nose and the cornea. A lesion on the nose tip indicates the virus is active in this nerve, and thus the cornea is also at high risk. Reason (R) is incorrect. The afferent fibers from the cornea and the tip of the nose run in the ophthalmic division (CN V1) of the trigeminal nerve, not the maxillary division (CN V2).

Question 46

BECAUSE

The vagus nerve (CN X) has an auricular branch (Arnold's nerve) that supplies sensory innervation to part of the external auditory meatus. This sensory input can trigger the vagal reflex, which includes parasympathetic responses like coughing or vomiting. Therefore, (R) is a valid explanation for (S).

Question 47

BECAUSE

(R) fibres of the XIth cranial nerve supply the intrinsic muscles of the larynx.

The intrinsic muscles of the larynx are supplied by fibers that originate in the cranial root of the accessory nerve but travel with the vagus nerve (via the recurrent laryngeal nerve). So, while fibers from CN XI are involved, the premise in S is wrong.

Question 120

During the stage of swallowing the:

  3. Thyroid cartilage reaches its highest level
  4. Laryngeal aditus is pressed against the posterior pharyngeal wall
