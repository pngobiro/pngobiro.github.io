Anatomy and Physiology

Duration: 50 hours Level: Beginner

Course Overview

This course provides a comprehensive introduction to the structure and function of the human body. Topics covered include anatomical terminology, basic chemistry, cell biology, and an overview of major body systems.

  • Describe the characteristics of living organisms.
  • List and describe the levels of structural organization of the human body.
  • Identify and use anatomical terms correctly.
  • Describe body cavities and serous membranes.
  • Explain the concept of homeostasis and provide examples of feedback mechanisms.
  • Describe basic chemical concepts including atoms, molecules, and chemical reactions.
  • Describe the structure and functions of cells.

  • Describe the basic divisions and functions of the nervous system.
  • Explain the electrophysiology of neurons.

Course Modules

  1. Module 1: Introduction to Human Anatomy and Physiology

    Introduction to the science of anatomy and physiology, characteristics of living organisms, and levels of structural organization.

    4 hours

  2. Module 2: Anatomical Terminology and Organization

    Explores the different types of anatomy and physiology, anatomical position, directional terms, regional terms, and body cavities.

    4 hours

  3. Module 3: Homeostasis and Basic Chemistry

    Discusses homeostasis, feedback loops, gradients, and the basic chemistry of life.

    4 hours

  4. Module 4: Chemical Principles and Reactions

    Covers isotopes, radioactivity, mixtures, chemical bonds, and different types of chemical reactions.

    4 hours

  5. Module 5: Biochemistry and Cell Biology

    Explores lipids, the structure of the cell, and the mechanisms of transport across the cell membrane.

    8 hours

  6. Module 6: Electrophysiology and Nervous System

    Introduces electrophysiology, the divisions and functions of the nervous system, and the electrophysiology of neurons.

    10 hours

Prerequisites

  • High school biology is recommended.
