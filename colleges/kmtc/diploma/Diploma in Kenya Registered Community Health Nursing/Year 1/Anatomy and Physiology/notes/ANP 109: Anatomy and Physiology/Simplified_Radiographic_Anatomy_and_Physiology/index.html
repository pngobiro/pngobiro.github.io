Simplified Radiographic Anatomy and Physiology - PART 1

Duration: 25 hours Level: Intermediate

Course Overview

This course provides a simplified yet comprehensive overview of radiographic anatomy and physiology, designed for medical imaging students, radiographers, and general practitioners. It covers the essential anatomical structures and their relationships within the human body, focusing on the basic physiological mechanisms and their radiological appearances.

Course Modules

  1. Module 1: General Principles and Anatomical Terminology

    Introduction to the basic concepts of anatomy and physiology, including medical imaging principles and anatomical terminology.

    6 hours

  2. Module 2: Body Tissues, Skeletal System, Skull, Vertebral Column and Thoracic Cage

    Detailed study of body tissues, the skeletal system, the skull, the vertebral column and the thoracic cage.

    9 hours

  3. Module 3: The Shoulder Girdle, Upper Limb Bones, Upper Limb Joints, The Pelvic Girdle, Lower Limb Bones and Lower Limb Joints

    Comprehensive overview of the shoulder girdle, upper limb bones, upper limb joints, the pelvic girdle, lower limb bones and lower limb joints

    10 hours

Prerequisites

  • Basic understanding of human biology.
