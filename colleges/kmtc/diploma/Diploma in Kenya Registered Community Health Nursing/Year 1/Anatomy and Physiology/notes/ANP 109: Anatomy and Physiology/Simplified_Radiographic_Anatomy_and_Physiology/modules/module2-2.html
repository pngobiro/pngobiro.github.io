Divisions of skeletal System

The axial skeleton:

The axial skeleton forms the central axis of the body. It includes the bones of the following parts (Figure 4.2):

  1. The Skull bones.
  2. The Ossicles of the middle ear.
  3. The Hyoid bone.
  4. The Thoracic cage.
  5. The Sternum.
  6. The Vertebral Column.

Functions of the Axial Skeleton: it has two main functions:

  1. It supports and protects soft tissue organs, such as the brain and the lung.
  2. It creates a surface for the attachment of muscles for movement.

The appendicular skeleton:

The appendicular skeleton forms the peripheral part of the skeletal system. It includes the bones of the following parts (Figure 4.3):

  1. The upper limb bones.
  2. The shoulder girdle that attaches the upper limb to the axial skeleton.
  3. The lower limb bones.
  4. The pelvic girdle that attaches the lower limb to the axial skeleton.

Function of the Appendicular Skeleton: The main function of the appendicular skeleton is locomotion beside grasping and manipulation of objects.

üìù Summary

  • The axial skeleton forms the central axis of the body and includes the skull, ossicles of the middle ear, hyoid bone, thoracic cage, sternum, and vertebral column. Its primary functions are to support and protect organs and provide attachment points for muscles.
  • The appendicular skeleton includes the bones of the upper and lower limbs, as well as the shoulder and pelvic girdles that attach them to the axial skeleton. Its main function is to enable movement and interaction with the environment.

üí° Pro Tip

Remember the axial skeleton as the "core" of the body, while the appendicular skeleton consists of the "appendages" or limbs and their connecting girdles.

The Axial Skeleton

The Axial Skeleton consists of 80 bones:

1. The Skull; formed of 22 bones (Figure 4.4):

  • One Frontal bone
  • Two Parietal bones
  • Two Temporal bones
  • One Occipital bone
  • One Ethmoid bone
  • One Sphenoid bone
  • One Mandible
  • Two Maxillae (singular: maxilla)
  • One Vomer
  • Two Palatine bones
  • Two Nasal bones
  • Two Zygomatic bones
  • Two Nasal conchae (singular: concha)
  • Two Lacrimal bones

2. One Hyoid bone.

3. The middle ear contains 6 ossicles (3 on each side) (Figure 4.5):

  • Malleus
  • Incus
  • Stapes

4. The vertebral column consists of 26 bones; 24 vertebrae, 1 sacrum and 1 coccyx (Figure 4.6).

5. The thoracic cage consists of 25 bones; 12 pairs of ribs, and one sternum (Figure 4.7).

üìù Summary

  • The axial skeleton is composed of 80 bones, including the skull (22 bones), hyoid bone (1), auditory ossicles (6), vertebral column (26), and thoracic cage (25).
  • The skull consists of cranial bones (protecting the brain) and facial bones (forming the face's structure).
  • The vertebral column is made up of individual vertebrae, sacrum, and coccyx, providing support and flexibility.
  • The thoracic cage, formed by ribs and sternum, protects thoracic organs and aids in breathing.

üí° Pro Tip

When learning the bones of the axial skeleton, start with the major structures (skull, vertebral column, thoracic cage) and then learn the individual bones within each structure. Using different colors for each structure can help in memorization.

üìö Key Takeaways

  • The skeletal system is divided into the axial and appendicular skeletons, each with distinct functions.
  • The axial skeleton forms the central axis of the body and includes the skull, hyoid bone, vertebral column, and thoracic cage.
