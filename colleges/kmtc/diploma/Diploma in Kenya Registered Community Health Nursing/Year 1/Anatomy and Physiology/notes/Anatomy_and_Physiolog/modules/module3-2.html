Module 3: Chemistry of Life, Atoms, Atomic Structure

üéØ Learning Objectives

  • Define matter and list its three states.
  • Define atom and describe the structure of an atom.
  • Explain the concept of electron shells.
  • Define atomic number and element.
  • List the four major elements found in the human body.

üìö Chemistry of Life

Matter: Anything that has mass and occupies space. Matter can exist as a solid, liquid, or gas. Chemistry is the study of matter and how it interacts.

üìù Summary

  • Matter is anything that has mass and occupies space.
  • Matter exists in three states: solid, liquid, and gas.
  • Chemistry is the study of matter and its interactions.

üìö Atoms and Atomic Structure

Atom: The smallest unit of matter that retains the original properties. Atoms are made up of even smaller structures called subatomic particles.

Subatomic particles exist in three basic forms:

  • Protons (p+): Found in the central core of the atom (the atomic nucleus). They have a positive charge.
  • Neutrons (n0): Found in the atomic nucleus. They are slightly larger than protons and have no charge (neutral).
  • Electrons (e-): Found outside the nucleus. They have a negative charge.

All atoms are electrically neutral, meaning they have no overall charge. The number of protons is equal to the number of electrons (p+ = e-).

üìù Summary

  • Atoms are the smallest unit of matter.
  • Atoms are made of protons, neutrons, and electrons.
  • Protons are positive, neutrons are neutral, and electrons are negative.
  • Atoms are electrically neutral (protons = electrons).

üìö Electron Shells and Atomic Number

Electron shells: Regions surrounding the atomic nucleus where electrons are likely to be found. Each shell can hold a certain number of electrons:

  • 1st shell: Holds 2 electrons
  • 2nd shell: Holds 8 electrons
  • 3rd shell: Holds up to 18 electrons, but is satisfied with 8

The atomic number is the number of protons in an atom. It defines the element.

An element is a substance that cannot be broken down into a simpler substance by chemical means.

Hydrogen, oxygen, carbon, and nitrogen are the four major elements that make up the human body. The body also contains 7 mineral elements and 13 trace elements.

üìù Summary

  • Electrons are found in shells around the nucleus.
  • The first shell holds 2 electrons, the second holds 8, and the third is satisfied with 8.
  • The atomic number is the number of protons and defines the element.
  • An element is a substance that cannot be broken down further by chemical means.
  • Hydrogen, oxygen, carbon, and nitrogen are the major elements in the human body.
