üìö Electrophysiology

Cell membranes are more leaky to K+ than to other ions. When K+ diffuses out of the cell (through leak channels), a negative charge develops on the inside of the membrane because the anions that were balancing the positive charge of K+ are too large to leave. This creates a thin layer of positive charges on the outside of the membrane and a thin layer of negative charges on the inside of the membrane.

This separation of charges creates an electrical gradient that provides energy to do work. This is called the membrane potential. The resting membrane potential is the value of the membrane potential when the cell is at rest (not stimulated), which is about -70mV.

üìù Summary

  • Electrophysiology is the study of electrical changes across cell membranes.
  • The membrane potential is the electrical gradient across the membrane.
  • The resting membrane potential is about -70mV.

üìö The Nervous System

The nervous system controls our perception and experience of the world. It directs voluntary movement and is the seat of consciousness, personality, learning, and memory. It also regulates many aspects of homeostasis along with the endocrine system, including respiratory rate, blood pressure, body temperature, the sleep/wake cycle, and blood pH.

üìù Summary

  • The nervous system controls perception, movement, consciousness, and homeostasis.

üìö Divisions of the Nervous System

Nervous system functions fall into three categories: sensory, integrative, and motor.

Sensory functions: The peripheral nervous system (PNS) gathers information about the internal and external environments of the body. It includes all nerves outside of the brain and spinal cord (12 cranial nerves and 31 spinal nerves). Sensory input is carried from receptors to the spinal cord and brain.

üìù Summary

  • The nervous system has sensory, integrative, and motor functions.
  • The peripheral nervous system (PNS) gathers sensory information.
