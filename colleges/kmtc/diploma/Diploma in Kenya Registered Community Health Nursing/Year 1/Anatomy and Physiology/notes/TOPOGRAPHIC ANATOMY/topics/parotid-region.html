üëÇ The Parotid Region

  • ‚Ä¢ The region on the lateral surface of the face that comprises the parotid gland & the structures immediately
    related to it

üíß Parotid Gland

  • ‚Ä¢ Largest of the salivary glands
  • ‚Ä¢ Located subcutaneously, below and in front of the external auditory meatus
  • ‚Ä¢ Occupies the deep hollow behind the ramus of the mandible
  • ‚Ä¢ Wedge-shaped when viewed externally, with the base above & the apex behind the angle of the mandible
  • ‚Ä¢ Wedge-shaped in horizontal section with the base in the lateral position and apex against the pharyngeal wall.
  • ‚Ä¢ It exhibits 3 surfaces:
  • - Lateral
  • - Anteromedial
  • - Posteromedial

üíß Lobes

  • ‚Ä¢ The facial nerve courses horizontally through the gland and divides it into:
  • - Superficial lobe
  • - Deep lobe

Facial nerve

üíß Processes

üíß The gland is an irregular lobulated mass, sends 'processes' in various directions. These include:

  • ‚Ä¢ Glenoid process, that extends upward behind the temporomandibular joint, in front of external auditory meatus
  • ‚Ä¢ Facial process, that extends anteriorly onto the masseter muscle
  • ‚Ä¢ Accessory process (part), small part of facial process lying along the parotid duct
  • ‚Ä¢ Pterygoid process, that extends forward from the deeper part, lies between the medial pterygoid muscle & the ramus of mandible
  • ‚Ä¢ Carotid process, that lies posterior to the external carotid artery

üõ°Ô∏è Capsules

  • ‚Ä¢ The parotid gland is enclosed in two capsules:
  • - An inner connective tissue capsule
  • - An outer dense fibrous capsule derived from the investing layer of the deep cervical fascia
  • ‚Ä¢ The deep cervical fascia extends upward, reaches the inferior border of parotid gland, splits into the superficial & the deep layer, to enclose the gland
  • ‚Ä¢ Above the gland, the:
  • - Superficial layer gets attached to the zygomatic arch
  • - Deep layer gets attached to the tympanic plate of temporal bone

A portion of fascia extending from the styloid process to the angle of mandible is called stylomandibular ligament. It separates the parotid gland from the submandibular gland

üíß Structures Coursing Within the Parotid Gland

üíß Parotid (Stensen's) Duct

  • ‚Ä¢ About 2 inches long
  • ‚Ä¢ Emerges from the facial process of the gland
  • ‚Ä¢ Passes forward over the lateral surface of the masseter muscle
  • ‚Ä¢ about a fingerbreadth below the zygomatic arch
  • ‚Ä¢ accompanied by the:
  • - transverse facial vessels & upper zygomatic branches of facial nerve above
  • - lower zygomatic branches of facial nerve below
  • ‚Ä¢ Turns around the anterior border of masseter muscle
  • ‚Ä¢ Pierces the:
  • - Buccal pad of fat
  • - Buccopharyngeal fascia
  • - Buccinator muscle &
  • - Buccal mucosa
  • ‚Ä¢ Opens into the vestibule of mouth on a small papilla, opposite the second upper molar tooth
  • ‚Ä¢ The oblique passage of the duct in the buccinator muscle acts as a valve-like mechanism & prevents inflation of the duct during blowing
  • ‚Ä¢ The duct can be rolled over the clenched masseter muscle
  • ‚Ä¢ The duct is represented by the middle $1 / 3$ of a line extending from the tragus of the auricle to a point midway between the ala of nose & upper lip

üè• Clinical Anatomy

  • ‚Ä¢ Parotid duct being a superficial structure, is prone to get damaged in injuries, or during surgical procedures on the face
  • ‚Ä¢ Parotid neoplasms (malignant) are very invasive and quickly involve the facial nerve causing facial palsy
  • ‚Ä¢ Inflammation of parotid gland results in painful swelling because of a tight capsule enclosing the gland. The swollen glenoid process exaggerates this pain on chewing
  • ‚Ä¢ Frey's syndrome: a disorder characterized by recurrent episodes of localized facial flushing and/or sweating in the area over the parotid gland in response to gustatory stimuli
  • - This is due to aberrant nerve regeneration after injury (a communication develops between the auriculo-temporal & greater auricular nerves such that parasympathetic fibers migrate into the cutaneous sympathetic nerves that supply the sweat glands)
