üß¥ Layer 1 - Skin

  • ‚Ä¢ It is thick, hairy with numerous sebaceous and sweat glands.
  • ‚Ä¢ Obstruction of the ducts of the sebaceous glands by secretions form Sebaceous cysts.

üß± Layer 2 - Connective tissue

  • ‚Ä¢ It is a fibro-fatty layer which is adherent to the skin and to the underlying aponeurosis by fibrous septa.
  • ‚Ä¢ It is richly supplied with vessels and nerves that run within it. The arteries freely anastomose with each other. Alternative/collateral blood supply.
  • ‚Ä¢ Wounds of the scalp bleed profusely because of:
  • 1. The abundant arterial anastomoses.
  • 2. Arteries do not retract when lacerated because they are held open by the dense connective tissue.

  • ‚Ä¢ Galea aponeurotica
  • ‚Ä¢ Deep scalp wounds gape widely when the epicranial aponeurosis is divided due to tension of the aponeurosis produced by the tone of the occipito-frontalis muscle.
