Student-Centred Learning (SCL)

üìñ Definition of SCL

Student-centred learning is learning that focuses on what the student does to achieve the learning rather than what the teacher does. (Haden & Crosby)

It involves:

  • Remaining active rather than passive learning.
  • Emphasizing deep learning and understanding.
  • Increased responsibility and accountability on the student.
  • Increased sense of autonomy.
  • Interaction between teacher and learner.
  • Mutual respect with the learners/educators.

üß† A Reflective Approach to Teaching and Learning (Leib et al.)

Orientation on Teaching

Teacher-Centered: This is a content-orientation concept where the learner is a recipient of information.

Student-Centered Learning (SCL) Orientation Concept: Knowledge is constructed by the students.

In the past, the teacher used to direct the process; the students were quietly receiving what was being given. A teacher was expected to transmit knowledge from the expert to the student. In contrast, with SCL, the focus is on what the student needs to achieve their learning. (Kimber)

Students should choose their area of learning and how that topic might interest them.

The SCL was identified as a concept for learning (person-to-person interaction) where the student feels sufficient security within himself, in relation to others, that they experience an essential truth in the capacity of others to think and learn for themselves. (Bernard Rogers)

Gibbs emphasized learner's activity rather than passive learning. The key decision about learning is made by students through negotiation with the teacher.
