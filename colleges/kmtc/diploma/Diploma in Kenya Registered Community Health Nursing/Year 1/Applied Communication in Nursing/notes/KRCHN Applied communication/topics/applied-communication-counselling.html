üìù Introduction

Counselling is one of the most important skills of a nurse. In fact, a nurse always does counselling without necessarily being aware of the process. E.g., patients, clients, relatives, and even colleagues. In so doing, the nurse offers information, support, and hope to these people.

üí° Note

According to studies, the type of therapy used in counselling is not as important to outcomes as are specific counsellor behaviors, such as:

  • Empathy
  • Confidence
  • Respect for patients' ability to change

Definition: Counselling is the act of working with patients/clients to help them clarify personal goals and find ways of overcoming their problems, with the aim of altering individual beliefs and behaviors that are interfering with attainment of basic needs.

üéØ Goals of Counselling

  1. 1.Listening to the client. This is termed as the main goal.
  2. 2.Identify the needs of the client. E.g., patients need counselling for their child's behavior problems, i.e., the core matter.
  3. 3.To make the client understand his/her feelings properly and help him/her to be aware of his/her own emotions and encourage him/her to be independent.
  4. 4.Main problem-focused so that the sub-problems should be identified by the client and he/she helps the client focus on the main problem.
  5. 5.Make the client to accept him/herself with his/her problem and help him/her to admit to it and to overcome it, i.e., despite the problem, he/she is a person, a human being.
  6. 6.To focus on his/her strengths by studying the case and provide positive attitude in him/her and ultimately help him/her to reduce his/her negativity.

  • To help them understand the problems they are experiencing, find answers, and coping mechanisms.
  • Give them information they are lacking to solve problems, i.e., add to what they know.
  • Help counselling, psychotherapy, physical, occupational, medical.
  • Help them express the problems and clarify their connecting issues.
  • Help them to admit to the problem or find other ways of coping with the problem. E.g., we are to learn to live with stressors in life, but can develop coping mechanisms, because we will not face all other stressors.

üåü Principles of Counselling

  1. 1.Principle of Acceptance: Accept the patient with his/her physical, psychological, social, economical, and cultural conditions.
  2. 2.Principle of Communication: Communication should be verbal as well as non-verbal and should be skillful.
  3. 3.Principle of Empathy: Instead of showing sympathy, put yourself in the patient's shoes and understand reflections accordingly. Empathy is the ability to identify with a person, i.e., understands the other person's world.
  4. 4.Principle of Non-Judgmental Attitude: Do not criticize or comment negatively regarding patient's complaints, i.e., prejudice, bias.
  5. 5.Principle of Confidentiality: Always keep the patient's name and the problem strictly secret and assure the patient about the same.
  6. 6.Principle of Individuality: Treat each and every patient as unique and respect his/her problems as well. E.g., those who know how to perceive others do not have comparison.
  7. 7.Principle of Non-Emotional Involvement: Not getting emotionally involved with the patient and avoid getting carried away with his/her feelings.

üìö Counselling Theories and Approaches

During their initial training, counsellors are typically introduced to a variety of different preferred counselling models. Following each of the six main theoretical categories, the counsellor's task is to determine through continued training and experience which model best suits the following:

  1. a)Their personal view of human nature and knowledge.
  2. b)Their preferred style of communication.
  3. c)The needs of the client population they are currently working with, and/or the client population they plan to work with in the future.

Most counsellors will find that some therapy models are a particularly good fit, while others may not be a good fit at all. Consequently, they are most likely to apply those models in counselling practice that fall within their comfort zone and avoid those that do not.

When confronted with client situations that fall outside their zone of comfort and/or competency, counsellors must decide between:

  1. a)Working to expand their comfort/competency zone to include an alternative model more appropriate to the client's needs, OR
  2. b)Referring the client to another counsellor who is more comfortable and competent in the needed alternative model.

A counsellor must always determine what action is needed to effectively meet the counselling needs of each client.
