Critical Thinking

üéØ Introduction

Critical thinking refers to the ability to analyse information objectively and make a reasoned judgement. It involves the evaluation of sources, such as data, facts, observable phenomena and research findings.

It is the ability to think in an organized manner in order to understand connections between ideas and facts.

  • ‚Ä¢ Critical thinking is the process that allows nurses to see the big picture (envision the overall perspective) instead of focusing only on details.
  • ‚Ä¢ Every day, nurses make decisions that are derived through critical thinking.
  • ‚Ä¢ Making clinical judgments relies on critical thinking.

üí° Skills Needed in Critical Thinking

  • ‚Ä¢ Interpretation - understanding the meaning of information or events.
  • ‚Ä¢ Analysis - ability to carefully examine something, whether it is a problem or a set of data, understand what it means and properly explain to others the implication of that information based on objective and subjective data.
  • ‚Ä¢ Evaluation - assessing the value of information and its credibility.
  • ‚Ä¢ Explanation - translating complicated and often complex medical information to patients and families in a way they can make decision about patient care.
  • ‚Ä¢ Inference - reach a conclusion or opinion.
  • ‚Ä¢ Self-regulation - avoiding the impact of unconscious bias with cognitive awareness.

üîç Essential Elements of Critical Thinking

  • ‚Ä¢ Keep an open mind
  • ‚Ä¢ Use rationale to support opinions or decisions
  • ‚Ä¢ Reflect on thoughts before reaching a conclusion
  • ‚Ä¢ Use past clinical experience to build knowledge
  • ‚Ä¢ Gather complete information
  • ‚Ä¢ Understand and define all terms
  • ‚Ä¢ Question the methods by which the facts are derived
  • ‚Ä¢ Question the conclusion
  • ‚Ä¢ Look for hidden assumptions and biases
  • ‚Ä¢ Question the source of facts
  • ‚Ä¢ Examine the big picture

üîß Components of Critical Thinking

Critical thinking is composed of three primary components:

Mental Operations

  • ‚Ä¢ Include activities such as decision making and reasoning that are used to find or create meaning.
  • ‚Ä¢ Nurses engage in such activities when they search for solutions based on sound rationale and develop outcomes accordingly.
  • ‚Ä¢ The result of these mental operations is creative and appropriate problem solving.

Knowledge

  • ‚Ä¢ Critical thinking calls for a knowledge base that includes declarative knowledge (specific facts or information) and operative knowledge (an understanding of the nature of that knowledge).
  • ‚Ä¢ Nursing education assists the student in learning specific facts about nursing and the delivery of quality care.
  • ‚Ä¢ Students are taught how to examine the beliefs underlying the facts in order to analyze and interpret those facts.

Attitude

  • ‚Ä¢ Certain attitudes enhance a person's ability to think critically.
  • ‚Ä¢ One of the most important attitudes needed by a critical thinker is a sense of curiosity that allows the person to question assumptions upon which decisions are based.
  • ‚Ä¢ Analysis of basic assumptions allows the learner to plan and act in a rational manner rather than out of habit or routine.

üìà Development of Critical Thinking

  • ‚Ä¢ Identify goals
  • ‚Ä¢ Determine what knowledge is required
  • ‚Ä¢ Assess the margin for error
  • ‚Ä¢ Determine the amount of time available for decision making
  • ‚Ä¢ Identify available resources

üö∂ Steps of Critical Thinking

  1. 1. Identify the problem or question
    Be precise as possible, the narrower the issue, the easier it is to find solutions or answers.
  2. 2. Gather data, opinions and arguments
    Try to find several sources that present different ideas and point of view.
  3. 3. Analyse and evaluate the data
    Are the sources reliable? Is there enough information to support given hypothesis?
  4. 4. Identify assumptions
    Are you sure the sources you found are unbiased? Are you sure you weren't biased in your search for answers?
  5. 5. Establish significance
    What piece of information is most important? Are all opinions and arguments even relevant to the problem you're trying to solve?
  6. 6. Make a decision/reach a conclusion
    Identify various conclusions that are possible and decide which of them are sufficiently supported. Weigh strengths and limitations of all possible options.
  7. 7. Present or communicate
    Once you have reached a conclusion, present it to all stakeholders.

üíâ Examples of Critical Thinking in Nursing

1. Prioritizing

Nurses decide which patient to see first based on the information gathered, and from there they must prioritize their actions when in a patient's room. A critical thinking in prioritization creates smooth workflow management to set priorities and help nurses manage time.

2. Patient Assessment

Doing a thorough assessment on patient can help detect that something is wrong. When change is noticed, the nurse uses critical thinking skills to decide what is the next step. Critical thinking allows the nurse to provide the best and safest care possible.
