Poisoning and Anaphylaxis

Management of Poisoning

Assessment

Ask for any containers found near the casualty.

Decontamination Methods

The primary goal is to remove or neutralize the poison. Common methods include:

  • Dilution
  • Gastric Lavage
  • Activated Charcoal
  • Hemodialysis
  • Antidotes

Detailed Decontamination Methods

Dilution

Milk or water is taken to dilute the poison if it is alkaline or acidic.

Warning

Dilution is not attempted if the patient has evidence of esophageal, gastric, or intestinal burn or perforation.

Gastric Lavage

A flexible tube is inserted via the oral route into the stomach. Stomach contents are then suctioned via the tube. Recommended for up to 1 hour in Salicylates.

Note on Emesis

Inducing vomiting (emesis) is not recommended due to the high risk of aspiration.

Activated Charcoal

  • Adsorbs toxic substances, thus inhibiting GI absorption.
  • Addition of sorbitol provides a laxative effect.
  • Dose: Oral: 25-100g as a single dose.
  • Repetitive doses are useful to enhance the elimination of certain drugs (e.g., theophylline, phenobarbital, carbamazepine, aspirin, sustained-release products).
  • Not effective for: cyanide, mineral acids, caustic alkalis, organic solvents, iron, ethanol, methanol poisoning, lithium.

Hemodialysis

Reserved for severe poisoning. Removes toxins from the blood. Useful for substances with low volume of distribution.

Antidotes

Look up the specific poison and their antidotes. Throughout detoxification, the patient's vital signs, CVP, and fluid and electrolyte balance are monitored closely. Hypotension and cardiac dysrhythmias are possible.

Anaphylactic Shock

Definition

A severe, life-threatening allergic reaction. The most common anaphylactic reactions are to foods, insect stings, medications, and latex.

Signs/Symptoms

  • Tightness in chest or throat
  • Wheezing (whistling sound from airway)
  • Urticaria (hives)
  • Tachycardia (fast heart rate) as the body is compensating
  • Anxiety - a sense of impending doom
  • Itchy, watery eyes

Treatment

  1. Remove the toxin (determine method of entry: inhalation, sting).
  2. Administer Epinephrine (EpiPen) if necessary. This constricts blood vessels, raises BP and pulse, limits the allergic reaction, and makes breathing easier.
  3. Administer Oxygen.
  4. Monitor vitals.
  5. Monitor for shock.

Alcohol Intoxication

Manifestations

Drowsiness, incoordination, slurring of speech, sudden mood changes, aggression, belligerence, grandiosity, and uninhibited behavior.

Management

The patient should be approached using a firm, consistent, accepting, and reasonable attitude. Determine sugar levels and give dextrose infusion if needed. Ensure ABCs are okay and do a thorough physical exam.
