Trauma and Emergency

By Kasiti, S.M.

Objectives

  • Initiate the management of patients with trauma, and other emergencies.
  • Participate in the management of patients with trauma, and other emergencies.

Types of Emergencies: Trauma

Common types of trauma emergencies include:

  • Head Injury
  • Severe Haemorrhage
  • Burns
  • Poisoning

Management of Trauma

The main aim of trauma care is to prevent early trauma mortality.

Early trauma deaths occur because of failure of oxygenation of vital organs or central nervous system injury, or both.

Aims of the Initial Evaluation of Trauma Patients

  • Stabilize the patient.
  • Identify life-threatening conditions in order to risk and initiate supportive treatment.
  • Organize definitive treatments or organize transfer for definitive treatments.

Phases of Trauma Care

Pre-Hospital Phase

During the pre-hospital phase, the focus is on immediate life-saving interventions.

  • Airway maintenance
  • Control of external bleeding and shock
  • Keep patient immobile
  • Information gathering about the injury

Hospital Phase

Once the patient arrives at the hospital, the following steps are prioritized:

  • Assessment of injuries
  • Establishing IV access
  • Methods of summoning extra medical help
  • Transfer arrangements with a trauma center
