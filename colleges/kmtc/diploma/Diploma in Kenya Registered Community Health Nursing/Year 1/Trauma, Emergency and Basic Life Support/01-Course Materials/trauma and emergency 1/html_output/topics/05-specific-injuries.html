Specific Injuries

Snake Bite

Management

  • Have the victim lie down.
  • Remove constrictive items such as rings.
  • Provide warmth.
  • Cleanse the wound.
  • Cover the wound with a light sterile dressing.
  • Immobilize the injured body part below the level of the heart.

Important Contraindications

Corticosteroids are contraindicated in the first 6 to 8 hours after the bite because they may depress antibody production and hinder the action of antivenin.

The patient is observed for some time then discharged.

Wounds (Soft Tissue Injuries)

Soft tissue injuries can range from minor tears to severe crush injuries.

Management

  1. The wound is irrigated gently and copiously with sterile isotonic saline solution to remove surface dirt.
  2. A non-adherent dressing is applied to protect the wound.
  3. Tetanus prophylaxis is administered as prescribed, based on the condition of the wound and the patient's immunization status.

Note on Antiseptics

Antibacterial agents, such as povidone-iodine (Betadine) or hydrogen peroxide, should not be allowed to get deep into the wound without thorough rinsing. These agents are used only for the initial cleansing because they can irritate exposed and healthy tissue, resulting in further cell injury.

Fractures

A partial or complete break of a bone.

Clinical Manifestations

  • Pain at the site of injury
  • Swelling (from blood, lymph, and exudate infiltrating the tissue)
  • Ecchymosis
  • Tenderness
  • Crepitation
  • Numbness and tingling
  • Deformity

Management

  1. If it is an open fracture, cover the wound with a sterile dressing and secure it with a bandage.
  2. Apply pressure around the wound to control any bleeding.
  3. Immobilize the injured part.
  4. Provide pain management.
