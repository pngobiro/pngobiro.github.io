Head Injury and Haemorrhage

Haemorrhage

Management of severe bleeding involves the following steps:

  • Assess for signs of shock and check neck veins.
  • Identify the source of bleeding and apply direct pressure.
  • Establish large-bore IV cannula access.
  • Take blood for group and cross match.
  • Give intravenous fluids.
  • Monitor volume replacement.

Head Injury

Definition

Any trauma to the scalp, skull, or brain. Head trauma includes an alteration in consciousness, no matter how brief.

Causes

  • Motor vehicle accidents
  • Falls
  • Assaults
  • Recreational accidents

Nursing Assessment

Key areas to assess in a patient with a head injury:

  • GCS score
  • Neurologic status
  • Presence of CSF leak
  • Pupillary response

Overall Goals

  • Maintain adequate cerebral perfusion.
  • Remain normothermic.
  • Be free from pain, discomfort, and infection.
  • Attain maximal cognitive, motor, and sensory function.
