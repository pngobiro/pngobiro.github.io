üî¨ Pathogenesis of IHD

  • ‚Ä¢The underlying pathogenesis of IHD is ATHEROSCLEROSIS.
  • ‚Ä¢Can affect any artery in the body, tends to occur at sites of altered arterial shear stress such as bifurcation.
  • ‚Ä¢Starts with any abnormal endothelial function
  • ‚Ä¢Inflammatory cells, predominantly monocytes, bind to receptor expressed by endothelial cells
  • ‚Ä¢Migrate to intima
  • ‚Ä¢Take up oxidized low density lipoprotein particles
  • ‚Ä¢Become lipid laden macrophages or foam cells

Disease Progression

  • ‚Ä¢As foam cells die, they release their lipid pool in intimal space with cytokines and growth factor
  • ‚Ä¢In response smooth muscle cells migrate from the media of the arterial wall into the intima
  • ‚Ä¢Lipid core will be covered by smooth muscle cells matrix
  • ‚Ä¢Form stable atherosclerotic plaque that will remain asymptomatic until it becomes large enough to obstruct arterial flow.

üìã Clinical Features and Complications

ANGINA

  • ‚Ä¢A type of chest pain, not a disease but a symptom of an underlying heart problem especially IHD
  • ‚Ä¢Described as heavy, tight or gripping.
  • ‚Ä¢ Types of Angina:
    • -Stable angina: pain that occurs on exertion when blood supply to myocardium is inadequate, usually due to severe significant narrowing of coronary arteries. Relieved by rest
    • -Variant (Prinzmetal's) angina: occurs without provocation usually at rest as a result of coronary artery spasm, more frequently in women.
    • -Unstable angina: clinically characterized by progressive symptoms

Myocardial Infarction

  • ‚Ä¢Is defined as death of myocardial tissue caused by ischaemia.
  • ‚Ä¢Popularly known as HEART ATTACK.
  • ‚Ä¢An MI occurs when there is diminished blood supply to heart which leads to myocardial cell damage
  • ‚Ä¢Contractile function stops in the necrotic area of heart

Clinical Features

  • ‚Ä¢Severe crushing substernal chest pain or discomfort that can radiate to the neck, jaw, epigastric or left arm
  • ‚Ä¢In contrast to the pain of Angina pectoris, the pain of MI typically lasts for 20 mins to several hours and is not relieved by rest or nitrate administration.
  • ‚Ä¢Pulse is generally rapid and weak and patients can be diaphoretic.
  • ‚Ä¢Dyspnoea is common and is caused by impaired myocardial contractility and dysfunction
  • ‚Ä¢With massive MI (>40% of left ventricle) cardiogenic shock develops
