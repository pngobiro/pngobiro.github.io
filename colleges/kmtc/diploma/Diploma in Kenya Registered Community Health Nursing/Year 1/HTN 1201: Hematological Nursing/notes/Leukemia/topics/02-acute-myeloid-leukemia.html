Acute Myeloid Leukemia (AML)

Overview

Acute Myeloid Leukemia is a disease of the pluripotent myeloid stem cell. It is characterized by the development/proliferation of immature myeloblasts in the bone marrow.

It can occur at any age but occurs most often at adolescence.

Symptoms result from insufficient production of normal cells.

Clinical Features

Symptoms are due to the replacement of normal marrow with leukemic cells, which cause a drop in the RBCs & platelets. They include:

  • •Fever due to infection (neutropenia)
  • •Weakness & fatigue (anemia)
  • •Bleeding tendencies (thrombocytopenia)
  • •Shortness of breath
  • •Easy bruising and bleeding
  • •Increased risk of infection

Proliferation of leukemic cells within organs leads to a variety of additional symptoms:

  • -Pain from enlarged spleen
  • -Hyperplasia of the gums
  • -Bone pain from expansion of the marrow

Diagnosis

  • • Complete blood count (CBC): shows decrease in both erythrocytes & platelets. The number of normal leukocytes is reduced.
  • • Bone marrow analysis: shows an excess of immature blast cells (> 20%).

Management of Acute Myeloid Leukemia

  • • Chemotherapy: doxorubicin, cytarabine, daunorubicin. Complete remission occurs in 50-70% of treated patients.
  • • Supportive therapy: blood components replacement/transfusion & antibiotic therapy.
  • • Bone Marrow Transplant
