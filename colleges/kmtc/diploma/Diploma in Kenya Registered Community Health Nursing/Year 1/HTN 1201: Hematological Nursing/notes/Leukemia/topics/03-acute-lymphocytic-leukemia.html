Acute Lymphocytic Leukemia (ALL)

Overview

A malignant disorder arising from uncontrolled (excess) proliferation of immature cells derived from the lymphoid stem cell specifically.

In 75% of cases, the cells of origin are B-lymphocytes, while the remaining 25% are made up of T-lymphocytes or precursor stem cells.

ALL most commonly affects children aged 2-4 years.

Diagnosis and Manifestations

Diagnosis is confirmed by bone marrow biopsy or aspiration.

Manifestations include:

  • •Fever
  • •Infection
  • •Anemia
  • •Bleeding
  • •Lymphadenopathy (enlarged lymph nodes)

Management

Management involves chemotherapy. Examples include:

  • -Vincristine
  • -Methotrexate
