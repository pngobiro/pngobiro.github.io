An Introduction to Leukemia

Definition and Characteristics

Leukemia is a malignant disorder of the hematopoietic system involving the bone marrow & lymph nodes.

It is characterized by uncontrolled proliferation of leukocytes (wbc), myelocytes & their precursors. This leaves no room for normal cell production.

The defect originates from the hematopoietic stem cell, the myeloid or the lymphoid stem cell.

Classification and Types

Leukemia is grouped into 4 primary categories according to the dominant cell type affected & the time taken for the signs and symptoms to appear:

  • • Acute leukemia: onset of s/s is abrupt within weeks, leukocyte development is halted in the blast stage, progress is rapid & death occurs within weeks.
  • • Chronic leukemia: s/s onset is slow over a period of months to years, leukocytes produced are mature, progress is slow & may extend over many years.

The main types of leukemia are:

  • -Acute myeloid & Chronic myeloid
  • -Acute lymphocytic & Chronic lymphocytic

Predisposing Factors

The cause is unknown but predisposing factors include:

  1. i. Specific chromosomal aberrations such as down syndrome, fanconi's anemia have an increased incidence of acute anemia.
  2. ii. Chronic exposure to chemicals eg benzene, drugs that cause aplastic anemia & radiation.
  3. iii. Viral pathogenesis eg human T- lymphotropic virus.
