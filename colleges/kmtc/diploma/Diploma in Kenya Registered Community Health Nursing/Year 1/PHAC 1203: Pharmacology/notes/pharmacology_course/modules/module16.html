📚 Weights & Measures in Pharmacology

Accurate drug dosage calculation and administration require a solid understanding of different systems of weights and measures. The primary systems used in pharmacology are:

  1. Metric System: The most commonly used system in medicine and pharmacology due to its simplicity and decimal base.
    • Units of weight: gram (gm or g), milligram (mg), microgram (mcg or µg), kilogram (kg).
    • Units of volume: liter (L), milliliter (ml).
    • Conversions:
      • 1 kg = 1000 grams (gm)
      • 1 gram (gm) = 1000 milligrams (mg)
      • 1 milligram (mg) = 1000 micrograms (mcg)
      • 1 Liter (L) = 1000 Milliliters (ML)
  2. Apothecary System: An older system, less commonly used now but still occasionally seen, especially in older references or prescriptions.
    • Units of volume: minim, fluid dram, fluid ounce, pint, quart, gallon.
    • Units of weight: grain (gr), dram (dr), ounce (oz), pound (lb).
    • Approximations:
      • 1 grain (gr) ≈ 60 milligrams (mg)
      • 1 dram (dr) ≈ 4 grams (g)
      • 1 ounce (oz) ≈ 30 grams (g)
      • 1 pound (lb) = 16 ounces (oz)
  3. Household System: Used primarily for patient instructions at home, but lacks precision and is discouraged for accurate dosing.
    • Units of volume: drop (gt or gtt), teaspoonful (tsp), tablespoonful (tbsp), ounce (oz), cup, pint, quart, gallon.
    • Approximations:
      • 1 drop (gtt) ≈ 0.05 ml (though highly variable)
      • 1 teaspoonful (tsp) ≈ 5 ml
      • 1 tablespoonful (tbsp) ≈ 15 ml
      • 1 fluid ounce (oz) ≈ 30 ml

📚 Conversions and Practical Units

Understanding key conversions is essential for accurate dosage calculations:

  • Common Metric Conversions:
    • 1 kilogram (kg) = 1000 grams (gm)
    • 1 gram (gm) = 1000 milligrams (mg)
    • 1 milligram (mg) = 1000 micrograms (mcg)
    • 1 liter (L) = 1000 milliliters (ml)
  • Approximations for Practical Use:
    • 1 teaspoonful (tsp) is approximately 5 ml
    • 1 tablespoonful (tbsp) is approximately 15 ml
    • 1 ml is approximately 15 drops (gtt)
    • 1 pound (lb.) = 454 gram
    • 1 kg = 2.2 Libra (lb.)
    • 1 gm = 15 grains

✍️ Practice Exercise

Question: Convert the following dosages and volumes:

  1. Convert 0.5 grams to milligrams.
  2. Convert 300 micrograms to milligrams.
  3. Convert 2 tablespoonsful to milliliters.
  4. Convert 5 lbs to kilograms.
View Solution
  1. 0.5 grams to milligrams: 0.5 g * 1000 mg/g = 500 mg
  2. 300 micrograms to milligrams: 300 mcg / 1000 mcg/mg = 0.3 mg
  3. 2 tablespoonsful to milliliters: 2 tbsp * 15 ml/tbsp = 30 ml
  4. 5 lbs to kilograms: 5 lbs / 2.2 lbs/kg = approximately 2.27 kg

📚 Key Takeaways

  • Understanding common medical abbreviations is crucial for interpreting prescriptions and medication orders accurately.
  • The metric system is the standard for drug measurements; apothecary and household systems are less precise but may be encountered.
  • Key metric conversions include kg to g, g to mg, mg to mcg, and L to ml.
  • Accurate dosage calculations and conversions are essential for safe medication administration and effective patient care.
