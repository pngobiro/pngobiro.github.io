üìö Antirheumatic & Nonsteroidal Anti-inflammatory Agents

Antirheumatic & Nonsteroidal Anti-inflammatory Agents (NSAIDs) are a diverse class of drugs used to treat inflammatory conditions, pain, and fever. They are termed "nonsteroidal" to differentiate them from corticosteroids, which are also anti-inflammatory but have a different mechanism and broader range of side effects. Key aspects of NSAIDs include:

  • Therapeutic Actions: NSAIDs share three primary therapeutic actions:
    • Analgesic: Reduce pain, particularly pain associated with inflammation.
    • Antipyretic: Reduce fever.
    • Anti-inflammatory: Reduce inflammation.
  • Mechanism of Action: The therapeutic actions of NSAIDs are primarily attributed to their inhibition of cyclo-oxygenase (COX) enzymes.
  • COX Inhibition: NSAIDs inhibit cyclo-oxygenase (COX-1 and COX-2) enzymes, which are essential for the synthesis of prostaglandins. Prostaglandins are lipid compounds that mediate pain, inflammation, and fever responses in the body.
  • Therapeutic Effects: By inhibiting COX enzymes and reducing prostaglandin synthesis, NSAIDs effectively:
    • Reduce joint swelling, pain & morning stiffness in arthritis.
    • Increase the mobility in arthritic patients.
    • Antipyretic action due to decreased production of prostaglandin from the hypothalamus.
    • Having irritating effect on the GIT.
