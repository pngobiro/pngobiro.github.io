üìö 1- Streptokinase Continued

Contraindications:

  • Active bleeding or high risk of bleeding conditions, as thrombolytic agents increase the risk of hemorrhage.

Side effects:

  • Bleeding: Major side effect, ranging from minor bruising to severe internal hemorrhage.
  • Allergic reactions: Fever, nausea, headache, and allergic reactions including anaphylaxis.

Nursing considerations:

  • Administer Streptokinase in a monitored setting, typically an ICU or cardiac care unit, due to the risk of serious side effects, particularly bleeding.
  • Monitor vital signs frequently, especially blood pressure and heart rate, to detect early signs of complications.
  • Assess for bleeding: Regularly assess patients for any signs of bleeding, including:
    • External bleeding: From puncture sites, surgical wounds, or mucous membranes.
    • Internal bleeding: Indicated by changes in vital signs, decreased hemoglobin and hematocrit, or symptoms like abdominal pain, back pain, or neurological changes.
  • Pre- and post-therapy blood tests: Obtain baseline coagulation studies (PT, aPTT, fibrinogen level, platelet count) before therapy and monitor frequently during and after treatment to assess the drug‚Äôs effect and bleeding risk.

üìö Antifibrinolytic agents: Promoting Clot Stability

Antifibrinolytic agents, in contrast to thrombolytics, promote clot stability and prevent excessive bleeding by inhibiting fibrinolysis‚Äîthe process that dissolves blood clots. These agents are valuable in managing conditions characterized by excessive bleeding or when clot stabilization is needed.

Antifibrinolytic Agents:

  • Mechanism of Action: Antifibrinolytics work by inhibiting the activation of plasminogen to plasmin, or by directly inhibiting plasmin, the enzyme that breaks down fibrin clots. This action helps to stabilize blood clots and reduce bleeding.
  • Clinical Uses: Used to treat or prevent bleeding in various conditions, including:
    • Post-surgical bleeding, especially after procedures with high bleeding risk.
    • Heavy menstrual bleeding (menorrhagia).
    • Bleeding disorders and hemophilia.
