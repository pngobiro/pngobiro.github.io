KMTC Pharmacology Notes

Duration: 20 hours Level: Intermediate

Course Overview

This pharmacology course provides a detailed overview of essential pharmacology concepts, drug classifications, mechanisms of action, pharmacokinetics, pharmacodynamics, therapeutic uses, and nursing considerations. It is designed for KMTC students to build a strong foundation in pharmacology for clinical practice.

  • Understand the fundamental principles of pharmacology, including pharmacokinetics and pharmacodynamics.
  • Identify and classify different branches of pharmacology and drug sources.
  • Describe various routes of drug administration and their impact on drug absorption and distribution.
  • Explain the processes of drug metabolism and excretion and factors influencing these processes.
  • Recognize different drug dosage forms and their implications for drug delivery and action.
  • Understand the concepts of drug tolerance, dependence, and drug interactions.
  • Identify major drug classes, their mechanisms of action, therapeutic uses, side effects, and nursing considerations.
  • Apply pharmacological knowledge to understand drug orders, prescriptions, and safe medication administration practices.
  • Develop a strong foundation in pharmacology to support safe and effective medication management in clinical settings.
