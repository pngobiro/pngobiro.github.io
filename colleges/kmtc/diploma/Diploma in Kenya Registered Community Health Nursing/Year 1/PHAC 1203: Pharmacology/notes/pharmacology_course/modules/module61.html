Acetaminophen, also known as paracetamol in many countries, is a widely used non-narcotic analgesic and antipyretic. It is effective in reducing fever and relieving mild to moderate pain but has minimal anti-inflammatory properties. Key features of acetaminophen include:

  • Trade names: Acamol, Panadol.
  • Class. : Non-narcotic analgesic, antipyretic. It is not classified as an NSAID because it has very weak anti-inflammatory effects.
  • Action: Acetaminophen's mechanisms are not fully understood, but it primarily acts in the central nervous system:
    • Antipyretic Effect: Reduces fever by acting on the hypothalamus, leading to peripheral vasodilation & sweating.
    • It also inhibits the effect of pyrogens on the heat-regulating center on the hypothalamus.
    • Analgesic Effect: It may cause analgesia by inhibiting CNS prostaglandin syntheses So it has no anti-inflammatory effect .
