A speech is an art of being able to pass information through a word of mouth or verbally. The spoken word wields great power. It can stir people to mutinies and rebellions; it can also make them dispirited and down cast. It can turn a hostile mob into a friendly gathering or a friendly gathering into a hostile gathering. It can build tension and it can relax tension. Most successful politician, a political leader, industrialists, business men and sales men success lies in their capability of making an efficient speech.

Characteristics of a good speech

  1. It's clear

    Clarity is the first best and major characteristic of a good speech. Your speech should be eminently successful in couriering to the audience does not instantly grasp your point you have failed as a speaker

  2. It is like an informal talk

    A good speech is closer to personal and informal chart between two intimate friends. When you speak there should be a perfect report between you and your audience.

  3. It is vivid and concrete

    Abstractions kill a speech. So make your speech vivid. Include in it concrete facts easy to comprehend and visualize.

  4. It is brief

    The concentration of an average audience does not last more than 15-20 minutes and so ideally your speech should not be longer than this unless the audience is motivated to know more. To achieve briefly it's desirable to include only a few points in your speech and elaborate them at some length.

  5. It is interacting

    Quotations, anecdotes and humorous touches often make a speech interesting. Quotations should be from accepted authorities. They should be familiar but not worked out. Anecdotes should be new, brief and good tact. Humor should be topical, original, spontaneous and gentle.

  6. It is audience oriented

    A good speech is always tuned to the wan length of the audience and therefore the following points should be put into consideration:-

    • Is the audience a general or specialized one? This will help you determine the depth of the subject that you can touch.
    • How large is the audience? With a small audience the speech will be more like a chart. In a large gathering you will leave to be tutorial.
    • What is the age-group of the lecture? Your references and illustration should confirm to the lecture of that particular age group.
    • What are the social religious, political and economic views and prejudices of the lecture? What is the expected response of the audience?

üìö LISTENING

Listening is an important aid to communication though its importance had not been realized till very recently. It is un-doubtable that if people are bad listeners they will also make bad communication.

We listen to:-

  1. To obtain information
  2. To solve problems
  3. To share experiences
  4. To persuade or dissuade

It's only easy to imagine that if people were bad listeners

  • Only inaccurate and incomplete information would be exchanged.
  • Problems would not be clearly understood and would remain unsolved
  • They would not be able to share one another experience
  • On account of being unable to understand each other through their own or others inattentiveness, would not be able to persuade or dissuade others. In addition, the following advantages of listening can easily be listed.
  1. Listening help to know the organization.

    Careful listening to the grape vine will enable you to know what the members of the staff think of the company's policies and activities, and therefore help one in understanding the organization better.

  2. Listening helps to make better policies.

    Listening to your subordinates carefully, one will be able to know which policies are suitable for your organization you will not keep on stumbling from one top heavy policy to another. You will chalk out the policies which are acceptable to other members and which will win their willing support

  3. Listening mollifies complaining employees

    Very often employees have certain grievances which exists more in their mind than in reality which are physiological. If you listen to the patiently and sympathetically, their anger will subside and they will leave mollified

  4. Listening is important for the success of the open door policy

    Many managers take pride in the fact that they believe in the open door policy they always keep their door opened for the employees to walk into their room and talk to them. Their doors might be opened and yet they are bad listeners and therefore their employees are unlimited to speak freely which will lead to a poor upward communication.

  5. Listening helps in spot sensitive areas before they become explosive

    In an engineering firm, the management decides to meet the woven union regularly whether or not there were problem to iron out. The result was that they began to listen to each other well for the first time. They were able to spot sensitive areas and find solutions before they became explosive and many other problems which could have led to serious disputes just did not arise.

How to become a good listener.

  • Concentrate on what a person is saying rather than on how he looks. Don't be destructed by his physical appearance or his mannerism.
  • Repeat the key ideas to yourself, particularly while listening to a long a lecture try to repeat yourself all the important ideas the speaker has put before you. It will help you understand the lecture even better.
  • Try to relate the speaker's remarks to your personal background and experience. It will also enable you to retain those remarks in your memory longer.
  • Do not let your mind wander away from what the speaker is saying as well as thinking pleasant an unpleasant experience of your own. Be determined that you're going to shut everything out of your, except for the speaker's words. It's also advisable to keep your eyes fixed on the speaker. If you start looking out of the window or around the room, your mind will start wandering away, to unnecessary things
  • Always listen with a positive attitude. And he mentally prepared to listen. Don't allow your prejudices close your mind to what the other person has to say.

  • Effective speeches are clear, informal, vivid, brief, engaging, and audience-oriented.
  • Good listening involves concentration, repeating key ideas, relating to personal experience, and staying focused.
