C.V is a document showing the history of your success and achievements. C.V. is basically a history of your academics, employments and achievements.

Reasons of preparing a C.V

  • Provide a detailed background of a candidate/person
  • Give the prospective employer a picture or first impression of the candidate/ person
  • It acts as a tool that convinces that an employer that one is not only qualified for the but also the best person, I the vacancy.

An employer will use the C.V to make the following decision

  • Is the candidate qualified?
  • Should I call the candidate for an interview?
  • What is the candidate serious enough?
  • Is the candidate going to help my business?
  • Will I need to train the candidate if I hire him/her?

Personal information

  • Name
  • Date of birth
  • Phone number
  • Physical details -gender, religion, merit
  • ID card number

Academic information/ education qualification

  • Schools you attended (most recent first) and the years you attend those schools even if it was only for a months
  • For the school you attend now include other subject you have taken and your favorites
  • Indicate any after school program you were involved in e.g. outside learning circle
  • Future objectives
  • You should include a description of what you would like to be doing in future and now you would want to advance your knowledge. In general this section is to

üìö INTERVIEWS

An interview in a formal exchange between two people or one person and small group,

Forms of interview within an organization

  • Selection interview- this takes place so as to decide whether or not source one will be given a job.
  • Promotion interview- occurs when an employee has applied for a job of higher grade within an organization.
  • Appraisal interview- it's a way of assessing the work done and the progress made by an individual employee. The main aim is to review the worker's progress for future prospect like promotion, staff training a Trans to other work or to a different department.
  • Disciplinary interview- they are least unpleasant and are carried out by senior employee when a worker has been accused of committing a breach of company regulations. It should be based on facts and conducted with impartiality.
  • Grievance interview- takes place when an employee feels she/he ha s mistreated by another member of the organization. It should be conducted on a basis of sound factual knowledge and understanding with an aim of arriving at the truth so as to take appropriate action.
  • Decision making interview- it's usually a meeting between the company members of the equal status. The arrangement should reflect equality with the view of all parties fully considered before a decision is reaction.

Planning and conducting an interview

  • Preparation- the preparation for an interview should consider the purpose of the interview, important information required and familiarizations with it a convenient setting and the people involved in the interview.
  • Activation- tries to put the interviewee at ease as possible by adopting a friendly and reassuring manner so as to have a full exchange of ideas. The best technique of achieving this is by using open questions.
  • Attention- ensures you give the interviewers and individual attention. You can employ all the listening skills like smiling, nodding and looking at the speaker.
  • Structure- draw up a list of point of discussion and make sure time available is used properly.
  • Summery- it's important to always stop after discussion to summarize what you have decided. This to ensure that everybody has a clear idea of what has been decided during the interview.
  • Action- this is concerned with the action to be taken after discussion and who is to take it to ensure the decision made are put into practice and the desire outcome achieved.

How to conduct yourself during an interview

Part of your preparation should be to anticitipate the questions that you might be asked. Your certificate and testimonials should be well prepared. Remember to carry only the originals of all this to an interview room. They should be kept in a neat closed folder that can protect documents from dirt, sun rains, and handling to avoid contamination.

Avoid A4 envelops size for keeping the certificates they are easily prone to wear and tear and further more it does not work well for your image. Imagine walking into an interview well dressed with an old brown soup stained envelope turn at the edges.

Most important however your appearance for that interview and the impression is the last that you will create in this process. Always remember first impression is the last impression the manner in which we dress speaks volumes to support ourselves, dress constitutes a vital part, it doesn't need to be a brand new but should be clear, neat and well fitting.

For an interview on should dress formally which include one foot wear which should be well polished, neat and clean. You may be academically bright, well mannered but the dressing style and hair could just let you down.

The miniskirts or hipsters are no if you are going for a formal job unless it is a modeling job. Avoid trendy wear i.e. design casuals like jeans, tight fitting shirts, screaming colors, color clashing, stylish hairs, excessive make ups, canvas, open shoes, military or timberlands boots. The dressing cord should be strictly formal. For men a well cut out and neat suit should do. You cannot wear a blue court and a red trouser and white shoes.

The preferable color should be black, grey and blue. These colors reflect a serious person. A blue court and a grey trouser are okay. Any court won on a black trouser looks good. Avoid neckties that are shouting in color e.g. bright red, yellow and cream colors. Stick to conservative colors (black, blue and grey). Neckties should be well tied, so that length reaches the area below the stomach. For ladies, also stick to these conservatives colors. A skirt, trouser or suit of the above colors will do well. Neck scurf would be a good compliment. Avoid long skirts, if it is trouser it should not be too tight. High heeled shoes are okay as long as they make you feel comfortable. Don't put them on if they give you problems when walking. Surely you would not want to trip one when you have walked out the interview room.

The hair recommended one is combined it backwards and then typing it with a ribbon, hair band or you could plait it in cornrows have it blown out but at least it should be neat and smart. For men we recommend well trimmed hair. Avoid bold or punk shaving. Teeth should be clean; no foul smell should manatee from your mouth, body as clothes. If you use any kind of perfume, it should not be too strong. Make sure that your nails are clean. Before you enter the interview room always knocks at the door even its wide open. Don't just walk in and when allowed in you should walk briskly with confidence don't sit wait to be told. Shoulders should be pulled up and head held erect. Salute the interviewers in a polite manner. Don't wave to them. Reach others nicely in a proper way of shaking hands. Handshaking must be warm, heartily and of a good grip accompanied by a warm and polite good morning, good afternoon or good evening.

Carry yourself gracefully. Ask the interviewer whether you can take a sit e.g. may I have a sit will be polite than just offering yourself a sit. Don't cross your legs or arms, arms should be well rested on the table with minimal gesturing and gesture only when you stress a point and always make direct eye contact while talking to others because eyes reflect what is inside the heart.

Avoid using expressions such as ammm........., yeah, ok, I mean eeeee........ Make yourself audible enough; voice must be firm, crisp and ringing. Avoid clearing your throughout as you attempt to speak. Blowing your nose during an interview is a minus.

This doesn't work to your verbal skills and manners. Smile and if the interviewers crack jokes don't laugh too loudly. Always appear energetic, active and keen. Show enthusiasm, urge and interest, towards the interviewers, always be self confidence and optimistic but don't overdo.

If you are asked questions that you do not know well. Always admit you do not know rather than attempting to answer something you do not know.

They are really not testing your knowledge but the manner approach that you use. After the interview is over, remember to thank the interviewers for their time and finish with a remark ‚ÄúI hope to hear from you soon". Walk out of the room with the same confidence and always close the door behind you gentle without turning your back.

Tricks for a job hunting interview

Some of the questions asked are:

  • Tell me something about yourself. Say you will be happy to talk about yourself and ask what the interviewer wants to know. If this point is clarified, response if not tell why you feel your skills will contribute to the job and the organization.
  • What is important to you in a job mention specific rewards other than pay cheque e.g. challenging the feeling of the accomplishment and knowing that you have made a contribution. The feeling that I have succeeded in accomplishment a difficult task and the ability to overcome any challenges caused by my job.
  • Why do you want to work for this organization site its reputation? The opportunities it offers and the working conditions. Stress that you want to work for this organization and not just any other organization. The kind of work your esteemed organization is involved in particularly interacts me, or this a reputable and successful organization in the area of customer service and rehabilitation or street children and I have been always yearning to be part of that success which I believe I will be able to achieve if given a chance to work with you.
  • Why should we employ you point out your academics preparation, job skills and enthusiasm about working for the firm. Mention your performance in school or previous employment as evidence of your ability to learn and to become productive quickly. If the job involves the management responsibility refer to past activities as part of proof of your ability to get along with others and work as a team.
  • If we hire you how long will you stay with us as long as my position allows me to learn and to advance at a pace with my abilities.
  • What is your greatest strength? I can see something that needs to be done and do it or am willing to make decision or I can work well with others or I can organize my time efficiently.
  • What is your greatest weakness? Identify one or two such as the following; I tend to drive myself too hard or I expect others to perform beyond their capabilities and I like to see the job done quickly and am critical if it's not.
  • What you did not like in the previous jobs you held discuss the things you did not like but avoid making negatives reference to any of your former employers.
  • How do you spend your leisure time here you mention a cross section of interest, social ones, reading books, listening to music.
  • Are there any weakness in your education and experience take stoke of your weakness before the interview practice discussing them in a positive light and you will find that they will minor when discussed a long with the positive things you have to offer.
  • Where do you want to be in five years from now? You might say in five year I would to have my boss job.

What are your salary expectations? If you are asked at the outset, its best to say, why don't we discuss the salary after deciding if is the right candidate for the job. But if the interviewer asks you this after showing real interest in you then speaks up he/she will try to probably try to meet your price. If you need a clue about what to ask for, say can you discuss salary range with me.

Do you have any question about the organization or the job? Employer likes a candidate who is interested in the organization and this the perfect time to convey your interest and enthusiasm.

What type of position are you interested in? job titles and responsibilities vary from firm to firm so state your skills e.g. am good at figure work and the position that require those skills
