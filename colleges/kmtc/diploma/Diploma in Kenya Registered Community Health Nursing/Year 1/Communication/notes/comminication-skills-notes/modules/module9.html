This is where members of an organization come together to discuss matter related to the growth/ development of the organization.

Purpose of the meetings

To bring organization members to;

  • Exchange information
  • Solve problems
  • Make decision
  • Make sure each department know what is going on other areas of the organizations.

Terminologies used in meetings

  • Quorum- the numbers of people present that is enough to start a meeting.
  • Uttra vires- this a matter that is beyond committees terms of reference(beyond the powers)
  • To adjourn- this is when a meeting is closed (adjourn) without fixing a new date for the next meeting.
  • To pass nem con the motion is passed without anyone disagreeing(nemine contradicente)
  • Constitution- it's the actual membership of the committee that defines the number of members, those who qualifies for membership and dictates how members are chosen.
  • Regularity of meetings- it's an order specifying the frequency with which the meeting should be held.
  • Quadrate attendance- this is a specified number of members needed for meeting to start.
  • Terms of reference- specify what the committee has the power to discuss and any other business outside its area of concern.

Procedure of conducting a meeting

  • Starting the meeting,

    The chairman calls the meeting to order when everyone is present for the meeting or when he/she is satisfied there is a quorum. He will declare the meeting open and recording of the minutes formally starts at that exact time.

  • Apologies for absence,

    The secretary will then read out the names of those who did not to make to the meeting but have sent apologies for their absence giving reasons for their absence.

  • Minutes of the last meeting

    This is the first business of the meeting where the members agree that the minutes of the last meetings are a true and accurate record of what happen.

  • Matters arising from the last meeting

    At this stage the matters that were left unconcluded at the last meeting a matter arising directly from that meeting are discussed. However the discussion is kept short to avoid repetition of what was covered in the last meeting.

Discussion

This is the stage where the main business of the meeting which is usually outlined in the agenda is discussed.

During the discussion the following are observed

  • Speaking through the chair- members contributes to the discussion by addressing through the chair or Mr. Chairman. This is to reduce the risk of personality clashes and ensure that only one person is speaking at anytime.
  • Ruling from the chair- this one of the chair role's to maintain order and make ruling a possible contravention for instance the chain can rule the matter ultra vies.
  • Progress- this is the stage where there are no new points are being introduced and no further action can be taken and various options are open to the committee. A member can also raise a point of information in the need further details and it is answered by any member with necessary data. When things reach deadlock or the available time is up the meeting can them be adjourned.

Proposing and seconding a motion

This is a suggestion of a course where a member forwards an idea to be adopted as the conclusion. The proposed motion must be seconded i.e. giving his/her support through sometimes the chair asks if there is a seconder.

Voting

The proposed motion is taken voted for if there is a seconder by a show of hands. A motion may either be carried or defeated depending on the majority of votes and the figures should be accurately recorded in the minutes in the form.

Amendments

This is the changing in the wording of the proposed motion which alters its meaning making it more precise or more specific.

Correspondence

This is the response announced by the secretary after the details of the main business of the meeting, has been worked on to establish whether there are any actions to be discussed and voted for.

Any other business (AOB)

This is the final item in the agenda that provide an opportunity for additional matters to be raised and discussed. New important business should not be

introduced at this stage but placed on the agenda for the next meeting to allow members to consider and conduct necessary research on it.

Closing the meetings

When any other business has been dealt with, the chair fixes the date of the next meeting. When all the members agree with the date, the chair declares the meeting closed at the next time.

Roles of chairman

  • To prepare list of items to be discussed at the meeting (agenda), with the help of the secretary,
  • To declare the meeting formally open at the right time,
  • To introduce each item for discussion and the member to talk about it where necessary,
  • To ensure the committee works through each item for discussion at a reasonable pace.
  • To ensure no one is allowed to dominate the discussion.
  • To supervise the voting procedure, count votes and announce the results
