A report is a communication of information or advice from someone who has collected and studied the facts to a person who asked for the report. Often the ultimate purpose of writing a report is to provide a basis for decision and action.

Types of reports

Reports can be transmitted in the form

  • Conversation
  • Demonstration
  • Letters
  • Memos
  • Fill in forms
  • Many pages documents.

Reports can also be classified according to

  • Length- can be short or long
  • Tone- can be informal, semi formal or formal.
  • Subject matter- engineering, financial, marketing
  • Timing- daily, week ly, monthly, annually
  • Important- is it special, routine or urgent
  • Style- narrative descriptive, expository, pictorial or statistics.

Note! Whatever form of a report takes, it must always be planned and communicated in a way which suits the receiver and their purpose consequently some reports will require very little planning, others will require more. Some report will be spoken others written, some will be short others long.

Essentials of good report

  1. The report should be unified i.e. it should be about one subject and should contain nothing that the reader does not need or nothing that is off the subject.
  2. It should be complete i.e. include every time the reader wants
  3. All information should be accurate and all the reasoning from the facts must be varied.
  4. It should present the subject matter according to the logical plan based on logical analysis and classification of the materials.
  5. It should be written in a simple consisted style is easy to read and understand.
  6. The report should be readily intelligible to all who are likely to read it even though they may not know the details of the other subject.
  7. The manner of presentation should make the plan clear so that the reader is never in doubt as to where they are in report and why they are there.

Fundamental structure of a report

A standard report is always divided into three parts namely

  • Introduction
  • Body
  • conclusion

a) Introduction

The function of introduction is to prepare a reader for the report. The structure generally follows a standard plan which has been found in practice to read the best way of avoiding incoherent, badly proportion and wrongly emphasized openings. The standard plans include

  • A clear and unambiguous of the real subject
  • An indication of the purpose together with any background information necessary to clear understand of that purpose.
  • A brief description of the methods used to obtain the information.
  • A summary of conclusion, findings and recommendation in their briefest forms.
  • An announcement of the plan on which the body of the report in arrange.

Within the structure the introduction should

  • Be as brief as consistent with clarity
  • Correctly focus the reader's attentions on your real theme or purpose.
  • Harmonize with what follows i.e. it should promise nothing that is not done later on in the report and it should not appear in any way in consisted with final; section.

b) The body of report

It lies between the introduction and final section. In it are set out all the facts such as characteristics of investigation, a detailed explanation of the methodology used the procedure followed and the result obtained.

c) Conclusion

The function of the final sector is to present clearly, briefly and finally the conclusion reached and recommendation to be made.

Report formats

  • Letter report format- this is presented in the form of a report.
  • Schematic report format- this would use systems heading but still contains the essential elements
  • Mixed report format- combines aspects in a letter and schematic format.

Business reports

Is an orderly presentation of facts about specific business activities as program? It can also be communication from someone who has some information to someone who wants to use that information.

Oral and written reports

An oral report is simple and easy to present. It may consist in the communication of an impression or an observation. Sometimes it can be quite useful but a written report is always preferred.

Advantages of written reports ones and oral reports

  • An oral report can be denied any time but written report is a permanent record.
  • A written report can be referred to again and again
  • An oral report tends to be vague; it may be filled with irrelevant facts, while leaving out some significant ones. In written tries to accurate and precise
  • A written report can change hands without any danger of distortion during transmission.

Types of formal report

  • Statutory reports

    They reports prepared and presented according to the firm and procedure laid down by the law. The director and the secretary of a company are required by the companies act to prepare and submit statutory report e.g. report submitted at the statutory meeting of the share holders.

  • Non statutory reports

    They are formal reports which are not required under the any law but which are prepared to held the management in framing policies or taking other important decisions.

Reports on the basis on a number of persons interested with the drafting of it

  • Reports of individuals

    they are submitted by executive heads of various departments. The company's secretary, auditor e.t.c. they are naturally related to the work in own departments.

  • Reports by committees/ sub committees

    sometimes reports are needed on subjects that do not only cover one department. They can be so important that its thoughts advisable to associate more than one person with them. In such cases, committees or sub committees are formed to prepare reports. They are written after a careful and courteous deliberation of members. They are formal in style and impersonal in tone. They may be signed by the members of the committees or the chairman and secretary

Reports on the basis of nature

  • Periodic/ routine reports-

    they are prepare and presented at regular prescribed interval in the usual routine of an organization operations. They may be submitted annually, monthly, weekly etc. they contain mere statements of facts in details or in summarized form and without any opinion or recommendation.

  • Progress reports-

    they are meant to describe and assess progress made during a particular period. They present an account of the work already done. Work in progress with other relevant facts and details of the work, yet to be completed.

  • Examination reports-

    they are specially commissioned to cover important aspects or events. They are prepare after thorough investigation (old files studies) personal interviews contacted, questioner circulated among people, surveys are contacted and important facts compiled and analyzed and curtain conclusions arrived at many as many not contain, recommendation but the findings as conclusion of those who have prepared them definitely influence the final decision.

  • Recommendation reports -

Statically reports- they are largely made up of financial data. Mathematical charts and tabular columns of figures.
