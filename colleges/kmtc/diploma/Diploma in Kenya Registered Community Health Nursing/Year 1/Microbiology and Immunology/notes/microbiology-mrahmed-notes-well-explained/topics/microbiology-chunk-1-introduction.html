Subdivision of microbiology

  1. 1.Medical microbiology
  2. 2.Industrial microbiology
  3. 3.Solid/land/ microbiology
  4. 4.plant microbiology
  5. 5.Food microbiology

In medical microbiology we are concern with the study of human pathogens, animal pathogens how they are transmitted to man and those commensals which on occasion particularly when outside their normal habitat causes serious diseases to main host.

üåü Aim/ importance of microbiology

One is required to know the;-

  1. 1.Diagnosis- one need to know the cause (etiology) of a disease by isolation and identification of causative organism from pathological specimen
  2. 2.Mode of transmission i.e blood, sexual intercourse, ingestion, and through skin so that to stop the spread.
  3. 3.Prognosis of the disease-Laboratory results and interpretation determine whether treatment is effective and the disease cure or infective or presence of active disease
  4. 4.Guidance in treatment of microorganism respond to treatment either further investigation is needed or not.
  5. 5.Epidemiology- to know the disease pattern, distribution and how it spread and factors contributing to spread.(sources of infection).
  6. 6.Detections of new pathogens and then development

The areas covered in microbiology are:

  1. 1.Parasitology- study of parasites causing diseases to human beings
  2. 2.Mycology- study of fungi causing disease in human body
  3. 3.Bacteriology-study of bacteria causing diseases in human body
  4. 4.Virology- study of virus
  5. 5.Immunology- concerned with mechanism involved in the development of resistance by body to infectious diseases
  6. 6.Genetics- study of heredity and variation
