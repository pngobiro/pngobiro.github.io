Preparation of media

Presently, a wide range of culture media are available commercially in the form of dehydrated media. These media are simply reconstituted by weighing the required quantities and by adding distilled water, as per the manufacturer's instructions.

The pH determination can be conveniently done with the use of Lovibond comparator with phenol red indicator disc.

  • Take two clean test tubes and add 5 ml of the medium to each of the tubes. One serves as a blank while phenol red indicator is added to the other tube.
  • Compare the colour of the medium with the phenol red indicator at the appropriate pH marking.
  • Add N/10 NaOH or N/10 HCl, drop by drop till the colour of the medium matches the colour of the disc at the required pH reading.
  • Calculate the volume of the NaOH or HCL of 1/10 strength for 5 ml of the medium to get the required pH .
  • Based on the calculation, the volume of 1 N NaOH or IN HCl required for the total volume of medium can be calculated and added.
  • Check the pH of the medium once again before use.

The quantity of agar given in the formulae of media may have to be changed depending upon the quality of agar used. The concentration varies from batch to batch and should be such that will produce a sufficiently firm surface on solidification. This can be tested by streaking with inoculating wire.

In some laboratories media are prepared by individual measurement of ingredients and then mixing the same. Hence the method of preparation is given likewise:

Nutrient Broth

Ingredients:

  • Peptone: 10 gm
  • Meat extract (Lab Lemco): 10 gm
  • Sodium chloride: 5 gm
  • Water (distilled): 1000 ml

Mix the ingredients and dissolve them by heating in a steamer. When cool, adjust the pH to 7.5-7.6.

Nutrient agar

To the ingredients as in nutrient broth, add 15 gm agar per litre. Dissolve the agar in nutrient broth and sterilize by autoclaving at $121^{\circ} \mathrm{C}$ for 15 minutes. Prepare plates and slopes as required.

Glucose broth

Ingredients:

  • Nutrientbroth: 900 ml
  • Glucose (10% solution): 100 ml
  • Dissolve 9 gm glucose in distilled water and sterilize by tyndallisation.
  • Add 100 ml of the glucose solution to 900 ml of sterile nutrient broth.
  • Dispense 60 ml each in 100 ml pre-sterilized culture bottles.
  • Sterilize by open steaming at $100^{\circ} \mathrm{C}$ for one hour.

Blood agar

Ingredients:

  • Nutrient agar: 100 ml
  • Sheep blood (defibrinated): 10 ml
  • Melt the sterile nutrient agar by steaming, cool to $45^{\circ} \mathrm{C}$.
  • Add required amount of sheep blood aseptically with constant shaking.
  • Mix the blood with molten nutrient agar thoroughly but gently, avoiding froth formation.
  • Immediately pour into petri dishes or test tubes and allow to set.

Chocolate agar

The ingredients are essentially the same as in blood agar.

  • Melt the sterile nutrient agar by steaming and cool to about $75^{\circ} \mathrm{C}$.
  • Add blood to the molten nutrient agar and allow to remain at $75^{\circ} \mathrm{C}$ after gently mixing till it is chocolate brown in colour.
  • Pour in petri dishes or test tubes for slopes as desired.

MacConkey Agar

Ingredients:

  • Peptone: 20 gm
  • Sodium taurocholate (bile salt): 5 gm
  • Lactose: 10 gm
  • Neutral red (1% aqueous solution): 5 ml
  • Agar: 20 gm
  • Distilled water: 1000 ml

Weigh the ingredients into a flask and add distilled water. Mix the contents well and steam it for 15 minutes (do not autoclave). Cool to $56^{\circ} \mathrm{C}$ and pour in plates.

Buffered glycerol saline

Ingredients:

  • Glycerol: 300 ml
  • Sodiumchloride: 4.2 gm
  • Disodiumhydrogenphosphate ($\text{Na}_2\text{HPO}_4$ Anhydrous): 10.0 gm (or 15.0 gm if not anhydrous, MMD text is ambiguous)
  • Phenolredaqueoussolution 0.02 percent: 15.0 ml
  • Water: 700 ml (pH should be 7.2 as per MMD image, but text says 8.4)
  • Add phenol red and adjust pH to 8.4 (as per text).
  • Dissolve NaCl in water and add glycerol.
  • Distribute 6 ml in universal containers (screw capped bottles) and sterilize by autoclaving at $121^{\circ} \mathrm{C}$ for 15 minutes.

Loeffler serum medium

Ingredients:

  • Nutrientbroth: 250 ml
  • Serum(sheeporhorseorox): 750 ml
  • Glucose: 10 gm
  • Dissolve glucose in nutrient broth and sterilize at $121^{\circ} \mathrm{C}$ for 15 minutes.
  • Add serum aseptically.
  • Mix thoroughly but gently, avoiding froth formation.
  • Distribute in sterile test tubes or quarter ounce screw-cap bottles.
  • Inspissate the medium in a slanting position in a water inspissator at $82^{\circ} \mathrm{C}$ for two hours.
  • In the absence of an inspissator, the medium may be coagulated by standing over the top of a steam sterilizer for 6-7 minutes.

Blood tellurite agar

Agar base

  • Proteose peptone: 20 gm
  • Sodium chloride: 5 gm
  • Agar: 20 gm
  • Distilled water: 1000 ml

Dissolve the ingredients and adjust the pH to 7.6 . Distribute in 100 ml quantities in a bottle and autoclave at $121^{\circ} \mathrm{C}$ for 15 minutes.

Glycerolated blood tellurite mixture

  • Sterile defibrinated sheep blood: 14 ml
  • Sterileglycerol: 6 ml
  • Sterile potassium tellurite solution (1% in water): 4 ml
  • Sterilize the glycerol in hot air oven at $160^{\circ} \mathrm{C}$ for 60 minutes and the tellurite solution by autoclaving at $115^{\circ} \mathrm{C}$ for 20 minutes. Mix the ingredients in a sterile flask, incubate for $1-2 \mathrm{hrs}$. at $37^{\circ} \mathrm{C}$, then refrigerate.
  • Haemolysis is complete after 24 hrs . The mixture keeps well in a refrigerator. One per cent solution of good quality tellurite is sufficient but $2 \%$ of some batches may be required.

Preparation of complete medium

  • Glycerolated blood tellurite mixture: 24 ml
  • Agar base: 100 ml

Melt the agar, cool to $45^{\circ} \mathrm{C}$, add blood and tellurite and pour in sterile petri dishes.

R57. Voges-Proskauer (VP) Test Reagents

Solution 1: alpha-Naphthol 5 g, Alcohol (absolute) 100 ml.

Solution 2: Potassium hydroxide 40 g, Distilled water to make 100 ml.

Voges-Proskauer (VP) test. Transfer 1 ml of 48 h culture to test tube and add 0.6 ml solution 1 and 0.2 ml solution. Shake after adding each solution.
