Section A: Multiple Choice Questions

40 Marks
1.
The double vertical lines "||" in a voltaic cell represents
a. Phase boundary
b. Metal connection
c. Salt bridge
d. Gas electrode
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c. Salt bridge

Explanation: In standard cell notation, a single vertical line `|` represents a phase boundary (e.g., solid electrode to aqueous solution), while a double vertical line `||` represents the salt bridge connecting the two half-cells.

2.
The oxidation number of chromium in $\mathrm{Na}_{2} \mathrm{Cr}_{2} \mathrm{O}_{7}$ is?
a. +3
b. +6
c. +7
d. +12
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b. +6

Calculation: Na is +1, O is -2. Let Cr be $x$.

$2(1) + 2x + 7(-2) = 0$

$2 + 2x - 14 = 0$

$2x = 12 \implies x = +6$

3.
Which of the following does not affect the rate of a reaction?
a. Temperature
b. Time
c. Pressure
d. Concentration
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b. Time

Explanation: Reaction rate factors are Temperature, Concentration, Surface Area, Catalyst, and Pressure (for gases). Time is the dimension over which the reaction occurs, not a parameter that alters the inherent speed (rate constant) of the reaction itself.

4.
The heat of a system is called:
a. Entropy
b. Enthalpy
c. Gibb's free energy
d. Enthalpy change
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b. Enthalpy

Explanation: Enthalpy ($H$) is the thermodynamic quantity equivalent to the total heat content of a system.

5.
The half life of a second order reaction is equal to:
a. k/0.693
b. $1 / k[A]_0$
c. 0.693/k
d. k ~
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b. $1 / k[A]_0$

Explanation: For a second order reaction, half life depends on initial concentration. $t_{1/2} = \frac{1}{k[A]_0}$. (Note: 0.693/k is for first order).

6.
Which of the following is not observed in the cell $\mathrm{Zn} / \mathrm{Zn}^{2+} // \mathrm{Cu}^{2+} / \mathrm{Cu}$?
a. Increase in size of copper metal electrode
b. Fading of the blue CuSO4 solution
c. Reduction of the size of zinc metal electrode
d. Effervescence
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d. Effervescence

Explanation: Effervescence implies gas production. In a standard Daniell cell, metal deposits and dissolves; no gas is produced.

7.
Which statement is false regarding a catalyst?
a. Alters the reaction mechanism
b. Increases the forward reaction without affects the reverse reaction
c. Alters the activation energy
d. Its altered in the reaction but its regenerated
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b. Increases the forward reaction without affects the reverse reaction

Explanation: A catalyst lowers the activation energy for both the forward and reverse reactions equally, thus speeding up both rates. It does not shift the equilibrium position.

8.
The flow of electrons & current in an electrochemical cell is:
a. Electrons move from positive to negative...
b. Electrons move from negative to positive, current from positive to negative
c. Both move same direction
d. Both move negative to positive
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b. Electrons move from negative to positive, current from positive to negative

Explanation: Electrons flow from Anode (Negative) to Cathode (Positive). Conventional current flows in the opposite direction, from Positive to Negative.

Explanation: In a galvanic/voltaic cell, the cathode is the positive electrode (where reduction occurs). Note: In an electrolytic cell, it is negative.

12.
One of the following is not a requirement of electrolysis to occur
a. Direct current
b. Salt bridge

d. Electrolyte

Explanation: Electrolysis typically occurs in a single cell where electrodes are immersed in the same electrolyte. A salt bridge is used in Galvanic cells to connect separate half-cells.

13.
All spontaneous electron transfer reactions have:
a. Positive $E^0$ and negative $\Delta G$
b. Positive $E^0$ and positive $\Delta G$
c. Negative $E^0$ and positive $\Delta G$
d. Negative $E^0$ and negative $\Delta G$
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a. Positive $E^0$ and negative $\Delta G$

Explanation: $\Delta G = -nFE$. For spontaneous reactions ($\Delta G < 0$), $E$ must be positive.

19.
In the Arrhenius equation, the slope of $\ln k$ vs $1/T$ is equal to
a. k
b. -k
c. $-E_a/R$
d. $E_a$
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c. $-E_a/R$

Explanation: $\ln k = -\frac{E_a}{R}(\frac{1}{T}) + \ln A$. This is in the form $y = mx + c$.

20.
The following reaction has an $E^{\circ}$ value = 0.76 V. Calculate $\Delta G$. ($\mathrm{Zn} \rightarrow \mathrm{Zn}^{2+} + 2\mathrm{e}$)
a. -1.467 x 10^5, spontaneous
b. -1.467 x 10^5, non-spontaneous
c. 1.467 x 10^5, non-spontaneous
d. 1.467 x 10^5, spontaneous
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a. -1.467 x 10^5, spontaneous

Calculation: $\Delta G = -nFE$. $n=2$, $F=96500$, $E=0.76$.

$\Delta G = -2 \times 96500 \times 0.76 = -146,680 \text{ J} = -1.467 \times 10^5$.

Since $\Delta G$ is negative, it is spontaneous.

26.
A first order reaction has a rate constant of 0.33/min. How long does it take to reactant concentration from 0.13 M to 0.065 M?
a. 1.05 min
b. 4.2 min
c. 2.1 min
d. 1.2 min
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c. 2.1 min

Calculation: Going from 0.13 to 0.065 is exactly half (one half-life).

$t_{1/2} = 0.693 / k = 0.693 / 0.33 = 2.1 \text{ min}$.

43.
Citing 2 examples, describe redox reactions (4 marks)
Show Answer

Answer:

Redox (Reduction-Oxidation) reactions involve the transfer of electrons between two species.

  • Example 1: Rusting of Iron.
    $4Fe + 3O_2 \rightarrow 2Fe_2O_3$
    (Iron is oxidized, Oxygen is reduced).
  • Example 2: Displacement reaction.
    $Zn(s) + CuSO_4(aq) \rightarrow ZnSO_4(aq) + Cu(s)$
    (Zn oxidized to Zn2+, Cu2+ reduced to Cu).
44.

State and explain the effect of the following on the reaction rate:

  • a. Decreasing temperature (3 marks)
  • b. Increasing reactants concentration (3 marks)
  • c. Addition of a catalyst (3 marks)
  • d. Increasing the pressure (3 marks)
Show Answer

Answer:

a. Decreasing Temperature: Decreases rate. Particles have less kinetic energy, leading to fewer collisions and fewer particles overcoming the activation energy barrier.

b. Increasing Concentration: Increases rate. More particles per unit volume lead to more frequent collisions.

c. Addition of Catalyst: Increases rate. Provides an alternative reaction pathway with a lower activation energy, allowing more particles to react.

d. Increasing Pressure: Increases rate (for gases). Compresses gas molecules closer together (effectively increasing concentration), increasing collision frequency.

45.
Describe ion-selective electrodes and indicate an example of such (4 marks)
Show Answer

Answer:

Ion-Selective Electrodes (ISE): These are analytical sensors that convert the activity of a specific ion dissolved in a solution into an electrical potential. The potential generated is logarithmically related to the ionic activity (Nernstian behavior).

Example: The pH electrode (Glass electrode) is selective for $H^+$ ions.

46.
Explain the term mass defect in radiochemistry (4 marks)
Show Answer

Answer:

Mass Defect: The difference between the actual mass of an atomic nucleus and the sum of the individual masses of its constituent protons and neutrons. The "missing" mass is converted into nuclear binding energy that holds the nucleus together, according to Einstein's equation $E=mc^2$.
