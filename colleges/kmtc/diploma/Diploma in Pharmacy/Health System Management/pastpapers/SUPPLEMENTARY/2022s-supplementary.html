Section 1: Multiple Choice Questions

1.
The ______________ and strengthen primary healthcare in Kenya
A. Facility Improvement Fund
B. Health Sector Service Improvement Fund
C. National Hospital Insurance Fund
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B. Health Sector Service Improvement Fund

The HSSIF (Health Sector Services Fund) was specifically established to provide financial resources directly to primary health care facilities (dispensaries and health centers) to improve service delivery.

2.
The following are the common project management functions except:
A. Planning
B. Control
C. Team development
D. Delegation
Show Answer

Correct Answer: D. Delegation

While delegation is a tool used by managers, Planning, Control, and Team Development are considered core functions or processes of project management.

3.
A project proposal format should have all the following sections except?
A. Should be clear and concise
B. Have a title page and name of organization
C. Feasibility study report
D. Cost and benefit analysis
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A. Should be clear and concise

"Should be clear and concise" is a quality of the writing, not a section of the document (like Title Page, Feasibility, or CBA).

4.
The following is TRUE about user fees in health financing EXCEPT.
A. Also known as user charges
B. Represents a market based solution for health care financing
C. It is an ideal way of promoting utilization of services.
D. User fees are a good way of raising funds for the facility.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C. It is an ideal way of promoting utilization of services.

User fees typically reduce or deter utilization of services, especially among the poor, rather than promoting it.

5.
The following statement is TRUE about a Facility Improvement Fund. Which one
A. The collected fees are collected from the public to defray the costs of running the facility.
B. It is collected and managed by the Parliament
C. It is donated by International Agencies
D. None of the above
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A. The collected fees are collected from the public to defray the costs of running the facility.

FIF is generated from user fees (cost sharing) and is retained by the facility to improve services and cover operational costs.

6.
At what phase of project management should the control function be undertaken?
A. The initiating phase
B. The implementation/executing phase
C. The planning phase
D. The closing phase
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B. The implementation/executing phase

Monitoring and Control typically runs parallel to Execution. You control the work as it is being done.

7.
The following are common types of evaluation in a health system except
A. Informative evaluation
B. Summative evaluation
C. Internal evaluation
D. Ongoing evaluation.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A. Informative evaluation

"Informative" is not a standard technical term for a type of evaluation (like Formative, Summative, Process, or Impact).

8.
The following are the major principles of quality management except.
A. Process approach
B. Focus on measurement
C. Focus on customer
D. Interpersonal relations
E. Continual improvement
Show Answer

Correct Answer: D. Interpersonal relations

Standard QM principles (ISO 9001) include Customer Focus, Process Approach, Improvement, and Evidence-based Decision Making (Measurement). Interpersonal relations is not a formal principle.

9.
The following are examples of tools/ techniques used in measuring quality. Which tool is also called the cause and effect diagram
A. Flow charts
B. Bench marking
C. Fish bone technique
D. Pareto charts/ diagram
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C. Fish bone technique

The Fishbone diagram (Ishikawa) is the Cause-and-Effect diagram.

10.

A vote book is used to record and monitor expenditure in the public sector. Which of the following are the characteristics of a vote book

  1. Ensure no extra budget spending
  2. Highlight unnecessary expenditure
  3. Shows balance available on expenditure
  4. The claimant can only spend what they have
  5. Allows only replenishment of what is spent
A. I, II, III
B. I, II, IV
C. I, IV, V
D. I, II,V,IV
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A. I, II, III (or I, II, IV)

A Vote Book ensures spending is within budget (I), shows running balances (III), and helps identify/control expenditure (II/IV).

11.
The concept of quality assurance concerns:
A. Quality design
B. Quality measuring
C. Quality improvement
D. All the above.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: D. All the above.

Quality Assurance is a total system encompassing design, measurement, and improvement.

12.

13.

Project planning process involves all the following processes. What is the formal order of the planning process?

  1. Analysis of the situation
  2. Priorities setting
  3. Assessment of resources
  4. Formulation of Plan
  5. Implementation
  6. evaluation
  7. Monitoring
A. I, III, II,IV,V, VII, VI
B. I,II, IV, V, VII, VI, III,
C. II, IV, I, III, V, VII
D. None of the above sequences
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A. I, III, II,IV,V, VII, VI

Logical Order: Situational Analysis -> Resource Assessment -> Priority Setting -> Plan Formulation -> Implementation -> Monitoring -> Evaluation.

14.

A. Determinant of health
B. Health system inputs

Correct Answer: D. Planning frameworks

A "Planning framework" is a tool, not a measurable indicator like determinants, inputs, or outputs.

15.
Areas of evaluation of a project exists in the following except
A. Purpose
B. Programs
C. Staff
D. Financial administration
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C. Staff

Project evaluation focuses on the project's purpose, programs, and efficiency (finance). Staff evaluation is typically Performance Appraisal, a separate HR process.

16.
Which of the following is NOT considered as monitoring.
A. Counting the number of people trained
B. Attributing changes in health outcomes to an intervention
C. Tracking the number of health brochures distributed
D. Collecting monthly data on clients served in a hospital

Correct Answer: B. Attributing changes in health outcomes to an intervention

Attribution of causality (impact) is a function of Evaluation, not Monitoring.

17.

B. Impact evaluation

Monitoring tracks the implementation process.

18.
What are the 3 main components of a logic framework approach that process monitoring focuses on
A. Inputs, activities, outputs.
B. Intermediate results, strategic objective, goal
C. Output, intermediate results, strategic objective
D. Inputs, outputs, intermediate results

Correct Answer: A. Inputs, activities, outputs.

Process monitoring looks at the operational level: what we put in (inputs), what we did (activities), and what we produced (outputs).

24.
The following are examples of provider payment mechanisms in health financing except.
A. Fee for service
B. Per diem
C. Per capita
D. Line item
E. All the above
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B. Per diem

Fee-for-service, Capitation (Per capita), and Line-item budgeting are recognized provider payment methods. Per diem is a staff allowance for travel/sustenance, not a method to pay a healthcare provider/facility for patient care services.

Section 2: Short Answer Questions

28.
An imprest is an example of a financial accounting system. List some of the general characteristics of an imprest system
Show Answer

Answer:

  1. It involves a fixed sum of money (float).
  2. Used for small, petty cash expenses.
  3. Replenishment involves reimbursing the exact amount spent to restore the float.
  4. Requires receipts/vouchers for all expenditure.
  5. The holder is personally accountable for the funds.
29.
Briefly list the advantages of fee for service as a method of financing health care.
Show Answer

Answer:

  1. Encourages physicians to deliver care/work harder.
  2. Patients only pay for services actually received.
  3. High transparency in billing.
  4. Can improve access as providers are motivated to see patients.
  5. Allows for detailed tracking of services provided.

Show Answer

Answer:

  1. Government Revenues (Taxes) - National and County allocations.
  2. Social Health Insurance (NHIF contributions).
  3. Out-of-Pocket payments (User fees) from households.
  4. Development Partners/Donors (External aid).
  5. Private Health Insurance premiums.
31.
Distinguish between monitoring and evaluation with reference to health system management
Show Answer

Answer:

  • Monitoring is the continuous process of collecting data on specified indicators to track progress against objectives (efficiency/outputs). It asks "Are we doing things right?"
  • Evaluation is a periodic assessment of the design, implementation, and results of a project (effectiveness/impact). It asks "Are we doing the right things?"

Section 3: Long Answer Question

20 Marks
38.
With the help of an illustration, describe the main processes involved in a project management cycle (20 marks)
Show Answer

Answer:

The Project Management Cycle consists of the following phases (Illustration: A circular diagram connecting these steps):

  1. Initiation/Identification:
    • Defining the problem or opportunity.
    • Conducting needs assessment.
    • Developing the project charter.
    • Identifying stakeholders.
  2. Planning/Design:
    • Defining scope, goals, and deliverables.
    • Creating the Work Breakdown Structure (WBS).
    • Developing the budget and schedule (Gantt charts).
    • Risk management planning.
  3. Execution/Implementation:
    • Mobilizing resources (staff, funds).
    • Carrying out the planned activities.
    • Team leadership and coordination.
    • Communication with stakeholders.
  4. Monitoring and Control:
    • Tracking progress against the plan (time, cost, quality).
    • Reporting performance (KPIs).
    • Managing changes and risks.
    • Quality control.
  5. Closure/Evaluation:
    • Handing over deliverables to the client.
    • Releasing resources.
    • conducting end-of-project evaluation.
    • Documenting lessons learned.
