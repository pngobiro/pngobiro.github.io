Multiple Choice Questions

1.
Qualities of good entrepreneurs include:
a) Conformists, risk takers, critical thinkers.
b) Critical thinkers, tolerant, conformists.
c) Conformists, tolerant, risk takers.
d) Critical thinkers, risk takers, tolerant
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Critical thinkers, risk takers, tolerant

Entrepreneurs are typically innovators (not conformists) who take calculated risks and must be tolerant of ambiguity and failure.

2.
Factors to remember while purchasing commodities include the following except?
a) Quantity & payment discounts
b) Locality of the supplier
c) Gender of supplier
d) Primary contract
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Gender of supplier

Procurement decisions should be based on objective criteria like price, quality, delivery time, and reliability. Gender is not a professional purchasing factor (though specific affirmative action procurement rules like AGPO exist in Kenya, in general purchasing principles, gender is not a product factor).

3.
Which of the following is the correct sequence of the procurement process?
a) Planning, Documentation, Product evaluation, Awarded and Administration, Inspection and Delivery
b) Planning, Awarded and Administration, Documentation, Product evaluation, Inspection and Delivery
c) Planning, Documentation, Awarded and Administration, Inspection and Delivery, Product evaluation
d) Planning, Documentation, Product evaluation, Awarded and, Inspection and Delivery, Administration
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Planning, Documentation, Product evaluation, Awarded and Administration, Inspection and Delivery

The standard cycle is: Plan needs -> Prepare documents -> Evaluate bids/products -> Award contract -> Administer/Inspect delivery.

4.
Feedback to subordinate from hospital managers:
a) Should be given in private always
b) Should be done daily
c) Should be both objective and subjective
d) Should have negative comments
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Should be done daily (Continuous feedback)

Feedback is most effective when it is timely and continuous (daily/regular). While "private" is good for negative feedback, positive feedback can be public. Feedback should primarily be objective.

5.
The following theorist emphasised on the use of rules, regulations and policies to govern employees:
a) Frederic Taylor
b) Max Weber
c) Henri Fayol
d) Douglas McGregor
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Max Weber

Max Weber is the father of Bureaucratic Management, characterized by formal rules, hierarchy, and impersonal application of regulations.

6.
Principles of customer care that you can utilize include;
a) Understanding what you do, ensuring you are compensated for what you do
b) Finishing your tasks, being happy with your job
c) Understanding your customer needs, Ensuring division of labour
d) Being hospitable, proper time management
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Being hospitable, proper time management (or arguably c depending on context)

Customer care focuses on the interaction. Hospitality (welcoming/polite) and time management (not keeping customers waiting) are key. "Understanding customer needs" (in C) is also correct, but "division of labour" is a general management principle, not specific to customer care.

7.
Positive non-verbal communication includes the following;
a) Personal appearance
b) Dancing
c) Laughing
d) Frowning
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Personal appearance (Professional grooming)

Professional appearance communicates competence and respect. Frowning is negative. Dancing is situational. Laughing can be positive but depends on context.

8.
One of the following is an example of an activity involved in monitoring and evaluation
a) Registers
b) Outreach
c) Blood Pressure machine
d) Meeting venue
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Registers

Registers are data collection tools used to track (monitor) patient visits, treatments, and outcomes.

9.
Of all the resources managed by a supervisor, the most important resource is
a) Machinery
b) Human resources
c) Money
d) Materials
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Human resources

People (HR) are the only resource that can think, innovate, and mobilize all other resources.

10.
One of the more difficult tasks for a manager when he or she is coordinating activities is
a) Securing cooperation
b) Identifying the activities
c) Utilizing automation
d) Planning the coordination effort
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Securing cooperation

Coordination requires people to work together. Securing their voluntary and active cooperation is often the hardest human element.

11.
Categories of local resources include;
a) Human Resources,
b) Organizational Resources,
c) Infrastructural Resources,
d) Biological Resource
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Human Resources,

(Note: This question likely has multiple correct options in reality, but HR is the primary resource category listed).

12.
Importance of resource mobilization is to
a) Diversify and expand resources
b) Compromise financial sustainability
c) Promote dependency
d) Increase competition.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Diversify and expand resources

Mobilization aims to bring in more and different types of resources to ensure sustainability, not dependency.

13.
Strategies of time management include
a) Goal setting,
b) Time analysis,
c) Prolonged meetings,
d) Delegation
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Goal setting, (d) Delegation is also correct

Both goal setting and delegation are key strategies. "Prolonged meetings" is a time waster.

14.
The following categories of patients are automatically exempted:
a) Prisoners,
b) National Youth Service personnel,
c) Adults Foreigners
d) Children under five years of age
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Children under five years of age

In Kenya's public health system, children under 5 are automatically exempted from user fees. Prisoners are also typically treated at state expense, but "under 5s" is the most common automatic policy exemption in general facilities.

15.
In advocacy, a constituency refers to
a) The people in positions of authority
b) The champions who lead the advocacy process
c) The persons affected by the advocacy issued.
d) The networks formed to advocate for change
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) The persons affected by the advocacy issued.

The constituency is the group of people or "base" that the advocacy effort represents and who are affected by the issue.

16.
A manager who is involved in the process of grouping the necessary responsibilities and activities into workable units engages in a role of
a) Job designing
b) Staffing
c) Scheduling
d) Organizing
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Organizing

Organizing is the management function of arranging people and resources to work toward a goal, including grouping tasks into units (departmentalization).

17.
The skills of an effective manager are;
a) Conceptual, human, technical
b) Human, communication, technical
c) Technical, political, social
d) Conceptual, social, communication
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Conceptual, human, technical

These are the three classic management skills identified by Robert Katz.

18.
Positive nonverbal communication includes the following;
a) Personal appearance
b) Dancing
c) Laughing
d) Frowning
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Personal appearance

(Repeat of Question 7)

19.
Henry Fayol: -
a) Introduced innovations in how jobs were designed and how workers were trained to perform.
b) Identified specific functions of a manger
c) Developed the bureaucratic model
d) Examined motivation from the perspective of why people choose to follow a particular course of action
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Identified specific functions of a manger

Henry Fayol is known for identifying the 5 functions of management (Planning, Organizing, Commanding, Coordinating, Controlling) and 14 principles of management. Bureaucracy was Weber (C).

20.
Espirit de corps is a principle of management that:
a) Ensures a just working environment for employees
b) Encourages harmony and general good feelings among employees
c) Encourages worker initiative
d) Ensures retention of productive employees
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Encourages harmony and general good feelings among employees

Esprit de corps refers to team spirit, unity, and harmony within the organization.

21.
Managerial functions include:
a) Planning, controlling and staffing
b) Planning, negotiating and liaising
c) Organizing, planning, monitoring
d) Resource allocation, negotiating, monitoring
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Planning, controlling and staffing

Planning, Organizing, Staffing, Leading, and Controlling are the standard functions. "Negotiating" and "Liaising" are roles (Mintzberg), not functions.

22.
Which of the following leadership theory states that leaders are born and not made?
a) Trait
b) Charismatic
c) Great man
d) Situational
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Great man

The "Great Man" theory explicitly posits that leaders are born with inherent traits that make them leaders, not made.

23.
Recruitment involves the following except one?
a) Advertising a vacancy,
b) Short-listing,
c) Controlling
d) Induction
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Controlling

Recruitment includes advertising and short-listing. Induction is onboarding. Controlling is a general management function, not a step in recruitment itself.

24.
Evaluation errors in performance appraisal include
a) Formal effect
b) Informal effect
c) Halo effect
d) Double effect
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Halo effect

The Halo Effect is a cognitive bias where one positive trait influences the overall rating of a person.
