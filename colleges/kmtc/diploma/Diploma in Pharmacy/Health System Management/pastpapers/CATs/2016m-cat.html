Multiple Choice Questions

30 Marks
1.
The budgetary process in Kenya comprises of four stages. Which ones are these?
A. Drafting, analysing, audit/evaluation and synthesising.
B. Legislating, initiating, audit/evaluation and implementing.
C. Planning, designing & implementing and audit/evaluation.
D. Drafting, legislation, implementation, and audit/evaluation.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: D. Drafting, legislation, implementation, and audit/evaluation.

The standard public budget cycle consists of Formulation (Drafting), Approval (Legislation), Execution (Implementation), and Oversight/Audit.

2.
The following statements are true except one. Which is false?
A. Budget cycle is based on the medium term expenditure framework cycle (MTEF).
B. Health sector financing is done on a needs basis by the Treasury.
C. Allocation of funds to various sectors is guided by inputs, processes and outcomes.
D. Budget is prepared through Sector Working Groups. (SWGs)
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B. Health sector financing is done on a needs basis by the Treasury.

Financing is typically constrained by available revenue (resource-based/ceilings) rather than purely on a "needs basis". The Treasury sets ceilings based on macro-fiscal projections.

3.
In the six-level hospital health structure in Kenya, which level receives the highest budgetary allocation?
A. Level 1.
B. Level 3.
C. Level 4.
D. Level 6.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: D. Level 6.

National Referral Hospitals (Level 6) like KNH and MTRH historically absorb a disproportionately large share of the national health budget compared to lower levels.

4.
Which hospital level has serious challenges of resource allocation?
A. Level 1.
B. Level 2.
C. Level 5.
D. Level 6.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A. Level 1.

Community Health Services (Level 1) are often the most underfunded and under-resourced level in the structure.

5.
The most common cost recovery techniques adopted by the ministries of health is?
A. Private insurance arrangements
B. Local government or community based financing.
C. User fees for services and medicines
D. Pre-payment plans.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C. User fees for services and medicines

Cost sharing (User fees) has been the primary mechanism for cost recovery in public facilities.

10.
A written order by an account holder with the bank (drawer) to pay on demand a specified amount to a named person (bearer) is referred to as a
A. Receipt
B. Cheque
C. Invoice
D. Voucher
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B. Cheque

This is the standard financial definition of a cheque.

11.
In accounting, the following are reasons for sending a Debit Note to a buyer except one: Which one?
A. If there are mistakes in the calculation
B. If there are omitted items in the invoice
C. If price of items have been undercharged
D. When goods are returned to the seller
Show Answer

Correct Answer: D. When goods are returned to the seller

When goods are returned, the seller owes the buyer money, so a Credit Note is issued. A Debit Note is issued when the buyer owes more (e.g., undercharge).

12.
In direct taxation
A. the real burden of paying the tax falls directly on person responsible for paying them.
B. Real burden of payment of tax can be pushed from firm to consumers.
C. Real burden is shared between firm and customers.
D. None of the above.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A. the real burden of paying the tax falls directly on person responsible for paying them.

Direct taxes (like Income Tax) cannot be shifted to others, unlike indirect taxes (like VAT) which are passed to consumers.

13.
Indirect taxes are mostly based on?
A. Capital & incomes.
B. Tobacco & alcohols.
C. Exports & Imports.
D. None of the above.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B. Tobacco & alcohols.

These attract Excise Duty, a common form of indirect tax. Capital & incomes (A) attract direct tax.

14.
Salaries of health personnel, construction of health facilities, clinics and the purchase of medical supplies and drugs can be referred to as?
A. Outputs.
B. Processes.
C. Inputs.
D. Impacts
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C. Inputs.

These are the resources (Human, Physical, Financial) put into the system to enable service delivery.

15.
Fees paid voluntarily by the public in return for a service or goods provided by the government is referred to as?
A. User charges.
B. Taxes.
C. per diem.
D. Revenue.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A. User charges.

Unlike taxes which are mandatory levies without direct exchange, user charges are paid specifically for a service received (e.g., paying for an X-ray).

20.
In health information systems, mortality, morbidity, disease outbreaks, health statistics and disabilities are referred to as?
A. Health determinants
B. Health inequities.
C. Health outcomes.
D. Health system outputs.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C. Health outcomes.

These measure the final status of health (death, disease state) resulting from health interventions.

21.
Data generated in health information systems should be?
A. Reliable and authoritative.
B. Usable and comparative.
C. Comprehensive and of high quality.
D. All of the above.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: D. All of the above.

High-quality health data must possess all these characteristics to be useful for decision-making.

22.
Consider the following true sentence: "The Kenyan hospital network is oversized and concentrated in large towns: it absorbs a large proportion of available resources". Based on the above remarks which of the following statements is correct?
A. The Kenya hospital network suffers from severe shortage of resources.
B. The Kenyan health sector suffers from severe allocative efficiency.
C. Existing hospitals should be rehabilitated and fitted with state-of-the-art equipment.
D. Large hospitals are necessarily the main healthcare providers in urban settings.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B. The Kenyan health sector suffers from severe allocative efficiency (Inefficiency).

Allocative efficiency refers to distributing resources to where they are most needed (usually primary care). Concentration in urban hospitals indicates poor allocative efficiency.

23.
When government allocates funds for buying drugs, equipment & payment of salaries to health staff, it is referred to as?
A. Recurrent expenditure.
B. Capital expenditure.
C. Emergency expenditure.
D. None of the above.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A. Recurrent expenditure.

However, note that "equipment" is often Capital. Salaries and drugs (consumables) are Recurrent. If the question implies routine replenishment and operations, it leans to Recurrent. Typically, major equipment is capital.

24.
Building and equipping a clinic to service a community is a good example of
A. Recurrent expenditure.
B. Capital expenditure.
C. Emergency expenditure.
D. None of the above.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B. Capital expenditure.

Construction and initial equipping create fixed assets, which is capital expenditure.

25.
One of the following is not a principle of good budgeting
A. Comprehensiveness.
B. Transparency.
C. Periodicity.
D. Equitability
Show Answer

Correct Answer: D. Equitability

While equity is a policy goal, standard technical budgeting principles (e.g., by OECD/World Bank) explicitly list Comprehensiveness, Transparency, Accuracy, Authoritativeness, and Periodicity. "Equitability" is an outcome of the budget, not a process principle.

26.
Allocative efficiency in health financing refers to?
A. Budgets being sustainable over the mid-term and beyond.
B. Agencies producing goods and services at affordable costs which can compete with market prices.
C. Expenditure being based on government priorities and effectiveness of public programme
D. Budget totals should be the result of explicit, enforced decisions;
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C. Expenditure being based on government priorities and effectiveness of public programme

Allocative efficiency is about spending on the "right things" (priorities) to maximize impact.

27.
The ratio of resources spent by government agencies on healthcare financing to the outputs produced by them is called?
A. Allocative efficiency
B. Operational efficiency.
C. Aggregate fiscal discipline
D. Medium Term Expenditure
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B. Operational efficiency.

Operational (or technical) efficiency focuses on the input-output ratio—getting the most output for a given input.

28.
When the Auditor General focuses on government accounts to check on accuracy and fairness in expenditure, it is referred to as a
A. Financial audit.
B. Value-for-money audit.
C. Resource audit.
D. None of the above.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A. Financial audit.

Financial audits focus on accuracy, compliance, and fair representation of accounts.

29.
Auditing that focuses on the three E's of economy, efficiency and effectiveness is referred to as
A. Resource audit.
B. Value for money audit.
C. Financial audit.
D. None of the above.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B. Value for money audit.

The 3 Es are the definition of Value for Money (Performance) Auditing.

30.
Which of the following is not an indicator of a country's capacity to validate, synthesize and analyze health data?
A. There is a functional institution designated to handle all health statistics.
B. There is a national microdata archive on health surveys.
C. Country has a 10 year costed survey plan that covers all priority health topics.
D. A burden of disease study has been conducted within the last 5 years by national stakeholders.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: D. A burden of disease study has been conducted within the last 5 years by national stakeholders.

According to standard M&E capacity frameworks (e.g., World Bank/WHO), having institutions (A), archives (B), and plans (C) are structural capacity indicators. Conducting a specific study (D) is an output or activity of that capacity, not the capacity itself.
