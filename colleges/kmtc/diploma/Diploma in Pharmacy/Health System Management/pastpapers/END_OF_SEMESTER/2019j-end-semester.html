Section A: Multiple Choice Questions

30 Marks
1.
The following are the common project management functions EXCEPT:
A. Planning
B. Control
C. Team development
D. Delegation
Show Answer

Correct Answer: D. Delegation

Delegation is a skill or tool used within management, but Planning, Control, and Team Development are considered core functions of project management.

2.
All following are the major principles of quality management EXCEPT:
A. Process approach
B. Focus on measurement
C. Focus on customer
D. Interpersonal relations
E. Continual improvement
Show Answer

Correct Answer: D. Interpersonal relations

Standard Quality Management principles (ISO 9001) include Customer Focus, Process Approach, Improvement, and Evidence-based Decision Making (Measurement). "Interpersonal relations" is not a formal principle of QM, though relevant to leadership.

3.
The following are examples of tools / techniques used in measuring quality. Which tool is also called the cause and effect diagram?
A. Flow charts
B. Bench marking
C. Fish bone diagram
D. Pareto charts
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C. Fish bone diagram

The Fishbone diagram is also known as the Ishikawa diagram or Cause-and-Effect diagram.

4.

A vote book is used to record and monitor expenditure in the public sector. Which of the following are the characteristics of a vote book?

  1. Ensure no extra budget spending
  2. Highlight unnecessary expenditure
  3. Shows balance available on expenditure
  4. The claimant can only spend what they have
  5. Allows only replenishment of what is spent
A. I, II, III
B. I, II, IV
C. I, IV, V
D. I, II, V, IV
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A. I, II, III (Note: Usually I, III, IV are key)

A Vote Book ensures expenditure does not exceed the vote (I), shows the running balance (III), and by doing so can highlight when expenditure is becoming unnecessary or excessive (II). Option IV is also true in principle (spending within limits).

5.
The concept of quality assurance concerns:
A. Quality design
B. Quality measuring
C. Quality improvement
D. All the above.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: D. All the above.

Quality Assurance is a comprehensive system that includes designing quality into the process, measuring performance against standards, and improving processes to prevent defects.

6.
The following are activities and sub activities involved in quality design EXCEPT:
A. Leadership development
B. Designing regulation mechanism
C. Evaluation and implementation
D. Developing standards
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C. Evaluation and implementation

Evaluation and Implementation are typically subsequent phases (checking and doing), whereas Leadership, Regulation design, and Standard setting are part of the initial Design phase.

A. Facility Improvement Fund
B. Health Sector Service Improvement Fund
C. Beyond Zero Campaign
D. National Hospital Insurance Fund

8.
The following are the indicators of a Health information system except;
A. Determinant of health
B. Health system inputs
C. Outputs
D. Planning frameworks
Show Answer

Correct Answer: D. Planning frameworks

Indicators measure determinants, inputs, outputs, outcomes, and impact. A "Planning framework" is a tool or structure for planning, not an indicator itself.

9.
Areas of evaluation of a project exists in the following EXCEPT:
A. Purpose
B. Programs
C. Staff
D. Financial administration
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C. Staff

While staff performance is appraised, Project Evaluation focuses on Purpose (relevance), Programs (effectiveness), and Finance (efficiency). Staff appraisal is usually distinct from project evaluation.

10.
The process of evaluating overall project performance on a regular basis to provide confidence that the project will satisfy the relevant standards is called;
A. Quality control
B. Quality assurance
C. Quality planning
D. Quality review
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B. Quality assurance

This is the PMI definition of Quality Assurance: auditing quality requirements and results to ensure appropriate standards are used.

11.
The following are purposes of a work breakdown schedule in Monitoring and Evaluation EXCEPT:
A. Assigning responsibilities
B. Initiating the project
C. Resource allocation
D. Controlling the project

12.

Project planning process involves all the following processes. What is the formal order of the planning process?

  1. Analysis of the situation
  2. Priorities setting
  3. Assessment of resources
  4. Formulation of Plan
  5. Implementation
  6. Evaluation
  7. Monitoring
A. I, III, II, IV, V, VII, VI
B. I, II, IV, V, VII, VI, III
C. II, IV, I, III, V, VII
D. None of the above sequences
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A. I, III, II, IV, V, VII, VI

Logical flow: Analyze situation -> Assess Resources -> Set Priorities -> Formulate Plan -> Implement -> Monitor -> Evaluate.

13.

18.

A. The timeliness of a programs activities
B. How closely a program kept to its budget
C. The outcomes and input of a programs activities

Correct Answer: C. The outcomes and input of a programs activities

19.
The following are examples of provider payment mechanisms in health financing EXCEPT:
A. National budget
B. Per diem
C. Per capita
D. Line item
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A. National budget

The National Budget is a source of funds, not a payment mechanism to a provider. Capitation (Per capita), Per diem, and Line Item budgets are methods of paying providers.

20.
Which of the following indicators is used for measuring output during monitoring?
A. Use of inputs and sustained production of benefits
B. Difference made from original problem situation

D. Number of hours worked by project personnel

Correct Answer: C. Extent of implemented activities

Output monitoring measures the immediate results of activities (e.g., number of activities implemented). Difference made (B) is impact.

32.
An imprest is an example of a financial accounting system. List some of the general characteristics of an imprest system. (5 marks)
Show Answer

Answer:

  1. Fixed Float: A set amount of money is established.
  2. Replenishment: The fund is topped up by the exact amount spent.
  3. Receipts Required: Every expenditure must be supported by a voucher/receipt.
  4. Accountability: The holder is personally liable for shortages.
  5. Petty Expenses: Used for small, low-value cash payments.

Distinguish between monitoring and evaluation with reference to health system management. (5 marks)
Show Answer

Answer:

  • Monitoring: Continuous process, focuses on activities/inputs/outputs (efficiency), internal, aims at immediate correction ("Are we doing things right?").

Section C: Long Answer Question

20 Marks (Answer Any 1)
41.

a) With the use of relevant examples, discuss the following topics with reference to monitoring and evaluation:

i. Tools of monitoring (5 marks)

ii. The major goals of conducting monitoring of a health project. (5 marks)

b) Describe the following types of evaluation; (10 marks)

i. Participatory evaluation

ii. Process based evaluation

iii. Outcome based evaluation

iv. Goal based evaluation

v. Self-evaluation (360 degrees evaluation)

Show Answer

Answer:

a) i. Tools of Monitoring:

  • Gantt Charts: Visual timeline for tracking schedule progress.
  • Logframe Matrix: Tracks indicators against objectives.
  • Field Visits/Checklists: Direct observation of activities.
  • Progress Reports: Periodic written updates (monthly/quarterly).
  • Budget Tracking Sheets: Monitors financial expenditure.

a) ii. Goals of Monitoring:

  • To ensure activities are implemented as planned (compliance).
  • To provide early warning of problems (risk management).
  • To ensure resources are used efficiently (accountability).
  • To provide feedback for management decision-making.
  • To motivate staff through performance tracking.

b) Types of Evaluation:

  • Participatory: Involves stakeholders (beneficiaries, staff) in the evaluation process to build ownership.
  • Process-based: Evaluates how the program was implemented (fidelity to plan, operations).
  • Outcome-based: Measures the immediate effects/changes in the target population (e.g., increased knowledge).
  • Goal-based: Measures if the ultimate objectives/goals were met.
  • Self-evaluation: Internal assessment by the project team or individual on their own performance.
42.

a) Discuss Financial resource management under the following headings:

i. The role of parliament in financial management (3 marks)

ii. The functions of the Auditor General and the Treasury (3 marks)

iii. The functions of the County government (4 marks)

b) Describe the following financial accounting systems and terminologies:

i. Per diem (3 marks)

ii. Imprest system (2 marks)

iii. Facility improvement fund (3 marks)

iv. Vouchers system (2 marks)

Show Answer

Answer:

a) Financial Resource Management:

  • i. Parliament: Approves the national budget (appropriation), provides oversight through committees (PAC/PIC), and legislates financial laws (PFM Act).
  • ii. Auditor General & Treasury: Auditor General audits public accounts to ensure lawful and effective use of funds. Treasury formulates financial policy, manages the consolidated fund, and oversees budget implementation.
  • iii. County Government: Prepares county budgets, collects local revenue (cess/fees), manages county funds, and ensures service delivery (health, roads) using allocated resources.

b) Terminologies:

  • i. Per Diem: Daily allowance paid to employees traveling for work to cover meals and accommodation. It is a fixed rate based on job group and location.
  • ii. Imprest System: A method of advancing cash for specific purposes which must be accounted for (surrendered) before getting more.
  • iii. Facility Improvement Fund (FIF): Revenue generated by health facilities (user fees) that is retained or ring-fenced for use by that specific facility to improve its own services (buy drugs, repairs).
  • iv. Vouchers System: Use of authorized documents (payment vouchers) to approve and record expenditure, serving as evidence of the transaction.
