1.
By studying the various approaches to management analysis, we can understand the concept of management and have a better understanding of managerial functions. Which approach to management emphasizes managing people by understanding their individual psychological needs?
A. Empirical Approach
B. Contingency Approach
C. Management Science Approach
D. Interpersonal Behavior Approach
Show Answer

Correct Answer: D. Interpersonal Behavior Approach

This approach focuses on interpersonal behavior, human relations, leadership, and motivation, emphasizing that management involves getting things done through people.

2.
Forces/traits within the manager that determine effective leadership behavior include his/her:
I. Values.
II. Confidence in subordinates.
III. Hunger for power.
IV. Aggressiveness.
A. Both (I) and (II) above
B. Both (I), (II) and (IV) above
C. Both (III) and (IV) above
D. (I) and (IV) above
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A. Both (I) and (II) above

Effective leadership is typically driven by positive values and trust (confidence) in the team, rather than negative traits like hunger for power or aggressiveness.

3.
One of the following statements is not true as concerns organizational culture:
A. Cultures are uniform across organizations
B. It is the social glue that binds organization members together
C. It includes reward systems, risk-taking and treatment of employees
D. It helps employees to make sense of the organization
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A. Cultures are uniform across organizations

Organizational cultures are unique to each entity; they are distinct personalities of organizations and vary significantly from one to another.

4.
Which of the following statements concerning grapevine is not correct?
A. Grapevine can and often does generate harmful rumors
B. Grapevine is used largely to serve the self-interests of people within it
C. Some managers use grapevine to their advantage
D. In time, and with proper pressure, grapevine can be eliminated.

Correct Answer: D. In time, and with proper pressure, grapevine can be eliminated.

The grapevine (informal communication) is a natural part of human interaction in any group and cannot be completely eliminated, though it can be managed.

5.
The conflict-resolution approach that corresponds with a high level of assertiveness and a low level of cooperativeness, is referred to as:
A. Compromising
B. Collaborating

Correct Answer: C. Forcing

Forcing (or Competing) involves pursuing one's own concerns at the other person's expense (High Assertiveness, Low Cooperativeness).

6.

7.
Identify the sequence of different steps in the function of Controlling.
I. Comparison of actual and standard performance
II. Measurement of actual performance.
III. Corrective action plan.
IV. Analysis of causes of deviation.
A. (III), (IV), (I), (II)
B. (II), (III), (IV), (I)
C. (I), (II), (III), (IV)
D. (II), (I), (IV), (III)
Show Answer

Correct Answer: D. (II), (I), (IV), (III)

The standard control process is: 1. Establish Standards (implied) -> 2. Measure Performance (II) -> 3. Compare with Standard (I) -> 4. Analyze Deviations (IV) -> 5. Take Corrective Action (III).

8.
"When managers have substantial but not complete confidence and trust in subordinates and solicit advice from subordinates while retaining the right to make final decision", Likert's four systems of management describes it as:
A. Participative leadership style
B. Trait theory of leadership style
C. Consultative leadership style
D. Laisez-faire leadership style
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C. Consultative leadership style

This describes Likert's "System 3" (Consultative), where managers seek input but hold the final decision-making power.

9.
Which of the following is/are postulates of the path - goal theory?
I. The leader clearly defines the path to goal attainment for subordinates.
II. The leader motivates subordinates to participate in decision-making.
III. The leader sets clear and specific goals for subordinates.
IV. The leader suitably rewards employees as per their performance.
A. Only (II) above
B. Only (IV) above
C. Both (II) and (IV) above
D. All (I), (II), (III) and (IV) above.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: D. All (I), (II), (III) and (IV) above.

Path-Goal theory involves the leader defining goals, clarifying the path, removing obstacles, and providing rewards (expectancy theory basis). Participative leadership is one of the styles within Path-Goal theory.

10.
Span of control an important factor, which is to be taken into account when undertaking organizational design. Maintaining a large span of control within an organization is most effective in all except one of the following situations?
A. When subordinates prefer autonomy
B. When tasks are routine
C. When jobs are similar but have varying performance measures
D. When managers are competent.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C. When jobs are similar but have varying performance measures

A large span of control works well for routine tasks, competent managers, and autonomous subordinates. However, if performance measures vary significantly even for similar jobs, it requires more close supervision, making a wide span less effective.

11.
Management By Objectives (MBO) is a system for achieving organizational objectives, enhancement of employee commitment and participation. Which of the following is not an advantage of MBO?
A. Flexibility
B. Clarity in organizational action
C. Personnel satisfaction

Correct Answer: A. Flexibility

Section 3: Long Answer Questions

20 Marks (Answer Any TWO)
1.
'If you fail to plan, you plan to fail', In light of this statement, discuss the steps involved in planning in an organization (10 mks)
Show Answer

Answer:

The planning process typically involves the following steps:

  1. Analyzing Opportunities (Situational Analysis): Assessing the internal and external environment (SWOT analysis) to understand where the organization stands.
  2. Establishing Objectives: Setting clear, measurable goals for the entire organization and for each subordinate unit.
  3. Determining Planning Premises: Making assumptions about the future environment (e.g., markets, prices, policies) in which plans will operate.
  4. Identifying Alternatives: Searching for and examining alternative courses of action to achieve the objectives.
  5. Evaluating Alternatives: Weighing the pros and cons of each alternative in light of premises and goals.
  6. Selecting a Course of Action: Making the actual decision; adopting the plan.
  7. Formulating Derivative Plans: Creating supportive plans (e.g., buying equipment, hiring staff) to ensure the main plan succeeds.
  8. Budgeting: Quantifying the plan by allocating financial resources.
2.
In his Bureaucratic Theory of Management, Marx Weber stressed that there is need for a strictly defined hierarchy of management, governed by clearly defined rules, regulations and lines of authority. Discuss five advantages that would be gained by an organization practicing such bureaucracy (10 mks)
Show Answer

Answer:

  1. Specialization: Division of labor leads to expertise and efficiency in specific tasks.
  2. Structure and Clarity: Clear hierarchy ensures everyone knows who they report to and what their role is, reducing confusion.
  3. Rationality and Predictability: Decisions are based on rules, not personal whims, leading to consistent outcomes.
  4. Impersonality: Rules are applied equally to everyone, reducing favoritism and bias in treatment of employees and clients.
  5. Record Keeping: Emphasis on written documents ensures an institutional memory and continuity even if staff leave.
  6. Merit-based Advancement: Promotion is based on technical competence and qualifications rather than connections.
3.
Discuss the stages of Group Development (10 mks)
Show Answer

Answer: (Based on Bruce Tuckman's Model)

  1. Forming: The team meets and learns about the opportunity and challenges, and then agrees on goals and begins to tackle the tasks. Members tend to behave quite independently.
  2. Storming: The group starts to sort itself out and gain each other's trust. This stage often involves conflict as members compete for status and differing ideas on how to proceed.
  3. Norming: The team resolves disagreements, personality clashes decrease, and a spirit of co-operation emerges. Roles and responsibilities are clear and accepted.
  4. Performing: The team is now knowledgeable, motivated, and competent. They can function autonomously with high efficiency and shared vision.
  5. Adjourning (Mourning): The project ends, and the team disbands. This involves wrapping up tasks and recognizing members' contributions.
4.
Citing relevant examples, discuss the importance of doing a Performance Appraisal in an organization? (10 mks)
Show Answer

Answer:

  1. Performance Improvement: Feedback allows employees to correct poor performance (e.g., a nurse improving patient charting accuracy after review).
  2. Compensation Adjustments: Helps determine who deserves pay raises or bonuses based on merit.
  3. Placement Decisions: Identifying talent for promotion, transfer, or even termination (e.g., promoting a high-performing pharmacist to manager).
  4. Training Needs Assessment: Identifying skill gaps that require training (e.g., noticing a staff member struggles with new software).
  5. Career Planning: Helping employees set career goals and path within the organization.
  6. Documentation: Providing legal evidence for HR decisions (e.g., justification for firing due to incompetence).
