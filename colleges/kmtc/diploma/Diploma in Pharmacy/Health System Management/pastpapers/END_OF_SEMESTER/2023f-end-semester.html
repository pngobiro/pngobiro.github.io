Section 1: Multiple Choice Questions

40 Marks
1.
The statement "Any collection of components that work together to achieve a common objective" best describe as a:
A. System
B. Health System
C. Information
D. Management
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A. System

A system is defined as a set of interacting or interdependent component parts forming a complex/intricate whole to achieve a common goal.

2.
What the difference between an M&E framework, a performance framework and a performance monitoring plan (PMP)?
A. The M&E framework measures program performance and the others measure staff performance
B. Only the M&E framework has inputs and process, outputs, outcomes and impact
C. They all serve the same purpose
D. The PMP is the only one that details on indicators and means of measuring them
Show Answer

Correct Answer: D. The PMP is the only one that details on indicators and means of measuring them

While frameworks (like Results Frameworks) show the logic of change, a Performance Monitoring Plan (PMP) is a specific operational document that defines the exact indicators, data sources, frequency, and responsibility for measurement.

3.
Meaningless point in space and time, without reference to its surroundings is referred to
A. Knowledge
B. Data
C. Information
D. Wisdom
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B. Data

Data consists of raw facts and figures without context. Once processed and given context, it becomes information.

C. Encourage common data collection methods, uniform analysis and joint annual reviews in a country

5.

6.

A. Design
B. Testing
C. System
D. Logic
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C. System

Quality management is a systematic approach (a system) that encompasses all activities of the overall management function that determine the quality policy, objectives, and responsibilities.

7.
The ultimate objective of health management information system is to improve health through:
A. Evidence based decision making
B. Providing management tool
C. Option appraisal

Correct Answer: A. Evidence based decision making

The primary goal of HMIS is to produce relevant and timely information that allows managers to make informed, evidence-based decisions to improve health services.

8.
Evaluations measure:
A. The timeliness of a program's activities.
B. The outcomes and impact of a program's activities.
C. How closely a program kept to its budget.
D. How well the program was implemented.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B. The outcomes and impact of a program's activities.

Evaluation focuses on effectiveness, outcomes, and impact ("Are we doing the right things?"), whereas monitoring focuses on timeliness and implementation ("Are we doing things right?").

14.
Which of the following records pertaining to the health is more comprehensive?
A. Electronic Medical Record (EMR)
B. Electronic Health Record (HER)

15.
All the following are limitations paper record except
A. The handwriting is unreadable
B. Expensive to copy
C. Easy to analyze
D. Missing of important documents
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C. Easy to analyze

Paper records are notoriously difficult to analyze (requiring manual data entry), whereas digital records allow for easy aggregation and analysis. Thus, "Easy to analyze" is NOT a limitation of paper records (it's actually false/the opposite of reality).

21.

41.
Describe five disadvantages of an e-learning system (5 marks)
Show Answer

Answer:

  1. Technology Dependence: Requires reliable internet and devices; technical issues can disrupt learning.
  2. Lack of Social Interaction: Reduced face-to-face interaction can lead to feelings of isolation and lack of peer support.
  3. Limited Practical Application: Difficult to teach hands-on clinical or technical skills purely online.
  4. Self-Discipline Required: Students must be highly motivated; procrastination is easier than in a classroom setting.
  5. Cost of Access: High cost of data bundles or acquiring computers/tablets can be a barrier for some students.
42.
Outline five reasons why resources mobilization is critical in health care organization (5 marks)
Show Answer

Answer:

  1. Sustainability: Ensures the organization can continue operations in the long term.
  2. Service Expansion: Allows for the introduction of new services or reaching more patients.
  3. Quality Improvement: Funds are needed to upgrade equipment, train staff, and improve facilities.
  4. Financial Independence: Reduces over-reliance on a single donor or government source.
  5. Staff Motivation: Adequate resources ensure staff are paid well and have the tools they need to work effectively.

44.
State five factors affecting quality assurance (5 marks)
Show Answer

Answer:

  1. Resource Availability: Lack of equipment, drugs, or funds compromises quality.
  2. Staff Competence: Quality depends on the skills and training of the workforce.
  3. Management Support: Leadership commitment is essential for enforcing standards.
  4. Workload: High patient-to-staff ratios can lead to errors and burnout.
  5. Infrastructure: Poor facilities (e.g., lack of water/electricity) hinder safe care.

46.

Show Answer

47.
Differentiate between quality assurance and quality control (5 marks)
Show Answer

Answer:

  • Focus: Quality Assurance (QA) is process-oriented (focuses on preventing defects), while Quality Control (QC) is product-oriented (focuses on identifying defects).
  • Timing: QA is proactive (done before/during), while QC is reactive (done after production).
  • Scope: QA involves the whole system/management, while QC is a specific technical function.
  • Goal: QA aims to improve the process so defects don't arise; QC aims to identify defects before they reach the customer.

Section 3: Long Answer Question

20 Marks
48.

Covid and drought are some of the major disasters in our country:

a. State types of disasters and give two examples each (4 mks)

b. List four psychological effects of disasters (4 mks)

c. Describe four strategies that could be put in place to manage and control the impact of COVID within an organization (8 mks)

d. Explain four disaster management phases (4mks)

Show Answer

Answer:

a. Types of Disasters:

  • Natural Disasters: Caused by natural forces. Examples: Earthquakes, Floods, Droughts, Volcanic Eruptions.
  • Man-made (Technological) Disasters: Caused by human error or intent. Examples: Industrial accidents, Oil spills, Terrorist attacks, Transport accidents.

b. Psychological effects of disasters:

  1. Post-Traumatic Stress Disorder (PTSD)
  2. Anxiety and panic attacks
  3. Depression
  4. Grief and bereavement
  5. Sleep disturbances/Insomnia

c. Strategies to manage and control COVID-19 in an organization:

  1. Infection Prevention Control (IPC) Protocols: Implementing mandatory hand hygiene, wearing of masks, and regular disinfection of surfaces.
  2. Social Distancing Measures: Reorganizing workspaces to ensure safe distance, limiting meeting sizes, and encouraging remote work where possible.
  3. Health Screening and Surveillance: conducting temperature checks at entry points and encouraging staff to report symptoms/stay home if sick.
  4. Education and Sensitization: Regularly training staff on risks, prevention methods, and company policies regarding the pandemic.

d. Disaster Management Phases:

  1. Mitigation: Activities designed to reduce the risk or likelihood of a disaster occurring (prevention) or to minimize its potential impact (e.g., building codes, vaccination).
  2. Preparedness: Planning and organizing to ensure an effective response when a disaster strikes (e.g., stockpiling supplies, training drills).
  3. Response: Immediate actions taken during or immediately after a disaster to save lives and property (e.g., search and rescue, emergency medical care).
  4. Recovery: Long-term activities to restore the community to normal or better conditions (e.g., reconstruction, psychological support, economic rehabilitation).
