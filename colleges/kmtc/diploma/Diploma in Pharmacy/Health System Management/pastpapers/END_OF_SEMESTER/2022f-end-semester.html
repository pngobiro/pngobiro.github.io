Section 1: Multiple Choice Questions

1.
The role of the Health Information System (HIS) is:
A. Collection of health services data to facilitate evidence based decision-making at all levels especially at the point of collection.
B. Planning and management of health services and programmes through collection of accurate data in the facilities.
C. Collection of health service data, Planning and management of health services the programmes for quality care.
D. Health planning based on information and strategies designed to redress the identified inequalities in health care.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C. Collection of health service data, Planning and management of health services the programmes for quality care

HIS is a system that integrates data collection, processing, reporting, and use of the information necessary for improving health service effectiveness and efficiency.

2.
In commodity management cycle, Product Selection is:
A. The process of identifying which commodity should be made available as per the national guidelines.
B. This is the process of obtaining the required medicines and supplies through purchase, donations or manufacturing.
C. The process transferring commodities from a storage facility/supplier to a point of use or from one point of use to another.
D. The process that includes dispensing the prescribed medicines to patients, patient for efficacy and compliance.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A. The process of identifying which commodity should be made available as per the national guidelines.

Selection involves defining what products are necessary to meet basic health needs (e.g., Essential Medicines List). Option B is Procurement, C is Distribution, D is Use.

3.
The following is the correct sequence of commodity management cycle:
A. Distribution, product selection, procurement, use
B. Product selection, procurement, distribution, use
C. Procurement, distribution, product selection, use
D. Distribution, product selection, use, procurement
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B. Product selection, procurement, distribution, use

The standard cycle starts with deciding what you need (Selection), buying it (Procurement), moving it to where it's needed (Distribution), and finally administering it (Use).

A. Integration, durability, continuity
B. Affordability, acceptability, serviceability

Correct Answer: C. Acceptability, relevance, continuity

Section 2: Short Answer Questions

40 Marks
1.
Discuss Five (5) Types of Procurement Methods
Show Answer

Answer:

  1. Open Tender: The preferred method where the opportunity is advertised publicly, allowing all interested and qualified suppliers to compete.
  2. Restricted Tender: Competition is limited to a selected number of suppliers who are invited to tender, usually for specialized goods.
  3. Direct Procurement (Single Sourcing): Buying from a specific supplier without competition, used in emergencies or when only one supplier exists.
  4. Request for Quotations (RFQ): Used for low-value, standard goods. Quotations are requested from at least three suppliers.
  5. Low Value Procurement: For very small purchases where administrative costs of other methods would be too high.
2.
Explain Five (5) Sources of Healthcare Financing
Show Answer

Answer:

  1. Government Revenue (Taxation): Allocations from the national budget derived from taxes.
  2. Social Health Insurance (NHIF): Mandatory contributions from formal sector employees and voluntary from informal sector.
  3. Out-of-Pocket Payments (User Fees): Direct payments made by patients at the point of service.
  4. Private Health Insurance: Voluntary premiums paid to private insurance companies.
  5. Donor Funding (External Aid): Grants and loans from international organizations (e.g., USAID, Global Fund).
3.
Describe Any Five (5) Principles of Total Quality Management
Show Answer

Answer:

  1. Customer Focus: Meeting and exceeding customer expectations is the primary goal.
  2. Leadership: Leaders establish unity of purpose and create an environment for people to engage in achieving objectives.
  3. Engagement of People: Competent, empowered, and engaged people at all levels are essential.
  4. Process Approach: Consistent results are achieved more efficiently when activities are managed as interrelated processes.
  5. Continual Improvement: A permanent objective of the organization is to improve performance.
4.
Explain Five (5) Objectives of a Health Information System
Show Answer

Answer:

  1. To provide reliable, timely, and relevant data for evidence-based decision making.
  2. To facilitate monitoring and evaluation of health programs and services.
  3. To support disease surveillance and outbreak response.
  4. To assist in resource allocation and planning.
  5. To enhance accountability and transparency in the health sector.

Section 3: Long Answer Question

20 Marks
1.
Discuss The Various Activities in Each of the Disaster Management Phases
Show Answer

Answer:

The Disaster Management Cycle consists of four main phases:

1. Mitigation (Prevention): Activities to prevent a disaster or reduce its impact.

  • Risk assessment and vulnerability analysis.
  • Land-use planning and zoning to avoid hazardous areas.
  • Building codes and structural reinforcements.
  • Public awareness and education campaigns.

2. Preparedness: Planning to ensure an effective response when a disaster strikes.

  • Developing emergency response plans.
  • Stockpiling supplies (food, medicine, equipment).
  • Training response teams and conducting drills/simulations.
  • Setting up early warning systems and communication networks.

3. Response: Immediate actions taken during or immediately after impact to save lives.

  • Search and rescue operations.
  • Emergency medical care and triage.
  • Provision of food, water, and temporary shelter.
  • Evacuation of affected populations.

4. Recovery: Actions to restore normalcy and rebuild better.

  • Rehabilitation: Restoring essential services (power, water, roads).
  • Reconstruction: Rebuilding infrastructure and housing.
  • Psychological support and trauma counseling.
  • Economic recovery programs to restore livelihoods.
