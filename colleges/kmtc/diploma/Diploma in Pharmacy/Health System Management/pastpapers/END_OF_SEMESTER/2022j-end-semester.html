Which one of the following is not a component of project cycle?

b) Financing

Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Determination

The standard project cycle includes Programming (Identification), Formulation/Preparation, Appraisal, Financing, Implementation, and Evaluation. "Determination" is not a standard phase name in this context.

2.
The following are principles of project management except
a) Consistency
b) Effective stakeholder management
c) Flexibility
d) Sustainability
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Flexibility (Note: Context dependent)

While projects need to adapt, "Consistency" (in methodology), "Stakeholder Management", and "Sustainability" are core pillars. However, in some contexts, flexibility is a virtue. Often, Consistency implies rigidity which opposes modern agile management, but standard PM principles usually list defined roles, management by exception, etc. In the context of older curriculum, Flexibility might be framed as a lack of control, or Consistency might be the answer if referring to the "Iron Triangle" rigidity. Based on standard KMTC notes, Consistency is often a goal (quality), making Flexibility the "except" in rigid frameworks, though arguably all are good. Let's select c) Flexibility as the likely intended answer for "principles" which usually emphasize structure.

42.
State and explain the Bruce Tuckman's four stages of team development (8mks)
Show Answer

Answer:

  1. Forming: The team meets and learns about the opportunities and challenges, and then agrees on goals and begins to tackle the tasks. Team members tend to behave quite independently.
  2. Storming: This is the second stage of team development, where the group starts to sort itself out and gain each other's trust. This stage often involves conflict as members vie for position and control.
  3. Norming: Resolved disagreements and personality clashes result in greater intimacy, and a spirit of co-operation emerges. Roles and responsibilities are clear and accepted.
  4. Performing: The team is now more strategically aware; the team knows clearly why it is doing what it is doing. The team has a shared vision and is able to stand on its own feet with no interference or participation from the leader.
43.
Make an explanation on the structure of healthcare system with focus on services provided at different levels (10mks)
Show Answer

Answer:

The Kenya Health System is structured into six levels of care:

  • Level 1: Community Health Services: Focuses on health promotion and disease prevention at the village level. Services are provided by Community Health Volunteers (CHVs) and include hygiene education, immunization mobilization, and basic first aid.
  • Level 2: Dispensaries/Clinics: The first physical interface with the health system. Provides basic curative services (treatment of common ailments like malaria, flu), preventive services (ANC, immunization), and pharmacy services. Managed by nurses/clinical officers.
  • Level 3: Health Centres: Provide comprehensive primary care. In addition to Level 2 services, they offer maternity services (labor wards), minor theater services, basic laboratory diagnostics, and limited inpatient capacity.
  • Level 4: County Referral Hospitals: Provide holistic services including X-ray, ultrasound, surgical theater, specialized clinics (dental, eye), and substantial inpatient wards. They act as referral centers for lower levels.
  • Level 5: County Referral Hospitals (Former Provincials): Offer specialized consultant services, training, and research. They handle complex cases referred from Level 4 and have ICUs, HDUs, and specialized surgeons.
  • Level 6: National Referral Hospitals: The apex of the system (e.g., KNH, MTRH). They offer highly specialized services (neuro-surgery, oncology, renal transplants), conduct advanced research, and formulate national health policies.

Section 3: Long Answer Question

47.

In this question:

a) Define project cycle (1mk)

b) Explain the phases of project cycle (7mks)

c) What is the importance of project planning and design (5mks)

d) List 5 principles of project management (5mks)

e) Discuss any two types of tools used in planning (2mks)

Show Answer

Answer:

a) Project Cycle: The sequence of phases that a project goes through from its initiation to its closure.

b) Phases of Project Cycle:

  1. Identification: Determining the needs or problems that the project aims to address.
  2. Preparation/Design: Developing the project details, including objectives, activities, budget, and schedule.
  3. Appraisal: Assessing the feasibility and viability of the project before approval.
  4. Implementation: Executing the planned activities to achieve the project outputs.
  5. Monitoring: Continuous tracking of progress against the plan during implementation.
  6. Evaluation: Assessing the outcomes and impacts of the project after completion.

c) Importance of Project Planning and Design:

  1. Resource Allocation: Ensures resources (money, time, people) are used efficiently.
  2. Risk Management: Helps identify potential risks early and plan mitigation strategies.
  3. Clear Direction: Provides a roadmap for the team, ensuring everyone understands the goals and their roles.
  4. Performance Measurement: Establishes baselines and targets against which progress can be measured.
  5. Stakeholder Alignment: Ensures all stakeholders agree on the project scope and deliverables before work begins.

d) Principles of Project Management:

  1. Clear goals and objectives.
  2. Defined roles and responsibilities.
  3. Manage by exception (intervene only when necessary).
  4. Focus on products/deliverables.
  5. Learn from experience (lessons learned).
  6. Tailor to suit the project environment.

e) Tools used in Planning:

  1. Gantt Chart: A bar chart that illustrates a project schedule, showing the start and finish dates of the terminal elements and summary elements of a project.
  2. Logical Framework Approach (LFA): A matrix used to design, execute, monitor, and evaluate projects, linking objectives to indicators and assumptions.
  3. Work Breakdown Structure (WBS): A hierarchical decomposition of the total scope of work to be carried out by the project team.
