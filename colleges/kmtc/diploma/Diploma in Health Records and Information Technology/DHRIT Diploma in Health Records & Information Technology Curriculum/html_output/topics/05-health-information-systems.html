07. MODULE TITLE: HEALTH INFORMATION SYSTEMS

Module Overview

TIME: 60 HOURS

CREDITS: 6 CREDITS

MODULE COMPETENCE

The module is designed to equip the learner with the necessary knowledge, skills and attitude for organizing and managing health information systems.

Units Covered:

  • UNIT 1: Introduction Health Information Systems (04 HOURS)
  • UNIT 2: Organization of Health Information in Kenya (04 HOURS)
  • UNIT 3: Health Sector Indicators (08 HOURS)
  • UNIT 4: Data collection tools in Health Information systems (14 HOURS)
  • UNIT 5: Sources of Health Information (04 HOURS)
  • UNIT 6: Use of computers in Health Information Systems (12 HOURS)
  • UNIT 7: Data Analysis and Interpretation (10 HOURS)
  • UNIT 8: Information Presentation and Dissemination (04 HOURS)

Module Outcomes

At the end of the module the learner will be able to:

  1. Introduce health information systems
  2. Describe the organization of health information systems in Kenya
  3. Discuss the health sector indicators
  4. Explain data collection tools in health information systems
  5. Describe the sources of health information
  6. Organize and manage the procedures for completing and forwarding health information services reports accurately and promptly
  7. Apply ICT in organizing and managing health information services reports
  8. Organize and manage the collection, collation, analysis, presentation, and dissemination of health information

Module Content

UNIT CODE; DHR 1107 HEALTH INFORMATION SYSTEMS - I (Time: 30 hours)

1: Introduction Health Information Systems

Description of health information; historical background, health information services in Kenya; concept of health information services (WHO); goals of health information systems; the value and importance of health information; the role of health information in managing health activities and socioeconomic activities; health information system resources; policies and regulation; outline the link between health information services and the user.

2: Organization of Health Information Systems in Kenya

Description of the tiers of care: Tier One - Community Level; Tier Two-dispensary and Health Centres; Tier Three -county and sub-county Hospitals; Tier Four-National referrals.

3: Health Sector Indicators

Definition; types of indicators; domains (input, process, output, impact); units of measure (rates, ratios); purpose; frequency; coverage and performance; data source (numerators and denominators); application level (sector, programme, national, county, facility, community, Millennium development goals [MDGs]).

UNIT CODE; DHR 1207 HEALTH INFORMATION SYSTEMS - II (Time: 30 hours)

1: Data Collection Tools in Health Information Systems

Description of national tools used in collection of health information; the sources of health information; types of tools; primary (registers, forms), secondary (weekly notification of infectious diseases, monthly notification of infectious diseases); outpatient morbidity report; inpatient morbidity mortality report forms; district outpatient morbidity summary forms; integrated RH, HIB, malaria, TB, and nutrition; vaccine and immunization; nutrition monthly reporting, HIV reporting tools; community Based Health Information System (CBHIS); district Health Information System (DHIS); Geographic Information system (GIS); Master Facilities List (MFL); procedures of completing HIS reporting tools.

2: Sources of Health Information

Explanation of sources of health information; surveys; demographic data; health activities; health status; resources, community information; statutory reporting tools (birth registration and notification; death registration ad notification); statutory agencies (central Bureau of Statistics, Attorney General Chambers).

UNIT CODE; DHR 2107 HEALTH INFORMATION SYSTEMS - III (Time: 30 hours)

1: Uses of computers in Health Information systems

Description of data handling; computer application; the role of computers in health information systems; application of relevant existing HIS software (EMR, DHIS, MFL); computer operations' preparation of data entry sheets; procedure of computer data input and output; various input media, computer operation; micro-and mini-computer; validation of data; data processing (analysis); Explanation of the various information output form computers.

2: Data Analysis and Interpretation

Computation of health indicators; target setting; response rate; immunization coverage rate; maternal and child health; maternal death rate; child survival rate; antenatal and postnatal coverage rate, family planning; acceptance rate; continuation rate; dropout rate; coverage rate; morbidity rates; prevalence and incidence rates; data interpretation; computer us in HIS.

3: Information Presentation and Dissemination

Outline of procedures of report writing and dissemination of health information; administrative; routine; the need for accuracy; thoroughness; timeliness and promptness of information; explanation of determination of suitable methods of information presentation; the importance of information usage for evidence-based decision making.
