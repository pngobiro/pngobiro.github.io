17. MODULE TITLE: HEALTH CARE DELIVERY

Module Overview

TIME: 40 HOURS

CREDITS: 4 CREDITS

MODULE COMPETENCE

This course is designed to equip the learner with the necessary knowledge, skills, and understand health care delivery in Kenya and other countries.

Units Covered:

  • UNIT 1: National health care delivery system (20 HOURS)
  • UNIT 2: Operations of health care delivery system (20 HOURS)

Module Outcomes

By the end of the unit the learner should be able to:

  1. Describe the Kenyan national health care delivery system
  2. Identify the determinants of an effective health care delivery system
  3. Explain the operations of health care delivery system
  4. Explain the regulations of health care service organization
  5. Explain the health care service planning

Module Content (UNIT CODE: DHR2211)

1: The Kenya national health care delivery system

Structure of the health care service organization, national, provincial, district, health centre, dispensary, the role of various agencies in health promotion, government, multi-laterals (e.g. WHO), bi-laterals (e.g. USAID), NGOs (e.g. AMREF), private providers (PO's), human resource, medical staff, administrative staff, supportive staff, health service programs, major goals of HSDP-III and the national chatters, maternal-child health/PHC, HIV/AIDS, malaria, tuberculosis and other diseases, determinants of an effective health care delivery system.

2: Operations of health care delivery system

Types of services provided, promotive, preventive, curative, rehabilitative, regulations of health care service organization, regulation, licensure, certification, registration, accreditation, professional association, health information system policies and procedures, health information related initiatives, health care service planning, description and importance of health service planning, health service process, situational analysis, setting goals, objectives and targets, strategy development, planning, implementation, evaluation, strategies used in health care planning, approaches to planning, resource identification, facilities, equipment, human resource, financial.

TEACHING METHODOLOGY

  • Lecture-centered
  • Discussion
  • Theoretical and practice training integrated
  • Consideration of both individuals and group based practices/exercises
  • Lecture
  • Group discussion
  • Visiting health facilities
  • Application exercise in a classroom
  • Practical exercise
  • Assignments
  • Independent study of Specified topics

RESOURCE CONDITIONS

  • Training, teaching, and learning materials
  • Computer lab with internet
  • Trainer's guide
  • Reference manuals and books
  • Learner's guide
  • HIS Policy, Kenya ICT Policy, NHSSP 2, HIS Strategic Plan, Kenya Vision 2030
  • Handout, Books, Current textbooks

17. MODULE TITLE: ENTREPRENEURSHIP SKILLS

Module Overview

TIME: 30 HOURS

CREDITS: 3 CREDITS

MODULE COMPETENCE

This module is designed to enable the learners to acquire the necessary knowledge, skills and attitudes on entrepreneurship and commitment necessary to successfully operate an entrepreneurial venture, review, challenges and rewards of entrepreneurship.

Units Covered:

  • UNIT 1: Introduction, concepts and roles of an entrepreneurship
  • UNIT 2: Entrepreneurship and economy
  • UNIT 3: Economic principles
  • UNIT 4: Business structures - registering a business, marketing of the service
  • UNIT 5: E- business in health information
  • UNIT 6: Writing a Business plan

Module Outcomes

At the end of the module, the learner will be able to:

  • Apply knowledge and skills in designing and implementing an entrepreneurial venture
  • Apply skills and knowledge on how to increase institution profit and competitive positions.

Module Content (UNIT CODE; CCU 1102)

What is entrepreneurship, the role of the entrepreneur, challenges of the entrepreneurship in different country, economic principles applicable to the business, profit and loss in a business, direct and indirect competition, Law of supply and demand, economic utility, product lifecycle, business ethics and legal issues, developing business ideas, personality and skills, buying and using inventory, business structures taxation and implications, writing a business plan, marketing, market penetration and brand image and marketing mix; advertising.

18. MODULE TITLE: FIRST AID & EMERGENCY MEDICINE

Module Overview

TIME: 30 HOURS

CREDITS: 3 CREDITS

MODULE COMPETENCE

This module is designated to enable the learner to apply first aid knowledge and skills.

Units Covered:

  • UNIT 1: Introduction, concept and Practice of First Aid (02 HOURS)
  • UNIT 2: Human Body Systems, Respiration, Circulation and Resuscitation (04 HOURS)
  • UNIT 3: Conditions of First Aid Shock (06 HOURS)
  • UNIT 4: Fractures, Sprain, Burns, Scalds, and Poisoning (06 HOURS)
  • UNIT 5: Dressings and Bandages (06 HOURS)
  • UNIT 6: Casualty Incident management and Lifting and Carrying Casualties (06 HOURS)

Module Outcomes

At the end of the module the learner will be able to:

  1. Understand what a first aider is expected to do in emergency circumstances
  2. Institute a Plan of Action to manage incidents
  3. Distinguish between incident management and casualty management

Module Content (UNIT CODE; CCU 1104)

The application of first aid knowledge and skills in assessment, diagnosis, and management of casualties in emergency situations.

1: Introduction, Concept, and Practice of First Aid

Definition of first aid; principles of first aid; scope, practice, and aim of first aid; determining diagnosis through DRABC, AMEGA, and procedure of examination; characteristics of a good first aider; attitudes of a good first aider and application.

2: Human Boy Systems, Respiration, Circulation and Resuscitation

Statement of the human body systems and organs theirn (also refer to Anatomy and Physiology objectives); definition of resuscitation; description of mechanism of respiration; conditions of respiratory system, their causes, and management; description of mechanism of circulation; conditions of the circulatory system, causes, and management; organs forming the circulatory system; recognition of: pulse rate, temperature level, wounds, bleeding and breathing rates, application of skills in controlling internal and external bleeding, swelling, and guarding against shock; types of resuscitation in adults and children: manual and electrical; various methods of respiration in adults: mouth to mouth, mouth to nose, mouth to mouth and nose, and mouth to stoma; various methods of respiration in children: mouth to mouth, mouth to mouth and nose, and mouth to stoma.

3: Conditions of First Aid Shock

Definition; recognition, features, and management; shock; compression; concussion; confusion; fainting; frost bite; hyperthermia.

4: Fractures, Sprains, Burns, Scalds, and Poisoning

Definition of fractures; dislocation; sprains and strains; sign and symptoms of fractures; types of fractures (open and closed fractures); management of sprained or torn muscles and ligaments and fractures; definition and types of burns; signs and symptoms of burns, scalds and poisoning; management of burns, scalds, and poisoning.

5: Dressings and Bandages

Definition of a dressing and bandage; description of triangular and roller bandages; application of a dressing; functions of a dressing; application of roller and triangular bandages.

6: Casualty Incident Management and Lifting and Carrying Casualties

Definition and management of incidents; Practical session; recovery position types; systematic steps; ode of seeking help; messages ato ambulance crew and computation of casualty handing-over message; application of lifting and carrying casualty methods and procedures; methods involving one first aider (human crutch, drag method, fireman's lift, pick-back); methods involving multiple first aiders (four-handed seat, fireman's lift, cradle, two-handed seat, chair lift); stretcher-dressing, loading, lifting, and loading casualties into an ambulance.

TEACHING METHODOLOGY

  • Lecture
  • Focus group discussion
  • Application exercise and practices in a classroom
  • Assignments
  • Independent study
  • Off-and on-the-job training integrated
  • Paying visits and attachments to health facilities

ASSESSMENT METHODS

  • Theory paper examination (Multiple choice questions, Matching variable statements, True or false answer questions, Essay questions)
  • Practical examination (OSPEs, Demonstration)

REFERENCES

  1. Anatomy and Physiology - Ross and Wilson, Anatomy and Physiology in Health and Illness, 11th Edition
  2. Pathology - Merck Manual, Medical dictionary, Muir's Textbook of Pathology
  3. First Aid - St John's Ambulance, Essential of firs Aid, 10th edition; St John's Ambulance, first Aid at Work; St John's Ambulance, Life Saver International
