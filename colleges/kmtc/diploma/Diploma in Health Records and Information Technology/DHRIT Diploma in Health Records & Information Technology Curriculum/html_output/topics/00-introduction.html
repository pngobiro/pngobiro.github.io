Preface

We must admit that this is a dream come true. It is a truly daring act to resolve to pursue this mission. We are sure that the energy and enthusiasm currently burning in us at this point of consolidating our efforts to bring up the department of health records and information will not expire any soon. Surely, there is no better time than now.

This curriculum was developed to steer professionalism among the health records and information students through instilling knowledge and skills required in the present, dynamic world today. It is also geared towards reengineering and sharing vast experience in the field of health records and Information management.

Albert Einstein asserted that "Education is what you learn after you have forgotten everything you learnt in school" It is our hope that this guide will help in meeting all expectations of diverse, potential and future employers through sufficient exposure of the students to different orientations in health records and information management.

Entry Requirements

The minimum entry requirements for a Diploma in Health Records and Information Technology shall be;

Pre-Service (Direct Entry):

At (KCSE) Kenya Certificate of Secondary Education (8-4-4 System) level: The candidate must have a mean Grade of C plain with C- in Mathematics and English or Kiswahili and C- in Biology or chemistry.

In-Service Entry:

This applies to those officers already in the service. He/she must have:

  • a. A minimum of Division III at KCE level or its equivalent in the KCSE (8-4-4 system)
  • b. A certificate in Health Records and Information Technology (Technician)

Roles and Responsibilities

  1. Establish, organize, and manage health information systems
  2. Plan, supervise, and co-ordinate work in health records services
  3. Organize and manage health records and information services
  4. Conduct research services
  5. Conduct monitoring and evaluation (M&E) of health records services
  6. Participate in health and related project management
  7. Establish, organize and manage health records and archive operations
  8. Data processing and dissemination of information
  9. Apply ICT in health records and health data management
  10. Apply anatomy, physiology, pathology and terminology in classifying, coding and indexing of disease and procedures in medicine
  11. Store and retrieve health records
  12. Manage departmental resources (both human and non-human)
  13. Management of disease surveillance and response system
  14. Participate in the national organization of health service delivery system
  15. Management of departmental supplies

Course Outcomes

Upon completion of the three year DHRIT programme, the graduate is expected to exhibit the following units of competence where each unit of competence has the prescribed areas of competence:

  1. Maintain and practice professionalism in the workplace
  2. Establish a Health record Department in a health care facility
  3. Apply leadership skills
  4. Acquire leadership skills
  5. Communicate effectively with the patients, colleagues, and the public
  6. Solve departmental and professional problems
  7. Manage health records and information department personnel
  8. Exercise professional integration
  9. Manage a disaster
  10. Design, organize, manage, and evaluate health information systems
  11. Participate in planning and organization of national health services in relation to information flow
  12. Participate in the provision of community health in relation to health information
  13. Practice professional ethics ad medico-legal aspects of health records and information
  14. Organize and manage the reception, registration, and initiation of records
  15. Organize and manage appointment systems
  16. Organize and manage clinic preparation
  17. Organize and manage patients ADT systems
  18. Manage and maintain health records storage, retrieval, and control
  19. Manage other types of records
  20. Organize and manage a health records and information office
  21. Apply research methods in conduction research
  22. Process and analyze data
  23. Utilize computer applications in research, data analysis, and presentation
  24. Conduct epidemiological studies
  25. Collect, compute and interpret demographic data

Assessment

Evaluation of students is by Continuous Assessment tests both in theory and practical and written examination at the end of each semester. A student must pass in all subjects so as to be awarded with a Diploma in Health Records & Information Technology

EXAMINATIONS

END OF TRIMESTER EXAMS
CAT - 40%
END TRIMESTER - 60%
TOTAL - 100%

FINAL QUALIFYING EXAMINATION

Upon completion of all the units and practicums the student will be required to sit for a Final Qualifying Exam.
