MODULE TITLE: HEALTH RECORDS MANAGEMENT

Module Overview

TIME: 240 HOURS

CREDITS: 24 CREDITS

MODULE COMPETENCE

This module is designed to equip the learner with the knowledge, skills, and attitudes to effectively and efficient manage health records.

Units Covered:

  • UNIT 1: Introduction to Health Records Management
  • UNIT 2: Receiving, Registration and Initiation of Health Records
  • UNIT 3: Creating and Maintaining Health Records Indices
  • UNIT 4: Scheduling and Follow-Up, Preparing of In/Out Clinic, Patients
  • UNIT 5: Admitting and Discharging Patients/Clients
  • UNIT 6: Storage and Retrieval of Health Records
  • UNIT 7: Acquiring and Maintain Health Records Equipment
  • UNIT 8: Legal Aspects of Health Records
  • UNIT 9: Managing Special Records
  • UNIT 10: Managing a Health Records And Information Office
  • UNIT 11: Establishing a Health Records Department in a Health Care Facility
  • UNIT 12: Developing and Designing Health Records Forms
  • UNIT 13: Ensuring Quality Assurance in Health Care Services
  • UNIT 14: Introductions to Electronic Health Records

Module Outcomes

At the end of the module the learner will be able to:

  1. Give an overview of the health records discipline and its organization
  2. Describe the procedures used in reception, registration and initiation of patient/client records
  3. Describe the appointment systems
  4. Describe the various methods of creating and maintaining health records indices
  5. Display ability to create and maintain health records
  6. Explain the various procedures of clinic preparation
  7. Describe the admission and discharge procedures
  8. Maintain the efficient system of storage, retrieval and control of health records
  9. Explain the various system of storage and retrieval of heath records
  10. Describe different methods of acquiring and maintaining health records equipment, health records forms, and office stationery
  11. Demonstrate ability to acquire health record forms and equipment and office stationery and supplies
  12. Describe legal aspects of health records
  13. Enumerate the various notifiable diseases
  14. Describe various types of special records
  15. Describe how to establish a health records department
  16. Educate the community and other health workers on the importance of health records
  17. Describe how to design and develop health record forms
  18. Appreciate the importance of quality assurance in health care
  19. Display thoroughness and efficiency in managing health records
  20. Appreciate the importance of confidentiality in dealing with health records
  21. Definition of electronic health records and electronic medical records system
  22. Explain the concept of electronic health records system

Module Content

UNIT CODE; DHR1101 - HEALTH RECORDS MANAGEMENT-I (Time: 40 hours)

1: Introduction to Health Records Management

Definition of health records; brief historical background; uses and value; organization of a health records department; functions of a health records department; relationship between health records department and other health disciplines; qualities of a good health records officer; documents contained in a health record; health records ethics; duties and responsibilities of medical records technician.

2: Receiving, Registration and Initiation of Health Records

Procedures of receiving patients; decision-making and interviewing; qualities of a good receptionist in in/out patient and accidents and emergency units; procedure of registering patients; type of registration (centralized and decentralized); unit system (alphabetical, chronological, straight numerical); attendance card; master index; case record; classification of records; pre-registration procedure of document.

UNIT CODE: DHR1201 HEALTH RECORDS MANAGEMENT- II (Time: 40 hours)

1: Creating and Maintaining Health Records Indices

Definition and description of various health records indices (patient master, inpatient waiting list, diagnostic and operations indices); importance of health records indices; various methods of filing indices; procedures of creating various types of health indices; identification details (record the details, sort the index, file the index, alphabetical, numerical); ways of maintaining health records indices and equipment used in storage (sorting file, file cabinet, strip index); trays; International Classification of Diseases (ICD); books; computers; microfilming

2: Scheduling and Follow-Up; Preparing Of In/Out Clinic, Patients

Definition of scheduling; concept of follow-up; purpose; types of appointment systems (centralized and decentralized appointment systems); various procedures of clinic preparation (new patient, reattendances, ward discharges); tracing of the records to various destinations (completion of tracer cards, sorting of tracer cards, retrieval of the notes, review filing and editing procedures); verification of the completeness of the file; compilation of the records checklist form

UNIT CODE: DHR1301 HEALTH RECORDS MANAGEMENT - III (Time: 40 hours)

1: Admitting and Discharging Patients/Clients

Admission procedures, normal emergency and waiting list admissions; collection and registration of identification particulars; initiation of the inpatient record; (bed bureau) discharge procedures; collection, compilation, analysis, dissemination, and verification of returns, editing of health records; coding and indexing procedures; registration of births and deaths; review appointment procedures (ward discharges); health records audit; symptoms of various conditions; review reception procedures; review relevant legal procedures for admission and discharge; relevant admission forms; review of government office routine

2: Storage and Retrieval Health Records

Storage of medical records; filing systems (alphabetical, numerical, terminal digit, chronological, automation); filing procedures of health records; security of health records; tracing systems (definition, tracing procedures, common tracer cards, library tracer card, personal tracer card, other tracing systems such as control registers); confidentiality of health records; review of editing procedures of health records; equipment required in storage and retrieval (cabinets, shelves, trolleys, kik tools/ladders, chairs, box files, tables, pigeon holes, computer, microfilm, etc); preservation of health records (retention period, workmen's compensation act, weeding, date ladder)

UNIT CODE: DHR2101 HEALTH RECORDS MANAGEMENT - IV (Time: 40 hours)

1: Acquiring and Maintaining Health Records Equipment

Acquisition procedure; use of accountable forms; maintenance of inventories; types of health records (medical stationery, office equipment, office stationery); storage and control of health equipment and supply expendables; non-expendables; maintenance of health records equipment (repair, contracting for service of equipment)

2: Legal Aspects of Health Records

Concepts of professional ethics and legal aspects of medical records (confidentiality, security, ownership, retention, disclosure); higher duty; court cases; insurance claims; various acts: Public Health Act (list of notification diseases med 26: procedure of disposal of dead bodies, health and safety, notification of infectious diseases); Public Records Act (retention and disposal or records); Safety Radiation Act; Mental Health Act; admission and discharge procedures; Government Official Secrets Act (confidentiality); Civil Registration Act (procedures of notification of births and deaths); Workmen's Compensation Act (procedures of workman's compensation); Criminal Procedure Code (completion of P3 forms, post mortems, consent for operations, release of information (e.g., in post mortems)

UNIT CODE: DHR2301 HEALTH RECORDS MANAGEMENT - V (Time: 40 hours)

1: Managing Special Records

Importance of special records; types of special records (psychiatric records, maternity records, radiological records, accident and emergency records, maternal and child health/family planning records, infectious diseases records); procedures for completion and management of special records; review, storage, and retrieval as pertaining to special records; confidentiality of special records, review legal aspects of medical records; security and control procedures as relates to special records procedures.

2: Managing a Health Records and Information Office

Definition, purpose and functions of an office; location of an office; types of offices; advantages and disadvantages, requirement of an office, organization and management of an office

UNIT CODE; DHR3101 HEALTH RECORDS MANAGEMENT - VI (Time: 40 hours)

1: Establishing a Health Records Department in a Health Care Facility

Needs assessment (demography, hospital workers, clients); procedures in establishing a health records department; review storage and retrieval of health records; organization of various the sections; review admission and discharge procedures; concept of teamwork; commissioning procedures for a health records department.

2: Developing and Designing Health Record Forms

Basic principles of form design; importance of form designs; standardization of forms; qualities of a good form; formatting of health records forms (layout/size); various health records forms used in health records departments; pre-testing of health records forms; reproduction process (using government process of reproduction/acquisition, private printers, health facility resources); costing of printing work; equipment needed for development and designing of health records forms; storage, control and retrieval of forms.

UNIT CODE: DHR3301 HEALTH RECORDS MANAGEMENT - VII (Time: 40 hours)

1: Ensuring Quality Assurance in Health Care Services

Definition of quality assurance; importance of quality assurance; method of quality assurance in health care records; definition of health records auditing of health records (process)

2: Introduction to Electronic Health Records

Define Electronic Medical Records (EMR); role of EMR system; privacy and security of EMR; key EMR functional requirements in Kenya; demonstrate how an EMR is used to improve public health
