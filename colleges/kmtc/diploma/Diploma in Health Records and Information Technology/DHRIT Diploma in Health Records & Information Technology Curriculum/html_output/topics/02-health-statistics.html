02. MODULE TITLE: HEALTH STATISTICS

Module Overview

TIME: 220 HOURS

CREDITS: 24 CREDITS

MODULE COMPETENCE

To enable the learner to collect, compute, analyze, interpret ad disseminate data for better healthcare service delivery.

Units Covered:

  • UNIT 1: Introduction to Health Statistics and Research (40 HOURS)
  • UNIT 2: Data Presentation (20 HOURS)
  • UNIT 3: Standard Health Facility Administrative Statistics And Formulae (40 HOURS)
  • UNIT 4: Measures of Central Tendency and Dispersion & Probability Distributions (40 HOURS)
  • UNIT 5: Linear Algebra, Graphs and Functions (40 HOURS)
  • UNIT 6: Hypothesis Testing and Sampling (40 HOURS)

Module Outcomes

At the end of the module the learner will be able to:

  1. Apply basic skills in health research methodology
  2. Utilize basic statistical skills for evidence -based decision making
  3. Present data using various methods
  4. Analyze and process data using basis ICT skills

Module Content

UNIT CODE: DHR1102 HEALTH STATISTICS I (40 HOURS)

INTRODUCTION TO HEALTH STATISTICS AND RESEARCH

Definition; importance, types, and uses of health statistics. Basic research methodology: introduction, key terms, and steps; Identification of a research problem; Definition of a problem; Sources of data (primary and secondary); Identification of the population; Definition and description of the population; Review of literature and critical review of literature; Justification for the study; Formulation of objective and research question; Methods of data collection (direct observation, interviewing, abstraction from published statistics, questionnaires, data collection tools); Description of the tools; Principles of preparation of tools; interview schedules; questionnaires; Sampling (definition of a sample; sampling methods; random, systematic, stratified, multistage, cluster, and quota sampling); Pretesting of data collection tools (instruments): definition, purpose, procedure; Data cleaning: definition, purpose, importance, procedures, different methods; Data organization: definition, importance; interpretation and dissemination.

UNIT CODE: DHR1202 HEALTH STATISTICS II (20 HOURS)

I: DATA PRESENTATION

Definition, purpose, importance and characteristics of data; Types and presentation of data; Definition, description, purpose and importance of data presentation methods; narrative; tabulation; simple tables; multiple column; Principles of table construction (array presentation, frequency distribution, cumulative frequency); Different ways of presentation of data in charts and graphs (description, types, principles of construction, simple bar charts, competent bar charts, percentage component bar charts, multiple bar charts, pie charts, graph, histograms, frequency polygon curve. Ogive, break-even charts); Computer application in data processing, presentation and dissemination.

UNIT CODE: DHR2102 HEALTH STATISTICS III (40 HOURS)

1: STANDARD HEALTH FACILITY ADMINISTRATIVE STATISTICS AND FORMULAE

Description of health facility administrative statistics; procedures of collecting health administrative statistics; tools for collecting health administrative statistics; uses and importance of health administrative statistics; description of the standard terms in health administrative statistics; computation of health facility administrative statistics; occupied bed days; available bed days; vacant bed days; admission, discharges, and deaths; well people; average occupancy; percentage occupancy; average length of stay; turnover interval; turnover per bed; crude hospital death rate; average discharge rate; average admission rate; applying standard health facility administrative terms and formulae accurately; collection, computation, presentation and analysis of outpatient statistics; maternal and child health evaluation techniques; family planning evaluation techniques; health activity analysis: interpretation, presentation, and dissemination of health administrative statistics.

2: VITAL HEALTH STATISTICS

Definition, description, and importance of vital health statistics; the various vital and health statistics rates/type of vital statistics; morbidity rate; fertility rate; crude birth rate; infant mortality rate; crude death rate; incidence and prevalence rates; formulae for calculating vital statistics; computer application in vital health data analysis and processing.

UNIT CODE: DHR2202 HEALTH STATISTICS IV (40 HOURS)

I: MEASURES OF CENTRAL TENDENCY

Calculation; mean; simple arithmetic mean; weighted arithmetic mean; arithmetic mean of ungrouped frequency distribution; arithmetic mean of a frequency distribution; mean deviations from the mean; the geometric mean; mode; computation; finding mode using histogram; obtaining mode of a grouped frequency; median; ungrouped and grouped data; measures of dispersion: definition, description, calculation; range, quartiles, and deciles; standard deviation; skewness; regression; coefficient of correlation; electronic data processing; definition, description, and advantages; basic statistical packages; computer application in data analysis.

II: MEASURES OF DISPERSION

Measures of dispersion: definition, description, calculation; range, quartiles, and deciles; standard deviation; skewness; regression; coefficient of correlation; electronic data processing; definition, description, and advantages; basic statistical packages; computer application in data analysis.

III: PROBABILITY AND NORMAL DISTRIBUTION

Probability; normal distribution; standard normal deviate; binomial distribution; Poisson distribution; confidence interval; X²-test/Chi-square; t-test; electronic computation of probability and normal distributions.

UNIT CODE: DHR3102 HEALTH STATISTICS V (40 HOURS)

I: ALGEBRA

Content
  • Solving quadratic equations (factorization, completing the square, formula method, graphical method)
  • Reducing equations to quadratic and solving them
  • Solving linear equations with three unknowns
  • Forming linear equations with three or more unknowns
  • Defining a matrix (row, column, rectangular, square, singular, non-singular, null, identity)
  • Carrying out matrix operations (addition/subtraction, compatibility, multiplication, commutability, division by a scalar)
  • Determining the inverse of matrices up to order 3 (co-factor method, row reduction method)
  • Using matrices to solve simultaneous equations (Cramer's rule, inverse method, row reduction/Gaussian Elimination)
  • Applying matrices in real-life problems (industrial demand, message coding, information storage)

GRAPHS AND FUNCTION

Specific Objectives

By the end of this topic, the trainee should be able to:

  • a) plot linear graph
  • b) make interpretations from linear graph
  • c) plot parabolic curves
  • d) solve simultaneous and quadratic equations by the graphical methods
  • e) present data in appropriate chart
Content
  • Plotting linear graphs (Intercepts of the axes y=0, x=0)
  • Making interpretations (Intercepts, Gradients, y=mx+c)
  • Solution of simultaneous and quadratic equations by plotting (linear and parabolic) graphs.
  • Presentation of data in charts (Pie chart, Bar chart, Pictogram)

UNIT CODE: DHR3202 HEALTH STATISTICS VI (40 Hours)

1. HYPOTHESIS TESTING

Content
  • Estimation (definition, need)
  • Types of estimators (point, interval)
  • Sampling distribution (central limit problems, x and 6 distribution, distribution of difference between means ( x ) and between proportions (A/P))
  • Confidence interval and interpretations
  • Hypothesis (definitions, design and rules, types)
  • Types of errors (type I, type II)

2. DECISION THEORY

Content
  • Definition of mathematical expectation
  • Calculation of mathematical expectation
  • Statement of Bayer's rule
  • Construction of pay-off table
  • Statement of maxima rule
  • Solution of decision tree problem

3. SAMPLING

Content
  • Definition of terms (random sampling, sampling with replacement, population, samples, sampling distributions)
  • Explanation of the concepts of sampling distribution
  • Calculation of standard errors
  • Relationship between mean and SD of the sampling distribution of the mean and the SD of the parent distribution.
  • Recognition that the distributions of the sample mean of normal distribution is normal distribution.
  • Determination of population mean and SD from sample data (when sample size is large, when sample size is small)
