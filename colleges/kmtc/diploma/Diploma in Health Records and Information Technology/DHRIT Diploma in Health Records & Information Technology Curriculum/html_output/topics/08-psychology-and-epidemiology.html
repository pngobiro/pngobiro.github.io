MODULE TITLE: HUMAN PSYCHOLOGY

Module Overview

TIME: 30 HOURS

CREDITS: 3 CREDITS

MODULE COMPETENCE

This module is designed to equip the learner with the knowledge, skills, and attitudes necessary to provide services at the health care facility and community level.

Units Covered:

  • UNIT 1: Introduction to Psychology (02 HOURS)
  • UNIT 2: Human growth and development (04 HOURS)
  • UNIT 3: Bio psychological Processes (04 HOURS)
  • UNIT 4: Cognitive Psychology (02 HOURS)
  • UNIT 5: Motivation and Emotions (04 HOURS)
  • UNIT 6: Personality (04 HOURS)
  • UNIT 7: Psychology and Health (02 HOURS)
  • UNIT 8: Social Psychology (04 HOURS)
  • UNIT 9: Abnormal Psychology and Therapeutic Approaches (04 HOURS)

Module Outcomes

At the end of the module, the learner will be able to:

  1. Apply psychological principles in understanding human behavior
  2. Demonstrate understanding of the human lifespan development and its influence on health
  3. Appreciate the different psychological and personality influences on health and health care delivery
  4. Apply learned knowledge, attitude and skills in therapeutic approaches to health care

Module Content (UNIT CODE: DHR2315/DHR2214 HUMAN PSYCHOLOGY I)

1: Introduction to Psychology

Concepts and principles; historical background; goals of psychology; methods used in studying psychology; branches; relevance of psychology in medical practice system.

2: Human Growth and Development

Hereditary and the environment; prenatal development (germinal, embryonic, fetal stages); infant and child development (physical, perceptual, cognitive, psychosocial); adolescent development (physical, cognitive, psychosocial); adulthood (physical, cognitive, psychosocial, ageing, death and dying).

3: Bio psychological Processes

Nervous system; functions of the brain (sensation and perception); sensory processes (sensory thresholds, adaptation, vision, hearing, smell, taste, touch, temperature, pain, kinesthetic, vestibular); consciousness (sleep and dreams, patterns, duration, functions, hypnosis, nature, effects and induction, meditation, psychoactive drugs and consciousness).

4: Cognitive Psychology

Learning; theories; behaviorists; cognitive; conditioning; social learning theory; observational learning; imitation perception; attention memory (information processing and memory, sensory memory, short-term and long-term memory); encoding; storage; retrieval; forgetting; improving memory; biopsychology of memory; thinking (concept formation, problem solving, creativity, decision-making, artificial intelligence); language (structure, acquisition, relationship between language and thinking); intelligence (theories, extremes, testing).

5: Motivation and emotions

Motivation (concepts, sources); hereditary; drives; incentives; Maslow's hierarchy of needs; hunger motive (factors regulating hunger, obesity, eating disorders); sex motive (physiological factors, psychological factors, sexual dysfunction, gender identify, sexual orientation); arousal motive |(sensory deprivation, sensation seeking); achievement motive (need for achievement, goal setting, intrinsic motivation); emotion (physiology of emotion, autonomic nervous system, brain and emotion, chemistry of emotion, expression of emotion, experience of emotion, theories of emotion).

6: Personality

Concepts; bio psychological approach (physique, hereditary, assessment); psychoanalytic approach ( psychosexual theory, psychosocial theory, analytical, individual psychology, psychoanalytic assessment of personality); dispositional approach (type, traits theories, dispositional assessment); behavioral approach (operant conditioning, social-cognitive theory, assessment); cognitive approach (personal construct theory; assessment); humanistic approach (self-actualization theory, self-theory, assessment).

7: Psychology and Health

Stress and illness (sources, effects, mediation, coping): health impairing habits (nutrition, smoking, exercise, unsafe sexual practices); reactions to illness (distress, seeking treatment, adherence to medical regimens).

8: Social Psychology

Social cognition (impression, attribution); social attraction (attraction, liking love); social attitudes (formation, persuasion, attitudes and behavior, prejudice); group processes |(group decision-making, performance, social influence); pro-social behavior (altruism, bystander intervention); aggression (theories, group violence, television and aggression).

9: Abnormal Psychology and Therapeutic approaches

Abnormal psychology; psychological disorders (criteria, viewpoints, classification); anxiety disorders (generalized anxiety disorders, panic disorder, phobias, obsessive compulsive disorder, somatoform disorders, conversion disorders, hypochondriasis); dissociative disorders (dissociative fugue, dissociative amnesia, multiple personality); mood disorders ( major depression, bipolar disorder, causes); schizophrenia (causes, characteristics, types); personality disorders; therapeutic approaches (history, orientations/styles).

11. MODULE TITLE: EPIDEMIOLOGY

Module Overview

TIME: 100 HOURS

CREDITS: 12 CREDITS

MODULE COMPETENCE

This module is designed to equip the learner with the knowledge and skills of disease determinants and distribution for effective prevention and control of diseases.

Units Covered:

  • UNIT 1: Introduction to Epidemiology (06 HOURS)
  • UNIT 2: Dynamics of Disease Transmission (24 HOURS)
  • UNIT 3: Measures of morbidity, mortality and association (30 HOURS)
  • UNIT 4: Disease Surveillance and Outbreak Investigations (20 HOURS)
  • UNIT 5: Epidemiological Data and Study Designs (06 HOURS)
  • UNIT 6: Principles of disease Measurements (14 HOURS)

Module Outcomes

At the end of the module, the learner will be able to:

  1. Apply the concepts of epidemiology in health care service
  2. Describe the distribution and determinant of diseases
  3. Calculate, interpret and apply basic measures of morbidity, mortality and association
  4. Describe the major types of epidemiologic research
  5. Carry out epidemiological studies
  6. Demonstrate the principles of disease measurements

Module Content

UNIT CODE: DHR2111 EPIDEMIOLOGY I (30 HOURS)

1: Introduction to Epidemiology

Definition of epidemiology and its origin; terms used in epidemiology; determinants of disease; occurrences of diseases; natural history of disease; level of disease prevention; ecological approach to disease causation; scope of epidemiology; uses and purposes of epidemiology.

2: Dynamics of Disease Transmission

Communicable disease epidemiology; chain of disease transmission; methods of controlling communicable disease; vaccine preventable disease; expanded programme on immunization (EPI).

UNIT CODE: DHR2211 EPIDEMIOLOGY II (30 HOURS)

1: Measures of Morbidity, Mortality and Association

Rates, rations and proportions; measures of morbidity (incidence, prevalence); measures of mortality; relative risk; absolute risk; attributable risk; odds ratio; causation; causal and non-causal relationships; sources of epidemiological data (census, vital statistics/registration systems, health service records [health information sources], morbidity and mortality surveys, demographic and health survey).

UNIT CODE: DHR3111 EPIDEMIOLOGY III (40 HOURS)

1: Disease Surveillance and Outbreak Investigations

Definitions; types of epidemics; detection of epidemics; investigation of epidemics/outbreak; control of epidemics (definition, types of surveillance, purpose of surveillance, components of surveillance, sources of surveillance data, steps in surveillance, strategy in disease surveillance); Integrated Disease Surveillance and Response (IDSR).

2: Epidemiology Data and Study Designs

Sources of epidemiological data; experimental studies; observational studies; descriptive studies; analytical studies; cross-sectional studies; longitudinal studies; case-control studies.

3: Principles of Disease Measurements

Reliability; observer variation; subject variation; variability; test bias and confounding; error and systematic error; repeatability; validity (content, concurrent, predictive, and construct); sensitivity and Specificity.
