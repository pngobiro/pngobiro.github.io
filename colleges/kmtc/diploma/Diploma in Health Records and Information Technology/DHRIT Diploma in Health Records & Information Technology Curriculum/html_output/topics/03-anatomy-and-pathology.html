03. MODULE TITLE: ANATOMY AND PHYSIOLOGY

Module Overview

TIME: 80 HOURS

CREDITS: 6 CREDITS

MODULE COMPETENCE

This module is designed to equip the learner with the knowledge, skills and attitude in anatomy and physiology necessary for effective classification of diseases, conditions, and medical procedures according to International Classification of Diseases (ICD) and International Classification of Procedures in Medicine (ICPM), as well as effective communication and participation in research activities in health.

Units Covered:

  • UNIT 1: Introduction to Anatomy and Physiology (10 HOURS)
  • UNIT 2: Human Skeleton and Joints (10 HOURS)
  • UNIT 3: Muscular System (10 HOURS)
  • UNIT 4: Circulatory System (10 HOURS)
  • UNIT 5: Respiratory System (10 HOURS)
  • UNIT 6: Digestive System (10 HOURS)
  • UNIT 7: Urinary System (10 HOURS)
  • UNIT 8: Nervous System (10 HOURS)
  • UNIT 9: Reproductive System (14 HOURS)
  • UNIT 10: Endocrine System (12 HOURS)
  • UNIT 11: Sensory Organs (Skin, Nose, Tongue, Ear and Eye) (14 HOURS)

Module Outcomes

At the end of the module the learner will be able to:

  1. Define and state the common terms used in anatomy and physiology
  2. Describe the embryology processes
  3. Name major structures of the human body organs and systems
  4. Describe the human body cavities
  5. Define the term skeleton and list types of human skeleton and bones forming the human skeleton
  6. Describe the anatomy and physiology of the human skeleton and various classifications of joints.
  7. List and define the various types of human body structures, organs and systems.
  8. Describe the anatomy and physiology of human body systems (muscular, circulatory, respiratory, digestive, urinary, nervous, reproductive and endocrine)
  9. Describe the anatomy and physiology of the sensory organs (skin, nose, tongue, ear, and eye)
  10. Use diagrammatic illustrations and explain the various human body organs and systems
  11. Demonstrate ability to locate the various organs in the living body, cadaver or in models.

Module Content

This module entails introduction to anatomy and physiology; description of human skeleton and joints; muscular, circulatory, respiratory, digestive, urinary, nervous, reproductive and endocrine, systems, and sensory organs.

UNIT CODE: DHR1103 ANATOMY AND PHYSIOLOGY - I (40 HOURS)

1: Introduction to Anatomy and Physiology

Definition of the terms anatomy and physiology; statement of the common terms used in anatomy and physiology; description of the embryology processes; definition of the term organ; naming of all body organs, description of the anatomy of the organs of the human body; describe the human body cavities.

2: Human Skeleton and Joints

Definition of the term skeleton; listing types of skeletons; listing all the bones forming the human skeleton; description of the structure and functions of the human skeleton; definition of the term joint; description of the various of the various classifications of joints; description of the structures and functions of the joints.

3: Muscular System

Definition of the muscular system; listing types of muscles; description of the structure and function of types of muscles

4: Circulatory System

Definition of the circulatory system (blood and lymph); naming major structures of the circulatory system; explanation of the systematic, pulmonary, and portal blood and lymph circulation.

UNIT CODE: DHR1203 ANATOMY AND PHYSIOLOGY - II (40 HOURS)

1: Respiratory System

Definition of the respiratory system; listing of major structures of the respiratory system; description of the structure and function of the respiratory system

2: Digestive System

Definition of the digestive system; listing of major organs of digestion system; description of the structures and functions of the digestive system and accessory organs of digestion

3: Urinary System

Definition of the urinary system; naming of major structures of the urinary system; description of the structure and functions of the urinary system

4: Nervous System

Definition of the nervous system; description of the structure and function of the nervous system

UNIT CODE: DHR2103 ANATOMY AND PHYSIOLOGY - III (40 HOURS)

1: Reproductive System

Definition of the reproductive system; listing of major structures of the male and female reproductive systems; description of the structures and functions of the male and female reproductive systems

2: Endocrine System

Listing of major structures of the endocrine system; description of the function of the endocrine system

3: Sensory Organs

Description of the structures and functions of the skin, nose, tongue, ear, and eye.

04. MODULE TITLE: HUMAN PATHOLOGY

Module Overview

TIME: 60 HOURS

CREDITS: 6 CREDITS

MODULE COMPETENCE

This module is designed to equip the learner with the knowledge, skills and attitude in pathology necessary for effective classification of diseases, conditions, and medical procedures according to ICD and ICPM, as well as effective communication and participation in research activities in health.

Units Covered:

  • UNIT 1: General Concepts of Disease and Principles of Diagnosis (04 HOURS)
  • UNIT 2: Inflammatory Processes and Its Role In Disease And Injury (04 HOURS)
  • UNIT 3: Body Immunity (06 HOURS)
  • UNIT 4: Role of Pathogenic Microorganisms and Parasites In Disease (04 HOURS)
  • UNIT 5: Communicable Disease Transmission and Control (06 HOURS)
  • UNIT 6: Congenital and Hereditary Diseases (06 HOURS)
  • UNIT 7: Neoplasms (08 HOURS)
  • UNIT 8: Abnormalities of blood coagulation and circulatory disorders (10 HOURS)
  • UNIT 9: major disease conditions related to Specific body systems and basic diagnostic tests (06 HOURS)
  • UNIT 10: Treatments commonly used (06 HOURS)

Module Outcomes

At the end of the module the learner will be able to:

  1. Discuss general concepts of disease and principles of diagnosis
  2. Describe the inflammatory process and its role in disease and injury
  3. Describe cell mediated and humeral immunity
  4. Describe the role of pathogenic micro-organisms and parasites in disease
  5. Describe communicable disease transmission and control
  6. Describe congenital and hereditary disease causes and manifestations
  7. Describe types and characteristics of neoplasm and modalities of treatment
  8. Describe the abnormalities of blood coagulation and circulatory disorders
  9. Describe major disease conditions related to Specific body systems and basic diagnostic tests
  10. Describe treatments commonly used

Module Content

This module entails explanation of general concepts of disease and principles of diagnosis; inflammatory process and its role in disease and injury; body immunity; role of pathogenic microorganisms and parasites in disease; description of communicable disease transmission and control; congenital and hereditary disease; neoplasm; abnormalities of blood coagulation and circulatory disorders; major disease conditions related to Specific body systems and identification of basic diagnostic tests.

UNIT CODE: DHR1104 PATHOLOGY I (Time: 40 hours)

1: General Concepts of Disease and Principles of Diagnosis

Characteristics of disease process; basic principles of diagnosis; use of diagnostic tests and procedures

2: Inflammatory Process and its Role In Disease And Injury

Characteristics and clinical manifestation of an acute infection; outcomes of an inflammatory reaction and comparison between infection and inflammation

3: Body Immunity

Role of lymphocytes in the immune response; compare immunity and hypersensitivity; classes of antibodies and allergic manifestations and methods of treatment

4: Role of Pathogenic Micro-organisms and Parasites in Disease

Major groups of pathogenic bacteria; mechanism by which antibodies inhibit the growth of bacteria; mode of action of virus infection and he body's response; spectrum of infections caused by fungi and common parasitic infestations and how they are acquired

UNIT CODE: DHR1204 PATHOLOGY II (Time: 40 hours)

1: Communicable Disease Transmission and Control

Communicable disease transmission and control; common sexually transmitted diseases and their clinical manifestation; complication and methods of treatment

2: Congenital and Hereditary Disease

Common causes of congenital malformation; abnormalities of sex chromosomes and their clinical manifestation; common genetic abnormalities

3: Neoplasms

Characteristics of benign and malignant tumors; principal modalities of cancer treatment

4: Abnormalities of Blood Coagulation and Circulatory Disorders

Common causes of disturbance in hemostasis; causes and effect of arterial and venous thrombosis and factors disturbing the regulation of circulation

5: Major Disease Conditions Related To Specific Body Systems and Basic Diagnostic Tests

Cardiovascular and circulatory system; respiratory system; male and female reproductive system (including conditions occurring during pregnancy); urinary system; gastrointestinal system; endocrine system; nervous system; musculoskeletal system.

6: Treatments Commonly Used

Pharmacology; revision of all the disease affecting the various human body systems
