16. MODULE TITLE: COMMUNICATION SKILLS

Module Overview

TIME: 30 HOURS

CREDITS: 4 CREDITS

INTRODUCTION

At the end of this course unit, the trainee should comprehend and effectively use communication as required in a modern health records office.

MODULE OUTCOMES

By the end of this course unit the trainee should be able to:

  • a) learn and apply the principles of communication effectively
  • b) use proper language in communication
  • c) write and use various documents such as business correspondence, speeches, documents of meeting
  • d) use oral, non verbal and visual communication effectively.

Module Content (CCU1101: COMMUNICATION SKILLS)

MEANING OF COMMUNICATION

  • Definition of communication
  • Role of communication in organization

THEORY AND PROCESS

  • a) Conceiving the message
  • b) Encoding the message
  • c) Selecting the communication medium
  • d) Decoding the message
  • e) Interpreting the message
  • f) Providing feedback

PRINCIPLES OF COMMUNICATION

  • Communication patterns in firms (vertical, lateral, diagonal, grapevine)
  • External communication (to outside firms) - Role of the Public Relations Department
  • Communication difficulties in firms (Barriers: technical, physical)
  • Language in Communication (written, spoken)

THE SYNTAX AND SEMANTICS

  • Clear Wording
  • Arrangement of ideas
  • Sentences construction
  • Paragraphing

SUMMARY

  • Summarizing correspondence
  • Summarizing a whole or a part of a given passage
  • Considerations in summary writing (length, vocabulary, layout, continuity)
  • Summarizing speeches and telephone conversations

BUSINESS CORRESPONDENCE

  • a) Business letters (all types)
  • b) Memorandum
  • c) Telegram/Telex/Cables
  • d) Press Advertisement
  • e) Questionnaire
  • f) Messages
  • g) Posters

MEETINGS (Group Decision and Managerial Decisions)

  • Different types: formal, committee, command
  • Purpose of meetings
  • Terminologies used in meetings
  • Documents used in meetings
  • Advantages and disadvantages of meetings

REPORTS

  • Types of Reports (solicited, unsolicited, annual, routine)
  • Purpose of reports
  • Structure and content
  • Format and presentation (letter, memorandum, schematic form)
  • Style and Language

ORAL COMMUNICATION AND NON-VERBAL COMMUNICATION

  • Face-to-Face Communication (characteristics, preparation, delivery, interviews)
  • Barriers to effective face-to-face communication
  • Talking to Groups (effective listening, the telephone, speech writing)
  • Non-verbal communication (importance, types: facial expression, eye-contact, tone, gestures, postures)

VISUAL COMMUNICATION

  • Organization charts, Charts, Tables, Line graphs, Bar-charts
  • Pictographs, Pie charts, Histograms, Flow-charts
  • Boards, Tape recorders, Overhead projectors, Plastigraphs, Filmstrips and Slides

14. MODULE TITLE: MANAGEMENT (PRINCIPLES & PRACTICE OF MANAGEMENT)

Module Overview

TIME: 60 HOURS

CREDITS: 6 CREDITS

MODULE COMPETENCE

This module is intended to equip the learner with the knowledge, skills and attitudes in leadership and management to enable him/her manage health records and information services.

Units Covered:

  • UNIT 1: Principles of management (20 HOURS)
  • UNIT 2: Organizational management (10 HOURS)
  • UNIT 3: Office administration (30 HOURS)

MODULE OUTCOMES

At the end of the module the learner will be able to:

  1. Describe principles of management.
  2. Describe organizational management.
  3. Explain the concepts of office administration.

Module Content

UNIT CODE; DHR2111 MANAGEMENT I

Principles of management:

Concepts in management, types of management, administrative, humanistic, operational, contemporary, describe various functions of management, budgeting, description of budgeting, types of budgets, steps involved in budgeting, financial control, resource acquisition and utilization.

UNIT CODE; DHR2206 MANAGEMENT II

Organizational management

Organizational theory, definition of organizational theory, properties of an organization, concepts of systems and organizations, organizational effectiveness, concepts of effectiveness, elements of organizational effectiveness, factors that influence organizational effectiveness, organizational culture and climate, environmental, employees, management policies and practices, managing effective institutions, roles of the manager, time management, effective time management, improvement of time management, develop effective leadership skills, definition of leadership, process of leading, types of leadership, leadership styles, qualities of an effective leader, team building.

UNIT CODE; DHR2306 MANAGEMENT III

Office administration

Organizational change, rational for organizational change, stimulants to change, objectives of organizational development, organizational development interventions, strategic planning and strategic goal setting, techniques of overcoming resistance to change, office administration and human resource management, office management, supply management, human resource management (Personnel), definition, functions, human resource planning, recruitment procedures, staff training and development, time management, definition, description.

TEACHING METHODOLOGY

  • Lectures
  • Discussions
  • Illustrations/demonstrations
  • Case studies
  • Role play
  • Group work
  • Self-directed learning

ASSESSMENT METHODS

CATs, quizzes, written assignment. End of semester exam.
