12. MODULE TITLE: MEDICAL DEMOGRAPHY

Module Overview

TIME: 60 HOURS

CREDITS: 6 CREDITS

The module is designed to equip the learner with the knowledge and skills for collection, analysis, interpretation, presentation and dissemination of health demographic information.

Units Covered:

  • UNIT 1: Introduction and Concepts of Demography (20 HOURS)
  • UNIT 2: Population measures (20 HOURS)
  • UNIT 3: Fertility & Mortality Measures (40 HOURS)
  • UNIT 4: Construction of life tables (30 HOURS)

Module Outcomes

At the end of the module, the learner will be able to:

  1. Apply the concepts of demography
  2. Demonstrate understanding of the characteristics of population composition
  3. Demonstrate techniques of standardizing mortality and fertility measures
  4. Demonstrate techniques uses in working out life expectancies of various age groups

Module Content

UNIT CODE: DHR2112 - MEDICAL DEMOGRAPHY I (40HRS)

UNIT 1: Introduction and Concepts of Demography (20 HOURS)

Description of demography, introduction to demography, definition of demography, definition of population studies, measures in demography, rates, ratios, proportions, importance and uses of demographic data, sources of demographic data, census, registration, demographic health surveys, sample surveys, other sources, population growth and policies related to socio-economic development, population growth, definition of population, population growth, population census, factors and effects of population growth, population studies, definition of sex composition, determinants of sex composition, calculations of sex ration, definition of age composition , determinants of age composition, calculation of the age dependency ration, measures of age, population policies.

UNIT 2: Population measures (20 HOURS)

Definition, Population census, Population growth, Factors and effects of population growth, Population control, demographic transition theories, sex composition, Determinants of sex composition, Calculations of sex ratio, age composition, Determinants of age composition, Calculation of the age dependency ratio, Measures of age, Population Pyramids.

UNIT CODE: DHR2212 - MEDICAL DEMOGRAPHY II (40HRS)

Fertility and Mortality Measures (40 HOURS)

Description and analysis of fertility, explain fertility, description and calculations of measure of fertility, child woman ratio, Crude birth rate (CBR), General fertility rate (GFR), Age Specific fertility rate (ASFR), Total fertility rate (TFR), other Specific fertility rate (SFR), description and analysis of mortality, description of mortality, description of historical background/development of mortality measurement, description of mortality characterization, description and calculation of measures of mortality, Crude death rate, Age Specific death rate, Neonatal death rate, Infant mortality rate, Child mortality rate, Still birth, Case fatality rate, Maternal mortality rate.

UNIT CODE: DHR3112 - MEDICAL DEMOGRAPHY III (20HRS)

UNIT 4: Construction of life tables

1: Life tables

Construction of life tables.

TEACHING METHODOLOGY

  • Lectures
  • Group discussions
  • Demonstration
  • Assignments

ASSESSMENT METHODS

  • CATs
  • Take-away assignments
  • End-of-semester exam

REFERENCES

  1. An Introduction to Demography by Dudley L : June 2010
  2. Demographic Methods and Concepts: Donald T. Rowland
  3. The Methods and Materials of Demography by Shryock AS, Siege JS
  4. The Methods and Materials of Demography by David A. Swanson, Jacob S.Siege

13. MODULE TITLE: RESEARCH METHODOLOGY

Module Overview

TIME: 60 HOURS

CREDITS: 6 CREDITS

MODULE COMPETENCE

This course is designed to equip the learner with the necessary knowledge, skills, and attitudes in research methodology to enable him/her to participate in or carry out research related to health care and community.

Units Covered:

  • UNIT 1: Introduction to Research & Identification of a Problem (20 HOURS)
  • UNIT 2: Research Design/Methodology (20 HOURS)
  • UNIT 3: Findings, Discussions, and Recommendations (10 HOURS)
  • UNIT 4: Report Writing/Documentation (10 HOURS)

Module Outcomes

At the end of the module the learner will be able to:

  1. Describe research and its contribution toward solving priority problems in health care
  2. Design and carry out research

Module Content

UNIT CODE; DHR2113 RESEARCH METHODOLOGY

1: Introduction to Research

Definition of research; uses of research; types of research (experimental and non-experimental). Approaches to identifying a research problem; statement of the problem; justification; development of objectives; research questions/hypothesis; review of literature; sources of information; citing references; research plan/proposal stage.

2: Research Design/Methodology

Research format and research design; description of sample and sampling techniques (probability and non-probability); study area; population and sub-populations; variables. Ethical considerations; inclusion exclusion criterion; construction of research instrument; pilot survey; pre-testing; actual data collection.

UNIT CODE; DHR3220 RESEARCH PROJECT

PRACTICUM

Design and carry out research in the field.

UNIT 3: Findings, Discussions, and Recommendations

Data cleaning and classification; data processing and analysis; discussions and recommendations.

Report Writing /Documentation

Format; steps.

TEACHING METHODOLOGY

  • Lectures
  • Group discussions/group work
  • Self-directed learning

ASSESSMENT METHODS

CATs, quizzes, assignments written reports/dissertation. End of semester exam (multiple choices and essay questions).
