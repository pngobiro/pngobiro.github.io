09. MODULE TITLE: COMMUNITY HEALTH

Module Overview

TIME: 140 HOURS

CREDITS: 12 CREDITS

MODULE COMPETENCE

This module is designed to enable the learner to acquire the necessary knowledge, skills, and attitudes on sociodemographic characteristics for carrying out community diagnosis and research.

Units Covered:

  • UNIT 1: Introduction Concepts in Community Health (06 HOURS)
  • UNIT 2: Communicable and Non-communicable diseases (16 HOURS)
  • UNIT 3: Disease prevention (18 HOURS)
  • UNIT 4: Community Diagnosis (08 HOURS)
  • UNIT 5: Community Health Surveys (08 HOURS)
  • UNIT 6: Community Strategy (08 HOURS)
  • UNIT 7: Environmental and Health (08 HOURS)
  • UNIT 8: Drug and Substance Abuse (08 HOURS)
  • UNIT 9: Primary Health Care (24 HOURS)
  • UNIT 10: HIV/AIDs and STIs (16 HOURS)
  • UNIT 11: Maternal and Child Health (06 HOURS)
  • UNIT 12: Nutrition and Health (06 HOURS)
  • UNIT 13: Planning and Evaluating Community Health Services (08 HOURS)

Module Outcomes

At the end of the module, the learner will be able to:

  1. Apply knowledge and skills in designing and implementing community health interventions
  2. Apply knowledge and skills in monitoring and evaluation of community health interventions
  3. Apply knowledge and skills in conducting community research

Module Content

UNIT CODE: DHR 1109 COMMUNITY HEALTH I

1: Introduction and Concepts in Community Health

Concepts, aims, services; definition of terms used in community health (population coverage, high risk groups, need for health services , demand for health services, primary health care); needs and demands of health services; risk of diseases; services in primary health care; problem solving in community medicine; MDGs.

2: Communicable and Non-Communicable diseases

Introduction to communicable diseases, epidemiologic triangle model of disease causation (agent, host, and environment); the transmission cycle; principles for managing communicable disease; investigation and control of epidemics; methods of control and eradication of diseases; introduction to non-communicable diseases; risk factors; prevention strategies, basic treatment, community participation in disease control programmes.

3: Disease Prevention

Primary, secondary, and tertiary prevention; health education at all stages.

UNIT CODE: DHR 1209 - COMMUNITY HEALTH II

1: Community Diagnosis

Description, steps, aims; types of information in community diagnosis and intervention sources; evaluation of community diagnosis.

2: Community Health Services

Definition of term; types of survey; reasons for carrying out surveys; planning surveys; biased sample and response rates in relation to survey; conducting the survey; evaluation; feedback; errors that may lead to inaccuracy when completing survey tools; points to be considered in constructing a questionnaires; various methods of presenting information; plan a community survey.

3: Community Strategy

Introduction, objectives; roles of households and communities; implementation framework and process; community mobilization; involvement and participation, procedures of community-based research; income-generating activities, skills in training community resource persons.

4: Environment and Health

Definition of key terms; environmental factors that affect our health; relationship of individual, family, and community; members of health teams and their responsibilities; how beliefs and customs affects the health of an individual; role of community in preserving the environment.

5: Drug and Substance Abuse

Definition of key terms; classification of drugs; commonly abused drugs; effects of drugs (individuals, households, and community); illness associated with drug abuse; behavior change communication (BCC) strategy.

UNIT CODE: DHR 3109 - COMMUNITY HEALTH III

1: Primary Health Care

Definition of primary health care/community based health care; principles and concepts; strategies; elements; levels of health care services; roles and functions of Health Records and Information Officer in the implementation of primary health care programmes; roles and functions of community and health workers in the primary health care programmes.

2: HIV/AIDS and STIs

Causes; spread; impacts; management; prevention.

UNIT CODE: DHR 3209 - COMMUNITY HEALTH IV

1: Maternal and Child Health

Introduction; services (immunization, family planning, antenatal); health education; community-based maternal and child health care.

2: Nutrition and Health

Introduction; basic nutrition; community nutrition; nutrition in pregnancy and lactation; malnutrition and other related conditions.

c: Planning and Evaluation Community Health Services

Introduction; objectives; community health cycle; choosing priorities; setting goals; organizations; selecting measures of success; outcomes; evaluation of programmes.

Practical Session: Community Diagnosis and Planning a Community Survey

Visit a community unit; observe the organization of health services at the community unit; data collection tools; linkage between community unit and facility; community diagnosis; plan a community survey.

TEACHING METHODOLOGY

  • Lectures
  • Group discussions
  • Practical (community diagnosis and plan a community survey)
  • Assignments
