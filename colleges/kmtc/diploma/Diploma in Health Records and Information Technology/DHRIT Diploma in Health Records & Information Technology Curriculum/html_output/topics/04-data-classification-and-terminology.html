05. MODULE TITLE: HEALTH DATA CLASSIFICATION

Module Overview

TIME: 60 HOURS

CREDITS: 6 CREDITS

Units Covered:

  • UNIT 1: Introduction and the Structure of ICD and ICPM (08 HOURS)
  • UNIT 2: Organization and Categories of ICD and ICPM (12 HOURS)
  • UNIT 3: Coding and Indexing (10 HOURS)
  • UNIT 4: Certification of Underlying Causes of Death (16 HOURS)
  • UNIT 5: Statistical Presentation (14 HOURS)

Module Outcomes

At the end of the module the learner will be able to:

  1. Describe the structure and categories of ICD and ICPM
  2. Code and index diseases and underlying causes of disease
  3. Explain the certification of underlying causes of death
  4. Analyze and present disease and underlying causes of death
  5. Analyze and present reports

Module Content

UNIT CODE: DHR1105 HEALTH DATA CLASSIFICATION I (40 HOURS)

1: INTRODUCTION AND STRUCTURE OF ICD AND ICPM

Definition, importance and historical development of ICD and ICPM; prominent earlier classifications; current ICD and ICPM; general arrangement of ICD and ICPM operations and their application.

2: ORGANIZATION AND CATEGORIES OF ICD AND ICPM

Organization of Vol. I and Vol. II of ICD books; organization of both Vol. I and Vol. II of ICPM; tabular list Vol. I; diseases, nature of injury, alphabetical index Vol. II; diseases, injuries and supplemental classification; adverse effects of drugs and other chemical substances; comparison of the two editions; arrangement of ICPM Vol. I and vol. II; alphabetical index; numerical index of procedures in medicine; introduction chapters and the categories.

3: CODING AND INDEXING

Definition; purpose; principles, and procedures, standardization, management, planning, research and teaching; statistical analysis; systematic arrangement of health information: primary arrangement (indices ad modifier), eponyms (main entries, modifiers, adjectives, comparison of previous editions and present editions); tabular list; category codes; use of category codes; use of more than one code; combined category code; use of inclusions and exclusions; provisional diagnosis; dual classification; coding of neoplasms (classification; grouping, Specific coding instruction, special consideration, undetermined primary site, metastatic site, suspected neoplasm rule out); use of M-code; mental disorders; combined category codes; mental conditions and physical condition; intended primarily for psychiatric conditions; use of two codes to express mental and physical condition; mental retardations, forth digit; sub-divisions; obstetric coding (pregnancy, normal, completion of pregnancy, combine category code for certain condition complication pregnancy, associated conditions (abortions, deliveries, normal complicated, premature, multiple and still birth, obstetrical procedures, tabular list of operation, spontaneous deliveries), postpartum conditions.

UNIT CODE: DHR1205 HEALTH DATA CLASSIFICATION II (30 HOURS)

1: CERTIFICATION OF UNDERLYING CAUSES OF DEATH

Rules and guidelines for morbidity and mortality: causes of death recorded; selecting the underlying cause; determine the originating antecedent cause of death; general principle of selecting he guiding rules; clinical judgment in completing the medical certificate of cause of death; limiting the rage of diagnosis; accuracy and usefulness of the report.

2: STATISTICAL PRESENTATION

Definition of terms; sources of obtaining mortality data; selection; reporting requirements related to fetal, perinatal, neonatal, and infant mortality; presentation and statistical analysis; definition of data security ad confidentiality.

TEACHING METHODOLOGY

  • Lecture
  • Focus group discussion
  • Brainstorming
  • Student-centered
  • Self-directed learning
  • Practical integrated with other modules
  • Practices/exercises

ASSESSMENT METHODS

Theory paper examinations (mid-semester, end-of-semester, and final qualifying examinations):

  • Multiple choice questions
  • Matching variable statements
  • True or false answer questions
  • Essay questions

Practical examinations

  • OSPEs based on those in the prescribed practical assessment centres, including but not limited to national, provincial and district hospitals

REFERENCES

  1. ICD-10
  2. ICPM

06. MODULE TITLE; MEDICAL TERMINOLOGY

Module Overview

TIME: 60 HOURS

CREDITS: 6 CREDITS

Units Covered:

  • UNIT 1: Introduction to Medical Terminology (04 HOURS)
  • UNIT 2: components of Medical Terms (06 HOURS)
  • UNIT 3: Terms related to Body Systems (20 HOURS)
  • UNIT 4: Medical Abbreviation (15 HOURS)
  • UNIT 5: Disease and Treatment (15 HOURS)

Module Outcomes

At the end of each unit the learner will be able to:

  1. Explain the scope and nature of terminology used in medicine
  2. Identify words that describe general anatomical features, medical tools, equipment, and procedures
  3. Identify words that describe pharmacological terms
  4. Identify words that describe physiological and anatomical features in the body parts and systems

Module Content

UNIT CODE: DHR1106 MEDICAL TERMINOLOGY - I (Time: 40 hours)

1: INTRODUCTION TO MEDICAL TERMINOLOGY

Definition of medical terminology; importance of medical terminology; origin (historical background) of medical terms.

2: COMPONENT OF MEDICAL TERMS

Component part of medical terms; commonly used prefixes, suffixes, stems, medical abbreviation, signs, and symbols in disease and procedures in medicine; component parts of medical and pharmacological terms; examples of prefixes, suffixes, and terms.

3: TERMS RELATED TO BODY SYSTEMS

Disease and conditions of each body system; terms related to diagnostic procedures of each body system; abbreviations used in each body system.

UNIT CODE: DHR1206 MEDICAL TERMINOLOGY - II (Time: 40 hours)

1: MEDICAL ABBREVIATION

Abbreviations used in each body system.

2: DISEASE AND TREATMENT

Describe types of diseases and infections; responses to diseases; word part pertaining to diseases; diagnosis and treatment; surgery; cancer.

TEACHING METHODOLOGY

  • Lecture
  • Focus group discussion
  • Brainstorming
  • Student-centered
  • Self-directed learning
  • Practical integrated with other modules
  • Practices/exercises

ASSESSMENT METHODS

Theory paper examinations (cats, end-of-semester, and final qualifying examinations):

  • Multiple choice questions
  • Matching variable statements
  • True or false answer questions
  • Essay questions

Practical examinations

  • OSPEs based on those in the prescribed practical assessment centres, including but not limited to national, county and sub-county hospitals.
