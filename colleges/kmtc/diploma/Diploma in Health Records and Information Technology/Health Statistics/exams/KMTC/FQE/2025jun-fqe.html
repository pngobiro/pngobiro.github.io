41.
The mean and mode of given data is given as 30 and 34 respectively what would be the median of the data set (5 Marks)
Show Answer

Answer:

We use the empirical relationship between Mean, Median, and Mode:

$$Mode = 3(Median) - 2(Mean)$$

Given:
Mean = 30
Mode = 34

Substitute the values:

$$34 = 3(Median) - 2(30)$$

$$34 = 3(Median) - 60$$

$$34 + 60 = 3(Median)$$

$$94 = 3(Median)$$

$$Median = 94 / 3$$

$$Median \approx 31.33$$

42.
Its imperative to observe some rules when presenting data using bar charts. Briefly explain five points to consider when constructing bar charts (5 Marks)
Show Answer

Answer:

  1. Equal Width: All bars should have the same width to avoid visual bias.
  2. Equal Spacing: The gap between bars should be uniform and typically smaller than the width of the bars.
  3. Scaling: The y-axis (frequency) should start at zero to prevent exaggeration of differences.
  4. Labeling: Both axes (x and y) must be clearly labeled, and the chart should have a descriptive title.
  5. Ordering: For qualitative data, bars can be arranged in ascending or descending order (Pareto chart) to enhance readability, unless there is a natural order (e.g., months).
43.
In a sample of 25 patients, the probability of a patient recovering from a certain disease is 0.8. What is the probability that exactly three will recover? (5 Marks)
Show Answer

Answer:

This follows a Binomial Distribution formula: $P(X=k) = \binom{n}{k} p^k q^{n-k}$

  • $n = 25$ (sample size)
  • $p = 0.8$ (probability of success/recovery)
  • $q = 1 - p = 0.2$ (probability of failure)
  • $k = 3$ (number of recoveries)

$$P(X=3) = \binom{25}{3} (0.8)^3 (0.2)^{22}$$

Calculation:

$$\binom{25}{3} = \frac{25 \times 24 \times 23}{3 \times 2 \times 1} = 2300$$

$$P(X=3) = 2300 \times 0.512 \times (4.19 \times 10^{-16})$$

$$P(X=3) \approx 4.9 \times 10^{-13}$$

Note: The probability is extremely small because with an 80% recovery rate, it is highly unlikely that only 3 out of 25 patients will recover.

44.
A population was found to have a mean height of 171.17cm and standard deviation 3.30cm. Using sample of 400 male students with a mean height of 171.38 cm, test whether there is a difference between the means at 95% confidence level. (5 Marks)
Show Answer

Answer:

We use the Z-test formula since sample size is large ($n > 30$).

$$Z = \frac{\bar{x} - \mu}{\sigma / \sqrt{n}}$$

Given:
$\bar{x} = 171.38$ (Sample mean)
$\mu = 171.17$ (Population mean)
$\sigma = 3.30$ (Population SD)
$n = 400$

$$Z = \frac{171.38 - 171.17}{3.30 / \sqrt{400}}$$

$$Z = \frac{0.21}{3.30 / 20}$$

$$Z = \frac{0.21}{0.165} \approx 1.27$$

Conclusion: At 95% confidence level, the critical Z-value is 1.96. Since the calculated Z (1.27) is less than 1.96, we fail to reject the null hypothesis. There is no significant difference between the sample mean and the population mean.

45.
Briefly explain the assumptions of ANOVA (5 Marks)
Show Answer

Answer:

  1. Normality: The values in each group are normally distributed.
  2. Homogeneity of Variance (Homoscedasticity): The variance within each group is approximately equal.
  3. Independence of Observations: The samples are independent of each other (no relationship between participants in different groups).
  4. Random Sampling: Samples are selected randomly from the population.
  5. Additivity: The effects of the factors are additive.
46.
Distinguish between a histogram and a bar chart construction (5 Marks)
Show Answer

Answer:

  • Data Type: Histograms are used for continuous data (e.g., weight, height), while bar charts are used for categorical/discrete data.
  • Spacing: In histograms, bars touch each other to show continuity. In bar charts, there are gaps between bars.
  • X-Axis: The x-axis in a histogram represents continuous class intervals. In a bar chart, it represents distinct categories.
  • Width of Bars: In histograms, the width of the bar has meaning (represents class interval size). In bar charts, width is arbitrary (though usually kept uniform).
  • Area: The area of a bar in a histogram represents the frequency. In a bar chart, the height represents the frequency.
