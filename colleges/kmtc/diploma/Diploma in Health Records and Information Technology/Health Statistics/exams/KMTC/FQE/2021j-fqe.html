6.
The first quartiles divide a frequency distribution in the ratio of
a) 4:1
b) 1:4
c) 3:1
d) 1:3
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) 1:3

Explanation: The first quartile ($Q1$) marks the 25th percentile. It divides the distribution into the bottom 25% and the top 75%. The ratio is $25:75$, which simplifies to $1:3$.

7.
The following are measures of dispersion except
a) Mean
b) Mean deviation
c) Skewness
d) Standard deviation
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Mean

Explanation: Mean is a measure of central tendency. Mean deviation, Standard deviation, and Range are measures of dispersion. (Note: Skewness is a measure of shape/symmetry, but Mean is definitely central tendency, making it the primary exception here).

  2. Brevity: Keep questions short and to the point.

42.
Describe the stratified sampling method approach (5 marks)
Show Answer

Answer:

  1. Identify Strata: Divide the population into distinct, non-overlapping subgroups (strata) based on a specific characteristic (e.g., age, gender).
  2. Determine Sample Size: Decide the total sample size required.
  3. Allocate Sample: Determine the number of participants to select from each stratum (often proportional to the stratum's size in the population).
  4. Random Selection: Use simple random sampling to select subjects from within each stratum.
  5. Combine: Combine the selected subjects to form the final sample.
43.

Show Answer

Answer:

  1. Aggregate of Facts: Statistics deals with groups of numbers, not single isolated figures.
  2. Numerically Expressed: Data must be quantitative or capable of being counted.
  3. Affected by Multiplicity of Causes: Statistics are influenced by various factors, not just one.
  4. Enumerated or Estimated: Data is collected through counting or estimation with reasonable accuracy.
  5. Systematic Collection: Data must be collected in a planned and systematic manner for a specific purpose.

46.
In the year 2018, Kocholia sub-county hospital had 800 beds permanently allocated for in-patient use during the same period the percentage occupancy was 110% and 1500 patients were discharged home alive while 20 died. Calculate:
(i) Turn over per bed (3 marks)
(ii) Available bed days ABD (2 marks)
Show Answer

Answer:

Given:
Beds = 800
Occupancy = 110%
Discharges = 1500
Deaths = 20
Total Discharges & Deaths ($D\&D$) = $1500 + 20 = 1520$.

(i) Turnover per Bed (TOB):
Formula: Total Discharges & Deaths / Average Bed Count
$TOB = 1520 / 800 = 1.9$ patients per bed.

(ii) Available Bed Days (ABD):
Formula: Bed Count $\times$ Days in Year (2018)
$ABD = 800 \times 365 = 292,000$ bed days.

49.
A random of 50 male employees is taken at the end of a year and the mean number of hours of absenteeism for the year is found to be 63 hours. A similar sample of 50 female employees has a mean of 66 hours. Could these samples be drawn from a population with the same mean and standard deviation of 10 hours? (10 marks)
Show Answer

Answer:

1. Hypotheses:
$H_0$: $\mu_1 = \mu_2$ (Means are equal, samples from same population)
$H_1$: $\mu_1 \neq \mu_2$ (Means are different)

2. Given Data:
$n_1 = 50$, $\bar{x}_1 = 63$
$n_2 = 50$, $\bar{x}_2 = 66$
Population SD ($\sigma$) = 10 (Assumed common for both)

3. Test Statistic (Z-test for two means):
Formula: $Z = \frac{(\bar{x}_1 - \bar{x}_2) - (\mu_1 - \mu_2)}{\sqrt{\frac{\sigma^2}{n_1} + \frac{\sigma^2}{n_2}}}$
$Z = \frac{63 - 66}{\sqrt{\frac{100}{50} + \frac{100}{50}}}$
$Z = \frac{-3}{\sqrt{2 + 2}} = \frac{-3}{\sqrt{4}} = \frac{-3}{2} = -1.5$

4. Critical Value:
At 95% confidence ($\alpha = 0.05$), critical Z = $\pm 1.96$.

5. Conclusion:
$|-1.5| < 1.96$.
We Fail to Reject $H_0$.
Yes, these samples could be drawn from a population with the same mean. The difference is not statistically significant.

50.
In 2001, Moding Health Centre recorded 11,676 inpatients days, 130 discharges and death and the health facility has a capacity of 30 beds. Calculate (10 marks)

(i) ABD (1 mark)

(ii) EVBD (1 mark)

(iii) Percentage occupancy (1 mark)

(iv) Average length of stay (ALOS) (2 marks)

(v) Supposing this unit represent 25% of the entire health facility bed and inpatients days. Calculate the entire health facility statistics. (5 marks)

Show Answer

Answer:

(i) Available Bed Days (ABD):
$30 \text{ beds} \times 365 \text{ days} = 10,950$ (Note: IPSD 11,676 > ABD 10,950 implies >100% occupancy or temporary beds).

(ii) Empty/Vacant Bed Days (EVBD):
Usually $ABD - IPSD$. Here, $10,950 - 11,676 = -726$.
Since negative vacancy is impossible, it implies overcrowding (EVBD = 0 effectively, or expressed as overcrowding of 726 bed-days).

(iii) Percentage Occupancy:
$(IPSD / ABD) \times 100$
$(11,676 / 10,950) \times 100 = 106.6\%$.

(iv) Average Length of Stay (ALOS):
$IPSD / (D\&D)$
$11,676 / 130 = 89.8$ days. (Very long stay, perhaps a chronic care facility or data anomaly).

(v) Entire Facility Statistics (if unit is 25%):
If Unit Bed Days = 25% of Total, Total Bed Days = $10,950 / 0.25 = 43,800$.
If Unit IPSD = 25% of Total, Total IPSD = $11,676 / 0.25 = 46,704$.
Total Beds = $30 / 0.25 = 120$.
