43.
A die having 4 red and 2 yellow faces is thrown twice. For each red face showing, the thrower is paid ksh 90 and for each yellow face ksh 270 is paid. What is the expectation? (5 Marks)
Show Answer

Answer:

Probability of Red ($P(R)$) = $4/6 = 2/3$. Payout = 90.

Probability of Yellow ($P(Y)$) = $2/6 = 1/3$. Payout = 270.

Expected value per throw ($E(X)$):

$E(X) = (2/3 \times 90) + (1/3 \times 270)$

$E(X) = 60 + 90 = 150$.

Since the die is thrown twice, the total expectation is:

$150 \times 2 = \text{Ksh } 300$.

44.
What is the probability of having 2 girls and 1 boy in a three-child family if the probability of having a boy is 0.5? (5 Marks)
Show Answer

Answer:

Using Binomial Distribution: $P(X=k) = \binom{n}{k} p^k q^{n-k}$

  • $n = 3$ (children)
  • $k = 2$ (girls)
  • $p = 0.5$ (prob of girl, assuming boy is 0.5)

$P(X=2) = \binom{3}{2} (0.5)^2 (0.5)^1$

$P(X=2) = 3 \times 0.25 \times 0.5$

$P(X=2) = 0.375$ (or 3/8)

46.
Compute the median of the following set of data (5 Marks)
Minutes 0-4 4-8 8-12 12-16 16-20 Total
Frequency 11 13 7 9 4 44
Show Answer

Answer:

1. Calculate Cumulative Frequency (CF):
0-4: 11
4-8: 24 (11+13)
8-12: 31
12-16: 40
16-20: 44

2. Find Median Class: $N/2 = 44/2 = 22$. The 22nd value falls in the **4-8** class.

3. Formula: $Median = L + \frac{(N/2 - CF)}{f} \times w$
$L = 4$ (Lower limit)
$CF = 11$ (CF of previous class)
$f = 13$ (Frequency of median class)
$w = 4$ (Class width)

$Median = 4 + \frac{(22 - 11)}{13} \times 4$

$Median = 4 + \frac{11}{13} \times 4$

$Median = 4 + 0.846 \times 4$

$Median = 4 + 3.38 = 7.38$ minutes

48.
In the year 2004, Hospital B recorded the following data: 280 allocated beds, 24,800 Inpatient Service Days, deaths and discharges 3248, 120 beds were closed for four months with effect from 1st September 2004 to 31st December, 2004. On 1st January, 2005 they were opened to make the hospital become fully operational. Calculate
a) Daily average occupancy
b) Average available beds
Show Answer

Answer:

Given:
Year 2004 (Leap year, 366 days).
Standard Beds = 280.
120 beds closed for Sep, Oct, Nov, Dec (30+31+30+31 = 122 days).
Inpatient Service Days (IPSD) = 24,800.

a) Daily Average Occupancy:

Formula: Total IPSD / Days in Year

$24,800 / 366 = 67.76$ patients.

b) Average Available Beds:

1. Calculate Total Available Bed Days:
Days with 280 beds: Jan-Aug ($366 - 122 = 244$ days).
$280 \times 244 = 68,320$.
Days with $280 - 120 = 160$ beds: Sep-Dec (122 days).
$160 \times 122 = 19,520$.
Total Available Bed Days = $68,320 + 19,520 = 87,840$.

2. Calculate Average:
Total Bed Days / Days in Year
$87,840 / 366 = 240$ beds.

49.

a) Define the following terms as used in correlation: (5 Marks)

  • Correlation
  • Coefficient
  • Spurious correlation
  • Covariance
  • Rank correlation

b) The finance division of a large company is investigating its procedures for the selection of new accountancy trainees. Potential applicants are given prior to appointment both a written test and formal interview. The performances of eight successful applicants were rated after their first full year with the company. The independent rankings of written test, interview assessment and job performance for the eight trainees are given as follows: (1 = best, 2 second best etc)

TraineeABCDEFGH
Written Test62741538
Interview14236587
Job Performance12345678

You are required to:

a) Calculate a rank correlation coefficient between:

  • i. Job performance and written test
  • ii. Job performance and interview assessment

b) Interpret your results (15 Marks)

Show Answer

Answer:

Part A: Definitions

  • Correlation: A statistical measure that indicates the extent to which two or more variables fluctuate together.
  • Coefficient: A numerical value that indicates the strength and direction of the relationship between variables (e.g., Pearson's r).
  • Spurious Correlation: A mathematical relationship in which two or more events or variables are associated but not causally related, due to either coincidence or the presence of a certain third, unseen factor.
  • Covariance: A measure of the joint variability of two random variables (how much they change together).
  • Rank Correlation: A statistical method (like Spearman's) used to discover the strength of a link between two sets of data that are ranked (ordinal data).

Part B: Calculations

Formula for Spearman's Rank Correlation: $\rho = 1 - \frac{6 \sum d^2}{n(n^2 - 1)}$

$n = 8$

i) Job Performance vs Written Test

TraineePerf (Rx)Written (Ry)d (Rx-Ry)d²
A16-525
B2200
C37-416
D4400
E51416
F6511
G73416
H8800
Total74

$\rho = 1 - \frac{6(74)}{8(64-1)} = 1 - \frac{444}{504} = 1 - 0.881 = \mathbf{0.119}$

ii) Job Performance vs Interview Assessment

TraineePerf (Rx)Interview (Ry)d (Rx-Ry)d²
A1100
B24-24
C3211
D4311
E56-11
F6511
G78-11
H8711
Total10

$\rho = 1 - \frac{6(10)}{8(63)} = 1 - \frac{60}{504} = 1 - 0.119 = \mathbf{0.881}$

Part C: Interpretation

1. Written Test ($r \approx 0.12$): There is a very weak positive correlation between the written test scores and job performance. This suggests the written test is a poor predictor of how well a trainee will perform.

2. Interview ($r \approx 0.88$): There is a strong positive correlation between interview assessments and job performance. This indicates that the interview process is a highly effective predictor of future job performance.

Recommendation: The company should rely more on the interview process or revise the written test.
