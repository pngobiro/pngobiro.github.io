41.
Differentiate between the two types of hypothesis and give an example in each case (5 marks)
Show Answer

Answer:

  • Null Hypothesis ($H_0$): A statement that there is no significant difference or relationship between variables. It assumes any observed difference is due to chance.
    Example: "There is no difference in recovery time between Drug A and Drug B."
  • Alternative Hypothesis ($H_1$ or $H_a$): A statement that contradicts the null hypothesis, suggesting a significant difference or relationship exists.
    Example: "Drug A results in faster recovery times than Drug B."
42.
Use the data below for a certain sample to calculate the given measures of central tendency and dispersion (5 marks)
8, 12, 23, 28, 22, 26, 30, 35, 42
a) Mean (2 marks)
b) Standard Deviation(3 marks)
Show Answer

Answer:

Data: 8, 12, 22, 23, 26, 28, 30, 35, 42 ($n=9$)

a) Mean ($\bar{x}$):
Sum = $8+12+22+23+26+28+30+35+42 = 226$.
Mean = $226 / 9 \approx 25.11$.

b) Standard Deviation ($s$):
1. Calculate $(x - \bar{x})^2$:
$(8-25.1)^2 = 292.4$, $(12-25.1)^2 = 171.6$, $(22-25.1)^2 = 9.6$, $(23-25.1)^2 = 4.4$, $(26-25.1)^2 = 0.8$, $(28-25.1)^2 = 8.4$, $(30-25.1)^2 = 24.0$, $(35-25.1)^2 = 98.0$, $(42-25.1)^2 = 285.6$.
2. Sum of Squares $\approx 894.8$.
3. Variance ($s^2$) = $894.8 / (9-1) = 894.8 / 8 = 111.85$.
4. SD ($s$) = $\sqrt{111.85} \approx 10.58$.

43.
Briefly explain the types of correlation (5 marks)
Show Answer

Answer:

  1. Positive Correlation: Both variables move in the same direction (e.g., height and weight).
  2. Negative Correlation: Variables move in opposite directions (e.g., speed and travel time).
  3. Zero/No Correlation: No relationship exists between the variables.
  4. Linear Correlation: The change in one variable bears a constant ratio to the change in the other (straight line graph).
  5. Non-linear (Curvilinear) Correlation: The ratio of change is not constant (curved line).

47.
Using a period of 25 days, allocated bed of 35 and OBD of 900. Average length of stay of 20 days. Calculate
a. Percentage occupancy (2 marks)
b. Discharges and Deaths (2 marks)
c. Turn over per Bed (2 marks)
d. Explain your results in a and c above (4 marks)
Show Answer

Answer:

a. Percentage Occupancy:
Formula: $(OBD / (Beds \times Days)) \times 100$
$= (900 / (35 \times 25)) \times 100$
$= (900 / 875) \times 100 = 102.86\%$.

b. Discharges and Deaths (D&D):
Formula derived from ALOS: $ALOS = OBD / (D\&D)$
$20 = 900 / (D\&D)$
$D\&D = 900 / 20 = 45$.

c. Turnover per Bed (TOB):
Formula: $(D\&D) / Beds$
$= 45 / 35 \approx 1.29$ patients per bed.

d. Explanation:
Occupancy (102.9%): The hospital is overcrowded, operating beyond its capacity. Patients may be sharing beds or using temporary beds.
TOB (1.29): On average, each bed was used by approximately 1.3 patients during the 25-day period, indicating a relatively slow turnover rate given the long ALOS of 20 days.

48.
Test whether there is a significant association between smoking and Lung cancer at 95% confidence level. (10 marks)
Lung Cancer YES Lung Cancer NO Total
SMOKING YES 39 21 60
SMOKING NO 29 19 48
Total 68 40 108
Show Answer

Answer:

1. Hypotheses:
$H_0$: Smoking and Lung Cancer are independent (No association).
$H_1$: Smoking and Lung Cancer are dependent (Significant association).

2. Expected Frequencies ($E$):
Formula: $E = (RowTotal \times ColTotal) / GrandTotal$
$E_{11} (Yes/Yes) = (60 \times 68) / 108 = 37.78$
$E_{12} (Yes/No) = (60 \times 40) / 108 = 22.22$
$E_{21} (No/Yes) = (48 \times 68) / 108 = 30.22$
$E_{22} (No/No) = (48 \times 40) / 108 = 17.78$

3. Calculate $\chi^2$:
$\chi^2 = \sum \frac{(O-E)^2}{E}$
1. $(39-37.78)^2 / 37.78 = 1.488 / 37.78 = 0.039$
2. $(21-22.22)^2 / 22.22 = 1.488 / 22.22 = 0.067$
3. $(29-30.22)^2 / 30.22 = 1.488 / 30.22 = 0.049$
4. $(19-17.78)^2 / 17.78 = 1.488 / 17.78 = 0.084$
Total $\chi^2 = 0.039 + 0.067 + 0.049 + 0.084 = 0.239$.

4. Critical Value:
Degrees of Freedom ($df$) = $(2-1)(2-1) = 1$.
Level of Significance $\alpha = 0.05$.
Critical Value from table = 3.841.

5. Conclusion:
Calculated $\chi^2$ (0.239) < Critical Value (3.841).
We Fail to Reject $H_0$.
There is no significant association between smoking and lung cancer based on this specific dataset (at 95% confidence level).

49.
The finance division of a large company... (Rank Correlation Question). Calculate rank correlation coefficient between: i. Job performance and written test, ii. Job performance and interview. Interpret results. (10 marks)
TraineeABCDEFGH
Written Test62741538
Interview14236587
Job Performance12345678
Show Answer

Answer:

Using Spearman's Rank Correlation: $\rho = 1 - \frac{6 \sum d^2}{n(n^2 - 1)}$ ($n=8$).

i) Job Performance vs Written Test:
$\sum d^2 = 74$.
$\rho = 1 - \frac{6(74)}{504} = 1 - 0.88 = 0.12$.
Interpretation: Weak positive correlation. Written tests are poor predictors of performance.

ii) Job Performance vs Interview:
$\sum d^2 = 10$.
$\rho = 1 - \frac{6(10)}{504} = 1 - 0.12 = 0.88$.
Interpretation: Strong positive correlation. Interviews are excellent predictors of performance.
