Section 1: Multiple Choice Questions

40 Marks
1.
The following are probability distributions except
a) Binomial distribution
b) Frequency distribution
c) Poisson distribution
d) Normal distribution
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Frequency distribution

Explanation: Binomial, Poisson, and Normal are standard probability distributions. A frequency distribution is a method of organizing data, not a probability model itself.

2.
Another name for chain referral sampling method is
a) Snowball
b) Purposive
c) Convenience
d) Quota
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Snowball

Explanation: Chain referral sampling is commonly known as snowball sampling, where existing study subjects recruit future subjects.

3.
The size of a sample depends on the following except
a) The indicators chosen
b) The baseline value of the indicators
c) The amount of change you would want to measure accurately
d) The geographical area
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) The geographical area

Explanation: While geography affects the logistics and method of sampling, the statistical calculation of sample size depends on variance, confidence level, and precision (amount of change), not directly on the physical area size.

4.
Which of the following is not a feature of a standard normal curve
a) Its mean is 1
b) Its standard deviation is 1
c) Its total area is 1
d) Its symmetrical and bell shaped
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Its mean is 1

Explanation: A standard normal distribution has a mean of 0, not 1. Its standard deviation is 1.

5.
The following are examples of errors during sampling except
a) Error of origin
b) Biased error
c) Sampling error
d) Non sampling error
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Error of origin

Explanation: "Error of origin" is not a standard statistical term for survey errors. The main types are sampling errors and non-sampling errors (which include bias).

6.
Which one of the following is NOT true about primary sources of data?
a) It gives very reliable data
b) It is very cheap to conduct
c) There is a possibility of sampling errors
d) It has a short-term value
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) It is very cheap to conduct

Explanation: Primary data collection (surveys, experiments) is typically expensive and time-consuming compared to secondary data collection.

7.
Which of the following is NOT TRUE?
a) A chi square test measures how expectations compare to actual observed data
b) Degrees of freedom is just the number of categories
c) Independent samples in a t-test compares the means for two groups
d) In probability distribution, the mean is a product of probability for success and the sample size

Correct Answer: b) Degrees of freedom is just the number of categories

Section 2: Short Answer Questions

40 Marks
1.
State five types of events in probability (5 marks)
Show Answer

Answer:

  1. Simple Event: An event with a single outcome.
  2. Compound Event: An event involving two or more outcomes.
  3. Independent Events: Events where the occurrence of one does not affect the other.
  4. Dependent Events: Events where the occurrence of one affects the probability of the other.
  5. Mutually Exclusive Events: Events that cannot occur simultaneously.
  6. Exhaustive Events: A set of events that includes all possible outcomes.
2.
List five properties of normal distribution (5 marks)
Show Answer

Answer:

  1. It is bell-shaped.
  2. It is symmetrical about the mean.
  3. The Mean, Median, and Mode are equal.
  4. The total area under the curve is equal to 1 (or 100%).
  5. The curve is asymptotic to the x-axis (approaches but never touches it).
3.
Calculate the mean of the following data: 9, 5, 6, 12, 18, 25, 32, 46, 8, 7, 10, 12, 16, 17, 21, 24, 26, 29, 32, 35, 38 (5 marks)
Show Answer

Answer:

Data: 9, 5, 6, 12, 18, 25, 32, 46, 8, 7, 10, 12, 16, 17, 21, 24, 26, 29, 32, 35, 38.

1. Count (n): There are 21 numbers.

2. Sum ($\sum x$): $9+5+6+12+18+25+32+46+8+7+10+12+16+17+21+24+26+29+32+35+38 = 428$.

3. Mean ($\bar{x}$):
$\bar{x} = \sum x / n$
$\bar{x} = 428 / 21 \approx 20.38$.

4.
Briefly describe the two types of numerical data (5 marks)
Show Answer

Answer:

  1. Discrete Data: Data that can only take specific, separate values (usually integers). There are "gaps" between possible values.
    Example: Number of children in a family (1, 2, 3... not 2.5).
  2. Continuous Data: Data that can take any value within a given range. There are no gaps between values; they are usually measurements.
    Example: Height, Weight, Blood Pressure (can be 165.2 cm).
5.
Outline 5 important points to consider while constructing a histogram (5 marks)
Show Answer

Answer:

  1. Continuous Classes: The x-axis must represent a continuous variable.
  2. No Gaps: Bars must be drawn adjacent to each other (touching) to show continuity.
  3. Area vs Height: The area of the bar represents the frequency (if class widths vary), though height is used if widths are equal.
  4. Class Boundaries: Use real limits (boundaries) for the x-axis labels to ensure no gaps.
  5. Labeling and Title: Clearly label axes (Frequency on Y, Variable on X) and provide a descriptive title.

  1. Range
  2. Interquartile Range (IQR)
  3. Variance
  4. Standard Deviation
  5. Mean Deviation (or Coefficient of Variation)
7.
Briefly explain any 2 methods of data collection (5 marks)
Show Answer

Answer:

  1. Questionnaires: A set of written questions given to respondents to answer. Useful for collecting data from a large population. Can be self-administered or mailed.
  2. Interviews: Direct, face-to-face (or telephone) conversations between the researcher and the respondent. Allows for clarification and in-depth answers but can be time-consuming.
  3. (Alternative) Observation: Recording behavioral patterns of people, objects, and events in a systematic manner without questioning or communicating with them.

Section 3: Long Answer Questions

20 Marks
1.

a) In a study to determine the adoption of EMR systems in Maragua Sub county hospital, 150 health professionals were studied. Determine the sample size if the proportion of the characteristic being measured was 30%. Use the fishers et al formula at 95% confidence level. (10 marks)

b) Pona hospital had a bed complement of 150 and an average length of stay of 7 days. The hospital had a daily occupancy of 119 with bed turn over of 57 patients. At discharge each patient paid 250 shillings per day as fees. Calculate:

  • i. Percentage occupancy (3 marks)
  • ii. Total discharges and death (3 marks)
  • iii. Turn over interval (2 marks)
  • iv. Total fees collected in the year (2 marks)
Show Answer

Answer:

Part A: Sample Size Calculation

Given:
Population ($N$) = 150
Proportion ($P$) = 30% = 0.3
Confidence Level = 95% ($Z = 1.96$)
Precision ($d$) is standard 0.05 (assumed as not given).

Step 1: Calculate Sample Size for Infinite Population ($n_0$)
$n_0 = \frac{Z^2 P(1-P)}{d^2}$
$n_0 = \frac{1.96^2 \times 0.3 \times (1-0.3)}{0.05^2}$
$n_0 = \frac{3.8416 \times 0.21}{0.0025} = \frac{0.8067}{0.0025} \approx 322.7$

Step 2: Adjust for Finite Population ($n$)
Since the calculated $n_0$ (323) > Population (150), use correction.
$n = \frac{n_0}{1 + \frac{n_0 - 1}{N}}$
$n = \frac{322.7}{1 + \frac{321.7}{150}} = \frac{322.7}{1 + 2.145} = \frac{322.7}{3.145} \approx 102.6$

Recommended Sample Size = 103 professionals.

Part B: Hospital Statistics

Given:
Beds = 150
ALOS = 7 days
Daily Occupancy ($OBD_{avg}$) = 119
Period = 1 Year (365 days assumed)

i. Percentage Occupancy:
Formula: $\frac{\text{Daily Occupancy}}{\text{Bed Complement}} \times 100$
$\frac{119}{150} \times 100 = \mathbf{79.33\%}$

ii. Total Discharges and Death ($D\&D$):
Using ALOS formula: $ALOS = \frac{\text{Total Patient Days}}{\text{Total D\&D}}$
Total Patient Days = Daily Occupancy $\times$ 365 = $119 \times 365 = 43,435$.
$7 = \frac{43,435}{D\&D}$
$D\&D = \frac{43,435}{7} = \mathbf{6,205}$ patients.

iii. Turnover Interval ($TOI$):
Formula: $TOI = \frac{\text{Available Bed Days} - \text{Patient Days}}{\text{Discharges}}$
Available Bed Days ($ABD$) = $150 \times 365 = 54,750$.
Patient Days = 43,435.
$TOI = \frac{54,750 - 43,435}{6,205} = \frac{11,315}{6,205} = \mathbf{1.82}$ days.

iv. Total Fees Collected:
Rate = 250 shillings per day.
Total Fees = Total Patient Days $\times$ Rate
$43,435 \times 250 = \mathbf{10,858,750}$ shillings.
