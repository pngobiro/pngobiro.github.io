üìö Medical Specialities

A feature of modern medicine is specialization. Here is an overview of some medical specialities:

  • Emergency Medicine: Concerned with the diagnosis and treatment of conditions resulting from trauma and sudden illness.
  • Anesthesiology: Anesthetists produce loss of sensation to touch or pain under general or local administration of an anesthetic.
  • Andrology: The study of diseases of males, especially masculinity, male reproduction and secondary sex characteristics (e.g. undescended testes, impotence and infertility)
  • Aviation Medicine: The study of diseases and pathologic states arising from air travel.
  • Bacteriology: The study of microorganisms or bacteria.
  • Biochemistry: The chemistry of living organisms and life processes.
  • Cardiology: The study of heart diseases and the functioning of the heart, blood vessels and the circulatory system.
  • Cytogenetics: The study of the origin and the development of cells, especially chromosomes through karyotyping analysis.
  • Cytology: The study of the formation, structure and functions of cells carried out by microscopic examination.
  • Dentistry: The diagnosis and treatment of teeth and associated structures in the oral cavity.
  • Dermatology: The diagnosis and treatment of skin diseases.
  • Endocrinology: The study of the function and diseases of glands of internal secretion and the endocrine system.
  • Epidemiology: The study of distribution and determinants of diseases in human populations, their morbidity and mortality.
  • Forensic Pathology: Forensic pathology pertains to the law; the study of death due to violence, suicide, or a person dying in custody.
  • Gastroenterology: The study of the upper digestive system- the oesophagus, stomach, intestines, gallbladder and liver.
  • Genetics: The study of hereditary, whether it is natural or a variation in development.

üìö More Medical Specialities

Here is an overview of other medical specialities:

  • Gerontology/ Geriatric Medicine: The study of all aspects regarding the aging process and old age.
  • Gynaecology: The study of the diseases of female reproductive, genital, urinary and rectal systems; includes some conditions of the breasts.
  • Haematology: The study of the function, nature and diseases of the blood and blood forming tissues, including the lymphatic system.
  • Histopathology: The study of tissue and the function of its cells.
  • Hyperbaric Medicine: The use of pure oxygen in the treatment of decompression sickness (bends), severe infection, tumor and carbon monoxide poisoning.
  • Immunology: The study of the immune system, natural and acquired body defense from infection, allergies and antibody reaction.
  • Internal Medicine: Treatment used in non-invasive. Also known as physical medicine or general medicine.
  • Microbiology: The study of micro-organisms (e.g. viruses, protozoa, bacteria) and their effects on living cells.
  • Mycology: The study of fungi and fungal diseases such as ringworms.
  • Neonatology: The study of the diseases of the newborn.
  • Nephrology: The study of diseases of the kidney and their associated structures; and treatment given, including dialysis and transplant.
  • Neurology: The study of diseases of nervous system (e.g. brain, spinal cord and nerves)
  • Neuropathology: The investigation of diseases, structural and functional changes in the brain and nervous system. Usually associated with deceased patients.
  • Nuclear Medicine: The use of nuclear radiation (radio-isotopes) in the investigation, diagnosis, and treatment of diseases.
  • Obstetrics: The study of pregnancy, birth and puerperium (6 weeks period after birth)
  • Occupational Medicine: The study of work related diseases, their treatment and prevention.

üìö Medical Suffixes

A suffix is a word element placed at the end of a word that changes the meaning of the word. Changing the suffix changes the meaning of the word. In medical terminology, a suffix usually describes a pathology (disease or abnormality), symptom, surgical or diagnostic procedure, or part of speech. Suffixes can denote singular and plural forms of a word, as well as a part of speech. Suffixes also often determine a term‚Äôs pronunciation.

üìö Grammatical Suffixes

Grammatical suffixes are used to form parts of speech, such as adjectives and nouns. Grammatical suffixes are also used to denote a diminutive form of a word. Here are some examples of the grammatical suffixes used:

Suffix Meaning Example

-al pertaining to neural

-eal pertaining to esophageal

-ic pertaining to thoracic
-ous pertaining to cutaneous
-tic pertaining to acoustic
-esis condition diuresis
-ia condition pneumonia
-ism condition hyperthyroidism

üí° Pro Tip

Pay close attention to the ending of each medical term as this is where you will find the suffix.

üìö Key Takeaways

  • Different medical specialties focus on specific areas of medicine.
  • Suffixes are used to modify word roots and combining forms by describing a condition or procedure.
  • Surgical suffixes are used to describe a type of invasive procedure.
  • Diagnostic suffixes denote a procedure or test performed to identify a disease.
  • Pathological suffixes describe an abnormal condition or disease.
  • Grammatical suffixes form parts of speech or denote diminutives.
