The nervous system includes the brain, spinal cord, nerves, and sensory organs. It works to coordinate body functions, control sensory input, and enable thought and emotions. Here are some medical word elements related to the nervous system:

  • Cerebrum: cerebr/o
  • Cranium (skull): crani/o
  • Tree: dendr/o
  • Brain: encephal/o
  • Ganglion (knot or knot-like mass): gangli/o
  • Glue; (composed of) neuroglial tissue: gli/o
  • Movement: kinesi/o
  • Thin, slender: lept/o
  • Word, phrase: lex/o
  • Meninges (membranes covering brain and spinal cord): mening/o
  • Bone marrow; spinal cord: myel/o
  • Numbness; sleep: narc/o
  • Nerve: neur/o
  • Nerve root: radicul/o
  • Strength: sthen/o
  • Thalamus: thalam/o
  • Sheath (usually refers to meninges): thec/o
  • Tension: ton/o
  • Ventricle (of heart or brain): ventricul/o

  • Eyelid: blephar/o

üìö Pathological Terms

Here are some of the common pathological terms related to the nervous system and the special senses:

  • Cerebrovascular Disease: Disorders of the blood vessels of the brain which may result in a stroke.
  • Seizure Disorders: Conditions characterized by sudden changes in behavior or consciousness due to uncontrolled electrical activity in the brain.
  • Parkinson Disease: A progressive neurological disorder affecting movement.
  • Multiple Sclerosis (MS): A progressive, degenerative disease of the central nervous system.
  • Alzheimer Disease (AD): A progressive neurological disorder that causes memory loss and serious mental deterioration.
  • Labyrinthitis: Inflammation of the inner ear.
  • M√©ni√®re Disease: Disorder of the labyrinth that leads to progressive hearing loss and tinnitus.
  • Otosclerosis: An abnormal hardening of the bones of the middle ear causing hearing loss.
  • Cataracts: Opacities that form on the lens and impair vision.
  • Glaucoma: A condition characterized by increased intraocular pressure caused by a failure of aqueous humor to drain from the eye.
  • Strabismus: A condition in which one eye is misaligned with the other.
  • Macular Degeneration: A deterioration of the macula, the most sensitive portion of the retina.

üìö Diagnostic, Symptomatic, and Related Terms

Here are some of the common symptomatic and diagnostic terms related to the nervous system and special senses:

  • Agnosia: Inability to comprehend sensory stimuli.
  • Asthenia: Weakness, debility or loss of strength.
  • Ataxia: Lack of muscle coordination.
  • Lethargy: Abnormal inactivity or lack of response to stimuli.
  • Palsy: Paralysis, usually partial, and commonly characterized by weakness and shaking or uncontrolled tremor.
  • Syncope: Temporary loss of consciousness due to sudden decline in blood flow to the brain.
  • Tinnitus: Perception of ringing, hissing or other sounds in the ears or head when no external sound is present.
  • Vertigo: Hallucination of movement or feeling of spinning or dizziness.
  • Aura: Premonitory awareness of an approaching physical or mental disorder.
  • Scotoma: A dark or tumor like spot in the visual field.
  • Diplopia: Double Vision.

üìö Therapeutic Procedures

Here are some of the most common therapeutic procedures relating to the nervous system and special senses:

  • Cryosurgery: Exposes abnormal tissue to extreme cold to destroy it.
  • Laser Ablation: A procedure to remove or treat varicose veins using heat to collapse the blood vessel.
  • Trabeculectomy: Creation of a passage in the eye for aqueous humor to drain.
  • Transluminal coronary angioplasty (PTCA): A procedure to widen the lumen of a diseased blood vessel by inserting a balloon.
  • Cochlear Implant: An artificial hearing device that produces useful hearing sensations by electrically stimulating nerves inside the inner ear.
  • Enucleation: Removal of the eyeball from the orbit.
  • Evisceration: Removal of the contents of the eye while leaving the sclera and cornea intact.
  • Pneumatic Otoscopy: Procedure that assesses the ability of the tympanic membrane to move.

üí° Pro Tip

Note that while you are studying different disorders and procedures related to the nervous system and special senses, many of the terms include elements you have studied in previous modules. By memorizing root words, combining forms, suffixes, and prefixes you can easily decode new terms.

üìö Key Takeaways

  • The nervous system is responsible for coordinating body functions, controlling sensory input, and enabling thought and emotions.
  • The special senses allow the body to interact with the world.
  • Common pathologies include stroke, seizures, multiple sclerosis, and Alzheimer's.
  • Diagnostic procedures often include EEGs, MRIs, and CT Scans.
  • Therapeutic procedures often include surgery, laser treatment, radiation, and medications.
