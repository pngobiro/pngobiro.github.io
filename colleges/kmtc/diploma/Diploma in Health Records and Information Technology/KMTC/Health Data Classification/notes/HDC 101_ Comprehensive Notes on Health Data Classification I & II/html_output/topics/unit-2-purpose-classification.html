UNIT 2: PURPOSE OF DISEASE CLASSIFICATION

Purpose of Disease Classification

  • To permit the systematic recording, analysis, interpretation and comparison of mortality and morbidity data collected in different countries or areas and at different times.
  • To translate diagnoses of conditions and other health problems from words into an alphanumeric code, which permits easy storage, retrieval and analysis.

Importance of Classification of Diseases

  • It provides a common language for reporting and monitoring diseases. This allows the world to compare and share data in a consistent and standard way - between hospitals, regions and countries and over periods of time.
  • It facilitates the collection, storage and retrieval of data for analysis and evidence-based decision-making.
  • It allows systematic recording, analysis and interpretation of mortality and morbidity data.
  • It allows standard comparison of data between hospitals, counties, regions, countries and globally
  • It allows comparisons of morbidity and mortality in the same location across different time periods.

Uses of Diseases Classification

  • Epidemiological studies (Comparisons, Case definitions, Measure burdens of disease)
  • Clinical research
  • Health services planning and management
  • Assessing the effectiveness of care
  • Resource allocations
