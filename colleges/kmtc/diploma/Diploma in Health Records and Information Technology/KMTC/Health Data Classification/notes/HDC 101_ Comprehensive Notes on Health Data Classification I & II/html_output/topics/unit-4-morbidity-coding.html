UNIT 4: CODING AND INDEXING MORBIDITY DATA AND RELATED RULES

Coding

Description

  • It is the translating of narrative descriptions of diseases, injuries and procedures into numeric codes
  • The coding process involves assigning numbers to disease and procedural terms
  • The principal source of coded information is the medical record. The medical record lists final diagnosis and operations and is completed by the attending doctor
  • A code number for each disease and operation is recorded in the system by the medical records assistant

What to Code

  • Main condition or primary diagnosis
  • Other or secondary conditions
  • Procedures, operations and interventions
  • External Causes of Injury (circumstances and specific sites of injury)

Definition of terms

Morbidity usually relates to a single episode of health care. An episode of health care may be defined as:

  • A period of inpatient care,
    or
  • Contact (or a series of contacts in a specific time period) with a health care practitioner in relation to the same condition or its immediate consequences

Morbidity Coding is the assignment of diagnoses written in a medical record or other source document at the end of an episode of care, to the ICD standard

Description of terms

Main condition

For morbidity classification, the definition of main condition has been revised to:

  • 'The one condition that is determined to be the reason for encounter or admission, established at the end of the episode of health care'.
  • The definition of main condition relates to describing an episode of hospital-based care
  • The main condition recorded should be accepted for coding and reporting
  • unless it is obvious that the health care practitioner did not follow the guidelines for recording diagnostic information for morbidity data analysis
  • Whenever possible, a record with an obviously inconsistent or incorrectly recorded main condition should be returned to the health care practitioner for clarification.
  • If clarification of potential erroneous documentation is not possible,
  • one of the morbidity coding rules for reselection of main condition can be applied by the clinical coder and
  • the main condition reselected for reporting purposes.

Multiple conditions contributing to need for admission

  • Where an episode of health care concerns more than one condition contributing to the need for admission
  • (e.g. congestive heart failure and pneumonia;
  • acute cerebral haemorrhage and hip fracture
  • multiple injuries - concussion, rib fracture, right femur fracture after MVA;
  • or influenza A and Type 1 diabetic ketoacidosis),
  • the health care practitioner should record/identify the main condition to be the one condition that is seemed to be most clinically significant reason for admission.

Other conditions

  • In addition to the main condition, documentation should also list separately all other conditions or problems dealt with during the episode of healthcare.

Other conditions are defined as: 'those conditions that coexist or develop during the episode of health care and affect the management of the patient'.
* Conditions related to an earlier episode that have no bearing on the current episode should not be recorded as other conditions.

Morbidity Coding Responsibility

  • The clinician documents the diagnoses
  • The coder translates the clinical documentation into code(s)
  • The responsibility for accurate documentation rests with the treating clinician and the responsibility for accurate codes rest with the coder
  • When coding a morbidity, case notes or discharge summary is used
  • The coder should therefore consult with the certifier
  • Do not use the case notes for mortality coding

ℹ️ Note

Coding rules for morbidity and mortality are different

Morbidity coding

Introduction

  • ICD-11 allows for the classification of clinical concepts, to provide systematic and standardized recording, analysis, interpretation and comparison of morbidity data
  • While some statistical reporting is conducted within a research context, it is also applied to inform health care system and public health care agency decision making, resource allocation, casemix, patient safety and quality of care, as well as primary care and research.
  • To standardize production of morbidity data, ICD-11 has morbidity and sanctioning rules used in data collection, coding and classification.

The rules are for international reporting and analyzing purposes but are also recommended for standard national use.

Coder guidelines for selecting 'Main condition' and 'Other Conditions' for coding purposes

Morbidity Coding Rule MB1: Several conditions recorded as 'main condition'

  • If several different conditions (that cannot be classified to a single stem code) are recorded as the 'main condition', and other details on the record point to one of them being the 'main condition' (one condition determined to be the reason for admission established at the end of the episode of care), select that condition; otherwise, select the condition first recorded.
  • If there is the desire to also report other discharge diagnosis types i.e. main resource condition or initial reason for encounter or admission, then the applicable extension code(s) from Section X Extension codes, should be assigned to indicate the different types of discharge diagnosis that are reported.

Morbidity Coding Rule MB1: Example 1

  • A patient who has a history of Chronic obstructive pulmonary disease (COPD) was admitted for a biopsy of the prostate. Patient was evaluated for COPD. Biopsy was performed and the final diagnosis from pathology results was benign prostatic hypertrophy.
  • Main condition as per the patient discharge summary:
    Chronic obstructive pulmonary disease (COPD), hypertrophy of prostate.

Answer

Main condition: GA90 Hyperplasia of prostate

Other condition(s):

CA22.Z Chronic obstructive pulmonary disease, unspecified
In this example, one condition was determined to be the reason for admission, established at the end of the episode of care. That is, benign prostatic hypertrophy.

COPD is coded as an 'other condition', as the physician documented that it had affected the management of the patient

Example 2

  • A patient is admitted to the hospital with pneumonia and congestive heart failure.
  • Main condition as per the patient discharge summary: Pneumonia and congestive heart failure.

Answer

Main condition: CA40.Z Pneumonia, organism unspecified
Other condition(s): BD10.Z Congestive heart failure, unspecified
In this example, two conditions have been recorded as the main condition and querying the health care practitioner is not possible. There are no other details on the record that point to one of the conditions as being the main condition.

In this instance, the coder should assign a code for the first listed condition, that is, pneumonia. Congestive heart failure is coded as the 'other condition'.

Morbidity Coding Rule MB2: Condition recorded as 'main condition' is presenting symptom of diagnosed, treated condition

If a symptom or sign or a problem is recorded as the 'main condition', and this is obviously the presenting sign, symptom or problem of a diagnosed condition recorded elsewhere and care was given for the latter, reselect the diagnosed condition as the 'main condition'.

Morbidity Coding Rule MB2: Example 3

  • The patient presents to hospital with abdominal pain. Investigations reveal acute appendicitis with localised peritonitis and the patient undergoes an appendicectomy.
  • Main condition as per the patient discharge summary: Abdominal pain
  • Other condition(s) as per the patient discharge summary: Acute appendicitis with localised peritonitis

Answer

Main condition:

DB10.01 Acute appendicitis with localised peritonitis

In this example, the symptom 'abdominal pain' was recorded as the main condition, however it was determined to be caused by the appendicitis.

Therefore, the coder should reselect and code acute appendicitis with localised peritonitis as the 'main condition'.

Morbidity Coding Rule MB3

  • Signs and symptoms recorded as 'main condition' with alternative conditions recorded as the cause
  • Where a symptom or sign is recorded as the 'main condition' with documentation that it may be due to either one condition or another, select the symptom or sign as the 'main condition'.

Morbidity Coding Rule MB3: Example 4

A patient is admitted to the hospital with a headache.
* Main condition as per the patient discharge summary:

Headache due to tension or acute sinusitis.

Answer

Main condition: MB6Y Other specified symptoms or signs involving the nervous system
In this example, the symptom 'headache' is recorded as the main condition, with two possible causes. Therefore, the coder should code headache as the 'main condition'.

Coder use of extension codes

Extension codes should never be used alone and must always be linked to a stem code. One or more extension codes can be linked when coding a specific condition. Extension codes are provided for use as supplementary or additional codes when it is desired to identify more detail in entities classified elsewhere.

There are two main types of Extension codes:

Type 1 extension codes allow the user to add detail to a stem code. The category refers to the same diagnosis with or without the Type I extension code. These extension codes provide important additional information, such as whether a condition is acute or chronic - and where it is located.

Type 2 extension codes represent diagnosis code descriptors. The meaning of the code refers to the same condition, but the use of type 2 - diagnosis code descriptor extension code alters its interpretation.
