Section 1: Definitions

5 Marks
1.
Explain the meaning of the following terms:

a) Subpoena Duces Tecum

Show Answer

Answer:

A Subpoena Duces Tecum is a court summons ordering the recipient (usually the custodian of records) to appear before the court and produce documents or other tangible evidence (such as medical records) for use at a hearing or trial.

b) Court order

Show Answer

Answer:

A Court Order is an official proclamation by a judge (or panel of judges) that defines the legal relationships between the parties to a hearing, a trial, an appeal or other court proceedings. In health records, it typically mandates the release of confidential patient information that overrides standard privacy policies.

c) Master patient index (MPI)

Show Answer

Answer:

The Master Patient Index (MPI) is the permanent database (card or electronic) that lists every patient who has ever been admitted or treated at the facility. It serves as the key to locating the medical record, linking the patient's name to their unique medical record number.

d) Electronic medical records (EMR)

Show Answer

Answer:

An Electronic Medical Record (EMR) is the digital version of the traditional paper-based medical chart for an individual. It contains the medical and treatment history of the patients in one practice and is used by providers for diagnosis and treatment.

e) Medical worker assessment form

Show Answer

Answer:

A Medical Worker Assessment Form typically refers to the standardized documentation used to assess an injury or disability sustained by an employee during work, often for Workmen's Compensation purposes (e.g., determining the percentage of incapacity).

Note: In some contexts, this may also refer to staff performance appraisal forms, but in a medical records/legal context, it usually refers to injury assessment/compensation forms.

Section 2: Short Answer Questions

5 Marks
2.
Outline the minimum functional requirements to be considered in the introduction of electronic medical records (5 marks)
Show Answer

Answer:

The minimum functional requirements for an EMR system include:

  1. Patient Management/Demographics: Ability to capture and store unique patient identifiers (Name, Age, Sex, ID).
  2. Clinical Documentation: Capabilities for entering progress notes, history, and vitals.
  3. Order Entry (CPOE): Functionality to order lab tests, radiology, and prescriptions electronically.
  4. Results Management: Ability to receive, display, and manage lab and imaging results.
  5. Security and Access Control: Robust user authentication (passwords), role-based access, and audit trails to ensure confidentiality.
  6. Reporting Capability: Ability to generate summary reports (e.g., daily attendance, morbidity stats).

Section 3: Long Answer Questions

20 Marks
1.
Discuss the devise strategies in the preparation for an Electronic Health Record (10 Marks)
Show Answer

Answer:

Preparation is crucial for the successful adoption of an EHR system. Key strategies include:

1. Readiness Assessment:

  • Conduct an organizational audit to determine if the staff (computer literacy) and infrastructure (power, network) are ready for digitization.

2. Stakeholder Engagement & Leadership:

  • Form an implementation committee including clinicians, nurses, IT staff, and administrators to ensure buy-in from all users. Leadership support is vital for funding and driving change.

3. Workflow Analysis and Redesign:

  • Map out current paper-based workflows and design new electronic workflows to ensure the system improves efficiency rather than creating bottlenecks.

4. Infrastructure Upgrade:

  • Procure necessary hardware (computers, servers) and ensure reliable power backup (UPS/Generators) and network connectivity (LAN/Wi-Fi).

5. Training and Capacity Building:

  • Develop a comprehensive training schedule for all staff levels. Training should be role-specific (e.g., doctors trained on ordering, clerks on registration).

6. Data Migration Strategy:

  • Decide how to handle existing paper records (scan them, enter summary data, or run parallel systems) during the transition.
2.
Electronic medical records plays an important role in ensuring data quality, list and explain factors that hinders implementation of electronic medical records (10 marks)
Show Answer

Answer:

1. High Initial Cost:

The cost of purchasing hardware, software licenses, and infrastructure setup is often prohibitive for many facilities, especially in resource-constrained settings.

2. Resistance to Change:

Staff accustomed to paper records may resist new technologies due to fear of the unknown, perceived increase in workload, or lack of computer skills.

3. Lack of Technical Expertise:

Shortage of qualified IT personnel to maintain the system, troubleshoot issues, and ensure data security can lead to system failure.

4. Inadequate Infrastructure:

Unreliable power supply and poor internet connectivity disrupt system availability, making it unreliable for critical patient care.

5. Interoperability Issues:

Different systems often cannot "talk" to each other (e.g., Lab system not linking with Pharmacy system), creating data silos instead of a unified record.

6. Privacy and Security Concerns:

Fears regarding data breaches, hacking, and unauthorized access to sensitive patient information can slow down adoption.
