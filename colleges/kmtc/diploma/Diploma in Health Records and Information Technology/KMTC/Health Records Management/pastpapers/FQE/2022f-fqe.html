Section 1: Multiple Choice Questions

40 Marks
1.
The largest elevator file for filing patient master index cards would probably hold... index cards.
a) 900,000
b) 9,000
c) 90,000
d) 190,000
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) 90,000

Standard large mechanical elevator files (like Remington Rand Kard-Veyer) used for 5x3 cards typically held capacities in the range of 50,000 to 100,000 cards depending on the model, with 90,000 being a common upper limit for standard units.

2.
Which one of the following is not a type of appointment system?
a) Centralized appointment
b) Decentralized appointment
c) Combined appointment
d) Returned appointment
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Returned appointment

Centralized, Decentralized, and Combined are the three structural types of appointment systems. "Returned appointment" is a type of patient visit, not a system structure.

3.
Which one of the following is an equipment for recording appointment?
a) Visible edge sheet
b) Shelf filing
c) Circular carousel
d) Mobile racking
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Visible edge sheet

Visible edge sheets (or binders) are commonly used for manual appointment scheduling. Shelf filing, carousels, and mobile racking are for file storage.

4.
Which of the following information is not included on the DBR?
a) Patient's parole
b) Patient's admission
c) Patient's appointment
d) Patients who have died
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Patient's appointment

The Daily Bed Return (DBR) tracks inpatient ward activity (Admissions, Discharges, Deaths, Parole). Appointments are outpatient activities and not part of the bed census.

5.
The following are ways of conveying information to the waiting list. Which one is not?
a) Cards
b) Telephone calls
c) List
d) Letters
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) List

Information usually arrives via individual TCI (To Come In) cards, letters from GPs, or telephone calls. A "List" is generally the output or the register itself, not the medium of conveying a request to add a specific patient.

6.
Which of the following departments need to be notified of patients discharged from the ward?
a) Reception
b) The chaplain
c) The ward
d) Accident and emergency department
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Reception

The Reception (or Admission/Discharge office) needs to know about discharges to update the bed state (PMI). The ward *originates* the discharge. A&E doesn't need to know unless the patient is going there.

7.
The following are problems faced by hospitals in the proper maintenance of records except
a) Inactive records need storage at appointed place
b) Always trained personnel are needed for maintenance
c) Constant revision of outdated form is maintained
d) No need of determining retention period
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) No need of determining retention period

This is the exception because NOT determining a retention period is a major problem (leading to overcrowding). The statement implies that "no need to determine" is a problem faced, or rather, the problem is the *lack* of retention schedules. However, contextually, A, B, and C are ongoing challenges/tasks. Option D is an incorrect statement about records management principles (there IS a need).

8.
The disclosure of health information for a person with covid-19 by the Ministry of Health is not regarded as a breach of confidentiality because
a) It is an express consent
b) It is a court order
c) It is a higher duty
d) It is a subpoena
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) It is a higher duty

This falls under "Public Interest" or Higher Duty to protect the public from serious harm (infectious disease control/Public Health Act), which supersedes the individual's right to privacy.

9.
Which of the following is not a source of admission in the hospital?
a) Inpatient waiting list
b) Hospital bed bureau
c) Accident and emergency department
d) Other hospital
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Hospital bed bureau

The Bed Bureau is the department that manages the admission, not the source of the patient. Patients come from A&E, Waiting List, or transfers (Other Hospitals).

10.
The International Federation of Health Records Organization (IFHRO) now IFHIMA was formed in the year in Sweden.
a) 1958
b) 2010
c) 1752
d) 1968
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) 1968

IFHRO (now IFHIMA) was established in 1968.

11.
The famous terminal digit filing sequence generally works with ____ Digit number
a) Eight
b) Four
c) Six
d) Three
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Six

Standard Terminal Digit Filing works best with a six-digit number (broken into 3 pairs: Primary, Secondary, Tertiary).

12.
One linear shelf of medical records filing can accommodate about ____ pounds of medical files.
a) 40
b) 50
c) 75
d) 100
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) 100

While variable based on paper density, standard planning guides often estimate that a full 36-48 inch shelf of tightly packed records weighs approximately 100 pounds (approx 45-50kg).

13.
Which of the following is not a basic principle of form design?
a) Title
b) Standard format
c) Economical
d) Standard size
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Title

While every form needs a title, "Title" itself is a component, not a principle. The principles are Standardization, Simplicity, Economy, and Effectiveness.

14.
Procedures for disposal of dead bodies is spelled out in which Act?
a) Public records
b) Public health act
c) Government secret act
d) Civic registration act
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Public health act

The Public Health Act (Cap 242 in Kenya) contains regulations regarding the safe disposal of bodies, burial permits, and handling of infectious corpses.

15.
The following are types of special health records except
a) Tuberculosis records
b) Psychiatric records
c) Gender based evidence records
d) Malaria records
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Malaria records

TB, Psychiatric, and GBV records often have specific legal/confidentiality protocols distinguishing them as "special". Malaria records are typically part of general outpatient/inpatient morbidity data.

16.
The procedure for completion of P3 forms, post-mortem forms are attained in which act?
a) Public records act
b) Factory and workshop act
c) Civil registration act
d) Criminal procedure code
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Criminal procedure code

P3 Forms (Medical Examination Report for Assault) and police post-mortems are legal evidence documents governed by the Criminal Procedure Code and Evidence Act.

17.
Which one of the following is not a disposal method of government assets?
a) Giving out as a gift
b) Transfer by public tender
c) Sale by public auction
d) Destruction
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Giving out as a gift

Government assets are public property and cannot simply be "gifted". Accepted methods include Auction, Tender, Transfer to other government entities, or Destruction (if valueless).

18.
In the stores management cycle, implementation procedures involve the following except?
a) Issuing
b) Authorization
c) Receiving
d) Reporting by asset holders
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Reporting by asset holders

Authorization, Receiving, and Issuing are active implementation steps in the flow of goods. Reporting is typically part of the Monitoring/Control or Audit phase.

19.
Which one of the following is a similarity of the library trace system and personal trace system?
a) Both are created at the same time with the file
b) Both have the same size
c) Both are filed in a small tracer card index
d) Both have small pocket inside where to file
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Both have the same size

Tracer cards (whether personal or library/common) are typically designed to be the same size as the file folder or slightly larger/distinctive to stand out on the shelf (Guide card size). They take the place of the file.

20.
Which one of the following correctly match the principles of terminal digit filing?
a) The library and the hospital number
b) The major sections and sub-sections
c) Primary digit and middle digit
d) Primary digit and terminal digit
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Primary digit and terminal digit

Terminal Digit Filing is based on matching the Primary digits (the terminal end) first, then the Secondary digits.

21.
The following are types of health records except
a) Outpatient records
b) Personnel records
c) Case records
d) Diagnostic records
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Personnel records

Personnel records relate to staff/HR (Human Resources), not patient health care. Outpatient, Case, and Diagnostic records are clinical health records.

22.
The filing of patient master index should strictly be filed
a) Numerically
b) Alphabetically
c) Chronologically
d) Terminally
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Alphabetically

The PMI is the key to finding the medical record number when only the name is known. Therefore, it must be filed Alphabetically (often phonetically).

23.
The following are information to be recorded on the disease index card except
a) Age
b) Outcome
c) Chronologically
d) Terminally
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Terminally

A Disease Index card records patient demographics (Age, Sex), the Diagnosis code, Result/Outcome, and date (Chronological). "Terminally" is a filing method, not a data field on the index card.

24.
A type of appointment system where appointment of new cases is done in one office and the re-attendance appointment done in various consultant clinics is called
a) Centralized appointment
b) Decentralized appointment
c) Combined appointment
d) Centralized and decentralized appointment
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Combined appointment

This hybrid approach, utilizing both a central booking office for new patients and local clinic booking for follow-ups, is a Combined system.

25.
Which of the following hospital departments should not be notified of patients admitted to the hospital?
a) Telephone switch board
b) Reception
c) The bed bureau
d) Accident and emergency department
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) The bed bureau

The Bed Bureau is the one coordinating the admission. It already knows (it generates the notification). The Switchboard and Reception need to be told so they can direct visitors.

26.
The fundamental requirement of filing system that ensures speedy location and positive means of identification of documents in the system is called?
a) Elasticity
b) Economic
c) Accessibility
d) Simplicity
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Accessibility

Accessibility refers to how quickly and easily records can be located and retrieved.

27.
The first records procedure at the accident and emergency records department is?
a) Filing
b) Maintaining of the registers
c) Disposal of patients
d) Appointment
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Maintaining of the registers

When a patient arrives at A&E, the first step is entering their details into the Casualty Register (Day Book) before creating/retrieving the file or treating the patient.

28.
Which of the following is not the main filing equipment used to file case records and x-rays film?
a) Shelf filing
b) Filing cabinet
c) Suspended filing
d) Filing trolley
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Filing trolley

A Filing trolley is a transport/temporary sorting device, not "main filing equipment" for permanent storage like shelves or cabinets.

29.
The documentation of the patient's social data using the general practitioner's referral letter is called
a) Recording
b) Registration
c) Coding
d) Pre-registration
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Pre-registration

Pre-registration uses referral letters to capture data before the patient physically arrives at the hospital desk.

30.
The section of a health records and information department which is responsible for arranging of emergency admissions is called
a) Bed board
b) Bed bureau
c) Bed stand
d) Accident and emergency department
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Bed bureau

The Bed Bureau (Admissions Office) allocates beds for emergency cases.

31.
The following are policies and emergency department for use in an electronic health records system except
a) Information policy
b) Decision policy
c) Work flow policy
d) Downtime policy
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Decision policy

Common EHR policies include Information Security, Workflow, and Downtime (business continuity). "Decision policy" is not a standard term for EHR system administration policies.

32.
...... and ....... are the filing methods of a waiting list
a) Alphabetical index and consultant list
b) Sort-file and pigeon holes
c) Numerical and chronological filing
d) Letters and list
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Alphabetical index and consultant list

Waiting lists are typically maintained in two forms to allow cross-referencing: An Alphabetical Index (to find if a patient is on the list) and a Consultant/Specialty List (to select patients for admission).

33.
The first process when planning to dispose health records in a health facility is
a) Determine the retention period
b) Identify active and inactive files
c) Purging of files
d) Identify destruction methods
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Determine the retention period

Before you can identify which files to dispose of (active vs inactive), you must first have a policy that sets the Retention Period (the rule for how long files are kept).

34.
Which one of the following is not a factor to consider when establishing a health records and information department?
a) Location
b) Space for equipment
c) System communication
d) Minimum qualification of staff
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Minimum qualification of staff

While staffing is a factor, the physical establishment focuses on Location, Space, Layout, and Communication systems. Staff qualification is an HR/Recruitment issue, not a facility establishment factor.

35.
The following are the contents of a discharge register except
a) Name of the patient
b) Age
c) Diagnosis
d) Date of birth
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Date of birth

Registers typically record Age rather than Date of Birth to save space and provide immediate demographic context. Name, Age, Diagnosis, and Date of Discharge are standard.

36.
Which one of the following is a concept of scheduling?
a) Overloading of the clinic should be avoided
b) To reduce patient's waiting time
c) Provide special clinic arrangement
d) Allow hospital to prepare in advance
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Overloading of the clinic should be avoided

Options B, C, and D are purposes or benefits of scheduling. Avoiding overloading is a core concept or operational principle of managing the schedule.

37.
The following are the contents of a unit health records except
a) Treatment form
b) Anaesthetic form
c) Surgical operation sheet
d) Appointment card
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Appointment card

The Appointment Card is given to the patient to take home. It does not stay in the Unit Health Record (file) like the clinical forms (Treatment, Anaesthetic, Operation sheets).

38.
Which of the following is not an ancillary equipment tin a medical record library?
a) Filing trolley
b) Ladders
c) Mobile racks
d) Kik stool
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Mobile racks

Mobile racks are primary storage equipment. Trolleys, ladders, and kick stools are ancillary (supporting) equipment.

39.
The following are duties and responsibilities of a health records and information officer except
a) Maintain health records indices
b) Disseminate health information and provide feedback
c) Maintain legal aspects and security of health records
d) Notification of infectious diseases to the public
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Notification of infectious diseases to the public

HRIOs notify the Ministry of Health authorities (statutory notification). Notifying the public is typically the role of the Public Health Officer or Medical Officer of Health (MOH).

40.
Hippocrates was .... by nationality
a) Egyptian
b) Roman
c) Greek
d) Cypriot

Hippocrates, the "Father of Medicine," was an ancient Greek physician.

Section 2: Short Answer Questions

40 Marks
1.
Outline at least five (5) major contents of a daily ward statements (5 marks)
Show Answer

Answer:

  1. Total number of patients remaining from the previous day (Bed State at midnight).
  2. Total Admissions during the day.
  3. Total Discharges during the day.
  4. Number of Deaths.
  5. Transfers In and Transfers Out.
  6. Number of patients on Parole/Leave.
2.
State five (5) advantages of a centralized waiting list (5 marks)
Show Answer

Answer:

  1. Maximized Occupancy: Any available bed can be filled immediately from the central pool.
  2. Fairness: Admissions are based on clinical priority and waiting time, reducing favoritism.
  3. Standardization: Uniform procedures for adding and removing patients.
  4. Avoids Duplication: Prevents the same patient from being listed on multiple consultant lists for the same condition.
  5. Better Statistics: Provides accurate data on total waiting times and demand for the hospital management.
3.
Outline five critical strategies for implementation of electronic health records (5 marks)
Show Answer

Answer:

  1. Stakeholder Engagement: Involving doctors, nurses, and admin staff early in the process.
  2. Training: Comprehensive training programs for all users before launch.
  3. Phased Implementation: Rolling out module by module or department by department rather than a "big bang".
  4. Workflow Analysis: Redesigning clinical workflows to fit digital processes.
  5. Infrastructure Readiness: Ensuring adequate hardware, network connectivity, and power backup.
4.
State any five (5) legal aspects in medical records (5 marks)
Show Answer

Answer:

  1. Confidentiality: The duty to keep patient information private.
  2. Consent: Requirement for patient permission before treatment or disclosure.
  3. Ownership: The physical record belongs to the hospital; the information belongs to the patient.
  4. Disclosure: Rules governing when and to whom information can be released.
  5. Retention and Destruction: Legal requirements for how long records must be kept.
5.
Outline five factors leading to pre-registration of patients (5 marks)
Show Answer

Answer:

  1. Efficiency: Saves time at the admission desk on the day of arrival.
  2. Accuracy: Allows time to verify demographic and insurance details.
  3. Planning: Helps the hospital anticipate bed and resource needs.
  4. Reduced Waiting Time: Patients can go directly to the ward or clinic upon arrival.
  5. File Preparation: Allows existing files to be retrieved or new ones created in advance.
6.
Explain how the following qualities assist heath records officers to perform daily activities in the hospital (5 marks)
Show Answer

Answer:

  • (i) Politeness: Ensures good customer service and reduces conflict with stressed patients.
  • (ii) Discretion: Essential for maintaining patient confidentiality and handling sensitive information appropriately.
  • (iii) Calmness: Enables the officer to work effectively under pressure, especially in emergencies.
  • (iv) Neat appearance: Instills confidence in patients regarding the professionalism and hygiene of the hospital.
  • (v) Integrity: Ensures honesty in data recording and security of records (incorruptibility).
7.
State five (5) reasons of maintaining a waiting list in a hospital (5 marks)
Show Answer

Answer:

  1. To ensure fair allocation of beds based on clinical need and date of referral.
  2. To maximize bed occupancy by having a pool of patients ready for admission.
  3. To allow patients time to prepare for admission (arrange leave, childcare).
  4. To enable the hospital to plan operating theatre lists and resources.
  5. To provide statistics on demand for services (backlog) for planning purposes.
8.
Outline procedures used for admitting patients from the waiting list (5 marks)
Show Answer

Answer:

  1. Selection: The consultant selects patients from the list based on priority and bed availability.
  2. Notification: The patient is contacted (letter/phone) with a TCI (To Come In) date.
  3. Verification: On arrival, patient details are verified against the waiting list.
  4. Removal: The patient's name is removed/marked as "Admitted" on the waiting list.
  5. File Retrieval: The patient's previous medical record is retrieved and sent to the ward.

Section 3: Long Answer Question

20 Marks
1.

Discuss the value of health records documentation to each of the following persons:

  • i. For patients
  • ii. For hospital
  • iii. For health care worker
  • iv. For researcher
  • v. For teaching
Show Answer

Answer:

i. For Patients:

  • Continuity of Care: Ensures that their medical history is available for future treatment, preventing repetition of tests.
  • Evidence: Serves as proof of illness for insurance claims, sick leave, or legal cases (malpractice/injury).
  • Safety: Protects them from contraindicated medications (allergies recorded) or wrong procedures.

ii. For Hospital (Administration):

  • Planning: Provides statistics (morbidity/mortality) for budgeting and resource allocation.
  • Legal Defense: Serves as the primary evidence to defend the hospital against malpractice suits.
  • Revenue: Essential for accurate billing and insurance reimbursement auditing.
  • Evaluation: Used to assess the quality of care provided by the facility.

iii. For Health Care Worker (Doctors/Nurses):

  • Communication: Allows different team members to communicate patient status and treatment plans effectively.
  • Decision Making: Provides the history and test results needed to make accurate diagnoses.
  • Protection: Proves that they followed standard procedures and provided appropriate care.

iv. For Researcher:

  • Data Source: Primary source of clinical data for retrospective studies on disease patterns and treatment outcomes.
  • Advancement: Helps in discovering new treatments or epidemiological trends.

v. For Teaching (Education):

  • Case Studies: Real patient records are used to train medical students, nurses, and HRIOs on disease management and documentation.
  • Reference: Provides practical examples of clinical course and management.
