6.
The principles of medical records professional acknowledges the undermentioned except
A) Respects the dignity of each client/patient
B) Refuses to participate and to conceal the legal or ethical acts
C) Strives to improve personal competence and quality of service
D) Demonstrate the behavior that reflects integrity
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B) Refuses to participate and to conceal the legal or ethical acts

The correct ethical principle is to "Refuse to participate in or conceal unethical or illegal acts." Option B says "Refuses to conceal legal acts," which implies a distortion of the standard principle (you generally shouldn't conceal illegal acts, but you do keep legal patient confidences - however, the phrasing "Refuses to participate in... legal acts" is the error/exception).

8.
The medical records can serve the below mentioned apart from one
A) To provide a means of communication amongst the health care providers
B) To serve as an inference in continuity of patient care
C) To provide documentary evidence of care given in the facility
D) To provide clinical and administrative data for use in management services medical education and medical research
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B) To serve as an inference in continuity of patient care

Records serve as a direct tool/reference for continuity, not merely an "inference". While all options describe uses, option B uses the term "inference" (a conclusion reached on basis of evidence) which is slightly less direct than "communication tool" or "evidence". In many past papers, this phrasing is used to identify the "odd one out" due to terminology, although records do support continuity.

15.

17.

18.
Cards may be filed "blind" in drawers or filed one behind the other in an upright position in which of the following equipment
A) Drawer filing
B) Strip index
C) Elevator files
D) Carousel type files
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A) Drawer filing

Drawer Filing (Vertical Filing): Cards are filed upright in drawers. It is called "blind" because you cannot see the details of the card until you pull the drawer open and separate the cards (unlike a visible strip index).

When considering a retention policy, the hospital must consider the following except

The eight core capabilities that electronic medical records should possess are? (4 Marks)
Show Answer

Answer:

The Institute of Medicine (IOM) defines 8 core capabilities:

  1. Health Information and Data (Patient demographics, medical history).
  2. Result Management (Lab/Radiology reports).
  3. Order Entry/Management (CPOE - ePrescribing).
  4. Decision Support (Alerts, reminders).
  5. Electronic Communication and Connectivity (Provider-provider, provider-patient).
  6. Patient Support (Patient education materials, portals).
  7. Administrative Processes (Scheduling, billing).
  8. Reporting and Population Health Management (Public health reporting, QC).

45.
State:
a) 4 concepts of scheduling (4 Marks)
b) 4 purposes of appointment/scheduling (4 Marks)
Show Answer

Answer:

a) Concepts of Scheduling:

  1. Overloading of clinics should be avoided.
  2. The doctor should be punctual.
  3. There should be a continuous flow of patients (no gaps).
  4. Patients should be seen in order of appointment time.

b) Purposes of Appointment:

  1. To reduce patient waiting time.
  2. To allow staff/hospital to prepare in advance (pull files).
  3. To regulate the workload of the clinic.
  4. To ensure even distribution of patients across available consultants.
46.
State:
a) 5 advantages of the secretarial pool system (5 Marks)
b) State disadvantages of the pool system (2 Marks)
Show Answer

Answer:

a) Advantages:

  1. Continuity: Work continues even if one staff member is absent.
  2. Standardization: Easier to train staff and maintain uniform standards.
  3. Economy: More efficient use of equipment and space.
  4. Even Workload: Work can be distributed evenly among typists.
  5. Flexibility: Easier to handle peak workloads.

b) Disadvantages:

  1. Lack of Specialization: Typists may not know specific medical terminology for a specialty as well as a dedicated secretary.
  2. Impersonal: Less personal relationship between the doctor and the secretary.

48.
Microforms are mainly used to store records that are in the cycle. Microforms offer a number of advantages. State and explain the 4 advantages. (4 Marks)
Show Answer

Answer:

Microforms (Microfilm/Microfiche) are used for inactive/archived records.

Advantages:

  1. Space Saving: Reduces storage space requirements by up to 98% compared to paper.
  2. Security/Preservation: Durable format (lasts 100+ years) and prevents alteration of the original document.
  3. Security: Easy to duplicate and store a backup copy off-site for disaster recovery.
  4. Easy Distribution: Cheap to mail/distribute compared to heavy paper files.

Section 3: Long Answer Question

20 Marks
49.
As a recently qualified health records and information officer from the best medical training college, explain to the interview panel the procedures of establishing a new Health Records Department from the existing one. (20 Marks)
Show Answer

Answer:

Establishing a new department involves a systematic planning process, typically divided into four phases:

1. The Definition Phase (Feasibility Study):

  • Assess Needs: Analyze the volume of patients (inpatient/outpatient), expected growth, and types of services offered.
  • Determine Scope: What functions will the department handle? (Coding, storage, stats, admissions, etc.)
  • Budgeting: Estimate costs for construction, equipment, and staffing.

2. The Brief Phase (Preparation of Brief):

  • Detailed Specifications: Write a detailed "Architect's Brief" specifying room requirements.
  • Location: Ensure it is centrally located, accessible to OPD, A&E, and Admissions.
  • Space Calculation: Calculate floor space needed for filing (based on 5-10 year growth), staff desks, and waiting areas.
  • Flow: Define the workflow (e.g., file movement from storage to reception).

3. The Design Phase:

  • Layout: Work with architects to design the floor plan. Ensure wide gangways (3ft+), proper lighting, and ventilation.
  • Safety: Include fire exits, sprinklers/extinguishers (vital for paper records), and secure access points.
  • Furniture/Equipment: Select shelving (Mobile vs. Static), computers, and desks.

4. The Construction & Commissioning Phase:

  • Supervision: Monitor construction to ensure it meets the brief.
  • Fitting Out: Install shelving and IT systems.
  • Staffing: Recruit and train staff on new workflows.
  • Migration: Systematically move existing records to the new location (if applicable) without disrupting care.
  • Go-Live: Open the department for operations.
