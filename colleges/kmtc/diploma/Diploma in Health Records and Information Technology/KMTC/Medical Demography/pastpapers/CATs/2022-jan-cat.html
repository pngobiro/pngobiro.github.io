Part 1: Multiple Choice Questions

10 Marks
1

In the modern stage of transition theory, the birth rates are very low because of the following except?

A) Improved economy
B) Improved female education
C) Ideal family planning practice
D) None of the above
Show Answer

Correct Answer: D) None of the above

Reasoning: Options A, B, and C are all valid reasons for declining birth rates in the modern stage of demographic transition. Since the question asks for the exception, and all listed options are true causes, "None of the above" is the logical choice (implying there is no exception listed among A, B, or C).

2

Which of the following is a source document of demographic data?

A) Population sample survey
B) Various publications
C) Population census report of 1999
D) Registration system data
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C) Population census report of 1999

Reasoning: While surveys and registration systems are methods or sources of data collection, a "Census Report" is a specific published document containing demographic data.

3

The overall sex ratio of a population depends on the following except?

A) Sex differences in mortality and fertility
B) Sex differences in mortality and migration
C) Sex ratio at birth
D) Sex differences in migration
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A) Sex differences in mortality and fertility

Reasoning: The overall sex ratio is determined by three main factors: 1) Sex ratio at birth, 2) Sex differentials in mortality, and 3) Sex differentials in migration. Fertility (the number of children born) affects the population size and age structure, but does not directly alter the sex ratio unless specific sex-selection is involved (which falls under sex ratio at birth).
