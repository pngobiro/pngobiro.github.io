Section 1: Multiple Choice Questions

40 Marks
1.
____ is a scientific study of human population with respect to their size, structure and change
a) Population growth
b) Formal demography
c) Scientific demography
d) Population studies
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Formal demography

Explanation: Formal demography (or pure demography) deals with the quantitative study of human populations, specifically their size, structure, and change (dynamics) through fertility, mortality, and migration.

2.
____ refers to the distribution of the population among its sex and age groupings.
a) Size
b) Structure
c) Change
d) Distribution
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Structure

Explanation: Population structure (or composition) refers to the distribution of the population by age and sex.

3.
The following are uses of demographic information except
a) Economic Planning
b) Policy Making
c) Administrative purposes
d) Disease prevention
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Disease prevention

Explanation: While demographic data informs health planning, "disease prevention" itself is a medical/public health action, not a direct "use" of the information in the same administrative/planning sense as the others. However, this is a subtle distinction.

4.
Census is the procedure of systematically acquiring and recording information about members of a given population. When conducting census, the following errors may occur except
a) Errors of contents
b) Estimating errors
c) Errors of coverage
d) Sampling error
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Sampling error

Explanation: A census, by definition, covers the *entire* population. Therefore, "sampling error" (which arises from observing only a sample) does not theoretically exist in a full census. Coverage and content errors are the main types of census errors.

5.
The difference between birth and death is called ____ of a population
a) Natural increase
b) Natural decrease
c) Growth rate
d) Population change
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Natural increase

Explanation: Natural increase is the difference between the number of live births and the number of deaths during the year. (If deaths exceed births, it is natural decrease).

6.
Which one of the following is not an example of life table?
a) Period or static life tables
b) Cohort life tables
c) Clinical life table
d) Cross-sectional life table
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Cross-sectional life table

Explanation: The standard types are Period (Current) Life Tables and Cohort (Generation) Life Tables. "Cross-sectional" is another name for Period, but "Clinical life table" is also a valid term in survival analysis. However, "Cross-sectional life table" is often used synonymously with Period. If we must choose "not an example", the phrasing "Cross-sectional" is less standard as a distinct *type* separate from Period, but "Clinical" is specific to medicine. In standard demography, Period and Cohort are the main two.

7.
In life table qx denotes
a) Probability that person of age x will not survive till x+1 (mortality ratio)
b) Probability that a person will survive survival rate
c) Average number person living between age x and x+1
d) Total person living above age x years
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Probability that person of age x will not survive till x+1 (mortality ratio)

Explanation: $q_x$ represents the probability of dying between age $x$ and age $x+1$.

8.
Population change is calculated as
a) Natural increase + Net migration
b) Birth + deaths
c) Birth - deaths
d) Migration in- migration out
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Natural increase + Net migration

Explanation: Total population change is the sum of Natural Increase (Births - Deaths) and Net Migration (Immigration - Emigration).

9.
Moses a diploma student in health records and information technology was detecting age errors using whipples index and its answer was between 105-110 what would be its interpretation
a) Fairly accurate
b) Approximately Accurate
c) Rough accurate
d) Highly accurate
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Fairly accurate

Explanation: Whipple's Index scale: < 105 (Highly Accurate), 105-109.9 (Fairly Accurate), 110-124.9 (Approximate), 125-174.9 (Rough),>= 175 (Very Rough).

10.
In district X the population with age ending 0 and 5 ending between age 23 and 62 years was 2513 . The total population was 5265 . Calculate the Whipples index
a) 230
b) 210
c) 120
d) 105
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) 238.6 (Closest option is not listed, but let's re-calculate)

Calculation:

$$ W = \frac{\sum (P_{25}, P_{30}, ... P_{60})}{\frac{1}{5} \sum P_{23-62}} \times 100 $$

Actually, the simplified formula is: (Sum of ages ending in 0 and 5 / Total Population in range) * 500.

$$ W = \frac{2513}{5265} \times 500 = 0.4773 \times 500 = 238.65 $$

None of the options exactly match 238. However, if the formula used was simply (2513/5265)*100 = 47.7%, that's not it. Let's check if the range is standard. Standard range is 23-62. The denominator should be 1/5 of total. So 5 * (2513/5265) * 100 = 238.6. Maybe there's a typo in the question numbers or options. 230 is the closest.

11.
When constructing population pyramids male is usually in
a) Right
b) Left
c) Horizontal
d) Vertical

Correct Answer: b) Left

Explanation: By convention, males are shown on the left side of the population pyramid and females on the right.

12.
Which of the following is not a determinant of age structure?
a) Migration
b) Fertility
c) Mortality
d) Morbidity
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Morbidity

Explanation: The three main determinants of population age structure are Fertility, Mortality, and Migration. Morbidity (illness) affects mortality but is not a direct demographic determinant of structure in itself.

13.
Mortality levels, trends and prospects are presented through 3 indicators namely:
a) Maternal mortality
b) Life expectancy at birth
c) Infant Mortality Rate
d) Mortality of children under 5 years.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Life expectancy at birth

Explanation: While all are mortality indicators, Life Expectancy at Birth is the most comprehensive summary measure of mortality levels in a population.

14.
Standard incidence Rate would refer to
a) Morbidity data

c) Incidence rate

Correct Answer: c) Incidence rate

Explanation: Incidence rates refer to new cases of a disease, which is a measure of morbidity.

15.
____ to the number expected in the study population.
a) Standardized mortality ratio
b) Standardized Incidence Ratio
c) Standard/Reference population
d) Index population
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Standardized mortality ratio

Explanation: The Standardized Mortality Ratio (SMR) is the ratio of observed deaths in the study group to expected deaths in the general population.

16.
____ is calculated as Males/Females (M/F) *100
a) Sex ratio
b) Dependency ratio
c) Age structure
d) Population projection
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Sex ratio

Explanation: The sex ratio is defined as the number of males per 100 females.

17.
A population pyramid showing an unchanging pattern of fertility and mortality
a) Stable pyramid
b) Stationary pyramid
c) Constrictive pyramid
d) Expansive pyramid
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Stationary pyramid

Explanation: A stationary pyramid (often rectangular) represents a population with low birth and death rates that remain constant, leading to little or no growth.

18.
Which two measures determine the extent of demographic transition
a) CBR and CDR
b) ASFR and GFR
c) BR and TFR
d) GFR and TFR
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) CBR and CDR

Explanation: The demographic transition model is based on the changes and relationship between the Crude Birth Rate (CBR) and Crude Death Rate (CDR) over time.

19.
____ support. The number or the largest number of individuals that a given environment can
a) Carrying capacity
b) Population
c) Community
d) Resources
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Carrying capacity

Explanation: Carrying capacity is the maximum population size of a biological species that can be sustained by that specific environment.

20.
The population pyramid of a country has a broad base and tapering apex. Which of the following characterize population growth in this country?
a) Low fertility and low childhood mortality
b) Low fertility and high childhood mortality
c) High fertility and high childhood mortality
d) High fertility and low childhood mortality
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) High fertility and high childhood mortality

Explanation: A broad base indicates high fertility (many children). A tapering apex indicates high mortality (fewer people surviving to old age). This is typical of an expansive pyramid.

21.
Characteristics like age, marital status, education and gender are examples of ____
a) demographic information
b) psychographic information
c) firmographic information
d) customer intelligence
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) demographic information

Explanation: Demographic information refers to statistical data about a population, such as age, gender, income, education, and marital status.

22.
The following circumstances in the country or origin may lead people to emigrate: Push factors except
a) Economic opportunity
b) Poverty
c) War
d) Environmental disasters
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Economic opportunity

Explanation: "Push factors" drive people *away* from a place (e.g., poverty, war). "Economic opportunity" is a "Pull factor" that attracts people *to* a new place.

23.
The following are elements common to population policies except

b) Objectives and Goals

Explanation: A policy typically consists of a Rationale, Objectives/Goals, and Program Measures (strategies). "Population growth" is the *subject* of the policy, not a structural element of the policy document itself.

25.
The overall sex ratio is entirely determined by the following expect
a) The sex ratio at birth

28.
In 2009, the population of country X was 35 million. What was be the population of Country X in 2014 If the growth rate was 1.5%
a) 37,704,940
b) 367045800
c) 234789000
d) 289500000
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) 37,704,940

Explanation: Using the formula $P_t = P_0(1+r)^t$: $P_{2014} = 35,000,000 \times (1 + 0.015)^5$. $1.015^5 \approx 1.07728$. $35,000,000 \times 1.07728 \approx 37,704,940$.

Section 2: Short Answer Questions

40 Marks
41.
List and explain features of census data. (5 marks)
Show Answer

Answer:

  1. Individual Enumeration: Each individual is counted separately, and their characteristics recorded separately.
  2. Universality within a defined territory: The census should cover a precisely defined territory (e.g., the entire country) and include every person present or residing within it.
  3. Simultaneity: The census is taken at a specific point in time (census moment) to avoid double counting or omissions.
  4. Periodicity: Censuses are taken at regular intervals (usually every 10 years) to allow for comparable data.
  5. Government Sponsorship: It is a major administrative task usually mandated by law and funded by the government.
42.
Briefly describe uses of basic demographic equation (5 marks)
Show Answer

Answer: The basic demographic equation is $P_2 = P_1 + (B - D) + (I - E)$. Its uses include:

  1. Estimating Population Size: Calculating the population at a future date ($P_2$) given the current population and vital events.
  2. Estimating Net Migration: If $P_1$, $P_2$, Births, and Deaths are known, migration (which is hard to measure directly) can be estimated as a residual.
  3. Checking Data Quality: Comparing census counts ($P_2$) with expected counts derived from vital registration data to identify errors.
  4. Decomposing Growth: Analyzing how much of the population growth is due to natural increase vs. migration.
  5. Planning: Projecting future population needs based on current trends in the components of change.
43.
Constant growth rate in a country may cause overpopulation. Explain the effect of overpopulation (10 marks)
Show Answer

Answer:

  1. Strain on Resources: Depletion of natural resources like water, land, and forests.
  2. Unemployment: The labor market cannot absorb the growing workforce, leading to high unemployment and underemployment.
  3. Pressure on Social Services: Overcrowding in schools, hospitals, and housing; inadequate infrastructure.
  4. Environmental Degradation: Increased pollution, deforestation, and loss of biodiversity due to human activity.
  5. Poverty: Lower per capita income as economic growth may not keep pace with population growth.
  6. Food Insecurity: Difficulty in producing enough food to feed the population, leading to malnutrition.
  7. Increased Crime/Social Unrest: Competition for scarce resources can lead to conflict and higher crime rates.
  8. Migration: People may be forced to migrate to other areas or countries in search of better opportunities (brain drain).
  9. Housing Shortage: Growth of slums and informal settlements with poor sanitation.
  10. Congestion: Traffic jams and overcrowding in urban areas.
44.
Death is the vital event for which statistics are collected by the vital statistics registration system. Outline the uses of mortality data (5 marks)
Show Answer

Answer:

  1. Health Planning: Identifying health problems and allocating resources for disease control programs.
  2. Analyzing Trends: Monitoring changes in death rates over time to evaluate the effectiveness of health interventions.
  3. Life Tables: Constructing life tables to calculate life expectancy and insurance premiums.
  4. Epidemiology: Investigating the causes of death and risk factors for specific diseases.
  5. Population Projections: Mortality assumptions are essential for projecting future population size and structure.
45.
In life table x denote age of person and lx Number of person living at age x . Given the data below calculate: (5 marks)
a) dx
b) qx
c) px
d) Lx
x lx dx qx px Lx
0 20 a)..... b)...... c)....... d) ........
1 19
2 15
3 5
Show Answer

Calculations for Age 0 (x=0):

  • Given: $l_0 = 20$, $l_1 = 19$.
  • a) $d_x$ (Deaths): $d_0 = l_0 - l_1 = 20 - 19 = 1$.
  • b) $q_x$ (Probability of dying): $q_0 = \frac{d_0}{l_0} = \frac{1}{20} = 0.05$.
  • c) $p_x$ (Probability of surviving): $p_0 = 1 - q_0 = 1 - 0.05 = 0.95$. (Or $p_0 = \frac{l_1}{l_0} = \frac{19}{20} = 0.95$).
  • d) $L_x$ (Person-years lived): Assuming uniform distribution of deaths (or using standard formula $L_x = \frac{l_x + l_{x+1}}{2}$): $L_0 = \frac{20 + 19}{2} = 19.5$.

Answer: a) 1, b) 0.05, c) 0.95, d) 19.5

46.
Explain the different between age heaping and age shifting. (5 marks)
Show Answer

Answer:

  • Age Heaping (Digit Preference): This occurs when respondents report their age ending in certain preferred digits (usually 0 and 5) rather than their exact age. It results in spikes in the population distribution at ages like 20, 25, 30, etc. It is usually due to ignorance of exact age.
  • Age Shifting: This occurs when respondents systematically overstate or understate their age. For example, older people might exaggerate their age to appear more venerable, or young men might understate their age to avoid military service. It shifts the population distribution from one age group to another.
47.
List and explain five main sources of demographic data. (5 marks)
Show Answer

Answer:

  1. Population Census: The total process of collecting, compiling, evaluating, analyzing and publishing demographic, economic and social data pertaining, at a specified time, to all persons in a country.
  2. Vital Registration System: The continuous, permanent, compulsory and legal recording of the occurrence and characteristics of vital events (births, deaths, marriages, divorces, fetal deaths).
  3. Sample Surveys: Collecting data from a selected subset of the population to infer characteristics of the whole population (e.g., Demographic and Health Surveys - DHS).
  4. Population Registers: A continuous recording system where each individual's major demographic events (birth, marriage, migration) are recorded in a file maintained by the government.
  5. Administrative Records: Data collected for administrative purposes that can be used for demographic analysis (e.g., school enrollment records, hospital records, immigration records, social security records).
