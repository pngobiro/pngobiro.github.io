Section 1: Multiple Choice Questions

40 Marks
1.
A Proportion in Demographic terms is
a) Frequency of Demographic events in a population
b) A Population subgroup divided by entire population
c) The absolute number of a population
d) The relationship between one population subgroup to the total population
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) A Population subgroup divided by entire population

Explanation: A proportion is a ratio where the numerator is included in the denominator (k = 1). It represents a part of the whole, such as the proportion of males in the total population.

2.
Sex Ratio is computed as
a) Male/Female X 100
b) Female/Male X 100
c) Male Female/ Females X 100
d) Male/ Male Female X100
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Male/Female X 100

Explanation: The Sex Ratio is defined as the number of males per 100 females. Formula: $\frac{Males}{Females} \times 100$.

3.
The Demographic Transition Theory has ____ stages on average
a) Six
b) Seven
c) Four
d) Two
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Four

Explanation: The classic Demographic Transition Model has four stages: 1. High Stationary, 2. Early Expanding, 3. Late Expanding, 4. Low Stationary. (Some modern versions add a 5th stage).

4.
The digit preference of age misreporting is also known as
a) Meyers Index
b) Age Shifting
c) Age Heaping
d) Whipple's Index
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Age Heaping

Explanation: Age heaping refers to the tendency of respondents to report ages ending in certain preferred digits (usually 0 and 5) instead of their exact age.

5.
Fertility can be studied as:
a) Period and sex
b) Cohort and sex
c) Period and cohort
d) Period and age
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Period and cohort

Explanation: Fertility analysis is typically conducted using two approaches: Period analysis (cross-sectional, specific time interval) and Cohort analysis (longitudinal, following a group over time).

6.
The two type of Life tables include the complete Life table and
a) Abbreviated
b) Abridged
c) Radix
d) Cohort
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Abridged

Explanation: Life tables are classified as Complete (data for every single year of age) or Abridged (data for age groups, usually 5-year intervals).

7.
The Radix is referred to as
a) The specified number of births at the beginning of a Life table
b) Mortality in a year
c) Morbidity in a year
d) Number of persons attaining a particular age
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) The specified number of births at the beginning of a Life table

Explanation: The Radix ($l_0$) is the hypothetical initial population of a life table, usually set at 100,000 live births.

8.
Given that a county had a population of 1,171,000 and there were 10,063 deaths the Crude Death Rate (CDR) would be
a) 6.72
b) 5.05
c) 8.59
d) 3.23
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) 8.59

Explanation: $CDR = \frac{\text{Total Deaths}}{\text{Total Population}} \times 1000 = \frac{10,063}{1,171,000} \times 1000 \approx 8.59$.

9.
A Whipple's Index result of 105-110 is interpreted on the United Nation Scale as
a) Highly accurate
b) Fairly accurate
c) Approximate
d) Rough
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Fairly accurate

Explanation: UN Scale for Whipple's Index: < 105 (Highly Accurate), 105-109.9 (Fairly Accurate), 110-124.9 (Approximate), 125-174.9 (Rough),> 175 (Very Rough).

10.
A ratio in Demography is:
a) A stated numerical ratio between values
b) A special type of ratio where the numerator is included in the denominator
c) A number is divided by the other given number
d) A special type of ratio in which the denominator is included in the numerator
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) A stated numerical ratio between values

Explanation: A ratio is simply the relationship between two numbers, often expressed as the quotient of one divided by the other ($a/b$), where the numerator is not necessarily part of the denominator.

11.
The following are sources of Demographic data except
a) Census
b) Registration of persons
c) Research
d) Demographic Health surveys
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Research

Explanation: Census, Vital Registration, and Surveys (like DHS) are the primary sources of demographic data. "Research" is a broad term for the process of inquiry, not a specific data source itself in this context.

12.
Compute the demographic proportion where X is 3.33 and Y is 2.45
a) 0.23
b) 0.53
c) 0.33
d) 0.58
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) 0.58

Explanation: A proportion of X in the total (X+Y) is calculated as $\frac{X}{X+Y} = \frac{3.33}{3.33 + 2.45} = \frac{3.33}{5.78} \approx 0.576$, which rounds to 0.58.

13.
One of the following students is correct on what an intermediate population is comprised of
a) Has 30% - 40% under 15 years and 5% - 10% of above 65 years
b) More than 40% under 15 years
c) Less than 30% under 15 years and more than 10% and above 65 years
d) More than 30% under 15 years and less than 10% and above 65 years
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Has 30% - 40% under 15 years and 5% - 10% of above 65 years

Explanation: An intermediate population structure falls between a young (expansive) and old (constrictive) population, typically characterized by moderate proportions of youth (30-40%) and elderly (5-10%).

14.
State the demographic measurement you would obtain by finding the number of deaths per 1000 population.
a) Birth Rate
b) Fertility Rate
c) Death Rate
d) Migration Rate
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Death Rate

Explanation: The number of deaths per 1,000 population is the definition of the Crude Death Rate (CDR).

15.
In demographic measurements Fertility relates to all the following except
a) Means that a woman was a mother.
b) The number of live births a woman is expected to have
c) The number of live births a woman has actually had.
d) Means that a woman is or was a mother.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) The number of live births a woman is expected to have

Explanation: Fertility refers to actual reproductive performance (actual live births). "Expected" births relates more to fecundity (potential) or projections, not the definition of fertility itself in this context.

16.
Which of the following demographic statements is correct on population Size:
a) The number of persons in a given population.
b) The arrangement of the population in space at a given time
c) The distribution of the population among its sex and age grouping.
d) The growth or decline of the total population or one of its structural units.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) The number of persons in a given population.

Explanation: Population size refers simply to the total number of individuals in the population.

17.
Which of the following demographic statements is correct on population distribution
a) The number of persons in a given population.
b) The arrangement of the population in space at a given time
c) The distribution of the population among its sex and age grouping.
d) The growth or decline of the total population or one of its structural units.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) The arrangement of the population in space at a given time

Explanation: Population distribution refers to how the population is spread out geographically or spatially.

18.
Which of the following demographic statements is correct on population Structure
a) The number of persons in a given population.
b) The arrangement of the population in space at a given time
c) The distribution of the population among its sex and age grouping.
d) The growth or decline of the total population or one of its structural units
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) The distribution of the population among its sex and age grouping.

Explanation: Population structure (or composition) refers to the makeup of the population in terms of characteristics like age and sex.

19.
Which one of the following demographic statements is correct on population Change
a) The number of persons in the population.
b) The arrangement of the population in space at a given time
c) The distribution of the population among its sex and age grouping.
d) The growth or decline of the total population or one of its structural units.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) The growth or decline of the total population or one of its structural units.

Explanation: Population change refers to the increase or decrease in population size or changes in its composition over time.

20.
In Demographic population characteristics the following are correct on ageing population except;
a) The pupation has Less than 30%
b) The pupation has More than 10%
c) The pupation has below 30 years
d) The pupation has Less than 30
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) The pupation has below 30 years

Explanation: An ageing population is typically characterized by a median age above 30 years and a proportion of elderly (65+) exceeding 10%. A population with a median age "below 30 years" is considered young or intermediate, not ageing.

21.
A truncated population pyramid refers to;
a) One cut at the top with an open ended interval
b) One representing a developing country
c) One representing a developed country
d) One elongated and needle like at the peak
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) One cut at the top with an open ended interval

Explanation: A truncated pyramid often refers to one where the top age group is an open-ended interval (e.g., 80+ or 85+), "cutting off" the detailed distribution at the very top.

22.
The following are fertility measurements except
a) Age specific fertility rate
b) Total fertility
c) Crude birth rate
d) General fertility rate
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Total fertility

Explanation: The standard measures are Age Specific Fertility Rate (ASFR), Crude Birth Rate (CBR), and General Fertility Rate (GFR). "Total fertility" is likely a shorthand for Total Fertility Rate (TFR), but if one must be chosen as an exception, it might be the least precise term or a typo for something else.

23.
All the following are required when constructing a population pyramid except
a) Knowledge of X and Y working principles
b) Population data with age distribution
c) Total population distribution
d) Population data with sex distribution
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Knowledge of X and Y working principles

Explanation: Constructing a pyramid requires population data distributed by age and sex. "Knowledge of X and Y working principles" is a vague or irrelevant phrase in this context.

24.
In Civil registration of births the United Nations recommends that all the following details be collected at a minimum for live birth registration: except
a) Type of past birth/delivery
b) Date of occurrence and registration
c) Place of occurrence
d) Attendance at birth
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Type of past birth/delivery

Explanation: Essential details for current birth registration include date, place, and attendance. Details about *past* births are usually collected in censuses or surveys for fertility analysis, not necessarily as a minimum requirement for the civil registration of the *current* birth itself.

25.
In population data computation subtracting the number of deaths from number of births results into
a) Mortality Rate
b) Natural Increase
c) Fertility Rate
d) Net Migration
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Natural Increase

Explanation: Natural Increase = Births - Deaths.

26.
In Demography to compute Child to woman ratio we require the following except
a) the population of under five years
b) Expressing the ratio as a percentage
c) divided the under-five by number of women aged 15-49 years
d) Expressing the ratio by per 1000 population of women of reproductive age.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Expressing the ratio as a percentage

Explanation: The Child-Woman Ratio (CWR) is calculated as $\frac{\text{Children 0-4}}{\text{Women 15-49}} \times 1000$. It is expressed per 1,000 women, not as a percentage.

27.
Which of the following Demographic measurement is commonly used to determine the health status of a community
a) Crude death Rate
b) Age specific fertility rate
c) Age dependence ratio
d) infant mortality
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) infant mortality

Explanation: The Infant Mortality Rate (IMR) is widely considered the single most sensitive indicator of the overall health status and socio-economic development of a community.

28.
Which of the following demographic measurements is commonly used to show the risk in women of child bearing age in a community
a) Still birth rate
b) Maternal mortality rate
c) Crude death rate
d) Total mortality rate
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Maternal mortality rate

Explanation: The Maternal Mortality Rate (MMR) measures the risk of death associated with pregnancy and childbirth among women of reproductive age.

29.
In demographic analysis a technique particularly useful in representing mortality situation of a given population is referred to as :
a) Ratio
b) Radix

d) Life table

Correct Answer: d) Life table

Explanation: A life table is a powerful tool used to summarize the mortality experience of a population and calculate life expectancy.

Population Census is conducted every after

Explanation: In Kenya (and many other countries), the national population census is conducted every 10 years.

31.
Which of the following characteristic is not an important factor in Mortality analysis

Complete the demographic statements below with relevant missing terms: (5 Marks)
a. ____ is the number of deaths of children under one year of age per 1,000 live births in a given region.
b. ____ is the complete expulsion or extraction from its mother of a product of conception, irrespective of the duration of pregnancy, which after such separation, breathes or shows any other evidence of life...
c. ____ Permanent disappearance of all evidence of life at any time after live birth has taken place...
d. ____ The total process of collecting, compiling, analyzing, and publishing or otherwise disseminating demographic, economic, and social data pertaining to all persons in a country...
e. ____ is the aggregate of all units that experience a particular demographic event during a specific time interval in a population...
Show Answer

Answer:

  1. Infant Mortality Rate (IMR)
  2. Live Birth
  3. Death
  4. Population Census
  5. Cohort
2.
The Basic Demographic Equations is represented by $P_{t1} = P_{t0} + (B - D) + (I - O)$. State the demographic events that each of the equation components represents (5 Marks)
Show Answer

Answer:

  • $P_{t1}$: Population at the end of the period (time $t_1$).
  • $P_{t0}$: Population at the beginning of the period (time $t_0$).
  • B: Number of Births during the period.
  • D: Number of Deaths during the period.
  • I: Number of Immigrants (In-migrants) during the period.
  • O: Number of Emigrants (Out-migrants) during the period.
3.
State any five (5) sources of demographic data (5 Marks)
Show Answer

Answer:

  1. Population Census
  2. Vital Registration System (Civil Registration)
  3. Sample Surveys (e.g., KDHS)
  4. Population Registers
  5. Administrative Records (Health, Education, Immigration records)
4.
Explain three (3) determinants of sex composition in a given population (5 Marks)
Show Answer

Answer:

  1. Sex Ratio at Birth: Biologically, slightly more males are born than females (approx 105 males per 100 females).
  2. Sex Differentials in Mortality: Females generally have lower mortality rates than males at most ages, leading to more females in older age groups.
  3. Sex Selective Migration: Migration is often sex-selective (e.g., males migrating for work), which can skew the sex ratio in both sending and receiving areas.
5.
Explain age shifting as a type of age misreporting in demography (5 Marks)
Show Answer

Answer: Age shifting involves the systematic overstatement or understatement of age by respondents. Unlike age heaping (digit preference), which clusters ages at specific digits, age shifting moves a cohort from one age group to another. For example, older individuals may exaggerate their age to appear more respected, or young adults might understate their age for economic or social reasons (e.g., marriage, employment, military service avoidance).

6.
Describe any five (5) important reasons for demographic studies (5 Marks)
Show Answer

Answer:

  1. Development Planning: To plan for future needs in education, health, housing, and infrastructure based on population projections.
  2. Resource Allocation: To distribute resources equitably among different regions and population groups.
  3. Policy Formulation: To design policies related to family planning, migration, and social security.
  4. Market Research: Businesses use demographic data to identify target markets and potential demand for goods and services.
  5. Understanding Social Change: To analyze trends in fertility, mortality, and migration and their impact on society.
  6. Health Administration: To determine health indicators (like IMR, MMR) and evaluate the effectiveness of health programs.
7.
Describe the demographic transition theory showing all the stages (5 Marks)
Show Answer

Answer: The Demographic Transition Theory describes the transition from high birth and death rates to low birth and death rates as a country develops.

  • Stage 1 (High Stationary): High birth rates and high death rates. Population growth is slow and fluctuating. (Pre-industrial society).
  • Stage 2 (Early Expanding): Death rates fall rapidly due to improvements in health and sanitation, while birth rates remain high. Population grows rapidly.
  • Stage 3 (Late Expanding): Birth rates begin to fall due to social changes (urbanization, contraception), while death rates continue to fall slightly. Population growth slows down.
  • Stage 4 (Low Stationary): Low birth rates and low death rates. Population growth is low or zero. (Industrialized society).
  • (Stage 5 - Declining): Birth rates fall below death rates, leading to population decline.

Section 3: Long Answer Questions

20 Marks
1.
The following table shows data recovered from Magadi Sub County Vital Health Records. Use the Magadi data to answer the following questions:
a. Compute the following:
i. General fertility rate
ii. Total Fertility Rate
b. State the Meaning of your answer in (b) above
Age Women Population Birth
10 to 14 1039 5
15 to 19 832 245
20 to 24 507 216
25 to 29 475 190
30 to 34 424 138
35 to 39 233 78
40 to 44 258 72
45 to 49 158 12
50 to 54 253 1
55 to 59 60 1
Show Answer

a) Calculations:

i. General Fertility Rate (GFR):

Formula: $GFR = \frac{\text{Total Live Births}}{\text{Mid-year Female Population (15-49)}} \times 1000$

  • Total Births (15-49): $245 + 216 + 190 + 138 + 78 + 72 + 12 = 951$
  • Total Women (15-49): $832 + 507 + 475 + 424 + 233 + 258 + 158 = 2887$
  • $GFR = \frac{951}{2887} \times 1000 \approx 329.4$ births per 1000 women aged 15-49.

ii. Total Fertility Rate (TFR):

Formula: $TFR = 5 \times \sum ASFR$ (where ASFR = Births/Women)

Age Group Births Women ASFR
15-19 245 832 0.2945
20-24 216 507 0.4260
25-29 190 475 0.4000
30-34 138 424 0.3255
35-39 78 233 0.3348
40-44 72 258 0.2791
45-49 12 158 0.0759
Sum 2.1358

$TFR = 5 \times 2.1358 = 10.68$ children per woman.

b) Meaning of TFR:

A Total Fertility Rate of 10.68 means that, on average, a woman in Magadi Sub County would give birth to approximately 11 children during her reproductive lifetime if she were to experience the current age-specific fertility rates throughout her childbearing years.

2.
The following data was extracted from the Isiolo County Development profile. Use this data to compute the following Demographic measurement for Isiolo County:
a. Crude death Rate (2 Marks)
b. Infant Mortality rate (2 Marks)
c. Age specific death rate for age groups above 60 year
d. Give the meaning of the answer computed in (b) above with relation to Isiolo County (2 Marks)
Age Groups Population Deaths
0-1 174078 3720
1-4 754758 1220
5-9 879129 396
10-14 808510 298
15-19 720161 561
20-24 622988 673
25-29 733057 752
30-34 732312 965
35-39 612825 1113

a) Crude Death Rate (CDR):

Total Population = Sum of Population column = 8,551,204 (approx check: 174k+754k+879k+...)

Total Deaths = 3720+1220+396+298+561+673+752+965+1113+1405+1226+2878+3266+5212+6866+6182+8199 = 44,932

$CDR = \frac{44,932}{8,551,204} \times 1000 \approx 5.25$ deaths per 1000 population.

b) Infant Mortality Rate (IMR):

Note: Standard IMR uses Live Births. Since births are not given, we use Age Specific Death Rate for age 0-1 as a proxy or calculate based on the given population 0-1.

$ASDR_{0-1} = \frac{\text{Deaths } 0-1}{\text{Population } 0-1} \times 1000 = \frac{3720}{174078} \times 1000 \approx 21.37$ per 1000.

c) Age Specific Death Rates (ASDR) for ages > 60:

  • 60-64: $\frac{5212}{374317} \times 1000 = 13.92$
  • 65-69: $\frac{6866}{256247} \times 1000 = 26.79$
  • 70-74: $\frac{6182}{154623} \times 1000 = 39.98$
  • 75+: $\frac{8199}{149917} \times 1000 = 54.69$

d) Meaning of IMR (b):

An Infant Mortality Rate of approximately 21 per 1000 means that out of every 1,000 infants in Isiolo County, about 21 die before celebrating their first birthday. This indicates the health status and quality of neonatal/post-neonatal care in the county.
