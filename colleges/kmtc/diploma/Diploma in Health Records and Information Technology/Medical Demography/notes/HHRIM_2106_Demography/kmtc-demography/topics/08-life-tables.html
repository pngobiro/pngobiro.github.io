9.1 LIFE TABLE

A life table is a life history of a hypothetical group or cohort of people as it is diminished gradually by deaths.

The Uses Of A Life Table

The construction and handling of a life table are matters of professional demographers.
Health and rural planners should however at least to be aware of the data that can be obtained from life tables so that they may be applied usefully in the course of their work.

In particular they should be aware that a life table can show:-

  • The probability of dying within some specific time period of persons within some age groups.
  • The average number of years a new born infant can be expected to live
  • The average number of years remaining for any person of any age.
  • The probability of surviving for any given age to any other age.
  • The probability of surviving for any given number of years for persons of any age.

Terms Used In Life Table

  • dx = the deaths during a year of persons aged x
  • px = mid year population
  • nmx = ASMR age specific mortality rate
  • nqx = probability of dying between exact ages x and x+1
  • npx = probability of surviving between exact age x and x+1
  • nax = proportion of the year lived by those who died.
  • Ix = life table population.
  • ndx = life table deaths
  • nlx = exact person years lived, proportion of the year lived by those who died.
  • tx = total person years lived after x
  • ex = life expectancy

THE FORMULAS

$$ nmx = \frac{dx (\text{death of persons aged } x)}{px (\text{population of persons aged } x)} $$
$$ nax = \text{proportion of the year lived by those who died and it is always given.} $$
$$ nqx = \frac{n \cdot nmx}{1 + n(n - nax) \cdot nmx} $$

Where n = class interval

Calculations of nqx

$$ \begin{aligned} nq1 &= \frac{n \cdot nmx}{1+n(1-nax) \cdot nmx} \\ &= \frac{1 * 0.021016}{1+1(1-0.3) * 0.021016} \\ &= 0.0207113 \end{aligned} $$
$$ \begin{aligned} nq4 &= \frac{4 * 0.016549}{1+4(1-0.4)* 0.016549} = 0.063667 \\ nq9 &= \frac{5 * 0.011396}{1+5(1-0.5)* 0.011396} = 0.055402 \\ nq14 &= \frac{5 * 0.009341}{1+5(1-0.5)* 0.009341} = 0.045639 \\ nq25 &= \frac{5 * 0.001983}{1+5(1-0.5) * 0.00198} = 0.0098 \\ nq74 &= \frac{0.477941 * 5}{1+5(1-0.5)* 0.477941} = 1.088777 \end{aligned} $$

npx = 1 - nqx (the probability of surviving)
Ix = Start with a rounded figure - assuming = 100,000
= 100,000 * chances of children surviving
= 100000 * npx
= npx * Ix (upper)

eq. TABLE Ix = npx * Ix (upper)
First Ix is given i.e for (0-1) age group = 100,000

$$ \begin{aligned} 1-4(\text{age}) &= npx (1^{st} \text{ class } * Ix 1^{st} \text{ class}) \\ &= 0.979289 * 100000 \\ &= 97929 \end{aligned} $$

Therefore $I_4 = 97929$

$$ \begin{aligned} ndx &= nqx * Ix (\text{upper}) \\ &= 100,000 * nqx \text{ or } (nqx * Ix) \\ (0-4) &= 020207113 * 100000 \\ &= 2071 \end{aligned} $$
$$ \begin{aligned} nlx &= \frac{(lo + l1)i}{2} \\ &= \frac{Ix(\text{lower}) + Ix(\text{upper}) * 5(\text{Class interval})}{2} \\ &= \frac{Ix(\text{upper}) + Ix(\text{lower}) * 5}{2} \\ &= \frac{(100000+97929) * 5}{2} \\ &= 494823 \end{aligned} $$
$$ nlx \text{ last class i.e } 85+ = \frac{ndx (\text{last})}{nmx (\text{last})} $$

To get the last figure for the last class in the nlx column, you get figures for the last ndx and nmx for the last class and divide as above. The figure you will get is added cumulatively from the bottom i.e. the last figure of column nlx thus forming column Tx as shown on chart.

Tx = Cumulative total from bottom of nlx
= cumulatively down - up
= cumulative from last class nlx upwards eg. 118161+282370-400531

Expectancy is then calculated by dividing Tx/Ix of each age group

$$ \begin{aligned} Ex &= \frac{Tx}{Lx} \\ &= 6116560/100000 \\ &= 61.1 \\ &= 61 \end{aligned} $$

(e.g.)
$0<1 = 6649664/100000 = 66.4 = 66$
$1-4 = Tx(\text{row } 1-4) / Ix(\text{row } 1-4) = 6166596/93227$
$5-9 = Tx(\text{row } 5-9) / Ix(\text{row } 5-9) = 5709774/92080$
$85+ = Tx(\text{last row } 1-4) / Ix(\text{last row } 1-4) = 30625/7990 = 3.8 = 4$
