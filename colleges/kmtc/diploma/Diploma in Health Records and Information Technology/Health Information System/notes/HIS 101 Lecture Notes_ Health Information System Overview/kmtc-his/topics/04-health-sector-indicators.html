Definition of indicators

An indicator; is a specific, observable and measurable characteristic that can be used to show changes or progress a programme is making toward achieving a specific outcome. There should be at least one indicator for each outcome. The indicator should be focused, clear and specific.

Health indicator; is a variable susceptible to direct measurement that reflects the state of health of a person, place, and a phenomenon. OR A quantifiable characteristics of a population which researchers use as supporting evidence for describing the health of a population..

Quality of care can be defined as the degree to which services for individuals and populations increase the like hood of desired health.

Indicator as a measure that assess a particular health care process.

  • Qualitative measures that can be used to monitor and evaluate the quality of important governance, management, clinical and support functions that affect patient's outcome
  • Are measurement tools, screens or flags that are used as guides to monitor, evaluate and improve the quality of patient care, clinical and support functions that affect patient outcomes
  • Indicators provide quantitative basis for clinicians, organizations and planners aiming to achieve improvement in care and processes by which patients care is provided.
  • Indicators measurement and monitoring serve many purposes, they make possible to document the quality of care, make comparisons (benchmarking) overtime between places (e.g. hospitals, make judgment and set priorities (e.g. choosing a hospital or surgery or organizing medical care) support accountability, regulation and accreditation support quality improvement and support patient choice of providers. The use of indicators enables professionals and organizations to monitor and evaluate what happens to patients because of how well professionals and organizational system function to provide for needs of patients.

Key characteristics of ideal indicators

An ideal indicator RAVES:

  • Reliable - It gives the same result if used by different people
  • Appropriate - It is the best way of measuring what we want to know
  • Valid - It is measures what you want to measure
  • Easy - It is feasible to collect the data to produce this indicator.
  • Sensitive - It reflects changes in events being measured

Additionally, an ideal indicator:

  • Is based on agreed definitions and described exhaustively and exclusively.
  • Highly or optimally specific and sensitive i.e. detects few false positive and false negative
  • Indicators discriminates well.
  • Identifies events for the user i.e. meant for clinical providers, it is relevant to clinical practice.
  • Permits useful comparison.
  • Is evidence based.

Example of health status indicators

They inform about the causes of diseases and death in a given population

Example; morbidity rates of measles, death rates of TB, incidence of diarrhea, low weight rates

TYPES OF INDICATORS (Inputs, Process, Output, Impacts) System classification

Input:

Monitors affordability of resources,
Measures availability/Quality of resource

Process:

Monitors activities that are carried out
Measures accessibility of services coverage and quality

Output:

Monitors results of activities
Measures acceptability, use change performance, coverage and quality
They show to what extent the expected result was attained with available resources. The degree to which outputs affect outcomes and impacts

Example:

  • Percentage of fully immunized children
  • Incidence rates of EPI targeted disease
  • Reported new cases of acute flaccid paralysis

Impacts:

The impact of a programme is the effect that the programme induced on the overall health status and social economic conditions of the target population.

Example:

  • Nutritional status of children
  • Percentage of new family planning acceptors
  • Incidence and mortality rates due to HIV
  • Infant mortality rates
