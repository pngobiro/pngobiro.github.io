Department Functions and Communication

Functions of the Health Records and Information Department

By the end of the session, participants should be able to:-

  1. Identify the main function of Health Records and Information Department
  2. Relationship between Health Records Department and other Departments
  1. Reception
    This is the area where patients/clients are welcomed and sorted and directed elsewhere for treatment or other services
  2. Registration
    This is the recording of identification and social details of the patient/ client i.e. name, address, sex, residence and other identification information
  3. Admission
    This is the procedure carried out when the patient is required to be confirmed to a hospital ward/bed for further treatment or service
  4. Discharge
    This is the procedure that is carried out when the patient has undergone treatment or consumed services and has been allowed to return to their home/ residence
  5. Appointment
    This is the procedure that allows for the patient/ client who has been ordered by the attending doctor/ clinician to return to the hospital at a future date for treatment
  6. Filing
    This is a systematic way of keeping individual health care document and aggregate documents in appropriate equipment and using appropriate methods
  7. Tracing
    This is the control of the movement of patient/ client records or documents
  8. Clinic preparation
    This is the getting ready all patient/clients documents at least two days before the client/patient returns to hospital for treatment or other service
  9. Follow up
    This is a mechanism put in place so that patients/client diagnosed with certain diseases/conditions usually long term, or infectious and who have been discharged are treated away from the hospital, either at their homes or nearby hospitals to ensure the disease/condition is monitored
  10. Coding and indexing
    This is the allocation of symbols/numbers that are predetermined for each condition to the disease that the patient/ client is suffering from: This code is then recorded together with all similar disease hence indexing. Collection tabulation, analysis, interpretation and dissemination of health care data. Data on individual patients and on segment of/or the who, population is gathered processed, made meaningful and given to those who need the information
  11. Maintenance of health record equipment
    All machines: tools, technology and so forth supports the management of information is taken care of by the Health Records and information Department. Maintenance of information confidentiality, safe custody of all patients/clients Information, captured in all media, to prevent unauthorized disclosure, is one of the Health Records and information Department function
  12. Manage special health records
    Tuberculosis, maternity, genitor-urinary, HIV/AIDS, assault etc. information is usually given special attention
  13. Design medical forms and computer views (E.g. DHIS)
    The Health Records and Information department is expected to ensure capturing of information is neat, accurate, and consistent. This is achieved by capturing the data uniformly in standard forms and in consultation with the users.
  14. Ensuring quality assurance
    The Health Records Information Department ensures that the highest standards of information capture, use, maintenance/storage and disposal are established and maintained
  15. Coordination with other departments
    As may be noted during this HR&I Professionals training and working. The HR&I is one of a larger team of people who render services to the patients/clients. Because all other departments entrust the HR&I department with this keeping of information they captured. Coordination is important; no one department can serve patient or its own

Relationship Between a Health Records Department and Other Departments

The medical records officer in a health facility is expected to co-ordinated the day administrative functions with other departments in a hospital. Some of the departments in a health facility are listed below:-

  1. Laboratory
  2. X- Ray
  3. Radiography
  4. Orthopedic
  5. Dental
  6. Physiotherapy
  7. Occupational Therapy
  8. Department of Nursing
  9. Department of Oral Health
  10. Supplies
  11. Accounts
  12. Transport
  13. Maintenance
  14. Nutrition
  15. Pharmacy

All this co-ordination is done to enhance the treatment of the patient. Therefore the stands in the middle and all these activities revolve around the patient. No one department is more important than the other, they should work together towards the achievement of this common goal.

Effective Communication

This chapter is an important one since it is a management function that the health records officers needs in his/her day to day work. It covers communication process, skills and barriers to effective communication.

Learning Objectives

  1. Define communication
  2. Explain why we communicate
  3. Describe the communication process
  4. Describe the methods and media of communication
  5. Explain barriers to effective communication
  6. Explain how to overcome these barriers

Define

Communication- Is the transmission and reception of messages or Any means by which a thought may be transferred

Objectives of communication

  • To get others to think or act the way we want them to think or act
  • To inform others
  • To ask and answer question
  • To listen to others (Listening is one of the most important communication skills)

Communication Process

We spend most of our lives communicating in to someone or another even when we are alone. We are picking up communication through our senses. The average manager or supervisor spends up to 80% of his time communicating during the working day.

Methods and Media of Communication

There are at least nine different ways in which we can communicate or receive a message. They are;-Speech, Sight, Sound, Action, Mannerisms, Taste, Smell, Touch, Silence.

Speech

Is an obvious method of communication usually is the one we think of first.

Sight

Is the most effective of all methods of communication because the mind thinks in pictures. The average person is able to remember a mental picture of up to 80% of what he has seen. Usually he can only remember or grasp 20% of what he has heard. This is why visual aids (Pictures, diagrams, charts, and films) are such valuable aids to teaching and why practical demonstrations are essential.

Sound

It plays quite a part in communicating a message too (a whistle, a car horn, a bomb blast military, music a love song)

Actions

Speaks louder than words, so it is said and it is often how he reacts physically, conveys a clear message both to the communicator and the receiver.

Mannerisms

Covers facial expression and other behavioural signals (a smile, a nod) to show understanding, a frown or head scratching to show lack of understanding. Look for mannerism when to communicate with a receiver.

Taste, Smell & Touch

All convey message to the receiver. For example there is no sugar in our today. The bearing in that machine smells hot.

Silence

We sometimes forget that we can also communicate through silence. This could show deep understanding of a speaker's words and unwillingness to interrupt him, but it could also show our strong disapproval of what he said.

Barriers to Effective Communication

There are many barriers which makes it difficult for the communicator to reach his goal. Some of the important barriers are;-

  1. Age
  2. Socio-economic status
  3. Language Vocabulary
  4. Attitude- Senders or Receivers
  5. Competition for attention

I. Age-The age difference between the sender and the receiver

These age barrier almost always exist when two people of different age brackets are communicating with each other. A common language has to be found that will suit both the sender, who may happen to be a sophisticated middle-aged executive and the receiver and upcoming recent college graduate.

II. Socio-economic status

The economic gap between two different income groups is another barrier. For example a two car owner is not communicating when he tells someone who cannot even afford to take a taxi hat safety belt should be installed in the car.

III. Language Vocabulary

Barriers exist when the sender uses technical language to a group of laymen who have had no previous experiences in the area.

IV. Attitude-Senders or Receivers

Men of different political persuasions belonging to different schools of thought of political ideology or parties seldom communicate effectively of influence each other.

V. Competition for attention

There is much competition for attention of the audience at any given time. To be an effective communicator, you must be able to catch the audience's attention and hold it throughout the period of the presentation. A good communicator should recognize and remove all communication barriers before starting to communicate.

How to Overcome Barriers

The sender must know his audience's

  • Background
  • Interest
  • Language

As a communicator you must remember that your audience is a human not a machine or mere cogs in a machine. Every audience is unique. Each responds to the sender's message according to his/her own drive, attitude, motivation, his education or lack of it, his social background and his interests.

As a communicator, it should be remembered that knowing your audience is not as simple as it sounds. One must understand human nature and human needs. Study your audience thoroughly and use language that your audience understands in order to minimize communication barriers. Secondly the message must be timely, Meaningful and applicable to the situation.
