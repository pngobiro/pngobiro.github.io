Section 1: Definitions

5 Marks
1.
Define the following terms as used in health records management:

i) Subpoena duces tecum

Show Answer

Answer:

A Subpoena Duces Tecum is a legal order or writ issued by a court of law requiring a witness (typically the Health Records Officer) to appear in court and bring specific relevant documents, records, or evidence (such as a patient's medical file) with them.

ii) Worker's compensation

Show Answer

Answer:

Worker's Compensation is a form of insurance providing wage replacement and medical benefits to employees injured in the course of employment. Health records play a vital role here in documenting the extent of injury and disability for claim assessment.

iii) Unit health record system

Show Answer

Answer:

The Unit Health Record System is a filing method where a patient is assigned a single unique identification number (Unit Number) at their first visit. All subsequent records, whether inpatient or outpatient, are filed in that one single folder bearing that number, ensuring a comprehensive view of the patient's history.

iv) Electronic medical record (EMR)

Show Answer

Answer:

An Electronic Medical Record (EMR) is a computerized legal medical record created in an organization that delivers care, such as a hospital or physician's office. It contains the standard medical and clinical data gathered in one provider's office.

v) Implied consent

Show Answer

Answer:

Implied Consent is consent which is not expressly granted by a person, but rather implicitly granted by a person's actions and the facts and circumstances of a particular situation (e.g., a patient rolling up their sleeve for a blood pressure check implies consent for the procedure).

Section 2: Short Answer Questions

10 Marks
2.
Explain the concept of disclosure as used in legal aspects of health records. (10 marks)
Show Answer

Answer:

Disclosure refers to the release, transfer, provision of access to, or divulging in any other manner of information outside the entity holding the information.

Key aspects include:

  1. Authorized Disclosure: Information released with the patient's written, informed consent (e.g., to insurance companies or lawyers).
  2. Mandatory Disclosure (Statutory Reporting): Release of information required by law without patient consent, such as:
    • Notification of infectious diseases (Public Health Act).
    • Reporting births and deaths.
    • Reporting child abuse or gunshot wounds.
  3. Disclosure for Treatment (Continuity of Care): Sharing information with other healthcare providers involved in the patient's care (Implied Consent).
  4. Disclosure due to Higher Duty (Public Interest): Breaching confidentiality when there is a serious threat to the public or an individual (e.g., a psychiatric patient threatening violence).
  5. Judicial Disclosure: Release of records pursuant to a valid court order or subpoena.

Section 3: Long Answer Questions

15 Marks
3.

As an HRIO at Kehancha Sub-County Hospital, you have been allocated a room measuring 25 by 50 feet for the library construction.

Parameters:

  • Standard height of one shelf is 7 feet.
  • 3 feet of gangways (aisles) all round.
  • Width of a single shelf is 1 foot.

Questions:

a) Sketch a diagram of the above measurement and make sure you are as economical as possible. (10 mks)

b) Putting into consideration the width of a single shelf is one foot, how many double-sided shelves can be constructed? (1 mk)

c) Calculate the number of files which can be accommodated by this library. (4 mks)

Show Answer

Answer:

Note on Space Planning: To be "economical" means maximizing storage capacity. Typically, rows of shelves should run parallel to the shorter wall to reduce the number of cross-gangways needed, or arranged in long bays with main aisles.

a) Diagram Description (Mental Sketch):

  • Total Area: 25ft x 50ft.
  • Clearance: 3ft gangway all around the perimeter.
  • Effective Usable Area:
    • Length: 50ft - 3ft (left) - 3ft (right) = 44 feet.
    • Width: 25ft - 3ft (top) - 3ft (bottom) = 19 feet.
  • Shelf Unit Dimensions:
    • Double-sided shelf depth = 1ft + 1ft = 2 feet.
    • Standard aisle between shelves = 3 feet.
    • Total Unit (Shelf + Aisle) = 5 feet.
  • Layout: Ideally, double-sided shelves running parallel to the 19ft side.

b) Number of Double-Sided Shelves:

We arrange the units along the longer available dimension (44 feet).

  • Unit Width (Shelf + Aisle) = 2ft + 3ft = 5ft.
  • Number of units = Available Length / Unit Width
  • Calculation: $44 \text{ ft} \div 5 \text{ ft} = 8.8$.
  • We can fit 8 full double-sided bays with aisles.
  • (Note: The last shelf doesn't strictly need a 3ft aisle after it if it abuts the perimeter gangway, so you might squeeze 9, but 8 is the standard safe calculation).

Answer: 8 Double-sided shelving units (approx).

c) File Capacity Calculation:

Assumptions (Standard HRIO values):

  • Shelf Length: The effective width of the room is 19ft. Let's assume shelves are ~15ft long to allow for structural pillars/gaps, or use the full 19ft. Let's use 18 feet per row for calculation.
  • Tiers: A 7ft high shelf usually has 6 tiers (shelves) for filing.
  • Files per foot: Approx. 50 files per linear foot (thin folders) or 12 files (thick 1-inch folders). We will calculate Linear Feet first.

Calculation Steps:

  1. Total Linear Feet of Shelving:
    $$ \text{Rows} \times \text{Sides} \times \text{Length} \times \text{Tiers} $$
    $$ 8 \text{ (rows)} \times 2 \text{ (sides)} \times 18 \text{ ft (length)} \times 6 \text{ (tiers)} $$
    $$ = 1,728 \text{ Linear Feet} $$
  2. Total Files:
    Assuming 50 files per foot (standard outpatient folder):
    $$ 1,728 \times 50 = 86,400 \text{ files} $$

Result: Approximately 86,400 files (depending on file thickness assumption used in class).
