Section 1: Multiple Choice Questions

20 Marks
1.
The following are special health records except
A. Psychiatric records
B. Diagnostic records
C. Maternity records
D. Casualty records
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B. Diagnostic records

Psychiatric, Maternity, and Casualty records often have specific legal retention schedules or sensitivity levels. "Diagnostic records" (like X-rays/Lab reports) are typically components of the general patient file rather than a distinct "Special Health Record" category in the same legal sense as psychiatric notes.

2.
______________________ans patient information should not be released to unauthorized person
A. Security
B. Confidentiality
C. Disclosure
D. Consent
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B. Confidentiality

Confidentiality is the ethical and legal duty to restrict access to patient information to only authorized individuals.

3.
____ can be expressed or implied
A. Consent
B. Disclosure
C. Retention
D. Ownership
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A. Consent

Consent for treatment or information release can be Expressed (written/verbal) or Implied (through actions like visiting a doctor).

4.
The medical form that is used by doctors for exchange of information is ____
A. Discharge summary
B. Correspondence
C. Report forms
D. Continuation sheet
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B. Correspondence

Correspondence (Referral letters, reply letters) is the primary formal method for doctors to exchange information about a patient between facilities.

5.
Confidential of medical records is regarded under the following three points of view except
A. Legal aspect
B. Ethical aspect
C. Common sense aspect
D. Logical aspect
Show Answer

Correct Answer: D. Logical aspect

Confidentiality is typically framed by Law (Legal), Professional Conduct (Ethical), and Practicality (Common Sense/Administrative). "Logical aspect" is not a standard category in this context.

6.
________ is the obligation of health-care provider to maintain patient information in a manner that will not permit dissemination beyond the health care providers
A. Disclosure
B. Ownership
C. Confidentiality
D. Ethics
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C. Confidentiality

This is the classic definition of confidentiality in healthcare.

7.
________ is an agreement by an individual to participate in research after receiving information about the purpose of the study and nature of the treatment
A. Implied consent
B. Informed consent
C. Patients' rights
D. Confidentiality
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B. Informed consent

Informed Consent is mandatory for research participation, ensuring the subject understands risks and benefits before agreeing.

8.
Master patient index should be retained for how long before being destroyed
A. 10 years
B. Permanently
C. 25 years
D. 5 years
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B. Permanently

The Master Patient Index (MPI) is the permanent key to all patient records. Even if the physical files are destroyed after their retention period, the index card (or entry) is kept permanently to show that a record once existed.

9.
________ is the use of a form on a trial basis in a small pilot study to determine how well the form works or serves the purpose designed for.
A. Post test
B. Pre-test
C. Multi-test
D. Bio test
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B. Pre-test

Pre-testing (or piloting) a form is a crucial step in form design to catch errors and usability issues before mass printing.

10.
When determining location of health records department, consideration must be given to the need for the department to be centrally located where it will provide the following services except
A. Prompt service for all patients...
B. Ready accessibility for medical officers...
C. Easy unavailability for administrative use.
D. Adjacent to the Admission Office...
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C. Easy unavailability for administrative use.

The goal is availability/accessibility. "Easy unavailability" is clearly the opposite of a desirable location attribute.

11.
When a patient signs a document authorizing the hospital to disclose his medical history for some specific purpose it is referred to as
A. Implied consent
B. Expressed consent
C. Disclosure due to high duty
D. Confidentiality
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B. Expressed consent

Written authorization is the highest form of Expressed Consent, required for releasing information to third parties.

12.
When selecting a folder the following points should be noted except?
A. Strength of manila
B. Cost of the folder
C. Clarity of numbering on the inside cover
D. Method of fastening documents
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C. Clarity of numbering on the inside cover

Folders are numbered on the outside (tabs/edge) for filing visibility. Strength, Cost, and Fastening mechanisms are critical selection criteria. Numbering inside the cover is not a standard requirement for retrieval.

13.
The following have no medico-legal aspects
A. Confidentiality
B. Professional ethics
C. Security of records
D. Family planning records
Show Answer

Correct Answer: D. Family planning records

A, B, and C are fundamental concepts that define medico-legal aspects. "Family Planning Records" is a type of clinical record; while it has legal implications (e.g., age of consent), in the context of this list comparing "Concepts" vs "Items," it is the odd one out. (Note: The question phrasing is slightly ambiguous, but D is the distinct distractor).

14.
Consent forms may be signed by the following except;-
A. Child under 6 years old
B. Parent
C. Wife
D. Husband
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A. Child under 6 years old

A minor (especially under 6) lacks the legal capacity to give consent. A parent or guardian must sign on their behalf.

15.
Patients have no right to their notes even when fee have been paid "The general word for this statement is
A. Security
B. Privacy
C. Ownership
D. Confidentiality
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C. Ownership

Ownership: The physical record belongs to the healthcare provider/facility, while the information contained within belongs to the patient. Paying fees does not transfer ownership of the physical file.

16.
Admissions psychiatric records are carried out in reference to?
A. Mental competency Act
B. Public health Act
C. Mental Health Act
D. Data protection 2019
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C. Mental Health Act

The Mental Health Act (Cap 248 in Kenya) specifically governs the admission (voluntary and involuntary) and treatment of patients with mental disorders.

17.
________ is a detailed nomination of the estimated facilities to meet the defined need in establishment of a health records department
A. Definition phase
B. Brief phase
C. Total facility phase
D. Department phase
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B. Brief phase

The Preparation of the Brief phase involves translating the needs defined in the Definition Phase into detailed specifications (facilities, rooms, equipment) for the architect/designers.

18.
The following describes the criteria for record retention in major health facilities
A. The level of activity for readmission rate, inpatients and outpatients.
B. The type of the health care given in the health facility
C. The availability of the information given.
D. The design of medical records department
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A. The level of activity for readmission rate, inpatients and outpatients.

Retention schedules often consider how often records are accessed (activity level/readmission). Active records are kept close; inactive ones are archived or destroyed based on this activity rate.

19.
Disposal of medical records equipment can be done through ________
A. Burning
B. Pulverization
C. Sale by public auctions
D. Shredding
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C. Sale by public auctions

Equipment (shelves, cabinets, computers) is an asset. When no longer needed, it is disposed of via Sale (Auction) or Transfer. Burning, Pulverization, and Shredding are methods for destroying records (paper/media), not durable equipment.

20.
If a request is made for the release of information the request should contain the following except?
A. Extent and nature of information to be released...
B. Recently undated authorization...
C. Full name of patient, address and date of birth
D. Name of person or institution requesting information
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B. Recently undated authorization...

A valid authorization must be DATED (and usually recent, e.g., within 90 days). An "undated" authorization is invalid and should be rejected.

Section 2: Short Answer Questions

40 Marks
21.
State 5 instances under which disclosure due to higher duty may happen. (5 marks)
Show Answer

Answer:

  1. Public Health Threat: Notification of communicable diseases (e.g., Cholera, TB, Ebola) to prevent outbreaks.
  2. Threat of Violence: If a patient threatens serious harm to a specific identifiable person (Tarasoff rule).
  3. Child Abuse/Neglect: Mandatory reporting to protect a minor from harm.
  4. Prevention of Crime: Reporting gunshot wounds or injuries related to criminal activities.
  5. National Security: Requests under terrorism prevention acts.
22.
State the recommended retention period of the following patient's records (5 marks)
Show Answer

Answer:

  • i. Diagnostic images (X-Ray films): 5 years (unless relevant to ongoing litigation).
  • ii. Operative index: Permanently (or at least 10 years).
  • iii. Registers of birth: Permanently.
  • iv. Maternity records: 25 years (to cover the child's minority plus limitation period).
  • v. Master patient index (MPI): Permanently.
23.
Enumerate 5 uses of forms designed in health records department (5 marks)
Show Answer

Answer:

  1. To standardize data collection (ensures consistency).
  2. To identify the patient and the provider (Header information).
  3. To communicate instructions (e.g., X-ray request forms).
  4. To record clinical findings and treatment (History sheets).
  5. To obtain legal authorization (Consent forms).
24.
Explain briefly 5 principles of form design that should be followed when designing medical forms (5 marks)
Show Answer

Answer:

  1. Simplicity: The form should be easy to understand and fill (avoid clutter).
  2. Identification: Every form must have a unique form number/ID and Title.
  3. Sequence: Layout should follow the logical flow of work/data entry (e.g., left to right, top to bottom).
  4. Spacing: Adequate space must be provided for the expected answers (e.g., larger space for address than for date).
  5. Standardization: Use standard paper sizes (A4, A5) and standard terminology/fonts.
25.
Outline 5 Considerations of a health records office space (5 marks)
Show Answer

Answer:

  1. Floor Loading Capacity: Must be strong enough to support heavy paper records.
  2. Security: Doors/windows must be secure to prevent unauthorized access.
  3. Safety/Fire Protection: Installation of sprinklers, smoke detectors, and fire exits.
  4. Environment: Proper ventilation and humidity control to preserve paper.
  5. Workflow: Layout should facilitate smooth movement of staff and files (ergonomics).
26.
Name 5 instances in which medical records are used as evidence (5 marks)
Show Answer

Answer:

  1. Medical Negligence/Malpractice suits.
  2. Personal Injury/Assault cases (P3 forms).
  3. Insurance Claims disputes.
  4. Disability Assessment/Workmen's Compensation.
  5. Criminal investigations (e.g., cause of death, rape).
27.
Briefly explain the term subpoena. (5 marks)
Show Answer

Answer:

A Subpoena is a formal written order issued by a court of law commanding a person to appear in court to testify as a witness (Subpoena Ad Testificandum) or to produce documents/records (Subpoena Duces Tecum). Failure to comply with a subpoena can result in punishment for contempt of court.

28.
State 5 checks made when conducting a pre-test of health records forms. (5 marks)
Show Answer

Answer:

  1. Clarity: Are the instructions and headings clear to the user?
  2. Spacing: Is there enough space provided for the answers?
  3. Sequence: Does the order of items match the workflow?
  4. Paper Quality: Is the paper durable enough for handling/archiving?
  5. Relevance: Is all the information requested actually necessary? (Eliminate redundancy).

Section 3: Long Answer Questions

40 Marks
29.
Describe phases one should be observe when designing a new hospital health records department (20 marks)
Show Answer

Answer:

Designing a new department involves a structured approach typically divided into four main phases:

1. The Definition Phase (Feasibility):

  • Needs Assessment: Establish the need for the department based on patient volume, types of services (Inpatient/Outpatient), and expected growth (5-10 years).
  • Policy Definition: Define the scope of services (Admission, Coding, Storage, Statistics).
  • Budgeting: Estimate financial resources required.

2. The Brief Phase (Preparation of the Brief):

  • Detailed Specifications: Translate needs into a written brief for the architect.
  • Space Calculation: Determine precise square footage for filing areas, staff offices, and waiting areas.
  • Location: Specify the relationship with other departments (e.g., near Main Entrance/OPD).
  • Flow: Define the movement of people and records.

3. The Design Phase:

  • Layout Planning: Work with architects to draw floor plans. Ensure functional areas (Reception, Coding, Filing) are logically arranged.
  • Structural Requirements: Specify floor loading (weight bearing), lighting (lux levels), and ventilation (humidity control).
  • Equipment Selection: Choose between static shelving or mobile racking (compactus). Plan for IT infrastructure.

4. The Construction and Commissioning Phase:

  • Monitoring: Supervise construction to ensure adherence to the brief (e.g., gangway widths).
  • Fitting Out: Install shelving and furniture.
  • Staffing: Recruit and train staff.
  • Record Migration: Systematically move records from the old location to the new one.
  • Go-Live: Operationalize the department.
30.
Explain five measures taken to ensure that special health records are secure (20 marks)
Show Answer

Answer:

Special health records (e.g., Psychiatric, HIV/AIDS, Staff records) require heightened security due to their sensitive nature. Measures include:

1. Separate Storage (Segregation):

  • These records should be filed in a separate, dedicated room or a separate lockable section within the main library, distinct from general files.

2. Lock and Key / Controlled Access:

  • The storage cabinets or room must be kept locked at all times. Keys should be held only by a senior authorized officer (e.g., Department In-Charge), not accessible to all clerical staff.

3. Restricted Access List:

  • Maintain a specific list of personnel (doctors/nurses) who are authorized to access these files. Requests from others must be denied or require higher-level approval (Medical Superintendent).

4. Access Register (Audit Trail):

  • Maintain a strict "Sign-in/Sign-out" register. Every retrieval must be logged with the name of the requester, date, time, and purpose. This deters unauthorized browsing.

5. Special Handling Protocols (Envelopes):

  • When the file is moved (e.g., to a clinic), it should be placed in a sealed confidential envelope to prevent casual viewing by porters or messengers during transit.
