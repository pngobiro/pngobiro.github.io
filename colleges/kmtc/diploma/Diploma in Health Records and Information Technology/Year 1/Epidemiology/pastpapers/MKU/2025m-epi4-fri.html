Show Answer

  • b) Rate: A measure of the frequency of a health event in a defined population during a specified period (includes a time dimension).

  • Lower Quartile (Q1): Median of lower half (1, 3, 4, 7) = $\frac{3+4}{2} = 3.5$

5.
The data listed below shows different incubation period for cholera: 2, 4, 6, 10, and 12 days. Using the same data, calculate the following:

a) Mid-range (4 Marks)

b) Standard deviation (4 Marks)

c) Standard error (4 Marks)

Show Answer

a) Mid-range:

$Midrange = \frac{Min + Max}{2} = \frac{2 + 12}{2} = 7$

b) Standard Deviation:

Mean ($\bar{x}$) = $\frac{2+4+6+10+12}{5} = \frac{34}{5} = 6.8$

Sum of squared deviations $\sum(x-\bar{x})^2$:

  • $(2-6.8)^2 = 23.04$
  • $(4-6.8)^2 = 7.84$
  • $(6-6.8)^2 = 0.64$
  • $(10-6.8)^2 = 10.24$
  • $(12-6.8)^2 = 27.04$
  • Sum = 68.8

Variance = $68.8 / (5-1) = 17.2$

Standard Deviation $s = \sqrt{17.2} \approx 4.15$

c) Standard Error:

$SE = \frac{s}{\sqrt{n}} = \frac{4.15}{\sqrt{5}} = \frac{4.15}{2.236} \approx 1.86$

6.
Calculate the 95% confidence interval for a mean total cholesterol level of 90, if the standard error of the mean is 8 (4 Marks)
Show Answer

Formula: $CI = \bar{x} \pm (Z \times SE)$

For 95% confidence, $Z \approx 1.96$

Lower Limit = $90 - (1.96 \times 8) = 90 - 15.68 = 74.32$

Upper Limit = $90 + (1.96 \times 8) = 90 + 15.68 = 105.68$

Confidence Interval: (74.32, 105.68)

Assumption: "Exposed" acts as the screening test positive.

$PPV = \frac{\text{True Positives}}{\text{Total Positives}} = \frac{\text{Exposed Cases}}{\text{Total Exposed}} = \frac{20}{30} = 66.7\%$

8.
Discuss five properties and uses of proportion in epidemiology. (15 Marks)
Show Answer

Properties:

  1. Dimensionality: Proportions are dimensionless (no units).
  2. Range: Values must lie between 0 and 1 (or 0-100%).
  3. Structure: The numerator is a subset of the denominator (unlike a ratio).

Uses:

  1. Prevalence: To estimate the burden of disease in a population at a specific time (Point Prevalence).
  2. Descriptive Statistics: To describe population demographics (e.g., proportion of males, proportion >65 years).
  3. Cumulative Incidence: To estimate the risk of developing a disease over a period (Risk).
  4. Resource Allocation: To determine the share of health resources needed for specific subgroups.
  5. Comparing Groups: To compare the frequency of characteristics between cases and controls.

10.
Give an illustration and discuss the five values that can provide a good description of the center, spread and shape of a distribution. (15 Marks)
Show Answer

To comprehensively describe a data distribution (Summary Statistics), we typically use the "Five Number Summary" or a combination of central and dispersion measures.

BOX PLOT ILLUSTRATION: |----[ | ]----| Min Q1 Med Q3 Max
  1. Measure of Center (Median/Mean):
    • Describes the typical or middle value. The Median is robust against outliers; the Mean includes all values.
  2. Minimum Value:
    • The smallest observation in the dataset. Indicates the lower bound of the range.
  3. Maximum Value:
    • The largest observation. Indicates the upper bound. (Min and Max together give the Range).
  4. Measure of Spread (Standard Deviation/IQR):
    • Describes how varied the data is. SD measures spread around the mean; IQR (Q3-Q1) measures the spread of the middle 50% of data.
  5. Measure of Shape (Skewness/Kurtosis):
    • Describes symmetry. Skewness indicates if the tail is longer on the left or right. Kurtosis indicates if the peak is steep or flat.

(Alternatively, the question may refer specifically to the "Five Number Summary" for a boxplot: Min, Q1, Median, Q3, Max).
