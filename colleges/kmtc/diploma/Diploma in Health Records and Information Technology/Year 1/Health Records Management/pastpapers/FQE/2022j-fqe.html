Section 1: Multiple Choice Questions

40 Marks
1.
Which of the following activities is not conducted during Clinic preparation?
a) Sorting
b) Retrieval
c) Editing
d) Tracing
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Editing

Clinic preparation involves Sorting (arranging appointment lists), Tracing (locating files), and Retrieval (pulling files from storage). Editing typically occurs during the analysis/assembly phase after discharge, not during clinic preparation.

2.
What is the main function of a waiting list?
a) Be able to make full use of the available beds
b) Individual patients can know when they are supposed to be admitted in wards
c) Registration can easily be done
d) Consultants can easily know how many patients are in their waiting list
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Be able to make full use of the available beds

While all options are benefits, the main administrative function of a waiting list is to optimize hospital bed occupancy by ensuring a steady flow of elective admissions to fill available vacancies.

3.
Who was the first leader in the movement to establish the first incorporated hospital within the United States?
a) Imhotep
b) Grace Mayers
c) Hippocrates
d) Benjamin Franklin
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Benjamin Franklin

Benjamin Franklin, along with Dr. Thomas Bond, was instrumental in founding the Pennsylvania Hospital in 1751, the first incorporated hospital in America.

4.
Which of the following information is not contained in a unit number Register?
a) Address
b) Date of birth
c) Full names of patient
d) Diagnosis
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Diagnosis

The Unit Number Register (or Master Patient Index) focuses on demographic and identification data (Name, DOB, Address, Sex, Number). Diagnosis is clinical information found in the patient's medical record or disease index, not usually in the number register.

5.
Patients have no right to their notes even when they have paid. What is the general word for this statement?
a) Security
b) Privacy
c) Ownership
d) Confidentiality
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Ownership

Physical Ownership: The physical medical record (paper or digital media) belongs to the healthcare facility/provider, while the information contained within belongs to the patient.

6.
____ is the act of testing a form whether it has been approximately designed for the work it is going to be used for.
a) Reproduction
b) Costing
c) Pre-testing
d) Auditing
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Pre-testing

Pre-testing (or piloting) involves trialing a new form with a small user group to identify design flaws before mass printing and implementation.

7.
____ is the objective method of applying a yardstick to the quality of professional performance.
a) Quality control
b) Quality assurance
c) Medical audit
d) Medical Edit
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Medical audit

Medical Audit is defined as the systematic, critical analysis of the quality of medical care, including the procedures used for diagnosis and treatment, the use of resources, and the resulting outcome and quality of life for the patient.

8.
Which of the following is not a paper based method of destruction?
a) Burning
b) Shredding
c) Pulping
d) Magnetic degaussing
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Magnetic degaussing

Magnetic Degaussing is used to destroy data on magnetic media (tapes, hard drives) by disrupting the magnetic domains. Burning, Shredding, and Pulping are methods used for paper records.

9.
The determination of the value of records before they are disposed is referred to as:
a) Appraisal records
b) Auditing
c) Destruction
d) Weeding
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Appraisal records

Record Appraisal is the process of evaluating records to determine their value (administrative, legal, fiscal, historical) to decide on their final disposition (retention or destruction).

10.
Which of the following records are not supposed to be stored permanently?
a) Register of births
b) Register of deaths
c) Register of surgical procedures
d) Outpatient attendance card
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Outpatient attendance card

Registers (Births, Deaths, Operations) are statutory records typically kept permanently. Outpatient attendance cards usually have a defined retention period (e.g., 6-10 years) after the last attendance.

11.
____ is the document used to acquire goods from the store.
a) S 12
b) S 11
c) S 13
d) S 14
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) S 11

In the Kenyan government procurement system, S 11 is the Counter Requisition and Issue Voucher used to request and issue items from stores.

12.
____ is a consent which arises when a patient signs a document authorizing the hospital to disclose his history.
a) Implied
b) Express
c) Patient consent
d) Medical ethics
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Express

Express consent is given in writing or verbally. Signing a document to authorize disclosure is a clear form of express consent. "Patient consent" is too general.

13.
Which of the following is the most economical type of filing equipment as far as space is concerned?
a) Suspended filing
b) Elevator filing
c) Shelve filing
d) Mobile racking
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Mobile racking

Mobile racking (Compactus) eliminates fixed aisles between shelves, allowing them to move together. This can increase storage capacity by up to 100% compared to static shelving in the same footprint.

14.
Which of the following documents should ideally be filed at the patient folder?
a) Front sheet of identification
b) Discharge summary
c) Nursing cardex
d) Confirmation sheet
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Front sheet of identification

The Identification Sheet (Face Sheet) contains critical demographic and summary data and is standardly filed at the very front of the patient folder for immediate reference.

15.
Which one of the following equipment is not suitable for recording appointment?
a) Loose leaf binders
b) Visible edge sheet
c) Diaries
d) Carousel type filing
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Carousel type filing

Carousel type filing is used for storing file folders or index cards, not for recording appointment schedules. Diaries, sheets, and binders are standard tools for manual appointment scheduling.

16.
Which of the following is not included in a daily bed return?
a) Parole
b) Admissions
c) Deaths
d) Outpatient attendances
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Outpatient attendances

The Daily Bed Return (DBR) tracks inpatient bed occupancy (Admissions, Discharges, Deaths, Transfers, Parole/Leave). Outpatient data is tracked in separate outpatient registers/returns.

17.
Which tracer card is created the same time as the case folder and is filed inside the folder?
a) Library tracer card
b) Personal tracer card
c) Common tracer card
d) Inpatient tracer card
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Personal tracer card

The Personal Tracer Card (or permanent tracer) is often created with the file and stays inside it until the file is removed, at which point the tracer is left in the file's place on the shelf.

18.
Confidentiality related to medical records can be regarded in three points of view below except:
a) Legal aspect
b) Ethical aspect
c) Common sense aspect
d) Express and implied aspect
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Common sense aspect

Confidentiality is typically viewed through Legal, Ethical, and Administrative/Professional frameworks. "Common sense" is not a formal aspect of confidentiality governance.

19.
____ refers to protection of information system of medical records against unauthorized disclosure.
a) Security
b) Retention
c) Confidentiality
d) Medical health records ethics
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Security

Security refers to the physical and technical mechanisms used to protect data. Confidentiality is the authorized restriction of access. Security protects confidentiality.

20.
Who in the hospital is supposed to fill the death notification?
a) Nursing Manager
b) Health Records Officer
c) Medical Officer
d) Social Worker
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Medical Officer

Certification of death is a medical responsibility. The Medical Officer (Doctor) attending the patient must verify death and complete the notification/certificate.

21.
A court's order to the physician to produce the records in court is referred to as:
a) Subpoena
b) Primafacie
c) Plaintiff
d) Complainant
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Subpoena

A Subpoena (specifically Subpoena Duces Tecum) is a legal order requiring a person (witness) to produce documents or records at a trial or hearing.

22.
Which of the following is an example of method of filing patient master index?
a) Sorting
b) Terminal digit filing
c) Phonetic treatment of surname
d) Drawer filing
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Phonetic treatment of surname

The Master Patient Index (MPI) is usually filed alphabetically. To handle name variations, a Phonetic system (like Soundex) is often used. Terminal digit is used for numerical medical records, not usually for the alphabetical name index.

23.
____ is a health record system in which all records notes relating to one patient are contained in one case folder
a) Unit numbering
b) Unit system
c) Editing
d) Registration
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Unit system

The Unit System ensures that a patient receives one unique number and one file that accumulates all documentation from all visits (inpatient and outpatient) over time.

24.
Taking down the identification details of a patient through telephone and through writing a letter before the patient attends the hospitals is referred to as:
a) Registration
b) Pre-registration
c) Appointment
d) Follow-up
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Pre-registration

Pre-registration involves collecting patient data prior to their arrival to streamline the admission process and reduce waiting times.

25.
What is the process through which information is reproduced mechanically once a master document containing this information has been created?
a) Medical auditing
b) Mechanical documentation
c) Re-production
d) Pre-testing
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Re-production

Reproduction (or Reprography) refers to the mechanical copying or duplicating of documents (e.g., embossing plastic cards to imprint forms).

Imhotep, the ancient Egyptian polymath, is often considered the author of the Edwin Smith Papyrus, which is one of the oldest known medical texts.

27.
The records that are initiated for patients who get admitted into the wards are referred to as:
a) Diagnostic records

Case records (or Inpatient records) are comprehensive files generated for patients admitted to the hospital, distinct from outpatient or casualty notes.

28.
____ is the ability of an individual to use his/her own knowledge to make decisions.
a) Integrity
b) Adaptability
c) Discretion

Discretion involves using judgment and knowledge to make prudent decisions, especially regarding sensitive information.

29.
Which of the following is not an example of Special Health Records?
a) Psychiatric record
b) Tuberculosis records
c) Maternity records
d) Surgical records
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Surgical records

Surgical records are typically part of the general patient case file. Psychiatric, TB, and Maternity records are often classified as "Special" due to specific legal, retention, or confidentiality requirements that differ from general records.

30.
Which one of the following is not an equipment used to maintain Master Index Cards?
a) Elevator files
b) Cart wheel
c) Carousel type files
d) Suspended filing
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Suspended filing

Suspended filing is used for standard size paper files (A4/Folio). Master Index Cards are small (e.g., 5x3 inches) and use Elevator files, Rotary (Cart wheel), or Card drawers.

In a Centralized system, appointment scheduling and the Master Patient Index are located in one central area, allowing immediate cross-referencing.

b) Quality control

d) Editing

Quality Assurance (QA) is the broad process focused on preventing defects and ensuring the service meets customer (patient) needs and satisfaction.

37.
Which of the following checks is NOT made on the waiting list?
a) Checking for completeness
b) Checking for accuracy
c) Checking for the diagnosis of a patient
d) Checking for deaths
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Checking for the diagnosis of a patient

Administrative staff check the list for data completeness, accuracy, and remove patients who have died or been admitted. They do not typically verify or clinically audit the diagnosis itself, as that is a medical responsibility.

38.
Which of the following is NOT a reason for ensuring a medical record contains sufficient data?
a) To identify the patient
b) Support the disclosure of the patient details
c) Justify the treatment, support the diagnosis
d) Accurately document the results
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Support the disclosure of the patient details

Records are kept for identification, continuity of care (justify treatment/diagnosis), and documenting results. Supporting disclosure is a consequence/function of the record, not the primary reason for ensuring data sufficiency during clinical documentation.

Section 2: Short Answer Questions

40 Marks
41.
State five (5) importance of Quality Assurance (5 Marks)
Show Answer

Answer:

  1. Improved Patient Care: Ensures patients receive standard, safe, and effective treatment.
  2. Error Reduction: Identifies and minimizes administrative and clinical errors.
  3. Cost Effectiveness: Reduces waste and repetition of tests/procedures.
  4. Compliance: Ensures adherence to legal and accreditation standards.
  5. Staff Motivation: Creates a culture of excellence and continuous improvement.
  6. Patient Satisfaction: Increases confidence and satisfaction with hospital services.
42.
Five (5) basic principles of form design (5 Marks)
Show Answer

Answer:

  1. Simplicity: The form should be easy to understand and fill out.
  2. Standardization: Use standard paper sizes (A4/A5) and font styles.
  3. Identification: Every form must have a unique title, form number, and date.
  4. Sequence: Data fields should follow a logical flow (e.g., left to right, top to bottom).
  5. Spacing: Adequate space must be provided for the intended information.
43.
What are the five importance of scheduling patients' appointment (5 Marks)
Show Answer

Answer:

  1. Reduces Waiting Time: Minimizes patient congestion and waiting hours.
  2. Resource Optimization: Ensures efficient use of staff and equipment.
  3. Continuity of Care: Allows preparation of records before patient arrival.
  4. Workload Management: Spreads patient volume evenly throughout the day.
  5. Patient Convenience: Allows patients to plan their visits.
44.
Explain 5 qualities of an appraiser (5 Marks)
Show Answer

Answer:

An appraiser (one who evaluates records for retention/destruction) should possess:

  1. Knowledgeable: Deep understanding of the organization's history and functions.
  2. Objective: Ability to assess value without bias.
  3. Thoroughness: Attention to detail to ensure no vital record is destroyed.
  4. Experience: Familiarity with legal and administrative requirements.
  5. Foresight: Ability to anticipate future research or legal needs for the records.
45.
State 5 Acts of medical records (5 Marks)
Show Answer

Answer:

Note: This refers to legislation affecting medical records in Kenya/General practice.

  1. Public Health Act (Cap 242): Notification of infectious diseases.
  2. Mental Health Act: Regulations for psychiatric records/admissions.
  3. Evidence Act: Admissibility of records in court.
  4. Registration of Births and Deaths Act: Statutory notification requirements.
  5. Medical Practitioners and Dentists Act: Professional conduct regarding confidentiality.
  6. Data Protection Act: Privacy and handling of personal data.
46.
Briefly explain 5 instances where health records can be used as evidence (5 Marks)
Show Answer

Answer:

  1. Malpractice Suits: To prove or disprove negligence in treatment.
  2. Criminal Cases: To document injuries in assault, rape, or murder cases.
  3. Insurance Claims: To verify treatment for compensation/reimbursement.
  4. Worker's Compensation: To prove work-related injury.
  5. Will Contests: To prove the mental capacity (sanity) of a testator at the time of writing a will.
47.
State 5 functions of a bed bureau (5 Marks)
Show Answer

Answer:

  1. Monitor Bed Occupancy: Maintaining an up-to-date count of available beds.
  2. Coordinate Admissions: Allocating beds to emergency and waiting list patients.
  3. Liaison: Acting as a link between wards, A&E, and doctors.
  4. Waitlist Management: Selecting patients from the waiting list when beds become available.
  5. Reporting: Producing the Daily Bed Return (DBR) and occupancy statistics.
48.
Define the following (5 Marks)

Answer:

  • a) Correspondence: The exchange of written communication (letters, emails, memos) regarding a patient's care between the hospital and external parties (GPs, other hospitals, relatives, insurance). It forms part of the administrative section of the medical record.

Section 3: Long Answer Question

20 Marks
49.

a) Explain 8 core-care delivery functions that electronic HR system should be capable of performing in order to promote greater safety, quality and efficiency in health care delivery (16 Marks)

b) State four benefits of EMR (4 Marks)

Show Answer

Answer:

a) 8 Core Functions of EHR (IOM Standards):

  1. Health Information and Data: The system must provide immediate access to key information such as diagnoses, allergies, lab results, and medications to support decision-making.
  2. Result Management: The ability to manage all test results (labs, radiology) electronically, ensuring providers receive results quickly to treat patients effectively.
  3. Order Entry/Management (CPOE): Computerized Provider Order Entry allows electronic prescribing and ordering of tests, reducing errors caused by illegible handwriting.
  4. Decision Support: Providing alerts, reminders, and clinical guidelines (e.g., drug-interaction warnings) at the point of care to enhance safety.
  5. Electronic Communication and Connectivity: Enabling secure communication between healthcare team members (doctors, nurses, pharmacists) and external partners.
  6. Patient Support: Tools for patient engagement, such as access to their own records (portals), educational materials, and home monitoring.
  7. Administrative Processes: Streamlining scheduling, billing, and registration to improve efficiency and reduce wait times.
  8. Reporting and Population Health: The capacity to aggregate data for public health reporting, quality improvement, and disease surveillance.

b) Four Benefits of EMR:

  1. Improved Quality and Safety: Reduces medical errors through legible notes and decision support alerts.
  2. Efficiency and Cost Savings: Reduces duplicate testing, storage costs, and time spent searching for files.
  3. Accessibility: Multiple authorized users can access the record simultaneously from different locations.
  4. Durability: Electronic records do not tear, age, or deteriorate like paper records.
