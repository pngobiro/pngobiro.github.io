The quote "Health records are as necessary for the practice of medicine as medications are for effective treatment" highlights the fundamental role of documentation in healthcare.

Imhotep (circa 2650 BC) was an Egyptian polymath and physician, often credited with being the first to use systematic medical records.

"Grand Vizier" was a title held by Imhotep in Egypt. Hippocrates was a Greek physician known for establishing medicine as a profession and scientific discipline.

6.
The principles of medical records professional acknowledges the undermentioned except
a) Respects the dignity of each client/patient
b) Refuses to participate and to conceal the legal or ethical acts
c) Strives to improve personal competence and quality of service

8.
The medical records can serve the below mentioned apart from one

b) To serve as an inference in continuity of patient care
c) To provide documentary evidence of care given in the facility
d) To provide clinical and admistrative data for use in management services medical education and medical research

Correct Answer: b) To serve as an inference in continuity of patient care

Medical records do not serve as an "inference" (a guess); they serve as the direct instrument or basis for continuity of care. All other options describe direct functions (communication, evidence, data source).

15.

b) Registers of births
c) Registers of deaths

6.

a) 5 advantages of the secretarial pool system (5 Marks)

b) State disadvantages of the pool system (2 Marks)

Show Answer

Answer:

a) Advantages:

  1. Ensures coverage during staff absence (sickness/leave).
  2. Standardizes work quality and format.
  3. Easier supervision and training of staff.
  4. More even distribution of workload.
  5. Economical use of equipment (computers/printers).

b) Disadvantages:

  2. Loss of personal touch/relationship between secretary and doctor.
  3. Potential for delays if priorities are not managed well.

Section 3: Long Answer Question

20 Marks
1.
As a recently qualified health records and information officer from the best medical training college, explain to the interview panel the procedures of establishing a new Health Records Department from the existing one.
Show Answer

Answer:

Establishing a new department (or restructuring an existing one) involves a systematic approach:

  1. Situation Analysis (Assessment): Evaluate the current state. Identify gaps, volume of work, patient flow, and deficiencies in the existing system.
  2. Planning: Define objectives, scope, and goals. Determine the type of system (centralized/decentralized) and filing method to be used.
  3. Budgeting: Estimate costs for space renovation, equipment (shelves, computers), stationery, and staffing.
  4. Designing/Layout: Create a floor plan that ensures efficient workflow (Reception -> Registration -> Filing -> Coding -> Doctors). Ensure adequate space for staff and storage.
  5. Procurement: Acquire the necessary equipment, furniture, and supplies according to specifications.
  6. Staffing: Determine the number and mix of staff required. Recruit, train, and deploy staff. Define job descriptions.
  7. Policy Formulation: Develop Standard Operating Procedures (SOPs) for registration, numbering, filing, retention, and confidentiality.
  8. Implementation: Roll out the new system. This may be done in phases (e.g., starting with new admissions) to minimize disruption.
  9. Monitoring and Evaluation: continuously assess the performance of the new department against set targets and make adjustments as necessary.
