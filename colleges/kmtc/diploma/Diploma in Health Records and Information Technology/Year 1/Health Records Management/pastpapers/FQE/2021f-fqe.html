Section 1: Multiple Choice Questions

40 Marks
1.
The largest elevator file for filing patient master index cards would probably hold... index cards.
A) 900,000
B) 9,000
C) 90,000
D) 190,000
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C) 90,000

Elevator files (automated rotary files) are designed for high-density storage. While capacities vary by manufacturer, standard large units for $5 \times 3$ inch cards typically hold around 60,000 to 100,000 cards. 90,000 is a standard figure often cited in health records management literature for large units.

2.
Which one of the following is not a type of appointment system?
A) Centralized appointment
B) Decentralized appointment
C) Combined appointment
D) Returned appointment
Show Answer

Correct Answer: D) Returned appointment

The standard systems of managing appointments are:

  • Centralized: One office books for all clinics.
  • Decentralized: Each clinic books its own.
  • Combined: A mix of both (e.g., centralized for new patients, decentralized for follow-ups).

"Returned appointment" refers to a type of visit (follow-up), not a management system.

3.
Which one of the following is an equipment for recording appointment?
A) Visible edge sheet
B) Shelf filing
C) Circular carousel
D) Mobile racking
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A) Visible edge sheet

Visible Edge Sheets (e.g., Kalamazoo system): These are commonly used for manual appointment scheduling as they allow clerks to see available slots quickly. Shelf filing, circular carousels, and mobile racking are equipment for filing/storage of records, not primarily for recording appointment dates.

4.
Which of the following information is not included on the DBR?
A) Patient's parole
B) Patient's admission
C) Patient's appointment
D) Patients who have died
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C) Patient's appointment

DBR (Daily Bed Return / Ward Census): Tracks inpatient movement to calculate occupancy. It includes:

  • Admissions
  • Discharges (including deaths)
  • Transfers
  • Patients on Leave/Parole

Appointments relate to future outpatient visits and are not part of the daily inpatient census count.

5.
The following are ways of conveying information to the waiting list. Which one is not?
A) Cards
B) Telephone calls
C) List
D) Letters
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A) Cards

This implies methods of *communication* to add someone to the list. Doctors send lists, departments send letters, or calls are made. Creating a "Card" is usually the result of the process (the index card itself), not the method of conveying the initial request/information *to* the list manager.

6.
Which of the following departments need to be notified of patients discharged from the ward?
A) Reception
B) The chaplain
C) The ward
D) Accident and emergency department
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A) Reception

The Reception/Admission office (often controlling the Bed Bureau) must be notified of discharges immediately so they know that a bed has become free for a new admission.

7.
The following are problems faced by hospitals in the proper maintenance of records except
A) Inactive records need storage at appointed place
B) Always trained personnel are needed for maintenance
C) Constant revision of outdated form is maintained
D) No need of determining retention period
Show Answer

Correct Answer: D) No need of determining retention period

This is the exception because determining a retention period is a necessity (and often a challenge), not something that there is "no need" for. The statement "No need of determining retention period" is incorrect; if hospitals didn't determine this, they would face massive storage problems. Therefore, the *lack* of this need is not a problem; the *need itself* is the challenge.

8.
The disclosure of health information for a person with covid-19 by the Ministry of Health is not regarded as a breach of confidentiality because
A) It is an express consent
B) It is a court order
C) It is a higher duty
D) It is a subpoena
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C) It is a higher duty

Higher Duty (Public Interest): Confidentiality can be breached when there is a risk of harm to the public. In the case of infectious diseases like COVID-19, the duty to protect the public health supersedes the individual's right to privacy.

9.
Which of the following is not a source of admission in the hospital?
A) Inpatient waiting list
B) Hospital bed bureau
C) Accident and emergency department
D) Other hospital
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B) Hospital bed bureau

The Bed Bureau is the administrative unit that manages/allocates the beds. It is not the *source* of the patient.
- Patients come from A&E.
- Patients come from Waiting Lists.
- Patients come from Other Hospitals (Transfers).
The Bed Bureau processes them, but the patient doesn't originate "from" the bureau itself.

10.
The International Federation of Health Records Organization (IFHRO) now IFHIMA was formed in the year ____ in Sweden.
A) 1958
B) 2010
C) 1752
D) 1968
Show Answer

Correct Answer: D) 1968

IFHRO (now IFHIMA - International Federation of Health Information Management Associations) was established in 1968.

11.
The famous terminal digit filing sequence generally works with ____ Digit number
A) Eight
B) Four
C) Six
D) Three
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C) Six

Terminal Digit Filing (TDF) typically uses a 6-digit number broken into three pairs:
Example: 12-34-56
- 56: Primary digits (Terminal)
- 34: Secondary digits
- 12: Tertiary digits

12.
One linear shelf of medical records filing can accommodate about ____ pounds of medical files.
A) 40
B) 50
C) 75
D) 100
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C) 75

Paper is dense and heavy. A standard 36-inch (3ft) shelf full of medical records can weigh roughly 75 to 80 pounds. This is why heavy-duty shelving is required for medical records libraries.

13.
Which of the following is not a basic principle of form design?
A) Title
B) Standard format
C) Economical
D) Standard size
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C) Economical

While forms should be economical to print, "Economical" is usually a result, not a structural design principle in the same way Title, Format (layout), and Standard Size (A4, A5) are. However, standard texts list "Simplicity," "Standardization," and "Instruction" as key principles. "Title" and "Standard Size" are specific attributes. In this context, ensuring a form is "Economical" is often considered a procurement/management goal rather than a design layout principle.

14.
Procedures for disposal of dead bodies is spelled out in which Act?
A) Public records
B) Public health act
C) Government secret act
D) Civic registration act
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B) Public health act

The Public Health Act (Cap 242) contains regulations regarding the handling of corpses, mortuaries, and burial/cremation to prevent the spread of disease and ensure sanitation.

15.
The following are types of special health records except
A) Tuberculosis records
B) Psychiatric records
C) Gender based evidence records
D) Malaria records
Show Answer

Correct Answer: D) Malaria records

Special Health Records typically refer to those requiring extra legal protection or specific handling due to sensitivity or stigma.
- TB, Psychiatric, and SGBV (Sexual/Gender Based Violence) records are highly sensitive.
- Malaria records are generally treated as standard general medical records without the "special" legal classification of the others.

16.
The procedure for completion of P3 forms, post-mortem forms are attained in which act?
A) Public records act
B) Factory and workshop act
C) Civil registration act
D) Criminal procedure code
Show Answer

Correct Answer: D) Criminal procedure code

P3 Forms (Medical Examination Report): These are legal evidentiary documents used in police cases (assault, rape, etc.). Their completion and use are governed by the Criminal Procedure Code (and Evidence Act) as they form part of criminal investigations.

17.
Which one of the following is not a disposal method of government assets?
A) Giving out as a gift
B) Transfer by public tender
C) Sale by public auction
D) Destruction
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A) Giving out as a gift

Government assets (public property) cannot simply be given away as gifts. Approved disposal methods under Public Procurement and Asset Disposal Act include Transfer to other public entities, Public Tender, Public Auction, Destruction/Dumping, or Trade-in.

18.
In the stores management cycle, implementation procedures involve the following except?
A) Issuing
B) Authorization
C) Receiving
D) Reporting by asset holders
Show Answer

Correct Answer: D) Reporting by asset holders

Implementation of stores procedures involves the active movement of goods: Receiving goods, Authorizing their use, and Issuing them. "Reporting" is usually a monitoring/control function rather than the implementation (action) phase of the cycle.

19.
Which one of the following is a similarity of the library trace system and personal trace system?
A) Both are created at the same time with the file
B) Both have the same size
C) Both are filed in a small tracer card index
D) Both have small pocket inside where to file
Show Answer

Correct Answer: D) Both have small pocket inside where to file

Often, tracer cards (especially personal ones in a library system) are designed to hold a small requisition slip or movement slip. The defining feature of a "Library Tracer" is usually its pocket. If the Personal Tracer is designed similarly, the pocket is the structural similarity.

20.
Which one of the following correctly match the principles of terminal digit filing?
A) The library and the hospital number
B) The major sections and sub-sections
C) Primary digit and middle digit
D) Primary digit and terminal digit
Show Answer

Correct Answer: D) Primary digit and terminal digit

Terminal Digit Filing relies on breaking the number into parts: the Terminal digits (Primary sorting key), Secondary digits, and Tertiary digits. The system is defined by these digit groups.

21.
The following are types of health records except
A) Outpatient records
B) Personnel records
C) Case records
D) Diagnostic records
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B) Personnel records

Personnel records (Staff HR records) are administrative records relating to employees, not clinical health records relating to patient care.

22.
The filing of patient master index should strictly be filed
A) Numerically
B) Alphabetically
C) Chronologically
D) Terminally
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B) Alphabetically

The Master Patient Index (MPI) is the key that links a patient's Name (known) to their Medical Record Number (unknown). Therefore, it must be filed Alphabetically by patient name to be searchable.

23.
The following are information to be recorded on the disease index card except
A) Age
B) Outcome
C) Chronologically
D) Terminally
Show Answer

Correct Answer: D) Terminally

A Disease Index card typically lists patients who had a specific disease. Columns include Age, Sex, Result/Outcome, and Date (Chronological order). "Terminally" is a filing term, not a data field or sorting order usually found on the card itself (unlike chronological listing of cases).

24.
A type of appointment system where appointment of new cases is done in one office and the re-attendance appointment done in various consultant clinics is called
A) Centralized appointment
B) Decentralized appointment
C) Combined appointment
D) Centralized and decentralized appointment
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C) Combined appointment

Combined System: New patients are booked centrally (to manage intake), while follow-ups (re-attendance) are booked by the specific clinic/consultant (decentralized).

25.
Which of the following hospital departments should not be notified of patients admitted to the hospital?
A) Telephone switch board
B) Reception
C) The bed bureau
D) Accident and emergency department
Show Answer

Correct Answer: D) Accident and emergency department

A&E is a source of admission. Once a patient is admitted to the ward, A&E's job with that patient is done.
- Switchboard needs to know (for inquiries).
- Reception/Bed Bureau needs to know (for census).
- A&E does not need a notification of admissions coming from other sources (like clinics).

26.
The fundamental requirement of filing system that ensures speedy location and positive means of identification of documents in the system is called?
A) Elasticity
B) Economic
C) Accessibility
D) Simplicity
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C) Accessibility

Accessibility: The ability to locate and retrieve a record quickly and correctly. This is the primary functional requirement of any filing system.

27.
The first records procedure at the accident and emergency records department is?
A) Filing
B) Maintaining of the registers
C) Disposal of patients
D) Appointment
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B) Maintaining of the registers

When a patient arrives at A&E, they are immediately entered into the A&E Register (Casualty Register). This is the primary legal record of their attendance, done before treatment or file creation.

28.
Which of the following is not the main filing equipment used to file case records and x-rays film?
A) Shelf filing
B) Filing cabinet
C) Suspended filing
D) Filing trolley
Show Answer

Correct Answer: D) Filing trolley

A Filing Trolley is auxiliary transport equipment used to move files around. It is not used for permanent storage (filing) of records like shelves or cabinets.

29.
The documentation of the patient's social data using the general practitioner's referral letter is called
A) Recording
B) Registration
C) Coding
D) Pre-registration
Show Answer

Correct Answer: D) Pre-registration

Pre-registration: Collecting patient demographic details (social data) before the patient actually arrives at the reception desk, often using information sent ahead in referral letters.

30.
The section of a health records and information department which is responsible for arranging of emergency admissions is called
A) Bed board
B) Bed bureau
C) Bed stand
D) Accident and emergency department
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B) Bed bureau

The Bed Bureau (Admission Office) is responsible for bed management and coordinating admissions, including emergency ones (finding them a bed).

31.
The following are policies and emergency department for use in an electronic health records system except
A) Information policy
B) Decision policy
C) Work flow policy
D) Downtime policy
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B) Decision policy

Standard EHR implementation policies include:
- Information Policy: What data is captured.
- Workflow Policy: How processes change.
- Downtime Policy: What to do when the system is offline.
"Decision Policy" is vague and usually covered by Clinical Decision Support (CDS) logic rather than a standalone administrative policy category in this context.

32.
____ and ____ are the filing methods of a waiting list
A) Alphabetical index and consultant list
B) Sort-file and pigeon holes
C) Numerical and chronological filing
D) Letters and list
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A) Alphabetical index and consultant list

Waiting lists are typically maintained in two ways to ensure control:
1. Alphabetical Index (Patient Card): To check if a specific patient is on the list.
2. Consultant/Specialty List: To see who is waiting for which doctor (often chronological within the list).

33.
The first process when planning to dispose health records in a health facility is
A) Determine the retention period
B) Identify active and inactive files
C) Purging of files
D) Identify destruction methods
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A) Determine the retention period

Before you can identify which files to move (inactive) or destroy, you must first have established the Retention Schedule/Policy (how long records must be kept). This is the foundational rule for the entire disposal process.

34.
Which one of the following is not a factor to consider when establishing a health records and information department?
A) Location
B) Space for equipment
C) System communication
D) Minimum qualification of staff
Show Answer

Correct Answer: D) Minimum qualification of staff

Similar to Q11 in the 2023 paper. Location, Space, and Communication Systems are structural/physical planning factors for the *establishment of the department facility*. Staff qualifications are HR/Operational policies determined *after* or separate from the physical establishment.

35.
The following are the contents of a discharge register except
A) Name of the patient
B) Age
C) Diagnosis
D) Date of birth
Show Answer

Correct Answer: D) Date of birth

While DOB is useful, registers often use Age for quick statistical analysis (e.g., age groups). Since "Age" is listed as option B, "Date of Birth" becomes the redundant one often omitted in compact registers which prioritize Name, Diagnosis, and Discharge Date.

Which one of the following is a concept of scheduling?
A) Overloading of the clinic should be avoided
B) To reduce patient's waiting time
C) Provide special clinic arrangement
D) Allow hospital to prepare in advance
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A) Overloading of the clinic should be avoided

"Overloading should be avoided" is a Concept/Principle.
"Reduce waiting time" and "Prepare in advance" are Purposes/Objectives.

The Appointment Card is given to the patient to keep. It does not stay in the Unit Health Record folder. The folder contains the clinical documentation (Treatment, Anaesthetic, Surgery sheets).

Which of the following is not an ancillary equipment tin a medical record library?

39.

Section 2: Short Answer Questions

40 Marks
1.
Outline at least five (5) major contents of a daily ward statements (5 marks)
Show Answer

Answer:

The Daily Ward Statement (Daily Bed Return) typically contains:

  1. Bed State at Previous Midnight: Number of patients in the ward at the start of the day.
  2. Admissions: Number of new patients admitted during the last 24 hours.
  3. Discharges: Number of patients discharged home.
  4. Deaths: Number of patients who died.
  5. Transfers In/Out: Number of patients moved to/from other wards.
  6. Current Bed State (Midnight Census): Calculated total of patients remaining.
  7. Beds Available: Number of vacant beds.
2.
State five (5) advantages of a centralized waiting list (5 marks)
Show Answer

Answer:

  1. Fairness/Uniformity: Patients are prioritized based on standardized criteria across the hospital.
  2. Better Bed Management: Central view of demand allows efficient allocation of beds across specialties (e.g., using surgical beds for medical overflow if needed).
  3. Cost Effective: Reduces duplication of staff and resources needed to manage multiple separate lists.
  4. Avoids Duplication: Prevents the same patient from being on multiple lists for the same condition.
  5. Standardized Communication: Consistent procedures for notifying patients of their admission dates.
3.
Outline five critical strategies for implementation of electronic health records (5 marks)
Show Answer

Answer:

  1. Stakeholder Engagement: Involve clinicians, nurses, and admin staff early in the selection and design process.
  2. Comprehensive Training: Extensive training programs for all users before go-live.
  3. Phased Implementation: Rolling out the system in stages (e.g., one department at a time) rather than a "big bang" approach to manage risks.
  4. Infrastructure Readiness: Ensuring reliable power, network connectivity, and hardware are in place.
  5. Data Migration Strategy: Planning how to move essential data from old paper records to the new system.
4.
State any five (5) legal aspects in medical records (5 marks)
Show Answer

Answer:

  1. Confidentiality: The duty to protect patient information from unauthorized disclosure.
  2. Consent: Requirement for patient permission before treatment or information sharing.
  3. Ownership: The physical record belongs to the facility; the information belongs to the patient.
  4. Retention: Legal requirements to keep records for a specific period (e.g., limitation period for negligence claims).
  5. Admissibility as Evidence: Records must be accurate and authentic to be used in court (Subpoena).
5.
Outline five factors leading to pre-registration of patients (5 marks)
Show Answer

Answer:

  1. Referral Letters: Receiving details from GPs allows setup before patient arrival.
  2. Scheduled Admissions: Patients on waiting lists are known in advance.
  3. Reduce Waiting Time: Speeds up the process on the actual day of attendance.
  4. Verify Insurance: Allows time to check insurance coverage/eligibility beforehand.
  5. Resource Planning: Helps the hospital anticipate volume and prepare files/resources.
6.
Explain how the following qualities assist heath records officers to perform daily activities in the hospital (5 marks)
Show Answer

Answer:

  • (i) Politeness: Helps in public relations, calming anxious patients, and creating a welcoming reception environment.
  • (ii) Discretion: Essential for maintaining confidentiality; knowing when NOT to speak about patient details in public areas.
  • (iii) Calmness: Vital in emergency situations (A&E) or when dealing with aggressive patients to maintain control and efficiency.
  • (iv) Neat appearance: Instills confidence in the patient regarding the professionalism and hygiene standards of the hospital.
  • (v) Integrity/Honesty: Crucial for accurate data entry (not falsifying records) and handling medico-legal issues truthfully.
7.
State five (5) reasons of maintaining a waiting list in a hospital (5 marks)
Show Answer

Answer:

  1. Demand Management: To handle the excess of patients requiring non-emergency care compared to available beds.
  2. Prioritization: To ensure patients with more urgent clinical needs are treated first (Triage).
  3. Resource Utilization: Ensures beds and operating theatres are fully utilized by having a queue of patients ready.
  4. Planning: Helps in scheduling staff and ordering supplies based on known future admissions.
  5. Fairness: Provides a systematic, "first-come-first-served" (adjusted for urgency) approach to access care.
8.
Outline procedures used for admitting patients from the waiting list (5 marks)
Show Answer

Answer:

  1. Selection: Select patients from the list based on clinical urgency and time waited.
  2. Notification: Send a letter or call the patient ("To Come In" notice) giving a specific date.
  3. Confirmation: Receive confirmation from the patient that they will attend.
  4. File Preparation: Retrieve and prepare the medical file in advance (Pre-registration).
  5. Arrival & Admission: On the day, verify details at the admission desk and send the patient to the ward.

Section 3: Long Answer Question

20 Marks
1.
Discuss the value of health records documentation to each of the following persons (20 marks)
Show Answer

Answer:

i. For Patients:

  • Continuity of Care: Ensures that future doctors know the patient's history, avoiding repetitive tests and conflicting treatments.
  • Legal Protection: Provides evidence in case of malpractice, insurance claims, or personal injury suits.
  • Health Awareness: Allows patients to understand their own health history and make informed decisions.

ii. For Hospital (Administration):

  • Planning & Management: Statistics derived from records (bed occupancy, disease prevalence) help in resource allocation and budgeting.
  • Legal Defense: The record is the hospital's primary defense in medico-legal cases to prove that standard of care was met.
  • Revenue/Billing: Accurate documentation supports insurance claims and funding justification.
  • Accreditation: Good records are a requirement for hospital licensing and quality accreditation.

iii. For Health Care Worker (Doctors/Nurses):

  • Communication Tool: The primary means of communication between the multidisciplinary team (e.g., nurse to doctor, doctor to specialist).
  • Decision Making: Provides the data (labs, history) needed to make accurate diagnoses and treatment plans.
  • Evaluation: Allows the provider to review the patient's response to treatment over time.
  • Protection: Documents that the provider followed proper protocols.

iv. For Researcher:

  • Data Source: Records provide the raw data for retrospective studies on disease patterns, treatment efficacy, and epidemiology.
  • Clinical Trials: Helps identify eligible candidates for new drug trials.
  • Public Health: Aggregated data helps track outbreaks and health trends.

v. For Teaching (Education):

  • Case Studies: Real patient records are used to teach medical students about disease progression and management.
  • Audit & Review: Junior doctors learn by reviewing past cases and participating in mortality audits based on records.
