Section 1: Multiple Choice Questions

40 Marks
1.
The following are aspect of data quality of birth and death except
a) Timeliness
b) Accuracy
c) Accessibility
d) Consistency
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Accessibility

While accessibility is important for data use, the inherent quality attributes of the data itself are typically Accuracy, Timeliness, Completeness, and Consistency.

2.
Which one of the following departments do not need notification of the patient's admission from the waiting list?
a) Chaplain
b) Social worker
c) Bed bureau
d) None of the above
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Bed bureau

The Bed Bureau is the one sending the notification (managing the admission). They already know. It is the Chaplains, Social Workers, and Wards who need to be notified.

3.
The following are data entry process except
a) Sorting
b) Editing
c) Feedback
d) Validation
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Feedback

Sorting, Editing, and Validation are steps in the data entry/processing cycle. Feedback is an output or control mechanism after the process.

4.
The state of not being emotional in an emergency situation is a state of being
a) Calm
b) Polite
c) Consistent
d) Honest
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Calm

Calmness is the professional quality of remaining composed and unemotional during emergencies.

5.
There are various health records indices used and maintained in a health records department. Which one is not?
a) Waiting list index
b) The diagnostic index
c) Operation index
d) Terminal digit index
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Terminal digit index

Terminal digit is a filing system (method of arrangement), not an index. Disease, Operation, and Waiting List indices are actual tools for retrieving specific data.

6.
Which one of the following is equipment for recording appointments?
a) Circular carousel
b) Visible edge sheets
c) Mobile racking
d) Shelf filing
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Visible edge sheets

Appointments are typically recorded in diaries or Visible Edge Sheets/Binders (Kalamazoo type). Carousels, mobile racking, and shelves are storage equipment for files.

7.
Clinic preparation entails the following except
a) Retrieval
b) Sorting
c) Editing
d) Tracing
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Editing

Clinic prep involves Sorting the list, Tracing the files, and Retrieval. Editing (checking for completeness/order) is typically done after the patient is discharged, before filing.

8.
The following factors are considered during purchase of case folder, except
a) Methods of fastening the documents
b) Manufacturing company
c) Cost of the folder
d) Strength of the manila
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Manufacturing company

The specifications (strength/durability, cost, fastening method) are critical. The specific Manufacturing company is less relevant as long as the specifications are met.

9.
The following system where the two numbers at the end are used is known as: -
a) Chronological filing system
b) Alphabetical filing system
c) Terminal digit filing system
d) Numerical filing system
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Terminal digit filing system

Terminal Digit Filing organizes files based on the last two digits (primary digits) of the health record number.

10.
The following are types of diagnostic records except: -
a) Radiology
b) Immunization
c) Electrocardiography
d) Pathology
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Immunization

Radiology, Pathology, and ECG are investigative/diagnostic reports. Immunization records are preventative health history records.

11.
The following is the information included in daily ward statement except
a) Patients who have died
b) Patients on appointment
c) Patient discharged
d) Patients transferred in
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Patients on appointment

The Daily Ward Statement (Census) tracks inpatient bed activity (Admissions, Discharges, Deaths, Transfers). Appointments relate to outpatient clinics, not the ward census.

12.
In the terminal digit filing system, the library is finally divided into sections
a) 10,000
b) 1,000
c) 100,000
d) 1,000,000
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) 10,000

The system uses 100 Primary sections (00-99). Each primary has 100 Secondary sections (00-99). Total sections = 100 x 100 = 10,000.

13.
In a unit medical records system, the patient is the
a) Unit
b) Surname
c) Terminal digit
d) Master index
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Unit

The concept of the Unit system is that the patient (the unit) has one number and one file that collects all data.

14.
Which one of the following is true about the documents in the patient folder on which coding of diseases and conditions are based?
a) Prescription chart
b) Clinical photographs
c) Case summary
d) Charge sheets
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Case summary

The Case Summary (Discharge Summary) contains the final diagnosis and procedures, which are the authoritative source for clinical coding.

15.
Which one of the following is the most appropriate method of sorting in a master index?
a) Sorting by disease type
b) Strip index
c) Vertical index method
d) Pigeon holes
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Pigeon holes

Before filing cards into the master index, they are typically sorted alphabetically using a sorting guide or Pigeon holes (sorter graph) to speed up the process.

16.
The following information is TRUE about a clinical use of medical records except?
a) Public health monitoring
b) Billing the patient
c) Future treatment
d) Direct patient care
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Billing the patient

Billing is an Administrative/Fiscal use, not a Clinical use. Direct care, future treatment, and monitoring are clinical.

17.
In 1948 the Health Records Association was started in ____
a) Britain
b) Egypt
c) USA
d) Greece
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Britain

The Association of Medical Records Officers (AMRO), now IHRIM, was founded in Britain (UK) in 1948.

18.
In the unit number register, the following will be included except
a) Full names of patients
b) Date of birth
c) Diagnosis
d) Registration date
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Diagnosis

The Unit Number Register (MPI) contains demographic data (Name, DOB, Date Reg, Number). Diagnosis is clinical data stored in the file or disease index.

19.
The following are contents of case folder related to clinical records, which one is not?
a) Discharge summary
b) Treatment sheet
c) Charge sheet
d) Diagnostic form
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Charge sheet

The Charge sheet is a financial/administrative document. Discharge summary, Treatment sheet, and Diagnostic forms are clinical records.

20.
One of the following is not the 3 common formats found in the organization of the paper based health records
a) Problem oriented
b) Chronologically filed integrated
c) Market oriented
d) Source oriented
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Market oriented

Standard formats are Source Oriented (SOMR), Problem Oriented (POMR), and Integrated/Chronological. "Market oriented" is not a record format.

21.
Which one of the following is not a section in the department of health records in a facility
a) Records library
b) Customer care
c) Bed bureau
d) Reception and Registration
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Customer care

While customer care is a function, it is typically a separate desk or general hospital function. Records Library, Bed Bureau, and Registration are specific technical sections of the Health Records Department.

22.
There are three common types of control system of records, except
a) Collection and reporting system
b) Forms control system
c) Directives control system
d) Reports control system
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Collection and reporting system

The standard administrative controls are: Forms Control, Reports Control, and Policy/Directives Control.

23.
The following are special health records in a facility except
a) Tuberculosis records
b) Maternity records
c) Community outreach services records
d) Cancer treatment records
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Community outreach services records

Maternity, TB, and Psychiatric/Cancer records are often legally defined as "Special Records" due to specific retention or confidentiality laws. Outreach records are often extensions of general outpatient notes.

24.
Which one of the following records should be retained in the health facility for a minimum of six years after the last attendance?
a) Accident and emergency records
b) Outbreak of diseases records
c) Clinical outpatient records
d) Maternity cases records
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Clinical outpatient records

General outpatient records typically have a retention period of 6 years (or 7 in some jurisdictions like Kenya under limitation of actions).

25.
The following are benefits of using filing cabinets as a method of filing system except
a) Dust proof
b) Provide attractive appearance
c) Capture large volume of records
d) Good access
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Capture large volume of records

Compared to shelf filing or mobile racking, filing cabinets actually hold a smaller volume of records per square foot of floor space. They are dust-proof and tidy, but not space-efficient.

26.
The most economical filing method as far as space is concerned is
a) Mobile ranking
b) Suspended filing
c) Self-filing
d) Circular, carousel filing
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Mobile ranking

Mobile Racking (Compactus) is the most space-efficient system.

27.
Which one of the following is not the basic principles of form design?
a) Uniformity in size and style
b) Clear instructions on form
c) Simplicity
d) Compability with the functions of a health records department
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Compability with the functions of a health records department

While compatibility is good, form design principles specifically focus on the form itself: Simplicity, Standardization (Uniformity), and Clarity (Instructions). "Compatibility with functions" is a broader systemic issue, not a design principle of the paper form itself.

28.
Which of the information is not included on the DBR
a) Patient transfer in
b) Patient on appointment
c) Patient who died
d) Patient on parole
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Patient on appointment

The DBR (Daily Bed Return) tracks inpatients. Appointments are outpatient events.

29.
All are true about ancillary equipment used in the records library except
a) Kick stool
b) Filing trolley
c) Filing cabinet
d) Sorting equipment
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Filing cabinet

Filing Cabinets are primary storage equipment. Kick stools, trolleys, and sorters are ancillary (supporting) equipment.

30.
Which one among the following provides a primary use of health records
a) Medico legal
b) Research
c) Administrative
d) Patient care
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Patient care

Patient Care is the primary use. All others are secondary.

31.
Which of the following is not a source of patient admissions?
a) From other hospital
b) Accident and emergency department
c) In-patient waiting
d) From home
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) From home

While patients come "from home", in hospital statistics, the source is usually classified as Waiting List, A&E, or Transfer. "From home" is not a formal admission source category in the same way (usually implies waiting list).

32.
Patients files are kept for ____ years in the accident and emergency department before being moved to the main filing library
a) 5 years
b) 2 years
c) 6 years
d) 10 years
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) 2 years

Active files might be kept locally for a short period (e.g., 2 years) for frequent re-attenders before archiving, although practices vary.

33.
Health records used in accident and emergency department should be kept in lock ____ requirement
a) Research
b) Legal
c) Future treatment
d) Insurance
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Legal

A&E records are frequently subject to police and court cases (assaults, accidents). Security/Lock and key is a strict Legal requirement to preserve chain of evidence.

34.
Which one of the following refers to the name given to the act of removing files that are not correctly in active use to give space for new ones in the library
a) Retrieval
b) Disposal
c) Weeding
d) Filing

Correct Answer: c) Weeding

Weeding (or culling) is the process of removing inactive files from active storage.

35.

Integrity is the professional quality defined as being honest, having strong moral principles, and being incorruptible.

40.
Which one of the following card can be expensive because each case folder will have its own
a) Common tracer
b) Library tracer
c) Personal tracer

The Personal Tracer card is dedicated to a specific file (one per file). Since every file needs one, it is more expensive than common tracers (which are reused for any file).

Section 2: Short Answer Questions

40 Marks
1.
State 5 categories under which contents of patients' records can be disclosed (5 marks)
Show Answer

Answer:

  1. With Patient Consent: Written authorization from the patient (e.g., for insurance, employers).
  2. Continuity of Care: Sharing with other healthcare providers involved in the patient's treatment.
  3. Statutory Requirement (Law): Reporting infectious diseases, births/deaths, or gunshot wounds as required by Acts of Parliament.
  4. Court Order (Subpoena): When ordered by a judge for legal proceedings.
  5. Public Interest: In rare cases where non-disclosure presents a serious risk to the public (e.g., serious crime or epidemic).
2.
Outline 5 purposes of scheduling and follow up (5 marks)
Show Answer

Answer:

  1. Efficient Resource Use: Balances provider workload and facility resources.
  2. Reduced Waiting Times: Prevents overcrowding and minimizes patient wait times.
  3. Preparation: Allows staff time to retrieve records and prepare equipment before patient arrival.
  4. Continuity of Care: Ensures patients are seen at appropriate intervals for their condition.
  5. Monitoring Non-Attendance: Identifies patients who miss appointments (defaulters) for necessary follow-up action.
3.
State the advantages of using terminal digit filing system (5 marks)
Show Answer

Answer:

  1. Even Distribution: New files are spread evenly throughout the 100 sections, preventing congestion at one end.
  2. Easier Sorting: Records can be presorted by terminal digits, speeding up filing.
  3. Faster Retrieval: Workers can be assigned to specific sections, increasing accountability and speed.
  4. Misfile Detection: Misfiles are easier to spot because adjacent files should have identical last two digits.
  5. Weeding Stability: Old files are removed evenly from all shelves, so no major shifting of remaining files is needed.
4.
List some of the information contained on the waiting list letter (5 marks)
Show Answer

Answer:

  1. Patient Details: Name, Address, Age, Hospital Number.
  2. Admission Details: Date and Time of admission.
  3. Location: Ward or Unit to report to.
  4. Instructions: What to bring (toiletries, NHIF card), fasting instructions if applicable.
  5. Contact Info: Hospital phone number to call if unable to attend.
5.
Briefly state the procedure involved in filing a patient master card (5 marks)
Show Answer

Answer:

  1. Checking: Verify the card is complete and accurate.
  2. Sorting: Arrange cards in alphabetical order (or phonetic order) using a sorter/guide.
  3. Locating: Open the appropriate drawer/tray in the index cabinet.
  4. Filing: Place the card in the correct position relative to existing cards (e.g., Strict Alphabetical: Surname -> First Name -> Middle Name).
  5. Verification: Quickly check adjacent cards to ensure sequence is maintained.
6.
Explain what is meant by "Medical Records Ethics" (5 marks)
Show Answer

Answer:

Medical Records Ethics refers to the moral principles and professional code of conduct that govern the management of health information. Key aspects include:

  • Confidentiality: Keeping patient information private.
  • Integrity: Ensuring data is not altered or falsified.
  • Access: Ensuring only authorized persons access records.
  • Professionalism: Serving the patient's interest above all else.
7.
Name 5 departments in a health facility and explain how the department are related and relevant to the records department (10 marks)
Show Answer

Answer:

  1. Administration: Relies on records dept for statistics (occupancy, morbidity) to plan budgets and resources.
  2. Nursing (Wards): Needs records for patient care history; Records dept needs daily ward returns (census) from nurses.
  3. Outpatient Clinics: Records dept provides files for clinics; Clinics generate new data for the files.
  4. Radiology/X-Ray: Reports generated here must be filed in the main record; X-ray films are often managed by records staff.
  5. Laboratory: Lab results are sent to records for filing; Records provide patient demographics for lab requests.
  6. Pharmacy: Verification of prescriptions may require checking the patient file/history.

Section 3: Long Answer Question

20 Marks
1.
Explain some of the problems associated with lack of proper records management in a health facility (20 marks)
Show Answer

Answer:

Lack of proper records management leads to serious consequences for the patient, hospital, and staff:

  1. Poor Patient Care: Without previous history, allergies, or test results, doctors may make wrong diagnoses or prescribe contraindicated medications (Medical Errors).
  2. Delay in Treatment: Time wasted searching for missing files delays critical care, potentially leading to worsening conditions or death.
  3. Legal Liability: The hospital cannot defend itself against malpractice suits without documentation proving standard of care was met.
  4. Financial Loss: inability to prove services were rendered leads to rejected insurance claims and loss of revenue. Duplication of tests also increases costs.
  5. Poor Planning: Without accurate statistics from records, administration cannot plan for staffing, equipment, or drug supplies effectively.
  6. Breach of Confidentiality: Poor security leads to unauthorized access to sensitive patient data, damaging the hospital's reputation.
  7. Research Hindrance: Retrospective studies are impossible if records are lost or incomplete, stalling medical advancement.
  8. Continuity of Care Failure: Patients seeing different doctors get fragmented care because the new doctor doesn't know what the previous one did.
