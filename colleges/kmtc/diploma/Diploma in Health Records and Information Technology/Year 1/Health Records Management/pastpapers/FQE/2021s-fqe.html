Section 1: Multiple Choice Questions

40 Marks
1.
The following are advantages of terminal digit filing except
a) No annual shift back of notes after weeding
b) Sorting of files is simple
c) All new files are added to one end
d) New and old notes are evenly distributed
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) All new files are added to one end

In terminal digit filing, new files are distributed evenly throughout the system (based on the last two digits) rather than bunching at the end. Adding all files to one end is characteristic of consecutive/straight numeric filing.

2.
Which one of the following is not a filing equipment for case notes
a) Shelf filing
b) Filing cabinets
c) Mobile raking
d) Cards
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Cards

Cards are the media/content (e.g., index cards), not the equipment used to store bulky case notes. Shelf filing, cabinets, and mobile racking are equipment.

3.
The following are the contents of a common tracer card except
a) Hospital number
b) Destination
c) Borrowers signature
d) Date of birth
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Date of birth

Tracer cards focus on tracking the file's location. Essential fields are File Number, Destination, Date Removed, and Signature. Date of Birth is identifying information usually found on the file cover or index, but is less critical for a temporary tracking card compared to the file number.

4.
The following are procedures used in the acquisition of health records equipment except.
a) Specification
b) Requisition of quotation
c) Opening of quotation
d) Disposal of the quotation
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Disposal of the quotation

Procurement involves specifications, tendering (requisition/opening quotations), and evaluation. Disposal refers to getting rid of assets at the end of their life cycle, not a step in acquiring them.

5.
____ is a document used for receiving goods from the supplies department before storage.
a) S11
b) S12
c) S13
d) S21
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) S13

In the government supplies system, S13 is the Counter Receipt Voucher (used when receiving goods), whereas S11 is for issuing/requisitioning.

6.
When formulating the retention policy the hospital must consider the following except.
a) The readmission rate of inpatient
b) Cost involved in finding inactive filing space
c) Patients full names and date of birth
d) The volume of medical research undertaken
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Patients full names and date of birth

Retention policies are based on aggregate factors like readmission rates, space costs, legal requirements, and research needs. Specific patient demographics (names/DOB) do not dictate the general policy.

7.
The following are factors hindering the implementation of electronic health records except.
a) Financial factors
b) Technical matters
c) Incompatibility between systems
d) Insurance information
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Insurance information

Cost, technical challenges, and interoperability (incompatibility) are major barriers. Insurance information is a type of data stored within the system, not a hindrance to its implementation.

8.
____ is a systematic process of checking to see whether a product or service being developed is meeting specified requirements.
a) Quality assurance
b) Quality control
c) Quality improvement
d) Quality assessment
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Quality control

Quality Control (QC) focuses on the product/service output (inspection/checking). Quality Assurance (QA) focuses on the process to prevent defects.

9.
The act that provides the ability to access information held by public authorities but recognize the need of protection to personal data is called
a) Safety radiation act
b) Civil registration act
c) Public records act
d) Government official secret act
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Public records act

The Public Records Act (or Access to Information Act in newer contexts) generally governs the management and public access to government records while balancing privacy needs.

10.
"No burial or release of body without a burial permit" the statement is outlined in which medical records act
a) Public records act
b) Public health act
c) Safety radiation act
d) Government official secret act
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Public health act

The Public Health Act (and Registration of Births and Deaths Act) mandates that a burial permit must be obtained before disposing of a body to ensure health safety and legal registration.

11.
____ is the protection of information system or medical records against unauthorized disclosure, alteration or destruction.
a) Security
b) Disclosure
c) Ownership
d) Retention
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Security

Security refers to the physical and technical safeguards used to protect data integrity and confidentiality.

12.
When the patient is seen subsequently by another doctor, strictly the doctor has no ____ of access to the notes made by the previous doctor
a) Legal rights
b) Moral rights
c) Ethical obligation
d) Common sense aspects
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Legal rights

Generally, for continuity of care within a team, access is permitted. However, in strict legal terms regarding ownership of intellectual property, specific consultation notes belong to the author/hospital. In practice, this question often refers to the principle that access is based on "need to know" rather than an absolute legal right of one doctor to see another's private notes without context, though in a hospital unit system, sharing is standard.

13.
____ is a process through which the level of desired quality is defined, pursued, achieved and maintained in a hospital.
a) Quality control
b) Quality assurance
c) Quality audit
d) Quality activity
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Quality assurance

Quality Assurance (QA) is the comprehensive process of defining standards and ensuring they are met throughout the system.

14.
Who among the following officers issues births and deaths certificates
a) Medical officer of health
b) Area chief
c) Registrar of persons
d) Health records officer
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Registrar of persons

The Department of Civil Registration (Registrar of Persons/Births & Deaths) is the legal authority that issues the actual certificates. Health Records Officers issue the notification (D1/B1 forms), not the certificate itself.

15.
In a hospital, an appointment can be made in various areas including the main reception. This type of appointment is called ____
a) Chronological
b) Centralized appointment
c) Decentralized appointment
d) Combined appointment
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Combined appointment

A Combined system uses elements of both. If appointments are made in various areas (decentralized) AND the main reception (centralized elements), it is often a combined approach, though "Decentralized" usually means appointments happen at individual clinics. "Centralized" means ALL happen at one place.

16.
The location of the admission office should ideally be;
a) Near the nursing department
b) Near the main entrance to the hospital and ward
c) Near the radiology department
d) Close to the administration block
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Near the main entrance to the hospital and ward

For accessibility and efficiency, the admission office should be easily reachable from the main entrance for arriving patients and close to wards for transferring them.

17.
The following are benefits of computerized medical records except.
a) Minimizes use of papers
b) Improves patient care
c) Less space is required
d) Decrease efficiency
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Decrease efficiency

Computerized records are designed to increase efficiency through faster retrieval and concurrent access. Decreasing efficiency is a disadvantage (if the system is poor), not a benefit.

18.
The membership of the health records committee includes the following cadres except
a) Medical records officer
b) Representative of nursing department
c) Doctors representative
d) Patients representative
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Patients representative

While modern governance encourages patient involvement, the traditional Health Records Committee is a technical internal committee comprising MROs, Doctors, Nurses, and Administrators. Patient representatives are not typically standard members.

19.
Admission of psychiatric patients may be formal or informal. This is prescribed in which act.
a) Public health act
b) Mental health act
c) Government secret act
d) Public records act
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Mental health act

The Mental Health Act governs the legal procedures for voluntary (informal) and involuntary (formal) admission of psychiatric patients.

20.
................................ is a method of destroying computerized data.
a) Demagnetizing
b) Magnetic degaussing
c) Shredding
d) Burning
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Magnetic degaussing

Degaussing destroys data on magnetic storage media (like hard drives) by scrambling the magnetic domains.

21.
The following are statutory obligations in disclosing the patients information when the higher duty supersedes the private duty except
a) Notification of cause of death under Births and Deaths registration
b) Notification of industrial poisoning under the factory and workshop act
c) Notification on claims of sickness benefit under the national insurance act
d) Notification of infectious disease
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Notification on claims of sickness benefit under the national insurance act

Notifications for public safety (Infectious diseases, industrial poisoning) and vital statistics (Births/Deaths) are mandatory "Higher Duty" disclosures. Sickness benefit claims usually require the patient's consent (private duty) rather than being a mandatory statutory override for public safety.

22.
One of the following contents of medical records is used to carry out diagnostic coding?
a) Nursing cardex
b) Lab request form

d) Case summary

The Case Summary (Discharge Summary) contains the final diagnosis and principal procedures, which are the primary sources for clinical coding.

25.
The following are important medical records policies except one
a) Patients access to their medical records
b) The retention of medical records
c) The destruction of inactive medical records
d) Notification of infectious disease
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Notification of infectious disease

Access, Retention, and Destruction are internal policies managed by the department. Notification of infectious diseases is a statutory law/act (Public Health Act), not just a departmental policy.

26.

The Day Book (Register) is an administrative/statistical record of attendance, whereas cards and envelopes are the clinical case notes used to document treatment.

Information typically comes via individual referral Letters, Cards (Admission cards), or Telephone. A "List" is usually the output (the waiting list itself) rather than the method of conveying a single patient's addition request.

32.
A library in terminal digit filing is divided into how many sub-sections.
a) 100 sub-section
b) 1000 sub-section
c) 10,000 sub-section
d) 10 sub-section
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) 10,000 sub-section

Terminal digit filing uses 3 sets of 2 digits (Primary, Secondary, Tertiary). The combination allows for division into 100 Primary sections, each with 100 Secondary sections = 10,000 divisions.

Section 2: Short Answer Questions

40 Marks
1.
Outline five advantages of a centralized waiting list. (5 marks)
Show Answer

Answer:

  1. Fairness: Admissions are based on clinical priority and date, not favoritism.
  2. Efficiency: Available beds are filled immediately from a central pool, maximizing occupancy.
  3. Standardization: Procedures for adding/removing patients are consistent.
  4. Better Statistics: Provides accurate data on total wait times and demand.
  5. Reduced Duplication: Prevents the same patient from being on multiple lists for the same condition.
2.
State five factors to be considered when establishing a health records department. (5 marks)
Show Answer

Answer:

  1. Location: Accessibility to patients and medical staff (central vs. decentralized).
  2. Space: Sufficient area for filing, staff working, and future expansion.
  3. Workflow: Logical flow of patients and records (Reception -> Filing -> Doctors).
  4. Equipment: Type of filing systems (mobile vs. static) and furniture needed.
  5. Staffing: Number and qualification of personnel required.
3.
Outline five responsibilities of the medical records committee. (5 marks)
Show Answer

Answer:

  1. Policy Formulation: Developing rules for record content, retention, and release.
  2. Forms Control: Approving the design and introduction of new medical forms.
  3. Quality Assurance: Reviewing records for completeness and clinical accuracy.
  4. Standardization: Ensuring compliance with accreditation and legal standards.
  5. Disciplinary Action: Recommending actions for breach of confidentiality or poor documentation.
4.
Briefly explain any five capabilities of an Electronic Health Records. (5 marks)
Show Answer

Answer:

  1. Interoperability: Ability to exchange data with other systems (labs, pharmacy).
  2. Decision Support: Providing alerts for drug interactions or clinical guidelines.
  3. Concurrent Access: Multiple users can view the record simultaneously.
  4. Audit Trails: Tracking who accessed or modified the record and when.
  5. Data Aggregation: Generating automated reports for research and public health.
5.
State five functions of a bed bureau. (5 marks)
Show Answer

Answer:

  1. Maintains an up-to-date record of bed states (occupancy).
  2. Allocates beds for emergency admissions.
  3. Coordinates elective admissions from the waiting list.
  4. Acts as a liaison between wards and admission sources.
  5. Compiles daily statistical returns (DBR) regarding admissions and discharges.
6.
State five importance of telemedicine. (5 marks)
Show Answer

Answer:

  1. Accessibility: Brings specialist care to remote or underserved areas.
  2. Cost-Effective: Reduces travel costs for patients and providers.
  3. Speed: Provides quicker diagnosis and consultation in emergencies.
  4. Education: Facilitates remote training and mentorship for health workers.
  5. Convenience: Reduces the need for hospital visits for minor follow-ups.
7.
Outline five advantages of return appointment made in the same clinic. (5 marks)
Show Answer

Answer:

  1. Continuity: The patient is likely to see the same consultant/team.
  2. Convenience: The patient knows the date immediately before leaving.
  3. Accuracy: The doctor can specify the exact timing needed for follow-up.
  4. Reduced Admin: Eliminates the need for mailing appointment letters later.
  5. Compliance: Patients are more likely to attend if they agree to the date in person.
8.
List five advantages of using a unit number for filing. (5 marks)
Show Answer

Answer:

  1. Continuity: All patient records are in one folder under one number.
  2. Efficiency: Staff only need to look in one place for the patient's history.
  3. Cost-Effective: Saves space by avoiding duplicate folders for the same patient.
  4. Simplicity: Only one number needs to be maintained in the Master Index.
  5. Research: Provides a complete longitudinal health picture for studies.

Section 3: Long Answer Question

20 Marks
1.

(a) Outline the procedures in ensuring quality assurance. (10 marks)

(b) Explain five (5) dimensions of quality considered in filing of case records. (10 marks)

Show Answer

Answer:

(a) Procedures in Ensuring Quality Assurance:

  1. Setting Standards: Define acceptable levels of performance (e.g., 95% filing accuracy, 100% complete discharge summaries).
  2. Data Collection: Gather data through routine monitoring, audits, and feedback mechanisms.
  3. Evaluation/Assessment: Compare the collected data against the set standards to identify gaps.
  4. Identification of Problems: Pinpoint specific areas of weakness (e.g., misfiles, incomplete forms).
  5. Corrective Action: Implement training, process changes, or resource allocation to fix the problems.
  6. Monitoring: Continuously check to ensure the corrective actions are effective.
  7. Documentation: Record all QA activities for accountability and future reference.
  8. Feedback: Communicate findings to staff to encourage improvement.

(b) Five Dimensions of Quality in Filing Case Records:

  1. Accuracy: Files must be placed in the exact correct order (numerical/terminal digit). A misfile effectively loses the record.
  2. Timeliness (Retrieval Speed): Records must be available immediately when needed for patient care. Delays compromise quality.
  3. Completeness: The file itself must contain all loose sheets and recent documents before being returned to the shelf.
  4. Security/Integrity: Files must be physically maintained (not torn) and secure from unauthorized access while in storage.
  5. Accessibility: The filing system must be organized so that authorized personnel can easily access it (e.g., not overcrowded, proper guides).
