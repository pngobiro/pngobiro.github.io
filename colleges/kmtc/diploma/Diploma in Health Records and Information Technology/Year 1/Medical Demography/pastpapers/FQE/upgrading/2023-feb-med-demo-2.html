Part 2: Short Answer Questions

40 Marks
1
What is a life table? (5 marks)
Show Answer

A life table is a tabular display of life expectancy and the probability of dying at each age (or age group) for a given population, based on the age-specific death rates prevailing at that time. It tracks a hypothetical cohort of 100,000 people from birth until all have died.

2
Explain how education level has helped in reducing maternal mortality in less developing countries (LDCs). (5 marks)
Show Answer
  • Educated women are more likely to delay marriage and childbirth.
  • They are more likely to seek prenatal and antenatal care.
  • They have better knowledge of nutrition and hygiene.
  • They are more empowered to make decisions regarding their reproductive health (contraceptive use).
  • They can better recognize danger signs during pregnancy.
3
List 5 uses of a life table. (5 marks)
Show Answer
  1. To calculate life expectancy at birth and other ages.
  2. To project future population size and structure.
  3. Used by insurance companies to determine premium rates (actuarial science).
  4. To compare mortality levels between different populations or time periods.
  5. To study the impact of specific causes of death (via cause-deleted life tables).
4
Outline 5 measures of fertility. (5 marks)
Show Answer
  1. Crude Birth Rate (CBR)
  2. General Fertility Rate (GFR)
  3. Age Specific Fertility Rate (ASFR)
  4. Total Fertility Rate (TFR)
  5. Gross Reproduction Rate (GRR)
  6. Net Reproduction Rate (NRR)
5
Identify disadvantages of indirect method of standardization. (5 marks)
Show Answer
  • The resulting standardized rates are not directly comparable with each other (only with the standard).
  • It assumes the age-specific rates of the standard population apply to the index population, which may not be true.
  • It hides differences in age-specific rates between populations.
  • It requires a reliable standard population which may not always be available or appropriate.
6
Briefly describe a life table as used in medical demography. (5 marks)
Show Answer

A life table acts as a history of a hypothetical cohort of people born at the same time and subject to the mortality rates of a specific period. It is used to analyze mortality, survival, and life expectancy, helping public health officials understand the longevity and health status of a population.

7
In the year 2021, Sebuleni District had 1050 deaths among the population aged 40-44 years. The total population in that age category was 236,472. Calculate the age specific death rate for that group during that year. (5 marks)
Show Answer

Formula: ASDR = (Deaths in age group / Population in age group) × 1000

Calculation: (1050 / 236,472) × 1000

Answer: 4.44 deaths per 1,000 population aged 40-44.

8

Give the meaning of the following abbreviations as used in life table (5 marks):

a) nmx   b) px   c) ex   d) tx   e) lx

Show Answer
  • nmx: Age-specific death rate (central death rate).
  • px: Probability of surviving from age x to x+n.
  • ex: Expectation of life at age x.
  • Tx: Total person-years lived after age x.
  • lx: Number of survivors at exact age x.

Under the 2010 Constitution of Kenya, legalizing of abortion is important in order to reduce the maternal deaths which results from unsafe abortion. As a student learning health records and information, explain the circumstances permitted by law. (20 Marks)

Show Answer

According to Article 26(4) of the Constitution of Kenya 2010, abortion is not permitted unless:
