Section 2: Short Answer Questions

40 Marks
1.
Defining all the symbols used, describe briefly the balancing equation which relates to the number of people in a population at two points in time. (5 marks)
Show Answer

Answer:

The demographic balancing equation is:

$$P_2 = P_1 + (B - D) + (I - E)$$

Where:

  • $P_2$ = Population at time 2 (end of period)
  • $P_1$ = Population at time 1 (start of period)
  • $B$ = Births during the period
  • $D$ = Deaths during the period
  • $I$ = Immigration (in-migration)
  • $E$ = Emigration (out-migration)
2.
What is population doubling time? Compute the doubling time for a population whose growth rate is 3%. (5 marks)
Show Answer

Answer:

Population doubling time is the time it takes for a population to double in size at a constant growth rate.

Formula: $T_d \approx \frac{70}{r}$ (Rule of 70)

Calculation:

$$T_d = \frac{70}{3} = 23.33 \text{ years}$$
