Section 1: Multiple Choice Questions (40 Marks)

40 Marks
1.
Formal demography deals with all of the following except
a) Density of the population
b) Size of the population
c) Growth of the population
d) Age structure
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Density of the population

Formal demography focuses on the study of population size, growth, and structure (including age distribution). Population density refers to the number of people per unit area and is more related to geographic distribution rather than formal demographic analysis.

2.
The following are examples of administrative records except
a) Data on school facilities
b) Data on personnel and harmful
c) Data on teachers and pupils
d) Individual social data facilities
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Data on personnel and harmful

Administrative records typically include educational data (schools, teachers, pupils) and facilities data. Option b appears to contain a typographical error ("personnel and harmful") and doesn't represent standard administrative record categories.

3.
These're most suitable reasons for shifting age in various populations except
a) To avoid state taxes
c) To implement policies
b) To gain recognition of special
d) To pay taxes as required by status legislation
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) To implement policies

Policy implementation is a legitimate administrative reason for age adjustments in demographic data. Options a, b, and d represent manipulative or incentive-driven reasons for age misreporting, which are common issues in demographic data collection.

4.
Which of these cohorts is commonly preferred when collecting age data
a) 5-0
b) 10-15
c) 5-1
d) 5-10
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) 5-10

The 5-10 age cohort (ages 5-9) is preferred because children in this age group are typically in school and more likely to accurately report their ages. Younger children (0-4) may have less accurate age reporting, and the other options are not standard cohort groupings.

5.
Sex ratio is much affected by the following factors except
a) The sex ratio at birth
b) Fertility rate
c) Difference in migration
d) Sex difference in mortality
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Fertility rate

Sex ratio is primarily affected by biological factors (sex ratio at birth), mortality differences between sexes, and migration patterns. Fertility rate affects population growth but doesn't directly influence the sex ratio of the existing population.

6.
The following are goals of population policy except
a) To reduce population growth
c) To reduce rural to urban and rate urban to rural migration
b) To reduce fertility level
d) To encourage people to marry
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) To encourage people to marry

Population policies typically focus on controlling population growth through fertility reduction and managing migration patterns. Encouraging marriage is not a standard goal of population policy; in fact, some policies might focus on family planning regardless of marital status.

7.
When calculating Whipple's index, the denominator considers ages between
a) 21 and 52 years
b) 23 and 62 years
c) 21 and 62 years
d) 23 and 52 years
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) 23 and 62 years

Whipple's index measures age heaping by comparing the number of persons reported in ages ending with 0 or 5 to those in adjacent ages. It uses ages 23-62 as the denominator because these ages are less likely to be affected by age misreporting.

8.
The sex ratio means one of the following in a given population
a) Males in relation to female in a given population
b) The proportion of females in the present population
c) The population of males that existed before in a population
d) Female in relation to males in a given population
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Males in relation to female in a given population

Sex ratio is conventionally expressed as the number of males per 100 females (or sometimes per 1,000 females) in a population. It measures the balance between males and females in a given population.

9.
Which of the following is true about census?
a) Census cover non-defined territory
b) Another name of census is surveys
c) There is common registration of characteristics of individuals
d) Enumeration is done with respect to a well-defined time and intervals
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) There is common registration of characteristics of individuals

A census involves the systematic collection of demographic and socioeconomic data from all individuals in a defined geographic area. The key characteristic is that it covers everyone (universal coverage) and collects data on multiple characteristics simultaneously.

10.
Which of the following is not an importance of demography?
a) Planning for employment and education level
b) Political and administrative purposes
c) Planning for food and security safety
d) Planning for distribution of medical equipment
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Planning for food and security safety

Demography is crucial for planning education, employment, political representation, and healthcare resource distribution. Food security planning is more related to agricultural economics and nutrition rather than core demographic analysis.

11.
To ensure demographic data is of good quality, we need to do the following
a) Prior planning
b) Have a good questionnaire
c) Have well trained and adequate personnel
d) All of the above
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) All of the above

High-quality demographic data requires comprehensive preparation including careful planning, well-designed data collection instruments, and properly trained enumerators. All these elements work together to ensure accurate and reliable demographic information.

12.
The following are errors experienced in demographic data except
a) Errors of coverage
b) Errors of contents
c) Sampling errors
d) Individual bias errors
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Sampling errors

Sampling errors occur in sample surveys, not in complete enumeration methods like censuses. Demographic data collection errors include coverage errors (missing or duplicate individuals), content errors (inaccurate reporting), and individual biases (deliberate misreporting).

13.
Which of the following is the ability and capacity of a woman to give birth?
a) Mortality
b) Natality
c) Fertility
d) Fecundity
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Fecundity

Fecundity refers to the biological ability to reproduce, while fertility measures actual childbearing performance. Mortality is death rate, and natality is birth rate.

14.
Which of the following is among the causes of population growth?
a) Net increase
b) Migration
c) Mortality
d) Morbidity
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Migration

Migration (both immigration and emigration) directly affects population size. Mortality and morbidity reduce population, while net increase is a result of population growth, not a cause.

15.
The following causes change in a population in a given geographical area except
a) People crossing the border into it
b) People born in it
c) People dying in it
d) People getting sick in it
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) People getting sick in it

Morbidity (illness) doesn't change population size directly - only births, deaths, and migration affect population numbers. Immigration, births, and deaths are the three components of population change.

16.
The following is the characteristic of crude birth rate except
a) It includes both sexes in the denominator
b) It is expressed per 1000 population
c) It is influenced by sex
d) It includes all ages in the denominator
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) It is influenced by sex

Crude birth rate uses total population (both sexes, all ages) as denominator and is expressed per 1,000 population. Sex composition affects the rate indirectly through population structure, but the rate itself is not sex-specific.

17.
Which of the following defines a population with young or old in terms of work?
a) Beneficiaries
b) Dependence
c) Recent
d) Lazy
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Dependence

Dependency ratio measures the proportion of dependent population (young children and elderly) relative to the working-age population. High dependency ratios indicate either young populations (high youth dependency) or aging populations (high elderly dependency).

18.
The main collector of demographic information in Kenya is
a) KDHS
b) UNICEF
c) UN
d) KNBS
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) KNBS

The Kenya National Bureau of Statistics (KNBS) is the official statistical agency responsible for conducting censuses and collecting demographic data in Kenya. KDHS is a survey program, while UNICEF and UN provide technical assistance.

19.
Demographic data is compiled quin quenelle
a) 15 years
b) 10 years
c) 5 years
d) 9 years
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) 10 years

Population censuses in most countries, including Kenya, are conducted every 10 years. This interval allows for significant population changes to be measured while maintaining data relevance and managing resource requirements.

20.
A demographer needs not consider future occurrences
a) Undecided
b) Untrue statement
c) True statement
d) None of the above
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Untrue statement

Demographers must consider future population trends and projections for effective planning. Population projections are essential for anticipating future needs in healthcare, education, housing, and other services.

21.
Given that a country had a population of 1,171,000 and there were 10,065 deaths, the Crude Death Rate (CDR) would be
a) 6.72 per one thousand population
b) 5.05 per one thousand population
c) 8.59 per one thousand population
d) 3.23 per one thousand population
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) 8.59 per one thousand population

CDR = (Deaths / Total Population) × 1000 = (10,065 / 1,171,000) × 1000 ≈ 8.59 per 1,000 population.

22.
The two types of life tables include the complete life table and the
a) Abbreviated
b) Abridged
c) Radix
d) Cohort
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Abridged

Life tables can be complete (showing mortality for each year of age) or abridged (showing mortality for age groups, typically 5-year intervals). Both are used in demographic analysis.

23.
Fertility can be studied as
c) Period and cohort
a) Period and sex
d) Period and age
b) Cohort and sex
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Period and cohort

Fertility can be analyzed from a period perspective (fertility rates for a specific time period) or cohort perspective (fertility experience of women born in the same time period). Age and sex are components used in fertility measures but not the primary dimensions of analysis.

24.
In a population data computation, subtracting the number of deaths from the number of births results into
a) Mortality rate
b) Natural increase
c) Fertility rate
d) Net migration
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Natural increase

Natural increase = Births - Deaths. This represents the population growth due to natural causes, excluding migration effects.

25.
In civil registration of births, all the following details are recommended to be collected on live births except
c) Place of occurrence
a) Type of past birth/delivery
d) Attendance of birth
b) Date of occurrence and
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Type of past birth/delivery

Civil registration of births focuses on current birth details: date, place, and birth attendant. Information about previous births/delivery types would be collected in health records but not standard civil registration.

26.
All the following are required when constructing a population pyramid except registration
a) Knowledge of X and Y working principles
b) Population data with age distribution
c) Total population distribution
d) Population data with sex distribution
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Knowledge of X and Y working principles

Population pyramids require age and sex distribution data. Knowledge of graphing principles is helpful but not essential - the core requirements are the demographic data itself.

27.
The following are fertility measures except
a) Age specific fertility rate
b) Total fertility rate
c) Crude fertility rate
d) General fertility rate
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Crude fertility rate

Standard fertility measures include age-specific fertility rate (fertility by age group), total fertility rate (lifetime fertility), and general fertility rate (fertility among women of reproductive age). "Crude fertility rate" is not a standard demographic term.

28.
A truncated population pyramid refers to
a) One cut at the open ended interval
b) One representing a developing country
c) One representing a developed country
d) One elongated and needle like at the peak
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) One cut at the open ended interval

A truncated pyramid has a cut-off appearance at the top (older ages) due to low life expectancy or incomplete age data. The other descriptions don't accurately define truncation.

29.
Which one of the following demographic statements is correct on population structure?
a) The number of persons in the population
b) The arrangement of the population in space at a given time
c) The distribution of the population among its sex and age grouping
d) The growth or decline of the total population or one of its structural units
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) The distribution of the population among its sex and age grouping

Population structure refers to the composition of a population by age and sex. Population size is population quantity, spatial arrangement is population distribution, and growth/decline is population dynamics.

30.
Which one of the following demographic statements is correct on population distribution
a) The number of persons in the population
b) The arrangement of the population in space at a given time
c) The distribution of the population among its sex and age grouping
d) The growth or decline of the total population or one of its structural units
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) The arrangement of the population in space at a given time

Population distribution refers to how people are spread across geographical space. Population size is quantity, structure is age/sex composition, and growth is dynamics.

31.
Vital events include all the below except
a) Enumeration records
b) Adoption records
c) Separation records
d) Legitimization records
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Enumeration records

Vital events include births, deaths, marriages, divorces, and legal changes in family status (adoption, separation, legitimization). Enumeration refers to census activities, not vital events.
