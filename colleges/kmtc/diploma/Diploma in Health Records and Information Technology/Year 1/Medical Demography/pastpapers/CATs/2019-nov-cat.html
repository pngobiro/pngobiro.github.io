Short Answer Questions

30 Marks Total
1

Define population as used in demography and briefly explain four main aspects involved in the study of human populations. (10 Marks)

Show Key Points

Definition: In demography, population refers to a collection of human beings living in a specific geographical area at a specific time.

Four Main Aspects:

  1. Size: The total number of individuals in the population.
  2. Structure/Composition: The makeup of the population in terms of age, sex, marital status, etc.
  3. Distribution: How the population is arranged spatially (density, rural vs urban).
  4. Change: How the population size or structure alters over time due to fertility, mortality, and migration.

Show Answers

  • Sample Surveys (e.g., KDHS)
  • Administrative Records (Health, School, Church registers)
  • International Publications (UN Demographic Yearbooks)
3

There are two main different schemes for enumerating a population census. State them giving two advantages of each. (10 Marks)

Show Answers

1. De Facto (Enumerated where found)

  • Advantage: Simple to conduct (no ambiguity about "usual residence").
  • Advantage: Reduces chance of double counting if done on a single night.

2. De Jure (Enumerated at usual residence)

  • Advantage: Gives a more accurate picture of permanent population distribution for planning.
  • Advantage: Vital statistics (births/deaths) are usually recorded by residence, making rates (like birth rates) more comparable.
4

If the growth rate of Kenya is assumed to be 5.5%, when are we expecting the population to be twice as it is now? (5 Marks)

Show Calculation

Concept: Doubling Time.

Formula: 70 / Growth Rate (%) (Rule of 70)

Calculation: 70 / 5.5 = 12.72 years

(Alternatively using Rule of 69: 69 / 5.5 = 12.5 years)
