UNIT 8: VERBAL AUTOPSY(VA)

Overview of community cause of death

  • Assistant chiefs just do lay reporting of death and are not competent in establishing the correct cause of death
  • From the prescribed causes of death on Form D2, the assistant chiefs tick one of the causes of death among many provided on the form
  • The symptoms and modes of dying such as headache, vomiting given on form D2 are not considered as causes of death for ICD 11

Definitions and terms

Verbal autopsy - a method used to ascertain the cause of a death based on an interview with next of kin or other caregivers

It uses a standardized questionnaire that elicits information on signs, symptoms, medical history and circumstances preceding death.

Importance

  1. VA is a primary source of information about causes of death in populations lacking a robust vital registration and medical certification system e.g. Kenya
  2. Ideal in settings where deaths occur at home and therefore not medically certified by a clinician
  3. It is an essential public health tool for
  4. obtaining a reasonable direct estimation of the cause structure of mortality at a community level,
  5. Providing an accurate method for attributing causes of death at the individual level.
  6. Standardized tools used during VA allow for consistency & cross-comparability of mortality data
  7. Allows for assignment of a group level for causes of death corresponding to ICD-11 codes

ℹ️ Note

NB: Of all events that are registered in the country, 60% of the births and 70% of the deaths occur at home, which are registered by assistant chiefs

Purpose

The purpose of VA is to describe the causes of death at the community level in situations where no better alternative sources of mortality data exist.

VA serves as a limited but essential substitute for medical certification.
The quality of information of the diagnoses varies depending on the skills of the interviewer and the memory of the respondents.

Requirements- Personnel required for verbal autopsy

  1. VA interviewer
  2. VA Supervisor
  3. Data manager
  4. Software developer
  5. Field coordinator
  6. Any other depending on implementation

Procedures

VA is a two-step procedure:

  • an interview carried out with family members/caregivers of the deceased using a structured questionnaire to elicit signs and symptoms and other pertinent information
  • Assignment of a probable underlying cause of death. Methods include;
    • a) physician review of VA data
    • b) ICD certification, coding, and tabulation
    • c) computerised algorithms for population fractions

Assigning cause of death in verbal autopsy

A completed verbal autopsy questionnaire contain information on diseases, signs and symptoms, the age and sex of the deceased as well as his or her history and medical reports (if available).

This information is used to assign the causes that led to death. It can be done through;
Assigning diseases from signs and symptoms
The use of a standard set of diagnostic criteria ensures that the results of evaluation and selection are determined in a standard fashion by staff involved in this step. This could be a physician or any other person who has been medically trained.

Diagnostic criteria (algorithms)
They describe which combination of symptoms, duration and severity may lead to a specific diagnosis.

Timing schedule for the VA

  • Consider social cultural aspects
  • Memory of the respondents / Recall period

Scheduling VA

  • VA supervisors and Interviewers can contact the family of the deceased to schedule appropriate date for conducting VA.
  • Normally this period is 30 days after the death to give room for grieving period.

Conducting VA

In the scheduled date for VA the interviewer as trained follows key steps in VA

  • Determines key/appropriate respondent
  • Introduces themselves and explains the VA interview process
  • Obtains consent to proceed with the interview
  • Conducts the interview
  • Thanks respondent
  • Submits the data

Data analysis and reporting

VA and COD data are normally in CSV format which can be analysed by various statistical softwares like STATA, R

It is recommended to prepare data quality protocol that highlights various indicators to monitor during data quality checks

The protocol needs to highlight allowable values and invalid values
Develop an error resolution protocol that highlights steps/stages required to resolve data errors

Sample verbal Autopsy Questionnaire

Sample VA questionnaire 1: death of a child aged under four weeks
determine causes of early neonatal deaths, late neonatal deaths, perinatal deaths and stillbirths to determine causes of those perinatal events and deaths.

Sample VA questionnaire 2: death of a child aged four weeks to 14 years
designed to ascertain the major causes of post-neonatal child mortality (i.e. starting from the fourth week of life), as well as causes of death that may be seen through 14 years.

Sample VA questionnaire 3: death of a person aged 15 years and above
designed to identify all major causes of death among adolescents and adults (i.e. starting at age 15), including deaths related to pregnancy and childbirth.
