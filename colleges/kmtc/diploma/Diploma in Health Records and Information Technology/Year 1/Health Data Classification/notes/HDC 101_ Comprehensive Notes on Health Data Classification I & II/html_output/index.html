Health Data Classification I & II

Kenya Medical Training College - Department of Health Records and Information Technology

Course Overview

This comprehensive guide covers the principles and practices of health data classification, focusing on the International Classification of Diseases (ICD) and related systems. It details the structure and application of ICD-10 and ICD-11, morbidity and mortality coding rules, and the legal and ethical frameworks governing health records in Kenya.

Table of Contents

Part I: Health Data Classification I

  • Unit 1: Introduction to ICD, ICD-11, ICPM, ICHI and the Structure of Classification

    Definitions of ICD, ICPM, and ICD-11; historical development; and the structure of classification volumes.

  • Unit 2: Purpose of Disease Classification

    The importance, uses, and principles of classifying diseases for statistical and health management purposes.

  • Unit 3: Major Categories of ICD-10, ICD-11, ICPM/ICHI and Conventions

    Detailed breakdown of chapters, blocks, categories, and the essential coding conventions (inclusion/exclusion terms, symbols).

  • Unit 4: Coding and Indexing Morbidity Data and Related Rules

    Guidelines for selecting 'Main Condition', rules for morbidity coding (MB1-MB5), and the use of coding tools.

Part II: Health Data Classification II

  • Unit 5: International Form of Medical Certificate of Cause of Death Classification

    Understanding the MCCoD, definitions of cause of death, sequence, and the structure of death certificates.

  • Unit 6: Mortality Coding Rules and Guideline for Coding Special Conditions

    Rules for selecting the underlying cause of death (General Principle, Rules 1-3), and guidelines for special conditions like pregnancy and neoplasms.

  • Unit 7: Legal and Ethical Issues in Births and Deaths

    Kenya's legal framework (Births and Deaths Act, Data Protection Act) and ethical principles (Beneficence, Justice) in health data.

  • Unit 8: Verbal Autopsy (VA)

    Overview of community cause of death determination, VA procedures, questionnaires, and data analysis.
