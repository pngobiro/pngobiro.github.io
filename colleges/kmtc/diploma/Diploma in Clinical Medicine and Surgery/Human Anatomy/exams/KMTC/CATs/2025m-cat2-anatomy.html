Section 1: Short Answer Questions (40 Marks)

1.
Draw well labelled diagrams of the following bones:
  1. Femur (5 marks)
  2. Humerus (5 marks)
  3. Clavicle (5 marks)
  4. Scapula (5 marks)
Show Answer

Note for Exams: When asked to draw, ensure lines are clear, uncrossed, and labels are horizontal. Below are the key features that must be included in your diagrams.

i. Femur (Anterior/Posterior View):

  • Proximal End: Head (with fovea capitis), Neck, Greater Trochanter, Lesser Trochanter, Intertrochanteric line (anterior) / crest (posterior).
  • Shaft: Gluteal tuberosity, Linea Aspera (posterior).
  • Distal End: Medial and Lateral Condyles, Medial and Lateral Epicondyles, Adductor Tubercle, Patellar surface (anterior), Intercondylar fossa (posterior).

ii. Humerus (Anterior/Posterior View):

  • Proximal End: Head, Anatomical Neck, Greater and Lesser Tubercles, Intertubercular (Bicipital) Groove, Surgical Neck.
  • Shaft: Deltoid Tuberosity, Radial Groove (posterior).
  • Distal End: Capitulum (lateral), Trochlea (medial), Medial and Lateral Epicondyles, Coronoid Fossa (anterior), Olecranon Fossa (posterior).

iii. Clavicle (Superior/Inferior View):

  • Sternal (Medial) End: Quadrangular/Blocky.
  • Acromial (Lateral) End: Flattened.
  • Shaft: Medial 2/3 convex anteriorly, Lateral 1/3 concave anteriorly.
  • Inferior Features: Conoid Tubercle, Trapezoid Line, Subclavian Groove.

iv. Scapula (Anterior/Posterior View):

  • Surfaces: Subscapular fossa (Anterior), Supraspinous and Infraspinous fossae (Posterior).
  • Borders: Superior, Medial (Vertebral), Lateral (Axillary).
  • Angles: Superior, Inferior, Lateral (Glenoid cavity).
  • Processes: Spine (Posterior), Acromion, Coracoid Process.
  • Features: Suprascapular notch, Glenoid Cavity.

5.
Classify joints and give characteristics of each type of joints. (5 marks)
Show Answer

Joints are structurally classified into three main types:

  1. Fibrous Joints (Synarthroses):
    • Characteristics: Bones are held together by dense fibrous connective tissue. No joint cavity is present. Usually immovable.
    • Examples: Sutures (skull), Gomphosis (teeth sockets), Syndesmosis (distal tibiofibular).
  2. Cartilaginous Joints (Amphiarthroses):
    • Characteristics: Bones are joined by cartilage (hyaline or fibrocartilage). No joint cavity. Slightly movable.
    • Examples: Synchondrosis (growth plates - hyaline), Symphysis (pubic symphysis, intervertebral discs - fibrocartilage).
  3. Synovial Joints (Diarthroses):
    • Characteristics:
      • Presence of a synovial cavity (fluid-filled space).
      • Articular ends covered by hyaline cartilage.
      • Enclosed by an articular capsule.
      • Reinforced by ligaments.
      • Freely movable.
    • Examples: Knee, Elbow, Hip, Shoulder.
