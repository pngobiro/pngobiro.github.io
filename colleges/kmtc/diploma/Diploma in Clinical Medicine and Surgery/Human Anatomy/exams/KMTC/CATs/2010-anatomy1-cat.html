Section A: Multiple Choice Questions

1.
Which of the following cells plays a role in immunological reactions in the skin?
a) Melanocyte
b) Capillary epithelial cell
c) Langerhans cell

Explanation: Langerhans cells are dendritic cells in the epidermis that function as antigen-presenting cells (macrophages) involved in the immune response.

ii) Ribosomes
iii) Golgi apparatus

a) Functions of skin: Protection, Thermoregulation, Sensation, Vitamin D synthesis, Excretion.

b) Neuroglia:

  • Astrocytes: Support BBB, metabolic support.
  • Oligodendrocytes: Myelination in CNS.
  • Microglia: Phagocytosis (immune defense).
  • Schwann Cells: Myelination in PNS.

c) Somatic vs Autonomic:

  • Somatic is voluntary; Autonomic is involuntary.
  • Somatic targets skeletal muscle; Autonomic targets smooth/cardiac muscle and glands.
  • Somatic is a 1-neuron chain; Autonomic is a 2-neuron chain (pre/post-ganglionic).

d) Brain stem parts: Midbrain, Pons, Medulla Oblongata.

e) Organelle functions:

  • i) Mitochondria: ATP production (energy).
  • ii) Ribosomes: Protein synthesis.
  • iii) Golgi: Packaging and modifying proteins.
  • iv) Lysosomes: Intracellular digestion/waste removal.
  • v) RER: Synthesis of secretory proteins.

Section C: Long Answer Questions

35.
a) Describe the 3 types of muscles (12marks)
b) Describe the classification of bone by shape (8marks)
Show Answer

a) Types of Muscles:

  • Skeletal Muscle: Striated, Voluntary, Multinucleated (peripheral nuclei), Cylindrical shape. Attached to bones. Responsible for locomotion.
  • Cardiac Muscle: Striated, Involuntary, Single nucleus (central), Branched fibers with Intercalated discs. Found in the heart.
  • Smooth Muscle: Non-striated, Involuntary, Single nucleus (central), Fusiform (spindle) shape. Found in walls of hollow organs (gut, vessels).

b) Bone Classification by Shape:

  1. Long Bones: Length > Width. Shaft + 2 ends. (e.g., Femur, Humerus).
  2. Short Bones: Cube-shaped. (e.g., Carpals, Tarsals).
  3. Flat Bones: Thin, curved. (e.g., Sternum, Scapula, Skull bones).
  4. Irregular Bones: Complex shapes. (e.g., Vertebrae, Hip bones).
  5. Sesamoid Bones: Embedded in tendons. (e.g., Patella).
