Section 1: Multiple Choice Questions (20 Marks)

1.
What’s the origin of the hamstring muscle?
a) iliac crest
b) Clavicle acromion process
c) The body of the clavicle
d) Anterior superior iliac spine
Show Answer

Note on Options: None of the provided options are the correct anatomical origin for the Hamstrings. The Hamstring muscles (Semitepdinosus, Semimembranosus, Biceps Femoris Long Head) originate from the Ischial Tuberosity.

Analysis of distractor options:

  • Iliac Crest: Origin of Tensor Fasciae Latae, Gluteus Maximus.
  • ASIS: Origin of Sartorius.

2.
Which of the following is not rotator cuff muscle?
a) Supraspinatus
b) subscapularis
c) teres minor
d) teres major
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) teres major

Explanation: The Rotator Cuff muscles are remembered by the mnemonic SITS: Supraspinatus, Infraspinatus, Teres minor, and Subscapularis. Teres Major is NOT part of the cuff (it acts more like the Latissimus Dorsi).

3.
The following statement is false about the muscles of the leg
a) The anterior compartment muscles are supplied by the deep peroneal nerve.
b) The lateral compartment muscles are supplied by the superficial peroneal nerve.
c) Posterior compartment muscles are supplied by the tibia nerve
d) The anterior compartment muscles are supplied by the anterior peroneal nerve.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) The anterior compartment muscles are supplied by the anterior peroneal nerve.

Explanation: Option A is true (Deep Peroneal supplies anterior compartment). Option D is false because "Anterior Peroneal Nerve" is not the standard name; it is the Deep Peroneal Nerve (or Deep Fibular). Furthermore, it contradicts option A which is correct.

4.
The following are muscles of the gluteal region except
a) Gluteus medius
b) Gluteus minimus
c) Piriformis
d) Gracilis muscle.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Gracilis muscle.

Explanation: The Gracilis is a muscle of the medial compartment of the thigh (adductor group), not the gluteal region.

5.
About the compartments of the thigh (Which is False?)
a) Anterior compartment supplied by the femoral nerve
b) Posterior compartment supplied by the sciatic nerve
c) Medial compartment supplied by the obturator nerve.
d) Anterior compartment muscles flex the leg at kneel joint.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Anterior compartment muscles flex the leg at kneel joint.

Explanation: Anterior compartment muscles (Quadriceps) extend the knee joint. It is the posterior compartment (Hamstrings) that flexes the knee.

6.
The hormone produced by the testes is
a) Testosterone
b) Progesterone
c) Estrogen
d) Luteinizing hormone.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Testosterone

Explanation: Testosterone is the primary male sex hormone produced by the Leydig cells of the testes.

7.
Which of the following organ qualifies to be called a primary sex organ?
a) Uterine tubes
b) Fallopian tubes
c) Oviduct
d) Ovaries.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Ovaries.

Explanation: Primary sex organs (Gonads) produce gametes (eggs/sperm). In females, these are the ovaries. The tubes and uterus are secondary (accessory) sex organs.

8.
Which part of the fallopian tube is responsible for fertilization
a) Infundibulum
b) Ampulla
c) Isthmic
d) Interstitial
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Ampulla

Explanation: The ampulla is the widest and longest part of the tube where fertilization most commonly occurs.

9.
The pouch that is formed as the peritoneum is reflected over the urinary bladder is
a) Vesico uterine pouch
b) Porch of Douglas
c) Recto uterine
d) Vagina uterine pouch
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Vesico uterine pouch

Explanation: In females, the peritoneum reflects from the bladder to the uterus, forming the Vesicouterine pouch. The Rectouterine pouch (Pouch of Douglas) is between the uterus and rectum.

10.
Cells found between the seminiferous tubules and are considered specialized endocrine cells is
a) Cells of leydig
b) Sertoli cells
c) Nurse cells
d) Sustentacular cells
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Cells of leydig

Explanation: Leydig cells (interstitial cells) are located between the tubules and produce testosterone. Sertoli cells (Nurse/Sustentacular) are located inside the tubules and support spermatogenesis.

11.
Regarding the layer of the uterine wall, the _______ is the muscular layer.
a) Endometrium
b) Fundus
c) Myometrium
d) Perimetrium
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Myometrium

Explanation: The uterus has three layers: Endometrium (inner mucosal), Myometrium (middle muscular), and Perimetrium (outer serosal).

12.
Which ligament attaches the ovaries and the uterus to the pelvic wall
a) Broad ligament
b) Mesovarium
c) Ovarian ligament
d) Suspensory ligament.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Suspensory ligament.

Explanation: The Suspensory Ligament of the ovary attaches the ovary to the pelvic wall and contains the ovarian vessels. The Ovarian ligament attaches ovary to uterus.

13.
Name the organ that lies posterior to the bladder and anterior to the rectum and secrets an alkaline, fructose filled fluid
a) Prostate
b) Cowpers gland
c) Seminal glands
d) Urethra.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Seminal glands (Seminal Vesicles)

Explanation: Seminal vesicles are located posterior to the bladder and secrete a fructose-rich fluid.

14.
The male urethra is divided into the following except
a) Corpus urethra
b) Membranous urethra
c) Bulbourethra
d) Prostatic urethra
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Corpus urethra

Explanation: The parts are: Prostatic urethra, Membranous urethra, and Spongy (Penile) urethra. "Corpus urethra" is not a standard term. "Bulbourethra" usually refers to the gland, but the spongy urethra does pass through the bulb of the penis.

15.
The part of the ear that functions in hearing is the ...
a. Cochlea
b. Eustachian tube
c. Round window
d. Labyrinth
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a. Cochlea

Explanation: The cochlea contains the Organ of Corti, which is the receptor organ for hearing. The labyrinth includes both cochlea (hearing) and vestibule (balance).

16.
The sense of smell is part of the special sense and it is made possible by the
a. Sense of receptors
b. Nasal receptors
c. Olfactory receptors
d. Taste receptors.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c. Olfactory receptors

Explanation: Olfaction is mediated by olfactory receptors located in the olfactory epithelium of the nasal cavity.

17.
The following are Accessory digestive organs except
a. Salivary glands
b. Liver
c. Pancreas
d. Cecum
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d. Cecum

Explanation: The Cecum is part of the Alimentary Canal (Digestive Tract) itself. The others are accessory organs that secrete into the tract.

18.
The following muscles make up the wall of the axilla except
a. Pectoralis major
b. Lattissimus dorsi
c. Teres major
d. Deltoid
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d. Deltoid

Explanation:

  • Anterior Wall: Pectoralis Major/Minor.
  • Posterior Wall: Latissimus Dorsi, Teres Major, Subscapularis.
  • Medial Wall: Serratus Anterior.
  • Lateral Wall: Intertubercular groove of humerus.
The Deltoid covers the shoulder but is not a boundary wall of the axillary pyramid.

19.
The following muscles take part in active breathing except.
a) Sternocleidomastoid
b) Scalene
c) Abdominal muscles
d) Latisimus muscle
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Latisimus muscle

Explanation: SCM and Scalenes are accessory muscles of inspiration. Abdominal muscles are muscles of active expiration. Latissimus dorsi is primarily an arm mover (coughing/sneezing uses it, but it's least likely to be classified as a primary "breathing" muscle compared to the others).

20.
About the pancreases (Which is FALSE?)
a) It’s a retroperitoneal gland
b) It’s an intraperitoneal gland
c) Functions as endocrine and exocrine gland
d) It has head and tail
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) It’s an intraperitoneal gland

Explanation: The pancreas is a Retroperitoneal organ (located behind the peritoneum), except for a small portion of the tail.

  1. Walls:
    • Endometrium: Inner mucosal lining, sheds during menstruation.
    • Myometrium: Thick middle smooth muscle layer.
    • Perimetrium: Outer serous layer (peritoneum).
  2. Blood supply:
    • Supplied by the Uterine Arteries (branch of Internal Iliac) and Ovarian Arteries.
  3. Parts of the uterine tube (Fallopian Tube):

    • Ampulla: Widest part, usual site of fertilization.

    • Intramural (Interstitial): Part passing through uterine wall.

3.
Using the knowledge learnt during the urinary system class...
Show Answer

a) Discuss the mechanism of urine formation (3 marks)

  • Filtration: Glomerulus filters blood under pressure to create filtrate in Bowman's capsule.
  • Reabsorption: PCT and Loop of Henle reclaim water, glucose, and ions back into blood.
  • Secretion: DCT and Collecting duct actively move wastes (H+, K+, drugs) from blood into urine.

b) Outline the 2 classes of nephrons (2 marks)

  • Cortical Nephrons: Short loops of Henle, mostly in cortex (85%).
  • Juxtamedullary Nephrons: Long loops extending deep into medulla, important for concentrating urine (15%).

c) Outline Summary of Blood Vessels Supplying the Kidney (5 marks)

Renal Artery $\rightarrow$ Segmental Arteries $\rightarrow$ Interlobar Arteries $\rightarrow$ Arcuate Arteries $\rightarrow$ Cortical Radiate (Interlobular) Arteries $\rightarrow$ Afferent Arteriole $\rightarrow$ Glomerulus $\rightarrow$ Efferent Arteriole $\rightarrow$ Peritubular Capillaries/Vasa Recta $\rightarrow$ Venous return.

4.
Respiratory Anatomy Questions
Show Answer

a) Describe layers of tracheal wall (5 marks)

  • Mucosa: Pseudostratified ciliated columnar epithelium with goblet cells.
  • Submucosa: Connective tissue with seromucous glands.
  • Hyaline Cartilage: C-shaped rings supporting the airway.
  • Adventitia: Outer connective tissue layer.

b) Describe 3 parts of the pharynx

  • Nasopharynx: Behind nasal cavity, contains pharyngeal tonsils and Eustachian tube opening.
  • Oropharynx: Behind oral cavity, passageway for food and air, contains palatine tonsils.
  • Laryngopharynx: Inferior part, posterior to larynx, diverges into esophagus and larynx.
