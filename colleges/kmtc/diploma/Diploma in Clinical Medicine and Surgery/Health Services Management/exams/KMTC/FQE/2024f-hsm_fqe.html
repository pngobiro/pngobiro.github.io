Section 1: Multiple Choice Questions

40 Marks
1.
Which of the following scenarios is correctly matched with the appropriate dimension of quality;
A. The hospital nurse treated in sign language on duty at all times in the labour ward - efficiency.
B. The grass in the area surrounding the health center is cut and paths are clean - access to service.
C. The manager patiently waits for an explanation from the employee and gives useful feedback- effectiveness.
D. The clinician accurately counts the respirations of a coughing child - technical performance.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: D. The clinician accurately counts the respirations of a coughing child - technical performance.

Explanation: Technical performance refers to the application of medical science and knowledge in a way that maximizes health benefits (doing the right thing right). Accurate clinical assessment is a technical skill.

2.

A. Access to care
B. Choice of care
C. Safety of care
D. Effectiveness of care

Correct Answer: C. Safety of care

Explanation: Safety is the prevention of harm to patients. Medication errors are a primary patient safety issue.

14.
The following are methods used while issuing goods from the stores;
A. Last in first out (LIFO)
B. First in first expiry (FIFE)
C. First expiry first out (FEFO)
D. First in last out (FILO)
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C. First expiry first out (FEFO)

Explanation: For medical supplies (which have expiration dates), FEFO is the mandatory issuance method to prevent wastage. FIFO is also used, but FEFO is more precise for perishables.

Ms. Kamau has been attending the Ante natal clinic at a nearby dispensary. The nurse has advised that she needs to plan for the birth of her baby by deciding where she will deliver. She has three options; to deliver at home assisted by a traditional birth attendant, go to the county hospital 60 Kms away, or go to a nearby private nursing home. Explain how her choice of where to deliver can be affected by any five dimensions of quality. (5 Marks)
Show Answer
  1. Access (Geographic): The 60km distance to the county hospital is a barrier compared to the nearby private home.
  2. Access (Financial/Affordability): The private nursing home may be expensive compared to the public hospital or home birth.
  3. Technical Competence/Safety: The hospital offers better safety and skills than the TBA, influencing her choice if she prioritizes safety.
  4. Interpersonal Relations (Human Relations): If staff at the hospital are rude, she may choose the TBA or private home.
  5. Amenities/Comfort: The private home may offer better physical comfort than the public hospital.
  6. Continuity of Care: If the dispensary nurse refers her, continuity is maintained.

Section 3: Long Answer Question

20 Marks
49.
Describe the major components of strategic plan outlining the key steps in the strategic management process. (20 Marks)
Show Answer

Key Steps in the Strategic Management Process:

1. Environmental Scanning (Situational Analysis)

Analyzing the internal and external environment to understand current position.

  • Internal Analysis: Assessing Strengths and Weaknesses (Resources, capabilities).
  • External Analysis: Assessing Opportunities and Threats (PESTEL analysis - Political, Economic, Social, Tech, etc.).
  • Tool: SWOT Analysis.

2. Strategy Formulation

Developing the direction of the organization based on the analysis.

  • Vision Statement: Where we want to be in the future.
  • Mission Statement: What we do, for whom, and how (Purpose).
  • Core Values: Guiding principles.
  • Strategic Objectives: Setting SMART goals (Specific, Measurable, Achievable, Relevant, Time-bound).
  • Developing Strategies: Choosing the best course of action to achieve objectives.

3. Strategy Implementation

Putting the plan into action.

  • Resource Allocation: Budgeting and staffing.
  • Organizational Structure: Aligning structure to strategy.
  • Change Management: Communicating and motivating staff.
  • Action Plans: Creating detailed work plans/operational plans.

4. Strategy Monitoring and Evaluation (Control)

Checking if the strategy is working.

  • Performance Measurement: Tracking Key Performance Indicators (KPIs).
  • Review: Comparing actual performance against targets.
  • Corrective Action: Adjusting the strategy or implementation if targets are missed.
