1.
Identify the statement that best defines a manager:
A. Organizations need strong manager to help them achieve their goal.
B. A manager controls and coordinate resources effectively
C. A manager is appointed automatically
D. All managers are leaders
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B. A manager controls and coordinate resources effectively

Explanation: Management involves the planning, organizing, coordinating, and controlling of resources to achieve organizational goals.

2.
Which one of the following is NOT a factor that determine the culture and structure of an organization?
A. Organizations environment
B. Technology used by the organization
C. Strategy used by an organization
D. The management team
Show Answer

Correct Answer: D. The management team

Explanation: According to organizational theory, the primary determinants of structure and culture are the Environment, Technology, Size, and Strategy. While the management team influences it, they are actors within the structure rather than a structural determinant like technology or strategy.

10.
In medical law, a paramedic threatens a patient during the course of procedure and performed the threatened act. Which of the following offense may be claimed?
A. Assault
B. Battery
C. Negligence
D. False imprisonment
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A. Assault

Explanation: Technically, the *threat* is assault, and the *act* is battery. However, in many multiple choice questions of this type, if "Assault" is the primary option for threatening behavior, it is selected. If the question emphasizes the *threat*, it is assault.

41.
Explain five (5) challenges faced by the health sector in Kenya due to Devolution. (5 Marks)
Show Answer
  1. Human Resource Issues: Frequent strikes by health workers due to delayed salaries and lack of uniform terms of service across counties.
  2. Inequitable Distribution of Resources: Disparities in funding and equipment between different counties.
  3. Political Interference: Local politics influencing recruitment, procurement, and management of health facilities.
  4. Capacity Gaps: Inadequate management and leadership skills among some county health management teams.
  5. Supply Chain Disruptions: Challenges in the timely procurement and distribution of essential medicines (KEMSA debt issues).
42.
Describe three advantages of electronic data archiving and two disadvantages of traditional data archiving. (5 Marks)
Show Answer

Advantages of Electronic Data Archiving:

  • Space Saving: Stores huge amounts of data in very little physical space compared to paper files.
  • Easy Retrieval: Data can be searched and accessed instantly using keywords.
  • Data Security (Backups): Easier to create redundant backups to prevent data loss from disasters.

Disadvantages of Traditional (Paper) Archiving:

  • Space Consuming: Requires large physical storage rooms and cabinets.
  • Vulnerability: High risk of damage or loss due to fire, water, pests, or wear and tear.
43.
You are part of the health management team and you are in the process of introducing new technology in your health facilities. Describe two steps within the change process. (5 Marks)
Show Answer

Using Kurt Lewin's Change Model:

  1. Unfreezing: Preparing the organization for change. This involves creating awareness of the need for the new technology, addressing resistance, and motivating staff to accept that the old way is no longer adequate.
  2. Changing (Moving): The implementation phase. Training staff on the new technology, installing systems, and supporting employees as they learn new behaviors and processes.
  3. Refreezing: Stabilizing the change. Integrating the new technology into standard operating procedures and culture to ensure staff don't revert to old methods.
44.
Identify five benefits of logistics and commodity management. (5 Marks)
Show Answer

46.
Using the levels of care, describe the structure of health care system in Kenya. (5 Marks)
Show Answer

The Kenya Health System is structured into 6 levels:

  • Level 1 (Community Health Services): Community Health Units (CHUs) providing health promotion and basic preventive services.
  • Level 2 (Dispensaries/Clinics): First physical interface, offering outpatient curative and preventive services.
  • Level 3 (Health Centres): Provide curative, preventive, and basic inpatient services (maternity).
  • Level 4 (County Hospitals): Primary referral hospitals offering comprehensive inpatient and outpatient specialist services.
  • Level 5 (County Referral Hospitals): Secondary referral level with specialized services and training capacities.
  • Level 6 (National Referral Hospitals): Tertiary care, specialized research, and training (e.g., KNH, MTRH).
47.
You are invited to give a talk to the security team at Kenya Medical training college as an employee tasked with training and awareness at the Kenya National Disaster Management Authority. As you prepare your slides, distinguish between disaster reduction and readiness. (5 Marks)
Show Answer
  • Disaster Reduction (Mitigation/Prevention): Measures taken before a disaster to minimize its impact or likelihood. It involves structural measures (building dykes) and non-structural measures (policies, land-use planning) to reduce vulnerability and exposure.
  • Readiness (Preparedness): Measures taken to ensure an effective response when a disaster occurs. It involves planning, training, drills, stockpiling supplies, and establishing early warning systems to ensure quick action during an emergency.

Section 3: Long Answer Question

20 Marks
49.
While rotating in the procurement department during your rural health attachment, you took part in processing a schedule to procure some commodities.
a) Formulate KEY steps you followed in the process (14 Marks)
b) Describe four conditions that must be met when establishing the essential medicine list (6 Marks)
Show Answer

a) Key Steps in the Procurement Process:

  1. Needs Assessment/Quantification: determining what commodities are needed and in what quantities (forecasting).
  2. Specification Definition: detailed description of the quality, strength, and type of items required.
  3. Budgeting/Allocation of Funds: Ensuring sufficient funds (AIE) are available for the purchase.
  4. Sourcing/Selection of Suppliers: Advertising tenders or requesting quotations from pre-qualified suppliers.
  5. Evaluation of Bids: Assessing received bids based on technical (quality) and financial (price) criteria.
  6. Awarding of Contract: Selecting the best bidder and issuing a Local Purchase Order (LPO).
  7. Receipt and Inspection: Receiving the delivered goods and inspecting them to ensure they match specifications (Inspection and Acceptance Committee).
  8. Storage and Inventory Entry: entering items into stock cards/bin cards and storing them correctly.
  9. Payment: Processing payment for the supplier upon successful delivery.

b) Conditions for Establishing the Essential Medicine List (WHO Criteria):

  1. Relevance to Public Health: Medicines selected must address the priority healthcare needs/diseases of the population.
  2. Evidence of Efficacy and Safety: There must be solid clinical evidence that the medicines are effective and safe for use.
  3. Cost-Effectiveness: The medicines should offer good value for money compared to alternatives.
  4. Quality and Stability: The medicines must be available in a form that ensures quality and stability under local storage conditions.
