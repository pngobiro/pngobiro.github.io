Section 1: Multiple Choice Questions

40 Marks
1.
The following is not a building blocks of health system
a) Leadership and governance
b) Health financing
c) Service delivery
d) County government
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) County government

Explanation: The WHO Health System Building Blocks are: Service Delivery, Health Workforce, Information, Medical Products/Vaccines/Technologies, Financing, and Leadership/Governance. "County government" is an administrative structure, not a fundamental building block itself.

2.
At which Health service delivery level are health behavior change activities carried out
a) Community
b) Dispensary/clinic
c) Health centre
d) Primary hospital
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Community

Explanation: Level 1 (Community Health Units) is the primary level for health promotion, disease prevention, and behavior change communication (BCC).

3.
The following is a demerit of division of labour
a) Specialization
b) Efficiency
c) Boredom
d) Time saving
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Boredom

Explanation: While division of labor increases efficiency and specialization, the repetition of the same small task can lead to monotony and boredom for the worker.

4.
The principle of management which promotes unity of interest between employees and management is
a) Unity of command
b) Esprit De corps
c) Stability of staff
d) Unity of direction
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Esprit De corps

Explanation: Esprit de corps refers to promoting team spirit and harmony within the organization.

5.
Functions of management do not include
a) Organizing
b) Planning
c) Disciplining
d) Controlling
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Disciplining

Explanation: The standard functions of management are Planning, Organizing, Staffing, Directing/Leading, and Controlling. Disciplining is a sub-activity of staffing/directing, not a primary function.

6.
Monitoring and evaluation is important in management. Which statement is false?
a) It helps you to know successes and failures
b) It will help you formulate new objectives
c) Re-planning can be done
d) Is not useful in organization
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Is not useful in organization

Explanation: M&E is critical for assessing progress and making informed decisions.

7.
The ability to know how each part of the organization interrelates and contributes to overall objectives is
a) Technical skills
b) Analytical skills
c) Conceptual skills
d) Human skills
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Conceptual skills

Explanation: Conceptual skills involve the ability to see the "big picture" and understand how various parts of the organization work together.

8.
A disadvantage of control is
a) Agreeing to controlling
b) Providing acute information
c) Not following up rules to the letter
d) Game playing
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Game playing

Explanation: Excessive or poorly designed control systems can lead to employees "gaming the system" (manipulating data or behaviors) to look good rather than achieving actual goals.

9.
The leadership style where employees feel and believe that their input are desired and can influence a decision is;
a) Free reign
b) Laissez faire
c) Consultative
d) Authoritarian
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Consultative

Explanation: Also known as democratic or participative leadership, where the leader consults with subordinates before making decisions.

10.
An advantage of internal recruitment is
a) Promotion is seen as an incentive to other employees
b) Promotion of one may upset others
c) Expensive to organization
d) There will be new ideas
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Promotion is seen as an incentive to other employees

Explanation: Internal recruitment boosts morale as employees see career progression opportunities.

11.
The following is not a type of decision
a) Emergency
b) Routine
c) Debatable
d) Short term
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Debatable

Explanation: Standard types include Routine (Programmed) vs Strategic (Non-programmed), Short vs Long term. "Debatable" is not a classification.

12.
Outcome of action when making decision is
a) Strategic action
b) Contingency action
c) Administration action
d) Operating action
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Operating action

Explanation: Decisions are made to be implemented, resulting in operations/actions.

13.
Part of performance appraisal which will be filled by the reporting supervisor is
a) Performance of the appraisee
b) Personal particulars of appraisee
c) Employment records of appraisee
d) Qualification of appraisee
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Performance of the appraisee

Explanation: The supervisor assesses and scores the actual performance. Personal details are usually filled by the appraisee or HR.

14.
The following is the responsibility of the supervisor
a) Build and maintain an efficient organization
b) Maintain an effective work force
c) Control performance
d) Responsible for giving instructions
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Maintain an effective work force

Explanation: While all options are related, a key supervisory role is ensuring the workforce is effective and productive on a daily basis.

15.
Which is not an important role of agenda of a meeting
a) Allows members to know what to expect
b) Enables members to prepare
c) To make members have less information
d) Allows responsibility to be delegated
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) To make members have less information

Explanation: An agenda is meant to inform, not to restrict information.

16.
Supervisors’ reason for avoiding delegation exclude:
a) Desire for shared recognition
b) Fear of criticism
c) Lack of confidence
d) Lack necessary information
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Desire for shared recognition

Explanation: Supervisors usually avoid delegation due to fear of losing control/recognition, or lack of trust. A "desire for shared recognition" would usually motivate delegation, not prevent it. (Assuming the question implies "Which is NOT a reason for avoiding").

17.
Purpose of job description is not
a) Used for induction
b) Serve as basis for performance appraisal
c) Disciplining staff
d) Provide criteria for recruiting
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Disciplining staff

Explanation: While failure to meet a JD can lead to discipline, the primary purposes are recruitment (d), induction (a), and appraisal (b). Discipline is a punitive measure, not the primary "purpose" of the document.

18.
The commonest type of communication in management is
a) Vertical
b) Lateral
c) Informal
d) Grapevine
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Grapevine

Explanation: The informal communication network (grapevine) is often the most pervasive and fastest method of communication in many organizations.

19.
Which is not among early theories of motivation
a) Maslow’s Hierarchy of needs
b) Theory X and Y
c) Theory Z
d) Hawthorne studies
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Theory Z

Explanation: Theory Z (William Ouchi) was developed in the 1980s, much later than Maslow, McGregor (X/Y), and the Hawthorne studies (early 20th century).

20.
Hygiene factors in Herzberg’s motivation hygiene theory include:
a) Good Salary
b) Company policies and administration
c) Recognition
d) responsibility
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Company policies and administration

Explanation: Hygiene factors (dissatisfiers) include policy, supervision, salary, and working conditions. Recognition and responsibility are Motivators (satisfiers).

21.
Strategies for effective team development exclude:
a) Support and trust
b) Individual development
c) Clear objective
d) Make decisions by consensus
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Individual development

Explanation: Team development focuses on the group's cohesion, goals, and trust. While individuals grow, "Individual development" in isolation is not a strategy for *team* development; the focus is on the collective.

22.
Disadvantage of team work include
a) Enhance unity
b) Conflicts
c) Sustainability
d) Saves time

Correct Answer: b) Conflicts

Explanation: Teams often experience interpersonal conflict, personality clashes, and disagreement on methods, which can be a significant disadvantage.

Purpose of organizational charts and structure is

c) Span of control cannot be seen

Section 2: Short Answer Questions

40 Marks
1.
Explain five power bases of an effective leader. (5 marks)
Show Answer
  1. Legitimate Power: Authority derived from a formal position or title within the organization.
  2. Reward Power: The ability to provide incentives or rewards (pay raises, promotions) to influence compliance.
  3. Coercive Power: The ability to punish or withhold rewards (demotion, firing) to ensure obedience.
  4. Expert Power: Influence based on special skills, knowledge, or expertise that others depend on.
  5. Referent Power: Influence derived from the followers' admiration, respect, and desire to identify with the leader (Charisma).

3.

a) List the steps in the process of policy formulation (3 marks)

b) State two broad parts of health management information systems (2 marks)

Show Answer

a) Steps in Policy Formulation:

  1. Problem Identification: Recognizing an issue that needs government action (Agenda setting).
  2. Policy Formulation: Developing options and drafting the policy.
  3. Policy Adoption: Formal approval by relevant authorities (e.g., Parliament/Cabinet).
  4. Policy Implementation: Putting the policy into effect.
  5. Policy Evaluation: Assessing the impact and effectiveness.

b) Broad Parts of HMIS:

  1. Data Collection and Processing: Tools and procedures to gather raw data (e.g., registers, tally sheets).
  2. Data Analysis and Utilization: Transforming data into information for decision-making (e.g., reports, dashboards).
4.
Explain Health System Resources. (5 marks)
Show Answer

Health system resources are the inputs required to deliver health services:

  1. Human Resources for Health (HRH): The clinical and non-clinical staff (doctors, nurses, administrators).
  2. Financial Resources: Funds required for operations, salaries, and infrastructure (Budget).
  3. Physical Infrastructure: Buildings, hospitals, clinics, and vehicles.
  4. Medical Products and Technology: Essential medicines, vaccines, and medical equipment.
  5. Information: Data and knowledge for decision-making.
  6. Time: A critical resource for service delivery and planning.
5.
Describe an effective delegation process. (5 marks)
Show Answer
  1. Define the Task: Clearly identify the activity or responsibility to be delegated.
  2. Select the Right Person: Choose a subordinate with the necessary skills and capability.
  3. Assign Responsibility and Authority: Clearly communicate the task and grant the necessary power to act/use resources.
  4. Establish Accountability/Deadlines: Set clear expectations, standards, and timeframes for completion.
  5. Monitor and Support: Provide guidance without micromanaging and review progress.
  6. Feedback/Evaluation: Review the final output and provide constructive feedback.
6.
Discuss quality management principles in a health facility. (5 marks)
Show Answer
  1. Customer Focus: Meeting patient needs and exceeding expectations.
  2. Leadership: Leaders establish unity of purpose and direction.
  3. Engagement of People: Involving staff at all levels.
  4. Process Approach: Managing activities as interrelated processes.
  5. Continuous Improvement: Ongoing effort to improve products, services, and processes (Kaizen).
  6. Evidence-based Decision Making: Decisions based on data analysis.
  7. Relationship Management: Managing relationships with suppliers and partners.
7.
What is the purpose of communication in a health system? (5 marks)
Show Answer
  1. Information Exchange: To transmit necessary data, policies, and instructions for operations.
  2. Coordination: To synchronize activities between different departments and levels of care.
  3. Motivation: To encourage staff and provide feedback on performance.
  4. Control/Instruction: To clarify duties, authority, and responsibilities.
  5. Emotional Expression: To allow staff to express frustrations and build social satisfaction.
  6. Decision Making: To provide the information needed to evaluate alternatives.
8.
Highlight advantages of starting sole proprietorship. (5 marks)
Show Answer
  1. Ease of Formation: Simple and inexpensive to start with few legal formalities.
  2. Quick Decision Making: The owner has total control and can make decisions immediately without consultation.
  3. Profit Retention: The owner keeps all the profits generated by the business.
  4. Privacy: Financial secrets and business strategies are kept confidential.
  5. Personal Satisfaction: The owner derives satisfaction from working for themselves.
  6. Flexibility: Easy to change business direction or dissolve.

Section 3: Long Answer Question

20 Marks
1.

Discuss the following:

a) The role of a clinical officer working at a health facility (10 marks)

b) Principles of financial management in health service (10 marks)

Show Answer

a) Role of a Clinical Officer at a Health Facility (Managerial & Clinical):

  1. Administrative/Managerial Role:
    • Often serves as the Facility In-Charge (in Health Centres/Dispensaries).
    • Chairing the Health Facility Management Committee/Team.
    • Planning and budgeting for facility resources (AWP).
    • Supervising other staff and ensuring discipline.
  2. Clinical Role:
    • Diagnosing and treating patients (outpatient and inpatient).
    • Performing minor surgical procedures.
    • Conducting ward rounds and patient reviews.
  3. Public Health Role:
    • Participating in disease surveillance and reporting.
    • Promoting community health education.
  4. Resource Management:
    • Ensuring proper use of drugs and commodities.
    • Managing facility infrastructure.

b) Principles of Financial Management:

  1. Accountability: Being answerable for how funds are used and being able to explain actions to stakeholders.
  2. Transparency: Openness in financial operations, ensuring records are accessible and accurate.
  3. Integrity: Honesty and reliability in handling funds, avoiding fraud or misuse.
  4. Stewardship: Taking good care of the entrusted resources to ensure they are used for the intended purpose.
  5. Viability/Sustainability: Managing finances so that the facility can continue to operate in the long term (expenditure should not exceed income).
  6. Compliance: Adhering to legal frameworks (PFM Act), standards, and regulations.
  7. Consistency: Using consistent accounting methods over time to allow for comparison.
