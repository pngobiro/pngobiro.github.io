Section 1: Multiple Choice Questions

1.
Which of the following is true?
a. Podocytes and epithelial cells of the glomerulus are permeable to most proteins and blood cells
b. Glomerular filtration membrane consists of two layers
c. Slits between foot processes of podocytes are impermeable to glucose
d. Glucose is completely (100%) absorbed in the proximal convoluted tubule
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d. Glucose is completely (100%) absorbed in the proximal convoluted tubule

Explanation: Under normal physiological conditions (blood glucose < 180 mg/dL), 100% of filtered glucose is reabsorbed in the PCT via SGLT transporters. Option A is false (podocytes prevent protein loss). Option B is false (it has 3 layers: endothelium, basement membrane, podocytes). Option C is false (glucose is freely filtered).

2.
In renin-angiotensin-aldosterone system (RAAS) renin is produced by
a. Adrenal glands
b. Liver
c. Juxtaglomerular cells
d. Lungs
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c. Juxtaglomerular cells

Explanation: Renin is secreted by the Juxtaglomerular (JG) cells of the kidney in response to low blood pressure, low NaCl, or sympathetic stimulation. Adrenal glands produce aldosterone; Liver produces angiotensinogen; Lungs produce ACE.

3.
Concerning glomerular filtration rate (GFR)
a. Low GFR increases the rate of tubular reabsorption
b. High GFR increase the rate of tubular reabsorption
c. High blood pressure at afferent arteriole lowers GFR
d. Low blood pressure at efferent arteriole raises GFR
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a. Low GFR increases the rate of tubular reabsorption

Explanation: If GFR is too low, the fluid flows slowly through the tubules, allowing more time for reabsorption, potentially reabsorbing wastes that should be excreted. Conversely, high GFR can lead to loss of nutrients because flow is too fast for reabsorption.

4.
The tidal volume
a. Is the volume of air inspire or expired with each normal breath
b. Amounts to about 5000mm in the adult male
c. Is the amount of air expired after maximal expiratory effort
d. Amount of air that enters the lungs but does not participate in gas exchange
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a. Is the volume of air inspire or expired with each normal breath

Explanation: Tidal Volume (TV) is the volume of air moved during a normal breath (approx 500ml). Option D describes Dead Space.

5.
Which of the following is not an organ of lower respiratory tract
a. Larynx
b. Trachea
c. Bronchi
d. Bronchioles
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a. Larynx

Explanation: The larynx is generally considered the dividing line or part of the upper respiratory tract (depending on strict classification, often the cut-off is the vocal cords). However, in many contexts, the Upper Respiratory Tract = Nose, Pharynx, Larynx. Lower = Trachea, Bronchi, Lungs. Thus, Larynx is the "most upper" of the options.

6.
Which of the following is not a function of bronchi
a. To support and maintain patency of respiratory airways
b. Mucociliary escalation
c. Cough-reflex action
d. Production of sound
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d. Production of sound

Explanation: Sound production (phonation) is the function of the Larynx (voice box), specifically the vocal cords. The bronchi conduct air, trap particles (mucociliary), and can trigger cough reflexes.

7.
The following are functions of the epinephrine except
a. Increase stroke volume
b. Increase basal metabolic rate
c. Pupillary constriction
d. Increases heart rate
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c. Pupillary constriction

Explanation: Epinephrine (Adrenaline) is a sympathetic hormone. It causes pupillary dilation (mydriasis) for better vision in "fight or flight". Constriction (miosis) is a parasympathetic response.

8.
Which of the following is a true statement
a. Exocrine function of pancreas is to regulate glucose metabolism
b. Exocrine function of pancreas is to secrete digestion enzymes into duodenum
c. The alpha cells of islets secrete insulin
d. The beta cells of islets secrete glucagon
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b. Exocrine function of pancreas is to secrete digestion enzymes into duodenum

Explanation: The exocrine pancreas secretes enzymes (acinar cells). Glucose regulation (a) is the endocrine function. Alpha cells secrete Glucagon (c is wrong). Beta cells secrete Insulin (d is wrong).

9.
Which of the following is true about insulin
a. Released when blood glucose level is low
b. Causes glycogen to be broken into glucose
c. Increases breakdown of fatty acids
d. Promotes glucose storage in muscle cells as glycogen
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d. Promotes glucose storage in muscle cells as glycogen

Explanation: Insulin is anabolic. It lowers blood sugar by promoting uptake and storage (Glycogenesis). Options A, B, and C describe the functions of Glucagon.

10.
About a gland
a. Endocrine gland secretes into a lumen/duct
b. Exocrine gland secreted into blood system
c. Does not produce secretion
d. Paracrine gland exerts effect locally
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d. Paracrine gland exerts effect locally

Explanation: Paracrine signaling affects nearby cells. Option A describes Exocrine. Option B describes Endocrine.

11.
which of the following is not a parts of non-myelinated neuron
a. Neurilemma
b. Axon
c. Nodes of ranvier
d. Schwann cells
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c. Nodes of ranvier

Explanation: Nodes of Ranvier are the gaps between myelin sheaths where saltatory conduction occurs. In non-myelinated neurons, the myelin sheath is absent or continuous/embedded without these distinct functional gaps for jumping impulses.

12.
Sensory division of nervous system
a. Conducts impulses form the CNS to the effectors
b. Mobilizes body system during activity (fight or flight)
c. Conducts impulses from receptors to be CNS
d. Conducts impulses from the CNS to skeletal muscles
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c. Conducts impulses from receptors to be CNS

Explanation: The sensory (afferent) division carries information towards the CNS. Options A and D describe the motor (efferent) division. Option B describes the Sympathetic nervous system.

13.
The efferent division of peripheral nervous system
a. Conducts impulses form the CNS to cardiac muscles
b. Conducts impulses From the CNS to the muscles and glands
c. Conducts impulses from receptors to the CNS
d. Is also known as sensory nerves
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b. Conducts impulses From the CNS to the muscles and glands

Explanation: Efferent means "carrying away from". The efferent division carries motor commands from the CNS to effectors (muscles and glands).

14.
Which of the following is a function of sympathetic nerves
a. Pupillary constriction
b. Reduces the heart rate
c. Relaxation of the bronchial muscles
d. Relaxation of sphincters
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c. Relaxation of the bronchial muscles

Explanation: Sympathetic activation ("Fight or Flight") causes bronchodilation (relaxation of bronchial smooth muscles) to increase airflow. It dilates pupils (a is wrong), increases heart rate (b is wrong), and constricts sphincters (d is wrong).

15.
What is the function of cranial nerve member VIII
a. Facial and mouth sensation
b. Taste and swallowing
c. Hearing and balance
d. Eyeball movement
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c. Hearing and balance

Explanation: CN VIII is the Vestibulocochlear nerve, responsible for hearing (cochlea) and equilibrium/balance (vestibule).

16.
The following are parts of pharynx except
a. Nasopharynx
b. Oropharynx
c. Laryngopharynx
d. Tracheopharynx
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d. Tracheopharynx

Explanation: The pharynx has three parts: Nasopharynx, Oropharynx, and Laryngopharynx. "Tracheopharynx" is not an anatomical term.

17.
Which of the following is a function of canine teeth
a. Grinding of food
b. Chewing
c. Cutting and biting
d. Crushing
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c. Cutting and biting

Explanation: Canines (cuspids) are pointed and designed for tearing, piercing, or holding food. Incisors are for cutting. Molars are for grinding/crushing.

18.
Which of the following is an inactive enzyme in the pancreatic juice
a. Amylase
b. Lipase
c. Trypsinogen
d. Secretin
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c. Trypsinogen

Explanation: Trypsinogen is a zymogen (inactive form) that is activated into trypsin in the duodenum. Amylase and lipase are secreted in active forms.

19.
The following enzymes are involved in the completion of the chemical digestion of food in the enterocytes except
a. Peptidases
b. Lipase
c. Maltose
d. Amylase
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d. Amylase

Explanation: Brush border enzymes (on enterocytes) include peptidases, maltase, sucrase, lactase. Pancreatic Amylase acts in the lumen, not at the final brush border stage (which digests disaccharides). Maltase (often written as Maltose in options confusingly) digests maltose. Lipase is mainly pancreatic.

20.
Bile pigments (bilirubin and biliverdin) are the formations due to breakdown products of
a. Leucocytes
b. Hemoglobin
c. Platelets
d. cholesterol
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b. Hemoglobin

Explanation: Bile pigments are formed from the heme portion of hemoglobin released during the breakdown of old Red Blood Cells (Erythrocytes).

Section 3: Short Answer Questions

1.
State any five functions of biliary secretions (5 marks)
Show Answer
  1. Emulsification of Fats: Bile salts break large fat globules into smaller droplets to increase surface area for lipase.
  2. Absorption of Fats: Formation of micelles to transport lipids to the intestinal wall.
  3. Excretion of Waste: Elimination of bilirubin (from RBC breakdown) and cholesterol.
  4. Neutralization of Acid: Bicarbonate in bile helps neutralize acidic chyme from the stomach.
  5. Absorption of Fat-Soluble Vitamins: Aids in absorbing Vitamins A, D, E, and K.
2.
State any five functions of pancreatic juice (5 marks)
Show Answer
  1. Carbohydrate Digestion: Pancreatic Amylase breaks down starch into maltose.
  2. Protein Digestion: Trypsin, Chymotrypsin, and Carboxypeptidase break down proteins into peptides/amino acids.
  3. Fat Digestion: Pancreatic Lipase breaks down triglycerides into fatty acids and monoglycerides.
  4. Neutralization: High Bicarbonate content neutralizes gastric acid in the duodenum (pH buffering).
  5. Nucleic Acid Digestion: Nucleases (RNase, DNase) break down DNA and RNA.

Section 4: Long Answer Questions

1.
Discuss Resin-Angiotensin-Aldosterone System (RAAS) (20 marks)
Show Answer

Overview: The RAAS is a hormone system involved in the regulation of plasma sodium concentration and arterial blood pressure.

1. Activation

  • Trigger: Low blood pressure, low blood volume, or low Na+ sensed by the macula densa cells in the kidney.
  • Renin Release: Juxtaglomerular (JG) cells secrete the enzyme Renin into the blood.

2. Formation of Angiotensin II

  • Substrate: Liver produces Angiotensinogen (inactive).
  • Conversion 1: Renin converts Angiotensinogen $\rightarrow$ Angiotensin I.
  • Conversion 2: Angiotensin Converting Enzyme (ACE), primarily in the lungs, converts Angiotensin I $\rightarrow$ Angiotensin II (active potent vasoconstrictor).

3. Effects of Angiotensin II

  • Vasoconstriction: Constricts arterioles to increase Total Peripheral Resistance (TPR) and Blood Pressure.
  • Aldosterone Secretion: Stimulates Adrenal Cortex to release Aldosterone.
  • ADH Release: Stimulates release of ADH (Vasopressin) for water reabsorption.
  • Thirst: Stimulates thirst center in hypothalamus.

4. Effects of Aldosterone

  • Acts on the Distal Convoluted Tubule (DCT) and Collecting Duct.
  • Increases Na+ reabsorption (water follows by osmosis).
  • Increases K+ secretion into urine.
  • Result: Increased blood volume and blood pressure.

Clinical Relevance: ACE inhibitors (e.g., Enalapril) are drugs that block this pathway to lower blood pressure in hypertension.
