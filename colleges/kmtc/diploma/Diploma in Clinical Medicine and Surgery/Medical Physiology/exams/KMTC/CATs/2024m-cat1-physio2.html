Section 1: Multiple Choice Questions

1.
Which of the following statements correctly describe the activation of autonomic nervous system function within the circulatory system.
A) Sympathetic nervous system activation constricts arteries found within the salivary glands
B) Sympathetic nervous system activation decreases cardiac output.
C) Parasympathetic nervous system activation constricts arteries found within erectile tissues.
D) Parasympathetic nervous system activation increases heart rate.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A) Sympathetic nervous system activation constricts arteries found within the salivary glands (Best available option)

Explanation:
A: Sympathetic stimulation generally causes vasoconstriction via alpha-adrenergic receptors. In salivary glands, it reduces blood flow (leading to viscous saliva).
B: False. Sympathetic increases cardiac output.
C: False. Parasympathetic causes vasodilation in erectile tissues (erection).
D: False. Parasympathetic decreases heart rate.
Note: While statement B implies "Sympathetic decreases..." which is wrong, if the question meant "Parasympathetic decreases cardiac output", that would be true. As written, A is the most physiologically correct description of sympathetic action.

Correct Answer: C) calcium

Section 2: Short Answer Questions (40 Marks)

1.
Highlight the functions of cerebral spinal fluid (4 mks)
Show Answer
  1. Protection/Shock Absorption: Acts as a cushion to protect the brain from trauma against the skull.
  2. Buoyancy: Reduces the effective weight of the brain, preventing it from crushing blood vessels and nerves at the base.
  3. Nutrition: Transports nutrients (glucose, etc.) to neural tissue.
  4. Waste Removal: Removes metabolic waste products from the brain.
  5. Homeostasis: Maintains a stable chemical environment (pH, ions) for neuronal function.
2.
With the aid of a well labeled diagram explain the functions of parts of a neuron (6 mks)
Show Answer

Diagram description: A standard motor neuron drawing showing dendrites, cell body (soma), axon hillock, axon, myelin sheath, Nodes of Ranvier, and axon terminals.

Functions of Parts:

  • Dendrites: Receive signals/impulses from other neurons or receptors and transmit them towards the cell body.
  • Cell Body (Soma): Contains the nucleus and organelles; the metabolic center of the neuron; integrates incoming signals.
  • Axon: Conducts electrical impulses (Action Potentials) away from the cell body to the terminals.
  • Myelin Sheath: Insulates the axon and increases the speed of impulse transmission (Saltatory conduction).
  • Nodes of Ranvier: Gaps in myelin where ion exchange occurs, allowing impulse regeneration.
  • Axon Terminals (Synaptic knobs): Release neurotransmitters to communicate with the next neuron or effector.
3.
Explain the mechanism and process under which the antidiuretic hormone is produced from the posterior pituitary gland (5mks)
Show Answer
  1. Synthesis: ADH is synthesized in the cell bodies of the Supraoptic (and Paraventricular) nuclei of the Hypothalamus.
  2. Transport: It is packaged into vesicles and transported down the axons (hypothalamo-hypophyseal tract) to the posterior pituitary.
  3. Stimulus: Osmoreceptors in the hypothalamus detect increased blood osmolality (dehydration) or baroreceptors detect decreased blood volume.
  4. Release: Nerve impulses trigger the exocytosis of ADH from the axon terminals in the posterior pituitary into the bloodstream.
  5. Action: ADH travels to the kidneys, increasing water reabsorption.
4.
a) Highlight the 3 classifications of hormones and give an example in each case (3mks)
b) highlight four properties of hormones(2mks)
Show Answer

a) Classification of Hormones:

  • Proteins/Peptides: e.g., Insulin, Glucagon, ADH.
  • Steroids: e.g., Cortisol, Testosterone, Estrogen.
  • Amino Acid Derivatives (Amines): e.g., Thyroxine (T4), Adrenaline.

b) Properties of Hormones:

  • Produced in small quantities (Trace amounts).
  • Transported via the blood.
  • Act on specific target cells/organs possessing specific receptors.
  • Regulate metabolic processes (do not initiate new ones).
  • Are inactivated/metabolized by the liver and excreted by kidneys.

Long essay questions

1.
Explain the process of transmission of a nerve impulse. (10mks)
Show Answer

The transmission of a nerve impulse (Action Potential) involves the following stages:

  1. Resting Membrane Potential: The neuron is polarized (approx -70mV), with High Na+ outside and High K+ inside.
  2. Stimulus & Threshold: A stimulus causes local depolarization. If it reaches the threshold (-55mV), voltage-gated Na+ channels open.
  3. Depolarization: Na+ rushes into the cell rapidly, reversing the polarity inside from negative to positive (+30mV).
  4. Repolarization: Na+ channels close and Voltage-gated K+ channels open. K+ flows out of the cell, restoring the negative charge inside.
  5. Hyperpolarization: K+ channels stay open slightly longer, causing the potential to dip below resting levels.
  6. Refractory Period: The Na+/K+ pump works to restore the original ionic balance (3 Na+ out, 2 K+ in). The neuron cannot fire immediately.
  7. Propagation: The local depolarization triggers adjacent voltage-gated channels, causing the impulse to travel down the axon like a wave. In myelinated neurons, this occurs via Saltatory Conduction (jumping from node to node), which is faster.
