Part I: Multiple Choice Questions (40 Marks)

1.
Which body compartment contains the largest volume of water?
A. Intravascular space
B. Interstitial space
C. Intracellular space
D. Extracellular space
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C. Intracellular space

Explanation: The Intracellular Fluid (ICF) accounts for approximately 2/3 of total body water (about 28L in a 70kg male), while Extracellular Fluid (ECF) accounts for 1/3.

2.
Which of these statements is true?
A. About 60% of body water is located in blood vessels.
B. Approximately 60% of body water is in the internal environment.
C. The internal environment surrounds all body cells.
D. The total amount of water within body cells is 42 litres.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C. The internal environment surrounds all body cells. (See note)

Explanation:
C is true: The "internal environment" refers to the Extracellular Fluid (ECF), which bathes all cells.
D is incorrect: Total body water is ~42L, but water *within* cells (ICF) is ~28L. However, sometimes "Total amount of water... is 42 litres" is the intended fact about the *whole body*. If the option meant "Total body water is 42 litres", it would be correct. Given the phrasing "within body cells", C is the most accurate definition.
A is false (Plasma is ~3L).
B is false (ECF is ~20% of body weight, not 60% of water).

3.
What is the role of effectors in a homeostasis mechanism?
A. They make the mechanism very sensitive to small changes
B. They act to restore changes in the set point
C. They detect changes from the normal set point
D. They identify the appropriate corrective response to a change
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B. They act to restore changes in the set point

Explanation: Effectors (muscles/glands) carry out the response that reverses the deviation, bringing conditions back to the set point.
C is the role of receptors.
D is the role of the control center.

4.
Which of the following hormones is involved in positive feedback mechanism of regulation?
A. Vasopressin
B. Insulin
C. Glucagon
D. Oxytocin
Show Answer

Correct Answer: D. Oxytocin

Explanation: Oxytocin release during labor (Fergusson reflex) involves positive feedback: uterine contractions stimulate oxytocin release, which causes stronger contractions.

5.
Which of these statements about cell division refers to meiosis?
A. It takes place during human growth.
B. It preserves the diploid constitution of chromosomes in humans.
C. It ensures that chromosome numbers do not double in the next human generation.
D. It ensures that the chromosomes type in the human genome does not change.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C. It ensures that chromosome numbers do not double in the next human generation.

Explanation: Meiosis reduces the chromosome number by half (diploid to haploid) during gamete formation. This ensures that upon fertilization, the diploid number is restored rather than doubled.

6.
The smooth muscle that is located in walls of various body cavities and tubules helps in
A. Making the cavities excitable.
B. Involuntary contraction to aid onward propulsion of contents.
C. Dilating the structures in order to hold more contents.

Correct Answer: B. Involuntary contraction to aid onward propulsion of contents.

Explanation: Smooth muscle contraction (peristalsis) propels contents through tubular organs (GIT, ureters, etc.). It is involuntary.

7.
Which of these statements is not true as far as the anatomical dead space is concerned?
A. It made up of alveolar sacs hence is the area where exchange of gases takes place.
B. It is the conducting zone of the respiratory system.
C. It does not favour exchange of respiratory gases.
D. It comprises the nose, mouth, larynx, trachea, bronchi and bronchioles.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A. It made up of alveolar sacs hence is the area where exchange of gases takes place.

Explanation: The question asks what is NOT true. Anatomical dead space is the conducting zone (nose to terminal bronchioles) where NO gas exchange occurs. Alveolar sacs are the respiratory zone where exchange DOES occur.

8.
During quiet breathing as seen when students sit listening to a lecture, the expiratory phase of the breathing cycle is described as passive because
A. Expiration is aided by the elastic recoil of the ribcage when diaphragm and internal intercostals are relaxed.
B. Expiration is aided by contraction of abdominal muscles while respiratory muscles are relaxed.
C. Expiration is a reflex reaction soon after inspiration and reflexes don't utilize energy.
D. Expiration is controlled from the rhythmicity centre of the medulla oblongata which is an involuntary activity.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A. Expiration is aided by the elastic recoil of the ribcage when diaphragm and internal intercostals are relaxed.

Explanation: Quiet expiration relies on the stored elastic energy of the lungs and chest wall recoiling, not on active muscle contraction.

9.
Internal respiration is the respiration which involves
A. Production of oxygen.
B. Ventilation and gas exchange.
C. Removal of carbondioxide.
D. Gas exchange and utilization of oxygen.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: D. Gas exchange and utilization of oxygen.

Explanation: Internal (tissue) respiration refers to gas exchange between blood and tissues and the metabolic utilization of O2 by cells.

10.
Which of the following has no effect on the regulation of respiration?
A. The contribution of the nervous system.
B. The involvement of hormones.
C. The concentration of ions and other chemical substances in the blood or the tissues.
D. The non-chemical influences such as coughing, sneezing, yawning and hiccups.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B. The involvement of hormones.

Explanation: While hormones (like adrenaline/progesterone) can modulate breathing, respiration is primarily regulated by the nervous system (A), chemical stimuli like CO2/pH (C), and reflexes (D). Hormones are the least significant direct regulators among the choices.

11.
Which of the following is a normal occurrence with increasing age?
A. Vital capacity decreases.
B. Residual volume decreases.
C. Expiratory reserve volume increases.
D. Inspiratory capacity increases.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A. Vital capacity decreases.

Explanation: Aging leads to loss of lung elasticity and chest wall compliance, increasing Residual Volume and decreasing Vital Capacity.

12.
Which of the following happens during inspiration?
A. The ribs move downward.
B. The diaphragm lifts up.
C. The antero-posterior dimensions of the chest are increased.
D. The tranverse dimensions of the thorax are decreased.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C. The antero-posterior dimensions of the chest are increased.

Explanation: Contraction of intercostals raises the ribs (Bucket/Pump handle movement), increasing AP and transverse diameters. The diaphragm descends.

13.
Which sensory stimulus causes the hypothalamus to stimulate the secretion of vasopressin?
A. Decreased plasma volume
B. Decreased osmolality
C. Increased plasma volume

Correct Answer: A. Decreased plasma volume

Explanation: Vasopressin (ADH) is secreted in response to increased osmolality (hypertonicity) or decreased plasma volume (hypovolemia).

14.
While relaxation in skeletal muscle results from dissociation of calcium-troponin C complex, in smooth muscle relaxation occurs after dissociation of
A. Calcium-tropomyosin complex
B. Calcium-calmodulin complex
C. Calcium-troponin C complex
D. Calcium-troponin I complex
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B. Calcium-calmodulin complex

Explanation: Smooth muscle lacks troponin. Ca2+ binds to Calmodulin, activating MLCK. Relaxation involves removing Ca2+ and dissociation of the Ca-Calmodulin complex, allowing Myosin Light Chain Phosphatase to dephosphorylate myosin.

17.
Which of the following statements about buffers is true?
A. One buffer system can correct changes in acidity or alkalinity.
B. Kidneys perform their role as the body's most powerful buffer organs quite rapidly.
C. Buffer systems of the body are located in the lungs and kidneys only.
D. A buffer replaces an acid with an alkali.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: D. A buffer replaces an acid with an alkali. (See explanation)

Explanation:
D is a simplified description of buffering action: A buffer reacts with a strong acid to form a weak acid (replacing a strong H+ donor with a weak one), essentially neutralizing it (acting like an alkali).
B is false: Kidneys are powerful but slow.
C is false: Buffers exist in blood, ICF, bone, etc.
A is false: Multiple systems work in concert.

18.
The body has two ways of responding to thirst which follows an increase in plasma osmolality. The voluntary response is drinking water... How does the body respond involuntarily?
A. Through shivering.
B. Through sweating.
C. Through reduced urine output.
D. Through increased urine output.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C. Through reduced urine output.

Explanation: The involuntary response involves ADH secretion, which increases water reabsorption in the kidneys, reducing urine volume to conserve water.

19.
What is oxidative phosphorylation?
A. The hydrolysis of adenosine triphosphate...
B. The synthesis of adenosine triphosphate from organic base, ribose and phosphoric acid.
C. The regeneration of adenosine triphosphate from adenosine diphosphate and phosphorylcreatine.
D. The breakdown of adenosine triphosphate to adenosine diphosphate and inorganic phosphate.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B. The synthesis of adenosine triphosphate... (See explanation)

Explanation: This question has poor options. Oxidative phosphorylation is the synthesis of ATP from ADP and Inorganic Phosphate using energy from the electron transport chain. Option B ("Synthesis of ATP") is the closest general description of the *outcome* (making ATP), even though the substrates listed (organic base + ribose + acid) describe *de novo* synthesis rather than phosphorylation. However, options A and D describe breakdown (hydrolysis), and C describes Substrate Level Phosphorylation (Creatine Phosphate system). Thus B is the only option describing "making ATP".

20.
What is the role of myosin light chain kinase?
A. It activates Ca2+-Calmodulin complex.
B. It activates myosin cross-bridges.
C. It phosphorylates myosin cross-bridges.
D. It phosphorylates myosin-actin complex.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B. It activates myosin cross-bridges. (Or C)

Explanation: MLCK phosphorylates the regulatory light chain of myosin (C), which enables the myosin head to interact with actin (activation of cross-bridges, B). Both B and C are chemically correct, but "activating" (B) describes the physiological role.

21.
Six children... marasmic-kwashiorkor... How did the high protein diet help in managing the oedema?

43.
Write short notes on the cardiac cycle under the following sub-headings (5 marks)
A. Definition (1 mark)
B. Phases of the cardiac cycle (3 marks)
C. Physiological importance (1 mark).
Show Answer

A. Definition:

The cardiac cycle refers to the sequence of mechanical and electrical events that occur during one complete heartbeat, consisting of systole (contraction) and diastole (relaxation).

B. Phases of the cardiac cycle:

  1. Atrial Systole: Atria contract, pushing blood into ventricles.
  2. Isovolumetric Contraction: Ventricles contract, valves close (S1), pressure rises.
  3. Ventricular Ejection: Semilunar valves open, blood ejected.
  4. Isovolumetric Relaxation: Ventricles relax, valves close (S2).
  5. Rapid/Passive Filling: AV valves open, blood fills ventricles.

C. Physiological importance:

Ensures the efficient, unidirectional pumping of blood to the lungs and body tissues to maintain adequate perfusion and pressure.
