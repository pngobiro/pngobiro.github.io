REHABILITATION AND SUMMARY

Rehabilitation Principles

Rehabilitation is a crucial component of orthopaedic care that focuses on restoring function, preventing complications, and helping patients return to their normal activities of daily living.

Key Rehabilitation Principles:

  • Early Mobilization: Initiate movement as soon as medically safe to prevent complications
  • Pain Management: Adequate pain control to facilitate participation in therapy
  • Progressive Loading: Gradual increase in activity and weight-bearing as healing progresses
  • Patient Education: Teaching patients about their condition and rehabilitation process
  • Multidisciplinary Approach: Involvement of physical therapists, occupational therapists, and other specialists

Common Rehabilitation Techniques:

  • Range of motion exercises
  • Strengthening exercises
  • Gait training
  • Balance and coordination training
  • Functional training

Course Summary

This Orthopaedic Surgery course has covered comprehensive content including:

Core Topics Covered:

  1. Principles of Orthopaedics: Basic definitions, fracture classifications, and diagnostic approaches
  2. Fracture Management: Treatment principles including reduction, immobilization, and rehabilitation
  3. Open Fractures: Gustilo classification and emergency management protocols
  4. Fracture Complications: General and local complications including compartment syndrome
  5. Upper Limb Injuries: Shoulder, elbow, wrist, and hand fractures and dislocations
  6. Lower Limb Injuries: Hip, femur, knee, ankle, and foot trauma management
  7. Bone and Joint Infections: Osteomyelitis and tuberculosis recognition and treatment
  8. Bone Tumors: Benign and malignant lesions with their management approaches
  9. Peripheral Nerve Injuries: Nerve anatomy, injury mechanisms, and treatment strategies
  10. Congenital Deformities: Developmental conditions and their management

Key Learning Points

✓ Essential Clinical Concepts

  • ABC Approach: Always assess Airway, Breathing, and Circulation first in trauma patients
  • Golden Period: Open fractures require urgent debridement within 6-8 hours
  • Compartment Syndrome: High index of suspicion with pain out of proportion to injury
  • Neurovascular Assessment: Essential in all extremity injuries
  • Age-Appropriate Management: Children's bones have different healing properties

Critical Time Intervals:

  • Shoulder dislocation reduction: <24 hours to prevent AVN
  • Supra-condylar fracture: Absolute emergency
  • Compartment syndrome: Fasciotomy within 4-6 hours
  • Open fracture debridement: 6-8 hours optimal
