Course Description

ORTHOPAEDICS is a branch of surgery concerned with conditions involving the musculoskeletal system like musculoskeletal trauma, spine diseases, sports injuries, degenerative diseases, infections, tumors, and congenital disorders.

Table of Contents

  1. 1. Principles of Orthopaedics Basic definitions, fracture types, and diagnostic approaches
  2. 2. Principles of Fracture Management Treatment principles, reduction, immobilization, and rehabilitation
  3. 3. Open Fractures & Fracture Complications Gustilo classification, emergency management, and complications
  4. 4. Fractures & Dislocations of Upper Limb Shoulder, elbow, wrist, and hand injuries
  5. 5. Fractures & Dislocations of Lower Limb Hip, femur, knee, ankle, and foot trauma management
  6. 6. Bone and Joint Infections & Tuberculosis Osteomyelitis and tuberculosis recognition and treatment
  7. 7. Bone Tumors Benign and malignant lesions with their management approaches
  8. 8. Peripheral Nerve Injury Nerve anatomy, injury mechanisms, and treatment strategies
  9. 9. Congenital Deformities Developmental conditions including DDH, clubfoot, and Perthes disease
  10. 10. Rehabilitation and Summary Rehabilitation principles and course summary

Learning Objectives

By the end of this course, students should be able to:

  • Understand the fundamental principles of orthopaedic surgery
  • Recognize and classify various types of fractures
  • Apply appropriate treatment protocols for different injuries
  • Identify complications and manage them appropriately
  • Understand the principles of rehabilitation in orthopaedic care
