Section 1: Multiple Choice Questions

40 Marks
1.
The surgical term for excision of an organ starts with the name of the organ to be excised and ends with the suffix
a) -otomy
b) -oplasty
c) -ostomy
d) -ectomy
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) -ectomy

-ectomy = removal (e.g., Appendectomy). -otomy = incision into. -ostomy = creating an opening. -oplasty = repair/reconstruction.

2.
A 25-year-old female patient with moderately enlarged thyroid gland is suspected to have hyperthyroidism. She is likely to have all the following features except
a) Bradycardia
b) Palpitations
c) Fine tremors in the hands
d) Exophthalmos
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Bradycardia

Hyperthyroidism typically causes Tachycardia (fast heart rate) due to increased adrenergic tone. Bradycardia is a feature of Hypothyroidism.

3.
Which one of the following statements about spinal column injuries is untrue?
a) Significant disruption of the thoracic spine requires major energy transfer and associated visceral and vascular injuries are common
b) The thoracolumbar junction is quite flexible and is thus not prone to injury
c) Upper cervical cord injury is uncommon as the canal is spacious
d) The cervico-thoracic junction is a transitional zone, as is thus prone to injury
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) The thoracolumbar junction is quite flexible and is thus not prone to injury

This statement is Untrue. The thoracolumbar junction (T11-L2) is a transition zone between the rigid thoracic spine and mobile lumbar spine, making it highly susceptible to injury (most common site of spinal fractures).

4.
A 5 year old boy presents with severe pain in his leg just below the knee... fever 39Â°C... markedly tender. No crepitus. Which one of the following actions will you carry out first for this patient?
a) Give high dose intravenous antibiotics immediately to neutralize any infection
b) Administer intravenous fluids immediately
c) Take blood sample for culture and sensitivity then start on antibiotics
d) Send the patient for x-ray of the limb immediately to confirm the diagnosis
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Take blood sample for culture and sensitivity then start on antibiotics

This is classic Acute Hematogenous Osteomyelitis. The priority is to obtain cultures (blood) to identify the organism before starting empirical antibiotics. X-rays are often normal in the first 10-14 days.

6.
A 28 year old male patient weighing 60 kg... burns of his whole anterior trunk... Using Parkland's formula, how much fluid will you administer to him over the 24 hour period?
a) 2400 ml
b) 4320 ml
c) 2160 ml
d) 4800 ml
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) 4320 ml

Calculation:
TBSA = Anterior Trunk (Chest 9% + Abdomen 9%) = 18%.
Formula = 4 mL x Weight x TBSA
Volume = 4 x 60 x 18 = 4,320 mL.

13.
A young adult male patient with some septic spots on the legs... pain in his thigh... fever... markedly tender and indurated. Aspiration... produces pus. The most likely diagnosis is;
a) Cellulitis
b) Pyomyositis
c) Carbuncle
d) Necrotizing fasciitis
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Pyomyositis

Pyomyositis (Tropical Myositis) is a bacterial infection of skeletal muscle leading to pus formation (abscess) deep within the muscle (quadriceps), often in tropical regions. It presents with fever, pain, and woody induration.

19.
An elderly female patient... Bence Jones protein in urine. Bone X-ray shows punched out osteolytic lesions in her skull, ribs and pelvic bones. Her condition is consistent with the diagnosis of;
a) metastatic cancer of the breast
b) krukenberg tumour
c) multiple myeloma
d) osteogenic sarcoma
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) multiple myeloma

Classic triad for Multiple Myeloma: Bence Jones proteinuria, Punched-out lytic bone lesions (skull/pelvis), and Marrow plasmacytosis (often with hypercalcemia/anemia in elderly).

21.
An adult male patient has a painless swelling in his groin... finger over his internal inguinal ring, and it restrains the swelling from protruding when the patient stands. The most likely diagnosis is;
a) femoral hernia
b) direct inguinal hernia
c) strangulated hernia
d) indirect inguinal hernia
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) indirect inguinal hernia

Deep Ring Occlusion Test: If the hernia is controlled by occluding the deep (internal) ring, it is an Indirect Inguinal Hernia (which passes through the deep ring). A direct hernia would still bulge through Hesselbach's triangle medial to the occluding finger.

26.
In a child with hydrocephalus, a CT scan finding showing dilated lateral and third ventricles with normal fourth ventricle, indicates the cause of the hydrocephalus to be;
a) stenosis of foramina of Munro
b) cerebrospinal fluid overproduction
c) stenosis of foramina of Magendie and Luschika
d) stenosis of aqueduct of Sylvius
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) stenosis of aqueduct of Sylvius

The Aqueduct of Sylvius connects the 3rd and 4th ventricles. Obstruction here causes dilation upstream (Lateral + 3rd) while the 4th ventricle remains normal size (non-communicating hydrocephalus).

29.
A cause of functional intestinal obstruction is;
a) hernia
b) spinal injury
c) ascaris lumbricoides infestation
d) adhesions
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) spinal injury

Functional obstruction (Paralytic Ileus/Pseudo-obstruction) is due to loss of peristalsis without a mechanical blockage. Spinal injury causes neurogenic ileus. Hernias, Ascaris, and Adhesions are causes of mechanical obstruction.

39.
A 22-year-old male presents with severe pain in the right-lower quadrant of his abdomen. Which of the following differential diagnosis would you not enlist?
a) acute appendicitis
b) acute salpingitis
c) acute ureteric colic
d) Meckel's diverticulitis
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) acute salpingitis

Salpingitis is inflammation of the fallopian tubes, which occurs in females. The patient is a 22-year-old male.

40.
"Onion Peel" appearance on X-ray of a long bone in a patient with painful swelling of his limb is characteristic of;
a) osteomyelitis
b) osteosarcoma
c) giant cell tumor
d) Ewing's sarcoma
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Ewing's sarcoma

Ewing's Sarcoma classically presents with a multi-layered periosteal reaction resembling an "onion peel" or "onion skin". Osteosarcoma typically shows a "sunburst" appearance.

Section 2: Short Answer Questions

40 Marks
1.
Briefly describe the clinical presentation of infantile hypertrophic pyloric stenosis, highlighting at least ten features.
Show Answer

Answer:

  1. Non-bilious vomiting: The obstruction is proximal to the ampulla of Vater.
  2. Projectile vomiting: Forceful expulsion of gastric contents.
  3. Onset at 2-8 weeks: Typically presents in the first few months of life.
  4. Hungry vomiter: Infant is eager to feed immediately after vomiting.
  5. Visible gastric peristalsis: Waves moving from left to right across the epigastrium.
  6. Palpable "Olive" mass: Firm, mobile mass in the right upper quadrant (pylorus).
  7. Weight loss / Failure to thrive: Due to inability to retain food.
  8. Dehydration: Sunken fontanelle, poor skin turgor.
  9. Constipation: Due to lack of food reaching the bowel.
  10. Hypochloremic Hypokalemic Metabolic Alkalosis: Due to loss of gastric acid (HCl).
2.
List five (5) post-operative complications of thyroidectomy.
Show Answer

Answer:

  • Hemorrhage/Haematoma: Can cause airway compression (Emergency).
  • Recurrent Laryngeal Nerve Injury: Hoarseness (unilateral) or Airway obstruction (bilateral).
  • Hypocalcemia (Tetany): Due to damage/removal of Parathyroid glands.
  • Thyroid Storm (Thyrotoxic crisis): If patient was not euthyroid pre-op.
  • Wound Infection.
  • Hypothyroidism: Long-term consequence.

6.
Highlight five (5) key clinical features of tension pneumothorax.
Show Answer

Answer:

  • Severe respiratory distress / Air hunger.
  • Tracheal deviation away from the affected side.
  • Distended neck veins (Elevated JVP).
  • Hyper-resonance on percussion of affected side.
  • Absent breath sounds on affected side.
  • Hypotension / Shock (Obstructive).

Section 3: Long Answer Question

20 Marks
1.

Following a road traffic accident involving a head-on collision, a passenger in one of the vehicles sustains an injury you diagnose as posterior dislocation of the hip.

i) Describe the mechanisms of injury of this patient's hip

ii) Describe the patient's clinical presentation

iii) Describe the X-ray findings for this injury

iv) Describe the treatment of this patient's conditions

v) State the possible complications associated with this patient's injury

Show Answer

Answer:

i) Mechanism of Injury (Dashboard Injury):

Occurs when a significant force is applied to the knee while the hip is flexed and adducted (e.g., knee hitting the dashboard in a head-on collision). The force drives the femoral head posteriorly out of the acetabulum.

ii) Clinical Presentation:

  • Severe pain in the hip region.
  • Limb is Shortened.
  • Limb is Adducted (crossed over other leg).
  • Limb is Internally Rotated.
  • Palpable femoral head in the gluteal region (sometimes).

iii) X-ray Findings (AP Pelvis):

  • Femoral head seen outside the acetabulum, usually superolaterally to the acetabular roof.
  • Femoral head appears smaller than the contralateral side (due to being further from the film).
  • Loss of Shenton's line continuity.
  • Possible associated fracture of the posterior acetabular wall.

iv) Treatment:

  • Emergency Reduction: Closed reduction under General Anaesthesia or deep sedation/muscle relaxation.
  • Methods: Allis maneuver, Bigelow maneuver, or Stimson's gravity method.
  • Post-reduction: Confirm stability and concentric reduction with X-ray/CT.
  • Immobilization: Skin or skeletal traction for a period (e.g., 2-6 weeks) to allow capsule healing.
  • Open Reduction if closed fails or if there are intra-articular fragments.

v) Complications:

  • Sciatic Nerve Injury: (Foot drop) - nerve runs posteriorly.
  • Avascular Necrosis (AVN) of Femoral Head: High risk due to medial circumflex artery damage.
  • Post-traumatic Osteoarthritis: Secondary to cartilage damage.
  • Recurrent Dislocation.
  • Myositis Ossificans.
