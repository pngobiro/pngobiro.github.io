Section 1: Multiple Choice Questions

40 Marks
1.
A reason for documenting patient's full names in clerkship is to:
a) make a diagnosis
b) maintain records
c) formulate treatment plan
d) assess economic status
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) maintain records

Patient identification is crucial for medical records management and legal purposes. It also helps in establishing rapport.

2.
In taking a surgical history
a) Past medical history is irrelevant
b) leading questions are prohibited
c) use of medical terms is advocates in history of presenting illness
d) occupational history is important
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) occupational history is important

Occupational history helps identify risk factors (e.g., heavy lifting for hernias, prolonged standing for varicose veins, chemical exposure for cancers). PMH is very relevant. Leading questions should generally be avoided but aren't strictly prohibited if clarification is needed. HPI should use patient's terms where possible.

3.
The following surgical conditions run in families EXCEPT
a) hypertension
b) haemorrhoids
c) peptic ulcer
d) cancer of thyroid gland
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) peptic ulcer

Note: This is a common past paper question. While blood group O is a genetic risk factor for PUD, and H. pylori can spread in families, hypertension, haemorrhoids (connective tissue weakness), and certain thyroid cancers (Medullary/MEN syndromes) have stronger recognized genetic links in this context. Some sources might argue Hypertension is medical, but it is often listed as a risk factor.

4.
The best method of palpating the kidney is
a) light palpation
b) deep palpation
c) Bimanual palpation
d) Bidigital palpation
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Bimanual palpation

The kidney is a retroperitoneal organ. Bimanual palpation (one hand anterior, one posterior) allows the examiner to feel the kidney "ballot" between the hands.

5.
Definitive investigation in a patient with haematemesis is
a) plain abdominal x-ray
b) blood for haemoglobin levels
c) oesophagogastroduodenoscopy
d) barium follow through
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) oesophagogastroduodenoscopy

OGD (Upper GI Endoscopy) visualizes the bleeding source directly and allows for therapeutic intervention (banding, injection, clipping).

6.
Which of the following medical condition is associated with increased risk of perioperative infections
a) bronchial asthma
b) diabetes mellitus
c) hypertension
d) epilepsy
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) diabetes mellitus

Hyperglycemia impairs neutrophil function (chemotaxis and phagocytosis), significantly increasing the risk of Surgical Site Infections (SSI).

7.
Surgery performed to correct a non-life threatening condition is referred to as;
a) Exploratory surgery
b) cosmetic surgery
c) elective surgery
d) extirpation
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) elective surgery

Elective surgery is planned in advance for a condition that is not immediately life-threatening. Cosmetic surgery is a subtype, but "elective" is the broad term for non-emergency correction.

8.
Which of the following drug is given preoperatively to prevent emesis
a) loperanide
b) adrenaline
c) atropine
d) metoclopramide
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) metoclopramide

Metoclopramide is an antiemetic and prokinetic agent. Atropine is an antisialagogue (reduces secretions). Loperamide is an antidiarrheal.

9.
Post-operative management of a diabetic patient entails the following EXCEPT
a) frequent monitoring of blood glucose levels
b) immediate removal of urinary catheter
c) continued insulin glucose infusion for at least 24 hours
d) close monitoring of the wound
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) immediate removal of urinary catheter

Diabetic patients are prone to autonomic neuropathy which can cause urinary retention. While early removal is good to prevent UTI, "immediate" might be contraindicated if there is retention risk. However, strict monitoring (a), GKI infusion (c), and wound care (d) are standard mandatory protocols. Option B is the exception or variable.

10.
A middle aged man presents with diffuse swelling of the left leg associated with pain following a thorn prick. He has a temperature of 39.0Â°C. Which is the most likely diagnosis
a) abscess
b) erysipelas
c) cellulitis
d) furuncullitis
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) cellulitis

Cellulitis is a spreading bacterial infection of the skin and subcutaneous tissue, often following minor trauma (thorn prick), characterized by diffuse swelling, redness, pain, and fever.

12.
A female patient weighing 60 kgs is admitted with 35% burns. Using the Parklands formula, how much fluid should she be infused in the first 8 hours.
a) 8400 mls
b) 2100 mls
c) 4200 mls
d) 7200 mls
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) 4200 mls

Total fluid = 4 mL x Weight (kg) x %TBSA
Total = 4 x 60 x 35 = 8,400 mL.
First 8 hours = 50% of total = 4,200 mL.

18.
The following is a form of a dynamic intestinal obstruction
a) Bezoar
b) paralytic ileus
c) meconium
d) enteroliths
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) paralytic ileus (See Note)

Correction Note: This question likely contains a typo in the question or expected answer. "Dynamic" obstruction usually refers to mechanical obstruction (where peristalsis works against a blockage, e.g., bezoar, stone). "Adynamic" refers to Paralytic Ileus. If the question asks for "Dynamic", A, C, and D are all forms. If it meant "Adynamic", then B is the answer. In single-best-answer exams with multiple "dynamic" options and one "adynamic" option, the unique one (Paralytic Ileus) is often the intended answer to a malformed question like "The following is a form of adynamic...".

20.
Which one of the following is a characteristic of a direct inguinal hernia
a) commonly extends to the scrotum
b) reduces spontaneously on lying down
c) more prone to strangulation
d) recurrence after surgery is rare
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) reduces spontaneously on lying down

Direct hernias have a wide neck (Hesselbach's triangle) and reduce easily. They rarely strangulate compared to indirect hernias.

24.
A head injury patient who opens eyes to pain, utters inappropriate words and has abnormal flexion of the limbs is considered to have a Glasgow coma scale of
a) 8
b) 6
c) 9
d) 10
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) 8

Eye Opening (Pain) = 2
Verbal (Inappropriate Words) = 3
Motor (Abnormal Flexion/Decorticate) = 3
Total = 2 + 3 + 3 = 8.

29.
The most appropriate method of treating a severely displaced fracture of the olecranon process is
a) tension band wiring
b) external fixation
c) intramedullary nail
d) functional brace
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) tension band wiring

Tension band wiring converts the distracting force of the triceps muscle into a compressive force at the fracture site, promoting healing.

35.
Soap bubble appearance on x-ray is a feature of
a) Ewing's sarcoma
b) chacro sarcoma
c) osteogenic sarcoma
d) giant cell tumour
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) giant cell tumour

The "Soap Bubble" appearance is the classic radiological description of an osteoclastoma (Giant Cell Tumour), usually found in the epiphysis of long bones.

3.
List five medical indications of male circumcision
Show Answer

Answer:

  1. Phimosis: Inability to retract foreskin (pathological).
  2. Paraphimosis: Retracted foreskin cannot be returned (Emergency).
  3. Recurrent Balanitis/Balanoposthitis: Infection of glans/foreskin.
  4. HIV Prevention: Reduces risk of heterosexually acquired HIV (VMMC).
  5. UTI Prevention: In infants with recurrent UTIs.
  6. Cancer Prevention: Reduces risk of penile cancer and cervical cancer in partners (HPV).
5.
Outline treatment of a patient with tension pneumothorax
Show Answer

Answer:

  • Immediate Decompression: Insert a large-bore needle/cannula (14G) into the 2nd intercostal space, mid-clavicular line (or 5th ICS mid-axillary line per new ATLS) to convert tension to simple pneumothorax.
  • Definitive Treatment: Insert an Intercostal Chest Tube (Underwater Seal Drainage) in the 5th intercostal space, mid-axillary line.
  • Oxygen Therapy: Administer high-flow oxygen.
  • Analgesia: Pain management.

Section 3: Long Answer Question

20 Marks
1.

A 60-year-old male patient presents to you with history of inability to pass urine for the last 8 hours. On examination, he is restless with a tender suprapubic mass.

a) What is your diagnosis

b) List five causes of this condition

c) Outline the management of this condition

Show Answer

Answer:

a) Diagnosis: Acute Urinary Retention (AUR).

b) Causes:

  • Benign Prostatic Hyperplasia (BPH) - most common in elderly men.
  • Urethral Stricture (post-infection/trauma).
  • Carcinoma of the Prostate.
  • Clot Retention (from haematuria).
  • Bladder Neck Stenosis.
  • Neurogenic Bladder (spinal injury, diabetes).
  • Drugs (Anticholinergics, antihistamines).

c) Management:

Immediate:

  • Catheterization: Attempt urethral catheterization (Foley's).
  • If urethral catheterization fails, perform Suprapubic Puncture/Cystostomy (SPC) to relieve bladder distension.
  • Monitor urine output (volume drained immediately).
  • Analgesia for pain relief.

Definitive/Work-up:

  • Urinalysis and Culture (treat infection).
  • Urea & Electrolytes (Check for renal impairment/Obstructive uropathy).
  • Pelvic/Prostate Ultrasound.
  • Start Alpha-blockers (e.g., Tamsulosin) for BPH (Trial Without Catheter - TWOC - later).
  • Surgery (e.g., TURP) if BPH is confirmed and medical management fails.
