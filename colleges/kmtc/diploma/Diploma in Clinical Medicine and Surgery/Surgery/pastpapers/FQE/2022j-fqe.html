Section 1: Multiple Choice Questions

40 Marks
1.
One of the reasons of recording patients' full name in clerkship is to;
a) Make a diagnosis
b) Establish rapport
c) Assess social economic status
d) Predilection of diseases
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Establish rapport

While identification and record-keeping are primary, addressing a patient by name is a key step in establishing rapport. Predilection of diseases (d) relates more to age, gender, and ethnicity (which a name might suggest, but is not the primary reason for recording the name itself).

2.
Which of the following best describes a surgical procedure performed to aid or confirm a diagnosis?
a) Relantation
b) Extirpation
c) Exploratory surgery
d) Transplant surgery
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Exploratory surgery

Exploratory surgery (e.g., exploratory laparotomy) is performed to visualize the body interior and confirm a diagnosis when other methods fail.

3.
Which of the following refers to surgical fusion of a joint?
a) Arthrodesis
b) Arthroplasty
c) Arthroscopy
d) Arthrography
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Arthrodesis

  • Arthrodesis: Surgical fusion of a joint.
  • Arthroplasty: Joint replacement or reconstruction.
  • Arthroscopy: Visual examination of a joint.
4.
Fixation of a testis after untwisting of testicular torsion is referred to as;
a) Orchidectomy
b) Orchideplasty
c) Orchidopexy
d) Ochiectomy
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Orchidopexy

Orchidopexy is the surgical fixation of the testis to the scrotal wall to prevent torsion or treat undescended testis. Orchidectomy is removal.

5.
In the 'Ebb and Flow' model, ebb phase is characterized by;
a) Hypovolaemia
b) Increased basal metabolic rate
c) Leucocystosis
d) Increased cardiac output
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Hypovolaemia

The Ebb phase (immediate response to injury) is characterized by "shock" features: hypovolemia, decreased metabolic rate, and decreased cardiac output. The Flow phase follows with hypermetabolism.

6.
Metabolic stress response to surgery can be reduced by;
a) Administering steroids
b) Enhancing bed rest
c) Adequate analgesic
d) Maximizing pre-operative fasting.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Adequate analgesic

Pain is a major driver of the neuroendocrine stress response. Adequate pain relief (analgesia), along with minimal invasive surgery and avoiding prolonged fasting (ERAS protocols), helps attenuate the stress response.

7.
Pre-operative sedation in an anxious patient can be achieved by administering
a) Atopine
b) Cimetidine
c) Glycopyrronium
d) Benzodiazepines
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Benzodiazepines

Benzodiazepines (e.g., Midazolam, Diazepam) are anxiolytics used for premedication/sedation. Atropine/Glycopyrronium are anticholinergics (dry secretions). Cimetidine is an H2 blocker (acid reduction).

8.
Secondary post-operative haemorrhage is caused by;
a) Fall in blood pressure
b) Infection
c) Vessel cut during surgery
d) Prolonged bed rest
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Infection

Secondary hemorrhage occurs typically 7-10 days post-operatively due to infection eroding a vessel. Primary is during surgery; Reactionary is within 24 hours (due to BP rise/slippage of ligature).

9.
Concerning wound dehiscence;
a) Occurs from 1st to 4th post-operative day.
b) Steroids have a role in its management
c) Is associated with skin colour
d) Post-surgical haematoma is a risk factor
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Post-surgical haematoma is a risk factor

Hematomas can increase tension on the wound edges and serve as a nidus for infection, leading to dehiscence. It typically occurs around day 5-10 (when collagen is weak), not day 1-4. Steroids delay healing and increase risk.

10.
General anaesthetic triad comprises which of the following?
a) Pain relief, amnesia, muscle relaxation
b) Unconsciousness, pain relief and muscle relaxation
c) Amnesia, unconsciousness and muscle relaxation
d) Immobility, amnesia, unconsciousness
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Unconsciousness, pain relief and muscle relaxation

The "Triad of General Anesthesia" traditionally includes: Hypnosis (Unconsciousness/Amnesia), Analgesia (Pain relief), and Muscle Relaxation.

11.
Which of the following local anaesthetic drug is an amide?
a) Lidocaine
b) Cocaine
c) Tetracaine
d) Benzocaine
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Lidocaine

Mnemonic: Amides have an 'i' before the 'caine' (Lidocaine, Bupivacaine). Esters generally do not (Cocaine, Procaine, Tetracaine, Benzocaine).

12.
Low grade fever within the first 24 hours after surgery is caused by;
a) Wound infection
b) Release of inflammatory medications
c) Cannula infection
d) Deep venous thrombosis
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Release of inflammatory medications

Fever in the first 24 hours is usually physiological/metabolic response to surgical trauma (release of cytokines/inflammatory mediators) or atelectasis. Wound infection typically appears after day 3. Note: "medications" in option b likely refers to "mediators".

13.
Which of the following is true concerning a diabetic patient scheduled for an elective surgery?
a) Should be admitted one day prior to surgery
b) Fasting period should be minimized
c) Insulin is contra indicated
d) Oral hypoglycaemic drugs should be continued up to the day of operation.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Should be admitted one day prior to surgery

Diabetics are often admitted early for stabilization of blood glucose. Oral hypoglycemics are usually stopped on the morning of surgery (or 24-48h before for some like Metformin/Sulfonylureas). Insulin is used (sliding scale/infusion).

14.
Fracture mid shaft left femur in a 13-year-old male patient is managed by;
a) Internal fixation
b) Skeletal traction
c) Application of PoP hip spica
d) Balanced skin traction
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Skeletal traction

While management is evolving (flexible nails often used), in many traditional/resource-limited settings, conservative management for a 13-year-old often starts with skeletal traction (skin traction is insufficient for weight/muscle mass) followed by casting, or internal fixation. Skeletal traction is a classic appropriate conservative answer.

15.
Pain that presents most of the times and requires continuous treatment is graded as
a) Grade II
b) Grade IV
c) Grade I
d) Grade III
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Grade III

Assuming a standard 4-point descriptive scale (0=None, 1=Mild, 2=Moderate, 3=Severe), persistent pain requiring continuous treatment typically corresponds to Severe (Grade III) pain.

16.
Avascular necrosis is likely to occur in the following fracture;
a) Monteggia fracture
b) Bennett's fracture
c) Fracture scaphoid
d) Lateral molleollar fracture
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Fracture scaphoid

The scaphoid bone (and femoral neck/talus) has a retrograde blood supply, making it highly susceptible to avascular necrosis (AVN) following fracture, especially of the proximal pole.

17.
In acute osteomyelitis;
a) X-ray features are evident in the first three days of infections
b) Commonest cause is streptococcus pyogens
c) Heals spontaneously
d) Antibiotics are administered for more than two weeks
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Antibiotics are administered for more than two weeks

Treatment requires prolonged antibiotics (usually 4-6 weeks) to penetrate bone. X-ray changes appear late (10-14 days). Staphylococcus aureus is the most common cause.

18.
Which of the following is a feature of sun burst spiculation on an x-ray?
a) Chronic osteomyelitis
b) Osteogenic sarcoma
c) Multiple myeloma
d) Ewings’ sarcoma
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Osteogenic sarcoma

"Sunburst" appearance (and Codman's triangle) is characteristic of Osteosarcoma (Osteogenic Sarcoma). Ewing's sarcoma typically shows "Onion skinning".

19.
In management of open fractures;
a) Surgical toilet and debridement
b) Antibiotics are started after debridement
c) Wound cover in Gustillo IIIB is done using local tissues
d) External fixation is preferred for fracture stabilization.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Surgical toilet and debridement

Thorough debridement (removal of devitalized tissue/debris) is the single most important step to prevent infection. Antibiotics should be started immediately upon presentation. External fixation is useful, but debridement is the cornerstone. Note: Gustilo IIIB requires flap coverage (local or free), as local tissue is insufficient.

20.
A 24-year-old male patient presents in casualty with pain and inability to use his left shoulder joint following a fall on outstretched hand.
a) Request for an x-ray
b) Perform physical examination
c) Refer to an orthopedic surgeon
d) Admit in surgical ward
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Perform physical examination

Standard clinical practice: History -> Examination -> Investigations. Examination determines the need for X-ray and checks for neurovascular status.

21.
An x-ray of a 75 year old female patient with history of severe pain and stiffness of her right knee joint reveals reduced joint space, multiple osteophytes and genu valgum deformity. What is the diagnosis?
a) Tuberculosis arthritis
b) Gouty arthritis
c) Pyogenic arthritis
d) Osteoarthritis
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Osteoarthritis

Classic radiographic features of Osteoarthritis (OA): LOSS - Loss of joint space, Osteophytes, Subchondral sclerosis, Subchondral cysts. Age (75y) is also a strong risk factor.

22.
Epiphyseal (Salter Harris) injury with fracture of the articular surface with extension across the growth plate into metaphysisis is classified as;
a) Type IV
b) Type V
c) Type III
d) Type II
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Type IV

Salter-Harris IV: Fracture passes Through everything (Metaphysis, Physis, and Epiphysis).
Type II (Above) involves metaphysis + physis. Type III (Lower) involves epiphysis + physis.

23.
The following test is performed to detect tear of anterior cruciate ligament:
a) Pivot shift test
b) Posterior drawers test
c) Barrows test
d) Patella tap
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Pivot shift test

Specific tests for ACL tear include the Pivot Shift Test, Lachman's Test, and Anterior Drawer Test. Posterior Drawer is for PCL.

24.
Which one of the following is an x-ray feature of rickets?
a) Cortical thickening
b) Narrowing and cupping of metaphysis
c) Narrowing of the growth plate
d) Widening and fanning of metaphysis
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Widening and fanning of metaphysis

Rickets is characterized by accumulation of unmineralized osteoid, leading to widening of the growth plate and cupping/fraying/splaying of the metaphysis.

25.
The following is a developmental vascular disease that affects proximal femoral epiphysis:
a) Slipped upper femoral epiphysis
b) Perthe's disease
c) Osteogenesis imperfect
d) Congenital dislocation of the hip joint
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Perthe's disease

Legg-Calvé-Perthes disease is idiopathic avascular necrosis of the proximal femoral epiphysis in children.

26.
Typical deformities of the foot in congenital club foot;
a) Eversion, adduction, and plantar flexion
b) Abduction, adduction, and plantar flexion
c) Inversion, adduction and dorsi-flexion
d) Inversion, adduction and plantar flexion
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Inversion, adduction and plantar flexion

Clubfoot (Talipes Equinovarus) acronym CAVE: Cavus (midfoot), Adduction (forefoot), Varus (hindfoot/Inversion), Equinus (ankle/Plantar flexion).

27.
Which one of the following is an indication for emergency laparotomy?
a) Haematemesis in peptic ulcer disease
b) Cholelithiasis
c) Pancreatitis
d) Diaphragmatic rupture
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Diaphragmatic rupture

Traumatic rupture of the diaphragm requires surgical repair (laparotomy or thoracotomy) to prevent herniation and strangulation of viscera. The other conditions are often managed conservatively or endoscopically/electively initially.

28.
Which of the following is a 'mural' cause of intestinal obstruction?
a) Bezoar
b) Cystic fibrosis
c) Volvulus
d) Adhesions
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Volvulus (See Note)

Note: Standard classification is Intraluminal (Bezoar), Mural/Intramural (Tumor, Stricture), Extramural (Adhesions, Volvulus). However, Volvulus involves the bowel wall and mesentery twisting. Of the options, Adhesions are extrinsic. Bezoar is intraluminal. Cystic fibrosis causes meconium ileus (intraluminal). "Mural" refers to lesions in the wall. This question may have a specific context or typo. Bezoar is the best distractor for "luminal". Volvulus is structural. (Adhesions are the most common cause overall). In some contexts, congenital defects (like in CF) might be linked, but Volvulus is structural/mural integrity issue.

29.
Which of the following is true about small bowel obstruction?
a) Present with lower abdominal colic
b) Is commonly of a sudden onset
c) Vomiting is a late presentation
d) Barium enema is the preferred investigative modality
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Is commonly of a sudden onset

Small bowel obstruction (e.g., from adhesions or hernia) typically presents acutely (sudden onset) with central colicky pain and early vomiting. (Lower abdominal pain suggests large bowel. Vomiting is early in SBO, late in LBO).

30.
Concerning post hepatic (obstructive) jaundice;
a) Urine contains urobilinogens
b) Liver enzymes are within normal values
c) Conjugated serum bilirubin are elevated
d) Alkaline phosphatase levels are reduced
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Conjugated serum bilirubin are elevated

In biliary obstruction, conjugated bilirubin cannot be excreted into the gut, so it regurgitates into blood (Elevated Conjugated Bilirubin) and urine (Dark urine). Urobilinogen is absent in urine (Complete obstruction). Alk Phos is typically elevated.

31.
The following is a clinical feature of superficial second degree burns;
a) Waxy white appearance
b) Blisters
c) Absent capillary return on pressure
d) Leathery texture
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Blisters

Superficial partial-thickness (2nd degree) burns are characterized by blisters, moisture, redness, and severe pain.

32.
A chronic facial ulcer with raised edges is most likely;
a) Tuberculous ulcer
b) Rodent's ulcer
c) Syphilitic ulcer
d) Healing ulcer
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Rodent's ulcer

Basal Cell Carcinoma (Rodent Ulcer) classically presents as an ulcer with a raised, rolled, pearly edge, usually on the face.

33.
One of the following is true about carotid body tumor (chemodectoma):
a) Fine needle aspirate cytology is contraindicated
b) Commonly occurs in teenagers
c) Surgery is contraindicated
d) Is a differential diagnosis of posterior neck swelling
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Fine needle aspirate cytology is contraindicated

Due to high vascularity and location at the carotid bifurcation, biopsy/FNAC is contraindicated (risk of hemorrhage). Diagnosis is by imaging (angiography/Doppler). It is an Anterior Triangle mass.

34.
A head injury patient who was fully alert... developed rapid neurological deterioration... one hour after admission. What is the most likely diagnosis?
a) Acute subdural haematoma
b) Acute epidural haematoma
c) Subarachnoid haemorrhage
d) Intraventricular haemorrhage
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Acute epidural haematoma

The classic presentation of an Epidural (Extradural) Haematoma is the Lucid Interval: a period of consciousness between the initial concussion and subsequent rapid deterioration due to expanding arterial bleed (Middle Meningeal Artery).

35.
Primary brain injury can occur due to;
a) Hypoxia
b) Hypotension
c) Meningitis
d) Penetrating injury
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Penetrating injury

Primary injury is the mechanical damage occurring at the moment of impact (e.g., contusion, laceration from penetrating object). Hypoxia and hypotension cause secondary brain injury.

36.

a) Cellulitis
b) Sebaceous cysts

Correct Answer: b) Sebaceous cysts

Sebaceous cysts are common in the scrotal skin/subcutaneous tissue. Haematocele is in the tunica vaginalis. Epidermoid cancer is testicular.

37.
Which one of the following is an irritative symptom in a patient with benign prostatic hypertrophy?
a) Hesitancy
b) Dribbling
c) Urge incontinence
d) Intermittent stream

Correct Answer: c) Urge incontinence

Irritative (Storage) Symptoms: Frequency, Urgency, Nocturia, Urge Incontinence.

38.
Concerning voluntary medical male circumcision (VMMC);
a) Dorsal block is achieved by injecting lignocaine at 12 and 6 o'clock sites
b) Scrubbing is unnecessary
c) Hypospadias is an indication
d) Sexual intercourse should be avoided for six weeks after the procedure
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Sexual intercourse should be avoided for six weeks after the procedure

Abstinence for 6 weeks is the standard recommendation to allow complete wound healing. Dorsal block is usually a ring block or specific nerve block (dorsal nerve at 10 & 2 or 1 & 11). Hypospadias is a contraindication (prepuce needed for repair).

39.
One of the following is true about direct inguinal hernia:
a) Commonly strangulates
b) Reduces spontaneously on lying supine
c) Can be controlled by applying pressure over internal inguinal ring
d) Recurrence after surgery is uncommon.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Reduces spontaneously on lying supine

Direct hernias have a wide neck (Hesselbach's triangle) and easily reduce. They rarely strangulate compared to indirect hernias. Pressure on the internal ring controls indirect, not direct hernias.

41.
A 40-year-old was brought to casualty after having suffered blunt chest injury. After history taking and physical examination, a diagnosis of left haemothorax was made. Enumerate five (5) clinical features that support this diagnosis.
Show Answer

Answer:

  • Pain: Severe chest pain aggravated by breathing.
  • Dyspnea: Difficulty in breathing/Respiratory distress.
  • Stony Dullness: On percussion of the affected side.
  • Reduced/Absent Breath Sounds: On auscultation of the affected side.
  • Reduced Chest Expansion: On the affected side.
  • Signs of Shock: Tachycardia, hypotension, pallor (due to blood loss).
42.
A 25-year-old female patient weighing 55 kg sustained 2nd degree burns involving the entire anterior trunk, perineum as well as entire right and left anterior thighs. Using Parkland's formula, calculate the amount of I/V fluids to be administered in the first 24 hours.
Show Answer

Answer:

Step 1: Calculate TBSA%

  • Anterior Trunk = 18%
  • Perineum = 1%
  • Anterior Thighs = ~4.5% each (Whole leg is 18%, Anterior is 9%, Thigh is approx half of leg = 4.5%). Total for both thighs = 9%.
  • Total TBSA = 18 + 1 + 9 = 28%.

Step 2: Parkland Formula

Volume = 4 mL x Weight (kg) x %TBSA

Volume = 4 x 55 x 28 = 6,160 mL of Ringer's Lactate.

Administration: 3,080 mL in first 8 hours; 3,080 mL in next 16 hours.

43.
A middle aged male patient presents to you with an ulcer on his right leg. Outline five (5) enquiries you would consider to make in history taking.
Show Answer

Answer:

  1. Onset and Duration: How did it start? (Trauma, spontaneous) How long has it been present?
  2. Pain: Is it painful? (Arterial is painful, Neuropathic is painless, Venous is achy).
  3. Associated Symptoms: Swelling of legs (Venous), Claudication/Rest pain (Arterial), Loss of sensation (Neuropathic).
  4. Past Medical History: Diabetes Mellitus, Varicose Veins, DVT, Peripheral Vascular Disease, TB.
  5. Social History: Smoking status, Occupation (prolonged standing).
44.
List five (5) indications of limb amputation.
Show Answer

Answer: (The 3 D's)

  • Dead Limb: Gangrene (from PVD, Diabetes, Embolism).
  • Dangerous Limb: Malignant tumors (Osteosarcoma), Crush syndrome (Sepsis risk), Gas Gangrene.
  • Damn Nuisance: Severe pain, loss of function, recurrent chronic infection (chronic osteomyelitis) making the limb a burden.
  • Trauma: Severely mangled extremity where salvage is impossible (Traumatic amputation).
  • Congenital Anomalies: Severe deformity where prosthetic fitting is better.
45.
Outline the management of penetrating joint injury.
Show Answer

Answer:

  1. Emergency Assessment (ATLS): Stabilize patient.
  2. Antibiotics and Tetanus: Immediate broad-spectrum IV antibiotics and Tetanus prophylaxis.
  3. Surgical Exploration (Arthrotomy/Arthroscopy):
    • Debridement of damaged tissue.
    • Removal of foreign bodies.
    • Copious irrigation (Joint washout) with saline.
  4. Closure: Synovium and capsule may be closed loosely over a drain; skin often left for delayed primary closure if contamination is high.
  5. Immobilization: Splinting the joint initially.
46.
Outline the principles of treating chronic osteomyelitis.
Show Answer

Answer:

  • Sequestrectomy: Surgical removal of dead bone (sequestrum).
  • Saucerization: Unroofing the bone cavity to allow drainage.
  • Curettage: Scraping out infected granulation tissue and debris.
  • Antibiotics: Long-term culture-specific systemic antibiotics.
  • Dead Space Management: Filling the cavity with antibiotic beads, muscle flaps, or bone grafts.
  • Stabilization: External fixation if stability is compromised.
47.
List five (5) complications of Colle's fracture.
Show Answer

Answer:

  • Median Nerve Compression: Acute Carpal Tunnel Syndrome.
  • Stiffness: Of wrist and fingers (Shoulder-Hand Syndrome).

48.
Describe the ortolani test of detecting congenital dislocation of the hip joint.
Show Answer

Answer:

The Ortolani Test is a maneuver to reduce a dislocated hip.

  • Position: Infant supine, hips and knees flexed to 90 degrees.
  • Action: The examiner grasps the thigh with the thumb on the inner thigh and fingers on the greater trochanter. The hip is gently abducted while lifting the greater trochanter anteriorly.
  • Result: A positive test is a palpable "clunk" as the femoral head slides back into the acetabulum, indicating a reducible dislocation.

Section 3: Long Answer Question

20 Marks
49.

A 32 year old male patient has been admitted in surgical ward with a diagnosis of intestinal obstruction.

a) List four (4) GIT clinical features that support this diagnosis.

b) Outline the pre-operative management of this patient

c) List four (4) complications of intestinal obstruction.

Show Answer

Answer:

a) Clinical Features (Cardinal Signs):

  1. Abdominal Pain: Colicky in nature, usually central (small bowel) or lower abdominal (large bowel).
  2. Vomiting: Early and bilious in high obstruction; late and feculent in low obstruction.
  3. Abdominal Distension: More prominent in distal obstruction.
  4. Constipation: Absolute constipation (obstipation) - failure to pass stool or flatus.

b) Pre-operative Management ("Drip and Suck"):

  • Resuscitation (Drip): Establish wide-bore IV access. Correct dehydration and electrolyte imbalance (esp. Hypokalemia) using crystalloids (Normal Saline/Ringer's Lactate).
  • Gastrointestinal Decompression (Suck): Insert a Nasogastric Tube (NGT) to aspirate stomach contents, reduce vomiting, and prevent aspiration.
  • Monitoring: Insert urinary catheter to monitor urine output (organ perfusion). Monitor Vitals (BP, Pulse, RR). Maintain Input/Output chart.
  • Analgesia: Administer pain relief.
  • Antibiotics: Prophylactic broad-spectrum antibiotics to cover gut flora (e.g., Ceftriaxone + Metronidazole).
  • Investigations: U&Es, CBC, Group & Cross-match, Abdominal X-rays (supine/erect).
  • Consent: Obtain informed consent for laparotomy.

c) Complications:

  1. Dehydration and Electrolyte Imbalance: Leading to shock and renal failure.
  2. Aspiration Pneumonia: From vomiting.
  3. Strangulation/Ischemia: Compromised blood supply leading to bowel gangrene.
  4. Perforation: Leading to fecal peritonitis and sepsis.
  5. Sepsis/Septic Shock: From bacterial translocation or peritonitis.
