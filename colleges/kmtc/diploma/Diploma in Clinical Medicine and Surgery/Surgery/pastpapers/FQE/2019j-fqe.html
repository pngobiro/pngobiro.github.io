Section 1: Multiple Choice Questions

40 Marks
1.
Avascular necrosis:
a) Means death of blood vessels
b) Is commonly encountered in skull fractures
c) Is death of bone from deficient blood supply
d) Is caused by osteoporosis
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Is death of bone from deficient blood supply

Avascular necrosis (AVN) is the death of bone tissue due to a lack of blood supply. It is common in fractures of the femoral neck, scaphoid, and talus.

2.
A military recruit who began training six weeks ago comes to the hospital with complaints of pain in his right mid foot for one week... X-ray reveals a fracture in his third metatarsal. He most likely has
a) A pathological fracture
b) A fatigue fracture
c) A segmental fracture
d) A traumatic fracture
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) A fatigue fracture

Also known as a "March Fracture," this is a type of stress fracture occurring in metatarsals (commonly 2nd or 3rd) due to repetitive stress, typical in military recruits.

3.
Which one of the following is characteristic of osteogenic sarcoma?
a) Onion peel appearance
b) Multiple punched out lesions
c) The tumour is capped with cartilage
d) Sun-rays appearance
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Sun-rays appearance

"Sun-ray" or "Sunburst" spiculation and Codman's triangle are classic X-ray features of Osteosarcoma. (Onion peel = Ewing's; Punched out = Myeloma; Cartilage cap = Osteochondroma).

4.
In acute haematogenous osteomyelitis, the most likely causative organism is
a) Beta haemolytic streptococcus
b) Salmonella typhi
c) Staphylococcus aureus
d) Neisseria gonorrhea
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Staphylococcus aureus

Staph. aureus is the most common causative organism overall. Salmonella is characteristic in Sickle Cell patients.

5.
Regarding bulbocavernosus reflex in a spinal injury patient with acute paralysis:
a) Presence of the reflex indicates spinal cord severance
b) Presence of the reflex indicates presence of head injury
c) Absence of the reflex indicates obvious complete cord transection
d) Absence of the reflex indicates that there is no spinal injury
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Presence of the reflex indicates spinal cord severance

Usually, the return of the bulbocavernosus reflex marks the end of spinal shock. If the reflex returns but the patient still has complete motor/sensory paralysis, it indicates a complete spinal cord injury (severance/transection) with poor prognosis for recovery. Absence of the reflex indicates the patient is still in spinal shock.

6.
Which one of the following statements is not true regarding surgical jaundice?
a) Carcinoma of the head of pancreas is a cause
b) Urine test for urobilirogen is usually positive
c) Patients usually present with itchiness of the skin
d) Patients pass pale stool
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Urine test for urobilirogen is usually positive

This statement is Not True. In surgical (obstructive) jaundice, bilirubin does not reach the intestine, so no urobilinogen is formed to be reabsorbed and excreted in urine. Thus, urine urobilinogen is absent.

7.
Which one of the following statements correctly presents brain concussion in head injury?
a) It is the commonest cause of persistent coma in head injury
b) It causes tears of the neuron in the brain
c) It is a bruise of the brain
d) It is a mild brain injury that temporarily impairs brain functions
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) It is a mild brain injury that temporarily impairs brain functions

Concussion is a transient physiological dysfunction without structural damage. Bruising is contusion. Tearing of axons is Diffuse Axonal Injury.

8.
The following is true regarding hydrocephalus
a) It presents with sunken fontanelles
b) Overriding of skull bones is diagnostic
c) Endoscopic third ventriculostomy is an effective method of treatment
d) Stunting is mostly done on the left side of the head
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Endoscopic third ventriculostomy is an effective method of treatment

ETV is an alternative to shunting. Hydrocephalus presents with bulging fontanelles and separated sutures (not overriding). Shunts are usually placed on the right to avoid the dominant hemisphere.

9.
A surgery that is performed to fuse a joint is termed
a) Arthrodesis
b) Arthrotomy
c) Arthroscopy
d) Arthroplasty
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Arthrodesis

Arthrodesis = Fusion. Arthroplasty = Replacement/Reconstruction. Arthrotomy = Opening a joint.

10.
A patient with history of passage of hard faeces associated with severe burning, tearing, sharp pain in the anal region, most likely has;
a) Hirschsprungs disease
b) Fissure - in - ano
c) Fistula - in - ano
d) First degree haemorrhoids
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Fissure - in - ano

Severe "tearing" pain during defecation, often with a sentinel pile and bright red blood on tissue, is classic for an anal fissure.

11.
The most common causative organism in cellulitis is;
a) Pseudomonas aeruginosa
b) Streptococcus pneumonia
c) Staphylococcus epidermidis
d) Beta haemolytic streptococcus
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Beta haemolytic streptococcus

(Group A Streptococcus) and Staph. aureus are the primary causes. Strep is noted for spreading factors leading to the diffuse nature of cellulitis.

12.
Right sided pleural effusion presents with;
a) Tracheal deviation towards the right
b) Hyper resonant note on percussion of the right chest
c) Stony dullness on the right chest
d) Good air entry bilaterally
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Stony dullness on the right chest

Stony dullness is the hallmark percussion note for fluid in the pleural space. The trachea deviates away (left) in massive effusion.

13.
Tension band wiring is used in;
a) displaced patella fractures
b) Fracture of the neck of femur
c) Fractured ribs
d) Fracture of the scaphoid bone
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) displaced patella fractures

Tension band wiring is used for fractures where muscle pull distracts fragments (e.g., Patella, Olecranon). It converts the distraction force into a compressive force at the fracture site.

14.
An epiphyseal injury in which there is separation of the epiphysis with a characteristics triangular fragment of the metaphysis attached to the displaced epiphysis is classified as salter- Harris type;
a) I
b) II
c) III
d) IV
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) II

Type II is the most common. The fracture line passes through the physis and exits through the metaphysis, creating the "Thurston Holland fragment" (triangular metaphyseal fragment).

15.
An antimicrobial that is used in surgical patients to cater for infection by anaerobic organisms is;
a) Ceftriaxone
b) Gentamycin
c) Flucloxacillin
d) Metronidazole
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Metronidazole

Metronidazole is specifically effective against anaerobes (e.g., Bacteroides, Clostridium). Ceftriaxone/Gentamicin target aerobes/Gram negatives.

16.
Which one of the following statements does not correctly apply in congenital hypertrophic pyloric stenosis?
a) It is commoner in first born male infants
b) The patient has projectile vomiting
c) The vomitus is bilious and occurs shortly after feeding
d) Presents within the first eight weeks of life
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) The vomitus is bilious and occurs shortly after feeding

In Pyloric Stenosis, the obstruction is proximal to the ampulla of Vater, so the vomitus is non-bilious. Bilious vomiting suggests obstruction distal to the ampulla (e.g., duodenal atresia, volvulus).

17.
A pre-operative medication that is used to reduce secretions to prevent aspiration during general anesthesia is
a) Pethidine
b) Atropine
c) Diazepam
d) Omeprazole
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Atropine

Atropine is an anticholinergic that dries up secretions (antisialagogue).

18.
One of the following indications for amputation is classified as constituting a deadly extremity
a) Severe contracture
b) Dry gangrene from peripheral vascular disease
c) Malignant tumour in a limb
d) Congenital abnormality such as polydactyl
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Malignant tumour in a limb

Indications for amputation are often classified as: Dead (Gangrene), Dangerous (Malignancy, Gas Gangrene - "Deadly"), and Damn Nuisance (Pain, Functionless). A malignant tumour poses a threat to life, hence "Deadly/Dangerous".

19.
A fracture of the upper/proximal end of ulna with dislocation of the head of radius is called
a) Galeazzi fracture-dislocation
b) Smith's fracture
c) Cole's fracture
d) Monteggia fracture-dislocation
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Monteggia fracture-dislocation

Mnemonic MUGR: Monteggia = Ulna fracture (+ Radial head dislocation). Galeazzi = Radius fracture (+ DRUJ dislocation).

20.
Free gas underneath the diaphragm seen in a plain abdominal X-ray is indicative of
a) Pneumothorax
b) Perforated abdominal viscus
c) Pleural effusion
d) Ascites
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Perforated abdominal viscus

Pneumoperitoneum (air under diaphragm on erect CXR/AXR) typically results from a perforated hollow viscus (e.g., stomach, duodenum, colon).

21.
A congenital abnormality in which the urethral meatus is located on the dorsal aspect of the penile shaft is termed as
a) Epispadias
b) Phimosis
c) Paraphimosis
d) Hypospadias
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Epispadias

Epispadias = Dorsal (Top). Hypospadias = Ventral (Bottom).

22.
A hyper resonant percussion note would be produced by which of the following conditions
a) Empyema thoracis
b) Haemothorax
c) Pneumothorax
d) Pleural effusion
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Pneumothorax

Air in the pleural space (Pneumothorax) causes hyper-resonance. Fluid (Blood, Pus, Effusion) causes stony dullness.

23.
A patient who sustains injury of the right lower limb after a road traffic accident... limb is shortened, adducted and internally rotated. The patient most likely has
a) Fracture neck of femur
b) Posterior dislocation of the hip
c) Trochanteric fracture of femur
d) Central fracture-dislocation of the hip
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Posterior dislocation of the hip

Shortened, Adducted, Internally rotated = Posterior Dislocation.
Shortened, Abducted, Externally rotated = Anterior Dislocation.
Shortened, Externally rotated = Fracture Neck of Femur.

24.
Which one of the following statements is true regarding management of fresh cut wounds?
a) Ketamine is used as a local anaesthetic
b) Suture size 1/0 is best for the face.
c) Hydrogen peroxide is recommended for cleaning of fresh wounds
d) Lignocaine with adrenaline should not be used to anaesthetize fingers
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Lignocaine with adrenaline should not be used to anaesthetize fingers

Adrenaline causes vasoconstriction and can lead to digital ischemia/gangrene in end-artery areas like fingers, toes, penis, and nose.

25.
Regarding swallowed foreign bodies, which one of the following statements is not true
a) Urgent intervention is required if the ingested object is sharp
b) A swallowed high powered magnet is inconsequential
c) A coin in the lower oesophagus may be observed for 24 hours to monitor its passage into the stomach
d) Foreign body in the oesophagus can be removed endoscopically
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) A swallowed high powered magnet is inconsequential

Swallowing magnets (especially multiple) is dangerous as they can attract each other across bowel loops, causing pressure necrosis, perforation, and fistulas.

26.
About the deformities in congenital Talipes Equinovourus
a) The foot is excessively dorsiflexed
b) The foot is in eversion
c) The Achilles tendon is usually shorter than the normal side
d) The leg is usually in lateral rotation
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) The Achilles tendon is usually shorter than the normal side

Clubfoot deformities (CAVE): Cavus, Adductus, Varus (Inversion), Equinus (Plantar flexion - due to short Achilles). The foot is Inverted and Plantarflexed (not dorsiflexed/everted).

27.
The following are complications of fracture shafts of radius and ulna, except
a) Avascular necrosis
b) Cross union
c) Non- union
d) Compartment syndrome
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Avascular necrosis

AVN is common in Scaphoid, Talus, Femoral head. It is rare in the shafts of radius/ulna. Cross union (synostosis), non-union, and compartment syndrome are recognized complications.

28.
The surgical term used to denote excision of an organ ends in -
a) Otomy
b) Ostomy
c) Ectomy
d) Oplasty
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Ectomy

Ectomy = Removal. Otomy = Cutting into. Ostomy = Creating opening. Oplasty = Repair.

29.
The following are complications of duodenal ulcer, except
a) Perforation
b) Malignant change
c) Haemorrhage
d) Gastric outlet obstruction
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Malignant change

Duodenal ulcers are almost always benign and rarely undergo malignant transformation. Gastric ulcers have a higher risk of malignancy.

30.
Which one of these hernias frequently strangulates?
a) Direct inguinal hernia
b) Umbilical hernia
c) Hiatus hernia
d) Indirect inguinal hernia
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Indirect inguinal hernia

Indirect hernias have a narrow neck (deep ring) prone to constriction. Direct hernias have a wide neck. Femoral hernias have the highest rate, but among the options provided, Indirect is more prone than Direct.

31.
Osteosclerotic deposits in Lumbar spine could be due to:
a) Metastasis from breast cancer
b) Metastasis from prostatic cancer
c) Metastasis from renal malignancies
d) Metastasis from cancer of the stomach.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Metastasis from prostatic cancer

Prostate cancer typically causes osteoblastic (sclerotic) metastases. Breast and others can be mixed or lytic.

32.
An investigation used for staging carcinoma of the oesophagus is:
a) Oesophagoscopy
b) Endoscopic ultrasound
c) Barium swallow
d) Barium meal
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Endoscopic ultrasound

EUS is the gold standard for locoregional staging (T and N stage) of esophageal cancer.

33.
Regarding spina bifida
a) Spina bifida occulta is obvious on the skin surface
b) Rachischisis has nerve elements openly exposed
c) The condition has no relationship with lower limb deformities
d) All cases present with neurological deficits
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Rachischisis has nerve elements openly exposed

Rachischisis (Myeloschisis) is the most severe form where neural folds fail to fuse, leaving exposed neural tissue. Occulta is often asymptomatic/hidden (except for tuft of hair). Cystica causes limb deformities/deficits.

34.
Pyomyoscitis
a) Presents with blistering
b) Is inflammation of tendons
c) Is accumulation of pus in muscles
d) Is treated with antibiotics only.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Is accumulation of pus in muscles

Pyomyositis is a bacterial infection of skeletal muscle leading to abscess formation. Treatment usually requires drainage.

35.
Compartment syndrome following fracture is treated surgically by
a) Escharotomy
b) Incision and drainage
c) Arthrotomy
d) Fasciotomy
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Fasciotomy

Fasciotomy releases the pressure within the muscle compartments. Escharotomy is for burns.

36.
Which one of the following fluids is a colloid?
a) Haemacel
b) Normal saline
c) Ringer's lactate
d) 5% dextrose
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Haemacel

Haemacel is a gelatin-based colloid. Saline, Ringer's, and Dextrose are crystalloids.

37.
Regarding carcinoma of the breast

b) Breast cancer is found most frequently in the upper outer quadrant

d) Chest radiography are not necessary

The upper outer quadrant (Tail of Spence) contains the most breast tissue and is the most common site for carcinoma.

38.
Complications of gallstones include all these, except
a) Acute cholangitis
b) Intestinal obstruction
c) Acute appendicitis
d) Acute pancreatitis

Appendicitis is not a complication of gallstones. Gallstone Ileus (Obstruction), Pancreatitis, and Cholangitis are.

39.
Acute intussusception
a) Is a twisting of a loop of intestine

c) Occurs when a portion of intestine is invaginated within an adjacent segment

Intussusception is "telescoping" (invagination). Twisting is Volvulus. It is most common in infants.

40.
The Ochsner-Sherren regime is used in
a) Management of an appendicular mass

Section 2: Short Answer Questions

40 Marks
1.
State five (5) causes of non- union of a fracture.
Show Answer

Answer:

  • Infection: (e.g., in open fractures).
  • Inadequate Immobilization: Excessive movement at fracture site.
  • Poor Blood Supply: (e.g., Scaphoid, Femoral Neck).
  • Soft Tissue Interposition: Muscle/fascia between fragments.
  • Wide Distraction: Gap between bone ends (e.g., excessive traction).
  • Patient Factors: Smoking, Diabetes, Malnutrition, Steroid use.
2.
Describe five (5) pathological changes that occur in a persistently dislocated hip joint.
Show Answer

Answer:

  1. Acetabulum: Becomes shallow and filled with fibro-fatty tissue (pulvinar).
  2. Femoral Head: Becomes flattened and deformed.
  3. Ligamentum Teres: Becomes elongated, thickened, or ruptures.
  4. Capsule: Becomes hourglass-shaped (constricted).
  5. Muscles: Surrounding muscles shorten and contract (contractures).
  6. False Acetabulum: May form on the ilium.
3.
A patient is diagnosed with 3rd degree haemorrhoids. State five (5) possible complications of haemorrhoids this patient could develop.
Show Answer

Answer:

  • Anaemia: From chronic bleeding (Iron deficiency).
  • Strangulation: Blood supply cut off.
  • Thrombosis: Painful clot formation.
  • Ulceration: Of the mucosa.
  • Gangrene: Following strangulation.
  • Infection/Abscess: Perianal abscess (rare but possible).
  • Prolapse: Permanent protrusion.
4.
Outline five (5) aetiological factors that contribute to the causation of abdominal hernias.
Show Answer

Answer:

  • Chronic Cough: (e.g., COPD, TB) increases intra-abdominal pressure.
  • Straining: (e.g., Constipation, BPH/Urethral stricture).
  • Obesity: Increases pressure and weakens muscles.
  • Heavy Lifting: Occupational strain.
  • Congenital Defects: Patent Processus Vaginalis.
  • Abdominal Distension: Ascites, Pregnancy, Tumours.
  • Weakness of Abdominal Wall: Post-surgical incisions, aging, collagen disorders.
5.
State five (5) features of chronic osteomyelitis as seen on a radiograph of a long bone.
Show Answer

Answer:

  • Sequestrum: Dense, detached dead bone fragment.
  • Involucrum: New bone formation surrounding the sequestrum.
  • Cloaca: Opening/defect in the involucrum discharging pus.
  • Bony Sclerosis: Increased density.
  • Cortical Thickening/Irregularity.
  • Lytic lesions: Areas of bone destruction.
6.
An adult female patient presents to you with a neck swelling that you believe is the thyroid gland. You notice features suggestive of hyperthyroidism. State five (5) of these features.
Show Answer

Answer:

  • Exophthalmos: Protruding eyes.
  • Tremors: Fine tremors of hands.
  • Tachycardia: Fast pulse rate.
  • Warm, moist skin: Sweaty palms.
  • Weight loss: Despite increased appetite.
  • Lid Lag/Lid Retraction.
  • Pre-tibial Myxoedema.
  • Bruit: Over the thyroid gland.
7.
A 30-year-old male weighing 50 kg is brought to causality with burns of the trunk and thighs anteriorly, you estimate the percentage to be 25%. Clearly outline your fluid resuscitation of this patient in the first 24 hours using parkland's formula.
Show Answer

Answer:

Formula: 4 mL x Weight (kg) x %TBSA

Calculation: 4 mL x 50 kg x 25 = 5,000 mL (Total Volume).

Administration:

  • Give 2,500 mL (50%) in the first 8 hours from the time of burn.
  • Give 2,500 mL (50%) in the next 16 hours.
  • Fluid of choice: Ringer's Lactate.
8.
An adult patient is brought to hospital with paraplegia. State five (5) condition or injuries of the vertebral Colum that could cause compression or injury to the spinal cord or cauda equina.
Show Answer

Answer:

  • Trauma: Vertebral Fractures/Dislocations (e.g., Burst fracture).
  • Disc Prolapse: Massive central disc herniation.
  • Pott's Disease: Spinal Tuberculosis (Cold abscess/Gibbus).
  • Tumours: Metastatic deposits or Primary bone tumours.
  • Spinal Stenosis: Degenerative narrowing of the canal.
  • Epidural Abscess/Haematoma.

Section 3: Long Answer Question

20 Marks
1.
A motorcycle rider is knocked down by a speeding vehicle on a busy highway, He sustains multiple injuries including an open severely comminuted fracture of the tibia and fibula of his right leg. Describe his management from the time he presents to causality in a county referral hospital to the time of discharge.
Show Answer

Answer:

A. Initial Assessment & Resuscitation (ATLS Protocol):

  1. Primary Survey (ABCDE):
    • Airway: Ensure patency with C-spine control.
    • Breathing: Administer oxygen, check for chest injuries.
    • Circulation: Control external hemorrhage (pressure dressing), establish 2 wide-bore IV lines, start IV fluids (Hartmann's), cross-match blood.
    • Disability: Assess GCS and pupils.
    • Exposure: Fully undress to assess other injuries, prevent hypothermia.

B. Specific Management of Open Fracture:

  1. Antibiotics & Tetanus: Immediate IV antibiotics (Cephalosporin + Aminoglycoside + Metronidazole) and Tetanus Toxoid/ATG.
  2. Wound Care: Photograph wound, cover with sterile saline-soaked dressing. Splint the limb to reduce pain and further damage.
  3. Imaging: X-rays (AP/Lateral of Tib/Fib + joint above/below), Chest X-ray, Pelvis X-ray.

C. Surgical Management (Theatre):

  1. Debridement (within 6 hours): thorough irrigation (6-9L saline), excision of dead muscle/skin/bone fragments.
  2. Fracture Stabilization: External Fixator is ideal for severe open fractures (Gustilo III) to allow wound access. IM Nail may be used for lower grades.
  3. Soft Tissue Coverage: Delayed primary closure, skin graft, or flap (local/free) depending on defect.

D. Post-operative & Ward Care:

  • Monitoring: Vitals, Compartment Syndrome signs (Pain, Pallor, etc.), Neurovascular status.
  • Medication: Continue antibiotics, Analgesia, DVT prophylaxis.
  • Elevation: Limb elevation to reduce edema.
  • Physiotherapy: Mobilization (crutches/non-weight bearing) as appropriate.

E. Discharge Planning:

  • Wound healed/clean.
  • Patient mobile with assistive devices.
  • Follow-up appointment for fixator care/removal and X-rays.
