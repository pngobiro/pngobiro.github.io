Section A: Multiple Choice Questions (30%)

1.
The most commonly used crystalloid is:
a) Blood
b) Half strength Darrow's solution
c) Hartmann's solution
d) Normal saline
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Normal saline (or c) Hartmann's solution

Both Normal Saline and Hartmann's are common crystalloids. Normal Saline is often the most widely available, though Hartmann's is preferred for balanced resuscitation.

2.
Which of the following is not an indication for administration of 5% dextrose?
a) Correction of hypernatremia
b) Pre and post operation fluid replacement
c) Prevention and treatment of dehydration
d) Treatment of hypovolaemic shock
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Treatment of hypovolaemic shock

5% Dextrose is isotonic in the bag but becomes hypotonic in the body as glucose is metabolized, leaving free water. It does not stay in the intravascular space and is therefore useless for resuscitation in shock.

3.
Regarding hernias:
a) A direct inguinal hernia takes time to reach its full size on standing but retracts back easily
b) An indirect inguinal hernia can be controlled by pressure over the internal ring and appears immediately on standing and vice versa
c) Femoral hernias appear above and medial to the pubic tubercle and are rarely associated with strangulation
d) Ventral hernias are composed mainly of abdominal organs, fat and omentum
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) An indirect inguinal hernia can be controlled by pressure over the internal ring and appears immediately on standing and vice versa

Indirect hernias pass through the internal ring; pressure there prevents protrusion. Direct hernias pop out directly through the posterior wall and are not controlled by internal ring pressure. Femoral hernias are below and lateral to the pubic tubercle and have high strangulation risk.

4.
A three-week old boy presents with persistent projectile vomiting. What is the most likely diagnosis?
a) Annular pancreas
b) Malrotation of the gut
c) Oesophageal atresia
d) Pyloric stenosis
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Pyloric stenosis

Classic presentation: First born male, 2-6 weeks old, non-bilious projectile vomiting.

5.
A 35-year-old man presents with severe central abdominal colicky pain for the past 7 hours. It is associated with vomiting and abdominal distention. He has not opened bowels since the onset of the pain. On examination, bowel sounds are exaggerated and the tongue is furred. What is the most likely diagnosis?
a) Acute appendicitis
b) Mechanical intestinal obstruction
c) Paralytic ileus
d) Renal colic
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Mechanical intestinal obstruction

Colicky pain, distension, vomiting, constipation, and hyperactive (exaggerated) bowel sounds are classic for mechanical obstruction. Paralytic ileus would have absent bowel sounds.

6.
On large bowel obstruction:
a) Anorexia and abdominal distention are always present
b) Localized deep pain is an early symptom
c) Localized deep visceral pain is a late symptom
d) Vomiting develops early with the vomitus being a foul smell
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Anorexia and abdominal distention are always present

Distension is a hallmark of large bowel obstruction. Vomiting is usually a late sign (unlike small bowel obstruction).

7.
Small bowel obstruction presents with:
a) Early peri-umbilical pain with long intervals between cramps
b) Large amounts of bilious, watery, non-foul smelling vomitus
c) Normal bowel sounds with localized deep visceral pain
d) Small volumes of bilious watery foul smelling vomitus
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Early peri-umbilical pain with long intervals between cramps

Small bowel colic is typically periumbilical (midgut). Vomiting is early and bilious.

8.
Which of the following is not a complication of duodenal ulcer?
a) Haemorrhage
b) Malignancy
c) Obstruction
d) Perforation
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Malignancy

Duodenal ulcers are extremely rarely malignant. Gastric ulcers have a higher risk of malignancy.

9.
The commonest site for peptic ulcer disease (PUD) is:
a) Distal duodenum
b) Lower oesophagus
c) Proximal duodenum
d) Stomach
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Proximal duodenum

The first part of the duodenum (duodenal cap) is the most common site for ulcers.

10.
A 40-year-old who sustained injury three weeks ago while gardening presents with difficulty in swallowing and jaw movements followed by generalized motor spasms. He is finding it difficult to breathe. On examination you find opisthotonos. What is the most likely diagnosis?
a) Cellulitis of the jaw
b) Gas gangrene
c) Myasthenia gravis
d) Tetanus
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Tetanus

Gardening injury (soil exposure), trismus (lockjaw), dysphagia, and opisthotonos are classic signs of Tetanus.

11.
Effects of spinal cord lesions following injuries at:
a) C4 results in partial paralysis of the hands, arms and lower body
b) C6 results in paraplegia with paralysis below the chest
c) L1 results in paraplegia with paralysis on the left side
d) T6 results in paraplegia with paralysis below the chest
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) T6 results in paraplegia with paralysis below the chest

T6 injury typically causes loss of function below the chest (nipple line is T4, xiphisternum T6). C4 and C6 would cause quadriplegia/tetraplegia.

12.
Subarachnoid haemorrhage presents with:
a) Contralateral mydriasis, aphasia and motor neurological deficits
b) Hypernatraemia, hyperglycaemia and pulmonary oedema
c) Hypotension, bradycardia and fever
d) Nuchal rigidity, back pain and photophobia
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Nuchal rigidity, back pain and photophobia

SAH causes meningeal irritation, leading to neck stiffness (nuchal rigidity), photophobia, and "thunderclap" headache.

13.
Features of increased intracranial pressure (ICP) following a head injury include:
a) 3rd and 6th cranial nerve palsies with false localizing signs
b) 3rd and 6th cranial nerve palsies with lateralizing signs
c) 3rd and 6th nerve palsies with a bursting headache worse in the evening
d) 3rd, 6th and 8th cranial nerve palsies
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) 3rd and 6th cranial nerve palsies with false localizing signs

The 6th nerve (Abducens) has a long intracranial course and is easily compressed by raised ICP, often leading to a palsy that does not localize the lesion (false localizing).

14.
In acute peritonitis:
a) Dehydration and electrolyte imbalance are largely unlikely to occur
b) There is generalised abdominal pain in the absence of peristalsis in severe cases
c) There is generalized abdominal pain and tenderness in all cases
d) There is increased peristalsis and bowel sounds with abdominal distention
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) There is generalised abdominal pain in the absence of peristalsis in severe cases

Peritonitis leads to paralytic ileus (silent abdomen) and guarding/rigidity.

15.
In a flail chest:
a) Affected segment of chest wall is displaced inwards on inspiration
b) Affected segment of chest wall is displaced outwards on inspiration
c) Diagnosis is made both clinically and radiologically
d) The affected segment of the chest wall moves in tandem with inspiration and expiration
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Affected segment of chest wall is displaced inwards on inspiration

This is "paradoxical respiration". The flail segment moves opposite to the rest of the chest wall due to negative intrathoracic pressure during inspiration.

16.
Acute Compartment syndrome:
a) Presents with increased peripheral temperature
b) Rarely presents with pain
c) Requires immobilization with plaster of Paris
d) Is treated with urgent fasciotomy
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Is treated with urgent fasciotomy

Compartment syndrome is a surgical emergency requiring release of pressure (fasciotomy). Pain out of proportion to injury is the hallmark sign.

17.
Which nerve injury is associated with fracture mid shaft of the humerus?
a) Brachial plexus
b) Median nerve
c) Radial nerve
d) Ulna nerve
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Radial nerve

The radial nerve runs in the spiral groove of the humerus and is vulnerable to injury in mid-shaft fractures (causing wrist drop).

18.
The best traction for supracondylar fracture of the humerus?
a) Calcaneal traction
b) Dunlop traction
c) Extension traction
d) Gallow's traction
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Dunlop traction

Dunlop traction is a specific skin traction used for supracondylar humeral fractures in children. Gallow's is for femoral fractures in infants.

19.
The best splint for fracture shaft of femur is:
a) Bohler Braun splint
b) Thomas splint
c) Halo-thoracic splint
d) Cervical collar
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Thomas splint

The Thomas splint is the standard appliance for splinting femoral shaft fractures.

20.
A patient present to you with an open fracture following a road traffic accident. There is a 3 cm wide clean wound with no skin flaps. An x-ray shows a segmental fracture of the tibia. This is Gustilo-Anderson type:
a) I
b) II
c) III
d) IV
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) III (Specifically IIIA)

While the wound is small (suggesting Type II), segmental fractures are high-energy injuries and are automatically classified as Type III regardless of wound size.

21.
A 40-year-old woman complains of pain on the right arm two hours after a fall. Physical examination reveals swelling and deformity of the right arm. She is unable to dorsiflex the wrist joint. An x-ray taken shows a spiral mid shaft fracture of the humerus. Which of the following nerves is most likely affected by the fracture?
a) Circumflex nerve
b) Median nerve
c) Radial nerve
d) Ulnar nerve
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Radial nerve

Inability to dorsiflex the wrist (wrist drop) is the classic sign of Radial nerve palsy.

22.
Advantages of closed reduction of fractures include:
a) Adequacy for intra-articular fractures
b) Lack of ossification in children making is easier
c) Minimal damage to blood supply and soft tissues
d) Reliance on soft tissue attachments to reduce the fragments
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Minimal damage to blood supply and soft tissues

Closed reduction preserves the fracture hematoma and periosteal blood supply, which are crucial for healing.

23.
Regarding open reduction of fractures:
a) Incision made is less extensive so the soft tissues cover is possible
b) Is indicated in open fractures and fractures involving articular surfaces
c) Reduces the risk of damage to the blood supply to the bone as it allows cleaning
d) Requires minimal soft tissue cover as there is no risk of infection
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Is indicated in open fractures and fractures involving articular surfaces

Open reduction is necessary to restore the joint surface in intra-articular fractures and to debride open fractures.

24.
Which of the following internal fixation techniques is correctly matched with the respective fracture?
a) Interlocking nail and screws - proximal and distal ends of femur
b) Kirschner wire - fore arm and metaphysis
c) Plate and screw - elbow and wrist
d) Tension band wiring - olecranon and patella fractures
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Tension band wiring - olecranon and patella fractures

Tension band wiring is the standard fixation for avulsion fractures like the patella and olecranon to convert distracting forces into compression forces.

25.
The correct order of appearance of signs and symptoms of ischaemia in compartment syndromes is:
a) Pain - pallor - paraesthesia - paralysis - pulselessness
b) Pain - paraesthesia - pallor - paralysis - pulselessness
c) Pain - paralysis - pallor - pulselessness - paraesthesia
d) Pain - paralysis - paraesthesia - pallor- pulselessness
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Pain - paraesthesia - pallor - paralysis - pulselessness

Pain is the earliest sign. Pulselessness is a very late sign. The sequence is typically Pain -> Paraesthesia -> Pallor -> Paralysis -> Pulselessness.

26.
In acute dislocation of the shoulder the arm is:
a) Abducted
b) Externally rotated
c) Flexed
d) Internally rotated
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Abducted (and Externally Rotated)

In the common anterior dislocation, the patient holds the arm in slight abduction and external rotation.

27.
The most common fracture in childhood is:
a) Colle's fracture
b) Condylar fractures
c) Supracondylar fractures
d) Trochanteric fractures
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Supracondylar fractures

Supracondylar fracture of the humerus is the most common elbow fracture and one of the most common fractures overall in children.

28.
Anterior dislocation of the hip occurs when:
a) a posteriorly directed force impacts on an abducted and externally rotated hip
b) a posteriorly directed force impacts on an adducted and internally rotated hip
c) an anteriorly directed force impacts on an abducted and externally rotated hip
d) an anteriorly directed force impacts on an adducted and internally rotated hip
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) an anteriorly directed force impacts on an abducted and externally rotated hip

Posterior dislocation (more common) occurs with adduction/internal rotation. Anterior dislocation requires abduction and external rotation.

29.
The most common cause of avascular necrosis of the femur is:
a) Infection
b) Perthe's disease
c) Posterior dislocation of the hip
d) SUFE (slipped upper femoral epiphysis)
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Posterior dislocation of the hip (and fracture neck of femur)

In the context of trauma, posterior dislocation disrupts the blood supply (medial circumflex femoral artery) leading to AVN.

30.
Which of the following form of ORIF is correctly matched with the site or type of fracture?
a) Angle plate fossa - fracture extending to pisiform
b) Dynamic hip screw and plate - pathological fractures
c) Full length nail - lesser trochanter
d) Interlocking nail - below lesser trochanter
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Interlocking nail - below lesser trochanter

Interlocking intramedullary nails are the standard treatment for femoral shaft fractures (which occur below the lesser trochanter).

Section B: True/False Questions (30%)

Indicate whether each statement is True or False.

1.
Concerning a flail chest:
  • a) Affected segment of chest wall is displaced inwards on inspiration - True
  • b) Affected segment of chest wall is displaced outwards on inspiration - False
  • c) Diagnosis is made clinically - True
  • d) Diagnosis is made radiologically - True
  • e) Paradoxical chest movement is evident - True
2.
Contraindications of using 5% dextrose include:
  • a) Cerebral oedema - True (Hypotonic fluid worsens edema)
  • b) Hyponatremia - True (Dilutes sodium further)
  • c) Hypernatremia - False (Often used to treat it by providing free water)
  • d) Acute ischaemic stroke - True (Hyperglycemia worsens outcomes)
  • e) Water intoxication - True
3.
Features of small or proximal bowel obstruction includes:
  • a) Bilious vomitus - True
  • b) Peri-umbilical pain - True
  • c) Foul smelling vomitus - True (Late sign)
  • d) Abdominal distention - True (Though less than large bowel)
  • e) Vomiting containing food particles - True
4.
Large bowel obstruction is associated with:
  • a) Localized deep visceral pain - True
  • b) Peri-umbilical pain - False (Usually suprapubic/hypogastric)
  • c) Increased bowel sounds - True (Initially)
  • d) Foul smelling vomitus - False (Vomiting is late/rare)
  • e) Long intervals between cramps - True
5.
Predisposing factors for perianal abscess include:
  • a) Chemotherapy - True
  • b) Anal sex - True
  • c) Diabetes - True
  • d) Immunosuppression - True
  • e) Medication - True (e.g. Steroids)
6.
Differential diagnosis of a groin swelling include:
  • a) Femoral hernia - True
  • b) Hydrocele - True
  • c) Undescended testis - True
  • d) Lymphogranuloma inguinale (LGI) - True
  • e) Supralevator abscess - False
7.
A femoral hernia:
  • a) Is more common in males than females - False
  • b) Passes through the femoral canal - True
  • c) Requires surgical intervention due to the risk of strangulation - True
  • d) Runs above and medial to the pubic tubercle - False (Below and lateral)
  • e) Strangulation is less common in males than females - False
8.
Signs of bleeding oesophageal varices include:
  • a) Pallor - True
  • b) Hypotension - True
  • c) Oliguria - True (Due to shock)
  • d) Confusion - True (Encephalopathy/Shock)
  • e) Tachycardia - True
9.
Intussusception:
  • a) Commonly occurs in children - True
  • b) Can be associated with recent abdominal surgery - True
  • c) Presents with sudden onset severe colicky abdominal pain - True
  • d) Presents with gradual onset generalized abdominal pain - False
  • e) Is associated with diarrhoea and vomiting - True (Red currant jelly stool)
10.
Complications of spinal anaesthesia include:
  • a) Bradycardia - True
  • b) Hypotension - True
  • c) Urine retention - True
  • d) Paraesthesia - True
  • e) Paralysis - True (Rare but possible)
11.
In testicular torsion:
  • a) The affected testis lies lower that the normal one - False (Lies higher/high riding)
  • b) Pain is minimal and bearable - False (Severe)
  • c) Definitive treatment is orchidopexy - True
  • d) Definitive treatment is untwisting - False (Must fix both testes)
  • e) Management can be conservative - False
12.
Important parameters in management of a paraplegic patient include:
  • a) Care of the skin - True
  • b) Care of the bladder - True
  • c) Care of the bowels - True
  • d) Physiotherapy - True
  • e) Psychotherapy - True
13.
Tracheostomy may be associated with complications such as:
  • a) Aphonia - True
  • b) Dysphagia - True
  • c) Pneumothorax - True
  • d) Haemorrhage - True
  • e) Subcutaneous emphysema - True
