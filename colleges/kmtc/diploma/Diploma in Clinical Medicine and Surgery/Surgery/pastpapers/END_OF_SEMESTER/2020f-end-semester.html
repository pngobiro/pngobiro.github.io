Section 1: Multiple Choice Questions

20 Marks
1.
Which of the following is an accessory organ of the digestive system?
a. The stomach
b. The esophagus
c. The salivary glands
d. The intestines
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c. The salivary glands

Accessory organs aid digestion but are not part of the alimentary canal (tract). They include salivary glands, liver, gallbladder, and pancreas.

2.
The following is a stage of bone healing:
a. Scaring
b. Consolidation
c. Recollection
d. Mending
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b. Consolidation

Stages of fracture healing: Haematoma -> Cellular proliferation -> Callus formation -> Consolidation -> Remodelling.

3.
Concerning ranula:
a. It is a malignant tumour of the tongue
b. It is common in adults
c. It is a cyst arising from sublingual glands
d. It is managed conservatively
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c. It is a cyst arising from sublingual glands

A ranula is a mucous extravasation cyst involving the sublingual gland and is found on the floor of the mouth.

4.
Which of this is the best treatment mode for tongue tie?
a. Reassure patient
b. Admit patient for bed rest and observation
c. Excise part of the tongue that is affected
d. Surgical decision of the frenulum with scissors
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d. Surgical decision of the frenulum with scissors

Frenulotomy (snipping the frenulum) is the standard treatment for ankyloglossia (tongue-tie).

5.
Commonest site for carcinoma of the lip is:
a. The upper lip
b. The lower lip
c. In the mouth
d. Soft palate
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b. The lower lip

Squamous cell carcinoma is most common on the lower lip due to higher sun exposure.

6.
Ramsteds operation is done in the following condition:
a. Congenital oesophageal
b. Intussusception
c. Chron's disease
d. Congenital hypertrophic stenosis
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d. Congenital hypertrophic stenosis

Ramstedt's Pyloromyotomy is the surgical treatment for Infantile Hypertrophic Pyloric Stenosis.

7.
The following is not a cause of paralytic ileus
a. Intestinal worms
b. Hypokalemia
c. Peritonitis
d. Fractures of the spine
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a. Intestinal worms

Intestinal worms typically cause mechanical obstruction (Ascaris bolus), whereas Hypokalemia, Peritonitis, and Spinal injury cause neurogenic/paralytic ileus.

8.
The most common site for volvulus is: -
a. Caecum
b. Sigmoid colon
c. Small intestines
d. Gall bladder
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b. Sigmoid colon

Sigmoid volvulus is the most common form of volvulus, especially in elderly or institutionalized patients.

9.
Second degree hemorrhoids:
a. They bleed and permanently prolapse outside
b. They bleed with no prolapse
c. They prolapse but do not bleed
d. They bleed and prolapse on defecation
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d. They bleed and prolapse on defecation

Classification: 1st: Bleed, no prolapse. 2nd: Prolapse on defecation, reduce spontaneously. 3rd: Prolapse, manual reduction needed. 4th: Permanently prolapsed.

10.
Concerning osteogenic sarcoma:
a. Common site is upper end of the tibia
b. Common tumour in the actual over 50 years of age
c. It is a giant cell tumour
d. It arises from cartilage
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a. Common site is upper end of the tibia

Osteosarcoma typically affects the metaphysis of long bones around the knee (Distal Femur, Proximal Tibia) in young adults. It arises from bone (osteoid), not cartilage.

11.
Mary is a 40-year-old lady... complaints pain, swelling and loss of mobility in the proximal joints of the fingers. She also complains of stiffness after period of inactivity... The most likely condition is:
a. Ankylosing spondylitis
b. Rheumatoid arthritis
c. Pyogenic arthritis
d. Polyarticular gout
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b. Rheumatoid arthritis

Classic presentation: Middle-aged female, small joint involvement (PIP/MCP), morning stiffness >30 mins, inactivity gelling.

12.
Which of the following examination is least important in the assessment and diagnosis of rectal cancer?
a. Digital rectal examination
b. Sigmoidoscopy and biopsy
c. Fundoscopy
d. Colonoscopy
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c. Fundoscopy

Fundoscopy (eye exam) is not relevant for rectal cancer diagnosis. DRE, Colonoscopy, and Biopsy are essential.

13.
Which of the following is not a mode of treatment for hemorrhoids?
a. Laparotomy
b. Injection of sclerosant
c. Hemorrhoidectomy
d. Banding
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a. Laparotomy

Laparotomy (opening the abdomen) is not used for hemorrhoids (anal condition). Injection, Banding, and Excision (Hemorrhoidectomy) are standard.

14.
Causes of pruritis ani include
a. Threadworm
b. Peptic ulcer disease
c. Douches
d. Smoking
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a. Threadworm

Enterobius vermicularis (Threadworm) is a common cause of anal itching, especially at night.

15.
In doing ano-rectal examination, which of this position is ideal?
a. Sims position
b. Prone position
c. Sitting up position
d. Extended knee position
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a. Sims position

Left lateral decubitus (Sims position) is the standard position for rectal examination.

16.
Rhabdomyosarcoma:
a. It is malignant tumor of skeletal muscles
b. It is malignant tumors of the fatty tissue
c. Radiotherapy has no role in treatment
d. It is a lump caused by rapture of a muscle
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a. It is malignant tumor of skeletal muscles

Rhabdomyosarcoma arises from striated (skeletal) muscle progenitors.

17.
Hemangioma:
a. Originate from fibrous tissue
b. Cavernous type is the most common
c. They always turn malignant
d. It is seen during coachwood (childhood)
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d. It is seen during childhood

Capillary hemangiomas (Strawberry nevus) are common in childhood and often involute. They originate from blood vessels (endothelium), not fibrous tissue.

18.
Neurofibroma are benign tumors... which of this statement is NOT TRUE?
a. It originates from the nerve fibers...
b. Lesions are associated with skeletal abnormalities...
c. Patient may present with paraesthesia...
d. Tumors have no risk of recurrence
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d. Tumors have no risk of recurrence

Neurofibromas can recur after excision and carry a risk of malignant transformation (Malignant Peripheral Nerve Sheath Tumor), especially in Neurofibromatosis Type 1.

19.
Which of this is true concerning fibromatosis?
a. Duputyren's contractures is a type
b. Plain x-rays are very useful...
c. Non operative treatment is recommended for all...
d. There is high risk of metastasis...
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a. Duputyren's contractures is a type

Dupuytren's contracture is Palmar Fibromatosis. Fibromatoses are locally aggressive but generally non-metastasizing.

20.
Which of the following represents the classification of the fractures of the neck of femur?
a. Transcervical fractures
b. Gorgeous classification
c. Weber classification
d. Gastric classification
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a. Transcervical fractures

Fractures of the femoral neck are classified anatomically (Subcapital, Transcervical, Basicervical) or by Garden's classification (I-IV). "Weber" is for ankle fractures.

Section 3: Short Answer Questions

40 Marks
1.
Kati is a known peptic ulcer disease patient...
a. Help Kati distinguish between duodenal and gastric ulcers (5 marks)
b. Indications of surgical treatment? (3 marks)
c. Name any 2 operations done (2 marks)
Show Answer

a) Differences:

  • Pain timing: Gastric (immediately after food), Duodenal (2-3 hrs after food/hunger pain).
  • Weight: Gastric (Weight loss - fear of eating), Duodenal (Weight gain - eats to relieve pain).
  • Vomiting: Common in Gastric, Rare in Duodenal.
  • Hematemesis vs Melena: Hematemesis more common in Gastric, Melena in Duodenal.
  • Age: Gastric usually older (>50), Duodenal younger (25-50).

b) Surgical Indications: Perforation, Uncontrollable Hemorrhage, Gastric Outlet Obstruction (Pyloric Stenosis), Intractability (failure of medical rx), Suspected Malignancy.

c) Operations: Truncal Vagotomy & Pyloroplasty, Highly Selective Vagotomy, Billroth I or II Gastrectomy.

2.
a) What are hemorrhoids? (1 mark)
b) List the predisposing factors (2 marks)
c) Clinical features (2 marks)
Show Answer

a) Definition: Dilated, engorged veins of the anal cushion (internal) or perianal venous plexus (external).

b) Predisposing Factors: Chronic constipation, Straining, Pregnancy, Low fiber diet, Portal hypertension, aging.

c) Clinical Features: Bright red rectal bleeding (splash in pan), Prolapse, Pruritus ani, Mucus discharge, Pain (if thrombosed).

3.
A 50 year old lady falls on an out stretched hand... fracture lower end of radius...
a. Deformity? (2 marks)
b. Treatment? (5 marks)
c. Complications? (3 marks)
Show Answer

a) Deformity: Dinner Fork Deformity (Colles Fracture).

b) Treatment: Closed Reduction under local/regional anesthesia (traction, flexion, ulnar deviation), Immobilization with Colles Cast (Below elbow POP) for 6 weeks, Check X-rays, Rehabilitation.

c) Complications: Malunion, Median nerve compression (Carpal tunnel), Stiffness (Shoulder-hand syndrome), Rupture of Extensor Pollicis Longus tendon, Sudeck's atrophy.

4.
A) Classify pelvic fractures giving an example in each case (4 marks)
b) State the complications of pelvic fractures (1 mark each)
Show Answer

a) Classification (Tile's):

  • Type A (Stable): Avulsion fractures (e.g., ASIS), isolated iliac wing fracture.
  • Type B (Rotationally Unstable, Vertically Stable): Open book fracture (APC), Lateral compression.
  • Type C (Rotationally & Vertically Unstable): Vertical shear fracture, Malgaigne fracture.

b) Complications: Massive Hemorrhage (Shock), Bladder/Urethral rupture, Rectal injury, Nerve injury (Sciatic/Lumbosacral), DVT/PE.

5.
Congenital talipes Equino Varus.
a. Name other types of talipes deformities. (2marks)
b. Explain changes involved. (2marks)
c. Mention three principles of management. (6marks)
Show Answer

a) Other types: Talipes Calcaneovalgus, Talipes Equinovalgus, Talipes Calcaneovarus.

b) Changes: Bone deformity (Talus deviation), Soft tissue contracture (Achilles tendon, medial ligaments), Muscle atrophy (Calf).

c) Principles (Ponseti Method): Serial manipulation and casting (weekly), Tenotomy of Achilles tendon (if needed), Bracing (Dennis Brown splint) to prevent recurrence.

Section 4: Long Answer Question

20 Marks
1.

Jadi is a male patient... 8th post-operative day after an abdominal surgery. Yesterday he noticed that the wound had opened and some abdominal viscera had extruded.

a) What impression would you make?(1 mark)

b) What would be the causes of Jadi's condition? (8marks)

c) You are a C.O intern... How would you manage Jadi? (10marks)

Show Answer

a) Impression: Burst Abdomen (Abdominal Wound Dehiscence) with Evisceration.

b) Causes:

  • Local Factors: Wound Infection (most common), Poor suturing technique, Knot slippage, Hematoma, Seroma, Increased intra-abdominal pressure (Coughing, distension).
  • General Factors: Malnutrition (Hypoproteinemia), Anemia, Diabetes Mellitus, Jaundice, Steroid use, Obesity, Advanced Age, Cancer.

c) Management:

Immediate Ward Management:

  • Reassure the patient and relieve anxiety/pain (Analgesia).
  • Do NOT attempt to push the bowel back into the abdomen in the ward.
  • Cover the protruding viscera with sterile gauze soaked in warm normal saline (to prevent desiccation).
  • Keep patient Nil Per Os (NPO).
  • Insert IV line and start fluids (resuscitation).
  • Pass a Nasogastric tube (to decompress stomach) and Urinary catheter.
  • Start Broad-spectrum IV antibiotics.
  • Arrange for urgent return to theatre.

Definitive Management (Theatre):

  • General Anaesthesia.
  • Clean the skin and bowel with warm saline.
  • Inspect bowel for injury/viability.
  • Return viscera into abdomen.
  • Resuture the abdominal wall using tension sutures (mass closure) with non-absorbable material (e.g., Nylon 1).
  • Post-op care: Abdominal binder, treat underlying cause (cough, etc.), nutrition.
