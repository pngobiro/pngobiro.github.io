Section 1: Multiple Choice Questions

20 Marks
1.
Which one of the following is an immediate complication of major abdominal operation?
a) Hypertrophic scar
b) Haemorrhage
c) Wound dehiscence
d) Dumping syndrome
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Haemorrhage

Primary or Reactionary haemorrhage is an immediate complication (within 24 hours). Dehiscence is usually later (day 7-10), and hypertrophic scars take months.

2.
One of the drugs below is usually administered pre-operatively
a) Adrenaline
b) Penicillin
c) Atropine
d) Diclofenac
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Atropine

Atropine is often used as a pre-medication to reduce bronchial and salivary secretions (antisialagogue) and prevent bradycardia.

3.
Spreading inflammation of connective tissues following a thorn prick is referred to as
a) An abscess
b) Cellulitis
c) Carbuncle
d) Boil
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Cellulitis

Cellulitis is a diffuse, spreading acute inflammation of the skin and subcutaneous tissues, often following minor trauma like a prick.

4.
The following is a general side effect of local anaesthesia
a) Vasovagal episode
b) Nerve ablation
c) Transcutaneous electrical nerve stimulation
d) Site stimulation and relaxin
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Vasovagal episode

Fainting (vasovagal attack) is a common systemic reaction to the administration of LA, often due to anxiety or pain. (Note: True toxic side effects are CNS/CVS toxicity).

5.
All the following are indication of burns except (implied: admission criteria)
a) Electrical burns
b) Burns on flexure sites
c) All burns in elderly cohorts
d) Inhalation burns
Show Answer

Correct Answer: N/A (Question phrasing issue)

Note: Electrical burns, Inhalation burns, Burns in the elderly, and Burns on flexure sites (major joints) are ALL standard indications for admission to a burns unit. There is no clear "except" answer here based on standard guidelines (ABA), unless the question implies minor/superficial burns on flexures might be managed as outpatient, but generally, special sites warrant admission.

6.
The following is an early complication of fractures
a) Fat embolism
b) Avascular necrosis
c) Mal-union
d) Myositis ossificans
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Fat embolism

Fat embolism occurs within 24-72 hours (early). AVN, Mal-union, and Myositis ossificans are late complications.

7.
Which one below is not a radiological method of investigating bone conditions
a) Radioisotope scanning
b) Magnetic resonant imaging
c) Nerve conduction studies
d) Arthrography
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Nerve conduction studies

Nerve conduction studies assess nerve function (electrophysiology), not bone structure/radiology. All others are imaging modalities.

8.
Surgical cutting of a bone is referred to as
a) Bone grafting
b) Osteotomy
c) Arthrodesis
d) Arthroplasty
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Osteotomy

Osteo (Bone) + Otomy (Cutting). Usually done to correct deformity.

9.
The following is a type of fracture displacement
a) Oblique
b) Comminutted
c) Spiral
d) Angulation
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Angulation

Displacements include: Translation (Shift), Angulation (Tilt), Rotation (Twist), and Length (Shortening/Distraction). Oblique, Comminuted, and Spiral describe the fracture pattern, not the displacement.

10.
A systemic factor which can hinder healing of fractures includes
a) Mobility
b) Inadequate blood supply
c) Steroid therapy
d) Bone infection
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Steroid therapy

Corticosteroids inhibit osteoblast function and collagen synthesis, delaying healing. Mobility, blood supply, and infection are local factors.

11.
Regarding compartment syndrome
a) Normal pressure is less than 10 mmHg
b) Volk man's ischaemic contracture is a common complication in fore arm
c) Fasciotomy is never indicated
d) Myoglobinuria is uncommon complication in dehydrated patients
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Volk man's ischaemic contracture is a common complication in fore arm

Volkmann's contracture is the classic sequela of untreated compartment syndrome in the forearm. (Option 'a' is also true as normal pressure is 0-8 mmHg, but 'b' is a specific clinical outcome often tested).

12.
The type of bone cells useful in remodelling during fracture healing is
a) Osteoblasts
b) Osteoclasts
c) Osteocytes
d) Osteons
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Osteoclasts

Osteoclasts resorb bone, smoothing the callus during the remodelling phase. Osteoblasts build bone.

13.
An abnormal complete displacement of articulate surfaces after injury is referred to as
a) Fracture
b) Subluxation
c) Dislocation
d) Deformity
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Dislocation

Dislocation is complete loss of contact. Subluxation is partial loss of contact.

14.
Dirty and contaminated wounds include the following except
a) Incision and drainage wound after an abscess
b) Herniorrhaphy
c) Empyema bladder
d) Faecal peritonitis
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Herniorrhaphy

Hernia repair (Herniorrhaphy) is a Clean surgical wound (Class I), provided there is no break in technique or bowel resection.

15.
Modifiable factors affecting wound healing include
a) Age
b) Nutrition
c) Race
d) Congenital neuropathies
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Nutrition

Nutrition (protein, vitamins) can be improved/modified. Age, Race, and Genetics are non-modifiable.

16.
DR-ABCDe used in surgical patient management E stands for the following except
a) Environoment
b) Ivaluation
c) Evacuation
d) Exposure
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Exposure (See Note)

Correction: E stands for Exposure and Environment control in ATLS. The question asks what E stands for "except". This is a bit ambiguous. Likely "Ivaluation" (Evaluation) or "Evacuation" are the intended wrong answers for 'E', but spelled with 'E'? "Evacuation" is sometimes included in extended protocols (transfer). "Evaluation" starts with E? No. This looks like a typo-heavy question. In standard ATLS, E = Exposure/Environmental Control. So A and D are correct meanings. B and C are likely the "except" targets.

17.
Dry gangrene occurs when there is
a) Occlusion of venous returns
b) Occlusion of arterial supply
c) Cut wound
d) Insect sting
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Occlusion of arterial supply

Dry gangrene is caused by gradual arterial occlusion (ischemia) without infection or venous obstruction.

18.
Prevention of bed sores include the following except
a) 2 hourly turning
b) Urethral catheterization
c) Avoiding wrinkled sheets
d) Remaining seated in bed all the time
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Remaining seated in bed all the time

Immobility (remaining seated/lying) causes pressure sores. Mobilization and turning prevent them.

19.
4th type of diabetic foot occurs when there is
a) Osteomyelitis
b) Superficial wound
c) Gangrene of the entire foot
d) Gangrene of two toes
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Gangrene of two toes

Wagner Classification Grade 4: Gangrene of forefoot/toes. Grade 5 is gangrene of the entire foot.

20.
The best fluids to use when treating dehydration in severe burns is
a) Blood
b) 5% dextrose
c) Haemacil
d) crystalloids
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) crystalloids

Specifically, Ringer's Lactate (a crystalloid) is the fluid of choice for burn resuscitation (Parkland formula) in the first 24 hours.

Section 3: Short Answer Questions

Short Answer
1.
Describe the Rule of 9 in estimation of percentage surface area in burns (10 marks)
Show Answer

Answer:

The Wallace Rule of Nines divides the body surface area (BSA) into sections that are multiples of 9%:

  • Head and Neck: 9%
  • Each Arm: 9% (Total 18%)
  • Anterior Trunk (Chest + Abdomen): 18%
  • Posterior Trunk (Back): 18%
  • Each Leg: 18% (Total 36%)
  • Perineum/Genitalia: 1%
2.
Discuss an abscess under the following: a) Definition b) Clinical features c) Treatment
Show Answer

a) Definition: A localized collection of pus within a cavity in tissues, organs, or confined spaces, usually caused by bacterial infection.

b) Clinical Features: (Celsus signs)

  • Rubor (Redness)
  • Tumor (Swelling/Induration)
  • Calor (Heat)
  • Dolor (Pain/Throbbing)
  • Functio laesa (Loss of function)
  • Fluctuation (in late stages)

c) Treatment: Incision and Drainage (I&D) under antibiotic cover (if systemic signs present).

3.
A) State 6 methods of internal fixation
b) List 4 indications of external fixation
Show Answer

a) Internal Fixation Methods:

  • Plates and Screws
  • Intramedullary Nails (K-nails, Interlocking nails)
  • Kirschner Wires (K-wires)
  • Tension Band Wiring
  • Cannulated Screws
  • Dynamic Hip Screw (DHS)

b) Indications for External Fixation:

  • Severe Open Fractures (Gustilo Type III) - severe soft tissue damage.
  • Infected Non-Union.
  • Pelvic fractures ("Open book" stabilization).
  • Limb lengthening procedures.
4.
Define the following terms (2 marks each): a) Bone grafting b) Bone transfer c) Arthrodesis d) Arthroplasty e) Osteotomy
Show Answer
  • Bone grafting: Transplantation of bone tissue to repair bone defects/aid healing (autograft/allograft).
  • Bone transfer: Moving vascularized bone to another site.
  • Arthrodesis: Surgical fusion of a joint to eliminate movement.
  • Arthroplasty: Surgical reconstruction or replacement of a joint.
  • Osteotomy: Surgical cutting of a bone to correct deformity.

Section 4: Long Answer Question

20 Marks
1.
Discuss pre-operative management of a patient scheduled to undergo exploratory laparotomy (20 marks)
Show Answer

Answer:

1. Admission and Assessment:

  • Complete History and Physical Examination.
  • Confirm diagnosis and indication for surgery.

2. Investigations:

  • Full Haemogram, U&Es, Group and Cross-match.
  • ECG/CXR (if indicated/elderly).
  • Abdominal Imaging (X-ray/US/CT).

3. Consent: Obtain informed written consent explaining risks/benefits.

4. Resuscitation & Optimization:

  • Correct dehydration/shock with IV fluids (Ringer's Lactate).
  • Nasogastric Tube insertion (Decompression).
  • Urinary Catheterization (Monitor output).

5. Medications:

  • Prophylactic Antibiotics (IV Cephalosporin + Metronidazole).
  • DVT Prophylaxis (Heparin/Stockings).
  • Analgesia.

6. Preparation:

  • Nil Per Os (NPO) for at least 6 hours.
  • Skin preparation (bath/shave).
  • Pre-medication (Anxiolytics/Antacids).

7. Theatre: Ensure blood is available. Transfer to theatre checklist.
