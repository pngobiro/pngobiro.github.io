Section 1: Multiple Choice Questions

40 Marks
1.
The most common hernia in females is;
A) Spigelian hernia
B) Obturator hernia
C) Direct inguinal hernia
D) Femoral hernia
Show Answer

Correct Answer: D) Femoral hernia

While inguinal hernias are the most common hernia overall in both sexes, femoral hernias occur far more frequently in females than in males due to the wider female pelvis.

2.
The following relates to acute appendicitis;
A) Viral infection
B) Primary Clostridial infection
C) Acute gastroenteritis
D) Obstruction of the appendiceal lumen
Show Answer

Correct Answer: D) Obstruction of the appendiceal lumen

The primary pathophysiology of acute appendicitis is obstruction of the lumen (by fecalith, lymphoid hyperplasia, etc.), which leads to distension, ischemia, and secondary bacterial infection.

3.
The most helpful diagnostic imaging for intestinal obstruction is;
A) Supine/erect plain abdominal x-ray
B) CT scan of the abdomen
C) Barium meal/swallow
D) Abdominal ultrasound
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A) Supine/erect plain abdominal x-ray

Plain abdominal films (supine and erect) are the initial and most helpful screening tools to demonstrate air-fluid levels and dilated loops of bowel.

4.
Airway and cervical spine mobilization; choose the false statement.
A) Maintain cervical spine immobilization in all unconscious or symptomatic patients with cervical collar.
B) Inspect the chest movements and any signs of respiratory distress.
C) Inspect the mouth for any debris and sweep through
D) Perform chin left/jaw thrust to prevent the tongue from obstructing the airway.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: D) Perform chin left/jaw thrust to prevent the tongue from obstructing the airway.

The False statement is likely D because in trauma with suspected C-spine injury, the "Head Tilt-Chin Lift" maneuver is contraindicated as it moves the neck. Only the Jaw Thrust should be used.

5.
Which of the following enhances wound healing?
A) Malnutrition
B) Diabetes mellitus
C) Vascular sufficiency
D) Steroids
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C) Vascular sufficiency

Good blood supply (vascular sufficiency) provides oxygen and nutrients essential for healing. Malnutrition, Diabetes, and Steroids all impair wound healing.

6.
Which of the following statements is true in regards to abscess?
A) Incision and drainage is mandatory.
B) Pus usually extends below the fascia layer.
C) High doses of antibiotics will dry up the abscess.
D) After incision and drainage, stitch the incision wound immediately.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A) Incision and drainage is mandatory.

"Ubi pus, ibi evacua" (Where there is pus, let it out). Antibiotics alone cannot penetrate the abscess wall effectively. Abscess cavities are typically left open to heal by secondary intention, not stitched immediately.

7.
The following modalities can be used in the main management of hydrocephalus except;
A) Ventriculostomy
B) End to end anastomosis
C) Ventriculoperitoneal shunt
D) Ventriculoatrial shunt
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B) End to end anastomosis

End to end anastomosis is a technique for joining vessels or bowel, not for draining CSF. VP shunts, VA shunts, and Ventriculostomy (e.g., ETV) are standard treatments for hydrocephalus.

8.
The following is a primary brain injury;
A) Epidural haematoma
B) Subdural haematoma
C) Brain concussion/contusion
D) Subarachnoid haematoma
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C) Brain concussion/contusion

Primary brain injuries occur at the moment of impact (concussion, contusion, DAI). Hematomas (Epidural, Subdural) are often considered secondary sequelae of vascular injury, though classification can vary. Contusion is definitive parenchymal damage.

11.
In management of trauma patients;
A) The 'golden hour' interventions prevent secondary injuries and death.
B) Multiple injuries refers to injuries to one system.

12.
Thermal burns include;
A) Scalds
B) Petrol burns
C) Naked flame burns
D) All of the above.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: D) All of the above.

Thermal burns are caused by external heat sources which include hot liquids (scalds), open flames, and flammable liquids like petrol.

13.
Which of the following is the true 3rd degree burns?
A) Spontaneous healing in one week.
B) Blister formation
C) Leathery skin appearance.
D) Severe pain on presentation.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C) Leathery skin appearance.

3rd degree (full thickness) burns are characterized by a hard, leathery eschar, lack of blisters, and are often painless (insensate) due to destruction of nerve endings.

15.
Choose the correct statement regarding Glasgow Coma Scale (GCS).
A) The minimum score is 3.
B) The maximum score is 17
C) Eye opening to pain is graded as 3
D) Uttering inappropriate words is graded as 4.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A) The minimum score is 3.

GCS ranges from 3 to 15. Max is 15. Eye opening to pain is 2. Inappropriate words is 3 (Confused is 4).

16.
In fractures;
A) Comminuted fractures have less than one fragment.
B) Greenstick fractures are common in the elderly population.
C) Impacted fractures are always displaced.
D) In Varus deformity, the distal segment is angled towards the midline of the body.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: D) In Varus deformity, the distal segment is angled towards the midline of the body.

Varus = Distal part towards midline (Bow-legged). Valgus = Distal part away from midline (Knock-kneed). Greenstick is common in children.

17.
Which nerve injury is associated with fracture mid-shaft humerus?
A) Ulna nerve.
B) Median nerve.
C) Radial nerve.
D) Axillary nerve.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C) Radial nerve.

The radial nerve runs in the spiral groove of the humerus and is vulnerable in mid-shaft fractures, leading to wrist drop.

18.
Fracture complications;
A) Avascular necrosis is common on the femoral condyles.
B) Includes myositis ossificans
C) Fat embolism produced cerebral vascular accident.
D) Mal-union occurs when there is no sign of healing after >3-6 months.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B) Includes myositis ossificans

Myositis ossificans (calcification within muscle) is a known complication, especially around the elbow. AVN is common in femoral neck/scaphoid, not condyles. Fat embolism causes respiratory distress/petechiae, rarely CVA stroke. Option D describes Non-union or Delayed union, not Mal-union (which is healing in bad position).

19.
A flail chest;
A) Is characterized by paradoxical breathing.
B) Is also called pigeon chest.
C) Must be urgently stabilized and fixed with Kirschner Wires.
D) Is always associated with haemopneumothorax.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A) Is characterized by paradoxical breathing.

Flail chest occurs when a segment of the rib cage breaks under extreme stress and becomes detached from the rest of the chest wall, moving in the opposite direction to the rest of the chest wall during respiration (paradoxical movement).

20.
Closed fracture reduction;
A) Cannot be achieved by traction.
B) May fail if soft tissues are interposed.
C) Is not applicable in intra-articular fractures.
D) Is not necessary in un-displaced fractures.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B) May fail if soft tissues are interposed.

Soft tissue interposition is a common cause of failed closed reduction, requiring open reduction.

21.
Internal fixation may be achieved with the following except;
A) Interlocking nail
B) Plates and screws
C) Continuous balance traction
D) Tension band wiring.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C) Continuous balance traction

Traction is a method of external splintage/reduction, not internal fixation. Nails, plates, and wires are internal fixation implants.

22.
In management of an open fracture;
A) The pins of an external fixator are introduced through the fracture site.
B) Early skin closure is necessary to prevent infection.
C) Non-viable muscles appear pink and bloody.
D) Small bony fragments with no attachment should be removed.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: D) Small bony fragments with no attachment should be removed.

Devitalized bone fragments act as a nidus for infection. Skin is usually left open (delayed closure). Fixator pins should be away from the fracture site.

23.
Cardiac tamponade is characterized by;
A) Low blood pressure.
B) Elevated intracranial pressure
C) Paradoxical pulse
D) Collapsed neck veins.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C) Paradoxical pulse

Pulsus Paradoxus (drop in SBP >10mmHg on inspiration) is a hallmark of tamponade. Neck veins are distended (not collapsed). Hypotension is also a feature (Beck's triad), but C is a distinct specific sign often tested.

25.

28.

41.
Define the following terms as used in surgery:
i. Laparotomy
ii. Arthroplasty
iii. Fistula
iv. Crepitus
v. Elective surgery
Show Answer

Answer:

  1. Laparotomy: A surgical incision into the abdominal cavity for diagnosis or preparation for major surgery.
  2. Arthroplasty: A surgical procedure to restore the function of a joint, which can involve resurfacing the bones or using an artificial joint (prosthesis).
  3. Fistula: An abnormal connection or passageway between two epithelial-lined organs or vessels that normally do not connect (e.g., enterocutaneous fistula).
  4. Crepitus: A grating sound or sensation produced by friction between bone and cartilage or the fractured parts of a bone; also refers to the crackling sensation due to gas in tissues (surgical emphysema).
  5. Elective surgery: Surgery that is scheduled in advance because it does not involve a medical emergency and does not require immediate intervention to save life or limb.

Section 3: Long Answer Question

20 Marks
49.

a) Classify compound fractures.

b) Describe any five (5) causes of orthopaedic disorders.

Show Answer

Answer:

a) Classification of Compound (Open) Fractures: Gustilo-Anderson Classification

  • Type I: Clean wound < 1 cm long. Simple fracture pattern, minimal soft tissue damage.
  • Type II: Wound > 1 cm long without extensive soft tissue damage. Moderate contamination.
  • Type III: Extensive soft tissue damage (involving muscle, skin, and neurovascular structures). High contamination.
    • Type IIIA: Adequate soft tissue coverage of bone despite extensive laceration/flaps.
    • Type IIIB: Extensive soft tissue loss with periosteal stripping and bone exposure. Requires flap coverage.
    • Type IIIC: Open fracture associated with arterial injury requiring repair.

b) Causes of Orthopaedic Disorders:

  1. Trauma: Direct or indirect force causing fractures, dislocations, or soft tissue injuries (e.g., Road Traffic Accidents, falls).
  2. Congenital Anomalies: Defects present at birth affecting the musculoskeletal system (e.g., Club foot / Talipes Equinovarus, Developmental Dysplasia of the Hip).
  3. Infections: Bacterial, viral, or fungal infections of bone or joints (e.g., Osteomyelitis, Septic Arthritis, Skeletal Tuberculosis).
  4. Degenerative Changes: Wear and tear of joints over time (e.g., Osteoarthritis).
  5. Metabolic Disorders: Conditions affecting bone density and structure (e.g., Rickets/Osteomalacia due to Vitamin D deficiency, Osteoporosis).
