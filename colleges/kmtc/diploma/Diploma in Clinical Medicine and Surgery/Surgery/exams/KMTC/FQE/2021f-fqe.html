Section 1: Multiple Choice Questions

40 Marks
1.
The surgical term for excision of an organ starts with the name of the organ to be excised and ends with the suffix:
a) -otomy
b) -oplasty
c) -ostomy
d) -ectomy
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) -ectomy

Explanation: The suffix "-ectomy" means surgical removal or excision. Examples include appendectomy (removal of appendix), cholecystectomy (removal of gallbladder), and nephrectomy (removal of kidney).

Section 2: Short Answer Questions

40 Marks
1.
Briefly describe the clinical presentation of infantile hypertrophic pyloric stenosis, highlighting at least ten features. (5 marks)
Show Answer

Answer:

  1. Projectile Vomiting: Non-bilious, forceful vomiting after feeds
  2. Age: Typically presents at 2-8 weeks of age
  3. Gender: More common in males (4:1 ratio)
  4. Hunger: Baby remains hungry after vomiting (eager to feed)
  5. Weight Loss: Failure to thrive, dehydration
  6. Visible Peristalsis: Left to right gastric waves across upper abdomen
  7. Palpable Mass: Olive-shaped mass in right upper quadrant (pyloric tumor)
  8. Dehydration Signs: Sunken fontanelle, reduced skin turgor, dry mucous membranes
  9. Metabolic Alkalosis: Hypochloremic, hypokalemic alkalosis from vomiting
  10. Constipation: Reduced stool output
  11. Jaundice: Unconjugated hyperbilirubinemia may occur

Section 3: Long Answer Question

20 Marks
1.
Following a road traffic accident involving a head-on collision, a passenger in one of the vehicles sustains an injury you diagnose as posterior dislocation of the hip.
i) Describe the mechanisms of injury of this patient's hip (4 marks)
ii) Describe the patient's clinical presentation (4 marks)
iii) Describe the X-ray findings for this injury (4 marks)
iv) Describe the treatment of this patient's conditions (4 marks)
v) State the possible complications associated with this patient's injury (4 marks)
Show Answer

Answer:

i) Mechanisms of Injury:

  • Dashboard injury in motor vehicle accidents
  • Force transmitted along the femoral shaft when the hip is flexed and adducted
  • Knee strikes dashboard with hip flexed at 90 degrees
  • Posteriorly directed force on the flexed knee drives femoral head out of acetabulum posteriorly

ii) Clinical Presentation:

  • Severe hip pain
  • Leg appears shortened
  • Hip is flexed, adducted, and internally rotated
  • Patient unable to move the affected limb
  • May have associated sciatic nerve injury (foot drop, numbness)
  • Knee may show bruising or injury from dashboard impact

iii) X-ray Findings:

  • AP pelvis X-ray shows femoral head displaced posteriorly and superiorly
  • Affected femoral head appears smaller than contralateral side
  • Loss of Shenton's line
  • May show associated acetabular fracture
  • Lateral view confirms posterior displacement

iv) Treatment:

  • Emergency: Closed reduction under general anesthesia (within 6 hours to prevent AVN)
  • Method: Allis maneuver or Bigelow maneuver
  • Post-reduction: Skin traction for 2-3 weeks
  • Mobilization: Non-weight bearing for 6 weeks, then gradual weight bearing
  • If irreducible: Open reduction
  • Associated fractures: May require ORIF

v) Complications:

  • Avascular Necrosis (AVN): Most common, especially if reduction delayed >6 hours
  • Sciatic Nerve Injury: 10-20% of cases, usually neurapraxia
  • Post-traumatic Osteoarthritis: Long-term complication
  • Recurrent Dislocation: If capsule/ligaments severely damaged
  • Heterotopic Ossification: Bone formation in soft tissues
  • Vascular Injury: Superior gluteal artery injury (rare)
