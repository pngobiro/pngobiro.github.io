Section 1: Multiple Choice Questions

40 Marks
1.
Tom and Mary had a wedding and they sired a sickle cell trait child after 2 years. Which of the safe motherhood initiatives could have been missed out?
A. Antenatal care
B. Preconception care
C. Safe delivery
D. Post-natal care
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B. Preconception care

Explanation: Preconception care involves identifying and managing risks before pregnancy. Genetic screening for conditions like Sickle Cell Disease is a core component, allowing couples to understand their risks and make informed reproductive choices.

2.
Gladys presents to you in gynecological clinic complaining of a painful swelling at the entry of her introitus. Which of the following organ is affected by the swelling?
A. Labia minora
B. Labia majora
C. Perineal body
D. Bartholin gland
Show Answer

Correct Answer: D. Bartholin gland

Explanation: The Bartholin's glands are located at the posterior aspect of the vaginal opening (introitus). Obstruction of the duct leads to a cyst or abscess, presenting as a painful, unilateral swelling at the introitus (specifically at the 4 or 8 o'clock position).

3.
Victor opted to have vasectomy after his wife delivered quadruplets two times. Which of the following anatomical structure was resected?
A. Vas deferens
B. Seminiferous vesicles
C. Fallopian tubes
D. Epididymis
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A. Vas deferens

Explanation: Vasectomy involves cutting, tying, or sealing the vas deferens to prevent sperm from travelling from the testes to the urethra.

4.
Linnet after 6 months of studying her menstrual cycle, she discovered she has a regular cycle of 35 days. On which day of the cycle does she ovulate?
A. Day 14
B. Day 10
C. Day 21
D. Day 17
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C. Day 21

Explanation: The luteal phase is relatively constant at 14 days. To find the ovulation day, subtract 14 from the total cycle length.

Calculation: $35 - 14 = 21$.

5.
Joy 25-year-old woman presents to you with complain of profuse thick vaginal mucus discharge following use of a contraceptive Method. Which of the following method do you suspect she is in use?
A. Depo Provera injection
B. Oral combined pills
C. Intra uterine contraceptive Device
D. Oestrogen only pill
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A. Depo Provera injection

Explanation: The primary mechanism of action for progestin-only methods like Depo Provera (DMPA) is the thickening of cervical mucus, creating a barrier to sperm. While "profuse" is subjective, the key descriptor "thick" aligns with the progestogenic effect.

6.
Phillis a catholic believer has been using temperature monitoring method for family planning. Which of the following hormone is responsible for ovulation?
A. Estrogen
B. Luteinizing hormone
C. Follicle stimulating hormone
D. Ganado trophin releasing Hormone
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B. Luteinizing hormone

Explanation: The "LH Surge" triggers ovulation (release of the oocyte). While progesterone causes the temperature rise *after* ovulation, LH is the hormone responsible for the event itself.

7.
A 16-year-old girl is brought to you by the mother due to complain of delayed menarche. On examination there is absence of pubic hair. Which hormone is responsible for the delayed adrenarche?
A. progesterone
B. Estrogen
C. Androgens

Correct Answer: C. Androgens

Explanation: Adrenarche is the maturation of the adrenal cortex, leading to the production of weak androgens (DHEA/DHEAS) which stimulate the growth of pubic and axillary hair.

8.
Anna a 5-year-old girl is brought to you with history of breast budding and menstruation. Which of the following factors could have contributed to this condition?
A. Obesity
B. Defilement
C. Blindness
D. Sub bregmatic diameter
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A. Obesity

Explanation: Childhood obesity is a significant risk factor for precocious puberty (early onset of secondary sexual characteristics) due to increased leptin levels and peripheral conversion of androgens to estrogens in adipose tissue.

9.
Which one of the following arrangements of growth and development of a fertilized ovum is correctly arranged?
A. Morula-Blastocyst-Embryo-Fetus
B. Blastocyst-morula-Embryo-Fetus
C. Zygote-Morula-Embryo-Fetus
D. Zygote-Morula-Blastocyst-Embryo
Show Answer

Correct Answer: D. Zygote-Morula-Blastocyst-Embryo

Explanation: The correct chronological order of development is:
1. Zygote (Fertilized egg)
2. Morula (Solid ball of cells, day 3-4)
3. Blastocyst (Hollow ball, implants day 6-7)
4. Embryo (Up to 8 weeks)
5. Fetus (9 weeks to birth)
Option D lists the first four stages in the correct order.

10.
Linda a 26-year-old paral presents to you with complains of irregular and heavy menstruation for a year now. The impression is
A. Menorrhagia
B. Menometrorrhagia
C. Polymenorrhea
D. Oligomenorrhea
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B. Menometrorrhagia

Explanation:
Menometrorrhagia: Bleeding that is both heavy (menorrhagia) and irregular (metrorrhagia).
Menorrhagia: Heavy but regular.
Metrorrhagia: Irregular acyclic bleeding.
Oligomenorrhea: Infrequent menstruation.

11.
Huda a 16-year-old girl has had a manual vacuum aspiration following an incomplete abortion. Which of the following layer was aspirated?
A. Stratum basalis
B. Decidua capsularis
C. Decidua compactum
D. Stratum compactum
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C. Decidua compactum (or Decidua functionalis)

Explanation: The MVA procedure aspirates the pregnancy tissue and the functional layer of the endometrium (Decidua). The decidua functionalis consists of the stratum compactum (surface) and stratum spongiosum. The stratum basalis remains to regenerate the lining.

12.
Rita a para 0+6 provides a history of pregnancy losses that occurs between 7-12 weeks gestation without any specific order. Which of the following is the cause?
A. Cervical incompetence
B. Corpus luteum deficiency
C. Congenital uterine defect
D. Rhesus incompatibility
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B. Corpus luteum deficiency

Explanation:
7-12 Weeks: This is the period of the "Luteal-Placental Shift" where the placenta takes over progesterone production from the corpus luteum (around 7-9 weeks). Failure of this shift or insufficient luteal support is a classic cause of miscarriage in this window.
Cervical Incompetence: Typically causes mid-trimester losses (16-24 weeks).
Rhesus: Usually affects subsequent pregnancies later in gestation.

13.
Rose a primigravida at 34 weeks gestation is sent for an obstetric doppler ultrasound. Which of the following biophysical profile parameter do you expect to be reported?
A. Fetal weight
B. Fetal Gender
C. Placental location
D. Resistive index
Show Answer

Correct Answer: D. Resistive index

Explanation: Doppler ultrasound specifically measures blood flow velocity. The Resistive Index (RI) indicates resistance to blood flow in vessels like the umbilical artery, crucial for assessing fetal well-being and placental function.

14.
Emily a 30-year-old primigravida presents in active Labour. During vaginal examination what finding would give you an idea of pelvic adequacy?
A. Palpation of sacral promontory
B. Cervical dilation
C. Cervical thickness and length
D. Rupture of membranes
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A. Palpation of sacral promontory

Explanation: Attempting to reach the sacral promontory is used to measure the Diagonal Conjugate. If the promontory can be easily felt, the pelvic inlet might be contracted. This is a direct assessment of the bony pelvis (pelvimetry).

15.
Rhoda had a history of normal Labour and delivery. The presenting diameter was?
A. Occipital frontal diameter
B. Occipital bregmatic diameter
C. Bi parietal diameter
D. Sub bregmatic occipital diameter
Show Answer

Correct Answer: D. Sub bregmatic occipital diameter (Sub-occipito-bregmatic)

Explanation: The presenting diameter in a normal vertex presentation with a fully flexed head is the Sub-occipito-bregmatic diameter (approx 9.5 cm), which is the smallest anteroposterior diameter favorable for vaginal delivery.

16.
Miriam presents at 37 weeks gestation with increased urine frequency without dysuria. Urinalysis results are normal. Which of the following renal function test parameter would be considered normal in pregnancy?
A. Decrease in glomerular filtrate rate
B. Increase serum urea levels
C. Decrease in creatinine levels
D. Increase in serum nitrogen levels
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C. Decrease in creatinine levels

Explanation: In normal pregnancy, Glomerular Filtration Rate (GFR) increases by ~50%. This leads to increased clearance of creatinine and urea, resulting in lower serum creatinine and urea levels compared to the non-pregnant state.

17.
Malka, a primi gravida at a gestation of 20 weeks presents with complains of esophageal reflex. The other possible related complain is?
A. Increase in abdominal girth
B. Increase in gastric motility
C. Hyper alkalinity
D. Constipation
Show Answer

Correct Answer: D. Constipation

Explanation: Both esophageal reflux (heartburn) and constipation in pregnancy are largely caused by the hormone Progesterone, which relaxes smooth muscle. This relaxes the cardiac sphincter (causing reflux) and decreases intestinal motility (causing constipation).

18.
Deep venous Thrombosis is more common during puerperium as compared to antenatal period. The reason is due to?
A. Venous stasis
B. Vascular injury
C. Calf muscle compression
D. Breast feeding effects
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B. Vascular injury

Explanation: While venous stasis and hypercoagulability exist throughout pregnancy, the process of labor and delivery (especially separation of the placenta) causes significant vascular injury to pelvic vessels. This trauma completes Virchow's Triad (Stasis, Hypercoagulability, Endothelial Injury), spiking the risk in the puerperium.

19.
Labour is considered to be normal if
A. Augmentation is successful
B. Onset is spontaneous
C. Results in mild post-partum haemorrhage
D. Apgar score is satisfactory
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B. Onset is spontaneous

Explanation: WHO definition of normal labor involves:

  • Spontaneous onset
  • Low risk at start and throughout
  • Vertex presentation
  • Delivery without aid
  • Healthy mother and baby

20.

21.

22.
Mrs. Otieno presents with per vaginal (PV) bleeding due to a ruptured ectopic pregnancy at 8 weeks. What is the cause of the pv bleeding?
A. Ruptured fallopian tube
B. Aria stella phenomenon
C. Tubal abortion
D. Congestion of endometrium
Show Answer

Correct Answer: D. Congestion of endometrium (Decidual shedding)

Explanation: The *vaginal* bleeding in ectopic pregnancy comes from the uterus, not the tube. It is due to the shedding of the decidua (endometrium) because hormonal support from the corpus luteum/trophoblast is falling/insufficient. The ruptured tube causes *intra-abdominal* bleeding, not vaginal bleeding.

24.
A para 2 0 gravida 3 at term presents with lower abdominal pain, drainage of liquor and bloody mucoid discharge. The next step in care is?
A. Perform obstetric ultrasound
B. Shout for help
C. Digital vaginal examination
D. Start partographing

Correct Answer: C. Digital vaginal examination

25.
The best clinical pelvimetry parameter for assessing pelvic adequacy is?
A. Clinical pelvimetry
B. Previous history of spontaneous vaginal delivery of a baby weighing 3000gms and above
C. Digital vaginal examination
D. Obstetric ultrasound
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B. Previous history of spontaneous vaginal delivery of a baby weighing 3000gms and above

Explanation: The "Clinical Trial of Labor" (a past successful vaginal delivery of a normal-sized infant) is the most reliable proof of pelvic adequacy. Pelvimetry measurements are estimates, but function is proof.

27.
A 14-year-old girl presents to Family planning clinic for a contractive method. The appropriate care would be

Mary 28-year-old, delivered 14 days ago, presents to you at post-natal clinic for routine post-natal care. Which of the following is true?

B. Will have lochia serosa

Correct Answer: B. Will have lochia serosa

44.
Obstetric ultrasound is instrumental during instrumental during management of pregnancy. Explain. (5 marks)
Show Answer

Answer:

  1. Dating: Accurate estimation of gestational age (CRL in 1st trimester) to manage post-dates or preterm labor.
  2. Fetal Viability: Confirming cardiac activity and ruling out miscarriage or ectopic pregnancy.
  3. Anatomy Scan: Detecting congenital anomalies (e.g., neural tube defects) at 18-22 weeks.
  4. Placental Localization: Diagnosing Placenta Previa to plan safe delivery mode.
  5. Growth Monitoring: Assessing fetal weight and amniotic fluid volume (AFI) to detect IUGR or oligohydramnios.
45.
The health of a new born is directly related to placenta. Justify. (5 marks)
Show Answer

Answer:

  1. Nutrient Transfer: The placenta transports glucose, amino acids, and lipids essential for fetal growth. Placental insufficiency leads to IUGR.
  2. Gas Exchange: It acts as the fetal lung, providing Oxygen and removing CO2. Dysfunction leads to fetal hypoxia/asphyxia.
  3. Waste Elimination: Removes metabolic waste (urea, creatinine) preventing toxicity.
  4. Immune Protection: Transfers maternal IgG antibodies, providing passive immunity to the newborn.
  5. Hormone Production: Synthesizes HCG, Progesterone, and HPL to maintain pregnancy and support fetal development.
46.
A 14-year-old girl presents to you with complaints of painful menstruation. Briefly describe types of dysmenorrhea and their care plan. (5 marks)
Show Answer

Types:

  1. Primary Dysmenorrhea: Pain without pelvic pathology, due to excess prostaglandins. Common in teens.
  2. Secondary Dysmenorrhea: Pain due to underlying pathology (e.g., Endometriosis, Fibroids).

Care Plan (Primary):

  • Reassurance: Explain physiology.
  • Analgesics: NSAIDs (Ibuprofen, Mefenamic Acid) to inhibit prostaglandins.
  • Hormonal: COCs to suppress ovulation and thin lining.
  • Local Heat: Warm compress.
47.
Stella is a 24-year-old woman attending ANC for the first time. Getting information of HIV care. Using prong approach, relay the care plan. (5 marks)
Show Answer

Answer: The 4 Prongs of PMTCT (Prevention of Mother-to-Child Transmission):

  1. Prong 1: Primary prevention of HIV infection in women of reproductive age (Counseling, Condoms).
  2. Prong 2: Prevention of unintended pregnancies in women living with HIV (Family Planning).
  3. Prong 3: Prevention of HIV transmission from women living with HIV to their infants (ARVs for mom, Prophylaxis for baby, Safe delivery, Safe breastfeeding).
  4. Prong 4: Provision of ongoing care and support for mothers living with HIV, their children, and families.
48.
Urinary tract infection in pregnancy is very common. Explain 5 physiological changes that enhance its occurrence. (5 marks)
Show Answer

Answer:

  1. Ureteral Dilation: Progesterone relaxes smooth muscle, causing ureteral stasis.
  2. Mechanical Compression: The gravid uterus compresses ureters (especially right side), causing urinary stasis.
  3. Glycosuria: Increased GFR leads to more glucose in urine, providing a medium for bacterial growth.
  4. Bladder Displacement: The uterus displaces the bladder, reducing tone and increasing residual volume.
  5. Immune Suppression: Physiological immunosuppression of pregnancy may reduce local resistance.

Section 3: Long Answer Question

20 Marks
49.
Susan a primigravida at 34 weeks gestation, presents in maternity with complications of frequent blurred vision and convulsions that occurred at home. On examination the BP is 170/120mmHg and proteinuria of 2+. Identify the impression and manage the patient comprehensively including monitoring of the patient on care. (20 marks)
Show Answer

Impression: Eclampsia (Severe Pre-eclampsia complicated by convulsions).

Management Plan:

1. Immediate Stabilization (Resuscitation)

  • Airway: Place patient in left lateral position to prevent aspiration. Clear airway.
  • Breathing: Administer Oxygen (4-6L/min).
  • Circulation: Secure IV access with wide-bore cannula.
  • Call for Help: Activate emergency team.

2. Control of Convulsions (Magnesium Sulfate Protocol)

  • Loading Dose:
    • 4g MgSO4 (20% solution) IV slowly over 10-15 mins.
    • 10g MgSO4 (50% solution) IM (5g in each buttock).
  • Maintenance Dose: 5g MgSO4 IM every 4 hours in alternate buttocks.
  • Continue: For 24 hours after delivery or last fit.

3. Blood Pressure Control

  • Administer antihypertensives (BP > 160/110):
  • Hydralazine: 5mg IV slowly, repeat every 20 mins if needed (max 20mg).
  • Labetalol: 20mg IV, repeat 40mg, 80mg if needed (up to 300mg).
  • Nifedipine: 10-20mg orally (avoid sublingual).
  • Target: Diastolic 90-100 mmHg (avoid rapid drop).

4. Fluid Management

  • Restrict fluids to ~80ml/hr or 1L/24hr to prevent pulmonary edema.
  • Strict input/output chart. Insert Foley catheter to monitor output.

5. Obstetric Management (Delivery)

  • Definitive treatment is delivery.
  • Stabilize first, then deliver within 12 hours.
  • Assess cervix. If favorable, induce. If unfavorable or distress, Emergency CS.
  • Administer corticosteroids (Dexamethasone) for fetal lung maturity if time permits (<34w, but usually immediate delivery is priority in Eclampsia).

6. Monitoring (Magnesium Sulfate Toxicity)

Before each maintenance dose, check:

  • Respiratory Rate: Must be > 16/min.
  • Patellar Reflexes: Must be present.
  • Urine Output: Must be > 30ml/hr (or 100ml/4hr).
  • Antidote: Calcium Gluconate 10% (10ml IV) kept at bedside.

7. Investigations

  • FBC (Platelets/Hb), U&Es (Renal function), LFTs (Liver enzymes), Clotting profile.
