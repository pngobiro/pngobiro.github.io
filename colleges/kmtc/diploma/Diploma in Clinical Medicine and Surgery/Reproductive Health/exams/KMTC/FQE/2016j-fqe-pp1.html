Section A: Short Answer Questions

Answer All Questions
1.
a) Enumerate 5 symptoms and 5 signs of uterine fibroid (5mk)
b) List 5 complication of fibroid (5mk)
Show Answer

a) Symptoms and Signs (0.5 marks each)

Symptoms (Patient complaints):

  1. Menorrhagia: Heavy or prolonged menstrual bleeding (most common).
  2. Dysmenorrhea: Painful menstruation (secondary congestive type).
  3. Abdominal Swelling/Mass: Patient feels a lump in the lower abdomen.
  4. Pressure Symptoms: Urinary frequency (bladder pressure) or constipation (rectal pressure).
  5. Infertility: Difficulty conceiving or recurrent miscarriages.
  6. Dyspareunia: Pain during sexual intercourse (especially if posterior fibroids).

Signs (Elicited by clinician):

  1. Pallor: Signs of anemia due to heavy bleeding.
  2. Palpable Abdominal Mass: Firm, irregular, non-tender mass arising from the pelvis.
  3. Enlarged Uterus: On bimanual exam, uterus feels bulky, firm, and irregular.
  4. Mass Moves with Cervix: The mass is continuous with the uterus.
  5. Dullness on Percussion: Over the mass.

b) Complications of Fibroids:

  1. Severe Anemia: Due to chronic heavy bleeding.
  2. Degeneration: Red degeneration (painful, during pregnancy), Hyaline, Cystic, or Calcific degeneration.
  3. Torsion: Of a pedunculated subserosal fibroid (acute abdomen).
  4. Infertility: Blocking tubes or preventing implantation.
  5. Obstetric Complications: Miscarriage, malpresentation, obstructed labor, postpartum hemorrhage (PPH).
  6. Malignant Change: Sarcomatous change (rare, <0.5%).
  7. Pressure Effects: Hydronephrosis (ureter compression), urinary retention.
2.
Outline the classification of abortion (10mk)
Show Answer

Answer: Abortion is the expulsion of the fetus/embryo before the age of viability (usually <28 weeks in Kenya context, or <22 weeks/500g internationally).

1. Spontaneous Abortion (Miscarriage): Occurs naturally.

  • Threatened Abortion: PV bleeding, mild pain, cervix CLOSED, viable fetus.
  • Inevitable Abortion: Heavy bleeding, severe pain, cervix OPEN, pregnancy cannot be salvaged.
  • Incomplete Abortion: Partial expulsion of products, cervix OPEN, tissue remains in uterus.
  • Complete Abortion: Complete expulsion of all products, cervix CLOSED, pain/bleeding subsides.
  • Missed Abortion: Fetus dies but is retained in uterus, cervix CLOSED, no bleeding/pain initially.
  • Septic Abortion: Any abortion complicated by infection (fever, foul discharge, tender uterus).
  • Recurrent (Habitual) Abortion: Three or more consecutive spontaneous losses.

2. Induced Abortion: Deliberately initiated.

  • Therapeutic (Legal/Medical): Done to save the life of the mother or for other legal indications.
  • Criminal/Illegal (Unsafe): Performed outside the legal framework, often by unskilled persons or in unhygienic conditions.
3.
a) Outline 10 predisposing factor to cancer of the cervix (5mk)
b) Name the method of screening cancer of the cervix (5mk)
Show Answer

a) Predisposing Factors (Risk Factors):

  1. HPV Infection: Persistent infection with High-Risk Human Papillomavirus (Types 16, 18).
  2. Early coitarche: Early onset of sexual activity (<18 years).
  3. Multiple Sexual Partners: Increases exposure to HPV.
  4. High Parity: Multiparity (many births) increases risk.
  5. Smoking: Carcinogens accumulate in cervical mucus.
  6. Immunosuppression: HIV/AIDS or chronic corticosteroid use.
  7. Long-term COC Use: Combined Oral Contraceptives used for >5 years.
  8. Low Socioeconomic Status: Lack of screening access.
  9. History of STIs: Chlamydia, Herpes Simplex Virus 2.
  10. Family History: Genetic predisposition.

b) Screening Methods:

  1. Pap Smear (Cytology): Conventional (Papanicolaou) or Liquid-Based Cytology (LBC).
  2. Visual Inspection with Acetic Acid (VIA): Cervix turns acetowhite if lesions are present.
  3. Visual Inspection with Lugol's Iodine (VILI): Abnormal areas do not take up iodine (remain yellow/mustard).
  4. HPV DNA Testing: Testing for high-risk HPV types.
  5. Colposcopy: (Diagnostic/Secondary screening) Visualizing cervix with magnification.
4.
List 10 components of a partograph (10mk)
Show Answer

Answer:

  1. Patient Identification Details: Name, Gravida, Para, Hospital Number, Date/Time of admission, Rupture of membranes time.
  2. Fetal Heart Rate: Recorded every 30 minutes (Top graph).
  3. Amniotic Fluid (Liquor): State of membranes (I=Intact, R=Ruptured) and color (C=Clear, M=Meconium, B=Blood, A=Absent).
  4. Moulding of Fetal Skull: (0, +, ++, +++).
  5. Cervical Dilation: Plotting 'X' against time (Alert and Action lines).
  6. Descent of Fetal Head: Plotting 'O' (in fifths palpable) against time.
  7. Uterine Contractions: Frequency per 10 mins and Duration (dots, hatched, or solid blocks).
  8. Oxytocin: Dose and rate (drops/min) if used.
  9. Drugs and IV Fluids: Any medication given.
  10. Maternal Vital Signs: Blood Pressure (arrows), Pulse (dot), Temperature.
  11. Urine Analysis: Protein, Acetone (Ketones), Volume.
5.
a) Define true labour (2mk)
b) Outline the various stages of normal labour (8mk)
Show Answer

a) True Labour:

A physiological process characterized by regular, painful uterine contractions that increase in frequency, duration, and intensity, resulting in progressive cervical effacement and dilation, and usually accompanied by the "show" (expulsion of mucous plug).

b) Stages of Normal Labour:

  1. First Stage (Stage of Dilation): From onset of true labor to full cervical dilation (10cm).
    • Latent Phase: 0-3/4 cm dilation. Slow progress.
    • Active Phase: 4-10 cm dilation. Rapid dilation (>1cm/hr).
  2. Second Stage (Stage of Expulsion): From full dilation (10cm) to the delivery of the baby.
    • Propulsive Phase: Full dilation until head reaches pelvic floor (urge to push).
    • Expulsive Phase: Active pushing until baby is born.
  3. Third Stage (Placental Stage): From delivery of the baby to the expulsion of the placenta and membranes. (Active management involves Oxytocin, CCT, Massage).
  4. Fourth Stage (Observation): The first 1-2 hours after delivery of placenta. Critical for monitoring PPH and stabilization.
6.
a) List 4 predisposing factor of pregnancy induced hypertension (2mk)
b) List 4 maternal and 4 fetal complication of pregnancy induced hypertension (8mk)
Show Answer

a) Predisposing Factors (Risk Factors):

  1. Primigravida: First pregnancy.
  2. Age extremes: <18 years or >35 years.
  3. Multiple Gestation: Twins/Triplets (increased placental mass).
  4. Family History: Mother or sister with Pre-eclampsia.
  5. Obesity: BMI > 30.
  6. Pre-existing medical conditions: Chronic hypertension, Diabetes, Renal disease.

b) Complications:

Maternal Complications:

  1. Eclampsia: Development of convulsions.
  2. HELLP Syndrome: Hemolysis, Elevated Liver enzymes, Low Platelets.
  3. Abruptio Placentae: Premature separation of placenta.
  4. Cerebrovascular Accident (Stroke): Due to severe hypertension.
  5. Renal Failure: Acute kidney injury.
  6. Pulmonary Edema: Fluid in lungs.
  7. DIC: Disseminated Intravascular Coagulation.

Fetal Complications:

  1. Intrauterine Growth Restriction (IUGR): Due to placental insufficiency.
  2. Intrauterine Fetal Death (IUFD): Stillbirth.
  3. Prematurity: Iatrogenic preterm delivery required to save mother.
  4. Fetal Asphyxia/Hypoxia: Due to reduced placental perfusion or abruption.

Section B: Long Essay Questions

Answer Any Two Questions
1.
Briefly describe the component of focused antenatal care (20mk)
Show Answer

Answer: Focused Antenatal Care (FANC) emphasizes quality over quantity of visits, targeting specific interventions to improve outcomes.

The Four Pillars/Components of FANC:

1. Early Detection and Treatment of Existing Conditions (Pre-existing diseases)

  • History Taking: Screening for medical conditions (Hypertension, Diabetes, Cardiac disease, Asthma, Epilepsy) and obstetric history (Previous CS, PPH, Stillbirth).
  • Examination: BP measurement (PIH screening), weight, height.
  • Laboratory Tests: Hb (Anemia), Syphilis (VDRL), HIV status, Blood Group/Rhesus, Urinalysis (Protein/Sugar).
  • Management: Treating anemia, malaria, STIs, or referring high-risk cases.

2. Prevention of Complications (Prophylaxis)

  • Tetanus Toxoid (TT): Immunization to prevent neonatal tetanus.
  • Iron and Folate Supplementation (IFAS): Daily supplementation to prevent anemia.
  • Intermittent Presumptive Treatment for Malaria (IPTp): SP/Fansidar in endemic areas (from 2nd trimester).
  • Deworming: Mebendazole/Albendazole in 2nd trimester.
  • Insecticide Treated Nets (ITNs): Provision to prevent malaria.
  • PMTCT: ARVs for HIV+ mothers.

3. Birth Preparedness and Complication Readiness (Individual Birth Plan)

  • Place of Delivery: Identifying a facility.
  • Skilled Attendant: Planning who will assist.
  • Transport: Arranging funds and vehicle for emergency.
  • Funds: Saving for costs.
  • Birth Companion: Identifying a support person.
  • Blood Donor: Identifying a potential donor.
  • Emergency Items: Mother/Baby kit.

4. Health Education and Counseling

  • Danger Signs: Educating on signs (bleeding, severe headache, reduced fetal movement, fever, drainage of liquor) requiring immediate return.
  • Nutrition: Balanced diet, hygiene.
  • Family Planning: Postpartum contraception counseling.
  • Breastfeeding: Exclusive breastfeeding benefits.
  • Self-care: Hygiene, rest.

Timing of Visits (Standard FANC Model):

  • 1st Visit: < 16 weeks (Booking).
  • 2nd Visit: 24-28 weeks.
  • 3rd Visit: 32 weeks.
  • 4th Visit: 36 weeks.
  • (Note: Newer WHO 2016 guidelines recommend 8 contacts, but 2016 exam context likely refers to the 4-visit model).
2.
A 28 year old lady delivered 24 hrs. ago. she is severely pale and placenta is insitu
a) What is the diagnosis? (3mk)
b) Briefly outline her management (17mk)
Show Answer

a) Diagnosis: Retained Placenta with Postpartum Hemorrhage (and likely Anaemia/Shock).

Note: "Placenta insitu" 24 hours after delivery defines Retained Placenta. "Severely pale" indicates significant blood loss (PPH).

b) Management:

1. Immediate Resuscitation (ABC):

  • Airway/Breathing: Ensure airway is patent, give Oxygen if needed.
  • Circulation: Secure 2 large-bore IV cannulae (16G/18G).
  • Fluids: Rapid infusion of crystalloids (Normal Saline/Ringer's Lactate) to restore volume.
  • Blood: Take blood for Group & Cross-match, Full Haemogram. Transfuse packed red cells/Whole blood urgently due to severe pallor/shock.
  • Position: Flat with legs elevated (Trendelenburg) to improve venous return.
  • Keep Warm: Prevent hypothermia.

2. Definitive Management (Removal of Placenta):

  • Since 24 hours have passed, the cervix may be closing, and infection risk is high. Simple Controlled Cord Traction (CCT) is unlikely to work and may be dangerous (inversion).
  • Manual Removal of Placenta (MROP): Must be done under General Anesthesia or Regional Block in theatre.
  • Procedure:
    • Lithotomy position, aseptic technique.
    • Insert hand into uterus following the cord.
    • Identify cleavage plane between placenta and uterine wall.
    • Gently separate placenta using a sawing motion.
    • Grasp placenta and withdraw hand.
    • Check uterine cavity for remaining fragments.

3. Control of Bleeding (Oxytocics):

  • Oxytocin: 20-40 IU in 1L fluids (infusion) to maintain contraction.
  • Ergometrine: 0.5mg IM/IV (if BP normal) to ensure tonic contraction.
  • Misoprostol: 600-1000mcg Rectally if needed.
  • Uterine Massage: To expel clots and stimulate contraction.

4. Prevention/Treatment of Infection (Sepsis):

  • Patient has retained tissue for 24h (High sepsis risk).
  • Broad-spectrum IV antibiotics (e.g., Ceftriaxone + Metronidazole).

5. Monitoring:

  • Vital signs (BP, Pulse, Temp) every 15-30 mins initially.
  • Vaginal bleeding (Lochia).
  • Urine output (catheterize if needed to monitor renal perfusion).
3.
John and Mary have been married for the last 8 yr. They present to you with history of lack of conception despite regular intercourse of 2-3 time a week. How would you evaluate this couple? (20mk)
Show Answer

Answer: Evaluation of Infertility

This is a case of Primary (or Secondary, history not specified) Infertility. Evaluation involves assessing both partners simultaneously.

1. History Taking (Both Partners)

  • General: Age, duration of infertility (8 yrs), coital frequency (2-3x/week is adequate), technique, lubricants (some are spermicidal).
  • Mary (Female):
    • Menstrual Hx: Regularity (ovulation indicator), dysmenorrhea (endometriosis).
    • Obstetric Hx: Previous pregnancies/abortions (if secondary).
    • Medical/Surgical: PID, STIs, pelvic surgeries (appendectomy, myomectomy), galactorrhea (prolactin).
  • John (Male):
    • Medical/Surgical: Mumps orchitis, trauma, hernia repair, STIs, erectile dysfunction, premature ejaculation.
    • Lifestyle: Smoking, alcohol, heat exposure (tight underwear, occupation).

2. Physical Examination

  • Mary:
    • BMI (Obesity/Underweight).
    • Secondary sexual characteristics (Hirsutism - PCOS).
    • Abdominal exam: Masses (Fibroids).
    • Pelvic exam: Speculum (cervicitis, anomaly), Bimanual (tenderness, mobility, masses).
  • John:
    • Secondary sexual characteristics.
    • Genital exam: Testicular size/consistency, varicocele, palpable vas deferens, hypospadias.

3. Investigations (The Work-up)

Male Factor:

  • Seminal Fluid Analysis (SFA): The first test. Parameters: Volume, Count (>15M/ml), Motility, Morphology.

Female Factor:

  • Ovulation Assessment:
    • Serum Progesterone (Day 21 / Mid-luteal).
    • Basal Body Temperature (BBT) charts.
    • FSH/LH/Prolactin/TSH (Day 2-5 profile).
  • Tubal & Uterine Factors:
    • Hysterosalpingogram (HSG): X-ray to check tubal patency and uterine cavity.
    • Pelvic Ultrasound: Fibroids, PCO, ovarian cysts.
    • Laparoscopy + Dye Test: Gold standard for tubes/endometriosis (if HSG abnormal or unexplained).

4. Conclusion/Plan

  • Review results.
  • Treat identifiable causes (e.g., ovulation induction, surgery for fibroids).
  • Counseling and Assisted Reproductive Technology (ART) referral if needed (IVF/IUI) given 8 years duration.
