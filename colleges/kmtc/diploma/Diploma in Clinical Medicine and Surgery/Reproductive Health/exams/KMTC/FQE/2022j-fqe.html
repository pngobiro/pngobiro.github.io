1)
One of the following is the most common direct cause of maternal death:
a) Complications of abortion
b) Obstructed labour
c) Haemorrhage
d) Sepsis
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Haemorrhage

Explanation: Postpartum Hemorrhage (PPH) is the leading direct cause of maternal mortality globally and in Kenya, accounting for roughly 25-30% of maternal deaths.

2)
Which of the following cohorts is correctly matched as delineated in NHSSP II?
a) Cohort 1 - Early childhood
b) Cohort 2 - Late childhood
c) Cohort 3 - Adulthood
d) Cohort 4 - Adolescence
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Cohort 4 - Adolescence (Generally defined as 13-24 yrs in some frameworks, though KEPH often uses Cohort 1: Pregnancy/Newborn, 2: Early Childhood, 3: Late Childhood/Adolescence, 4: Adulthood).
Correction: Under the Kenya Essential Package for Health (KEPH) defined in NHSSP II:

  • Cohort 1: Pregnancy and Newborn (up to 2 weeks).
  • Cohort 2: Early Childhood (2 weeks - 5 years).
  • Cohort 3: Late Childhood (6 - 12 years).
  • Cohort 4: Adolescents and Youth (13 - 24 years).
  • Cohort 5: Adults (>24 years).
  • Cohort 6: Elderly (>60 years).

Therefore, d) Cohort 4 - Adolescence is the correct match.

3)
Which of the following is NOT a basic emergency obstetric care function?
a) Administration of IV antibiotics
b) Administration of blood transfusion
c) Newborn resuscitation
d) Manual removal of retained placenta
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Administration of blood transfusion

Explanation: Basic Emergency Obstetric and Newborn Care (BEmONC) includes 7 signal functions: 1) IV Antibiotics, 2) IV Oxytocics, 3) IV Anticonvulsants, 4) Manual removal of placenta, 5) Removal of retained products, 6) Assisted vaginal delivery, 7) Newborn resuscitation.
Blood Transfusion and Caesarean Section are functions of Comprehensive EmONC (CEmONC).

c) 3rd degree - sutures overlapped and reducible
d) 2nd degree - sutures apppsed and no overlap

Correct Answer: b) 2nd degree - sutures have overlapped but reducible

9)
Which one of the following is a danger sign in labour?
a) Reduced fetal movements
b) Drainage of liquor
c) ROM
d) Cord prolapse
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Cord prolapse

Explanation: Cord prolapse is an obstetric emergency threatening fetal life. Drainage of liquor/ROM can be normal parts of labor, though they need monitoring. Reduced movements is a danger sign generally, but Cord Prolapse is an acute intrapartum emergency.

10)
Mary has delivered 2 sets of twins, 3 singletons and gives a history of an ectopic pregnancy at 8 weeks’ gestation age. She is currently pregnant at 36 weeks. What is her parity?
a) Para 4+1 gravida 6
b) Para 7+1 gravida 9
c) Para 8+0
d) Para 5+1 gravida 7
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Para 5+1 gravida 7

Explanation:
Parity (P): Number of delivery events >28 weeks.
Events: 2 (twins) + 3 (singletons) = 5.
+1: Ectopic pregnancy (loss <28wks).
Gravidity (G): Total number of pregnancies.
Pregnancies: 2 (twins) + 3 (singletons) + 1 (ectopic) + 1 (current) = 7.

c) RPR

Hb, RPR (Syphilis), and Rhesus grouping are mandatory for all pregnant women everywhere.

12)
Mary has an LMP of 12th March 2021. Calculate her EDD.
a) 19/12/2021
b) 12/12/2021
c) 14/12/2021
d) 14/11/2021
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) 19/12/2021

Explanation: Naegele's Rule: LMP + 7 days, - 3 months, + 1 year (or +9 months).
Days: 12 + 7 = 19
Months: March (3) + 9 = 12 (December)
Year: 2021
Date: 19th December 2021.

13)
Which one of the following is true about hormonal changes associated with menopause?
a) There is no reduction in circulating androgens since there is secretion from developing follicles.
b) Estrogen production is reduced
c) Progesterone remains high and its secreted by adrenal glands.
d) Both FSH & LH rise, but LH being higher than FSH
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Estrogen production is reduced

Explanation: The hallmark of menopause is the depletion of ovarian follicles, leading to drastically reduced Estrogen and Progesterone. The loss of negative feedback causes FSH and LH to rise (hypergonadotropic hypogonadism), with FSH typically rising higher than LH.

16)
Elective induction of labour may be performed when the Bishop score is;
a) 6
b) 3
c) 5
d) 10
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) 10

Explanation: A Bishop score >8 (or sometimes >6) indicates a favorable cervix and high likelihood of successful vaginal delivery similar to spontaneous labor. 10 is the best score listed for elective induction.

17)
One of the following is a fetal indication for induction of labour:
a) Prolonged pregnancy
b) Pre-eclampsia
c) D.M.
d) Chorioamnionitis
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Prolonged pregnancy

Explanation: Prolonged pregnancy (>41/42 weeks) is induced primarily to prevent fetal compromise (post-maturity syndrome, oligohydramnios, stillbirth). While b, c, and d are indications, they are often driven by maternal health deterioration (Pre-eclampsia/DM) or infection risk (Chorio). Prolonged pregnancy is the classic fetal indication in many classifications.

19)
Which one of the following is true concerning Intermittent Preventive Treatment for malaria (IPTp) in Kenya?
a) Is recommended in all areas, in all pregnant women
b) Administer IPTP any time during her pregnancy
c) One dose is sufficient
d) Folic acid should not be administered with IPTP
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Folic acid should not be administered with IPTP (Specifically high dose >5mg)

Explanation:
d: Folic acid antagonizes the action of Sulfadoxine-Pyrimethamine (SP). High doses (5mg) should be withheld for 14 days after SP intake. Low dose (0.4mg) is generally acceptable, but often the guideline simplifies to "withhold folic acid".
a: Only in endemic zones (Lake/Coast).
b: Only after 1st trimester (quickening), at least 4 weeks apart.
c: At least 3 doses (or monthly) are required.

20)
One of the following is true about the use of quinine in management of malaria in pregnancy:
a) Is contraindicated in severe malaria
b) Dose reduction is required in patients with renal insufficiency
c) Administer with glucose infusion to avoid hypoglycemia
d) Patients with hepatic insufficiency need dose reduction by half
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Administer with glucose infusion to avoid hypoglycemia

Explanation: Quinine stimulates pancreatic insulin secretion, causing severe hypoglycemia, especially in pregnant women (who are already prone to it). It should be given in 5% or 10% Dextrose.
a: It is the drug of choice for severe malaria in 1st trimester (and often others).
b: Dose reduction is required in renal failure.

21)
Tubal factor associated with development of ectopic pregnancy is;
a) Neural tube defects
b) Smoking
c) Multiple elective abortion
d) Salpingitis
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Salpingitis

Explanation: Salpingitis (inflammation of the tube, often from PID) causes scarring and damage to ciliated epithelium, preventing ovum transport. This is a direct "Tubal factor". Smoking affects motility (functional tubal factor), but Salpingitis is the classic pathological cause.

22)
Spontaneous isthmic rupture of ectopic pregnancy occurs at;
a) 6-8 weeks
b) 8-12 weeks
c) 12-16 weeks
d) 4-6 weeks
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) 6-8 weeks

Explanation: The isthmus is narrow and non-distensible, leading to early rupture (6-8 weeks). Ampullary ruptures later (8-12 weeks). Interstitial ruptures latest (12-16 weeks).

23)
Probable (symptoms) manifestations of pregnancy include the following except:
a) Leucorrhoea
b) Chloasma
c) Line nigra
d) Stretch marks
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Leucorrhoea

Explanation: This question is slightly confusingly phrased ("Probable (symptoms)"). In classification:
Presumptive (Symptoms): Amenorrhea, Nausea, Frequency, Breast changes.
Probable (Signs observed by examiner): Hegar's sign, Chadwick's sign, Abdominal enlargement, Pigmentation changes (Chloasma, Linea Nigra, Striae/Stretch marks).
Leucorrhoea (discharge) is a non-specific symptom often associated with pregnancy but not typically classified in the standard "Presumptive/Probable/Positive" signs lists in the same way as pigmentation changes. Alternatively, it might be asking which is NOT a cutaneous sign. Chloasma, Linea Nigra, and Stretch marks are skin changes. Leucorrhoea is vaginal.

24)
Jane a para 3+2 presents with per-vaginal bleeding for 1 day and lower abdominal pains radiating for the back – O/E. No pallor, fundal height corresponds to estimated gestation age of 24/40. The cervical OS is open and membranes bulging. What is the most probable diagnosis?
a) Premature labour
b) Inevitable abortion
c) Threatened abortion
d) Incomplete abortion
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Inevitable abortion (or Inevitable Miscarriage)

Explanation:
Features: Bleeding + Pain + Open Os + Bulging Membranes (products not yet expelled).
Differentiation:
- Threatened: Closed Os.
- Incomplete: Passage of fleshy masses/products.
- Premature Labor: Usually defined as >28 weeks in Kenya context (viability), though internationally >24 weeks. However, "Inevitable abortion" is the classic term for open os/bulging membranes before viability. 24 weeks is the borderline, but given the options "Abortion", the examiner likely considers this pre-viability.

35)
Which of the following is a maternal risk factor for MTCT during labour and delivery?
a) APH
b) Anemia
c) STI especially ulcerative
d) Smoking
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) APH (Antepartum Hemorrhage)

Section 2: Short Answer Questions (40 Marks)

41)
Define the following terms as used in R.H:
Show Answer

a) Fertility: The actual production of live offspring (births) by an individual or population.

b) Life expectancy: The average number of years a person is expected to live based on current mortality rates.

c) Perinatal mortality: The number of stillbirths and early neonatal deaths (first 7 days of life) per 1000 total births.

d) Low birth weight: Birth weight of less than 2500 grams (2.5 kg), regardless of gestational age.

e) Maternal mortality rate: The number of maternal deaths per 100,000 live births (Note: Technically "Ratio", but often used interchangeably in exams).

42)
Briefly outline stages of labour in a primigravida at term (5 marks)
Show Answer
  1. First Stage (Dilation): From onset of true labor to full cervical dilation (10cm). Duration approx 10-12 hours.
    • Latent Phase: 0-4cm dilation.
    • Active Phase: 4-10cm dilation.
  2. Second Stage (Expulsion): From full dilation to delivery of the baby. Duration approx 1-2 hours.
    • Propulsive phase: Head descent.
    • Expulsive phase: Mother pushes.
  3. Third Stage (Placental): From delivery of baby to expulsion of placenta and membranes. Duration 15-30 mins (shorter with active management).
  4. Fourth Stage (Observation): The first hour postpartum, monitoring for hemorrhage.
43)
Outline active management of third stage of labour (5 marks)
Show Answer

AMSTL consists of three main steps:

  1. Administration of Uterotonic: Give Oxytocin 10 IU IM within 1 minute of the baby's birth (after ruling out a second twin).
  2. Controlled Cord Traction (CCT): Apply gentle traction to the cord with one hand while applying counter-traction to the uterus just above the pubic bone (Brandt-Andrews maneuver) during a contraction.
  3. Uterine Massage: Massage the fundus immediately after placental delivery until firm to ensure contraction and expel clots. Monitor every 15 mins for 2 hours.

In focused antenatal care, one of its objectives is prevention of complications. Outline four ways how service providers ensure prevention of complications. (5 marks)

  2. Malaria Prophylaxis (IPTp): Administering SP in endemic areas to prevent malaria anemia and LBW.

45)
Mary Joan has delivered a live female infant weighing 3200gms with an APGAR score of 8/1, 9/5, 10/10 three hours ago. She is now on her puerperium.
a) Define puerperium. (2 marks)
b) Classify puerperium. (3 marks)
Show Answer

b) Classification:

46)
In Kenya, cancer of the cervix contributes to 12.9% new cancers and 11.8% cancer deaths annually. Screening programmes continue to have a vital role allowing early detection and achieve a maximal impact on prevention. Outline cervical cancer screening methods available in Kenya. (5 marks)
Show Answer
  1. Visual Inspection with Acetic Acid (VIA): Application of 3-5% acetic acid to cervix; acetowhite lesions indicate abnormality. (Common in low-resource settings).
  2. Visual Inspection with Lugol’s Iodine (VILI): Application of iodine; abnormal cells do not take up stain (yellow areas).

Section 3: Long Answer Question (20 Marks)

1.
Nafula, 22 years old primigravida, presents in maternity with complications of frequent and painful micturition for the last 3 days. On further enquiry, she is NOT sure of her last normal menstrual period since she had an irregular cycle.
a) Discuss how you will try to ascertain her gestational age through history and physical examination. (14 marks)
b) List one (1) investigation which confirms diagnosis of urinary tract infection (1 mark)
c) Write a treatment plan for Nafula. (5 marks)
Show Answer

a) Ascertaining Gestational Age (14 marks)

Through History:

  • Quickening: Ask when she first felt fetal movements. In a primigravida, this occurs around 18-20 weeks.
  • History of early symptoms: Ask about onset of morning sickness (nausea/vomiting), which typically starts at 4-6 weeks and peaks at 8-12 weeks.
  • First positive pregnancy test: Ask when she did the test; typically positive 4 weeks after LMP.
  • Date of conception/coitus: If she recalls a specific date of intercourse (rarely reliable but possible).
  • Early Ultrasound: Check if she had an early dating scan (first trimester), which is the most accurate method.

Through Physical Examination:

  • Fundal Height (Abdominal Palpation):
    • 12 weeks: Fundus palpable just at symphysis pubis.
    • 16 weeks: Midway between symphysis pubis and umbilicus.
    • 20-22 weeks: At the level of the umbilicus.
    • 36 weeks: At the xiphisternum.
    • McDoanld’s Rule: Measure fundal height in cm from symphysis pubis to fundus. After 24 weeks, height in cm approx equals weeks of gestation (+/- 2cm).
  • Fetal Heart Sounds: Audible by Pinard fetoscope from ~20-24 weeks (or Doppler at 10-12 weeks).
  • Abdominal Girth: Less reliable but supportive.

b) Investigation for UTI (1 mark)

Urine Culture and Sensitivity (C&S): This is the gold standard confirmatory test.

(Urinalysis/Dipstick showing nitrites/leukocytes suggests it, but culture confirms it).

c) Treatment Plan (5 marks)

  1. Antibiotics: Prescribe safe antibiotics for pregnancy (e.g., Nitrofurantoin 100mg BID for 7 days, or Amoxicillin/Cephalexin). Avoid Ciprofloxacin or Trimethoprim (1st trimester).
  2. Analgesia: Paracetamol 1g TDS for pain relief/fever.
  3. Hydration: Encourage increased oral fluid intake (water) to flush the urinary tract.
  4. Hygiene: Educate on perineal hygiene (wipe front to back).
  5. Follow-up: Review in 1 week with repeat urine culture (Test of Cure) to ensure clearance.
