1.
Which one of the following is an early sign/symptom of cancer of the cervix?
A. Unexplained weight loss
B. Post-coital bleeding
C. Change in bowel habits
D. Passing stools through vagina
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B. Post-coital bleeding

Explanation: Post-coital bleeding (contact bleeding) is the classic early clinical presentation of cervical cancer. While early stages are often asymptomatic, contact bleeding is the most significant early warning sign.

  • A. Unexplained weight loss: A sign of advanced malignancy (cachexia).
  • C. Change in bowel habits: Usually associated with colorectal cancer or advanced pelvic spread.
  • D. Passing stools through vagina: Indicates a rectovaginal fistula, a complication of very advanced disease or radiation.
2.
Which one of the following is not a risk factor for cervical cancer?
A. Having multiple sexual partners
B. Tobacco use
C. Late onset initiation of sexual intercourse
D. Multiple deliveries
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C. Late onset initiation of sexual intercourse

Explanation: Early onset of sexual activity (usually defined as before age 18 or within 1 year of menarche) is a risk factor because the immature cervical transformation zone is more susceptible to HPV infection. Therefore, late onset is protective, not a risk factor.

Why others are risk factors:

  • A: Increases exposure to HPV.
  • B: Smoking concentrates carcinogens in cervical mucus and suppresses local immunity.
  • D: High parity (multiple deliveries) is associated with increased risk, possibly due to hormonal changes or cervical trauma.

Correct Answer: C. Umbilicus draining pus (and D. Lochia discharge)

B. 8 g/dl

Correct Answer: C. 6-7 g/dl (or <7 g/dl)

7.
Jackline presents at casualty with painless PV bleeding for the last 2 hours. The bleeding is bright red and heavy. No history of lower abdominal pains O/E no tenderness in the abdomen, with a fundal height of 36/40. What is the most likely diagnosis?
A. Bloody show
B. Vasa placenta
C. Abruptio placenta
D. Placenta Previa
Show Answer

Correct Answer: D. Placenta Previa

Explanation: The classic triad for Placenta Previa is painless, causeless (no trauma), and recurrent vaginal bleeding. The uterus is typically soft and non-tender (unlike Abruptio Placenta where the uterus is woody hard and tender). The bleeding is bright red.

  • Abruptio Placenta: Painful, dark blood, tender/hard uterus.
  • Bloody show: Mucoid, sticky, small amount, associated with labor onset.

9.
Maternal causes of preterm labour include the following except;
A. Age <18 years
B. Polyhydramnios
C. UTI
D. Fibroids
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B. Polyhydramnios

Explanation: This is a classification question. Polyhydramnios is typically classified as a uterine or fetal factor (due to over-distension) or related to fetal anomalies/diabetes. The other options are strictly maternal characteristics or conditions: Age (demographic), UTI (maternal infection), Fibroids (maternal anatomy). However, distinction can be vague; Polyhydramnios is the best fit for "except" as it is often secondary to fetal issues (e.g., esophageal atresia) compared to the intrinsic maternal factors listed.

10.
All the following changes occur to the fetal circulation within minutes of birth except;
A. Constriction of umbilical vessels
B. Constriction of hepatic portal sinus
C. Closing of foramen ovale
D. Constriction of ductus venosus
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B. Constriction of hepatic portal sinus

Explanation: The "hepatic portal sinus" is not a standard fetal shunt that closes. The key shunts that close are:

  1. Umbilical arteries/vein: Constrict immediately.
  2. Ductus Venosus: Constricts/closes (functionally within minutes/days).
  3. Foramen Ovale: Closes functionally due to pressure changes (left atrial pressure > right).
  4. Ductus Arteriosus: Constricts.

The hepatic portal system remains patent as the permanent blood supply to the liver.

11.

A. Screening should be done at 1st contact
B. VDRL is a specific test
C. Rapid syphilis test can be used
D. Benzathine penicillin is the drug of choice

Explanation: VDRL (Venereal Disease Research Laboratory) is a non-specific screening test. It detects non-treponemal antibodies and can have false positives (e.g., in pregnancy, malaria, autoimmune diseases). Specific confirmatory tests include TPHA or FTA-ABS. All other statements are true.

Management of labour in a woman with heart disease;
A. Induction of labor
B. Put the patient in supine position
C. Give IV Fluids
D. Use prophylactic antibiotics
Show Answer

Correct Answer: D. Use prophylactic antibiotics (or A/C depending on context, but B is strictly contraindicated)

Explanation: This question might be asking "What is appropriate?" or "What is NOT appropriate?". The prompt phrasing "Management... [includes]" usually seeks the correct action.

24.
Purity, a 22-year-old primi-gravida comes for her 1st ANC visit. She tells you her last normal period was on 20th October, 2022. She has a cycle of 21 days. Which is her EDD?
A. 27/07/2023
B. 20/07/2023
C. 27/08/2023
D. 20/07/2023
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A. 27/07/2023

Explanation: Using Naegele's Rule: Add 7 days to the LMP, Subtract 3 months, Add 1 year.
LMP: 20/10/2022
Days: 20 + 7 = 27
Months: 10 - 3 = 7 (July)
Year: 2023
Result: 27/07/2023.
Note: The 21-day cycle might require an adjustment (-7 days from standard 28-day calculation), making it 20/07/2023, but usually, basic questions stick to the standard formula unless specifically testing cycle adjustments. Option A matches the standard calculation.

25.
Margaret, 36 years old at a gestation of 32 weeks comes to your facility for her routine clinic. She has five children at home, with history of twinning on 2 occasions, one ectopic at 10 weeks. What is her gravidity and parity?
A. P5+0 G 6
B. Para 5 +1 G6
C. Para 3 + 1 G 5
D. P 3 + 1 G 5 with 5 living children
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C. Para 3 + 1 G 5 (or D)

Explanation:
Parity (P): Number of deliveries >28 weeks.
Children: 5 kids. History of twins x2. This implies 2 deliveries produced 4 kids. To get 5 kids, she needs 1 more singleton delivery.
Deliveries = Twin(1) + Twin(1) + Single(1) = Para 3.
+1: Ectopic pregnancy (loss <28wks).
Gravidity (G): Total pregnancies.
G = 3 (Deliveries) + 1 (Ectopic) + 1 (Current Pregnancy) = G 5.
Result: Para 3 + 1, Gravida 5.

  • Testicular issues: Undescended testes (cryptorchidism), Varicocele, Mumps orchitis.
  • Obstruction: Blockage of vas deferens (post-infection or congenital).
  • Erectile/Ejaculatory dysfunction: Impotence, Retrograde ejaculation.
  • Lifestyle/Environmental: Excessive alcohol, Smoking, Heat exposure to testes.

ii. History Taking

Female History Factors (5):

  • Menstrual History: Regularity, duration, and pain (to assess ovulation/endometriosis).
  • Obstetric History: Previous pregnancies, abortions, or complications (e.g., sepsis).
  • Sexual History: Frequency of coitus, dyspareunia, use of lubricants.
  • Medical/Surgical History: History of PID, STIs, abdominal surgeries (appendectomy), or chronic illnesses (thyroid/diabetes).
  • Contraceptive History: Previous methods used (e.g., IUCD risk of infection).

Male History Factors (5):

  • Medical History: History of Mumps orchitis, STIs, or trauma to testes.
  • Surgical History: Hernia repair, Undescended testes surgery.
  • Sexual History: Ability to achieve erection/ejaculation, timing/technique.
  • Lifestyle/Occupational: Smoking, Alcohol, exposure to heat/chemicals/radiation.
  • Drug History: Use of medications affecting sperm (e.g., steroids, chemotherapy, antihypertensives).
