Section 1: Multiple Choice Questions

40 Marks
1.
Which of the fetal skull diameters presents in face presentation?
A. Submento-bregmatic
B. Mento-vertical
C. Frontal -occipital
D. Sub occipito – bregmatic
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A. Submento-bregmatic

Explanation: In a face presentation with full extension of the head, the engaging diameter is the submento-bregmatic diameter (approximately 9.5 cm). The mento-vertical diameter (13.5 cm) is the diameter of the head that attempts to engage if extension is incomplete (brow presentation), but in a fully extended face presentation, the submento-bregmatic engages.

2.
Mary, is a 22-year-old primigravida who presents to the outpatient clinic with history of amenorrhea of 8 weeks, per vaginal bleeding and lower abdominal pain for one day. On examination, she is in fair general condition, mildly pale, blood pressure at 100/60 mmHg, pulse rate at 100 beats per minute. She has abdominal tenderness on palpation. Pelvic examination reveals a bulky uterus and an adnexal mass. You make an impression of ectopic pregnancy. Which of the following investigations will confirm your diagnosis?
A. High levels of serum Human chorionic gonadotrophic hormone
B. Ultrasound showing carneous mole
C. Positive pregnancy test
D. Pelvic ultrasound showing gestation outside the uterine cavity
Show Answer

Correct Answer: D. Pelvic ultrasound showing gestation outside the uterine cavity

Explanation: A pelvic ultrasound (specifically transvaginal) visualizing a gestational sac or fetal pole with cardiac activity in the adnexa (outside the uterus) is the definitive confirmation of an ectopic pregnancy. A positive pregnancy test confirms pregnancy but not location. High HCG can occur in molar pregnancy. Carneous mole is associated with missed abortion.

3.
Which of the following is an indication of trial of scar?
A. One previous caesarian section due to fetal distress
B. Two previous scar with adequate pelvis
C. One previous caesarian section due to cephalo-pelvic disproportion
D. One previous caesarean section due to pelvic fractures
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A. One previous caesarian section due to fetal distress

Explanation: A trial of labor after cesarean (TOLAC) is indicated if the previous indication was non-recurrent (like fetal distress). CPD (C) and pelvic fractures (D) are recurrent/permanent causes of obstruction, making TOLAC unsafe. Two previous scars (B) generally contraindicate vaginal delivery due to high rupture risk.

4.
The following is true about placenta previa:
A. Associated with coagulopathy
B. May present with no per vaginal bleeding
C. Is associate with couvelaire uterus
D. Type II covers the internal os
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B. May present with no per vaginal bleeding

Explanation: While painless vaginal bleeding is the classic symptom, some cases of placenta previa may be asymptomatic (diagnosed by ultrasound) before bleeding starts. A and C are associated with Abruptio Placentae. Type II (Marginal) reaches the edge of the os but does not cover it (Type III/IV cover it).

5.
Concerning antenatal blood tests which of the following statements is correct?
A. Syphilis test is no longer necessary
B. HIV test requires the consent of the spouse
C. Indirect coombs tests is necessary in a rhesus negative mother
D. Hepatitis B surface antigen test is mandatory
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C. Indirect coombs tests is necessary in a rhesus negative mother

Explanation: The Indirect Coombs Test (Antibody Screen) is vital for Rh-negative mothers to detect if they have already been sensitized (developed antibodies) to Rh-positive blood, which guides the administration of Anti-D immunoglobulin.

6.
Ruptured ectopic pregnancy is associated with the following finding………
A. Defibrinated intraperitoneal blood
B. WBCs in urine
C. Decreased pelvic blood flow on Doppler study
D. Leukocytosis
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A. Defibrinated intraperitoneal blood

Explanation: When an ectopic pregnancy ruptures, blood accumulates in the peritoneal cavity. This blood undergoes defibrination (loses clotting factors) and does not clot on aspiration (culdocentesis), which is a diagnostic sign.

7.
Which of the following is the largest fetal skull diameter?
A. Suboccipito – bregmatic
B. Oocipito – frontal
C. Mento-vertical
D. Submento –bregmatic
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C. Mento-vertical

Explanation: The Mento-vertical diameter is approximately 13.5 cm, making it the largest anteroposterior diameter. This presents in a Brow presentation and usually prevents vaginal delivery. Suboccipito-bregmatic (9.5cm) is the smallest.

8.
Type 1 endometrial carcinoma is associated with?
A. Unopposed estrogen exposure
B. Low grade tumour
C. Associated with endometrial atrophy
D. Poor differentiation
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A. Unopposed estrogen exposure

Explanation: Type 1 endometrial cancer (Endometrioid) is estrogen-dependent and associated with unopposed estrogen stimulation (obesity, nulliparity, exogenous estrogen). Type 2 is non-estrogen dependent and associated with atrophy.

9.
Which one of the following is an ovarian germ cell tumor?
A. Dysgerminoma
B. Granulosa cell tumour
C. Dermoid cyst
D. Epithelial cell tumour
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C. Dermoid cyst (also A. Dysgerminoma)

Explanation: Both Dysgerminoma and Dermoid Cyst (Mature Cystic Teratoma) are germ cell tumors. However, Dermoid cysts are the most common benign ovarian tumors in young women. Often in MCQs, if multiple are correct, the most common or representative one is chosen. Granulosa is a sex cord-stromal tumor.

10.
The following statement is true about Cardiac disease in pregnancy:
A. Complicates 5% of pregnancies
B. Majority are due to acquired heart diseases
C. Class III is admitted in the hospital for the duration of the pregnancy from 28 weeks
D. Anticoagulants should be discontinued in the first trimester for patients with valve replacement
Show Answer

Correct Answer: D. Anticoagulants should be discontinued in the first trimester for patients with valve replacement

Explanation: Warfarin (a common anticoagulant) is teratogenic (causes birth defects) in the first trimester (Warfarin Embryopathy). It is typically switched to Heparin (LMWH or UFH) during the first trimester (6-12 weeks) and then resumed until late pregnancy.

11.
Which of the statements is true about endometriosis?
A. Is a condition that causes cyclical abdominal pains
B. Is a condition that causes constant abdominal pain
C. Is a common cause of tubal infertility
D. Is a condition that causes anovulation
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A. Is a condition that causes cyclical abdominal pains

Explanation: Endometriosis is defined by the presence of endometrial-like tissue outside the uterus. This tissue responds to the menstrual cycle, bleeding cyclically, which causes inflammation and cyclical pain (dysmenorrhea) coinciding with menstruation.

12.
The following are risk factors associated with endometrial carcinoma:
A. multiparity
B. smoking
C. Exogenous progesterone
D. Diabetes mellitus
Show Answer

Correct Answer: D. Diabetes mellitus

Explanation: Risk factors for endometrial cancer (Type 1) include obesity, nulliparity, diabetes mellitus, hypertension, and unopposed estrogen. Multiparity and smoking are actually protective (smoking reduces estrogen levels). Progesterone is protective.

13.
Warfarin during pregnancy…………
A. Enhances antithrombin III activity.
B. Exerts an anticoagulant effect on the breast fed infant
C. Peak effect occurs within 24 hours of drug administration
D. Enhances vitamin K dependent gamma-carboxylation of coagulation factors
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C. Peak effect occurs within 24 hours of drug administration (Wait, mechanism is wrong in D, A is Heparin). Actually Warfarin inhibits Vitamin K dependent factors. It does not affect breastfed infants (B is false, it's safe in breastfeeding). It takes 36-72 hours for full effect. The question might be looking for "Crosses placenta" (not an option). Let's re-evaluate options based on negatives.

Correction: Warfarin inhibits gamma-carboxylation (D is false). Heparin enhances Antithrombin III (A is false). Warfarin does not pass into breast milk significantly (B is false). Option C is the most plausible regarding pharmacokinetics, though full effect takes days, the onset starts. However, none are perfect. In many contexts, B is a distractor because it's safe. Warfarin is TERATOGENIC (not an option here).

Note: Based on elimination, C is the most likely intended answer in exam contexts, or there might be a typo in D (Inhibits vs Enhances).

14.
Which of the following is a common cause of infertility in males?
A. Undescended testis
B. Azoospermia
C. Impotence
D. Vasectomy
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A. Undescended testis

Explanation: Undescended testis (Cryptorchidism) is a major risk factor for testicular failure and infertility due to thermal damage to germ cells. Azoospermia (B) is a diagnosis/finding, not a cause itself (it has causes like obstruction). Vasectomy (D) is voluntary sterilization.

15.
The following anti-retroviral (ARV) drug is contraindicated in the first trimester of pregnancy:
A. Nevirapine
B. Lamivudine
C. Efavirenz
D. Zidovudine
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C. Efavirenz

Explanation: Historically, Efavirenz was contraindicated in the first trimester due to concerns about neural tube defects. While WHO guidelines have evolved to allow it, in many exam contexts based on older protocols, Efavirenz is the classic answer for teratogenicity concern in 1st trimester. DTG is now preferred.

16.
The following is true pertaining choriocarcinoma:
A. About 50% follow hydatidiform mole
B. Triple therapy is used for low risk
C. Cannon balls seen on chest X-ray is not suggestive
D. Suction curettage is the treatment of choice
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A. About 50% follow hydatidiform mole

Explanation: Choriocarcinoma is a malignant trophoblastic tumor. Approximately 50% of cases arise after a Hydatidiform Mole, 25% after abortion, and 25% after normal pregnancy. "Cannon ball" metastases in lungs are characteristic (so C is false). Chemotherapy (Methotrexate) is the treatment, not curettage (D).

17.
The following statement is true about labour:
A. Active stage starts from onset of labour up to full cervical dilatation
B. Multiparous women progress slower as compared to primigravida
C. Partograph is a useful tool to predict the outcome.
D. Precipitate labour lasts for 12 hours.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C. Partograph is a useful tool to predict the outcome.

Explanation: The partograph monitors labor progress (dilation, descent, contractions) and fetal/maternal condition, allowing early detection of abnormal labor and prediction of outcomes (need for intervention). A is false (Active phase starts at 4cm). B is false (Multis are faster). D is false (Precipitate labor is < 3 hours).

18.
Pap smear during pregnancy is?
A. Contraindicated
B. Of poor diagnostic importance
C. Part of normal work-up
D. Consistently misinterpreted.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C. Part of normal work-up

Explanation: A Pap smear can be safely performed during pregnancy (usually at the first ANC visit) if the patient is due for screening. It is not contraindicated, though endocervical brush use should be careful.

19.
The android pelvis………..
A. Occurs in 50% women
B. Is wedge shaped
C. Side wall are straight
D. The sacrum is inclined backwards
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B. Is wedge shaped (Inlet/Anterior segment)

Explanation: The Android pelvis (Male type) typically has a heart-shaped inlet. However, the anterior segment is often described as narrow and wedge-shaped. The sidewalls are convergent (funneling), not straight. The sacrum is flat/straight. Anthropoid is oval. Gynecoid is round.

20.
A primigravida has presented to antenatal clinic for the first time at 26 weeks. How many tetanus toxoid vaccine doses will you administer?
A. One at first contact
B. Three one month apart
C. One booster dose.
D. Two doses one month apart
Show Answer

Correct Answer: D. Two doses one month apart

Explanation: For a primigravida (assumed unimmunized), the schedule is:
TT1: At first contact (26 weeks)
TT2: 4 weeks after TT1
This ensures protection for birth. TT3 would be 6 months later.

30.
Which of the following is the earliest fetal sign of uterine rupture in women undergoing a trial of labour following prior caesarian section?
A. Change in presentation
B. Meconium staining
C. Loss of fetal station
D. Fetal heart rate deceleration
Show Answer

Correct Answer: D. Fetal heart rate deceleration (Prolonged Bradycardia)

Explanation: The most consistent and earliest sign of uterine rupture is Fetal Heart Rate abnormalities, specifically prolonged deceleration or bradycardia, often preceding maternal signs like pain or bleeding.

Section 2: Short Answer Questions

40 Marks
41.
Describe 2 types of delays associated with maternal morbidity and mortality and their contributing factors. (5 Marks)
Show Answer

Answer: The Three Delays Model:

  1. Phase 1 Delay: Decision to seek care
    • Contributing factors: Low status of women, poor understanding of complications/risk signs, previous bad experience with healthcare, financial cost, cultural beliefs.
  2. Phase 2 Delay: Reaching the medical facility
    • Contributing factors: Distance to facility, poor road infrastructure, lack of transportation, cost of transport.
  3. Phase 3 Delay: Receiving adequate care at the facility (Optional 3rd)
    • Contributing factors: Shortage of staff/supplies, lack of equipment, incompetence, poor referral systems.
42.
Explain five (5) secondary prevention measures of cervical cancer (5 Marks)
Show Answer

Answer: Secondary prevention focuses on early detection and treatment of precancerous lesions (screening). Measures include:

  1. Pap Smear (Cytology): Collection of cervical cells to detect abnormal changes (dysplasia).
  2. Visual Inspection with Acetic Acid (VIA): Application of acetic acid to visualize acetowhite lesions indicative of CIN.
  3. Visual Inspection with Lugol's Iodine (VILI): Using iodine to identify non-staining abnormal areas (Schiller's test).
  4. HPV DNA Testing: Screening for high-risk HPV types (e.g., 16, 18).
  5. Colposcopy: Diagnostic visualization of the cervix for biopsy of suspicious areas found during screening.

44.
A 30yr old Para 3+0 Gravida 4 presents to you with a history of 14 weeks of amenorrhea, per vaginal bleeding on and off and a positive pregnancy test done at 10weeks gestation. No palpable mass on abdominal examination and a pelvic scan showed a bulky uterus with fluid collection. A repeat pregnancy test is negative.
a. What is the most likely diagnosis. (1 Mark)
b. Outline the management options. (4 Marks)
Show Answer

a) Diagnosis: Missed Abortion (Early Fetal Demise).

The negative repeat test suggests HCG levels have fallen (fetal death). Fluid in uterus without a viable fetus suggests resorption/missed abortion.

b) Management Options:

  1. Expectant Management: Waiting for spontaneous expulsion (usually for 1-2 weeks) if patient is stable.
  2. Medical Management: Use of Misoprostol (Cytotec) vaginal/oral to induce uterine contractions and expulsion.
  3. Surgical Management: Manual Vacuum Aspiration (MVA) or Dilatation and Curettage (D&C) (Evacuation of Retained Products of Conception - ERPOC) if heavy bleeding, infection, or patient preference.
  4. Supportive Care: Analgesics, antibiotics if indicated, anti-D if Rh negative, and psychological support/counseling.
45.
Joan, 24 years primigravida at 20/40 is referred to you from ANC due blood group B Rhesus negative. Describe your management plan till delivery (5 Marks)
Show Answer

Answer: Management of Rh Negative Mother:

  1. Testing Spouse: Determine the blood group/Rhesus status of the father. If he is Rh Negative, no further action needed (fetus will be negative). If Positive, proceed.
  2. Antibody Screening (Indirect Coombs Test): Perform at booking (done), repeat at 28 weeks and 34 weeks to check for sensitization (antibodies).
  3. Prophylaxis (Anti-D): If Coombs test is negative (non-sensitized), administer Anti-D Immunoglobulin (RhoGAM) at 28 weeks to prevent sensitization during the third trimester.
  4. Event Management: Administer Anti-D within 72 hours of any sensitizing event (bleeding, amniocentesis, trauma).
  5. Delivery Plan: Plan for delivery in a facility. At birth, test baby's blood group. If baby is Rh Positive, give full dose Anti-D to mother within 72 hours.
46.
Enumerate 5 investigations done for infertility and state their significance (5 Marks)
Show Answer

Answer:

  1. Seminal Fluid Analysis (SFA): Assessing male factor (sperm count, motility, morphology).
  2. Hysterosalpingogram (HSG): To assess tubal patency and uterine cavity shape (tubal factor).
  3. Day 21 Progesterone: To confirm ovulation (ovulatory factor).

48.
A 28-year-old Para 3+0 Gravida 4 at 32 weeks’ gestation presents to you at the clinic with history of copious drainage of clear fluid per vaginally following a fall 4hours ago. However, she reports adequate fetal movements.
a. What is the most likely diagnosis (1 Mark)
b. Outline the plan of management (4 Marks)
Show Answer

a) Diagnosis: Preterm Premature Rupture of Membranes (PPROM) (Traumatic).

b) Management Plan (Conservative/Expectant):

  1. Admission: Admit for bed rest and monitoring.
  2. Prophylactic Antibiotics: Erythromycin (250mg QID x 10 days) to prevent chorioamnionitis and prolong latency.
  3. Corticosteroids: Dexamethasone (6mg IM q12h x 4 doses) to promote fetal lung maturity.
  4. Monitoring: Daily fetal kick counts, regular temperature checks (for chorioamnionitis), HVS for culture.
  5. Delivery: Plan for delivery at 34 weeks or sooner if signs of infection (fever, tachycardia) or fetal distress develop.

Section 3: Long Answer Question

20 Marks
49.
Quinter a 24-year-old woman presents to you at 30 weeks’ gestation at Bikeke health center for her second routine antenatal visit. However, she is complaining of severe headache. Severe epigastric pain and some visual disturbance. Her laboratory investigations indicate that she has elevated protein in urine, but no red or white blood cells. Her blood pressure is 190/120 temperature is 36.8 °C and her pulse is 76b/min. she has lost 2 other pregnancies at 22 and 24 weeks due to the same condition. Just when you are about to lead her into the examination room, she convulses.
a) Describe 4 other relevant investigations that can be done to Quinter and indicate their significance. (8 Marks)
b) Describe the Management of Quinter. (8 Marks)
c) State 4 (four) foetal complications that are likely to arise (4marks)
Show Answer

Diagnosis: Eclampsia (Severe Pre-eclampsia with convulsions).

a) Relevant Investigations & Significance:

  1. Full Blood Count (Platelets): To check for Thrombocytopenia (HELLP syndrome). Low platelets increase bleeding risk.
  2. Liver Function Tests (AST/ALT): To assess for liver enzyme elevation (HELLP syndrome) indicating liver damage/ischemia.
  3. Renal Function Tests (Urea/Creatinine/Uric Acid): To assess kidney function (renal impairment is a complication of severe PE).
  4. Coagulation Profile (PT/PTT): To rule out Disseminated Intravascular Coagulation (DIC).
  5. Obstetric Ultrasound/Doppler: To assess fetal growth (IUGR), amniotic fluid, and umbilical artery flow (fetal well-being).

b) Management of Quinter:

  1. Resuscitation (ABC): Place in left lateral position, clear airway, administer Oxygen. Protect from injury during fit.
  2. Control Convulsions (Magnesium Sulfate):
    • Loading Dose: 4g IV (20%) over 10-15 mins + 10g IM (50%) (5g each buttock).
    • Maintenance: 5g IM every 4 hours.
    • Monitor: RR >16, Reflexes present, Urine output >30ml/hr.
  3. Control Blood Pressure: Given BP 190/120 (Hypertensive Crisis).
    • Hydralazine (5mg IV slow) or Labetalol (20mg IV) or Nifedipine (10-20mg oral). Target DBP 90-100mmHg.
  4. Fluid Management: Restrict fluids (80ml/hr) to prevent pulmonary edema. Catheterize to monitor output.
  5. Obstetric Management: Stabilization is priority. Once stable, Deliver. Eclampsia is an absolute indication for delivery regardless of gestation (30 weeks). Corticosteroids may be given if time permits but should not delay urgent delivery if unstable.

c) Fetal Complications:

  1. Intrauterine Growth Restriction (IUGR): Due to placental insufficiency.
  2. Prematurity: Iatrogenic preterm delivery is often required.
  3. Intrauterine Fetal Death (IUFD): Due to abruption or severe hypoxia.
  4. Fetal Asphyxia/Hypoxia: During maternal seizures.
  5. Respiratory Distress Syndrome (RDS): Due to prematurity.
