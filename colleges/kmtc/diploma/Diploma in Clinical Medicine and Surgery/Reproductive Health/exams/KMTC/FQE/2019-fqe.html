Section A: Multiple Choice Questions (40 Marks)

1.
Which of the following is an ovarian germ cell tumour?
a. Dysgerminoma
b. Granulose cell tumor
c. Dermoid cyst
d. Epithelial cell tumor
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a. Dysgerminoma

Explanation: Dysgerminoma is the most common malignant germ cell tumor of the ovary. Dermoid cyst (Mature Teratoma) is also a germ cell tumor (benign), but in the context of classification questions listing "tumours", Dysgerminoma is the classic malignant prototype often tested. Granulosa cell tumor is a sex cord-stromal tumor.

2.
Which of the following is an absolute contra-indication to the IUCD use?
a. A history of pelvic inflammatory disease
b. Previous pregnancy with an IUCD four years ago
c. Nulliparity
d. Abnormal genital bleeding
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d. Abnormal genital bleeding

Explanation: Unexplained abnormal vaginal bleeding is an absolute contraindication (WHO MEC Category 4) because it may indicate underlying pathology like cervical or endometrial cancer, which needs investigation before insertion.

3.
Multiple pregnancy;
a. Has a lower risk for morbidity compared to singleton
b. Twins occur with an incidence of 1:6400 births globally
c. Dichorionic placentation is observed in both monozygotic and dizygotic twins
d. Delivery is by caesarian section.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c. Dichorionic placentation is observed in both monozygotic and dizygotic twins

Explanation: All dizygotic twins are dichorionic. Monozygotic twins that split early (Days 0-3, Morula stage) also develop dichorionic placentation.
b: 1:6400 is the rate for triplets (Hellin's rule: $1/80^2$). Twins are ~1:80.
a: Morbidity is higher.

4.
Cardiac disease,
a. Complicates 5% of pregnancies
b. 70% are congenital heart diseases
c. Class III is admitted in the hospital for the duration of the pregnancy from 28 weeks
d. Anticoagulants should be discontinued in the first trimester for patients with valve replacement
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c. Class III is admitted in the hospital for the duration of the pregnancy from 28 weeks

Explanation: NYHA Class III patients have marked limitation of activity and are at high risk of decompensation as hemodynamic burden peaks (28-32 weeks), requiring hospitalization/bed rest.
d: Warfarin is stopped, but anticoagulation continues (switched to Heparin). "Discontinued" implies stopping therapy entirely, which is incorrect.

5.
Endometriosis;
a. Is a condition that causes constant abdominal pains
b. Is a condition that causes cyclical abdominal pains
c. Is a common cause of tubal infertility
d. Is a condition that causes anovulation
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b. Is a condition that causes cyclical abdominal pains

Explanation: The hallmark symptom is secondary dysmenorrhea (cyclical pain corresponding to menstruation) due to bleeding of ectopic endometrial tissue.

6.
The following are associated with endometrial carcinoma except:
a. History of breast cancer
b. Chronic anovulation
c. Exogenous progesterones
d. Diabetes mellitus
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c. Exogenous progesterones

Explanation: Unopposed estrogen is the risk factor. Progesterones (e.g., in combined OCPs or Mirena) are protective against endometrial hyperplasia and cancer.

7.
Warfarin during pregnancy,
a. Enhances vitamin K dependent gamma-carboxylation of coagulation factors
b. Peak effect occurs within 24 hours of drug administration
c. Exerts an anticoagulant effect on the breast fed infant
d. Affects bone and cartilage formation
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d. Affects bone and cartilage formation

Explanation: Fetal Warfarin Syndrome (chondrodysplasia punctata) occurs if used in the 1st trimester, affecting bone/cartilage development.

8.
The following factors are responsible for the development of pregnancy induced insulin resistance except:
a. Increased placental secretion of growth hormone
b. Increased secretion of corticotrophin
c. Decreased secretion of human chorionic somatostatin
d. A Post receptor defect that may contribute to the decline in insulin action
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c. Decreased secretion of human chorionic somatostatin

Explanation: The primary diabetogenic hormones are hPL (Human Placental Lactogen), Progesterone, Cortisol, and Growth Hormone. Somatostatin changes are not a primary driver of resistance.

9.
Which of the following is not important in the diagnosis of pelvic abscess;
a. Lower abdominal pain and swelling
b. Positive VDRL test
c. Painful defecation and lower back pain
d. Leukocytosis on full haemogram
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b. Positive VDRL test

Explanation: VDRL screens for Syphilis, which causes ulcers/rashes, not acute pelvic abscesses. Abscesses present with pain, mass effect (painful defecation), and signs of infection (leukocytosis).

10.
Concerning antenatal blood tests,
a. Syphilis test is no longer necessary
b. HIV test requires the consent of the spouse
c. Indirect coombs test is not necessary in a rhesus negative mother
d. Hepatitis B surface antigen test is recommended.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d. Hepatitis B surface antigen test is recommended.

Explanation: HBsAg screening is standard to prevent vertical transmission.
c: Indirect Coombs IS necessary for Rh-negative mothers to check for sensitization.

11.
Which of the following is the earliest fetal sign of uterine rupture in women undergoing a trial of labour following prior caesarian section,
a. Change in presentation
b. Meconium staining
c. Loss of fetal station
d. Fetal heart rate deceleration
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d. Fetal heart rate deceleration

Explanation: Fetal bradycardia/decelerations are the most consistent early sign of uterine rupture due to placental compromise.

12.
Which of the following is a feature of missed abortion;
a. Hyperemesis gravidarum
b. Irregular gestational sac on ultrasound
c. Bright red heavy per vaginal bleeding
d. Absence of a fetal pole
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b. Irregular gestational sac on ultrasound

Explanation: A missed abortion (silent miscarriage) is characterized by retention of non-viable products. Ultrasound typically shows a deformed/irregular sac or absence of cardiac activity (embryonic demise). Heavy bleeding (c) suggests incomplete/inevitable abortion.

13.
The following is true about placenta Previa,
a. Associated with coagulopathy
b. May present with no per vaginal bleeding
c. Is associated with couvelaire uterus
d. Type II covers the internal os
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b. May present with no per vaginal bleeding

Explanation: It can be an incidental finding on ultrasound in an asymptomatic patient.
a/c: Associated with Abruptio Placenta.
d: Type II is marginal (reaches edge). Type III/IV cover the os.

14.
Which of the following statements is NOT true?
a. HPV has been detected in over 90% of cancer of the cervix
b. Chlamydia are able to grow in a cell free media
c. Chlamydia are responsible for lymphogranulomavenerium
d. Chlamydia salpingitis is associated with Fitz-Hugh Curtis syndrome
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b. Chlamydia are able to grow in a cell free media

Explanation: Chlamydia is an obligate intracellular pathogen and cannot grow in cell-free media.

15.
As regards MTCT of HIV,
a. MTCT is nearly 60% for a HIV positive mother
b. When the maternal viral load is undetectable MTCT does not occur
c. Mixed infant feeding does have no effect on HIV transmission rate
d. Proper interventions may reduce the transmission(MTCT) rate to 2%
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d. Proper interventions may reduce the transmission(MTCT) rate to 2%

Explanation: With comprehensive PMTCT (ART, safe delivery, prophylaxis), transmission rates can be reduced to <2%.

16.
In female surgical sterilization,
a. Tubal ligation is best done during the luteal phase
b. Non- absorbable suture material is ideal in tubal ligation
c. Histology of the fallopian segment is recommended
d. Tubal ligation is best done at the fimbrial part
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c. Histology of the fallopian segment is recommended

Explanation: To confirm that the structure ligated/excised was indeed the fallopian tube (medicolegal proof of sterilization).

17.
Induction of ovulation with clomiphene citrate:
a. Is indicated in hypothalamo-pituitary dysfunction
b. Multiple pregnancy rate is 15%
c. Is indicated in patients with premature ovarian failure
d. Is indicated in patients with Asherman syndrome
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a. Is indicated in hypothalamo-pituitary dysfunction (specifically WHO Group II - PCOS)

Explanation: It works by blocking estrogen feedback, increasing FSH.
c: Not effective in ovarian failure (no follicles).
b: Multiple rate is 5-10%.

18.
The stage of the gestational development at which endometrial implantation occurs is:
a. Capacitation
b. Cleavage
c. The blastocyst
d. Morula formation
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c. The blastocyst

Explanation: Implantation occurs 6-7 days post-fertilization at the blastocyst stage.

19.
Which of the following statements about amniotic fluid is correct?
a. The volume has no prognostic value in pregnancy
b. An increase in volume is usually associated with fetal chromosomal abnormalities
c. A decrease in volume is associated with fetal chromosomal abnormalities
d. Amniotic fluid is solely from the amnion at 36 weeks.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b. An increase in volume is usually associated with fetal chromosomal abnormalities

Explanation: Polyhydramnios is associated with anomalies (e.g., GI obstruction) and chromosomal defects.
d: Fetal urine is the main source at 36 weeks.

20.
Which of the following statements is true of maternal physiological changes during pregnancy?
a. Cardiac output increases by 20% in the first trimester
b. Respiratory rate increases secondary to the effects of estradiol
c. Renal plasma flow increases by 50%
d. There is no change in the stroke volume
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c. Renal plasma flow increases by 50%

Explanation: Significant increase in RPF and GFR occurs early in pregnancy.

21.
Hyperemesis gravidarum is commonly encountered in pregnancy. Which of the following is not an associated finding?
a. Dehydration
b. Electrolyte imbalance
c. Hematocrit reduction
d. Weight loss
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c. Hematocrit reduction

Explanation: Dehydration causes hemoconcentration (increase in hematocrit), not reduction.

22.
Serious complications of ovarian tumors during pregnancy include all of the following EXCEPT;
a. Torsion of the ovary
b. Malignancy
c. Rupture of the tumor
d. Premature labour
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d. Premature labour

Explanation: Torsion, Rupture, and Malignancy are intrinsic complications of the tumor itself. Premature labor is an obstetric consequence/outcome of the acute abdomen or surgery.

23.
Ruptured ectopic pregnancy is associated with;
a. Defibrinated intraperitoneal blood
b. WBCs in urine
c. Decreased pelvic blood flow on Doppler study
d. Leukocytosis
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a. Defibrinated intraperitoneal blood

Explanation: Blood in the peritoneal cavity loses its clotting factors (defibrination), which is why culdocentesis yields non-clotting blood.

24.
Which one of the following statements is not true regarding focused antenatal care(FANC);
a. First visit is before 12 weeks
b. Only four visits are recommended
c. Second visit is around 26 weeks
d. Forth visit can be at 36 weeks
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b. Only four visits are recommended

Explanation: 4 visits was the minimum in the old FANC model (now updated to 8 contacts). "Only four" implies a restriction, which is false; more are encouraged if needed.

25.
Concerning prolonged latent phase of labour,
a. Fetal distress is a likely outcome
b. The maximum cervical dilatation is 2cm
c. May be associated with urinary tract infection
d. Management may involve ARM together with prostaglandins
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c. May be associated with urinary tract infection

Explanation: UTI is a common cause of "false labor" or prolonged latent phase contractions.

26.
Type 1 endometrial carcinoma is associated with;
a. Unopposed estrogen exposure
b. Low grade tumor
c. Associated with endometrial atrophy
d. Poor differentiation
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a. Unopposed estrogen exposure

Explanation: Type 1 (Endometrioid) is estrogen-dependent. Type 2 is associated with atrophy (Option C).

27.
Which one of the following is a cause of secondary amenorrhea;
a. Sheehan’s syndrome
b. Addison’s disease
c. Pituitary stalk sarcoidosis
d. Polycystic ovarian syndrome
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d. Polycystic ovarian syndrome (and a, c)

Explanation: PCOS is the most common cause of secondary amenorrhea. Sheehan's syndrome (Postpartum pituitary necrosis) also causes it. In MCQs with multiple correct causes, the most common (PCOS) is typically the intended answer.

28.
The following statement is correct concerning the puerperium:
a. Regeneration of placental site in the endometrium is complete by 4th week
b. Endocervical glandular hyperplasia may persist up to 12 weeks
c. Lochia secretions cease by 3rd week
d. Proteinuria only occurs if there is hypertensive disease of pregnancy

Correct Answer: a. Regeneration of placental site in the endometrium is complete by 4th week (Standard text usually says 6 weeks, but A is the best approximation compared to others).

Explanation:
a: Placental site involution takes up to 6 weeks, but general endometrial regeneration occurs by week 3.
c: Lochia lasts 4-6 weeks.

Section B: Short Essay Questions (40 Marks)

Answer all questions. Each question carries 5 marks.

1.
Focused antenatal care (FANC) is one of the pillars of safe motherhood; Outline 5 elements of FANC (5 Marks)
Show Answer
  1. Early detection and treatment of complications: Screening for pre-eclampsia, anemia, syphilis.
  2. Prevention of diseases: Tetanus immunization, Malaria prophylaxis (IPTp), Deworming.
  3. Birth Preparedness and Complication Readiness (BP/CR): Identifying birth plan, funds, transport, donor.
  4. Health Promotion/Counseling: Nutrition, hygiene, danger signs, family planning.
  5. Management of existing conditions: HIV, Hypertension, Diabetes.
2.
Active management of third stage of labour (AMSTL) lowers the risk of post-partum haemorrhage; Describe how you will actively manage third stage of labour. (5 marks)
Show Answer
  1. Administer Uterotonic: Give Oxytocin 10 IU IM within 1 minute of delivery of the baby (after ruling out a second twin).
  2. Controlled Cord Traction (CCT): Apply gentle traction to the cord with one hand while applying counter-traction to the uterus (Brandt-Andrews maneuver) to deliver the placenta.
  3. Uterine Massage: Massage the fundus immediately after placental delivery until firm to maintain contraction and prevent atony. Check every 15 mins for 2 hours.
3.
Regarding abortion:
a. Outline 5 causes of recurrent/habitual abortion (2.5 marks)
b. Outline the elements of post abortal care (2.5 marks)
Show Answer

a. Causes of Recurrent Abortion:

  • Cervical Incompetence.
  • Genetic abnormalities (Chromosomal).
  • Uterine anomalies (Septate uterus, Fibroids).
  • Endocrine causes (Luteal phase defect, uncontrolled DM).
  • Antiphospholipid Syndrome (APLS).
  • Infections (TORCH).

b. Elements of PAC:

  • Emergency treatment of incomplete abortion (Evacuation).
  • Family Planning counseling and service provision.
  • Counseling (Psychological support).
  • Linkage to other reproductive health services (STI/HIV).
  • Community partnership.

6.
A married couple presents to you in your clinic complaining to you that they have not achieved any pregnancy in their five years of marriage and they are not on any contraception. Outline 5 causes and 5 investigations of female infertility. (5 marks)
Show Answer

Causes (Female):

  • Tubal factors (Blockage due to PID/Endometriosis).
  • Ovulatory disorders (PCOS, Premature Ovarian Failure).
  • Uterine factors (Fibroids, Asherman's).
  • Cervical factors (Stenosis).

7.
Mrs. J. W. who is a para 1+0 gravida 2 comes to you for her routine antenatal care. You discover that her blood pressure is elevated. Describe the classification of hypertensive diseases in pregnancy (5 marks)
Show Answer
  1. Gestational Hypertension: BP >140/90 after 20 weeks, no proteinuria.
  2. Pre-eclampsia: BP >140/90 after 20 weeks WITH proteinuria.
  3. Chronic Hypertension: BP elevated before pregnancy or before 20 weeks.
  4. Pre-eclampsia superimposed on Chronic Hypertension: Chronic HTN with new-onset proteinuria.
  5. Eclampsia: Pre-eclampsia complicated by seizures.

Show Answer

a. Degenerative changes:

  • Red degeneration (Pregnancy).
  • Hyaline degeneration (Most common).
  • Cystic degeneration.
  • Calcification.

Section C: Long Answer Question (20 Marks)

1.
Mrs. Mary K, 30 years old, para 2+0 gravida 3 at 32 weeks’ gestation presents to you with a one-day history of leakage of clear fluid from her vagina and mild lower backache. On further history she reports to have performed some heavy household chores prior to onset of the leakage, however she still perceives adequate fetal movements.
a. What is your likely diagnosis? (2marks)
b. List down 4 other differential diagnoses. (4marks)
c. Briefly describe how you will manage this patient until delivery. (12marks)
Show Answer

a. Likely Diagnosis

Pre-term Pre-labour Rupture of Membranes (PPROM).

Reasoning: Rupture of membranes (leakage of fluid) occurring before 37 weeks (she is 32 weeks) and before the onset of established labor contractions (only mild backache).

b. Differential Diagnoses

  • Urinary Incontinence.
  • Vaginal Infection (e.g., Bacterial Vaginosis, Candidiasis).
  • Passage of Mucus Plug (Show).
  • Vesico-vaginal Fistula.
  • Hydrorrhea gravidarum.

c. Management Plan

1. Assessment and Admission:

  • Admit the patient for bed rest.
  • History: Confirm dates to verify 32 weeks. Check for color/odor of fluid.
  • Examination: Perform sterile speculum exam (SSE) to visualize pooling of liquor. Avoid digital vaginal exam to reduce infection risk. Check maternal vitals (Temp, Pulse) for signs of chorioamnionitis.

2. Investigations:

  • Full Blood Count (WBC) to monitor for infection.
  • Urinalysis.
  • Ultrasound (Assess Amniotic Fluid Volume, Fetal presentation, Weight).
  • HVS (if infection suspected).

3. Pharmacological Management:

  • Corticosteroids: Dexamethasone 6mg IM 12hrly x 4 doses (to mature fetal lungs/prevent RDS).
  • Antibiotics: Erythromycin (or Ampicillin) prophylaxis to prevent chorioamnionitis and prolong latency.
  • Tocolytics: (e.g., Nifedipine) Use cautiously/temporarily only to allow steroid administration and transfer, provided no infection is present.

4. Surveillance (Conservative Management):

  • Monitor for signs of chorioamnionitis (Fever, uterine tenderness, foul discharge, fetal tachycardia).
  • Daily fetal movement charts.
  • Weekly WBC count.

5. Delivery:

  • Aim to deliver at 34 weeks.
  • Deliver immediately if frank chorioamnionitis, fetal distress, or active labor develops.
