Section 1: Multiple Choice Questions

40 Marks
1.
Which of the following is a component of the internal genitalia?
A. Vagina
B. Mons pubis
C. Skene's glands
D. Bartholin's glands
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A. Vagina

Explanation: The internal genitalia comprise the ovaries, fallopian tubes, uterus, and vagina. The Mons pubis, Skene's glands, and Bartholin's glands are parts of the vulva (external genitalia).

2.
Which of the following is true concerning the blood supply of the external genitalia?
A. The main supply is from the branches of the internal pudendal artery
B. The main supply is from the branches of the femoral artery
C. The deep and dorsal arteries supply the labia majora
D. The veins drain into the external pudendal vein
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A. The main supply is from the branches of the internal pudendal artery

Explanation: The internal pudendal artery is the primary blood supply to the perineum and external genitalia. External pudendal arteries (from the femoral) provide some supply but are secondary.

3.
The following statement is true about the relations of the vagina.
A. The 1/3 is related with the pouch of Douglas posteriorly
B. The middle 1/3 is related with the anterior rectal wall posteriorly
C. The upper 1/3 is related with the urethra anteriorly
D. The lower 1/3 is related with the bulbocavernosus muscles, vestibular bulbs and Bartholin's glands laterally,
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A. The 1/3 is related with the pouch of Douglas posteriorly (Upper 1/3)

Explanation: The upper posterior vaginal wall is covered by peritoneum of the Pouch of Douglas (Rectouterine pouch). This is clinically significant for culdocentesis.

4.
Which statement is correct about the anterior posterior diameter of the fetal head during engagement,
A. Suboccipito-bregmatic diameter measures 9.5 cm, complete flexion with brow presentation
B. Mentovertical diameter measures 11 cm, partial extension with brow preservation
C. Occipitofrontal diameter measures 10 cm, marked deflexion with vertex presentation
D. Sub-occipitofrontal diameter measures 10 cm, incomplete flexion with vertex presentation
Show Answer

Correct Answer: D. Sub-occipitofrontal diameter measures 10 cm, incomplete flexion with vertex presentation

Explanation:
Suboccipito-bregmatic (9.5cm) = Complete flexion (Vertex).
Suboccipito-frontal (10-10.5cm) = Incomplete flexion (Vertex).
Occipitofrontal (11.5cm) = Deflexed (Vertex/OP).
Mentovertical (13.5cm) = Brow.

5.
Concerning the diameters of the pelvic inlet, which one is correct.
A. The true conjugate measures 10 cm
B. The obstetric conjugate measures 12 cm
C. The diagonal conjugate measures 12 cm
D. Diagonal conjugate cannot be measured clinically
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C. The diagonal conjugate measures 12 cm

Explanation:
True Conjugate: ~11 cm.
Obstetric Conjugate: ~10.5 cm.
Diagonal Conjugate: ~12-12.5 cm (and IS measurable clinically).

6.
Emergency obstetric care functions are categorized as basic and comprehensive obstetric care. Which of the following is an exclusive comprehensive (CeMOC) function?
A. Administration of IV antibiotics
B. Administration of blood transfusion
C. Performing newborn resuscitation
D. Administration of magnesium sulphate
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B. Administration of blood transfusion

Explanation: CeMOC includes all 7 BEmONC functions PLUS:
1. Surgery (Cesarean Section)
2. Blood Transfusion.
Antibiotics, MgSO4, and Resuscitation are part of Basic (BEmONC).

7.
Which of the following is true about the menstrual cycle?
A. The luteal phase varies from cycle to cycle
B. The luteal phase is constant at 14 days
C. The follicular phase is constant at 14 days
D. Menstruation is related to ovulation
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B. The luteal phase is constant at 14 days

Explanation: The life span of the corpus luteum is relatively constant (14 days). Cycle variability is typically due to variations in the length of the follicular phase.

8.
Which statement is true concerning the Hypothalamo-Pituitary Ovarian hormones.
A. Luteinizing hormone stimulates proliferation of granulosa cells
B. Follicle Stimulating Hormone synthesis prostaglandins
C. Gonadotropin releasing hormone has a half-life of 15 minutes
D. Estrogens exerts a negative feedback effect on the release of Follicle Stimulating Hormone.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: D. Estrogens exerts a negative feedback effect on the release of Follicle Stimulating Hormone.

Explanation: Rising estrogen levels from the developing follicle inhibit FSH release (negative feedback) to select the dominant follicle. (Note: High estrogen eventually triggers the LH surge via positive feedback).

9.
A 55-year-old woman presents with per vaginal bleeding. Which of the following can be performed to exclude the cause?
A. Full haemogram
B. Cervical smear
C. Speculum examination
D. Digital vaginal examination
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C. Speculum examination (and/or biopsy)

Explanation: In post-menopausal bleeding, the priority is to rule out Cervical and Endometrial Cancer. A speculum exam allows direct visualization of the cervix and vagina to identify lesions, polyps, or atrophy. A smear is for screening, but examination is diagnostic for visible lesions.

10.
Which one of the following is a probable manifestation of pregnancy?
A. Palmar erythema
B. Linear nigra
C. Chadwick sign
D. Chloasma
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C. Chadwick sign

Explanation:
Presumptive: Subjective (Nausea, Amenorrhea).
Probable: Objective signs by examiner (Chadwick's, Hegar's, Goodell's signs, Abdominal enlargement).
Positive: Fetal heart, Fetal movement felt by examiner, Ultrasound.
Chadwick's sign (bluish discoloration of cervix/vagina) is a probable sign.

11.
Which of the following is true about physiological changes that take place in the respiratory system during pregnancy?
A. Residual volume decreases
B. Tidal volume decreases
C. Vital capacity increases
D. Functional capacity remains unchanged
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A. Residual volume decreases

Explanation: Due to the elevation of the diaphragm by the uterus, Residual Volume and Functional Residual Capacity decrease. Tidal Volume increases (by ~40%) to meet oxygen demands (progesterone effect).

12.
Which of the following is a physiological action of estrogen in the female reproductive system?
A. Increases vascularity of the uterus
B. Growth of axillary and pubic hair
C. Increases proliferation of the lobules of the breast
D. Causes hyperplasia of the cervix and increase cervical gland secretions
Show Answer

Correct Answer: D. Causes hyperplasia of the cervix and increase cervical gland secretions (and A is also correct).

Explanation: Estrogen stimulates growth (hyperplasia) of reproductive tissues, increases vascularity, and causes copious, thin cervical mucus (Spinnbarkeit). Progesterone is responsible for lobular development in breasts. Androgens cause hair growth. Estrogen specifically increases uterine vascularity (Option A is also very strong). However, cervical changes are a classic estrogenic sign.

13.
Antenatal profiles are tests done during pregnancy. Which one of the following is an auxiliary test?
A. Blood group
B. Urinalysis
C. Haemoglobin levels
D. Hepatitis B surface antigen
Show Answer

Correct Answer: D. Hepatitis B surface antigen (Context dependent)

Explanation: Hb, Blood Group/Rh, and Urinalysis/Syphilis/HIV are universally the "Basic" ANC profile in most Kenyan guidelines. Hepatitis B is often listed as recommended or essential but sometimes considered "auxiliary" or "additional" in resource-limited settings compared to the absolute basics. However, widely accepted now as standard.

14.
Aisha, 30-year-old presents to the antenatal clinic for her routine antenatal care clinic visit. Her last normal menstrual period was on 28/02/2023. She lost one previous pregnancy at 3 months, her second born died of pneumonia at 2 years and her first born is 5 years old. Use the above history to answer question 14 and 15.
What is Aisha's parity?
A. Para 1 + 1 gravida 3
B. Para 1 + 2 gravida 4
C. Para 2 + 1 gravida 4
D. Para 1 + 2 gravida 3
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C. Para 2 + 1 Gravida 4

Explanation:
1. 1st born (5 years) -> Term (Parity +1)
2. 2nd born (died at 2 yrs) -> Term (Parity +1)
3. Pregnancy loss at 3 months -> Abortion (+1 abortion)
4. Current pregnancy -> (+1 Gravida)
Total: Gravida 4, Para 2 (2 deliveries >28w), + 1 Abortion.

15.
When is her expected date of delivery? (LMP: 28/02/2023)
A. 5/11/2023
B. 6/11/2023
C. 6/12/2023
D. 5/12/2023
Show Answer

Correct Answer: D. 5/12/2023

Explanation: Using Nagele's Rule (LMP + 7 days + 9 months):
Day: 28 + 7 = 35. (Feb 2023 has 28 days). 35 - 28 = 7th March.
Month: March (3) + 9 = 12 (December).
Result: 7th December 2023.
Alternative (LMP + 1yr - 3mo + 7d):
Feb 28 + 7d = March 7.
Feb - 3 mo = Nov.
Wait, standard calculation often approximates.
280 days from Feb 28:
Mar(31)+Apr(30)+May(31)+Jun(30)+Jul(31)+Aug(31)+Sep(30)+Oct(31)+Nov(30) = 275 days.
Remaining: 5 days.
Date: Dec 5th.
Exact 280 days calculation = Dec 5th, 2023.

16.
Identify a true statement regarding anaemia in pregnancy?
A. Vitamin B12 deficiency anaemia occurs in pregnancy
B. Nutritional anaemia is the common form
C. Can cause anaemia in the newborn.
D. It is common in primigravidas.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B. Nutritional anaemia is the common form

Explanation: Iron Deficiency Anaemia (a nutritional anaemia) is overwhelmingly the most common cause of anaemia in pregnancy due to increased iron demands.

17.
A 28-year-old para 2 + 0 gravida 3 at 33 weeks gestation presents with painless heavy bright red per vaginal bleeding. On examination, she is sick looking, weak, Blood Pressure at 90/60mmHg, pulse rate at 120 b/min, fetal heart rate at 140 beats/minute and regular. What is your immediate management?
A. correct anaemia with hematinic
B. Bed rest
C. Emergency caesarian section
D. Transfusion with packed cells
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C. Emergency caesarian section (after resuscitation)

Explanation: The presentation is classic for Major Placenta Previa with hemodynamic instability (Shock: BP 90/60, Pulse 120). While resuscitation (D) is concurrent, the definitive obstetric management for life-threatening hemorrhage is delivery. Given the heavy bleeding and shock, immediate delivery via CS is required to save the mother.

19.
During clinical pelvimetry, which of the following features when noted indicates a contracted/inadequate pelvis.
A. Beaking of the iliopectineal line
B. Wide sacrosciatic notch
C. Rounded pubic arch accommodating at least 2 fingers
D. Smooth ischial spines
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A. Beaking of the iliopectineal line

Explanation: A "sharp" or "beaked" forepelvis is characteristic of an Android (male-type) pelvis, which is less favorable for delivery. Wide notch, rounded arch, and smooth spines are features of a healthy Gynecoid pelvis.

23.
Julia presents to the out-patient department in the health centre with complains of foul smelling per vaginal discharge, and lower abdominal pain for one week. You make an impression of pelvic inflammatory disease (PID). Which statement below is true?
A. Can be treated with 5-day course and doxycycline
B. Is caused by Neisseria gonorrhoea
C. Ultrasound reveals a beaded string tubal wall in acute disease
D. Laparotomy is indicated in acute disease
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B. Is caused by Neisseria gonorrhoea (and Chlamydia)

Explanation: Gonorrhea and Chlamydia are the primary causative agents. Treatment typically requires 14 days (not 5). "Beaded tubes" suggests TB. Laparotomy is for complications like ruptured abscess.

Section 2: Short Answer Questions

40 Marks
41.
Mary, Para 1+0 gravida 2 at 30 weeks gestation presents to you with complaints of lower abdominal pains which started at night. She describes the pains as on and off. She also reports of a blood mucoid discharge per vagina. No urinary symptoms reported. The fetal heart rate is 140b/min, on pelvic examination, the cervical os is at 2cm. Outline your plan of management. (5 marks)
Show Answer

Answer: Management of Preterm Labour

  1. Admission: Admit to antenatal ward for bed rest and monitoring.
  2. Corticosteroids: Administer Dexamethasone 6mg IM 12-hourly for 4 doses (or Betamethasone) to accelerate fetal lung maturity.
  3. Tocolysis: Administer tocolytics (e.g., Nifedipine 20mg stat then 10-20mg q4-6h) to suppress contractions and delay delivery for 48 hours to allow steroids to work/transfer.
  4. Antibiotics: If membranes are ruptured or infection suspected (Erythromycin).
  5. Monitoring: Monitor maternal vitals, FHR, and progression of labor.
  6. Neuroprotection: MgSO4 if < 32 weeks for fetal neuroprotection.
42.
Fatuma, 30-year-old primigravida at 33 weeks gestation presents to the outpatient department with complains of severe headache, epigastric pain, and swelling of the lower limbs. On Examination, her pulse rate is 86 beats/minute, Blood pressure 160/118mmHg, the Fetal Heart rate is at 132 beats/Minute and regular. Describe the principles of management of Fatuma. (5 marks)
Show Answer

Diagnosis: Severe Pre-eclampsia.

Principles of Management:

  1. Prevention of Seizures: Administer Magnesium Sulfate (Loading dose 4g IV + 10g IM) followed by maintenance.
  2. Control of Blood Pressure: Administer rapid-acting antihypertensives (e.g., Hydralazine 5mg IV, Labetalol 20mg IV, or Nifedipine 10mg PO) to lower BP safely (Target DBP 90-100).
  3. Fluid Management: Restrict fluids (80ml/hr) and insert catheter to monitor strict input/output.
  4. Fetal Monitoring/Maturation: Assess fetal well-being (CTG/US) and give corticosteroids for lung maturity.
  5. Delivery: Plan for delivery (stabilize then deliver). Severe PET is an indication for delivery once stable.
48.
A 32-year-old female presents to the casualty department with severe lower abdominal pain radiating to the shoulder and per vaginal bleeding. Her last normal menstrual period was 8 weeks ago and her pregnancy test is positive. On examination, she is sick looking, her pulse rate is 110 beats/minute and blood pressure is 90/60mmHg. Abdominal examination reveals tenderness.
a) What relevant investigation will you order to confirm your diagnosis (1 Mark)
b) Outline your plan of management for the patient (4 Marks)
Show Answer

Diagnosis: Ruptured Ectopic Pregnancy.

a) Investigation:

  • Pelvic Ultrasound: (FAST scan) to detect adnexal mass and free fluid in Pouch of Douglas/abdomen.
  • Alternatively: Culdocentesis (aspiration of non-clotting blood from POD) if US unavailable.

b) Management:

  1. Immediate Resuscitation: Secure 2 large-bore IV lines, infuse crystalloids/colloids, cross-match blood.
  2. Emergency Surgery (Laparotomy): Immediate transfer to theatre for salpingectomy (removal of affected tube) to stop bleeding.
  3. Blood Transfusion: Transfuse as needed to stabilize hemodynamics.
  4. Post-op Care: Monitor vitals, Anti-D if Rh negative.

Section 3: Long Answer Question

20 Marks
49.
A married couple living together presents to the reproductive health clinic with history of failure to conceive after eight years of marriage.
a) Outline the possible causes of infertility in this couple (6 marks)
b) Describe your approach on how you will evaluate this couple (14 marks)
Show Answer

a) Possible Causes of Infertility:

  • Male Factors (30-40%): Oligospermia, Azoospermia, Asthenospermia, Erectile dysfunction, Retrograde ejaculation.
  • Female Factors (40-50%):
    • Tubal: Blockage (PID, TB), adhesions.
    • Ovarian: Anovulation (PCOS, POI), Luteal phase defect.
    • Uterine: Fibroids, Asherman's syndrome, Congenital anomalies.
    • Cervical: Hostile mucus, stenosis.
  • Unexplained (10-20%)
  • Coital Problems: Infrequency, dyspareunia.

b) Evaluation Approach:

1. History Taking (Both Partners)

  • General: Age, duration of trying, coital frequency/timing, lifestyle (smoking, alcohol).
  • Female: Menstrual history (regularity), Obstetric history (past pregnancies/miscarriages), Medical/Surgical history (PID, surgeries).
  • Male: History of mumps, trauma, STIs, surgeries (hernia repair), impotence.

2. Physical Examination

  • Female: BMI, Secondary sexual characteristics, Abdominal exam (masses), Pelvic exam (tenderness, discharge, uterine size/mobility).
  • Male: Testicular volume/consistency, varicocele, penile abnormalities, secondary sexual characteristics.

3. Investigations

  • Male: Seminal Fluid Analysis (SFA): The first line test. Assess count (>15M), motility, morphology.
  • Female - Ovulation: Day 21 Serum Progesterone (mid-luteal) to confirm ovulation. Basal Body Temperature charts.
  • Female - Tubal Patency: Hysterosalpingogram (HSG) to visualize tubes and cavity. Laparoscopy (Gold standard) with dye test.
  • Female - Anatomy: Pelvic Ultrasound for fibroids, ovaries (PCO).
  • Hormonal Profile: FSH, LH, Prolactin, TFTs (Day 2-5).

4. Counseling

  • Explain findings, reassure, and plan treatment based on cause.
