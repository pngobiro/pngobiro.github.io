Section A: Multiple Choice Questions

30 Marks
1.
The following is not useful in the diagnosis of premature rupture of membranes:
a) Leakage of fluid per vagina
b) Positive fern test
c) Demonstration of amniotic fluid on speculum examination
d) Positive methylene blue test
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Positive methylene blue test

Explanation: While methylene blue injection into the amniotic sac can diagnose rupture (if blue fluid appears in the vagina), it is an invasive procedure and rarely used for routine diagnosis. Standard non-invasive methods include observing pooling of liquor (speculum), Fern test (crystallization), and Nitrazine test (pH). Digital vaginal examination is contraindicated.

2.
Which of the following is true about lochia?
a) Lochia loss can mimic post-partum hemorrhage
b) Alba is red in colour
c) Rubra occur up to the 10th day
d) Is a sign of infection
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Lochia loss can mimic post-partum hemorrhage

Explanation: A sudden gush of lochia (e.g., upon standing) can mimic hemorrhage.
- Lochia Alba is white/yellow (not red).
- Lochia Rubra (red) lasts 3-4 days (not 10).
- Lochia is normal physiology, not a sign of infection (unless foul-smelling/purulent).

3.
One of the following is not a sign of placental separation:
a) Umbilical cord lengthens
b) The uterine fundus rises up
c) The umbilical cord shortens
d) There is fresh flow of blood per vagina
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) The umbilical cord shortens

Explanation: Signs of placental separation include:
1. Lengthening of the cord at the vulva.
2. Fundus rises and becomes hard/globular.
3. A gush of fresh blood.
The cord does not shorten.

4.
During second stage of labour the cervical dilatation is:
a) 10 cm
b) 2 cm
c) < 4 cm
d) > 4 cm
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) 10 cm

Explanation: The second stage of labor begins at full cervical dilatation (10 cm) and ends with the delivery of the baby.

5.
Diabetes in pregnancy is associated with an increased risk of the following except:
a) Caesarian section
b) Shoulder dystocia
c) Intrauterine growth restriction
d) Fetal macrosomia
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Intrauterine growth restriction

Explanation: Gestational Diabetes typically causes Macrosomia (large baby) due to fetal hyperinsulinemia. This leads to higher risks of Shoulder Dystocia and C-sections. IUGR is typically associated with pre-existing diabetes with vascular complications or pre-eclampsia, but in the context of general diabetes/GDM questions, Macrosomia is the rule and IUGR is the exception.

6.
Anaemia in pregnancy is mostly due to:
a) Iron deficiency
b) Folate deficiency
c) Sickle cell disease
d) Hemolytic disease
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Iron deficiency

Explanation: Iron deficiency is the leading cause of anemia in pregnancy worldwide due to hemodilution and increased fetal iron demands.

7.
Immunological test for diagnosis of pregnancy:
a) Trans-vaginal ultra sound
b) History of amenorrhea
c) Serum HCG
d) Full haemogram
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Serum HCG

Explanation: Tests detecting Human Chorionic Gonadotropin (HCG) (urine or serum) are immunological tests (using antibodies to HCG). Ultrasound is imaging; Amenorrhea is a symptom.

8.
About augmentation of labour:
a) Cytotec is used
b) Oxytocin is given as an IV bolus
c) Oxytocin is the drug of choice titrated against the contraction
d) Ergometrine can used safely
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Oxytocin is the drug of choice titrated against the contraction

Explanation: Augmentation (speeding up established labor) uses Oxytocin via controlled IV infusion. It is titrated to achieve adequate contractions (3 in 10 mins).
- Bolus oxytocin can cause rupture.
- Cytotec is for induction.
- Ergometrine causes tetanic contractions and is dangerous in labor (used for PPH).

9.
The following are signs of fetal distress except:
a) Fetal blood PH is 7.4
b) Meconium stained liquor
c) Maternal exhaustion
d) Maternal hypoxia
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Fetal blood PH is 7.4

Explanation: Normal fetal pH is 7.35-7.45. Fetal distress is associated with acidosis (pH < 7.2). Meconium and hypoxia are signs/causes of distress.

10.
The following is a positive manifestation of pregnancy:
a) Hegar's sign
b) Nausea and vomiting
c) Amenorrhea
d) Auscultation of fetal heart sounds
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Auscultation of fetal heart sounds

Explanation: Positive signs are indisputable evidence of a fetus (Fetal Heart Sounds, Ultrasound visualization, Fetal movement felt by examiner). Hegar's sign is a probable sign. Nausea/Amenorrhea are presumptive signs.

11.
Common cause of UTI in pregnancy is:
a) Candida albicans
b) Mycobacterium tuberculi
c) Escherichia coli
d) Staph aureus
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Escherichia coli

Explanation: E. coli (a gut commensal) causes 80-90% of UTIs in pregnancy.

12.
Biophysical profile constitute the following:
a) Motor response of the fetus
b) Placenta state
c) Fetal presentation
d) Cord blood flow
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Motor response of the fetus

Explanation: BPP includes 5 variables: Fetal Movement (Motor), Fetal Tone, Fetal Breathing, Amniotic Fluid Volume, and Non-Stress Test.

13.
The following is a complication for multiple pregnancy:
a) Prolonged gestation
b) Macrosomic babies
c) Cord prolapsed
d) Oligohydramnios
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Cord prolapsed

Explanation: Cord prolapse is more common in multiple pregnancies due to malpresentations (breech/transverse) and polyhydramnios. Multiple pregnancies usually lead to preterm birth (not prolonged) and low birth weight (not macrosomia).

14.
Regarding cervical incompetence in pregnancy:
a) Patterns of abortion are in ascending manner
b) Present with acute painful per vaginal bleeding
c) Sex is not a contraindication
d) Mc Donald suture (cervical cerclage) is best performed at 14 weeks
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Mc Donald suture (cervical cerclage) is best performed at 14 weeks

Explanation: Cerclage is typically placed at 12-14 weeks to prevent second-trimester loss. It typically presents as painless cervical dilatation (not painful bleeding).

15.
Concerning vaginal birth after caesarian section (VBAC) delivery:
a) Induction of labor is possible
b) Indicated in a woman with two previous scars
c) Possible in a mother with one previous scar due to fetal distress
d) Possible in a mother with contracted pelvis
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Possible in a mother with one previous scar due to fetal distress

Explanation: VBAC is safe for one previous scar if the indication (like fetal distress) is non-recurrent. It is contraindicated in recurrent causes (contracted pelvis) or multiple scars. Induction carries high rupture risk and is usually avoided.

Section B: Clinical Scenarios & True/False

30 Marks
1.
Joan, a 35-year-old para 5+0 g6, presented with ruptured membranes and pulsating cord prolapse in the health center. How will you manage Joan until she arrives in the level 4 facility?
a) Grouping and cross matching
b) Knee-chest position
c) Place her in exaggerated lithotomy position
d) Complete bed rest
e) Prepare her for caesarean section if the cord has stopped pulsating
Show Answer

Correct Management: b) Knee-chest position

Explanation: The immediate management for cord prolapse is to relieve pressure on the cord to maintain fetal oxygenation. This is achieved by the Knee-chest position or Exaggerated Sim's position. Filling the bladder is another option. This is critical during transfer to a facility with CS capability.

2.
The following are indications of elective caesarean section:
a) Transverse lie
b) Two previous scar
c) POP
d) Breech presentation in 2nd stage of labour
e) Known cephalopelvic disproportion mother
Show Answer

Correct Options: b) Two previous scar, e) Known cephalopelvic disproportion (CPD)

Explanation:
- Two previous scars: High risk of rupture, absolute indication.
- Known CPD: Vaginal delivery impossible.
- Transverse lie: Indication if persists to term, but usually corrected or emergency.
- Breech in 2nd stage: Too late for elective CS, managed by assisted breech delivery.

3.
Concerning malaria in pregnancy:
a) Is severe in non pregnant than in the pregnant mothers
b) Can cause IUFD
c) First line treatment is by use of AL
d) Is associated IUGR
e) Use of long term insecticides treated nets is not advised
Show Answer

Correct Options: b) Can cause IUFD, d) Is associated with IUGR

Explanation: Malaria is more severe in pregnancy due to immunosuppression. It causes placental parasitemia leading to IUGR, Low Birth Weight, and IUFD. AL (Artemether-Lumefantrine) is used in 2nd/3rd trimesters (Quinine in 1st). ITNs are strongly recommended.

6.
In regard to eclampsia:
a) Is prevalent in extreme of ages i.e. below 18 and above 35 years
b) Presents with convulsions
c) Occurs in uncontrolled pre-eclampsia
d) Delivery can be delayed until term
e) Phenobarbitone is the drug of choice
Show Answer

Correct Options: a, b, c

Explanation: Eclampsia is characterized by convulsions (seizures). It occurs when pre-eclampsia is severe/uncontrolled. Extremes of maternal age are risk factors. Delivery cannot be delayed; it is the definitive treatment. Magnesium Sulfate is the drug of choice (not Phenobarbitone).

9.
Postpartum haemorrhage:
a) Is a loss of more than 500 mls in vaginal delivery and 1000 mls in caesarian delivery
b) Retained placenta is a cause
c) May cause infertility
d) Active management of third stage of labour is a cause
e) The main cause is unrepaired episiotomy and tears
Show Answer

Correct Options: a, b, c

Explanation:
- Definition (>500ml) is correct.
- Retained placenta (Tissue) prevents contraction.
- Sheehan's syndrome (pituitary necrosis from shock) can cause secondary infertility/amenorrhea.
- AMTSL *prevents* PPH, it doesn't cause it.
- Main cause is Uterine Atony (70%), not tears.

10.
A mother who is a known diabetic is pregnant and presents to your clinic at 30 weeks gestation. What forms part of your management?
a) Blood for oral glucose tolerance test
b) Urine analysis for proteinuria
c) Estimation of fetal weight
d) Should be managed as normal pregnancy since she is a known diabetic
e) Mother should wait for spontaneous onset of labour at term
Show Answer

Correct Options: b) Urine analysis for proteinuria, c) Estimation of fetal weight

Explanation:
- Proteinuria check is vital as diabetics are at high risk for Pre-eclampsia.
- Fetal weight estimation is crucial due to risk of Macrosomia.
- OGTT is diagnostic (not needed if already known).
- High risk pregnancy, not normal.
- Often induced early (38-39 weeks) to prevent stillbirth/shoulder dystocia.
