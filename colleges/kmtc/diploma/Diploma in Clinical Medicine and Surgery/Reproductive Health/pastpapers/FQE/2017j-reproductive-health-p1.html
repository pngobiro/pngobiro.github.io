Section A: Multiple Choice Questions

30 Marks
1.
The following is not useful in the diagnosis of premature rupture of membranes:
a) Leakage of fluid per vagina
b) Positive fern test
c) Demonstration of amniotic fluid on speculum examination
d) Positive methylene blue test
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Positive methylene blue test

Explanation: While methylene blue can theoretically diagnose membrane rupture (by injection into the amniotic sac), it is invasive and generally not used for routine diagnosis. Standard non-invasive methods include observing leakage (pooling), the Fern test (crystallization pattern), and Nitrazine test (pH). Digital vaginal examination is also contraindicated due to infection risk.

2.
Which of the following is true about lochia?
a) Lochia loss can mimic post-partum hemorrhage
b) Alba is red in colour
c) Rubra occur up to the 10th day
d) Is a sign of infection
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Lochia loss can mimic post-partum hemorrhage

Explanation: Excessive lochia can be confused with PPH. Lochia Alba is white/yellow (not red). Lochia Rubra (red) lasts 3-4 days (not 10). Lochia is normal, but foul-smelling lochia is a sign of infection.

3.
One of the following is not a sign of placental separation:
a) Umbilical cord lengthens
b) The uterine fundus rises up
c) The umbilical cord shortens
d) There is fresh flow of blood per vagina
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) The umbilical cord shortens

Explanation: Signs of placental separation include lengthening of the cord, the fundus rising (and becoming hard/globular), and a gush of blood. The cord does not shorten.

4.
During second stage of labour the cervical dilatation is:
a) 10 cm
b) 2 cm
c) < 4 cm
d) > 4 cm
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) 10 cm

Explanation: The second stage of labor begins at full cervical dilatation (10 cm) and ends with the expulsion of the fetus.

5.
Diabetes in pregnancy is associated with an increased risk of the following except:
a) Caesarian section
b) Shoulder dystocia
c) Intrauterine growth restriction
d) Fetal macrosomia
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Intrauterine growth restriction

Explanation: Gestational diabetes typically causes fetal macrosomia (large baby) due to high glucose levels, leading to risks like shoulder dystocia and C-section. IUGR is typically associated with pre-existing diabetes with vascular complications, but in the context of "Diabetes in pregnancy" (GDM), macrosomia is the hallmark.

6.
Anaemia in pregnancy is mostly due to:
a) Iron deficiency
b) Folate deficiency
c) Sickle cell disease
d) Hemolytic disease
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Iron deficiency

Explanation: Iron deficiency is the most common cause of anemia in pregnancy globally due to increased fetal demands and hemodilution.

7.
Immunological test for diagnosis of pregnancy:
a) Trans-vaginal ultra sound
b) History of amenorrhea
c) Serum HCG
d) Full haemogram
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Serum HCG

Explanation: Tests detecting Human Chorionic Gonadotropin (HCG) in serum or urine are immunological tests. Ultrasound is a biophysical/imaging method.

8.
About augmentation of labour:
a) Cytotec is used
b) Oxytocin is given as an IV bolus
c) Oxytocin is the drug of choice titrated against the contraction
d) Ergometrine can be used safely
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Oxytocin is the drug of choice titrated against the contraction

Explanation: Augmentation uses Oxytocin via controlled IV infusion (titrated). IV bolus can cause uterine rupture. Cytotec is typically for induction/cervical ripening. Ergometrine causes titanic contractions and is used for PPH, not augmentation.

9.
The following are signs of fetal distress except:
a) Fetal blood PH is 7.4
b) Meconium stained liquor
c) Maternal exhaustion
d) Maternal hypoxia
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Fetal blood PH is 7.4

Explanation: Normal fetal pH is 7.35-7.45. Acidosis (pH < 7.2) is a sign of distress. Meconium and hypoxia are associated with distress.

10.
The following is a positive manifestation of pregnancy:
a) Hegar's sign
b) Nausea and vomiting
c) Amenorrhea
d) Auscultation of fetal heart sounds
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Auscultation of fetal heart sounds

Explanation: Positive (diagnostic) signs are indisputable evidence of a fetus (Fetal heart sounds, Fetal movement felt by examiner, Ultrasound visualization). Nausea (presumptive) and Hegar's sign (probable) are not positive signs.

11.
Common cause of UTI in pregnancy is:
a) Candida albicans
b) Mycobacterium tuberculi
c) Escherichia coli
d) Staph aureus
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Escherichia coli

Explanation: E. coli is the most common pathogen causing UTIs in pregnancy.

12.
Biophysical profile constitute the following:
a) Motor response of the fetus
b) Placenta state
c) Fetal presentation
d) Cord blood flow
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Motor response of the fetus

Explanation: BPP includes 5 parameters: Fetal Tone, Fetal Movement (Motor response), Fetal Breathing, Amniotic Fluid Volume, and Non-Stress Test (Heart rate). Placenta state and cord flow are not part of standard BPP.

13.
The following is a complication for multiple pregnancy:
a) Prolonged gestation
b) Macrosomic babies
c) Cord prolapsed
d) Oligohydramnios
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Cord prolapsed

Explanation: Multiple pregnancies are associated with malpresentations and polyhydramnios, increasing the risk of cord prolapse. They are usually associated with preterm labor (not prolonged) and low birth weight (not macrosomia).

14.
Regarding cervical incompetence in pregnancy:
a) Patterns of abortion are in ascending manner
b) Present with acute painful per vaginal bleeding
c) Sex is not a contraindication
d) Mc Donald suture (cervical cerclage) is best performed at 14 weeks
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Mc Donald suture (cervical cerclage) is best performed at 14 weeks

Explanation: Cerclage is typically placed at 12-14 weeks. Cervical incompetence presents with painless dilatation/expulsion (not painful bleeding).

15.
Concerning vaginal birth after caesarian section (VBAC) delivery:
a) Induction of labor is possible
b) Indicated in a woman with two previous scars
c) Possible in a mother with one previous scar due to fetal distress
d) Possible in a mother with contracted pelvis
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Possible in a mother with one previous scar due to fetal distress

Explanation: VBAC is generally safe for one previous scar if the indication was non-recurrent (like fetal distress). It is contraindicated in 2+ scars or recurrent causes like contracted pelvis. Induction is generally avoided or done with extreme caution.

Section B: Clinical Scenarios & True/False

30 Marks
1.
Joan, a 35-year-old para 5+0 g6, presented with ruptured membranes and pulsating cord prolapse in the health center. How will you manage Joan until she arrives in the level 4 facility?
a) Grouping and cross matching
b) Knee-chest position
c) Place her in exaggerated lithotomy position
d) Complete bed rest
e) Prepare her for caesarean section if the cord has stopped pulsating
Show Answer

Correct Management: b) Knee-chest position

Explanation: The immediate management for cord prolapse is to relieve pressure on the cord. This is achieved by the Knee-chest position or Exaggerated Sim's position (filling the bladder also helps). This maintains fetal circulation until emergency C-section can be performed.

2.
The following are indications of elective caesarean section:
a) Transverse lie
b) Two previous scar
c) POP
d) Breech presentation in 2nd stage of labour
e) Known cephalopelvic disproportion mother
Show Answer

Correct Options: b) Two previous scar, e) Known cephalopelvic disproportion (CPD)

Explanation: Two previous scars and known CPD are absolute indications for elective CS. Transverse lie is an indication if it persists to term/labor. Breech in 2nd stage is usually delivered vaginally (assisted breech) or emergency CS, not elective.

3.
Concerning malaria in pregnancy:
a) Is severe in non pregnant than in the pregnant mothers
b) Can cause IUFD
c) First line treatment is by use of AL
d) Is associated IUGR
e) Use of long term insecticides treated nets is not advised
Show Answer

Correct Options: b) Can cause IUFD, d) Is associated with IUGR

Explanation: Malaria is more severe in pregnancy (immunity is suppressed). It causes IUFD, IUGR, and low birth weight. AL (Artemether-Lumefantrine) is used in the 2nd/3rd trimester, but Quinine is often preferred in 1st trimester or severe cases. ITNs are strongly advised.

4.
Observations charted on a partograph include:
a) Moulding
b) Fetal lie
c) Cervical dilatation
d) Intensity of uterine contractions
e) Fetal heart rate
Show Answer

Correct Options: a, c, d, e

Explanation: Fetal lie is usually recorded in admission notes but not plotted on the graph grid like Moulding, Dilatation, Contractions, and FHR.

6.
In regard to eclampsia:
a) Is prevalent in extreme of ages i.e. below 18 and above 35 years
b) Presents with convulsions
c) Occurs in uncontrolled pre-eclampsia
d) Delivery can be delayed until term
e) Phenobarbitone is the drug of choice
Show Answer

Correct Options: a, b, c

Explanation: Eclampsia is defined by convulsions. It occurs in uncontrolled pre-eclampsia and extremes of age are risk factors. Delivery cannot be delayed; stabilization and delivery are required. Magnesium Sulfate (not Phenobarbitone) is the drug of choice.

10.
A mother who is a known diabetic is pregnant and presents to your clinic at 30 weeks gestation. What forms part of your management?
a) Blood for oral glucose tolerance test
b) Urine analysis for proteinuria
c) Estimation of fetal weight
d) Should be managed as normal pregnancy since she is a known diabetic
e) Mother should wait for spontaneous onset of labour at term
Show Answer

Correct Options: b) Urine analysis for proteinuria, c) Estimation of fetal weight

Explanation: Known diabetics are high risk. They need monitoring for pre-eclampsia (proteinuria) and macrosomia (fetal weight). OGTT is for diagnosis (she is already known). They are not managed as normal and often require induction before term (38-39 weeks) to avoid macrosomia/stillbirth complications.
