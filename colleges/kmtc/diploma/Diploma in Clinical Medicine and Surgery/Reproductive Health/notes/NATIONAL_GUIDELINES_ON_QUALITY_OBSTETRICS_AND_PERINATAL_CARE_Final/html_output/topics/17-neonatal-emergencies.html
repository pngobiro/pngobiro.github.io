NEONATAL EMERGENCIES

PERINATAL ASPHYXIA

Prolonged impaired gas exchange leading to hypoxemia, hypercarbia, and metabolic acidosis.

Diagnosis

  • Apgar score $\le$ 3 at 5 minutes.
  • Cord pH < 7.0.
  • Neurological manifestations (HIE).
  • Multi-organ dysfunction.

Hypoxic Ischemic Encephalopathy (HIE)

Sarnat Staging:

Sign Stage 1 (Mild) Stage 2 (Moderate) Stage 3 (Severe)
LOC Hyperalert Lethargic Stupor/Coma
Tone Normal/Hyper Hypotonic Flaccid
Seizures None Common Common/Intractable
Suck Weak/Poor Weak/Absent Absent

Management

  • Resuscitation: ABCs.
  • Supportive Care: Maintain normothermia, normoglycemia, normal electrolytes. Treat seizures.
  • Therapeutic Hypothermia: For moderate/severe HIE (Stage 2/3) within 6 hours of birth. Target temp 33-34¬∞C for 72 hours.

SEIZURES

Etiology

  • HIE (Most common).
  • Intracranial Hemorrhage.
  • Metabolic (Hypoglycemia, Hypocalcemia, Hyponatremia).
  • Infection (Meningitis).

Treatment

  1. Correct metabolic causes (Glucose, Calcium, Magnesium).
  2. First Line: Phenobarbitone 20mg/kg IV loading dose.
  3. Second Line: Phenytoin 20mg/kg IV.
  4. Refractory: Midazolam, Lidocaine.

ACUTE KIDNEY INJURY (AKI)

Defined as urine output < 1ml/kg/hr or rising creatinine.

Management

  • Fluid challenge (10-20ml/kg NS) if pre-renal/hypovolemia suspected.
  • Fluid restriction if intrinsic/post-renal or fluid overloaded.
  • Furosemide trial.
  • Treat Hyperkalemia.
  • Dialysis (PD) if refractory.

RESPIRATORY DISTRESS

Causes

RDS, TTN, Meconium Aspiration, Pneumonia.

Silverman Anderson Score

Assess severity (Retractions, Grunting, Nasal flaring).

  • Mild (1-3): Oxygen (Nasal prongs).
  • Moderate (4-6): CPAP.
  • Severe (>6): Mechanical Ventilation.

Surfactant Therapy

For RDS. Prophylactic (< 28 wks) or Rescue.

NEONATAL APNOEA

Cessation of breathing > 20 sec or with bradycardia/cyanosis.

Management

  • Stimulation.
  • Oxygen/Bag-mask ventilation.
  • Caffeine Citrate: Loading 20mg/kg, Maintenance 5-10mg/kg.
  • Aminophylline: Loading 6mg/kg, Maintenance 2.5mg/kg q12h (if Caffeine unavailable).
  • CPAP.

ACID-BASE DISORDERS

Metabolic Acidosis: Bicarb < 18, BE < -5. Treat cause (Sepsis, Hypoxia). Bicarb replacement if severe (pH < 7.2).

Respiratory Acidosis: pCO2 > 60. Improve ventilation.

GLUCOSE DISORDERS

Hypoglycemia

Glucose < 2.6 mmol/L (< 47 mg/dL).

  • Asymptomatic: Feed (Breast/Formula). Recheck.
  • Symptomatic/Severe (< 1.8 mmol/L): 2ml/kg 10% Dextrose bolus, then infusion (6-8 mg/kg/min).

Hyperglycemia

Glucose > 8.3 mmol/L (> 150 mg/dL). Decrease glucose infusion rate. Insulin if refractory.
