POST-PARTUM HEMORRHAGE AND MATERNAL SEPSIS

POST-PARTUM HEMORRHAGE (PPH)

Approximately 30% of maternal deaths worldwide are due to hemorrhage mostly in the immediate postpartum period. Postpartum hemorrhage is defined as blood loss > 500 ml in a vaginal birth and > 1000 ml in a cesarean delivery.

Classification

  • Primary PPH: Occurs within first 24 hours. Mainly due to uterine atony (70%).
  • Secondary PPH: Occurs between 24 hours and 6 weeks postpartum. Mainly due to retained products or infection.

Etiology (4 T's)

  • Tone (70%): Uterine atony.
  • Trauma (20%): Lacerations, rupture, inversion.
  • Tissue (10%): Retained placenta/clots.
  • Thrombin (<1%):< /strong> Coagulopathy.

Prevention: Active Management of Third Stage of Labor (AMTSL)

  • Uterotonic: Oxytocin 10 IU IM within 1 minute of delivery.
  • Controlled Cord Traction (CCT): To deliver placenta.
  • Uterine Massage: After placenta delivery to ensure contraction.

MANAGEMENT OF UTERINE ATONY

Initial Response

  • Shout for help.
  • Insert 2 wide bore cannulae (16/18G).
  • IV fluids (NS/RL) + Oxytocin infusion.
  • Empty bladder.
  • Fundal massage.
  • Oxygen by mask.
  • Tranexamic acid 1g IV (within 3 hours).

Bimanual Compression

One hand in vagina against anterior wall, other hand on abdomen against posterior wall.

Uterine Balloon Tamponade (UBT)

Used for refractory atony. Can use condom catheter or commercial balloon.

Surgical Interventions

  • Compression sutures (B-Lynch).
  • Devascularization (Uterine/Ovarian/Internal Iliac arteries).
  • Hysterectomy (Last resort).

OTHER CAUSES OF PPH

Genital Tract Trauma

Cervical/vaginal lacerations, hematomas, uterine rupture, inversion. Requires surgical repair/correction.

Retained Tissue

Placenta/membranes. Manual removal or curettage under anesthesia.

Coagulopathy

Treat underlying cause (e.g., abruption, sepsis). Transfuse blood products (FFP, Platelets, Cryoprecipitate).

MATERNAL SEPSIS

Definition

Life-threatening organ dysfunction caused by a dysregulated host response to infection during pregnancy, childbirth, post-abortion, or postpartum.

Clinical Signs (NEWS Score)

  • Temp > 38¬∞C or < 36¬∞C
  • HR > 110 bpm
  • RR > 24 bpm
  • BP < 90 mmHg systolic
  • Altered mental status

Management ("Golden Hour")

  • Resuscitation: IV fluids, Oxygen.
  • Cultures: Blood, urine, swabs before antibiotics.
  • Antibiotics: Broad spectrum IV within 1 hour (e.g., Ceftriaxone + Metronidazole + Gentamicin).
  • Source Control: Deliver fetus, evacuate uterus, drain abscess.
