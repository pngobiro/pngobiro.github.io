NEWBORN COMPLICATIONS AND HIGH RISK MANAGEMENT

BIRTH INJURIES

Injuries sustained by the baby during the birth process.

1. Skin and Superficial Tissues

  • Caput Succedaneum: Edematous swelling under scalp, above periosteum. Resolves spontaneously. No treatment needed.
  • Cephalohaematoma: Blood effusion below periosteum. Resolves in 2-3 weeks. Observe for jaundice.
  • Subconjunctival Hemorrhage: Reassure mother.
  • Abrasions/Lacerations: Keep clean and dry. Antibiotics if infected. Suturing for deep lacerations.

2. Muscle Injury

Sternomastoid injury (Torticollis): Twisting of neck. Manage by positioning and physiotherapy.

3. Nerve Trauma

  • Facial Nerve Palsy: Eyelid open, mouth drawn to normal side. Assist with feeding.
  • Brachial Plexus Injury (Erb's/Klumpke's): Caused by difficult birth (shoulder dystocia, breech). No spontaneous arm movement. Handle gently to prevent further injury. Immobilize for pain relief initially. Physiotherapy.

4. Fractures

  • Clavicle: Reduced movement, crepitus. Strap arm if painful.
  • Humerus: Immobilize with strapping. Check for circulation (fingers).
  • Femur: Splint (Gallows traction/strapping). Check circulation (toes).

5. Head Injury

Intracranial hemorrhage, depressed fractures. Diagnosis by X-ray/Ultrasound. Manage complications (seizures, raised ICP).

MANAGEMENT OF THE HIGH-RISK NEWBORN

Infection Prevention Control in NBU

  • Hand hygiene (5 Moments).
  • Aseptic technique.
  • Equipment decontamination.
  • Waste disposal.
  • Cohorting/Isolation.
  • Traffic control.
  • Clean environment (Daily damp dusting/mopping).

LOW BIRTH WEIGHT NEONATE / PRETERM

Definitions

  • LBW: < 2500g
  • VLBW: < 1500g
  • ELBW: < 1000g
  • Preterm: < 37 weeks gestation

General Principles of Care

  • Warmth: Kangaroo Mother Care (KMC), Incubators, Radiant warmers. Maintain temp 36.5-37.5¬∞C. Prevent hypothermia ("Warm Chain").
  • Feeding: Breast milk is best.
    • < 1500g: Tube feeding (NGT/OGT).
    • 1500-2000g: Cup feeding/Spoon feeding.
    • > 2000g / > 34 weeks: Breastfeeding.
  • Infection Prevention: Strict hygiene, avoid overcrowding.
  • Breathing: Monitor for apnea/distress. Caffeine citrate for apnea of prematurity.

Kangaroo Mother Care (KMC)

Continuous skin-to-skin contact for stable LBW babies. Promotes warmth, breastfeeding, bonding, and growth.

Eligibility: Stable babies < 2000g. Mother willing and available.

Incubator Care

For unstable or very small babies. Monitor temperature settings closely.

TEMPERATURE DISORDERS

Hypothermia

Temperature < 36.5¬∞C.

  • Cold Stress (36.0-36.4¬∞C): Immediate warming (Skin-to-skin). Monitor glucose.
  • Moderate (32.0-35.9¬∞C): Immediate warming (Radiant warmer/Incubator). Antibiotics if sepsis suspected.
  • Severe (< 32.0¬∞C): Urgent resuscitation, slow rewarming. High risk of mortality.

Hyperthermia

Temperature > 37.5¬∞C. Causes: Overheating, Infection, Dehydration. Management: Adjust environment, undress, treat cause.
