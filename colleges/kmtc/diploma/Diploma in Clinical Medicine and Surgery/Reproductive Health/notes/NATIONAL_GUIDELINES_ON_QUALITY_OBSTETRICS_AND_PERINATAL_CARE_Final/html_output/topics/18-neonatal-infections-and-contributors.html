NEONATAL INFECTIONS AND CONTRIBUTORS

NEONATAL INFECTIONS

Definition

Infections occurring within the first 28 days of life. Can be Intrauterine (TORCHES), Perinatal (Early onset sepsis < 72hrs), or Postnatal (Late onset sepsis> 72hrs).

Neonatal Sepsis

Risk Factors: PROM > 18hrs, Maternal fever > 38¬∞C, Chorioamnionitis, Preterm/LBW, Asphyxia.

Clinical Signs: Poor feeding, Lethargy, Temperature instability (>37.5 or <35.5), Respiratory distress, Apnea, Jaundice, Abdominal distension.

Investigations: Blood culture (Gold standard), CBC (I/T ratio > 0.2), CRP, LP (if meningitis suspected).

Treatment

  • First Line (Early Onset): Ampicillin/Penicillin + Gentamicin.
  • Second Line (Late Onset/Hospital Acquired): Cefotaxime/Ceftazidime + Amikacin/Gentamicin OR Piperacillin-Tazobactam. (Avoid Ceftriaxone in jaundiced neonates).
  • Duration: 7-10 days for sepsis, 14-21 days for meningitis.

Other Infections

  • Ophthalmia Neonatorum: Ceftriaxone 50mg/kg IM stat or Kanamycin. Saline irrigation.
  • Oral Thrush: Nystatin drops.
  • Cord Infection (Omphalitis): Cloxacillin/Flucloxacillin if mild. Systemic antibiotics if severe (redness extending to skin).
  • Congenital Syphilis: Benzyl Penicillin X 10 days.

NECROTIZING ENTEROCOLITIS (NEC)

Life-threatening GIT emergency, common in preterms.

Clinical Presentation

Abdominal distension, Bilious vomiting/aspirates, Bloody stool, Apnea/Bradycardia.

X-ray Signs

Dilated loops, Pneumatosis intestinalis (pathognomonic), Portal venous gas, Pneumoperitoneum (perforation).

Management

  • NPO (Stop feeds).
  • OGT decompression.
  • IV Fluids/TPN.
  • Triple Antibiotics (Ampicillin + Gentamicin + Metronidazole).
  • Surgery for perforation.

NEONATAL JAUNDICE

Types

  • Physiological: Onset > 24hrs, peaks day 3-5. No treatment usually.
  • Pathological: Onset < 24hrs, rapid rise, conjugated hyperbilirubinemia, prolonged. Causes: Hemolysis (ABO/Rh), Sepsis, G6PD.

Management

Kramer's Rule: Clinical estimation (Face 5mg/dl, Chest 10, Abdomen 12, Legs 15, Palms/Soles >15).

Treatment:

  • Phototherapy: Blue light. Protect eyes/genitals. Increase fluids.
  • Exchange Transfusion: For severe hyperbilirubinemia/signs of Kernicterus.

BLEEDING DISORDERS

Hemorrhagic Disease of the Newborn (HDN)

Vitamin K deficiency. Presentation: GI bleeding (melena), umbilical bleeding, intracranial hemorrhage.

Prevention: Vitamin K 1mg IM at birth.

Treatment: Vitamin K 1-2mg IV/IM. FFP if severe.

CONGENITAL ANOMALIES

  • TEF/Esophageal Atresia: Excessive drooling, choking with feeds. NPO, OGT (coils), Surgery.
  • Gastroschisis/Omphalocele: Cover with sterile saline gauze/plastic wrap. IV fluids. Surgery.
  • Imperforate Anus: No meconium > 24hrs. NPO, NGT. Surgery.
  • Spina Bifida: Cover defect with sterile saline gauze. Antibiotics. Surgery.
