SECTION 2

PERINATAL CARE

CARE OF THE NORMAL NEWBORN

Introduction

Essential newborn care should begin immediately the newborn is born. This section will consider the immediate steps of care the newborn should receive following delivery. It will consider:

  1. The steps in preparation to receive the newborn after birth
  2. The methods used to keep new-borns warm and protect them from infection
  3. The rapid assessment of new-borns at birth to determine if resuscitation is required
  4. The steps of resuscitating a newborn
  5. Cord care by application of 7.1% Chlorhexidine digluconate gel
  6. The steps required to initiate breastfeeding
  7. The administration of essential medications immediately after birth

Preparing to receive the newborn

The delivery team must be prepared to:

  • Keep the newborn protected from infection
  • Keep the newborn warm in the delivery room and afterwards
  • Resuscitate the newborn if required
  • Initiate breastfeeding in the delivery room

Infection prevention and control

  • Clean and disinfect all delivery surfaces.
  • Strict hand hygiene (soap and water/alcohol rub).
  • Use sterile equipment and PPEs.
  • Discard waste appropriately.

Keeping the newborn warm

Newborns should have their skin temperature maintained between 36.5-37.5¬∞C. The newborn must be protected from losing heat.

  • Warm the delivery room to 25-28¬∞C.
  • Close windows/avoid draughts.
  • Place newborn on mother's abdomen (skin-to-skin).
  • Dry thoroughly and cover with warm dry towel.
  • Initiate breastfeeding within one hour.

Rapid assessment of the newborn

Ask the following 4 questions:

  1. Is the newborn breathing adequately or crying? (LOOK AT CHEST)
  2. Is the heart rate present? (FEEL UMBILICAL CORD)
  3. Is the newborn centrally pink? (LOOK AT TONGUE)
  4. Is the infant active with vigorous movement? (LOOK AT NEWBORN)

ALL YES: Routine care (Skin-to-skin, dry, delayed cord clamping).
ANY NO: Immediate resuscitation required.

Immediate Care Steps

Receiving the newborn

  • Place on mother's abdomen for skin-to-skin contact.
  • Dry and stimulate vigorously.
  • Discard wet towel, cover with warm dry towel.
  • Delay cord clamping 1-3 minutes.
  • Clamp and cut cord sterilely.
  • Apply 7.1% Chlorhexidine digluconate gel to cord.
  • Initiate breastfeeding.

Medications

  • Vitamin K: 1mg IM (>1.5kg) or 0.5mg IM (<1.5kg) to prevent hemorrhagic disease.
  • Eye care: Tetracycline eye ointment to prevent ophthalmia neonatorum.
  • Cord care: 7.1% Chlorhexidine digluconate gel.
  • Vaccines: BCG and Oral Polio Vaccine (OPV) birth dose.

Feeding the Newborn

Breast milk is the best food. Initiate within the first hour.

Benefits of breastfeeding

  • Builds immunity (Colostrum).
  • Nourishes and keeps baby warm.
  • Promotes bonding.
  • Better brain development and weight gain.
  • Helps uterine involution/reduces bleeding.

Examination of the Newborn

Examine from head to toe. Look for:

  • Respiration: Rate 30-60/min, grunting, chest indrawing.
  • Colour: Pink (normal), Pallor, Jaundice, Cyanosis.
  • Heart rate: 100-160 bpm.
  • Temperature: 36.5-37.5¬∞C.
  • Posture/Tone: Flexed posture, good tone.
  • Head: Fontanelles, moulding, clefts.
  • Abdomen: Distension, umbilicus condition.
  • Genitalia/Anus: Patency, ambiguous genitalia.

Discharge and Health Education

Counsel caregiver on:

  • Optimum feeding: Exclusive breastfeeding for 6 months.
  • Warmth: Appropriate clothing (one more layer than adult), head covering.
  • Hygiene: Hand washing, cord care (Chlorhexidine, keep dry), delaying bath for 24 hours.
  • Danger signs: Poor feeding, fast/difficult breathing, fever/cold, inactivity, jaundice, convulsions.
  • Follow up: Immunization schedule, growth monitoring.

Management of Breast Problems

Sore/Cracked Nipples

Correct positioning/attachment. Apply breast milk to cracks. Continue feeding.

Engorgement

Frequent feeding/emptying. Warm compresses before, cold compresses after feeding. Analgesics.

Mastitis

Antibiotics (Cloxacillin), Analgesics, Continue feeding from unaffected side, Express affected side.

Breast Abscess

Incision and drainage. Antibiotics. Discontinue feeding from affected breast until healed.
