SECTION C: LONG ANSWER ESSAY QUESTIONS

1. Stroke

(a) Define stroke (1mks)

Is a blockage of blood supply to the brain

(b) Mention and describe major two types of stroke (4mks)

  1. Ischemic stroke – is caused by blockage of artery supply to the brain
  2. Hemorrhagic stroke – is caused by bleeding in the brain when blood vessels in the brain ruptures causing blood to spill out

(c) What are the causes of stroke (5mks)

  • Open head injury
  • Cerebral thrombosis
  • Cerebral embolism
  • Cerebral ischemia
  • Cerebral hemorrhage

What are the signs and symptoms of stroke (5mks)?

  • Sudden numbness/weakness of face, arm & leg especially on one side of the body
  • Sudden confusion, trouble in speaking or understanding speech
  • Sudden trouble seeing in one side of or both eyes
  • Sudden trouble in walking, dizziness, loss of balance or coordination
  • Sudden severe headache with no known cause of it

What are the risk factors of stroke (5mks)

  • Hypertension
  • Diabetes
  • Heart diseases
  • Smoking
  • High cholesterol foods

2. Diabetes

Define diabetes (2mks)

Is a disease that is present due to either abnormal function of insulin or total failure of pancreases to produce enough insulin

Highlight 10 predisposing factor diabetes (5mks)

  • Obesity
  • Excessive alcohol intake
  • Prolonged medication example steroids
  • Pancreatitis
  • Chemical exposure
  • Poor dietary
  • Family history
  • Infections
  • Tumors
  • High blood pressure
  • Autoimmune systemic diseases

State and describe two types of diabetes (3mks)

  • Insulin dependent diabetes: Occurs when insulin producing cell in pancrease are damaged, occurs at the age of 30 & above, Insulin production is little or not produced.
  • Non-insulin dependent diabetes: When pancrease produces insulin which is not enough to regulate the blood sugar. Occurs at the age of 30 years and below.

Highlight 5 late signs & symptoms of diabetes

  • Headache
  • Poor coordination
  • Nightmares
  • Numbness around the mouth & tongue
  • Paleness of the skin
  • Sweating from the unknown reason
  • Dizziness

State 5 complication of diabetese

  • Retinopathy
  • Neuropathy
  • Diabetic foot
  • Nephropathy (damage of kidney)
  • Sexual dysfunction
