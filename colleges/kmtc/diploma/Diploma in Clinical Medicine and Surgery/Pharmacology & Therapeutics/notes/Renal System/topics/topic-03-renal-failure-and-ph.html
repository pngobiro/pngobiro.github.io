üíß Antidiuretics

  • Vasopressin, synthetic arginine vasopressin and ADH
  • Produced at the supraoptic and paraventricular nucleus of hypothalamus and stored posterior pituitary
  • Given parenterally, intranasal or intramuscular. No oral route.
  • Desmopressin, lypressin, and terlipressin are analogues of ADH

Mechanism & Clinical Uses

  • V1-mediated receptors act by constricting the blood vessel hence given to stop esophageal varices hemorrhage.
  • Act through v2-receptors in central diabetes insipidus
  • Also used in nocturnal enuresis.

Unwanted effects

  1. Nausea and vomiting
  2. Abdominal cramps
  3. Backache due to uterine contractions
  4. Precipitate attack of angina
  5. Local irritation during intranasal administration
  6. Fluid retention and hyponatremia
