üíä Drugs used for Erectile Dysfunction

This is the common disorder in males especially the old ones. Erectile dysfunction is the inability to maintain penile erection for successive performance of sexual activity.

Can be caused physiological or psychological issues like vascular diseases, diabetes, depression, medications and sequelae of prostatic surgery.

These drugs are phosphodiestrase-5 inhibitors (PDE-5); Sildenafil, tadalafil, vardenafil and avanafil.

üîÑ

Mechanism of Action (MOA)

  • Phosphodiestrase-5 inhibitors act by increasing blood flow to corpus cavernosum through smooth muscle relaxation.
  • Nitric oxide activates guanylyl cyclase which forms cyclic guanosine monophosphate(cGMP) from GTP.
  • This causes smooth muscle relation and reduction of intracellular $\mathrm{Ca} 2+$ influx.
  • Duration of action of cyclic nucteotides is controlled by action of phosphodiestrase-5.

Pharmacokinetics & Side Effects

  • Seldenafil and vardenafil should be taken 1 hr before sexual activity and action of onset is affected by fatty meal.
  • Tadalafil has slower onset of action.
  • Side effects- headache, flushing, dyspepsia and nasal congestion.

üß™ Urinary Antiseptics

  • Oral antibacterial agents that act on microbes in urine but have no systemic antibacterial effects.
  • Useful in treating lower urinary tract infections.

1. Nitrofurantoin

  • Bactericidal in therapeutic doses
  • Against many gram-positives and gram-negatives
  • Less or no activity against proteus and P. aeruginosa
  • Mechanism of action not well understood
  • Produce highly reactive intermediates in the bacterium which bind to ribosomal proteins leading to inhibition of protein synthesis, DNA, RNA and metabolic processes.
  • Well absorbed from git
  • Metabolized and excreted through urine.
  • Contraindicated in patients with renal insufficiency creatinine clearance $<60 \mathrm{ml} / \mathrm{min}$
  • Can be given for months to suppress chronic UTIs
  • The drug is enhanced in a urine $\mathrm{PH}<5.5$
  • Single dose of nitrofurantoin 100 mg is given once daily to prevent recurrent UTIs in some women.
  • Side effects:- anorexia, nausea and vomiting, neuropathies, hypersensitivity reactions, anaemia in G-6-dehydrogenase deficiency

Interaction

Nitrofurantoin blocks the action of nalidixic acid.

2. Methenamine Mandelate & Methenamine Hippurate

  • Below PH of 5.5 methenamine releases formaldehyde with is bactericidal.
  • Taken orally.
  • Excreted unchanged in urine.
  • Methenamine mandelate 1 g four times a day
  • Methenamine Hippurate 1 g twice a day
  • Used as urinary antiseptics to suppress the urinary tract infections but not treat

Interaction

Should not be used with sulphonamides because they form insoluble compounds with formaldehyde released by methenamine.
