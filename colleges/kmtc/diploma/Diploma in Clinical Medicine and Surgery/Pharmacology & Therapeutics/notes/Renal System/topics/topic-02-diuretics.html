üß™ Carbonic Anhydrase Inhibitors

  • Acetazolamide
  • Mainly act at proximal tubule
  • Inhibit carbonic anhydrate enzyme from forming $\mathrm{H}+$ ions hence prevent $\mathrm{Na}+, \mathrm{H}+$ exchange.
  • Leads to loss of $\mathrm{Na}+, \mathrm{K}+$ and HCO 3 - ions and the urine becomes alkaline.

Clinical uses

  1. Glaucoma
  2. Mountain sickness
  3. Alkalinize urine in acidic drug poisoning

  3. Metabolic acidosis

üíß Osmotic Diuretics

  • Mannitol, glycerol and isosorbide.
  • Draw water from the tissues through osmotic action
  • Act at loop of Henle and proximal tubule
  • Increase urine volume and excretion of $\mathrm{Na}+, \mathrm{K}+, \mathrm{Cl}-, \mathrm{HCO} 3-$, Phosphates, calcium and magnesium.
üè•

Clinical Uses

  1. Acute glaucoma
  2. Used to reduce increased intracranial pressure
  3. Used to prevent kidney shut down in shock
  4. Maintain osmolality of ECF after dialysis
03

üîÑ Loop Diuretics

  • Act on thick ascending limb loop of Henle.
  • Bind $\mathrm{Na}+-\mathrm{K}+-2 \mathrm{Cl}-$ cotransporter and blocks it
  • Called high ceiling diuretics because they are efficacious.
  • Furosemide, torsemide and bumetanide are well absorbed from GIT.
  • Given orally or parenterally.
üè•

Clinical Uses

  1. Acute pulmonary oedema
  2. Chronic heart failure
  3. Nephrotic syndrome
  4. Renal failure
  5. Liver cirrhosis
  6. Treatment of hypercalcemia
  7. Hypertension associated with CCF/renal failure
04

üßÇ Thiazides

  • Inhibit sodium-chloride symport at distal tubule.
  • Increase sodium and chloride excretion.
  • Administered orally
  • Chlorothiazide, chlorthalidone, hydrochlorothiazide
üè•

Clinical Uses

  1. Hypertension
  2. Oedema
  3. Hypercalciuria
  4. Diabetes insipidus
05

‚öñÔ∏è Potassium Sparing

  • Aldosterone antagonist, spironolactone
  • Binds aldosterone receptors at collecting ducts leading to loss of sodium but potassium is retained.
  • Administered orally and highly protein bound.
  • Used in oedema, CCF, and hypertension.
  • Amiloride and triamterene are potassium sparing that act directly by blocking sodium channel at collecting ducts
