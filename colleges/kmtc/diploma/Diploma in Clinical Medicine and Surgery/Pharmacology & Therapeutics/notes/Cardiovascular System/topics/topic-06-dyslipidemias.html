Anti-dyslipidemias

01

ü©∫ Introduction to Dyslipidemia

  • Coronary artery disease is the leading cause of death world wide.
  • CAD is associated with elevation of low density lipoprotein cholesterol (bad cholesterol) and triglycerides.
  • There is reduced amount of high density lipoprotein (good cholesterol).

Other risk factors of CHD are; smoking, obesity, diabetes and hypertension.

Cholesterol levels can be raised due to lack of exercise and diet containing high saturated fats.

Hyperlipidemias can also result from genetic defects in lipoprotein metabolism.

03

üíä HMG-COA Reductase Inhibitors (Statins)

  • 3-hydroxy-3-methylglutaryl Coenzyme A (HMG COA reductase inhibitors)
  • Commonly known as statins
  • Reduce LDL-C
  • First line treatment in patients with atherosclerosis disease

Benefits of HMG-COA Reductace Inhibitors

  1. Plaque stabilization
  2. Inhibition of platelet thrombus formation.
  3. Improvement of coronary endothelial function
  4. Anti-inflammatory activity.

Mode of Action of HMG-COA

  • Competitive inhibitors of HMG-COA reductase ratelimiting step in cholesterol synthesis.
  • This leads to reduced intracellular supply of cholesterol.
  • Reduced intracellular cholesterol supply eventually results to increase in number of cell surface LDL receptors that bind and internalize circulating LDLs.
  • Examples; atorvastatin, simvastatin, pitavastatin, rosuvastatin, fluvastatin, lovastatin and pravastatin.
  • Also decrease triglyceride levels and increase HDL in some patients.
  • The most potent statins are:- atorvastatin, pitavastatin and rosuvastatin.
  • Pharmacokinetics; absorption of statins varies from 30-85%, simvastatin and lovastatin are given in inactive forms. Metabolized in the liver and excreted in feces and bile and some through urine.

Adverse Effects of HMG-CoA

  1. Elevated liver enzymes
  2. Myopathy
  3. Rhabdomyolysis
  4. Hepatitis
  5. Contraindicated in pregnancy-teratogenic

Drug interactions; block the action of warfarin leading to accumulation of high levels in the body. Monitor the INR

04

üíä Niacin (Nicotinic acid)

  • Reduce LDL-C in 10-20% and is the most effective in increasing HDL-C.
  • Also lowers triglycerides 20-35%.
  • Used in combination with statins
  • M.O.A. - inhibit lipolysis in adipose tissue leading to reduced production of free fatty acids.

The liver uses free fatty acids to produce triglycerides which later are converted by the liver to VLDL-C consequently, this leads to decreased LDL-C plasma concentrations.

  • Uses; used to treat familial hyperlipidemias and other hypercholestrolemia with combination with other agents.
  • Pharmacokinetics; niacin is taken orally, converted in the body to nicotinamide. Excreted in urine
‚ö†Ô∏è Side Effects

Side effects: cutaneous flush, pruritus, nausea and vomiting, inhibit tubular secretion of uric acid this can predispose to hyperuricemia and gout, glucose intolerance and hepatotoxicity.

05

üíä Fibrates

Fenofibrate and gemfibrozil

Derivatives of fibric acid

  • Lowers triglycerides and increase HDL.
  • M.O.A;- bind peroxisome proliferator-activated receptors leading to decreased triglyceride concentrations through increased expression of lipoprotein lipase and reduced apolipoprotein CII concentrations.
  • Fenofibrate is more active in lowering triglycerides than gemfibrozil.
  • Uses; used to treat hypertriglyceridemias and type III hyperlipidemia.
  • Pharmacokinetics;- given orally, plasma bound and distributed widely. Fenofibrate given as a prodrug, metabolized in liver and excreted in urine as glucuronide conjugates.
  • Side effects;- GIT disturbances, predispose to gallstones, myositis, myopathy, rhabdomyolysis, not used in renal dysfunction and hepatic dysfunction.
  • Drug interactions;- do not use gemfibrozil and simvastatin together, fibrates increase effects of warfarin.
06

üß¨ Bile acid-Binding Resins

  • Lower LDL-cholesterol though less effective than statins.
  • Cholestyramine, colestipol, colesevelam
  • M.O.A; bind negatively charged bile acids and bile salts in the small intestine leading to excretion in feces hence reduced bile acid concentration. This leads to hepatocytes converting cholesterol to bile acids.

uses; treatment of type IIA and type IIB hyperlipidemias. Used in combination with diet or niacin. Colesevelam can used to lower blood sugar in type 2 DM, cholestyramine is used to reduce pruritus in biliary stasis.

Pharmacokinetics; water insoluble, given orally, retained in the lumen not reabsorbed due to its large molecules. Excreted in feces.

Adverse effects; nausea, constipation, flatulence, impair fatsoluble vitamins,

Drug interactions; interfere with absorption of warfarin, digoxin and thyroid hormone.

07

üõë Cholesterol Absorption Inhibitor

Ezetimibe

Selectively inhibit absorption of dietary and biliary cholesterol from the small intestine.

This leads to decreased delivery of cholesterol from the small intestine to the liver.

The end results in reduction of hepatic cholesterol stores which leads to hepatic clearance of cholesterol from the blood.

Metabolized in the small intestine and the liver.

Excreted in urine and bile.

Lowers LDL-C hence used as adjunct treatment with statins.

08

üêü Omega-3-Fatty Acids

Omega-3-polyunsaturated fatty acids are used to lower triglycerides.

Inhibit VLDL and triglycerides synthesis in the liver.

Icosapent are capsules for fish oil suppliments.

Side effects: GI disturbances, fishy after taste.

Drug interactions: increased risk of bleeding when used concomitantly with anticoagulants.
