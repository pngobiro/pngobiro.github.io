Enzyme Inducers

These drugs increase the activity of liver enzymes, speeding up the metabolism of other drugs and decreasing their therapeutic effect.

  • Rifampicin: Reduces levels of oral contraceptives, warfarin, and antiretrovirals (NNRTIs).
  • Phenytoin & Carbamazepine: Induce metabolism of theophylline and oral contraceptives.
  • Phenobarbital: General inducer reducing effects of many drugs.

Enzyme Inhibitors

These drugs inhibit liver enzymes, slowing down metabolism and increasing the risk of toxicity of other drugs.

  • Cimetidine: Inhibits metabolism of warfarin, phenytoin, theophylline, and lidocaine.
  • Erythromycin/Clarithromycin: Increases levels of theophylline and carbamazepine.
  • Azole Antifungals (Ketoconazole): Increases levels of warfarin and sulfonylureas.
  • Ritonavir (PI): Potent inhibitor; increases levels of sildenafil and benzodiazepines.

Digoxin + Diuretics

Mechanism: Diuretics (Loop/Thiazide) cause Hypokalemia (low K+). Low K+ enhances Digoxin binding.

Result: Digoxin Toxicity (Arrhythmias, vomiting, halo vision).

ü©∏

Warfarin + NSAIDs/Aspirin

Mechanism: NSAIDs damage GI mucosa and inhibit platelets; Warfarin inhibits clotting factors. Aspirin displaces warfarin from protein binding.

Result: Severe risk of GI bleeding and hemorrhage.

CNS Depressants Synergism

Combination: Opioids + Benzodiazepines + Alcohol + Antihistamines.

Result: Profound respiratory depression, coma, and death.

‚ö°

PDE5 Inhibitors (Sildenafil) + Nitrates

Mechanism: Synergistic vasodilation.

Result: Life-threatening hypotension (shock).
