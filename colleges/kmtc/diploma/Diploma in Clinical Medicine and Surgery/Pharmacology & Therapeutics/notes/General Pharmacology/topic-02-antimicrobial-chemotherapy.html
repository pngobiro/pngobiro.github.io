üß™ Principles of Antimicrobial Therapy

Antimicrobial chemotherapy involves the use of drugs to selectively kill or inhibit the growth of microorganisms while causing minimal damage to the host.

üîë

Key Concepts

  • ‚óè
    Bactericidal: Drugs that kill bacteria (e.g., Penicillins, Aminoglycosides). Essential in life-threatening infections or immunocompromised patients.
  • ‚óè
    Bacteriostatic: Drugs that inhibit bacterial growth/replication (e.g., Tetracyclines, Sulfonamides), relying on the host's immune system to clear the infection.
  • ‚óè
    Spectrum: Range of activity. Narrow (specific groups) vs Broad (covers Gram +ve and -ve).
02

üß± Cell Wall Synthesis Inhibitors (Beta-Lactams)

Beta-lactam antibiotics inhibit bacterial cell wall synthesis by binding to penicillin-binding proteins (PBPs), preventing cross-linking of peptidoglycan. They are generally bactericidal.

1. Penicillins

Class Examples Notes
Narrow Spectrum Benzylpenicillin (Pen G), Phenoxymethylpenicillin (Pen V) Pen G is acid-labile (parenteral). Pen V is acid-stable (oral). Active against Gram +ve cocci.
Anti-Staphylococcal Cloxacillin, Flucloxacillin, Methicillin Resistant to penicillinase (beta-lactamase). Used for Staph infections.
Broad Spectrum Ampicillin, Amoxicillin Better Gram -ve coverage. Amoxicillin has better oral absorption.
Anti-Pseudomonal Piperacillin, Ticarcillin Extended spectrum including Pseudomonas aeruginosa.
üí°

Beta-Lactamase Inhibitors

Substances like Clavulanic Acid are combined with penicillins (e.g., Co-amoxiclav = Amoxicillin + Clavulanic Acid) to protect the antibiotic from destruction by bacterial beta-lactamase enzymes.

2. Cephalosporins

Classified by generations. As you move from 1st to 4th generation, Gram-negative activity increases, and Gram-positive activity generally decreases (though 4th gen retains Gram +ve activity).

  • 1
    1st Gen (e.g., Cephalexin): Good for Gram +ve skin/soft tissue infections.
  • 2
    2nd Gen (e.g., Cefuroxime): Better Gram -ve coverage (H. influenzae).
  • 3
    3rd Gen (e.g., Ceftriaxone): Excellent Gram -ve coverage, crosses Blood-Brain Barrier (Meningitis).
  • 4
    4th Gen (e.g., Cefepime): Broad spectrum, resistant to beta-lactamases.
‚ö†Ô∏è

Limitations ("LAME")

Cephalosporins are generally NOT active against:

  • Listeria
  • Atypicals (Mycoplasma, Chlamydia)
  • MRSA
  • Enterococci
03

üß¨ Protein Synthesis Inhibitors

Tetracyclines (e.g., Doxycycline)

MOA: Bind 30S ribosomal subunit. Bacteriostatic.
Adverse Effects: Tooth discoloration (avoid in children <8yrs & pregnancy), phototoxicity, GI distress.
Note: Absorption impaired by milk/antacids (calcium/magnesium chelation).

Aminoglycosides (e.g., Gentamicin, Streptomycin)

MOA: Bind 30S ribosomal subunit. Bactericidal. Poor oral absorption (given IV/IM).
Adverse Effects: Ototoxicity (irreversible deafness/vertigo) and Nephrotoxicity (reversible).

Macrolides (e.g., Erythromycin, Azithromycin)

MOA: Bind 50S ribosomal subunit. Bacteriostatic.
Uses: Penicillin-allergic patients, atypical pneumonia, H. pylori.
Adverse Effects: GI upset (nausea/vomiting), cholestatic jaundice, enzyme inhibition (Erythromycin).

Chloramphenicol

MOA: Binds 50S subunit. Broad spectrum but toxic.
Adverse Effects: Bone marrow suppression (Aplastic anemia), Gray Baby Syndrome in neonates (due to inability to conjugate the drug).

04

üß¨ Nucleic Acid & Metabolic Inhibitors

Fluoroquinolones (e.g., Ciprofloxacin)

MOA: Inhibit DNA Gyrase (Topoisomerase II) involved in DNA replication. Bactericidal.
Adverse Effects: Tendonitis/tendon rupture, cartilage damage (avoid in children), CNS effects.

Sulphonamides & Trimethoprim (Antifolates)

MOA: Inhibit steps in folic acid synthesis (essential for DNA).
Cotrimoxazole (Septrin): Combination of Sulfamethoxazole + Trimethoprim (Synergistic).
Adverse Effects: Crystalluria (drink water), Stevens-Johnson Syndrome (hypersensitivity), Kernicterus in newborns.

Metronidazole

MOA: Active against anaerobes and protozoa. Disrupts DNA structure.
Uses: Amoebiasis, Giardiasis, Trichomoniasis, anaerobic bacterial infections.
Warning: Disulfiram-like reaction with alcohol (severe vomiting).

ü´Å Antimycobacterial Agents (TB & Leprosy)

Anti-Tuberculosis Drugs

Standard treatment involves combination therapy to prevent resistance. Two phases: Intensive (2 months) & Continuation (4 months).

Drug Key Adverse Effects Notes
Isoniazid (H) Peripheral neuropathy, Hepatotoxicity Give Vitamin B6 (Pyridoxine) to prevent neuropathy.
Rifampicin (R) Hepatotoxicity, Orange/Red body fluids Potent enzyme inducer (drug interactions).
Pyrazinamide (Z) Hepatotoxicity, Hyperuricemia (Gout) Active in acidic pH (macrophages).
Ethambutol (E) Optic Neuritis (Red/Green color blindness) Dose adjustment in renal failure.
Streptomycin (S) Ototoxicity, Nephrotoxicity Injectable aminoglycoside.

Anti-Leprosy Drugs

Dapsone: Sulfonamide-like. SE: Hemolysis (G6PD deficiency), Methemoglobinemia.
Clofazimine: Red-brown skin discoloration.
Rifampicin: Also used in leprosy.

üçÑ Antifungal & Antiviral Agents

Antifungals

  • Polyenes (Amphotericin B, Nystatin): Bind ergosterol, creating pores. Amphotericin B is for severe systemic fungal infections (very nephrotoxic).
  • Azoles (Fluconazole, Ketoconazole): Inhibit ergosterol synthesis. Drug interactions common (inhibit CYP enzymes).
  • Griseofulvin: Oral drug for dermatophyte (skin/nail) infections. Take with fatty meal.

Antivirals

  • Anti-Herpes (Acyclovir): Inhibits viral DNA polymerase. Used for HSV, VZV.
  • Anti-Retrovirals (ARVs for HIV):
    ‚Ä¢ NRTIs: Zidovudine (Bone marrow suppression), Lamivudine.
    ‚Ä¢ NNRTIs: Efavirenz, Nevirapine (Hepatotoxicity, Rash).
    ‚Ä¢ Protease Inhibitors: Ritonavir, Lopinavir (Metabolic syndrome).

ü¶ü Antiparasitic Agents

Antimalarials

  • Artemether-Lumefantrine (AL): First-line for uncomplicated malaria.
  • Quinine: For severe/complicated malaria. SE: Cinchonism (tinnitus, headache), Hypoglycemia.

Anthelminthics

  • Benzimidazoles (Albendazole, Mebendazole): Inhibit microtubule synthesis. Used for nematodes (worms).
  • Praziquantel: For Schistosomiasis and tapeworms.
