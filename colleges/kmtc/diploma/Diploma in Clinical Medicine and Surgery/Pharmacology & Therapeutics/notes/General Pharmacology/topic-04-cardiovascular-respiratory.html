üìâ Antihypertensive Drugs

1. Angiotensin-Converting Enzyme (ACE) Inhibitors

Examples: Captopril, Enalapril, Lisinopril ("-prils").

MOA: Block conversion of Angiotensin I to Angiotensin II (a potent vasoconstrictor) and decrease aldosterone secretion.

‚ö†Ô∏è

Key Adverse Effects

  • Dry Cough: Due to bradykinin accumulation.
  • Hyperkalemia: Potassium retention.
  • Angioedema: Life-threatening swelling.
  • Teratogenic: Contraindicated in pregnancy.

2. Angiotensin II Receptor Blockers (ARBs)

Examples: Losartan, Valsartan ("-sartans").

Note: Similar to ACE inhibitors but do not cause dry cough.

3. Calcium Channel Blockers (CCBs)

MOA: Block calcium influx in smooth muscle/myocardium, causing vasodilation and decreased heart rate.

  • Dihydropyridines (Vascular selective): Nifedipine, Amlodipine. Used for HTN. SE: Peripheral edema, flushing.
  • Non-Dihydropyridines (Heart selective): Verapamil, Diltiazem. Used for arrhythmias/angina. SE: Bradycardia, constipation.

4. Sympatholytics

  • Beta-Blockers: Atenolol, Metoprolol, Propranolol. Decrease HR and contractility. Caution: Asthma (bronchoconstriction), Diabetics (masks hypoglycemia).
  • Alpha-2 Agonists (Central): Methyldopa (Safe in pregnancy), Clonidine.
  • Alpha-1 Blockers: Prazosin. SE: First-dose orthostatic hypotension.

‚ù§Ô∏è Heart Failure Agents

Digoxin (Lanoxin)

MOA: Inhibits Na+/K+ ATPase pump. Increases calcium in heart cells.

Effects: Positive Inotropic (Increased force of contraction), Negative Chronotropic (Decreased heart rate).

‚ò¢Ô∏è

Digoxin Toxicity

Therapeutic Index: Very Narrow (0.5 - 2.0 ng/mL).

Signs: Anorexia, Nausea/Vomiting, Visual disturbances (yellow/green halos), Bradycardia.

Nursing: Monitor Apical Pulse for 1 min. Hold if <60 bpm. Monitor K+ levels (Hypokalemia potentiates toxicity).

ü´Å Respiratory System (Asthma/COPD)

1. Bronchodilators (Relievers)

  • Beta-2 Agonists:
    • SABA (Short-acting): Albuterol/Salbutamol. Rescue inhaler for acute attacks. SE: Tremors, Tachycardia.
    • LABA (Long-acting): Salmeterol. Maintenance only (not for acute attacks).
  • Methylxanthines: Theophylline. Oral maintenance. Narrow therapeutic index (SE: Seizures, arrhythmias). Avoid caffeine.
  • Anticholinergics: Ipratropium. dries secretions, bronchodilation. Used in COPD.

2. Anti-Inflammatories (Controllers)

  • Glucocorticoids:
    • Inhaled: Beclomethasone. Rinse mouth after use to prevent oral thrush (Candidiasis).
    • Systemic: Prednisone. For severe exacerbations. Taper dose to prevent adrenal crisis.
  • Mast Cell Stabilizers: Cromolyn Sodium. Prophylaxis (e.g., exercise-induced asthma).
  • Leukotriene Modifiers: Montelukast. Oral prophylaxis.

Other Hematologic Drugs

  • Antiplatelets: Aspirin, Clopidogrel. Prevent arterial clots (MI, Stroke).
  • Thrombolytics (Fibrinolytics): Streptokinase, Alteplase (tPA). "Clot busters." Convert plasminogen to plasmin to dissolve clots. Used in acute MI, Ischemic stroke. High bleeding risk.
  • Hematinics: Iron (Ferrous sulfate - causes black stools), Folic Acid (Neural tube defects prevention), Vitamin B12.
