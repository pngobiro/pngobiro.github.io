Anthelminthic Drugs Overview

Generally these act by either one of these mechanism:

  1. 1 Causing paralysis of the worm
  2. 2 Damaging the worm leading to partial digestion or rejection by immune mechanisms
  3. 3 Interfere with the metabolism of the worm

Worms or larvae live in tissues of host body like muscles, viscera, menninges, subcutaneous tissues

Nematodes

  • Examples: Ascaris, pinworm ,whipworm, strongyloides, ancylostoma ( hookworm)
  • They cause infections of the intestine as well as the blood and tissues
  • Drugs used in their management include:
  • Diethylecarbamazine
  • Ivermectin
  • Mebendazole
  • Pyrantel pamoate
  • Thiabendazole

06

Benz-imidazoles

  • Include drugs like:
  • Mebendazole, albendazole, triclabendazole, metronidazole, tinidazole, thiabendazole
  • Used to treat intestinal helminthes infections, especially those caused by nematodes and cestodes

Mechanism of action:

  • Inhibits the polymerization of tubulin into microtubules
  • This inhibition prevents cellular division and the absorption of glucose in its intestines
07

ALBENDAZOLE

  • A broad-spectrum oral anthelmintic, is the drug of choice or RT of hydatid disease and cysticercosis
  • Also used in the treatment of pinworm and hookworm infections, ascariasis, trichuriasis, and strongyloidiasis

PK

  • Erratically absorbed after oral administration
  • Increased with a fatty meal
  • Rapidly undergoes first-pass metabolism in the liver to the active metabolite albendazole sulfoxide
  • It reaches variable maximum plasma concentrations about 3 hours following a 400 mg oral dose
  • Has a plasma half-life is 8-12 hours
  • The sulfoxide is mostly protein-bound,
  • Distributes well to tissues, and enters bile, cerebrospinal fluid, and hydatid cysts
  • Metabolites are excreted in the urine

Mechanism Of Action

  • Inhibits microtubule synthesis by binding to $\beta$ tubulin
  • Inhibits mitochondrial reductase causing reduced glucose transport
  • Intestinal parasites are immobilized and die slowly
  • Has larvicidal effects in hydatid disease, cysticercosis, ascariasis, and hookworm infection
  • Has ovicidal effects in ascariasis, ancylostomiasis, and trichuriasis.

Clinical uses

  • Used on empty stomach when used against intraluminal parasites but with a fatty meal when used against tissue parasites.
  1. 1 In ascariasis, trichuriasis, hookworm, pin worm infections : children over 2 years & adults (single dose 400 mg , repeated for 2-3 day in heavy ascaris infection. For 2 wks for pin worm infection
  2. 2 Hydatid diseases: drug of choice for medical therapy& adjunctive to surgical removal or aspiration of cysts.
  3. 3 Neurocysticercosis: Used with corticosteroid to decrease the inflammation caused by dying organism and it also reduces the duration of course for 21 days
  4. 4 Other infections: Drug of choice in cutaneous and visceral larva migrans, intestinal capillariasis, giardiasis & taeniasis.
  • Albendazole is the drug of choice in the treatment of cutaneous larva migrans ( 400 mg daily for 3 days),
  • Visceral larva migrans ( 400 mg twice daily for 5 days),
  • Intestinal capillariasis ( 400 mg daily for 10 days), microsporidial infections ( 400 mg twice daily for 2 weeks or longer)
  • It also has activity against trichinosis ( 400 mg twice daily for 1-2 weeks) and clonorchiasis ( 400 mg twice daily for 1 week)

ADRs

  • Used for 1-3 days, albendazole is nearly free of significant adverse effects
  • Mild and transient epigastric distress, diarrhea, headache, nausea, dizziness, lassitude, and insomnia can occur
  • In long-term use for hydatid disease, albendazole is well tolerated, but it can cause abdominal distress, headaches, fever, fatigue, alopecia, increases in liver enzymes, and pancytopenia (reduction of blood cell count - RBC, WBC & platelets).

  • Should not be given to patients with known hypersensitivity to other benzimidazole drugs or to those with cirrhosis
  • The safety of albendazole in pregnancy and in children younger than 2 years of age has not been established

MEBENDAZOLE

  • Synthetic benzimidazole
  • Wide spectrum and low incidence of adverse effects

Mechanism of action:

  • Inhibits microtubule synthesis.
  • It kills hookworm, pin worm, ascaris and trichuris eggs
  • Effective against a wide spectrum of nematodes
  • Drug of choice in treatmnent of infections by whipworm(trichuris trichura), pinworm(enterobius vermicularis), and hookworm(necator americanas and ancylostoma duodenale)
  • Bind and interfere with the synthesis of the parasite microtubules and by decreasing glucose uptake.
  • Affected parasite are then expelled with the faeces

Pk

  • Little of the oral dose is absorbed, therefore Free of toxicity
‚ö†Ô∏è Caution
  • Its embryotoxic and teratogenic and therefore Contraindicated in pregnancy

Pyrantel pamoate

  • A Broad spectrum: For treatment of infections by roundworms, pinworms, and hookworms
  • Poorly absorbed orally, its effect limited to the GIT
  • MOA: act as a depolarizing neuromuscular blocking agent, that result in the worm paralysis which is then expelled from the host

Clinical uses

  • Very effective against luminal organisms( mature or immature forms)
  • Not effective against migratory stages in the tissues or against ova
  • Given orally with or without food

Adverse Effects

  • Infrequent mild transient GI disturbance, drowsiness, headache ,insomnia, Rash ,fever
  • Mild: nausea, Vomiting and Diarrhoea
‚ö†Ô∏è Contraindications & Cautions
  • Should be used with caution in liver dysfunction, Pregnancy, and Children under 2 years of age
10

Thiabendazole

  • A systemic benzimindazole
  • Effective against thread worm(strongyloides stercoralis), strongyloides cutaneous larva migrants and trichinosis (trichinella spiralis)
  • Readily absorbed on oral administration
  • Half-life of 1-2 hrs
  • Completely metabolized in liver and 90% is excreted in urine
  • Can also be absorbed through skin
  • Similar to other benzimidazoles
  • It is ovicidal for some parasites

Clinical uses

  • Should be given after meals and tablets should be chewed
  • Strongyloidal infections & cutaneous larva migrans
  • Thiabendazole cream available for topical application
  • Drug also given orally for 2 days

More toxic than other benzamidazoles

  • Potentially toxic: toxicities include: dizziness, anorexia, nausea, and vomiting
  • CNS symptoms
  • Erythema multiforme and Stevens-Johnson syndrome
  • Irreversible liver failure
  • Not used in young children, pregnancy, hepatic and renal diseases
11

Diethylcarbamazine

  • Drug of choice for treatment of filariasis caused by wuchereria bancrofti
  • MOA: immobilize the organism, there surface membrane undergo alterations and become susceptible to the host defence

PK

  • Rapidly absorbed after oral administration
  • Partially metabolized
  • Excreted in urine

ADRs

  • Mild, due to the dying parasite that affect the skin; pruritus, wheals, but can also be systemic
12

DRUGS FOR TREATMENT OF TREMATODES

  • Praziquantel
  • Agent of choice in treatment of schistosomiasis
  • MOA increase permiability of the cell membrane to calcium causing contracture and paralysis of the organism

PK

  • Rapidly absorbed after oral administration
  • Distribute to CSF
  • Its extensively metabolized to an inactive metabolite, resulting in a short life
  • Excreted in urine

ADR

  • GIT disturbances, drowsiness, dizziness, malaise, and anorexia
  • Not recommended for pregnant, or nursing mothers
13

Drugs for treatment of cestodes (tapeworms)

Niclosamide

  • Drug of choice for most cestodes
  • Second-line drug for treatment of most tapeworm infections
  • MOA: inhibit parasite mitochondria anaerobic phosphorilation thus kill the worm

Clinical Uses

  • Treatment of most forms of tapeworms
  • Not effective against cysticercosis or hydatic disease
  • Given in the morning on empty stomach
  • Poorly absorbed from gut
  • Excreted in urine
  • Purgative is necessary to purge all dead segments& prevent liberation of ova

Adverse effects & Contraindications

  • Mild ,infrequent and transitory GI disturbance
  • Alcohol consumption should be avoided
  • Not indicated in children under 2 years of age or in pregnancy.
14

PIPERAZINE

  • Only recommended for the treatment of ascariasis
  • cure rate $90 \%$ for 2 days treatment
  • Readily absorbed orally and excreted mostly unchanged in urine

Mechanism of action

Causes paralysis of ascaris by blocking acetylcholine at myoneural junction, the live worms expelled by normal peristalsis

  • Treatment is continued for 3-4 days or repeated after one week in case of heavy infections.

Adverse Effects

  • GI disturbance
  • Neurotoxicity ,allergic reactions

Contraindications

  • Epilepsy or a history of epilepsy
  • Impaired liver or kidney functions
  • Pregnancy
  • Chronic neurologic disease
