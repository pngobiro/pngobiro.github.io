Skeletal Muscle Relaxants

üìö Peripheral Nervous System
‚è±Ô∏è 40 min read
01

üìä Classification

Skeletal muscle relaxants are drugs that reduce muscle tone or cause paralysis. They are broadly classified based on their site of action.

1. Peripherally Acting

Act at the neuromuscular junction (NMJ) or directly on the muscle.

  • Neuromuscular Blockers: Used for surgery/ICU.
    • Nondepolarizing (Competitive): d-Tubocurarine, Pancuronium, Vecuronium, Rocuronium, Atracurium.
    • Depolarizing: Succinylcholine (SCh).
  • Directly Acting: Dantrolene.

2. Centrally Acting

Act in the cerebrospinal axis to reduce tone (spasticity). Used for chronic neurological diseases or acute muscle spasms.

  • Diazepam
  • Baclofen
  • Tizanidine
  • Methocarbamol
02

‚ö° Neuromuscular Blockers: Mechanism

These drugs interfere with the action of Acetylcholine (ACh) at the Nicotinic ($N_M$) receptors on the motor end plate.

Feature Competitive (e.g., Curare) Depolarizing (e.g., SCh)
Mechanism Competes with ACh for $N_M$ receptors. No intrinsic activity. Prevents depolarization. Stimulates $N_M$ receptors (agonist) causing persistent depolarization $\rightarrow$ desensitization (Phase I block).
Initial Effect Flaccid paralysis immediately. Fasciculations (twitching) followed by paralysis.
Neostigmine Effect Reverses block (Increases ACh which displaces blocker). Potentiates block (Inhibits ACh breakdown, worsening depolarization).
Tetanic Stimulation Shows Fade (Post-tetanic potentiation present). Sustained contraction (No fade in Phase I).
03

‚ö†Ô∏è Succinylcholine (SCh)

The only depolarizing blocker in clinical use. It consists of two ACh molecules joined together.

Features

  • Pharmacokinetics: Extremely short duration (5-10 min) because it is rapidly hydrolysed by Plasma Pseudocholinesterase.
  • Primary Use: Endotracheal Intubation (rapid onset < 1 min, short duration).
‚ò£Ô∏è

Adverse Effects & Toxicities

  • Prolonged Apnoea (Scoline Apnoea): In patients with atypical/deficient pseudocholinesterase (genetic). Treatment is fresh blood transfusion.
  • Hyperkalaemia: SCh causes $K^+$ efflux from muscles. Can cause cardiac arrest in burns, trauma, or nerve injury patients. Contraindicated in burns > 24 hrs.
  • Muscle Pain: Post-operative myalgia due to initial fasciculations.
  • Malignant Hyperthermia: Rare genetic disorder involving uncontrolled $Ca^{2+}$ release from sarcoplasmic reticulum. Treatment: Dantrolene.
  • Increased Intraocular/Intragastric Pressure.
04

üíä Competitive Blockers

Used for muscle relaxation during surgery (maintenance) and in ICU.

Drug Duration Elimination Special Features
d-Tubocurarine Long Renal Releases Histamine (Hypotension, Bronchospasm). Ganglion block. (Obsolete).
Pancuronium Long Renal Vagolytic (Causes Tachycardia, hypertension).
Vecuronium Intermediate Hepatic/Bile Cardiovascular stability. No histamine release. Preferred in cardiac patients.
Atracurium Intermediate Hofmann Elimination Safe in Renal & Hepatic Failure. Releases Histamine. Metabolite Laudanosine can cause seizures.
Cisatracurium Intermediate Hofmann Isomer of Atracurium. No histamine release. Potent. Drug of choice in organ failure.
Rocuronium Intermediate Hepatic Rapid onset (60-90 sec). Alternative to SCh for intubation.
05

üîÑ Reversal of Block

Standard Reversal

Neostigmine (Anticholinesterase) is used to reverse competitive block at the end of surgery. It increases ACh levels which displace the blocker.

Atropine or Glycopyrrolate must be given prior to Neostigmine to prevent muscarinic side effects (bradycardia, salivation).

Sugammadex

A novel selective relaxant binding agent (cyclodextrin). It encapsulates Rocuronium or Vecuronium molecules in plasma, terminating their action immediately. Does not involve cholinergic mechanisms (no atropine needed). Useful for rapid reversal of deep block.

06

üõë Directly Acting: Dantrolene

Mechanism: Uncouples excitation-contraction. Acts on Ryanodine Receptor (RyR1) calcium channels in the sarcoplasmic reticulum of skeletal muscle and prevents intracellular $Ca^{2+}$ release.

Uses:

  • Malignant Hyperthermia: Drug of choice (i.v.).
  • Spasticity: Paraplegia, hemiplegia, cerebral palsy (Oral). Reduces muscle power (weakness is a side effect).

Adverse Effects: Hepatotoxicity (dose dependent), weakness, sedation.

07

üß† Centrally Acting Muscle Relaxants

Reduce muscle tone without abolishing voluntary power. Act on spinal/supraspinal polysynaptic reflexes.

  • Diazepam: Facilitates GABA-A action. Sedative. Used for muscle spasms, tetanus.
  • Baclofen: Selective GABA-B agonist. Hyperpolarizes neurones ($K^+$ efflux). Less sedative. Drug of choice for spinal spasticity (MS, ALS, spinal injury).
  • Tizanidine: Central $\alpha_2$ adrenergic agonist (congener of clonidine). Inhibits release of excitatory amino acids. Used in spasticity. Side effects: Dry mouth, drowsiness, hypotension.
  • Methocarbamol/Chlorzoxazone: Used for acute muscle spasms (strains, backache).

ü§î Problem Directed Study

Case 25.1

A 30-year lady brought to emergency with 40% burn injury has to be operated under general anaesthesia.

(a) Preferred Muscle Relaxant: Rocuronium or Vecuronium.

(b) Reason: Succinylcholine is Contraindicated. In patients with extensive burns (and massive tissue injury), SCh causes massive efflux of potassium from muscles, leading to severe hyperkalaemia and cardiac arrest. This risk is maximal from 24 hours to 1-2 years after injury. Rocuronium provides rapid onset for intubation without this risk.
