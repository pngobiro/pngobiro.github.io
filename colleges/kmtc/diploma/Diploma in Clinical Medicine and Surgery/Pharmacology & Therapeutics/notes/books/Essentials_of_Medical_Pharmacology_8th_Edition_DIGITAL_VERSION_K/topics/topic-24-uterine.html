‚ö° Uterine Stimulants (Oxytocics)

These drugs increase uterine motility, especially at term. They are used for induction of labour, uterine inertia, and control of postpartum haemorrhage.

Major Classes:

  • Posterior Pituitary Hormone: Oxytocin.
  • Ergot Alkaloids: Ergometrine, Methylergometrine.
  • Prostaglandins: $PGE_2$, $PGF_{2\alpha}$, Misoprostol.
02

üß¨ Oxytocin

Oxytocin is a nonapeptide secreted by the posterior pituitary. It is synthesized in the hypothalamus and transported down the axon to the neurohypophysis.

Physiological Roles

  • Labour: Released during labour; uterus is highly sensitive at term. Facilitates parturition.
  • Milk Ejection Reflex: Suckling induces oxytocin release $\rightarrow$ contraction of myoepithelial cells of mammary alveoli $\rightarrow$ milk letdown.

Pharmacological Actions

  • Uterus: Increases force and frequency of contractions. At low doses, full relaxation occurs between contractions (preserving foetal blood supply). Sensitivity increases sharply at term.
  • CVS: High doses cause transient vasodilatation (fall in BP) and reflex tachycardia.
  • Kidney: High doses have ADH-like antidiuretic action (risk of water intoxication).
03

üíâ Clinical Uses of Oxytocin

Oxytocin is the drug of choice for induction and augmentation of labour.

1. Induction of Labour

Indication: Postmaturity, toxaemia, premature rupture of membranes, etc.

Method: Slow i.v. infusion. Start with low rate, increase progressively until contractions are adequate. Monitoring of foetal heart rate and uterine contractions is essential.

üõë

Prerequisites & Contraindications

Ensure: Presentation is correct, no cephalopelvic disproportion, no placenta previa, no foetal distress, and no previous uterine scar.

2. Uterine Inertia

To augment feeble contractions in cases of protracted labour (after ruling out obstruction).

3. Postpartum Haemorrhage (PPH)

5 IU i.m. or i.v. infusion. Acts by contracting the uterus and compressing blood vessels. Preferred over ergometrine in hypertensive women.

4. Breast Engorgement

Intranasal spray to elicit milk ejection reflex.

‚ö†Ô∏è

Adverse Effects

  • Uterine Rupture: If used injudiciously or in obstructed labour.
  • Foetal Asphyxia: Due to strong contractions reducing placental blood flow.
  • Water Intoxication: With high doses + electrolyte-free fluids (due to ADH-like action).
04

üçÑ Ergometrine & Methylergometrine

Amine ergot alkaloids. Methylergometrine is more potent.

Actions

Increases force, frequency, and duration of contractions. Unlike oxytocin, moderate doses cause increased basal tone and sustained tetanic contraction (contracture). This compresses uterine vessels effectively.

Uses

Control/Prevention of PPH: 0.2 mg i.m. at the delivery of anterior shoulder. Produces sustained contraction to arrest bleeding.

Contraindicated: Before delivery of the foetus (can cause asphyxia/uterine rupture) and in patients with hypertension/vascular disease (weak vasoconstrictor action).

05

üß™ Prostaglandins

$PGE_2$ (Dinoprostone), $PGF_{2\alpha}$ (Carboprost), and Misoprostol ($PGE_1$ analogue).

  • Cervical Priming (Ripening): $PGE_2$ gel applied intracervically softens the cervix.
  • Induction of Labour: Alternative to oxytocin.
  • Mid-trimester Abortion: Intra-amniotic or extra-amniotic injection.
  • PPH: Carboprost ($PGF_{2\alpha}$) is used for refractory PPH.
06

üõë Uterine Relaxants (Tocolytics)

Drugs that decrease uterine motility. Used to delay premature labour (to allow time for corticosteroid administration for foetal lung maturation).

1. $\beta_2$ Adrenergic Agonists

Ritodrine, Isoxsuprine, Terbutaline.

Adverse Effects: Maternal tachycardia, hypotension, pulmonary edema, hyperglycaemia, hypokalaemia.

2. Calcium Channel Blockers

Nifedipine. Oral administration. Efficacy comparable to $\beta_2$ agonists with fewer side effects. Now often preferred.

3. Oxytocin Antagonist

Atosiban. Peptide analogue. Competitively blocks oxytocin receptors. Fewer cardiovascular side effects. Used in Europe.

4. Magnesium Sulfate

Competes with Calcium. Also used for eclampsia. Not recommended now for tocolysis due to higher perinatal mortality risk.

Case 23.1

A full term primigravida aged 26 years presented with labour pains for 24 hours (prolonged labour). Passed meconium stained liquor 2 hours ago (Indicator of foetal distress). Patient is distressed. Head engaged but cervix incompletely dilated. Uterine contractions weak.

(a) Action: The presence of foetal distress (meconium staining, tachycardia/irregularity) along with uterine inertia and maternal exhaustion usually calls for expedited delivery, often by Caesarean section, rather than stimulation of labour.

(b) Use of Stimulant: NO. Administration of a uterine stimulant like Oxytocin is generally contraindicated in the presence of foetal distress because strengthening contractions further reduces placental blood flow, worsening foetal hypoxia and potentially causing foetal death or uterine rupture. Oxytocin should only be used when foetal condition is satisfactory.
