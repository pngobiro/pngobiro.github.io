The anterior pituitary (adenohypophysis) is the master endocrine gland. Its secretion is controlled by the hypothalamus through releasing and release-inhibitory hormones transported via the hypothalamo-hypophyseal portal system, and by feedback inhibition from target gland hormones.

Hypothalamic Regulatory Hormones

Hormone Function
GHRH Stimulates Growth Hormone (GH) release
Somatostatin Inhibits GH and TSH release
TRH Stimulates TSH and Prolactin release
CRH Stimulates ACTH release
GnRH Stimulates FSH and LH release
Dopamine (PRIH) Inhibits Prolactin release
02

üìà Growth Hormone (GH)

Growth hormone promotes somatic growth (bone, cartilage, soft tissue) and influences metabolism (anabolic, lipolytic, hyperglycaemic). Most growth-promoting actions are mediated by Insulin-like Growth Factor-1 (IGF-1) produced in the liver.

Clinical Uses (Somatropin)

Somatropin is recombinant human GH.

  • Pituitary Dwarfism: Restores growth if epiphyses are not fused.
  • Turner Syndrome: Increases height in girls.
  • Chronic Renal Insufficiency: In children with suboptimal growth.
  • Adult GH Deficiency: Increases lean body mass, bone density, and energy.
  • AIDS-related Wasting: Improves physical condition.
‚ö†Ô∏è

Adverse Effects

Edema (salt/water retention), myalgia, arthralgia, carpal tunnel syndrome, intracranial hypertension (rare), hyperglycaemia (diabetogenic), lipodystrophy at injection site.

Mecasermin: Recombinant human IGF-1. Used for growth failure due to GH receptor abnormality (Laron dwarfism) or GH antibodies.

03

üõë GH Inhibitors (Somatostatin Analogues)

Somatostatin inhibits GH, TSH, insulin, glucagon, and gastrin secretion. It has a very short half-life (2-3 min), limiting its utility. Octreotide and Lanreotide are synthetic, longer-acting analogues.

Octreotide

  • Acromegaly: Suppresses GH secretion.
  • Secretory Diarrhoeas: Associated with carcinoid syndrome, VIPomas, AIDS.
  • Esophageal Varices: Reduces portal pressure (preferred over vasopressin).

Pegvisomant: A GH receptor antagonist (mutant GH) used in acromegaly resistant to other treatments.

ü•õ Prolactin & Inhibitors

Prolactin stimulates milk production. Its secretion is tonically inhibited by Dopamine from the hypothalamus acting on D2 receptors.

Prolactin Inhibitors (Dopamine Agonists)

Used for hyperprolactinemia (galactorrhoea, amenorrhoea, infertility in women; impotence in men), acromegaly, and Parkinsonism.

‚öß Gonadotropins (FSH & LH)

FSH (Follicle Stimulating Hormone) and LH (Luteinizing Hormone) regulate gonadal function. hCG (Human Chorionic Gonadotropin) produced by placenta has LH-like activity.

Preparations

  • Menotropins (hMG): Urine of menopausal women (FSH + LH).
  • Urofollitropin: Purified FSH.
  • hCG: Urine of pregnant women (LH action).
  • Recombinant: Follitropin (rFSH), Choriogonadotropin (rhCG).

Therapeutic Uses

Infertility (Female)

Induction of Ovulation: Sequential use of Menotropins/FSH (to mature follicle) followed by hCG (to trigger ovulation) in anovulatory infertility.

IVF / ART

Used to induce superovulation for harvesting multiple oocytes.

Male Hypogonadism

hCG (stimulates testosterone) + FSH (stimulates spermatogenesis) for oligospermia.

Cryptorchidism

hCG was used to descend testes (surgery preferred now).

‚ö†Ô∏è

Risk: Ovarian Hyperstimulation Syndrome (OHSS)

Excessive stimulation can cause cystic ovarian enlargement, pain, fluid shift, ascites, and shock. Multiple pregnancy is another risk.

üîÑ GnRH Agonists & Antagonists

GnRH (Gonadorelin): Secreted in pulses. Pulsatile administration stimulates Gonadotropins (diagnostic use). Continuous administration inhibits Gonadotropins.

Superactive GnRH Agonists

Drugs: Leuprolide, Goserelin, Nafarelin, Triptorelin.

‚ÑπÔ∏è

Mechanism: Medical Castration

Continuous administration causes initial stimulation ("flare"), followed by downregulation of pituitary GnRH receptors $\rightarrow$ inhibition of FSH/LH secretion $\rightarrow$ suppression of gonadal steroids (testosterone/estrogen).

Uses: Prostate cancer (with androgen antagonist), Endometriosis, Uterine fibroids, Precocious puberty, Assisted reproduction (to suppress endogenous LH surge).

GnRH Antagonists

Drugs: Ganirelix, Cetrorelix, Degarelix.

Mechanism: Competitive blockade of GnRH receptors. Causes immediate suppression of FSH/LH without initial flare.

Uses: IVF (prevent premature ovulation), Advanced prostate cancer (Degarelix).

07

üß™ TSH & ACTH

Thyroid Stimulating Hormone (TSH)

Stimulates thyroid hormone synthesis and release. Used primarily for diagnostic differentiation of hypothyroidism (primary vs secondary) and in thyroid cancer follow-up.

Adrenocorticotropic Hormone (ACTH)

Stimulates adrenal cortex to secrete cortisol. Used for diagnosis of adrenal insufficiency. For therapeutic purposes, corticosteroids are preferred over ACTH.
