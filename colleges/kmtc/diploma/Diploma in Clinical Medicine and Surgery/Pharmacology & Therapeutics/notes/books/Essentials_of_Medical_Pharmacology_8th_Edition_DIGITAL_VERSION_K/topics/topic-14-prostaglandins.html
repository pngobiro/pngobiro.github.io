Prostaglandins, Leukotrienes (Eicosanoids) and Platelet Activating Factor

üìö Autacoids and Related Drugs
‚è±Ô∏è 40 min read
01

üß¨ Overview

Prostaglandins (PGs) and Leukotrienes (LTs) are biologically active derivatives of 20 carbon atom polyunsaturated essential fatty acids that are released from cell membrane phospholipids. They are collectively called Eicosanoids (eicosa = 20).

Unlike hormones, there are no preformed stores of PGs and LTs. They are synthesized locally in response to stimuli and act locally (autacoids). Practically every cell and tissue is capable of synthesizing one or more types of PGs or LTs.

üí°

Historical Note

The term 'prostaglandin' was coined in the 1930s assuming they were derived from the prostate. In the 1970s, it was discovered that aspirin-like drugs act by inhibiting PG synthesis. Bergstrom, Samuelsson, and Vane received the Nobel Prize in 1982 for their work on PGs and LTs.

02

‚öóÔ∏è Chemistry, Biosynthesis & Degradation

In human tissues, the fatty acid released from membrane lipids in largest quantity is 5, 8, 11, 14-eicosatetraenoic acid (Arachidonic acid). This is the main precursor. The rate of synthesis is governed by the rate of release of arachidonic acid from membrane lipids by Phospholipase A2.

Two Major Pathways

1. Cyclooxygenase (COX) Pathway

Generates eicosanoids with a ring structure:

  • Prostaglandins (PGs): $PGE_2$, $PGF_{2\alpha}$
  • Prostacyclin: $PGI_2$
  • Thromboxane: $TXA_2$

2. Lipoxygenase (LOX) Pathway

Generates open chain compounds:

  • Leukotrienes (LTs): $LTB_4$, $LTC_4$, $LTD_4$, $LTE_4$
  • Lipoxins

Degradation: Occurs rapidly in most tissues, fastest in lungs. Plasma $t_{1/2}$ is few seconds to minutes. $PGI_2$ is catabolized mainly in the kidney.

COX-3

A splice variant of COX-1, designated COX-3, has been found in dog brain. It is inhibited by paracetamol and may explain its central antipyretic/analgesic action, though its exact role in humans is still under investigation.

04

‚öôÔ∏è Actions & Pathophysiological Roles

The actions vary depending on the specific PG and the tissue involved. Key actions are summarized below.

1. Cardiovascular System

  • $PGE_2$: Vasodilatation in most beds; fall in BP.
  • $PGF_{2\alpha}$: Constricts larger veins and pulmonary arteries.
  • $PGI_2$ (Prostacyclin): Potent vasodilator (generated by vascular endothelium).
  • $TXA_2$ (Thromboxane): Potent vasoconstrictor (generated by platelets).
  • Ductus Arteriosus: $PGE_2$ maintains patency during fetal life. NSAIDs can induce closure.

2. Platelets

$TXA_2$ (Pro-aggregatory)

Produced by platelets (COX-1). Potent inducer of aggregation and release reaction.

$PGI_2$ (Anti-aggregatory)

Produced by endothelium (COX-2). Potent inhibitor of aggregation. Constitutes a balance with TXA2.

Aspirin Role: Irreversibly acetylates platelet COX-1. Since platelets cannot regenerate enzyme (no nucleus), thromboxane synthesis is blocked for the life of the platelet (~7 days), causing an antithrombotic effect.

3. Uterus

$PGE_2$ and $PGF_{2\alpha}$ consistently contract human uterus (pregnant and non-pregnant). They soften the cervix (cervical priming). They are involved in dysmenorrhoea and initiation of labour.

4. Bronchi

  • Constrictors: $PGF_{2\alpha}$, $PGD_2$, $TXA_2$, Cysteinyl Leukotrienes ($LTC_4, LTD_4$).
  • Dilators: $PGE_2$, $PGI_2$.
  • Aspirin-induced asthma: Inhibition of COX diverts arachidonic acid to LOX pathway $\rightarrow$ excess Leukotrienes $\rightarrow$ bronchoconstriction.

5. Stomach

$PGE_2$ and $PGI_2$ (via COX-1) are natural ulcer protectives. They decrease acid secretion, increase mucus/bicarbonate secretion, and improve mucosal blood flow. NSAIDs cause ulcers by blocking this protective mechanism.

6. Kidney

$PGE_2$ and $PGI_2$ increase water and electrolyte excretion (diuretic effect) and cause renal vasodilatation. They regulate renal blood flow. NSAIDs can cause Na+/water retention and counteract diuretics.

üì° Receptors & Mechanisms

All prostanoid receptors are G-protein coupled. They are grouped into Contractile, Relaxant, and Inhibitory types.

  • DP ($PGD_2$): Relaxant ($DP_1$, $\uparrow$ cAMP) or Inhibitory ($DP_2$, $\downarrow$ cAMP).
  • EP ($PGE_2$):
    • $EP_1$: Contractile ($G_q \rightarrow IP_3/DAG \rightarrow Ca^{2+}$).
    • $EP_2, EP_4$: Relaxant ($G_s \rightarrow \uparrow$ cAMP).
    • $EP_3$: Inhibitory ($G_i \rightarrow \downarrow$ cAMP), decreased lipolysis, gastric acid.
  • FP ($PGF_{2\alpha}$): Contractile (Uterus, Eye).
  • IP ($PGI_2$): Relaxant (Vasodilatation, anti-platelet).
  • TP ($TXA_2$): Contractile (Platelet aggregation, vasoconstriction).
07

üíâ Clinical Uses of PGs

Clinical application is restricted due to short duration, cost, and side effects. However, they are valuable in specific niches.

1. Abortion & Labour

  • Mifepristone + Misoprostol ($PGE_1$): Medical termination of pregnancy (MTP) up to 7 weeks (highly successful).
  • Carboprost (15-methyl $PGF_{2\alpha}$): Control of Postpartum Hemorrhage (PPH) due to uterine atony (2nd line to oxytocin/ergometrine).
  • Dinoprostone ($PGE_2$): Cervical priming (softening) before induction of labour.

2. Glaucoma

Latanoprost, Travoprost, Bimatoprost ($PGF_{2\alpha}$ analogues): Topical eye drops increase uveoscleral outflow. First choice drugs for wide angle glaucoma.

3. Peptic Ulcer

Misoprostol ($PGE_1$ analogue): Prevents NSAID-induced ulcers. (Less effective than PPIs for general ulcer healing).

4. Cardiovascular

  • Alprostadil ($PGE_1$): Keeps ductus arteriosus patent in neonates with congenital heart defects until surgery.
  • Epoprostenol ($PGI_2$): Pulmonary hypertension, preventing platelet aggregation in dialysis.
üí°

Other Uses

Erectile Dysfunction: Alprostadil injection into corpora cavernosa (largely replaced by Sildenafil).

08

ü©∏ Platelet Activating Factor (PAF)

PAF is an acetylglyceryl ether-phosphoryl choline. It is a potent lipid mediator synthesized by WBC, platelets, and endothelium (not stored).

Actions

  • Platelets: Induces aggregation and release reaction (releases $TXA_2$).
  • Inflammation: Potent chemotactic for neutrophils/eosinophils. Increases vascular permeability (more potent than histamine).
  • Visceral Smooth Muscle: Bronchoconstriction (long lasting), intestinal contraction.
  • Stomach: Highly ulcerogenic.

Pathophysiological Roles

PAF mediates inflammation, bronchial asthma (sustained hyper-reactivity), anaphylactic shock, and thrombosis.

Case 13.1

A full term primigravida presented with labour pains... After 8 hours the cervix was still firm and not adequately dilated.

(a) Medication for cervix: Yes. Prostaglandin $E_2$ (Dinoprostone) gel can be used for cervical priming (ripening). It softens the cervix and makes it compliant without stimulating strong uterine contractions at low doses. It is applied intracervically or intravaginally.

(b) Contraindications: Based on findings (cephalopelvic disproportion absent, head engaged, fetal heart normal), there are no specific contraindications in this case. It should be used with caution, monitoring uterine activity and fetal heart rate.
