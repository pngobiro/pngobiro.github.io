Estrogens, Progestins and Contraceptives

üìö Hormones and Related Drugs
‚è±Ô∏è 60 min read
01

üß¨ Estrogens

Estradiol is the major natural estrogen. It is rapidly metabolized in the liver, making it inactive orally.

Synthetic Estrogens

  • Steroidal: Ethinylestradiol, Mestranol, Tibolone.
  • Nonsteroidal: Diethylstilbestrol (DES) - rarely used now.

Actions

  • Reproductive: Growth of uterus/vagina, proliferation of endometrium, induction of watery cervical secretion, ductal growth in breast.
  • Metabolic: Anabolic (weaker than testosterone), fusion of epiphyses.
  • Bone: Retards bone resorption (maintains bone mass).
  • Lipid Profile: Beneficial. Increases HDL, decreases LDL.
  • Coagulation: Increases clotting factors (prothrombotic).
‚ÑπÔ∏è

Mechanism

Bind to nuclear Estrogen Receptors (ER$\alpha$ and ER$\beta$) $\rightarrow$ Dimerization $\rightarrow$ Binding to Estrogen Response Elements (ERE) on DNA $\rightarrow$ Gene transcription.

02

üëµ Hormone Replacement Therapy (HRT)

Used for menopausal symptoms (hot flashes, urogenital atrophy, osteoporosis risk). Generally restricted to women < 60 years or within 10 years of menopause.

Current Guidelines

  • Vasomotor Symptoms: Primary indication. Use smallest effective dose for shortest duration.
  • Intact Uterus: Must use Estrogen + Progestin (to prevent endometrial cancer).
  • Hysterectomized: Use Estrogen alone.
  • Osteoporosis: Not first line (Bisphosphonates are preferred). Used if other drugs are not tolerated.
‚ö†Ô∏è

Risks of Combined HRT (WHI Study)

  • Increased risk of Breast Cancer.
  • Increased risk of Venous Thromboembolism (DVT/PE).
  • Increased risk of Stroke and Coronary Artery Disease (in older women).
  • Increased risk of Dementia.

Tibolone: A tissue-specific steroid (STEAR) that has estrogenic, progestational, and weak androgenic actions. Relieves symptoms and prevents osteoporosis without stimulating endometrium.

üé≠ Antiestrogens, SERMs & Aromatase Inhibitors

1. Clomiphene Citrate

Mechanism: Blocks estrogen negative feedback on pituitary $\rightarrow$ Increased FSH/LH secretion $\rightarrow$ Induction of Ovulation.

Use: Drug of choice for infertility due to anovulatory cycles.

2. Selective Estrogen Receptor Modulators (SERMs)

Tissue-selective actions (Agonist in some, Antagonist in others).

Drug Breast Endometrium Bone Use
Tamoxifen Antagonist Agonist (Hyperplasia risk) Agonist Breast Cancer (ER+ive)
Raloxifene Antagonist Antagonist (Safe) Agonist Osteoporosis (Postmenopausal)
Toremifene Antagonist Agonist Agonist Breast Cancer

3. Aromatase Inhibitors (AIs)

Letrozole, Anastrozole, Exemestane.

Inhibit the enzyme aromatase (which converts androgens to estrogens). More effective than Tamoxifen for breast cancer in postmenopausal women (ovaries not functional; estrogen comes from peripheral aromatization). Not effective in premenopausal women unless ovarian suppression is done.

4. SERD

Fulvestrant: Selective Estrogen Receptor Down-regulator. Pure antagonist. Used in Tamoxifen-resistant breast cancer.

04

üß¨ Progestins

Natural: Progesterone (short acting, inactive orally). Micronized progesterone is orally active.

Synthetic:

  • Progesterone derivatives: Medroxyprogesterone acetate (MPA), Megestrol, Dydrogesterone.
  • 19-Nortestosterone derivatives (Estranes): Norethindrone (Norethisterone).
  • Gonanes (Potent): Levonorgestrel, Desogestrel, Norgestimate.

Uses

  • Contraception: (Most common use).
  • HRT: To protect endometrium.
  • Dysfunctional Uterine Bleeding (DUB): Norethindrone.
  • Endometriosis: Induces atrophy of ectopic endometrium.
  • Threatened Abortion: (Efficacy controversial).
05

üõë Antiprogestins

Mifepristone (RU-486)

A progesterone receptor antagonist (partial agonist in absence of progesterone). Also has antiglucocorticoid activity.

Actions: Sensitizes myometrium to prostaglandins, causes decidual necrosis and detachment of blastocyst, softens cervix.

Uses:

  • Medical Termination of Pregnancy (MTP): Up to 7 weeks (now extended to 9 weeks/63 days).
    Regimen: Mifepristone 200 mg (Oral) $\rightarrow$ 48 hrs later $\rightarrow$ Misoprostol 400-800 $\mu$g (Oral/Vaginal). Success rate > 90%.
  • Emergency Contraception.
  • Cervical Ripening.
  • Cushing's Syndrome (due to antiglucocorticoid action).
06

üíä Hormonal Contraceptives

1. Combined Oral Pills (COC)

Estrogen (Ethinylestradiol 30 $\mu$g) + Progestin (Levonorgestrel/Desogestrel). Taken for 21 days + 7 days gap/iron pills.

Mechanism:

  1. Inhibition of Ovulation: Suppression of FSH/LH surge (Primary mechanism).
  2. Cervical Mucus: Makes it thick and hostile to sperm.
  3. Endometrium: Makes it unsuitable for implantation.

2. Progestin-Only Pill (Minipill)

Taken continuously. No estrogen. Suitable for lactating women. Mechanism: Mainly cervical mucus thickening and endometrial atrophy. Ovulation inhibited in only ~50%.

3. Injectables

DMPA (Depot Medroxyprogesterone Acetate): 150 mg i.m. every 3 months. Highly effective. Causes amenorrhoea and delayed return of fertility.

‚ö†Ô∏è

Adverse Effects & Contraindications of COCs

Serious Risks: Venous Thromboembolism (DVT/PE), MI/Stroke (in smokers > 35 yrs), Hypertension.

Contraindications: H/o Thromboembolism/Stroke/CAD, Active Liver Disease, Breast/Genital Cancer, Undiagnosed vaginal bleeding, Pregnancy, Heavy smokers > 35 yrs.

‚úÖ

Non-Contraceptive Benefits

Reduced risk of Ovarian and Endometrial cancer. Reduced menstrual blood loss (anemia), dysmenorrhoea, benign breast disease.

07

üöë Emergency Contraception (Postcoital)

To prevent pregnancy after unprotected intercourse.

  1. Levonorgestrel (1.5 mg): Single dose within 72 hours. Standard method (i-Pill). High efficacy, less nausea.
  2. Ulipristal (30 mg): Selective Progesterone Receptor Modulator. Effective up to 120 hours (5 days). More effective than Levonorgestrel.
  3. Mifepristone (600 mg): Highly effective.
  4. Copper-T Insertion: Within 5 days. Most effective.

ü§î Problem Directed Study

üìù

Case 22.1

A 55-year-old postmenopausal woman on Tamoxifen adjuvant therapy for breast cancer (ER+ive) for 1 year is found to have thickening of endometrium on ultrasound.

(a) Cause: Tamoxifen acts as an estrogen agonist on the endometrium (while being antagonist in breast). This causes endometrial proliferation/hyperplasia, increasing the risk of endometrial carcinoma.

(b) Recommendation: Since she is postmenopausal, the therapy should ideally be switched to an Aromatase Inhibitor (Letrozole or Anastrozole). AIs are more effective in postmenopausal women and do not cause endometrial hyperplasia (though they increase osteoporosis risk).

üìù

Case 22.2

28-year-old mother (9 month baby) wants oral contraceptives.

Advice: History: Check for contraindications (Thromboembolism, Liver disease, Breast cancer, Smoking, Migraine). Exam: BP, Breast exam, Pelvic exam. Investigations: Urine sugar/protein. If normal, a low-dose Combined Oral Pill is suitable. Since baby is 9 months old, lactation is less likely to be affected significantly, though progestin-only pill is safer for lactation. However, combined pills are more reliable for cycle control.
