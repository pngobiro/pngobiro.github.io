Androgens and Drugs for Erectile Dysfunction

01

üß¨ Androgens (Testosterone)

Testosterone is the principal male sex hormone produced by the interstitial (Leydig) cells of the testes (5-12 mg/day) under the influence of LH. In many target tissues (prostate, skin), it is converted to the more potent Dihydrotestosterone (DHT) by the enzyme $5\alpha$-reductase.

Actions

  • Sex Organs: Growth of penis, scrotum, seminal vesicles, prostate.
  • Secondary Sex Characters: Male pattern hair growth (beard, pubic), thickening of skin, deepening of voice, libido.
  • Anabolic: Increases muscle mass, bone growth (and epiphyseal fusion), nitrogen retention.
  • Haematological: Stimulates erythropoiesis.

Preparations

Testosterone undergoes high first-pass metabolism and is inactive orally.

Type Drugs Route/Duration
Esters (Parenteral) Testosterone propionate i.m. short acting (1-2 days)
Testosterone enanthate/cypionate i.m. long acting (2-3 weeks)
Alkylated (Oral) Methyltestosterone, Fluoxymesterone Oral (Hepatotoxic)
Others Testosterone undecanoate Oral (Absorbed via lymphatics)
Transdermal Testosterone Gel/Patch Skin application

Adverse Effects

  • Virilization: In women (hirsutism, voice change, amenorrhoea) and children.
  • Hepatic: Cholestatic jaundice (17-alkylated compounds), hepatic carcinoma.
  • Prostate: Progression of prostate cancer (Contraindicated).
  • Others: Acne, edema, salt retention, precocious puberty, oligozoospermia (suppression of Gn), lowered HDL.
02

üí™ Anabolic Steroids

Synthetic androgens with higher anabolic:androgenic activity ratio. However, pure separation of effects is not possible.

Drugs: Methandienone, Nandrolone (Deca-Durabolin), Stanozolol, Oxymetholone.

Uses

  • Catabolic States: Acute illness, trauma, surgery (short term benefit).
  • Osteoporosis: In elderly males (Bisphosphonates are preferred).
  • Anaemia: Hypoplastic/haemolytic (stimulates erythropoietin).
  • Athletic Performance: Abuse is common ("Doping"). Increases muscle strength but illegal and carries health risks.
03

üö´ Antiandrogens

Receptor Antagonists

Block androgen receptors. Used mainly in prostate cancer.

  • Flutamide: Nonsteroidal. Increases LH secretion (needs to be combined with GnRH agonist). Hepatotoxic.
  • Bicalutamide: More potent, longer acting, better tolerated. Used in metastatic prostate carcinoma.
  • Cyproterone acetate: Steroidal (progestational). Inhibits LH release also. Used for precocious puberty, female hirsutism/acne.
04

üìâ 5$\alpha$-Reductase Inhibitors

Finasteride and Dutasteride.

Mechanism: Inhibit the enzyme $5\alpha$-reductase which converts testosterone to DHT. Since DHT is the active hormone in prostate and hair follicles, its deficiency causes prostate atrophy and hair regrowth.

Uses

  • Benign Prostatic Hypertrophy (BPH): Reduces prostate size, improves flow. Effect takes months.
  • Male Pattern Baldness: Finasteride (1 mg) prevents hair loss.

Side Effects: Decreased libido, impotence, decreased ejaculate volume.

05

üíä Drugs for Erectile Dysfunction

Erectile dysfunction (ED) is treated primarily by enhancing the Nitric Oxide (NO) - cGMP pathway.

06

üõë Phosphodiesterase-5 (PDE-5) Inhibitors

Sildenafil (Viagra), Tadalafil, Vardenafil.

Mechanism: Selectively inhibit PDE-5 enzyme in the corpus cavernosum $\rightarrow$ prevent breakdown of cGMP $\rightarrow$ sustain NO-mediated smooth muscle relaxation $\rightarrow$ penile erection.

üí°

Note

They do not cause erection in the absence of sexual arousal (require NANC nerve stimulation to release initial NO).

Drug Duration Onset Notes
Sildenafil 4-6 hours 30-60 min Take on empty stomach (food delays absorption). Visual disturbances (Blue vision).
Tadalafil 24-36 hours 30-60 min "Weekend pill". Less visual side effects. May cause back pain.
Vardenafil 4-5 hours 30 min Similar to Sildenafil.

Contraindication: NITRATES

PDE-5 inhibitors markedly potentiate the hypotensive action of organic nitrates (Nitroglycerine, Isosorbide mononitrate). Concomitant use is absolutely contraindicated as it can cause precipitous fall in BP, MI, and death.

Other Drugs

Alprostadil ($PGE_1$): Injected intracavernosally. Used in patients not responding to PDE-5 inhibitors.

üìù

Case 21.1

A 65-year-old man with prostate cancer was prescribed Triptorelin (GnRH agonist). After 1 week, he reported increased bone pain and urinary difficulty. PSA levels rose.

(a) Cause of Flare: This is the "Flare phenomenon". GnRH agonists initially stimulate pituitary GnRH receptors causing a surge in LH and Testosterone secretion before downregulation sets in. The initial rise in testosterone stimulated the prostate cancer, worsening symptoms.

(b) Prevention: Yes, it could be avoided by starting an Antiandrogen (like Flutamide or Bicalutamide) a few days before and continuing for weeks after starting Triptorelin. This blocks the action of the initial testosterone surge.

(c) Relief of Bone Pain: Bisphosphonates (like Zoledronate) or Denosumab can be given to relieve bone pain and prevent skeletal events associated with metastasis.
