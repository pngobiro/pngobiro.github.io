Gonadotropins and GnRH

GnRH Analogues (Gonadorelin, Leuprolide, Goserelin):
Mechanism: Pulsatile administration stimulates gonadotropin release. Continuous administration causes receptor downregulation and inhibition of FSH/LH release (Medical Castration).
Uses: Prostate cancer (continuous), Endometriosis, Precocious puberty, IVF (to prevent LH surge).

Gonadotropins (FSH, LH, hCG):
Uses: Induction of ovulation (Infertility), Cryptorchism (hCG), Hypogonadotropic hypogonadism.

Estrogens

Natural: Estradiol, Estrone, Estriol.
Synthetic: Ethinyl estradiol (Oral), Mestranol, Diethylstilbestrol (Non-steroidal).
Actions: Growth of genital tract, secondary sex characteristics, feedback regulation of GnRH, maintenance of bone mass (inhibit osteoclasts), favorable lipid profile (HDL $\uparrow$, LDL $\downarrow$).

Therapeutic Uses:
1. Hormone Replacement Therapy (HRT): For menopausal symptoms (hot flushes, vaginitis) and osteoporosis prevention.
2. Contraception: Combined with progestins.
3. Primary Hypogonadism: Turner's syndrome.

Estrogen Adverse Effects

Nausea, fluid retention, Thromboembolism (DVT, PE), increased risk of endometrial cancer (if used without progestin), breast tenderness, gallstones.

SERMs (Selective Estrogen Receptor Modulators)

Compounds with tissue-selective estrogen agonist or antagonist activity.

Drug Agonist Effect Antagonist Effect Clinical Use
Clomiphene - Hypothalamus/Pituitary Induction of Ovulation (blocks negative feedback).
Tamoxifen Bone, Endometrium Breast Estrogen-receptor positive Breast Cancer.
Raloxifene Bone Breast, Uterus Osteoporosis in postmenopausal women.
Ormeloxifene (Centchroman) Bone Uterus Oral Contraceptive (Saheli), DUB.

Progestins

Natural: Progesterone.
Synthetic: Medroxyprogesterone acetate (MPA), Norethisterone, Levonorgestrel, Desogestrel.
Uses: Contraception, DUB, Endometriosis, Endometrial cancer, HRT (to protect endometrium).

Antiprogestin (Mifepristone/RU-486):
Used for medical termination of pregnancy (with Misoprostol) and as emergency contraception.

üíä Antifertility Agents

Hormonal Contraceptives

1. Combined Oral Contraceptives (COC)

Contains Estrogen (Ethinyl estradiol) + Progestin. Taken for 21 days with 7 days gap.
Mechanism:
1. Inhibit Ovulation: Suppression of FSH and LH surge.
2. Cervical Mucus: Makes it thick and hostile to sperm.
3. Endometrium: Makes it unsuitable for implantation.
Contraindications: History of thromboembolism, stroke, breast cancer, liver disease, heavy smokers >35 years.

2. Progestin Only Pills (POP / Mini-pills)

Contains only progestin (e.g., Norethisterone). Taken continuously.
Mechanism: Mainly cervical mucus thickening and endometrial atrophy. Ovulation inhibited in only 50%.
Indication: Lactating women (estrogens suppress milk).

3. Emergency Contraception ("Morning After" Pill)

Used within 72 hours of unprotected intercourse.
Levonorgestrel: 1.5 mg single dose (or 0.75 mg x 2 doses).
Ulipristal: Selective progesterone receptor modulator. Effective up to 5 days.
Mifepristone: Antiprogestin.

4. Injectables and Implants

DMPA (Depot Medroxyprogesterone Acetate): IM every 3 months.
NET-EN: IM every 2 months.
Implants (Norplant): Subcutaneous levonorgestrel rods (5 years).

‚ôÇÔ∏è Androgens, Anabolic Steroids and Antiandrogens

Androgens

Testosterone: Natural androgen. High first pass metabolism (ineffective orally).
Preparations: Testosterone enanthate/cypionate (IM depot), Methyltestosterone (Oral - hepatotoxic), Transdermal patches/gels.
Uses: Hypogonadism (Replacement), Delayed puberty.

Anabolic Steroids

Nandrolone, Oxandrolone, Stanozolol.
Synthetic derivatives with higher anabolic:androgenic ratio.
Uses: Catabolic states (severe trauma, burns), Bone marrow stimulation (aplastic anemia - historical), Osteoporosis.
Abuse: Used by athletes to increase muscle mass. Causes hepatotoxicity, aggression ("Roid rage"), sterility, cardiovascular damage.

Drugs for Erectile Dysfunction

Sildenafil (Viagra), Tadalafil.
Mechanism: Inhibit PDE-5 enzyme $\rightarrow$ increase cGMP in corpus cavernosum $\rightarrow$ smooth muscle relaxation $\rightarrow$ erection.
ADR: Headache, flushing, visual disturbances (blue vision - PDE6 inhibition).

üö´

Contraindication

Sildenafil is absolutely contraindicated with Nitrates. Potentiation of vasodilation causes severe, fatal hypotension.

Antiandrogens

5-Alpha Reductase Inhibitors

Finasteride, Dutasteride.
Inhibit conversion of Testosterone to Dihydrotestosterone (DHT) (the potent androgen in prostate/skin).
Uses: Benign Prostatic Hyperplasia (BPH), Male pattern baldness.

Receptor Antagonists

Flutamide, Bicalutamide.
Block androgen receptors.
Uses: Prostate Cancer (combined with GnRH agonists to block "flare").
Cyproterone acetate: Progestin with antiandrogenic activity. Used for hirsutism, precocious puberty.

ü¶¥ Calcium, Phosphorus, Vitamin D and Bisphosphonates

Hormonal Regulation

  • Parathyroid Hormone (PTH): Increases plasma $Ca^{++}$ (Bone resorption, Renal reabsorption, Vit D activation). Decreases plasma Phosphate.
  • Vitamin D (Calcitriol): Increases plasma $Ca^{++}$ and Phosphate (increases intestinal absorption).
  • Calcitonin: Decreases plasma $Ca^{++}$ (inhibits bone resorption).

Pharmacological Agents

1. Vitamin D Preparations

Cholecalciferol (D3), Ergocalciferol (D2).
Calcitriol ($1,25(OH)_2 D_3$): Active form. Used in renal osteodystrophy (where kidney cannot activate D3) and hypoparathyroidism.

2. Bisphosphonates

Alendronate, Risedronate (Oral); Zoledronic acid, Pamidronate (IV).
Mechanism: Bind to hydroxyapatite crystals in bone and inhibit osteoclast activity (inhibit bone resorption).
Uses: Osteoporosis (Postmenopausal, Steroid-induced), Paget's disease, Hypercalcemia of malignancy.
ADR: Esophagitis (Oral agents - must take with water and stand upright for 30 min), Osteonecrosis of the jaw (rare, high dose).

3. Other Agents

Teriparatide: Recombinant PTH. Intermittent low doses stimulate osteoblasts (anabolic effect on bone). Used for severe osteoporosis.
Cinacalcet: Calcimimetic. Increases sensitivity of calcium-sensing receptor. Used in secondary hyperparathyroidism.
Sevelamer: Phosphate binder. Used in chronic renal failure.

üöë

Management of Hypercalcemia

  1. Saline Rehydration: IV Normal Saline (Critical first step).
  2. Loop Diuretics (Furosemide): Increases Ca++ excretion (only after rehydration).
  3. Bisphosphonates (Zoledronate): Potent, long-acting inhibition of resorption.
  4. Calcitonin: Rapid but short-lived effect.
