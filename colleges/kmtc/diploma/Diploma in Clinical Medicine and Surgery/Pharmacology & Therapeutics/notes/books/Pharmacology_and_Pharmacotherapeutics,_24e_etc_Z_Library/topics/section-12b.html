üçÑ Antifungal Agents

Classification

Polyenes (Antibiotics)

Amphotericin B, Nystatin. Bind to ergosterol in fungal cell membrane, creating pores and increasing permeability.

Azoles (Synthetic)

Imidazoles: Ketoconazole, Miconazole, Clotrimazole.
Triazoles: Fluconazole, Itraconazole, Voriconazole.
Inhibit ergosterol synthesis (14-alpha-demethylase).

Specific Drugs

Amphotericin B: Gold standard for systemic mycoses (Cryptococcus, Histoplasma, Aspergillus). Highly toxic (Nephrotoxicity, infusion reactions). Liposomal preparations reduce toxicity.

Fluconazole: High oral bioavailability, good CSF penetration. Drug of choice for Cryptococcal meningitis and Candidiasis.

Ketoconazole: Inhibits human steroid synthesis (Anti-androgenic). Used in Cushing's syndrome.

Griseofulvin: Keratophilic. Deposited in keratin precursor cells. Used for Dermatophytosis (Ringworm). Induces CYP450.

ü´Å Chemotherapy of Tuberculosis

First Line Drugs

Drug Action Mechanism Key Adverse Effects
Isoniazid (H) Bactericidal Inhibits mycolic acid synthesis Peripheral neuropathy (prevent with Pyridoxine), Hepatotoxicity
Rifampicin (R) Bactericidal Inhibits DNA-dependent RNA polymerase Hepatotoxicity, Red/Orange urine, Enzyme induction
Pyrazinamide (Z) Bactericidal (Intracellular) Unknown (active at acid pH) Hepatotoxicity, Hyperuricemia (Gout)
Ethambutol (E) Bacteriostatic Inhibits arabinosyl transferase Optic neuritis (Red-green color blindness), Hyperuricemia
Streptomycin (S) Bactericidal Inhibits protein synthesis (30S) Ototoxicity, Nephrotoxicity

Treatment Regimens (DOTS)

New Cases (Category I): 2 (HRZE)3 + 4 (HR)3. (Intensive phase: 2 months of 4 drugs; Continuation phase: 4 months of 2 drugs). All given thrice weekly.

MDR-TB: Resistance to both Isoniazid and Rifampicin. Requires treatment with Second Line drugs (Kanamycin, Fluoroquinolones, Ethionamide, Cycloserine, PAS) for 24 months.

üéóÔ∏è Chemotherapy of Leprosy

Drugs

  • Dapsone (DDS): Folate synthesis inhibitor (like sulfonamides). Bacteriostatic. ADR: Hemolysis (G6PD deficiency), Lepra reaction.
  • Rifampicin: Rapidly bactericidal. Kills 99.9% bacilli in days.
  • Clofazimine: Dye with anti-inflammatory properties. Useful in Lepra reactions (ENL). Causes skin pigmentation (red/black).

Multidrug Therapy (MDT) Protocols

Type Regimen Duration
Paucibacillary (PB) Rifampicin (600mg monthly)
Dapsone (100mg daily)		 6 months
Multibacillary (MB) Rifampicin (600mg monthly)
Clofazimine (300mg monthly + 50mg daily)
Dapsone (100mg daily)		 12 months

ü¶ü Chemotherapy of Malaria

Drug Classification by Action

  • Erythrocytic Schizonticides (Clinical Cure): Chloroquine, Quinine, Mefloquine, Artemisinins. (Treat the acute attack).
  • Tissue Schizonticides (Radical Cure): Primaquine. (Kills hypnozoites in liver for P. vivax/ovale).
  • Gametocidals: Primaquine (kills gametocytes, prevents transmission).

Specific Drugs

Chloroquine: Concentrates in parasite food vacuole, prevents polymerization of toxic heme to hemozoin. Drug of choice for sensitive malaria and pregnancy.

Primaquine: Radical cure (14 days). Risk of hemolysis in G6PD deficiency. Contraindicated in pregnancy.

Artemisinin Derivatives (Artesunate, Artemether): Fastest acting. Producing free radicals. First line for **Severe/Cerebral Malaria** and Chloroquine-resistant malaria (ACT - Artemisinin Combination Therapy).

Mefloquine: Long half-life. Neuropsychiatric side effects.

üöë

Severe Falciparum Malaria

Medical emergency. Treatment: IV Artesunate (preferred) or IV Quinine. Watch for hypoglycemia (Quinine stimulates insulin).

ü¶† Amoebiasis and Giardiasis

Amoebicides

  • Tissue Amoebicides: Metronidazole, Tinidazole, Emetine, Chloroquine (Liver only). Used for invasive dysentery and liver abscess.
  • Luminal Amoebicides: Diloxanide furoate, Iodoquinol. Used to treat cyst passers and after tissue amoebicides to prevent relapse.

Metronidazole: Drug of choice for Amoebiasis, Giardiasis, Trichomoniasis, and Anaerobic bacterial infections.
Mechanism: Nitro group reduction forms toxic radicals producing DNA damage.
ADR: Metallic taste, Disulfiram-like reaction with alcohol, Neuropathy.

üß¨ Antiviral Drugs and HIV

Anti-Herpes Drugs

Acyclovir: Activated by viral thymidine kinase. Inhibits viral DNA polymerase. Drug of choice for HSV and VZV.

Anti-Influenza Drugs

Oseltamivir (Tamiflu): Neuraminidase inhibitor. Prevents release of virus.

Anti-Retroviral Drugs (HIV)

Class Examples Mechanism/Notes
NRTI Zidovudine (AZT), Lamivudine (3TC), Tenofovir Nucleoside Reverse Transcriptase Inhibitors. Need phosphorylation. Mitochondrial toxicity (Lactic acidosis).
NNRTI Nevirapine, Efavirenz Non-Nucleoside RTI. Bind directly to enzyme. Rash, Hepatotoxicity.
Protease Inhibitors Ritonavir, Indinavir, Lopinavir Prevent viral maturation. Lipodystrophy, metabolic syndrome. Ritonavir used as "booster".
Integrase Inhibitors Raltegravir Prevent integration of viral DNA into host genome.

HAART: Highly Active Anti-Retroviral Therapy. Usually 2 NRTIs + 1 NNRTI or 1 PI.

üéóÔ∏è Cancer Chemotherapy (Brief)

Principles: Kill rapidly dividing cells. Low therapeutic index. Combination therapy used to prevent resistance and reduce toxicity.

Alkylating Agents

Cyclophosphamide, Cisplatin. Cross-link DNA. Cell cycle non-specific. Toxicity: Bone marrow depression, Cystitis (Cyclophosphamide - Acrolein).

Antimetabolites

Methotrexate (Folate antagonist), 5-Fluorouracil (Pyrimidine antagonist). S-phase specific.

Microtubule Inhibitors

Vinca Alkaloids (Vincristine): Inhibit polymerization. M-phase specific. Neurotoxicity.
Taxanes (Paclitaxel): Stabilize microtubules.

Antibiotics

Doxorubicin: DNA intercalation. Cardiotoxicity.
Bleomycin: Pulmonary fibrosis.
