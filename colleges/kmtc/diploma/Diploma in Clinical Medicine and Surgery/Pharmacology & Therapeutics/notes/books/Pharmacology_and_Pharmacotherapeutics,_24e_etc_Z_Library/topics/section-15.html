üíâ Vaccines and Antisera

Immunity may be Active (production of antibodies by the individual) or Passive (transfer of antibodies from a donor).

Active Immunisation (Vaccines)

Vaccines are suspensions of dead or attenuated live microorganisms.

Type Examples Comments
Live Attenuated BCG, Oral Polio (Sabin), Measles, Mumps, Rubella (MMR), Varicella, Yellow Fever. Long lasting immunity (cellular + humoral). Avoid in pregnancy and immunocompromised.
Inactivated (Killed) Pertussis (Whole cell), Injectable Polio (Salk), Rabies, Hepatitis A, Cholera, Plague. Humoral immunity mainly. Safe in immunocompromised.
Toxoids Tetanus, Diphtheria. Modified toxins (non-toxic but immunogenic).
Subunit/Recombinant Hepatitis B (HBsAg), HPV, Hib (Polysaccharide). Safe.

Passive Immunisation (Antisera/Immunoglobulins)

Provides immediate but short-lived protection.
Human Ig: Tetanus Ig, Rabies Ig, Anti-D Ig (RhoGAM), Hepatitis B Ig.
Animal Sera (Equine): Tetanus Antitoxin (ATS), Diphtheria Antitoxin (ADS), Anti-Snake Venom (ASV), Anti-Rabies Serum (ARS). Risk of anaphylaxis and serum sickness (Sensitivity test mandatory).

Management of Specific Bites/Stings

1. Snake Bite

Polyvalent Anti-Snake Venom (ASV): Effective against Cobra, Krait, Russell's viper, and Saw-scaled viper.
Sensitivity Test: 0.1 ml diluted serum SC. If negative, give IV infusion.
Note: Neostigmine (+ Atropine) may be useful in cobra bite (neurotoxic) to reverse neuromuscular blockade.

2. Scorpion Sting

Venom causes massive release of catecholamines (Sympathetic storm) $\rightarrow$ Hypertension, pulmonary edema, arrhythmias.
Treatment:
1. Prazosin: Alpha-blocker. Physiological antidote. 0.5 mg orally.
2. Insulin-Glucose infusion: For metabolic support.
3. Anti-Scorpion Venom: Specific antidote.
Avoid: Atropine, Morphine, Steroids (may worsen condition).

3. Rabies

Post-exposure prophylaxis:
1. Wound Care: Wash with soap/water immediately. Apply alcohol/iodine. Do not suture.
2. Passive Immunisation: Rabies Immunoglobulin (RIG). Infiltrate around the wound.
3. Active Immunisation: Anti-Rabies Vaccine (Cell culture vaccines) on days 0, 3, 7, 14, 28.

üß¨ Immunopharmacology

Monoclonal Antibodies (mAbs)

Antibodies produced by a single clone of B cells. Highly specific.
Nomenclature: -omab (mouse), -ximab (chimeric), -zumab (humanised), -umab (fully human).

Drug Target Indication
Infliximab / Adalimumab TNF-$\alpha$ Rheumatoid Arthritis, Crohn's, Psoriasis.
Rituximab CD20 (B cells) Non-Hodgkin's Lymphoma, RA.
Trastuzumab HER2/neu Breast Cancer.
Bevacizumab VEGF Colorectal cancer, Macular degeneration (Intravitreal).
Abciximab GpIIb/IIIa Antiplatelet (PCI).
Omalizumab IgE Bronchial Asthma.
Basiliximab / Daclizumab IL-2 Receptor (CD25) Renal transplant rejection prophylaxis.

Immunosuppressants

Used to prevent organ transplant rejection and treat autoimmune diseases.

1. Calcineurin Inhibitors

Cyclosporine: Inhibits calcineurin $\rightarrow$ blocks IL-2 production $\rightarrow$ inhibits T-cell activation.
Uses: Transplant rejection, Psoriasis, RA.
Toxicity: Nephrotoxicity, Hypertension, Hyperkalemia, Gum hypertrophy, Hirsutism.

Tacrolimus: Macrolide antibiotic. Similar mechanism (binds FKBP). More potent. Toxicity: Nephrotoxicity, Neurotoxicity, Hyperglycemia.

2. Antiproliferative Agents

Azathioprine: Converted to 6-mercaptopurine. Inhibits DNA synthesis.
Mycophenolate Mofetil: Inhibits IMP dehydrogenase (purine synthesis). Selective for lymphocytes.

3. mTOR Inhibitors

Sirolimus (Rapamycin): Inhibits mTOR (mammalian Target of Rapamycin). Blocks T-cell proliferation. Side effects: Hyperlipidemia, Thrombocytopenia. Used in drug-eluting stents.

Immunomodulators (Immunostimulants)

  • Thalidomide: Inhibits TNF-$\alpha$ and angiogenesis.
    Uses: Erythema Nodosum Leprosum (Type 2 Lepra reaction), Multiple Myeloma.
    Adverse Effects: Teratogenicity (Phocomelia), Peripheral neuropathy.
  • Levamisole: Anthelmintic with immunostimulant property. Used in colon cancer (historical) and frequent viral infections.
  • Interferons:
    IFN-$\alpha$: Hepatitis B/C, CML, Kaposi sarcoma.
    IFN-$\beta$: Multiple Sclerosis.
    IFN-$\gamma$: Chronic Granulomatous Disease.
  • BCG: Intravesical instillation for bladder cancer.
