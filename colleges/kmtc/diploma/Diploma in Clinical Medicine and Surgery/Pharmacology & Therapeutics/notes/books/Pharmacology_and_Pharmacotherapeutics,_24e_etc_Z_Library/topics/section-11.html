ü§∞ Pharmacology of Oxytocics and Tocolytics

Oxytocics (Ecbolics) are drugs that stimulate uterine contractions. They are used to induce/augment labour, minimize placental blood loss (PPH), and induce abortion.
Tocolytics are uterine relaxants used to prevent/arrest premature labour.

1. Ergot Alkaloids

Derived from the fungus Claviceps purpurea.
Classification:
1. Amino acid alkaloids: Ergotamine. (Slow onset, potent vasoconstrictor).
2. Amine alkaloids: Ergometrine (Ergonovine). (Rapid onset, minimal vasoconstriction, potent oxytocic).
3. Dihydrogenated alkaloids: Dihydroergotamine (DHE). (Alpha-blockers, reduced vasoconstriction).

Pharmacological Actions

  • Uterus: Increases force, frequency, and tone. Large doses cause sustained tetanic contraction (contracture). Ergometrine is the most selective oxytocic.
  • CVS: Ergotamine causes potent vasoconstriction (can lead to gangrene/St. Anthony's Fire).
Feature Ergometrine Oxytocin
Character of Contraction Tetanic (high dose), Increases basal tone Rhythmic (physiological), Relaxes between contractions
Cervix Tends to contract (unfavourable for labour) Relaxes/Dilates (favourable)
Uses Post Partum Hemorrhage (PPH) only Induction of Labour, Augmentation, PPH
Contraindication 1st/2nd stage of labour, Hypertension, Sepsis Fetal distress, CPD, Uterine scar

Therapeutic Uses

1. Post Partum Hemorrhage (PPH): Ergometrine (0.2 mg IM/IV) or Methylergometrine. Produces firm tetanic contraction, compressing blood vessels.
2. Migraine: Ergotamine (vasoconstrictor action on cranial vessels).

2. Oxytocin

Posterior pituitary hormone. Synthetic preparations are used.
Actions:
1. Uterus: Increases force and frequency of contractions. Fundus contracts, cervix relaxes. Sensitivity increases as pregnancy advances (max at term).
2. Mammary Gland: Contraction of myoepithelium $\rightarrow$ Milk Ejection (Let-down) reflex.

Uses:
1. Induction of Labour: Drug of choice. IV infusion (start low dose, titrate).
2. Uterine Inertia: To augment contractions.
3. PPH: Active management of third stage.
4. Breast engorgement: Intranasal spray to assist milk ejection.

Adverse Effects of Oxytocin

  • Uterine Rupture/Fetal Asphyxia: Due to hyperstimulation.
  • Water Intoxication: Oxytocin has weak ADH-like activity. Large doses with electrolyte-free fluids can cause hyponatremia, convulsions, and coma.
  • Hypotension: Rapid IV bolus causes transient vasodilatation.

3. Prostaglandins (PGs)

Act on specific receptors (EP, FP) to contract uterine smooth muscle and soften/ripen the cervix.

Preparations & Uses:

  • Dinoprostone ($PGE_2$): Gel/Tablet. Used for Cervical Ripening and induction of labour.
  • Carboprost ($15\text{-methyl } PGF_{2\alpha}$): IM. Potent oxytocic. Used for refractory PPH. Side effects: Bronchospasm (avoid in asthma), vomiting, diarrhoea.
  • Misoprostol ($PGE_1$ analogue): Oral/Vaginal/Rectal. Used for medical abortion (with Mifepristone), cervical ripening, and PPH.

4. Uterine Relaxants (Tocolytics)

Used to delay preterm labour (between 24-34 weeks) to allow time for corticosteroid administration (fetal lung maturity) or transfer to NICU.

Classification

Calcium Channel Blockers

Nifedipine: Oral. Blocks Ca influx. Safer and effective. Current drug of choice.

Beta-2 Agonists

Ritodrine, Salbutamol, Terbutaline. Relax myometrium via cAMP. Side effects: Tachycardia, tremors, pulmonary edema, hyperglycemia. Efficacy is comparable to others but side effects are limiting.

COX Inhibitors (NSAIDs)

Indomethacin. Inhibits PG synthesis. Effective. Risk: Premature closure of ductus arteriosus and oligohydramnios. Avoid after 32 weeks.

Oxytocin Antagonists

Atosiban. Competitive antagonist. Effective with fewer cardiovascular side effects. Expensive.

Magnesium Sulfate

Traditionally used. Competes with Calcium. Also neuroprotective for fetus. Weak tocolytic efficacy.
