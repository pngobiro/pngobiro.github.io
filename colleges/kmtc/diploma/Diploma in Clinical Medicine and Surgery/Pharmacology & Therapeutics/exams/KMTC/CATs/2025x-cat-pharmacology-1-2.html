Section 1: Multiple Choice Questions

1.
The following pairs are 1st line antituberculosis drugs except
A) Cycloserine + Isoniazid
B) Ethambutol + Pyrazinamide
C) Rifampicin + Ethambutol
D) Pyrazinamide + Isoniazid
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A) Cycloserine + Isoniazid

Explanation: Cycloserine is a Second-line anti-TB drug used for Multi-Drug Resistant TB (MDR-TB). The standard first-line drugs are Rifampicin, Isoniazid, Pyrazinamide, and Ethambutol. Therefore, the pair containing Cycloserine is the exception.

2.
Which of the following is a 3rd generation cephalosporin
A) Cefadroxil
B) Cephalexin
C) Ceftriaxone
D) Cephalothin
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C) Ceftriaxone

Explanation:
- 1st Gen: Cephalexin, Cefadroxil, Cephalothin.
- 2nd Gen: Cefuroxime, Cefaclor.
- 3rd Gen: Ceftriaxone, Ceftazidime, Cefotaxime.
- 4th Gen: Cefepime.

3.
The following are examples of penicillin except
A) Amoxyclav
B) Flucloxacillin
C) Benzylpenicillin
D) Ciprofloxacin
Show Answer

Correct Answer: D) Ciprofloxacin

Explanation: Ciprofloxacin is a Fluoroquinolone, not a Penicillin. Amoxyclav (Amoxicillin + Clavulanic acid), Flucloxacillin, and Benzylpenicillin (Penicillin G) are all penicillin antibiotics.

4.
The frequency of administration of azithromycin is
A) Once a day
B) Twice a day
C) Three times a day
D) Four times a day
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A) Once a day

Explanation: Azithromycin has a very long half-life (approx 68 hours) and accumulates in tissues, allowing for once-daily dosing, typically for 3 to 5 days.

5.
Rifampicin has no significant drug interaction with one of the following drug
A) Diclofenac
B) Warfarin
C) Protease inhibitor
D) Combined oral contraceptives
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A) Diclofenac

Explanation: Rifampicin is a potent inducer of CYP450 liver enzymes. It significantly accelerates the metabolism of Warfarin (risk of clots), Protease Inhibitors (HIV treatment failure), and Oral Contraceptives (risk of pregnancy). While NSAIDs like Diclofenac are metabolized by the liver, the clinical impact of Rifampicin induction on them is far less critical/significant compared to the potentially life-altering failures of anticoagulation, HIV therapy, or contraception.

6.
Clavulanic acid is combined with amoxicillin because
A) It kills bacteria not killed by amoxicillin
B) It inhibits beta-lactamase enzymes
C) It reduces renal excretion of amoxicillin
D) It has poor antimicrobial activity
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B) It inhibits beta-lactamase enzymes (implied by "It kills bacteria not killed by amoxicillin" in context of mechanism)

Explanation: Clavulanic acid is a Beta-lactamase inhibitor. It has negligible antibacterial activity itself (Option D is technically true but not the reason for combination). By inhibiting the beta-lactamase enzyme produced by resistant bacteria, it protects Amoxicillin from degradation, effectively allowing Amoxicillin to kill bacteria that would otherwise be resistant (Option A describes the outcome, Option B describes the mechanism).

7.
The following are 3rd generation fluoroquinolones except
A) Gemifloxacin
B) Levofloxacin
C) Ofloxacin
D) Moxifloxacin
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C) Ofloxacin

Explanation:
- 2nd Gen: Ciprofloxacin, Ofloxacin.
- 3rd Gen: Levofloxacin.
- 4th Gen: Moxifloxacin, Gemifloxacin.
(Note: Classification varies slightly, but Ofloxacin is consistently older/2nd gen compared to the respiratory quinolones.)

8.
Chloramphenicol is useful in treatment of
A) Typhoid fever
B) TB management
C) Complicated UTI
D) Invasive otitis media
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A) Typhoid fever

Explanation: Historically, Chloramphenicol was the drug of choice for Typhoid Fever (Salmonella typhi). Although resistance has reduced its use, it is still a classic indication in pharmacology exams. It is also used for bacterial meningitis in patients allergic to penicillin.

9.
Cephalosporins
A) Are not effective against salmonella typhi
B) They do not contain beta lactam ring
C) Mode of action is different from that of penicillin
D) Have the ability to penetrate the blood brain barrier
Show Answer

Correct Answer: D) Have the ability to penetrate the blood brain barrier

Explanation: specifically 3rd and 4th generation cephalosporins (like Ceftriaxone) penetrate the BBB well and are used for meningitis.
A is false (Ceftriaxone treats typhoid).
B is false (They are beta-lactams).
C is false (Both inhibit cell wall synthesis).

10.
In treatment of leprosy the following drugs are used as 1st line except
A) Rifampicin
B) Dapsone
C) Ethionamide
D) Clofazimine
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C) Ethionamide

Explanation: The WHO standard Multi-Drug Therapy (MDT) for Leprosy consists of Rifampicin, Dapsone, and Clofazimine. Ethionamide is a second-line drug used for resistant cases.

Section 2: Short Answer Questions (10 Marks)

1.
State five clinical uses of cotrimoxazole (5mks)
Show Answer
  1. Pneumocystis jirovecii Pneumonia (PCP): Prevention and treatment in HIV/AIDS patients.
  2. Urinary Tract Infections (UTIs): Caused by susceptible E. coli.
  3. Respiratory Tract Infections: Bronchitis, Otitis media (in children).
  4. Gastrointestinal Infections: Shigellosis, Typhoid fever (if susceptible).
  5. Toxoplasmosis: Prophylaxis.
  6. Nocardiosis: Drug of choice.

Section 3: Long Answer Questions (20 Marks)

1.
Regarding TB:
a) Name the causative agent.
b) Classify anti-TB drugs and give examples in each class.
c) Outline ten drugs used in treatment of TB in Kenya.
Show Answer

a) Causative Agent:

Mycobacterium tuberculosis (Acid-fast bacillus).

b) Classification of Anti-TB Drugs:

  • First Line Drugs (Most effective, low toxicity):
    • Rifampicin (R)
    • Isoniazid (H)
    • Pyrazinamide (Z)
    • Ethambutol (E)
    • Streptomycin (S)
  • Second Line Drugs (Used for MDR-TB, higher toxicity/lower efficacy):
    • Fluoroquinolones: Levofloxacin, Moxifloxacin.
    • Injectables (Aminoglycosides/Polypeptides): Kanamycin, Amikacin, Capreomycin.
    • Oral Bacteriostatics: Ethionamide, Cycloserine, Para-aminosalicylic acid (PAS).
    • New Drugs: Bedaquiline, Delamanid.

c) Ten Drugs Used in Treatment of TB in Kenya:

  1. Rifampicin
  2. Isoniazid
  3. Pyrazinamide
  4. Ethambutol
  5. Streptomycin
  6. Levofloxacin
  7. Moxifloxacin
  8. Kanamycin (or Amikacin)
  9. Ethionamide
  10. Cycloserine
  11. Bedaquiline
  12. Clofazimine
  13. Linezolid
