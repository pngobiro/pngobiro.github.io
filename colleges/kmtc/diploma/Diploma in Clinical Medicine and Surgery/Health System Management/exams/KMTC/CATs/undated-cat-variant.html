Section A: Multiple Choice Questions (20 Marks)

1.
Who was behind the development of scientific management theory?
A. Henry Fayol
B. Chester Benard
C. Max Webber
D. Fredrick Taylor
Show Answer

Correct Answer: D. Fredrick Taylor

Explanation: Frederick Winslow Taylor is known as the "Father of Scientific Management". Henry Fayol is associated with Administrative Theory (14 Principles), and Max Weber with Bureaucratic Theory.

Note: Some older marking schemes may incorrectly list A, but D is the factually correct answer for Scientific Management.

Section 2: Short Answer Questions (40 Marks)

21.
State the principles of effective Human resource planning (5 marks)
Show Answer

Answer:

  1. The plan should be as detailed as possible.
  2. The plan should not extend too far into the future (accuracy decreases with time).
  3. All alternative courses of action envisaged should be assessed.
  4. Implications of the actions envisaged should be assessed.
  5. Instructions to individuals and departments must be incorporated into the plans.
  6. Plans should be concise and easy to understand.

Section 3: Long Answer Questions (40 Marks)

29.
You have been posted as an Assistant Community Health Officer in charge and you realized employees are resisting change to use of paperless method of reporting in your sub county.

i. Define change (2 marks)

Show Answer

Answer: Change is the process of moving from one system to another. It is also the process of making something different from what it was. Also, any shift in status from an undesirable current status to a desirable future status.

ii. Explain the change process (10 marks)

Show Answer

Answer:

  1. Communication with employees: Speak in person and privately with those who oppose the change. Get to the root of their reasons for the opposition.
  2. Educate the people: Emphasize the goals of change and how the individual or groups will benefit. Clarify information and provide accurate feedback.
  3. Facilitation: This can be done by providing the resources required. The change agent should also maintain a climate of support and confidence.
  4. Involve people affected by change: The change agent should be open to suggestions but clear about the overall purpose and goals. Do not compromise on the intended outcome.
  5. Negotiation: It is also important to discuss the consequences of resistance e.g. compromised patient care or closure of the organization so that the participants can see the importance of change.

iii. State reasons why people may resist change (8 marks)

Show Answer

Answer:

  1. Fear of unknown: This is where the participants want the status quo because they are not sure of what will happen when change has occurred.
  2. Lack of knowledge: When people do not know what is expected of them either during the change process or after change has occurred.
  3. Parochial self-interests: This is where people resist change because of personal interest is expense of organizational interests. May be they fear that they might lose their position if change occurs.
  4. Lack of information: About what the change entails and the implication of change.
  5. Mistrust: This occurs especially when people don't trust the leaders. They might assume that their leaders could be having a hidden agenda e.g. layoff of employees.
30.
You have been posted as an Assistant Community Health officer to community A and you have realized there is a conflict between the Lead CHV and other team members.

i. Outline the seven conflict resolution methods (14 marks)

Show Answer

Answer:

  1. Avoiding/Avoidance: Withdrawing from or ignoring the conflict.
  2. Accommodating: Putting the other party's needs above one's own to keep peace.
  3. Competing: Pursuing one's own concerns at the expense of others (Win-Lose).
  4. Compromising: Finding a middle ground where both parties give up something.
  5. Negotiation: Discussing to reach a mutually acceptable agreement.
  6. Collaborating: Working together to find a solution that satisfies both parties completely (Win-Win).
  7. Confronting: Addressing the issue directly to resolve it.

ii. State six causes of conflict (6 marks)

Show Answer

Answer:

  1. Competition for resources (e.g., money, skilled manpower).
  2. Inter group rivalry for rewards.
  3. Take difficulties (Task interdependence).
  4. Skill differences.
  5. Pressure to avoid failures.
  6. Unworkable organization structure.
  7. Differences in information, values, beliefs, and interest.
