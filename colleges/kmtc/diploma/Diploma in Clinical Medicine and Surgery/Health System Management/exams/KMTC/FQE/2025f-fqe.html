1.
In the concept of disaster management, which statement describes a "hazard"?
A. A situation generated by the real or eminent occurrence of an event that requires immediate attention
B. An event or series of events representing a critical threat to the health, safety wellbeing or a community.
C. A natural or human caused event to occur with negative consequences
D. A natural or human caused event which cause intensive negative impacts on people, services or environment exceeding the community's capacity to respond.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C. A natural or human caused event to occur with negative consequences

Explanation:

  • A hazard is a potential source of harm or an event with the potential to cause loss. It is the "threat" before the damage occurs.
  • Option B describes a crisis or emergency situation.
  • Option D describes a disaster (exceeding the community's capacity to respond).
2.
The correct sequence of the monitoring and evaluation conceptual framework is...
A. Process, input, output, impact, outcome
B. Output, input, process, outcome, impact
C. Output, input, outcome, process, impact
D. Input, process, output, outcome, impact
Show Answer

Correct Answer: D. Input, process, output, outcome, impact

Explanation: This is the standard logical framework (LogFrame) sequence:

  • Input: Resources used (e.g., money, staff).
  • Process: Activities performed (e.g., training, treating).
  • Output: Direct products (e.g., number of patients treated).
  • Outcome: Short/medium-term effects (e.g., improved health knowledge).
  • Impact: Long-term changes (e.g., reduced mortality rate).

7.
Quality management focuses on the means and processes of ensuring that the products or services offered in a facility are consistent. Identify the correctly matched dimension of quality.
A. Efficiency of care - deals with whether the kind of treatment given leads to the desired outcome
B. Technical competence - ability of the healthcare system to provide best returns for the resources provided
C. Effectiveness of care - where treatment or care provided is appropriate for the setting it provided in
D. Safety - where the patient receives the complete range of health services that he or she needs without cessation or interruption
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C. Effectiveness of care - where treatment or care provided is appropriate for the setting it provided in

Explanation: This is a tricky matching question based on elimination.

  • A is incorrect: "Leading to desired outcome" is Effectiveness. Efficiency is about cost/resource use.
  • B is incorrect: "Best returns for resources" is Efficiency. Technical competence is skill/accuracy.
  • D is incorrect: "Without cessation" describes Continuity of care, not Safety. Safety is doing no harm.
  • C is the best fit: Effectiveness often relates to doing the right thing (evidence-based) in the right setting.

B. Medical malpractice

Show Answer

Correct Answer: C. Serve a verbal warning (or Show Cause Letter)

A Disaster is an occurrence disrupting the normal conditions of existence and causing a level of suffering that exceeds the capacity by the affected community to cope with. Outline any five guiding principles of implementation for disaster management. (5 marks)
Show Answer

Answer:

  1. Humanity: Human suffering must be addressed wherever it is found; protecting life and health is the priority.
  2. Neutrality: Humanitarian actors must not take sides in hostilities or engage in controversies of a political, racial, or religious nature.
  3. Impartiality: Action must be based on need alone, giving priority to the most urgent cases of distress without discrimination.
  4. Independence: Humanitarian action must be autonomous from political, economic, or military objectives.
  5. Community Participation: Involving the affected community in planning and implementation to ensure sustainability and appropriateness.

Section 3: Long Answer Question (20 Marks)

49.
John is a Clinical officer in charge of Bura health center, Tana River County. He is expected to recruit new employees for the ministry of health in the County. Enumerate the government recommended steps he is required to use in the recruitment process. (20 marks)
Show Answer

Detailed Answer:

The recruitment process in the public sector generally follows a structured procedure to ensure fairness, transparency, and meritocracy. The steps include:

  1. Job Analysis:
    • Identify the need for the new employee (vacancy).
    • Determine the duties, responsibilities, and working conditions (Job Description).
    • Determine the qualifications, skills, and experience required (Job Specification).
  2. Requisition:
    • Formal request to the County Public Service Board (CPSB) or relevant hiring authority to fill the position, confirming budget availability.
  3. Advertisement:
    • Publicizing the vacancy internally and externally (newspapers, websites) to attract a pool of qualified candidates.
    • The ad must state the job title, grade, requirements, and deadline.
  4. Receiving Applications:
    • Collecting applications physically or digitally up to the set deadline.
  5. Shortlisting:
    • Screening applications against the set criteria (long-listing then short-listing).
    • Selecting candidates who meet the mandatory requirements for interview.
  6. Inviting Candidates for Interview:
    • Communicating to shortlisted candidates about the date, time, and venue of the interview (usually 7 days' notice).
  7. Selection Interview:
    • conducting the interviews using a panel.
    • Using tools like oral questions, written tests, or practicals.
    • Scoring candidates based on a marking scheme.
  8. Background Checks/Vetting:
    • Verifying academic certificates (KNEC/CUE).
    • Checking compliance with Chapter 6 of the Constitution (HELB, KRA, EACC, CID/Police Clearance).
  9. Appointment:
    • The successful candidate is offered the job formally via a Letter of Appointment.
    • The candidate accepts the offer in writing.
  10. Induction and Deployment:
    • Orienting the new employee to the organization, their role, and station.
    • Posting them to the specific duty station (Bura Health Center).

Key Concept: In the context of Counties, the County Public Service Board (CPSB) acts as the primary recruiting agency, but the user department (Health) defines the technical needs.
