Section 1: Multiple Choice Questions (40 Marks)

1.
Concerning devolution of health services in Kenya, which one of the following is not a functional role at the National Government?
A. Promotion of health care
B. Health policy development
C. Financing of health care services
D. Ensuring quality assurance and standards
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A. Promotion of health care

Explanation: Under the Fourth Schedule of the Constitution of Kenya 2010, the "promotion of primary health care" is explicitly a function of the County Government. The National Government retains roles in Health Policy (B), National Referral Facilities, Capacity Building, and Technical Assistance (Quality Assurance and Standards - D).

Note on C: While counties budget for their health services, the bulk of financing comes from the equitable share provided by the National Treasury, but the direct management/financing of service delivery is a county role.

Section 2: Short Answer Questions (40 Marks)

41.
Outline the procedure that would be followed for registering a medico-legal case (5 marks)
Show Answer

Answer:

  1. Reception and Triage: Receive the patient and provide immediate emergency care if necessary to save life.
  2. Registration: Register the patient in the standard outpatient/inpatient register and open a patient file. Mark the file clearly as "Medico-Legal".
  3. Consent: Obtain informed written consent from the patient (or guardian) for examination and collection of evidence.
  4. Examination and Documentation: Conduct a thorough physical examination. Document all findings (injuries, size, type, location) clearly in the patient notes and on the P3 form (if provided by police).
  5. Chain of Custody: Collect necessary specimens/samples, ensuring strict chain of custody procedures are followed (labelling, sealing, handing over).
  6. Reporting: Fill the P3 form accurately and sign. Keep a copy in the hospital records.
  7. Secure Records: Store the medico-legal file in a secure, lockable cabinet to maintain confidentiality and integrity of evidence.
42.
Explain five (5) power basis of an effective leader. (5 marks)
Show Answer

Answer:

  1. Legitimate Power: Power derived from the leader's formal position or title within the organization's hierarchy.
  2. Reward Power: The ability to provide rewards (promotions, bonuses, praise) to influence employee behavior.
  3. Coercive Power: The ability to punish or withhold rewards (disciplinary action, demotion) to ensure compliance.
  4. Expert Power: Power based on the leader's special knowledge, skills, or expertise that others need.
  5. Referent Power: Power derived from the leader's charisma and the admiration/respect others have for them.

Section 3: Long Answer Question (20 Marks)

49.

As a practicing clinical officer, you want to venture into entrepreneurship field besides your normal clinical practice. In the process of beginning your business, you are optimistic of its success and you opt to work on a business plan.

i. Explain at least five (5) purposes/benefits of writing your business plan (8 marks)

ii. Discuss the ten (10) successful traits of entrepreneur (12 marks)

Show Answer

Part i: Purposes/Benefits of a Business Plan (8 marks)

  1. Roadmap/Guidance: It serves as a blueprint, outlining the direction the business will take and the milestones to achieve.
  2. Feasibility Analysis: It helps determine if the business idea is viable and profitable before investing money.
  3. Attracting Investors/Funding: Banks and investors require a solid business plan to evaluate risk before lending capital.
  4. Operational Guide: It details organizational structure, roles, and operational procedures to ensure smooth running.
  5. Marketing Strategy: It defines the target market and competition, helping to strategize how to capture market share.
  6. Risk Management: It helps identify potential risks and challenges early, allowing for contingency planning.

Part ii: Successful Traits of an Entrepreneur (12 marks)

  1. Passion: Deep enthusiasm for their work keeps them motivated through difficult times.
  2. Resilience/Perseverance: The ability to recover quickly from failure and keep going despite setbacks.
  3. Self-Confidence: Belief in their own ability to achieve goals and overcome challenges.
  4. Visionary: Ability to see the big picture and spot opportunities where others see problems.
  5. Risk-Taking: Willingness to take calculated risks to achieve higher returns.
  6. Flexibility/Adaptability: Ability to change course when necessary in response to market changes.
  7. Decisiveness: Ability to make decisions quickly and firmly, even under pressure.
  8. Financial Management Skills: Understanding how to manage money, cash flow, and resources effectively.
  9. Leadership Skills: Ability to inspire, motivate, and manage a team towards a common goal.
  10. Networking Skills: Building strong relationships with customers, suppliers, and mentors.
