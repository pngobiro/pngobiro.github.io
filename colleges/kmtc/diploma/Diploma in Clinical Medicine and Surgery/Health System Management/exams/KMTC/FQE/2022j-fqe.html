Section 1: Multiple Choice Questions (40 Marks)

1.
Modern Management approaches include;
a) Management Information Systems theory, Management Science theory and Systems theory.
b) Management Science, Operations management and Management Information system theory.
c) The Systems theory, Contingency theory and Total Quality management.
d) The Systems theory, Theory X and Y and Maslow's Needs theory.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) The Systems theory, Contingency theory and Total Quality management.

Explanation: Modern management theories (developed post-1950s) focus on the complexity of organizations. The three pillars are Systems Theory (viewing org as parts), Contingency Theory (situational management), and TQM (continuous improvement). Options mentioning Theory X/Y or Maslow refer to Behavioral/Human Relations theories, which are transitional, not "Modern" in this specific classification.

2.
The term that best describes the number of subordinates directly controlled by a manager is;
a) Division of labour
b) Departmentalization
c) Span of management
d) Scalar chain
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Span of management

Explanation: Span of management (or span of control) refers to the number of people reporting directly to a single manager.
Other terms:

  • Division of labour: Breaking tasks into smaller jobs.
  • Departmentalization: Grouping jobs together.
  • Scalar chain: The line of authority from top to bottom.

3.
A supply chain is essentially a sequence of linked;
a) Customers and prospects
b) Suppliers and customers
c) Supplier and manufacturer
d) Warehousing and wholesaling units
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Suppliers and customers

Explanation: A supply chain is the entire network of entities, directly or indirectly interlinked and interdependent in serving the same consumer or customer. It links suppliers (upstream) to customers (downstream).

4.

d) S3 and S11

Correct Answer: a) S12 and S13

Explanation:

6.
The correct order of the procurement process by quotation is;
a) Market survey, raising quotation, receive, open and evaluate the quotation, award the winner
b) Raising quotation, market survey, receive and evaluate the quotations, ward the winner
c) Raising quotation, market survey, receive, open and evaluate the quotations, award the winner
d) Raising quotation, receive, open and evaluate the quotation, market survey, award the winner.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Raising quotation, market survey, receive, open and evaluate the quotations, award the winner

Explanation:

  1. Raising/Issuing Quotation: Requesting bids.
  2. Market Survey: Determining prevailing market prices (often done parallel or before to set estimates, but in this sequence, it validates the quotes).
  3. Receive/Open/Evaluate: Analyzing the bids.
  4. Award: Giving the contract.
Note: Often Market Survey comes first to establish budget, but in the specific context of "Quotation Process" steps, Option C represents the logical flow of handling the documents.

Section 3: Long Answer Question (20 Marks)

49.
Human resource is a key asset in every institution and its management comprise of different policies and processes.

a) Enumerate advantages and disadvantages of external recruitment. (10 Marks)

Show Answer

Advantages:

  1. New Ideas/Innovation: Brings fresh perspectives and new blood into the organization.
  2. Wider Pool of Candidates: Access to a larger talent selection, increasing chances of finding the best fit.
  3. Reduced Training Costs: Can hire already skilled/experienced professionals (headhunting).
  4. Minimizes Internal Politics: Avoids jealousy that might arise from promoting one internal staff member over another.
  5. Change Management: Easier to drive culture change with new people who aren't set in old ways.

Disadvantages:

  1. Higher Costs: Advertising, agency fees, and interviewing processes are expensive.
  2. Time Consuming: The process takes longer (advertising to onboarding) compared to internal promotion.
  3. Orientation/Onboarding: New hires need time to adapt to the culture and systems (learning curve).
  4. Demoralizes Internal Staff: Current employees may feel undervalued if they are bypassed for promotions.
  5. Risk of Bad Fit: The new hire may have the skills but fail to fit into the organizational culture.

b) Outline steps involved in staffing process (10 Marks)

Show Answer
  1. Human Resource Planning: Estimating the future manpower needs of the organization.
  2. Job Analysis: Determining the job description (tasks) and job specification (skills needed).
  3. Recruitment: Attracting a pool of qualified candidates (advertising vacancies).
  4. Selection: Screening, testing, and interviewing to choose the best candidate.
  5. Placement: Putting the selected person into the specific job position.
  6. Orientation and Induction: Introducing the new employee to the organization and their role.
  7. Training and Development: enhancing skills to meet job requirements.
  8. Performance Appraisal: Evaluating how well the employee is performing.
  9. Compensation/Remuneration: Determining fair pay and benefits.
  10. Separation/Exit: Managing retirement, resignation, or termination.
