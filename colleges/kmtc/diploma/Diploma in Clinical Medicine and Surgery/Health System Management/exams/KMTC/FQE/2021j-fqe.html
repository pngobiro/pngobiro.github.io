1.
The right to command or exact compliance in the organization:
a) Power
b) Authority
c) Influence
d) Congruence
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Authority

Explanation: Authority is the formal, legitimate right of a manager to issue orders and expect compliance. Power is the ability to influence behavior (which may not be formal), while influence is the effect itself.

Section 2: Short Answer Questions (40 Marks)

1.
Explain the different types of plans in project management (10 marks)
Show Answer

Answer:

Project management involves various hierarchy of plans:

  1. Strategic Plans: Long-term plans (3-5 years) that define the overall project goals and how they align with the organization's mission.
  2. Tactical Plans: Medium-term plans that break down strategic goals into specific objectives for departments or phases.
  3. Operational Plans: Short-term, detailed plans (weekly/monthly) for day-to-day activities and resource usage.
  4. Contingency Plans: "Plan B" designed to address potential risks or emergencies identified during risk assessment.
  5. Project Management Plan (PMP): The master document integrating subsidiary plans like:
    • Scope Management Plan
    • Cost/Budget Plan
    • Schedule/Time Plan
    • Quality Management Plan
2.
Discuss the different types and stages of disasters (10 marks)
Show Answer

Types of Disasters:

  1. Natural Disasters:
    • Geophysical: Earthquakes, Volcanoes.
    • Hydro-meteorological: Floods, Droughts, Cyclones.
    • Biological: Epidemics, Pandemics.
  2. Man-Made (Human Induced) Disasters:
    • Technological: Industrial accidents, Transport crashes.
    • Conflict/Complex: War, Terrorism, Civil unrest.

Stages of Disaster Management Cycle:

  1. Mitigation: Measures to reduce the impact or likelihood (e.g., building codes).
  2. Preparedness: Planning, training, and stockpiling before impact (e.g., early warning systems).
  3. Response: Immediate action during/after impact to save lives (e.g., Search & Rescue).
  4. Recovery: Restoring normalcy and rebuilding (Rehabilitation and Reconstruction).

4.
The responsibility of a head of a department is to appraise his staff. Discuss what you understand by the term staff performance appraisal (10 marks)
Show Answer

Answer:

Definition: Performance appraisal is the systematic, periodic, and impartial rating of an employee's excellence in matters pertaining to their present job and their potential for a better job.

Key Aspects/Process:

  1. Setting Standards: Establishing clear, measurable goals (SMART) at the beginning of the period.
  2. Measuring Performance: Monitoring actual work against these standards throughout the period.
  3. Comparing: Analyzing the gap between expected and actual performance.
  4. Feedback: Discussing the results with the employee to highlight strengths and weaknesses.
  5. Action: Making decisions on rewards (promotion/bonus), training needs, or corrective action.

Purpose: It serves as a basis for administrative decisions (pay/promotion) and developmental purposes (coaching/training).

1.
You are the in-charge of the health records and information section at the Nyandarua County Referral Hospital. Two members of staff in the section have just retired and the management has asked you to fill their vacant positions. State and explain how you will fill these two positions (10 marks)
Show Answer

Answer: The Staffing Process

  1. Job Analysis: Determine the specific duties of the vacant posts and the qualifications required (update the Job Description and Specification).
  2. Requisition: Formally request the Hospital Management/County Public Service Board to authorize the filling of the vacancies.
  3. Recruitment:
    • Internal: Advertise on notice boards to see if existing lower-cadre staff qualify for promotion.
    • External: If no internal candidates, advertise in media to attract outside talent.
  4. Selection:
    • Shortlisting: Screen applications against requirements.
    • Interviewing: Conduct interviews to assess skills and fit.
  5. Appointment: Issue offer letters to the successful candidates.
  6. Induction and Orientation: Introduce the new staff to the department, hospital policies, and their specific roles.
  7. Deployment: Assign them to their specific workstations within the records department.
2.
The Primary focus of disaster management is to mitigate against those disasters which are inevitable. Explain the importance of disaster mitigation. (10 marks)
Show Answer

Answer:

Disaster mitigation involves structural and non-structural measures undertaken to limit the adverse impact of natural hazards, environmental degradation, and technological hazards.

Importance:

  1. Saves Lives: By strengthening infrastructure (e.g., earthquake-resistant buildings), fewer people are killed during the event.
  2. Reduces Economic Losses: Protecting assets prevents the destruction of property and livelihoods, saving billions in potential damage.
  3. Ensures Business/Service Continuity: Hospitals and essential services can continue to operate if mitigated against risks (e.g., backup generators, flood walls).
  4. Cost-Effective: Investing in mitigation (prevention) is far cheaper than the cost of relief and reconstruction after a disaster ($1 spent on mitigation saves $4-$7 in response).
  5. Reduces Vulnerability: Helps communities bounce back faster (resilience) rather than being trapped in a cycle of poverty following disasters.
