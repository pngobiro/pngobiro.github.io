Hormones

üí¨ Overview

A hormone is a chemical that is made by specialist cells, usually within an endocrine gland, and it is released into the bloodstream to send a message to another part of the body. It is often referred to as a chemical messenger.

Hormones are found in all multicellular organisms and their role is to provide an internal communication system between cells located in distant parts of the body.

üåê

The Endocrine System

The complex interplay between the glands, hormones and other target organs is referred to as the endocrine system. Hormones affect many physiological activities including growth, metabolism, appetite, puberty and fertility.

02

üß™ Chemical Structure

The hormones of the human body can be structurally divided into three major groups:

1. Amine Hormones

Derived from the modification of amino acids (Tryptophan or Tyrosine).

Examples: Melatonin, Epinephrine, Norepinephrine, Dopamine, Thyroid hormones.

2. Peptide Hormones

Consist of multiple amino acids linked to form a chain. Can be short chains or long polypeptides.

Examples: Oxytocin, Insulin, Growth Hormone.

3. Steroid Hormones

Derived from the lipid cholesterol. Hydrophobic (not soluble in water), requiring transport proteins in blood.

Examples: Testosterone, Estrogens, Cortisol, Aldosterone.

‚è±Ô∏è

Half-Life

Binding to transport proteins extends the half-life of steroid hormones. For example, Cortisol (lipid-derived) has a half-life of 60-90 minutes, while Epinephrine (amino acid-derived) has a half-life of approximately one minute.

03

üè≠ Endocrine Glands

The endocrine system is a network of glands that make hormones. Major glands include:

‚ñ∂ Hypothalamus

Connects the endocrine system with the nervous system. Its main job is to tell the pituitary gland to start or stop making hormones. It controls body temperature, regulates emotions, hunger, thirst, sleep, and moods.

‚ñ∂ Pituitary Gland (Master Gland)

Uses information from the brain to tell other glands what to do. Makes growth hormone, prolactin, and luteinizing hormone.

‚ñ∂ Pineal Gland

Makes a chemical called melatonin that helps the body get ready to go to sleep.

‚ñ∂ Thyroid Gland

Makes thyroid hormone, which controls metabolism. Issues include Hypothyroidism (slows down) and Hyperthyroidism (speeds up).

‚ñ∂ Parathyroid

Set of four small glands behind the thyroid. They play a role in bone health and control levels of calcium and phosphorus.

‚ñ∂ Adrenals

Make the "fight or flight" hormone adrenaline (epinephrine) and corticosteroids. Affect metabolism and sexual function.

‚ñ∂ Pancreas

Makes digestive enzymes and hormones insulin and glucagon to ensure the right amount of sugar in the bloodstream.

‚ñ∂ Ovaries & Testes

Ovaries: Make estrogen and progesterone (breast development, menstrual cycle, pregnancy).

Testes: Make testosterone (facial/body hair, penis growth, sperm production).
