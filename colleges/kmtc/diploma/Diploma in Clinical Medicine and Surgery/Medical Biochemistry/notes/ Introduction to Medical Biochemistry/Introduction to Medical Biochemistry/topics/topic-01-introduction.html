üß™ What Are Biomolecules?

Biomolecules are the primary building blocks of every living organism. Together, they promote different biological processes, which are necessary for life. They vary in structures and sizes. Biomolecules primarily consist of carbon, hydrogen and oxygen.

The living systems synthesize four primary types of biomolecules within the body. They are:

  • Carbohydrates
  • Proteins
  • Fats/Lipids
  • Nucleic Acids (DNA & RNA)

With biomolecules definition, it is also important to know about the major types of biomolecules are basically polymers of simple elements. For example:

  • Amino acids form the basis for proteins.
  • Phosphate, sugar, and nitrogen for nucleic acids.
  • Fatty acids and glycerol for lipids /Fats
  • Simple sugar for carbohydrates.
üîó

Specialized Molecules

Some biomolecules can also be combined to get specialized molecules. For example:

  • Protein and carbohydrate in glycoproteins
  • Protein and lipid in lipoproteins
  • Lipid and carbohydrate in glycolipids

üî¨ BIOCHEMISTRY

Definition: Biochemistry is the study of the structure and function of biological biomolecules such as proteins, nucleic acids, carbohydrates, lipids among others.

Practitioners of biochemistry study the biochemical reactions that occur at the molecular level within living organisms. In medical biochemistry (also known as molecular biology), biochemical techniques are applied to human health and disease.

Application: Much of biochemical study deals with the structures, functions and interactions of biological macromolecules ‚Äî large molecules (such as proteins) which provide the structure of cells and perform many of the functions associated with life.

Medicine is just one area that has benefited from biochemistry. The discipline has made vital contributions to the fields of cell biology, physiology, pathology, pharmacology, microbiology, immunology, nutrition, forensic medicine, and toxicology, as well as the fields of inflammation, cell injury, and cancer.
