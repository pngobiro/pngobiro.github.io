Electrolytes & Biomolecules

1.
Intracellular Ca2+:
A) Regulates secretions of parathyroid hormone and calcitonin
B) Is important in cell coagulation pathways as a cofactor
C) Is involved in the maintenance of body pH
D) Has a role in proper bone formation
E) Has no role in neurotransmitter release from neurons
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A) Regulates secretions of parathyroid hormone and calcitonin

Explanation: Intracellular calcium levels are the primary regulator of PTH secretion. High intracellular calcium in parathyroid cells inhibits PTH secretion (a unique mechanism). Low calcium stimulates it. Conversely, high calcium stimulates Calcitonin release from thyroid C-cells.

Why other options are less appropriate for "Intracellular":

  • B & D: Coagulation (Factor IV) and Bone Formation (Hydroxyapatite) are primary functions of extracellular calcium.
  • E: Incorrect. Intracellular calcium influx is the direct trigger for neurotransmitter vesicle fusion and release (exocytosis).
2.
Sodium:
A) Assists with regulation of acid-base balance by combining with chloride or bicarbonate ions
B) Accounts for nearly 50% of plasma osmolality hence central in maintenance of normal water distribution and osmotic pressure
C) Reabsorption in the kidneys is regulated by Aldosterone
D) Hypernatremia occurs with excessive loss of water or dehydration
E) All of the above is true
Show Answer

Correct Answer: E) All of the above is true

Explanation: Sodium is the major extracellular cation. It dictates plasma osmolality/volume, is regulated by Aldosterone (which increases reabsorption), and is involved in acid-base balance (Na+/H+ exchange). Hypernatremia is typically caused by free water loss.

3.
The following elements are found in living organisms in trace amounts:
A) Iron
B) Cobalt
C) Potassium
D) Magnesium
E) Iodine
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B) Cobalt (and A, E)

Explanation: Iron, Cobalt, and Iodine are all Trace Elements (Microminerals). Potassium and Magnesium are Macro Elements (required in large amounts). Cobalt is specifically required as a component of Vitamin B12 (Cobalamin).

4.
The following are the major classes of biomolecules:
A) Carbohydrates
B) Nucleic acids
C) Proteins
D) Lipids
E) All of the above
Show Answer

Correct Answer: E) All of the above

Explanation: The four fundamental macromolecules of life are Carbohydrates, Lipids, Proteins, and Nucleic Acids.

5.
Water:
A) Influences non-covalent interactions among biomolecules, through hydrogen bonding
B) Is a reactant or a product in many metabolic reactions because it is an excellent electrophile
C) Is the predominant chemical component of living organisms
D) Regulation of water balance depends upon hypothalamic mechanisms that control thirst
E) All of the above are true
Show Answer

Correct Answer: E) All of the above are true

Explanation: Water is the solvent of life, stabilizing structures via hydrophobic effects/H-bonding, participating in hydrolysis/condensation reactions, making up ~60% of body weight, and its intake is regulated by the hypothalamus (thirst center/ADH).

Physiology & Enzymes

6.
Abnormal loss of electrolytes results from:
A) Vomiting
B) Diarrhea
C) Profuse sweating
D) Moderate to severe burns
E) All of the above
Show Answer

Correct Answer: E) All of the above

Explanation: Electrolyte imbalances can result from GI losses (vomiting - H+/Cl-; diarrhea - HCO3-/K+), Skin losses (sweating - Na+/Cl-; burns - plasma exudate containing all electrolytes).

7.
About Potassium:
A) Is the major intracellular cation
B) The K+ filtered by the glomeruli is almost all reabsorbed in the PCT of the nephron
C) It promotes conduction and transmission of nerve impulses
D) Levels of more than 10mmol/L are lethal
E) All of the above are true
Show Answer

Correct Answer: E) All of the above are true

Explanation: Potassium is essential for resting membrane potential (nerve conduction). It is mostly intracellular (140 mM vs 4 mM in blood). Hyperkalemia (>7-8 mM, certainly >10 mM) causes fatal cardiac arrhythmias.

8.
Enzymes:
A) Are classified on the basis of the reactions they catalyze
B) Are highly efficient, generally enhancing the rates of chemical reactions by factors of 1000 to 1,000,000 or more
C) The active site of an enzyme contains amino acid chains that create a three-dimensional surface complementary to the substrate
D) Some enzymes require metal ions such as Ca2+, K+, Mg2+, Fe2+, Cu2+, Zn2+, Mn2+, Co2+ for their activity
E) Are highly specific for the type of reaction they catalyze as well as the substrate to which they bind
Show Answer

Correct Answer: E) Are highly specific (Note: All options are True)

Explanation: All the statements listed describe fundamental properties of enzymes.

  • A: IUBMB classification.
  • B: Catalytic power.
  • C: Active site structure.
  • D: Cofactors/Metalloenzymes.
  • E: Specificity (often considered the most defining characteristic).

9.
About vitamins:
A) Vitamin E is an important antioxidant in the body
B) Vitamin K is obtained largely from gut microorganisms
C) Vitamin B12 is a constituent of FAD and FMN coenzymes
D) Deficiency of thiamine leads to pellagra
E) Deficiency of folic acid leads to anaemia and neural tube defects in developing embryos
Show Answer

Correct Answer: E) Deficiency of folic acid leads to anaemia and neural tube defects in developing embryos

Explanation:

  • True: Folic acid (B9) is crucial for DNA synthesis; deficiency causes Megaloblastic Anemia and Neural Tube Defects (Spina Bifida).
  • True: Vit E is an antioxidant.
  • True: Vit K is synthesized by gut flora.
  • False: FAD/FMN come from Riboflavin (B2), not B12.
  • False: Thiamine (B1) deficiency causes Beriberi. Niacin (B3) deficiency causes Pellagra.

10.
The basic structure of an amino acid consists of an alpha carbon attached to:
A) A hydrogen
B) A carboxyl group
C) An amino group
D) R-group which is a side chain containing C, H, S, O and N atoms in various combinations and structural arrangements
E) All of the above
Show Answer

Correct Answer: E) All of the above

Explanation: The general structure of an amino acid is a central alpha-carbon bonded to four groups: an Amino group (-NH2), a Carboxyl group (-COOH), a Hydrogen atom, and a variable Side Chain (R-group).

Amino Acids & Vitamins

11.
Essential amino acids that must be obtained from the diet include:
A) Histidine
B) Phenylalanine
C) Glutamate
D) Glycine
E) Tryptophan
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B) Phenylalanine (Also A and E)

Explanation: Essential amino acids (cannot be synthesized by the body) include: Phenylalanine, Valine, Threonine, Tryptophan, Isoleucine, Methionine, Histidine, Arginine, Leucine, Lysine (Mnemonic: PVT TIM HALL). Glutamate and Glycine are non-essential.

12.
Amino acids:
A) Form the monomer units from which peptides and long polypeptide chains are synthesized in the body
B) Biological systems only synthesize and use L-amino acids
C) Form the monomer units for peptides that function as hormones, hormone releasing factors, neurotransmitters in the neuroendocrine system of the body
D) Tyrosine is used in the synthesis of thyroid hormones
E) Phenylalanine is a precursor of epinephrine, norepinephrine
Show Answer

Correct Answer: D) Tyrosine is used in the synthesis of thyroid hormones (Note: A, B, C are also true)

Explanation:

  • True: Tyrosine residues are iodinated to form T3 and T4 (Thyroid hormones).
  • True: Amino acids are monomers of proteins (A).
  • True: Mammalian proteins use L-amino acids (B).
  • Correction for E: Tyrosine is the direct precursor of catecholamines (Epinephrine). Phenylalanine must first be converted to Tyrosine.

13.
About vitamin A:
A) The precursors are called carotenes
B) The stereoisomer involved in visual process is 11-cis Retinal
C) Retinoic acid isomer is active as an antioxidant
D) Deficiency causes scurvy
E) Retinol isomer has a role in reproduction
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B) The stereoisomer involved in visual process is 11-cis Retinal

Explanation: The visual cycle (Wald's cycle) relies on the light-induced isomerization of 11-cis Retinal to all-trans Retinal within the Rhodopsin molecule.
Other Options:

  • A: True, beta-carotene is a precursor.
  • D: False, Vitamin C deficiency causes Scurvy. Vit A deficiency causes Night Blindness/Xerophthalmia.
  • E: True, Retinol is essential for reproduction.
