Definitions

Law is a system of rules which a particular country or community recognizes as regulating the actions of its members and which it may enforce by the imposition of penalties.

Health Law is a branch of law dealing with various aspects of health care, including the practices of caregivers and the rights of patients.

Economic and Social Rights

Every person has the right to:

  • The highest attainable standard of health, which includes the right to health care services, including reproductive health care.
  • Accessible and adequate housing, and to reasonable standards of sanitation.
  • Be free from hunger, and to have adequate food of acceptable quality.
  • Clean and safe water in adequate quantities.
  • Social security.
  • Education.

Emergency Treatment

A person shall not be denied emergency medical treatment.

üíä Public Health Act on Food, Drugs and Chemicals

This Act provides rules for the placing on the market of food, drugs for man and animal and chemical substances. It establishes the Public Health (Standards) Board and makes provisions for the control of the quality and safety of these items.

Key Provisions:

  • Prohibitions: The Act prohibits the labelling, packaging, sale, treatment and processing of food that is presented to the public in a false or deceptive manner or that does not meet a prescribed standard.
  • Sanitation: Prohibits handling of food in unsanitary conditions.
  • Chemical Disposal: Disposal of any chemical substance in a manner likely to cause contamination of food or water, or liable to be injurious to health, is declared an offence.
  • Authorized Officers: Defines powers to inspect any animal intended for slaughter and to seize and examine any meat considered unfit for consumption.

Application for a Practising Licence

A registered or enrolled nurse who wishes to practise must apply to the Council. A licence permits the licensee to:

  • Render medical or nursing care services in a government health institution.
  • Render medical or nursing care services in a non-governmental health institution.
  • Engage in locum practice.
  • Engage in full-time private practice in a clinic or a medical centre.

Private Practice Regulations

Terms and Conditions

The Council specifies terms and conditions in every licence. A nurse shall engage in the practice for which they are qualified and which is specified in the licence.

Inspection Reports

No nurse shall be issued with a licence to open and operate a private clinic unless they have produced an inspection report in the prescribed form to the satisfaction of the Council.

üö´

Unlicenced Nurses

A registered or enrolled nurse who undertakes or offers medical or nursing care services without a valid licence commits professional malpractice.

Employment of Assistants

A licensee may employ the services of an assistant who has undergone approved training. If the assistant does not have medical training, the licensee must ensure the assistant does not undertake any form of medical treatment.

Fees

The Council may regulate the fees charged for consultations, visits, treatments and referrals in private clinics managed by nurses and investigate complaints relating to fees.

üë∂ Children and Young Person's Act

Key provisions regarding the rights and welfare of children:

Survival & Best Interests

Every child has an inherent right to life. In all actions concerning children, the best interests of the child shall be a primary consideration.

Non-discrimination

No child shall be subjected to discrimination on grounds of origin, sex, religion, disability, status, etc.

Right to Parental Care

A child has a right to live with and be cared for by parents. Separation should only occur if in the child's best interest.

Right to Education

Entitled to education (responsibility of Government and parents) and free basic compulsory education.

Right to Health Care

Right to health and medical care, the provision of which shall be the responsibility of parents and the Government.

Protection from Drugs

Entitled to protection from use of narcotics, alcohol, tobacco, and psychotropic drugs.

üèòÔ∏è Succession Act

This act is the general law of succession in the country, spelling out procedures for estate distribution upon death.

Feature Testate Succession Intestate Succession
Definition Deceased leaves a valid will or testament. Deceased does not leave behind a will.
Mechanism Property disposal carried out according to the deceased's wishes recorded in the will. Property devolution depends on law (Sections 32-42). Priority given to immediate family (spouse/children).
Beneficiaries Persons chosen by the testator. Spouse, children, blood relatives. Unmarried partners/in-laws generally excluded unless in a will.

Intestate Provisions (No Will)

  • Surviving Spouse: Entitled to personal/household effects. Holds estate in trust for children.
  • Polygamous Intestate: Personal effects and residue divided among houses according to the number of children in each house.
  • No Blood Relatives: Estate devolves to the state.

Importance of Law of Succession

  • Provides mechanisms for property transmission to heirs.
  • Identifies legal/rightful claimants.
  • Spells out procedures for acquiring property.
  • Provides tools for dispute resolution.

‚öñÔ∏è Mental Health Act & Penal Code

Mental Health Act

  • Voluntary Submission: Any person aged 16+ may voluntarily submit to treatment by written application.
  • Minors (Under 16): Parent or guardian submits application.
  • Leaving Hospital: Voluntary patients may leave upon giving 72 hours' notice in writing.
  • Incapacity: If a voluntary patient becomes incapable of expressing willingness to stay, they shall not be retained for more than 42 days unless they regain capacity or are formally retained under other sections.

Criminal Penal Code

Arrest by police officer without warrant: A police officer may arrest without a warrant any person who:

  • Is suspected upon reasonable grounds of having committed an indictable offence.
  • Commits any offence punishable by imprisonment in the officer's presence.
  • Obstructs a police officer in execution of duty or escapes lawful custody.
  • Is found in possession of anything reasonably suspected to be stolen property.
  • Is suspected of having housebreaking implements without lawful excuse.
