ü©∫ General Signs & Symptoms

The upper respiratory tract disorders are those that involve the nose, paranasal sinuses, and pharynx, larynx, trachea or bronchi.

Cough

It's an expectation against a closed glottis. It's a reflex action produced by stimulation of nerve endings in the membranes lining the air passage. Cough helps clear the airway of unwanted materials.

  • Unproductive cough: There is no sputum or mucus formed and it occurs in many mild disorders or the R.T.I e.g. early stages of infection or bronchial carcinoma.
  • Productive cough: It's one where sputum is produced and the air passage is cleared.

Dyspnoea

It is difficult in breathing or breathlessness. Deficiency of oxygen occurs in any disease which interferes with the proper uptake of oxygen and stimulates the respiratory centre so that there is increased respiration. Also any disease that the respiratory tract painful may cause dyspnoea.

Cyanosis

It is when the oxygen content of blood falls and carbon- dioxide is retained; this makes the blood in the skin, capillaries to take a bluish colour known as cyanosis. This is seen in many chest diseases and denotes a severe degree of involvement of the respiratory system.

Haemoptysis

This is coughing of blood and it varies from more staining sputum to frank blood. It is seen in serious chest diseases especially pulmonary tuberculosis and carcinoma of the lungs.

Pain

This due to pleurisy and inflammation which is seen in most chest disorders. The inflamed layer of the pleura when rubbed together during respiration causes pain.

ü§ß Rhinitis & Common Cold

1. Rhinitis

It is a group of disorders characterized by inflammation and irritation of the mucus membranes of the nose.

Causes

  • Commonest cause: cold viruses. It is very contagious for the first 2-3 days and is spread by droplets from sneezing/ blowing the nose of an infected person.
  • Allergic rhinitis: develops due to inhaling allergens eg some foods (eg peanuts, wheat, shellfish, cow's milk, eggs etc.), medications e.g. penicillins, aspirin and particles from indoors and out door environment.
  • Chronic rhinitis: may be due to repeated acute infection, allergy or vasomotor rhinitis (due to instability of autonomic nervous system, stress and tension).
  • Over use of nose drops or medication like nasal sprays (rebound phenomena).

Treatment

Decongestants are administered i.e.

  • Antihistamines (Chlorpheniramine): Adults (12 years and above) Tab Chlorpheniramine 4mg b.d; Children 6-12 years tbs Chlorpheniramine 2mg tds.
  • Cetirizine: Adults: Tabs citrizen 10mg od; Children 2-5 years 5mg od.
  • Loratadine: Adults Tabs 10mg od; Children 2-6 years Tab 5mg od.
  • Low dose steroids: Dexamethasone (Adults: Tabs Dexa 0.5mg ‚Äì 2mg od), Betamethasone, Hydrocortisone.

2. Common Cold (Corryza)

It is the acute inflammation of the upper respiratory tract. It's highly infectious which causes mild systematic upset and prominent nasal symptoms. It is due to infection by a wide range of respiratory viruses of which the rhino viruses are the commonest.

Clinical Features

  • Onset which is usually sudden
  • Tiredness with slight pyrexia
  • General malaise
  • Mild conjunctivitis
  • Anorexia
  • Sore dry nose and pharynx
  • Sneezing and profuse; watery nasal discharge followed by thick mucopurulent secretion
  • Nasal congestion
  • Loss of or swollen enlarged lymph nodes

Management Note

Common cold is a viral disease which needs only symptomatic treatment and no antibiotics are needed.

  • Increase fluid intake preferably warm drinks
  • Provide more fruits and vitamins
  • Nasal decongestants like nasal drops
  • Give analgesic like paracetamol

üëÑ Tonsillitis & Pharyngitis

3. Acute Tonsillitis

It is the painful inflammation of tonsils with general toxemia.

Causes: hemolytic streptococcus, Epstein-Barr virus.

Clinical Manifestations

  • Sore throat, Fever, Snoring and difficulty in breathing
  • Dysphagia, Draining ears, Foul smelling breath
  • On examination: Tonsils will be swollen, red white spots (exudates are seen on both tonsils). A peritonsilar abscess forms (quinsy).

Treatment

  • Penicillin e.g Pen V 500mg qid x 5/7
  • Analgesics like pcm 500mg tds 3/7
  • Mouth washes and warm water gargles

5. Acute Pharyngitis

It is the sudden painful inflammation of the pharynx, the back portion of the throat including the posterior 3rd of the tongue, soft palate and tonsils. Commonly called "Strep throat" when bacterial.

Causes

  • Viruses (Commonest): Adeno virus, influenza virus, herpes-simplex virus.
  • Bacteria: Group A Beta- hemolytic streptococcus (GABHS).
  • Fungal: infections or irritants.

Clinical Presentation

  • Sudden onset, Dryness and hotness of the throat
  • Severe soreness with dysphagia
  • Hacky dry cough, Fever, Headache
  • In fungal pharyngitis, white plaques in the mouth occur

Management

Relief can be obtained with a warm saline water gargles or an ice collar. Lozenges containing local anesthesia can also help. In case of hemolytic streptococci, the drugs of choice are penicillins (Pen V, Amoxicillin, co-amoxicillin or azithromycin) or erythromycin if allergic.

4. Acute Laryngitis

It's an inflammation of the larynx which often occurs due to voice abuse, exposure to dust, chemicals, smoke and other pollutants.

Signs and Symptoms

  • Hoarseness or ophonia (complete loss of the voice)
  • Severe cough
  • Sudden onset which is made worse by cold dry wind
  • Dry sore throat that worsens in the evening

Management

  • The voice should be rested and irritants avoided.
  • A steamy atmosphere is always advisable (steam tent).
  • Tincture or benzoin compound inhalation.
  • If high temperature: Benzyl-penicillin 1-2 mega units‚Äô b.d x 5/7 or Cotrimoxazole 960mg bd x 5/7.

Chronic Laryngitis

Causes: Prolonged over use of the voice, Excessive cigarette smoking, Tubercular laryngitis, Malignant diseases of the larynx (cancer), Syphilitic infection.

Clinical presentation: Chronic progressive hoarseness eventually leading to loss of voice.

ü§ï Sinusitis (Rhinosinusitis)

It's the inflammation of the paranasal sinuses and the nasal cavity.

‚è±Ô∏è

Classification

  • Acute ‚Äì less than 4 weeks
  • Sub- acute ‚Äì 4 to 12 weeks
  • Chronic ‚Äì more than 12 weeks

Clinical Manifestation

  • Continuous frontal throbbing headache
  • Purulent yellowish rhinorrhoea
  • Bad breath and cough
  • Facial pain with tenderness and fever
  • Nasal congestion and blockage
  • Patient presents with a sense of facial fullness.
  • NB: Bending and coughing or straining increases the pain

Treatment

  • Application of hot, wet towel over the face frequently to stimulate sinus drainage
  • For bacterial sinusitis: nasal decongestants like xylomethazoline, broad spectrum antibiotics like co-moxiclav therapy.
  • Anti-inflammation therapy with topical corticosteroids such as fluticasone propionates.
  • Steam inhalations may help to clear the blocked nose.
  • Functional endoscope sinus surgery (FESS) is used for ventilation and drainage if medical treatment fails.
