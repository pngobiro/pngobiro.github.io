üß¨ Chronic Cardiomyopathy

Cardiomyopathies: are a group of diseases of the myocardium that affect the mechanical or electrical function of the heart. These are frequently genetic or may be secondary to toxins, endomyocarditis, and autoimmune nutritional deficiencies.

Classifications

Hypertrophic cardiomyopathy (HCM)

This produces hypertrophy of the myocardium causing thickening of ventricular septum leading to obstruction of blood out flow from the left ventricles.

Dilated cardiomyopathy

Characterized by dilatation of the ventricular chambers

Signs and Symptoms

  • Chest pains
  • Dyspnoea
  • Cardiac arrhythmias
  • Oedema
  • Rapid thread pulse
  • Sudden death.

Management

  • Admit patient
  • Complete bed rest
  • Observations- vital and other observations
  • Ensure cardiac diet
  • Drug therapy- as in cardiac failure
  • Surgical mgt- surgical resection in case of hypertrophy

üöë Myocardial Infarction

‚ö°

Definition

Refers to the necrosis of heart muscles. This results from insufficient supply of oxygenated blood to an area of the heart. Or Results from total occlusion of a coronary artery by a thrombus or an embolus.

Signs and Symptoms

  • Severe chest pain lasting for more than 20 minutes
  • This pain does not respond to nitrates or opiate analgesia.. The pain may radiate to the left arm, neck or jaw.
  • Dyspnoea
  • Fatigue
  • Feeling of fainting
  • Nausea and vomiting
  • Rapid thread pulse (may be bradycardia or tachycardia)
  • General body weakness
  • Profuse sweating
  • Feeling of indigestion
  • Restlessness and irritability

Risk Factors

Cigarette smoking, Obesity, DM, Hypertension, Old age, Familial tendencies- history of heart disease, Previous cardiovascular diseases.

Management

Aims: To provide symptomatic relief, To prevent complications and recurrence.

Initial Management

  • Admit patient on a medical ward in a well-ventilated room
  • Position patient in a sitting up position in a cardiac bed
  • Ensure complete bed rest to rest the diseased heart
  • Reassure patient to allay anxiety.
  • Administer oxygen if patient is dyspnoe
  • Administer the prescribed pain relievers like aspirin 75-150mg three times a day orally for a month to reduce pain and the rate of re-infarction
  • Observations- vital observation temperature, pulse, respiration and blood pressure and other observations
Drug Class Specifics & Action
Analgesics aspirin 75-150mg three times a day for many days or months. Aspirin is also used as an antiplatelet.
Nitrates to relieve severe chest pain -glyceryl trinitrates 0.3-1mg sublingual (especially in angina).
Statins like simvastatin to reduce cholesterol levels and the risk of re-occurrence dose- 20-80mg/day.
Anti-hypertensive beta blockers, ACE inhibitors to reduce blood pressure

Long Term Management

  • Control hypertension.
  • Avoid smoking and alcoholism.
  • Carry out less strenuous exercises to reduce obesity.
  • Control DM.
  • Encourage cardiac diet.

üíì Angina Pectoris

Definition

It is a clinical syndrome characterized by episodes of retrosternal chest pain caused by any interference or reduction of oxygen supply to the heart. In angina, the pain is felt across the upper part of the chest and then radiates to the arm (from chest, neck and left arm).

The pain is mainly due to exertion or exercises and it's relieved by rest except in severe cases where it even occurs at rest. Pain usually lasts for a few minutes (3-5 minutes).

Warning Sign

NB: Angina is a warning sign that there is something wrong with the heart and quick relief is needed before damage re-occurs.

Causes & Predisposing Factors

Causes

  • Arteriosclerosis
  • Hypertension
  • Aortic aneurysm
  • Aortic stenosis
  • Severe anaemia

Predisposing Factors

  • Physical exertion
  • Temperature extremes
  • Consumption of heavy meals
  • Cigarette smoking
  • Emotional stress
  • Drugs like cocaine, opiates
  • Familial tendencies

Types of Angina Pectoris

Stable Angina

  • It's due to atherosclerosis.
  • The pain is constant, bearable and predictable.
  • Pain lasts for <5 minutes.
  • Pain is due exertion.
  • Pain is relieved by rest or nitroglycerine

Unstable Angina

  • It has a sudden onset.
  • Occurs due to rupture of thickened plague, clot
  • Symptoms are more severe and less predictable.
  • Pain lasts for >5 minutes pain occurs at rest or with minimal exertion.
  • Pain is not relieved by rest or medication.

Prinzmental (Variant)

  • Occurs primarily at rest
  • It is triggered by smoking and drugs like cocaine.
  • Occurs due to coronary heart spasms (vasospasms)
  • Pain is more frequent at night and less in the morning

Signs and Symptoms

  • Pain ‚Äì severe, constrictive, squeezing, sharp chest pain which is retrosternal and radiates from the chest to the neck, jaw, shoulder and left upper arm.
  • Chest tightness
  • General body weakness
  • Palpitation
  • Difficulty in breathing
  • Profuse sweating
  • Fainting attacks
  • Activity intolerance

Management

Aims: To relieve pain, To treat or abort the cause, To prevent complications. There is no specific treatment or cure for angina.

Nursing Care:

  • Admit in acute or severe case (unstable/prinzmental angina)
  • Position patient in a sitting up position
  • Complete bed rest in a well-ventilated room
  • Reassure patient to allay anxiety
  • Administer oxygen if there is difficulty in breathing
  • Monitor the vital observations and other observations
  • Inform the doctor
  • Monitor and balance the fluid balance chart
  • Relieve pain by administering pain relievers as prescribed by the doctor
  • Diet- cardiac diet

Drugs:

  • Nitrates- glyceryl trinitrate 0.3-1mg sublingual
  • Anticoagulants- aspirin 300 mg single dose; Heparin 5000-10000 units two times a day
  • Antihypertensive- beta blockers like propranolol 40mg 2-3 times a day, maintenance dose 120-240mg, calcium channel blockers e.g. Nifedipine 5-10mg three time a day
