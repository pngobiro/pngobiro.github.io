Definition

Refers to the reduction in the amount of red blood cells or haemoglobin or both for the age and sex of the individual.

Causes and Types of Anaemia

1. Haemorrhagic Anaemia

Anaemia due to excess blood loss.

Acute blood loss causes: Severe epistaxis, Injuries/ trauma e.g. RTA, operation, Child birth- APH, PPH, Blood vessel rapture-esophageal varices rupture, Severe hemophilia.

Chronic blood loss causes: Gastritis-haemoptysis (peptic ulcers), Menstrual flow (menorrhagia), Haemorrhoids.

2. Haemolytic Anaemia

Occurs due to excess destruction of red blood cells.

Causes: Infections- malaria, septicemia, hepatitis; Blood incompatibility during blood transfusion; Congenital haemolytic anaemia like sickle cell patients; Rhesus factor incompatibility; Enzyme deficiencies e.g. G6PD.

3. Iron Deficiency Anaemia

Occurs due to inadequate iron in the body.

Causes: Insufficient iron dietary intake; Malabsorption due to chronic diarrhea, inflammatory bowel diseases; Chronic blood loss- in haemorrhoids, GI bleeding, pph; Pregnancy.

4. Megaloblastic Anaemia

Impaired DNA synthesis which leads to production of large red blood cells (macrocytic).

Causes: Folic acid deficiency due to poor nutrition, malabsorption syndrome, drugs (anticancer drugs, oral contraceptives, anti-convulsants), alcohol abuse, hemodialysis; Vitamin B12 (cobalamine) deficiency.

5. Pernicious Anaemia

This is due to lack of absorption of vitamin B12 or absence of an intrinsic factor secreted by the parietal cells which results in a decrease in HCl by the stomach.

6. Aplastic Anaemia

Occurs as a result of bone marrow defects (decrease in all blood cell types like RBCs, WBCs and platelets).

Causes: Unknown-70%.

Predisposing factors: Drugs like chloramphenicol (it causes bone marrow depression), Radiations, Chemical agents like insectide, alcohol etc., Chromosomal alterations, Viral and bacterial infections e.g. hepatitis, TB, Chronic diseases like chronic rheumatoid arthritis, renal failure.

7. Sickle Cell Anaemia

Occurs due to excess destruction of RBCs due to their abnormal shape (sickle) making them more fragile and prone to breaking down.

ü©∫ Clinical Picture

General Signs and Symptoms

  • Dry skin
  • Paleness of the mucus membranes, skin, and finger nail beds.
  • Jaundice
  • Pruritus due to jaundice
  • Dizziness, drowsiness
  • Headache
  • General body weakness and fatigue
  • Rapid pulse and palpitations
  • Blurred vision
  • Difficulty in breathing with cyanosis
  • Anorexia
  • Oedema of the lower limbs
  • Chest pain- angina pectoris
  • Myocardial infarction signs/ symptoms
  • Restlessness and irritability
  • Insomnia
  • Bone pain- in aplastic and sickle cell anaemia
  • Weight loss
  • Signs and symptoms of cardiac failure.

Investigations

  • Signs and symptoms (history taking)
  • Blood for ‚Äì haemoglobin levels, serum iron and ferritin and iron binding capacity, WBCs, RBCs and platelets, grouping and cross matching, sickle cell test
  • Bone marrow puncture and biopsy
  • Gastric juice testing for levels of intrinsic factor
  • Serum bilirubin

üíâ Management

üéØ

Aims

  • To treat the cause
  • To health educate the patient about anaemia prevention
  • To prevent complications

Management of Mild Anaemia

  • Identify and treat the cause
  • Educate the patient on diet i.e. Red meat, liver greens leafy vegetables, pork, fish, legumes, eggs, potatoes milk, citrus fruits, strawberries, tomatoes
  • Oral Haematenics- ferrous sulphate 200-400mg three times a days, folic acid 5mg 2-3 times a day

Management of Moderate Anaemia

  • Identify and treat the cause e.g. malaria, worms, diarrhea
  • Diet- rich in iron and folic acid
  • I.V or I.M iron (interferon) 5mg once a day for 5-10 days, and then later iron tablets

Management of Severe Anaemia

  • Admit patient in a medical ward near nurses table in a well-ventilated room
  • Position- patient is put in a sitting up position in dyspnoea, in shock elevate the foot the bed
  • Administer oxygen in severe dyspnoea and cyanosis
  • Observations- vital observation temperature, pulse, respirations and blood pressure. In severe stage (shock) monitor vitals quarter hourly, half hourly then 2 hourly, 4 hourly until the conditions improves. Strictly document the vital observations findings and report the deviations. Observe patient for signs of shock, cyanosis and mental state.
  • Psychotherapy- reassure patient and patient‚Äôs relatives to allay anxiety
  • Investigations ‚Äì all the ordered investigations are taken off and taken to the laboratory and the results are brought as soon as they are ready
  • Patient is prepared for blood transfusion
  • Blood transfusion is started as soon as blood is ready
  • After blood transfusion, oral iron tablets are given (ferrous sulphate 200mg 3 times a day)

Alternative to Transfusion

NOTE: in health unit/ hospital where there is no blood for transfusion and the patient is severe anaemia, iron supplements are given i.e. injection inferno 5mg I.M for 5-10 days or total dose of inferon - 5mg x7 days infused in 500mls of normal saline (35mg in 500mls of normal saline I.V infusion slowly).

Additional Care for Severe Anaemia:

  • Diet: high protein diet, food rich in iron (as already mentioned), vitamins, plenty of fluids and fruits.
  • Hygiene: complete bed rest in severely anaemia patients and therefore everything is done for the patient like bed bath, mouth care, regular turning, pressure area care
  • Bladder and bowel care: monitor and balance the fluid balance chart and prevent constipation
  • Exercise: passive and active exercises

Discharge Advice

  • To eat a well-balanced diet rich in iron, vitamins, proteins and vegetables
  • To take the drugs as prescribed.
  • To come back for review on the appointed date
  • To have enough rest.

Prevention of Anaemia

Health educate on:

  • Sanitation (personal and environmental sanitation). The importance of having a pit latrine
  • Eat a well-balanced diet
  • Ensure proper weaning of children to avoid nutrition anaemia
  • Deworming children/ and adults against worms.
  • Child spacing to avoid excess blood loss in delivery
  • Proper management of labor
  • Regular medical check-up for any abnormalities
  • Patients with sickle cell must be prevented from any infections and other triggering factors

Complications of Anaemia

  • Brain damage
  • Respiratory failure
  • Renal failure
  • Low resistance to infections
  • Heart failure
  • Malnutrition
