üíî Congestive Cardiac Failure (CCF)

üìù

Definition

This is an abnormal condition involving impaired cardiac pumping OR: It's the inability of the heart to pump sufficient blood to meet the needs of the tissues for oxygen and nutrients.

It's a chronic condition in which the heart doesn't pump blood as well as it should. When the condition is chronic, there is congestion in the venous system; fluids accumulate in the tissues and cavities of the body causing congestion. This condition is referred to as congestive cardiac failure (CCF). CCF occurs when both the left and right sides of the heart fail.

Types of Heart Failure

Left sided heart failure

Here fluid in the lungs causing shortness of breath

Right sided heart failure

Fluids may back up in the abdomen, legs, feet causing swelling

Causes

Any of the causes of the heart disease can cause CCF like:

  • Myocardial infarction.
  • Allergic reactions
  • Blood clot
  • Severe infection
  • Sleep apnea
  • Smoking using tobacco
  • Obesity
  • Rheumatic heart disease.
  • Pericarditis, Myocarditis and endocarditis.
  • DM
  • Hypertension
  • Coronary artery disease.
  • COPD

ü©∫ Clinical Picture of CCF

Signs and Symptoms

  • Activity intolerance like in walking, digging, carrying etc.
  • Dyspnoea‚Äîparoxysmal nocturnal dyspnoea
  • Orthopnea
  • Anxiety.
  • Raised jugular venous pressure causing distended jugular veins.
  • Nausea and vomiting
  • Loss of appetite. Hepatomegaly due to portal congestion.
  • Oedema mainly of the lower limbs (lower body parts)
  • Heart burn and feeling of indigestion
  • Constipation.
  • Rapid irregular pulse rate.
  • Haemoptysis due to pulmonary congestion.
  • Palpitations.
  • Restlessness and irritability
  • Reduced urine output (oliguria) sometimes total urine absence (anuria).
  • Ascites-fluid accumulation in the peritoneal space.
  • Weight loss with prominent ribs.
  • Chest pain with cyanosis.
  • Anaemia.
  • Cough which may be initially dry and later becomes productive.
  • Pigeon-shaped chest with asymmetrical alignment.
  • On auscultation‚Äîcrackling sounds will be heard in the lungs due to pleural effusion.
  • Chest x-ray reveals congested and enlarged heart.

Investigations/Diagnosis

  • History taking
  • General examinations from head to toe
  • Blood tests i.e blood for culture and sensitivity, HB, grouping and cross matching, serum electrolytes, liver function test, renal function test
  • Stress tests
  • Chest x-ray, these images show the condition of the heart
  • MRI

üíä Management of CCF

üéØ

Aims of Management

  • To rest the patient‚Äîmentally and physically
  • To relieve symptoms
  • To prevent complications

Nursing Care & Interventions

  • Admit patient on a medical ward in a well-ventilated room which is quiet near the nurse‚Äôs station for close monitoring
  • Give a complete bed rest to rest the heart.
  • Position the patient in a sitting up position to aid breathing, to relieve pressure of fluids in the lungs (fluid gravity).
  • Loosen anything of constrictive nature from the patient‚Äôs body to aid breathing and promote comfort.
  • Use a bed cradle to lift the weight of beddings off the patient
  • Observations ‚Äì vital observations i.e. Pulse, respirations, temperature, and blood pressure must be measured 4 hourly and accurately recorded in the patient‚Äôs file. Pulse may be done more frequently. Observe assess patient for oedema, respiratory status and signs of cyanosis. Continuous pulse oximetry is needed
  • Provide a cardiac table to help the patient relax.
  • Administer oxygen 2-5 litres to support breathing and correct cyanosis.
  • Psychotherapy- patient and patient‚Äôs relative are reassured to allay anxiety. This is started right from the time of admission up to discharge, always attend to patient‚Äôs queries/ questions and if possible stay with the patient.
  • Support the patient‚Äôs feet with a foot rest (small pillows) to prevent foot drop.
  • Place a soft cushion beneath the oedematous sacral area to relieve pressure.
  • Daily weighing of the patient to assess oedema and Ascites improvement.
  • Provide a loose jacket or shawl to cover the patient in order to keep him/her warm.
  • Hygiene- in acute phase of CCF, everything is done for the patient like bed bath, mouth care regular pressure area treatment especially the oedematous areas. Provide a sputum mug with disinfectant for expectoration, which must be regularly emptied, cleaned and kept covered.

Dietary Management

Provide a highly nutritious diet with less sugar and carbohydrates (starch) which require a lot of energy metabolism. Provide a salt and fat free diet; give plenty of fruits, and vegetables/roughages to prevent constipation. Give little food at a time but frequently to avoid a distended stomach or abdomen. Restrict fluids in oedema; however give adequate amount of fluids.

Drug Therapy

  • Digitalis group- like digoxin: Digitalis help to strengthens the heart and reduce on the contractility and conductivity of the heart. NB: digoxin should not be given when the pulse rate is <60 b/m as it causes bradycardia.
  • Diuretics: to promote renal excretion of salt and water thus correcting oedema. These include: furosemide, Bendrofluazide, Potassium sparing diuretics.
  • Hypotensive: to normalize the blood pressure if high like: ACE inhibitors like captopril, Beta blockers.
  • Sedatives: like diazepam or phenytoin to promote rest and sleep.
  • Supportive drugs: like Haematenics e.g. Ferrous sulphate to prevent or treat anaemia, multivitamins to stimulate appetite.

Additional Procedures & Care

  • Abdominal paracentesis to relieve abdominal pressure caused by Ascites.
  • Exercises ‚Äì initially passive and when condition improves, active exercises can be commenced.
  • Bladder and bowel care ‚Äì fluid balance chart must be strictly monitored and balanced every after 24 hours to assess kidney function. Provide roughages and fruits with just enough fluids to avoid constipation.

Health Education

Health educate patient and the patient‚Äôs relative about:

  • The nature of the disease and how it‚Äôs managed in the hospital and at home.
  • To adopt and comply with a cardiac diet- salt and fat free diet.
  • About the drugs, how to take them and then drug compliance.
  • To maintain a complete bed rest and the condition improves to carry out less strenuous exercises.
  • To reduce or stop all the predisposing factors to cardiac failure (CCF) like stopping smoking, reduce weight (obesity) control DM, stop/reduce high fat diet.
  • Vaccination of all patients against pneumococcal diseases, influenza, measles etc.
  • Return to the hospital for review on the appointed date.
  • NB: All mothers with cardiac failure (CCF) who want to conceive again must first consult their Cardiologists before conception.

Complications of CCF

  • Renal failure
  • Respiratory failure
  • Brain oedema leading to brain damage
  • Peritonitis due to infected ascetic fluid
  • Liver failure
  • Multiple organ dysfunction syndromes (mods)
  • Shock

üõë Hypertension

Definition

Hypertension refers to the sustained elevation of the blood pressure above normal i.e. systolic blood pressure equal or above 140mmhg or diastolic blood pressure greater than or equal to 90mmhg for extended period of time. High blood pressure means that the heart is working harder than normal thus putting the heart and the blood vessels on a high pressure.

Types of Hypertension

Primary / Essential

This has no known cause however it is associated with the following factors: Genetic or familial/hereditary, Obesity, Alcohol consumption, Excess salt intake, Anxiety/stress, Age, DM high fat diet, Cigarette smoking.

Secondary

This is when high blood pressure results from a specific and potentially treatable cause. These causes include: Cushing syndrome, Thyroid disease, Hyper-parathyroid disease, Phaeochromocytoma (tumor in adrenal gland), Renal vascular disease, Chronic glomerulonephritis, Diabetic nephropathy, Aortic coarctation, Drugs like NSAIDS, steroids, vasopressin, oral contraceptives, Peripheral vascular disease, Pre-eclampsia and eclampsia.

Malignant

This is when blood pressure rises rapidly and suddenly with measurements of 200mmhg and above (systolic pressure) and >140mmhg (diastolic pressure). It originates from any form or cause of hypertension. If untreated it may lead to death from progressive renal failure, heart failure, aortic dissection or stroke. Death takes place within 2 years or less.

üíä Management of Hypertension

Signs and Symptoms

Patients with mild hypertension/ essential hypertension are usually symptoms. In severe hypertension, the onset is sudden with:

  • Attacks of sweating.
  • Headache
  • Dizziness and giddiness
  • Haemoptysis and hematemesis
  • Epistaxis
  • Haematuria
  • Impaired vision
  • Ringing in the ears (tinnitus)
  • Breathlessness
  • Altered conscious levels.
  • Proteinuria
  • Rapid thread pulse
  • Palpitations
  • Insomnia
  • Restlessness and irritability
  • Fits or convulsions
  • On measuring the blood pressure with an accurately working blood pressure machine, the blood pressure will be abnormally elevated.

Investigations

  • History taking, including visual and bleeding tendencies.
  • Take blood pressure with an accurately working blood pressure machine
  • General examination from head to toe to rule out any masses
  • Urine for urinalysis and microscopy (blood and proteins)
  • Random blood sugar
  • Serum lipids and cholesterol
  • Chest x-ray
  • Electro and echocardiogram.
  • Serum urea, Creatinine and electrolytes to access renal state.

Nursing Care & Management

Aims: To provide both physical and mental rest, To reduce the blood pressure within normal ranges or safe limits, To relieve symptoms and prevent complications.

  • Admit patient with severe or malignant hypertension.
  • Encourage enough rest.
  • Position patient in a comfortable position.
  • Accurate and frequent vital observation measurements (temperature, pulse, respiration and blood pressure) regularly measure blood pressure as instructed by the doctor. And accurately record the findings.
  • Reassure patient to allay anxiety.
  • Diet: reduce fat and salt intake. Reduce or stop alcohol taking and cigarette smoking. Take plenty of proteins, fresh fruits, fluids and vegetables.
  • Monitor the urine output (monitor and balance the fluid balance chat).
  • Exercises: regular exercises when the patient‚Äôs condition improves to reduce weight (obesity).
  • Health education on stress management.

Drug Therapy

These are given in combination i.e. hypotensive, diuretics and sedatives.

Complications of Hypertension

  • CCF
  • Renal failure
  • Cerebral vascular disease (stroke)
  • Blindness due to retinal haemorrhage
  • Angina pectoris
  • Myocardial infarction
