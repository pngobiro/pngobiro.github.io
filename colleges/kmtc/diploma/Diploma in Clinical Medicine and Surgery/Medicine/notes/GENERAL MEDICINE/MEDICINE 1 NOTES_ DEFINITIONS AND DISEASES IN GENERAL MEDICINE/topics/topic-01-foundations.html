Core Concepts

Medicine: Is the science and or practice that involves treating diseases using external treatment or remedies as opposed to mechanical and operational procedures (use of non-surgical methods). Is the science and or practice of the diagnosis, treatment and prevention of diseases.

Health: Is the state of complete wellbeing of a person mentally, physically, socially and spiritually and not merely in the absence of a disease.

Disease: Is a disorder with specific causes and recognizable signs and symptoms.

Clinical Terminology

  • Etiology: Is the study of disease causation.
  • Pathology: Is the study of the course of disease or disease process and the structures that are involved.
  • Symptoms: Are the complaints of the patients about any presenting condition or disease.
  • Signs: Are features that are seen by the naked eyes on the patient.
  • Physical signs: Are ones discovered on physical examination.
  • Complications: Are those signs, symptoms lesions or diseases that may result from an original disease.
  • Syndrome: It‚Äôs a combination of signs and symptoms that form a distinct clinical picture indicative of a particular disorder.
  • Diagnosis: Is the process of determining the nature of a disorder by considering the patient‚Äôs signs and symptoms, medical background, results of laboratory tests and radiological examinations.
  • Differential diagnosis: Is the knowledge of other diseases which resemble the disease that the patient is suspected to be having and the point of difference which help in giving the final conclusion.
  • Prophylaxis: Is any means taken to prevent a disease like immunization, hygiene chemotherapy etc.
  • Prognosis: Is an assessment of the future course and outcomes of a patient‚Äôs disease.

ü¶† General Causes of Diseases

Diseases may arise from various factors including:

üë∂ Congenital Causes

This is when someone is born with an abnormality. It mainly occurs during intra-uterine life. It may be due to infections of the mother, certain drugs including native medicine, x-ray.

Examples include: Hare lip and cleft palate, congenital heart disease.

üë™ Hereditary

This is when a disease is transmitted from parents to their off springs (children) like sickle cell disease, hemophilia etc.

‚ùì Idiopathic

Is when the exact cause of disease is unknown like some cancers, epilepsy, mental disorders etc.

ü§ß Allergic Disorders

This is when someone reacts with physical symptoms and signs to normal substances which other people do not react to. The allergens may be inhaled, injected into tissue or direct contact to grass, fumes, powder or ingested e.g. Asthma skin diseases, anaphylactic shock etc.

ü§ï Injury

It is a physical harm to a person.

ü¶† Microorganisms

The disease may be brought about by bacteria, viruses, fungi, parasites, protozoa etc. like measles, malaria, pneumonia etc.

üß™ Metabolic Disorders

This is when the body is unable to deal with certain elements of the diet or complex organic constituents of the body resulting into their accumulation in the body thus becoming dangerous in the body. E.g. DM, Mental confusion, hyperlipidemia, hypocalcaemia, etc.

‚öïÔ∏è Endocrine Disorders

üìâ Degeneration

üè• Iatrogenic
