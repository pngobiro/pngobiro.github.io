üìù Definition

This is abnormal dilation of bronchi which is irreversible due to destruction of the muscular and elastic supporting tissue of the bronchial wall. It can be accompanied by massive collapse of distal part of bronchiole

02

ü¶† CAUSES

A. Congenital

Ciliary dysfunction syndromes, cystic fibrosis, primary hypogammaglobulinacemia

B. Acquired (Children)

Results following- Pneumonia, Whooping cough, measles, primary pulmonary tuberculosis, foreign body

C. Post-Infection/Obstruction

Follow supportive [suppurative] pneumonia. Pulmonary Tuberculosis, pulmonary obstruction/Bronchial Tumours

D. Ciliary dysfunction syndromes

  • Primary ciliary dysfunction syndromes
  • Kartegeners syndrome (bronchiectasis, sinusitis, transposition or visceral)
  • Youngs syndrome

  4. Recurrent febril episodes and malaria [malaise] and episodes of pneumonia
  5. Haemoptysis
  6. Difficulty in breathing
