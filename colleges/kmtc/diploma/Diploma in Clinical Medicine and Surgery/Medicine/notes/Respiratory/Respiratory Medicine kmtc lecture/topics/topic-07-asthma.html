Bronchial Asthma ‚Äì This is a disease characterized by increased responsiveness of bronchioles to various stimulants manifested narrowing of airway leading to reversible or potentially reversible airway obstruction

üîÑ PATTERNS / TYPES OF ASTHMA

  1. Atopic Asthma ( Extrinsic Asthma )
  2. Non ‚Äì Atopic ( Intrinsic Asthma )

ATOPIC (EXTRINSIC ASTHMA)

Has a definite external cause and occurs in atopic individuals accounts for 90% of childhood asthma and 50% in adults. Attacks are provoked by IgE Type I hypersensitive reactions to inhaled allergens.

NON ATOPIC (INTRINSIC ASTHMA)

Usually occur in middle age. No hx of atopy family hx is uncommon. Cause unknown.

üíä MANAGEMENT

AIMS

  • Abolish symptoms
  • Restore normal or best possible lung function
  • Reduce risk of severe attack
  • Enable growth to occur in children
  • Minimise absence from school or employment
  • Give guidance and education to patient and family about asthma
  • Involve patient and family in asthma ...
  • Counsel patient to avoid identifiable causes as possible
  • Use of lowest effective doses of convenient medicine

DRUG MANAGEMENT

  1. Aerosol powders
    Drug is delivered directly into the lungs. Avoid metabolism of the liver. They require smaller doses and SE are minimal.

BETA ADRENERGIC AGONISTS

Example Fenoteral, Salbutamol, Terbutalin

They have a short duration of action and rapid onset of action. They stimulate beta receptors and relax bronchial smooth muscle by increasing cellular levels of cyclic AMP. Most cell degradation lead to release of Histamine, Serotonin, Bradykinin, Kallikrein, Eosinophilic, Chemotactic, factor platelet activating factor

2. STEROIDS

Examples Aerosols, Budesonide, Fluticasone, Beclomethasone

ORALS Prednisolone, injectable, Hydrocortisone. They reduce bronchial inflammation and reduce responsiveness of bronchial airways

3. XANTHINES

Examples- Aminophylline, Theophyline

They act by relaxing bronchial smooth muscle by inhibiting Phosphodiesterase. The enzyme which breaks down cyclic AMP. They should be taken regularly for prophylaxis and are usually used in conjunction with inhaled beta agonists and steroids.

4. ANTICHOLINERGICS

Examples- Ipratropium, Oxitropium

They are often used as regular in treatment in conjunction with other agents in airway disease. They relax bronchiole smooth muscle by blocking reflex bronchoconstriction. They have a slow onset but prolonged duration of action.

5. MEDIATOR RELEASE INHIBITORS

Cromoglycate sodium, Nedocromil

These drugs are used regularly and prophylactically to prevent the release of mediators from most cells in the airways.
