<p>This is air accumulation in the pleural space by partial or complete lung collapse. Increase in atmospheric pressure within the pleural cavity leads to lung recoil hence collapse</p>
<p>Aetiological types</p>
<p>Traumatic</p>
<p>Tension</p>
<p>Spontaneous</p>
<p>Traumatic pneumothorax</p>
<p>Can be open or closed</p>
<p>Open</p>
<p>Penetrative chest wound creates a communication between the atmosphere and pleural space thus permeating air inflow to pleural space from the atmosphere. The air inflow causes increase in pressure more than in lungs hence lung recoil and collapse. It occurs in stab wounds.</p>
<p>Closed</p>
<p>Chest wall is air tight. Air flows from lungs in to pleural space increasing pleural space pressure hence lung collapse</p>
<p>Causes</p>
<p>Mediastinal procedures e.g. pleural aspiration or puncture, lung perforation by puncture by broken rib, thoracocentesis in pleural tap using improperly sealed needles, Oesphageal surgery</p>
<p>Spontaneous pneumothorax</p>
<p>Air enters the pleural space without history of trauma</p>
