üî¨ PATHOLOGY

First infection with Mycobacterium Tuberculosis is primary Tuberculosis. Usually subpleural and often in the mid to upper zones. Within 1 hour of reaching the lung TB bacilli reach draining Lymph node at the hillum of the lung and few escape to blood stream.

Initial reaction comprises exudation and infiltration with neutrophil granulocytes.

These are rapidly replaced by macrophages that ingest the bacilli. These interact with T. Lymphocytes with development of cellular immunity that can be demonstrated 3-8 weeks after mitral infection by development of a positive reaction in the skin to an intradermal injection of protein from tubercle bacilli tuberculin.

Granulomatous lesion consist of a central area of necrotic material of cheesy nature called caseatrum surrounded by epitheliolid cells and Langerhan giant cells with multiple nuclei.

Caseated areas heal completely and may become calcified. Often contains tuberculous bacilli and becomes reactivated with reduced host immunity.
