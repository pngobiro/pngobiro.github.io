üìù Definition

This is a clinical syndrome characterized by excessive production of mucous. Persistent cough on most days for a period of at least 3 months in at least 2 consecutive years.

02

üö¨ AETIOLOGY AND PREDISPOSING FACTORS

Occurs in people of 45 years and above. More common in male

  1. Cigarette smokers
  2. Atmospheric pollution- dust, smoke, sulphur
  3. Poor socioeconomic status
  4. Chest infections bacterial viral
03

üîÑ PATHOPHYSIOLOGY

  1. Chronic irritation of bronchial epithelium
  2. Hyperplasia and hypertrophy of mucus gland
  3. Increased number and proportion of goblet cells
  4. Excessive production of mucus and loss of ciliated epithelium
  5. Accumulation of mucus
  6. Obstruction by mucus
  7. Infection - Organisms
    1. Haemophillus influenza
    2. Strep pneumoniae
    3. Pneumophilia
    4. Pseudomonas A.
    5. Moraxella cattarhalis
04

  1. Cough- production of clear to whitish sputum only becomes purulent or yellow if there is secondary bacterial infection and worse on waking up in the morning.
  2. Difficulty in breathing
  3. Chest tightness
  4. Wheeze
