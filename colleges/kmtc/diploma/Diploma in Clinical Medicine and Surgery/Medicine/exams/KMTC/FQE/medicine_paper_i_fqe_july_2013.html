100 Marks
1.
Kamau a 70 year old man present with generalized body swelling, difficulty in breathing, easy fatigability and denies exercise. On examination, he is sick looking, uncomfortable when examined in supine position and has edema. He is not pale but has elevated jugular venous pressure.
a) What is the most probable diagnosis? (2 marks)
b) Outline the treatment for Kamau. (8 marks)
Show Answer

a) Diagnosis: Congestive Cardiac Failure (CCF).

Key indicators: Anasarca (generalized swelling), Dyspnea, Orthopnea (uncomfortable supine), Raised JVP.

b) Treatment Outline:

  • Non-pharmacological:
    • Nurse in cardiac position (propped up) to relieve dyspnea.
    • Oxygen therapy via mask/nasal prongs.
    • Salt restriction (Low sodium diet).
    • Fluid restriction.
  • Pharmacological:
    • Diuretics: Furosemide (Lasix) to reduce fluid overload (preload).
    • ACE Inhibitors: Enalapril/Captopril to reduce afterload.
    • Beta-Blockers: Carvedilol (once stable) to improve survival.
    • Positive Inotropes: Digoxin (if AF present or severe failure).
    • Aldosterone Antagonists: Spironolactone.
2.
A 26 year old lady comes to your clinic with history of productive cough for 21 days. She complains of dyspnea and accepts she has lost weight significantly. She has insomnia and night sweats. On examination she has cervical lymphadenopathy, a respiratory rate of 26 cycles per minute and bilateral coarse crepitation.
a) What is your probable diagnosis? (2 marks)
b) Outline the investigation you will do to support your diagnosis. (4 marks)
c) Outline the specific treatment for this patient. (6 marks)
Show Answer

a) Diagnosis: Pulmonary Tuberculosis (PTB).

b) Investigations:

  • Sputum Analysis: GeneXpert (preferred) or Sputum for AAFBs (ZN Stain).
  • Chest X-ray: To check for cavitation, infiltration, or lymphadenopathy.
  • ESR: Likely elevated.
  • HIV Test: As a common co-morbidity.

c) Specific Treatment (Anti-TB Therapy):

  • Intensive Phase (2 Months): 4 drugs - Rifampicin (R), Isoniazid (H), Pyrazinamide (Z), Ethambutol (E) [2RHZE].
  • Continuation Phase (4 Months): 2 drugs - Rifampicin (R), Isoniazid (H) [4RH].
  • Dosage: Based on body weight (Fixed Dose Combinations).
  • Adjuncts: Pyridoxine (Vitamin B6) to prevent Isoniazid-induced neuropathy.
3.
Mrs. Sharon presents with history of polyuria, polydipsia and polyphagia. On examination, she is restless and disoriented in time, place and person. She is neither febrile nor pale, but her eyes are sunken with poor skin turgor. You are able to elicit a sweet smell in her breath. Her BP is 80/30mm/hg while the random blood sugar is 33mmol/litre.
a) What is your diagnosis? (2 marks)
b) Discuss the management of this patient. (8 marks)
Show Answer

a) Diagnosis: Diabetic Ketoacidosis (DKA).

Triad: Hyperglycemia (33mmol/L), Dehydration (Sunken eyes/Low BP), Ketosis (Sweet breath).

b) Management:

  • Resuscitation (ABC): Secure airway, give oxygen if needed.
  • Fluids (Aggressive Rehydration):
    • 0.9% Normal Saline.
    • 1L in 1st hour, 1L over next 2 hours, 1L over next 4 hours.
    • Switch to 5% Dextrose when RBS < 14mmol/L to prevent hypoglycemia.
  • Insulin Therapy:
    • Soluble (Regular) Insulin IV infusion (0.1 units/kg/hr).
    • Goal: Reduce ketones and blood sugar gradually.
  • Potassium Replacement:
    • Insulin shifts K+ into cells causing hypokalemia. Add KCL to fluids once renal function is confirmed and K+ is < 5.5mmol/L.
  • Treat Precipitating Cause: Antibiotics if infection is suspected.
  • Monitoring: Hourly RBS, 4-hourly electrolytes, Vitals.
4.
A patient presents with confusion, headache and fever. On examination, she is febrile and disoriented in time, place and person. The neck is stiff and kerning sign is positive.
a) What is your probable diagnosis? (2 marks)
b) Outline the investigation you will carry out on this patient. (8 marks)
Show Answer

a) Diagnosis: Meningitis (Likely Bacterial).

b) Investigations:

  • Lumbar Puncture (CSF Analysis): The definitive test. Check for:
    • Appearance (Turbid/Cloudy).
    • Biochemistry (Low glucose, High protein).
    • Microscopy (Neutrophils/Pus cells).
    • Gram Stain & Culture (Identify organism).
    • India Ink (Rule out Cryptococcus).
  • Full Hemogram: Leucocytosis (High WBC).
  • Blood Culture: To rule out septicemia.
  • Random Blood Sugar: To compare with CSF glucose.
  • Serum Electrolytes: Check for SIADH.
5.
A patient is brought to you unconscious. The only history present is alleged intoxication of unknown substance following a domestic quarrel. On examination, he has excessive sweating, dyspnoea, pin-point pupils reactive to light and a wet chest. The respiratory rate is 30 cycles/min, BP of 70/30mmHg and pulse rate of 30 beats per minute.
a) What is your probable diagnosis? (2 marks)
b) Outline your treatment. (8 marks)
Show Answer

a) Diagnosis: Organophosphate Poisoning.

Signs: Pinpoint pupils (miosis), Bradycardia, Hypotension, Secretions (Sweating, Wet chest/Bronchorrhea). Typical Cholinergic toxidrome.

b) Treatment:

  • Resuscitation (ABC): Clear airway (suction secretions), high-flow Oxygen.
  • Antidote 1 (Atropine):
    • Give IV Atropine (1.8 - 3mg) immediately.
    • Repeat every 5-10 minutes doubling the dose until signs of "Atropinization" appear (Clear chest, Pupils dilate, Heart rate increases, Skin dries).
  • Antidote 2 (Oximes): Pralidoxime usually given early to reactivate acetylcholinesterase.
  • Decontamination: Remove contaminated clothing, wash skin with soap and water (if dermal exposure). Gastric lavage if ingested recently (protect airway first).
  • Supportive: IV Fluids for hypotension (careful with pulmonary edema), Diazepam for seizures.
6.
Discuss the management of cerebral malaria. (10 marks)
Show Answer
  • Admission: Admit to ICU or HDU.
  • Nursing Care: Assess GCS, maintain airway (lateral position), turn every 2 hours to prevent sores.
  • Antimalarial Therapy:
    • First Line: IV Artesunate (2.4mg/kg) at 0, 12, 24 hours, then daily for 6 days.
    • Alternative: IV Quinine loading dose (20mg/kg) in 5% Dextrose over 4 hours, then maintenance (10mg/kg) every 8 hours.
  • Seizure Control: IV Diazepam or Phenobarbitone if convulsions occur.
  • Fluid Management: Restrict fluids to maintenance to prevent pulmonary edema.
  • Hypoglycemia Management: Monitor RBS frequently. Treat with 25-50% Dextrose.
  • Fever Control: Tepid sponging, Paracetamol.
  • Blood Transfusion: If Hb < 5g/dl or signs of cardiac failure.
7.
Discuss nephritic syndrome under the following headings:
a) Causes (4 marks)
b) Investigation and management (6 marks)
Show Answer

a) Causes:

  • Post-Streptococcal Glomerulonephritis (PSGN) - most common in children.
  • IgA Nephropathy (Berger's disease).
  • Systemic Lupus Erythematosus (SLE).
  • Infective Endocarditis.
  • Vasculitis (e.g., Wegener's granulomatosis).

b) Investigation & Management:

Investigations:

  • Urinalysis: RBC casts, proteinuria, hematuria.
  • UECs: Elevated Urea/Creatinine.
  • ASOT (Anti-Streptolysin O Titer) for PSGN.
  • C3/C4 Complement levels (Low in PSGN/SLE).
  • Renal Biopsy (if diagnosis unclear).

Management:

  • Strict Fluid Balance & Salt Restriction.
  • Diuretics (Furosemide) for edema/hypertension.
  • Antihypertensives (Nifedipine, ACEIs) for BP control.
  • Antibiotics (Penicillin) if active strep infection.
  • Dialysis if acute renal failure occurs (severe hyperkalemia, fluid overload).
8.
Concerning anemia:
a) Define anemia. (2 marks)
b) Enumerate features of iron deficiency anemia. (8 marks)
Show Answer

a) Definition:

Anemia is a condition characterized by a decrease in the number of red blood cells or hemoglobin concentration below the normal range for age and sex, resulting in reduced oxygen-carrying capacity of the blood.

b) Features of Iron Deficiency Anemia:

  • General: Fatigue, pallor (conjunctiva/palms), dizziness, palpitations.
  • Koilonychia: Spoon-shaped nails.
  • Angular Stomatitis: Cracks at the corners of the mouth.
  • Glossitis: Smooth, red, sore tongue.
  • Pica: Craving for non-food items like soil (geophagia) or ice (pagophagia).
  • Brittle hair and nails.
  • Dysphagia: Plummer-Vinson syndrome (Esophageal webs).
9.
You are called upon to review a patient who has been on treatment for pneumonia. The treatment sheet shows he has been on gentamycin and benzyl penicillin for 3 days. However, the patient is still coughing, dyspnoeic, afebrile with bilateral basal crepitations.
a) List down four possible reasons/causes why this patient is not responding or improving following treatment.
b) What investigations will you carry out in this patient? (2 marks)
c) Outline the complications of pneumonia. (4 marks)
Show Answer

a) Reasons for non-response:

  1. Wrong Diagnosis: Could be Heart Failure (CCF), TB, or Pulmonary Embolism, not pneumonia.
  2. Wrong Antibiotic/Resistant Organism: Organism not sensitive to Penicillin/Gentamycin (e.g., Atypical pneumonia like Mycoplasma, or MRSA).
  3. Complications developed: Empyema, Lung Abscess.
  4. Host Factors: Immunosuppression (HIV, Diabetes).

b) Investigations:

  • Repeat Chest X-ray (Check for abscess/fluid/CCF signs).
  • Sputum C&S (Culture and Sensitivity) / GeneXpert.
  • Echocardiogram (Rule out heart failure).

c) Complications of Pneumonia:

  • Pleural Effusion / Empyema (Pus in pleural space).
  • Lung Abscess.
  • Septicemia / Septic Shock.
  • Acute Respiratory Distress Syndrome (ARDS).
  • Respiratory Failure.
10.
Discuss liver cirrhosis under the following headings.
a) Causes. (5 marks)
b) Investigations. (5 marks)
Show Answer

a) Causes:

  • Chronic Alcohol Abuse: Alcoholic liver disease.
  • Viral Hepatitis: Chronic Hepatitis B and C.
  • Non-Alcoholic Fatty Liver Disease (NAFLD/NASH): Associated with obesity/diabetes.
  • Autoimmune Hepatitis.
  • Metabolic Disorders: Wilson's disease (Copper), Hemochromatosis (Iron).
  • Biliary causes: Primary Biliary Cirrhosis.

b) Investigations:

  • Liver Function Tests (LFTs): Low Albumin, Elevated INR (Clotting factors), Elevated AST/ALT/GGT.
  • Abdominal Ultrasound: Shows shrunken/nodular liver, splenomegaly, ascites.
  • Viral Screen: HBsAg, HCV antibodies.
  • Autoimmune Screen: ANA, AMA.
  • Liver Biopsy: Gold standard for confirmation (fibrosis/nodules).
  • Endoscopy: To screen for Esophageal Varices (complication).
