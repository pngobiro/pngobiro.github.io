Section 1: Multiple Choice Questions

40 Marks
1.
Atypical pneumonia is caused by the following micro-organisms
a) Streptococci pneumonia
b) Cryptococcal
c) Chlamydia
d) H. Influenza
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Chlamydia

Explanation: Atypical pneumonia is typically caused by Mycoplasma pneumoniae, Chlamydia pneumoniae, or Legionella pneumophila. Streptococci and H. Influenza cause typical pneumonia.

2.
About antibiotics and pneumonias, choose the incorrect statement
a) Preferred treatment is with Ampicillin
b) Atypical pneumonia should be treated for not less than 14 days
c) Add anaerobic antibiotic if suspected aspiration
d) Levofloxacin can be used as an alternative for Erythromycin
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Preferred treatment is with Ampicillin

Explanation: While Ampicillin treats pneumonia, modern guidelines often prefer Amoxicillin (oral) or Penicillin G/Ceftriaxone (IV) for typical CAP, and Macrolides (Erythromycin/Azithromycin) for atypical. "Preferred treatment is with Ampicillin" is generally considered the least accurate or outdated statement in this context compared to the specific rules in B, C, and D.

3.
Which one of the following is not a feature of bronchiectasis?
a) Trachea deviation away from affected side
b) Halitosis
c) finger clubbing
d) large quantities of sputum
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Trachea deviation away from affected side

Explanation: Bronchiectasis typically causes lung fibrosis and collapse, which would pull the trachea towards the affected side, or cause no deviation. Deviation away is a sign of pleural effusion or tension pneumothorax.

4.
In the current management of acute bronchial asthma;
a) Aminophylline injection is the first line treatment drug
b) I.V. adrenaline is very important
c) Beta blockers are given
d) Nebulization with salbutamol is key in management
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Nebulization with salbutamol is key in management

Explanation: Short-acting beta-agonists (SABA) like Salbutamol via nebulizer or spacer are the first-line intervention. Beta blockers are contraindicated (cause bronchospasm). Aminophylline is a later-line adjunct.

5.
Haemoptysis could be due to the following except
a) Bronchial asthma
b) Pulmonary tuberculosis
c) Mitral stenosis
d) Bronchogenic carcinoma
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Bronchial asthma

Explanation: Asthma typically presents with wheeze, cough, and dyspnea. Hemoptysis is rare and not a classic feature unless there is a complication. TB, Mitral Stenosis, and Lung Cancer are classic causes of hemoptysis.

6.
Concerning rabies
a) Is a bacterial disease
b) Hydrophobia is a feature
c) All dogs have rabies
d) Anti-rabies vaccine given as stat dose is enough
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Hydrophobia is a feature

Explanation: Rabies is a viral disease characterized by hydrophobia (fear of water) due to pharyngeal spasms. The vaccine requires a course (0, 3, 7, 14, 28 days), not a stat dose.

7.
The following are true about herpes simplex virus type II except
a) Causes genital ulcers
b) Is sexually transmitted
c) Is caused by varicella zoster
d) It does not affect the rectum
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Is caused by varicella zoster

Explanation: Herpes Simplex Type II (HSV-2) is a distinct virus from Varicella Zoster Virus (VZV), which causes chickenpox and shingles.

8.
About Taenia Capitis
a) Is caused by taenia saginata
b) Is more common in adults
c) Causes alopecia
d) Drug of choice is albendazole
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Causes alopecia

Correction: This question likely contains a typo and meant Tinea Capitis (Ringworm of the scalp), which is a fungal infection causing alopecia. Taenia refers to tapeworms, which do not cause "Capitis" (scalp infection). If read as Tinea Capitis, option C is true. If read literally as tapeworm, none are standard.

9.
The following factors affect resistance of malaria except
a) Living in an endemic area
b) Sickle cell anaemia
c) Frequent malaria treatment
d) Antimalarial prophylaxis
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Frequent malaria treatment

Explanation: Frequent treatment does not confer host resistance; it treats the infection. Living in endemic areas confers partial immunity. Sickle cell trait provides genetic resistance. Prophylaxis provides chemical resistance.

10.
The following feature is consistent with stage IV WHO HIV clinical staging
a) Recurrent upper respiratory tract infection
b) Herpes zoster infection
c) Unexplained chronic diarrhea for 1 month
d) Cryptococcal meningitis
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Cryptococcal meningitis

Explanation: Extrapulmonary cryptococcosis (including meningitis) is an AIDS-defining condition (Stage IV). Chronic diarrhea >1 month is Stage III.

11.
Infective endocarditis
a) May follow a tooth extraction
b) I.v drug users are not at risk
c) a ten-day oral treatment is adequate for treatment
d) Haematuria is not a presentation
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) May follow a tooth extraction

Explanation: Dental procedures can cause transient bacteremia, leading to endocarditis in susceptible hearts. IV drug users are high risk. Treatment requires prolonged IV antibiotics (4-6 weeks). Microscopic hematuria is a common finding.

12.
Radiographic abnormalities associated with heart failure include the following
a) Consolidation
b) Cardiomegaly
c) Hilar lymphadenopathy
d) Cavitation
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Cardiomegaly

Explanation: An enlarged heart (Cardiomegaly) is a hallmark sign of heart failure on Chest X-ray. Other signs include pulmonary edema, Kerley B lines, and pleural effusion.

13.
Which one of the following is a high output state etiology of heart failure
a) Mitral stenosis
b) Thyrotoxicosis
c) Cardiomyopathy
d) Ischaemic heart disease
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Thyrotoxicosis

Explanation: High output failure occurs when the heart pumps more blood than normal but still cannot meet metabolic needs. Causes include severe anemia, thyrotoxicosis (hyperthyroidism), and AV fistulas.

14.
Which one of the following drugs can be used in hypertensive emergencies
a) Sodium Nitropruside
b) Hydrochlorothiazide
c) Amlodipine
d) Enalapril
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Sodium Nitropruside

Explanation: Sodium Nitroprusside is a potent IV vasodilator used for immediate BP reduction in hypertensive emergencies. The other options are oral agents for chronic management.

15.
Minor criteria in diagnosis of rheumatic fever
a) Fever
b) Carditis
c) Chorea
d) Arthritis
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Fever

Explanation: Jones Criteria: Major = Carditis, Polyarthritis, Chorea, Erythema Marginatum, Subcutaneous Nodules. Minor = Fever, Arthralgia, Elevated ESR/CRP, Prolonged PR interval.

16.
Which one of the following is a non-modifiable risk factor for stroke
a) Hypertension
b) Ethnicity
c) Smoking
d) Hypercoagubility state
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Ethnicity

Explanation: Non-modifiable factors include Age, Gender, Ethnicity, and Genetics. Hypertension and Smoking are modifiable.

17.
About CVA (stroke)
a) Ischaemic stroke is the most common type
b) Haemorrhagic stroke is the most common type
c) Haemorrhagic stroke is less severe
d) Aspirin is indicated if haemorrhagic stroke is confirmed
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Ischaemic stroke is the most common type

Explanation: Ischemic strokes account for approximately 85% of all strokes. Aspirin is contraindicated in hemorrhagic stroke.

18.
About lower motor neuron lesion
a) Paralysis affects movements rather than muscles
b) Wasting is pronounced
c) Tendon reflexes are increased
d) Babinski's sign is positive
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Wasting is pronounced

Explanation: LMN lesions cause muscle wasting, flaccidity, and loss of reflexes. UMN lesions cause spasticity, increased reflexes, and positive Babinski.

19.
Which of the following is a characteristic CSF finding in pyogenic meningitis
a) Clear colour
b) Positive indian ink
c) Elevated glucose
d) Lymphocytosis
Show Answer

Correct Answer: (None match perfectly, likely typo). Standard Pyogenic findings are: Turbid color, Low glucose, High protein, Neutrophilia.

Analysis: A (Clear) = Viral/TB. B (Indian Ink) = Cryptococcal. C (Elevated glucose) = Normal/Hyperglycemia (Meningitis causes LOW glucose). D (Lymphocytosis) = Viral/TB. The question might have intended "Cryptococcal" (Ans B) or "Viral" (Ans D), but "Pyogenic" means Bacterial. Given the options, there is no correct answer for *bacterial* meningitis.

20.
Which of the following anti-epileptic drugs is correctly matched with the type of seizures
a) Partial seizures - Diazepam
b) Absence seizure - Phenobarbitone
c) Tonic clonic - Phenytoin
d) Myoclonic jerks - Ethosuximide
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Tonic clonic - Phenytoin

Explanation: Phenytoin/Carbamazepine are standard for Tonic-Clonic. Absence seizures are treated with Ethosuximide or Valproate. Diazepam is for acute status epilepticus, not maintenance.

21.
In haemolytic jaundice there is
a) Pale stools
b) Pale conjunctiva, Mucous Membranes
c) Itching and scratching of the skin
d) Ca head of pancreas is a cause
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Pale conjunctiva, Mucous Membranes

Explanation: Hemolytic jaundice involves the destruction of RBCs, leading to Anemia (Pallor). Pale stools and itching are signs of Obstructive Jaundice.

22.
Which one is correct about viral hepatitis
a) Hepatitis A is transmitted via faeco-oral route
b) Hepatitis B rarely proceeds to chronicity
c) Is the only cause of chronic liver disease
d) Hepatitis A vaccine is routinely administered in children
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Hepatitis A is transmitted via faeco-oral route

Explanation: Hep A and E are fecal-oral. Hep B, C, D are blood/fluid borne. Hep B chronic rate is ~5-10% in adults (rare) but high in infants. Hep A vaccine is not routine in many national programs (like Kenya) unless specified.

23.
Which one of the following is incorrect concerning comprehensive management of sickle cell disease?
a) Hydroxyurea reduces the level of haemoglobin
b) Proguanil is indicated in prophylaxis against malaria
c) Lifelong supplementation of iron is recommended to deal with chronic anaemia
d) Pneumococcal vaccine plays a great role in management of infection
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Lifelong supplementation of iron is recommended to deal with chronic anaemia

Explanation: Iron supplementation is generally CONTRAINDICATED in sickle cell disease unless iron deficiency is proven, due to the risk of iron overload from chronic hemolysis and transfusions.

24.
Which of the following complications arise due to faulty techniques during blood transfusion
a) Infections
b) Air embolism
c) Iron overload
d) Transfusion reactions
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Air embolism

Explanation: Air embolism is caused by air entering the giving set (technique error). Infections are screening failures. Iron overload is a long-term complication. Reactions are matching issues.

25.
Which of the following is not part of the goals in DKA management
a) Correction of dehydration
b) Correction of acidosis
c) Identify and treat precipitating factor
d) Correct hyperglycemia to 4mmol/l to avoid further complications
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Correct hyperglycemia to 4mmol/l to avoid further complications

Explanation: Blood sugar should be lowered gradually to approx 10-12 mmol/L, then dextrose added. Lowering to 4mmol/L rapidly risks hypoglycemia and cerebral edema.

26.
True about hyperthyroidism
a) Weight gain is a feature
b) TSH is increased
c) Treatment involves thyroid replacement with levothyroxine
d) Propranolol is indicated
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Propranolol is indicated

Explanation: Propranolol (Beta blocker) is used to control adrenergic symptoms (tachycardia, tremors). Weight loss and LOW TSH are features. Levothyroxine treats HYPOthyroidism.

27.
Which of the following sexually transmitted infection has correctly matched statement
a) Trichomoniasis – painless genital ulcer
b) Syphilis – painful genital ulcer
c) Gonorrhea – whitish purulent vaginal discharge
d) Chancroid – itchy yellowish foul smelly discharge
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Gonorrhea – whitish purulent vaginal discharge

Explanation: Gonorrhea causes mucopurulent discharge. Syphilis causes a painless ulcer (chancre). Chancroid causes a painful ulcer. Trichomoniasis causes frothy yellow-green discharge.

28.
Side effects of prednisolone include
a) Hypotension
b) Peptic ulcers
c) Hypoglycaemia
d) Diarrhoea
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Peptic ulcers

Explanation: Steroids (prednisolone) can cause PUD, Hypertension, Hyperglycemia, and weight gain.

29.
A 16-year-old boy is from Kitui is brought to you with history of easy fatigability and general malaise for the last 3 weeks. On examination, the patient has poor nutritional status is pale with splenomegaly of 10cm, below subcostal margin. Temperature is 37.5°C. Which one is untrue.
a) This is typhoid fever
b) The most likely diagnosis is visceral leishmaniasis
c) Splenic aspirate for microscopy is diagnosis
d) The drug of choice is sodium stibogluconate
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) This is typhoid fever

Explanation: The presentation (Massive splenomegaly, wasting/fatigue, Kitui endemicity) is classic for Visceral Leishmaniasis (Kala-azar), not Typhoid. Therefore, statement A is the untrue one.

30.
Side effects of paracetamol;
a) Liver damage
b) Nausea and vomiting
c) Gastritis
d) Light headedness
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Liver damage

Explanation: Hepatotoxicity is the major serious side effect of paracetamol overdose.

31.
Which one of the following is an antidote in organophosphate poisoning
a) Atropine
b) Pyridooxine
c) Pralidoxime
d) Pabrinex
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Atropine (and c) Pralidoxime)

Explanation: Atropine is the physiological antidote used immediately to reverse muscarinic effects. Pralidoxime is a cholinesterase reactivator. Atropine is the most critical initial antidote.

32.
Concerning renal stones, the main chemical component is
a) Uric acid
b) Calcium
c) Magnesium
d) Cysteine
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Calcium

Explanation: Calcium oxalate and calcium phosphate stones make up the vast majority (approx 80%) of renal stones.

33.
Which one is not a feature of chronic renal failure
a) Uraemic frost
b) Anaemia
c) Gout
d) Treatment includes administering kayerxatate
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Treatment includes administering kayerxatate

Explanation: The question asks for a "Feature" (clinical sign/symptom). Kayexalate (Resonium) is a *treatment* for hyperkalemia, not a feature of the disease itself. Uremic frost, anemia, and gout are clinical features/complications.

34.
Which one of the following is not a contraindication of emetic drug administration
a) unconscious patient
b) acid poisoning
c) organophosphate poisoning
d) carbon monoxide poisoning
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) organophosphate poisoning

Explanation: Emetics are absolutely contraindicated in unconscious patients (aspiration risk) and corrosive/acid poisoning (re-burn injury). CO poisoning is inhaled, so emetics are irrelevant/useless. Organophosphate ingestion can technically be treated with gastric emptying (if recent), making it the "least" absolute contraindication among the list, although lavage is preferred over emetics due to aspiration risk.

35.
Pernicious anaemia
a) Is usually familial
b) Is due to lack of vitamin c
c) Is due to intrinsic factor deficiency
d) Is the commonest cause is vitamin b12 deficiency
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Is due to intrinsic factor deficiency

Explanation: Pernicious anemia is an autoimmune condition where Intrinsic Factor is lacking, preventing B12 absorption. Option D is poorly phrased ("Cause is B12 deficiency" - no, the *result* is B12 deficiency, the *cause* is Autoimmune/IF deficiency).

36.
Complications of scabies may lead to:
a) Nephrotic syndrome
b) Parasitaemia
c) Bacterial endocarditis
d) Scar formation
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Nephrotic syndrome

Explanation: Scabies lesions often get superinfected with Streptococcus pyogenes, which can lead to Post-Streptococcal Glomerulonephritis (Nephritic) and rarely Nephrotic syndrome.

37.
A known hypertensive patient presents with a blood pressure reading of 200/140mmHg. Which one of the following is the right thing to do
a) give the patient I.V fluids and monitor blood pressure
b) prescribe high doses of propranolol and allow the patient back home
c) lower the blood pressure to less than 100mmHg diastolic blood pressure to prevent complications
d) give hydralazine I.V. slowly over 5 minutes
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) give hydralazine I.V. slowly over 5 minutes

Explanation: This is a Hypertensive Emergency (BP > 180/120). Immediate but controlled reduction with IV agents (like Hydralazine or Labetalol) is required. Lowering BP too fast or too low (Option C) can cause stroke/ischemia.

38.
Observable features of iron deficiency anaemia include the following except
a) Pica
b) Angular stomatitis
c) Koilonychia
d) Jaundice
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Jaundice

Explanation: Jaundice (yellowing) indicates hemolysis or liver disease. Iron deficiency causes pallor (whiteness), not jaundice. Pica, angular stomatitis, and koilonychia (spoon nails) are classic signs.

39.
Which one of the following is an articular manifestation of rheumatoid arthritis
a) Joint deformity
b) Pericarditis
c) Scleritis
d) Vasculitis
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Joint deformity

Explanation: Articular means "related to joints". Joint deformity (e.g., Swan neck, Boutonniere) is articular. Pericarditis, Scleritis, and Vasculitis are Extra-articular manifestations.

40.
About cerebellar lesions, which one is not a finding
a) Rigidity
b) Tremors
c) Ataxia
d) Negative romberg’s test
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Rigidity

Explanation: Cerebellar lesions cause hypotonia (loss of tone), Ataxia, and Intention Tremors. Rigidity is a sign of Basal Ganglia disease (e.g., Parkinson's). Romberg's is typically negative (sway with eyes open and closed) vs sensory ataxia (positive - sway only with eyes closed).

Section 2: Short Answer Questions

40 Marks
1.
A 50-year-old male patient presents to you in the casualty with history of sudden onset of haematemesis and quantifies 1 liter of blood loss.
a) State 4 differential diagnosis (2 marks)
b) List the investigations for this patient (3 marks)
Show Answer

a) Differential Diagnosis:

  • Bleeding Peptic Ulcer Disease (PUD).
  • Esophageal Varices (Portal Hypertension).
  • Mallory-Weiss Tear.
  • Gastric Carcinoma / Esophageal Cancer.
  • Erosive Gastritis.

b) Investigations:

  • Full Hemogram (Hb/Platelets): To assess degree of anemia and clotting.
  • Urea, Electrolytes & Creatinine (UECs): Elevated Urea/Creatinine ratio suggests Upper GI bleed.
  • Grouping and Cross-match: For urgent blood transfusion.
  • Liver Function Tests (LFTs): To check for underlying liver disease.
  • Upper GI Endoscopy (OGD): Diagnostic and therapeutic (Gold standard).
2.
A 14-year-old boy presents to you with a two day history of passing scanty smoky urine. On examination he is noted to have impetigized lesions on the skin. He has edema and blood pressure reading is 160/100mmHg. State the management of this patient (5 marks)
Show Answer

Diagnosis: Acute Post-Streptococcal Glomerulonephritis (Nephritic Syndrome).

Management:

  • Admission: Admit for monitoring (BP, output, complications).
  • Rest: Strict bed rest until hematuria clears and BP normalizes.
  • Diet: Salt restriction and fluid restriction (to insensible loss + output).
  • Diuretics: IV Furosemide (Lasix) to manage edema and hypertension.
  • Antihypertensives: Nifedipine or Hydralazine if BP remains high despite diuretics.
  • Antibiotics: Penicillin (e.g., Benzathine Penicillin or Oral Pen V) to eradicate streptococcal infection.
  • Monitoring: Daily weight, BP charting, Fluid balance chart.
3.
Outline 5 predisposing factors to deep venous thrombosis (5 marks)
Show Answer
  1. Immobility/Stasis: Prolonged bed rest, long-distance travel, paralysis.
  2. Surgery/Trauma: Major orthopedic surgery (hip/knee), pelvic surgery.
  3. Hypercoagulability: Cancer/Malignancy, Genetic disorders (Factor V Leiden), Nephrotic syndrome.
  4. Hormonal: Pregnancy, Post-partum period, Oral Contraceptive Pills (OCPs).
  5. Obesity.
4.
Classify anemias giving an example in each classification (5 marks)
Show Answer
  • Microcytic Hypochromic Anemia: Iron Deficiency Anemia.
  • Macrocytic Anemia: Megaloblastic Anemia (B12/Folate deficiency).
  • Normocytic Normochromic Anemia: Anemia of Chronic Disease, Acute Hemorrhage, or Aplastic Anemia.
  • Hemolytic Anemia: Sickle Cell Disease, Malaria.
5.
State the clinical presentation of a patient with organophosphate poisoning (5 marks)
Show Answer

Based on Muscarinic (DUMBELS) and Nicotinic effects:

  • Secretions: Salivation, Lacrimation, Sweating, Bronchorrhea (frothy sputum).
  • Eye: Miosis (Pinpoint pupils), Blurred vision.
  • GI: Diarrhea, Vomiting, Abdominal cramps.
  • Respiratory: Bradycardia, Bronchospasm (Wheezing), Dyspnea.
  • Neuromuscular: Muscle fasciculations/twitching, Weakness, Paralysis.
  • CNS: Confusion, Seizures, Coma.
6.
State the management of a patient with cerebral malaria (5 marks)
Show Answer
  • Admission: Admit to ICU or High Dependency Unit.
  • Resuscitation (ABC): Maintain airway (lateral position to prevent aspiration), Oxygen if hypoxic.
  • Antimalarial: IV Artesunate (2.4mg/kg) at 0, 12, and 24 hours (First Line). Alternative: IV Quinine loading dose.
  • Seizure Control: IV Diazepam or Phenobarbital.
  • Hypoglycemia: Monitor RBS and correct with IV Dextrose 50% or 10%.
  • Fluids: Maintenance fluids carefully to avoid pulmonary edema.
  • Supportive: Antipyretics for fever, Nursing care for unconscious patient.
7.
Outline the work up of a 40-year-old woman who has been found with BP of 160/110mm/Hg in OPD (5 marks)
Show Answer
  • History: Confirm symptoms (headache, blurred vision), family history, risk factors (smoking, diet), drug history (OCPs).
  • Physical Exam: Retest BP to confirm, BMI assessment, Fundoscopy (Retinopathy), Cardiovascular exam (S4, apex beat).
  • Urinalysis: Check for proteinuria/hematuria (Renal damage).
  • UECs (Kidney Function): Creatinine and Electrolytes.
  • Blood Sugar (RBS/FBS): Screen for Diabetes.
  • Lipid Profile: Screen for Hyperlipidemia.
  • ECG: Check for Left Ventricular Hypertrophy (LVH).
8.
A 28-year-old female patient presents with acute shortness of breath, cough and wheeze. He has been having history of recurrent wheeze.
a) What is your impression (2 marks)
b) State your plan of management (3 marks)
Show Answer

a) Impression: Acute Severe Asthma (Status Asthmaticus).

b) Plan of Management:

  • Immediate: Prop up patient, Administer high flow Oxygen.
  • Bronchodilators: Nebulize with Salbutamol (Ventolin) and Ipratropium Bromide back-to-back.
  • Steroids: IV Hydrocortisone (200mg stat) or Oral Prednisolone (40-60mg).
  • Reassess: Check response (SpO2, air entry). If poor, consider IV Magnesium Sulphate or Aminophylline.

Section 3: Long Answer Question

20 Marks
1.
A 38-year-old comatose patient is brought to you at the OPD. Upon examination, you notice a fruity breath from the patient's mouth.
a) What is your most likely diagnosis (2 marks)
b) Outline the differential diagnosis (6 marks)
c) State your management plan (12 marks)
Show Answer

a) Diagnosis: Diabetic Ketoacidosis (DKA) with Coma.

Reason: Coma + Fruity (acetone) breath is classic for DKA.

b) Differential Diagnosis (Causes of Coma/Metabolic issues):

  • Hyperosmolar Hyperglycemic State (HHS).
  • Severe Hypoglycemia (Though breath differs, common in diabetics).
  • Uraemic Encephalopathy (Renal failure).
  • Alcoholic Ketoacidosis.
  • Salicylate (Aspirin) Poisoning.
  • Methanol Poisoning.
  • Cerebrovascular Accident (Stroke).

c) Management Plan:

  • Resuscitation: Assess Airway, Breathing, Circulation. Insert IV line. Catheterize to monitor output.
  • Fluid Resuscitation (Critical):
    • Normal Saline (0.9%).
    • 1 Liter stat (1st hour).
    • 1 Liter over next 2 hours.
    • 1 Liter over next 4 hours.
    • Switch to 5% Dextrose when RBS drops to <14 mmol/L.
  • Insulin Therapy:
    • Soluble Insulin (Regular) IV Infusion at 0.1 units/kg/hour.
    • Or IM route if IV infusion not possible.
    • Continue until ketosis resolves.
  • Potassium Replacement:
    • Insulin shifts K+ into cells causing hypokalemia.
    • Add KCl to IVF once K+ is <5.5 mmol/L and urine output is adequate.
  • Monitoring:
    • Hourly RBS.
    • 4-hourly UECs (Potassium).
    • Urinary Ketones.
    • Vitals (BP, Pulse, GCS).
  • Treat Precipitating Cause: Antibiotics for infection (e.g., UTI, Pneumonia).
