Section 1: Multiple Choice Questions

40 Marks
1.
Which one of the following is NOT a characteristic of cardiac pain?
A. Pain radiates to the neck jaw and upper or lower arms
B. The pain is dull and constricting
C. Angina pain occurs during exertion and is relieved by rest
D. A sharp chest pain triggered by deep breathing or coughing
Show Answer

Correct Answer: D. A sharp chest pain triggered by deep breathing or coughing

Explanation: Sharp pain triggered by breathing (pleuritic pain) is characteristic of respiratory conditions (like Pleurisy, Pneumonia, or PE) or Pericarditis, not typical cardiac ischemia. Cardiac pain is usually dull, constricting/crushing, and not affected by respiration.

2.
A patient with acute coronary syndrome presents to you. Which of the following is a likely sign on physical examination?
A. Nausea and vomiting
B. Anxiety
C. Breathlessness
D. Raised jugular venous pressure
Show Answer

Correct Answer: D. Raised jugular venous pressure

Explanation: Nausea, Anxiety, and Breathlessness are symptoms (subjective). Raised JVP is a physical sign (objective) that can occur in ACS, particularly if there is Right Ventricular Infarction or acute heart failure.

3.
A 55-year-old presents with cough, night sweats and gradual weight loss. The cough is productive with blood stained sputum. What is the impression?
A. Pulmonary edema
B. Pulmonary Tuberculosis
C. Pulmonary embolism
D. Bronchitis.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B. Pulmonary Tuberculosis

Explanation: The classic "B symptoms" (night sweats, weight loss) combined with a chronic cough and hemoptysis (blood-stained sputum) are the hallmark presentation of Pulmonary TB.

4.
A 21-year-old female is brought to the accident and emergency department with complains of sudden onset of breathlessness, chest tightness and wheezing. Physical examination reveal; respiratory rate of 30 breaths per minute, cyanosis and inability to complete a sentence in one breath what is your impression in this case?
A. Pneumonia
B. Acute asthmatic attack.
C. Pulmonary embolism
D. Diabetic ketoacidosis
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B. Acute asthmatic attack.

Explanation: The triad of breathlessness, chest tightness, and wheezing in a young patient, along with signs of severe respiratory distress (cyanosis, inability to complete sentences), points to a severe Acute Asthmatic Attack (Status Asthmaticus).

5.
A 60-year-old male presents with a cough that is productive with blood stains, fever and anorexia for 1 week. He also reports to have lost weight. Which investigation will you order to help diagnose him?
A. Urea and electrolytes
B. Sputum for gene expert
C. Thoracic ultrasound.
D. Full Haemogram
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B. Sputum for gene expert

Explanation: The symptoms suggest Pulmonary TB or Lung Cancer. Sputum GeneXpert is the specific diagnostic test to confirm TB (Mycobacterium tuberculosis). While a haemogram helps, it is not diagnostic.

6.
A 35-year-old male presents with difficulty in breathing and bilateral chest pain. He has history of heavy smoking for the last 5 years. On physical examination you elicit a stony dull percussion note and diminished breath sounds bilaterally. What is your diagnosis
A. Pleural effusion
B. Pneumonia
C. Emphysema.
D. Heart failure
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A. Pleural effusion

Explanation: "Stony dullness" on percussion is the pathognomonic sign of Pleural Effusion (fluid in the pleural space). Diminished breath sounds occur because fluid blocks sound transmission.

7.
A 14-year-old presents in the casualty department with history of easy fatigability and dyspnea on exertion. Physical examination findings reveal a murmur at the left 2nd intercostal space. What is the impression?
A. Coarctation of the aorta
B. Pulmonary stenosis
C. Patent ductus arteriosus
D. Atrial septal defect
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B. Pulmonary stenosis

Explanation: The Left 2nd Intercostal space is the Pulmonary Area. A murmur heard here, associated with fatigue/dyspnea in a young patient, is typically due to Pulmonary Stenosis (Ejection Systolic Murmur). PDA causes a continuous "machinery" murmur.

8.
Cardiac asthma occurs in
A. Left ventricular failure
B. Aortic stenosis.
C. Infective endocarditis
D. Right ventricular failure
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A. Left ventricular failure

Explanation: Cardiac asthma refers to wheezing caused by pulmonary edema and congestion due to Left Ventricular Failure (LVF), mimicking bronchial asthma.

9.
Rose spots are characteristic diagnostic finding in which of the following condition?
A. Brucellosis.
B. Enteric fever.
C. Shigellosis.
D. Cutaneous Tuberculosis.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B. Enteric fever.

Explanation: Rose spots (faint pink rash on the trunk) are a classic, though rare, physical sign of Enteric Fever (Typhoid).

10.
A 25-year-old female presented with 5 days’ history of frothy, foul smelling greenish yellow vaginal discharge. There is history of multiple unprotected peno-vaginal intercourse. What is the impression?
A. Trichomonas Vaginalis infection.
B. Candidiasis
C. Bacterial vaginosis
D. Pubic lice
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A. Trichomonas Vaginalis infection.

Explanation:

  • Trichomoniasis: Frothy, greenish-yellow, foul-smelling discharge + strawberry cervix.
  • Candidiasis: Thick, white, curd-like discharge + itching.
  • Bacterial Vaginosis: Thin, grey-white discharge with fishy odor (not typically frothy/green).

11.
A patient present with history of hotness that is increasing steadily, headache and joint pains for 5 days. On examination, the axillary temperature is 39.2°C. What is your impression?
A. Malaria
B. Cholera
C. Salmonellosis.
D. Brucellosis
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C. Salmonellosis.

Explanation: The "steady increase" in temperature (step-ladder pyrexia) combined with severe headache and high fever is characteristic of Typhoid fever (Salmonellosis). Malaria typically presents with irregular or spiking fevers.

12.
A patient present with hotness of the body, joint pains and headache and history of having been treated for malaria with Artemether-Lumefantrine a week ago. On examination, the temperature is 39.0° C, there is yellowness of the sclera. Laboratory results reveal Malaria parasites in peripheral blood film. What is your diagnosis
A. Uncomplicated malaria.
B. Relapsed malaria.
C. Malaria treatment failure.
D. Severe malaria.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: D. Severe malaria.

Explanation: The presence of jaundice (yellowness of sclera) is a sign of organ dysfunction/severe hemolysis, which classifies the condition as Severe Malaria according to WHO guidelines.

13.
Katayama syndrome is a common manifestation in?
A. Cysticercosis
B. Leishmaniasis
C. Schistosomiasis
D. Trypanosomiasis
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C. Schistosomiasis

Explanation: Katayama syndrome is acute schistosomiasis, a systemic hypersensitivity reaction to the migrating schistosome larvae and egg deposition, presenting with fever, urticaria, and eosinophilia.

14.
Kito, 18-year-old comes with complains of severe abdominal pain, offensive diarrhea alternating with constipation for the last 2 weeks. She reports to pass stool that is mucoid and sometimes with streaks of blood. Laboratory stool analysis reveal charcot leyden crystals.
A. Typhoid fever
B. Amoebic dysentery
C. Giardiasis
D. Malaria
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B. Amoebic dysentery

Explanation:

  • Symptoms: Bloody/mucoid stool, alternating diarrhea/constipation.
  • Lab: Charcot-Leyden crystals in stool are breakdown products of eosinophils and are often associated with parasitic infections like Amoebiasis (Entamoeba histolytica).

A fungal skin infection presenting with itchy and erythematous plaques affecting the groin area is.
A. Tinea cruris
B. Tinea pedis
C. Tinea corporis
D. Tinea capitis
Show Answer

16.
The drug of choice in the treatment of scabies is
A. Ketoconazole
B. Podophyllin
C. Azithromycin
D. Permethrin
Show Answer

Correct Answer: D. Permethrin

Explanation: Topical Permethrin 5% cream is the first-line treatment for Scabies. (Benzyl benzoate is an alternative). Ketoconazole is antifungal, Podophyllin is for warts, Azithromycin is antibiotic.

17.
A 20-year-old female patient presents with smoothening of the tongue, spooning of the nails and pallor. Laboratory investigations reveals haemoglobin level of 6.8 g/dl. What is your impression?
A. Megaloblastic anaemia.
B. Iron deficiency anaemia.
C. Aplastic anaemia.
D. Spurious anaemia.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B. Iron deficiency anaemia.

Explanation:

  • Koilonychia: Spoon-shaped nails.
  • Atrophic Glossitis: Smooth tongue.
  • These are specific signs of severe Iron Deficiency Anaemia.

18.
A 58-year-old female presents with heat intolerance, excessive sweating palpitations and tremors. On examination, there is irregular pulse, thin hair, lid lag and lid retraction. These findings are in consistent with?
A. Parkinson’s disease.
B. Hypoparathyroidism
C. Thyrotoxicosis
D. Myxedema
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C. Thyrotoxicosis

Explanation: The constellation of symptoms (Hypermetabolic state: heat intolerance, sweating) and signs (Eye signs: lid lag/retraction; Cardiac: Atrial Fibrillation/Irregular pulse) confirms Thyrotoxicosis (likely Graves' disease).

19.
A patient presents with history of vomiting fresh blood. What is the appropriate diagnostic modality for this patient?
A. Barium meal.
B. Oesophagogastroscopy.
C. Barium swallow.
D. Chest X-ray
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B. Oesophagogastroscopy.

Explanation: For Upper GI Bleeding (Hematemesis), Upper GI Endoscopy (Oesophagogastroscopy) is the gold standard for both diagnosis (locating the bleed) and therapeutic intervention (banding/clipping).

20.
Which one of the following is associated with acute Gastritis?
A. Intake of Brufen for 2 weeks.
B. Intake of misoprostol for 1 week.
C. Trauma.
D. Intestinal helminthiasis.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A. Intake of Brufen for 2 weeks.

Explanation: NSAIDs (like Brufen/Ibuprofen) inhibit prostaglandins which protect the gastric mucosa, directly leading to Acute Gastritis and ulceration. Misoprostol is actually protective.

21.
A 25-year-old female patient presents with moderate to severe head pain, pulsating bilaterally. There is history of intake of red wine 5 glasses the previous night in a house party. What is the impression?
A. Tension headache.
B. Migraine headache.
C. Cluster headache.
D. Malaria.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B. Migraine headache.

Explanation: Although Migraine is often unilateral, it can be bilateral. The key features pointing to Migraine are the pulsating quality and the specific trigger (Red Wine is a classic migraine trigger due to tyramine/sulfites). Tension headache is "band-like/dull", not pulsating.

22.
The characteristic anchovy sauce brown chocolate appearance of aspirated hepatic lesion is a diagnostic feature of?
A. Amoebic liver abscess
B. Filariasis
C. Gastric carcinoma
D. Pancreatitis
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A. Amoebic liver abscess

Explanation: "Anchovy sauce" or "Chocolate pus" is the pathognomonic description of the aspirate from an Amoebic Liver Abscess (caused by Entamoeba histolytica).

23.
Which of the following is a characteristic feature of ulcerative colitis in a patient?
A. There is severe oral ulceration
B. There is perianal fistula
C. Symptoms include rectal bleeding, passage of mucus and bloody diarrhea
D. Diarrhea is watery and not blood stained
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C. Symptoms include rectal bleeding, passage of mucus and bloody diarrhea

Explanation: Ulcerative Colitis almost always involves the rectum and presents with bloody diarrhea and mucus. Oral ulcers and fistulas (Options A and B) are characteristic of Crohn's Disease, not UC.

24.
A 30-year-old male presented with right upper quadrant pain that radiates to the right shoulder tip accompanied by fever. Examination finding revealed right hypochondrial tenderness and rigidity worse on inspiration. What is your diagnosis?
A. Pancreatitis
B. Acute Cholecystitis
C. Fatty liver
D. Portal hypertension
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B. Acute Cholecystitis

Explanation:

  • Murphy's Sign: Tenderness/arrest of inspiration on palpation of RUQ.
  • Referred Pain: Radiates to right shoulder tip (Boas' sign) via phrenic nerve irritation.
  • These are classic for Acute Cholecystitis.

25.
Which of the following is a contraindication to renal biopsy?
A. Coagulation disorder
B. Inadequately explained acute kidney injury
C. Nephrotic syndrome
D. Chronic kidney disease in normal kidney size
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A. Coagulation disorder

Explanation: Renal biopsy carries a high risk of bleeding. Uncontrolled coagulopathy (bleeding disorder) is an absolute contraindication. Options B and C are actually indications for biopsy.

27.

A clinical presentation associated with renal cell carcinoma is?

31.
A 70-year-old presents to you with chronic joint pains and morning stiffness. Which drug will you prescribe for long term relief of his pain?
A. Prednisolone
B. Paracetamol
C. Diclofenac
D. Ibuprofen
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B. Paracetamol

Explanation: In a 70-year-old with chronic joint pain (likely Osteoarthritis), NSAIDs (Diclofenac/Ibuprofen) carry high risks of GI bleeding and renal impairment. Steroids (Prednisolone) have severe long-term side effects. Paracetamol is the safest first-line analgesic for long-term use in the elderly.

A. Osteoarthritis
B. Osteomyelitis
C. Rheumatoid arthritis
D. Arthritis.

Correct Answer: C. Rheumatoid arthritis (Correction: Rheumatic Fever)

33.
Which of the following is a risk factor of post renal acute Kidney Injury?
A. Dehydration
B. Amphotericin B
C. Dislodged renal calculi
D. Rhabdomyolysis.

Correct Answer: C. Dislodged renal calculi

34.
A patient presents with sudden onset of ascending paralysis with a one-week history of diarrhea. What is your impression?
A. Poliomyelitis
B. Gullaine Barre syndrome
C. Ben Sequard Syndrome
D. Acute flaccid paralysis (AFP)

35.
A 13-year-old boy presents with history of attempted suicide by ingestion of herbicide Diazinon. On examination, there is muscular fasciculation, excessive tearing, pin point pupils and diarrhea. Which of the following accounts for this presentation?
A. Excessive destruction of acetylcholine at the post synaptic receptors.
B. Accumulation of acetylcholine at the post synaptic receptors.
C. Deficiency of acetylcholinesterase.
D. Hypersecretion of catecholamine.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B. Accumulation of acetylcholine at the post synaptic receptors.

Explanation: Diazinon is an Organophosphate. It works by inhibiting acetylcholinesterase. This prevents the breakdown of Acetylcholine (ACh), leading to the accumulation of ACh at synapses, causing continuous stimulation (Muscarinic/Nicotinic effects).

36.
Which of the following is a minor manifestation of rheumatic fever based on the modified Ducket-Jones criteria?
A. Chorea
B. Fever
C. Subcutaneous nodules
D. Migratory poly arthritis.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B. Fever

Explanation:

  • Major Criteria (JONES): Joints (Polyarthritis), O (Carditis), Nodules, Erythema marginatum, Sydenham's Chorea.
  • Minor Criteria: Fever, Arthralgia, Elevated ESR/CRP, Prolonged PR interval.

37.
Which one of the following is a cause of painful genital ulcer?
A. Herpes simplex
B. Syphilis
C. HIV
D. Haemophilus ducreyi
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A. Herpes simplex

Explanation:

  • Painful Ulcers: Herpes Simplex (multiple vesicles/ulcers) and Chancroid (Haemophilus ducreyi).
  • Painless Ulcers: Syphilis (Chancre), LGV.
  • Both A and D cause painful ulcers. However, Herpes is generally more common and causes "painful genital ulcer" (singular/plural). H. ducreyi causes a "soft chancre" which is very painful. In many contexts, Herpes is the primary answer for "painful".

40.
A patient presents with uncoordinated repetitive jerky movements of the limbs, with stiffening and frothing for 5 minutes per episode. Which one of the following will cause this presentation?
A. Alcohol withdrawal syndrome
B. Cocaine use
C. Overdose of benzodiazepine
D. Infections such as encephalitis
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A. Alcohol withdrawal syndrome

Explanation: The patient is having a Generalized Tonic-Clonic Seizure ("stiffening" = tonic, "jerky movements" = clonic).

  • Alcohol Withdrawal: Common cause of "Rum Fits" (withdrawal seizures) in adults.
  • Benzo overdose: Causes CNS depression/coma, not seizures (withdrawal does).
  • Cocaine: Can cause seizures but usually associated with extreme agitation/sympathetic overdrive.

Show Answer

Answer:

  1. Hypotension: Low blood pressure (Part of Beck's Triad).
  2. Distended Neck Veins (Raised JVP): Due to impaired right heart filling (Part of Beck's Triad).

45.
Manage a patient diagnosed of organophosphate poisoning (5 Marks)
Show Answer

Answer:

  1. Resuscitation (ABC): Secure airway, suction secretions, give Oxygen.
  2. Decontamination: Remove clothes, wash skin with soap/water.
  3. Atropine: Give IV Atropine (2-4mg) stat, repeat every 5-10 mins until signs of atropinization (dry mouth/pupil dilation).
  4. Antidote: Pralidoxime (PAM) to regenerate cholinesterase.
  5. Seizure control: Diazepam if needed.

Section 3: Long Answer Question

20 Marks
49.
A 37-year-old male patient presents with history of sudden onset repetitive jerky movements preceded by a cry. There is an associated frothing on the mouth, urine and stool incontinence and deep sleep for 30 minutes after each episode of similar presentation.

a) Enumerate the differential diagnoses. (4 Marks)

b) Manage this patient. (16 Marks)

Show Answer

Answer:

The case describes a Generalized Tonic-Clonic Seizure (Grand Mal) with post-ictal state (deep sleep).

a) Differential Diagnoses (Causes):

  1. Epilepsy: Idiopathic or secondary.
  2. Infections: Meningitis, Cerebral Malaria, Encephalitis.
  3. Metabolic: Hypoglycemia, Hyponatremia.
  4. Toxicology: Alcohol withdrawal, Drug overdose/toxicity.
  5. Intracranial: Head trauma, Tumor, Stroke.

b) Management:

1. Immediate/First Aid (During Seizure):

  • Ensure safety: Remove dangerous objects.
  • Protect head with cushion.
  • Do NOT restrain or put objects in mouth.
  • Turn to lateral recovery position to prevent aspiration.

2. Emergency Medical Management:

  • Airway/Breathing: Ensure patent airway, give O2.
  • Stop the Seizure: IV Diazepam 10-20mg slowly (or Rectal diazepam).
  • Loading Dose: If seizures recur, load with Phenytoin (15-20mg/kg IV) or Phenobarbitone.

3. Investigations:

  • RBS (Rule out hypoglycemia).
  • BS for MPS (Malaria).
  • U&Es (Electrolytes).
  • CT Scan/MRI (Structural causes).
  • EEG (Electroencephalogram).

4. Long-term Management:

  • Start maintenance Anti-Epileptic Drugs (AEDs) like Carbamazepine or Sodium Valproate.
  • Counseling: Adherence to meds, avoid triggers (alcohol, sleep deprivation).
  • Safety advice: Avoid swimming alone, driving, working with machinery.
