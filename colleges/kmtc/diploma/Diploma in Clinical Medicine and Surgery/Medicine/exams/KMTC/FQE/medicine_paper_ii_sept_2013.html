Section A: True / False Questions

Negative Marking
1.
Major manifestations of Jones criteria in diagnosis of rheumatic fever includes:
Statement Answer
a. Carditis TRUE
b. Chorea TRUE
c. Migratory poly arthritis TRUE
d. Erythema marginatum TRUE
e. Fever FALSE (Minor Criteria)
2.
The following are the main reasons for altering a patient's ARV drug regimen:
Statement Answer
a. Pregnancy TRUE (Some drugs like Efavirenz were previously avoided in 1st trimester)
b. Drug interactions TRUE (e.g., with TB treatment)
c. Treatment failure TRUE (Clinical, Immunological, or Virological)
d. Minor drug toxicity FALSE (Managed symptomatically, change only for major toxicity)
e. Interruption of drug FALSE
3.
A 24 year old woman has been on TB treatment for 3 months. Recently she tested positive for HIV test. She needs:
Statement Answer
a. Pregnancy test TRUE (Before starting ART if Efavirenz is considered)
b. CD4 count TRUE (To determine baseline immunity)
c. Total lymphocyte count TRUE
d. Renal profile tests TRUE (Especially if Tenofovir is considered)
e. Cotrimoxazole therapy TRUE (Prophylaxis against opportunistic infections)
4.
Signs and symptoms of infective endocarditis includes:
Statement Answer
a. Weight loss TRUE
b. Night sweats TRUE
c. Conjunctival haemorrhage TRUE (Petechiae)
d. Cough FALSE (Unless heart failure develops)
e. Janeway lesions TRUE
5.
Bronchial asthma triggers include:
Statement Answer
a. Smoking TRUE
b. Injections FALSE
c. Drugs TRUE (e.g., Aspirin, Beta-blockers)
d. Emotional stress TRUE
e. Acid reflux TRUE (GERD)
6.
Pulmonary tuberculosis:
Statement Answer
a. Major diagnostic test is tuberculin skin test FALSE (Sputum microscopy/GeneXpert is major)
b. They present with drenching night sweats TRUE
c. Chronic cough for more than two weeks is significant TRUE
d. Treatment for a new case takes 8 months FALSE (Current standard is 6 months)
e. Treatment takes at least nine months FALSE (Except for osteo/meningeal TB)
7.
The following are classes of antihypertensive:
Statement Answer
a. Diuretics e.g. Lasix TRUE
b. Beta blockers TRUE
c. Angiotension converting enzymes inhibitors TRUE
d. Direct vasodilators TRUE
e. Calcium channel blockers TRUE
8.
The following parasitic infections are associated with AIDS:
Statement Answer
a. Cryptococcal meningitis TRUE (Fungal)
b. Histoplasmosis TRUE (Fungal)
c. Herpes zoster TRUE (Viral)
d. Pneumocystic carinii pneumonia TRUE (Fungal/Protozoal historical classification)
e. Toxoplasmosis TRUE (Protozoal)
12.
Which of the following is the most common symptom of peptic ulcer disease:
Statement Answer
a. Epigastric pain TRUE
b. Nausea FALSE (Associated but not most common)
c. Diarrhea FALSE
d. Anorexia FALSE
e. Mid back pain FALSE (Suggests penetration)

Section B: Multiple Choice Questions

Single Best Answer
1.
Which of the following regime for a new TB case (adult)?
a) 2 RHZ/4RHE
b) 2 RHZE/4HRE
c) 2RHZE/4RH
d) 1RHZE/5RH
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) 2RHZE/4RH

Explanation: The standard regimen for new adult TB cases is 2 months of intensive phase with Rifampicin, Isoniazid, Pyrazinamide, and Ethambutol, followed by 4 months of continuation phase with Rifampicin and Isoniazid.

2.
One of the following is a way of diagnosing rheumatic fever in patient
a) 2 major criteria
b) 2 major and 2 minor
c) 2 major and 1 minor
d) 2 minor
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) 2 major and 1 minor

Explanation: Diagnosis requires 2 Major criteria OR 1 Major + 2 Minor criteria, along with evidence of antecedent Streptococcal infection.

3.
Which one of the following anti tuberculosis drugs is used for prophylaxis
a) Rifampicin
b) Isoniazide
c) Pyrazinamide
d) Erthambutol
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Isoniazide

Explanation: Isoniazid Preventive Therapy (IPT) is the standard prophylaxis for latent TB infection.

4.
The following is correctly matched
a) Tinea corporis – fungal infection of the trunk
b) Tinea carpitis – fungal infection of toes
c) Tinea cruris – fungal infection of the scalp
d) Tinea pedis – fungal infection of the pharynx
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Tinea corporis – fungal infection of the trunk

Correction: Tinea capitis is scalp (not toes), Tinea cruris is groin (not scalp), Tinea pedis is feet (not pharynx).

5.
Herpes simplex virus type II has the following features except
a) Causes genital ulcers
b) It is a sexually transmitted disease
c) It is caused by varicella zoster virus
d) Does not affect the rectum
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) It is caused by varicella zoster virus

Explanation: This is an "Except" question. HSV-2 is caused by the Herpes Simplex Virus, not Varicella Zoster Virus (which causes Chickenpox/Shingles). Note: Option D is also technically incorrect as HSV can cause proctitis, but C is a definitive classification error.

6.
Immune reconstitution syndrome is the
a) Worsening of the clinical condition of the patient
b) Appearance of new symptoms after starting ART
c) Major side effects caused by some ARV drugs
d) Is the same with viral load count
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Worsening of the clinical condition of the patient

Explanation: IRIS (Immune Reconstitution Inflammatory Syndrome) is a paradoxical worsening of pre-existing infectious processes following the initiation of ART.

8.
HIV makes the body weak and unable to fight opportunistic disease by
a) Destroying red blood cells
b) Destroying white blood cells
c) Destroying liver cells
d) Suppressing plasma
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Destroying white blood cells

Explanation: HIV specifically targets CD4+ T-lymphocytes (White Blood Cells), which are crucial for the immune response.

9.
Peripheral neuropathy may be a complication of the following drugs
a) Pyridoxine
b) Isoniazide
c) Dapsone
d) Stavudine
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Stavudine (and b Isoniazid)

Explanation: Both Isoniazid and Stavudine (d4T) cause peripheral neuropathy. However, Stavudine is notorious for this in the context of ARVs. The paper likely targets Stavudine here.

10.
The following is a urinary tract infection
a) Gonorrhea
b) Candidiasis
c) Balanitis
d) Pyelonephritis
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Pyelonephritis

Explanation: Pyelonephritis is an infection of the kidney (Upper UTI). Gonorrhea/Candidiasis are reproductive tract infections (though Gonorrhea can cause urethritis). Balanitis is inflammation of the glans penis.

12.
The following are bacteria infections except
a) Shigella
b) Staphylococcal food poisoning
c) Cholera
d) Giardiasis
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Giardiasis

Explanation: Giardiasis is caused by Giardia lamblia, which is a protozoa (parasite), not a bacteria.

14.
A long term complication of diabetes mellitus is
a) Cataract
b) Hypoglyceamia
c) Diabetic ketoacidosis
d) Dehydration
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Cataract

Explanation: Cataracts are a chronic (long-term) complication associated with diabetes. Hypoglycemia, DKA, and Dehydration are acute complications.
