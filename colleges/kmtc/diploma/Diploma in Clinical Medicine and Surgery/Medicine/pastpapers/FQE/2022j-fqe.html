1.
A 60 years old male with a 2 years' history of malignancy of colon was reported to be well, but one week ago he had been treated for deep venous thrombosis and now he reports sudden chest pain on right associated with difficulty in breathing, which of the following is most likely diagnosis?
a) Severe pneumonia
b) Pulmonary thrombosis
c) Status asthmatics
d) Could – disease
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Pulmonary thrombosis (Pulmonary Embolism)

The clinical picture is classic for Pulmonary Embolism (PE).

  • Risk Factors: Malignancy (hypercoagulable state) and recent Deep Vein Thrombosis (DVT) are major risk factors.
  • Symptoms: Sudden onset chest pain (pleuritic) and dyspnea (difficulty breathing) are hallmark signs.
  • Note: While "Pulmonary thrombosis" is the option given, the condition is clinically referred to as Pulmonary Embolism (migration of the thrombus from the DVT to the lungs).
2.
Josphat was walking home and reported to have suffered snake bite which is suspected to be black mamba on examination leg swollen, dark and areas oozing blood which is the immediate help to give to Josphat before reaching hospital?
a) Place a stone and tie around the bite site
b) Apply a tourniquet above bite and one below the bite site
c) Slightly slice the bite area and Suck out the poison making sure you do not swallow
d) Just reassure and immobilize the patient as you seek for help
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Just reassure and immobilize the patient as you seek for help

  • Reassurance: Keeps the patient calm, lowering heart rate and slowing the spread of venom.
  • Immobilization: Movement promotes lymphatic spread of venom. The limb should be kept still.
  • Contraindications: Tourniquets (cause ischemia/gangrene), cutting/sucking (introduces infection and is ineffective), and "black stones" are not recommended standard medical practices.
3.
A patient is reported to have started coughing out blood and started sweating, dizziness, cold extremities, distant heartbeat. Which of the following statements is true?
a) He has lower gastrointestinal bleeding
b) Give a lot of raw eggs to remove the poison
c) Encourage him to continue coughing
d) He has hypovolemic shock
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) He has hypovolemic shock

The symptoms presented are classic signs of uncompensated shock due to blood loss (haemoptysis/internal bleeding):

  • Signs of Shock: Sweating (diaphoresis), dizziness (hypotension/cerebral hypoperfusion), cold extremities (peripheral vasoconstriction), and distant/weak heartbeat.
  • Incorrect Options: Coughing blood is haemoptysis (respiratory/upper GI), not lower GI. Encouraging coughing worsens bleeding. Raw eggs are not a treatment.

5.
My grandmother has lost left nasal fold, unable to completely close her left eye and her tongue is deviated to the right. Which of the following statements is true?
a) Test for malaria parasite
b) She has a psychotic disorder
c) That's a typical presentation of facial palsy
d) Prescribe face emollients and advise her to stay away from sunlight
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) That's a typical presentation of facial palsy

The presentation involves Cranial Nerve VII (Facial Nerve):

  • Signs: Loss of nasolabial fold and inability to close the eye (Lagophthalmos) on the ipsilateral side are classic signs of Bell's Palsy (Lower Motor Neuron facial palsy).
  • Note on Tongue: Tongue deviation involves CN XII (Hypoglossal). Deviation to the right usually implies a right-side lesion (tongue licks the wound). This suggests a more complex pathology (e.g., stroke or base of skull lesion) rather than simple Bell's palsy, but among the options provided, the facial signs point strongly to facial nerve pathology.
6.
What is the best action to take when your neighbour starts to have seizures?
a) Hold arms and legs tight
b) Offer warm coffee or oral glucose
c) Call for ambulance without delay
d) Let them be on a soft surface and pillow under the head
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Let them be on a soft surface and pillow under the head

First aid for seizures focuses on preventing injury:

b) Nowadays there is cure for mild disease

Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Lifelong disease modifying drugs are prescribed

a) Insert a gauge 16 urethral catheter
b) Give meloxicam as is a good analgesic and see him after 2 days

Correct Answer: c) Single intramuscular injection of ceftriaxone is used for treatment

The presentation is classic for Gonococcal Urethritis (Gonorrhea).

10.
Which of the following key messages regarding sexually transmitted infections are true?
a) Condom use is recommended only when the sexual partner has signs of infection
b) All patients treated for sexually transmitted infections should be tested for HIV
c) Sharing of drugs with spouses is ok when the partners refuses to come for treatment
d) All sexually transmitted infections are symptomatic on day 2
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) All patients treated for sexually transmitted infections should be tested for HIV

  • Protocol: There is a strong epidemiological link between STIs and HIV transmission. Any patient presenting with an STI must be counseled and offered HIV testing (Provider Initiated Testing and Counseling - PITC).
  • False options: Condoms should be used consistently (asymptomatic shedding occurs). Partner notification and treatment is mandatory, but "sharing drugs" is poor practice; the partner needs their own assessment/prescription. Many STIs (like Chlamydia) can be asymptomatic for long periods.

Is a soil transmitted helminth, commonest cause of anaemia and presents with eosinophilia, Which of the following organisms fits the above description?
a) Ancylostoma duodenale
b) Onchocerca volvulus
c) Schistosoma mansoni
d) Loaloa
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Ancylostoma duodenale

25.
Which of the following drugs is not used in treatment of angina?
a) Nifedipine
b) Amlodipine
c) Atenolol
d) Verapamil
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Nifedipine (specifically short-acting)

Note on ambiguity: All listed drugs (Calcium Channel Blockers and Beta Blockers) are theoretically anti-anginals. 41.
A 13-year-old girl is reported to have ingested pyrophosphate 30 minutes ago. Outline 5 important steps to consider while giving care. (5 Marks)
Show Answer

Answer:

Management of Organophosphate/Pyrophosphate poisoning:

  1. Resuscitation (ABCDE): Secure Airway, Breathing (administer oxygen), and Circulation.
  2. Decontamination: Remove contaminated clothing and wash skin thoroughly to prevent further absorption.
  3. Atropinization: Administer Atropine IV (to dry secretions and counteract muscarinic effects) until signs of atropinization (dry mouth, dilated pupils, tachycardia) appear.
  4. Antidote Administration: Give Pralidoxime (PAM) if available to reactivate cholinesterase (most effective if given early).
  5. Supportive Care & Monitoring: Monitor vitals, treat seizures (diazepam), and observe for respiratory failure.
42.
List 5 factors that aggravates a gouty attack in a patient already diagnosed with gout (5 Marks)
Show Answer

Answer:

  1. Dietary intake: High purine foods (red meat, organ meats, seafood).
  2. Alcohol consumption: Especially beer and spirits (interferes with uric acid excretion).
  3. Dehydration: Increases uric acid concentration.
  4. Medications: Thiazide diuretics, low-dose aspirin.
  5. Rapid weight loss or starvation: Ketosis inhibits uric acid excretion.
  6. Trauma or Surgery: Acute physical stress.
43.
During management of cardiac arrest at community level, the term chain of survival is used.

a) What is chain of survival? (1 Mark)

b) Describe the steps in chain of survival (4 Marks)

Show Answer

Answer:

a) Chain of Survival: It is a series of critical actions that, when executed in sequence, optimize the likelihood of survival from sudden cardiac arrest.

b) Steps in Chain of Survival (Out-of-Hospital):

  1. Early Recognition and Call for Help: Recognize arrest and activate EMS (Emergency Medical Services).
  2. Early CPR: Immediate high-quality chest compressions to maintain flow to heart and brain.
  3. Early Defibrillation: Use of AED (Automated External Defibrillator) to restart the heart.
  4. Early Advanced Cardiac Life Support (ACLS): Care by paramedics/ambulance crew.
  5. (Bonus/5th link) Post-Resuscitation Care: Hospital treatment and recovery.

  4. Stress: Physiological stress (trauma, burns, major surgery).
  5. Smoking: Interferes with mucosal healing.

49.
A 28-year-old female was brought to your health facility having been found along the road by a good Samaritan. On examination she has 18 breaths/min, pulse rate of 78 beats/min, temperature of 36.8°c and, blood pressure of 110/70mm/Hg. She is unresponsive to any stimuli. You make an impression of a comatose patient.

a) What are the possible causes of coma in this patient? (8 Marks)

b) How will you investigate this patient? (4 Marks)

c) Discuss supportive management of this comatose patient. (8 Marks)

Show Answer

Answer:

a) Possible Causes of Coma (Differential Diagnosis):

Use the mnemonic AEIOU TIPS to generate causes.

  1. Alcohol/Drugs: Acute alcohol intoxication or overdose (sedatives/opioids) - found on road implies possible foul play or self-poisoning.
  2. Traumatic Brain Injury: Subdural/Epidural hematoma (found on road, possible hit and run).
  3. Hypoglycemia: Severe low blood sugar (common cause of sudden coma).
  4. Hyperglycemia: DKA (Diabetic Ketoacidosis) or HHS.
  5. Infection: Meningitis, Cerebral Malaria, Encephalitis.
  6. Post-Ictal State: Following a severe seizure (Epilepsy).
  7. Stroke: CVA (hemorrhagic or ischemic), though less common in 28yo without risk factors.
  8. Uremia/Organ Failure: Renal or hepatic failure.

b) Investigations:

  1. Random Blood Sugar (RBS): To rule out hypo/hyperglycemia immediately.
  2. Blood Slide for Malaria Parasite (BS for MPS): To rule out cerebral malaria.
  3. Full Hemogram (CBC): To check for infection (WBC) or anemia.
  4. Urinalysis: Check for ketones (DKA), protein/blood (Renal), or toxicology screen if available.
  5. CT Scan Head (Non-contrast): To rule out intracranial hemorrhage or space-occupying lesions.
  6. UECs/LFTs: To check kidney and liver function.

c) Supportive Management:

Primary Goal: Stabilize the patient and prevent secondary brain injury.

  1. Airway: Ensure patent airway. Insert Guedel airway if necessary to prevent tongue fallback. Suction secretions.
  2. Breathing: Administer oxygen if SpO2 < 94%. Monitor respiratory rate.
  3. Circulation: Secure IV access (cannula). Start IV Fluids (Normal Saline) to maintain perfusion.
  4. Dextrose: If RBS is low (or not yet known in emergency), administer IV 50% Dextrose (preceded by Thiamine if alcohol suspected) to treat potential hypoglycemia.
  5. Positioning: Place in recovery position (left lateral decubitus) to prevent aspiration of vomit. Turn every 2 hours to prevent bedsores (pressure area care).
  6. Catheterization: Insert urethral catheter to monitor urine output (renal perfusion) and keep bladder empty.
  7. Nursing Care: Eye care (tape eyes shut/apply saline to prevent corneal drying), mouth care, and maintain warmth.
  8. Monitoring: Glasgow Coma Scale (GCS) monitoring every 15-30 mins, Vitals monitoring.
