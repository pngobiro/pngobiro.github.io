1.
A 27-year-old patient previously treated for brucellosis, presents to your clinic with history of dull headache and undulating fever. Repeat Brucella Antigen Test reveals elevated titres for BA and BM. Which organism is implicated in this recurrent chronic infection?
A. Brucella Suis
B. Brucella canis
C. Brucella melitensis
D. Brucella abortus
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C. Brucella melitensis

Explanation: Brucella melitensis (associated with goats/sheep) is the most virulent species and is most commonly associated with severe, chronic, and relapsing brucellosis (Undulating/Malta Fever). The high titers and recurrence point to this species.

2.
A 30-year-old lady presents with history of headache and joint pains. She reports to be under treatment for peptic ulcer disease. Stool SAT test reveals the presence of Salmonella antigens. In this patient, which of the following host factors is associated with increased susceptibility to salmonellosis?
A. Advanced age
B. During treatment of peptic ulcer disease
C. Blood group A+
D. African race
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B. During treatment of peptic ulcer disease

Explanation: Gastric acid is a major defense barrier against Salmonella. Patients on PUD treatment (PPIs or antacids) have reduced gastric acidity (hypochlorhydria), which significantly lowers the infectious dose required for Salmonella to cause infection.

3.
A patient diagnosed with Cholera is referred to the county referral hospital emergency department. Treatment history at the referring facility indicate administration of Loperamide during treatment. Which of the following is an anticipated risk with the management administered prior referral?
A. Drug addiction
B. Risk of paralytic ileus
C. Causes increased episode of bloody diarrhea
D. Gastric perforation
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B. Risk of paralytic ileus

Explanation: Loperamide is an antimotility drug. In invasive or toxin-mediated diarrheas like Cholera, stopping motility prevents the flushing out of toxins, leading to sequestration of fluid in the bowel, toxic megacolon, and paralytic ileus.

4.
5 patients are referred to your facility with episodes of vomiting, diarrhea and abdominal pains 24 hours after consuming food while attending a pool party. Stool analysis reveals motile clostridium botulinum bacteria. Which of the following describe the patients condition in relation to the food poisoning?
A. Food infection
B. Food contamination
C. Food expiry
D. Food sepsis
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A. Food infection

Explanation:

  • Food Infection: Caused by the ingestion of viable pathogens (bacteria/viruses) that multiply in the gut (e.g., Salmonella).
  • Food Intoxication: Caused by ingestion of pre-formed toxins (e.g., Staph aureus).
  • While C. botulinum is classically an intoxication, the presence of the bacteria in stool suggests ingestion of the organism (Infection). However, pure Botulism is neurotoxic. Given the GI symptoms (vomiting/diarrhea), it mimics a food infection scenario.

5.
You are asked to review a patient with recurrent episodes of intestinal amoebiasis. Which of the following is a cause of the relapse?
A. Administration of metronidazole alone at the symptomatic phase
B. Administration of Iodoquinol at the symptomatic phase
C. Administration of metronidazole and Ditoxanide furoate
D. Immune suppression
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A. Administration of metronidazole alone at the symptomatic phase

Explanation: Metronidazole is effective against the tissue-invasive trophozoites (treating symptoms) but is ineffective against cysts in the bowel lumen. If a luminal amoebicide (like Diloxanide furoate or Paromomycin) is not given, the remaining cysts can cause relapse/re-infection.

A. Administration of antibiotics.
B. Isolation of infected patients.
C. Fluid rehydration therapy.

Correct Answer: C. Fluid rehydration therapy.

Explanation: Death in cholera is caused by severe dehydration and electrolyte imbalance. Immediate and aggressive rehydration (IV or Oral) is the absolute priority to save lives. Antibiotics are secondary.

9.
You are treating patients in a medical camp and you are tasked with performing malaria rapid diagnostic test. You are informed that the malaria parasite responsible for most malaria cases in that area is plasmodium falciparum. When selecting the malaria rapid diagnostic test kit for the plasmodium falciparum, which parasitic antigen will guide your choice of the test kit for plasmodium falciparum?
A. Histidine Rich Protein – 2 (HRP2)
B. Parasitic Lactate Dehydrogenase (pLDH)
C. Duffy Coat antigen
D. Intercellular adhesion molecule type I (ICAM –a)
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A. Histidine Rich Protein – 2 (HRP2)

Explanation: HRP2 is the specific antigen produced by Plasmodium falciparum. Most RDTs targeting falciparum detect HRP2. pLDH is used for pan-malaria or non-falciparum species.

10.
You are posted at the airport medical clinic to screen and treat travelers. 2 tourists intend to visit a malaria endemic zone and you expected to advise them on the choice of malaria chemoprophylaxis. Which drug would you recommend?
A. Cotrimoxazole.
B. Mefloquine.
C. Artesunate
D. Quinine
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B. Mefloquine.

Explanation: Mefloquine is a standard chemoprophylactic drug for travelers. Artesunate and Quinine are treatment drugs (short half-life, toxicity). Cotrimoxazole is used for opportunistic infection prophylaxis in HIV, not standard malaria prophylaxis for tourists.

16.
A 19-year-old student presents with history of wheezing, cough and difficulty in breathing. The patient has had similar episodes that were relieved by an inhaled drug. The patient reports the attacks to be increasing in frequency with the onset of the cold season. What is emergency management?
A. Nebulize with ventolin
B. I.V adrenaline administration
C. Give Aminophylline tablets.
D. Ventolin tablets
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A. Nebulize with ventolin

Explanation: For an acute asthma exacerbation (emergency), the first-line treatment is a Short-Acting Beta Agonist (SABA) like Salbutamol (Ventolin) via nebulizer or spacer. Oral tablets act too slowly.

17.
The unique radiological feature of pneumocystis Jirovecii pneumonia is attributed to the appearance of which of the following pattern in chest X-ray?

Which of the following is a non-modifiable risk for asthma?
A. Respiratory infection

19.
Which of the following is a long term complication of acute rheumatic fever?
A. Fever
B. Arthritis
C. Sydenham chorea
D. Erythema marginatum
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C. Sydenham chorea

Explanation: While the primary long-term complication is Rheumatic Heart Disease (Valvular damage), among the clinical features listed (Jones Criteria): Arthritis, Fever, and Erythema marginatum are acute features. Sydenham's Chorea is a late-onset manifestation (can occur months after infection) and can be persistent/recurrent, distinguishing it from the acute transient signs.

28.
A 35-year-old man presents with generalized massive oedema and dyspnea. He reports to be passing frothy urine. Upon performing urinalysis there is elevated proteins in urine 4+. In this patient, the oedema is triggered by?
A. Hypernatremia
B. Polydipsia
C. Hypoalbuminemia
D. Anuria
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C. Hypoalbuminemia

Explanation: The patient has Nephrotic Syndrome (Proteinuria 4+, Frothy urine). Massive loss of albumin in urine leads to low serum albumin (Hypoalbuminemia), which reduces plasma oncotic pressure, causing fluid to leak into tissues (Oedema).

B. Vasculitis

32.
Which of the following is associated with post hepatic portal hypertension?
A. Hepatic Cirrhosis
B. Heart Failure
C. Schistosomiasis
D. Hepatic vein thrombosis
Show Answer

Correct Answer: D. Hepatic vein thrombosis

Explanation:

  • Post-hepatic: Obstruction after the liver (Hepatic vein thrombosis/Budd-Chiari, Right Heart Failure).
  • Intra-hepatic: Cirrhosis.
  • Pre-hepatic: Portal vein thrombosis, Schistosomiasis.
  • While Heart Failure is also post-hepatic, Hepatic Vein Thrombosis is the classic specific vascular cause often tested.

41.
Outline the management of TB in a 23-year-old breastfeeding mother. (5 Marks)
Show Answer

Answer:

  1. Treatment of Mother: Initiate standard first-line anti-TB regimen (2RHZE / 4RH) immediately. The drugs are safe for breastfeeding.
  2. Breastfeeding: Encourage continued breastfeeding. Do not separate mother and baby.
  3. Infection Prevention: Advise mother to wear a mask while breastfeeding or handling the baby (cough hygiene).

  5. Vaccination: Delay BCG vaccination for the infant until IPT is completed (or as per national guidelines).

Section 3: Long Answer Question

20 Marks
49.
An 85-year-old patient presents with episode of morning joint stiffness, redness and swelling of the knee and elbow joints. There is no history of trauma,

a. What are the differential diagnoses? (4 Marks)

b. Manage this patient (16 Marks)

Show Answer

Answer:

a) Differential Diagnoses:

  1. Rheumatoid Arthritis (RA): Morning stiffness, polyarticular (knee/elbow), inflammatory (redness).
  2. Gouty Arthritis: Acute redness/swelling, common in elderly (usually monoarticular but can be polyarticular).
  3. Septic Arthritis: Red, hot, swollen joint (Emergency to rule out).
  4. Pseudogout (CPPD): Affects knees/elbows in elderly, mimics gout/RA.

b) Management:

1. Investigations:

  • Joint Aspiration (Synovial Fluid Analysis): The definitive test. Check for crystals (Gout/Pseudogout), Gram stain/Culture (Septic), WBC count.
  • Blood Tests: ESR/CRP (Inflammatory markers), Rheumatoid Factor/Anti-CCP (Rule out RA), Serum Uric Acid (Supportive for Gout).
  • X-Ray: Check for erosions (RA), chondrocalcinosis (Pseudogout), or joint destruction.

2. Pharmacological Treatment:

  • Analgesia/Anti-inflammatory: NSAIDs (e.g., Naproxen/Diclofenac) cautiously due to age/renal function. Or COX-2 inhibitors.
  • Steroids: Intra-articular corticosteroid injection (if septic arthritis ruled out) or short course oral Prednisolone for rapid relief.
  • Disease Specific:
    • If Gout: Colchicine (acute). Allopurinol (long term).
    • If RA: Start DMARDs (Methotrexate) if confirmed.
    • If Septic: IV Antibiotics.

3. Non-Pharmacological/Supportive:

  • Rest: Joint rest during acute inflammation.
  • Physiotherapy: To maintain range of motion and prevent stiffness once acute pain subsides.
  • Assistive Devices: Walking stick/cane to offload the knee.
