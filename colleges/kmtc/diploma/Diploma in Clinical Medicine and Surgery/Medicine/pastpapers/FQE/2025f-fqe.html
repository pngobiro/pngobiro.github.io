A 29 years old patient presents with symptoms of frothy urine, facial puffiness and oedema. What is the impression?
A. Nephrotic syndrome
B. Pyelonephritis
C. Heart failure
D. Hyperlipidaemia
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A. Nephrotic syndrome

Explanation: The triad of frothy urine (proteinuria), facial puffiness (periorbital edema), and generalized oedema is classic for Nephrotic Syndrome. Frothy urine specifically indicates significant loss of albumin (protein) in urine.

Explanation: The symptoms described are the cardinal signs of Parkinsonism: Bradykinesia (slowness of movement), Festinating gait (shuffling), and Tremors (though typically resting pill-rolling tremors, advanced cases can present variably). The combination points strongly to Parkinson's.

5.
An 18-year-old patient is brought with history of progressive headache, joint pains, hotness of the body and loss of appetite. On examination, there is splenomegaly and a tinge of jaundice. The patient is febrile clinically with a temperature of 38°C. What is the impression?

B. Meningitis
C. Enteric fever

Correct Answer: D. Malaria

7.
Which statement best describes the Mode of Action of colchicine?
A. Uric acid synthesis blockage
B. Anti-inflammatory
C. Inhibition of cyclooxygenase enzyme
D. Inhibition of prostaglandins
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B. Anti-inflammatory

Explanation: Colchicine works by inhibiting microtubule polymerization, which prevents neutrophil migration to the joint, thereby reducing inflammation. It is not an NSAID (doesn't inhibit COX/Prostaglandins directly) and doesn't block uric acid synthesis (that's Allopurinol).

B. Osteophytes and tophi

14.
A patient with type II diabetes mellitus is brought in coma. The wife reports he has been unwell for the last one-week worsening. On examination he is unresponsive. Blood pressure is 90/60mmHg. Which statement would be true for the pathophysiology and management?
A. Serum ketoacidosis will be markedly elevated
B. Marked elevation of serum sodium levels
C. Vigorous rehydration while maintaining electrolyte homeostasis
D. High insulin levels to correct hyperglycemia
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C. Vigorous rehydration while maintaining electrolyte homeostasis

Explanation: Type 2 DM patients presenting with coma and dehydration (low BP) typically have Hyperosmolar Hyperglycemic State (HHS). The priority is fluid resuscitation (rehydration) to fix the hyperosmolarity and hypotension. Unlike DKA (Type 1), ketoacidosis is usually minimal or absent.

15.
As a clinician in the casualty, a patient with acute kidney injury is brought to you. While managing the patient which of the following intervention would be considered as a priority?
A. Fluid resuscitation to correct dehydration.
B. Urgent correction of severe alkalosis
C. Correction of life-threatening hyperkalaemia
D. Dialysis urgently as a first line of treatment mode
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C. Correction of life-threatening hyperkalaemia

Explanation: In Acute Kidney Injury (AKI), the most immediate life-threatening complication is Hyperkalemia (high potassium), which can cause fatal cardiac arrhythmias. Stabilizing the cardiac membrane (Calcium gluconate) and shifting potassium (Insulin/Dextrose) is the top priority.

The following is TRUE in regard to the pathophysiology of Cohns disease.

25.
The following describes the clinical correlation and parasitaemia in malaria classification?
A. Levels of ≤100 parasites/UL are likely to be asymptomatic in individuals living in endemic or non-endemic regions.
B. Levels between 10,000-50,000 parasites/UL carries an increased mortality and is considered hyper parasitaemia.
C. Maximum parasitaemia for P-Vivax and P-Ovale should be 10,000 parasites/UL
D. Levels >10,000 parasites/UL are likely to be asymptomatic in endemic regions.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A. Levels of ≤100 parasites/UL are likely to be asymptomatic in individuals living in endemic or non-endemic regions.

Explanation: Very low parasitemia can be asymptomatic. Hyperparasitemia is typically defined as >100,000 parasites/µL (or >2-5%). In endemic regions (Option D), immunity allows higher loads to be asymptomatic, but >10,000 is usually the threshold for "clinical malaria" fever.

The presentation is classic for Cholera.

44.
A 29 years old patient presents with fatigue and restlessness. On examination, a sweet fruity smell, rapid pulse and BP of 79/55mmHg is noted. Past medical history indicates the patient is on treatment and follow up for diabetes Type I. Outline the immediate management of this patient. (5 marks)
Show Answer

Answer:

The patient is in Diabetic Ketoacidosis (DKA) with shock.

  1. Resuscitation (Fluids): Start IV Normal Saline (0.9% NaCl) immediately to restore volume and BP (1L in first hour).
  2. Insulin Therapy: Start Soluble Insulin (Regular) IV infusion (0.1 units/kg/hr) to lower blood sugar and suppress ketogenesis.
  3. Electrolyte Replacement: Add Potassium (K+) to fluids once urine output is established (insulin drives K+ into cells, risking hypokalemia).
  4. Treat Precipitating Cause: Screen for and treat infection (e.g., antibiotics).
  5. Monitoring: Hourly Blood Glucose, Vitals, and Urine Ketones.

Enumerate five predisposing factors of iron deficiency anaemia in a man aged 35 years. (5 marks)

Answer:

In men, iron deficiency is almost always pathological (blood loss) rather than nutritional.

  1. Chronic Gastrointestinal Bleeding: Peptic Ulcer Disease (PUD), Gastritis, or NSAID use.
  2. Hookworm Infestation: Chronic blood loss in the gut.

47.
Outline the steps followed in cardiopulmonary resuscitation to increase the odd of surviving following a cardiac event in a hospital set up. (5 marks)
Show Answer

Answer:

The In-Hospital Chain of Survival:

  1. Surveillance and Prevention: Early recognition of deteriorating patient (Early Warning Scores) to prevent arrest.
  2. Recognition and Emergency Call: Confirm unresponsive/no breathing and activate Code Blue/Resuscitation Team immediately.
  3. High-Quality CPR: Immediate chest compressions (push hard, push fast) with minimal interruptions.
  4. Rapid Defibrillation: Apply pads and shockable rhythms (VF/VT) shocked within minutes.
  5. Advanced Life Support (ALS) & Post-Arrest Care: Airway management, drugs (Adrenaline/Amiodarone), and targeted temperature management.

49.
A 19 years old patient presents with history of headache, fever and vomiting. On examination, there is neck stiffness. Laboratory investigation reveals elevated white blood cells and Cerebrospinal fluid (CSF) analysis shows elevated proteins. Manage this patient. (20 marks)
Show Answer

Answer:

Diagnosis: The clinical triad of fever, headache, and neck stiffness (meningism), combined with elevated CSF protein and WBCs, confirms Acute Bacterial Meningitis.

1. Admission & Resuscitation:

  • Admit the patient to an isolation ward (droplet precautions).
  • Airway/Breathing: Ensure patent airway, give Oxygen if SpO2 < 94%.
  • Circulation: Secure IV access and start IV Fluids (Normal Saline) for maintenance (restrict if SIADH suspected).

2. Specific Pharmacological Management (Antibiotics):

  • Start empiric antibiotics immediately after taking cultures (do not delay for LP results).
  • Ceftriaxone: 2g IV BD (Twice daily) OR 4g IV OD for 10-14 days. (High dose for CNS penetration).
  • OR Crystalline Penicillin: 4 MU IV every 4 hours.
  • Adjunct Steroids: IV Dexamethasone 10mg given 15-20 mins before or with first antibiotic dose to reduce hearing loss and neurological sequelae.

3. Symptomatic & Supportive Care:

  • Analgesia: IV Paracetamol 1g for headache and fever control.
  • Anticonvulsants: If seizures occur, give IV Diazepam 10mg stat, load with Phenytoin.
  • Antiemetics: IV Metoclopramide for vomiting.
  • Raised ICP Management: Elevate head of bed 30 degrees. Consider Mannitol if signs of severe raised ICP present.

4. Monitoring:

  • Monitor Glasgow Coma Scale (GCS) every 2-4 hours.
  • Monitor Vitals (Temp, BP, Pulse) regularly (watch for Cushing's triad of raised ICP).
  • Fluid Input/Output charting.

5. Public Health Measures:

  • Notify disease surveillance authorities (Meningitis is a notifiable disease).
  • Contact tracing and Chemoprophylaxis for close contacts (e.g., Ciprofloxacin 500mg stat or Rifampicin).
