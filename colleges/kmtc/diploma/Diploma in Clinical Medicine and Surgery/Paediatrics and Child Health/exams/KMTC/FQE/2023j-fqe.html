SECTION 1: MULTIPLE CHOICE QUESTIONS (40 MARKS)

1.
Low birth weight babies require special care. Which one of the following is TRUE about a very low birth weight baby?
A. Is a new born weighing less than 1500g at birth
B. The baby should be given dexamethasone to mature the lungs
C. Should be started on broad spectrum antibiotics routinely
D. Should be nursed in an incubator until 44 weeks post menstrual age
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A. Is a new born weighing less than 1500g at birth

Explanation:

  • LBW: < 2500g
  • VLBW: < 1500g
  • ELBW: < 1000g
Dexamethasone is given antenatally to the mother, not the baby. Antibiotics are given only if infection is suspected. Incubator care usually stops when the baby maintains temperature (around 1.8-2.0kg), not necessarily 44 weeks.

2.
During examination of the new born, you find a fluctuant mass on the head, at the parietal, that is restricted by suture line. The likely impression is?
A. Caput succedaneum
B. Cephalohaematoma
C. Periventricular haemorrhage
D. Fractured skull
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B. Cephalohaematoma

Explanation: A cephalohaematoma is a subperiosteal hemorrhage that does not cross suture lines. Caput succedaneum is subcutaneous edema that crosses suture lines.

3.
A 3 weeks old baby presents to you with jaundice. The mother reports pale stools, dark brown urine and distended abdomen. The likely diagnosis is?
A. ABO – Rhesus incompatibility
B. Neonatal sepsis
C. Biliary atresia
D. Physiological jaundice
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C. Biliary atresia

Explanation: The triad of jaundice persisting beyond 2 weeks, pale (acholic) stools, and dark urine (conjugated hyperbilirubinemia) in a neonate is classic for Biliary Atresia. Immediate referral for Kasai procedure is critical.

4.
A neonate developed fever and inability to breast feed. On examination he had a positive bacteremia. He weighs 4 Kgs. Which of the following is correct first line treatment and dose?
A. IV ceftriaxone 200 mg once daily for 10 days
B. IV x pen 200 000iu 12 hourly for 10 days plus Gentamycin
C. Vancomycin 500mg BD x 10/7
D. Benzathine penicillin 1200mu stat
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B. IV x pen 200 000iu 12 hourly for 10 days plus Gentamycin

Explanation: First-line treatment for neonatal sepsis is Benzylpenicillin (X-pen) or Ampicillin + Gentamicin.
Dose Calculation for 4kg:
X-pen: 50,000 IU/kg/dose = 200,000 IU. Frequency 12 hourly is appropriate for first week of life (or 6-8 hourly depending on age).
Gentamicin is the standard adjunct.

5.
A 2-year-old child had a MUAC of 10cm. on examination, you find visible severe wasting. which one of the following statements is true?
A. Moderate acute malnutrition is likely
B. The likely cause of death in this patient is skin infection
C. Severe acute malnutrition is the impression,
D. Prescribe high protein diet for the child
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C. Severe acute malnutrition is the impression,

Explanation: MUAC < 11.5cm indicates Severe Acute Malnutrition (SAM). High protein diet (D) is contraindicated in the initial stabilization phase (reductive adaptation). Hypoglycemia, hypothermia, and electrolytes are more common immediate causes of death than skin infection alone.

6.
Which of the following treatment implication is correct based on reductive adaptation in severe malnutrition?
A. Give IV fluids in some or severe dehydration
B. Give high protein diet
C. Aggressive feeding with nutrient dense diet
D. Empirically give broad spectrum antibiotics
Show Answer

Correct Answer: D. Empirically give broad spectrum antibiotics

Explanation: In "reductive adaptation" (where body systems slow down to survive), the immune system is suppressed, and typical signs of infection (fever) may be absent. Therefore, broad-spectrum antibiotics are given empirically to all SAM patients. IV fluids (A) are avoided unless in shock due to heart failure risk. High protein (B) and aggressive feeding (C) overload the fragile liver/metabolism (Refeeding syndrome risk).

10.
Poliomyelitis is a paralyzing condition. Which of the following statements describes poliomyelitis presentation?
A. Symmetrical flaccid paralysis.

11.
What drug and dose would you give to a 20kg child diagnosed of severe malaria?
A. IV artesunate 60 mg at 0, 12, 24 hours then full dose of AL
B. IV artesunate 48 mg at 0, 12, 24 hours
C. IV quinine 400mg in 5% dextrose loading dose
D. Tabs AL 2 tablets at 0, 8, 24, 36, 48 and 60 hours
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B. IV artesunate 48 mg at 0, 12, 24 hours

Explanation:

  • Dosing Rule: For children <20kg: 3mg/kg. For children $\ge$ 20kg: 2.4mg/kg.
  • At exactly 20kg, guidelines often shift to the 2.4mg/kg tier.
  • Calculation: $20\text{kg} \times 2.4\text{mg/kg} = 48\text{mg}$.
(If applying 3mg/kg, it would be 60mg. However, 48mg is the standard strict guideline dose for 20kg+).

16.

B. Transposition of great arteries

32.
Which of the following statements about a blood slide for malaria parasites is NOT correct?

B. Thick film is for qualification.

Show Answer

Correct Answer: C. 1000 parasites per microliter in non endemic region is hyperparasetemia.

34.
About paediatric emergency, which one is NOT CORRECT?

40.
A child presents to you with high fever convulsions and neck stiffness. One of your investigations is lumbar puncture for CSF analysis. What would be a contra indication to performing a lumbar puncture in a child is?
A. Coma
B. Bulging fontanel
C. Poorly responding pupils

Correct Answer: C. Poorly responding pupils

43.
A 2-year-old weighing 10 Kgs is brought to you with history of profuse diarrhea and vomiting. The mother reports of many bouts of diarrhea, watery, just staining the diaper. On examination there is sunken eyes, lethargy and unable to drink. Manage this child. (5 Marks)
Show Answer

Diagnosis: Severe Dehydration (Plan C).

Management:

  1. Resuscitation: Start IV Fluids immediately (Ringer's Lactate or Normal Saline).
  2. Volume/Rate: Give 100ml/kg total (1000ml).
    • First: 30ml/kg (300ml) over 30 minutes.
    • Then: 70ml/kg (700ml) over 2.5 hours.
  3. Reassess: Monitor pulse and hydration status frequently.
  4. Zinc: 20mg daily for 14 days once vomiting stops.
  5. Antibiotics: Give Ciprofloxacin/Erythromycin if Cholera is suspected (Rice watery stool description).
  6. Feeding: Continue breastfeeding/feeding once patient can drink.
44.
Daisy 4 years old is brought to your clinic with history of fever and convulsions. A blood slide for malaria parasites was positive. Random blood sugar was 2 mmol/L and Hb 4g/Dl. She weighs 12kgs, Write a treatment sheet for this child, (5Marks).
Show Answer

Diagnosis: Severe Malaria (Complicated by Hypoglycemia and Severe Anemia).

Treatment Sheet:

  1. Hypoglycemia: Give 10% Dextrose 5ml/kg (60ml) IV bolus immediately.
  2. Antimalarial: IV Artesunate 3mg/kg (36mg) at 0, 12, and 24 hours. Then daily until oral.
  3. Anemia: Transfuse Packed Red Blood Cells 10ml/kg (120ml) or Whole Blood 20ml/kg (240ml). Give Furosemide 1mg/kg mid-transfusion.
  4. Convulsions: IV Diazepam 0.3mg/kg if seizing.
  5. Antipyretic: Paracetamol 15mg/kg (180mg) for fever.
45.
A 3-year-old presents with history of convulsions, neck stiffness and head enlargement. On examination is febrile, has sunset eyes.
Show Answer

a. What is your impression? (1 Mark)

Bacterial Meningitis complicated by Hydrocephalus (Sunset eyes and head enlargement indicate increased ICP).

b. Write a treatment sheet. (4 Marks)

  1. Admit and prop up head 30 degrees.
  2. Antibiotics: IV Ceftriaxone 100mg/kg/day.
  3. Manage ICP: IV Mannitol or Furosemide (Consult neurosurgery for possible shunt/EVD).
  4. Steroids: IV Dexamethasone 0.15mg/kg to reduce inflammation.
  5. Seizure Control: Anticonvulsants if needed.
46.
Diagnosis of Leukemia in children is by high index of suspicion, outline the management of leukemia in a child. (5 Marks).
Show Answer
  1. Induction Phase: Aggressive chemotherapy (Vincristine, Steroids, L-Asparaginase) to achieve remission (kill >99% blasts).
  2. Consolidation/Intensification: Intensive chemo to kill remaining cells (prevent resistance).
  3. Maintenance Phase: Low dose chemo (Methotrexate, 6-MP) for 2-3 years.
  4. CNS Prophylaxis: Intrathecal Methotrexate to prevent CNS relapse.
  5. Supportive Care: Blood products for anemia/thrombocytopenia, Antibiotics for febrile neutropenia, Allopurinol/Hydration for tumor lysis.
47.
A 10-year-old attempted suicide, he took an unknown substance about 30 minutes prior. On examination you find him unconscious, pin point pupils, tachycardia with widespread coarse crepitations.
Show Answer

a. What is your impression. (1 Mark).

Organophosphate Poisoning.

b. Manage this patient. (4 Marks).

  1. Resuscitation: Secure airway (suction secretions), administer Oxygen.
  2. Antidote: IV Atropine 0.05mg/kg every 5-10 minutes until atropinization (drying of secretions, clearing of chest, tachycardia).
  3. Decontamination: Remove clothes, wash skin. Gastric lavage (if within 1 hour and airway protected).
  4. Monitoring: Pulse oximetry, cardiac monitoring.
48.
A 6-year-old weighing 16 Kgs is brought to you with history of polyuria, polydipsia and enuresis. On examination you find KUSSMAUL breathing with a fruity breath. A random blood sugar done is 20mmol/L. Outline your management, (5 Marks).
Show Answer

Diagnosis: Diabetic Ketoacidosis (DKA).

Management:

  1. Fluids: Rehydrate with Normal Saline (10-20ml/kg bolus if in shock, then deficit replacement over 48 hours). Avoid rapid correction to prevent cerebral edema.
  2. Insulin: Start soluble Insulin infusion 0.1 units/kg/hour (after 1 hour of fluids). Do NOT give bolus insulin.
  3. Potassium: Add Potassium Chloride to fluids once urine output is established (insulin drives K+ into cells).
  4. Monitoring: Hourly blood glucose (drop not >5mmol/L/hr), Vitals, Neurological status (for cerebral edema).
  5. Identify/Treat precipitating cause (e.g., infection).

SECTION 3: LONG ANSWER QUESTIONS (20 MARKS)

49.
A 6-year-old child is brought to you with history of fever, joint pains and swelling, difficulty in breathing. She has had 3 transfusions prior. On examination, she is febrile, pale, jaundiced and swelling of the small joints.
Show Answer

a. What is your impression? (2 Marks).

Sickle Cell Disease (Vaso-occlusive crisis with Acute Chest Syndrome).

b. Manage this patient. (18 Marks)

  1. Admission: Admit to acute pediatric ward.
  2. Oxygen Therapy: Administer oxygen to maintain SpO2 > 94% (Critical for Acute Chest Syndrome).
  3. Analgesia (Pain Control):
    • NSAIDs (Ibuprofen) + Paracetamol for mild/moderate pain.
    • Opioids (Morphine 0.1mg/kg) for severe pain.
  4. Hydration: IV Fluids (Maintenance x 1.5) to dilute HbS and improve flow. Encourage oral intake.
  5. Antibiotics: IV Ceftriaxone (Broad spectrum coverage for ACS/Osteomyelitis). Add Macrolide (Azithromycin) for ACS coverage.
  6. Blood Transfusion: Top-up transfusion or Exchange Transfusion indicated for Acute Chest Syndrome and severe anemia (Pale + 3 prior transfusions).
  7. Malaria treatment: Test and treat if positive.
  8. Monitoring: Respiratory rate, SpO2, Pain score.
  9. Long term plan: Hydroxyurea, Folic acid, Penicillin V prophylaxis, Vaccinations (Pneumococcal/Flu).
