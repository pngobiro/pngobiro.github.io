Section 1: Multiple Choice Questions (40 Marks)

1.
Which of the following will not cause Intra-uterine growth restriction (IUGR)?
A. Nicotine
B. Alcohol
C. Propronolol
D. Phenothiazine
Show Answer

Correct Answer: D. Phenothiazine

Explanation:

  • Nicotine: Smoking is a well-established cause of IUGR due to vasoconstriction and reduced oxygen delivery.
  • Alcohol: Causes Fetal Alcohol Syndrome, which includes growth restriction.
  • Propranolol: Beta-blockers can increase uterine vascular resistance and are associated with lower birth weights/IUGR.
  • Phenothiazines: (e.g., Chlorpromazine) are generally not primarily associated with IUGR, though they may have other side effects like neonatal withdrawal.
2.
Reduced height and weight in a 7-year old may be due to;
A. Malnutrition
B. Lymphoma
C. Chronic infection
D. Measles
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A. Malnutrition

Explanation: While chronic infections and malignancies (Lymphoma) can cause growth failure, Malnutrition is the most direct and common cause of stunting (reduced height) and wasting (reduced weight) globally. Measles is an acute infection (though it can precipitate malnutrition, it is not a direct chronic cause of stunted growth on its own without the malnutrition link).

3.
Diarrhoea, dermatitis and dementia is a classic presentation of;
A. Beriberi
B. Pellagra
C. Scurvy
D. Rickets
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B. Pellagra

Explanation: Pellagra is caused by Niacin (Vitamin B3) deficiency. It presents with the classic "4 Ds": Dermatitis, Diarrhea, Dementia, and Death (if untreated).

4.
Blueberry Muffin rash is associated with the following congenital infection:
A. Toxoplasmosis
B. Herpex simplex
C. Rubella
D. Cytomegalovirus (CMV)
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C. Rubella

Explanation: The "Blueberry Muffin" rash (dermal erythropoiesis) is classically described in Congenital Rubella Syndrome. It can also occur in CMV, but Rubella is the historical classic association often tested.

5.
The following is termed as very low birth weight;
A. 1500-2499 gm
B. 2500-3999 gm
C. 1000-1499 gm
D. 500-999 gm
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C. 1000-1499 gm

Explanation:

  • Low Birth Weight (LBW): < 2500g (1500-2499g).
  • Very Low Birth Weight (VLBW): < 1500g (1000-1499g).
  • Extremely Low Birth Weight (ELBW): < 1000g.
  • Normal Weight: 2500g - 3999g.
6.
In management of hypoglyceamia in paediatrics, the fluid of choice is;
A. 5% dextrose
B. Ringer's lactate
C. 50% dextrose
D. 10% dextrose
Show Answer

Correct Answer: D. 10% dextrose

Explanation: For symptomatic hypoglycemia in infants and children, 10% Dextrose (2-5 ml/kg bolus) is the standard treatment. 50% Dextrose is too hypertonic and can cause vein sclerosis or cerebral injury in young children. 5% Dextrose is usually maintenance.

7.
Of caput succedaneum, the following is not true;
A. May occur on suture lines
B. May present at birth
C. Overlies skull bone fracture
D. Treatment of choice is Crystalline Penicillin and gentamycin
Show Answer

Correct Answer: D. Treatment of choice is Crystalline Penicillin and gentamycin

Explanation: Caput succedaneum is a diffuse edema of the fetal scalp that crosses the suture lines. It is caused by pressure during delivery.

  • A (Crosses sutures) is True.
  • B (Present at birth) is True.
  • C (Overlies fracture) is generally False/Rare (Cephalohematoma is more often associated with fractures, but Caput is just edema, though technically D is the *most* blatantly false statement regarding management).
  • D (Treatment is antibiotics) is False. Caput succedaneum resolves spontaneously within a few days and requires no specific treatment, certainly not antibiotics.
8.
In the paediatric population, the most common presentation of extra-pulmonary TB (Tuberculosis) is;
A. TB Bone
B. TB Adenitis
C. TB Meningitis
D. TB Peritonitis
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B. TB Adenitis

Explanation: Tuberculous lymphadenitis (TB Adenitis/Scrofula), particularly of the cervical nodes, is the most common form of extra-pulmonary tuberculosis in children.

9.
Anaemia may be associated with which of the following vitamins?
A. Vitamin B1
B. Vitamin B6
C. Vitamin B12
D. Vitamin B3
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C. Vitamin B12

Explanation: Vitamin B12 (Cobalamin) deficiency causes Megaloblastic Anemia. Vitamin B6 deficiency can cause sideroblastic anemia (less common). Vitamin B1 (Thiamine) deficiency causes Beriberi. Vitamin B3 (Niacin) deficiency causes Pellagra.

10.
Which of the following is the most sensitive index used for the assessment of nutritional status?
A. Height for Age
B. Weight for Height
C. Weight for Age
D. Head circumference for age
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B. Weight for Height

Explanation: Weight for Height (Wasting) is the most sensitive indicator of acute nutritional status (current malnutrition). Weight for Age is a composite index. Height for Age indicates chronic malnutrition (stunting). In acute settings/growth monitoring, Weight for Height is critical for identifying wasting.

11.
The most common cause of jaundice in the newborn is;
A. Liver disease
B. Kidney disease
C. Physiological
D. Obstructive Jaundice
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C. Physiological

Explanation: Physiological Jaundice affects 60% of term and 80% of preterm infants, making it by far the most common cause. It typically appears after 24 hours and resolves within a week or two.

12.
Which of the following is a major risk factor for developing neonatal jaundice;
A. Exclusive bottle feeding
B. Labour lasting more than 24 hours
C. Blood incompatibility with the sibling
D. Prematurity
Show Answer

Correct Answer: D. Prematurity

Explanation: Prematurity is a major risk factor because of the immature liver's inability to conjugate bilirubin effectively. While blood incompatibility (ABO/Rh) is a cause, it is with the mother, not the sibling. Breastfeeding issues can contribute, but prematurity is a stronger independent physiological risk factor.

13.
The following is NOT a clinical presentation of rheumatic fever;
A. Truncal rash

C. Changes in vision
D. Nausea
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C. Changes in vision

Explanation: Major criteria (Jones) include Polyarthritis (Joint tenderness - B) and Erythema Marginatum (Truncal rash - A). Nausea is a non-specific symptom that can occur. Changes in vision are not a feature of Rheumatic Fever.

A. Rota virus
B. Klebsiell.

D. Amoeba

Correct Answer: A. Rota virus

15.
The following is a priority investigation in an unconscious child with severe acute malnutrition;
A. Random blood sugar
B. Serum albumin
C. Fasting blood sugar
D. Urea electrolyte and creatinine
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A. Random blood sugar

Explanation: Hypoglycemia is a silent killer in children with Severe Acute Malnutrition (SAM). An unconscious SAM child must be assumed to be hypoglycemic and treated immediately. Testing Random Blood Sugar is the immediate priority investigation to confirm/monitor this life-threatening complication.

16.
A child with recurrent UTI (Binary Tract Infections) is MOST likely to have;
A. Posterior urethral values

C. Neurogenic bladder
D. Renal and ureteric calculi
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B. Vesico-ureteric reflux

Explanation: Vesicoureteral Reflux (VUR) is the most common anatomical abnormality associated with recurrent urinary tract infections in children (present in 30-50% of such children).

17.
The following are risk factors for UTI (Urinary Tract Infection) EXCEPT;
A. Male gender
B. Tight clothing (underwear)
C. Pinworm infection
D. Poor toilet training
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A. Male gender

Explanation: Female gender is a risk factor for UTI (due to short urethra). Male gender is generally protective (except in uncircumcised infants <1 year). Tight clothing, pinworms, and poor hygiene/toilet training are established risk factors.

18.
Cystitis in children will most likely present with which of the following features?
A. Fever
B. Polyuria
C. Nausea
D. Suprapubic pain
Show Answer

Correct Answer: D. Suprapubic pain

Explanation: Cystitis (lower UTI) is characterized by dysuria, frequency, urgency, and suprapubic pain/tenderness. Systemic signs like high fever (A) are more indicative of Pyelonephritis (upper UTI).

19.
Growth and development is influenced by a number of factors. Which of the following factors will not influence growth and development;
A. Hereditary characteristics
B. Nutritional status
C. Marital status of the parents/caregivers
D. Parental socio-economic status
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C. Marital status of the parents/caregivers

Explanation: While family dynamics affect stress, Marital status per se is not a direct determinant of biological growth compared to Heredity (Genetics), Nutrition, and Socio-economic status (access to food/healthcare).

20.
The cause of death in neonates with bilateral renal agenesis is;
A. Renal failure
B. Hypertension
C. Pulmonary insufficiency
D. Congenital Heart disease
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C. Pulmonary insufficiency

Explanation: In bilateral renal agenesis (Potter Syndrome), lack of urine production leads to oligohydramnios. Amniotic fluid is essential for lung development. The immediate cause of death is Pulmonary Hypoplasia (Insufficiency), not renal failure itself (which would kill later if they could breathe).

21.
The following is NOT a common cause of GIT (Gastrointestinal Tract) bleeding;
A. Bacterial enteritis
B. Intussusception
C. Meckel diverticulum
D. Duodenal Ulcer
Show Answer

Correct Answer: D. Duodenal Ulcer

Explanation: In pediatrics, common causes of lower GI bleeding include Bacterial Enteritis (infectious colitis), Intussusception (Red currant jelly stool), and Meckel's diverticulum (painless bleeding). Duodenal ulcers are relatively rare in children compared to adults and less common than the other listed causes for GI bleeding presentations in this demographic.

22.
With regards to gross motor reflexes, at what age does the sucking and rooting reflexes disappear??
A. 7-9 months
B. 4-6 months
C. 5-7 months
D. 9-12 months

Correct Answer: B. 4-6 months

23.
A baby was born at home and brought to the hospital with a sudden onset of bleeding spontaneously from the cord stump 24 hours after delivery despite clamping it. Which of the following statements is correct with regards to this patient.
A. This is a typical case of Ebola virus and should be investigated

24.
The following is NOT a common cause of vomiting in childhood;
A. Gastroenteritis
B. Toxic ingestion
C. Pertussis syndrome
D. Peptic ulcer
Show Answer

Correct Answer: D. Peptic ulcer

Explanation: Gastroenteritis is the most common cause. Toxic ingestion and Pertussis (post-tussive vomiting) are also common/significant causes. Peptic ulcer disease is rare in children and not a "common" cause of vomiting compared to the others.

25.
The following are known to cause iron deficiency, EXCEPT;
A. Hookworm infestation
B. Prolonged intravascular haemolysis
C. Celiac disease
D. Prolonged use of INH (isoniazid)
Show Answer

Correct Answer: D. Prolonged use of INH (isoniazid)

Explanation:

  • Hookworm: Causes blood loss (Iron Deficiency).
  • Intravascular Hemolysis: Chronic hemoglobinuria leads to iron loss (Iron Deficiency).
  • Celiac Disease: Malabsorption of iron.
  • INH (Isoniazid): Interferes with Vitamin B6 (Pyridoxine) metabolism, causing Sideroblastic Anemia, not Iron Deficiency Anemia (iron levels are actually high in sideroblastic anemia).

27.
The standard care for most children with severe haemophilia is;
A. Prevention of spontaneous bleeding and joint deformities by Factor VIII replacement
B. Aggressive treatment by Factor VIII replacement when significant bleeding occurs
C. Avoid trauma
D. Avoid aspirin and other NSAID (Non-steroidal anti-inflammatory drugs)

Correct Answer: A. Prevention of spontaneous bleeding and joint deformities by Factor VIII replacement

Explanation: The gold standard for severe hemophilia is Prophylaxis (regular Factor VIII replacement) to prevent spontaneous bleeds and hemophilic arthropathy (joint deformities), rather than just on-demand treatment (B).

28.
In a patient presenting with shock and acidosis, the acidosis mainly results from;
A. Renal potassium loss
B. Fatty acid oxidation
C. Lactic acid from reduced tissue perfusion
D. Renal phosphate loss
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C. Lactic acid from reduced tissue perfusion

Explanation: Shock is defined by inadequate tissue perfusion. This leads to anaerobic metabolism, the byproduct of which is Lactic Acid (Lactate), causing metabolic acidosis.

32.
In a patient with Cushings' syndrome, which statement below is accurate

33.
With regards to young infant Basic life support, which statement is incorrect;
A. Give five rescue breaths with bag valve mask connected to 100% oxygen
B. Put the neonate in a sniffing position
C. Suck all secretions as deep as possible
D. Give fifteen (15) chest compressions for each two (2) breaths for two (2) minutes
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C. Suck all secretions as deep as possible

Explanation: Deep suctioning can cause vagal stimulation leading to bradycardia or apnea in infants. Guidelines recommend suctioning only visible secretions if the airway is obstructed. Sniffing position, rescue breaths, and 15:2 ratio (for 2 rescuers) are standard.

34.

35.
The following is not known to cause cerebral palsy (CP);
A. Kernicterus
B. Meningitis
C. Pneumonia
D. Intra Uterine disorder
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C. Pneumonia

Explanation: Cerebral Palsy is caused by brain injury. Kernicterus (bilirubin toxicity), Meningitis (infection), and Intrauterine disorders (hypoxia/ischemia) are direct causes of brain damage. Pneumonia is a respiratory infection and, unless it leads to severe hypoxia/arrest, is not a direct cause of CP.

36.
In CSF (Cerebral Spinal Fluid) examination, which statement is false
A. Urgent LP (Lumbar Puncture) must be done in meningitis before commencement of Cardio pulmonary Resuscitation (CPR) in a collapsed child
B. When the taping is traumatic, the CSF is always under pressure
C. Sometimes CSF harvesting may be delayed if patient has unequal pupils
D. The glucose content (levels) are of clinical consequence
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A. Urgent LP (Lumbar Puncture) must be done in meningitis before commencement of Cardio pulmonary Resuscitation (CPR) in a collapsed child

37.
Childhood CNS (Central Nervous System) tumours;
A. Usually low grade
B. Usually primary
C. Are mostly of embryonic tissues
D. Usually respond poorly to chemotherapy
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B. Usually primary

Explanation: Most CNS tumors in children are primary tumors (originating in the brain), unlike in adults where metastatic (secondary) tumors are more common. Many are infratentorial (e.g., medulloblastoma, astrocytoma).

38.
Neuroblastomas do not spread (metastasize) to the;
A. Liver
B. Bone Marrow
C. Lungs
D. Lymph nodes
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C. Lungs

Explanation: Neuroblastoma frequently metastasizes to the Bone, Bone Marrow, Liver (Pepper syndrome), Lymph nodes, and Skin (Blueberry muffin). Lung metastases are relatively rare (unlike Wilms tumor which classically goes to the lungs).

39.

43.
Write short notes on APGAR score in the newborn. (5 marks)
Show Answer

Answer:

The APGAR score is a rapid method to assess the clinical status of a newborn at 1 and 5 minutes of age.

  • Components (0-2 points each):
    • Appearance (Skin Color): Blue/Pale vs Acrocyanosis vs Pink.
    • Pulse (Heart Rate): Absent vs <100 vs >100.
    • Grimace (Reflex Irritability): No response vs Grimace vs Cry/Pull away.
    • Activity (Muscle Tone): Limp vs Some flexion vs Active motion.
    • Respiration: Absent vs Slow/Irregular vs Good cry.
  • Scoring:
    • 7-10: Normal/Reassuring.
    • 4-6: Moderately abnormal (may need stimulation/oxygen).
    • 0-3: Low (needs immediate resuscitation).
44.
Outline the measures you would take before starting a blood transfusion. (5 marks)
Show Answer

Answer:

  1. Verify Indication: Ensure transfusion is absolutely necessary (Hb level, clinical state).
  2. Informed Consent: Explain procedure and risks to parents/guardians and obtain consent.
  3. Grouping & Cross-matching: Ensure patient blood sample is taken and compatible blood is available.
  4. Check Blood Pack: Two staff members must verify:
    • Patient Name/ID match with Blood Pack.
    • Blood Group and Rh type match.
    • Expiration date.
    • Pack integrity (no leaks/clots/hemolysis).
  5. Baseline Vitals: Record Temp, HR, RR, and BP immediately before starting.
  6. IV Access: Ensure patent large-bore cannula is available.
45.
John, who is two years old, presents with cough and difficulty in breathing for the past four days. He had some fevers on and off. On examination AVPU was "P" and was grunting. He was diagnosed as having severe pneumonia.
i. What other clinical features does Severe Pneumonia present with? (2 marks)
ii. Write your management plan for this patient (3 marks)
Show Answer

i. Other Clinical Features of Severe Pneumonia:

  • Lower chest wall indrawing.
  • Central cyanosis.
  • Inability to breastfeed or drink.
  • Lethargy or unconsciousness.
  • Stridor in a calm child.
  • Nasal flaring.

ii. Management Plan:

  1. Admit the child to the ward.
  2. Oxygen Therapy: Give oxygen (since grunting is a danger sign) to maintain SpO2 >90%.
  3. Antibiotics: Start IV Crystalline Penicillin (50,000 IU/kg 6 hourly) AND IV Gentamicin (7.5 mg/kg once daily).
  4. Supportive Care: Treat fever (Paracetamol), ensure hydration (IV fluids if unable to drink, then NGT feeding), keep warm.
  5. Monitoring: Review vitals regularly. Switch to oral Amoxicillin when improved.
46.
Mariam is 2 years and has had two days diarrhoea which is watery and not blood stained. She has had more than six episodes every day, is well nourished.
i. Differentiate the clinical features of severe dehydration and shock. (3 marks)
ii. How would you rehydrate the patient in the FIRST ONE HOUR? (2 marks)
Show Answer

i. Differentiation:

  • Severe Dehydration (Plan C): Lethargic/Unconscious, Sunken eyes, Unable to drink, Skin pinch goes back very slowly (>2s).

  • The question asks for the "First One Hour": Technically, you give the bolus rapidly.

Section 3: Long Answer Questions (20 Marks)

49.
Susan, a 14 years old student, went to Serena Beach Hotel in Mombasa for a holiday as a reward for her good school performance. Upon arrival, she went to the ocean and swam the whole day. She later came to the hotel's swimming pool for a last swim. A few minutes later, a waiter noticed her floating limply in the pool with little movement. He raised the alarm and pulled her out of the water. She was unconscious and had poor respiratory effort. You were at the hotel for a healthcare convention and you are called upon to assist Susan.
a) What is your immediate management/action? (5 marks)
b) What is the rationale reason for admission in such a situation? (5 marks)
c) Outline the goals of management in this patient (what is to be corrected)? (5 marks)
d) Outline your treatment plan for Susan in Hospital? (5 marks)
Show Answer

a) Immediate Management (On Scene):

  • Safety: Ensure scene is safe.
  • ABCDE Approach:
    • Airway: Open airway (Head tilt/Chin lift) - check for foreign bodies/vomit. Suction if available.
    • Breathing: Give 5 rescue breaths immediately (drowning protocol). Check for spontaneous breathing. If absent, start CPR (30:2 or 15:2).
    • Circulation: Check pulse. If no pulse, continue Chest Compressions.
  • Position: Recovery position if breathing spontaneously to prevent aspiration.
  • Warmth: Remove wet clothes, cover with warm blankets to prevent hypothermia.
  • Call for Help: Activate emergency response/Ambulance transfer.

b) Rationale for Admission:

  • Delayed Pulmonary Edema: Can occur up to 24-48 hours after submersion ("Secondary Drowning").
  • Monitoring: Risk of arrhythmias, electrolyte imbalances, and respiratory deterioration.
  • Hypoxia complications: Brain edema or hypoxic ischemic encephalopathy needs observation.
  • Pneumonia risk: Aspiration of pool water (chemicals) or ocean water (pathogens) can cause chemical pneumonitis or infection.

c) Goals of Management (To be corrected):

  1. Hypoxia: Restore and maintain adequate oxygenation (SpO2 >94%).
  2. Hypothermia: Rewarm the patient to normal body temperature.
  3. Acidosis: Correct metabolic/respiratory acidosis.
  4. Hypovolemia/ hypotension: Maintain perfusion and blood pressure.
  5. Electrolyte Imbalance: Correct abnormalities caused by water ingestion (salt vs fresh water).

d) Treatment Plan in Hospital:

  • Respiratory Support: High flow oxygen, CPAP, or Intubation/Ventilation if GCS low or respiratory failure persists.
  • Fluid Management: IV fluids (Isotonic) to maintain BP and perfusion. Avoid fluid overload (pulmonary edema risk).
  • Nasogastric Tube: Decompress stomach (likely swallowed water) to facilitate breathing and prevent aspiration.
  • Antibiotics: Prophylactic or therapeutic if signs of infection/aspiration pneumonia develop.
