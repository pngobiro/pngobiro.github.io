1.
Paediatrics is an independent and unique specialty. Care for pediatric patients is therefore individualized and different. Which of the following is NOT CORRECT in regard to the factors to consider in managing paediatric patients?
A. Children's response to illness is influenced by age
B. Health problems of children differ from those of adults
C. Management of childhood illnesses vary significantly from that of adults
D. The child is actually a mini adult and should be treated as such
Show Answer

Correct Answer: D. The child is actually a mini adult and should be treated as such

Explanation: A fundamental principle of paediatrics is that "Children are not small adults." They have distinct anatomical, physiological, immunological, and developmental differences that require specialized care, dosing, and equipment.

2.
Examination parameters may differ in children. Which of the following is TRUE as regards to physical examination in children?
A. Pleural effusion gives a resonant note on percussion
B. Abdominal palpation should be done before auscultation
C. Ear Nose & Throat (ENT) examination should be done at the beginning of systemic examination
D. Apex beat is usually found in the 5th intercostal space mid clavicular line
Show Answer

Correct Answer: D. Apex beat is usually found in the 5th intercostal space mid clavicular line

Explanation: While in children under 7 years, the apex beat is typically in the 4th ICS lateral to the MCL, in older children it moves to the 5th ICS MCL. Among the given options, D is the only statement that *can* be true (for older children). The other options are factually incorrect:

  • A: Pleural effusion causes stony dullness, not resonance.
  • B: Auscultation precedes palpation in abdominal exams to avoid altering bowel sounds.
  • C: ENT exams are distressing and should be performed last.

B. Use of forceps as vacuum extraction
C. Multigravida patient
D. Cephalopelvic disproportion
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C. Multigravida patient

9.
In which of the following condition are you likely to get an innocent murmur?
A. PDA
B. CCF
C. Severe anaemia
D. Mitral stenosis
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C. Severe anaemia

Explanation: Severe anemia causes a hyperdynamic circulation, leading to increased blood flow turbulence. This produces a "hemic" or "flow" murmur, which is functional (innocent) and resolves when the anemia is treated. PDA and Mitral Stenosis cause pathological murmurs.

19.
Which of the following is TRUE in regard to sickle cell disease?
A. The sickle cell crisis appears at birth?
B. Blood transfusion is indicated in all sickle cell crisis.
C. Iron supplement is administered to all sicklers.
D. Hydroxyurea prevents sickling by promoting the production of HbF.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: D. Hydroxyurea prevents sickling by promoting the production of HbF.

Explanation: Hydroxyurea increases Fetal Hemoglobin (HbF), which interferes with the polymerization of HbS, preventing sickling. Symptoms rarely appear at birth due to high HbF. Iron is contraindicated unless deficiency is proven (due to risk of overload). Transfusion is reserved for specific complications (e.g., splenic sequestration, aplastic crisis, stroke).

41.
A 2-year-old child weighing 12kgs, presents with cough, difficult breathing and lower chest in-drawing. On examination you find cyanosis. SPO2 is 80%.
Show Answer

a) What is your impression? (1 Mark)

Severe Pneumonia (Based on IMNCI/WHO classification: Cough + difficulty breathing + Danger signs [Cyanosis/low SpO2]).

b) Write a treatment sheet for this patient (4 Marks)

  1. Admit the patient to the ward.
  2. Oxygen Therapy: Start oxygen via nasal prongs or mask to maintain SpO2 > 90%.
  3. Antibiotics:
    • IV Benzylpenicillin 50,000 IU/kg (600,000 IU) every 6 hours AND IV Gentamicin 7.5mg/kg (90mg) once daily.
    • OR IV Ceftriaxone 50-80mg/kg (600mg-1g) once daily.
  4. Supportive Care:
    • Maintain patent airway.
    • IV Fluids (maintenance) if unable to feed, or encourage breastfeeding if able.
    • Antipyretics (Paracetamol 180mg) if febrile.
  5. Monitoring: Monitor vitals (RR, HR, SpO2) frequently.

This patient has Severe Malaria (Severe Anemia Hb 3.5, Hypoglycemia RBS 1.8, Convulsions, Jaundice).

1. Emergency Resuscitation & Stabilization:

  • Airway/Breathing: Ensure patent airway, give Oxygen if SpO2 < 90% or respiratory distress.
  • Hypoglycemia (RBS 1.8): Administer 10% Dextrose 5ml/kg IV bolus immediately. Recheck RBS after 30 mins. Maintain on Dextrose-containing fluids.
  • Convulsions: Give Rectal Diazepam (0.5mg/kg) or IV Diazepam (0.3mg/kg). If refractory, use Phenobarbitone or Phenytoin.

  • Drug of Choice: IV Artesunate.

  • Fever: Tepid sponging and Paracetamol 15mg/kg.
  • Fluids: Maintenance fluids (avoid overhydration).

  • Turn patient every 2 hours if unconscious.
