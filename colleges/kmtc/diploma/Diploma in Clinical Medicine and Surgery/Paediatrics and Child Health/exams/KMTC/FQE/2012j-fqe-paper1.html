1.

Joseph is 2 years old. He was brought to your outpatient with 2 weeks history of swollen feet, peeling of the skin with exudation like burns, refusal to feed and loose stools. On examination he is apathetic and weighs 6.5 kg.

(a) What is the most likely diagnosis? [1 mark]

(b) What are the complications in this condition? [2 marks]

(c) How will you treat the child on the first day? [7 marks]

Show Answer

(a) Diagnosis: Kwashiorkor (Severe Acute Malnutrition with oedema and dermatosis).

(b) Complications: Hypoglycemia, Hypothermia, Severe Infection (Septicemia), Dehydration/Electrolyte imbalance, Heart Failure.

(c) Treatment (Day 1):

  • Prevent Hypoglycemia: Give 10% glucose (50ml bolus) or F-75 feed immediately. Feed every 2-3 hours.
  • Prevent Hypothermia: Keep warm (Kangaroo care/blankets), avoid drafts, feed regularly.
  • Treat Dehydration: Use ReSoMal (5ml/kg every 30 mins for 2 hrs), NOT standard ORS. Monitor closely.
  • Infection: Give broad-spectrum antibiotics (e.g., Amoxicillin or Penicillin + Gentamicin) even if no fever.
  • Diet: Start F-75 therapeutic milk (130ml/kg/day).
  • Do NOT give Iron or high protein/fluids initially.
2.

Baby Margaret is 14 days old... history of refusal to feed, abnormal movements and yellowness of the skin... mild jaundice, apnoeic episodes, jerking... Temp 38.2°C, palpable spleen... Weight 3kg.

(a) List the three main differential diagnoses. [3 marks]

(b) Give only two most beneficial investigations in this child. [2 marks]

(c) Write a comprehensive treatment sheet... specific management on admission. [15 marks]

Show Answer

(a) Differentials: Neonatal Sepsis, Meningitis, Kernicterus (Bilirubin Encephalopathy).

(b) Investigations: Total & Direct Serum Bilirubin, Blood Culture/FBC, Lumbar Puncture.

(c) Management:

  • Admit to NBU.
  • Antibiotics: IV Benzyl Penicillin + Gentamicin (or Ceftriaxone if meningitis suspected).
  • Seizures: IV Phenobarbitone (20mg/kg loading dose).
  • Jaundice: Start Phototherapy immediately. Prepare for Exchange Transfusion if bilirubin is critical.
  • Supportive: NGT feeding, maintenance fluids, thermal control.
3.
In a child presenting with a history of cough, vomiting and diarrhoea, outline the relevant aspects of history and physical examination you will take in order to arrive at a diagnosis. [10 marks]
Show Answer

History: Duration of cough/diarrhea; Nature of stool (blood/mucus/watery); Vomiting (frequency, bile-stained?); Fever history; Convulsions; Urine output (hydration status); Feeding history; HIV status/Immunization; Contact with TB.

Examination:

  • General: Alert/Lethargic/Unconscious (AVPU), Wasting/Edema, Pallor, Jaundice.
  • Vitals: Temperature, Respiratory Rate, Pulse.
  • Hydration Signs: Sunken eyes, skin pinch, fontanelle, dry mouth.
  • Respiratory: Chest indrawing, flaring, grunting, crepitations/wheeze.
  • Abdomen: Distension, tenderness, bowel sounds.
4.

(a) List five organisms commonly associated with diarrhoeal disease in under-fives. [2½ marks]

(b) Discuss the management of a 10 kg, 1 year old baby with severe dehydration. [7½ marks]

Show Answer

(a) Organisms: Rotavirus, E. coli, Shigella, Salmonella, Vibrio cholerae, Giardia lamblia, Entamoeba histolytica.

(b) Management (Plan C):

  • Start IV Fluids immediately: Ringer's Lactate or Normal Saline.
  • Total Volume: 100ml/kg = 1000ml.
  • Rate (Infant): First 30ml/kg (300ml) in 1 hour; Next 70ml/kg (700ml) in 5 hours.
  • Monitor: Reassess every 15-30 mins. Repeat bolus if pulse weak.
  • Follow-up: Start ORS (5ml/kg/hr) when able to drink. Give Zinc (20mg OD x 14 days).
5.
Outline the treatment of a child with rheumatic fever presenting with arthritis and carditis but without cardiomegaly. [10 marks]
Show Answer
  • Bed Rest: Strict bed rest for 2-4 weeks until ESR normalizes.
  • Eradicate Strep: Benzathine Penicillin G (IM stat) or Oral Penicillin V for 10 days.
  • Anti-inflammatory:
    • Aspirin: 80-100mg/kg/day in divided doses for arthritis.
    • Prednisolone: 1-2mg/kg/day (indicated for carditis, though some guidelines reserve steroids for severe carditis/CCF; aspirin may suffice for mild carditis).
  • Management of Heart Failure: (If it develops) Digoxin/Diuretics (unlikely here without cardiomegaly).
  • Secondary Prophylaxis: Start Penicillin prophylaxis after acute phase.
6.

Mbiyu a 6 month old infant... 10 bouts watery stools... vomiting... lethargic... sunken eyes. Weighs 8 kg.

(a) Classify her illness.

(b) What immediate treatment would you give this child?

Show Answer

(a) Classification: Severe Dehydration (Danger signs: Lethargy, inability to drink).

(b) Treatment: Immediate IV Ringer's Lactate. 100ml/kg (800ml total). First 240ml in 1 hr, then 560ml over 5 hrs. NGT if IV fails. Antibiotics only if specific infection suspected.

7.
John (6 months)... feeding poorly, vomiting... bulging fontanelle. Later develops fever and stiff neck.
Show Answer

Diagnosis: Meningitis.

Management: Admit. Lumbar Puncture (unless raised ICP contraindicated). IV Ceftriaxone (100mg/kg/day) or X-Pen + Chloramphenicol. Dexamethasone. Fluid restriction (2/3 maintenance).

8.
6 year old... difficulty breathing, wheezing worsens at night... history of eczema.
Show Answer

Diagnosis: Bronchial Asthma.

Treatment: Salbutamol Nebulization (2.5mg) or MDI with spacer. Oral Prednisolone (1mg/kg) for 3 days. Oxygen if hypoxic.

Complications: Status Asthmaticus, Pneumothorax, Atelectasis, Respiratory Failure.

9.
4 year old... abdominal swelling 1 month... left side, painless.
Show Answer

Differentials: Wilms Tumor (Nephroblastoma), Neuroblastoma, Hydronephrosis, Splenomegaly.

Features: Mass crosses midline? (Neuroblastoma yes, Wilms no). Hematuria? Hypertension? Hemi-hypertrophy? Aniridia?

10.
Kezia 5 years... fever, joint pains... pallor, jaundice, splenomegaly... transfused twice.
Show Answer

Diagnosis: Sickle Cell Disease (Vaso-occlusive crisis + Hemolysis).

Management: IV Fluids (Hyperhydration), Analgesia (Morphine/Paracetamol), Antibiotics, Treat Malaria, Transfuse if Hb <5. Folic acid.
