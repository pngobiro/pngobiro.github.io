Section 2: Short Answer Questions (40 Marks)

1.

i. Outline the diagnostic criteria for Nephrotic Syndrome (5 marks)

ii. Discuss 5 possible complications of Nephrotic syndrome (5 marks)

Show Answer

i. Diagnostic Criteria for Nephrotic Syndrome:

  1. Massive Proteinuria: Urine protein > 3+ or 4+ on dipstick, or > 40 mg/m²/hour (or > 50 mg/kg/day).
  2. Hypoalbuminemia: Serum albumin < 25 g/L (2.5 g/dL).
  3. Generalized Oedema: Typically starts periorbitally and becomes generalized (ascites, pedal edema).
  4. Hyperlipidemia: Elevated serum cholesterol (> 5.2 mmol/L) and triglycerides.

ii. 5 Possible Complications:

  1. Infections: (e.g., Peritonitis, Pneumonia, Cellulitis) due to loss of immunoglobulins (IgG) and complement factors in urine.
  2. Thromboembolism: (e.g., DVT, Renal Vein Thrombosis) due to loss of Antithrombin III, increased fibrinogen, and hemoconcentration.
  3. Hypovolemia/Shock: Due to low oncotic pressure causing fluid shift into the interstitium.
  4. Acute Kidney Injury (AKI): Due to severe hypovolemia or renal vein thrombosis.
  5. Growth Failure/Malnutrition: Due to massive protein loss (negative nitrogen balance).
2.
Joan is a 6 year old girl brought to the hospital with a history of multiple painful joint swelling for 3days. Temp. 37.6c and Mother reports that Joan also received a dose of paracetamol and warm water sips 3 weeks ago when the girl complained of a sore throat.
Show Answer

a) What is the most likely diagnosis (2marks)

Acute Rheumatic Fever (ARF).
Rationale: History of sore throat 3 weeks prior (Group A Strep infection), followed by fever and migratory polyarthritis (painful joint swelling).

b) What other signs will examine Joan for (4marks)

  1. Carditis: Murmurs (new or changing), Tachycardia out of proportion to fever, Cardiomegaly.
  2. Sydenham's Chorea: Involuntary, purposeless movements of face/limbs (St. Vitus Dance).
  3. Erythema Marginatum: Painless, pink rash on trunk/limbs with clear centers.
  4. Subcutaneous Nodules: Painless, firm lumps over bony prominences/extensor surfaces.

c) How will you treat Joan (4marks)

  1. Eradicate Streptococcus: Benzathine Penicillin G (IM stat dose) or Oral Penicillin V for 10 days.
  2. Anti-inflammatory: High dose Aspirin (for arthritis/mild carditis) or Prednisolone (if severe carditis/heart failure).
  3. Bed Rest: To reduce cardiac workload (duration depends on carditis severity).
  4. Supportive: Manage heart failure if present (Diuretics/Digoxin).
  5. Secondary Prophylaxis: Initiate long-term Penicillin prophylaxis to prevent recurrence.
3.
Julie is a 8 year Old girl who has had a cough lasting 3 weeks. The mother reports that she has lost quiet amount of weight currently weighing 16kgs, has been experiencing drenching night sweats and they have been staying as IDPS in a nearby school due to floods. On examination she has bilateral crepitations with poor air entry.
Show Answer

a) What is your probable diagnosis (2marks)

Pulmonary Tuberculosis (PTB).
Rationale: Chronic cough (>2 weeks), significant weight loss, night sweats, environmental risk factor (crowded IDP camp), and lung signs.

b) How will you investigate Julie (4marks)

  1. Sputum/Gastric Lavage for GeneXpert: To detect MTB and Rifampicin resistance.
  2. Chest X-ray: To check for infiltrates, hilar lymphadenopathy, or cavitation.
  3. Tuberculin Skin Test (Mantoux): To screen for TB exposure/infection.
  4. ESR/CBC: Nonspecific markers of inflammation (high ESR) and anemia.

c) Which drugs will you use to treat Julie (4marks)

Intensive Phase (2 Months): RHZE

  • Rifampicin (R)
  • Isoniazid (H)
  • Pyrazinamide (Z)
  • Ethambutol (E)
Continuation Phase (4 Months): RH
  • Rifampicin (R)
  • Isoniazid (H)
(Plus Pyridoxine/Vit B6 to prevent neuropathy).

4.
Outline 10 Prevention and Control measures of Diarrhoea (10marks)
Show Answer
  1. Rotavirus Vaccination: Routine immunization at 6 and 10 weeks.
  2. Measles Vaccination: Prevents post-measles diarrhea complications.
  3. Exclusive Breastfeeding: For the first 6 months of life.
  4. Vitamin A Supplementation: Boosts mucosal immunity.
  5. Hand Washing: With soap and water after toileting and before handling food.
  6. Safe Water: Treatment of drinking water (boiling, chlorination).
  7. Proper Sanitation: Use of latrines/toilets and safe disposal of child's stool.
  8. Food Hygiene: Washing fruits/vegetables, cooking food thoroughly, covering food.
  9. Zinc Supplementation: During diarrheal episodes to reduce severity and prevent recurrence.
  10. Health Education: Educating the community on hygiene and danger signs.

Section 3: Long Answer Question (40 Marks)

1.
Miles is a two-year-old patient brought to you. She has history of loose motions of approximately 7 times per 24 hours in the last 3 days. She weighs 12 kgs. On examination you find that she is lethargic, AVPU is at A, not able to drink, capillary refill of 3 seconds and has sunken eyes.
Show Answer

a) What is the most likely diagnosis of Miles? (2Marks)

Severe Dehydration secondary to Acute Gastroenteritis.
Evidence: Lethargic, unable to drink, sunken eyes, prolonged capillary refill (>2s).

b) Elucidate Five (5) investigations that you will request for this patient (5marks)

  1. Urea, Electrolytes and Creatinine (UECs): To check for electrolyte imbalances (hypokalemia, hypernatremia) and renal function (AKI).
  2. Stool Microscopy/Culture: To identify causative organism (ova, cysts, bacteria).
  3. Random Blood Sugar (RBS): To rule out hypoglycemia common in sick children.
  4. Full Haemogram (CBC): To check for signs of infection (leukocytosis) or anemia.
  5. Blood Gas Analysis: To check for metabolic acidosis.

c) Write a treatment plan for Miles (8marks)

Plan C (Severe Dehydration Management):

  1. Immediate Resuscitation: Start IV fluids immediately.
  2. Fluid Choice: Ringer's Lactate (preferred) or Normal Saline.
  3. Volume & Rate (100ml/kg for 12kg = 1200ml total):
    • First 30ml/kg (360ml): Give over 30 minutes.
    • Next 70ml/kg (840ml): Give over 2 ½ hours.
  4. Reassessment: Reassess the child every 15-30 minutes. If radial pulse remains weak, repeat the first bolus.
  5. Oral Rehydration: Start ORS (5ml/kg/hr) as soon as the child can drink, usually after 3-4 hours.
  6. Zinc: 20mg once daily for 14 days.
  7. Feeding: Resume feeding once hydration is improved.

d) Outline Five (5) complications for the above diagnosis (5marks)

  1. Hypovolemic Shock: Leading to tissue hypoxia.
  2. Acute Kidney Injury (Renal Failure): Due to hypoperfusion (Prerenal).
  3. Electrolyte Imbalances: Hypokalemia (arrhythmias), Hyper/Hyponatremia (seizures).
  4. Metabolic Acidosis: Due to bicarbonate loss in stool and poor perfusion.
  5. Hypoglycemia: Due to poor intake and metabolic stress.
  6. (Others: Cerebral edema if rehydrated too fast, seizures).
2.
Pendo is a 1 year old child brought to the clinic with 2 days history of cough and severe breathing problems. On examination respiratory rate is 62BPM, lethargic with chest in drawing. Mother also explains that Pendo has not eaten nor breastfed for the last 12hours.
Show Answer

a) What is Pendo suffering from? (2marks)

Severe Pneumonia.
Criteria: Cough + Fast Breathing (62 > 50 for 1yr old) + Danger Signs (Lethargic, Inability to feed, Severe chest indrawing).

b) What investigations will you carry out for Pendo? (6marks)

  1. Pulse Oximetry: To check oxygen saturation (Hypoxia).
  2. Chest X-ray: To visualize consolidation/infiltrates.
  3. Complete Blood Count (CBC): To check WBC count (infection).
  4. Blood Culture: To identify the causative organism for targeted therapy.
  5. Random Blood Sugar: To rule out hypoglycemia (common cause of lethargy).
  6. Urea/Electrolytes: To assess hydration status.

c) With correct dosage, Prescribe the needed treatment for this patient (8marks)

  1. Admission: Admit to paediatric ward.
  2. Oxygen Therapy: Start oxygen via nasal prongs to maintain SpO2 > 90% (if hypoxic).
  3. Antibiotics (First Line for Severe Pneumonia):
    • IV Benzylpenicillin: 50,000 IU/kg every 6 hours for at least 48 hours.
    • PLUS IV Gentamicin: 7.5 mg/kg once daily for at least 5 days.
    • (Alternatively: IV Ceftriaxone 50mg/kg once daily).
  4. Supportive Care:
    • IV Fluids (Maintenance) if unable to feed initially.
    • Antipyretics (Paracetamol 15mg/kg) for fever.
    • Bronchodilators (Salbutamol nebulization) if wheezing is present.

d) List Four (4) differential diagnosis for Pendo (4marks)

  1. Bronchiolitis: (Viral, common in <2yrs, presents with wheeze/indrawing).
  2. Foreign Body Aspiration: (Sudden onset, unilateral signs).
  3. Severe Malaria: (Acidotic breathing can mimic pneumonia).
  4. Congestive Cardiac Failure: (Tachypnea, hepatomegaly).
  5. Severe Anaemia: (Respiratory distress due to hypoxia).
