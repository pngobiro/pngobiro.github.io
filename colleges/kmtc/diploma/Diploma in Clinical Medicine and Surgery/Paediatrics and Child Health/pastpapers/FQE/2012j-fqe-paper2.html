Questions 1 - 20

1.
The following investigations are done in a child with haematuria:
a) Urine culture
b) Urinalysis
c) Blood for Urea and electrolytes
d) Ultrasound of the renal system
e) Renal biopsy
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a, b, c, d (True)

Explanation: In a child with haematuria, urinalysis confirms presence of red blood cells. Urine culture rules out UTI. U/Es assess renal function. Ultrasound checks for structural anomalies or tumors (Wilms). Renal biopsy is usually reserved for persistent unexplained haematuria or suspected glomerular disease not resolving.

2.
The following are the major criteria for rheumatic fever:
a) Myocarditis (Carditis)
b) Polyarthralgia
c) Osler's nodes
d) Erythema Marginatum
e) Sydenham's chorea
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a, d, e (True)

Explanation: The Jones Major Criteria are: Carditis, Migratory Polyarthritis (not just arthralgia), Sydenham's Chorea, Erythema Marginatum, and Subcutaneous Nodules. Polyarthralgia is a minor criterion. Osler's nodes are a sign of Infective Endocarditis.

3.
The cardinal signs of congestive cardiac failure are:
a) Tender splenomegally
b) Tender hepatomegally
c) Cyanosis is always present
d) Tachycardia
e) Cardiomegally
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b, d, e (True)

Explanation: The cardinal signs of CCF in children include Tachycardia (at rest), Tachypnea, Cardiomegaly, and Tender Hepatomegaly. Cyanosis is not always present (e.g., in acyanotic heart disease). Splenomegaly is not a cardinal sign of CCF (though may occur in infective endocarditis).

4.
The main causative organisms of acute rhinitis are:
a) Influenza virus
b) Para influenza
c) Haemophilus influenza
d) Rota virus
e) Adenovirus
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a, e (True) - Rhinovirus is commonest

Explanation: Acute rhinitis (common cold) is primarily viral. Common causes include Rhinovirus, Adenovirus, Influenza, and Parainfluenza. Haemophilus influenza is bacterial. Rotavirus causes gastroenteritis.

5.
The following are true of post-streptococcal Acute glomerulonephritis:
a) History of skin infection 2 weeks prior to the disease
b) Hypertension is the main clinical feature
c) Heavy proteinuria
d) ASOT is positive
e) Main treatment is prednisolone
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a, b, d (True)

Explanation: APSGN typically follows a skin infection (impetigo) or throat infection. Hypertension, edema, and hematuria are classic features. ASOT (Antistreptolysin O Titer) is elevated indicating recent strep infection. Heavy proteinuria is a feature of Nephrotic Syndrome, not Nephritic. Prednisolone is used for Nephrotic syndrome; APSGN treatment is supportive (diuretics, anti-hypertensives).

6.
The first line regimen for HIV/AIDS in a six (6) month old child is:
a) Abacavir/Lamivudine/Niverapine
b) Zidovudine/Lamivudine/Efavirenz
c) Abacavir/Lamivudine/Efavirenz
d) Abacavir/Zidovudine/Lamivudine
e) Stavudine/Lamivudine/Niverapine
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a or e (Guideline dependent at the time)

Explanation: In 2012, recommended first-line for infants < 3 years was typically AZT/3TC/NVP (Zidovudine/Lamivudine/Nevirapine) or d4T/3TC/NVP. Efavirenz is contraindicated in children < 3 years or < 10kg. Current guidelines prefer Lopinavir/ritonavir (LPV/r) based regimens for infants. Based on the options and era, option E or A (with Nevirapine) would have been correct.

7.

a) Maculopapular rash
b) Necrotising ulcerative gingivitis
c) Oral thrush
d) Oesophageal candidiasis
e) Cryptococcal meningitis

Correct Answer: c (True)

Explanation:

8.
The following are true concerning neonatal sepsis:
a) First line treatment includes procaine penicillin
b) Convulsions are an indication for lumbar puncture
c) Neutropenia is a danger sign
d) Blood cultures are definitive in diagnosis but takes 72 hours
e) Home delivery could be a predisposing factor
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b, c, d, e (True)

Explanation: First line treatment is usually Benzyl Penicillin (Crystalline) + Gentamicin, not Procaine Penicillin (avoided in neonates). Convulsions suggest meningitis, requiring LP. Neutropenia indicates severe sepsis. Blood culture is the gold standard. Home delivery increases risk of infection.

9.

10.
On examination of a newborn:
a) The normal head circumference should range between 25-30 cm
b) Subconjunctival haemorrhages need immediate attention
c) Single palmar crease may indicate Down's syndrome
d) Hepatomegally of 2 cm should be investigated
e) Prophylactic antibiotics should be given in cephalo-hematoma
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c (True)

Explanation:

11.
Tuberculosis in children:
a) HIV status of the child is mandatory before commencing treatment
b) TB adenitis is the commonest form of TB in children
c) All children suffering from TB can be treated as outpatient
d) A baby born to a mother with an open TB should be vaccinated straight away
e) Pleural effusion is a common finding in infants
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a (True)

Explanation: HIV status is crucial for management. Pulmonary TB is the commonest form (Adenitis is common extra-pulmonary). Severe TB requires admission. Babies born to mothers with open TB should receive prophylaxis (Isoniazid) first, not BCG immediately. Pleural effusion is rare in infants, more common in older children/adolescents.

12.
Causes of stridor in children include:
a) Laryngo-tracheo bronchitis
b) Acute epiglottitis
c) Foreign body in the eosophagus
d) Acute nasopharyngitis
e) Acute tonsillitis
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a, b (True)

Explanation: Stridor indicates upper airway obstruction. Laryngotracheobronchitis (Croup) and Acute Epiglottitis are classic causes. Foreign body in the trachea/larynx causes stridor (esophageal FB usually causes dysphagia). Nasopharyngitis causes snuffles. Tonsillitis causes snoring/voice changes.

13.
Bronchial asthma in children:
a) Has better prognosis than in adults
b) First line management includes nebulization
c) Is best managed by giving a bolus of intravenous Aminophylline
d) Subcutaneous adrenaline should be administered every ten (10) minutes
e) On auscultation, the chest may be silent
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a, b, e (True)

Explanation: Childhood asthma often improves with age. Nebulized salbutamol is first line for acute attacks. IV Aminophylline is not first line (safety issues). Subcutaneous adrenaline is for anaphylaxis or cardiac arrest, not routine asthma. A "silent chest" indicates severe obstruction and is a danger sign.

14.
Vaccination in children:
a) Vitamin A is a form of vaccination
b) Pentavalent is usually given at birth
c) Measles vaccine is a live attenuated vaccine
d) BCG can be given to children with symptomatic HIV infection
e) In measles outbreak children 6 months and above receive measles vaccine
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c, e (True)

Explanation: Vitamin A is supplementation, not a vaccine. Pentavalent is given at 6, 10, 14 weeks. Measles is live attenuated. BCG is contraindicated in symptomatic HIV (risk of disseminated BCG disease). In outbreaks, vaccination age drops to 6 months.

15.
The following are causes of a massive spleen:
a) Schistosomiasis
b) Acute lymphocytic leukemia
c) Kala-azar
d) Repeated malaria attacks
e) Ascariasis
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a, b, c, d (True)

Explanation: Tropical Splenomegaly Syndrome (Hyperreactive Malarial Splenomegaly), Schistosomiasis (portal hypertension), Leukemia, and Kala-azar (Visceral Leishmaniasis) are classic causes of massive splenomegaly. Ascariasis affects the gut and does not cause splenomegaly.

16.
Severe malaria in children:
a) May present with convulsions
b) Is a cause of infant mortality in our country
c) The drug of choice is oral sulphur-pyrimethamine combination
d) Is rare in our country
e) May present with anaemia
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a, b, e (True)

Explanation: Severe malaria often presents with cerebral malaria (convulsions), severe anaemia, or respiratory distress. It is a leading cause of mortality. Drug of choice is parenteral Artesunate or Quinine, not oral SP.

17.
The following are complications of mumps:
a) Pancreatitis
b) Myocarditis
c) Orchitis
d) Encephalitis
e) Cataract
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a, c, d (True)

Explanation: Mumps can cause Orchitis (inflammation of testes), Pancreatitis, and Meningoencephalitis. Cataracts are associated with Rubella, not Mumps.

18.
The following features are associated with Down's syndrome:
a) Micrognathia
b) Hypertension
c) Imperforate anus
d) Flat occiput
e) Ventricular septal defect
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c, d, e (True)

Explanation: Features include Flat occiput, low set ears, Mongoloid slant, Simian crease, Congenital heart defects (VSD, AV canal), and GI anomalies (Duodenal atresia, Imperforate anus). They have hypotonia, not hypertension.

19.
A stiff neck may be found in:
a) Meningitis
b) Acute ear infection
c) Malaria
d) Tonsillitis
e) Pneumonia
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a, c, d, e (True - Meningism)

Explanation: While classic for Meningitis, neck stiffness (Meningism) can occur in severe pneumonia (upper lobe), severe malaria, and tonsillitis due to lymphadenopathy or retropharyngeal irritation. However, Meningitis is the primary pathological cause.

20.
Cerebral palsy:
a) Progresses as the child grows
b) Common in term babies than in preterm babies
c) Occupational therapy has a minimal role in its management
d) There is always an identifiable cause
e) Some patients may lead a normal life later
Show Answer

Correct Answer: e (True)

Explanation: CP is a non-progressive disorder of posture and movement. It is more common in preterms. OT is crucial. Cause is not always identified. Mild cases can lead near-normal lives.

Questions 21 - 40

21.
About anaemia:
a) Hookworm causes iron deficiency anaemia
b) Bone marrow depression is a cause
c) Iron should always be part of the treatment
d) The type of anaemia is easily known from a peripheral blood film
e) Ascariasis is a cause
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a, b, d (True)

Explanation: Hookworm causes blood loss leading to IDA. Bone marrow failure (Aplastic) causes anaemia. Iron is only used for iron deficiency, not for Sickle cell or Thalassemia (overload risk). PBF helps classify (micro/normo/macrocytic).

Explanation: Social smile occurs at 6 weeks. Birth weight doubles by 5-6 months and triples by 1 year. First tooth appears around 6 months. Infants can see at birth (limited range).

23.
The following may cause seizures in children:
a) High fever
b) Septicaemia
c) Hypoxia
d) Cardiac arrhythmias
e) Hypernatraemia
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a, b, c, d, e (All True)

Explanation: Seizures can be febrile (High fever), infectious (Septicaemia/Meningitis), metabolic (Hypoxia, Hyper/Hyponatraemia), or vascular (Arrhythmias leading to hypoxia).

25.

26.
In the triage process, emergency cases include:

c) Child with airway obstruction

Correct Answer: a, b, c, e (True)

27.
The following are true of birth asphyxia:
a) May result in permanent brain damage
b) Results from insufficient supply of oxygen to the brain
c) Associated with an APGAR score of 8/1, 10/5, 10/10
d) May result from prolonged labour
e) Severe preeclampsia may be a cause
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a, b, d, e (True)

Explanation: Asphyxia (hypoxic-ischemic encephalopathy) results from oxygen deprivation, often due to prolonged labor or maternal conditions like preeclampsia. It causes brain damage. APGAR scores of 8-10 are normal; asphyxia is associated with low scores (<7).

28.
In patent ductus arteriosus (PDA):
a) Blood shunts from left to right through a duct
b) Blood shunts from right to left through a duct
c) Always presents with cyanosis
d) A small duct does not usually have any symptoms
e) Patients with PDA live a normal life
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a, d, e (True)

Explanation: PDA is an acyanotic defect where blood shunts from high-pressure Aorta (Left) to Pulmonary Artery (Right). Small PDAs are asymptomatic and compatible with normal life. Cyanosis only occurs if pulmonary hypertension develops (Eisenmenger syndrome), reversing the shunt.

29.
About infective endocarditis:
a) Never occurs in a previously healthy heart
b) Commonly of a bacterial cause
c) Commonly of a viral cause
d) Benzathine penicillin is necessary for prevention in rheumatic heart disease
e) Associated with vegetation of the valves
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b, d, e (True)

Explanation: IE is typically bacterial (Staph/Strep) and involves vegetations. While common on damaged valves (Rheumatic), it can occur in healthy hearts (e.g., with virulent Staph aureus). Benzathine penicillin prevents Rheumatic fever recurrences, thus preventing valve damage that predisposes to IE.

30.
The following symptoms may suggest meningitis:

e) Fever

Explanation: Classic signs: Fever, Neck stiffness, positive Kernig's/Brudzinski's. In infants, the fontanelle bulges due to raised intracranial pressure; a sunken fontanelle suggests dehydration.

31.
Prevention of mother to child transmission of HIV entails:

b) Mixed feeding for 6 months
c) Avoiding breastfeeding completely and using replacement feeds
d) Exclusive breastfeeding for 2 years
e) Use of ARVs

Explanation: Key strategies: ARVs for mother and infant prophylaxis. Feeding options: Exclusive breastfeeding for 6 months (then complementary) OR Exclusive replacement feeding (if AFASS). Mixed feeding is strictly contraindicated as it increases transmission risk.

32.
The immediate causes of death in a child with severe marasmic-kwash include:
a) Hypoglycemia
b) Flaky skin dermatitis
c) Dehydration
d) Hypothermia
e) Anaemia
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a, c, d, e (True)

Explanation: The "deadly triad" in malnutrition is Hypoglycemia, Hypothermia, and Infection (Sepsis). Severe Dehydration and Anaemia (heart failure) are also major killers. Dermatitis is a clinical feature but not an immediate cause of death.

33.

39.

a) A weight of 10.5 kg
b) Doubles the birth 'height'

40.
The following are medical contraindications to breast feeding:
a) Neonatal tetanus
b) HIV positive mother
c) Baby born to a mother with an open case of tuberculosis
d) A baby born to a hypertensive mother
e) A child with cleft palate
Show Answer

41.
The following are problems associated with a baby born to a diabetic mother:
a) Low birth weight
b) Respiratory distress syndrome
c) Large for gestational age
d) Neonatal jaundice
e) Anaemia

Correct Answer: b, c, d (True)

42.

43.
Problems usually associated with iron therapy include:
a) Bacterial super-infection
b) GIT irritation
c) Haemosiderosis
d) Polycythaemia
e) Bone marrow suppression

46.
Respond to the following about malaria:

49.
An infant who is born gasping, having a weak cry, heart rate less than 100/min, pink tongue and low muscle tone has an APGAR score of:
a) 6
b) 5
c) 8
d) 7

54.
