Section A: Multiple Choice Questions

1.
The following are true of Infective Endocarditis:
A. Never occurs in previously unhealthy heart
B. Commonly caused by a bacteria
C. Commonly caused by a virus
D. Prophylactic benzathine penicillin is necessary for prevention
E. Is associated with vegetation of the valves
Show Answer

Correct: B, D, E (True)

  • A (False): It can occur in healthy hearts (e.g., with Staph aureus).
  • C (False): It is primarily bacterial.
2.
The following are presentations of Wilms' Tumour (Nephroblastoma):
A. Low grade intermittent fever
B. Haematuria
C. Hypertension
D. Obesity
E. Abdominal mass
Show Answer

Correct: C, E (True)

  • A (False): Fever is not a classic presentation (unlike Neuroblastoma).
  • B (False): Gross haematuria is rare; microscopic may occur.
  • D (False): Not associated with obesity.
  • Note: Hypertension is due to renin secretion. Abdominal mass is the commonest sign.
3.
The term Immunization refers to:
A. Injection with toxin
B. The process of protecting a person from a specific disease
C. The process of immunity developing into infection
D. The process in which immunity occurs when a vaccine is given against a disease
E. The process in which the vaccine acts in place of a natural antibody
Show Answer

Correct: B, D, E (True)

Immunization is the process whereby a person is made immune or resistant to an infectious disease, typically by the administration of a vaccine.

13.

Correct: C, D, E (True)

Section B: Essay Questions

1.
List complications of phototherapy.
Show Answer

Answer:

  1. Dehydration: Increased insensible water loss through the skin.
  2. Loose stools (Diarrhea): Due to increased bowel transit and bile salts.
  3. Hyperthermia (Overheating): From the heat of the lights.
  4. Retinal damage: If eyes are not adequately covered.
  5. Bronze Baby Syndrome: Skin discoloration if phototherapy is used in conjugated hyperbilirubinemia.
  6. Skin Rash: Erythematous rash.
2.
Outline the routes through which infection can spread to the CNS resulting in meningitis in a child.
Show Answer

Answer:

  1. Hematogenous spread (Blood-borne): Most common route. Bacteria enter the bloodstream (bacteremia) from a distant site (e.g., lungs, nasopharynx) and cross the blood-brain barrier.
  2. Direct extension: Spread from adjacent infected structures, such as:
    • Otitis media (Middle ear infection)
    • Mastoiditis
    • Sinusitis
  3. Direct implantation: Following trauma (skull fracture), neurosurgery, or congenital defects (e.g., myelomeningocele, dermal sinus tracts).
  4. Neural spread: Along nerves (e.g., Rabies virus, Herpes Simplex), though this is less common for typical bacterial meningitis.
3.
Outline the 10 steps used in the management of severe malnutrition.
Show Answer

Answer:

Phase 1: Stabilization (Days 1-7)

  1. Treat/prevent Hypoglycemia.
  2. Treat/prevent Hypothermia.
  3. Treat/prevent Dehydration (Use ReSoMal, not standard ORS).
  4. Correct Electrolyte Imbalance (Potassium, Magnesium).
  5. Treat/prevent Infection (Broad-spectrum antibiotics).
  6. Correct Micronutrient Deficiencies (Vit A, Folic acid, etc. No iron yet).
  7. Start Cautious Feeding (F-75 formula).

Phase 2: Rehabilitation (Weeks 2-6)

  1. Achieve Catch-up Growth (Transition to F-100 or RUTF; add Iron).
  2. Provide Sensory Stimulation and emotional support.
  3. Prepare for Follow-up and discharge.
4.
Describe five congenital malformations commonly found in the gastro-intestinal tract.
Show Answer

Answer:

  1. Esophageal Atresia (with/without Tracheoesophageal Fistula): The esophagus ends blindly; food cannot reach the stomach.
  2. Hypertrophic Pyloric Stenosis: Thickening of the pylorus muscle causing gastric outlet obstruction and projectile vomiting.
  3. Duodenal Atresia: Complete closure of the duodenum ("Double bubble" sign on X-ray).
  4. Hirschsprung's Disease (Congenital Aganglionic Megacolon): Absence of nerve cells in the distal colon causing obstruction and constipation.
  5. Imperforate Anus (Anorectal Malformations): Absence or abnormal position of the anal opening.
  6. Malrotation and Volvulus: Abnormal rotation of the gut during development, predisposing to twisting (volvulus).
