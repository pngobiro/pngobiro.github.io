Section A: Multiple Choice Questions

30 Marks
1.
Rickets can present with the following features except:
a) Widening of the wrist joints
b) Pigeon chest deformity
c) Diarrhea
d) Genu valgum (knock knees)
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Diarrhea

Explanation: Rickets is a bone disorder caused by Vitamin D/Calcium deficiency. Classic signs include wrist widening, chest deformities (rachitic rosary/pigeon chest), and leg deformities (genu valgum/varum). Diarrhea is not a direct feature.

2.
One of the following is not a pre-renal cause of the acute kidney failure:
a) Burns
b) Acute Glomerulonephritis (AGN)
c) Shock
d) Hemorrhage
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Acute Glomerulonephritis (AGN)

Explanation: Pre-renal failure is caused by reduced blood flow to the kidneys (Shock, Hemorrhage, Burns/Dehydration). AGN is an intrinsic (renal) cause affecting the glomeruli directly.

3.
True in regards to hemophilia:
a) Hemophilia A is due to deficiency of Christmas factor
b) All daughters of hemophiliacs are carriers
c) Hemophilia B is the most common subtype
d) All sons of hemophiliacs are carriers
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) All daughters of hemophiliacs are carriers

Explanation: Hemophilia is X-linked recessive. A father transmits his X chromosome to all his daughters. Since his X is affected, all daughters become obligate carriers.
- Hemophilia A is Factor VIII deficiency (Christmas factor is Factor IX/Hemophilia B).
- Hemophilia A is more common than B.
- Sons receive the Y chromosome from the father, so they are not affected/carriers if the mother is normal.

4.
The following will prevent neonatal infections except:
a) Keeping the child warm
b) Hand washing
c) Antenatal care
d) Daily cord cleaning with saliva
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Daily cord cleaning with saliva

Explanation: Applying saliva (or cow dung/mud) to the cord is a harmful practice that introduces bacteria and causes neonatal sepsis/tetanus. Cord care should be dry or with Chlorhexidine. Keeping warm prevents hypothermia-induced immune suppression.

5.
Which of the following measures is NOT used in the management of Cyanotic spells in a patient with TOF?
a) Knee chest position
b) High flow oxygen
c) Morphine should never be used since it may cause respiratory deterioration
d) Propranolol
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Morphine should never be used...

Explanation: This statement is false (and thus the correct answer to "Which is NOT a valid measure/principle"). Morphine IS used in hypercyanotic spells to suppress the respiratory drive and relax the infundibular spasm. Propranolol, oxygen, and knee-chest position are also standard treatments.

6.
One of the following is not a complication of meningitis:
a) Mental retardation
b) Hydrocephalus
c) Tuberculosis
d) Cerebral abscess
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Tuberculosis

Explanation: Tuberculosis is a specific infection (cause), not a complication of general meningitis. TB can cause meningitis (TB Meningitis), but you don't "get TB" as a complication of pyogenic meningitis. Retardation, hydrocephalus, and abscesses are complications.

7.
Which statement is true concerning HIV exposed infants?
a) They should never be breastfed.
b) Nevirapine has no role in its management.
c) Positive antibody test after 18 months defines if the child is HIV positive.
d) They do not require treatment with ARVS.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Positive antibody test after 18 months defines if the child is HIV positive.

Explanation: Before 18 months, maternal antibodies persist, so PCR is required for diagnosis. After 18 months, an antibody test is diagnostic.
- Breastfeeding IS recommended (with maternal ART) in developing countries (WHO).
- Nevirapine IS used for prophylaxis.
- They require prophylaxis (NVP/AZT).

8.
The following are predisposing factors of PTB except:
a) Sharing utensils
b) Diabetes mellitus
c) HIV / AIDS
d) Malnutrition
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Sharing utensils

Explanation: Pulmonary TB is airborne (droplet nuclei). It is not spread by sharing utensils/food. HIV, Malnutrition, and Diabetes weaken the immune system, predisposing to active TB.

9.
One of the following is a feature of severe malaria:
a) Hypothermia
b) Sweating
c) Increased appetite
d) Convulsions
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Convulsions

Explanation: Multiple convulsions (>2 in 24h) are a defining criterion for Severe Malaria (Cerebral Malaria). Hypothermia can occur but is less specific/common than fever; sweating is a symptom of uncomplicated malaria; appetite is usually lost.

10.
All are complications of long term use of steroids therapy except:
a) Skin atrophy
b) Decreased immune response
c) Hypersensitivity reaction
d) Growth retardation
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Hypersensitivity reaction

Explanation: Steroids are immunosuppressants used to treat hypersensitivity/allergic reactions. They don't typically cause them (though rare anaphylaxis to formulation exists). Long term side effects include skin thinning (atrophy), growth suppression, and immunosuppression.

12.
Concerning Burkitt's lymphoma:
a) Abdomen is the commonest site
b) It is a slow growing tumor
c) Occurs commonly in children of more than 10yrs
d) Epstein Barr virus are highly related
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Epstein Barr virus are highly related

Explanation: EBV is strongly associated with Endemic Burkitt's Lymphoma.
- In endemic areas (Kenya), the Jaw is the commonest site (though abdomen is common in sporadic).
- It is the fastest growing human tumor (doubling time 24h).
- Peak incidence is 4-7 years (under 10).

14.
The following feature is found in a patient with diarrhea and shock:
a) Pulse of normal character
b) Capillary refill of more than 3 seconds
c) Warm extremities
d) AVPU equals to A
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Capillary refill of more than 3 seconds

Explanation: Shock is characterized by poor perfusion: Prolonged capillary refill (>3s), weak/thready pulse, cold extremities, and altered consciousness (AVPU usually not A - Alert, often lethargic/unconscious).

15.
In IMCI danger signs includes the following except:
a) Convulsing now
b) Vomiting everything
c) Inability to feed
d) Hyperpyrexia
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Hyperpyrexia

Explanation: The 4 General Danger Signs in IMCI are: 1. Unable to drink/breastfeed, 2. Vomits everything, 3. Convulsions (history or now), 4. Lethargic or Unconscious. Hyperpyrexia is a symptom of severe disease (like malaria/meningitis) but not one of the 4 cardinal "General Danger Signs".

18.
Measles vaccine:
a) Is a toxoid
b) May lead to constipation
c) Is a live attenuated
d) Contraindicated in malnutrition
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Is a live attenuated

Explanation: Measles vaccine contains live weakened virus. It is NOT contraindicated in malnutrition; in fact, malnourished children are at higher risk of severe measles and should be prioritized for vaccination.

19.
The least likely cause of death in severe marasmic kwashiorkor is:
a) Hypothermia
b) Hypoglycaemia
c) Flaky skin dermatitis
d) Dehydration
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Flaky skin dermatitis

Explanation: The major killers in severe malnutrition are metabolic complications: Hypoglycemia, Hypothermia, Dehydration/Electrolyte imbalance, and Sepsis. Dermatitis is a clinical feature but not a cause of death.

Section B: Scenarios & True/False

30 Marks
1.
In pyogenic meningitis, cerebral spinal fluid (CSF) has the following characteristics:
a) Cloudy in color (True)
b) Protein is < 0.4gm/l (False - Protein is high >1g/L)
c) Sugar is > 2.5mmo/l (False - Sugar is low <2.2)
d) Polymorphs are in hundreds to thousands (True)
e) Protein is high (True)
Show Answer

Correct/True Options: a, d, e

Explanation: Bacterial (pyogenic) meningitis CSF is turbid/cloudy, has elevated protein, low glucose (bacteria consume it), and high neutrophils (polymorphs).

2.
The following are causes of finger clubbing:
a) Empyema thoracis (True)
b) Pulmonary tuberculosis (True - if chronic/extensive)
c) Acute leukemia (False)
d) Cyanotic congenital heart diseases (True)
e) Acyanotic congenital heart diseases (False)
Show Answer

Correct/True Options: a, b, d

Explanation: Clubbing is associated with chronic hypoxia or suppurative lung disease (Empyema, Bronchiectasis, CF, Cyanotic heart disease, Infective endocarditis).

3.
When admitting a child with severe acute malnutrition, the key clinical features for admission are:
a) Oedema of both feet (True)
b) Weight and height (False - used for Z-score calculation, but not a feature itself)
c) Visible severe wasting (True)
d) MUAC (True - MUAC < 11.5cm)
e) Flaky paint dermatoses (False - sign of kwashiorkor but not the primary admission criteria)
Show Answer

Correct/True Options: a, c, d

Explanation: Admission criteria for SAM are: MUAC < 11.5cm, OR WHZ < -3SD (Severe Wasting), OR Bilateral Pitting Edema.

5.
The 1st line treatment for HIV/AIDS regimens for children below three years are:
a) Abacavir/Lamivudine/Lopinavir/ritonavir (True)
b) Zidovudine/lamivudine/ Lopinavir/ritonavir (True - AZT is alternative to ABC)
c) Abacavir/lamivudine/nevirapine (False - LPV/r preferred for <3yrs)
d) Abacavir/lamivudine/efarirenz (False - EFV not used <3yrs)
e) Zidovudine/lamivudine/nevirapine (False)
Show Answer

Correct/True Options: a, b

Explanation: Guidelines recommend Lopinavir/ritonavir (LPV/r) based regimens for children < 3 years regardless of NNRTI exposure, due to high resistance to Nevirapine. Backbone is usually ABC/3TC or AZT/3TC.

14.
Features of UTI in a neonate include:
a) Failure to thrive (True)
b) Jaundice (True)
c) Diarrhoea (True)
d) Fever (True)
e) Increased WBCs in urine (True)
Show Answer

Correct/True Options: All are True

Explanation: Neonatal UTI symptoms are non-specific. They present with sepsis-like signs: fever, poor feeding, jaundice (conjugated), vomiting/diarrhea, and failure to thrive.

20.
Lawrence is a two-year-old child admitted with bacterial pneumonia. He should be treated with:
a) Parenteral gentamycin alone
b) Crystalline penicillin alone
c) Intramuscular streptomycin
d) Parenteral gentamycin and crystalline penicillin (True)
e) Intramuscular procaine penicillin
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Parenteral gentamycin and crystalline penicillin

Explanation: Standard inpatient management for severe pneumonia in children involves a combination of Benzylpenicillin (Crystalline Penicillin) and Gentamycin to cover both Gram-positive (Pneumococcus) and Gram-negative organisms.
