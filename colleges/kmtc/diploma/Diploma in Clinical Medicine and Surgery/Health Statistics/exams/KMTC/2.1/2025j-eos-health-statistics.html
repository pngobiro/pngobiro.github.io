Section 1: Multiple Choice Questions

Use the following to answer questions (1-2):

Suppose that you have the following set of numbers: 8, 2, 5, 3, 6, 7, 4, 5.

1.
What is the value of the mean?
a) 5
b) 4.25
c) 4
d) 6.1
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) 5

Explanation: The mean is the sum of all values divided by the number of values.

$$ \text{Sum} = 8 + 2 + 5 + 3 + 6 + 7 + 4 + 5 = 40 $$

$$ \text{Count} (n) = 8 $$

$$ \text{Mean} (\bar{x}) = \frac{40}{8} = 5 $$

2.
The value of the mode is ........
a) 2
b) 5
c) 4
d) 3
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) 5

Explanation: The mode is the number that appears most frequently in the dataset.

  • 5 appears twice.
  • 8, 2, 3, 6, 7, 4 each appear once.

Therefore, 5 is the mode.

3.
The variance is
a) The sum of all observations
b) Arithmetic averages of the mean
c) Sum of squares of deviations from the mean
d) Square root of standard deviation
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Sum of squares of deviations from the mean (Best Fit)

Explanation: While the strict definition of variance is the average of the squared deviations (Sum of Squares divided by N or N-1), option C describes the core numerator component of the formula: $\sum(x - \bar{x})^2$. Among the choices provided:

  • a) is just the Sum ($\sum x$).
  • b) is meaningless.
  • d) is incorrect because Standard Deviation is the square root of Variance ($SD = \sqrt{Var}$), implying Variance is the square of SD, not the square root.

Thus, C is the intended answer concept, referring to the variation component.

4.
The most appropriate measure of central tendency for the values 2, 3, 6, and 100 is the ........
a) mean
b) median
c) mode
d) midrange
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) median

Explanation: The dataset contains an extreme outlier (100) compared to the other values (2, 3, 6). The median is a robust statistic that is not skewed by outliers, whereas the mean would be significantly pulled towards 100, making it unrepresentative of the "center" of the data.

5.
If the sample is not a representative of the population
a) Its said to be biased
b) The conclusions made about population shall be true
c) Pilot study is needed to get correct values
d) Confounders are a must
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Its said to be biased

Explanation: A sample that does not accurately reflect the population it was drawn from is termed biased. Bias introduces systematic error, leading to incorrect conclusions about the population.

6.
What is the statistical term for the mean that is obtained by using the data values of a sample drawn from a specific population.
a) parameter
b) statistic
c) variable
d) quantity
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) statistic

Explanation: A numerical measure describing a characteristic of a sample is called a Statistic (e.g., sample mean $\bar{x}$). A numerical measure describing a characteristic of a population is called a Parameter (e.g., population mean $\mu$).

Memory Aid: Sample = Statistic. Population = Parameter.

7.
The stages of a malignant disease (cancer) is recorded using the symbols 0, I, II, III, IV. We say that the scale used is:
a) Alphanumeric
b) Numerical
c) Ordinal
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Ordinal

Explanation: An Ordinal scale categorizes data in a specific order or rank. Stage I is less severe than Stage II, which is less than Stage III, etc. However, the exact "distance" between stages is not measurable or equal (unlike Interval/Ratio scales).

Section 2: Short Answer Questions

3.
Compute the Variance of 5, 7, 3? (5 marks)
Show Answer

Step 1: Calculate the Mean ($\bar{x}$)

$$ \bar{x} = \frac{5 + 7 + 3}{3} = \frac{15}{3} = 5 $$

Step 2: Calculate Deviations from the Mean ($x - \bar{x}$)

  • $5 - 5 = 0$
  • $7 - 5 = 2$
  • $3 - 5 = -2$

Step 3: Square the Deviations $(x - \bar{x})^2$

  • $0^2 = 0$
  • $2^2 = 4$
  • $(-2)^2 = 4$

Step 4: Sum of Squares ($\sum(x - \bar{x})^2$)

$$ 0 + 4 + 4 = 8 $$

Step 5: Calculate Sample Variance ($s^2$)

$$ s^2 = \frac{\text{Sum of Squares}}{n - 1} $$

$$ s^2 = \frac{8}{3 - 1} = \frac{8}{2} = 4 $$

Answer: The Variance is 4.

4.
With examples outline the four main scales of measurements used in statistics (5 marks)
Show Answer
  1. Nominal Scale: Used for labeling variables without any quantitative value. The data can only be categorized.
    Example: Gender (Male/Female), Blood Type (A, B, AB, O), Eye Color.
  2. Ordinal Scale: The order of the values is important and significant, but the differences between each one are not really known.
    Example: Satisfaction rating (Satisfied, Neutral, Dissatisfied), Pain scale (Mild, Moderate, Severe), Cancer stages (I, II, III).
  3. Interval Scale: Numeric scales in which we know both the order and the exact differences between the values. It has no "true zero" (zero does not mean absence of the variable).
    Example: Temperature in Celsius or Fahrenheit (0°C is not "no heat"), Calendar years.
  4. Ratio Scale: Has all the features of interval scales but also has a clear definition of zero (true zero).
    Example: Height, Weight, Age, Blood Pressure (0 kg means no weight).
5.
Identify the three measures of central tendancy and state their properties (5 marks)
Show Answer
  1. Mean (Arithmetic Average):
    • Sum of all values divided by the number of observations.
    • Property: It is the most commonly used measure but is sensitive to outliers (extreme values). Uses every data point.
  2. Median:
    • The middle value when data is arranged in ascending or descending order.
    • Property: It is robust (not affected by outliers) and is preferred for skewed distributions. Divides the data into two equal halves.
  3. Mode:
    • The value that appears most frequently in the dataset.
    • Property: Can be used for categorical (nominal) data. A set may have no mode, one mode (unimodal), or multiple modes (multimodal).
6.
Distinguish the following as used in data presentation- Frequency Distribution Class mid point and class interval, (5 marks)
Show Answer

Frequency Distribution:

A table that displays the frequency of various outcomes in a sample. It summarizes raw data by grouping it into classes and showing how many data points fall into each class.

Class Mid-point (Class Mark):

The central value of a class interval, used to represent the class in calculations (like mean) and graphs (like frequency polygons). calculated as:

$$ \text{Midpoint} = \frac{\text{Lower Limit} + \text{Upper Limit}}{2} $$

Class Interval (Class Width):

The range of values covered by a single class. It represents the width of the bin in a histogram. Calculated as:

$$ \text{Interval} = \text{Upper Boundary} - \text{Lower Boundary} $$

Or the difference between consecutive lower class limits.

7.
State four properties of a bar graph? (5 marks)
Show Answer
  1. Gaps between bars: Unlike histograms, bar graphs have equal spaces between the bars to indicate that the categories are distinct (discrete/nominal data).
  2. Height represents frequency: The length or height of the bar corresponds to the frequency or value of the category it represents.
  3. Equal Width: All bars should have the same width for visual consistency, though the width itself has no mathematical meaning.
  4. Categorical Axis: One axis represents the categories (nominal/ordinal data) and the other represents the numerical value (frequency/count).
  5. Base Line: All bars usually start from a common zero baseline.
8.
With an illustration, describe the properties of a normal distribution curve? (5 marks)
Show Answer

Properties of Normal Distribution Curve:

  1. Bell-Shaped and Symmetrical: The curve is perfectly symmetric around the center. The left side is a mirror image of the right.
  2. Central Tendency: The Mean, Median, and Mode are all equal and located at the peak (center) of the curve.
  3. Asymptotic: The tails of the curve approach the x-axis indefinitely but never touch or cross it.
  4. Total Area: The total area under the curve is equal to 1 (or 100%).
  5. Empirical Rule:
    • ~68% of data falls within 1 Standard Deviation ($\sigma$) of the mean.
    • ~95% falls within 2 $\sigma$.
    • ~99.7% falls within 3 $\sigma$.

10.
The following were student scores in statics CAT marked out of 50.
Marks No of students
0 - 104
10 - 206
20 - 309
30 - 404
40 - 502

a) Calculate the mean (4 marks)
b) Calculate the variance (8 marks)
c) What's the standard deviation (4 marks)
d) What the Coefficience of variation (4 marks)
Show Answer
Class Midpoint ($x$) Freq ($f$) $fx$ $fx^2$
0-105420100
10-20156901350
20-302592255625
30-403541404900
40-50452904050
Total2556516025

a) Calculate the Mean:

$$ \bar{x} = \frac{\sum fx}{\sum f} = \frac{565}{25} = 22.6 $$

b) Calculate the Variance ($s^2$):

Using the formula: $$ s^2 = \frac{\sum fx^2 - \frac{(\sum fx)^2}{n}}{n-1} $$

$$ s^2 = \frac{16025 - \frac{565^2}{25}}{24} $$

$$ \frac{565^2}{25} = \frac{319225}{25} = 12769 $$

$$ \text{Numerator} = 16025 - 12769 = 3256 $$

$$ s^2 = \frac{3256}{24} = 135.67 $$

c) Calculate the Standard Deviation ($s$):

$$ s = \sqrt{\text{Variance}} = \sqrt{135.67} \approx 11.65 $$

d) Coefficient of Variation (CV):

$$ CV = \left( \frac{s}{\bar{x}} \right) \times 100 $$

$$ CV = \left( \frac{11.65}{22.6} \right) \times 100 \approx 51.55\% $$
