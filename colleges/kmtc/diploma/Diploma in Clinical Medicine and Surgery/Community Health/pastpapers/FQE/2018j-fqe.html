Section A: Multiple Choice Questions

10 Marks
1.
Quarantine:
a) Involves separation of people who are sick from those who are not sick
b) Restricts movement of healthy people who may develop the disease after exposure
c) Personal protection equipment (PPE) is required for contact with people who are not ill
d) Allows for appropriate treatment in a hospital setting
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Restricts movement of healthy people who may develop the disease after exposure

Quarantine applies to healthy contacts exposed to a disease, whereas Isolation applies to sick individuals (Option a).

2.
Modifiable risk factors for development of coronary heart disease include:
a) Diabetes, age and smoking
b) Diabetes, physical inactivity and smoking
c) Hypertension, sex and obesity
d) Hypertension, smoking and age
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Diabetes, physical inactivity and smoking

Age and Sex are non-modifiable risk factors. Options a, c, and d include non-modifiable factors. Option b lists three modifiable factors.

3.
In control of communicable diseases interruption of transmission involves;
a) Immunization
b) Isolation
c) Surveillance
d) Vector control
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Vector control (or b Isolation)

Vector control directly interrupts transmission. Immunization protects the host. Surveillance is detection. Isolation targets the source. (Vector control is the classic "interruption of route" example).

4.
Environmental factors influencing prevalence of asthma include:
a) Cold air, drugs and gender
b) Allergens, drugs and infections
c) Cold air, Drugs and genetic
d) Allergens, cold air and gender
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Allergens, drugs and infections

Environmental triggers include allergens (pollen, dust), infections, and drugs (aspirin). Cold air is physical. Gender and genetics are host factors.

5.
On emerging and re-emerging (resurging) diseases:
a) Emerging diseases in Kenya include leprosy, poliomyelitis and SARS
b) Emerging diseases include Ebola, Marburg, malaria and tuberculosis
c) Emerging diseases occur due to breakdown in health infrastructure
d) Malaria and tuberculosis are resurging global pandemics
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Malaria and tuberculosis are resurging global pandemics

Malaria and TB are classic examples of re-emerging (resurging) diseases. Ebola and Marburg are emerging.

6.
The following disease give lifelong immunity after an attack:
a) Diphtheria
b) Mumps
c) Tetanus
d) Typhoid
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Mumps

Natural infection with Mumps (and Measles, Rubella) confers lifelong immunity. Tetanus, Diphtheria, and Typhoid do not.

7.
Regarding poliomyelitis:
a) Outbreaks would be greatly reduced if majority of individuals in a community attain herd immunity
b) The polio vaccine should not be given to adults
c) Other vaccines can be administered together with the polio vaccine during outbreaks
d) Physiotherapy and occupational therapy are effective during the acute phase
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Outbreaks would be greatly reduced if majority of individuals in a community attain herd immunity

Herd immunity is key to stopping Polio transmission. Physio is contraindicated in the acute phase (rest is needed).

8.
Natural history of non-communicable diseases entails:
a) Progress over a long duration of time
b) Association with microorganisms
c) Easy of prevention at the community level
d) Rapid progression
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Progress over a long duration of time

NCDs are chronic conditions with slow progression and long duration.

9.
Water borne (washed) diseases include:
a) Cholera, typhoid and dysenteries
b) Malaria, cholera and filariasis
c) Skin infections, trachoma and schistosomiasis
d) Trachoma, malaria and typhoid
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Skin infections, trachoma and schistosomiasis

Correction: The question phrasing "Water borne (washed)" is ambiguous. Cholera/Typhoid are Water-Borne. Trachoma/Skin infections are Water-Washed. Option A lists purely water-borne. Option C lists water-washed (skin/trachoma) and water-based (schisto). Given typical exam patterns, C is often the intended answer for "Water Washed" despite the typo grouping.

10.
Herd immunity:
a) Is conferred when a high percentage of the population is vaccinated
b) Is conferred when a high percentage of the population has acquired passive immunity
c) Offers protection against all vaccine-preventable diseases
d) gives a high level of individual protection thus a good alternative to getting vaccinated.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Is conferred when a high percentage of the population is vaccinated

3.

a) Ascariasis TRUE
b) Hookworm TRUE
c) Schistomiasis TRUE
d) Ebola virus FALSE
e) Hepatitis B TRUE
Show Answer Key

Key: T T T F T

Section C: Short Answer Questions

40 Marks
1.
Outline the risk factors for Ischemic heart disease (10 marks)
Show Answer

Answer:

a) Fixed factors (2 marks): Age, Male sex, Positive family history.

b) Potentially changeable with treatment (5 marks):
- Hyperlipidaemia
- Cigarette smoking
- Hypertension
- Diabetes mellitus

c) Weak Association (3 marks):
- Personality (Type A)
- Obesity
- Physical inactivity
- Gout
- Contraceptive pill
- Heavy alcohol consumption

2.
Give five (5) factors that would trigger the authorities to take a rapid action to control a disease outbreak. (10 marks)
Show Answer

Answer: (2 marks for each)

  1. Severity of the disease: High case fatality or serious complications.
  2. Risk of spread: Factors like crowding, poor sanitation, water availability, which facilitate transmission.
  3. Number of cases: More than expected for the population size.
  4. Risk of social and economic disruption: Threat to trade, tourism, transport, or schools.
  5. Danger of spread: Potential for regional, national, or international spread.
3.
Concerning cancers of the reproductive system in women, state ten (10) risk factors and its possible type of cancer (10 marks)
Show Answer

Answer: (Any ten - 1 mark each)

  • During menopause - Cancer of the breast
  • Low parity - Cancer of endometrium or breast
  • High parity - Cancer of cervix
  • Spontaneous abortions - Cancer of endometrium
  • History of pelvic radiations - Ca ovary
  • Exposure to chemicals - Ca ovary
  • Promiscuity - Cancer of cervix, vagina
  • Genital herpes - Cancer of cervix
  • Human papilloma virus - Cancer of cervix
  • History of infertility - Endometrial cancer
  • Use of hormonal drugs - Endometrial cancer, ovarian
  • Multiple pregnancies - Cancer of cervix
4.
Outline five (5) current HIV/AIDS prevention strategies in Kenya (10 marks)
Show Answer

Answer: (Any 5 - 2 marks each)

  1. Health education: Emphasis on prevention and adherence to treatment.
  2. Abstinence and safe sex: Reduce incidence and acquisition of new strains.
  3. Behavior change modification: Reducing sexual partners.
  4. Treatment as Prevention: Early treatment of HIV and STIs.
  5. Condom distribution: In institutions, bars, recreation centers.
  6. Harm reduction: Clean syringes and needles for drug users.
  7. Exchange programs: Sharing experiences on positive living.
  8. PMTCT/ART: Improve quality of life and prevent vertical transmission.
  9. VMMC: Voluntary Medical Male Circumcision.
  10. Counseling services: For adolescents to encourage behavior change.

Section D: Long Answer Questions

40 Marks
1.
Prevalence of obesity among communities is on the rise.
a) Describe the risk factors for obesity? (10 marks)
b) Outline five (5) effects of obesity (10 marks)
Show Answer

Answer:

a) Risk Factors (2 marks each):

  • Genetics/hereditary: Influence body fat distribution and metabolism.
  • Age: Common among middle aged, but increasing in childhood/adolescence.
  • Socio-economic: Affluent lifestyles often lead to higher calorie intake and sedentary behavior.
  • Drug use: Steroids, contraceptives, antidepressants can cause weight gain.
  • Endocrine factors: Hormonal influence at adolescence, pregnancy, menopause (e.g., Hypothyroidism, Cushing's).

b) Effects of Obesity (2 marks each):

  1. Type 2 Diabetes: Insulin resistance.
  2. Metabolic disorders: Gout, gall stones, cataracts.
  3. Cardiovascular complications: Atherosclerosis, hypertension, ischaemic heart disease.
  4. Mechanical disabilities: Osteoarthritis, back pain, injuries from falls.
  5. Psychological effects: Depression, anxiety, low self-esteem, psychosexual challenges.
  6. Reduced life expectancy.
2.
Outline the modes of communicable disease transmission (20 marks)
Show Answer

Answer:

Mode Type Description/Example
Direct Transmission Contact Direct body contact (skin-to-skin) or contact with soiled objects (fomites). E.g., Scabies, Leprosy.
Droplet Inhalation of droplets expelled during sneezing/coughing within close range. E.g., Flu, Pertussis.
Indirect Transmission Air-borne Infective agents float in air for long distances. E.g., TB, Measles.
Vehicle-borne Ingestion of contaminated food or water (fecal-oral). E.g., Cholera, Typhoid.
Vector-borne Transmission via insects (Mechanical or Biological). E.g., Malaria, Plague.
Blood/Body fluid Transfusion, needle sharing, sexual intercourse. E.g., HIV, Hep B.
Congenital (Vertical) Mother to fetus. E.g., Syphilis, HIV.
Soil/Fomites Contact with contaminated soil (Hookworm, Tetanus).
