1.
Answer the following questions about community under the following sub headings;
a) Define the term community (2marks)
b) List any four types of communities (2marks)
c) State characteristics of members of a community (2marks)
d) With reference to a specific community, state factors that hold that Community together (4marks)
Show Answer

a) Definition of Community:

A community is a social group of any size whose members reside in a specific locality, share government, and often have a common cultural and historical heritage. It can also be defined as a group of people sharing common interests, values, and norms.

b) Types of Communities:

  • Geographical/Territorial communities: (e.g., Neighborhoods, Villages).
  • Functional/Interest-based communities: (e.g., Professional associations, Religious groups).
  • Virtual/Online communities: (e.g., Social media groups).
  • Special needs communities: (e.g., Support groups for specific diseases).

c) Characteristics of members:

  • Shared identity or sense of belonging.
  • Common values and norms.
  • Common language or communication style.
  • Mutual interdependence (reliance on each other).

d) Factors holding a community together (e.g., A rural village community):

  1. Kinship/Blood ties: Extended family relationships create strong bonds.
  2. Shared Beliefs/Religion: Common spiritual practices unify the group.
  3. Economic Interdependence: Communal farming or trade where members rely on each other for livelihood.
  4. Cultural Traditions: Shared ceremonies (birth, marriage, death) and festivals reinforce social cohesion.
  5. Leadership Structure: Respect for elders or local chiefs provides order and conflict resolution.
2.
a) Outline causes of malnutrition using UNICEF conceptual framework (5marks)
b) Enumerate five hindrances facing the practice of breastfeeding (2.5 marks)
c) Outline factors that promote and support breastfeeding (2.5marks)
Show Answer

a) Causes of Malnutrition (UNICEF Conceptual Framework):

  1. Immediate Causes (Individual Level):
    • Inadequate dietary intake (not eating enough nutrients).
    • Disease (infections like diarrhea or worms interfering with absorption).
  2. Underlying Causes (Household/Family Level):
    • Household food insecurity (lack of access to food).
    • Inadequate maternal and child care practices (poor weaning, neglect).
    • Poor water/sanitation and inadequate health services.
  3. Basic Causes (Societal Level):
    • Political, economic, and ideological structure (poverty, unequal distribution of resources).
    • Lack of education and potential resources.

b) Hindrances to Breastfeeding:

  • Return to work/school (lack of maternity leave or supportive environment).
  • Maternal illness (e.g., severe breast abscess, though rare).
  • Misconceptions/Myths (e.g., "milk is not enough").
  • Stigma associated with breastfeeding in public.
  • Aggressive marketing of breastmilk substitutes (formula).

c) Factors Promoting/Supporting Breastfeeding:

  • Baby Friendly Hospital Initiative (BFHI): Hospitals encouraging early initiation and rooming-in.
  • Workplace Support: Lactation rooms and nursing breaks.
  • Health Education: Counseling mothers during ANC and PNC.
  • Legislation: Laws regulating marketing of breastmilk substitutes (BMS Act).
  • Community Support Groups: Mother-to-mother support groups.
3.
The following data was obtained from a youth friendly clinic on the number of youths seeking counseling on reproductive health during the first ten days of school holidays at a local sub county hospital 15, 20, 16, 14, 28, 30, 15, 10, 16, 32
a) Determine the range (1mark)
b) What is the mean? (2marks)
c) Calculate the standard deviation for the data (3marks)
d) Represent the data in a bar graph (4marks)
Show Answer

Data set: 15, 20, 16, 14, 28, 30, 15, 10, 16, 32

Ordered Data: 10, 14, 15, 15, 16, 16, 20, 28, 30, 32 (Count, n = 10)

a) Range:

Range = Highest Value - Lowest Value

Range = $32 - 10 = 22$

b) Mean:

Mean ($\bar{x}$) = $\frac{\sum x}{n}$

Sum = $15 + 20 + 16 + 14 + 28 + 30 + 15 + 10 + 16 + 32 = 196$

Mean = $\frac{196}{10} = 19.6$

c) Standard Deviation:

Formula: $SD = \sqrt{\frac{\sum(x - \bar{x})^2}{n}}$

  1. $x$: 10, 14, 15, 15, 16, 16, 20, 28, 30, 32
  2. Deviations $(x - 19.6)$: -9.6, -5.6, -4.6, -4.6, -3.6, -3.6, 0.4, 8.4, 10.4, 12.4
  3. Squared Deviations: 92.16, 31.36, 21.16, 21.16, 12.96, 12.96, 0.16, 70.56, 108.16, 153.76
  4. Sum of Squares = $524.4$
  5. Variance = $\frac{524.4}{10} = 52.44$
  6. SD = $\sqrt{52.44} \approx 7.24$

Note: If sample standard deviation ($n-1$) is used: $\frac{524.4}{9} = 58.26$, $\sqrt{58.26} \approx 7.63$. Given $n=10$ is small, $n-1$ is statistically preferred, but in basic KMTC stats, population formula ($n$) is often accepted unless specified. Both 7.24 and 7.63 would likely be marked correct with working shown.

d) Bar Graph Representation:

Drawing Instructions:

  • X-axis (Horizontal): Days (Day 1 to Day 10).
  • Y-axis (Vertical): Number of Youths (Scale 0 to 35).
  • Draw separate vertical bars for each day corresponding to the values:
    Day 1: 15, Day 2: 20, Day 3: 16, Day 4: 14, Day 5: 28,
    Day 6: 30, Day 7: 15, Day 8: 10, Day 9: 16, Day 10: 32.
  • Bars should be of equal width with gaps between them.
  • Title: Number of Youths Seeking Counseling Over 10 Days.
4.
Regarding refuse;
a) List five effects of indiscriminate disposal of refuse (4marks)
b) Classify hospital refuse and give examples of where each is stored (6Marks)
Show Answer

a) Effects of Indiscriminate Disposal:

  1. Breeding of vectors: Flies, mosquitoes, and rats breed in piles of refuse, spreading diseases like malaria and cholera.
  2. Air pollution: Bad odors (smell) and toxic smoke if burned indiscriminately.
  3. Water pollution: Leachate can contaminate groundwater or runoff can pollute rivers.
  4. Aesthetic nuisance: Makes the environment look dirty and unsightly.
  5. Fire hazard: Piles of waste can catch fire spontaneously or accidentally.
  6. Injuries: Sharp objects (glass, metal) can injure children or animals scavenging.

b) Classification of Hospital Refuse & Storage:

Class/Category Color Code / Storage Container Examples
Infectious Waste Yellow (Liner/Bin) Cotton wool, dressings, used gloves, blood bags.
Sharps Yellow Safety Box (Puncture-proof) Needles, scalpels, broken ampoules.
Pathological/Anatomical Waste Yellow (or Red in some systems) Placentas, body parts, tissues.
General/Non-infectious Waste Black Bin/Liner Office paper, food wrappers, boxes.
Pharmaceutical/Chemical Waste Brown (often) Expired drugs, laboratory reagents.
Highly Infectious Red (marked Biohazard) Lab cultures, Ebola isolation waste.
5.
Answer the following questions about tuberculosis under the following sub headings
a) What is the causative agent? (1mark)
b) How is it spread (1mark)
c) Outline its prevention and control (8marks)
Show Answer

a) Causative Agent:

Mycobacterium tuberculosis (Bacteria).

b) Mode of Spread:

Airborne droplet transmission. It spreads when an infected person coughs, sneezes, or talks, releasing droplets containing the bacteria which are then inhaled by others.

c) Prevention and Control:

  1. BCG Vaccination: Administered at birth to protect children against severe forms of TB (Meningitis/Miliary TB).
  2. Early Detection and Treatment: Identifying cases early (sputum tests, X-ray) and treating them promptly to stop transmission (GeneXpert).
  3. Directly Observed Therapy (DOTS): Ensuring patients swallow their medication under supervision to prevent drug resistance and failure.
  4. Contact Tracing: Screening family members and close contacts of confirmed TB patients.
  5. Isolation/Infection Control: Keeping infectious patients in well-ventilated areas and teaching cough etiquette (covering mouth).
  6. Isoniazid Preventive Therapy (IPT): Giving prophylactic drugs to high-risk groups (e.g., HIV+ patients, children under 5 in contact with active TB).
  7. Health Education: Educating the community about symptoms (cough > 2 weeks) and the importance of adherence.
  8. Improving Ventilation: Ensuring houses and public transport (Matatus) have open windows to dilute infectious droplets.

1.
a) Explain the components of the epidemiologic triad (8 Marks)
b) Enumerate the major causes of maternal mortality in Kenya. (5 Marks)
c) Describe strategies used to control maternal mortality in Kenya (7marks)
Show Answer

a) Components of the Epidemiologic Triad:

The epidemiologic triad describes the interaction between three key factors required for disease transmission:

  1. Agent: The entity that causes the disease. It can be:
    • Biological (Bacteria, Virus, Parasite).
    • Chemical (Poison, Alcohol).
    • Physical (Trauma, Radiation).
  2. Host: The susceptible human or animal that harbors the disease. Host factors include:
    • Age, Sex, Genetics.
    • Immunity status.
    • Behavior and lifestyle.
  3. Environment: The external factors that allow transmission. It includes:
    • Physical (Climate, Water, Housing).
    • Social (Culture, Poverty).
    • Biological (Vectors like mosquitoes).

Disease occurs when there is an interaction between the Agent and the Host in a conducive Environment.

b) Major Causes of Maternal Mortality in Kenya:

  • Post-partum Hemorrhage (PPH): Severe bleeding after childbirth (Leading cause).
  • Sepsis/Infection: Infections resulting from unhygienic deliveries.
  • Hypertensive disorders: Eclampsia and Pre-eclampsia.
  • Obstructed Labour: Due to small pelvis or large baby, leading to rupture of uterus.
  • Unsafe Abortion: Complications arising from abortions performed by unskilled persons.

c) Strategies to Control Maternal Mortality in Kenya:

  1. Free Maternity Services (Linda Mama): Removing financial barriers to encourage hospital deliveries.
  2. Skilled Birth Attendance: Ensuring all deliveries are conducted by trained professionals (nurses/midwives) rather than Traditional Birth Attendants.
  3. Antenatal Care (ANC): Promoting at least 4 ANC visits to monitor pregnancy and treat complications early (e.g., anemia, syphilis).
  4. Emergency Obstetric Care (EmOC): Strengthening facilities to handle emergencies like C-sections and blood transfusions.
  5. Family Planning: promoting spacing of pregnancies to ensure mothers recover fully and avoiding high-risk pregnancies (too young, too old, too many).
  6. Community Education: Sensitizing women on danger signs of pregnancy (bleeding, headache) and individual birth preparedness.
  7. Iron and Folic Acid Supplementation: Preventing anemia during pregnancy.

a) Outline the lifecycle of Ancylostoma duodenale using a diagrammatic representation (14 marks)
b) Outline the control and preventive measures of Ancylostoma duodenale (6 Marks)
Show Answer

a) Lifecycle of Ancylostoma duodenale (Hookworm):

Diagrammatic Representation (Text Flow):

1. EGGS in Soil
   (Passed in feces of infected person)
       |
       v
2. RHABDITIFORM LARVAE
   (Hatch in soil after 1-2 days)
       |
       v
3. FILARIFORM LARVAE
   (Infective stage develops in 5-10 days)
       |
       v
4. PENETRATION
   (Larvae penetrate skin of feet/legs of human host)
       |
       v
5. MIGRATION
   (Bloodstream -> Heart -> Lungs)
       |
       v
6. ASCENT
   (Break into alveoli -> Bronchi -> Trachea -> Swallowed)
       |
       v
7. ADULT WORMS
   (Attach to small intestine wall, feed on blood, lay eggs)

Key Stages:

  • Egg: Passed in stool.
  • Larva: Develops in soil (warm, moist conditions).
  • Infection: Occurs via skin penetration (walking barefoot).
  • Migration: Through lungs (Loeffler's syndrome).
  • Habitation: Small intestine (Duodenum/Jejunum).

b) Control and Preventive Measures:

  1. Wearing Shoes: Preventing skin contact with contaminated soil, especially in endemic areas.
  2. Proper Sanitation: Use of latrines/toilets to prevent soil contamination with human feces.
  3. Mass Drug Administration (Deworming): Periodic treatment of school children and at-risk groups with Albendazole or Mebendazole.
  4. Health Education: Teaching personal hygiene and the importance of sanitation.
  5. Treatment of Cases: Identifying and treating infected individuals to reduce the reservoir of infection.
  6. Iron Supplementation: To treat anemia caused by the blood-sucking worms.
3.
Drug abuse has become a major health and social problem in Kenya and needs to be addressed urgently.
a) In a systematic way, explain the describe the problems associated with drug and substance abuse in Kenya (12marks)
b) Explain any 2 strategies you will employ in controlling drug and substance abuse among the youth in Kenya (8marks)
Show Answer

a) Problems associated with Drug and Substance Abuse:

  1. Health Problems:
    • Physical: Liver cirrhosis (alcohol), Lung cancer (tobacco), HIV/AIDS (injecting drugs/risky sex).
    • Mental: Psychosis, depression, anxiety, and addiction/dependence syndrome.
  2. Social Problems:
    • Family breakdown: Domestic violence, neglect of children, and divorce.
    • Crime: Theft and violence to get money for drugs; gang activity.
    • School dropout: Poor academic performance and truancy among youth.
  3. Economic Problems:
    • Loss of Productivity: Absenteeism from work and reduced workforce efficiency.
    • Financial strain: Money spent on drugs instead of basic needs; cost of healthcare and rehabilitation.
  4. Legal Problems: Arrests and imprisonment lead to criminal records affecting future employment.

b) Strategies to control drug abuse among youth:

  1. Health Education and Awareness (Prevention):
    • Conducting campaigns in schools and youth groups about the dangers of drugs.
    • Using peer educators and role models to influence behavior change.
    • Life skills training to help youth resist peer pressure and cope with stress.
  2. Rehabilitation and Counseling (Treatment):
    • Establishing youth-friendly counseling centers (like NACADA accredited centers).
    • Providing medical treatment for withdrawal and addiction.
    • Social reintegration programs to help recovering addicts return to school or find employment.
  3. (Alternative Strategy): Strict Law Enforcement and Policy: Limiting access by enforcing laws on age limits for alcohol, banning sale of drugs near schools, and cracking down on traffickers.
