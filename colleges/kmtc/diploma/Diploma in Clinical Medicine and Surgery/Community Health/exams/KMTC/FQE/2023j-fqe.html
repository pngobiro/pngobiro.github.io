Section 1: Multiple Choice Questions (40 Marks)

1.
You are invited to give a health talk on child weaning. Which of the following would form part of your information?
A) It is advisable to mix weaning foods with high energy concentration into the basic staple food
B) Your children should be fed more than four times a day
C) Weaning mixtures should be avoided
D) Breast feeding should be stopped during weaning to avoid food interactions
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A) It is advisable to mix weaning foods with high energy concentration into the basic staple food

Explanation: Basic staples (like maize porridge) are bulky but low in energy. Enriching them with oil, milk, or groundnuts (high energy/protein) is essential for the growing child. Frequency (Option B) should be age-appropriate (usually 3-5 times), but enrichment is a critical quality aspect. Breastfeeding should continue up to 2 years (D is false).

2.
A Clinical Officer who does blood pressure screening and related health education is conducting activities in the level of
A) Primary prevention
B) Secondary prevention
C) Tertiary prevention
D) Focused prevention
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B) Secondary prevention

Explanation:

  • Secondary Prevention: Early diagnosis and treatment (Screening).
  • Primary Prevention: Preventing disease onset (Immunization, Health Education on risk factors).
  • Although "health education" is mentioned, "Screening" (detecting existing but asymptomatic disease) is the defining activity for Secondary prevention.

3.
The habitual presence of a disease or infections agent with a given geographical area is
A) Epidemic
B) Pandemic
C) Sporadic
D) Endemic
Show Answer

Correct Answer: D) Endemic

Explanation: Endemic refers to the constant presence or usual prevalence of a disease within a geographic area.

4.
The health care activities that are representing primary prevention include,
A) Getting immunized and practicing safe sex
B) Quitting smoking
C) Avoiding alcohol and using drugs
D) Starting an exercise and height reduction program
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A) Getting immunized and practicing safe sex

Explanation: Primary prevention aims to prevent the disease from occurring. Immunization (Specific Protection) and Safe Sex (preventing STI transmission) are classic examples. Quitting smoking (B) is also primary (health promotion) or secondary if disease exists, but Immunization is the most definitive "activity representing primary prevention".

6.
For primary health care to improve health, people must first have;

8.
Choose the vitamin deficiency commonly found in Chronic alcoholics.

B) Vitamin B2
C) Vitamin B12
D) Vitamin B6

Correct Answer: A) Vitamin B1

Explanation: Chronic alcoholism leads to Thiamine (Vitamin B1) deficiency, which can cause Wernicke-Korsakoff syndrome (neurological disorder).

9.
Determine the vitamin with anticancer properties.
A) Vitamin A
B) Vitamin B12
C) Vitamin B17
D) Vitamin C
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A) Vitamin A (and D. Vitamin C)

Explanation: Vitamins A, C, and E are known as antioxidants and help prevent cell damage that can lead to cancer. Vitamin B17 (Amygdalin) was once promoted as an anti-cancer agent but is scientifically disproven and toxic. In exam contexts, Vitamin A (Retinoids) or Vitamin C are standard answers for "anticancer properties". Vitamin A helps in cell differentiation.

10.
Which of the following is a result of high exposure of radiation?
A) Loss of white blood cells
B) Hair loss
C) Tooth decay
D) Leg fractures
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A) Loss of white blood cells

Explanation: High doses of radiation damage rapidly dividing cells. The bone marrow is very sensitive, leading to a rapid drop in white blood cells (Leukopenia), causing immunosuppression. Hair loss (Alopecia) also occurs but leukopenia is a critical acute systemic effect.

  • Cholera/Typhoid: Water-borne.

12.
Select borne water borne disease.
A) Cholera trachoma, schistosomiasis and yellow fever
B) Cholera, typhoid, amoebic and bacillary dysentery
C) Filariasis schistosomiasis, scabies and trachoma
D) Scabies trachoma, schistosomiasis and yellow fever
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B) Cholera, typhoid, amoebic and bacillary dysentery

  • A includes Trachoma (Washed) and Yellow Fever (Vector).
  • C includes Filariasis (Vector) and Scabies (Washed).
13.
The most appropriate age for implementation of primordial measures for prevention of cervical cancer is
A) Below 9 year
B) 9-14 years
C) Over 30 years
D) 25-45 years
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B) 9-14 years

Explanation: This is the target age for the HPV vaccine, which prevents infection with the virus that causes cervical cancer. While "Primordial" usually refers to broad social determinants, in this specific context of cervical cancer prevention timeline, 9-14 years (before sexual debut) is the critical window for the primary intervention (vaccine) that prevents the risk factor (HPV) establishment. Strictly speaking: Primordial prevents the emergence of risk factors (e.g., sexual education to delay debut). 9-14 is the age for this education and vaccination.

14.
Determine the correct response that implies communicable diseases.
A) Host who can transmit the pathogens before showing symptoms are described as convalescent carriers
B) Host who can transmit the pathogen beyond the period of illness are described as incubating carriers
C) The line between the onset and end of communicability is described as the latent period
D) The period of communicability is the state where the host is infectious and shedding pathogens into the environment
Show Answer

Correct Answer: D) The period of communicability is the state where the host is infectious and shedding pathogens into the environment

Explanation:

  • Period of communicability: Time during which an infectious agent may be transferred from an infected person to another.
  • Option A describes Incubatory carriers (before symptoms), not convalescent.
  • Option B describes Convalescent/Chronic carriers (after illness), not incubating.

15.
Select the appropriate characteristic of non-communicable disease;
A) Contagious origin of limited latency
B) Long latency and aetiology
C) Short latency with functional impairment
D) Sudden onset and curability
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B) Long latency and aetiology

Explanation: NCDs (Chronic diseases) typically have a long latency period (time between exposure to risk factors and disease onset) and complex/multifactorial etiology. They are generally not contagious and have a slow onset.

16.
Categorize zoonotic diseases from responses below.
A) Covid-19, rabies, trachoma and scabies
B) Rabies, plague, salmonellosis and brucellosis
C) Scabies, pediculosis, chicken pox and herpes
D) Trachoma, rabies, plague and Leishmaniasis
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B) Rabies, plague, salmonellosis and brucellosis

Explanation: All listed are zoonoses (transmitted from animals to humans).

17.
Mechanisms of disease transmission in communicable disease include;
A) Biological droplet and airborne
B) Droplet airborne and water borne
C) Droplet, trans placental and physical contact
D) Vector borne, mechanical transfer and vehicle borne
Show Answer

Correct Answer: D) Vector borne, mechanical transfer and vehicle borne (or C)

Explanation: All options list valid mechanisms. Option D lists three distinct types (Vector, Mechanical [e.g. flies], Vehicle [water/food]). Option C lists Droplet, Vertical, and Direct contact. This is likely an "All of the above" type question or checking specific terminology. However, "Mechanical transfer" usually refers to vectors, so D is consistent.

18.
The major cardiovascular disease is
A) Cerebrovascular accident
B) Hypertension ischemia
C) Arthrosclerosis
D) Rheumatic heart disease
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B) Hypertension / Ischemia (Coronary Heart Disease)

Explanation: Ischaemic heart disease (Coronary artery disease) and Stroke (CVA) are the top killers. Hypertension is the leading risk factor/disease. "Arthrosclerosis" is a typo for Atherosclerosis (the process). Rheumatic is common in developing countries but Ischemic/Hypertension are the "Major" global ones.

19.
Determine advantage of the population strategy in disease prevention.
A) Behavioral appropriateness
B) Heath care provider motivation
C) Limited effect
D) Motivation of subjects
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A) Behavioral appropriateness

Explanation: The population strategy (Rose's Theorem) attempts to shift the whole distribution of exposure. It is often "Radical" and "Behaviorally appropriate" because it establishes new social norms (e.g., non-smoking becomes the norm), making it easier for individuals to adopt healthy behaviors compared to the High Risk strategy where the individual fights against the norm.

20.
Select factors that influence the severity of post-crash outcomes in road traffic accidents in Kenya.
A) Inappropriate pre-hospital care, delayed rescue and demographic factors such as age and sex
B) Inappropriate pre-hospital care, delayed rescue and inappropriate care at the emergency centers at the hospital
C) Inappropriate pre-hospital care, delayed rescue and presence of road side objects
D) Inappropriate pre-hospital care, fatigue and road designs
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B) Inappropriate pre-hospital care, delayed rescue and inappropriate care at the emergency centers at the hospital

Explanation: "Post-crash outcomes" depend on what happens after the crash: Response time, First Aid (Pre-hospital care), and Hospital care (Trauma management). Road design and fatigue (D) are crash causes, not post-crash factors.

28.
The following strategies can be used to reduce alcohol abuse
A) Identify and publicly condemn problem drinkers
B) Make water and soft drinks easily available
C) Increase the price of illicit liquor and reduce price of beer
D) Enforce drunk-driving cane
Show Answer

Correct Answer: D) Enforce drunk-driving cane (laws)

33.
Family planning in Kenya is very key as far as social-economic development is concerned... If all the couple in the nation decides to bear one child what is the probable outcome in the population.
A) Continue to grow for at least 60-70 years then stop
B) Stop growing immediately
C) Start declining
D) Continue to grow indefinitely
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A) Continue to grow for at least 60-70 years then stop (Population Momentum)

Determine factors that influence development of a disease (5 marks)

Epidemiological Triad Factors:

  1. Agent factors: Virulence, infectivity, pathogenicity, dose of the organism.
  2. Host factors: Age, sex, genetic constitution, immunity status, nutritional status, lifestyle/behavior.
  3. Environmental factors: Physical (climate), Biological (vectors), Social (poverty, overcrowding).

Section 3: Long Answer Question (20 Marks)

49.
You are the team leader of a sub-county disease surveillance team in sub county Y. It has been brought to the attention of the sub-county health management team of an outbreak of cases of diarrhoea and vomiting of sudden onset from one of the rural health facilities. The diarrhoea is rice water type. Vomiting is watery and non-projectile. Design an approach you would use to handle this situation. (20 marks)
Show Answer

Management of a Suspected Cholera Outbreak

1. Verification of Diagnosis and Confirmation of Outbreak (Preliminary Phase):

  • Visit the facility immediately.
  • Review clinical records and examine patients (Check for "Rice water stool", dehydration).
  • Specimen Collection: Collect stool samples (rectal swabs) in Cary-Blair transport medium.
  • Send to reference lab for culture and sensitivity (Vibrio cholerae confirmation).
  • Define a case: Establish a working case definition.
  • Compare current cases with baseline data (if > threshold, declare outbreak).

2. Notification and Coordination:

  • Notify higher authorities (County/National Disease Surveillance Unit).
  • Activate the Sub-County Outbreak Management Team/Task Force.
  • Coordinate with other sectors (Water, Administration, NGOs).

3. Case Management (Treatment):

  • Rehydration: Set up Oral Rehydration Therapy (ORT) corners for mild cases and IV fluids (Ringer's Lactate) for severe dehydration.
  • Antibiotics: Administer appropriate antibiotics (Doxycycline/Azithromycin) based on sensitivity.
  • Isolation: Set up a Cholera Treatment Centre (CTC) or isolation ward to prevent spread within facility.

4. Surveillance and Epidemiology:

  • Active Case Finding: Search for cases in the community (house-to-house).
  • Contact Tracing: Follow up on contacts and provide chemoprophylaxis if indicated.
  • Data Analysis: Map cases (Spot map) to identify the source (e.g., contaminated well, funeral feast). Track Time, Place, and Person.

5. Environmental Control (WASH):

  • Water: Chlorinate water sources, distribute water treatment tabs (Aqua tabs) to households.
  • Sanitation: Ensure proper disposal of feces (latrines) and disinfection of vomitus/stool (with chlorine).
  • Food Safety: Ban hawking of open food; inspect food handlers.

6. Social Mobilization and Health Education:

  • Educate community on:
    • Hand washing with soap.
    • Boiling/treating drinking water.
    • Cooking food thoroughly and eating it hot.
    • Seeking early treatment.

7. Monitoring and Evaluation:

  • Monitor daily trends (Epi-curve).
  • Evaluate the effectiveness of interventions.
  • Declare outbreak over when no new cases occur for 2 incubation periods.
  • Write a final report.
