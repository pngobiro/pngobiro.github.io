‚öõÔ∏è Radioactivity and Decay

Unstable nuclei, having a neutron excess or deficit, are radioactive and 'decay' to become stable nuclei with the emission of alpha, beta, and gamma radiation. Radioactive disintegration is a stochastic process.

üìâ

Physical Half-Life ($t_{1/2}$)

The half-life of a radionuclide is the time taken for its activity to decay to 1/2 of its original value. It is characteristic for the radionuclide and unaffected by external agencies like heat or pressure.

$$A = A_0 e^{-\lambda t}$$

Types of Decay

  • $\beta^-$ Decay: A neutron changes into a proton and an electron (ejected as a negative beta particle). $Z$ increases by 1.
  • Isomeric Transition (IT): Gamma ray is emitted from a metastable nucleus (e.g., $^{99\mathrm{m}}\mathrm{Tc} \rightarrow ^{99}\mathrm{Tc} + \gamma$).
  • $\beta^+$ (Positron) Decay: A proton changes into a neutron and a positive electron. $Z$ decreases by 1. PET imaging uses this.
  • K-electron Capture (KC): Nucleus captures an inner-shell electron, converting a proton to a neutron. $Z$ decreases by 1.
5.3

üì∑ The Gamma Camera

The gamma camera collects gamma rays from the patient to produce an image of radioactive distribution. It consists of a multihole collimator, a large NaI crystal, and an array of photomultiplier tubes (PMTs).

Multihole Collimator

A lead disk (25 mm thick) with thousands of holes. It rejects oblique rays, ensuring only photons traveling parallel to the holes reach the crystal. Higher sensitivity is achieved with wider, shorter holes at the cost of resolution.

NaI(Tl) Crystal

A single large crystal (~500 mm diameter). It absorbs gamma photons and emits light flashes (scintillations). For $^{99\mathrm{m}}\mathrm{Tc}$, it absorbs ~90% of photons.

üíä Radiopharmaceuticals and Generators

The most common radionuclide, $^{99\mathrm{m}}\mathrm{Tc}$, is preferred due to its 6-hour half-life and 140 keV energy, which is ideal for collimation and detection with minimal patient dose.

üè•

The Technetium Generator

Uses $^{99}\mathrm{Mo}$ (parent, half-life 67h) absorbed on an alumina column. The daughter $^{99\mathrm{m}}\mathrm{Tc}$ is "eluted" or washed off daily with saline.

Common Agents

  • Diphosphonates: Bone imaging.
  • HSA Macroaggregates: Lung perfusion.
  • MAG3 / DMSA: Renal studies.
  • Sestamibi: Cardiac perfusion.
