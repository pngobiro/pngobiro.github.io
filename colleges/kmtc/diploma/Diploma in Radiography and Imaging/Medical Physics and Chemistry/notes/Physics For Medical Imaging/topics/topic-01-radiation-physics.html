Overview

1.1

üî¨ Structure of the Atom

An atom consists mainly of empty space. Its mass is concentrated in a central nucleus that contains a number $A$ of nucleons (protons and neutrons), where $A$ is called the mass number. The nucleus comprises $Z$ protons, where $Z$ is the atomic number of the element, and $(A - Z)$ neutrons.

A nuclide is a species of nucleus characterized by the two numbers $Z$ and $A$. The atomic number is synonymous with the name of the element. Thus, $^{12}\mathrm{C}$ refers to a carbon atom with $A = 12$ and $Z = 6$.

Binding Energy

The binding energy ($E$) of an electron in an atom is the energy expended in completely removing the electron from the atom against the attractive force of the positive nucleus. This energy depends on the shell ($E_{\mathrm{K}} > E_{\mathrm{L}} > E_{\mathrm{M}} \dots$) and on the element, increasing as the atomic number increases.
