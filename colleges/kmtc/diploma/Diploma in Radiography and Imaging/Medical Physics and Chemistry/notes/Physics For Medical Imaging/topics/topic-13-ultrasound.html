9.1

üíé The Piezoelectric Effect

Ultrasound involves mechanical vibrations at frequencies above the range of human hearing (>20 kHz). In medical imaging, frequencies of 2‚Äì15 MHz are used. These waves are generated and detected using the piezoelectric effect.

üîÑ

Mechanism

Certain crystals (e.g., Lead Zirconate Titanate or PZT) change shape when an electric field is applied. Conversely, they produce an electrical signal when mechanically compressed. This allows the same transducer to act as both a transmitter and a receiver.

Resonance Frequency

The frequency ($f$) at which a crystal vibrates most efficiently is determined by its thickness ($d$):

$$f = \frac{c}{2d}$$

Where $c$ is the speed of sound in the crystal material. Higher frequency transducers use thinner crystals.

üîä Acoustic Impedance and Interfaces

As the ultrasound beam travels through tissue, it interacts with interfaces between different materials. The degree of reflection depends on the Acoustic Impedance ($Z$):

$$Z = \rho c$$

Where $\rho$ is the density of the tissue and $c$ is the velocity of sound in that tissue (average $\approx 1540\text{ m/s}$ in soft tissue).

‚ö†Ô∏è

Reflection Coefficient

The fraction of intensity reflected ($R$) at a boundary between two media is:

$$R = \left( \frac{Z_2 - Z_1}{Z_2 + Z_1} \right)^2$$

Large differences in $Z$ (e.g., tissue/air or tissue/bone) cause nearly 100% reflection, necessitating the use of coupling gel to eliminate air gaps.

üèéÔ∏è Doppler Methods

The Doppler effect is the change in frequency that occurs when a wave is reflected from a moving object (e.g., red blood cells). This is used to measure blood flow velocity.

$$\Delta f = \frac{2 f v \cos \theta}{c}$$
  • $\Delta f$ = Doppler frequency shift
  • $f$ = transmitted frequency
  • $v$ = velocity of the blood cells
  • $\theta$ = angle between the beam and the direction of flow
‚ú®

Angle Dependency

The shift is maximal when the beam is parallel to flow ($\theta = 0^\circ, \cos \theta = 1$) and zero when the beam is perpendicular to flow ($\theta = 90^\circ, \cos \theta = 0$).
