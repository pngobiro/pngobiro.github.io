4.1

‚ú® Intensifying Screens

Intensifying screens absorb the energy in the x-ray beam that has penetrated the patient and convert it into a light pattern. This light pattern is recorded as a latent image on the x-ray film. They markedly decrease the x-ray dose to the patient and allow the use of shorter exposure times to minimize motion blurring.

Construction

An intensifying screen consists of four layers:

  • Base: Polyester or cardboard (0.25 mm) for structural support.
  • Phosphor layer: 0.1-0.5 mm thick layer of fine phosphor crystals (3-10 Œºm) like Calcium Tungstate or Rare Earths (Gadolinium, Lanthanum).
  • Reflecting layer: Titanium dioxide ($TiO_2$) reflects light back toward the film.
  • Protective layer: Outermost layer for physical protection.

Intensification Factor (IF)

The IF measures the dose reduction achieved by screens:

$$IF = \frac{\text{air kerma to produce D=1 with film alone}}{\text{air kerma to produce D=1 with film plus screens}}$$

Typical values range from 30 to 100.

4.2

üéûÔ∏è Radiographic Film

Radiographic film typically consists of a polyester base coated on both sides with a photographic emulsion (suspension of silver iodobromide crystals in gelatine). Formation of the latent image involves the migration of electrons and silver ions within the crystal lattice.

The Gurney-Mott Theory

1. Light photons eject electrons from Bromine ions.
2. Electrons are trapped at the sensitivity speck.
3. Negatively charged specks attract mobile interstitial $Ag^+$ ions.
4. Neutral silver atoms form a submicroscopic speck (the latent image).

Processing Stages

  1. 1 Development: Chemical reduction of silver ions to black metallic silver. This process is initiated at the latent image specks.
  2. 2 Fixation: An acid solution of thiosulfate ("hypo") dissolves undeveloped silver halide, making the image stable and permanent.
  3. 3 Washing and Drying: Removes residual chemicals to prevent staining/degradation over time.

Photographic Density

Optical density ($D$) is measured as a logarithm of light transmission:

$$D = \log_{10} \frac{I_0}{I_t}$$

A density of 1.0 means 10% of light is transmitted; a density of 2.0 means 1% is transmitted.
