7.1

üéØCT Principles

In CT scanning, a transverse slice of the patient is imaged, avoiding the superposition of structures. The slice is subdivided into a matrix of volume elements (voxels), and the image is reconstructed as picture elements (pixels).

üí°

CT Numbers (Hounsfield Units)

The linear attenuation coefficient $\mu_t$ of each tissue is compared with water $\mu_w$:

$$\text{CT number} = 1000 \frac{(\mu_t - \mu_w)}{\mu_w}$$

Standard values: Air = -1000, Water = 0, Dense Bone = +1000.

7.4

üß¨Spiral and Helical Scanning

Spiral scanning allows continuous data acquisition as the table moves through the gantry. This is measured by the Pitch:

$$\text{Pitch} = \frac{\text{Distance moved by table per rotation}}{\text{Slice thickness}}$$

A pitch of 1.0 means contiguous coverage. Pitch > 1.0 reduces dose but may decrease axial resolution.

‚≠ê Key Points

  • CT provides superior contrast resolution compared to planar radiography.

  • Beam hardening occurs as low-energy photons are filtered out by the patient's body.

  • Partial volume effect occurs when a structure occupies only a portion of a voxel.
