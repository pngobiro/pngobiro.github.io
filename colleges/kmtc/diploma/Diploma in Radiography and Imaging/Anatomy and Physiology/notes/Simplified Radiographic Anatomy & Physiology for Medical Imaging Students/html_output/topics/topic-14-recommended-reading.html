Recommended Reading

üìö Bibliography & References

This simplified text book is intended to be read and referred to by medical imaging students, radiographers, and general practitioners working in small hospitals and with little or no access to other radiographic material. As the interested readers will wish to supplement their knowledge and seek out other sources of information, the following list of books are few suggestions of recommended texts as will support their knowledge:

01

üîñ Reference List

  1. Pocket Atlas of Radiographic Anatomy 3rd Edition by Torsten B. Moeller and Emil Reif - A classic pocket guide to basic radiographic anatomy, an ideal reference for anyone involved in the interpretation of commonly performed radiographic studies.

  2. X-Ray Anatomy 1st edition by George Simon and W. J. Hamilton - This book presents the radiograph of the various parts of the human body, including the head, neck, upper limb, lower limb, abdomen, thorax, and the vertebral column.

  3. Applied Radiological Anatomy $2^{\text {nd }}$ Edition by Paul Butler, Adam Mitchell and Jeremiah C. Healy - This book is an exhaustive yet practical imaging resource of every organ system using all diagnostic modalities.

  4. Bontrager's Textbook of Radiographic Positioning and Related Anatomy 9th Edition by John P. Lampignano and Leslie E. Kendrick - a classic text book includes all of the positioning and projection information that are need to be known in a clear, bulleted format.

  5. Atlas of Radiologic Anatomy 7th Edition by Lothar Wicke MD - This classic atlas incorporates the latest technological advances in radiologic anatomy, including x-ray, sonography, computed tomography, magnetic resonance, positron emission tomography, and single-photon emission computed tomography.

  6. Anatomy for Diagnostic Imaging 3rd Edition by Stephanie Ryan, Michelle Mc Nicholas and Stephen J Eustace - This book covers the normal anatomy of the human body as seen in the medical imaging. It does so by initial traditional and new imaging techniques.

  7. Radiographic Anatomy: A Working Atlas by Harry W. Fischer - A visual aid to first year medical students learning gross anatomy. Radiographic images as well as CT and MRI images document and enhance the course materials needed for later education and practice.

  8. Sectional Anatomy for Imaging Professionals 4th Edition by Lorrie L. Kelley and Connie Petersen - this book provides a comprehensive and highly visual approach to the sectional anatomy of the entire body.

  9. Imaging Atlas of Human Anatomy 5th Edition by Jonathan D. Spratt FRCS, FRCR, Lonie R Salkowski MD, Marios Loukas MD PhD, Tom Turmezei FRCR, Jamie Weir MB, BS, FRCP, and FRCR - this atlas provides a superb foundation for understanding applied human anatomy, offering a complete view of the structures and relationships within the body using the very latest imaging techniques.

  10. Gray's Anatomy for Students 4th Edition by Richard Drake PhD FAAA, A. Wayne Vogl PhD FAAA, and Adam W. M. Mitchell MB BS FRCS FRCR - this illustrated text book provides essential information in human anatomy for medical students.

  11. Snell's Clinical Anatomy by Regions $10^{\text {th }}$ Edition by Dr. Lawrence e. Wineski PhD this book delivers a proven learning and teaching resource on the practical application of anatomy.

  12. Guyton and Hall Textbook of Medical Physiology $13^{\text {th }}$ Edition by John E. Hall PhD this textbook summarize key concepts in physiology and pathophysiology, it focuses on the contents that are most relevant to clinical and pre-clinical students.
