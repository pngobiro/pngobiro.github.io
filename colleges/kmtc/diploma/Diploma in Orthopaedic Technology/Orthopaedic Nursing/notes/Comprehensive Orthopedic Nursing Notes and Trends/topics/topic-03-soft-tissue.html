ü©π SOFT TISSUES INJURY

  • This is injury to the skin, muscle, nerves and blood vessels

CAUSE OF SOFT TISSUE INJURY

  • Direct, blunt or lacerative trauma ‚Äì due to fall, road traffic accident, assault etc
  • Indirect trauma ‚Äì due to sudden violent muscle pull when we are not aware of it

Common injured soft tissues include;

  1. Muscle and tendon; injury to the muscle and tendon is classified into four categories on the basis of damage to the muscle tissue as follow;
    • Grade 1 strain; superficial contusion; injury resulting from blunt injury
    • Grade 2 strain; partial rupture of muscle or tendon; tearing of a greater number of muscle fibers
    • Grade 3 strain; complex rupture of muscle and tendon; usually from lacerative trauma or violent stretching
  2. Ligament; injury to the ligament is also known as sprain. According to the degree of damage, ligament injury is classified into 3 grades;
    • Grade 1; minor sprain, usually caused by a sudden jerk or minor trauma.
    • Grade 2; moderate sprain, the ligament is either torn or detached from the bone
    • Grade 3; complete rupture, is more severe, the ligament is completely broken into two part
  3. Nerves; occasionally there may be injury or entrapment of the peripheral nerve as a result of soft tissue injury
  4. Blood vessels; usually remain susceptible to injury during fractures or dislocation.
  5. Synovium
  6. Bursa; a thin membranous pouch or sac lined with the synovial membrane, it protects the tissue especially the joint from trauma pressure, repetitive friction etc.
  7. Joint capsule
  8. Skin
  9. Subcutaneous tissue.

ü©π WOUNDS MANAGEMENT

CLOSED WOUND;

is injury to the soft tissue beneath unbroken skin

Signs and symptoms of closed wound;

  • Swelling
  • Tenderness
  • Discoloration
  • Possible deformity

Management of closed wound;

  • Use the universal precautions and secure the scene
  • Apply RICE (REST, ICE COMPRESSOR) method, apply ice for 20 minutes in one hour
  • Monitor the patient an check the vital signs
  • Treat for shock
  • Transport the patient to the emergency room as soon as possible

OPEN WOUND;

a soft tissue injury resulting in break of the continuity of the skin.

Types of open wounds;

  • Scratches and abrasions
  • Lacerations ‚Äì regular and irregular
  • Penetrations and puncture wounds
  • Avulsions
  • Amputations
  • Crushing injury
  • Gunshot wounds
  • Impaled object

Management of open wounds:

  • Use universal precautions and secure the scene
  • Remove or cut the cloth by the help of trauma scissor from open wound area
  • Control bleeding by the help of direct pressure and use pressure points also if needed
  • Prevent from contamination
  • Apply dressing and bandage; dressing is any material used to cover a wound that helps control bleeding and also aids in the prevention of additional contamination. A BANDAGE is any material used to hold a dressing in place
  • Cover the patient
  • Treat for shock
  • Transport the patient to the emergency room as soon as possible
ü©π How to apply dressings and bandages:
  • Control bleeding
  • Apply the dressing using decontamination technique
  • Cover the wound completely
  • Ensure that the dressing and the bandages are firm, flexed and comfortable but not so tight as to affect circulation
  • Ensure there are no loose ends that can get caught
  • Avoid covering the fingertips.

‚öΩ SPORTS INJURIES;

The most common include;

  1. Sprain; this is a stretched or torn ligament. Ligaments that connect bones at a joint, falling, twisting or getting hit can all cause a sprain.

    Ankle and wrist sprains are common

    Symptoms include, pain, swelling, bruising and being unable to move your joint. You might feel a pop or tear when the injury happens

  2. Strain; is a stretched or torn muscle or tendon. Tendons are tissues that connect muscle to bone. Twisting or pulling these tissues can cause a strain.

    Strains can happen suddenly or develop over time

    Back and hamstring muscles are common

    Symptoms of strains include; pain, muscle spasms, swelling and trouble moving the muscle

  3. Tennis elbow; when muscles in the forearm that attach to the bone on the outside of the elbow, become used overtime, small tears develop in the tendon. This leads to irritation and pain where the tendon is attached to the bone.

    Symptoms include; elbow pain that gradually worsens, pain radiating from the outside of the elbows to the forearm, weak grasp, pain when lifting objects and also pain radiating down the forearm

  4. Impingement and rotator cuff tears
  5. Knee injuries (ACL injury, meniscal injury, overuse syndrome)

ü©∏ VASCULAR INJURIES;

This are injuries to the arteries and veins.

The anatomical regions affected are, the extremities ‚Äì limbs, abdomen and pelvis, thorax, head and neck

Causes;

  • Road traffic accidents
  • Trap gun
  • Home accidents
  • Cuts and stabs
  • Mechanism of the injuries
  • Sharp /penetrating
  • Blunt

Signs and symptoms of vascular injury

  • Active bleeding
  • Signs of distal ischemia
  • Expanding hematoma
  • Hematoma
  • Injury close to a known neurovascular bundle
  • Reduced pulse
  • Paresthesia in the limbs
  • Anesthesia and paralysis in the limbs

Investigations

  • CT angiography

Management;

  • Resuscitate
  • Assess viability and extent of injury
  • Repair as soon as possible
  • Post intervention monitoring
  • Rehabilitation
  • Surgical repair; general anesthesia, clean the entire affected limb. Thrombectomy can be done. Vein grafting etc.
