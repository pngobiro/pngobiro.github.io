ü©∫ CARE OF PATIENTS WITH ORTHOPEDIC DEVICES;

  1. POSITIONING; after the initial assessment of the patient is made, attention is turned to positioning, too ensure proper body alignment.

    The goal is optimal comfort and safety, the upper extremities should be held close to the body, elevation should be achieved without undue pressure on the elbow or shoulder. The lower extremities are in neutral position with support provided for the entire length and heels are off the bed

    The soft knee should be checked for proper placement and closure

  2. A cast is a rigid immobilization device moulded to the contours of the part which it is applied. It has a dual purpose of immobilization in a specific position and provision of uniform pressure on the encased soft tissue.

    It should be inspected for visibility of fingers and toes for neurovascular assessment.

    If the cast is bi valved, the edges should be inspected for roughness to avoid discomfort and portentila skin breakdown

    A wet cast should be handled carefully with the palms of the hand.

    A cast should be supported on a pillow

  3. Wound care, all surgical wound dressing should be checked for drainage and closure. Drains should be placed in the wound to minimize blood accumulation and the possibility of infection.

‚öôÔ∏è TRACTION;

is the application of a pulling force to a part of the body for medical purposes, to treat muscle or skeletal disorder

Principles of traction;

  • Provide counter traction using the patient‚Äôs body or pull weights in the opposite direction
  • Maintain continuous traction in a correct line pull
  • Prevent friction
  • Provide daily traction care

Purpose of traction;

  • Controls pain
  • Reduces fracture
  • Maintain reduction
  • Prevents and correct deformity

Types of traction;

Based on principle

  • Fixed traction; traction is applied to the leg against a fixed point of counter pressure
  • Sliding traction; when the weight of all or part of the body, acting under the influence of gravity is utilized to provide counter traction

Based on application;

  • Skin traction; traction force is applied over a large are of a skin, e.g. applied over a limb distal to fracture site
  • Skeletal traction; it may be used as a means of reducing or maintaining the reduction of a fracture. It should be reserved for those cases in which skin traction is contraindicated

üè• REHABILITATION;

Rehabilitation is the process of helping a person to reach the fullest physical, psychological, social, vocational and educational potential consistent with his physiologic or anatomic impairment, environmental limitations and desires of life plans

Components rehabilitation;

  • Medical rehabilitation
  • Social rehabilitation
  • Vocational rehabilitation

Considerations of rehabilitation;

  • Goal setting
  • Understanding of functions of the team members
  • Autonomy of each team member
  • Understanding the needs of the patient

Scope of rehabilitation;

  • Physiotherapy
  • Vocational guidance
  • Speech therapy
  • Health education
  • Psychotherapy

Rehabilitation team comprises;

  • Nurses
  • Physiotherapist
  • Psychologist
  • Dietician
  • Speech therapist
  • Vocational rehabilitation counsellor
  • Social worker
  • Recreational therapist
  • Occupational therapist
  • Psychiatrist ‚Äì leader of the team.

üìã PRINCIPLES & GOALS

Why rehabilitate orthopedic patients;

  • To resolve the clinical symptoms; use modalities, use appropriate medications, protect, relative rest, icing compression and elevation. Surgical indications
  • Return to normal activity; retard muscle decline in strength and mass. To stop complications brought about by immobility. Maintain or improve flexibility
  • For general fitness; improve cardiovascular fitness, overall strength is improved and improve tolerance and endurance
  • Decreases the chance for further injury; to prevent re injury, prevent permanent immobility due to apprehension.

Principles of rehabilitation of orthopedic patients;

  • Rehabilitation should begin during the initial contact with the patient
  • Restoring the patient to independent or regain his pre ‚Äì illness / pre disability level of function in a short time as possible
  • Maximizing independence within the limits of disability
  • He must be an active participant
  • The activities of daily living are facilitated
  • The individual with disability is encouraged to wear his clothing enhances self-esteem and dignity
  • Motivates the patient and helps him to attain social independence
  • Focus is on the needs of groups of people with specific conditions
  • Every patient has a right to rehabilitation services
05

üìà STAGES OF REHABILITATION

  1. Acute stage; there is focus on treatment of the clinical symptoms and tissue injury. The tools of rehabilitation in the acute stage include, rest and immobilization of the patient, use of medications, initial passive or light exercises. Need for pain control, adequate tissue healing and tolerance to the patient
  2. Recovery stage; focuses on complete tissue healing, decreased pain and good flexibility
  3. Functional stage; focuses on subclinical adaptation, exercises, skills instructions and specific function program. There is no pain, there is normal strength and balance and good general fitness.

Role of a community health nurse in rehabilitation

  1. Assessment of patients physical, mental, socioeconomic and vocational status
  2. Diagnosis of the therapeutic, physiotherapeutic, vocational, educational, training and supportive needs of the client
  3. Deciding the minimum acceptable improvement in the patient‚Äôs health capacity for independence and productivity
  4. Formulation of a plan of action
  5. Implementing the action plan
  6. Evaluation of the result of interventions
  7. Modification of plan of action if necessary

Qualities of community health nurse in rehabilitation;

  • Excellent listening skills
  • High order counselling abilities
  • Effective problem solving skills
  • The ability to adopt innovative methods to help the patient realize themselves.
