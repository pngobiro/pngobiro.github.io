BENIGN BONE TUMORS

Classification; revised WHO classification 1994

Clinical features of osteoid osteoma;

‚ñ∂ Osteblastoma

This is a giant osteoid osteoma. Larger >1.5 cm. It forms 3% of all benign tumors. Common in young adults (10 ‚Äì 25 years). The common sites are the spine, metaphysis /diaphysis of long bones, small bones of hands /feet.

Clinical features of osteoblastoma;

  • Pain. No night pain. Not relieved by NSAIDS.

Diagnosis;

  • X-ray, show a well demarcated osteolytic lesion
  • CT scan is investigation of choice
  • Radioisotope scan: increased uptake
  • MRI: shows soft tissue and intraspinal extension

Treatment;

  • Excision and bone grafting
  • Local recurrence common
  • Malignant transformation reported

üìä STAGING & MALIGNANT TUMORS

Enneking staging system for benign tumors;

Stage Description
1. Latent has well demarcated borders. Grows slowly and stops. Heals spontaneously (osteoid osteoma). Negligible recurrence after intracapsular resection.
2. Active has well defined borders but may show cortical thinning. Growth is limited by natural barriers (compartment). Negligible recurrence after marginal resection.
3. Aggressive indistinct borders. Growth is limited by natural barriers. 5% may have metastases. High recurrence after intracapsular or marginal resection. Wide resection preferred.

MALIGNANT BONE TUMORS;

Classification of malignant bone tumors;

1. Primary bone tumor;

Tumors from bone tissue; osteosarcoma, chondrosarcoma and fibrosarcoma

Tumors from bone afflicted tissues; ewing‚Äôs sarcoma, reticulum cell sarcoma of bone and myeloma

2. Metastatic tumor; carcinoma, sarcoma, neuroblastoma

Characteristic of malignant tumors;

  • Most common presents as painful bony mass
  • Extends to soft tissue through broken cortex
  • Rarely multifocal
  • Radio graphically has poorly defined borders,
  • Wide zones of transition
  • Moth eaten or permeative type of bone destruction
  • Interrupted type of periosteal reaction.

Diagnosis;

  • History and local examination
  • Laboratory test e.g. hemogram, ESR, CRP, Serum calcium, serum phosphate and bone marrow examination
  • Radiological tests e.g. X ray, CT scan, MRI, bone scan, chest X-ray, CT abdomen, thyroid scan, bone scan
  • Histopathological examination

üè• MANAGEMENT OF MALIGNANT TUMORS

Goal of management;

  • In case of primary malignant bone tumor, to make the patient free
  • In secondary malignant bone tumor, to minimize pain to presence function and to diagnose the site of primary tumor

Pain management is the prime principle in the management

Physical therapy ‚Äì use of splints

Behavioral approaches such as relaxation techniques

Optimal treatment often requires combination of radiotherapy, chemotherapy and surgery

Biopsy is the most conclusive test because it confirms if the tumor is malignant or benign, the bone cancer type (primary or secondary bone cancer)

Principles of management of bone tumors

Benign, asymptomatic, if no doubt treatment is not needed. If in doubt, biopsy is recommended

Benign symptomatic and enlarging tumors, biopsy is recommended. Remove by marginal excision

Suspected malignant tumors, further investigation should be carried out, imaging and staging. Treatment options should be discussed with the patient including pros and cons

Tumor excision;

  • Intracapsular excision only for benign lesions with low risk of recurrence.
  • Marginal excision for benign symptomatic lesions
  • Wide excision for low grade intercompartmental lesions.
  • Radial excision for high grade lesions

Limb salvage in high grade sarcoma, and local control is good.

Amputation if in doubt whether the lesion is intra or extra compartmental, limb amputation and early rehabilitation is wise.

Chemotherapy; for chemosenstive lesions, to reduce the size and prevent metastases seeding in. improve chances of survival. 8 ‚Äì 12 weeks pre operatively, and maintainance for 6- 12 months post operatively

Radiotherapy; in radiosensitive lesions. Used with caution, may cause complications such as spindle-cell sarcoma and pathological fractures.
