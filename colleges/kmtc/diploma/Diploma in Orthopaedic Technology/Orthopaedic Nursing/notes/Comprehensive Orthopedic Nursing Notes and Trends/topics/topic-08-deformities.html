ü¶¥ SCOLIOSIS;

This is a condition involving an abnormal sideways curvature of the spine. It can be due to congenital, developmental or degenerative problems

Scoliosis usually develops in the thoracic spine or the thoracolumbar area of the spine. The curvature of the back may develop as a single curve (shaped like letter C) or as two curves (shaped like letter S).

Scoliosis appears in adolescence and indicators include unevenness in the shoulder height, shoulder blades, rib cages and hips.

Types of scoliosis;

  • Non Structural scoliosis; the spine works normally but looks curved e.g. one leg being longer than the other
  • Structural scoliosis; the curve of the spine is rigid and can‚Äôt be reversed

Causes;

  • Unknown / idiopathic scoliosis
  • Genetic conditions
  • Cerebral palsy
  • Muscular dystrophy
  • Birth defects
  • Infections
  • Tumors
  • Developmental causes

Symptoms of scoliosis;

  • Uneven shoulders; one shoulder blade appears more prominent than the other
  • Uneven waist, one hip higher than the other
‚ñ∂ Symptoms in adolescents:
  • The head is slightly off center
  • The ribcage is not symmetrical ‚Äì the ribs may be at different heights
  • One hip is more prominent than the other
  • Clothes do not hang properly
  • One shoulder / shoulder blade is slightly higher than the other
  • The individual may learn to one side
  • Uneven leg lengths
‚ñ∂ Symptoms in infants:
  • A bulge on one side of the chest
  • The baby may consistently lie curved to one side
  • In severe cases, the heart and lungs may not work properly and the patient may experience shortness of breath and chest pain

Patterns of scoliosis; there are 4 common patterns of curvature in scoliosis

  1. Right thoracic scoliosis; the major scoliosis is concentrated in the thoracic (upper or mid back) region and curves to the right. There may also be a less severe counter curve to the left in the lumbar ( lower back)
  2. Left lumbar scoliosis; the major curve is to the left in the lumbar. There may be a less extreme curve to the right in the thoracic
  3. Right thoracolumbar scoliosis; the major curve is to the right in both the lower thoracic and the lumbar. This is commonly known as a C curve. (it looks like a C from the front, a reverse C from the back)
  4. Right thoracic ‚Äì left lumbar scoliosis; the major curve is in the thoracic region, with an equal counter curve to the left in the lumbar region. This is commonly known as an S when viewed from the front)

For unknown reasons most curves in the thoracic bend to the right and most curves in the lumbar arch bend to the left

Complications of scoliosis;

  • Lung and heart damage, since the rib cage may press against the heart and lungs.
  • Back problems; chronic back pain
  • Changes in appearance

Diagnosis and testing

  • CT scan to measure scoliosis three dimensionally
  • Riser- ferguson method; one draws dots in the center of the superior apical and inferior vertebrae and lines to connect them,
  • MRI basically used to measure scoliosis when there is an underlying pathology e.g. spinal cord tumor.

Management;

The following factors will be considered by the doctors when deciding on treatment options; sex (females are more likely than males to have scoliosis that gradually gets worse), the severity of the curve (the larger the curve the greater the risk of worsening), curve position (curves at the centre of the spine are likely to get worse than those at the lower or upper section) and bone maturity.

Casting instead of bracing is used in infants with scoliosis to help the infant‚Äôs spine to go back to its normal position as it grows, this can be made of plaster of paris.

Braces for patients who have moderate scoliosis and the bones are still growing, this to prevent further curvature. When bones stop growing braces are no longer used.

Scoliosis surgery (spinal fusion), this surgery reduces the spine and stops it from getting worse. Other forms of surgeries include; bone grafts

The operations last 4 ‚Äì 8 hours, and so the first 48 hrs, post surgery should be managed in the ICU. Ando discharge the patient should be followed up every 6 months.

Prevention;

Most cases of scoliosis cannot be prevented except in osteoposis related scoliosis

Early diagnosis and management in infants can prevent further damage in adulthood.

üö∂ KYPHOSIS;

Is a curving of the spine that causes a bowing or rounding of the back, which leads to a hunchnack or stouching posture.

Types of kyphosis; there are 3 main types of kyphosis;

‚ñ∂ 1. Postural kyphosis;

Is the most common type of kyphosis. Common in girls than in boys and is typically noticed in adolescent age. Its caused by a poor posture and weakening of the muscle, ligaments in the back (paraspinous muscle). The vertebrae are typically placed in postural kyphosis and it gets worse overtime. These people will have symptoms of pain and muscle fatigue

‚ñ∂ 2. Scheuemann‚Äôs kyphosis;

It is also first noticed during adolescence period. It results from structural deformity. It commonly develops with scoliosis. Reasons are not well understood.

‚ñ∂ 3. Congenital kyphosis;

It the least common type of abnormal kyphosis. It is caused by abnormal development of the vertebrae prior to birth. Occurs due to fusion of many vertebrae together

Etiology:

  • It occurs at any age. Very rare at birth.
  • Postural kyphosis occurs as a result of poor posture and weakening of muscles and ligaments.
  • Congenital kyphosis occurs as a result of improper development of vertebrae or fusion of vertebrae

Other causes include;

  • Certain endocrine disorders
  • Connective tissue disorders
  • Infection
  • Muscular dystrophy
  • Poliomyelitis
  • Spina bifida
  • Tumors

Clinical features:

  • Difficulty in breathing (in severe cases)
  • Mild back pain
  • Round back appearance
  • Tenderness and stiffness

Diagnostic evaluation:

X ‚Äì Ray of the spine

MRI

Management:

Treatment depends upon the cause of disorder

Congenital kyphosis requires corrective surgery at early age

Scheuemann‚Äôs disorder is treated with braces and physical therapy

Kyphosis caused by tumors needs surgical correction

Prognosis;

Scheuemann‚Äôs disorder in adolescents stops once their growth stops.

If the disorder occurs due to degenerative types, then they may need a surgical correction.

Complications:

  • Decreased lung capacity
  • Disabling pain
  • Neurological symptoms including leg weakening or paralysis
  • Round back deformity

üö∂ LORDOSIS;

A normal spine when viewed from behind appears straight.

Lordosis is a medical term used to describe an inward curvature of a portion of the lumbar and cervical vertebral column

Etiology:

Cause of lordosis is linked to achondroplasia and spondylolistesis.

Lordosis is also associated with poor posture, congenital problem with vertebrae, neuromuscular problems, back surgery or a hip problem.

In children rickets may also lead to lordosis

Clinical features:

  • Prominence of buttocks
  • Back pain
  • Pain down the legs

Diagnostic evaluation:

  • X-Rays done to measure and evaluate the curve
  • Bone scans to evaluate degenerative or arthritic changes, also helps to find bone tumors
  • CT scan
  • Blood investigations
  • Early detection of lordosis is important for proper and successful treatment

Management;

Based on the child‚Äôs age, overall health, medical history, cause and extent of the condition

The goal of treatment is to stop the progression of the curve, prevent deformity

Simple exercises are sufficient, if lordosis is associated with poor posture.

Prognosis:

  • Depends on the child‚Äôs age, amount of curvature
  • Requires frequent examination by the physician to monitor curve as the child grows and develops
  • Early detection helps in proper management.
