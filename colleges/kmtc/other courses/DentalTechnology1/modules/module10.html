10. Sprayvit, Aspirator and Auxiliary Equipment

This module covers essential auxiliary dental equipment including Sprayvit, aspirator, suction nozzle, tumbler filling, cuspidor bowl, and dental light, detailing their functions and operational needs.

3.3 Sprayvit

The Sprayvit is used to clean and dry out prepared cavities before filling. It can spray air, water, or a mixture of both.

Operation and Supply Requirements:

  • Spray Options: Can deliver air, water, or a mixture.
  • Pre-heated Air and Water: Both air and water need to be pre-heated for patient comfort.
  • Electrical Supply: Required to pre-heat air and water.

3.4 Aspirator and Suction Nozzle

The aspirator and suction nozzle are crucial for maintaining a clear and dry field of operation in the patient's mouth during dental procedures.

Functions:

  • Aspirator: Removes saliva and small particles. Ensures patient does not need to swallow saliva frequently.
  • Suction Nozzle: Removes larger particles and significant amounts of fluid that accumulate during drilling.

Supply Requirements:

  • Underpressure: Both tools require underpressure (vacuum) to operate suction.
  • Drainage Connection: Needed for disposal of removed fluids and materials.

3.5 Tumbler Filling

Tumbler filling provides pre-heated water into a glass for patients to rinse their mouths after dental work.

Supply Requirements:

  • Water Supply: Source of potable water.
  • Electricity (if heater installed): If a water heater is installed to pre-heat the water.

3.6 Cuspidor Bowl

The cuspidor bowl, typically made of porcelain, is used to drain water used for rinsing the patient's mouth.

Supply Requirements:

  • Water Connection: To flush and clean the basin.
  • Drain: For disposal of rinse water.

3.7 Dental Light

The dental light is essential for providing sufficient illumination of the patient's mouth, enabling the dentist to perform detailed work. It is suspended from a support arm and often uses a halogen lamp.

Key Features and Supply Requirements:

  • Halogen Lamp: Typically uses a 12V halogen lamp.
  • Adjustable Beam: Some models allow adjustment of light beam sharpness.
  • Braking Devices: Support arm includes braking devices to maintain position.
  • Electricity: Primary supply requirement for the light.

üìö Key Takeaways

  • Sprayvit provides air, water, or a mixture for cleaning and drying tooth cavities, requiring pre-heated supplies and electrical power.
  • Aspirators remove saliva and small particles, while suction nozzles handle larger debris and fluids, both needing underpressure and drainage.
  • Tumbler filling dispenses pre-heated water for rinsing, requiring water supply and electricity if heating is used.
  • Cuspidor bowls drain rinse water and need water connections for flushing and a drainage system.
  • Dental lights use halogen lamps to provide bright, adjustable illumination and are essential for visibility during dental procedures, requiring electrical supply.
