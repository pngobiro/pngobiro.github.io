5. Dental Equipment Safety and Maintenance

This module covers essential safety precautions and routine maintenance procedures for dental equipment to ensure optimal performance and longevity.

Oil Separator Cleaning

Oil separator cleaning:

Release oil by opening the release screw during the time the handpiece is operating, till all the old oil is released.

Caution: Never reuse old oil.

Compressor Air Lines

Compressor air lines:

Clean air filter and test for leaks.

Very 3 Month Check (by technician)

VERY 3 MONTH: (done by technician)

Dental unit function test:

  • Check the function of all valves and elements.
  • Check for leaks.

Dental chair maintenance:

  • Check hydraulic oil level and top up if necessary.
  • Test for leaks.
  • Lubricate links, chain, spindle.
  • Check all functions.

SAFETY ASPECTS

SAFETY ASPECTS

  1. Isolate
  2. Ensure that the current is not switched on while work is in progress.
  3. Ensure that there is no voltage.

Currents upwards of 50 mA (0,05 A) can be fatal if they pass through the heart.

Therefore a maximum voltage of 24 V has to be used at those elements (e.g. sprayvit) getting in direct contact with the dentist or the patient.

After consultation the dental unit is to be switched off.

üìö Key Takeaways

  • Regular oil separator cleaning is crucial, and old oil should never be reused.
  • Compressor air lines require clean filters and leak testing for efficient operation.
  • A 3-month function test by a technician includes checking valves, elements, leaks, and comprehensive dental chair maintenance.
  • Safety precautions are paramount, including isolation, ensuring no live current, and verifying no voltage during maintenance.
  • Electrical safety is critical; voltages should be limited to 24V for patient-contact elements, and insulation resistance tests are necessary.
