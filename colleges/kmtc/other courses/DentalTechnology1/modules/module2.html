2. Dental Anatomy

This module delves into the field of dentistry and the anatomy of teeth. Understanding tooth structure and types is crucial for dental technology.

Dentistry Defined

Dentistry is defined as the branch of Medical Science that deals with prevention, diagnosis and treatment of diseases and malformations of, and injuries to, the teeth, mouth and jaws and of replacing lost teeth and associated structures. Tooth Structure

Parts of a Tooth:

  • Enamel: The outer, hardest layer of the tooth.
  • Dentine: Layer beneath the enamel and cementum, forming the bulk of the tooth.
  • Pulp Cavity: The inner part of the tooth containing pulp, nerves and blood vessels.
  • Gum: Soft tissue surrounding the necks of teeth.
  • Neck: The constricted part between the crown and the root.
  • Cement: A bone-like substance covering the root of a tooth.
  • Blood vessels and nerves: Supply nutrients and sensation to the tooth.
  • Crown: The enamel-covered part of the tooth above the gum line.
  • Root: The portion of the tooth that anchors it into the jaw bone.

Types of Teeth

There are different types of teeth, each with a specific shape and function:

  • Incisors: Located at the front, used for cutting food. Have one root.
  • Canines: Pointed teeth next to incisors, used for tearing food. Have one root.
  • Premolars: Located between canines and molars, used for grinding and crushing food. Have two roots.
  • Molars: Located at the back of the mouth, used for chewing and grinding food. Have three roots.

üìö Key Takeaways

  • Dentistry is the branch of medical science focused on teeth, mouth, and jaw health, including prevention, diagnosis, and treatment.
  • A tooth consists of enamel, dentine, pulp cavity, gum, neck, cement, blood vessels and nerves, crown, and root.
  • There are four types of teeth: incisors, canines, premolars, and molars, each designed for specific functions in food processing.
  • Dental formulas provide a standardized way to represent the number and types of teeth present in each quadrant of the mouth.
