7. Dental Unit Layout and Tooth Numbering

This module introduces the typical layout of a dental department and explains the standard tooth numbering systems used in dentistry for clear communication and record-keeping.

Typical Layout of a Dental Department

A typical dental department includes several key areas:

  • Treatment rooms
  • Sterilization area
  • Private office
  • Staff room
  • Hygiene area
  • Reception and waiting room
  • Laboratory
  • X-ray and dark room
  • Accounting office

Tooth Numbering

For the convenience of reference and to eliminate confusion between the deciduous and permanent, upper and lower, teeth from the right side or the left side of the jaw etc, the teeth are conveniently numbered as shown below.

Permanent Dentition

Quadrant I (Upper Right): Teeth numbered 18-11

Quadrant II (Upper Left): Teeth numbered 21-28

Quadrant III (Lower Left): Teeth numbered 31-38

Quadrant IV (Lower Right): Teeth numbered 48-41

Deciduous Dentition

Quadrant V (Upper Right): Teeth numbered 55-51

Quadrant VI (Upper Left): Teeth numbered 61-65

Quadrant VII (Lower Left): Teeth numbered 71-75

Quadrant VIII (Lower Right): Teeth numbered 85-81

Example: Tooth number 34 refers to a permanent tooth, in the lower jaw, on the left side, and it is the first premolar. Number 61 denotes a deciduous tooth, in the upper jaw, on the left side, and it is the central incisor.

üìö Key Takeaways

  • A standard dental department layout typically includes treatment rooms, sterilization, office spaces, laboratory, and support areas.
  • Dental units within treatment rooms are equipped for dentist and assistant use, incorporating chair, light, and instrument delivery systems.
  • Tooth numbering systems provide a universal method for identifying each tooth, crucial for dental records and communication.
  • Permanent teeth are numbered 11-48, and deciduous teeth are numbered 51-85, categorized by quadrant and tooth type.
  • Understanding tooth numbering is essential for accurately documenting and discussing dental treatments and conditions.
