15. Dental Chairs - Hydraulic and Electro-hydraulic

This module details the hydraulic and electro-hydraulic systems used in dental chairs, explaining their mechanisms, components, and operational principles.

9. DENTAL CHAIRS

Dental chairs are used in a dental unit to position a patient in the most convenient position for the dentist. This makes it easy for the dentist to access any teeth that require treatment.

To perform this function, the dental chair requires at least two basic movements:

  1. the up and down movement (base)
  2. the backward and forward tilt movement (backrest)

These movements commonly use one of three types of energy transmission systems namely,

9.1 Hydraulic

9.1 Hydraulic

This uses oil as the energy transmission medium. A small force on the pump (small cross-sectional area) imparts energy to the oil, which then acts on a piston in the cylinder (larger cross-sectional area). This greatly multiplies the force thus making it possible for even a lightly built dentist to easily raise and position a burly patient.

Hydraulic chairs normally use the foot pump which draws oil from the reservoir tank in the base of the chair. This oil flows through a filter and a check valve, into the pump, A second check valve traps the oil in the cylinder to ensure that the chair remains in its raised position. Oil discharge from the pump causes the check valve to the tank to close while flowing through to the cylinder, thus raising the chair with every discharge troke of the pump. A mechanical valve releases the oil back into the tank lowering the chair.

Backrest movement is achieved by manipulating purely mechanical linkages.

This type of chair does not require an electrical supply and is therefore suitable for rural health facilities which do not have electricity.

9.2 Electro-hydraulic

9.2 Electro-hydraulic

Works on the same principle as hydraulic except using an electrically operated pump. The main components are the electrical pump, a single-acting cylinder for the base, a double-acting cylinder for the backrest, solenoid valves and switchgear. An electrical circuit operates the pump motor and relevant valves for various movements.

üìö Key Takeaways

  • Dental chairs are designed for patient positioning, requiring up/down base movement and backward/forward backrest tilt.
  • Hydraulic dental chairs use oil and a manual foot pump system to generate force for chair movement, ideal for areas without electricity.
  • Electro-hydraulic chairs utilize an electric pump to power hydraulic movements, offering smoother and easier operation compared to purely hydraulic systems.
  • Electro-hydraulic systems include electrical pumps, single and double-acting cylinders, solenoid valves, and electrical circuits for controlled chair movements.
  • Both hydraulic and electro-hydraulic chairs achieve similar positioning functionalities but differ in their power source and control mechanisms.
