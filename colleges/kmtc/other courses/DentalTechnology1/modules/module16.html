16. Dental Chairs - Electro-mechanical and Maintenance

This module focuses on electro-mechanical dental chairs, detailing their drive assemblies, electric modules, and maintenance procedures, and provides a comprehensive maintenance checklist for all types of dental chairs.

9.3 Electro-mechanical

9.3 Electro-mechanical

The main components of this chair besides the framework and cushions are:

9.3.1 Chair base drive assembly

9.3.1 Chair base drive assembly

This is for the purpose of raising and lowering the chair. It includes a motor, gearbox for speed reduction, chain or belt drive for motion transmission and a spindle with a nut which converts angular motion of the motor into linear motion to raise and lower the chair.

9. 3. 2 Back rest drive assembly

9. 3. 2 Back rest drive assembly

With components similar to those in 9.3.1. above.

Works in conjunction with various linkages to effect movement of the backrest to required positions.

9.3.3 Electric Module

9.3.3 Electric Module

Incorporates the electric/electronic components that make it possible to operate the chair. Works in conjunction with the various switches to move the chair to a position required by the dentist. Limit switches determine the extreme positions for both base and backrest movements.

A programme switch, when momentarily operated (pressed), quickly brings the chair back to its initial position, which allows the patient to get into or out of the chair.

9.4 Maintenance checklist

9. 4. 1 Hydraulic chairs

9. 4. 1 Hydraulic chairs

Daily (preferably by dental staff)

General cleaning of the chair at the end of each consulting day.

Weekly (by the technician)

  • check for correct function
  • check for any traces of oil leakage

3-monthly (by technician)

  • Check oil level, top up if necessary

Yearly (by technician)

  • Complete service of the chair
  • Change oil
  • Check correct function

9.4.2 Electro-hydraulic

General cleaning of the chair at the end of each consulting day.

  • Check for correct function
  • Check for any traces of oil leakage at various tube joints

3-monthly (by technician)

  • Check oil level, top up if necessary
  • Check electrical components for correct function and termination of wires.

Yearly (by technician)

  • Complete service of the chair
  • Change oil
  • Check correct function

üìö Key Takeaways

  • Electro-mechanical dental chairs use motors, gearboxes, and spindles to achieve chair base and backrest movements.
  • Chair base drive assemblies in electro-mechanical chairs utilize motors to convert angular motion into linear motion for raising and lowering.
  • Electric modules in these chairs incorporate electrical and electronic components, controlled by switches and limited by safety switches.
  • A program switch allows quick return of the chair to its initial position for easy patient access.
  • Maintenance checklists vary for hydraulic, electro-hydraulic, and electro-mechanical chairs, including daily cleaning, weekly to yearly checks of functions, oil levels, electrical components, and comprehensive servicing.
