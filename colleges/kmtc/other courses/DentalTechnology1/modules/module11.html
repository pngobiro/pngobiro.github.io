11. Dental Unit Accessories

This module introduces key dental unit accessories: the ultrasonic handpiece (scaler), fibre optic light, and surgery handpiece, detailing their functions, operations, and supply needs.

4.1 Ultrasonic Handpiece (Scaler)

The ultrasonic handpiece, or scaler, is used to remove scale and plaque buildup from teeth, particularly important for patients with poor dental hygiene.

Operation and Supply Requirements:

  • Oscillator: Fitted in the handpiece, it generates vibrations.
  • Exchangeable Oscillator Tip: Vibrations are transmitted to an exchangeable tip.
  • Electrical Supply: Powers the oscillator to generate ultrasonic vibrations.
  • Cooling Water: Required to cool the teeth and handpiece during scaling.

It is recommended to undergo scale removal treatment using an ultrasonic scaler at 6-month intervals for maintaining good dental health.

4.2 Fibre Optic Light

The fibre optic light is used primarily for fillings that require halogen light to harden. It provides focused, intense light directly to the treatment area.

Features and Supply Requirements:

  • Halogen Light: Employs halogen light for hardening fillings.
  • Light Conductor and Guide: Fibre optics conduct and guide the light.
  • Integrated Safety Thermal Filter: Eliminates infra-red light to prevent tissue damage.
  • Electrical Supply: Needed to power the halogen lamp.

4.3 Surgery Handpiece

The surgery handpiece, utilizing High Frequency (HF) current, is used for cutting and coagulation in dental surgery. It precisely controls tissue removal and minimizes bleeding.

Operation Principles and Supply Requirements:

  • HF Current: Delivers high-frequency current through the patient.
  • Active Electrode: Concentrates current, causing cell liquid evaporation and cutting.
  • Current Density: Customary densities range from 1 to 6 A/cm¬≤.
  • Shape of Electrode: Influences the precision and type of cut.
  • Shape of Current (waveform): Affects cutting and coagulation properties.
  • Electrical Supply: Only electrical supply is required to operate the handpiece.

Frequencies above 2 MHz are avoided to prevent stray current injuries. The surgery handpiece seals small blood vessels, making cutting relatively bloodless, suitable for minor dental interventions.

  • Fibre optic lights provide intense halogen light for hardening fillings, equipped with safety filters to remove infra-red radiation and require electrical power.
  • Surgery handpieces employ high-frequency electrical current for precise cutting and coagulation, needing only an electrical supply to operate.
