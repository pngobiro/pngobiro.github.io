1. Dental Diseases and Hygiene

This module introduces the fundamental concepts of dental diseases and the critical role of hygiene in maintaining oral health. We will explore common dental ailments and preventive measures.

1.1 Introduction

Sugar and starch become lodged between the teeth and teeth cavities, where they are attacked by micro-organisms. These micro-organisms use up the sugar for their own food and produce acid as waste products. The acid attacks the enamel (tooth material we can see) and dentine (tooth material covered by gum and enamel) causing them to desolve.

1.2 Main Dental Diseases

There are two main dental diseases:

  • Dental caries
  • Periodontal disease

1.2.1 Dental Caries

Dental caries is caused by:

  • Lack of hard food
  • Too much sweet or sugary food
  • Lack of calcium in the diet
  • Lack of vitamin D
  • Lack of teeth cleaning

1.2.2 Periodontal Disease

Periodontal disease is caused by:

  • Lack of vitamin A and C
  • Lack of massaging the gums
  • Imperfect cleaning of the gums

1.3. Dental Hygiene

Proper care of the teeth requires the following:

  1. Regular cleaning or brushing of teeth after every meal.
  2. Avoid eating too many sugary foods.
  3. Eating hard food e.g. carrots, cassava (this helps to remove the soft material from the gums and teeth).
  4. Eating diets rich in calcium, phosphates and vitamins A, D and C.

üìö Key Takeaways

  • Dental diseases are primarily caused by micro-organisms that thrive on sugars and starches left on teeth.
  • The two main dental diseases are dental caries (tooth decay) and periodontal disease (gum disease).
  • Dental caries is linked to factors like diet, nutrition, and oral hygiene.
  • Periodontal disease is associated with vitamin deficiencies and inadequate gum cleaning.
  • Proper dental hygiene involves regular brushing, a balanced diet, and consuming hard foods to help clean teeth and gums.
