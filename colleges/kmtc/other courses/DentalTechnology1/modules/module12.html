12. Medium Flow in Hydraulic/Pneumatic Elements

This module explains the medium flow within the hydraulic and pneumatic systems of a dental unit, emphasizing the function of various components in regulating air and water supply.

5. Medium Flow in the hydraulic/pneumatic elements

In case of repairs on the water or air lines at the dental unit, main cut-off valves are fitted. A water filter in the water line as well as a water separator in the airline are installed to ensure the quality of the water and air supply especially to the sensitive parts of the unit.

In the Pneumatic-Hydraulic diagram/dental unit, pressure adjustment valves are controlling the pressure for the handpiece, the Sprayvit and the tumbler filling.

The venturi tube (jet effect), responsible for the creation of underpressure for the suction nozzle and the water line to flush the cuspidor bowl are directly supplied.

An immersion heater to pre-heat any water getting in touch with the patient's teeth is installed for his/her comfort. For protection in case the thermostat fitted on the heater fails, an overpressure relief valve is mounted in the water line to the handpiece, which is used for cooling during drilling. Water is allowed to the handpiece only when air supply pressure is sufficient to drive the turbine.

A mechanical valve controls the water flow to the cuspidor bowl and another valve controls the flowrate through the venturi tube to increase or decrease the suction volume. The two press buttons on the Sprayvit control mechanical valves, and are closed by springs, if not pressed. A heating element installed on the Sprayvit ensures the pre-heating of the air before reaching the tooth, which gets sensitive after drilling. A number of solenoid valves are activated either by a foot switch or switches mounted at the tray, by the dentist or in case of the tumbler, at the assistant's unit.

6. MAINTENANCE OF DENTAL UNITS

This section introduces daily care routines for dental units, focusing on handpiece maintenance.

6.1 Daily care (done by dentist)

This part outlines the daily maintenance tasks typically performed by the dentist.

6.1.1 Handpiece maintenance

6.1.1 Handpiece maintenance

At the end of every mid-day and evening, apply the maintenance spray for one second to the treatment heads used. Treatment heads have to be removed overnight in order to prevent oil contained in the treatment head from running into the motor. This handpiece maintenance is not necessary on treatment heads mounted in dental units with installed and well-maintained oil nebulizers.

üìö Key Takeaways

  • Dental units are equipped with cut-off valves, water filters, and air-line separators to maintain supply quality and facilitate repairs.
  • Pressure adjustment valves regulate pressure for handpieces, Sprayvit, and tumbler filling, ensuring optimal equipment performance.
  • Venturi tubes create underpressure for suction and cuspidor bowl flushing, essential for maintaining a clean work environment.
  • Immersion heaters pre-heat water for patient comfort, with overpressure relief valves ensuring safety in water lines.
  • Daily handpiece maintenance, including spray lubrication and overnight removal, is crucial for preventing oil-related motor issues, although oil nebulizers can reduce this need.
