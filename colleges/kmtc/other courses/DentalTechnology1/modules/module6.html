6. Air Turbine and Syringe Systems

This module details the operational aspects of the air turbine and triple syringe systems, crucial components of modern dental equipment.

A. Air Turbine Pro-Ure Arrangement

Turn on Main Switch.

Procedure for Air Turbine Handpiece:

  1. Remove the air turbine handpiece by turning the union nut from the mount metal of the tubing and eliminate any foreign material inside the tubing by way of passing air and water through the bores. In order to get water through the bore, simply push down the foot lever for spray water of the foot controller.
  2. After sufficient cleaning up, attach the handpiece to the tubing and operate it with foot controller and ascertain whether the pressure gauge placed on the front of table would indicate the right point (1.8kg/cm¬≤ - 2kg/cm¬≤).
  3. If the working pressure is beyond the limit, turn Air pressure regulating valve to LEFT so as to get the right pressure (1.8 kg/cm¬≤-2 kg/cm¬≤).
  4. Otherwise the bearing used inside the head of Air turbine handpiece will be damaged or worn out soon.

B. Air Turbine Operation

  1. Connect handpiece and air turbine tubing by fitting bores A A' (for air), BB' (for water) and C C' (exhaust).
  2. After attaching handpiece, operate the foot controller for testing.
  3. It is required to ascertain whether pressure gauge indicates the right point (1.8 kg/cm¬≤) as soon as handpiece starts to work.
  4. Push down the water foot lever and see to it that water spray will come out right from a bore provided beside bur.

It suffices as far as the water spray strikes evenly the tip of bur.

Triple Syringe And Mirror & Cautery

A. Triple syringe

  1. By pushing the forward part of the lever, water jets out.
  2. Push the center part for medicine spray.
  3. By pushing the backward port, it works as a warm air syringe.

B. Mirror & Cautery

Either of Mirror & Cautery can be easily and interchangeably attached to the same handler in front of Unit Table.

When drawn out of the holder, the mirror is lit automatically.

There is provided a push type hand switch on the body of cautery.

Note: When medicine is run out, unscrew the cap of medicine bottle and replenish the bottle with medicine liquid.

üìö Key Takeaways

  • Air turbine systems require specific pressure settings (1.8-2 kg/cm¬≤) for optimal and safe operation to prevent bearing damage.
  • Proper setup of the air turbine handpiece involves connecting air, water, and exhaust lines correctly and ensuring no blockages.
  • The triple syringe offers water jet, medicine spray, and warm air functionalities through different lever actions.
  • Mirror & cautery units are interchangeable and designed for easy attachment to the dental unit, with automatic lighting for the mirror and a manual switch for the cautery.
  • Regular checks and adjustments of air and water flow are essential for maintaining the functionality of air turbine and syringe systems.
