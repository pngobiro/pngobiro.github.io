13. Dental Equipment Maintenance (Part 2)

This module continues the discussion on dental equipment maintenance, focusing on monthly and tri-monthly checks and essential safety protocols.

6.3 Monthly Care (by technician)

Monthly maintenance tasks are typically performed by a trained technician to ensure the dental unit's continued functionality and longevity.

6.3.1 Oil Nebulizer Check

6.3.1 Oil Nebulizer Check

In the upper sight-glass of the oil nebulizer, a small amount of turbine oil should be visible within a certain period (study unit instructions). If necessary, adjust. In the lower sight-glass, the oil level has to be between minimum and maximum mark. When the minimum mark is reached, pour the remaining oil and fill with fresh one.

VERY IMPORTANT: Absolute clean work is essential.

6.3.2 Oil Separator Cleaning

6.3.2 Oil Separator Cleaning

Release oil by opening the release screw during the time the handpiece is operating, till all oil is released.

CAUTION: Never re-use old oil.

6.3.4 Compressor Air Lines

6.3.4 Compressor Air Lines

Clean air filters and test for leaks.

6.4 Every 3 Months (done by the technician)

Maintenance checks every three months are more comprehensive and are also conducted by a technician.

Dental Unit Function Test

Dental Unit Function Test

  1. Check the function of all the valves and elements. Check for leaks.

Dental Chair Maintenance

Dental Chair Maintenance:

  1. Check hydraulic oil level and top up if necessary. Test for leaks.
  2. Lubricate links, chain, spindle. Check all functions.

7. SAFETY ASPECTS

7. SAFETY ASPECTS

Safety remains paramount during all maintenance procedures.

7.1 For the Technician at work

  1. Isolate the Unit
  2. Ensure that the current is not switched on while work is in progress
  3. Ensure that there is no voltage

7.2 General

7.2 General:

Currents upward of 50 mA (0.05 A) can be fatal if they pass through the heart.

Therefore a maximum voltage of 24 V has to be used at those elements (e.g. Sprayvit) getting in direct contact with the dentist or the patient. Carry out an insulation resistance test (minimum 3 MŒ©. After consultation, the dental unit should be switched off.

üìö Key Takeaways

  • Monthly technician care includes checking oil nebulizer levels, cleaning oil separators, and maintaining compressor air lines.
  • Oil nebulizer checks involve ensuring visible turbine oil in the sight-glass and maintaining oil levels between minimum and maximum marks.
  • Oil separator cleaning requires releasing old oil while the handpiece is running, with a strict caution against reusing old oil.
  • Tri-monthly checks include comprehensive function tests of the dental unit, dental chair maintenance, and lubrication of mechanical parts.
  • Safety during maintenance is critical, emphasizing unit isolation, power disconnection, voltage checks, and insulation resistance testing to prevent electrical hazards.
