4. Dental Equipment

This module provides an overview of dental equipment, focusing on the basic components and necessary supplies required for a functional dental unit.

Teeth Treating Procedures

Dental equipment is used for various teeth treating procedures, including:

  • Drilling
  • Grinding
  • Filling
  • Extraction
  • Cleaning
  • Surgery

Necessary Supplies for Dental Unit

Necessary types of supplies to the connection box of a dental unit include:

  1. Electricity
  2. Water
  3. Compressed air
  4. Underpressure
  5. Drainage

Respective Requirements of Supplies

Respective requirements of the different supplies are as follows:

  1. Electricity: Power consumption, Voltage rating
  2. Water: Pressure, Quality
  3. Compressed air: Pressure, Quality, flowrate
  4. Underpressure: rating, pipe dimensions, exhaust location
  5. Drainage: Pipe dimensions

Connections to Following Elements

Connections to the following elements of a dental unit require specific supplies:

  • Micromotor handpiece (2500--> 40000rpm): Electricity, water, compressed air
  • Turbine handpiece (600-> 300000rpm): Water, compressed air
  • Sprayvit: Electricity, water, compressed air
  • Surgery handpiece: Electricity
  • Ultrasonic handpiece: Electricity
  • Fibre optic light: Electricity
  • Suction nozzle (Aspirator): Underpressure, drainage

üìö Key Takeaways

  • Dental equipment facilitates various procedures like drilling, grinding, filling, extraction, cleaning, and surgery.
  • A dental unit requires essential supplies such as electricity, water, compressed air, underpressure, and drainage.
  • Each supply type has specific requirements, including voltage, pressure, quality, and flow rate.
  • Different components of dental equipment, like handpieces, sprayvit, and suction nozzles, require specific combinations of these supplies for operation.
