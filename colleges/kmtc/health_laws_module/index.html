Health Law Module

Duration: Approximately 20 hours Level: Intermediate

Course Overview

This course unit is designed to provide the trainee with knowledge and attitudes in public health laws of Kenya.

🎯 Course Objectives

  • Explain the relevant legal terms used in law and outline the history of public health laws.
  • Describe the source of Kenya's public health law.
  • Explain criminal law and its application in the enforcement of health laws.
  • Describe the arms of the government.
  • Explain the various public health laws and regulations.

Course Modules

  1. Module 1: Sources of Law in Kenya

    Explore the origins and hierarchy of laws in Kenya, including written and unwritten sources.

    2 hours

  2. Module 2: Structure/Hierarchy of Courts in Kenya

    Understand the organization and jurisdiction of courts in Kenya, from subordinate to superior courts.

    2 hours

  3. Module 3: Public Health Act CAP 242 - Part 1

    Learn about the notification, prevention, and suppression of infectious diseases as outlined in the Public Health Act.

    3 hours

  4. Module 4: Public Health Act CAP 242 - Part 2

    Further explore the Public Health Act, focusing on special provisions for epidemic diseases and venereal diseases.

    3 hours

  5. Module 5: Public Health Act CAP 242 - Part 3

    Continue with the Public Health Act, covering sanitation, housing, foodstuff protection, mosquito control, and cemeteries.

    3 hours

  6. Module 6: Food Drugs and Chemical Substances Act (CAP 254)

    Delve into the regulations concerning food, drugs, and chemical substances, including prosecution and penalties.

    2 hours

  7. Module 7: Law of Torts

    Gain an understanding of civil wrongs, damages, defamation, and trespass in the context of health law.

    2 hours

  8. Module 8: Criminal Law

    Explore the principles of criminal law, criminal responsibility, parties to offenders, and punishments.

    3 hours

Prerequisites

  • Basic understanding of legal terminology.
  • Interest in public health and law.
