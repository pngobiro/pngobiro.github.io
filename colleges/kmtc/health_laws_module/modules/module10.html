Module 10: Public Health Act (CAP 242) - Rules & Venereal Diseases

🎯 Learning Objectives

  • Describe the rules for prevention of disease under the act.
  • Explain the roles of health authorities and medical officers in relation to outbreaks.
  • Outline the provisions for venereal disease under the act.

📚 Rules for Prevention of Disease

Whenever any part of Kenya appears to be threatened by any formidable epidemic, endemic or infectious disease, the Cabinet Secretary may make rules for all or any of the following purposes:

  1. The speedy interment of the dead;
  2. House to house visitation;
  3. The provision of medical aid and accommodation, the promotion of cleansing, ventilation and disinfection and guarding against the spread of disease;
  4. Preventing any person from leaving any infected area without undergoing certain measures such as medical examination, disinfection, inoculation etc.
  5. The formation of hospitals and observation camps or stations, and placing therein persons who are suffering from or have been in contact with persons suffering from infectious disease;
  6. The destruction or disinfection of buildings, furniture, goods or other articles, which have been used by persons suffering from infectious disease, or which are likely to spread the infection;
  7. The removal of persons who are suffering from an infectious disease and persons who have been in contact with such persons;
  8. The removal of corpses;
  9. The destruction of rats and precautions to prevent them from spreading infection.
  10. The regulation of hospitals used for the reception of persons suffering from an infectious disease and of observation camps and stations;
  11. The removal and disinfection of articles which have been exposed to infection;
  12. Prohibiting any person living in any building or using any building for any other purposes if the spread of infection is likely;
  13. Any other purpose for the prevention, control or suppression of infectious diseases.

💡 Pro Tip

These rules allow the health authorities to react quickly to threatened or ongoing epidemics in order to minimise spread and fatalities.

📚 Health authority, Power of entry, Notification and Reporting

The health authority of any area within which any rules are in force shall do all that is necessary for mitigating any disease and aiding in the execution of the rules, and may direct any prosecution for the violation of the rules. The Director of Medical Services and his officers shall have power of entry on any premises or vessels for the purpose of executing the rules.

Every person who becomes aware of any unusual sickness or mortality among rats, mice, cats, dogs or other animals susceptible to plague or other formidable epidemic diseases, shall immediately report the fact to the medical officer of health. Medical officers of health must immediately report notifications of such to the Director of Medical Services.

Where an outbreak of any formidable epidemic disease exists or is threatened, the Director of Medical Services may requisition any land or buildings not occupied, or any materials and equipment required for fighting the outbreak.

📚 Part IV: Venereal Diseases - Duties & Infection

The provisions of this Act, unless otherwise expressed, in so far as they concern venereal diseases, shall be deemed to apply to syphilis, gonorrhoea, gonorrheal ophthalmia, soft chancre, venereal warts and venereal granuloma.

Every person who knows or has reason to believe that he is suffering from any venereal disease shall forthwith consult a medical practitioner with respect thereto, and shall place himself under treatment.

Every person undergoing treatment for any venereal disease shall, until cured or free from such disease in a communicable form, continue to submit himself to treatment. Any person who fails to comply is guilty of an offence.

Every medical practitioner attending or advising a patient in respect of any venereal disease shall:

  • Direct the attention of the patient to the infectious nature of the disease and to the penalties prescribed by this Act for infecting any other person with such disease.
  • Warn the patient against contracting marriage unless and until he has been cured of such disease or is free from such disease in a communicable form
  • Give to the patient printed information relating to the treatment of venereal disease.

A parent/ guardian of a child with venereal disease is responsible for ensuring they get treatment. If the parent fails to do this, they will be guilty of an offence.

Every person with a venereal disease in a communicable form who accepts employment where they handle food or children are guilty of an offence. Likewise any person who employs a person with venereal disease that handles food or children is guilty of an offense.

💡 Pro Tip

These measures allow for the control of venereal disease and ensure that people take responsibilty to seek treatment, and protect others from contracting such disease.

📚 Key Takeaways

  • The Cabinet Secretary may make rules for prevention of disease in the event of an outbreak.
  • Health authorities have wide powers to mitigate disease and ensure adherence to rules.
  • The Act provides for rules concerning venereal disease and seeks to protect others from infection.
