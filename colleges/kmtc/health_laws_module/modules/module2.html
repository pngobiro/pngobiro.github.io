Module 2: Structure/Hierarchy of Courts in Kenya

🎯 Learning Objectives

  • Describe the role of the judiciary in the balance of power.
  • Explain the concept of judicial independence.
  • Identify the key personnel in the judiciary.
  • Understand the functions of the Judicial Service Commission.
  • Outline the duties of the Attorney General.
  • Explain the hierarchy of authority among courts.

The Judiciary

The judiciary plays an important role in this balance of power among the three arms of Government.

To enable the judiciary to perform its functions impartially it has to be independent, for example judges do not hold office at the pleasure of the president and they can be removed only after an elaborate procedure set out in article 62 of the constitution has been followed.

The Judiciary

This is a collective name for the body of judges who preside over the courts.

Judges are appointed by the president on the advice of the judicial service commission. The chief justice is also appointed by the president. He is head of the judiciary system.

Judicial Service Commission

It make appointments to the judiciary both of the judges and magistrates. The chief justice is chairman. The commission is also responsible for the discipline and removal from office of magistrates and registrars.

The duties of an attorney general include:

  • To represent the government in civil cases and to act as prosecutor in important criminal cases.
  • Principal Legal Advisor to the Government:
  • Provides legal advice to the President, Cabinet, and government departments on legal matters.
  • Drafts government bills and legal instruments.
  • Publishes and maintains up-to-date records of all laws enacted by Parliament.
  • Supervises the Director of Public Prosecutions (DPP), ensuring the DPP's actions are lawful and consistent with public interest.
  • Protection of Public Interest:
  • Intervenes in legal proceedings involving significant public interest.
  • Ensures public institutions respect the rights of individuals and operate within the law.

These roles ensure that the AG upholds the rule of law, supports good governance, and contributes to the administration of justice in Kenya.

💡 Pro Tip

Understanding the role of the judiciary is crucial for grasping how laws are interpreted and enforced.

Hierarchy of Authority

The rule of hierarchy is that every higher court binds the lower courts. If the case has gone to the court of appeal, its decision is binding on all lower courts but not on itself.

Similarly the decisions of the high court are binding upon all the lower courts but do not absolutely bind the high court itself. A high court judge may, if he feels strongly enough refuse to follow another high court judge and the result will be a conflict of authority that will eventually be resolved by the court of appeal. In other words a high court judge cannot overrule one of his colleagues he can only disapprove his decision and not follow him, refusal to follow is however rare. Decisions of inferior courts do not bind anybody not even themselves.

Decisions of the foreign courts do not have any binding authority particularly when Kenya authority is lacking or unsatisfactorily.

Hierarchy of Courts

The Courts operate at two levels: Superior Courts and Subordinate Courts.

Important aspects the Structure of Courts are:

  • The structure – The hierarchy or levels of Courts
  • Establishment – The composition or who presides in that Court
  • Jurisdiction – The powers of different Courts to hear and determine disputes

The arrow on the figure shows the hierarchy of courts in Kenya.

There are two levels of courts:

  • Superior Court (consist of Supreme Court, Court of Appeal and High Court)
  • Subordinate Courts (Resident Magistrate Court, Kadhi Courts, Court Martial's, Tribunals, District Magistrate Courts Classes 1st, 2nd and 3rd.)

The arrows show flow of appeal from one level to the next. The arrows represent flow of appeals in both civil and criminal appeals except criminal appeals from District Magistrate class III which go to Resident Magistrates courts. This structure of the courts is based on the provisions of the Constitution, the Magistrates Court Act (Cap. 10), the Kadhis Court Act (Cap. 11) and the Armed Forces Act (Cap. 199) Laws of Kenya.

-Jurisdiction in the court refers to the authority or power of a court to hear and decide a case. It is the legal authority that a court has to adjudicate legal matters and render judgments.

💡 Pro Tip

Pay close attention to the hierarchy of courts, as it determines the flow of appeals and the binding nature of decisions.

✍️ Practice Exercise

1. Explain the role of the judiciary in maintaining the balance of power within the government.

2. Discuss the importance of judicial independence and how it is ensured in Kenya.

3. Describe the functions of the Judicial Service Commission in Kenya.

4. Outline the main duties of the Attorney General in Kenya.

5. Explain the principle of hierarchy of authority among courts and its significance.

View Solution

1. The judiciary plays a crucial role in maintaining the balance of power by interpreting laws and ensuring that the actions of the executive and legislative branches are constitutional. It acts as a check on the other branches, preventing any one branch from becoming too powerful.

2. Judicial independence is vital for impartial and fair administration of justice. In Kenya, it is ensured by provisions that judges do not hold office at the pleasure of the president and can only be removed through an elaborate constitutional procedure, protecting them from undue influence.

3. The Judicial Service Commission in Kenya is responsible for appointing judges and magistrates, overseeing their discipline, and handling their removal from office. It plays a key role in maintaining the integrity and independence of the judiciary.

4. The main duties of the Attorney General include representing the government in civil cases, acting as a prosecutor in important criminal cases, serving as the Principal Legal Advisor to the Government, drafting government bills, maintaining records of laws, supervising the DPP, and intervening in legal proceedings involving significant public interest.

5. The principle of hierarchy of authority among courts dictates that decisions of higher courts are binding on lower courts. This ensures consistency and predictability in the application of law. It also provides a mechanism for correcting errors and developing legal principles through appeals.

📚 Key Takeaways

  • The judiciary is essential for maintaining the balance of power in the government.
  • Judicial independence is ensured through constitutional provisions.
  • The Judicial Service Commission plays a vital role in judicial appointments and discipline.
  • The Attorney General has significant duties in representing and advising the government.
  • Higher courts' decisions are binding on lower courts, ensuring legal consistency.
