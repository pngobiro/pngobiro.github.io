Section 1: Multiple Choice Questions (20 Marks)

1.
The following are importance of data classification, except
a. It makes data easier to locate
b. It makes data easier to retrieve
c. It makes data duplication possible
d. It makes data more secure.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c. It makes data duplication possible

Explanation: The goal of classification is organization, retrieval, and analysis. Data duplication is generally a negative outcome (redundancy) that systems try to avoid.

2.
The current version of international classification of diseases used is?
a. ICD-10
b. Volume 3
c. ICD -11
d. Volume 1
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a. ICD-10

Explanation: In the context of the exam period (2021), ICD-10 was the standard implemented version.

3.
The following are true about SNOMED, except one
a. It is a systematized nomenclature of medicine
b. It is a reference terminology in health care
c. It provides consistent way of capturing, sharing and aggregating health data.
d. It is international classification of diseases
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d. It is international classification of diseases

Explanation: SNOMED is a nomenclature (terminology system), not a classification system like ICD.

4.
What is certification as used in health morbidity and mortality?
a. It is the underlying cause of death
b. It is the immediate cause of death
c. It is the recording of death
d. It is the confirmation of death
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d. It is the confirmation of death

Explanation: Certification is the process where a medical practitioner confirms the fact and cause of death.

5.
The following are true about health ethics, except.
a. It is professionalism
b. It is privacy
c. It is the standards and guidelines
d. It is the norms for operational
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b. It is privacy

Explanation: Privacy is a right protected by ethics, but not the definition of ethics itself (which encompasses standards, norms, and professionalism).

6.
The following are sources of data coding and indexing, except
a. Medical operations
b. Mortality
c. Symptoms
d. Prescriptions
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d. Prescriptions

Explanation: Prescriptions are typically tracked via pharmacy systems (ATC codes), whereas medical records coding focuses on diagnoses (morbidity/mortality) and procedures (operations).

7.
Historical development of ICD and ICPM discusses the statistical analyses of morbidity and mortality in health data classification
a. True
b. False
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a. True

8.
The basic structure and principles of classification (according to William Farr) is grouped into 5 areas. Which ones are they?
a. i, ii, iii, iv and v
b. i, ii, iii, v, and vi
c. i, ii, iv, v and vi
d. All of the above
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a. i, ii, iii, iv and v

Explanation: Farr's groups: Epidemic, Constitutional, Local, Developmental, Violence/Injuries.

Section 2: Short Answer Questions (30 Marks)

1.
Give examples of reference classification of disease coding based on WHO family of international classifications. (5mks)
Show Answer
  1. International Classification of Diseases (ICD)
  2. International Classification of Functioning, Disability and Health (ICF)
  3. International Classification of Health Interventions (ICHI)
2.
State five uses of a death certificate (5mks)
Show Answer
  1. Legal proof of death.
  2. Settlement of estate/inheritance.
  3. Claiming life insurance.
  4. Compilation of mortality statistics.
  5. Evidence for remarrying (widowhood).
3.
Define the following terminologies as used in health care data (5mks)
Show Answer

a. Clinical coding: Translating medical diagnoses/procedures into code numbers.

b. Classification: System of categories for assigning entities based on established criteria.

c. Maternal death: Death of a woman while pregnant or within 42 days of termination.

d. Diagnostic indexing: Organizing records by disease diagnosis for retrieval.

e. Nomenclature: A systematic listing of proper names of diseases.

4.
Give the meaning of the following conventions as used in the ICD-10. (5mks)
Show Answer

Point dash (.-): Indicates a 4th character is required.

Colon (:): Used when preceding words are incomplete; requires a modifier.

NOS: Not Otherwise Specified (Unspecified).

Dagger (†): Underlying cause (Etiology) code.

NEC: Not Elsewhere Classified (Other specified).

5.
List down five sources of mortality data (5mks)
Show Answer
  1. Civil Registration (Death Certificates).
  2. Health Facility Records.
  3. Police Records.
  4. Census.
  5. Surveys (Verbal Autopsy).
6.
Outline five uses of coded data in the health facility (5mks)
Show Answer
  1. Easy retrieval of records.
  2. Research and education.
  3. Hospital statistics and reporting.
  4. Reimbursement/Billing.
  5. Resource planning.

Section 3: Long Answer Question (20 Marks)

1.

You are the new Health Records and Information in charge A of facility X. The hospital uses manual data processing.

a. Explain five reasons for the need for use of computers (10 marks)

b. Advise the hospital management on the factors to consider before installation of the computers (5 marks)

c. Give five hindrances that you may face as a HRIO in implementing the use of computers. (5 marks)

Show Answer

a. Reasons for Computers: Speed of retrieval, space saving, data accuracy (validation), easy sharing/access, advanced analysis/reporting.

b. Factors to Consider: Cost, staff training needs, security/privacy, power supply/infrastructure, software compatibility.

c. Hindrances: Staff resistance, high cost, technical failures, lack of skilled personnel, privacy concerns.
