1.
The first attempt of disease classification was made by:
a. John Grant
b. Francoise Bossier de Lacroix
c. Francoise Bossier de Lacrois

8.
The fourth digit sub division in the ICD represents:
a. Site affected
b. Anatomical site and condition
c. Place of occurrence
d. Injuries
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b. Anatomical site and condition (or specific etiology)

Explanation: The 4th character typically provides more specificity regarding etiology, site, or manifestation. In Chapter XX (External Causes), it often denotes the place of occurrence.

9.
All the following are external causes of injuries in the index except:
a. Dislocation
b. Displacement
c. Fractures
d. Disorder
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c. Fractures (and a/b)

Explanation: "Fracture", "Dislocation", and "Displacement" are types of injuries (Nature of injury), found in Section I of the Index. "External causes" (Section II) lists how the injury happened (e.g., Fall, Collision). The question likely asks which is NOT an external cause, and since "Fracture" is a diagnosis, it's not an external cause.

10.
All the following are evidences of a live birth except:
a. Complete expulsion or extraction
b. Heart Beat
c. Pulsation of the heart
d. Definite movement of the voluntary muscles
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a. Complete expulsion or extraction

Explanation: "Complete expulsion" is part of the *definition* of the event of birth, but the *evidence* of life refers to the signs observed *after* expulsion (Heartbeat, Breathing, Pulsation of cord, Movement).

7.
Describe the layout of the ICD-10 Alphabetical Contents (6 Marks)
Show Answer

Volume 3 (Alphabetical Index) is divided into three sections:

  • Section I: Alphabetical Index to Diseases and Nature of Injury. This includes main terms for diseases, conditions, and injuries.
  • Section II: External Causes of Injury. This indexes the causes of accidents and violence (V, W, X, Y codes).
  • Section III: Table of Drugs and Chemicals. This table lists substances and codes for poisoning (accidental, intentional, etc.) and adverse effects.

Section 3: Long Answer Questions (20 Marks)

1.

The diagnostic index maintenance is a key function in Health Data Classification

a. State five methods in which the diagnostic index can be maintained (5 Marks)

b. Identify one of the stated methods of the diagnostic index maintenance above and describe how it is used, its equipment and advantages when used (15 Marks)

Show Answer

a. Methods of Maintenance:

  1. Card Index (Loose card system)
  2. Register Book (Bound book)
  3. Punch Card System
  4. Loose Leaf Binder
  5. Computerized Index

b. Description (e.g., Card Index):

  • Description: This method uses individual cards for each disease code. Cards are arranged in code number order in a cabinet.
  • How it is used:
    • A card is created for every disease entity (code).
    • When a patient is discharged with a specific diagnosis, their particulars (Name, IP No, Consultant, etc.) are entered onto the card corresponding to that disease code.
    • Cards are filed vertically in drawers.
  • Equipment:
    • Index Cards (Standard size e.g., 5x3 or 8x5 inches).
    • Filing Cabinet/Drawers.
    • Guide Cards (to separate sections).
  • Advantages:
    • Easy to add new cards for new diseases.
    • Allows for multiple entries per disease.
    • Simple to use and understand.
    • Flexible expansion.
