Section 1: Multiple Choice Questions (40 Marks)

Note: Questions 1-35 are missing from the source document. Only Questions 36-40 were available.

36.
The E codes are applicable to?
a) Injuries
b) Fractures and bones
c) Burns and dislocation
d) Fractures and dislocation
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Injuries

Explanation: In ICD-9, 'E codes' were used for External causes of injury. In ICD-10, this concept is covered by Chapters XIX (Injury, poisoning) and XX (External causes - V, W, X, Y codes). Contextually, E codes refer to the external cause/injury classification framework.

37.
Which of the following describes malignant neoplasm?
a) Tumor cells extend beyond primary cells
b) Is not invasive in nature
c) Do not spread to adjacent or distant sites
d) Undergo malignant changes but contained to part of origin
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Tumor cells extend beyond primary cells

Explanation: Malignancy is characterized by the ability of cells to invade surrounding tissue (extend beyond primary site) and metastasize. Options B and C describe benign tumors. Option D describes Ca in situ.

38.
The following are instructional terms except?
a) Excludes
b) Includes
c) NEC
d) Codes
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Codes

Explanation: "Includes", "Excludes", and "NEC" (Not Elsewhere Classified) are specific instructional notations/conventions in ICD. "Codes" is the output, not an instruction term itself.

39.
In data classification, the data collected is used for the following except?
a) Monitoring and evaluation of hospital statistics
b) Planning of hospital activities
c) Teaching and learning
d) Classifying of patients for priority management
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Classifying of patients for priority management

Explanation: Classifying patients for priority management (Triage) is a clinical operational process done in real-time. Data classification (coding) is usually a retrospective administrative/statistical process used for monitoring, planning, and research (teaching).

40.
ICD-11 was released
a) First in Australia in 2011
b) In Geneva by WHO
c) Yet to be released
d) Not yet worked on
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) In Geneva by WHO

Explanation: ICD-11 was released by the World Health Organization (headquartered in Geneva) in 2018 for testing and adoption.

Section 2: Short Answer Questions (40 Marks)

1.
Indicate whether the following statements are true or false (5marks)
Show Answer
  • a) Codes from chapter XX must be used in addition to a code from another chapter. (True) - External causes are secondary codes.
  • b) Mortality is the state of being diseased (False) - This defines morbidity. Mortality refers to death.
  • c) The ICD-10 has a set of three books with no relation (False) - They are interrelated volumes of the same system.
  • d) Excludes indicate the code and the title do not embrace the terms but are classified elsewhere (True).
  • e) The ICD is revised every 10 Years (True/False depending on context) - Historically true (decennial revisions), but ICD-10 broke this cycle lasting 30 years. In exam context, usually False or referring to the old principle.
2.
List the first eight chapters of ICD-10 (8 marks)
Show Answer
  1. I. Certain infectious and parasitic diseases
  2. II. Neoplasms
  3. III. Diseases of the blood and blood-forming organs and certain disorders involving the immune mechanism
  4. IV. Endocrine, nutritional and metabolic diseases
  5. V. Mental and behavioural disorders
  6. VI. Diseases of the nervous system
  7. VII. Diseases of the eye and adnexa
  8. VIII. Diseases of the ear and mastoid process
3.
Outline five rules and guidelines one should consider during indexing (5marks)
Show Answer
  1. Identify the lead term: Usually the noun expressing the pathological condition.
  2. Read notes: Check for "see" or "see also" cross-references.
  3. Verify in Tabular List: Never code directly from the index.
  4. Check for modifiers: Look for essential (indented) and non-essential (parenthetical) modifiers.
  5. Be specific: Code to the highest level of specificity (4th/5th character).
4.
Explain two methods used in maintaining a diagnostic index (4marks)
Show Answer
  1. Manual Card Index: Using physical cards arranged by code number, where patient details are written on the card corresponding to their diagnosis.
  2. Computerized Indexing: Using database software (like MS Access or hospital systems) to input coded data, allowing for automatic sorting, searching, and report generation.
5.
Explain William Farr’s suggested criteria in classification of diseases (5marks)
Show Answer

Farr proposed a classification based on the mode of origin or the anatomical site affected:

  1. Epidemic diseases: Diseases that strike many people (contagious).
  2. Constitutional (General) diseases: Affecting the whole body.
  3. Local diseases: Categorized by the anatomical system affected (respiratory, circulatory, etc.).
  4. Developmental diseases: Related to growth and development.
  5. Violence/Injuries: Resulting from external causes.
6.
Explain dual classification as used in ICD-10 in disease classification (5marks)
Show Answer

Dual classification allows a condition to be coded in two ways to capture both the underlying cause and the manifestation in a specific organ.

  • Dagger (†) code: Represents the etiology (underlying cause) and is the primary code.
  • Asterisk (*) code: Represents the manifestation (result) in a body system and is optional/secondary.
  • Example: Tuberculosis of the lung (A15.0†) with Tuberculous pneumonia (J15.x*).

7.
Outline the basic procedure one should follow during coding (8 marks)
Show Answer
  1. Analyze the medical record: Determine the main condition to be coded.
  2. Identify the lead term: Find the key word (condition) in the diagnosis.
  3. Locate the term in the Index (Vol 3): Search alphabetically.
  4. Follow instructions: Pay attention to cross-references ("see", "see also").
  5. Verify in the Tabular List (Vol 1): Check the alphanumeric code found.
  6. Read conventions: Check inclusion/exclusion notes and symbols at the category level.
  7. Assign the full code: Ensure 4th or 5th characters are used if required.
  8. Sequence codes: Order primary and secondary diagnoses correctly.

Section 3: Long Answer Questions (20 Marks)

1.
Patient Information is confidential and should not be disclosed to unauthorized persons. Describe circumstances which may lead to patient disclosure (10 marks)
Show Answer

Circumstances allowing disclosure of confidential information:

  1. Patient Consent: When the patient gives written or verbal permission to share data (e.g., for insurance or family).
  2. Legal Requirement (Court Order): When a judge or court orders the release of records for a legal case.
  3. Public Health Interest: Reporting notifiable diseases (e.g., Cholera, TB, COVID-19) to authorities to prevent spread.
  4. Statutory Reporting: Births and deaths must be reported to the registrar of persons by law.
  5. Medical Emergency: Sharing info with other clinicians to save the patient's life when they cannot consent (Continuity of Care).
  6. Research and Education: Using anonymized data for medical studies or teaching purposes.
  7. Child Protection: If there is evidence of child abuse or neglect.
  8. Prevention of Crime: If disclosure prevents a serious crime or harm to others (e.g., gunshot wounds).
2.
Discuss the importance and use of disease classification in the health sector (10 marks)
Show Answer
  1. Statistical Analysis: Enables the compilation of consistent morbidity and mortality statistics for comparison across regions and time.
  2. Resource Allocation: Helps governments and hospitals plan budgets, staff, and equipment based on the prevalence of specific diseases.
  3. Epidemiological Surveillance: Critical for tracking disease outbreaks, trends, and patterns (e.g., malaria zones).
  4. Reimbursement: Used by insurance companies (NHIF) to process claims and determine payments (DRGs).
  5. Clinical Research: Facilitates the retrieval of patient records by diagnosis for medical research and clinical trials.
  6. Standardization: Provides a common language for healthcare professionals globally, reducing confusion.
  7. Quality Assurance: Allows for the audit of medical care and outcomes for specific conditions.
