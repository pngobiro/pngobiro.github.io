19.
The mean of ten numbers is 58, if one of the numbers is 40, what is the mean of the other nine?
A. 18
B. 60
C. 162
D. 540
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B. 60

Explanation: Sum of 10 numbers = $58 \times 10 = 580$. Sum of remaining 9 = $580 - 40 = 540$. Mean of 9 = $540 / 9 = 60$.

35.
What is the standard deviation of the following series; 5,8,7,11,14
A. 3.24
B. 3.16
C. 3.53
D. 3.65
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B. 3.16 (or A. 3.24)

Explanation: Values: 5, 8, 7, 11, 14. Mean = 45/5 = 9. Deviations: -4, -1, -2, 2, 5. Squared Devs: 16, 1, 4, 4, 25. Sum = 50. Variance (pop) = 50/5 = 10. SD = $\sqrt{10} \approx 3.16$. Variance (sample) = 50/4 = 12.5. SD = $\sqrt{12.5} \approx 3.53$. Option B (3.16) is the population SD. Option C (3.53) is the sample SD. In general stats questions without specifying "sample", population formula is often used, but sample is more correct for datasets.

1.

Use the following data from a discrete series to compute median (5 marks)

Height (in cm)160150152161156154155
No. of students12844337
Show Answer

Steps:

  1. Arrange data in ascending order of Height.
  2. Calculate Cumulative Frequency (cf).
  3. Find position $(N+1)/2$.

Ordered Table:

  • 150 (f=8, cf=8)
  • 152 (f=4, cf=12)
  • 154 (f=3, cf=15)
  • 155 (f=7, cf=22)
  • 156 (f=3, cf=25)
  • 160 (f=12, cf=37)
  • 161 (f=4, cf=41)

Total N = 41. Position = $(41+1)/2 = 21$.

The 21st value falls in the group 155 (cf 22 covers 16-22). Median = 155 cm.

2.

The mean of the following data is 2.96, compute the mode (5 marks)

x12345
f359a2
Show Answer

Step 1: Find 'a' using Mean.

Mean = $\sum fx / \sum f$

$\sum f = 3+5+9+a+2 = 19+a$

$\sum fx = (1\times3)+(2\times5)+(3\times9)+(4\times a)+(5\times2) = 3+10+27+4a+10 = 50+4a$

$2.96 = (50+4a) / (19+a)$

$2.96(19+a) = 50+4a$

$56.24 + 2.96a = 50 + 4a$

$6.24 = 1.04a \Rightarrow a = 6$

Step 2: Find Mode.

Frequencies: 3, 5, 9, 6, 2. Highest frequency is 9.

Therefore, Mode = 3.

7.
Outline procedures of carrying out stratified sampling technique (5 marks)
Show Answer
  1. Define the population.
  2. Identify the relevant stratification variable(s) (e.g., age, gender).
  3. Divide the population into mutually exclusive subgroups (strata).
  4. Determine the sample size for each stratum (proportionate or disproportionate).
  5. Randomly select subjects from each stratum using simple random sampling.
8.
State the 5 differences between an interview schedule and a questionnaire (5 marks)
Show Answer
  1. Method: Questionnaire is filled by the respondent; Schedule is filled by the interviewer.
  2. Cost: Questionnaire is cheaper; Schedule is more expensive.
  3. Coverage: Questionnaire covers wide area; Schedule is limited.
  4. Bias: Questionnaire has respondent bias; Schedule has interviewer bias.
  5. Literacy: Questionnaire requires literate respondents; Schedule can be used with illiterate respondents.

9.

Use the table below to compute the given measures (20 Marks)

Ages in yearsNo. of Persons
0-1020
10-2025
20-3032
30-4040
40-5042
50-6035
60-7010
70-808

a) Mean using the arbitrary points method using class (40-50) (3 marks)

b) Median (3 marks)

c) Standard deviation (3 marks)

d) Quartile deviation (3 marks)

e) Construct a graphical presentation showing mode (8 marks)

Show Answer

Data Preparation: N = 212. Midpoints (x): 5, 15, 25, 35, 45, 55, 65, 75.

a) Mean (Assumed Mean A=45):

d = (x-A)/10 -> -4, -3, -2, -1, 0, 1, 2, 3.

fd -> -80, -75, -64, -40, 0, 35, 20, 24. Sum = -180.

Mean = $A + (\frac{\sum fd}{N} \times i) = 45 + (\frac{-180}{212} \times 10) = 45 - 8.49 = 36.51$.

b) Median:

N/2 = 106. Class 30-40 (cf=77 before, f=40). No, 20+25+32=77. 77+40=117. Class is 30-40.

Median = $30 + (\frac{106-77}{40} \times 10) = 30 + 7.25 = 37.25$.

e) Graph: Histogram with frequency polygon to show mode.
