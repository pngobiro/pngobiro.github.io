Section 1: Multiple Choice Questions (40 Marks)

1.
Which organization is responsible for maintaining and updating the international classification of diseases?
A. World health organization
B. United nations
C. Center for disease control
D. International classification of disease ICD foundations
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A. World health organization

Explanation: The WHO is the agency responsible for the ICD system.

2.
A 3-year-old boy was admitted at YY Sub-county hospital with congenital/heart condition. as a medical coder what is the appropriate way to assign code for this condition
A. Use general code for cardiovascular disease
B. Code any heart condition
C. Use most specific code possible to describe the diagnosis
D. Use a code for patient's symptom and sign
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C. Use most specific code possible to describe the diagnosis

Explanation: Coding principles dictate assigning the code to the highest level of specificity available (e.g., the specific congenital anomaly).

3.
Hospital XY is implementing ICD-11 and wants to ensure that HRIOs are using it consistently. How will the hospital ensure consistent coding and indexing?
A. Ensure training for all the HRIOs
B. Use both ICD-10 and ICD-11
C. Train only senior HRIOs
D. Each HRIO to develop their coding approach
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A. Ensure training for all the HRIOs

Explanation: Consistent implementation requires comprehensive training for all staff involved in the process.

4.
During the Historical development of ICD how often was the ICD revised
A. After every 2 years
B. After every 5 years
C. After every 10 years
D. When need Arose
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C. After every 10 years

Explanation: Historically, the ICD underwent decennial revisions (approx every 10 years) until ICD-10.

5.
A patient was diagnosed with hemorrhagic fevers which major category of the ICD-11 does this condition fall
A. Chapter 2: Neoplasm
B. Chapter 4: Endocrine nutritional and metabolic disease
C. Chapter 1: certain infectious and parasitic disease
D. Chapter 3: disease of the blood and blood forming organs
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C. Chapter 1: certain infectious and parasitic disease

Explanation: Hemorrhagic fevers (like Ebola, Marburg) are infectious diseases.

6.
Which convention of International classification of disease ICD10 is used to avoid unnecessary duplication while coding
A. Cross-reference
B. Parenthesis
C. Brace
D. Colon
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A. Cross-reference

Explanation: Cross-references (See, See Also) direct the coder to the correct location, preventing the listing of every possible term under every heading.

8.
A patient comes to the hospital with symptoms of fever, headache and muscle pain. Upon examination, he was diagnosed with dengue fever. As medical coder which condition will be selected as the main condition
A. Fever
B. Muscle pain
C. Dengue fever
D. Headache
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C. Dengue fever

Explanation: Code the definitive diagnosis (Dengue fever) rather than the symptoms (fever, pain) when the diagnosis is established.

9.
What is the purpose of monitoring and mortality reports?
A. To track the spread of diseases
B. Employ more personnel
C. Identify which facility is doing well
D. Find out factors of the disease
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A. To track the spread of diseases

Explanation: Mortality and morbidity reports are primary tools for epidemiological surveillance and tracking disease trends.

10.
Which of the following is an example of a morbidity report?
A. The number of hospitalization per 1000 people
B. The number of death per 1000 people
C. The number of new and old cases of a disease per 100,000 people
D. The number of years of life lost due to a disease
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C. The number of new and old cases of a disease per 100,000 people

Explanation: Morbidity refers to illness/disease state. Option C describes prevalence/incidence rates.

11.
Colon is one of the convention used in the ICD-10 for use in
A. Referring to previous notes
B. Used in 4th character sub-category code to replace a dash
C. Used in listing for inclusion and exclusion terms to indicate that neither the word that precedes it is not a complete term for assignment to that rubric
D. Used to avoid unnecessary duplication of term in the index
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C. Used in listing for inclusion and exclusion terms...

Explanation: A colon indicates that the preceding term is incomplete and requires one of the modifiers listed after the colon.

12.
The international classification procedures in medicine (ICPM) chapter 5 is concerned with
A. Surgical procedures
B. Procedures for medical diagnosis
C. Radiology and certain other application of physics in Medicine
D. Laboratory procedures
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A. Surgical procedures

Explanation: Chapter 5 of the original ICPM (1978) covers Surgical Operations.

13.
Disease classification is used to
A. Develop new medication for a disease
B. Determine the cause of a disease
C. Categorize disease based on symptoms and severity
D. Identify risk factors for a particular disease
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C. Categorize disease based on symptoms and severity (Best fit)

Explanation: Classification involves grouping (categorizing) diseases. It doesn't inherently determine cause or develop meds.

14.
When coding a patient’s medical records you come across a diagnosis that is not clear. What is the appropriate action to take?
A. Consult any clinician
B. Consult your fellow colleague
C. Consult the clinician who wrote the diagnosis
D. Consult your in-charge
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C. Consult the clinician who wrote the diagnosis

Explanation: The attending clinician is the source of truth for the patient's record. Queries should be directed to them for clarification.

15.
You are coding medical records for your friend who has a disease that carries a social stigma, such as HIV/AIDS, what is the appropriate action to take
A. Code and maintain confidentiality
B. Give your colleague to code
C. Code and discuss the condition with others
D. Discuss the condition with others then code

Correct Answer: A. Code and maintain confidentiality

Explanation: Professional ethics require maintaining confidentiality regardless of personal relationship or the nature of the disease.

29.
When coding an injury using ICD-11 medical coder should first consider
A. Age of the patient
B. The cause of the injury
C. Location of the injury
D. Severity of the injury
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B. The cause of the injury (or nature)

Explanation: While the nature of injury (Diagnosis) is usually the primary code, in ICD-11's new structure, understanding the mechanism/cause is crucial. However, typically one codes the injury (diagnosis) first, then the external cause. Options are ambiguous, but "Cause" is a major component of the coding block.

42.
State five challenges of using morbidity and mortality reports to assess the burden of disease in a facility (5 marks)
Show Answer
  1. Incompleteness: Not all cases are reported or recorded.
  2. Inaccuracy: Errors in diagnosis or coding affecting data quality.
  3. Timeliness: Delays in reporting reduce relevance for current decision making.
  4. Facility-based Bias: Data only reflects those who seek care, missing community burden.
  5. Lack of Standardization: Variations in data collection between departments or clinicians.
43.

A 40year old female suffers from dyspepsia, acid reflux and sharp abdominal pain... Gastroscopy revealed ulcer... scheduled for gastrectomy.

  1. What is hematemesis (1mark)
  2. Dyspepsia is classified under which chapter of the icd-10 (1mark)
  3. Gastrectomy is classified under which chapter of ICPM (1mark)
  4. The above conditions are classified under which principle of disease classification (1mark)
  5. Anaemia is classified under which chapter of ICD-11 (1mark)
Show Answer
  • i. Hematemesis: Vomiting of blood.
  • ii. Dyspepsia: Chapter XI (Diseases of the digestive system) - Code K30.
  • iii. Gastrectomy: Chapter 5 (Surgical Operations) in ICPM.
  • iv. Principle: Anatomical Site (Digestive System) or Etiology.
  • v. Anaemia (ICD-11): Chapter 03 (Diseases of the blood or blood-forming organs).
44.

A 16-year-old pregnant girl... pre-mature rapture membrane... gave birth to a live male infant... developed PPH. After six hours she died.

  1. What is the meaning of PPH (2 marks)
  2. Outline the immediate cause of death, underlying cause of death and antecedent cause of death (3 marks)
  3. Elaborate the legal procedure of issuance of B1 and D1 (5 marks)
Show Answer

a. PPH: Postpartum Hemorrhage (excessive bleeding after childbirth).

b. Causes of Death:

  • Immediate Cause: Postpartum Hemorrhage (PPH)
  • Antecedent Cause: Premature Rupture of Membranes (PROM)
  • Underlying Cause: Pregnancy / Childbirth (or specific complication leading to PROM).

c. B1 and D1 Issuance:

  • B1 (Burial Permit): Issued by the registrar of persons/chief upon presentation of the D1 (Death Notification).
  • D1 (Death Notification): Filled by a medical practitioner (if death in hospital) or police/chief (if at home), certifying the death. The original goes to the registrar for the death certificate, duplicate to family for burial permit.
45.

A 56-year-old male was brought to the hospital... died... brother and son came to pick the body... brother being his wife and the other was his 30year old first born for his first wife.

  1. Describe the legal procedure for issuance of burial permit (5marks)
  2. State advantages of legal procedure followed in the above scenario (5marks)
  3. Outline the content of a diagnostic index card (5marks)
  4. Design a diagnostic index card (5marks)

i. Burial Permit Procedure: Notification of death (D1) filled by clinician -> Family takes D1 to Civil Registrar -> Registrar verifies details -> Issues Burial Permit (Form B1).

ii. Advantages: Prevents illegal disposal of bodies; Ensures accurate mortality statistics; Legal proof for inheritance/insurance; Controls communicable diseases (public health).

iii. Content of Diagnostic Index Card: Disease Code, Disease Name, Patient Name, IP Number, Age, Sex, Consultant/Doctor, Date of Admission, Date of Discharge, Result.

Section 3: Long Answer Questions (20 Marks)

46.
While coding using ICD 11, the patient biodata is first captured in frame 1 of the KHIS dashboard. Name and explain the importance of each biodata captured. (20 marks)
Show Answer

Key Biodata Fields & Importance:

  1. Name: For unique identification and tracking of the patient.
  2. Age/Date of Birth: Crucial for epidemiological analysis (age-specific rates) and clinical management.
  3. Sex: Important for analyzing gender-specific disease prevalence and identifying specific health needs.
  4. Residence (Location): Identifying catchment areas, disease hotspots/outbreaks, and resource planning.
  5. Next of Kin: For emergency contact and legal/consent purposes.
  6. Marital Status: Socio-economic indicator that can influence health outcomes.
  7. Occupation: Identifying occupational health hazards and socio-economic status.

Note: Answer should elaborate on how these fields aid in the KHIS/ICD-11 context for public health surveillance and facility management.
