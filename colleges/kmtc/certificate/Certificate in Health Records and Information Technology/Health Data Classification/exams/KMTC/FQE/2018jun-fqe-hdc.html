Section 1: Multiple Choice Questions (20 Marks)

1.
Which one of the following is not a purpose of disease classification?
a. Systematic arrangement of health information
b. Management, Planning, Teaching and Research
c. Standardization and statistical analysis
d. None of the above
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d. None of the above

Explanation: Options A, B, and C are all valid purposes of disease classification. Therefore, "None of the above" is the correct choice as the exception (meaning none of the listed items are *not* a purpose).

2.
Which of the following statements is not correct?
a. ICD seventh revision was held in Paris in the year 1955
b. Michael Huber was succeeded by Jacques Bertillon
c. The eighth ICD revision was held in Geneva in 1965
d. Initially the ICD development purpose was for epidemiological reasons
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d. Initially the ICD development purpose was for epidemiological reasons

Explanation: Initially, the ICD (International List of Causes of Death) was developed primarily for mortality statistics (causes of death), not general epidemiology. The inclusion of morbidity (epidemiology) came much later (6th Revision, 1948).

3.
Which of the following statements is correct?
a. Aetiology refers to scientific study of causes of disease
b. The sixth decennial conference did not mark the beginning of the new era in the International vital health statistics
c. ICPM and ICD-9 were not developed in the same period
d. Codes U00-U99 are set aside for special purposes and additional codes
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a. Aetiology refers to scientific study of causes of disease

Explanation: This is the definition of aetiology. Option B is false (it DID mark a new era). Option C is false (ICPM 1978 and ICD-9 1975 were in the same era). Option D is true for ICD-10, but option A is the most definitional statement.

4.
Principal purposes of diagnostic index entails
a. Research, Planning, Teaching, Reporting
b. Planning, Reduction of work load, Filling, Retrieval
c. Research, Teaching, Retrieval, Reporting
d. Management, Research, Planning and M/E
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c. Research, Teaching, Retrieval, Reporting

Explanation: A diagnostic index is primarily a retrieval tool used to find records for research, education (teaching), and statistical reporting.

5.
Which one the following are not ICD-10 Conventions
a. Daggers and asterisk
b. Parentheses and square brackets
c. Colon and brace
d. None of the above
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d. None of the above

Explanation: All options (a, b, c) list valid ICD-10 conventions. Therefore, none of them are "not" conventions.

6.
Which one of the following statement is correct?
a. ICPM and ICD-9 were developed in the same period
b. Manifestation refers to scientific study of causes of diseases
c. Codes U00-U99 were not set aside for research and new additional codes diseases
d. None of the above
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a. ICPM and ICD-9 were developed in the same period

Explanation: ICD-9 was published in 1975/1977, and ICPM was published in 1978. They belong to the same era of development.

7.
Which of the following statement is incorrect?
a. Common terminologies is characteristic of ICD-10
b. Sample of morbidity and mortality is one of the sections of volume I of ICD-10
c. Statistical presentation is not a section in Volume 2 of ICD-10
d. Mental and behavioural disorders is the 5th chapter of ISCD-10
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c. Statistical presentation is not a section in Volume 2 of ICD-10

Explanation: "Statistical presentation" IS a section in Volume 2 (Instruction Manual). Therefore, stating it is *not* a section is incorrect.

8.
Which of the following is not a factor for better disease classification system?
a. The need for required vital health statistics
b. The different types of hospitals
c. Comprehensive list of Morbidity and Mortality
d. The type of medical service emphasized (curative/preventive)
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b. The different types of hospitals

Explanation: A disease classification system is designed to be applicable across all settings. While the *type* of service (curative/preventive) matters for procedural coding, the "type of hospital" itself is not a fundamental factor for the *design* of the disease classification system compared to the need for comprehensive lists and statistics.

9.
In ICD development for his good work Sir George Knibbs credited
a. Michael Hubber
b. Jacques Bertillon
c. Francaise Bossuer de Lacroix
d. William Cullein
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c. Francaise Bossuer de Lacroix (Sauvages)

Explanation: Sir George Knibbs (Australia), at the First International Conference, credited Fran√ßois Boissier de Sauvages with the first attempt at a systematic classification (Nosologia Methodica).

10.
In ICD-10 during disease translation to special number eponymous refers to
a. Varieties, sites of circumstances that affect coding
b. Diseases names after inventors/discoveries
c. Modifiers that do affect coding
d. Pathological names of diseases
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b. Diseases names after inventors/discoveries

Explanation: An eponym is a disease, structure, operation, or procedure named after the person who discovered or described it first.

11.
The cause of a disease or condition can also be known as
a. Topography
b. Morphology
c. Eatiology
d. Anatomical site
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c. Eatiology (Etiology)

Explanation: Etiology is the study of causation or origination.

12.
Parenthesis are used in volume I in the following
a. To enclose supplementary words which may not affect the code number
b. To enclose the code to which exclusion term refers
c. To enclose the three character codes of category included in that block
d. For enclosing synonyms, alternative words or explanatory phrases
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c. To enclose the three character codes of category included in that block

Explanation: In Volume 1 (Tabular List), parentheses are notably used in block titles to show the code range (e.g., A00-A09). (Option A is also true generally for the index/tabular content, but Option C is specific to the *structure* of Volume 1 blocks).

13.
ICD-9 has
a. 21 chapters
b. 18 chapters
c. 17 chapters
d. 9 categories
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c. 17 chapters

Explanation: The ICD-9 classification was organized into 17 chapters.

14.
The following are some of the information to be entered in a diagnostic index except?
a. IP number
b. Patients name
c. Age
d. IP days
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d. IP days

Explanation: While Length of Stay might be useful for analysis, the *essential* identifiers for retrieval are Name, IP Number, Age, Sex, and Diagnosis/Operation codes. "IP days" is a derived statistic, not typically a primary indexing field compared to the identifiers.

15.
The process of writing down the patient particulars into an identified material/medium once coding has been completed is:
a. Filling
b. Coding
c. Indexing
d. Sorting
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c. Indexing

Explanation: Indexing involves extracting pertinent data (codes and patient details) and recording them in an index (card, book, or computer) to facilitate retrieval.

16.
Which term best describes cancer
a. Viral manifestation
b. Abnormal growth of cell
c. Bacterial infection
d. Sexually transmitted infections
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b. Abnormal growth of cell

Explanation: Cancer (malignant neoplasm) is fundamentally characterized by uncontrolled, abnormal cell growth.

17.
The abbreviations NEC stands for?
a. Not Else Classified
b. Not Elsewhere Classified
c. Not Elsewhere Specified
d. Not Anywhere Classified
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b. Not Elsewhere Classified

18.
...........................commences at 22 completed weeks (154 days) of gestation (the time when birth weight in normally 500g), and ends seven completed days after birth.
a. Birth weight
b. Perinatal period
c. Neonatal period
d. Postnatal period
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b. Perinatal period

Explanation: This is the standard WHO definition of the perinatal period.

19.
Which of the following texts was produced along ICD-9
a. Alphabetical index
b. Tabular list
c. ICPM
d. ICSD
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c. ICPM

Explanation: The International Classification of Procedures in Medicine (ICPM) was published in 1978 as a supplement to ICD-9 for coding procedures.

20.
ISCD is:
a. A set of two large volumes whose functions is to enable translation of diagnosis into numbers
b. A set of three large volumes whose functions is to enable translation of diagnosis into numbers
c. A set of three with two rather large volumes whose functions is to enable translation of diagnosis into code numbers
d. None of the above
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c. A set of three with two rather large volumes whose functions is to enable translation of diagnosis into code numbers

Explanation: Refers to the structure of ICD-10 (International Statistical Classification of Diseases), which has three volumes (Tabular, Manual, Index). Option C provides the most specific description of the physical format.

Section 2: True or False Questions (20 Marks)

1.
"And" when included in rubric also means "or"
Show Answer

TRUE. In ICD titles, "and" stands for "and/or".

2.
John Grant was credited with the London Bill of Mortality
Show Answer

TRUE. John Graunt analyzed the London Bills of Mortality in the 17th century.

3.
Jacques Bertillon eventually died in 1932 after being a major guiding force in the International Classification of diseases (ICD)
Show Answer

FALSE. Jacques Bertillon died in 1922 (born 1851).

4.
Codes for chapter three runs from D 50 to D 99
Show Answer

FALSE. Chapter III (Diseases of the blood...) runs from D50 to D89. Codes D90-D99 do not exist in this chapter.

5.
Chapter XX of the ICD should be used hand to hand with chapter XVIII while coding
Show Answer

FALSE. Chapter XX (External Causes) is used with Chapter XIX (Injury, Poisoning). Chapter XVIII is Symptoms/Signs.

6.
While indexing, age should always be indicated in the indexing material as this would not interfere with the confidentiality of the patients medical information
Show Answer

TRUE. Age is a key variable for statistical analysis and indexing, and recording it in the index is standard practice.

7.
The behaviour of a neoplasm refers to the way in which cells acts and spreads within the body
Show Answer

TRUE. Behavior (malignant, benign, in situ, uncertain) describes the biological characteristics and spread potential.

8.
ICD-10 volume III has three sections of which section two is the external cause of morbidity and mortality
Show Answer

TRUE. Section II of Volume 3 is "External causes of injury".

9.
Zero (0) when added as a fourth character in a code means unspecified
Show Answer

FALSE. Usually, the digit **9** is used for "unspecified" and **0** or **8** for "other specified". However, this can vary by category, but 9 is the standard for NOS.

10.
ICPM is a mandatory tool which should be used by all hospitals practicing medical coding
Show Answer

FALSE. ICPM was a trial publication. Most countries adopted national procedure classifications (like OPCS-4, CPT, ACHI) or waited for ICHI. It was never universally mandatory.
