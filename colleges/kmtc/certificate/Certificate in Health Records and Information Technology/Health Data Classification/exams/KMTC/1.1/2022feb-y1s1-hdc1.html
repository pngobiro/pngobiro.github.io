1.
The Classification of William Cullen was published in?
A. 1707
B. 1790
C. 1777
D. 1785
Show Answer

Correct Answer: D. 1785

Explanation: William Cullen's Synopsis Nosologiae Methodicae was first published in 1769, but the 1785 edition is considered the standard edition that influenced subsequent classifications.

2.
The Alphabetical index Volume 3 of ICD has the following sections, except?
A. Table of drugs and chemicals
B. Guidelines for ICD coding
C. External causes of injury
D. Disease and nature of injury
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B. Guidelines for ICD coding

Explanation: Guidelines for coding are found in Volume 2 (Instruction Manual). Volume 3 contains the Alphabetical Index (Section I), External causes of injury (Section II), and Table of drugs and chemicals (Section III).

3.
The 6th revision of ICD books was done in which year?
A. 1965
B. 1954
C. 1948
D. 1946
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C. 1948

Explanation: The Sixth Revision Conference was held in Paris in 1948. This was a landmark revision as it marked the beginning of the WHO's responsibility for the ICD and included causes of morbidity for the first time.

4.
Neoplasm is categorized into:
A. Four types
B. Three types
C. Five types
D. Two types
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A. Four types

Explanation: Neoplasms are primarily categorized by behavior: 1. Malignant, 2. Benign, 3. In situ, 4. Uncertain or unknown behavior.

5.
Name of a disease or procedure that honors the discoverer is also known as?
A. Antonyms
B. Eponyms
C. Morphology
D. Diagnosis
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B. Eponyms

Explanation: An eponym is a person, place, or thing after whom or which something is named (e.g., Parkinson's disease, Alzheimer's disease).

6.
Table of drugs and chemicals is divided into:
A. Four sections
B. Three sections
C. Five sections
D. Six sections
Show Answer

Correct Answer: D. Six sections

Explanation: The Table of Drugs and Chemicals in ICD-10 Volume 3 typically has 6 columns corresponding to the intent: 1. Substance, 2. Poisoning (Accidental), 3. Poisoning (Intentional self-harm), 4. Poisoning (Assault), 5. Poisoning (Undetermined), 6. Adverse effect.

7.
External cause of morbidity and mortality means:
A. The disease has been caused by nature factor
B. The disease has been caused by external factor
C. The code number should be found in section one of the alphabetical index
D. None of the above
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B. The disease has been caused by external factor

Explanation: External causes codes (V, W, X, Y in ICD-10) classify environmental events and circumstances as the cause of injury, poisoning, and other adverse effects.

8.
.......................is used to enclose synonyms, alternative words or explanatory phrases.
A. Round brackets
B. Colon
C. Square brackets
D. Brace
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C. Square brackets

Explanation: Square brackets [ ] are used to enclose synonyms, alternative words, or explanatory phrases. Parentheses ( ) are used to enclose supplementary words (modifiers).

9.
The following are the principals of William Farr, except?
A. Developmental diseases
B. Diseases was according to the nature
C. Epidemic disease
D. General diseases
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B. Diseases was according to the nature

Explanation: William Farr's classification was based on 5 groups: Epidemic diseases, Constitutional (General) diseases, Local diseases (by anatomical site), Developmental diseases, and Injuries (Violence). "Diseases according to nature" is more characteristic of earlier nosologies like Sauvages or Cullen, whereas Farr focused on anatomical site and epidemic nature.

10.
Which one of the following is not true about ICD-10?
A. Has 22 chapters
B. Has 9 categories
C. Has 3 volumes
D. It's green in colour
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B. Has 9 categories

Explanation: ICD-10 contains thousands of categories (A00-Z99), not just 9. It has 22 chapters (originally 21) and consists of 3 volumes. The published books from WHO are often green (Vol 2), red (Vol 1), etc., but "9 categories" is definitely factually incorrect.

11.
The most important book among the three ICD books/are
A. Volume 3
B. Volume 1, 2 and 3
C. Volume 1 and 3
D. Volume 1, 2, 3 and ICPM
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B. Volume 1, 2 and 3

Explanation: All three volumes are essential for accurate coding. Volume 1 contains the codes, Volume 2 the rules, and Volume 3 the index to find the codes.

12.
The person who recommended the merger of the two institutions (ISC and Health organization of the league of the nations was.
A. Michael Huber
B. Jacques Bertillon

Section 2: Short Answer Questions (30 Marks)

1.
List the five groups in which Farr's classification principles were arranged? (5 Marks)
Show Answer
  1. Epidemic diseases: Diseases that attack numbers of people at the same time.
  2. Constitutional (General) diseases: Diseases affecting the general state of the body (e.g., cancer, gout).
  3. Local diseases: Arranged according to the anatomical site or organ system involved.
  4. Developmental diseases: Disorders of growth and development.
  5. Violence/Injuries: Diseases that are the direct result of violence or accidents.
2.
Give any five chapters of ICPM (5 Marks)
Show Answer

International Classification of Procedures in Medicine (ICPM) Chapters:

  1. Procedures for Medical Diagnosis
  2. Laboratory Procedures
  3. Radiology and certain other applications of physics in medicine
  4. Preventive Procedures
  5. Surgical Operations
  6. Drugs, Medicaments, and Biological Agents
  7. Other Therapeutic Procedures
  8. Ancillary Procedures
3.
List six sections of Instructional Manual of ICD-10. (6 Marks)
Show Answer

Sections of ICD-10 Volume 2 (Instruction Manual):

  1. Introduction
  2. Description of the International Statistical Classification of Diseases and Related Health Problems
  3. How to use the ICD
  4. Rules and guidelines for mortality and morbidity coding
  5. Statistical presentation
  6. History of the development of the ICD
4.
Mention any five characteristics of the 10th revision of the ICD. (5 Marks)
Show Answer
  1. Alphanumeric Coding: Uses codes starting with a letter followed by numbers (e.g., A00.0).
  2. Three Volumes: Consists of Tabular List (Vol 1), Instruction Manual (Vol 2), and Alphabetical Index (Vol 3).
  3. Chapter Structure: Contains 21 (now 22) chapters covering all health conditions.
  4. Specific Conventions: Uses symbols like the dagger (†) for etiology and asterisk (*) for manifestation.
  5. Expansion Capability: The U codes are left open for future additions and temporary assignments.
  6. Extensive Detail: Provides greater specificity than ICD-9, often using 4th character subdivisions.
5.
List five purposes of disease classification. (5 Marks)
Show Answer
  1. Statistical Analysis: To compile morbidity and mortality statistics for health planning.
  2. Resource Allocation: To determine funding and resource distribution based on disease burden.
  3. Epidemiological Surveillance: To monitor disease outbreaks and trends over time.
  4. Reimbursement: To facilitate billing and payment systems (like DRGs).
  5. Clinical Research: To assist in retrieving records for medical research and education.
6.
Give the Major classifications of Neoplasms. (4Marks)
Show Answer
  1. Malignant: Primary or secondary (metastatic) cancers.
  2. Benign: Non-cancerous growths.
  3. In Situ: Pre-cancerous cells that have not invaded surrounding tissue.
  4. Uncertain or Unknown Behavior: Tumors where malignancy cannot be confirmed or determined.

Section 3: Essay Questions (20 Marks)

1.
Outline the Basic Diagnostic Coding Guidelines. (10 Marks)
Show Answer

Basic Diagnostic Coding Guidelines:

  1. Identify the statement to be coded: Read the medical record to determine the main condition (diagnosis) treated or investigated.
  2. Locate the Lead Term: Identify the main word (condition, disease, or problem) in the diagnosis and look it up in the Alphabetical Index (Volume 3). Do not code directly from the alphabetical index alone.
  3. Read Notes and Modifiers: Check for any "see", "see also", or essential modifiers (subterms) under the lead term that might affect code selection.
  4. Verify in Tabular List: Take the code found in the Index and verify it in the Tabular List (Volume 1).
  5. Check Conventions and Notes: Look for inclusion notes, exclusion notes (Excludes1, Excludes2), and other instructional notes at the chapter, block, or category level in Volume 1.
  6. Assign the Code: Assign the code to the highest level of specificity (use 4th or 5th characters if available).
  7. Dual Classification: If applicable, apply the dagger (†) code for the underlying cause (etiology) and the asterisk (*) code for the manifestation.
  8. Sequence Correctly: Ensure the main condition (primary diagnosis) is sequenced first, followed by comorbidities and complications.
2.
Highlight the purposes of Disease classification. (10 Marks)
Show Answer

Purposes of Disease Classification:

  • Standardization: It provides a common language for reporting and monitoring diseases globally, allowing for comparison of data across countries and time periods.
  • Health Statistics: It enables the collection, processing, classification, and presentation of mortality (death) and morbidity (illness) statistics.
  • Epidemiology: Facilitates the study of disease patterns, causes, and effects in populations, helping to identify epidemics and health trends.
  • Health Planning and Management: Helps governments and organizations plan health services, allocate resources, and evaluate the effectiveness of health programs based on disease burden.
  • Reimbursement and Administration: Used in insurance and billing systems (e.g., Casemix, DRGs) to determine payment for healthcare services provided.
  • Clinical Research: Allows researchers to easily retrieve patient records by diagnosis for studies on treatment outcomes, drug efficacy, and disease progression.
  • Quality of Care: Supports the evaluation of medical care quality by enabling the review of outcomes for specific conditions.
