Questions (30 Marks)

1.
Differentiate between the term Classification and Disease Classification (3Mks)
Show Answer
  • Classification: A general system of categories to which entities are assigned according to established criteria. It involves grouping similar items together to provide a structure for analysis.
  • Disease Classification: A specific system of categories to which morbid entities (diseases, injuries, and health conditions) are assigned according to established criteria. It transforms medical terms into alphanumeric codes to permit systematic recording, analysis, and comparison of mortality and morbidity data.
2.
Explain the concept of derived classifications (3Mks)
Show Answer

Derived Classifications are part of the WHO Family of International Classifications (WHO-FIC). They are:

  • Based on reference classifications (like ICD and ICF).
  • Adopt the reference classification structure and categories.
  • Provide additional detail for a specific purpose or specialty (e.g., Dentistry, Oncology, Neurology).
  • Examples: ICD-O (Oncology), ICD-NA (Neurology), ICD-DA (Dentistry).
3.
In the disease classification according to alphabetical index in ICD 10, the letter U was not assigned. Elaborate some of the reasons as per to why the code U was left out. (4Mks)
Show Answer

The letter 'U' was intentionally left vacant in the original ICD-10 structure to allow for:

  1. Future Expansion: To accommodate permanent assignment of new diseases that may be discovered in the future or changes in classification needs without disrupting the numbering of existing chapters.
  2. Provisional Assignment: To be used for the temporary assignment of new diseases of uncertain etiology (e.g., U04 for SARS, U07 for COVID-19/Vaping-related illness).
  3. Research Purposes: Codes U50-U99 are available for research projects to test alternative sub-classifications.
  4. Antimicrobial Resistance: Codes U80-U89 were later assigned to classify resistance to antimicrobial drugs.
4.
Distinguish the definition of Primary and Secondary diagnoses according to WHO (3Mks)
Show Answer
  • Primary (Principal) Diagnosis: The condition, diagnosed at the end of the episode of health care, primarily responsible for the patient's need for treatment or investigation. It is the main reason for the encounter/admission.
  • Secondary Diagnosis: A condition that coexists with the primary diagnosis at the time of admission or develops during the stay, which affects the patient care, requiring clinical evaluation, therapeutic treatment, diagnostic procedures, extended length of hospital stay, or increased nursing care and monitoring (e.g., comorbidities and complications).
5.
Discuss the convention of Dagger “†” and Asterisk “*” Symbols as used in ICD (6Mks)
Show Answer

This convention is used for Dual Coding or "Etiology and Manifestation" coding:

  • Dagger (†) Code: Represents the primary code or the underlying disease (etiology). It describes the cause of the illness. It is mandatory for coding and is used for primary tabulation of morbidity/mortality statistics.
  • Asterisk (*) Code: Represents the manifestation of the disease in a specific organ or body site. It is an optional, additional code that provides further clinical detail. It must never be used alone; it must always follow the dagger code.
  • Example: Tuberculosis of the lung is coded as A15.0† (Etiology) and Tuberculous pneumonia J15.0* (Manifestation).
6.
Give at least 5 reasons as to why it’s important to have a uniform system of disease classification (5Mks)
Show Answer
  1. Comparability: Enables the comparison of health data between different hospitals, regions, and countries.
  2. Communication: Provides a standard language for health professionals, ensuring everyone understands the diagnosis clearly.
  3. Statistical Analysis: Facilitates the aggregation of morbidity and mortality statistics for epidemiological surveillance.
  4. Trend Monitoring: Allows for the tracking of disease patterns over time (longitudinal studies).
  5. Resource Allocation: Helps governments and organizations plan health services and allocate resources based on reliable data.
  6. Retrieval: Makes indexing and retrieval of medical records easier for research and education.
7.

Give the meaning of the following Key abbreviations as used in the ICD 10 (6Mks)

  1. ICD-O
  2. WHO-FIC
  3. ICF
  4. NOS
  5. ICPM
  6. NEC
Show Answer
  • a. ICD-O: International Classification of Diseases for Oncology.
  • b. WHO-FIC: World Health Organization Family of International Classifications.
  • c. ICF: International Classification of Functioning, Disability and Health.
  • d. NOS: Not Otherwise Specified (Implies "Unspecified").
  • e. ICPM: International Classification of Procedures in Medicine.
  • f. NEC: Not Elsewhere Classified (Implies "Other specified").
