Q1.

If you are to attend a meeting with Members of Health Management Team (HMT) to discuss the information generated in the Institution. As a Health Records and Information Officer; Explain to the meeting the following; Importance, Purpose and Uses of disease classification in your Health facility

a) Importance of disease classification (5 Marks)

b) Purpose of classification (5 Marks)

c) Uses of disease classification (5 Marks)

Show Answer

a) Importance of Disease Classification:

  • Standardization: It provides a common language for health professionals to communicate about diseases across different regions and countries.
  • Comparability: Enables the comparison of morbidity and mortality data between different hospitals, regions, and time periods.
  • Efficient Retrieval: Allows for the easy indexing and retrieval of medical records based on diagnosis for patient care and research.
  • Monitoring Trends: Essential for tracking disease patterns, outbreaks (epidemics), and the overall health status of the population.
  • Quality Assurance: Facilitates the evaluation of medical care quality by allowing review of outcomes for specific conditions.

b) Purpose of Classification:

  • Statistical Aggregation: To convert detailed medical terminologies into grouped categories useful for statistical analysis.
  • Data Storage: To transform verbal descriptions of diseases into alphanumeric codes, making storage in databases efficient.
  • Decision Making: To provide structured data that management can use for planning and resource allocation.
  • Epidemiological Surveillance: To enable the tracking of incidence and prevalence of diseases.
  • Stability: To provide a stable framework that can be updated periodically (e.g., ICD revisions) while maintaining historical continuity.

c) Uses of Disease Classification:

  • Reimbursement: Used by insurance companies (e.g., NHIF) and billing departments to determine payments based on diagnosis (DRGs).
  • Resource Allocation: Helps the HMT determine where to allocate budget, staff, and medicine based on the most common disease burdens.
  • Clinical Research: Researchers use codes to identify patient cohorts for studies on treatment effectiveness.
  • Health Planning: Governments use the data to design public health programs (e.g., Malaria control) based on coded data.
  • Administrative Reporting: Essential for generating monthly and annual hospital workload reports (Morbidity/Mortality returns).
Q2.
Explain the Role of Health Records and Information Officer in Disease Classification (15 marks)
Show Answer

Roles of the HRIO in Disease Classification:

  1. Clinical Coding: The primary role is to accurately translate medical diagnoses and procedures written by clinicians into standard ICD codes (e.g., ICD-10).
  2. Analysis of Medical Records: Reviewing patient files (discharge summaries) to identify the principal diagnosis, comorbidities, and complications to ensure all conditions are captured.
  3. Quality Assurance/Auditing: Ensuring codes assigned are accurate and consistent with coding guidelines. Verifying that the diagnosis matches the treatment given.
  4. Querying Clinicians: Communicating with doctors to clarify ambiguous, incomplete, or conflicting diagnoses to ensure the correct code is assigned.
  5. Indexing: Maintaining the Disease and Operation Indices to facilitate the retrieval of records for research and administrative purposes.
  6. Confidentiality: ensuring that coded data is handled securely and patient privacy is maintained during the classification and reporting process.
  7. Reporting: Compiling coded data into statistical reports (morbidity and mortality returns) for the Ministry of Health and hospital management.
  8. Training/Education: Orienting other staff and students on the importance of clear documentation to facilitate accurate classification.
  9. System Maintenance: ensuring the coding manuals (ICD books) are up to date and physically available, or maintaining the computerized coding software.
