Section 1: True or False (10 Marks)

Indicate whether the following statements are TRUE or FALSE.

1.
In ICD-10 parenthesis is used to enclose the code to which exclusive term refer to.
Show Answer

TRUE

Explanation: While parentheses generally enclose supplementary words (modifiers), in the context of Exclusion notes (Excludes 1/2), parentheses are used to enclose the code of the excluded condition, directing the coder to the correct location.

2.
ICPM contain nine categories and two volumes.
Show Answer

TRUE

Explanation: The International Classification of Procedures in Medicine (ICPM, 1978) was published in two volumes and divided into 9 chapters (categories).

3.
The general rule in code is to look for site and not condition.
Show Answer

FALSE

Explanation: The general rule is to look for the Condition (Lead term) first, then the site (modifier). Looking for the site first is a common error.

4.
The first attempt classified disease was made by Mr. John Grant.
Show Answer

FALSE

Explanation: François Boissier de Sauvages is credited with the first systematic attempt to classify diseases (Nosologia Methodica). John Graunt is the father of vital statistics/mortality bills.

5.
The first international conference for the revision of the berttillion was held in peris in August 1001.
Show Answer

FALSE

Explanation: It was held in Paris in August 1900 (not 1001).

6.
In 1944 a provisional classification of diseases and injuries were published in both UK and USA for use in the tabulation of morbidity statistic.
Show Answer

TRUE

Explanation: This refers to the "Manual for Coding Causes of Illness" (1944) which was a precursor to including morbidity in the 6th Revision.

7.
The proposal of various meeting on the revision was discussed by WHO in 1975.
Show Answer

TRUE

Explanation: The International Conference for the Ninth Revision of the International Classification of Diseases was convened by WHO in Geneva in 1975.

8.
8th revision conference was held in peris in February 1957
Show Answer

FALSE

Explanation: The 8th Revision Conference was held in Geneva in 1965. (The 7th was Paris, 1955).

9.
The ICD-10 has four main recommended mortality list summaries.
Show Answer

TRUE

Explanation: ICD-10 includes four special tabulation lists for mortality: General Mortality (Condensed list), General Mortality (Selected list), Infant/Child Mortality (Condensed), and Infant/Child Mortality (Selected).

10.
ICPM has sex section.
Show Answer

FALSE

Explanation: ICPM (Procedures) is organized by body system (e.g., Surgery) and modality (Radiology, Lab). While some procedures are specific to a sex (e.g., Gynecological surgery), there is no specific "Sex Section" or chapter titled "Sex".

Section 3: Theory Questions

1.
State the steps and precaution involved when indexing diseases and health related condition (10mrks)
Show Answer

Steps in Indexing:

  1. Review the Record: Read the medical record (discharge summary, operation notes) to identify the diagnosis/procedure to be indexed.
  2. Select the Code: Identify the correct ICD code that was assigned during the coding process.
  3. Locate the Index Card/Record: Find the diagnostic index card corresponding to that code number.
  4. Entry: Record the patient's identification details (IP Number, Name, Age, Sex, Physician) onto the index card.
  5. Filing: Return the index card to its correct place in the sequence.

Precautions:

  • Ensure accuracy of the code before entry.
  • Ensure legibility of handwriting if manual.
  • Verify patient identity (IP number) to avoid mismatching.
  • Ensure data confidentiality during the process.
  • Regularly audit the index for filing errors.
2.
Explain the meaning of the following terms (5mrks)
Show Answer
  • a) Diagnostic indexing: The process of extracting patient data and diagnosis codes from medical records and organizing them into a secondary record system (index) to facilitate retrieval by diagnosis.
  • b) Disease coding: The translation of medical terminology describing diseases, injuries, and conditions into alphanumeric codes (e.g., ICD-10).
  • c) Cause of death and underline cause of death:
    • Cause of death: Any condition listed on the death certificate that contributed to death.
    • Underlying cause: The disease or injury which initiated the train of morbid events leading directly to death, or the circumstances of the accident or violence which produced the fatal injury.
  • d) Lead terms: The word or phrase used as the starting point to look up a condition in the Alphabetical Index (usually the pathological condition).
  • e) Eponyms: Diseases, procedures, or anatomical structures named after a person (usually the discoverer, e.g., Parkinson's Disease).
3.
Describe the layer out of ICPM (15mrks)
Show Answer

Note: This likely refers to the "Layout" or Structure of the International Classification of Procedures in Medicine.

Structure of ICPM (1978):

  • Volumes: Published in two volumes.
  • Chapters: Divided into 9 Chapters based on medical specialty or type of procedure:
    1. Procedures for Medical Diagnosis
    2. Laboratory Procedures
    3. Radiology and related physics
    4. Preventive Procedures
    5. Surgical Operations
    6. Drugs, Medicaments, and Biological Agents
    7. Other Therapeutic Procedures
    8. Ancillary Procedures
    9. Other Procedures
  • Coding System: Uses a numeric code (often 4 digits). E.g., 5-123.
  • Purpose: To classify surgical, diagnostic, and preventive procedures for statistical and administrative use.
4.
List the last nine chapter of the ICD-10 (10mrks)
Show Answer

ICD-10 has 21/22 chapters. The last nine would be XIV to XXII.

  1. XIV (N00-N99): Diseases of the genitourinary system.
  2. XV (O00-O99): Pregnancy, childbirth and the puerperium.
  3. XVI (P00-P96): Certain conditions originating in the perinatal period.
  4. XVII (Q00-Q99): Congenital malformations, deformations and chromosomal abnormalities.
  5. XVIII (R00-R99): Symptoms, signs and abnormal clinical and laboratory findings, NEC.
  6. XIX (S00-T98): Injury, poisoning and certain other consequences of external causes.
  7. XX (V01-Y98): External causes of morbidity and mortality.
  8. XXI (Z00-Z99): Factors influencing health status and contact with health services.
  9. XXII (U00-U99): Codes for special purposes.

6.
Give five reason why U code was left aside (5mrks)
Show Answer
  1. Future Expansion: To accommodate new diseases that may be discovered in the future without disrupting the numbering of existing chapters.
  2. Provisional Assignment: For temporary assignment of new diseases of uncertain etiology (e.g., U04 for SARS, U07 for COVID-19).
  3. Research Purposes: To allow researchers to test alternative classifications or study specific temporary groups (U50-U99).
  4. Antibiotic Resistance: Used to code resistance to antimicrobial drugs (e.g., U80-U89).
  5. System Stability: Prevents the need to renumber the entire classification system whenever a significant new health issue emerges.
