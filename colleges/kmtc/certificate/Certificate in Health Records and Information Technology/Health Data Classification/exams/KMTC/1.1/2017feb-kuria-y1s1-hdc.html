Questions

Q1.

(a) Differentiate between the term Classification and Disease Classification (2 Marks)

(b) In the disease classification according to alphabetical index in ICD 10, the letter U was not assigned. Elaborate some of the reasons why the code U was left out. (3 Marks)

(c) Give at least 5 reasons as to why is important to have a uniform system of disease classification (5 Marks)

Show Answer

(a) Differentiation:

  • Classification: A system of categories to which entities are assigned according to established criteria. It is a method of generalization.
  • Disease Classification: A system of categories to which morbid entities (diseases, injuries, conditions) are assigned according to established criteria. It transforms medical terms into alphanumeric codes for statistical purposes.

(b) Reasons for Letter U:

  • Future Expansion: To allow for the addition of new chapters or diseases that were not known or classified at the time of publication.
  • Provisional Assignment: Used for temporary assignment of new diseases of uncertain etiology (e.g., U07.1 for COVID-19).
  • Research Purposes: Codes U50-U99 are often used in research projects to test alternative sub-classifications.

(c) Importance of Uniform System:

  1. Comparability: Enables comparison of morbidity and mortality data between different regions, countries, and time periods.
  2. Communication: Provides a common language for health professionals, reducing ambiguity in terminology.
  3. Statistical Analysis: Facilitates the aggregation of data for epidemiological studies and health trends analysis.
  4. Resource Allocation: Helps health administrators plan and allocate resources (staff, budget) based on standardized disease burden data.
  5. Retrieval: Makes it easier to index and retrieve medical records for research, education, and patient care.
  6. Reimbursement: Standardizes billing and insurance claims processing.
