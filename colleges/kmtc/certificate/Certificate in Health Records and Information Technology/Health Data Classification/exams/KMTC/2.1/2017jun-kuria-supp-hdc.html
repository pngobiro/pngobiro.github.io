Section 1: Multiple Choice Questions (20 Marks)

1.
Which one of the following is not a purpose of disease classification?
a. Systematic arrangement of health information
b. Management, Planning, Teaching and Research
c. Standardization and statistical analysis
d. None of the above
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d. None of the above

Explanation: Options A, B, and C are all valid purposes of disease classification. Therefore, none of them is the exception.

2.
Which of the following statements is not correct?
a. ICD seventh revision was held in Paris in the year 1955
b. Michael Huber was succeeded by Jacques Bertillon
c. The eighth ICD revision was held in Geneva in 1965
d. Initially the ICD development purpose was for epidemiological reasons
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d. Initially the ICD development purpose was for epidemiological reasons

Explanation: Initially, the ICD (International List of Causes of Death) was developed primarily for mortality statistics (causes of death). The extension to morbidity (epidemiology) occurred in later revisions.

3.
Which of the following statements is correct?
a. Aetiology refers to scientific study of causes of disease
b. The sixth decennial conference did not mark the beginning of the new era in the International vital health statistics
c. ICPM and ICD-9 were not developed in the same period
d. Codes U00-U99 are set aside for special purposes and additional codes
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a. Aetiology refers to scientific study of causes of disease

Explanation: This is the definition of aetiology. Option D is also technically true for ICD-10, but Option A is the most fundamental definition.

4.
Principal purposes of diagnostic index entails
a. Research, Planning, Teaching, Reporting
b. Planning, Reduction of work load, Filling, Retrieval
c. Research, Teaching, Retrieval, Reporting
d. Management, Research, Planning and M/E
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c. Research, Teaching, Retrieval, Reporting

Explanation: The diagnostic index is a retrieval tool used to locate records for research, education, and statistical reporting.

5.
Which one the following are not ICD-10 Conventions
a. Daggers and asterisk
b. Parentheses and square brackets
c. Colon and brace
d. None of the above
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d. None of the above

Explanation: All listed options (a, b, c) are valid ICD-10 conventions.

6.
Which one of the following statement is correct?
a. ICPM and ICD-9 were developed in the same period
b. Manifestation refers to scientific study of causes of diseases
c. Codes U00-U99 were not set aside for research and new additional codes diseases
d. None of the above
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a. ICPM and ICD-9 were developed in the same period

Explanation: ICD-9 was released in 1975, and ICPM (International Classification of Procedures in Medicine) in 1978.

7.
Which of the following statement is incorrect?
a. Common terminologies is characteristic of ICD-10
b. Sample of morbidity and mortality is one of the sections of volume I of ICD-10
c. Statistical presentation is not a section in Volume 2 of ICD-10
d. Mental and behavioural disorders is the 5th chapter of ISCD-10
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c. Statistical presentation is not a section in Volume 2 of ICD-10

Explanation: "Statistical presentation" IS a section in Volume 2. Therefore, the statement saying it is *not* a section is the incorrect one.

8.
Which of the following is not a factor for better disease classification system?
a. The need for required vital health statistics
b. The different types of hospitals
c. Comprehensive list of Morbidity and Mortality
d. The type of medical service emphasized (curative/preventive)
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b. The different types of hospitals

Explanation: A standard disease classification system is universal and not dependent on the type of hospital.

Indicate True or False
Show Answers
  1. "And" when included in rubric also means "or". TRUE
  2. John Grant was credited with the London Bill of Mortality. TRUE
  3. Jacques Bertillon eventually died in 1932... FALSE (1922).
