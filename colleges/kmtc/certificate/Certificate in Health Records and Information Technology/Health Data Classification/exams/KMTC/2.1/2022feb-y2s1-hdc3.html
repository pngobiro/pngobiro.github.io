Section 1: Multiple Choice Questions (20 Marks)

1.
The following are importance of data classification, except
a. It makes data easier to locate
b. It makes data easier to retrieve
c. It makes data duplication possible
d. It makes data more secure.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c. It makes data duplication possible

Explanation: Data classification aims to organize data to facilitate retrieval, security, and analysis. Enabling duplication is not a goal; in fact, classification often helps reduce redundancy.

2.
The current version of international classification of diseases used is?
a. ICD-10
b. Volume 3
c. ICD -11
d. Volume 1
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a. ICD-10 (or c. ICD-11 depending on adoption)

Explanation: At the time of this exam (2022), ICD-10 was the widely implemented standard in Kenya. ICD-11 was released by WHO in 2018 and came into effect in 2022, but full adoption takes years. In an academic context in 2022, ICD-10 was likely the intended answer for "currently used".

3.
The following are true about SNOMED, except one
a. It is a systematized nomenclature of medicine
b. It is a reference terminology in health care
c. It provides consistent way of capturing, sharing and aggregating health data.
d. It is international classification of diseases
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d. It is international classification of diseases

Explanation: SNOMED CT is a clinical terminology (nomenclature), whereas ICD is a classification system. They are distinct systems with different purposes (input vs. output).

4.
What is certification as used in health morbidity and mortality?
a. It is the underlying cause of death
b. It is the immediate cause of death
c. It is the recording of death
d. It is the confirmation of death
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d. It is the confirmation of death

Explanation: Certification involves a qualified medical practitioner confirming the fact and cause of death and issuing a certificate. (Note: Option 'c' implies registration).

5.
The following are true about health ethics, except.
a. It is professionalism
b. It is privacy
c. It is the standards and guidelines
d. It is the norms for operational
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b. It is privacy

Explanation: Privacy is a *concept* protected by ethics, but "Health Ethics" itself is the system of moral principles (standards, guidelines, professionalism) that govern conduct. Privacy is a result or a right, not the definition of ethics itself.

6.
The following are sources of data coding and indexing, except
a. Medical operations
b. Mortality
c. Symptoms
d. Prescriptions
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d. Prescriptions

Explanation: Coding is typically done from diagnoses (morbidity/mortality), procedures (operations), and symptoms recorded in patient notes. Prescriptions are usually handled by pharmacy systems (ATC codes), not standard disease coding/indexing processes in medical records.

7.
Historical development of ICD and ICPM discusses the statistical analyses of morbidity and mortality in health data classification
a. True
b. False
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a. True

Explanation: The history of classification systems is rooted in the need for statistical analysis of causes of death (mortality) and later illness (morbidity).

8.
The basic structure and principles of classification (according to William Farr) is grouped into 5 areas
i. Epidemic diseases
ii. Constitutional or general diseases
iii. Local diseases arranged by site
iv. Developmental diseases
v. Injuries
vi. Table of drugs and chemicals
Which ones are they?
a. i, ii, iii, iv and v
b. i, ii, iii, v, and vi
c. i, ii, iv, v and vi
d. All of the above
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a. i, ii, iii, iv and v

Explanation: William Farr's classification included: Epidemic, Constitutional, Local (by site), Developmental, and Violence/Injuries. The "Table of drugs and chemicals" is a section of ICD-10 Volume 3, not one of Farr's original groups.

9.
For one to accurately and correctly code a diagnosis or diagnostic statement the following must apply
a. Must have volume 2 and 1 only
b. Must have volume 2 and 3 only
c. Must have volumes 3, 1 and 2
d. Must have an index card
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c. Must have volumes 3, 1 and 2

Explanation: Proper coding requires Volume 3 (Index) to locate the term, Volume 1 (Tabular) to verify the code, and Volume 2 (Instruction Manual) for rules and guidelines.

10.
The 22 chapters of ICD-10 are divided in two groups; which ones are they?
a. Body systems and special groups of conditions
b. Special groups of conditions and body organs
c. Body organs and special organs
d. All the above
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a. Body systems and special groups of conditions

Explanation: ICD chapters generally fall into "Body Systems" (e.g., Respiratory, Circulatory) and "Special Groups" (e.g., Infectious diseases, Neoplasms, Injuries, External causes).

11.
The main elements to the structure of ICD-10 include the following except
a. Three volumes
b. 22 chapters
c. The structure of the code is alphanumeric
d. The blocks
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a. Three volumes

Explanation: While ICD-10 *has* three volumes, the question asks about the *structure* of the classification itself (Chapters, Blocks, Categories). "Three volumes" describes the physical publication format, not the hierarchical structure of the codes.

12.
Volume three of ICD-10 consists of.............
a. Section one
b. Section two
c. Section three
d. Sections a, b, c above
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d. Sections a, b, c above

Explanation: Volume 3 contains three sections: Section I (Alphabetical Index), Section II (External Causes), and Section III (Drugs and Chemicals).

13.
Which of the following statements are correct?
a. Asterisk describes either the patient's actual disease or pathological condition or the reason for seeking medical attention
b. Modifiers usually refer to varieties of the condition, or the anatomical sites affected
c. Parenthesis enclose explanatory words that follow a diagnostic term that affects the code number
d. A dagger indicates the current manifestation which is an optional code
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b. Modifiers usually refer to varieties of the condition, or the anatomical sites affected

Explanation: - A is false; Dagger describes the underlying disease. - C is false; Parentheses enclose *non-essential* modifiers that *do not* affect the code. - D is false; Asterisk indicates manifestation. - B is true; Essential modifiers affect the code selection.

14.
How many letters does chapter X use
a) 2
b) 1
c) 4
d) 3
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) 1

Explanation: Chapter X (Diseases of the respiratory system) uses codes J00-J99. It utilizes only one letter: **J**.

15.
The ICPM volume I consist of
a) Alphabetical list and Inclusion terms
b) Tabular list and inclusion terms
c) Alphabetical list and Tabular list
d) Alphabetical list, Tabular list and Inclusion terms
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Tabular list and inclusion terms

Explanation: Volume 1 of classification systems (like ICD/ICPM) typically contains the Tabular List (the codes) and their associated inclusion/exclusion terms. Volume 2 is usually instructions/index.

16.
Volume I of the ICD-10 contains all the following except
a) Morphology of neoplasms
b) Special tabulation list
c) Glossary
d) Nomenclature regulations
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Glossary

Explanation: ICD-10 Volume 1 contains the main classification, Morphology of Neoplasms, Special Tabulation Lists, and Nomenclature regulations. Glossaries (definitions of mental health disorders, etc.) are generally part of specific chapter notes or separate publications (like the "Blue Book" for Chapter V), not a general component of Vol 1 structure.

17.
___________ is a system of categories to which morbid entities are assigned according to established criteria.
a) Indexing
b) Coding
c) Classification
d) Tabulation
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Classification

Explanation: This is the standard definition of a statistical classification of diseases.

18.
Volume 2 of the ICD which is _________________ provides guidance to users of the ICD.
a. Instruction manual
b. Alphabetical index
c. Tabular list
d. None of the above
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a. Instruction manual

Explanation: Volume 1 = Tabular List; Volume 2 = Instruction Manual; Volume 3 = Alphabetical Index.

19.
While the dagger and asterisk system provide alternative classifications for the presentation of statistics, it is a principle of the ICD that the dagger code is the _______ code and must always be used
a. Manifestation code
b. Underlying Code
c. Tertiary code
d. All the above
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b. Underlying Code

Explanation: The Dagger (†) code represents the primary, underlying disease (etiology) and is mandatory. The Asterisk (*) code represents the manifestation and is optional/secondary.

20.
A ________ is used in listings of inclusion and exclusion terms when the words that precede it are not complete terms for assignment to that rubric.
a. Asterisk
b. Point dash
c. Brace
d. Colon
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d. Colon

Explanation: A colon (:) is used in lists of inclusion and exclusion terms when the words preceding it are not complete terms. One of the modifiers following the colon is required to make the assignment.

Section 2: Short Answer Questions (30 Marks)

1.
Give examples of reference classification of disease coding based on WHO family of international classifications. (5mks)
Show Answer
  1. ICD (International Classification of Diseases): For mortality and morbidity.
  2. ICF (International Classification of Functioning, Disability and Health): For measuring health and disability.
  3. ICHI (International Classification of Health Interventions): For health interventions (under development/implementation).

Note: These are the core "Reference Classifications". Other members include Derived (e.g., ICD-O) and Related (e.g., ICPC) classifications.

2.
State five uses of a death certificate (5mks)
Show Answer
  1. Legal Proof: Provides legal evidence of death for burial or cremation.
  2. Statistical Data: Source of mortality statistics and causes of death.
  3. Public Health: Identifying disease outbreaks and health priorities.
  4. Insurance Claims: Required for processing life insurance and benefits.
  5. Property/Inheritance: Necessary for settling estates and transferring property titles.
3.
Define the following terminologies as used in health care data (5mks)
Show Answer
  • a. Clinical coding: The translation of medical diagnoses, procedures, and other health problems into alphanumeric codes (e.g., ICD-10).
  • b. Classification: A system of categories to which entities (diseases) are assigned according to established criteria.
  • c. Maternal death: The death of a woman while pregnant or within 42 days of termination of pregnancy, from any cause related to or aggravated by the pregnancy or its management (not accidental).
  • d. Diagnostic indexing: The process of organizing and filing patient records based on disease codes to facilitate retrieval for research and statistics.
  • e. Nomenclature: A systematic listing of proper names for diseases or health conditions (e.g., SNOMED).
4.
Give the meaning of the following conventions as used in the ICD-10. (5mks)
Show Answer
  • a. Point dash (.-): Indicates that a fourth character exists and must be sought in the appropriate category to complete the code.
  • b. Colon (:): Used in inclusion/exclusion notes when preceding words are incomplete; requires a modifier from the list.
  • c. NOS: "Not Otherwise Specified" - implies unspecified information in the record.
  • d. Dagger (†): Indicates the primary underlying disease code (etiology).
  • e. NEC: "Not Elsewhere Classified" - indicates the condition is specific but no specific code exists in the system for it.
5.
List down five sources of mortality data (5mks)
Show Answer
  1. Death Certificates (Civil Registration System).
  2. Hospital Inpatient Records (Death registers).
  3. Police Records (for accidents/violence).
  4. Coroner/Post-mortem Reports.
  5. Census Data (for population denominators).
  6. Verbal Autopsy (in community settings).
6.
Outline five uses of coded data in the health facility (5mks)
Show Answer
  1. Retrieval: Facilitates easy retrieval of records for research and education.
  2. Reimbursement: Essential for billing insurance (e.g., NHIF) and claims processing.
  3. Statistics: compiling hospital activity reports (morbidity/mortality stats).
  4. Resource Allocation: Helps management plan based on disease burden (staffing, drugs).
  5. Quality Audit: Used to audit clinical care and outcomes for specific diseases.

Section 3: Long Answer Question (20 Marks)

1.

You are the new Health Records and Information in charge A of facility X. The hospital uses manual data processing.

a. Explain five reasons for the need for use of computers (10 marks)

b. Advise the hospital management on the factors to consider before installation of the computers (5 marks)

c. Give five hindrances that you may face as a HRIO in implementing the use of computers. (5 marks)

Show Answer

a. Reasons for Computerization (10 marks):

  1. Storage Capacity: Computers can store massive amounts of patient data in very little physical space compared to paper files.
  2. Retrieval Speed: Electronic records allow for instant retrieval of patient information, reducing waiting times.
  3. Data Accuracy: Built-in validation checks reduce errors in data entry (e.g., age validation).
  4. Data Sharing: Enables simultaneous access by multiple providers (doctor, lab, pharmacy) improving care coordination.
  5. Analysis & Reporting: Computers can automatically generate complex statistical reports, graphs, and trends for decision making.

b. Factors to Consider before Installation (5 marks):

  1. Cost: Budget for hardware, software, installation, and ongoing maintenance.
  2. Training Needs: The level of computer literacy of the staff and cost of training.
  3. Compatibility: Whether the new system can integrate with existing infrastructure or other systems.
  4. Security: Requirements for data protection, backups, and access controls.
  5. Vendor Support: Availability of technical support and updates from the software provider.

c. Hindrances in Implementation (5 marks):

  1. Resistance to Change: Staff may prefer manual methods and resist learning new technology.
  2. High Initial Cost: Expensive hardware and software acquisition.
  3. Technical Issues: Power outages, system crashes, or lack of internet connectivity.
  4. Lack of Skills: Staff may lack computer literacy requiring extensive training.
  5. Privacy Concerns: Fear of data breaches or unauthorized access to sensitive patient info.
