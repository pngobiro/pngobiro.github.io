A. Predictive statistics
B. Inferential statistics
C. Descriptive statistics

B. Tape recorders
C. Cameras
D. Questionnaire

Correct Answer: D. Questionnaire

4.
The method of obtaining sample from the population whereby the nth item is selected by a pre-determined interval is known as?
A. Quota sampling
B. Snow-balling sampling
C. Systematic random sampling
D. Stratified random sampling
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C. Systematic random sampling

Explanation: Systematic sampling involves selecting every $k^{th}$ (or $n^{th}$) element from a list after a random start.

5.
Which one of the following is a non-probability method of sampling?
A. Stratified random sampling
B. Systematic random sampling
C. Multi-stage sampling
D. Purposive sampling
Show Answer

Correct Answer: D. Purposive sampling

Explanation: Purposive sampling is a non-probability technique where the researcher selects subjects based on their own judgment. The others are probability methods.

6.
Errors in statistics can arise from the following sources except?
A. Sampling errors
B. Errors of origin
C. Errors of manipulation
D. Errors of inadequacy
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B. Errors of origin

Explanation: While "Error of origin" can technically exist (bias in source), standard statistical error classifications usually group them into Sampling errors and Non-sampling errors (which include adequacy and manipulation). However, "Errors of origin" is often the exception in this specific context of "Sources of Error".

11.
Which one of the following statements is incorrect about TOI?
A. TOI is negative when hospital beds are under-utilized
B. TOI is zero when bed compliment is 100% utilized
C. Short positive TOI is an indication that beds are optimally utilized
D. Long positive TOI is a clear indicator that the hospital should dispose beds
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A. TOI is negative when hospital beds are under-utilized

Explanation: TOI (Turnover Interval) becomes negative only when occupancy exceeds 100% (Over-utilization, where patient days > bed days). A positive TOI means the bed is empty for some time (under-utilization or idle time). Therefore, statement A is incorrect.

13.
Sturge’s formula states that
A. K= 1+ 3.332logn
B. K= 1+ 3.322logn
C. K= 1-5
D. K= 1+ 3.22logn
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A. K= 1+ 3.332logn (or B depending on precision)

Explanation: The standard formula is $k = 1 + 3.322 \log_{10} N$. Option A (3.332) or B (3.322) are close, but 3.322 is the exact value derived from $1/\log_{10}(2)$. B is the mathematically correct approximation.

17.
The mean of eight students is found to be 45, during calculation two students marks was wrongly captured as 20 and 26 instead of 2 and 62. Find the correct mean
A. 45
B. 47
C. 46
D. 47.25
Show Answer

Correct Answer: D. 47.25

Explanation:
Sum = $45 \times 8 = 360$.
Wrongly added: 20 + 26 = 46.
Correct values: 2 + 62 = 64.
Difference: 64 - 46 = +18.
New Sum = 360 + 18 = 378.
New Mean = $378 / 8 = 47.25$.

43.
XYZ hospital had a bed state of 12 in the month of January to March 2022. It was reported that the hospital had 960 inpatients in the same period and an average length of stay of 3 days. Calculate:

i. Discharges and deaths (2 marks)

ii. Turn-over interval (1 mark)

iii. Interpret the turn over interval for XYZ Hospital (2 marks)

Show Answer

Calculations:

Period = Jan-Mar (90 days). Bed State = 12.

Available Bed Days = $12 \times 90 = 1080$.

Total Patient Days = $960 \text{ patients} \times 3 \text{ days (ALOS)} = 2880$ days.

i. Discharges and Deaths:
Since ALOS = Patient Days / Discharges, Discharges = Patient Days / ALOS. But we are given "960 inpatients" and ALOS 3. If 960 is the number of discharges, then Discharges = 960.

ii. Turnover Interval (TOI):
Formula: $\frac{\text{Available Bed Days} - \text{Patient Days}}{\text{Discharges}}$
TOI = $\frac{1080 - 2880}{960} = \frac{-1800}{960} = -1.875$ days.

iii. Interpretation:
A negative Turnover Interval indicates that the hospital is severely overcrowded (Occupancy > 100%). Patient days exceed the available bed capacity, possibly due to extra beds being added temporarily or sharing of beds.

46.
A train moved at a speed of 63km/h for 2 hours to point A, 96km/h for 4 hours to point B, 106km/h for 3 hours to point C, and 60km/h for 3 hours to point D. Calculate the average speed from point A to D using harmonic mean (5 marks)
Show Answer

Note on Method: The question asks for "Harmonic Mean". However, for average speed with different times/distances, the weighted arithmetic mean (Total Distance / Total Time) is standard. If strictly applying weighted harmonic mean formula:

Total Distance = $(63 \times 2) + (96 \times 4) + (106 \times 3) + (60 \times 3) = 126 + 384 + 318 + 180 = 1008$ km.

Total Time = $2 + 4 + 3 + 3 = 12$ hours.

Average Speed = $1008 / 12 = 84$ km/h.

Step 1: Create Frequency Table (Midpoints $x$, $fx$, $cf$)

  • 0-20: x=10, f=4, cf=4
  • 20-40: x=30, f=10, cf=14
  • 40-50: x=45, f=8, cf=22

ii. Mean Weight:

i. Median Weight:
Position = $N/2 = 31$. Class is 50-60.
$L = 50, cf = 22, f = 15, i = 10$.
