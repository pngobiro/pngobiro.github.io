Section 1: Multiple Choice Questions (20 Marks)

1.
The following are types of statistics except?
a. Discretion statistics
b. Inferential statistics
c. Descriptive statistics
d. Correlational statistics
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a. Discretion statistics

Explanation: The two main branches of statistics are Descriptive and Inferential. "Discretion statistics" is not a standard term.

2.
What is datum?
a. Statistical analysis process
b. Singular of data
c. Plural of data
d. Processed data
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b. Singular of data

Explanation: 'Data' is the plural form of the Latin word 'datum'.

3.
When you compile data using the central bureau of statistics sources, you are using
a. Primary data
b. Scientific information
c. Secondary data
d. Hospital data
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c. Secondary data

Explanation: Data collected by one entity (CBS) and used by another is considered secondary data.

4.
Data is divided into two main categories
a. Discrete and continuous
b. Nominal and ordinal
c. Independent and dependent
d. Qualitative and quantitative
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d. Qualitative and quantitative

Explanation: The broadest classification of data is into Qualitative (categorical) and Quantitative (numerical).

5.
Which graph is the best to show growth of a baby in terms of height and weight?
a. Histogram
b. Bar graph
c. Line graph
d. Pie chart
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c. Line graph

Explanation: Line graphs are used to track changes over short and long periods of time, making them ideal for growth charts.

6.
The following are principles of constructing a table except?
a. Table should have rows and columns
b. Table should vary y axis
c. Table should have a title
d. Table should be clear
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b. Table should vary y axis

Explanation: Tables do not have axes (x and y); graphs do. Therefore, varying the y-axis is not a principle of table construction.

7.
Which of the following is a stage in a statistical process?
a. Data pruning
b. Data correcting
c. Data registration
d. Data interpretation
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d. Data interpretation

Explanation: The standard stages are Collection, Organization, Presentation, Analysis, and Interpretation.

8.
The following symbol in statistics means? (Symbol not shown, inferred from options)
a. Equal to
b. Approximately equal
c. Round off
d. Round up
Show Answer

Likely Answer: b. Approximately equal

Explanation: Symbols like $\approx$ are common in basic stats questions to denote approximation.

9.
Which of the following is not a source of health data
a. Published commercial surveys
b. Civil registration data
c. Census
d. Hospital medical records department
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a. Published commercial surveys

Explanation: While surveys provide data, commercial surveys are less likely to be standard sources of public *health* data compared to Census, Civil Registration, and Hospital records.

10.
The interval between two successive patients (A discharge and admission to a bed)
a. ALOS
b. TOI
c. TOB
d. VBD
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b. TOI (Turnover Interval)

Explanation: Turnover Interval is the average time a bed remains empty between the discharge of one patient and the admission of the next.

11.
Which of the following is an absolute measure of dispersion?
a. Range
b. Variance
c. Standard deviation
d. Percentile
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a. Range (and b, c)

Explanation: Range, Variance, and Standard Deviation are all absolute measures of dispersion. Percentile is a measure of position. In exam contexts, Range is often cited as the simplest absolute measure.

12.
Which of the following divides data into four subgroups?
a. Percentile
b. Quartile
c. Decile
d. Standard deviation
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b. Quartile

Explanation: Quartiles divide a data set into four equal parts.

13.
Primary data is also ___________ data
a. Ungrouped
b. Grouped
c. Secondary
d. None of the above
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a. Ungrouped

Explanation: Primary data, when first collected and not yet organized into frequency distributions, is referred to as raw or ungrouped data.

14.
A questionnaire is used to collect?
a. Secondary data
b. Primary data
c. Organized data
d. None of the above
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b. Primary data

Explanation: Questionnaires are a tool for collecting original data directly from respondents (primary source).

15.
A sample in statistics means
a. All the items/subjects being investigated
b. A portion of the sample
c. A portion of the population
d. All the above
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c. A portion of the population

Explanation: A sample is a subset or portion of the entire population selected for study.

16.
Which of the following is a term refers to inverse in statistics
a. Harmonic mean
b. Geometric mean
c. Arithmetic mean
d. Mode
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a. Harmonic mean

Explanation: The harmonic mean is calculated as the reciprocal (inverse) of the arithmetic mean of the reciprocals of the data.

17.
CBR in vital statistics means
a. Centre of bureaucracy
b. Crude death rate
c. Crude birth rate
d. Crude birth ratio
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c. Crude birth rate

18.
ASFR in vital statistics means?
a. Age specified fecundity rate
b. Age specific fertility rate
c. Age specific fatality rate
d. Age specified fertility ratio
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b. Age specific fertility rate

19.
The difference between a value and the arithmetic mean distribution is
a. Mean deviation
b. Standard deviation
c. Variance
d. None of the above
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d. None of the above (Technically just "Deviation")

Explanation: The difference ($x - \bar{x}$) is simply the deviation from the mean. Mean deviation is the average of these differences.

20.
What is the denominator in measuring age specific fertility rate
a. Total number of life births
b. Total number of women in that age group
c. Mid year population
d. Total population in a year

Correct Answer: b. Total number of women in that age group

Explanation: ASFR = (Births to women of age X / Total women of age X) * 1000.

Section 4: Calculations & Essay Questions (40 Marks)

1.

In the year 2018, a hospital in Kuria district had 70 allocated beds, a total of 37,162 inpatient days although 112 patients had absconded, 88 died and 12,121 recovered and were discharged. Calculate: (20 Marks)

  1. The percentage occupancy
  2. Excess patients/vacant bed days
  3. Average length of stay
  4. Total number of beds required to accommodate all the patients
Show Answer

Data Given:

  • Beds = 70
  • Total Inpatient Days = 37,162
  • Discharges (Alive) = 12,121
  • Deaths = 88
  • Absconded = 112
  • Total Discharges (Events) = 12,121 + 88 + 112 = 12,321
  • Days in Year = 365 (2018)

a. Percentage Occupancy:

Formula: $\frac{\text{Total Patient Days}}{\text{Available Bed Days}} \times 100$

Calculation: $\frac{37,162}{70 \times 365} \times 100 = \frac{37,162}{25,550} \times 100 = 145.45\%$

b. Excess patients/vacant bed days:

Since occupancy > 100%, there are excess patient days.

Excess = Actual Days - Available Days = $37,162 - 25,550 = 11,612 \text{ excess days}$

c. Average Length of Stay (ALOS):

Formula: $\frac{\text{Total Inpatient Days}}{\text{Total Discharges (including deaths)}}$

Calculation: $\frac{37,162}{12,321} = 3.02 \text{ days}$

d. Beds Required:

Formula: $\frac{\text{Average Daily Census}}{\text{Target Occupancy (assume 100%)}}$

Average Daily Census = $\frac{37,162}{365} = 101.8$

Required Beds = 102 beds (approx).
