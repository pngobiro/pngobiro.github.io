CERTIFICATE 19th June 2018

Health Records Management

Certificate Final Qualifying Examination (Sept 2016 Class)

Section 1: Multiple Choice Questions (20 Marks)

1.
The following special health records are initiated and handled differently from other records except?
a. Tuberculosis records
b. Psychiatric records
c. Sexually transmitted diseases
d. None of the above
Show Answer

Answer: d. None of the above

All listed records (TB, Psychiatric, STDs) are classified as "Special Health Records" requiring stricter confidentiality measures and often separate filing/registers due to social stigma or legal requirements.

2.
The following are health records indices except?
a. Waiting list index
b. Surgical index
c. Operations index
d. Patient master index
Show Answer

Answer: a. Waiting list index

A "Waiting list" is a scheduling tool/register for future admissions. It is not a standard retrieval index (like Disease, Operation, or Master Patient Index) used to locate past records for research.

3.
There are three (3) major types of health records, which one is not
a. X-ray records
b. Case records
c. Outpatient record
d. Diagnostic records
Show Answer

Answer: a. X-ray records

X-ray records fall under the category of Diagnostic records. The three major functional categories are typically Inpatient (Case), Outpatient, and Diagnostic/Departmental.

4.
Which one of the following is not ideal for filing case records
a. Cabinets
b. Shelving unit (static)
c. Strip index
d. Mobile racks
Show Answer

Answer: c. Strip index

Strip indexes (like Kalamazoo) are used for listing names (Indexing), not for the physical storage (Filing) of bulky patient folders.

5.
Which one of the following should be included in the unit number register
a. Address
b. Date of birth
c. Registration date
d. All of the above
Show Answer

Answer: d. All of the above

The Unit Number Register (or Master Patient Index) must contain sufficient demographic data (Name, DOB, Address, Date Reg) to positively identify the patient and avoid duplication.

6.
The following are sources of appointment except?
a. Patient communicating personally to make appointment
b. Patient already discharged coming for follow-up
c. Physician making a call for booking of his/her patient
d. Hospital administrator requesting appointment for a patient from private clinic
Show Answer

Answer: d. Hospital administrator requesting appointment for a patient from private clinic

Appointments are clinical scheduling activities initiated by patients or clinicians. Administrators managing facility operations do not typically book individual clinical appointments for external patients.

7.
The department of health records was started in Kenyatta National Hospital in the year
a. 1978
b. 1967
c. 1948
d. 1990
Show Answer

Answer: b. 1967

The formal Medical Records Department at KNH was established around 1967/1968, marking the beginning of professional records management in Kenya.

8.
In the unit medical records system, the unit is the
a. Master index
b. Record
c. Patient
d. Folder
Show Answer

Answer: c. Patient

The system is patient-centric; the "Unit" refers to the patient being treated as a single entity with one comprehensive record.

9.
Which one of the following is not an ancillary equipment in a records library
a. Filing cabinets
b. Kick-stool
c. Sorting equipment
d. Filing trolley
Show Answer

Answer: a. Filing cabinets

Filing cabinets are Primary Storage Equipment. Ancillary equipment refers to support tools like trolleys and stools used to access or process the files.

10.
Which act gives guidelines on the completion of P3 forms
a. Public health act
b. Criminal procedure code
c. Civil registration act
d. Public records act
Show Answer

Answer: b. Criminal procedure code

P3 Forms (Medical Examination Reports) are evidentiary documents used in police cases, governed by the Criminal Procedure Code and Evidence Act.

11.
The following information should be collected during registration except?
a. Marital Status
b. Age
c. Diagnosis
d. Patient's full names
Show Answer

Answer: c. Diagnosis

Registration collects Demographic/Social Data. Diagnosis is Clinical Data arrived at after the patient has been registered and examined by a doctor.

12.
Duplication of unit numbers can be prevented by doing the following except?
a. Not giving the same number to two patients
b. Not giving the same patient more than one number
c. Pre-printing of the unit folder in advance
d. Maintaining the unit number register
Show Answer

Answer: c. Pre-printing of the unit folder in advance

Merely pre-printing folders does not prevent a clerk from accidentally assigning a new folder to an existing patient. Checking the MPI and registers (A, B, D) are the active prevention measures.

13.
The records initiated for patients who get admitted into the wards are called?
a. Case records
b. Outpatient records
c. Diagnostic records
d. Hospital records
Show Answer

Answer: a. Case records

Traditionally, "Case Records" refers specifically to the inpatient file containing the history of the admission "case".

14.
The Outpatient appointment list should contain the following except?
a. Hospital number
b. Source of request
c. Date of admission
d. Patient's identity.
Show Answer

Answer: c. Date of admission

An Outpatient list tracks clinic visits. "Date of Admission" is relevant to Inpatient waiting lists or ward registers, not outpatient clinic lists.

15.
The following are purposes of scheduling and follow up, except
a. To facilitate teaching arrangement
b. To reduce workload
c. To enable even distribution of patients in various clinics
d. To reduce patient waiting time
Show Answer

Answer: b. To reduce workload

Scheduling regulates or distributes workload evenly, preventing peaks and troughs, but it does not inherently reduce the total amount of work (patients) to be seen.

16.
In the year ____ , the American hospital Association discussed health records for the first time at a convention
a. 1948
b. 1902
c. 1967
d. 1903
Show Answer

Answer: b. 1902

Historical records indicate early discussions by the AHA regarding standardization of hospital records occurred around 1902-1905.

17.
The tracer system in which the tracer card has a small pocket inside the cover in which a small card is held is called ____
a. Personal tracer card
b. Common tracer card
c. Library tracer card
d. Patient tracer card
Show Answer

Answer: c. Library tracer card

Also sometimes referred to as a "pocket tracer," it holds the requisition slip so the filer knows where the file went without writing on the tracer itself.

18.
Which one of the following is not a method of destroying paper records?
a. Burning
b. Pulverizing
c. Degaussing
d. Pulping
Show Answer

Answer: c. Degaussing

Degaussing is the process of eliminating data from magnetic media (hard drives, tapes). Burning, Pulverizing, and Pulping are methods for destroying paper.

19.
The following are sources from which a patient is admitted, which one is not?
a. From home
b. From A and E department
c. In-patient waiting list
d. From other hospitals
Show Answer

Answer: a. From home

A patient cannot admit themselves directly from home. They must be processed through a source like A&E (Emergency), a Clinic (Outpatient), or a Waiting List (Planned).

20.
Which one of the following departments has no working relationship with health records department
a. Transport department
b. Accounts department
c. Maintenance department
d. None of the above
Show Answer

Answer: d. None of the above

Health Records interacts with ALL departments: Transport (moving files), Accounts (billing data), Maintenance (office repairs/shelving). Therefore, "None of the above" is correct as there is no department listed that has no relationship.

Section 2: True or False (20 Marks)

1. Bound volumes, box files, shelve filing are all peripheral filing equipment
Show Answer

False: They are types of storage/filing methods or primary equipment, not "peripheral" (ancillary) equipment.

2. Editing is the objective method of applying yardstick to the quality of care given
Show Answer

False: Editing is the assembly/organization of the file. Medical Audit or Quality Assurance is the yardstick for quality of care.

3. It makes sense to provide a kick-stool where cabinets are used for filing of case records
Show Answer

False: Filing cabinets are usually low enough to access without stools. Stools are essential for Shelf Filing which goes much higher.

4. Patient master index is the numerical key to alphabetically filed case records
Show Answer

False: It is the Alphabetical Key to Numerically filed records.

5. One disadvantage of chronological filing system is that there is growth at one end
Show Answer

True: In chronological/serial filing, all new records are added to the end, creating congestion (hotspots) in one area of the library.

6. You can get names of all the patients who have ever attended a health facility from patient master index
Show Answer

True: The MPI is the permanent list of every patient ever registered.

7. Planning is one of functions of health records department
Show Answer

True: The department plans for retention, space, staffing, and information needs.

8. Most waiting lists are initiated in the wards
Show Answer

False: They are usually initiated in Outpatient Clinics where the decision to admit is made.

9. Scheduling is the same as patient's appointment
Show Answer

True: Conceptually, they refer to the same process of booking a time for service.

10. Admission of psychiatric patients can be formal or informal
Show Answer

True: Under the Mental Health Act, patients can be admitted voluntarily (informal) or involuntarily (formal/sectioned).

11. Clinical research is considered a primary purpose of health records
Show Answer

False: It is a Secondary purpose. Primary is patient care.

12. The medical records department at Kenyatta National Hospital was first established in 1957
Show Answer

False: As per Question 7, it was established around 1967.

13. Before Kenya attained independence, only death of Africans were registered
Show Answer

False: Typically, colonial registration systems prioritized Europeans/Asians. Registration of Africans was often incomplete or non-existent in rural areas.

14. The secondary purpose of keeping Health Records in a hospital is for planning of health care services
Show Answer

True: Administrative planning, research, and education are secondary uses.

15. The EMR systems main advantage is to ensure non duplication of patient information
Show Answer

True: A key feature of EMR is the ability to search effectively and enforce unique identifiers.

16. A list is synonymous to Index
Show Answer

False: A list acts as a register/log. An Index points to a location (like a catalogue) to facilitate retrieval.

17. Allocation of filing activities can be evenly distributed when the terminal digit filing sequence is in operation
Show Answer

True: TDF spreads activity across 100 sections, allowing efficient staff allocation.

18. For a file already outside a filing area, a common tracer card will show its whereabouts when you check in its usual filling space in the filing area
Show Answer

True: That is the exact function of a tracer card.

19. A unit system consists of records relating to several patients
Show Answer

False: A Unit system consolidates records for ONE patient.

20. Patients' attendance cards should be well kept in cabinets to ensure confidentiality
Show Answer

True: All patient records, including attendance cards, are confidential.

Section 4: Essay Questions (40 Marks)

1.

(a) Discuss instances where health records can act as evidence (10 marks)

(b) Explain the personal attributes and qualities that health records personnel should possess while carrying out their duties (10 marks)

Show Answer

Answer (a): Evidence Instances

  • Personal Injury Claims: Documentation of injuries from accidents to support compensation claims.
  • Medical Negligence: Proof of standard of care provided in malpractice suits.
  • Workmen's Compensation: Determining degree of disability for workplace injuries.
  • Criminal Cases: Evidence in cases of assault, rape, or murder (P3 forms).
  • Insurance Claims: Proof of treatment for reimbursement.
  • Testamentary Capacity: Proving mental state during the signing of a will.
  • Custody Cases: Evidence of mental stability in child custody disputes.

Answer (b): Attributes of HRIO

  • Integrity/Honesty: Maintaining trust and confidentiality.
  • Discretion: Knowing what to say and to whom (confidentiality).
  • Accuracy/Attention to Detail: Preventing errors in filing and data entry.
  • Patience/Politeness: Dealing with difficult patients/staff effectively.
  • Adaptability: Adjusting to new technologies and workflows.
  • Team Player: Working well with clinical and admin staff.
  • Observant: Noticing errors or missing documents.
2.

(a) Explain the various functions of Electronic Health Records (10 marks)

(b) State the merits and demerits of a decentralized waiting list (10 marks)

Show Answer

Answer (a): Functions of EHR

  • Patient Management: Registration, admission, discharge, and transfer (ADT).
  • Clinical Documentation: Electronic storage of notes, history, and physicals.
  • Order Entry (CPOE): Ordering labs, radiology, and prescriptions electronically.
  • Results Viewing: Instant access to test results.
  • Decision Support: Drug-drug interaction alerts, reminders for screenings.
  • Reporting: Generating statistical reports automatically.
  • Communication: Secure messaging between providers.

Answer (b): Decentralized Waiting List

Merits:

  • Better relationship between patient and clinic staff (personal touch).
  • Clinic staff are more familiar with specific consultant requirements.
  • Less bureaucracy; quicker decisions within the department.
  • Specialty-specific management of priorities.

Demerits:

  • Duplication: Patients might be on multiple lists in different clinics.
  • Inefficiency: More staff required (one per clinic) compared to a central office.
  • Lack of Standardization: Different clinics may use different rules for prioritization.
  • Bed Mismanagement: Harder to see the total hospital demand for beds.
