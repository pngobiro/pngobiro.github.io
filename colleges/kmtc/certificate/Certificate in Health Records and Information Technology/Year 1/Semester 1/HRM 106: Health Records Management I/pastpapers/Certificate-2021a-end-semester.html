Section 1: Multiple Choice Questions (20 Marks)

1.
Which of the following is not a medical record
a) Cardiography notes
b) X-ray films
c) Immunization records
d) Encephalogram records
Show Answer

Answer: b) X-ray films

While part of the patient's history, X-ray films are technically Diagnostic Images kept in Radiology, whereas notes and charts are the "Medical Record" proper. In many contexts, they are distinct from the paper file.

2.
The health records association was started in
a) Egypt
b) USA
c) Greece
d) Britain
Show Answer

Answer: b) USA

The first association (AHA/AMRA) was formed in the USA around 1928.

3.
The following are secondary uses of health records except one.
a) Administrative
b) Patient care
c) Research
d) Medical legal
Show Answer

Answer: b) Patient care

Patient Care is the Primary use. All others are secondary.

4.
The HRIO Quality of using own knowledge to make decisions is.

Answer: d) Discretion

Discretion (or Judgment) allows an officer to make appropriate decisions based on knowledge.

5.

6.
____ is the act of bringing together files of one patient which were created different times in the same facility.

Amalgamation (merging) combines multiple files into one Unit Record.

10.
Prevention of duplication in health records unit system can be done through the following except.
a) By not giving the same number to two patients
b) By not giving the same patients more than one number

13.

14.

Section 2: Short Answer Questions (30 Marks)

1. After retention period is completed; health records that are not subjects to permanent preservation are destroyed. Discuss the methods of records destruction (10MKS)
Show Answer

Answer:

  • Shredding: Cutting paper into unreadable strips. Best for confidentiality.
  • Incineration (Burning): Burning records to ash. Effective but has environmental concerns.
  • Pulping: Reducing paper to pulp using water/chemicals. Recyclable and secure.
  • Pulverizing: Grinding records into dust.
  • Degaussing/Wiping: For electronic media.
2. Explain 4 ways security for health records should be maintained ( 4 MKS )
Show Answer

Answer:

  • Lockable filing cabinets/rooms.
  • Restricted access (Staff only).
  • Sign-out registers (Tracking).
  • Fire/Flood protection (Safe environment).
3. Discuss any 5 committees related to procurement of health records equipments ( 10 MKS )
Show Answer

Answer:

  • User Committee: Identifies needs.
  • Tender Committee: Adjudicates bids.
  • Inspection and Acceptance Committee: Verifies goods upon arrival.
  • Procurement Committee: Oversees process.
  • Standardization Committee: Ensures compatibility.
4. Recognize 6 categories of special health records (6MKS)
Show Answer

Answer:

  • Psychiatric Records.
  • HIV/AIDS Records.
  • Staff/Personnel Health Records.
  • Obstetric/Maternity Records.
  • Adoption Records.
  • Genetic Testing Records.

Section 3: Long Answer Questions (20 Marks)

1. There should be an interaction of 3 people in the planning of establishment of a new hospital health records department. Discuss (10Mks)
Show Answer

Answer:

  • The Administrator: Provides policy direction, budget approval, and defines the scope of services.
  • The Architect: Translates needs into physical design, structure, safety features, and layout.
  • The Health Records Officer: Provides technical expertise on workflow, storage requirements, filing systems, and equipment needs.
2. Interpret the basic principles in designing and developing health records forms (10MKS)
Show Answer

Answer:

  • Necessity: Does the form serve a distinct purpose?
  • Standardization: Paper size (A4), fonts, and headers.
  • Simplicity: Clear instructions and clutter-free design.
  • Sequence: Logical flow of data entry (e.g., demographic -> clinical).
  • Identification: Unique form number and title.
  • Spacing: Adequate room for written responses.
