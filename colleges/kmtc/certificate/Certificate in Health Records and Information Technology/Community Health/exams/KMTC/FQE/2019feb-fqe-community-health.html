Section 1: Multiple Choice Questions (20 Marks)

1.
Community empowerment takes place at various levels EXCEPT
a. Individual
b. Community
c. Health facility
d. Organizational
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c. Health facility

Explanation: Empowerment typically focuses on the Individual, the Organization (groups), and the Community levels. "Health facility" is a location/institution, not a level of *community* empowerment in standard frameworks.

2.
The following are health care delivery indicators EXCEPT
a. Doctor/population ratio
b. Clinical survey
c. Doctor/nurse ratio
d. Population/Bed ratio
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b. Clinical survey

Explanation: A clinical survey is a method of data collection, whereas ratios (Doctor/Pop, Bed/Pop) are statistical indicators used to measure health care delivery capacity.

3.
Primary health care does the following EXCEPT
a. It is intended to reach everybody
b. It is limited to health facilities
c. It is intended to reach homes and family level
d. All of the above
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b. It is limited to health facilities

Explanation: A core principle of PHC is that it extends beyond the hospital walls into the community and homes. It is NOT limited to facilities.

4.
The period between infection and first appearance of symptoms and signs is referred to as
a. Clinical infection of disease
b. Carriers period
c. Widow period
d. Incubation period
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d. Incubation period

Explanation: The incubation period is the time elapsed between exposure to a pathogenic organism and when symptoms and signs are first apparent.

5.
HIV/AIDS can be transmitted from one person to another through all of the following EXCEPT
a. Unprotected vaginal and anal intercourse with an infected person
b. Infected mother passing it to her child during pregnancy, child birth or breastfeeding
c. Infection with uncontaminated needles when health workers are involved in needles prick accidents
d. Use of contaminated surgical instruments e.g. during traditional circumcision
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c. Infection with uncontaminated needles...

Explanation: "Uncontaminated" means clean/sterile. You cannot transmit HIV with a needle that does not contain the virus (uncontaminated), even if there is a prick injury (unless the person pricked is the source, but the question implies transmission *to* the worker from the needle).

6.
Worn out clothes, waste paper and food leftovers are examples of ..............waste
a. Hospital
b. Industrial
c. Domestic
d. Street
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c. Domestic

Explanation: These are typical examples of household or domestic waste.

7.
Three of the following diseases are communicable diseases EXCEPT (Likely meaning: Which is the exception to the group pattern?)
a. Kalazar
b. Coronary heart disease
c. Asthma
d. Cervical cancer
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a. Kalazar

Explanation: This question contains a logical error in its phrasing. B, C, and D are Non-Communicable Diseases (NCDs). A (Kalazar/Leishmaniasis) is a Communicable Disease. Therefore, Kalazar is the "odd one out" or the exception to the group of NCDs.

8.
Which of the following best explains a family with a husband and a wife without children?
a. Extended family
b. Modern family
c. Nuclear family
d. Exotic family
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c. Nuclear family

Explanation: A nuclear family (conjugal family) consists of a married couple and their children, or a married couple without children.

9.
Which of the following statements is NOT true about Bamako initiative?
a. It was adopted by African health ministers in 1987 in Mali
b. It targeted drug revolving fund as an income generating activity
c. It was meant for training community health workers on health data collection and management
d. Meant to implement strategies designed to increase availability of essential drugs and other health care services
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b. It targeted drug revolving fund as an income generating activity

Explanation: The Bamako Initiative focused on *cost-recovery* and community financing to ensure sustainability and availability of drugs, not profit-making ("income generating activity") in the commercial sense.

10.
The following are processes of community entry EXCEPT
a. Knock and enter upon response
b. First greet the elders and then introduce yourself
c. Inform them on your mission
d. Use the authority from the armed forces to state your mission e.g. introduction of new health care programs
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d. Use the authority from the armed forces...

Explanation: Community entry relies on building trust, respect, and rapport with community leaders. Using armed force or authority is coercive and contrary to community health principles.

11.
Which of the following diseases is spread by droplets?
a. Liver cirrhosis and TB
b. Polio and measles
c. TB and malaria
d. All of the above
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b. Polio and measles

Explanation: - TB is airborne (droplet nuclei). - Measles is airborne/droplet. - Polio is transmitted via the fecal-oral route, but can also be transmitted via pharyngeal secretions (droplets) in the early stage. - Malaria is vector-borne. Cirrhosis is not infectious. - Option B contains two diseases that *can* be transmitted via droplets/secretions, making it the best fit among flawed options.

12.
MDGs were developed in the year
a. 2002
b. 2014
c. 2015
d. 2000
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d. 2000

Explanation: The Millennium Development Goals (MDGs) were established following the Millennium Summit of the United Nations in 2000.

13.
Immunization is a form of
a. Secondary prevention
b. Primary prevention
c. Tertiary prevention
d. None of the above
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b. Primary prevention

Explanation: Primary prevention aims to prevent disease or injury before it ever occurs. Vaccination is the classic example.

14.
Which of the following is NOT a disability indicator?
a. Discharge and admission rates
b. Bed disability days
c. Expectation of life free of disability
d. Number of days of restricted activity
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a. Discharge and admission rates

Explanation: Admission/Discharge rates are indicators of health service utilization, not disability status. Bed-disability days and restricted-activity days are Sullivan's indices for disability.

15.
A susceptible person can be protected through all of the following EXCEPT
a. Chemoprophylaxis
b. Personal protection
c. Immunization
d. Better nutrition
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d. Better nutrition

Explanation: While better nutrition improves general resistance, Immunization, Chemoprophylaxis (drugs), and Personal protection (PPE/Barriers) are specific protection measures against specific agents.

16.
Which of the following is considered to be live attenuated vaccine?
a. BCG
b. Measles
c. Diphtheria
d. Polio
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c. Diphtheria

Explanation: The question likely asks which is the EXCEPTION (not live). BCG, Measles, and Oral Polio Vaccine (OPV) are live attenuated. Diphtheria vaccine is a Toxoid (inactivated toxin). If the question is strictly "Which IS live", there are three correct answers (A, B, D). Assuming it asks for the exception: C.

17.
The following are natural family planning methods EXCEPT
a. Cervical cap
b. Safe periods
c. Cervical mucous
d. Coitus interruptus
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a. Cervical cap

Explanation: A cervical cap is a barrier method (device), not a natural method.

18.
While managing communicable diseases we must do all the following EXCEPT
a. Attack the source of the infection
b. Protect sustainable host
c. Interrupt the route of transmission
d. Isolate to avoid infection
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d. Isolate to avoid infection

Explanation: The general principles are: 1. Control reservoir/source, 2. Interrupt transmission, 3. Protect the susceptible host. Isolation is a *method* of controlling the source or interrupting transmission, not a separate principle "to avoid infection" in the general sense. Also, we usually isolate the *infected* person, not the general population "to avoid infection" (unless referring to reverse isolation).

19.
Which of the following is not true about maternal child health and family planning?
a. All pregnant women are attended at the clinic
b. Treatment of minor ailment in children below 5 years
c. Adolescence and counselling services are available
d. Those accompanying pregnant women should remain at the reception
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d. Those accompanying pregnant women should remain at the reception

Explanation: MCH encourages male involvement and family support; keeping companions at reception is contrary to modern MCH practices.

20.
Which of the following PHC elements was added by the Kenyan government?
a. Dental health
b. Community based rehabilitation
c. Mental health
d. Essential drug supply
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c. Mental health

Explanation: Mental health (and Dental health) were additions to the original 8 Alma Ata elements in the Kenyan context, with Mental Health often cited as the 9th element.

Section 4: Essay Questions (40 Marks)

1.
State all the elements of Primary Health Care (14 Marks)
Show Answer
  1. Education concerning prevailing health problems and the methods of preventing and controlling them.
  2. Promotion of food supply and proper nutrition.
  3. Adequate supply of safe water and basic sanitation.
  4. Maternal and child health care, including family planning.
  5. Immunization against the major infectious diseases.
  6. Prevention and control of locally endemic diseases.
  7. Appropriate treatment of common diseases and injuries.
  8. Provision of essential drugs.
  9. (Additional for Kenya): Mental Health.
  10. (Additional for Kenya): Dental Health.
2.
State six reasons why health records and information students should study community health (6 Marks)
Show Answer
  • To understand the burden of disease in the catchment area.
  • To manage community-based health information systems (CBHIS).
  • To assist in epidemiological surveillance and reporting.
  • To facilitate better planning and resource allocation.
  • To improve communication with community health workers.
  • To understand the social determinants of health affecting data quality.
3.
Community diagnosis is a very informative subject in community health. Discuss the stages of community diagnosis (20 Marks)
Show Answer

1. Initiation/Preparation: Recognizing the need, defining objectives, selecting the team.

2. Data Collection: Using methods like surveys, focus group discussions, key informant interviews to gather demographic, epidemiological, and socio-economic data.

3. Data Analysis: Processing and organizing the data to identify patterns and trends.

4. Problem Identification: Identifying the health problems and needs of the community based on the analysis.

5. Prioritization: Ranking the problems based on criteria like magnitude, severity, feasibility of intervention, and community concern.

6. Dissemination: Sharing the findings with the community and stakeholders.

7. Action Plan: Developing a plan to address the prioritized problems.
