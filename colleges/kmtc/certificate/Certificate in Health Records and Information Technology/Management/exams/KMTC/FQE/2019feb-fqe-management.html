Section 1: Multiple Choice Questions (20 Marks)

1.
Which one of the following is not the best description of personnel management?
a. Obtaining and retaining employees in an organization
b. Employing and giving the workers conducive working environment
c. Employing and retaining the staff in the organization
d. It involves the 9 recruitment steps
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d. It involves the 9 recruitment steps

Explanation: Personnel management is broadly about obtaining, retaining, and developing staff (Options A, B, C). Option D is too specific ("9 steps") and procedural, making it the least "best description" of the overall concept.

2.
In human resource management, employment involves the following except:
a. Manpower planning
b. Forecasting on the future human resource needs
c. Acquisition, utilization, improvement and preservation of staff
d. None of the above
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d. None of the above

Explanation: All options (Manpower planning, Forecasting, Acquisition/Utilization) are integral parts of the employment/HRM process.

3.
The following statements are true about recruitment of staff except:
a. The process of obtaining and retaining employees in and organization
b. Acquisition, utilization, improvement and preservation of staff
c. Planning for fresh employees when there is a scarcity of workers or when some staff have retired, promoted, died, dismissed or terminated.
d. Getting fresh employees through advertisement when there is a scarcity of workers.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b. Acquisition, utilization, improvement and preservation of staff

Explanation: This describes the broader Human Resource Management function. Recruitment specifically refers to the process of attracting candidates (Options C and D), while "Utilization and Preservation" occur after recruitment.

4.
Determining the need for filling a vacant post:
a. Is the 1st stage in the recruitment
b. Is the job analysis
c. Is the turnover interval
d. A and B
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d. A and B

Explanation: Determining the need is the first step in recruitment, and it often involves analyzing the job (Job Analysis) to understand requirements.

5.
The following are correct about advertisement of a vacant post except:
a. Getting the most effective response at the lowest cost
b. To encourage the most qualified persons to apply
c. To discourage the unqualified interest persons from applying
d. None of the above
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d. None of the above

Explanation: A good advertisement aims to be cost-effective, attract qualified candidates, and self-screen unqualified ones (discourage them). Thus, all statements A, B, and C are correct purposes.

6.
Which of the following is not the branch of classical management theory?
a. Scientific Management
b. Systems approach to management
c. Bureaucratic Management
d. Administrative principles
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b. Systems approach to management

Explanation: Scientific (Taylor), Bureaucratic (Weber), and Administrative (Fayol) are the three pillars of Classical Theory. Systems approach is a Modern Management theory.

7.
........................ is not among the 14 Fayol's principles of management?
a. Recruitment
b. Equity
c. Discipline
d. Order
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a. Recruitment

Explanation: Equity, Discipline, and Order are among Fayol's 14 Principles. Recruitment is a function, not one of the 14 named principles (though "Stability of Tenure of Personnel" is related).

8.
Which of the following following is a way of employee separation from employment?
a. Transfer
b. Delegation
c. Dismissal
d. Posting
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c. Dismissal

Explanation: Dismissal is the termination of employment (separation). Transfer, Delegation, and Posting involve moving within the organization or assigning tasks, not leaving it.

9.
In communication channel, vertical communication is a communication between?
a. Top to bottom Managers
b. Bottom to top managers
c. A and B
d. None of the above
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c. A and B

Explanation: Vertical communication flows up and down the hierarchy (both Top-down and Bottom-up).

10.
Who is the scholar behind the development of scientific management theory?
a. Chester Bernard
b. Max Weber
c. Henri Fayol
d. None of the above
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d. None of the above

Explanation: The father of Scientific Management is Frederick Winslow Taylor. Max Weber is associated with Bureaucracy, and Henri Fayol with Administrative Management.

11.
Who founded the administrative management theory?
a. Frederick Taylor
b. Chester Barnard
c. Henri Fayol
d. None of the above
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c. Henri Fayol

Explanation: Henri Fayol is known as the father of Administrative Management Theory (or Modern Operational Management theory).

12.
Which of the following is not one of the characteristics of communication?
a. Process that is ongoing dynamics
b. It is irreversible
c. It takes place within a situation context
d. All of the above
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d. All of the above

Explanation: All listed options (A, B, C) ARE characteristics of communication. Since the question asks "which is NOT", and all of them ARE, this is likely a "None of the above" type answer, or D implies that all the options listed above are actually characteristics (meaning there is no incorrect one listed).

13.
Which of the following is the best description of written Communication?
a. Involves face to face
b. It provides opportunity to ask questions
c. It is inappropriate for lengthy communication
d. None of the above
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d. None of the above

Explanation: A, B, and C describe oral/verbal communication. Written communication is NOT face to face, does NOT provide immediate Q&A, and IS appropriate for lengthy communication. Therefore, none of the descriptions fit.

14.
Which of the following is true about non-verbal messages :
a. Present in all communications
b. Mean different things
c. May be intentional or unintentional
d. All of the above
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d. All of the above

Explanation: Non-verbal cues are omnipresent, context-dependent (mean different things), and can be conscious or subconscious.

15.
Which of the following is not an element of group dynamics ?
a. Roles
b. Membership
c. Feelings
d. None of the above
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d. None of the above

Explanation: Roles, Membership, and Feelings/Cohesion are all key elements of group dynamics. Thus, none of the listed options is "not an element".

16.
................is not one of the the written communication?
a. Memo
b. Memomrentum
c. Letter written
d. Lecture
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d. Lecture

Explanation: A lecture is a form of oral communication. (Note: "Memomrentum" is likely a typo for Memorandum, which is written).

17.
The art of managing the spread of information between an individual or an organization and public is called ?
a. Customer care
b. Customer service
c. Public relations
d. All of the above
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c. Public relations

Explanation: Public Relations (PR) is specifically defined as the management of information spread between an organization and the public.

18.
Office administration is
a. the monitoring and controlling the efforts of a group of individuals
b. the transmission and reception of message
c. the conceptualizing and anticipating future action
d. The management of stores
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a. the monitoring and controlling the efforts of a group of individuals

Explanation: Office administration involves planning, organizing, and controlling the office activities and people to achieve objectives. Option B is Communication, C is Planning.

19.
S11 is a tool used to
a. Order goods from the store
b. Order goods and services from supplies department
c. check with supplies during delivery of goods
d. All of the above
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a. Order goods from the store

Explanation: In Kenyan government procedures, the S11 (Counter Requisition and Issue Voucher) is used to requisition items from the store.

20.
Greeting of a patient at the hospital reception is trying to establish
a. The full names of the patient
b. If patient had attended that hospital before
c. If the patient is really sick
d. Rapport
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d. Rapport

Explanation: Greeting creates a welcoming environment and establishes a positive connection (rapport) before data collection begins.

Section 4: Essay Questions (40 Marks)

1.
State 5 purposes of an office (10 marks)
Show Answer
  1. Receiving Information: Collecting data from internal and external sources.
  2. Recording Information: Creating records for future reference.
  3. Arranging Information: Sorting and organizing data for processing.
  4. Giving Information: Disseminating information to management and stakeholders.
  5. Safeguarding Assets: Protecting records and company property.
2.
State 5 function of human resource department (10 marks)
Show Answer
  1. Recruitment and Selection: Hiring the right people for the job.
  2. Training and Development: Improving employee skills and performance.
  3. Compensation and Benefits: Managing salaries, wages, and perks.
  4. Employee Relations: Handling disputes, grievances, and union negotiations.
  5. Performance Appraisal: Evaluating employee performance against standards.
3.
State 5 reasons why employees leave organization after being trained (10 marks)
Show Answer
  1. Better Opportunities: Trained employees become more marketable and seek higher pay elsewhere.
  2. Lack of Career Growth: No room for promotion or applying new skills.
  3. Poor Management: Dissatisfaction with leadership or supervision.
  4. Low Compensation: Salary not commensurate with new skills.
  5. Poor Working Conditions: Stressful environment or lack of work-life balance.
4.
Your have been posted to a new hospital without health records department, list 10 requirement for starting the department (10 marks)
Show Answer
  1. Space/Office: Adequate room for registration, filing, and staff.
  2. Furniture: Desks, chairs, filing cabinets/shelves.
  3. Stationery: Patient files, registration books, index cards, forms.
  4. Staffing: Qualified HRIOs and support staff.
  5. Equipment: Computers, printers, telephones.
  6. Filing System: Designing a system (e.g., Terminal Digit) and purchasing folders.
  7. Budget: Funds for operations and supplies.
  8. Policies & Procedures: Guidelines for registration, coding, confidentiality.
  9. Patient Identification System: Master Patient Index (MPI) setup.
  10. Security Measures: Locks, access controls to protect records.
