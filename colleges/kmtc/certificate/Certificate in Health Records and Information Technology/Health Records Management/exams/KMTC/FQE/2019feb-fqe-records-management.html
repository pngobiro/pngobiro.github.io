Section 1: Multiple Choice Questions (20 Marks)

1.
The following is a function of health records, which one is it?
A. Admission
B. Statistics
C. Communication
D. Research
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C. Communication

Explanation: The primary function of the health record is to serve as a vehicle of communication between the healthcare providers treating the patient (continuity of care). Admission, Statistics, and Research are secondary uses or departmental functions, not the primary function of the record itself.

2.
Admission details includes the following except?
A. Name of the patient
B. Age
C. Date of birth
D. Name of the ward
Show Answer

Correct Answer: D. Name of the ward

Explanation: Admission details (Master Patient Index data) typically include demographic identifiers (Name, Age, DOB) which are permanent. "Name of the ward" is episode-specific and can change during admission, making it a variable clinical/administrative detail rather than a core identification detail.

3.
The goods are received from the bidder by use of?
A. S10
B. S11
C. S12
D. S13
Show Answer

Correct Answer: D. S13

Explanation: In Kenyan government procurement, the **S13** is the **Goods Received Note (GRN)** used to verify items received from a supplier.

4.
The procedure for filling the P3 forms is spelled out in which act?
A. Public records act
B. Public health act
C. Criminal procedure code
D. Civil registration act
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C. Criminal procedure code

Explanation: The P3 form is a Police Medical Examination form used in cases of assault/injury. Its usage and admissibility are governed by the Criminal Procedure Code and Evidence Act.

5.
The health records association was started in?
A. Egypt
B. Greece
C. Britain
D. United states
Show Answer

Correct Answer: D. United states

Explanation: The first professional organization for medical records, the Association of Record Librarians of North America (now AHIMA), was established in the United States in 1928.

6.
Which of the following is not an ancillary equipment?
A. Preparation tables
B. Filing trolleys
C. Kik stools
D. Pigeon holes
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A. Preparation tables

Explanation: Filing trolleys, Kick (Kik) stools, and Pigeon holes (sorting racks) are specifically designed to assist the *filing process* (ancillary to filing). A preparation table is general furniture.

7.
The following are ways in which pre-registration information is collected except?
A. General practitioner request form
B. Telephone appointment
C. Patient attending in person
D. Patient attending with G.P letter
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C. Patient attending in person

Explanation: "Pre-registration" implies collecting data *before* the patient arrives at the clinic. If a patient attends in person, they are registering *on arrival*, not pre-registering.

8.
The following are indices that are maintained in a health records department except?
A. The outpatient waiting list index
B. Alphabetical index
C. Diagnostic index
D. Operation index
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A. The outpatient waiting list index

Explanation: While waiting lists exist, an "index" typically refers to permanent secondary records like the Master Patient Index (Alphabetical), Disease (Diagnostic) Index, and Operation Index.

9.
The following are examples of special health records except?
A. Psychiatric records
B. Tuberculosis records
C. Inventory records
D. (Missing option)
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C. Inventory records

Explanation: Inventory records are administrative supply records, not *health/clinical* records. Psychiatric and TB records are specialized clinical records.

10.
Which one of the following is not the basic principle of form design?
A. Simplicity
B. Uniformity in size and style
C. Clear instructions on form
D. Compatibility with the functions of a health records department
Show Answer

Correct Answer: D. Compatibility with the functions of a health records department

Explanation: While important, "Compatibility..." is not usually listed as a standard *design principle* like Simplicity, Uniformity, Ease of entry, etc.

11.
Clinic preparation entails the following activities except
A. Sorting
B. Retrieval
C. Editing
D. Tracing
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C. Editing

Explanation: Clinic preparation involves finding records (Retrieval/Tracing) and organizing them (Sorting) for the appointment. HRIOs do not "edit" the content of the medical record during preparation.

12.
Which one of the following information is not included in the Patient Master Index card?
A. Name of the patient
B. Diagnosis of the patient
C. Patient hospital number
D. Space left for change of address
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B. Diagnosis of the patient

Explanation: The Master Patient Index (MPI) is a permanent index for identification. Diagnosis is clinical data that changes with each visit and is confidential; it is generally not recorded on the MPI card.

13.
These are the checks made on the waiting list except?
A. Completeness
B. Eligibility
C. Accuracy
D. Death
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C. Accuracy

Explanation: Waiting list validation typically checks if the patient is still waiting (Validation), still eligible/needs procedure, or has died (Death). "Accuracy" is a general data quality dimension, but death checks and eligibility checks are specific waiting list maintenance activities.

14.
Which one of the following is equipment for recording appointment?
A. Visible edge sheets
B. Circular carousel
C. Shelf filing
D. Mobile racking
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A. Visible edge sheets

Explanation: Visible edge systems (like Kardex) allow for quick viewing of appointment slots. Carousel, Shelf, and Mobile racking are high-density *filing* equipment for storage.

15.
Before destruction of paper based medical records the following procedures is recommended except?
A. Microfilming
B. Digital recording
C. Scanning
D. Photocopying
Show Answer

Correct Answer: D. Photocopying

Explanation: Microfilming and Scanning (Digital recording) are methods of *archiving* the data to save space before destroying the original paper. Photocopying just creates another paper copy.

16.
__________ Consent arises when records are disclosed to another medical agency for purpose of continued treatment.
A. Normal
B. Express
C. Implied
D. Forced
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C. Implied

Explanation: When a patient agrees to be referred or treated, there is an implied consent that their relevant medical information will be shared with the treating team.

17.
Which of the information below is not included on the DBR (Daily Bed Return)?
A. Patient on parole
B. Patient transfer in
C. Patient in appointment
D. Patient who have died
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C. Patient in appointment

Explanation: The Daily Bed Return tracks inpatient bed activity (Admissions, Discharges, Transfers, Deaths). Appointments relate to outpatients.

18.
Which of the following hospital departments need not to be notified of patients discharge?
A. Reception
B. The ward
C. The chaplain
D. Accident and Emergency
Show Answer

Correct Answer: D. Accident and Emergency

Explanation: Once a patient is admitted, A&E is usually done with the case. Discharge notification goes to Reception (for census), Ward (actioning it), and Chaplain (pastoral care closure). A&E does not track ward discharges.

19.
The fundamental requirement of filing system that ensures speedy location and positive means of identification of items in the system is called?
A. Elasticity
B. Simplicity
C. Economic
D. Accessibility
Show Answer

Correct Answer: D. Accessibility

Explanation: Accessibility refers to how quickly and easily records can be located and retrieved.

20.
Which tracer systems have the similarity in terms of time of creation?
A. Personal tracer and library tracer card
B. Personal tracer card and common tracer card
C. Library tracer and common tracer card
D. Person, library and common tracer card
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B. Personal tracer card and common tracer card

Explanation: Tracers are created/used at the time a record is removed from the file. Personal tracers (assigned to a staff member) and Common tracers (filled out per request) serve a similar function at the moment of retrieval.

SECTION FOUR: ESSAY QUESTIONS (40 MARKS)

1.

(a) Define the waiting list index (1 Mark).

(b) There are many ways in which the necessary information can be conveyed to the waiting list. Discuss the three major ways (9 Marks).

Show Answer

(a) Definition: A waiting list index is a record system (manual or electronic) used to manage patients who have been advised to have admission or treatment but for whom a bed or appointment is not immediately available.

(b) Ways to convey information:

  • Letters: Formal written communication sent via post. Provides a physical record but is slow.
  • Telephone: Direct verbal communication. Fast and allows for immediate confirmation, but requires correct contact details.
  • Electronic/SMS/Email: Modern digital methods. Very fast and cost-effective, but depends on patient tech access.
2.
Discuss the two main principles of terminal digit filing (10 Marks).
Show Answer
  1. Division of Numbers: The filing number (e.g., 6 digits) is divided into three parts: Primary (last 2 digits), Secondary (middle 2 digits), and Tertiary (first 2 digits).
  2. Filing Order: Filing is done by reading the number from right to left (Primary -> Secondary -> Tertiary). This distributes records evenly throughout the filing system (00-99 sections), preventing congestion at the end of the file which occurs in straight numerical filing.
3.
Instructions relating to confidentiality should be given to the new member of staff. Outline the instructions to be explained (10 Marks).
Show Answer
  • Definition of Confidentiality: What constitutes confidential patient data.
  • Legal Responsibility: The legal implications of breaching confidentiality (e.g., Data Protection Act).
  • Access Control: Do not access records for patients not under your care (e.g., VIPs, family).
  • Disclosure: Who is authorized to receive information (consent requirements).
  • Physical Security: Locking computers, not leaving files unattended.
  • Social Media: Prohibition of sharing patient info online.
  • Disciplinary Action: Consequences of breach (dismissal, prosecution).
  • Oath of Secrecy: Signing the confidentiality agreement.
4.
Discuss the principles of form design (10 Marks).
Show Answer
  1. Need/Purpose: The form must have a clear purpose and eliminate unnecessary data collection.
  2. Standardization: Use standard paper sizes (A4, A5) for filing compatibility.
  3. Simplicity: Layout should be clean, uncluttered, and easy to understand.
  4. Sequence: Data fields should follow a logical workflow (e.g., left to right, top to bottom).
  5. Identification: Every form must have a unique ID/Title and patient ID space on every page.
  6. Spacing: Adequate space must be provided for handwriting or typing.
  7. Paper Quality: Durable paper for permanent records, lighter for temporary.
  8. Instructions: Clear instructions on how to fill the form should be included.
