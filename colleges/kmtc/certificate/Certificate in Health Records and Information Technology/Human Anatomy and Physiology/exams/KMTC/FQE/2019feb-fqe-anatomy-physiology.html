Section 1: Multiple Choice Questions (20 Marks)

1.
The following are membranes covering the brain and the spinal cord EXCEPT?
a. Dura mater
b. Pia mater
c. Sub-arachnoid mater
d. Arachnoid mater
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c. Sub-arachnoid mater

Explanation: The meninges consist of three layers: Dura mater, Arachnoid mater, and Pia mater. "Sub-arachnoid" refers to the space between the arachnoid and pia mater, not a distinct membrane layer called "Sub-arachnoid mater".

2.
The following are parts of the small intestines EXCEPT?
a. Ileum
b. Duodenum
c. Jejunum
d. Large intestine
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d. Large intestine

Explanation: The three parts of the small intestine are the Duodenum, Jejunum, and Ileum. The large intestine is a separate section of the GI tract.

3.
The major heat producing organ in the body is?
a. Liver
b. Spleen
c. Heart
d. Kidneys
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a. Liver

Explanation: The liver is a highly metabolically active organ and is a major source of body heat, especially at rest.

4.
The following are parts of the eye EXCEPT?
a. Retina
b. Choroid
c. Iris
d. Pinna
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d. Pinna

Explanation: The pinna (auricle) is the visible part of the external ear, not the eye.

5.
There are...................parts of cranial nerves?
a. 31
b. 12
c. 21
d. 30
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b. 12

Explanation: There are 12 pairs of cranial nerves. (31 refers to spinal nerve pairs).

6.
Which one of the following is the muscular layer of the heart?
a. Endometrium
b. Endocardium
c. Pericardium
d. Myometrium
Show Answer

Correct Answer: [None/Typo]

Explanation: The correct answer is Myocardium.
- Endometrium and Myometrium are layers of the uterus.
- Endocardium is the inner lining of the heart.
- Pericardium is the sac surrounding the heart.
In the context of the exam, it is highly likely that option D was intended to be Myocardium or was a typo.

7.
The largest artery that carries oxygenated blood from the heart to the body is called
a. Jugular vein
b. Aorta
c. Superior vena cava
d. Coronary artery
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b. Aorta

Explanation: The aorta is the main artery carrying oxygenated blood from the left ventricle to the rest of the body.

8.
Fertilization takes place within the...................
a. Uterus
b. Fallopian tubes
c. Vagina
d. Abdomen
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b. Fallopian tubes

Explanation: Fertilization typically occurs in the ampulla of the uterine (fallopian) tube.

9.
The movement of chemical substance from an area of high concentration to an area of low concentration is called?
a. Active transport
b. Osmosis
c. Diffusion
d. Facilitated diffusion
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c. Diffusion

Explanation: Diffusion is the passive movement of particles down their concentration gradient.

10.
The ear is divided into ...................parts
a. 2
b. 3
c. 4
d. 5
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b. 3

Explanation: The ear is divided into the External ear, Middle ear, and Inner ear.

11.
The lymph organs include the following EXCEPT?
a. Payers patches
b. Spleen
c. Liver
d. Tonsils
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c. Liver

Explanation: While the liver has immune functions (Kupffer cells), it is primarily a metabolic/digestive organ. Peyer's patches, Spleen, and Tonsils are distinct lymphatic organs/tissues.

12.
Which of the following is not a type of muscle tissue?
a. Skeletal
b. Cardiac
c. Smooth
d. Glands
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d. Glands

Explanation: Glands are epithelial tissue, not muscle. The three types of muscle tissue are Skeletal, Cardiac, and Smooth.

13.
Ball and socket joints allow the movement along
a. Two axes
b. All axes
c. Three axes
d. One axis
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c. Three axes (or b. All axes)

Explanation: Ball and socket joints are multiaxial, allowing movement in three planes (flexion/extension, abduction/adduction, rotation).

14.
The smallest living unit of structure and function are called
a. Cell
b. Tissue
c. Organ
d. System

Correct Answer: a. Cell

15.
Which one of the following cell organelles is the site of energy production?
a. Mitochondria
b. Ribosome
c. Golgi apparatus
d. Nuclei
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a. Mitochondria

Explanation: Mitochondria are the "powerhouses" of the cell, generating ATP.

16.
The functional unit of the kidney is called?
a. Loop henle

17.
The main source of energy in the body is...................

b. Fats

Explanation: Carbohydrates (glucose) are the body's preferred primary energy source.

18.
Which one of the following hormones are produced by the beta cells of the pancreas?
a. Insulin
b. Glucagon
c. Somatostatin
d. Pancreatic polypeptide
Show Answer

19.
The following are three auditory ossicles found in the middle ear EXCEPT?
a. Stapes
b. Incus
c. Malleus
d. Sphenoid
Show Answer

20.
The primary site of gaseous exchange in the lung is

b. Diaphragm
c. Bronchioles

Explanation: The actual site is the Alveoli. Among the options provided, Bronchioles are the closest conducting structures, but none are the primary site. (Note: Option D 'Sphenoid' is a bone). This question likely has an error in the options.

Section 2: True/False Questions (20 Marks)

Indicate whether the following statements are True or False:
Show Answers
  1. The coronary artery supply blood to the heart. (True)
  2. The nervous system consist of a vast number of cells called neurons. (True)
  3. The mitochondria process proteins from amino acids. (False) - Ribosomes do this.
  4. The retina is the outermost layer of the eye. (False) - Sclera/Cornea is outer; Retina is inner.
  5. Red bone marrow is the primary site for hemopoiesis. (True)
  6. All hormones are secreted by endocrine glands. (False) - Some by tissues (e.g. heart, kidney).
  7. Platelets contribute to hemostasis. (True)
  8. The lungs receive both pulmonary and systemic blood. (True) - Pulmonary for gas exchange, Bronchial (systemic) for tissue nutrients.
  9. Osteoblast are bone-forming cells. (True)
  10. The inner ear contains the organs of hearing only. (False) - Also equilibrium (Vestibular system).
  11. The sperm has a whip-like tail used for motility. (True)
  12. The oesophagus is in the dorsal body cavity. (False) - Ventral/Thoracic.
  13. Arteries have thinner walls than veins. (False) - Arteries are thicker.
  14. During inspiration, the diaphragm contracts and flatten to increase the volume of the chest cavity. (True)
  15. Heart rate is controlled by the somatic nervous system. (False) - Autonomic nervous system.
  16. The urethra carry urine from the kidneys to the urinary bladder. (False) - Ureters do this.
  17. Osteoblast cells breakdown the bone. (False) - Osteoclasts break down bone.
  18. Apoptosis is a programmed cell death. (True)
  19. Hypoxia is caused by insufficient oxygen to cells and tissues. (True)
  20. Hypertension is communicable disease. (False) - Non-communicable.

Section 4: Long Essay Questions (40 Marks)

1.
State at-least 10 functions of the liver (10 marks)
Show Answer
  1. Production of bile.
  2. Metabolism of carbohydrates (Glycogenesis/Glycogenolysis).
  3. Metabolism of lipids (Cholesterol synthesis).
  4. Metabolism of proteins (Deamination).
  5. Storage of vitamins (A, D, E, K, B12) and minerals (Iron, Copper).
  6. Detoxification of drugs and toxins.
  7. Synthesis of plasma proteins (Albumin, Clotting factors).
  8. Immune function (Kupffer cells phagocytosis).
  9. Heat production.
  10. Storage of glycogen.
2.
Highlight the importance of skeletons in the body (10 Marks)
Show Answer
  1. Support: Provides structural framework for the body.
  2. Protection: Protects vital internal organs (Brain, Heart, Lungs).
  3. Movement: Provides attachment points for muscles acting as levers.
  4. Blood Cell Production: Hematopoiesis occurs in red bone marrow.
  5. Mineral Storage: Reservoir for calcium and phosphorus.
  6. Triglyceride Storage: Yellow bone marrow stores fat.
3.
Explain five causes of cancer (10 Marks)
Show Answer
  1. Tobacco Use: Smoking is a leading cause of lung and other cancers.
  2. Infections: Viruses like HPV (Cervical), Hepatitis B/C (Liver).
  3. Radiation: UV rays (Skin cancer), Ionizing radiation.
  4. Diet/Obesity: High fat, low fiber, processed meats; excess body weight.
  5. Genetic Factors: Inherited mutations (e.g., BRCA1/2).
  6. Environmental Carcinogens: Asbestos, Benzene, Pollution.
4.
Explain 5 functions of the skin (10 Marks)
Show Answer
  1. Protection: Barrier against pathogens, UV light, and dehydration.
  2. Sensation: Contains receptors for touch, pressure, pain, and temperature.
  3. Thermoregulation: Regulates body temperature via sweating and blood flow.
  4. Vitamin D Synthesis: Produces Vitamin D when exposed to sunlight.
  5. Excretion: Eliminates small amounts of waste (salts, water) via sweat.
  6. Absorption: Absorbs lipid-soluble substances/medications.
