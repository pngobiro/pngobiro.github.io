Section 1: Multiple Choice Questions (20 Marks)

1.
The study of diseases of males especially masculinity, male reproductive and secondary sex characteristics is called
a. Urology
b. Andrology
c. Gynecology
d. Venerology
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b. Andrology

Explanation: Andrology is the medical specialty that deals with male health, particularly relating to the problems of the male reproductive system and urological problems that are unique to men.

2.
Medical term for impaired vision in dim light
a. Nyctalopia
b. Nystagmus
c. Epiphora
d. Entropian
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a. Nyctalopia

Explanation: Nyctalopia, also known as night blindness, is the inability to see well in dim light.

3.
Which of the following is not an auto-immune disease
a. Rheumatoid arthritis
b. Haemophilia
c. Lupus erythematosus
d. HIV/AIDS
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d. HIV/AIDS (and b. Haemophilia)

Explanation: HIV/AIDS is an acquired immunodeficiency syndrome caused by a virus. Haemophilia is a genetic disorder. Rheumatoid arthritis and Lupus are autoimmune diseases. In the context of "acquired vs auto-immune", HIV is the classic acquired example, but Haemophilia is also definitely not auto-immune.

4.
The abnormal accumulation of fluids within the peritoneal cavity is known as
a. Ascites
b. Pleural effusion
c. Anal fissure
d. Hirschsprung
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a. Ascites

Explanation: Ascites is the accumulation of fluid in the peritoneal cavity (abdomen).

5.
Total collapse of the lung is termed as
a. Embolism
b. Sarcoidosis
c. Pulmonary ptosis
d. Atelectasis
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d. Atelectasis

Explanation: Atelectasis is the collapse or closure of a lung resulting in reduced or absent gas exchange.

6.
A surgical incision made to enlarge the vaginal orifice to facilitate child birth is termed as
a. Cesarean section
b. Episiotomy
c. Colpectomy
d. Dystocia
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b. Episiotomy

Explanation: An episiotomy is a surgical cut made at the opening of the vagina during childbirth to prevent rupture of tissues.

7.
Leukemia mean
a. Excess of white blood cells
b. Excess of red blood cells
c. Inadequate white blood cells
d. Inadequate red blood cells
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a. Excess of white blood cells

Explanation: Leukemia is a cancer of the blood-forming tissues, characterized by a rapid production of abnormal white blood cells.

8.
Hyperopia means
a. Farsightedness
b. Nearsightedness
c. Blurred vision
d. Night blindness
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a. Farsightedness

Explanation: Hyperopia (farsightedness) is a condition where distant objects are seen clearly, but close objects appear blurred.

9.
Enuresis is
a. Inconsistence or involuntary discharge of urine
b. The act of urination
c. Consistence urine output
d. Absence of urine
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a. Inconsistence or involuntary discharge of urine

Explanation: Enuresis is involuntary urination, especially by children at night (bedwetting).

10.
Hospital acquired infections are called
a. Idiopathic
b. Iatrogenic
c. Nosocomial
d. Chronic
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c. Nosocomial

Explanation: Nosocomial infections are those contracted in a hospital or healthcare facility.

11.
The fundamental unit of a medical term is the
a. Combining form
b. Suffix
c. Prefix
d. Root word
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d. Root word

Explanation: The root word is the foundation of a medical term and provides its primary meaning.

12.
A neoplasm that involves the connective tissue, muscle or bone is
a. Malignant
b. Benign
c. Sarcoma
d. Carcinoma
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c. Sarcoma

Explanation: Sarcomas are cancers arising from mesenchymal (connective) tissues like bone, muscle, fat, or cartilage.

13.
Which of the following suffixes refers to eating
a. Plegia
b. Phagia
c. Phasia
d. Phlagia
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b. Phagia

Explanation: -phagia means eating or swallowing (e.g., dysphagia).

14.
Graphospasm means
a. Stretching of tissue
b. Wrist drop
c. Wrist cramp
d. Wrist fall
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c. Wrist cramp

Explanation: Graphospasm is commonly known as writer's cramp.

15.
A plane that divides the body into right and left part
a. Coronal (Frontal)
b. Sagittal
c. Transverse
d. Frontal
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b. Sagittal

Explanation: The sagittal plane divides the body into left and right sections.

16.
Drugs used to treat bacterial diseases are called
a. Antiduretic
b. Antiretrovirals
c. Antihistamines
d. Antibiotics
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d. Antibiotics

Explanation: Antibiotics are medications that destroy or slow down the growth of bacteria.

17.
Chest pain caused by reduced blood flow to the heart
a. Ischeamia
b. Algia
c. Angina
d. Dynia
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c. Angina

Explanation: Angina pectoris is chest pain due to ischemia of the heart muscle.

18.
Stenosis means
a. Lengthening
b. Narrowing
c. Expansion
d. Widening
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b. Narrowing

Explanation: Stenosis refers to the abnormal narrowing of a body channel (e.g., spinal stenosis, aortic stenosis).

19.
Sexually attracted to more than one gender
a. Bisexual
b. Homosexual
c. Heterosexual
d. Polysexual
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a. Bisexual

Explanation: Bisexuality refers to attraction to both males and females.

20.
Excessive menstrual flow
a. Menorrhea
b. Menorrhagia
c. Metrorrhagia
d. Dysmenorrhea
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b. Menorrhagia

Explanation: Menorrhagia is abnormally heavy or prolonged bleeding at menstruation.

Section 2: True or False (20 Marks)

Indicate whether the following statements are True or False:
Show Answers
  1. Drugs that prohibits secretion of urine are called diuretic. False (Diuretics promote secretion).
  2. Adding a vowel to a root word results to a complex word. False (Results in a Combining Form).
  3. Antitussives are drugs that relief cough. True.
  4. Vitiligo is a localized loss of skin pigment. True.
  5. The suffix algia and dynia both mean pain. True.
  6. Proximal pertains to the sides of the body. False (Proximal means nearer to the center/point of attachment; Lateral pertains to the side).
  7. Nephroptosis describes the prolapse of the kidney. True.
  8. Homeostasis means to stop or control bleeding. False (Hemostasis stops bleeding; Homeostasis is equilibrium).
  9. Eupnea mean labored breathing. False (Eupnea is normal breathing; Dyspnea is labored).
  10. Abduction is movement away from the midsagittal plane of the body. True.
  11. The curvature of spine is known as kyphosis. True (Specifically thoracic curvature/hunchback).
  12. Myositis means inflammation of the muscles. True.
  13. Arthrodesis means surgical breaking of a stiff joint. False (Arthrodesis is surgical fusion/binding; Arthroclasia is breaking).
  14. Talipes means foot inverted, walking on toes. True (Clubfoot).
  15. Localized abnormal dilation of a vessel is not aneurysm. False (It is the definition of aneurysm).
  16. Excess condition of acidity of body fluid is referred to as acidosis. True.
  17. Accumulation of fluid in the testis is known as hydrocephalus. False (Hydrocele; Hydrocephalus is fluid in the brain).
  18. Visual examination of the vaginal with an instrument is called colposcope. True.
  19. Onychia is the inflammation of the fingernails. True.
  20. Natriuresis refers to the excretion of sodium in the urine. True.

Section 4: State the Meaning of Words (20 Marks)

  1. Dyspareunia: Painful sexual intercourse.
  2. Pertussis: Whooping cough.
  3. Hypospadias: Congenital defect where the urethra opens on the underside of the penis.
  4. Menarche: First occurrence of menstruation.
  5. Adenoid: Resembling a gland (also refers to pharyngeal tonsil).
  6. Ischaemia: Reduced blood flow to a body part.
  7. Aneurysm: Abnormal dilation or ballooning of a blood vessel.
  8. Myocardium: The muscular tissue of the heart.
  9. Hypotension: Low blood pressure.
  10. Dermatome: Area of skin supplied by a single spinal nerve (or instrument to cut skin).
  11. Hypohydrosis: Abnormally diminished sweating.
  12. Trichology: Study of hair and its diseases.
  13. Ankylosis: Stiffening and immobility of a joint.
  14. Amnesia: Loss of memory.
  15. Ataxia: Lack of voluntary muscle coordination.
  16. Euphoria: Exaggerated feeling of well-being or elation.
  17. Cachexia: Severe wasting of the body (weight loss/muscle atrophy).
  18. Cholestasis: Stoppage or reduction of bile flow.
  19. Eructation: Belching.
  20. Amniocentesis: Surgical puncture of the amniotic sac to remove fluid for testing.
