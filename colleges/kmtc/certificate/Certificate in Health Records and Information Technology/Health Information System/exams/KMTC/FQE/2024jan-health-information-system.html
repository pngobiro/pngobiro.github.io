Section 1: Multiple Choice Questions (40 Marks)

1.
Which of the following is a health information system component?
A. Processing
B. Reliability
C. Validity
D. Accessible
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A. Processing

Explanation: A system typically consists of Inputs, Processing, and Outputs. Reliability and Validity are data quality attributes.

2.
The process where raw data is gathered and expressed in summary form for statistical analysis is
A. Data analysis
B. Data disposal
C. Data aggregation
D. Data collection
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C. Data aggregation

Explanation: Aggregation involves combining individual data points into summaries (e.g., total malaria cases per month).

3.
Manual validation of data entails the following except
A. Consistency
B. Proof reading
C. Completeness
D. Correctness
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B. Proof reading

Explanation: Data validation checks for completeness, consistency, and correctness (accuracy). Proofreading is typically associated with checking text documents for grammar/spelling, rather than validating data values against rules.

4.
In HIS, KEPH stands for?
A. Kenya Essential Program for Health
B. Kenya Essential Package for Health
C. Kenya Essential Parcel for Health
D. Kenya Expanded Program for Health
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B. Kenya Essential Package for Health

5.
Product of data processing is
A. Data
B. Information
C. Software program
D. System
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B. Information

Explanation: Data is the raw input; Information is the processed data.

6.
The following are functions of primary health care level, which one is not?
A. Disease prevention
B. Rehabilitative services
C. Promotion services
D. Outpatient diagnostic services
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B. Rehabilitative services

Explanation: While some basic rehabilitation may occur, specialized rehabilitative services are typically a function of higher-tier facilities. Primary care focuses on promotion, prevention, and basic curative/diagnostic services.

7.
Identify the correctly matched level of organization of healthcare in Kenya
A. Tier 1- dispensaries
B. Tier 2- county hospital
C. Tier 3- community
D. Tier 4 – national referral hospitals
Show Answer

Correct Answer: D. Tier 4 – national referral hospitals

Explanation: In the 4-tier system: Tier 1 = Community; Tier 2 = Primary Care (Dispensaries/Health Centres); Tier 3 = County Hospitals; Tier 4 = National Referral Hospitals.

8.
Pick the correctly named MOH tool
A. MOH 258- appointment card
B. MOH 520- Immunization card
C. MOH 301- Daily activity register
D. MOH 711- Malaria commodity form
Show Answer

Correct Answer: D. MOH 711- Malaria commodity form (Integrated Summary)

Explanation: MOH 711 is the National Integrated Summary Form which includes Malaria data. (Note: MOH 511 is the Child Welfare Register, MOH 362 is often the daily activity register). D is the closest accurate match in this context.

9.
Which of the following is a primary data collection tool in HIS
A. Tally sheet
B. Diagnostic index card
C. Morbidity summary sheet
D. Workload report
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A. Tally sheet

Explanation: Tally sheets are used at the point of care to collect raw data. Summary sheets and reports are secondary tools.

10.

A. MOH 515
B. MOH 717
C. MOH 516
D. MOH 731
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A. MOH 515 (CHEW Summary) or C. MOH 516 (Community Log)

Explanation: The standard referral form is MOH 100. However, among the options, MOH 515/516 are community-level tools. This question likely refers to the Community Referral Form which is part of the community strategy toolset (often linked with the 513/514/515 series).

11.
The number of people living within a geographical area served by a health facility is known as
A. Catchment population
B. Patient population
C. Attendance
D. Health population

Correct Answer: A. Catchment population

12.
As a HRIO working in a sub-county hospital, which characteristic of an indicator would you consider adhering to?
A. Specificity
B. Measurable
C. Attainable
D. Timely
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B. Measurable

Explanation: While all are important (SMART criteria), measurability is the fundamental characteristic that defines an indicator (something that can be measured).

13.
Assessment of how the project is progressing periodically best describes
A. Operational planning
B. Evaluation
C. Monitoring
D. Monitoring and Evaluation
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C. Monitoring

Explanation: Monitoring is the continuous/periodic tracking of progress. Evaluation is usually episodic (mid-term, end-term).

14.

C. Impact
D. Output

Correct Answer: D. Output

Explanation: Outputs are the direct products of activities (e.g., number of people trained, number of condoms distributed).

Section 2: Short Answer Questions (40 Marks)

41.
Define the following terms (5 marks):
a) Data
b) Health information
c) Health system
d) Health management information system
e) Information system
Show Answer
  • Data: Raw facts and figures that have no meaning on their own.
  • Health Information: Processed health data that is organized and interpreted to be useful for decision-making.
  • Health System: All organizations, people, and actions whose primary intent is to promote, restore, or maintain health.
  • HMIS: A system designed to collect, analyze, report, and use health information to improve health service management.
  • Information System: An organized system for the collection, organization, storage, and communication of information.
42.
Outline five health services offered at tier 2 (5 marks)
Show Answer
  1. Curative outpatient services
  2. Immunization (EPI)
  3. Antenatal Care (ANC) and Maternity
  4. Family Planning
  5. Health Promotion and Education
  6. Growth Monitoring
43.
Explain five uses of information generated from outpatient morbidity report at a facility level (5 marks)
Show Answer
  1. Disease Surveillance: Identifying disease trends and outbreaks.
  2. Drug Procurement: Estimating drug needs based on disease prevalence.
  3. Staffing: Determining workload to allocate staff appropriately.
  4. Planning: Budgeting and resource allocation.
  5. Reporting: Providing data to higher levels (Sub-county/County).

Section 3: Long Answer Questions (20 Marks)

49.
With relevant examples, discuss the criteria used in selection of core health indicators (20 marks)
Show Answer

Criteria for Selecting Indicators (SMART + others):

  1. Validity: The indicator must measure what it is intended to measure. Example: Maternal Mortality Ratio is a valid measure of maternal health safety.
  2. Reliability: The indicator should produce consistent results when repeated.
  3. Specificity: It should reflect changes only in the situation concerned.
  4. Sensitivity: It should be sensitive to changes in the situation.
  5. Measurability: It must be quantifiable using available data.
  6. Relevance: It must be relevant to the priority health problems (e.g., Malaria incidence in a malaria-endemic zone).
  7. Cost-effectiveness (Feasibility): Data collection should not be too expensive or difficult.
  8. Timeliness: Data should be available when needed for decision-making.
  9. Comparability: Should allow comparison over time and between regions (standard definitions).
  10. Ethical: Data collection should not violate ethical standards.
