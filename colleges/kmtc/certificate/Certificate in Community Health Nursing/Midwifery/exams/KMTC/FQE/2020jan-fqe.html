Section 1: Multiple Choice Questions (40 Marks)

1.
Neonatal death occurs
a) In the first 60 days of life
b) In the first 28 days of life
c) In the uterus
d) In the first 12 month of life
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) In the first 28 days of life

Explanation: A neonatal death is defined as the death of a live-born baby within the first 28 completed days of life. (0-28 days).

2.
The positive signs of pregnancy include
a) Morning sickness, Hegar’s sign, fetal parts palpated
b) Quickening, jacquimiers’s sign, ballotment of the fetus
c) Amenorrhea, uterine growth, fetal parts palpated
d) Visualization of the fetus by ultrasound, fetal movements, fetal parts palpated
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Visualization of the fetus by ultrasound, fetal movements, fetal parts palpated

Explanation: Positive signs are those that offer conclusive proof of pregnancy, attributed only to the fetus. These include: Fetal heart sounds heard by examiner, Visualization of fetus (USG), and Fetal movements palpated by examiner. (Note: "Fetal parts palpated" in option A might refer to the examiner but is grouped with presumptive/probable signs like Hegar's/Morning sickness. Option D contains the strongest definitive signs).

3.
Second degree tear of the perineum involves
a) The fourchette, bulbocavernosus and transverse perineal muscles
b) Fourchette only
c) Fourchette, pubococcygeus and anal sphincter
d) Fourchette, anal sphincter and rectal mucosa
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) The fourchette, bulbocavernosus and transverse perineal muscles

Explanation:

  • 1st Degree: Skin, fourchette, vaginal mucosa.
  • 2nd Degree: Perineal muscles (bulbocavernosus, superficial transverse perineal) but anal sphincter is intact.
  • 3rd Degree: Involves anal sphincter (Option C).
  • 4th Degree: Involves rectal mucosa (Option D).

4.
Intramural fibroid
a) Is Embedded within the walls of the uterus
b) Lies below the perimetrium
c) Is found within the endometrium
d) Is found within decidua
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Is Embedded within the walls of the uterus

Explanation:

  • Intramural: Within the myometrium (muscle wall).
  • Subserosal: Below the perimetrium (outer surface).
  • Submucosal: Below the endometrium (inner surface).

5.
In a single pregnancy, the fundus reaches the umbilicus at
a) 16 weeks to 20 weeks
b) 22 weeks to 24 weeks
c) 26 weeks to 30 weeks
d) 19 weeks to 21 weeks
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) 22 weeks to 24 weeks

Explanation: The fundus typically reaches the level of the umbilicus at approximately 22-24 weeks of gestation. (At 20 weeks it is often cited as just below or at the umbilicus, but 22-24 is the standard range provided in this context).

6.
Fetal movement are first felt by a primigravid woman at
a) 16 weeks gestation
b) 20 weeks gestation
c) 24 weeks gestation
d) 26 weeks gestation
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) 20 weeks gestation

Explanation: Quickening (first fetal movement felt by mother) occurs at 18-20 weeks in a primigravida (first pregnancy) and slightly earlier (16-18 weeks) in a multigravida.

7.
Supplementation of folic acid during antenatal period reduces the incidences of
a) Maternal constipation
b) Fetal neural tube defect
c) Fetal infection
d) Miscarriages
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Fetal neural tube defect

Explanation: Folic acid is essential for the closure of the neural tube in the first month of pregnancy, preventing defects like spina bifida and anencephaly.

8.
Temporary structures in fetal circulation include
a) The umbilical vein, Ductus Ductus
b) Ductus Ductus, Foramen ovale
c) Hypogastric arteries, Umbilical vein
d) Superior venacava, Foramen ovale
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Hypogastric arteries, Umbilical vein (Note: Options are poorly edited)

Explanation: The temporary fetal structures are: 1. Umbilical Vein 2. Ductus Venosus 3. Foramen Ovale 4. Ductus Arteriosus 5. Umbilical Arteries (Internal Iliac/Hypogastric arteries).
Analysis of options: - a) "Ductus Ductus" is a typo (Likely Ductus Venosus/Arteriosus). - b) "Ductus Ductus" typo. - c) "Hypogastric arteries" (which become umbilical arteries) and "Umbilical vein". These ARE temporary fetal structures. - d) Superior Vena Cava is permanent. Therefore, C is the technically correct answer listing two temporary structures.

9.
Rhesus iso-immunization can be prevented by
a) Administration of Anti-D immunoglobulin to Rh (D) positive blood type mother who has a baby with a Rh (D) positive blood type
b) Administration of Anti-D immunoglobulin to Rh (D) negative blood type mother who has a baby with a Rh (D) positive blood type
c) Administration of Anti-D immunoglobulin to all expectant mothers
d) Administration of Anti-D immunoglobulin to Rh (D) negative baby
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Administration of Anti-D immunoglobulin to Rh (D) negative blood type mother who has a baby with a Rh (D) positive blood type

Explanation: Anti-D Ig (Rhogam) is given to an Rh-negative mother within 72 hours of delivering an Rh-positive baby (or after sensitizing events) to prevent her immune system from forming antibodies against Rh+ blood cells.

10.
The heart rate for a normal full-term infant range between
a) 100 – 140 beats per minute
b) 120 – 160 beats per minute
c) 90 – 130 beats per minute
d) 160 – 180 beats per minute
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) 120 – 160 beats per minute

Explanation: The normal resting heart rate for a newborn is typically 120-160 bpm.

11.
Switching off fans prior to delivery helps to minimize heat loss by
a) Conduction
b) Radiation
c) Convection
d) Radiation and conduction
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Convection

Explanation: Convection is the loss of heat to moving air currents. Turning off fans stops the airflow, reducing convective heat loss.

12.
Deep vein thrombosis in pregnancy is managed by
a) Mobilization, administering heparin
b) Administering heparin, restricting mobilization
c) Administering heparin and vitamin K
d) Administering vitamin K and mobilization
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Administering heparin, restricting mobilization

Explanation: Initial management of acute DVT involves anticoagulation (Heparin - usually LMWH) and strict bed rest (restricting mobilization) to prevent the clot from dislodging and causing a pulmonary embolism. Mobilization is started gradually once anticoagulation is established and pain subsides. Vitamin K (Warfarin) is generally avoided in pregnancy (teratogenic).

13.
When a hand or a foot lies alongside the head, the presentation is said to be
a) Unstable presentation
b) Shoulder presentation
c) Compound presentation
d) Compound unstable presentation
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Compound presentation

Explanation: Compound presentation occurs when an extremity (hand or foot) prolapses alongside the presenting part (usually the head) and enters the pelvis simultaneously.

14.
In brow presentation, the presenting diameter is
a) Submentobregmatic
b) Submentovertical
c) Occipitofrontal
d) Mentovertical
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Mentovertical

Explanation: In brow presentation, the head is partially extended. The engaging diameter is the Mentovertical, which is the largest anteroposterior diameter of the fetal head (approx 13.5 cm), usually causing obstructed labor.

15.
The sequential mechanism of normal labour is
a) Lateral flexion, flexion, descent, internal rotation of the head, crowning, extension of the head, restitution, internal rotation of the shoulders
b) Descent, flexion, internal rotation of the head, crowning, extension of the head, restitution, internal rotation of the shoulders, lateral flexion
c) Restitution, crowning, flexion, descent, internal rotation of the head, lateral flexion
d) Descent, flexion, crowning, restitution, lateral flexion
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Descent, flexion, internal rotation of the head, crowning, extension of the head, restitution, internal rotation of the shoulders, lateral flexion

Explanation: This lists the classic cardinal movements of labor in the correct order: 1. Descent 2. Flexion 3. Internal Rotation 4. Extension (Crowning occurs here) 5. Restitution 6. External Rotation (matches Internal rotation of shoulders) 7. Lateral Flexion (Expulsion).

16.
Latent phase of labour
a) Starts when the cervix is 4 cm dilated
b) Starts when the cervix is 8 cm dilated
c) Ends when the cervix is 6 cm dilated
d) Ends when the cervix is 3 cm dilated
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Ends when the cervix is 3 cm dilated (or 4cm depending on guideline).

Explanation:

  • Latent Phase: 0 cm to 3/4 cm.
  • Active Phase: 4 cm to 10 cm.
Older guidelines (often used in these exams) define Latent phase ending at 3 cm or 4 cm. Option D (Ends at 3cm) fits the traditional definition where Active phase starts at 4cm. Note: WHO 2018 guidelines updated Active phase to start at 5cm, but papers from 2020 often follow the 0-3cm Latent / 4-10cm Active model.

17.
Antidote for magnesium sulphate toxicity is
a) Vitamin K
b) Heparin
c) Calcium gluconate
d) Diazepam
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Calcium gluconate

Explanation: 10% Calcium Gluconate (10ml IV) is the specific antidote for Magnesium Sulphate toxicity (respiratory depression, loss of reflexes).

18.
The effect of anaemia in pregnancy to the mother includes
a) Pre-term birth, reduced ability to withstand postpartum haemorrhage
b) Low birth weight, intrauterine hypoxia
c) Intrauterine hypoxia, reduced resistance to infection
d) Reduced ability to withstand post-partum hemorrhage, reduced enjoyment of pregnancy
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Reduced ability to withstand post-partum hemorrhage, reduced enjoyment of pregnancy (fatigue)

Explanation: The question asks for effects to the mother.

  • a) Pre-term birth (Fetal/Obstetric outcome), PPH (Maternal).
  • b) LBW, IU Hypoxia (Fetal).
  • c) IU Hypoxia (Fetal).
  • d) Reduced ability to withstand PPH (Maternal) + Fatigue/Reduced enjoyment (Maternal). This option focuses entirely on the mother.

19.
Causes of uterine rapture which can be prevented by a mid-wife include
a) Neglected labour, injudicious use of oxytocin
b) High parity, extension of severe cervical lacerations
c) Neglected labour, trauma to the uterus due to a blast injury
d) Perforation of non-pregnant uterus, injudicious use of oxytocin
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Neglected labour, injudicious use of oxytocin

Explanation: These are iatrogenic or management-related causes. A midwife can prevent rupture by monitoring labor progress (preventing neglected/obstructed labor) and managing Oxytocin infusion carefully according to protocol.

20.
On vaginal examination, descent of the presenting part is determined by the level of
a) The presenting part in relation to the maternal ischial spines
b) Caput succedaneum in relation to the maternal ischial spines
c) The presenting part in relation to curve of sacrum
d) The presenting part in relation to the dilatation of cervix
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) The presenting part in relation to the maternal ischial spines

Explanation: Station is measured by the relationship of the lowest bony part of the fetal head to the maternal ischial spines (Station 0).

21.
The outcome of occipito posterior position in which long rotation with complete flexion occurs during labour is
a) Face to pubis birth
b) Face to face presentation
c) Vertex presentation
d) Deep transverse arrest
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Vertex presentation (Normal vaginal delivery as OA)

Explanation: In Occipito Posterior (OP) position:

  • Long Rotation (135 degrees): The occiput rotates all the way to the front (Anterior). The baby delivers as Occipito Anterior (Vertex). This is the favorable outcome (90% of cases).
  • Short Rotation (45 degrees): The occiput rotates to the back (Posterior). The baby delivers Face to Pubis.

22.
The recommended methods of family planning for a post-natal mother with cardiac disease include
a) Barrier methods with spermicides, intrauterine contraceptive devices
b) Progesterone only pill, barrier methods with spermicides
c) Combined pill, intrauterine device, progesterone only pill
d) Intrauterine contraceptive device, progesterone only pill
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Progesterone only pill, barrier methods with spermicides

Explanation:

  • Combined Pill (Estrogen): Contraindicated due to risk of thrombosis/fluid retention.
  • IUCD: Often avoided in valvular heart disease due to risk of infection/endocarditis on insertion (though guidelines vary, non-invasive is safer).
  • Safe methods: Barrier methods (Condoms) and Progesterone-only methods (POP/Implants) which do not affect clotting.

23.
Intrapartum prevention of puerperal sepsis includes
a) Aseptic technique, repair of perineal tears
b) Aseptic technique, treatment of urinary tract infection
c) Aseptic technique, prevention of trauma to lower extremities
d) Aseptic techniques, treatment of engorged breasts
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Aseptic technique, repair of perineal tears

Explanation: During labor (Intrapartum), maintaining strict asepsis during exams/delivery and properly repairing tears (closing portals of entry) are key preventive measures. UTI treatment is usually antenatal. Engorged breasts is postpartum.

24.
Fetal indication for caesarean section includes
a) Cephalopelvic disproportion, locked twins
b) Cephalopelvic disproportion, shoulder presentation
c) Shoulder presentation, locked twins
d) Cephalopelvic disproportion, breech presentation
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Shoulder presentation, locked twins

Explanation:

  • Fetal Indications: Fetal distress, Cord prolapse, Malpresentations like Shoulder, Brow, Face (mento-posterior), Locked twins.
  • Maternal Indications: CPD, Pre-eclampsia, Previous CS.
Option C lists two purely fetal malpresentations/complications requiring CS. CPD involves the mother's pelvis.

25.
The transitional stages of normal stool of a breastfed newborn 3-4 days is
a) Greenish- yellow
b) Brownish
c) Whitish
d) Pinkish
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Greenish- yellow

Explanation:

  • Day 1-2: Meconium (Black/Dark Green, sticky).
  • Day 3-4: Transitional Stool (Greenish-yellow/brown, loose).
  • Day 5+: Milk Stool (Yellow/Mustard, seedy).

26.
Light for dates is defined as a baby who weighs
a) Below the 10th percentile of the expected weight for gestational stage
b) Below the 20th percentile of the expected weight for gestational stage
c) Below the 5th percentile of the expected weight for gestational stage
d) Below the 15th percentile of the expected weight for gestational stage

27.
The most common microorganism that causes abnormal vaginal discharge in antenatal mother is
a) Candida albicans
b) Trichomoniasis
c) E.Choli
d) Pneumonae
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Candida albicans

Explanation: Candidiasis (Yeast infection) is very common in pregnancy due to increased estrogen levels which increase glycogen in vaginal cells, favoring yeast growth.

28.
Immediately the placenta is delivered, a midwife should
a) Congratulate the mother
b) Massage the uterus and expel clots
c) Feed the mother
d) Show the baby to the mother
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Massage the uterus and expel clots

Explanation: The immediate priority after placental delivery (in AMTSL) is to massage the uterus to ensure it is contracted (hard) and to prevent postpartum hemorrhage. Other actions follow.

29.
The management of a tight cord around the neck during delivery is
a) Clamping the cord with artery forceps and cutting in between
b) Pushing the body back into the uterus
c) Taking the mother for caesarian section
d) Just observe as the baby advance with each contraction
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Clamping the cord with artery forceps and cutting in between

Explanation: If the cord is too tight to slip over the head or shoulders, it must be clamped (two clamps) and cut immediately to allow delivery and prevent cord avulsion or hypoxia.

30.
Maternal death is defined as death that occurs
a) During pregnancy, intrapartum and within 42 days post-partum
b) During pregnancy, intrapartum and within 60 days post-partum
c) During pregnancy and intrapartum
d) Intrapartum and post-partum
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) During pregnancy, intrapartum and within 42 days post-partum

Explanation: Maternal mortality is defined as the death of a woman while pregnant or within 42 days of termination of pregnancy, irrespective of the duration and site of the pregnancy, from any cause related to or aggravated by the pregnancy or its management.

32.
Health message given to a post-natal mother who develops engorged breasts after normal delivery include
a) Feed the baby with other supplement, express the milk
b) Breastfeed the baby on demand, express the surplus milk
c) Warm compress on the breast, not to breast feed
d) Warm compress on the breasts, take pain medications
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Breastfeed the baby on demand, express the surplus milk

Explanation: The most effective treatment for engorgement is frequent removal of milk. The mother should breastfeed frequently (on demand) and express any remaining milk if breasts are still full/uncomfortable.

33.
Health message to share with a mother after repair of ruptured uterus is
a) To conceive before one-year ends
b) Not to conceive before two years end
c) To resume sexual activity immediately
d) To conceive immediately
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Not to conceive before two years end

Explanation: After a major uterine surgery like repair of rupture, it is recommended to delay the next pregnancy for at least 2 years to allow the scar to heal fully and regain strength. Future deliveries must be by CS.

34.
The anterior posterior measurements of pelvic brim is
a) 11 cm

c) 12 cm
d) 14 cm
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) 11 cm

Explanation: The Anatomical Conjugate (True Conjugate) diameter of the pelvic inlet (brim) is typically 11 cm. (Transverse is 13cm, Oblique is 12cm).

35.
The layers of the uterus are
a) The cavity, endometrium, internal os

c) Endometrium, cornua, internal os

Correct Answer: d) Endometrium, myometrium, perimetrium

36.
Sagittal suture separates the:
a) Frontal bones
b) Occipital bones
c) Parietal bones
d) Sphenoid bones
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Parietal bones

Explanation: The sagittal suture lies between the two parietal bones. The frontal suture lies between frontal bones. Coronal between frontal/parietal. Lambdoid between parietal/occipital.

37.
Cephalhaematoma is
a) Present at birth
b) Not present at birth
c) Intracranial effusion of blood
d) Pit on pressure and ross the suture line
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Not present at birth (appears hours later)

Explanation: Cephalhematoma is a subperiosteal collection of blood. It typically appears 12-24 hours after birth (unlike Caput which is present at birth). It does NOT cross suture lines. "Pit on pressure" refers to edema (Caput).

Explanation: The rooting reflex is elicited by stroking the newborn's cheek or corner of the mouth; the baby turns the head toward the stimulus and opens the mouth to find the nipple.

Section 2: Short Answer Questions (40 Marks)

1.
Explain the THREE major components charted in a partograph that depict the well-being of the mother (6 marks)
Show Answer

Answer:

  1. Vital Signs:
    • Blood Pressure: Recorded every 4 hours to monitor for pre-eclampsia or shock.
    • Pulse: Recorded every 30 minutes to assess hemodynamic status.
    • Temperature: Recorded every 2 hours to detect infection/fever.
  2. Urinalysis:
    • Protein: To check for pre-eclampsia.
    • Acetone: To check for starvation/dehydration (ketosis).
    • Volume: To ensure bladder emptying (prevent obstruction).
  3. Drugs and IV Fluids:
    • Record of any Oxytocin, analgesics, antibiotics, or fluids given to manage the mother's condition.
2.
State FOUR assessment findings during a vaginal examination that indicate pelvic adequacy (4 marks)
Show Answer

Answer:

  1. Sacral Promontory: Cannot be reached with the examining finger (Diagonal conjugate >12.5cm).
  2. Sacral Curve: The sacrum is well curved (hollow), not flat.
  3. Ischial Spines: Blunt and not prominent (not encroaching on the cavity).
  4. Sidewalls: Straight or divergent (not convergent).
  5. Sub-pubic Arch: Wide (admits 2-3 fingers, angle >90 degrees).
3.
Explain FOUR indicators of the onset of second stage of labour (8 marks)
Show Answer

Answer:

  1. Full Cervical Dilatation: Confirmed 10cm on vaginal examination (Definitive sign).
  2. Urge to Push: Involuntary, uncontrollable bearing-down reflex (Ferguson reflex) due to head pressure on pelvic floor.
  3. Anal Pouting/Dilatation: The anus opens and perineum bulges during contractions.
  4. Gaping of the Vagina: The vaginal introitus opens, and the presenting part may become visible.
  5. Rupture of Membranes: Spontaneous rupture often occurs at the onset of the second stage.
  6. Change in Contractions: Contractions become stronger, longer, and expulsive in nature.
4.
State FOUR causes of prolonged labour (4 marks)
Show Answer

Answer:

Based on the "3 Ps":

  1. Power (Uterine Inefficiency): Hypotonic uterine contractions (Inertia), uncoordinated contractions.
  2. Passenger (Fetal factors): Malpresentation (Breech, Brow), Malposition (OP), Macrosomia, Congenital anomalies (Hydrocephalus).
  3. Passage (Pelvic factors): Cephalopelvic Disproportion (CPD), Contracted pelvis, Pelvic tumors/fibroids.
  4. Psychological: Maternal severe anxiety/fear causing catecholamine release inhibiting oxytocin.
5.
Define ectopic pregnancy (1 mark)
Show Answer

Answer:

Ectopic pregnancy is the implantation of a fertilized ovum (zygote) outside the uterine cavity, most commonly in the fallopian tube.

6.
Draw a well labeled diagram indicating five sites of ectopic pregnancy (5 marks)

The diagram should illustrate the uterus, fallopian tubes, and ovaries, marking these sites:

7.
Explain FOUR types of spontaneous abortions (4 marks)
Show Answer

Answer:

  1. Threatened Abortion: Vaginal bleeding with a closed cervix; pregnancy may continue.
  2. Inevitable Abortion: Vaginal bleeding with an open cervix; pregnancy loss is unavoidable.
  3. Incomplete Abortion: Partial expulsion of products of conception; some tissue (usually placenta) remains.
  4. Complete Abortion: Total expulsion of all products of conception.
  5. Missed Abortion: Fetus dies but is retained in utero for weeks.
  6. Septic Abortion: Any abortion complicated by infection.
8.
State FOUR effects of diabetes in pregnancy (4 marks)
Show Answer

Answer:

Maternal Effects:

  • Increased risk of Pre-eclampsia/Hypertension.
  • Polyhydramnios (Excess amniotic fluid).
  • Recurrent infections (UTIs, Candidiasis).
  • Obstructed labor (due to big baby).

Fetal/Neonatal Effects:

  • Macrosomia (Big baby).
  • Congenital anomalies (Neural tube, Cardiac).
  • Neonatal Hypoglycemia.
  • Respiratory Distress Syndrome.

Section 3: Long Answer Question (20 Marks)

1.
Hypertensive disorders in pregnancy could be fatal if prompt and appropriate management is not instituted to a pregnant woman with it.

a) Briefly explain THREE clinical features of pre-eclampsia (6 marks)

Show Answer

Answer:

  1. Hypertension: Blood pressure ≥ 140/90 mmHg on two occasions at least 4 hours apart after 20 weeks of gestation in a previously normotensive woman.
  2. Proteinuria: Presence of protein in urine (≥ 300mg/24h or ≥ ++ on dipstick), indicating renal involvement/glomerular damage.
  3. Pathological Edema: Sudden, excessive swelling, particularly of the face and hands (not just dependent ankle edema), indicating fluid retention and capillary leak.
  4. Other features (Severe): Severe frontal headache, Visual disturbances (blurring/flashing lights), Epigastric pain.

b) Briefly explain TWO effects of pre-eclampsia on the mother (4 marks)

Show Answer

Answer:

  1. Eclampsia: Progression to generalized tonic-clonic seizures, which can lead to hypoxia, trauma, and death.
  2. HELLP Syndrome: A severe variant involving Hemolysis, Elevated Liver enzymes, and Low Platelets, causing liver rupture or failure and bleeding disorders.
  3. Placental Abruption: Premature separation of the placenta due to high pressure, causing severe hemorrhage and shock.
  4. Renal Failure: Acute kidney injury due to vasospasm and glomerular damage.

c) Explain FIVE reasons why it is important for the midwife to look out for the impending signs of eclampsia in a client with pre-eclampsia (10 marks)

Show Answer

Answer:

  1. To initiate timely treatment (MgSO4): Identifying impending signs (severe headache, visual disturbances, epigastric pain, hyperreflexia) allows immediate administration of Magnesium Sulphate to prevent the onset of convulsions.
  2. To prevent Maternal Mortality: Eclampsia is a leading cause of maternal death due to intracranial hemorrhage, aspiration, or cardiac arrest. Early detection and intervention save lives.
  3. To expedite delivery: Impending eclampsia is an indication for stabilization and immediate delivery (the only cure), preventing further deterioration of maternal condition.
  4. To prevent Fetal Distress/Demise: Seizures cause severe maternal and fetal hypoxia. Preventing seizures protects the fetus from hypoxic brain injury or death.
  5. To ensure safety/Prevent injury: Recognizing impending signs allows the midwife to nurse the patient in a safe environment (quiet room, cot sides up), protecting her from physical injury if a seizure were to occur.
