Section 1: Multiple Choice Questions (40 Marks)

1.
In shoulder dystocia the cardinal sign is:
a) No fetal advancement
b) Turtule sign
c) Macrosomic baby
d) face flushing
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Turtule sign (Turtle sign)

Explanation: The "Turtle sign" is the cardinal diagnostic sign of shoulder dystocia. It occurs when the fetal head retracts against the maternal perineum after delivery, resembling a turtle pulling its head back into its shell. This happens because the anterior shoulder is impacted behind the symphysis pubis.

2.
Zavanelli is one of the last resort maneuver in shoulder dystocia, it means:
a) Cutting the fetal head
b) Breaking the symphysis
c) Returning the fetal head back to the uterus
d) Allowing normal labor after fetal death
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Returning the fetal head back to the uterus

Explanation: The Zavanelli maneuver involves cephalic replacement (pushing the fetal head back into the vagina and uterus) followed by an immediate Cesarean section. It is a desperate, last-resort measure when vaginal delivery is impossible.

3.
The denominator in face presentation is:
a) Sacrum
b) Mentum
c) Occiput
d) Sinciput
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Mentum

Explanation: In face presentation, the head is fully extended, and the chin (Mentum) acts as the denominator (the reference point on the presenting part).

  • Sacrum: Denominator for Breech.

  • Sinciput/Frontal: Denominator for Brow.

6.
The management of a mother in first stage of labour with placenta previa type two posterior is:
a) Allow progress to normal vaginal birth
b) Emergency Cesarean Section
c) Elective Cesarean Section
d) Await the obstetrician to make decision
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Elective Cesarean Section (or b if bleeding actively/in labor)

Explanation: Type 2 Posterior Placenta Previa reduces the anterior-posterior diameter of the pelvic inlet and prevents the fetal head from engaging. A posterior placenta previa sits on the sacral promontory, obstructing descent. Vaginal delivery is dangerous. Since she is already in the first stage of labor (implying contractions have started), an Emergency Cesarean Section is typically indicated to prevent hemorrhage, though ideally, this would have been an Elective CS scheduled before labor.

Note: Given the options, if she is already in labor, it becomes an emergency situation, but the clinical indication is for CS.

7.
During an antenatal clinic the mother reports painless per vaginal bleeding. The most likely diagnosis would be
a) Placenta previa
b) Placenta abruption
c) Ruptured vasa previa
d) Placenta acretta
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Placenta previa

Explanation: The hallmark sign of Placenta Previa is painless, bright red vaginal bleeding, often recurrent. Placenta abruption typically presents with painful bleeding and a tense, tender uterus.

8.
The hormonal factors responsible for uterine contractions at the onset of labour are:
a) Increase release of oxytocin and progesterone from decidua at term
b) Formation of oxytocin and oestrogen in the fetal circulation
c) Rise of oxytocin and oestrogen in the fetal circulation
d) Optimum levels of oestrogen and rise of prostaglandins in maternal circulation
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Optimum levels of oestrogen and rise of prostaglandins in maternal circulation

Explanation: The onset of labor is complex. Key changes include:

  • Progesterone withdrawal: Progesterone (which relaxes the uterus) levels drop relative to estrogen.
  • Estrogen dominance: Increases gap junctions and oxytocin receptors.
  • Prostaglandins: Synthesis increases, causing cervical ripening and contractions.
  • Oxytocin: Receptors increase, though blood levels might not rise until late labor.

Note: Option C focuses on fetal circulation, but maternal prostaglandin/estrogen balance is the primary driver of uterine activation.

9.
Continuity of care of a postnatal mother after discharge mainly depends on:-
a) The number of postnatal clinic the midwife can make
b) The availability of health facility
c) The ability of the midwife to detect problems and intervene
d) The adequate health messages shared with the mother
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) The adequate health messages shared with the mother

Explanation: While the midwife's skill (C) is crucial, continuity of care at home relies heavily on the mother's education. If she receives adequate health messages (discharge plan), she knows how to care for herself and the baby and when to seek help (danger signs), effectively continuing the care initiated at the facility.

10.
Which one of the following is an indication for giving cardiopulmonary resuscitation in an adult?
a) Absence of breathing in the presence of an open airway
b) Presence of circulation in the absence of breathing
c) Presence of circulation in the presence of weak chest movements
d) Patient subconscious in the absence of a defibrillator
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Absence of breathing in the presence of an open airway (implying cardiac arrest/apnea)

Explanation: CPR is indicated when a patient is unresponsive and not breathing (or only gasping). While technically CPR involves chest compressions for cardiac arrest (absence of circulation), absence of breathing in an unresponsive patient is the trigger to start BLS assessment/CPR.

  • If circulation is present but breathing is absent (Option B), the treatment is Rescue Breathing, not full CPR (compressions).
  • However, in lay/basic settings, "CPR" is often the term used for the response to an unresponsive, non-breathing victim.
11.
The reproductive tract fistulae are caused by
a) Prolonged labor, Multiple pregnancy
b) Un attended obstructed labor, prolonged labor
c) Surgical injuries, IUCD
d) Malpresentation, IUCD
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Un attended obstructed labor, prolonged labor

Explanation: The leading cause of obstetric fistula (VVF/RVF) in developing countries is prolonged, obstructed labor. The compression of soft tissues between the fetal head and the maternal pelvis leads to ischemia and necrosis, resulting in a fistula.

12.
In which of the following conditions will a midwife consider performing a vacuum extraction in a term pregnancy with a cephalic presentation?
a) Intrauterine fetal death at full cervical dilatation
b) Severe pre-eclampsia at full cervical dilatation
c) Fetal distress at 8cm cervical dilatation
d) Cord prolapse at 5cm cervical dilatation
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Severe pre-eclampsia at full cervical dilatation

Explanation: Vacuum extraction requires the cervix to be fully dilated (10cm). It is used to shorten the second stage of labor in conditions like severe pre-eclampsia (to avoid maternal effort/BP spikes) or maternal exhaustion.

  • c) and d) are incorrect because the cervix is not fully dilated (vacuum is contraindicated).
  • a) Is controversial; usually, destructive operations or simple expulsion are preferred for IUFD, but vacuum can be used. However, protecting the mother in pre-eclampsia is a classic indication for assisted delivery.
13.
The precaution that a midwife should take to avoid retained placenta is: -
a) Discourage premature pushing during first stage
b) Administer intravenous fluids during childbirth
c) Administration of oxytocin following childbirth
d) Administer prophylactic antibiotics during labor
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Administration of oxytocin following childbirth

Explanation: This is a component of Active Management of the Third Stage of Labor (AMTSL). AMTSL (oxytocin, controlled cord traction, uterine massage) significantly reduces the risk of postpartum hemorrhage and retained placenta compared to expectant management.

14.
Mrs. X was admitted at the hospital at 9 a.m and diagnosed with features of severe pre-eclampsia and started on the recommended Magnesium Sulphate as per national protocols. She was monitored and had a successful spontaneous vaginal birth at 1p.m though she experienced a fit at 4 p. m while still on the Magnesium Sulphate treatment. Which of the following is the correct management option for Mrs. X?
a) Continue with Magnesium Sulphate maintenance dose and stop at 9am the following day
b) Continue with Magnesium Sulphate maintenance dose and stop at 1pm the following day
c) Continue with Magnesium Sulphate maintenance dose and stop at 4pm the following day
d) Continue with Magnesium Sulphate maintenance dose and stop at 1am the following day
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Continue with Magnesium Sulphate maintenance dose and stop at 4pm the following day

Explanation: The protocol for Magnesium Sulphate maintenance in eclampsia is to continue treatment for 24 hours after delivery OR 24 hours after the last fit, whichever is later. Since she had a fit at 4 p.m., treatment must continue until 4 p.m. the next day.

15.
During monitoring of labor progress of a woman using a partograph, which of the following fetal wellbeing indicators are reassuring to a midwife?
a) Fetal heart rate 120-160 beats/minute, Moulding +, Clear liquor
b) Fetal heart rate 160-180 beats/minute, Moulding 2+, Fetal descent 2/5
c) Moulding 3+, Fetal heart rate 110 beats/minute, Meconium stained liquor
d) Meconium stained liquor, Moulding 2+, Fetal descent 0/5
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Fetal heart rate 120-160 beats/minute, Moulding +, Clear liquor

Explanation: Reassuring signs include:

  • FHR: 110-160 bpm (Normal).
  • Liquor: Clear (No meconium).
  • Moulding: 0 or + (Bones separated or touching but not overlapping).

Moulding 2+ or 3+ and Meconium are signs of distress or obstruction.

16.
One of the following statement is true regarding obstetric hemorrhages
a) In every case of placental abruption, be prepared for postpartum hemorrhage
b) Conduct digital vaginal examination first to diagnose placenta praevia
c) If cervix is fully dilated but fetal heart rate is abnormal, do not attempt vaginal delivery
d) Always commence IV fluids then secure airway and breathing in hemorrhage
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) In every case of placental abruption, be prepared for postpartum hemorrhage

Explanation:

  • a) True: Abruption consumes clotting factors (DIC) and causes Couvelaire uterus (atony), leading to high PPH risk.
  • b) False: Digital exam is CONTRAINDICATED in placenta previa (provokes massive bleeding).
  • c) False: If fully dilated, expedited vaginal delivery (vacuum/forceps) is often faster than CS for distress.
  • d) False: ABC rule applies (Airway, Breathing, then Circulation/Fluids).
17.
Which of the following statements is true about newborn resuscitation?
a) Stimulate newborn using a dry warm cloth then clear meconium-filled airway
b) Start chest compressions if newborn is not breathing with open airway immediately
c) Continue ventilation for 1 minute if heart rate is more than 60 beats/minute
d) Consider chest compressions if heart rate is more than 60 but less than 100/minute
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Stimulate newborn using a dry warm cloth then clear meconium-filled airway

Explanation: The initial steps are: Dry, Warm, Stimulate, and Position Airway. Suctioning is reserved for obstruction (meconium). Compressions are only started if HR < 60 despite adequate ventilation.

18.
Oral sex is not recommended for mother during their postpartum period because of:
a) Possible abdominal pain
b) Infection to the spouse
c) Embolism due to blowing air in to uterus
d) Pain in the perineum
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Embolism due to blowing air in to uterus

Explanation: Forceful blowing of air into the vagina (cunnilingus) during pregnancy or postpartum can cause a fatal air embolism. The air enters the exposed venous sinuses of the placental site.

20.
A midwife should expect a woman who develops deep venous thrombosis to be given heparin sodium to:-
a) Prevent further enlargement of the vein
b) Reduce calf tissue oedema
c) Improve the venous return
d) Dissolve the thrombosis
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Prevent further enlargement of the vein (clot extension)

Explanation: Heparin is an anticoagulant. It does not dissolve existing clots (thrombolytics do that), but it prevents the clot from getting bigger (extension) and prevents new clots from forming, allowing the body's natural mechanisms to lyse the clot over time.

21.
First stage of labour is defined as a period from onset of:-
a) Labour pains to full dilatation
b) Labour pains to full crowning of the head
c) True labour to birth of the baby
d) True labour to full dilation of the cervix
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) True labour to full dilation of the cervix

Explanation: The first stage of labor begins with the onset of regular, painful uterine contractions (true labor) that cause cervical change and ends when the cervix is fully dilated (10cm).

22.
A condition in which a midwife prepares a mother for emergency cesarean section include:-
a) Previous cesarean section
b) Cord presentation
c) Twin pregnancy
d) Meconium stained amniotic fluid
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Cord presentation

Explanation: Cord presentation (where the cord lies below the presenting part with intact membranes) or Cord Prolapse (after rupture) is an absolute obstetric emergency requiring immediate delivery, usually by C-section, to prevent cord compression and fetal asphyxia.

23.
A woman had rupture of the membranes 24 hours prior to birth, the midwife should assess this mother for:-
a) Elevate temperatures
b) Proteinuria
c) Chorioamnoitis
d) Persistent drainage of liqour
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Chorioamnoitis (and check for 'a' Elevated temperature as a sign)

Explanation: Prolonged rupture of membranes (>18-24 hours) is a major risk factor for ascending infection, leading to Chorioamnionitis. The midwife assesses for signs like fever, maternal/fetal tachycardia, and foul-smelling liquor.

24.
Administration of magnesium sulphate in pregnancy is known to have the following advantages: -
a) Reduces the rate of respiratory distress, Neural tube protection, maternal convulsions
b) Reduces the rate of cerebral palsy, Neural tube protection, intraventricular hemorrhage
c) Reduces the rate of cerebral palsy, Neural tube protection, maternal convulsions
d) Reduces the rate of preterm labor, Neural tube protection, maternal convulsions
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Reduces the rate of cerebral palsy, Neural tube protection (Neuroprotection), intraventricular hemorrhage

Explanation: Magnesium Sulphate is used for seizure prophylaxis in eclampsia (maternal convulsions) AND for fetal neuroprotection in preterm births, reducing the risk of Cerebral Palsy and IVH. It is NOT primarily a tocolytic for stopping preterm labor long-term.

Note: The options are slightly jumbled, but B highlights the fetal neuroprotective benefits which are distinct advantages beyond just seizure control. If emphasizing eclampsia, it prevents convulsions.

25.
The following is the best way to diagnose invasive placenta like placenta percreta
a) Magnetic Resonance Imaging
b) Trans Vaginal Ultrasound
c) Trans Abdominal Ultrasound
d) Clinical features
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Magnetic Resonance Imaging (MRI)

Explanation: While Ultrasound is the initial screening tool, MRI is the gold standard for defining the depth of invasion (accreta, increta, percreta) and involvement of adjacent organs (like the bladder) when diagnosis is unclear on ultrasound.

27.
Delayed cord clamping for one to three minutes after birth of baby:
a) Allows blood in cord and placenta to push pumped to the mother
b) Increase the risk of transmission of HIV from mother to child
c) Increase the risk of PPH
d) Decreases neonatal risk of anaemia

Correct Answer: d) Decreases neonatal risk of anaemia

Explanation: Delayed cord clamping allows placental transfusion, increasing the infant's iron stores and reducing the risk of iron deficiency anemia in infancy.

28.
How frequent should the fetal heart rate be assessed for a mother in second stage?
a) After every 15 minutes
b) After every contraction
c) After every 30 minutes
d) Not of any importance for the fetus is almost out
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) After every contraction (or every 5 minutes)

29.
The nursing diagnosis that would be given priority in the care plan of a newborn at one hour age is
a) Risk for infection
b) Ineffective thermoregulation
c) Impaired skin integrity
d) Altered nutrition
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Ineffective thermoregulation

Explanation: Newborns are prone to rapid heat loss (evaporation, conduction, convection, radiation) immediately after birth. Hypothermia can lead to metabolic acidosis and hypoglycemia. Maintaining temperature is a critical priority in the first hour ("Golden Hour").

31.
The laboratory test likely to be omitted from the routine antenatal care without jeopardizing the health of the mother and fetus is:
a) Serological test for syphilis

32.
The temporary structure in the fetal circulation that shunts blood around the lungs is called?
a) Ductus arteriosus
b) Ductus venosus
c) Foramen ovale
d) Ligamentum venosus
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Ductus arteriosus

Explanation: The Ductus Arteriosus connects the pulmonary artery to the aorta, bypassing the fluid-filled lungs. (Note: Foramen Ovale also bypasses lungs by shunting right atrium to left atrium, but Ductus Arteriosus is the major shunt "around" the lungs from the pulmonary trunk).

35.
The clinical problems of small for dates babies include:-

Correct Answer: c) Temperature instability (Hypothermia)

37.
Infant born to diabetic mother is likely to suffer from:-
a) Respiratory distress syndrome, hypoglycaemia
b) Respiratory distress syndrome, hyperglycaemia
c) Respiratory distress syndrome, hypercalcaemia

41.
State five (5) causes of atonic uterus (5 marks)
Show Answer

Answer:

  1. Overdistension of the uterus: Multiple pregnancy, Polyhydramnios, Macrosomia.
  2. Grand Multiparity: Weakened uterine muscle tone from repeated pregnancies.
  3. Prolonged Labor: Muscle exhaustion.
  4. Precipitate Labor: Very rapid labor can lead to shock and atony.
  5. Use of drugs: Prolonged use of Oxytocin (receptor downregulation), Magnesium Sulphate, or General Anesthesia.
  6. Full Bladder: Prevents contraction.
  7. Placenta issues: Placenta previa or retained tissue.
42.
State the four (4) types of placenta previa (4 marks)
Show Answer

Answer:

  1. Type I (Lateral): The placenta is implanted in the lower segment but does not reach the internal os.
  2. Type II (Marginal): The placental edge reaches the margin of the internal os but does not cover it.
  3. Type III (Partial): The placenta partially covers the internal os when the cervix is closed, but covers less when dilated.
  4. Type IV (Complete/Central): The placenta completely covers the internal os even when fully dilated.

47.
State five (5) variables assessed in apgar scoring that has a score of 10 (5 marks)
Show Answer

Answer:

To achieve a score of 10 (2 points per variable), the findings are:

  1. Appearance (Color): Pink all over (No cyanosis).
  2. Pulse (Heart Rate): > 100 beats per minute.
  3. Grimace (Reflex Irritability): Cry or active withdrawal on stimulation/suction.
  4. Activity (Muscle Tone): Active movement, well flexed.
  5. Respiration: Good, strong cry.
48.
Outline four (4) types of perineal trauma (4 marks)
Show Answer

Answer:

  1. First Degree Tear: Injury to skin of the fourchette and vaginal mucosa only.
  2. Second Degree Tear: Injury to perineal muscles but not the anal sphincter.
  3. Third Degree Tear: Injury to the perineum involving the anal sphincter complex (EAS/IAS).
  4. Fourth Degree Tear: Injury involving the anal sphincter and the anal epithelium (rectal mucosa).
  5. Other types: Episiotomy, Sulcus tears, Anterior labial tears.

Section 3: Long Answer Question (20 Marks)

49.
Mrs Y para 6+ 0 who is in second stage of labor develops sudden onset of maternal respiratory distress. A diagnosis of amniotic fluid embolism is made:

a) Define amniotic fluid embolism (1 mark)

Show Answer

Answer:

Amniotic Fluid Embolism (AFE) is a rare but catastrophic obstetric emergency where amniotic fluid, fetal cells, hair, or other debris enter the maternal pulmonary circulation, causing sudden cardiorespiratory collapse.

b) State five (5) predisposing factors to amniotic fluid embolism (5 marks)

Show Answer

Answer:

  1. Advanced Maternal Age (usually >35 years).
  2. Multiparity (High parity like Mrs. Y).
  3. Tumultuous/Hypertonic Labor (Strong, frequent contractions).
  4. Induction of Labor (Use of Oxytocin).
  5. Placental Abnormalities (Placenta Previa, Abruption).
  6. Intrauterine Fetal Death (IUFD).
  7. Operative Delivery (C-Section or Forceps).

c) Describe the specific management Mrs. Y would receive until she is stable (8 marks)

Show Answer

Answer:

Management is supportive and focuses on Resuscitation (A-B-C):

  1. Call for Help: Activate the emergency response team (Obstetrician, Anesthetist, Pediatrician).
  2. Airway & Breathing: Administer high-flow oxygen (100%) via non-rebreather mask or intubate and ventilate if necessary to treat hypoxia.
  3. Circulation:
    • Start CPR immediately if cardiac arrest occurs.
    • Secure 2 large-bore IV lines.
    • Rapid fluid resuscitation with crystalloids/colloids to support blood pressure.
    • Administer vasopressors (e.g., Epinephrine, Norepinephrine) as needed for hypotension.
  4. Delivery: Expedite delivery of the fetus. If in cardiac arrest, perform Perimortem Cesarean Section within 4-5 minutes to relieve aortocaval compression and improve maternal resuscitation chances.
  5. Blood Products: Transfuse Packed Red Blood Cells, Fresh Frozen Plasma, and Platelets early to manage massive hemorrhage and DIC (Disseminated Intravascular Coagulation).
  6. Positioning: Tilt the mother 30 degrees to the left to prevent supine hypotension.
  7. Monitoring: Continuous monitoring of vitals, O2 saturation, and urine output. Transfer to ICU when feasible.

d) Explain three (3) complications that Mrs. Y may develop (6 marks)

Show Answer

Answer:

  1. Disseminated Intravascular Coagulation (DIC): AFE triggers a massive inflammatory response and consumption of clotting factors, leading to uncontrollable bleeding from IV sites, the uterus, and incision sites.
  2. Acute Renal Failure: Due to prolonged hypotension (shock) and hypoperfusion of the kidneys, or microthrombi from DIC clogging renal vessels.
  3. Adult Respiratory Distress Syndrome (ARDS) / Pulmonary Edema: Damage to the lung capillaries causes fluid to leak into the alveoli, causing severe hypoxia even after the initial event.
  4. Sheehan's Syndrome: Pituitary necrosis due to severe shock/blood loss, leading to hormonal deficiencies later (e.g., failure to lactate).
  5. Maternal Death: High mortality rate due to cardiovascular collapse.
