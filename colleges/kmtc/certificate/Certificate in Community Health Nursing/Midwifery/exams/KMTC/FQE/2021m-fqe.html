Part 1 - Multiple Choice Questions (40 Marks)

1.
A midwife diagnoses pregnancy when:
a) The woman complains of lower abdominal pain
b) There is amenorrhea
c) Presence of human chorionic gonadotrophin hormone in urine
d) Frequency of micturition
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Presence of human chorionic gonadotrophin hormone in urine

Explanation: While amenorrhea (b) and frequency of micturition (d) are presumptive signs, and abdominal pain (a) is non-specific, the detection of hCG in urine is a probable sign of pregnancy used for diagnosis in clinical settings (UPT). It is the most reliable diagnostic indicator among the choices provided.

2.
The reproductive organs that form the external female genitalia include:
a) Labia majora, Mons pubis, Cervix
b) Clitoris, Vestibule, Labia minora
c) Vagina, Labia minora, Cervix
d) Vestibule, Vagina, Clitoris
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Clitoris, Vestibule, Labia minora

Explanation: The external genitalia (vulva) includes the mons pubis, labia majora, labia minora, clitoris, and vestibule.

  • Options (a) and (c) are incorrect because the Cervix is an internal organ.
  • Option (d) and (c) are incorrect because the Vagina is an internal organ (the vaginal orifice is external, but the vagina itself is internal).

3.
The innominate bone consist of:
a) Ileum, Ischium, Pubis
b) Pubis, Coccyx, Ileum
c) Ischium, Sacrum, Coccyx
d) Sacrum, Ileum, Ischium
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Ileum, Ischium, Pubis

Explanation: The innominate bone (hip bone) is formed by the fusion of three bones: the Ilium (note spelling in medical terms is Ilium, though paper says Ileum which is small intestine, this is a common typo in exams), Ischium, and Pubis. The Sacrum and Coccyx are separate bones of the pelvis.

5.
The marginal insertion of the umbilical cord is referred to as:
a) Velamentous
b) Succeturiate
c) Battledore
d) Circumvallate
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Battledore

Explanation:

  • Battledore: The cord is inserted at the very margin (edge) of the placenta, resembling a table tennis bat.
  • Velamentous: Cord inserts into the membranes, and vessels run through membranes to the placenta.
  • Succenturiate: An extra placental lobe.
  • Circumvallate: A ring of membranes on the fetal surface.

6.
The growth and maturation of graafian follicle is determined by:
a) Luteinizing hormone
b) Follicle stimulating hormone
c) Progesterone
d) Oestrogen
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Follicle stimulating hormone

Explanation: FSH (Follicle Stimulating Hormone) from the anterior pituitary stimulates the growth and maturation of ovarian follicles. LH triggers ovulation. Progesterone is dominant in the luteal phase.

12.
Mrs. Tue aged 35 years para 0+0 gravida 1 comes to ANC Clinic. Her last menstrual period is 12/4/2019. Her expected date of delivery is:
a) 19/7/19
b) 21/7/20
c) 21/1/20
d) 19/1/20
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) 19/1/20

Calculation (Naegele's Rule):

  • LMP: 12/4/2019 (12th April 2019)
  • Add 7 days to days: 12 + 7 = 19
  • Subtract 3 months from month: 4 - 3 = 1 (January)
  • Add 1 year to year: 2019 + 1 = 2020
  • Result: 19/1/2020
13.
Folic acid supplements given during the preconception and antenatal period is for prevention of :
a) Microcytic anaemia
b) Neural tube defects
c) Nutritional anaemia
d) Maternal Congenital abnormalities
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Neural tube defects

Explanation: Folic acid is critical in the first trimester (and preconception) to close the neural tube, preventing defects like spina bifida and anencephaly. (Note: Iron prevents microcytic anemia; B12/Folate deficiency causes Macrocytic/Megaloblastic anemia, but the primary reason for Folic Acid emphasis is NTDs).

14.
First stage of labour is considered to have occurred from:
a) Dilatation of the cervical os
b) Onset of labour until full dilation of the cervix
c) Contractions of the uterus
d) Contractions until delivery of the baby
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Onset of labour until full dilation of the cervix

Explanation: The first stage of labor is defined as the period from the onset of true labor contractions (leading to cervical change) until the cervix is fully dilated (10cm).

16.
The purpose of frequent emptying of urinary bladder during labour is:
a) To assess urinary output
b) Allow descent of presenting part
c) Prevent urinary bladder damage in the process of delivery
d) Augment labour
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Allow descent of presenting part

Explanation: A full bladder occupies space in the pelvis, obstructing the descent of the fetal head, inhibiting efficient uterine contractions, and increasing the risk of postpartum hemorrhage. While preventing damage (c) is also true, the primary obstetric management goal is facilitating descent.

17.
Following delivery of the fetal head , the immediate action of the midwife is:
a) Encourage the mother to push with every contraction
b) Continue supporting the perineum to prevent injury
c) Ask the mother to breathe by mouth as you check the cord round the neck
d) Cut the cord immediately
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Ask the mother to breathe by mouth (pant) as you check the cord round the neck

Explanation: Once the head is born, the midwife checks for a nuchal cord (cord around the neck). To prevent the mother from pushing the body out before this check is done (which could tighten the cord), she is asked to pant/breathe through the mouth.

18.
In the mechanism of labour, the sequence of labour follows:
a) Flexion, restitution, Lateral flexion
b) Internal rotation, Complete flexion, Crowning
c) External rotation, Lateral flexion, Restitution
d) Complete flexion, Internal rotation, Crowning
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Complete flexion, Internal rotation, Crowning

Explanation: The correct sequence for Vertex presentation is: Descent -> Flexion -> Internal Rotation -> Extension (Crowning occurs here) -> Restitution -> External Rotation -> Lateral Flexion. Option D lists three correct sequential steps.

21.
The hormone responsible initiation of lactation is:
a) Oxytocin
b) Prolactin
c) Oestrogen
d) Progesterone
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Prolactin

Explanation: Prolactin (Anterior Pituitary) is responsible for milk production (initiation and maintenance). Oxytocin (Posterior Pituitary) is responsible for the ejection reflex (let-down). The fall in Estrogen/Progesterone triggers Prolactin release.

22.
Pain after delivery as the baby breastfeeds is associated with:
a) Infection of the reproductive organs
b) Involution of the uterus
c) Injury to the reproductive system
d) Inadequate supply analgesics
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Involution of the uterus (Afterpains)

Explanation: Breastfeeding releases Oxytocin, which causes uterine contractions (afterpains). These contractions facilitate involution (return of uterus to pre-pregnant size) and prevent hemorrhage.

24.
One of the following is an indication of vacuum extraction:
a) High head
b) Rigid perineum
c) Fetal distress in second stage
d) Cephalo - pelvic dislocation
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Fetal distress in second stage

Explanation: Vacuum is indicated to shorten the second stage of labor for fetal distress (when the head is engaged and cervix fully dilated).

  • a) High head is a Contraindication.
  • b) Rigid perineum might need Episiotomy, not necessarily vacuum.
  • d) Cephalo-pelvic disproportion (CPD) is a Contraindication.

25.
The midwife would prevent physiological Jaundice by:
a) Administering Vitamin K.
b) Phototherapy
c) Early breastfeeding
d) Providing warmth to the baby
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Early breastfeeding

Explanation: Early and frequent breastfeeding promotes bowel movements (passage of meconium), which reduces the enterohepatic circulation of bilirubin, helping to clear bilirubin from the body.

26.

a) The mothers blood is Rhesus Positive and the baby is Rhesus negative
b) The mothers blood group is O and the baby is A or B
c) The mothers’ blood group is Rhesus Negative and the father is unknown
d) The mother’s blood group is Rhesus negative and the baby is Rhesus positive
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) The mother’s blood group is Rhesus negative and the baby is Rhesus positive

Explanation: Rhesus isoimmunization occurs when an Rh-negative mother carries an Rh-positive fetus. Maternal antibodies form against fetal RBCs.

27.
The commonest causes of puerperal pyrexia are:
a) Pneumonia, malaria
b) Genital tract infection, malaria
c) Deep venous thrombosis, Breast abscess.
d) Mastitis, Pneumonia
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Genital tract infection, malaria

Explanation: Puerperal sepsis (genital tract infection) is a leading cause. In endemic zones like Kenya, Malaria is also a very common cause. Other causes include UTIs, Mastitis, etc.

28.
Excessive bleeding after delivery of the baby amounting to over 300mls of blood accompanied by shock is:
a) Antepartum haemorrhage
b) Anaphylactic shock
c) Postpartum haemorrhage
d) Placenta abruption
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Postpartum haemorrhage

Explanation: Bleeding after delivery is PPH. While the standard definition is >500ml (Vaginal) or >1000ml (CS), any amount causing hemodynamic instability (shock) is considered PPH clinically. (Note: 300ml is low for the definition, but "accompanied by shock" confirms the diagnosis).

Neonatal death occurs:

31.
Causes of pre-term labour include:
a) Multiple gestation, Placenta praevia, Congenital abnormality
b) Placenta abruption, Chorioamnitis, Oligohydrannios

32.

34.

35.

2.
Outline five (5) functions of the placenta (5 Marks)
Show Answer

Answer:

  1. Respiration: Gas exchange (O2 to fetus, CO2 to mother).
  2. Nutrition: Transfer of nutrients (glucose, amino acids) to fetus.
  3. Excretion: Removal of waste products (urea).
  4. Endocrine: Production of hormones (hCG, HPL, Estrogen, Progesterone).
  5. Protection/Barrier: Prevents some bacteria/viruses from crossing (though not all).
  6. Immunity: Transfer of maternal IgG antibodies.

  2. Confidential/Private: Ensuring privacy in consultation.
  3. Non-judgmental: Staff are trained to be respectful and supportive.
  4. Affordable: Free or low cost.
  5. Comprehensive: Offering a wide range of services (counseling, FP, STI treatment).
  6. Peer involvement: Involving youth in design/delivery.

Show Answer

5.
Explain three (3) roles of the midwife in the prevention of heat loss in the newborn (6 Marks)
Show Answer

Answer:

  1. Evaporation prevention: Immediately drying the baby thoroughly after birth and removing wet linens.
  2. Conduction prevention: Placing the baby on a warm surface (pre-warmed towel/resuscitaire) or Skin-to-Skin contact with mother.
  3. Convection prevention: Closing windows/doors to prevent drafts and covering the baby.
  4. Radiation prevention: Keeping the baby away from cold walls/windows and covering the head with a cap.

  5. Reflexes: Presence of primitive reflexes (Moro, Sucking, Rooting, Grasp).

  7. Leakage of fluid (ROM) before term.

Outline five (5) complications of breech presentation (5 Marks)
Show Answer

Answer:

  1. Cord Prolapse: Especially in footling breech.
  2. Birth Trauma: Fractures (humerus/clavicle), brachial plexus injury (Erb's palsy).
  3. Head Entrapment: The after-coming head gets stuck behind a partially dilated cervix.
  4. Birth Asphyxia: Due to cord compression or delay in delivery of the head.
  5. Premature Rupture of Membranes (PROM).
  6. Maternal Trauma: Lacerations from manipulation.

Part 111 - Long Answer Question (20 Marks)

1.
Mrs Dedeeto aged 30years old Para 0+0 gravida 1 is admitted to labour ward at 38 weeks gestation with complains of lower abdominal and back pain.

a) Explain three (3) signs of true labour (6 Marks)

Show Answer

Answer:

  1. Regular Uterine Contractions: Contractions that increase in frequency, duration, and intensity over time.
  2. Cervical Changes: Progressive effacement (thinning) and dilatation (opening) of the cervix.
  3. Show: Passage of the mucous plug mixed with blood (bloody show).
  4. Note: Rupture of membranes can happen but isn't a definitive sign of onset of true labor alone without contractions/cervical change.

b) Describe the admission process of Mrs. Dedeeto (12 Marks)

Show Answer

Answer:

1. Welcome and Rapport: Greet Mrs. Dedeeto, introduce self, offer a seat, and ensure privacy/comfort.

2. History Taking:

  • Bio-data: Confirm name, age (30), Parity (0+0), Gravida (1).
  • Present Pregnancy: LMP, EDD (confirm 38 weeks), ANC attendance, relevant tests (HIV, Syphilis, Hb).
  • History of Labor: Onset of pain, frequency, membrane status, show.
  • Medical/Surgical History: Allergies, chronic illnesses.

3. Physical Examination:

  • General: Vitals (BP, Pulse, Temp), Edema, Pallor.
  • Abdominal: Fundal height, Lie, Presentation, Descent (levels), Fetal Heart Rate (FHR), Contractions (strength/frequency).

4. Vaginal Examination (if not contraindicated):

  • Assess Cervical dilatation, effacement, station, membrane status, pelvic adequacy.

5. Investigation: Urine test (Protein/Sugar). Hb check if needed.

6. Documentation: Record all findings in the admission register and start the Partograph if in active labor (>4cm). Fill the Antenatal card/Labor record.

7. Care/Hygiene: Instruct to empty bladder, offer hospital gown if policy, orient to ward.

c) State two (2) nursing diagnoses in normal labour (2 Marks)

Show Answer

Answer:

  1. Acute Pain related to uterine contractions and cervical dilatation.
  2. Anxiety related to the labor process and outcome for the baby (especially as she is a Primigravida).
  3. Risk for Fluid Volume Deficit related to reduced intake and sweating/physical effort.
  4. Risk for Infection related to vaginal examinations and membrane rupture.
