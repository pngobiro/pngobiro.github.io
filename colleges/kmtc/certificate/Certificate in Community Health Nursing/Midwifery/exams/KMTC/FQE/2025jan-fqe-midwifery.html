Section 1: Multiple Choice Questions (40 Marks)

1.
Mrs A. who is in labour appears restless and says; am not certain if am going to go through this labour without complications. The midwife's appropriate nursing diagnosis will be;
A. Discomfort related to labour pains evidenced by verbalization
B. Anxiety related to outcome of labour evidenced by verbalisation
C. Knowledge deficit related to process of labour evidenced by woman asking many questions
D. Risk of injury related to the delivery process
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B. Anxiety related to outcome of labour evidenced by verbalisation

Explanation: The client's verbalization ("not certain if am going to go through this... without complications") specifically expresses fear and uncertainty about the outcome, which defines Anxiety.

2.
The rationale of doing vaginal examination when the membranes rupture during first stage of labour is to;
A. Confirm labour
B. Assess the adequacy of the pelvis
C. Assess cervical dilatation
D. Rule out cord prolapse
Show Answer

Correct Answer: D. Rule out cord prolapse

Explanation: When membranes rupture (SROM/ARM), there is a risk of the umbilical cord washing down in front of the presenting part (prolapse). Immediate VE is performed to exclude this emergency.

3.
The correct client education that will be given by the midwife to a pregnant mother in first stage of labour includes;
A. Rub the back and breath in and out during a contraction
B. Hold your breath and push with every contraction
C. Walk around when membranes rupture and push with every contraction
D. Rub the back and Walk around when membranes rupture
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A. Rub the back and breath in and out during a contraction

Explanation: In the first stage, breathing techniques (in and out) and back rubbing (effleurage/counter-pressure) help manage pain. Pushing (B) is contraindicated before full dilation. Walking is encouraged, but not "when membranes rupture" specifically without checking for cord prolapse first.

4.
History taken on time labour started, presence of show, state of membranes and strength of uterine contractions is;
A. Family and social
B. Past medical and surgical
C. Past obstetric
D. Present obstetric
Show Answer

Correct Answer: D. Present obstetric

Explanation: These details relate to the current (present) pregnancy and labour process.

5.
The true signs of labor, which indicate that your body is preparing for childbirth, include:
A. Progressive cervical dilation and presence of show
B. Irregular painless uterine contraction, contraction last 3-4 minutes
C. Progressive cervical dilation, contraction last 3-4 minutes
D. Presence of show, Irregular painless uterine contraction
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A. Progressive cervical dilation and presence of show

Explanation: True labour is characterized by regular, painful contractions that result in progressive cervical dilation and effacement, often accompanied by "show". Irregular/painless contractions describe false labour (Braxton Hicks).

6.
The stage of labour that starts from when the cervix is fully dilated until the baby is delivered is considered what stage:
A. First
B. Second
C. Third
D. Fourth
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B. Second

Explanation:
1st Stage: Onset of labour to full dilation.
2nd Stage: Full dilation to delivery of baby.
3rd Stage: Delivery of baby to delivery of placenta.
4th Stage: Observation period (1-2 hours postpartum).

7.
The circular constriction or contraction ring that forms around the uterus, often between the upper and lower segments known as the Bandl's ring is a sign of labour that is;
A. Obstructed
B. Precipitate
C. Prolonged
D. Premature
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A. Obstructed

Explanation: A Pathological Retraction Ring (Bandl's Ring) is a classic sign of obstructed labour, indicating imminent uterine rupture.

8.
State if the following statements are true or false;
A. Steps of mechanism of spontaneous vertex delivery include; Flexion of the head, internal rotation of the head, crowning of the head, extension of the head, restitution of the head/external rotation of the head, internal rotation of the shoulders, Lateral flexion of the body
B. Perinium gives attachment to the muscles of the pelvic floor.
Show Answer

Answer:

  • A. TRUE: This correctly lists the cardinal movements of labour in order.
  • B. TRUE: The perineal body is the central attachment point for the pelvic floor muscles.
9.
Placenta separation occurs after the baby is delivered, and the placenta detaches from the uterine wall to be expelled. The methods of placenta separation include:
A. Battledore, Shultze
B. Shultze, Matthew Duncan
C. Battledore, Velamentous
D. Matthew Duncan, Battledore,
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B. Shultze, Matthew Duncan

Explanation: Schultze (central separation - fetal side appears first) and Matthews Duncan (marginal separation - maternal side appears first) are the two mechanisms of separation. Battledore and Velamentous are cord abnormalities.

10.
When examining a normal placenta after delivery, healthcare providers typically look for specific characteristics to ensure it has functioned properly during pregnancy and has been expelled correctly. Normal findings during examination of a placenta include;
A. 18-22 lobes, infarcts
B. Maternal surface is dark red in colour, infarcts
C. 18-22 lobes, foetal surface is greyish white, shinny
D. Maternal surface is greyish white, shinny; Foetal surface is dark red in colour
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C. 18-22 lobes, foetal surface is greyish white, shinny

Explanation: A normal placenta has 15-20 (approx 18-22) cotyledons/lobes on the maternal surface. The fetal surface is covered by amnion, making it greyish-white and shiny. Infarcts are abnormal (necrosis).

11.
Lochia is the vaginal discharge that occurs after childbirth as the uterus sheds the tissue and blood that supported the pregnancy. The characteristics of a normal post-natal period lochia loss include;
A. Lochia rubra day 1-4, Profuse red lochia
B. Red lochia on day 10-14, Lochia serosa – For day 5-9
C. Lochia rubra day 1-4, Lochia serosa – For day 5-9
D. Profuse red lochia, Red lochia on day 10-14
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A. Lochia rubra day 1-4, Profuse red lochia

Explanation: Normal progression:
- Lochia Rubra (Red): Days 1-4.
- Lochia Serosa (Pink/Brown): Days 5-9.
- Lochia Alba (White/Yellow): Days 10-14+.
Option A correctly describes Rubra. Option C also lists correct timings but A is descriptive.

12.
To control other people by giving money for sex, giving good grades in exchange for sex, or even sexual abuse or rape refers to;
A. Sexuality
B. Sexualisation
C. Sex
D. Sexual intimacy
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B. Sexualisation (or Sexual Exploitation)

Explanation: This definition best fits "Sexual Exploitation". Among the given options, "Sexualisation" (making someone/something sexual) is the closest concept to objectification/commodity, though "Sexual Exploitation" would be the precise term.

13.
When a woman assumes the knee-chest position, she kneels on the bed or floor with her hips raised, and her chest close to the ground. The midwife assists a pregnant woman to Knee-chest position during management of;
A. Cord prolapse and cord presentation
B. Obstructed labour, Cord prolapse
C. Obstructed labour, prolonged labour
D. Prolonged labour, cord presentation
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A. Cord prolapse and cord presentation

Explanation: The knee-chest position uses gravity to relieve pressure of the presenting part on the umbilical cord, preventing fetal hypoxia in cord prolapse.

14.
Indicate if the following statements are True or False;
A. Lovset manoeuvre is used for breech delivery with extended legs
B. The aim of Mauriceau smellie veit manoeuvre is to increase extension of the after coming head in breech delivery
Show Answer

Answer:

  • A. FALSE/PARTIAL: Lovset manoeuvre is specifically used for the delivery of extended arms in breech, not just legs. (Legs are Pinard's).
  • B. FALSE: The aim of Mauriceau-Smellie-Veit is to maintain flexion of the head (not extension) to facilitate delivery.
15.
Induction of labor is the process of stimulating uterine contractions before the natural onset of labor, with the goal of achieving vaginal delivery. The contra-indications of induction include:
A. Post maturity, fetal hypoxia
B. Fetal hypoxia, polyhydramnios
C. Cephalopelvic disproportion, polyhydramnios
D. Cephalopelvic disproportion, fetal hypoxia
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C. Cephalopelvic disproportion, polyhydramnios (Note: CPD is absolute)

Explanation: CPD is an absolute contraindication. Fetal hypoxia (fetal distress) is also a major contraindication for induction (immediate CS preferred). Post maturity and Polyhydramnios are usually indications for induction.
Wait, re-evaluating D: CPD and Fetal Hypoxia are both strong contraindications. C has Polyhydramnios which is often an indication (to stabilize). D lists two strong contraindications.
Selection: D is the best answer as both are contraindications.

16.
The presenting part in labor refers to the part of the fetus that is nearest to the cervix and will enter the birth canal first. The presenting diameter in brow presentation is;
A. Submento bregmatic 9.5cm
B. Suboccipitobregmatic 9.5cm
C. Suboccipitofrontal 10cm
D. Mentovertical 13.5cm
Show Answer

Correct Answer: D. Mentovertical 13.5cm

Explanation: In Brow presentation, the head is midway between flexion and extension, presenting the Mentovertical diameter (13.5 cm), which is usually too large for vaginal delivery.

17.
A cesarean section (C-section) is a surgical procedure used to deliver a baby through incisions made in the mother's abdomen and uterus. The definite indications of caeserian section are;
A. Cephalopelvic disproportion, breech presentation
B. Cephalopelvic disproportion, multiparity
C. Breech presentation, antepartum haemorrhage
D. Antepartum haemorrhage, cephalopelvic disproportion
Show Answer

Correct Answer: D. Antepartum haemorrhage, cephalopelvic disproportion

Explanation: Severe APH (like Placenta Previa Type 3/4) and Major CPD are absolute/definite indications. Breech can sometimes be delivered vaginally.

18.
The menstrual cycle phase when growth and development of ovarian follicles takes place is;
A. Regenerative
B. Proliferative
C. Secretory
D. Menstrual flow
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B. Proliferative

Explanation: The Proliferative phase of the uterus corresponds to the Follicular phase of the ovary, where follicles grow under FSH influence.

19.
The hormone that is involved in the ejection of breast milk during lactation is;
A. Prolactin
B. Oestrogen
C. Oxytocin
D. Progesterone
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C. Oxytocin

Explanation: Oxytocin causes contraction of myoepithelial cells (Milk Ejection Reflex/Let-down). Prolactin stimulates milk production.

20.
You would expect the mother to report they can feel the fetal movements at how many weeks:
A. 8-12

C. 16-20
D. 4-8

21.
The pelvic joint that allows widening of the pelvic outlet by moving the coccyx backwards during delivery is;
A. Symphysis pubis
B. Sacroiliac
C. Sacro cocygeal
D. Sacral promontory

22.
You are assessing a mother in labour to determine progress of labour. The ideal pelvis for spontaneous vaginal delivery is;
A. Anthropoid
B. Palatypelloid

23.
In assessment of the pelvic outlet, the diameter that the midwife measures by fitting four knuckles between perineum and the ischial tuberosities is;
A. Bi-tuberous
B. Bi-spinous
C. Transverse
D. Sub-pubic arch

24.
You are in antenatal clinic and a pregnant woman tells you her last monthly period (LMP) was on 30/6/2021; the expected date of delivery (EDD) will be on;
A. 6/3/2022
B. 6/4/2022
C. 7/3/2022
D. 7/4/2022
Show Answer

Correct Answer: D. 7/4/2022

Explanation: Using Naegele's Rule: Add 7 days to days, subtract 3 months from months (or add 9 months), add 1 year to year.
30 + 7 = July 7th.
June (6) + 9 months = March... wait.
Calculation: 30/6/21.
+7 days = 7/7/21.
+9 months = April 7th 2022. (July->Aug->Sept->Oct->Nov->Dec->Jan->Feb->Mar->Apr).

27.
A Client who is managed with macdonald stitch to preserve pregnancy will be advised on;
A. Bed for at least 2-3 days, removal at 37 weeks
B. Abstain from coitus for 2-3, removal at 32 weeks
C. Total bedrest throughout pregnancy, removal at 37 weeks
D. Bed for at least 2-3 days, removal at 32 weeks

Correct Answer: A. Bed for at least 2-3 days, removal at 37 weeks

28.
A mother has been brought to the post-natal clinic with report of self-neglect and loss of interest in the newborn. The earliest sign of puerperal sepsis is usually;
A. Abdominal pains
B. Headache
C. Fever
D. Rigors
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C. Fever

29.
A post-natal mother complains of increasing full and painful breasts for the last twenty-four hours. The correct intervention will be:
A. Suspend breastfeeding, empty breast by expressing milk
B. Continue breast feeding, antibiotics
C. Continue breast feeding, take milk for culture and sensitivity
D. Suspend breastfeeding, taking milk for culture and sensitivity is unnecessary
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B. Continue breast feeding (Antibiotics if mastitis suspected, but 'Continue feeding' is key)

Explanation: For engorgement or early mastitis, the most important intervention is to remove milk effectively. This means continuing to breastfeed frequently. Suspending feeding worsens the condition.

Section 2: Short Answer Questions (40 Marks)

41.
You have been newly posted to the labour ward and many mothers are in labour at the same time. As a midwife explain three (3) factors that determine the outcome of labour (6 Marks)
Show Answer

Model Answer: The 3 Ps

  1. The Powers: Uterine contractions (strength, frequency, duration) and maternal bearing down efforts. Strong, coordinated contractions are needed for dilation and expulsion.
  2. The Passages: The maternal pelvis (bony pelvis size/shape) and soft tissues (cervix, vagina). The passage must be adequate for the fetus to pass through.
  3. The Passenger: The fetus (size, presentation, position, lie). A large baby or malposition can obstruct labour.
  4. (Bonus) Psyche: The mother's psychological state and ability to cope with pain.

44.
Explain three (3) signs the midwife will use to diagnose actual uterine rupture in a woman with obstructed labour (6 Marks)
Show Answer
  1. Cessation of Contractions: Sudden stopping of uterine contractions, often accompanied by relief of pain followed by continuous abdominal pain.
  2. Loss of Fetal Station: The presenting part recedes (goes back up) from the pelvis on vaginal exam.
  3. Signs of Shock: Maternal tachycardia, hypotension, and pallor due to internal hemorrhage.
  4. Fetal Distress/Death: Sudden loss of fetal heart rate.
  5. Abdominal Tenderness/Irregularity: Fetal parts may be felt easily under the skin (if extruded).

47.
While educating a couple on birth preparedness, outline four (4) advantages of Kangaroo Mother Care to the baby that you would expect them to adopt. (4 Marks)
Show Answer
  1. Thermal Control: Maintains baby's body temperature (prevents hypothermia) via body heat.
  2. Promotes Breastfeeding: Facilitates exclusive breastfeeding and weight gain.
  3. Infection Prevention: Reduces risk of nosocomial infections compared to incubators.
  4. Bonding: Enhances emotional bonding between mother and baby.
  5. Stability: Stabilizes heart rate and breathing.
48.
A mother in the second stage of labour starts to push the baby and refuses to cooperate with the midwife putting the life of the baby and mother at risk. Outline four (4) risks that the midwife would be concerned about related to the mothers uncooperative behaviour (4 Marks)
Show Answer
  1. Perineal Tears: Uncontrolled pushing can cause severe lacerations (3rd/4th degree tears).
  2. Fetal Distress/Hypoxia: Ineffective pushing prolongs the second stage, leading to fetal compromise.
  3. Maternal Exhaustion: Wasting energy prevents effective expulsion.
  4. Postpartum Hemorrhage: Trauma and prolonged labour increase risk of atony and bleeding.
  5. Intracranial Injury to Baby: Rapid, uncontrolled expulsion can cause injury.

Section 3: Long Answer Question (20 Marks)

49.
Mrs F a para 1 + 0, gravida 2 at 38 weeks, comes to labour ward in active stage of labour. After examination the midwife decides to admit the client and commence monitoring the labour and determines a spontaneous vertex delivery will take place. Describe the midwife's plan and interventions for this mother until the baby is born.
Show Answer

Management Plan for 2nd Stage of Labour:

1. Initial Assessment & Preparation:

  • Confirm full dilation (10cm) via vaginal exam.
  • Ensure bladder is empty.
  • Position Mrs. F comfortably (lithotomy, semi-sitting, or squatting).
  • Prepare delivery pack, sterile gloves, and resuscitation equipment.

2. Monitoring:

  • Maternal: Monitor BP, Pulse every 15-30 mins. Monitor contractions (strength/frequency). Offer emotional support and encouragement.
  • Fetal: Auscultate Fetal Heart Rate (FHR) every 5 minutes or after every contraction to detect distress.

3. Management of Expulsive Phase:

  • Coach Mrs. F to push only during contractions and relax in between.
  • Ensure hydration (sips of water).
  • Maintain perineal hygiene (swab with antiseptic).

4. Delivery of the Head:

  • Control the head to prevent rapid expulsion (prevent tears).
  • Support the perineum with a sterile pad.
  • Encourage panting/breathing to control crowning.
  • Once head is born, check for cord around the neck (somersault if loose, clamp/cut if tight).
  • Wipe baby's face/mouth.

5. Delivery of Shoulders & Body:

  • Wait for restitution and external rotation.
  • Apply gentle downward traction to deliver anterior shoulder.
  • Apply upward traction to deliver posterior shoulder.
  • Deliver the rest of the body following the curve of Carus.

6. Immediate Newborn Care:

  • Place baby on mother's abdomen (skin-to-skin).
  • Dry the baby and assess APGAR score.
  • Note time of birth and sex of baby.
