Section 1: Multiple Choice Questions (20 Marks)

1.
Tier one health care delivery units includes;
a) Clinics
b) Dispensaries
c) Health centres
d) Community
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Community

Explanation: Under the Kenya Essential Package for Health (KEPH), Tier 1 is the Community level (Community Units). Tier 2 includes Dispensaries and Clinics. Tier 3 includes Health Centres.

2.
Efforts to detect and treat existing health problems at the earliest possible state when disease or impairment already exist is
a) Primary prevention
b) Secondary prevention
c) Tertiary prevention
d) Rehabilitative prevention
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Secondary prevention

Explanation: Secondary prevention focuses on early diagnosis and prompt treatment to halt disease progression (e.g., screening). Primary prevents onset; Tertiary reduces disability.

3.
Roles of families with teenagers include:-
a) Encouraging children to make marital relationship
b) Releasing children as young adults
c) Maintaining a supportive home-base
d) Encouraging open parent-child communication
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Encouraging open parent-child communication

Explanation: During the teenager stage, balancing freedom with responsibility through open communication is key. "Releasing young adults" typically comes in the next stage (Launching center).

4.
Screening in school health services involves;
a) Treatment of the sick school children
b) Taking note of any unusual signs or symptoms
c) Referral of sick pupils for further treatment
d) Health examination to determine the pupil’s health status
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Health examination to determine the pupil’s health status

Explanation: Screening is the periodic health examination (e.g., vision, hearing, dental) to detect unrecognized health needs.

5.
An example of a guiding principle in school health services include;
a) Equality, technology
b) Privacy and confidentiality
c) Discrimination and availability
d) Access to information and injustice
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Privacy and confidentiality

Explanation: School health services must respect student privacy and confidentiality. Discrimination and injustice are contrary to principles.

6.
The following is a step in evaluating the school health program
a) Taking corrective action
b) Implementing interventions
c) Discussing with stakeholders
d) Formulating school health objectives
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Taking corrective action

Explanation: Evaluation involves measuring progress against objectives and taking corrective action to improve the program. Implementing is the execution phase; formulating objectives is the planning phase.

7.
The holistic approach to the achievement of wholesome health for the family is;
a) Family nursing
b) Family health care
c) Family health nursing
d) Family nursing process
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Family health care

Explanation: Family health care focuses on the family as the unit of care to achieve holistic health.

8.
A rural health unit under the Ministry of health that is charged with the responsibility of providing services to a particular catchment area is;
a) Clinic
b) Dispensary
c) Health centre
d) Sub-county hospital
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Health centre

Explanation: In the traditional structure, the Health Centre (RHC) is the primary facility responsible for a defined geographic catchment area, supervising dispensaries within that area.

9.
The following preventive/promotive services are appropriate to Late Childhood (6- 12 yrs)
a) Antenatal care, breastfeeding
b) Vaccination, growth monitoring
c) Supplementary feeding, de-worming
d) Nutritional care, insecticide treated net use
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Supplementary feeding, de-worming

Explanation: This age group corresponds to school-going age. De-worming and school feeding programs are key interventions. Vaccination/growth monitoring is primarily for under-5s.

10.
The component of a school health programme include:
a) Health Education in Schools, Healthful School Environment, Mobile Clinics
b) Home Visiting, Dental Health, Healthful School Environment
c) Parent-Teacher Association, Community Mobilization, Deworming
d) School Health Services, Health Education In Schools, Healthful School Environment
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) School Health Services, Health Education In Schools, Healthful School Environment

Explanation: The traditional "triad" of school health includes: Health Services, Health Education, and Healthful Environment.

11.
The interventions that will promote healthy school children during school health programme include:
a) Protection from diseases, appropriate medical and dental care
b) Treatment of cases, referral to healthy facility
c) Medical examination, immunization
d) Daily observation, nutritional programmes
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Medical examination, immunization

Explanation: Preventive interventions include periodic medical examinations (screening) and immunizations (e.g., HPV, Tetanus boosters).

12.
Which is the primary goal of community health nursing?
a) To support and supplement the efforts of the medical profession
b) To enhance the capacity of individuals, families and communities to cope with their health needs
c) To increase the productivity of the people by providing them with services
d) To contribute to national development through promotion of family welfare
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) To enhance the capacity of individuals, families and communities to cope with their health needs

Explanation: The core goal is empowerment and capacity building for self-reliance in health.

13.
Tier IV Hospital are classified as?
a) Primary
b) Secondary
c) Intermediate
d) Tertiary
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Secondary

Explanation: Tier IV facilities (County/Sub-county hospitals) provide secondary care, receiving referrals from primary care facilities (Dispensaries/Health Centres). Tier VI is Tertiary.

14.
Some of the concepts of primary health care include;
a) Accessibility, Affordability, Community Participation
b) Availability, Accessibility, Community Involvement
c) Accessibility, Affordability, Accessibility
d) Community Mobilization, Affordability, Accessibility
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Accessibility, Affordability, Community Participation

Explanation: These are key principles/concepts of PHC defined at Alma Ata.

15.
Which level of health facility is the usual point of entry of a client into the health care delivery system?
a) Primary
b) Secondary
c) Intermediate
d) Tertiary
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Primary

Explanation: Primary Health Care facilities (Dispensaries/Health Centres) are the first point of contact.

16.
The Nurse is the supervisor of rural health midwives. Which of the following is a supervisory function of the Nurse?
a) Referring cases or patients to the midwife
b) Providing technical guidance to the midwife
c) Proving nursing care to cases referred by the midwife
d) Formulating and implementing training programs for midwives
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Providing technical guidance to the midwife

Explanation: Supervision involves guidance, support, and technical assistance to ensure quality care.

17.
Isolation of a child with measles belongs to what level of prevention?
a) Primary
b) Secondary
c) Intermediate
d) Tertiary
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Primary

Explanation: While treating the child is secondary for *that* child, isolation is primarily done to prevent the spread of infection to *others* in the community (Primary Prevention for the community).

18.
The process of creating awareness of health issues in the community is:-
a) Community participation
b) Community involvement
c) Community mobilization
d) Community sensitization
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Community sensitization

Explanation: Sensitization specifically refers to making people aware or sensitive to an issue. Mobilization involves moving them to action.

19.
A common outcome from the willingness of a community to participate in initiated health programs is;
a) Resource mobilization
b) Improved health workforce
c) Creation of partnership between development agencies and the community
d) Overall improvement of the community’s health status
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Overall improvement of the community’s health status

Explanation: The ultimate goal and outcome of community participation in health programs is the improvement of health status/outcomes.

20.
Assessment of a community by a nurse involves?
a) Identifying present and potential needs and resources related to health of patients.
b) Participation in planning for community health nursing services
c) Providing health education to inform the community on health problems
d) Ensuring that all records on reactions of clients, degree of satisfaction, are well kept
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Identifying present and potential needs and resources related to health of patients.

Explanation: Community diagnosis/assessment involves identifying health needs (problems) and available resources.

Section 2: Short Answer Questions (40 Marks)

21.
Outline five (5) principles of community health according to the Alma Ata Declaration of 1978 (5 marks)
Show Answer

Complete Answer:

  1. Equity: Equitable distribution of health services.
  2. Community Participation: Active involvement of the community in planning and implementation.
  3. Intersectoral Coordination: Collaboration with other sectors (agriculture, education, housing).
  4. Appropriate Technology: Use of scientifically sound and socially acceptable methods.
  5. Focus on Prevention: Emphasis on promotive and preventive health services.
22.
State five (5) components of high priority package in the Kenya essential package for health (5 marks)
Show Answer

Complete Answer:

The high priority packages typically focus on the main burdens of disease:

  1. Maternal and Newborn Health: Antenatal, delivery, and postnatal care.
  2. Child Health: Immunization and IMCI (Integrated Management of Childhood Illnesses).
  3. HIV/AIDS and TB Control: Prevention and treatment.
  4. Malaria Control: Prevention and case management.
  5. Non-Communicable Diseases (NCDs): Prevention and management of diabetes, hypertension, etc.
23.
Outline five (5) possible causes of home accidents (5 marks)
Show Answer

Complete Answer:

  1. Slippery Floors: Wet surfaces causing falls.
  2. Burns/Scalds: Unsafe cooking areas, hot liquids, or open fires.
  3. Poisoning: Improper storage of medicines, cleaning agents, or chemicals.
  4. Choking: Small objects left within reach of children.
  5. Sharp Objects: Knives or broken glass left exposed.
  6. Electric Shock: Exposed wires or faulty sockets.
24.
State five (5) responsibilities of a school nurse (5 marks)
Show Answer

Complete Answer:

  1. Health Screening: conducting vision, hearing, and physical exams.
  2. Health Education: Teaching students about hygiene, nutrition, and reproductive health.
  3. Emergency Care: Providing first aid for injuries and sudden illness.
  4. Immunization Monitoring: Ensuring students' vaccination schedules are up to date.
  5. Communicable Disease Control: Identifying and managing outbreaks in school.
25.
State four (4) activities carried out during outreaches (5 marks - Note: question asks for 4 but allocated 5 marks)
Show Answer

Complete Answer:

  1. Immunization: Vaccinating children and pregnant women (KEPI).
  2. Antenatal Care (ANC): Monitoring pregnant mothers.
  3. Growth Monitoring: Weighing children to detect malnutrition.
  4. Health Education: Teaching community on health topics.
  5. Vitamin A Supplementation and Deworming.
26.
Outline six (6) basic needs of a school going child (6 marks)
Show Answer

Complete Answer:

  1. Nutrition: Adequate food for growth and energy.
  2. Shelter/Clothing: Protection from elements.
  3. Security/Safety: A safe home and school environment.
  4. Love and Affection: Emotional support from family.
  5. Health Care: Access to medical services.
  6. Education/Stimulation: Opportunity to learn and play.
27.
State five (5) services provided in an integrated mother and child health/FP (IMCH/FP) clinic (5 marks)
Show Answer

Complete Answer:

  1. Antenatal Care (ANC): For pregnant women.
  2. Child Welfare Clinic (CWC): Immunization and growth monitoring.
  3. Family Planning (FP): Provision of contraceptives and counseling.
  4. Postnatal Care (PNC): Care after delivery.
  5. PMTCT: Prevention of Mother-to-Child Transmission of HIV.
  6. Nutrition Counseling: Breastfeeding support and dietary advice.
28.
Outline (5) priority areas of Ouagadougou declaration (5 marks)
Show Answer

Complete Answer:

The Ouagadougou Declaration on Primary Health Care and Health Systems in Africa (2008) focuses on:

  1. Leadership and Governance for Health.
  2. Health Service Delivery.
  3. Human Resources for Health.
  4. Health Financing.
  5. Health Information Systems.
  6. Community Ownership and Participation.

Section 3: Long Answer Questions (40 Marks)

29.
You have been deployed as a Nurse in a dispensary within your sub-County with a principal role of planning and conducting weekly home visits to your clients.

a. Outline five (5) specific objectives of home visiting that you will consider in your weekly home visits to your clients (5 marks)

Show Answer

Answer:

  1. To assess the home environment and its impact on health.
  2. To detect cases of illness early and refer appropriate cases.
  3. To provide health education tailored to the family's needs.
  4. To provide follow-up care for chronic patients or postnatal mothers.
  5. To promote family health and prevent disease spread within the household.

b. Explain five (5) relevant principles of family health nursing that you maintain during your weekly home visits to your clients (10 marks)

Show Answer

Answer:

  1. Establish a Professional Relationship: Maintain good rapport with the family to ensure trust and cooperation.
  2. Planned Purpose: Visits must have a clear objective based on identified needs, not random.
  3. Flexibility: Be adaptable to the family's situation and schedule.
  4. Scientific Approach: Use technical skills and nursing process (assessment, planning, intervention) professionally.
  5. Educative: Every visit should be a learning opportunity for the family towards self-reliance.

c. Outline five ways you will conduct yourself during the visits (5 marks)

Show Answer

Answer:

  1. Proper Grooming: Wear appropriate/professional attire (often uniform or modest dress).
  2. Respectful Entry: Knock, greet culturally appropriately, and wait to be invited in.
  3. Non-Judgmental Attitude: Respect the family's culture, beliefs, and socioeconomic status.
  4. Effective Communication: Use simple, clear language and listen actively.
  5. Confidentiality: Maintain privacy of information shared by the family.
30.
The delivery of health care services in Kenya is depended on past health conventions and declarations.

a. Describe three pillars of the Kenyan vision 2030 (6 marks)

Show Answer

Answer:

  1. Economic Pillar: Aims to achieve an average economic growth rate of 10% per annum and sustain the same till 2030.
  2. Social Pillar: Aims to create a just and cohesive society enjoying equitable social development in a clean and secure environment (Health falls here).
  3. Political Pillar: Aims to realize a democratic, people-centered, result-oriented, and accountable political system.

b. Explain seven (7) sustainable development goals (14 marks)

Show Answer

Answer:

  1. Goal 1: No Poverty: End poverty in all its forms everywhere.
  2. Goal 2: Zero Hunger: End hunger, achieve food security and improved nutrition.
  3. Goal 3: Good Health and Well-being: Ensure healthy lives and promote well-being for all at all ages.
  4. Goal 4: Quality Education: Ensure inclusive and equitable quality education.
  5. Goal 5: Gender Equality: Achieve gender equality and empower all women and girls.
  6. Goal 6: Clean Water and Sanitation: Ensure availability and sustainable management of water and sanitation for all.
  7. Goal 13: Climate Action: Take urgent action to combat climate change and its impacts.
