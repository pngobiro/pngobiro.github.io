Section 1: Multiple Choice Questions (20 Marks)

Note: Source material contains Questions 1-10.

1.
Community participation depends on one the following concept of primary health care:
A) Self-initiative.
B) Affordability.
C) Dependency.
D) Empowerment.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: D) Empowerment.

Explanation: Community participation in Primary Health Care (PHC) is rooted in the concept of empowerment. It involves communities taking responsibility for their own health, making decisions, and actively planning and implementing health solutions, rather than being passive recipients of care.

Why other options are incorrect:

  • A) Self-initiative: While related, empowerment is the broader concept that enables self-initiative.
  • B) Affordability: This is related to the principle of "Appropriate Technology" or "Equity," not specifically participation.
  • C) Dependency: PHC aims to eliminate dependency by making communities self-reliant.
2.
Indicate TRUE or FALSE:
i) Community diagnosis help the community to identify their own health problems.
ii) Map is a tool that we use in community diagnosis and patient diagnosis.
Show Answer

Correct Answer:

  1. TRUE - The primary goal of community diagnosis is to help the community recognize and prioritize their own health issues.
  2. FALSE (technically) or TRUE depending on interpretation. However, in the context of the question comparing "community diagnosis" and "patient diagnosis":
    • A map is a key tool in Community Diagnosis (social mapping, resource mapping).
    • A map is NOT typically used in individual patient diagnosis (clinical diagnosis relies on history, exam, and labs).
    • Therefore, the statement implies it is used for both, which makes the statement FALSE.

Study Tip: Compare tools: Community Diagnosis uses maps, surveys, and FGDs. Patient Diagnosis uses thermometers, stethoscopes, and lab tests.

3.
Qualities of an interviewer include:
A) Be illiterate and well known to the community.
B) Display the right attitude and be judgmental.
C) Be able to explain the data collection tools effectively to the community.
D) Be a good listener and insensitive towards other people’s feelings.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C) Be able to explain the data collection tools effectively to the community.

Explanation: A good interviewer must understand the tools (questionnaires/guides) well enough to explain them clearly to respondents to ensure accurate data collection.

Why other options are incorrect:

  • A) Interviewers must be literate to read questions and record answers.
  • B) Interviewers must be non-judgmental.
  • D) Interviewers must be sensitive (empathetic) to respondents' feelings.
4.
Survey addresses some specific areas in the community, which one is NOT?
A) Gathering information about an incidence.
B) Seeking to understand and identify ways of getting rid of negative cultural beliefs and behavior that is causing ill health in the community.
C) Assessing the utilization of the available health services.
D) Screening people for diseases.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: D) Screening people for diseases.

Explanation: A Survey is a research method used to collect data/information (options A, B, and C describe data collection on incidence, beliefs, and utilization). Screening involves clinical testing or examination to detect disease, which is a different activity from a survey, although they may happen during the same campaign.

5.
Community diagnosis is defined as:
A. A process through which workers identify the community’s health problems.
B. A process through which health workers together with members of the community identify the community’s priority health problems and together make a plan of action and implement them.
C. A process through which health workers together with members of parliament identify the community’s priority health problems and together make a plan of action and implement them.
D. A process through which community members identify the community’s health problems.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B. A process through which health workers together with members of the community identify the community’s priority health problems and together make a plan of action and implement them.

Explanation: This is the comprehensive definition of Community Diagnosis. It emphasizes partnership between health workers and the community, covering identification, prioritization, planning, and implementation.

6.
Community diagnosis can be summarized in following four basic steps:
A) Assessment, diagnosis, implementation, evaluation.
B) History taking, diagnosis, implementation, evaluation.
C) History taking, planning, diagnosis, evaluation.
D) Collecting basic information, implementation, treatment, evaluation.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A) Assessment, diagnosis, implementation, evaluation.

Explanation: These steps mirror the Nursing Process applied at the community level:

1. Assessment (Data Collection)
2. Diagnosis (Problem Analysis)
3. Planning/Implementation (Action)
4. Evaluation (Reviewing Outcome)

Option B uses "History taking" which is a clinical term for individuals. Option D uses "Treatment" which is also clinical.

7.
The exploration phase is made up of the following activities:
A. Seeking permission and informing the various leaders, seeking reaction of the community members gathering background data.
B. Gathering background data, screening people for diseases, seeking to understand and identify ways of getting rid of negative beliefs.
C. Looking for funds to conduct surveys, gathering background data, screening for diseases.
D. Seeking permission, screening, screening for diseases, gathering background data.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A. Seeking permission and informing the various leaders, seeking reaction of the community members gathering background data.

Explanation: The Exploration Phase is the "Entry" phase. Key activities involve entering the community, meeting leaders (gatekeepers), getting permission/buy-in, and understanding the community's initial reaction. Screening (mentioned in B, C, D) is a later activity.

8.
The process of data analysis in the correct order is;
A) Data cleaning, sorting or tallying, analysis of results, codding and entering data.
B) Sorting or tallying, data cleaning, analysis of results coding and entering data.
C) Analysis of results, data cleaning, sorting or tallying, coding and entering data.
D) Data cleaning, sorting or tallying, coding and entering data, analysis of results.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: D) Data cleaning, sorting or tallying, coding and entering data, analysis of results.

Explanation: Logical data processing flow:

  1. Cleaning: Removing errors/incomplete forms.
  2. Sorting/Tallying: Organizing the raw data.
  3. Coding/Entering: Converting data to analyzable format (computer entry).
  4. Analysis: Interpreting the final data.
9.
The following is a positive indicator.
A. Death rate.
B. Infant mortality rate.
C. Perinatal mortality rate.
D. Life expectancy at birth.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: D. Life expectancy at birth.

Explanation: A positive indicator reflects good health or improvement (e.g., living longer). Death rates and mortality rates (A, B, C) are negative indicators (measures of ill health/death).

Section 2: Short Answer Questions (20 Marks)

1.
State THREE (3) types of interviews during community diagnosis. (3 Marks)
Show Answer

Answer:

  1. Structured Interview: Uses a strict questionnaire with pre-set questions asked in a specific order.
  2. Semi-structured Interview: Uses an interview guide but allows flexibility for probing and follow-up questions.
  3. Unstructured (In-depth) Interview: Informal and conversational, often used with Key Informants to explore complex issues.

Other acceptable answer: Focus Group Discussion (though often categorized separately, it is a form of group interview).

2.
State five (5) qualities of a good questionnaire. (5 Marks)
Show Answer

Answer:

  • Simplicity/Clarity: Questions should be easy to understand and unambiguous.
  • Relevance: Questions must relate directly to the objectives of the study.
  • Brevity/Conciseness: It should be short enough to avoid respondent fatigue.
  • Objectivity/Unbiased: Questions should not suggest a "correct" answer or lead the respondent.
  • Logical Sequence: Questions should flow naturally (e.g., from general to specific).
  • Acceptability: It should not contain offensive or culturally insensitive questions.
3.
State how you ensure quality of data during community diagnosis. (4 Marks)
Show Answer

Answer:

  1. Training of Data Collectors: Ensuring interviewers understand the tools and methodology.
  2. Pre-testing/Piloting: Testing the tools in a small sample to correct errors before the main survey.
  3. Supervision: Monitoring data collectors in the field to ensure protocols are followed.
  4. Data Checking/Cleaning: Reviewing filled questionnaires daily for completeness and consistency.
  5. Proper Sampling: Using correct sampling methods to ensure the data represents the community.
4.
Explain FOUR (4) factors that affect incidence of a disease in a community. (8 Marks)
Show Answer

Answer:

The epidemiological triad (Host, Agent, Environment) determines disease incidence:

  1. Host Factors:
    • Immunity status: Low herd immunity increases incidence (e.g., low vaccination coverage).
    • Demographics: Age and gender distribution (e.g., measles affects children).
  2. Agent Factors:
    • Virulence/Pathogenicity: Highly infectious agents spread faster, increasing incidence.
    • Drug Resistance: Resistant strains linger longer and spread more.
  3. Environmental Factors:
    • Sanitation/Hygiene: Poor waste disposal and water supply increase water-borne diseases.
    • Climate/Season: Rainy seasons may increase vector-borne diseases like Malaria.
  4. Health System Factors:
    • Availability and accessibility of preventive services (e.g., lack of mosquito nets or vaccines increases incidence).

Section 3: Long Answer Questions (20 Marks)

1.
Disease prevention is better than cure. Community Diagnosis help to identify community health problems.

a) Outline FOUR (4) factors that affect the health of a community. (4 Marks)
b) State FOUR (4) health indicators. (4 Marks)
c) Discuss the steps of conducting a community diagnosis. (12 marks)
Show Answer

a) Factors affecting community health (Lalonde Model):

  1. Human Biology/Genetics: Genetic predisposition to diseases within the population.
  2. Environment: Physical (pollution, housing), Social (poverty, education), and Cultural environments.
  3. Lifestyle/Behavior: Diet, smoking, alcohol consumption, and physical activity levels.
  4. Health Care Organization: Access, quality, and affordability of medical services.

b) Health Indicators:

  1. Morbidity Indicators: Incidence and Prevalence rates of diseases.
  2. Mortality Indicators: Infant Mortality Rate (IMR), Maternal Mortality Rate (MMR), Crude Death Rate (CDR).
  3. Disability Rates: DALYs (Disability Adjusted Life Years).
  4. Nutritional Status Indicators: Percentage of underweight children, stunting rates.
  5. Health Care Utilization Rates: Immunization coverage, hospital bed occupancy rate.

c) Steps of conducting a Community Diagnosis:

The process is cyclical and systematic.

  1. Preparatory Phase:
    • Selection of the community/area.
    • Logistical planning (budget, transport).
    • Selection and training of the team.
  2. Entry/Exploration Phase:
    • Meeting community leaders (Gatekeepers).
    • Explaining the purpose to gain acceptance (Community Mobilization).
    • Reviewing existing secondary data (records).
  3. Planning for Data Collection:
    • Designing and pre-testing tools (questionnaires, checklists).
    • Determining sampling method and sample size.
  4. Data Collection:
    • Fieldwork using methods like interviews, surveys, FGDs, and observation (Windshield survey).
  5. Data Analysis and Interpretation:
    • Compiling, cleaning, and analyzing data.
    • Identifying health problems and community needs.
  6. Community Diagnosis (Prioritization):
    • Presenting findings to the community.
    • Prioritizing problems together with the community based on urgency, magnitude, and feasibility.
  7. Action Plan & Implementation:
    • Developing a plan to address the prioritized problems.
    • Carrying out the interventions (e.g., health education, clean-up campaigns).
  8. Evaluation:
    • Assessing if the objectives were met and the impact of the interventions.
