Section 1: Multiple Choice Questions (40 Marks)

1.
A communication style that involves expressing thoughts and feelings openly and respectfully is;
A. Passive
B. Aggressive
C. Assertive
D. Submissive
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C. Assertive

Explanation: Assertive communication allows an individual to express their needs, thoughts, and feelings clearly and directly while respecting the rights of others.

2.
Active listening is an appropriate communication skill that implies;
A. Hearing what is said without feedback
B. Nodding along to what the speaker says
C. Interrupting the speaker to seek for clarity with questions
D. Fully concentrating on the speaker, understanding and responding
Show Answer

Correct Answer: D. Fully concentrating on the speaker, understanding and responding

Explanation: Active listening goes beyond just hearing or nodding; it requires full engagement, processing the information (understanding), and providing appropriate feedback (responding) to ensure communication is effective.

3.
You are assessing developmental stages and conclude older adults have reached Erickson's developmental stage of ego, integrity, when they: -
A. Acknowledge that one cannot get everything one wants in life
B. Assess their lives and identify actions that have values and purpose
C. Express a wish that life could be relived differently
D. Feel that they are being punished for things they did not do
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B. Assess their lives and identify actions that have values and purpose

Explanation: In the stage of Integrity vs. Despair, achieving integrity involves looking back on life with a sense of fulfillment, seeing value and purpose in what was lived. Despair involves regret (Option C) or feeling life was wasted.

4.
You are following up clients in the urban health centers to ensure they meet the primary goal of community health nursing which is;
A. To support and supplement the effort of the medical profession in the promotion of health and prevention of illness.
B. To enhance the capacity of individuals, families, and communities to cope with their health needs.
C. To increase the productivity of the people by providing them with services that will increase their level of health.
D. To contribute to the national development through promotion of family welfare. Focusing particularly on the mothers and children.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B. To enhance the capacity of individuals, families, and communities to cope with their health needs.

Explanation: The overarching goal of Community Health Nursing is to empower the community (capacity building) to maintain and improve their own health (self-reliance), rather than just supporting the medical profession (A).

5.
You are determining an individual's and family life stage and environment. A primary agent of the socialization process you may consider is;
A. Laws
B. Family
C. Government
D. Economy
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B. Family

Explanation: The family is the first and most fundamental agent of socialization, where an individual learns initial values, norms, and behaviors.

6.
In an MCH clinic a health talk is ongoing. The health educator will be considered to be "paraphrasing" when:
A. Saying exactly what the other person said
B. Ignoring the main message
C. Restating the message in their own words
D. Speaking in a different language
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C. Restating the message in their own words

Explanation: Paraphrasing involves capturing the essence of the speaker's message and repeating it back using different words to check understanding.

7.
Mrs. K, an elderly woman needs to limit her kcalorie intake without sacrificing needed nutrients. While keeping in mind the food pyramid, which of the following would she do?
A. Eliminate carbohydrate
B. Count kcalories and not worry about the food groups
C. Eliminate dairy foods.
D. Carefully select foods rich in nutrients but low in kcalories
Show Answer

Correct Answer: D. Carefully select foods rich in nutrients but low in kcalories

Explanation: Older adults have lower energy needs but constant or higher micronutrient needs. The strategy is to choose nutrient-dense foods (high nutrients, lower calories) rather than eliminating whole groups (A, C) or ignoring balance (B).

8.
A nurse is caring for a patient experiencing dysphagia. Which interventions help decrease the risk of aspiration during feeding;
A. Sit the patient upright in a chair
B. Give liquids at the end of the meal
C. Provide thin foods to make it easier to swallow
D. Feed the patient slowly, allowing time to chew and swallow
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A. Sit the patient upright in a chair

Explanation: Positioning upright (90 degrees) uses gravity to help food go down the esophagus and protects the airway. Option D is also good advice, but upright positioning is the primary physical intervention to prevent aspiration. (Note: Thickened liquids are usually preferred over thin foods (C) for dysphagia).

9.
A parasite that is completely dependent on the host during a segment or all of its life cycle is said to be;
A. Obligate parasite
B. Erratic parasite
C. Paratenic parasite
D. Facultative parasite
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A. Obligate parasite

Explanation: An obligate parasite cannot complete its life cycle or survive without a host. A facultative parasite (D) can live freely or parasitically.

10.
A handkerchief from a child with chickenpox is likely to be contaminated with the virus that causes chickenpox (varicella zoster virus). The handkerchief in this case plays the role of a;
A. Host
B. Fomite
C. Pathogen
D. Vector
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B. Fomite

Explanation: A fomite is an inanimate object (like a handkerchief, door handle, or bedding) that can carry and spread disease and infectious agents.

11.
While feeding on a blood meal of a susceptible mammalian host, an infected tsetse fly (Glossina palpalis) injects;
A. Mesenteric amastigotes
B. Lymphatic amastigotes
C. Metacyclic trypomastigotes
D. Blood stream trypomastigotes
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C. Metacyclic trypomastigotes

Explanation: The infective stage of the Trypanosome (sleeping sickness parasite) that develops in the tsetse fly's salivary glands and is injected into the host is the metacyclic trypomastigote.

12.
A process by which a community is made alert of its problems and the available resources, such as, manpower, money, materials, ideas and time is community refers to;
A. Sensitization
B. Awareness
C. Mobilization
D. Participation
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A. Sensitization

Explanation: Sensitization involves creating awareness and making the community "alert" to issues. Mobilization involves the actual gathering/use of resources.

13.
You are organizing a feeding program for special needs communities. An Act of the Kenyan parliament that prescribes food safety is;
A. Nurses act CAP 257
B. Public health Act CAP 242
C. Clinical officers act CAP 241
D. Medical practitioners act CAP 253
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B. Public health Act CAP 242

Explanation: CAP 242 is the principal law governing public health, sanitation, and food safety in Kenya.

14.
A community health nurse fails in their professional duty to care leading to harm on the patient. They are liable for;
A. Dereliction
B. Negligence
C. Malpractice
D. Misunderstanding
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C. Malpractice

Explanation: While negligence is the failure to take reasonable care, when this failure occurs in a professional capacity (violating professional standards of care), it is specifically termed Malpractice (Professional Negligence).

15.
Baby Kay is brought to your facility for immunization at the age of ten (10) weeks. Vaccines that you are likely to administer to the infant include;
A. Measles, BCG, rotavirus vaccine
B. Vitamin A, oral polio 2(OPV 2), pneumococcal
C. Oral Polio (OPV 2), pneumococcal, pentavalent 2
D. Inactivated polio vaccine (IPV), measles, hepatitis B
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C. Oral Polio (OPV 2), pneumococcal, pentavalent 2

Explanation: At 10 weeks, the standard KEPI schedule is: OPV 2, DPT-HepB-Hib 2 (Pentavalent 2), Pneumococcal (PCV 10) 2, and Rotavirus 2.

16.
The right sequence in the KEPI logistic management cycle is: -
A. Estimate the vaccine requirement, order vaccines, receive vaccines, store vaccines, issue and use
B. Estimate vaccine requirements, issue and use, order vaccines, receive vaccines, store vaccines
C. Receive vaccines, issue and use, estimate vaccine requirements, order vaccine, receive vaccines
D. Receive vaccines, order vaccines, issue and use, estimate vaccine requirements to store vaccines
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A. Estimate the vaccine requirement, order vaccines, receive vaccines, store vaccines, issue and use

Explanation: The logical cycle begins with determining needs (Estimate), then procuring (Order), followed by logistics (Receive/Store), and finally service delivery (Issue/Use).

17.
A 30 year old lady has opted to take oral contraceptivves as her FP method. The mode of action of combined oral contraceptive includes;
A. Ovulation inhibition, thickening of mucus
B. Slippery mucus, delayed ovulation
C. Slowed motility, enhanced ovulation
D. Increased motility in uterine tubes, thickening of endometrium
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A. Ovulation inhibition, thickening of mucus

Explanation: COCs work by suppressing FSH/LH to stop ovulation and by thickening cervical mucus to block sperm entry.

18.
A method of family planning that involves recording body temperature while observing changes in the cervical mucus is;
A. Fertility awareness method
B. Symptom-thermal method
C. Lactation amenorrhea method
D. Basal Body Temperature method
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B. Symptom-thermal method

Explanation: The Symptothermal method combines basal body temperature (Thermal) with cervical mucus observation (Symptom) and cervical palpation.

19.
You are a health care worker assigned to carry out community mobilization. Resources that you will target include;
A. Money, land, materials, manpower
B. Money, materials, time, manpower.
C. Community participation, time, manpower.
D. Community participation, money and land.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B. Money, materials, time, manpower.

Explanation: The "4 Ms" of resource mobilization are typically Money, Manpower (People), Materials, and Minutes (Time). Land is usually considered a material asset or infrastructure.

20.
As a provider of home based care services you are expected to uphold a principle of ethical reasoning that allows client assume the right to make own decisions about their health. This is in line with the principle of: -
A. Veracity
B. Fidelity
C. Autonomy
D. Paternalism
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C. Autonomy

Explanation: Autonomy is the ethical principle of self-determination, respecting the client's right to make their own choices.

21.
A nurse is teaching a client with genital herpes. Education for this client should include an explanation of:
A. Why the disease is transmittable only when visible lesions are present
B. The need for the use of petroleum products.
C. Option of disregarding safer-sex practices now that he's already infected.
D. The importance of informing his partners of the disease.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: D. The importance of informing his partners of the disease.

Explanation: Partner notification is crucial for preventing spread and enabling partners to protect themselves. Transmission can occur without lesions (asymptomatic shedding).

22.
While caring for a patient with a sexually transmitted infection (STI), the most appropriate nursing intervention to provide emotional support to support he be to;
A. Use concerned listening when the patient expresses negative feelings.
B. Reassure the patient that the disease is curable with appropriate treatment.
C. Offer many alternatives that the patient can use to change sexual relationships.
D. Help the patient who is an innocent sexual partner forgive the infecting partner.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A. Use concerned listening when the patient expresses negative feelings.

Explanation: Concerned (active) listening validates the patient's experience and reduces stigma/anxiety. False reassurance (B) or giving advice (C) is less therapeutic.

23.
While carrying out community health programs a nurse is expected to also test whether the expected outcome has been achieved. This activity is refers to;
A. Assessment
B. Planning
C. Implementation
D. Evaluation
Show Answer

Correct Answer: D. Evaluation

Explanation: Evaluation is the phase of the nursing process where the nurse determines if the goals and expected outcomes have been met.

24.

A. 2 weeks to 5 years of life
B. 6 years to 12 years of life

D. Pregnancy and newborn up to 28days of life

25.
In construction of living quarters, the biological criteria for an adequate house aims at: -
A. Providing security for the occupants
B. Facilitating effective family functioning
C. Minimizing the risk of disease transmission
D. Fostering healthy child upbringing
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C. Minimizing the risk of disease transmission

Explanation: "Biological" criteria specifically relate to health and disease (preventing spread of pathogens, vectors, etc.). Security (A) is physical/psychological. Family functioning (B/D) is social/psychological.

26.
You are caring for a patient with tuberculosis in HIV. The patient is to be on pyridoxine throughout the course of treatment in order to;
A. Prevent visual disturbances
B. Prevent peripheral neuropathy
C. Treat other opportunistic infections
D. Prevent drug toxicities
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B. Prevent peripheral neuropathy

Explanation: Isoniazid (a TB drug) competes with Vitamin B6 (Pyridoxine), leading to deficiency which causes peripheral neuropathy. Supplementation prevents this.

27.
Symptoms in stage 2 of WHO clinical staging of HIV/AIDS for adults include: -
A. Herpes zoster, seborrheic dermatitis
B. Oral candidiasis, pulmonary TB
C. Thrombocytopenia, pyomyositis
D. Neutropenia, sinusitis
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A. Herpes zoster, seborrheic dermatitis

Explanation: WHO Stage 2 is characterized by mild symptoms: Herpes zoster (within last 5 years), minor mucocutaneous manifestations (seborrheic dermatitis, fungal nail infections), and recurrent URTI. Oral thrush/TB are Stage 3.

28.
During your home visiting, a client with stage 2 cancer expresses that, "If I can just live long enough to attend my son's graduation, I'll be ready to die". Which phrase of coping with death is the client expressing;
A. Anger
B. Denial
C. Depression
D. Bargaining
Show Answer

Correct Answer: D. Bargaining

Explanation: Bargaining involves making a "deal" (usually with God or fate) to postpone death until a specific event or milestone is reached.

29.
Individuals under the age of majority who are self-supporting and no longer require parental consent for health care are referred to as: -
A. Mature minors
B. Incompetent minors
C. Superior minors
D. Emancipated minors
Show Answer

Correct Answer: D. Emancipated minors

Explanation: An emancipated minor is one who has been legally freed from parental control, often through marriage, military service, or financial independence, and can consent to their own care. "Mature minor" usually refers to ability to understand specific treatments, not necessarily financial/legal independence status.

30.
A mental health nurse asks a psychiatric patient for their three names, name of the hospital they are in and the month of the year. The nurse will be assessing the patient for;
A. Perception
B. Attention
C. Abstraction
D. Orientation
Show Answer

Correct Answer: D. Orientation

Explanation: Orientation checks awareness of Person (names), Place (hospital), and Time (month/year).

31.
While managing a patient on antipsychotics, you observe that the patient has developed sustained contraction of neck, mouth, tongue and other muscles. This side effect is known as;
A. Torticolis
B. Akathisia
C. Acute dystonia
D. Tardive dyskinesia
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C. Acute dystonia

Explanation: Acute dystonia is a type of Extrapyramidal Side Effect (EPSE) characterized by involuntary, sustained muscle contractions (e.g., oculogyric crisis, torticollis). Tardive dyskinesia is chronic/late-onset. Akathisia is restlessness.

32.
The major reason as to why milieu therapy is a good mode of treatment for antisocial personality disorders is: -
A. Provides a system for punishment and rewards for behavior modification.
B. Emulates a social community in which the patient may learn to live harmoniously with others.
C. Provides mostly one to one interaction between the patient and the therapist.
D. Provides a very structured setting which the patients have very little input into the planning of their care.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B. Emulates a social community in which the patient may learn to live harmoniously with others.

Explanation: Milieu therapy uses the therapeutic community environment to model healthy social interactions, peer feedback, and group norms, which is effective for antisocial patients who struggle with societal rules.

33.
In the multi axial assessment of mentally ill persons, general medical conditions are classified under;
A. Axis I
B. Axis II
C. Axis III
D. Axis IV
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C. Axis III

Explanation: DSM-IV Multiaxial System:
- Axis I: Clinical Disorders.
- Axis II: Personality Disorders/Mental Retardation.
- Axis III: General Medical Conditions.
- Axis IV: Psychosocial/Environmental Problems.
- Axis V: Global Assessment of Functioning (GAF).

34.
While sharing health messages on sources of water, as a nurse you will inform the community that sources of ground water include: -
A. Shallow wells, springs, rivers
B. Springs, deep wells, rivers
C. Springs, shallow wells, deep wells
D. Springs, shallow wells, deep wells
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C. Springs, shallow wells, deep wells

Explanation: Ground water originates from beneath the earth's surface. This includes wells (shallow/deep) and springs (where ground water surfaces). Rivers are surface water.

A. Implementation of issues

36.
A number of families in village X have been diagnosed to be suffering from malnutrition. External factors that influence family health include: -
A. Family size, family locality
B. Family locality, biological characteristics, climate
C. Family values, family locality, climatic condition
D. Family locality, land terrain, climatic condition
Show Answer

Correct Answer: D. Family locality, land terrain, climatic condition

Explanation: External factors are environmental. Locality, terrain (geography), and climate are external. Family size (A) and Biology (B) are internal/inherent factors. Values (C) are internal cultural factors.

37.
Cases of cervical cancer have been diagnosed in school B. Educating teenage girls on need for regular cervical cancer screening as an example of;
A. Secondary prevention
B. Primary prevention
C. Primordial prevention
D. Tertiary prevention

Correct Answer: A. Secondary prevention

Explanation: Screening allows for early detection and treatment of disease before symptoms appear, which is the definition of Secondary Prevention.

i. Advocacy is a process, which enables people to put their efforts together for carrying out the joint activities unifying resources and building solidarity

39.
You are endeavoring to create the greatest impact in a community and hope to produce lasting change that promotes healthy behavior. As a a nurse you should: -
A. Talk to patients about needing to quit smoking
B. Talk to patients about losing weight

40.

A. Antenatal kit, family folder, anesthetic kit

41.
As a nurse conducting community diagnosis you are required to select a competent interviewer for the exercise. Outline five (5) qualities of an interviewer that you will consider in your selection process (5 marks)

42.
State five (5) challenges that you are likely to face while applying the multidisciplinary approach to health care service delivery (5 marks)
Show Answer
  1. Role Confusion/Conflict: Overlap or lack of clarity in roles leading to friction.
  2. Communication Barriers: Different professional jargons or poor information sharing.
  3. Differing Professional Ideologies: Conflicts in care philosophies or priorities.
  4. Scheduling/Time Constraints: Difficulty coordinating meetings and joint rounds.
  5. Resource Allocation: Competition for limited resources among disciplines.
43.
In village Y, substance abuse has led to many school drop outs. State five (5) factors that motivate youths to substance use/abuse in a community (5 marks)
Show Answer
  1. Peer Pressure: Desire to fit in with friends who use drugs.
  2. Unemployment/Idleness: Lack of productive activities leads to boredom.
  3. Stress/Trauma: Using substances as a coping mechanism for family problems or poverty.
  4. Availability/Accessibility: Easy access to drugs and alcohol in the community.
  5. Media Influence: Glamorization of drug use in movies and music.
44.
You are a newly recruited nurse in a Non-Governmental Organization whose key function is to carry out health promotion activities. Your first assignment is to help in the selection of appropriate health promotion interventions. State five (5) considerations that will guide your selection process (5 marks)
Show Answer
  1. Community Needs/Priorities: Does the intervention address a significant health problem identified by the community?
  2. Feasibility: Is it practical to implement given the time and context?
  3. Cost-Effectiveness/Resources: Are there sufficient funds and materials available?
  4. Cultural Acceptability: Is the intervention compatible with local customs and beliefs?
  5. Evidence-Base: Is there proof that this intervention works?
45.
When planning for outreach services in your facility catchment area, outline five (5) activities that you will include in your plan (5 marks)

  1. Community Mobilization: Informing leaders and the public about the upcoming outreach.
  2. Service Delivery: Planning specific services (Immunization, ANC, Family Planning, Treatment of minor ailments).
  3. Health Education: Preparing talks on relevant health topics.

46.
Outline four (4) determinants of nutrition that you will share with an audience in your community that is in need of nutrition counseling (4 marks)
Show Answer
  1. Economic Status (Income): Ability to afford a variety of foods.
  2. Food Availability/Security: Access to sufficient food supply (agriculture/markets).
  3. Cultural Practices/Taboos: Beliefs that restrict or encourage certain foods.
  4. Knowledge/Education: Understanding of balanced diet and food preparation.
47.
The number of cases of HIV in health centre K is on the increase. Explain three (3) strategies employed by the Kenyan health sector towards combating HIV/AIDS (6 marks)
Show Answer
  1. Prevention of Mother to Child Transmission (PMTCT): Providing ART to pregnant mothers and prophylaxis to infants to eliminate vertical transmission.
  2. HIV Testing Services (HTS): Promoting the "95-95-95" targets by identifying positive cases through widespread testing.
  3. Antiretroviral Therapy (ART): Universal "Test and Treat" policy to suppress viral loads and prevent transmission.
  4. (Other valid strategies: VMMC, Condom promotion, Pre-Exposure Prophylaxis - PrEP).
48.
You have been employed as the team leader of a home based care program. Outline five (5) limitations to networking that you are likely to encounter. (5 marks)
Show Answer
  1. Competition: Partners may view each other as rivals for funding or recognition.
  2. Loss of Autonomy: Organizations may fear losing their independence or identity.
  3. Unequal Benefits: Perception that some partners benefit more than others.
  4. Time and Resource Costs: Networking requires significant time for meetings and coordination.
  5. Lack of Trust: Suspicions about motives or reliability of other partners.

Section 3: Long Answer Question (20 Marks)

49.
As a nurse manager in a rural health facility, your primary task of overseeing proper planning and ensuring the overall functioning of the facility will require you to utilize a variety of leadership functions. Describe the key leadership functions you would utilize to meet your objectives.
Show Answer

Model Answer: Key Leadership/Management Functions

1. Planning:

  • Setting clear goals and objectives for the facility (e.g., increasing immunization coverage).
  • Developing work plans and schedules (rosters) to ensure 24/7 coverage.
  • Budgeting and forecasting resource needs (drugs, equipment) to prevent stock-outs.

2. Organizing:

  • Assigning tasks and responsibilities to staff based on their skills and qualifications.
  • Structuring the facility departments (MCH, Outpatient, Lab) for efficient patient flow.
  • Coordinating resources (human and material) to ensure they are available where needed.

3. Staffing (Human Resource Management):

  • Identifying staffing gaps and advocating for recruitment.
  • Orienting and inducting new staff members.
  • Providing continuous medical education (CME) and training to update staff skills.

4. Directing/Leading:

  • Motivating and inspiring the team to work towards the facility's vision.
  • Providing supervision and mentorship to junior staff.
  • Communicating effectively to ensure instructions are understood.
  • Resolving conflicts among staff members to maintain a harmonious work environment.

5. Controlling/Monitoring:

  • Establishing performance standards and indicators.
  • Conducting regular audits and performance appraisals to measure progress.
  • Taking corrective action when targets are not met or standards are violated.
  • Managing inventory and ensuring accountability for resources.
