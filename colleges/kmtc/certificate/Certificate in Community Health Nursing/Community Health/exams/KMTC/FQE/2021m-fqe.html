Section 1: Multiple Choice Questions (40 Marks)

1.
The examination of a patient with a psychiatric disorder should include:
a) Psychological tests
b) Laboratory studies
c) A mental status examination
d) Screening test for stress levels
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) A mental status examination

Explanation: The Mental Status Examination (MSE) is the core assessment tool in psychiatry, equivalent to the physical exam in general medicine. It systematically describes the patient's mental state at that specific point in time.

2.
Positive symptoms of Schizophrenia include:
a) Hallucinations, delusions and disorganize thinking
b) Waxy flexibility, alogia and apathy
c) Somatic delusions, echolalia and a flat affect
d) Flat affect, a volition and anheclonia
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Hallucinations, delusions and disorganize thinking

Explanation: Positive symptoms add to normal behavior (hallucinations, delusions). Negative symptoms take away from normal behavior (apathy, flat affect, alogia, anhedonia).

3.
Substance dependence
a) Includes characteristics of adverse consequences and repeated use
b) Requires long term treatment in a hospital-based program
c) Produces less severe symptoms than that of abuse
d) Includes characteristics of tolerance and withdrawal
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Includes characteristics of tolerance and withdrawal

Explanation: Dependence is physiologically characterized by tolerance (needing more for the same effect) and withdrawal symptoms upon cessation. Abuse generally refers to maladaptive patterns of use despite adverse consequences.

4.
A person who is always striving to be the center of attention may be diagnosed with personality disorder called
a) Border line
b) Histrionic
c) Antisocial
d) Impulsive
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Histrionic

Explanation: Histrionic Personality Disorder is characterized by a pattern of excessive attention-seeking emotions, usually beginning in early adulthood, including inappropriately seductive behavior and an excessive need for approval.

5.
The age of onset of attention deficit disorder is between
a) 4-7 years
b) 10–18 years
c) 2-4 years
d) Above 8 years
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) 4-7 years

Explanation: Symptoms of ADHD typically appear early in life. DSM-5 criteria require several symptoms to be present prior to age 12, but they are often identified during early school years (4-7 years).

6.
Regarding pressure sore ulcers
a) Stage one is characterized by erythema with skin breakage on pressure
b) Stage three is also classified as partial thickness wound
c) Stage four the ulcer extends into the subcutaneous tissue
d) Stage two looks like a blister filled with clear fluid
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Stage two looks like a blister filled with clear fluid

Explanation: Stage 2 pressure injury involves partial-thickness loss of dermis presenting as a shallow open ulcer with a red-pink wound bed, without slough. It may also present as an intact or open/ruptured serum-filled blister.

7.
One of the following is a principle task in the latent stage of growth and development according to FREUD
a) Toilet training
b) Weaning
c) Learning
d) Sexual identity
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Learning

Explanation: Freud's Latency Stage (6 years to puberty) is characterized by the suppression of sexual impulses. The child's energy is channeled into developing social skills, learning, and peer relationships.

8.
Planning process can be sequenced as follows:
a) Setting goals, implementation, assessment, evaluation
b) Assessment, setting goals, implementation, evaluation
c) Assessment, implementation, setting goals, evaluation
d) Setting goals, assessment, implementation, evaluation
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Assessment, setting goals, implementation, evaluation

Explanation: This follows the standard management or nursing process cycle: Assess the situation, Plan (Set goals), Implement the plan, and Evaluate the results.

9.
The correct order of steps in disciplinary process is
a) Preliminary investigation, discussion with the offender, strong verbal warning, written warning, suspension
b) Strong verbal warning, discussion with the offender, preliminary investigation, written warning, suspension
c) Discussion with the offender, strong verbal warning, preliminary investigation, written warning, suspension
d) Preliminary investigation, strong verbal warning, written warning, discussion with the offender, suspension
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Preliminary investigation, discussion with the offender, strong verbal warning, written warning, suspension

Explanation: Due process requires establishing facts first (investigation), hearing the employee's side (discussion), then escalating sanctions (verbal -> written -> suspension/dismissal).

10.
Indicate whether the following are True or False:
a) A diagnosis and confirmation of only one case of measles at a health facility is and epidemic
b) Incidence is the total number of new and old cases of a disease in a community
Show Answer

Answer:

  • a) True: For diseases targeted for elimination like Measles, a single confirmed case constitutes an outbreak/epidemic and requires immediate investigation.
  • b) False: Incidence refers only to new cases. Prevalence refers to total (new + old) cases.
11.
The role of reinforcement during teaching is:
a) Promotion of student’s participation
b) Evaluation of student’s prior knowledge
c) Assignment of tasks and discussions
d) Achievement of mental closure
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Promotion of student’s participation

Explanation: Reinforcement (positive feedback) encourages students to continue engaging and participating in the learning process.

12.
A disadvantage of self-directed learning would include:
a) Learning at individual pace
b) Lack of maintenance of programmed materials
c) Covers limited amount of subject matter
d) There is no contact between learner and teacher
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) There is no contact between learner and teacher

Explanation: The lack of direct interaction, guidance, and immediate feedback from an instructor is a primary disadvantage of pure self-directed learning.

13.
The following are components of a vaccine vial
a) Vaccine vial monitor, expiry date, refrigeration period
b) Batch number, used dosages, right cold chain temperature
c) Vaccine reaction, manufacturer’s literature, expiry date
d) Vaccine vial monitor, expiry date, batch number
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Vaccine vial monitor, expiry date, batch number

Explanation: These are standard labels on a vaccine vial. The VVM monitors heat exposure, expiry ensures potency, and batch number tracks specific lots.

14.
The following vaccines should be discarded after a vaccination session:
a) Measles, BCG, Yellow Fever
b) Measles, BCG, Polio
c) Polio, Pentavalent, Tetanus
d) Tetanus, BCG, Yellow Fever
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Measles, BCG, Yellow Fever

Explanation: Reconstituted live vaccines (BCG, Measles, Yellow Fever) must be discarded at the end of the session or after 6 hours (whichever comes first) because they lose potency and are prone to contamination.

15.
The vaccine that is heat stable, not destroyed rapidly at room temperature and remains active for at least 6 weeks at 37 degrees is
a) Pentavalent
b) BCG
c) Tetanus Toxoid
d) Measles
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Tetanus Toxoid

Explanation: Tetanus toxoid is highly heat stable compared to live vaccines. However, it is sensitive to freezing.

16.
The following principle of growth and development is correctly defined:
a) Cephalocaudal which states that development proceeds from the center of the body outward
b) Proximodistal which states that development proceeds from top to bottom
c) Orthogenic which states that development progresses from lack of differentiation to increasing differentiation
d) Hierarchical which states that development proceeds from the outward to the centre and simple skills develop together
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Orthogenic which states that development progresses from lack of differentiation to increasing differentiation

Explanation: - Cephalocaudal means head-to-toe (Top to Bottom). - Proximodistal means center-to-outward. - Orthogenic principle describes development from simple/undifferentiated to complex/differentiated.

17.
Wilms tumor is the most common malignant tumor of the kidneys and it's characterized by the following manifestations
a) A firm mass within the abdomen, haematuria, hypotension, abdominal pains
b) Hypertension, abdominal pains, headache, fever
c) A firm mass within the abdomen, haematuria, abdominal pains, fever
d) A firm mass within the abdomen, anorexia and vomiting, hiccups, abdominal pains
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) A firm mass within the abdomen, haematuria, abdominal pains, fever

Explanation: Classic signs include a palpable abdominal mass (often discovered while bathing), hematuria (blood in urine), abdominal pain, and sometimes fever. Hypertension (not hypotension) is common due to renin secretion.

18.
In partent ductus arteriosus, there is an abnormal opening between:
a) Aorta and pulmonary artery
b) Right and left atria
c) Pulmonary vein and pulmonary artery
d) Aorta and pulmonary vein
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Aorta and pulmonary artery

Explanation: PDA is the failure of the ductus arteriosus (connecting the aorta and pulmonary artery) to close after birth.

19.
Organophosphate poisoning should be suspected in a child who presents with:
a) Tremors, sweating, excessive salivation, diarrhea
b) Excessive diarrhea, salivation, sweating, dyspnea
c) Pinpoint pupils, dyspnea, sweating, muscle tremors
d) Sweating, tremors, excessive salivation, pin point pupils
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Sweating, tremors, excessive salivation, pin point pupils

Explanation: Based on the cholinergic crisis signs (SLUDGE/DUMBELS mnemonic): Salivation, Lacrimation, Urination, Defecation, GI cramps, Emesis. Also Miosis (pinpoint pupils) and muscle tremors.

20.
The main emphasis of community health nursing is
a) Treatment of health problems
b) Identification of health problems
c) Identification and assessment of health problems
d) Prevention of health problems and promoting optimum health
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Prevention of health problems and promoting optimum health

Explanation: While CHN involves care of the sick, its primary focus is on health promotion and disease prevention (Preventive and Promotive).

21.
In epidemiology, the types of analytical studies include
a) Observational and experimental studies
b) Cross-sectional and case studies
c) Cross-sectional and observational studies
d) Experimental and descriptive studies
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Observational and experimental studies

Explanation: Analytical epidemiology tests hypotheses. It includes Observational studies (Cohort, Case-Control) and Experimental studies (RCTs).

23.
The diagnostic stage of malaria is
a) Erythrocytic
b) Sporozoitic
c) Hypnozoitic
d) Oocyst

The stage in HIV/AIDS characterized by recurrent respiratory infections and herpes zoster is:

32.
The direct health effects of ozone depletion include:
a) Increased risk of skin cancer and breast cancer
b) Increased risk of skin cancer and cataracts
c) Increased exposure to vector bone diseases and skin cancer
d) Increased risk of cataracts and exposure to vector borne diseases
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Increased risk of skin cancer and cataracts

Explanation: Ozone depletion allows more UV-B radiation to reach Earth, causing increased rates of skin cancer (melanoma and non-melanoma) and cataracts.

33.
In the treatment of tuberculosis, the "DOTs" approach refers to
a) The patient getting TB treatment as close to home as possible

Section 2: Short Answer Questions (40 Marks)

41.
As a nurse conducting community diagnosis you are required to select a competent interviewer for the exercise. Outline five (5) qualities of an interviewer that you will consider in your selection process (5 marks)
Show Answer

Answer:

  1. Communication Skills: Ability to speak clearly, listen actively, and understand the local language/dialect.
  2. Interpersonal Skills: Friendly, approachable, and able to build rapport with community members.
  3. Objectivity/Non-judgmental: Ability to record answers accurately without influencing the respondent or showing bias.
  4. Integrity/Honesty: Trustworthy to collect real data and not falsify information.
  5. Knowledgeable: Understanding of the survey tool/questionnaire and the purpose of the study.
  6. Respectful: Culturally sensitive and respectful of community norms and privacy.
42.
State five (5) challenges that you are likely to face while applying the multidisciplinary approach to health care service delivery (5 marks)
Show Answer

Answer:

  1. Role Conflict/Ambiguity: Unclear boundaries regarding who is responsible for what.
  2. Communication Breakdown: Different professions using different jargon or failing to share information effectively.
  3. Power Struggles/Hierarchy Issues: Dominance by certain professions (e.g., physicians) over others.
  4. Scheduling Conflicts: Difficulty coordinating meetings or care plans among busy professionals.
  5. Differing Philosophies: Conflicting approaches to patient care (e.g., medical model vs. social model).
  6. Resource Allocation: Competition for limited funds or equipment among disciplines.
43.
In village Y, substance abuse has led to many school drop outs. State five (5) factors that motivate youths to substance use/abuse in a community (5 marks)
Show Answer

Answer:

  1. Peer Pressure: Desire to fit in or belong to a group.
  2. Curiosity/Experimentation: Desire to experience new feelings or "highs."
  3. Stress relief/Coping mechanism: Using substances to escape poverty, family problems, or academic pressure.
  4. Availability and Accessibility: Easy access to cheap drugs/alcohol in the community.
  5. Lack of Guidance/Role Models: Poor parenting, lack of supervision, or family history of abuse.
  6. Idleness/Unemployment: Lack of recreational activities or jobs.
44.
You are a newly recruited nurse in a Non-Governmental Organization whose key function is to carry out health promotion activities. Your first assignment is to help in the selection of appropriate health promotion interventions. State five (5) considerations that will guide your selection process (5 marks)
Show Answer

Answer:

  1. Community Needs: Is the intervention relevant to the priority health problems identified?
  2. Available Resources: Are there sufficient funds, personnel, and materials?
  3. Cost-Effectiveness: Does the intervention provide good value/impact for the money spent?
  4. Cultural Acceptability: Is the intervention compatible with local customs and beliefs?
  5. Feasibility/Sustainability: Can it be implemented practically and maintained over time?
  6. Evidence-Based Practice: Has this intervention proven effective elsewhere?
45.
When planning for outreach services in your facility catchment area, outline five (5) activities that you will include in your plan (5 marks)

  1. Immunization services: Providing vaccines to eligible children and mothers.
  2. Antenatal Care (ANC): Providing checkups for pregnant women.

46.
Outline four (4) determinants of nutrition that you will share with an audience in your community that is in need of nutrition counseling (4 marks)
Show Answer

Answer:

  1. Food Availability/Security: Physical access to sufficient food (farming, markets).
  2. Economic Status/Income: Ability to purchase diverse and nutritious foods.
  3. Knowledge/Education: Understanding of balanced diets, cooking methods, and hygiene.
  4. Cultural/Religious Beliefs: Taboos or preferences affecting food choices.
  5. Health Status: Disease conditions (e.g., worms, chronic illness) affecting appetite or absorption.
47.
The number of cases of HIV in health centre K is on the increase. Explain three (3) strategies employed by the Kenyan health sector in response towards combating HIV/AIDS (6 marks)
Show Answer

Answer:

  1. Prevention of Mother-to-Child Transmission (PMTCT): Providing ARVs to pregnant mothers, safe delivery practices, and infant prophylaxis to prevent vertical transmission.
  2. Voluntary Medical Male Circumcision (VMMC): Promoting male circumcision which reduces the risk of heterosexually acquired HIV infection in men.
  3. Antiretroviral Therapy (ART) / Test and Treat: Initiating treatment immediately upon diagnosis for all HIV-positive individuals to suppress viral load and reduce transmission (Treatment as Prevention).
  4. Condom Promotion and Distribution: Ensuring availability and proper use of condoms to prevent sexual transmission.
  5. Health Education and Behavior Change Communication (BCC): Promoting abstinence, faithfulness, and risk reduction.
48.
You have been employed as the team leader of a home based care program. Outline five (5) limitations to networking that you are likely to encounter. (5 marks)
Show Answer

Answer:

  1. Competition for Resources: Organizations may be reluctant to share funding or credit.
  2. Communication Barriers: Lack of clear channels or regular meetings between stakeholders.
  3. Lack of Commitment: Some partners may not actively participate or follow through on agreements.
  4. Different Organizational Goals/Mandates: Conflicting priorities between NGOs, government, and CBOs.
  5. Time Constraints: Networking requires time for meetings and coordination which may be seen as taking away from actual service delivery.
  6. Bureaucracy/Red Tape: Rigid protocols in some organizations slowing down collaborative decision making.

Section 3: Long Answer Question (20 Marks)

49.
As a nurse manager in a rural health facility, your primary task of overseeing proper planning and ensuring the overall functioning of the facility will require you to utilize a variety of leadership functions. Describe the key leadership functions you would utilize to meet your objectives.
Show Answer

Answer:

The key management/leadership functions required to run the facility effectively include:

1. Planning:

  • Setting goals and objectives for the facility (e.g., increasing immunization coverage).
  • Developing strategies and policies to achieve these goals.
  • Budgeting and resource allocation (anticipating needs for drugs, equipment).

2. Organizing:

  • Structuring the work: Assigning tasks and defining roles/responsibilities.
  • Grouping activities (e.g., MCH, Outpatient, Laboratory).
  • Establishing lines of authority and communication channels.

3. Staffing (Human Resource Management):

  • Recruiting and selecting competent staff (or lobbying for them).
  • Orientation and induction of new staff.
  • Staff development/training (CME) to update skills.
  • Creating duty rotas/schedules to ensure coverage.

4. Directing/Leading:

  • Providing guidance, supervision, and motivation to staff.
  • Communicating effectively with the team.
  • Resolving conflicts among staff members.
  • Modeling professional behavior.

5. Controlling/Monitoring:

  • Setting standards of performance.
  • Measuring actual performance against standards (Audits).
  • Taking corrective action when deviations occur (e.g., addressing drug shortages or absenteeism).
  • Evaluating the overall success of facility programs.

6. Coordinating:

  • Synchronizing activities across different departments to ensure smooth patient flow.
  • Networking with community leaders and other stakeholders.
