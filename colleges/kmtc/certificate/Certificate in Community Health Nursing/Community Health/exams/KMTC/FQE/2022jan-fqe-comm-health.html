Section 1: Multiple Choice Questions (40 Marks)

1.
Developmental tasks of a launching centre family include: -
a) Child socialization
b) Cultivating leisure time activities
c) Releasing children as young adults
d) Open parent-child communication
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Releasing children as young adults

Explanation: The "launching center" stage (Stage V of Duvall's family life cycle) is characterized by the first child leaving home until the last child leaves. The primary task is releasing young adults into the world while maintaining a supportive home base.

2.
A process by which a community is made alert of its problems and the available resources, such as, manpower, money, materials, ideas and time is community;
a) Sensitization
b) Awareness
c) Mobilization
d) Participation
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Sensitization

Explanation: Sensitization is the process of creating awareness and making the community "alert" or responsive to specific issues and available resources. Mobilization involves the actual bringing together of resources for action.

3.
The ability of a person to make and maintain acceptable interactions with other people in a community is:-
a) Physical health
b) Mental health
c) Social health
d) Emotional health
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Social health

Explanation: Social health is defined as the ability to form satisfying interpersonal relationships and adapt comfortably to different social situations and act appropriately in a variety of settings.

4.
Preventive health services offered at a MCH/FP clinic include;
a) Exercises, good nutritional practises, personal hygiene,
b) Growth monitoring and screening, breast examination
c) Diagnosis and treatment of minor ailments and referral
d) Immunization, health education, antenatal and postnatal care
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Immunization, health education, antenatal and postnatal care

Explanation: Immunization (preventing disease) and health education are core preventive services. Antenatal/Postnatal care involves monitoring to prevent complications. Diagnosis and treatment (C) are curative.

5.
Functions of the division of family health included in the county department of health are :-
a) Referral and ambulance, health facilities, mental health
b) Occupational health, waste management, vector control
c) Nutritional services, expanded program of immunization, adolescent health
d) Infrastructure and maintenance, procurement, health facility management
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Nutritional services, expanded program of immunization, adolescent health

Explanation: The Division of Family Health typically oversees Reproductive, Maternal, Newborn, Child, and Adolescent Health (RMNCAH) and Nutrition. Option B is Public/Environmental Health; Option A is Clinical Services; Option D is Administration.

6.
The following Act of the Kenyan laws prescribes food safety;
a) Nurses act CAP 257
b) Public health Act CAP 242
c) Clinical officers act CAP 241
d) Medical practitioners act CAP 253
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Public health Act CAP 242

Explanation: The Public Health Act CAP 242 is the primary legislation in Kenya governing public health matters, including food safety and hygiene standards.

7.
The role of a community health nurse that involves advising other health care providers on the needs of the community is;
a) Planner
b) Manager
c) Advocator
d) Educator
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Advocator

Explanation: As an advocate, the nurse speaks on behalf of the community, ensuring their needs are understood and met by other healthcare providers and policymakers. While "Educator" fits "advising", "Advocator" better fits "advising *providers* on *community needs*" (representing the client).

8.
The following vaccines are administered to infants at ten (10) weeks;
a) Measles, BCG, rotavirus vaccine
b) Vitamin A, oral polio 2(OPV 2), pneumococcal
c) Oral Polio (OPV 2), pneumococcal, pentavalent 2
d) Inactivated polio vaccine (IPV), measles, hepatitis B
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Oral Polio (OPV 2), pneumococcal, pentavalent 2

Explanation: The KEPI schedule at 10 weeks includes: OPV 2, DPT-HepB-Hib 2 (Pentavalent), PCV 10 (Pneumococcal) 2, and Rotavirus 2.

9.
The following is used to measure success in health education process;
a) Changes in behavior and attitudes.
b) The ability to incorporate advance technology
c) The willingness of all stakeholders to support the program.
d) The attentiveness of the audience during health education.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Changes in behavior and attitudes.

Explanation: The ultimate goal and measure of success for health education is the adoption of positive health behaviors and attitudes by the target audience.

10.
A concept of Primary Health Care (PHC) that ensures rural health services are provided by methods that are within the social cultural norms of a community is:-
a) Accessibility
b) Affordability
c) Availability
d) Acceptability
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Acceptability

Explanation: Acceptability means that the health services are offered in a way that respects the culture, traditions, and social norms of the community, ensuring they are willing to use them.

11.
The following are additional elements of primary health care (PHC) added by the Kenyan government;
a) Education, nutrition, local disease control, malaria control
b) Mental health, dental health, community based rehabilitation, malaria control
c) Family planning, expanded programme of immunization, essential drug supply, local disease control
d) Community based rehabilitation, safe water supply, nutrition and adequate food supply, mental health
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Mental health, dental health, community based rehabilitation, malaria control

Explanation: The original Alma Ata declaration had 8 elements. Kenya added Mental Health, Dental Health, and Community Based Rehabilitation (and often control of HIV/AIDS is cited) to tailor it to local needs.

12.
Resources targeted during Community mobilization include the following;
a) Money, land, materials, manpower
b) Money, materials, time, manpower.
c) Community participation, time, manpower.
d) Community participation, money and land.

Explanation: Resource mobilization typically targets the "4 Ms" (Manpower, Money, Materials, Minutes/Time) or similar variations. Time is a critical resource to mobilize.

13.
Level one health care delivery in Kenya is provided by.
a) The family and community members.
b) The enrolment community health workers
c) Trained public health nurses
d) Traditional birth attendants.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) The family and community members.

Explanation: Under the KEPH structure, Level 1 is the Community strategy level, involving households, families, and community members, supported by Community Health Promoters (CHPs).

14.

a) Praziquantel 40mg/kg body weight as a single dose
b) Metronidazole tabs 400mg tds x5/7
c) Amphotericin B 3mg/kg body weight for 10days
d) Amoxicillin caps 500mg tds x5/7
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Praziquantel 40mg/kg body weight as a single dose

Explanation: Praziquantel is the standard, effective treatment for all forms of Schistosomiasis (Bilharzia).

15.
Incubation period is;
a) The time of infection and death
b) Appearance of clinical symptoms and death
c) Time of infection and appearance of antibodies
d) The time of infection and appearance of clinical symptoms
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) The time of infection and appearance of clinical symptoms

Explanation: Incubation period is the time elapsed between exposure to a pathogen and the first appearance of symptoms.

16.
The correct stages in the progression of measles are:-
a) Prodromal phase, eruptive phase, post-measles phase
b) Pre-measles phase, prodromal phase, post-measles phase
c) Pre-measles phase, eruptive phase, post-measles phase
d) Eruptive phase, pre-measles phase, post-measles phase
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Pre-measles phase, eruptive phase, post-measles phase

Explanation: The clinical course is typically: 1. Pre-measles/Prodromal (fever, cough, Koplik spots), 2. Eruptive phase (rash), 3. Post-measles/Convalescence (recovery/desquamation). Option C captures this sequence best.

17.
Environmental factors whose effects are commonly expressed as stress, depression, hysteria are;
a) Chemical hazards
b) Physical hazards
c) Psychological hazards
d) Biological hazards
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Psychological hazards

Explanation: These symptoms (stress, depression, hysteria) are psychological in nature and result from psychological hazards such as workplace stress, trauma, or social isolation.

18.
You are a Nurse working with local NGO and you have been assigned to conduct community dialogue in village X. What will be your first action?
a) Meet with the local leaders in the village
b) Develop and action plan
c) Do a transect walk in the village
d) Prioritize community needs
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Meet with the local leaders in the village

Explanation: Community entry is the first step. Meeting local leaders establishes rapport, gains permission, and ensures the safety and acceptance of the nurse in the community.

19.
The following preventive/promotive services are appropriate to Late Childhood (6- 12 yrs) cohort.
a) Antenatal care, breastfeeding
b) Vaccination, growth monitoring
c) Supplementary feeding, de-worming
d) Nutritional care, insecticide treated net use
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Supplementary feeding, de-worming

Explanation: School-going children (6-12 years) are often targeted for de-worming programs and school feeding (supplementary feeding) to improve nutritional status and school attendance. Vaccination/growth monitoring is primarily for Under-5s.

20.
The fundamental learning process aimed at changing behaviour and attitude with respect to health practices thus involves family individual and community is :-
a) Health analysis
b) Health education
c) Health promotion
d) Family counseling
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Health education

Explanation: Health education is defined as any combination of learning experiences designed to help individuals and communities improve their health, by increasing their knowledge or influencing their attitudes.

21.
Voluntary action in health education is;
a) The process of creating favorable conditions for learning in the community
b) An interactive process aimed at changing behavior specific messages and different communication approaches
c) The taking up behavioral measures by a person to achieve an intended health effect with full understanding and acceptance of purposes
d) The process of sharing ideas, information, knowledge, and experience among people for behavior change

Correct Answer: c) The taking up behavioral measures by a person to achieve an intended health effect with full understanding and acceptance of purposes

Explanation: "Voluntary action" implies that the individual chooses to adopt the behavior willingly, understanding the benefits, rather than being coerced.

22.
Health promotion implies;
a) Offering curative services to the community
b) Detecting diseases in good time
c) Enabling people to increase control over their heath
d) Averting diseases in specific populations
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Enabling people to increase control over their heath

Explanation: The WHO Ottawa Charter defines Health Promotion as "the process of enabling people to increase control over, and to improve, their health."

23.
The correct sequence of activities during community diagnosis is;
a) Planning of the survey, exploration, pretesting survey tools, report writing, dissemination and community action
b) Exploration, planning of the survey, pretesting survey tools, report writing, dissemination and community action
c) Pretesting survey tools, planning of the survey, exploration, report writing, dissemination and community action
d) Exploration, pretesting survey tools, planning of the survey, report writing, dissemination and community action.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Exploration, planning of the survey, pretesting survey tools, report writing, dissemination and community action

Explanation: The logical flow is: 1. Exploration (Community Entry/Social mapping), 2. Planning the full survey, 3. Pretesting tools, 4. Data collection/Analysis/Reporting, 5. Dissemination, 6. Action.

24.
Which of the following participatory methods would best be used to get information from the opinion leaders in the community?
a) Focus group discussion
b) Transect walk
c) Seasonal calendars
d) Key informant interviews
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Key informant interviews

Explanation: Opinion leaders are "Key Informants" who possess specialized knowledge or influence. One-on-one interviews (Key Informant Interviews - KII) are best suited for them. FGDs are for groups; Transect walks for observation; Calendars for temporal events.

25.
A rural health unit under the Ministry of health that is charged with the responsibility of providing services to a particular catchment area is;
a) Clinic
b) Dispensary
c) Health centre
d) Sub-county hospital
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Health centre

Explanation: A Health Centre is historically defined as the basic rural health unit (Level 3) serving a specific catchment population (approx 50,000-70,000), offering curative and preventive services. Dispensaries are smaller.

a) Veracity
b) Fidelity

27.
The mode of action for combined oral contraceptive is;
a) Ovulation inhibition, thickening of mucus

c) Slowed motility, enhanced ovulation
d) Increased motility in uterine tubes, thickening of endometrium

28.
The following family planning method involves recording body temperature with observing cervical mucus;
a) Fertility awareness method
b) Symptom-thermal method
c) Lactation amenorrhea method
d) Basal Body Temperature method

29.
A nurse is teaching a client with genital herpes. Education for this client should include an explanation of
a) Why the disease is transmittable only when visible lesions are present
b) The need for the use of petroleum products.
c) Option of disregarding safer-sex practices now that he's already infected.
d) The importance of informing his partners of the disease.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) The importance of informing his partners of the disease.

Explanation: Partners need to be informed to make choices about their health. Transmission can occur without lesions (asymptomatic shedding), petroleum damages condoms, and safer sex is still required.

30.
An appropriate nursing intervention to provide emotional support to a patient with an STD is;
a) Use concerned listening when the patient expresses negative feelings.
b) Reassure the patient that the disease is curable with appropriate treatment.
c) Offer many alternatives that the patient can use to change sexual relationships.
d) Help the patient who is an innocent sexual partner forgive the infecting partner.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Use concerned listening when the patient expresses negative feelings.

Explanation: Concerned (active) listening validates the patient's feelings and provides emotional support. Reassurance (B) may be false (some STDs like HIV/Herpes are not "curable"). Judging relationships (C) or forcing forgiveness (D) is not therapeutic support.

31.
The ability of a disease causing agent to penetrate, multiply and produce some detectable change (establish itself) in the host is;
a) Infectivity
b) Stability
c) Virulence
d) Pathogenicity
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Pathogenicity

Explanation: Pathogenicity is the ability to cause disease (detectable change/clinical symptoms). Infectivity is the ability to enter and multiply. Virulence is the severity of the disease produced.

32.
A therapeutic intervention applied on mentally ill persons that involves exposing the patient to dreaded or fearful stimuli while preventing avoidant behavior is;
a) Flooding
b) Desensitization
c) Modeling
d) Reattribution
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Flooding

Explanation: Flooding (or exposure with response prevention) involves intense exposure to the feared stimulus without allowing the patient to escape (avoid), until the anxiety subsides.

33.
The abnormal sexual behavior in which a man fantasizes having sex with a woman whom he has been observing undress is
a) Exhibitionism
b) Voyeurism
c) Frotteurism
d) Transvestitism
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Voyeurism

Explanation: Voyeurism involves deriving sexual gratification from observing unsuspecting people who are naked, undressing, or engaging in sexual activity. Exhibitionism is exposing oneself.

35.
A statistical measure in which the numerator is not related to the denominator is: -
a) Rate
b) Ratio
c) Proportion
d) Percentage
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Ratio

Explanation: A ratio expresses the relationship between two separate quantities (e.g., Male:Female) where the numerator is not necessarily a subset of the denominator. In proportions and rates, the numerator is usually a subset of the denominator.

36.
The persistent occurrence of a disease at a low to moderate level in a community is known as: -
a) Pandemic level
b) Sporadic level
c) Endemic level
d) Epidemic level
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Endemic level

Explanation: Endemic refers to the constant presence or usual prevalence of a disease within a geographic area. Epidemic is an outbreak above expected levels. Sporadic is irregular/infrequent.

a) Planning
b) Evaluation

38.
Which of the following approaches is likely to be most effective for a school nurse to use in working with families in a respectful and nondiscriminatory manner?
a) Being professional and sensitive to factors related to socioeconomic, ethnic, and cultural backgrounds
b) Attempting to discover pertinent characteristics of families' backgrounds before meeting with them

39.
Screening in school health services involves;
a) Treatment of the sick school children
b) Taking note of any unusual signs or symptoms
c) Referral of sick pupils for further treatment
d) Health examination to determine the pupil's health status
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Taking note of any unusual signs or symptoms

Explanation: Screening involves identifying unrecognized disease or defects (unusual signs) in an apparently healthy population. D is a broader "examination". Screening is specifically looking for "red flags" (signs/symptoms) to filter who needs diagnosis.

Section 2: Short Answer Questions (40 Marks)

41.
Outline five (5) components of the high priority package according to the Kenya Essential Package of Health (KEPH) (5 marks)
Show Answer

Model Answer:

  1. Reproductive, Maternal, Neonatal, Child, and Adolescent Health (RMNCAH): Includes family planning, antenatal care, delivery services.
  2. Control of Communicable Diseases: HIV/AIDS, TB, Malaria control.
  3. Control of Non-Communicable Diseases (NCDs): Diabetes, hypertension, cancer screening/management.
  4. Environmental Health/Sanitation: Water quality, waste disposal.
  5. Mental Health Services: Counseling, substance abuse management.
  6. Health Promotion and Community Health Services: Education and community strategy.
42.
Outline four (4) characteristics of a healthy community (4 marks)
Show Answer

Model Answer:

  1. Strong Leadership: Effective leaders who can mobilize the community.
  2. Active Participation: Community members are involved in identifying and solving their own problems.
  3. Access to Resources: Availability of essential services like healthcare, clean water, and schools.
  4. Shared Values/Cohesion: A sense of belonging and ability to work together towards common goals.
  5. Ability to Solve Problems: Capacity to identify issues and implement solutions.
  6. Safe Environment: Low crime rates and absence of environmental hazards.
43.
State four (4) ways through which home accidents can be prevented in the family (4 marks)
Show Answer

Model Answer:

  1. Safe Storage of Chemicals/Meds: Keeping medicines, cleaning agents, and pesticides out of reach of children (locked cupboards).
  2. Proper Lighting: Ensuring hallways and staircases are well-lit to prevent falls.
  3. Kitchen Safety: Turning pot handles inward on stoves; keeping sharp knives secured.
  4. Eliminating Trip Hazards: Removing loose rugs, cords, or clutter from walkways.
  5. Fire Safety: Having smoke detectors; keeping matches away from children.
  6. Water Safety: Covering wells/tanks; never leaving children unattended in bathtubs.
44.
Explain three (3) portals of entry of disease causing agents into the human body (6 marks)
Show Answer

Model Answer:

  1. Respiratory Tract: Pathogens enter through inhalation of droplets or dust (e.g., TB, Influenza, Measles).
  2. Gastrointestinal Tract (Oral): Pathogens enter through ingestion of contaminated food or water (e.g., Cholera, Typhoid, Polio).
  3. Skin/Mucous Membranes: Pathogens enter through breaks in the skin (wounds), bites (vectors), or direct penetration (e.g., Tetanus, Malaria, Schistosomiasis).
  4. Genitourinary Tract: Pathogens enter through sexual contact (e.g., HIV, Syphilis, Gonorrhea).
  5. Transplacental (Vertical): From mother to fetus via the placenta (e.g., HIV, Rubella).
45.
Outline five (5) factors that influence nutrition status of a community (5 marks)
Show Answer

Model Answer:

  1. Economic Status (Poverty): Ability to purchase adequate and diverse food.
  2. Food Availability/Security: Seasonal variations, drought, or agricultural productivity.
  3. Cultural Beliefs/Taboos: Restrictions on certain foods for specific groups (e.g., women/children) limiting intake.
  4. Knowledge/Education: Understanding of balanced diets and cooking methods.
  5. Disease Burden: High prevalence of infections (diarrhea, worms) that affect nutrient absorption.
  6. Family Size: Larger families may have less food per person.
46.
Outline five (5) features of avoidant personality disorder (5 marks)
Show Answer

Model Answer:

  1. Social Inhibition: Extreme shyness and avoidance of social activities due to fear of criticism.
  2. Feelings of Inadequacy: Low self-esteem and viewing oneself as socially inept or inferior.
  3. Hypersensitivity to Negative Evaluation: Intense fear of rejection, disapproval, or ridicule.
  4. Avoidance of Interpersonal Contact: Avoiding jobs or activities involving significant social contact.
  5. Reluctance to take risks: Fear of embarrassment prevents trying new things.
  6. Desire for affection: Unlike schizoid personality, they *want* relationships but are too afraid to pursue them.
47.
Explain three (3) roles of a community health nurse in the prevention of Covid-19 disease (5 marks)
Show Answer

Model Answer:

  1. Health Education: Teaching the community about transmission, hand hygiene, mask-wearing, and social distancing.
  2. Contact Tracing and Surveillance: Identifying cases, tracing contacts, and monitoring them for symptoms to break the chain of transmission.
  3. Vaccination: Administering COVID-19 vaccines and mobilizing the community to accept them.
  4. Home Based Care Support: Monitoring and guiding patients isolating at home to prevent complications and spread.
48.
State five (5) factors than can hinder effective community participation (5 marks)
Show Answer

Model Answer:

  1. Poverty/Lack of Resources: Daily survival needs take precedence over meeting attendance.
  2. Poor Leadership: Lack of trust in community leaders or health workers.
  3. Illiteracy/Ignorance: Inability to understand the issues or proposed interventions.
  4. Cultural Barriers: Norms that exclude certain groups (e.g., women, youth) from decision making.
  5. Lack of Communication: Poor information flow between health system and community.
  6. Donor Dependency: Expectation of handouts rather than self-reliance.

Section 3: Long Answer Question (20 Marks)

49.
You have been deployed as a Nurse in a health centre within your sub-County with a principal role of planning and conducting weekly home visits to clients within your catchment area.

a) Outline four (4) specific objectives of home visiting that will applicable in ensuring successful weekly home visits (4 marks)

Show Answer
  1. Assessment: To assess the health status of the family and their home environment/living conditions.
  2. Case Finding: To identify new health problems or individuals needing care (e.g., defaulters, malnourished children).
  3. Health Education: To provide tailored health education to the family in their own setting.
  4. Care Provision: To provide nursing care (e.g., wound dressing, palliative care) to bedridden or home-bound clients.
  5. Follow-up: To monitor progress of clients discharged from hospital or on long-term treatment.

b) Describe how you will apply the relevant principles of family health nursing as a guide to the weekly home visits (10 marks)

Show Answer

To ensure success, I would apply the following principles:

  1. Family as the Unit of Care: I would focus not just on the sick individual, but assess how the illness affects the whole family and vice versa.
  2. Needs-Based Approach: I would base my interventions on the identified health needs of the family, prioritizing the most urgent ones.
  3. Community Participation: I would involve the family members in planning their own care to ensure they accept and own the health decisions (Self-care agency).
  4. Non-Judgmental Attitude: I would respect the family's values, culture, and lifestyle without imposing my own, building a trusting relationship.
  5. Health Promotion Focus: I would emphasize preventive measures (hygiene, nutrition) rather than just curative tasks.
  6. Resource Utilization: I would help the family identify and utilize available local resources to solve their health problems.
  7. Continuity of Care: I would ensure regular follow-up (weekly as planned) to evaluate progress and adjust care plans.

c) Explain three (3) ethics you will adhere to during the visits (6 marks)

Show Answer
  1. Confidentiality: Ensuring that private information gathered within the home (about family dynamics, HIV status, etc.) is not shared with neighbors or unauthorized persons.
  2. Informed Consent: Explaining the purpose of the visit and procedures to the family and seeking their permission before entering the home or providing care.
  3. Respect/Dignity: Treating all family members with respect, regardless of their socioeconomic status or living conditions, and honoring their home as their private sanctuary.
  4. Beneficence (Do Good): Acting in the best interest of the family, ensuring the visit brings positive health outcomes.
