Section 1: Multiple Choice Questions (40 Marks)

1.
The practice of nursing directed towards maximizing the health and well-being of all individuals within a family system is referred to as;
a) Family nursing
b) Family health care
c) Family nursing process
d) Family health nursing
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Family health nursing

Explanation: Family health nursing is defined as the practice of nursing directed towards maximizing the health and well-being of all individuals within a family system. It views the family as a unit of care.

2.
The incubation period is the interval between: -
a) The time of infection and death
b) Appearance of clinical symptoms and death
c) The time of infection and appearance of clinical symptoms
d) Time of infection and appearance of antibodies
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) The time of infection and appearance of clinical symptoms

Explanation: The incubation period is strictly defined as the time elapsed between exposure to a pathogenic organism (infection) and when symptoms and signs are first apparent.

3.
The following theory proposes that families go through competitive social systems, existence of family relationships with negotiations and problem solving;
a) Conflict theory
b) Exchange theory
c) Structural theory
d) Developmental theory
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Conflict theory

Explanation: Conflict theory views the family as a system where members have different interests and power is distributed unequally, leading to competition/negotiation. It posits that conflict is natural and inevitable in social groups.

  • Exchange theory: Focuses on cost-benefit analysis in relationships.
  • Structural functional theory: Focuses on the roles and functions of family members for stability.
4.
Assessment of a community by a nurse involves?
a) Identifying present and potential needs and resources related to health of patients.
b) Participation in planning for community health nursing services
c) Providing health education to inform the community on health problems
d) Ensuring that all records on reactions of clients, degree of satisfaction, are well kept
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Identifying present and potential needs and resources related to health of patients.

Explanation: Community assessment (diagnosis) is the process of identifying the community's health needs, strengths, and resources. Options B, C, and D describe planning, implementation, and evaluation phases respectively.

5.
Post-visit Activities in home visiting include;
a) Establishing trust and rapport with the patient and the family so that there can be a positive interpersonal relationship
b) Gathering information regarding the location of the house, distance from your health facility and the physical address
c) Recording and reporting important events of the home visits and sharing the reports with the appropriate stakeholders
d) Sharing information with the patient on the reason and purposes for home visits.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Recording and reporting important events of the home visits and sharing the reports with the appropriate stakeholders

Explanation: Post-visit activities occur after the visit is concluded. Documentation and reporting are key post-visit tasks. Options A and D happen during the visit (introductory/working phase), and B happens in the pre-visit phase.

6.
Efforts to detect and treat a health problem at its earliest possible state are referred to as:
a) Tertiary prevention
b) Primary prevention
c) Secondary prevention
d) Rehabilitative prevention
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Secondary prevention

Explanation: Secondary prevention focuses on early diagnosis and prompt treatment to halt disease progression (e.g., screening). Primary prevention prevents disease onset; Tertiary prevention limits disability after disease is established.

7.
One of the following is a reason for evaluation in health promotion?
a) To identify ways in which fewer resources can be used to achieve fewer objectives
b) To reflect on our practice and process to ensure that we are promoting and protecting health
c) To establish ways in which more resources can be used to achieve fewer objectives
d) To determine ways in which community participation can be limited.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) To reflect on our practice and process to ensure that we are promoting and protecting health

Explanation: Evaluation determines the effectiveness, efficiency, and impact of health promotion interventions. It helps in reflecting on practice to ensure goals (promoting and protecting health) are met.

8.
The following is an enabling factor in behavior change process:
a) Motivation to change from external means
b) Practicing trial behavior among communities that require change
c) Community networks that allow easy access to care
d) Effective communication and involvement of communities in assessment
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Community networks that allow easy access to care

Explanation: According to the PRECEDE-PROCEED model, enabling factors are antecedents that facilitate a behavior, such as availability of resources, accessibility of services, and community networks.

10.
Passive natural immunity is conveyed through: -
a) Having a clinical disease
b) Administration of tetanus toxoid
c) Getting serum immunoglobulin

11.
One of the following statements describe Direct Contact Transmission: -
a) Respiratory droplets carrying infectious pathogens
b) Dissemination of droplet nuclei containing infectious agents
c) Microbes transferred through contaminated intermediate object
d) Microbes directly transferred from an infected person to another person
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Microbes directly transferred from an infected person to another person

Explanation: Direct contact transmission requires physical contact between an infected person and a susceptible host (e.g., touching, kissing, sexual contact).

12.
The organism associated with cervical cancer is: -
a) Chlamydia trachomatis.
b) Candida albicans.
c) Trichomonas vaginalis
d) Human papillomavirus
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Human papillomavirus

Explanation: Human Papillomavirus (HPV), specifically high-risk types like 16 and 18, is the primary cause of cervical cancer.

13.
Benefits of intra-uterine contraceptive device include;
a) Protection against STIs and HIV/AIDS
b) Long-term and permanent protection
c) Destroys spermatozoa and fertilized ovum
d) Highly effective and long-term protection
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Highly effective and long-term protection

Explanation: IUCDs provide long-term (3-10+ years) and highly effective reversible contraception. They do not protect against STIs (Option A). They are not permanent (Option B, which refers to tubal ligation/vasectomy). While copper acts as spermicide (Option C), Option D is the best description of the *benefit*.

14.
The following appropriate drug prescribed for a patient suffering from gonorrhea is:
a) Tetracycline
b) Ceftriaxone
c) Penicillin
d) Levofloxacin
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Ceftriaxone

Explanation: Due to widespread resistance to penicillins, tetracyclines, and fluoroquinolones, Ceftriaxone (a cephalosporin) is the recommended first-line treatment for gonorrhea.

15.
The areas of interest in environmental health include;
a) Preventive and promotive of Health services
b) Curative and rehabilitation Health services
c) Monitoring of livestock for quality meat
d) Teaching people to grow enough food for nutrition
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Monitoring of livestock for quality meat

Explanation: Environmental health involves food hygiene and safety (meat inspection), water sanitation, waste management, etc. Monitoring livestock for meat quality is a direct environmental health function (food safety).

16.
A nurse is teaching a client with genital herpes. Education for this client should include an explanation of
a) The need for the use of petroleum products.
b) The importance of informing his partners of the disease.
c) Why the disease is transmittable only when visible lesions are present.
d) The option of disregarding safer-sex practices now that he's already infected.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) The importance of informing his partners of the disease.

Explanation: Partner notification is crucial to prevent spread. Herpes can be transmitted even when asymptomatic (viral shedding), making C incorrect. Petroleum products can damage condoms (A is unsafe). Safe sex is still needed to prevent superinfection or transmitting other strains (D is incorrect).

17.
When teaching a patient infected with HIV regarding transmission of the virus to others, which of the following statements made by the patient would identify a need for further education:
a) I will need to isolate any utensils I use so as not to infect my family."
b) I will notify all of my sexual partners so they can get tested for HIV."
c) Unprotected sexual contact is the most common mode of transmission."
d) I do not need to worry about spreading this virus to others by sweating at the gym."
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) I will need to isolate any utensils I use so as not to infect my family."

Explanation: HIV is not transmitted through casual contact like sharing utensils. If the patient believes this, they need further education (correction). Statements B, C, and D are factually correct.

23.
A person's belief in his ability to change his behavior is?
a) Locus of control
b) Self determination
c) Health promotion
d) Self-efficacy
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Self-efficacy

Explanation: Self-efficacy (a concept from Bandura's Social Cognitive Theory) refers to an individual's belief in their capacity to execute behaviors necessary to produce specific performance attainments.

<

27.
An illusion is best described as?
a) Pathological coining of new words
b) Misperception of an actual external stimuli
c) A perceptual experience that do not exist in reality
d) Incoherent mixture of words/phrases that make no sense to the listener
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Misperception of an actual external stimuli

Explanation: An illusion is a distortion of the senses, revealing how the brain normally organizes and interprets sensory stimulation (e.g., seeing a rope and thinking it is a snake). A hallucination (Option C) is a perception in the absence of external stimulus.

28.
Events that occur shortly before the onset of a mental disorder and appear to have induced it are referred to as;
a) Perpetuating factors
b) Predisposing factors
c) Precipitating factors
d) Aggravating factors
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Precipitating factors

Explanation: Precipitating factors are the immediate triggers or events that cause the onset of the disorder (the "straw that broke the camel's back"). Predisposing factors are underlying vulnerabilities (genetics, history).

d) Typhoid fever

31.
The most effective ventilation system for a rural home is
a) Ceiling fans
b) Open windows
c) Ventilation fans in the kitchen and bathroom
d) A mechanical system that exhausts air to the outside
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Open windows

Explanation: In rural settings, natural ventilation (cross-ventilation via open windows) is the most practical, cost-effective, and common method. While fans (C) are effective, they require electricity which may not be consistently available, making natural ventilation the standard "most effective" sustainable solution.

Section 2: Short Answer Questions (40 Marks)

41.
Outline five (5) Community Health Activities (5 marks)
Show Answer

Model Answer:

  1. Community Diagnosis/Assessment: Identifying health needs and resources.
  2. Health Education/Promotion: Educating the community on disease prevention and healthy lifestyles.
  3. Immunization Services: Conducting static and outreach vaccination programs.
  4. Maternal and Child Health (MCH) Services: Antenatal, postnatal, and well-baby clinics.
  5. Disease Surveillance and Control: Monitoring disease outbreaks and implementing control measures.
  6. Home Visiting: Following up on clients at the household level.
  7. Water and Sanitation Hygiene (WASH) Activities: Inspecting water sources and promoting latrine use.
42.
State the four (4) heat treatment methods of destroying micro-organism in food (4 marks)
Show Answer

Model Answer:

  1. Pasteurization: Heating to a specific temperature for a set time (e.g., milk) to kill pathogens.
  2. Boiling: Heating food/water to 100C.
  3. Sterilization (Autoclaving/Canning): Using high heat under pressure to kill all microorganisms and spores.
  4. Cooking (Baking/Roasting/Frying): Thorough cooking at high temperatures.
  5. Blanching: Brief boiling used for vegetables before freezing.
43.
Explain three (3) nurse responsibilities for a patient receiving antipsychotics (6 marks)
Show Answer

Model Answer:

  1. Monitoring for Side Effects: Specifically watching for Extrapyramidal Side Effects (EPSEs) like tremors, rigidity, or Tardive Dyskinesia, and Neuroleptic Malignant Syndrome.
  2. Patient Education: Teaching the patient about the importance of adherence, potential side effects, and avoiding alcohol/drugs while on medication.
  3. Monitoring Vital Signs: Regular checks of blood pressure (risk of orthostatic hypotension) and temperature.
  4. Ensuring Safety: Preventing falls due to sedation or dizziness and assessing for suicide risk during treatment initiation.
44.
Outline five (5) factors than can hinder community participation (5 marks)
Show Answer

Model Answer:

  1. Poverty/Economic Constraints: Community members may be too focused on daily survival to participate.
  2. Poor Leadership: Lack of trusted or effective leaders to mobilize the community.
  3. Lack of Awareness/Information: Community not understanding the importance of the program.
  4. Cultural/Social Barriers: Traditions or beliefs that conflict with the proposed health interventions.
  5. Poor Communication: Top-down approach by health workers without listening to community needs.
  6. Previous Negative Experiences: History of failed projects leading to apathy.
45.
Describe the pathogenesis of poliomyelitis (5 marks)
Show Answer

Model Answer:

  1. Entry: The virus enters through the mouth (fecal-oral route).
  2. Multiplication: It multiplies in the pharynx and the gastrointestinal tract (intestines).
  3. Lymphatic Invasion: It invades local lymphatic tissue (Peyer's patches) and enters the bloodstream (minor viremia).
  4. Spread: It may spread to the Central Nervous System (CNS) if not cleared by the immune system.
  5. Damage: In the CNS, it specifically destroys the anterior horn cells (motor neurons) of the spinal cord.
  6. Result: This destruction leads to Acute Flaccid Paralysis of the muscles innervated by those nerves.
46.
State five (5) factors influencing adult learning (5 marks)
Show Answer

Model Answer:

  1. Relevance: Adults learn best when the topic is relevant to their immediate needs or jobs.
  2. Prior Experience: Adults bring a wealth of experience that should be utilized/respected in the learning process.
  3. Motivation: Internal motivation (e.g., job promotion, solving a problem) is crucial.
  4. Readiness to Learn: Must correspond to their social roles.
  5. Respect: The learning environment must be respectful and collaborative, not authoritarian.
  6. Physical Environment: Comfort (lighting, seating) is important for aging sensory abilities.
47.
State four (4) effects of nosocomial infections to patients (4 marks)
Show Answer

Model Answer:

  1. Prolonged Hospital Stay: Recovery is delayed.
  2. Increased Cost: Higher medical bills due to extra treatment and drugs.
  3. Increased Morbidity/Disability: Long-term health complications.
  4. Increased Mortality: Risk of death, especially in immunocompromised patients.
  5. Psychological Stress: Anxiety and stress for the patient and family.
48.
Explain three (3) barriers to male involvement in reproductive health (6 marks)
Show Answer

Model Answer:

  1. Cultural Beliefs/Stereotypes: Reproductive health is often viewed as a "woman's affair" in many communities, leading to stigma for men who participate.
  2. Health Facility Environment: Clinics are often female-oriented (MCH), lacking privacy for men or having operating hours that conflict with men's work schedules.
  3. Attitude of Health Workers: Staff may be unwelcoming or judgmental towards men attending antenatal or family planning clinics.
  4. Lack of Information: Men may not understand their role or the benefits of their involvement in RH services.

Section 3: Long Answer Question (20 Marks)

49.
You have been deployed as a Nurse in a health center within your sub-County whereby you will be required to assist in planning for all primary health care activities within the immediate community.

a) Outline five (5) pillars of PHC that will guide your daily activities (5 marks)

Show Answer
  1. Community Participation: Involving the community in planning, implementing, and maintaining health services.
  2. Inter-sectoral Collaboration: Working with other sectors (agriculture, education, water) to address health determinants.
  3. Appropriate Technology: Using methods and equipment that are scientifically sound, adaptable to local needs, and affordable.
  4. Support Mechanism/Equity: Ensuring fair distribution of resources so that care reaches the most vulnerable.
  5. Focus on Prevention: Emphasizing preventive and promotive care over curative services.

b) State five (5) qualities that you will consider in selection of your community health workers (5 marks)

Show Answer
  1. Community Residency: Must be a permanent resident of the community they serve.
  2. Literacy: Ability to read and write (basic education) to keep records.
  3. Respect and Integrity: Must be a respected member of the community with good morals.
  4. Motivation/Voluntarism: Willingness to serve the community with minimal supervision.
  5. Communication Skills: Ability to communicate effectively in the local language.
  6. Availability: Have time to dedicate to community health activities.

c) Describe characteristics of a successful primary health care concept (10 marks)

Show Answer

A successful PHC concept is characterized by the 4 'A's and other key features:

  1. Accessibility: Health services must be geographically within reach (e.g., within 5km) and physically accessible to all, including the disabled.
  2. Affordability: The cost of health care should not prevent people from using the services; it should be within the economic means of the community.
  3. Acceptability: Services must be culturally appropriate and provided in a manner that respects the dignity and traditions of the community.
  4. Availability: Services, personnel, and supplies (drugs, equipment) must be available when needed.
  5. Appropriateness: The care provided must address the relevant health needs of the community using appropriate technology.
  6. Comprehensive Care: It should offer a mix of promotive, preventive, curative, and rehabilitative services.
  7. Continuous Care: It should provide longitudinal care over time rather than just episodic treatment.
