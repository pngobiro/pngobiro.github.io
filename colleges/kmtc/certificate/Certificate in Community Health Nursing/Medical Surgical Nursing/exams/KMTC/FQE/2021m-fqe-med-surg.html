Section 1: Multiple Choice Questions (40 Marks)

1.
Leucorrhea is a cardinal sign of the following eye conditions
a) Cataracts
b) Keratitis
c) Conjunctivitis
d) Retinoblerstoma
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Retinoblerstoma (Retinoblastoma)

Explanation: This question contains a terminology error. "Leucorrhea" refers to whitish vaginal discharge. The intended term is Leukocoria (white pupil reflex), which is the cardinal sign of Retinoblastoma, a childhood eye cancer.

2.
Which of the following statements best describes psoriasis
a) Is a type one hypersensitivity disorder
b) An inflammation of the deep dermal and subcutaneous tissues
c) A chronic non-infectious inflammatory disease of the skin
d) A serious autoimmune disease of the skin and mucous membrane
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) A chronic non-infectious inflammatory disease of the skin

Explanation: Psoriasis is classically defined as a chronic, non-infectious, inflammatory skin condition characterized by red patches with silver scales. While it is autoimmune (Option D), it primarily affects the skin and joints, whereas mucous membrane involvement is rare (unlike Pemphigus).

3.
A patient with a calculi in the gall bladder is said to have
a) Choledochotomy
b) Choledocholiathiasis
c) Cholelithiasis
d) Cholecystitis
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Cholelithiasis

Explanation:
- Cholelithiasis = Stones in the gallbladder.
- Choledocholithiasis = Stones in the common bile duct.
- Cholecystitis = Inflammation of the gallbladder.

4.
The main goal of nursing care of a patient in PACU include
a) Normal vital signs, bowel sounds have returned
b) Blood is running well, patient has passed urine
c) Vital signs are stable, absence of hemorrhage
d) Absence of hemorrhage, absence of infection
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Vital signs are stable, absence of hemorrhage

Explanation: The priority in the Post-Anesthesia Care Unit (PACU) is airway patency, circulatory stability (vital signs), and prevention of immediate complications like hemorrhage.

5.
Bad dreams, delirium and hallucinations are side effects of
a) Morphine sulphate
b) Suxamethonium
c) Ketamine hydrochloride
d) Thiopentone
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Ketamine hydrochloride

Explanation: Ketamine is known for causing "emergence phenomena," which include vivid dreams, hallucinations, and delirium upon waking.

6.
The innermost layer of the meninges of the spinal cord is the
a) Arachnoid mater
b) Subarachnoid mater
c) Dura mater
d) Pia mater
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Pia mater

Explanation: The meninges layers from outer to inner are: Dura mater -> Arachnoid mater -> Pia mater.

7.
The major causes of pyelonephritis include
a) Pseudomonas euroginosa
b) Streptococcus aureus
c) Mycobacterium tuberculi
d) Escherichia coli
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Escherichia coli

Explanation: E. coli is the most common causative agent of urinary tract infections, including pyelonephritis (kidney infection).

8.
Clinical signs of hemolytic blood reaction include
a) Fever, hypotension, dry skin
b) Rapid onset of facial flushing, headache, fever
c) Dyspnoea, dry cough, pulmonary edema
d) Itching, urticaria, general body malaise
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Rapid onset of facial flushing, headache, fever

Explanation: Acute hemolytic reaction signs include fever, chills, flank/back pain, headache, hypotension, and red/dark urine. Facial flushing is often an early sign. Option A is incorrect due to "dry skin" (usually sweating occurs). Option D is allergic.

9.
The blood pressure readings that indicate stage 1 hypertension are
a) Systolic 140 – 149, diastolic 90 – 99 Mmhg
b) Systolic 150 – 159, diastolic 100 – 110 Mmhg
c) Systolic 100 – 120, diastolic 60 – 80 Mmhg
d) Systolic 120 – 139, diastolic 80 – 89 Mmhg
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Systolic 140 – 149, diastolic 90 – 99 Mmhg

Explanation: Traditionally (JNC 7), Stage 1 Hypertension is 140-159 / 90-99 mmHg. Option A fits within this range. Option D is Pre-hypertension.

10.
A patient is admitted with suspected cirrhosis. During assessment, the nurse is most likely to detect
a) An increase in chest hair
b) Testicular hypertrophy
c) An increased clothing tendency
d) Muscle wasting
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Muscle wasting

Explanation: Signs of cirrhosis include muscle wasting (due to poor protein metabolism), testicular atrophy (not hypertrophy), loss of chest hair (due to increased estrogen), and bleeding tendency.

11.
A complication of folic acid deficiency may lead to
a) Acute chest syndrome
b) Neural tube defects
c) Congestive heart failure
d) Rheumatoid arthritis
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Neural tube defects

Explanation: Folic acid deficiency during pregnancy is a primary cause of neural tube defects (e.g., Spina Bifida) in the fetus.

12.
The priority assessment by a first aider while attending a casualty is
a) Next of kin
b) Age
c) Contacts of the casualty
d) Life threatening situations
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Life threatening situations

Explanation: The Primary Survey (DRSABCD) prioritizes identifying and managing life-threatening conditions (Airway, Breathing, Circulation) first.

13.
The sequence of performing abdominal examination is
a) Inspection, Palpation, Percussion, Auscultation
b) Auscultation, Inspection, Palpation, Percussion
c) Inspection, Auscultation, Palpation, Percussion
d) Palpation, Inspection, Percussion, Auscultation
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Inspection, Palpation, Percussion, Auscultation (Note: Option a is listed, but the correct medical sequence is typically Inspection -> Auscultation -> Percussion -> Palpation)

Explanation: For the abdomen, Auscultation should follow Inspection (before Palpation/Percussion) to avoid altering bowel sounds.
Analysis of Options:
- Option A: I -> P -> P -> A (Wrong order).
- Option B: A -> I... (Inspection is always first).
- Option C: Inspection, Auscultation, Palpation, Percussion. This is the correct sequence.
- Option D: P -> I...
Selection: Option C is the correct medical sequence.

14.
Nasal flaring is a common sign in
a) Bronchitis
b) Pneumonia
c) Pulmonary oedema
d) Coryza
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Pneumonia

Explanation: Nasal flaring is a sign of respiratory distress/dyspnea, commonly seen in severe infections like pneumonia, especially in children.

15.
Degeneration of the nerve in the Central Nervous System is referred to as
a) Epilepsy
b) Bell's palsy
c) Myasthenia grams
d) Multiple sclerosis
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Multiple sclerosis

Explanation: Multiple Sclerosis involves demyelination and degeneration of nerves within the CNS (Brain/Spinal Cord). Bell's palsy is peripheral (CN VII). Myasthenia is NMJ.

16.
The medical management of gout include
a) Prednisolone, Colchicine
b) Probenecid, Allopurinol
c) Calcitonin, Probenecid
d) Fusomax, Allopurinol
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Probenecid, Allopurinol

Explanation: These are the standard urate-lowering therapies. Allopurinol inhibits uric acid production; Probenecid increases excretion. (Colchicine is for acute attacks, but 'Medical Management' broadly favors the chronic preventive treatment in this pairing).

17.
A fracture that produces several bones fragments is referred to as
a) Comminuted
b) Greenstick
c) Avulsion
d) Impacted
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Comminuted

Explanation: A comminuted fracture is one where the bone is broken into more than two fragments (shattered).

18.
Causes of hydronephrosis include the following
a) Nephrotic syndrome
b) Twist at the uteropelvic junction
c) Glomeruli inflammation
d) Acute urinary tract infection
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Twist at the uteropelvic junction

Explanation: Hydronephrosis (swelling of the kidney) is caused by obstruction to urine flow. A twist (torsion) or stricture at the uteropelvic junction is a classic obstructive cause.

19.
The cranial nerve x innervates which part (s) of the body
a) Tongue
b) Trapezius muscle
c) Ear
d) Stomach
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Stomach

Explanation: The Vagus Nerve (CN X) provides parasympathetic innervation to the heart, lungs, and most of the digestive tract (including the stomach).

20.
Conservative management of osteoarthritis include
a) Adequate intake of calcium and vitamins
b) Limit daily intake of meat and fish in diet
c) Weight reduction, avoiding joint overuse
d) Weight bearing exercise for middle age
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Weight reduction, avoiding joint overuse

Explanation: Osteoarthritis is degenerative (wear and tear). Management focuses on reducing stress on the joints (weight loss, avoiding overuse/impact) while maintaining mobility.

21.
The major symptoms of pancreatitis that brings the patient to medical care is
a) Severe abdominal pain
b) Sepsis
c) Fever
d) Mental agitation
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Severe abdominal pain

Explanation: The hallmark symptom of acute pancreatitis is severe, constant epigastric pain that often radiates to the back.

22.
The percentage of the burns of the back and lower limbs in an adult is
a) 28
b) 72
c) 36
d) 50
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) 36

Explanation: Using the Rule of Nines:
Back (Posterior Trunk) = 18%.
Back of Left Leg = 9%.
Back of Right Leg = 9%.
Total "Back of body" = 18 + 9 + 9 = 36%.
(Note: If it meant "Back" + "Entire Legs" it would be 18 + 18 + 18 = 54%. 36% fits the "Back of Trunk + Back of Legs" interpretation or "Back + One Leg").

23.
A patient develops a gangrenous toe because the nurse forgot to untie the tourniquet after vene-puncture. The nurse would be charged with
a) Negligence
b) Gross misconduct
c) Misdemeanor
d) Gross violation
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Negligence

Explanation: Negligence is the failure to take reasonable care or exercise the level of care that a reasonably prudent nurse would use, resulting in harm to the patient.

24.
Medical management of thyroid crisis includes
a) Hyperthermia measures and oxygen therapy
b) Intravenous saline fluid and oxygen therapy
c) Intravenous dextrose fluids and hyperthermia
d) Intravenous dextrose fluids and oxygen therapy
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Hyperthermia measures and oxygen therapy

Explanation: Thyroid storm causes severe hypermetabolism leading to hyperpyrexia (high fever) and increased oxygen demand. Cooling measures (for hyperthermia) and Oxygen are critical first-line interventions.

25.
One of the following conditions causes hypoxia
a) Sinusitis
b) Lung disease
c) Angina pectoris
d) Deep venous thrombosis
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Lung disease

Explanation: Lung disease (e.g., COPD, Pneumonia) directly impairs gas exchange, leading to systemic hypoxia. Angina is local ischemia.

26.
An abnormal constituent of urine is
a) Urea
b) Glucose
c) Potassium
d) Creatinine
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Glucose

Explanation: Glucose is normally reabsorbed by the kidneys. Its presence in urine (glycosuria) is abnormal and usually indicates diabetes mellitus.

27.
A nurse is directed to administer an Isotonic Intravenous solution. Looking at the following labelled solutions, she/he should choose
a) 0.045% Sodium Chloride
b) 5% dextrose in water
c) 0 – 90% Sodium Chloride
d) 5% dextrose in normal saline solution
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) 5% dextrose in water (D5W) - *Or intended 0.9% NaCl*

Explanation:
- D5W is Isotonic in the bag (though physiologically hypotonic).
- 0.045% (Half-Normal) is Hypotonic.
- D5NS (Option D) is Hypertonic.
- Option C says "0-90% Sodium Chloride". This is likely a typo for 0.9% Sodium Chloride (Normal Saline), which is the standard Isotonic solution. If C meant 0.9%, C is the best answer. Without 0.9%, D5W is the only isotonic option listed.

28.
Complications that may arise due to mal-absorption syndrome include
a) Pernicious anaemia, Osteomalacia
b) Abdominal pain, Constipation
c) Osteomalacia, Abdominal distention
d) Constipation, Pernicious anaemia
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Pernicious anaemia, Osteomalacia

Explanation: Malabsorption leads to deficiency of nutrients. Vitamin B12 deficiency causes Pernicious Anemia. Vitamin D/Calcium deficiency causes Osteomalacia.

29.
Modifiable risk factor of arteriosclerosis and atherosclerosis include
a) Race
b) Obesity
c) Environment
d) Age
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Obesity

Explanation: Obesity is a lifestyle factor that can be changed (modified). Race and Age are non-modifiable.

30.
Erythropoiesis is
a) Destruction of red blood cells
b) Hemolysis of red blood cells
c) Reduction in cellular contents of blood
d) Production of red blood cells
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Production of red blood cells

31.
Complication of acute glomerulonephritis include
a) Enlarged prostate gland, hypertension, nephrotic syndrome
b) Cerebrovascular accident, pyelonephritis, cardiac failure
c) Nephrotic syndrome, congestive cardiac failure, renal failure
d) Hypertension, pyelonephritis, Nephrotic syndrome
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Nephrotic syndrome, congestive cardiac failure, renal failure

Explanation: Major complications include Hypertensive Encephalopathy, Heart Failure (due to fluid overload), and Acute Renal Failure. It can also progress to Nephrotic syndrome.

32.
What discharge instruction should the nurse teach a client with anaemia
a) Take the prescribed iron supplements and an iron rich diet
b) Monitor pulse and blood pressure weekly and a balanced diet
c) Have a complete blood count checked regularly and complete iron supplements
d) Perform isometric exercises three times a week and an iron rich diet
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Take the prescribed iron supplements and an iron rich diet

Explanation: Compliance with iron supplements and dietary modification (rich in iron) is the cornerstone of managing iron-deficiency anemia post-discharge.

33.
An effective health education to a patient with Hietal hernia should
a) Increase the amount of heavy lifting
b) Lie down immediately after meals high in fibre
c) Eat frequent small meals higher in fibre
d) Eat three large meals daily without restriction
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Eat frequent small meals higher in fibre

Explanation: Small, frequent meals reduce gastric distension. Patients should avoid heavy lifting and lying down after meals to prevent reflux.

34.
The condition that is most likely to have a nursing diagnosis of fluid volume deficit is
a) Pancreatitis
b) Appendicitis
c) Cholecystitis
d) Gastric ulcer
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Pancreatitis

Explanation: Acute pancreatitis causes severe third-spacing of fluid into the abdominal cavity and retroperitoneal space, leading to significant hypovolemia (fluid volume deficit) and shock.

35.
The stage in epilepsy where there is muscle relaxation and incontinence is referred to as
a) Tonic stage
b) Warning stage
c) Prodrome stage
d) Clonic stage
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Clonic stage

Explanation:
- Tonic: Stiffening.
- Clonic: Rhythmic jerking (contraction alternating with relaxation). Incontinence often occurs as muscles relax during/after this phase or in the post-ictal state.

36.
An indication of nasogastric tube insertion includes
a) A patient with left leg amputation
b) Congestive cardiac failure
c) A patient with platelet count of 50
d) A patient with stroke who failed their swallow evaluation
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) A patient with stroke who failed their swallow evaluation

Explanation: Patients who cannot swallow (dysphagia) due to stroke require enteral feeding (NG Tube) to prevent aspiration and malnutrition.

Which of the following is a conservative treatment of osteoarthritis?
a) Adequate intake of calcium and vitamins
b) Weight reduction, avoiding joint overuse
c) Weight bearing, exercises for middle age
d) Limit daily intake of meat and fish in the diet

38.
Which of the following is a diagnostic feature of pleuritis?
a) Dull chest pain
b) Stabbing chest pain
c) Diffuse chest pain
d) Epigastric pain
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Stabbing chest pain

Explanation: Pleuritis (Pleurisy) is characterized by sharp, stabbing pleuritic chest pain that worsens with inspiration or coughing.

39.
Excessive growth hormone production in adulthood results to
a) Gigantism
b) Acromegaly
c) Dwarfism
d) Macro-babies
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Acromegaly

Explanation: If GH excess occurs after the epiphyseal plates have closed (adulthood), it causes Acromegaly (thickening of bones). If it occurs before closure (childhood), it causes Gigantism.

40.
A drug that causes ototoxicity is
a) Brufen
b) Promethazine
c) Quinine
d) Omeprazole
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Quinine

Explanation: Quinine (and other Cinchona alkaloids) is well-known for causing Cinchonism, a syndrome including tinnitus, hearing loss (ototoxicity), and vertigo.

Section 2: Short Answer Questions (40 Marks)

41.
Outline at least FIVE approaches to management of peptic ulcers (5 marks)
Show Answer
  1. Antibiotics: Use of Triple Therapy (e.g., Clarithromycin + Amoxicillin) to eradicate H. Pylori.
  2. Acid Suppression: Proton Pump Inhibitors (e.g., Omeprazole) or H2 Blockers (e.g., Ranitidine) to reduce acid secretion.
  3. Cytoprotective Agents: Drugs like Sucralfate or Bismuth to coat and protect the ulcer bed.
  4. Dietary Modification: Avoiding irritants (spicy foods, caffeine, alcohol) and eating small frequent meals.
  5. Lifestyle Changes: Smoking cessation and stress reduction.
42.
State FIVE types of shock (5 marks)
Show Answer
  1. Hypovolemic Shock
  2. Cardiogenic Shock
  3. Septic Shock
  4. Anaphylactic Shock
  5. Neurogenic Shock
43.
Outline FIVE principles of reverse/isolation nursing (5 marks)
Show Answer
  1. Patient Placement: Place patient in a single room with positive pressure ventilation if possible.
  2. Hand Hygiene: Strict hand washing before entering the room and before patient contact.
  3. PPE: Wearing sterile gowns, masks, and gloves to protect the immunocompromised patient from staff.
  4. Limit Visitors/Staff: Restricting the number of people entering to minimize pathogen exposure.
  5. Dedicated Equipment: Using dedicated equipment (stethoscope, thermometer) for that patient only.
44.
State the FOUR types of burns according to the depth of injury (4 marks)
Show Answer
  1. Superficial (First Degree): Epidermis only (e.g., sunburn).
  2. Superficial Partial Thickness (Second Degree): Epidermis and upper dermis.
  3. Deep Partial Thickness (Second Degree): Epidermis and deep dermis.
  4. Full Thickness (Third Degree): Entire epidermis and dermis destroyed.
45.
Explain THREE principles of effective traction (6 marks)
Show Answer
  1. Maintain Counter-traction: The pull of traction must be balanced by an opposing force (usually the patient's body weight).
  2. Continuous Force: Traction must be continuous; weights should hang freely and not touch the floor.
  3. Line of Pull: The line of pull must be maintained in alignment with the long axis of the bone. Ropes must move freely through pulleys.
46.
State THREE nursing consideration for insulin use and storage (3 marks)
Show Answer
  1. Storage: Store unopened vials in the refrigerator (2-8°C). In-use vials can be kept at room temperature for up to 28 days.
  2. Site Rotation: Rotate injection sites (abdomen, thigh, arm) to prevent lipodystrophy.
  3. Preparation: Gently roll cloudy insulin (NPH) to mix; do not shake. Verify expiration dates.
47.
Outline SIX points on the care given to a patient with underwater seal drainage (6 marks)
Show Answer
  1. Positioning: Keep the drainage bottle below the level of the chest at all times to prevent backflow.
  2. Patency: Check for oscillation/tidaling in the tube during respiration to ensure it is patent.
  3. Monitoring: Observe quantity and character of drainage; mark levels on the bottle.
  4. Air Leak: Monitor for continuous bubbling in the water seal chamber (indicates air leak).
  5. Dressing: Ensure the insertion site dressing is clean, dry, and occlusive.
  6. Emergency Care: Keep clamps and sterile petrolatum gauze at the bedside in case of tube disconnection.
48.
Explain three principles of fracture management (6 marks)
Show Answer
  1. Reduction: Restoring the bone fragments to their normal anatomical alignment (Closed or Open reduction).
  2. Immobilization: Holding the bone fragments in position to allow healing (Casts, Splints, Traction, Fixation).
  3. Rehabilitation: Restoring function and strength to the affected part and preventing muscle atrophy/stiffness (Physiotherapy).

Section 3: Long Answer Question (20 Marks)

49.
Mr. X who is 48 years old is admitted in medical ward with a diagnosis of acute renal failure.

a) State four phase of acute renal failure (4 marks)

Show Answer
  1. Onset (Initiation) Phase: From the initial insult until cell injury occurs.
  2. Oliguric (Anuric) Phase: Decrease in urine output (<400ml/day), accumulation of wastes (urea/creatinine) and electrolytes (K+).
  3. Diuretic Phase: Kidney starts to recover; urine output increases significantly (polyuria) but unable to concentrate urine.
  4. Recovery Phase: GFR returns to normal, BUN/Creatinine stabilize.

b) Briefly explain six pre-renal causes of acute renal failure (6 marks)

Show Answer

Pre-renal causes involve reduced blood flow (perfusion) to the kidneys:

  1. Hypovolemia: Dehydration, Hemorrhage, or Burns reducing circulating volume.
  2. Decreased Cardiac Output: Heart failure, MI, or Dysrhythmias reducing pump effectiveness.
  3. Systemic Vasodilation: Sepsis or Anaphylaxis causing pooling of blood and hypotension.
  4. Renal Artery Stenosis: Blockage or narrowing reducing direct flow to the kidney.
  5. Medications: NSAIDs or ACE inhibitors altering intra-renal hemodynamics.
  6. Thrombosis: Clot in the renal artery.

c) Describe specific management of Mr. X until discharge (10 marks)

Show Answer
  1. Fluid Management: Strict Intake/Output monitoring. In oliguric phase, restrict fluids (Output + 500ml). In diuretic phase, replace fluids to prevent dehydration.
  2. Electrolyte Control: Treat Hyperkalemia (Kayexalate, Insulin/Glucose). Restrict dietary Potassium and Sodium. Monitor ECG.
  3. Dietary Management: Low protein (to reduce urea), high carbohydrate/fat (for energy), low potassium/phosphate diet.
  4. Medication: Avoid nephrotoxic drugs. Adjust dosages. Diuretics (Furosemide) may be used to challenge kidneys.
  5. Monitoring: Daily weights (fluid status), Monitor BP, Monitor BUN/Creatinine/Electrolytes daily. Assess for edema/crackles.
  6. Skin Care: Prevent breakdown due to edema and uremic frost (itching).
  7. Dialysis: Prepare for dialysis if conservative management fails (Severe acidosis, Hyperkalemia, Fluid overload).
  8. Education: Teach regarding diet, medication adherence, and signs of relapse before discharge.
