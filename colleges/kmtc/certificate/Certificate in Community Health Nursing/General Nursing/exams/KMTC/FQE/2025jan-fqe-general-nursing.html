1.
The communication process can be influenced by various factors that affect how the message is received and interpreted. The factors that affect communication process to the receiver include:
A. Knowledge, experience, defense mechanism
B. Motivation, mood, language
C. Physical condition, attention, intensity
D. Movement, repetition, mood
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A. Knowledge, experience, defense mechanism

Explanation: Factors affecting the receiver's decoding of a message include their prior knowledge, past experiences, and psychological defense mechanisms (which might block or distort the message).

2.
When taking blood pressure, the point at which the first sound is heard or felt is referred to as the:
A. Diastolic pressure
B. Systemic pressure
C. Systolic pressure
D. Diastole and systole pressure
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C. Systolic pressure

Explanation: The first Korotkoff sound indicates the systolic blood pressure (the maximum pressure exerted when the heart contracts). The point where the sound disappears is the diastolic pressure.

3.
Pressure sores also known as bedsores or decubitus ulcers can be prevented by the following measures:
A. Relieving pressure, improving circulation, keeping the skin dry
B. Improving circulation, good nutrition, dry skin
C. Relieving pressure, ambulation, application of Vaseline
D. Application of powder, good nutrition, relieving pressure
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A. Relieving pressure, improving circulation, keeping the skin dry

Explanation: The most effective prevention measures involve frequent turning (relieving pressure), maintaining tissue perfusion (circulation), and managing moisture (keeping skin dry/clean).

4.
Liver biopsy is contraindicated in patients with one of the following conditions:
A. Liver disease
B. Anaemia
C. Bleeding disorder
D. Blood dyscrasias
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C. Bleeding disorder

Explanation: Because the liver is highly vascular, a biopsy carries a significant risk of hemorrhage. It is contraindicated in patients with uncorrected bleeding disorders (coagulopathies). "Blood dyscrasias" is a broader term, but "Bleeding disorder" is the specific contraindication.

6.

15.

16.
The correct procedure is of eye swabbing is:
A. From the unaffected eye from the inner canthus to the outer canthus
B. Affected eye from outer canthus towards the inner canthus.
C. From eyelids to the inner canthus to the outer canthus
D. From the unaffected eye from the outer canthus towards the inner canthus.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A. From the unaffected eye from the inner canthus to the outer canthus

Explanation: The standard principle is to clean from the inner canthus (less contaminated) to the outer canthus (more contaminated) to prevent sweeping debris into the lacrimal duct. If both are to be cleaned, start with the cleaner (unaffected) eye.

18.
Epistaxis can occur due to a variety of underlying factors, and recognizing risk factors can help in both prevention and management. These factors include:

19.
Conductive and sensorineural hearing loss can be detected through different diagnostic methods like:
A. Whisper test
B. Weber test
C. Tuning fork
D. Rinne test
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B. Weber test

Explanation: Both Weber and Rinne tests (using a tuning fork) distinguish between conductive and sensorineural hearing loss. The Weber test lateralizes sound to the affected ear in conductive loss and the unaffected ear in sensorineural loss.

  1. Hormonal changes: Fluctuations in estrogen and progesterone during the cycle.
  2. Chemical changes in the brain: Fluctuations in serotonin levels (affecting mood).

43.
State four (4) health messages you would share with patients who had sustained eye injuries at home upon discharge. (4 marks)
Show Answer
  1. Do not rub: Advise not to rub the eye to prevent further damage.
  2. Protection: Wear protective eyewear or a patch if prescribed to shield from light and dust.
  3. Medication adherence: Apply eye drops/ointments exactly as prescribed.
  4. Return warning: Seek immediate help if pain increases, vision decreases, or discharge occurs.
44.
Explain three (3) nursing interventions in the management of left sided heart failure (6 marks)
Show Answer
  1. Positioning: Place patient in High Fowler's position to facilitate lung expansion and reduce venous return (preload).
  2. Oxygen Therapy: Administer supplemental oxygen to correct hypoxemia caused by pulmonary congestion.
  3. Fluid/Medication Management: Restrict fluid intake and administer prescribed diuretics (e.g., Lasix) to reduce fluid overload and pulmonary edema.
45.
Outline four (4) physiological system changes in the aging population (4 marks)
Show Answer
  1. Integumentary: Loss of skin elasticity, thinning of skin, graying hair.
  2. Cardiovascular: Reduced cardiac output, stiffening of arteries (arteriosclerosis).
  3. Musculoskeletal: Loss of bone density (osteoporosis), decreased muscle mass.
  4. Sensory: Decreased visual acuity (presbyopia) and hearing loss (presbycusis).
46.
Outline the five (5) stages of the nursing process and indicate the purpose of each (5 marks)
Show Answer
  1. Assessment: To gather and analyze data about the patient's health status.
  2. Diagnosis: To identify the patient's problems or needs based on data.
  3. Planning: To set goals and select interventions to address the diagnoses.
  4. Implementation: To put the care plan into action (perform interventions).
  5. Evaluation: To determine if goals were met and effectiveness of care.
47.
Explain three (3) nursing interventions applicable in the care of a patient with a stroke (6 marks)
Show Answer
  1. Airway & Breathing: Maintain patent airway and prevent aspiration (keep NPO if swallowing impaired, elevate head).
  2. Mobility/Skin Care: Turn patient every 2 hours and perform passive Range of Motion (ROM) exercises to prevent pressure ulcers and contractures.
  3. Communication: Use communication boards or simple questions if the patient has aphasia to reduce frustration.
48.
Outline five (5) danger signs to look out for in a patient with a hip fracture at the casualty department (5 marks)
Show Answer
  1. External Rotation: The affected leg appearing turned outward.
  2. Shortening: The affected leg appearing shorter than the other.
  3. Severe Pain: Intense pain in the hip or groin, worsened by movement.
  4. Inability to Bear Weight: Inability to move the leg or stand.
  5. Signs of Shock: Hypotension or tachycardia indicating internal bleeding (hemorrhage).

Section 3: Long Answer Question (20 Marks)

49.
A patient has just been brought in from the casualty department to the ward with a flail chest. Describe the main clinical features the patient may present with and describe the specific nursing and medical management the patient will receive within the next 48 hours.
Note: The source text for Q49 is slightly cut off or references Mr. K with bleeding in a different context in other papers, but the visible text here is "flail chest". Wait, checking the previous paper Q48 was Mr. K. This paper's Q49 is Flail Chest.
Actually, checking page 8 of Chunk 2: "49. A patient has just been brought in... with a flail chest...". I will answer based on Flail Chest.
Show Answer

1. Clinical Features (Clinical Presentation):

  • Paradoxical Respiration: The chest wall moves in during inspiration and out during expiration (opposite of normal).
  • Severe Chest Pain: Sharp pain, exacerbated by breathing.
  • Dyspnea: Difficulty breathing and tachypnea (rapid breathing).
  • Hypoxia/Cyanosis: Due to poor ventilation.
  • Tachycardia and Hypotension: Signs of shock or stress.

2. Medical Management:

  • Pain Control: Aggressive analgesia (e.g., Intercostal nerve blocks, epidural, or IV opioids) to allow deep breathing.
  • Oxygen Therapy: Administer humidified oxygen to maintain saturation.
  • Mechanical Ventilation: In severe cases (respiratory failure), intubation and positive pressure ventilation (PEEP) to internally stabilize the chest wall.
  • Fluid Management: Judicious fluid administration to prevent fluid overload (pulmonary contusion worsening).

3. Nursing Management:

  • Respiratory Monitoring: Monitor ABGs, SpO2, and respiratory rate frequently. Assess lung sounds.
  • Positioning: Semi-Fowler's position to maximize chest expansion.
  • Pulmonary Hygiene: Encourage coughing and deep breathing (with splinting of the chest) to prevent pneumonia/atelectasis. Suction if intubated.
  • Monitoring Vitals: Watch for signs of shock or pneumothorax/hemothorax.
