Section 1: Multiple Choice Questions (40 Marks)

1.
The examination of a patient with a psychiatric disorder should include:
a) Psychological tests
b) Laboratory studies
c) A mental status examination
d) Screening test for stress levels
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) A mental status examination

Explanation: The Mental Status Examination (MSE) is the core diagnostic tool in psychiatry, analogous to the physical examination in general medicine. It assesses current cognitive, emotional, and behavioral functioning.

2.
Positive symptoms of Schizophrenia include:
a) Hallucinations, delusions and disorganize thinking
b) Waxy flexibility, alogia and apathy
c) Somatic delusions, echolalia and a flat affect
d) Flat affect, a volition and anheclonia
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Hallucinations, delusions and disorganize thinking

Explanation: Positive symptoms are "added" experiences that are not normally present, such as hallucinations and delusions. Negative symptoms (Options B, D) involve a loss of function (alogia, apathy, flat affect).

3.
Substance dependence
a) Includes characteristics of adverse consequences and repeated use
b) Requires long term treatment in a hospital-based program
c) Produces less severe symptoms than that of abuse
d) Includes characteristics of tolerance and withdrawal
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Includes characteristics of tolerance and withdrawal

Explanation: Physical dependence is physiologically characterized by tolerance (needing more for same effect) and withdrawal symptoms upon cessation. Option A describes substance abuse (harmful use).

4.
A person who is always striving to be the center of attention may be diagnosed with personality disorder called
a) Border line
b) Histrionic
c) Antisocial
d) Impulsive
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Histrionic

Explanation: Histrionic Personality Disorder is characterized by a pattern of excessive emotionality and attention-seeking behavior. They are uncomfortable when not the center of attention.

5.
The age of onset of attention deficit disorder is between
a) 4-7 years
b) 10 – 18 years
c) 2 -4 years
d) Above 8 years
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) 4-7 years

Explanation: ADHD symptoms typically begin in early childhood. Criteria often state symptoms were present prior to age 12, but diagnosis is common in the early school years (4-7) when attention demands increase.

6.
Regarding pressure sore ulcers
a) Stage one is characterized by erythema with skin breakage on pressure
b) Stage three is also classified as partial thickness wound
c) Stage four the ulcer extends into the subcutaneous tissue
d) Stage two looks like a blister filled with clear fluid
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Stage two looks like a blister filled with clear fluid

Explanation: Stage 2 pressure ulcers present as partial-thickness loss of dermis presenting as a shallow open ulcer or an intact or open/ruptured serum-filled blister. Stage 1 is non-blanchable erythema without breakage. Stage 4 extends beyond subcutaneous to muscle/bone.

7.
One of the following is a principle task in the latent stage of growth and development according to FREUD
a) Toilet training
b) Weaning
c) Learning
d) Sexual identity
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Learning

Explanation: The Latency Stage (6 years to puberty) is when sexual energy is repressed and channeled into intellectual and social pursuits (school, sports, friendships) - i.e., learning and social skills.

8.
Planning process can be sequenced as follows:
a) Setting goals, implementation, assessment, evaluation
b) Assessment, setting goals, implementation, evaluation
c) Assessment, implementation, setting goals, evaluation
d) Setting goals, assessment, implementation, evaluation
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Assessment, setting goals, implementation, evaluation

Explanation: The nursing process sequence is Assessment -> Diagnosis -> Planning (Setting Goals) -> Implementation -> Evaluation.

9.
The correct order of steps in disciplinary process is
a) Preliminary investigation, discussion with the offender, strong verbal warning, written warning, suspension
b) Strong verbal warning, discussion with the offender, preliminary investigation, written warning, suspension
c) Discussion with the offender, strong verbal warning, preliminary investigation, written warning, suspension
d) Preliminary investigation, strong verbal warning, written warning, discussion with the offender, suspension
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Preliminary investigation, discussion with the offender, strong verbal warning, written warning, suspension

Explanation: Due process requires establishing facts (investigation) and hearing the employee's side (discussion) before administering progressive discipline (warning -> written -> suspension).

10.
Indicate whether the following are True or False:
a) A diagnosis and confirmation of only one case of measles at a health facility is and epidemic
b) Incidence is the total number of new and old cases of a disease in a community
Show Answer

Answers:

  • a) TRUE: For diseases targeted for elimination like measles or polio, a single confirmed case constitutes an outbreak (epidemic).
  • b) FALSE: Incidence refers only to new cases. Prevalence refers to total (new + old) cases.
11.
The role of reinforcement during teaching is:
a) Promotion of student's participation
b) Evaluation of student's prior knowledge

Correct Answer: a) Promotion of student's participation

12.
A disadvantage of self-directed learning would include:
a) Learning at individual pace
b) Lack of maintenance of programmed materials
c) Covers limited amount of subject matter

Correct Answer: d) There is no contact between learner and teacher

14.
The following vaccines should be discarded after a vaccination session:
a) Measles, BCG, Yellow Fever
b) Measles, BCG, Polio
c) Polio, Pentavalent, Tetanus
d) Tetanus, BCG, Yellow Fever
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Measles, BCG, Yellow Fever

Explanation: These are reconstituted vaccines without preservatives. Once reconstituted, they must be used within 6 hours or at the end of the session, whichever comes first, due to risk of contamination and loss of potency.

15.
The vaccine that is heat stable, not destroyed rapidly at room temperature and remains active for at least 6 weeks at 37 degrees is
a) Pentavalent
b) BCG
c) Tetanus Toxoid
d) Measles
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Tetanus Toxoid

Explanation: Tetanus Toxoid is one of the most heat-stable vaccines. However, it is sensitive to freezing. Measles, BCG, and OPV are heat-sensitive.

16.
The following principle of growth and development is correctly defined:
a) Cephalocaudal which states that development proceeds from the center of the body outward
b) Proximodistal which states that development proceeds from top to bottom
c) Orthogenic which states that development progresses from lack of differentiation to increasing differentiation
d) Hierarchial which states that development proceeds from the outward to the centre and simple skills develop together
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Orthogenic which states that development progresses from lack of differentiation to increasing differentiation

Explanation:
a) Incorrect: Cephalocaudal is Head to Tail (Top to Bottom).
b) Incorrect: Proximodistal is Center to Outward.
c) Correct: The orthogenetic principle states development moves from simple/undifferentiated to complex/differentiated.

17.
Wilms tumor is the most common malignant tumor of the kidneys and it's characterized by the following manifestations
a) A firm mass within the abdomen, haematuria, hypotension, abdominal pains
b) Hypertension, abdominal pains, headache, fever
c) A firm mass within the abdomen, haematuria, abdominal pains, fever
d) A firm mass within the abdomen, anorexia and vomiting, hiccups, abdominal pains
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) A firm mass within the abdomen, haematuria, abdominal pains, fever

Explanation: Classic signs of Wilms tumor (Nephroblastoma) include a firm, non-tender abdominal mass (often unilateral), hematuria, abdominal pain, fever, and often *hypertension* (not hypotension as in A).

18.
In partent ductus arteriosus, there is an abnormal opening between:
a) Aorta and pulmonary artery
b) Right and left atria
c) Pulmonary vein and pulmonary artery
d) Aorta and pulmonary vein
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Aorta and pulmonary artery

Explanation: Patent Ductus Arteriosus (PDA) is a persistent opening between the two major blood vessels leading from the heart: the aorta and the pulmonary artery.

19.
Organophosphate poisoning should be suspected in a child who presents with:
a) Tremors, sweating, excessive salivation, diarrhea
b) Excessive diarrhea, salivation, sweating, dyspnea
c) Pinpoint pupils, dyspnea, sweating, muscle tremors
d) Sweating, tremors, excessive salivation, pin point pupils
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Sweating, tremors, excessive salivation, pin point pupils

Explanation: Organophosphates cause cholinergic crisis (SLUDGE syndrome: Salivation, Lacrimation, Urination, Diarrhea, GI distress, Emesis). Key signs include pinpoint pupils (miosis), excessive salivation/sweating, and tremors/fasciculations.

20.
The main emphasis of community health nursing is
a) Treatment of health problems
b) Identification of health problems
c) Identification and assessment of health problems
d) Prevention of health problems and promoting optimum health
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Prevention of health problems and promoting optimum health

Explanation: The primary goal of CHN is health promotion and disease prevention (primary prevention) to improve the overall health status of the population, rather than just treating existing illness (curative).

21.
In epidemiology, the types of analytical studies include
a) Observational and experimental studies
b) Cross-sectional and case studies
c) Cross-sectional and observational studies
d) Experimental and descriptive studies
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Observational and experimental studies

Explanation: Analytical epidemiology tests hypotheses. It includes Observational studies (Cohort, Case-control) and Experimental studies (RCTs). Descriptive studies generate hypotheses but are not analytical.

22.
Which of the following childhood illnesses are targeted for eradication?

b) Acute flaccid paralysis, Measles, Neonatal Tetanus
c) Measles, Acute flaccid paralysis, Pneumonia
d) Measles, Neonatal Tetanus, Poliomyelitis

23.
The diagnostic stage of malaria is
a) Erythrocylic
b) Sporozoitic
c) Hypnozoitic
d) Oocyst
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Erythrocylic

Explanation: Diagnosis of malaria via blood smear detects the parasite during the Erythrocytic (Red Blood Cell) cycle (trophozoites, schizonts, gametocytes). Sporozoites are infective; Hypnozoites are dormant in liver; Oocysts are in the mosquito.

28.
Excretion of drugs is altered in older adults due to the following renal changes
a) Decreased renal tubular function and blood flow
b) Decreased water content in the body

32.
The direct health effects of ozone depletion include:
a) Increased risk of skin cancer and breast cancer
b) Increased risk of skin cancer and cataracts
c) Increased exposure to vector bone diseases and skin cancer
d) Increased risk of cataracts and exposure to vector borne diseases
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Increased risk of skin cancer and cataracts

Explanation: Ozone depletion allows more UV-B radiation to reach Earth, causing increased rates of skin cancer (melanoma and non-melanoma) and cataracts (eye damage). Vector-borne diseases are linked to climate change (warming), not direct UV exposure.

33.
In the treatment of tuberculosis, the "DOTS" approach refers to
a) The patient getting TB treatment as close to home as possible
b) Patient adheres to treatment during the intensive phase only
c) Directly observed treatment with weekly monitoring
d) Patient takes every drug dosage under supervision during the intensive phase
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Patient takes every drug dosage under supervision during the intensive phase

Explanation: DOTS (Directly Observed Treatment, Short-course) specifically mandates that a health worker or trained volunteer watches the patient swallow the medication, particularly during the intensive phase, to ensure adherence.

34.
The following is an adverse effect of Nevirapine
a) Bone marrow suppression
b) Hepatotoxicity
c) Peripheral neuropathy
d) Gastro-intestinal intolerance
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Hepatotoxicity

Explanation: Major side effects of Nevirapine (NNRTI) are severe skin rash (Stevens-Johnson syndrome) and hepatotoxicity (liver damage). Bone marrow suppression (A) is Zidovudine; Peripheral neuropathy (C) is Stavudine; GI intolerance (D) is common with Protease Inhibitors or Zidovudine.

Explanation: Accessibility encompasses physical (geographic), economic (financial), and sometimes cultural reach. Though "Affordable" matches financial, "Accessible" is the broader term covering "within reach" in all aspects.

41.
Outline SIX signs that may indicate a patient's suicidal intent in suicide risk assessment (6 marks)
Show Answer

Model Answer:

  1. Verbal threats: Making statements like "I want to end it all" or "I won't be a burden much longer".
  2. Giving away possessions: Distributing prized belongings to friends or family.
  3. Sudden calmness: A sudden improvement in mood after a period of depression (deciding to act brings relief).
  4. Social withdrawal: Isolating oneself from family and friends.
  5. Preparatory behaviors: Stockpiling pills, buying a weapon, or writing a will/suicide note.
  6. Hopelessness: Expressing feelings that the situation will never improve.
42.
Outline the differences between dementia and delirium (3 marks)
Show Answer

Model Answer:

  • Onset: Delirium has a sudden/acute onset (hours to days), while Dementia has a slow/insidious onset (months to years).
  • Course: Delirium is fluctuating (waxes and wanes) and often reversible. Dementia is progressive and irreversible.
  • Consciousness/Attention: In Delirium, consciousness is clouded and attention is impaired. In early Dementia, consciousness and attention are usually intact.
43.
Briefly explain FIVE risk factors to development of bedsores in a bed ridden patient (5 marks)
Show Answer

Model Answer:

  1. Immobility/Pressure: Constant pressure on bony prominences restricts blood flow, causing tissue necrosis.
  2. Moisture/Incontinence: Urine/feces macerate the skin, making it susceptible to breakdown.
  3. Poor Nutrition: Malnutrition (low protein/vitamin C) impairs tissue integrity and healing.
  4. Friction and Shear: Sliding down in bed stretches blood vessels and tears skin layers.
  5. Sensory Impairment: Inability to feel pain/discomfort prevents the patient from shifting position.

State THREE factors affecting choice of a refrigerator to use for storage of vaccines (3 marks)
Show Answer

  1. Power Source Availability: Is there reliable electricity (mains), or is solar/gas (absorption) needed?
  2. Storage Capacity Needs: Volume of vaccines to be stored based on catchment population size.
  3. Reliability/Hold-over Time: Ability to maintain temperature during power outages.
  4. Cost: Budget for purchase and maintenance.
45.
Explain the FOUR stages of growth and development in children (8 marks)
Show Answer

(Based on Piaget's Cognitive Development or General Stages)
  1. Infancy (0-1 year) / Sensorimotor Stage: Rapid physical growth. Learns through senses and motor actions. Develops object permanence.

48.
Using a well labelled diagram, briefly describe the life cycle of Ascaris (5 marks)
Show Answer

Model Answer:

(Diagram Description)

  1. Ingestion: Humans ingest infective eggs from contaminated soil/food.
  2. Hatching: Larvae hatch in the small intestine.
  3. Migration (Lungs): Larvae penetrate intestinal wall -> blood -> lungs (alveoli).
  4. Swallowing: Larvae ascend trachea, are coughed up and swallowed.
  5. Maturation: Larvae return to small intestine, mature into adult worms, and lay eggs which are passed in feces.

Section 3: Long Answer Question (20 Marks)

49.
One role of community health nurse is to carry out community diagnosis in order to help the community meet their health needs.

a) Define community diagnosis (1 mark)

Show Answer

Community diagnosis is the process of identifying and quantifying the health problems of a community, as well as identifying the factors influencing these problems (resources, environment), for the purpose of planning interventions.

b) State five (5) preparations done before executing the process (5 marks)

Show Answer
  1. Literature Review: Reviewing existing records/data about the community.
  2. Team Building: Selecting and training the survey team.
  3. Tool Development: preparing questionnaires and checklists.
  4. Resource Mobilization: Securing transport, stationery, and funds.
  5. Community Entry: Meeting leaders to explain the purpose and gain permission.
  6. Pre-testing: Testing the tools in a pilot area.

c) Describe steps followed in community diagnosis process (14 marks)

Show Answer
  1. Preparatory Phase: Planning, assembling team, budgeting, and tool design.
  2. Community Entry: Meeting village elders/admin to build rapport and explain mission.
  3. Social Survey/Mapping: Drawing a map of the area and identifying boundaries and resources.
  4. Pre-testing: Testing the questionnaire to ensure clarity.
  5. Data Collection: Administering questionnaires, FGDs, and observation.
  6. Data Analysis: Compiling data (tallying), calculating rates/percentages, and interpreting findings.
  7. Report Writing: documenting the findings, challenges, and recommendations.
  8. Dissemination: Sharing results with the community (Baraza) and stakeholders.
  9. Community Action Plan: developing a work plan with the community to address identified problems.
  10. Implementation & Evaluation: Carrying out the plan and assessing impact.
