ðŸ“š Inclusivity

DQAs should be as inclusive as possible. Teams should be composed of members from relevant health stakeholders. At the national level, team members should include representatives from the Ministry of Health (MOH), the County Health Management Team, implementing partners, and facility staff. Sub-national level DQA teams should include the sub-county health management team, implementing partners, and facility staff.

ðŸ“š Standardization

DQAs should be standardized using nationally recommended DQA tools and procedures. Data should be collected and transmitted electronically to the national DQA data repository for archiving. Data verification, should always be conducted. That is a comparison of what is in the source documents against various data reporting systems. Any indicator reported to DATIM should be compared with what was reported to KHIS. Data should be triangulated with other data sources such as commodity records and tracking logs.

ðŸ“š Country-Led Data Ownership

The data collected during the health program DQA is owned by the government of Kenya and the other stakeholders participating in the DQA will be granted access to the data by the government of Kenya. As such, priority of authorship of any manuscripts coming from this data will be given to the MOH.

ðŸ“š Frequency of DQAs

The frequency of conducting DQAs at the national and sub-national levels is indicated as follows:

  • National DQAs should be done annually.
  • County DQAs should be done semi-annually.
  • Sub-County DQAs should be done quarterly.

ðŸ“š Data Security

Data should be collected with appropriate security measures. This includes that:

  • No personal identifiers shall be transmitted during the collection of DQA data.
  • All electronic gadgets used for the collection and transmission of data shall be password protected.
  • DQA data shall be encrypted during transmission.
  • Access to DQA data shall be restricted to relevant stakeholders with clearly articulated justification for granting access.
  • All DQA data shall have a secure backup.

ðŸ“š Dissemination

DQA findings should be disseminated to ensure that the necessary data quality improvement is addressed by the relevant parties. Preliminary DQA findings should be shared with the facility staff on site during the exercise. A wider dissemination to stakeholders should also be done, through a report that is disseminated on the NASCOP website for HIV related DQA, a Technical Working Group (TWG) dissemination meeting, among other forums.

ðŸ’¡ Pro Tip

By adhering to these principles, you will ensure that your data quality audits are thorough, inclusive, and ethical. This will lead to valuable insights and improved data for healthcare decision making.

ðŸ“š Key Takeaways

  • DQAs should be inclusive of all relevant stakeholders.
  • Standardization of DQA methods ensures consistency and comparability.
  • Data collected during a DQA is owned by the government of Kenya.
  • DQAs should be performed with specified frequency.
  • Data security during collection and transmission is critical, and DQA findings should be disseminated to relevant stakeholders.
