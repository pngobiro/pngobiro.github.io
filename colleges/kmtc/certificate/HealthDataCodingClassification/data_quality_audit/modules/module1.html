ðŸ“š Importance of Data Quality

In the health sector, data is used for program planning, financing, monitoring and evaluation. It is, therefore, very important that data is of good quality. Good quality data ensures that planning, results reporting, and target-setting are informed by accurate and reliable information. It allows for reporting of health information in a consistent and organized manner. Ultimately, attention to data quality leads to improved program performance and more efficient resource management.

The Kenyan health sector is committed to building a comprehensive performance and information management system. The sector uses an integrated Health Information System (HIS) to harmonize planning, financing, monitoring and evaluation. The HIS uses the District Health Information Software (DHIS2) to collect, capture, and report health facility data.

ðŸ“š Definition of a Data Quality Audit (DQA)

A Data Quality Audit (DQA) is a systematic assessment and improvement process that focuses on evaluating the quality of data. It involves a detailed review of data to identify errors, inconsistencies, and any other anomalies that may compromise its reliability and validity. The goal of a DQA is to ensure that data is fit for its intended use, whether for program planning, monitoring, evaluation, or research. A DQA does not just look for incorrect figures, but also for a lack of completeness or old data that has become meaningless.

ðŸ“š Measuring Data Quality

When measuring data quality, consider the following questions:

  • Is the data complete?
  • Is it collected on time?
  • Is it correct?

Poor data quality can take many forms, not just incorrect figures, but also lack of completeness or data being too old for meaningful use.

ðŸ“š What to look for during a DQA

During a data quality audit, you should specifically look for:

  • Missing data values
  • Obvious fluctuations
  • Inconsistencies between data elements
  • Mathematical errors

ðŸ“š Reasons for Poor Data Quality

There are many reasons why data may have poor quality:

  • Excessive amounts of data collected which leads to less time to collect it and shortcuts in reporting.
  • Too many manual steps in recording data, such as moving figures between paper forms.
  • Unclear definitions or misinterpretation of the fields being filled out.
  • Lack of use of information, meaning there is no incentive to improve quality.
  • Fragmentation of information systems, which can lead to duplication of reporting.

ðŸ’¡ Pro Tip

Regular DQAs help identify areas for improvement and ensure that the data used in healthcare systems is reliable, accurate, and useful.

ðŸ“š Key Takeaways

  • A Data Quality Audit (DQA) is a process for assessing and improving the quality of data.
  • Data quality is important for program planning, monitoring and evaluation in the health sector.
  • Measuring data quality includes looking for completeness, timeliness, and correctness.
  • Poor data quality can result from various issues including excessive data collected, unclear definitions, and fragmented information systems.
