ðŸ“š The 22 Major Chapters of Disease and Injury

The ICD is organized into 22 major chapters, each representing a broad class of diseases or conditions. These chapters provide a high-level framework for categorizing health-related data. The chapters are as follows:

  1. Certain infectious and parasitic diseases
  2. Neoplasms
  3. Diseases of the blood and blood-forming organs and certain disorders involving the immune mechanism
  4. Endocrine, nutritional and metabolic diseases
  5. Mental and behavioral disorders
  6. Diseases of the nervous system
  7. Diseases of the eye and adnexa
  8. Diseases of the ear and mastoid process
  9. Diseases of the circulatory system
  10. Diseases of the respiratory system
  11. Diseases of the digestive system
  12. Diseases of the skin and the subcutaneous tissue
  13. Diseases of the musculoskeletal system and connective tissue
  14. Diseases of the genitourinary system
  15. Pregnancy, childbirth and the puerperium
  16. Certain conditions originating in the perinatal period
  17. Congenital malformations, deformations and chromosomal abnormalities
  18. Symptoms, signs and abnormal clinical and laboratory findings, not elsewhere classified
  19. Injury, poisoning and certain other consequences of external causes
  20. External causes of morbidity
  21. Factors influencing health status and contact with health services

ðŸ“š ICD Organization

As covered previously, the ICD has three volumes:

  • Volume I: The Tabular List which contains the main classification. This volume begins with an introduction of the practical developments of the ICD. The code numbers are arranged according to the alphabetical prefix.
  • Volume II: The Instructional Manual contains the guidelines and explanations for users. This has six sections: Instructions, descriptions of the ICD and related health problems, how to use the ICD, rules and guidelines for mortality and morbidity coding, statistical presentation, and the historical development of the ICD.
  • Volume III: The Alphabetical Index contains a list of diseases, injuries, external causes, drugs, and chemicals written in alphabetical order.

ðŸ“š ICPM Layout

The ICPM also has a specific layout:

The ICPM has nine categories:

  1. Procedures for medical diagnosis
  2. Laboratory procedures
  3. Radiology and certain other applications of physical medicine
  4. Preventive procedures
  5. Surgical procedures
  6. Drugs, medications and biological agents
  7. Other therapeutic procedures
  8. Ancillary procedures

The ICPM has two volumes:

  • Volume I: Contains the tabular list which includes the procedures for medical diagnosis, laboratory procedures, preventative procedures, and surgical procedures.
  • Volume II: The alphabetical index which includes categories on radiology and certain other applications of physical medicine, drugs, medications, and biological agents.

ðŸ“š Numbering Systems of ICPM

The numbering system for the ICPM is significant:

  • The first digit represents the number of the chapter which is numbered from 1-999.
  • The classification is significant at the 3-digit level.
  • The 4th digit provides greater details and precision.
  • Subdivisional are provided for some categories.

Some of the chapters of the ICPM can be used separately by different departments.
