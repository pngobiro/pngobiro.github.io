ðŸ“š Medical Word Elements of the Genitourinary System

The genitourinary system includes the urinary system (kidneys, ureters, bladder, and urethra) and the male reproductive system (testes, epididymis, vas deferens, seminal vesicles, prostate gland, and penis). These systems work closely together to eliminate waste and maintain homeostasis, as well as enable reproduction. Here are some of the key word elements related to these systems:

  • Bladder: cyst/o or vesic/o
  • Glomerulus: glomerul/o
  • Stone, calculus: lith/o
  • Opening, meatus: meat/o
  • Kidney: nephr/o or ren/o
  • Renal Pelvis: pyel/o
  • Urine, urinary tract: ur/o
  • Ureter: ureter/o
  • Urethra: urethr/o
  • Male: andr/o
  • Glans penis: balan/o
  • Epididymis: epididym/o
  • Testis (plural, testes): orch/o
  • Perineum: perine/o
  • Prostate gland: prostat/o
  • Spermatozoa, sperm cells: spermat/o or sperm/o
  • Dilated vein: varic/o
  • Vas deferens, duct: vas/o
  • Seminal vesicle: vesicul/o

ðŸ“š Pathological Terms

Here are some of the common pathological terms related to the genitourinary system:

  • Pyelonephritis: A kidney infection or complicated urinary tract infection.
  • Glomerulonephritis: A condition that causes the glomerular walls to become inflamed.
  • Nephrolithiasis: A condition where stones form in any part of the urinary tract.
  • Benign prostatic hyperplasia (BPH): A condition associated with aging where the prostate decreases the urethral lumen.
  • Cryptorchidism: Failure of the testes to descend into the scrotal sac.
  • Acute Tubular Necrosis (ATN): Injury of the tubular portion of the nephron, usually due to decreased blood supply or toxins.
  • Carcinoma of the Prostate: A form of cancer that commonly occurs in men over age 50

ðŸ“š Diagnostic, Symptomatic, and Related Terms

Here are some of the most common symptomatic and diagnostic terms relating to the genitourinary system:

  • Anuria: Absence of urine production or urinary output.
  • Azotemia: Retention of nitrogenous compounds in the blood.
  • Bladder Neck Obstruction (BNO): Blockage at the base of the bladder that reduces or prevents urine flow.
  • Chronic Renal Failure: Renal failure that occurs over a period of years.
  • Dysuria: Painful or difficult urination.
  • Enuresis: Involuntary discharge of urine.
  • Fistula: An abnormal passage from a hollow organ to the surface or from one organ to another.

ðŸ“š Diagnostic and Therapeutic Procedures

Here are some of the common diagnostic and therapeutic procedures related to the genitourinary system:

  • Cystoscopy: Endoscopy of the urinary bladder to observe pathology, obtain biopsies, and remove polyps.
  • Nephroscopy: Endoscopy of the kidney.
  • Urethroscopy: Endoscopy of the urethra.
  • Blood urea nitrogen (BUN): A test to measure the level of nitrogen in the blood.
  • Culture and Sensitivity (C&S): A test to determine the causative agent of a disease and which antibiotics are effective.
  • Prostate-specific antigen (PSA): A blood test used to detect prostatic disorders, especially prostatic cancer.
  • Semen analysis: An analysis of a semen sample to determine volume, sperm count and motility.
  • Urinalysis (UA): Tests performed on a urine sample.
  • Computed Tomography (CT): Imaging technique that rotates an x-ray emitter around an area to be scanned.
  • Cystography: Radiographic examination of the urinary bladder using contrast medium.
  • Cystometrography: Procedure that assesses volume and pressure in the bladder.

ðŸ’¡ Pro Tip

Focus on the combining forms related to the urinary structures such as nephr/o (kidney) and cyst/o (bladder) as these will be most helpful as you work with terms related to the genitourinary system.

ðŸ“š Key Takeaways

  • The genitourinary system includes the urinary organs and the male reproductive organs which are described using specific medical terminology.
  • Common pathologies of this system include kidney stones, urinary infections, and prostate disorders.
  • Terms such as cystitis, dysuria, and pyelonephritis are helpful to know in the context of genitourinary disorders.
  • Common diagnostic and therapeutic procedures include cystoscopy, urinalysis, and TURP.
