ðŸ“š Pathological Terms

Here are some of the common pathological terms relating to the musculoskeletal system:

  • Osteoporosis: A metabolic bone disorder characterized by decreased bone density.
  • Osteomyelitis: Infection of the bone and bone marrow.
  • Paget Disease: A chronic inflammation of bones resulting in thickening and softening of the bones.
  • Fracture: A broken bone.
  • Spondylosis: Degeneration of the vertebrae and related tissues.
  • Arthritis: A general term for joint diseases, an inflammation of a joint.
  • Rheumatoid Arthritis (RA): A systemic disease characterized by inflammatory changes in joints and their related structures.
  • Osteoarthritis: A degenerative joint disease involving cartilage destruction and new bone formation.
  • Gouty Arthritis (Gout): A metabolic disease caused by the accumulation of uric acid crystals in the blood.
  • Muscular Dystrophy: A genetic disease characterized by atrophy and weakening of muscles.

ðŸ“š Diagnostic, Symptomatic, and Related Terms

Here are some of the most common diagnostic and symptomatic terms related to the musculoskeletal system:

  • Acromegaly: A chronic metabolic disorder characterized by a gradual enlargement of the bones of the face and jaw.
  • Arthralgia: Pain in a joint.
  • Dislocation: Displacement of a bone from a joint.
  • Dystonia: A movement disorder characterized by sustained muscle contractions.
  • Subluxation: Partial or incomplete dislocation.
  • Tinnitus: Perception of ringing, hissing, or other sounds in the ears.

ðŸ“š Therapeutic Procedures

Here are some common therapeutic procedures related to the musculoskeletal system:

  • Laminectomy: Excision of the posterior arch of a vertebra.
  • Arthrocentesis: Surgical puncture of a joint space to remove fluid.
  • Arthroplasty: Plastic surgery or reconstruction of a joint.
  • Arthrotomy: Incision of a joint.
  • Osteotomy: Incision into a bone.
  • Spinal Fusion: Surgical immobilization of vertebrae.
  • Reduction: Procedure that restores a bone to its normal position
  • Casting: Application of a solid dressing to immobilize a body part.

ðŸ’¡ Pro Tip

ðŸ“š Key Takeaways

  • The musculoskeletal system provides the body with shape, support, stability, and movement.
  • Common pathologies of this system include fractures, osteoporosis, osteomyelitis, and various arthritic conditions.
  • Terms such as arthralgia, kyphosis, and subluxation are important for working with musculoskeletal disorders.
  • Therapeutic procedures often include bone reductions, casting, and surgery.
