üìö Pathological Processes

Understanding pathology is essential to understanding medical language. From a clinical point of view, disease is a pathological or morbid condition that presents a group of signs, symptoms, and clinical findings. Signs are objective indicators that are observable, while a symptom is subjective and is experienced by only the patient.

Etiology is the study of the cause of a disease or disorder. Establishing the cause and nature of a disease is called diagnosis. A prognosis is the prediction of the course of a disease and its probable outcome.

üìö Diagnostic, Symptomatic, and Related Terms

Here are some common diagnostic, symptomatic, and related terms:

  • Adhesion: Abnormal fibrous band that holds or binds tissues together.
  • Analyte: Substance analyzed or tested in a laboratory.
  • Contrast Medium: Substance used to help visualize internal structures on an X-ray or other image.
  • Dehiscence: Bursting open of a wound.
  • Febrile: Feverish.
  • Homeostasis: Relative constancy or balance in the internal environment of the body.
  • Inflammation: Body defense against injury marked by swelling and pain.
  • Morbid: Diseased.
  • Nuclear Medicine: A branch of medicine that uses radioactive substances for diagnosis.
  • Radiology: The study of electromagnetic radiation and imaging techniques.
  • Interventional radiology: Radiological practice that employs fluoroscopy, CT, and ultrasound in nonsurgical treatment.
  • Therapeutic: Treatment
  • Radionuclides: Substances that emit radiation spontaneously

üìö Therapeutic Procedures

Here are some common therapeutic procedures:

  • Ablation: Removal of a part, pathway, or function by surgery, chemical destruction or freezing.
  • Anastomosis: Surgical joining of two ducts, vessels, or bowel segments.
  • Cauterize: Destroy tissue by electricity, freezing, heat, or corrosive chemicals.
  • Incision and drainage (I&D): Incision made to allow the free flow or withdrawal of fluids from a wound or cavity.
  • Laser Surgery: Surgical technique employing a device that emits intense heat and power at close range to cut, burn, vaporize, or destroy tissues.
  • Radical Dissection: Surgical removal of tissue in an extensive area surrounding the surgical site.

üìö Integumentary System

The integumentary system refers to the skin and its accessory organs (such as hair, nails, and glands), which provide a protective covering for the body. Here are some of the medical word elements related to the integumentary system:

  • adip/o: fat
  • lip/o: fat
  • steat/o: fat
  • cutane/o: skin
  • dermat/o: skin
  • derm/o: skin
  • hidr/o: sweat
  • sudor/o: sweat
  • ichthy/o: dry, scaly
  • kerat/o: horny tissue, hard cornea
  • melan/o: black

üí° Pro Tip

Focus on learning the meaning of common root words and suffixes, so that you can better decipher complex terms.

üìö Key Takeaways

  • Pathology provides the basis for understanding the nature and cause of disease and their related signs and symptoms.
  • Understanding basic procedures and terms is crucial for working in healthcare.
  • The integumentary system, including the skin, hair, nails and glands, can be described using a variety of medical terms.
