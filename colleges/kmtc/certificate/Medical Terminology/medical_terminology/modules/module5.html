The digestive system includes the organs of the gastrointestinal tract, from the mouth to the anus, along with the accessory digestive organs, including the salivary glands, liver, gallbladder, and pancreas. Learning the word elements of this system will aid your understanding of disorders of the digestive tract. Here are some of the medical word elements related to the digestive system:

  • Mouth: or/o or stomat/o
  • Tongue: gloss/o or lingu/o

ðŸ“š Pathological Terms

Here are some of the common pathological terms related to the digestive system:

  • Ulcer: A circumscribed open sore on the skin or mucous membranes within the body. Peptic ulcers are the most common type of ulcer that occurs in the digestive system.
  • Peptic Ulcer Disease (PUD): A condition caused by exposure to hydrochloric acid and pepsin, which breaks down mucous membranes. It is often associated with the bacteria H. pylori.
  • Ulcerative Colitis: A chronic inflammatory disease of the large intestine and rectum.
  • Intestinal Obstruction: A complete or partial blockage that prevents forward flow of digestive products.
  • Hemorrhoids: Enlarged veins in the mucous membrane of the anal canal.
  • Hepatitis: An inflammatory condition of the liver.
  • Diverticulosis: A condition in which small, blister like pockets (diverticula) develop in the lining of the large intestine.
  • Pancreatic Cancer: A cancer of the pancreas that is often difficult to diagnose and treat.

ðŸ“š Diagnostic, Symptomatic, and Related Terms

Here are some of the most common symptomatic and diagnostic terms relating to the digestive system:

  • Anorexia: Lack or loss of appetite.
  • Appendicitis: Inflammation of the appendix.
  • Ascites: Abnormal accumulation of fluid in the abdomen.
  • Borborygmus: Rumbling or gurgling noises that are audible at a distance.
  • Cachexia: Physical wasting that includes loss of weight and muscle mass.
  • Cholelithiasis: Presence or formation of gallstones in the gallbladder or common bile duct
  • Cirrhosis: Scarring and dysfunction of the liver.

ðŸ“š Diagnostic and Therapeutic Procedures

Here are some of the more common diagnostic and therapeutic procedures relating to the digestive system:

  • Endoscopy: Visual examination of a body cavity or canal using a flexible fiberoptic instrument.
  • Barium Swallow: Radiographic examination of the esophagus, stomach, and small intestine following oral administration of barium sulfate.
  • Barium Enema (BE): Radiographic examination of the rectum and colon following enema administration of barium sulfate.
  • Cholesystography: Radiographic images taken of the gallbladder after administration of contrast material.
  • Computed Tomography (CT): Imaging technique that uses x-rays to produce three-dimensional images.
  • Ultrasonography: Imaging procedure using high-frequency sound waves (ultrasound).
  • Biopsy: Representative tissue sample removed from the body for microscopic examination.
  • Frozen Section (FS): Ultra thin slice of tissue cut from a frozen specimen for immediate pathological examination.
  • Chemical Peel: Chemical removal of the outer layers of skin to treat acne, scaling, and keratoses.
  • Debridement: Removal of necrotic tissue from a wound by surgical excision, enzymes or chemical agents.
  • Dermabrasion: Rubbing using wire brushes to mechanically scrape away the epidermis.
  • Fulguration: Tissue destruction by means of high-frequency electric current.
