ðŸ“š Medical Word Elements of the Blood, Lymph and Immune Systems

The blood, lymph, and immune systems work together to protect and regulate the body. The blood is responsible for transporting oxygen, carbon dioxide, nutrients, wastes, and hormones. The lymphatic system drains fluid and debris from tissue and aids in immunity. The immune system protects the body from pathogens. ðŸ“š Pathological Terms

Here are some of the common pathological terms related to the blood, lymph, and immune systems:

  • Anemia: Any condition in which the oxygen carrying capacity of blood is deficient.
  • Acquired Immune Deficiency Syndrome (AIDS): An infectious disease caused by HIV which destroys the immune system.
  • Leukemia: An oncological disorder of the blood forming organs characterized by overgrowth of blood cells.
  • Hemophilia: A hereditary disorder in which the blood-clotting mechanism is impaired.
  • Infectious Mononucleosis: An acute infection caused by the Epstein-Barr virus (EBV).
  • Multiple Sclerosis (MS): A progressive, degenerative disease of the central nervous system that causes loss of myelin throughout the spinal cord and brain.
  • Alzheimer Disease (AD): A progressive neurological disorder that causes memory loss and serious mental deterioration.

ðŸ“š Diagnostic, Symptomatic, and Related Terms

Here are some of the most common symptomatic and diagnostic terms relating to the blood, lymph, and immune systems:

  • Residual Effect: Refers to conditions which are a residual effect of an earlier condition.
  • Skull Fracture: A fracture in any bone of the skull.
  • Gastric Ulcers: A sore or lesion in the stomach.
  • Fetal Disease: Refers to any condition that develops in a fetus.
  • Solar Keratosis: A rough or scaly patch of skin caused by overexposure to the sun.
  • Prolonged Labor: Labor that is longer than normal.
  • Meningococcal Meningitis: Meningitis caused by the bacteria Neisseria meningitidis.
  • Post Procedural Wound Infection: An infection of a wound following a procedure.
  • Family history of diabetic mellitus: A family history of diabetes.

ðŸ“š Therapeutic Procedures

Here are some common therapeutic procedures related to the blood, lymph and immune systems:

  • Bone Marrow Aspiration and Biopsy: Removal of bone marrow fluid for examination.
  • Transfusion: Infusion of blood or blood components into the bloodstream.
  • Ligation and Stripping: Tying a varicose vein followed by removal of the affected area.

ðŸ’¡ Pro Tip

Remember that the blood, lymph, and immune systems are interconnected, with the lymphatic system helping to regulate and drain fluids and the immune system helping to protect against infection.

ðŸ“š Key Takeaways

  • The blood, lymph, and immune systems work together to transport oxygen, carbon dioxide, nutrients, and wastes, as well as protect the body from disease.
  • Common pathologies of the blood, lymph, and immune systems include anemias, AIDS, leukemias, hemophilia, and infections.
  • Terms such as lymphadenopathy, splenomegaly, and immunodeficiency are essential to know.
  • Diagnostic and therapeutic procedures include methods such as blood transfusions, biopsies, and imaging techniques.
