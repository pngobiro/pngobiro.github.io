üìö Medical Word Elements of the Respiratory System

The respiratory system provides the body with oxygen and rids it of carbon dioxide. It includes organs such as the nose, pharynx, larynx, trachea, bronchi, and lungs. Here are some of the medical word elements related to the respiratory system:

  • Nose: nas/o or rhin/o
  • Septum: sept/o
  • Sinus: sinus/o
  • Adenoids: adenoid/o
  • Tonsils: tonsill/o
  • Pharynx: pharyng/o
  • Epiglottis: epiglott/o
  • Larynx (voice box): laryng/o
  • Trachea (windpipe): trache/o
  • Bronchus (plural, bronchi): bronchi/o
  • Bronchiole: bronchiol/o
  • Alveolus (plural, alveoli): alveol/o
  • Pleura: pleur/o
  • Lung: pulmon/o or pneumon/o

üìö Pathological Terms

Here are some of the common pathological terms related to the respiratory system:

  • Chronic Obstructive Pulmonary Disease (COPD): Respiratory disorders that produce a chronic partial obstruction of the air passages, including asthma, chronic bronchitis and emphysema.
  • Asthma: A condition that produces spasms in the bronchial passages, leading to difficulty breathing.
  • Chronic Bronchitis: Inflammation of the bronchi caused mainly by smoking or air pollution.
  • Emphysema: A condition characterized by decreased elasticity of the alveoli and a ‚Äúbarrel chested‚Äù appearance.
  • Influenza (flu): An acute infectious respiratory viral disease.
  • Pleural Effusions: Abnormal fluid in the pleural cavity between the visceral and parietal pleura.
  • Tuberculosis (TB): A communicable disease caused by the bacterium Mycobacterium tuberculosis.
  • Pneumonia: Inflammatory disease of the lungs caused by bacteria, viruses, or fungi.

üìö Diagnostic, Symptomatic, and Related Terms

Here are some of the more common symptomatic and diagnostic terms relating to the respiratory system:

  • Residual Effect: Refers to conditions which are a residual effect of an earlier condition.
  • Skull Fracture: A fracture in any bone of the skull.
  • Gastric Ulcers: A sore or lesion in the stomach.
  • Fetal Disease: Refers to any condition that develops in a fetus.
  • Solar Keratosis: A rough or scaly patch of skin caused by overexposure to the sun.
  • Prolonged Labor: Labor that is longer than normal.
  • Meningococcal Meningitis: Meningitis caused by the bacteria Neisseria meningitidis.
  • Post Procedural Wound Infection: An infection of a wound following a procedure.
  • Family history of diabetic mellitus: A family history of diabetes.

üìö Therapeutic Procedures

Here are some common therapeutic procedures relating to the respiratory system:

  • Aerosol Therapy: Using various techniques to deliver medications in a mist form.
  • Lavage: Irrigating or washing out an organ or body cavity.
  • Pneumonectomy: Excision of a lung.
  • Pleurectomy: Excision of part of the pleura, usually parietal.

üí° Pro Tip

When studying diseases and conditions, take note of the various root words and suffixes used, as this will help solidify your knowledge of medical terminology in the respiratory system.

üìö Key Takeaways

  • The respiratory system involves organs for breathing such as the nose, pharynx, larynx, trachea, bronchi and lungs.
  • Respiratory disorders include COPD, asthma, bronchitis, emphysema, influenza and pleural effusions.
  • Diagnostic and therapeutic procedures include methods such as aerosol therapy, lavage, pneumonectomy and pleurectomy.
