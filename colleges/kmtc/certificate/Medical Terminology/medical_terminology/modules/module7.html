ðŸ“š Medical Word Elements of the Cardiovascular System

The cardiovascular system is responsible for the transport of blood, containing oxygen, carbon dioxide, nutrients, and wastes, throughout the body. This system includes the heart, blood vessels, and lymphatic vessels. Here are some of the medical word elements related to this system:

  • Widened blood vessel: aneurys/o
  • Vessel: angi/o or vascul/o
  • Aorta: aort/o
  • Artery: arteri/o
  • Arteriole: arteriol/o
  • Atrium: atri/o
  • Fatty Plaque: ather/o
  • Heart: cardi/o
  • Electricity: electr/o
  • Blood vessel: hemangi/o
  • Muscle: my/o
  • Vein: phleb/o or ven/o
  • Sclera (white of eye): scler/o
  • Septum: sept/o
  • Pulse: sphygm/o
  • Narrowing; structure: sten/o
  • Blood clot: thromb/o
  • Ventricle (of the heart or brain): ventricul/o

ðŸ“š Pathological Terms

Here are some of the common pathological terms relating to the cardiovascular system:

  • Arteriosclerosis: Hardening of the arterial walls, which become thickened and brittle.
  • Coronary Artery Disease (CAD): A disease characterized by the failure of the coronary arteries to adequately supply blood to the myocardium.
  • Endocarditis: Inflammation of the inner lining of the heart and its valves.
  • Varicose veins: Enlarged, twisted, superficial veins.
  • Hemorrhoids: Enlarged veins in the mucous membrane of the anal canal.

ðŸ“š Diagnostic, Symptomatic, and Related Terms

Here are some of the more common symptomatic and diagnostic terms relating to the cardiovascular system:

  • Aneurysm: Localized abnormal dilation of a vessel, usually an artery.
  • Arrest: Condition of being stopped or bringing to a stop.
  • Arrhythmia: Inability of the heart to maintain a normal sinus rhythm.
  • Bruit: Soft blowing sound heard on auscultation.
  • Cardiomyopathy: Any disease or weakening of heart muscle that diminishes cardiac function
  • Catheter: Thin, flexible, hollow plastic tube that is small enough to be threaded through a vein or artery.
  • Coarctation: Narrowing of a vessel, especially the aorta.
  • Deep Vein Thrombosis: Blood clot that forms in the deep veins of the body, especially those in the legs.
  • Ejection: Calculation of how much blood a ventricle can eject with one contraction.

ðŸ“š Therapeutic Procedures

Here are some common therapeutic procedures related to the cardiovascular system:

  • Percutaneous transluminal coronary angioplasty (PTCA): A procedure to widen the lumen of a diseased blood vessel by inserting a balloon.
  • Coronary artery bypass graft (CABG): A procedure to reroute blood around a blocked area by using a graft vessel.
  • Atherectomy: The removal of material from an occluded vessel.
  • Angiography: Radiographic imaging of the heart and blood vessels after injection of contrast dye.
  • Echocardiography: A noninvasive diagnostic method that uses ultrasound to visualize the heart and blood vessels.
  • Doppler Ultrasound: A noninvasive adaptation of ultrasound technology in which blood flow velocity is assessed.
  • Magnetic Resonance Imaging (MRI): A noninvasive imaging technique that uses radio waves and a strong magnetic field.

ðŸ’¡ Pro Tip

When considering the cardiovascular system, think of it as a system that provides essential materials and removes waste products. Understanding the organs and processes involved will help you to apply the terminology more effectively.

ðŸ“š Key Takeaways

  • The cardiovascular system includes organs for circulating blood, such as the heart and vessels.
  • Common pathological conditions of this system include arteriosclerosis, coronary artery disease, endocarditis, and varicose veins.
  • Understanding common procedures like angiography, echocardiography, and angioplasty are key.
  • Terms such as aneurysm, arrhythmia, embolus, and fibrillation are important for working with the cardiovascular system.
