üìö Introduction to Medical Terminology

Medical terminology is a professional language used by doctors and other healthcare practitioners to communicate facts and ideas. Medical terms can seem daunting at first, but understanding the basic elements of medical words, along with rules for combining these elements, will allow you to read and interpret almost all medical terms. In this module, you will learn the basic word elements, and how they combine to form medical terms.

üìö Importance of Learning Medical Terminology

Learning medical terminology is important as it:

  • Helps you to read and understand medical literature
  • Facilitates clear communication with other health professionals.
  • Allows for understanding of standard abbreviations used in the field of medicine.
  • Gives you the ability to write and spell medical terms.
  • Helps you appreciate the logical methods in medical terminology.
  • Allows you to apply medical terminology to coding and indexing according to the International Classification of Diseases and related health problems.

üìö Basic Elements of Medical Words

The language of medicine is a specialized vocabulary used by healthcare practitioners. Many medical terms originated in the first century BC. In modern times, new terms have evolved to reflect modern medicine and technologies such as magnetic resonance imaging (MRI) and ultrasound (US).

Medical words consist of a word root, combining form, suffix, and prefix, which may or may not be present in every term. The primary elements are:

  • Word Roots: This is the foundation of a medical term and contains its primary meaning. Most word roots are derived from Greek or Latin.
  • Combining Forms: Created when a word root is combined with a vowel (usually an ‚Äúo‚Äù) which enables it to be connected to other word elements.
  • Suffixes: A word element placed at the end of a word that changes the meaning of the word, and often describes a pathology, symptom, surgical or diagnostic procedure, or part of speech.
  • Prefixes: A word element attached to the beginning of a word or word root to further define the meaning of the word. Not all medical terms have prefixes.

üí° Pro Tip

Try breaking down medical terms into their individual elements as you encounter them. This will help you decipher and understand their meanings.

üìö Key Takeaways

  • Medical terminology is a specialized language used for clear communication in the healthcare field.
  • Medical terms include word roots, combining forms, suffixes, and prefixes.
  • Word roots form the foundation of medical terms and carry the core meaning.
  • Combining forms combine word roots with vowels to enable them to join with other elements.
  • Suffixes and prefixes change and specify the meanings of medical terms.
  • Medical terms have pronunciation rules based in Greek or Latin.
