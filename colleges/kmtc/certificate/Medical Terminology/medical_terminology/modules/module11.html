ðŸ“š Medical Word Elements of the Female Reproductive System

The female reproductive system is involved in hormone production, reproduction, and development of offspring. The female reproductive system includes the ovaries, fallopian tubes, uterus, cervix, vagina, and breasts. Here are some of the medical word elements related to this system:

  • Amnion (amniotic sac): amni/o
  • Neck; cervix uteri (neck of uterus): cervic/o
  • Vagina: colp/o or vagin/o
  • Milk: galact/o or lact/o
  • Woman, female: gynec/o
  • Uterus (womb): hyster/o or metr/o
  • Breast: mamm/o or mast/o

ðŸ“š Pathological Terms

Here are some of the most common pathological terms related to the female reproductive system:

  • Endometriosis: The presence of functional endometrial tissue in areas outside the uterus.
  • Pelvic Inflammatory Disease (PID): Inflammation of the uterus, fallopian tubes, ovaries, and adjacent pelvic structures, usually caused by bacterial infection.
  • Vaginitis: Inflammations of the vagina caused by viruses, bacteria, or yeast.
  • Uterine Fibroids: Benign tumors composed of muscle and fibrous tissue.
  • Breast Cancer: A cancer of the breast tissue that can metastasize if not treated.
  • Cervical Cancer: A cancer that develops in the cervix and which can be prevented with regular screening tests.
  • Malignant Melanoma: A highly malignant form of skin cancer.

ðŸ“š Diagnostic, Symptomatic, and Related Terms

Here are some common diagnostic, symptomatic, and related terms used when discussing conditions of the female reproductive system:

  • Atresia: Congenital absence or closure of a normal body opening.
  • Dyspareunia: Occurrence of pain during sexual intercourse.
  • Menarche: Beginning of menstrual function.
  • Oligomenorrhea: Scanty or infrequent menstrual flow.
  • Ectopic Pregnancy: Pregnancy in which the fertilized ovum does not reach the uterine cavity but implants in another area.

ðŸ“š Diagnostic and Therapeutic Procedures

Here are some common diagnostic and therapeutic procedures related to the female reproductive system:

  • Amniocentesis: Transabdominal puncture of the amniotic sac under ultrasound guidance to remove amniotic fluid for testing.
  • Colposcopy: Visual examination of the vagina and cervix using an optical magnifying instrument.
  • Laparoscopy: Visual examination of the abdominal cavity using a laparoscope.
  • Mammography: Radiographic examination of the breast to screen for breast cancer.
  • Hysterosalpingography: Radiography of the uterus and fallopian tubes following injection of a contrast medium.
  • Breast Implant Revision: Surgery to correct a problem with a prior breast implant or to replace an old implant.
  • Lumpectomy: Excision of a small primary breast tumor.

ðŸ’¡ Pro Tip

When studying the female reproductive system, it is helpful to relate the function of each organ with the symptoms, pathologies, and procedures that relate to that specific structure.

ðŸ“š Key Takeaways

  • The female reproductive system includes the ovaries, fallopian tubes, uterus, cervix, vagina and breasts, each with their own specific medical terminology.
  • Common pathologies of this system include endometriosis, pelvic inflammatory disease, vaginitis, uterine fibroids, and breast cancer.
  • Terms such as dysmenorrhea, menorrhagia, and ectopic pregnancy are important for working with disorders of the female reproductive system.
  • Diagnostic and therapeutic procedures often include mammography, laparoscopy, and biopsies.
