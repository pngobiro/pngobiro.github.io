Health Information Systems Course

Duration: 24 hours Level: Intermediate

Course Overview

This course provides a comprehensive introduction to health information systems, covering fundamental concepts, data management, and the use of technology in healthcare. It explores the historical development of HIS, the different types of data collection methods, and the use of data in planning and policy-making. The course also focuses on the role of ICT and specific systems like DHIS2 and Electronic Medical Records in modern healthcare settings.

Course Modules

  1. Module 1: Introduction to Health Information Systems

    Explore the fundamental concepts of health information systems, their components, historical context, and policy frameworks.

    6 hours

  2. Module 2: HIS Policy, Structure, and Tiers of Care

    Examine the national HIS policy, system structure, and the organization of health service delivery from the community level upwards.

    4 hours

  3. Module 3: Sources of Health Data & Collection Tools

    Learn about various sources of health data, the use of registers and tally sheets, and other data collection methodologies.

    30 hours

  4. Module 4: Health Sector Indicators and Data Quality

    Study the different types of indicators, their role in health planning, and the importance of data quality in HIS.

    20 hours

  5. Module 5: Use of ICT in Health Information Systems

    Explore how Information and Communication Technologies, such as DHIS2, EMRs, and GIS, are applied in modern health information systems.

    30 hours

Prerequisites

  • Basic understanding of healthcare operations
  • Familiarity with computer systems
  • Interest in health data management
