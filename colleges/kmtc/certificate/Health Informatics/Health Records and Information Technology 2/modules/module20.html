Module 20: Utilization and Criteria for Health Information

  • Describe the utilization of health information for personal health, community health, health status, health planning, and research.

  • Explain the Goals of Health Information System regarding accessibility, comprehensiveness, and confidentiality.

üìö Utilization of health information

There are multiple uses for health information once it is collected, processed and analyzed.

The following are some ways in which health information may be utilized at different stages

  1. Personal health

    Records are kept about individual patients during the course of their diagnosis and treatments or when preventive services such as ante-natal or family planning services are provided to them.

  2. Community health

    Information about promotive and preventive services to the community is useful to better understand the behavior of the community as a whole and plan and deliver health information.

  3. Health status

    Through the aggregation of information, about diagnosis and death, a picture of the community's morbidity and mortality can be acquired. This information is necessarily to assess the health status of the population and whether their trend is developing.

  4. Health planning

    Health information is used to;

    • Monitor the quantity and quality of services being provided to set goals. Measures progress towards goals to allocate resources (financial, personnel facilities) and to determine the economics of health care
  5. Research

    Health information is used to study a wide variety of aspects of the health of the nation. Information is required for research when new health problems confront the nation such as COVID-19.

üìö CRITERIA FOR INFORMATION INCLUDED IN THE HEALTH INFORMATION

The MoH will exercise its prerogative in judging whether items of health information are to be included in the health information system. In general for a health information to be included, it must be deemed to be of essential value in furthering the understanding of the overall health picture of the nation.

The overall thrust of the policy is to limit the amount of information collected and reported so as not to place an unmanageable burden on the personnel required to collect it.

The following criteria will be applied in determining whether to include items of information in the health information system.

  1. Useful - the information must have value, facilitating the management of health services.
  2. Ease of collection ‚Äì the information which is complicated or time consuming to collect should be excluded. Even if the information in question has value, but places an unordinary burden on health personnel to collect especially at the expense of the health care activities it should not be included in the health information system
  3. Quality of information ‚Äì information collected should be of high quality n measures should be put in place to ensure the information meets the required standards
  4. Meaningful ‚Äì the information must add significant meaning to the understanding of the overall health pictures.
  5. Utilization of information ‚Äì information which is collected and reported not utilized must be excluded from the health information system. It happens that information piles in offices without being utilized.
  6. Who requires the information ‚Äì at issue here is whether the information is basically to satisfy the needs of the nation or the needs of foreign donors. The health services must not be "built‚Äù by donor organizations who requires excessive information connected with funding.

üìö GOALS OF HEALTH INFORMATION SYSTEM

The ultimate of goals of health information is to construct an accessible and comprehensive data base that meets the requirements of the patients, doctors, managers, donors, evaluators and other allied personnel.

Health information personnel must ensure that confidentiality of personal health information is safeguarded though appropriate documentation, storage, handling and communication practices

Communication practices to ensure confidentiality include;

  1. Only authorized personnel should have access to personal health information
  2. Documented personal health information is securely stored and protected
  3. Whenever documented personal health information is transmitted through any mail system, it is properly packaged and labeled

üìö Key Takeaways

  • Health information utilization spans personal health records, community health programs, health status assessment, health planning, and research.
  • Criteria for including health information in HIS are: Usefulness, Ease of collection, Quality, Meaningfulness, Utilization, and serving national needs over donor demands.
  • Goals of HIS include creating an accessible and comprehensive database for all stakeholders and ensuring confidentiality through secure documentation, storage, handling, and communication practices.
  • Confidential communication practices involve limiting access to authorized personnel, secure storage, and proper packaging and labeling during transmission.
