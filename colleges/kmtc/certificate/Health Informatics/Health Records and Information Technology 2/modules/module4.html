üìö 4. Immunization and vitamin a tally sheet ‚Äì MoH ‚Äì 702 (Continued vaccine types)

The different types of vaccines include;

  1. BCG ‚Äì T.B vaccine
  2. OPV/IPV ‚Äì polio vaccine
  3. PENTAVALENT -
    1. Tetanus toxoid
    2. Diphtheria
    3. Whooping cough
    4. Haemophilus influenza
    5. Hepatitis B
  4. T.T - Tetanus toxoid for women of child bearing age
  5. Vitamin A ‚Äì
    1. For children under one year
    2. For children above one year
    3. For lactating mothers (breast feeding mothers)

üìö 5. Immunization and vitamin A summary sheet ‚Äì MoH 710

This shows the various vaccines given and is compiled on daily and monthly basis. It is fed by the immunization and vitamin A tally sheet and gives a summary of the whole month per each facility

üìö 6. Notification of infectious disease

This is a summary of infectious disease such as;

  • T.B - tuberculosis
  • Ebola
  • Sars

This summary shows how to prevent the spread. This is normally compiled on monthly basis from the infectious disease department or clinic

üìö 7. Disease surveillance report (MoH 504)

This report gives an information of any targeted disease e.g. Acute Flaccid paralysis (AFP), measles, neonatal tetanus (NNT) etc.

The sub-county surveillance coordinator collects the specimen and takes it to the (KEMRI) Kenya Medical Research Institute for investigation. If the results are positive, a preventive measure is taken e.g. Measles Campaign, a quarantine is carried out to prevent the spread of the disease.

üìö Key Takeaways

  • Immunization and vitamin A summary sheets (MoH 710) provide monthly summaries of vaccine and vitamin A administrations, based on daily tally sheets.
  • Notification of infectious disease reports summarize cases of diseases like tuberculosis, Ebola, and SARS to monitor and prevent their spread, compiled monthly.
  • Disease surveillance reports (MoH 504) track targeted diseases such as AFP, measles, and NNT, with sub-county coordinators investigating cases and implementing preventive measures.
