üìö GOALS OF HEALTH INFORMATION SYSTEM (continued)

  1. Personal health information is communicated to authorized person on need to know basis only
  2. Health personnel should be oriented to the personnel health information practices at the time of their employment
  3. Disciplinary action should be taken against any staff not observing proper confidentiality of personal health information

üìö Who are the users of these information?

  1. Managers ‚Äì they use the information on planning on effective delivery of health care services
  2. Donors - they use the information for funding new health care projects that may arise.
  3. Researchers ‚Äì they use the information on studying disease patterns that are presenting unique problem to the community
  4. Evaluators ‚Äì to evaluate health services that they planned for implementation
  5. Policy makers ‚Äì to formulate health polices
  6. Community ‚Äì to improve their health status and environmental conditions though their full participation.

  8. Epidemiology ‚Äì use information to control the disease especially epidemic outbreaks and the causes of various disease in in particular environment.

üìö ANALYSIS OF HEALTH INFORMATION

Each level of the health system that collects health information is required to perform an appropriate degree of analysis of that information.

This analysis is performed for the purpose of monitoring the changing health needs of the population and evaluating the effectiveness of health system in meeting those needs

Once the health information that has been collected is processed, the essential steps of analyzing that information must be performed. Interpretive report is required

An interpretive report is an examination of the descriptive information with view towards providing insight into how the information can be used to improve the health status of the population and the services delivered to that population.

Interpretation of information gives managers ideas about collective action to take, how better to display resources and how to make improvement in the structure and organization of their operations.

Information collected, analyzed and interpreted need to be shared/ disseminated with colleagues and other users of health information. Sharing of information can take place in three ways;

  1. Distribution of reports
  2. Presenting information in meetings
  3. Discussing information with colleagues

üìö GENERATING INDICATORS

The steps of meaningful analysis of health information is to develop indicators:

INDICATORS: are statistical values which give insight into the quality of health status or health services

Indicators assist managers in making judgement of quality. Up to this point the data that has been collected, reported and processed have provided a quantitative picture. What is needed now is to see how this quantitative information gives insight into the qualitative nature of health status and services. Developing the correct indicator helps this happen since many indicators can be identified, it is necessary to select Key Indicators. Key indicators are the most important items of data that allow managers to have rapid assessment of the health picture in their area

üìö Key Takeaways

  • Further confidentiality measures include need-to-know communication, staff orientation on confidentiality practices at employment, and disciplinary actions for breaches.
  • Users of health information are diverse, including managers, donors, researchers, evaluators, policymakers, communities, clinicians, and epidemiologists, each using HIS for specific purposes like planning, funding, research, evaluation, policy, community health, clinical management, and disease control.
  • Analysis of health information is essential at all system levels to monitor health needs and system effectiveness, requiring interpretive reports to provide insights for service improvement and resource management.
  • Indicators are statistical values that provide insight into health status or service quality, aiding managers in qualitative assessments and rapid health picture evaluations through key indicators.
