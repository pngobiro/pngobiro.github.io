Module 8: Analytical Formulae Part 1

üìö Terminology (continued)

  1. Percentage occupancy ‚Äì the % of actual patient days to the maximum available patients days as determined by bed capacity during any given period
  2. Occupied bed days ‚Äì the total number of patients remaining in the hospital / wards each day added together over the reference period.
  3. Length of stay ‚Äì the average number of days patients occupy a bed
  4. Average occupancy / average number of patients ‚Äì it means the average number of patients in a hospital during a specified period
  5. Amenity bed ‚Äì refers to a bed provided in a single room / small ward for the time being required by any patients on medical ground for which the patients may be charged part of the cost.
  6. In-patient - a person who has gone through the whole admission procedure of the hospital and who is occupying a bed in the in-patient departments at the hour of fixed count.
  7. Day cases the persons attending as non-residence patients for investing therapeutic test, operative procedures or other treatment and who requires some form of preparation period of recovery or both involving the provision of accommodation and service

üìö ANALYTICAL FORMULAE

  1. Available bed days (ABD) / Stuffed bed days = available beds * days in period

    ABD = AB*DIP

  2. Available beds / average available beds / staffed beds = total authorized beds in a hospital or ward during a specified period

    Average available beds or AB = ABD/DIP

  3. Occupied bed days / patient days/ in patient days = total patient remaining each day/ in- patient days added together to the days in the period

    OBD = OB *DIP

    OB = OBD/DIP

  4. OBD = % Occupancy *ABD/ 100

    %Occupancy = OBD/ABD *100%

  5. Average number of occupied beds or average number of occupied beds = Occupied bed days / days in period

    OB=OBD/DIP

  6. Vacant bed days = available bed days ‚Äì(minus) occupied bed days

üìö Key Takeaways

  • Percentage occupancy measures the proportion of actual patient days to the maximum possible patient days.
  • Occupied bed days (OBD) is the cumulative count of patients in the hospital each day over a period.
  • Length of stay is the average duration patients occupy a bed.
  • Average occupancy is the mean number of patients in the hospital over a specified period.
  • Amenity bed refers to a private bed for which patients may be charged extra.
  • Analytical formulae are used to calculate key bed utilization statistics:
    • Available Bed Days (ABD) = Available Beds (AB) * Days in Period (DIP)
    • Available Beds (AB) = ABD / DIP
    • Occupied Bed Days (OBD) = Occupied Beds (OB) * DIP
    • Occupied Beds (OB) = OBD / DIP
    • Percentage Occupancy = (OBD / ABD) * 100%
    • Vacant Bed Days (VBD) = ABD - OBD
