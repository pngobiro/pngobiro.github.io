üìö SOURCES OF HEALTH INFORMATION (continued)

  1. Notification of diseases
  2. Registration of deaths and births
  3. Surveys
  4. Community based health care projects

üìö Notification of diseases

These are limited to infectious diseases and are important indicators of the presences of a communicable disease in a community. These help to identify the need for control and prevention actions.

üìö Survey

Surveys are periodic methods of collecting health care data by using a sample of a population. There are two types of surveys that we are going to consider in this version. These are:

  1. Those using samples and are based on scientific design and analysis
  2. Less formal surveys such as home visits which are conducted by health workers and community leaders.

üìö Community based health care projects

The projects are mainly run by non-government organizations. The development of community health care is influenced and used to stimulate individuals and local communities to determine their own health needs and at the same time to initiate interventions. In community based health care project, people determine their own needs and decide on priority health care activities to solve their health problems

On self-help basis, individuals, families and community members should be encouraged to participate in health promoting activities. The types of data likely to be collected from a community based care project may include information about the;

  1. Local people and their environment
  2. Number of people and their distribution
  3. Disease they suffer from
  4. Organization of the local health services.

üìö HIS HEAD OFFICE Diagram

The HIS HEAD OFFICE diagram illustrates the flow of health information from junior health facilities up to the head office and back, showing the interconnectedness of different levels within the health system.

üìö Key Takeaways

  • Notification of diseases, especially infectious ones, is a vital source for identifying and controlling communicable diseases in communities.
  • Registration of deaths and births provides essential demographic and health status data for population health monitoring.
  • Surveys, both scientific sample-based and informal community-led, are periodic methods for collecting health data directly from populations.
  • Community-based health care projects, often NGO-led, empower communities to identify and address their health needs, generating valuable local health information.
  • The HIS HEAD OFFICE diagram visually represents the hierarchical flow of health information within the system, from local facilities to national and back, emphasizing feedback loops.
