Module 12: Efficiency and Types of Information Systems

üéØ Learning Objectives

  • Understand the flow of data from bottom to top and top to bottom in HIS.
  • Describe the characteristics of an efficient health information system.
  • Identify and differentiate between Inpatient, Outpatient, and Administrative/Management health information systems.

üìö Systems within health information system. (continued explanation)

By now you should have realized that the flow of data is from bottom to top and from top to bottom. This feedback facilitates quality decision making and formulation health policies. Planners and managers such as ScHMT should use this report in order to do the following,

  • Organize, plan and manage priorities at the sub-county and national level
  • Allocate finances and resources to programs
  • Enhance (promote) inter-sectorial collaboration between government and NGOs
  • Promote community involvement
  • Develop human resource

üìö For the system to be efficient

For the system to be efficient the flow of information must be regular and continuous.

Let us now look in details at the different health information system;

There are three different information within the health information system. These are;

  1. Inpatient health information system
  2. Out-patient health information system
  3. Administrative / management health information system

üìö A. Inpatient health information system

The in-patient health information system collects information on curative services from all patients stay at a health facility, upon discharge, the health records personnel collects all patient records and calculate the following hospital statistics

  • ‚úì Bed occupancy (BO)
  • Average length of stay (ALS)
  • Turn over interval (TOI)
  • Hospital bed population ratio
  • Hospital death rate (D&D)

These statistics are used for monitoring the operational efficiency of the health facility and may be important for measuring the impact of cost-sharing activities in the sub-county

üìö b. Out-patient information system

The out-patient health information system collect information on preventive and curative health services from the following vertical programs

  • Kenya expanded program in immunization (KEPI)
  • Control of diarrhea diseases (CDD)
  • Acquired Immune Deficiency Syndrome (AIDS)
  • Sexually transmitted disease (STD)
  • Laboratory services
  • Maternal & child health and family planning (MCH/FP)

Note: Each of these vertical programs has its own health information system. Each information system gives different health statistics e.g. from KEPI we can get immunization coverage and the number of anti-natal mothers who have received tetanus toxoid

üìö Key Takeaways

  • Data flow in HIS is bidirectional, from bottom-up for reporting and top-down for feedback and policy implementation.
  • An efficient HIS requires regular and continuous information flow to facilitate decision-making and policy formulation.
  • There are three main types of information systems within HIS: Inpatient, Outpatient, and Administrative/Management.
  • Inpatient health information systems focus on curative services, calculating statistics like bed occupancy, length of stay, and death rates to monitor hospital efficiency.
  • Outpatient health information systems track preventive and curative services from vertical programs such as KEPI, CDD, AIDS, STD, Laboratory, and MCH/FP, each potentially having its own subsystem.
