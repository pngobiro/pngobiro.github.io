üìö CONCEPTS OF HEALTH INFORMATION

  1. Health information provides an orderly approach to the production of information from all possible sources that have a bearing on health. The use of this information leads to more efficient management of health services.
  2. A health information system should aim at providing adequate and timely information for the determination of health services, planning, management and evaluation of health services and programs

üìö Demographic data

Refers to the study of the population including such factors as size, structure and distribution.

  • Size - refers to the number of persons in the population
  • Structure ‚Äì is the distribution of the population among its sex and age grouping?
  • Distribution ‚Äì refers to the arrangement of the population in space at a given time i.e. geographically or among various residential areas or occupations.

Population is defined as a number of people living in a given area in a given time

Population is dynamic, it is always changing. The growth or the decline of population is a function of fertility, mortality and migration. The Kenya's population growth rate is high because of the high fertility and decline in mortality.

üìö Key Takeaways

  • Health information systems offer a structured method for producing health-related information from diverse sources, enhancing health service management.
  • The goal of HIS is to deliver sufficient and timely information for effective health service delivery, planning, management, and evaluation.
  • Demographic data encompasses population size, structure (sex and age distribution), and distribution (geographical arrangement or residential/occupational areas).
  • Population dynamics are influenced by fertility, mortality, and migration, with Kenya's high population growth rate attributed to high fertility and declining mortality.
