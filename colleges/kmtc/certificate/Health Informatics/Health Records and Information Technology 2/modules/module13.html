üìö c. Management information system

Like in the outpatient information system, there are a number of management information system, each of this gives different health statistics e.g. the personnel information system gives us an available manpower and how they are deployed. A transport information system tell us the number of vehicles in good working condition in a particular sub-county

We collect health administrative information in order to:

  • Assist in the administration and coordination of health services at the sub-county and national level
  • Assess whether health services are accomplishing their objectives and whether they are doing so in the most efficient way
  • Conduct research into problems of health and disease and their effects in then provision of health services
  • Provide background data that may be required from time to time by health agencies

üìö Key Takeaways

  • Common HIS reporting forms include Diagnostic index card (MoH 268), Discharge summary sheet (MoH 304), Monthly Activity Report (MoH 705), Laboratory report (MoH 706), Immunization Summary Sheet (MoH 710), Weekly Notification of Infectious disease (MoH 712), District outpatient morbidity report (MoH 719), and Workload services statistics (MoH 717).
  • Management information systems in HIS provide data for resource management, such as personnel and transport, to aid in administration, coordination, objective assessment, research, and providing background data to health agencies.
  • Common abbreviations in HIS include KEPI, UNICEF, CHANIS, AMREF, NASCOP, WHO, UNDP, USAID, UNEP, CDD, and UNESCO, each representing key programs or organizations in health and development.
