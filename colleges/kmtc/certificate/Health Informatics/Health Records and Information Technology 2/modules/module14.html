  • Explain the uses of outpatient attendance reports at the institutional level.
  • Describe the applications of immunization summary reports.
  • Understand the utility of outpatient morbidity reports.

üìö USES OF INFORMATION GENERATED AT INSTITUTIONAL LEVEL

Health information generated at institutional level has numerous uses, following are some of the uses;

üìö 1. Outpatient attendance report

The information of outpatient attendance which is on daily, monthly and annual basis can be used for the following uses

  • Finding outpatient coverage workload
  • Planning out for waiting and operation areas
  • Planning for man power needed
  • Evaluating the service rendered
  • Estimating the amount of stationary and supply required to run or give services

üìö 2. Immunization summary report

  • To evaluate immunization coverage
  • Estimate amount of vaccine required in a particular facility
  • Planning for storage facilities
  • Evaluate immunization dropout rate

üìö 3. Outpatient morbidity report

  • This help to identify the main health problems of a catchment area ( the area to be covered)
  • Assist in deciding the amount of various suppliers and drugs to be purchased depending on a disease pattern
  • Assist in planning on the preventive measures to be taken in case of an epidemic
  • Help us to study or run the trend of the common diseases noting the prevalence and incidence cases

üìö 4. Growth monitoring report

  • With the knowledge of nutritional status of the community, it will be possible for the government and non-government organizations e.g. UNICEF to consider on nutrition improvement
  • Having reports of the children needing follow up i.e. those with kwashiorkor, marasmus etc. then it will be possible for the nutrition department to see to it
  • Decide on what health information to be given to the community

üìö Key Takeaways

  • Outpatient attendance reports are used to understand workload, plan service areas, manage manpower, evaluate services, and estimate supply needs.
  • Immunization summary reports help evaluate coverage, estimate vaccine needs, plan storage, and assess dropout rates.
  • Outpatient morbidity reports identify main health problems in a catchment area, inform drug procurement based on disease patterns, and aid in epidemic preparedness and trend analysis.
  • Growth monitoring reports provide nutritional status insights, help in follow-up for malnourished children, and guide community health information strategies, potentially involving organizations like UNICEF.
