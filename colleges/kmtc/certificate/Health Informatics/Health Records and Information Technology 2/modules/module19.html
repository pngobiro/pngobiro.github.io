Module 19: Concepts of Health Information Part 2 - Types and Recording Points

üéØ Learning Objectives

  • Describe Health status information and its components.
  • Explain Health service information and its components.
  • Understand data recording points at Dispensaries/health posts.
  • Understand data recording points at Health centers.
  • Understand data recording points at Hospitals.
  • Understand data recording points at Private hospitals.

üìö Types of health information (continued)

  1. Health status information

    Which includes the information on the health of population such as births and deaths, incidence or prevalence of disease and the characteristics of the individuals who server from such diseases. With this information, the health of the population can be monitored by observing whether the health status is improving or deteriorating over time

  2. Health service information

    Which includes information about the quality and quantity of health services provided and utilized as well as the status of various support services and programs

    The proportion of the population covered with essential health services can be determined by using this information.

üìö Data recording points

The amount and type of health information collected at each health facility will depend on the level of services provided and qualifications of personnel staffing such facilities. In general the more basic the facility the more limited the amount and complexity of information required to be collected and reported.

üìö Dispensaries / health posts

Health posts are the most peripheral units operated by the services. Basic outpatient and preventive services are provided. Inpatient and maternity services are not available at these facilities

üìö Health centers

These facilities provide in addition (to outpatient) maternal services as with health posts. Data on all outpatient services, notifiable diseases, preventive health activities and births and deaths will be collected and reported. In addition, records on all inpatient services must be collected and reported.

üìö Hospitals

Sub-County hospitals offer a range of outpatient and inpatient services as well as preventive services. Such facilities are responsible for reporting on those services. Referral hospitals are basically responsible for tertiary care and may not provide certain primary care services.

üìö Private hospitals

Private hospitals, clinics and individual medical practitioners and also mission hospitals or non- government organization should submit reports as required to the ministry of health.

üìö Key Takeaways

  • Health status information includes data on births, deaths, disease incidence/prevalence, and patient characteristics, used to monitor population health trends.
  • Health service information covers the quality and quantity of health services, utilization rates, and the status of support services, helping determine service coverage.
  • Data recording at health facilities varies by service level; basic facilities like dispensaries/health posts collect limited outpatient and preventive data.
  • Health centers, in addition to dispensary services, also collect data on maternal services, notifiable diseases, births, deaths, and inpatient services.
  • Hospitals, including sub-county and referral hospitals, report on a broad range of outpatient, inpatient, and preventive services, with referral hospitals focusing on tertiary care.
  • Private and mission hospitals, clinics, and individual practitioners are also required to submit reports to the ministry of health.
