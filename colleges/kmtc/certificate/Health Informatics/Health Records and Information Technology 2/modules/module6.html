Step A. DBR ‚Äì daily bed return (MoH 328)

This is a report compiled daily for the inpatient in a hospital ward. It includes the following information (continued).

üìö E. Physical count ward ‚Äì return figures

  1. Previous days figures
    • Patients number (number of patients previous day)
    • Accommodated
    • Vacant beds
    • totals
  2. Today's figures
    • Patients number
    • Accommodated
    • Vacant beds
    • totals

üìö F. Records office use only (balancing return)

  • Previous figures of patients
  • Admissions
  • From parole/ abscondees
  • Discharges /to parole/abscondees

üìö Step B. ward monthly summary sheet

This summary sheet reflects the daily statistics per day for each ward, it gives the monthly totals for each and every ward e.g. total discharges, admission, parole, transfers in etc.

The data are compiled from the wards DBR's for each day

üìö Step C facility inpatient administrative statistics (Bed Utilization)

This summary sheet reflects the bed utilization data for all the wards in the facility. It shows the totals for the monthly bed utilization figures per ward. Therefore the monthly summary sheet feeds the facility in-patient bed utilization with the required data. From this summary, therefore the following statistics can be done.

üìö Key Takeaways

  • The Physical count ward section of the DBR records patient numbers from the previous and current day, including accommodated and vacant beds.
  • The Records office use only section is for balancing returns and includes previous patient figures, admissions, parole/absconder movements, and discharges.
  • Ward monthly summary sheets compile daily statistics from DBRs to provide monthly totals for discharges, admissions, parole, and transfers per ward.
  • Facility inpatient bed utilization statistics summarize bed utilization data across all wards monthly, derived from ward summary sheets, to enable further statistical analysis.
  • The Daily Bed Return form structure includes sections for patient movement to/from hospital, within hospital, on leave, and self-discharge, along with physical bed counts and records office balancing.
