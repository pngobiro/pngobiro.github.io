Module 1: Introduction to Health Information Systems

üéØ Learning Objectives

  • Define Health Information System (HIS) and related terms.
  • Understand the summary and objectives of HIS.
  • Identify the goals of implementing a Health Information System.

üìö HEALTH INFORMATION SYSTEM

DEFINATION: THERE ARE MANY DEFINATIONS OF HIS

  1. Health information has been defined as, information about people's health and how the community, government and non-government organizations use the information to plan and provide health care services
  2. Health care information describes the incidence, prevalence and cause of major diseases. It also tells us about the availability and effectiveness of curative services. e.g. the hospital activity analysis can tell us how effective curative services are by looking at the average length of stay and the status of the patient when being discharged
  3. It refers to organized and interpreted data about health status, resources, activities and events as related to health.
  4. H.I.S is also being defined as a system that collects and represents health information that is responsible for the creation and maintenance of a data base.

üìö Summary of H.I.S

H.I.S is information about people's health, and what the community, government and non- government are doing about it. It describes the incidence and prevalence and cause of major diseases as well as the availability and effectiveness of curative services.

üìö Objectives

A good and effective health information system should;

  1. Provide qualitative and quantitative data which is essential for identifying major health problems.
  2. It should generate information which can be used to evaluate health policies for planning health programs and efficient management of health services
  3. It should help us open up a continuous dialogue between those who collect and the users of information with aim of improving health services.
  4. Help us disseminate (provide) timely information to users including those who work in research and those who work in health administration.
  5. It should provide regular feedback to all personnel in the information system which helps to emphasize the important role each of them plays.
  6. Create and maintain a health information database

üìö The Goals of H.I.S

The ultimate goal is to develop an accessible and comprehensive health information system which is flexible to the needs of the patients, health care providers, health managers, evaluators and other allied health personnel.

üìö Key Takeaways

  • Health Information System (HIS) is broadly defined as information concerning people's health and its utilization by various organizations.
  • HIS is crucial for understanding disease patterns, service effectiveness, and health resource management.
  • Objectives of HIS include providing data for problem identification, policy evaluation, continuous dialogue, timely information dissemination, feedback, and database maintenance.
  • The primary goal of HIS is to create a flexible, accessible, and comprehensive system catering to all stakeholders in healthcare.
