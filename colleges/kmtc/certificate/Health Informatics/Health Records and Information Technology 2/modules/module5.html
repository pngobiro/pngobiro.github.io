üìö 8. In patient administrative statistics

Step A. DBR ‚Äì daily bed return (MoH 328)

This is a report compiled daily for the inpatient in a hospital ward. It includes the following information.

üìö A. Patient movement to and from the hospital that is

  1. Admissions
  2. Discharges and deaths

üìö B. Patient movement within the hospital i.e.

  1. Transfer in
  2. Transfer out

üìö C. Patients in a hospital leave (Parole) i.e.

  1. From leave (have reported back to the hospital)
  2. To leave (have gone for leave)

This applies to psychiatric cases

üìö D. Patients on self-discharge (absconders)

This section records patients who discharge themselves from the hospital without formal approval.

üìö Key Takeaways

  • Inpatient administrative statistics are collected through the Daily Bed Return (DBR) form (MoH 328).
  • DBR form tracks patient movement into and out of the hospital, including admissions, discharges, and deaths.
  • It also records patient transfers within the hospital wards.
  • DBR includes information on patients granted hospital leave (parole), particularly relevant in psychiatric care.
  • Self-discharges (absconders) are also documented in the DBR to account for all patient departures.
