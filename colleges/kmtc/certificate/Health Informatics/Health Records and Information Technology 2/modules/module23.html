  • Understand the importance of interpretation in health information analysis.

  • Explain "Coverage" as a form of interpretation and its application.
  • Define "Utilization" in the context of health service interpretation.

üìö INTERPRETATION OF ANALYSIS

With good set of indicators available, the next step in the analysis process can take place. Indicators represent numerical values which help provide insight into quality. They do not however tell what these numerical values mean

To understand the meaning and significance of indicators, professional management engage in an interpretation of those indicators. There are several methods of interpretation of health indicators, but the basic concept is to compare the indicator with some other pieces of information. Such comparison may be between the indicator and the population, geography, personnel or the resources.

Any description of interpretation is likely to seem incomplete, this is because interpretation is based on an analyzed thought process which is not mechanical procedures. It is a creative process in which the health professional must pull together a diverse array of information in trying to explain or make sense out of certain events. The health professional must always be interposed between information and action.

Interpretation is a creative process with judgement made as to the relationship between various elements of the situation. It is the creative process that forms the challenge that health professional face.

Some forms of interpretation include;

  • Trends
  • Coverage
  • Utilization

üìö Trend

To determine the trend of an indicator, it is necessary to look at where there is a change in the value of that indicator overtime. Generally the most important trends involves the determination of patterns of change. The significance of trend is that they can be observed repeatedly. They provide predictive value, i.e. they can be used to give advance notice that a set of events is likely to occur. This help to give an early warning so that recourses can be prepared to meet the problem or opportunities that those events may present.

üìö Coverage

Coverage refers to the proportion of the population which is actively reached by a particular service provided by the health information. e.g. "coverage of antenatal care services‚Äù means the % of pregnant women in the population who actively attend ANC and receive such services.

üìö Utilization

Utilization is useful in comparing the level of effort of different facility.

Utilization is an important interpretation of information when making decision about upgrading facilities. Health posts may be recommended for upgrading to a health Centre if it can be shown that utilization of the existing health post is large enough to justify its upgrading. Some health Centre's can be recommended for down grading to a health post if their utilization is too small to justify a health Centre.

üìö Key Takeaways

  • Interpretation is a crucial, creative, and judgement-based process in health information analysis, essential for understanding indicator values and guiding action.
  • Trend analysis involves examining changes in indicator values over time to identify patterns, providing predictive insights for proactive health management.
  • Coverage interpretation measures the proportion of a population reached by specific health services, such as antenatal care, indicating service accessibility and reach.
  • Utilization, when interpreted, is used to compare service effort across different facilities and to inform decisions on upgrading or downgrading health facilities based on service usage.
