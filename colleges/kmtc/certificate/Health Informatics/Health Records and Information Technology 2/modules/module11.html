Module 11: Systems Within HIS - Unit, Sub-County, and National Levels

üéØ Learning Objectives

  • Understand the different levels within a health information system.
  • Describe the data flow and reporting mechanisms at the Unit Level.
  • Explain the role of the Sub-county level in HIS.
  • Detail the functions of the National level in the health information system.

üìö Systems within health information system.

There are various levels through which health information flows;

üìö a. Unit Level

All the data collected by the staff at dispensary as well as data from missions and private hospitals which are non-governmental organizations is submitted to the district information office for further processing. At this level, the following reports are supposed to be sent every month to the sub-county head quarters

  1. Outpatient morbidity report MoH 705
  2. Family planning report form MCH/FP
  3. Laboratory report MoH 706
  4. Immunization report MoH 710
  5. Hospital activity report MoH 105
  6. Facility workload report MoH 717

üìö b. Sub-county level

The sub-county information office receives report from all the units and enters the information in to the KHIS (Kenya health information system) previously known as DHIS. The keyed in data is therefore processed and analyzed at the sub-county level. This analyzed report is used by the sub-county health management team (ScHMT)

üìö c. The National level

The health information system at the national headquarters receives summarized reports from all the sub-counties. Here, they are processed, analyzed and interpreted. At this level a feedback is written and sent back to the sub-counties. Copies of such reports may also be sent to the international bodies such as WHO, UN, Children emergency fund (UNCEF), UN developmental programs as well as donor agencies to justify expenditure and identify future funding.

üìö Key Takeaways

  • Health information systems operate at multiple levels: Unit, Sub-county, and National.
  • At the Unit Level (dispensaries, hospitals), data is collected and monthly reports are generated for sub-county headquarters.
  • Reports sent from Unit Level include Outpatient morbidity (MoH 705), Family planning (MCH/FP), Laboratory (MoH 706), Immunization (MoH 710), Hospital activity (MoH 105), and Facility workload (MoH 717) reports.
  • The Sub-county level information office compiles unit-level reports into KHIS/DHIS, processes and analyzes the data for use by the Sub-county Health Management Team (ScHMT).
  • The National level headquarters receives summarized reports from sub-counties, conducts further analysis, provides feedback, and disseminates reports to international health organizations and donor agencies.
