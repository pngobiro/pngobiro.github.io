üìö ORGANIZATION OF THE MINISTRY OF HEALTH (MoH)

Duties and Responsibilities

The Cabinet Secretary is the political head in the ministry of heal. His/her duties and responsibilities among others involves;

  • Overseeing or monitoring the ministry services
  • Implementation of government policies in regard to health matters e.g. NHIF services, donor services etc.
  • Coming up with development matters e.g. infrastructure ‚Äì building of hospital wards etc.
  • Coordinating the ministry of health with other ministries e.g. finance for ministerial funds, education of training etc.
  • Procurement of drugs under the laid down guidelines and also coordinate the distribution of drugs etc.
  • Coordinate employment & staff training

üìö PERMANENT SECRETARY (PS)

He is the secretary in the MoH, his duties and responsibilities among others involve;

The minister can delegate his duties to the PS therefore he:

  • Oversees or monitors the ministry services
  • Implementation of government policies
  • Staff employment and deployment
  • Procurement of drugs and equipment etc.
  • Maintains the discipline of the staff
  • Under the ministry of health, the PS oversees the curative services offered from all levels i.e. National, Provincial, District, Sub district, Health Centres and Dispensaries
  • Under ministry of public health services, the PS monitors the preventive and promotive health services. He mostly deals with community health and primary health care.

üìö Key Takeaways

  • The Cabinet Secretary is the political head of the Ministry of Health (MoH), responsible for overseeing ministry services and policy implementation.
  • Key duties of the Cabinet Secretary include monitoring ministry services, implementing health policies (e.g., NHIF, donor services), infrastructure development, inter-ministerial coordination, drug procurement and distribution, and staff training.
  • The Permanent Secretary (PS) is the administrative head, delegated duties by the minister, and is responsible for the day-to-day operations of the MoH.
  • The PS's responsibilities include overseeing ministry services, policy implementation, staff management, procurement, maintaining discipline, and specifically overseeing both curative and preventive/promotive health services across all levels of care.
