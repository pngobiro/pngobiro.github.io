üìö Terminology

  1. Bed compliment ‚Äì the number of beds permanently allocated in a hospital for treatment of in patients
  2. Bed state ‚Äì the number of patients occupying hospital beds at any given time
  3. Stuffed beds allocated ‚Äì the number of available stuffed beds and temporarily un- available stuffed beds
  4. Bed turn over/ turn over per bed/ through put per bed ‚Äì this means the average number of patients expected to be treated per bed
  5. Turn over interval ‚Äì the average number of days beds lie vacant between successive patients

üìö Key Takeaways

  • The DAILY BED RETURN form is divided into sections for patient self-discharge, physical count, and records office use.
  • The self-discharge section records patients who returned after self-discharge and those who self-discharged.
  • The physical count section compares patient and bed statistics between the previous day and the current day.
  • The records office section is used for balancing the daily return and is verified by nursing and records staff.
  • Key terminology includes: Bed compliment (permanent beds), Bed state (occupied beds at a point), Stuffed beds allocated (available and temporarily unavailable beds), Bed turnover (average patients per bed), and Turnover interval (average vacancy days between patients).
