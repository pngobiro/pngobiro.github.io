üìö 8. Notification of infectious diseases (disease surveillance)

  • The information help us in controlling and preventing the spread of an outbreak within a specific area
  • Calls for program intervention in case of an outbreak e.g. measles campaign by KEPI

üìö 9. Facility workload report

  • The information helps us in learning of the general utilization of a facility
  • Helps the ministry to plan for the funding needed for each facility
  • Helps the ministry to plan for drugs needed for each facility
  • Helps the ministry plan staffing needed by each facility (employment and deployment)
  • Helps the ministry plan for transport availability for each facility
  • Helps the facility plan for stationary equipment and instruments needed by each facility
  • Gives a clear picture of the facility utilization and its effectiveness to the service of the community in the catchment area
  • Helps the ministry plan for the expansion of the facility and therefore the entire management of the facility on it.

üìö 10. Monthly laboratory report

  • This information is used in estimating the amount of reagents and other equipment and instruments needed to run the service effectively and effectively
  • Decide on staff training and specialization
  • Personnel utilization (man power)
  • Research on diseases
  • Investigating the epidemic disease

üìö SOURCES OF HEALTH INFORMATION

There are a number of sources of health information. These include data collected from;

  1. Outpatient records
  2. Hospital inpatient records

In and outpatient records

These are varied types of health data that can be collected from both in and outpatient as discussed previously (refer to the notes)

üìö Key Takeaways

  • Notification of infectious diseases is crucial for disease surveillance, enabling outbreak control, prevention, and triggering program interventions like KEPI measles campaigns.
  • Facility workload reports provide insights into facility utilization, aiding ministry-level planning for funding, drug supply, staffing, transport, equipment, and facility expansion, as well as assessing service effectiveness.
  • Monthly laboratory reports assist in estimating reagent and equipment needs, staff training and specialization decisions, personnel utilization assessments, disease research, and epidemic investigations.
  • Outpatient and inpatient records serve as primary sources of diverse health data, collected and utilized as detailed in previous modules.
