üìö Various forms (indicators) used in generating health information

Secondary data is one that had been used in an investigation but originally collected by someone else. Therefore if someone else uses the data becomes a secondary user. Primary data in the hands of one person becomes a secondary in another users.

Various forms (indicators) used in generating health information:

üìö 1. Out-patient attendance summary sheet.

This gives a summary of patient attendance, there is pediatrics summary i.e. below 5 years of age and a summary of all those above 5 years of age. It is reported as new clients and revisits. It is further broken down according to sex i.e. male or female. The information is usually compiled on daily or monthly basis

üìö 2. Out-patient tally sheet ‚Äì MoH 701

This is a summary sheet containing all the diagnosis and normally completed or tallied by the clinicians attending the clients. The tally sheet is tallied in regard to diagnosis of each particular client. They are compiled on daily basis.

üìö 3. Facility monthly out-patient activity report ‚Äì MoH 705

This is a summary sheet containing all the diagnosis information generated by tally sheet is fed to monthly activity summary sheet. It's completed on daily basis to give a monthly report by the end of the month

üìö 4. Immunization and vitamin a tally sheet ‚Äì MoH ‚Äì 702

This is a tally sheet of all the vaccines and vitamin A given in a day

The different types of vaccines include;

  1. BCG ‚Äì T.B vaccine
  2. OPV/IPV ‚Äì polio vaccine
  3. PENTAVALENT -
    1. Tetanus toxoid
    2. Diphtheria
    3. Whooping cough
    4. Haemophilus influenza
    5. Hepatitis B
  4. T.T - Tetanus toxoid for women of child bearing age
  5. Vitamin A ‚Äì
    1. For children under one year
    2. For children above one year
    3. For lactating mothers (breast feeding mothers)

üìö Key Takeaways

  • Out-patient attendance summary sheets provide an overview of patient visits, categorized by age and gender, compiled daily or monthly.
  • Out-patient tally sheets (MoH 701) summarize diagnoses made by clinicians, tallied daily per client.
  • Facility monthly out-patient activity reports (MoH 705) consolidate diagnosis data from tally sheets into monthly summaries.
  • Immunization and vitamin A tally sheets (MoH 702) track daily administration of vaccines and vitamin A, including BCG, Polio, Pentavalent, TT, and Vitamin A supplements for different age groups and mothers.
