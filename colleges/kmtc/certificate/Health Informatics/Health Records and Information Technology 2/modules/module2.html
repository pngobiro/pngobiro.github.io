Module 2: History, Personnel, and Uses of HIS

üéØ Learning Objectives

  • Outline the history and development of Health Information Systems in Kenya.
  • Identify the personnel involved in a typical health information system.
  • Describe the flow of health information within a system.
  • Explain the various uses of a Health Information System.
  • Understand how data is collected and the different types of data in HIS.

üìö History of H.I.S

In 1972 a committee including representatives from the ministry of health, the central bureau of statistics and the attorney general chamber was formed. The task of this committee was to design a health information system for Kenya.

A pilot project was designed and tested in 3 districts, Mombasa which represented an urban, Kwale which represented peri-urban district and Kitui which represented rural district. The pilot project was completed in 1976 and the following suggestions and recommendations were adopted. One of the recommendations was to merge two units, the research and evaluation unit of the division of family health and vital and health statistics units of the division of communicable disease control into one entity the Health Information System.

Another recommendation was to develop a system of code numbers for the reporting forms

How were the code numbers of the reporting forms established?

In 1982, another committee of professionals was formed to investigate on the accuracy and efficiency of all health data collection forms. The committee suggested a number of changes and as a result, all officially approved forms were changed from MED to MOH and were accompanied by a corresponding code numbers.

In 1984 the national policy on districts focus for rural development was initiated in response to this new development. The ministry of health decentralizing health information system offices in all districts would be processed.

üìö The personnel involving health information system

There are various individuals and field staff who are involved in a typical health information system. These include;

  • Families and communities
  • Field health workers such as public health technicians
  • Clinical staff such as doctors and nurses
  • District health managers
  • Health records and information personnel

Information system work well if these people carry out their responsibilities and coordinate their activities such as data collection and use. The people who work in the system at each level are responsible for the completeness and accuracy of either the data they collect or the reports they submit.

üìö Information flow

Health information originates with individual clients, and communities, it passes through various levels of the system to reach national managers, evaluators and planners.

This information should flow back in an aggregate and interpreted form to individual health workers.

üìö Uses of Health Information System

H.I is one of the most essential components of health delivery system. It has numerous uses including:-

  1. Parents use height and weight information to monitor nutritional status of their children.
  2. Community groups use health information to identify and control environmental hazards
  3. Health workers use such information to identify and screen persons who need treatment
  4. Clinical staff use patients history to diagnose and treat individual health problems
  5. Managers use information to maximize program effectiveness and efficiency
  6. Administrators use information to identify health problems and audit the use of program funds and other resources to justify expenditure and future funding.
  7. Epidemic control units use information for disease surveillance
  8. Donor agencies use information to compare alternative methods of health care delivery and assess program effectiveness
  9. International agencies use health information to monitor progress
  10. Health workers use health information system to identify persons needing treatment and follow up those who miss appointments and seek out those who may be candidates to special services
  11. Health information system help to evaluate the impact or input of programs of health
  12. Health information helps the MoH to justify man power training and assess if there is need to train more workers or employ more
  13. Research purposes ‚Äì research is very vital function in health institutions. Analysis and conclusions of proper research can come up with recommendations which can be a guide to any changes concerning the ministry, health facilities or even the community involved

Two principals need to be emphasized about health information collection and use

These are:

  • Data flows in systems and is made up of individuals and departments. If one unit is weak, the entire system suffers.
  • Data collection is closely related to data use. Any improvement in the collection of data is not enough without corresponding improvement in its use. Managers who use data are more likely to encourage its collection. Health information is a vital resource for health planning, implementation and evaluation.
  • Data users include everyone in health delivery system ranging from individual parents and patients to national planners and international donor agencies. Uses of data likewise range from monitoring of individual health and nutritional programs, evaluation and long term policy development. It is absolutely essential to be selective and identify priority areas for data collection, since data collection have limited capacity and data users have limited interest.

üìö How data is collected in H.I.S

HIS collects data from all possible sources that have a bearing on health in a systematic way. The data is processed and turned into information that is used to plan and manage health services efficiently. HIS therefore aims at providing adequate and timely information for the determination of health policies and evaluation of health services and programs

The H.I.S should give:

  1. Good quality and quantity data
  2. Essential data for identifying agent health program
  3. Generate good health information for evaluation of health policies for effective management to health services
  4. Open up a continuous dialogue between the users and producers
  5. Disseminate timely information
  6. Give regular feedback to the producers
  7. Create and maintain database

üìö Types of data

There are two types of data;

  1. Primary data
  2. Secondary data

Primary data is data that has been collected by an investigator himself for a certain objective and is therefore original in character e.g. POPULATION CENSUS collected by central bureau of statistics.

üìö Key Takeaways

  • The development of HIS in Kenya started in 1972 with committee formations and pilot projects.
  • Key personnel in HIS range from community members to health professionals and managers.
  • Information flow is bidirectional, moving from individuals to national levels and back.
  • HIS has diverse uses, including monitoring nutritional status, disease surveillance, program evaluation, and resource justification.
  • Data collection in HIS is systematic, aiming for quality and quantity, and includes both primary and secondary data.
