üìã Birth Planning

A birth plan is an outline of your preferences during your labor and delivery. It gives the expectant mother a 'voice' for her body and a say in the decisions that are made. Birth plans should be discussed well in advance of giving birth.

Components of a Birth Plan

  • Environment (Music, lighting)

Positions: Lying, kneeling, standing, squatting, use of birthing ball/stool.

‚ñ∂ Delivery & After Care
  • Assisted Delivery: Preferences on forceps/vacuum.

Flexibility is Key

The most important thing to remember about a birth plan is that births do not always go to plan! Unexpected complications may arise. Be open to last-minute changes; the health of mother and baby is paramount.

ü§∞ Safe Motherhood Initiative

Safe motherhood encompasses initiatives ensuring women receive high-quality care to achieve optimum health for mother and infant. The goal is to reduce maternal and neonatal morbidity and mortality.

Pillars of Safe Motherhood

1. Family Planning

Ensures individuals can plan timing and spacing of pregnancies.

2. Antenatal Care (ANC)

Early detection of complications, supplementation, and vaccination.

3. Obstetric & Newborn Care

Clean and safe delivery, emergency care for high-risk cases.

4. Postnatal Care (PNC)

Care for mother and baby after delivery.

5. Post-abortion Care

Emergency treatment of complications and counseling.

6. STD/HIV Control

Screening and PMTCT (Prevention of Mother-to-Child Transmission).

ü§± Postnatal Care (PNC)

PNC is the care given to the mother and newborn from one hour after delivery up to 6 weeks post-delivery.

Objectives

  • Assess health status of mother and treat defects (e.g., anemia, hypertension).
  • Ascertain outcome of pregnancy and survival of newborn.
  • Promote breastfeeding and physical well-being.
  • Provide Family Planning guidance.
  • Immunization schedule for the infant.

Components of Care

  • History: Check for complications (hemorrhage, fever, pain), lochia characteristics (Rubra ‚Üí Serosa ‚Üí Alba).
  • Physical Exam (Mother): BP, Temp, Abdomen (involution), Perineum (tears/episiotomy), Breasts, Legs (thrombosis).
  • Physical Exam (Newborn): Vital signs, cord stump, suckling, color (cyanosis/jaundice).
  • Treatment: Vitamin A (200,000 IU), Iron supplementation.

ü©∫ Abortion Management

Definition: Termination of pregnancy by any means, resulting in expulsion of an immature nonviable fetus of less than 28 weeks.

Classification

  • Threatened abortion
  • Inevitable abortion
  • Incomplete abortion
  • Complete abortion
  • Missed abortion
  • Septic abortion
  • Criminal/Induced abortion

Causes

  • Chromosomal (70%): Genetic errors during gametogenesis.
  • Infections (15%): Viral or bacterial.
  • Cervical Incompetence: Often causes 2nd trimester abortions.
  • Metabolic: Diabetes, Hypertension, Renal disease.
üè•

General Management

  • Admit for observation and treatment.
  • Monitor vital signs and blood loss.
  • IV fluids (Normal Saline/Ringers Lactate) if bleeding severely.
  • MVA (Manual Vacuum Aspiration): Method of choice for incomplete/inevitable abortion (< 14 weeks).
  • Antibiotics for septic cases.
  • Post-abortion family planning counseling.
