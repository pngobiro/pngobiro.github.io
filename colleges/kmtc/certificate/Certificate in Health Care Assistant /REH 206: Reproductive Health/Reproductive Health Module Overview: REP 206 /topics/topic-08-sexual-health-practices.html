Pelvic Inflammatory Disease (PID)

Usually a consequence of STIs (beginning as vulvovaginitis) or introduced during procedures/childbirth. Infection spreads upwards to cervix, uterus, tubes, and ovaries.

Complications of PID

  • Infertility (due to tube obstruction).
  • Peritonitis.
  • Ectopic Pregnancy.
  • Bacteremia (can lead to meningitis/endocarditis).

Structural & Tumor Disorders

‚ñ∂ Uterine Disorders

Endometriosis: Growth of endometrial tissue outside the uterus. Causes "chocolate cysts" in ovaries, pain, adhesions, and infertility.

Leiomyoma (Fibroids): Benign smooth muscle tumors. Can cause menorrhagia, pain, and reduced fertility.

Endometrial Carcinoma: Malignancy often associated with high estrogen levels (obesity, nulliparity). Presents with bleeding.

‚ñ∂ Cervical Carcinoma

Strongly associated with Human Papillomavirus (HPV). Develops over 15-20 years from dysplasia (CIN). Screening allows early detection. Risk factors include multiple partners and early sexual onset.

‚ñ∂ Breast Disorders

Mastitis: Inflammation often during lactation (caused by Staphylococcus aureus).

Tumors: Most are benign (e.g., Fibroadenoma). Malignant tumors often present as painless lumps with skin retraction/ulceration.

‚ôÇÔ∏è Diseases of Male Reproductive System

Infections & Swellings

  • Urethritis: Often Gonococcal or non-specific. Can cause strictures.
  • Orchitis: Inflammation of testis. Mumps virus is a common cause; can lead to sterility.
  • Hydrocele: Accumulation of fluid in the tunica vaginalis. Common scrotal swelling.
  • Cryptorchidism: Undescended testis. Risk of infertility and cancer.

Prostate Disorders

Benign Prostatic Hyperplasia (BPH)

Common in men over 50. Nodules obstruct urethra causing urinary retention and infection.

Prostate Cancer

Common cause of death. Often metastasizes to bone (lumbar vertebrae) causing bone formation.

Sexual Dysfunction

Problems preventing satisfaction from sexual activity. Can be physical (diabetes, heart disease) or psychological (stress, guilt, trauma).

Male Dysfunction Female Dysfunction
Erectile Dysfunction (Impotence) Vaginismus (Muscle spasm)

Infertility

Definition

Male Factors

  • Defective spermatogenesis
  • Obstruction of deferent duct
  • Failure of erection/ejaculation
  • Endocrine disorders

Female Factors

  • Ovulation problems
  • Tubal blockage (PID)
  • Endometriosis
  • Anatomical abnormalities

Common Harmful Practices

Female Genital Mutilation (FGM)

Surgical removal of parts or all of female genitalia. Violates human rights. Causes severe health complications (bleeding, infection, obstructed labour, psychological trauma).

  • Nutritional Taboos: Restricting protein/energy foods during pregnancy (fear of large baby), leading to malnutrition.
  • Forced Feeding: Forcing young girls to feed to gain weight.
  • Early Marriage: Giving away young girls (11-13 yrs) for marriage/dowry. Leads to VVF and complications.
  • Dry Sex: Removal of vaginal fluid. Increases friction and HIV transmission risk.
  • Wife Inheritance: Practice of a widow being "inherited" by a relative.

Violence Against Women (VAW)

Any act of gender-based violence resulting in physical, sexual, or psychological harm.

Forms of Violence:

Physical

Spousal battering, non-spousal violence.

Sexual

Rape, marital rape, sexual abuse of children, trafficking.

Psychological

Harassment, intimidation, coercion.

Economic/Cultural

Dowry violence, FGM, deprivation of liberty.
