üõë Barrier Methods

Barrier methods prevent sperm from meeting the ovum. They are unique in that condoms (both male and female) provide dual protection against pregnancy and STIs/HIV.

‚ñ∂ Diaphragms & Spermicides

Fig. 6.3: Diaphragm

Diaphragm: Soft latex cup covering cervix. Must be used with spermicide. 83-84% effective. No STI protection.

Spermicides: Sperm-killing substances (e.g., Nonoxynol-9). Least effective (79%). Frequent use may increase risk of HIV infection due to irritation.

ü©π Implants (LARC)

Small plastic rods placed under the skin of the upper arm. They release progestin slowly. They are safe for breastfeeding mothers.

Fig. 6.4: Jadelle (2 rods) / Implanon (1 rod)

Fig. 6.5: Norplant (6 capsules)

Types & Duration

  • Jadelle: 2 rods, effective for 5 years.
  • Implanon: 1 rod, effective for 3 years.
  • Sino-Implant (Zarin): 2 rods, effective for 4 years.
  • Norplant: 6 capsules, labeled for 5 years.

‚úÇÔ∏è Permanent Methods

Intended to provide lifelong, permanent protection. Reversal is usually impossible or difficult.

Female Sterilization (Tubal Ligation)

Cutting or blocking the fallopian tubes. Eggs cannot meet sperm.

  • Effectiveness: Immediate (>99%).
  • Procedure: Minilaparotomy or Laparoscopy.
  • Benefits: No hormonal side effects. Reduces ovarian cancer risk.

Vasectomy (Male Sterilization)

Cutting or blocking the vas deferens. Sperm is kept out of semen.

  • Effectiveness: Delayed. Not effective for first 3 months.
  • Procedure: Minor surgery (scalpel or no-scalpel).
  • Myths: Does NOT affect sex drive, erection, or ejaculation fluid volume.

üåø Natural Family Planning

Fertility Awareness Methods

Based on identifying the fertile window (usually days 8-19) and avoiding unprotected sex during that time.

Standard Days & Cycle Beads

For women with cycles 26-32 days. Use beads to track: White beads = Fertile (unsafe).

Fig. 6.7: Cycle Beads

Calendar Rhythm Method

Calculated based on 6 months of cycle history. Subtract 18 from shortest cycle (start of fertile time) and 11 from longest (end).

Lactational Amenorrhea Method (LAM)

Temporary method based on breastfeeding. All three criteria must be met:

LAM Criteria

  1. Mother's monthly bleeding has not returned.
  2. Baby is fully or nearly fully breastfed (fed often, day and night).
  3. Baby is less than 6 months old.

Other Methods

  • Withdrawal (Coitus Interruptus): Man withdraws penis before ejaculation. Effectiveness depends on user control.
  • Abstinence: 100% effective. Only way to absolutely prevent pregnancy and STIs.

üó£Ô∏è Family Planning Counseling

Counseling helps clients make informed decisions. Use the GATHER acronym:

  • G - Greet: Welcome client, assure confidentiality.
  • A - Ask: Ask about needs, health history, concerns.
  • T - Tell: Tell about available methods and choices.
  • H - Help: Help them make an informed choice.
  • E - Explain: Explain how to use the chosen method (side effects, instructions).
  • R - Return: Schedule follow-up visit.
