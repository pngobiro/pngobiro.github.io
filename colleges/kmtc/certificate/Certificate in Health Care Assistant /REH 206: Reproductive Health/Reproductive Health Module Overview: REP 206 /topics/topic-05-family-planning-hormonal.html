üë®‚Äçüë©‚Äçüëß‚Äçüë¶ Introduction to Family Planning

Family planning involves planning the number, frequency, and timing of pregnancy. It allows individuals and couples to anticipate and attain their desired number of children and the spacing and timing of their births.

Purpose

  • Ensures resources (time, social, financial) are available for raising a child.
  • Improves the health of the mother and the child.
  • Helps prevent HIV/AIDS (barrier methods).

Criteria for Ideal Contraceptive

  • Safe for use (free from side effects).
  • Reliable.
  • Easy to administer and convenient.
  • Cost-effective.
  • Culturally feasible and acceptable.
02

üíä Combined Oral Contraceptives (COCs)

COCs are pills containing low doses of two hormones: estrogen and progestin (similar to natural progesterone). Examples include Microgynon or Femiplan.

Fig. 5.1: Combined Oral Contraceptive Pills

Mechanism of Action

Prevent release of egg (ovulation) from ovaries each month. Also thickens cervical mucus to block sperm.

Effectiveness

  • Typical Use: 92% effective (8 pregnancies per 100 women).
  • Perfect Use: >99% effective (less than 1 pregnancy per 100).
  • Return to Fertility: No delay when stopped.
üö´

Who Cannot Use COCs?

  • Women who are pregnant.
  • Breastfeeding mothers with babies less than 6 months old.
  • Smokers over 35 years of age.
  • History of blood clots (DVT/PE), heart attack, or stroke.
  • Liver disease (yellow eyes/jaundice).
  • Breast cancer.
üí°

Missed Pill Instructions

  • 1 or 2 pills missed: Take missed pill ASAP and continue daily.
  • 3+ pills missed (in a row): Use condoms/abstain for 7 days. Keep taking pills.
  • 3+ pills missed in Week 3: Skip placebo pills and start new pack immediately. Use backup protection for 7 days.

ü§± Progestin-Only Pills (POPs)

Also called "minipills". These contain very low doses of progestin only (no estrogen). They are safe and most effective for breastfeeding mothers.

Key Features

  • Mechanism: Thickens cervical mucus, thins uterine lining, may suppress ovulation.
  • Effectiveness (Breastfeeding): 99% effective (1 pregnancy per 100).
  • Effectiveness (Not Breastfeeding): 90-97% effective (3-10 pregnancies per 100).
  • Usage: Must be taken at the same time every day.

Late Pill Rules

  • Breastfeeding: Take as soon as remembered. Continue schedule.
  • Not Breastfeeding: If more than 3 hours late, use condoms for next 2 days and keep taking pills.

Advantages vs. Disadvantages

Advantages Disadvantages
Does not affect breast milk. Must be taken at same time daily.
Simple and convenient. Irregular bleeding is common.
Can be used by smokers/older women. No protection against STIs/HIV.
04

üö® Emergency Contraceptive Pills (ECPs)

Also called "morning after pills". Used to prevent pregnancy within 120 hours (5 days) after unprotected sex. The sooner they are taken, the more effective they are.

Mechanism

Works primarily by preventing or delaying the release of eggs from ovaries (ovulation). It does not disrupt an existing pregnancy (it is not an abortion pill).

When to Use

  • Condom broke or slipped.
  • Missed pills (3+ COCs or started pack late).
  • Late injection (more than 4 weeks late for DMPA).
  • Sexual assault.
  • Failure to withdraw before ejaculation.
‚ö†Ô∏è

Important Note

ECPs do not protect against future acts of sex. A woman can become pregnant immediately after taking ECPs if she has unprotected sex again. A regular method should be started immediately.

05

üíâ Injectable Contraceptives

Contains progestin (like natural progesterone). Given by intramuscular injection into the arm or buttocks.

Types

  • DMPA (Depo Provera): Given every 3 months (13 weeks).
  • NET-EN: Given every 2 months.

Effectiveness & Usage

97% effective (3 pregnancies per 100 women). Effectiveness depends on getting injections on time.

‚è≥

Return to Fertility

After stopping injections, there is a delay in return to fertility. It takes an average of about 4 months for DMPA users to become pregnant.

Bleeding Changes

Bleeding changes are common and safe:

  • First 3 months: Irregular or prolonged bleeding.
  • After 1 year: No monthly bleeding (amenorrhea) is very common.

Grace Period

  • DMPA: Can be given up to 4 weeks late without backup.
  • NET-EN: Can be given up to 2 weeks late without backup.
  • If later than this, use condoms and rule out pregnancy before injecting.
