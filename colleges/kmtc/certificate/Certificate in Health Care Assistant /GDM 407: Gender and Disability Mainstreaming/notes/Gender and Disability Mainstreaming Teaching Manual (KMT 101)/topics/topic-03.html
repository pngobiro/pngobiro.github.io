üìñ Understanding Disability

Disability is a physical, sensory, mental, or other impairment including visual, hearing or physical disability which has a substantial long term adverse effect on a person's ability to carry out usual day to day activities.

Any restriction or lack (resulting from impairment) ability to perform an activity in a manner or range considered normal for human beings.

üí° Key Concept

Disability is not just a medical condition but also a social issue. The interaction between people with impairments and attitudinal and environmental barriers hinders their full and effective participation in society on an equal basis with others.

üåê Disability Mainstreaming

Disability mainstreaming is the process of ensuring that disability perspectives and the needs of persons with disabilities are integrated into all aspects of policy development, program planning, implementation, and evaluation.

üéØ Goal of Disability Mainstreaming

To ensure that persons with disabilities have equal access to opportunities, services, and resources, and can participate fully in all aspects of life on an equal basis with others.

Key Principles

  • Nothing About Us Without Us: Persons with disabilities must be involved in decision-making processes that affect them
  • Universal Design: Creating environments, products, and services that are accessible to all people
  • Reasonable Accommodation: Making necessary and appropriate modifications to ensure equal participation
  • Inclusive Approach: Considering disability in all policies and programs from the outset

üõ§Ô∏è Strategies for Disability Inclusion in Community Health

Community Health Assistants can implement various strategies to ensure inclusion of persons with disabilities:

1

Accessibility Assessment

Regularly assess health facilities and services for accessibility and make necessary modifications

2

Disability Data Collection

Collect and maintain disaggregated data on persons with disabilities in the community

3

Capacity Building

Train health workers and CHVs on disability rights and inclusive service delivery

4

Community Sensitization

Raise awareness about disability rights and challenge negative stereotypes in the community

5

Partnership Development

Collaborate with disability organizations and support groups for better service delivery

6

Referral Systems

Establish strong referral pathways to specialized disability services and support

7

Home-Based Care

Provide home-based care for persons with severe disabilities who cannot access facilities

8

Assistive Devices

Facilitate access to and maintenance of assistive devices (wheelchairs, hearing aids, etc.)

Community Health Assistants play a crucial role in promoting disability inclusion:

Key Responsibilities

CHAs should actively work to ensure persons with disabilities receive equitable healthcare services and support.

1

Identification and Registration

Identify persons with disabilities in the community and ensure they are registered for appropriate services

2

Needs Assessment

Conduct regular needs assessments to understand the specific needs of persons with disabilities

3

Health Education

Provide tailored health education using accessible formats and communication methods

4

Home Visits

Conduct regular home visits to persons with disabilities who face mobility challenges

5

Advocacy

Advocate for the rights and needs of persons with disabilities at community and facility levels

6

Monitoring and Follow-up

Monitor health outcomes and provide ongoing support to persons with disabilities
