üåç Social Determinants of Mental Health

  • Economic Factors: Poverty, unemployment, and low income increase stress and mental health risks.
  • Education: Limited access to education reduces awareness of mental health issues.
  • Social Inclusion: Discrimination, stigma, and lack of social support contribute to poor mental health.
  • Environment: Poor housing, unsafe neighborhoods, and exposure to violence are risk factors.
  • Healthcare Access: Lack of affordable or accessible healthcare services leads to untreated mental health conditions.
  • Cultural Factors: Cultural stigma or traditional beliefs may prevent people from seeking help.
  • Family Dynamics: Family conflicts, neglect, or abuse contribute to mental health challenges.

üéØ Goals and Principles

Goals

  1. Improve access to mental health services for all individuals.
  2. Reduce stigma and discrimination related to mental health.
  3. Promote recovery and social reintegration of individuals with mental health issues.
  4. Enhance community awareness and understanding of mental health.
  5. Develop sustainable and culturally appropriate mental health services.

Principles

  1. Equity: Ensure services are accessible to all, regardless of socioeconomic status.
  2. Integration: Combine mental health services with general health care.
  3. Community Participation: Engage community members in mental health programs.
  4. Continuity of Care: Provide long-term support and follow-up for individuals.
  5. Empowerment: Empower individuals and communities to take control of their mental health.
