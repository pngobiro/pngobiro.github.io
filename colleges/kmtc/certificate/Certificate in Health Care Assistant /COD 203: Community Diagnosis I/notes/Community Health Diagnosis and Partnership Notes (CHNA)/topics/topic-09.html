üîç Identifying Collaboration Partners

Inter-sectoral collaboration (ISC) involves working across different sectors (health, education, agriculture, etc.) to achieve common goals through shared decision-making and resource coordination.

üí°

Why Inter-Sectoral?

Complex health issues like malnutrition or HIV/AIDS cannot be solved by the health sector alone. They require a multi-faceted approach involving education, social welfare, agriculture, and economic development.

6 Steps to Identify Partners

  1. 1
    Define Scope: Outline specific health/social issues, target population, and geographic area.
  2. 2
    Stakeholder Analysis: Identify those with an interest and map their influence/contributions.
  3. 3
    Identify Potential Sectors: Determine which sectors (Health, Education, Water, etc.) can contribute.
  4. 4
    Assess Capacity & Commitment: Evaluate resources, expertise, and willingness to collaborate.
  5. 5
    Build Relationships: Establish rapport and trust through open communication.
  6. 6
    Formalize Partnerships: Develop agreements outlining roles and shared objectives.
02

üìù Developing Terms of Reference (ToR)

ToR provide a clear framework for the project, outlining roles, responsibilities, and expectations. This document is essential for structured collaboration.

‚ñ∂ Standard ToR Structure
  • Introduction: Background, objectives, and expected outcomes.
  • Project Scope: Boundaries, geography, target population.
  • Objectives: SMART objectives and expected results.
  • Methodology: Data collection tools and analysis techniques.
  • Roles & Responsibilities: Defined roles for each sector.
  • Timeframe & Deliverables: Timeline with key milestones.
  • Budget: Estimated costs and fund allocation.
  • Reporting: Requirements, frequency, and evaluation mechanisms.
üìö

Commissioning Case Studies

Case studies showcase the effectiveness of collaboration. Steps include identifying successful examples, developing guidelines, commissioning qualified individuals, and disseminating findings via reports or media.

üó£Ô∏è Partnership Dialogue

Partnership dialogue is the cornerstone of effective collaboration. It involves open, honest communication to identify shared goals and foster trust.

Key Components

  • Shared Vision: Common objectives.
  • Role Clarification: Clear expectations.
  • Open Communication: Safe, inclusive space.
  • Trust Building: Reliability and integrity.
  • Conflict Resolution: Managing disagreements.
  • Decision-Making: Consensus building.

Facilitation Strategies

  • Regular Meetings: Consistent schedule.
  • Clear Agenda: Defined topics.
  • Neutral Facilitation: Guide conversation.
  • Documentation: Detailed records.
  • Active Participation: Encourage all voices.
‚ö°

Challenges to Anticipate

Be prepared for power imbalances, competing priorities, different organizational cultures, and resource constraints. Addressing these openly turns challenges into opportunities for growth.
