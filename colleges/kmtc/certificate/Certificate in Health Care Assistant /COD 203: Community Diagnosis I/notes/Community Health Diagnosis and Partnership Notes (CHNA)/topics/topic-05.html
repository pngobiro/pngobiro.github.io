üéØ Developing Measurable Goals

Measurable goals provide clear direction and allow for tracking progress. They should adhere to the SMART criteria.

üí°

SMART Goals

  • Specific: Clearly defined and focused.
  • Measurable: Quantifiable with specific metrics.
  • Achievable: Realistic within resources.
  • Relevant: Aligned with health needs.
  • Time-bound: Set with a clear deadline.
Type Example Goal
Non-Measurable "Improve physical activity among children."
Measurable "Increase the percentage of children participating in at least 30 minutes of moderate-to-vigorous physical activity daily by 20% within two years."

üìâ Metrics & Challenges

Common Metrics

  • Population-level: Prevalence rates, mortality rates, quality of life.
  • Process metrics: Number of people reached, participation rates, adherence.
  • Outcome metrics: Changes in behaviors, improvements in health status.

Overcoming Challenges

  • Resource Constraints: Prioritize strategies and seek additional funding.
  • Community Engagement: Build trust and address barriers.
  • Data Availability: Use available data creatively.
  • Sustainability: Develop long-term capacity plans.
