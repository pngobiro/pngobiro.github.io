üîç Data Identification & Collection

Understanding Primary vs. Secondary Data

Data Type Definition Examples in CHNA
Primary Data Information collected firsthand for a specific research purpose. Surveys, focus groups, interviews, direct observations.
Secondary Data Information collected by others for different purposes but still relevant. Government records, health statistics, census data, existing research.

Steps for Identification of Relevant Data

  1. 1
    Define the community: Clearly outline the geographic and demographic boundaries of your target population.
  2. 2
    Identify health indicators: Determine key health measures relevant to the community (e.g., mortality rates, morbidity rates, prevalence of diseases).
  3. 3
    List potential data sources: Consider both primary and secondary data sources based on available resources, time, and data needs.

üß† Interpretation & Analysis

Data Interpretation Methods

üìä
Descriptive Analysis: Summarize data using measures of central tendency (mean, median, mode) and dispersion (range, standard deviation).
üî¨
Inferential Analysis: Draw conclusions about the population based on sample data (e.g., hypothesis testing, correlation analysis).
üìù
Qualitative Analysis: Analyze textual data from interviews and focus groups to identify themes and patterns.

Analysis Focus Areas for CHNA

  • Health Status Assessment: Analyze mortality, morbidity, and disease prevalence to identify disparities.
  • Risk Factor Assessment: Examine behaviors, environmental factors, and socioeconomic conditions.
  • Access & Utilization: Evaluate healthcare access, utilization rates, and barriers to care.
  • Gap Analysis: Identify discrepancies between community health needs and available services.
üí°

Example Analysis: Infant Mortality

Scenario: High rates of infant mortality in a specific community.

Analysis: Analyze data on maternal health services, access to prenatal care, socioeconomic factors, and infant mortality rates.

Interpretation: Identify potential causes such as lack of prenatal care, poverty, or limited access to healthcare.

Data visualization is a powerful tool for communicating complex information in a clear and engaging manner. It is essential for understanding trends, patterns, and disparities.
