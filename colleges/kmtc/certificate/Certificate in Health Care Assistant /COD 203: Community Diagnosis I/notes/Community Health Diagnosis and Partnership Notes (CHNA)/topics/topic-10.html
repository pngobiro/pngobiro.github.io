üîó Developing a Partnership Network

Developing a robust partnership network is crucial for the success of inter-sectoral collaborations (ISC). It requires a strategic approach grounded in shared goals, trust, and equity.

6 Steps to Develop a Network

  1. 1
    Identify Problem & Goals: Analyze the issue and set SMART goals. Map stakeholders relevant to the problem.
  2. 2
    Build Relationships & Trust: Initiate contact and engage in trust-building activities. Develop a shared vision.
  3. 3
    Form a Core Group: Select committed partners to initiate the work and create a formal partnership agreement.
  4. 4
    Develop Structure: Establish governance models, communication plans, and resource sharing mechanisms.
  5. 5
    Implement Activities: Execute work plans through collaboration and manage conflicts constructively.
  6. 6
    Sustain the Partnership: Promote continuous learning, adaptation, and celebrate successes.

Mobilization Strategies

  • Needs Assessment: Identify common challenges.
  • Stakeholder Engagement: Map interests and build relationships.
  • Capacity Building: Provide training and mentorship.
  • Resource Mobilization: Develop funding proposals and leverage assets.

Strategy Area Action Examples
Improving Access to Care Expand services in underserved areas, provide transportation, implement patient navigation.
Addressing Social Determinants Housing improvements, education partnerships (literacy), workplace wellness programs.
Promoting Health Community-based health promotion, food security initiatives (food banks), safe physical activity spaces.
Data & Analysis Robust monitoring systems to identify trends and evaluate effectiveness.

‚öôÔ∏è Incorporating Inter-Sectoral Action

Incorporating inter-sectoral action involves systematic steps to integrate collaboration into community planning. This approach ensures sustainable solutions for complex challenges.

‚ñ∂ 8 Steps for Incorporation
  1. Needs Assessment: Identify pressing issues and root causes.
  2. Stakeholder Mapping: Assess roles and build trust.
  3. Formation of Collaborative Body: Create a platform for collaboration.
  4. Shared Vision & Goals: Align objectives among stakeholders.
  5. Resource Mobilization: Pool resources from different sectors.
  6. Action Planning: Assign responsibilities and timelines.
  7. Implementation & Coordination: Coordinate activities and address challenges.
  8. M&E: Assess progress and adjust strategies.
üí°

Examples of Action

  • Youth Unemployment: Education + Business + Government vocational training.
  • Improving Public Health: Healthcare + Community Orgs + Government preventive care.
  • Reducing Poverty: Social Services + Private Sector job training + Housing.
