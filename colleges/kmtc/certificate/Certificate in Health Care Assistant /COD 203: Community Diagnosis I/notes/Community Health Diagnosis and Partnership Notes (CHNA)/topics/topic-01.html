Core Definition: Health

Health is defined as a state of complete physical, mental, and social well-being, not merely the absence of disease or infirmity.

  • Community: A group of people living in a defined geographical area, sharing common interests and values.
  • Diagnosis: The process of identifying a disease or condition through careful examination and analysis.
  • Community Health Diagnosis: A systematic process of identifying and quantifying health problems within a community. It involves collecting data, analyzing it, and prioritizing health needs based on the findings.

Epidemiological & Assessment Terms

Term Definition
Mortality Rate The number of deaths occurring within a population per unit of time.
Morbidity Rate The incidence of disease within a population.
Prevalence The total number of cases of a disease or condition at a specific time (existing cases).
Incidence The number of new cases of a disease or condition occurring over a specific period.
Risk Factors Factors that increase the likelihood of developing a disease or condition.
Health Indicators Variables used to assess the health of a community (e.g., mortality rates, immunization coverage).
Stakeholders Individuals or groups who have an interest in or are affected by the health of a community.

üìä Community Health Needs Assessment (CHA)

A Community Health Needs Assessment (CHA) is a systematic process used to identify the most significant health needs and issues within a specific community. It involves collecting and analyzing data, engaging community members, and developing strategies to address identified health priorities.

üéØ

Purpose of a CHA

  • Identify key health needs: Pinpoint the most pressing health issues affecting the community.
  • Prioritize issues: Determine which health issues require immediate attention based on severity and impact.
  • Develop action plans: Create strategies to address identified health needs.
  • Allocate resources effectively: Optimize the use of resources to achieve maximum impact.

Key Components of a CHA

  1. Data Collection: Gathering demographics, health statistics, social determinants, and community feedback.
  2. Data Analysis: Identifying patterns, trends, and disparities; prioritizing needs based on severity.
  3. Community Engagement: Involving members via focus groups, meetings, and surveys.
  4. Needs Identification: Determining significant health needs based on data and community input.
  5. Development of a Community Health Improvement Plan (CHIP): Creating a plan with measurable goals, strategies, and evaluation metrics.
