üéØ Developing Vision, Goals & Objectives

A successful Community Action Plan (CAP) requires a solid foundation built on community assessment and stakeholder engagement. This foundation supports the creation of a clear vision, specific goals, and actionable objectives.

Component Definition & Characteristics Example
Vision Long-term, inspiring picture of the desired future. Should be clear, motivating, and community-owned. "A community where all residents enjoy optimal health and well-being throughout their lives."
Goals Broad statements of what the community wants to achieve. Should be specific, measurable, achievable, relevant, and time-bound. "Reduce the prevalence of childhood obesity by 20% within the next five years."
Objectives Specific, actionable steps contributing to goals. Must be SMART. "Implement a school-based nutrition education program for all elementary students by the end of the next school year."
üí°

SMART Criteria

Objectives should be: Specific, Measurable, Achievable, Relevant, and Time-bound.

üå± Community-Based Initiatives (CBIs)

Community-based initiatives empower local communities to identify challenges, prioritize needs, and implement solutions. They are the backbone of sustainable development within a CAP.

‚ñ∂ 7 Steps to Develop CBIs
  1. 1
    Needs Assessment: Identify pressing issues using data-driven approaches.
  2. 2
    Capacity Building: Train members, foster leadership, and strengthen organizations.
  3. 3
    Initiative Identification: Select initiatives based on feasibility and impact.
  4. 4
    Partnership Building: Collaborate with government, NGOs, and businesses.
  5. 5
    Resource Mobilization: Identify funding (grants, donations) and sustainable strategies.
  6. 6
    Implementation Planning: Create detailed plans with timelines and roles.
  7. 7
    Monitoring & Evaluation: Track progress and share lessons learned.

Examples of CBIs

üè• Health

Community health centers, nutrition programs.

üìö Education

Literacy classes, vocational training.

üí∞ Economic

Microfinance, cooperative development.

üå≥ Environment

Waste management, reforestation.

üß± Determining Required Resources

Identifying resources ensures the plan's feasibility and sustainability. Resources are categorized into four main types.

Category Description Examples
Human Resources People contributing time and skills. Volunteers, paid staff, partners, experts.
Financial Resources Funding required for implementation. Grants, donations, in-kind contributions, budget allocations.
Material Resources Physical items needed. Equipment, supplies, infrastructure, facilities.
Information Resources Knowledge and data. Statistics, research findings, expertise, technology.
üìù

Process for Resource Determination

  1. Analyze the Action Plan.
  2. Identify specific needs per action step.
  3. Quantify the needs (exact amounts).
  4. Prioritize based on importance.
  5. Consider alternatives (substitutes).
  6. Develop a mobilization plan.
