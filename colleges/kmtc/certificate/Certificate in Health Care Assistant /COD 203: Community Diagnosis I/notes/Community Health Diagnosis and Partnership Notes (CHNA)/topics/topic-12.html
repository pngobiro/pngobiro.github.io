Community health information (CHI) is crucial for effective healthcare delivery in Kenya. While strides have been made, the landscape presents unique challenges.

üîí

Privacy Concerns

Concerns about data privacy and security persist, hindering the willingness to share sensitive health information. There is also a need for increased capacity building on data importance.

02

üöß Key Challenges

Several barriers hinder the effective sharing and maintenance of health information.

  • Infrastructure: Inadequate internet connectivity and limited access to computers, especially in rural areas.
  • Human Resources: Shortage of skilled healthcare workers impacts data quality.
  • Financial Constraints: Limited resources for system development and training.
  • Lack of Standardization: Inconsistent data collection tools hinder analysis.
  • Cultural Barriers: Traditional beliefs may influence data sharing.
  • Data Governance: Weak structures and policies regarding ownership and access.

üöÄ Strategies for Improvement

To overcome challenges, Kenya is adopting several strategic approaches:

‚ñ∂ Strategic Approaches
  • Strengthen CHW Network: Equip with mobile devices and digital platforms.
  • Enhance Facility Systems: Invest in interoperable EHRs and data standardization.

  • Privacy & Security: Implement robust protection measures to build trust.
  • Capacity Building: Train providers on data management and analysis.
  • Leverage Tech: Explore AI and machine learning for decision support.
  • Public-Private Partnerships: Foster collaboration for resources and expertise.
