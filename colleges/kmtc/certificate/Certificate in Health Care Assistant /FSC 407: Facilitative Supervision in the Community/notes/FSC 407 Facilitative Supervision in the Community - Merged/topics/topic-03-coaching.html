Introduction to Coaching

  • A leadership tool that is designed to help produce results
  • A developmental approach to working and interacting with other people.
  • Helps people develop their personal capabilities, interpersonal skills and capacity to understand and empathize with others
  • Can help people understand more about their own challenges and make informed choices about their future
  • Helps people strengthen their ability to use a range of influencing and leadership styles.
  • Helps people feel more empowered to make a difference and improve their own effectiveness

Coaching

A training approach that seeks to achieve continuous improvement in performance. This occurs through motivation, modeling, practice, constructive feedback and gradual transfer of skills.

This is a training or development process via which an individual is supported while achieving a specific personal or professional competence result or goal. The individual receiving the coaching may be referred to as a coachee. Coaching is balanced, concrete and respectful.

Characteristics of Coaching

  1. 1It takes place within the confines of a formal working set up
  2. 2Focuses on developing individuals within their current jobs
  3. 3Interest is functional arising out of the need to ensure that individuals can perform the tasks required to the best of their abilities
  4. 4Relationship tends to be initiated and driven by an individual manager
  5. 5Relationship is finite ends as an individual transfers to another job
  6. 6It allows staff to learn on the job and immediately apply what they are learning and see how it works

üë£ Steps in Effective Coaching

01
Motivate

Start by motivating the staff to gain commitment to acquire the new behavior

02
Demonstrate

Demonstrate competently and explain the new behavior with an opportunity for the trainee to ask questions

03
Practice

Give the trainee an opportunity to practice, apply and demonstrate their ability to perform under the supervisor

04
Feedback

Provide constructive feedback by sharing the evaluation of the trainee in a concrete, respectful, two way interchange of ideas

05
Transfer Skills

Allow the trainee transfer skills gradually, the trainee is provided an opportunity to undertake and demonstrate an increasing number of the sub skills involved in the new behavior after which the trainee becomes competent to carry out the new behavior without supervision.

What to avoid when coaching...

  • Giving solutions: The goal of coaching is to help the employee improve their own judgment and skill so that they can eventually succeed on their own. By jumping in with the solution, the coach does not support the employee to develop the ability to problem solve on their own.
  • Judging: giving verdict, jumping into conclusion, deciding ...
  • Assessing the employee‚Äôs performance
  • Blaming and criticizing: from a supervisor undermines an employee‚Äôs perception that the supervisor is confident in their ability to solve the problem ‚Äì can undermine the employee‚Äôs self-confidence.

üéÅ Benefits of Coaching

General Benefits

  1. 1It allows staff to learn on the job
  2. 2It allows staff to immediately apply what they are learning and see how well it works
  3. 3It fosters a positive working relationship with staff who previously may have been critics
  4. 4It makes staff feel supported and important
üßë‚Äçüéì

Benefits to the Coachee

  • A chance to discuss issues, blockages and/or concerns in your career development
  • Having someone (other than friends/colleagues/tutors) available to share difficult situations
  • Having someone to believe in you and your ability
  • Being given help to work out what it is you want from life and work
  • Being given help to develop a greater confidence
  • Learning to cope with the informal and formal structure of your future
üßë‚Äçüè´

Benefits for the Coach

  • A chance to discover and work with colleagues/students from a different viewpoint
  • An opportunity to share their knowledge, skills and experience
  • The satisfaction of knowing that they have made a difference to someone else
  • The huge amount of personal learning that can be taken from the experience
  • The opportunity to create some reflective space in a hectic daily work schedule
  • An increased skills base, credibility and reputation

üîÅ Steps in Coaching during Facilitative Supervision

01
Stage One - Rapport Building Stage

Here the mentor/coach and mentee/coachee explore whether they can work together.

02
Stage Two - Setting Direction Stage

Coach and coachee clarify and refine what the relationship should achieve on both sides. Linking the medium to long term goals with what happens on a day to day basis is done.

03
Stage Three - Core Stage/ Progression

Coach and coachee become more relaxed about challenging each other, exploring issues more deeply and experience mutual learning. Coachee takes more of the lead in managing the relationship & the process.

04
Stage Four - Winding up

This occurs when the coachee has achieved a large part of their goals or feels equipped with the confidence, plans and insight to continue the journey under their own steam. Planning for a good ending is critical if both parties are to emerge from the process with a positive perception of the experience.

05
Stage Five ‚Äì Moving on

This stage is about reformulating the relationship, typically into a friendship in which both parties can meet each other on an ad hoc basis as a sounding board or a source of networking contacts. Individuals develop feelings of parting ways.
