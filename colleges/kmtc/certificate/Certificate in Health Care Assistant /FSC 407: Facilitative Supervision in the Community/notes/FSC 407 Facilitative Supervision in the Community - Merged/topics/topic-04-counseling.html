Counseling

This is the service offered to the individual who is undergoing a problem and needs professional help to overcome it. It is a more specialized service requiring training in personality development and handling exceptional groups of individuals.

üö´

Counseling is NOT:

  • Giving advice
  • Judgmental
  • Attempting to sort out the problems of the client
  • Expecting or encouraging a client to behave in a way which the counselor may have behaved when confronted with a similar problem in their own life
  • Getting emotionally involved with the client
  • Looking at a client‚Äôs problems from your own perspective based on your own value system

Principles of Good Counseling

This involves helping the individual to cope with their emotions and feelings and to help them make positive choices and decisions. It involves:

  • Establishing a trusting relationship
  • Helping the client tell their story
  • Listening carefully
  • Respecting the client
  • Being non-judgmental
  • Providing confidentiality
  • Providing correct information
  • Helping the individual make informed decisions
  • Help the client build a positive attitude
  • Maintaining a professional relationship
  • Help the client to recognize and build on their strengths

Role of a Counselor

  1. 1Enables the client to explore many aspects of their life and feelings by talking openly and freely
  2. 2The client is given an opportunity to express difficult feelings such as anger, resentment, guilt and fear in a confidential report
  3. 3The counselor encourages the client to examine parts of their lives that they may have found impossible to face before

üîÑ Egan's Model

It emphasizes that people need to manage their problems in living more effectively and develop unused opportunities more fully and this helps people become at helping themselves in their everyday lives.

01
Stage One: The Present

Help the client to tell their story but actively listening with open questions. Use reflection to help the client to break through any blind spots. The client prioritizes to find the right solution.

02
Stage Two: Preferred

In this model at this stage, the client is helped to use his/her imagination to find possibilities; help the client choose realistic and challenging goals. The client is then assisted to find incentives that will help with commitment.

03
Stage Three: How to get to where you want to

The client is helped to find possible actions and best fit strategies. The client is then helped to draft an action plan.

2. Non-Verbal Cues

Validates or invalidates verbal communication

  • Bodily behavior
  • Facial expressions
  • Voice related behavior
  • Observable physiological responses
  • Physical appearance

3. Active Listening

  • Use to detect gaps, distortions, dissonance and themes
  • Listen to allow empathy
  • Listen with appropriate interruption
  • Listening with your questions
  • Listen to oneself
  • Listen without diagnosing

6. Self Disclosure

This is the act of revealing personal information about oneself to others. It can involve sharing thoughts, feelings, experiences, or personal details that are not commonly known or easily discernible by others.

üîÑ Process & Benefits

Why do people need counseling?

  • Help them understand the problems they are experiencing.
  • Give them information they are lacking to solve problems
  • Assist them to understand alternative approaches to solving the problems facing them.
  • Help them to explore the problems and clarify conflicting issues
  • Assist them to adjust to the problem or find better ways to living with the problem
  • Assist others who may be experiencing similar problems

Potential benefits of counseling

  • Improved communication and interpersonal skills.
  • Greater self-acceptance and self-esteem ‚Äì self-awareness & introspection.
  • Ability to change self-defeating behaviors/habits.
  • Better expression & management of emotions, including anger.
  • Belief from depression, anxiety or other mental health conditions ‚Äì Weapon to mental health issues.
  • Relieves unpleasant emotions.
  • Develops openness & acceptance
  • Opportunity for self-exploration and self-discovery
  • Peace of mind ‚Äì changes one's thoughts
