Also known as Oxyuris Vermicularis (Pin worm).

Life Cycle

When eggs are swallowed they pass to the duodenum where they hatch. They move to small intestines where they mount (mating takes place) gravid females shift to the caecum. Eggs are laid and come out with feaces. They do not develop until they are swallowed by man again. The cycle continues.

ü™± Ascaris Lumbricoides

  • Disease: Ascariasis (roundworm infection)
  • Distribution: cosmopolitan
  • Definitive host: man, pigs
  • Intermediate host: nill

Life Cycle

01

Man ingest un-hatched larvae, they go via digestive system to intestine then penetrate intestinal wall, go via the blood circulatory system up to the lungs where they moult (cast off their outer covering) and then migrate to the bronchi and ascend to the mouth.

02

They travel through the digestive system again to the intestines where they develop into adults. Mating takes place and gravid eggs come out with feaces.

03

The eggs develop into infective larvae with soil or water. If man ingests infective larvae the cycle continues.

Larval Stage Symptoms

Fever (urticaria), change of skin colour, coughing

ü§¢

Adult Stage Symptoms

Inflammation of mucus membrane of intestines, pneumonia, abdominal discomforts, loss of appetite

üî¨

Management

Diagnosis: Ova in feaces

Treatment: Use of anti-helminths

Prevention/ Control

  1. Safe disposal of feaces
  2. Personal hygiene
  3. Boiling of water
  4. Cook vegetables
  5. Treatment of the sick
  6. Create awareness

ü¶ü Wucheria Barncrofti

  • Causes disease: filariasis
  • Distribution: Cosmopolitan mostly in tropical countries
  • Definitive host: man
  • Intermediate host: culex fatigans, anopheles gambiae, culex paedes are capable of aiding transmission

Life Cycle

  • Larvae enters the skin through penetration by a bite by mosquito pass via circulatory system or lymphatic to reach the nodes
  • In nodes they mature and develop into microfilarial.
  • Mature microfilarial pass through walls of the lymphatic system to the blood circulatory system.

  • Chronic stage: Lymph nodes are enlarged, obstruction of lymphatics causing genital enlargement and elephantiasis in various parts of the body (legs, hands) etc.

  • Disease: Onchocerciasis
  • Distribution: Central, west Africa and East Africa
  • Definitive host: man
  • Intermediate host: fly species simulium damnosum and simulium neaves

Life Cycle

  • Micro-fillarial are ingested by fly through a bite on the host (definitive)
  • In a short time microfillariae develop into infective fillarial form larvae.
  • After the bite they travel to the places of predilection, mate and produce microfillarial. They fly and ingest microfillaria to man again. The cycle continues

Common Name: Eye worm infection

  • Definative host: man
  • Intermediate host: horse fly/ margrare fly of species

Man is infected through a bite of the fly

üîç Click to zoom

Life Cycle

  • The microfilarial are ingested by fly through a bite; pass through stages of development in fly and then pass to the head.
  • When fly bites man the microfilarial are discharged into circulatory system of man. They travel to places of predilection where they mature and produce microfilarial. Preferred predilections; periphery of the body and during this time, they invade the eye.
  • On skin- development of calabar swellings (as large as an egg) usually on the hands, legs and disappear later.

  • Itching, irritation
  • Calabar swellings
  • Urticaria (change of colour of skin)- Urticarial dermatitis
  • Sensation, lacrimation, redness, pricking of the eye
