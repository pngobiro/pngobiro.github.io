They are mainly three types:

  2. Taenia solium
  3. Dyphyllobothrium latum - fish tapeworm
02

üêÑ Taenia Sagnata

  • Disease caused is taeniasis
  • Distributed in tropical Africa, Europe and parts of Asia

Life Cycle

01

Adults are found in the small intestines of man; mating takes place, eggs are laid and gravid proglotids (Ripe eggs come out together with feaces).

02

Animals (cattle) ingest the eggs and egg travel via digestive system to reach stomach and then to intestines (onchosphere).

03

Penetrates walls of small intestines to reach lymphatic system, travel via lymphatic system to reach their places of predilection (active muscles; heart, tongue, massetta muscles, diaphragm).

04

Here they become quiescent (inactive/dormant) and encyst forming cystcercus bovis. If animal is slaughtered man ingests infested beef and the cycle continues.

Symptoms/Pathology

  • Anemia in infested person
  • Fever
  • General malaise
  • Headaches
  • Blood spots in stool

03

üêü Dyphylobothrium latum

  • Distribution: cosmopolitan
  • Disease caused: Dyphyllobothriasis
  • Location: small intestines

Life Cycle

Adult are found in small intestines of man. Male and females are found in the intestines together. Mating takes place, eggs are laid and come together with feaces. These eggs develop into an ovum which develop into an ovum which develop into caracidium (ciliated larvae).

The caracidium escapes into water and swims. It is ingested by cylops (water fleas, planktons). It travels into the cards of the cyclops and develop into procercoid.

Fish ingests infested cyclop. The procercoid penetrate the muscles of fish and develop into procercoid. Here the larvae are encysted and remain infective throughout the life of fish. If man ingest infested fish either raw or uncooked the larvae emerge and go via digestive system to reach small intestines where they develop into adults.

  1. Abdominal pain.
  2. Vomiting
  3. Anaemia
  4. Multiple infestation; cause obstruction in the intestines

  2. Create awareness
  3. Safe disposal of feaces

  5. Safe water without cyclops

  7. Prevent discharge of raw sewage into water bodies.
  8. Proper cooking of fish

üêñ Taenia Solium

  • Distribution: cosmopolitan
  • Disease: Taeniasis

Life Cycle

Adults are found in the small intestines of man. Mating takes place, eggs are laid and come out with together with feaces. The eggs rapture after leaving man and hatch into larvae (onchosphere) if pig ingest the onchosphere, they travel via the digestive system and enter the circulatory system and commonly the lymphatic system where they are taken to places of predilection(skeletal muscles, visceral organs, heart, diaphragm, tongue and on abdominal muscles). If man ingests infested pork, the cycle continues.

ü•© Trichinella Spiralis

  • Disease: Trichinosis
  • Distribution: worldwide except Asia and Australia
  • It has definitive host and intermediate host together e.g. man, cats, dogs, pigs, rats and carnivorous animals.

Life Cycle

How man is infected- ingestion of raw pork. Larvae pass through digestive system to reach intestines and develop into adults. Females barrow walls of the intestines and lay eggs. Eggs hatch into larvae. Larvae enter the circulatory system and are transported to places of predilection (tongue, diaphragm, striated muscle) where they encyst and form coil. If man ingests infested pork, larvae emerge.

On Examination

  1. Swelling in muscles
  2. Calcified cysts (build up of calcium where it is not required)
  3. Caeseated cysts (cell death)

On Man

  1. Anaemia
  2. Diminished muscular tissue
  3. Punctiform haemorrhages in the intestines

Prevention/Control

  1. Treat the sick
  2. Create awareness
  3. Thorough meat inspection and judgment
  4. Thorough cooking of pork.
  5. Restrict pigs
