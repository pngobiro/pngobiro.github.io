üß¨ Introduction & Genetics

Definitions

Reproduction:
Biological process by which new individual organisms ("offspring") are produced from their "parent".
Genetics:
Study of how genes bring about characteristics (traits) and how they are inherited.
Genotype:
The genetic constitution of an individual organism (e.g., TT, Tt).
Phenotype:
Outward expression of a trait (e.g., Tall, Short, Green eyes).
Type Genotype Phenotype Example
Homozygous Dominant TT Tall
Homozygous Recessive tt Short
Heterozygous Tt Tall (hybrid)

Mendel's Laws

üìú

Principles of Inheritance

  1. 1Law of Segregation: Copies of a gene separate so that each gamete receives only one copy.
  2. 2Law of Independent Assortment: Alleles of different genes assort independently during gamete formation.
  3. 3Law of Dominance: Some alleles are dominant and some are recessive.

‚ôÇÔ∏è Male Reproductive System

Located outside the body and within the pelvis. Responsible for sperm production, hormone secretion, and urination.

‚ñ∂ External Organs

Penis

  • Root: Attaches to abdominal wall.
  • Shaft (Body): Contains erectile tissues that fill with blood.
  • Glans: Cone-shaped end, covered by foreskin. Contains urethra opening.

Scrotum

Loose pouch holding testicles. Acts as a climate control system (keeps testes cooler than body temp).

Testicles (Testes)

Produce testosterone and sperm (via seminiferous tubules).

Epididymis

Coiled tube on backside of testicle. Stores and matures sperm.

‚ñ∂ Internal Organs
  • Vas Deferens: Transports mature sperm to urethra.
  • Seminal Vesicles: Produce fructose-rich fluid (energy for sperm).
  • Prostate Gland: Walnut-sized; contributes fluid to nourish sperm.
  • Bulbourethral (Cowper's) Glands: Produce lubricating fluid that neutralizes acidity.
  • Urethra: Carries urine and semen out of the body.

External Genitals (Vulva)

  • Mons Pubis: Fat pad over pubic bone.
  • Labia Majora & Minora: Folds of skin protecting the urethra and reproductive entrance.
  • Clitoris: Organ for sexual sensation.
  • Bartholin's Glands: Produce lubrication.

üìÖ The Menstrual Cycle

Series of changes in uterine lining. Average length is 28 days.

Phase Days Key Events
Menstrual 1 - 5 Shedding of inner lining (Endometrium).
Follicular (Proliferative) 1 - 13 Endometrium rebuilds. Estrogen increases.
Ovulation 14 Release of matured egg. LH surge.
Luteal (Secretory) 15 - 28 Corpus luteum forms (Progesterone). Prepares for implantation.
