üìò Introduction

This unit covers three major body systems involved in the intake of essential materials and the removal of waste:

  1. 1Digestive System: Absorption of nutrients.
  2. 2Respiratory System: Gas exchange.
  3. 3Urinary System: Excretion of metabolic waste.

Organs of the Alimentary Canal

‚ñ∂ Upper GI Tract
Mouth (Oral Cavity):
Ingestion, mastication, and mixing with saliva (contains amylase). Includes teeth, tongue, and salivary glands.
Salivary Glands:
  • Major: Parotid, Submandibular, Sublingual.
  • Functions: Moisten food, chemical digestion (amylase), cleanse mouth, dissolve chemicals for taste.
Pharynx:
Throat region. Swallowing receptors stimulated here. Closes off trachea via epiglottis.
Esophagus:
Muscular tube (~20 cm) connecting pharynx to stomach. Uses peristalsis to move food.
Stomach:
Hollow organ for mixing and enzymatic breakdown (Pepsin/HCl). Forms chyme.
Functions: Storage, mechanical breakdown, chemical digestion, defense (acid), intrinsic factor secretion.

Fig. 5.2: Structure of the Stomach

‚ñ∂ Lower GI Tract
Small Intestine:
Three segments: Duodenum (breakdown), Jejunum & Ileum (absorption). Villi absorb nutrients into blood (capillaries) and lymph (lacteals).
Large Intestine (Colon):
Absorbs water, concentrates waste, bacterial fermentation. Stores feces in rectum prior to defecation.

Accessory Organs

Organ Function
Liver Produces bile (emulsifies fats), detoxifies chemicals, processes nutrients (body's chemical factory).
Gall Bladder Stores and concentrates bile; releases it into duodenum.
Pancreas Secretes pancreatic juice (enzymes + bicarbonate buffer) and insulin (hormone for sugar metabolism).

The Digestive Process

  1. 1Ingestion: Intake via mouth.
  2. 2Mechanical Processing: Crushing/shearing (chewing, churning).
  3. 3Chemical Digestion: Enzymatic breakdown of carbs, proteins, lipids.
  4. 4Secretion: Release of acids, enzymes, buffers.
  5. 5Absorption: Movement of organic substrates into fluids.
  6. 6Excretion (Defecation): Removal of waste products (feces).

Structure

‚ñ∂ Upper Respiratory Tract
  • Nose & Nasal Cavities: Filter (hairs), warm, and moisten air. Conchae increase surface area.
  • Pharynx: Common passageway for air and food (Nasopharynx, Oropharynx, Laryngopharynx).
‚ñ∂ Lower Respiratory Tract
  • Larynx (Voice Box): Contains vocal cords. Protected by Epiglottis.
  • Trachea (Windpipe): Held open by C-shaped cartilage rings.
  • Bronchi & Bronchioles: Branching tree. Right bronchus is wider/more vertical. Bronchioles lack cartilage, have smooth muscle.
  • Lungs & Alveoli: Alveoli are air sacs for gas exchange (single cell layer). Surfactant prevents collapse.

üöΩ Urinary System

Functions

  • Remove waste products (urea, drugs) from blood.
  • Balance body fluids (water, salts).
  • Regulate blood pressure (Renin hormone).
  • Control RBC production (Erythropoietin).

Organs

1. Kidneys

Bean-shaped, retroperitoneal organs.

  • Parts: Cortex (outer), Medulla (inner), Hilum (entry/exit point).
  • Nephron: Functional unit (>1 million per kidney). Filters blood.

Urine Formation

  1. Filtration: Glomerulus filters fluid into Bowman's capsule.
  2. Reabsorption: Tubules reabsorb 99% of water, glucose, ions.
  3. Secretion: Wastes (H+, K+) moved into tubules to form urine.

2. Ureters

Tubes carrying urine from kidneys to bladder via peristalsis.

3. Urinary Bladder

Hollow organ storing urine (300-400ml capacity). Contains Trigone area and sphincter muscles.

4. Urethra

Tube carrying urine outside.

  • Female: ~1.5 inches, urine only.
  • Male: ~8 inches, urine and sperm. Passes through prostate.
