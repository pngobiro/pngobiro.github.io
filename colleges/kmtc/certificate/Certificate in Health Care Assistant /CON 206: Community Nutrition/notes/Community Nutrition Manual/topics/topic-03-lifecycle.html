Pregnancy & Breastfeeding Guidelines

  • Eat two additional small meals or snacks each day to provide energy and nutrition for the growing baby.
  • Consume a minimum of 2 to 3 liters of liquids per day.
  • Eat nutritious foods: milk, fresh fruit/vegetables, meat, fish, eggs, grains, groundnuts, peas, and beans.
  • Avoid: Taking tea or coffee with meals (interferes with nutrient use).

Immediate Breastfeeding

‚è±Ô∏è

Within 1 Hour

Begin breastfeeding within the first hour. Hold newborn skin-to-skin immediately for one hour.

ü•õ

Colostrum

First thick yellowish milk. Protects from illness and helps pass meconium.

üö´

No Water

Do not give water or other liquids. Dangerous for newborn.

Exclusive Breastfeeding (0-6 Months)

‚úÖ

Definition

Feeding the baby ONLY breast milk for the first 6 months, without weaning. No water, no herbs, no other liquids.

üõ°Ô∏è

Benefits

Complete food for 6 months. Protects against diarrhea and respiratory infections.

‚ö†Ô∏è

Risks of Mixed Feeding

Diarrhea, pneumonia, malnutrition. Weaning too early causes GIT abrasions where infection can enter.

Breastfeeding on Demand

Breastfeed as often as the baby wants, day and night (every 2-3 hours). The more the baby suckles, the more milk is produced.

Signs of Hunger (Early)

  • Restlessness
  • Opening mouth and turning head
  • Putting tongue in and out
  • Suckling on fingers/fists

Note: Crying is a late sign or may mean discomfort, not necessarily hunger.

üí°

Foremilk vs. Hindmilk

Let baby finish one breast before offering the other.

  • Foremilk: More water, satisfies thirst.
  • Hindmilk: More fat, satisfies hunger.

üéí School-Going Children (6-12 Years)

Steady but slow growth rate. Usually eat 4-5 times a day. Habits influenced by family, friends, and media.

5 Essential Nutrients

ü•©

Protein

Failure to grow, irritability, weak immunity. Sources: Poultry, lean meat, eggs, dairy, legumes.

ü©∏

Iron

Deficiency leads to cognitive/motor delays. Sources: Lean meat, dark poultry, leafy greens.

ü¶¥

Vitamin D

Bone health. Sources: Fish, dairy, egg yolks, sunlight.

ü•õ

Calcium

Bone development, muscle function. Sources: Dairy, fortified cereals.

üêü

Omega-3s

Cognitive development. Sources: Fish, fish oil.

Mealtime Hints

  • Always serve breakfast (Fruit, milk, toast, cereal).
  • Set good examples.
  • Let children help with meal planning.
  • Serve meals at the table without distractions (TV, phones).

üë¥ Elderly Nutrition

Physiological Changes (>51 Years)

  • Decline in immune function, hormone production, muscle mass.
  • Reduced efficiency of heart and kidneys.
  • Lower efficiency in vitamin/mineral absorption.
  • Slight reduction in height.

Specific Nutritional Requirements

Nutrient Requirement/Note
Energy Lower needs due to reduced lean mass and metabolic rate.
Fiber Crucial to prevent constipation and diverticulitis.
Calcium & Vit D Increase intake to slow bone loss (Men >70, all women).
Vitamin B12 Low stomach acid reduces absorption. Use fortified foods/supplements.
Iron Higher levels no longer needed for post-menopausal women.
Fluids 8 glasses/day to prevent dehydration and constipation.
