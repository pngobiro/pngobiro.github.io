ü•ó Introduction to Food and Nutrition

What is nutrition?

  • The study of nutrients in food, how body uses them and the relationship between food/diet, health and disease nutrition
  • Food is composed of different chemical elements arranged in a variety of ways to form molecules
  • These molecules collectively give individual foods their flavour, colour and texture and affects their reaction to heat and their digestion
  • The body uses some of the molecules in food to function properly and stay health. These are the nutrients (nutrients are components of food)
  • There are several nutrients in the body and each as its own function in the body. There are several nutrients in the body and each as its own function in the body.
  • Each nutrient is vital to life and the health of an individual will suffer if any one of the nutrients is in short supply
  • Most food contains more than one nutrient so are of use to body in several ways.
  • Some food such as sugar have one nutrient and are of limited use to the food
  • However no single food provides all the nutrients required by the body in sufficient quantities so a variety of foods must be eaten.

üåü Importance of Good Nutrition

A healthy diet throughout life;

  • Promotes healthy pregnancy outcomes.
  • Supports normal growth.
  • Body development and ageing.
  • Helps to maintain a healthy body weight.
  • Reduces the risk of chronic diseases.
  • Leads to overall health and well-being.
üìà

Relationship between nutrition and development

  • During childhood, under-nutrition causes children to have less energy and less interest for learning, which negatively influences cognitive development and academic performance.
  • Under-nutrition will also affect physical growth and maturation, thus affecting growth rate, body weight and ultimately height.
  • Nutrition plays a critical role in human resource development.
  • This is because deficiencies in essential nutrients lead to malnutrition which affects an individual‚Äôs mental and physical state, resulting in poor work performance.
  • Good nutrition is important for physical and mental development and it allows people to reach their full potential (e.g in school and at work).
  • It also underpins a strong immune system, which protects us from both communicable and non-communicable diseases.

Benefits of healthy eating

  • The basic principle of what constitutes a healthy diet remains the same.
  • Healthy food starts with a healthy diet in pregnancy, continues with breast milk for babies and is important for children, teenagers, adults and with ageing.
  • People who regularly eat more foods high in energy, fats, free sugars or salt/sodium, and do not eat enough fruit, vegetables and foods with whole grain are more likely to develop chronic diseases like diabetes, heart diseases and cancer.
