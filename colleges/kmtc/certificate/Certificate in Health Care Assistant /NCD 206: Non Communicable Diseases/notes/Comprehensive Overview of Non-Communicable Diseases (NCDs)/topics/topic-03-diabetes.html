üìñ Definition

Diabetes mellitus is a group of metabolic diseases characterized by high blood sugar levels due to insulin deficiency or resistance. It affects energy utilization and can cause long-term complications if uncontrolled.

02

üß¨ Types of Diabetes

  • Type 1 Diabetes: Autoimmune destruction of insulin-producing cells; often develops in childhood.
  • Type 2 Diabetes: Insulin resistance with relative insulin deficiency; linked to obesity and lifestyle.
  • Gestational Diabetes: Occurs during pregnancy; may resolve postpartum but increases future risk.
03

üîç Causes

  • Type 1: Autoimmune, genetic susceptibility, environmental triggers.
  • Type 2: Genetic factors, obesity, physical inactivity, unhealthy diets.
  • Gestational: Hormonal changes during pregnancy impair insulin action.
04

‚ö†Ô∏è Signs and Symptoms

  • Increased thirst and urination
  • Extreme hunger
  • Unexplained weight loss
  • Fatigue and irritability
  • Blurred vision
  • Slow-healing wounds
  • Infections
05

üìä Risk Factors

  • Family history
  • Obesity
  • Sedentary lifestyle
  • Unhealthy diet
  • Age (more common over 40)
  • Gestational diabetes history
  • Certain ethnicities (e.g., Hispanic, African American, Asian)
06

üöë Complications

  • Cardiovascular diseases
  • Nerve damage (neuropathy)
  • Kidney failure (nephropathy)
  • Eye damage (retinopathy)
  • Foot ulcers and amputations
  • Skin infections
  • Increased risk of dementia
