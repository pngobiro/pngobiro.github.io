üìñ Overview

Cancer involves uncontrolled cell growth capable of spreading to other parts of the body. It is the second leading cause of death worldwide. Early detection and lifestyle changes are crucial for prevention.

02

ü¶† Major Types and Sites

  • Cervical Cancer: Linked to HPV; preventable via vaccination and screening.
  • Non-melanoma Skin Cancer: Common, affects sun-exposed areas; signs include persistent sores and scaly patches.
  • Breast Cancer: Signs include lumps, skin changes, nipple abnormalities.
  • Lung Cancer: Often asymptomatic early; symptoms include persistent cough and blood in sputum.
  • Prostate Cancer: Symptoms include urinary issues and pelvic pain.
  • Colorectal Cancer: Changes in bowel habits, blood in stool.
  • Leukemia: Blood and bone marrow disorder; symptoms include fatigue, bleeding.
  • Lymphoma: Affects lymph nodes; symptoms include swelling and fever.
  • Multiple Myeloma: Affects plasma cells; causes bone pain and anemia.
  • Melanoma: Skin cancer originating from melanocytes; signs include new or changing moles.
03

üîç Causes

  • Gene mutations disrupting normal cell regulation
  • Environmental factors: Tobacco, radiation, chemicals, infections
  • Lifestyle factors: Poor diet, physical inactivity, alcohol, and tobacco use
  • Infections: HPV, hepatitis B/C, H. pylori
