ü©∫ Definition

CVDs are disorders of the heart and blood vessels, including conditions like coronary artery disease, strokes, and rheumatic heart disease. They are primarily caused by blockages due to fatty deposits or blood clots, leading to acute events such as heart attacks and strokes.

02

ü´Ä Types of CVDs

  • Coronary heart disease: Affects blood vessels supplying the heart; causes angina and heart attacks.
  • Cerebrovascular disease: Affects brain blood vessels; leads to strokes.
  • Peripheral arterial disease: Affects vessels supplying limbs; causes pain and ischemia.
  • Rheumatic heart disease: Damage from rheumatic fever affecting heart valves.
  • Congenital heart disease: Structural defects present at birth.
  • Deep vein thrombosis and pulmonary embolism: Blood clots in leg veins and lungs.
03

üîç Causes

  • Lifestyle factors: Unhealthy diet, physical inactivity, smoking, excessive alcohol, and obesity.
  • Underlying conditions: Diabetes, hypertension, high cholesterol.
  • Other determinants: Poverty, stress, hereditary factors, globalization, and urbanization.
