<div data-testid="stHeadingWithActionElements" class="st-emotion-cache-1p9ibxm e1icttdg2"><h1 level="1" id="comprehensive-guide-to-non-communicable-diseases-ncds">Comprehensive Guide to Non-Communicable Diseases (NCDs)<span data-testid="stHeaderActionElements" class="st-emotion-cache-gi0tri e1icttdg3"><a href="#comprehensive-guide-to-non-communicable-diseases-ncds" class="st-emotion-cache-yinll1 e1icttdg1"><svg xmlns="http://www.w3.org/2000/svg" width="16" height="16" viewBox="0 0 24 24" fill="none" stroke="currentColor" stroke-width="2" stroke-linecap="round" stroke-linejoin="round"><path d="M15 7h3a5 5 0 0 1 5 5 5 5 0 0 1-5 5h-3m-6 0H6a5 5 0 0 1-5-5 5 5 0 0 1 5-5h3"></path><line x1="8" y1="12" x2="16" y2="12"></line></svg></a></span></h1></div>
<div data-testid="stHeadingWithActionElements" class="st-emotion-cache-1p9ibxm e1icttdg2"><h2 level="2" id="definition-and-overview-of-non-communicable-diseases-ncds">Definition and Overview of Non-Communicable Diseases (NCDs)<span data-testid="stHeaderActionElements" class="st-emotion-cache-gi0tri e1icttdg3"><a href="#definition-and-overview-of-non-communicable-diseases-ncds" class="st-emotion-cache-yinll1 e1icttdg1"><svg xmlns="http://www.w3.org/2000/svg" width="16" height="16" viewBox="0 0 24 24" fill="none" stroke="currentColor" stroke-width="2" stroke-linecap="round" stroke-linejoin="round"><path d="M15 7h3a5 5 0 0 1 5 5 5 5 0 0 1-5 5h-3m-6 0H6a5 5 0 0 1-5-5 5 5 0 0 1 5-5h3"></path><line x1="8" y1="12" x2="16" y2="12"></line></svg></a></span></h2></div>
<p>Non-communicable diseases (NCDs) are <strong>chronic conditions</strong> that do not result from <strong>acute infectious processes</strong> and are generally <strong>not transmissible</strong> from person to person. They typically develop <strong>gradually over time</strong>, often without initial symptoms, but eventually cause <strong>death, dysfunction, or impairment</strong> in quality of life. Globally, NCDs are the leading causes of death, accounting for approximately 71% of all deaths annually, which amounts to about 41 million deaths each year. The major NCDs include <strong>cardiovascular diseases</strong>, <strong>cancers</strong>, <strong>diabetes</strong>, and <strong>chronic respiratory diseases</strong>. They pose a significant health challenge, especially in low- and middle-income countries, where nearly 80% of NCD deaths occur, emphasizing the importance of prevention, early detection, and management.</p>
<div data-testid="stHeadingWithActionElements" class="st-emotion-cache-1p9ibxm e1icttdg2"><h2 level="2" id="classification-of-ncds">Classification of NCDs<span data-testid="stHeaderActionElements" class="st-emotion-cache-gi0tri e1icttdg3"><a href="#classification-of-ncds" class="st-emotion-cache-yinll1 e1icttdg1"><svg xmlns="http://www.w3.org/2000/svg" width="16" height="16" viewBox="0 0 24 24" fill="none" stroke="currentColor" stroke-width="2" stroke-linecap="round" stroke-linejoin="round"><path d="M15 7h3a5 5 0 0 1 5 5 5 5 0 0 1-5 5h-3m-6 0H6a5 5 0 0 1-5-5 5 5 0 0 1 5-5h3"></path><line x1="8" y1="12" x2="16" y2="12"></line></svg></a></span></h2></div>
<p>NCDs are classified into several major categories based on their nature and affected systems:</p>
<ul>
<li><strong>Cardiovascular diseases (CVDs)</strong>: Disorders of the heart and blood vessels, including heart attacks and strokes.</li>
<li><strong>Cancers</strong>: Uncontrolled growth and spread of abnormal cells, affecting various organs.</li>
<li><strong>Chronic respiratory diseases</strong>: Conditions like COPD and asthma affecting the lungs and airways.</li>
</ul>

<ul>
<li>Diabetes mellitus: Disorders affecting blood sugar regulation, primarily type 1, type 2, and gestational diabetes.</li>
<li>Nutritional diseases: Conditions such as scurvy, rickets, goiter, beriberi, pellagra, and dental caries caused by nutritional deficiencies.</li>
<li>Congenital problems: Birth defects like congenital heart diseases, spinal bifida, and Down's syndrome.</li>
<li>Mental illnesses: Disorders like psychosis, neurosis, anxiety, depression, and puerperal psychosis.</li>
<li>Accidents: Road traffic, industrial, and home accidents.</li>
<li>Degenerative diseases: Conditions such as arthritis.</li>
</ul>
<p>This broad classification underscores the diversity of NCDs and the need for targeted prevention and control strategies for each category.</p>
<div data-testid="stHeadingWithActionElements" class="st-emotion-cache-1p9ibxm e1icttdg2"><h1 level="1" id="epidemiology-of-ncds">Epidemiology of NCDs<span data-testid="stHeaderActionElements" class="st-emotion-cache-gi0tri e1icttdg3"><a href="#epidemiology-of-ncds" class="st-emotion-cache-yinll1 e1icttdg1"><svg xmlns="http://www.w3.org/2000/svg" width="16" height="16" viewBox="0 0 24 24" fill="none" stroke="currentColor" stroke-width="2" stroke-linecap="round" stroke-linejoin="round"><path d="M15 7h3a5 5 0 0 1 5 5 5 5 0 0 1-5 5h-3m-6 0H6a5 5 0 0 1-5-5 5 5 0 0 1 5-5h3"></path><line x1="8" y1="12" x2="16" y2="12"></line></svg></a></span></h1></div>
<p>NCDs are responsible for approximately 71% of all global deaths, translating to 41 million deaths annually. Notably:</p>
<ul>
<li>Over 15 million people die prematurely (between ages 30-69) from NCDs each year.</li>
<li>85% of these premature deaths occur in low- and middle-income countries.</li>
<li>In Africa, NCDs caused about 2.8 million deaths in 2008, with a rising trend projected to make NCDs the leading cause of death by 2030.</li>
<li>More than 62% of deaths in individuals over 45 years are attributable to NCDs.</li>
</ul>
<p>The epidemiological data highlight the urgent need for effective prevention, early detection, and management programs, especially in resource-limited settings.</p>

<div data-testid="stHeadingWithActionElements" class="st-emotion-cache-1p9ibxm e1icttdg2"><h1 level="1" id="risk-factors-for-ncds">Risk Factors for NCDs<span data-testid="stHeaderActionElements" class="st-emotion-cache-gi0tri e1icttdg3"><a href="#risk-factors-for-ncds" class="st-emotion-cache-yinll1 e1icttdg1"><svg xmlns="http://www.w3.org/2000/svg" width="16" height="16" viewBox="0 0 24 24" fill="none" stroke="currentColor" stroke-width="2" stroke-linecap="round" stroke-linejoin="round"><path d="M15 7h3a5 5 0 0 1 5 5 5 5 0 0 1-5 5h-3m-6 0H6a5 5 0 0 1-5-5 5 5 0 0 1 5-5h3"></path><line x1="8" y1="12" x2="16" y2="12"></line></svg></a></span></h1></div>
<p>Risk factors for NCDs are broadly categorized into <strong>behavioral</strong> and <strong>metabolic/physiological</strong> factors:</p>
<div data-testid="stHeadingWithActionElements" class="st-emotion-cache-1p9ibxm e1icttdg2"><h2 level="2" id="behavioral-risk-factors">Behavioral Risk Factors<span data-testid="stHeaderActionElements" class="st-emotion-cache-gi0tri e1icttdg3"><a href="#behavioral-risk-factors" class="st-emotion-cache-yinll1 e1icttdg1"><svg xmlns="http://www.w3.org/2000/svg" width="16" height="16" viewBox="0 0 24 24" fill="none" stroke="currentColor" stroke-width="2" stroke-linecap="round" stroke-linejoin="round"><path d="M15 7h3a5 5 0 0 1 5 5 5 5 0 0 1-5 5h-3m-6 0H6a5 5 0 0 1-5-5 5 5 0 0 1 5-5h3"></path><line x1="8" y1="12" x2="16" y2="12"></line></svg></a></span></h2></div>
<ul>
<li><strong>Tobacco use and exposure</strong>: Smoking causes about 71% of lung cancers, 42% of chronic respiratory diseases, and nearly 10% of cardiovascular diseases. The prevalence of smoking is increasing among youth, including young girls, especially in developing countries, driven by aggressive marketing by tobacco companies.</li>
<li><strong>Insufficient physical activity</strong>: About 3.2 million deaths annually are linked to physical inactivity. Regular physical activity reduces risks of cardiovascular diseases, diabetes, certain cancers, and depression.</li>
<li><strong>Harmful use of alcohol</strong>: Responsible for approximately 2.3 million deaths per year, alcohol consumption is linked to cancers, liver cirrhosis, and cardiovascular problems.</li>
<li><strong>Unhealthy diet</strong>: High salt intake, saturated fats, trans fats, and low fruit and vegetable consumption contribute to high blood pressure, obesity, and cardiovascular risk.</li>
</ul>
<div data-testid="stHeadingWithActionElements" class="st-emotion-cache-1p9ibxm e1icttdg2"><h2 level="2" id="metabolic-physiological-risk-factors">Metabolic/Physiological Risk Factors<span data-testid="stHeaderActionElements" class="st-emotion-cache-gi0tri e1icttdg3"><a href="#metabolic-physiological-risk-factors" class="st-emotion-cache-yinll1 e1icttdg1"><svg xmlns="http://www.w3.org/2000/svg" width="16" height="16" viewBox="0 0 24 24" fill="none" stroke="currentColor" stroke-width="2" stroke-linecap="round" stroke-linejoin="round"><path d="M15 7h3a5 5 0 0 1 5 5 5 5 0 0 1-5 5h-3m-6 0H6a5 5 0 0 1-5-5 5 5 0 0 1 5-5h3"></path><line x1="8" y1="12" x2="16" y2="12"></line></svg></a></span></h2></div>
<ul>
<li><strong>Raised blood pressure</strong>: Causes an estimated 7.5 million deaths annually and is a major risk factor for cardiovascular disease.</li>
<li><strong>Overweight and obesity</strong>: Responsible for at least 2.8 million deaths each year; increasing BMI elevates the risk of heart disease, stroke, diabetes, and certain cancers.</li>
<li><strong>Raised cholesterol</strong>: Contributes to 2.6 million deaths annually, increasing the risk of heart disease and stroke.</li>
</ul>

<ul>
<li>Cancer-associated infections: Infections like human papillomavirus (HPV), Hepatitis B and C, and Helicobacter pylori are responsible for a significant proportion of cancers globally. These infections are largely preventable through vaccination and hygiene measures.</li>
</ul>
<div data-testid="stHeadingWithActionElements" class="st-emotion-cache-1p9ibxm e1icttdg2"><h1 level="1" id="impact-and-effects-of-ncds">Impact and Effects of NCDs<span data-testid="stHeaderActionElements" class="st-emotion-cache-gi0tri e1icttdg3"><a href="#impact-and-effects-of-ncds" class="st-emotion-cache-yinll1 e1icttdg1"><svg xmlns="http://www.w3.org/2000/svg" width="16" height="16" viewBox="0 0 24 24" fill="none" stroke="currentColor" stroke-width="2" stroke-linecap="round" stroke-linejoin="round"><path d="M15 7h3a5 5 0 0 1 5 5 5 5 0 0 1-5 5h-3m-6 0H6a5 5 0 0 1-5-5 5 5 0 0 1 5-5h3"></path><line x1="8" y1="12" x2="16" y2="12"></line></svg></a></span></h1></div>
<p>NCDs impose a heavy burden on individuals, families, and health systems:</p>
<ul>
<li>Financial hardship: Long-term treatment costs lead to poverty, especially affecting low-income households.</li>
<li>Healthcare costs: Extended and expensive treatments, hospitalizations, and management of complications increase economic strain.</li>
<li>Family and community impact: Illness can lead to children dropping out of school, reduced household income, and decreased productivity.</li>
<li>Quality of life: Chronic pain, disability, and mental health issues diminish overall well-being.</li>

<div data-testid="stHeadingWithActionElements" class="st-emotion-cache-1p9ibxm e1icttdg2"><h2 level="2" id="definition-and-overview-of-non-communicable-diseases-ncds">Definition and Overview of Non-Communicable Diseases (NCDs)<span data-testid="stHeaderActionElements" class="st-emotion-cache-gi0tri e1icttdg3"><a href="#definition-and-overview-of-non-communicable-diseases-ncds" class="st-emotion-cache-yinll1 e1icttdg1"><svg xmlns="http://www.w3.org/2000/svg" width="16" height="16" viewBox="0 0 24 24" fill="none" stroke="currentColor" stroke-width="2" stroke-linecap="round" stroke-linejoin="round"><path d="M15 7h3a5 5 0 0 1 5 5 5 5 0 0 1-5 5h-3m-6 0H6a5 5 0 0 1-5-5 5 5 0 0 1 5-5h3"></path><line x1="8" y1="12" x2="16" y2="12"></line></svg></a></span></h2></div>
<p>Non-communicable diseases (NCDs) are chronic conditions that do not result from acute infectious processes and are generally not transmissible from person to person. They typically develop gradually</p>
<p>Downloaded by Patrick Ngobiro (<a href="mailto:kenyaewakili@gmail.com" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">kenyaewakili@gmail.com</a>)</p>
<p>over time, often without initial symptoms, but eventually cause death, dysfunction, or impairment in quality of life. Globally, NCDs are the leading causes of death, accounting for approximately 71% of all deaths annually, which amounts to about 41 million deaths each year. The major NCDs include cardiovascular diseases, cancers, diabetes, and chronic respiratory diseases. They pose a significant health challenge, especially in low- and middle-income countries, where nearly 80% of NCD deaths occur, emphasizing the importance of prevention, early detection, and management.</p>

<p>NCDs are classified into several major categories based on their nature and affected systems:</p>
<ul>
<li>Cardiovascular diseases (CVDs): Disorders of the heart and blood vessels, including heart attacks and strokes.</li>
<li>Cancers: Uncontrolled growth and spread of abnormal cells, affecting various organs.</li>
<li>Chronic respiratory diseases: Conditions like COPD and asthma affecting the lungs and airways.</li>
<li>Diabetes mellitus: Disorders affecting blood sugar regulation, primarily type 1, type 2, and gestational diabetes.</li>
<li>Nutritional diseases: Conditions such as scurvy, rickets, goiter, beriberi, pellagra, and dental caries caused by nutritional deficiencies.</li>
<li>Congenital problems: Birth defects like congenital heart diseases, spinal bifida, and Down's syndrome.</li>
<li>Mental illnesses: Disorders like psychosis, neurosis, anxiety, depression, and puerperal psychosis.</li>
<li>Accidents: Road traffic, industrial, and home accidents.</li>
</ul>

<ul>
<li>Degenerative diseases: Conditions such as arthritis.</li>
</ul>
<p>This broad classification underscores the diversity of NCDs and the need for targeted prevention and control strategies for each category.</p>
<div data-testid="stHeadingWithActionElements" class="st-emotion-cache-1p9ibxm e1icttdg2"><h1 level="1" id="epidemiology-of-ncds">Epidemiology of NCDs<span data-testid="stHeaderActionElements" class="st-emotion-cache-gi0tri e1icttdg3"><a href="#epidemiology-of-ncds" class="st-emotion-cache-yinll1 e1icttdg1"><svg xmlns="http://www.w3.org/2000/svg" width="16" height="16" viewBox="0 0 24 24" fill="none" stroke="currentColor" stroke-width="2" stroke-linecap="round" stroke-linejoin="round"><path d="M15 7h3a5 5 0 0 1 5 5 5 5 0 0 1-5 5h-3m-6 0H6a5 5 0 0 1-5-5 5 5 0 0 1 5-5h3"></path><line x1="8" y1="12" x2="16" y2="12"></line></svg></a></span></h1></div>
<p>NCDs are responsible for approximately 71% of all global deaths, translating to 41 million deaths annually. Notably:</p>
<ul>
<li>Over 15 million people die prematurely (between ages 30-69) from NCDs each year.</li>
<li>85% of these premature deaths occur in low- and middle-income countries.</li>
<li>In Africa, NCDs caused about 2.8 million deaths in 2008, with a rising trend projected to make NCDs the leading cause of death by 2030.</li>
<li>More than 62% of deaths in individuals over 45 years are attributable to NCDs.</li>
</ul>
<p>The epidemiological data highlight the urgent need for effective prevention, early detection, and management programs, especially in resource-limited settings.</p>
<div data-testid="stHeadingWithActionElements" class="st-emotion-cache-1p9ibxm e1icttdg2"><h1 level="1" id="risk-factors-for-ncds">Risk Factors for NCDs<span data-testid="stHeaderActionElements" class="st-emotion-cache-gi0tri e1icttdg3"><a href="#risk-factors-for-ncds" class="st-emotion-cache-yinll1 e1icttdg1"><svg xmlns="http://www.w3.org/2000/svg" width="16" height="16" viewBox="0 0 24 24" fill="none" stroke="currentColor" stroke-width="2" stroke-linecap="round" stroke-linejoin="round"><path d="M15 7h3a5 5 0 0 1 5 5 5 5 0 0 1-5 5h-3m-6 0H6a5 5 0 0 1-5-5 5 5 0 0 1 5-5h3"></path><line x1="8" y1="12" x2="16" y2="12"></line></svg></a></span></h1></div>
<p>Risk factors for NCDs are broadly categorized into behavioral and metabolic/physiological factors:</p>
<div data-testid="stHeadingWithActionElements" class="st-emotion-cache-1p9ibxm e1icttdg2"><h1 level="1" id="behavioral-risk-factors">Behavioral Risk Factors<span data-testid="stHeaderActionElements" class="st-emotion-cache-gi0tri e1icttdg3"><a href="#behavioral-risk-factors" class="st-emotion-cache-yinll1 e1icttdg1"><svg xmlns="http://www.w3.org/2000/svg" width="16" height="16" viewBox="0 0 24 24" fill="none" stroke="currentColor" stroke-width="2" stroke-linecap="round" stroke-linejoin="round"><path d="M15 7h3a5 5 0 0 1 5 5 5 5 0 0 1-5 5h-3m-6 0H6a5 5 0 0 1-5-5 5 5 0 0 1 5-5h3"></path><line x1="8" y1="12" x2="16" y2="12"></line></svg></a></span></h1></div>
<ul>
<li>Tobacco use and exposure: Smoking causes about 71% of lung cancers, 42% of chronic respiratory diseases, and nearly 10% of cardiovascular diseases. The prevalence of smoking is increasing among youth, including young girls, especially in developing countries, driven by aggressive marketing by tobacco companies.</li>
</ul>

<ul>
<li>Insufficient physical activity: About 3.2 million deaths annually are linked to physical inactivity. Regular physical activity reduces risks of cardiovascular diseases, diabetes, certain cancers, and depression.</li>
<li>Harmful use of alcohol: Responsible for approximately 2.3 million deaths per year, alcohol consumption is linked to cancers, liver cirrhosis, and cardiovascular problems.</li>
<li>Unhealthy diet: High salt intake, saturated fats, trans fats, and low fruit and vegetable consumption contribute to high blood pressure, obesity, and cardiovascular risk.</li>

<div data-testid="stHeadingWithActionElements" class="st-emotion-cache-1p9ibxm e1icttdg2"><h2 level="2" id="metabolic-physiological-risk-factors">Metabolic/Physiological Risk Factors<span data-testid="stHeaderActionElements" class="st-emotion-cache-gi0tri e1icttdg3"><a href="#metabolic-physiological-risk-factors" class="st-emotion-cache-yinll1 e1icttdg1"><svg xmlns="http://www.w3.org/2000/svg" width="16" height="16" viewBox="0 0 24 24" fill="none" stroke="currentColor" stroke-width="2" stroke-linecap="round" stroke-linejoin="round"><path d="M15 7h3a5 5 0 0 1 5 5 5 5 0 0 1-5 5h-3m-6 0H6a5 5 0 0 1-5-5 5 5 0 0 1 5-5h3"></path><line x1="8" y1="12" x2="16" y2="12"></line></svg></a></span></h2></div>
<ul>
<li>Raised blood pressure: Causes an estimated 7.5 million deaths annually and is a major risk factor for cardiovascular disease.</li>
<li>Overweight and obesity: Responsible for at least 2.8 million deaths each year; increasing BMI elevates the risk of heart disease, stroke, diabetes, and certain cancers.</li>
<li>Raised cholesterol: Contributes to 2.6 million deaths annually, increasing the risk of heart disease and stroke.</li>
<li>Cancer-associated infections: Infections like human papillomavirus (HPV), Hepatitis B and C, and Helicobacter pylori are responsible for a significant proportion of cancers globally. These infections are largely preventable through vaccination and hygiene measures.</li>
</ul>
