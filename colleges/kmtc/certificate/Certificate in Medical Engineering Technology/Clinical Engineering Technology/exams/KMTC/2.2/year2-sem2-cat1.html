1.
In reference to baby weighing scales define the following terms: (4 marks)
a. Accuracy
b. Taring
c. Corner load
d. Precision
Show Answer
  • a. Accuracy: The closeness of the measured weight to the true or actual weight of the baby.
  • b. Taring: The action of setting the scale display to zero to subtract the weight of containers, blankets, or diapers so that only the baby's net weight is measured.
  • c. Corner load (Eccentricity): The ability of the scale to display the same weight reading regardless of where the load (baby) is placed on the weighing pan (center vs corners).
  • d. Precision (Repeatability): The ability of the scale to provide consistent results when the same baby is weighed multiple times under the same conditions.

3.
Name and explain three performance tests carried to check the accuracy of the baby weighing scale. (6 marks)
Show Answer
  1. Linearity Test: Verifies accuracy across the full range of the scale. Calibrated weights (e.g., 2kg, 5kg, 10kg) are added incrementally to ensure the scale reads correctly at each step.
  2. Repeatability (Precision) Test: Placing the same weight on the scale multiple times to ensure it gives the same reading every time.
  3. Eccentricity (Corner Load) Test: Placing a weight (usually 1/3 of max capacity) at the center and then at the four corners of the pan to ensure the reading remains within tolerance regardless of position.
4.
With the aid of circuit diagram, explain the principle of operation of strain gauge weighing scale. (8 marks)
Show Answer

Principle: It operates based on the principle that the electrical resistance of a conductor changes when it is mechanically deformed (stretched or compressed). Strain gauges are bonded to a load cell (metal element).

Circuit (Wheatstone Bridge):

      V_excitation
          |
      +---+---+
      |       |
     R1      R2
      |       |
A ----+-------+---- B  (V_out)
      |       |
     R3      R4 (Strain Gauges)
      |       |
      +---+---+
          |
         GND

Operation:

  1. Four strain gauges are arranged in a Wheatstone bridge configuration.
  2. When no load is applied, the bridge is balanced ($V_{out} = 0$).
  3. When a baby is placed on the scale, the load cell deforms.
  4. This deformation causes the strain gauges to stretch or compress, changing their resistance ($\Delta R$).
  5. The bridge becomes unbalanced, producing a small output voltage ($V_{out}$) proportional to the weight.
  6. This voltage is amplified and converted to a digital reading.
5.
Give four ways of achieving sterility in baby incubator. (4 marks)
Show Answer
  1. Micro-filtration: Incoming air passes through a bacterial filter (micro-filter) to remove dust and airborne pathogens.
  2. Use of Distilled/Sterile Water: In the humidification reservoir to prevent bacterial breeding (e.g., Pseudomonas).
  3. Regular Cleaning/Disinfection: Routine cleaning of the hood, mattress, and water reservoir with approved hospital-grade disinfectants between patients.
  4. Isolation: The enclosed canopy physically isolates the infant from the external environment and potential cross-contamination.
  5. Changing Filters: Periodic replacement of air filters as per maintenance schedule.

8.
Name four maintenance procedure carried on baby weighing scale after six months. (4 marks)
Show Answer
  1. Calibration Verification: Checking accuracy with standard weights and re-calibrating if necessary.
  2. Physical Inspection: Checking for loose screws, damaged pan, or worn feet pads.
  3. Battery/Power Check: Inspecting internal rechargeable batteries for leakage or capacity loss; checking power cord integrity.
  4. Zero Point Adjustment: Ensuring the scale returns to exactly zero when unloaded.
  5. Cleaning: Deep cleaning of the chassis and load cell area (removal of dust/debris affecting mechanism).

Give one advantage and disadvantage of using electrode as a way on monitoring the fetus. (2 marks)
Show Answer

Refers to Fetal Scalp Electrode (Internal Monitoring)

Advantage: Provides a continuous, highly accurate ECG signal (R-R interval) that is not affected by maternal or fetal movement/obesity.

Disadvantage: Invasive procedure requiring ruptured membranes; carries a small risk of infection or injury to the fetal scalp.

12.
With aid of circuit diagram, explain how the thermostat switches on and off the heater. (5 marks)
Show Answer

Explanation: A thermostat typically uses a bimetallic strip (two metals with different expansion coefficients bonded together). When the temperature rises, the strip bends away from the contact point, breaking the circuit and turning off the heater. When it cools, it straightens, closing the contact and turning the heater back on.

14.
Name four factors that determines the effect of current on the mouth cavity during dental surgery. (4 marks)
Show Answer

Refers to electrosurgery/cautery risks or electrical safety:

  1. Current Density: Concentration of current (small contact area = high heat).
  2. Duration of Contact: Longer time increases tissue heating/damage.
  3. Frequency of Current: High frequency avoids neuromuscular stimulation (shock).
  4. Moisture/Saliva: Wet environment lowers resistance, altering current path.
  5. Tissue Resistance: Different tissues (gum vs bone) offer different resistance.
15.
Name three advantages of micro-motor handpiece over air driven handpiece. (3 marks)
Show Answer
  1. Constant Torque: Electric motors maintain torque/cutting power even at low speeds or under load (air turbines stall).
  2. Precise Speed Control: Allows exact RPM selection for different procedures.
  3. Less Noise and Vibration: Smoother operation improves patient comfort and precision.
  4. Versatility: Can run very slowly for polishing or fast for cutting.
16.
Name five maintenance procedures carried on dental hand pieces. (5 marks)
Show Answer
  1. External Cleaning: Wiping down with alcohol/disinfectant after use.
  2. Lubrication (Oiling): Spraying lubricant into the drive air line to oil bearings/turbines before sterilization.
  3. Sterilization (Autoclaving): Putting in autoclave to kill pathogens.
  4. Check Chuck/Bur Retention: Ensuring the bur is held tightly.
  5. Run-out check: Ensuring the bur rotates concentrically without wobble.
  6. Cleaning Optical Fibers: Wiping light ports to maintain brightness.
