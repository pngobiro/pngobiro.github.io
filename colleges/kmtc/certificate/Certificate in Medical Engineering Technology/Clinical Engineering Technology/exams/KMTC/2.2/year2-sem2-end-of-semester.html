Section I: Multiple Choice Questions

1.
The image formed by the objective lens of a compound microscope is?
A. Virtual
B. Virtual and diminished
C. Real and diminished
D. Real and enlarged
Show Answer

Correct Answer: D. Real and enlarged

Explanation: The objective lens creates a real, inverted, and magnified image of the specimen, which is then further magnified by the eyepiece (which creates a virtual image).

2.

C. Condenser lens
D. Magnifying lens
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C. Condenser lens

Explanation: The condenser is located beneath the stage and concentrates light from the illuminator onto the specimen.

Mention the function of the following safety devices in autoclaves (5 marks)
i. Float switch
ii. Safety valve
iii. Release valve
iv. Thermal cutout
v. Pressure switch
Show Answer
  • Float switch: Protects heaters from dry burning by cutting power if water level is low.
  • Safety valve: Releases excess pressure mechanically if it exceeds the rated limit to prevent explosion.
  • Release valve: Manually releases pressure/steam from the chamber after cycle.
  • Thermal cutout: Over-temperature protection; cuts power if temp exceeds safety limit.
  • Pressure switch: Controls the heating element to maintain the desired operating pressure.

Section III: Long Answer Questions

1.
With the aid of a diagram explain the operation of flame photometer (10 marks)
Show Answer

Operation:

  1. Sample Aspiration: The liquid sample is drawn into the nebulizer by compressed air.
  2. Atomization: The sample is turned into a fine mist (aerosol) and mixed with fuel gas.
  3. Excitation: The mixture enters the flame. The heat evaporates the solvent and excites the metal atoms (e.g., Na, K) to higher energy levels.
  4. Emission: As atoms return to the ground state, they emit light of a characteristic wavelength (Color: Na=Yellow, K=Violet).
  5. Filtration: An optical filter selects the specific wavelength of interest.
  6. Detection: A photodetector measures the intensity of the light, which is proportional to the concentration of the element.
      [Sample] --> [Nebulizer] --> [Flame] --> [Filter] --> [Detector] --> [Display]
                       ^              ^
                       |              |
                    [Air]          [Fuel]

3.

Show Answer

Ray Diagram Description:

  1. Objective Lens: The object is placed just outside the focal point ($f_o$) of the objective lens. This forms a Real, Inverted, and Magnified intermediate image.
  2. Eyepiece Lens: This real image falls within the focal length ($f_e$) of the eyepiece lens. The eyepiece acts as a magnifying glass.
  3. Final Image: The eyepiece forms a Virtual, Erect (relative to intermediate), and Highly Magnified final image at the distance of distinct vision.

4.
With aid of a diagram explain how oxygen concentration is achieved in oxygen concentrator in hospital setup (10 marks)
Show Answer

Pressure Swing Adsorption (PSA) Process:

  1. Compression: Ambient air is compressed and cooled.
  2. Adsorption: Compressed air enters a sieve bed filled with Zeolite (Molecular Sieve). Under high pressure, Zeolite adsorbs Nitrogen, allowing Oxygen to pass through.
  3. Collection: The purified oxygen is collected in a product tank.
  4. Regeneration: The pressure is vented from the saturated sieve bed. Without pressure, the Nitrogen desorbs and is exhausted to the atmosphere.
  5. Cycling: The system switches between two sieve beds (one producing, one regenerating) to provide continuous oxygen flow.
5.
Name and explain four processes performed by surgical diathermy, and in each case provide the type of current used (8 marks)
Show Answer
  1. Cutting (Electrotomy): Uses continuous undamped high-frequency current (Sine wave). Intense heat vaporizes cells, slicing tissue.
  2. Coagulation: Uses damped/interrupted bursts of HF current. Slower heating dries out cells (desiccation) to seal blood vessels.
  3. Fulguration (Spray): Uses high-voltage, modulated current sparking to tissue (non-contact). Creates superficial charring/coagulation.
  4. Desiccation: Probe contacts tissue. Current dries out cells (dehydration). Can use Cut or Coag waveform but at lower power/contact.
