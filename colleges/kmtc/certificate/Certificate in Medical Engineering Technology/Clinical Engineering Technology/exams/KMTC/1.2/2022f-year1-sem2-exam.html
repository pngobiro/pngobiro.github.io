Section 1: Multiple Choice Questions

20 Marks
1.
What is the colour coding of nitrous oxide cylinder
a) Blue and white shoulder
b) yellow and white shoulder
c) black and white shoulder
d) grey and white shoulder
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Blue and white shoulder (Contextual: Entonox)

Explanation: Pure Nitrous Oxide cylinders are typically Blue. The option "Blue and white shoulder" corresponds to Entonox (a 50:50 mixture of Oxygen and Nitrous Oxide). Given the options, 'a' is the only one containing Blue (the N2O identifier), likely referring to the mixture often used for analgesia.

Note: Black/White shoulder is Oxygen (UK/ISO). Grey/White shoulder isn't standard (Grey is CO2).

2.
What is the main function of a flow meter?
a) Control flow of gases to the patient
b) Reduce the cylinder pressure
c) Releases excess pressure
d) To regulate pressure of the gas to the patient automatically
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Control flow of gases to the patient

Explanation: Flow meters (rotameters) are used to set and indicate the volumetric flow rate (Liters per minute) of gas delivered to the patient.

3.
Which statement best explains need of color code of medical gas pipe lines?
a) For beautification
b) To match with color of the gases
c) Logo used by the manufacturers
d) For easier identification
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) For easier identification

Explanation: Color coding is a critical safety standard to prevent cross-connection of gases (e.g., connecting Nitrogen instead of Oxygen), ensuring quick and accurate identification of gas lines.

4.
Which of the following statement is false for medical gases?
a) Oxygen is used to provide supplemental oxygen for respiratory system
b) Nitrogen is used to provide pneumatic pressure in medical equipment
c) Nitrous oxide is used to stimulate respiration
d) Carbon dioxide is used to inflate areas of the body for ‘keyhole’ surgery
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Nitrous oxide is used to stimulate respiration

Explanation: This is false. Nitrous oxide ($N_2O$) is an anesthetic and analgesic gas. Carbon Dioxide ($CO_2$) is the gas sometimes used to stimulate respiration (respiratory stimulant). Statement (d) is true (insufflation in laparoscopy).

5.
Which of the following parts and functions of oxygen concentrator were WRONGLY matched?
a) Cabinet filter.......................filters dust from cooling air
b) Inlet filter..............................filters particles to protect compressor
c) Compressor.............................pumps air into the system
d) Wick assembly.......................remove dust from the air
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Wick assembly.......................remove dust from the air

Explanation: A wick assembly is typically used in humidifiers to aid evaporation of water. Filters remove dust.

6.
If oxygen leaks from the safety valve of oxygen when the flow meter is closed, what could be the possible cause?
a) Leaking throttle valve
b) Excess oxygen in the cylinder
c) Body cover is leaking
d) Flow meter sight glass leaking
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Leaking throttle valve (Needle valve seat damage)

Explanation: If the flow meter is closed but pressure builds up to pop the safety valve (downstream), it suggests the regulator or flow valve is passing gas when it shouldn't, or there is an internal leak bypassing the control valve.

7.
In the design of a theatre room, who among the following personnel operate in the sterile zone?
a) The anesthetist
b) The theatre assistant
c) The theatre Bio-medical technician
d) The theatre nurse.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) The theatre nurse (Scrub Nurse)

Explanation: The Scrub Nurse and Surgeons work within the sterile field. The Anesthetist, Technician, and Circulating Nurse/Assistant typically operate in the non-sterile zone of the theatre.

8.
The following statements describe requirements for surgical theatre, which statement is true?
a) Three fluorescent tube lights arranged in triangle can give good light
b) Good and easily regulated ventilation
c) Insect screening is necessity
d) All of the above
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) All of the above

Explanation: Theatres require excellent lighting (A), strict ventilation (HEPA filters, positive pressure) (B), and must be sealed against pests (C).

9.
Vacuum is one of the supplies in the hospital departments, its pipeline is coded:
a) Blue
b) Yellow
c) Orange
d) Grey
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Yellow (ISO/British Standard)

Explanation: Medical Vacuum pipelines are color-coded Yellow. (White is O2, Black/White is Air, Blue is N2O).

10.
The following statements are true about operation theatre. Which one is not?
a) Should have few corners
b) Should be upstream of the wind
c) Should have air conditioners
d) Should be built near laboratory
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Should have few corners (Actually, "Should have ROUNDED corners" is the rule)

Explanation: Corners trap dust. OT walls usually have curved junctions (coving) to facilitate cleaning. Option B is about ventilation location. Option D is about workflow (close to pathology/blood bank). "Few corners" is vague; rounded corners are required. However, B is often cited as "Ideally not facing prevailing wind" for dust. Usually, the design avoids sharp corners.

13.
Mrs. Nema who works in the ENT section complained that her ophthalmoscope does not vary the brightness of the bulb, when switched ON it gives full light. What is the most probable fault?
a) Bulb used is meant for LOW voltage
b) Rheostat open circuited
c) Wrong size of batteries were used
d) Rheostat short circuited
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Rheostat short circuited

Explanation: The rheostat controls brightness by adding resistance. If it is short-circuited, resistance is zero, and full battery voltage is applied to the bulb continuously, resulting in maximum brightness that cannot be dimmed.

15.
Which of the following items cannot be safely used in the theatre?
a) Silk cloth
b) Safari boots
c) Studded boots
d) Antistatic rubber boots
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Silk cloth (and c)

Explanation: Silk generates significant static electricity, which is a hazard in operating theatres (fire risk with flammable gases, ESD damage). Studded boots can cause sparks. Antistatic rubber is the required standard.

20.
Which of the following is odd one out
a) theatre light
b) electrosurgical unit
c) baby incubator
d) anaesthetic machine
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) baby incubator

Explanation: Theatre light, ESU, and Anaesthetic machine are standard equipment found in the Operating Theatre. A baby incubator is found in the Nursery/NICU.

2.

a) Differentiate between systolic and diastolic blood pressure measurement. (2 mks)

b) State three types of blood pressure machines. (3 mks)

Show Answer

a) Difference:

  • Systolic: The peak pressure in the arteries during heart contraction (beat).
  • Diastolic: The lowest pressure in the arteries during heart relaxation (rest).

b) Types:

  1. Mercury Sphygmomanometer
  2. Aneroid Sphygmomanometer
  3. Digital (Electronic) Sphygmomanometer
3.

a) What are three effects of electric current on human tissues. (3 mks)

b) Differentiate between monopolar and bipolar surgical diathermy machines. (2 mks)

Show Answer

a) Effects:

  1. Thermal Effect: Heating (burns/coagulation).
  2. Stimulation (Faradic) Effect: Nerve/muscle contraction.
  3. Electrolytic Effect: Chemical changes (ion migration).

b) Difference:

  • Monopolar: Current flows from active electrode -> patient -> return plate -> machine. Used for large/general surgery.
  • Bipolar: Current flows between two tips of a forceps (no return plate). Used for delicate/microsurgery.
4.

a) What is the process of switching the sieve beds in oxygen concentrator (Pressure Swing Adsorption). (1 mk)

b) What is the function of switch valve in oxygen concentrator. (1 mk)

c) Explain how nitrogen is adsorbed in the first molecular sieve bed of an oxygen concentrator. (2 mks)

Show Answer

a) Process: Pressure Swing Adsorption (PSA) cycling.

b) Switch Valve: To alternate the airflow between the two sieve beds (directing compressed air to the producing bed and venting the regenerating bed).

c) Nitrogen Adsorption: Under high pressure, the Zeolite crystals in the sieve bed attract and trap (adsorb) Nitrogen molecules due to their polarity and size, while allowing Oxygen molecules to pass through.

6.

a) State the functions of manifold in the central medical gas supply. (2 mks)

b) State three risks of fire explosions in operating theatre. (3 mks)

Show Answer

a) Manifold Function: To connect multiple gas cylinders (banks) to a common supply line, ensuring a continuous supply by automatically switching from an empty bank to a full one.

b) Fire Risks:

  • Ignition Sources: Electrosurgery sparks, Lasers, Defibrillators.
  • Fuel: Alcohol prep solutions, surgical drapes, intestinal gases.
  • Oxidizers: Oxygen-enriched atmosphere, Nitrous Oxide.

Section 3: Long Answer Questions

40 Marks
1.

a) State and briefly explain any 5 supplies required in theatre. (10 mks)

b) State any five medical gases used to stimulate respiratory centre and their uses. (Note: Question text says "medical gases... and their uses") (10 mks)

c) What are four types of theatre operating tables. (4 mks)

d) State and explain any five requirements a theatre light should meet for it work optimally. (10 mks)

Show Answer

a) Theatre Supplies

  1. Surgical Instruments: Scalpels, forceps, retractors for performing procedures.
  2. Sterile Drapes/Gowns: To maintain the sterile field and protect staff.
  3. Sutures/Ligatures: For closing wounds.
  4. Anesthetics: Drugs for sedation and pain relief.
  5. Swabs/Gauze: For cleaning and hemostasis.

b) Medical Gases

  1. Oxygen ($O_2$): Life support, prevents hypoxia.
  2. Nitrous Oxide ($N_2O$): Analgesia and weak anesthesia.
  3. Medical Air: Driving pneumatic tools and blending with Oxygen.
  4. Carbon Dioxide ($CO_2$): Insufflation for laparoscopic surgery.
  5. Entonox ($O_2/N_2O$): Pain relief during labor/procedures.

c) Types of Operating Tables

  1. General Surgical Table
  2. Orthopedic Table (with traction attachments)
  3. Gynecological/Urological Table (with lithotomy stirrups)
  4. Neurological Table

d) Theatre Light Requirements

  1. Shadowless: Multiple light sources to prevent shadows from the surgeon's head/hands blocking the view.
  2. Cool Light (Heat Free): Dichroic reflectors to filter out infrared heat, preventing tissue drying and surgeon discomfort.
  3. High Intensity (Lux): Sufficient brightness (up to 160,000 Lux) to illuminate deep cavities.
  4. Color Temperature (CRI): Natural daylight color (~4500K) to allow accurate identification of tissue types/pathology.
  5. Maneuverability: Easy to position with sterile handles.
