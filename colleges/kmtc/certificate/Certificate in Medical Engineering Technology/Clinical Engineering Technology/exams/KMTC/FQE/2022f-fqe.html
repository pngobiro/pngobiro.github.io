1.
What is the maximum reading for BP machine?
a) 300mmHg
b) 3000mmHg
c) 200mmHg
d) 180mmHg
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) 300mmHg

Explanation: Standard medical mercury and aneroid sphygmomanometers are calibrated up to 300 mmHg, which is sufficient to measure human blood pressure, including severe hypertensive crises.

2.
Listed below are part of vacuum extractor, which one is not
a) Vacuum bottle
b) Pressure gauge
c) Vacuum regulator
d) Bacterial filter
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Pressure gauge (Contextual)

Explanation: While suction machines have vacuum gauges (measuring negative pressure), they do not technically have "pressure gauges" (which measure positive pressure). However, in common terminology, it's called a vacuum gauge. If strictly speaking about "Vacuum Extractors" (used in obstetrics), all listed components are present. The question might be referring to the fact that it measures vacuum (negative pressure) rather than positive pressure. Alternatively, some sources might list "Bacterial filter" as an accessory rather than a core part, but it is essential for safety. Based on typical exam patterns, distinguishing between "pressure" and "vacuum" gauges is a common trick.

3.
Which among the following is not a unit of air pressure measurement?
a) Bar
b) Psi
c) N/m²
d) mmHg
Show Answer

Correct Answer: All are valid units, but typically b) Psi is the odd one out in metric/SI contexts.

Explanation:

  • Bar: Metric unit of pressure.
  • Psi: Imperial unit (Pounds per square inch).
  • N/m²: SI unit (Pascal).
  • mmHg: Metric unit (manometric).
In many strict scientific contexts, Psi is not used, whereas the others are metric-based. However, mechanically, all are units of pressure.

5.
What causes the bulb of the BP machine to remain squeezed when squeezed?
a) Inlet valve blockage
b) Outlet valve blockage
c) Release valve blockage
d) None of the above
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Inlet valve blockage

Explanation: The bulb has a one-way intake valve (inlet) at the rear. If this valve is blocked, air cannot re-enter the bulb after it is squeezed, causing it to remain collapsed (negative pressure inside the bulb holds it flat).

Explanation: Sterility and hygiene in incubators are maintained by filtering intake air (micro filters), using sterile/distilled water in humidifiers to prevent bacterial growth, and designing the unit for easy cleaning (transparent canopy).

9.
Semi-permanent dirt in the optical section of the diagnostic set is removed by
a) Wire brush
b) Soft brush
c) Soaking in hydraulic acid
d) Soaking in xylene
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Soft brush (or appropriate lens cleaner)

Explanation: Optical components are delicate. Wire brushes would scratch them. Acids are too corrosive. Xylene is used for removing immersion oil, but general dirt is removed with a soft camel-hair brush or lens blower. "Semi-permanent" is an unusual term, but among options, the soft brush is the safest first step for optics.

14.
Why is it important to test between live and body of the equipment before you hand it over to the user
a) To ensure the equipment is safe
b) To ensure the equipment has enough current
c) To ensure the equipment has enough voltage

Correct Answer: a) To ensure the equipment is safe

Explanation: Testing insulation resistance between Live/Neutral and the Body (Chassis) ensures there is no electrical leakage that could shock the user. This is a primary electrical safety test.

20.
Biomedical engineering students on attachment at Kenyatta Hospital were required to replace a faulty power cable of a centrifuge. Which one below is the correct steps of ordering of the material
a) 1.5m2 twin with earth 2 meters
b) 1.5m2 two wire cable 2 meters
c) 2.5m2 twin with earth 2 meters long
d) 2.5m2 two wire cable 2 meters long
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) 2.5mm² twin with earth 2 meters long

Explanation: Centrifuges are motorized equipment that may draw significant starting current. A 2.5mm² cable is robust for such loads. Crucially, metal-cased medical equipment MUST have an earth connection ("twin with earth" implies Live, Neutral, Earth). Two-wire cables lack an earth and are unsafe for Class I equipment.

5.
Outline the five steps when checking the proper functioning of a blood pressure (BP) machine (5 marks)
Show Answer
  1. Zero Check: Ensure the needle is exactly at zero when no pressure is applied.
  2. Leak Test: Inflate cuff to ~200mmHg and check if pressure holds (drops < 4mmHg/min).
  3. Valve Function: Check that the release valve operates smoothly (controls deflation rate).
  4. Cuff/Bulb Condition: Inspect for cracks, wear, or blockages in the bulb inlet.
  5. Accuracy Check: Compare readings against a calibrated standard (master gauge) at multiple points (e.g., 50, 100, 150 mmHg).

Section 3: Long Answer Question

20 Marks
1.

a) Explain three functions of air compressor in a dental unit (3 marks)

b) Explain the importance of maternity bed (2 marks)

c) Explain how basic requirement of an infant incubator is achieved (8 marks)

d) Discuss maintenance done on electric suction machine made of vane pumps under the following major parts:
i. Pumps (3 marks)
ii. Motors (4 marks)

Show Answer

a) Functions of Air Compressor in Dental Unit

  1. Driving Pneumatic Tools: Provides compressed air to drive the turbine of the high-speed drill (handpiece).
  2. Drying/Clearing: Supplies air to the 3-way syringe for drying teeth or clearing debris from the working area.
  3. Operating Valves: Actuates pneumatic switches and valves within the dental chair control system.

b) Importance of Maternity Bed

Designed to support the mother during labor, delivery, and recovery. It is adjustable (Lithotomy position) to facilitate the delivery process and provide access for the medical team, ensuring safety and comfort for both mother and baby.

c) How Basic Requirements of Infant Incubator are Achieved

An incubator provides a controlled environment (NTE - Neutral Thermal Environment) for premature infants. The basic requirements are Temperature, Humidity, and Oxygen:

  • Temperature Control: Achieved via a heater element and a fan (forced convection). A temperature sensor (air probe or skin probe) provides feedback to a controller (thermostat/microcontroller) which cycles the heater to maintain the set temperature (Servo mode or Air mode).
  • Humidity Control: Achieved by passing air over a water reservoir or using an active humidifier/atomizer. This prevents the baby from dehydration (water loss through skin).
  • Oxygen Concentration: Achieved by admitting supplemental oxygen into the air intake. Monitored by an O2 sensor to prevent toxicity (Retinopathy of Prematurity) or hypoxia.
  • Air Filtration: Intake air passes through a bacterial filter to maintain a clean environment.

d) Maintenance of Electric Suction Machine (Vane Pump)

i. Pump Maintenance:

  • Cleaning/Inspection: Open pump head, clean vanes and rotor slots with solvent to remove gummy deposits.
  • Vane Replacement: Check vanes for wear/chipping. Replace if worn down beyond manufacturer limits.
  • Lubrication: If oil-sealed, check oil level and change oil periodically. If dry type, ensure graphite vanes are not sticking.

ii. Motor Maintenance:

  • Brush Inspection (if Universal/DC): Check carbon brushes for wear. Replace if too short. Clean commutator.
  • Bearings: Check for noise/play. Lubricate or replace sealed bearings if noisy.
  • Capacitor Check: For induction motors, test the start/run capacitor for proper capacitance.
  • Cooling: Ensure cooling fan is intact and vents are free of dust to prevent overheating.
