Section 1: Multiple Choice Questions

40 Marks
1.
What is the process of restoring the ion exchange resins of a demineralizer?
A. Softening
B. Restoration
C. Exhaustion
D. Regeneration
Show Answer

Correct Answer: D. Regeneration

Explanation: Regeneration involves washing the resin with a strong acid (for cation resin) or strong base (for anion resin) to replace the accumulated ions with Hydrogen ($H^+$) or Hydroxyl ($OH^-$) ions, restoring its capacity.

2.
What are the applications of centrifugation?
A. To separate two miscible substances...
B. To separate two miscible substances and water treatment
C. Purification of mammalian cells and water treatment
D. Analyse the hydro-dynamic properties of macromolecules and water treatment
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C. Purification of mammalian cells and water treatment (Context dependent)

Explanation: Centrifugation is widely used for separating cells, organelles, and macromolecules (like proteins/DNA) and in water treatment (sludge dewatering). Options involving "miscible" substances are incorrect as miscible fluids form a single phase.

4.
Direct blood pressure measurement is sometimes known as?
A. Auscultatory method
B. Palpatory method
C. Oscillometric method
D. Invasive method
Show Answer

Correct Answer: D. Invasive method

Explanation: Direct BP measurement involves inserting a catheter/cannula directly into an artery connected to a pressure transducer.

5.
Which one of the following is NOT a part of a diagnostic set?
A. The ophthalmoscope
B. The otoscope
C. The laryngoscope
D. The microscope
Show Answer

Correct Answer: D. The microscope

Explanation: A standard diagnostic set (ENT set) typically includes handheld instruments like otoscope, ophthalmoscope, and sometimes laryngoscope blades/handle. A microscope is a large standalone benchtop instrument.

6.
Which one of the following is not an electrical power source to the theatre lamp?
A. Generators
B. Batteries D.C
C. Conduction
D. Solar panel
Show Answer

Correct Answer: D. Solar panel

Explanation: While solar can charge batteries, direct solar power is not a standard reliable power source for critical theatre lamps in hospital infrastructure design. Mains (Conduction), Generators, and Battery backups are standard.

7.
How often should a weighing scale be calibrated?
A. Everyday
B. Every alternative day
C. Every week
D. Every month
Show Answer

Correct Answer: D. Every month (or A. Everyday zero check)

Explanation: Full calibration against standards is typically done periodically (e.g., monthly or annually). Daily checks are "verification" or "zeroing". In exam context, D is a common interval for maintenance checks.

8.
The active electrode of a monopolar diathermy produces:
A. High pressure
B. High volume
C. High current density
D. High velocity
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C. High current density

Explanation: The small surface area of the active electrode concentrates the current, creating high current density that generates the heat required for cutting or coagulation.

13.
Which of the following show the correct examples of limb leads?
A. aVR, V6
B. V1, V4
C. aVF, V2
D. aVF, aVR
Show Answer

Correct Answer: D. aVF, aVR

Explanation: The standard limb leads include the Bipolar leads (I, II, III) and Augmented Unipolar Limb leads (aVR, aVL, aVF). V1-V6 are Chest (Precordial) leads.

14.
Which of the following examines the eye?
A. Laryngoscope
B. Ophthalmoscope
C. Otoscope
D. None of the above
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B. Ophthalmoscope

Explanation: Ophthalmoscope is for the eye, Otoscope for the ear, Laryngoscope for the throat.

15.
Images formed by a plane mirror are?
A. Virtual
B. Real
C. Inverted
D. Reduced
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A. Virtual

Explanation: Plane mirrors form virtual, upright, laterally inverted images of the same size as the object.

Section 2: Short Answer Questions

40 Marks
1.

a) State three examples of diagnostic set and their functions. (3 marks)

b) What are any two maintenance procedures carried out on diagnostic set. (2 marks)

Show Answer

a) Examples:

  • Ophthalmoscope: For examining the interior of the eye (retina).
  • Otoscope: For examining the ear canal and tympanic membrane.
  • Laryngoscope: For viewing the larynx/vocal cords (often for intubation).

b) Maintenance:

  • Cleaning lenses with soft cloth/alcohol wipe.
  • Removing batteries if stored for long periods to prevent leakage.
  • Checking and replacing bulbs/LEDs.
2.

a) Differentiate between systolic and diastolic blood pressure measurement. (2 marks)

b) State three types of blood pressure machines. (3 marks)

Show Answer

a) Difference:

  • Systolic: The maximum pressure in the arteries when the heart contracts (beats).
  • Diastolic: The minimum pressure in the arteries when the heart rests between beats.

b) Types:

  1. Mercury Sphygmomanometer
  2. Aneroid Sphygmomanometer
  3. Digital (Electronic) Sphygmomanometer
3.

a) What are three effects of electric current on human tissues. (3 marks)

b) Differentiate between monopolar and bipolar surgical diathermy machines. (2 marks)

Show Answer

a) Effects:

  1. Thermal Effect: Heating of tissue (burns/coagulation).
  2. Electrolytic Effect: Chemical changes/ion movement.
  3. Stimulation Effect: Muscle contraction or nerve stimulation (shock).

b) Difference:

  • Monopolar: Current flows from active electrode, through the patient's body, to a large return plate (patient plate). Used for large incisions.
  • Bipolar: Current flows between two tips of a forceps; only the tissue between the tips is affected. No patient plate required. Used for delicate microsurgery.

d) State and explain any five requirements a theatre light should meet for it work optimally. (10 marks - Wait, total marks exceed 20? Section seems to have parts totaling more or choice? Let's implement Part D from handwriting notes).

Show Answer

d) Requirements for Theatre Light

  1. Shadow Free (Shadowless): Must minimize shadows cast by the surgeon's head and hands using multiple light sources/reflectors.
  2. Depth of Illumination: Must provide a cylindrical column of light to illuminate deep cavities without needing constant refocusing.
  3. Colour Temperature (High CRI): Must produce light close to daylight (~4000K-5000K) to render tissue colors accurately for diagnosis.
  4. Minimum Heat (Cool Light): Must filter out infrared radiation (heat) to prevent drying tissues or making the surgeon uncomfortable.
  5. Size of Illuminated Area: Spot size should be adjustable to match the surgical field.
  6. Easy Maneuverability: Must be easy to position and stable (drift-free).
  7. Hygiene/Sterility: Easy to clean, with detachable sterilizable handles.
