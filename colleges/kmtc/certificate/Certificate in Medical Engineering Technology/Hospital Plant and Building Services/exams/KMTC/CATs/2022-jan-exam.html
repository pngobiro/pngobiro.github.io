Section A: Multiple Choice Questions

20 Marks
1.
Steel pipes are classified into three grades. Which one is the odd one out?
A. Class B
B. Class A
C. Class C
D. Class D
Show Answer

Correct Answer: D. Class D

Explanation: Steel pipes (specifically galvanized iron pipes) are typically classified under BS 1387 into three grades based on wall thickness: Class A (Light, Yellow band), Class B (Medium, Blue band), and Class C (Heavy, Red band). Class D is not a standard grade in this classification.

2.
Which one of the following criteria is used to specify pipes used in water supply system?
A. Inside Diameter
B. Outside Diameter
C. Inside and Outside Diameter
D. Outside Bore
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A. Inside Diameter (Nominal Bore)

Explanation: Traditional steel and iron pipes are specified by their Nominal Bore (NB), which approximates the inside diameter. Modern plastic and copper pipes are often specified by Outside Diameter (OD), but given the context of "Outside Bore" (D) being an incorrect term, and "Inside Diameter" being the traditional flow specification, A is the most appropriate answer for general pipe sizing principles in this context.

3.
Which of the following taps are used on wash hand basins?
A. Stop Valves
B. Stop Cocks
C. Bib Taps
D. Pillar Taps
Show Answer

Correct Answer: D. Pillar Taps

Explanation: Pillar taps are designed to be mounted on the horizontal surface (deck) of the basin or bath. Bib taps (C) are wall-mounted. Stop valves/cocks are for isolation.

4.
Hard water contains high concentration of which mineral?
A. Iron and Aluminium
B. Chloride and Sulphate
C. Calcium and Magnesium
D. Chloride and Aluminium
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C. Calcium and Magnesium

Explanation: Water hardness is primarily caused by dissolved calcium ($Ca^{2+}$) and magnesium ($Mg^{2+}$) ions.

5.
Another name for check valve is
A. Foot valve
B. Non return valve
C. Ball cock
D. Float valve
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B. Non return valve

Explanation: A check valve is designed to allow fluid to flow in only one direction, preventing backflow. It is commonly known as a non-return valve (NRV).

9.
Which of the following appliances does not discharge soiled water?
A. Water closet
B. Urinal
C. Wash hand basin
D. None of the above
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C. Wash hand basin

Explanation: In plumbing, "Soil water" refers to discharge containing human excreta (from WCs and Urinals). "Waste water" refers to discharge from sinks, basins, baths, etc. Therefore, a Wash Hand Basin discharges waste water, not soil water.

11.
In designing a cold-water supply distribution, a ¾” supply pipe is recommended to supply?
A. Four ½” diameter branches
B. One 1” diameter pipe branch
C. Two ¼” diameter pipe branches
D. Two ½” diameter pipe branches
Show Answer

Correct Answer: D. Two ½” diameter pipe branches

Explanation: Based on cross-sectional area, a 3/4" pipe has roughly twice the capacity of a 1/2" pipe ($0.75^2 / 0.5^2 = 2.25$). Thus, it can adequately supply two 1/2" branches.

12.
Which of the following statements should not be taken into consideration in the design of a drainage system?
A. The layout of the system should be simple and direct
B. Pipes should be non-absorbent
C. Foul water drains should be well ventilated
D. Pipes can pass under buildings and trees
Show Answer

Correct Answer: D. Pipes can pass under buildings and trees

Explanation: Good design practice avoids laying drains under buildings or near trees (where roots can damage pipes) unless absolutely necessary and with special protection. Options A, B, and C are valid design requirements.

13.
The type of control valve fitted to a cold distribution cistern to prevent back flow is called?
A. Ball cock
B. Float valve
C. Gate valve
D. Check valve
Show Answer

Correct Answer: D. Check valve (or potentially A/B with Air Gap context)

Explanation: A Check Valve is specifically designed to prevent backflow. However, a Float Valve (Ball cock) prevents the cistern from overflowing. The question is slightly ambiguous: if referring to the valve that prevents water from the cistern flowing back into the mains, that is achieved by an Air Gap (position of float valve) or a Check Valve on the supply. If "fitted to" implies the valve controlling entry, it is the Float Valve. But strictly for "preventing back flow" as a primary function, Check Valve is the component name.

5.
Explain the working principle of the solar collector using a well labelled sketch. (5 marks)
Show Answer

Working Principle:

A solar collector (typically a flat plate) works on the greenhouse effect. Short-wave solar radiation passes through the transparent cover (glazing) and strikes the black absorber plate. The absorber plate converts this radiation into heat. The heat is trapped inside because the glazing is opaque to the long-wave thermal radiation re-emitted by the plate. This heat is transferred to the fluid (water) flowing through tubes attached to the absorber plate. As the water heats up, it becomes less dense and rises (thermosiphon effect) to the storage tank, being replaced by cooler water.

Section C: Long Answer Questions

50 Marks
1.

a) What do you understand by the term water hammer? (2 marks)

b) Explain how the water hammer occurs. (6 marks)

c) Explain how water hammer can be prevented. (2 marks)

Show Answer

a) Definition

Water hammer is a pressure surge or shockwave resulting when a fluid (usually a liquid like water) in motion is forced to stop or change direction suddenly (momentum change). It is characterized by a loud banging or hammering noise in the pipes.

b) How it Occurs

When water flows through a pipe, it has kinetic energy due to its mass and velocity. If a valve is closed suddenly (e.g., a quarter-turn ball valve or washing machine solenoid):

  1. The water flow stops abruptly at the valve.
  2. The kinetic energy of the water column is rapidly converted into potential energy (pressure).
  3. This creates a high-pressure shockwave that travels back upstream at the speed of sound.
  4. The wave hits the next obstacle (bend, valve, or main), reflects back, and bounces back and forth causing the pipe to vibrate and bang.

c) Prevention

  • Install Air Vessels or Water Hammer Arrestors near quick-closing valves to absorb the shock.
  • Use slow-closing valves (e.g., gate valves) instead of quick-closing ones where possible.
  • Reduce the velocity of water in the pipes (using larger pipes).
  • Secure pipes firmly with clips to reduce vibration.
2.
With the help of neat diagram describe the natural water cycle. (10 marks)
Show Answer

The Natural Water Cycle (Hydrologic Cycle) describes the continuous movement of water on, above, and below the surface of the Earth.

  1. Evaporation: The sun's heat causes water from oceans, lakes, and rivers to turn into vapor and rise into the atmosphere. Transpiration from plants also contributes.
  2. Condensation: As water vapor rises, it cools and condenses into tiny liquid droplets, forming clouds.
  3. Precipitation: When water droplets in clouds become heavy, they fall back to Earth as rain, snow, or hail.
  4. Collection/Runoff: Water flows over the ground (runoff) into rivers, lakes, and oceans, or infiltrates the ground (groundwater), restarting the cycle.

3.
Explain water treatment process from the source to an indirect water installation system. (10 marks)
Show Answer
  1. Screening: Raw water passes through screens to remove large floating objects (branches, rags).
  2. Aeration: Water is sprayed into air to release trapped gases (like CO2, H2S) and absorb oxygen, improving taste.
  3. Coagulation & Flocculation: Chemicals (Alum) are added. Impurities clump together (floc) to form larger particles.
  4. Sedimentation: Water flows slowly through tanks where the heavy floc settles to the bottom as sludge.
  5. Filtration: Clear water passes through sand/gravel filters to remove remaining fine particles and bacteria.
  6. Disinfection (Chlorination): Chlorine is added to kill pathogenic bacteria and viruses.
  7. Storage & Distribution: Treated water is stored in reservoirs and pumped through mains to the building.
  8. Indirect Installation: Water enters the building via the service pipe, goes to the kitchen sink (direct), and rises to a Cold Water Storage Cistern in the roof space. All other outlets are fed from this cistern.
4.

a) Differentiate between bib tap and pillar tap. (4 marks)

b) Define the following terms: (6 marks)
i. Fittings
ii. Man hole
iii. Soil tank

Show Answer

a) Difference

  • Bib Tap: Has a horizontal inlet and is designed to be mounted on a wall or vertical surface. Used for outdoor taps, janitorial sinks, etc.
  • Pillar Tap: Has a vertical inlet (shank) and is designed to be mounted on a horizontal surface (deck/shelf) of a basin, bath, or sink.

b) Definitions

  • Fittings: Components used in a pipe system to connect straight pipes or sections of tubes, adapt to different sizes or shapes, and for other purposes such as regulating fluid flow (e.g., elbows, tees, unions).
  • Manhole: An inspection chamber with a removable cover, large enough for a person to enter, used for access to sewers or drains for cleaning, inspection, and maintenance.
  • Soil Tank (Septic Tank): A watertight chamber where sewage (soil water) flows for primary treatment. Solids settle as sludge and undergo anaerobic decomposition, while liquid effluent flows out to a soakaway.

Differentiate between deep wells and shallow wells with the aid of sketches. (4 marks)

  • Shallow Well: Taps water from the unconfined aquifer above the first impervious layer. Usually less than 15-30m deep. Water level fluctuates with rainfall. Higher risk of contamination.
