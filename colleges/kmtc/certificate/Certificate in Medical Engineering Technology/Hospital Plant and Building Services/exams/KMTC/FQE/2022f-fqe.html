Section 1: Multiple Choice Questions

40 Marks
1.
Which of the following statement best describe shallow wells?
a) Are wells where water is found
b) Are wells where its water is found below the first impervious rock
c) Are wells where its water is found above the first impervious strata
d) Are wells where its water is tapped above the first pervious strata
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Are wells where its water is found above the first impervious strata

Explanation: Shallow wells typically tap into the unconfined aquifer, which sits above the first impermeable (impervious) layer of soil or rock. Wells tapping below the first impervious layer are considered deep wells (confined aquifers).

2.
The following are gas installation accessories which one is NOT?
a) Pressure governors
b) Pressure gauges
c) Manifolds and multi-points
d) Pressure release valve
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Pressure release valve (Contextual)

Explanation: All listed items are used in gas installations. However, pressure release valves are safety devices, whereas governors, gauges, and manifolds are operational accessories for distribution. In some contexts, safety devices are categorized separately from "accessories".

3.
What is the general safety requirement in laundry equipment?
a) Emergency stop
b) Locking mechanism
c) Mounting
d) All of the above
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Emergency stop (or d)

Explanation: An Emergency Stop button is a mandatory general safety requirement for all industrial machinery, including laundry equipment, to halt operation immediately in case of hazard. Locking mechanisms (interlocks) are also critical.

Explanation: This statement describes a mechanical safety requirement, not an electrical one. The other options (Earthing, ELCB, Switch placement) are electrical safety measures.

5.
Which of the following is true of a positive displacement pump?
a) Maintain a constant pressure
b) Move a constant volume of fluid
c) Vary the flow with Vpm
d) Vary the flow with gear changes
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Move a constant volume of fluid

Explanation: Positive displacement pumps move a fixed volume of fluid for each cycle or rotation, regardless of the discharge pressure (unlike centrifugal pumps).

9.
By boiling water, hardness can be removed if it is due to?
a) Calcium sulphate
b) Magnesium sulphate

Correct Answer: d) Calcium bicarbonate

Explanation: Calcium bicarbonate causes temporary hardness, which can be removed by boiling (it decomposes into insoluble calcium carbonate). Sulphates and nitrates cause permanent hardness.

15.
Which pump is preferred for small discharge at a high pressure?
a) Centrifugal pump
b) Axial flow pump
c) Mixed flow pump
d) Reciprocating pump
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Reciprocating pump

Explanation: Reciprocating pumps are positive displacement pumps ideal for low flow rates (small discharge) and high pressure heads. Centrifugal and axial flow pumps are better for high flow rates at lower pressures.

5.

a) State function of the following equipment refrigeration (2 marks)

ii. Line tap valve

i. T.E.V
ii. Capillary tube

Show Answer

a) Functions:

  • System Analyser (Manifold Gauge): To measure system pressures (high/low side) and aid in charging or diagnosing faults.

  • T.E.V (Thermostatic Expansion Valve): Regulates the flow of liquid refrigerant into the evaporator based on the superheat of the suction gas.

  • Filter (Drier): Removes moisture and solid debris from the refrigerant to prevent blockages and acid formation.
6.
State five advantages of a two stroke engine over a four stroke engine (5 marks)
Show Answer
  1. Simpler construction (fewer moving parts, no valves/cams).
  2. Lighter weight and more compact.
  3. Higher power-to-weight ratio (power stroke every revolution).
  4. Lower manufacturing cost.
  5. Can operate in any orientation (no oil sump).
7.
Outline the sequence of operation of petrol engine (5 marks)
Show Answer
  1. Intake Stroke: Piston moves down, inlet valve opens, air-fuel mixture is drawn in.
  2. Compression Stroke: Piston moves up, valves closed, mixture is compressed.
  3. Ignition/Power Stroke: Spark plug ignites mixture, expansion forces piston down.
  4. Exhaust Stroke: Piston moves up, exhaust valve opens, burnt gases pushed out.

a) Differences between Window AC and Split AC

Feature Window AC Split AC
Construction Single box containing all components (Compressor, Condenser, Evaporator). Two separate units: Indoor (Evaporator) and Outdoor (Compressor/Condenser).
Noise Noisier as the compressor is in the room. Quieter as the compressor is outside.
Installation Requires a large hole in the wall or a window sill. Requires only a small hole for piping; flexible placement.
Capacity Limited capacity (usually up to 2 tons). Available in higher capacities (up to 3-5 tons or more).
Cost Generally cheaper to buy and install. More expensive to buy and install.

b) Working of Centrifugal Pump

Working Principle:

It operates on the principle of centrifugal force. The pump consists of an impeller rotating inside a casing.

  1. Priming: The casing is filled with liquid.
  2. Rotation: The electric motor rotates the impeller at high speed.
  3. Centrifugal Action: Water at the center (eye) of the impeller is thrown outwards by centrifugal force.
  4. Suction: This creates a partial vacuum at the eye, causing water from the sump to be sucked in through the suction pipe.
  5. Discharge: The kinetic energy of the water leaving the impeller is converted into pressure energy in the volute casing, forcing water out the delivery pipe.

c) Procedure for Adding Refrigerant

  1. Preparation: Connect the manifold gauge set to the service ports (Low side). Purge air from the charging hoses.
  2. Connection: Connect the center hose to the refrigerant cylinder.
  3. System State: Ensure the compressor is running (for gas charging).
  4. Charging: Slowly open the low-side valve on the manifold to allow refrigerant vapor to be drawn into the system. Monitor the pressure gauge and ammeter.
  5. Verification: Close the valve periodically to check the stabilized pressure. Stop when the correct suction pressure/weight is reached.
