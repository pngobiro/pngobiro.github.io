Section 1: Short Answer Questions

1.
The sum of eight terms of an arithmetic progression is 42 and the common difference is 2. Determine the first term of the series. (4 marks)
Show Answer

Solution:

Given:
Sum of 8 terms, $S_8 = 42$
Common difference, $d = 2$
Number of terms, $n = 8$

Formula for Sum of an AP:
$S_n = \frac{n}{2}[2a + (n-1)d]$

Calculation:

$$42 = \frac{8}{2}[2a + (8-1)(2)]$$

$$42 = 4[2a + 7(2)]$$

$$42 = 4[2a + 14]$$

$$10.5 = 2a + 14$$

$$2a = 10.5 - 14$$

$$2a = -3.5$$

$$a = -1.75$$

Answer: The first term $a = -1.75$.

2.
The $1^{st}$, $80^{th}$ and $120^{th}$ numbers of an arithmetic progression are $2\frac{1}{2}, 121$ and $181$ respectively. Determine the sum of all the terms. (4 marks)
Show Answer

Solution:

Given:
$a = T_1 = 2.5$
$T_{80} = 121$
$T_{120} = 181$ (Assume this is the last term of the series implies $n=120$)

Verify Common Difference ($d$):
Formula: $T_n = a + (n-1)d$

Using $T_{80}$:
$121 = 2.5 + (80-1)d$
$118.5 = 79d$
$d = \frac{118.5}{79} = 1.5$

Verify with $T_{120}$:
$T_{120} = 2.5 + 119(1.5) = 2.5 + 178.5 = 181$ (Consistent)

Calculate Sum of all 120 terms:
Formula: $S_n = \frac{n}{2}(a + l)$ where $l$ is the last term.
$S_{120} = \frac{120}{2}(2.5 + 181)$
$S_{120} = 60(183.5)$
$S_{120} = 11,010$

Answer: The sum of all terms is 11,010.

3.
The first term of a geometric series is 10, and the fourth term is 33.75. Determine the sixth term and the tenth term, each correct to 5 significant figures. (4 marks)
Show Answer

Solution:

Given:
$a = 10$
$T_4 = ar^3 = 33.75$

Find Common Ratio ($r$):
$10r^3 = 33.75$
$r^3 = 3.375$
$r = \sqrt[3]{3.375} = 1.5$

Calculate 6th Term ($T_6$):
$T_6 = ar^5 = 10 \times (1.5)^5$
$T_6 = 10 \times 7.59375 = 75.9375$
To 5 s.f.: 75.938

Calculate 10th Term ($T_{10}$):
$T_{10} = ar^9 = 10 \times (1.5)^9$
$T_{10} = 10 \times 38.443359...$
$T_{10} = 384.43359...$
To 5 s.f.: 384.43

4.
From the first principles, differentiate $y = 4x$. (4 marks)
Show Answer

Solution:

Formula for first principles:
$f'(x) = \lim_{h \to 0} \frac{f(x+h) - f(x)}{h}$

Given $f(x) = 4x$.
Then $f(x+h) = 4(x+h) = 4x + 4h$.

Substitute into the limit formula:
$\frac{dy}{dx} = \lim_{h \to 0} \frac{(4x + 4h) - 4x}{h}$

Simplify numerator:
$\frac{dy}{dx} = \lim_{h \to 0} \frac{4h}{h}$

Cancel $h$:
$\frac{dy}{dx} = \lim_{h \to 0} (4)$

Result: $\frac{dy}{dx} = 4$

5.
Differentiate $\sin(6x + 1)$. (4 marks)
Show Answer

Solution:

Let $y = \sin(u)$ where $u = 6x + 1$.

Using Chain Rule:
$\frac{dy}{dx} = \frac{dy}{du} \cdot \frac{du}{dx}$

1. Differentiate outer function:
$\frac{d}{du}(\sin u) = \cos u$

2. Differentiate inner function:
$\frac{d}{dx}(6x + 1) = 6$

3. Multiply:
$\frac{dy}{dx} = \cos(6x + 1) \cdot 6$

Answer: $6\cos(6x + 1)$

6.
Find the value of the mean, median and mode of the following set of data: 52, 51, 52, 59, 60, 58, 54, 53, 50. (4 marks)
Show Answer

Solution:

Step 1: Order the data
50, 51, 52, 52, 53, 54, 58, 59, 60
Count ($n$) = 9

Mean:
$\bar{x} = \frac{\sum x}{n} = \frac{50+51+52+52+53+54+58+59+60}{9}$
$\bar{x} = \frac{489}{9} = 54.33$

Median:
Position = $\frac{n+1}{2} = \frac{10}{2} = 5^{th}$ value.
The 5th value is 53.

Mode:
Most occurring value is 52 (appears twice).

Answer: Mean = 54.33, Median = 53, Mode = 52

7.
If matrix $A = \begin{pmatrix} 2 & 4 \\ -1 & -3 \end{pmatrix}$ , $B = \begin{pmatrix} 3 & -7 \\ 4 & -5 \end{pmatrix}$. Determine $A \cdot B$. (4 marks)
Show Answer

Solution:

$$ A \cdot B = \begin{pmatrix} 2 & 4 \\ -1 & -3 \end{pmatrix} \begin{pmatrix} 3 & -7 \\ 4 & -5 \end{pmatrix} $$

Row 1, Col 1: $(2 \times 3) + (4 \times 4) = 6 + 16 = 22$
Row 1, Col 2: $(2 \times -7) + (4 \times -5) = -14 - 20 = -34$
Row 2, Col 1: $(-1 \times 3) + (-3 \times 4) = -3 - 12 = -15$
Row 2, Col 2: $(-1 \times -7) + (-3 \times -5) = 7 + 15 = 22$

Answer:

$$ A \cdot B = \begin{pmatrix} 22 & -34 \\ -15 & 22 \end{pmatrix} $$

8.
Solve $\int_{1}^{2} (x^2 - 3) dx$ (4 marks)
Show Answer

Solution:

Integrate the function:
$\int (x^2 - 3) dx = \frac{x^3}{3} - 3x$

Apply limits from 1 to 2:
$\left[ \frac{x^3}{3} - 3x \right]_1^2$

Substitute upper limit (2):
$(\frac{2^3}{3} - 3(2)) = (\frac{8}{3} - 6) = (\frac{8}{3} - \frac{18}{3}) = -\frac{10}{3}$

Substitute lower limit (1):
$(\frac{1^3}{3} - 3(1)) = (\frac{1}{3} - 3) = (\frac{1}{3} - \frac{9}{3}) = -\frac{8}{3}$

Subtract Lower from Upper:
$(-\frac{10}{3}) - (-\frac{8}{3}) = -\frac{10}{3} + \frac{8}{3} = -\frac{2}{3}$

Answer: $-\frac{2}{3}$ or $-0.6667$

9.
Express $492.731_{10}$ in octal form. (4 marks)
Show Answer

Solution:

1. Integer Part (492):
Divide by 8 repeatedly and keep remainders:
$492 \div 8 = 61$ rem 4
$61 \div 8 = 7$ rem 5
$7 \div 8 = 0$ rem 7
Read remainders upwards: $754_8$

2. Fractional Part (0.731):
Multiply by 8 and keep integer part:
$0.731 \times 8 = 5.848$ $\rightarrow$ 5
$0.848 \times 8 = 6.784$ $\rightarrow$ 6
$0.784 \times 8 = 6.272$ $\rightarrow$ 6
$0.272 \times 8 = 2.176$ $\rightarrow$ 2
Read integers downwards: $.5662..._8$

Answer: $754.5662_8$

10.
Express as a single fraction $\frac{4}{x+1} + \frac{x}{1+x} - 3$. (4 marks)
Show Answer

Solution:

Note that $x+1$ is the same as $1+x$. The common denominator is $(x+1)$.

Rewrite the expression:
$\frac{4}{x+1} + \frac{x}{x+1} - \frac{3(x+1)}{x+1}$

Combine numerators:
$\frac{4 + x - 3(x+1)}{x+1}$

Expand and simplify:
$\frac{4 + x - 3x - 3}{x+1}$

Group like terms:
$\frac{(4-3) + (x-3x)}{x+1} = \frac{1 - 2x}{x+1}$

Answer: $\frac{1 - 2x}{x+1}$

Section 2: Long Answer Questions

11.

a) Determine the value of arbitrary constant of integration if $m = \int \frac{t^2 - 4t}{t} dt$ when $t = 2$ and $m = 3$. (5 marks)

b) Prove that $\frac{1+\tan^2 B}{1+\cot^2 B} = \tan^2 B$. (5 marks)

c) The total surface area of a closed cylinder is $125\text{cm}^2$. Calculate the radius of the cylinder if its height is $5.24\text{cm}$. (10 marks)

Show Answer

Part (a)

Simplify the integrand first:
$\frac{t^2 - 4t}{t} = t - 4$

Integrate:
$m = \int (t - 4) dt = \frac{t^2}{2} - 4t + C$

Substitute known values ($t=2, m=3$) to find $C$:
$3 = \frac{2^2}{2} - 4(2) + C$
$3 = 2 - 8 + C$
$3 = -6 + C$
$C = 9$

Answer: The arbitrary constant is 9.

Part (b)

Proof:
LHS = $\frac{1+\tan^2 B}{1+\cot^2 B}$

Using identities: $1+\tan^2 B = \sec^2 B$ and $1+\cot^2 B = \csc^2 B$.

LHS = $\frac{\sec^2 B}{\csc^2 B}$

Convert to sine and cosine:
$\sec^2 B = \frac{1}{\cos^2 B}$
$\csc^2 B = \frac{1}{\sin^2 B}$

LHS = $\frac{1/\cos^2 B}{1/\sin^2 B} = \frac{1}{\cos^2 B} \times \frac{\sin^2 B}{1} = \frac{\sin^2 B}{\cos^2 B}$

Since $\frac{\sin B}{\cos B} = \tan B$, then $\frac{\sin^2 B}{\cos^2 B} = \tan^2 B$.

LHS = RHS ($\tan^2 B$). Proven.

Part (c)

Formula for Total Surface Area (TSA) of closed cylinder:
$A = 2\pi r^2 + 2\pi rh$

Given: $A = 125$, $h = 5.24$.

Equation:
$125 = 2\pi r^2 + 2\pi r(5.24)$

Divide by $2\pi$ to simplify ($\approx 6.283$):
$19.894 = r^2 + 5.24r$

Rearrange into quadratic form $ar^2 + br + c = 0$:
$r^2 + 5.24r - 19.894 = 0$

Solve using Quadratic Formula $r = \frac{-b \pm \sqrt{b^2 - 4ac}}{2a}$:
$a=1, b=5.24, c=-19.894$

$r = \frac{-5.24 \pm \sqrt{(5.24)^2 - 4(1)(-19.894)}}{2}$
$r = \frac{-5.24 \pm \sqrt{27.4576 + 79.576}}{2}$
$r = \frac{-5.24 \pm \sqrt{107.0336}}{2}$
$r = \frac{-5.24 \pm 10.3457}{2}$

Since radius cannot be negative, take the positive root:
$r = \frac{5.1057}{2} \approx 2.55$

Answer: The radius is approximately 2.55 cm.

12.

a) Solve the equation $3^{x+1} = 2^{2x-3}$ for the value of $x$. (5 marks)

b) Find the differential coefficient of $\tan x$. (10 marks)

c) If $\sin \theta = \frac{5}{13}$, determine: (5 marks)
i. $\cos \theta$
ii. $\tan \theta$
iii. $\sec \theta$

Show Answer

Part (a)

Take natural log ($\ln$) or log base 10 on both sides:
$\ln(3^{x+1}) = \ln(2^{2x-3})$
$(x+1)\ln 3 = (2x-3)\ln 2$

Expand:
$x\ln 3 + \ln 3 = 2x\ln 2 - 3\ln 2$

Group $x$ terms:
$x\ln 3 - 2x\ln 2 = -3\ln 2 - \ln 3$
$x(\ln 3 - 2\ln 2) = -(3\ln 2 + \ln 3)$

Solve for $x$:
$x = \frac{-(3\ln 2 + \ln 3)}{\ln 3 - 2\ln 2}$

Calculate numerical values ($\ln 3 \approx 1.0986, \ln 2 \approx 0.6931$):
Numerator: $-(3(0.6931) + 1.0986) = -(2.0793 + 1.0986) = -3.1779$
Denominator: $1.0986 - 2(0.6931) = 1.0986 - 1.3862 = -0.2876$
$x = \frac{-3.1779}{-0.2876} \approx 11.05$

Answer: $x \approx 11.05$

Part (b)

Find $\frac{d}{dx}(\tan x)$.

Recall $\tan x = \frac{\sin x}{\cos x}$. Use the Quotient Rule:
$\frac{d}{dx}\left(\frac{u}{v}\right) = \frac{v u' - u v'}{v^2}$

Here $u = \sin x, u' = \cos x$.
$v = \cos x, v' = -\sin x$.

Substitute:
$\frac{d}{dx}(\tan x) = \frac{(\cos x)(\cos x) - (\sin x)(-\sin x)}{(\cos x)^2}$
$= \frac{\cos^2 x + \sin^2 x}{\cos^2 x}$

Use identity $\cos^2 x + \sin^2 x = 1$:
$= \frac{1}{\cos^2 x}$

Since $\frac{1}{\cos x} = \sec x$:
$= \sec^2 x$

Answer: $\sec^2 x$

Part (c)

Given $\sin \theta = \frac{5}{13}$. This corresponds to a right-angled triangle where Opposite = 5 and Hypotenuse = 13.

Find Adjacent side using Pythagoras:
$Adj^2 + 5^2 = 13^2$
$Adj^2 + 25 = 169$
$Adj^2 = 144 \Rightarrow Adj = 12$

i. $\cos \theta$: $\frac{Adj}{Hyp} = \frac{12}{13}$

ii. $\tan \theta$: $\frac{Opp}{Adj} = \frac{5}{12}$

iii. $\sec \theta$: $\frac{1}{\cos \theta} = \frac{13}{12}$

13.

a) In a triangle ABC, angle $A = 53^\circ, B = 61^\circ$ and the length of $a = 12.60\text{cm}$. Find the unknown sides and angle. (10 marks)

b) Using Binomial expansion theorem, determine the value of $(1.05)^3 (0.98)^4$ correct to 5 significant figures. (10 marks)

Show Answer

Part (a)

1. Find Angle C:
Sum of angles in triangle = $180^\circ$.
$C = 180^\circ - (53^\circ + 61^\circ) = 180^\circ - 114^\circ = 66^\circ$.

2. Use Sine Rule to find sides $b$ and $c$:
$\frac{a}{\sin A} = \frac{b}{\sin B} = \frac{c}{\sin C}$

Finding side $b$:
$\frac{12.6}{\sin 53^\circ} = \frac{b}{\sin 61^\circ}$
$b = \frac{12.6 \times \sin 61^\circ}{\sin 53^\circ}$
$b = \frac{12.6 \times 0.8746}{0.7986} \approx 13.799 \text{ cm}$

Finding side $c$:
$\frac{12.6}{\sin 53^\circ} = \frac{c}{\sin 66^\circ}$
$c = \frac{12.6 \times \sin 66^\circ}{\sin 53^\circ}$
$c = \frac{12.6 \times 0.9135}{0.7986} \approx 14.413 \text{ cm}$

Answer: Angle $C = 66^\circ$, side $b \approx 13.80\text{cm}$, side $c \approx 14.41\text{cm}$.

Part (b)

Step 1: Expand terms using Binomial Theorem $(1+x)^n \approx 1 + nx + \frac{n(n-1)}{2}x^2$

For $(1.05)^3 = (1 + 0.05)^3$:
$= 1 + 3(0.05) + 3(0.05)^2 + (0.05)^3$
$= 1 + 0.15 + 0.0075 + 0.000125$
$= 1.157625$

For $(0.98)^4 = (1 - 0.02)^4$:
$= 1 + 4(-0.02) + 6(-0.02)^2 + 4(-0.02)^3 + (-0.02)^4$
$= 1 - 0.08 + 0.0024 - 0.000032 + ...$
$\approx 0.922368$

Step 2: Multiply results
Value $= 1.157625 \times 0.922368$
$\approx 1.06774$

Answer: 1.0677 (to 5 s.f.)
