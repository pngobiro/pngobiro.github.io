Section 1: Short Answer Questions

40 Marks Total
1.

(a) Express the following denary number in binary form: $245_{10}$ (2 marks)

(b) Express the following denary decimal in octal form: $0.526_{10}$ (2 marks)

Show Answer

Solution:

(a) Convert $245_{10}$ to binary:
Repeated division by 2:
$245 \div 2 = 122$ rem 1
$122 \div 2 = 61$ rem 0
$61 \div 2 = 30$ rem 1
$30 \div 2 = 15$ rem 0
$15 \div 2 = 7$ rem 1
$7 \div 2 = 3$ rem 1
$3 \div 2 = 1$ rem 1
$1 \div 2 = 0$ rem 1
Reading remainders upwards: $11110101_2$

(b) Convert $0.526_{10}$ to octal:
Repeated multiplication by 8:
$0.526 \times 8 = 4.208 \to$ Integer 4
$0.208 \times 8 = 1.664 \to$ Integer 1
$0.664 \times 8 = 5.312 \to$ Integer 5
$0.312 \times 8 = 2.496 \to$ Integer 2
Reading integers downwards: $0.4152..._8$

Answer: (a) $11110101_2$ (b) Approx $0.4152_8$

2.
Express $384.426_{10}$ in duodecimal form. (4 marks)
Show Answer

Solution:

Duodecimal is Base 12 (Symbols: 0-9, A, B).

Integer part (384):
$384 \div 12 = 32$ rem 0
$32 \div 12 = 2$ rem 8
$2 \div 12 = 0$ rem 2
Read upwards: $280_{12}$

Fractional part (0.426):
$0.426 \times 12 = 5.112 \to 5$
$0.112 \times 12 = 1.344 \to 1$
$0.344 \times 12 = 4.128 \to 4$
Read downwards: $.514..._{12}$

Answer: $280.514_{12}$

3.
Find the logarithm of $\log_{3.4} 0.293$ correct to three decimal places. (4 marks)
Show Answer

Solution:

Using the Change of Base formula:
$\log_a b = \frac{\ln b}{\ln a}$ or $\frac{\log_{10} b}{\log_{10} a}$

$\log_{3.4} 0.293 = \frac{\ln(0.293)}{\ln(3.4)}$

$\ln(0.293) \approx -1.2276$
$\ln(3.4) \approx 1.2238$

Ratio: $\frac{-1.2276}{1.2238} \approx -1.0031$

Answer: $-1.003$

4.
Find the coordinates of midpoints of the following:
(a) A(4,2) and B(6,10) (2 marks)
(b) A(-5,6) and B(3,2) (2 marks)
Show Answer

Solution:

Midpoint Formula: $M = \left( \frac{x_1+x_2}{2}, \frac{y_1+y_2}{2} \right)$

(a) A(4,2), B(6,10):
$x = \frac{4+6}{2} = \frac{10}{2} = 5$
$y = \frac{2+10}{2} = \frac{12}{2} = 6$
Midpoint: $(5, 6)$

(b) A(-5,6), B(3,2):
$x = \frac{-5+3}{2} = \frac{-2}{2} = -1$
$y = \frac{6+2}{2} = \frac{8}{2} = 4$
Midpoint: $(-1, 4)$

5.
The radius of a circle is 3cm. Find the angle contained by a sector of area $18cm^2$. (4 marks)
Show Answer

Solution:

Formula for Area of a Sector (radians): $A = \frac{1}{2}r^2\theta$

Given: $A = 18$, $r = 3$.

Substitute values:
$18 = \frac{1}{2} \times 3^2 \times \theta$
$18 = 0.5 \times 9 \times \theta$
$18 = 4.5 \theta$

Solve for $\theta$:
$\theta = \frac{18}{4.5} = 4$ radians

To convert to degrees (optional unless specified):
$4 \times \frac{180}{\pi} \approx 229.18^\circ$

Answer: 4 radians

6.

Solution:

$$ A \cdot B = \begin{pmatrix} 2 & 4 \\ 1 & -3 \end{pmatrix} \begin{pmatrix} 3 & -7 \\ 4 & -5 \end{pmatrix} $$

7.
Express $(3, -7)$ in polar coordinates. (4 marks)
Show Answer

Solution:

Cartesian $(x, y) = (3, -7)$.
Polar $(r, \theta)$.

Find $r$:
$r = \sqrt{x^2 + y^2} = \sqrt{3^2 + (-7)^2}$
$r = \sqrt{9 + 49} = \sqrt{58} \approx 7.616$

Find $\theta$:
$\tan \alpha = |\frac{y}{x}| = \frac{7}{3}$
$\alpha = \tan^{-1}(2.333) \approx 66.8^\circ$

Since point $(3, -7)$ is in the 4th Quadrant ($x>0, y<0$):
$\theta = 360^\circ - 66.8^\circ = 293.2^\circ$ (or $-66.8^\circ$)

Answer: $(7.62, 293.2^\circ)$ or $(7.62, -66.8^\circ)$

8.
Find the centroid of a triangle whose coordinates are A(1,2), B(3,7) and C(2,3). (4 marks)
Show Answer

Solution:

Formula for centroid $(G)$:
$G = \left( \frac{x_1+x_2+x_3}{3}, \frac{y_1+y_2+y_3}{3} \right)$

Substitute values:
$x = \frac{1+3+2}{3} = \frac{6}{3} = 2$
$y = \frac{2+7+3}{3} = \frac{12}{3} = 4$

Answer: The centroid is $(2, 4)$.

9.
Use factorisation method to solve the quadratic equation $x^2 - 6x + 9 = 0$. (4 marks)
Show Answer

Solution:

Equation: $x^2 - 6x + 9 = 0$

Find two numbers that multiply to $+9$ and add to $-6$.
These are $-3$ and $-3$.

Rewrite the middle term:
$x^2 - 3x - 3x + 9 = 0$

Factor by grouping:
$x(x-3) - 3(x-3) = 0$
$(x-3)(x-3) = 0$

Thus, $x - 3 = 0 \implies x = 3$.

Answer: $x = 3$ (repeated root).

10.
Using binomial expansion, find the expansion of $(1.003)^{10}$ correct to 6 decimal places. (4 marks)
Show Answer

Solution:

Write $(1.003)^{10}$ as $(1 + 0.003)^{10}$.

Formula: $(1+x)^n \approx 1 + nx + \frac{n(n-1)}{2!}x^2 + \dots$

Here $n=10, x=0.003$.

Term 1: $1$

Term 2: $10(0.003) = 0.03$

Term 3: $\frac{10 \times 9}{2}(0.003)^2 = 45(0.000009) = 0.000405$

Term 4: $\frac{10 \times 9 \times 8}{6}(0.003)^3 = 120(0.000000027) = 0.00000324$

Sum:
$1.000000$
$+ 0.030000$
$+ 0.000405$
$+ 0.000003$
$= 1.030408$

Answer: $1.030408$

Section 2: Long Answer Questions

60 Marks Total
1.

(a) Differentiate $y = x^2$ from the first principles and find the gradient of the curve at $x = 3$. (8 marks)

(b) Use matrix to solve the following simultaneous equations: (12 marks)
$$\frac{10}{a} - \frac{4}{b} = 3$$ $$\frac{6}{a} + \frac{8}{b} = 7$$

Show Answer

Part (a)

First Principles: $f'(x) = \lim_{h \to 0} \frac{f(x+h) - f(x)}{h}$

Let $y = x^2$.
$f(x+h) = (x+h)^2 = x^2 + 2xh + h^2$.

Difference quotient:
$\frac{(x^2 + 2xh + h^2) - x^2}{h} = \frac{2xh + h^2}{h} = 2x + h$

Take limit as $h \to 0$:
$\frac{dy}{dx} = 2x$

Gradient at $x=3$:
Substitute $x=3$ into derivative:
$2(3) = 6$

Answer: Derivative is $2x$, Gradient is 6.

Part (b)

Let $u = \frac{1}{a}$ and $v = \frac{1}{b}$.

System becomes:
$10u - 4v = 3$
$6u + 8v = 7$

Matrix Form $AX = B$:
$\begin{pmatrix} 10 & -4 \\ 6 & 8 \end{pmatrix} \begin{pmatrix} u \\ v \end{pmatrix} = \begin{pmatrix} 3 \\ 7 \end{pmatrix}$

Find Determinant of A:
$|A| = (10 \times 8) - (-4 \times 6) = 80 + 24 = 104$

Find Inverse $A^{-1}$:
$A^{-1} = \frac{1}{104} \begin{pmatrix} 8 & 4 \\ -6 & 10 \end{pmatrix}$

Solve for $\begin{pmatrix} u \\ v \end{pmatrix} = A^{-1}B$:
$\begin{pmatrix} u \\ v \end{pmatrix} = \frac{1}{104} \begin{pmatrix} 8 & 4 \\ -6 & 10 \end{pmatrix} \begin{pmatrix} 3 \\ 7 \end{pmatrix}$

$u = \frac{1}{104} [(8 \times 3) + (4 \times 7)] = \frac{24 + 28}{104} = \frac{52}{104} = 0.5$
$v = \frac{1}{104} [(-6 \times 3) + (10 \times 7)] = \frac{-18 + 70}{104} = \frac{52}{104} = 0.5$

Find $a$ and $b$:
$u = 0.5 \implies \frac{1}{a} = \frac{1}{2} \implies a = 2$
$v = 0.5 \implies \frac{1}{b} = \frac{1}{2} \implies b = 2$

Answer: $a = 2, b = 2$

2.

(a) In a geometric progression, the fifth term is 8 times the second term and the sum of the 6th and 8th terms is 160. Determine: (12 marks)
(i) The common ratio
(ii) The first term
(iii) The sum of the fourth to tenth terms, inclusive

(b) Find the value of the mean, median and mode of the following set of data: 52, 51, 52, 59, 60, 58, 54, 53 and 50. (5 marks)

(c) Integrate $\int (r + \frac{1}{r})^2 dr$. (3 marks)

Show Answer

Part (a)

(i) Common Ratio:
Given $T_5 = 8 \times T_2$.
$ar^4 = 8(ar)$
Divide by $ar$ (assuming $a,r \neq 0$):
$r^3 = 8 \implies r = 2$.

(ii) First Term:
Given $T_6 + T_8 = 160$.
$ar^5 + ar^7 = 160$
Substitute $r=2$:
$a(2^5) + a(2^7) = 160$
$32a + 128a = 160$
$160a = 160 \implies a = 1$.

(iii) Sum 4th to 10th terms:
This is $S_{10} - S_3$.
Sum formula $S_n = \frac{a(r^n - 1)}{r-1}$.
$S_{10} = \frac{1(2^{10} - 1)}{2-1} = 1023$.
$S_3 = \frac{1(2^3 - 1)}{1} = 7$.
Sum = $1023 - 7 = 1016$.
Alternatively: Sum is geometric series with $a' = T_4 = 1(2)^3 = 8$, $n=7$ (terms 4,5,6,7,8,9,10).
Sum = $\frac{8(2^7 - 1)}{1} = 8(127) = 1016$.

Part (b)

Data: 52, 51, 52, 59, 60, 58, 54, 53, 50.
Ordered: 50, 51, 52, 52, 53, 54, 58, 59, 60.
$n = 9$.

Mean: $\frac{\sum x}{9} = \frac{489}{9} = 54.33$.

Median: Middle term (5th) = 53.

Mode: Most frequent = 52.

Part (c)

Expand integrand:
$(r + r^{-1})^2 = r^2 + 2(r)(r^{-1}) + (r^{-1})^2$
$= r^2 + 2 + r^{-2}$

Integrate term by term:
$\int (r^2 + 2 + r^{-2}) dr$
$= \frac{r^3}{3} + 2r + \frac{r^{-1}}{-1} + C$
$= \frac{r^3}{3} + 2r - \frac{1}{r} + C$

3.

(a) If $\sin\theta = \frac{8}{17}$. Find the value of the other five trigonometric ratios. (7 marks)

(b) A cone has a radius of $4.86cm$ and perpendicular height of $10.58cm$. Find: (8 marks)
(i) Volume
(ii) Total surface area

(c) Integrate $\int (\frac{r^3 + 2r - 1}{r^2}) dr$ (5 marks)

Show Answer

Part (a)

Given $\sin\theta = \frac{Opp}{Hyp} = \frac{8}{17}$.
Find Adjacent ($Adj$) using Pythagoras:
$Adj = \sqrt{17^2 - 8^2} = \sqrt{289 - 64} = \sqrt{225} = 15$.

Ratios:
1. $\cos\theta = \frac{Adj}{Hyp} = \frac{15}{17}$
2. $\tan\theta = \frac{Opp}{Adj} = \frac{8}{15}$
3. $\csc\theta = \frac{1}{\sin\theta} = \frac{17}{8}$
4. $\sec\theta = \frac{1}{\cos\theta} = \frac{17}{15}$
5. $\cot\theta = \frac{1}{\tan\theta} = \frac{15}{8}$

Part (b)

Radius ($r$) = 4.86 cm, Height ($h$) = 10.58 cm.

(i) Volume:
$V = \frac{1}{3}\pi r^2 h$
$V = \frac{1}{3} \times \pi \times (4.86)^2 \times 10.58$
$V = \frac{1}{3} \times 3.1416 \times 23.6196 \times 10.58$
$V \approx 261.69 \, cm^3$

(ii) Total Surface Area:
Find slant height ($l$):
$l = \sqrt{r^2 + h^2} = \sqrt{4.86^2 + 10.58^2} = \sqrt{23.62 + 111.94} = \sqrt{135.56} \approx 11.643 \, cm$.
$TSA = \pi r(r + l)$
$TSA = \pi \times 4.86 \times (4.86 + 11.643)$
$TSA = 15.268 \times 16.503 \approx 251.97 \, cm^2$

Part (c)

Simplify integrand by dividing each term by $r^2$:
$\frac{r^3}{r^2} + \frac{2r}{r^2} - \frac{1}{r^2}$
$= r + \frac{2}{r} - r^{-2}$

Integrate term by term:
$\int (r + 2r^{-1} - r^{-2}) dr$
$= \frac{r^2}{2} + 2\ln|r| - \frac{r^{-1}}{-1} + C$
$= \frac{r^2}{2} + 2\ln|r| + \frac{1}{r} + C$
