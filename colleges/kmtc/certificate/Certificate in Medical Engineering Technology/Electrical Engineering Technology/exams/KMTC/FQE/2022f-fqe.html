Section 1: Multiple Choice Questions

40 Marks
1.
Which of the following statements is false?
a) A DC motor converts electrical energy to mechanical energy
b) The efficiency of a DC motor is the ratio output power to input power
c) A DC generator converts mechanical power to electrical power
d) The efficiency of a DC generator is the ratio of input power to output power
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) The efficiency of a DC generator is the ratio of input power to output power

Explanation: Efficiency is always Output Power divided by Input Power ($\eta = \frac{P_{out}}{P_{in}}$). Option D reverses this ratio.

2.
In a DC generator, iron losses are made up of
a) Hysteresis and frictional loses
b) Hysteresis, eddy currents and brush and contact losses
c) Hysteresis and eddy current losses
d) Hysteresis, eddy current losses and copper losses
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Hysteresis and eddy current losses

Explanation: Iron losses (Core losses) consist of Hysteresis loss (due to magnetic reversal) and Eddy current loss (circulating currents in the core).

3.
In a DC motor, the stator resistor (starter)
a) Limits armature current to a safe starting value
b) Controls the speed of the machine
c) Prevents field current from flowing through the machine
d) Limits other field current to a scale starting value
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Limits armature current to a safe starting value

Explanation: At start, back EMF is zero. Without a starter resistor, the armature current $I_a = V/R_a$ would be dangerously high.

4.
Laminations of a core are generally made of
a) Cast iron
b) Carbon
c) Silicon steel
d) Stainless steel
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Silicon steel

Explanation: Silicon steel is used to reduce hysteresis losses, and lamination reduces eddy current losses.

5.
The commutator segments are connected to the armature conductors by means of
a) Copper lugs
b) Resistance
c) Insulation pads
d) Brazing
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Copper lugs (Risers)

Explanation: Copper lugs or risers extend from the commutator segments to facilitate connection/soldering to the armature coil ends.

6.
A welding generator will have
a) Lap winding
b) Wave winding
c) Delta winding
d) Duplex wave winding
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Lap winding

Explanation: Welding requires low voltage and high current. Lap winding provides more parallel paths ($A=P$), suitable for high current applications.

7.
In a DC machine armature reaction is produced by
a) Field current
b) Armature conductors
c) Field pole winding
d) Armature current
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Armature current

Explanation: Armature reaction is the effect of the magnetic flux produced by the current flowing in the armature conductors on the main field flux.

8.
Which one of the following is the rotating part of an electrical machine?
a) Yoke
b) Brushes
c) Commutator
d) Field poles
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Commutator

Explanation: In a DC machine, the Yoke, Brushes, and Field poles are stationary (stator). The Commutator is mounted on the shaft and rotates with the armature.

9.
The speed of DC motor may be increases by
a) Increasing the armature current
b) Decreasing the field current
c) Decreasing the applied voltage
d) Increasing the filed current
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Decreasing the field current

Explanation: Speed is inversely proportional to flux ($N \propto 1/\phi$). Decreasing field current decreases flux, which increases speed.

10.
The armature torque of a DC motor is a function of
a) Field for alone
b) Armature current alone
c) Speed alone
d) Both field flux and armature current
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Both field flux and armature current

Explanation: Torque equation: $T \propto \phi I_a$. It depends on both Flux ($\phi$) and Armature Current ($I_a$).

11.
DC motors are considered suitable for their application in
a) Fans
b) Water pumps
c) Traction
d) Floor mills
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Traction

Explanation: DC Series motors are ideal for traction (trains, trams) due to their very high starting torque.

12.
Which one of the following is the primary reason of placing field magnets on the rotor of an alternator?
a) Small power in the stator
b) Insulation of high-voltage is made easy on the stator than rotor
c) Large power on the stator
d) Large current on the stator
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Insulation of high-voltage is made easy on the stator than rotor

Explanation: Alternators generate very high voltages. It is easier to insulate the stationary part (stator) than the rotating part. Also, only low voltage DC is needed for the rotating field.

13.
If the diameter and length of the stator of a high speed turbo alternator and low speed hydro generator of identical ratings are compared
a) Turbo-generator has a larger diameter and larger length
b) Turbo-generator has a smaller diameter and larger length
c) The turbo generator has a larger diameter and small length
d) The turbo generator has a smaller diameter and larger lengths (Duplicate)
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Turbo-generator has a smaller diameter and larger length

Explanation: High-speed rotors (Turbo) must have small diameters to reduce centrifugal forces, so they are long (axial length). Low-speed hydro generators have large diameters (for many poles) and short lengths.

14.
Synchronous reactance of an alternator represents
a) Armature reactance and leakage reactance
b) A reactance operating at synchronous speed
c) Field winding reactance
d) A reactance connected in series with capacitance of the generator
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Armature reactance and leakage reactance

Explanation: Synchronous Reactance ($X_s$) is the sum of Armature Reaction Reactance ($X_a$) and Leakage Reactance ($X_l$).

15.
An electric motor voltage in which both the rotor and station field rotates with the same speed is called
a) Pc
b) Induction
c) Synchronous
d) Universal
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Synchronous

Explanation: In a synchronous motor, the rotor locks into step with the rotating magnetic field of the stator and runs at the synchronous speed.

16.
The direction of rotation of a synchronous motor can be reversed by?
a) Reversing current to the field winding
b) Reversing supply phase sequence
c) Reversing polarity of the rotor poles
d) None of the above
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Reversing supply phase sequence

Explanation: Swapping any two leads of the 3-phase supply reverses the direction of the rotating magnetic field, thus reversing the motor.

17.
An induction motor works with
a) DC only
b) AC only
c) Both AC and DC
d) None of the above
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) AC only

Explanation: Induction motors rely on a rotating magnetic field which is produced by AC. They cannot operate on DC.

18.
The rotor of a three phase wound induction motor is provided with
a) Single phase winding
b) Three phase winding
c) Heavy copper and copper aluminum bars placed in rotor slots
d) Heavy short circuited end rings
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Three phase winding

Explanation: A wound rotor (slip ring) induction motor has a 3-phase winding on the rotor similar to the stator. Options C and D describe a Squirrel Cage rotor.

19.
Which induction motor has the highest torque
a) Capacitor start
b) Squirrel pole
c) Shaded pole
d) Split phase
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Capacitor start

Explanation: Capacitor start motors have a large phase shift between windings, creating a strong starting field and high starting torque compared to split phase or shaded pole motors.

20.
The basic function of a transformer is to change
a) Level of voltage
b) The power level
c) The power factor
d) The frequency
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Level of voltage

Explanation: Transformers step up or step down AC voltage. Power (ideally) remains constant, frequency remains constant.

Section 2: Short Answer Questions

1.

a) State two methods of connecting armature coils to the commutator segments (2 marks)

b) Name any three types of d.c generators (3 marks)

Show Answer

a) Methods of Connection:

  • Lap Winding: Adjacent coils overlap; connected to adjacent segments. ($A = P$)
  • Wave Winding: Coils spread apart; connected to segments spaced by two pole pitches. ($A = 2$)

b) Types of DC Generators:

  1. Series Generator
  2. Shunt Generator
  3. Compound Generator (Long Shunt / Short Shunt)
2.

a) State three functions of a commutator in a d.c motor. (3 marks)

b) State two methods used to improve commutation (2 marks)

Show Answer

a) Functions of Commutator in DC Motor:

  1. To reverse the direction of current in the armature coil as it passes the magnetic neutral axis.
  2. To maintain a unidirectional torque.
  3. To facilitate electrical connection between the rotating armature and stationary external circuit via brushes.

b) Methods to Improve Commutation:

  1. Resistance Commutation: Using high-resistance carbon brushes.
  2. EMF Commutation: Using Interpoles (Commutating poles) to neutralize reactance voltage.
5.

A 230V series motor draws 50A from the supply. The resistances of the armature and series field windings are $0.2\Omega$ and $0.1\Omega$ respectively. Calculate:

a) Brush voltage (1 mark)
b) Back EMF (2 marks)
c) Power wasted in the armature (2 marks)

Show Answer

Solution:
$V = 230V$, $I_a = 50A$, $R_a = 0.2\Omega$, $R_{se} = 0.1\Omega$.

a) Brush Voltage (Drop):
Often assumed to be 2V total if not specified. Or the question might mean "Voltage drop across brushes + resistance".
Let's assume standard brush drop $V_{brush} \approx 2V$ (or 0 if ideal).
If asking for voltage drop across windings: $V_{drop} = I_a(R_a + R_{se}) = 50(0.3) = 15V$.
Assuming question implies contact drop, typically 2V.

b) Back EMF ($E_b$):
Formula: $E_b = V - I_a(R_a + R_{se}) - V_{brush}$
Without brush drop: $E_b = 230 - 50(0.2 + 0.1) = 230 - 15 = 215V$.
With 2V brush drop: $E_b = 213V$.
Result: $215V$ (ignoring brush contact drop).

c) Power wasted in armature:
$P_{loss} = I_a^2 R_a$
$P_{loss} = 50^2 \times 0.2 = 2500 \times 0.2 = 500 Watts$.

Section 3: Long Answer Question

20 Marks
1.

i. State and describe five parts of a DC machine. (10 marks)

ii. Draw and label the diagram of capacitor start capacitor run single phase induction motor (5 marks)

iii. State five applications where a capacitor start capacitor run single phase induction motor are used. (5 marks)

Show Answer

i. Parts of a DC Machine

  1. Yoke (Frame): The outer cast iron/steel cover that protects the machine and provides a return path for the magnetic flux.
  2. Pole Cores and Pole Shoes: Laminated steel cores bolted to the yoke. They carry the field winding and spread the flux over the armature (shoes).
  3. Field Winding: Copper coils wound around the poles which produce the magnetic flux when current passes through them.
  4. Armature Core: A cylindrical laminated drum mounted on the shaft with slots to hold the armature winding. It provides a low reluctance path for flux.
  5. Commutator: A split copper ring structure insulated with mica. It rectifies AC induced in armature to DC (generator) or acts as a mechanical inverter (motor).

ii. Diagram of Capacitor Start Capacitor Run Motor

      L ------------------------+-----+
                                |     |
                          Start |     | Run
                           Cap  |     | Cap
                          (Cs)  |     | (Cr)
            Centrifugal    ||   |     ||
            Switch (S)     ||   |     ||
                 /         ||   |     ||
       +-------o/  o-------++---++----++
       |                   |          |
       |                   |          |
   Auxiliary              Main        |
   Winding                Winding     |
   (Start)                (Run)       |
       |                   |          |
       |      (Rotor)      |          |
       +----------O--------+----------+
                                      |
      N ------------------------------+

Note: In a true "Capacitor Start Capacitor Run" (Two-Value Capacitor) motor, the start capacitor is disconnected by the switch, while the run capacitor stays in circuit.

iii. Applications

  1. Air compressors
  2. Refrigerators and Air conditioners
  3. Conveyor belts
  4. Pumps
  5. Machine tools (Lathes, Drills)
