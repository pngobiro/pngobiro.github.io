Section 1: Short Answer Questions

40 Marks
1.
If $A = \begin{pmatrix} 2 & 3 \\ 4 & 5 \end{pmatrix}$ and $B = \begin{pmatrix} -4 & 4 \\ 5 & -3 \end{pmatrix}$, find the value of $AB - (2A + 5B)$. (4 marks)
Show Answer

Solution:

Step 1: Calculate AB
$AB = \begin{pmatrix} 2 & 3 \\ 4 & 5 \end{pmatrix} \begin{pmatrix} -4 & 4 \\ 5 & -3 \end{pmatrix}$
$= \begin{pmatrix} (2)(-4)+(3)(5) & (2)(4)+(3)(-3) \\ (4)(-4)+(5)(5) & (4)(4)+(5)(-3) \end{pmatrix}$
$= \begin{pmatrix} -8+15 & 8-9 \\ -16+25 & 16-15 \end{pmatrix} = \begin{pmatrix} 7 & -1 \\ 9 & 1 \end{pmatrix}$

Step 2: Calculate 2A + 5B
$2A = \begin{pmatrix} 4 & 6 \\ 8 & 10 \end{pmatrix}$
$5B = \begin{pmatrix} -20 & 20 \\ 25 & -15 \end{pmatrix}$
$2A + 5B = \begin{pmatrix} 4-20 & 6+20 \\ 8+25 & 10-15 \end{pmatrix} = \begin{pmatrix} -16 & 26 \\ 33 & -5 \end{pmatrix}$

Step 3: Subtract
$AB - (2A + 5B) = \begin{pmatrix} 7 & -1 \\ 9 & 1 \end{pmatrix} - \begin{pmatrix} -16 & 26 \\ 33 & -5 \end{pmatrix}$
$= \begin{pmatrix} 7 - (-16) & -1 - 26 \\ 9 - 33 & 1 - (-5) \end{pmatrix} = \begin{pmatrix} 23 & -27 \\ -24 & 6 \end{pmatrix}$

Answer: $\begin{pmatrix} 23 & -27 \\ -24 & 6 \end{pmatrix}$

2.
The first term of an arithmetic progression series is 2.5 and the tenth term is 16. Find the fifteenth term. (4 marks)
Show Answer

Solution:

Given: $a = 2.5$, $T_{10} = 16$.
Formula: $T_n = a + (n-1)d$

$T_{10} = 2.5 + 9d = 16$
$9d = 16 - 2.5 = 13.5$
$d = \frac{13.5}{9} = 1.5$

Calculate $T_{15}$:
$T_{15} = a + 14d = 2.5 + 14(1.5)$
$T_{15} = 2.5 + 21 = 23.5$

Answer: 23.5

3.
Convert $58.3125_{10}$ to binary number. (4 marks)

Solution:

4.
Solve the following quadratic equation by formula method: $2x^2 + 5x - 4 = 0$. (4 marks)
Show Answer

Solution:

Formula: $x = \frac{-b \pm \sqrt{b^2 - 4ac}}{2a}$
$a = 2, b = 5, c = -4$

$x = \frac{-5 \pm \sqrt{5^2 - 4(2)(-4)}}{2(2)}$
$x = \frac{-5 \pm \sqrt{25 + 32}}{4}$
$x = \frac{-5 \pm \sqrt{57}}{4}$

$\sqrt{57} \approx 7.55$
$x_1 = \frac{-5 + 7.55}{4} = \frac{2.55}{4} \approx 0.6375$
$x_2 = \frac{-5 - 7.55}{4} = \frac{-12.55}{4} \approx -3.1375$

Answer: $x = 0.64$ or $x = -3.14$ (2 d.p.)

5.
Find the middle term of the expansion $(2p - \frac{1}{2q})^{10}$. (4 marks)
Show Answer

Solution:

The expansion has $n+1 = 11$ terms. The middle term is the 6th term ($r=5$).

Formula: $T_{r+1} = \binom{n}{r} a^{n-r} b^r$
$n=10, r=5, a=2p, b=-\frac{1}{2q}$

$T_6 = \binom{10}{5} (2p)^{5} (-\frac{1}{2q})^5$
$\binom{10}{5} = \frac{10 \cdot 9 \cdot 8 \cdot 7 \cdot 6}{5 \cdot 4 \cdot 3 \cdot 2 \cdot 1} = 252$
$T_6 = 252 \cdot (32p^5) \cdot (-\frac{1}{32q^5})$
$T_6 = -252 \cdot \frac{32p^5}{32q^5}$
$T_6 = -252 \frac{p^5}{q^5}$

Answer: $-252 \frac{p^5}{q^5}$

6.
Simplify and hence evaluate $\frac{3^2 \times 5^5 + 3^3 \times 5^3}{3^4 \times 5^4}$ using laws of indices. (4 marks)
Show Answer

Solution:

Factor out common terms in numerator ($3^2 \times 5^3$):
Numerator $= 3^2 \times 5^3 (5^2 + 3^1) = 3^2 \times 5^3 (25 + 3) = 3^2 \times 5^3 (28)$

Expression: $\frac{3^2 \times 5^3 \times 28}{3^4 \times 5^4}$

Use laws of indices ($\frac{a^m}{a^n} = a^{m-n}$):
$= 3^{2-4} \times 5^{3-4} \times 28$
$= 3^{-2} \times 5^{-1} \times 28$
$= \frac{28}{3^2 \times 5^1} = \frac{28}{9 \times 5} = \frac{28}{45}$

Answer: $\frac{28}{45}$ or $0.622$

7.
Differentiate $5x^2 \cos x$. (4 marks)
Show Answer

Solution:

Use Product Rule: $\frac{d}{dx}(uv) = u\frac{dv}{dx} + v\frac{du}{dx}$
$u = 5x^2 \implies u' = 10x$
$v = \cos x \implies v' = -\sin x$

$\frac{dy}{dx} = 5x^2(-\sin x) + (\cos x)(10x)$
$= -5x^2 \sin x + 10x \cos x$
$= 5x(2\cos x - x\sin x)$

Answer: $10x \cos x - 5x^2 \sin x$

8.
Evaluate $\int (\frac{4r^5 - 3 + r}{r^3}) dr$. (4 marks)
Show Answer

Solution:

Simplify the integrand first:
$\frac{4r^5}{r^3} - \frac{3}{r^3} + \frac{r}{r^3} = 4r^2 - 3r^{-3} + r^{-2}$

Integrate term by term:
$\int (4r^2 - 3r^{-3} + r^{-2}) dr$
$= 4(\frac{r^3}{3}) - 3(\frac{r^{-2}}{-2}) + (\frac{r^{-1}}{-1}) + C$
$= \frac{4}{3}r^3 + \frac{3}{2}r^{-2} - r^{-1} + C$
$= \frac{4}{3}r^3 + \frac{3}{2r^2} - \frac{1}{r} + C$

9.
Determine the mean, median and mode values of the following set of data: 26, 31, 21, 29, 32, 26, 25, 28. (4 marks)
Show Answer

Solution:

Data: 26, 31, 21, 29, 32, 26, 25, 28.
Ordered: 21, 25, 26, 26, 28, 29, 31, 32.
$n = 8$.

Mean: $\frac{\sum x}{n} = \frac{21+25+26+26+28+29+31+32}{8} = \frac{218}{8} = 27.25$.

Median: Average of 4th and 5th terms.
4th = 26, 5th = 28.
Median = $\frac{26+28}{2} = 27$.

Mode: Most frequent value = 26.

Answer: Mean=27.25, Median=27, Mode=26.

10.
Solve the equation $\frac{4x+7}{3} - \frac{x-7}{5} = \frac{x+6}{9}$. (4 marks)
Show Answer

Solution:

Multiply by LCM of 3, 5, 9 (LCM = 45):
$45(\frac{4x+7}{3}) - 45(\frac{x-7}{5}) = 45(\frac{x+6}{9})$
$15(4x+7) - 9(x-7) = 5(x+6)$
$60x + 105 - 9x + 63 = 5x + 30$

Combine like terms:
$51x + 168 = 5x + 30$
$51x - 5x = 30 - 168$
$46x = -138$
$x = \frac{-138}{46} = -3$

Answer: $x = -3$

11.

a) Solve the following simultaneous equations using determinants: (10 marks)
$$\frac{m}{3} + \frac{n}{4} = 6$$ $$\frac{m}{6} - \frac{n}{8} = 0$$

b) Use matrices to solve the following simultaneous equations: (10 marks)
$$\frac{3}{V_1} - \frac{2}{V_2} = \frac{1}{2}$$ $$\frac{5}{V_1} + \frac{3}{V_2} = \frac{29}{12}$$

Show Answer

Part (a)

Simplify equations:
1) Multiply by 12: $4m + 3n = 72$.
2) Multiply by 24: $4m - 3n = 0$.

Determinants:
$D = \begin{vmatrix} 4 & 3 \\ 4 & -3 \end{vmatrix} = (4)(-3) - (3)(4) = -12 - 12 = -24$.
$D_m = \begin{vmatrix} 72 & 3 \\ 0 & -3 \end{vmatrix} = (72)(-3) - (0) = -216$.
$D_n = \begin{vmatrix} 4 & 72 \\ 4 & 0 \end{vmatrix} = 0 - (72)(4) = -288$.

$m = \frac{D_m}{D} = \frac{-216}{-24} = 9$.
$n = \frac{D_n}{D} = \frac{-288}{-24} = 12$.
Answer: $m = 9, n = 12$.

Part (b)

Let $x = \frac{1}{V_1}$ and $y = \frac{1}{V_2}$.
Equations: $3x - 2y = 0.5$ and $5x + 3y = \frac{29}{12}$.
Matrix form: $\begin{pmatrix} 3 & -2 \\ 5 & 3 \end{pmatrix} \begin{pmatrix} x \\ y \end{pmatrix} = \begin{pmatrix} 0.5 \\ 2.416 \end{pmatrix}$.

Determinant $|A| = 9 - (-10) = 19$.
Inverse $A^{-1} = \frac{1}{19} \begin{pmatrix} 3 & 2 \\ -5 & 3 \end{pmatrix}$.

Solve for x, y:
$\begin{pmatrix} x \\ y \end{pmatrix} = \frac{1}{19} \begin{pmatrix} 3 & 2 \\ -5 & 3 \end{pmatrix} \begin{pmatrix} 1/2 \\ 29/12 \end{pmatrix}$
$x = \frac{1}{19} [3(0.5) + 2(29/12)] = \frac{1}{19} [1.5 + 4.833] = \frac{1}{19}[\frac{3}{2} + \frac{29}{6}] = \frac{1}{19}[\frac{9+29}{6}] = \frac{1}{19}(\frac{38}{6}) = \frac{2}{6} = \frac{1}{3}$.
$y = \frac{1}{19} [-5(0.5) + 3(29/12)] = \frac{1}{19} [-2.5 + 7.25] = \frac{1}{19}(4.75) = 0.25 = \frac{1}{4}$.

Find $V_1, V_2$:
$\frac{1}{V_1} = \frac{1}{3} \implies V_1 = 3$.
$\frac{1}{V_2} = \frac{1}{4} \implies V_2 = 4$.

13.

a) Prove that $\frac{\text{cosec}\theta + \cot\theta}{\tan\theta + \sec\theta} = \cos\theta \cot\theta$. (10 marks)

b) Given that $\cos A = \frac{24}{25}$, find the other five trigonometric ratios in fraction form. (10 marks)

Show Answer

Part (a)

Simplify LHS:
$\frac{\csc\theta + \cot\theta}{\tan\theta + \sec\theta} = \frac{\frac{1}{\sin} + \frac{\cos}{\sin}}{\frac{\sin}{\cos} + \frac{1}{\cos}}$
$= \frac{\frac{1+\cos}{\sin}}{\frac{1+\sin}{\cos}}$
$= \frac{1+\cos}{\sin} \times \frac{\cos}{1+\sin}$
$= \frac{\cos}{\sin} \times \frac{1+\cos}{1+\sin} = \cot\theta \times \frac{1+\cos}{1+\sin}$

Note: The question likely intended for an identity that simplifies cleanly. Based on the text provided, LHS simplifies to $\cot\theta \frac{1+\cos\theta}{1+\sin\theta}$. This is not equal to $\cos\theta \cot\theta$ unless specific conditions are met or there is a typo in the question text. Assuming standard proof structure, student would show the expansion.

Part (b)

Given: $\cos A = \frac{24}{25}$ (Adjacent/Hypotenuse)

Find Opposite:
$Opp = \sqrt{25^2 - 24^2} = \sqrt{625 - 576} = \sqrt{49} = 7$.

Other 5 Ratios:

  • $\sin A = \frac{Opp}{Hyp} = \frac{7}{25}$
  • $\tan A = \frac{Opp}{Adj} = \frac{7}{24}$
  • $\csc A = \frac{1}{\sin A} = \frac{25}{7}$
  • $\sec A = \frac{1}{\cos A} = \frac{25}{24}$
  • $\cot A = \frac{1}{\tan A} = \frac{24}{7}$
