Section 1: Multiple Choice Questions

40 Marks
1.
Which of the following statements is NOT true about atoms?
a) A negative body will attract electrons
b) A positive body will attract electrons
c) Neutrons have no charge
d) Number of electrons is equal to the number of protons in a balanced atom
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) A negative body will attract electrons

Explanation: According to the laws of electrostatics, like charges repel and unlike charges attract. Electrons are negatively charged, so a negative body will repel them, not attract them.

2.
E.M.F. is induced by which principle of electromagnetism?
a) By using a small cross-sectional area coil
b) By increasing the size of the core
c) By increasing the strength of the magnetic field
d) By changing the direction of rotating the conductor
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) By changing the direction of rotating the conductor (Note: Question is vague, refers to methods of induction)

Explanation: Faraday's Law states EMF is induced when there is a change in magnetic flux linkage. Options A, B, C affect the magnitude, but the fundamental principle requires relative motion or changing flux (cutting lines of force).

3.
According to Kirchoff's first law
a) Currents flowing towards a junction from opposites will cancel one another
b) Current going to each branch of a parallel circuit will divide equal
c) Following the second Kirchoff's Law the voltage sum of voltage drops in a series circuit will be same in all the resistors connected
d) Voltage can be measured using an integrated ohmmeter frequency meter in a circuit
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Currents flowing towards a junction from opposites will cancel one another (Algebraic sum is zero)

Explanation: KCL states that the algebraic sum of currents entering a node is zero (Sum in = Sum out). Option A describes the summation/cancellation effect at a node.

4.
Which one among the following is NOT an effect of electricity on the human body?
a) Burns on the skin
b) Causes an irregular heart beat
c) Relaxes the body muscles
d) Causes an increase in the blood flow
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Relaxes the body muscles

Explanation: Electric shock typically causes violent muscle contraction (tetanus), not relaxation. It can also cause burns and arrhythmia.

5.
Which of the following is NOT a form of an energy in an AC circuit?
a) Constant power
b) Reactive power
c) Real power
d) Apparent power
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Constant power

Explanation: AC power oscillates. The standard forms are Real (Active) Power (kW), Reactive Power (kVAR), and Apparent Power (kVA).

6.
Which one of the following is NOT a factor determining the magnitude of magnetic field?
a) Magnetic field density (B)
b) Orientation with respect to surface area
c) The area of the surface (A)
d) The manner in which windings are mounted on the core
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) The manner in which windings are mounted on the core

Explanation: Magnetic flux ($\Phi = B \cdot A \cos \theta$) depends on field density, area, and orientation. The winding mounting method affects inductance but is not a primary variable in the basic flux definition formula.

7.
When current flows through an ideal conductor?
a) The conductor resistance increases
b) The conductor resistance decreases
c) A magnetic field is created around the conductor
d) The conductor becomes heated up
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) A magnetic field is created around the conductor

Explanation: Oersted's discovery states that current flowing through any conductor creates a magnetic field around it. An ideal conductor has zero resistance, so it would not heat up (d).

8.
Which one among the following is NOT a force acting on the pointer of a measuring instrument?
a) Deflecting force
b) Centrifugal force
c) Damping force
d) Controlling force
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Centrifugal force

Explanation: The three essential torques in analog instruments are Deflecting, Controlling, and Damping torques. Centrifugal force is not relevant to the pointer's operation.

10.
The following equations are true about DC machines. Which one is NOT correct?
a) $V = E_b + I_a R_a$ (Motor)
b) $V = E_b - I_a R_a$ (Incorrect form for Motor)
c) $E_g = V + I_a R_a$ (Generator)
d) $I_a = (V - E_b) / R_a$ (Motor current)
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) $V = E_b - I_a R_a$

Explanation: For a motor, $V = E_b + I_a R_a$. For a generator, $V = E_g - I_a R_a$. Option B mixes signs incorrectly for standard conventions.

11.
Which one of the following is NOT a contributing factor to losses in DC generators?
a) Frequency
b) Flux density
c) Volume and type of core
d) The resistance of coil
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Frequency (Contextual)

Explanation: While magnetic reversals cause core losses (frequency dependent), the DC output has $f=0$. However, the armature does experience AC. But usually, resistance (Copper loss), Flux density/Volume (Core loss) are primary. Frequency is intrinsic to speed. If one must be chosen, in DC output context, frequency is zero.

12.
Which of the following is NOT an advantage of flux control method used to control speed of a DC motor?

b) It provides relatively smooth and easy control

Explanation: This is a limitation (disadvantage), not an advantage. Flux control is typically used for speeds above rated speed by weakening the flux.

13.
The speed of a 4 pole, 60HZ motor in RPM is
a) 2400
b) 1800
c) 1200
d) 1500
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) 1800

Explanation: Synchronous speed $N_s = \frac{120f}{P} = \frac{120 \times 60}{4} = \frac{7200}{4} = 1800$ RPM.

17.
The direction of rotation of a DC series motor can be changed by
a) Interchanging supply terminals
b) Interchanging field terminals
c) Either A or B
d) None of the above
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Interchanging field terminals

Explanation: To reverse a DC motor, you must reverse the current in EITHER the armature OR the field, but not both. Interchanging supply terminals (A) reverses both, keeping direction same.

21.
Wave winding is composed of
a) An even number of conductors
b) An odd number of conductors
c) An even number which is exact multiple of poles + 2
d) An even number which is exact multiple of poles
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) An even number which is exact multiple of poles + 2 (Commutator Pitch related)

Explanation: For wave winding, the number of commutator segments/coils ($C$) relates to poles ($P$) by $C = (P \times Y_c) \pm 1$. This implies specific integer constraints. Usually, $Z = 2C \pm 2$. Option C reflects the $2P \pm 2$ formula structure often taught.

Explanation: In a resistive AC circuit, there is no phase shift ($\phi = 0$) between voltage and current.

(b) State the two factors considered when selecting a capacitor. (2 marks)

Show Answer

(a) Factors affecting Capacitance ($C = \frac{\epsilon A}{d}$):

  1. Area of plates (A): Directly proportional.
  2. Distance between plates (d): Inversely proportional.
  3. Permittivity of dielectric ($\epsilon$): Directly proportional.

  2. Capacitance Value.
  3. Type of dielectric (Application).
  4. Tolerance.
42.
Determine the capacitance of a capacitor of area $400cm^2$ separated by a dielectric of thickness 0.25mm, whose relative permittivity is 5. Take $\epsilon_0 = 8.85 \times 10^{-12}$. (5 marks)
Show Answer

Formula: $C = \frac{\epsilon_0 \epsilon_r A}{d}$

Given:
$A = 400 cm^2 = 400 \times 10^{-4} m^2 = 0.04 m^2$
$d = 0.25 mm = 0.25 \times 10^{-3} m$
$\epsilon_r = 5$, $\epsilon_0 = 8.85 \times 10^{-12}$

Calculation:
$C = \frac{8.85 \times 10^{-12} \times 5 \times 0.04}{0.25 \times 10^{-3}}$
$C = \frac{1.77 \times 10^{-12}}{0.25 \times 10^{-3}}$
$C = 7.08 \times 10^{-9} F = 7.08 nF$

43.
What is the function of the following devices incorporated in a motor protection switch gear?
(a) Thermal Trip (2.5 marks)
(b) Magnetic Trip (2.5 marks)
Show Answer

(a) Thermal Trip: Provides protection against Overload. A bimetallic strip bends when heated by sustained excessive current over time, tripping the mechanism.

(b) Magnetic Trip: Provides protection against Short Circuit (Instantaneous high current). An electromagnet attracts an armature instantly when current spikes, tripping the breaker immediately.

Section 3: Long Answer Question

20 Marks
49.

(a) What is the difference between lap and wave windings? (4 marks)

(b) Why are slip-rings used in AC generators? (1 mark)

(c) A three phase 16 pole alternator has a star connected winding with 144 slots and 10 conductors per slot. The flux per pole is 30mW sinusoidally distributed and the speed is 375 rpm. Winding factor = 1. Determine:
i. The frequency and phase voltage. (6 marks)
ii. The line voltage. (2 marks)

(d) State three ideal characteristics of a transformer. (3 marks)

(e) State the effect of the following on induction motors:
i. Over-voltage (2 marks)
ii. Under voltage (2 marks)

Show Answer

(a) Lap vs Wave Winding

  • Lap Winding: Number of parallel paths ($A$) = Number of Poles ($P$). Used for low voltage, high current. Coils overlap.
  • Wave Winding: Number of parallel paths ($A$) = 2. Used for high voltage, low current. Coils spread out in waves.

(b) Slip Rings

To collect alternating current from the rotating armature (or supply DC to rotating field) while allowing rotation without twisting wires.

(c) Calculations

Given: $P=16$, $Slots=144$, $Z_{slot}=10$, $\phi=30mWb=0.03Wb$, $N=375rpm$, $K_w=1$, Star connected.

i. Frequency ($f$):
$f = \frac{PN}{120} = \frac{16 \times 375}{120} = 50 Hz$.

Phase Voltage ($E_{ph}$):
Formula: $E_{ph} = 4.44 K_w f \phi T_{ph}$
Total Conductors $Z = 144 \times 10 = 1440$.
Conductors per phase $Z_{ph} = 1440 / 3 = 480$.
Turns per phase $T_{ph} = Z_{ph} / 2 = 240$.
$E_{ph} = 4.44 \times 1 \times 50 \times 0.03 \times 240$
$E_{ph} = 1598.4 V$.

ii. Line Voltage ($E_L$):
Star connection: $E_L = \sqrt{3} E_{ph}$
$E_L = 1.732 \times 1598.4 \approx 2768.5 V$.

(d) Ideal Transformer Characteristics

  1. No winding resistance (Zero copper loss).
  2. No core losses (Zero hysteresis/eddy current).
  3. 100% coupling (Zero flux leakage).
  4. Infinite permeability of core.

(e) Effects on Induction Motor

  • Over-voltage: Saturation of core, increased magnetizing current, overheating, lower power factor.
  • Under-voltage: Reduced torque ($T \propto V^2$), increased current for same load (overheating), failure to start.
