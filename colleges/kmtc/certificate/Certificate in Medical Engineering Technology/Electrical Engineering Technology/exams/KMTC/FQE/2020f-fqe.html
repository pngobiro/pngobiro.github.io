Section 1: Multiple Choice Questions

40 Marks
1.
The greater the number of turns of coil
a) The smaller will be the strength of the magnetic field
b) The greater will be the strength of magnetic field
c) The smaller will be the strength of the electric field
d) The greater will be the strength of the electric field
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) The greater will be the strength of magnetic field

Explanation: Magnetic field strength (MMF = N·I) is directly proportional to the number of turns (N) and the current (I).

2.
What is the disadvantage of using a short-pitched coil?
a) Total voltage around the coils will be reduced
b) Losses are reduced
c) Efficiency increases
d) Total voltage around the coil is increased
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Total voltage around the coils will be reduced

Explanation: Short pitching reduces the generated EMF by a factor called the pitch factor ($K_p = \cos(\alpha/2)$), where $\alpha$ is the short-pitch angle. While it improves waveform by reducing harmonics, it slightly reduces total voltage.

3.
A one winding consists of how many coils and contractors (conductors)?
a) Two, one
b) One, one
c) One, two
d) Half, one
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) One, two (Contextual interpretation)

Explanation: A single turn (simple coil) consists of two conductors (coil sides) connected at the back. The question likely asks about the basic relationship: 1 Turn = 2 Conductors.

4.
For a 2-pole machine, $360^0$ of electrical cycle is equal to how many degree of mechanical operation?
a) $180^0$
b) $360^0$
c) $90^0$
d) $270^0$
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) $360^0$

Explanation: $\theta_{elec} = \frac{P}{2} \times \theta_{mech}$. For $P=2$, $\theta_{elec} = 1 \times \theta_{mech}$. Thus $360^\circ$ electrical = $360^\circ$ mechanical.

5.
Which law is used in computation of developed E.M.F.?
a) Faraday’s law
b) Amperes law
c) Maxwells law
d) Biot saxart’s law (Biot-Savart)
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Faraday’s law

Explanation: Faraday's Law of Electromagnetic Induction states that the magnitude of induced EMF is proportional to the rate of change of magnetic flux.

6.
The process of producing magnetic field in a system is known as
a) Induced EMF
b) Excitation
c) Experienced force
d) Torque development
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Excitation

Explanation: Excitation refers to supplying current to the field windings of an electric machine to produce the working magnetic flux.

7.
Where is the field winding mounted in a DC machine?
a) Stator
b) Rotor
c) Commutator
d) Brushes
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Stator

Explanation: In a DC machine, the field windings (poles) are stationary (stator), while the armature winding is on the rotating part (rotor).

8.
What is the material used for the brushes in DC machines?
a) Iron
b) Carbon
c) Aluminum
d) Steel
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Carbon

Explanation: Carbon is used because it is a good conductor, self-lubricating, and softer than the copper commutator segments (preventing wear on the commutator).

9.
In a DC machine, rectification process is created out in order to get unidirectional output (DC). The rectification process is carried out by
a) Half way rectifier
b) Full wave rectifier
c) Mechanical rectifier composed of commutator brushes assembly
d) Centre tapped rectifier
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Mechanical rectifier composed of commutator brushes assembly

Explanation: The commutator and brush assembly acts as a mechanical rectifier, converting the AC induced in the armature coils into DC at the terminals.

10.
DC shunt motors are commonly used in
a) Cranes
b) Electric traction
c) Elevator
d) Ladle machines (Lathe machines)
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Ladle machines (Likely typo for Lathe machines/Machine tools)

Explanation: DC shunt motors are constant speed motors, suitable for machine tools like lathes, drills, and centrifugal pumps. Cranes and traction require high starting torque (Series motors).

11.
When a motor runs on no load, then
a) Back EMF is almost equal to the applied voltage
b) Back EMF will be greater than the applied voltage
c) Back EMF will be less than applied voltage
d) Back EMF is exactly equal to the applied voltage
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Back EMF is almost equal to the applied voltage

Explanation: At no load, the armature current is very small (just enough to overcome losses). Since $V = E_b + I_aR_a$ and $I_aR_a$ is negligible, $E_b \approx V$.

12.
In the biomedical instruments like the artificial heart pumps, the commonly used motor is
a) Permanent magnet DC motor
b) Brushless DC motor
c) Shunt motor
d) Series motor
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Brushless DC motor (BLDC)

Explanation: BLDC motors are preferred for medical implants and pumps due to their high reliability, efficiency, low noise, and lack of brushes (which cause wear and particles).

13.
Due to the effect of armature reaction in a DC machine, the value of generated voltage and flux per pole
a) Increase, decrease
b) Decrease, decrease
c) Decrease, increase
d) Increase, increase
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Decrease, decrease

Explanation: Armature reaction causes demagnetization (net reduction in flux per pole) and cross-magnetization. The reduction in flux ($E \propto \phi$) leads to a decrease in generated voltage.

14.
When the speed of an alternator is increased, the frequency
a) Increases
b) Decreases
c) Remains constant
d) May increase or decrease depending on power factor
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Increases

Explanation: Frequency $f = \frac{PN}{120}$. It is directly proportional to speed (N).

15.
A generator which gives DC supply to the rotor of an alternator is called
a) Converter
b) Exeter (Exciter)
c) Inverter
d) Rectifier
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Exciter

Explanation: The DC source used to energize the field windings of an alternator is called the exciter (often a small DC shunt generator mounted on the shaft).

16.
The advantage of salient pole in an alternator is
a) Reduce windage losses
b) Reduce noise
c) Reduce bearing loads and noise
d) Adaptability to low and medium operation span
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Adaptability to low and medium operation span (speed)

Explanation: Salient pole rotors have large diameters and many poles, making them suitable for low-speed drives like hydro-turbines. Cylindrical rotors are for high speeds (turbo).

17.
The EMF generated in an alternator depends on
a) Frequency
b) Flux per pole
c) Coil span factor
d) All of the above
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) All of the above

Explanation: Equation: $E = 4.44 K_c K_d f \phi T$. It depends on Frequency, Flux, and Winding factors (Coil span).

18.
The exciter for an AC generator is
a) Shunt motor
b) Serra motor (Series motor)
c) Shunt generator
d) Compound generator
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Shunt generator

Explanation: A DC shunt generator is commonly used as an exciter because of its self-excited nature and voltage build-up characteristics.

19.
Alternators with a cylindrical pole type of rotors are generally used with prime movers of
a) High speed
b) Low speed
c) Medium speed
d) Both low and high speed
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) High speed

Explanation: Cylindrical (non-salient) rotors are robust, have small diameters, and are balanced for very high speeds (e.g., steam turbines).

20.
The frequency of voltage generated by an alternator having 8 poles and rotating at 250RPM
a) 60Hz
b) 50Hz
c) 25Hz
d) 16 2/3 Hz
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) 16 2/3 Hz (Calculate: $f = \frac{8 \times 250}{120} = \frac{2000}{120} = 16.66$)

Calculation: $f = \frac{PN}{120} = \frac{8 \times 250}{120} = \frac{2000}{120} = 16.666... Hz = 16 \frac{2}{3} Hz$.

3.

i) What is slip as used in a 3-phase induction motor? (2 marks)

ii) A 6-pole, 3-phase induction motor is connected to a 50Hz supply. If it is running at 970 R.P.M, calculate the slip. (3 marks)

Show Answer

i) Slip: The difference between the synchronous speed ($N_s$) of the rotating magnetic field and the actual rotor speed ($N$), usually expressed as a percentage of synchronous speed. $S = \frac{N_s - N}{N_s}$.

ii) Calculation:
$N_s = \frac{120f}{P} = \frac{120 \times 50}{6} = 1000$ RPM.
$N = 970$ RPM.
Slip $S = \frac{1000 - 970}{1000} = \frac{30}{1000} = 0.03$ or $3\%$.

4.
State three (3) advantages and two (2) disadvantages of separately excited generator. (5 marks)
Show Answer

Advantages:

  • Output voltage is stable and independent of load current.
  • Can operate over a wide range of voltages (even low voltage).
  • Positive voltage build-up (no residual magnetism needed).

Disadvantages:

  • Requires a separate DC power source for excitation.
  • More expensive and complex due to extra source.

i) Define a transformer? (1 mark)

ii) State four (4) important applications of a transformer. (4 marks)

Show Answer

i) Definition: A static device that transfers electrical energy from one circuit to another through mutual induction, usually changing voltage and current levels while keeping frequency constant.

ii) Applications:

  • Stepping up voltage for transmission.
  • Stepping down voltage for distribution/utilization.
  • Impedance matching in electronics.
  • Isolation of circuits (Safety).

Section 3: Long Answer Question

20 Marks
49.

a) A shunt generator supplies 96 A at a terminal voltage of 200 V. The armature and field shunt resistance is $1.0\Omega$ and $50\Omega$ respectively. The iron and frictional losses are 2500W. Find:
(i) E.M.F generated (2 marks)
(ii) Copper losses (4 marks)
(iii) Commercial efficiency (4 marks)

b) i) State four (4) characteristics of transformers. (4 marks)
ii) An ideal 25KVA transformer has 500 turns on the primary winding and 40 turns on the secondary winding. The primary connected to 3000V, 50 Hz supply. Calculate:
a) Primary and secondary currents on full-load (2 marks)
b) Secondary E.M.F (2 marks)
c) The maximum core flux (2 marks)

Show Answer

Part (a) Shunt Generator

Given: $V = 200V$, $I_L = 96A$, $R_a = 1.0\Omega$, $R_{sh} = 50\Omega$, Stray Losses = 2500W.

(i) EMF Generated ($E_g$):
Shunt Field Current $I_{sh} = V / R_{sh} = 200 / 50 = 4A$.
Armature Current $I_a = I_L + I_{sh} = 96 + 4 = 100A$.
$E_g = V + I_a R_a = 200 + (100 \times 1) = 200 + 100 = 300V$.

(ii) Copper Losses:
Armature Cu Loss = $I_a^2 R_a = 100^2 \times 1 = 10,000 W$.
Shunt Field Cu Loss = $I_{sh}^2 R_{sh}$ (or $V \cdot I_{sh}$) = $200 \times 4 = 800 W$.
Total Cu Loss = $10,800 W$.

(iii) Commercial Efficiency:
Output Power $P_{out} = V \times I_L = 200 \times 96 = 19,200 W$.
Total Losses = Cu Loss + Iron/Friction = $10,800 + 2500 = 13,300 W$.
Input Power = Output + Losses = $19,200 + 13,300 = 32,500 W$.
Efficiency $\eta = \frac{19,200}{32,500} \times 100\% = 59.08\%$.

Part (b) Transformer

i) Characteristics: High efficiency, No moving parts, Constant frequency, Power in $\approx$ Power out.

ii) Calculation:
$S = 25 KVA = 25,000 VA$, $N_1 = 500$, $N_2 = 40$, $V_1 = 3000V$.

a) Currents:
$I_1 = S / V_1 = 25,000 / 3000 = 8.33 A$.
Transformation Ratio $k = N_2 / N_1 = 40 / 500 = 0.08$.
$I_2 = I_1 / k = 8.33 / 0.08 = 104.17 A$ (or $S/V_2$).

b) Secondary EMF ($V_2$):
$V_2 = V_1 \times k = 3000 \times (40/500) = 3000 \times 0.08 = 240V$.

c) Maximum Flux ($\Phi_m$):
Formula: $E = 4.44 f N \Phi_m$.
Using Primary: $3000 = 4.44 \times 50 \times 500 \times \Phi_m$.
$3000 = 111,000 \Phi_m$.
$\Phi_m = 3000 / 111,000 = 0.027 Wb$ (Webers).
