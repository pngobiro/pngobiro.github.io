Section 1: Multiple Choice Questions

40 Marks
1.
The armature resistance of DC is 0.5, the supply voltage is 200v and back EMF of 196v at full speed. Armature current is?
a) 4A
b) 8A
c) 400A
d) 392A
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) 8A

Explanation: $I_a = \frac{V - E_b}{R_a} = \frac{200 - 196}{0.5} = \frac{4}{0.5} = 8A$.

2.
A commutator is a device fitted to a DC generator so as to
a) Convert AC into placating (pulsating) DC
b) Provide carbon brushes
c) Produce flus
d) Increase voltage
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Convert AC into placating (pulsating) DC

Explanation: The commutator acts as a mechanical rectifier, converting the alternating emf induced in the armature coils into a unidirectional (pulsating) DC current at the external circuit.

3.
The base requirement of a DC machine, armature winding is that it must be?
a) Closed one
b) Wave
c) Lap
d) Open winding
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Closed one

Explanation: Armature windings in DC machines are always closed-circuit windings (re-entrant) to allow continuous current flow and commutation.

4.
The armature of a DC machine is laminated to
a) Reduce the bulk
b) Provide the bulk
c) Insulate eddy currents
d) Reduce eddy currents
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Reduce eddy currents

Explanation: Laminations increase the resistance of the core to eddy currents, thereby reducing eddy current losses.

5.
In a lap winding the number of brushes is always
a) Double the number of poles
b) Same as the number of poles
c) Half the number of poles
d) Two
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Same as the number of poles

Explanation: In Simplex Lap winding, the number of parallel paths ($A$) equals the number of poles ($P$), requiring $P$ brush sets to collect current.

6.
In a DC generator the generated EMF is directly proportional to the
a) Field current
b) Armature conductors
c) Field pole winding
d) Armature current
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Field current (which determines Flux)

Explanation: $E_g \propto \phi N$. Flux ($\phi$) is determined by the field current. Armature conductors ($Z$) are constant for a given machine.

7.
Compensating windings are used in D.C generators
a) Mainly to reduce the eddy currents and provide local short circuits
b) To provide part for circulation of casting air
c) To neutralize the cross magnetism effect of armature reaction
d) None of the above
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) To neutralize the cross magnetism effect of armature reaction

Explanation: Compensating windings are placed in pole faces to neutralize the cross-magnetizing effect of armature reaction under the pole faces, preventing flashover.

8.
Brushes are physically placed in the
a) Magnetic natural axis
b) Geometric natural axis
c) Both magnetic and geometric neutral axis
d) Interpoles
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Magnetic natural (neutral) axis (MNA)

Explanation: For sparkless commutation, brushes should be placed along the Magnetic Neutral Axis (MNA) where the induced EMF in the coil undergoing commutation is zero.

9.
The effect of inserting a resistance in series with the field winding of a shunt motor is
a) Increase the magnetic field
b) Increase the speed of the motor
c) Decrease the armature current
d) Increase the field current
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Increase the speed of the motor

Explanation: Adding resistance reduces the field current ($I_f$), which reduces the flux ($\phi$). Since speed is inversely proportional to flux ($N \propto 1/\phi$), the speed increases.

10.
The shaft torque of a motor is less than armature torque because
a) Copper losses
b) Iron losses
c) Rotational losses
d) Hysteresis losses
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Rotational losses (Iron + Friction & Windage)

Explanation: $T_{shaft} = T_{armature} - T_{losses}$. The losses involved are mechanical (friction/windage) and iron losses, collectively called rotational losses.

11.
In small AC generators
a) The magnetic field is produced by DC electromagnets placed on the stationery member
b) The current induced in the rotating armature is collected by means of slip rings
c) Armature is the revolving member
d) All of the above
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) All of the above

Explanation: In small alternators (unlike large ones), it is common to have a stationary field system and a rotating armature, similar to DC generators but using slip rings instead of a commutator.

12.
Which one of the following types of alternators is used for in hydroelectric power station?
a) None salient pole
b) Turbo-generator
c) Salient pole
d) Steam turbine
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Salient pole

Explanation: Hydro turbines run at low speeds. Salient pole rotors have a large number of poles required to generate 50/60Hz at low RPM.

13.
The maximum speed at which an alternator can be driven to generate 50Hz and 400V having 2 poles is
a) 400 RPM
b) 3600 RPM
c) 3000 RPM
d) 1500 RPM
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) 3000 RPM

Explanation: $N = \frac{120f}{P} = \frac{120 \times 50}{2} = 3000$ RPM.

14.
Three phase alternators are invariably star connected because
a) Magnetic losses are minimized
b) Less number of turns of wire required
c) Smaller conductors can be used
d) Higher terminal voltage is obtained
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Higher terminal voltage is obtained

Explanation: In Star connection, Line Voltage $V_L = \sqrt{3} V_{ph}$. This gives a higher output voltage for the same phase winding compared to Delta ($V_L = V_{ph}$).

15.
While running, a synchronous motor is compelled to run at synchronous speed because
a) Damper windings in its field winding
b) Magnetic lock between stator and rotor poles
c) Induced E.M.F in the rotor field winding by stator flux
d) Compulsion due to Lenz’s Law
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Magnetic lock between stator and rotor poles

Explanation: The rotor poles magnetically lock with the rotating stator magnetic field poles, forcing them to rotate at the same speed.

17.
In a three phase squirrel cage induction motor
a) Rotor conductors re short-circuited through end rings
b) Rotor conductors end are short circuited through slip rings
c) Rotor conductors are kept open
d) None of the above
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Rotor conductors re short-circuited through end rings

Explanation: The defining feature of a squirrel cage rotor is that the bars are permanently shorted at both ends by conducting rings.

18.
In order to operate, a single phase induction motor requires
a) Slip rings
b) Brushes
c) Commutator
d) Starting system
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Starting system

Explanation: Single phase motors are not self-starting (pulsating field, not rotating). They require an auxiliary winding/capacitor (starting system) to create a phase shift.

19.
The capacitor in a capacitor start induction motor is connected in series with the ______ winding
a) Start
b) Run
c) Squirrel cage
d) Compensating
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Start (Auxiliary)

Explanation: The capacitor is placed in the starting winding circuit to create the necessary phase shift for a rotating magnetic field.

20.
Transformer action requires
a) Constant magnetic flux
b) Increasing magnetic flux
c) Alternating magnetic flux
d) Alternating electric flux
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Alternating magnetic flux

Explanation: Transformers operate on mutual induction, which requires a changing (alternating) magnetic flux to induce voltage in the secondary winding.

21.
For the same rating a d.c machine has __________ an a.c machine
a) Less weight than
b) The same weight as
c) More weight than
d) Equal weight as
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) More weight than

Explanation: DC machines are generally heavier and larger than AC machines of the same power rating due to the presence of the commutator and brush gear.

22.
Which formula is used to calculate the generated voltage in a generator?
a) $E_g = V - I_aR_a$
b) $E_g = V + I_aR_a$
c) $E_g = V + I_aR_a / \phi$
d) $E_g = E_b + I_aR_a$
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) $E_g = V + I_aR_a$

Explanation: The generated EMF ($E_g$) must overcome the terminal voltage ($V$) and the internal voltage drop ($I_aR_a$).

23.
DC series motors are used in these applications where ________ is required
a) High torque
b) Constant speed
c) Low-no-load speed
d) High no-load speed
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) High torque (High starting torque)

Explanation: Series motors produce very high starting torque ($T \propto I_a^2$), making them ideal for traction, cranes, and hoists.

24.
In a DC machine, maximum losses occur due to
a) Mechanical losses
b) Iron losses
c) Copper losses
d) Friction and windage losses
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Copper losses (Usually)

Explanation: Under full load conditions, the $I^2R$ (Copper) losses in the armature and field windings typically constitute the largest component of total losses.

25.
The iron-core is used to ________ of the transformer
a) Increase the weight
b) Provide high magnetic coupling
c) Reduce core losses
d) Increase magnetic flux
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Provide high magnetic coupling (Decrease reluctance)

Explanation: The iron core provides a low reluctance path for magnetic flux, ensuring tight coupling between primary and secondary windings.

31.
In a DC machine, the number of commutator segments is equal to
a) Twice the number of poles
b) Number of conductors
c) Number of coils
d) Number of brushes
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Number of coils

Explanation: Each armature coil has two ends. These ends connect to commutator segments. In a standard winding, each segment is connected to two coil ends (one from one coil, one from another). Thus, the number of segments equals the number of coils.

34.
The starting torque of a squirrel cage induction motor is
a) Low
b) Negligible
c) Same as full-load torque
d) Slightly more than full-load torque
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Low (Typically 1.5x FLT, but considered 'low' vs Slip Ring)

Explanation: Standard squirrel cage motors have relatively low starting torque compared to wound rotor types due to low rotor resistance. Option A or D might be valid depending on context, but "Low" is the classic disadvantage taught.

40.
The following are methods of improving commutation except?
a) Using interpoles
b) Resistance commutation
c) Emf commutation
d) Increasing magnetic field
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Increasing magnetic field (Main field)

Explanation: Commutation is improved by Interpoles (EMF commutation) and Carbon brushes (Resistance commutation). Increasing the main magnetic field does not improve commutation and might worsen saturation.

Section 2: Short Answer Questions

40 Marks
41.
A 230V series motor draws 50A from the supply. The resistances of the armature and series field windings are 0.2Ω and 0.1Ω respectively. Calculate:
a) Brush voltage (1 mark)
b) Back EMF (2 marks)
c) Power wasted in the armature (2 marks)
Show Answer

a) Brush Voltage: Assuming a standard drop of 2V total (1V per brush). Or if asking for voltage drop across armature resistance only ($I_a R_a = 50 \times 0.2 = 10V$). Usually implies brush contact drop $\approx 2V$.

b) Back EMF ($E_b$):
$E_b = V - I_a(R_a + R_{se}) - V_{brush}$
Assuming $V_{brush} = 0$: $E_b = 230 - 50(0.2 + 0.1) = 230 - 15 = 215V$.

c) Power Wasted in Armature ($I^2R$):
$P = I_a^2 R_a = 50^2 \times 0.2 = 2500 \times 0.2 = 500W$.

42.
Calculate the speed and open-circuit line and phase voltages of a 4pole 3phase 50Hz star connected alternator with 36 slots and 30 conductors per slot. The flux per pole is 0.05wb and winding factor is 1. (5 marks)
Show Answer

Speed ($N$): $N = \frac{120f}{P} = \frac{120 \times 50}{4} = 1500$ RPM.

Total Conductors ($Z$): $36 \text{ slots} \times 30 \text{ cond/slot} = 1080$.

Conductors per Phase ($Z_{ph}$): $1080 / 3 = 360$.

Turns per Phase ($T_{ph}$): $360 / 2 = 180$.

Phase Voltage ($E_{ph}$): $4.44 K_w f \phi T_{ph} = 4.44 \times 1 \times 50 \times 0.05 \times 180 = 1998V$.

Line Voltage ($E_L$): $\sqrt{3} E_{ph} = 1.732 \times 1998 = 3460.5V$.

43.
i) Name the two types of rotors used in induction motors. (2 marks)
ii) A 4 pole 3phase induction motor operates from a supply whose frequency is 50Hz. Calculate a synchronous speed with which the magnetic field of the stator is rotating and the speed of the rotor. (3 marks)
Show Answer

i) Rotor Types: Squirrel Cage Rotor and Wound (Slip Ring) Rotor.

ii) Synchronous Speed ($N_s$):
$N_s = \frac{120f}{P} = \frac{120 \times 50}{4} = 1500$ RPM.
Note: Rotor speed ($N_r$) depends on slip, which is not given. Usually $N_r \approx 1440$ RPM at full load (4% slip). Without slip data, exact rotor speed cannot be calculated, only $N_s$.

44.
i) A transformer is the most efficient electrical machine. What are the main loses in a transformer? (3 marks)
ii) Name any two types of transformers? (2 marks)
Show Answer

i) Losses:
1. Iron (Core) Losses: Hysteresis and Eddy current.
2. Copper ($I^2R$) Losses: In windings.

ii) Types:
Based on construction: Core type, Shell type.
Based on function: Step-up, Step-down.

49.

i. State and describe five parts of a DC machine. (10 marks)

ii. Draw and label the diagram of capacitor start capacitor run single phase induction motor. (5 marks)

iii. State five applications where a capacitor start capacitor run single phase induction motor are used. (5 marks)

Show Answer

i. Parts of DC Machine

  1. Yoke: Outer frame, provides mechanical protection and magnetic path.
  2. Poles/Pole Shoes: Support field coils and spread flux over armature.
  3. Field Winding: Produces magnetic flux when current flows.
  4. Armature Core: Rotating part, holds armature winding, laminated to reduce losses.
  5. Commutator: Rectifies AC to DC (in generator) or maintains unidirectional torque (in motor).

                           |
      N --------(Main Winding)-----+

iii. Applications

  1. Air Compressors
  2. Refrigerators

  4. Conveyors
