Section 1: Multiple Choice Questions

40 Marks
1.
What is the value of a resistor whose colour code is orange, orange, black, yellow and silver?
A. 330 KΩ
B. 3.3 MΩ
C. 3.3 KΩ
D. 33 KΩ
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B. 3.3 MΩ

Explanation: This is a 5-band code. Band 1 (Orange) = 3, Band 2 (Orange) = 3, Band 3 (Black) = 0, Band 4 (Multiplier Yellow) = $\times 10,000$, Band 5 (Tolerance Silver) = 10%.
Value: $330 \times 10,000 = 3,300,000 \Omega = 3.3 M\Omega$.

2.
Which one of the following is not a factor considered when selecting a capacitor?
A. Tolerance
B. Stability
C. Leakage current
D. Capacitive reactance
Show Answer

Correct Answer: D. Capacitive reactance

Explanation: Capacitive reactance ($X_c$) is a property that depends on frequency during operation ($X_c = 1/2\pi fC$), not a manufacturing specification selected during component purchase like tolerance, stability, or leakage.

3.
A trivalent impurity is added to silicon to create
A. Germanium
B. P-type semiconductor
C. N-type semiconductor
D. A depletion region
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B. P-type semiconductor

Explanation: Trivalent atoms (like Boron, Gallium) have 3 valence electrons and create "holes" (positive charge carriers) when added to Silicon, creating P-type material.

4.
In the forward region of its characteristics, a diode appears as
A. An off switch
B. A high resistance
C. A capacitor
D. An on switch
Show Answer

Correct Answer: D. An on switch

Explanation: When forward biased, a diode conducts current with very low resistance, acting similarly to a closed (on) switch.

5.
For a junction FET in the pinch – off region when the drain voltage is increased, the drain current
A. Becomes zero
B. Abruptly decreases
C. Abruptly increases
D. Remains constant
Show Answer

Correct Answer: D. Remains constant

Explanation: In the pinch-off (saturation) region, the JFET acts as a constant current source; $I_D$ remains relatively constant ($I_{DSS}$) despite increases in $V_{DS}$.

6.
Which MOSFET allows the drain current to flow even with zero gate to source voltage?
A. Depletion MOSFET
B. Enhancement MOSFET
C. Depletion – enhancement MOSFET
D. All of the above
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A. Depletion MOSFET

Explanation: Depletion-mode MOSFETs have a physical channel and are "normally on" devices, conducting current at $V_{GS} = 0$. Enhancement types are "normally off".

7.
When the BJT is switched on, it is said to be
A. Shorted
B. Open
C. Saturated
D. Cut-off
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C. Saturated

Explanation: A BJT used as a switch is driven into Saturation (ON state) or Cut-off (OFF state). "Shorted" describes the result (collector to emitter), but "Saturated" is the technical operating mode.

8.
In a BJT, the largest current flows
A. In the emitter
B. In the collector
C. In the base
D. Through the collector base junction
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A. In the emitter

Explanation: Emitter current is the sum of Collector and Base currents ($I_E = I_C + I_B$). Therefore, it is always the largest current.

9.
In properly biased BJT, an increase in base current causes increase in
A. Ic only
B. IE only
C. Both Ic and IE
D. Leakage current
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C. Both Ic and IE

Explanation: Since $I_C = \beta I_B$ and $I_E = (\beta + 1)I_B$, increasing the base current ($I_B$) increases both the collector current ($I_C$) and the emitter current ($I_E$).

10.
The emitter of a transistor is generally heavily doped because it
A. Has to dissipate maximum power
B. Has to supply charge current
C. It is the first region of the transistor
D. Must possess low resistance
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B. Has to supply charge current (Inject carriers)

Explanation: The emitter is heavily doped to inject a large number of charge carriers (electrons in NPN, holes in PNP) into the base region.

11.
The dc load line of a transistor circuit
A. Has a negative slope
B. It is a curved line
C. Gives graphic relationship between Ib & Ic
D. Does not contain a point
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A. Has a negative slope

Explanation: The DC load line represents $V_{CE} = V_{CC} - I_C R_C$, which is a straight line with a negative slope of $-1/R_C$.

12.
After firing an SCR, the gate current is removed. The current in the SCR will
A. Remain the same
B. Immediately falls to zero
C. Rise up
D. Rise a little bit and then fall to zero
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A. Remain the same

Explanation: An SCR is a latching device. Once it turns on (fires), the gate loses control, and the anode current continues to flow even if the gate signal is removed.

13.
Triac is a semiconductor power electronic device which contains
A. Two SCRs connected in reverse parallel
B. Two SCRs connected in parallel
C. Two SCRs connected in series
D. Two BJTs connected in series
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A. Two SCRs connected in reverse parallel (Anti-parallel)

Explanation: A TRIAC behaves functionally as two thyristors connected in inverse parallel with a common gate, allowing it to conduct in both directions.

14.
__________ is a self-generation type transducer that can produce an electrical voltage or current output without utilizing any external power source.
A. A passive transducer
B. An active transducer
C. A primary transducer
D. A secondary transducer
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B. An active transducer

Explanation: Active transducers generate their own electrical output (e.g., Piezoelectric crystals, Thermocouples, Photovoltaic cells) without external power.

15.
Chose the correct statement
A. MOSFET is a unipolar voltage controlled two terminal device
B. MOSFET is a bipolar current controlled three terminal device
C. MOSFET is unipolar voltage controlled, three terminal device
D. MOSFET is a bipolar, current controlled two terminal device
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C. MOSFET is unipolar voltage controlled, three terminal device

Explanation: MOSFETs use only one type of charge carrier (Unipolar), are controlled by Gate Voltage (Voltage controlled), and have three terminals (Gate, Drain, Source - ignoring Body).

16.
Amplifier stages in dc amplifier are?
A. Directly coupled
B. Transformer coupled
C. RC coupled
D. Impedance coupled
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A. Directly coupled

Explanation: DC amplifiers (Direct Current/Direct Coupled) must amplify signals down to 0Hz, so capacitors or transformers (which block DC) cannot be used.

17.
Why is the circuit of DC amplifier cheaper?
A. More resolutions used
B. More components accommodated
C. Complex
D. No expensive coupling devices
Show Answer

Correct Answer: D. No expensive coupling devices

Explanation: Direct coupling eliminates the need for expensive and bulky coupling capacitors or transformers, reducing cost.

18.
__________ multi-vibrator is a square wave oscillator.
A. Monostable
B. Astable
C. Bistable
D. None of the above
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B. Astable

Explanation: An astable multivibrator free-runs, switching continuously between two states, generating a square wave output without external triggers.

19.
A circuit which consist of quasi stable state is called?
A. Bistable
B. Monostable
C. Tristable
D. Astable
Show Answer

Correct Answer: D. Astable (Both states quasi-stable) or B (One quasi-stable)

Explanation: Astable has only quasi-stable states (0 stable). Monostable has 1 stable and 1 quasi-stable. "Consist of quasi stable state" usually refers to the oscillating nature of the Astable.

20.
Multi-vibrators belong to a family oscillator commonly called?
A. Relaxation oscillators
B. Dynamic oscillators
C. Sinusoidal oscillator
D. Harmonic oscillator
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A. Relaxation oscillators

Explanation: Multivibrators rely on the charging and discharging of capacitors (relaxing) to time their switching, producing non-sinusoidal waves.

21.
Multi-vibrator which can produce very short pulse or a much longer rectangular shaped wave form is called
A. Astable
B. Bistable
C. Monostable
D. Tristable
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C. Monostable

Explanation: A monostable multivibrator (One-Shot) produces a single output pulse of a defined duration (determined by RC time constant) when triggered.

22.
__________ multi-vibrator can be used as an electronic delay line.
A. Monostable
B. Astable
C. Bistable
D. All of the above
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A. Monostable

Explanation: By creating a pulse of a specific width, the trailing edge of the pulse can be used to trigger another event a fixed time after the initial trigger, creating a time delay.

23.
An oscillator converts
A. AC power to DC
B. DC power to AC
C. Mechanical power to AC
D. AC power to mechanical power
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B. DC power to AC

Explanation: An electronic oscillator takes energy from a DC power supply and converts it into a periodic AC signal.

24.
An oscillator produces __________ oscillators (oscillations).
A. Damped
B. Undamped
C. Modulated
D. None of the above
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B. Undamped

Explanation: A functioning oscillator produces continuous, constant-amplitude (undamped) signals by replenishing energy losses via positive feedback.

25.
An oscillator employs __________ feedback.
A. Positive
B. Negative
C. Positive and negative
D. None of the above
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A. Positive

Explanation: Positive (regenerative) feedback is required to sustain oscillations.

26.
Which of the following is correct for digital circuit?
A. Less susceptible to noise
B. Uses transistor to create logic gates
C. Easier to perform error detection
D. All of the above
Show Answer

Correct Answer: D. All of the above (A is the primary characteristic often cited)

Explanation: Digital circuits are robust against noise (A), built from transistors (B), and allow error correction/detection (C).

__________ are basic logic gates.
A. NOT, OR and NOR
B. NAND, NOR and AND
C. AND, OR and EXOR
D. NOT, AND, and OR

Correct Answer: D. NOT, AND, and OR

Explanation: The three fundamental logic operations are Inversion (NOT), Conjunction (AND), and Disjunction (OR).

28.
__________ are universal gates.
A. NOT and OR
B. NAND and NOR
C. AND and OR
D. NOT, AND & OR
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B. NAND and NOR

Explanation: NAND and NOR gates are universal because any Boolean function (AND, OR, NOT) can be implemented using only one type of these gates.

29.
The output of an AND gate with three inputs A, B & C is high when
A. A=1, B=1 and C=0
B. A = 0, B = 0 and C= 0
C. A=1, B=1 and C=1
D. A = 1, B=0 and C=1

Correct Answer: C. A=1, B=1 and C=1

30.
If a 3-input NOR gate has eight input possibilities, how many of those possibilities will result in a HIGH output?
A. 1
B. 2
C. 7
D. 8
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A. 1

Explanation: A NOR gate output is High ONLY when all inputs are Low (0,0,0). Any other combination produces a Low output.

31.
What logic function is produced by adding inverters to the inputs of an OR gate?
A. NOR
B. NAND
C. AND
D. XNOR
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B. NAND

Explanation: By De Morgan's Theorem, $\bar{A} + \bar{B} = \overline{A \cdot B}$. Thus, an OR gate with inverted inputs performs the NAND function.

32.
The output of a OR gate is high
A. Only when all the inputs are low
B. Only when all the inputs are high
C. Only when at least one input is high
D. Only when at least one input is low
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C. Only when at least one input is high

Explanation: The definition of the OR function is that the output is high if any (one or more) of the inputs are high.

An oscillator differs from an amplifier because
A. Has more gain
B. Requires no input signal
C. Requires no dc supply
D. Always has the same input

Correct Answer: B. Requires no input signal

Explanation: An amplifier requires an AC input signal to amplify. An oscillator generates an AC signal from DC power without an external AC input.

37.
The V – I characteristics for a triac in the first and third quadrants are essentially identical to those of

Explanation: A TRIAC behaves like two SCRs in inverse parallel. Its conduction characteristic in Quadrant 1 and Quadrant 3 looks like the forward conduction characteristic of an SCR.

42.
Explain the following in respect to semiconductors: (5 marks)
i. Depletion region (1 mark)
ii. Zener breakdown (2 marks)
iii. Avalanche breakdown (2 marks)
Show Answer
  • Depletion region: The area near the PN junction where mobile charge carriers (electrons and holes) have recombined and been depleted, leaving immobile ions.
  • Zener breakdown: Occurs in heavily doped diodes with narrow depletion regions. High electric field causes direct rupture of covalent bonds (quantum tunneling). Occurs at lower voltages (< 6V).
  • Avalanche breakdown: Occurs in lightly doped diodes. Minority carriers accelerate, collide with atoms, and knock out more electrons (impact ionization), creating a chain reaction. Occurs at higher voltages.
