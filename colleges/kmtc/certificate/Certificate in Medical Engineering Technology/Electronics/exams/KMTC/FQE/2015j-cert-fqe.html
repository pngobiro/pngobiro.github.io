Section A: Multiple Choice Questions

20 Marks
1.
In the active region, the collector current of a transistor is not changed significantly by
a) Base supply voltage
b) Base current
c) Current gain
d) Collector resistance
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Collector resistance

Explanation: In the active region, the collector current ($I_C$) behaves like a constant current source controlled by the base current ($I_B$). $I_C = \beta I_B$. While $I_B$ depends on the base supply and biasing, and $\beta$ is the gain, changing the collector resistance ($R_C$) changes the collector voltage ($V_{CE}$) but has very little effect on $I_C$ as long as the transistor remains in the active region (output curves are nearly horizontal).

2.
Which of the following is not a characteristic of a UJT?
a) intrinsic standoff ratio
b) negative resistance
c) peak-point voltage
d) bidirectional conduction
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) bidirectional conduction

Explanation: A Unijunction Transistor (UJT) conducts current primarily in one direction (Emitter to Base 1) once triggered. It is not a bidirectional device like a DIAC or TRIAC.

3.
An n-channel D-MOSFET with a positive VGS is operated in
a) The depletion-mode
b) The enhancement-mode
c) Cut off
d) Saturation
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) The enhancement-mode

Explanation: A Depletion-mode MOSFET (D-MOSFET) is normally on ($I_{DSS}$ flows at $V_{GS}=0$). Applying a negative $V_{GS}$ (for n-channel) depletes the channel (Depletion mode). Applying a positive $V_{GS}$ enhances the channel, increasing current (Enhancement mode).

4.
A unijunction transistor has
a) Anode, cathode and gate
b) Two bases and one emitter
c) Two anodes and one gate
d) Anode, cathode and two gates
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Two bases and one emitter

Explanation: The UJT has three terminals: Emitter (E), Base 1 (B1), and Base 2 (B2).

5.
After firing an SCR, the gating pulse is removed. The current in the SCR will
a) Remain the same
b) Immediately fall to zero
c) Rise up
d) Rise a little then fall to zero
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Remain the same

Explanation: The SCR is a latching device. Once triggered (fired) into conduction, the gate loses control. The SCR remains ON even if the gate pulse is removed, provided the anode current is above the holding current ($I_H$).

6.
If the reverse bias on the gate of a JFET is increased, the width of the conducting channel is
a) decreased
b) increased
c) remain the same
d) tunnelled
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) decreased

Explanation: Increasing the reverse bias on the Gate-Source junction widens the depletion layers, which encroach into the channel, effectively narrowing (decreasing) the width of the conducting channel and reducing current.

7.
An astable multivibrator has
a) One stable state
b) Three stable states
c) Two stable states
d) No stable state
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) No stable state

Explanation: An astable multivibrator continuously oscillates between two quasi-stable states, never settling in a stable state. (Monostable has 1, Bistable has 2).

8.
An amplifier is said to suffer from distortion when its output is
a) low
b) different from its output
c) noisy
d) larger than its input
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) different from its output (Likely typo for "different from its input wave shape")

Explanation: Distortion occurs when the output signal wave shape differs from the input signal wave shape (excluding amplitude/phase changes). Option B is the intended answer despite the typo.

9.
Feed back in amplifier always helps to
a) Control the output
b) Increase its gain
c) decrease input impedance
d) stabilizes its gain
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) stabilizes its gain

Explanation: Negative feedback is used in amplifiers primarily to stabilize the gain against variations in device parameters, temperature, etc., at the cost of reducing the overall gain.

10.
If the value of collector current IC increases, then the value of VCE
a) remain the same
b) decreases
c) increases
d) oscillate
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) decreases

Explanation: From the load line equation $V_{CE} = V_{CC} - I_C R_C$. As $I_C$ increases, the voltage drop across the collector resistor ($I_C R_C$) increases, causing $V_{CE}$ to decrease.

Section D: Essay Questions (Answer ANY 5)

50 Marks (10 each)
1.

a) Explain the following terms; (6 marks)
(i) Capacitor
(ii) Resistor
(iii) Capacitance
(iv) Tolerance as applied to i & ii
(v) Passive element
(vi) Active element

b) State four applications of capacitors. (4 marks)

Show Answer

a) Definitions:

  • Capacitor: A passive electronic component that stores electrical energy in an electric field between two conductive plates.
  • Resistor: A passive electrical component that implements electrical resistance as a circuit element, used to reduce current flow or drop voltage.
  • Capacitance: The ability of a component (capacitor) to store an electric charge, measured in Farads (F). Ratio of charge to voltage ($C=Q/V$).
  • Tolerance: The permissible deviation of the actual value of a component (resistance or capacitance) from its nominal (rated) value, usually expressed as a percentage.
  • Passive element: A component that cannot generate energy or provide gain (amplification). It consumes, stores, or releases energy (e.g., Resistor, Capacitor, Inductor).
  • Active element: A component capable of providing power gain or controlling current/voltage (e.g., Transistors, Op-amps, Batteries).

b) Applications of Capacitors:

  1. Filtering (smoothing rectifiers).
  2. Coupling (passing AC, blocking DC) between amplifier stages.
  3. Decoupling/Bypassing noise to ground.
  4. Timing circuits (with resistors).
  5. Tuning (LC tank circuits).
  6. Power factor correction.

(ii) Negative feedback
(iii) Distortion

a) Definitions:

  • Feedback: The process of feeding a fraction of the output signal back to the input terminal.
  • Negative Feedback: Feedback where the signal fed back is 180Â° out of phase with the input signal, opposing it.

4.

a) What is a transducer? (2 marks)

b) State four types of transducers and their applications. (8 marks)

Show Answer

a) Transducer: A device that converts one form of energy into another. In electronics/instrumentation, it typically converts a physical quantity (non-electrical) into an electrical signal.

b) Types and Applications:

5.

a) Explain the operation of an SCR using a two- transistor analogy. (5 marks)

b) Draw a V/I characteristics of SCR and label. (3 marks)

c) How does the gate voltage affect the switching voltage of an SCR? (2 marks)

Show Answer

a) Two-Transistor Analogy:

An SCR can be modeled as two interconnected transistors: a PNP ($Q_1$) and an NPN ($Q_2$). The collector of $Q_1$ feeds the base of $Q_2$, and the collector of $Q_2$ feeds the base of $Q_1$. This creates a positive feedback loop. When a small gate current triggers $Q_2$, it draws collector current from $Q_1$'s base, turning $Q_1$ on. $Q_1$'s collector current then feeds $Q_2$'s base, reinforcing the conduction. Both saturate, latching the device ON.

b) V/I Characteristics:

          ^
          |   / (Conduction)
          |  /
          | / <-- Holding Current
Breakover |/
Voltage   |
 <--------+-----------------> Voltage (V)
(Reverse) |        (Forward Blocking)
          |

c) Gate Voltage Effect:

Increasing the gate current (via gate voltage) reduces the forward breakover voltage required to switch the SCR on. With sufficient gate drive, the SCR switches on almost like a normal diode at low voltage.

6.
Explain the following terms: (10 marks)
a) Insulators
b) Conductor
c) Semiconductor
d) Holding current
e) Dielectric
Show Answer
  • a) Insulators: Materials with very high resistivity and large energy band gaps that do not allow electric current to flow freely (e.g., Rubber, Glass).
  • b) Conductor: Materials with low resistance and overlapping energy bands containing many free electrons, allowing current to flow easily (e.g., Copper, Aluminum).
  • c) Semiconductor: Materials with conductivity between conductors and insulators (e.g., Silicon). Their conductivity can be modified by doping or temperature.
  • d) Holding Current ($I_H$): The minimum anode current required to maintain a thyristor (SCR) in the ON (conducting) state. If current drops below this, it turns OFF.
  • e) Dielectric: An insulating material that can be polarized by an applied electric field, typically used between the plates of a capacitor to store energy electrostatically.
7.
Explain the following terms of the output characteristics of common emitter amplifier. (8 marks)
a) Load line
b) Cut-off region
c) Saturation region
d) Active region
e) Breakdown region (Added to list from context)
Show Answer
  • a) Load Line: A linear line superimposed on the characteristics representing the circuit constraints ($V_{CC}$ and $R_L$), defining all possible operating points.
  • b) Cut-off Region: The region where base current $I_B = 0$ and collector current $I_C \approx 0$ (leakage only). The transistor is OFF (Switch open).
  • c) Saturation Region: The region where $I_C$ is maximum ($V_{CC}/R_L$) and $V_{CE}$ is minimum ($\approx 0.2V$). Both junctions are forward biased. Transistor is fully ON (Switch closed).
  • d) Active Region: The region between cut-off and saturation where the Emitter-Base junction is forward biased and Collector-Base is reverse biased. Used for linear amplification.
  • e) Breakdown Region: The region where $V_{CE}$ exceeds the device limit, causing avalanche breakdown and potential destruction of the transistor.
