Section 1: Multiple Choice Questions

40 Marks
1.
Number of valence electrons in a silicon atom are?
a) 1
b) 4
c) 8
d) 16
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) 4

Explanation: Silicon is a tetravalent element (Group 14), meaning it has 4 valence electrons in its outer shell.

2.
The most commonly used semi-conductor element is?
a) Silicon
b) Germanium
c) Gallium
d) Carbon
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Silicon

Explanation: Silicon is abundant, cheap, and has superior thermal stability compared to Germanium, making it the industry standard.

3.
The valence electrons of a conductor also called?
a) Bound electrons
b) Free electrons
c) Nucleus
d) Proton
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Free electrons

Explanation: In conductors, valence electrons are loosely bound and can move freely through the lattice, hence "free electrons".

4.
An intrinsic semi-conductor at room temperature has?
a) A few electrons and holes
b) Many holes
c) Many free electrons
d) No holes
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) A few electrons and holes

Explanation: At room temperature, thermal energy breaks some covalent bonds, generating a small number of electron-hole pairs.

5.
At room temperature, an intrinsic semiconductor has some holes in it due to?
a) Doping
b) Free electrons
c) Thermal energy
d) Valence electrons
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Thermal energy

Explanation: Thermal excitation lifts valence electrons into the conduction band, leaving behind holes.

6.
Electrons are the minority carrierâ€™s in
a) Extrinsic semiconductors
b) P-type semiconductors
c) Intrinsic semiconductors
d) N-type semiconductors
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) P-type semiconductors

Explanation: In P-type material, holes are the majority carriers (from doping) and electrons are minority carriers (thermally generated).

7.
In a semi-conductor diode, depletion layer is caused by
a) Doping
b) Recombination
c) Barrier potential
d) Ions
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Recombination (or Diffusion)

Explanation: Electrons from N-side diffuse to P-side and recombine with holes, creating a region depleted of mobile carriers.

8.
Avalanche in a diode occurs at
a) Barrier potential
b) Depletion layer
c) Knee voltage
d) Break down voltage
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Break down voltage

Explanation: Avalanche breakdown occurs when the reverse bias voltage reaches the breakdown point.

9.
The potential barrier at a silicon diode is
a) 0.3V
b) 0.7V
c) 1V
d) 5V
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) 0.7V

Explanation: Silicon diodes have a barrier potential of ~0.7V, whereas Germanium is ~0.3V.

10.
A diode is a
a) Bilateral device
b) Non-linear device
c) Linear device
d) Unipolar device
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Non-linear device

Explanation: The V-I characteristic of a diode is exponential, not a straight line (linear), so it is a non-linear device.

19.
If base current is $100\mu A$ and current gain is 100, then collector current is
a) 1A
b) 10A
c) 1mA
d) 10mA
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) 10mA

Explanation: $I_C = \beta \times I_B = 100 \times 100\mu A = 10,000\mu A = 10mA$.

23.
The relation between $\alpha$ and $\beta$ is
a) $\alpha = \beta/(\beta + 1)$
b) $\beta = \alpha/(\alpha + 1)$ (Incorrect formula shown in options)
c) $\alpha = \beta(\beta + 1)$
d) $\alpha = \beta/(\beta - 1)$
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) $\alpha = \beta/(\beta + 1)$

Explanation: Current gain $\alpha = I_C/I_E$ and $\beta = I_C/I_B$. Since $I_E = I_B + I_C$, the relationship is $\alpha = \frac{\beta}{1+\beta}$.

39.
The universal gate is
a) NAND gate
b) OR gate
c) AND gate
d) None of the above
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) NAND gate

Explanation: NAND and NOR gates are universal gates because any boolean logic function can be constructed using only them.

40.
The inverter is
a) NOT gate
b) OR gate
c) AND gate
d) None of the above
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) NOT gate

Explanation: A NOT gate performs the function of logical inversion.

44.

i) Differentiate between zener breakdown and avalanche breakdown across a PN junction. (3 marks)

ii) State two main applications of zener diodes in electronics. (2 marks)

Show Answer

i) Difference:

  • Zener Breakdown: Occurs in heavily doped diodes at low voltages (< 6V) due to high electric field rupturing bonds. Negative temp coefficient.
  • Avalanche Breakdown: Occurs in lightly doped diodes at higher voltages due to collision of accelerated carriers (impact ionization). Positive temp coefficient.

ii) Applications:

  1. Voltage Regulation (Stabilization).
  2. Voltage Reference.
  3. Waveform Clipping/Limiting.

Section 3: Long Answer Question

20 Marks
49.

(a) With the aid of a well labelled diagram, explain the working principle of a UJT relaxation oscillator. (10 marks)

(b) You have been provided with the following digital circuit.

 A ---\
       OR )-----\
 B ---/          \
                  AND )---- Y
 C --------------/

(i) Work out the Boolean expression for output Y. (5 marks)
(ii) Develop the truth table for the output Y using the three inputs A, B and C. (5 marks)

Show Answer

a) UJT Relaxation Oscillator

Working Principle:

  1. Capacitor $C$ charges through resistor $R$ until the voltage across it reaches the Peak Point Voltage ($V_P$) of the UJT.
  2. At $V_P$, the UJT fires (Emitter-Base 1 junction conducts), discharging the capacitor rapidly through $R_{B1}$ and the UJT.
  3. This discharge creates a sharp pulse across $R_{B1}$.
  4. When capacitor voltage drops below the Valley Point ($V_V$), the UJT turns off.
  5. The capacitor starts charging again, repeating the cycle to produce a sawtooth wave at the emitter and pulses at the base.

b) Digital Logic

(i) Expression:
Gate 1 is OR ($A+B$).
Gate 2 is AND ($Input 1 \cdot Input 2$).
$Y = (A+B) \cdot C$.

(ii) Truth Table:

ABCA+BY

01111
