Section 1: Multiple Choice Questions

40 Marks
1.
The number of PN junctions in a thyristor are
a) 1
b) 2
c) 3
d) 4
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) 3

Explanation: A thyristor (SCR) is a 4-layer device (PNPN structure) which creates 3 PN junctions (J1, J2, J3).

2.
A thyristor can be termed as a
a) DC switch
b) AC switch
c) Both A and B
d) Square ware switch
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) DC switch

Explanation: A thyristor (SCR) is a unidirectional device. While it is used in AC circuits for phase control, fundamentally it acts as a latching switch for DC or rectified current. TRIACs are AC switches.

3.
The following are statements regarding a TRIAC (labeled TRAC):
i. It is unidirectional
ii. It is equivalent to two SCRs connected to ANTI parallel when in operation
iii. It can be turned on by applying the positive and negative voltage to the gate with respect to the MTI terminal.
Which statement(s) is correct?
a) i
b) i and ii
c) ii only
d) ii and iii only
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) ii and iii only

Explanation: A TRIAC is a bidirectional device (i is false). It behaves like two SCRs in inverse parallel (ii is true) and can be triggered by either positive or negative gate pulses (iii is true).

4.
Given that the holding current level of an SCR is $I_H$, latching current level of an SCR is $I_L$ and its anode current is $I_A$, then an SCR is turned off when
a) $I_A > I_H$
b) $I_A < I_H$
c) $I_A = I_L$
d) $I_A < I_L$
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) $I_A < I_H$

Explanation: An SCR turns off (commutes) only when the anode current drops below the **Holding Current** ($I_H$).

5.
As gate current increases forward break over voltage will
a) Remain the same
b) Decrease
c) Increase
d) Increase and then decrease
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Decrease

Explanation: Injecting current into the gate reduces the forward voltage required to break over and turn on the thyristor. Higher gate current allows it to turn on at lower anode voltages.

6.
Linear Variable Differential Transformer (LVDT) is a
a) Capacitive transducer
b) Resistive transducer
c) Inductive transducer
d) None of the above
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Inductive transducer

Explanation: LVDT operates on the principle of mutual inductance between a primary coil and two secondary coils, varied by the position of a magnetic core.

7.
Piezoelectric crystals are used for measurement of ________ charges.
a) Static
b) Dynamic
c) Static and dynamic
d) All of the above
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Dynamic

Explanation: Piezoelectric sensors are excellent for measuring dynamic changes (vibration, shock, changing pressure) but cannot measure static forces effectively because the generated charge leaks away over time.

8.
A piezoelectric crystal produces an E.M.F. when
a) External mechanical force is applied
b) External magnetic field is applied
c) Radiant energy stimulates
d) When the junction of the two crystal is treated
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) External mechanical force is applied

Explanation: The piezoelectric effect is the generation of electric charge in response to applied mechanical stress.

9.
Which one of the following instruments can be used for the measurement of temperature above 1500°C?
a) Mercury thermometer
b) Gas thermometer
c) Thermal electric pyrometer
d) All of the above
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Thermal electric pyrometer (or Optical/Radiation Pyrometer)

Explanation: Pyrometers are non-contact devices used for measuring very high temperatures where contact thermometers would melt or fail.

10.
Thermistors are made of
a) Pure metals
b) Pure insulators
c) Sintered mixtures of metallic oxides
d) Pure semiconductors
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Sintered mixtures of metallic oxides

Explanation: Thermistors are ceramic semiconductors made by sintering oxides of metals like manganese, nickel, cobalt, copper, and iron.

11.
The main feature of a large signal amplifier circuits is
a) Power efficiency
b) Distortion introduced by the circuit
c) Heat dissipated by the circuit
d) All of the above
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Power efficiency (Primary concern)

Explanation: Large signal (Power) amplifiers handle large currents and voltages. Their primary design feature/concern is power handling and efficiency to convert DC power to AC signal power.

12.
Class _______ amplifiers are normally operated in a push pull configuration in order to produce an output that is a replica of the input.
a) A
b) B
c) C
d) AB
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) B (and AB)

Explanation: Class B amplifiers conduct for only 180°. To reproduce the full 360° input wave (replica), two transistors are arranged in push-pull, one handling the positive half and the other the negative half.

13.
The Q-point is at cut off for class ________ operation.
a) A
b) B
c) C
d) AB
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) B

Explanation: In Class B operation, the transistor is biased exactly at the cut-off point ($I_C = 0$). It conducts only when the input signal is present.

14.
How many transistors must be used in a class B amplifier to obtain an output for the full cycle of the input signal?
a) 0
b) 1
c) 2
d) 3
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) 2

Explanation: Since one transistor in Class B conducts for only half a cycle, two transistors (Push-Pull pair) are required to cover the full cycle.

15.
By how much does the output signal vary for a class A power amplifier?
a) 360°
b) 180°
c) Between 180° and 360°
d) Less than 180°
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) 360°

Explanation: Class A amplifiers conduct current throughout the entire cycle of the input signal (360° conduction angle).

24.
An oscillator employs ________ feedback.
a) Positive
b) Negative
c) Both positive and negative
d) Open loop feedback
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Positive (Regenerative)

Explanation: Oscillators use positive feedback to sustain oscillations (Barkhausen criterion: loop gain = 1, phase shift = 0/360°).

27.
In crystal oscillator, frequency is stable due to ________ of the crystal.
a) Rigidity
b) Vibrations
c) Low Q
d) High Q
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) High Q (Quality Factor)

Explanation: Quartz crystals have an extremely high Quality factor (Q), which results in a very narrow resonance bandwidth and thus high frequency stability.

29.
In a colpitts’ crystal oscillator, feedback is obtained
a) By magnetic induction
b) By a tickle coil
c) Form center of split capacitors
d) From center of split inductors
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Form center of split capacitors

Explanation: A Colpitts oscillator is characterized by a tank circuit with two capacitors in series (split capacitor) and an inductor. Feedback is taken from the junction of the capacitors.

31.
When a transistor is driven to saturation, ideally the output voltage is
a) Vcc
b) 0
c) Vcc/2
d) 2Vcc
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) 0 (or close to 0V)

Explanation: In saturation, the transistor is fully ON, acting like a closed switch connecting the collector to the emitter (ground). Ideally $V_{CE(sat)} = 0V$ (Practically ~0.2V).

32.
When a transistor is driven to cut off, ideally the output voltage is
a) Vcc
b) 0
c) 2Vcc
d) Vcc/2
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Vcc

Explanation: In cut-off, the transistor is OFF (open switch). No current flows through the collector resistor, so there is no voltage drop. The collector voltage rises to the supply voltage ($V_{CC}$).

36.
The binary number 10101 is equivalent to decimal number
a) 19
b) 12
c) 27
d) 21
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) 21

Explanation: $1(16) + 0(8) + 1(4) + 0(2) + 1(1) = 16 + 4 + 1 = 21$.

40.
Decimal 10 is equal to binary number
a) 1110
b) 1010
c) 1001
d) 1000
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) 1010

Explanation: $10 = 8 + 2 = 1(2^3) + 0(2^2) + 1(2^1) + 0(2^0) \rightarrow 1010_2$.

44.

a) State and explain three conditions that must be fulfilled for a tank circuit to produce undamped oscillations. (3 marks)

b) Convert $12_{10}$ to octal number system. (2 marks)

Show Answer

a) Barkhausen Criteria:

  1. Positive Feedback: The feedback signal must be in phase with the input signal ($0^\circ$ or $360^\circ$ phase shift).
  2. Loop Gain: The product of amplifier gain ($A$) and feedback factor ($\beta$) must be equal to or greater than unity ($|A\beta| \ge 1$) to sustain oscillations.
  3. Low Loss: Circuit resistance must be minimized (or compensated by active element) to prevent damping.

b) Conversion:

$12 \div 8 = 1$ remainder 4.
Read up: $14_8$.

46.
A transistor is used as a switch. If $V_{CC} = 10V$, $R_C = 1K\Omega$. Determine the value of $V_{CE}$ at cutoff and $I_C$ at saturation. (5 marks)
Show Answer

At Cutoff:
Transistor acts as an open switch ($I_C = 0$).
$V_{CE} = V_{CC} - I_C R_C = 10 - 0 = 10V$.

At Saturation:
Transistor acts as a closed switch ($V_{CE} \approx 0$).
$I_{C(sat)} = \frac{V_{CC}}{R_C} = \frac{10V}{1000\Omega} = 0.01A = 10mA$.

49.

a) With the aid of a well labelled diagram, explain the working principle of a UJT relaxation oscillator. (10 marks)

b) You have been provided with the following digital circuit.

 A ------\
          OR )---- X ---\
 B ------/               AND )---- Y
                        /
 C --------------------/

(i) Work out the Boolean expression for output Y. (5 marks)
(ii) Develop the truth table for the output Y using the three inputs A, B and C. (5 marks)

Show Answer

a) UJT Relaxation Oscillator

Diagram: A UJT (Unijunction Transistor) connected with a Capacitor ($C_E$) and Resistor ($R_E$) at the Emitter, and resistors ($R_1, R_2$) at Bases B1 and B2.

Operation:

  1. When power is applied, Capacitor $C_E$ charges through $R_E$ towards $V_{BB}$.
  2. The UJT Emitter is initially OFF (reverse biased).
  3. When capacitor voltage $V_E$ reaches the peak point voltage ($V_P = \eta V_{BB} + V_D$), the Emitter-B1 junction becomes forward biased and the UJT fires (turns ON).
  4. The resistance $R_{B1}$ drops drastically, and $C_E$ discharges rapidly through the Emitter and Base 1.
  5. When $V_E$ drops to the Valley Point ($V_V$), the UJT turns OFF.
  6. The capacitor starts charging again, repeating the cycle. This produces a sawtooth wave at the Emitter and pulses at Base 1.

b) Logic Circuit

(i) Boolean Expression:
Gate 1 is OR: Output = $A + B$.
Gate 2 is AND: Inputs are $(A+B)$ and $C$.
Output $Y = (A + B) \cdot C$.

(ii) Truth Table:

ABCA+BY = (A+B)C
