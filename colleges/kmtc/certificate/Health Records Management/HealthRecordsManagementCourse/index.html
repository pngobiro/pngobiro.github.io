Health Records Management Course

Duration: 25 hours Level: Intermediate

Course Overview

This module provides a comprehensive overview of health records management, essential for healthcare professionals and administrators. It covers the fundamental principles, historical context, practical applications, and legal aspects of managing health records in both traditional and electronic systems. The course is designed to equip learners with the knowledge and skills to effectively manage health information, ensure data quality, and understand the critical role of health records in patient care and healthcare administration.

Course Modules

  1. Introduction to Health Records Management

    This module introduces the basic concepts of health records management, definitions, and the importance of health records in healthcare.

    30 minutes

  2. Historical Background and Purpose of Health Records

    Explore the history of health records and understand the various purposes they serve in modern healthcare systems.

    45 minutes

  3. Purpose and Organizational Structure of Health Records Department

    60 minutes

    60 minutes

    Delving into the Unit Health Records System, covering initiation, prevention of duplication, and the unit number register.

    60 minutes

  7. Unit Health Records System - Part 2 & Types of Health Records Introduction

    Exploring the contents of the unit health record and introducing different types of health records.

    60 minutes

  8. Types of Health Records - Part 1 & Values and Uses Introduction
