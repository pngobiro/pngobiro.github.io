ðŸ“š Admission Procedures

Admission Procedures are the formal steps taken when a patient is accepted into a healthcare facility for in-patient care. These procedures ensure that patients are properly welcomed, informed, and guided through the initial stages of their hospital stay. Key aspects of admission procedures include:

  • Patient Booklet Provision:
    • Information Booklet: Patients/clients should be supplied with a booklet containing information about the hospital before they are admitted. This booklet serves as a welcome and informational guide.
    • Emergency Patients: If they are emergency patients, then they should be supplied with the booklet after admission, ensuring even emergency admissions receive the necessary information as soon as feasible.
    • Booklet Contents: The booklet should typically contain:
      • Layout of the Hospital: Maps and directions to help patients and visitors navigate the facility.
      • Visiting Times for Relatives and Visitors: Clear guidelines on visiting hours to manage visitor flow and patient rest.
      • Ward Identification: Information to help easily identify wards and departments within the hospital.
      • Accommodation Information: Details about accommodation for relatives of very ill patients, mothers, and ill babies, if available, ensuring family support and comfort.
  • Guidance to the Ward:
    • Staff Guidance: After admission, patients/clients should be guided to the ward by a member of staff. This personal escort helps patients feel more comfortable and oriented in a new environment.
    • Introduction to Ward Staff: Patients should be introduced to the sister or nurse responsible for reception and documentation of patients in the ward, establishing initial contact with their ward care team.
  • Feedback to Health Records Department:
    • Information Feedback Loop: Guiding patients and documentation in the ward will ensure a feedback of information to the health records/information department. This loop ensures that admission details are accurately recorded and communicated to the health records department for proper record management.

These admission procedures are designed to make the hospital entry process as smooth and welcoming as possible for patients and their families, while also ensuring that necessary administrative and informational steps are completed.

ðŸ“š Sources of Admission

Patient admissions to a hospital can originate from various sources, reflecting the diverse pathways patients take to access in-patient care. Common Sources of Admission include:

  1. (i) Accident and Emergency Department:
    • Emergency Admissions: Patients who present at the Accident and Emergency (A&E) department with acute conditions or injuries requiring immediate in-patient care are admitted directly from A&E.
    • Urgent Care Needs: Admissions from A&E often involve urgent medical needs that cannot be managed in an outpatient setting, necessitating immediate hospitalization.
  2. (ii) Outpatient clinic:
    • Clinic Referrals: Patients seen in outpatient clinics, during routine or specialist appointments, may be determined to require in-patient admission based on their assessment and diagnosis.
    • Planned Admissions: Some outpatient clinic visits may be for pre-admission assessments, leading to planned admissions for surgeries or treatments.
  3. (iii) Other hospitals:
    • Transfer Admissions: Patients may be transferred from other hospitals for specialized care, procedures, or services not available at the referring facility.
    • Higher Level Care: Transfers often occur when patients require a higher level of care or specialist expertise available at a larger or more specialized hospital.
  4. (iv) In-patient waiting list:
    • Scheduled Admissions: Patients who are placed on an in-patient waiting list for elective procedures or treatments are admitted when their turn comes and beds become available.
    • Elective Care: Admissions from waiting lists are typically for non-emergency, planned care that can be scheduled in advance.

Understanding these sources of admission helps healthcare facilities manage patient flow, allocate resources appropriately, and streamline admission processes based on patient entry points.

ðŸ“š Discharge Procedures

Discharge Procedures are the steps taken when a patient is ready to leave the hospital after receiving in-patient care. These procedures ensure that the discharge process is systematic, well-documented, and that patients receive all necessary information for their continued care. Key components of discharge procedures include:

  • Discharge Registers:
    • Maintenance of Registers: Admissions and discharge registers should be maintained in all health institutions. These registers are crucial for tracking patient flow and maintaining accurate records.
    • Register Content: The discharge register should provide comprehensive information, including:
      • Full name of the patient
      • Date of birth
      • Admission date
      • Discharge date
      • Diagnosis at discharge
      • Length of stay at the hospital

Maintaining accurate and complete discharge registers is essential for administrative purposes, statistical reporting, and ensuring continuity of patient care post-hospitalization.

ðŸ“š Maintenance of a Bed Bureau: Hospital Bed Bureau

Maintenance of a Bed Bureau is a critical function in hospital administration, focused on managing bed availability and allocation efficiently. A Hospital Bed Bureau is defined and maintained as follows:

Definition:

  • System for Bed Management: A hospital bed bureau is a system used to manage the availability, allocation, and utilization of beds by the hospital to admit patients from the waiting list. It is essentially a central coordination unit for bed management.
  • Key Functions: The bed bureau performs several key functions:
    • Availability Tracking: Shows the current availability of beds across different wards.
    • Bed Allocation: Manages the allocation of available beds to patients waiting for admission, particularly from waiting lists or emergency departments.
    • Utilization Management: Optimizes the use of hospital beds to ensure efficient patient flow and minimize bed shortages.
    • Bed Location Information: Indicates the location of the bed in the ward, providing precise information on bed occupancy.
    • Vacancy Prediction: Projects when a bed will fall vacant for the next admission, allowing for proactive planning of admissions.

Maintenance of a bed bureau:

  • Manual or Electronic Management: A hospital bed bureau could either be managed manually or electronically, depending on the resources and technological infrastructure of the facility.
  • Manual Bed Bureau: In a manual setup:
    • Officer in Charge: In the manual environment, the officer in charge of bed allocation monitors both admissions and discharges routinely.
    • Routine Monitoring: The officer continuously tracks admissions and discharges to keep bed status up-to-date.
    • Reporting Bed Availability: In order to report on available beds and inform the officer maintaining the waiting list about bed availability.
    • Vacancy Projection: Waiting list of such beds as and when they fall vacant or even project when beds are most likely to fall vacant. The officer projects bed vacancies based on discharge schedules and typical patient lengths of stay.
    • Notice Board System: This is done by keeping a notice board with the location of all the wards and their bed capacity. A physical notice board is used to display bed status information.
    • Color-Coded Cards: The notice board has two color strip cards which are put in pocket bags, red for occupied beds and green for vacant beds. Color-coded cards visually represent bed occupancy status, with red for occupied and green for vacant beds.
  • Electronic Bed Bureau: An electronic system offers more advanced and user-friendly features:
    • User-Friendly and Efficient: The electronic aspect is more user friendly because the patient waiting list is automatically integrated and interfaced. Electronic systems automate many manual tasks, improving efficiency and accuracy.
    • Real-Time Data Integration: Integrated and interfaced (the whole process is on line and data is available to all at real-time) with the admission and discharge modules. The system integrates in real-time with admission and discharge data, providing up-to-the-minute bed availability information.
    • Automatic Updates: Bed availability is automatically accessed automatically as and when a bed falls vacant, reducing manual updates and ensuring data accuracy.
    • Efficient Bed Placement: As and when a bed falls vacant, the officer in charge of bed placement can therefore admit patients on the waiting list as beds become available. The system enables efficient and immediate bed allocation to patients on the waiting list, optimizing bed utilization and reducing wait times for admission.

Whether manual or electronic, a well-maintained bed bureau is crucial for optimizing bed utilization, managing patient admissions effectively, and ensuring smooth hospital operations.

ðŸ“š Key Takeaways

  • Admission procedures include providing patients with information booklets, guiding them to wards, and ensuring feedback to the health records department.
  • Sources of admission include Accident and Emergency Department, Outpatient clinics, other hospitals, and in-patient waiting lists, each representing different patient entry pathways.
  • Discharge procedures require maintaining admission and discharge registers that record patient details, admission and discharge dates, diagnosis, and length of stay.
  • A hospital bed bureau is a system to manage bed availability, allocation, and utilization, maintained either manually with notice boards or electronically for real-time, integrated management.
