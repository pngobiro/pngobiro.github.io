ðŸ“š Filing Equipment: (1) Shelf Filing

Shelf Filing is a widely used method for storing health records, particularly suitable for large volumes of case notes and X-ray films. It involves storing files on open shelves rather than in drawers or cabinets. Key aspects of shelf filing include:

(1) Shelf filing: This is the most used filling equipment and probably more suitable for filing large quantities of notes or x-ray films than any else. Shelf filing is a popular and practical choice for health records departments due to its efficiency and capacity.

Material Preference:

  • Metal over Wood: Metal is more suitable than wood for shelf filing units in health records departments. Metal shelving offers greater durability, fire resistance, and load-bearing capacity compared to wood.

Advantages of Shelf Filing:

  1. (i) Advantages:
  2. (a) Easy and Quick Access:
    • Open Shelves for Quick Access: Records can easily be filed and pulled quickly since the shelves are open. Open shelf design allows for direct visibility and access to files, speeding up both filing and retrieval processes.
  3. (b) High Capacity:
    • More Records per Area: More records can be filed on the shelves than in the cabinets. Shelf filing generally offers a higher storage density compared to filing cabinets, maximizing the use of available space.

Disadvantages of Shelf Filing:

  1. (ii) Disadvantages:
  2. (a) Light Penetration Issues:
    • Lower Shelf Darkness: If the shelves are constructed high, light cannot penetrate the lowest shelves. High shelf units can create shadows, making it difficult to see labels and retrieve files from the lower shelves, particularly in poorly lit rooms.
  3. (b) Accessibility for Short Staff:
    • Ladder or Stool Requirement: If shelves are constructed high, the shortest clerk has to climb the ladders or the kit stools to reach top shelves. Accessing high shelves requires ladders or stools, posing physical strain and potential safety hazards for shorter staff members.
  4. (c) Not Dust Proof:
    • Dust Exposure: Shelves are not dust proof. Open shelves expose records to dust accumulation, requiring regular cleaning to maintain record integrity and readability.
  5. (d) Not Fire Proof:
    • Fire Vulnerability: Shelves are not fire proof. Open shelving provides minimal protection against fire, increasing the risk of record loss in case of fire incidents.
  6. (e) Not Water Proof:
    • Water Damage Risk: Shelves are not water proof. Open shelves offer no protection against water damage from leaks, spills, or floods, posing a risk to record preservation.

Shelf filing is a practical and high-capacity storage solution, but it's important to consider its disadvantages, particularly regarding accessibility for all staff, dust and environmental protection, and security, especially in settings where these factors are critical.

ðŸ“š Filing Equipment: (2) Filing Cabinets

Filing Cabinets are a traditional storage solution, particularly recommended for smaller health records libraries where enhanced protection and organization are prioritized. They offer several ideal filing conditions but also have disadvantages compared to shelf filing.

Filing cabinets: Filing cabinets are recommended for smaller libraries. They provide ideal filing conditions:-

Ideal Filing Conditions:

  1. a) Good access:
    • Easy Access to Files: Filing cabinets typically feature drawers that slide out, providing good access to files stored within. Drawers allow for organized and easy retrieval of individual files.
  2. b) Dust-proof:
    • Dust Protection: Dust-proof nature of closed drawers protects records from dust accumulation, maintaining cleanliness and readability over time.
  3. c) Convenient height:
    • Ergonomic Access: Convenient height of filing cabinets ensures that files are accessible without the need for ladders or excessive bending, improving ergonomics for staff.
  4. d) Provide attractive appearance:
    • Professional Look: Provide attractive appearance. Filing cabinets often have a more polished and professional look compared to open shelves, contributing to a neater office environment.

Disadvantages of Filing Cabinets:

  1. Disadvantages:
  2. 1. They take up ore space:
    • Larger Footprint: They take up ore space compared to shelf filing for the same volume of records. Filing cabinets generally require more floor space due to the drawer extension space and cabinet structure.
  3. 2. More expensive than shelves:
    • Higher Cost: More expensive than shelves. Filing cabinets are typically more costly to purchase and install than open shelving units, especially for large-scale storage needs.

Filing cabinets are best suited for smaller health records departments that prioritize record protection, aesthetic appeal, and ease of access at a closer range, despite the higher space and cost requirements compared to shelf filing.

ðŸ“š Filing Equipment: (3) Suspended Filing

Suspended Filing is a system where files are hung from rails rather than stacked or placed in drawers. While it offers certain organizational benefits, it is generally not suitable for a large number of case records and x-ray films due to cost and space inefficiency. Key characteristics include:

Suspended filing: This form of filing is never suitable for a large number of case records and x-ray films on the ground of cost and amount of space taken up. Suspended filing is generally impractical and uneconomical for large health records libraries.

Design and Components:

  • Manila Pockets: Basically it consists of manila pockets, specialized pockets made of manila card stock.
  • Metal Hanging Bars: hanging from two metal bars. These pockets are designed to hang from parallel metal bars or rails.
  • V-shaped Space: and providing a v-shaped space into which notes can be filed. The pockets create a V-shape, allowing files to be inserted and hang vertically.
  • Flat Tops for Labeling: The metal hanging bars have flat tops to indicate the unit numbers filed in each pocket. The flat tops of the metal bars provide a space for labeling, typically with unit numbers, to identify the contents of each suspended file.

Installation and Adaptability:

  • Installation in Cabinets: Suspended filing can be installed in filing cabinets, with addition of special framework on which to hang the pockets. Suspended filing systems can be retrofitted into existing filing cabinets by adding a framework to support the hanging rails.
  • Horizontal Units: or it can take place of the shelves in horizontal units. Alternatively, suspended filing can replace shelves in horizontal units, creating a fully suspended filing setup.

Disadvantages of Suspended Filing:

  1. Disadvantages:
  2. 1. Provide less filing space:
    • Lower Density Storage: Provide less filing space compared to shelf or cabinet filing for the same area. Suspended filing generally offers lower storage density as the hanging pockets take up more vertical space and do not maximize shelf depth as efficiently as stacked or drawer filing.
  3. 2. It is expensive to install:
    • Higher Installation Cost: It is expensive to install, especially for large systems. The cost of specialized manila pockets, metal hanging bars, and framework for installation can be considerable, making it a less economical option for large-scale health records storage.

Advantages of Suspended Filing:

  1. Advantages:
  2. Ideal for Slim Files: It is ideal for filing administrative records where files are slim. Suspended filing is better suited for lighter, less bulky files, such as administrative documents or correspondence, where the lower storage density and higher cost are less of a concern.

Suspended filing is niche solution best suited for administrative or light-volume filing needs, not for the primary storage of bulky case records and X-ray films in a large health records department.

ðŸ“š Filing Equipment: (4) Mobile Raking

Mobile Raking, also known as mobile shelving or compact shelving, is designed to maximize storage capacity in limited space. It is considered the most economical filing method as far as space is concerned for high-volume health records storage. Key features include:

Mobile raking: This is the most economical filing method as far as space is concerned. Mobile raking is optimized for high-density storage.

Design and Operation:

  • Shelves on Rails: A series of shelves, contained in a rigid frame with backing, are run on rails. Shelving units are mounted on rails or tracks, allowing them to move laterally along the floor.
  • Fixed and Mobile Sets: One set of shelving is fixed and then there maybe three mobile sets. Typically, one shelving unit is stationary (fixed), while the adjacent units are mobile, sliding along the rails to create access aisles.

Space Efficiency:

  • Gangway Reduction: It will be immediately obvious from this that if all five sets of racking were static, far more room would be taken up by the gangways necessary to gain access to both sides of the racks. Mobile raking drastically reduces the need for multiple fixed aisles (gangways) between shelving units. In traditional static shelving, aisles between each row consume significant floor space. Mobile raking eliminates most of these aisles, creating access only when and where needed.

Disadvantages of Mobile Raking:

  1. Disadvantages:
  2. 1. No gangways which could cause hold ups:
    • Potential Access Bottlenecks: No gangways which could cause hold ups. The very feature that saves spaceâ€”eliminating fixed aislesâ€”can become a bottleneck. Only one aisle can be open at a time, which can cause delays if multiple clerks need access to different sections simultaneously.
  3. 2. Access Limitation:
    • Limited Simultaneous Access: Access may not be possible by more than two clerks at a time. Only a limited number of clerks can access the system concurrently, as opening one aisle blocks access to others.
  4. 3. Physical Effort Required:
    • Effortful Movement: The pushing of the racks could be a formidable task. Moving heavily loaded shelving units, especially manual systems, can be physically demanding and cumbersome, particularly for frequent access.
  5. 4. It is expensive:
    • Cost Factor: It is expensive to install, although the space savings often justify the investment in high-density storage contexts. Mobile raking systems involve significant upfront costs for rails, mobile units, and installation.

Advantage of Mobile Raking:

  1. Advantage:
  2. It saves space:
    • Primary Benefit: It saves space. The most significant advantage of mobile raking is its exceptional space-saving capability, allowing for maximum record storage in a limited area.

Mobile raking is ideal for healthcare facilities that need to maximize record storage capacity in constrained spaces, despite the potential for access limitations and higher initial costs. It is particularly valuable in archive rooms or record centers where space optimization is paramount.

ðŸ“š Filing Equipment: (5) Circular, Carousel Filing and (6) Elevator Files

Circular, Carousel Filing and Elevator Files represent advanced, often automated, solutions for high-volume, efficient health records storage and retrieval. These systems are designed to maximize space utilization and access speed, though they come with higher costs and complexity.

Circular, carousel filing: These units consists of a series of shelves or suspended files rotating round a fixed spindle- a large version of the carousel described in the section dealing with the master index. Circular, or rotary, carousel filing extends the carousel concept to larger units suitable for full-size files, not just index cards.

Design and Operation:

  • Rotary Shelves or Files: Units feature rotating shelves or suspended filing sections around a central spindle.
  • Large Carousel Version: A scaled-up version of carousel index files, designed for larger health records.

Advantage - Space Efficiency and Access:

  • Space Saving and Access: Their great virtue is that the files can be placed against a wall and rotated to bring the files that are needed to the front- they are therefore space saving. Carousel filing is highly space-efficient as it utilizes vertical space and eliminates the need for extensive aisle space. Files are brought to the user by rotation, improving access speed and ergonomics.

Cost:

  • Higher Expense: This type of installation is undoubtedly more expensive than many others. Carousel filing systems are more costly than traditional shelving or cabinet systems due to their mechanical complexity and automated features.

Suitability:

  • Interest and Space Saving Principle: It should be looked at last for interest and for the space saving principle. Carousel filing is considered when space saving is a critical priority and budget allows for a higher investment in advanced filing technology.

Elevator files: These are very large relatives of the elevator files described in the master index section. They are extremely expensive and quite out of the financial range of most records departments. However, they should be recognised as a possible form of filing. Elevator files are the most sophisticated and high-tech option, representing a significant investment.

Design and Operation:

  • Automated Vertical Storage: Large, automated systems that use vertical space extensively, similar in concept to multi-story car parks but for files.
  • Large-Scale Elevator Files: Scaled-up versions of elevator index card files, designed for full-size health records.
  • Extensive Height: The difference is that elevator files for notes probably extend upwards for twenty feet or more. Elevator files can extend vertically up to twenty feet or more, maximizing vertical storage capacity.
  • Automated Retrieval: As the operator presses the correct button, the shelves will rotate until the correct one presents itself at filing level. Operation is automated; users press a button corresponding to the desired file, and the system retrieves and presents the file at an accessible level.

Advantages - Convenience and Space Saving:

  • Convenience and Space Saving: The argument for their installation apart from the convenience of never having to walk, bend or stretch to pull file records is that valuable ground floor space no longer needs to be allotted to the records library as most of the notes are stored on one or two floors above while still being available at the most convenient point. Elevator files offer unparalleled convenience by eliminating physical strain and movement for file retrieval. They also maximize space utilization by storing records vertically, freeing up valuable floor space for other uses.

Disadvantage - Cost:

  • High Cost: They are extremely expensive and quite out of the financial range of most records departments. Elevator files are the most expensive filing option, typically beyond the budget of many health records departments unless space and labor efficiency are extremely high priorities.

Suitability:

  • Possible Filing Form: However, they should be recognised as a possible form of filing for facilities with very large record volumes, space constraints, and budgets that can justify the investment in advanced technology.

Carousel and elevator filing systems are at the high end of filing technology, offering significant benefits in space utilization, access speed, and ergonomics, but require substantial financial investment and are typically considered by very large healthcare institutions with significant resource capacity and space challenges.

ðŸ“š Filing Equipment: Ancillary Equipment

Ancillary Equipment is essential for enhancing the efficiency and functionality of a well-organized health records library. These items support day-to-day operations and improve workflow. Key ancillary equipment includes:

Ancillary equipment: The efficiency of a well laid out, carefully filed records or X ray records library can be significantly increased if certain basic items are provided in addition to the most suitable type of shelving. Ancillary equipment complements the main filing systems to optimize library operations.

  1. (a) Filing Trolleys:
    • Purpose: To facilitate the movement of records within the filing area.
    • Clerk Assistance: If clerks are to walk round the filing area with armfuls of notes, putting them down each time they withdraw or file a record they will be very tired by the end of the day. Trolleys reduce physical strain on staff by minimizing the need to carry heavy loads of files manually over distances within the library.
    • Trolley Specifications: Trolleys should be strong and large enough to be able to carry heavy loads of case records and X ray films. They need to be robust and spacious enough to handle substantial volumes of files and large items like X-ray film jackets.
  2. (b) Kik stools/ladders:
    • Purpose: To enable access to high shelves.
    • Accessibility for All Staff: These are necessary for the shortest clerk to climb on to reach of shelf as high as 7 feet. Kik stools or ladders ensure that all staff members, regardless of height, can safely access records stored on high shelves.
    • Lightweight and Mobile: Any ladder or Kik-stool should be light and easily moved. Mobility and lightweight design are important for ease of use and safety, allowing staff to reposition them quickly and safely as needed.
  3. (c) Sorting equipment:
    • Purpose: To aid in sorting records before filing.
    • Pigeon Holes: Sorting equipment for case records and X ray films will certainly be some form of pigeon hole. Pigeon holes or similar sorting stations are essential for organizing records into manageable batches before final filing.
    • Vital Task Support: It is usually very important that this vital task is not attempted with inadequate equipment. Proper sorting equipment is crucial for efficient and accurate filing; inadequate sorting tools can lead to errors and delays.
    • Terminal Digit Sorting: Where terminal digit is used, it is desirable to have one hundred pigeon holes, one for each terminal digit. These should be clearly numbered. For terminal digit filing systems, having 100 pigeon holes, each labeled 00-99, facilitates the initial sorting of files by terminal digits.
    • Workflow Integration: As the files come back to the library, form, clinics, secretaries, wards, they can be filed straight into the appropriate pigeon hole of the sorting unit. The filing clerk responsible for each section of the library will ten take on pigeon hole numbers, sort them out and file them. Incoming files from various sources can be directly sorted into these pigeon holes for efficient processing and filing by designated clerks.
  4. (d) Preparation tables:
    • Purpose: To provide workspace for sorting and preparing records.
    • Sorting and Clinic Prep: These tables can be used for sorting the notes for filing for preparing the clinics. Preparation tables offer a dedicated space for tasks like sorting files, preparing clinic lists, and organizing documents.
    • Adequate Space: There should be enough space for each clerk and the table should be large enough. Tables need to be spacious enough to accommodate multiple clerks working simultaneously and provide ample surface area for spreading out and organizing files.

Providing appropriate ancillary equipment significantly enhances the functionality and efficiency of a health records library, improving workflow, staff ergonomics, and overall record management quality.

ðŸ“š Key Takeaways

  • Shelf filing offers high capacity and easy access but lacks dust and fire protection, and can be challenging for shorter staff to reach high shelves.
  • Filing cabinets provide dust-proof, convenient, and aesthetically pleasing storage, ideal for smaller libraries but are more space-consuming and expensive than shelves.
  • Suspended filing is not suitable for large case records due to less filing space and high installation costs, best suited for slim administrative files.
  • Mobile raking maximizes space by using movable shelves on rails, economical for space but can cause access bottlenecks and requires physical effort to move racks.
  • Circular carousel filing and elevator files are advanced, space-saving, and efficient for high-volume retrieval, though they are costly and complex.
  • Ancillary equipment like filing trolleys, kik stools/ladders, sorting equipment (pigeon holes), and preparation tables are crucial for enhancing library efficiency and workflow.
