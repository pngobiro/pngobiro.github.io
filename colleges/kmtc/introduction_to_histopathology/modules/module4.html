Module 4: Specimen Collection and Transport

🎯 Learning Objectives

  • Identify the factors that affect cellular and molecular integrity of tissue specimens.
  • Describe the general considerations for tissue collection to maintain specimen integrity.
  • Explain the principles and procedures for transporting human tissue.
  • Outline the safety measures for transporting unfixed tissues and reagents.

Tissue (Specimen) Collection

General Considerations

The scientific utility of the data obtained from analysis of tissues is directly related to the quality of the tissue specimen.

Cellular and molecular integrity are most affected by:

  • Specimen and tissue type
  • Condition of tissue hypoxia
  • Method of preservation
  • Condition of storage
  • Pre-excision hypoxia
  • Tissue product extraction methods.

To obtain and maintain tissue with suitable integrity, the following factors must be considered:

  • Minimize the time of exposure of tissue to hypoxic conditions (which initiates cell death mechanisms & degradation processes).
  • Use agents or treatments to inactivate degrading enzymes for preserving nucleic acid integrity.
  • Preservation of frozen tissue as fresh frozen if the intended use is for nucleic acid analysis.
  • Storage of frozen tissue and products at appropriate temperatures.
  • Avoid contamination with surrounding.

Transporting of Human Tissue

General Principles

  • No human tissue may be transported from one establishment to another unless both establishments have full ethical approval from a recognized ethical approval service or are subject to an appropriate Human Tissue Authority (HTA) licence.
  • Any recipient of transported tissue must be the subject of and compliant with an appropriate ethical approval or HTA licence.
  • Human tissue samples must be tracked and recorded, from collection to disposal.
  • Appropriate modes of transport, suitable routes and arrangements with people involved must be planned and arranged in advance, and considered when preparing risk assessments.

Transporting Tissue from Operation Room (OR) to the Lab

  • Health and safety issues in transporting human tissues: all processes in handling and transporting human tissues must be subjected to risk assessment and made available to everyone involved.
  • All unfixed tissues and their primary containment should be treated as potentially hazardous material and appropriate protection and decontamination procedures must be employed.
  • Safe handling of reagents used in preservation and fixing tissues.
  • Slides and blocks should be contained in secure containers able to withstand falls and knocks that may be expected during transport.
  • Use rapid specimen transport protocol.
  • Transport on ice or formalin for embedded tissue.
  • Frozen sections should be transported in liquid nitrogen, dry ice, or in case of mRNA samples, use RNA Later reagent.
  • No more than 30 min should elapse between the time of biopsy and time of freezing of a sample/ fixation should be done immediately.
  • Prepare tissue collection kits in advance.

The technical responsibility should be:

  • Specimen preservation
  • Specimen labeling, logging and identification
  • Preparation of specimen to facilitate their gross and microscopic examination.
  • Record keeping.

💡 Pro Tip

Proper specimen collection and transport are critical for obtaining reliable and accurate results in histopathological analysis.

✍️ Practice Exercise

1. List four factors that can affect the cellular and molecular integrity of tissue specimens.

2. Describe the safety precautions for transporting unfixed tissues.

View Solution

1. Specimen type, tissue hypoxia, preservation method, storage condition, pre-excision hypoxia, extraction methods.

2. Treat as potentially hazardous, use appropriate protection and decontamination procedures, use secure containers, and follow rapid transport protocols.

📚 Key Takeaways

  • The quality of the tissue specimen directly impacts the reliability of the analysis.
  • Maintaining cellular and molecular integrity requires careful consideration of various factors during collection and storage.
  • Transporting human tissue requires ethical approval, proper tracking, and adherence to safety protocols.

  4. Avoiding contamination

Question 2: What is the recommended transport medium for frozen sections?

  1. Formalin
