Module 2: Tissue Preparation Methods

🎯 Learning Objectives

  • Describe the fresh method of tissue preparation, including teased preparation, squash preparation, and smears.
  • Explain the fixed method of tissue preparation, including fixation, dehydration, clearing, infiltration, embedding, and microtomy.
  • Understand the advantages and disadvantages of each method.

Fresh Method of Tissue Preparation

📚 The fresh method allows for the examination of living cells. Several techniques can be applied:

1. Teased Preparation

This preparation is made from fresh tissues using dissecting needles. The dissection is carried out on the specimen that has been immersed in physiological saline or Ringer's solution, which has osmotic pressure equal to the cell cytoplasm. The selected piece of tissue is transferred on a slide and mounted as a wet preparation under a cover slip. This technique allows examination of living cells.

2. Squash Preparation

This is made by taking a small piece of tissue and pressing it on a glass slide; a cover slip is added and gently pressed until the cells spread out. Staining can be done using the irrigation method.

3. Smears

Smears can be prepared in 3 ways:

Bacteriological Smears

  • This is prepared by using a sterile wire loop to scope cells from a freshly chosen cut surface of tissue.
  • The cells are spread out on the glass slide while still fresh, and a cover slip is placed on top.

Apposition Smear

  • This is prepared by crushing a selected piece of small soft tissue between 2 glass slides which are then pressed together and drawn apart in the opposite direction in a horizontal plane.
  • Both slides can then be stained and observed under the microscope.

Touch or Impression Smear (Imprints)

These are made by bringing into contact a freshly cut selected surface of a tissue with a clean glass slide.

  • The cells are transferred from the surface of the tissue and can be observed under the microscope.

4. Maceration

It is the process by which tissues, particularly muscles and nerves, are separated into individual fibers using macerating solutions. Some of the macerating solutions act as fixatives.

The most commonly used macerating solutions are:

  • 20% Nitric acid
  • Formalin 1 part in NaCl solution
  • 30% alcohol
  • 1% NaCl

Fixed Method of Tissue Preparation

📚 The fixed method employs fixed tissue and involves several stages of tissue treatment:

  • Fixation: Preserving the tissue.
  • Dehydration: Removing water from the tissue.
  • Clearing: Replacing the dehydrating agent with a substance miscible with the embedding medium.
  • Infiltration: Permeating the tissue with the embedding medium.
  • Embedding: Surrounding the tissue with the embedding medium to provide support.
  • Microtomy: Cutting thin sections of the embedded tissue.

The method can be named according to the medium used in the embedding stage. The most common is the paraffin wax method, where paraffin wax is used as the infiltration and embedding media. It is the commonest method in routine work.

Advantages of Paraffin Wax Method

  • It allows thin sections to be cut.
  • The sections are easy to cut.
  • It allows many staining techniques to be performed.
  • The storage of the wax blocks and stained tissue sections is not a problem.
  • Serial sections are possible.
  • It is relatively faster than other methods, e.g., Celloidin method.

Disadvantages of Fresh Methods of Tissue Preparation

  • They are uneven in thickness.
  • They are temporary and can't be stored for long.

Advantages of Fresh Methods

  • They offer results in a short time and therefore are useful for urgent biopsies.
  • They do not require any chemical solutions and are therefore cheap.
  • They allow tissue/cells to be observed in their living state.

💡 Pro Tip

Choosing the appropriate tissue preparation method depends on the specific requirements of the examination and the type of tissue being studied.

✍️ Practice Exercise

1. Compare and contrast the teased preparation and squash preparation methods.

2. List the stages involved in the fixed method of tissue preparation.

View Solution

1. Teased preparation involves dissecting tissue in saline, while squash preparation involves pressing tissue on a slide. Both allow for examination of living cells.

2. The stages are fixation, dehydration, clearing, infiltration, embedding, and microtomy.

📚 Key Takeaways

  • The fresh method of tissue preparation allows for the examination of living cells using techniques like teased preparation, squash preparation, and smears.
  • The fixed method of tissue preparation involves several stages, including fixation, dehydration, clearing, infiltration, embedding, and microtomy.
  • The paraffin wax method is the most common fixed method used in routine work.
