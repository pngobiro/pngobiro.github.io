Part I: General Medical-Surgical Nursing

Q26 - Q50
26.
A patient with a history of chronic obstructive pulmonary disease (COPD) is experiencing respiratory distress. Which nursing intervention is the priority for managing the patient's respiratory status?
A) Administering bronchodilators
B) Positioning the patient in high Fowler's position
C) Performing chest physiotherapy
D) Administering oxygen therapy
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B) Positioning the patient in high Fowler's position

Explanation: In respiratory distress, the immediate priority is to optimize lung expansion. Positioning in High Fowler's allows for maximum chest expansion and reduces the work of breathing. While oxygen (D) and bronchodilators (A) are critical, positioning is the immediate "first" action a nurse can take.

27.
A patient post-thyroidectomy develops signs of hypocalcemia, including tingling around the mouth and muscle cramps. Which action should the nurse take first?
A) Administer oral calcium supplements
B) Notify the healthcare provider
C) Assess the patient's calcium level
D) Encourage increased intake of dairy products
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B) Notify the healthcare provider

Explanation: Symptomatic hypocalcemia (tetany, tingling) after thyroidectomy is a medical emergency that may require IV calcium gluconate. While assessment is important, the presence of symptoms warrants immediate notification for treatment orders. Oral calcium (A) is too slow for acute symptoms.

28.
A patient with a history of heart failure presents with dependent edema in the lower extremities. Which nursing action is most appropriate for managing the patient's edema?
A) Applying compression stockings
B) Elevating the legs above heart level
C) Administering diuretics as prescribed
D) Encouraging increased fluid intake
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B) Elevating the legs above heart level (and C is also correct pharmacologically, but B is a direct nursing action for edema management).

Explanation: Elevation promotes venous return and reduces hydrostatic pressure in the legs. Diuretics (C) are the medical treatment, but elevation is a standard nursing intervention. Note: Compression stockings (A) should be used with caution in severe HF if not specifically ordered, but elevation is safe.

29.
A patient with a history of diabetes mellitus is admitted with a foot ulcer. Which nursing intervention is essential for preventing infection in the foot ulcer?
A) Applying topical antibiotics
B) Performing daily wound debridement
C) Keeping the ulcer covered with a sterile dressing
D) Administering oral antibiotics prophylactically
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C) Keeping the ulcer covered with a sterile dressing

Explanation: Maintaining a sterile environment prevents the entry of pathogens. Prophylactic antibiotics (D) are generally not recommended without signs of infection. Debridement (B) is a provider/specialist task.

30.
A patient with a history of cirrhosis is at risk for developing hepatic encephalopathy. Which nursing intervention is most effective in preventing and managing hepatic encephalopathy?
A) Administering lactulose as prescribed
B) Restricting dietary protein intake
C) Monitoring serum ammonia levels
D) Encouraging increased fluid intake
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A) Administering lactulose as prescribed

Explanation: Lactulose traps ammonia in the gut and promotes its excretion, which is the primary treatment for reducing ammonia levels in hepatic encephalopathy.

31.
A patient with a history of stroke is at risk for impaired skin integrity due to immobility. Which nursing intervention is most effective in preventing pressure ulcers in this patient?
A) Turning and repositioning the patient every 2 hours
B) Massaging bony prominences regularly
C) Applying heating pads to areas of erythema
D) Using a pressure-reducing mattress
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A) Turning and repositioning the patient every 2 hours

Explanation: Regular repositioning relieves pressure and allows perfusion to tissues. Massaging bony prominences (B) is contraindicated as it can damage capillary beds.

32.
A patient with a history of chronic kidney disease is prescribed a low-phosphorus diet. Which dietary choice indicates understanding of the prescribed diet?
A) Grilled chicken breast
B) Cheese omelet
C) Whole wheat toast with peanut butter
D) Spinach salad with almonds
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A) Grilled chicken breast

Explanation: Dairy (B), nuts/seeds (C, D), and whole grains are high in phosphorus. White breads and lean meats (like chicken) are lower in phosphorus relative to the other options. While meat has phosphorus, it is less than dairy/nuts/legumes in concentration.

33.
A patient with a history of peptic ulcer disease is prescribed proton pump inhibitors (PPIs) for ulcer healing. Which information is important for the nurse to include in patient education about PPI therapy?
A) PPIs should be taken on an empty stomach.
B) PPIs should be discontinued once symptoms resolve.
C) PPIs may increase the risk of gastrointestinal bleeding.
D) PPIs should be taken with antacids for optimal effect.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A) PPIs should be taken on an empty stomach.

Explanation: PPIs are best taken 30-60 minutes before the first meal of the day to effectively inhibit proton pumps when they become active.

34.
A patient with a history of chronic obstructive pulmonary disease (COPD) is prescribed a metered-dose inhaler (MDI) with a spacer. Which instruction should the nurse include in the demonstration of MDI use?
A) Inhale slowly and deeply while pressing the canister.
B) Exhale completely before inhaling the medication.
C) Shake the inhaler vigorously before each puff.
D) Hold the breath for 10 seconds after inhaling the medication.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: D) Hold the breath for 10 seconds after inhaling the medication. (Note: All options are steps, but D is the critical post-inhalation step. However, B is also a critical preparatory step. The question asks what to include. Usually, holding breath is emphasized to ensure deposition).

Correction: All steps are correct. However, "Hold breath" is often the specific instruction checked for efficacy in retention.

35.
A patient with a history of type 2 diabetes mellitus is prescribed insulin glargine (Lantus) once daily. Which statement indicates understanding of insulin glargine administration?
A) "I will inject insulin glargine 30 minutes before meals."
B) "I will shake the insulin vial vigorously before drawing the dose."
C) "I will rotate injection sites within the same body region."
D) "I will administer insulin glargine with a rapid-acting insulin."
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C) "I will rotate injection sites within the same body region."

Explanation: Glargine is long-acting, has no peak, and does not need to be timed with meals (A). It should never be mixed (D). Shaking insulin is contraindicated (roll gently) (B). Rotation prevents lipodystrophy.

36.
A patient with a history of heart failure is prescribed digoxin. Which assessment finding indicates a potential adverse effect of digoxin therapy?
A) Bradycardia
B) Hypotension
C) Hyperkalemia
D) Confusion
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A) Bradycardia

Explanation: Digoxin slows AV node conduction. Bradycardia (< 60 bpm) is a primary sign of toxicity. Confusion (D) is also a sign (neurotoxicity), but Bradycardia is the classic cardiac sign monitored before every dose.

37.
A patient with a history of coronary artery disease is prescribed aspirin for antiplatelet therapy. Which information is important for the nurse to include in patient education about aspirin therapy?
A) "Take aspirin with a full glass of milk to minimize gastrointestinal upset."
B) "Avoid using nonsteroidal anti-inflammatory drugs (NSAIDs) while taking aspirin."
C) "Discontinue aspirin therapy if you develop a fever."
D) "Take aspirin on an empty stomach for better absorption."
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B) "Avoid using nonsteroidal anti-inflammatory drugs (NSAIDs) while taking aspirin."

Explanation: NSAIDs can interfere with the antiplatelet effect of aspirin and increase the risk of bleeding/GI ulcers.

38.
A patient with a history of stroke is prescribed clopidogrel (Plavix) for secondary prevention of thrombotic events. Which laboratory test should the nurse monitor closely during clopidogrel therapy?
A) Prothrombin time (PT)
B) Activated partial thromboplastin time (aPTT)
C) Platelet count
D) International normalized ratio (INR)
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C) Platelet count

Explanation: Clopidogrel is an antiplatelet agent. While it doesn't typically require INR/PT monitoring like Warfarin, monitoring for thrombocytopenia (low platelets) is standard safety practice, though less common than with some other agents.

39.
A patient with a history of chronic kidney disease is prescribed erythropoietin-stimulating agents (ESAs) for anemia management. Which parameter should the nurse monitor closely during ESA therapy?
A) Serum potassium levels
B) Hemoglobin levels
C) Serum creatinine levels
D) White blood cell count
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B) Hemoglobin levels

Explanation: Monitoring Hb is critical to prevent it from rising too high (typically >11 g/dL), which increases cardiovascular risk.

40.
A patient with a history of chronic obstructive pulmonary disease (COPD) is prescribed prednisone for an acute exacerbation. Which instruction is important for the nurse to include in patient education about prednisone therapy?
A) "Take prednisone with antacids to minimize gastrointestinal upset."
B) "Avoid abruptly stopping prednisone therapy."
C) "Increase your intake of potassium-rich foods while taking prednisone."
D) "Prednisone may cause weight loss, so monitor your weight regularly."
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B) "Avoid abruptly stopping prednisone therapy."

Explanation: Abrupt cessation of systemic corticosteroids can lead to adrenal crisis due to suppression of the HPA axis.

41.
A patient with a history of chronic kidney disease is prescribed a low-protein diet. Which dietary choice indicates understanding of the prescribed diet?
A) Grilled salmon
B) Lentil soup
C) Beef stew
D) Chicken Caesar salad
Show Answer

Correct Answer: D) Chicken Caesar salad (likely implies small portion of chicken) or potentially a vegetarian option if listed. However, among options: Salmon, Lentils, Beef are high protein. Caesar salad typically has less protein than a steak or fillet, but "Low protein" usually means restricting all these. This question is tricky without knowing the *portion* size implied. However, salads are often chosen as lighter options. Lentils are plant protein but significant. Beef and Salmon are high biological value protein.

Correction: Often in CKD exams, they look for *high biological value* protein in *small* amounts, or simply the item with the *least* protein. A salad (mostly veg) with some chicken is likely the lowest total protein load compared to a slab of salmon or beef stew. (Note: Lentils are high in potassium and phosphorus too).

42.
A patient with a history of deep vein thrombosis (DVT) is prescribed warfarin therapy. Which instruction is essential for the nurse to include in patient education about warfarin therapy?
A) "Limit your intake of green leafy vegetables."
B) "Avoid activities that may increase your risk of bleeding."
C) "Take a double dose if you miss a dose to catch up."
D) "Report any unusual bleeding or bruising to your healthcare provider."
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A) "Limit your intake of green leafy vegetables." (Actually, modern advice is "Consistent intake", but traditionally "Limit/Avoid fluctuation" is taught). D is also essential. However, diet interaction is specific to Warfarin. Between A and D, D is a safety *reporting* instruction, A is a *preventative* instruction regarding efficacy. Often A is the "knowledge" answer tested for Warfarin specifically.

Refined Answer: A is the classic Warfarin-specific diet point (Vitamin K interaction).

43.
A patient with a history of chronic obstructive pulmonary disease (COPD) is prescribed tiotropium (Spiriva) inhaler. Which instruction should the nurse include in patient education about tiotropium inhaler use?
A) "Use the inhaler only when you experience symptoms."
B) "Rinse your mouth with water after each use."
C) "Exhale forcefully before inhaling the medication."
D) "Store the inhaler in the refrigerator between uses."
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C) "Exhale forcefully before inhaling the medication." (Standard MDI/DPI technique). Spiriva is a long-acting anticholinergic taken *daily*, not PRN (A is wrong). Rinsing (B) is for steroids. Refrigerator (D) is not required for Spiriva capsules.

44.
A patient with a history of myocardial infarction is prescribed atorvastatin for lipid-lowering therapy. Which parameter should the nurse monitor closely during atorvastatin therapy?
A) Serum potassium levels
B) Liver function tests
C) Blood glucose levels
D) Serum sodium levels
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B) Liver function tests

Explanation: Statins are hepatotoxic and can elevate liver enzymes. Baseline and periodic LFTs are recommended.

45.
A patient with a history of heart failure is prescribed lisinopril for blood pressure management. Which adverse effect is most commonly associated with lisinopril therapy?
A) Hyperkalemia
B) Hypertension
C) Dry cough
D) Bradycardia
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C) Dry cough

Explanation: The "ACE cough" is the most common annoying side effect leading to discontinuation. (Hyperkalemia is also a risk, but cough is "most common").

46.
A patient with a history of peptic ulcer disease is prescribed famotidine (Pepcid) for gastric acid suppression. Which instruction should the nurse include in patient education about famotidine therapy?
A) "Take famotidine on an empty stomach."
B) "Avoid taking antacids while on famotidine therapy."
C) "Increase your intake of spicy foods while taking famotidine."
D) "Notify your healthcare provider if you develop black, tarry stools."
Show Answer

Correct Answer: D) "Notify your healthcare provider if you develop black, tarry stools."

Explanation: Black tarry stools (melena) indicate GI bleeding, a complication of PUD that requires immediate attention.

47.
A patient with a history of stroke is prescribed aspirin and clopidogrel for dual antiplatelet therapy. Which nursing intervention is essential for preventing bleeding complications in this patient?
A) Administering proton pump inhibitors (PPIs) concomitantly
B) Encouraging increased fluid intake
C) Monitoring for signs of thrombocytopenia
D) Providing education on fall prevention measures
Show Answer

Correct Answer: D) Providing education on fall prevention measures

Explanation: Falls while on dual antiplatelet therapy can lead to severe intracranial hemorrhage. Preventing injury is a key nursing intervention.

48.
A patient with a history of chronic kidney disease is prescribed phosphate binders. Which instruction should the nurse include in patient education about phosphate binder therapy?
A) "Take phosphate binders with meals."
B) "Avoid taking phosphate binders with calcium supplements."
C) "Increase your intake of foods high in phosphorus."
D) "Take phosphate binders on an empty stomach."
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A) "Take phosphate binders with meals."

Explanation: They bind phosphate in food, so they must be taken *with* the food.

49.
A patient with a history of coronary artery disease is scheduled for coronary artery bypass graft (CABG) surgery. Which preoperative nursing intervention is essential for preparing the patient for surgery?
A) Administering aspirin to prevent thrombosis
B) Providing education about postoperative pain management
C) Assisting the patient with deep breathing and coughing exercises
D) Obtaining informed consent for the surgical procedure
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C) Assisting the patient with deep breathing and coughing exercises (Pre-operative teaching).

Note: While B is also correct, teaching respiratory exercises (C) is critical for preventing post-op pneumonia. Informed consent (D) is the surgeon's responsibility (nurse witnesses). Aspirin (A) is often *held* before surgery to prevent bleeding.

50.
A patient with a history of heart failure is prescribed a beta-blocker. Which assessment finding indicates a therapeutic effect of beta-blocker therapy?
A) Decreased heart rate
B) Increased blood pressure
C) Elevated respiratory rate
D) Worsening dyspnea
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A) Decreased heart rate

Explanation: The goal of beta-blockers in HF is to reduce heart rate and myocardial workload. (Note: It should not cause *severe* bradycardia, but a decreased rate is the intended effect).

Part II: Theatre Nursing

Q76 - Q100
76.
During a surgical procedure, the nurse notices a sudden decrease in the patient's oxygen saturation on the monitor. What immediate action should the nurse take?
A) Inform the surgeon and anesthesiologist
B) Increase the flow rate of oxygen
C) Check the endotracheal tube placement
D) Assess the patient's respiratory status
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A) Inform the surgeon and anesthesiologist

Explanation: In the OR, the anesthesiologist manages the airway. The nurse must communicate the finding immediately so the expert can assess/intervene (e.g., check tube). While assessment (D) is usually first, in the intraoperative setting with a ventilated patient, alerting the anesthesia provider is the critical communication step.

77.
Before transferring the patient to the operating room (OR), the nurse notices a discrepancy between the surgical consent form and the planned procedure. What should the nurse do?
A) Proceed with the planned procedure
B) Inform the patient about the discrepancy
C) Consult the surgeon for clarification
D) Document the discrepancy in the patient's chart
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C) Consult the surgeon for clarification

Explanation: The surgeon is responsible for obtaining consent and performing the procedure. Any discrepancy acts as a "hard stop" and must be resolved by the surgeon before the patient enters the OR.

78.
During a surgical procedure, the nurse observes excessive bleeding from the surgical site. What intervention should the nurse prioritize?
A) Apply pressure to the bleeding site
B) Administer intravenous fluids rapidly
C) Notify the surgeon immediately
D) Request blood products from the blood bank
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C) Notify the surgeon immediately (assuming the nurse is the circulator/scrub noting it, though the surgeon is likely there). If the surgeon is operating, they know. This question likely refers to *post-op* or a specific scenario. If *during* surgery, the surgeon controls it. If the nurse is observing (e.g., suction canister filling rapidly), notifying the surgeon of the volume is key. However, if this refers to a *complication* the nurse sees, notifying is step 1. If it refers to *action*, providing supplies/suction is key. Given options: C is best for communication.

79.
After positioning the patient for surgery, the nurse notices signs of pressure injury on the patient's heels. What should the nurse do?
A) Apply pressure-relieving devices to the heels
B) Document the findings in the patient's chart
C) Reposition the patient to relieve pressure
D) Notify the surgeon about the pressure injuries
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A) Apply pressure-relieving devices to the heels

Explanation: Prevention of worsening injury is the priority. Padding the heels is the standard of care in the OR.

80.
While preparing the surgical instruments for sterilization, the nurse notices visible residue on some of the instruments. What action should the nurse take?
A) Re-sterilize the instruments
B) Use the instruments for the procedure as they are
C) Document the findings in the instrument log
D) Notify the sterile processing department
Show Answer

Correct Answer: D) Notify the sterile processing department (and do not use them). They must be cleaned *before* sterilization. Sterilization does not clean; residue prevents sterilization. They must be returned for cleaning.

81.
During surgery, the nurse notices an abnormal heart rhythm on the patient's cardiac monitor. What is the nurse's priority action?
A) Administer antiarrhythmic medication
B) Assess the patient's vital signs and symptoms
C) Document the rhythm in the patient's chart
D) Inform the anesthesiologist immediately
Show Answer

Correct Answer: D) Inform the anesthesiologist immediately

Explanation: The anesthesia provider manages hemodynamics in the OR.

82.
After the surgical procedure, the nurse assists with transferring the patient to the post-anesthesia care unit (PACU). What information should the nurse provide to the PACU nurse?
A) Details of the surgical procedure
B) Intraoperative complications and interventions
C) Patient's preoperative vital signs and laboratory results
D) Plan for postoperative pain management
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B) Intraoperative complications and interventions (All are important, but B is critical for immediate PACU monitoring. Typically, a full SBAR includes A, B, and D).

83.
While preparing the operating room (OR) for a surgical procedure, the nurse notices a malfunctioning anesthesia machine. What is the nurse's priority action?
A) Use an alternative anesthesia machine
B) Document the malfunction in the equipment log
C) Inform the anesthesia provider immediately
D) Continue with the surgical procedure as scheduled
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C) Inform the anesthesia provider immediately (and replace it). The surgery cannot proceed with a broken machine.

84.
During surgery, the nurse notices a discrepancy between the surgical count and the number of instruments on the sterile field. What should the nurse do?
A) Re-count the instruments on the sterile field
B) Document the discrepancy in the surgical count log
C) Continue with the procedure as planned
D) Inform the surgical team and initiate a thorough search
Show Answer

Correct Answer: D) Inform the surgical team and initiate a thorough search

Explanation: A count discrepancy is a medical emergency (retained object risk). The team must stop and search immediately before closing.

85.
After administering anesthesia to the patient, the nurse notices a sudden drop in blood pressure. What is the nurse's priority action?
A) Administer vasopressors to increase blood pressure
B) Assess the patient's airway, breathing, and circulation
C) Document the blood pressure readings in the anesthesia record
D) Notify the anesthesiologist immediately
Show Answer

Correct Answer: D) Notify the anesthesiologist immediately (if not already aware/managing it). The anesthesia provider manages BP.

86.
During surgery, the nurse observes a sudden change in the patient's level of consciousness. What is the nurse's immediate action?
A) Administer a reversal agent for anesthesia
B) Document the change in the patient's chart
C) Check the patient's vital signs
D) Notify the anesthesia provider immediately
Show Answer

Correct Answer: D) Notify the anesthesia provider immediately

Explanation: Intraoperative awareness or changes in sedation depth are anesthesia responsibilities.

87.
While positioning the patient for surgery, the nurse notices that the patient's skin is not adequately protected from pressure injuries. What should the nurse do?
A) Apply a pressure-relieving device to the bony prominences
B) Document the observation in the preoperative checklist
C) Reposition the patient to alleviate pressure on vulnerable areas
D) Continue with the positioning as planned
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A) Apply a pressure-relieving device to the bony prominences

Explanation: Padding is essential before the procedure starts.

88.
While preparing the surgical site, the nurse notices that the skin preparation solution has expired. What should the nurse do?
A) Use the expired solution as it is still effective
B) Document the expiration date in the patient's chart
C) Discard the expired solution and obtain a new one
D) Dilute the solution with sterile water to extend its shelf life
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C) Discard the expired solution and obtain a new one

Explanation: Infection control standards require current, effective supplies.

89.
During the surgical procedure, the nurse notices a break in sterile technique by a member of the surgical team. What should the nurse do?
A) Document the break in sterile technique in the surgical log
B) Continue with the procedure and address the issue later
C) Inform the surgeon immediately and request corrective action
D) Report the incident to the hospital's quality assurance department
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C) Inform the surgeon immediately and request corrective action

Explanation: The nurse must act as the patient's advocate (Surgical Conscience) and stop the contamination immediately to prevent infection.

90.
After the surgical procedure, the nurse assists with transferring the patient to the post-anesthesia care unit (PACU). What information should the nurse provide to the PACU nurse?
A) Details of the surgical procedure and anesthesia administration
B) Patient's allergies and medication history
C) Patient's vital signs and intraoperative complications
D) Plan for postoperative pain management and discharge instructions
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A, B, C (All are correct, but usually C is the most dynamic change to report). The question is likely "All of the above" or "A" as a comprehensive summary. Let's select A/C combination as best handover practice (SBAR includes Situation/Background/Assessment).

Correction: The best answer encompasses the immediate critical info. Options A and C are vital.

91.
While preparing the surgical instruments for sterilization, the nurse notices that the autoclave is not reaching the required temperature. What should the nurse do?
A) Document the issue in the equipment log and continue with sterilization
B) Adjust the settings on the autoclave to increase the temperature
C) Notify the sterile processing department and request maintenance
D) Use an alternative method of sterilization for the instruments
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C) Notify the sterile processing department and request maintenance

Explanation: Do not use faulty equipment. Sterility cannot be guaranteed.

92.
During surgery, the nurse observes an unusual odor emanating from the surgical site. What should the nurse do?
A) Document the observation in the patient's chart
B) Ignore the odor and continue with the procedure
C) Inform the surgeon immediately and assess for signs of infection
D) Request additional suctioning to remove the odor
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C) Inform the surgeon immediately and assess for signs of infection

Explanation: Odor may indicate necrotic bowel, abscess, or infection.

93.
After the surgical procedure, the nurse assists with applying the surgical dressing. What is the nurse's priority action?
A) Documenting the dressing change in the patient's chart
B) Assessing the surgical incision for signs of complications
C) Providing instructions to the patient about wound care
D) Ensuring that the dressing is applied securely and correctly
Show Answer

Correct Answer: D) Ensuring that the dressing is applied securely and correctly (to maintain sterility). B is done *before* dressing.

94.
During the surgical procedure, the nurse notices a discrepancy between the patient's identification bracelet and the surgical consent form. What should the nurse do?
A) Proceed with the procedure as planned
B) Document the discrepancy in the patient's chart
C) Inform the surgeon and anesthesia provider immediately
D) Request clarification from the patient and family members

Correct Answer: C) Inform the surgeon and anesthesia provider immediately

Explanation: This is a "Time Out" stop. Identity must be verified before incision.

95.
While preparing the operating room (OR) for a surgical procedure, the nurse notices a spill of bodily fluids on the floor. What is the nurse's priority action?
A) Clean up the spill using appropriate infection control measures

Correct Answer: A) Clean up the spill using appropriate infection control measures

Explanation: Immediate cleanup prevents falls and infection transmission.

96.

A) Administer antihypertensive medication
B) Document the blood pressure readings in the anesthesia record
C) Notify the anesthesia provider for further assessment
D) Continue monitoring the patient's blood pressure closely
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C) Notify the anesthesia provider for further assessment

Explanation: Could indicate pain, light anesthesia, or other issues.

97.
While positioning the patient for surgery, the nurse notices that the patient's arms are not adequately padded. What should the nurse do?
A) Proceed with the positioning as planned

C) Apply soft padding to the patient's arms to prevent pressure injuries
D) Inform the surgeon about the inadequate padding

Correct Answer: C) Apply soft padding to the patient's arms to prevent pressure injuries

98.
After the surgical procedure, the nurse assists with transferring the patient to the post-anesthesia care unit (PACU). What information should the nurse provide to the PACU nurse?
A) Patient's intraoperative vital signs and hemodynamic parameters
B) Details of the surgical procedure and anesthesia administration
C) Plan for postoperative pain management and analgesic medications
D) Documentation of the surgical count and instrument inventory

Correct Answer: A, B, C (Similar to Q90, broad handover is key). A is critical for monitoring trends.

99.
During surgery, the nurse notices that the patient's temperature is dropping below the normal range. What should the nurse do?
A) Increase the ambient room temperature in the operating room
B) Administer a warming blanket or forced-air warming device
C) Document the temperature trend in the patient's chart
D) Continue monitoring the patient's temperature closely
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B) Administer a warming blanket or forced-air warming device

Explanation: Active warming is the most effective treatment for intraoperative hypothermia.

100.
While preparing the operating room (OR) for a surgical procedure, the nurse notices that the surgical instrument tray is incomplete. What should the nurse do?
A) Proceed with the procedure using the available instruments

Part III: Specialized Nursing Procedures

Q126 - Q150
126.
A nurse is preparing to perform a tracheostomy tube change for a patient. What action should the nurse prioritize to ensure patient safety during the procedure?
A) Using sterile technique during the tube change
B) Administering sedation to the patient before the procedure
C) Skipping the suctioning step to minimize patient discomfort
D) Changing the tracheostomy ties after completing the tube change
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A) Using sterile technique during the tube change

Explanation: Tracheostomy care requires sterile technique to prevent lower respiratory tract infection.

127.
A nurse is preparing to perform a wound irrigation procedure for a patient with a contaminated wound. What solution should the nurse use for wound irrigation?
A) Sterile water
B) Normal saline
C) Hydrogen peroxide
D) Betadine solution
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B) Normal saline

Explanation: Normal saline is isotonic and non-cytotoxic, making it the preferred solution. Hydrogen peroxide and Betadine can damage healthy tissue.

128.
A nurse is preparing to perform a urinary catheterization for a patient. What action should the nurse take to ensure proper insertion technique?
A) Inserting the catheter without using sterile gloves
B) Using aseptic technique and sterile equipment during catheter insertion
C) Performing catheterization without using lubricating jelly
D) Advancing the catheter rapidly to minimize patient discomfort
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B) Using aseptic technique and sterile equipment during catheter insertion

Explanation: Aseptic technique is mandatory to prevent CAUTI (Catheter-Associated Urinary Tract Infection).

129.
A nurse is preparing to administer a nasogastric (NG) tube feeding for a patient. What action should the nurse prioritize before initiating tube feeding?
A) Verifying the NG tube placement by auscultating for bowel sounds
B) Securing the NG tube to prevent dislodgement during feeding
C) Checking the patency of the NG tube by aspirating gastric contents
D) Elevating the head of the bed to a semi-Fowler's position
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C) Checking the patency of the NG tube by aspirating gastric contents (and pH checking). D is also critical for aspiration prevention, but checking placement is the *first* step before pouring feed.

130.
A nurse is preparing to perform a central venous catheter (CVC) dressing change for a patient. What action should the nurse prioritize to minimize the risk of infection?
A) Wearing sterile gloves and a mask during the dressing change
B) Cleaning the insertion site with alcohol-based antiseptic solution
C) Removing the old dressing and applying a new dressing using sterile technique
D) Changing the CVC tubing along with the dressing to prevent contamination
Show Answer
