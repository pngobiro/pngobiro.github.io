Part I: Reproductive Health & Gynaecology

Q276 - Q300
276.
A 35-year-old woman presents with irregular menstrual cycles, hirsutism, and acne. Which of the following conditions is most likely to be associated with these symptoms?
A) Polycystic ovary syndrome (PCOS)
B) Endometriosis
C) Ovarian cancer
D) Uterine fibroids
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A) Polycystic ovary syndrome (PCOS)

Explanation: The classic triad of oligomenorrhea/amenorrhea (irregular cycles), hyperandrogenism (hirsutism, acne), and polycystic ovaries is diagnostic for PCOS (Rotterdam criteria).

277.
A 28-year-old woman presents with lower abdominal pain, dyspareunia, and dysmenorrhea that worsens during menstruation. On pelvic examination, tender nodules are palpated along the uterosacral ligaments. Which condition is most likely to be responsible for these findings?
A) Polycystic ovary syndrome (PCOS)
B) Endometriosis
C) Pelvic inflammatory disease (PID)
D) Uterine fibroids
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B) Endometriosis

Explanation: The triad of dysmenorrhea, dyspareunia, and chronic pelvic pain, along with palpable tender nodules (often on uterosacral ligaments), is highly suggestive of endometriosis.

278.
Which of the following laboratory findings is characteristic of a patient with a diagnosis of gestational diabetes mellitus (GDM)?
A) Fasting blood glucose ≥ 126 mg/dL
B) Random plasma glucose ≥ 200 mg/dL
C) Hemoglobin A1c (HbA1c) ≥ 6.5%
D) Oral glucose tolerance test (OGTT) 2-hour plasma glucose ≥ 140 mg/dL
Show Answer

Correct Answer: D) Oral glucose tolerance test (OGTT) 2-hour plasma glucose ≥ 140 mg/dL (depending on criteria, e.g., WHO/IADPSG). However, options A, B, and C are diagnostic for pre-existing (overt) diabetes. GDM is specifically diagnosed with OGTT values (e.g., 2h ≥ 140 mg/dL or 7.8 mmol/L).

279.
A 32-year-old pregnant woman presents with painless vaginal bleeding at 10 weeks of gestation. On ultrasound, a gestational sac with no embryo is visualized within the uterus. Which of the following conditions is most likely to be responsible for these findings?
A) Threatened abortion
B) Inevitable abortion
C) Missed abortion
D) Ectopic pregnancy
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C) Missed abortion (or Blighted Ovum/Anembryonic pregnancy).

Explanation: A gestational sac without an embryo (anembryonic pregnancy) with no expulsion of tissue (painless bleeding can occur or be absent) is a missed abortion. Threatened involves a live fetus. Inevitable involves cervical dilation. Ectopic is outside the uterus.

280.
Which of the following diagnostic tests is most appropriate for evaluating a woman with suspected cervical dysplasia?
A) Pap smear
B) Endometrial biopsy
C) Colposcopy with biopsy
D) Transvaginal ultrasound
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C) Colposcopy with biopsy

Explanation: Pap smear is a screening test. Colposcopy with biopsy is the diagnostic test used to evaluate and confirm suspected dysplasia found on screening.

281.
A 45-year-old woman presents with heavy menstrual bleeding and iron deficiency anemia. On pelvic examination, the uterus is enlarged, and multiple irregularly shaped masses are palpated. Which condition is most likely to be responsible for these findings?
A) Leiomyomas (uterine fibroids)
B) Endometrial hyperplasia
C) Endometrial polyps
D) Adenomyosis
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A) Leiomyomas (uterine fibroids)

Explanation: "Multiple irregularly shaped masses" causing an enlarged uterus and menorrhagia is the classic presentation of fibroids.

282.
Which of the following conditions is characterized by the presence of high-risk human papillomavirus (HPV) types and is associated with an increased risk of cervical cancer?
A) Condyloma acuminatum
B) Lichen sclerosus
C) Cervical intraepithelial neoplasia (CIN)
D) Trichomoniasis
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C) Cervical intraepithelial neoplasia (CIN)

Explanation: CIN is the precursor lesion to cervical cancer, driven by high-risk HPV (types 16, 18, etc.). Condyloma (A) is usually low-risk HPV (6, 11).

283.
A 28-year-old woman presents with cyclic pelvic pain, dysmenorrhea, and infertility. On pelvic examination, tender nodules are palpated along the uterosacral ligaments. Which of the following conditions is most likely to be responsible for these findings?
A) Polycystic ovary syndrome (PCOS)
B) Endometriosis
C) Adenomyosis
D) Leiomyomas (uterine fibroids)
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B) Endometriosis

Explanation: (Similar to Q277) This clinical picture is diagnostic for endometriosis.

284.
A 20-year-old woman presents with sudden onset of severe lower abdominal pain and missed menstrual periods for the past two months. She has a positive urine pregnancy test. On transvaginal ultrasound, an empty uterus is visualized, and there is fluid in the cul-de-sac. Which condition is most likely to be responsible for these findings?
A) Ovarian cyst rupture
B) Ectopic pregnancy
C) Septic abortion
D) Ovarian torsion
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B) Ectopic pregnancy

Explanation: Positive pregnancy test + Empty uterus + Free fluid + Pain = Ectopic pregnancy (likely ruptured) until proven otherwise.

285.
Which of the following conditions is characterized by excessive menstrual bleeding due to abnormal proliferation of endometrial tissue outside the uterus?
A) Endometriosis
B) Endometrial hyperplasia
C) Leiomyomas (uterine fibroids)
D) Adenomyosis
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A) Endometriosis (Note: The definition is "endometrial tissue outside the uterus". However, "excessive bleeding" is less common in endometriosis than dysmenorrhea. Adenomyosis (D) is tissue *within* the myometrium and causes bleeding. But based on the "outside" definition, A is the match. If "within the myometrium" was the query, it would be Adenomyosis).

286.
A 38-year-old woman presents with cyclic pelvic pain, dysmenorrhea, and dyspareunia. On pelvic examination, the uterus is retroverted and fixed. Which of the following conditions is most likely to be responsible for these findings?
A) Endometriosis
B) Adenomyosis
C) Leiomyomas (uterine fibroids)
D) Pelvic inflammatory disease (PID)
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A) Endometriosis

Explanation: A "fixed, retroverted uterus" is a hallmark sign of adhesions caused by endometriosis.

287.
A 25-year-old woman presents with cyclic pelvic pain, dysmenorrhea, and dyspareunia. On pelvic examination, the uterus is retroverted, and a tender, nodular mass is palpated behind the uterus. Which of the following conditions is most likely to be responsible for these findings?
A) Endometriosis
B) Adenomyosis
C) Leiomyomas (uterine fibroids)
D) Pelvic inflammatory disease (PID)
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A) Endometriosis

Explanation: The tender nodular mass behind the uterus (in the Cul-de-sac of Douglas) is characteristic of endometriosis implants.

288.
Which of the following is the most common risk factor for the development of ovarian cancer?
A) Nulliparity
B) Early menarche
C) Late menopause
D) Family history of breast cancer
Show Answer

Correct Answer: D) Family history of breast cancer (BRCA mutation) (Note: All options are risk factors related to uninterrupted ovulation, but Family History/Genetics confers the highest relative risk).

289.
A 30-year-old woman presents with cyclic pelvic pain, dysmenorrhea, and infertility. On pelvic ultrasound, a chocolate cyst is visualized within the ovary. Which of the following conditions is most likely to be responsible for these findings?
A) Polycystic ovary syndrome (PCOS)
B) Endometriosis
C) Ovarian torsion
D) Ovarian cyst rupture
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B) Endometriosis

Explanation: A "chocolate cyst" is an endometrioma, a form of endometriosis on the ovary.

290.
Which of the following screening tests is recommended for cervical cancer prevention in women aged 21 to 65 years?
A) Human papillomavirus (HPV) testing alone
B) Cytology (Pap smear) alone
C) Co-testing with cytology and HPV testing
D) Endometrial biopsy
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C) Co-testing with cytology and HPV testing (Preferred for 30-65; Cytology alone for 21-29. However, "Co-testing" is the most comprehensive answer for the broad age range in many guidelines, though technically HPV testing starts at 25 or 30).

291.
A 55-year-old postmenopausal woman presents with vaginal bleeding. On pelvic examination, the cervix appears healthy, and there is no cervical motion tenderness. Which of the following conditions is most likely to be responsible for these findings?
A) Cervical cancer
B) Endometrial cancer
C) Vaginal cancer
D) Cervical polyp
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B) Endometrial cancer

Explanation: Postmenopausal bleeding is Endometrial Cancer until proven otherwise.

292.
Which of the following conditions is characterized by the presence of multiple fluid-filled sacs within the ovaries and is associated with menstrual irregularities and hyperandrogenism?
A) Polycystic ovary syndrome (PCOS)
B) Ovarian torsion
C) Ovarian cyst rupture
D) Ovarian cancer
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A) Polycystic ovary syndrome (PCOS)

Explanation: Definition of PCOS.

293.
Which of the following interventions is recommended for managing a patient with suspected pelvic inflammatory disease (PID)?
A) Empiric antibiotic therapy directed against common pathogens.
B) Surgical exploration and drainage of pelvic abscesses.
C) Hormonal therapy to suppress ovarian function.
D) Symptomatic treatment with nonsteroidal anti-inflammatory drugs (NSAIDs).
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A) Empiric antibiotic therapy directed against common pathogens.

Explanation: Immediate antibiotic treatment (e.g., Ceftriaxone + Doxycycline) prevents long-term sequelae like infertility.

294.
A 22-year-old woman presents with cyclic pelvic pain, dysmenorrhea, and infertility. On pelvic examination, the uterus is retroverted and enlarged. Which of the following conditions is most likely to be responsible for these findings?
A) Endometriosis
B) Adenomyosis
C) Leiomyomas (uterine fibroids)
D) Pelvic inflammatory disease (PID)
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A) Endometriosis (Note: Adenomyosis also causes an enlarged, tender uterus, but usually in older women. In a 22-year-old with infertility and retroverted uterus (adhesions), Endometriosis is the classic exam answer).

295.
Which of the following is a common complication of untreated gonorrhea or chlamydia infection in women?
A) Cervical dysplasia
B) Ectopic pregnancy
C) Urinary incontinence
D) Pelvic organ prolapse
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B) Ectopic pregnancy

Explanation: These infections cause PID, which leads to tubal scarring and a high risk of ectopic pregnancy.

296.
Which of the following is a hallmark symptom of premenstrual syndrome (PMS)?
A) Pelvic pain
B) Breast tenderness
C) Postmenopausal bleeding
D) Dyspareunia
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B) Breast tenderness

Explanation: Mastalgia (breast tenderness), bloating, and mood changes are classic PMS symptoms.

297.
Which of the following conditions is characterized by the presence of abnormal cervical cells that are classified as low-grade squamous intraepithelial lesions (LSIL) on cytology?
A) Cervical cancer
B) Cervical intraepithelial neoplasia (CIN)
C) Cervical polyps
D) Cervicitis
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B) Cervical intraepithelial neoplasia (CIN) (Specifically CIN 1 correlates with LSIL).

298.
A 24-year-old woman presents with cyclic pelvic pain, dysmenorrhea, and dyspareunia. On pelvic examination, tender nodules are palpated along the uterosacral ligaments. Which of the following conditions is most likely to be responsible for these findings?
A) Pelvic inflammatory disease (PID)
B) Endometriosis
C) Ovarian cyst rupture
D) Uterine fibroids
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B) Endometriosis

Explanation: (Repeated concept) Classic endometriosis signs.

299.
Which of the following is the most common type of ovarian tumor in women of reproductive age?
A) Serous cystadenoma
B) Mucinous cystadenoma
C) Teratoma (dermoid cyst)
D) Endometrioma
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C) Teratoma (dermoid cyst)

Explanation: Mature cystic teratomas are the most common germ cell tumors and the most common ovarian neoplasm in reproductive-age women (excluding functional cysts).

300.
Which of the following is a risk factor for the development of ovarian cancer?
A) Multiparity
B) Use of oral contraceptives
C) Early age at menarche
D) Family history of breast cancer
Show Answer

Correct Answer: D) Family history of breast cancer

Explanation: B and A are protective. Early menarche (C) is a risk factor, but Family History (BRCA) is a *stronger* risk factor.

Part II: Midwifery & Obstetrics

Q601 - Q725
601.
A pregnant woman presents with severe abdominal pain and vaginal bleeding at 8 weeks gestation. On examination, the cervix is closed. Which of the following conditions is the most likely cause of these symptoms?
A) Ectopic pregnancy
B) Threatened abortion
C) Placenta previa
D) Gestational trophoblastic disease
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A) Ectopic pregnancy (or Threatened). Severe pain points to Ectopic. Threatened abortion usually has mild cramping. A closed cervix is present in both.

602.
A pregnant woman presents with painless, bright red vaginal bleeding at 34 weeks gestation. On examination, the cervix is found to be closed. Which of the following conditions is the most likely cause of these symptoms?
A) Ectopic pregnancy
B) Abruptio placentae
C) Placenta previa
D) Gestational trophoblastic disease
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C) Placenta previa

Explanation: Painless bright red bleeding in the third trimester is the hallmark of Placenta Previa. Abruptio (B) is painful.

626.
During the active phase of labor, the nurse observes that the cervix is dilated to 6 cm and the contractions are regular, lasting 60 seconds each, occurring every 3 minutes. What action should the nurse take?
A) Encourage the mother to push.
B) Administer oxytocin to augment labor.
C) Prepare for delivery.
D) Continue to monitor the progress of labor.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: D) Continue to monitor the progress of labor.

Explanation: This is a normal progression for active labor (6cm, regular contractions). Pushing (A) is for 10cm. Oxytocin (B) is for dystocia. Delivery (C) is premature.

635.
A woman in active labor requests hydrotherapy for pain relief. What is the nurse's priority action before initiating hydrotherapy?
A) Obtain informed consent from the mother.
B) Ensure that the labor room is equipped with a hydrotherapy tub.
C) Assess the maternal and fetal conditions for eligibility.
D) Review the mother's medical history for contraindications.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C) Assess the maternal and fetal conditions for eligibility.

Explanation: Safety first. Hydrotherapy is contraindicated in certain conditions (e.g., fetal distress, fever). Assessment determines safety.

651.
During the active phase of labor, a woman's cervical dilation is progressing slowly despite regular contractions. What maternal condition should the nurse assess for that may contribute to abnormal labor progression?
A) Maternal exhaustion
B) Pelvic outlet obstruction
C) Fetal macrosomia
D) Uterine hyperstimulation
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C) Fetal macrosomia (CPD - Cephalopelvic Disproportion).

Explanation: If contractions are adequate but dilation is slow, CPD/Macrosomia is a major mechanical factor.

676.
During the postpartum assessment, a new mother reports experiencing mild cramping and uterine contractions while breastfeeding. What physiological process is responsible for these symptoms?
A) Prolactin release
B) Oxytocin release
C) Estrogen surge
D) Progesterone withdrawal
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B) Oxytocin release

Explanation: Breastfeeding stimulates oxytocin release ("Let-down reflex"), which also causes uterine contractions (afterpains) to prevent hemorrhage.

692.
A postpartum client who delivered preterm expresses concern about breastfeeding her premature infant. What nursing intervention should be prioritized to support successful breastfeeding in this situation?
A) Providing education on strategies for initiating and maintaining milk supply
B) Recommending the use of supplemental formula feedings to ensure adequate nutrition
C) Encouraging the client to postpone breastfeeding until the infant reaches full term
D) Referring the client to a lactation consultant for specialized support
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A) Providing education on strategies for initiating and maintaining milk supply (e.g., pumping) since the infant may not suckle well yet.
