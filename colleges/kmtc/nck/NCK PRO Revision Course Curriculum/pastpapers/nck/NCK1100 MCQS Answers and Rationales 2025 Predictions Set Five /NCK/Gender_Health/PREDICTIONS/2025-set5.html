Instructions

  • This paper consists of 50 Multiple Choice Questions focusing on Gender and its intersection with healthcare.
  • Each question has four options; select the most appropriate one.
  • Detailed rationales are provided for each answer to facilitate effective revision.
  • Total Marks: 50 | Time Allowed: 60 Minutes.

Section 1: Gender Concepts & Roles

25 Marks
1.
Which term best describes the socially constructed roles, behaviors, and attributes that a society considers appropriate for men and women?
A) Sex
B) Gender
C) Sexual orientation
D) Biological determinism
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B) Gender

Rationale: Gender refers to the socially constructed roles, behaviors, and attributes that society assigns to individuals based on their perceived sex. It differs from sex, which is biologically determined.

2.
What does "gender identity" primarily refer to?
A) The biological differences between males and females
B) A person’s internal sense of being male, female, or another gender
C) The roles assigned by society to men and women
D) Sexual preference or attraction
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B) A person’s internal sense of being male, female, or another gender

Rationale: Gender identity is an individual's deeply felt internal experience of gender, which may or may not correspond with their biological sex.

3.
Which of the following best exemplifies "gender stereotypes"?
A) Men are naturally better leaders than women
B) Gender identity is fluid
C) Sex is determined by chromosomes
D) Both men and women can perform any job equally
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: A) Men are naturally better leaders than women

Rationale: Gender stereotypes are oversimplified and widely held beliefs about characteristics or roles appropriate for men and women, such as men being better leaders.

4.
Which term refers to the biological and physiological characteristics that define men and women?
A) Gender
B) Sex
C) Gender role
D) Gender expression
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B) Sex

Rationale: Sex refers to the biological attributes such as chromosomes, hormones, and anatomy that categorize individuals as male, female, or intersex.

5.
What is "gender equity"?
A) Treating everyone the same regardless of gender
B) Fairness in treatment according to individual needs and circumstances based on gender
C) Ensuring men dominate leadership roles
D) Ignoring gender differences entirely
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B) Fairness in treatment according to individual needs and circumstances based on gender

Rationale: Gender equity involves fairness and justice in the distribution of benefits and responsibilities between men and women, recognizing different needs and conditions.

6.
Which of the following terms refers to discrimination based on gender?
A) Sexism
B) Racism
C) Ageism
D) Ableism
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: A) Sexism

Rationale: Sexism is prejudice or discrimination based on a person’s gender, often resulting in disadvantages or unequal treatment.

7.
Which of the following is an example of gender socialization?
A) Learning mathematics in school
B) Boys being encouraged to play with trucks, while girls are given dolls
C) Learning to swim
D) Reading a book on history
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B) Boys being encouraged to play with trucks, while girls are given dolls

Rationale: Gender socialization is the process by which society teaches individuals the gender roles and expectations, such as toys associated with a particular gender.

8.
What does "toxic masculinity" refer to?
A) Harmful cultural norms that encourage men to suppress emotions and behave aggressively
B) A medical condition
C) Biological traits exclusive to men
D) Positive male behavior
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: A) Harmful cultural norms that encourage men to suppress emotions and behave aggressively

Rationale: Toxic masculinity refers to cultural norms that associate masculinity with dominance, aggression, and emotional repression, which can be harmful to men and others.

9.
What is meant by "gender expression"?
A) Biological sex assigned at birth
B) How a person publicly expresses or presents their gender
C) A person’s sexual orientation
D) The social roles assigned by society
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B) How a person publicly expresses or presents their gender

Rationale: Gender expression is about how individuals show their gender through behavior, clothing, voice, and other forms.

10.
Which term describes a person whose gender identity does not align with their sex assigned at birth?
A) Cisgender
B) Transgender
C) Intersex
D) Androgynous
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B) Transgender

Rationale: Transgender people have a gender identity or expression different from the sex they were assigned at birth.

11.
Which of the following is a key goal of feminist movements?
A) Maintaining traditional gender roles
B) Achieving gender equality and women's rights
C) Promoting male superiority
D) Encouraging gender stereotypes
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B) Achieving gender equality and women's rights

Rationale: Feminism seeks to challenge gender inequalities and advocate for equal rights and opportunities for women.

12.
Which term describes people born with physical sex characteristics that do not fit typical binary notions of male or female bodies?
A) Transgender
B) Intersex
C) Non-binary
D) Gender fluid
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B) Intersex

Rationale: Intersex individuals are born with variations in sex characteristics that differ from typical male or female definitions.

13.
Which of the following is an example of gender-based violence?
A) Verbal abuse targeting someone’s gender
B) Physical assault motivated by gender
C) Sexual harassment
D) All of the above
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: D) All of the above

Rationale: Gender-based violence includes acts that cause harm or suffering based on gender, including verbal, physical, and sexual forms.

14.
Which of the following best defines "patriarchy"?
A) A system where women hold all power
B) A system where men hold dominant power in society
C) Equal power sharing between genders
D) A matrilineal society
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B) A system where men hold dominant power in society

Rationale: Patriarchy is a social system in which men hold primary power and predominate roles of political leadership, moral authority, and control of property.

15.
What does the term "gender mainstreaming" mean?
A) Ignoring gender differences
B) Integrating a gender perspective into all policies and programs
C) Focusing only on women’s issues
D) Promoting only men’s rights
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B) Integrating a gender perspective into all policies and programs

Rationale: Gender mainstreaming aims to assess the implications for different genders in all policies and ensure gender equality is considered in decision-making.

16.
Which of these is a gender-neutral pronoun?
A) He
B) She
C) They
D) Him
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: C) They

Rationale: "They" can be used as a singular gender-neutral pronoun to refer to someone whose gender is unknown or to people who identify outside the binary.

17.
What is the significance of "intersectionality" in gender studies?
A) Focuses only on gender as a factor of identity
B) Examines how multiple social identities (race, class, gender) intersect to affect experiences
C) A theory about biological sex differences
D) A type of gender role
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B) Examines how multiple social identities (race, class, gender) intersect to affect experiences

Rationale: Intersectionality acknowledges that people’s experiences are shaped by overlapping social identities and systems of oppression.

18.
Which of the following terms describes rejection of the gender binary (male/female)?
A) Cisgender
B) Non-binary
C) Transgender
D) Gender conforming
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B) Non-binary

Rationale: Non-binary refers to gender identities that do not fit within the traditional categories of male or female.

19.
Which of the following is NOT a common barrier to gender equality?
A) Gender stereotypes
B) Unequal pay
C) Gender quotas in leadership
D) Lack of education opportunities for girls
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: C) Gender quotas in leadership

Rationale: Gender quotas aim to reduce barriers and increase women's representation, whereas the other options represent barriers to equality.

20.
What does "patriarchal bargain" refer to?
A) Women actively conforming to patriarchal norms for personal advantage
B) Men giving up power to women
C) Equal sharing of power
D) A legal contract in patriarchal societies
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: A) Women actively conforming to patriarchal norms for personal advantage

Rationale: It describes how some women may comply with patriarchal expectations to gain social or economic benefits.

21.
Which organization is known for promoting gender equality globally?
A) World Health Organization (WHO)
B) United Nations Entity for Gender Equality and the Empowerment of Women (UN Women)
C) World Trade Organization (WTO)
D) International Monetary Fund (IMF)
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B) United Nations Entity for Gender Equality and the Empowerment of Women (UN Women)

Rationale: UN Women focuses on promoting gender equality and empowering women worldwide.

22.
What is "hegemonic masculinity"?
A) The dominant ideal of masculinity that subordinates women and other masculinities
B) Biological maleness
C) A feminist theory
D) The practice of gender neutrality
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: A) The dominant ideal of masculinity that subordinates women and other masculinities

Rationale: Hegemonic masculinity represents the culturally dominant ideal of manhood that legitimizes men's dominant position in society.

23.
Which of these best illustrates "gender fluidity"?
A) A person strictly identifies as male
B) A person’s gender identity changes over time or situation
C) Gender is determined by biology alone
D) Gender roles never change
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B) A person’s gender identity changes over time or situation

Rationale: Gender fluidity refers to a flexible range of gender expression and identity that may shift over time.

24.
Which of the following is a key principle in achieving gender-sensitive health care?
A) Treating all patients identically
B) Recognizing gender differences in health risks and outcomes
C) Ignoring gender in treatment plans
D) Providing only male doctors
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B) Recognizing gender differences in health risks and outcomes

Rationale: Gender-sensitive health care acknowledges and addresses gender-specific health needs and disparities.

25.
What does "gender pay gap" refer to?
A) Equal pay for men and women
B) Difference in average earnings between men and women
C) Men earning less than women
D) Women getting promoted faster
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B) Difference in average earnings between men and women

Rationale: The gender pay gap is the average difference in wages earned by men and women, often reflecting systemic inequality.

Section 2: Gender Identity & Social Impact

25 Marks
26.
Which of the following best describes gender identity?
A) The biological differences between males and females
B) A person’s deeply felt internal experience of gender
C) The roles society assigns to men and women
D) Physical characteristics used to assign sex at birth
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B) A person’s deeply felt internal experience of gender

Rationale: Gender identity refers to an individual's personal sense of their gender, which may or may not correspond with the sex assigned at birth. It is an internal, deeply felt experience, unlike biological sex or societal gender roles.

27.
What is the term for social expectations and behaviors considered appropriate for men and women in a society?
A) Gender identity
B) Sex
C) Gender roles
D) Sexual orientation
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: C) Gender roles

Rationale: Gender roles are the societal norms dictating appropriate behaviors and responsibilities for men and women, often based on cultural, social, and historical contexts.

28.
Which concept explains the idea that gender is not fixed but can vary across cultures and over time?
A) Gender binary
B) Gender fluidity
C) Gender essentialism
D) Gender conformity
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B) Gender fluidity

Rationale: Gender fluidity recognizes that gender is not strictly binary or fixed; it can change over time and across cultural settings, reflecting a spectrum of identities.

29.
Which of the following is an example of gender nonconformity?
A) A man wearing traditionally female clothing
B) A woman identifying as heterosexual
C) A person assigned female at birth who identifies as female
D) A man who prefers traditionally male jobs
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: A) A man wearing traditionally female clothing

Rationale: Gender nonconformity occurs when a person expresses gender in ways that differ from societal expectations for their assigned sex, such as a man wearing clothing traditionally associated with women.

30.
What does the term “cisgender” mean?
A) Someone whose gender identity matches their sex assigned at birth
B) Someone who identifies as transgender
C) Someone who is attracted to multiple genders
D) Someone who rejects gender labels altogether
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: A) Someone whose gender identity matches their sex assigned at birth

Rationale: Cisgender individuals identify with the gender corresponding to their biological sex assigned at birth, contrasting with transgender people whose gender identity differs from their assigned sex.

31.
Which term describes a person who does not exclusively identify as male or female?
A) Transgender
B) Non-binary
C) Cisgender
D) Heterosexual
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B) Non-binary

Rationale: Non-binary individuals do not identify strictly as male or female; they may identify as a mix of both, neither, or a different gender altogether.

32.
What is the main focus of feminist theory in gender studies?
A) To study biological differences between sexes
B) To analyze how society constructs and perpetuates gender inequalities
C) To promote gender conformity
D) To reinforce traditional gender roles
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B) To analyze how society constructs and perpetuates gender inequalities

Rationale: Feminist theory critically examines the social constructions and power dynamics that create and sustain gender inequalities, seeking to challenge and change these structures.

33.
Which is an example of institutional sexism?
A) A law that restricts women’s access to education
B) A man refusing to hire a woman for a job
C) Gender stereotypes in media
D) A woman choosing to stay home with her children
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: A) A law that restricts women’s access to education

Rationale: Institutional sexism involves policies or laws that systematically discriminate against a gender, such as restricting educational opportunities for women.

34.
How does intersectionality relate to gender?
A) It focuses only on gender differences
B) It examines how gender intersects with other identities like race and class
C) It promotes a single gender perspective
D) It denies the existence of multiple identities
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B) It examines how gender intersects with other identities like race and class

Rationale: Intersectionality studies how various social identities (gender, race, class, sexuality) overlap and affect experiences of discrimination or privilege.

35.
Which of the following best defines “gender expression”?
A) The biological sex someone is born with
B) How a person publicly presents their gender through appearance and behavior
C) A person’s sexual preference
D) A cultural idea of masculinity
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B) How a person publicly presents their gender through appearance and behavior

Rationale: Gender expression refers to external manifestations of gender, such as clothing, hairstyle, mannerisms, and voice, through which individuals communicate their gender to others.

36.
What does the term “toxic masculinity” refer to?
A) Healthy male behaviors
B) Socially harmful norms that pressure men to behave aggressively or suppress emotions
C) The biological nature of males
D) Feminine traits in men
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B) Socially harmful norms that pressure men to behave aggressively or suppress emotions

Rationale: Toxic masculinity refers to cultural norms that encourage men to act in damaging ways, including emotional repression, dominance, and aggression, often harming themselves and others.

37.
Which is a common challenge faced by transgender individuals?
A) Universal acceptance in all cultures
B) Discrimination and lack of access to healthcare
C) Preference for traditional gender roles
D) No challenges related to gender
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B) Discrimination and lack of access to healthcare

Rationale: Transgender people often face significant challenges, including discrimination, violence, and barriers to accessing gender-affirming healthcare.

38.
Which phrase best describes “gender equity”?
A) Treating everyone exactly the same regardless of need
B) Providing fairness by addressing different needs and circumstances related to gender
C) Ignoring gender differences
D) Promoting one gender over another
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B) Providing fairness by addressing different needs and circumstances related to gender

Rationale: Gender equity involves fairness by recognizing different needs or barriers faced by different genders and addressing them to achieve equal outcomes.

39.
Which of the following is an example of gender socialization?
A) A child learns that boys should play with trucks, girls with dolls
B) Biological differences between males and females
C) Sexual orientation development
D) Gender identity formation
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: A) A child learns that boys should play with trucks, girls with dolls

Rationale: Gender socialization is the process through which individuals learn culturally appropriate gender roles and behaviors, such as children being encouraged toward gender-stereotypical toys.

40.
What is the main purpose of gender mainstreaming in policies?
A) To eliminate gender considerations in decision-making
B) To incorporate gender perspectives into all stages of policy and program development
C) To focus exclusively on women’s issues
D) To maintain traditional gender roles
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B) To incorporate gender perspectives into all stages of policy and program development

Rationale: Gender mainstreaming aims to integrate gender considerations systematically in all policies and programs to promote gender equality.

41.
Which of the following terms relates to attraction to people of the same gender?
A) Bisexuality
B) Heterosexuality
C) Homosexuality
D) Pansexuality
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: C) Homosexuality

Rationale: Homosexuality refers to romantic or sexual attraction to people of the same gender.

42.
What does “patriarchy” mean?
A) A social system where men hold primary power and dominate roles of leadership
B) Equal power shared by all genders
C) Matriarchal family structures
D) Female domination in society
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: A) A social system where men hold primary power and dominate roles of leadership

Rationale: Patriarchy is a societal structure where men hold primary authority and control political, economic, and social power, often subordinating women.

43.
Which of the following is an example of gender-based violence?
A) Equal pay for equal work
B) Domestic abuse targeted at a person because of their gender
C) Gender-neutral sports teams
D) Gender equality policies
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B) Domestic abuse targeted at a person because of their gender

Rationale: Gender-based violence includes violence directed at individuals due to their gender, such as domestic abuse, sexual violence, and harassment.

44.
What is “gender pay gap”?
A) Equal wages between men and women
B) The difference in average earnings between men and women
C) Pay differences due to experience only
D) Salary differences based on education level only
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B) The difference in average earnings between men and women

Rationale: The gender pay gap refers to the disparity in average earnings between men and women, often due to discrimination, occupational segregation, and unequal opportunities.

45.
What role do “gender stereotypes” play in society?
A) They promote individual freedom
B) They create fixed expectations about how genders should behave
C) They ensure gender equality
D) They eliminate discrimination
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B) They create fixed expectations about how genders should behave

Rationale: Gender stereotypes impose rigid and often limiting ideas about appropriate behaviors and roles for men and women, which can perpetuate inequality.

46.
Which term best describes an individual whose gender identity differs from their sex assigned at birth?
A) Cisgender
B) Transgender
C) Non-binary
D) Androgynous
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B) Transgender

Rationale: Transgender individuals have a gender identity that differs from their assigned sex at birth.

47.
Which of the following is NOT a way to promote gender equality?
A) Challenging gender stereotypes
B) Providing equal educational opportunities
C) Enforcing discriminatory laws
D) Promoting women’s participation in leadership
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: C) Enforcing discriminatory laws

Rationale: Enforcing discriminatory laws contradicts the goal of gender equality, which seeks to eliminate such laws and promote fairness.

48.
What does “gender binary” refer to?
A) The idea that there are only two distinct genders: male and female
B) Recognition of multiple gender identities
C) Nonconforming gender expression
D) Fluid gender identity
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: A) The idea that there are only two distinct genders: male and female

Rationale: Gender binary is the classification of gender into two distinct and opposite forms, male and female, which is often challenged by gender diversity advocates.

49.
Which of the following is true about “sexual orientation”?
A) It is the same as gender identity
B) It relates to who one is attracted to emotionally, romantically, or sexually
C) It determines gender roles
D) It can be changed through therapy
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B) It relates to who one is attracted to emotionally, romantically, or sexually

Rationale: Sexual orientation concerns an individual’s pattern of attraction to others, distinct from gender identity, which is about personal sense of gender.

50.
What does “gender empowerment” mean?
A) Restricting roles based on gender
B) Increasing the ability of individuals to make choices and transform those choices into actions regardless of gender
C) Maintaining traditional gender hierarchies
D) Enforcing gender conformity
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B) Increasing the ability of individuals to make choices and transform those choices into actions regardless of gender

Rationale: Gender empowerment focuses on enabling all individuals to have the capacity, resources, and freedom to make decisions and act upon them, breaking down gender-based barriers.
