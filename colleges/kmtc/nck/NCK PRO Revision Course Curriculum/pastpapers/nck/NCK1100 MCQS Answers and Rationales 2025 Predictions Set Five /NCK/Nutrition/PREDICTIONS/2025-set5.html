Section 1: Macronutrients & Vitamins

25 Marks
1.
Which vitamin is essential for calcium absorption in the gut?
A) Vitamin C
B) Vitamin A
C) Vitamin D
D) Vitamin K
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: C) Vitamin D

Rationale: Vitamin D enhances calcium absorption in the intestine, which is crucial for bone health and preventing conditions like rickets.

2.
What is the primary function of carbohydrates in the body?
A) Provide structural support
B) Serve as the main energy source
C) Build muscle tissue
D) Act as enzymes
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B) Serve as the main energy source

Rationale: Carbohydrates are the bodyâ€™s preferred source of energy, especially for brain and muscle function.

3.
Which mineral is most important in oxygen transport in the blood?
A) Calcium
B) Iron
C) Zinc
D) Magnesium
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B) Iron

Rationale: Iron is a critical component of hemoglobin, the protein responsible for carrying oxygen in red blood cells.

4.
Which nutrient deficiency is the leading cause of preventable blindness worldwide?
A) Vitamin B12
B) Vitamin A
C) Vitamin C
D) Vitamin E
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B) Vitamin A

Rationale: Vitamin A deficiency can cause xerophthalmia and night blindness, and is a major cause of preventable blindness.

5.
What is the main source of saturated fats in the diet?
A) Olive oil
B) Butter
C) Fish oil
D) Canola oil
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B) Butter

Rationale: Butter contains high levels of saturated fats, which can raise LDL cholesterol levels.

6.
Which vitamin is necessary for collagen synthesis?
A) Vitamin C
B) Vitamin D
C) Vitamin K
D) Vitamin B6
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: A) Vitamin C

Rationale: Vitamin C is a cofactor in the hydroxylation of collagen, which is essential for wound healing and tissue integrity.

7.
Which macronutrient yields the highest calories per gram?
A) Carbohydrates
B) Protein
C) Fat
D) Fiber
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: C) Fat

Rationale: Fat provides 9 calories per gram, more than double the 4 calories per gram from carbohydrates and proteins.

8.
Which nutrient deficiency can lead to megaloblastic anemia?
A) Vitamin B12
B) Iron
C) Vitamin D
D) Vitamin C
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: A) Vitamin B12

Rationale: Vitamin B12 deficiency impairs DNA synthesis in red blood cells, causing large, immature cells (megaloblasts).

9.
Which of the following is a water-soluble vitamin?
A) Vitamin A
B) Vitamin D
C) Vitamin B6
D) Vitamin E
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: C) Vitamin B6

Rationale: Vitamin B6 is a water-soluble vitamin, unlike fat-soluble vitamins A, D, and E.

10.
What is the primary role of dietary fiber?
A) Provide energy
B) Support immune function
C) Promote digestive health and bowel regularity
D) Aid in calcium absorption
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: C) Promote digestive health and bowel regularity

Rationale: Dietary fiber adds bulk to stool and promotes intestinal motility, preventing constipation.

11.
Which mineral plays a key role in thyroid hormone production?
A) Zinc
B) Selenium
C) Iodine
D) Copper
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: C) Iodine

Rationale: Iodine is essential for synthesizing thyroid hormones; deficiency leads to goiter and hypothyroidism.

12.
Which vitamin is critical for blood clotting?
A) Vitamin D
B) Vitamin A
C) Vitamin K
D) Vitamin E
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: C) Vitamin K

Rationale: Vitamin K is necessary for synthesis of clotting factors; deficiency increases bleeding risk.

13.
Which of the following is an essential fatty acid?
A) Oleic acid
B) Linoleic acid
C) Palmitic acid
D) Stearic acid
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B) Linoleic acid

Rationale: Linoleic acid is an omega-6 essential fatty acid the body cannot synthesize.

14.
Which vitamin deficiency is linked to neural tube defects during pregnancy?
A) Vitamin B9 (Folate)
B) Vitamin C
C) Vitamin A
D) Vitamin D
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: A) Vitamin B9 (Folate)

Rationale: Adequate folate intake reduces risk of neural tube defects like spina bifida.

15.
Which nutrient is important for DNA synthesis and repair?
A) Vitamin C
B) Folate
C) Vitamin D
D) Calcium
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B) Folate

Rationale: Folate participates in nucleotide synthesis necessary for DNA replication and repair.

16.
Which of these nutrients is primarily stored in the liver?
A) Vitamin C
B) Vitamin B12
C) Vitamin A
D) Vitamin B6
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: C) Vitamin A

Rationale: Vitamin A is fat-soluble and stored in the liver for future use.

17.
Excess intake of which vitamin can lead to hypercalcemia?
A) Vitamin A
B) Vitamin D
C) Vitamin E
D) Vitamin C
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B) Vitamin D

Rationale: High doses of vitamin D increase calcium absorption, potentially causing hypercalcemia.

18.
Which of the following is a trace mineral?
A) Calcium
B) Magnesium
C) Zinc
D) Phosphorus
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: C) Zinc

Rationale: Zinc is required in small amounts (trace mineral), unlike calcium or magnesium.

19.
Which nutrient deficiency causes scurvy?
A) Vitamin A
B) Vitamin B12
C) Vitamin C
D) Vitamin D
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: C) Vitamin C

Rationale: Lack of vitamin C impairs collagen synthesis leading to symptoms of scurvy.

20.
Which of the following is a good plant-based source of protein?
A) Rice
B) Lentils
C) Butter
D) Corn oil
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B) Lentils

Rationale: Lentils are rich in protein and important in vegetarian diets.

21.
What is the recommended daily intake of protein for an average adult?
A) 0.8 grams per kg body weight
B) 1.5 grams per kg body weight
C) 2.5 grams per kg body weight
D) 3.0 grams per kg body weight
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: A) 0.8 grams per kg body weight

Rationale: This is the standard recommended protein intake for healthy adults to maintain nitrogen balance.

22.
Which vitamin deficiency is associated with pernicious anemia?
A) Vitamin B9
B) Vitamin B12
C) Vitamin C
D) Vitamin A
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B) Vitamin B12

Rationale: Pernicious anemia results from impaired absorption of vitamin B12 due to lack of intrinsic factor.

23.
Which of these is a monosaccharide?
A) Sucrose
B) Lactose
C) Glucose
D) Maltose
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: C) Glucose

Rationale: Glucose is a single sugar unit (monosaccharide); sucrose and lactose are disaccharides.

24.
What is the effect of excessive sodium intake?
A) Hypertension
B) Hypotension
C) Anemia
D) Osteoporosis
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: A) Hypertension

Rationale: High sodium intake increases blood pressure and risk of cardiovascular diseases.

25.
Which of the following is a source of complete protein?
A) Beans
B) Quinoa
C) Wheat
D) Rice
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B) Quinoa

Rationale: Quinoa contains all essential amino acids, making it a complete protein source.

Section 2: Clinical Nutrition & Storage

25 Marks
26.
What is the main role of antioxidants in nutrition?
A) Provide energy
B) Prevent oxidative damage
C) Promote digestion
D) Increase protein synthesis
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B) Prevent oxidative damage

Rationale: Antioxidants neutralize free radicals that can damage cells and DNA.

27.
Which vitamin can be synthesized in the skin upon sunlight exposure?
A) Vitamin C
B) Vitamin D
C) Vitamin A
D) Vitamin E
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B) Vitamin D

Rationale: UV rays convert 7-dehydrocholesterol in skin to vitamin D3.

28.
Which mineral deficiency causes goiter?
A) Iron
B) Iodine
C) Zinc
D) Calcium
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B) Iodine

Rationale: Iodine deficiency reduces thyroid hormone synthesis, causing thyroid gland enlargement (goiter).

29.
Which vitamin is important for vision and immune function?
A) Vitamin A
B) Vitamin C
C) Vitamin E
D) Vitamin K
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: A) Vitamin A

Rationale: Vitamin A supports retina function and maintains epithelial tissue integrity.

30.
Which nutrient is most likely deficient in a vegan diet without supplementation?
A) Vitamin C
B) Vitamin B12
C) Vitamin E
D) Vitamin A
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B) Vitamin B12

Rationale: B12 is primarily found in animal products and may be deficient in vegans.

31.
Which of the following is an example of a saturated fatty acid?
A) Oleic acid
B) Linoleic acid
C) Stearic acid
D) Alpha-linolenic acid
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: C) Stearic acid

Rationale: Stearic acid is a saturated fatty acid found in animal fats.

32.
Which vitamin deficiency causes beriberi?
A) Vitamin B1 (Thiamine)
B) Vitamin B2 (Riboflavin)
C) Vitamin B3 (Niacin)
D) Vitamin B6 (Pyridoxine)
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: A) Vitamin B1 (Thiamine)

Rationale: Thiamine deficiency impairs energy metabolism, causing beriberi.

33.
What is the role of potassium in the body?
A) Blood clotting
B) Muscle contraction and nerve function
C) Oxygen transport
D) Collagen formation
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B) Muscle contraction and nerve function

Rationale: Potassium is essential for maintaining cell membrane potential and muscle function.

34.
Which vitamin is known as ascorbic acid?
A) Vitamin A
B) Vitamin C
C) Vitamin E
D) Vitamin K
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B) Vitamin C

Rationale: Ascorbic acid is another name for vitamin C.

35.
Excess intake of which vitamin can cause hypervitaminosis A?
A) Vitamin A
B) Vitamin D
C) Vitamin C
D) Vitamin K
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: A) Vitamin A

Rationale: High doses of vitamin A can be toxic, causing symptoms like headache and liver damage.

36.
Which nutrient deficiency is linked to delayed wound healing?
A) Vitamin C
B) Iron
C) Zinc
D) Calcium
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: C) Zinc

Rationale: Zinc is critical for immune function and tissue repair.

37.
Which of the following is the main storage form of energy in the body?
A) Glycogen
B) Protein
C) Fat (triglycerides)
D) ATP
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: C) Fat (triglycerides)

Rationale: Fat stores more energy per gram and is the primary long-term energy reserve.

38.
Which of these is a fat-soluble vitamin?
A) Vitamin B12
B) Vitamin C
C) Vitamin E
D) Vitamin B6
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: C) Vitamin E

Rationale: Vitamin E is fat-soluble, along with vitamins A, D, and K.

39.
Which vitamin deficiency leads to pellagra?
A) Vitamin B1
B) Vitamin B2
C) Vitamin B3 (Niacin)
D) Vitamin B6
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: C) Vitamin B3 (Niacin)

Rationale: Pellagra results from niacin deficiency causing dermatitis, diarrhea, and dementia.

40.
Which macronutrient is primarily used for tissue repair and enzyme production?
A) Carbohydrates
B) Proteins
C) Fats
D) Vitamins
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B) Proteins

Rationale: Proteins provide amino acids essential for building tissues and enzymes.

41.
Which mineral is important for bone formation along with calcium?
A) Sodium
B) Phosphorus
C) Iron
D) Potassium
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B) Phosphorus

Rationale: Phosphorus combines with calcium to form hydroxyapatite in bones.

42.
Which vitamin deficiency causes rickets in children?
A) Vitamin A
B) Vitamin D
C) Vitamin E
D) Vitamin K
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B) Vitamin D

Rationale: Vitamin D deficiency impairs calcium absorption leading to soft, deformed bones.

43.
Which of the following contains probiotics?
A) Yogurt
B) Milk
C) Cheese
D) Butter
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: A) Yogurt

Rationale: Yogurt contains live beneficial bacteria that support gut health.

44.
Which vitamin helps in iron absorption from the gut?
A) Vitamin A
B) Vitamin C
C) Vitamin D
D) Vitamin K
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B) Vitamin C

Rationale: Vitamin C reduces ferric iron to ferrous iron, enhancing absorption.

45.
Which nutrient is mainly involved in the synthesis of neurotransmitters?
A) Vitamin B6
B) Vitamin A
C) Vitamin D
D) Vitamin E
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: A) Vitamin B6

Rationale: Vitamin B6 is a coenzyme in synthesis of neurotransmitters like serotonin.

46.
Which of the following is a complete protein source?
A) Almonds
B) Chicken
C) Rice
D) Lentils
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B) Chicken

Rationale: Animal proteins like chicken contain all essential amino acids.

47.
Which nutrient deficiency causes hypokalemia?
A) Excess potassium
B) Low potassium
C) Low calcium
D) Excess sodium
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B) Low potassium

Rationale: Hypokalemia is a condition due to insufficient potassium, causing muscle weakness.

48.
Which vitamin is essential for red blood cell formation and neurological function?
A) Vitamin B9 (Folate)
B) Vitamin B12
C) Vitamin C
D) Vitamin D
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B) Vitamin B12

Rationale: B12 is necessary for erythropoiesis and myelin sheath maintenance.

49.
Which macronutrient can be converted into glucose via gluconeogenesis?
A) Carbohydrates
B) Fatty acids
C) Proteins
D) Vitamins
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: C) Proteins

Rationale: Amino acids from protein can be converted into glucose in the liver.

50.
Which vitamin is important for vision and is found in orange vegetables?
A) Vitamin C
B) Beta-carotene (Vitamin A precursor)
C) Vitamin D
D) Vitamin E
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B) Beta-carotene (Vitamin A precursor)

Rationale: Beta-carotene is converted to vitamin A, supporting vision; abundant in carrots.
