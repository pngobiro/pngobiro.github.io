Special Needs People

NCK, KRCHN, PAPER 2 - 1100 MCQS Answers and Rationales

Instructions

  • This paper consists of 50 Multiple Choice Questions focusing on Special Needs and Disability care.
  • Each question has four options; select the most appropriate one.
  • Detailed rationales are provided for each answer to facilitate effective revision.
  • Total Marks: 50 | Time Allowed: 60 Minutes.

Section 1: Definitions and Accessibility

25 Marks
1.
Which of the following is the best definition of a person with special needs?
A. Someone who requires assistance due to physical, mental, or emotional disability
B. A person with minor temporary illnesses
C. Someone who is taller than average
D. A person with excellent physical fitness
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: A. Someone who requires assistance due to physical, mental, or emotional disability

Rationale: A person with special needs has long-term impairments that limit daily activities or require support. Temporary illnesses or physical fitness are unrelated.

2.
Which of the following is a common barrier faced by people with disabilities in accessing healthcare?
A. Availability of specialized equipment
B. High level of health literacy
C. Physical accessibility challenges
D. Excessive healthcare staff
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: C. Physical accessibility challenges

Rationale: Many health facilities lack ramps, wide doors, or other accommodations, making access difficult. Equipment availability may vary, but physical access is a frequent barrier.

3.
Which legal framework supports the rights of people with disabilities internationally?
A. The United Nations Convention on the Rights of Persons with Disabilities (CRPD)
B. The Geneva Convention
C. The Kyoto Protocol
D. The Paris Agreement
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: A. The United Nations Convention on the Rights of Persons with Disabilities (CRPD)

Rationale: The CRPD specifically addresses the rights and inclusion of persons with disabilities globally.

4.
What is the primary goal of inclusive education for children with special needs?
A. Segregate them into special schools
B. Integrate them into regular classrooms with support
C. Teach them separately without peers
D. Limit their education to vocational skills
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B. Integrate them into regular classrooms with support

Rationale: Inclusive education promotes social integration and equal learning opportunities alongside peers.

5.
Which of the following is an example of a physical disability?
A. Autism spectrum disorder
B. Cerebral palsy
C. Dyslexia
D. Anxiety disorder
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B. Cerebral palsy

Rationale: Cerebral palsy affects muscle coordination and movement, a physical disability, whereas autism, dyslexia, and anxiety are neurodevelopmental or psychological.

6.
Which professional primarily assesses the learning needs of children with special needs?
A. Pediatrician
B. Occupational therapist
C. Educational psychologist
D. Nutritionist
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: C. Educational psychologist

Rationale: Educational psychologists evaluate cognitive and learning difficulties to tailor education plans.

7.
What type of support is most appropriate for a visually impaired student?
A. Braille materials and assistive technology
B. Large print textbooks only
C. Audio recordings without any tactile tools
D. Oral exams exclusively
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: A. Braille materials and assistive technology

Rationale: Braille and technology like screen readers help visually impaired students access learning materials effectively.

8.
Which intervention is important to promote independence for individuals with intellectual disabilities?
A. Total supervision at all times
B. Skill-building and adaptive training
C. Isolation from community activities
D. Repetitive medical tests
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B. Skill-building and adaptive training

Rationale: Teaching daily living and social skills empowers independence, unlike constant supervision or isolation.

9.
Which condition is characterized by repetitive behaviors and difficulties in social interaction?
A. Autism spectrum disorder
B. Down syndrome
C. Spina bifida
D. Hearing impairment
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: A. Autism spectrum disorder

Rationale: Autism includes social and behavioral challenges, while other conditions have different core characteristics.

10.
What is the purpose of a care plan for a person with special needs?
A. To document medical history only
B. To coordinate multidisciplinary support and interventions
C. To limit family involvement
D. To standardize treatment without personalization
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B. To coordinate multidisciplinary support and interventions

Rationale: Care plans tailor support across health, education, and social services for holistic care.

11.
Which sensory impairment can be addressed using hearing aids?
A. Visual impairment
B. Hearing impairment
C. Speech impairment
D. Cognitive impairment
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B. Hearing impairment

Rationale: Hearing aids amplify sounds for individuals with hearing loss.

12.
Which of the following is a congenital cause of special needs?
A. Stroke in adulthood
B. Genetic disorders like Down syndrome
C. Car accident injuries
D. Workplace injuries
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B. Genetic disorders like Down syndrome

Rationale: Congenital conditions are present from birth, unlike injuries or adult-onset diseases.

13.
Which term refers to difficulties in reading and writing?
A. Dyspraxia
B. Dyslexia
C. Dyscalculia
D. Aphasia
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B. Dyslexia

Rationale: Dyslexia specifically affects literacy skills.

14.
Which assistive device would benefit a person with mobility impairment?
A. Hearing aid
B. Wheelchair
C. Braille book
D. Communication board
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B. Wheelchair

Rationale: Wheelchairs provide mobility support for those with movement difficulties.

15.
What is a common psychological challenge for people with special needs?
A. Increased resilience only
B. Higher risk of depression and anxiety
C. Immunity to stress
D. Lack of emotional responses
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B. Higher risk of depression and anxiety

Rationale: Social isolation and discrimination can increase mental health issues in this population.

16.
Which service provides training to improve daily living skills in people with disabilities?
A. Physiotherapy
B. Occupational therapy
C. Radiology
D. Dentistry
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B. Occupational therapy

Rationale: Occupational therapists help develop skills for independence in everyday tasks.

17.
What is a major challenge in delivering education to children with special needs in rural areas?
A. Excessive teacher specialization
B. Lack of trained special education teachers
C. Too many assistive devices
D. Overcrowded schools
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B. Lack of trained special education teachers

Rationale: Rural schools often lack educators skilled in special needs education.

18.
Which communication method is often used with non-verbal children with autism?
A. Sign language or picture exchange communication systems (PECS)
B. Whispering only
C. Standard verbal instructions only
D. Ignoring non-verbal cues
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: A. Sign language or picture exchange communication systems (PECS)

Rationale: These methods facilitate communication when speech is limited.

19.
What role do family members play in the care of persons with special needs?
A. Only providing financial support
B. Active involvement in care and decision-making
C. Isolating the person from community
D. Leaving care entirely to professionals
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B. Active involvement in care and decision-making

Rationale: Family participation improves outcomes and emotional support.

20.
Which disorder is characterized by intellectual disability and distinctive facial features?
A. Autism
B. Down syndrome
C. ADHD
D. Dyslexia
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B. Down syndrome

Rationale: Down syndrome includes intellectual disability and characteristic physical traits.

21.
What is the main aim of rehabilitation for people with special needs?
A. To cure all disabilities
B. To maximize functional abilities and quality of life
C. To limit social interaction
D. To keep individuals dependent on caregivers
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B. To maximize functional abilities and quality of life

Rationale: Rehabilitation focuses on enhancing independence, not curing all conditions.

22.
Which is an example of a neurological disorder causing special needs?
A. Cerebral palsy
B. Fracture of the arm
C. Skin infection
D. Allergic rhinitis
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: A. Cerebral palsy

Rationale: Cerebral palsy results from brain injury affecting movement and posture.

23.
Which strategy best supports social inclusion of people with disabilities?
A. Segregation in separate facilities
B. Community awareness and accessibility improvements
C. Restricting public participation
D. Ignoring their presence
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B. Community awareness and accessibility improvements

Rationale: Raising awareness and removing barriers fosters inclusion.

24.
What is the function of a sign language interpreter?
A. Translate spoken language into sign language
B. Speak for the hearing person
C. Write letters for communication
D. Ignore communication barriers
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: A. Translate spoken language into sign language

Rationale: Interpreters facilitate communication between hearing and deaf individuals.

25.
Which approach is important when teaching children with ADHD?
A. Short, structured tasks with breaks
B. Long lectures without breaks
C. Ignoring their behavior
D) Punishing inattentiveness
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: A. Short, structured tasks with breaks

Rationale: This helps maintain attention and reduce hyperactivity.

Section 2: Clinical Care & Independence

25 Marks
26.
What is a common cause of hearing impairment in children?
A. Chronic ear infections
B. Excessive reading
C. Lack of exercise
D. Vitamin overdose
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: A. Chronic ear infections

Rationale: Recurrent infections can damage hearing structures in children.

27.
Which intervention supports communication for people with speech impairments?
A. Speech therapy
B. Physical therapy
C. Surgery only
D. Dietary changes
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: A. Speech therapy

Rationale: Speech therapists help improve articulation and communication skills.

28.
Which of the following best describes intellectual disability?
A. Limitations in intellectual functioning and adaptive behavior
B. Temporary mood changes
C. Physical weakness only
D. Enhanced memory capacity
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: A. Limitations in intellectual functioning and adaptive behavior

Rationale: Intellectual disability affects cognition and everyday functioning.

29.
Which environmental adaptation assists wheelchair users?
A. Narrow doorways
B. Ramps and accessible toilets
C. Stairs without rails
D. High shelves
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B. Ramps and accessible toilets

Rationale: These modifications allow ease of movement and independence.

30.
What is the primary cause of spina bifida?
A. Genetic mutation
B. Neural tube defect during fetal development
C. Infectious diseases
D. Traumatic injury
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B. Neural tube defect during fetal development

Rationale: Spina bifida results from incomplete spinal cord formation in the womb.

A. ADHD

C. Down syndrome
D. Dyslexia

Correct Answer: A. ADHD

Rationale: ADHD involves these specific behavioral symptoms.

D. Only treating physical symptoms

Correct Answer: B. Prompt assessment and therapy

Rationale: Early intervention improves long-term outcomes.
