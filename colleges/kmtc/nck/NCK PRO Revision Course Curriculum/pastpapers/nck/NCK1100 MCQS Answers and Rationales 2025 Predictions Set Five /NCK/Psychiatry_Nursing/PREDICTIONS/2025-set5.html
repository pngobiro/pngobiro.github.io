Mental Health and Psychiatric Nursing Revision - Paper 2

Instructions

  • This paper consists of 50 Multiple Choice Questions focusing on Mental Health disorders and nursing interventions.
  • Each question has four options; select the most appropriate one.
  • Detailed rationales are provided for each answer to facilitate effective revision.
  • Total Marks: 50 | Time Allowed: 60 Minutes.

Section 1: Mood and Psychotic Disorders

25 Marks
1.
Which of the following is the most common symptom of major depressive disorder?
A) Hallucinations
B) Persistent sadness
C) Grandiosity
D) Hyperactivity
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B) Persistent sadness

Rationale: Major depressive disorder is characterized primarily by persistent sadness or depressed mood, lasting for at least two weeks. Hallucinations and grandiosity are more typical in psychotic or bipolar disorders, while hyperactivity is seen in mania or ADHD.

2.
Which neurotransmitter is most commonly associated with anxiety disorders?
A) Dopamine
B) Serotonin
C) Acetylcholine
D) Norepinephrine
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B) Serotonin

Rationale: Serotonin plays a crucial role in mood regulation and anxiety. Many anxiolytic medications target serotonin pathways. Dopamine is more linked to reward and psychosis; acetylcholine primarily affects cognition, and norepinephrine relates to stress response.

3.
Which of the following is a first-line treatment for schizophrenia?
A) SSRIs
B) Antipsychotics
C) Benzodiazepines
D) Mood stabilizers
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B) Antipsychotics

Rationale: Antipsychotic medications are the primary treatment for schizophrenia to manage psychotic symptoms. SSRIs are for depression and anxiety, benzodiazepines for short-term anxiety relief, and mood stabilizers are used in bipolar disorder.

4.
A patient with bipolar disorder in a manic phase is most likely to exhibit which behavior?
A) Lethargy
B) Rapid speech
C) Social withdrawal
D) Memory loss
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B) Rapid speech

Rationale: During mania, patients often show pressured or rapid speech, increased energy, and decreased need for sleep. Lethargy and social withdrawal are symptoms of depression; memory loss is not typical in mania.

5.
What is the primary purpose of electroconvulsive therapy (ECT) in psychiatric treatment?
A) To reduce anxiety
B) To treat medication-resistant depression
C) To manage hallucinations
D) To improve cognition
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B) To treat medication-resistant depression

Rationale: ECT is mainly used for severe depression that does not respond to medications. It can also be effective for certain psychotic disorders but is not a first-line treatment for anxiety or cognitive enhancement.

6.
Which personality disorder is characterized by disregard for others’ rights and lack of remorse?
A) Borderline
B) Narcissistic
C) Antisocial
D) Histrionic
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: C) Antisocial

Rationale: Antisocial personality disorder involves repeated violations of others’ rights, deceitfulness, impulsivity, and lack of remorse. Borderline involves emotional instability; narcissistic is characterized by grandiosity; histrionic involves excessive emotionality and attention-seeking.

7.
Which of the following is a positive symptom of schizophrenia?
A) Flat affect
B) Avolition
C) Hallucinations
D) Social withdrawal
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: C) Hallucinations

Rationale: Positive symptoms include hallucinations and delusions, which add abnormal experiences. Flat affect and avolition are negative symptoms, indicating diminished emotional expression and motivation.

8.
A hallmark symptom of post-traumatic stress disorder (PTSD) is:
A) Disorganized speech
B) Intrusive memories
C) Euphoria
D) Delusions
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B) Intrusive memories

Rationale: PTSD involves recurrent, involuntary intrusive memories or flashbacks of the traumatic event. Disorganized speech and delusions are psychotic symptoms; euphoria is related to mania.

9.
Which medication is commonly used as a mood stabilizer in bipolar disorder?
A) Lithium
B) Haloperidol
C) Fluoxetine
D) Diazepam
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: A) Lithium

Rationale: Lithium is the gold standard mood stabilizer for bipolar disorder, helping to control both manic and depressive episodes. Haloperidol is an antipsychotic, fluoxetine an antidepressant, and diazepam a benzodiazepine.

10.
The term “anosognosia” in psychiatric nursing refers to:
A) Memory loss
B) Lack of awareness of illness
C) Hallucinations
D) Emotional numbness
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B) Lack of awareness of illness

Rationale: Anosognosia is a common symptom in schizophrenia and other psychiatric disorders where the patient is unaware or denies having a mental illness, complicating treatment adherence.

11.
What is the most appropriate nursing intervention for a patient experiencing a panic attack?
A) Encourage deep breathing
B) Administer antipsychotics immediately
C) Isolate the patient
D) Reduce fluid intake
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: A) Encourage deep breathing

Rationale: Deep breathing helps reduce hyperventilation and anxiety during a panic attack. Antipsychotics are not first-line treatment for panic attacks, isolation may worsen anxiety, and fluid intake is unrelated.

12.
Which of the following is an example of a cognitive distortion commonly seen in depression?
A) Magical thinking
B) Overgeneralization
C) Thought broadcasting
D) Echolalia
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B) Overgeneralization

Rationale: Overgeneralization is a cognitive distortion where a person applies one negative event to all situations, typical in depression. Magical thinking is seen in psychosis; thought broadcasting and echolalia are symptoms of schizophrenia.

13.
The main goal of milieu therapy in psychiatric nursing is to:
A) Provide medication education
B) Create a therapeutic environment
C) Conduct physical examinations
D) Offer family counseling
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B) Create a therapeutic environment

Rationale: Milieu therapy uses the therapeutic community environment to support patient recovery by promoting safety, structure, and social interaction. Medication education and family counseling are important but not the main focus of milieu therapy.

14.
Which symptom is most characteristic of borderline personality disorder?
A) Grandiosity
B) Unstable relationships
C) Social isolation
D) Ritualistic behaviors
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B) Unstable relationships

Rationale: Borderline personality disorder involves intense and unstable interpersonal relationships, fear of abandonment, and emotional dysregulation. Grandiosity is linked to narcissistic personality disorder; social isolation to schizoid personality disorder; ritualistic behaviors to OCD.

15.
A patient with schizophrenia reports hearing voices commanding them to harm others. This is an example of:
A) Visual hallucination
B) Command auditory hallucination
C) Delusion of grandeur
D) Thought insertion
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B) Command auditory hallucination

Rationale: Command hallucinations are auditory hallucinations where voices instruct the person to perform specific acts, often dangerous. Visual hallucinations involve seeing things; delusions of grandeur are false beliefs of importance; thought insertion is a psychotic symptom.

16.
Which of the following medications is classified as a selective serotonin reuptake inhibitor (SSRI)?
A) Amitriptyline
B) Fluoxetine
C) Haloperidol
D) Lithium
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B) Fluoxetine

Rationale: Fluoxetine is an SSRI used primarily to treat depression and anxiety disorders. Amitriptyline is a tricyclic antidepressant, haloperidol is an antipsychotic, and lithium is a mood stabilizer.

17.
The term “catatonia” refers to:
A) Excessive talking
B) Immobility and stupor
C) Rapid mood swings
D) Memory impairment
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B) Immobility and stupor

Rationale: Catatonia is a syndrome characterized by motor immobility, stupor, or excessive motor activity, often seen in schizophrenia and mood disorders. Excessive talking relates to mania, rapid mood swings to bipolar disorder, and memory impairment to dementia.

18.
Which nursing diagnosis is most appropriate for a patient with schizophrenia who has poor hygiene and social withdrawal?
A) Risk for injury
B) Impaired social interaction
C) Anxiety
D) Ineffective breathing pattern
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B) Impaired social interaction

Rationale: Poor hygiene and social withdrawal indicate impaired social interaction, a common nursing diagnosis in schizophrenia. Risk for injury and anxiety are possible but less specific here; ineffective breathing pattern is unrelated.

19.
Which symptom is NOT part of the diagnostic criteria for generalized anxiety disorder (GAD)?
A) Restlessness
B) Excessive worry
C) Hallucinations
D) Muscle tension
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: C) Hallucinations

Rationale: Hallucinations are psychotic symptoms and not part of GAD. Restlessness, excessive worry, and muscle tension are core symptoms of GAD.

20.
Which type of therapy is considered most effective for treating phobias?
A) Cognitive-behavioral therapy (CBT)
B) Psychoanalysis
C) Electroconvulsive therapy
D) Pharmacotherapy only
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: A) Cognitive-behavioral therapy (CBT)

Rationale: CBT, especially exposure therapy, is the most effective treatment for phobias by helping patients confront and manage fears. Psychoanalysis is less used for phobias, ECT is for severe depression, and pharmacotherapy alone is usually insufficient.

21.
What is a common side effect of antipsychotic medications?
A) Tachycardia
B) Extrapyramidal symptoms
C) Hyperactivity
D) Weight loss
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B) Extrapyramidal symptoms

Rationale: Antipsychotics, especially first-generation, often cause extrapyramidal symptoms such as tremors, rigidity, and tardive dyskinesia. Tachycardia and weight changes can occur but are less common; hyperactivity is not typical.

22.
Which defense mechanism involves attributing one’s unacceptable thoughts to others?
A) Projection
B) Regression
C) Sublimation
D) Denial
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: A) Projection

Rationale: Projection is attributing one’s own unacceptable feelings to others. Regression is reverting to an earlier developmental stage; sublimation is channeling impulses into socially acceptable activities; denial is refusing to accept reality.

23.
Which mental disorder is characterized by alternating periods of mania and depression?
A) Schizophrenia
B) Bipolar disorder
C) Major depressive disorder
D) Panic disorder
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B) Bipolar disorder

Rationale: Bipolar disorder features episodes of mania and depression. Schizophrenia is primarily psychosis, major depressive disorder involves only depression, and panic disorder is anxiety-related.

24.
A patient who repeatedly checks the door lock to reduce anxiety is exhibiting a symptom of:
A) Compulsions
B) Delusions
C) Hallucinations
D) Amnesia
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: A) Compulsions

Rationale: Repetitive behaviors performed to reduce anxiety are compulsions, characteristic of obsessive-compulsive disorder (OCD). Delusions and hallucinations are psychotic symptoms; amnesia relates to memory loss.

25.
Which scale is commonly used to assess depression severity?
A) Glasgow Coma Scale
B) Hamilton Depression Rating Scale
C) Mini-Mental State Exam
D) Apgar Score
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B) Hamilton Depression Rating Scale

Rationale: The Hamilton Depression Rating Scale is a standardized tool to measure depression severity. Glasgow Coma Scale assesses consciousness, MMSE assesses cognition, and Apgar Score assesses newborn health.

Section 2: Clinical Interventions & Safety

25 Marks
26.
Which of the following is the most appropriate nursing intervention for a patient experiencing auditory hallucinations?
A) Encourage the patient to isolate themselves
B) Provide reality orientation and distraction techniques
C) Advise the patient to accept the hallucinations
D) Increase sensory stimulation by loud music
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B

Rationale: Reality orientation and distraction help redirect the patient’s attention from hallucinations and reinforce a sense of reality. Encouraging isolation or accepting hallucinations can worsen symptoms. Loud music may increase sensory overload, which is not advisable.

27.
A patient with depression is prescribed an SSRI. What is the nurse’s priority assessment in the first two weeks of treatment?
A) Blood pressure and heart rate
B) Risk of suicidal ideation
C) Appetite and weight changes
D) Skin rash or allergic reactions
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B

Rationale: SSRIs can increase suicidal thoughts, especially during the first two weeks. Close monitoring for suicidality is critical. While vital signs and side effects are important, suicide risk is the immediate safety concern.

28.
What is the main therapeutic goal in the acute phase of schizophrenia?
A) Prevent relapse
B) Manage positive symptoms
C) Support medication adherence
D) Improve social skills
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B

Rationale: The acute phase focuses on managing positive symptoms like hallucinations and delusions to stabilize the patient. Preventing relapse and improving social skills are goals for later phases.

29.
Which defense mechanism is characterized by attributing one’s unacceptable thoughts to others?
A) Projection
B) Rationalization
C) Regression
D) Displacement
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: A

Rationale: Projection involves attributing one’s own unacceptable thoughts or feelings to others. Rationalization justifies behavior, regression is reverting to earlier behavior, and displacement shifts emotions to a safer target.

30.
A patient with bipolar disorder in a manic episode shows hyperactivity and pressured speech. The nurse should:
A) Allow uninterrupted conversation to build trust
B) Redirect the patient to a quieter activity
C) Encourage the patient to express all feelings verbally
D) Avoid interaction to prevent overstimulation
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B

Rationale: Redirecting to quieter activities helps reduce stimulation and control manic behavior. Allowing nonstop talk or encouraging excessive verbalization can escalate symptoms. Avoiding interaction may increase isolation.

31.
Which symptom is a negative symptom of schizophrenia?
A) Hallucinations
B) Delusions
C) Flat affect
D) Disorganized speech
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: C

Rationale: Flat affect (reduced emotional expression) is a negative symptom, reflecting loss or diminution of normal functions. Hallucinations and delusions are positive symptoms. Disorganized speech is also a positive symptom.

32.
What is the most important nursing consideration for a patient on lithium therapy?
A) Monitoring blood glucose
B) Ensuring adequate hydration
C) Restricting salt intake
D) Encouraging high protein diet
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B

Rationale: Adequate hydration is essential to prevent lithium toxicity, as lithium excretion depends on kidney function and fluid balance. Blood glucose is unrelated. Salt intake should be consistent, not restricted. High protein diet is not specifically required.

33.
A patient with PTSD frequently relives traumatic events. The nurse should:
A) Encourage avoidance of triggers
B) Assist with grounding techniques
C) Suggest suppression of memories
D) Minimize patient’s feelings
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B

Rationale: Grounding techniques help the patient stay connected to the present and reduce distress from flashbacks. Avoidance can worsen symptoms, suppression is unhealthy, and minimizing feelings can damage trust.

34.
Which medication is commonly used to manage acute anxiety?
A) Haloperidol
B) Diazepam
C) Fluoxetine
D) Lithium carbonate
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B

Rationale: Diazepam, a benzodiazepine, is used for short-term management of acute anxiety due to its rapid calming effects. Haloperidol is an antipsychotic, fluoxetine is an SSRI for depression/anxiety long-term, and lithium is for mood stabilization.

35.
Which is a common side effect of antipsychotic medication?
A) Hypotension
B) Extrapyramidal symptoms
C) Hypoglycemia
D) Bradycardia
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B

Rationale: Extrapyramidal symptoms (EPS) like tremors, rigidity, and tardive dyskinesia are common side effects of many antipsychotics. Hypotension can occur but less common. Hypoglycemia and bradycardia are not typical side effects.

36.
When a patient shows suicidal ideation, the nurse’s first action should be:
A) Inform the family
B) Ensure patient safety through observation
C) Offer counseling immediately
D) Administer sedative medication
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B

Rationale: Ensuring safety by close observation or precautions is the priority to prevent self-harm. Informing family and counseling are important but secondary. Sedation should not be automatic without assessment.

37.
What is the purpose of cognitive-behavioral therapy (CBT) in mental health?
A) To change unconscious motives
B) To modify dysfunctional thoughts and behaviors
C) To provide medication adherence support
D) To analyze childhood trauma exclusively
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B

Rationale: CBT focuses on identifying and modifying dysfunctional thoughts and behaviors. It is structured and goal-oriented. Changing unconscious motives is more psychodynamic therapy. Medication support is separate, and childhood trauma analysis is broader.

38.
Which condition is characterized by recurrent panic attacks and fear of future attacks?
A) Generalized anxiety disorder
B) Panic disorder
C) Obsessive-compulsive disorder
D) Post-traumatic stress disorder
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B

Rationale: Panic disorder involves recurrent, unexpected panic attacks and persistent concern about having more attacks. GAD is chronic anxiety without discrete attacks. OCD involves compulsions, and PTSD follows trauma.

39.
A patient with anorexia nervosa has a BMI of 16. Which nursing diagnosis is priority?
A) Risk for imbalanced nutrition
B) Ineffective coping
C) Social isolation
D) Disturbed sleep pattern
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: A

Rationale: Severe malnutrition due to low BMI requires immediate attention. Other diagnoses are important but secondary to life-threatening nutritional deficiency.

40.
What is the hallmark of mania in bipolar disorder?
A) Depressed mood
B) Euphoria and grandiosity
C) Social withdrawal
D) Auditory hallucinations
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B

Rationale: Mania typically involves elevated mood, euphoria, and grandiose ideas. Depressed mood is from the depressive phase, social withdrawal is common in depression or schizophrenia, and hallucinations may occur but are not hallmark.

41.
Which neurotransmitter is most involved in depression?
A) Dopamine
B) Serotonin
C) Acetylcholine
D) Glutamate
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B

Rationale: Serotonin is primarily implicated in depression, with low levels linked to mood symptoms. Dopamine is more related to reward and psychosis, acetylcholine to cognition, and glutamate is involved in excitation but less specific.

42.
A patient on clozapine must have regular blood tests due to the risk of:
A) Liver toxicity
B) Agranulocytosis
C) Kidney failure
D) Hyperglycemia
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B

Rationale: Clozapine can cause agranulocytosis (severe drop in white blood cells), requiring frequent blood monitoring. Liver toxicity and kidney failure are less common. Hyperglycemia may occur but is not the main concern.

43.
In a patient with obsessive-compulsive disorder (OCD), the compulsions are:
A) Voluntary behaviors to reduce anxiety
B) Uncontrollable motor tics
C) Hallucinations to relieve stress
D) Episodes of disorganized speech
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: A

Rationale: Compulsions are repetitive, voluntary behaviors aimed at reducing anxiety caused by obsessions. Tics, hallucinations, and disorganized speech belong to other disorders.

44.
Which of the following is a priority nursing action when caring for a patient with delirium?
A) Encourage cognitive stimulation
B) Monitor for changes in consciousness
C) Promote sleep deprivation
D) Limit family visits
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B

Rationale: Monitoring for fluctuations in consciousness and orientation is crucial as delirium can worsen rapidly. Cognitive stimulation is helpful but secondary. Sleep deprivation worsens delirium, and family visits usually help.

45.
What is the main feature of borderline personality disorder?
A) Stable mood and self-image
B) Intense fear of abandonment and unstable relationships
C) Delusions and hallucinations
D) Obsession with order and cleanliness
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B

Rationale: Borderline personality disorder is marked by instability in relationships, self-image, and intense fear of abandonment. Delusions and hallucinations suggest psychosis, and obsession with order is typical of obsessive-compulsive personality disorder.

46.
Which nursing intervention is best for a patient with mania who is at risk of injury?
A) Encourage group therapy participation
B) Provide a low-stimulation environment
C) Allow unrestricted activity
D) Limit fluid intake
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B

Rationale: A low-stimulation environment helps reduce risk of injury due to hyperactivity. Group therapy may be overwhelming. Unrestricted activity increases injury risk, and limiting fluids is not indicated.

47.
Which symptom is a positive symptom of schizophrenia?
A) Social withdrawal
B) Delusions
C) Anhedonia
D) Flat affect
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B

Rationale: Delusions are positive symptoms involving added abnormal experiences. Social withdrawal, anhedonia, and flat affect are negative symptoms.

48.
What is a common side effect of tricyclic antidepressants?
A) Diarrhea
B) Dry mouth
C) Insomnia
D) Weight loss
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B

Rationale: Dry mouth is a common anticholinergic side effect of tricyclic antidepressants. Diarrhea is uncommon, insomnia may occur but less so, and weight loss is rare.

49.
Which statement best describes mental health recovery?
A) Cure of all symptoms
B) Living a fulfilling life despite illness
C) Avoidance of all stressors
D) Medication adherence only
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B

Rationale: Recovery emphasizes living a meaningful life despite ongoing symptoms. Cure is often unrealistic. Avoidance and medication are components but not the full picture.

50.
When caring for a patient with alcohol withdrawal, the nurse should first assess for:
A) Seizure activity
B) Liver enzymes
C) Nutritional status
D) Skin integrity
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: A

Rationale: Alcohol withdrawal can precipitate seizures, which are life-threatening and require immediate attention. Liver function, nutrition, and skin are important but secondary in acute withdrawal.
