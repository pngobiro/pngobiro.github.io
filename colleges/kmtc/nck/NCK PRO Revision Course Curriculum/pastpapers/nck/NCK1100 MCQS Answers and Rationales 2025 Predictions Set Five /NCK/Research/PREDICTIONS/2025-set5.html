Research Methodology

NCK, KRCHN, PAPER 2 - 1100 MCQS Answers and Rationales

Instructions

  • This paper consists of 50 Multiple Choice Questions focusing on Nursing Research and Statistics.
  • Each question has four options; select the most appropriate one.
  • Detailed rationales are provided for each answer to facilitate effective revision.
  • Total Marks: 50 | Time Allowed: 60 Minutes.

Section 1: Research Design & Ethics

25 Marks
1.
What is the primary purpose of a literature review in research?
A) To collect data from participants
B) To summarize existing knowledge on a topic
C) To develop the research hypothesis
D) To present research findings
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B

Rationale: A literature review synthesizes and summarizes previous studies and knowledge relevant to the research topic, helping to establish a foundation and justify the need for the current study.

2.
Which research design is best suited for establishing cause-and-effect relationships?
A) Cross-sectional
B) Case study
C) Experimental
D) Descriptive
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: C

Rationale: Experimental designs allow for manipulation of variables and control of confounding factors, making it possible to infer causality between independent and dependent variables.

3.
In research, what does validity refer to?
A) Consistency of results over time
B) Accuracy in measuring what the study intends to measure
C) Number of participants in a study
D) Ethical approval of the study
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B

Rationale: Validity indicates how well a test or instrument measures what it is supposed to measure, ensuring the credibility of study findings.

4.
Which sampling method is considered non-probability sampling?
A) Simple random sampling
B) Systematic sampling
C) Convenience sampling
D) Stratified sampling
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: C

Rationale: Convenience sampling involves selecting participants based on ease of access rather than random selection, and it does not guarantee representativeness.

5.
What is the purpose of the hypothesis in quantitative research?
A) To describe the research methods
B) To propose a testable prediction
C) To analyze data
D) To collect qualitative data
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B

Rationale: A hypothesis is a clear, testable statement predicting the relationship between variables that the research will investigate.

6.
Which of the following best describes qualitative research?
A) Numerical data analysis
B) Exploration of meanings and experiences
C) Use of control groups
D) Statistical testing of hypotheses
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B

Rationale: Qualitative research focuses on understanding human experiences, meanings, and perspectives, often through interviews or observations.

7.
In research ethics, informed consent means:
A) Participants must agree without knowing study details
B) Researchers can use data without permission
C) Participants are fully informed about the study and voluntarily agree
D) Consent is unnecessary for observational studies
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: C

Rationale: Informed consent ensures participants understand the purpose, risks, benefits, and their rights before voluntarily participating.

8.
Which of the following is an example of a dependent variable?
A) Age of participants
B) Intervention type
C) Blood pressure reading after treatment
D) Gender of participants
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: C

Rationale: The dependent variable is the outcome or response measured in the study, such as blood pressure after an intervention.

9.
What does reliability in research imply?
A) The results are generalizable to the population
B) The study measures what it is supposed to measure
C) The study results are consistent and repeatable
D) The study adheres to ethical guidelines
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: C

Rationale: Reliability refers to the consistency and stability of measurements or results over time or across different observers.

10.
A double-blind study means:
A) Only participants know their group assignment
B) Both participants and researchers do not know group assignments
C) Neither group receives treatment
D) Data analysis is blinded to the hypothesis
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B

Rationale: Double-blind studies prevent both researchers and participants from knowing who is in the control or experimental group, reducing bias.

11.
What is the main purpose of a pilot study?
A) To publish final results
B) To test research instruments and procedures before the main study
C) To recruit participants
D) To analyze data
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B

Rationale: A pilot study is a small-scale trial run to test the feasibility, time, cost, and procedures for the main research.

12.
Which of the following is a characteristic of descriptive research?
A) Manipulation of variables
B) Identification of cause and effect
C) Description of phenomena without intervention
D) Random assignment
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: C

Rationale: Descriptive research aims to accurately describe characteristics or phenomena without manipulating variables.

13.
What is the primary goal of inferential statistics in research?
A) To organize data into charts
B) To summarize data trends
C) To make conclusions about populations based on sample data
D) To collect data from participants
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: C

Rationale: Inferential statistics help researchers draw conclusions and make predictions about a population using sample data.

14.
Which of the following data collection methods is commonly used in qualitative research?
A) Structured questionnaire
B) Focus group discussion
C) Blood pressure monitoring
D) Laboratory tests
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B

Rationale: Focus groups facilitate detailed discussion and exploration of participants' views, suitable for qualitative research.

15.
What does a p-value less than 0.05 typically indicate?
A) The results are not statistically significant
B) The null hypothesis should be rejected
C) The sample size is too small
D) The research is qualitative
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B

Rationale: A p-value < 0.05 suggests that there is less than a 5% probability that the observed results occurred by chance, indicating statistical significance.

16.
What is the main difference between primary and secondary data?
A) Primary data is collected firsthand; secondary data is collected by others
B) Secondary data is always qualitative
C) Primary data is cheaper to collect
D) Secondary data is more reliable
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: A

Rationale: Primary data refers to data collected directly by the researcher for a specific purpose, whereas secondary data was collected by others for different purposes.

17.
What type of research approach is best for exploring new phenomena where little is known?
A) Quantitative
B) Qualitative
C) Experimental
D) Longitudinal
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B

Rationale: Qualitative research is ideal for exploring new areas with limited prior knowledge because it seeks to understand meaning and context.

18.
What is a key advantage of randomized controlled trials (RCTs)?
A) They are inexpensive
B) They minimize selection bias
C) They are easy to conduct
D) They do not require control groups
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B

Rationale: Randomization helps distribute confounding factors evenly, reducing bias and strengthening causal inference.

19.
Which of the following is a limitation of cross-sectional studies?
A) They are time-consuming
B) They cannot establish causality
C) They require large sample sizes
D) They involve multiple follow-ups
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B

Rationale: Cross-sectional studies provide a snapshot in time, so they cannot establish temporal relationships needed to infer causality.

20.
What is the function of a control group in experimental research?
A) To receive the intervention
B) To serve as a comparison without the intervention
C) To collect data only
D) To design the study
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B

Rationale: Control groups do not receive the experimental treatment and provide a baseline to compare effects of the intervention.

21.
What is the meaning of the term "operational definition" in research?
A) The statistical method used for analysis
B) A clear, precise definition of a variable for measurement
C) The background of the study
D) The ethical guidelines for the study
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B

Rationale: Operational definitions specify exactly how variables are measured or manipulated to ensure clarity and replicability.

22.
Which of the following best describes longitudinal research?
A) Data collected at one point in time
B) Data collected from different groups simultaneously
C) Data collected from the same subjects over time
D) Experimental manipulation of variables
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: C

Rationale: Longitudinal studies follow the same participants over extended periods to observe changes and developments.

23.
What is a major ethical concern in research involving human subjects?
A) Randomization
B) Confidentiality and privacy
C) Data analysis techniques
D) Literature review
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B

Rationale: Protecting participant confidentiality and privacy is crucial to maintain trust and adhere to ethical standards.

24.
Which type of validity refers to the extent to which research findings can be generalized?
A) Internal validity
B) Content validity
C) External validity
D) Construct validity
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: C

Rationale: External validity assesses whether the results of a study can be applied to other populations or settings beyond the sample studied.

25.
Which of the following is an example of qualitative data?
A) Number of participants in a study
B) Participants' descriptions of their experiences
C) Blood glucose levels
D) Age in years
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B

Rationale: Qualitative data consists of non-numerical information such as descriptions, narratives, and perceptions.

Section 2: Data Collection & Analysis

25 Marks
26.
What is the main advantage of using a questionnaire in research?
A) Provides in-depth data
B) Allows for data collection from a large number of participants efficiently
C) Ensures accurate responses
D) Eliminates all bias
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B

Rationale: Questionnaires can be distributed widely and are efficient for collecting data from many participants.

27.
What is the role of a research hypothesis?
A) To analyze data
B) To state a tentative explanation or prediction that can be tested
C) To select participants
D) To review literature
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B

Rationale: Hypotheses provide direction and focus for research by proposing specific relationships to be tested.

28.
Which statistical test would be appropriate to compare means between two independent groups?
A) Chi-square test
B) Independent t-test
C) Pearson correlation
D) ANOVA
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B

Rationale: An independent t-test compares the means of two separate groups to determine if there is a statistically significant difference.

29.
What does the term "sampling bias" mean?
A) Equal chance of selection for all members of the population
B) Systematic error due to non-random sampling leading to unrepresentative samples
C) The sample size is too large
D) Participants refusing to join the study
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B

Rationale: Sampling bias occurs when certain members of the population are systematically excluded or overrepresented, affecting representativeness.

30.
Which research method is best for studying rare diseases?
A) Case-control study
B) Cross-sectional study
C) Randomized controlled trial
D) Cohort study
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: A

Rationale: Case-control studies are efficient for studying rare conditions by comparing cases with matched controls retrospectively.

31.
In research, what does triangulation refer to?
A) Using multiple researchers to collect data
B) Using multiple methods or data sources to enhance credibility
C) Three groups of participants in the study
D) The three phases of research
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B

Rationale: Triangulation involves combining different methods or data sources to cross-verify findings and increase study validity.

32.
Which of the following best defines a confounding variable?
A) A variable that is deliberately manipulated
B) A variable that may influence both independent and dependent variables, causing bias
C) The main outcome variable
D) A variable that remains constant
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B

Rationale: Confounders distort the true relationship between variables and must be controlled to ensure valid results.

33.
What does ethical clearance from an Institutional Review Board (IRB) ensure?
A) Funding for the research
B) Approval that the research meets ethical standards to protect participants
C) Recruitment of participants
D) Data analysis accuracy
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B

Rationale: IRB review ensures that the research respects participants' rights, safety, and welfare before the study begins.

34.
Which of the following is a key characteristic of a qualitative interview?
A) Closed-ended questions only
B) Structured format
C) Flexibility to explore participant perspectives in-depth
D) Use of numerical scales
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: C

Rationale: Qualitative interviews are often semi-structured or unstructured, allowing participants to express their experiences in their own words.

35.
What is meant by the term "sample size"?
A) The number of variables in a study
B) The number of participants included in a study
C) The duration of the study
D) The number of researchers involved
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B

Rationale: Sample size refers to the total number of individuals or units selected for participation in a study.

36.
Which research component involves breaking down data into themes and patterns?
A) Data collection
B) Data coding and analysis
C) Literature review
D) Hypothesis formulation
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B

Rationale: Data coding organizes qualitative data into themes and patterns for meaningful interpretation.

37.
In research, what does the term "generalizability" imply?
A) Results apply only to the sample studied
B) Results can be applied to broader populations or contexts
C) Results are only descriptive
D) Results are biased
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B

Rationale: Generalizability is the extent to which findings from a study can be applied to other settings, groups, or populations.

38.
What type of variable is gender in most research studies?
A) Continuous
B) Categorical (nominal)
C) Ordinal
D) Dependent
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B

Rationale: Gender is typically treated as a categorical variable with categories like male, female, or other.

39.
Which of the following is an example of a qualitative research method?
A) Survey questionnaire
B) Ethnography
C) Randomized controlled trial
D) Cohort study
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B

Rationale: Ethnography involves in-depth study of people and cultures, characteristic of qualitative research.

40.
In data analysis, what is a frequency distribution?
A) The spread of data points across different categories or values
B) Testing hypothesis validity
C) Collecting raw data
D) Ethical clearance procedure
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: A

Rationale: Frequency distribution shows how often each value or category occurs in a dataset, useful in summarizing data.

41.
What does confidentiality mean in research?
A) Publishing participant names
B) Keeping participant information private and secure
C) Sharing data with unauthorized individuals
D) Deleting all data after study completion
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B

Rationale: Confidentiality protects participant privacy by ensuring personal data is not disclosed without permission.

42.
Which is an example of a quantitative data collection method?
A) Participant observation
B) Structured survey
C) Narrative interview
D) Focus group
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B

Rationale: Structured surveys collect numerical data suitable for statistical analysis, characteristic of quantitative research.

43.
What does the term bias refer to in research?
A) The use of control groups
B) Systematic errors that affect the validity of study results
C) Random selection of participants
D) Ethical approval
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B

Rationale: Bias refers to any systematic deviation from the truth in data collection, analysis, or interpretation that can distort results.

44.
Which type of research is best suited for testing the effectiveness of a new drug?
A) Qualitative
B) Experimental
C) Descriptive
D) Observational
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B

Rationale: Experimental research, such as clinical trials, is designed to test the efficacy and safety of new drugs under controlled conditions.

45.
What is the role of a research proposal?
A) To collect data
B) To outline the plan and justification for conducting the research
C) To analyze findings
D) To publish results
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B

Rationale: A research proposal describes the objectives, methodology, significance, and feasibility of a study before it begins.

46.
What does the term sampling frame mean?
A) The method of data analysis
B) The actual list or database from which a sample is drawn
C) The research hypothesis
D) The time period of the study
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B

Rationale: The sampling frame is the accessible list of all members of the population from which the sample is selected.

47.
Which of the following is a non-parametric statistical test?
A) Pearson correlation
B) Mann-Whitney U test
C) ANOVA
D) Independent t-test
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B

Rationale: The Mann-Whitney U test is a non-parametric test used when data do not meet parametric assumptions, often for ordinal data.

48.
What is the significance of a null hypothesis in research?
A) It is the expected outcome of the experiment
B) It states no effect or no relationship exists between variables
C) It describes the research method
D) It identifies the population
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B

Rationale: The null hypothesis assumes no association or difference exists and serves as the default position tested against the alternative hypothesis.

49.
What does peer review in research refer to?
A) The review of research by the researchers friends
B) Evaluation of research by independent experts before publication
C) Reviewing participant data
D) Reviewing literature
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B

Rationale: Peer review is a quality control process where experts critically assess a studys methodology and findings before publication.

50.
Which factor is crucial when selecting a sample size for a study?
A) Researcher preference
B) Statistical power and precision
C) Number of researchers involved
D) Time of data collection
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B

Rationale: Sample size affects the power of the study to detect true effects and the precision of estimates; it should be calculated based on these considerations.
