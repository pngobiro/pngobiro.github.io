Community Diagnosis

NCK, KRCHN, PAPER 2 - 1100 MCQS Answers and Rationales

Instructions

  • This paper consists of 50 Multiple Choice Questions focusing on Community Diagnosis.
  • Each question has four options; select the most appropriate one.
  • Detailed rationales are provided for each answer to facilitate effective revision.
  • Total Marks: 50 | Time Allowed: 60 Minutes.

Section 1: Community Assessment

25 Marks
1.
What is the primary purpose of conducting a community diagnosis?
A. To identify individual health problems only
B. To assess community health needs and resources
C. To evaluate hospital services
D. To conduct clinical trials
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B. To assess community health needs and resources

Rationale: Community diagnosis focuses on identifying health problems, needs, and resources at the community level to guide planning and interventions. It is broader than individual assessments or hospital evaluations and is not related to clinical trials.

2.
Which of the following is an essential component of community diagnosis?
A. Laboratory analysis of blood samples
B. Collection of community data and health indicators
C. Performing surgical procedures
D. Administering medication
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B. Collection of community data and health indicators

Rationale: Community diagnosis relies on data collection regarding demographics, morbidity, mortality, and social determinants. Laboratory tests or clinical interventions are not part of the diagnosis process but may follow from its findings.

3.
Which method is commonly used to gather qualitative data in community diagnosis?
A. Structured questionnaires
B. Focus group discussions
C. Blood pressure measurement
D. X-ray imaging
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B. Focus group discussions

Rationale: Focus group discussions allow for in-depth understanding of community perceptions, beliefs, and needs. Structured questionnaires collect quantitative data, while clinical tests like BP measurement or X-rays are irrelevant to community-level qualitative data collection.

4.
What type of data is usually collected through a community survey?
A. Subjective opinions only
B. Quantitative and qualitative data
C. Clinical laboratory data
D. Individual medical records
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B. Quantitative and qualitative data

Rationale: Community surveys typically collect both quantitative data (e.g., number of cases) and qualitative data (e.g., community attitudes). Laboratory data and individual medical records are not usually the focus of community surveys.

5.
Which of the following is an example of a secondary data source for community diagnosis?
A. Community interviews
B. Hospital records
C. Focus group discussions
D. Observations
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B. Hospital records

Rationale: Secondary data come from existing sources such as hospital records, census reports, or health registries. Primary data are collected directly through interviews, focus groups, or observations.

6.
What does the term “health indicator” refer to in community diagnosis?
A. A person’s medical history
B. A measurable factor reflecting the health status of a community
C. A health professional’s opinion
D. A health facility’s location
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B. A measurable factor reflecting the health status of a community

Rationale: Health indicators are statistics like morbidity rates, mortality rates, or immunization coverage that provide measurable insights into the community’s health.

7.
Which is the first step in the community diagnosis process?
A. Planning interventions
B. Identifying community boundaries
C. Data analysis
D. Implementing health programs
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B. Identifying community boundaries

Rationale: Before collecting data, it is important to define the community geographically and socially to focus assessment efforts accurately. Planning and implementation come after data collection and analysis.

8.
Which of the following best describes “morbidity rate” used in community diagnosis?
A. The number of deaths per 1,000 people
B. The rate of new cases of a disease in a population over a specific time
C. The total number of healthcare facilities
D. The average age of the population
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B. The rate of new cases of a disease in a population over a specific time

Rationale: Morbidity rate refers to incidence (new cases) or prevalence (existing cases) of disease, providing insight into disease burden. Deaths relate to mortality, not morbidity.

9.
Which group is most important to involve during the community diagnosis?
A. Hospital staff only
B. Community members and local leaders
C. Pharmaceutical companies
D. Foreign tourists
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B. Community members and local leaders

Rationale: Engaging community members and leaders ensures accurate information, community ownership, and successful intervention planning. Hospital staff and outsiders have limited roles in the diagnosis process.

10.
Which of the following tools is best for mapping health resources in a community?
A. GIS (Geographic Information Systems)
B. Blood glucose meters
C. Ultrasound machines
D. Clinical record forms
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: A. GIS (Geographic Information Systems)

Rationale: GIS helps visualize spatial distribution of health facilities, resources, and population, assisting in identifying gaps. Medical devices and forms are unrelated to resource mapping.

11.
What is the significance of social determinants in community diagnosis?
A. They have no impact on health
B. They influence community health outcomes and risk factors
C. They only relate to economic data
D. They are irrelevant to public health
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B. They influence community health outcomes and risk factors

Rationale: Social determinants like education, income, and environment significantly affect health behaviors and outcomes, making them critical in diagnosis.

12.
In community diagnosis, “prevalence” refers to:
A. The number of new cases of a disease
B. The total number of existing cases of a disease at a point in time
C. Deaths caused by a disease
D. Number of vaccinated individuals
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B. The total number of existing cases of a disease at a point in time

Rationale: Prevalence measures the proportion of individuals with a disease in a population at a specific time, differing from incidence which counts new cases.

13.
Which is a limitation of community diagnosis?
A. Provides comprehensive health data
B. Requires extensive resources and time
C. Identifies all diseases perfectly
D. Does not involve community participation
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B. Requires extensive resources and time

Rationale: Community diagnosis can be resource-intensive and time-consuming. It may also miss some data but involves community participation and provides valuable insight.

14.
How can bias be minimized during community data collection?
A. Only collecting data from health workers
B. Using multiple data collection methods
C. Ignoring community input
D. Relying solely on secondary data
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B. Using multiple data collection methods

Rationale: Triangulating data from different sources and methods reduces bias, improving validity and reliability.

15.
What role does a community health worker play in community diagnosis?
A. Perform surgeries
B. Facilitate data collection and community engagement
C. Manage hospital finances
D. Manufacture medical equipment
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B. Facilitate data collection and community engagement

Rationale: Community health workers bridge between health systems and the community, helping gather data and mobilize local participation.

16.
Which of the following best describes a “risk factor” identified in community diagnosis?
A. A healthcare facility
B. A characteristic that increases likelihood of disease
C. A clinical symptom
D. A vaccination program
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B. A characteristic that increases likelihood of disease

Rationale: Risk factors, such as smoking or poor sanitation, predispose individuals or communities to health problems.

17.
When is it appropriate to use participatory rural appraisal (PRA) in community diagnosis?
A. To impose external solutions
B. To engage the community in identifying problems and solutions
C. To replace surveys entirely
D. To conduct laboratory tests
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B. To engage the community in identifying problems and solutions

Rationale: PRA encourages community involvement in problem identification, promoting ownership and relevance of interventions.

18.
Which of the following is NOT a direct outcome of community diagnosis?
A. Identification of health priorities
B. Development of intervention strategies
C. Immediate cure of diseases
D. Enhanced understanding of community resources
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: C. Immediate cure of diseases

Rationale: Community diagnosis guides planning and prioritization but does not directly treat or cure diseases.

19.
How often should community diagnosis ideally be conducted?
A. Once every 50 years
B. Periodically, depending on changes in community conditions
C. Only once in a community’s lifetime
D. Every day
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B. Periodically, depending on changes in community conditions

Rationale: Repeated assessments allow adaptation to new health challenges and changing social dynamics.

20.
Which of the following would best improve accuracy in community diagnosis data?
A. Ignoring non-response
B. Training data collectors thoroughly
C. Collecting data only during holidays
D. Avoiding the use of technology
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B. Training data collectors thoroughly

Rationale: Skilled data collectors reduce errors, ensure consistency, and build rapport with respondents for better data quality.

21.
Which of the following best defines “community health profile”?
A. Individual medical records summary
B. A summary of health status, resources, and needs of a community
C. A list of diseases in a hospital
D. Financial report of health expenditures
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B. A summary of health status, resources, and needs of a community

Rationale: A community health profile synthesizes data to provide an overview of community health for planning.

22.
Which of the following is a qualitative data collection method used in community diagnosis?
A. Blood sugar testing
B. Key informant interviews
C. Population census
D. Mortality statistics
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B. Key informant interviews

Rationale: Key informant interviews provide detailed, contextual information, complementing quantitative data like census and statistics.

23.
What is the role of surveillance data in community diagnosis?
A. To provide ongoing information about health events
B. To provide treatment for patients
C. To replace surveys
D. To train healthcare workers
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: A. To provide ongoing information about health events

Rationale: Surveillance tracks disease trends and outbreaks, essential for timely community health assessment.

24.
Which of the following best represents a community diagnosis report?
A. Detailed clinical charts of patients
B. A document summarizing community health issues and priorities
C. A financial audit of health institutions
D. A pharmaceutical catalog
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B. A document summarizing community health issues and priorities

Rationale: The report synthesizes findings and guides interventions; clinical charts and financial audits are separate documents.

25.
How does community participation benefit community diagnosis?
A. Reduces community ownership
B. Ensures data relevance and sustainability of interventions
C. Delays the process unnecessarily
D. Increases cost without benefits
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B. Ensures data relevance and sustainability of interventions

Rationale: Participation empowers communities, making interventions culturally appropriate and supported.

Section 2: Health Indicators & Implementation

25 Marks
26.
Which health indicator would best measure infant health in community diagnosis?
A. Infant mortality rate
B. Adult literacy rate
C. Number of hospitals
D. Road infrastructure quality
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: A. Infant mortality rate

Rationale: Infant mortality rate directly reflects infant health status, while other indicators relate indirectly or not at all.

27.
Which of the following is NOT considered when defining community boundaries?
A. Geographic limits
B. Social and cultural characteristics
C. Population size
D. Brand of local products
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: D. Brand of local products

Rationale: Community boundaries focus on population and social factors, not commercial product brands.

28.
Which of the following is a potential barrier to accurate community diagnosis?
A. Strong community trust
B. Language differences
C. Use of mixed methods
D. Adequate funding
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B. Language differences

Rationale: Language barriers can hinder communication and data collection accuracy.

29.
What is the best way to verify data collected during community diagnosis?
A. Accept data without checks
B. Triangulation with multiple sources
C. Use only one data collection method
D. Avoid community involvement
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B. Triangulation with multiple sources

Rationale: Triangulation improves validity by comparing data from different methods or sources.

30.
Which of the following best describes the “needs assessment” in community diagnosis?
A. Identifying the services provided by hospitals
B. Determining gaps between current health status and desired conditions
C. Listing health professionals available
D. Cataloging medications used
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B. Determining gaps between current health status and desired conditions

Rationale: Needs assessment identifies priority health problems needing intervention.

31.
Which of the following is a quantitative indicator in community diagnosis?
A. Community attitudes toward vaccination
B. Number of malaria cases reported
C. Community stories about health
D. Perceptions of healthcare quality
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B. Number of malaria cases reported

Rationale: Quantitative data are numeric and measurable; qualitative data include attitudes and perceptions.

32.
Which of the following is the best source of information for demographic data?
A. Community health workers
B. Census reports
C. Focus groups
D. Clinic patient interviews
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B. Census reports

Rationale: Census data provide comprehensive demographic info; health workers and focus groups provide other data types.

33.
How does environmental assessment contribute to community diagnosis?
A. It identifies physical factors affecting health
B. It replaces clinical diagnosis
C. It only focuses on water sources
D. It is irrelevant to community diagnosis
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: A. It identifies physical factors affecting health

Rationale: Environmental assessment looks at sanitation, pollution, housing, which influence health outcomes.

34.
Which of the following is a limitation of using hospital data in community diagnosis?
A. Data are always complete
B. They may not represent the entire community
C. They are always up-to-date
D. They replace need for community surveys
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B. They may not represent the entire community

Rationale: Hospital data reflect only those who seek care, possibly excluding community members who do not visit hospitals.

35.
Which approach helps in prioritizing health problems in community diagnosis?
A. Random selection
B. Use of criteria such as severity, feasibility, and community preference
C. Selecting the most common diseases only
D. Ignoring community input
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B. Use of criteria such as severity, feasibility, and community preference

Rationale: Prioritization considers multiple factors to select problems most important and manageable.

36.
In which stage of community diagnosis are solutions developed?
A. Data collection
B. Data analysis and interpretation
C. Planning and intervention design
D. Data reporting
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: C. Planning and intervention design

Rationale: Solutions arise after understanding the data, during planning.

37.
What is the significance of “benchmarking” in community diagnosis?
A. Setting performance standards by comparing with other communities
B. Measuring height and weight of individuals
C. Lab testing of samples
D. Clinical diagnosis
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: A. Setting performance standards by comparing with other communities

Rationale: Benchmarking helps assess how a community’s health compares to others, guiding improvements.

38.
Which type of community diagnosis involves the community actively in all steps?
A. Participatory community diagnosis
B. Hospital-based diagnosis
C. Laboratory diagnosis
D. Administrative diagnosis
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: A. Participatory community diagnosis

Rationale: This approach ensures community involvement, empowerment, and relevance of findings.

39.
What does the “logic model” help with in community diagnosis?
A. Visualizing resources, activities, outputs, and outcomes
B. Conducting lab tests
C. Diagnosing individual patients
D. Calculating health budgets
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: A. Visualizing resources, activities, outputs, and outcomes

Rationale: Logic models help plan, implement, and evaluate community health programs.

40.
Which is a key ethical consideration during community diagnosis?
A. Ensuring confidentiality of community data
B. Publishing all data publicly without consent
C. Ignoring community concerns
D. Using data only for research purposes
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: A. Ensuring confidentiality of community data

Rationale: Protecting privacy and securing informed consent are ethical imperatives.

41.
How can technology improve community diagnosis?
A. By automating clinical procedures
B. By facilitating data collection, storage, and analysis
C. By replacing community participation
D. By diagnosing diseases instantly
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B. By facilitating data collection, storage, and analysis

Rationale: Technology enhances efficiency and accuracy in handling community health data.

42.
Which factor is least likely to affect community health status?
A. Socioeconomic status
B. Education level
C. Climate conditions
D. Brand preference of mobile phones
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: D. Brand preference of mobile phones

Rationale: Health determinants relate to social, economic, and environmental factors, not consumer preferences.

43.
What is the function of the “health problem ranking” in community diagnosis?
A. To ignore minor health issues
B. To prioritize health problems for intervention
C. To select only infectious diseases
D. To assign blame to health workers
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B. To prioritize health problems for intervention

Rationale: Ranking helps focus resources on the most important health challenges.

44.
Which of the following best explains “community empowerment” in diagnosis?
A. Community members make decisions about their health needs
B. Health professionals make all decisions
C. Community members only provide data
D. External agencies manage community programs
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: A. Community members make decisions about their health needs

Rationale: Empowerment involves active community participation and ownership.

45.
Which is a key output of community diagnosis?
A. Community health action plan
B. Laboratory test results
C. Medical prescriptions
D. Hospital admission forms
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: A. Community health action plan

Rationale: Diagnosis leads to actionable plans, guiding health interventions.

46.
Which of the following is an example of direct observation in community diagnosis?
A. Conducting household surveys
B. Inspecting water sources for contamination
C. Reviewing hospital records
D. Analyzing census data
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B. Inspecting water sources for contamination

Rationale: Direct observation involves firsthand assessment of environmental factors affecting health.

47.
What is the role of epidemiology in community diagnosis?
A. To study disease distribution and determinants in populations
B. To perform individual patient diagnosis
C. To conduct surgical procedures
D. To manufacture vaccines
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: A. To study disease distribution and determinants in populations

Rationale: Epidemiology provides the scientific basis for community health assessments.

48.
Why is it important to include vulnerable populations in community diagnosis?
A. They have no impact on data quality
B. Their health needs might be underrepresented otherwise
C. They should be excluded to simplify analysis
D. They provide unreliable data
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B. Their health needs might be underrepresented otherwise

Rationale: Vulnerable groups may have distinct needs and risks requiring attention.

49.
Which of the following is the best definition of “health needs”?
A. All illnesses in a community
B. Differences between current health status and optimal health
C. Number of hospitals available
D. Population size
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B. Differences between current health status and optimal health

Rationale: Health needs represent gaps requiring intervention to improve health.

50.
Which of the following is a challenge commonly faced in community diagnosis?
A. Overabundance of resources
B. Limited community cooperation
C. Instant availability of all data
D. Absence of any health problems
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B. Limited community cooperation

Rationale: Community mistrust, apathy, or poor engagement often limit effective data collection.
