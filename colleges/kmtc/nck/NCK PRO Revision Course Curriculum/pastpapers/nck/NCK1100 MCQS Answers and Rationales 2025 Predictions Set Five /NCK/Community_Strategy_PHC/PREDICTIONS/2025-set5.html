Community Strategy & PHC

NCK, KRCHN, PAPER 2 - 1100 MCQS Answers and Rationales

Instructions

  • This paper consists of 50 Multiple Choice Questions focusing on Community Strategy and Primary Health Care (PHC).
  • Each question has four options; select the most appropriate one.
  • Detailed rationales are provided for each answer to facilitate effective revision.
  • Total Marks: 50 | Time Allowed: 60 Minutes.

Section 1: Community Strategy Framework

25 Marks
1.
Which level of Kenya’s community health strategy involves the Community Health Volunteer (CHV)?
A. Tertiary level
B. Primary level
C. Community level
D. County referral level
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: C. Community level

Rationale: The Community Health Volunteer (CHV) operates at the community level, where they play a critical role in health promotion, disease prevention, and community mobilization. CHVs are the link between households and the formal health system and are crucial in implementing community strategy at the grassroots.

2.
One of the key principles of Primary Health Care (PHC) is:
A. Specialist services
B. Hospital-based care
C. Community participation
D. Advanced diagnostic services
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: C. Community participation

Rationale: Community participation ensures that the people are actively involved in planning, implementing, and evaluating health services. This principle empowers communities to take ownership of their health, making interventions more acceptable, sustainable, and effective at the grassroots level.

3.
What is the core function of the Community Health Committee (CHC)?
A. Performing surgeries
B. Supervising national hospitals
C. Overseeing community health activities
D. Approving medical licenses
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: C. Overseeing community health activities

Rationale: CHCs are responsible for coordinating, monitoring, and supporting health activities within their communities. They ensure that community health workers are supported and that health plans reflect local needs. They play a governance and advocacy role, not a clinical one.

4.
Which of the following is not a key element of PHC?
A. Health education
B. Immunization
C. Disease surveillance
D. Open-heart surgery
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: D. Open-heart surgery

Rationale: Open-heart surgery is a specialized, tertiary-level service, not part of Primary Health Care. PHC emphasizes accessible, low-cost, essential services such as immunization, health promotion, sanitation, and basic curative care, which are relevant to the majority of the population.

5.
Which component of the community strategy addresses early identification of illness at home?
A. Community midwifery
B. Level 6 hospital services
C. Household visits by CHVs
D. National disease registry
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: C. Household visits by CHVs

Rationale: CHVs conduct regular household visits to assess health needs, identify danger signs early, and make timely referrals. This activity helps reduce morbidity and mortality by promoting early detection and prompt intervention at the community level.

6.
The role of Community Health Extension Workers (CHEWs) includes:
A. Performing surgeries
B. Supervising CHVs
C. Managing county budgets
D. Running private clinics
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B. Supervising CHVs

Rationale: CHEWs act as a bridge between CHVs and the formal health system. They provide supervision, mentorship, training, and data verification to ensure quality service delivery by CHVs. They are part of the primary health care team at the health facility level.

7.
Which of the following best describes the term “PHC”?
A. Tertiary medical care
B. Curative hospital care only
C. Essential health care made universally accessible
D. Complex surgical interventions
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: C. Essential health care made universally accessible

Rationale: PHC emphasizes universal access to essential care that is scientifically sound, socially acceptable, and affordable. It includes promotive, preventive, curative, and rehabilitative services and is meant to address the main health problems of the community.

8.
In Kenya’s community strategy, the Community Health Unit (CHU) typically covers how many households?
A. 50
B. 1000
C. 5000
D. 20,000
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B. 1000

Rationale: A Community Health Unit (CHU) in Kenya typically serves about 5,000 people, which translates to approximately 1,000 households. This scale allows for effective health promotion, disease surveillance, and household-level engagement through CHVs and CHEWs.

9.
What is the purpose of the Community Health Information System (CHIS)?
A. To track surgical performance
B. To manage pharmaceutical sales
C. To collect and analyze community-level health data
D. To regulate insurance premiums
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: C. To collect and analyze community-level health data

Rationale: CHIS is used by CHVs and CHEWs to gather data on health indicators, service utilization, and disease patterns. This information guides planning, resource allocation, and evaluation of health interventions at the community level.

10.
Which strategy helps in strengthening the referral system in PHC?
A. Ignoring minor cases
B. Community health dialogues
C. Proper documentation and communication
D. Closing small clinics
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: C. Proper documentation and communication

Rationale: A strong referral system ensures timely access to higher levels of care. Proper documentation and communication between CHVs, CHEWs, and health facilities allow for efficient, coordinated, and continuous care for patients, reducing morbidity and mortality.

11.
A major advantage of PHC is:
A. It is expensive and exclusive
B. It excludes rural communities
C. It empowers communities to take charge of their health
D. It requires high-level technology
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: C. It empowers communities to take charge of their health

Rationale: PHC encourages community ownership and involvement in health decisions. This empowerment leads to sustainable behavior change and health improvements, as people are more likely to adopt interventions they understand and have helped shape.

12.
What is the ultimate goal of the community health strategy?
A. Establishing urban hospitals
B. Improving social media outreach
C. Enhancing health outcomes at the household level
D. Promoting medical tourism
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: C. Enhancing health outcomes at the household level

Rationale: The community health strategy aims to extend essential services to households, where health problems often begin. By focusing on the household as the unit of intervention, the strategy reduces disease burden and improves overall health indicators.

13.
Which principle is emphasized in both PHC and Kenya’s community health strategy?
A. Free surgery
B. Community participation
C. Specialist referrals
D. Capital investment
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B. Community participation

Rationale: Both PHC and the community strategy prioritize active involvement of communities in identifying problems, setting priorities, and implementing solutions. This participatory approach ensures sustainability and relevance of health interventions.

14.
The Community Dialogue Day is primarily aimed at:
A. Educating about new laws
B. Entertainment and recreation
C. Discussing and resolving community health issues
D. Distributing pharmaceuticals
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: C. Discussing and resolving community health issues

Rationale: Community Dialogue Days are platforms where community members, CHVs, and health workers come together to discuss health data, identify challenges, and jointly plan solutions. They foster transparency, accountability, and ownership in community health matters.

15.
Which of the following is NOT typically a function of a CHV?
A. Administering surgery
B. Conducting health education
C. Referring clients
D. Promoting sanitation
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: A. Administering surgery

Rationale: CHVs are trained for basic health functions such as health promotion, sanitation, immunization follow-ups, and referrals. Surgery is a specialized medical service performed in hospitals by licensed practitioners, not CHVs.

16.
Which tool helps CHVs report health conditions at the household level?
A. Community Health Register (MoH 513)
B. CT scan machine
C. NHIF claims form
D. X-ray requisition form
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: A. Community Health Register (MoH 513)

Rationale: The MoH 513 is the official tool used by CHVs to record and report health data from household visits. It includes information on illnesses, referrals, sanitation, maternal health, and other indicators critical for planning.

17.
What is a key outcome of successful implementation of the community strategy?
A. Higher urban migration
B. Increase in hospital admissions
C. Improved household health indicators
D. Abandonment of traditional practices
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: C. Improved household health indicators

Rationale: When the community strategy is well implemented, there is a marked improvement in health outcomes such as immunization coverage, maternal health, sanitation, and early disease detection—all at the household level.

18.
PHC was internationally endorsed through which declaration?
A. Abuja Declaration
B. Alma-Ata Declaration
C. Geneva Health Charter
D. Nairobi Accord
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B. Alma-Ata Declaration

Rationale: The Alma-Ata Declaration of 1978 established PHC as the key to achieving "Health for All." It emphasized community involvement, equity, accessibility, and intersectoral collaboration in health care delivery.

19.
Who is responsible for updating the Community Health Information System?
A. Hospital administrator
B. CHEW and CHVs
C. National Treasury
D. Ministry of Transport
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B. CHEW and CHVs

Rationale: CHVs collect health data from households, while CHEWs verify, compile, and submit the data to higher levels. Their collaboration ensures that CHIS reflects accurate and timely information for decision-making.

20.
What enables community health activities to be more culturally appropriate?
A. Hiring international consultants
B. Engaging traditional healers exclusively
C. Community participation and local input
D. Applying foreign policies
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: C. Community participation and local input

Rationale: Engaging community members ensures that health interventions are adapted to local customs, beliefs, and practices. This increases the acceptance and effectiveness of programs, especially in rural or conservative communities.

21.
What is one way to enhance accountability in community health delivery?
A. Avoiding household visits
B. Ignoring community concerns
C. Holding regular community dialogue days
D. Centralizing decision-making in the capital
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: C. Holding regular community dialogue days

Rationale: Community dialogue days provide an opportunity for community members to review health data, question services, and participate in planning. This increases transparency and helps build trust and ownership in the health system, strengthening accountability.

22.
Which aspect of the PHC approach addresses water and sanitation?
A. Specialized surgical services
B. Environmental health
C. Radiological services
D. Internal medicine
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B. Environmental health

Rationale: Environmental health is a key component of PHC. It focuses on safe water supply, proper sanitation, waste management, and hygiene promotion—essential interventions for disease prevention, particularly in resource-limited settings.

23.
One major challenge facing CHVs in Kenya is:
A. High salaries
B. Overstaffing
C. Lack of financial incentives and motivation
D. Excess medical equipment
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: C. Lack of financial incentives and motivation

Rationale: CHVs often volunteer their time without regular remuneration, which affects morale and sustainability. Limited resources and delayed support can hinder their ability to consistently perform household visits or participate in training, reducing overall impact.

24.
PHC emphasizes:
A. Equity and accessibility
B. Profit generation
C. Complex diagnostics
D. Exclusive specialist care
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: A. Equity and accessibility

Rationale: PHC aims to ensure that essential health services are accessible to all, especially vulnerable populations. Equity ensures fair resource distribution, while accessibility means people can receive services regardless of their location or income level.

25.
Which of the following best describes a CHEW’s role?
A. Conducting surgery
B. Supervising CHVs and supporting health facilities
C. Managing private pharmacies
D. Importing medical supplies
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B. Supervising CHVs and supporting health facilities

Rationale: CHEWs are health professionals responsible for mentoring and supervising CHVs, coordinating data collection, and linking communities with health facilities. Their support improves the quality and effectiveness of community health interventions.

Section 2: Implementation & Surveillance

25 Marks
26.
How does community health strategy contribute to Universal Health Coverage (UHC)?
A. By excluding rural areas
B. By focusing only on urban populations
C. Through preventive services at the household level
D. By eliminating traditional birth attendants
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: C. Through preventive services at the household level

Rationale: The strategy emphasizes preventive and promotive services delivered at the community and household levels, increasing coverage and reducing the need for costly curative care. This supports the broader UHC goal of equitable access to essential health services.

27.
What role do CHVs play in family planning?
A. Conducting sterilization
B. Performing surgeries
C. Providing information and referrals
D. Creating pharmaceutical advertisements
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: C. Providing information and referrals

Rationale: CHVs offer education on available family planning methods, dispel myths, and refer clients to health facilities for services. This enhances contraceptive uptake and promotes reproductive health in the community.

28.
What is one indicator of successful community health implementation?
A. Increased emergency admissions
B. Higher rates of untreated disease
C. Reduced maternal and child mortality
D. Decreased immunization rates
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: C. Reduced maternal and child mortality

Rationale: Effective community health programs result in early detection, prompt referrals, and increased use of antenatal and postnatal care services. These interventions directly reduce maternal and child mortality rates and reflect successful community-level engagement.

29.
Why are CHVs essential for PHC implementation in rural areas?
A. They prescribe medication
B. They provide laboratory services
C. They bridge the gap between the health system and households
D. They operate ambulances
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: C. They bridge the gap between the health system and households

Rationale: CHVs are often the first point of contact in rural settings. They promote health-seeking behavior, educate the population, identify illnesses early, and refer clients. Their role ensures the continuum of care from the home to the facility.

30.
The Kenya Community Health Strategy is primarily focused on:
A. Urban hospital expansion
B. Delivering services at the household level
C. Constructing diagnostic labs
D. Outsourcing to foreign health professionals
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B. Delivering services at the household level

Rationale: The strategy seeks to strengthen health service delivery through community units, CHVs, and CHEWs. By focusing on household-level engagement, it ensures people receive care before complications arise, which reduces the burden on hospitals.

31.
Which of the following is a preventive PHC service?
A. Appendectomy
B. Health education
C. Blood transfusion
D. Dialysis
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B. Health education

Rationale: Health education empowers individuals with knowledge about disease prevention, healthy behaviors, and available services. As a key preventive intervention, it is central to the PHC approach in promoting wellness and reducing illness burden.

32.
What is a key benefit of community-based health data collection?
A. Increases drug prices
B. Encourages dependence on foreign aid
C. Improves planning and targeted interventions
D. Limits community participation
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: C. Improves planning and targeted interventions

Rationale: Accurate and timely data from CHVs allows health workers and policymakers to identify trends, plan interventions, and allocate resources where most needed. It ensures decisions are evidence-based and tailored to community needs.

33.
The most appropriate way for CHVs to ensure client confidentiality is:
A. Disclosing information to family
B. Discussing cases in public
C. Keeping client records private
D. Posting data on social media
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: C. Keeping client records private

Rationale: Maintaining confidentiality is crucial for trust and ethical health care delivery. CHVs must keep personal health information private and only share it with authorized personnel to protect the rights and dignity of clients.

34.
The use of Community Health Dialogues allows:
A. Political mobilization
B. Public entertainment
C. Community-driven health action planning
D. Passive reporting of illness
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: C. Community-driven health action planning

Rationale: Community dialogues enable members to review data, prioritize problems, and agree on solutions. This participatory approach leads to tailored interventions and ensures the community is actively engaged in improving its own health.

35.
CHVs promote sanitation in communities by:
A. Distributing expensive soaps
B. Building septic tanks alone
C. Conducting hygiene education and inspections
D. Avoiding households with poor hygiene
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: C. Conducting hygiene education and inspections

Rationale: CHVs educate households on proper waste disposal, handwashing, latrine use, and water treatment. They often perform hygiene checks during household visits, helping reduce waterborne diseases and environmental health risks.

36.
CHVs identify malnutrition by:
A. Conducting surgery
B. Using Mid Upper Arm Circumference (MUAC) tapes
C. Prescribing supplements
D. Drawing blood samples
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B. Using Mid Upper Arm Circumference (MUAC) tapes

Rationale: MUAC is a quick, non-invasive tool for assessing nutritional status in children and pregnant women. CHVs are trained to use MUAC tapes during household visits to identify and refer cases of malnutrition early.

37.
Community health units are supported by:
A. National parliaments only
B. Police departments
C. Health facilities and CHEWs
D. Agricultural officers only
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: C. Health facilities and CHEWs

Rationale: CHUs are linked to nearby health facilities where CHEWs offer supervision, training, and support to CHVs. This integration strengthens referral systems and ensures consistency in service delivery.

38.
The first level of contact in the health system in rural Kenya is often:
A. Tertiary hospitals
B. CHVs during household visits
C. Air ambulances
D. International NGOs
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B. CHVs during household visits

Rationale: In rural and underserved communities, CHVs are usually the first contact for health education, disease identification, and referrals. Their proximity to households and routine visits make them vital in early health intervention.

39.
A major principle of PHC is:
A. Minimal involvement of communities
B. Centralized decision-making
C. Intersectoral collaboration
D. Use of expensive technologies
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: C. Intersectoral collaboration

Rationale: Health is influenced by multiple sectors like education, water, agriculture, and housing. PHC promotes collaboration across these sectors to address the broader determinants of health and improve outcomes.

40.
What is the main role of the Community Health Committee (CHC)?
A. Providing surgeries
B. Overseeing CHU activities and mobilizing resources
C. Administering vaccines
D. Recruiting hospital staff
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B. Overseeing CHU activities and mobilizing resources

Rationale: CHCs are community governance structures that support CHVs, monitor health initiatives, advocate for health priorities, and mobilize resources. Their involvement enhances community ownership and strengthens accountability.

41.
Effective PHC requires:
A. Exclusively private hospitals
B. Vertical programs only
C. Community participation and multisectoral approach
D. Importing foreign medicines
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: C. Community participation and multisectoral approach

Rationale: Sustainable PHC implementation relies on engaging communities and integrating services from multiple sectors. This ensures that health interventions address the root causes of illness and meet actual community needs.

42.
A barrier to CHV performance is:
A. Strong referral systems
B. Regular community support
C. Delayed supply of reporting tools and commodities
D. Collaboration with CHEWs
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: C. Delayed supply of reporting tools and commodities

Rationale: When CHVs lack tools like MUAC tapes, registers, or reporting forms, their work becomes difficult and inefficient. Timely supply of resources is essential for accurate data collection and quality service delivery.

43.
PHC is ideally provided at:
A. Specialized referral hospitals
B. Community and primary care levels
C. National medical centers only
D. Exclusive urban clinics
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B. Community and primary care levels

Rationale: PHC is designed to be the first contact and main level of health service delivery. It emphasizes localized, accessible services that meet the needs of individuals and families in their communities.

44.
Community strategy improves health by:
A. Focusing only on treatment
B. Ignoring traditional practices
C. Combining promotion, prevention, and basic treatment
D. Isolating health care from society
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: C. Combining promotion, prevention, and basic treatment

Rationale: The strategy promotes health holistically by integrating education, disease prevention, early detection, and basic curative services at the household level, reducing the overall disease burden.

45.
PHC is designed to:
A. Replace hospitals
B. Operate independently of communities
C. Address main health problems of communities
D. Focus on non-communicable diseases only
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: C. Address main health problems of communities

Rationale: PHC provides essential services to manage common illnesses and prevent disease outbreaks. It adapts to local needs and helps communities become more resilient by focusing on their actual health challenges.

46.
Who coordinates health activities in a Community Health Unit?
A. CHV
B. Hospital specialist
C. Community Health Committee and CHEW
D. Laboratory technologist
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: C. Community Health Committee and CHEW

Rationale: CHEWs provide technical guidance while CHCs offer governance and oversight. Their collaboration ensures that community health activities are well-organized, responsive to local needs, and aligned with national goals.

47.
Which of the following is a key strategy for achieving Sustainable Development Goal 3 (Health and Wellbeing)?
A. Focusing on surgical interventions
B. Implementing PHC and community strategies
C. Privatizing public health services
D. Avoiding preventive measures
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B. Implementing PHC and community strategies

Rationale: SDG 3 emphasizes access to quality health services for all. Strengthening PHC and community health systems promotes equity, reduces preventable deaths, and enhances resilience at the grassroots.

48.
Community-based surveillance by CHVs helps to:
A. Replace medical officers
B. Delay interventions
C. Detect disease outbreaks early
D. Avoid reporting deaths
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: C. Detect disease outbreaks early

Rationale: CHVs are trained to identify unusual illness patterns or symptoms during household visits. Early reporting of such events enables public health authorities to act quickly and contain outbreaks.

49.
Community Strategy promotes health through:
A. Preventive, promotive, and referral services
B. Surgery and transplants
C. Chemotherapy and radiation
D. Exclusive use of ambulances
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: A. Preventive, promotive, and referral services

Rationale: Community Strategy focuses on strengthening the foundation of health systems by empowering CHVs and CHEWs to deliver preventive education, promote good practices, and refer clients to appropriate levels of care.

50.
Which statement is true about CHUs?
A. They operate only in cities
B. They exclude families from planning
C. They are units of community-based health services
D. They function without any supervision
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: C. They are units of community-based health services

Rationale: CHUs are the foundational units of Kenya's community health strategy. Each unit comprises CHVs supervised by CHEWs and supported by a CHC. They deliver services directly to households and are integral to PHC delivery.
