Community Health

NCK, KRCHN, PAPER 2 - 1100 MCQS Answers and Rationales

Instructions

  • This paper consists of 50 Multiple Choice Questions focusing on Community Health principles.
  • Each question has four options; select the most appropriate one.
  • Detailed rationales are provided for each answer to facilitate effective revision.
  • Total Marks: 50 | Time Allowed: 60 Minutes.

Section 1: Community Engagement & Planning

25 Marks
1.
A community health nurse is planning an intervention to reduce teenage pregnancies in a semi-urban settlement. Which approach best reflects an effective application of community empowerment principles?
A. Providing free contraceptives at the local clinic
B. Launching a media campaign to discourage early sex
C. Organizing youth forums to discuss reproductive health and make decisions
D. Training teachers to deliver sex education in schools
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: C

Rationale: Community empowerment emphasizes active participation and ownership by the target population. Organizing youth forums allows adolescents to be actively involved in discussing and determining solutions for their own health issues. While providing resources or education is helpful, empowerment focuses on enabling people to make informed choices, enhancing long-term outcomes.

2.
During a home visit, a community nurse notices that several children in a household are underweight despite adequate food availability. Which of the following actions demonstrates a synthesis of nutritional knowledge and social assessment?
A. Advise the caregiver on proper portion sizes
B. Refer the children to a nutritionist
C. Assess for caregiver's understanding of feeding practices and cultural beliefs
D. Recommend food supplements from the health facility
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: C

Rationale: Addressing undernutrition in the presence of sufficient food requires more than just nutritional advice—it involves analyzing the interplay of knowledge, beliefs, and social factors. Assessing the caregiver’s understanding and cultural practices provides insight into behavioral patterns that might hinder proper feeding, aligning with a more holistic and synthesized intervention.

3.
A rural health survey reveals high incidences of diarrheal diseases. Which action would best reflect an evidence-based response that integrates community participation?
A. Distribute water purification tablets
B. Initiate household-level water and sanitation training led by local volunteers
C. Educate mothers at the clinic on handwashing
D. Launch a district-level sanitation policy
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B

Rationale: Integrating community participation involves empowering local members to take responsibility for health initiatives. Training local volunteers to lead education fosters sustainability, ownership, and relevance. While policies and resources are important, participatory approaches have shown greater success in maintaining behavior change in rural settings.

4.
A community health nurse is analyzing the outcome of a health promotion project. Which indicator best reflects long-term community transformation?
A. Number of people attending health talks
B. Reduction in disease incidence after 3 months
C. Community-led planning and continuation of health programs
D. Availability of educational posters in community centers
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: C

Rationale: Long-term transformation is evident when the community sustains and owns health initiatives independently. While attendance and short-term outcomes show engagement, community-led continuation signals empowerment and internalization of health practices. It demonstrates that the change is not solely dependent on external interventions.

5.
A newly posted nurse in a remote community finds that the population mistrusts healthcare services due to past neglect. What is the most appropriate initial step to rebuild trust?
A. Provide incentives for clinic attendance
B. Conduct health talks during market days
C. Engage respected community leaders in dialogue and planning
D. Offer free check-ups to gain goodwill
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: C

Rationale: Gaining trust in a previously neglected community requires leveraging respected local structures and leadership. Involving leaders in planning and dialogue demonstrates respect for local knowledge and promotes ownership, which is more sustainable than temporary incentives or services. Trust is built through inclusive relationships, not just services.

6.
A nurse is evaluating a community's readiness to implement a smoking cessation program. The residents acknowledge the harm of smoking but do not believe change is possible. Which stage of community readiness is this?
A. Pre-contemplation
B. Contemplation
C. Preparation
D. Action
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B

Rationale: In the contemplation stage, individuals recognize a problem but are ambivalent or unsure about taking action. This reflects the described situation. Identifying this stage helps tailor interventions—such as motivational interviewing—to move the community toward preparation and action.

7.
A health program on exclusive breastfeeding fails despite extensive education efforts. What critical element was likely overlooked during planning?
A. Number of lactating mothers
B. Cultural beliefs and norms around infant feeding
C. Cost of formula milk
D. Distance to health facilities
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B

Rationale: Behavioral health interventions must consider socio-cultural contexts. Educational efforts alone may not succeed if cultural norms promote early weaning or mixed feeding. Understanding and integrating local beliefs into program design is essential for behavior change, underscoring the importance of cultural competence.

8.
Which of the following demonstrates an evaluative role of a community health nurse in disease prevention programs?
A. Implementing health education sessions
B. Assessing the decline in malaria cases after bed net distribution
C. Conducting house-to-house net distributions
D. Training community health workers
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B

Rationale: Evaluation involves measuring outcomes to determine program effectiveness. Assessing the impact of an intervention (e.g., reduction in malaria) reflects this role. Implementing, distributing, or training are important operational roles but do not address program assessment.

9.
A community nurse is choosing between two intervention strategies: community drama vs. radio talk shows. What factor should primarily guide this decision?
A. Cost of each strategy
B. Availability of actors
C. Literacy and media access patterns of the target community
D. Nurse’s personal preference
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: C

Rationale: Effective communication strategies depend on understanding how the target community receives and processes information. In populations with low literacy or limited access to radio, drama may be more effective. The decision must be informed by a socio-demographic analysis, not just cost or convenience.

10.
A community-based TB control project aims to enhance DOTS (Directly Observed Therapy, Short-course) compliance. Which strategy ensures the best integration with existing community systems?
A. Hire external health workers to supervise treatment
B. Use mobile phone reminders
C. Train and involve local community health volunteers
D. Provide incentives for every completed dose
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: C

Rationale: Sustainability and compliance improve when community systems are utilized. Local health volunteers understand the context and can provide consistent follow-up. This enhances accountability and trust compared to externally imposed or technology-driven solutions, which may not be sustainable or context-appropriate.

11.
A community health officer is planning to allocate resources for a sanitation improvement project. Which population data is most relevant for effective planning?
A. Number of households with electricity
B. Age distribution of the community
C. Percentage of households practicing open defecation
D. Number of school-going children
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: C

Rationale: To plan a sanitation-focused project, understanding the proportion of households that lack toilet facilities or practice open defecation is essential. This directly impacts sanitation outcomes. While other data may be informative, they are not central to the specific objective of improving sanitation.

12.
After conducting a participatory rural appraisal (PRA), which indicator best reflects community ownership of a health initiative?
A. Community members attending meetings
B. Local leaders giving approval for the project
C. Community contributions in form of labor and materials
D. Distribution of printed brochures about the project
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: C

Rationale: Community ownership is evidenced when members actively contribute to the initiative—either through labor, materials, or funds. Passive approval or attendance does not equate to ownership. Active participation in resource provision reflects genuine engagement and commitment to success.

13.
A nurse finds that a malaria prevention program led by a donor agency is failing after handover to local health workers. What is the most likely reason for this failure?
A. Inadequate funding
B. Poor supply of insecticides
C. Lack of community involvement in planning
D. Poor training of health workers
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: C

Rationale: Programs designed without community input often lack cultural relevance and sustainability. When the community is not involved from the beginning, ownership and continuity suffer post-handover. Community engagement is crucial for long-term success, more so than even funding or supplies.

14.
What is the most appropriate method to assess a community’s perception of health needs during a baseline survey?
A. Focus group discussions with community elites
B. Interviews with health workers only
C. Household interviews and community mapping
D. Collection of health facility statistics
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: C

Rationale: A combination of household interviews and participatory methods like community mapping captures the voices of diverse community members and provides a realistic assessment of perceived needs. Relying solely on elites or health data may miss key contextual issues.

15.
Which strategy best demonstrates intersectoral collaboration in a nutrition improvement program?
A. Nutrition education sessions led by nurses
B. Involvement of agriculture officers in food production campaigns
C. Mass deworming by public health officers
D. Providing supplements in schools
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B

Rationale: Nutrition is affected by factors beyond health. Involving agriculture officers reflects a collaborative approach across sectors, recognizing the importance of food access and production. Health-only interventions may not address root causes of malnutrition, thus intersectoral strategies are more effective.

16.
A community health worker observes a rising trend in adolescent alcohol use. Which action reflects a systems-thinking approach?
A. Reporting cases to local authorities
B. Conducting individual counseling sessions
C. Addressing family, school, and community norms influencing alcohol use
D. Referring affected adolescents to a rehabilitation center
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: C

Rationale: A systems-thinking approach considers multiple interconnected factors and stakeholders influencing a health issue. In this case, addressing norms at the family, school, and community levels is a more holistic strategy compared to isolated interventions like referrals or counseling.

17.
A nurse evaluates a 6-month sanitation campaign and finds minimal behavior change. Which planning flaw most likely contributed to this?
A. Poor selection of materials
B. Insufficient financial resources
C. Exclusion of community values in designing messages
D. Lack of trained health workers
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: C

Rationale: Behavior change programs must align with local beliefs and values to be effective. Messaging that disregards cultural context often fails to resonate. While resources and staff are important, culturally inappropriate messaging can significantly undermine impact.

18.
What is the most effective way to assess the impact of a handwashing promotion campaign in a school setting?
A. Distributing a post-intervention questionnaire
B. Measuring reduction in absenteeism due to diarrheal illness
C. Asking teachers to report observed behaviors
D. Counting the number of handwashing posters
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B

Rationale: Impact should be measured by actual health outcomes. A decrease in absenteeism due to preventable illness is a strong indicator of improved hygiene practices. Self-reports and posters may reflect exposure but do not confirm behavior change or health impact.

19.
Which factor is most critical when setting priorities during community health planning?
A. Government policy preferences
B. Severity and frequency of the health problem
C. Availability of external funding
D. Health workers’ interests
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B

Rationale: Prioritization in health planning should be guided by data on the burden of disease, specifically severity and frequency. This ensures the most pressing issues are addressed first. Political or financial factors may influence implementation but should not override health needs.

20.
Which of the following best reflects community mobilization rather than mere information dissemination?
A. Broadcasting health messages on local radio
B. Hosting a community drama on hygiene
C. Involving community groups in planning a clean-up campaign
D. Printing pamphlets for disease prevention
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: C

Rationale: Community mobilization involves engaging people in collective action and decision-making. Simply providing information (via media or materials) may raise awareness but does not foster ownership or participation. Planning together fosters empowerment and sustained action.

21.
A community health nurse aims to reduce maternal mortality in a marginalized population. What strategy ensures the greatest impact?
A. Distribute maternal health booklets
B. Encourage traditional birth attendants to refer mothers early
C. Improve skilled birth attendance by training and stationing midwives locally
D. Offer transport vouchers to the nearest hospital
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: C

Rationale: Ensuring access to skilled care at delivery has the highest impact on maternal outcomes. Training and deploying midwives addresses both access and quality of care, more effectively than indirect strategies like booklets or transport support alone.

22.
A nutrition program fails despite availability of food supplies. A review shows that caregivers lack time due to labor demands. What does this scenario highlight?
A. The need for food variety
B. Ineffectiveness of the health team
C. Importance of considering gender roles in planning
D. Need for more nutrition education
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: C

Rationale: Programs often fail when they ignore social dynamics, including gender roles that affect time allocation. Women’s workload can hinder childcare practices. Effective planning must consider such sociocultural and economic realities, not just material or educational gaps.

23.
A community health worker is using a health belief model to design a hypertension screening campaign. Which factor should be prioritized?
A. Community’s knowledge of dietary salt intake
B. Perceived seriousness and susceptibility to hypertension
C. Number of available screening kits
D. Local health worker training
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B

Rationale: The Health Belief Model emphasizes perception of risk and consequences as key motivators for action. If individuals do not perceive themselves at risk, they are unlikely to act, regardless of knowledge or service availability. Behavioral models guide effective communication.

24.
A flood-prone area is experiencing frequent outbreaks of waterborne diseases. What long-term strategy would best address this public health issue?
A. Distribute chlorine tablets after floods
B. Relocate the population to higher ground
C. Construct flood-resistant water and sanitation infrastructure
D. Conduct regular community health talks
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: C

Rationale: Long-term control of disease requires addressing root causes. Flood-resistant infrastructure mitigates contamination and ensures continuity of safe water access. Emergency responses like chlorine tablets are temporary. Permanent solutions reduce vulnerability and dependence on crisis interventions.

25.
A nurse designing a health outreach program must choose between high-cost mobile clinics and lower-cost community peer groups. The budget is limited. What should guide the choice?
A. Nurse’s experience with mobile clinics
B. Preference of local health authorities
C. Comparative cost-effectiveness and community acceptance
D. International donor recommendations
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: C

Rationale: Decision-making should be evidence-based and context-sensitive. Cost-effectiveness combined with community acceptance ensures sustainability and impact. Personal preferences or external influences should not outweigh local relevance and efficiency.

Section 2: Implementation & Sustainability

25 Marks
26.
Which outcome best reflects successful integration of health services in a community?
A. Reduction in facility workload
B. Increased community access to multiple services in one visit
C. Expansion of facility infrastructure
D. Increase in specialized providers in urban centers
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B

Rationale: Integration aims to enhance service delivery efficiency and access. When individuals can access comprehensive services—like immunization, antenatal care, and family planning—in one visit, the system becomes more user-friendly and effective. Facility expansion or staff increase without integration does not achieve this.

27.
A community nurse wants to advocate for improved maternal health services. What strategy will likely yield the best support from policymakers?
A. Organizing public demonstrations
B. Presenting stories from affected mothers
C. Sharing community-level maternal mortality statistics
D. Writing letters to the newspaper
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: C

Rationale: Evidence-based advocacy that presents hard data is more persuasive to policymakers. While personal stories are powerful, decision-makers often require quantifiable data to justify investment. Well-documented statistics underscore the magnitude of the issue and guide policy prioritization.

28.
When developing a program to reduce teenage drug use, which component is most critical for sustainability?
A. Peer education and community mentorship structures
B. Use of external behavior change experts
C. Free drug testing services
D. Enforcement of strict school policies
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: A

Rationale: Sustainable programs are embedded in the community. Peer education and mentorship promote ownership and cultural relevance, encouraging long-term change. External experts or punitive approaches may have short-term effects but lack community anchoring.

29.
A nurse designing a health education program aims to reach the most vulnerable. Which tool should be used to identify them?
A. Community maps and vulnerability ranking
B. Random household surveys
C. Clinic attendance registers
D. Census demographic data
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: A

Rationale: Participatory tools like vulnerability ranking help identify marginalized groups based on local definitions and perceptions. This ensures that interventions are targeted where most needed. Data from clinics or census may miss hidden or underserved populations.

30.
In a disaster-prone region, which community health strategy enhances resilience?
A. Stockpiling food and medicines
B. Creating a community disaster response plan
C. Conducting monthly drills
D. Relocating high-risk families
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B

Rationale: A community-led disaster response plan builds preparedness and defines clear roles, enabling quick, coordinated action. While stockpiling and drills are helpful, planning ensures long-term resilience and reduces dependency on external aid.

31.
A community-based health insurance (CBHI) scheme in a rural area has low enrollment. Which is the most effective intervention to increase uptake?
A. Lowering premium payments
B. Involving community leaders in sensitization and education
C. Distributing pamphlets in local dialects
D. Hiring insurance agents to enroll people door-to-door
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B

Rationale: Community leaders are influential and trusted, and their involvement fosters credibility and trust. Sensitization by local leaders addresses misconceptions, improves understanding, and increases voluntary participation. While cost and materials help, community ownership and trust are more impactful for long-term enrollment.

32.
A nurse is planning a community health fair in an urban slum. Which of the following components ensures equity in service delivery?
A. First-come, first-served model
B. Allocation of tokens to early arrivals
C. Prioritizing the most vulnerable populations
D. Serving all age groups equally
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: C

Rationale: Equity emphasizes meeting the needs of the most disadvantaged. Prioritizing vulnerable groups ensures they are not left out due to systemic barriers. Serving everyone equally (equality) may still result in exclusion if access issues are not addressed intentionally.

33.
Which community health approach most effectively supports sustainable improvement in child nutrition?
A. Mass feeding programs led by NGOs
B. Training mothers on kitchen gardening and balanced diets
C. Providing iron supplements to all children
D. Periodic growth monitoring at clinics
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B

Rationale: Empowering mothers through skills like kitchen gardening supports long-term food security and improved nutrition practices. Mass feeding and supplements address symptoms, not root causes. Behavior change and self-reliance foster sustainable improvements in child health.

34.
In designing a community health plan, which source of information is most valuable for understanding prevailing local health problems?
A. Previous year’s clinic reports
B. National demographic and health surveys
C. Community-based participatory research findings
D. Published literature from health journals
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: C

Rationale: Participatory research directly involves the community and reveals locally perceived health issues, causes, and solutions. It reflects ground realities better than clinic data or national surveys, which may not capture localized nuances. It ensures relevance and ownership.

35.
A nurse uses GIS (Geographic Information System) mapping to identify households affected by waterborne diseases. What is the most appropriate next step?
A. Refer cases to the health facility
B. Start blanket treatment with antibiotics
C. Engage water and sanitation stakeholders for targeted intervention
D. Distribute health education leaflets
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: C

Rationale: GIS reveals spatial patterns that should guide multi-sectoral responses. Waterborne diseases often stem from infrastructure issues, so engaging WASH stakeholders enables targeted, systemic solutions. Referrals or education alone are insufficient for addressing environmental determinants.

36.
During a household survey, a nurse notes that many respondents give answers they think are expected rather than their true practices. What strategy improves data reliability?
A. Increase number of questions
B. Conduct interviews in group settings
C. Use indirect questioning and triangulate with observation
D. Only interview heads of households
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: C

Rationale: Social desirability bias can be reduced by triangulating data sources—observation, indirect questioning, and validating across respondents. This improves the accuracy of data, unlike increasing quantity or changing the interview target. Group settings may worsen bias.

37.
A health worker evaluating a community tuberculosis program finds that treatment default rates are high. What is the best way to address this issue?
A. Provide incentives for completing treatment
B. Increase the drug supply
C. Conduct community education campaigns only
D. Use directly observed treatment (DOT) supported by community volunteers
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: D

Rationale: DOT ensures adherence through observation and support, especially when done by trusted community members. This method addresses practical and psychological barriers to compliance. Incentives may help but are often not sustainable or address core issues like forgetfulness or stigma.

38.
Which of the following best demonstrates a shift from a curative to a preventive model in community health practice?
A. Increasing the number of hospital beds
B. Training nurses in surgical wound care
C. Strengthening home-based care for chronic illness
D. Promoting immunization and hygiene education
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: D

Rationale: Preventive care focuses on reducing disease before it occurs. Immunization and hygiene education prevent illness, reduce burden on curative services, and are cornerstones of public health. Home care and hospital expansion, while helpful, remain within the curative framework.

39.
In a cholera outbreak, a nurse identifies that affected households are using a common water source. What public health action is most appropriate?
A. Provide ORS to all affected individuals
B. Educate the community on handwashing
C. Treat and secure the contaminated water source
D. Deploy additional nurses to the clinic
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: C

Rationale: While symptomatic treatment is essential, addressing the source of contamination stops further spread. Water treatment and protection directly eliminate the environmental cause. Education and extra staff help manage but do not prevent recurrence.

40.
A nurse wants to involve men in maternal health services. What is the most effective strategy?
A. Organize male-only ANC classes
B. Include men in community dialogues on family health
C. Penalize men who do not accompany spouses to clinics
D. Require male signatures on antenatal forms
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B

Rationale: Involving men through respectful community dialogues fosters understanding and behavior change. It creates a supportive environment for maternal health. Penalizing or coercing participation can backfire, causing resistance or token compliance.

41.
Which health intervention best embodies the principle of primary health care accessibility?
A. Establishing a private clinic in the community
B. Creating a telemedicine center in the regional capital
C. Building a dispensary within walking distance of the village
D. Referring rural clients to urban specialists
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: C

Rationale: Accessibility in PHC refers to proximity and ease of service use. A local dispensary provides timely, basic care near people’s homes. Telemedicine and referrals may be helpful, but they depend on infrastructure and may exclude the poorest.

42.
A community health nurse plans a sanitation campaign using CLTS (Community-Led Total Sanitation). What is the key component of this approach?
A. Building latrines for every household
B. Community-driven identification of sanitation problems and solutions
C. Mass distribution of chlorine
D. Regular fines for open defecation
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B

Rationale: CLTS empowers the community to recognize and solve its own sanitation issues. It emphasizes behavioral change rather than subsidies or enforcement. Success depends on internal motivation rather than external input alone.

43.
A nurse conducting a community health training wants to assess participants’ understanding effectively. What method is most appropriate?
A. Giving a written test after the session
B. Asking for oral summaries
C. Observing participants applying skills during a simulation
D. Handing out certificates of attendance
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: C

Rationale: Application of knowledge is best assessed through demonstration. Simulations allow real-time evaluation of skills, unlike written or oral tests that may not reflect true competence. Attendance does not equate to learning.

44.
In a community affected by domestic violence, what is the most effective primary prevention strategy?
A. Establishing safe houses for survivors
B. Prosecuting offenders
C. Promoting gender equity and respectful relationships through community education
D. Providing trauma counseling services
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: C

Rationale: Primary prevention targets the root causes before harm occurs. Promoting respectful relationships and challenging harmful gender norms can reduce violence. Safe houses and counseling are secondary or tertiary interventions, important but not preventive.

45.
What is the most appropriate community health intervention in a nomadic population with high rates of vaccine-preventable diseases?
A. Establish permanent clinics in settled areas
B. Conduct mobile vaccination outreach based on movement patterns
C. Encourage them to settle near health facilities
D. Distribute vaccine schedules for self-administration
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B

Rationale: Mobile outreach respects cultural lifestyles while ensuring access to services. Tailoring interventions to the mobility of the community enhances effectiveness. Asking communities to change their lifestyle undermines trust and feasibility.

46.
A nurse finds high rates of teenage pregnancies in a region with school-based reproductive health education. What is the most plausible explanation?
A. Inadequate training of teachers
B. Poor availability of contraceptives
C. Lack of parental involvement and cultural barriers
D. Too many young teachers in schools
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: C

Rationale: Community norms and lack of open family dialogue often undermine formal education efforts. Cultural resistance and taboos may prevent adolescents from acting on knowledge. Comprehensive strategies must include families and cultural leaders.

47.
A newly established community clinic has low attendance. What should the nurse do first?
A. Increase clinic operating hours
B. Conduct a community dialogue to understand perceptions and barriers
C. Advertise the clinic services on radio
D. Employ more nurses
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B

Rationale: Understanding the community’s perception and identifying barriers enables targeted solutions. The issue might not be timing or staffing but lack of awareness, trust, or perceived need. Engagement builds trust and guides planning.

48.
A health worker is designing a program to promote early cancer screening. Which of the following best enhances participation?
A. Offering free screening days only
B. Educating people about survival rates of early detection
C. Scaring people with statistics on late-stage cancer deaths
D. Mandating screening for all adults
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B

Rationale: Empowering messages that highlight benefits of early action motivate individuals better than fear-based or mandatory approaches. Positive framing builds hope and personal agency, encouraging voluntary uptake of preventive services.

49.
Which of the following best represents social accountability in community health?
A. Posting facility budgets publicly
B. Conducting patient satisfaction surveys
C. Holding regular community-facility dialogue days
D. Filing complaints anonymously
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: C

Rationale: Dialogue forums allow direct interaction, feedback, and problem-solving between communities and health providers. They foster mutual accountability. Budget posting and surveys are passive and may not lead to action or transparency unless discussed.

50.
A nurse notices that a women’s group is more effective in mobilizing for health campaigns than formal village elders. What should be the appropriate response?
A. Replace village elders with the women’s group
B. Use only the women’s group for future campaigns
C. Collaborate with both while leveraging the women’s group’s strengths
D. Dismiss traditional leadership structures
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: C

Rationale: Effective community health programs respect existing structures while also recognizing grassroots influencers. Collaborative engagement ensures sustainability, reduces conflict, and builds inclusive participation. Ignoring formal leaders can cause resistance and fragmentation.
