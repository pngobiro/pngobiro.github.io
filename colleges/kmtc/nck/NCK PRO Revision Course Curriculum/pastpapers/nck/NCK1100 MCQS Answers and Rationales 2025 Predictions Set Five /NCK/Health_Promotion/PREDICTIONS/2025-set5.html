Health Promotion

NCK, KRCHN, PAPER 2 - 1100 MCQS Answers and Rationales

Instructions

  • This paper consists of 50 Multiple Choice Questions focusing on Health Promotion strategies and models.
  • Each question has four options; select the most appropriate one.
  • Detailed rationales are provided for each answer to facilitate effective revision.
  • Total Marks: 50 | Time Allowed: 60 Minutes.

Section 1: Health Promotion Models & Goals

25 Marks
1.
What is the primary goal of health promotion?
A. To cure diseases
B. To prevent disease and improve quality of life
C. To treat injuries
D. To provide emergency care
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B

Rationale: Health promotion focuses on preventing disease, enhancing health, and improving quality of life through education, lifestyle changes, and supportive environments rather than treating illness.

2.
Which model emphasizes the importance of individual beliefs in health behavior change?
A. Transtheoretical model
B. Health belief model
C. Social cognitive theory
D. Diffusion of innovations model
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B

Rationale: The Health Belief Model centers on individual perceptions of susceptibility, severity, benefits, and barriers to adopting health behaviors.

3.
Which of the following is an example of a primary prevention strategy?
A. Screening for diabetes
B. Immunization against measles
C. Rehabilitation after stroke
D. Medication adherence in hypertension
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B

Rationale: Immunization prevents the onset of disease, which is the essence of primary prevention, whereas screening and rehabilitation are secondary and tertiary prevention, respectively.

4.
Which is the most effective way to promote behavior change in a community?
A. Top-down enforcement of rules
B. Engaging community members in planning
C. Only distributing pamphlets
D. Providing medical treatments
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B

Rationale: Community participation in planning ensures programs are culturally relevant and sustainable, leading to better behavior change outcomes.

5.
What does the “social determinants of health” refer to?
A. Genetic factors only
B. Economic and social conditions influencing health
C. Medical care access alone
D. Individual lifestyle choices only
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B

Rationale: Social determinants include socioeconomic status, education, environment, and social support affecting health outcomes beyond genetics or individual behaviors.

6.
Which health promotion strategy focuses on creating supportive environments?
A. Policy advocacy
B. Health education alone
C. Clinical treatment
D. Disease surveillance
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: A

Rationale: Creating supportive environments often requires policy changes and advocacy to enable healthy choices, such as smoke-free zones or access to parks.

7.
What is the best method to evaluate the success of a health promotion program?
A. Measuring knowledge gain only
B. Assessing behavioral changes and health outcomes
C. Counting attendance at sessions
D. Monitoring program costs
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B

Rationale: Evaluation should focus on actual changes in behavior and health indicators to determine true effectiveness.

8.
Which theory explains behavior change as a process through stages like precontemplation and maintenance?
A. Health belief model
B. Transtheoretical model
C. Theory of planned behavior
D. Ecological model
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B

Rationale: The Transtheoretical Model (Stages of Change) describes progression through stages before sustained behavior change.

9.
Which of the following is a key component of effective health education?
A. One-way communication
B. Culturally sensitive materials
C. Ignoring learner’s background
D. Technical jargon use
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B

Rationale: Tailoring educational materials to cultural context improves understanding and receptiveness.

10.
What role do peer educators play in health promotion?
A. They replace professional healthcare workers
B. They provide relatable and credible information
C. They impose health policies
D. They diagnose illnesses
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B

Rationale: Peer educators can effectively influence behaviors by sharing experiences and serving as trusted sources.

11.
Which intervention is best suited for reducing tobacco use in a population?
A. Mass media campaigns only
B. Tax increases, advertising bans, and cessation support
C. Clinical treatment of lung cancer
D. Ignoring smoking behaviors
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B

Rationale: Combining policy (taxes), regulation (advertising bans), and support (cessation programs) produces the greatest impact.

12.
What is the significance of the Ottawa Charter in health promotion?
A. It focuses solely on medical treatment
B. It identifies key action areas for health promotion globally
C. It ignores social aspects of health
D. It supports only individual responsibility
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B

Rationale: The Ottawa Charter outlines five priority areas including building healthy public policy and strengthening community action.

13.
Which of the following best describes a “health literacy” approach?
A. Ensuring individuals can access, understand, and use health information
B. Providing complex medical terminology
C. Assuming all populations have the same understanding
D. Limiting information to professionals only
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: A

Rationale: Health literacy empowers people to make informed decisions by improving their ability to comprehend and use health information.

14.
Which group is most likely to benefit from targeted health promotion interventions?
A. High-income urban populations
B. Vulnerable and marginalized groups
C. Only elderly people
D. Only children
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B

Rationale: Targeted interventions address disparities and reach groups with greater health risks or less access to services.

15.
Which strategy best addresses obesity at the community level?
A. Personal dieting only
B. Increasing access to healthy foods and physical activity spaces
C. Prescribing weight loss drugs only
D. Ignoring environmental factors
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B

Rationale: Environmental changes support sustainable healthy behaviors more effectively than individual efforts alone.

16.
What is the key focus of “empowerment” in health promotion?
A. Controlling individuals’ behaviors
B. Enabling people to take control over their health decisions
C. Providing only medical care
D. Reducing personal responsibility
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B

Rationale: Empowerment involves increasing individual and community capacity to make choices and transform those choices into desired actions.

17.
Which setting is commonly used for health promotion activities?
A. Only hospitals
B. Schools, workplaces, and community centers
C. Isolated medical clinics only
D. None of the above
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B

Rationale: Schools, workplaces, and community centers offer access to groups in natural settings for effective health promotion.

18.
Which of the following is an example of secondary prevention?
A. Health education on healthy eating
B. Mammography screening for breast cancer
C. Vaccination against HPV
D. Rehabilitation after stroke
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B

Rationale: Secondary prevention focuses on early detection and prompt treatment to reduce severity.

19.
What is a major barrier to effective health promotion in low-income communities?
A. High health literacy
B. Limited access to resources and information
C. Overabundance of health services
D. Excessive government funding
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B

Rationale: Low-income communities often lack access to necessary resources, limiting their ability to engage in health-promoting behaviors.

20.
Which of the following best describes the ecological model of health?
A. Focus on individual behavior only
B. Considers multiple levels including individual, interpersonal, community, and policy
C. Emphasizes genetics exclusively
D. Ignores environmental factors
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B

Rationale: The ecological model recognizes that health is influenced by factors at many levels, from personal to societal.

21.
What is a key feature of a successful health promotion campaign?
A. Ignoring community input
B. Clear, consistent messaging across multiple channels
C. Using medical terminology without explanation
D. Randomly chosen messages
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B

Rationale: Consistency and clarity improve comprehension and retention, essential for behavior change.

22.
Which of the following is NOT a principle of health promotion?
A. Participation
B. Holistic approach
C. Disease diagnosis
D. Equity
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: C

Rationale: Health promotion focuses on preventing illness and enhancing well-being, rather than diagnosing diseases.

23.
Which health promotion intervention targets individual knowledge and skills?
A. Policy development
B. Health education
C. Environmental restructuring
D. Legislation
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B

Rationale: Health education aims to increase knowledge and skills for health decision-making at the individual level.

24.
Which is the most appropriate evaluation method for long-term health promotion impact?
A. Short-term surveys only
B. Longitudinal studies tracking behavior and health outcomes
C. Counting the number of flyers distributed
D. Feedback from staff only
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B

Rationale: Longitudinal studies provide insights into sustained behavior changes and health improvements over time.

25.
Which of the following is an example of tertiary prevention?
A. Vaccination
B. Physical therapy after a stroke
C. Smoking cessation counseling
D. Routine health screening
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B

Rationale: Tertiary prevention aims to reduce disability and improve quality of life after disease has occurred.

Section 2: Implementation & Sustainability

25 Marks
26.
How does cultural competence improve health promotion?
A. By ignoring cultural differences
B. By tailoring interventions to cultural norms and values
C. By using a one-size-fits-all approach
D. By focusing only on medical facts
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B

Rationale: Cultural competence enhances relevance and acceptability of health messages, improving outcomes.

27.
Which health promotion activity would be most effective in a school setting?
A. Providing healthy meals and nutrition education
B. Offering only sports facilities
C. Delivering medical care only
D. Banning all physical activity
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: A

Rationale: Combining healthy meals with education addresses both behavior and environment to improve student health.

28.
What is the role of policy in health promotion?
A. To restrict health information
B. To create environments that support healthy choices
C. To eliminate healthcare access
D. To enforce medical treatment
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B

Rationale: Policies like tobacco bans and food labeling facilitate healthier choices on a broad scale.

29.
What is “motivational interviewing” used for in health promotion?
A. Diagnosing disease
B. Enhancing intrinsic motivation for behavior change
C. Administering medications
D. Evaluating health programs
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B

Rationale: Motivational interviewing is a counseling method that helps individuals resolve ambivalence and commit to change.

30.
Which method best increases community participation in health promotion?
A. Imposing health programs without input
B. Engaging community leaders and stakeholders
C. Delivering lectures only
D. Ignoring community culture
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B

Rationale: Involving leaders and stakeholders fosters ownership and sustainability of interventions.

31.
Which of the following is a health promotion tool aimed at raising awareness?
A. Mass media campaigns
B. Surgical interventions
C. Medication distribution
D. Laboratory testing
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: A

Rationale: Mass media effectively spreads health messages to large audiences, increasing awareness.

32.
What does “capacity building” refer to in health promotion?
A. Increasing individual and community abilities to manage health
B. Providing hospital beds only
C. Training doctors exclusively
D. Limiting access to health information
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: A

Rationale: Capacity building empowers people and communities to identify and address their health needs.

33.
Which is the best example of an environmental health promotion strategy?
A. Improving air quality through regulations
B. Prescribing medications
C. Providing health education only
D. Hospital-based treatments
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: A

Rationale: Environmental strategies reduce exposure to hazards and improve overall community health.

34.
Which of the following best defines "preventive health behavior"?
A. Actions taken to detect and treat illness
B. Actions taken to avoid disease before it occurs
C. Medical treatment after diagnosis
D. Rehabilitation after injury
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B

Rationale: Preventive behaviors like vaccination and exercise reduce risk of disease occurrence.

35.
What is a limitation of health education if used alone?
A. It always leads to behavior change
B. It may increase knowledge but not change behavior
C. It is the only needed strategy
D. It ignores information dissemination
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B

Rationale: Knowledge alone may not lead to action without supportive environments and motivation.

36.
Which of the following is a characteristic of a health-promoting school?
A. Restricts physical activity
B. Integrates health education in curriculum
C. Ignores student health needs
D. Focuses only on academics
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B

Rationale: Health-promoting schools combine education, environment, and services to support student health.

37.
Which approach is key to reducing health disparities?
A. Universal health promotion ignoring differences
B. Targeted interventions for vulnerable populations
C. Focusing only on affluent groups
D. Restricting access to services
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B

Rationale: Tailoring interventions addresses specific needs and barriers in disadvantaged groups.

38.
What is the most important factor in sustaining health promotion programs?
A. Short-term funding
B. Community ownership and involvement
C. External expert control
D. Ignoring local culture
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B

Rationale: Programs with community ownership are more likely to continue and be effective long-term.

39.
Which of the following is a common health promotion outcome indicator?
A. Number of hospital beds
B. Changes in smoking prevalence
C. Number of surgeries performed
D. Prescription rates
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B

Rationale: Measuring behavior changes such as smoking rates reflects health promotion impact.

40.
What is an example of an interpersonal level intervention in health promotion?
A. Mass media campaign
B. Family counseling and support groups
C. Environmental policy change
D. National legislation
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B

Rationale: Interpersonal interventions focus on relationships and social support influencing health behaviors.

41.
What is the first step in planning a health promotion program?
A. Implementation
B. Needs assessment
C. Evaluation
D. Resource allocation
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B

Rationale: Understanding the health needs and priorities guides program design and focus.

42.
Which strategy helps overcome health literacy barriers?
A. Using complex language
B. Providing information in simple, clear formats
C. Assuming all individuals understand medical terms
D. Ignoring communication barriers
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B

Rationale: Clear, jargon-free communication improves comprehension among diverse populations.

43.
Which of the following best illustrates a policy-level health promotion activity?
A. Implementing a sugar tax to reduce consumption
B. Teaching handwashing in schools
C. Conducting health fairs
D. Providing individual counseling
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: A

Rationale: Policy changes, like taxation, affect population-level behavior by modifying the environment.

44.
Which principle is critical when developing health promotion messages?
A. Use fear appeals only
B. Tailor messages to the audience’s values and beliefs
C. Provide irrelevant information
D. Use only technical data
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B

Rationale: Tailored messages are more persuasive and effective in motivating behavior change.

45.
What does “sustainability” mean in the context of health promotion programs?
A. Programs ending quickly
B. Maintaining benefits and activities over time
C. Using only short-term funding
D. Ignoring community involvement
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B

Rationale: Sustainable programs continue to deliver health benefits beyond initial implementation phases.

46.

A. Individual smoking cessation counseling
B. Legislation banning indoor smoking in public places
C. Surgery for lung cancer patients
D. Hospital-based care
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B

Rationale: Population-based interventions target entire communities to prevent disease and promote health.

47.
What is the role of a health promoter?
A. Diagnosing diseases
B. Facilitating health education and community mobilization
C. Providing emergency medical care
D. Prescribing medications
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B

Rationale: Health promoters educate, advocate, and mobilize communities to adopt healthier behaviors.

48.
Which is a barrier to health promotion in rural areas?
A. Easy access to health services
B. Geographic isolation and limited resources
C. Overpopulation
D. Excessive healthcare funding
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B

Rationale: Rural areas often face transportation difficulties and lack of healthcare infrastructure.

49.
How can technology support health promotion?
A. By increasing misinformation
B. By delivering tailored health messages and tracking progress
C. By replacing all healthcare workers
D. By limiting access to health information
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B

Rationale: Technology like mobile apps and social media can personalize communication and monitor behavior change.

50.
What is the focus of “health in all policies” approach?
A. Limiting health considerations to healthcare sector
B. Integrating health considerations into policymaking across all sectors
C. Ignoring health in non-health sectors
D. None of the above
View Answer & Rationale

Rationale: The "health in all policies" approach acknowledges that health is influenced by policies across all sectors and aims to integrate health considerations into policymaking broadly.
