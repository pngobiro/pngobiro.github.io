Communication & Counseling

NCK, KRCHN, PAPER 2 - 1100 MCQS Answers and Rationales

Instructions

  • This paper consists of 50 Multiple Choice Questions focusing on Communication and Networking in Nursing.
  • Each question has four options; select the most appropriate one.
  • Detailed rationales are provided for each answer to facilitate effective revision.
  • Total Marks: 50 | Time Allowed: 60 Minutes.

Section 1: Foundations of Nursing Communication

25 Marks
1.
Which of the following best describes therapeutic communication in nursing?
A) Exchanging personal opinions with patients
B) A purposeful interaction to promote patient well-being
C) Sharing gossip about patients with colleagues
D) Using medical jargon when talking to patients
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B) A purposeful interaction to promote patient well-being

Rationale: Therapeutic communication is a purposeful interaction aimed at improving the patient’s emotional and physical well-being, promoting trust, and fostering understanding.

2.
What is the primary purpose of SBAR (Situation, Background, Assessment, Recommendation) in nursing communication?
A) To document patient care for legal reasons
B) To facilitate clear and concise information transfer
C) To write nursing care plans
D) To provide informal updates to patients’ families
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B) To facilitate clear and concise information transfer

Rationale: SBAR is a standardized communication tool that helps nurses and healthcare providers share critical information clearly and concisely, especially during handoffs or emergencies.

3.
Which form of communication is most effective for ensuring accurate exchange of complex clinical data?
A) Face-to-face conversation
B) Written documentation
C) Telephone conversation
D) Non-verbal cues
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B) Written documentation

Rationale: Written documentation provides a permanent, detailed record of complex clinical data, reducing misinterpretation and supporting continuity of care.

4.
Which barrier commonly affects effective communication between nurses and patients?
A) Using simple language
B) Active listening
C) Cultural differences
D) Maintaining eye contact
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: C) Cultural differences

Rationale: Cultural differences can create misunderstandings or discomfort, affecting communication effectiveness if not acknowledged and managed properly.

5.
In a nursing team, which type of communication is essential to avoid errors in patient care?
A) Gossip
B) Assertive communication
C) Passive communication
D) Aggressive communication
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B) Assertive communication

Rationale: Assertive communication promotes clear, respectful exchange of ideas and concerns, reducing errors and enhancing teamwork.

6.
What is the main purpose of networking among healthcare professionals?
A) To socialize outside of work
B) To improve patient care through collaboration and resource sharing
C) To compete for promotions
D) To avoid work responsibilities
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B) To improve patient care through collaboration and resource sharing

Rationale: Professional networking in healthcare facilitates sharing knowledge, resources, and support, leading to improved patient outcomes.

7.
When documenting patient care, which of the following is a key principle?
A) Including personal opinions
B) Using vague descriptions
C) Writing in chronological order
D) Omitting abnormal findings
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: C) Writing in chronological order

Rationale: Documenting care chronologically ensures clarity and helps trace the patient’s progress over time accurately.

8.
What does non-verbal communication in nursing primarily involve?
A) Spoken words
B) Written reports
C) Body language and facial expressions
D) Medical terminology
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: C) Body language and facial expressions

Rationale: Non-verbal communication includes body posture, gestures, facial expressions, and eye contact, which can convey emotions and attitudes.

9.
Which of these is the best example of active listening in nursing?
A) Interrupting to give advice
B) Nodding and summarizing the patient’s concerns
C) Checking the time while the patient talks
D) Changing the subject frequently
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B) Nodding and summarizing the patient’s concerns

Rationale: Active listening involves showing understanding through nodding, clarifying, and summarizing to confirm the patient’s message.

10.
Which communication method is considered most secure for sharing sensitive patient information?
A) Text messaging
B) Encrypted email systems
C) Social media platforms
D) Verbal discussions in public areas
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B) Encrypted email systems

Rationale: Encrypted email systems protect patient information confidentiality in compliance with privacy laws like HIPAA.

11.
What is the role of a nurse leader in facilitating communication within the team?
A) Ignoring conflicts to avoid confrontation
B) Encouraging open dialogue and resolving misunderstandings
C) Making unilateral decisions without input
D) Limiting communication to formal meetings only
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B) Encouraging open dialogue and resolving misunderstandings

Rationale: Nurse leaders promote open communication, facilitate collaboration, and address conflicts to ensure effective team functioning.

12.
Which of the following best describes a healthcare network?
A) A group of patients sharing similar diseases
B) A system of interconnected healthcare providers and services
C) An informal social club for nurses
D) A hospital administrative board
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B) A system of interconnected healthcare providers and services

Rationale: A healthcare network links various providers and services to coordinate patient care efficiently across settings.

13.
When communicating with a patient who has hearing impairment, the nurse should:
A) Speak loudly and quickly
B) Use written materials and clear visual cues
C) Avoid eye contact
D) Use medical jargon to simplify
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B) Use written materials and clear visual cues

Rationale: Written materials and clear visual cues help overcome hearing barriers, ensuring understanding.

14.
What is a key advantage of electronic health records (EHR) in nursing communication?
A) They eliminate the need for verbal communication
B) They allow instant access and updating of patient information
C) They are less secure than paper records
D) They limit data sharing among providers
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B) They allow instant access and updating of patient information

Rationale: EHRs improve communication by providing real-time access to accurate, up-to-date patient data across healthcare teams.

15.
Which of the following is an example of a closed-ended question in nursing assessment?
A) “How do you feel today?”
B) “Do you have any pain right now?”
C) “Can you describe your symptoms?”
D) “What concerns do you have?”
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B) “Do you have any pain right now?”

Rationale: Closed-ended questions typically elicit a yes/no or brief factual response, helpful for quick assessments.

16.
What is the most effective way for nurses to handle a communication breakdown with a colleague?
A) Avoiding further interaction
B) Discussing the issue openly and seeking resolution
C) Reporting immediately to management without discussion
D) Ignoring the problem hoping it resolves
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B) Discussing the issue openly and seeking resolution

Rationale: Open discussion helps identify misunderstandings and promotes collaborative problem-solving.

17.
Which type of communication is primarily involved when a nurse educates a patient about medication side effects?
A) Informal communication
B) Therapeutic communication
C) Aggressive communication
D) Passive communication
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B) Therapeutic communication

Rationale: Therapeutic communication supports patient understanding, adherence, and health outcomes through clear education.

18.
What should a nurse do to ensure confidentiality during patient communication?
A) Discuss patient information in the nurse’s lounge
B) Speak quietly in private settings and avoid sharing information unnecessarily
C) Share patient details on social media for awareness
D) Record conversations without patient consent
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B) Speak quietly in private settings and avoid sharing information unnecessarily

Rationale: Confidentiality is maintained by sharing information only when necessary and in private environments.

19.
Which of the following best represents a collaborative network in healthcare?
A) Independent nurses working in isolation
B) Healthcare professionals coordinating care through shared plans and meetings
C) Patients managing their care alone
D) Managers giving orders without consulting staff
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B) Healthcare professionals coordinating care through shared plans and meetings

Rationale: Collaborative networks involve professionals working together to plan and deliver comprehensive patient care.

20.
What is a common disadvantage of using email for nurse-to-nurse communication?
A) It provides a permanent record
B) It may delay urgent communication due to response times
C) It improves documentation accuracy
D) It is secure for all types of information
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B) It may delay urgent communication due to response times

Rationale: Email may not be suitable for urgent messages due to potential delays in reading or responding.

21.
When communicating with a non-English speaking patient, the nurse should:
A) Use a professional interpreter or translation service
B) Speak louder in English
D) Avoid communication until the patient learns English
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: A) Use a professional interpreter or translation service

Rationale: Using professional interpreters ensures accurate and respectful communication, reducing misunderstandings.

22.
Which of the following is NOT a function of nursing documentation?
A) Communicating patient status to the healthcare team
B) Legal evidence of care provided
C) Personal notes for the nurse’s memory only
D) Supporting billing and reimbursement
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: C) Personal notes for the nurse’s memory only

Rationale: Documentation is professional and objective, not personal notes; it serves communication, legal, and financial purposes.

23.
What communication style involves expressing needs and feelings while respecting others?
A) Passive
B) Aggressive
C) Assertive
D) Passive-aggressive
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: C) Assertive

Rationale: Assertive communication balances self-expression with respect for others, leading to effective interactions.

24.
Which wireless technology is commonly used in hospitals to enable nurse call systems?
A) Bluetooth
B) Wi-Fi
C) Infrared
D) NFC (Near Field Communication)
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: C) Infrared

Rationale: Infrared technology is often used for nurse call systems due to its line-of-sight communication and reliability indoors.

25.
Which of the following best defines a healthcare intranet?
A) The public internet accessed by nurses
B) A private, secure network used within a healthcare organization
C) A social media platform for patients
D) A wireless network accessible to the public
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B) A private, secure network used within a healthcare organization

Rationale: A healthcare intranet is a secure internal network facilitating communication and access to information within the organization.

Section 2: Advanced Communication & Feedback

25 Marks
26.
Which communication style is most effective when giving feedback to a colleague?
A) Aggressive
B) Passive
C) Assertive
D) Manipulative
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: C) Assertive

Rationale: Assertive communication respects both the speaker’s and listener’s feelings and opinions, promoting clarity and respect, which is vital for effective feedback in nursing.

27.
What is the primary purpose of a nurse using SBAR communication?
A) To escalate conflicts
B) To provide structured information
C) To avoid patient interaction
D) To delay decision-making
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B) To provide structured information

Rationale: SBAR (Situation, Background, Assessment, Recommendation) is a standardized framework designed to facilitate clear, concise, and structured communication among healthcare professionals, ensuring patient safety and efficient care.

28.
Which of the following is a barrier to effective communication in nursing?
A) Active listening
B) Cultural differences
C) Clear documentation
D) Empathy
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B) Cultural differences

Rationale: Cultural differences can create misunderstandings due to varying beliefs, languages, or customs, posing a significant barrier to effective communication in nursing.

29.
What is the most appropriate method to maintain confidentiality during nurse-to-nurse communication?
A) Discuss patient information loudly in the nurse’s station
B) Use secure electronic health records
C) Share patient details on social media for advice
D) Leave patient files open on the desk
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B) Use secure electronic health records

Rationale: Maintaining confidentiality is crucial; using secure, authorized platforms like electronic health records ensures information is shared responsibly, protecting patient privacy.

30.
When communicating with a non-English speaking patient, what should a nurse do first?
A) Use complex medical terminology
B) Speak slowly without checking understanding
C) Use a professional interpreter
D) Assume the patient understands basic English
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: C) Use a professional interpreter

Rationale: Using a professional interpreter ensures accurate communication, avoiding misunderstandings, and enhancing patient care quality.

31.
What role does nonverbal communication play in nursing?
A) It has no effect on patient care
B) It complements and reinforces verbal messages
C) It always contradicts verbal communication
D) It replaces the need for verbal communication
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B) It complements and reinforces verbal messages

Rationale: Nonverbal cues such as facial expressions and gestures enhance the meaning of verbal communication, improving understanding and rapport with patients.

32.
In networking among healthcare professionals, what is the primary benefit?
A) Reducing workload by delegating tasks
B) Improving patient care through collaboration
C) Increasing hospital bureaucracy
D) Limiting access to resources
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B) Improving patient care through collaboration

Rationale: Networking promotes sharing knowledge, resources, and support among professionals, which enhances teamwork and patient care quality.

33.
Which of the following is an example of active listening?
A) Interrupting to give advice
B) Nodding and summarizing what was said
C) Looking at a phone while someone is speaking
D) Changing the topic frequently
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B) Nodding and summarizing what was said

Rationale: Active listening involves giving full attention, providing feedback such as nodding, and summarizing to confirm understanding, which improves communication effectiveness.

34.
Which communication channel is best for urgent information between nurses?
A) Email
B) Written reports
C) Phone call
D) Monthly meetings
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: C) Phone call

Rationale: Phone calls allow immediate, direct communication, making them suitable for urgent matters, while emails and reports may delay responses.

35.
When documenting patient care, which of the following is most important?
A) Using subjective language
B) Writing legibly and accurately
C) Leaving out negative outcomes
D) Documenting only when convenient
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B) Writing legibly and accurately

Rationale: Accurate and legible documentation ensures continuity of care, legal protection, and clear communication among healthcare providers.

36.
What is an effective way to manage conflict during team communication?
A) Avoiding the person involved
B) Listening to all perspectives and seeking compromise
C) Ignoring the conflict hoping it resolves itself
D) Assigning blame to one party
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B) Listening to all perspectives and seeking compromise

Rationale: Effective conflict management requires understanding all sides and collaboratively finding a solution, which maintains team cohesion and patient safety.

37.
How can a nurse best support a patient who is anxious during communication?
A) Speaking rapidly to get information quickly
B) Maintaining a calm and reassuring tone
C) Avoiding eye contact
D) Using medical jargon
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B) Maintaining a calm and reassuring tone

Rationale: A calm tone helps reduce patient anxiety, builds trust, and facilitates clearer communication. Rapid speech and jargon may increase anxiety.

38.
Which of the following networking tools is commonly used for professional nursing communication?
A) Facebook
B) LinkedIn
C) Instagram
D) Snapchat
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B) LinkedIn

Rationale: LinkedIn is designed for professional networking and is used by healthcare professionals to connect, share knowledge, and collaborate.

39.
What should a nurse do when receiving unclear instructions from a physician?
A) Ignore the instructions
B) Follow the instructions as is
C) Clarify by asking questions
D) Ask a colleague to interpret
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: C) Clarify by asking questions

Rationale: Clarifying ensures patient safety and prevents errors by confirming understanding of instructions before proceeding.

40.
Which principle is essential when communicating bad news to patients?
A) Avoiding all eye contact
B) Being honest and empathetic
C) Using technical terms to sound professional
D) Giving the news abruptly
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B) Being honest and empathetic

Rationale: Delivering bad news with honesty and empathy supports patient dignity and emotional wellbeing, fostering trust.

41.
In nursing handoffs, which practice ensures effective communication?
A) Omitting irrelevant patient details
B) Using a standardized handoff tool
C) Sharing information informally only
D) Relying on memory

Correct Answer: B) Using a standardized handoff tool

Rationale: Standardized tools reduce errors by ensuring all critical patient information is consistently communicated during handoffs.

42.
What type of question encourages patients to express their feelings?
A) Closed-ended question
B) Leading question
C) Open-ended question
D) Rhetorical question
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: C) Open-ended question

Rationale: Open-ended questions allow patients to elaborate on thoughts and feelings, enhancing patient-centered communication.

43.
Which method best supports interprofessional communication?
A) Regular multidisciplinary team meetings
B) Individual emails only
C) Avoiding collaboration
D) Sharing patient info only verbally
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: A) Regular multidisciplinary team meetings

Rationale: Team meetings foster collaboration, shared decision-making, and comprehensive care planning.

44.
How should a nurse respond when a patient asks a question outside their scope?
A) Provide a detailed answer anyway
B) Refer the question to the appropriate professional
C) Ignore the question
D) Tell the patient to look it up

45.
What is a key component of therapeutic communication?
A) Giving advice unsolicited
B) Maintaining confidentiality
C) Changing the subject frequently
D) Interrupting to correct
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B) Maintaining confidentiality

Rationale: Confidentiality builds trust and safety, which are fundamental in therapeutic communication.

46.
When using email for professional communication, which practice is most appropriate?
A) Using informal language and emojis
B) Including patient identifiers
C) Keeping messages concise and clear
D) Copying all staff indiscriminately
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: C) Keeping messages concise and clear

Rationale: Professional emails should be clear and to the point to avoid misunderstandings and maintain professionalism.

47.
What is the best approach when a nurse disagrees with a physician’s order?
A) Ignore the order
B) Discuss concerns respectfully with the physician
C) Disregard the physician’s authority
D) Complain to patients
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B) Discuss concerns respectfully with the physician

Rationale: Open, respectful communication supports patient safety and professional collaboration.

48.
Which factor most influences successful nurse-patient communication?
A) The nurse’s attire
B) The environment and privacy
C) The patient’s age only
D) Time of day
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B) The environment and privacy

Rationale: A private, quiet environment reduces distractions and promotes open, honest communication.

49.
Which technique helps overcome communication barriers with a hearing-impaired patient?
A) Speaking louder without gestures
B) Using written communication and sign language
C) Ignoring their attempts to communicate
D) Talking only to family members
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B) Using written communication and sign language

Rationale: Adapting communication methods like writing or sign language ensures the patient understands and participates fully.

50.
What is the best way for a nurse to confirm understanding after giving instructions?
A) Asking yes/no questions
B) Using the teach-back method
C) Assuming the patient understands
D) Speaking faster
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B) Using the teach-back method

Rationale: Teach-back asks patients to repeat instructions in their own words, confirming comprehension and improving adherence.
