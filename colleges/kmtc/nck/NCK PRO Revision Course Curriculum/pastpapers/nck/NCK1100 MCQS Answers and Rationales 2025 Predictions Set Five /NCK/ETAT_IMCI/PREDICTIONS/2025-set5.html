ETAT & IMCI

Emergency Triage Assessment & Integrated Management of Childhood Illness

Instructions

  • This paper consists of 50 Multiple Choice Questions focusing on ETAT and IMCI protocols.
  • Each question has four options; select the most appropriate one.
  • Detailed rationales are provided for each answer to facilitate effective revision.
  • Total Marks: 50 | Time Allowed: 60 Minutes.

Section 1: Triage and Initial Assessment

25 Marks
1.
What is the primary purpose of ETAT in healthcare?
A) To manage chronic illnesses in children
B) To provide emergency care and triage for severely ill children
C) To vaccinate children against common diseases
D) To offer nutritional counseling
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B) To provide emergency care and triage for severely ill children

Rationale: ETAT focuses on rapid identification and management of life-threatening conditions in children to reduce mortality.

2.
In IMCI, which symptom is a key danger sign requiring urgent referral?
A) Mild cough
B) Persistent vomiting
C) Slight fever
D) Occasional sneezing
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B) Persistent vomiting

Rationale: Persistent vomiting in children can indicate severe illness needing urgent evaluation and referral in IMCI.

3.
According to ETAT guidelines, a child who is lethargic and unconscious should be:
A) Sent home with advice
B) Treated with oral antibiotics
C) Given immediate emergency treatment and admission
D) Monitored for 24 hours before decision
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: C) Given immediate emergency treatment and admission

Rationale: Lethargy and unconsciousness indicate severe illness requiring urgent ETAT emergency care.

4.
In IMCI, what is the recommended first step when a child presents with cough and difficulty breathing?
A) Assess respiratory rate and chest indrawing
B) Prescribe cough syrup
C) Refer for chest X-ray
D) Wait and review after 3 days
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: A) Assess respiratory rate and chest indrawing

Rationale: IMCI emphasizes assessment of respiratory signs to classify severity and guide treatment.

5.
Which is NOT a danger sign in the ETAT triage system?
A) Convulsions
B) Severe respiratory distress
C) Mild fever
D) Inability to drink or breastfeed
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: C) Mild fever

Rationale: Mild fever alone is not a danger sign; danger signs indicate severe illness needing urgent care.

6.
IMCI guidelines classify a child with a respiratory rate of 60 breaths/min as having:
A) No pneumonia
B) Pneumonia
C) Severe pneumonia
D) Asthma
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B) Pneumonia

Rationale: IMCI defines pneumonia in children aged 2-12 months as respiratory rate ≥50 and in older children ≥40 breaths/min.

7.
What is the main goal of the IMCI strategy?
A) To improve the quality of outpatient care for children under five
B) To provide adult emergency care
C) To manage chronic illnesses in adults
D) To increase vaccination coverage only
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: A) To improve the quality of outpatient care for children under five

Rationale: IMCI is designed to reduce child morbidity and mortality by improving outpatient child care.

8.
In ETAT, what action should be taken immediately for a child with severe respiratory distress and cyanosis?
A) Provide oxygen and call for urgent help
B) Administer oral fluids only
C) Monitor for 12 hours
D) Advise parents to watch at home
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: A) Provide oxygen and call for urgent help

Rationale: Severe respiratory distress and cyanosis require immediate oxygen and emergency interventions.

9.
According to IMCI, which sign indicates dehydration in a child?
A) Sunken eyes
B) Runny nose
C) Mild cough
D) Normal skin turgor
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: A) Sunken eyes

Rationale: Sunken eyes are a clinical sign suggesting dehydration, which IMCI assesses for fluid management.

10.
What is the priority treatment for a child identified with severe malaria during ETAT triage?
A) Oral rehydration
B) Immediate intravenous antimalarial therapy
C) Antibiotics
D) Routine immunization
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B) Immediate intravenous antimalarial therapy

Rationale: Severe malaria is life-threatening and requires urgent IV antimalarials as per ETAT protocols.

11.
In IMCI, which of the following is an indication for antibiotic treatment in a child with cough?
A) Cough lasting 1 day
B) Fast breathing or chest indrawing
C) Mild sneezing
D) Absence of fever
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B) Fast breathing or chest indrawing

Rationale: IMCI recommends antibiotics when pneumonia is suspected, indicated by fast breathing or chest indrawing.

12.
During ETAT triage, a child presenting with a prolonged generalized seizure should be:
A) Given oral glucose
B) Reassured and sent home
C) Treated urgently with anticonvulsants and emergency care
D) Monitored without treatment
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: C) Treated urgently with anticonvulsants and emergency care

Rationale: Seizures lasting longer than 15 minutes are a medical emergency requiring immediate treatment.

13.
IMCI recommends nutritional assessment primarily to:
A) Identify children at risk of malnutrition
B) Diagnose diabetes
C) Detect neurological disorders
D) Assess growth hormone deficiency
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: A) Identify children at risk of malnutrition

Rationale: IMCI includes nutritional assessment to improve outcomes by managing malnutrition.

14.
Which sign in a child indicates severe dehydration in IMCI?
A) Capillary refill >3 seconds
B) Normal urine output
C) Normal skin pinch test
D) Alert behavior
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: A) Capillary refill >3 seconds

Rationale: Delayed capillary refill indicates poor perfusion and severe dehydration requiring urgent treatment.

15.
ETAT guidelines recommend that a child with severe anemia be:
A) Observed for 24 hours
B) Given iron supplements only
C) Referred for blood transfusion
D) Treated with antibiotics
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: C) Referred for blood transfusion

Rationale: Severe anemia can be life-threatening and needs urgent blood transfusion.

16.
According to IMCI, when should a child with diarrhea be referred urgently?
A) Mild watery diarrhea
B) Presence of blood in stool and signs of dehydration
C) Occasional loose stool
D) Normal appetite
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B) Presence of blood in stool and signs of dehydration

Rationale: Bloody diarrhea and dehydration are danger signs requiring urgent referral.

17.
In ETAT, a child who cannot drink or breastfeed is classified as:
A) Having no emergency signs
B) A child needing emergency care
C) Stable and ready for discharge
D) Having mild illness
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B) A child needing emergency care

Rationale: Inability to drink is a danger sign indicating severe illness needing urgent treatment.

18.
IMCI emphasizes vaccination mainly to:
A) Cure existing infections
B) Prevent common childhood illnesses
C) Treat malnutrition
D) Provide nutritional supplements
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B) Prevent common childhood illnesses

Rationale: Vaccination is key in preventing infectious diseases targeted by IMCI.

19.
ETAT triage categories include:
A) Emergency, priority, and non-urgent
B) Mild, moderate, severe
C) Acute and chronic
D) High, medium, low risk
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: A) Emergency, priority, and non-urgent

Rationale: ETAT uses these categories to prioritize treatment based on severity.

20.
In IMCI, what is the first treatment step for a child with non-severe pneumonia?
A) Oral antibiotics
B) Intravenous fluids
C) Hospital admission
D) Immediate referral
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: A) Oral antibiotics

Rationale: Non-severe pneumonia is treated at home with oral antibiotics as per IMCI.

21.
Which of the following is a key danger sign in ETAT assessment?
A) Mild cough
B) Severe chest indrawing
C) Normal breathing rate
D) Slight fever
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B) Severe chest indrawing

Rationale: Severe chest indrawing indicates respiratory distress requiring emergency care.

22.
According to IMCI, fever in a child under 2 months is considered:
A) Non-serious
B) A danger sign requiring urgent assessment
C) Always malaria
D) Normal and not worrying
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B) A danger sign requiring urgent assessment

Rationale: Fever in neonates is an emergency and needs urgent evaluation.

23.
ETAT treatment for a child with severe dehydration includes:
A) Immediate oral fluids only
B) Intravenous rehydration
C) Antibiotics only
D) No treatment needed
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B) Intravenous rehydration

Rationale: Severe dehydration requires rapid IV fluids to restore fluid balance.

24.
In IMCI, the classification of “severe pneumonia” includes:
A) Fast breathing only
B) Chest indrawing and danger signs
C) Mild cough
D) No respiratory symptoms
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B) Chest indrawing and danger signs

Rationale: Severe pneumonia involves chest indrawing and other signs like inability to feed.

25.
Which of the following is NOT part of the ETAT emergency treatment?
A) Oxygen administration
B) Antibiotics for bacterial infections
C) Nutritional counseling only
D) Control of convulsions
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: C) Nutritional counseling only

Rationale: ETAT focuses on emergency treatment, not solely on nutrition counseling.

Section 2: Specialized Pediatric Management

25 Marks
26.
What is the primary danger sign in a child with severe pneumonia according to IMCI?
A) Chest indrawing
B) Mild cough
C) Fever only
D) Nasal congestion
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: A) Chest indrawing

Rationale: Chest indrawing indicates increased work of breathing and respiratory distress, a critical danger sign requiring urgent treatment in severe pneumonia under IMCI guidelines.

27.
In ETAT, which sign requires immediate referral to inpatient care?
A) Ability to drink
B) Convulsions lasting more than 15 minutes
C) Mild dehydration
D) Normal mental status
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B) Convulsions lasting more than 15 minutes

Rationale: Prolonged convulsions are a life-threatening emergency indicating possible severe neurological or systemic illness requiring urgent inpatient care.

28.
According to IMCI, how should a child with moderate dehydration be managed?
A) Immediate IV fluids
B) Oral rehydration solution (ORS) and increased fluids at home
C) No treatment needed
D) Restrict fluids
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B) Oral rehydration solution (ORS) and increased fluids at home

Rationale: Moderate dehydration is best managed with ORS to prevent progression and dehydration-related complications, as per IMCI protocols.

29.
What does the presence of lethargy or unconsciousness in ETAT indicate?
A) Mild illness
B) Severe illness needing urgent attention
C) Normal behavior for age
D) No concern
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B) Severe illness needing urgent attention

Rationale: Lethargy or unconsciousness signifies severe illness or possible brain involvement, requiring immediate triage and treatment in ETAT.

30.
Which antibiotic is first-line for treating non-severe pneumonia in IMCI?
A) Amoxicillin
B) Ciprofloxacin
C) Metronidazole
D) Vancomycin
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: A) Amoxicillin

Rationale: Amoxicillin is recommended as the first-line oral antibiotic for non-severe pneumonia in children under IMCI guidelines.

31.
Which sign does NOT require urgent ETAT referral?
A) Severe pallor
B) Fast breathing
C) Central cyanosis
D) Persistent vomiting
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B) Fast breathing

Rationale: Fast breathing alone, without danger signs, is classified as pneumonia or mild illness, managed outpatient in IMCI, but does not mandate urgent ETAT referral.

32.
In IMCI, how is wheezing with cough in a child best managed?
A) Antibiotics immediately
B) Bronchodilator trial
C) No treatment required
D) Surgery
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B) Bronchodilator trial

Rationale: Wheezing with cough suggests possible asthma or reactive airway disease; bronchodilator therapy is the initial management step in IMCI.

33.
What is the recommended action in ETAT when a child is unable to drink or breastfeed?
A) Advise home care
B) Initiate IV fluids urgently
C) Provide ORS at home
D) Wait and observe
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B) Initiate IV fluids urgently

Rationale: Inability to drink or breastfeed signals severe dehydration or illness; ETAT protocols require immediate IV fluid resuscitation.

34.
Which feature is classified as a "danger sign" in IMCI?
A) Cough lasting less than 2 days
B) Severe chest indrawing
C) Mild fever
D) Normal appetite
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B) Severe chest indrawing

Rationale: Severe chest indrawing is a critical danger sign indicating respiratory distress and severe pneumonia, necessitating urgent care.

35.
In ETAT, what is the next step for a child with severe anemia?
A) Immediate blood transfusion
B) No intervention needed
C) Oral iron supplements only
D) Observation only
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: A) Immediate blood transfusion

Rationale: Severe anemia can be life-threatening; ETAT guidelines recommend urgent blood transfusion to restore oxygen-carrying capacity.

36.
How does IMCI classify a child with cough and fast breathing but no danger signs?
A) Severe pneumonia
B) Pneumonia
C) No pneumonia
D) Urgent referral
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B) Pneumonia

Rationale: Presence of cough and fast breathing without danger signs is classified as pneumonia, manageable on outpatient basis under IMCI.

37.
According to ETAT, which is NOT an emergency?
A) Severe respiratory distress
B) Hypoglycemia
C) Mild dehydration
D) Shock
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: C) Mild dehydration

Rationale: Mild dehydration is not an emergency; it can be managed with oral fluids. Severe respiratory distress, hypoglycemia, and shock are urgent emergencies.

38.
What is the first-line treatment for diarrhea with no dehydration in IMCI?
A) IV fluids
B) Continue feeding and give ORS
C) Antibiotics immediately
D) Withhold fluids
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B) Continue feeding and give ORS

Rationale: IMCI recommends continued feeding and ORS for diarrhea without dehydration to maintain nutrition and hydration.

39.
In ETAT, how is hypoglycemia managed?
A) Wait and recheck later
B) Immediate administration of glucose
C) No treatment
D) Only monitor vitals
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B) Immediate administration of glucose

Rationale: Hypoglycemia is a medical emergency; ETAT stresses immediate correction with glucose to prevent brain damage and death.

40.
According to IMCI, which child requires referral to hospital?
A) Mild cough without danger signs
B) Child with convulsions
C) Mild diarrhea with no dehydration
D) Child with mild fever
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B) Child with convulsions

Rationale: Convulsions are a danger sign requiring hospital referral per IMCI guidelines for further evaluation and treatment.

41.
What is a key sign of shock in ETAT?
A) Normal blood pressure
B) Weak and fast pulse
C) Pink mucous membranes
D) Alert mental status
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B) Weak and fast pulse

Rationale: Shock causes poor perfusion, presenting as weak, rapid pulse, and requires immediate emergency treatment under ETAT.

42.
How does IMCI recommend managing a child with ear discharge and fever?
A) Antibiotics
B) No treatment needed
C) Surgery immediately
D) Antipyretics only
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: A) Antibiotics

Rationale: Ear discharge with fever suggests bacterial otitis media, for which antibiotics are recommended in IMCI.

43.
Which is NOT an ETAT triage category?
A) Emergency
B) Priority
C) Routine
D) Chronic
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: D) Chronic

Rationale: ETAT categories include Emergency, Priority, and Routine; "Chronic" is not a triage category.

44.
What does a persistent vomiting in a child with diarrhea indicate in IMCI?
A) No concern
B) Possible dehydration requiring urgent attention
C) Allergy only
D) Normal symptom
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B) Possible dehydration requiring urgent attention

Rationale: Persistent vomiting limits fluid intake and indicates risk of dehydration, a danger sign warranting urgent treatment.

45.
In ETAT, which is a sign of severe respiratory distress?
A) Mild cough
B) Grunting and nasal flaring
C) Normal breathing rate
D) No cyanosis
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B) Grunting and nasal flaring

Rationale: Grunting and nasal flaring are signs of severe respiratory distress indicating urgent need for oxygen and treatment.

46.
According to IMCI, when should antibiotics be given for diarrhea?
A) Always
B) Only if bloody diarrhea or dysentery
C) Never
D) For mild diarrhea only
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B) Only if bloody diarrhea or dysentery

Rationale: Antibiotics are reserved for bloody diarrhea or dysentery per IMCI to prevent antibiotic misuse and resistance.

47.
What is a danger sign for severe malnutrition in IMCI?
A) Mild weight loss
B) Edema of both feet
C) Normal weight
D) Good appetite
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B) Edema of both feet

Rationale: Bilateral pitting edema indicates kwashiorkor, a severe form of malnutrition needing urgent treatment.

48.
In ETAT, what immediate intervention is needed for a child with airway obstruction?
A) Position airway and suction if needed
B) Wait for spontaneous improvement
C) Start IV antibiotics
D) Give oral fluids
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: A) Position airway and suction if needed

Rationale: Airway obstruction is life-threatening; ETAT protocols require immediate airway management including positioning and suction.

49.
According to IMCI, what is the recommended action for a child with fever and stiff neck?
A) Treat at home with paracetamol
B) Urgent referral for possible meningitis
C) No concern if fever resolves
D) Only give fluids
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B) Urgent referral for possible meningitis

Rationale: Fever with stiff neck is a danger sign suggesting meningitis, requiring immediate hospital referral.

50.
Which of the following is NOT a component of ETAT?
A) Emergency triage
B) Assessment
C) Treatment
D) Routine immunization
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: D) Routine immunization

Rationale: ETAT focuses on Emergency triage, Assessment, and Treatment; routine immunization is outside its scope.
