Instructions

Section 1: Core Concepts & Theories

25 Marks
1.
What is the primary focus of cognitive psychology?
A) Unconscious motives
B) Observable behavior
C) Mental processes like thinking and memory
D) Social interactions
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: C) Mental processes like thinking and memory

Rationale: Cognitive psychology studies internal mental processes such as perception, memory, problem-solving, and decision-making, distinguishing it from behavioral or psychoanalytic approaches.

2.
Which psychologist is best known for the hierarchy of needs theory?
A) Sigmund Freud
B) Abraham Maslow
C) B.F. Skinner
D) Carl Rogers
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B) Abraham Maslow

Rationale: Maslow proposed a hierarchy of needs, suggesting that human motivation progresses through levels from basic physiological needs to self-actualization.

3.
In classical conditioning, the neutral stimulus becomes a:
A) Unconditioned stimulus
B) Conditioned stimulus
C) Unconditioned response
D) Conditioned response
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B) Conditioned stimulus

Rationale: After repeated pairing with the unconditioned stimulus, the neutral stimulus gains the ability to elicit a response and becomes the conditioned stimulus.

4.
Which part of the brain is primarily responsible for regulating emotions?
A) Cerebellum
B) Hippocampus
C) Amygdala
D) Medulla
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: C) Amygdala

Rationale: The amygdala plays a key role in processing emotions such as fear, anger, and pleasure.

5.
Which theory explains behavior as a result of rewards and punishments?
A) Psychoanalytic theory
B) Social learning theory
C) Operant conditioning
D) Humanistic theory
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: C) Operant conditioning

Rationale: Operant conditioning, developed by B.F. Skinner, suggests that behavior is influenced by consequences, including reinforcements and punishments.

6.
The “Big Five” personality traits include all EXCEPT:
A) Openness
B) Neuroticism
C) Introversion
D) Conscientiousness
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: C) Introversion

Rationale: The Big Five traits are openness, conscientiousness, extraversion, agreeableness, and neuroticism. Introversion is considered part of the extraversion spectrum, not a separate trait.

7.
Piaget’s stage of cognitive development where abstract thinking begins is:
A) Sensorimotor
B) Preoperational
C) Concrete operational
D) Formal operational
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: D) Formal operational

Rationale: The formal operational stage (starting around age 12) involves the ability to think abstractly and reason hypothetically.

8.
Which defense mechanism involves attributing one’s unacceptable feelings to others?
A) Repression
B) Projection
C) Sublimation
D) Denial
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B) Projection

Rationale: Projection occurs when individuals defend themselves against their own impulses by attributing them to others.

9.
The process of observing and imitating others' behavior is called:
A) Classical conditioning
B) Modeling
C) Habituation
D) Shaping
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B) Modeling

Rationale: Modeling, a core concept in social learning theory, involves learning through observing others.

10.
What is the main function of the frontal lobe?
A) Processing sensory input
B) Coordinating movement
C) Executive functions like planning and decision-making
D) Regulating heartbeat and breathing
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: C) Executive functions like planning and decision-making

Rationale: The frontal lobe is involved in higher cognitive processes including problem-solving, planning, and impulse control.

11.
Which of the following is a positive symptom of schizophrenia?
A) Emotional flattening
B) Hallucinations
C) Social withdrawal
D) Apathy
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B) Hallucinations

Rationale: Positive symptoms are excesses or distortions of normal functions, such as hallucinations and delusions.

12.
Which term describes a mental set characterized by persistent, excessive worry?
A) Bipolar disorder
B) Generalized anxiety disorder
C) Post-traumatic stress disorder
D) Schizophrenia
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B) Generalized anxiety disorder

Rationale: GAD involves chronic anxiety and worry about multiple areas without a specific focus.

13.
The placebo effect demonstrates the importance of:
A) Biological factors
B) Expectancy and belief
C) Classical conditioning
D) Genetic predisposition
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B) Expectancy and belief

Rationale: The placebo effect shows that belief and expectation can influence physical and psychological outcomes.

14.
Which psychologist is associated with operant conditioning?
A) Ivan Pavlov
B) Carl Jung
C) B.F. Skinner
D) Albert Bandura
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: C) B.F. Skinner

Rationale: Skinner expanded on behaviorism through operant conditioning, focusing on consequences that influence behavior.

15.
In research, a variable that is manipulated by the experimenter is called the:
A) Dependent variable
B) Independent variable
C) Confounding variable
D) Control variable
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B) Independent variable

Rationale: The independent variable is the factor the researcher changes to observe its effect on the dependent variable.

16.
Which of the following best defines “intelligence” in psychology?
A) The ability to memorize facts
B) Adaptation to the environment through learning and problem-solving
C) Physical strength and endurance
D) The speed of reaction time
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B) Adaptation to the environment through learning and problem-solving

Rationale: Intelligence involves cognitive abilities to learn from experience, solve problems, and adapt to new situations.

17.
The id, ego, and superego are components of which theory?
A) Behaviorism
B) Psychoanalytic theory
C) Humanistic psychology
D) Cognitive psychology
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B) Psychoanalytic theory

Rationale: Freud’s psychoanalytic theory divides personality into the id (instincts), ego (reality), and superego (morality).

18.
Which brain imaging technique measures electrical activity?
A) MRI
B) PET
C) EEG
D) CT scan
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: C) EEG

Rationale: EEG records electrical activity of the brain, useful for studying brain waves and seizures.

19.
What is the term for the ability to understand another’s thoughts and feelings?
A) Sympathy
B) Empathy
C) Apathy
D) Altruism
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B) Empathy

Rationale: Empathy involves feeling and understanding another’s emotional state.

20.
Which psychological approach emphasizes free will and self-actualization?
A) Behavioral
B) Humanistic
C) Cognitive
D) Psychodynamic
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B) Humanistic

Rationale: Humanistic psychology focuses on personal growth, free will, and achieving potential.

21.
Which is NOT a stage in Erikson’s psychosocial development?
A) Trust vs. mistrust
B) Autonomy vs. shame
C) Concrete operational
D) Identity vs. role confusion
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: C) Concrete operational

Rationale: Concrete operational is a cognitive development stage by Piaget, not Erikson’s psychosocial stages.

22.
The “foot-in-the-door” technique is an example of:
A) Cognitive dissonance
B) Persuasion strategy
C) Social loafing
D) Operant conditioning
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B) Persuasion strategy

Rationale: This technique involves getting someone to agree to a small request first, increasing the likelihood of agreeing to a larger request later.

23.
The variable that researchers try to keep constant to prevent bias is called:
A) Dependent variable
B) Independent variable
C) Control variable
D) Extraneous variable
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: C) Control variable

Rationale: Control variables are kept constant to prevent confounding effects on the experiment’s outcome.

24.
What does “confirmation bias” refer to?
A) Seeking information that supports one’s beliefs
B) Avoiding making decisions
C) Changing beliefs based on new evidence
D) Forgetting important information
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: A) Seeking information that supports one’s beliefs

Rationale: Confirmation bias causes people to favor information that confirms their existing views, ignoring contradictory evidence.

25.
Which neurotransmitter is most associated with mood regulation?
A) Dopamine
B) Serotonin
C) Acetylcholine
D) GABA
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B) Serotonin

Rationale: Serotonin plays a key role in mood, sleep, and appetite regulation, often linked with depression.

Section 2: Social Psychology & Mental Health

25 Marks
26.
Which type of memory involves conscious recall of facts and events?
A) Procedural memory
B) Implicit memory
C) Declarative memory
D) Sensory memory
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: C) Declarative memory

Rationale: Declarative memory involves facts and events that can be consciously recalled, unlike procedural memory which involves skills.

27.
The “bystander effect” refers to:
A) Helping behavior increasing in groups
B) People being less likely to help when others are present
C) Increased awareness in emergencies
D) Personal responsibility increasing with more witnesses
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B) People being less likely to help when others are present

Rationale: The bystander effect occurs when individuals are less likely to offer help in an emergency if other people are present, often due to diffusion of responsibility.

28.
Which of the following best describes intrinsic motivation?
A) Motivation driven by external rewards
B) Motivation driven by internal satisfaction
C) Motivation caused by fear of punishment
D) Motivation influenced by social pressure
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B) Motivation driven by internal satisfaction

Rationale: Intrinsic motivation comes from within the individual, based on personal interest or enjoyment in the task itself.

29.
The term “neuroplasticity” refers to:
A) The brain’s ability to change and adapt
B) Degeneration of neurons over time
C) The brain’s chemical imbalance
D) Genetic influence on brain structure
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: A) The brain’s ability to change and adapt

Rationale: Neuroplasticity is the brain’s capacity to reorganize and form new neural connections throughout life, especially after injury or learning.

30.
Which concept describes learning that occurs without direct reinforcement?
A) Classical conditioning
B) Operant conditioning
C) Latent learning
D) Observational learning
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: C) Latent learning

Rationale: Latent learning occurs without obvious reinforcement and may not be demonstrated until there is motivation to perform the behavior.

31.
Which psychological test measures personality traits?
A) MMPI
B) IQ test
C) PET scan
D) Rorschach inkblot test
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: A) MMPI

Rationale: The Minnesota Multiphasic Personality Inventory (MMPI) is a widely used test to assess personality structure and psychopathology.

32.
Which stage in Kohlberg’s moral development is characterized by obeying rules to avoid punishment?
A) Pre-conventional
B) Conventional
C) Post-conventional
D) Autonomous
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: A) Pre-conventional

Rationale: At the pre-conventional level, moral reasoning is based on avoiding punishment and gaining rewards.

33.
The primary purpose of the placebo in research is to:
A) Increase the drug’s effectiveness
B) Control for the psychological effects of treatment
C) Provide an alternative therapy
D) Ensure participants are aware of their treatment
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B) Control for the psychological effects of treatment

Rationale: Placebos help isolate the physiological effects of the drug by controlling for participants’ expectations.

34.
Which disorder is characterized by mood swings between mania and depression?
A) Major depressive disorder
B) Bipolar disorder
C) Schizophrenia
D) Generalized anxiety disorder
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B) Bipolar disorder

Rationale: Bipolar disorder involves alternating episodes of mania (elevated mood) and depression.

35.
The concept of “learned helplessness” was first demonstrated in:
A) Humans in social settings
B) Animals exposed to uncontrollable shocks
C) Children with cognitive delays
D) Patients with anxiety disorders
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B) Animals exposed to uncontrollable shocks

Rationale: Learned helplessness occurs when subjects exposed to uncontrollable adverse stimuli stop trying to avoid them, demonstrated originally in dogs.

36.
Which part of the nervous system is activated during a “fight or flight” response?
A) Parasympathetic nervous system
B) Central nervous system
C) Sympathetic nervous system
D) Somatic nervous system
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: C) Sympathetic nervous system

Rationale: The sympathetic nervous system prepares the body to respond to stress by increasing heart rate, blood pressure, and energy availability.

37.
What type of psychologist would most likely treat patients with mental disorders?
A) Clinical psychologist
B) Developmental psychologist
C) Cognitive psychologist
D) Experimental psychologist
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: A) Clinical psychologist

Rationale: Clinical psychologists diagnose and treat mental, emotional, and behavioral disorders.

38.
Which of the following is NOT a symptom of post-traumatic stress disorder (PTSD)?
A) Flashbacks
B) Avoidance behavior
C) Mania
D) Hypervigilance
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: C) Mania

Rationale: Mania is associated with bipolar disorder, not PTSD, which involves flashbacks, avoidance, and hypervigilance after trauma.

39.
Which method is best for establishing cause-and-effect relationships?
A) Correlational study
B) Case study
C) Experimental research
D) Survey research
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: C) Experimental research

Rationale: Experiments manipulate variables and control conditions to determine causal relationships.

40.
The term “schema” in psychology refers to:
A) A mental framework for organizing knowledge
B) A neurotransmitter involved in learning
C) A specific brain region controlling memory
D) A behavioral reflex
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: A) A mental framework for organizing knowledge

Rationale: Schemas help individuals interpret information by providing cognitive frameworks.

41.
Which of the following is an example of positive reinforcement?
A) Removing a chore after good behavior
B) Giving praise after completing homework
C) Ignoring bad behavior
D) Taking away a privilege for misbehavior
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B) Giving praise after completing homework

Rationale: Positive reinforcement adds a pleasant stimulus (praise) to increase the likelihood of a behavior.

42.
Which theory suggests that personality develops through the interaction of traits, environment, and behavior?
A) Trait theory
B) Social-cognitive theory
C) Psychoanalytic theory
D) Humanistic theory
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B) Social-cognitive theory

Rationale: Bandura’s social-cognitive theory emphasizes the reciprocal interaction between personal factors, behavior, and environment.

43.
The psychological term for the gradual weakening of a conditioned response is:
A) Extinction
B) Generalization
C) Discrimination
D) Habituation
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: A) Extinction

Rationale: Extinction occurs when the conditioned stimulus is repeatedly presented without the unconditioned stimulus, reducing the conditioned response.

44.
Which type of memory has the shortest duration?
A) Long-term memory
B) Working memory
C) Sensory memory
D) Episodic memory
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: C) Sensory memory

Rationale: Sensory memory holds information for a very brief period (milliseconds to seconds) before it is either discarded or passed to short-term memory.

45.
The Rorschach inkblot test is classified as a:
A) Objective personality test
B) Projective personality test
C) Intelligence test
D) Neuropsychological assessment
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B) Projective personality test

Rationale: The Rorschach test uses ambiguous stimuli to reveal unconscious aspects of personality.

46.
Which of the following best defines “cognitive dissonance”?
A) Learning by observation
B) Holding conflicting beliefs causing discomfort
C) Rapid memory loss
D) A developmental disorder
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B) Holding conflicting beliefs causing discomfort

Rationale: Cognitive dissonance occurs when an individual experiences psychological discomfort from contradictory attitudes or behaviors.

47.
Which hormone is commonly associated with stress?
A) Dopamine
B) Serotonin
C) Cortisol
D) Melatonin
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: C) Cortisol

Rationale: Cortisol is released by the adrenal glands during stress and helps mobilize energy.

48.
Which of the following best explains “confirmation bias”?
A) Remembering information that supports your beliefs
B) Forgetting important details
C) Changing opinions after new evidence
D) Avoiding decision-making
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: A) Remembering information that supports your beliefs

Rationale: Confirmation bias leads individuals to seek or recall information that confirms their existing beliefs.

49.
A psychologist studying how cultural differences influence behavior is most likely working in the field of:
A) Clinical psychology
B) Cultural psychology
C) Cognitive psychology
D) Neuropsychology
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B) Cultural psychology

Rationale: Cultural psychology examines how cultural contexts shape behaviors, thoughts, and emotions.

50.
Which factor most affects the quality of life in people with special needs?
A) Level of social support and inclusion
B) Access to entertainment only
C) Isolation from community
D) Ignoring medical needs
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: A) Level of social support and inclusion

Rationale: Supportive social environments greatly enhance wellbeing and participation.
