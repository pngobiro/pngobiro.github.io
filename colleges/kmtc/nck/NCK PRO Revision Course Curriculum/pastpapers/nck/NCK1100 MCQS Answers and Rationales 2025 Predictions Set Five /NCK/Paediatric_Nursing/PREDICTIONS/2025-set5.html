Section 1: Growth, Development & Immunization

25 Marks
1.
At what age should a baby start receiving the first dose of the measles vaccine?
A) At birth
B) At 6 weeks
C) At 9 months
D) At 12 months
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: C

Rationale: The first dose of the measles vaccine is typically given at 9 months of age to ensure adequate maternal antibody decline and effective immune response. At birth or 6 weeks, maternal antibodies may interfere with vaccine effectiveness.

2.
Which of the following is the most common cause of acute diarrhoea in children?
A) Rotavirus
B) Salmonella
C) Giardia lamblia
D) E. coli
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: A

Rationale: Rotavirus is the leading viral cause of acute diarrhoea in young children worldwide, especially under 5 years. Salmonella and E. coli are bacterial causes but less common in acute diarrhoea.

3.
What is the most appropriate first-line treatment for a child with mild dehydration?
A) Intravenous fluids
B) Oral rehydration solution (ORS)
C) Plain water only
D) Electrolyte tablets
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B

Rationale: Oral rehydration solution is the gold standard for mild to moderate dehydration as it replaces fluids and electrolytes effectively. IV fluids are reserved for severe dehydration or inability to take oral fluids.

4.
Which developmental milestone is expected around 12 months?
A) Walking independently
B) Sitting without support
C) Saying two-word sentences
D) Using a spoon
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: A

Rationale: Most children begin to walk independently around 12 months. Sitting without support typically occurs around 6 months. Saying two-word sentences and using a spoon generally develop later, between 18-24 months.

5.
What is the normal respiratory rate range for a 2-year-old child?
A) 20-30 breaths per minute
B) 30-40 breaths per minute
C) 40-60 breaths per minute
D) 60-80 breaths per minute
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B

Rationale: The normal respiratory rate for toddlers (1-3 years) is approximately 30-40 breaths per minute. Rates above this may indicate respiratory distress.

6.
Which vitamin deficiency causes rickets in children?
A) Vitamin A
B) Vitamin C
C) Vitamin D
D) Vitamin K
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: C

Rationale: Vitamin D deficiency leads to impaired calcium and phosphorus metabolism causing rickets, characterized by bone deformities. Vitamin A deficiency affects vision, C causes scurvy, and K causes bleeding issues.

7.
Which congenital heart defect is characterized by a "machine-like" murmur?
A) Atrial septal defect
B) Ventricular septal defect
C) Patent ductus arteriosus
D) Tetralogy of Fallot
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: C

Rationale: Patent ductus arteriosus (PDA) produces a continuous "machine-like" murmur heard throughout systole and diastole due to persistent communication between the aorta and pulmonary artery.

8.
What is the common cause of bronchiolitis in infants?
A) Influenza virus
B) Respiratory syncytial virus (RSV)
C) Adenovirus
D) Parainfluenza virus
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B

Rationale: RSV is the most common cause of bronchiolitis in infants, leading to inflammation and obstruction of small airways.

9.
Which of the following is a sign of increased intracranial pressure in infants?
A) Bulging fontanelle
B) Sunken eyes
C) Bradycardia
D) Hyperactive reflexes
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: A

Rationale: A bulging fontanelle is a key sign of increased intracranial pressure in infants. Sunken eyes indicate dehydration; bradycardia and hyperactive reflexes can occur but are less specific.

10.
What is the most appropriate immunization schedule for BCG vaccine?
A) At birth
B) At 6 weeks
C) At 9 months
D) At 12 months
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: A

Rationale: BCG vaccine is usually given at birth or shortly thereafter to protect against tuberculosis.

11.
What is the earliest sign of iron deficiency anemia in children?
A) Pale conjunctiva
B) Brittle nails
C) Spoon-shaped nails (koilonychia)
D) Fatigue
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: A

Rationale: Pale conjunctiva is one of the earliest clinical signs of anemia due to decreased hemoglobin. Spoon-shaped nails and brittle nails occur later; fatigue is subjective and harder to assess in young children.

12.
Which of the following is a classic feature of nephrotic syndrome in children?
A) Hematuria
B) Edema
C) Hypertension
D) Polyuria
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B

Rationale: Edema due to protein loss in urine and hypoalbuminemia is a hallmark of nephrotic syndrome. Hematuria is more common in nephritic syndrome. Hypertension and polyuria can occur but are less typical.

13.
What is the common causative agent of otitis media in children?
A) Staphylococcus aureus
B) Haemophilus influenzae
C) Streptococcus pneumoniae
D) Pseudomonas aeruginosa
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: C

Rationale: Streptococcus pneumoniae is the most common bacterial cause of otitis media. Haemophilus influenzae is also common but less frequent. Staphylococcus and Pseudomonas are less common in typical cases.

14.
Which test confirms cystic fibrosis diagnosis?
A) Sweat chloride test
B) Serum immunoglobulin levels
C) Urine electrolytes
D) Chest X-ray
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: A

Rationale: The sweat chloride test measuring elevated chloride concentration is the gold standard for diagnosing cystic fibrosis.

15.
Which is the most appropriate feeding method for an infant with cleft lip?
A) Breastfeeding only
B) Spoon feeding
C) Use of specialized feeding bottles
D) Delayed feeding until surgery
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: C

Rationale: Specialized feeding bottles help infants with cleft lip feed effectively by compensating for poor suction, whereas breastfeeding or spoon feeding may be difficult.

16.
Which of the following vaccines is given orally?
A) BCG
B) Oral polio vaccine (OPV)
C) Measles vaccine
D) DPT vaccine
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B

Rationale: Oral polio vaccine is administered orally; BCG, measles, and DPT vaccines are given by injection.

17.
What is the most common cause of neonatal jaundice within the first 24 hours?
A) Breast milk jaundice
B) Physiologic jaundice
C) Hemolytic disease of the newborn
D) Biliary atresia
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: C

Rationale: Jaundice appearing within 24 hours usually indicates pathological causes such as hemolytic disease. Physiologic jaundice appears after 24 hours.

18.
What is the recommended treatment for febrile seizures?
A) Immediate anticonvulsant therapy
B) Control fever and observe
C) Administer antibiotics
D) Initiate long-term anticonvulsants
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B

Rationale: Febrile seizures are generally benign; managing fever and observation is appropriate. Long-term anticonvulsants are not usually required.

19.
Which congenital anomaly is associated with maternal diabetes?
A) Neural tube defects
B) Cleft palate
C) Congenital heart defects
D) Clubfoot
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: C

Rationale: Maternal diabetes is strongly associated with congenital heart defects in infants.

20.
Which of the following is a sign of respiratory distress in a child?
A) Nasal flaring
B) Bradycardia
C) Hypothermia
D) Weak cry
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: A

Rationale: Nasal flaring indicates increased work of breathing and respiratory distress. Bradycardia, hypothermia, and weak cry are serious signs but not specific to respiratory distress.

21.
What is the main cause of iron deficiency in toddlers?
A) Excessive cow's milk intake
B) Poor appetite
C) Frequent infections
D) Genetic disorders
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: A

Rationale: Excess cow's milk can interfere with iron absorption and cause occult intestinal blood loss, leading to deficiency in toddlers.

22.
Which vaccine protects against Haemophilus influenzae type b?
A) DPT vaccine
B) Hib vaccine
C) MMR vaccine
D) BCG vaccine
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B

Rationale: Hib vaccine specifically protects against Haemophilus influenzae type b, which causes meningitis and epiglottitis.

23.
What is the common site of intramuscular injection in infants?
A) Deltoid muscle
B) Gluteus maximus
C) Vastus lateralis
D) Rectus abdominis
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: C

Rationale: The vastus lateralis muscle (anterolateral thigh) is preferred for IM injections in infants due to muscle size and safety.

24.
Which of the following is a sign of dehydration in infants?
A) Increased urination
B) Sunken eyes
C) Moist mucous membranes
D) Hyperactive reflexes
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B

Rationale: Sunken eyes are a classic sign of dehydration. Increased urination and moist membranes indicate good hydration.

25.
What is the first-line treatment for Kawasaki disease?
A) Intravenous immunoglobulin (IVIG)
B) Oral steroids
C) Antibiotics
D) NSAIDs only
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: A

Rationale: IVIG combined with aspirin is the first-line treatment for Kawasaki disease to reduce coronary artery complications.

Section 2: Clinical Disorders & Management

25 Marks
26.
Which of the following is a cause of failure to thrive in infants?
A) Congenital heart disease
B) Frequent vaccinations
C) Excessive feeding
D) Routine teething
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: A

Rationale: Congenital heart disease may cause failure to thrive due to increased metabolic demands and poor feeding tolerance.

27.
What is the appropriate first aid step for a child with a partial thickness burn?
A) Apply ice directly
B) Cool the burn with running water
C) Cover with adhesive bandage
D) Rub with oil
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B

Rationale: Cooling the burn with running water reduces tissue damage. Ice can cause frostbite; oils and adhesives can trap heat or contaminate.

28.
Which sign suggests pyloric stenosis in an infant?
A) Projectile vomiting
B) Diarrhea
C) Jaundice
D) Constipation
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: A

Rationale: Projectile vomiting in a 2-8 week old infant is classic for pyloric stenosis due to hypertrophied pylorus obstructing gastric emptying.

29.
What is the primary cause of sudden infant death syndrome (SIDS)?
A) Infection
B) Unknown multifactorial causes
C) Genetic abnormalities
D) Congenital heart disease
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B

Rationale: The exact cause of SIDS remains unknown but is believed to be multifactorial, including sleep environment and intrinsic vulnerabilities.

30.
Which of the following is NOT a symptom of asthma in children?
A) Wheezing
B) Coughing
C) Stridor
D) Shortness of breath
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: C

Rationale: Stridor is a high-pitched sound usually caused by upper airway obstruction, not typical in asthma, which produces wheezing.

31.
What is the main benefit of exclusive breastfeeding for the first 6 months?
A) Prevents all infections
B) Provides ideal nutrition and immunity
C) Promotes early tooth eruption
D) Ensures rapid weight gain
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B

Rationale: Exclusive breastfeeding provides balanced nutrition and passive immunity, protecting infants from many infections but does not prevent all.

32.
Which of the following is a sign of developmental dysplasia of the hip in infants?
A) Positive Ortolani test
B) Babinski reflex
C) Moro reflex
D) Tonic neck reflex
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: A

Rationale: The Ortolani test detects hip dislocation or instability in infants, aiding early diagnosis of developmental dysplasia of the hip.

33.
Which pathogen commonly causes meningitis in neonates?
A) Neisseria meningitidis
B) Group B Streptococcus
C) Streptococcus pneumoniae
D) Haemophilus influenzae
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B

Rationale: Group B Streptococcus is the leading cause of bacterial meningitis in neonates; N. meningitidis is more common in older children.

34.
What is the appropriate treatment for acute otitis media in children?
A) Watchful waiting for 48-72 hours
B) Immediate antibiotics
C) Antiviral therapy
D) Surgical drainage
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: A

Rationale: Mild cases can be observed for 48-72 hours as many resolve spontaneously; antibiotics are used if symptoms worsen or persist.

35.
Which is the primary reason for iron supplementation in infants?
A) Prevent rickets
B) Prevent anemia
C) Improve cognitive function directly
D) Treat infections
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B

Rationale: Iron supplementation prevents iron deficiency anemia, which is common in infants with insufficient dietary iron.

36.
What is the classic triad of symptoms in infantile botulism?
A) Constipation, poor feeding, hypotonia
B) Fever, rash, cough
C) Vomiting, diarrhea, dehydration
D) Seizures, high fever, irritability
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: A

Rationale: Infant botulism causes constipation, poor feeding, and hypotonia due to neurotoxin effects on neuromuscular junction.

37.
Which congenital infection causes cataracts, deafness, and heart defects?
A) Rubella
B) Cytomegalovirus
C) Toxoplasmosis
D) Herpes simplex virus
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: A

Rationale: Congenital rubella syndrome classically causes the triad of cataracts, deafness, and cardiac defects.

38.
What is the first-line therapy for neonatal hypoglycemia?
A) Intravenous glucose
B) Oral feeding with breast milk
C) Insulin therapy
D) Glucagon injection
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B

Rationale: For mild neonatal hypoglycemia, early oral feeding is first-line; IV glucose is reserved for severe or symptomatic cases.

39.
What is the characteristic rash in Kawasaki disease?
A) Maculopapular rash
B) Bullous rash
C) Petechial rash
D) Erythema multiforme
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: A

Rationale: Kawasaki disease causes a polymorphous maculopapular rash often involving the trunk and extremities.

40.
Which neonatal reflex is elicited by stroking the cheek?
A) Moro reflex
B) Rooting reflex
C) Babinski reflex
D) Grasp reflex
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B

Rationale: The rooting reflex causes an infant to turn their head toward the cheek stimulus, helping with breastfeeding.

41.
Which of the following is the best indicator of adequate hydration in infants?
A) Moist mucous membranes
B) Heart rate
C) Blood pressure
D) Skin color
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: A

Rationale: Moist mucous membranes reliably indicate hydration status. Heart rate and blood pressure can be late indicators in dehydration.

42.
Which is a common feature of congenital hypothyroidism in newborns?
A) Macroglossia
B) Hyperactivity
C) Early tooth eruption
D) Excessive sweating
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: A

Rationale: Congenital hypothyroidism presents with macroglossia, lethargy, and feeding difficulties, while hyperactivity and excessive sweating are not typical.

43.
What is the primary cause of neonatal sepsis?
A) Bacterial infection from birth canal
B) Viral infection from breastfeeding
C) Fungal infection from environment
D) Parasitic infection
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: A

Rationale: Neonatal sepsis most commonly results from bacterial infection acquired during passage through the birth canal.

44.
Which nutrient deficiency causes scurvy in children?
A) Vitamin A
B) Vitamin C
C) Vitamin D
D) Iron
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B

Rationale: Vitamin C deficiency leads to scurvy, characterized by bleeding gums, bruising, and poor wound healing.

45.
Which is the most appropriate management for a febrile infant under 3 months?
A) Immediate hospitalization and full septic workup
B) Antipyretics only at home
C) Antibiotics without investigations
D) Observation without intervention
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: A

Rationale: Febrile infants under 3 months are at high risk for serious bacterial infections; they require hospital evaluation and investigation.

46.
What is the most common site for a congenital diaphragmatic hernia?
A) Right posterolateral (Bochdalek)
B) Left anterior (Morgagni)
C) Umbilical region
D) Central tendon of diaphragm
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: A

Rationale: Bochdalek hernia, occurring posterolaterally on the left side, is the most common type of congenital diaphragmatic hernia.

47.
Which condition is associated with "salt-wasting" and dehydration in neonates?
A) Congenital adrenal hyperplasia
B) Down syndrome
C) Neonatal tetanus
D) Spina bifida
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: A

Rationale: Congenital adrenal hyperplasia due to 21-hydroxylase deficiency can cause salt-wasting leading to dehydration and electrolyte imbalance.

48.
What is the leading cause of neonatal death worldwide?
A) Prematurity and low birth weight
B) Neonatal sepsis
C) Congenital anomalies
D) Birth asphyxia
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: A

Rationale: Prematurity and low birth weight remain the leading causes of neonatal mortality worldwide.

49.
Which test is used to screen for phenylketonuria in newborns?
A) Guthrie test
B) Heel prick blood glucose
C) Urine dipstick
D) Serum bilirubin
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: A

Rationale: The Guthrie test is a newborn screening method to detect elevated phenylalanine levels indicating phenylketonuria.

50.
What is the common presentation of croup in children?
A) Barking cough and stridor
B) Wheezing and cyanosis
C) Persistent diarrhea
D) Seizures and fever
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: A

Rationale: Croup presents with a characteristic barking cough and inspiratory stridor due to upper airway inflammation.
