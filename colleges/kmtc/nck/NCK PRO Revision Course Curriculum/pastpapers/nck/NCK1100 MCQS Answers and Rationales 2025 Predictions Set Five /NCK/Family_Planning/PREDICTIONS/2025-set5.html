Instructions

  • Total Marks: 50 | Time Allowed: 60 Minutes.

Section 1: Contraceptive Methods & Mechanisms

25 Marks
1.
Which of the following is a long-acting reversible contraceptive?
A) Oral contraceptive pill
B) Intrauterine device (IUD)
C) Condom
D) Emergency contraception
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B) Intrauterine device (IUD)

Rationale: IUDs provide long-term contraception (3-10 years) and are reversible. Oral pills and condoms require frequent use, while emergency contraception is short-term and used after unprotected sex.

2.
What is the primary mechanism of combined oral contraceptive pills?
A) Blocking sperm motility
B) Preventing ovulation
C) Destroying sperm in the uterus
D) Thickening cervical mucus only
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B) Preventing ovulation

Rationale: Combined pills contain estrogen and progestin, which inhibit ovulation by suppressing the hypothalamic-pituitary-ovarian axis. Thickening cervical mucus is a secondary effect.

3.
Which family planning method also provides protection against sexually transmitted infections (STIs)?
A) Injectable contraceptives
B) Condoms
C) Oral contraceptives
D) Copper IUD
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B) Condoms

Rationale: Condoms are the only contraceptive method that provides significant protection against STIs by acting as a physical barrier.

4.
Which of the following is a contraindication for combined oral contraceptives?
A) Well-controlled hypertension
B) Migraine with aura
C) Mild anemia
D) Polycystic ovary syndrome (PCOS)
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B) Migraine with aura

Rationale: Migraine with aura increases the risk of stroke with estrogen-containing contraceptives, so they are contraindicated.

5.
The lactational amenorrhea method (LAM) is effective as contraception only if:
A) The woman has been breastfeeding for more than 6 months
B) The infant is exclusively breastfed, less than 6 months old, and amenorrheic
C) The mother supplements infant feeding with water
D) The mother has resumed menstruation
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B) The infant is exclusively breastfed, less than 6 months old, and amenorrheic

Rationale: LAM relies on exclusive breastfeeding and absence of menstruation to suppress ovulation, effective only up to 6 months postpartum.

6.
Which contraceptive method is considered permanent and irreversible?
A) Vasectomy
B) Injectable contraceptives
C) Copper IUD
D) Oral contraceptive pills
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: A) Vasectomy

Rationale: Vasectomy is a surgical sterilization procedure considered permanent, while others are reversible.

7.
What is the main side effect commonly associated with depot medroxyprogesterone acetate (DMPA) injections?
A) Weight loss
B) Irregular bleeding or amenorrhea
C) Increased fertility
D) Severe headaches
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B) Irregular bleeding or amenorrhea

Rationale: DMPA often causes changes in bleeding patterns, including irregular spotting or cessation of menstruation.

8.
Which of the following methods relies on fertility awareness?
A) Male condom
B) Calendar method
C) Oral contraceptives
D) Tubal ligation
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B) Calendar method

Rationale: The calendar method tracks the menstrual cycle to predict fertile days to avoid intercourse or use barrier methods.

9.
Emergency contraception is most effective when taken within:
A) 12 hours after unprotected sex
B) 72 hours after unprotected sex
C) 1 week after unprotected sex
D) 2 weeks after unprotected sex
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B) 72 hours after unprotected sex

Rationale: Emergency contraceptive pills are most effective if taken within 72 hours, with effectiveness declining over time.

10.
Which hormone is primarily responsible for thickening cervical mucus to prevent sperm penetration?
A) Estrogen
B) Progesterone
C) Testosterone
D) Follicle-stimulating hormone (FSH)
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B) Progesterone

Rationale: Progesterone causes cervical mucus thickening, making it difficult for sperm to enter the uterus.

11.
What is the recommended interval for replacement of the copper IUD?
A) 1 year
B) 3 years
C) 5-10 years
D) 12 years
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: C) 5-10 years

Rationale: Copper IUDs provide effective contraception for 5 to 10 years, depending on the type.

12.
Which family planning method can help reduce menstrual bleeding and cramps?
A) Copper IUD
B) Combined oral contraceptives
C) Male condom
D) Emergency contraception
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B) Combined oral contraceptives

Rationale: Combined oral contraceptives regulate menstrual cycles and reduce bleeding and dysmenorrhea.

13.
What is the failure rate of perfect use of male condoms?
A) 1%
B) 5%
C) 15%
D) 25%
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: A) 1%

Rationale: Perfect use of condoms leads to a failure rate of about 1%, though typical use failure rates are higher due to incorrect usage.

14.
What should be done if a woman misses two consecutive combined oral contraceptive pills?
A) Skip the missed pills and continue with the pack
B) Take the missed pills immediately and use backup contraception for 7 days
C) Stop the pills and wait until the next menstrual cycle
D) Double the dose next month
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B) Take the missed pills immediately and use backup contraception for 7 days

Rationale: Taking missed pills promptly and using backup methods reduces pregnancy risk.

15.
Which contraceptive method involves blocking the fallopian tubes?
A) Vasectomy
B) Tubal ligation
C) Copper IUD
D) Diaphragm
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B) Tubal ligation

Rationale: Tubal ligation is a surgical procedure that blocks or seals the fallopian tubes to prevent fertilization.

16.
Which of the following methods requires insertion by a trained healthcare provider?
A) Male condom
B) Oral contraceptive pill
C) Copper IUD
D) Fertility awareness method
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: C) Copper IUD

Rationale: Copper IUD insertion must be performed by a trained provider to ensure correct placement.

17.
The primary disadvantage of natural family planning methods is:
A) High cost
B) Requirement for strict adherence and daily monitoring
C) Side effects
D) Permanent infertility
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B) Requirement for strict adherence and daily monitoring

Rationale: Natural methods require careful daily observation of fertility signs and consistent abstinence or barrier use during fertile periods.

18.
Which hormone is found in most injectable contraceptives?
A) Estrogen
B) Progestin
C) FSH
D) LH
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B) Progestin

Rationale: Injectable contraceptives like DMPA contain progestin only.

19.
Which contraceptive method may increase the risk of pelvic inflammatory disease if used in women with multiple sexual partners?
A) Copper IUD
B) Male condom
C) Oral contraceptive pill
D) Injectable contraceptive
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: A) Copper IUD

Rationale: IUDs can slightly increase PID risk if an infection is present at insertion, especially in high-risk women.

20.
Which contraceptive method offers immediate protection once inserted?
A) Oral contraceptive pill
B) Copper IUD
C) Injectable contraceptives
D) Fertility awareness method
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B) Copper IUD

Rationale: Copper IUDs provide immediate contraceptive protection upon insertion.

21.
What is the primary advantage of male sterilization over female sterilization?
A) Reversible
B) Simpler and less invasive procedure
C) No need for anesthesia
D) Higher success rate
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B) Simpler and less invasive procedure

Rationale: Vasectomy is simpler and less invasive compared to tubal ligation, with quicker recovery.

22.
Which of the following is a barrier method of contraception?
A) Oral contraceptive pill
B) Diaphragm
C) Injectable contraceptives
D) Implant
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B) Diaphragm

Rationale: Diaphragms physically block sperm entry and are barrier contraceptives.

23.
Which contraceptive method should be avoided in a woman with a history of deep vein thrombosis (DVT)?
A) Progestin-only pill
B) Combined oral contraceptives
C) Copper IUD
D) Condom
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B) Combined oral contraceptives

Rationale: Estrogen in combined pills increases thrombosis risk; women with DVT history should avoid them.

24.
Which of the following is a reversible contraceptive method?
A) Tubal ligation
B) Vasectomy
C) Contraceptive implant
D) None of the above
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: C) Contraceptive implant

Rationale: Implants release hormones and are reversible once removed, unlike permanent sterilization.

25.
What is the main action of emergency contraceptive pills?
A) Terminate an existing pregnancy
B) Prevent or delay ovulation
C) Destroy fertilized egg
D) Prevent implantation
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B) Prevent or delay ovulation

Rationale: Emergency pills primarily prevent or delay ovulation to avoid fertilization.

Section 2: Clinical Management & Considerations

25 Marks
26.
Which method can be affected by antibiotics, potentially reducing its effectiveness?
A) Copper IUD
B) Combined oral contraceptive pills
C) Condom
D) Injectable contraceptives
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B) Combined oral contraceptive pills

Rationale: Some antibiotics can interfere with hormone metabolism, reducing pill effectiveness.

27.
The implant contraceptive is effective for how many years?
A) 1 year
B) 3 years
C) 5 years
D) 10 years
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B) 3 years

Rationale: Most implants provide contraception for up to 3 years.

28.
Which contraceptive method requires user action at or before each sexual act?
A) Oral contraceptive pill
B) Male condom
C) Injectable contraceptive
D) Implant
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B) Male condom

Rationale: Condoms must be used correctly every time during intercourse.

29.
Which of the following is NOT a hormonal contraceptive?
A) Copper IUD
B) Combined oral contraceptive pills
C) Injectable contraceptives
D) Contraceptive implant
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: A) Copper IUD

Rationale: Copper IUDs are non-hormonal and act by releasing copper ions toxic to sperm.

30.
What is the main reason to switch from combined oral contraceptive pills to progestin-only pills?
A) Desire for longer cycles
B) Breastfeeding mother
C) Increased risk of pregnancy
D) Weight gain
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B) Breastfeeding mother

Rationale: Progestin-only pills are safer during breastfeeding as estrogen can reduce milk supply.

31.
Which contraceptive method can cause delayed return of fertility after discontinuation?
A) Copper IUD
B) Injectable contraceptives
C) Male condom
D) Oral contraceptive pills
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B) Injectable contraceptives

Rationale: Fertility may be delayed several months after stopping injectable contraceptives.

32.
Which of the following methods is best for a woman wanting contraception with no systemic hormones?
A) Combined oral contraceptive pill
B) Copper IUD
C) Injectable contraceptives
D) Contraceptive implant
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B) Copper IUD

Rationale: Copper IUDs provide hormone-free contraception.

33.
Which of these family planning methods can be initiated immediately postpartum?
A) Copper IUD insertion
B) Combined oral contraceptive pills
C) Tubal ligation
D) Fertility awareness method
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: A) Copper IUD insertion

Rationale: Copper IUDs can be inserted immediately after delivery if no infection is present.

34.
Which family planning method requires reapplication or re-insertion every month?
A) Injectable contraceptives
B) Oral contraceptive pills
C) Contraceptive implant
D) None
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B) Oral contraceptive pills

Rationale: Pills must be taken daily to maintain contraceptive effect, but are dispensed monthly.

35.
Which of the following is a disadvantage of the rhythm method?
A) Requires no equipment
B) High failure rate due to irregular cycles
C) Protects against STIs
D) Can be used by breastfeeding mothers
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B) High failure rate due to irregular cycles

Rationale: Irregular menstrual cycles reduce the reliability of the rhythm method.

36.
What advice is given to women starting combined oral contraceptives to reduce nausea?
A) Take pills on an empty stomach
B) Take pills with food or at bedtime
C) Double the dose initially
D) Avoid water intake
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B) Take pills with food or at bedtime

Rationale: Taking pills with food or before sleep reduces gastrointestinal side effects.

37.
Which contraceptive method requires the woman to avoid using oil-based lubricants?
A) Male condom
B) Copper IUD
C) Injectable contraceptives
D) Oral contraceptive pills
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: A) Male condom

Rationale: Oil-based lubricants degrade latex condoms, increasing the risk of breakage.

38.
What is the mechanism of action of progestin-only pills?
A) Inhibit ovulation only
B) Thicken cervical mucus and inhibit ovulation variably
C) Destroy sperm
D) Prevent implantation
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B) Thicken cervical mucus and inhibit ovulation variably

Rationale: Progestin-only pills mainly thicken cervical mucus; ovulation inhibition is less consistent.

39.
Which of the following is a common side effect of copper IUD insertion?
A) Weight gain
B) Increased menstrual bleeding and cramping
C) Decreased libido
D) Hair loss
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B) Increased menstrual bleeding and cramping

Rationale: Copper IUDs may increase bleeding and dysmenorrhea, especially in the first months.

40.
Which contraceptive method should not be initiated immediately postpartum due to increased risk of thromboembolism?
A) Copper IUD
B) Combined oral contraceptive pills
C) Injectable contraceptives
D) Condoms
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B) Combined oral contraceptive pills

Rationale: Estrogen-containing pills are avoided postpartum due to increased thrombosis risk.

41.
Which contraceptive method can be used as emergency contraception?
A) Copper IUD
B) Male condom
C) Injectable contraceptive
D) Contraceptive implant
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: A) Copper IUD

Rationale: Copper IUDs can be used up to 5 days after unprotected intercourse as emergency contraception.

42.
What is the main purpose of fertility awareness methods?
A) Permanent contraception
B) Identify fertile days to avoid or plan pregnancy
C) Protect against STIs
D) Provide hormone replacement
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B) Identify fertile days to avoid or plan pregnancy

Rationale: Fertility awareness helps users recognize fertile days to manage conception.

43.
Which of the following methods requires refrigeration to maintain effectiveness?
A) Oral contraceptive pills
B) Injectable contraceptives
C) None of the above
D) Fertility awareness method
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: C) None of the above

Rationale: Most contraceptives are stable at room temperature; refrigeration is not needed.

44.
Which contraceptive method is associated with an increased risk of osteoporosis with long-term use?
A) Copper IUD
B) Injectable contraceptives
C) Oral contraceptives
D) Male condom
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B) Injectable contraceptives

Rationale: Prolonged use of injectable contraceptives like DMPA can reduce bone mineral density.

45.
Which family planning method allows immediate return to fertility upon discontinuation?
A) Injectable contraceptives
B) Oral contraceptive pills
C) Tubal ligation
D) Vasectomy
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B) Oral contraceptive pills

Rationale: Fertility typically returns quickly after stopping oral contraceptives.

46.
Which of the following is an advantage of the implant over injectable contraceptives?
A) Requires less frequent administration
B) Causes more irregular bleeding
C) More invasive insertion
D) Higher failure rate
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: A) Requires less frequent administration

Rationale: Implants last up to 3 years, whereas injections require every 2-3 months dosing.

47.
The copper IUD works primarily by:
A) Releasing hormones to prevent ovulation
B) Creating a spermicidal intrauterine environment
C) Blocking fallopian tubes
D) Thickening cervical mucus
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B) Creating a spermicidal intrauterine environment

Rationale: Copper ions are toxic to sperm, preventing fertilization.

48.
What should be done if a woman experiences severe abdominal pain after IUD insertion?
A) Reassure and monitor
B) Remove the IUD immediately without further evaluation
C) Evaluate for possible perforation or infection
D) Prescribe painkillers only
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: C) Evaluate for possible perforation or infection

Rationale: Severe pain post-insertion could indicate complications needing urgent evaluation.

49.
Which family planning method is most suitable for a woman with frequent vaginal infections?
A) Male condom
B) Copper IUD
C) Combined oral contraceptives
D) Fertility awareness method
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: A) Male condom

Rationale: Condoms reduce infection risk by preventing contact and can protect vaginal flora.

50.
Which of the following is a key counseling point for women using contraceptive implants?
A) They must take the implant daily
B) They may experience irregular bleeding
C) They should use condoms only
D) They cannot breastfeed
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B) They may experience irregular bleeding

Rationale: Irregular bleeding is a common side effect; women should be counseled to expect this.
