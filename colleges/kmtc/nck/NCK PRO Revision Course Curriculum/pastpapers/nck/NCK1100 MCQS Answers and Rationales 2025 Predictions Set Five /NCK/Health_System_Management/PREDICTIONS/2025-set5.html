Health System Management

NCK, KRCHN, PAPER 2 - 1100 MCQS Answers and Rationales

Instructions

  • This paper consists of 50 Multiple Choice Questions focusing on Health System Management principles.
  • Each question has four options; select the most appropriate one.
  • Detailed rationales are provided for each answer to facilitate effective revision.
  • Total Marks: 50 | Time Allowed: 60 Minutes.

Section 1: Governance and Financing

25 Marks
1.
What is the primary goal of a health system?
A) To maximize profits
B) To improve population health
C) To increase hospital admissions
D) To reduce staff workload
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B) To improve population health

Rationale: The main goal of any health system is to improve the overall health outcomes of the population it serves, not financial gains or administrative convenience.

2.
Which of the following is a key function of health system governance?
A) Resource allocation
B) Policy formulation
C) Patient treatment
D) Clinical diagnosis
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B) Policy formulation

Rationale: Governance in health systems involves setting policies, laws, and regulations to guide the functioning and priorities of the system.

3.
What does the term “health system financing” refer to?
A) Delivery of services
B) How money is collected and spent for health
C) Training of healthcare workers
D) Building infrastructure
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B) How money is collected and spent for health

Rationale: Financing encompasses mechanisms for raising funds, pooling resources, and purchasing health services.

4.
Which level of health system typically provides preventive care and basic treatment?
A) Tertiary care
B) Primary care
C) Specialized care
D) Quaternary care
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B) Primary care

Rationale: Primary care is the first level of contact in the health system and focuses on health promotion, disease prevention, and basic treatment.

5.
What is a major challenge in human resource management in health systems?
A) Oversupply of specialists
B) Inadequate workforce distribution
C) Too many administrative staff
D) Excessive funding
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B) Inadequate workforce distribution

Rationale: Many health systems face challenges with uneven distribution of health workers, especially in rural versus urban areas.

6.
Which health system model emphasizes universal coverage funded through taxation?
A) Beveridge model
B) Bismarck model
C) Out-of-pocket model
D) Private insurance model
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: A) Beveridge model

Rationale: The Beveridge model, used in countries like the UK, funds health services primarily through general taxation to provide universal access.

7.
What is the role of health information systems?
A) Provide clinical care
B) Collect, analyze, and use health data
C) Fund hospitals
D) Manufacture medicines
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B) Collect, analyze, and use health data

Rationale: Health information systems support decision-making by providing timely and accurate health data.

8.
Which component of the health system ensures availability of essential medicines?
A) Health workforce
B) Service delivery
C) Medical products and technologies
D) Governance
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: C) Medical products and technologies

Rationale: This component focuses on the procurement, distribution, and regulation of medicines and medical supplies.

9.
What does “decentralization” in health management imply?
A) Central control of all health decisions
B) Transfer of authority to local levels
C) Privatization of health services
D) Eliminating local health offices
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B) Transfer of authority to local levels

Rationale: Decentralization means shifting decision-making power closer to the community or district levels to improve responsiveness.

10.
Which of these is a key indicator for health system performance?
A) Number of hospital beds
B) Population health outcomes
C) Number of pharmaceutical companies
D) Hospital profits
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B) Population health outcomes

Rationale: The effectiveness of a health system is best measured by health outcomes like morbidity, mortality, and life expectancy.

11.
What is “task shifting” in human resource management?
A) Moving tasks to lower-level workers to improve efficiency
B) Changing staff shifts frequently
C) Reducing workload by hiring more doctors
D) Increasing administrative tasks for nurses
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: A) Moving tasks to lower-level workers to improve efficiency

Rationale: Task shifting reallocates duties from specialized professionals to less specialized workers to address workforce shortages.

12.
What is the purpose of a health system’s referral system?
A) To restrict patient access
B) To ensure appropriate care level for patients
C) To reduce hospital costs
D) To avoid use of primary care
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B) To ensure appropriate care level for patients

Rationale: Referral systems guide patients to the correct care level, improving resource use and health outcomes.

13.
What is the main advantage of electronic health records (EHR)?
A) Increased paperwork
B) Faster data retrieval and improved continuity of care
C) Higher printing costs
D) More staff needed
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B) Faster data retrieval and improved continuity of care

Rationale: EHRs facilitate efficient information sharing, reducing errors and improving care coordination.

14.
Which financing method involves patients paying directly at the point of service?
A) Social health insurance
B) Out-of-pocket payments
C) Government funding
D) Donor funding
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B) Out-of-pocket payments

Rationale: Out-of-pocket payments are direct fees paid by patients, often leading to financial hardship and barriers to care.

15.
What is the role of leadership in health system management?
A) Dictating treatment protocols
B) Setting vision, motivating staff, and overseeing operations
C) Conducting lab tests
D) Maintaining hospital buildings
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B) Setting vision, motivating staff, and overseeing operations

Rationale: Effective leadership guides the system toward its goals and ensures staff engagement.

16.
What is a major limitation of donor funding in health systems?
A) Unconditional and long-term
B) Often short-term and disease-specific
C) Controlled by local governments
D) Covers infrastructure costs only
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B) Often short-term and disease-specific

Rationale: Donor funding may be limited in scope and duration, which can affect sustainability of programs.

17.
What is “universal health coverage” (UHC)?
A) Free healthcare for all regardless of resources
B) Access to quality health services without financial hardship
C) Only emergency care coverage
D) Exclusive private insurance coverage
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B) Access to quality health services without financial hardship

Rationale: UHC aims to ensure everyone can obtain necessary health care without suffering financial ruin.

18.
Which tool helps managers monitor resource use and performance?
A) SWOT analysis
B) Budgeting and financial reports
C) Clinical guidelines
D) Staff attendance sheets
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B) Budgeting and financial reports

Rationale: Budgets and financial reports track expenditures and help manage resources efficiently.

19.
What is the main focus of health promotion in health systems?
A) Treating diseases
B) Preventing disease and encouraging healthy behaviors
C) Hospital administration
D) Pharmaceutical sales
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B) Preventing disease and encouraging healthy behaviors

Rationale: Health promotion aims to reduce disease burden by improving health literacy and behavior.

20.
How can a health system ensure equity in access to services?
A) Providing free care to the wealthy
B) Targeting vulnerable populations and reducing barriers
C) Building hospitals only in cities
D) Charging more for rural patients
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B) Targeting vulnerable populations and reducing barriers

Rationale: Equity involves making sure marginalized groups have access to necessary health services.

21.
What is “health system resilience”?
A) The ability to maintain operations during crises
B) Reducing staff workload
C) Increasing hospital beds
D) Investing only in urban areas
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: A) The ability to maintain operations during crises

Rationale: Resilience allows health systems to adapt and continue functioning during emergencies or shocks.

22.
What does “health system stewardship” involve?
A) Direct clinical care
B) Guiding and overseeing system policies and accountability
C) Manufacturing medicines
D) Providing health insurance
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B) Guiding and overseeing system policies and accountability

Rationale: Stewardship refers to responsible management and leadership of the health system.

23.
Which of the following is a health system output?
A) Number of patients treated
B) Prevalence of disease
C) Social determinants of health
D) Climate change effects
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: A) Number of patients treated

Rationale: Outputs are the direct results of health system activities like services delivered.

24.
What is a critical barrier to effective health system financing in low-income countries?
A) Excess government revenue
B) High out-of-pocket expenses
C) Oversupply of healthcare workers
D) Overuse of technology
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B) High out-of-pocket expenses

Rationale: Out-of-pocket costs deter people from seeking care and increase inequities.

25.
What strategy can improve health workforce retention?
A) Reduce salaries
B) Provide incentives and career development opportunities
C) Increase working hours without benefits
D) Limit training programs
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B) Provide incentives and career development opportunities

Rationale: Retention improves when health workers feel valued and see growth opportunities.

Section 2: Policy and Management Tools

25 Marks
26.
What is the main purpose of health policy?
A) To guide decision-making and resource allocation
B) To replace clinical protocols
C) To increase hospital size
D) To control pharmaceutical prices only
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: A) To guide decision-making and resource allocation

Rationale: Health policies create a framework for priorities, actions, and resource use in the health system.

27.
Which method is commonly used to assess health system efficiency?
A) Patient satisfaction surveys
B) Cost-effectiveness analysis
C) Counting hospital staff
D) Measuring hospital size
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B) Cost-effectiveness analysis

Rationale: This method compares costs and health outcomes to determine optimal resource use.

28.
How does health system integration benefit patients?
A) By providing fragmented care
B) By coordinating services across providers and levels of care
C) By limiting referrals
D) By reducing patient autonomy
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B) By coordinating services across providers and levels of care

Rationale: Integration improves continuity and quality of care, reducing duplication and gaps.

29.
Which of the following is an example of a public health intervention?
A) Performing surgery
B) Vaccination campaigns
C) Hospital administration
D) Clinical diagnostics
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B) Vaccination campaigns

Rationale: Public health interventions target population-level disease prevention and health promotion.

30.
Which financing approach pools risks and resources?
A) Out-of-pocket payments
B) Social health insurance
C) Direct billing
D) Charity funding
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B) Social health insurance

Rationale: Insurance pools financial risks across a population, protecting individuals from high costs.

31.
What role do community health workers play in health systems?
A) Specialized surgical procedures
B) Bridging communities and formal health services
C) Managing hospitals
D) Setting national health policy
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B) Bridging communities and formal health services

Rationale: They provide culturally appropriate education, basic care, and referrals.

32.
What is a major benefit of performance-based financing?
A) Increasing hospital bureaucracy
B) Incentivizing quality and efficiency improvements
C) Reducing healthcare coverage
D) Ignoring outcomes
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B) Incentivizing quality and efficiency improvements

Rationale: Linking funding to performance encourages better health service delivery.

33.
What does “health system decentralization” aim to improve?
A) Centralized decision-making
B) Responsiveness and local ownership
C) Uniform national policies only
D) Reducing local health budgets
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B) Responsiveness and local ownership

Rationale: Decentralization aims to tailor services to local needs and empower local managers.

34.
What is a major health system goal related to quality of care?
A) Increasing number of health facilities only
B) Ensuring safe, effective, and patient-centered services
C) Limiting patient feedback
D) Maximizing profits
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B) Ensuring safe, effective, and patient-centered services

Rationale: Quality care improves health outcomes and patient satisfaction.

35.
Which approach helps in identifying gaps and opportunities in health systems?
A) SWOT analysis
B) Patient discharge forms
C) Laboratory tests
D) Pharmacy audits
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: A) SWOT analysis

Rationale: SWOT assesses strengths, weaknesses, opportunities, and threats to inform strategic planning.

36.
How does effective supply chain management benefit health systems?
A) Increases stockouts
B) Ensures continuous availability of medical products
C) Raises costs unnecessarily
D) Limits access to medicines
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B) Ensures continuous availability of medical products

Rationale: Good supply chains prevent shortages and wastage of essential medicines and supplies.

37.
Which of the following is an example of a health system output?
A) Improved life expectancy
B) Number of vaccinations given
C) Socioeconomic status
D) Climate conditions
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B) Number of vaccinations given

Rationale: Outputs are direct products of health system activities, while outcomes reflect long-term impacts.

38.
Which stakeholder is primarily responsible for policy-making in health systems?
A) Health facility managers
B) Government and health ministries
C) Patients
D) Pharmaceutical companies
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B) Government and health ministries

Rationale: They set laws, regulations, and policies guiding the health system.

39.
What is a critical success factor in health system reform?
A) Ignoring stakeholder input
B) Strong leadership and stakeholder engagement
C) Cutting funding suddenly
D) Avoiding data collection
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B) Strong leadership and stakeholder engagement

Rationale: Reforms succeed when leaders guide the process and stakeholders are involved.

40.
How can technology improve health system efficiency?
A) Increasing paperwork
B) Streamlining data management and service delivery
C) Limiting access to information
D) Increasing costs without benefits
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B) Streamlining data management and service delivery

Rationale: Technology reduces errors, improves communication, and speeds up processes.

41.
What is the function of health system monitoring and evaluation?
A) Punishing staff
B) Assessing performance and guiding improvements
C) Building hospitals
D) Distributing medicines
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B) Assessing performance and guiding improvements

Rationale: M&E helps track progress and identify areas needing change.

42.
Which financing source is most likely to ensure sustainable health funding?
A) Donor funding only
B) Domestic taxation and insurance contributions
C) Out-of-pocket payments alone
D) Private donations only
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B) Domestic taxation and insurance contributions

Rationale: These provide steady funding less vulnerable to external shocks.

43.
What is the “triple aim” framework in health systems?
A) Cost, bureaucracy, and technology
B) Improving patient experience, health outcomes, and cost-efficiency
C) Building more hospitals, hiring staff, and buying equipment
D) Reducing salaries, increasing taxes, and cutting services
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B) Improving patient experience, health outcomes, and cost-efficiency

Rationale: The triple aim balances quality, population health, and cost control.

44.
What does “health equity” focus on?
A) Providing equal services to everyone regardless of need
B) Fair access to health services based on needs
C) Reducing total healthcare costs
D) Limiting services to urban areas
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B) Fair access to health services based on needs

Rationale: Equity addresses disparities by allocating resources according to need.

45.
Which of the following describes “vertical health programs”?
A) Integrated with all health services
B) Target specific diseases or populations
C) Decentralized management
D) Community-based only
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B) Target specific diseases or populations

Rationale: Vertical programs focus narrowly, e.g., on HIV or malaria control.

46.
What is a common effect of health system fragmentation?
A) Improved coordination
B) Duplication of services and inefficiency
C) Reduced costs
D) Stronger governance
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B) Duplication of services and inefficiency

Rationale: Fragmentation leads to poor coordination and wasted resources.

47.
What is the role of accreditation in health systems?
A) Punishing hospitals
B) Ensuring facilities meet quality standards
C) Reducing patient numbers
D) Increasing costs without benefit
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B) Ensuring facilities meet quality standards

Rationale: Accreditation promotes adherence to safety and quality benchmarks.

48.
Which of the following best describes “health system responsiveness”?
A) Speed of data entry
B) Meeting legitimate expectations of patients regarding care
C) Number of doctors employed
D) Hospital building size
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B) Meeting legitimate expectations of patients regarding care

Rationale: Responsiveness includes respect, confidentiality, and timely service.

49.
What is the impact of weak health information systems?
A) Improved data use
B) Delayed and inaccurate decision-making
C) Better resource allocation
D) Increased patient satisfaction
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B) Delayed and inaccurate decision-making

Rationale: Poor information systems hinder evidence-based planning and response.

50.
What is a key benefit of community participation in health systems?
A) Increased costs
B) Improved relevance and acceptability of services
C) More bureaucracy
D) Reduced accountability
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B) Improved relevance and acceptability of services

Rationale: Involving communities ensures services address local needs and gain trust.
