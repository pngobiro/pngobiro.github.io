Sociology & Anthropology

Social Sciences in Nursing Revision - Paper 2

Instructions

  • This paper consists of 50 Multiple Choice Questions focusing on Sociology and Anthropology in healthcare.
  • Each question has four options; select the most appropriate one.
  • Detailed rationales are provided for each answer to facilitate effective revision.
  • Total Marks: 50 | Time Allowed: 60 Minutes.

Section 1: Foundations of Social Science

25 Marks
1.
Sociology primarily studies:
A) Human biology and genetics
B) Human social behavior and institutions
C) Ancient cultures and artifacts
D) Psychological processes in individuals
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B) Human social behavior and institutions

Rationale: Sociology focuses on the study of social behavior, relationships, and institutions within societies.

2.
Which of the following is a key concept in anthropology?
A) Social stratification
B) Cultural relativism
C) Social roles
D) Deviance
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B) Cultural relativism

Rationale: Cultural relativism is the anthropological principle of understanding cultures on their own terms without ethnocentric bias.

3.
The term “norm” in sociology means:
A) A legal rule
B) An expected pattern of behavior
C) An individual's opinion
D) A scientific theory
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B) An expected pattern of behavior

Rationale: Norms are socially accepted rules or standards guiding behavior in a group or society.

4.
The process by which individuals learn and adopt the values, beliefs, and norms of their culture is called:
A) Enculturation
B) Assimilation
C) Acculturation
D) Globalization
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: A) Enculturation

Rationale: Enculturation is the lifelong process of learning culture and social norms.

5.
Which sociological perspective views society as a system of interconnected parts that work together?
A) Symbolic interactionism
B) Functionalism
C) Conflict theory
D) Feminist theory
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B) Functionalism

Rationale: Functionalism sees society as composed of parts that maintain stability and social order.

6.
In anthropology, “ethnography” refers to:
A) Statistical analysis of social data
B) The detailed study and description of a culture
C) The study of past societies through artifacts
D) Psychological analysis of individuals
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B) The detailed study and description of a culture

Rationale: Ethnography involves immersive fieldwork to understand and describe a culture from the insider’s perspective.

7.
Social stratification is best defined as:
A) Random allocation of resources
B) Structured ranking of groups in society
C) Equal distribution of wealth
D) Individual achievements only
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B) Structured ranking of groups in society

Rationale: Social stratification refers to the hierarchical arrangement of individuals or groups based on factors like wealth, power, and status.

8.
The term “role conflict” describes:
A) Confusion about social norms
B) Incompatibility between roles held by one person
C) Competition between different social groups
D) Changing roles over time
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B) Incompatibility between roles held by one person

Rationale: Role conflict occurs when expectations from different social roles clash for an individual.

9.
Which of the following is NOT a subfield of anthropology?
A) Archaeology
B) Linguistic anthropology
C) Political science
D) Biological anthropology
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: C) Political science

Rationale: Political science studies governance, not anthropology; archaeology, linguistic, and biological anthropology are key subfields.

10.
Max Weber is best known for his theory on:
A) Social evolution
B) Bureaucracy and rationalization
C) Class conflict
D) Cultural materialism
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B) Bureaucracy and rationalization

Rationale: Weber analyzed the rise of bureaucratic organizations and rational systems in society.

11.
Cultural diffusion refers to:
A) The loss of traditional culture
B) The spread of cultural traits from one group to another
C) Isolation of cultures
D) Internal cultural changes
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B) The spread of cultural traits from one group to another

Rationale: Cultural diffusion explains how ideas, technologies, and practices spread across societies.

12.
The “looking-glass self” theory emphasizes:
A) How people develop self-identity through social interactions
B) Psychological analysis of the mind
C) Biological inheritance of behavior
D) The role of language in culture
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: A) How people develop self-identity through social interactions

Rationale: Proposed by Cooley, the theory states self-concept emerges through others’ perceptions.

13.
Which term describes a society in which kinship ties are the main social bonds?
A) Industrial society
B) Tribal society
C) Post-industrial society
D) Bureaucratic society
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B) Tribal society

Rationale: Tribal societies are typically organized around kinship and familial ties.

14.
The concept of “anomie” was introduced by:
A) Emile Durkheim
B) Karl Marx
C) Max Weber
D) Talcott Parsons
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: A) Emile Durkheim

Rationale: Anomie describes a state of normlessness and social instability.

15.
“Symbolic interactionism” focuses primarily on:
A) Macro-level social structures
B) Micro-level social interactions and meanings
C) Economic systems
D) Social class conflicts
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B) Micro-level social interactions and meanings

Rationale: This perspective analyzes everyday interactions and the symbols used to communicate.

16.
Which anthropological method involves participant observation?
A) Surveys
B) Laboratory experiments
C) Fieldwork
D) Historical analysis
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: C) Fieldwork

Rationale: Fieldwork involves living with a community to observe and participate in their daily life.

17.
The “culture shock” experience occurs when:
A) A person adapts quickly to a new culture
B) A person is confused or disoriented in an unfamiliar culture
C) Cultural traits are spread rapidly
D) Societies merge into one
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B) A person is confused or disoriented in an unfamiliar culture

Rationale: Culture shock results from unfamiliarity and stress when encountering a new cultural environment.

18.
The primary focus of conflict theory is:
A) Social harmony
B) Power struggles and inequality
C) Cultural norms
D) Individual psychology
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B) Power struggles and inequality

Rationale: Conflict theory highlights how social groups compete over scarce resources.

19.
Which is an example of a “material culture” item?
A) Language
B) Religion
C) Tools
D) Values
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: C) Tools

Rationale: Material culture consists of physical objects and artifacts made and used by a society.

20.
The term “socialization” refers to:
A) The process of making laws
B) Learning to become a functioning member of society
C) Creating social institutions
D) Isolating individuals from groups
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B) Learning to become a functioning member of society

Rationale: Socialization is how individuals acquire knowledge, values, and norms to participate in society.

21.
The study of human language and communication in anthropology is called:
A) Ethnography
B) Linguistic anthropology
C) Archaeology
D) Biological anthropology
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B) Linguistic anthropology

Rationale: Linguistic anthropology focuses on language’s role in culture and society.

22.
A “subculture” is:
A) A dominant culture
B) A culture within a larger culture that shares distinct traits
C) An isolated culture with no contact
D) A culture that has disappeared
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B) A culture within a larger culture that shares distinct traits

Rationale: Subcultures maintain unique norms and values within a larger society.

23.
Which sociologist is known for studying class conflict?
A) Max Weber
B) Karl Marx
C) Emile Durkheim
D) George Herbert Mead
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B) Karl Marx

Rationale: Marx focused on class struggle between the bourgeoisie and proletariat.

24.
Which of the following best defines “cultural lag”?
A) When one culture adopts traits faster than another
B) The time delay in cultural adjustments to technological changes
C) The loss of culture due to globalization
D) The rejection of cultural norms
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B) The time delay in cultural adjustments to technological changes

Rationale: Cultural lag occurs when non-material culture struggles to keep pace with material culture changes.

25.
Which anthropologist is famous for studying the “Nacirema” culture?
A) Bronislaw Malinowski
B) Clifford Geertz
C) Horace Miner
D) Margaret Mead
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: C) Horace Miner

Rationale: Miner wrote about the “Nacirema” as a satire to highlight ethnocentrism.

Section 2: Social Dynamics & Healthcare

25 Marks
26.
Which of the following is an example of “social mobility”?
A) Changing jobs within the same social class
B) Moving up or down the social hierarchy
C) Living in a different country
D) Joining a new social club
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B) Moving up or down the social hierarchy

Rationale: Social mobility refers to changes in an individual's or group’s social status.

27.
What does the term “culture shock” imply?
A) Excitement about a new culture
B) Psychological stress from new cultural environments
C) Rejection of one's own culture
D) Cultural superiority
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B) Psychological stress from new cultural environments

Rationale: It is a feeling of disorientation experienced in a different cultural setting.

28.
Which sociological concept explains how people conform to social norms due to fear of sanctions?
A) Social control
B) Socialization
C) Role conflict
D) Cultural diffusion
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: A) Social control

Rationale: Social control mechanisms enforce conformity to norms through rewards or punishments.

29.
The study of kinship systems is most closely associated with which field?
A) Sociology
B) Anthropology
C) Psychology
D) Economics
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B) Anthropology

Rationale: Anthropologists analyze kinship patterns to understand social organization.

30.
The concept of “status inconsistency” refers to:
A) Holding multiple social statuses that conflict in prestige
B) Having no social status
C) Changing social status frequently
D) Equal prestige among all social statuses
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: A) Holding multiple social statuses that conflict in prestige

Rationale: Status inconsistency happens when someone’s social roles differ in prestige or rank.

31.
“Ethnocentrism” is best described as:
A) Believing one’s culture is superior to others
B) Understanding cultural differences objectively
C) Adopting foreign cultural traits
D) Rejecting cultural norms
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: A) Believing one’s culture is superior to others

Rationale: Ethnocentrism is judging other cultures by one’s own cultural standards.

32.
“Cultural relativism” opposes:
A) Cultural diffusion
B) Ethnocentrism
C) Socialization
D) Acculturation
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B) Ethnocentrism

Rationale: Cultural relativism promotes understanding cultures in their own context, avoiding bias.

33.
Which of the following best describes a “total institution”?
A) A social club
B) A place where all aspects of life are controlled and regulated
C) An informal social group
D) A government agency
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B) A place where all aspects of life are controlled and regulated

Rationale: Total institutions (e.g., prisons, military) control and isolate individuals from society.

34.
Which concept is central to symbolic interactionism?
A) Power struggles
B) Symbols and meanings in social interactions
C) Economic inequality
D) Social structure
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B) Symbols and meanings in social interactions

Rationale: Symbolic interactionism analyzes how people create meaning through interaction.

35.
The “functional prerequisites” of society refer to:
A) Basic needs for survival and social order
B) Economic wealth
C) Population size
D) Political power
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: A) Basic needs for survival and social order

Rationale: Functional prerequisites are essential functions a society must fulfill to survive.

36.
Which anthropologist introduced the concept of “thick description”?
A) Franz Boas
B) Clifford Geertz
C) Margaret Mead
D) Bronislaw Malinowski
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B) Clifford Geertz

Rationale: Geertz emphasized detailed, context-rich descriptions to interpret cultures.

37.
The term “patriarchy” refers to:
A) Female dominance in society
B) Male dominance in society
C) Equal gender roles
D) Child-centered family structures
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B) Male dominance in society

Rationale: Patriarchy is a social system where men hold primary power.

38.
“Assimilation” involves:
A) Complete adoption of another culture by an individual or group
B) Maintaining distinct cultural traits
C) Rejecting new cultures
D) Spreading cultural traits
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: A) Complete adoption of another culture by an individual or group

Rationale: Assimilation is when a minority group adopts the dominant culture’s traits fully.

39.
Which sociological theorist emphasized the concept of “the self” developing through social interaction?
A) Karl Marx
B) George Herbert Mead
C) Emile Durkheim
D) Max Weber
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B) George Herbert Mead

Rationale: Mead theorized the self emerges from social processes and interaction.

40.
“Cultural materialism” focuses on:
A) The symbolic meanings of culture
B) The material conditions and economic factors shaping culture
C) Psychological traits
D) Language development
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B) The material conditions and economic factors shaping culture

Rationale: Cultural materialism examines how infrastructure influences culture and social organization.

41.
Which term refers to a belief system that justifies social inequality?
A) Ideology
B) Ethnocentrism
C) Culture shock
D) Acculturation
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: A) Ideology

Rationale: Ideologies legitimize or challenge social arrangements and inequalities.

42.
Which is an example of a “folkway”?
A) Laws against theft
B) Shaking hands as a greeting
C) Ritual sacrifice
D) Marriage ceremonies
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B) Shaking hands as a greeting

Rationale: Folkways are informal norms governing everyday behavior.

43.
The term “social institution” refers to:
A) A government building
B) Organized patterns of beliefs and behavior centered on basic societal needs
C) Informal groups of friends
D) Individual habits
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B) Organized patterns of beliefs and behavior centered on basic societal needs

Rationale: Social institutions (family, religion) meet societal needs through established structures.

44.
The idea that language shapes thought and perception is called:
A) Linguistic determinism
B) Cultural diffusion
C) Socialization
D) Functionalism
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: A) Linguistic determinism

Rationale: This concept, linked to the Sapir-Whorf hypothesis, argues language influences cognition.

45.
In anthropology, the term “emic” refers to:
A) The outsider’s perspective on a culture
B) The insider’s perspective within a culture
C) The historical perspective
D) The economic perspective
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B) The insider’s perspective within a culture

Rationale: Emic refers to understanding culture from within, by its members.

46.
The sociological term for an expected behavior attached to a social status is:
A) Role
B) Norm
C) Value
D) Status
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: A) Role

Rationale: Roles are the behavioral expectations associated with social positions.

47.
Which of the following best illustrates “social construction of reality”?
A) Biological differences between people
B) How societies create and maintain shared meanings
C) Physical laws
D) Psychological traits
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B) How societies create and maintain shared meanings

Rationale: Social constructionism studies how reality is shaped through social processes.

48.
“Cultural imperialism” is:
A) The spread of culture by choice
B) The dominance of one culture over others through power and influence
C) Voluntary cultural exchange
D) Cultural isolation
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B) The dominance of one culture over others through power and influence

Rationale: Cultural imperialism involves imposing one culture’s values on others, often through colonization.

49.
The “glass ceiling” metaphor describes:
A) Visible social barriers
B) Invisible barriers preventing marginalized groups from advancing
C) Economic wealth accumulation
D) Technological progress
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B) Invisible barriers preventing marginalized groups from advancing

Rationale: The glass ceiling refers to subtle obstacles limiting career progression.

50.
In anthropology, “participant observation” is:
A) Quantitative data collection
B) Active involvement in a community to understand their culture
C) Laboratory experiments
D) Reading historical documents
View Answer & Rationale

Correct Answer: B) Active involvement in a community to understand their culture

Rationale: Participant observation requires researchers to engage in daily life of the study group for in-depth understanding.
