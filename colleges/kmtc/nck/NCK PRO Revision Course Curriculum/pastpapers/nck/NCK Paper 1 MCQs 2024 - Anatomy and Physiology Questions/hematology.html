351.
A patient presents with fatigue, pallor, and dyspnea on exertion. Laboratory tests reveal a low hemoglobin level, decreased mean corpuscular volume (MCV), and decreased mean corpuscular hemoglobin concentration (MCHC). Which of the following conditions is most likely to cause these findings?
A) Iron deficiency anemia
B) Vitamin B12 deficiency anemia
C) Thalassemia
D) Sickle cell anemia
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A) Iron deficiency anemia

Explanation: Microcytic (Low MCV) and Hypochromic (Low MCHC) anemia is classic for Iron Deficiency.

352.
A patient with a history of chronic liver disease presents with bruising, petechiae, and mucosal bleeding. Laboratory tests reveal prolonged prothrombin time (PT) and activated partial thromboplastin time (aPTT) with decreased platelet count. Which of the following conditions is most likely to cause these findings?
A) Immune thrombocytopenic purpura (ITP)
B) Disseminated intravascular coagulation (DIC)
C) Hemophilia A
D) Von Willebrand disease
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B) Disseminated intravascular coagulation (DIC)

Explanation: Liver disease can predispose to DIC. The combination of prolonged PT/aPTT (consumption of factors) AND low platelets (consumption of platelets) is characteristic of DIC.

357.
A patient presents with petechiae, purpura, and mucosal bleeding. Laboratory tests reveal thrombocytopenia, elevated serum creatinine, and schistocytes on peripheral blood smear. Which of the following conditions is most likely to cause these findings?
A) Thrombotic thrombocytopenic purpura (TTP)
B) Idiopathic thrombocytopenic purpura (ITP)
C) Hemolytic uremic syndrome (HUS)
D) Disseminated intravascular coagulation (DIC)
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C) Hemolytic uremic syndrome (HUS) (or TTP)

Explanation: The triad of Thrombocytopenia + Renal Failure (Elevated Creatinine) + Microangiopathic Hemolytic Anemia (Schistocytes) is classic for HUS (or TTP). TTP usually includes neuro symptoms and fever (Pentad). HUS is more renal-dominant.

359.

A) Anemia of chronic disease
B) Hemolytic uremic syndrome (HUS)

Correct Answer: D) Renal failure-associated anemia

Explanation: The low erythropoietin level is the key. The failing kidneys cannot produce EPO, leading to anemia.

360.
A patient presents with petechiae, ecchymoses, and gingival bleeding. Laboratory tests reveal prolonged bleeding time, normal platelet count, and normal PT and aPTT. Which of the following conditions is most likely to cause these findings?
A) Glanzmann thrombasthenia
B) Bernard-Soulier syndrome
C) Acquired von Willebrand syndrome
D) Hemophilia A
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A) Glanzmann thrombasthenia

Explanation: Glanzmann's is a platelet function defect (normal count, prolonged bleeding time). Bernard-Soulier usually has giant platelets and thrombocytopenia.

375.
A patient presents with generalized weakness, headache, and difficulty concentrating. Laboratory tests reveal normocytic normochromic anemia, normal iron studies, and elevated serum erythropoietin levels. Which of the following conditions is most likely to cause these findings?
A) Chronic kidney disease (CKD)
B) Iron deficiency anemia
C) Thalassemia
D) Aplastic anemia
Show Answer

Correct Answer: D) Aplastic anemia

Explanation: If EPO is high but the marrow isn't responding (anemia persists), and iron is normal, it suggests marrow failure (Aplastic Anemia). CKD would have *low* EPO.
