Immunology

NCK KRCHN Paper 1 - 2024

301.
A patient is exposed to the hepatitis B virus (HBV) and develops immunity against future infections. Which type of immunity is primarily responsible for this protection?
A) Passive immunity
B) Innate immunity
C) Humoral immunity
D) Cell-mediated immunity
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C) Humoral immunity

Explanation: Humoral immunity involves the production of antibodies (immunoglobulins) by B cells, which circulate in the blood and protect against specific pathogens like viruses upon re-exposure.

302.
Which of the following cell types is primarily responsible for presenting antigens to T cells during the immune response?
A) B cells
B) Natural killer (NK) cells
C) Macrophages
D) Eosinophils
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C) Macrophages

Explanation: Macrophages are key Antigen-Presenting Cells (APCs) that phagocytose pathogens and display their antigens on MHC molecules to T cells.

303.
A patient with a severe bacterial infection has elevated levels of C-reactive protein (CRP) in their blood. Which of the following functions does CRP primarily serve during the acute phase response?
A) Opsonization of pathogens
B) Activation of complement system
C) Phagocytosis of pathogens
D) Neutralization of toxins
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A) Opsonization of pathogens

Explanation: CRP binds to the surface of dead or dying cells and some bacteria, acting as an opsonin to activate the complement system and enhance phagocytosis.

304.
Which of the following types of T cells is primarily involved in the regulation of the immune response by suppressing excessive inflammation and preventing autoimmunity?
A) Helper T cells (Th1)
B) Cytotoxic T cells (CTLs)
C) Regulatory T cells (Tregs)
D) Memory T cells
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C) Regulatory T cells (Tregs)

Explanation: Regulatory T cells (Tregs) play a crucial role in maintaining tolerance to self-antigens and abrogating autoimmune disease.

305.
Which of the following mechanisms is responsible for the phenomenon of immunological memory, wherein the immune system mounts a faster and more robust response upon re-exposure to a previously encountered pathogen?
A) Clonal expansion of memory B cells
B) Differentiation of naïve T cells into effector T cells
C) Production of antibodies by plasma cells
D) Activation of the complement system
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A) Clonal expansion of memory B cells

Explanation: Memory B cells persist after an infection. Upon re-exposure, they rapidly proliferate (clonal expansion) and differentiate into antibody-secreting plasma cells.

306.
A patient with a severe fungal infection demonstrates impaired neutrophil function. Which of the following cellular processes is most likely to be affected in this patient?
A) Phagocytosis
B) Antibody production
C) Cytokine secretion
D) Mast cell degranulation
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A) Phagocytosis

Explanation: Neutrophils are the primary phagocytes of the innate immune system, responsible for ingesting and destroying pathogens like fungi.

307.
Which of the following statements accurately describes the role of natural killer (NK) cells in the immune response?
A) NK cells directly kill virus-infected cells and tumor cells.
B) NK cells produce antibodies against pathogens.
C) NK cells present antigens to T cells to initiate adaptive immunity.
D) NK cells release cytokines to recruit other immune cells to the site of infection.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A) NK cells directly kill virus-infected cells and tumor cells.

Explanation: NK cells recognize and destroy stressed cells (viral-infected or tumor) without prior sensitization (innate immunity).

308.
Which of the following cytokines is primarily responsible for promoting differentiation of naïve T cells into Th1 cells and enhancing cell-mediated immune responses?
A) Interleukin-2 (IL-2)
B) Interleukin-4 (IL-4)
C) Interferon-gamma (IFN-γ)
D) Tumor necrosis factor-alpha (TNF-α)
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C) Interferon-gamma (IFN-γ)

Explanation: IFN-gamma is the signature cytokine of the Th1 response, driving cell-mediated immunity.

309.
A patient presents with symptoms of severe allergies, including difficulty breathing, hives, and swelling. Which of the following cells is primarily responsible for mediating these allergic responses?
A) Eosinophils
B) Basophils
C) Mast cells
D) Neutrophils
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C) Mast cells

Explanation: Mast cells release histamine and other mediators when IgE binds to allergens, causing the symptoms of anaphylaxis and allergy.

310.
Which of the following immunoglobulin classes is primarily involved in providing passive immunity to newborn infants through breast milk?
A) IgA
B) IgG
C) IgE
D) IgM
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A) IgA

Explanation: Secretory IgA is found in high concentrations in colostrum and breast milk, protecting the infant's gut.

311.
A patient with a chronic viral infection demonstrates persistently elevated levels of CD8+ cytotoxic T cells in their blood. Which of the following mechanisms is most likely responsible for this observation?
A) Activation-induced cell death (AICD)
B) T cell exhaustion
C) Thymic involution
D) Antigenic variation
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B) T cell exhaustion

Explanation: In chronic viral infections, constant antigen stimulation can lead to T cell exhaustion, where cells persist but lose function.

312.
Which of the following cell types is primarily responsible for the production of antibodies during the humoral immune response?
A) Plasma cells
B) T helper cells (Th cells)
C) Memory B cells
D) Macrophages
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A) Plasma cells

Explanation: Plasma cells are differentiated B cells that specialize in the mass production and secretion of antibodies.

313.
A patient with a suspected autoimmune disorder exhibits antibodies directed against self-antigens, leading to tissue damage and inflammation. Which of the following mechanisms is primarily responsible for the development of autoimmune diseases?
A) Loss of self-tolerance
B) Failure of innate immunity
C) Defective T cell activation
D) Impaired phagocytosis
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A) Loss of self-tolerance

Explanation: Autoimmunity results from a breakdown in the mechanisms that normally prevent the immune system from attacking the body's own tissues (self-tolerance).

314.
Which of the following statements accurately describes the role of dendritic cells in the immune response?
A) Dendritic cells directly kill infected cells and tumor cells.
B) Dendritic cells produce antibodies against pathogens.
C) Dendritic cells activate naïve T cells and initiate adaptive immune responses.
D) Dendritic cells release histamine and other inflammatory mediators.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C) Dendritic cells activate naïve T cells and initiate adaptive immune responses.

Explanation: Dendritic cells are the most potent professional Antigen-Presenting Cells (APCs) capable of activating naïve T cells.

315.
A patient with a severe viral infection exhibits reduced levels of circulating lymphocytes. Which of the following mechanisms is most likely responsible for this observation?
A) Apoptosis of infected lymphocytes
B) Clonal expansion of memory T cells
C) T cell anergy
D) Upregulation of adhesion molecules on lymphocytes
Show Answer

Correct Answer: D) Upregulation of adhesion molecules on lymphocytes

Explanation: During infection, lymphocytes upregulate adhesion molecules to migrate (extravasate) out of the blood and into the tissues/lymph nodes to fight infection, leading to a temporary drop in blood count (margination). Note: Apoptosis is also possible, but migration is the functional response.

316.
Which of the following cell types is primarily responsible for secreting pro-inflammatory cytokines such as interleukin-1 (IL-1) and tumor necrosis factor-alpha (TNF-α) during the acute phase response?
A) Macrophages
B) T lymphocytes
C) B lymphocytes
D) Natural killer (NK) cells
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A) Macrophages

Explanation: Activated macrophages are the primary source of IL-1 and TNF-alpha, key mediators of inflammation.

317.
Which of the following immune cells is primarily responsible for coordinating and regulating immune responses by producing cytokines that direct the activities of other immune cells?
A) T helper cells (Th cells)
B) Cytotoxic T cells (CTLs)
C) B cells
D) Natural killer (NK) cells
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A) T helper cells (Th cells)

Explanation: CD4+ Helper T cells are the "generals" of the immune system, directing the response via cytokine signaling.

318.
Which of the following mechanisms is responsible for the generation of diversity in the antigen-binding sites of immunoglobulins?
A) Somatic hypermutation
B) Gene rearrangement
C) Isotype switching
D) Clonal expansion
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B) Gene rearrangement

Explanation: V(D)J recombination (gene rearrangement) in the bone marrow creates the vast diversity of initial antibody specificities. Somatic hypermutation further refines affinity later.

319.
Which of the following statements accurately describes the role of major histocompatibility complex (MHC) molecules in antigen presentation?
A) MHC class I molecules present exogenous antigens to CD4+ T cells.
B) MHC class I molecules present endogenous antigens to CD8+ T cells.
C) MHC class II molecules present endogenous antigens to CD4+ T cells.
D) MHC class II molecules present exogenous antigens to CD8+ T cells.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B) MHC class I molecules present endogenous antigens to CD8+ T cells.

Explanation: Rule of 8: MHC I x CD8 = 8 (Endogenous/Intracellular pathway). MHC II x CD4 = 8 (Exogenous/Extracellular pathway).

320.
Which of the following statements accurately describes the process of opsonization during the immune response?
A) Opsonins facilitate the lysis of target cells by forming membrane attack complexes.
B) Opsonins promote the phagocytosis of pathogens by coating them with proteins.
C) Opsonins neutralize toxins produced by pathogens.
D) Opsonins stimulate the release of histamine from mast cells.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B) Opsonins promote the phagocytosis of pathogens by coating them with proteins.

Explanation: Opsonization is the "buttering of the bread" to make pathogens more appetizing and easier for phagocytes to ingest.

321.
A patient is diagnosed with selective IgA deficiency, a primary immunodeficiency disorder. Which of the following complications is most commonly associated with this condition?
A) Recurrent bacterial infections
B) Severe combined immunodeficiency (SCID)
C) Chronic granulomatous disease (CGD)
D) Autoimmune hemolytic anemia
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A) Recurrent bacterial infections

Explanation: IgA protects mucous membranes. Deficiency leads to recurrent sino-pulmonary and GI infections.

322.
A patient with a history of multiple myeloma demonstrates hypogammaglobulinemia. Which of the following immunoglobulin classes is most likely to be deficient in this patient?
A) IgG
B) IgA
C) IgM
D) IgE
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A) IgG

Explanation: IgG is the most abundant immunoglobulin in serum. In myeloma, despite high levels of a specific monoclonal protein, normal functional immunoglobulins (especially IgG) are suppressed.

323.
A patient with systemic lupus erythematosus (SLE) demonstrates a positive antinuclear antibody (ANA) test and elevated levels of anti-double-stranded DNA (anti-dsDNA) antibodies. Which of the following mechanisms is most likely responsible for the production of these autoantibodies?
A) Loss of self-tolerance
B) Failure of innate immunity
C) Defective T cell activation
D) Impaired phagocytosis
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A) Loss of self-tolerance

Explanation: SLE is the prototype autoimmune disease caused by a loss of tolerance to nuclear self-antigens.

324.
Which of the following terms refers to the process by which antibodies coat the surface of pathogens, marking them for destruction by phagocytes or complement proteins?
A) Opsonization
B) Neutralization
C) Agglutination
D) Precipitation
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A) Opsonization

Explanation: This is the definition of opsonization.

325.
Which of the following cellular receptors is responsible for recognizing pathogen-associated molecular patterns (PAMPs) and initiating innate immune responses?
A) Toll-like receptors (TLRs)
B) B cell receptors (BCRs)
C) T cell receptors (TCRs)
D) Fc receptors
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A) Toll-like receptors (TLRs)

Explanation: TLRs are key Pattern Recognition Receptors (PRRs) of the innate immune system.
