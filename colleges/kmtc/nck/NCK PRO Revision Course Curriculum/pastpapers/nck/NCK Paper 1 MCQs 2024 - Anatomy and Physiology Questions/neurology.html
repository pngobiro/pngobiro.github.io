NCK KRCHN Paper 1 - 2024

376.
A patient presents with sudden onset of weakness and numbness on one side of the body, along with difficulty speaking and understanding speech. Imaging reveals an acute infarction involving the left middle cerebral artery territory. Which of the following neurological conditions is most likely responsible for these symptoms?
A) Transient ischemic attack (TIA)
B) Intracerebral hemorrhage
C) Ischemic stroke
D) Subarachnoid hemorrhage
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C) Ischemic stroke

Explanation: The imaging confirms an "infarction" (tissue death due to lack of blood flow), which defines an ischemic stroke.

377.
A patient presents with tremors, rigidity, bradykinesia, and postural instability. On examination, the patient demonstrates a shuffling gait, stooped posture, and a masked facies. Which of the following neurological conditions is most likely responsible for these symptoms?
A) Alzheimer's disease
B) Parkinson's disease
C) Multiple sclerosis (MS)
D) Amyotrophic lateral sclerosis (ALS)
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B) Parkinson's disease

Explanation: This is the classic tetrad of Parkinsonian symptoms (TRAP: Tremor, Rigidity, Akinesia/Bradykinesia, Postural instability).

378.
A patient presents with severe headache, photophobia, and neck stiffness. On examination, there is nuchal rigidity and positive Kernig and Brudzinski signs. Which of the following neurological conditions is most likely responsible for these symptoms?
A) Migraine headache
B) Cluster headache
C) Meningitis
D) Subarachnoid hemorrhage
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C) Meningitis

Explanation: The triad of headache, nuchal rigidity, and photophobia (meningismus) along with positive Kernig/Brudzinski signs strongly points to Meningitis.

379.
A patient presents with sudden-onset severe headache, vomiting, and altered mental status. Imaging reveals a berry-shaped aneurysm at the junction of the anterior communicating artery and anterior cerebral artery. Which of the following neurological conditions is most likely responsible for these symptoms?
A) Ischemic stroke
B) Intracerebral hemorrhage
C) Subdural hematoma
D) Subarachnoid hemorrhage
Show Answer

Correct Answer: D) Subarachnoid hemorrhage

Explanation: Rupture of a berry aneurysm typically causes a Subarachnoid Hemorrhage (SAH), often described as the "worst headache of my life" (thunderclap headache).

380.
A patient presents with sudden-onset unilateral facial droop, arm weakness, and slurred speech. Symptoms began approximately 30 minutes ago but have partially resolved since then. Which of the following neurological conditions is most likely responsible for these symptoms?
A) Transient ischemic attack (TIA)
B) Ischemic stroke
C) Hemorrhagic stroke
D) Intracerebral hemorrhage
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A) Transient ischemic attack (TIA)

Explanation: TIA symptoms mimic a stroke but resolve spontaneously, typically within 24 hours (usually much less), without permanent infarction.

381.
A patient presents with acute onset of severe vertigo, nausea, vomiting, and nystagmus. Symptoms are worsened with head movement. Which of the following neurological conditions is most likely responsible for these symptoms?
A) Ménière's disease
B) Vestibular neuritis
C) Benign paroxysmal positional vertigo (BPPV)
D) Labyrinthitis
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C) Benign paroxysmal positional vertigo (BPPV)

Explanation: The key is that symptoms are *triggered/worsened by head movement* and are episodic/positional.

382.
A patient presents with gradual-onset memory loss, impaired judgment, and personality changes. Over time, the patient develops difficulty with language, motor function, and coordination. Which of the following neurological conditions is most likely responsible for these symptoms?
A) Parkinson's disease
B) Alzheimer's disease
C) Huntington's disease
D) Amyotrophic lateral sclerosis (ALS)
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B) Alzheimer's disease

Explanation: The progressive decline in memory, judgment, language, and function is characteristic of Alzheimer's dementia.

383.
A patient presents with sudden-onset severe headache, altered mental status, and focal neurological deficits. Imaging reveals a hemorrhagic lesion within the right basal ganglia. Which of the following neurological conditions is most likely responsible for these symptoms?
A) Ischemic stroke
B) Subarachnoid hemorrhage
C) Intracerebral hemorrhage
D) Transient ischemic attack (TIA)
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C) Intracerebral hemorrhage

Explanation: Bleeding into the brain parenchyma (basal ganglia is a common site for hypertensive bleeds) defines Intracerebral Hemorrhage.

384.
A patient presents with sudden-onset weakness and sensory loss on one side of the body along with difficulty speaking and understanding speech. Imaging reveals an acute infarction involving the right middle cerebral artery territory. Which of the following neurological conditions is most likely responsible for these symptoms?
A) Transient ischemic attack (TIA)
B) Intracerebral hemorrhage
C) Ischemic stroke
D) Subarachnoid hemorrhage
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C) Ischemic stroke

Explanation: Again, "acute infarction" indicates tissue death, confirming ischemic stroke. (Note: Right MCA stroke usually causes left-sided weakness and neglect, while Left MCA causes aphasia. The question mentions speech difficulty with a *Right* MCA infarct, which is atypical for right-handed individuals, but "infarction" is the diagnostic keyword).

387.

389.
A patient presents with acute onset of severe headache, visual disturbances, and vomiting. Imaging reveals a tumor compressing the optic chiasm. Which of the following neurological conditions is most likely responsible for these symptoms?
A) Meningioma
B) Glioblastoma multiforme
C) Pituitary adenoma
D) Medulloblastoma
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C) Pituitary adenoma

Explanation: The pituitary gland sits directly below the optic chiasm. Tumors here (adenomas) often compress it, causing bitemporal hemianopsia (visual disturbances).

390.
A patient presents with recurrent episodes of severe headache associated with ipsilateral lacrimation, rhinorrhea, and ptosis. Symptoms are often triggered by alcohol consumption. Which of the following neurological conditions is most likely responsible for these symptoms?
A) Migraine headache
B) Cluster headache
C) Tension-type headache
D) Trigeminal neuralgia
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B) Cluster headache

Explanation: Severe unilateral pain with autonomic symptoms (tearing, runny nose, ptosis) is characteristic of Cluster Headache.

391.
A patient presents with progressive weakness, muscle atrophy, and fasciculations, primarily involving the upper and lower extremities. Over time, the patient develops dysphagia and dysarthria. Which of the following neurological conditions is most likely responsible for these symptoms?
A) Parkinson's disease
B) Guillain-Barré syndrome (GBS)
C) Amyotrophic lateral sclerosis (ALS)
D) Myasthenia gravis
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C) Amyotrophic lateral sclerosis (ALS)

Explanation: The combination of Upper Motor Neuron signs (spasticity - implied by progression) and Lower Motor Neuron signs (atrophy, fasciculations) is the hallmark of ALS.

395.
A patient presents with sudden-onset unilateral headache, along with ipsilateral ptosis, miosis, and anhidrosis. Which of the following neurological conditions is most likely responsible for these symptoms?
