176.
A patient presents with unilateral nasal congestion, facial pain, and purulent nasal discharge. Upon examination, tenderness is noted over the affected sinus. Which of the following conditions is most likely responsible for this presentation?
A) Allergic rhinitis
B) Acute sinusitis
C) Nasal polyps
D) Deviated nasal septum
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B) Acute sinusitis

Explanation: The classic triad for acute bacterial sinusitis includes purulent nasal discharge, facial pain/pressure/tenderness, and nasal obstruction. The unilaterality and tenderness over the sinus (maxillary or frontal) strongly point to an acute infectious process rather than allergy or structural deviation.

177.
A patient presents with hoarseness, dysphonia, and occasional throat pain. Direct laryngoscopy reveals vocal cord nodules. Which of the following interventions is most appropriate for managing this condition?
A) Vocal cord polypectomy
B) Vocal cord injection with corticosteroids
C) Voice therapy and vocal hygiene education
D) Partial laryngectomy
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C) Voice therapy and vocal hygiene education

Explanation: Vocal nodules (Singer's nodules) are typically caused by vocal abuse or misuse. The first-line treatment is conservative management through speech/voice therapy to correct the underlying behavior. Surgery (A) is reserved for cases resistant to therapy.

178.
A patient presents with recurrent episodes of vertigo, nausea, and nystagmus, often triggered by head movements. Dix-Hallpike maneuver elicits positional vertigo and rotary nystagmus. Which of the following conditions is most likely responsible for this presentation?
A) Ménière's disease
B) Benign paroxysmal positional vertigo (BPPV)
C) Vestibular neuritis
D) Labyrinthitis
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B) Benign paroxysmal positional vertigo (BPPV)

Explanation: BPPV is characterized by brief episodes of vertigo triggered by changes in head position. The Dix-Hallpike maneuver is the diagnostic test of choice, confirming BPPV if it elicits vertigo and nystagmus.

179.
A patient presents with severe sore throat, fever, and enlarged tonsils with exudate. Rapid antigen testing confirms group A beta-hemolytic Streptococcus (GAS) infection. Which of the following medications is the first-line treatment for this condition?
A) Amoxicillin
B) Azithromycin
C) Cephalexin
D) Clindamycin
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A) Amoxicillin

Explanation: Penicillin V or Amoxicillin is the first-line antibiotic treatment for Strep Throat (GAS) to prevent complications like Rheumatic Fever. Macrolides (Azithromycin) are alternatives for penicillin-allergic patients.

180.
A patient presents with sudden onset of severe, unilateral ear pain, diminished hearing, and purulent otorrhea. Otoscopic examination reveals a bulging, erythematous tympanic membrane with obscured landmarks. Which of the following conditions is most likely responsible for this presentation?
A) Otitis externa
B) Acute otitis media
C) Mastoiditis
D) Chronic suppurative otitis media
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B) Acute otitis media

Explanation: A bulging, red tympanic membrane with acute pain is the hallmark of Acute Otitis Media (AOM). Otorrhea implies perforation. Otitis Externa (A) involves the ear canal, not a bulging TM.

181.
A patient presents with a painless, gradually enlarging mass in the left neck, anterior to the sternocleidomastoid muscle. Fine-needle aspiration cytology reveals thyroid follicular cells. Which of the following conditions is most likely responsible for this presentation?
A) Thyroglossal duct cyst
B) Lymphadenopathy
C) Thyroid adenoma
D) Thyroid carcinoma
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A) Thyroglossal duct cyst

Explanation: Although Thyroglossal duct cysts are typically midline, they contain thyroid remnants (follicular cells). A "lateral" presentation is less common but possible, or the question implies a midline mass that moved. However, finding thyroid cells in a neck mass outside the thyroid bed is often a Thyroglossal cyst (benign) or Metastatic Papillary Carcinoma. Given the options, A is the developmental anomaly associated with ectopic thyroid tissue.

182.
A patient presents with recurrent episodes of epistaxis, particularly after blowing the nose or during dry weather. Anterior rhinoscopy reveals a friable, vascular lesion in Little's area. Which of the following interventions is most appropriate for managing this condition?
A) Nasal packing with anterior nasal tampons
B) Application of silver nitrate cautery
C) Endoscopic cauterization of the sphenopalatine artery
D) Surgical excision of the nasal polyp
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B) Application of silver nitrate cautery

Explanation: For a visible, anterior bleeding source (Little's Area/Kiesselbach's plexus) that is not actively spurting, chemical cautery with silver nitrate is a first-line office procedure. Packing (A) is for bleeding that doesn't stop or is not visualized.

183.
A patient presents with progressive hearing loss, tinnitus, and vertigo. Audiometric testing reveals sensorineural hearing loss with a "carrot-shaped" (likely meant "cookie-bite" or peak) configuration. Which of the following conditions is most likely responsible for this presentation?
A) Presbycusis
B) Noise-induced hearing loss
C) Ménière's disease
D) Acoustic neuroma
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C) Ménière's disease

Explanation: The classic triad of Ménière's disease is Vertigo, Tinnitus, and Hearing Loss. The audiogram typically shows low-frequency sensorineural hearing loss (rising configuration) initially, though the term in the question ("carrot-shaped") is non-standard but often used in local exams to denote the specific curve associated with Ménière's.

184.
A patient presents with a palpable, non-tender mass in the right neck, just below the angle of the mandible. Ultrasound imaging demonstrates a well-defined, hypoechoic lesion with cystic components. Which of the following conditions is most likely responsible for this presentation?
A) Branchial cleft cyst
B) Thyroglossal duct cyst
C) Lymphadenopathy
D) Parotid gland tumor
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A) Branchial cleft cyst

Explanation: A Second Branchial Cleft Cyst typically presents as a painless mass anterior to the sternocleidomastoid muscle, below the angle of the mandible. Thyroglossal cysts (B) are midline.

185.
A patient presents with a sudden onset of severe vertigo, nausea, vomiting, and nystagmus. The patient reports a recent upper respiratory tract infection. Which of the following conditions is most likely responsible for this presentation?
A) Vestibular neuritis
B) Benign paroxysmal positional vertigo (BPPV)
C) Ménière's disease
D) Acoustic neuroma
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A) Vestibular neuritis

Explanation: The association with a recent viral URI and the "sudden severe" nature without hearing loss points to Vestibular Neuritis. If hearing loss were present, it would be Labyrinthitis.

186.
A patient presents with a small, painless, well-defined nodule on the lateral aspect of the neck, just above the clavicle. Fine-needle aspiration cytology reveals clusters of polygonal cells with abundant granular cytoplasm. Which of the following conditions is most likely responsible for this presentation?
A) Thyroglossal duct cyst
B) Branchial cleft cyst
C) Lymphadenopathy
D) Parathyroid adenoma
Show Answer

Correct Answer: D) Parathyroid adenoma

Explanation: Ectopic parathyroid adenomas can be found in the neck. The cytology description ("polygonal cells with granular cytoplasm" - Oxyphil cells) matches parathyroid tissue. Supraclavicular location is a possible site for ectopic glands.

187.
A patient presents with chronic nasal congestion, hyposmia, and anosmia. Nasal endoscopy reveals polypoid masses obstructing the nasal cavity and sinuses. Which of the following conditions is most likely responsible for this presentation?
A) Allergic rhinitis
B) Chronic sinusitis
C) Nasal polyps
D) Deviated nasal septum
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C) Nasal polyps

Explanation: The direct visualization of "polypoid masses" confirms the diagnosis of Nasal Polyps. They are often associated with chronic inflammation, asthma, and aspirin sensitivity (Samter's Triad).

188.
A patient presents with a unilateral, painless enlargement of the thyroid gland. Fine-needle aspiration cytology reveals numerous microfollicles and psammoma bodies. Which of the following conditions is most likely responsible for this presentation?
A) Thyroglossal duct cyst
B) Hashimoto's thyroiditis
C) Thyroid adenoma
D) Papillary thyroid carcinoma
Show Answer

Correct Answer: D) Papillary thyroid carcinoma

Explanation: Psammoma bodies (calcific laminations) are a classic pathological hallmark of Papillary Thyroid Carcinoma. Microfollicles can also be seen.

189.
A patient presents with recurrent episodes of throat pain, odynophagia, and fever. Physical examination reveals tonsillar enlargement with yellow-white exudates and tender cervical lymphadenopathy. Which of the following organisms is most likely responsible for this presentation?
A) Streptococcus pneumoniae
B) Haemophilus influenzae
C) Group A beta-hemolytic Streptococcus (GAS)
D) Epstein-Barr virus (EBV)
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C) Group A beta-hemolytic Streptococcus (GAS)

Explanation: This is the classic presentation of Streptococcal Pharyngitis ("Strep Throat"): fever, tonsillar exudates, tender anterior cervical lymphadenopathy, and absence of cough (Centor Criteria).

190.
A patient presents with a sensation of fullness and pressure in the left ear, along with hearing loss and occasional tinnitus. Otoscopic examination reveals a retracted tympanic membrane with decreased mobility on pneumatic otoscopy. Which of the following conditions is most likely responsible for this presentation?
A) Acute otitis media
B) Otitis externa
C) Serous otitis media (otitis media with effusion)
D) Cholesteatoma
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C) Serous otitis media (otitis media with effusion)

Explanation: A retracted, immobile TM with fullness and hearing loss, but *without* signs of acute infection (fever, pain, bulging), indicates fluid behind the ear (Serous Otitis Media).

191.
A patient presents with recurrent episodes of sudden, severe vertigo lasting hours, accompanied by nausea, vomiting, and nystagmus. Vestibular function tests demonstrate unilateral weakness. Which of the following conditions is most likely responsible for this presentation?
A) Vestibular neuritis
B) Benign paroxysmal positional vertigo (BPPV)
C) Ménière's disease
D) Acoustic neuroma
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C) Ménière's disease

Explanation: The duration of vertigo (hours) distinguishes Ménière's from BPPV (seconds). Vestibular neuritis can last days but is usually a single event, not recurrent "episodes" in the same way without hearing loss mentioned. However, Ménière's is the classic recurrent episodic vertigo lasting 20 min to hours.

192.
A patient presents with a sensation of a foreign body stuck in the throat, difficulty swallowing, and odynophagia. Flexible laryngoscopy reveals a submucosal mass arising from the posterior pharyngeal wall. Which of the following conditions is most likely responsible for this presentation?
A) Zenker's diverticulum
B) Laryngopharyngeal reflux (LPR)
C) Hypopharyngeal carcinoma
D) Tonsillar hypertrophy
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C) Hypopharyngeal carcinoma

Explanation: A "mass arising from the posterior pharyngeal wall" causing dysphagia and odynophagia is highly suspicious for malignancy (carcinoma) until proven otherwise. Zenker's is a pouch, not typically described as a "mass" on laryngoscopy (barium swallow is diagnostic). LPR is inflammatory, not a mass.

193.
A patient presents with recurrent episodes of postnasal drip, chronic cough, and halitosis. Nasal endoscopy reveals a foul-smelling, yellowish discharge draining from the middle meatus. Which of the following conditions is most likely responsible for this presentation?
A) Chronic rhinosinusitis with nasal polyps
B) Allergic rhinitis
C) Nasal vestibulitis
D) Adenoid hypertrophy
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A) Chronic rhinosinusitis with nasal polyps

Explanation: Purulent (yellowish/foul) discharge from the middle meatus (where maxillary/frontal/anterior ethmoid sinuses drain) is the hallmark of Chronic Sinusitis.

194.
A patient presents with unilateral facial paralysis, inability to close the eye on the affected side, and loss of taste sensation on the anterior two-thirds of the tongue. Which of the following conditions is most likely responsible for this presentation?
A) Bell's palsy
B) Ramsay Hunt syndrome
C) Glossopharyngeal neuralgia
D) Acoustic neuroma
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A) Bell's palsy

Explanation: Bell's Palsy (Idiopathic Facial Paralysis) involves the Facial Nerve (CN VII). The symptoms described (motor paralysis, Chorda Tympani involvement causing taste loss) match the specific deficits of CN VII injury.
