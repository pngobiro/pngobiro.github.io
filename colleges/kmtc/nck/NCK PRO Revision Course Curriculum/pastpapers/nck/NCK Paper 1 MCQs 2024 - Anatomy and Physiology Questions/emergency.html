251.
A patient with a suspected spinal injury is found unconscious. Which of the following actions should be taken first?
A) Move the patient to a flat surface.
B) Perform a jaw thrust maneuver.
C) Apply a cervical collar.
D) Check for responsiveness.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: D) Check for responsiveness.

Explanation: In Basic Life Support (BLS) and trauma protocols, the very first step upon approaching a collapsed patient is to ensure scene safety and then **check for responsiveness** (tap and shout). Only after determining responsiveness do you proceed to airway management (Jaw thrust) or circulation checks.

252.
During CPR, which of the following actions should be performed immediately after activating the emergency response system?
A) Start chest compressions.
B) Check for breathing.
C) Perform abdominal thrusts.
D) Assess pulse.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: D) Assess pulse.

Explanation: For healthcare providers, the sequence is: Check responsiveness -> Activate EMS -> Check Pulse and Breathing (simultaneously for < 10 seconds). If no pulse, *then* start chest compressions (Option A). (Note: Layperson CPR skips the pulse check and goes straight to compressions, but exams typically test the Healthcare Provider BLS standard).

253.
You encounter a conscious adult choking on a piece of food. The person is coughing forcefully but unable to speak or breathe effectively (Partial obstruction turning severe). What is the most appropriate action?
A) Encourage continued coughing.
B) Perform abdominal thrusts (Heimlich maneuver).
C) Deliver back blows.
D) Administer rescue breaths.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A) Encourage continued coughing.

Explanation: As long as the patient is coughing forcefully, they have a *partial* obstruction and are moving air. Coughing is the most effective way to clear it. Abdominal thrusts (B) are indicated only when the cough becomes ineffective (silent cough), the patient cannot speak/breathe (severe obstruction), or becomes cyanotic.

254.
Which of the following actions is recommended for controlling severe external bleeding from an extremity?
A) Applying a tourniquet proximal to the injury site.
B) Elevating the extremity above the level of the heart.
C) Applying direct pressure with a sterile dressing.
D) Removing any impaled objects.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C) Applying direct pressure with a sterile dressing.

Explanation: **Direct pressure** is the first-line and most effective method for controlling external hemorrhage. Tourniquets (A) are used only if direct pressure fails or for life-threatening arterial bleeds in tactical/severe settings. Impaled objects (D) should never be removed.

255.
What is the appropriate ratio of chest compressions to rescue breaths for adult CPR?
A) 15 compressions: 2 breaths
B) 30 compressions: 2 breaths
C) 30 compressions: 1 breath
D) 15 compressions: 1 breath
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B) 30 compressions: 2 breaths

Explanation: The universal compression-to-ventilation ratio for a single rescuer (and two rescuers) for adults is 30:2.

256.
What is the first step in the management of a chemical splash to the eyes?
A) Irrigate the eyes with clean water.
B) Instill antibiotic eye drops.
C) Cover both eyes with a sterile dressing.
D) Assess visual acuity.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A) Irrigate the eyes with clean water.

Explanation: Immediate, copious irrigation with water or saline for at least 15-20 minutes is critical to dilute and remove the chemical, preventing corneal burns and blindness. Assessment (D) comes after irrigation.

257.
Which of the following actions should be taken first when encountering a person experiencing an allergic reaction with signs of respiratory distress?
A) Administer an epinephrine auto-injector.
B) Position the person comfortably.
C) Monitor vital signs.
D) Assess the severity of the reaction.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A) Administer an epinephrine auto-injector.

Explanation: Respiratory distress indicates Anaphylaxis, a life-threatening emergency. Epinephrine is the only medication that can reverse the airway constriction and hypotension. It must be given immediately.

258.
What is the primary purpose of splinting a fractured extremity in the pre-hospital setting?
A) Immobilization to prevent further injury.
B) Pain relief during transport.
C) Reduction of swelling.
D) Stabilization of the fracture site.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A) Immobilization to prevent further injury.

Explanation: The main goal is to prevent the sharp bone ends from damaging nerves, blood vessels, and muscles (converting a closed fracture to an open one). While it does provide pain relief and stabilization (B and D), preventing further neurovascular damage is the priority.

259.
Which of the following signs is indicative of shock in a trauma patient?
A) Increased heart rate
B) Hypertension
C) Rapid capillary refill
D) Hyperthermia
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A) Increased heart rate

Explanation: Tachycardia (Increased HR) is one of the earliest compensatory mechanisms in shock to maintain cardiac output. Patients in shock typically have Hypotension (not B), Delayed capillary refill (not C), and cool skin.

260.
What is the appropriate sequence of steps in the assessment of a conscious trauma patient?
A) Airway, breathing, circulation, disability, exposure (ABCDE).
B) Circulation, airway, breathing, disability, exposure (CABDE).
C) Breathing, airway, circulation, disability, exposure (BACDE).
D) Exposure, disability, circulation, airway, breathing (EDCAB).
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A) Airway, breathing, circulation, disability, exposure (ABCDE).

Explanation: The Primary Survey in trauma follows the ABCDE algorithm to identify and treat life-threatening conditions in order of priority. Note: In cardiac arrest (CPR), the sequence is C-A-B, but for a conscious trauma patient, A-B-C remains the standard assessment protocol.

261.
In a conscious trauma patient with a suspected head injury, which of the following actions is appropriate?
A) Applying direct pressure to the wound.
B) Administering aspirin for pain relief.
C) Maintaining cervical spine immobilization.
D) Assessing pupil reaction to light.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C) Maintaining cervical spine immobilization.

Explanation: Any patient with a significant head injury is assumed to have a cervical spine injury until proven otherwise. Immobilization prevents secondary spinal cord damage.

262.
Which of the following actions is indicated in the management of a patient with an open chest wound (sucking chest wound)?
A) Applying a sterile dressing and occlusive dressing.
B) Administering supplemental oxygen.
C) Providing reassurance and emotional support.
D) Elevating the patient's legs to improve venous return.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A) Applying a sterile dressing and occlusive dressing.

Explanation: An occlusive dressing (taped on three sides) prevents air from entering the pleural space during inspiration but allows air to escape during expiration, preventing a tension pneumothorax.

263.
What is the recommended position for a conscious patient experiencing an acute asthma exacerbation?
A) High-Fowler's position
B) Supine position with legs elevated
C) Left lateral recumbent position
D) Semi-Fowler's position
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A) High-Fowler's position

Explanation: Sitting upright (High-Fowler's) facilitates maximum lung expansion and allows the accessory muscles of respiration to work more effectively.

264.
Which of the following actions is appropriate when managing a patient with a suspected heat stroke?
A) Administering warm intravenous fluids.
B) Applying ice packs to the patient's skin.
C) Encouraging the patient to drink cold water rapidly.
D) Removing the patient from the hot environment and cooling the body.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: D) Removing the patient from the hot environment and cooling the body.

Explanation: Heat stroke is a medical emergency. The priority is rapid cooling (evaporative cooling, ice packs to axilla/groin) and removing the patient from the heat source.

265.
Which of the following interventions is appropriate for managing a conscious patient with a severe nosebleed (epistaxis)?
A) Tilting the head back.
B) Pinching the soft part of the nose.
C) Packing the nose with cotton gauze.
D) Applying direct pressure to the forehead.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B) Pinching the soft part of the nose.

Explanation: The patient should sit up and lean forward (to prevent swallowing blood) while pinching the soft part of the nose (alae) against the septum for 10-15 minutes.

266.
Which of the following actions is recommended for managing a patient with a suspected snakebite?
A) Applying a tourniquet proximal to the bite site.
B) Immobilizing the affected limb at the level of the heart.
C) Making an incision over the bite site to facilitate venom extraction.
D) Transporting the patient to a medical facility for antivenom administration.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: D) Transporting the patient to a medical facility for antivenom administration.

Explanation: Definitive treatment is antivenom. First aid involves immobilization (usually below heart level) and transport. Tourniquets (A) and cutting/sucking the wound (C) are harmful and contraindicated.

267.
What is the primary purpose of a cervical collar in the management of trauma patients?
A) Maintaining spinal alignment.
B) Immobilizing the cervical spine.
C) Providing comfort and support.
D) Facilitating airway management.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B) Immobilizing the cervical spine.

Explanation: The collar minimizes movement of the neck to prevent worsening of potential spinal cord injuries.

268.
Which of the following actions is appropriate for managing a conscious patient with a foreign object lodged in the eye?
A) Attempting to remove the object with tweezers.
B) Rinsing the eye with sterile saline solution.
C) Applying pressure to the eyelid to dislodge the object.
D) Placing a bandage over the affected eye.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: D) Placing a bandage over the affected eye.

Explanation: If an object is "lodged" (embedded), it should **not** be removed or washed out (B), as this can cause further damage. The eye should be protected (often a rigid shield or cup is best, but a loose dressing/bandage is better than manipulation) and the patient referred to a specialist.

269.
Which of the following interventions is recommended for managing a patient with a suspected opioid overdose?
A) Administering naloxone intravenously.
B) Providing respiratory support with bag-valve-mask ventilation.
C) Encouraging the patient to drink fluids rapidly.
D) Administering benzodiazepines for sedation.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A) Administering naloxone intravenously.

Explanation: Naloxone (Narcan) is the specific antidote for opioid overdose. Respiratory support (B) is also crucial, but Naloxone is the definitive pharmacological intervention.

270.
What is the appropriate management for a conscious patient experiencing a seizure?
A) Restrain the patient to prevent injury.
B) Place the patient in a supine position.
C) Protect the patient from injury, clear the area, and monitor the duration of the seizure.
D) Administer antiepileptic medication immediately.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C) Protect the patient from injury, clear the area, and monitor the duration of the seizure.

Explanation: Safety is the priority. Never restrain the patient (A) or put anything in their mouth. Removing dangerous objects prevents trauma.

271.
Which of the following actions is appropriate for managing a conscious patient with a suspected heat cramp?
A) Massaging the affected muscles vigorously.
B) Applying cold packs to the affected area.
C) Providing oral rehydration with electrolyte solutions.
D) Encouraging the patient to continue strenuous activity.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C) Providing oral rehydration with electrolyte solutions.

Explanation: Heat cramps are caused by fluid and electrolyte loss. Replacing sodium and water is the primary treatment.

272.
What is the appropriate initial management for a conscious patient experiencing a syncopal episode (fainting)?
A) Elevating the legs above the level of the heart.
B) Administering intravenous fluids rapidly.
C) Providing reassurance and assisting the patient to a lying position.
D) Administering oxygen via nasal cannula.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A) Elevating the legs above the level of the heart.

Explanation: Elevating the legs promotes venous return to the heart and brain, helping to restore consciousness and blood pressure.

273.
Which of the following actions is appropriate for managing a conscious patient with a dislocated shoulder?
A) Attempting to reduce the dislocation by pulling on the affected arm.
B) Applying a splint to immobilize the arm in the dislocated position.
C) Providing analgesia and applying ice packs to the affected shoulder.
D) Administering intravenous fluids to prevent dehydration.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C) Providing analgesia and applying ice packs to the affected shoulder.

Explanation: Reduction (A) should generally be done by a trained professional after X-ray. Immediate care involves pain management and immobilization (often a sling, but splinting in position of comfort/dislocation is also correct protocol depending on context). C is a safe and effective immediate nursing/first aid action.

274.
Which of the following actions is appropriate for managing a conscious patient with a suspected stroke?
A) Administering aspirin immediately.
B) Placing the patient in a supine position.
C) Encouraging the patient to eat and drink.
D) Activating emergency medical services (EMS) for rapid transport to a stroke center.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: D) Activating emergency medical services (EMS) for rapid transport to a stroke center.

Explanation: "Time is Brain." Rapid transport for CT scan is critical to determine if thrombolytics can be used. Aspirin (A) is contraindicated until hemorrhage is ruled out.

275.
What is the primary goal of cardiopulmonary resuscitation (CPR) in a cardiac arrest situation?
A) Reversing the underlying cause of cardiac arrest.
B) Restoring spontaneous circulation and oxygenation.
C) Providing pain relief and comfort to the patient.
D) Preventing further complications such as organ failure.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B) Restoring spontaneous circulation and oxygenation.

Explanation: The immediate goal of CPR is to manually pump blood (circulation) and provide oxygen to the brain and heart until ROSC (Return of Spontaneous Circulation) is achieved.
