151.
A patient presents with a rash characterized by erythematous papules and vesicles arranged in a linear distribution. Which of the following conditions is most likely responsible for this presentation?
A) Psoriasis
B) Contact dermatitis
C) Scabies
D) Atopic dermatitis
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B) Contact dermatitis

Explanation: A linear distribution of vesicles is the hallmark of Allergic Contact Dermatitis caused by plants like poison ivy or poison oak (Rhus dermatitis). The rash appears exactly where the leaf brushed against the skin.

152.
A patient presents with pruritic, erythematous patches with silvery scales on the elbows, knees, and scalp. Which of the following conditions is most likely responsible for this presentation?
A) Eczema
B) Psoriasis
C) Pityriasis rosea
D) Seborrheic dermatitis
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B) Psoriasis

Explanation: Plaque Psoriasis is classically described as well-demarcated erythematous plaques with overlying silvery scales, predominantly affecting extensor surfaces (elbows, knees) and the scalp.

153.
A patient presents with a pruritic rash with linear streaks and small, fluid-filled blisters. The patient reports recent exposure to poison ivy while gardening. Which of the following conditions is most likely responsible for this presentation?
A) Pemphigus vulgaris
B) Allergic contact dermatitis
C) Bullous pemphigoid
D) Herpes zoster (shingles)
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B) Allergic contact dermatitis

Explanation: This is a classic presentation of Rhus dermatitis (poison ivy), which is a Type IV delayed hypersensitivity reaction (Allergic Contact Dermatitis). The linear arrangement corresponds to where the plant oil (urushiol) touched the skin.

154.
A patient presents with multiple, flesh-colored, dome-shaped papules with a central umbilication on the face. Which of the following conditions is most likely responsible for this presentation?
A) Molluscum contagiosum
B) Acne vulgaris
C) Sebaceous hyperplasia
D) Basal cell carcinoma
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A) Molluscum contagiosum

Explanation: Dome-shaped, flesh-colored papules with central umbilication (a central dimple) are pathognomonic for Molluscum Contagiosum, a viral skin infection caused by a poxvirus.

155.

A) Pityriasis rosea
B) Psoriasis
C) Erythema multiforme
D) Lichen planus

Correct Answer: B) Psoriasis

Explanation: The description of "silvery scales" on "extensor surfaces" along with a family history (genetic component) strongly points to Psoriasis.

156.
A patient presents with a pruritic, annular rash with central clearing and a raised, scaly border. The lesions have a tendency to spread outward in a centrifugal pattern. Which of the following conditions is most likely responsible for this presentation?
A) Tinea corporis (ringworm)
B) Nummular eczema
C) Pityriasis rosea
D) Erythema migrans (Lyme disease rash)
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A) Tinea corporis (ringworm)

Explanation: Tinea corporis presents as a ring-shaped (annular) lesion with a scaly, active border and central clearing. It is a fungal infection.

157.
A patient presents with a painful, vesicular rash in a dermatomal distribution on the left thorax. The patient reports a history of chickenpox during childhood. Which of the following conditions is most likely responsible for this presentation?
A) Herpes simplex virus infection
B) Herpes zoster (shingles)
C) Varicella (chickenpox)
D) Impetigo
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B) Herpes zoster (shingles)

Explanation: Herpes zoster represents the reactivation of the Varicella-Zoster Virus (VZV) that has remained dormant in the dorsal root ganglia since a primary chickenpox infection. It is characterized by a painful, blistering rash that strictly follows a dermatome and does not cross the midline.

158.
A patient presents with grouped vesicles on an erythematous base, affecting the lips and perioral region. The patient reports a history of similar episodes triggered by sunlight exposure and stress. Which of the following conditions is most likely responsible for this presentation?
A) Herpes simplex labialis (cold sores)
B) Perioral dermatitis
C) Angular cheilitis
D) Allergic contact dermatitis
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A) Herpes simplex labialis (cold sores)

Explanation: "Grouped vesicles on an erythematous base" is the classic description of Herpes Simplex Virus (HSV) infection. Recurrence is often triggered by UV light, stress, or illness.

159.
A patient presents with a target-like rash with concentric erythematous rings and central clearing on the trunk and extremities. The patient reports recent exposure to a new medication. Which of the following conditions is most likely responsible for this presentation?
A) Erythema multiforme
B) Pityriasis rosea
C) Urticaria (hives)
D) Seborrheic dermatitis
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A) Erythema multiforme

Explanation: Erythema multiforme is characterized by "target" or "iris" lesions (three zones of color). It is a hypersensitivity reaction often triggered by infections (like HSV) or medications.

160.
A patient presents with multiple grouped vesicles on an erythematous base, affecting the genital area. The patient reports a history of similar lesions in the past, occurring during periods of stress. Which of the following conditions is most likely responsible for this presentation?
A) Herpes simplex genitalis
B) Genital warts (condyloma acuminata)
C) Syphilis (chancre)
D) Molluscum contagiosum
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A) Herpes simplex genitalis

Explanation: Painful grouped vesicles on an erythematous base in the genital region, particularly with a history of recurrence, are indicative of Genital Herpes (usually HSV-2).

161.
A patient presents with intensely pruritic, linear burrows on the web spaces of the hands and wrists. The patient reports a history of close contact with an individual diagnosed with a similar condition. Which of the following conditions is most likely responsible for this presentation?
A) Scabies
B) Pediculosis (lice infestation)
C) Tinea corporis (ringworm)
D) Contact dermatitis
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A) Scabies

Explanation: Scabies is characterized by intense itching (especially at night) and linear burrows, often found in the web spaces of fingers, wrists, and axillae. It is highly contagious via skin-to-skin contact.

162.
A patient presents with well-demarcated, hypopigmented macules with fine scaling, affecting the trunk and proximal extremities. The patient reports a history of sun exposure and outdoor activities. Which of the following conditions is most likely responsible for this presentation?
A) Tinea versicolor
B) Vitiligo
C) Pityriasis rosea
D) Seborrheic keratosis
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A) Tinea versicolor

Explanation: Tinea versicolor (Pityriasis versicolor) is a fungal infection caused by *Malassezia* yeast. It presents as hypo- or hyperpigmented macules that do not tan, becoming more visible after sun exposure. Fine scale is often present.

163.
A patient presents with a pruritic, eczematous rash with erythematous papules, vesicles, and excoriations on the flexural surfaces of the elbows and knees. The patient reports a personal history of asthma and hay fever. Which of the following conditions is most likely responsible for this presentation?
A) Atopic dermatitis
B) Contact dermatitis
C) Seborrheic dermatitis
D) Nummular eczema
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A) Atopic dermatitis

Explanation: Atopic dermatitis (Eczema) typically affects flexural surfaces (antecubital and popliteal fossae) in older children and adults. The "Atopic Triad" consists of Asthma, Allergic Rhinitis (Hay fever), and Atopic Dermatitis.

164.
A patient presents with a well-demarcated, hyperpigmented patch with irregular borders and asymmetric color distribution on the face. The lesion has increased in size over the past few months. Which of the following conditions is most concerning for this presentation?
A) Melanocytic nevus (mole)
B) Lentigo simplex
C) Seborrheic keratosis
D) Melanoma
Show Answer

Correct Answer: D) Melanoma

Explanation: This describes the ABCDE warning signs of Melanoma: Asymmetry, Border irregularity, Color variation, Diameter >6mm, and Evolution (change in size/shape). This requires immediate biopsy.

165.
A patient presents with multiple, dome-shaped, translucent papules with central umbilication on the face and trunk. The lesions are asymptomatic and have been gradually increasing in number. Which of the following conditions is most likely responsible for this presentation?
A) Basal cell carcinoma
B) Molluscum contagiosum
C) Sebaceous hyperplasia
D) Keratoacanthoma
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B) Molluscum contagiosum

Explanation: The description of "multiple, dome-shaped... central umbilication" is classic for Molluscum Contagiosum. While Basal Cell Carcinoma can be translucent, it is rarely multiple and eruptive in this manner without a specific syndrome, and Molluscum is the most common cause of multiple umbilicated lesions.

166.
A patient presents with a pruritic, erythematous rash with edematous plaques and vesicles on the hands and fingers. The patient reports a history of exposure to detergents and frequent hand washing. Which of the following conditions is most likely responsible for this presentation?
A) Atopic dermatitis
B) Contact dermatitis
C) Psoriasis
D) Pityriasis rosea
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B) Contact dermatitis

Explanation: This describes Irritant Contact Dermatitis, caused by physical or chemical damage to the skin barrier from frequent washing and detergents (unlike allergic contact dermatitis which is immune-mediated).

167.
A patient presents with multiple, flesh-colored, papular lesions with a central dell on the face and trunk. The lesions have a tendency to bleed upon minor trauma and have been increasing in number over time. Which of the following conditions is most likely responsible for this presentation?
A) Basal cell carcinoma
B) Squamous cell carcinoma
C) Seborrheic keratosis
D) Actinic keratosis
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A) Basal cell carcinoma

Explanation: While "multiple" lesions can be confusing (usually BCC is solitary), the key feature here is "tendency to bleed upon minor trauma" (friability), which is a classic sign of malignancy, specifically Basal Cell Carcinoma. NOTE: In a typical exam context, if "multiple" is emphasized with "central dell", Molluscum is usually the answer, but Molluscum doesn't typically bleed easily. However, Basal Cell Nevus Syndrome can present with multiple BCCs. Given the options and the bleeding characteristic, BCC is the intended oncology answer.

168.
A patient presents with a pruritic, annular rash with fine scaling and central clearing, affecting the trunk and proximal extremities. The patient reports recent exposure to a new soap and laundry detergent. Which of the following conditions is most likely responsible for this presentation?
A) Tinea corporis (ringworm)
B) Nummular eczema
C) Pityriasis rosea
D) Lichen planus
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B) Nummular eczema

Explanation: While "annular with central clearing" sounds like Tinea (A), the history of "new soap and detergent" suggests an eczematous or contact reaction. Nummular eczema presents with coin-shaped (discoid) lesions that can clear centrally and is often exacerbated by soaps/dryness. Tinea is fungal and not caused by soap.

169.
A patient presents with multiple, hyperpigmented, velvety plaques in flexural areas such as the axillae and neck. The lesions are associated with obesity and insulin resistance. Which of the following conditions is most likely responsible for this presentation?
A) Acanthosis nigricans
B) Seborrheic keratosis
C) Dermatofibroma
D) Epidermal nevus
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A) Acanthosis nigricans

Explanation: Acanthosis nigricans is characterized by dark, velvety thickening of the skin in body folds (neck, axillae). It is strongly associated with insulin resistance (Type 2 Diabetes) and obesity.

170.
A patient presents with a painful, vesicular rash following a linear pattern on the left forearm. The patient reports a history of gardening without gloves. Which of the following conditions is most likely responsible for this presentation?
A) Allergic contact dermatitis
B) Herpes simplex virus infection
C) Scabies
D) Poison ivy dermatitis
Show Answer

Correct Answer: D) Poison ivy dermatitis

Explanation: Poison ivy causes a specific type of Allergic Contact Dermatitis (Option A is also technically correct, but D is the specific diagnosis). The linear pattern of vesicles is pathognomonic for plant dermatitis where the leaf dragged across the skin.

172.
A patient presents with a pruritic, erythematous rash with scaly plaques and satellite papules and pustules in the inguinal folds and gluteal cleft. The patient reports recent antibiotic use for a urinary tract infection. Which of the following conditions is most likely responsible for this presentation?
A) Candidiasis (cutaneous candidiasis)
B) Tinea cruris (jock itch)
C) Erythrasma
D) Intertrigo
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A) Candidiasis (cutaneous candidiasis)

Explanation: The key differentiating feature of Candidiasis is "satellite lesions" (papules/pustules outside the main rash). It is common in skin folds (intertrigo areas) and often follows antibiotic use which kills normal flora.
