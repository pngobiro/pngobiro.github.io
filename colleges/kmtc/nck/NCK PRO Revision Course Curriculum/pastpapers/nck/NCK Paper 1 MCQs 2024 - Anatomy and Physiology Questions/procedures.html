126.
A nurse is preparing to perform a tracheostomy tube change for a patient. What action should the nurse prioritize to ensure patient safety during the procedure?
A) Using sterile technique during the tube change
B) Administering sedation to the patient before the procedure
C) Skipping the suctioning step to minimize patient discomfort
D) Changing the tracheostomy ties after completing the tube change
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A) Using sterile technique during the tube change

Explanation: Maintaining sterile technique is critical to prevent introducing pathogens into the lower airway, which could lead to pneumonia. While Option D is also a correct step (changing ties after the new tube is secure prevents accidental decannulation), sterile technique is the broader safety priority listed here. Note: Keeping an obturator and a smaller size tube at the bedside is also a critical safety measure often tested.

127.
A nurse is preparing to perform a wound irrigation procedure for a patient with a contaminated wound. What solution should the nurse use for wound irrigation?
A) Sterile water
B) Normal saline
C) Hydrogen peroxide
D) Betadine solution
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B) Normal saline

Explanation: Normal saline (0.9% Sodium Chloride) is an isotonic solution that does not damage granulation tissue. Hydrogen peroxide (C) and Betadine (D) can be cytotoxic to healthy new tissue and delay healing, so they are generally avoided for routine irrigation unless specifically ordered for debridement of necrotic tissue.

128.
A nurse is preparing to perform a urinary catheterization for a patient. What action should the nurse take to ensure proper insertion technique?
A) Inserting the catheter without using sterile gloves
B) Using aseptic technique and sterile equipment during catheter insertion
C) Performing catheterization without using lubricating jelly
D) Advancing the catheter rapidly to minimize patient discomfort
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B) Using aseptic technique and sterile equipment during catheter insertion

Explanation: Urinary catheterization is a sterile procedure. Aseptic technique is mandatory to prevent Catheter-Associated Urinary Tract Infections (CAUTI). Lubrication is essential to prevent trauma (C is incorrect).

129.
A nurse is preparing to administer a nasogastric (NG) tube feeding for a patient. What action should the nurse prioritize before initiating tube feeding?
A) Verifying the NG tube placement by auscultating for bowel sounds
B) Securing the NG tube to prevent dislodgement during feeding
C) Checking the patency of the NG tube by aspirating gastric contents
D) Elevating the head of the bed to a semi-Fowler's position
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C) Checking the patency of the NG tube by aspirating gastric contents

Explanation: Verifying placement is the absolute priority to prevent aspiration into the lungs. While pH testing of aspirate is the bedside standard (and X-ray is the gold standard), aspirating contents confirms the tube is likely in the stomach. Auscultating air bolus (A) is no longer considered reliable.

130.
A nurse is preparing to perform a central venous catheter (CVC) dressing change for a patient. What action should the nurse prioritize to minimize the risk of infection?
A) Wearing sterile gloves and a mask during the dressing change
B) Cleaning the insertion site with alcohol-based antiseptic solution
C) Removing the old dressing and applying a new dressing using sterile technique
D) Changing the CVC tubing along with the dressing to prevent contamination
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A) Wearing sterile gloves and a mask during the dressing change

Explanation: Central Line-Associated Bloodstream Infections (CLABSI) are fatal. Maximal sterile barrier precautions (sterile gloves and mask for both nurse and patient) are required during dressing changes. Chlorhexidine is the preferred cleanser (B mentions alcohol-based, which is okay if chlorhexidine-alcohol, but PPE is the broader priority).

131.
A nurse is preparing to perform a bedside bronchoscopy procedure for a patient. What action should the nurse take to ensure patient safety during the procedure?
A) Administering sedation to the patient before the bronchoscopy
B) Obtaining informed consent from the patient or their legal representative
C) Skipping the use of topical anesthesia to minimize patient discomfort
D) Allowing the patient to eat or drink up to one hour before the procedure
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B) Obtaining informed consent from the patient or their legal representative

Explanation: Bronchoscopy is an invasive procedure requiring informed consent. Ensuring consent is signed and on the chart is a key nursing responsibility before the procedure begins. NPO status (D) usually requires 4-8 hours fasting, not 1 hour.

132.
A nurse is preparing to assist with a lumbar puncture procedure for a patient. What action should the nurse take to maintain procedural asepsis?
A) Wearing sterile gloves and a surgical mask during the procedure
B) Cleansing the puncture site with povidone-iodine solution
C) Using a sterile drape to cover the patient during the procedure
D) Avoiding unnecessary movement or talking during the procedure
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A) Wearing sterile gloves and a surgical mask during the procedure

Explanation: Both the provider and the assistant handling sterile items must wear masks to prevent droplet contamination of the spinal canal (meningitis risk). Sterile gloves are required for anyone touching the site or equipment.

133.
A nurse is preparing to assist with a cardiopulmonary resuscitation (CPR) procedure for a patient in cardiac arrest. What action should the nurse prioritize during the initial assessment?
A) Administering oxygen via nasal cannula at 2 liters per minute
B) Checking for responsiveness and calling for help immediately
C) Placing the patient in the recovery position to facilitate breathing
D) Assessing the patient's airway, breathing, and circulation (ABCs)
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B) Checking for responsiveness and calling for help immediately

Explanation: The first step in the BLS algorithm is to verify unresponsiveness and activate the emergency response system ("Call for help") before initiating compressions or breathing.

134.
A nurse is preparing to perform a chest tube insertion for a patient with a pneumothorax. What action should the nurse prioritize to ensure proper placement of the chest tube?
A) Positioning the patient in a semi-Fowler's position with arms elevated
B) Marking the insertion site with a pen to guide tube placement
C) Administering analgesia to the patient before the procedure
D) Confirming the insertion site and direction using imaging guidance
Show Answer

Correct Answer: D) Confirming the insertion site and direction using imaging guidance

Explanation: While positioning (A) is important, verifying the location of the pneumothorax via X-ray or Ultrasound is critical to ensure the tube enters the pleural space correctly and avoids organ damage. Marking the site is often done by the operator based on imaging.

135.
A nurse is preparing to perform a wound debridement procedure for a patient with a necrotic wound. What action should the nurse prioritize to minimize patient discomfort during the procedure?
A) Administering systemic analgesia before the procedure
B) Using sharp debridement techniques to minimize manipulation
C) Applying a topical anesthetic cream to the wound site
D) Performing the debridement procedure quickly to reduce duration
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A) Administering systemic analgesia before the procedure

Explanation: Debridement is painful. Pre-procedural analgesia (systemic) provides the most effective pain control compared to topical agents (C) which may not penetrate deep necrotic tissue effectively.

136.
A nurse is preparing to assist with a peripherally inserted central catheter (PICC) insertion for a patient. What action should the nurse prioritize to maintain procedural asepsis?
A) Wearing sterile gloves and a surgical mask during the procedure
B) Cleansing the insertion site with alcohol-based antiseptic solution
C) Using a sterile drape to cover the patient during the procedure
D) Avoiding unnecessary movement or talking during the procedure
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C) Using a sterile drape to cover the patient during the procedure

Explanation: Maximal sterile barrier precautions for PICC insertion include a large sterile full-body drape. This is a critical component along with mask, cap, gown, and sterile gloves to prevent CLABSI.

137.
A nurse is preparing to perform an intramuscular (IM) injection for a patient. What action should the nurse take to ensure proper injection technique?
A) Selecting a small-gauge needle to minimize patient discomfort
B) Administering the injection rapidly to reduce patient anxiety
C) Aspirating the syringe to confirm needle placement before injection
D) Inserting the needle at a 90-degree angle into the muscle
Show Answer

Correct Answer: D) Inserting the needle at a 90-degree angle into the muscle

Explanation: IM injections require a 90-degree insertion angle to ensure the medication reaches the muscle layer. Note: Aspiration (C) is no longer routinely recommended for vaccines, though practice varies for other medications.

138.
A nurse is preparing to perform a bladder catheterization for a patient with urinary retention. What action should the nurse prioritize to minimize the risk of infection?
A) Using sterile gloves and a surgical mask during catheterization
B) Cleansing the perineal area with povidone-iodine solution before catheter insertion
C) Administering prophylactic antibiotics before the catheterization procedure
D) Using aseptic technique and sterile equipment during catheter insertion
Show Answer

Correct Answer: D) Using aseptic technique and sterile equipment during catheter insertion

Explanation: Breaking the sterile technique is the most common cause of catheter-associated UTIs during insertion. Strict asepsis is mandatory.

139.
A nurse is preparing to perform a gastric lavage procedure for a patient with gastrointestinal bleeding. What action should the nurse prioritize to ensure procedural effectiveness?
A) Administering antacid medication to the patient before the procedure
B) Using lukewarm water for gastric lavage to avoid thermal injury
C) Limiting the lavage fluid volume to prevent fluid overload
D) Positioning the patient in a left lateral decubitus position during lavage
Show Answer

Correct Answer: D) Positioning the patient in a left lateral decubitus position during lavage

Explanation: This position decreases the risk of aspiration if the patient vomits and also prevents the lavage fluid from passing through the pylorus into the small intestine, keeping it in the stomach for removal.

140.
A nurse is preparing to assist with a bone marrow biopsy procedure for a patient. What action should the nurse prioritize to ensure patient comfort during the procedure?
A) Administering intravenous sedation to the patient before the biopsy
B) Providing distraction techniques such as music or relaxation exercises
C) Applying a topical anesthetic cream to the biopsy site
D) Allowing the patient to eat or drink up to one hour before the procedure
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A) Administering intravenous sedation to the patient before the biopsy

Explanation: Bone marrow biopsies can be very painful (especially the aspiration part). Systemic sedation (conscious sedation) alongside local anesthesia is the standard of care to ensure comfort. Topical cream alone (C) does not penetrate deep enough to the periosteum.

141.
A nurse is preparing to assist with a thoracentesis procedure for a patient with pleural effusion. What action should the nurse prioritize to ensure procedural safety?
A) Administering pre-procedure antibiotics to prevent infection
B) Ensuring the patient empties their bladder before the procedure
C) Positioning the patient upright with arms supported on a bedside table
D) Confirming the patient's informed consent before the procedure
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C) Positioning the patient upright with arms supported on a bedside table

Explanation: This position (orthopneic position) expands the intercostal spaces and allows fluid to accumulate at the base of the lungs, making it easier and safer to insert the needle without hitting the lung parenchyma.

142.
A nurse is preparing to perform a continuous bladder irrigation (CBI) procedure for a patient following urological surgery. What action should the nurse prioritize to prevent complications during CBI?
A) Adjusting the irrigation flow rate based on the patient's urine output
B) Changing the irrigation solution every 24 hours to maintain sterility
C) Using tap water for irrigation to prevent electrolyte imbalances
D) Limiting the duration of CBI to minimize the risk of bladder distention
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A) Adjusting the irrigation flow rate based on the patient's urine output

Explanation: The goal of CBI is to prevent clot formation. The nurse must titrate the irrigation rate to keep the urine pink and free of clots. If the urine becomes bloody, the rate is increased; if clear, it is decreased. Tap water (C) is never used; sterile saline is standard.

143.
A nurse is preparing to assist with a lumbar epidural steroid injection (LESI) procedure for a patient with chronic back pain. What action should the nurse prioritize to ensure procedural accuracy?
A) Confirming the patient's identity and procedure site using two identifiers
B) Positioning the patient in a prone position with a pillow under the abdomen
C) Providing conscious sedation to the patient to minimize discomfort
D) Administering local anesthesia to the skin before needle insertion
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A) Confirming the patient's identity and procedure site using two identifiers

Explanation: While positioning and anesthesia are important, the absolute priority for procedural *accuracy* and safety (National Patient Safety Goal) is the "Time Out" to verify correct patient, correct site, and correct procedure.

144.
A nurse is preparing to assist with a transesophageal echocardiogram (TEE) procedure for a patient. What action should the nurse prioritize to ensure patient safety during TEE?
A) Placing the patient in a supine position with arms at their sides
B) Administering intravenous sedation to the patient before the procedure
C) Instructing the patient to refrain from eating or drinking for six hours before the procedure
D) Ensuring the availability of emergency equipment and resuscitation medications
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C) Instructing the patient to refrain from eating or drinking for six hours before the procedure

Explanation: The TEE probe is inserted into the esophagus. NPO status is critical to prevent aspiration of gastric contents during the procedure, especially since the gag reflex is anesthetized.

145.
A nurse is preparing to perform a colposcopy procedure for a patient with abnormal cervical cytology results. What action should the nurse prioritize to enhance patient comfort during colposcopy?
A) Administering intravenous sedation to the patient before the procedure
B) Explaining the procedure steps and providing emotional support to the patient
C) Applying a topical anesthetic cream to the cervix before colposcopy
D) Allowing the patient to have a support person present during the procedure
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B) Explaining the procedure steps and providing emotional support to the patient

Explanation: Colposcopy can be anxiety-inducing. Education reduces fear. The procedure is generally not painful enough to warrant IV sedation (A), though a biopsy might cause a cramp. Emotional support is the key comfort measure.

146.
A nurse is preparing to perform an intracranial pressure (ICP) monitoring procedure for a patient with traumatic brain injury. What action should the nurse prioritize to ensure procedural accuracy?
A) Ensuring proper zeroing and calibration of the ICP monitoring system
B) Positioning the patient in a high-Fowler's position during the procedure
C) Administering prophylactic antibiotics to prevent infection
D) Placing the ICP monitor in the subarachnoid space via lumbar puncture
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A) Ensuring proper zeroing and calibration of the ICP monitoring system

Explanation: For the data to be reliable, the transducer must be leveled (usually to the tragus of the ear/Foramen of Monro) and zeroed. Incorrect calibration leads to false readings and potentially inappropriate treatment.

147.
A nurse is preparing to assist with a cardiopulmonary exercise stress test for a patient. What action should the nurse prioritize to ensure patient safety during the test?
A) Administering a beta-blocker medication to the patient before the test
B) Monitoring the patient's electrocardiogram (ECG) rhythm continuously during the test
C) Encouraging the patient to consume a heavy meal two hours before the test
D) Allowing the patient to resume normal activities immediately after the test
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B) Monitoring the patient's electrocardiogram (ECG) rhythm continuously during the test

Explanation: The goal of the stress test is to provoke ischemia or arrhythmia. Continuous ECG monitoring is essential to detect dangerous arrhythmias or ST changes immediately so the test can be stopped if safety is compromised.

148.
A nurse is preparing to assist with a magnetic resonance imaging (MRI) procedure for a patient. What action should the nurse prioritize to ensure procedural safety?
A) Screening the patient for contraindications to MRI, such as metal implants or claustrophobia
B) Administering intravenous sedation to the patient before the procedure
C) Placing the patient in a supine position with arms at their sides during the MRI scan
D) Allowing the patient to wear metallic jewelry or accessories during the procedure
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A) Screening the patient for contraindications to MRI, such as metal implants or claustrophobia

Explanation: The MRI magnet is always on. Ferromagnetic metal implants (pacemakers, aneurysm clips) can move or heat up, causing fatal injury. Screening is the absolute safety priority.

149.
A nurse is preparing to assist with a transurethral resection of the prostate (TURP) procedure for a patient with benign prostatic hyperplasia (BPH). What action should the nurse prioritize to prevent fluid overload during TURP?
A) Monitoring the patient's serum electrolyte levels during the procedure
B) Administering diuretic medications to the patient before the procedure
C) Limiting the duration of irrigation fluid instillation during TURP
D) Adjusting the irrigation fluid temperature to promote hemostasis
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C) Limiting the duration of irrigation fluid instillation during TURP

Explanation: "TURP Syndrome" is caused by the absorption of large amounts of irrigation fluid through open prostatic veins, leading to fluid overload and dilutional hyponatremia. The most effective prevention is limiting the surgical time and fluid exposure.

150.
A nurse is preparing to assist with a percutaneous endoscopic gastrostomy (PEG) tube insertion for a patient requiring long-term enteral feeding. What action should the nurse prioritize to ensure procedural success?
A) Confirming the patient's fasting status before the PEG tube insertion
B) Positioning the patient in a supine position with the head of the bed elevated
C) Administering prophylactic antibiotics to prevent infection
D) Coordinating with radiology or gastroenterology for procedural guidance
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A) Confirming the patient's fasting status before the PEG tube insertion

Explanation: Since the procedure involves endoscopy and potential sedation, NPO status is critical to prevent aspiration of gastric contents. While antibiotics (C) are often given, NPO is the immediate pre-procedure safety check.
