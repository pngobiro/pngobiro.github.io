Dental, Alimentary, & Biliary

NCK KRCHN Paper 1 - 2024

Instructions

Select the most appropriate answer for each question. Click "Show Answer" to view the correct option and detailed explanation.

226.
Which of the following dental conditions is characterized by the irreversible demineralization of the enamel and dentin, leading to cavitation and destruction of tooth structure?
A) Gingivitis
B) Dental caries
C) Periodontitis
D) Dental abscess
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B) Dental caries

Explanation: Dental caries (cavities/tooth decay) involves the demineralization of tooth structure (enamel and dentin) caused by acids produced by bacterial fermentation of dietary carbohydrates.

227.
Which of the following is a common oral manifestation of celiac disease characterized by enamel defects with horizontal or vertical grooves on the teeth?
A) Dental caries
B) Enamel hypoplasia
C) Periodontal disease
D) Oral candidiasis
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B) Enamel hypoplasia

Explanation: Celiac disease causes malabsorption of nutrients (like calcium and vitamin D) critical for tooth development. This often manifests as Enamel Hypoplasia, appearing as grooves, pitting, or discoloration.

228.
Which of the following dental conditions is characterized by inflammation of the gingival tissues surrounding the teeth, often caused by poor oral hygiene?
A) Dental caries
B) Periodontitis
C) Dental abscess
D) Gingivitis
Show Answer

Correct Answer: D) Gingivitis

Explanation: Gingivitis is the inflammation of the gums (gingiva) typically caused by plaque accumulation due to poor oral hygiene. Unlike periodontitis, it does not involve bone loss and is reversible.

229.
Which of the following dental conditions is characterized by a localized collection of pus resulting from a bacterial infection of the tooth pulp?
A) Dental caries
B) Periodontitis
C) Dental abscess
D) Oral candidiasis
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C) Dental abscess

Explanation: An abscess is a collection of pus. A periapical abscess typically results from infection of the pulp (often a sequela of untreated caries).

230.
Which of the following structures is responsible for the initial mechanical breakdown of food during mastication?
A) Tongue
B) Pharynx
C) Salivary glands
D) Teeth
Show Answer

Correct Answer: D) Teeth

Explanation: Mastication (chewing) is the process of mechanical breakdown performed primarily by the teeth.

231.
Which of the following salivary glands is located inferior and posterior to the mandible and produces serous saliva rich in enzymes?
A) Parotid gland
B) Sublingual gland
C) Submandibular gland
D) Submaxillary gland
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C) Submandibular gland

Explanation: The submandibular glands are located beneath the mandible. They produce a mixture of serous and mucous saliva (though mostly serous). Note: The Parotid is purely serous but is located anterior/inferior to the ear. The location description "inferior and posterior to the mandible" best fits the Submandibular gland (also known as Submaxillary).

232.
Which of the following structures is responsible for transporting food from the oral cavity to the esophagus during swallowing?
A) Tongue
B) Pharynx
C) Esophagus
D) Epiglottis
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B) Pharynx

Explanation: The pharynx is the common pathway for food and air, connecting the oral cavity to the esophagus (and larynx). It facilitates the swallowing reflex (deglutition).

233.
Which of the following structures is responsible for secreting bile, which aids in the emulsification and digestion of fats?
A) Liver
B) Gallbladder
C) Pancreas
D) Spleen
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A) Liver

Explanation: The Liver produces and secretes bile. The Gallbladder stores and concentrates it.

234.
Which of the following dental conditions is characterized by inflammation and infection of the soft tissues surrounding the apex of a tooth root?
A) Gingivitis
B) Periodontitis
C) Dental abscess
D) Oral candidiasis
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C) Dental abscess

Explanation: Specifically, a periapical abscess occurs at the apex (tip) of the root.

235.
Which of the following salivary glands is located superior and anterior to the ear and produces mainly serous saliva?
A) Parotid gland
B) Sublingual gland
C) Submandibular gland
D) Submaxillary gland
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A) Parotid gland

Explanation: The Parotid gland is the largest salivary gland, located in front of the ear, and produces purely serous (watery) saliva rich in amylase.

236.
Which of the following structures is responsible for the mechanical and chemical digestion of food, as well as the absorption of nutrients into the bloodstream?
A) Liver
B) Stomach
C) Pancreas
D) Large intestine
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B) Stomach (by exclusion)

Explanation: This question has imperfect options. The Small Intestine is the primary site for nutrient absorption. However, among the choices:

  • Stomach: Performs significant mechanical (churning) and chemical (HCl/Pepsin) digestion, though limited absorption (alcohol, aspirin).
  • Large Intestine: Absorbs water/electrolytes, not main nutrients.
  • Liver/Pancreas: Accessory organs, food does not pass through them.
In the absence of "Small Intestine," "Stomach" is the best fit for the digestion component.

237.
Which of the following dental conditions is characterized by inflammation and bleeding of the gingival tissues, often caused by bacterial plaque accumulation?
A) Dental caries
B) Gingivitis
C) Periodontitis
D) Dental abscess
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B) Gingivitis

Explanation: (Repeated concept). Inflammation and bleeding of gums = Gingivitis.

238.
Which of the following dental conditions is characterized by inflammation and destruction of the supporting structures of the teeth, including the gingiva, periodontal ligament, and alveolar bone?
A) Dental caries
B) Gingivitis
C) Periodontitis
D) Dental abscess
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C) Periodontitis

Explanation: The key distinction between Gingivitis and Periodontitis is the involvement of the supporting structures (ligament and bone), leading to irreversible damage.

239.
Which of the following structures is responsible for the production of digestive enzymes and bicarbonate-rich pancreatic juice?
A) Liver
B) Gallbladder
C) Pancreas
D) Spleen
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C) Pancreas

Explanation: The exocrine pancreas produces enzymes (amylase, lipase, trypsin) and bicarbonate to neutralize stomach acid in the duodenum.

240.
Which of the following dental conditions is characterized by a localized accumulation of pus within the periodontal tissues, often associated with deep periodontal pockets?
A) Dental caries
B) Gingivitis
C) Periodontitis
D) Dental abscess
Show Answer

Correct Answer: D) Dental abscess

Explanation: Specifically, a periodontal abscess. It occurs within the periodontal pocket.

241.
Which of the following dental conditions is characterized by the abnormal wearing away of tooth structure due to factors such as bruxism or acidic erosion?
A) Dental caries
B) Attrition
C) Abrasion
D) Erosion
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B) Attrition (or D based on context)

Explanation: This question combines two causes.

  • Attrition: Wear from tooth-to-tooth contact (Bruxism).
  • Erosion: Wear from chemical action (Acids).
  • Abrasion: Wear from foreign objects (Toothbrush).
If the question emphasizes "bruxism," the answer is Attrition. If it emphasizes "acidic," it is Erosion. Given Bruxism is listed first, Attrition is likely the intended key, but the phrasing is ambiguous.

242.
Which of the following structures is responsible for the production of saliva, which aids in lubricating the oral cavity and initiating the digestion of carbohydrates?
A) Liver
B) Gallbladder
C) Salivary glands
D) Pancreas
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C) Salivary glands

Explanation: Salivary glands produce saliva containing salivary amylase (ptyalin) which begins starch digestion.

243.
Which of the following structures is responsible for storing and concentrating bile produced by the liver until it is needed for digestion?
A) Liver
B) Gallbladder
C) Pancreas
D) Spleen
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B) Gallbladder

Explanation: The gallbladder stores and concentrates bile, releasing it into the duodenum upon stimulation by CCK.

244.
Which of the following structures is responsible for propelling food from the oral cavity to the stomach through coordinated muscular contractions?
A) Tongue
B) Pharynx
C) Esophagus
D) Epiglottis
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C) Esophagus

Explanation: The esophagus connects the pharynx to the stomach and uses peristalsis to propel the food bolus.

245.
Which of the following structures is responsible for producing hydrochloric acid and intrinsic factor, essential for the digestion of proteins and absorption of vitamin B12, respectively?
A) Liver
B) Stomach
C) Pancreas
D) Small intestine
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B) Stomach

Explanation: Parietal cells in the stomach lining secrete both HCl and Intrinsic Factor.

246.
Which of the following dental conditions is characterized by the involuntary grinding or clenching of teeth, often leading to tooth wear, muscle pain, and temporomandibular joint dysfunction?
A) Dental caries
B) Attrition
C) Bruxism
D) Periodontitis
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C) Bruxism

Explanation: Bruxism is the *action* (grinding/clenching). Attrition is the *result* (wear). The question describes the action leading to the result.

247.
Which of the following structures is responsible for absorbing water and electrolytes from undigested food residue, forming feces?
A) Liver
B) Stomach
C) Pancreas
D) Large intestine
Show Answer

Correct Answer: D) Large intestine

Explanation: The primary function of the colon (large intestine) is the reabsorption of water and electrolytes to consolidate waste into feces.

248.
Which of the following dental conditions is characterized by the wearing away of tooth structure due to friction from external sources such as toothbrushing or abrasive toothpaste?
A) Dental caries
B) Attrition
C) Abrasion
D) Erosion
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C) Abrasion

Explanation: Abrasion is mechanical wear from foreign objects (like a toothbrush). Attrition is tooth-to-tooth. Erosion is chemical.

249.
Which of the following structures is responsible for producing digestive enzymes and bicarbonate-rich pancreatic juice, which are essential for the digestion of carbohydrates, fats, and proteins in the small intestine?
A) Liver
B) Gallbladder
C) Pancreas
D) Spleen
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C) Pancreas

Explanation: (Similar to Q239). The pancreas secretes the major digestive enzymes into the duodenum.

250.
Which of the following dental conditions is characterized by the destruction of tooth structure due to exposure to acidic substances, such as gastric acid or acidic beverages?
A) Dental caries
B) Attrition
C) Abrasion
D) Erosion
Show Answer

Correct Answer: D) Erosion

Explanation: Erosion is the chemical dissolution of tooth structure by acids not of bacterial origin (e.g., GERD, soda, citrus).
