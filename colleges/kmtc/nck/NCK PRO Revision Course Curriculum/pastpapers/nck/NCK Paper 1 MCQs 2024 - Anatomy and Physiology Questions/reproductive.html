Reproductive Health & Gyn

NCK KRCHN Paper 1 - 2024

276.
A 35-year-old woman presents with irregular menstrual cycles, hirsutism, and acne. Which of the following conditions is most likely to be associated with these symptoms?
A) Polycystic ovary syndrome (PCOS)
B) Endometriosis
C) Ovarian cancer
D) Uterine fibroids
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A) Polycystic ovary syndrome (PCOS)

Explanation: The triad of irregular periods (oligoovulation), hyperandrogenism (hirsutism/acne), and polycystic ovaries defines PCOS (Rotterdam criteria).

277.
A 28-year-old woman presents with lower abdominal pain, dyspareunia, and dysmenorrhea that worsens during menstruation. On pelvic examination, tender nodules are palpated along the uterosacral ligaments. Which condition is most likely to be responsible for these findings?
A) Polycystic ovary syndrome (PCOS)
B) Endometriosis
C) Pelvic inflammatory disease (PID)
D) Uterine fibroids
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B) Endometriosis

Explanation: The classic triad of Endometriosis is Dysmenorrhea, Dyspareunia, and Dyschezia (painful defecation, often implied). Uterosacral nodules are a specific finding.

278.
Which of the following laboratory findings is characteristic of a patient with a diagnosis of gestational diabetes mellitus (GDM)?
A) Fasting blood glucose ≥ 126 mg/dL
B) Random plasma glucose ≥ 200 mg/dL
C) Hemoglobin A1c (HbA1c) ≥ 6.5%
D) Oral glucose tolerance test (OGTT) 2-hour plasma glucose ≥ 140 mg/dL
Show Answer

Correct Answer: D) Oral glucose tolerance test (OGTT) 2-hour plasma glucose ≥ 140 mg/dL

Explanation: GDM is typically diagnosed with OGTT. The other values (A, B, C) are criteria for *overt* diabetes (Type 2), not specifically GDM which has lower thresholds.

279.
A 32-year-old pregnant woman presents with painless vaginal bleeding at 10 weeks of gestation. On ultrasound, a gestational sac with no embryo is visualized within the uterus. Which of the following conditions is most likely to be responsible for these findings?
A) Threatened abortion
B) Inevitable abortion
C) Missed abortion
D) Ectopic pregnancy
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C) Missed abortion

Explanation: An empty sac (blighted ovum) or embryo with no heartbeat, with the cervix closed and often no expulsion, defines a Missed Abortion.

280.
Which of the following diagnostic tests is most appropriate for evaluating a woman with suspected cervical dysplasia?
A) Pap smear
B) Endometrial biopsy
C) Colposcopy with biopsy
D) Transvaginal ultrasound
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C) Colposcopy with biopsy

Explanation: Pap smear is a *screening* test. Colposcopy with biopsy is the *diagnostic* test for confirmation.

281.
A 45-year-old woman presents with heavy menstrual bleeding and iron deficiency anemia. On pelvic examination, the uterus is enlarged, and multiple irregularly shaped masses are palpated. Which condition is most likely to be responsible for these findings?
A) Leiomyomas (uterine fibroids)
B) Endometrial hyperplasia
C) Endometrial polyps
D) Adenomyosis
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A) Leiomyomas (uterine fibroids)

Explanation: "Multiple irregularly shaped masses" and enlarged uterus are classic for fibroids.

282.
Which of the following conditions is characterized by the presence of high-risk human papillomavirus (HPV) types and is associated with an increased risk of cervical cancer?
A) Condyloma acuminatum
B) Lichen sclerosus
C) Cervical intraepithelial neoplasia (CIN)
D) Trichomoniasis
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C) Cervical intraepithelial neoplasia (CIN)

Explanation: CIN is the precursor lesion to cervical cancer, driven by high-risk HPV.

283.
A 28-year-old woman presents with cyclic pelvic pain, dysmenorrhea, and infertility. On pelvic examination, tender nodules are palpated along the uterosacral ligaments. Which of the following conditions is most likely to be responsible for these findings?
A) Polycystic ovary syndrome (PCOS)
B) Endometriosis
C) Adenomyosis
D) Leiomyomas (uterine fibroids)
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B) Endometriosis

Explanation: Repetitive question (see Q277). Infertility is another key feature of Endometriosis.

284.
A 20-year-old woman presents with sudden onset of severe lower abdominal pain and missed menstrual periods for the past two months. She has a positive urine pregnancy test. On transvaginal ultrasound, an empty uterus is visualized, and there is fluid in the cul-de-sac. Which condition is most likely to be responsible for these findings?
A) Ovarian cyst rupture
B) Ectopic pregnancy
C) Septic abortion
D) Ovarian torsion
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B) Ectopic pregnancy

Explanation: Positive pregnancy test + Empty uterus + Free fluid (blood) = Ruptured Ectopic Pregnancy.

285.
Which of the following conditions is characterized by excessive menstrual bleeding due to abnormal proliferation of endometrial tissue outside the uterus?
A) Endometriosis
B) Endometrial hyperplasia
C) Leiomyomas (uterine fibroids)
D) Adenomyosis
Show Answer

Correct Answer: D) Adenomyosis (or A with flawed definition)

Explanation: *Correction:* Endometriosis is tissue *outside* the uterus but typically causes pain, not heavy bleeding. Adenomyosis is tissue *inside* the myometrium (muscle wall) causing heavy bleeding and pain. The question asks about tissue "outside the uterus" causing "excessive bleeding". This phrasing is tricky. Technically, Adenomyosis is "endometrium within myometrium". Endometriosis is "outside uterus". Heavy bleeding is the hallmark of Adenomyosis/Fibroids. Endometriosis bleeding is internal/cyclic pain. However, some sources conflate the two. Given "Outside", A fits the location description, but D fits the *symptom* (heavy bleeding) best. If strictly interpreting "outside uterus", it's A. If interpreting "abnormal proliferation... causing bleeding", it might be D or B. But B is *inside*. Let's stick to the definition: "Tissue outside the uterus" = Endometriosis.

286.
A 38-year-old woman presents with cyclic pelvic pain, dysmenorrhea, and dyspareunia. On pelvic examination, the uterus is retroverted and fixed. Which of the following conditions is most likely to be responsible for these findings?
A) Endometriosis
B) Adenomyosis
C) Leiomyomas (uterine fibroids)
D) Pelvic inflammatory disease (PID)
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A) Endometriosis

Explanation: A "fixed, retroverted uterus" is a classic sign of adhesions from Endometriosis.

287.
A 25-year-old woman presents with cyclic pelvic pain, dysmenorrhea, and dyspareunia. On pelvic examination, the uterus is retroverted, and a tender, nodular mass is palpated behind the uterus. Which of the following conditions is most likely to be responsible for these findings?
A) Endometriosis
B) Adenomyosis
C) Leiomyomas (uterine fibroids)
D) Pelvic inflammatory disease (PID)
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A) Endometriosis

Explanation: Nodularity in the Cul-de-sac (Pouch of Douglas) is highly specific for Endometriosis.

288.
Which of the following is the most common risk factor for the development of ovarian cancer?
A) Nulliparity
B) Early menarche
C) Late menopause
D) Family history of breast cancer
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A) Nulliparity (or D)

Explanation: Family history (BRCA) is the *strongest* risk factor, but Nulliparity (uninterrupted ovulation) is the most *common* physiologic risk factor listed.

289.
A 30-year-old woman presents with cyclic pelvic pain, dysmenorrhea, and infertility. On pelvic ultrasound, a chocolate cyst is visualized within the ovary. Which of the following conditions is most likely to be responsible for these findings?
A) Polycystic ovary syndrome (PCOS)
B) Endometriosis
C) Ovarian torsion
D) Ovarian cyst rupture
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B) Endometriosis

Explanation: A "Chocolate Cyst" (Endometrioma) is pathognomonic for Ovarian Endometriosis.

290.
Which of the following screening tests is recommended for cervical cancer prevention in women aged 21 to 65 years?
A) Human papillomavirus (HPV) testing alone
B) Cytology (Pap smear) alone
C) Co-testing with cytology and HPV testing
D) Endometrial biopsy
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B) Cytology (Pap smear) alone (for 21-29) or C (for 30-65)

Explanation: Guidelines vary by age. 21-29 is Pap only. 30-65 is Co-testing. Given the broad range "21 to 65", Pap smear is the common denominator, but Co-testing is the preferred standard for the older group. Often exams look for "Pap smear" as the fundamental answer.

291.
A 55-year-old postmenopausal woman presents with vaginal bleeding. On pelvic examination, the cervix appears healthy, and there is no cervical motion tenderness. Which of the following conditions is most likely to be responsible for these findings?
A) Cervical cancer
B) Endometrial cancer
C) Vaginal cancer
D) Cervical polyp
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B) Endometrial cancer

Explanation: Postmenopausal bleeding is Endometrial Cancer until proven otherwise.

292.
Which of the following conditions is characterized by the presence of multiple fluid-filled sacs within the ovaries and is associated with menstrual irregularities and hyperandrogenism?
A) Polycystic ovary syndrome (PCOS)
B) Ovarian torsion
C) Ovarian cyst rupture
D) Ovarian cancer
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A) Polycystic ovary syndrome (PCOS)

Explanation: The name "Polycystic" refers to the multiple follicles (fluid-filled sacs).

293.
Which of the following interventions is recommended for managing a patient with suspected pelvic inflammatory disease (PID)?
A) Empiric antibiotic therapy directed against common pathogens.
B) Surgical exploration and drainage of pelvic abscesses.
C) Hormonal therapy to suppress ovarian function.
D) Symptomatic treatment with nonsteroidal anti-inflammatory drugs (NSAIDs).
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A) Empiric antibiotic therapy directed against common pathogens.

Explanation: Early antibiotics prevent long-term sequelae like infertility.

294.
A 22-year-old woman presents with cyclic pelvic pain, dysmenorrhea, and infertility. On pelvic examination, the uterus is retroverted and enlarged. Which of the following conditions is most likely to be responsible for these findings?
A) Endometriosis
B) Adenomyosis
C) Leiomyomas (uterine fibroids)
D) Pelvic inflammatory disease (PID)
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B) Adenomyosis

Explanation: Enlarged ("boggy") uterus + dysmenorrhea in a multiparous woman is classic for Adenomyosis. (Endometriosis uterus is usually normal size but fixed).

295.
Which of the following is a common complication of untreated gonorrhea or chlamydia infection in women?
A) Cervical dysplasia
B) Ectopic pregnancy
C) Urinary incontinence
D) Pelvic organ prolapse
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B) Ectopic pregnancy

Explanation: PID scars the tubes, leading to ectopic risk or infertility.

296.
Which of the following is a hallmark symptom of premenstrual syndrome (PMS)?
A) Pelvic pain
B) Breast tenderness
C) Postmenopausal bleeding
D) Dyspareunia
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B) Breast tenderness

Explanation: Mastalgia is a common somatic symptom of PMS.

297.
Which of the following conditions is characterized by the presence of abnormal cervical cells that are classified as low-grade squamous intraepithelial lesions (LSIL) on cytology?
A) Cervical cancer
B) Cervical intraepithelial neoplasia (CIN)
C) Cervical polyps
D) Cervicitis
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B) Cervical intraepithelial neoplasia (CIN)

Explanation: LSIL on Pap correlates with CIN 1 on biopsy.

299.
Which of the following is the most common type of ovarian tumor in women of reproductive age?
A) Serous cystadenoma
B) Mucinous cystadenoma
C) Teratoma (dermoid cyst)
D) Endometrioma
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C) Teratoma (dermoid cyst)

Explanation: Mature cystic teratoma is the most common *benign* germ cell tumor in this age group.

300.
Which of the following is a risk factor for the development of ovarian cancer?
A) Multiparity
B) Use of oral contraceptives
C) Early age at menarche
D) Family history of breast cancer
Show Answer

Correct Answer: D) Family history of breast cancer

Explanation: BRCA mutations increase risk for both. (A and B are *protective* factors due to ovulation suppression).
