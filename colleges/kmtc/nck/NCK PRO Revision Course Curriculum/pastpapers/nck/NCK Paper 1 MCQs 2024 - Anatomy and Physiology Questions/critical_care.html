Critical Care / ICU

NCK KRCHN Paper 1 - 2024

501.
A patient in the intensive care unit (ICU) develops acute respiratory distress syndrome (ARDS) characterized by hypoxemia and bilateral pulmonary infiltrates. What intervention should the healthcare team prioritize to manage the patient's condition?
A) Initiate mechanical ventilation with low tidal volume strategy.
B) Administer intravenous fluids to maintain hemodynamic stability.
C) Prescribe broad-spectrum antibiotics for suspected pneumonia.
D) Perform chest physiotherapy to promote airway clearance.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A) Initiate mechanical ventilation with low tidal volume strategy.

Explanation: Lung protective ventilation (6ml/kg PBW) reduces mortality in ARDS.

502.
A patient admitted to the ICU develops septic shock with refractory hypotension despite fluid resuscitation. Which intervention should the healthcare team prioritize to improve the patient's hemodynamic status?
A) Administer vasopressor medications to increase systemic vascular resistance.
B) Initiate continuous renal replacement therapy (CRRT) for fluid removal.
C) Perform a bedside echocardiogram to assess cardiac function.
D) Recommend a transfusion of packed red blood cells to optimize oxygen delivery.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A) Administer vasopressor medications to increase systemic vascular resistance.

Explanation: After fluid resuscitation fails, vasopressors (e.g., Norepinephrine) are the next step in septic shock management.

503.
A patient in the ICU develops acute kidney injury (AKI) secondary to sepsis. What intervention should the healthcare team prioritize to manage the patient's renal function?
A) Initiate continuous veno-venous hemofiltration (CVVH) for renal replacement therapy.
B) Administer diuretic medications to promote urine output.
C) Implement strict fluid restriction to prevent further renal injury.
D) Perform a renal biopsy to assess the underlying cause of AKI.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A) Initiate continuous veno-venous hemofiltration (CVVH) for renal replacement therapy.

Explanation: In hemodynamically unstable septic patients, CVVH (CRRT) is preferred over intermittent hemodialysis.

504.
A patient admitted to the ICU develops delirium characterized by acute onset confusion and agitation. What intervention should the healthcare team prioritize to manage the patient's delirium?
A) Administer antipsychotic medications to reduce agitation.
B) Implement environmental modifications to promote orientation.
C) Refer the patient to a psychiatrist for further evaluation.
D) Prescribe benzodiazepines for sedation and calming effect.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B) Implement environmental modifications to promote orientation.

Explanation: Non-pharmacological interventions (sleep hygiene, reorientation) are the first-line treatment for delirium. Antipsychotics are used if safety is at risk.

505.
A patient undergoing mechanical ventilation in the ICU develops ventilator-associated pneumonia (VAP). What intervention should the healthcare team prioritize to manage the patient's respiratory infection?
A) Administer broad-spectrum antibiotics targeting common pathogens.
B) Perform endotracheal suctioning to clear airway secretions.
C) Implement oral hygiene measures to reduce bacterial colonization.
D) Recommend early mobilization to prevent complications of immobility.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A) Administer broad-spectrum antibiotics targeting common pathogens.

Explanation: Once VAP is diagnosed (developed), treatment is antibiotics. Oral hygiene (C) is prevention.

506.
A patient admitted to the ICU develops acute gastrointestinal bleeding requiring urgent intervention. What intervention should the healthcare team prioritize to manage the patient's bleeding?
A) Perform endoscopic hemostasis with mechanical or thermal techniques.
B) Administer proton pump inhibitors (PPIs) to reduce gastric acid secretion.
C) Implement strict bed rest to minimize physical exertion and bleeding risk.
D) Recommend transfusion of fresh frozen plasma to correct coagulopathy.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A) Perform endoscopic hemostasis with mechanical or thermal techniques.

Explanation: Endoscopy is both diagnostic and therapeutic for stopping the bleed.

507.
A patient in the ICU develops acute exacerbation of chronic obstructive pulmonary disease (COPD) characterized by worsening dyspnea and increased sputum production. What intervention should the healthcare team prioritize to manage the patient's exacerbation?
A) Administer bronchodilator medications via nebulization for bronchodilation.
B) Initiate non-invasive positive pressure ventilation (NIPPV) for respiratory support.
C) Perform arterial blood gas analysis to assess oxygenation and ventilation.
D) Recommend chest physiotherapy to promote airway clearance.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B) Initiate non-invasive positive pressure ventilation (NIPPV) for respiratory support.

Explanation: Repetitive concept. NIPPV is the key ventilatory support for severe COPD exacerbation to prevent intubation.

508.
A patient admitted to the ICU develops acute myocardial infarction (MI) with ST-segment elevation on electrocardiogram (ECG). What intervention should the healthcare team prioritize to manage the patient's MI?
A) Perform emergent coronary angiography for revascularization.
B) Administer fibrinolytic therapy to restore coronary blood flow.
C) Implement strict bed rest to minimize cardiac workload.
D) Recommend beta-blocker medications to reduce myocardial oxygen demand.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A) Perform emergent coronary angiography for revascularization.

Explanation: PCI (Angiography/Stenting) is the gold standard for STEMI if available within 90 minutes.

509.
A patient admitted to the ICU develops acute pancreatitis with severe abdominal pain and elevated pancreatic enzymes. What intervention should the healthcare team prioritize to manage the patient's pancreatitis?
A) Administer intravenous fluids to maintain adequate hydration.
B) Perform an abdominal ultrasound to assess pancreatic morphology.
C) Implement total parenteral nutrition (TPN) to meet nutritional needs.
D) Recommend proton pump inhibitors (PPIs) for gastric acid suppression.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A) Administer intravenous fluids to maintain adequate hydration.

Explanation: Aggressive fluid resuscitation is the most critical early intervention in acute pancreatitis.

510.
A patient in the ICU develops catheter-related bloodstream infection (CRBSI) associated with a central venous catheter. What intervention should the healthcare team prioritize to manage the patient's infection?
A) Remove the central venous catheter to eliminate the source of infection.
B) Administer broad-spectrum antibiotics targeting common pathogens.
C) Perform blood cultures to identify the causative organism.
D) Implement sterile dressing changes and catheter care protocols.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A) Remove the central venous catheter to eliminate the source of infection.

Explanation: Source control (removing the line) is essential for CRBSI management.

512.
A patient undergoing mechanical ventilation in the ICU develops ventilator-associated pneumonia (VAP) caused by multidrug-resistant organisms (MDROs). What intervention should the healthcare team prioritize to manage the patient's infection?

513.
A patient in the ICU develops acute kidney injury (AKI) secondary to hypovolemic shock. What intervention should the healthcare team prioritize to manage the patient's renal function?
A) Administer intravenous fluids to restore circulating volume.
B) Initiate continuous renal replacement therapy (CRRT) for RRT.
C) Implement strict dietary restrictions to reduce renal workload.
D) Recommend nephrotoxic medications for therapeutic purposes.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A) Administer intravenous fluids to restore circulating volume.

Explanation: Pre-renal AKI (hypovolemia) is treated by restoring perfusion (fluids).

516.
A patient undergoing mechanical ventilation in the ICU develops acute exacerbation of chronic obstructive pulmonary disease (COPD). What intervention should the healthcare team prioritize to manage the patient's exacerbation?
A) Initiate non-invasive positive pressure ventilation (NIPPV).
B) Administer bronchodilator medications for bronchodilation.
C) Perform endotracheal intubation for invasive mechanical ventilation.
D) Recommend chest physiotherapy to promote airway clearance.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B) Administer bronchodilator medications for bronchodilation.

Explanation: If already on a ventilator (invasive), "Initiate NIPPV" is a step backward/extubation strategy. The question implies managing the exacerbation *while* ventilated, so increasing bronchodilators/steroids is key. However, if the patient *develops* exacerbation leading to ventilation need, A is first line. Given "undergoing mechanical ventilation", they are already intubated. Thus, treatment is meds (B). *Correction:* Wait, the question says "undergoing mechanical ventilation... develops acute exacerbation". This implies the patient is already intubated. B is the best pharmacological intervention.

517.
A patient in the ICU develops acute myocardial infarction (MI) with cardiogenic shock. What intervention should the healthcare team prioritize to manage the patient's shock?
A) Perform emergent percutaneous coronary intervention (PCI).
B) Administer inotropic medications to improve cardiac contractility.
C) Initiate therapeutic hypothermia to reduce myocardial oxygen demand.
D) Recommend anticoagulant therapy to prevent thromboembolism.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A) Perform emergent percutaneous coronary intervention (PCI).

Explanation: Revascularization (PCI/CABG) is the only therapy that improves survival in shock caused by MI.

518.
A patient admitted to the ICU develops acute intracerebral hemorrhage (ICH) with elevated intracranial pressure (ICP). What intervention should the healthcare team prioritize to manage the patient's ICP?
A) Initiate hypertonic saline therapy for osmotic diuresis.
B) Perform emergent craniotomy for hematoma evacuation.
C) Implement head-of-bed elevation to promote cerebral venous drainage.
D) Recommend sedative medications to reduce agitation and anxiety.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A) Initiate hypertonic saline therapy for osmotic diuresis.

Explanation: Hypertonic saline or Mannitol is used to acutely lower ICP. (Surgery B depends on size/location, but osmolar therapy is a primary medical intervention for ICP spikes). C is also correct but less "prioritized" as an active intervention compared to meds for crisis. A is a strong active medical management choice.

520.
A patient admitted to the ICU develops acute renal failure requiring renal replacement therapy (RRT). What intervention should the healthcare team prioritize to initiate RRT?
A) Insertion of a temporary hemodialysis catheter.
B) Placement of a peritoneal dialysis catheter.
C) Initiation of continuous renal replacement therapy (CRRT).
D) Administration of loop diuretics to enhance urine output.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A) Insertion of a temporary hemodialysis catheter.

Explanation: Vascular access (Vas Cath) is the prerequisite step to starting any form of RRT (HD or CRRT).

521.
A patient in the ICU develops acute respiratory distress syndrome (ARDS) with severe hypoxemia refractory to conventional oxygen therapy. What intervention should the healthcare team prioritize to improve the patient's oxygenation?
A) Initiate prone positioning to optimize lung recruitment.
B) Administer inhaled pulmonary vasodilators for vasodilation.
C) Perform recruitment maneuvers to open collapsed alveoli.
D) Recommend extracorporeal membrane oxygenation (ECMO) for respiratory support.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A) Initiate prone positioning to optimize lung recruitment.

Explanation: Prone positioning has a proven mortality benefit in severe ARDS.

522.
A patient admitted to the ICU develops acute exacerbation of chronic heart failure (CHF) with pulmonary edema and respiratory distress. What intervention should the healthcare team prioritize to manage the patient's exacerbation?
A) Administer intravenous loop diuretics for fluid removal.
B) Initiate positive pressure ventilation for respiratory support.
C) Perform emergent pericardiocentesis for tamponade relief.
D) Recommend inotropic medications to improve myocardial contractility.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A) Administer intravenous loop diuretics for fluid removal.

Explanation: IV Lasix is the cornerstone of ADHF therapy to offload volume.

B) Perform bronchoalveolar lavage for diagnostic purposes.

D) Recommend prophylactic antifungal therapy to prevent candidiasis.

Correct Answer: A) Administer vancomycin or linezolid for MRSA coverage.

Explanation: Targeted therapy for the specific pathogen.

524.
A patient in the ICU develops acute exacerbation of chronic kidney disease (CKD) with hyperkalemia and metabolic acidosis. What intervention should the healthcare team prioritize to manage the patient's metabolic derangements?
A) Initiate intravenous sodium bicarbonate for acid-base correction.
B) Administer insulin and glucose to promote cellular uptake of potassium.
C) Recommend dietary potassium restriction and potassium-binding agents.
D) Perform emergent hemodialysis for potassium removal.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B) Administer insulin and glucose to promote cellular uptake of potassium.

Explanation: Insulin/Glucose is the standard temporary measure to shift K+ into cells. (Dialysis is definitive but takes time to set up; Insulin is "priority" for immediate stabilization).
