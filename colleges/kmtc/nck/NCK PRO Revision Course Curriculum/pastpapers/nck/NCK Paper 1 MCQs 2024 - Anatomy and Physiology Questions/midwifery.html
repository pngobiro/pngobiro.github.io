601.
A pregnant woman presents with severe abdominal pain and vaginal bleeding at 8 weeks gestation. On examination, the cervix is closed. Which of the following conditions is the most likely cause of these symptoms?
A) Ectopic pregnancy
B) Threatened abortion
C) Placenta previa
D) Gestational trophoblastic disease
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A) Ectopic pregnancy (or B depending on severity)

Explanation: Severe pain at 8 weeks suggests ectopic. Threatened abortion usually presents with bleeding but mild or no pain and a closed cervix. Severe pain alerts to ectopic.

631.
A primigravida at 40 weeks gestation is in active labor. The nurse notes late decelerations on the fetal monitor tracing. What action should the nurse take first?
A) Administer oxygen to the mother.
B) Change the mother's position.
C) Prepare for immediate delivery.
D) Discontinue oxytocin infusion.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B) Change the mother's position. (or D)

Explanation: Standard resuscitation: 1. Position (Left Lateral), 2. Fluids, 3. Oxygen, 4. Stop Pitocin. Positioning is the most immediate non-invasive step to improve perfusion. (Note: Many protocols say "Stop Pitocin" first if running. If no Pitocin, Position is first. Usually "Change position" is the safest first answer).

632.
A woman in active labor requests pain relief. Which pharmacological option is safe and effective for pain management during labor?
A) Ibuprofen
B) Nitrous oxide
C) Morphine
D) Diazepam
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B) Nitrous oxide

Explanation: Nitrous oxide (gas and air) is a safe, self-administered analgesic. Ibuprofen is contraindicated (bleeding/ductus arteriosus). Morphine causes respiratory depression in the fetus near birth. Diazepam causes floppy infant.

641.
A woman in active labor is receiving intravenous fentanyl for pain relief. What fetal assessment finding indicates potential neonatal opioid withdrawal syndrome (NOWS)?
A) Hypertonicity
B) Bradycardia
C) Hypoglycemia
D) Respiratory depression
Show Answer

Correct Answer: D) Respiratory depression (Acute effect)

Explanation: *Correction:* The question asks about NOWS (Withdrawal). NOWS symptoms are Hypertonicity/irritability. However, *Intrapartum* fentanyl causes acute *depression* (Respiratory depression/Bradycardia) in the neonate at birth, not withdrawal (which takes days). The question phrasing "fetal assessment... potential NOWS" is confusing. Fentanyl during labor causes temporary depression. Chronic use causes NOWS. Given the context of "receiving IV fentanyl" (acute use), the immediate risk is **Respiratory Depression** at birth. But if asking about *Withdrawal* signs specifically, it's Hypertonicity. I will assume the question asks about the immediate adverse effect on the newborn, which is D.
