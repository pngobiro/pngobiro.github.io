101.
A nurse is caring for a patient who is expressing concerns about their upcoming surgical procedure. What action demonstrates therapeutic communication by the nurse?
A) Providing minimal responses to the patient's questions
B) Offering reassurance and encouragement to the patient
C) Ignoring the patient's concerns and focusing on tasks
D) Interrupting the patient to redirect the conversation
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B) Offering reassurance and encouragement to the patient

Explanation: Therapeutic communication involves active listening, empathy, and validation. Offering reassurance (when realistic) and encouragement validates the patient's feelings and helps alleviate anxiety. Options A, C, and D block communication.

102.
During a shift handover, the nurse receives information about a patient's condition from the outgoing nurse. What action by the nurse demonstrates effective communication during the handover process?
A) Interrupting the outgoing nurse to ask questions
B) Writing down the information without asking for clarification
C) Summarizing key points and asking clarifying questions as needed
D) Disregarding the information and relying on personal assessment later
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C) Summarizing key points and asking clarifying questions as needed

Explanation: Summarizing (read-back) and asking for clarification (check-back) are key components of effective SBAR communication, ensuring accuracy and patient safety during transitions of care.

103.
A nurse is preparing to administer medications to a patient and discovers a discrepancy between the medication order and the patient's medication record. What is the nurse's priority action?
A) Administer the medication as ordered to avoid delays
B) Document the discrepancy and inform the charge nurse
C) Verify the medication order with the prescriber immediately
D) Contact the pharmacy for clarification on the medication
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C) Verify the medication order with the prescriber immediately

Explanation: The priority in medication safety is to clarify the original order with the provider who wrote it. Administering based on an unclear order (A) puts the patient at risk. The prescriber is the ultimate authority on the intent of the order.

104.
A nurse is caring for a patient with limited mobility and is planning interventions to prevent pressure injuries. What action by the nurse demonstrates evidence-based practice in pressure injury prevention?
A) Applying moisturizing lotion to the patient's skin daily
B) Turning and repositioning the patient every 2 hours
C) Placing the patient on an alternating pressure mattress
D) Massaging the bony prominences to improve circulation
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B) Turning and repositioning the patient every 2 hours

Explanation: Regular repositioning (every 2 hours) is the gold standard for preventing pressure injuries by relieving tissue compression. Massaging bony prominences (D) is contraindicated as it may damage compromised capillaries.

105.
A nurse is teaching a patient about medication adherence. What approach by the nurse promotes patient empowerment and active participation in self-care?
A) Providing written instructions and expecting compliance
B) Demonstrating medication administration techniques
C) Encouraging the patient to ask questions and express concerns
D) Directing the patient to follow the healthcare provider's instructions
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C) Encouraging the patient to ask questions and express concerns

Explanation: Empowerment involves engaging the patient as a partner in care. Encouraging questions allows the nurse to address specific barriers to adherence and validates the patient's role in decision-making.

106.
A nurse is delegating tasks to a nursing assistant. What principle should guide the nurse's delegation decisions?
A) Delegating tasks according to the assistant's job description
B) Assigning tasks based on the assistant's level of experience
C) Delegating tasks that the nurse prefers not to perform
D) Providing tasks that are routine and do not require nursing judgment
Show Answer

Correct Answer: D) Providing tasks that are routine and do not require nursing judgment

Explanation: The "Five Rights of Delegation" state that nurses can delegate *tasks* but not the *nursing process* (assessment, planning, evaluation, judgment). Tasks must be within the UAP's scope and typically routine/standardized.

107.
A nurse is caring for a patient who refuses to comply with a prescribed treatment regimen. What ethical principle should guide the nurse's approach to addressing the patient's refusal?
A) Beneficence
B) Autonomy
C) Nonmaleficence
D) Justice
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B) Autonomy

Explanation: Autonomy is the right of a competent individual to self-determination, including the right to refuse medical treatment, even if that choice may result in harm (which conflicts with beneficence).

108.
A nurse is documenting a patient's care in the electronic health record (EHR). What principle should guide the nurse's documentation practices?
A) Recording only objective data to maintain accuracy
B) Documenting all care provided, including assessments and interventions
C) Avoiding documentation of errors or adverse events to prevent liability
D) Delaying documentation until the end of the shift to save time
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B) Documenting all care provided, including assessments and interventions

Explanation: "If it wasn't documented, it wasn't done." Comprehensive documentation is essential for continuity of care and legal protection. It must include both objective data and relevant subjective data (e.g., patient statements).

109.
A nurse is collaborating with other healthcare providers to develop a plan of care for a patient. What is the primary purpose of interdisciplinary collaboration in nursing practice?
A) Promoting professional development among healthcare providers
B) Improving communication and coordination of patient care
C) Establishing hierarchy and authority within the healthcare team
D) Reducing workload and increasing efficiency in healthcare delivery
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B) Improving communication and coordination of patient care

Explanation: The main goal of collaboration is to pool expertise to improve patient outcomes through coordinated, holistic care. While efficiency (D) is a benefit, quality of patient care is the primary purpose.

110.
A nurse is assessing a patient's pain using a pain rating scale. What action by the nurse demonstrates cultural competence in pain assessment?
A) Assuming that the patient experiences pain similarly to other patients
B) Using nonverbal cues to assess the patient's pain intensity
C) Asking the patient about their cultural beliefs and preferences related to pain
D) Administering pain medication without assessing the patient's pain level
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C) Asking the patient about their cultural beliefs and preferences related to pain

Explanation: Cultural competence requires avoiding assumptions. Different cultures express and manage pain differently (e.g., stoicism vs. expressiveness). Asking the patient directly respects their individual cultural context.

111.
A nurse is caring for a patient who is experiencing conflicting emotions about a recent diagnosis. What therapeutic communication technique should the nurse use to help the patient explore their feelings?
A) Offering advice on coping strategies
B) Providing reassurance and false hope
C) Reflecting the patient's feelings and expressions
D) Redirecting the conversation to a different topic
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C) Reflecting the patient's feelings and expressions

Explanation: Reflection involves repeating or rephrasing the patient's emotional statement (e.g., "It sounds like you're feeling overwhelmed"). This validates the patient's feelings and encourages them to elaborate and process their emotions.

112.
During a patient assessment, the nurse observes signs of distress and discomfort. What action should the nurse take to address the patient's needs?
A) Ignoring the patient's distress to avoid making them uncomfortable
B) Documenting the findings and informing the healthcare provider later
C) Offering emotional support and actively listening to the patient's concerns
D) Administering pain medication without further assessment
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C) Offering emotional support and actively listening to the patient's concerns

Explanation: Immediate acknowledgment and support are required when distress is observed. This builds trust and allows for proper assessment of the cause of distress.

113.
A nurse is teaching a group of nursing students about the importance of professional boundaries in nursing practice. What statement by a student indicates understanding of this concept?
A) "It's okay to share personal information with patients to build rapport."
B) "Maintaining professional boundaries helps protect both patients and nurses."
C) "Establishing friendships with patients enhances trust and communication."
D) "Being emotionally involved with patients improves patient outcomes."
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B) "Maintaining professional boundaries helps protect both patients and nurses."

Explanation: Boundaries define the limits of the therapeutic relationship. They protect the patient from exploitation and the nurse from burnout or accusations of impropriety. The relationship should remain professional, not personal (social).

114.
A nurse is caring for a patient with a history of substance abuse who requests pain medication. What ethical principle should guide the nurse's decision-making in this situation?
A) Beneficence
B) Nonmaleficence
C) Justice
D) Veracity
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C) Justice

Explanation: Justice refers to fairness and treating people equally. In this context, it means treating the patient's pain seriously and providing appropriate relief, regardless of their history of substance abuse, just as one would for any other patient.

115.
A nurse is advocating for a patient's rights within the healthcare system. What action by the nurse demonstrates advocacy?
A) Making decisions for the patient without their input
B) Collaborating with the healthcare team to develop a care plan
C) Encouraging the patient to follow the healthcare provider's orders
D) Speaking up on behalf of the patient to ensure their needs are met
Show Answer

Correct Answer: D) Speaking up on behalf of the patient to ensure their needs are met

Explanation: Advocacy involves protecting the patient's rights and interests, often by being their voice when they cannot speak for themselves or when their wishes are being overlooked by the system.

116.
A nurse is conducting an admission assessment for a newly admitted patient. What aspect of cultural competence should the nurse prioritize during the assessment?
A) Assuming that the patient shares the nurse's cultural beliefs
B) Asking the patient about their cultural background and preferences
C) Avoiding discussions about cultural differences to prevent discomfort
D) Disregarding the patient's cultural practices and beliefs
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B) Asking the patient about their cultural background and preferences

Explanation: Cultural humility involves asking questions rather than making assumptions. Gathering information about specific cultural needs (diet, modesty, family involvement) allows for culturally congruent care.

117.
A nurse is participating in a healthcare team meeting to discuss a patient's care plan. What is the nurse's role in interdisciplinary collaboration?
A) Dictating decisions and delegating tasks to other team members
B) Listening actively, contributing expertise, and sharing information
C) Assuming a passive role and deferring to other healthcare providers
D) Working independently without considering input from other team members
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B) Listening actively, contributing expertise, and sharing information

Explanation: Collaboration is a partnership. The nurse brings a unique perspective (holistic, continuous patient monitoring) that complements the medical, therapy, and social work perspectives.

118.
A nurse is documenting a patient's care in the electronic health record (EHR). What principle should guide the nurse's documentation practices?
A) Recording only objective data to maintain accuracy
B) Documenting all care provided, including assessments and interventions
C) Avoiding documentation of errors or adverse events to prevent liability
D) Delaying documentation until the end of the shift to save time
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B) Documenting all care provided, including assessments and interventions

Explanation: (Repeated concept). Complete documentation is the legal record of care. Errors should be documented factually in the record (and in an incident report separately), not hidden. Real-time documentation is safer than delayed documentation (D).

120.
A nurse is caring for a patient who expresses concerns about the potential side effects of a prescribed medication. What action should the nurse take to address the patient's concerns?
A) Disregard the patient's concerns and reassure them about the medication's safety
B) Provide accurate information about the medication, including potential side effects
C) Encourage the patient to stop taking the medication if they are worried about side effects
D) Minimize the importance of the patient's concerns and focus on other aspects of care
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B) Provide accurate information about the medication, including potential side effects

Explanation: Informed consent and patient education require providing honest, balanced information about risks (side effects) and benefits. This allows the patient to make an informed choice.

121.
A nurse is caring for a patient with limited English proficiency. What action should the nurse take to ensure effective communication with the patient?
A) Speak loudly and slowly to help the patient understand
B) Use medical jargon to convey information more efficiently
C) Obtain interpreter services or language assistance as needed
D) Ignore the patient's language barriers and focus on nonverbal communication
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C) Obtain interpreter services or language assistance as needed

Explanation: Using a professional medical interpreter is the legal and ethical standard. Speaking loudly (A) is ineffective and can be offensive. Family members should generally be avoided for medical translation due to confidentiality and accuracy issues.

123.
A nurse is caring for a patient who is experiencing end-of-life symptoms. What action by the nurse demonstrates palliative care principles?
A) Focusing solely on physical comfort and pain management
B) Encouraging the patient to make decisions about life-sustaining treatments
C) Providing emotional support and addressing psychosocial needs
D) Withholding information about the patient's prognosis to avoid distress

Correct Answer: C) Providing emotional support and addressing psychosocial needs

Explanation: Palliative care is holistic. While pain management (A) is crucial, palliative care explicitly includes psychological, social, and spiritual support for the patient and family.

124.
A nurse is caring for a patient with complex healthcare needs. What action by the nurse demonstrates effective care coordination?
A) Working in isolation without consulting other healthcare providers
B) Providing fragmented care without considering the patient's preferences
C) Collaborating with interdisciplinary team members to develop a comprehensive care plan
D) Focusing solely on completing tasks without addressing the patient's holistic needs
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C) Collaborating with interdisciplinary team members to develop a comprehensive care plan

Explanation: Care coordination involves organizing patient care activities and sharing information among all participants (nurses, doctors, therapists, social workers) to achieve safer and more effective care.

125.
A nurse is conducting a discharge planning assessment for a patient preparing to transition home. What action by the nurse demonstrates continuity of care?
A) Providing the patient with written discharge instructions only
B) Discharging the patient without coordinating follow-up care or support services
C) Communicating with the patient's primary care provider and community resources
D) Disregarding the patient's concerns and preferences during the discharge process
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C) Communicating with the patient's primary care provider and community resources

Explanation: Continuity of care requires a seamless transition. Ensuring the next level of care (primary provider, home health) is informed and that the patient has necessary resources prevents readmissions and care gaps.
