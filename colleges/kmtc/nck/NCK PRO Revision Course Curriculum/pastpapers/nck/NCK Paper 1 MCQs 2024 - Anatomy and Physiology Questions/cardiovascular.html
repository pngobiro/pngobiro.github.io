Part 19Questions 451 - 475

Cardiovascular Nursing

NCK KRCHN Paper 1 - 2024

451.
A patient presents with chest pain that worsens with exertion and improves with rest. An electrocardiogram (ECG) shows ST-segment depression. Which cardiovascular disorder is most likely responsible for these symptoms?
A) Stable angina
B) Unstable angina
C) Acute myocardial infarction
D) Prinzmetal's angina
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A) Stable angina

Explanation: Predictable chest pain with exertion relieved by rest is classic stable angina. ST depression indicates ischemia, not infarction.

452.
A patient presents with sudden onset of severe chest pain radiating to the back, hypotension, and a widened mediastinum on chest X-ray. Which cardiovascular disorder is most likely responsible for these symptoms?
A) Stable angina
B) Unstable angina
C) Acute myocardial infarction
D) Aortic dissection
Show Answer

Correct Answer: D) Aortic dissection

Explanation: "Tearing" pain radiating to the back + widened mediastinum is the classic board presentation for Aortic Dissection.

453.
A patient presents with crushing chest pain that lasts longer than 20 minutes, diaphoresis, and dyspnea. An electrocardiogram (ECG) shows ST-segment elevation in the anterior leads. Which cardiovascular disorder is most likely responsible for these symptoms?
A) Stable angina
B) Unstable angina
C) Acute myocardial infarction
D) Prinzmetal's angina
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C) Acute myocardial infarction

Explanation: STEMI (ST-Elevation Myocardial Infarction).

454.
A patient presents with chest pain at rest, unrelated to exertion, and not relieved by nitroglycerin. An electrocardiogram (ECG) shows ST-segment depression. Which cardiovascular disorder is most likely responsible for these symptoms?
A) Stable angina
B) Unstable angina
C) Acute myocardial infarction
D) Prinzmetal's angina
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B) Unstable angina

Explanation: Chest pain at rest + ST depression (ischemia) without mention of elevated troponins (which would be NSTEMI) fits Unstable Angina. (Prinzmetal's typically has ST *elevation*).

455.
A patient presents with palpitations, dizziness, and syncope. An electrocardiogram (ECG) shows ventricular tachycardia. Which cardiovascular disorder is most likely responsible for these symptoms?
A) Stable angina
B) Unstable angina
C) Atrial fibrillation
D) Ventricular tachycardia
Show Answer

Correct Answer: D) Ventricular tachycardia

Explanation: The diagnosis is provided in the question stem (ECG shows VT).

456.
A patient presents with chest pain that occurs at rest and is exacerbated by deep breathing or changes in body position. The pain improves with leaning forward. Which cardiovascular disorder is most likely responsible for these symptoms?
A) Stable angina
B) Unstable angina
C) Pericarditis
D) Myocarditis
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C) Pericarditis

Explanation: Positional chest pain (better leaning forward) is pathognomonic for Acute Pericarditis.

457.
A patient presents with swelling of the ankles, dyspnea on exertion, and orthopnea. An echocardiogram reveals decreased left ventricular ejection fraction and dilated left ventricle. Which cardiovascular disorder is most likely responsible for these symptoms?
A) Stable angina
B) Heart failure with preserved ejection fraction
C) Heart failure with reduced ejection fraction
D) Valvular heart disease
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C) Heart failure with reduced ejection fraction

Explanation: HFrEF (Systolic Heart Failure).

458.
A patient presents with chest pain that worsens with inspiration and is relieved by sitting forward. There is also evidence of deep vein thrombosis (DVT) in the lower extremity. Which cardiovascular disorder is most likely responsible for these symptoms?
A) Stable angina
B) Pulmonary embolism
C) Pericarditis
D) Aortic dissection
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B) Pulmonary embolism

Explanation: Pleuritic chest pain (worse with inspiration) + DVT = PE until proven otherwise. (Note: "Relieved by sitting forward" is usually Pericarditis, but the DVT makes PE the priority diagnosis. The prompt might be mixing features, but DVT is the strongest link to PE).

459.
A patient presents with chest pain, dyspnea, and a friction rub heard on auscultation. An electrocardiogram (ECG) shows diffuse ST-segment elevation. Which cardiovascular disorder is most likely responsible for these symptoms?
A) Stable angina
B) Unstable angina
C) Acute myocardial infarction
D) Pericarditis
Show Answer

Correct Answer: D) Pericarditis

Explanation: Friction rub + Diffuse ST elevation = Pericarditis.

460.
A patient presents with chest pain that occurs during emotional stress and resolves spontaneously. An electrocardiogram (ECG) may show transient ST-segment elevation. Which cardiovascular disorder is most likely responsible for these symptoms?
A) Stable angina
B) Unstable angina
C) Acute myocardial infarction
D) Takotsubo cardiomyopathy
Show Answer

Correct Answer: D) Takotsubo cardiomyopathy

Explanation: "Broken Heart Syndrome" triggered by emotional stress, mimicking MI.

461.
A patient presents with chest pain, dyspnea, and a history of recent surgery or trauma. An echocardiogram reveals a large pericardial effusion with right ventricular collapse during diastole. Which cardiovascular disorder is most likely responsible for these symptoms?
A) Stable angina
B) Pericardial effusion
C) Myocardial infarction
D) Aortic dissection
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B) Pericardial effusion

Explanation: Specifically *Cardiac Tamponade* (indicated by RV collapse), caused by the effusion.

462.
A patient presents with chest pain, dyspnea, and hemoptysis. A ventilation-perfusion (V/Q) scan reveals a mismatched defect. Which cardiovascular disorder is most likely responsible for these symptoms?
A) Stable angina
B) Pulmonary embolism
C) Pericarditis
D) Aortic dissection
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B) Pulmonary embolism

Explanation: V/Q mismatch is diagnostic for PE.

463.
A patient presents with chest pain that worsens with swallowing and is relieved by leaning forward. An electrocardiogram (ECG) shows diffuse ST-segment elevation. Which cardiovascular disorder is most likely responsible for these symptoms?
A) Stable angina
B) Unstable angina
C) Acute myocardial infarction
D) Pericarditis
Show Answer

Correct Answer: D) Pericarditis

Explanation: Pain on swallowing (odynophagia) can occur if the posterior pericardium is inflamed near the esophagus. Combined with diffuse ST elevation, it's Pericarditis.

A) Stable angina
B) Unstable angina
C) Acute myocardial infarction
D) Prinzmetal's angina
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A) Stable angina

Explanation: Classic stable angina presentation.

465.
A patient presents with chest pain that occurs at rest and is relieved by nitroglycerin. An electrocardiogram (ECG) may show transient ST-segment elevation. Which cardiovascular disorder is most likely responsible for these symptoms?
A) Stable angina
B) Unstable angina
C) Acute myocardial infarction
D) Prinzmetal's angina
Show Answer

Correct Answer: D) Prinzmetal's angina

Explanation: Prinzmetal's (Variant) angina causes *transient* ST elevation due to vasospasm, often at rest.

466.
A patient presents with chest pain, dyspnea, and signs of hypotension. An electrocardiogram (ECG) shows ST-segment elevation in the inferior leads. Which cardiovascular disorder is most likely responsible for these symptoms?
A) Stable angina
B) Unstable angina
C) Acute myocardial infarction
D) Aortic dissection
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C) Acute myocardial infarction

Explanation: Inferior STEMI.

468.
A patient presents with chest pain, dyspnea, and signs of hypotension. An echocardiogram reveals right ventricular dilation and hypokinesis. Which cardiovascular disorder is most likely responsible for these symptoms?
A) Pulmonary embolism
B) Pericardial effusion
C) Acute myocardial infarction
D) Aortic dissection
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A) Pulmonary embolism

Explanation: Massive PE causes Right Heart Strain (RV dilation/hypokinesis).

469.
A patient presents with chest pain, dyspnea, and syncope. An electrocardiogram (ECG) shows a wide QRS complex with absence of P waves. Which cardiovascular disorder is most likely responsible for these symptoms?
A) Stable angina
B) Atrial fibrillation
C) Ventricular tachycardia
D) Supraventricular tachycardia
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C) Ventricular tachycardia

Explanation: Wide complex tachycardia is VT until proven otherwise.
