26.
A patient with a history of chronic obstructive pulmonary disease (COPD) is experiencing respiratory distress. Which nursing intervention is the priority for managing the patient's respiratory status?
A) Administering bronchodilators
B) Positioning the patient in high Fowler's position
C) Performing chest physiotherapy
D) Administering oxygen therapy
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B) Positioning the patient in high Fowler's position

Explanation: Positioning is the priority independent nursing action. High Fowler's position allows for maximum chest expansion and diaphragmatic excursion, providing immediate mechanical relief for breathing. Medications (A, D) require orders or time to work, and physiotherapy (C) could worsen acute distress.

27.
A patient post-thyroidectomy develops signs of hypocalcemia, including tingling around the mouth and muscle cramps. Which action should the nurse take first?
A) Administer oral calcium supplements
B) Notify the healthcare provider
C) Assess the patient's calcium level
D) Encourage increased intake of dairy products
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B) Notify the healthcare provider

Explanation: Symptomatic hypocalcemia (tetany, laryngospasm risk) is a medical emergency post-thyroidectomy. The nurse must notify the provider immediately to obtain an order for IV Calcium Gluconate. Oral supplements (A, D) act too slowly, and assessing levels (C) delays necessary treatment for symptomatic patients.

28.
A patient with a history of heart failure presents with dependent edema in the lower extremities. Which nursing action is most appropriate for managing the patient's edema?
A) Applying compression stockings
B) Elevating the legs above heart level
C) Administering diuretics as prescribed
D) Encouraging increased fluid intake
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B) Elevating the legs above heart level

Explanation: Elevating the legs promotes venous return through gravity, reducing dependent edema. While diuretics (C) are the medical treatment, leg elevation is the primary *nursing* action for symptom management. Compression (A) must be used with caution in severe HF.

29.
A patient with a history of diabetes mellitus is admitted with a foot ulcer. Which nursing intervention is essential for preventing infection in the foot ulcer?
A) Applying topical antibiotics
B) Performing daily wound debridement
C) Keeping the ulcer covered with a sterile dressing
D) Administering oral antibiotics prophylactically
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C) Keeping the ulcer covered with a sterile dressing

Explanation: A sterile dressing acts as a barrier against environmental pathogens, maintaining a clean wound bed. Debridement (B) is a specialized procedure, not routine nursing preventative care.

30.
A patient with a history of cirrhosis is at risk for developing hepatic encephalopathy. Which nursing intervention is most effective in preventing and managing hepatic encephalopathy?
A) Administering lactulose as prescribed
B) Restricting dietary protein intake
C) Monitoring serum ammonia levels
D) Encouraging increased fluid intake
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A) Administering lactulose as prescribed

Explanation: Lactulose acts as a laxative and acidifies the gut lumen, trapping ammonia (as ammonium) and promoting its excretion in stool. This is the gold standard pharmaceutical intervention to lower serum ammonia levels.

31.
A patient with a history of stroke is at risk for impaired skin integrity due to immobility. Which nursing intervention is most effective in preventing pressure ulcers in this patient?
A) Turning and repositioning the patient every 2 hours
B) Massaging bony prominences regularly
C) Applying heating pads to areas of erythema
D) Using a pressure-reducing mattress
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A) Turning and repositioning the patient every 2 hours

Explanation: Regular repositioning relieves prolonged pressure on bony prominences, restoring blood flow to tissues. This is the single most effective nursing intervention. Massaging (B) is contraindicated as it can damage fragile capillaries.

32.
A patient with a history of chronic kidney disease is prescribed a low-phosphorus diet. Which dietary choice indicates understanding of the prescribed diet?
A) Grilled chicken breast
B) Cheese omelet
C) Whole wheat toast with peanut butter
D) Spinach salad with almonds
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A) Grilled chicken breast

Explanation: Patients with CKD must avoid foods high in phosphorus. Cheese/dairy (B), whole grains/nuts/peanut butter (C), and nuts (D) are very high in phosphorus. White meat like chicken breast is lower in phosphorus compared to the other options.

33.
A patient with a history of peptic ulcer disease is prescribed proton pump inhibitors (PPIs) for ulcer healing. Which information is important for the nurse to include in patient education about PPI therapy?
A) PPIs should be taken on an empty stomach.
B) PPIs should be discontinued once symptoms resolve.
C) PPIs may increase the risk of gastrointestinal bleeding.
D) PPIs should be taken with antacids for optimal effect.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A) PPIs should be taken on an empty stomach.

Explanation: PPIs (e.g., omeprazole) are most effective when taken 30-60 minutes before the first meal of the day (breakfast) to inhibit the proton pumps activated by food intake.

34.
A patient with a history of chronic obstructive pulmonary disease (COPD) is prescribed a metered-dose inhaler (MDI) with a spacer. Which instruction should the nurse include in the demonstration of MDI use?
A) Inhale slowly and deeply while pressing the canister.
B) Exhale completely before inhaling the medication.
C) Shake the inhaler vigorously before each puff.
D) Hold the breath for 10 seconds after inhaling the medication.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A) Inhale slowly and deeply while pressing the canister.

Explanation: Proper MDI technique involves a slow, deep inhalation to carry the medication into the lungs. Note: While B, C, and D are also parts of the technique, A describes the critical coordination action. Often "Hold breath" (D) is also a key instruction, but if asked for the inhalation technique specifically, "Slow and deep" prevents the medication from impacting the back of the throat.

35.
A patient with a history of type 2 diabetes mellitus is prescribed insulin glargine (Lantus) once daily. Which statement indicates understanding of insulin glargine administration?
A) "I will inject insulin glargine 30 minutes before meals."
B) "I will shake the insulin vial vigorously before drawing the dose."
C) "I will rotate injection sites within the same body region."
D) "I will administer insulin glargine with a rapid-acting insulin."
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C) "I will rotate injection sites within the same body region."

Explanation: Rotation of sites prevents lipodystrophy. Glargine is a long-acting basal insulin given at the same time daily, not dependent on meals (A). It should never be shaken (B) or mixed with other insulins (D).

36.
A patient with a history of heart failure is prescribed digoxin. Which assessment finding indicates a potential adverse effect of digoxin therapy?
A) Bradycardia
B) Hypotension
C) Hyperkalemia
D) Confusion
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A) Bradycardia

Explanation: Digoxin slows AV node conduction. Bradycardia (Heart Rate < 60 bpm) is a primary sign of toxicity. Confusion and visual halos are also signs, but Bradycardia is the most direct cardiovascular indicator requiring pulse assessment before administration.

37.
A patient with a history of coronary artery disease is prescribed aspirin for antiplatelet therapy. Which information is important for the nurse to include in patient education about aspirin therapy?
A) "Take aspirin with a full glass of milk to minimize gastrointestinal upset."
B) "Avoid using nonsteroidal anti-inflammatory drugs (NSAIDs) while taking aspirin."
C) "Discontinue aspirin therapy if you develop a fever."
D) "Take aspirin on an empty stomach for better absorption."
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A) "Take aspirin with a full glass of milk to minimize gastrointestinal upset."

Explanation: Aspirin is a gastric irritant. Taking it with food or milk helps protect the gastric mucosa. Avoiding other NSAIDs (B) is also correct to reduce bleeding risk, but managing GI upset is a primary daily instruction for compliance.

38.
A patient with a history of stroke is prescribed clopidogrel (Plavix) for secondary prevention of thrombotic events. Which laboratory test should the nurse monitor closely during clopidogrel therapy?
A) Prothrombin time (PT)
B) Activated partial thromboplastin time (aPTT)
C) Platelet count
D) International normalized ratio (INR)
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C) Platelet count

Explanation: Clopidogrel is an antiplatelet agent. While it doesn't require routine monitoring like Warfarin (INR) or Heparin (aPTT), rare but serious side effects include Thrombotic Thrombocytopenic Purpura (TTP), making platelet monitoring relevant. Bleeding time is the specific functional test.

39.
A patient with a history of chronic kidney disease is prescribed erythropoietin-stimulating agents (ESAs) for anemia management. Which parameter should the nurse monitor closely during ESA therapy?
A) Serum potassium levels
B) Hemoglobin levels
C) Serum creatinine levels
D) White blood cell count
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B) Hemoglobin levels

Explanation: The goal of ESA therapy is to raise hemoglobin, but not too high (>11 g/dL), as this increases cardiovascular risks (stroke, MI, hypertension).

40.
A patient with a history of chronic obstructive pulmonary disease (COPD) is prescribed prednisone for an acute exacerbation. Which instruction is important for the nurse to include in patient education about prednisone therapy?
A) "Take prednisone with antacids to minimize gastrointestinal upset."
B) "Avoid abruptly stopping prednisone therapy."
C) "Increase your intake of potassium-rich foods while taking prednisone."
D) "Prednisone may cause weight loss, so monitor your weight regularly."
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B) "Avoid abruptly stopping prednisone therapy."

Explanation: Systemic corticosteroids suppress the adrenal glands (HPA axis). Abrupt cessation can lead to an acute Addisonian crisis (adrenal insufficiency), which can be life-threatening. Tapering is required.

41.
A patient with a history of chronic kidney disease is prescribed a low-protein diet. Which dietary choice indicates understanding of the prescribed diet?
A) Grilled salmon
B) Lentil soup
C) Beef stew
D) Chicken Caesar salad
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A) Grilled salmon

Explanation: This question assesses understanding of protein quality in a restricted diet. In CKD, while protein is restricted, the protein consumed should be of High Biological Value (HBV) like fish, eggs, or poultry, which produce less nitrogenous waste than plant proteins (like lentils) or red meats. Although salmon is protein, it is the best *quality* choice among the restricted options compared to high-phosphate lentils or red meat.

42.
A patient with a history of deep vein thrombosis (DVT) is prescribed warfarin therapy. Which instruction is essential for the nurse to include in patient education about warfarin therapy?
A) "Limit your intake of green leafy vegetables."
B) "Avoid activities that may increase your risk of bleeding."
C) "Take a double dose if you miss a dose to catch up."
D) "Report any unusual bleeding or bruising to your healthcare provider."
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B) "Avoid activities that may increase your risk of bleeding."

Explanation: Bleeding is the primary risk of anticoagulant therapy. Patients should use soft toothbrushes, electric razors, and avoid contact sports. Regarding Vitamin K (Option A): Patients should maintain a *consistent* intake, not necessarily limit it, to keep INR stable.

43.
A patient with a history of chronic obstructive pulmonary disease (COPD) is prescribed tiotropium (Spiriva) inhaler. Which instruction should the nurse include in patient education about tiotropium inhaler use?
A) "Use the inhaler only when you experience symptoms."
B) "Rinse your mouth with water after each use."
C) "Exhale forcefully before inhaling the medication."
D) "Store the inhaler in the refrigerator between uses."
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C) "Exhale forcefully before inhaling the medication."

Explanation: Tiotropium is usually delivered via a Dry Powder Inhaler (DPI). To ensure deep lung deposition, the patient must empty their lungs (exhale fully *away* from the device) before sealing their lips around the mouthpiece and inhaling deeply.

44.
A patient with a history of myocardial infarction is prescribed atorvastatin for lipid-lowering therapy. Which parameter should the nurse monitor closely during atorvastatin therapy?
A) Serum potassium levels
B) Liver function tests
C) Blood glucose levels
D) Serum sodium levels
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B) Liver function tests

Explanation: Statins (HMG-CoA reductase inhibitors) can cause hepatotoxicity. Baseline and periodic monitoring of liver enzymes (AST/ALT) is recommended. They can also cause myopathy (monitor CK), but liver function is the standard routine screen listed here.

45.
A patient with a history of heart failure is prescribed lisinopril for blood pressure management. Which adverse effect is most commonly associated with lisinopril therapy?
A) Hyperkalemia
B) Hypertension
C) Dry cough
D) Bradycardia
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C) Dry cough

Explanation: A persistent, dry, non-productive cough is the most common side effect of ACE inhibitors like lisinopril, leading many patients to switch to ARBs.

46.
A patient with a history of peptic ulcer disease is prescribed famotidine (Pepcid) for gastric acid suppression. Which instruction should the nurse include in patient education about famotidine therapy?
A) "Take famotidine on an empty stomach."
B) "Avoid taking antacids while on famotidine therapy."
C) "Increase your intake of spicy foods while taking famotidine."
D) "Notify your healthcare provider if you develop black, tarry stools."
Show Answer

Correct Answer: D) "Notify your healthcare provider if you develop black, tarry stools."

Explanation: Famotidine treats ulcers. Black, tarry stools (melena) indicate gastrointestinal bleeding, suggesting the medication is ineffective or the condition is worsening, requiring immediate medical attention.

47.
A patient with a history of stroke is prescribed aspirin and clopidogrel for dual antiplatelet therapy. Which nursing intervention is essential for preventing bleeding complications in this patient?
A) Administering proton pump inhibitors (PPIs) concomitantly
B) Encouraging increased fluid intake
C) Monitoring for signs of thrombocytopenia
D) Providing education on fall prevention measures
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C) Monitoring for signs of thrombocytopenia

Explanation: Both drugs affect platelet function. Monitoring for signs of low platelets (bruising, petechiae, bleeding gums) or Thrombotic Thrombocytopenic Purpura (associated with Plavix) is critical safety monitoring.

48.
A patient with a history of chronic kidney disease is prescribed phosphate binders. Which instruction should the nurse include in patient education about phosphate binder therapy?
A) "Take phosphate binders with meals."
B) "Avoid taking phosphate binders with calcium supplements."
C) "Increase your intake of foods high in phosphorus."
D) "Take phosphate binders on an empty stomach."
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A) "Take phosphate binders with meals."

Explanation: The mechanism of action relies on the drug binding with phosphorus *in the food* while it is in the GI tract. Taking it on an empty stomach renders it ineffective.

49.
A patient with a history of coronary artery disease is scheduled for coronary artery bypass graft (CABG) surgery. Which preoperative nursing intervention is essential for preparing the patient for surgery?
A) Administering aspirin to prevent thrombosis
B) Providing education about postoperative pain management
C) Assisting the patient with deep breathing and coughing exercises
D) Obtaining informed consent for the surgical procedure
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C) Assisting the patient with deep breathing and coughing exercises

Explanation: Preoperative teaching of deep breathing, coughing, and splinting is a critical *nursing* intervention to prevent postoperative pulmonary complications (atelectasis/pneumonia). Informed consent (D) is the surgeon's responsibility to obtain; the nurse only witnesses.

50.
A patient with a history of heart failure is prescribed a beta-blocker. Which assessment finding indicates a therapeutic effect of beta-blocker therapy?
A) Decreased heart rate
B) Increased blood pressure
C) Elevated respiratory rate
D) Worsening dyspnea
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A) Decreased heart rate

Explanation: Beta-blockers reduce sympathetic outflow, lowering heart rate and myocardial oxygen demand. This allows the heart to fill better during diastole, improving efficiency in heart failure.
