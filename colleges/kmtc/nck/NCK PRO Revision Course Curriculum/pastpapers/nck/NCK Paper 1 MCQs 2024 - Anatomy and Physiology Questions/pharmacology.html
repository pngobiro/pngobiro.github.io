51.
A patient is prescribed a beta-blocker for the management of hypertension. Which adverse effect should the nurse monitor closely in the patient?
A) Bradycardia
B) Hyperglycemia
C) Hypertension
D) Tachycardia
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A) Bradycardia

Explanation: Beta-blockers (e.g., Metoprolol, Atenolol) work by blocking beta-adrenergic receptors, which decreases heart rate and myocardial contractility. A heart rate below 60 bpm (bradycardia) is a direct therapeutic extension and a common adverse effect requiring monitoring.

52.
A patient is prescribed an angiotensin-converting enzyme (ACE) inhibitor for heart failure. Which laboratory parameter should the nurse monitor closely during ACE inhibitor therapy?
A) Serum potassium levels
B) Serum sodium levels
C) Blood glucose levels
D) Serum creatinine levels
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A) Serum potassium levels

Explanation: ACE inhibitors inhibit the formation of Angiotensin II, which subsequently reduces Aldosterone secretion. Since Aldosterone normally excretes potassium, blocking it causes potassium retention, leading to a risk of **Hyperkalemia**. Creatinine (D) is also monitored for renal function, but Potassium is the primary electrolyte concern.

53.
A patient is prescribed an opioid analgesic for postoperative pain management. Which nursing intervention is essential for preventing respiratory depression in the patient?
A) Administering naloxone prophylactically
B) Monitoring oxygen saturation with pulse oximetry
C) Encouraging deep breathing exercises
D) Administering bronchodilators as needed
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B) Monitoring oxygen saturation with pulse oximetry

Explanation: Respiratory depression is the most severe adverse effect of opioids. Continuous monitoring of respiratory rate and oxygen saturation (SpO2) allows for early detection. Naloxone (A) is a reversal agent, not given prophylactically. Deep breathing (C) prevents atelectasis but doesn't prevent central respiratory depression.

54.
A patient is prescribed a loop diuretic for the management of heart failure. Which electrolyte imbalance is most commonly associated with loop diuretic therapy?
A) Hyponatremia
B) Hypokalemia
C) Hyperkalemia
D) Hypocalcemia
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B) Hypokalemia

Explanation: Loop diuretics (e.g., Furosemide) inhibit the Na-K-2Cl cotransporter in the Loop of Henle, causing significant excretion of Potassium along with Sodium and water. **Hypokalemia** is the most frequent and clinically significant electrolyte imbalance.

C) "Take the medication 30 minutes before meals."

Explanation: PPIs are most effective when the proton pumps are activated by food intake. Therefore, they should be administered 30â€“60 minutes before the first meal of the day.

56.
A patient is prescribed a calcium channel blocker for the management of hypertension. Which adverse effect should the nurse monitor closely in the patient?
A) Hypoglycemia
B) Peripheral edema
C) Hypokalemia
D) Tachycardia
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B) Peripheral edema

Explanation: Calcium Channel Blockers (especially dihydropyridines like Amlodipine) cause precapillary vasodilation, which leads to fluid extravasation and **peripheral edema**, typically in the ankles/legs.

57.
A patient is prescribed a selective serotonin reuptake inhibitor (SSRI) for the treatment of depression. Which adverse effect should the nurse monitor closely in the patient?
A) Bradycardia
B) Hypotension
C) Hypernatremia
D) Suicidal ideation
Show Answer

Correct Answer: D) Suicidal ideation

Explanation: All antidepressants carry a "Black Box Warning" regarding the increased risk of suicidal thinking and behavior in children, adolescents, and young adults, particularly during the initial weeks of therapy.

58.
A patient is prescribed a statin medication for lipid-lowering therapy. Which adverse effect should the nurse monitor closely in the patient?
A) Muscle pain or weakness
B) Dry cough
C) Hypertension
D) Hyperkalemia
Show Answer

Correct Answer: A) Muscle pain or weakness

Explanation: Statins can cause myopathy, characterized by muscle pain and weakness. In severe cases, this can progress to **rhabdomyolysis**, a life-threatening condition involving muscle breakdown and renal failure.

59.
A patient is prescribed a benzodiazepine for the management of anxiety. Which instruction should the nurse include in patient education about benzodiazepine therapy?
A) "Take the medication with grapefruit juice for enhanced absorption."
B) "Avoid consuming alcohol while on benzodiazepine therapy."
C) "Take the medication on an empty stomach for better effectiveness."
D) "Discontinue the medication abruptly if you experience side effects."
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B) "Avoid consuming alcohol while on benzodiazepine therapy."

Explanation: Both benzodiazepines and alcohol are CNS depressants. Consuming them together can cause severe respiratory depression, excessive sedation, and even death. Abrupt discontinuation (D) can cause seizures.

60.
A patient is prescribed a nonsteroidal anti-inflammatory drug (NSAID) for the management of pain. Which adverse effect should the nurse monitor closely in the patient?
A) Hypotension
B) Hyperkalemia
C) Gastrointestinal bleeding
D) Hyperglycemia
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C) Gastrointestinal bleeding

Explanation: NSAIDs (e.g., Ibuprofen, Naproxen) inhibit COX-1, which produces prostaglandins that protect the gastric lining. Inhibition leads to mucosal damage, ulcers, and GI bleeding.

61.
A patient is prescribed a loop diuretic for the management of edema. Which electrolyte imbalance is most commonly associated with loop diuretic therapy?
A) Hyponatremia
B) Hypomagnesemia
C) Hyperkalemia
D) Hypocalcemia
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B) Hypomagnesemia

Explanation: While Hypokalemia is the *most* common, loop diuretics also cause the loss of Magnesium and Calcium. Among the choices provided, Hypomagnesemia (B) is a correct associated imbalance. Note: Hypocalcemia is also possible, but magnesium loss is often clinically significant alongside potassium loss.

62.
A patient is prescribed an anticoagulant for the prevention of venous thromboembolism (VTE). Which laboratory parameter should the nurse monitor closely during anticoagulant therapy?
A) Platelet count
B) International normalized ratio (INR)
C) Serum sodium levels
D) Blood urea nitrogen (BUN)
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B) International normalized ratio (INR)

Explanation: Assuming Warfarin is the oral anticoagulant often implied in such questions, INR is the monitoring standard. If Heparin, aPTT is used. If LMWH, platelets (A) are monitored for HIT. Given the context of "anticoagulant" generally in exam settings, INR is the standard laboratory answer unless Heparin is specified.

63.
A patient is prescribed a beta-adrenergic agonist for the management of asthma. Which adverse effect should the nurse monitor closely in the patient?
A) Hypotension
B) Hyperkalemia
C) Tachycardia
D) Hypoglycemia
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C) Tachycardia

Explanation: Beta-agonists (like Albuterol) stimulate beta-2 receptors in lungs but can also stimulate beta-1 receptors in the heart, causing tachycardia and palpitations.

64.
A patient is prescribed a proton pump inhibitor (PPI) for the treatment of peptic ulcer disease. Which adverse effect should the nurse monitor closely in the patient?
A) Constipation
B) Hypokalemia
C) Diarrhea
D) Clostridium difficile infection
Show Answer

Correct Answer: D) Clostridium difficile infection

Explanation: By suppressing gastric acid, PPIs alter the gut flora and defense mechanisms, significantly increasing the risk of *Clostridium difficile*-associated diarrhea (CDAD).

65.
A patient is prescribed an antipsychotic medication for the management of schizophrenia. Which adverse effect should the nurse monitor closely in the patient?
A) Hypertension
B) Hypoglycemia
C) Extrapyramidal symptoms
D) Hypercalcemia
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C) Extrapyramidal symptoms

Explanation: Extrapyramidal symptoms (EPS) such as dystonia, akathisia, and pseudoparkinsonism are common side effects of antipsychotics (especially First Generation) due to dopamine blockade in the basal ganglia.

66.
A patient is prescribed a tricyclic antidepressant for the management of depression. Which adverse effect should the nurse monitor closely in the patient?
A) Hypertension
B) Hypotension
C) Hypernatremia
D) Hyperkalemia
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B) Hypotension

Explanation: TCAs (e.g., Amitriptyline) block alpha-adrenergic receptors, leading to **orthostatic hypotension**, which is a significant fall risk, especially in the elderly.

67.
A patient is prescribed a selective serotonin reuptake inhibitor (SSRI) for the treatment of depression. Which adverse effect should the nurse monitor closely in the patient?
A) Bradycardia
B) Hyperkalemia
C) Hyponatremia
D) Hypertension
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C) Hyponatremia

Explanation: SSRIs can cause SIADH (Syndrome of Inappropriate Antidiuretic Hormone), leading to water retention and dilutional **Hyponatremia**, particularly in older adults.

68.
A patient is prescribed a nonsteroidal anti-inflammatory drug (NSAID) for the management of osteoarthritis. Which adverse effect should the nurse monitor closely in the patient?
A) Hypoglycemia
B) Hyperkalemia
C) Gastrointestinal bleeding
D) Hypertension
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C) Gastrointestinal bleeding

Explanation: Chronic NSAID use for osteoarthritis significantly increases the risk of GI erosion, ulceration, and bleeding due to systemic inhibition of protective prostaglandins.

69.
A patient is prescribed a benzodiazepine for the management of insomnia. Which adverse effect should the nurse monitor closely in the patient?
A) Hypotension
B) Hyperglycemia
C) Daytime drowsiness
D) Bradycardia
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C) Daytime drowsiness

Explanation: The "hangover effect" or daytime drowsiness is a common adverse effect of benzodiazepines used for sleep, increasing the risk of accidents and falls.

70.
A patient is prescribed a beta-blocker for the management of migraine headaches. Which adverse effect should the nurse monitor closely in the patient?
A) Hypertension
B) Bradycardia
C) Hyperglycemia
D) Constipation
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B) Bradycardia

Explanation: Regardless of the indication (migraine prophylaxis vs hypertension), the pharmacological mechanism of beta-blockers remains the same: blocking sympathetic input to the heart, leading to **bradycardia**.

71.

A) Hypoglycemia
B) Hyperkalemia
C) Clostridium difficile infection
D) Osteoporosis

Correct Answer: C) Clostridium difficile infection

Explanation: (See Q64). Additionally, Option D (Osteoporosis) is also a valid long-term concern (bone fracture risk), but *Clostridium difficile* is an acute infection risk often emphasized in infection control protocols.

72.
A patient is prescribed an antipsychotic medication for the management of bipolar disorder. Which adverse effect should the nurse monitor closely in the patient?
A) Hypertension
B) Hyperglycemia
C) Hypotension
D) Hypernatremia
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B) Hyperglycemia

Explanation: Atypical (Second Generation) antipsychotics (often used in Bipolar) carry a high risk of metabolic syndrome, including **Hyperglycemia**, weight gain, and dyslipidemia.

73.
A patient is prescribed an opioid analgesic for the management of acute pain. Which adverse effect should the nurse monitor closely in the patient?
A) Hypertension
B) Hypoglycemia
C) Respiratory depression
D) Hyperkalemia
Show Answer

Correct Answer: C) Respiratory depression

Explanation: Respiratory depression is the priority safety concern with opioids as it can be fatal.

74.
A patient is prescribed a loop diuretic for the management of heart failure. Which electrolyte imbalance is most commonly associated with loop diuretic therapy?
A) Hypercalcemia
B) Hypokalemia
C) Hypernatremia
D) Hypomagnesemia
Show Answer

Correct Answer: B) Hypokalemia

Explanation: (Repeated concept). Loop diuretics cause potassium wasting. Note: They cause *Hypocalcemia*, unlike Thiazides which cause *Hypercalcemia*. Hypokalemia remains the most common significant imbalance.

75.
A patient is prescribed an angiotensin-converting enzyme (ACE) inhibitor for the management of hypertension. Which adverse effect should the nurse monitor closely in the patient?
A) Hyperkalemia
B) Hypoglycemia
C) Hypercalcemia
D) Hypotension
Show Answer

Correct Answer: D) Hypotension

Explanation: While Hyperkalemia (A) is a lab risk, **Hypotension** (especially first-dose hypotension) is a direct clinical adverse effect that requires monitoring of vital signs to prevent falls and syncope.
