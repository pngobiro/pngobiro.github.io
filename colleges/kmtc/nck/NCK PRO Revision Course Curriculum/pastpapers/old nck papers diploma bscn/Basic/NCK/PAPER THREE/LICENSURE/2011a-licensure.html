PAPER THREE

Examination for Admission to the Register of Community Health Nurses (Basic)

Part I: Multiple Choice Questions

20 Marks
1.
Socio-economic factors that have a bearing on health include:-
a) Education, live birth, blood test.
b) Blood test, housing, beliefs.
c) Housing, education, food.
d) Fertility, beliefs, live birth.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Housing, education, food.

Explanation: Socio-economic determinants of health include income, education, occupation, family structure, and physical environment (housing, food security). "Blood test" and "live birth" are medical/biological indicators, not socio-economic factors.

2.
The type of immunity a child acquires after receiving measles vaccine is:-
a) Humoral immunity.
b) Artificial active immunity.
c) Natural passive immunity.
d) Innate immunity.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Artificial active immunity.

Explanation:
• Artificial: Introduced via medical intervention (vaccine).
• Active: The body's immune system produces its own antibodies.
• Natural passive is maternal antibodies; Natural active is post-infection recovery.

3.
Vaccine potency can be maintained in the refrigerator by:-
a) Placing vaccines in the correct compartments, defrosting the refrigerator regularly, ensuring the maintenance of ideal temperature of 0° to 8°C.
b) Defrosting the refrigerator regularly, packing vaccines with enough space in-between to allow circulation of air, opening refrigerator only when necessary.
c) Avoiding contact of vaccines with evaporator, regulating the thermostat regularly, lining the frozen icepacks in the inner walls of the refrigerator.
d) Ensuring the maintenance of ideal temperature of between +2° to +8°C, lining frozen icepacks in the inner walls of the refrigerator, placing vaccines in the correct compartments.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Ensuring the maintenance of ideal temperature of between +2° to +8°C...

Explanation: The critical factor for potency is the temperature range of +2°C to +8°C.
• Option A lists "0° to 8°C", which risks freezing sensitive vaccines (DPT, Hep B, TT) at 0°C.
• Option B describes good practices (airflow, limited opening) but lacks the specific temp range.
• Note on "lining frozen icepacks": In standard practice, water bottles are used for thermal stability in fridges, while ice packs line cold boxes. However, identifying the correct temperature range (+2 to +8) makes D the best answer choice provided.

4.
Deficiency of vitamin B1 (Thiamine) leads to:-
a) Heart failure, abnormal nerve and brain function, emotional disturbances.
b) Rupture of red blood cells, muscle spasm, bleeding.
c) Night blindness, heart failure, impaired sensory perception.
d) Emotional disturbances, rupture of red blood cells, nerve damage.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Heart failure, abnormal nerve and brain function, emotional disturbances.

Explanation: Thiamine deficiency causes Beriberi:
• Wet Beriberi: Affects the cardiovascular system (Heart failure).
• Dry Beriberi: Affects the nervous system (Nerve damage/neuropathy).
• Wernicke-Korsakoff syndrome: Affects the brain (confusion, ataxia).

5.
Hypokalemia is a condition characterized by low:-
a) Blood calcium levels.
b) Magnesium in the blood.
c) Potassium levels in the blood.
d) Sodium levels in the blood.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Potassium levels in the blood.

Explanation:
• Kalium is the Latin name for Potassium.
• Hypo- (low) + Kalemia (potassium in blood).
• Calcium = Hypocalcemia.
• Sodium = Hyponatremia.

6.
The stage of syphilis characterized by the presence of gamma lesions is:-
a) Primordial.
b) Latent.
c) Tertiary.
d) Secondary.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Tertiary.

Explanation: Gummas (granulomatous lesions) are the hallmark of Tertiary (Late) Syphilis. They can affect skin, bone, or internal organs.

7.
Recommended second line treatment of urethritis in an adult is:-
a) Norfloxacine 800mg stat plus doxycycline 100 mg BD for 7 days.
b) Norfloxacine 800mg twice a day for 7 days and spectinomycin 2mg I.M. stat.
c) Erthromycin 100mg BD for 14 days and ceftriaxone 62.5mg I.M. stat.
d) Spectinomycin 2mg I.M. stat and doxycycline 100 mg BD for 7 days.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Spectinomycin 2mg I.M. stat and doxycycline 100 mg BD for 7 days.

Explanation: In the syndromic management of urethritis (covering Gonorrhea and Chlamydia), if first-line treatment fails or resistance is suspected (and compliance was good), Spectinomycin is often used as the alternative for Gonorrhea, while Doxycycline covers Chlamydia.

8.
The occupational disease that persons working in information technology are likely to develop is:-
a) Lead poisoning.
b) Radiation sickness.
c) Carpal tunnel syndrome.
d) Mesothelioma.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Carpal tunnel syndrome.

Explanation: This is a Repetitive Strain Injury (RSI) caused by prolonged use of keyboards and mice, compressing the median nerve at the wrist.
• Mesothelioma is related to Asbestos.
• Lead poisoning is related to battery/paint industries.

9.
The principle of unity of command in any organization is important because:-
a) The superior is able to assign part of his job domain to his/her subordinate, thus avoids overworking.
b) It avoids divided loyalty since each employee receives orders from one superior.
c) Employees are self-disciplined and self-controlled, so require very little supervision.
d) It ensures smooth working at all levels of the organization.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) It avoids divided loyalty since each employee receives orders from one superior.

Explanation: Unity of Command (Henri Fayol) states that a subordinate should receive orders from and report to only one superior. This prevents confusion, conflicting instructions, and divided loyalty.

10.
Indicate whether the statements are TRUE or FALSE on the answer sheet provided.
a) According to Douglas McGregor's motivation theory X, an employee will exercise self discipline and self control in certain circumstances.
b) When reprimanding an employee, the supervisor should avoid arguments and listen carefully to all comments.
Show Answer

a) False. Theory X assumes employees dislike work, are lazy, and need coercion. Theory Y assumes employees can exercise self-direction and self-control.

b) True. Effective disciplinary action requires fairness, which includes giving the employee a chance to explain (hearing) and avoiding emotional arguments to maintain professionalism.

11.
Methods of food preservation which inhibit multiplication of bacteria include:-
a) Smoking, pasteurization, salting.
b) Pasteurization, canning, salting.
c) Canning, blanching, drying.
d) Drying, freezing, smoking.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Drying, freezing, smoking.

Explanation:
• Inhibition (Bacteriostatic): Drying (removes moisture), Freezing (low temp), Smoking (chemicals/drying), Salting (osmosis).
• Killing (Bactericidal): Pasteurization, Canning, Blanching (use high heat).
Option D contains methods that primarily slow down or stop growth rather than sterilizing.

12.
Match the vectors in column A with the organism transmitted by the vector in column B.
COLUMN A COLUMN B
a) Anopheles mosquito. 1. Wuchereria Bancrofti.
b) Ornithodoros moubata. 2. Onchocerca volvulus.
3. Borrelia duttonii.
4. Trypanosoma brucei gambiense.
Show Answer

Matches:

  • a) Anopheles mosquito matches with 1: Wuchereria bancrofti (Anopheles transmits Filariasis in many rural settings, as well as Malaria).
  • b) Ornithodoros moubata matches with 3: Borrelia duttonii (This soft tick transmits Tick-borne Relapsing Fever).
13.
Clinical features of typhoid fever in the first week following the prodromal phase include:-
a) Meningism, constipation, enlarged spleen.
b) Pea soup diarrhoea, meningism, stepwise rise in temperature.
c) Constipation, abdominal distension, broncho-pneumonia.
d) Mental confusion, enlarged spleen, pea soup diarrhea.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Pea soup diarrhoea, meningism, stepwise rise in temperature.

Explanation: The classic sign of the first week is the stepwise rise in temperature (Step-ladder pyrexia). While "pea soup" diarrhea and splenomegaly typically appear later (Week 2-3) and constipation is common early on, the "stepwise rise" is the most specific Week 1 identifier in the list. *Note: In MCQ contexts, 'Pea soup' is often associated with Typhoid generally, but technically occurs later.*

14.
Progestin only pills work primarily by:-
a) Thickening cervical mucus, thus blocking sperm from meeting an egg.
b) Causing a chemical change that damages sperms and eggs before they can meet.
c) Suppressing the release of gonadotrophins.
d) Plugging the fallopian tubes thus preventing egg from meeting sperm.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Thickening cervical mucus, thus blocking sperm from meeting an egg.

Explanation: The primary mechanism of POPs (Mini-pills) is altering the cervical mucus to make it hostile to sperm. Suppression of ovulation (Option C) occurs in about 50% of cycles but is not the consistent primary mechanism (unlike COCs).

15.
The goals of triaging in disaster management are:-
a) Early patient assessment, control infection, determination of urgency of need of care.
b) Document initial patient assessment, teach health care to the patient...
c) Obtain past history records if needed...
d) Brief overall patient assessment, control the flow of patients and visitors...
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Early patient assessment, control infection, determination of urgency of need of care.

Explanation: The primary goal is to assess severity quickly (urgency) to allocate resources effectively and save the most lives.

16.
A tooth cavity that gets close to or reaches the pulp causes:-
a) Pain only when one drinks something cold or eats candy.
b) Sensitivity to the tooth when one bites.
c) Lingering pain even after a stimulus (e.g. cold water) is removed.
d) Swelling due to pus in the adjacent gum.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Lingering pain even after a stimulus (e.g. cold water) is removed.

Explanation: This describes Irreversible Pulpitis. The inflammation has reached the pulp and it cannot heal.
• Option A describes Reversible Pulpitis (early decay).
• Option D describes a Dental Abscess (pulp death).

17.
Growth and development increases in a rapid spurt at ages of:-
a) 2 to 5 years.
b) 9 to 12 years.
c) 6 to 12 months.
d) 5 to 8 years.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) 9 to 12 years.

Explanation: This corresponds to the onset of the Adolescent Growth Spurt (Puberty), particularly in girls. Infancy is also rapid, but "spurt" in school-age context refers to puberty.

18.
Primary Health Care activities at the district level are co-ordinated by the:-
a) Medical officer of health.
b) District development committee.
c) Public health officer.
d) District health management team.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) District health management team.

Explanation: The DHMT is the management body responsible for coordinating, planning, and supervising all health activities, including PHC, within the district.

19.
Activities of pre-visit phase of home visiting include:-
a) Sharing information on reason and purpose of visit...
b) Recording important events...
c) Gathering information on the location of the house, gathering some information on need of visit, assembling supplies needed for the visit.
d) Investigating community resources...
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Gathering information on the location of the house...

Explanation: The pre-visit phase is the preparation stage. You must know where you are going, why (review records), and pack your bag (supplies) before leaving the health facility.

20.
Factors that promote development in children include:-
a) Good nutrition, emotional support, play.
b) Child spacing, nutrition, immunization.
c) Good antenatal care, play, follow-up clinic.
d) Birth Order, child spacing, emotional support.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Good nutrition, emotional support, play.

Explanation: These are the direct pillars of holistic child development: Physical (Nutrition), Emotional/Social (Support), and Cognitive/Psychomotor (Play). Option B focuses more on survival/health interventions.

Part II: Short Answer Questions

40 Marks
1.
Outline the four (4) stages in the progression of HIV infection. (4 marks)
Show Answer
  1. Stage 1 (Acute/Primary Infection): Flu-like illness, seroconversion occurs.
  2. Stage 2 (Clinical Latency/Asymptomatic): No symptoms, but virus is replicating; minor signs like herpes zoster or URTI may occur.
  3. Stage 3 (Symptomatic): More severe signs appear (oral thrush, weight loss, chronic diarrhea, prolonged fever).
  4. Stage 4 (AIDS): Severe opportunistic infections (PCP, Toxoplasmosis, Cryptococcal meningitis) and malignancies (Kaposi's sarcoma). CD4 count is usually very low.
2.
a) Define a missed opportunity for immunization. (1 mark)
b) State five (5) ways to reduce missed opportunities for immunization. (5 marks)
Show Answer

a) Definition:
A missed opportunity occurs when a child (or woman) eligible for vaccination visits a health facility for any service (curative or preventive) but does not receive the vaccine(s) for which they are eligible.

b) Ways to Reduce Missed Opportunities:

  1. Daily Vaccination: Immunize every day, not just on specific "clinic days".
  2. Screening All Children: Check the immunization status of every sick child attending the OPD.
  3. Integration of Services: Combine immunization with other services (e.g., weighing, treatment).
  4. Open Vial Policy: Do not turn away a child because you don't want to open a multi-dose vial for just one person.
  5. Health Education: Educate staff and mothers on the importance of completing the schedule.
  6. Stock Management: Prevent vaccine stock-outs.
3.
State four (4) reasons why people are resistant to change in any organization. (4 marks)
Show Answer
  1. Fear of the Unknown: Uncertainty about how the change will affect them.
  2. Loss of Control: Feeling powerless or excluded from decision-making.
  3. Lack of Understanding/Trust: Not seeing the reason or benefit for the change.
  4. Fear of Job Loss/Status: Concern that technology or new structures will make them redundant.
  5. Inertia/Habit: Comfort with the existing way of doing things.
4.
With the aid of a diagram, illustrate the life cycle of Ancylostoma duodenale (hookworm). (5 marks)
Show Answer

Diagram Description:

  1. Eggs in Soil: Eggs passed in feces hatch in warm, moist soil.
  2. Larvae Development: First stage (Rhabditiform) larvae feed on soil bacteria, molt twice to become Filariform (L3).
  3. Infective Stage: Filariform larvae (non-feeding) wait on grass/soil.
  4. Penetration: Larvae penetrate human skin (feet).
  5. Migration: Enter venous circulation → Heart → Lungs.
  6. Swallowing: Break into alveoli, ascend trachea, are coughed up and swallowed.
  7. Adult Stage: Reach small intestine, attach to mucosa, mature, suck blood, and lay eggs.
5.
State the four (4) goals of community health nursing. (4 marks)
Show Answer
  1. Promotion of Health: Enhancing wellness.
  2. Prevention of Disease: Immunization, hygiene.
  3. Care of the Sick (Curative): Management of minor ailments.
  4. Rehabilitation: Restoring function/limiting disability.
  5. (Also: Prolonging life).
6.
Explain three (3) ways in which family planning providers can help prevent mother-to-child transmission of HIV. (6 marks)
Show Answer
  1. Prevention of Unintended Pregnancies: By providing effective contraception to HIV-positive women who do not wish to conceive, the risk of having an HIV-positive child is eliminated (Prong 2 of PMTCT).
  2. Dual Protection Counseling: Promoting condom use along with another method to prevent both pregnancy and HIV transmission/re-infection.
  3. Pre-conception Care/Counseling: Helping HIV-positive couples plan pregnancies safely (e.g., when viral load is suppressed) to minimize transmission risk.
7.
a) Define socialization. (1 mark)
b) Briefly explain two (2) agencies of socialization. (4 marks)
Show Answer

a) Socialization: The lifelong process through which individuals learn and internalize the values, beliefs, and norms of their society and learn to function as members of that society.

b) Agencies:

  • Family: The primary agent. It provides the initial social identity, language, and emotional support. It teaches basic norms and hygiene.
  • School: A secondary agent. It teaches formal knowledge, discipline, social rules, and interaction with peers outside the family.
  • (Others: Peer groups, Mass media, Religion).
8.
Explain three (3) challenges faced by the girl child due to teenage pregnancy. (6 marks)
Show Answer
  1. School Dropout/Education Disruption: Pregnancy often leads to expulsion or dropping out of school, limiting future career prospects and economic independence.
  2. Health Risks/Complications: Teenage girls are physically immature, leading to higher risks of Cephalo-Pelvic Disproportion (CPD), obstructed labor, fistulas, and anemia.
  3. Stigma and Social Isolation: They often face rejection from family, peers, and the community, leading to depression and low self-esteem.
  4. Poverty: Lack of skills and the burden of childcare perpetuate the cycle of poverty.

Part III: Essay/Long Answer Questions

40 Marks
1.
Due to the drought situation, large populations are moving from Somalia to refugee camps in Kenya, raising concern about the spread of measles.

a) Outline four (4) factors that can lead to the failure of measles vaccination. (4 marks)
b) Describe the prevention and control measures you would institute to curb the spread of measles in the camps. (10 marks)
c) State six (6) complications of measles. (6 marks)
Show Answer

a) Factors leading to Measles Vaccination Failure:

  1. Cold Chain Failure: Exposure to heat/light rendering the live attenuated vaccine ineffective.
  2. Maternal Antibodies: Vaccination too early (<9 months) when maternal antibodies neutralize the vaccine.
  3. Host Factors: Severe malnutrition or immunosuppression preventing adequate immune response.
  4. Improper Administration: Wrong route, dosage, or using expired diluent.

b) Prevention and Control in Camps:

  • Mass Vaccination (Campaign): Immediately vaccinate all children aged 6 months to 15 years upon arrival, regardless of history, to boost herd immunity.
  • Vitamin A Supplementation: Administer Vitamin A to boost mucosal immunity and reduce severity.
  • Surveillance: Set up active surveillance to detect cases early (case definition: fever + rash + cough/coryza/conjunctivitis).
  • Isolation: Isolate identified cases to prevent spread (airborne precautions).
  • Crowd Management: Reduce overcrowding in shelters to minimize transmission.
  • Case Management: Treat complications (pneumonia, eye infections) promptly. Support nutrition.
  • Health Education: Educate refugees on signs of measles and the importance of vaccination.

c) Complications of Measles:

  1. Pneumonia (Major cause of death).
  2. Diarrhoea and dehydration.
  3. Otitis Media (Ear infection).
  4. Corneal ulceration/Blindness (xerophthalmia).
  5. Severe Malnutrition (Measles precipitates kwashiorkor).
  6. Encephalitis (Brain inflammation).
  7. Stomatitis/Mouth ulcers.
2.
As the Nursing Officer incharge of the maternity unit, you have been informed that nursing staff in the antenatal and labour ward were engaged in a bitter argument over the management of an antenatal mother.

a) Define conflict. (1 mark)
b) State three (3) types of conflicts. (3 marks)
c) List six (6) factors contributing to a conflict. (3 marks)
d) Describe how you will resolve this situation. (13 marks)
Show Answer

a) Definition:
Conflict is a state of disagreement or disharmony between persons or ideas, resulting from a clash of interests, values, actions, or directions.

b) Types of Conflicts:

  1. Intrapersonal: Conflict within oneself.
  2. Interpersonal: Conflict between two individuals.
  3. Intergroup: Conflict between different teams/departments.

c) Factors Contributing to Conflict (6 @ 0.5 marks):

  1. Poor communication/misunderstanding.
  2. Scarcity of resources (staff, equipment).
  3. Role ambiguity (unclear responsibilities).
  4. Personality clashes.
  5. Stress and fatigue (heavy workload).
  6. Differing values or beliefs regarding patient care.

d) Resolution Process:

  1. Immediate Intervention: Separate the parties to stop the public argument and restore order in the ward.
  2. Fact Finding: Call the staff members individually to your office to hear their sides of the story without interruption (Active Listening).
  3. Identify the Root Cause: Determine if it was a clinical disagreement, personality issue, or stress.
  4. Joint Meeting: Bring the parties together in a neutral environment.
  5. Facilitate Dialogue: Allow them to express their views respectfully. Ensure focus remains on the *issue* (patient care), not the *person*.
  6. Clarify Protocols: Refer to standard guidelines/SOPs regarding the management of the antenatal mother to settle the technical disagreement.
  7. Negotiation/Compromise: Help them find a middle ground or agree on the correct professional approach.
  8. Reinforce Professionalism: Remind them of the code of conduct regarding arguing in front of patients.
  9. Action Plan: Agree on how to handle similar situations in future.
  10. Follow-up: Monitor their relationship and performance to ensure the conflict is truly resolved.
