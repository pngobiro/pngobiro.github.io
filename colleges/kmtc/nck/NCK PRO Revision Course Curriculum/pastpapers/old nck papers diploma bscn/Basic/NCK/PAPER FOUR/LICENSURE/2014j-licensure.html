Part I: Multiple Choice Questions

20 Marks
1.
The initial symptoms of lithium toxicity include:-
a) Tremors, shuffling gait, and rigidity.
b) Stiff neck, occipital headache and increased blood pressure.
c) Ataxia, blurred vision and severe nausea.
d) Fever, sore throat and cardiac arrhythmias.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Ataxia, blurred vision and severe nausea.

Explanation: Early signs of Lithium toxicity (levels > 1.5 mEq/L) typically involve the GI tract (severe nausea, vomiting, diarrhea) and CNS (ataxia, blurred vision, coarse tremors, confusion).

  • a) Describes Parkinsonism/Extrapyramidal side effects (typical of antipsychotics).
  • b) Describes Hypertensive Crisis (e.g., MAOI interaction).
  • d) Describes Agranulocytosis (e.g., Clozapine) or Neuroleptic Malignant Syndrome.
2.
In acute mania a patient presents with:-
a) Cheerful and expansive mood.
b) Excessive psychomotor activity.
c) Increased motor activity.
d) Organized thought content and process.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Cheerful and expansive mood.

Explanation: The hallmark of mania is an elevated, expansive, or irritable mood. While excessive psychomotor activity is present, the primary presentation described in diagnostic criteria is the mood disturbance. Option (a) captures the classic "elation" or "high". Note: In multiple choice questions where both mood and activity are listed, mood is often considered the primary defining feature of the affect.

3.
In helping a child with a gender identity problem the most appropriate nursing intervention is:-
a) Give positive reinforcement when he/she engages in opposite sex behaviour.
b) Give positive reinforcement when he/she engages in assigned sex behaviour.
c) Do not allow the child to interact with others until he/she demonstrates only appropriate behaviour.
d) Withdraw privileges if the child is noncompliant.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Give positive reinforcement when he/she engages in assigned sex behaviour.

Explanation: In a traditional behavioral modification approach (which this question reflects), the goal is to reinforce behaviors congruent with the child's assigned biological sex to reduce distress/dysphoria. Punitive measures (c, d) are inappropriate and harmful.

5.
The unipolar mood disorders include:-
a) Cyclothymia and mixed manic episodes.
b) Hypomania and dysthymia.
c) Major depression and mixed manic states.
d) Cyclothymia and mood disorders related to manic conditions.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: Note: This question has flawed options.

Explanation: "Unipolar" refers to depression only, without mania.
- Dysthymia is a unipolar disorder.
- Major Depression is a unipolar disorder.
- Cyclothymia, Hypomania, and Mixed states are Bipolar spectrum disorders.
There is no perfect option here, but usually, unipolar covers Major Depression and Dysthymia. None of the options list only unipolar conditions perfectly without mixing mania/cyclothymia, but clinically, Major Depression is the primary unipolar disorder.

6.
Micro-teaching is:-
a) Real teaching focusing on developing pupil abilities.
b) Composed of a scaled up class size, content and time.
c) Mainly focused on training for the development of specific teaching skills.
d) Not individualized but largely depends on group feedback.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Mainly focused on training for the development of specific teaching skills.

Explanation: Micro-teaching is a teacher training technique where the teacher practices a specific skill (e.g., questioning, introduction) for a short time (5-10 mins) with a small group of peers/students (scaled down, not up).

7.
The steps of demonstrating a psychomotor skill are:-
a) Formulate behaviour objectives, perform skill analysis, assess entry behaviour of learners and formulate a lesson plan.
b) Formulate a lesson plan, perform skills analysis, assess learners entry behaviour and formulate behavioural objectives.
c) Assess entry behaviour of learners, formulate lesson plan, formulate behavioural objectives and perform skills analysis.
d) Perform skills analysis, formulate behavioral objectives, formulate lesson plan and assess the entry behaviours of learners.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Formulate behaviour objectives, perform skill analysis, assess entry behaviour of learners and formulate a lesson plan.

Explanation: The logical flow of planning a demonstration involves knowing what you want to achieve (objectives), breaking down the skill (analysis), knowing who you are teaching (entry behavior), and then putting it all into a plan.

8.
Objective questions:-
a) Measure student’s proficiency in performing procedures.
b) Activates several senses in a learner.
c) Improves on student’s reflective thinking and skills on patient care.
d) Measures student’s achievement in relation to knowledge, skills and attitudes.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Measures student’s achievement in relation to knowledge, skills and attitudes.

Explanation: Objective questions (like MCQs, matching, true/false) are efficient at sampling a wide range of content to measure achievement, primarily in the cognitive domain (knowledge), but can touch on others.

9.
A good report should be:-
a) Written instantly, concise and clear.
b) Contextualized, brief and enriched with the writers opinion.
c) Free of extraneous materials, concise and complete.
d) Promptly written, data enriched and to detail in all aspects.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Free of extraneous materials, concise and complete.

Explanation: A professional report should be factual (objective), concise (brief but comprehensive), complete (covering all necessary info), and free of irrelevant (extraneous) details. It should not contain the writer's opinion (subjectivity).

10.
Brain storming as a teaching strategy:-
a) Ensures all group members have put up their ideas.
b) Encourages discovery of knowledge.
c) It yields into convincing solutions in most of the cases.
d) It usually ends in useful conclusions and solutions to problems.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Encourages discovery of knowledge.

Explanation: Brainstorming stimulates creativity and free association of ideas, encouraging students to discover new angles or solutions. It focuses on quantity of ideas rather than immediate quality or solutions.

11.
Anonymity in research is aimed at:-
a) Preventing disclosure of privileged information.
b) Conferring protection to the source of information.
c) Selectively disclosing information to specific individuals.
d) Keeping names of respondents only known to the research supervisor.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Conferring protection to the source of information.

Explanation: Anonymity means the data cannot be linked to the participant by anyone, including the researcher. This protects the source. Option (d) describes Confidentiality (where names are known but kept secret).

12.
The problem statement in a research proposal should include:-
a) The background of the study.
b) An outline of the study objectives.
c) The magnitude of the issue under study.
d) The benefits the study shall contribute against challenges.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) The magnitude of the issue under study.

Explanation: A good problem statement articulates the gap in knowledge and the severity/magnitude of the problem to justify why the research is needed.

13.
The Hawthorne effect is as a result of:-
a) A researcher lacking expertise to analyze data.
b) Study subjects being aware they are participating in a study.
c) Study tools being disclosed to non study subjects.
d) Respondents not completing the interview schedule.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Study subjects being aware they are participating in a study.

Explanation: The Hawthorne effect is the alteration of behavior by the subjects of a study due to their awareness of being observed.

14.
The study design that is appropriate in observing the behaviour changes of drug addicts under rehabilitation is:-
a) Retrospective study design.
b) Longitudinal study design.
c) Prospective study design.
d) Cross sectional design.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Longitudinal study design.

Explanation: Longitudinal studies involve repeated observations of the same variables (e.g., behavior) over long periods of time, making them ideal for tracking changes during rehabilitation.

15.
The most appropriate method of studying characteristics of wound healing would be:-
a) Questionnaire.
b) Participants observation.
c) Pure observation.
d) Context analysis.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Pure observation.

Explanation: Wound healing is a physical process that is best assessed through direct observation/inspection using a checklist (observation checklist). The researcher does not participate in the healing (unlike participant observation which is social).

16.
A convulsing one month old infant should be given:-
a) Phenobarbitone 20 mg/kg im stat.
b) Diazepam 0.3 mg/kg iv 4 hourly over 1 minute.
c) Phenobarbitone 5mg/kg im stat.
d) Diazepam 1 mg/kg per rectal stat.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Phenobarbitone 20 mg/kg im stat.

Explanation: For neonates and young infants (< 1 month or slightly older), Phenobarbital is the first-line anticonvulsant. The loading dose is 20 mg/kg IV or IM.

17.
The immediate management for severe dehydration in a child with severe malnutrition according to IMCI strategy includes:-
a) Infusion of half strength darrows in 5% dextrose 15 mls/kg iv in 1 hr.
b) Infusion of 0.9% normal saline 20ml/kg in 1 hr.
c) Transfusion of whole blood 40 ml/kg over 3hrs as soon as it is available.
d) NGT feeding with Resomal 15ml/kg/hr for 10 hours.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) NGT feeding with Resomal 15ml/kg/hr for 10 hours. (Best Fit)

Explanation: In severe malnutrition (SAM), standard IV rehydration is dangerous due to heart failure risk. The IMCI guideline is oral or NGT rehydration using ReSoMal (Rehydration Solution for Malnutrition). The standard rate is slower (5-10 ml/kg/hr), but compared to the other options (IV fluids which are contraindicated unless in shock), ReSoMal via NGT is the correct mode of management.

18.
The ratio of infant chest compressions to ventilation where rescue is involved is:-
a) 30:2.
b) 30:1.
c) 15:2.
d) 15:1.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) 15:2.

Explanation: For infants and children, the compression-to-ventilation ratio is 15:2 if two rescuers (healthcare providers) are present. If a single rescuer, it is 30:2. The phrase "where rescue is involved" implies professional/team rescue.

19.
Indicate whether the statements are true or false.
a) The iliac crest is the site of choice for bone marrow aspiration in a 5 year old child.
b) Acute renal failure is the leading complication associated with acute Glomerulonephritis.

2.
Define the following terms: (a) Counseling (1 mark). (b) Guidance (1 mark). (c) Psychotherapy (1 mark).
Show Answer

Answers:

a) Counseling: A professional helping relationship where a counselor assists a client to explore their problems, understand their feelings, and find their own solutions to personal or psychological issues.

b) Guidance: A process of helping individuals make choices and adjustments in relation to their educational, vocational, or personal lives, often involving giving information and advice.

c) Psychotherapy: The treatment of mental or emotional disorders through psychological means (talk therapy) rather than medical means, aiming to change maladaptive thoughts, feelings, and behaviors.

3.
Explain four (4) rules of a nurse in Milieu therapy. (4 marks)
Show Answer

Answer:

  1. Ensuring Safety: Maintaining a safe physical and psychological environment, removing dangerous objects, and managing aggression.
  2. Providing Structure: Establishing and maintaining a consistent daily schedule and clear rules/expectations to provide predictability.
  3. Facilitating Socialization: Encouraging patient interaction, group participation, and using opportunities to model appropriate social skills.
  4. Emotional Support: Being available to listen, offering reassurance, and helping patients process their feelings in the here-and-now.
4.
Describe the acronym ‘SPECIES’ in relation to innovative teaching/learning methods. (5 marks)
Show Answer

Answer:

The acronym SPICES (often referred to as SPECIES in some contexts) represents the features of an innovative medical/nursing curriculum:

  • S - Student-centered: Focus is on what the student learns rather than what the teacher teaches.
  • P - Problem-based: Learning is driven by clinical problems/scenarios.
  • I - Integrated: Boundaries between disciplines (e.g., anatomy, physiology, clinical practice) are broken down.
  • C - Community-based: Training occurs in the community, not just hospitals.
  • E - Electives: Students can choose areas of interest to study in depth.
  • S - Systematic: The curriculum is planned and structured, not opportunistic.
5.
Explain the first five (5) stages of social change. (5 marks)
Show Answer

Answer (Rogers' Innovation-Decision Process):

  1. Awareness/Knowledge: The individual is exposed to the innovation’s existence and gains some understanding of how it functions.
  2. Interest/Persuasion: The individual forms a favorable or unfavorable attitude toward the innovation.
  3. Evaluation/Decision: The individual engages in activities that lead to a choice to adopt or reject the innovation.
  4. Trial/Implementation: The individual puts the innovation to use (often on a small scale).
  5. Adoption/Confirmation: The individual seeks reinforcement of the innovation-decision already made (full use).

7.
State the first six (6) phases of psychological debriefing. (6 marks)
Show Answer

Answer (Mitchell Model - CISD):

  1. Introduction Phase: Rules are set, confidentiality explained, purpose defined.
  2. Fact Phase: Participants describe what happened from their perspective (facts only).
  3. Thought Phase: Participants describe their first thoughts during the event.
  4. Reaction Phase: Participants describe the worst part of the event and their emotional reactions.
  5. Symptom Phase: Participants describe physical/cognitive symptoms experienced during or after the event.
  6. Teaching Phase: Leaders normalize the reactions and provide stress management strategies.
  7. (Re-entry Phase: Summary and closure).
8.
Outline four (4) characteristics of good teaching aids. (4 marks)
Show Answer

Answer:

  1. Relevance: Directly related to the topic and helps achieve learning objectives.
  2. Accuracy: Contains correct and up-to-date information.
  3. Visibility/Audibility: Large enough to be seen (e.g., charts) or loud enough to be heard by the whole class.
  4. Simplicity: Clear, uncluttered, and easy to understand; not distracting.
  5. Durability/Manageability: Easy to handle, transport, and use.
9.
State six (6) qualities of a good questionnaire. (6 marks)
Show Answer

Answer:

  1. Clarity: Questions should be clear, simple, and unambiguous.
  2. Relevance: Questions must relate directly to the study objectives.
  3. Brevity/Conciseness: Should be as short as possible to maintain respondent interest.
  4. Objectivity/Unbiased: Questions should not be leading or loaded.
  5. Logical Sequence: Questions should flow naturally (e.g., general to specific).
  6. Valid and Reliable: Accurately measures what it intends to measure consistently.

Part III: Essay/Long Answer Questions

40 Marks
1.

Mrs. X 35 years old, a single mother of four is admitted in a psychiatric unit with a diagnosis of schizophrenia.

a) List any other two (2) types of schizophrenia. (1 mark)

b) List four (4) predisposing factors to Mrs. X’s condition. (2 marks)

c) Describe the management of Mrs. X while in the ward till discharge. (12 marks)

d) Formulate three (3) nursing diagnosis for Mrs. X. (5 marks)

Show Answer

a) Types of Schizophrenia (other than the implied diagnosis):

  • Paranoid Schizophrenia.
  • Catatonic Schizophrenia.
  • Disorganized (Hebephrenic) Schizophrenia.
  • Residual Schizophrenia.
  • Undifferentiated Schizophrenia.

b) Predisposing Factors:

  • Genetic predisposition: Family history of schizophrenia.
  • Neurobiological factors: Dopamine hypothesis (excess dopamine activity).
  • Psychosocial stressors: Severe stress, trauma, or poverty (single mother of 4).
  • Family dynamics: High expressed emotion environments.
  • Substance abuse: History of drug use (cannabis).

c) Management (Medical & Nursing):

  • Pharmacotherapy: Administration of antipsychotics (e.g., Haloperidol, Chlorpromazine, or atypicals like Risperidone) to control hallucinations/delusions. Monitor for side effects (EPS).
  • Safety: Ensure a safe environment; remove harmful objects; monitor for suicide risk.
  • Therapeutic Relationship: Establish trust; be consistent, honest, and non-judgmental.
  • Managing Delusions/Hallucinations: Do not reinforce delusions; acknowledge feelings but present reality gently ("I find it hard to believe...").
  • Self-Care Deficit: Assist with ADLs (hygiene, feeding) if she is unable to perform them.
  • Psychotherapy: Individual counseling, cognitive behavioral therapy (CBT) to manage symptoms.
  • Social Support: Family therapy to educate the family and reduce stress; social skills training.
  • Rehabilitation: Preparing for discharge; occupational therapy; medication adherence education.

d) Nursing Diagnoses:

  1. Disturbed Sensory Perception (Auditory/Visual) related to biochemical imbalance as evidenced by hallucinations.
  2. Disturbed Thought Processes related to inability to process information as evidenced by delusions.
  3. Impaired Social Interaction related to withdrawal and trust issues.
  4. Self-Care Deficit (Bathing/Hygiene) related to withdrawal and lack of motivation.
  5. Risk for Violence: Self-directed or Other-directed related to command hallucinations.
2.

Master K. 10 years old is admitted in a paediatric ward with a diagnosis of rheumatic fever.

a) Draw and label a diagram of a synovial joint. (4 marks)

b) List four (4) specific investigations used to diagnose rheumatic fever. (2 marks)

c) Describe the specific management for Master K from admission till discharge. (12 marks)

d) List four (4) complications associated with rheumatic fever. (2 marks)

Show Answer

a) Diagram of Synovial Joint:

(Imagine a diagram showing two bones meeting). Key labels should include:

  • Articular Cartilage: Covers ends of bones.
  • Synovial Membrane: Lines the capsule.
  • Synovial Fluid: Inside the joint cavity.
  • Joint Capsule: Outer fibrous layer.
  • Bone: The articulating bones.
  • Ligaments: Connecting bone to bone.

b) Specific Investigations:

  • ASO Titer (Anti-Streptolysin O): Elevated levels indicate recent Strep infection.
  • Throat Swab Culture: To isolate Group A Beta-Hemolytic Streptococcus.
  • ESR (Erythrocyte Sedimentation Rate) and CRP: Elevated markers of inflammation.
  • ECG (Electrocardiogram): To check for prolonged PR interval (heart block).
  • Echocardiogram: To assess heart valve damage (carditis).

c) Specific Management:

  • Bed Rest: Absolute bed rest during the acute phase to reduce cardiac workload, especially if carditis is present.
  • Antibiotic Therapy: Penicillin (e.g., Benzathine Penicillin G IM stat) to eradicate residual streptococcal infection. Long-term prophylaxis will follow.
  • Anti-inflammatory Drugs:
    • Aspirin: High dose for arthritis/fever.
    • Corticosteroids (Prednisone): Indicated for severe carditis/heart failure.
  • Pain Management: Careful handling of painful joints; bed cradle to keep sheets off joints.
  • Heart Failure Management: Digoxin and Diuretics if cardiac compromise is present.
  • Nutrition: High protein, high calorie diet; salt restriction if in heart failure.
  • Education: Teach parents about the importance of long-term penicillin prophylaxis (monthly injections) to prevent recurrence.

d) Complications:

  1. Rheumatic Heart Disease (RHD): Permanent valve damage (e.g., Mitral Stenosis/Regurgitation).
  2. Congestive Heart Failure: Due to severe carditis.
  3. Infective Endocarditis: Damaged valves are prone to infection.
  4. Atrial Fibrillation: Arrhythmias due to atrial enlargement.
  5. Sydenham's Chorea: (If considered a late complication/sequelae).
