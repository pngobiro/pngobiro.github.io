Part I: Multiple Choice Questions

20 Marks
1.
Antiparkinsonian agents act through restoring the natural balance of:-
a) Acetylcholine and dopamine neurotransmitters.
b) Serotonin and adrenaline neurotransmitters.
c) Dopamine and serotonin neurotransmitters.
d) Dopamine and nor-adrenaline neurotransmitters.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Acetylcholine and dopamine neurotransmitters.

Explanation: Parkinson's disease is characterized by a depletion of dopamine, leading to an imbalance where acetylcholine activity is relatively excessive. Antiparkinsonian drugs work by either increasing dopamine (e.g., Levodopa) or blocking acetylcholine (anticholinergics) to restore this balance in the basal ganglia.

2.
The primary nursing consideration in managing an anorexic patient is to:-
a) Identify stressors.
b) Include family members.
c) Relieve depression.
d) Restore nutrition.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Restore nutrition.

Explanation: Following Maslow's Hierarchy of Needs, physiological survival is the priority. Anorexia nervosa patients are often in a state of severe malnutrition and electrolyte imbalance which can be life-threatening (e.g., cardiac arrhythmias). Therefore, restoring nutritional status and weight is the immediate priority before psychological issues can be effectively addressed.

3.
Individuals with histrionic personality disorder:-
a) Belittle themselves and their abilities.
b) Inappropriately overreact to minor stimuli.
c) Are suspicious and mistrustful of others.
d) Have a lifelong pattern of social withdrawal.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Inappropriately overreact to minor stimuli.

Explanation: Histrionic Personality Disorder is characterized by excessive emotionality and attention-seeking behavior. They tend to be dramatic, theatrical, and may overreact emotionally to minor events.

  • c) Describes Paranoid Personality Disorder.
  • d) Describes Schizoid or Avoidant Personality Disorder.
4.
The withdrawal symptoms associated with Heroin addiction are:-
a) Diaphoresis, increased heart rate, increased blood pressure.
b) Tremors, insomnia, seizures.
c) Inco-ordination, nausea, unsteady gait.
d) Vomiting, diarrhoea, diaphoresis.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Vomiting, diarrhoea, diaphoresis.

Explanation: Opioid (Heroin) withdrawal presents with "flu-like" symptoms including rhinorrhea, lacrimation, diaphoresis (sweating), nausea, vomiting, diarrhea, and muscle aches. While heart rate may increase, the combination of GI upset and sweating is classic.

  • b) Seizures are typical of Alcohol or Benzodiazepine withdrawal, not Opioids.
5.
An individual who has mistaken ideas that he/she is someone of extreme importance is said to have:-
a) Delusion of grandeur.
b) Somatic delusions.
c) Erotomanic delusions.
d) Persecutory delusions.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Delusion of grandeur.

Explanation: Grandiose delusions involve the false belief that one has exceptional abilities, wealth, or fame (e.g., believing they are Jesus or a famous billionaire).

6.
Micro-teaching skills include:-
a) Lecturing, illustrating and testing.
b) Stimulus variation, paraphrasing and probing.
c) Closure, silence and non verbal cues.
d) Explaining, attending behaviour and animation.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Stimulus variation, paraphrasing and probing.

Explanation: Micro-teaching focuses on practicing specific teaching skills. Standard skills include "Stimulus Variation" (changing teaching style to hold attention) and "Probing Questions" (deepening student response). While other options list some skills, Option B lists distinct, defined micro-teaching skills.

7.
Projected teaching aids include:-
a) Films, models, recordings and slides.
b) Slides, film strips, opaque projector and overhead projector.
c) Photographs, models, flash cards and videotapes.
d) Chalkboards, puppets, slides and cartoons.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Slides, film strips, opaque projector and overhead projector.

Explanation: Projected aids require a machine to project an image onto a screen. Slides, film strips, and overhead projectors fit this definition perfectly. Models and chalkboards are non-projected aids.

8.
In an objective structured practical examination:-
a) Each student starts the examination at a different station.
b) Each student is timed separately performing a task.
c) Only decision making and manual skills are assessed.
d) The entire examination does not take more than 1 hour.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Each student starts the examination at a different station.

Explanation: In an OSPE (Objective Structured Practical Examination), students rotate through a circuit of stations. At any given time, multiple students are performing the exam, each starting at a different station and moving to the next when the bell rings.

9.
A learning discussion consists of:-
a) Group moderator, observer, evaluator, 15 - 25 members.
b) Group recorder, leader, evaluator, 10 - 12 members.
c) Group leader, recorder, observer, 6 - 20 members.
d) Group motivator, leader, note taker, 6 - 10 members.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Group recorder, leader, evaluator, 10 - 12 members.

Explanation: An effective small group discussion usually has 6-12 members. Groups larger than 12-15 become difficult to manage and reduce individual participation. The roles typically include a leader (chair), recorder (secretary), and members.

10.
The higher teaching/learning techniques include:-
a) Conferences, seminars, symposia and workshops.
b) Field trips, seminars, group discussions and role playing.
c) Role playing, simulation, seminars and field trips.
d) Group discussions, assignments, lecturing and tutorials.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Conferences, seminars, symposia and workshops.

Explanation: These techniques (Conferences, Symposia, Seminars) are typically associated with higher education or professional development where learners contribute significantly and analysis/synthesis of information occurs.

11.
A research question:-
a) Examines the characteristics of variables.
b) States which variables are to be manipulated.
c) Focuses on what relationship might exist among variables.
d) Focuses on the pattern for conducting an investigation.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Focuses on what relationship might exist among variables.

Explanation: A research question primarily interrogates the relationship between variables (e.g., "What is the relationship between diet and recovery time?").

12.
A sampling method where research subjects recommend other interviewees is known as:-
a) Random sampling.
b) Stratified random sampling.
c) Snowball sampling.
d) Convenient sampling.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Snowball sampling.

Explanation: Snowball sampling relies on initial subjects nominating other participants, who in turn nominate others, causing the sample to grow like a rolling snowball. This is useful for hard-to-reach populations.

13.
In data collection, the term reliability refers to:-
a) Stability or repeatability of the data collected.
b) Originality of the tools being used for data collection.
c) The independence of the study investigators.
d) The extent to which the study instruments collect what is intended.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Stability or repeatability of the data collected.

Explanation: Reliability means consistency. If the same instrument measures the same thing multiple times, it should yield the same result. Option (d) defines Validity.

14.
An example of an intervention study design is:-
a) Quasi experimental design.
b) Evaluative study design.
c) Exploratory study design.
d) Descriptive study design.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Quasi experimental design.

Explanation: Intervention studies (experimental designs) involve the researcher manipulating a variable. Quasi-experimental designs involve manipulation but lack random assignment. Exploratory and Descriptive designs are observational.

15.
Plagiarism occurs if a researcher:-
a) Quotes the exact words of another author and gives reference.
b) Paraphrases a passage of another author and gives no reference.
c) Uses an idea or material based directly on the work of another author but gives reference.
d) Summarizes a passage by another author and states the publisher.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Paraphrases a passage of another author and gives no reference.

Explanation: Plagiarism is presenting someone else's work or ideas as your own without proper credit. Even if paraphrased, the source must be cited.

16.
A 9 month old infant with 40 breaths per minute, a cough and a stridor will be having:-
a) Pneumonia.
b) Severe pneumonia.
c) Asthma.
d) Bronchitis.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Severe pneumonia.

Explanation: According to IMCI guidelines:
1. Cough + Stridor in a calm child classifies as Severe Pneumonia (or Very Severe Disease).
2. Cough + Fast Breathing classifies as Pneumonia.
For a 9-month-old, the cut-off for fast breathing is 50 breaths/minute. Since the child has 40 bpm (normal), but has stridor, the classification is based on the danger sign (stridor), making it Severe Pneumonia.

17.
The treatment of choice for an infant with bacillary dysentery is:-
a) Mebendazole 125 mg stat and vit A 100,000 IU.
b) Erythromycin 125 mg stat and multivitamin syrup 2.5 ml od for 1 week.
c) Ciproxin 125 mg qid for five days and vit A 100,000 IU start.
d) Metronidazole 250 mg 8 hourly p.o. for 5 days, vit A 200,000 IU stat.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Ciproxin 125 mg qid for five days and vit A 100,000 IU start.

Explanation:
1. Dysentery (Bloody diarrhea): The first-line antibiotic for Shigella (Bacillary dysentery) is Ciprofloxacin (Ciproxin).
2. Vitamin A: Is given to all children with dysentery to prevent complications.
3. Metronidazole (Option D) is used for Amoebic dysentery, which is less common in infants.

18.
Clinical manifestations of pyloric stenosis include:-
a) Projectile vomiting, colicky abdominal pains.
b) Constipation, olive-like mass in upper abdomen.
c) Observable peristaltic movements, diarrhea.
d) Failure to thrive, tenesmus.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Constipation, olive-like mass in upper abdomen.

Explanation: The classic triad of Pyloric Stenosis includes:
1. Non-bilious projectile vomiting.
2. Visible peristalsis (left to right).
3. Palpable "olive-like" mass in the epigastrium.
Infants are often constipated (due to dehydration/vomiting) and are "hungry vomiters". Diarrhea is incorrect.

19.
Indicate whether the statements are true or false.
a) Recovery from acute glomerulonephritis confers specific immunity.
b) Metastasis of Burkitt’s lymphoma is limited to the salivary glands.
Show Answer

Answers:

a) True: Immunity to Group A Streptococcus is type-specific (M protein). Recovery confers immunity to that specific nephritogenic strain, so recurrence with the same strain is rare.

b) False: Burkitt's lymphoma is an aggressive B-cell lymphoma that can spread rapidly to the CNS, bone marrow, and other abdominal organs, not just salivary glands.

20.
Complications associated with Tracheo-oesophageal atresia include:-
a) Pneumonia, malnutrition, intestinal obstruction.
b) Stenosis of the oesophagus, leakage into the mediastinum, intestinal obstruction.
c) Abdominal distension, constipation, fistula formation.
d) Gastro-oesophageal reflux, oesophagus constriction, leakage into the chest cavity.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Pneumonia, malnutrition, intestinal obstruction. (Best Fit for Immediate/Late)

Explanation: The primary lethal complication is aspiration pneumonia due to saliva or gastric contents entering the lungs via the fistula. Malnutrition follows feeding difficulties. Anastomotic leak and stricture (stenosis) are surgical complications. Gastro-oesophageal reflux is very common post-repair.
