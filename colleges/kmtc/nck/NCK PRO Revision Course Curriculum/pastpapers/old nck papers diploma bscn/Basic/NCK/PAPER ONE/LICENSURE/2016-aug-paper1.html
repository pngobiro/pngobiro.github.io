Part I: Multiple Choice Questions

20 Marks
1.
Obstruction of the hepatopancreatic sphincter impairs digestion by reducing the availability of:-
a) Hcl and bile.
b) Hcl and intestinal juice.
c) Pancreatic juice and intestinal juice.
d) Pancreatic juice and bile.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Pancreatic juice and bile.

Explanation: The hepatopancreatic sphincter (Sphincter of Oddi) controls the flow of both bile (from the common bile duct) and pancreatic juice (from the pancreatic duct) into the duodenum. Obstruction here blocks both fluids.
Note: HCl comes from the stomach, and intestinal juice comes from the intestinal wall, so they are not affected by this sphincter.

2.
Non-specific body defense mechanisms include:-
a) T-lymphocytes, B-lymphocytes.
b) Natural killer cells, phagocytes.
c) Antibodies, mucus membranes.
d) Phagocytes, B-lymphocytes.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Natural killer cells, phagocytes.

Explanation: Non-specific (Innate) immunity includes physical barriers (skin, mucus), chemical barriers, and cells like Phagocytes (macrophages/neutrophils) and Natural Killer (NK) cells that attack any foreign pathogen without prior sensitization.
Why others are wrong: B-lymphocytes, T-lymphocytes, and Antibodies are part of Specific (Adaptive) immunity.

3.
Mycoses that cause systemic infection include:-
a) Histoplasma capsulatum, coccidioides immitis.
b) Modurella mycetomatis, sporothrix schenckii.
c) Malassezia furfur, microsporum.
d) Candida albicans, epidermophyton floccosum.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Histoplasma capsulatum, coccidioides immitis.

Explanation:

  • Systemic Mycoses: Histoplasmosis and Coccidioidomycosis affect internal organs (lungs, etc.).
  • Subcutaneous: Sporothrix (b).
  • Superficial/Cutaneous: Malassezia (c), Microsporum/Epidermophyton (ringworm) (d).

4.
Patients with COPD are encouraged to exercise because it:-
a) Improves respiratory muscle strength.
b) Reduces the number of exacerbations.
c) Prevents over â€“ reliance on medication.
d) Enhances cardiovascular compliance.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Improves respiratory muscle strength. (Or d - Enhances cardiovascular conditioning).

Explanation: Pulmonary rehabilitation involves exercise training to improve the efficiency of skeletal muscles (reducing oxygen demand) and breathing exercises to strengthen respiratory muscles. This improves exercise tolerance and reduces dyspnea. While it doesn't "cure" the lung disease, it optimizes the body's use of oxygen.

5.
The most appropriate treatment goal for a client with status asthmaticus is:-
a) Avoiding intubation.
b) Determining the cause of attack.
c) Improving exercise tolerance.
d) Reducing secretions.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Avoiding intubation. (Preventing respiratory failure).

Explanation: Status asthmaticus is a life-threatening medical emergency where the airway obstruction is unresponsive to standard treatment. The immediate priority goals are to reverse obstruction, correct hypoxemia, and prevent respiratory failure that would necessitate mechanical ventilation (intubation). Determining the cause (b) is important later, but not the acute treatment goal.

6.
When caring for a client with emphysema, the oxygen flow rate should not exceed:-
a) 1 L/min.
b) 6 L/min.
c) 10 L/min.
d) 3 L/min.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) 1 L/min. (Typically 1-2 L/min).

Explanation: Patients with chronic CO2 retention (like in emphysema) rely on the "hypoxic drive" to breathe. Administering high-flow oxygen can remove this drive, causing hypoventilation and CO2 narcosis. Low flow (1-2 L/min) is the safety standard.

7.
Modified risk factors for cardiovascular disease include:-
a) Use of OCPs, gender.
b) Hyperlipidemia, hyperglycemia.
c) Obesity, family history.
d) Age, family history.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Hyperlipidemia, hyperglycemia.

Explanation: Modifiable risk factors are those that can be changed or treated.

  • Modifiable: High lipids (Diet/Meds), High blood sugar/Diabetes (Lifestyle/Meds), Obesity, Smoking.
  • Non-modifiable: Age, Gender, Family History/Genetics.
Option B contains two modifiable factors. Options A, C, and D contain non-modifiable factors.

8.
The correct landmark for obtaining apical pulse is left:-
a) Intercostal space, mid-axillary line.
b) Fifth intercostal space, midclavicular line.
c) Second intercostal space, mid calvicular line.
d) Fifth intercostal space, mid axillary line.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Fifth intercostal space, midclavicular line.

Explanation: This is the Point of Maximal Impulse (PMI) or the apex of the heart, where the apical pulse is heard loudest.

9.
A positive Chvostek's sign is a manifestation of:-
a) Hypocalcemia.
b) Hypercalcemia.
c) Hypokalemia.
d) Hypermagnesemia.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Hypocalcemia.

Explanation: Chvostek's sign is facial muscle twitching elicited by tapping the facial nerve anterior to the ear. It indicates neuromuscular irritability caused by low calcium levels (Hypocalcemia) or low magnesium (Hypomagnesemia).

10.
The appropriate medication to reverse hypertensive crisis caused by pheochromocytoma is:-
a) Mannitol (osmitrol).
b) Methyldopa (Aldomet).
c) Phentolamine (Regitine).
d) Felodopine (Plendil).
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Phentolamine (Regitine).

Explanation: Pheochromocytoma is a tumor releasing excessive catecholamines (epinephrine/norepinephrine). Phentolamine is a rapid-acting alpha-adrenergic blocker used specifically to block these receptors and lower blood pressure during a hypertensive crisis in this condition.

11.
A technique used to assess a patient's peripheral response to pain is:-
a) Sternal rub.
b) Nail bed pressure.
c) Pressure on the orbital rim.
d) Pressure on the sternocleidomastoid muscle.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Nail bed pressure.

Explanation:

  • Peripheral Pain Stimulus: Nail bed pressure (assesses spinal reflex arc or peripheral sensation).
  • Central Pain Stimulus: Sternal rub, Orbital rim pressure, Trapezius squeeze (assesses brain function).

12.
Nursing intervention arrived at preventing automatic dysreflexia include:-
a) Frequent monitoring of GCS.
b) Preventing bladder distention.
c) Increasing skin simulation.
d) Administering mannitol as ordered.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Preventing bladder distention.

Explanation: Autonomic dysreflexia (in spinal cord injuries above T6) is triggered by noxious stimuli below the level of injury. The most common trigger is a distended bladder (blocked catheter), followed by constipation/impaction.

13.
The pathogenesis of compartment syndrome involves:-
a) Injury to the nerve supply in the affected area.
b) Fascia expanding in the affected area.
c) Bleeding and edema increasing pressure in the affected area.
d) Impaired perfusion in the affected area due to arterial damage.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Bleeding and edema increasing pressure in the affected area.

Explanation: Compartment syndrome occurs when pressure within a confined anatomical space (compartment) increases due to bleeding or swelling (edema). Since the fascia (b) cannot expand, this increased pressure compresses blood vessels and nerves, leading to ischemia.

14.
A laboratory diagnosis that primarily indicates pancreatitis is:-
a) Increased lactate dehydrogenase.
b) Elevated blood urea nitrogen.
c) Elevated aspartate aminotransferase.
d) Elevated serum lipase.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Elevated serum lipase.

Explanation: Both Amylase and Lipase elevate in pancreatitis, but Lipase is more specific to the pancreas and remains elevated longer. LDH and AST are non-specific liver/tissue markers.

15.
A contra-indication to ear irrigation is:-
a) Ear pain.
b) Hearing loss.
c) Otitis external.
d) Perforated tympanic membrane.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Perforated tympanic membrane.

Explanation: Irrigating an ear with a perforated drum can wash bacteria into the middle ear, causing severe infection (otitis media) or damage to the ossicles. Vegetable matter is also a contraindication (it swells).

16.
The appropriate fluid to administer during emergency fluid resuscitation in burns is:-
a) Colloids.
b) Crystalloids.
c) Fresh frozen plasma.
d) Packed red blood cells.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Crystalloids. (Specifically Ringer's Lactate).

Explanation: Isotonic crystalloids, particularly Ringer's Lactate, are the standard for initial burn resuscitation (Parkland formula) to replace the massive loss of fluid and electrolytes into the tissues. Colloids are avoided in the first 24 hours due to capillary leak.

17.
The first action to take when you find an unconscious victim is:-
a) Check for responsiveness.
b) Ensure scene safety.
c) Begin chest compressions.
d) Open the airway.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Ensure scene safety. (However, options are often "Check responsiveness" in BLS context after safety is implied).

Explanation: In standard BLS algorithms: 1. Scene Safety (Check for danger). 2. Check Responsiveness (Shake and shout). 3. Call for help. 4. Check pulse/breathing. Based on the specific list provided, if safety is already assumed or if focusing on the *victim*, checking responsiveness (a) is the first direct interaction.

18.
The appropriate drug to administer in acute narrow angle glaucoma is:-
a) Acetazolamide.
b) Atropine.
c) Furosemide.
d) Urokinase.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Acetazolamide.

Explanation: Acetazolamide (Diamox) is a carbonic anhydrase inhibitor that rapidly decreases the production of aqueous humor, lowering intraocular pressure. Miotics like Pilocarpine are also used.
Warning: Atropine (b) dilates the pupil and would worsen narrow-angle glaucoma.

19.
The appropriate health message for a client treated to chronic renal failure is:-
a) "Eat meat protein every day".
b) "Eat plenty of bananas".
c) "Increase carbohydrate intake".
d) "Increase fluid and salt in diet".
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) "Increase carbohydrate intake".

Explanation:

  • Carbohydrates: Should be increased to provide energy and spare protein (preventing muscle breakdown which increases nitrogenous waste).
  • Protein: Often restricted to reduce urea build-up.
  • Potassium (Bananas): Restricted because kidneys cannot excrete K+.
  • Fluid/Salt: Restricted to prevent overload/hypertension.

20.
A diagnostic test for hemophilia is:-
a) Bleeding time.
b) Tourniquet test.
c) Clot retraction test.
d) Partial Thromboplastin time (PTT) test.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Partial Thromboplastin time (PTT) test.

Explanation: Hemophilia A (Factor VIII deficiency) and Hemophilia B (Factor IX deficiency) affect the intrinsic clotting pathway. This prolongs the aPTT (or PTT). Bleeding time measures platelet function, which is typically normal in hemophilia.

Part II: Short Answer Questions

40 Marks
1.
Mr. T is on carbamazepine:-
  1. State the mechanism of action of carbamazepine. (2 marks)
  2. State two (2) indications of carbamazepine. (2 marks)
  3. List two (2) effects of carbamazepine. (1 mark)
Show Answer

a) Mechanism of Action:

Carbamazepine blocks voltage-gated sodium channels in neuronal membranes, stabilizing hyperexcited nerve membranes and inhibiting repetitive neuronal firing (limiting seizure spread).

b) Indications:

  1. Epilepsy: Partial seizures and generalized tonic-clonic seizures.
  2. Trigeminal Neuralgia: First-line treatment for pain relief.
  3. Bipolar Disorder (Mood stabilization).

c) Side Effects (Adverse Effects):

  1. CNS effects: Dizziness, drowsiness, ataxia.
  2. Hematological: Aplastic anemia, agranulocytosis (requires blood monitoring).
  3. Dermatological: Skin rash (Stevens-Johnson syndrome).
2.
Describe the specific nursing care for a client with osteomyelitis. (5 marks)
Show Answer
  1. Immobilization: Handle the affected limb gently and keep it immobilized/splinted to reduce pain and prevent pathological fracture.
  2. Medication Administration: Administer high-dose IV antibiotics strictly as prescribed (long-term therapy) to penetrate bone tissue.
  3. Pain Management: Administer analgesics and use pillows for support/elevation.
  4. Wound Care: Perform sterile dressing changes if there is a draining sinus or post-surgical wound (sequestrectomy).
  5. Diet/Nutrition: Provide a high-protein, high-vitamin diet to promote bone healing and tissue repair.
3.
Outline six (6) specific interventions for a client with increased intracranial pressure. (6 marks)
Show Answer
  1. Positioning: Elevate the head of the bed to 30-45 degrees (neutral position) to promote venous drainage.
  2. Oxygenation: Maintain patent airway and ensure adequate oxygenation (prevent hypoxia/hypercapnia which dilates cerebral vessels).
  3. Avoid Straining: Prevent coughing, vomiting, or straining at stool (Valsalva maneuver) which spikes ICP. Administer stool softeners/antiemetics.
  4. Fluid Management: Administer osmotic diuretics (Mannitol) or hypertonic saline as prescribed to draw fluid out of the brain. restrict fluids if indicated.
  5. Thermoregulation: Prevent hyperthermia (fever increases metabolic demand) using antipyretics or cooling blankets.
  6. Minimal Stimulation: Space out nursing activities and maintain a quiet, dark environment to reduce stress.
4.
Describe the pathophysiology of acne vulgaris. (5 marks)
Show Answer

Acne vulgaris involves four key factors:

  1. Androgen Stimulation: Increased production of androgens (during puberty) stimulates sebaceous glands.
  2. Sebum Overproduction: Sebaceous glands enlarge and produce excessive sebum (oil).
  3. Follicular Hyperkeratosis: Dead skin cells stick together and clog the pore (follicle), forming a plug (comedone).
  4. Bacterial Proliferation: Propionibacterium acnes (P. acnes) bacteria, which naturally live on skin, multiply in the clogged pore.
  5. Inflammation: The bacteria induce an inflammatory response, leading to red papules, pustules, or cysts.
5.
Outline five (5) specific nursing care for a client with pulmonary edema. (5 marks)
Show Answer
  1. Positioning: Place patient in High Fowler's position (upright) with legs dangling to pool blood in periphery and ease breathing.
  2. Oxygen Therapy: Administer high-flow oxygen (or assist with CPAP/BiPAP) to correct hypoxemia.
  3. Medication: Administer diuretics (Furosemide) IV to remove fluid, and vasodilators (Nitroglycerin) or Morphine to reduce preload/anxiety.
  4. Monitoring: Continuous monitoring of SpO2, heart rate, and strict Intake/Output recording.
  5. Reassurance: Stay with the patient to reduce extreme anxiety (air hunger is terrifying), which reduces oxygen demand.
6.
State five (5) clinical manifestations of dumping syndrome. (5 marks)
Show Answer

Symptoms typically occur 15-30 minutes after eating:

  1. Generalized Weakness/Faintness.
  2. Dizziness/Vertigo.
  3. Diaphoresis (Sweating).
  4. Palpitations/Tachycardia.
  5. Abdominal Cramping/Pain.
  6. Explosive Diarrhea.
7.
Outline four (4) health messages you will share with a client discharged after treatment of renal stones. (4 marks)
Show Answer
  1. Hydration: Drink plenty of fluids (3-4 liters/day) to produce at least 2L of urine daily and flush out crystals.
  2. Dietary Modification: Depending on stone type (e.g., limit sodium and animal protein; limit oxalates like spinach/nuts for calcium oxalate stones).
  3. Activity: Maintain an active lifestyle to prevent urinary stasis and bone calcium loss.
  4. Medication Compliance: Take prescribed medications (e.g., Allopurinol) to prevent recurrence.
  5. Monitoring: Report signs of UTI or recurrent pain immediately.
8.
Mr. K is diagnosed with arrhythmia:-
  1. List any four (4) types of arrhythmia. (2 marks)
  2. State three (3) nursing interventions for Mr. K. (3 marks)
Show Answer

a) Types of Arrhythmia:

  1. Atrial Fibrillation (AF).
  2. Ventricular Tachycardia (VT).
  3. Ventricular Fibrillation (VF).
  4. Sinus Bradycardia.
  5. Premature Ventricular Contractions (PVCs).

b) Nursing Interventions:

  1. Monitoring: Continuously monitor cardiac rhythm (ECG) and vital signs (Apical pulse).
  2. Medication: Administer anti-arrhythmic drugs (e.g., Amiodarone, Beta-blockers, Digoxin) as prescribed.
  3. Safety: Maintain a calm environment to reduce stress; keep emergency equipment (defibrillator/crash cart) nearby.

Part III: Essay/Long Answer Questions

40 Marks
1.

Mr. X is admitted for total hip replacement.

a) Draw a diagram to illustrate a synovial joint. (5 marks)

b) Describe the specific pre-operative care for Mr. X. (4 marks)

c) Describe the post-operative care for Mr. X. (11 marks)

Show Answer

a) Diagram of Synovial Joint:

Your diagram should clearly label:

  • Articular Cartilage: Covering the ends of the two bones.
  • Synovial Membrane: Lining the inner capsule.
  • Synovial Fluid: Filling the joint cavity.
  • Joint Capsule (Fibrous capsule): Enclosing the joint.
  • Bones: The two articulating bones.
  • Ligaments: Connecting bone to bone (optional but good).

b) Pre-operative Care:

  1. Education: Teach post-op exercises (quadriceps setting, gluteal setting), use of trapeze bar, and deep breathing.
  2. Infection Control: Ensure no septic foci (dental, UTI, skin). Administer prophylactic antibiotics. Skin preparation.
  3. Labs/Blood: Ensure cross-match for blood is done (high bleeding risk).
  4. Consent: Verify informed consent is signed.

c) Post-operative Care:

  1. Positioning (Critical): Maintain abduction of the legs using a wedge pillow to prevent dislocation of the prosthesis. Do not cross legs. Do not flex hip >90 degrees.
  2. Neurovascular Checks: Monitor the operated leg for color, warmth, sensation, movement, and pedal pulses to detect nerve damage or ischemia.
  3. Wound/Drain Care: Monitor Hemovac drain output (report excessive bleeding >250ml in first 8h). Change dressings aseptically.
  4. DVT Prophylaxis: Administer anticoagulants (LMWH), apply anti-embolism stockings, and encourage foot exercises.
  5. Pain Control: Administer analgesics (PCA or epidural) to facilitate movement.
  6. Mobilization: Early ambulation (often day 1) with walker/crutches. Weight bearing as per surgeon's order (cemented vs non-cemented).
2.

Mr. P, 45 years old is admitted with a diagnosis of hyperosmolar, Non-Ketotic Syndrome (HNKS).

a) State two (2) differences between DKA and HNKS. (2 marks)

b) Describe the pathophysiology of HNKS. (6 marks)

c) Describe the management of Mr. P until discharge. (12 marks)

Show Answer

a) Differences between DKA and HNKS:

Feature DKA (Diabetic Ketoacidosis) HNKS (Hyperosmolar Hyperglycemic State)
Ketones Present in urine/blood (Ketosis). Absent or minimal (No ketosis).
pH Acidotic (< 7.35). Normal (> 7.3).
Blood Glucose High (> 250 mg/dL). Very High (> 600 mg/dL).
Patient Type Usually Type 1 Diabetes. Usually Type 2 Diabetes.

b) Pathophysiology of HNKS:

  1. Relative Insulin Deficiency: There is enough insulin to prevent the breakdown of fats (lipolysis) and ketone formation, but not enough to prevent hyperglycemia.
  2. Extreme Hyperglycemia: Blood glucose levels rise dramatically (often >600 mg/dL).
  3. Osmotic Diuresis: High glucose causes kidneys to excrete massive amounts of water and electrolytes to clear the sugar.
  4. Severe Dehydration: This leads to profound fluid volume deficit (often greater than in DKA) and hyperosmolarity of the blood.
  5. Intracellular Dehydration: Fluid shifts from cells into the blood, causing cellular dehydration, particularly in the brain (leading to altered mental status/coma).

c) Management:

  1. Fluid Resuscitation (Priority):
    • Rapid IV rehydration with Isotonic Saline (0.9% NaCl) to restore vascular volume and BP.
    • Once stable, may switch to 0.45% saline to treat cellular dehydration.
  2. Insulin Therapy:
    • Administer regular insulin IV (bolus then infusion) to lower blood glucose slowly.
    • Monitor glucose hourly. Decrease rate as glucose drops to prevent cerebral edema.
  3. Electrolyte Replacement:
    • Monitor Potassium (K+) closely. Insulin drives K+ into cells, causing hypokalemia. Replace K+ IV as needed to prevent arrhythmias.
  4. Monitoring:
    • Strict Intake/Output chart. Monitor vital signs and neurological status (GCS) frequently.
  5. Treat Underlying Cause:
    • Administer antibiotics if infection (pneumonia/UTI) triggered the event.
  6. Education (Discharge):
    • Teach signs of hyperglycemia, importance of hydration (sick day rules), and medication compliance.
