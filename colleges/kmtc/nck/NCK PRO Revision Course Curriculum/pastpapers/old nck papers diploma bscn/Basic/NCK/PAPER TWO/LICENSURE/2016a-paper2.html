Part I: Multiple Choice Questions

20 Marks
1.
The deep muscles of the pelvis floor include:-
a) Pubococcygeus, bulbo-carvenosus.
b) Iliococcygeus, ischiocavernosus.
c) Ischiococcygeus, pubococcygeus.
d) Pubococcygeus, ischiocavernosus.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Ischiococcygeus, pubococcygeus.

Explanation: The deep muscle layer (pelvic diaphragm) consists mainly of the Levator Ani muscles (Pubococcygeus, Iliococcygeus) and the Coccygeus (Ischiococcygeus). Bulbo-cavernosus and Ischiocavernosus are superficial muscles.

2.
The hormone which is responsible for the growth of the myometrium during pregnancy is:-
a) Progesterone.
b) Follicle stimulating hormone.
c) Oestrogen.
d) Luteinizing hormone.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Oestrogen.

Explanation: Oestrogen causes hypertrophy (increase in size) and hyperplasia (increase in number) of the myometrial muscle fibers, causing the uterus to grow. Progesterone relaxes the smooth muscle (quiescence).

3.
Minor disorders of pregnancy include:-
a) Nausea and vomiting, heartburn, ptyalism, backache.
b) Nausea and vomiting, heart burn, ptyalism, hyperemesis gravidarum.
c) Nausea and vomiting, heart burn, backache, vaginal bleeding.
d) Heart burn, ptyalism, backache, reduced foetal movements.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Nausea and vomiting, heartburn, ptyalism, backache.

Explanation: These are all common, physiological "minor" disorders. Hyperemesis gravidarum (in b), vaginal bleeding (in c), and reduced fetal movements (in d) are major complications or danger signs requiring medical intervention.

4.
The specific aims of performing an abdominal examination on a pregnant woman include:-
a) To observe signs of pregnancy, assess fetal size and growth, assess fetal health.
b) To observe signs of pregnancy, assess fetal size and growth, assess maternal well being.
c) To diagnose the location of fetal parts, assess fetal health, assess maternal well being.
d) To diagnose the location of fetal parts, assess fetal size and growth, assess maternal pulse rate.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) To observe signs of pregnancy, assess fetal size and growth, assess fetal health.

Explanation: The key objectives are assessing fetal size/growth (fundal height), fetal lie/presentation/position (location of parts), and fetal well-being (Fetal Heart Rate). Maternal well-being is usually assessed via BP/Urine, not primarily abdominal palpation, though skin changes are observed.

5.
The commonest cause of pyelonephritis in pregnancy is:-
a) Streptococcus aureus.
b) Streptococcus faecals.
c) Haemophilus influenza.
d) Escherichia coli.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Escherichia coli.

Explanation: E. coli is responsible for 70-80% of urinary tract infections and pyelonephritis in pregnancy, due to the anatomical proximity of the urethra to the rectum.

6.
During labour, the level of the presenting part is estimated in relation to the maternal ischial spines in order to:-
a) Assess the state of the cervix.
b) Assess the presenting diameter.
c) Assess the descent of the fetus in labour.
d) Assess the descent of the head.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Assess the descent of the fetus in labour.

Explanation: Station is the relationship of the presenting part to the ischial spines, measured in centimeters (-5 to +5). It is the standard method for measuring fetal descent.

7.
At the beginning of third stage of labour, the fundal height is:-
a) 2 - 5 below the umbilicus and 10 cm above the symphysis pubis.
b) 2 - 5 cm below the umbilicus and 15 cm above the symphysis pubis.
c) 1.5 cm above the umbilicus and 19 cm above the symphysis pubis.
d) 1.5 cm below the umbilicus and 19 cm above the symphysis pubis.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) 1.5 cm below the umbilicus and 19 cm above the symphysis pubis. (Or option A/B depending on specific text, but standard is slightly below umbilicus).

Explanation: Immediately after the birth of the baby (beginning of 3rd stage), the uterus contracts down to a level roughly midway between the symphysis pubis and umbilicus, or just below the umbilicus (approx 20 weeks size). 19-20cm above symphysis is roughly umbilical level.

8.
The third degree perineal tear involves:-
a) The fourchette and superficial perineal muscles.
b) The fourchette and the anal sphincter.
c) The fourchette, superficial perineal muscles and the anal sphincter.
d) The fourchette and the rectal mucosa.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) The fourchette, superficial perineal muscles and the anal sphincter.

Explanation:
- 1st degree: Fourchette, vaginal mucosa, skin.
- 2nd degree: Plus perineal muscles.
- 3rd degree: Plus anal sphincter complex (EAS/IAS).
- 4th degree: Plus rectal mucosa.

9.
The immediate action a midwife takes on diagnosing cord presentation is to:-
a) Allow the woman to continue in labour.
b) Put the woman in knee chest position.
c) Assess for cord pulsation.
d) Rupture the membranes.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Put the woman in knee chest position.

Explanation: Cord presentation (cord below presenting part with membranes intact) carries a high risk of cord prolapse if membranes rupture. The priority is to relieve pressure on the cord by adopting the knee-chest or Trendelenburg position and preparing for CS. DO NOT rupture membranes (d) as this causes prolapse.

10.
The indications for emergency caesarean section include:-
a) Cord prolapse, uterine rupture.
b) Eclampsia, diabetes.
c) Fetal distress, antepartum haemorrhage.
d) Cephalopelvic disproportion, fetal distress.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Cord prolapse, uterine rupture. (Also c and d are strong candidates, but rupture/prolapse are absolute immediate emergencies).

Explanation: Cord prolapse and uterine rupture are catastrophic events requiring immediate delivery to save life. While fetal distress (c, d) is an indication, CPD (d) is often diagnosed earlier or allows a short trial. Diabetes (b) is usually managed, not an immediate emergency indication unless complications arise.

11.
Indicate whether the statements are True or False.
a) A normal full term baby weighs approximately 3.5kg, length of 50cm and a head circumference of 34 - 35cm.
b) A newborn whose birth weight is below 2.5kgs is premature baby.
Show Answer

Answers:

  • a) True: These are the standard anthropometric measurements for a healthy term infant.
  • b) False: A baby < 2.5kg is defined as Low Birth Weight (LBW). They may be premature OR small for gestational age (SGA). Prematurity is defined by gestation (< 37 weeks), not weight.
12.
The management of physiological jaundice is a term baby include:-
a) Early feeding and exposure to sunlight.
b) Early and frequent feeding.
c) Intravenous fluids and breastfeeding.
d) Exposure to sunlight and breastfeeding.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Early feeding and exposure to sunlight.

Explanation: Physiological jaundice usually resolves on its own. Frequent feeding promotes bowel movements (excreting bilirubin). Sunlight (heliotherapy) was traditionally used, though modern guidelines caution against direct sun due to burn risk, it is still a common answer in older exams. Frequent feeding is the most critical component.

13.
A baby whose weight is 1450 gms should be fed via:-
a) Cup.
b) Breast.
c) Cup and spoon.
d) Nasogastric tube.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Nasogastric tube.

Explanation: Infants < 1500g (VLBW) or < 32-34 weeks gestation usually lack the coordinated suck-swallow-breathe reflex required for breast or cup feeding. NGT feeding is the standard method until this reflex matures.

14.
The causes of intra-uterine growth retardation include:-
a) Diabetes mellitus, multiple gestation.
b) Pre-eclampsia, pneumonia.
c) Intra-uterine infection, skin conditions.
d) Chromosomal abnormality, rhesus incompatibility.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Pre-eclampsia, pneumonia. (Pre-eclampsia is a major cause. Multiple gestation (a) and Chromosomal (d) are also causes, but Diabetes usually causes macrosomia unless vascular). Option B is a strong mix of chronic/acute stress.

Analysis: Actually, Pre-eclampsia causes IUGR. Multiple gestation causes IUGR. Chromosomal abnormalities cause IUGR. Diabetes usually causes Large for Dates (Macrosomia), but long-standing diabetes with vasculopathy causes IUGR.
However, Multiple gestation (a) and Chromosomal abnormality (d) are very common causes.
Let's look at the grouping. Pre-eclampsia is the most common maternal cause.
In exams, Pre-eclampsia is the classic cause. Pneumonia (chronic/severe) causes hypoxia -> IUGR.
Let's accept B or D.

15.
In normal puerperium the term given to the vaginal discharge during the next 5 - 9 days is:-
a) Lochia rubra.
b) Lochia alba.
c) Lochia serosa.
d) Lochia albicans.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Lochia serosa.

Explanation:
- Lochia Rubra (Red): Days 1-4.
- Lochia Serosa (Pink/Brown): Days 5-9.
- Lochia Alba (White/Yellow): Days 10-14 (up to 28).

16.
The common causative organisms of puerperal infection include:-
a) Clostridium Welchii, haemolytic streptococcus.
b) Clostridium Welchii, phylococcus aureus.
c) Staphylococcus aureus, haemophylococus influenza.
d) Phylococcus aureus, haemolytic streptococcus.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Clostridium Welchii, haemolytic streptococcus.

Explanation: Group A Beta-Hemolytic Streptococcus (Streptococcus pyogenes) is the most historically significant and dangerous cause. E. coli, Staph aureus, and anaerobes are also common.

17.
The role of the midwife in the management of deep venous thrombosis include:-
a) Administering appropriate anticoagulant, mobilizing the affected limb.
b) Administering appropriate anticoagulant, restricting mobilization of affected limb.
c) Administering antibiotics, restricting mobilization of affected limb.
d) Administering analgesics, and antibiotics.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Administering appropriate anticoagulant, restricting mobilization of affected limb.

Explanation: Anticoagulation (Heparin) is the primary treatment. In the acute phase, bed rest/restricting mobilization was traditionally recommended to prevent embolization (though modern guidelines encourage early mobilization with compression, in exam contexts "rest" is often the answer to prevent clot dislodgement). Antibiotics are not routine unless septic thrombophlebitis is present.

18.
A postnatal mother who does not breastfeed and seeks to use contraception immediately should be advised to use:-
a) Progesterone only injectable, lactational amenorrhoea.
b) Progesterone only oral pill, diaphragm.
c) Combined oral pill, implant.
d) Progesterone only oral pill, intrauterine copper device.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Combined oral pill, implant.

Explanation: Since she is not breastfeeding, estrogen-containing methods (Combined Oral Contraceptive Pill) are not contraindicated (after 3 weeks usually due to VTE risk, but "immediately" usually implies upon discharge/early). Progesterone methods (Implant) are also safe. Lactational amenorrhea (a) is impossible as she isn't breastfeeding. IUCD (d) usually requires waiting 4-6 weeks unless inserted within 48 hours.

19.
The main causes of perinatal deaths include:-
a) Low birth weight, smoking.
b) Intrauterine hypoxia, intracranial injury.
c) Congenital abnormality, smoking.
d) Respiratory depression at birth, pre-eclampsia.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Intrauterine hypoxia, intracranial injury. (Usually listed as Asphyxia, Prematurity, Infection, Birth Trauma).

Explanation: Intrauterine hypoxia (Asphyxia) and birth trauma (intracranial injury) are major direct causes of perinatal mortality.

20.
The signs and symptoms of puerperal psychosis include:-
a) Mother refuses to feed, loses interest on baby.
b) Auditory hallucinations, feeds well.
c) Mother develops suicidal tendencies, likes her baby.
d) Mother develops suicidal tendencies, feeds well.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Mother refuses to feed, loses interest on baby.

Explanation: Puerperal psychosis is severe and involves losing touch with reality. Symptoms include hallucinations, delusions, confusion, severe mood swings, and often rejection of the infant or fear of harming the infant/themselves. "Loses interest/refuses to feed" is a behavioral manifestation of this severe detachment.

2.
State three (3) functions of the pelvic floor muscles. (3 marks)
Show Answer

Functions:

  1. Support: Support the pelvic organs (uterus, bladder, rectum) and abdominal contents against gravity and intra-abdominal pressure.
  2. Control: Maintain continence of urine and faeces by reinforcing the sphincters of the urethra and rectum.
  3. Sexual/Reproductive: Allow for distension during childbirth (and recoil afterwards) and contribute to sexual sensation.
3.
State five (5) aims of antenatal care. (5 marks)
Show Answer

Aims of ANC:

  1. To monitor the progress of pregnancy and fetal well-being.
  2. To detect and manage complications early (e.g., pre-eclampsia, anemia).
  3. To prepare the mother physically and psychologically for labor, birth, and parenthood.
  4. To provide health education and counseling (nutrition, hygiene, danger signs).
  5. To formulate a birth preparedness and complication readiness plan.
4.
Outline five (5) functions of a mature placenta. (5 marks)
Show Answer

Functions of Placenta:

  1. Respiration: Gas exchange (O2 uptake and CO2 excretion) between maternal and fetal blood.
  2. Nutrition: Transfer of nutrients (glucose, amino acids, vitamins) to the fetus.
  3. Excretion: Removal of fetal waste products (urea, creatinine).
  4. Endocrine: Production of hormones (HCG, HPL, Estrogen, Progesterone) to maintain pregnancy.
  5. Protection/Barrier: Filters some bacteria and allows transfer of IgG antibodies (passive immunity).
5.
Explain four (4) signs of obstructed labour. (8 marks)
Show Answer

Signs of Obstructed Labour:

  1. Bandl's Ring (Pathological Retraction Ring): A visible depression across the abdomen marking the junction between the thinned lower segment and the thick, retracted upper segment.
  2. Maternal Distress: The mother looks exhausted, anxious, may be dehydrated with a rapid pulse (>100 bpm) and elevated temperature, and may have ketones in urine.
  3. Edematous Vulva/Vagina: Due to prolonged pressure and venous congestion.
  4. Fetal Distress: Fetal heart rate abnormalities (tachycardia, bradycardia, or decelerations) and meconium-stained liquor due to prolonged compression and hypoxia.
  5. Severe Caput Succedaneum/Moulding: On vaginal exam, a large caput and excessive moulding (overlap of skull bones) indicate the head is being forced against resistance.
6.
Outline five (5) roles of the midwife in the immediate care of the newborn. (5 marks)
Show Answer

Roles of Midwife:

  1. Maintain Airway/Resuscitation: Dry the baby to stimulate breathing; suction if meconium is present and baby is not vigorous.
  2. Thermoregulation: Keep the baby warm (dry immediately, skin-to-skin contact, cover head).
  3. Apgar Scoring: Assess the baby's condition at 1 and 5 minutes.
  4. Cord Care: Clamp and cut the cord using sterile technique.
  5. Identification & Security: Place ID bands on the baby immediately.
  6. Initiate Breastfeeding: Help mother initiate breastfeeding within the first hour.
7.
List four (4) possible sources of infection of the umbilical cord. (2 marks)
Show Answer

Sources of Infection:

  1. Unsterile cutting instruments (razor, scissors).
  2. Unclean hands of the birth attendant.
  3. Unsterile cord ties or clamps.
  4. Application of harmful substances (cow dung, mud, ash) to the stump.
  5. Soiled nappies/diapers covering the stump.
8.
Explain three (3) benefits of PMTCT as per the National Guidelines. (6 marks)
Show Answer

Benefits of PMTCT (Prevention of Mother-to-Child Transmission):

  1. Reduction of Infant HIV Infection: Use of ARVs significantly lowers the risk of transmitting HIV from mother to baby during pregnancy, birth, and breastfeeding.
  2. Improved Maternal Health: Early identification of HIV+ mothers allows for early initiation of ART, improving their own health and survival.
  3. Family Health/Spousal Testing: Encourages partner testing and disclosure, allowing the family to access care and prevention services together.

Part III: Essay/Long Answer Questions

1.
Mrs. J, a para 2 + 0 gravida 3 is admitted in the labour ward at 38 weeks gestation in active phase of labour.

a) Differentiate between true and false labour. (6 marks)

Show Answer
Feature True Labour False Labour
Contractions Regular, increase in frequency, duration, and intensity. Irregular, erratic, do not increase in intensity.
Cervix Progressive effacement and dilatation. No significant change in cervix.
Pain Location Starts in back and radiates to abdomen. Usually confined to lower abdomen/groin.
Effect of Sedation/Walking Contractions continue/intensify with walking; not stopped by sedation. Relieved by walking or sedation/rest.
Show Usually present (blood-stained mucus). Usually absent.

b) Describe the physiological changes that take place during first stage of labour. (12 marks)

Show Answer

Physiological Changes:

  1. Uterine Contractions:
    • Fundal Dominance: Contractions start at the fundus and spread downwards, being strongest and longest at the fundus.
    • Polarity: Neuromuscular harmony where the upper pole contracts and retracts (shortens) while the lower pole contracts slightly and dilates (lengthens).
    • Retraction: Muscle fibers of the upper segment shorten permanently after contraction, reducing uterine cavity size and aiding descent.
  2. Formation of Upper and Lower Uterine Segments:
    • The uterus divides into an active upper segment (thick, muscular) and a passive lower segment (thin, stretched).
    • The junction between them is the Physiological Retraction Ring.
  3. Cervical Changes:
    • Effacement: Taking up of the cervical canal into the lower uterine segment (thinning).
    • Dilatation: Opening of the cervical os from 0 to 10cm, caused by the pressure of the presenting part/forewaters and uterine action.
  4. Formation of Forewaters: As the lower segment stretches, the chorion detaches, creating a bag of waters in front of the head (forewaters) which aids dilatation.

c) List four (4) complications of labour. (2 marks)

Show Answer

Complications:

  1. Obstructed labour.
  2. Fetal distress / Birth asphyxia.
  3. Postpartum hemorrhage (PPH).
  4. Cord prolapse.
  5. Uterine rupture.
  6. Perineal tears (3rd/4th degree).
2.
Mrs. X, para 2 + 0 gravida 3 comes to the labour ward at 34 weeks gestation in active labour and progresses well giving birth to a live infant weighing 1400 gms.

a) Define low birth weight. (1 mark)

Show Answer

Low Birth Weight (LBW):

A birth weight of less than 2,500 grams (2.5 kg), regardless of gestational age.

b) Differentiate between a preterm and small for dates baby. (6 marks)

Show Answer

Preterm Baby:

  • Born before 37 completed weeks of gestation.
  • Weight is appropriate for gestational age (usually).
  • Physical signs of immaturity present (lanugo, abundant vernix, lack of ear cartilage, empty scrotum/labia minora prominent).
  • Problems relate to organ immaturity (RDS, jaundice).

Small for Dates (SFD) / Small for Gestational Age (SGA):

  • Can be term or preterm, but weight is below the 10th percentile for that gestation.
  • Caused by intrauterine growth restriction (IUGR).
  • Look "starved" - loose skin, lack of subcutaneous fat, alert/anxious look.
  • Problems relate to malnutrition (hypoglycemia, hypothermia).

c) Describe the specific management of baby X for the first 72 hours. (11 marks)

Show Answer

Management of Baby X (1400g - VLBW):

  1. Admission to NBU: Admit to Newborn Unit/Incubator for specialized care.
  2. Thermoregulation:
    • Nurse in incubator to maintain neutral thermal environment.
    • Monitor temperature every 2-4 hours.
    • Use Kangaroo Mother Care (KMC) once stable.
  3. Respiratory Support:
    • Monitor for signs of Respiratory Distress Syndrome (RDS).
    • Administer oxygen if needed (monitor saturation).
  4. Nutrition/Fluids:
    • Start IV fluids (10% Dextrose) initially if suck/swallow poor or respiratory distress present.
    • Commence Nasogastric Tube (NGT) feeding with expressed breast milk (EBM) as 1400g is too small to suckle effectively.
    • Feed small amounts frequently (every 2-3 hours) to prevent hypoglycemia.
  5. Infection Prevention:
    • Strict hand hygiene.
    • Cord care with chlorhexidine.
    • prophylactic antibiotics if indicated by protocol.
  6. Monitoring:
    • Monitor blood glucose (risk of hypoglycemia).
    • Monitor bilirubin (risk of jaundice).
    • Monitor weight daily.
  7. Vitamin K: Administer Vitamin K (0.5mg IM) to prevent hemorrhagic disease.

d) List four (4) complications of preterm babies. (2 marks)

Show Answer

Complications:

  1. Respiratory Distress Syndrome (RDS) due to surfactant deficiency.
  2. Hypothermia due to large surface area and lack of fat.
  3. Hypoglycemia due to low glycogen stores.
  4. Neonatal Jaundice (Hyperbilirubinemia) due to liver immaturity.
  5. Infection (Sepsis) due to immune immaturity.
  6. Intraventricular Hemorrhage (IVH).
  7. Necrotizing Enterocolitis (NEC).
