Part I: Multiple Choice Questions

20 Marks
1.
The following are the membranes that cover the brain from inside out:-
a) Dura, arachnoid, pia.
b) Dura, pia, arachnoid.
c) Pia, arachnoid, dura.
d) Pia, dura, arachnoid.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Pia, arachnoid, dura.

Explanation: The meninges are arranged from the brain surface outward as: 1. Pia mater (innermost, adheres to brain). 2. Arachnoid mater (middle, web-like). 3. Dura mater (outermost, tough/fibrous).
Therefore, "inside out" is Pia -> Arachnoid -> Dura.

2.
Food poisoning is often caused by :-
a) Dysentery bacilli.
b) Salmonella bacilli.
c) Coliform bacilli.
d) Influenza bacilli.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Salmonella bacilli.

Explanation: Salmonella species are classic causes of bacterial food poisoning (Salmonellosis), often associated with contaminated poultry, eggs, and dairy. Dysentery (Shigella) is also food/water-borne but typically classified specifically as dysentery.

3.
The following observations are noted on the patient on underwater seal drainage:-
a) Vital signs, patient connections, colour of draining.
b) Vital signs, colour of drainage, amount of drainage.
c) Vital signs, patient understanding of the procedure, amount of drainage.
d) Vital signs, patient understanding of the procedure, cyanosis.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Vital signs, patient connections, colour of draining.

Explanation: Priority observations for Underwater Seal Drainage (UWSD) include: 1. Patient Status: Vital signs and respiratory effort. 2. System Integrity: Checking patient connections to ensure an airtight seal (critical to prevent tension pneumothorax). 3. Drainage: Color and amount. While Option B includes "amount", Option A includes "connections", which is a fundamental safety check for the device itself. In exam contexts, system integrity is often prioritized.

4.
Fractures of the base of the skull will manifest with:-
a) Hemorrhage from the nose, the pharynx or the ears.
b) Cerebrospinal fluid leakage from the ears.
c) Bloody spinal fluid.
d) Persistent localized pain on swelling.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Cerebrospinal fluid leakage from the ears.

Explanation: Otorrhea (leakage of CSF from the ear) is a classic sign of a basilar skull fracture. Other signs include Raccoon eyes (periorbital ecchymosis), Battle's sign (mastoid ecchymosis), and Rhinorrhea (CSF from nose).

5.
Which cranial nerve is involved when the patient presents with facial pain, diminished corneal reflex and chewing dysfunction:-
a) III.
b) IV.
c) V.
d) VI.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) V.

Explanation: The Trigeminal Nerve (CN V) has three branches and is responsible for: 1. Sensation: Facial sensation/pain. 2. Reflex: Corneal reflex (sensory arm). 3. Motor: Muscles of mastication (chewing). (CN VII is facial expression, not chewing).

6.
After liver biopsy the patient should be nursed:-
a) On abdomen for 4 – 6 hours.
b) Flat on his back for 6 – 8 hours.
c) On the right side with a small pillow on the site for 4 – 6 hours.
d) On the left side with a small pillow on the site for 4 – 6 hours.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) On the right side with a small pillow on the site for 4 – 6 hours.

Explanation: The liver is located on the right side. Lying on the right side (operative side) applies direct pressure to the biopsy site against the abdominal wall/pillow, which helps prevent bleeding (hemostasis).

7.
In the management of impetigo, antibiotics are used to:-
a) Treat deep seated infection.
b) Prevent reinfection.
c) Reduce contagious spread.
d) Reduce sensitivity.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Reduce contagious spread. (and/or Treat the infection).

Explanation: Impetigo is highly contagious. Antibiotic treatment (topical or systemic) eliminates the bacteria (Staph/Strep), thereby healing the lesion and rapidly making the patient non-contagious (usually within 24 hours), preventing spread to others and to other parts of the body.

8.
Match the type of procedure in column A with correct description in column B.
Column A Column B
a) Vagotomy 1. Removal of ¼ - ¾ of the stomach.
b) Antrectomy 2. Removal of the lower portion of the stomach and portion of the duodenum.
3. Severing the vagus nerve.
4. Incision made to enlarge the outlet and relax pyloric muscle.
Show Answer

Correct Matches:

  • a) Vagotomy: Matches 3. Severing the vagus nerve. (Done to reduce gastric acid secretion).
  • b) Antrectomy: Matches 2. Removal of the lower portion of the stomach... (The antrum is the lower part; often combined with Billroth procedures). Note: Item 4 describes Pyloroplasty.
9.
One major immediate complication following Cystoscopy procedure is:-
a) Burning upon voiding.
b) Blood tinged urine.
c) Frequency of micturition.
d) Urine retention.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Urine retention.

Explanation: Burning, mild hematuria (blood-tinged urine), and frequency are expected side effects due to urethral irritation. Urine retention (due to edema or clot obstruction) is a significant complication that requires intervention (catheterization).

10.
Following renal trauma, in order to priority the nurse monitors the patient for:-
a) Signs of infection, urine output, heamaturia.
b) Heamaturia, urine output, signs of infection.
c) Urine output, signs of infection, heamaturia.
d) Urine output, heamaturia, signs of infection.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Urine output, heamaturia, signs of infection. (Or b).

Explanation: Priorities are ABCs (Shock) -> Function -> Infection. 1. Urine Output/Hematuria: Monitoring for hypovolemic shock (output) and extent of injury (hematuria) are acute priorities. 2. Infection: This is a later complication. Option (d) prioritizes Output (Systemic perfusion) and Hematuria (Injury sign) before Infection.

11.
Buck's extension traction is indicated for fractures of:-
a) Humerus.
b) Radius.
c) Tibia.
d) Cervical spine.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Tibia. (See note).

Explanation: Buck's traction is a skin traction applied to the lower limb, typically used to reduce muscle spasms and immobilize Hip or Femur fractures pre-operatively. Among the choices, Tibia is the lower leg bone where the traction boot/tape is applied, making it the only lower limb option. It is not used for upper limbs (Humerus/Radius) or spine.

12.
Osteoarthritis may be defined as:-
a) Degenerative disease of the joint.
b) Infection of the joint.
c) Metabolic disease of the joint.
d) Systematic disease of the joint.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Degenerative disease of the joint.

Explanation: Osteoarthritis (OA) is a non-inflammatory degenerative joint disease characterized by the breakdown of articular cartilage ("wear and tear").

13.
The main reason superficial burns are more painful is that:-
a) Oedema causes pressure on the blood vessels.
b) Superficial nerves become hypersensitive due to impaired circulation.
c) Sensory nerve ending have been exposed.
d) The motor nerve ending have been destroyed.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Sensory nerve ending have been exposed. (For partial thickness/superficial).

Explanation: In superficial partial-thickness burns (2nd degree), the nerve endings are exposed and irritated by air and touch, causing severe pain. In deep full-thickness burns, the nerves are destroyed, making them painless.

14.
Long term complications of burns include:-
a) Scaring, contracture, deep venous thrombosis, infection.
b) Scaring, contracture, deep venous thrombosis, hypovolumia.
c) Scaring, contracture, disfigurement, psychological trauma.
d) Scaring, infection, contracture, hypovolumia.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Scaring, contracture, disfigurement, psychological trauma.

Explanation: Hypovolemia and Infection are acute/intermediate complications. Scarring (hypertrophic/keloids), Contractures (limiting movement), Disfigurement, and Psychological trauma (PTSD, body image) are chronic/long-term sequelae.

15.
Indicate whether the following statements are true or false:
a) Culdoscopy is a procedure used to investigate infertility or ectopic pregnancy.
b) Colposcopy is a procedure used to visualize fallopian tubes.
Show Answer

Answers:

  • a) True. Culdoscopy allows visualization of the pelvic organs (behind the uterus) via the vaginal wall (cul-de-sac) to check for adhesions or ectopic pregnancy.
  • b) False. Colposcopy is the examination of the cervix, vagina, and vulva using a microscope. Fallopian tubes are visualized via Laparoscopy or Salpingoscopy.
16.
The main long-term complication folloing radical mastectomy is:-
a) Phantom pain.
b) Tingling and numbness.
c) Lymphoedema.
d) Paralysis.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Lymphoedema.

Explanation: Radical mastectomy involves the removal of axillary lymph nodes. This disrupts lymph drainage, leading to chronic accumulation of lymph fluid in the arm (Lymphedema), which is a major long-term concern.

17.
The following are sequential steps undertaken by the nurse if anaphylactic reaction occurs during management of status asthmaticus:-
a) Establish airway, give epinephrine, apply a tourniquet above injection site.
b) Establish airway, apply tourniquet above injection site, give epinephrine.
c) Give epinephrine, establish airway, apply tourniquet above injection site.
d) Apply tourniquet above injection site, establish airway, give epinephrine.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Establish airway, give epinephrine, apply a tourniquet above injection site.

Explanation: 1. Airway (A): Priority is ensuring patency due to laryngeal edema risk. 2. Epinephrine: The definitive antidote to reverse the reaction. 3. Tourniquet: Can be applied proximal to an injection site (if applicable/extremity) to slow allergen absorption, but is less critical than Airway and Epi.

18.
Following management of nose fractures, swelling can be reduced by:-
a) Use of anti-inflammatory drugs.
b) Use of warm (hot) compression.
c) Use of cold (ice) compression.
d) Use of anti-biotics.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Use of cold (ice) compression.

Explanation: Ice packs cause vasoconstriction, which reduces bleeding, edema (swelling), and pain immediately after injury. Warm compresses (b) would increase swelling.

19.
Uveitis is the inflammation of the:-
a) Iris, cilliary body, choroid.
b) Vitreous body, iris, choroid.
c) Iris, retina, sclera.
d) Cilliary body, sclera, choroid.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Iris, cilliary body, choroid.

Explanation: The Uvea (Uveal tract) consists of the Iris, Ciliary body, and Choroid.

20.
Terminal cleaning in the operating theatre refers to:-
a) Cleaning and preparation of operating theatre between cases in preparation for the next patient.
b) Cleaning and disinfecting operating theatre when a patient dies in theatre.
c) Cleaning and disinfecting of theatre at the completion of the days schedule.
d) Preparation of the operating theatre before arrival of the next patient.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Cleaning and disinfecting of theatre at the completion of the days schedule.

Explanation: Terminal cleaning is the thorough, deep cleaning and disinfection performed at the end of the day's operating list (or weekly), ensuring all surfaces and equipment are sanitized for the next day.

Part II: Short Answer Questions

40 Marks
1.
Explain the first four (4) steps in the nursing process. (4 marks)
Show Answer
  1. Assessment: Systematic collection of data (subjective and objective) about the patient's health status.
  2. Diagnosis: Analyzing the data to identify actual or potential health problems (Nursing Diagnosis).
  3. Planning: Setting goals/outcomes and determining appropriate nursing interventions to address the diagnoses.
  4. Implementation: Putting the care plan into action by performing the interventions.
  5. *(Evaluation is the 5th step).*
2.
Write short notes on benzyl penicillin under the following heading:
  1. Classification (1 mark)
  2. Mode of action (1 mark)
  3. Two (2) side effects (2 marks)
  4. Two (2) nursing considerations (2 marks)
Show Answer

a) Classification: Beta-lactam Antibiotic (Penicillin group).

b) Mode of Action: Bactericidal - inhibits bacterial cell wall synthesis causing cell lysis.

c) Side Effects:

  • Hypersensitivity reactions (Rash, Anaphylaxis).
  • Gastrointestinal upset (Nausea, Diarrhea).
  • Pain at injection site.

d) Nursing Considerations:

  • Assess for history of Penicillin allergy before administration.
  • Monitor patient for 20-30 minutes after injection for anaphylaxis.
  • Administer by deep Intramuscular (IM) injection to reduce pain.

3.
Explain three (3) specific nursing management of a patient diagnosed with ascites. (6 marks)
Show Answer
  1. Positioning: Place in Semi-Fowler's or High-Fowler's position to facilitate breathing by relieving pressure on the diaphragm from the ascitic fluid.
  2. Fluid & Diet Management: Enforce strict fluid restriction and a Low-Sodium diet to prevent further fluid retention.
  3. Monitoring: Measure abdominal girth daily at the umbilicus and record daily weights to track fluid accumulation/loss.
  4. Skin Care: Provide meticulous skin care and frequent turning, as edematous skin is prone to breakdown.
4.
List the (4) types of jaundice. (2 marks)
Show Answer
  1. Hemolytic (Pre-hepatic) Jaundice: Excessive breakdown of RBCs.
  2. Hepatocellular (Hepatic) Jaundice: Liver disease/damage.
  3. Obstructive (Post-hepatic) Jaundice: Blockage of bile ducts.
  4. Neonatal (Physiological) Jaundice: Immaturity of liver in newborns.
5.
State methods of waste segregation in hospitals in infection prevention. (6 marks)
Show Answer

Waste is segregated into color-coded bins at the point of generation:

  1. Black Bin: General/Non-infectious waste (e.g., paper, wrappers).
  2. Yellow Bin: Infectious waste (e.g., soiled cotton, dressings, anatomical waste).
  3. Red Bin: Highly infectious waste or Plastics (depending on facility protocol, often used for soiled plastics/gloves).
  4. Safety Box (Puncture-proof): Sharps (e.g., needles, blades, broken glass).
6.
State five (5) specific health messages on exercise precautions to a diabetic patient. (5 marks)
Show Answer
  1. Proper Footwear: Always wear well-fitting, comfortable shoes to prevent foot injury/blisters.
  2. Carry a Snack: Always carry a fast-acting carbohydrate (e.g., candy, juice) in case of hypoglycemia.
  3. Timing: Exercise after meals when blood glucose is rising, not when insulin is peaking.
  4. Identification: Wear a medical alert bracelet indicating diabetes.
  5. Check Levels: Monitor blood glucose before and after exercise.
  6. Inspection: Inspect feet for injuries immediately after exercise.
7.
State five (5) clinical manifestations of pleural effusion. (5 marks)
Show Answer
  1. Dyspnea: Shortness of breath.
  2. Pleuritic Chest Pain: Sharp pain worse on inspiration.
  3. Dry, Non-productive Cough.
  4. Decreased Breath Sounds: Reduced or absent sounds over the affected area.
  5. Dullness on Percussion: Over the fluid-filled area.
  6. Decreased Chest Expansion: On the affected side.

Part III: Essay/Long Answer Questions

40 Marks
1.

Mrs. Jay aged 35 years is admitted to the ward and a diagnosis of status asthmaticus is made.

a) Explain the pathophysiology of asthma. (5 marks)

b) Describe the specific nursing management of Mrs. Jay during an attack. (11 marks)

c) Formulate four (4) health messages to be shared to Mrs. Jay on discharge. (4 marks)

Show Answer

a) Pathophysiology:

  • Trigger Exposure: Allergens/irritants trigger IgE antibodies on mast cells.
  • Mediator Release: Degranulation releases histamine, leukotrienes, and prostaglandins.
  • Immediate Response:
    • Bronchospasm: Contraction of smooth muscles narrows airways.
    • Edema: Inflammation causes swelling of bronchial mucosa.
    • Mucus: Hypersecretion of thick mucus plugs the airways.
  • Result: Airway resistance increases, leading to air trapping, hyperinflation, V/Q mismatch, and hypoxia.

b) Nursing Management (During Attack):

  1. Airway & Positioning: Place in High Fowler’s or orthopneic position to maximize chest expansion.
  2. Oxygen Therapy: Administer humidified oxygen (high flow) to maintain SpO2 >90%.
  3. Medication Administration:
    • Nebulize with bronchodilators (Salbutamol/Ipratropium) immediately.
    • Administer IV Corticosteroids (Hydrocortisone) to reduce inflammation.
    • Administer IV Aminophylline or Magnesium Sulfate if prescribed for severe bronchospasm.
  4. Monitoring: Continuously monitor SpO2, Respiratory Rate, and lung sounds (wheezing vs silent chest).
  5. Hydration: Establish IV access and give fluids to liquefy secretions.
  6. Anxiety Reduction: Stay with the patient, remain calm, and encourage pursed-lip breathing.

c) Health Messages:

  1. Trigger Avoidance: Identify and avoid allergens (dust, pollen, smoke).
  2. Medication Compliance: Take maintenance inhalers (steroids) daily even when well; keep reliever inhaler accessible.
  3. Inhaler Technique: Demonstrate proper use of inhaler and spacer device.
  4. Early Recognition: Recognize early signs of worsening asthma and follow the action plan.
2.

Mrs. X 48 years old who had been diagnosed with cancer of the colon is admitted for surgery and a colostomy is made.

a) Outline five (5) nursing diagnosis that the nurse may come up with while managing Mrs. X. (5 marks)

b) Describe the specific nursing management of Mrs. X while on the colostomy. (12 marks)

c) State three (3) complications that Mrs. X may develop after the colostomy. (3 marks)

Show Answer

a) Nursing Diagnosis:

  1. Acute Pain related to surgical incision.
  2. Risk for Infection related to fecal contamination of surgical wound.
  3. Disturbed Body Image related to presence of stoma.
  4. Deficient Knowledge related to stoma care.
  5. Risk for Impaired Skin Integrity related to peristomal irritation from fecal output.
  6. Imbalanced Nutrition: Less than body requirements related to NPO status/disease process.

b) Nursing Management (Colostomy Care):

  1. Stoma Assessment: Inspect stoma frequently. It should be beefy red/pink and moist. A dusky/black stoma indicates necrosis (emergency).
  2. Skin Care: Clean skin around stoma with warm water and dry thoroughly. Apply barrier cream/paste to prevent excoriation from enzymes.
  3. Appliance Management: Ensure the wafer fits snugly (1/8 inch clearance) to prevent leakage. Empty bag when 1/3 to 1/2 full to prevent weight from breaking the seal.
  4. Dietary Advice: Introduce foods slowly. Avoid gas-forming foods (beans, cabbage) and odor-causing foods initially. Drink plenty of fluids (2-3L).
  5. Odor Control: Teach use of odor-proof pouches or drops.
  6. Psychological Support: Encourage patient to look at and touch the stoma. Provide privacy. Involve family in care.

c) Complications:

  1. Stoma Necrosis: Due to compromised blood supply.
  2. Parastomal Hernia: Protrusion of bowel through the muscle wall.
  3. Stoma Prolapse: Bowel protruding too far out.
  4. Stoma Retraction: Stoma sinking below skin level.
  5. Peristomal Dermatitis: Skin irritation/infection around stoma.
