Part I: Multiple Choice Questions

20 Marks
1.
The correct loading dose for quinine therapy in severe malaria for an 8 Kg infant is:-
a) 40 mg.
b) 80 mg.
c) 100 mg.
d) 160 mg.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) 160 mg.

Explanation: The standard loading dose for Quinine in severe malaria is 20 mg salt/kg. For an 8 kg infant: $20 \text{ mg} \times 8 \text{ kg} = 160 \text{ mg}$.

Note: The maintenance dose is typically 10 mg/kg every 8 hours.

2.
While caring for a 3 year old child admitted with asthma, a priority nursing intervention would be:-
a) Performing nebulization with albuterol upto 6 doses in the first hour.
b) Giving humidified oxygen via nasal prongs titrated by oximetry.
c) Chest physiotherapy after every nebulization till discharge.
d) Administer prescribed cough suppressants to improve child’s comfort.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Performing nebulization with albuterol upto 6 doses in the first hour.

Explanation: In acute asthma exacerbation, the priority is to relieve bronchoconstriction to improve airway patency. Short-acting beta-agonists (like Albuterol/Salbutamol) via nebulization are the first-line treatment. Rapid administration (back-to-back or continuous) in the first hour is often indicated for severe distress.

  • b) Oxygen is important but bronchodilation is the priority intervention to allow air entry.
  • c) Chest physiotherapy is generally contraindicated in acute asthma as it may increase bronchospasm.
  • d) Cough suppressants are contraindicated as the cough is needed to clear secretions.
3.
A correct discharge instruction to parents of a 12 month old infant with gastro-esophageal reflux disease will be:-
a) Give the infant blended thinned feeds for easy digestion.
b) Watch for blood-stained vomitus which requires urgent medical attention.
c) Give prescribed proton-pump inhibitors such as ranitidine to inhibit gastric acid secretion.
d) Surgical management is the ultimate intervention for consideration.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Watch for blood-stained vomitus which requires urgent medical attention.

Explanation: Parents must be educated on warning signs (red flags) such as hematemesis (blood-stained vomit), which indicates complications like esophagitis or bleeding and requires immediate attention.

  • a) Feeds should be thickened, not thinned, to reduce reflux.
  • c) While medication may be prescribed, Ranitidine is an H2 blocker, not a proton-pump inhibitor (PPI like Omeprazole), though the concept of medication is valid, the classification in the option is technically mixed. However, recognizing warning signs is a critical discharge instruction for safety.
  • d) Surgery (Nissen fundoplication) is reserved for severe, intractable cases, not a routine "instruction" for all parents.
4.
Indicate whether the following statement is true or false:-
a) BCG vaccine is contraindicated in HIV-exposed babies.
b) A HIV-exposed infant should be immediately started on ARV therapy irrespective of clinical staging once PCR test is confirmed positive.
Show Answer

Analysis:

  • Statement (a) is False: BCG is given to HIV-exposed infants unless they are showing signs of symptomatic HIV infection (AIDS). Exposure alone is not a contraindication.
  • Statement (b) is True: Under "Test and Treat" guidelines, any infant with a confirmed positive PCR for HIV should start ART immediately, regardless of CD4 count or clinical stage, to reduce mortality.

Note: In MCQ format, usually one option is the intended "correct" statement. Statement (b) is the factually correct medical management.

5.
Primary irritant diaper dermatitis:-
a) The affected area is most commonly colorized by candida albicans.
b) Lesions appear as discrete follicular papules.
c) The treatment of choice is low potency topical steroids.
d) Plastic pants should be used in preference to diapers.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) The treatment of choice is low potency topical steroids.

Explanation: While the primary management is keeping the area dry and using barrier creams (zinc oxide), low potency topical steroids (like 1% hydrocortisone) are used to reduce the inflammation in irritant dermatitis.

  • a) & b) These describe Candida diaper dermatitis (secondary infection), which presents with "beefy red" plaques, satellite lesions, and discrete follicular papules. Primary irritant dermatitis typically spares the skin folds.
  • d) Plastic pants are contraindicated as they are occlusive and trap moisture/heat, worsening the condition.
6.
According to Erickson’s developmental theory, developmental tasks during “stage of industry versus inferiority” are:-
a) Develop a sense of purpose and ability to initiate and direct own activities.
b) Gain some self-control and independence within the environment.
c) Achieve life goals established for oneself while considering welfare of future generations.
d) Achieve a sense of self-confidence by learning, completing and receiving recognition from others.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Achieve a sense of self-confidence by learning, completing and receiving recognition from others.

Explanation: Industry vs. Inferiority covers school age (6-12 years). The task is to develop social and academic skills. Success leads to a sense of competence (industry), while failure results in feelings of inferiority.

  • a) Initiative vs. Guilt (Preschool).
  • b) Autonomy vs. Shame and Doubt (Toddler).
  • c) Generativity vs. Stagnation (Middle Adulthood).
7.
Phenothiazines are thought to exert their antipsychotic effect by:-
a) Blocking post-synaptic dopamine receptors in the basal ganglia.
b) Enhancing dopamine-mediated transmission of neural impulses at the synapses.
c) Altering sodium metabolism within muscle cells and nerves.
d) Selectively inhibiting the re-uptake of amines such as nor-epinephrine.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Blocking post-synaptic dopamine receptors in the basal ganglia.

Explanation: Phenothiazines (e.g., Chlorpromazine) are typical antipsychotics that work primarily by blocking dopamine D2 receptors in the brain (mesolimbic and nigrostriatal pathways).

8.
A priority nursing diagnosis for a 14 year old teenager admitted with Bulimia nervosa is:-
a) Body image disturbance related to retarded ego development.
b) Ineffective denial related to fear of losing an aspect of life she has control over.
c) High risk for fluid deficit related to abuse of laxatives and diuretics.
d) Disturbed self esteem related to dysfunctional family system.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) High risk for fluid deficit related to abuse of laxatives and diuretics.

Explanation: In nursing prioritization (Maslow's hierarchy), physiological safety comes first. Bulimia nervosa often involves purging (vomiting, laxatives) leading to severe electrolyte imbalances (hypokalemia) and dehydration, which can be life-threatening (cardiac arrhythmias). Psychological diagnoses (Body image, Denial) are addressed after physiological stability is ensured.

9.
In Korsakoff’s psychosis:-
a) Thiamine deficiency results in pain, burning and tingling sensation in the extremities.
b) Symptoms of palpitations, anxiety, diaphoresis and arrhythmias occur.
c) The resultant symptoms of confusion, confabulation and memory loss occur due to azotemia.
d) Treatment requires parenteral or oral Thiamine plus complete alcohol abstinence.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Treatment requires parenteral or oral Thiamine plus complete alcohol abstinence.

Explanation: Korsakoff's psychosis is caused by severe Thiamine (Vitamin B1) deficiency, usually due to alcohol abuse. Treatment involves Thiamine replacement and stopping alcohol. Note: Option C describes the symptoms correctly (confusion, confabulation, memory loss) but attributes the cause to "azotemia" (high urea), which is incorrect; it's caused by thiamine deficiency.

10.
Prominent symptoms of schizophrenia during the residual phase include:-
a) Flat affect, impaired role functioning, social withdrawal.
b) Eccentric peculiar behavior, bizarre delusions, neglect of personal hygiene.
c) Hallucinations, loosening of association, lack of initiative.
d) Catatonic behavior, incoherence, bizarre ideas.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Flat affect, impaired role functioning, social withdrawal.

Explanation: The residual phase follows the active phase. Positive symptoms (hallucinations, delusions) have subsided, but negative symptoms (flat affect, withdrawal, lack of volition) persist.

12.
The action that a ward manager should take when a staff nurse has stolen ward drugs is to:-
a) Give the nurse a verbal warning.
b) Notify the police in the nearby station.
c) Report the case to the hospital nurse in-charge.
d) Dismiss the nurse immediately from the work station.

Correct Answer: c) Report the case to the hospital nurse in-charge.

16.
Which factors should the nurse assess to determine a patients ability to learn?

b) Overall physical health.

17.
The validity of a written examination can be guaranteed by:-
a) Developing a comprehensive marking key.
b) Ensuring a well-balanced content examination.
c) Subjecting written scripts to more than one examiner for marking.
d) Offering candidates a chance for a re-test.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Ensuring a well-balanced content examination.

Explanation: Validity refers to whether the test measures what it intends to measure. Content validity is ensured by a Table of Specifications (Test Blueprint) that ensures the exam covers the subject matter in a balanced way. Option (c) improves reliability (inter-rater reliability).

1.
State five (5) diagnostic criteria symptoms of delirium. (5 marks)
Show Answer

Answer:

  1. Disturbance in attention: Reduced ability to direct, focus, sustain, and shift attention.
  2. Acute onset and fluctuating course: The disturbance develops over a short period (hours to days) and tends to fluctuate in severity during the day.
  3. Disorganized thinking: Memory deficit, disorientation, language, visuospatial ability, or perception.
  4. Disturbance in consciousness: Reduced clarity of awareness of the environment.
  5. Evidence of a direct physiological cause: History/labs indicate the disturbance is caused by a medical condition, substance intoxication/withdrawal, or medication side effect.
2.
Explain three (3) goals of rehabilitation of mentally ill persons. (6 marks)
Show Answer

Answer:

  1. Restoration of Function: To help the individual regain their highest possible level of independent functioning (social, occupational, and personal care) after the acute phase of illness.
  2. Prevention of Relapse: To equip the patient with coping skills, medication adherence strategies, and symptom recognition tools to prevent future episodes and re-hospitalization.
  3. Community Integration: To assist the individual in reintegrating into society, reducing stigma, and facilitating participation in meaningful activities like work or education.

5.
Define the following terms: (a) Decision making process (1 mark). (b) Explain the five (5) steps of decision making process (5 marks).
Show Answer

a) Decision making process:

It is the cognitive process of selecting a course of action from among multiple alternatives to solve a problem or achieve a goal.

b) Steps of decision making process:

  1. Define the Problem: Clearly identify and understand the issue that requires a decision.
  2. Gather Information/Data: Collect relevant facts, constraints, and resources available.
  3. Analyze Alternatives: Generate possible solutions and evaluate the pros and cons of each.
  4. Select the Best Alternative: Choose the solution that offers the best outcome with the least risk/cost.
  5. Implement and Evaluate: Put the decision into action and monitor the results to see if the problem is solved.
6.
State five (5) categories of cognitive domain according to B.S. Bloom. (7 marks)
Show Answer

Answer: (Lowest to Highest complexity)

  1. Knowledge: Recall of data or information.
  2. Comprehension: Understanding the meaning of instructions or problems.
  3. Application: Using a concept in a new situation or unprompted use of an abstraction.
  4. Analysis: Separating material or concepts into component parts so that its organizational structure may be understood.
  5. Synthesis: Building a structure or pattern from diverse elements (creating something new).
  6. Evaluation: Making judgments about the value of ideas or materials.

8.
State six (6) nursing responsibilities while caring for a child admitted with febrile convulsions. (6 marks)
Show Answer

Answer:

  1. Maintain Safety/Prevent Injury: Pad side rails, clear the area of hard objects, do not restrain the child.
  2. Maintain Airway: Place the child in a side-lying (recovery) position to prevent aspiration of secretions.
  3. Manage Fever: Administer antipyretics (Paracetamol) as prescribed and perform tepid sponging to lower body temperature.
  4. Monitor Vital Signs: Frequently check temperature, pulse, and respiration.
  5. Observations: Document the characteristics of the seizure (duration, type of movements, body parts involved).
  6. Reassurance: Provide emotional support and explanation to the anxious parents.

Part III: Essay/Long Answer Questions

40 Marks
1.

Twenty four (24) year old Madam V has been referred to your psychiatric outpatient clinic due to history of a pervasive pattern of social inhibition and hyper sensitivity to negative comments. A diagnosis of anxious (avoidant) personality disorder is made.

a) State four (4) diagnostic criteria symptoms of anxious (avoidant) personality disorder. (4 marks)

b) Explain three (3) etiological factors in personality disorders. (6 marks)

c) Describe specific management approaches for Madam V. (10 marks)

Show Answer

a) Diagnostic Criteria (DSM-5):

  • Avoids occupational activities that involve significant interpersonal contact because of fears of criticism, disapproval, or rejection.
  • Is unwilling to get involved with people unless certain of being liked.
  • Shows restraint within intimate relationships because of the fear of being shamed or ridiculed.
  • Is preoccupied with being criticized or rejected in social situations.
  • Views self as socially inept, personally unappealing, or inferior to others.

b) Etiological Factors:

  • Biological/Genetic Factors: There is evidence of a genetic predisposition to anxiety and social inhibition (shy temperament). Infants who are naturally "slow to warm up" may be more prone to this disorder.
  • Psychological Factors (Childhood Experiences): Experiences of parental rejection, neglect, or lack of affection can lead to low self-esteem. Severe criticism or ridicule by parents or peers during childhood can foster feelings of inadequacy.
  • Social/Environmental Factors: Cultural influences or peer group rejection (bullying) during adolescence can reinforce social withdrawal and fear of negative evaluation.

c) Specific Management Approaches:

1. Psychotherapy (Primary Treatment):

  • Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (CBT): Helps Madam V identify and challenge her negative automatic thoughts ("Everyone hates me," "I am stupid"). Exposure techniques can gradually desensitize her to social situations.
  • Social Skills Training: Teaching her specific skills for social interaction (eye contact, conversation starters) to increase her confidence and competence.
  • Therapeutic Nurse-Patient Relationship: Establishing a trusting, accepting, and non-judgmental relationship is crucial. The nurse should be patient and reassuring to help her overcome her fear of rejection.

2. Pharmacotherapy:

  • Antidepressants (SSRIs): Drugs like Sertraline or Paroxetine may be prescribed to reduce the underlying anxiety and depression often associated with avoidant personality disorder.
  • Anxiolytics: Used cautiously for short-term relief of severe anxiety, though dependence is a risk.

3. Group Therapy:

  • Once individual trust is established, a supportive group setting can provide a safe environment to practice social skills and receive feedback that contradicts her negative self-view.
2.

You have been appointed the ward Manager in a newly established sub-county hospital which requires that you apply your management knowledge so as to be effective in patient care.

i) Outline three (3) basic managerial functions that you will apply in your daily duties. (3 marks)

ii) State five (5) objectives of ward management that will govern your daily functioning. (5 marks)

iii) Explain six (6) responsibilities of a ward manager that you will apply in managing the ward. (12 marks)

Show Answer

i) Basic Managerial Functions:

  • Planning: Setting goals, objectives, and determining the resources and actions needed to achieve them (e.g., duty rotas, budget).
  • Organizing: Arranging resources and tasks; assigning duties to staff members according to their competence.
  • Directing/Leading: Guiding, supervising, motivating, and communicating with staff to ensure tasks are done effectively.
  • Controlling: Monitoring performance against standards and taking corrective action when necessary (e.g., audits, appraisal).

ii) Objectives of Ward Management:

  • To provide high-quality, holistic nursing care to patients.
  • To ensure a clean, safe, and comfortable environment for patients and staff.
  • To ensure efficient and economic use of material and human resources.
  • To facilitate the education and training of nursing students and staff.
  • To maintain good public relations and communication with other departments.

iii) Responsibilities of a Ward Manager:

  1. Patient Care Management: Supervising the quality of care, conducting ward rounds, ensuring nursing care plans are updated, and handling complex patient issues.
  2. Staff Management/Rostering: Preparing duty rotas to ensure adequate coverage for all shifts, managing leave, and delegating duties appropriately.
  3. Resource/Inventory Management: Ordering drugs and supplies, ensuring equipment is functional, preventing wastage, and maintaining inventory records.
  4. Supervision and Teaching: Mentoring junior staff and students, conducting clinical teaching, and assessing staff performance (appraisals).
  5. Communication and Coordination: Acting as a liaison between the ward and hospital administration/other departments (lab, pharmacy), and holding staff meetings to disseminate information.
  6. Conflict Resolution: identifying and resolving disputes among staff or between staff and patients/relatives to maintain a harmonious working environment.
