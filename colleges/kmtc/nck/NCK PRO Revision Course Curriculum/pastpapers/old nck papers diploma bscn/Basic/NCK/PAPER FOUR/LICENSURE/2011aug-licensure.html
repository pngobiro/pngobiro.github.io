Part I: Multiple Choice Questions

20 Marks
1.
The behaviour expected of a person occupying a position is called:-
a) Role.
b) Rule.
c) Power.
d) Status.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Role.

Explanation: A "role" is the set of norms, behaviors, and expectations attached to a specific position or status in society (e.g., the role of a nurse, the role of a parent). "Status" is the position itself.

2.
Cognitive domain of learning is concerned with:-
a) Facts, muscular activities, attitudes.
b) Knowledge, attitudes, neuromuscular co-ordination.
c) Attitudes, values, maintenance of discipline.
d) Knowledge, information, concepts and principles.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Knowledge, information, concepts and principles.

Explanation: Bloom's Taxonomy divides learning into three domains: Cognitive (mental skills/knowledge), Affective (feelings/attitudes), and Psychomotor (manual/physical skills). Option D exclusively lists cognitive elements.

3.
The learned social heritage passed from one generation to another is called:-
a) Culture.
b) Rule.
c) Stratification.
d) Power.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Culture.

Explanation: Culture is defined as the complex whole which includes knowledge, belief, art, morals, law, custom, and any other capabilities and habits acquired by man as a member of society, transmitted from generation to generation.

4.
Intervention in schizophrenia at family level may include:-
a) Education about schizophrenia, improving communication, increased expressed emotions and daily contact.
b) Expanding social networks, education on schizophrenia, improving communication.
c) Increased expressed emotions and daily contact, reducing communication and hours of contact and social contact.
d) Adjusting expectations, reducing social networks and hours of contact, education about schizophrenia.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Expanding social networks, education on schizophrenia, improving communication.

Explanation: Family therapy focuses on psychoeducation and reducing "Expressed Emotion" (hostility, criticism, over-involvement). High Expressed Emotion (HEE) causes relapse. Therefore, options suggesting "increased expressed emotions" (a & c) are incorrect. The goal is to lower stress, improve communication, and support the family system.

5.
The five specific types of delusional disorders are:-
a) Jealous, persecutory, somatic, erotomanic and grandiose.
b) Paranoia, persecutory, sadness, grandiose and erotomanic.
c) Persecutory, unrealistic, indifference, somatic and jealous.
d) Indifference, somatic, jealous , erotomanic and grandiose.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Jealous, persecutory, somatic, erotomanic and grandiose.

Explanation: According to DSM criteria, the subtypes of Delusional Disorder are Erotomanic, Grandiose, Jealous, Persecutory, Somatic, Mixed, and Unspecified.

6.
The examination done at the end of the course is a form of:-
a) Impact evaluation.
b) Process evaluation.
c) Summative evaluation.
d) Formative evaluation.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Summative evaluation.

Explanation: Summative evaluation assesses learning at the end of an instructional unit or course (e.g., final exams) to assign a grade. Formative evaluation occurs during the process to improve learning.

7.
Factors predicting a good prognosis for depression in old age are:-
a) Onset before the age of 70, short duration of illness, good previous adjustment, absence of physical illness.
b) Good previous adjustment, wealth, absence of physical illness, long duration of illness.
c) Wealth, family support, onset before the age of 60, good previous adjustment.
d) All children employed, wealth, good family support, onset before the age of 50.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Onset before the age of 70, short duration of illness, good previous adjustment, absence of physical illness.

Explanation: Good prognostic factors generally include acute onset (short duration), good pre-morbid personality/adjustment, and absence of co-morbid physical pathology or brain changes (dementia). Late-onset depression (after 60/70) is often associated with organic changes and poorer prognosis.

8.
Psychological development that occur in puberty includes:-
a) Development of abstract thinking, greater independence from the family.
b) Increase in socialization, mate selection.
c) Development of intimacy, isolation.
d) Formation of personal and sexual identity, disregard of values.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Development of abstract thinking, greater independence from the family.

Explanation: Adolescence (puberty) corresponds to Piaget's Formal Operational stage (abstract thinking) and Erikson's Identity vs. Role Confusion. A key psychological task is separating from parents (independence) and developing one's own identity.

9.
In child development, crying at 3 – 4 months of life communicates:-
a) Pain, fatigue and hunger.
b) Fatigue, anger and urination.
c) Urination, pain and hunger.
d) Hunger, rest and anger.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Pain, fatigue and hunger.

Explanation: Infants use crying as their primary means of communication. By 3-4 months, cries become more differentiated for specific needs, most commonly hunger, pain/discomfort, and fatigue/tiredness.

10.
According to the guidelines on the integrated management of childhood illness, the drug of choice for treatment of chronic ear infection is:-
a) Oral Amoxillin for at least 10 days.
b) IV Augmentin for ten days.
c) Topical gentamicin ear drops for at least two weeks.
d) Topical quinolone ear drops for at least two weeks.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Topical quinolone ear drops for at least two weeks.

Explanation: For Chronic Suppurative Otitis Media (CSOM) - defined as ear discharge for 14 days or more - IMCI guidelines recommend wicking (dry ear) and topical quinolone eardrops (e.g., Ciprofloxacin) for 14 days. Oral antibiotics are generally not effective for CSOM.

11.
Factors that contribute to neonatal jaundice include:-
a) Reduced liver perfusion, increased uptake of bilirubin.
b) Enterohepatic circulation of bilirubin, shortened RBC life span.
c) Increased uptake of bilirubin, increased liver perfusion.
d) Increased liver perfusion, abnormal liver.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Enterohepatic circulation of bilirubin, shortened RBC life span.

Explanation: Physiological jaundice is caused by: 1) Increased bilirubin load due to high RBC volume and shorter RBC lifespan (80 days vs 120); 2) Immature liver (decreased uptake and conjugation); 3) Increased enterohepatic circulation (reabsorption of bilirubin from the gut).

12.
Principles of management of respiratory distress in children include:-
a) No feed by mouth, nasogastric tube aspiration, antibiotics.
b) Intravenous fluids, antibiotics, chest exercises.
c) Nasogastric tube aspiration, chest exercises, intravenous fluids.
d) No feed by mouth, intravenous fluids, tracheostomy care.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) No feed by mouth, nasogastric tube aspiration, antibiotics.

Explanation: In severe respiratory distress (e.g., fast breathing, chest indrawing), oral feeding is unsafe due to aspiration risk (NPO). NG tube may be used to decompress the stomach (aspiration) to allow diaphragm movement. Antibiotics are given for pneumonia (common cause). IV fluids maintain hydration. Tracheostomy is for obstruction, not general distress.

13.
Hypothesis is a statement that:-
a) Identifies a problem in research.
b) Maintains reliability and validity in research.
c) Proposes a relationship between two variables.
d) Explains relationships between descriptive and cross sectional research.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Proposes a relationship between two variables.

Explanation: A hypothesis is a tentative prediction or explanation of the relationship between two or more variables that is subject to testing.

14.

a) Clear, unambiguous and state the target population.
b) Declarative, ambiguous and state relationships among the variables.

15.
A sampling technique in which units are selected on the basis of availability is known as:-
a) Purposive.
b) Convenience.
c) Snowball.
d) Quota.

16.
Evaluation of students’ learning aim to:-
a) Ascertain their current level of knowledge.
b) Find out if there are slow learners.
c) Make judgment on the classroom arrangement.
d) Ensure all the students attend to classroom activities.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Ascertain their current level of knowledge.

Explanation: The primary purpose of evaluation is to determine the extent to which educational objectives have been achieved (i.e., the student's level of knowledge/competence).

17.
Work related stress may emanate directly from:-
a) Lack of support from co-workers, religious differences.
b) Unpleasant work environment, marital problems.
c) Role ambiguity, long working hours.
d) Religious differences, distance to work station.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Role ambiguity, long working hours.

Explanation: Direct work-related stressors are intrinsic to the job. Role ambiguity (not knowing what is expected), role overload (long hours), and poor working conditions are direct causes. Marital problems (b) and religious differences (a/d) are personal/external factors.

18.
The technique in which reinforcements or punishments are used to influence behaviour is:-
a) Classical conditioning.
b) Behaviour modification.
c) Operant conditioning.
d) Behaviorism.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Operant conditioning.

Explanation: B.F. Skinner's Operant Conditioning focuses on the relationship between behavior and its consequences (reinforcement increases behavior, punishment decreases it). Behavior modification is the application, but Operant Conditioning is the specific technique/theory.

19.
Drowning is defined as:-
a) Form of asphyxia in which access of air to the lungs is prevented.
b) Accidental submersion of the body in water or fluid medium.
c) Intentional submersion of the body in water or fluid medium.
d) Form of asphyxia in which the access of the air out of the lungs is prevented.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Form of asphyxia in which access of air to the lungs is prevented. (Best definition in context)

Explanation: The WHO definition involves "respiratory impairment from submersion/immersion in liquid". Option (a) describes the mechanism (asphyxia due to liquid preventing air entry). Drowning can be fatal or non-fatal.

20.
A variable is:-
a) An individual selected to represent a study population.
b) A measurable characteristic that assumes different values among the subjects.
c) An individual in the population who is willing to participate in the study.
d) A measurable characteristic which is independent.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) A measurable characteristic that assumes different values among the subjects.

Explanation: In research, a variable is any quality, attribute, or characteristic of a specific entity that can change (vary) or assume different values (e.g., age, height, diagnosis).

1.
Outline five (5) methods of probability sampling. (5 marks)
Show Answer

Answer:

  1. Simple Random Sampling: Every member has an equal chance of selection (e.g., lottery method).
  2. Stratified Random Sampling: Population divided into subgroups (strata) and random samples taken from each.
  3. Systematic Sampling: Selecting every n-th individual from a list.
  4. Cluster Sampling: Population divided into clusters; random clusters are selected and all members tested.
  5. Multi-stage Sampling: Combination of sampling methods in stages.

Show Answer

Answer:

  1. Denial: "This can't be happening to me." Temporary defense mechanism.
  2. Anger: "Why me?" Frustration and resentment directed at self, others, or God.
  3. Bargaining: "If I do this, will I live longer?" Negotiating for more time.
  4. Depression: Realization of the loss, withdrawal, and sadness.
  5. Acceptance: Coming to terms with the reality of the situation; finding peace.

Part III: Essay/Long Answer Questions

40 Marks
1.

Mr. X 35 years old is admitted in a psychiatric unit with drug induced psychosis.

a) Define the term "substance abuse". (2 marks)

b) List two (2) classifications of substance use disorders. (1 mark)

c) Explain the three (3) important contributory factors to drug abuse. (3 marks)

d) State how the acronym "CAGE" is used to diagnose drug and substance abuse. (4 marks)

e) Describe the specific management of Mr. X. (10 marks)

Show Answer

a) Substance Abuse:

A maladaptive pattern of substance use leading to clinically significant impairment or distress, characterized by failure to fulfill major role obligations, use in hazardous situations, or recurrent legal/social problems.

b) Classifications:

  • Substance Abuse.
  • Substance Dependence.
  • (Or by class: Depressants, Stimulants, Hallucinogens).

c) Contributory Factors:

  1. Biological/Genetic: Family history of addiction indicates genetic predisposition.
  2. Psychological: Underlying mental health issues (depression, anxiety), low self-esteem, or poor coping mechanisms.
  3. Social/Environmental: Peer pressure, availability of drugs, poverty, and dysfunctional family dynamics.

d) CAGE Questionnaire:

  • C: Have you ever felt you should Cut down on your drinking/drug use?
  • A: Have people Annoyed you by criticizing your drinking/drug use?
  • G: Have you ever felt bad or Guilty about your drinking/drug use?
  • E: Have you ever had a drink/drug first thing in the morning to steady your nerves or get rid of a hangover (Eye-opener)?

e) Specific Management:

  • Detoxification: Safe withdrawal from the substance, often using benzodiazepines to manage withdrawal symptoms.
  • Pharmacotherapy: Antipsychotics (e.g., Haloperidol) for psychosis; mood stabilizers if needed.
  • Safety: Constant observation if suicidal or aggressive; safe environment.
  • Psychotherapy:
    • CBT: To identify triggers and develop coping strategies.
    • Motivational Interviewing: To resolve ambivalence about quitting.
  • Rehabilitation: Group therapy (e.g., Narcotics Anonymous), family therapy, and vocational training to support reintegration.
  • Follow-up: Long-term support to prevent relapse.
2.

Mini, 5 years old, is brought to the emergency department and a diagnosis of status asthmaticus is made.

a) Define status asthmaticus. (1 mark)

b) Describe the pathophysiology of status asthmaticus. (6 marks)

c) Describe the specific management of Mini until she stabilizes. (10 marks)

d) State three (3) health messages that you would share with the mother on discharge. (3 marks)

Show Answer

a) Status Asthmaticus:

A severe, life-threatening asthma exacerbation that does not respond to standard initial bronchodilator therapy and requires aggressive medical intervention.

b) Pathophysiology:

  • Trigger: Exposure to allergen or infection triggers inflammatory response.
  • Bronchoconstriction: Smooth muscle spasm narrows airways.
  • Airway Edema: Inflammation causes swelling of bronchial mucosa.
  • Mucus Hypersecretion: Thick, tenacious mucus plugs the airways.
  • Air Trapping: Difficulty exhaling leads to hyperinflation of lungs.
  • Gas Exchange Failure: V/Q mismatch leads to Hypoxemia and eventually Hypercapnia (respiratory failure).

c) Specific Management:

  • Airway & Breathing: Prop up (High Fowler's) position. Administer humidified Oxygen to maintain Sat > 92%.
  • Pharmacotherapy:
    • SABA (Salbutamol): Continuous or frequent nebulization (every 20 mins).
    • Anticholinergics (Ipratropium): Added to nebulizer for synergistic effect.
    • Corticosteroids: IV Hydrocortisone or Methylprednisolone to reduce inflammation immediately.
    • Magnesium Sulfate: IV infusion for smooth muscle relaxation if resistant.
  • Hydration: IV fluids to correct dehydration and thin secretions (avoid overhydration).
  • Monitoring: Continuous pulse oximetry, ABGs, and vital signs monitoring.

d) Health Messages:

  1. Avoid Triggers: Identify and avoid allergens (dust, smoke, pollen).
  2. Medication Adherence: Correct use of inhalers (spacer technique) and importance of preventer (steroid) inhalers.
  3. Action Plan: Recognize early warning signs of an attack and know when to seek emergency help immediately.
