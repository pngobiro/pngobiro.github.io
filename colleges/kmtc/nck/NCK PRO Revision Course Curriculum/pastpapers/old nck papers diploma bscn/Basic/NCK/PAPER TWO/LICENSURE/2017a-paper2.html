Part I: Multiple Choice Questions

20 Marks
1.
Quickening in a multipara is experienced at:-
a) 24 weeks.
b) 22 weeks.
c) 20 weeks.
d) 18 weeks.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) 18 weeks.

Explanation: Quickening is the first perception of fetal movement by the mother. In a multipara (a woman who has given birth previously), this is typically felt earlier, around 16-18 weeks, because she recognizes the sensation. In a primigravida, it is usually felt later, around 18-20 weeks.

2.
In MCH, priority should be given to a woman who is:-
a) Pale, in pain, has difficulty in breathing, term.
b) Pale, in pain, difficulty in breathing, generalized edema.
c) Generalized edema, term, in pain, difficulty in breathing.
d) Term, generalized edema, pale, vomiting.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Pale, in pain, difficulty in breathing, generalized edema.

Explanation: This woman presents with signs suggestive of severe complications such as severe anemia or cardiac failure (pale, difficulty breathing) and potentially severe pre-eclampsia (generalized edema). The combination of respiratory distress and generalized edema is a medical emergency requiring immediate attention over others who might be stable at term.

3.
On palpating for fundal height the midwife formed four fingers below the xiphisternum. The possible gestation would be:-
a) 36 weeks, 40 weeks.
b) 32 weeks, 36 weeks.
c) 32 weeks, 40 weeks.
d) 38 weeks, 40 weeks.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) 32 weeks, 40 weeks.

Explanation: The fundal height reaches the xiphisternum at approximately 36 weeks. Around 32 weeks, it is midway between the umbilicus and xiphisternum (roughly 4 fingers below the xiphisternum). At 40 weeks, "lightening" occurs as the fetal head engages, and the fundal height drops back down to approximately the level of 32 weeks (4 fingers below xiphisternum). Therefore, this height corresponds to either 32 or 40 weeks.

4.
An infant with fetal alcohol syndrome is:-
a) Small for gestation, cognitively impaired, has craniofacial deformity.
b) Normal size, cognitively impaired, has craniofacial deformity.
c) Small for gestation, has craniofacial deformity, preterm.
d) Small for gestation, has craniofacial deformity, no cognitive impairment.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Small for gestation, cognitively impaired, has craniofacial deformity.

Explanation: The classic triad of Fetal Alcohol Syndrome (FAS) includes: 1) Growth deficiency (small for gestational age/failure to thrive), 2) CNS dysfunction (cognitive impairment/intellectual disability), and 3) Distinctive craniofacial dysmorphism (smooth philtrum, thin vermilion border, short palpebral fissures).

5.
Fetal surface of the placenta has:-
a) Chorion and visible blood vessels.
b) Amnion and visible blood vessels.
c) Chorion and amnion.
d) Decidua and amnion.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Amnion and visible blood vessels.

Explanation: The fetal surface of the placenta is smooth, glistening, and covered by the amnion. The umbilical cord inserts here, and blood vessels are clearly visible radiating from the insertion site under the amnion. The maternal surface is covered by the decidua basalis and consists of cotyledons.

6.
Occipitofrontal diameter measures of the fetal skull:-
a) 13.5 cm.
b) 9.5 cm.
c) 10.5 cm.
d) 11.5 cm.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) 11.5 cm.

Explanation: The Occipitofrontal diameter (OF) measures from the glabella (bridge of nose) to the occipital protuberance. It is the presenting diameter in a face presentation or a deflexed vertex (occipito-posterior) and measures approximately 11.5 cm.
Other diameters:
- Suboccipitobregmatic: 9.5 cm (vertex)
- Verticomental: 13.5 cm (brow)
- Occipitofrontal: 11.5 cm

7.
Antenatal exercise should be geared towards:-
a) Child birth and general physique.
b) Child birth and posture.
c) Posture and general physique.
d) Relaxation and posture.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Relaxation and posture. (Also accepted: b or a depending on source emphasis, but Relaxation is key for labor).

Explanation: Antenatal exercises aim to improve posture (to relieve backache), promote relaxation (essential for labor management), and strengthen pelvic floor muscles. While general physique is a benefit, the specific midwifery goals usually emphasize preparation for labor (relaxation) and managing the physical changes of pregnancy (posture).

8.
In second stage, the appropriate mode of delivery in a woman with cardiac disease grade III is:-
a) Vacuum extraction.
b) SVD.
c) C/S.
d) Laparatomy.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Vacuum extraction.

Explanation: For a woman with Grade III cardiac disease, maternal effort (pushing/Valsalva maneuver) in the second stage of labor is dangerous as it significantly increases cardiac workload and can lead to cardiac failure. Therefore, the second stage is shortened using assisted delivery methods like vacuum extraction or forceps to prevent the mother from pushing.

9.
In management of post partum hemorrhage the ideal canula gauge should be size :-
a) 18.
b) 16.
c) 20.
d) 22.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) 16.

Explanation: PPH management requires rapid volume replacement (fluids or blood). This necessitates a large-bore cannula. The gauge numbering system is inverse to the diameter (smaller number = larger needle). Therefore, size 16G (grey) is larger than 18G (green), 20G (pink), or 22G (blue) and allows for the fastest flow rate.

10.
Symptoms of second stage of labor are:-
a) Urge to bear down, excessive show.
b) Strong uterine contractions, gaping anus.
c) Excessive show, gaping anus.
d) Urge to bear down, strong uterine contractions.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Urge to bear down, strong uterine contractions.

Explanation: While gaping anus and show are signs (observable by the midwife), the symptom (felt by the mother) is the uncontrollable urge to bear down/push. Strong expulsive uterine contractions are also characteristic.

11.
Indicate whether the statement is true or false:
i) In face presentation the midwife maintains extension.
ii) An infant born through mentoanterior position has a normal face.
Show Answer

Answers:

  • i) True: In face presentation, the head is fully extended. Maintaining this extension is crucial for the mechanism of labor (mentum anterior) to proceed.
  • ii) False: Babies born via face presentation typically have significant facial edema, bruising, and distortion (caput formation on the face) which resolves after a few days. They do not look "normal" immediately at birth.
12.
Appropriate action following ketone + in urine during labour is to:-
a) Ambulate mother, massage her back.
b) Feed the mother and encourage plenty of oral fluids.
c) Put in left lateral position, massage her back.
d) Feed the mother, continue monitoring contractions.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Feed the mother and encourage plenty of oral fluids. (Or IV fluids if oral not tolerated).

Explanation: Ketonuria in labor indicates maternal starvation/exhaustion where the body is breaking down fats for energy due to lack of glucose. The appropriate management is rehydration and providing energy (glucose). While some guidelines restrict oral intake, "feeding" light easily digestible foods or glucose fluids is the direct intervention for ketosis.

13.
Signs of true labour are:-
a) Presence of show, erratic pain, cervical dilatation.
b) Presence of show, rhythmic uterine contractions, cervical dilatation.
c) Presence of show, rupture of membranes, cervical dilatation.
d) Presence of show, bandles ring, cervical dilatation.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Presence of show, rhythmic uterine contractions, cervical dilatation.

Explanation: True labor is characterized by regular, rhythmic uterine contractions that increase in frequency and intensity, accompanied by progressive cervical effacement and dilatation. The "show" (mucus plug) often accompanies this.

14.
The objective of first examination of the newborn is to:-
a) Assess maturity, rule out congenital abnormalities, ascertain condition at birth.
b) Rule out congenital abnormality, ascertain condition at birth, check meconium staining.
c) Assess maturity, birth injuries and rule out congenital abnormalities.
d) Assess maturity, check meconium staining, ascertain condition at birth.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Assess maturity, birth injuries and rule out congenital abnormalities. (A is also very similar).

Explanation: The initial full examination (head-to-toe) is done to detect any gross congenital abnormalities, identify any birth injuries (like cephalhematoma, fractures), and assess general maturity/well-being. Ascertaining condition at birth (Apgar) happens immediately, while the "examination" usually refers to the physical check within the first hour.

15.
Premature babies should be given:-
a) Vitamin K 1mg, BCG, birth polio.
b) Vitamin K 0.5 mg, BCG, polio.
c) Vitamin K 1 mg, BCG, polio 1.
d) Vitamin K 0.5 mg, BCG, birth polio.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Vitamin K 0.5 mg, BCG, birth polio. (Sometimes b is accepted if "polio" implies birth polio).

Explanation: Preterm infants (less than 1500g or 2500g depending on protocol) often receive a lower dose of Vitamin K (0.5mg IM or phytomenadione) compared to term infants (1.0mg) to prevent hemorrhagic disease of the newborn. They also receive standard birth vaccines (BCG and OPV0) if clinically stable.

16.
Post delivery, if combined oral contraceptive is not available the midwife can give:-
a) Progesterone only pills and condoms.
b) Intra uterine contraceptive devise and condoms.
c) Implants and progesterone only pills.
d) Condoms and implants.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Progesterone only pills and condoms. (C is also valid but A is more immediate/common).

Explanation: Combined Oral Contraceptives (COCs) contain estrogen, which can suppress lactation and are generally contraindicated in the first 6 weeks of breastfeeding. The appropriate alternative is Progesterone Only Pills (POPs) or barrier methods (condoms), as progesterone does not affect breast milk supply.

17.
Promotion of breast feeding can be enhanced by:-
a) Sounds of a baby, sight of a baby and touching the baby.
b) Sounds of a baby, pain and touching the baby.
c) Sounds of a baby, worries, touching the baby.
d) Sounds of a baby, full breasts, touching the baby.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Sounds of a baby, sight of a baby and touching the baby.

Explanation: The oxytocin reflex (let-down reflex) is psychological and sensory. It is enhanced by positive stimuli such as hearing the baby cry, seeing the baby, and skin-to-skin contact (touching). Negative factors like pain (b) and worries/stress (c) inhibit the reflex.

18.
Tetanus toxoid should be given at:-
a) > 36 weeks.
b) During the first visit.
c) After > 16 weeks.
d) During 4th visit.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) During the first visit.

Explanation: According to KEPI (Kenya Expanded Programme on Immunization) and WHO guidelines for antenatal care, the first dose of Tetanus Toxoid (TT1) should be given at the first contact with the pregnant woman (First Visit), regardless of gestation, followed by TT2 at least 4 weeks later.

19.
Complete involution of the uterus occurs at:-
a) 2 weeks.
b) 4 weeks.
c) 6 weeks.
d) 10 weeks.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) 6 weeks.

Explanation: Involution is the process by which the uterus returns to its pre-pregnant size and state. This process is generally considered clinically complete by the end of the puerperium, which is 6 weeks (42 days) postpartum.

20.
Maternal audit should focus on:-
a) Hospital morbidity.
b) Hospital mortality.
c) Maternal mortality.
d) Supplies management.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Maternal mortality.

Explanation: Maternal Perinatal Death Surveillance and Response (MPDSR) audits specifically focus on analyzing maternal mortalities (deaths) to identify avoidable factors, gaps in care, and implement corrective measures to prevent future deaths.

Part II: Short Answer Questions

1.
With the help of diagrams, illustrate the different types of placenta praevia. (5 marks)
Show Answer

Answer Guide:

You should draw the uterus with the cervical os and illustrate the four degrees of placenta praevia:

  1. Type I (Lateral/Low-lying): The placenta encroaches on the lower uterine segment but does not reach the internal os.
  2. Type II (Marginal): The edge of the placenta reaches the margin of the internal os but does not cover it.
  3. Type III (Partial): The placenta covers the internal os partially when the cervix is closed, but not completely when dilated.
  4. Type IV (Complete/Central): The placenta completely covers the internal os even when fully dilated.

Note: In an exam, diagrams must be clearly labeled showing the Uterine Wall, Lower Segment, Internal Os, and Placenta location relative to the os.

2.
Outline five (5) ways you are going to teach a woman kegel exercises following normal delivery. (5 marks)
Show Answer

Steps for teaching Kegel Exercises:

  1. Identify the Muscles: Instruct the mother to imagine she is trying to stop the flow of urine mid-stream or stop passing gas. (Note: Do not actually do this while voiding repeatedly as it may cause retention).
  2. Technique: Squeeze and lift the pelvic floor muscles inward and upward. Hold the squeeze for a count of 3-5 seconds.
  3. Relaxation: Completely relax the muscles for a count of 3-5 seconds between contractions.
  4. Repetition: Repeat the squeeze-hold-relax cycle 10 times to form one set.
  5. Frequency: Perform at least 3 sets per day (morning, afternoon, evening). Do them in different positions (lying, sitting, standing).
3.
Outline specific interventions by a midwife to a woman in second stage of labour with cord presentations. (3 marks)
Show Answer

Interventions for Cord Presentation:

  1. Do NOT Rupture Membranes: Keep membranes intact to provide a cushion and prevent cord prolapse.
  2. Change Maternal Position: Place the mother in Trendelenburg position (head down, hips elevated) or Knee-Chest position to relieve pressure of the presenting part on the cord.
  3. Immediate Delivery Preparation: Prepare for immediate Caesarean Section as vaginal delivery is risky if membranes rupture. Monitor fetal heart rate continuously.
4.
State four (4) benefits of breast feeding to the mother and four (4) benefits of breast feeding to the baby. (8 marks)
Show Answer

a) Benefits to the Mother:

  1. Promotes involution of the uterus and reduces postpartum hemorrhage (due to oxytocin release).
  2. Reduces risk of breast and ovarian cancer.
  3. Acts as a natural contraceptive (Lactational Amenorrhea Method) if exclusive.
  4. Promotes bonding with the infant.
  5. Helps in weight loss by burning extra calories.

b) Benefits to the Baby:

  1. Provides perfect nutrition tailored to the baby's needs (correct temperature, consistency).
  2. Provides passive immunity (antibodies/IgA) protecting against infections (diarrhea, respiratory).
  3. Reduces risk of allergies and eczema.
  4. Easy to digest, preventing constipation.
  5. Promotes proper jaw and teeth development.
5.
Explain two (2) environmental factors and two (2) newborn factors that predispose to hypothermia. (6 marks)
Show Answer

Environmental Factors:

  1. Cold delivery room/draughts: Low ambient temperature causes heat loss via radiation and convection.
  2. Wet surroundings: Failure to dry the baby immediately leads to rapid heat loss via evaporation.

Newborn Factors:

  1. Large Surface Area to Body Weight Ratio: Newborns lose heat 4 times faster than adults due to their relative size.
  2. Lack of Subcutaneous Fat: Minimal insulation (especially in preterm/LBW babies) allows heat to escape easily.
  3. Inability to Shiver: Newborns rely on non-shivering thermogenesis (brown fat metabolism), which is limited.
6.
Explain three (3) specific health messages you will give to a woman with cracked nipples. (6 marks)
Show Answer

Health Messages:

  1. Correct Attachment/Positioning: Ensure the baby takes a large mouthful of breast tissue (areola), not just the nipple. Poor attachment is the main cause of cracks.
  2. Breast Milk Application: Express a few drops of hindmilk after feeding and rub it on the nipples. It has healing and anti-infective properties. Let nipples air dry.
  3. Avoid Soap/Alcohol: Do not wash nipples with soap or alcohol as it dries out natural oils, worsening cracks. Use only water.
  4. Start Feed on Unaffected Side: If one side is very painful, start the feed on the less painful breast to initiate the let-down reflex before switching.
7.
State four (4) complications of polyhydramnios. (4 marks)
Show Answer

Complications:

  • Maternal:
    • Premature rupture of membranes (PROM).
    • Preterm labour due to overdistension.
    • Placental abruption due to sudden decompression.
    • Postpartum hemorrhage (due to uterine atony from overdistension).
    • Respiratory distress (maternal).
  • Fetal:
    • Unstable lie/Malpresentation.
    • Cord prolapse (when membranes rupture).
    • Preterm birth.
8.
State three (3) causes of haemorrhage after normal delivery. (3 marks)
Show Answer

Causes of Primary PPH (4 T's):

  1. Tone (Atonic Uterus): Failure of the uterus to contract (most common cause).
  2. Trauma: Lacerations of the cervix, vagina, or perineum.
  3. Tissue: Retained placental tissue or membranes.
  4. Thrombin: Coagulation disorders (e.g., DIC).

Part III: Essay/Long Answer Questions

1.
Mrs. B, 27 years old primigravida comes to labour ward in established labour at term.

a) Define normal labour. (1 mark)

Show Answer

Normal Labour (Eutocia):

The onset of regular, painful uterine contractions that bring about progressive effacement and dilatation of the cervix, leading to the expulsion of the fetus, placenta, and membranes via the birth canal. It occurs spontaneously at term (37-42 weeks), involves a fetus in vertex presentation, takes place without undue prolongation, and results in a healthy mother and baby.

b) Describe the management of Mrs. B till delivery. (11 marks)

Show Answer

Management of First Stage of Labour:

1. Admission & Assessment:

  • Welcome Mrs. B and review antenatal history.
  • Check vital signs (BP, Pulse, Temp).
  • Perform abdominal palpation (Fundal height, Lie, Presentation, Descent, FHR).
  • Perform vaginal examination (Cervical dilation, membrane status).

2. Monitoring (Partograph):

  • Start plotting the Partograph if in active phase (>4cm).
  • Maternal: BP & Pulse every 4 hours (Pulse every 30 mins in active), Temp every 4 hours, Urine for protein/ketones.
  • Fetal: Fetal Heart Rate every 30 minutes (15 mins in late active phase). Monitor liquor color.
  • Progress: Cervical dilation every 4 hours, Descent of head, Contractions (Frequency/Duration/Intensity) every 30 mins.

3. Nursing Care & Comfort:

  • Ambulation/Position: Encourage walking or upright positions if membranes intact/head engaged. Left lateral if in bed.
  • Hygiene: Encourage voiding every 2 hours (full bladder obstructs labor). Perineal care.
  • Nutrition: Light easily digested fluids/glucose to prevent ketosis.
  • Pain Relief: Back massage, breathing techniques, analgesics (Pethidine) if prescribed (not in late labor).
  • Emotional Support: Reassurance, explain procedures, allow companion.

4. Preparation for Second Stage:

  • Prepare delivery set.
  • Observe for signs of second stage (urge to push, anal gaping).

c) State four (4) potential nursing diagnoses for Mrs. B and four (4) potential nursing diagnoses for the baby following normal spontaneous vertex delivery. (8 marks)

Show Answer

i) Nursing Diagnoses for Mrs. B (Mother):

  1. Acute Pain related to perineal trauma/episiotomy or uterine involution/afterpains.
  2. Risk for Infection related to tissue trauma and open placental site.
  3. Risk for Bleeding (Deficient Fluid Volume) related to uterine atony.
  4. Fatigue related to energy expenditure during labor.
  5. Knowledge Deficit regarding breastfeeding and newborn care.

ii) Nursing Diagnoses for Baby:

  1. Risk for Ineffective Thermoregulation (Hypothermia) related to large surface area and environmental factors.
  2. Risk for Infection related to unhealed umbilical stump.
  3. Ineffective Airway Clearance related to presence of mucus in nasopharynx.
  4. Risk for Imbalanced Nutrition: Less than body requirements related to poor latching/sucking reflex.
2.
Baby X, born via spontaneous vertex delivery from a mother para 6 + 0 weighs 2 kgs at 39 weeks gestation.

a) Define small for gestational age. (1 mark)

Show Answer

Small for Gestational Age (SGA):

An infant whose birth weight is below the 10th percentile for their gestational age. (Note: Baby X is 39 weeks but 2kg, indicating significant growth restriction/SGA).

b) Describe the specific management of Baby X till discharge. (11 marks)

Show Answer

Management of SGA Baby:

1. Thermoregulation (Warmth):

  • Kangaroo Mother Care (KMC): Initiate immediately. Continuous skin-to-skin contact is the gold standard for stable LBW babies.
  • Keep room warm (25-28C).
  • Delay bathing for at least 24 hours.
  • Use warm clothes and hat.

2. Nutrition & Feeding:

  • Early Initiation: Breastfeed within 1 hour.
  • Frequency: Feed every 2-3 hours (at least 8-12 times/day) to prevent hypoglycemia (SGA babies have low glycogen stores).
  • Method: If suckling is weak, use cup feeding or NGT with expressed breast milk.
  • Monitor weight daily (aim for gain of 15-20g/kg/day).

3. Glucose Monitoring:

  • Monitor blood glucose levels frequently in first 24 hours to detect and treat hypoglycemia immediately.

4. Infection Prevention:

  • Strict hand washing.
  • Cord care (Chlorhexidine/dry care).
  • Limit visitors.
  • Administer Vitamin K and eye prophylaxis.

5. Monitoring & Discharge Planning:

  • Monitor for jaundice, apnea, or respiratory distress.
  • Discharge criteria: Gaining weight, feeding well, maintaining temperature, mother confident in care.

c) Explain preventive measures of small for gestation deliveries. (8 marks)

Show Answer

Preventive Measures (Antenatal Care):

  1. Adequate Maternal Nutrition: Encourage a balanced diet rich in protein and calories. Supplement with Iron and Folic Acid.
  2. Malaria Prevention: Give Intermittent Presumptive Treatment (IPTp) and encourage use of Insecticide Treated Nets (ITNs), as placental malaria causes SGA.
  3. Disease Management: Screen for and treat conditions like Syphilis, HIV, and Hypertension/Pre-eclampsia which restrict fetal growth.
  4. Avoid Harmful Substances: Counsel against smoking, alcohol, and drug use.
  5. Rest and Workload: Encourage adequate rest, especially in the third trimester, to improve placental blood flow.
  6. Family Planning: Birth spacing (at least 2 years) allows maternal nutrient stores to replenish. (Mother is Para 6+0, high parity is a risk factor).
  7. Monitoring: Regular ANC visits to monitor fundal height and weight gain for early detection of IUGR.
