Part I: Multiple Choice Questions

20 Marks
1.
The diameter of the pelvic brim that plays a role in determining the outcome of occipito posterior position:-
a) Transverse.
b) Oblique.
c) Obstetric conjugate.
d) Sacrocotyloid.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Sacrocotyloid.

Explanation: The sacrocotyloid diameter (distance from the promontory of the sacrum to the iliopectineal eminence) is the critical dimension in the posterior pelvis. A reduced sacrocotyloid diameter (common in android or platypelloid pelvises) prevents the fetal head from entering or rotating correctly, often leading to persistent occipito-posterior positions or deep transverse arrest.

2.
The hormone responsible for rise in temperature immediately after ovulation is:-
a) Oestrogen.
b) Luteinizing hormone.
c) Follicle stimulating hormone.
d) Progesterone.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Progesterone.

Explanation: Progesterone, secreted by the corpus luteum after ovulation, has a thermogenic effect. It acts on the hypothalamus to raise the basal body temperature (BBT) by about 0.2°C to 0.5°C.

3.
The positive signs of pregnancy include:-
a) Visualization of fetus by x-ray at 16 weeks, foetal heart sound by Doppler at 5+ weeks.
b) Visualization of gestational sac by trans-vaginal ultrasound at 4-5 weeks, foetal heart sound by fetal scope at 20 weeks.
c) Uterine souffle at 12 – 16 weeks, Braxton Hicks at 16 weeks.
d) Visualization of foetal pulsation by trans-abdominal ultrasound at 5 weeks, foetal heart sound by foetal scope at 16 + weeks.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Visualization of gestational sac by trans-vaginal ultrasound at 4-5 weeks, foetal heart sound by fetal scope at 20 weeks.

Explanation: Positive signs are indisputable evidence of pregnancy:

  • Visualization of the fetus/sac (USG).
  • Hearing fetal heart sounds.
  • Palpation of fetal movements by the examiner.
Option B lists reliable visualization and auscultation. Option C lists probable signs. Option A includes X-ray (unsafe) and Doppler at 5 weeks (too early, usually 10-12).

4.
Mrs. Y comes for her first antenatal clinic. On history taking her last menstrual period was 25.4.2011. Her expected date of confinement will be:-
a) 1.2.2012.
b) 2.2.2012.
c) 3.3.2012.
d) 4.3.2012.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) 2.2.2012.

Calculation (Naegele's Rule):

  • Add 7 days to the day: 25 + 7 = 32. (April has 30 days, so 32 - 30 = 2nd of May).
  • Subtract 3 months from the month: April (4) - 3 = January (1). Or add 9 months: May + 9 months = February.
  • Adjust Year: 2011 + 1 = 2012.
  • Result: 2nd February 2012.
5.
The effects of diabetes mellitus on pregnancy include:-
a) Nausea and vomiting, ketosis.
b) Glycosylated haemoglobin, reduced fertility rate.
c) Urinary tract infection, neuropathy.
d) Blindness, Polycytheamia.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Urinary tract infection, neuropathy.

Explanation: Urinary Tract Infections (UTI) are significantly more common in diabetic pregnancies due to glucosuria. Neuropathy can be a maternal complication. While Ketosis (a) is a risk (DKA), simple nausea is common to all. Polycythemia (d) is a fetal/neonatal complication, not a maternal effect on pregnancy.

6.
The definite indications for an episiotomy include:-
a) Tight perineum, first delivery.
b) Scaring from female genital cutting, fetal distress.
c) Multiple pregnancies, fetal distress.
d) Rigid perineum, preterm baby.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Rigid perineum, preterm baby.

Explanation: A rigid perineum that fails to stretch is a classic indication. For a preterm baby, an episiotomy is performed to reduce intracranial pressure on the soft, fragile fetal skull, preventing brain hemorrhage.

7.
Mrs. K. begins to bleed heavily immediately after delivery of the baby. In sequence, your actions as a midwife would be:-
a) Call for help, make rapid evaluation of Mrs K’s general condition, massage uterus, give oxytocin 10 I.U.
b) Massage uterus give oxytocin 20 I.U, perform urinary bladder catheterization, take blood to rule out blood disorders.
c) Call for help, perform urinary bladder catheterization, deliver placenta, examine birth canal.
d) Start I.V. line with crystalloids, deliver placenta if in-situ, examine birth canal, give ergometrine 0.5mg I.V. stat.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Call for help, make rapid evaluation of Mrs K’s general condition, massage uterus, give oxytocin 10 I.U.

Explanation: The immediate management of PPH follows a resuscitative sequence: 1. Call for help (don't leave patient). 2. Rub up a contraction (Massage) to stop bleeding. 3. Administer oxytocic. 4. Check vital signs (Evaluation). 5. IV fluids.

8.
In the course of managing 2nd stage of labour, you realize that after delivery of the head, it tries to return in the vagina. Your immediate action in sequence would be:-
a) Apply McRoberts Manoeuvre, perform episiotomy, apply supra-pubic pressure, apply Rubin’s Manoeuvre, apply Woods Manoeuvre.
b) Reassure the mother/partner summon for help, perform an episiotomy, change position of the mother, attempt Rubin’s Manoeuvre.
c) Summon for help, reassure Mother and partner, attempt change of position, apply McRoberts Manoeuvre, apply supra-pubic pressure.
d) Summon for help, attempt Rubin’s Manoeuvre, apply supra-pubic pressure, attempt Woods Manoeuvre, give an episiotomy.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Apply McRoberts Manoeuvre, perform episiotomy, apply supra-pubic pressure, apply Rubin’s Manoeuvre, apply Woods Manoeuvre.

Explanation: This describes the "Turtle Sign" of Shoulder Dystocia. The standard management protocol (HELPERR) is:
1. Help (Call for help).
2. Episiotomy (Consider).
3. Legs (McRoberts Manoeuvre - first line).
4. Pressure (Suprapubic).
5. Enter (Internal manoeuvres: Rubin/Woods).
6. Remove posterior arm.
7. Roll patient.
Option A follows the physical intervention sequence best.

10.
In breech delivery the principle of hands off the breech as the baby progressively descends so as to:-
a) Ascertain foetal wellbeing.
b) Allow enough room for the foetus to pass.
c) Allow gravitating descent of the foetus.
d) Avoid traction.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Avoid traction.

Explanation: The "hands off" rule is strictly maintained to avoid premature traction on the breech. Traction can cause the fetal head to extend (deflex), which results in the head getting stuck (entrapped) at the pelvic brim or outlet. It also stimulates the grasp reflex in the fetus.

11.
On abdominal examination during labour, the sinciput is felt and occiput just felt. The descent is:-
a) 4/5.
b) 3/5.
c) 2/5.
d) 1/5.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) 2/5.

Explanation: Using the rule of fifths per abdomen:
- 5/5: Head floating.
- 4/5: Sinciput high, Occiput high (not engaged).
- 3/5: Sinciput felt easily, Occiput felt but lower.
- 2/5: Sinciput felt, Occiput just felt or not felt (Head is Engaged).
- 1/5: Sinciput just felt, Occiput not felt.
- 0/5: Head not felt (Deeply engaged).

12.
On vaginal examination during labour, you elicit the following: Sagittal suture is in the left oblique diameter at the pelvis, occiput points to the right iliopectineal eminence. The position of the vertex presentation is:-
a) Right occipito anterior.
b) Right occipito posterior.
c) Left occipito posterior.
d) Left occipito anterior.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Right occipito anterior.

Explanation:
1. The Left Oblique Diameter connects the Left Sacroiliac Joint to the Right Iliopectineal Eminence.
2. The Occiput is pointing to the Right Iliopectineal Eminence.
3. The Iliopectineal eminence is in the anterior part of the pelvis.
4. Therefore, the Occiput is Right and Anterior = ROA.

13.
The maternal danger signs that a midwife should concern her/himself with during labour include:-
a) Meconium stained liquor, falling blood pressure, abnormal lower abdominal contour.
b) Pulse rate of 70-80 BPM, rising blood pressure above 140/90 mmHg, hyperactivity.
c) Increasing apprehension, inadequate contraction, abnormal lower abdominal contour.
d) Pathological retraction ring, falling blood pressure, meconium stained liquor.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Pathological retraction ring, falling blood pressure, meconium stained liquor.

Explanation: While Meconium is a fetal sign, this option clusters the most critical emergency signs:
- Pathological Retraction Ring (Bandl's Ring): Sign of obstructed labour and imminent uterine rupture (Maternal).
- Falling BP: Sign of shock/hemorrhage (Maternal).
- Meconium: Sign of fetal distress (often associated with difficult labour).

14.
The prophylactic drugs given to a term new born at birth are:-
a) 3% tetracycline eye ointment, vit. K 0.5mg i.m. stat.
b) Gentamycin eye drops, vit K. i.m. stat.
c) 1% tetracycline eye ointment, vit K, 1 mg i.m. stat.
d) Gentamycin eye drop, vit. K 1 mg 1.m. stat.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) 1% tetracycline eye ointment, vit K, 1 mg i.m. stat.

Explanation: Standard prophylaxis involves 1% Tetracycline Eye Ointment (to prevent Ophthalmia Neonatorum) and 1.0 mg Vitamin K IM (to prevent Hemorrhagic Disease of the Newborn). Preterm infants receive a lower dose of Vit K (0.5mg).

15.
A baby whose birth weight is 1800 gm is admitted in newborn unit with apnoiec attacks. The baby should be nursed in:-
a) Room temperature of 34°C-35°C, incubator temperature 35°C – 36°C.
b) Room temperature of 33°C-34°C, incubator temperature 32°C – 34°C.
c) Room temperature of 30°C-32°C, incubator temperature 32°C – 33°C.
d) Room temperature of 28°C-30°C, incubator temperature 31°C – 32°C.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Room temperature of 33°C-34°C, incubator temperature 32°C – 34°C.

Explanation: An 1800g (1.8kg) baby requires a Neutral Thermal Environment (NTE) to prevent hypothermia (which causes apnea). For this weight, the incubator air temperature is typically set around 33-34°C.

16.
Side effects that a midwife should look for while nursing a baby under conventional phototherapy include:-
a) Hypoglycaemia, irritability, hypocalcaemia.
b) Hypothermia, lethargy, alteration in neuro-behaviour organization.
c) Skin rashes, necrotizing, enterocolitis, isolation.
d) Skin burns, isolation, thrombocytopenia.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Skin rashes, necrotizing, enterocolitis, isolation.

Explanation: Common side effects include skin rashes (Bronze Baby Syndrome), frequent loose stools, dehydration, and hyperthermia. Psychosocially, "isolation" from the mother is a significant side effect. Thrombocytopenia (d) and Hypocalcemia (a) are also possible, but rashes are the most visible nursing observation.

17.
Following caesarian section, the wound and lochia loss should be observed at least:-
a) ½ hourly.
b) 1 hourly.
c) 2 hourly.
d) 4 hourly.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) ½ hourly.

Explanation: In the immediate postoperative period (first 2-4 hours), vital signs, wound dressing (for oozing), and lochia (for hemorrhage) are checked every 30 minutes (½ hourly) to detect complications early.

18.
By 9th to 10th day postnatally the uterus is usually palpated:-
a) At the umbilicus.
b) Midway between umbilicus and symphysis pubis.
c) Just above the symphysis pubis.
d) Not palpable per abdomen.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Not palpable per abdomen.

Explanation: The uterus involutes at a rate of approximately 1 cm per day. By day 10, it has usually descended into the true pelvis and is no longer palpable above the symphysis pubis.

19.
The causes of indirect maternal deaths include:-
a) Postpartum haemorrhage, obstructed labour, eclampsia.
b) Malaria, diabetes mellitus, sickle cell disease.
c) HIV/AIDS, abortion, ruptured uterus.
d) Tuberculosis, road traffic accident, puerperal sepsis.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Malaria, diabetes mellitus, sickle cell disease.

Explanation:
- Direct Causes: Obstetric complications (PPH, Sepsis, Eclampsia, Abortion, Ruptured Uterus).
- Indirect Causes: Pre-existing diseases aggravated by pregnancy (Malaria, Anemia, HIV, Cardiac disease, Diabetes).

20.
Delivery register is important because it gives information on:-
a) Utilizing of maternity services, mode of delivery, birth weight patterns.
b) Maternal and Perinatal morbidity and mortality pattern, socio-demographic and obstetric characteristics of women.
c) Bed occupancy, attendant and time of delivery, source of referral and reasons.
d) Quality of care, mode of delivery, birth patterns.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Maternal and Perinatal morbidity and mortality pattern, socio-demographic and obstetric characteristics of women.

Explanation: The delivery register is a comprehensive epidemiological tool that captures data on the mother, the birth process, outcomes (morbidity/mortality), and demographics, which is essential for health planning and auditing.

Part II: Short Answer Questions

1.
Draw a well labeled diagram to illustrate foetal circulation. (5 marks)
Show Answer

Diagram Guide:

You should draw a diagram showing the fetus, placenta, and cord with the following key structures labeled:

  1. Placenta: Site of gas/nutrient exchange.
  2. Umbilical Vein: Carries oxygenated blood from placenta to fetus.
  3. Ductus Venosus: Shunt connecting umbilical vein to Inferior Vena Cava (bypassing liver).
  4. Foramen Ovale: Opening between Right and Left Atrium (bypassing lungs).
  5. Ductus Arteriosus: Shunt connecting Pulmonary Artery to Aorta (bypassing lungs).
  6. Umbilical Arteries (2): Carry deoxygenated blood from fetus back to placenta.
2.
Outline five (5) physiological changes that take place in the uterus during pregnancy. (5 marks)
Show Answer

Physiological Changes:

  1. Hypertrophy and Hyperplasia: Massive increase in size (from 60g to 1000g) due to enlargement of existing muscle fibers and formation of new ones.
  2. Formation of Lower Uterine Segment: The isthmus stretches and thins to form the lower segment in later pregnancy.
  3. Softening: The lower part of the uterus softens (Hegar's sign) due to increased vascularity.
  4. Contractility: Irregular, painless contractions (Braxton Hicks) occur throughout pregnancy.
  5. Increased Vascularity: Blood vessels dilate and multiply to supply the placenta (uterine blood flow increases to ~750ml/min).
3.
State five (5) key steps observed when providing sulphadoxine pyrimethamine (SP) to a pregnant women. (5 marks)
Show Answer

Key Steps (IPTp Protocol):

  1. Directly Observed Therapy (DOT): Ensure the client swallows the tablets in the presence of the health worker.
  2. Timing: Administer from the second trimester (after quickening/16 weeks) at least one month apart.
  3. Exclusion of Allergy: Ask about history of allergy to Sulphur drugs before administration.
  4. Folic Acid Restriction: Do NOT give Folic Acid supplements on the same day (wait 14 days) as it antagonizes SP action.
  5. Hydration/Food: Ensure the mother has eaten and provide clean water for swallowing to prevent nausea.
4.
Outline five (5) roles of a companion during labour. (5 marks)
Show Answer

Roles of Companion:

  1. Emotional Support: Providing reassurance, encouragement, and reducing anxiety.
  2. Physical Comfort: Rubbing the mother's back, helping with position changes, wiping sweat.
  3. Hydration/Nutrition: Assisting the mother to drink fluids or eat light meals.
  4. Advocacy: Communicating the mother's wishes to the medical team.
  5. Monitoring: Alerting the nurse/midwife if the mother is in severe pain or needs help.
5.
State six (6) specific nursing interventions when managing a mother during trial of labour. (6 marks)
Show Answer

Interventions:

  1. Strict Partograph Monitoring: Ensure labor progress is plotted accurately to detect delay early.
  2. Frequent Fetal Heart Monitoring: Auscultate FHR every 15-30 minutes (or continuously) to detect fetal distress.
  3. Maternal Vital Signs: Monitor BP and Pulse closely; tachycardia may indicate exhaustion or impending rupture.
  4. Observe for Bandl's Ring: Watch for abnormal abdominal contour indicating obstruction/rupture.
  5. Hydration/Bladder Care: Ensure adequate hydration and keep bladder empty (catheterize if necessary) to prevent obstruction.
  6. Preparation for Emergency CS: Have blood grouped and cross-matched, IV access established, and theater team alerted.
6.
State six (6) benefits of Kangaroo care to primary carer. (6 marks)
Show Answer

Benefits to Mother (Primary Carer):

  1. Promotes Bonding: Enhances emotional attachment between mother and baby.
  2. Increases Milk Production: Skin-to-skin contact stimulates oxytocin and prolactin.
  3. Confidence: Increases the mother's confidence in handling and caring for her small baby.
  4. Reduced Stress: The baby cries less and is calmer, reducing maternal anxiety.
  5. Empowerment: The mother feels she is actively doing something to help her baby survive.
  6. Reduced Cost: Shortens hospital stay and reduces need for incubators.
7.
List six (6) clinical features of puerperal psychosis. (3 marks)
Show Answer

Clinical Features:

  1. Confusion/Delirium: Disorientation to time/place.
  2. Hallucinations: Usually auditory (hearing voices) or visual.
  3. Delusions: False beliefs (e.g., baby is dead or evil).
  4. Severe Mood Swings: Mania (hyperactivity) alternating with deep depression.
  5. Insomnia: Inability to sleep even when baby sleeps.
  6. Rejection of Infant: Refusal to feed or hold the baby, or thoughts of harming the baby.
8.
State the criteria of lactational amenorrhea method of family planning. (3 marks)
Show Answer

LAM Criteria (Triad):

  1. Amenorrhea: The mother's menses must not have returned.
  2. Exclusive Breastfeeding: The baby must be exclusively breastfed on demand, day and night, with no other food or liquids.
  3. Infant Age: The baby must be less than 6 months old.
9.
In the year 2010, one hospital had 1300 live births, 200 still births and 200 perinatal deaths. Using the above data calculate the Perinatal mortality rates of hospital. (2 marks)
Show Answer

Calculation:

Formula: Perinatal Mortality Rate (PMR) = (Stillbirths + Early Neonatal Deaths) ÷ (Total Births [Live + Still]) × 1000

Given Data:
- Live Births = 1300
- Still Births = 200
- Perinatal Deaths (Total) = 200 (Note: If Perinatal Deaths = SB + NND, and SB is 200, this implies 0 NND, or "200 perinatal deaths" refers specifically to the Early Neonatal Deaths. Assuming standard exam question structure, "200 perinatal deaths" likely refers to Early Neonatal Deaths).

Assumption 1 (200 = NND):
Total Deaths = 200 (SB) + 200 (NND) = 400
Total Births = 1300 + 200 = 1500
PMR = (400 ÷ 1500) × 1000 = 266.7 per 1000

Assumption 2 (200 = Total Perinatal Deaths):
Total Deaths = 200
Total Births = 1500
PMR = (200 ÷ 1500) × 1000 = 133.3 per 1000

Note: In NCK exams, carefully read if "Perinatal Deaths" implies the sum or the NND component. Usually, listed items are separate components.

Part III: Essay/Long Answer Questions

1.
Mrs. K para 0 + 0 gravida 1 comes for her first focused antenatal clinic (FANC) at 20 weeks gestation.

a) State three (3) aims of FANC. (3 marks)

Show Answer

Aims of Focused Antenatal Care:

  1. Early detection and treatment of complications/existing diseases.
  2. Prevention of diseases (e.g., Tetanus, Malaria, Anemia, HIV).
  3. Birth Preparedness and Complication Readiness (BP/CR) planning.
  4. Health education and counseling.

b) Describe the specific information you would share with Mrs. K. to assist her develop a quality individual birth plan. (12 marks)

Show Answer

Components of Individual Birth Plan:

  1. Place of Delivery: Identify a health facility for delivery and have a backup option.
  2. Birth Attendant: Identify the skilled attendant (Midwife/Doctor).
  3. Transport: Plan for transport (vehicle, money for fare) for both normal delivery and emergency referral.
  4. Finances: Save money for delivery costs, supplies, and emergencies.
  5. Blood Donor: Identify potential blood donors in case of hemorrhage.
  6. Companion: Choose a birth companion/support person.
  7. Supplies: Prepare necessary items (mother/baby clothes, pads, basin, soap).
  8. Emergency Decision Maker: Identify who will make decisions if the mother is incapacitated.
  9. Care of Home/Children: Plan for who will look after the home while she is away.

c) State five (5) blood investigations to be done on Mrs. K. (5 marks)

Show Answer

Investigations:

  1. Hemoglobin (Hb): To screen for anemia.
  2. Blood Group & Rhesus Factor: To anticipate incompatibility.
  3. VDRL/RPR: Screening for Syphilis.
  4. HIV Test: Screening for PMTCT.
  5. Blood Sugar (RBS): Screening for gestational diabetes (if indicated).
  6. Malaria Slide (BS): If symptomatic or in endemic zone.
2.
Baby K born at 32 weeks gestation is admitted to new born unit immediately after delivery with 9/10 at one minute. Four hours later baby K develops respiratory difficulties and a diagnosis of respiratory distress syndrome (RDS) is made.

a) Explain the three (3) cardinal signs of RDS. (6 marks)

Show Answer

Cardinal Signs:

  1. Tachypnea: Respiratory rate > 60 breaths/minute (attempting to increase oxygen intake).
  2. Expiratory Grunting: An audible sound made on expiration as the baby tries to close the glottis to maintain positive pressure in the alveoli (prevention of collapse).
  3. Chest Retractions/Recession: Sternal and intercostal indrawing due to the high negative pressure required to inflate the stiff, surfactant-deficient lungs. (Nasal flaring is also a sign).

b) Describe the specific management of baby K until stable. (10 marks)

Show Answer

Management:

  1. Respiratory Support:
    • Administer Oxygen (via nasal prongs or CPAP) to maintain saturation >90%.
    • Consider Surfactant replacement therapy if severe.
    • Position with neck slightly extended to open airway.
  2. Thermoregulation:
    • Nurse in an incubator to maintain neutral thermal environment (33-34°C).
    • Hypothermia worsens RDS by increasing oxygen consumption.
  3. Fluids & Nutrition:
    • Start IV fluids (10% Dextrose) to maintain blood sugar and hydration (oral feeding is contraindicated in severe RDS).
    • Monitor inputs and outputs strictly.
  4. Infection Prevention:
    • Administer prophylactic antibiotics (Penicillin/Gentamicin) as pneumonia can mimic RDS.
    • Strict aseptic technique.
  5. Monitoring:
    • Continuous monitoring of Vital Signs (RR, HR, Temp, O2 Sats).
    • Blood Gas Analysis (if available).
  6. Minimal Handling: Cluster care to reduce stress and oxygen consumption.

c) Outline four (4) ways of preventing RDS. (4 marks)

Show Answer

Prevention:

  1. Antenatal Corticosteroids: Administer Dexamethasone to mothers in threatened preterm labor (24-34 weeks) to accelerate fetal lung maturity.
  2. Prevention of Preterm Birth: Management of risk factors (e.g., UTIs, cervical incompetence).
  3. Management of Maternal Diabetes: Good glucose control reduces risk of delayed lung maturation.
  4. Prevention of Perinatal Asphyxia: Asphyxia inhibits surfactant production.
