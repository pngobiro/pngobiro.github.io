Part I: Multiple Choice Questions

20 Marks
1.
Cross immunity can be define as:-
a) The immunity level that is present in a population group.
b) Long term and sometimes life-long resistance that is acquired either naturally or artificially.
c) Short-term resistance that is acquired either naturally or artificially.
d) A situation in which a person’s immunity to one agent provides immunity to another related agent.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) A situation in which a person’s immunity to one agent provides immunity to another related agent.

Explanation: Cross immunity occurs when an immune response to one pathogen (e.g., Cowpox) provides protection against a different but antigenically related pathogen (e.g., Smallpox).

2.
Ergonomic factors include:-
a) The responses and behavior that workers exhibit on the job.
b) Living organism found in the work environment.
c) All the interactions between the worker, the demands of the job, the work setting and the environment.
d) Structural elements of the workplace that influence worker health and productivity.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) All the interactions between the worker, the demands of the job, the work setting and the environment.

Explanation: Ergonomics (human factors) is the scientific discipline concerned with the understanding of interactions among humans and other elements of a system, and the profession that applies theory, principles, data, and methods to design in order to optimize human well-being and overall system performance.

3.
A child growing within normal developmental milestones will be able to have good head control at the age of:
a) 3-6 months.
b) 6-9 months.
c) 12-18 months.
d) 20-24 months
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) 3-6 months.

Explanation: An infant typically achieves full head control (no head lag) by 3-4 months. By 6 months, head control is well established. Therefore, the 3-6 months range is the correct window.

4.
Campylobacter jejuni:-
a) Is isolated less frequently from infants and young adults.
b) Grows best in an environment of less oxygen than the amount in the atmosphere.
c) Tolerates drying.
d) Infections always occur as outbreaks.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Grows best in an environment of less oxygen than the amount in the atmosphere.

Explanation: Campylobacter jejuni is microaerophilic, meaning it requires reduced oxygen levels (about 5%) to grow compared to atmospheric levels (21%). It does not tolerate drying well and infections are usually sporadic rather than outbreaks.

5.
The clinical picture of trachoma in stage four would be:-
a) Trichiasis and follicles.
b) Pannus and follicles.
c) Entroplon and scars.
d) Entroplon and Trichiasis.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Entroplon and Trichiasis.

Explanation: Trachoma progresses from active infection (follicles/inflammation) to scarring (cicatrization). Stage IV (in classic classifications) or the "Trachomatous Trichiasis (TT)" stage involves the inturning of eyelashes (Trichiasis) and eyelids (Entropion) caused by severe scarring, which rubs against the cornea leading to blindness.

6.
Familiarization assessment as a type of community needs assessment involves:-
a) The researcher focusing on single problem and studies the community in terms of that problem.
b) Examining a single facet of community life.
c) An in-depth survey of the entire community.
d) Studying data already available in the community and gathering a certain amount of first hand data.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Studying data already available in the community and gathering a certain amount of first hand data.

Explanation: Familiarization (or "Windshield Survey") is a preliminary assessment that involves reviewing existing records and walking/driving through the community to get a general overview, rather than a comprehensive in-depth survey.

7.
The direct health effects of ozone depletion include:-
a) Increased risk for skin cancer and breast cancer.
b) Increased risk of skin cancer and cataracts.
c) Increased risks of cataracts and exposure to vector borne diseases.
d) Increased population exposure to vector borne diseases and increased risk for skin cancer and cataracts.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Increased population exposure to vector borne diseases and increased risk for skin cancer and cataracts.

Explanation: Ozone depletion increases UV radiation reaching Earth. Direct effects include skin cancers and cataracts. Indirectly, climate changes associated with atmospheric changes can alter vector habitats, potentially increasing vector-borne diseases. Option D covers the broadest range of associated risks.

8.
The vaccine that is heat stable not destroyed rapidly at room temperature and remains active for atleast 6 weeks at 37degrees c is:-
a) Pentavalent.
b) BCG
c) Tetanus toxoid
d) Measles.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Tetanus toxoid

Explanation: Tetanus Toxoid (TT) is one of the most heat-stable vaccines. It can withstand room temperature for months. Measles and OPV are very heat sensitive. BCG is also sensitive to heat and light (especially after reconstitution).

9.
the number of deaths from all causes during a given year divided by the population estimated at midyear times a thousand is referred to as the:-
a) Crude death rate.
b) Age specific death rate.
c) Cause-specific death rate.
d) Case fatality ratio.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Crude death rate.

Explanation: This is the standard definition of Crude Death Rate (CDR). It is "crude" because it doesn't account for age or cause structure.

10.
In the treatment of tuberculosis, the “DOTS” approach refers to:-
a) Patient getting TB treatment as close to home as possible.
b) Patient adheres to treatment during the intensive phase only.
c) Directly observed treatment with weekly monitoring.
d) Patient takes every drug dosage under supervision during the intensive phase.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Patient takes every drug dosage under supervision during the intensive phase.

Explanation: DOTS stands for Directly Observed Treatment, Short-course. A core component is ensuring the patient swallows the medication under the direct supervision of a health worker or trained supporter, especially during the initial intensive phase.

11.
The following is an adverse effect of Nevirapine:-
a) Bone marrow suppression
b) Hepatotoxicity.
c) Peripheral neuropathy.
d) Gastro-intestinal intolerance.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Hepatotoxicity.

Explanation: The major adverse effects of Nevirapine (NVP) are severe skin rashes (Stevens-Johnson syndrome) and hepatotoxicity (liver damage). Bone marrow suppression is associated with Zidovudine (AZT).

12.
When health care is geographically, financially and culturally within reach of the entire community, the service is said to be:-
a) Available
b) Acceptable.
c) Accessible.
d) Affordable.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Accessible.

Explanation: Accessibility encompasses geographical reach, financial affordability, and cultural acceptability. It means the services are effectively within reach of the users.

13.
The habit disorders you can discover while assessing the psychological aspect of school children include:-
a) Temper tantrums, thumb sucking.
b) Thumb sucking, bedwetting
c) Temper tantrums, delusions.
d) Bedwetting, hallucinations.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Thumb sucking, bedwetting

Explanation: Habit disorders in children include repetitive behaviors like thumb sucking, nail biting, and enuresis (bedwetting). Delusions and hallucinations are psychotic symptoms. Temper tantrums are often behavioral/emotional outbursts rather than simple habit disorders.

14.
In a community, evidence of continuity of quality health care includes:-
a) Lower all-cause mortality, better access to care.
b) Better access to care, increased uptake of disease-focused preventive care.
c) Lower all-cause mortality, increased satisfaction with services.
d) Better access to care, increased satisfaction with services.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Lower all-cause mortality, increased satisfaction with services.

Explanation: Continuity of care is linked to better health outcomes (lower mortality), better preventive care uptake, and higher patient satisfaction. Option C combines a hard outcome (mortality) with a patient-centered outcome (satisfaction).

15.
The purpose of a screening test is to:-
a) Diagnose persons with the disease.
b) Collect data for a mass campaign.
c) Check whether a population is at risk of a disease.
d) Detect person with risk factors or subclinical disease.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Detect person with risk factors or subclinical disease.

Explanation: Screening is not diagnostic (Option A). Its purpose is to identify apparently healthy individuals who may have the disease (subclinical) or risk factors, so they can be referred for diagnosis and treatment.

16.
One of the targets for improved maternal health under the Millennium Development Goals (MDG) is to reduce by:-
a) Half maternal ratio.
b) Two thirds maternal mortality ratio.
c) A quarter maternal mortality ratio.
d) Three quarter maternal ratio.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Three quarter maternal ratio. (Specifically 75%)

Explanation: MDG Goal 5 Target 5.A was to reduce by three quarters (75%) the maternal mortality ratio between 1990 and 2015.

17.
An assessment of family health will be most accurate if it incorporates the following guidelines:-
a) Focusing on the family as a total unit, collection of data once.
b) Collecting of data once, exercising professional judgment.
c) Focusing on the family as a total unit, combining quantitative and qualitative data.
d) Using quantitative data, exercising professional judgment.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Focusing on the family as a total unit, combining quantitative and qualitative data.

Explanation: Family health assessment requires a holistic view (family as a unit, not just individuals) and a mix of data types (objective numbers and subjective experiences/qualitative data) to be accurate and comprehensive.

18.
A major cause of “missed opportunity” in the immunization programme is:-
a) Failure of the cord chain.
b) Vaccine stock outs.
c) Use of vaccines that have lost potency.
d) High rates of drop outs among target group.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Vaccine stock outs.

Explanation: A "missed opportunity" occurs when a client is eligible for vaccination and is present at a health facility but does not receive the vaccine. Vaccine stock outs are a major system failure that leads to these missed opportunities.

19.
The methods commonly used in Africa to measure population include:-
a) Census and civil registration.
b) Census and special surveys.
c) Civil registration and special surveys.
d) Census and ethnographic surveys.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Census and special surveys.

Explanation: In many African countries, Civil Registration Systems (births/deaths) are incomplete. Therefore, countries rely heavily on the periodic Census and special surveys like the Demographic and Health Surveys (DHS) for population data.

20.
Non-organic growth failure in a child may be attributed to:-
a) Altered physiology due to improper feeding.
b) Congenital disorders of metabolism.
c) High incidence of child abuse.
d) A poor mother-child bond of attachment.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) A poor mother-child bond of attachment.

Explanation: Non-organic failure to thrive (NOFTT) is caused by environmental or psychosocial factors rather than medical/biological causes. A lack of emotional bonding or maternal deprivation is a classic cause. Options A and B are organic causes.

Part II: Short Answer Questions

20 Marks
1.
State five factors that influence acceptance and continuation of family planning.
Show Answer

Answer:

  1. Education Level: Higher literacy often correlates with better understanding and acceptance of FP.
  2. Religious Beliefs: Some denominations discourage or forbid artificial contraception.
  3. Cultural Norms: Traditions valuing large families or male dominance in decision-making can hinder acceptance.
  4. Accessibility and Cost: Ease of reaching clinics and affordability of commodities.
  5. Fear of Side Effects: Myths or previous negative experiences with side effects (e.g., weight gain, bleeding).
  6. Partner Support: Approval and communication between spouses significantly affect continuation.
2.
Explain three sources of information for epidemiological studies.
Show Answer

Answer:

  1. Census Data: Provides the denominator data (population size, age, sex distribution) essential for calculating rates.
  2. Vital Statistics (Civil Registration): Records of births, deaths (including cause of death), and marriages, which help track mortality and fertility trends.
  3. Health Facility Records: Hospital and clinic registers (morbidity data) provide information on disease prevalence among those seeking care.
  4. Disease Notification Systems: Mandatory reporting of specific infectious diseases (e.g., cholera, measles) to health authorities.
3.
Write the scientific names of organisms that cause:-
Show Answer

Answer:

  • a) Visceral elephantiasis: (Note: Likely typo in exam paper for Visceral Leishmaniasis) - Leishmania donovani.
  • b) Elephantiasis (Lymphatic Filariasis): Wuchereria bancrofti.
  • c) Cholera: Vibrio cholerae.

4.
Outline the six steps of problem solving process in sequential order.
Show Answer

Answer:

  1. Identify and Define the Problem: Recognizing that a problem exists and clearly stating it.
  2. Analyze the Problem: Gathering data to understand the root causes and magnitude.
  3. Generate Possible Solutions: Brainstorming various alternative courses of action.
  4. Select the Best Solution: Evaluating alternatives and choosing the most feasible/effective one.
  5. Implement the Solution: Putting the plan into action.
  6. Evaluate the Outcome: Assessing whether the solution solved the problem and identifying lessons learned.
5.
Explain the following elements of a disaster management policy.
Show Answer

a) Prevention:

Activities designed to provide permanent protection from disasters. It includes engineering and other physical protective measures (like building dams for flood control) and legislative measures (like land-use planning) to prevent hazards from turning into disasters.

b) Mitigation:

Measures taken to minimize the destructive and disruptive effects of hazards and thus lessen the scale of a possible disaster. This ranges from physical measures (strengthening buildings) to education and training to reduce vulnerability.
