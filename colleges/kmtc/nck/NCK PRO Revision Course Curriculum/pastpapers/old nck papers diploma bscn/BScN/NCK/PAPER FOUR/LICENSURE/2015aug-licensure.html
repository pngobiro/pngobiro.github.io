PART I: MULTIPLE CHOICE QUESTIONS

20 Marks
1.
Organizational structure primarily refers to:-
a) How activities are coordinated and controlled.
b) How resources are allocated.
c) The location of departments and offices.
d) The policy statement developed by the organization.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) How activities are coordinated and controlled.

Explanation: Organizational structure defines how tasks are formally divided, grouped, and coordinated. It determines the hierarchy, reporting relationships, and communication channels.

2.
Together, authority and responsibility are:-
a) Supplementary.
b) Complementary.
c) Contradictory.
d) The same.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Complementary.

Explanation: According to Fayol's principles of management, authority and responsibility must go hand in hand (parity of authority and responsibility). They complement each other; one cannot effectively exist without the other.

3.
The first step in decision making process is:-
a) Analyzing the problem.
b) Analyzing alternatives.
c) Identifying the problem.
d) Allocating weights to the criteria.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Identifying the problem.

Explanation: You cannot make a decision unless you know what the issue is. The process typically starts with defining or identifying the problem, followed by gathering information, analyzing alternatives, and making a choice.

4.
The following are characteristics of planned change:-
a) It is sudden.
b) It is deliberate.
c) It is not collaborative.
d) It always works towards the betterment of employees.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) It is deliberate.

Explanation: Planned change is a purposeful, calculated, and collaborative effort to bring about improvements in a system, as opposed to accidental or sudden (drift) change.

5.
Situational leadership theory:-
a) Embodies the idea that the leader directs goal-oriented behaviour.
b) Explains leadership through taking into account forces that occur in the situation, the leader and the followers.
c) Presents supportive evidence collected in organizations.
d) Suggests that a great leader will arise when controlled with appropriate situation.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Explains leadership through taking into account forces that occur in the situation, the leader and the followers.

Explanation: Situational leadership theories (like Hersey and Blanchard) propose that there is no single "best" style of leadership. Effective leadership is task-relevant, and the most successful leaders are those who adapt their leadership style to the performance readiness (ability and willingness) of the individual or group they are attempting to lead or influence.

6.
The following is an appropriate method that can be used to teach acquisition of psychomotor skills:-
a) Case studies.
b) Demonstration.
c) Micro teaching.
d) Brain storming.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Demonstration.

Explanation: Psychomotor skills involve physical movement, coordination, and the use of the motor-skill areas. Demonstration (showing how to do it) followed by return demonstration (practice) is the most effective method for teaching these skills.

7.
Negative reinforcement:-
a) Is an attempt to decrease the likelihood of behaviour occurring again.
b) Is the same as punishment.
c) Involves giving a reward to strengthen a response.
d) Involves the removal of unfavorable event in order to strengthen a response.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Involves the removal of unfavorable event in order to strengthen a response.

Explanation: In operant conditioning, negative reinforcement strengthens a behavior by removing an aversive (unpleasant) stimulus when the behavior occurs. Punishment (b) weakens behavior. Positive reinforcement (c) adds a pleasant stimulus.

8.
Summative assessment:-
a) Allows for fine tuning of instruction.
b) Provides ongoing assessment.
c) Seeks to monitor educational outcomes.
d) Identifies and remediates individual deficiencies.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Seeks to monitor educational outcomes.

Explanation: Summative assessment evaluates student learning at the end of an instructional unit by comparing it against some standard or benchmark (monitoring the final outcome). Options a, b, and d describe formative assessment.

9.
Educational objectives have been divided into:-
a) Two domains.
b) Three domains.
c) Four domains.
d) Five domains.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Three domains.

Explanation: Benjamin Bloom classified educational objectives into three domains: Cognitive (knowledge), Affective (attitude), and Psychomotor (skills).

10.
The documents that stipulates the details of content to be taught is:-
a) Curriculum.
b) Scheme of work.
c) Syllabus.
d) Lesson plan.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Syllabus.

Explanation: A syllabus is a document that outlines the specific topics and content to be covered in a course. A scheme of work breaks this down into a schedule, and a lesson plan details a single session.

11.
Which of the following is the primary source of data for the client's database:-
a) Nurses recording of health history.
b) Recent clinic or hospital records.
c) Physician, history and physical client.
d) Client.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Client.

Explanation: The client is always the primary source of data. All other sources (family, records, other health professionals) are considered secondary sources.

12.
Which of the following reasons is the most important, as well as the most widely accepted, reason for nurses using nursing process:-
a) Increase the unique body of knowledge known as nursing.
b) Help clients meet their actual and potential health problems.
c) Communicate with other members of the team.
d) Standardise the care of clients with the same diagnoses.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Standardise the care of clients with the same diagnoses.

Explanation: While the goal is helping clients (b), the reason for using the process itself is to provide a systematic, organized method that standardizes practice, ensuring consistency and quality of care while allowing for individualization.

13.
The singleness of purpose that makes possible the creation of one plan of action to guide managers in resource allocations is:-
a) Unity of direction.
b) Unity of command.
c) Unity of authority.
d) Unity of resources.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Unity of direction.

Explanation: Fayol's principle of Unity of Direction states that activities with the same objective should be directed by one manager using one plan.

14.
A reporting relationship in which an employee receives orders from, and reports to, only one supervisor is known as:-
a) Line of authority.
b) Centralisation.
c) Unity of direction.
d) Unity of command.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Unity of command.

Explanation: Unity of Command is the principle that an employee should report to no more than one manager to avoid conflicting instructions.

15.
Fayol's theory of equity specifies that:-
a) Employees should be paid equally regardless of their positions.
b) Managers should be treated equally.
c) Employees should be treated with respect and justice.
d) People should get equal pay for equal work.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Employees should be treated with respect and justice.

Explanation: Fayol's principle of Equity implies kindness and justice in dealing with employees to elicit loyalty and devotion. It does not necessarily mean equal pay for everyone (which would be unfair to those with more responsibility).

16.
Crime is always related to psychopathology.
True / False
Show Answer

Correct Answer: False.

Explanation: While some crimes are committed by individuals with mental illness, the vast majority of crimes are committed by individuals without any diagnosable psychopathology. Crime can be driven by social, economic, or situational factors.

17.
Mentally ill patients can be used in research.
True / False
Show Answer

Correct Answer: True.

Explanation: Mentally ill patients can participate in research, but they are considered a vulnerable population. Strict ethical guidelines, including obtaining informed consent (or consent from a legal guardian if capacity is impaired) and ensuring the research has potential benefits, must be followed.

Match the personality disorders in column A with the correct description in column B.
Column A Column B

ii) Dependent personality disorder e) Pervasive and excessive need to be cared for.

19.
Rattling of leaves interpreted as a reproaching voice is an example of:-
a) Hallucination.
b) Delusion.
c) Illusion.
d) Ideas of reference.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Illusion.

Explanation: An illusion is a misinterpretation of a real external sensory stimulus (in this case, the sound of leaves). A hallucination occurs without any external stimulus.

20.
A common manageable side effect of neuroleptics is:-
a) Ptsosis.
b) Jaundice.
c) Melanocytosis.
d) Unintentional tremors.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Unintentional tremors.

Explanation: Unintentional tremors are part of Extrapyramidal Side Effects (EPS), specifically pseudo-parkinsonism, which are common and manageable side effects of antipsychotic (neuroleptic) medications, often treated with anticholinergics.

PART II: SHORT ANSWER QUESTIONS

40 Marks
1.
Differentiate between the following terms:-

i) Obsession and compulsion (2 marks):

  • Obsession: Recurrent, persistent, intrusive, and unwanted thoughts, images, or impulses that cause anxiety or distress.
  • Compulsion: Repetitive behaviors (e.g., hand washing) or mental acts (e.g., counting) that the individual feels driven to perform in response to an obsession, aimed at preventing or reducing anxiety.

ii) Dependent variable and independent variable (2 marks):

  • Independent Variable: The variable that is manipulated or changed by the researcher to observe its effect (the cause).
  • Dependent Variable: The variable that is observed and measured for change in response to the manipulation of the independent variable (the effect).

iii) Leadership and management (2 marks):

  • Leadership: The ability to inspire and influence others to achieve a common vision or goal. It focuses on change, vision, and people.
  • Management: The process of planning, organizing, directing, and controlling resources to achieve organizational objectives efficiently. It focuses on order, consistency, and tasks.

iv) Curriculum and syllabus (2 marks):

  • Curriculum: The overall content, learning experiences, and goals of an educational program. It is broad and philosophical.
  • Syllabus: A document that outlines the specific subjects, topics, and schedule for a particular course. It is a subset of the curriculum.

State four (4) functions of the Kenya Board of Mental Health. (4 marks)

3.
Explain four (4) types of nursing care delivery systems. (8 marks)
Show Answer
  1. Functional Nursing: A task-oriented method where nurses are assigned specific tasks (e.g., medication nurse, treatment nurse) for all patients on a ward.
  2. Team Nursing: A group of nursing staff (RNs, LPNs, aides) works together under the leadership of a team leader to provide care for a group of patients.
  3. Primary Nursing: One nurse (Primary Nurse) has 24-hour responsibility for the plan of care for a specific group of patients from admission to discharge. Associate nurses provide care when the primary nurse is off.
  4. Case Management: A collaborative process of assessment, planning, facilitation, and advocacy for options and services to meet an individualâ€™s health needs, often focusing on cost-effective outcomes for specific diagnoses.
  5. Patient-Centered Care: Care is organized around the patient's needs and preferences, often involving cross-training of staff to perform multiple functions at the bedside.
4.
State six (6) roles of nurse managers. (6 marks)
Show Answer
  1. Planning: Setting goals and determining the course of action.
  2. Organizing: Allocating resources and assigning tasks.
  3. Staffing: Recruiting, hiring, and developing personnel.
  4. Directing/Leading: Motivating and guiding staff.
  5. Controlling: Monitoring performance and taking corrective action.
  6. Budgeting: Managing financial resources.
  7. Conflict Resolution: Mediating disputes among staff.
5.
Outline four (4) barriers to communication in a classroom and state one way of eliminating each. (8 marks)
Show Answer
  1. Barrier: Noise/Distractions (external noise, construction).
    Elimination: Close windows/doors or move to a quieter room.
  2. Barrier: Language Barrier (teacher using complex jargon).
    Elimination: Use simple, clear language appropriate for the learners' level.
  3. Barrier: Physiological Discomfort (hunger, fatigue, poor ventilation).
    Elimination: Ensure proper ventilation/lighting and schedule breaks.
  4. Barrier: Psychological Barriers (anxiety, lack of interest).
    Elimination: Establish a supportive environment and use engaging teaching methods.
  5. Barrier: Poor Teaching Aids (unclear slides, small writing).
    Elimination: Prepare clear, visible, and audible audio-visual aids.

  3. Rehabilitation: Helping clients regain skills for daily living and social functioning.

List four (4) indications for electro-convulsive therapy in the treatment of mental illness. (2 marks)

PART III: ESSAY/LONG ANSWER QUESTIONS

40 Marks
1.
Malaria is a common health problem in community X where you are working as a community health nurse.

a) Explain classification of malaria endemicity. (4 marks)

Malaria endemicity is classified based on the spleen rate or parasite rate in children aged 2-9 years:

  1. Holoendemic: Intense transmission occurs year-round. Spleen rate > 75% in children. Immunity is high in adults.
  2. Hyperendemic: Intense but seasonal transmission. Spleen rate 50-75%. Disease affects all ages but severe in children.
  3. Mesoendemic: Regular transmission. Spleen rate 11-50%. Some variation in transmission intensity.
  4. Hypoendemic: Low transmission. Spleen rate < 10%. Population has little immunity; risk of epidemics.

b) List eight (8) clinical pictures of severe, complicated malaria. (4 marks)

  1. Cerebral Malaria: Unarousable coma/impaired consciousness.
  2. Severe Anemia: Hb < 5 g/dl.
  3. Renal Failure: Oliguria or high creatinine.
  4. Hypoglycemia: Blood glucose < 2.2 mmol/L.
  5. Pulmonary Edema/ARDS: Difficulty breathing.
  6. Shock (Algid Malaria): Low blood pressure/circulatory collapse.
  7. Spontaneous Bleeding: Gum bleeding, DIC.
  8. Acidosis: Respiratory distress (deep breathing).
  9. Hyperpyrexia: Temperature > 40Â°C.

c) Describe the prevention and control of malaria. (12 marks)

  1. Vector Control:
    • Insecticide Treated Nets (ITNs): Use of LLINs to prevent mosquito bites during sleep.
    • Indoor Residual Spraying (IRS): Spraying walls to kill resting mosquitoes.
    • Larval Source Management: Draining stagnant water, clearing bushes around houses (environmental modification).
  2. Chemoprophylaxis:
    • Administration of antimalarials to vulnerable groups (e.g., travelers) to prevent infection.
    • Intermittent Preventive Treatment in Pregnancy (IPTp): Giving Sulfadoxine-Pyrimethamine (SP) to pregnant women.
  3. Case Management:
    • Prompt Diagnosis: Testing all fever cases with Microscopy or RDT.
    • Effective Treatment: Treating uncomplicated malaria with Artemisinin-based Combination Therapy (ACTs) and severe malaria with IV Artesunate.
  4. Health Education:
    • Educating the community on the importance of net use, clearing breeding sites, and seeking early treatment for fever.
2.
School Health Services are health promotion activities in the school environment targeting school going children.

a) State seven (7) objectives of School Health Program. (7 marks)

  1. To promote health and prevent disease among school children.
  2. To identify and treat health conditions early (screening).
  3. To provide a safe and healthy school environment (water, sanitation).
  4. To provide health education to pupils, teachers, and parents.
  5. To ensure proper nutrition through school feeding programs.
  6. To provide counseling and guidance services.
  7. To care for children with special needs/disabilities.

b) Using the nursing process describe how you can organize for a School Health Service. (13 marks)

  • Assessment:
    • Conduct a situational analysis of the school (population, facilities).
    • Identify health needs (common illnesses, nutritional status).
    • Assess available resources (first aid kits, nearby clinics).
    • Engage stakeholders (teachers, parents).
  • Diagnosis:
    • Formulate community diagnoses based on assessment (e.g., "High prevalence of dental caries" or "Risk of waterborne diseases due to poor sanitation").
  • Planning:
    • Set specific, measurable objectives (e.g., "To deworm 100% of pupils by end of term").
    • Develop a schedule for visits/activities.
    • Mobilize resources (dewormers, educational materials).
    • Assign roles to the health team and teachers.
  • Implementation:
    • Carry out planned activities:
      • Health education sessions on hygiene.
      • Screening (vision, hearing, dental).
      • Administering treatments (deworming, Vitamin A).
      • Inspection of school environment (latrines, kitchen).
  • Evaluation:
    • Measure outcomes against objectives (e.g., number of children treated).
    • Assess impact (reduction in absenteeism).
    • Write a report and give feedback to school administration and health authorities.
    • Plan for follow-up.
