Part I: Multiple Choice Questions

20 Marks
1.
In Primary Health Care, fair and responsible distribution of available resource to all individuals and families to enable them meet their fundamental and basic needs are the principle of:
a) Affordability
b) Equity
c) Accessibility
d) Acceptability
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Equity

Explanation: Equity in health care means that resources are distributed fairly according to need, ensuring that everyone has an opportunity to attain their full health potential. It addresses the "fair and responsible distribution" mentioned in the question.

2.
One of the fundamentals of Primary Health Care is:
a) Address the main health problem in the community, providing curative, preventive and rehabilitative services accordingly
b) The health services and structures are easily available to the community members
c) The services are geographically and culturally within easy reach to the whole community
d) provide services at a cost that the community can afford and maintain at every stage of their development
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) provide services at a cost that the community can afford and maintain at every stage of their development

Explanation: This specifically defines the principle of Affordability within Primary Health Care. The service must be sustainable and affordable for both the community and the country.

3.
The single most important infection-prevention procedure in family planning service is:
a) Decontaminating reusable instruments by soaking in 0.5% chlorine solution
b) Wearing a new pair of gloves for each vaginal examination
c) Washing hands with soap and clean running water before and after contact with each client
d) Cleaning the skin of the client appropriately with antiseptic before an injection
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Washing hands with soap and clean running water before and after contact with each client

Explanation: Hand washing is universally recognized as the single most effective way to prevent the spread of infections in any healthcare setting.

4.
The instruction a nurse would provide to a breastfeeding mother wishing to use progestin only contraception includes:
a) Take a pill every day for 21 days, rest and restart on the 5th day of menstrual flow
b) If 3hours in taking the pill, take the missed pill as soon as possible, take the next pill at the usual time and use condoms for protection
c) If 3 hours late in taking the pill, take the missed pill as soon as possible and avoid sex for 2days
d) Take the pill at the same time every day, when a packet is empty take the first pill from another packet on the next day
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Take the pill at the same time every day, when a packet is empty take the first pill from another packet on the next day

Explanation: Progestin-only pills (POPs) must be taken continuously without any break between packs to maintain their contraceptive effect. The timing is also critical (taking it at the same time every day).

5.
Indicate whether the following statements are TRUE or FALSE.
Show Answer

a) Tetanus Toxoid (TT3) is given at least 1 year after tetanus toxoid (TT2), or during a subsequent pregnancy: FALSE

Correction: TT3 is given 6 months after TT2. TT4 is given 1 year after TT3.

b) A breastfeeding infant of TB positive mother should be weaned at 6 months and a 2nd dose of BCG given if the first dose failed to take: FALSE

Correction: Infants of TB positive mothers should continue breastfeeding. They are usually given prophylactic Isoniazid. Weaning is not indicated based solely on maternal TB status if she is on treatment.

6.
Indicate whether the following statements are TRUE or FALSE.
Show Answer

a) Hepatitis B vaccine (HBV) should be stored between +0 c and 8 c: FALSE

Correction: It should be stored between +2°C and +8°C. Hepatitis B vaccine is freeze-sensitive and is damaged by freezing (0°C).

b) During national immunization days, children or other eligible people may be given any or all the KEPI vaccines: FALSE

Correction: National Immunization Days (NIDs) typically target specific diseases (usually Polio or Measles) and do not involve administering the entire KEPI schedule.

7.
A child growing within the normal development milestones will attain a weight of 10kg at the age of:
a) 6 months
b) 9 months
c) 12 months
d) 18 months
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) 12 months

Explanation: An average birth weight is about 3.0-3.5 kg. Infants typically double their birth weight by 6 months and triple it by 1 year. 3.5 kg x 3 = 10.5 kg. Thus, 10kg is expected around 12 months.

8.
A grossly handicapped child who is mentally challenged is best cared for in the:
a) Special school
b) Institution
c) Home
d) Community
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Home (or d Community)

Explanation: Modern rehabilitation philosophy (Community Based Rehabilitation) advocates for children with disabilities to be cared for at home and integrated into their community, rather than being isolated in institutions, to promote better social development and acceptance.

9.
One of the responsibilities of a community health nurse in facilitating networking in the community for the care of HIV/AIDS patients is:
a) Lobbying and advocating for the rights of the HIV/AIDS patients in the community
b) Ensuring continuity of care from family level to the individual patient
c) Co-ordinating the exchange of information amongst groups and establishing referral channels
d) Planning and organizing activity into the allocated time schedules for the volunteers
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Co-ordinating the exchange of information amongst groups and establishing referral channels

Explanation: "Networking" involves connecting different entities. Coordinating information exchange and referrals between various support groups, NGOs, and health facilities is the core of networking.

10.
The most effective method of finding sub-clinical cases of a disease in the community is:
a) Contact tracing
b) Isolation of cases
c) Surveillance of contacts
d) Notification and reports
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Contact tracing

Explanation: Sub-clinical cases (asymptomatic) do not come to the hospital. They are best found by actively tracing and screening the contacts of known clinical cases.

11.
One of the factors that increase the susceptibility to HIV infection among uncircumcised men in Kenya is:
a) Hereditary
b) Socio-cultural
c) Anatomical-physiologic
d) Geographical location
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Anatomical-physiologic

Explanation: The inner surface of the foreskin contains a high density of Langerhans cells (target cells for HIV) and provides a moist environment that favors the survival of the virus, making uncircumcised men more biologically susceptible to infection.

12.
Some of the factors that affect the prevalence of a disease are:
a) Change in incidence of a disease, out migration, severity of the disease
b) Loss of track of some cases, death of patients, population
c) Severity of the disease, migration into health centers, population, when some patients are cured
d) Incidence of disease, loss of track of some cases, when some patients get cured
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Severity of the disease, migration into health centers, population, when some patients are cured

Explanation: Prevalence depends on Incidence (new cases) and Duration. Severity affects duration (by death or survival). Cure reduces prevalence. Migration (in or out) changes the number of cases. Option C captures the most relevant determinants.

13.
One of the main purposes of assessing the nutritional status of a community is to:
a) Enable health workers to understand nutrition theory to promote good nutrition in the community
b) Detect the development of clinical malnutrition and prevent morbidity and mortality in the community
c) Mount community wide nutrition education with emphasis on good traditional foods and diets to eliminate the problems of western diets
d) Assess the influence of socio-economic, agricultural and climatic factors on the nutritional status of the people in the area
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Detect the development of clinical malnutrition and prevent morbidity and mortality in the community

Explanation: The primary health goal of assessment is early detection and intervention to prevent negative health outcomes (sickness and death).

14.
The drugs commonly used in the management of Brucellosis include:
a) Tetracycline, streptomycin, albendazole
b) Tetracycline, streptomycin, Clotrimazole
c) Amoxicillin, streptomycin, tetracycline
d) Ampicillin, tetracycline, albendazole
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Tetracycline, streptomycin, Clotrimazole (Possible typo for Cotrimoxazole)

Explanation: The standard treatment for Brucellosis typically involves Doxycycline (a Tetracycline) and Streptomycin. Option B is the best fit despite the likely typo "Clotrimazole" (which is an antifungal). In many older texts, "Cotrimoxazole" (Septrin) is also used for Brucellosis, so "Clotrimazole" may be a typo for "Cotrimoxazole".

15.
Diseases transmitted through faeco-oral route include:
a) Viral hepatitis, amoebiasis, hydatidosis
b) Poliomyelitis, mumps, typhoid
c) Poliomyelitis, bacillary dysentery, viral hepatitis
d) Cholera, schistosomiasis, poliomyelitis
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Poliomyelitis, bacillary dysentery, viral hepatitis

Explanation: Polio, Dysentery, and Viral Hepatitis (Type A/E) are all classically transmitted via the faeco-oral route. Mumps is respiratory; Schistosomiasis is water-contact.

Match the approaches used in studying family in Column A with the best description in Column B.
COLUMN A (Approach) COLUMN B (Description)
a) Developmental approach 5. Describes family as having practicable natural history with changing ages and composition
b) Interactional approach 1. Deals with internal family dynamics
c) Structural-functional approach 2. Family viewed as a social system with family objectives and its internal organizations
d) Institutional approach 4. Studies family function in society as they change over time (Historical)

Correct Matches:

  • Interactional approach: Match 1 (Focuses on internal dynamics/communication).
  • Structural-functional approach: Match 2 (Focuses on the family as a system and its functions).
18.
In syndromic management of sexually transmitted infections, a pregnant woman presenting with vaginal discharge without abdominal pains or tenderness should be treated with 4C's and:
a) Nystatin 1 perssary BID x 5days, metronidazole 2gm stat
b) Clotrimazole 1 tab intravaginally x 6 days
c) Amoxicillin 3gm stat, probenicid 1gm
d) Clotrimazole 1 tab vaginally x 6days, metronidazole 2gm stat
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Clotrimazole 1 tab vaginally x 6days, metronidazole 2gm stat (labeled as second 'b' in paper)

Explanation: Vaginal discharge is typically treated for Candidiasis, Bacterial Vaginosis, and Trichomoniasis. Clotrimazole covers Candida (safe in pregnancy). Metronidazole covers BV and Trichomonas (generally considered safe after 1st trimester). This combination corresponds to the standard "Vaginal Discharge Syndrome" treatment kit.

19.
The community health nurse works with the family because, family:
a) Mediates between the expedition of the society and the need of the individual
b) Serves as a buffer between the society and the individual
c) Is entirely affected by the health of the individual family member
d) Facilitates personality and socio-cultural development of the individual family member
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Is entirely affected by the health of the individual family member

Explanation: In community health nursing, the family is viewed as the unit of service because the health of one member impacts the entire unit, and the family unit is the primary environment for health behavior and care.

20.
One of the secondary prevention objectives in occupational health services is:
a) Preventing and dealing with toxic effects of substances contacted or inhaled
b) Reducing exposure to harmful substances by sucking or blowing away fumes from places where they are produced
c) Screening of the working environment to ascertain that recommended preventive measures are compiled with by employers and employees
d) Wearing protective and safety equipment’s against hazards
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Preventing and dealing with toxic effects of substances contacted or inhaled

Explanation:

  • Primary prevention: Reducing exposure (ventilation - b) and PPE (d).
  • Secondary prevention: Early diagnosis and treatment. "Dealing with toxic effects" (treatment) falls under secondary/tertiary. Option A is the only one mentioning management of effects rather than just prevention of exposure.

Part III: Long Answer Questions

20 Marks
1.
You are a newly posted District Public Health Nurse to KY District and you have identified home visiting as a critical component of community health services in the District.
Show Answer

a) State any 6 principles of home visit (6mrks)

  1. Planned and Purposeful: Visits should have a clear objective based on identified needs.
  2. Regularity: Visits should be regular to ensure continuity of care.
  3. Flexibility: The nurse should be adaptable to the family's situation and schedule.
  4. Mutuality/Involvement: The family must be involved in the planning and care process; it is a partnership.
  5. Scientific: Nursing care should be based on scientific principles and professional practice.
  6. Evaluation: Outcomes of the visit should be evaluated against the set objectives.
  7. Education: Every visit should be used as an opportunity for health education.

b) Describe the challenges of home visit and their possible solutions (14mrks)

  • Rejection by Family: Solution: Build rapport, explain purpose clearly, use community guides/leaders for introduction.
  • Safety/Security Risks: Solution: Visit in pairs, inform colleagues of itinerary, visit during safe hours.
  • Lack of Transport: Solution: Advocate for resources, use locally available transport (motorbikes), plan visits geographically to minimize travel.
  • Time Consuming: Solution: Proper planning and scheduling, prioritizing high-risk families.
  • Lack of Resources/Supplies: Solution: Improvisation using home materials, carrying a well-stocked home visiting bag.
  • Cultural Barriers: Solution: Cultural sensitivity training, involving family decision-makers, respecting customs.
  • Distractions in Home: Solution: Request a quiet area if possible, be patient and adaptable.
2.
Child abuse has become rampant and there is increasing concern among health workers and the public at large.
Show Answer

a) Explain the following four rights of the child as summarized by the United Nations (8mrks)

  • i. The right to live: Every child has the inherent right to life and survival. States must ensure the maximum extent possible the survival and development of the child (healthcare, nutrition, shelter).
  • ii. The right to enjoy parental care: Children have the right to live with their parents unless it is deemed incompatible with their best interests. They have the right to be cared for and guided by their parents/guardians.
  • iii. The right to education: Every child has the right to free and compulsory primary education to develop their personality, talents, and abilities to their fullest potential.
  • iv. The right to a culture: Children from minority or indigenous groups have the right to enjoy their own culture, practice their religion, and use their own language.

b) Describe the role of the community health nurse in the management of child abuse in the community (12mrks)

  1. Identification and Screening: Recognizing signs of physical, emotional, or sexual abuse during clinics, school health programs, or home visits.
  2. Treatment and Care: Providing immediate medical care for injuries and treating STI/preventing pregnancy in cases of sexual abuse.
  3. Reporting/Referral: Mandated to report suspected cases to relevant authorities (Children's Department, Police) and referring for legal/social support.
  4. Counseling and Support: Providing psychological support to the child and non-offending family members to deal with trauma.
  5. Prevention/Education: Educating the community and parents on child rights, positive parenting, and the dangers of abuse.
  6. Advocacy: Speaking up for the rights of children in community forums and policy discussions.
  7. Coordination: Working with social workers, police, and teachers (multidisciplinary team) to ensure the child's safety and rehabilitation.
