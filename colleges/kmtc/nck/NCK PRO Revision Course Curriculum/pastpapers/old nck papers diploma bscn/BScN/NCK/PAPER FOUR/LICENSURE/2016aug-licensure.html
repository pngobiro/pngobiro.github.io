PART I: MULTIPLE CHOICE QUESTIONS

20 Marks
1.
The primary purpose for regulating nursing practice is to protect:-
a) The public.
b) Practicing nurses.
c) The employing agency.
d) Professional standards.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) The public.

Explanation: The primary mandate of nursing regulatory bodies (like the Nursing Council of Kenya) is to protect the public health, safety, and welfare by ensuring that only qualified and competent individuals practice nursing.

2.
To utilize the nursing process, the nurse must first:-
a) Identify goals for nursing care.
b) State the clients nursing needs.
c) Obtain information about the client.
d) Evaluate the effectiveness of nursing actions.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Obtain information about the client.

Explanation: The first step of the nursing process is Assessment, which involves collecting data (obtaining information) about the client. Diagnosis (b), Planning (a), and Evaluation (d) follow subsequently.

3.
Transformational leadership:-
a) Focuses on effecting revolutionary change in the organization.
b) Values, collaborations, teamwork and interpersonal skills.
c) Ensures that individuals share the responsibility for achieving the organizational goals.
d) Originates from a desire to serve.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Focuses on effecting revolutionary change in the organization.

Explanation: Transformational leadership is defined by the ability to inspire followers to achieve extraordinary outcomes and, in the process, develop their own leadership capacity. It often involves effecting significant (revolutionary) change, vision, and empowering others. Option (d) describes Servant Leadership.

4.
The following is an element of classical theory:-
a) Hawthorne effect.
b) Chain of command.
c) Through put.
d) Ebbing effect.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Chain of command.

Explanation: Classical management theory (Fayol, Weber) emphasizes structure, hierarchy, and a clear chain of command. The Hawthorne effect (a) relates to Human Relations theory. Throughput (c) relates to Systems theory.

5.
The following is a reason for planning in management:-
a) To establish a hierarchy in the organization.
b) To provide adequate and appropriate personnel.
c) To motivate staff.
d) To focus attention on objectives.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) To focus attention on objectives.

Explanation: The primary purpose of planning is to define objectives and determine the best course of action to achieve them. It provides direction and focuses the organization's efforts on its goals.

6.
In relation to obtaining an informed consent from a seventeen year old adolescent the nurse should remember that the adolescent:-
a) Does not have the legal capacity to give consent.
b) Is not able to make an acceptable or intelligent choice.
c) Is able to give voluntary consent when parents are not available.
d) Will most likely be unable to choose between alternatives when asked to consent.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Does not have the legal capacity to give consent.

Explanation: Generally, the legal age of majority for consent is 18. A 17-year-old is a minor and legally requires a parent or guardian to provide informed consent, except in specific situations like "mature minor" provisions or emergencies.

7.
Personality theory suggesting that behavior and learning occurs through imitation is:-
a) Humanistic.
b) Psychoanalytic.
c) Cognitive.
d) Social-learning.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Social-learning.

Explanation: Albert Bandura's Social Learning Theory posits that people learn from one another via observation, imitation, and modeling.

8.
The learning where we associate a certain behaviour with a specific outcome is:-
a) Contingent learning.
b) Operant conditioning.
c) Classical conditioning.
d) Observational learning.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Operant conditioning.

Explanation: Operant conditioning (Skinner) involves learning through the consequences of behavior (rewards or punishments), thereby associating a behavior with an outcome. Classical conditioning (Pavlov) associates a stimulus with a response.

10.
An appropriate behavioral objective for a teaching session is:-
a) After a teaching session, the client list five tools in the class of proteins.
b) After a teaching session, the client will have knowledge of foods in a class of proteins.
c) After a teaching session, the client will verbalize understanding of foods in the class of proteins.
d) After a teaching session the client will know which foods are proteineous.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) After a teaching session, the client will verbalize understanding of foods in the class of proteins.

Explanation: While "list" (option a) is generally a better behavioral verb, the content "list five tools" appears to be a typo (should likely be foods). Given the options, "verbalize understanding" (option c) is often accepted in nursing contexts as a measurable action (the client states/explains). Options b and d use "have knowledge" and "know," which are non-measurable internal states and thus poor behavioral objectives.

11.
When providing group therapy, the nurse must focus on:-
a) Jointly experienced stress.
b) Behaviour of individual members.
c) Confrontation between members.
d) Personal feelings affecting behaviour.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Behaviour of individual members.

Note: In group therapy, the focus can be complex. However, monitoring the behavior of members and the group dynamics (interactions) is key. Some sources might argue for "Confrontation" as a therapeutic tool, but the nurse's *focus* is often facilitating the process and observing behavior.

12.

b) Psychodrama.
c) Group therapy.
d) Family therapy.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Play therapy.

Explanation: Preschool children often lack the verbal skills to articulate trauma. Play therapy allows them to express feelings, act out experiences, and resolve conflicts in a safe environment.

PART II: SHORT ANSWER QUESTIONS

40 Marks
1.
Differentiate between the following terms.

i) Formal leadership and informal leadership (2 marks):

  • Formal Leadership: Leadership based on a formal position or authority within an organization (e.g., manager, supervisor). The leader has official power and designated responsibilities.
  • Informal Leadership: Leadership that arises from influence, respect, or expertise rather than an official title. A person leads because others choose to follow them due to their charisma or knowledge.

ii) Homogeneous grouping and mainstreamed grouping in teaching (2 marks):

  • Homogeneous Grouping: Placing students with similar abilities, skills, or needs in the same group for instruction.
  • Mainstreamed Grouping (Heterogeneous): Including students with special needs or different ability levels in regular classrooms or groups with their peers.

iii) Illusion and hallucination (2 marks):

  • Illusion: A misinterpretation or distortion of a real external sensory stimulus (e.g., seeing a coat rack as a person in dim light).
  • Hallucination: A sensory perception that occurs in the absence of an external stimulus (e.g., hearing voices when no one is speaking).
2.
Explain four (4) types of health care organizations in Kenya. (8 marks)
Show Answer
  1. Public/Government Health Facilities: Funded and managed by the government (National or County). They provide subsidized care to the general public (e.g., Kenyatta National Hospital, County Referral Hospitals).
  2. Private For-Profit Organizations: Owned by individuals or corporations. They operate as businesses to generate profit while providing health services (e.g., Nairobi Hospital, Aga Khan Hospital).
  3. Faith-Based Organizations (FBOs)/Mission Hospitals: Run by religious institutions. They are often non-profit and serve underserved areas, filling gaps in government services (e.g., Kijabe Hospital, PCEA Chogoria).
  4. Non-Governmental Organizations (NGOs)/Community Based Organizations (CBOs): Non-profit entities that operate independently of the government, often focusing on specific health issues like HIV/AIDS, maternal health, or relief in marginalized areas (e.g., AMREF).
3.
Explain the phases of nurse patient relationship. (10 marks)
Show Answer

The nurse-patient relationship typically consists of four phases (Peplau's Theory):

  1. Pre-interaction Phase: Before meeting the patient. The nurse reviews data (medical record), explores self-awareness (biases/feelings), and plans the interaction.
  2. Orientation (Introductory) Phase: The nurse and patient meet. Roles are defined, rapport is established, problems are articulated, and a contract (goals/timeline) is agreed upon. Trust building begins here.
  3. Working Phase: The active part of the relationship. The nurse and patient work together to solve problems and achieve established goals. It involves implementing interventions and overcoming resistance/barriers.
  4. Termination Phase: The relationship ends (discharge or shift end). Goals are evaluated, feelings about termination are discussed, and the patient is prepared for future independence or transfer of care.
4.
State five disadvantages of mentoring as an instructional strategy/method. (5 marks)
Show Answer
  1. Time-consuming: Requires significant time investment from both the mentor and mentee.
  2. Personality Mismatch: Conflict or lack of chemistry between mentor and mentee can hinder learning.
  3. Subjective: Mentors may pass on their own bad habits or biases.
  4. Lack of Standardization: The quality of mentoring varies greatly depending on the mentor's skills and commitment.
  5. Dependency: The mentee may become overly dependent on the mentor, hindering independent growth.
  6. Resource Intensive: Requires trained mentors which may be scarce.
5.
Outline three (3) outcomes of clinical teaching. (3 marks)
Show Answer
  1. Integration of Theory and Practice: Students apply theoretical knowledge to real-life patient care.
  2. Skill Acquisition (Psychomotor Skills): Students develop competency in performing nursing procedures and technical skills.
  3. Development of Professional Attitude/Behavior: Students learn professional conduct, ethics, and communication skills (Affective domain).
  4. Critical Thinking and Decision Making: Students learn to assess situations and make clinical judgments.

List four (4) roles of a community psychiatric nurse. (2 marks)

  1. Case Manager: Coordinating care and services for clients.
  2. Advocate: Protecting the rights and interests of the mentally ill.
  3. Educator: Teaching clients, families, and community about mental health.
  4. Counselor/Therapist: Providing support and therapy.
  5. Consultant: Providing expertise to other health workers or agencies.

7.
State four (4) unwanted effects of psychotherapy. (6 marks)
Show Answer

Note: Marks seem high for "state four", implying some explanation might be needed or it's a generous allocation.

  1. Dependency: The client may become overly reliant on the therapist and unable to make decisions independently.
  2. Symptom Substitution: Resolving one symptom leads to the emergence of another if the underlying cause isn't addressed (a psychoanalytic concept).
  3. Emotional Distress: Uncovering repressed trauma or facing difficult truths can cause temporary worsening of anxiety or depression.
  4. Stigmatization: The client may feel stigmatized by the need for therapy.
  5. False Memories: Risk of implanting false memories during therapy (especially in regression).
  6. Deterioration: In some cases, patients may get worse due to poor therapist fit or incompetent therapy.

PART III: ESSAY/LONG ANSWER QUESTIONS

40 Marks
1.
Client education is a routine role of the nurses in hospital.

a) Explain the concept of client education. (2 marks)

Client education is a dynamic, interactive process of imparting knowledge, skills, and attitudes to clients and their families to assist them in maintaining health, preventing illness, managing existing conditions, and achieving independence in care. It empowers the patient to make informed health decisions.

b) Explain the three (3) domains of learning. (6 marks)

  1. Cognitive Domain (Thinking): Involves the acquisition of knowledge, comprehension, and intellectual skills. It ranges from simple recall of facts to complex evaluation and synthesis of information.
  2. Affective Domain (Feeling): Involves changes in attitudes, values, beliefs, and emotions. It deals with how a learner feels about the content and their motivation to learn.
  3. Psychomotor Domain (Doing): Involves the development of physical skills and coordination. It requires practice and manual dexterity (e.g., learning to inject insulin).

c) Explain the factors that determine adult learning. (6 marks)

  1. Relevance/Need to Know: Adults need to know why they need to learn something and how it applies to their immediate situation.
  2. Prior Experience: Adults come with a reservoir of life experiences that should be used as a resource for learning.
  3. Readiness to Learn: Learning is most effective when it coincides with a developmental task or a social role requirement.
  4. Self-Concept: Adults see themselves as responsible, self-directed individuals and resent being treated as dependent.
  5. Internal Motivation: Adults are more motivated by internal factors (quality of life, self-esteem) than external ones (grades).
  6. Problem-Centered: Adults prefer problem-centered or task-oriented learning over subject-oriented learning.

2.
Miss T is brought to the psychiatric outpatient department by two policemen. They allege that she was found on the street beating people. When asked why she was beating people she claims she is Jesus Christ and was cleansing the public of their sins. The doctor diagnoses bipolar I mood disorder, manic phase.

a) Explain the mode of admission under which Miss T will be committed to the ward. (2 marks)

Involuntary Admission (Emergency Admission): Miss T is a danger to others (beating people) and lacks insight into her condition (delusions of grandeur). Therefore, she requires admission against her will for safety and treatment under the Mental Health Act provisions for emergency involuntary commitment.

b) Differentiate between bipolar I mood disorder and bipolar II mood disorder. (2 marks)

  • Bipolar I Disorder: Characterized by the occurrence of at least one full Manic Episode (severe elevation of mood, impairment in functioning, may have psychosis) which may be preceded or followed by hypomanic or major depressive episodes.
  • Bipolar II Disorder: Characterized by the occurrence of at least one Hypomanic Episode (less severe than mania, no psychosis, less impairment) and at least one Major Depressive Episode. There has never been a full manic episode.

c) Utilizing the nursing process, describe Miss Tâ€™s management till parole. (Formulate only four priority nursing diagnoses). (16 marks)

1. Risk for Violence: Directed at Others related to manic excitement and delusional thinking (cleansing sins) as evidenced by history of beating people on the street.

  • Interventions: Maintain low stimulus environment; remove dangerous objects; set limits on aggressive behavior; observe frequently; administer chemical restraints (antipsychotics/sedatives) as ordered; use physical restraints only as last resort.

2. Disturbed Thought Processes related to biochemical imbalance as evidenced by delusions of grandeur (claims to be Jesus Christ).

  • Interventions: Do not argue with delusions but present reality ("I understand you believe that, but I see you as Miss T"); distract patient with reality-based activities; monitor for content of delusions that might indicate harm; administer antipsychotics.

3. Self-Care Deficit (Hygiene, Nutrition) related to hyperactivity and distractibility.

  • Interventions: Provide high-calorie finger foods and fluids; monitor intake/output; assist with hygiene and grooming; encourage rest periods; reduce stimuli during meals.

4. Impaired Social Interaction related to intrusive and aggressive behavior.

  • Interventions: Set limits on inappropriate behavior (e.g., intrusion into others' space); reinforce appropriate social behaviors; provide solitary activities initially, then gradually introduce small group activities as mania subsides.

General Management (Implementation & Evaluation):

  • Pharmacology: Administer Mood Stabilizers (e.g., Lithium, Valproate) and Antipsychotics (e.g., Olanzapine, Haloperidol) as prescribed. Monitor Lithium levels.
  • Therapy: Once stable, initiate psychoeducation, cognitive behavioral therapy, and family therapy.
  • Evaluation: Monitor for reduction in aggression, resolution of delusions, improved sleep/nutrition, and insight into illness before considering discharge/parole.
