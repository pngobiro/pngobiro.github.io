Part I: Multiple Choice Questions

20 Marks
1.
The trophoblast cells of the developing zygote form the:-
a) Fetus and amnion
b) Placenta and fetus
c) Chorion and amnion
d) Placenta and chorion
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Placenta and chorion

Explanation: The blastocyst differentiates into the inner cell mass (embryoblast) which forms the fetus and amnion, and the outer layer (trophoblast) which forms the placenta and chorion.

2.
If Mrs. Cola’s menstrual period was on 27.7.08, her expected date of delivery will be:-
a) 3.4.09
b) 4.5.09
c) 3.5.09
d) 4.4.09
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) 3.5.09 (3rd May 2009)

Explanation: Using Naegele's Rule: Add 7 days to the first day of LMP, subtract 3 months, and add 1 year.
27 + 7 = 34 (3rd of the next month)
July (7th month) - 3 months = April (4th month)
However, since 27+7 goes into August, subtracting 3 months from August gives May.
Simple calculation: 27/7/08 + 9 months + 7 days = 3/5/09.

3.
In focused antenatal care, the second dose of sulfadoxide pyrimethamine (SP) is administered at gestation of:-
a) 16-28 weeks
b) 20-30 weeks.
c) 28-34 weeks.
d) 34-36 weeks.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) 28-34 weeks.

Explanation: In the standard IPTp schedule (at the time), the first dose was given after quickening (around 16-24 weeks), and the second dose was given at least one month later, typically around 28-34 weeks (early 3rd trimester).

4.
The diastolic pressure is usually not affected by excitement, its increase is therefore more significant in assessment of pregnant women for pre-eclampsia. The range of change in mmHg is:-
a) 10-15
b) 15-20
c) 15-30
d) 20-30
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) 10-15

Explanation: A rise in diastolic blood pressure of 15 mmHg or more above baseline is considered significant for pre-eclampsia diagnosis (though absolute values like >90mmHg are now more commonly emphasized).

5.
The management of placenta previa type three when the fetus has died in utero includes:-
a) Preparation of woman for emergency caesarian section.
b) Induction of labour by use of syntocinon.
c) Gentle palpation through vaginal fornicles to determine whether posterior or anterior.
d) Complete bed rest and speculum examination on third day.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Preparation of woman for emergency caesarian section.

Explanation: In Type 3 Placenta Previa, the placenta covers the internal os partly or completely. Vaginal delivery is not possible or safe due to the risk of massive maternal hemorrhage, regardless of whether the fetus is dead or alive. C-section is required to save the mother.

6.
The hormonal factor responsible for uterine contractions at onset of labour are:-
a) Increase release of oxytocin progesterone from deciduas at term.
b) Formation of oxytocin and estrogen in the fetal circulation.
c) Rise of oxytocin and estrogen in the fetal circulation.
d) Optimum levels of estrogen and rise of prostaglandins in maternal circulation.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Optimum levels of estrogen and rise of prostaglandins in maternal circulation.

Explanation: The onset of labor involves the "Progesterone Withdrawal" and "Estrogen Dominance" theory. High estrogen increases oxytocin receptors and prostaglandin production. Prostaglandins soften the cervix and stimulate contractions.

7.
Fetal axis pressure is a physical change that is more significant during:-
a) Early stages of labour transmitting the force of contraction to the upper pole of fetus.
b) Second stage of labour when membranes are intact.
c) Active and second stages of labour when membranes are ruptured.
d) Second stage of labour transmitting the force of contraction to the lower pole of the uterus.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Active and second stages of labour when membranes are ruptured.

Explanation: Fetal axis pressure is the force transmitted from the fundus, down the fetal spine, to the presenting part. This is most effective when the membranes are ruptured and the uterus contracts directly against the fetal pole, driving descent.

8.
If membranes rupture at cervical dilation of 8 centimeters, a vaginal examination performed should rule out:-
a) Obstructed labour, cord prolapsed, fetal compromise.
b) Cord prolapsed, cord presentation, fetal distress.
c) Obstructed labour, cord prolapsed, prolonged labour.
d) Maternal distress, prolonged labour, cord presentation.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Cord prolapsed, cord presentation, fetal distress.

Explanation: The immediate risk upon rupture of membranes, especially with a high head or malpresentation, is Cord Prolapse. A VE checks for the cord and assesses liquor (meconium) for fetal distress.

9.
The partograph is an effective tool in management of women in labour, a good progress in the active phase will be:-
a) Cervical dilatation plotted progressively on alert line within 7 hours.
b) Uterine contractions increasing in strength form 20-25 seconds within 8 hours.
c) Cervical dilation plotted on the action line and progressive descent.
d) Cervical dilatation plotted the right of the action line.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Cervical dilatation plotted progressively on alert line within 7 hours.

Explanation: Good progress is defined as cervical dilation that stays on or to the left of the Alert Line (dilating at least 1cm/hour). The Action line is 4 hours to the right.

10.
At primary level of ant-retroviral prophylaxis for PMTCT (prevention of mother to child transmition), a HIV-positive woman should take:-
a) Nevirapine 300 mgs at onset of labour.
b) Nevirapine 200 mgs at onset of labour.
c) Zidovudine 300 mgs 3 hourly for 12 hours.
d) Zidovudine 2mg/ kilogram body weight within three days of delivery.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Nevirapine 200 mgs at onset of labour.

Explanation: The standard single-dose Nevirapine regimen (at that time) was 200 mg orally at the onset of labor.

11.
The initial characteristics of rigid cervix in cervical dystocia include:-
a) Thin, tight, unyielding.
b) Thick, tight, edematous.
c) Long, thick, edematous.
d) Thick, edematous, unyielding.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Thin, tight, unyielding.

Explanation: An anatomically rigid cervix (due to scarring etc.) typically feels thin and tight but refuses to dilate (unyielding), as opposed to an edematous cervix which suggests obstruction.

12.
Perineal trauma is common in delivery of face to pubis due to:-
a) Irregular shape of the face as a presenting part.
b) Persistent occipito-posterior position when occiput becomes the leading part.
c) Large presenting mentovertical diameter.
d) Large presenting ocipito-frontal and biparietal diameters.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Large presenting ocipito-frontal and biparietal diameters.

Explanation: "Face to Pubis" delivery refers to a persistent Occipito-Posterior position delivered without rotation. The head is born by flexion, but the larger Occipito-frontal diameter (11.5cm) sweeps the perineum instead of the smaller Sub-occipitobregmatic (9.5cm), causing greater distension and trauma.

13.
After birth, the functional closure of ductus arteriosus takes place within:-
a) 1-2hours.
b) 2-4 hours
c) 4-6 hours
d) 8-10 hours
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) 8-10 hours (or 10-15 hours typically)

Explanation: Functional closure of the ductus arteriosus usually occurs within the first 10-15 hours (or up to 24 hours) after birth due to increased oxygen tension and decreased prostaglandins. Anatomical closure takes weeks.

14.
The anti-opioid drug used in resuscitation of the newborn is:-
a) Konakion 0.5 mg intramuscularly.
b) Dexamethosone 1-2mg Intravenously.
c) Narcan 0.1 mg/kilogram body weight intravenously.
d) Naloxone 0.5 mg intravenously.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Narcan 0.1 mg/kilogram body weight intravenously.

Explanation: Naloxone (Narcan) is the specific opioid antagonist. The neonatal dose is typically 0.1 mg/kg. Konakion is Vitamin K.

15.
The anti-infective factor that is abundant in human milk but absent in cow’s milk is:-
a) Lysozyme.
b) Lactoferin.
c) Colostrums.
d) Bifidus.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Lysozyme.

Explanation: Human milk contains very high levels of Lysozyme (300 times more than cow's milk) and Lactoferrin, which are potent anti-infective agents.

16.
Injury to the fetal scalp due to pressure by girdle of contact is known as:-
a) Cephalhaematoma.
b) Pericranial hematoma.
c) Subdural oedema.
d) Caput succedaneum.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Caput succedaneum.

Explanation: Caput succedaneum is the edematous swelling of the fetal scalp caused by pressure from the cervix (girdle of contact) restricting venous return.

17.
The peak action syntocinon administered to prevent post-partum hemorrhage is felt in:-
a) 2 ½ minutes.
b) 2 minutes.
c) 2 seconds.
d) 2 ½ seconds.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) 2 ½ minutes (IM) or c) if IV.

Explanation: Syntocinon given Intramuscularly (IM) takes about 2.5 minutes to act. If given Intravenously (IV), it acts almost immediately (within seconds). Since the question asks "peak action" and implies the standard active management (often IM if not IV drip), 2.5 minutes is the standard textbook value for IM onset.

18.
The exogenous causative organisms to puerperal infection include:-
a) Escherichia coli, clostridium welchii.
b) Streptococcus faecalis, haemolytic streptococcus.
c) Staphylococcus aureus, Escherichia coli.
d) Haemolytic streptococcus aureus.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Haemolytic streptococcus aureus (Likely meant Beta-hemolytic Streptococcus and Staph aureus).

Explanation: "Exogenous" means introduced from outside (e.g., from the throat or skin of attendants). Beta-hemolytic Streptococcus (Group A) and Staphylococcus aureus are the classic exogenous pathogens. E. coli is usually endogenous (from the patient's bowel).

19.
Mrs Dodo para 6+ 0 comes to the postnatal clinic at six weeks and demands for bilateral tubal ligation, the midwife should:-
a) Reassure and encourage her to continue with lactational amenorrhea method.
b) Schedule for return date in order to prepare for the procedure.
c) Check whether pregnancy tests is negative then provide the demanded method accordingly.
d) Take in-depth medico-social history and encourage partner participation.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Check whether pregnancy tests is negative then provide the demanded method accordingly (or D depending on counseling protocol).

Explanation: While counseling (D) is important, if a multiparous client "demands" a method at 6 weeks postpartum, the priority is ensuring she is not pregnant (C) and facilitating the method. However, BTL is a surgical procedure usually requiring consent and planning. The most actionable nursing step is verifying eligibility (pregnancy status) and counseling.

20.
True or False questions
a) Immunization in the newborn should be administered earlier than the time when maternal antibodies diminish and his body defense mechanism mature. - TRUE
b) Maternal death is defined as death of a woman during pregnancy, labour or 42 days after delivery or abortion. - TRUE
Show Answer

a) True: Vaccines like BCG and Polio are given at birth to stimulate active immunity before passive maternal immunity fades completely.

b) True: This is the WHO definition of Maternal Death (irrespective of the site or duration of pregnancy, from any cause related to or aggravated by the pregnancy).

1.
Draw and label a diagram of the uterus showing the fundal heights at 12th, 24th, 30th, 36th, 40th weeks of gestation.
Show Answer

Key Landmarks:

  • 12th Week: At the level of the Symphysis Pubis.
  • 24th Week: At the Umbilicus.
  • 30th Week: Midway between Umbilicus and Xiphisternum.
  • 36th Week: At the Xiphisternum (Highest level).
  • 40th Week: Drops down to 32-34 week level (Lightening) but wider.
2.
Outline the second line of treatment for smear positive mild to moderate malaria in pregnancy.
Show Answer

Oral Quinine:

If the first line (often Artemether-Lumefantrine or SP depending on guidelines at the time) fails or is contraindicated, the standard second line is typically Oral Quinine 10mg/kg every 8 hours for 7 days (often combined with Clindamycin).

3.
Myometrium has a unique role in good progress of labour and control of bleeding during the third stage. Describe the muscular structure of the myometrium.
Show Answer

The myometrium consists of three layers of smooth muscle:

  1. Outer Longitudinal Layer: Used for expulsion.
  2. Middle Interlacing/Oblique Layer ("Living Ligatures"): These figure-of-eight fibers surround blood vessels. When they contract and retract after delivery, they clamp down on the vessels to stop bleeding.
  3. Inner Circular Layer: Prominent around the cervix and cornua, acting as sphincters.
4.
Write notes on retained placenta using the following headlines:
a) Definition
b) Preventive measures
c) Aspects of specific management
Show Answer

a) Definition: Failure of the placenta to be expelled within 30 minutes (active management) or 1 hour (physiological management) after the birth of the baby.

b) Preventive Measures:

  • Active Management of Third Stage of Labour (AMTSL): Oxytocin administration, Controlled Cord Traction.
  • Avoid fiddling with the fundus before separation.
  • Ensuring empty bladder.

c) Specific Management:

  • Ensure IV access and check blood group.
  • Empty bladder (catheterize).
  • Attempt Controlled Cord Traction (CCT) again with uterine contraction.
  • If fails, Manual Removal of Placenta (MROP) under anaesthesia.
  • Antibiotic prophylaxis.
5.
Outline five signs of actual rupture of uterus following obstructed labour.
Show Answer
  1. Sudden cessation of contractions: The continuous pain of obstruction stops, replaced by continuous abdominal pain.
  2. Signs of Shock: Tachycardia, hypotension, pallor (internal hemorrhage).
  3. Abdominal shape change: Two swellings may be felt (retracted uterus and fetus in abdomen).
  4. Fetal Parts easily palpable: Under the skin (if extruded).
  5. Loss of Fetal Heart Rate: Fetal distress or death.
  6. Vaginal Bleeding: May be present.
  7. Recession of presenting part: Head goes back up the canal.
6.
Discuss four predisposing factors to amniotic fluid embolism.
Show Answer
  1. Hypertonic Uterine Action/Tetany: Strong contractions force fluid into open vessels (e.g., oxytocin induction).
  2. Placental Abruption: Opens maternal venous sinuses.
  3. Uterine Rupture or Trauma: Exposes vessels.
  4. Caesarean Section: Surgical opening of vessels.
  5. Multiparity and Advanced Maternal Age.
7.
Describe the neuro-endocrine control of breast milk release.
Show Answer

The Let-down Reflex (Milk Ejection Reflex):

  • Stimulus: Suckling by the baby stimulates sensory nerve endings in the nipple.
  • Transmission: Impulses travel via the spinal cord to the Hypothalamus.
  • Hormone Release: The Posterior Pituitary gland is triggered to release Oxytocin.
  • Action: Oxytocin travels in the blood to the breast, causing contraction of myoepithelial cells surrounding the alveoli.
  • Result: Milk is squeezed into the ducts and lactiferous sinuses, making it available to the baby. (Prolactin is for production, Oxytocin is for release).
8.
State three tests performed on cord blood in management of haemolytic disease of the new born.
Show Answer
  1. Direct Coombs Test (DCT): To detect maternal antibodies attached to fetal red blood cells.
  2. Blood Group and Rhesus Type: To confirm incompatibility.
  3. Haemoglobin/PCV: To assess for anemia.
  4. Serum Bilirubin: To assess baseline level for jaundice.
9.
Mrs. Cole complains of heavy red lochia loss during a postnatal home visit on the 10th day post-partum.
a) Name and define the obstetric condition.
b) Outline three possible causes.
Show Answer

a) Condition: Secondary Postpartum Haemorrhage.

Definition: Excessive vaginal bleeding occurring from 24 hours after delivery up to 6 weeks postpartum.

b) Possible Causes:

  1. Retained Products of Conception (RPOC): Placental fragments or membranes.
  2. Infection (Endometritis): Sepsis causes the placental site to shed prematurely or fail to heal.
  3. Subinvolution: Failure of the uterus to return to normal size/tone.

Part III: Long Essay Questions

40 Marks
1.
Miss Kazi comes for her third focused antenatal care clinic visit at 32 weeks of gestation. A diagnosis of pregnancy induced hypertension is made on history and examination.
a) State six signs of impending eclamplasia.
b) Describe the stages of an eclamptic fit.
c) Describe the magnesium sulphate regimen used in the management of Miss Kazi.
d) State two complications associated with magnesium sulphate.
Show Answer

a) Signs of Impending Eclampsia:

  1. Severe frontal headache.
  2. Visual disturbances (blurring, flashing lights).
  3. Epigastric pain (liver ischemia).
  4. Vomiting.
  5. Hyperreflexia (brisk reflexes) / Clonus.
  6. Sudden rise in BP.
  7. Oliguria.

b) Stages of Eclamptic Fit:

  1. Premonitory Stage (10-20 secs): Eyes roll, head turns, facial twitching.
  2. Tonic Stage (10-20 secs): Muscles go rigid/spasm, opisthotonus, respiration stops, cyanosis occurs.
  3. Clonic Stage (1-2 mins): Violent jerking (convulsions), foaming at mouth, tongue biting.
  4. Coma Stage: Unconsciousness, deep noisy breathing (stertorous). May recover or fit again.

c) Magnesium Sulphate Regimen (Pritchard):

  • Loading Dose: 4g IV (20% solution) slowly over 5-10 mins + 10g IM (5g in each buttock).
  • Maintenance Dose: 5g IM every 4 hours in alternate buttocks.
  • Monitoring before each dose: Respiratory rate >16/min, Patellar reflexes present, Urine output >30ml/hr.

d) Complications of MgSO4:

  • Respiratory depression/arrest.
  • Cardiac arrest.
  • Loss of deep tendon reflexes.
  • Oliguria/Renal failure.
2.
Mrs. Kyoto has ruptured membranes and a loop of the umbilical cord is visible at the vulva.
a) Differentiate between cord prolapsed and cord presentation.
b) State six predisposing factors to cord prolapse.
Show Answer

a) Differentiation:

  • Cord Presentation: The cord lies in front of the presenting part but the membranes are intact.
  • Cord Prolapse: The cord descends in front of the presenting part and the membranes are ruptured (as in Mrs. Kyoto's case).

b) Predisposing Factors:

  1. Malpresentation: Breech (especially footling) or Transverse lie.
  2. Polyhydramnios: Sudden gush of fluid washes cord down.
  3. Prematurity: Small fetus allows space.
  4. Multiple Pregnancy: Second twin.
  5. High Head/Non-engagement: Cord slips past unengaged head.
  6. Long Umbilical Cord.
  7. Low lying placenta.
