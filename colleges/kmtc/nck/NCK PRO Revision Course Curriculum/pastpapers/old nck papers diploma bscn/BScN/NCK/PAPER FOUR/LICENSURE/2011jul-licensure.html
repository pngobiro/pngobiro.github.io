PART I: MULTIPLE CHOICE QUESTIONS

20 Marks
1.
The primary goal of hypnotizing a patient is to:
a) Increase the patients’ suggestibility without understanding their nature
b) Induce muscular relaxation in a prefixed and systematic order
c) Provide immediate feedback to the individual regarding his/her physiological activities
d) Correct maladaptive methods of thinking by teaching new coping skills
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Increase the patients’ suggestibility without understanding their nature

Explanation: Hypnosis defines a state of consciousness involving focused attention and reduced peripheral awareness, characterized by an enhanced capacity for response to suggestion. While it is used to induce relaxation, the core mechanism/goal is increased suggestibility to facilitate therapeutic change.

2.
Indicate whether the following statements are TRUE or FALSE on the answers sheet provided

a) Electroconvulsive therapy (ECT) is a treatment that uses an electrical current to cause a brief seizure of the central nervous system while the patient is not under anaesthesia.

Answer: FALSE. Modern ECT is "modified ECT," which is always performed under general anesthesia and with muscle relaxants to prevent physical injury during the seizure.

b) People with bipolar disorder are more likely to have manic episodes if they are put on antidepressants.

Answer: TRUE. Antidepressants can induce "switching," precipitating a manic or hypomanic episode in patients with bipolar disorder if used without a mood stabilizer.

3.
The age of onset of attention deficit disorder is between :
a) 4-7 years
b) 10- 18 years
c) 2-4 years
d) Above 8 years
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) 4-7 years

Explanation: ADHD symptoms typically appear early in childhood. Diagnostic criteria (DSM-IV at the time of this exam) required symptoms to be present before age 7. (DSM-5 now says before age 12, but 4-7 is the classic onset period for diagnosis).

4.
Drugs used in management of depression include:
a) Haloperidol, mirtazapine, trazodone
b) Risperidone, venflaxine, citalopram
c) Thioridazine, doxepine, venflaxine
d) Doxepine, imipramine, amitryptyline
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Doxepine, imipramine, amitryptyline

Explanation: This option contains only antidepressants (specifically Tricyclic Antidepressants - TCAs). The other options contain antipsychotics (Haloperidol, Risperidone, Thioridazine).

5.
Cognitive domain of learning is concerned with:
a) Decision making, application of principles, concepts
b) Manipulative skills, operation of equipments, concepts
c) Maintenance of discipline, readiness to work, principles of work
d) Values, understanding and principles
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Decision making, application of principles, concepts

Explanation: The cognitive domain involves mental skills (knowledge). It includes recall, application of principles, and decision making (evaluation). Option B is psychomotor; D contains values (affective).

6.
The mode of evaluation in which objectives of the course are set and the learner's performance compared with the target is;
a) Norm referenced evaluation
b) Criterion referenced evaluation
c) Qualitative referenced evaluation
d) Cumulative referenced evaluation
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Criterion referenced evaluation

Explanation: Criterion-referenced evaluation compares a student's performance against a set standard or objective (the criterion). Norm-referenced (a) compares students to each other.

7.
The functionist theory of stratification by Marx Weber rests on the assumption that:
a) All positions in the society are equal
b) Social phenomena exist because they have a positive function to perform in the society
c) Society members will take up the position to serve irrespective of the material reward
d) Profit is distributed equally in the society
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Social phenomena exist because they have a positive function to perform in the society

Explanation: Functionalist theory (Davis-Moore hypothesis, though the question mentions Weber who had a conflict/power theory) generally posits that social stratification exists because it serves a functional purpose (ensuring the most important roles are filled by the most qualified people through rewards).

8.
A mission statement:
a) Reflects the purpose and direction of the health care agency
b) Develops, negotiates and formalizes the targets of a health care agency
c) Identifies goals that are specific and adhere to a deadline of the health care agency
d) Is used to develop educational programs for health care professionals
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Reflects the purpose and direction of the health care agency

Explanation: A mission statement defines the organization's reason for existence, its core purpose, and its overall direction.

9.
An organizational structure designed to focus on both product and function is:
a) Flat
b) Matrix
c) Bureaucracy
d) Hybrid
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Matrix

Explanation: A matrix structure organizes employees by both function (e.g., nursing, pharmacy) and product/project (e.g., Oncology unit, Research team), creating a dual reporting line.

10.
A client with schizophrenia had just been admitted to the mental health unit. When working with this client the nurse's initial most therapeutic action would be to:
a) Use diversional activity and involve the client in occupational therapy
b) Build trust and demonstrate acceptance by spending some time with the client
c) Involve the client in multiple small group discussion to distract attention from the fantasy world
d) Delay one to one interaction until medication reduces the psychotic symptoms
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Build trust and demonstrate acceptance by spending some time with the client

Explanation: The foundation of all psychiatric nursing is the nurse-patient relationship. Building trust is the priority initial action, especially with a suspicious or withdrawn schizophrenic patient.

11.
Separation anxiety in Freud's psychosexual stages of development initially occurs during the:
a) Oral stage
b) Anal stage
c) Phallic stage
d) Latency stage
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Oral stage

Explanation: Separation anxiety typically begins around 8 months of age, which falls within the Oral stage (0-18 months) of Freud's development theory.

12.
When caring for the extremely depressed clients, specific goals towards helpig the client include:
a) Set realistic goals
b) Express hostile feelings
c) Get involved in activities
d) Develop trust in others
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Get involved in activities

Explanation: While setting realistic goals and developing trust are important, mobilizing the depressed patient to participate in activities (behavioral activation) is a specific, actionable goal to counteract psychomotor retardation and withdrawal. However, usually "Setting realistic goals" (a) is key to preventing failure and increasing self-esteem.

13.
Individuals with antisocial personality disorder:
a) Suffer from a great deal of anxiety
b) Are generally unable to postpone gratification
c) Rapidly learn by experience and punishment
d) Have a great sense of responsibility towards others
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Are generally unable to postpone gratification

Explanation: Antisocial personality disorder is characterized by impulsivity, lack of remorse, and failure to conform to social norms. They want immediate gratification and do not learn from punishment (c) or feel responsibility (d).

14.
In decentralized scheduling:
a) The nurse manager develops the schedule in isolation from all other units
b) Each staff does self scheduling then the unit manager balances the schedule depending on the need
c) The organization’s general manager does the scheduling
d) The organization sets up a staff scheduling committee
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) The nurse manager develops the schedule in isolation from all other units

Explanation: Decentralized scheduling implies that the scheduling decision is made at the unit level (by the Nurse Manager) rather than by a central staffing office (c). Option (b) describes self-scheduling, which is a specific type of decentralized scheduling, but (a) is the general definition.

15.
The basic elements of supervision include:
a) Regulating workload, providing opportunity to discuss stressful work issues, providing feedback to staff on their performance
b) Building and maintaining an organization structure, creating and maintaining an effective working force, controlling the work
c) Maximizing the economy, creating and maintaining effective work force, developing and maintaining motivation
d) Examining the list of duties given, maintaining social responsibility, negotiating pay hikes
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Regulating workload, providing opportunity to discuss stressful work issues, providing feedback to staff on their performance

Explanation: Supervision involves administrative (workload), supportive (stress issues), and educational (feedback) functions.

16.
Performance appraisal is essential as it:
a) Assesses the performance of the industry toward achieving it's goals
b) Gives the employees a clear indication of progress and performance
c) Obtains useful feedback for the consumer
d) Assesses the results of training among the colleagues
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Gives the employees a clear indication of progress and performance

Explanation: The primary purpose of performance appraisal is to provide feedback to the employee about their work performance, identifying strengths and areas for improvement.

17.
Random selection of subjects for a study is used to:
a) Maximize the generalizability of results
b) Reduce random measurement error
c) Control assignment error
d) Minimize Hawthorne effect
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Maximize the generalizability of results

Explanation: Random selection (random sampling) ensures the sample is representative of the population, thereby allowing the results to be generalized (External Validity). Random *assignment* controls for internal validity.

18.
Literature review in research is useful for;
a) Familiarization with practical or theoretical issues relating to a problem
b) Ensuring that the research superior is well informed
c) Controlling extraneous variables during experimentation
d) Testing for appropriate causal relationship
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Familiarization with practical or theoretical issues relating to a problem

Explanation: Reviewing literature helps the researcher understand the current state of knowledge, identify gaps, and refine the research problem and methodology.

19.
The application of operant conditioning in nursing education may be achieved b:
a) Stating the objective of a teaching session
b) Using punishment to extinct negative behaviour
c) Rewarding positive behavour
d) Stimulus variation in classroom teaching
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Rewarding positive behavour

Explanation: Operant conditioning (Skinner) relies on reinforcement. Positive reinforcement (rewarding positive behavior) increases the likelihood of that behavior being repeated.

20.
The gender of subjects in a research is an example of:
a) A ratio scale
b) A nominal scale
c) An ordinal scale
d) An interval scale
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) A nominal scale

Explanation: Nominal scales are used for naming or labeling variables without any quantitative value or order (e.g., Male/Female, Yes/No).

PART II: SHORT ANSWER QUESTIONS

40 Marks
1.
Schizophrenia Questions

a) Outline the four (4) Eugen Bleuler fundamental symptoms of schizophrenia (4 Marks)

  1. Affect: Inappropriate or flattened affect (emotional response).
  2. Associations: Loosening of associations (disordered thinking).
  3. Ambivalence: Holding conflicting attitudes or emotions simultaneously.
  4. Autism: Withdrawal into a private fantasy world (social withdrawal).

b) State four (4) factors that may increase the risk of developing catatonic schizophrenia. (4 Marks)

  • Genetics: Family history of schizophrenia or mood disorders.
  • Neurobiology: Abnormalities in brain structure or neurotransmitters (dopamine/GABA).
  • Environmental Stressors: Severe trauma or high stress levels.
  • Medical Conditions: History of viral infections or autoimmune disorders.
  • Substance Abuse: Use of psychoactive drugs.
2.
Outline five (5) types of non-probability sampling in research. (5 Marks)
Show Answer
  1. Convenience Sampling: Selecting readily available subjects.
  2. Purposive (Judgmental) Sampling: Selecting subjects based on the researcher's knowledge of the population.
  3. Quota Sampling: Ensuring certain subgroups are represented in specific proportions non-randomly.
  4. Snowball Sampling: Asking early participants to refer others (used for hard-to-reach populations).
  5. Consecutive Sampling: Including all accessible subjects as part of the sample.
3.
Differentiate between formative and summative evaluation. (4 Marks)
Show Answer
  • Formative Evaluation: Conducted during the instructional process to provide ongoing feedback to improve learning and teaching (e.g., quizzes, observations). It is diagnostic and remedial.
  • Summative Evaluation: Conducted at the end of an instructional unit or course to determine the extent to which objectives were achieved (e.g., final exams). It is used for grading and certification.
4.
Explain the first three stages of psychosocial development according to Eriksons theory in correct sequence. (6 Marks)
Show Answer
  1. Trust vs. Mistrust (Infancy, 0-18 months): The infant learns to trust the caregiver and the world if needs are met consistently; otherwise, mistrust develops.
  2. Autonomy vs. Shame and Doubt (Early Childhood, 18 months-3 years): The child learns to exercise will and do things for themselves (toilet training, feeding); failure leads to shame and doubt in abilities.
  3. Initiative vs. Guilt (Preschool, 3-5 years): The child learns to initiate activities and enjoy accomplishment; if restricted or punished, guilt develops over their desires.
5.
Questionnaire Design

a) Explain rating scale used in questionnaires. (2 Marks)

A rating scale allows respondents to rate their attitudes, beliefs, or behaviors on a continuum (e.g., "Strongly Agree" to "Strongly Disagree"). The most common type is the Likert scale.

b) Differentiate between contingency questions and matrix questions. (4 Marks)

  • Contingency Questions: Questions that are answered only if the respondent gave a specific answer to a previous question (e.g., "If Yes, go to Q5"). They act as filters.
  • Matrix Questions: A set of questions that share the same response categories (e.g., Likert scale) presented in a grid format to save space and time.
6.
Explain the following ethical terms used in research (6 Marks)

a) Plagiarism: The act of using another person's ideas, words, or work without giving appropriate credit (citation), presenting it as one's own.

b) Fraud: Deliberate deception or falsification of research data (fabricating results) or methods to mislead the scientific community.

c) Misuse of privileges: Abusing the researcher's power or access to confidential information, or exploiting subjects for personal gain rather than scientific benefit.

7.
State four (4) manifestations of somatization disorder. (4 Marks)
Show Answer
  1. Multiple physical complaints involving many organ systems (pain, GI, sexual, pseudo-neurological).
  2. Symptoms cannot be fully explained by a known medical condition.
  3. History of seeking treatment from multiple doctors ("doctor shopping").
  4. Impairment in social or occupational functioning due to symptoms.
  5. Symptoms are not intentionally produced (unlike malingering).

PART III: ESSAY/LONG ANSWER QUESTIONS

40 Marks
1.
You have been appointed the regional director in a non-governmental organization to manage malaria control project

a) Outline five (5) key principle of quality management. (10 Marks)

  1. Customer Focus: Understanding current and future needs of the community and striving to exceed expectations.
  2. Leadership: Establishing unity of purpose and direction; creating an environment where staff can become fully involved.
  3. Involvement of People: Ensuring staff at all levels are competent, empowered, and engaged.
  4. Process Approach: Managing activities and resources as a process to achieve desired results efficiently.
  5. Continuous Improvement: Making permanent improvement of overall performance a constant objective.
  6. Evidence-based Decision Making: Basing decisions on the analysis of data and information.
  7. Relationship Management: Managing relationships with interested parties (e.g., suppliers, government) to optimize performance.

b) Describe the phases of strategic planning process. (10 Marks)

  1. Phase 1: Environmental Scanning (Situation Analysis): Conducting a SWOT analysis (Strengths, Weaknesses, Opportunities, Threats) and PESTLE analysis to understand the internal and external environment.
  2. Phase 2: Strategy Formulation: Developing the vision, mission, and values. Setting strategic goals and objectives. choosing the best strategies to achieve these goals.
  3. Phase 3: Strategy Implementation: Translating the strategy into action. This involves allocating resources, assigning responsibilities, creating action plans (timelines), and managing change.
  4. Phase 4: Strategy Evaluation and Control: Monitoring progress towards goals, measuring performance against standards, and taking corrective action if deviations occur.
