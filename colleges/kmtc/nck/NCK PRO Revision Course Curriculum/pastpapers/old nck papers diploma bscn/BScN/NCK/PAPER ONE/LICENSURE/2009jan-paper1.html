Part I: Multiple Choice Questions

1.
The major factors associated with obesity include:
a) Genetic, nutritional, level of activity.
b) Nutritional, level of activity, socio-cultural.
c) Nutritional, social, genetic.
d) Level of activity, genetic, psychological.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Genetic, nutritional, level of activity.

Explanation: Obesity is a multifactorial condition primarily driven by an energy imbalance (Nutritional intake vs. Level of activity). However, genetic predisposition plays a significant role in susceptibility. While socio-cultural and psychological factors contribute, the physiological "major factors" triad is typically Genetics, Diet (Nutrition), and Physical Activity.

2.
During assessment of hearing, Rinne test is used to:
a) Evaluate bone conduction.
b) Evaluate the patient’s ability to hear high frequency sounds.
c) Compare bone conduction with air conduction in both ears.
d) Determine the degree of hearing.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Compare bone conduction with air conduction in both ears.

Explanation: The Rinne test distinguishes between conductive and sensorineural hearing loss by comparing air conduction (AC) to bone conduction (BC). Normal hearing or sensorineural loss shows AC > BC (Positive Rinne). Conductive loss shows BC > AC (Negative Rinne).

3.
A synergistic interaction between furosemide and gentamicin may cause:
a) Sedation.
b) Gout.
c) Retinopathy.
d) Ototoxicity.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Ototoxicity.

Explanation: Both loop diuretics (like Furosemide) and aminoglycoside antibiotics (like Gentamicin) have ototoxicity (damage to the ear) as a side effect. Using them together has a synergistic effect, significantly increasing the risk of hearing loss.

4.
In the elderly, peptic ulceration induced by non-steroidal anti-inflammatory drugs (NSAIDs) can best be treated with:
a) Bismuth chelate.
b) Misoprostol.
c) Metoclopramide.
d) Antacids.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Misoprostol.

Explanation: NSAIDs cause ulcers by inhibiting prostaglandin synthesis, which protects the gastric mucosa. Misoprostol is a synthetic prostaglandin E1 analog that replaces the protective prostaglandins, making it the specific prophylactic and treatment agent for NSAID-induced ulcers, especially in the elderly.

5.
Temper tantrums are common in:
a) School age children.
b) Infancy.
c) Toddlers.
d) Preschool age children.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Toddlers.

Explanation: Temper tantrums are a classic developmental behavior in toddlers (ages 1-3 years) as they strive for autonomy but lack the language skills to express frustration.

6.
Hordeolum eternal (sty) is the infection of:
a) Tarsal glands of eyelid.
b) Eyeball.
c) Lachrymal glands.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Tarsal glands of eyelid.

Explanation: A hordeolum (sty) is an acute infection of the glands of the eyelid. An external hordeolum involves the glands of Zeis or Moll (at the eyelash base), while an internal hordeolum involves the Meibomian (tarsal) glands. "Tarsal glands" (Option A) is the closest anatomical answer provided, referring to the glands within the eyelid structure.

7.
Pelvic inflammatory diseases (PID) include:
a) Salpingitis, cervitis, candidiasis
b) Cervicitis, endometritis, oophoritis.
c) Trichomoniasis, salpingitis, urethritis.
d) Candidiasis, toxic shock syndrome, endometritis.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Cervicitis, endometritis, oophoritis.

Explanation: PID is an infection of the upper female reproductive tract. It encompasses inflammation of the cervix (cervicitis), uterus (endometritis), fallopian tubes (salpingitis), and ovaries (oophoritis). Candidiasis and Trichomoniasis are lower tract infections/STIs but not classified as PID themselves unless they ascend.

8.
A clinical path (critical pathway) is:
a) Day to day activities that will be carried out for a particular patient.
b) An outline of steps a patient will take from admission to discharge.
c) A predetermined outline of patient care for a specific episode of care.
d) A predetermined outline of medical and surgical care procedures for a specific episode of care.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) A predetermined outline of medical and surgical care procedures for a specific episode of care.

Explanation: A clinical path (critical pathway) is a multidisciplinary tool that outlines the optimal sequencing and timing of interventions (medical, surgical, nursing) for a particular diagnosis or procedure, designed to minimize delays and resource utilization while maximizing quality of care.

9.
Cholelithiasis is associated with:
a) Abnormal metabolism of cholesterol and bile salts.
b) Abnormal metabolism of lipids and bile acids.
c) Chronic pancreatitis.
d) Hepatitis.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Abnormal metabolism of cholesterol and bile salts.

Explanation: Gallstones (cholelithiasis) are primarily formed due to an imbalance in the composition of bile. The most common type is cholesterol stones, which form when bile is supersaturated with cholesterol or has insufficient bile salts/lecithin to keep it in solution.

10.
Increased tactile fremitus may occur in:
a) Lung tumor, pulmonary fibrosis.
b) Pleural effusion chronic obstructive pulmonary disease(COPD)
c) Pneumonia, pleural effusion.
d) Pulmonary fibrosis, chronic obstructive pulmonary disease (COPD)
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Pneumonia (specifically Lobar Pneumonia/Consolidation).

Explanation: Tactile fremitus (vibration felt on chest wall when speaking) is increased when sound travels through solid/consolidated tissue (as in Pneumonia). It is decreased when sound transmission is blocked by air (COPD, Pneumothorax) or fluid (Pleural Effusion). Note: Option C lists "Pneumonia, pleural effusion". Pneumonia causes increased fremitus, but effusion causes decreased. In multiple choice contexts, identifying "Pneumonia" is the key positive finding for increased fremitus, even if the distractor pairing is imperfect.

11.
Atopic dermatitis commonly develops in individuals aged:
a) 30 years and above.
b) 1 month to 1 year.
c) Above 5 years.
d) Between 2 years and 5 years.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) 1 month to 1 year.

Explanation: Atopic dermatitis (eczema) typically has an early onset. Infantile eczema usually begins between 2 to 6 months of age (within the "1 month to 1 year" range).

12.
Chvostek’s sign is used for diagnosis of:
a) Hypercalcemia resulting from hyperparathyroidism.
b) Hypercalcemia resulting from hyperpituitarism.
c) Hypocalcaemia resulting from hypoparathyroidism.
d) Hypocalcaemia resulting from hypothyroidism.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Hypocalcaemia resulting from hypoparathyroidism.

Explanation: Chvostek's sign (facial twitching when the facial nerve is tapped) is a sign of latent tetany caused by hypocalcemia. Hypoparathyroidism is a primary cause of low calcium levels.

13.
Glomerulonephritis usually follows:
a) Staphylococcal infection.
b) Gonococcal infection.
c) Hemphilus influenza infection.
d) Streptococcal infection.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Streptococcal infection.

Explanation: Acute Post-Streptococcal Glomerulonephritis (APSGN) is a classic immune complex disorder that occurs 1-3 weeks after a Group A Beta-hemolytic streptococcal infection (usually pharyngitis or impetigo).

14.
Characteristics of Prinzmetal’s Angina (Variant Angina) include:
a) Occurs with known amount of activity, density of pain remain stable.
b) Occurs with increasing severity, pain is unpredictable.
c) Occurs same time each day, caused by coronary artery spasms.
d) Always occur due to atherosclerotic lesion, pain increases with increasing level of activity.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Occurs same time each day, caused by coronary artery spasms.

Explanation: Prinzmetal's (variant) angina typically occurs at rest (often at night or early morning), is cyclic (same time of day), and is caused by vasospasm of the coronary arteries rather than physical exertion or atherosclerosis alone.

15.
The teeth that commonly get impacted are the:
a) Molars.
b) Incisors.
c) Canine.
d) Wisdom.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Wisdom.

Explanation: Wisdom teeth (the third molars) are the most commonly impacted teeth because they are the last to erupt and often do not have enough space in the jaw.

16.
Body mechanics usually prescribed for clients with low back pain include:
a) Bending at knee and hips, standing straight, carrying objects close to the body.
b) Bending at the waist carrying objects close to the body, standing straight.
c) Sleeping on the side carrying objects farther from the body, bending at the knees and hips.
d) Bending at the knees and lips.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Bending at knee and hips, standing straight, carrying objects close to the body.

Explanation: Proper body mechanics to protect the back involve using the strong leg muscles (bending knees/hips) rather than the back (waist), keeping the load close to the center of gravity (body), and maintaining a neutral spine.

17.
The type of immunoglobulin’s involved in type III immune complex-mediated hypersensitivity are:
a) IgE, IgM
b) IgG, IgM.
c) IgE, IgD.
d) IgG, IgA.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) IgG, IgM.

Explanation: Type III hypersensitivity involves the formation of antigen-antibody complexes that deposit in tissues. The antibodies involved are typically classes IgG and IgM (the same as Type II, but the mechanism of complex deposition differs). Type I involves IgE.

18.
The major nursing interventions for a patient with hemolytic anemia focus on:
a) Protection from injury, understanding of the condition.
b) Reduced stress, absence of infection.
c) Improved nutrition status, absence of infection.
d) Protection from injury, absence of infection.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Protection from injury, absence of infection.

Explanation: Patients with anemia suffer from hypoxia causing fatigue/weakness (risk for injury/falls). Hemolytic anemia may involve spleen dysfunction (risk for infection) and treatment with steroids (immunosuppression). Therefore, preventing infection and injury are key priorities. Nutritional status (c) is less central for hemolytic anemia compared to deficiency anemias (like iron deficiency).

19.
During pre-operative period, the drugs used to achieve sedation, reduce and cause amnesia include:
a) Diazepam ranitidine.
b) Diazepam, scopolamine.
c) Midazolam, diazepam.
d) Atropine sulfate, midazolam.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Midazolam, diazepam.

Explanation: Benzodiazepines like Midazolam and Diazepam are the primary agents used for pre-operative sedation, anxiolysis (reducing anxiety), and anterograde amnesia. Atropine (d) is an anticholinergic used to dry secretions, not primarily for sedation/amnesia.

20.
When a nurse is monitoring a patient for cerebrospinal fluid (CSF) leak he/she should observe for:
Show Answer

Correct Answer: (Options not provided in source text, standard answer provided below)

Explanation: The nurse should observe for:

  • Rhinorrhea or Otorrhea: Clear fluid draining from the nose or ears.
  • Halo Sign: If fluid is blood-tinged, placing a drop on gauze results in a central blood spot surrounded by a clear/yellow ring (CSF).
  • Glucose Positive: CSF contains glucose (mucus does not).
  • Frequent Swallowing: May indicate post-nasal drip of CSF.
