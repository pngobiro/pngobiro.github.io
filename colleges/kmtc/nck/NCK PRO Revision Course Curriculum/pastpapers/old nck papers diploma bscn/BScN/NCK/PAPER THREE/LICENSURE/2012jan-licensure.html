Part I: Multiple Choice Questions

20 Marks
1.
In epidemiology a risk factor is defined as one that:-
a. If present increases susceptibility to a disease.
b. Causes the disease.
c. Determines that one actually gets a disease.
d. If changed will increase the severity of the disease.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a. If present increases susceptibility to a disease.

Explanation: A risk factor is an attribute, characteristic, or exposure of an individual that increases the likelihood of developing a disease or injury. It is not necessarily a direct cause (Option b) nor a guarantee of getting the disease (Option c).

2.
The coordinator of primary health care in a district is:-
a. District health management team.
b. District medical officer of health.
c. District public health officer.
d. District public health nurse.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d. District public health nurse.

Explanation: While the District Health Management Team (DHMT) oversees all health activities, the District Public Health Nurse (DPHN) is traditionally the focal person for coordinating Primary Health Care (PHC) activities, including immunizations, MCH, and community outreach services, within the nursing structure in Kenya.

3.
The responsibilities of a client during home based care include:-
a. Passive participation in care process, giving consent to care providers, collaborating with other care providers.
b. Giving consent to the care giver, collaborating with other clients, advocating in behavior change.
c. Identifying care givers, actively participating in care process, giving consent to the care givers.
d. Mobilizing maternal support, giving consent to the care giver, participating in care process.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c. Identifying care givers, actively participating in care process, giving consent to the care givers.

Explanation: Home Based Care emphasizes client autonomy and partnership. The client is responsible for identifying/accepting their primary caregiver (often a family member), actively participating in their own care plan (self-care), and providing consent for services.

4.
Herd immunity:-
a. Plays a major role in control of communicable disease.
b. Confers protection when a large population is immunized.
c. Protects only the population that is immunized.
d. Is only acquired when a large population suffers from the disease.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b. Confers protection when a large population is immunized.

Explanation: Herd immunity occurs when a significant portion of a community becomes immune to an infectious disease (usually through vaccination), thereby reducing the likelihood of transmission and providing protection to individuals who are not immune.

5.
Community health action is defined as the process of:-
a. Gathering information on the health problems affecting the community.
b. Analyzing the community’s health problem in order to plan for intervention.
c. Providing health services to the community based on the identified problems.
d. Identifying the health facilities available within the community.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c. Providing health services to the community based on the identified problems.

Explanation: "Action" implies implementation. Options A (Assessment), B (Diagnosis/Planning), and D (Inventory) are preparatory steps. Option C represents the actual execution or action taken to address the health needs.

6.
Comprehensive health and safety risk assessment in work place involves:-
a. Assessing risks, identifying hazards, applying controls and reviewing effects.
b. Identifying hazards, documenting the risks, post exposure prophylaxis.
c. Avoiding hazards, removing pollutants, ensuring proper lighting.
d. Identifying hazards in environment, work place policy implementation.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a. Assessing risks, identifying hazards, applying controls and reviewing effects.

Explanation: The standard risk management cycle is: Identify hazards -> Assess risks -> Implement control measures -> Review/Monitor.

7.
To calculate the case fatality, one needs to divide the:-
a. Number of deaths midyear by number of persons with disease.
b. Number of deaths from a specified disease by number of persons with the disease.
c. Number of deaths from a specified population by number of persons with the disease.
d. Number of deaths from any cause in the population by number of persons who died from the disease.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b. Number of deaths from a specified disease by number of persons with the disease.

Explanation: Case Fatality Rate (CFR) = (Deaths from disease X / Total cases of disease X) * 100. It measures the severity/virulence of the disease.

8.
Social change occurs through:-
a. Revolution, ethnocentrism, assimilation.
b. Discovery, assimilation, devolution.
c. Socialization, evolution, devolution.
d. Revolution, reforms, enculturation.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b. Discovery, assimilation, devolution. (Most appropriate among options)

Explanation: The classic mechanisms of social change are Discovery, Invention, and Diffusion (which includes assimilation). "Devolution" in this context likely refers to the transfer of power/responsibility, which causes structural social change.

9.
The sequence of growth monitoring in an MCH/FP clinic is:-
a. Counseling, weighing, plotting, interpretation.
b. Interpretation, counseling, weighing, plotting.
c. Weighing, plotting, interpretation, counseling.
d. Counseling, weighing, plotting, interpretation.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c. Weighing, plotting, interpretation, counseling.

Explanation: The logical flow is: 1) Measure the weight (Weighing), 2) Record it on the chart (Plotting), 3) Analyze the trend/curve (Interpretation), and 4) Advise the mother based on the findings (Counseling).

10.
The bottom up approach in design and management of community health projects ensures:-
a. Donor agents make development decisions for the community.
b. Government and donors support community projects.
c. Sustainability of projects initiated in the community.
d. Government supervision of the community projects.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c. Sustainability of projects initiated in the community.

Explanation: Bottom-up approaches involve the community in decision-making and ownership. This "felt need" and ownership significantly increases the likelihood that the project will be sustained after external funding or support ends.

11.
The characteristics of a rural community include:-
a. A limited kinship tie, main occupation is agriculture.
b. Limited social exchange, emphasis is on ascribed status.
c. Strong kinship ties, emphasis on ascribed status.
d. Strong relationship among family members, social change is important.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c. Strong kinship ties, emphasis on ascribed status.

Explanation: Rural communities are typically characterized by strong extended family (kinship) networks and "ascribed status" (status based on who you are/family, rather than "achieved status" based on what you do, which is more urban). Agriculture is common, but Option A says "limited kinship" which is incorrect.

12.
Indicate whether the statements are true or false:-
a. Monophasic combined oral contraceptives have equal amounts of estrogen and progesterone.
b. Progesterone hormone causes the basal body temperature to rise.
Show Answer

Answer:

  • a. TRUE: Monophasic pills contain the same (fixed) dose of estrogen and progestin in every active pill in the pack.
  • b. TRUE: Progesterone is thermogenic; it acts on the hypothalamus to raise the basal body temperature by about 0.5°C to 1.0°F after ovulation.
13.
A population pyramid with a broad base and a narrow apex indicates:-
a. High birth rate.
b. High mortality rate.
c. Low mortality rate.
d. Low dependence ratio.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a. High birth rate.

Explanation: A broad base represents a very large number of children (high fertility/birth rate). The narrow apex indicates few people surviving to old age (high mortality rate). However, the "broad base" specifically points to High Birth Rate.

14.
The component of healthful school living involves:-
a. Health assessment, accident prevention, health education.
b. Identification of children at risk, accident prevention, health education.
c. Classroom assessment, encourage teacher’s support, counseling of students.
d. Identification of children at risk, health education, classroom assessment.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a. Health assessment, accident prevention, health education.

Explanation: School Health Programs generally have three components: 1) Health Services (Assessment/Screening), 2) Health Education, and 3) Healthful School Environment (Healthful Living/Accident Prevention). Option A best covers these key aspects.

15.
According to Erikson’s theory of psychosocial development, the state of identity versus role of confusion occurs between ages:-
a. 6-11 years
b. 12-18 years
c. 18-22 years
d. 22-24 years
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b. 12-18 years

Explanation: This is the stage of Adolescence.

  • 6-11 years: Industry vs. Inferiority
  • 12-18 years: Identity vs. Role Confusion
  • 18-40 years: Intimacy vs. Isolation
16.
The evaluation done to assess programme outcome is referred to as:-
a. Strategic.
b. Process.
c. Formative.
d. Impact.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d. Impact.

Explanation:

  • Process evaluation: Assesses implementation (during the program).
  • Formative evaluation: Done during development to improve the program.
  • Outcome/Impact evaluation: Assesses the long-term effects or changes resulting from the program.

17.
The phase of home visiting where the health worker credits achievement and leaves a written identification of self is:-
a. Implementation.
b. Termination.
c. Initiation.
d. Post visit.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b. Termination.

Explanation: The termination phase involves summarizing the visit, crediting positive achievements, planning the next visit, and providing contact information (written identification) before leaving.

18.
The purpose of a research hypothesis is to:-
a. Postulate an outcome of research based upon inductive reasoning.
b. Assign numerical values to variable.
c. Accurately state expected relationship between the variables.
d. Design the overall plan with which the research will be conducted.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c. Accurately state expected relationship between the variables.

Explanation: A hypothesis is a tentative statement about the relationship between two or more variables. It is a specific, testable prediction.

19.
Water washed disease include:-
a. Malaria, schistosomiasis, trachoma.
b. Scabies, trachoma, diarrhea.
c. Malaria, onchocerciasis, typhoid.
d. Cholera, schistosomiasis, scabies.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b. Scabies, trachoma, diarrhea.

Explanation:

  • Water-washed: Diseases caused by lack of water for personal hygiene (skin/eye infections like Scabies, Trachoma; also faeco-oral diseases like Diarrhea due to poor handwashing).
  • Water-borne: Cholera, Typhoid.
  • Water-based: Schistosomiasis.
  • Water-related insect vector: Malaria.
Option B is the correct grouping of water-washed diseases.

Part II: Short Answer Questions

40 Marks
1.
State five factors that are associated with spread of sexually transmitted infections. (5 marks)
Show Answer

Answer:

  1. Multiple Sexual Partners: Having many partners increases the probability of encountering an infected person.
  2. Unprotected Sex: Lack of barrier protection (condoms) allows for direct contact with infectious fluids.
  3. Alcohol and Drug Abuse: Impairs judgment leading to risky sexual behaviors.
  4. Asymptomatic Infections: Infected individuals who show no signs may unknowingly transmit the disease.
  5. Socio-economic Factors: Poverty, migration, and overcrowding can limit access to healthcare and education.
  6. Cultural Practices: Practices such as wife inheritance or polygamy may facilitate spread.
  7. Biological Factors: Age (young women are more biologically susceptible) and gender (women have a larger mucosal surface area).
2.
Outline three ways by which each of the following demographic factors influence health status of a person.
Show Answer

i) Age (3 marks)

  • Immunity: Extremes of age (very young and very old) have weaker immune systems, making them more susceptible to infections.
  • Disease Patterns: Children are prone to communicable diseases (e.g., measles), while older adults are prone to degenerative/chronic diseases (e.g., arthritis, hypertension).
  • Risk-Taking Behavior: Adolescents and young adults are more likely to engage in risky behaviors (e.g., substance abuse, speeding) leading to accidents and injuries.

ii) Gender (3 marks)

  • Reproductive Health: Women face specific health risks related to pregnancy and childbirth (e.g., maternal mortality).
  • Anatomical Susceptibility: Women are more biologically susceptible to certain STIs due to mucosal surface area.
  • Occupational Risks: Men are often more exposed to hazardous occupational environments (e.g., construction, mining) leading to injuries.
  • Health Seeking Behavior: Cultural norms often dictate that women seek health care more frequently than men, or conversely, may be disempowered from making health decisions.
3.
Describe the process of establishing a diagnosis of yellow fever. (5 marks)
Show Answer

Answer:

  1. Clinical Assessment (History & Physical Exam): Identify symptoms such as sudden onset of fever, jaundice (yellowing of eyes/skin), bleeding (hemorrhagic signs), muscle pain, and history of travel to endemic areas.
  2. Serological Tests (IgM ELISA): Detection of Yellow Fever-specific IgM antibodies in blood serum. This indicates recent infection.
  3. Polymerase Chain Reaction (PCR): Detection of viral genome (RNA) in blood or tissue samples during the early viremic phase (first few days).
  4. Virus Isolation: Culturing the virus from blood samples.
  5. Post-mortem Liver Biopsy: In fatal cases, liver tissue can be examined for characteristic histopathological changes (though rarely done due to bleeding risk).
4.
Outline five roles of a family in disease prevention. (5 marks)
Show Answer

Answer:

  1. Hygiene and Sanitation: maintaining a clean home environment and practicing personal hygiene (e.g., hand washing) to prevent infection spread.
  2. Nutrition: Providing balanced meals to build strong immunity against diseases.
  3. Immunization: Ensuring all eligible family members, especially children, receive scheduled vaccinations.
  4. Health Seeking Behavior: Recognizing signs of illness early and seeking prompt medical attention to prevent complications or spread.
  5. Health Education/Socialization: Teaching children healthy habits and lifestyle choices (e.g., avoiding drugs/alcohol).
  6. Emotional Support: Providing a stress-free environment which supports mental health and immune function.
5.
State four factors that make school going children be considered a vulnerable group. (4 marks)
Show Answer

Answer:

  1. Rapid Growth and Development: They are undergoing physical and psychological changes that require high nutritional and emotional support.
  2. Cross-Infection: The school environment involves crowding and mixing, which facilitates the rapid spread of communicable diseases.
  3. Dependence: They rely on adults (parents/teachers) for care, decision-making, and protection, making them vulnerable to neglect or abuse.
  4. Risk-Taking Behavior: They are prone to experimenting with drugs, alcohol, and dangerous physical activities leading to accidents.
  5. Developing Immunity: Their immune systems are still encountering new pathogens.
6.
Outline five health messages you would share with a mother after administration of BCG vaccine. (5 marks)
Show Answer

Answer:

  1. Explain the Normal Reaction: Inform her that a small red pimple/wheal will form, which may turn into a small ulcer/sore and finally heal leaving a small scar. This is normal.
  2. Wound Care: Do not squeeze, rub, or apply any medicaments, oils, or plasters on the injection site. Keep it clean and dry.
  3. Management of Swelling: If a large abscess forms or lymph nodes swell significantly, return to the clinic.
  4. Fever Management: If the child develops a mild fever, keep them cool or breastfeed frequently (give paracetamol only if advised).
  5. Confirmation of Take: Advise her to check for the scar after 3 months; if no scar is visible, she should report back to the clinic for assessment (re-immunization may be needed).
