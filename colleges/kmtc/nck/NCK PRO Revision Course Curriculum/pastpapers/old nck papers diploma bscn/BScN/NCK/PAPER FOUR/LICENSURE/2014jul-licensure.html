PART I: MULTIPLE CHOICE QUESTIONS

20 Marks
1.
The most appropriate pictorial presentation to demonstrate the relationship between age and weight is:-
a) Histogram.
b) Bar chart.
c) Scatter diagrams.
d) Pie chart.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Scatter diagrams.

Explanation: A scatter diagram (or scatter plot) is used to display the relationship (correlation) between two quantitative variables (like age and weight). Histograms and bar charts are for distributions of single variables, and pie charts show proportions.

2.
The most appropriate statistical technique used to test for significant differences between two (2) group means from data is:-
a) Paired t-test.
b) Student's t-test.
c) ANOVA.
d) Pearson correlation.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Student's t-test.

Explanation: The Student's t-test (specifically the independent samples t-test) is used to compare the means of two independent groups. Paired t-test is for related groups. ANOVA is for 3 or more groups.

3.
The selection of a sample such that every $n^{th}$ (e.g. every $5^{th}$) person or element in a sampling frame is selected is known as:-
a) Quota sampling.
b) Simple random sampling.
c) Systematic sampling.
d) Snowball sampling.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Systematic sampling.

Explanation: Systematic sampling involves selecting elements at regular intervals (every $k^{th}$ or $n^{th}$ item) from an ordered sampling frame.

4.
Triangulation in research can be defined as:-
a) The collection of descriptive data through direct observation and interviews.
b) Statistical tests in which both ends of the sampling distribution are used to determine improbable values.
c) Collection of data and use of different statistical tests at different points to enhance accuracy.
d) Collection and interpretation of data using multiple methods to converge on an accurate representation reality.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Collection and interpretation of data using multiple methods to converge on an accurate representation reality.

Explanation: Triangulation involves using multiple data sources, researchers, or methods (e.g., mixing qualitative and quantitative) to cross-verify findings and increase validity.

5.
Negatively skewed distribution is:-
a) Asymmetric distribution of data values with a disproportionately high number of cases at the upper end.
b) Asymmetric distribution of values with a disproportionately high number of cases at the lower end.
c) Symmetric distribution consisting of values which are superimposed on one another.
d) Symmetric distribution with values where high frequency forms one peak.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Asymmetric distribution of data values with a disproportionately high number of cases at the upper end.

Explanation: In a negatively skewed (left-skewed) distribution, the "tail" points to the left (negative numbers). This means the bulk of the data (the peak) is pushed to the right (upper end), meaning there is a high number of high values.

6.
During interview with a patient, the nurse becomes uncomfortable and realizes that the behaviour of the client reminds him/her of an abusive mother. This feeling by the nurse is:-
a) Counter transference.
b) Transference.
c) Projection.
d) Reaction formation.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Counter transference.

Explanation: Countertransference occurs when the healthcare provider (nurse) transfers their own feelings, conflicts, or experiences onto the patient (e.g., seeing their mother in the patient). Transference is when the patient does this to the nurse.

7.
The nurse's primary role in the care of a client with chemical dependence requiring acute care is:-
a) Provide psycho-education on dangers of drug and alcohol use.
b) Monitor and provide withdrawal care based on the unit protocol.
c) Review the problems in the client's relapse prevention blow.
d) Facilitate administration of anti-craving medications.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Monitor and provide withdrawal care based on the unit protocol.

Explanation: In the acute phase of chemical dependence (detoxification), the priority is physiological safety. Withdrawal from substances (especially alcohol and benzodiazepines) can be life-threatening. Monitoring vital signs and managing withdrawal symptoms is the primary role.

8.
In planning the care of a young child with oppositional defiant disorder, it is important to include:-
a) Reminiscence therapy.
b) Cognitive retraining.
c) Emotive therapy.
d) Behaviour modification.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Behaviour modification.

Explanation: ODD is characterized by uncooperative, defiant, and hostile behavior. Behavior modification (rewarding positive behavior, setting limits for negative behavior) and parent management training are the most effective evidence-based interventions for young children.

9.
The most important intervention when working with a client in crisis is:-
a) Obtaining a complete assessment of the client's past history.
b) Determining the client's contribution to the problem.
c) Remaining focused on the immediate problem.
d) Assisting client identify similarities in his/her crisis.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Remaining focused on the immediate problem.

Explanation: Crisis intervention is time-limited and focuses on the "here and now." The goal is to resolve the immediate crisis and return the client to their pre-crisis level of functioning, not to explore deep past history or personality dynamics.

10.
A priority nursing diagnostic label for a newly admitted patient in manic phase of bipolar disorder is:-
a) Ineffective individual coping.
b) Imbalanced nutrition, less than body requirements.
c) Risk for violence, self directed.
d) Sleep pattern, disturbed.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Risk for violence, self directed.

Explanation: Safety is always the highest priority. Patients in mania are impulsive, hyperactive, and lack judgment, putting them at high risk for injury to self or others. Note: While "Risk for violence, other-directed" is also common, "self-directed" (due to exhaustion or accidental injury) is a critical safety diagnosis over nutrition or sleep (which are also important but secondary to immediate safety).

PART II: SHORT ANSWER QUESTIONS

40 Marks
1.
Explain four (4) types of power that the nurse leaders use. (8 marks)
Show Answer
  1. Legitimate Power: Power derived from one's official position or title in the organization (e.g., Nurse Manager).
  2. Reward Power: The ability to grant rewards (e.g., pay raises, preferred schedules, praise) to influence behavior.
  3. Coercive Power: The ability to punish or withhold rewards (e.g., disciplinary action) to ensure compliance.
  4. Expert Power: Power derived from knowledge, special skills, or expertise that others respect and rely on.
  5. Referent Power: Power derived from the admiration and respect others have for the leader's personality or charisma (leading by example).
2.
An incident report is used as an organizations internal risk management tool. List ten (10) items/components that should be included in an incident report. (5 marks)
Show Answer
  1. Name of the person(s) involved (Patient/Staff).
  2. Patient's hospital number/ID.
  3. Date of the incident.
  4. Time of the incident.
  5. Exact location where the incident occurred.
  6. Factual description of what happened (objective details).
  7. Names of any witnesses.
  8. Actions taken immediately (e.g., first aid, vitals check).
  9. Name of the physician notified.
  10. Time the physician was notified.
  11. Condition of the patient before and after the incident.
  12. Signature of the person reporting.
3.
Explain the concept of Quality Management systems in health care organizations. (7 marks)
Show Answer

Quality Management Systems (QMS) in healthcare refer to a formalized system that documents processes, procedures, and responsibilities for achieving quality policies and objectives. It involves:

  • Continuous Quality Improvement (CQI): An ongoing effort to improve services and processes.
  • Customer Focus: Meeting the needs and expectations of patients (internal and external customers).
  • Data-Driven Decision Making: Using evidence and data to guide improvements.
  • Standardization: Using protocols and guidelines to reduce variation in care.
  • Total Quality Management (TQM): Involving all staff members in quality initiatives.
  • The goal is to ensure safety, effectiveness, patient-centeredness, timeliness, efficiency, and equity in healthcare delivery.
4.
Explain four (4) components/elements of an instructional objective. (8 marks)
Show Answer

A complete instructional objective often follows the ABCD format:

  1. Audience (Learner): Who is the learner? (e.g., "The nursing student will...").
  2. Behavior (Performance): What will the learner be able to do? Must be an observable and measurable verb (e.g., "state," "demonstrate," "list").
  3. Condition: Under what circumstances will the behavior be performed? (e.g., "Given a diagram," "Without reference to notes").
  4. Degree (Criterion): How well must the behavior be performed to be considered acceptable? (e.g., "with 100% accuracy," "identifying at least 3 signs").
5.
Explain the following principles of curriculum design:

a) Scope (2 marks): Refers to the breadth and depth of content to be covered in the curriculum. It defines what topics are included and how much detail is required at each level.

b) Sequence (2 marks): Refers to the order in which content is presented. It ensures a logical progression, typically from simple to complex, or from known to unknown (e.g., teaching anatomy before pathology).

c) Continuing (Continuity) (2 marks): Refers to the vertical repetition of major curriculum elements (skills, concepts) over time. It ensures that key themes are revisited at increasing levels of complexity to reinforce learning.

d) Integration (2 marks): Refers to the horizontal relationship between different subjects or areas of the curriculum. It helps learners see connections between disciplines (e.g., relating pharmacology to physiology).

1.
Mr J. aged 55 years is admitted to the mental health unit where you work. He is under involuntary admission and is diagnosed with temporal lobe epilepsy. You notice that he is also very paranoid.

a) Explain the legal requirements for admission under involuntary order. (4 marks)

  1. Application: Must be made by a relative (spouse or close relative) or an authorized officer (e.g., police officer, chief) who has seen the patient within 14 days.
  2. Recommendation: Must be supported by a medical practitioner (doctor) stating that the person is suffering from a mental disorder and requires detention for their own safety or the safety of others.
  3. Reception Order: The person in charge of the mental hospital must scrutinize the documents to ensure they are valid before accepting the patient.
  4. Duration: The admission is valid for a specific period (usually up to 72 hours initially for emergency, or longer based on specific sections of the Mental Health Act) after which it must be reviewed.

b) Draw and label a diagram of the human brain to illustrate functional areas. (6 marks)

(Note: As an AI text model, I cannot draw, but you should sketch the brain and label the following areas:)

  • Frontal Lobe: Personality, behavior, emotions, intellectual function (Broca's area - speech production).
  • Parietal Lobe: Sensation (touch, pressure, pain).
  • Temporal Lobe: Hearing, taste, smell, memory (Wernicke's area - speech comprehension). *Critical for this patient due to temporal lobe epilepsy.*
  • Occipital Lobe: Visual reception.
  • Cerebellum: Motor coordination, equilibrium, balance.
  • Brain Stem: Vital functions (breathing, heart rate).

c) Describe three (3) priority nursing interventions for Mr. J in the first 24 hours of admission. (10 marks)

  1. Ensure Safety (Seizure & Violence Precautions):
    • Implement seizure precautions (padded side rails, suction equipment at bedside, bed in low position).
    • Monitor for signs of aggression due to paranoia. Maintain a safe distance, use a calm non-threatening tone.
    • Remove dangerous objects from the environment.
  2. Assessment and Monitoring:
    • Complete physical and mental status examination.
    • Observe for aura or seizure activity.
    • Monitor vital signs regularly.
    • Assess the content of paranoid delusions to evaluate risk of harm to self or others.
  3. Medication Administration and Management:
    • Administer prescribed anticonvulsants (to manage epilepsy) and antipsychotics (to manage paranoia) strictly on time.
    • Monitor for side effects of medications.
    • Ensure the patient actually swallows the medication (check for cheeking) especially since he is paranoid (suspicious of poisoning).
2.
The quality of a research study is dependent on extent to which a researcher ensures validity and reliability.

a) Describe methods/ways of controlling confounding variables to enhance validity. (12 marks)

  1. Randomization: Randomly assigning subjects to experimental and control groups ensures that extraneous variables are distributed equally, minimizing their systematic effect.
  2. Homogeneity (Restriction): Restricting the sample to subjects who are similar in terms of confounding variables (e.g., only including men to remove gender as a confounder).
  3. Matching: Consciously pairing subjects in the experimental and control groups based on specific characteristics (e.g., matching by age and weight).
  4. Statistical Control: Using statistical techniques (like Analysis of Covariance - ANCOVA) during data analysis to statistically adjust for the effects of confounding variables.
  5. Design Control: Using specific research designs (like crossover designs) where subjects serve as their own controls.

b) Explain the differences between experimental and experimental (likely meant Non-experimental or Quasi-experimental) research design. (4 marks)

Assuming the comparison is between True Experimental and Quasi-Experimental/Non-Experimental:

  • True Experimental Design: Characterized by three key properties: Randomization (random assignment to groups), Control (use of a control group), and Manipulation (of the independent variable). It provides the strongest evidence for causality.
  • Quasi-Experimental Design: Lacks one or more of the key properties of true experiments, usually Randomization. It involves manipulation but uses non-randomized groups (e.g., existing classes), making it weaker in establishing causality but more practical in some field settings.

c) Explain the difference between type I error and type II error. (4 marks)

  • Type I Error (False Positive): Occurs when the researcher rejects the null hypothesis when it is actually true. (claiming there is an effect/difference when there is none). It is related to the level of significance ($\alpha$).
  • Type II Error (False Negative): Occurs when the researcher accepts (fails to reject) the null hypothesis when it is actually false. (claiming there is no effect/difference when there actually is one). It is related to the power of the study.
