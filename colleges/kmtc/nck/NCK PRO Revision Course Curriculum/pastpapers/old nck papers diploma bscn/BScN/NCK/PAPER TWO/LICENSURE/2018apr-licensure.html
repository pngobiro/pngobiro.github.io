Part I: Multiple Choice Questions

20 Marks
1.
Hormones involved in lactation are:-
a) Prolactin and oestrogen.
b) Human placental lactogen and prolactin.
c) Progesterone and oxytocin.
d) Prolactin and oxytocin.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Prolactin and oxytocin.

Explanation: Prolactin is responsible for milk production (synthesis) by the alveoli, while Oxytocin is responsible for the milk ejection reflex (let-down) by contracting the myoepithelial cells.

2.
Kim is eight (8) weeks pregnant. On assessment, a nurse determines that she has gonococcal infection. The significance of this information is that the pregnancy is at risk of:-
a) Spontaneous abortion.
b) Result in thrush in the newborn.
c) Cause opthalmia neonatorum.
d) Increase the risk of acquired immune deficiency syndrome.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Cause opthalmia neonatorum.

Explanation: Untreated Gonorrhea can be transmitted to the newborn's eyes during passage through the birth canal, causing ophthalmia neonatorum (gonococcal conjunctivitis), which can lead to blindness if untreated.

3.
The advise you would give a rhesus-negative mother on RhO (D) Immune globulin administration would be:-
a) Administration of immune globulin at twenty eight (28) weeks gestation and within 72 hours after birth.
b) Administration of immune globulin before conception and within 48 hours after birth.
c) Administration of immune globulin at twenty (28) weeks gestation and within forty eight (48) hours after birth.
d) Administration of immune globulin before conception and within seventy two (72) hours after birth.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Administration of immune globulin at twenty eight (28) weeks gestation and within 72 hours after birth.

Explanation: Standard prophylaxis involves giving Anti-D Ig at 28 weeks of gestation (antenatal prophylaxis) and a second dose within 72 hours of delivery if the baby is Rh-positive.

4.
The manifestation in a woman who delivered a newborn thirty six (36) hours ago that would lead a midwife to suspect a post partum depression is:-
a) Expressing concern about taking the infant life.
b) Delay her morning shower.
c) Exhibiting prolonged periods of fatigue.
d) Asking repeated questions about infant care.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Expressing concern about taking the infant life.

Explanation: Thoughts of harming the infant are a serious sign of postpartum psychiatric illness (depression or psychosis). Fatigue (c), delaying showers (b), and anxiety/questions (d) can be normal adjustments or "baby blues," especially at 36 hours. Option A is a distinct pathological red flag.

5.
The complication likely to occur during delivery of shoulder dystocia is:-
a) Klumpke's palsy.
b) Shoulder dislocation.
c) Erb palsy.
d) Facial nerve damage.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Erb palsy.

Explanation: Erb's palsy (injury to the upper brachial plexus, C5-C6) is a common fetal complication associated with shoulder dystocia due to traction on the head and neck during delivery.

6.
To reduce chances of mothers having complications of pregnancy on nerval tube defects, the mother is preferably addressed on:-
a) Taking iron and ferrous sulphate tablet in pregnancy till delivery.
b) Taking iron and ferrous sulphate tablets before conception.
c) Taking iron and ferrous sulphate in the 2nd trimester of pregnancy.
d) Taking iron and ferrous sulphate tablets in the 3rd trimester of pregnancy.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Taking iron and ferrous sulphate tablets before conception.

Note: The question likely contains a typo and meant Folic Acid for neural tube defects (NTDs). Prevention of NTDs requires supplementation before conception (pre-conception care) and during the first trimester. Among the options provided, "before conception" is the correct timing for NTD prevention logic.

7.
For a woman who is at thirty two (32) weeks gestation and has been having vaginal bleeding for the last four (4) hours, the priority would be:-
a) Assess bleeding by giving a packet of pads, advice on bed rest, monitor fetal heart rate.
b) Conduct a speculum examination, monitor fetal heart rate, advocate for an ultrasound.
c) Assess bleeding by giving a packet of pads, call for help, monitor fetal heart rate.
d) Bed rest, ultrasound, speculum examination.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Assess bleeding by giving a packet of pads, call for help, monitor fetal heart rate.

Explanation: This is a case of Antepartum Haemorrhage (APH). The priority is stabilization and assessment of fetal wellbeing. "Call for help" indicates the urgency. Pads allow for quantification of bleeding. Speculum exams (b/d) should only be done after placenta praevia is ruled out or in a controlled setting, but immediate assessment of the fetus and maternal status (calling for help) takes precedence.

8.
The observation a midwife should anticipate from a pregnant mother with a history of heart disease is:-
a) Slow pulse rate.
b) Decreased blood pressure.
c) Dyspnea.
d) Haemorrhage.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Dyspnea.

Explanation: Progressive dyspnea (shortness of breath), along with tachycardia and fatigue, are classic signs of cardiac decompensation in pregnancy as the heart struggles with the increased hemodynamic burden.

9.
Prevention of deep venous thrombosis during pueperium involves:-
a) Confinement to bed, avoidance of pressure, postnatal exercises.
b) Early ambulation, plenty of oral fluid, avoidance of pressure.
c) Prolonged inactivity, postnatal exercises, plenty of oral fluids.
d) Operative deliveries, early ambulation, prolong inactivity.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Early ambulation, plenty of oral fluid, avoidance of pressure.

Explanation: Early ambulation is the most effective nursing intervention to prevent DVT. Hydration reduces blood viscosity. Inactivity (a, c) promotes stasis and thrombosis.

10.
The M C Roberts maneuver is applied in the delivery of shoulder dystocia by:-
a) To create gentle pressure on her abdomen, releasing the impaction of the anterior shoulder.
b) Applying pressure on the fetal back in order to help adduct the shoulders away from the symphysis pubis.
c) Identifying the posterior shoulder on vaginal examination, then to push the posterior shoulder towards the fetal chest.
d) The midwife inserting her hands into the vagina, identify the fetal chest and rotating the posterior fetal shoulder for delivery.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) To create gentle pressure on her abdomen, releasing the impaction of the anterior shoulder.

Explanation: The question likely refers to the management sequence involving McRoberts maneuver (flexing mother's legs) often combined with Suprapubic Pressure (described in option A). Suprapubic pressure is applied to dislodge the anterior shoulder. Option B, C, and D describe internal rotation maneuvers (Rubin/Woods).

11.
The type of breech in which thighs are flexed with legs extended is called:-
a) Complete breech.
b) Frank breech.
c) Footing breech.
d) Extended breech.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Frank breech.

Explanation: In a Frank breech, the fetal hips are flexed, and the knees are extended (feet near the head). This is the most common type of breech presentation.

12.
The immediate management of gestational trophoblastic disease is:-
a) Excavation, control of bleeding, administration of methotraxate.
b) Family planning, excavation, administration of methotraxate.
c) Control of bleeding, family planning, evacuation.
d) Evacuation, control of bleeding, anti D Immune globulin administration.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Evacuation, control of bleeding, anti D Immune globulin administration.

Explanation: The primary treatment is uterine evacuation (usually suction curettage). Anti-D is given if the mother is Rh-negative. Methotrexate is used for malignant/persistent disease, not always immediately for all moles.

13.
Sub-involution of the uterus is suspected when:-
a) The uterus remain bulky with persistent red lochia.
b) When after pains persist with breastfeeding.
c) When the fundal height reduces progressively.
d) When the lochia loss corresponds with a given post-partum period.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) The uterus remain bulky with persistent red lochia.

Explanation: Sub-involution is the failure of the uterus to return to its non-pregnant state. Signs include a higher-than-expected fundal height (bulky uterus) and prolonged/persistent lochia rubra (red bleeding).

14.
The daily observation of a postnatal mother in the ward include:-
a) Inspection of the perineum, taking weight, assessing involution of the uterus, observation of vital signs.
b) Observation of vital signs, lochia loss, involution of the uterus, inspection of the perineum.
c) Palpation of the calf muscles, urine testing, bowel movements, breast examination.
d) Urinalysis, vaginal examination, assessment of lochia loss, sleeping patterns.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Observation of vital signs, lochia loss, involution of the uterus, inspection of the perineum.

Explanation: These are the core components of the daily postnatal assessment (BUBBLE-LE or similar mnemonic) to detect hemorrhage, infection, and ensure proper recovery.

15.
The best management of varicose veins during pregnancy is:-
a) Using supporting tights or crepe bandage, resting with feet elevated.
b) Avoiding standing for too long, wearing loose clothing.
c) Avoid sitting for a long time, elevating feet when sleeping.
d) Wearing low heeled shoes, elevated feet when sleeping.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Using supporting tights or crepe bandage, resting with feet elevated.

Explanation: Compression (support tights) assists venous return, and elevation reduces hydrostatic pressure, alleviating symptoms and preventing worsening.

16.
The following is a correct statement about primary dysmenorrhea:-
a) Symptoms become more severe in multigravid women.
b) It is associated with an underlying pelvis pathology.
c) Spasmodic lower abdominal pain begins with onset of menses.
d) The probable cause is endometrial Prostagladin deficit.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Spasmodic lower abdominal pain begins with onset of menses.

Explanation: Primary dysmenorrhea is characterized by spasmodic pain starting just before or at the onset of menses. It is caused by an excess (not deficit) of prostaglandins and is not associated with pelvic pathology (which defines secondary dysmenorrhea).

17.
Surgical treatment of a client with Rectocele and a cytocele include :-
a) Anterior colporrhapy.
b) Posterior colporrhapy.
c) Abdominal hysterectomy.
d) Anterioposterior colporrhaphy.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Anterioposterior colporrhaphy.

Explanation: A cystocele (anterior wall prolapse) is repaired with Anterior Colporrhaphy. A rectocele (posterior wall prolapse) is repaired with Posterior Colporrhaphy. When both are present, an A-P Colporrhaphy is performed.

18.
Menometrorrhagis is defined as:-
a) Irregular and excessive flow.
b) Menstrual flow at irregular intervals.
c) Bleeding at intervals of less than twenty one (21) days.
d) Bleeding after sexual intercourse.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Irregular and excessive flow.

Explanation: Menometrorrhagia combines features of menorrhagia (excessive/prolonged bleeding) and metrorrhagia (irregular bleeding), resulting in excessive bleeding at irregular intervals.

19.
The nurse while assessing a primipara during the immediate postpartum period, puts one hand on the fundus to:-

Part II: Short Answer Questions

40 Marks
1.
Draw a well labeled diagram showing the antero posterior diameters of the fetal skull and their measurements. (7 marks)
Show Answer

The diagram should illustrate the following diameters:

  1. Sub-occipitobregmatic: 9.5 cm (Measured from the nape of the neck to the centre of the bregma). The presenting diameter in a fully flexed vertex presentation.
  2. Sub-occipitofrontal: 10.0 cm (From nape of neck to sinciput).
  3. Occipitofrontal: 11.5 cm (From occipital protuberance to glabella/bridge of nose). Presenting in deflexed vertex/OP.
  4. Mentovertical: 13.5 cm (From chin to highest point of vertex). The longest diameter; presenting in brow presentation.
  5. Sub-mentobregmatic: 9.5 cm (From junction of neck and chin to centre of bregma). Presenting in face presentation.
2.
State the four (4) PMTCT prongs in the management of HIV/AIDS. (4 marks)
Show Answer
  1. Prong 1: Prevention of primary HIV infection in women of reproductive age.
  2. Prong 2: Prevention of unintended pregnancies in HIV-positive women.
  3. Prong 3: Prevention of mother-to-child transmission of HIV (PMTCT) during pregnancy, delivery, and breastfeeding.
  4. Prong 4: Provision of care, treatment, and support to HIV-infected women, their infants, and families.
3.
State five (5) theories of onset of labour. (5 marks)
Show Answer
  1. Oxytocin Stimulation Theory: Increase in oxytocin receptors and sensitivity leads to contractions.
  2. Progesterone Withdrawal Theory: Drop in progesterone removes the "relaxing" effect on the myometrium.
  3. Estrogen Stimulation Theory: Rising estrogen levels enhance uterine sensitivity to oxytocin and prostaglandin synthesis.
  4. Uterine Distension/Stretch Theory: Mechanical stretching of the uterus by the growing fetus releases prostaglandins.
  5. Fetal Cortisol Theory: Fetal adrenal glands secrete cortisol, which triggers a cascade leading to prostaglandin release.
  6. Prostaglandin Theory: Increased synthesis of prostaglandins acts as a potent stimulator of contractions.

5.
State four (4) danger signs in labour. (4 marks)
Show Answer
  1. Vaginal Bleeding: Indicating abruption or placenta previa (APH).
  2. Cord Prolapse: Umbilical cord visible or felt at the vulva.
  3. Fetal Distress: Fetal heart rate <120 or >160 bpm, or meconium-stained liquor in cephalic presentation.
  4. Maternal Distress: High BP, rapid pulse, fever, or signs of exhaustion/dehydration.
  5. Obstructed Labour: Severe constant pain, Bandl's ring, edema of vulva.
  6. Severe Headache/Visual Disturbances: Signs of imminent eclampsia.
6.
State five (5) important messages you would give to a primigravida on exclusive breastfeeding. (5 marks)
Show Answer
  1. Definition: Give ONLY breast milk for the first 6 months (no water, formula, or other foods).
  2. Initiation: Initiate breastfeeding within the first hour of birth.
  3. Frequency: Breastfeed on demand (whenever baby is hungry), day and night (8-12 times a day).
  4. Technique: Proper positioning and attachment to prevent sore nipples and ensure effective feeding.
  5. No Artificial Teats: Avoid bottles and pacifiers to prevent nipple confusion.
  6. Maternal Care: Eat a balanced diet and drink plenty of fluids to maintain supply.
7.
Explain three (3) ways of expecting a diagnosis of polyhydramnios in a client without the use of an ultrasound. (6 marks)
Show Answer
  1. Abdominal Inspection/Palpation: Uterine size/Fundal height is significantly larger than expected for the period of gestation; skin appears tense and shiny with striae gravidarum.
  2. Fetal Palpation: Fetal parts are difficult to palpate due to excessive fluid; fluid thrill can be elicited. Fetal lie may be unstable.
  3. Auscultation: Fetal heart sounds are muffled or difficult to hear due to the increased distance between the fetus and the abdominal wall.
8.
State four (4) ways of preventing infection transmission during 2nd stage of delivery. (4 marks)
Show Answer
  1. Aseptic Technique: Use of sterile delivery packs, instruments, and drapes.
  2. Personal Protective Equipment (PPE): Midwife must wear sterile gloves, gown, mask, and eye protection.
  3. Perineal Hygiene: Cleaning the perineal area with antiseptic solution before delivery; preventing fecal contamination from the rectum (covering anus with sterile pad).
  4. Limit Vaginal Examinations: Reducing the frequency of digital exams to minimize introduction of pathogens.
  5. Hand Hygiene: Thorough scrubbing before donning sterile gloves.

Part III: Essay/Long Answer Questions

40 Marks
1.

Miss K is a primigravida who comes for her third focused antenatal care clinic visit at thirty two (32) weeks gestation. She is accompanied by relatives. On arrival, her vital signs are as follows: Blood pressure 180/160 mmHg, pulse 100 beats per minute (regular) temperature 37Â° C, respirations 30 breaths per minute. Fetal heart rate is irregular at 140 beats per minute. She complains of headache, heartburn and slight lower abdominal pains. Her relatives report of her losing consciousness once at home but later woke up.

a) What is the probable diagnosis for Miss K.? (2 marks)

b) Describe the pathophysiology of the condition that Miss K is having. (6 marks)

c) Using the nursing process, describe the management of Miss K. (12 marks)

Show Answer

a) Diagnosis:

Eclampsia (She has signs of severe Pre-eclampsia [BP 180/160, Headache, Epigastric pain/heartburn] and a history of "losing consciousness" implies a seizure/fit occurred).

b) Pathophysiology of Eclampsia/Pre-eclampsia:

  • Abnormal Placentation: Failure of trophoblast invasion into the spiral arteries leads to high resistance and placental ischemia/hypoxia.
  • Endothelial Dysfunction: Ischemic placenta releases factors causing widespread maternal endothelial damage.
  • Vasospasm: Generalized vasoconstriction leads to Hypertension (BP elevation) and tissue hypoperfusion.
  • Capillary Leakage: Increased permeability causes edema (cerebral edema leads to headaches/seizures; pulmonary edema; proteinuria).
  • Organ Damage:
    • Brain: Cerebral edema and vasospasm cause visual disturbances, hyperreflexia, and seizures (Eclampsia).
    • Liver: Ischemia causes epigastric pain (heartburn) and elevated enzymes (HELLP).
    • Kidney: Glomerular damage causes proteinuria.

c) Management (Nursing Process):

  1. Assessment:
    • Monitor GCS/Consciousness level.
    • Assess Vital Signs (BP, Pulse, Resp) frequently (every 15-30 mins).
    • Assess Fetal Heart Rate (irregular 140bpm indicates distress).
    • Check for impending signs of another fit: Hyperreflexia, severe headache, epigastric pain.
  2. Diagnosis:
    • Risk for injury (maternal/fetal) related to seizure activity.
    • Altered tissue perfusion (cerebral/placental) related to vasospasm.
    • Ineffective airway clearance related to potential aspiration during seizure.
  3. Planning:
    • Prevent further seizures.
    • Control Blood Pressure.
    • Deliver a live infant.
  4. Intervention:
    • Airway/Safety: Place in left lateral position to prevent aspiration and improve placental flow. Have airway and suction ready. Pad cot sides.
    • Seizure Control (Magnesium Sulphate): Administer Loading Dose (4g IV slowly + 10g IM) followed by maintenance dose. Monitor for toxicity (RR <12, absent reflexes, low urine output). antidote: Calcium Gluconate.
    • BP Control: Administer anti-hypertensives (e.g., Hydralazine 5mg IV or Labetalol) to lower BP slowly preventing stroke.
    • Fetal Monitoring: Continuous monitoring. Prepare for urgent delivery (CS likely due to fetal distress and maternal condition) once stable.
    • Environment: Nurse in a quiet, dark room to reduce stimuli.
  5. Evaluation: BP stabilizes, no further seizures, safe delivery of baby.
2.

Madam G is a para 1 who has just delivered a live baby girl through spontaneous vaginal delivery 2.0 Kgs. APGAR score was 9/1, 10/5, 10/10. Baby was term according to the dates and ultrasound confirmation. Baby has no congenital malformations mother reports having attended all antenatal clinics and followed instructions on screening, drug administration and vaccines. Mothers height is 5 feet 2 inches. Weight before delivery was 52 Kgs from a baseline of 42 Kgs. Impression of small for gestational age for the baby is made.

a) Define small for gestational age. (1 mark)

b) State five (5) causes for small for gestational age. (5 marks)

c) State six (6) benefits of kangaroo care. (6 marks)

d) Explain four (4) messages / health education that the nurse should give to the mother of the child. (8 marks)

Show Answer

a) Definition:

Small for Gestational Age (SGA) refers to an infant whose birth weight is below the 10th percentile for their gestational age.

b) Causes (Etiology):

  1. Maternal Malnutrition: Low pre-pregnancy weight (42kg) and poor weight gain.
  2. Maternal Medical Conditions: Hypertension (PIH), Anemia, Chronic Renal Disease.
  3. Placental Insufficiency: Poor blood flow, small placenta, infarction.
  4. Infections: Intrauterine infections (TORCH, Malaria).
  5. Substance Abuse: Smoking, alcohol consumption.
  6. Constitutional: Small parents (Mother is 5ft 2in, small build).

c) Benefits of Kangaroo Mother Care (KMC):

  1. Thermoregulation: Maintains baby's body temperature (prevents hypothermia).
  2. Breastfeeding: Facilitates exclusive breastfeeding and weight gain.
  3. Bonding: Promotes emotional bonding between mother and baby.
  4. Physiological Stability: Stabilizes heart rate and respiration (reduces apnea).
  5. Infection Control: Reduces risk of nosocomial infections.
  6. Early Discharge: Reduces length of hospital stay.

d) Health Education Messages:

  1. Nutrition/Breastfeeding: Emphasize exclusive breastfeeding on demand (at least every 2-3 hours) to promote catch-up growth and prevent hypoglycemia. Ensure mother eats a balanced diet.
  2. Thermoregulation: Keep the baby warm at all times (KMC, warm clothing, hat) as SGA babies have little subcutaneous fat and lose heat easily ("Warm Chain").
  3. Infection Prevention: Hand washing before handling baby, restricting visitors, and keeping the cord clean and dry to prevent sepsis.
  4. Follow-up/Monitoring: Importance of attending clinic for weight monitoring, immunization, and developmental assessment to detect any delays early.
