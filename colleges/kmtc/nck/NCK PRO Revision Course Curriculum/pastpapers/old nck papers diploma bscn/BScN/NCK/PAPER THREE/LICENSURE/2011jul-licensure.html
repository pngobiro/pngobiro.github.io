Part I: Multiple Choice Questions

20 Marks
1.
Activities categorized as secondary disease prevention include:-
a) Blood pressure screening, guarding against accidents, actual treatment of disease
b) Chemoprophylaxis, annual pap smear test, supply of safe drinking water
c) Routine mammography, blood pressure screening, annual pap smear test
d) Periodic determination of blood cholesterol, supply of safe drinking water
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Routine mammography, blood pressure screening, annual pap smear test

Explanation: Secondary prevention focuses on early diagnosis and prompt treatment to limit disability. Screening tests like mammograms, BP checks, and Pap smears are classic examples. Option B and D include "supply of safe drinking water," which is primary prevention.

2.
The occupational disease that would occur in workers employed in a sugar cane factory is:-
a) Agranulocytosis
b) Bagassosis
c) Byssinosis
d) Siderosis
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Bagassosis

Explanation: Bagassosis is a respiratory disease caused by inhaling dust from bagasse (the fibrous residue of sugar cane). Byssinosis is caused by cotton dust; Siderosis by iron dust.

3.
An abiotic environment that can affect health of the community is:-
a) Infective organisms
b) Insects
c) Vegetation
d) Chemicals
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Chemicals

Explanation: Abiotic factors are non-living chemical and physical parts of the environment. Chemicals are non-living. Infective organisms, insects, and vegetation are biotic (living).

4.
The measurement rate that is used to determine the number of new cases of a disease over an interval of time is:-
a) Incidence rate
b) Prevalence rate
c) Case fatality rate
d) Endemicities
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Incidence rate

Explanation: Incidence rate specifically measures new cases arising in a population over a given period. Prevalence measures existing cases (new + old).

5.
The term used to describe malaria endemicity patterns where transmission occurs all the year round is:-
a) Holoendemic
b) Hypoendemic
c) Hyperendemic
d) Mesoendemic
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Holoendemic

Explanation: Holoendemic areas are characterized by intense, perennial (year-round) transmission, where a high degree of immunity is found in the adult population.

6.
The fungal skin infection characterized by thickening and discoloration of the nails is called:-
a) Tinea versicolor
b) Tinea pedis
c) Intertrigo
d) Tinea ungium
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Tinea ungium

Explanation: Tinea unguium (also known as Onychomycosis) affects the nails, causing thickening and discoloration. Tinea pedis is Athlete's foot.

7.
The infection of the genital tract where the discharge has a fishy or ammonia odour is:-
a) Syphilis
b) Gonorrhoea
c) Trichomoniasis
d) Candidiasis
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Trichomoniasis

Explanation: While a "fishy" odor is classically associated with Bacterial Vaginosis (not listed), Trichomoniasis is the only STI among the options that produces a foul-smelling discharge. Candidiasis is odorless; Gonorrhea is purulent; Syphilis presents as an ulcer.

8.
A zoonotic disease that is caused by a spore forming bacillus is:-
a) Brucellosis
b) Rabies
c) Anthrax
d) Plague
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Anthrax

Explanation: Anthrax is caused by Bacillus anthracis, a spore-forming bacterium. Rabies is viral; Brucellosis and Plague are bacterial but not spore-forming in the same characteristic manner as Anthrax (bacillus).

9.
A research instrument that is able to give same measurements when recreated several times is said to be:-
a) Predictable
b) Precise
c) Valid
d) Reliable
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Reliable

Explanation: Reliability refers to the consistency of a measureâ€”whether it produces the same results under the same conditions.

10.
The statistical measurement that takes into account all scores obtained and responded to the exact position of every score relative to the mean of that distribution is the:-
a) Standard deviation
b) Interquartile range
c) Mode
d) Median
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Standard deviation

Explanation: Standard deviation is calculated using the mean and accounts for the distance of every single score from that mean. Median and Mode only look at position or frequency, not the value of every score relative to a mean.

11.
The hypothesis that rivals the null hypothesis is referred to as:-
a) Directional hypothesis
b) Subjective hypothesis
c) Statistical Inference
d) Alternative hypothesis
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Alternative hypothesis

Explanation: The alternative hypothesis (H1) is the statement that contradicts the null hypothesis (H0), usually stating that there is a significant difference or relationship.

12.
Family descent where the membership is acquired through the male line is:-
a) Matrilineal
b) Patrilineal
c) Matrilocal
d) Patrilocal
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Patrilineal

Explanation: Patrilineal descent is established by tracing descent exclusively through males. Patrilocal refers to residence/housing, not descent.

13.
The home visiting phase where the nurse designs a plan of action for the family is:-
a) Pre-visit phase
b) Initiation phase
c) Working phase
d) Termination phase
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Pre-visit phase

Explanation: During the pre-visit (planning) phase, the nurse reviews records and designs a preliminary plan of action before meeting the family.

14.
Biphasic pills include:-
a) Logynon, Trinordial
b) Microgynon, Lo-femenal
c) Ovanon, Normovlar
d) Nordette, Marvelon
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Ovanon, Normovlar

Explanation: This is a classic classification question. Ovanon is a sequential pill often categorized with biphasic in older texts because the composition changes once. Logynon is Triphasic. Microgynon, Nordette, and Marvelon are Monophasic.

15.
Following vasectomy procedure, the client should be advised to use condoms or another family planning method for a duration of:-
a) 1 month
b) 2 months
c) 3 months
d) 4 months
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) 3 months

Explanation: It typically takes about 3 months (or approximately 20 ejaculations) to clear the remaining sperm from the vas deferens after a vasectomy.

16.
The age at which a child is likely to conceptualize abstract ideas is:-
a) 6 years
b) 8 years
c) 10 years
d) 12 years
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) 12 years

Explanation: According to Piaget's stages of cognitive development, the "Formal Operational Stage" where abstract thinking develops begins around age 12.

17.
Water-washed diseases include:-
a) Typhoid, poliomyelitis
b) Scabies, Trachoma
c) Amoebiasis, cholera
d) Malaria, schistosomiasis
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Scabies, Trachoma

Explanation: Water-washed diseases (like Scabies and Trachoma) are caused by a lack of water for personal hygiene, rather than by contaminated water (Water-borne like Cholera).

18.
In health education, the principle that best addressed the felt needs of the people is:-
a) Interest
b) Motivation
c) Re-enforcement
d) Active learning
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Interest

Explanation: The principle of Interest states that people are most likely to listen to and learn about things that they feel a need for (their "felt needs") or are interested in.

19.
Some of the health issues classified under the high priority health package in Kenya are:-
a) Reproductive cancers, skin diseases
b) Accidents, drug and substance abuse
c) Reproductive health, malaria prevention
d) Mental health, control of vector-borne diseases
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Reproductive health, malaria prevention

Explanation: Under the Kenya Essential Package for Health (KEPH), reproductive health (including maternal/child health) and control of major communicable diseases like malaria are top priorities.

20.
Increasing the ability of the population to cope with a disaster is known as:-
a) Preparedness
b) Triaging
c) Mobilization
d) Mitigation
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Preparedness

Explanation: Preparedness involves building capacity and planning to respond effectively and "cope" when a disaster strikes. Mitigation aims to prevent or reduce the severity of the disaster itself.

Part II: Short Answer Questions

40 Marks
1.
Explain the normal reaction at the injection site following administration of BCG vaccine (5 marks)
Show Answer

Answer:

  1. Immediate: A wheal of about 6-10mm diameter is raised during the intradermal injection, which disappears within 30 minutes.
  2. 2-3 Weeks: A small red papule (swelling) develops at the site.
  3. 4-5 Weeks: The papule may break down to form a small ulcer or pustule (pus-filled spot), which is normal.
  4. Healing: The ulcer crusts over and heals slowly over several weeks.
  5. Final Outcome (12 Weeks): A small, permanent scar (about 5mm) remains, indicating successful immunization.
2.
Explain three anthropometric measurements used to monitor growth of children under five years of age (6 marks)
Show Answer

Answer:

  1. Weight-for-Age: Measures body mass; used to detect underweight and monitor general growth trajectory on the "Road to Health" chart. It is sensitive to acute changes.
  2. Height/Length-for-Age: Measures linear growth; used to detect stunting (chronic malnutrition). Length is measured for children under 2 years (lying down), height for those over 2 years (standing).
  3. Mid-Upper Arm Circumference (MUAC): Measures muscle mass and subcutaneous fat; used for rapid screening of wasting (acute malnutrition), especially in community settings (e.g., MUAC < 11.5cm indicates severe acute malnutrition).
3.
Outline eight factors to be considered in the planning and dissemination of group health education messages (8 marks)
Show Answer

Answer:

  1. Target Audience: Understanding their age, education level, culture, and beliefs.
  2. Topic Relevance: Choosing a subject that addresses a felt need or current health problem in the community.
  3. Objectives: Setting clear, measurable goals for what the session should achieve.
  4. Venue/Environment: Ensuring the location is comfortable, quiet, and accessible.
  5. Methods/Teaching Aids: Selecting appropriate tools (charts, demos) and methods (discussion, lecture) to enhance learning.
  6. Language: Using simple, clear language that the audience understands, avoiding jargon.
  7. Time/Duration: Scheduling at a convenient time and keeping the session concise to maintain attention.
  8. Evaluation: Planning how to assess if the message was understood (e.g., Q&A session).
4.
State four goals focused antenatal care (4 marks)
Show Answer

Answer:

  1. Early detection and treatment of pre-existing conditions and complications (e.g., anemia, hypertension).
  2. Prevention of complications and disease (e.g., Tetanus toxoid immunization, IPT for malaria, iron/folate supplementation).
  3. Birth Preparedness and Complication Readiness: Helping the mother plan for delivery, transport, and emergencies.
  4. Health Promotion and Counseling: Educating on nutrition, hygiene, breastfeeding, and family planning.
5.
State eight principles of community health nursing practice (8 marks)
Show Answer

Answer:

  1. The Community is the Client: Focus is on the group/population rather than just individuals.
  2. Prevention is Primary: Emphasis is on health promotion and disease prevention (primary prevention).
  3. Multidisciplinary Collaboration: Working with other sectors (agriculture, education) to address health determinants.
  4. Community Participation: Involving the community in planning and decision-making.
  5. Equity/Accessibility: Health services should be available to all, regardless of status or location.
  6. Use of Available Resources: Utilizing appropriate technology and local resources effectively.
  7. Health Education: Empowering people with knowledge to manage their own health.
  8. Evaluation and Research: Continuous assessment of health status and program effectiveness.
6.
Explain five sources of demographic information (5 marks)
Show Answer

Answer:

  1. Population Census: A complete enumeration of the population conducted periodically (usually every 10 years).
  2. Vital Registration Systems: Continuous recording of vital events like births, deaths, and marriages.
  3. Sample Surveys: Studies of a subset of the population (e.g., Kenya Demographic and Health Survey - KDHS).
  4. Health Facility Records: Data collected from clinics/hospitals (morbidity and mortality data).
  5. Migration Records: Data from immigration departments regarding movement of people across borders.
7.
State the criteria used to determine that a child is fully immunized (4 marks)
Show Answer

Answer:

A child is considered fully immunized (FIC) when they have received all the following standard KEPI vaccines by the age of 1 year:

  1. BCG: 1 dose (usually at birth).
  2. Polio (OPV): 4 doses (Birth, 6wks, 10wks, 14wks).
  3. Pentavalent (DPT-HepB-Hib): 3 doses (6wks, 10wks, 14wks).
  4. Measles: 1 dose (at 9 months).
  5. Pneumococcal (PCV): 3 doses (6wks, 10wks, 14wks) - [Note: Included in modern schedule].

Part III: Long Answer Questions

40 Marks
1.
Mr. Kuzi, aged 37yrs, has been diagnosed with multi-drug resistant tuberculosis.
Show Answer

a) DEFINE Multi-drug Resistant tuberculosis (1mrk)

MDR-TB is a form of tuberculosis caused by bacteria that are resistant to at least the two most powerful first-line anti-TB drugs: Isoniazid and Rifampicin.

b) Outline five important risk factors of MDR-TB (5mrks)

  1. Non-adherence to treatment: Patients stopping drugs or taking them irregularly.
  2. Inappropriate prescription: Health workers prescribing wrong drug combinations or dosages.
  3. Poor drug quality/supply: Stock-outs or expired medicines leading to interrupted treatment.
  4. History of previous TB treatment: Patients who have failed treatment or relapsed are at higher risk.
  5. Close contact with MDR-TB patient: Direct transmission of the resistant strain.

c) Describe the management of Mr. Kuzi (14mrks)

  1. Isolation: Admit Mr. Kuzi to an isolation ward (or advise strict home isolation) to prevent spread to community/family.
  2. Pharmacological Treatment (DOTS-Plus): Initiate second-line anti-TB regimen based on sensitivity patterns (e.g., Kanamycin/Amikacin injectable, Levofloxacin, Ethionamide, Cycloserine, Pyrazinamide) for a prolonged period (usually 18-24 months).
  3. Directly Observed Therapy (DOT): Ensure every dose is swallowed under the supervision of a health worker or trained supporter to ensure adherence.
  4. Monitoring: Conduct monthly sputum cultures to check for conversion (negative status) and monitor for drug side effects (e.g., hearing loss from injectables, liver function).
  5. Nutritional Support: Provide a high-protein, high-calorie diet to boost immunity and repair tissues.
  6. Health Education & Counseling: Educate on cough hygiene, importance of adherence, and side effects. Provide psychological support to handle the long treatment duration.
  7. Contact Tracing: Screen family members and close contacts for TB.

Part B: Implementation Strategies
