EXAMINATION FOR ADMISSION TO THE REGISTER OF BACHELOR OF SCIENCE IN NURSING (BScN)

Part I: Multiple Choice Questions

20 Marks
1.
The pelvic joints include:
a) One symphysis pubis, two sacroiliac, one sacrococcygeal
b) Two symphysis pubis, one sacroiliac, one sacrococcygeal
c) Two symphysis pubis, one sacroiliac, two sacrococcygeal
d) One symphysis pubis, two sacroiliac, two sacrococcygeal
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) One symphysis pubis, two sacroiliac, one sacrococcygeal

Explanation: The female pelvis consists of four joints:

  • Two Sacroiliac joints: Between the sacrum and the ilium on either side.
  • One Symphysis Pubis: Cartilaginous joint between the two pubic bones.
  • One Sacrococcygeal joint: Between the sacrum and the coccyx.

2.
The outcome of labour depends on:
a) Effectiveness of uterine contractions, descend of the fetal head
b) ‘Give’ of the pelvic joints, maternal effort
c) Moulding of the fetal head , ‘Give’ of the pelvic joints
d) Descend of the head ,maternal effort
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Effectiveness of uterine contractions, descend of the fetal head

Explanation: The outcome of labour depends on the Powers (uterine contractions and maternal effort), Passage (pelvis), and Passenger (fetus). Option A highlights the primary force (contractions) and the result (descent), which are critical indicators of progress.

3.
Possible signs of pregnancy include:
a) Human gonadotrophin hormones in urine, amenorrhea
b) Braxton hicks contractions, amenorrhea
c) Braxton hicks contractions, Ballottement of the fetus
d) Palpable fetal parts, Braxton hicks contractions
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Human gonadotrophin hormones in urine, amenorrhea

Explanation: Signs of pregnancy are classified as Presumptive, Probable, and Positive. Amenorrhea is a presumptive sign. HCG in urine is a probable sign. The question likely asks for signs that suggest pregnancy (Possible/Probable) rather than definitive positive signs (like FHR).

4.
Softening of the Isthmus during pregnancy is referred to as :
a) Jacquemies’s sign
b) Osiander’s signs
c) Positive sign
d) Hegars sign
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Hegars sign

Explanation: Hegar's sign is the softening of the lower uterine segment (isthmus) that occurs around 6-12 weeks of gestation. Osiander's sign is the pulsation of the vaginal fornices. Jacquemier's (Chadwick's) sign is the blueish discoloration of the vagina.

5.
The main function of the fetal sac is to:
a) Keep the fetus warm
b) Protect the fetus from ascending infection
c) Enclose the fetus
d) Enclose the placenta
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Protect the fetus from ascending infection

Explanation: While the amniotic sac (membranes) encloses the fetus and fluid (Option C), a critical physiological function of the intact membranes is to form a barrier against ascending infection from the vagina. Rupture of membranes significantly increases the risk of chorioamnionitis.

6.
The viscous fluid which keeps the sperm alive and motile is produced in the :
a) Prostate gland
b) Bulbourethral gland
c) Seminal vesicles
d) Spermatic cord
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Seminal vesicles

Explanation: The seminal vesicles produce about 60-70% of the seminal fluid volume. This fluid is viscous, alkaline, and rich in fructose, which provides energy for sperm motility and survival.

7.
In physiology of 1st stage of labour polarity is the term used to describe:
a) Uterine contractions that start at the fundus and spread across downwards
b) Neuro-muscular harmony that prevails between the two poles of segment of the uterus throughout labour
c) The shortening of the contraction retained by the muscle fibre which do not relax completely
d) The force of the fundal contractions transmitted to the upper pole of the fetus down the long axis
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Neuro-muscular harmony that prevails between the two poles of segment of the uterus throughout labour

Explanation: Polarity refers to the coordinated neuromuscular harmony where the upper uterine segment contracts and retracts (shortens) while the lower uterine segment and cervix relax and dilate.

8.
One definite indication of caesarean section is:
a) Severe pregnancy induced hypertension
b) Previous caesarean section scar
c) Antepartum hemorrhage
d) Cephalopelvic disproportion
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Cephalopelvic disproportion

Explanation: Cephalopelvic disproportion (CPD), where the fetal head is too large to pass through the maternal pelvis, is an absolute indication for Caesarean section. Other conditions like PIH or APH might be managed vaginally depending on severity and specific circumstances.

9.
The main cause of secondary postpartum hemorrhage is:
a) Retension of products of hemorrhage (conception)
b) Infection
c) Retained placenta
d) Fibroids
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Retension of products of hemorrhage (conception)

Explanation: Secondary PPH (occurring 24 hours to 6 weeks after delivery) is most commonly caused by retained products of conception (RPOC) and associated infection (endometritis).

10.
In management of primary postpartum haemorrhage the uterine contractions are maintained by giving:
a) 40 units of syntocinon in ½ litre intravenous fluid
b) 40 units of syntocinon in 1 litre intravenous fluid
c) 20 units of syntocinon in 1 litre intravenous fluid
d) 20 units of syntocinon start
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) 40 units of syntocinon in 1 litre intravenous fluid

Explanation: A common protocol for maintaining uterine contraction after initial control of PPH is an infusion of 40 units of oxytocin (Syntocinon) in 1 litre (or 500ml depending on fluid status) of crystalloid, infused at a rate to keep the uterus contracted (usually 125ml/hr or 40 drops/min).

11.
A woman visits the antenatal clinic for the first time at 36 weeks gestation.The most appropriate health message to be shared with her is:
a) Adequate nutrition and hydration
b) Avoidance of alcohol and tobacco
c) Danger signs in pregnancy
d) True signs of labour
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) True signs of labour

Explanation: At 36 weeks, the woman is near term. While all options are important, educating her on the true signs of labour and when to come to the hospital is the most immediate priority for birth preparedness at this late gestation.

12.
The investigations carried out only during the 1st visit to the antenatal clinic are:
a) Blood pressure, haemoglobin
b) Height, haemoglobin
c) Rhesus factor, HIV testing
d) Rhesus factor, urinalysis
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Height, haemoglobin

Note: Height is typically measured only at the first visit to assess pelvic adequacy potential. However, Rhesus factor is also done only once (if known). Haemoglobin is usually repeated (1st and 3rd trimester). Among the pairs, Height is the classic "one-time" measurement.

13.
Physiological jaundice:
a) Appears within the first 24 hours of age
b) Appears after 24 hours of age
c) Persists for 7-10 days in full term babies
d) Bilirubin level exceeds 200-215 mmol/litre
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Appears after 24 hours of age

Explanation: The defining characteristic distinguishing physiological from pathological jaundice is that physiological jaundice appears after the first 24 hours of life (usually day 2-3). Jaundice within the first 24 hours is always pathological.

14.
To prevent hypothermia babies should be nursed in rooms where temperature is maintained at:
a) 18-21 degrees celcious
b) 16-19 degrees celcious
c) 21-24 degrees celcious
d) 22-26 degrees celcious
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) 22-26 degrees celcious

Explanation: A warm delivery room and nursery environment (typically 25°C or 22-26°C) is recommended to prevent neonatal heat loss.

15.
Bishops score is
a) A method used to measure successfulness of induction
b) An objective method of assessing whether the cervix is favorable for induction
c) An objective method assessing whether the uterus is favorable for induction
d) Used to predict whether spontaneous vaginal delivery is possible
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) An objective method of assessing whether the cervix is favorable for induction

Explanation: The Bishop Score assesses cervical dilation, effacement, consistency, position, and station of the presenting part to predict the likelihood of successful induction of labour.

16.
Puerperal infection occurs 24 hours after delivery mostly due to:
a) Invasion of organisms due to poor personal hygiene
b) Invasion, incubation and multiplication of organisms
c) Endogenous organism present in the patients body
d) Localized infection in the patient
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Invasion of organisms due to poor personal hygiene

Explanation: While "invasion, incubation and multiplication" describes the process, the question asks for the cause ("due to"). Poor aseptic technique by attendants or poor personal hygiene allows the introduction of pathogens into the genital tract.

17.
Match the manouvre in A with the part used to deliver in B.
A: Mauriceau Smellie Veit
1. Extended head
2. Normal head in breech
3. Extended legs
4. Shoulder dystocia
Show Answer

Correct Match: 2. Normal head in breech (or After-coming head)

Explanation: The Mauriceau-Smellie-Veit maneuver is used to deliver the after-coming head in a breech presentation by maintaining flexion.

18.
Match the manouvre:
A: McRoberts manouevre
1. Extended head
2. Normal head in breech
3. Extended legs
4. Shoulder dystocia
Show Answer

Correct Match: 4. Shoulder dystocia

Explanation: McRoberts maneuver (hyperflexion of the mother's legs onto her abdomen) is the first-line intervention for shoulder dystocia.

19.
In response to stroking of the cheek on one side of the mouth, the baby will turn towards the source of the stimulus and open his mouth. This is called:
a. Moro reflex
b. Rooting reflex
c. Sucking and swallowing reflex
d. Gag reflex
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b. Rooting reflex

Explanation: The rooting reflex assists the baby in finding the breast/nipple for feeding.

20.
Conditions that predispose a mother to puerperal psychosis are:
a) Anaemia, severe puerperal sepsis
b) Anaemia, diabetes
c) Epilepsy, diabetes
d) Severe puerperal sepsis, polyhydramnios
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Anaemia, severe puerperal sepsis

Explanation: Severe infection (puerperal sepsis) can lead to toxic confusional states or precipitate psychosis. Physical exhaustion, anemia, and hormonal shifts are also contributory factors.

Part II: Short Answer Questions

Marks Varies
1.
Draw a well labeled diagram showing the anteroposterior diameters of the fetal skull and their measurements. (6 marks)
Show Answer

The diagram should illustrate:

  • Sub-occipitobregmatic: 9.5 cm (Fully flexed head).
  • Sub-occipitofrontal: 10.0 cm (Partially deflexed).
  • Occipitofrontal: 11.5 cm (Deflexed/OP position).
  • Mentovertical: 13.5 cm (Brow presentation - longest diameter).
  • Sub-mentobregmatic: 9.5 cm (Face presentation).
2.
Explain three anatomical variations of the:
a) Placenta (3 marks)
b) Umbilical cord (3 marks)
Show Answer

a) Placenta Variations:

  1. Succenturiate Lobe: A small accessory lobe separate from the main placenta, connected by vessels.
  2. Battledore Placenta: The cord is inserted at the margin/edge of the placenta.
  3. Velamentous Insertion: The cord inserts into the membranes, and vessels run through the membranes to the placenta (risk of Vasa Previa).
  4. Circumvallate Placenta: A raised ring of amnion/chorion on the fetal surface.

b) Umbilical Cord Variations:

  1. True Knots: Knots formed by fetal movement, can tighten and obstruct flow.
  2. Single Umbilical Artery: Presence of only one artery instead of two (associated with renal/cardiac anomalies).
  3. Long/Short Cord: Excessively long (>100cm, risk of prolapse/entanglement) or short (<30cm, risk of abruption).
3.
Outline four checks ensured before performing controlled cord traction to deliver the placenta. (4 marks)
Show Answer
  1. Uterus is contracted: The fundus must be hard/contracted to prevent inversion.
  2. Signs of Separation are present: (Lengthening of cord, gush of blood, fundus rises and becomes globular).
  3. Oxytocic drug has acted: Ensure the prophylactic oxytocin given at delivery of the shoulder has taken effect.
  4. Counter-traction is applied: One hand must be placed above the symphysis pubis to stabilize the uterus and prevent inversion while pulling.
4.
Explain five services offered during the 2nd visit in focused antenatal care. (5 marks)
Show Answer

The 2nd visit (usually at 24-28 weeks) includes:

  1. Review of Birth Plan: Confirming place of delivery, transport, and blood donor.
  2. Physical Examination: Monitoring BP, weight, and symphysial-fundal height (fetal growth).
  3. Anaemia Screening/Prevention: Checking Hb level and providing Iron/Folate supplementation.
  4. Tetanus Toxoid Immunization: Administering the second dose if due.
  5. Malaria Prophylaxis: Administering IPTp (SP) if in endemic area.
  6. Review of Lab Results: Discussing results from the 1st visit profile (HIV, Syphilis, Blood group).
5.
Outline four causes of obstructed labour. (4 marks)
Show Answer
  1. Cephalopelvic Disproportion (CPD): Mismatch between fetal head size and maternal pelvis.
  2. Malpresentation/Malposition: Brow, Face, Shoulder (Transverse lie), or persistent Occipito-Posterior positions.
  3. Fetal Abnormalities: Hydrocephalus, fetal ascites, or conjoined twins.
  4. Pelvic Tumors: Fibroids or ovarian cysts blocking the birth canal.
  5. Cervical Dystocia: Failure of the cervix to dilate due to scarring or rigidity.
