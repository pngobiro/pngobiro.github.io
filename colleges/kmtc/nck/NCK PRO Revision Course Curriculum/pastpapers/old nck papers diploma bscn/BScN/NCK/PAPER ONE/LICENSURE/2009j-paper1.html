Part I: Multiple Choice Questions

1.
The Aorta divides at the level of the 4th Lumbar vertebra into:
a) External and internal iliac arteries
b) Superior and inferior mesenteric arteries
c) Common iliac arteries
d) Lumbar arteries
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Common iliac arteries

Explanation: The abdominal aorta terminates by bifurcating into the left and right common iliac arteries at the level of the L4 vertebra. These further divide into the internal and external iliac arteries at the level of the sacroiliac joint.

2.
The organic causes of a child’s failure to thrive include:
a) Lack of active feeding, cleft palate, HIV infection
b) Chronic illness, hypothyroidism, congenital malformation
c) Emotional deprivation, diabetes, rickets
d) Metabolic disorders, poor nutrition, tuberculosis
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Chronic illness, hypothyroidism, congenital malformation

Explanation: Failure to thrive (FTT) causes are classified as organic (medical/physical) and non-organic (environmental/psychosocial).
- Organic: Chronic diseases (TB, HIV), endocrine disorders (hypothyroidism), congenital defects (heart defects, malformations).
- Non-organic: Emotional deprivation, poverty, poor feeding techniques.
Option (b) contains purely organic causes. Option (a) has "lack of active feeding" (non-organic). Option (c) has "emotional deprivation" (non-organic). Option (d) has "poor nutrition" (can be non-organic).

3.
Indirect bilirubin of 2.0 mg/dl (34 mol) indicates:
a) Multiple myeloma
b) Inflammatory condition
c) Iron deficiency anaemia
d) Haemolysis red blood cells
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Haemolysis red blood cells

Explanation: Indirect (unconjugated) bilirubin is produced from the breakdown of heme (from red blood cells). Elevated levels (>0.8 mg/dL) typically indicate excessive RBC destruction (hemolysis) or impaired conjugation (e.g., Gilbert's syndrome). It is not associated with iron deficiency or myeloma directly.

4.
The adults’ dose of Nitrofurantoin is:
a) 50 -100 mg four times a day
b) 25 -50 mg 6 hourly
c) 15-50 mg 8 hourly
d) 100-200 mg twice daily
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) 50 -100 mg four times a day

Explanation: The standard adult dosage for Nitrofurantoin (immediate release) in the treatment of uncomplicated UTIs is 50-100 mg four times daily (QID) for 7 days. For macrocrystals (slow release), it is often 100mg bid, but option (a) represents the classic dosing regimen.

5.
When inserting a naso-gastric tube, the nurse encourages patient to swallow as she/he pushes the tube down. This ensures that:
a) Patient evacuates secretion from oesophagus to facilitate passage of the tube to the stomach.
b) Minimal mucosal injury and irritation occurs as the tube passes through the oesophagus.
c) Epiglottis closes the trachea and directs tube to oesophagus.
d) The tube does not get entangled in the naso-pharynx.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Epiglottis closes the trachea and directs tube to oesophagus.

Explanation: Swallowing causes the larynx to rise and the epiglottis to cover the glottis (opening to the trachea). This natural mechanism helps direct the NG tube into the posterior esophagus rather than entering the airway (trachea).

6.
Appropriate nursing intervention for a patient with a nursing diagnosis of “ineffective airway clearance related to weak cough” is to:
a) Help the patient to sit up-right
b) Teach the patient how to cough.
c) Administer pain medication to promote comfortable coughing
d) Suction the patient’s airway.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Teach the patient how to cough.

Explanation: For a weak cough, teaching effective coughing techniques (like splinting, huff coughing, or staged coughing) is the primary intervention to improve clearance. While positioning (a) and hydration help, teaching the technique directly addresses the "weak cough" etiology. Suctioning (d) is a last resort if the patient cannot clear secretions independently.

7.
The features that suggest facial palsy include:
a) Loss of taste, insidious onset of paralysis on the affected side.
b) Sudden onset of paralysis on one side of the face, pain behind the ear of the affected side.
c) Papiloedema, difficulty with facial expression.
d) Headache, sounds that seem louder on the un-affected side.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Sudden onset of paralysis on one side of the face, pain behind the ear of the affected side.

Explanation: Bell's Palsy (facial nerve paralysis) is characterized by the sudden onset of unilateral facial weakness/paralysis. Patients often report pain behind the ear (mastoid) preceding the paralysis. Other symptoms include loss of taste on the anterior tongue and hyperacusis (sounds seem louder) on the affected side (not unaffected).

8.
Before any attempt to palpate the abdomen, the nurse should start by:
a) Gentle percussing the whole abdomen.
b) Checking for re-bound tenderness.
c) Asking the patient to point any area he/she feels pain.
d) Depressing the abdomen way using the palmer surface of the fingers.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Asking the patient to point any area he/she feels pain.

Explanation: The order of abdominal assessment is Inspection, Auscultation, Percussion, Palpation. However, before touching the abdomen (for percussion or palpation), the nurse must ask the patient to identify painful areas. This allows the nurse to examine those areas last to prevent muscle guarding and discomfort that would interfere with the rest of the exam.

9.
Surgical sutures that are digested and absorbed during the process of wound healing include:
a) catgut, vicryl, dexon
b) silk, catgut, linen
c) vicryl, polypropylene, silk
d) Polyamide, dexon, Terylene.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) catgut, vicryl, dexon

Explanation: Absorbable sutures are broken down by the body.
- Absorbable: Catgut (natural), Vicryl (polyglactin), Dexon (polyglycolic acid), PDS.
- Non-absorbable: Silk, Nylon (polyamide), Prolene (polypropylene), Linen, Terylene.

10.
In the management of a patient with breast cancer, systemic chemotherapy is indicated:
a) Only for post-menopausal women with nodal involvement.
b) In pre-menopausal women even when no nodal involvement is detected.
c) In all patients with evidence of nodal involvement
d) Only with estrogen receptor-negative tumors.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) In all patients with evidence of nodal involvement

Explanation: Systemic chemotherapy is generally indicated for most node-positive breast cancers (Option c) to reduce the risk of recurrence, regardless of menopausal status. While it is also used in high-risk node-negative cases (especially pre-menopausal), "all patients with nodal involvement" is the strongest standard indication listed here.

11.
The humoral immune response involves:
a) Production of antigen–specific immunoglobulin.
b) Release of cytokines responsible for destruction of antigens.
c) Direct attack of immunoglobulins.
d) Surveillance for malignant cell changes.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Production of antigen–specific immunoglobulin.

Explanation: Humoral immunity is mediated by B-lymphocytes (B-cells) which differentiate into plasma cells and produce specific antibodies (immunoglobulins) that circulate in the blood/humors to neutralize antigens.

12.
The health messages the nurse should share with a client suffering from urticaria include:
a) Use of over-the–counter antihistamines.
b) Application of topical benzene hexachloride weekly.
c) Avoidance of contact with the causative agent.
d) Exposing the affected area to sunshine.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Avoidance of contact with the causative agent.

Explanation: The primary management for urticaria (hives) is identifying and removing the trigger (allergen). Antihistamines (a) are used for treatment, but avoidance (c) is the key preventive health message. Benzene hexachloride is for scabies/lice.

13.
After subtotal thyroidectomy, the nurse should observe for manifestations of acute thyroid storm which include:
a) Hypothermia, arrhythmiasis, hypertension
b) Arrhythmias, laryngeal stridor, lethargy
c) Bradycardia, hoarse voice, bulging eyeballs.
d) Hyperthermia, tachycardia, hypotension.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Hyperthermia, tachycardia, hypotension.

Explanation: Thyroid storm (thyrotoxic crisis) is a life-threatening emergency characterized by exaggerated hyperthyroidism signs: high fever (Hyperthermia), extreme rapid heart rate (Tachycardia), and eventually cardiovascular collapse (Hypotension/Shock), agitation, and delirium.

14.
The intrinsic causes of airway obstruction which are indications for tracheostomy include:
a) Cancer of thyroid gland, Ludwig’s angina, barbiturate poisoning.
b) Multiple laryngeal papilloma, tracheal trauma, cancer of pharynx.
c) Severe head injury, poliomyelitis, fracture base of the skull.
d) Tetanus, coma, myasthenia gravis.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Multiple laryngeal papilloma, tracheal trauma, cancer of pharynx.

Explanation: Intrinsic causes are those arising from within the airway itself. Laryngeal papillomas, trauma to the trachea, and pharyngeal cancer physically block the airway lumen.
- Option (a): Thyroid cancer and Ludwig's angina cause extrinsic compression.
- Options (c) & (d): Head injury, coma, tetanus cause functional obstruction (inability to protect airway) or respiratory failure, but not necessarily a physical intrinsic blockage.

15.
An infant aged 2 months who has been categorized as having very severe pneumonia is likely to manifest with:
a) Difficulty in breathing, inability to breast feed.
b) Nasal flaring, cough.
c) Fast breathing of 50 breaths/ minute, cough.
d) Nasal discharge, mouth breathing.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Difficulty in breathing, inability to breast feed.

Explanation: According to IMCI guidelines, signs of Very Severe Disease/Pneumonia in an infant (2 months-5 years) include: central cyanosis, severe respiratory distress, or inability to drink/breastfeed (danger sign). Fast breathing alone (c) classifies as Pneumonia (non-severe). Chest indrawing classifies as Severe Pneumonia. Inability to feed elevates it to Very Severe.

16.
Intravenous Ringers solution prescribed to a one (1) year old infant with severe dehydration will be administered at:
a) 50 mls/ kg body weight in the 1st hour, then 50 mls /kg body weight in 5 hours.
b) 20mls/kg body weight bolus, then 80 mls/kg body weight in 6 hours.
c) 30 mls/kg body weight bolus then 70 mls /kg body weight in 6 hours
d) 30 mls /kg body weight in the 1st hour, then 70 mls /kg body weight in 5 hours.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) 30 mls /kg body weight in the 1st hour, then 70 mls /kg body weight in 5 hours.

Explanation: IMCI Plan C for Severe Dehydration specifies IV fluid (Ringer's Lactate) administration as follows:
- Infants (< 12 months): 30ml/kg in 1 hour + 70ml/kg in 5 hours (Total 6 hours).
- Children (12 months - 5 years): 30ml/kg in 30 mins + 70ml/kg in 2.5 hours (Total 3 hours).
The question specifies "one (1) year old infant". If treated as an infant (often done in exams to test the 1hr/5hr rule), Option D is the correct infant protocol.

17.
Complications of posterior dislocation of the hip include:
a) Shearing of the sciatic nerve, shortening of the limb.
b) Post–traumatic ossification, osteoarthritis.
c) Narrowing of the acetabulum, osteomyelitis.
d) Permanent semiflexin of the limb, atrophy of iliofemoral ligament
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Shearing of the sciatic nerve, shortening of the limb.

Explanation: In posterior hip dislocation (most common type), the femoral head is pushed backward. This can directly compress or shear the sciatic nerve which runs posterior to the hip joint (causing foot drop). The limb characteristically appears shortened, adducted, and internally rotated.

18.
Laboratory results of a patient suffering from massive thermal burns will reveal:
a) Hypocalcaemia
b) Increased liver enzymes
c) Decreased myoglobins in urine
d) Lymphocytosis.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Hypocalcaemia

Explanation: In the acute phase of massive burns:
- Hyperkalemia occurs due to cell lysis.
- Hyponatremia occurs due to fluid shifts.
- Hypocalcemia is common due to hypoalbuminemia (calcium bound to albumin is lost) and saponification in necrotic tissue.
- Myoglobins in urine would be increased (myoglobinuria) due to muscle damage.
- White blood cell count usually shows neutrophilia, not lymphocytosis.

19.
Indicate whether the following statements are TRUE or FALSE:
Show Answer

a) Bacterial acid in dental plaque facilitates deposition of minerals on the tooth thereby causing dental caries.

Answer: FALSE

Explanation: Bacterial acid causes demineralization (loss of minerals) of the tooth enamel, not deposition. This demineralization leads to cavities (caries).

b) One of the causative factors for dental caries is highly refined carbohydrates.

Answer: TRUE

Explanation: Refined carbohydrates (sugars) provide substrate for cariogenic bacteria to produce acid, directly contributing to tooth decay.

20.
Match the symptoms in column A with eye conditions in column B:
Show Answer

a) Occasional headaches, frequent change of glasses

Match: Refractive Errors (e.g., Presbyopia or Astigmatism)

Explanation: Frequent changes in prescription and headaches from eye strain are typical of uncorrected refractive errors.

b) Severe unilateral headaches, diminution of vision

Match: Acute Angle-Closure Glaucoma

Explanation: Sudden, severe eye pain/headache (often unilateral) accompanied by blurred vision and halos is the classic presentation of acute glaucoma, a medical emergency.
