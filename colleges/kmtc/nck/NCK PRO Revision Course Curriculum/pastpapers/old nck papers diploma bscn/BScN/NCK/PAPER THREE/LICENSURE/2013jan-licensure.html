Part I: Multiple Choice Questions

20 Marks
1.
The term that best describes a group to invasion and spread of an infectious agent is:-
a. Individual immunity
b. Natural active immunity
c. Herd immunity
d. Artificial active immunity
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c. Herd immunity

Explanation: Herd immunity (or community immunity) describes the resistance of a group or community to the invasion and spread of an infectious agent. This occurs when a significant portion of the population is immune (through vaccination or prior illness), thereby providing protection to unimmunized individuals by reducing the likelihood of transmission.

2.
The frequency of a phenomenon during a given time and which deals with both new and old cases is:-
a. Cause specific rate
b. Prevalence rate
c. Crude case rate
d. Incidence rate
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b. Prevalence rate

Explanation: Prevalence rate measures the total number of existing cases (both new and old) of a disease in a population at a specific point in time or over a specified period. Incidence rate (d) refers only to new cases.

3.
Demographic data describes:-
a) Characteristics and distribution of people in a given area and the changes in the population over time.
b) Any data that delineate health or health related events.
c) Adoption, annulment patterns of the population.
d) The extent and distribution of illness in a community.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Characteristics and distribution of people in a given area and the changes in the population over time.

Explanation: Demography is the statistical study of populations, including their size, structure (characteristics like age, sex, ethnicity), and distribution, as well as changes in these parameters over time due to birth, migration, and aging.

4.
Emergency contraceptives are effective if taken within:-
a. 48 hours
b. 72 hours
c. 5 days
d. 7 days
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b. 72 hours

Explanation: Levonorgestrel-based emergency contraceptive pills (common ECPs) are most effective when taken as soon as possible after unprotected intercourse, but generally licensed for use within 72 hours (3 days). Some newer types (like Ulipristal acetate) or copper IUDs can be effective up to 5 days, but in the context of standard exam questions regarding ECP pills, 72 hours is the standard benchmark.

5.
According to Erikson’s psychosocial development tasks, industry v/s inferiority occurs at:-
a. 3-6 years
b. 19-25 years
c. 5-12 years
d. 13-19 years
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c. 5-12 years

Explanation: The stage of Industry vs. Inferiority corresponds to the school-age child, typically ages 5/6 to 12 years. During this stage, children learn to be competent and productive or feel inferior and unable to do things well.

  • 3-6 years: Initiative vs. Guilt (Preschool)
  • 13-19 years: Identity vs. Role Confusion (Adolescence)
  • 19-25 years: Intimacy vs. Isolation (Young Adulthood)
6.
Cross sectional surveys give:-
a. Prevalence information
b. Incidence information
c. Longitudinal data.
d. Case study.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a. Prevalence information

Explanation: Cross-sectional surveys collect data at a single point in time, providing a "snapshot" of a population. This design is ideal for measuring prevalence (the proportion of a population with a specific characteristic/disease at that time). They cannot determine incidence (new cases over time) because they do not follow individuals over a period.

7.
Principles of primary health care include:-
a. Intersectorial action, decentralization, community participation.
b. Intersectorial action, integration of health programmes, education concerning prevailing health problems.
c. Maternal child health care and family planning, immunization, proper nutrition.
d. Community participation, safe water supply.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a. Intersectorial action, decentralization, community participation.

Explanation: The fundamental principles of Primary Health Care (PHC) derived from the Alma-Ata Declaration are Equity, Community Participation, Intersectoral Coordination, and Appropriate Technology. Option 'a' best captures these structural principles (Intersectorial action, participation, and decentralization which facilitates participation). Option 'c' lists *elements* or components of PHC, not the guiding principles.

8.
The correct sequence of community diagnosis process is:-
a. Planning the survey, community inventory, developing and pre testing survey tools, execution of the survey, data analysis, report writing, dissemination and community action.
b. Community inventory, planning the survey, developing and pre testing survey tools, execution of the survey, data analysis, report writing, dissemination and community action.
c. Developing and pre testing survey tools, planning the survey, execution of the survey, data analysis, report writing, dissemination and community action.
d. Community inventory, developing and pre testing survey tools, planning the survey, execution of the survey, data analysis, report writing, dissemination and community action.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a. Planning the survey, community inventory, developing and pre testing survey tools, execution of the survey, data analysis, report writing, dissemination and community action.

Explanation: The process typically begins with planning/initiation (defining objectives, team). Community inventory (mapping, resources) is part of the early data collection/reconnaissance. Then tools are developed and tested, followed by the full survey execution, analysis, and finally dissemination for action.

9.
The non-probability sampling methods include:-
a. Cluster sampling, quota sampling and systematic sampling.
b. Convenience sampling technique, cluster sampling and quota sampling.
c. Simple random sampling, systematic sampling and cluster sampling.
d. Convenience sampling, quota sampling, purposive sampling.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d. Convenience sampling, quota sampling, purposive sampling.

Explanation:

  • Non-probability sampling: Convenience, Quota, Purposive (Judgmental), Snowball. (Selection is not random).
  • Probability sampling: Simple random, Systematic, Stratified, Cluster. (Every member has a known chance of selection).
Option 'd' lists only non-probability methods. Options a, b, and c include probability methods (Cluster, Systematic, Simple random).

10.
The child understands a few words and tries to use them at:-
a. 9-12 months
b. 6-9 months
c. 12-18 months
d. 3-6 months
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a. 9-12 months

Explanation: By 9-12 months, an infant typically understands simple instructions (like "no" or "wave bye-bye") and begins to say their first single words (like "mama", "dada"). By 12-18 months (Option c), they usually have a growing vocabulary and are clearly using words, not just "trying".

11.
Tooth eruption in children is likely to occur at:-
a. 6 weeks of age
b. 24 weeks of age
c. 48 weeks of age
d. 36 weeks of age
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b. 24 weeks of age

Explanation: The first primary teeth (usually the lower central incisors) typically erupt around 6 months of age. 24 weeks is approximately 5.5 to 6 months, which is the standard expected time.

12.
Specific pharmacological management of ophthalmia neonatorum according to NASCOP flow chart of ophthalmia neonatorum includes:-
a. Procaine penicillin 300,000 IU IM and 1% tetracycline eye ointment TDS X 10 days.
b. Streptomycin 1% eye ointment, procaine penicillin 300,000 IU IM
c. Septrin 5 mg stat, 1% eye ointment tetracycline.
d. Procaine penicillin 300,000 IU IM and streptomycin 1% eye ointment.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a. Procaine penicillin 300,000 IU IM and 1% tetracycline eye ointment TDS X 10 days.

Explanation: Based on older NASCOP guidelines referenced in this 2013 paper, the management for ophthalmia neonatorum (presumed gonococcal) included systemic penicillin (Procaine Penicillin IM) plus topical prophylaxis/treatment with Tetracycline eye ointment. Note: Modern guidelines typically recommend Ceftriaxone or Kanamycin due to penicillin resistance, but in the context of this specific exam paper, Option A reflects the expected answer.

13.
‘Pulse’ saturation vaccination implies:-
a. A method used when static MCH services are too far away for mothers to bring their infants for immunization.
b. A routine immunization schedule.
c. Coverage of susceptible population with immunization.
d. Few susceptible due to increased immunization.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c. Coverage of susceptible population with immunization.

Explanation: "Pulse" immunization (like Pulse Polio) refers to the simultaneous mass administration of vaccines to a target population (often children 0-5 years) on a single day or days, regardless of previous immunization status. The goal is to "saturate" the population and break the chain of transmission by covering all susceptible individuals at once.

14.
Gross reproductive rate is:-
a) Number of live births per 1000 workers in specific age group.
b) Total number of children born to a woman.
c) Average number of girls born to a woman with current fertility pattern.
d) Number of daughters a new born girl will bear during her life time.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Average number of girls born to a woman with current fertility pattern.

Explanation: The Gross Reproduction Rate (GRR) is the average number of daughters a woman would have if she survived through the end of her childbearing years and experienced the current age-specific fertility rates. It focuses specifically on female births (daughters) and does not account for mortality (unlike the Net Reproduction Rate). Option 'c' best fits this definition by specifying "girls born" and "current fertility pattern".

15.
Stable litter contains mainly:-
a. Animal droppings and left over animal feeds.
b. Large proportions of purtriscible vegetables and broken glasses.
c. Rubbish and garbage from households.
d. Iron pieces, dead animals, animal droppings.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a. Animal droppings and left over animal feeds.

Explanation: "Stable litter" refers to the refuse generated in animal stables, which primarily consists of bedding material (straw), animal droppings (manure), and leftover animal feed.

16.
The permanent hardness of water can be removed by:-
a. Boiling
b. Addition of lime
c. Addition of sodium bicarbonate
d. Chlorination
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c. Addition of sodium bicarbonate (likely typo for Sodium Carbonate)

Explanation: Permanent hardness (caused by sulphates and chlorides of calcium and magnesium) cannot be removed by boiling (Option A) or simple addition of lime (Option B, which treats temporary hardness). It is removed by adding Sodium Carbonate (Washing Soda) or by ion exchange (Permutit process). Option C ("sodium bicarbonate") is likely a typo for "sodium carbonate" in the exam paper, as it is the closest chemical agent listed for this purpose.

17.
In epidemiology chi-square (x²) is :-
a. An attempt to establish relationship between two categorical variables.
b. A type of analysis to find out whether an independent variable predicts a given dependent variables.
c. A technique that deals with one independent and one dependent variable.
d. An attempt to determine whether a group of variables together predicts a given dependent variable.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a. An attempt to establish relationship between two categorical variables.

Explanation: The Chi-square test is a statistical test used to determine if there is a significant association (relationship) between two categorical variables (e.g., gender and smoking status).

18.
The situation where subjects’ awareness of being in an experimental group motivates them to perform better is known as:-
a. The John Henry effect.
b. Placebo effect.
c. The Hawthorne effect.
d. The halo effect.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c. The Hawthorne effect.

Explanation: The Hawthorne effect refers to the alteration of behavior by the subjects of a study due to their awareness of being observed.

19.
Match the diseases in column A with causative organism (or vector) in column B.
COLUMN A (Disease) COLUMN B (Vector/Agent)
a) Onchocerciasis 1. Simulium damnosum
b) Louse-borne relapsing fever 2. Wuchereria bancrofti
3. Pediculus corporis
4. Orinthodorus moubata
5. Borrelia duttoni
Show Answer

Correct Match:

  • a) Onchocerciasis matches with 1. Simulium damnosum: Simulium damnosum (the black fly) is the vector that transmits the parasite Onchocerca volvulus which causes Onchocerciasis (River Blindness).
  • b) Louse-borne relapsing fever matches with 3. Pediculus corporis: Pediculus humanus corporis (the body louse) is the vector for Borrelia recurrentis, the agent of Louse-borne relapsing fever.
20.
Indicate whether the statement is true or false: "Refrigeration preserves food by killing all bacteria."
True
False
Show Answer

Correct Answer: False

Explanation: Refrigeration is bacteriostatic, not bactericidal. It slows down the growth and reproduction of bacteria but does not kill them. Once the food is returned to room temperature, the bacteria can resume growth.
