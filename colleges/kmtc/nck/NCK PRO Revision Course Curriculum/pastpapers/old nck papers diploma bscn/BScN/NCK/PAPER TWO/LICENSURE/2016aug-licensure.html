Part I: Multiple Choice Questions

20 Marks
1.
The anterior diameter of the pelvic outlet in a gynaecoid pelvis measures:-
a) 13.5cm.
b) 13 cm.
c) 12 cm.
d) 11.5 cm.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) 13.5cm.

Explanation: The Antero-Posterior (AP) diameter of the outlet is measured from the lower border of the symphysis pubis to the tip of the coccyx. Anatomically it is ~12.5 cm, but during labour, the coccyx moves backward, increasing the diameter to approximately 13.0 - 13.5 cm, making it the largest diameter of the outlet.

2.
The causes of face presentation include:-
a) Hydrocephalus, uterine abnormality.
b) Anterior obliquity of the uterus, contracted pelvis, anencephaly.
c) Contracted pelvis, placenta praevia, anencephaly.
d) Polyhydramnios, uterine abnormalities, multiple pregnancies.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Anterior obliquity of the uterus, contracted pelvis, anencephaly.

Explanation: Face presentation is associated with full extension of the head. Causes include fetal anomalies like anencephaly (absence of cranial vault) and neck tumors, maternal factors like contracted pelvis (preventing flexion/engagement), and uterine obliquity (pendulous abdomen) in multiparous women.

3.
Immediately after delivering the baby, the midwife conducting the delivery should:-
a) Dry the baby.
b) Take the baby weight.
c) Apply identification band.
d) Check the umbilical cord for number of vessels.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Dry the baby.

Explanation: The immediate priority for newborn care is thermoregulation. Drying the baby prevents heat loss through evaporation and also provides tactile stimulation to initiate breathing. Weighing and identification are done subsequently.

4.
A woman with preterm labour is given magnesium sulphate for the following reasons:-
a) To enhance fetal lung maturity.
b) To prevent seizures.
c) To improve urine output.
d) To inhibit uterine contractions.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) To inhibit uterine contractions.

Explanation: Magnesium sulphate acts as a tocolytic agent (relaxes uterine smooth muscle) to delay delivery, allowing time for corticosteroid administration. It is also used for fetal neuroprotection.

5.
The reason for administering vitamin K is to:-
a) Boost the immune system.
b) Stimulate growth of gastro intestinal flora.
c) Protect against infection.
d) Prevent bleeding problems.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Prevent bleeding problems.

Explanation: Newborns have low levels of Vitamin K, putting them at risk for Hemorrhagic Disease of the Newborn. Vitamin K prophylaxis promotes the synthesis of clotting factors in the liver.

6.
A client makes a routine visit to the prenatal clinic. Although she is 14 weeks pregnant, the size of her uterus approximates that in an 18 â€“ 20 weeks pregnancy. A diagnosis of gestational trophoblastic disease is made. The nurse should prepare for:-
a) Evacuation, control of bleeding, administration of methotraxate.
b) Family planning health messages, evacuation, administration of methotraxate.
c) Control of bleeding, family planning health messages, evacuation.
d) Evacuation, control of bleeding, Anti D Immune globulin administration.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Evacuation, control of bleeding, Anti D Immune globulin administration.

Explanation: The priority management for a molar pregnancy is uterine evacuation (suction curettage) and managing potential hemorrhage. Anti-D immunoglobulin is indicated if the client is Rh-negative to prevent isoimmunization. Methotrexate is reserved for persistent or malignant disease (GTN) post-evacuation.

7.
A client with type I diabetes mellitus who is multigravida visits the clinic at 27 weeks gestation. The nurse should instruct the client that she:-
a) Do weekly fetal movements counts by the mother.
b) Contraction stress testing is performed weekly.
c) Induction of labor is begun at 34 weeks gestation.
d) Non stress testing is performed weekly until 32 weeks gestation.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Do weekly fetal movements counts by the mother.

Note: Standard care for high-risk pregnancies (like Type 1 DM) involves daily fetal movement counting (kick charts) starting around 28 weeks. Option A is the closest viable option regarding fetal monitoring instruction, despite the wording "weekly" which may be an error for "daily". Weekly NST (d) usually *starts* at 32 weeks, not ends.

8.
The following assessment finding on a patient would warrant the nurse to suspect DVT:-
a) Mid-calf pain, tendencies and redness along the vein.
b) Chills, fever, malaise, occurring 2 weeks after delivery.
c) Muscle pain the presence of Homan's sign and swelling in the affected limb.
d) Chills, fever, stiffness and pain occurring 10 to 14 days after delivery.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Muscle pain the presence of Homan's sign and swelling in the affected limb.

Explanation: Classic signs of Deep Vein Thrombosis (DVT) include unilateral swelling, calf pain/tenderness, and a positive Homan's sign (pain in the calf on dorsiflexion of the foot), although Homan's sign is not specific.

9.
The urinary symptoms that a pregnant mother will frequently experience during the first trimester would be:-
a) Dysuria.
b) Frequency.
c) Incontinence.
d) Burning.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Frequency.

Explanation: Urinary frequency is common in the first trimester due to the growing uterus within the pelvis compressing the bladder. It resolves in the second trimester as the uterus rises into the abdomen and returns in the third trimester.

10.
The following is a priority nursing diagnosis in a client with class 3 cardiac disease:-
a) Knowledge deficit related to self care.
b) Fear related to pregnancy outcome.
c) Alteration in nutrition related to sodium restricted diet.
d) Activity intolerance related to compromised cardiac status.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Activity intolerance related to compromised cardiac status.

Explanation: NYHA Class III cardiac disease involves marked limitation of physical activity. The priority is managing the physiological compromise (Activity Intolerance) to prevent cardiac decompensation.

11.
The following statement is correct about endometriosis:-
a) It often progresses to endometrial causes cancer.
b) Implants predose hormones that mimic the ovaries.
c) Trapped hematomas cause scarring and adhesions.
d) There is no known familiar tendency of occurrence.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Trapped hematomas cause scarring and adhesions.

Explanation: Endometriosis involves ectopic endometrial tissue that bleeds cyclically. Since the blood cannot exit, it forms trapped hematomas (chocolate cysts), leading to chronic inflammation, extensive scarring, and adhesions.

12.
If a papanicolaou smear report is abnormal, the next step is evaluating the client for cervical cancer is:-
a) A repeat pap smear.
b) An endocervical curettage.
c) A colposcopy.
d) A conization.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) A colposcopy.

Explanation: A colposcopy is the standard diagnostic follow-up for a significant abnormal Pap smear to visualize the cervix under magnification and identify areas for biopsy.

13.
Management of batholins abscess includes:-
a) Encourage moderate upright activity for drainage.
b) Apply cold packs to the vulva to reduce swelling.
c) Obtain a culture of any drainage for analysis.
d) Having the client wear support briefs to reduce skin irritation.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Obtain a culture of any drainage for analysis.

Explanation: Management involves incision and drainage (I&D) and antibiotics. Obtaining a culture of the pus/drainage is essential to identify the causative organism (often gonococcal or chlamydial) and target therapy.

14.
Manifestation of hypothyroidism in reproductive system include:-
a) Increased libido, oligospermia.
b) Impotence, metrorrhagia.
c) Anovulation, high incidence of spontaneous abortion.
d) Irregular menstrual cycles, increased libido.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Anovulation, high incidence of spontaneous abortion.

Explanation: Hypothyroidism disrupts the HPO axis, leading to anovulation (infertility) and menstrual irregularities (often heavy bleeding). If pregnancy occurs, there is a significantly increased risk of spontaneous abortion.

15.
The following are signs and symptoms of impending eclampsia:-
a) Sharp rise in blood pressure and epigastric pain.
b) Increase in proteinuria and vomiting.
c) Weight gain and epigastric pain.
d) Sharp rise in blood pressure and increase in proteinuria.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Sharp rise in blood pressure and epigastric pain.

Explanation: Warning signs that severe pre-eclampsia is progressing to eclampsia (seizures) include severe headache, visual disturbances, hyperreflexia, and epigastric pain (due to liver stretching/ischemia).

16.
The trophoblastic cells of the developing zygote form the:-
a) Fetus and amnion.
b) Placenta and fetus.
c) Chorion and amnion.
d) Placenta and chorion.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Placenta and chorion.

Explanation: The blastocyst differentiates into the inner cell mass (embryoblast), which forms the fetus and amnion, and the outer layer (trophoblast), which forms the placenta and chorion.

17.
Spermatogenesis takes place in the:-
a) Prostate glands.
b) Seminal vesicles.
c) Epididymis.
d) Seminiferous duct.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Seminiferous duct.

Explanation: Sperm production (spermatogenesis) occurs within the seminiferous tubules (ducts) of the testes. The epididymis is for storage and maturation.

18.
The most common cause of maternal death in Kenya today is:-
a) Haemorrhage.
b) Ruptured uterus.
c) Eclampsia.
d) Abortion.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Haemorrhage.

Explanation: Postpartum Haemorrhage (PPH) remains the leading cause of maternal mortality in Kenya and globally.

19.
When planning care of a 15 year old female who is pregnant, a midwife should recognize that the adolescent is at risk for:-
a) Glucose intolerance.
b) Foetal chromosomal abnormality.
c) Incompetent cervix.
d) Iron deficiency anemia.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Iron deficiency anemia.

Explanation: Pregnant adolescents are at high risk for nutritional deficiencies, particularly Iron Deficiency Anemia, due to the competing growth demands of the mother and the fetus, as well as often poor dietary habits.

Part II: Short Answer Questions

40 Marks
1.
Draw a well labeled diagram of a fetal circulation. (6 marks)
Show Answer

Key structures to label:

  • Placenta: Organ of gas exchange.
  • Umbilical Vein: Carries oxygenated blood to fetus.
  • Ductus Venosus: Shunts blood from umbilical vein to IVC, bypassing liver.
  • Inferior Vena Cava (IVC): Mixes oxygenated and deoxygenated blood.
  • Foramen Ovale: Shunts blood from Right Atrium to Left Atrium.
  • Ductus Arteriosus: Shunts blood from Pulmonary Artery to Aorta, bypassing lungs.
  • Umbilical Arteries (2): Return deoxygenated blood to placenta.
2.
State three (3) ways of expecting an occipito-posterior position in pregnancy without the use of an abdominal ultrasound scan. (3 marks)
Show Answer
  1. Abdominal Inspection: The lower abdomen appears flattened (saucer-shaped) rather than convex.
  2. Palpation: Limbs are felt on both sides of the midline; the back is difficult to palpate (felt well out in the flank).
  3. Auscultation: The fetal heart sound is heard in the flank or may be difficult to locate/heard over a wide area.
  4. Non-engagement: The head is often high/not engaged at term.
3.
State seven (7) components of safe motherhood. (7 marks)
Show Answer
  1. Family Planning / Pre-conception care.
  2. Focused Antenatal Care (FANC).
  3. Clean and Safe Delivery (Skilled Birth Attendance).
  4. Essential Obstetric Care (Basic and Comprehensive).
  5. Postnatal Care.
  6. Management of Complications (e.g., Abortion care).
  7. Newborn Care.
4.
Explain four (4) contraindications of breastfeeding. (8 marks)
Show Answer
  1. Maternal HIV Infection: (Note: This depends on national guidelines; often contraindicated if AFASS criteria for replacement feeding are met, otherwise exclusive BF with ARVs is recommended. However, medically it is a transmission route).
  2. Active Untreated Tuberculosis: Mother should not be in direct contact until treated (expressed milk can be given).
  3. Galactosemia in Infant: Infant lacks enzyme to metabolize galactose; absolute contraindication.
  4. Maternal Chemotherapy/Radioactive Isotopes: Drugs can pass through milk and harm the infant.
  5. Active Herpes Simplex Lesions on Breast: Direct contact risk.
  6. Severe Maternal Mental Illness (Psychosis): Risk of harm to infant during feeding.
5.
State three (3) early signs of obstructed labour. (3 marks)
Show Answer
  1. Slow cervical dilation: Crossing the Alert line on the partograph.
  2. Caput Succedaneum: Formation of a large caput (swelling) on the fetal head.
  3. Severe Moulding: Overlapping of skull bones (++ or +++).
  4. Edematous Cervix: Cervix feels thick and swollen.
6.
State five (5) nursing interventions to facilitate thermoregulation for a premature infant. (5 marks)
Show Answer
  1. Dry Immediately: Dry thoroughly after birth to prevent evaporative heat loss.
  2. Kangaroo Mother Care (KMC): Skin-to-skin contact with the mother.
  3. Use of Incubator/Radiant Warmer: Maintain neutral thermal environment.
  4. Cover the Head: Use a warm hat (large surface area for heat loss).
  5. Delay Bathing: Postpone first bath until baby is thermally stable.
  6. Warm Chain: Warm resuscitation surface, warm towels, warm transportation.
7.
State four (4) causes of primary post partum haemorrhage. (4 marks)
Show Answer

The 4 T's:

  1. Tone: Uterine Atony (failure of uterus to contract).
  2. Trauma: Lacerations of cervix, vagina, perineum; Uterine Rupture.
  3. Tissue: Retained placenta or membranes.
  4. Thrombin: Coagulation disorders (DIC).
8.
State four (4) components of a partograph. (4 marks)
Show Answer
  1. Fetal Condition: Fetal Heart Rate, Liquor (membranes/colour), Moulding.
  2. Progress of Labour: Cervical Dilation, Descent of Head, Uterine Contractions.
  3. Maternal Condition: Pulse, Blood Pressure, Temperature, Urine (protein/acetone/volume).
  4. Medications/Fluids: IV fluids, Oxytocin, Drugs given.

Part III: Essay/Long Answer Questions

40 Marks
1.

Miss M, is a primigravida who is admitted to labour ward at 40 weeks gestation for induction of labour by use of prostanglandins. She is rhesus B negative, PMTCT non reactive VDRL =non reaction, HB level = 10gdl.

a) Define induction of labour. (1 mark)

b) State five (5) contra-indications to induction of labour. (5 marks)

c) State four (4) factors to consider before commencing an induction process on a patient. (4 marks)

d) Using the nursing process, describe the management of Miss M. (10 marks)

Show Answer

a) Definition:

Induction of labour is the artificial initiation of uterine contractions after the period of viability, with the aim of achieving vaginal delivery.

b) Contraindications:

  1. Cephalopelvic Disproportion (CPD).
  2. Placenta Praevia (major degrees).
  3. Transverse Lie / Malpresentation.
  4. Active Genital Herpes.
  5. Previous Classical Caesarean Section (high risk of rupture).
  6. Vasa Praevia.

c) Factors to Consider:

  1. Gestational Age: Confirmation of term/maturity.
  2. Cervical Status (Bishop's Score): Is the cervix favorable?
  3. Fetal Wellbeing: CTG/NST to rule out distress before starting.
  4. Maternal Condition/Indication: Is the reason for induction valid and is she stable?
  5. Pelvic Adequacy: Clinical pelvimetry to rule out CPD.

d) Management (Nursing Process):

  • Assessment: Check vital signs, FHR, Bishop's score. Verify Rh Negative status (prepare Anti-D).
  • Diagnosis: Knowledge deficit regarding procedure; Anxiety related to process; Risk for altered fetal tissue perfusion.
  • Planning: Achieve safe vaginal delivery; Pain management; prevent complications.
  • Intervention:
    • Preparation: Explain procedure, obtain consent. IV access.
    • Administration: Insert Prostaglandin (PGE2) vaginally as ordered. Instruct to remain recumbent for 30-60 mins.
    • Monitoring: Monitor FHR and contractions continuously or every 30 mins. Observe for hyperstimulation.
    • Progress: Assess cervical changes. If labour establishes, manage as normal labour.
    • Rh Prophylaxis: Administer Anti-D Immunoglobulin within 72 hours of delivery since she is Rh Negative.
  • Evaluation: Successful onset of labour, healthy mother and baby.
2.

a) Fetal monitoring is important. Describe five fetal assessments/investigations that can be utilized to monitor the fetus in the uterus. (10 marks)

b) Pre-conception care should be initiated early for all women and men of reproductive age. Describe five (5) interventions you would advocate for, to facilitate it. (10 marks)

Show Answer

a) Fetal Assessments:

  1. Fetal Movement Counting (Kick Charts): Mother counts movements daily (e.g., Cardiff count-to-ten). Decrease indicates distress.
  2. Non-Stress Test (NST): Monitoring FHR accelerations with movements using CTG. Reactive = healthy.
  3. Ultrasound Scan: Assesses growth (biometry), liquor volume (AFI), fetal anatomy, and presentation.
  4. Biophysical Profile (BPP): Combines NST with Ultrasound (Breathing, Movements, Tone, Liquor volume). Score /10.
  5. Fetal Heart Rate Auscultation (Pinard/Doppler): Intermittent checks to ensure rate is 110-160 bpm and regular.

b) Pre-conception Care Interventions:

  1. Folic Acid Supplementation: Advocate for 400mcg daily intake before conception to prevent Neural Tube Defects.
  2. Screening and Management of Chronic Conditions: Stabilize Diabetes, Hypertension, Epilepsy, or Thyroid disease before pregnancy.
  3. Infection Screening and Treatment: Screen for HIV, Syphilis, Rubella (vaccinate if non-immune), and STIs.
  4. Nutritional Counseling: Address anemia, obesity (BMI management), and balanced diet.
  5. Lifestyle Modification: Cessation of smoking, alcohol, and drug use. Avoidance of teratogens.
