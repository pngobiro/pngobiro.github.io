PART I: MULTIPLE CHOICE QUESTIONS

20 Marks
1.
Role play is an effective means of developing:-
a) Acting skills which can be used after college.
b) Decision-making and problem-solving skills.
c) Interpersonal skills.
d) Communication skills.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Communication skills. (Interpersonal skills is also correct, but communication is often the primary objective).

Explanation: Role play allows learners to practice communication and interpersonal interactions in a safe, simulated environment, making it ideal for developing these soft skills and empathy (affective domain).

2.
A teaching strategy that is best used for linking of theory to practice is:-
a) Clinical concept mapping.
b) Clinical journaling.
c) Clinical instruction.
d) Clinical assessments.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Clinical concept mapping.

Explanation: Concept mapping is a tool specifically designed to help students organize and represent knowledge, visually showing relationships between theoretical concepts (pathophysiology, pharmacology) and clinical practice (patient presentation, interventions).

3.
The upper most level in cognitive domain is:-
a) Analysis.
b) Synthesis.
c) Evaluation.
d) Application.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Evaluation.

Explanation: In the original Bloom's Taxonomy (which is often referenced in these exams), Evaluation is the highest level. Note: In the revised Anderson & Krathwohl taxonomy, "Creating" (Synthesis) is the highest, but based on the options and traditional context, Evaluation is the answer.

4.
Examples of audio teaching aids include:-
a) Record discs and slides.
b) Film-strips and transparencies.
c) Audio cassettes and tapes.
d) Audio tapes and record discs.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Audio tapes and record discs.

Explanation: Slides, film-strips, and transparencies are visual aids. Only option (d) contains exclusively audio formats.

5.
The main purpose of clinical instruction is:-
a) Task mastery and professional identity mentoring.
b) Task mastery and patient interaction.
c) Professional - identity mentoring and student fulfillment.
d) Improvement of student theoretical knowledge and orientation into the clinical settings.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Task mastery and professional identity mentoring.

Explanation: Clinical instruction aims to help students master psychomotor skills (task mastery) and socialize them into the nursing role (professional identity), bridging the gap between classroom theory and real-world practice.

6.
A theory that is based on non-linear approaches of change is:-
a) Planned change models.
b) Chaos theory.
c) Kurt Lewin's force field analysis.
d) Innovation-Diffurian theory.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Chaos theory.

Explanation: Chaos theory deals with complex, unpredictable, and non-linear systems where small changes can lead to large, unpredictable outcomes (butterfly effect), unlike Lewin's linear 3-step model.

7.
In reference to organizational structures "chain of command" refers to:-
a) The channels of communication.
b) Lines of authority and responsibility.
c) Lines of authority in supervision.
d) Locations where decisions are made in an organization.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Lines of authority and responsibility.

Explanation: The chain of command is the continuous line of authority that extends from the top of the organization to the lowest level and clarifies who reports to whom.

8.
When a nurse manager is doing a "SWOT" analysis of an organization, strengths and weaknesses represent assessment of:-
a) The organizations external environment.
b) The financial and technological capabilities of an organization.
c) Internal environment of the organization.
d) Objectives and strategies of the organization.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Internal environment of the organization.

Explanation: In SWOT Analysis, Strengths and Weaknesses are Internal factors (within the organization's control), while Opportunities and Threats are External factors.

9.
The difference between delegation and assignment is that delegation entails:-
a) Transferring responsibility but retaining accountability.
b) Transferring both responsibility and accountability.
c) Transferring accountability and retaining responsibility.
d) Transferring responsibility, authority and accountability.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Transferring responsibility but retaining accountability.

Explanation: When a nurse delegates a task, they transfer the responsibility (authority to act) to the delegatee but retain the accountability for the outcome. In assignment, the accountability is also transferred (within the scope of practice).

10.
The pareto's principle, when applied to time management, enables nurse manager to:-
a) Delegate tasks.
b) Prioritize tasks.
c) Be more accountable.
d) Reduce time wasters.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Prioritize tasks.

Explanation: Pareto's Principle (80/20 rule) suggests that 80% of results come from 20% of efforts. In time management, it is used to prioritize the critical few tasks that yield the most significant outcomes.

11.
Variables that exist in a study and can affect the measurement of study variables and the relationship between these variables are called:-
a) Independent variables.
b) Extraneous variables.
c) Manipulatable variables.
d) Confounding variables.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Extraneous variables (or d) Confounding variables).

Explanation: Extraneous variables are any variables other than the independent variable that might influence the dependent variable. If they are not controlled and actually do influence the result, they become confounding variables. "Extraneous" is the broader term for variables that "exist and can affect".

12.
Limitations in a study are:-
a) Restrictions in a study that may reduce the generalizability of findings.
b) Restrictions in a study that specifically affect the sampling process.
c) Difficulties in a study due to scarcity of money and time.
d) Restrictions in a study that delay the reporting of study findings.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Restrictions in a study that may reduce the generalizability of findings.

Explanation: Limitations are potential weaknesses in a study (e.g., small sample size, methodology constraints) that are often out of the researcher's control and may restrict the extent to which the findings can be applied to the broader population.

13.
A primary source of literature is better than a secondary source in that:-
a) Secondary sources contain a lot of misinterpretations of the primary sources.
b) Interpretation of the primary sources may be negatively influenced by the authors perception and bias.
c) Secondary sources are always of lower quality compared to primary sources.
d) Primary sources of literature are more readily available than secondary sources.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Secondary sources contain a lot of misinterpretations of the primary sources.

Explanation: Primary sources are original accounts (e.g., the actual research study). Secondary sources interpret or summarize primary sources. Relying on primary sources avoids the risk of error, bias, or misinterpretation introduced by the author of a secondary source.

14.
An hypothesis that is used for statistical testing and interpreting statistical outcomes is called:-
a) Directional hypothesis.
b) Complex hypothesis.
c) Causal hypothesis.
d) Null hypothesis.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Null hypothesis.

Explanation: Statistical tests are designed to test the Null Hypothesis ($H_0$), which states there is no relationship or difference. The goal is often to reject the null hypothesis in favor of the alternative hypothesis.

15.
Internal validity refers to:-
a) The extent to which the effects detected in the study are a true reflection of reality rather than the result of the effects of extraneous variables.
b) The extent to which study findings can be generalized beyond the sample used in the study. (External Validity)
c) The extent to which the extraneous variables have influenced the study variables.
d) The extent to which the dependent variables influences the independent variable.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) The extent to which the effects detected in the study are a true reflection of reality rather than the result of the effects of extraneous variables.

Explanation: Internal validity asks, "Did the independent variable really cause the difference?" It is about the rigor of the study design in controlling for other explanations.

16.
Loosening of association often occurs in:-
a) Mania.
b) Drug induced psychosis.
c) Schizophrenia.
d) Temporal epilepsy.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Schizophrenia.

Explanation: Loose associations (derailment), where thoughts shift between unrelated topics, is a classic thought disorder symptom associated with schizophrenia.

17.
Body dysmorphic disorder is an example of a:-
a) Somatoform disorder.
b) Conversion disorder.
c) Dissociative disorder.
d) Personality disorder.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Somatoform disorder.

Explanation: In older classifications (DSM-IV), BDD was grouped under Somatoform disorders (disorders characterized by physical symptoms suggesting a medical condition). In DSM-5, it is grouped with Obsessive-Compulsive and Related Disorders, but historically/classically in nursing exams, it fits under Somatoform.

18.
The most appropriate therapy for a client who has been diagnosed with acrophobia is:-
a) Flooding.
b) Electroconvulsive therapy.
c) Modeling.
d) Systematic desensitization.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Systematic desensitization.

Explanation: Acrophobia is the fear of heights. Systematic desensitization is the standard behavioral therapy for specific phobias, involving gradual exposure to the feared stimulus while practicing relaxation techniques.

19.
Atypical neuroleptics produce less extra-pyramidal effects because of their reduced effect on:-
a) D5 receptors.
b) D4 receptors.
c) D2 receptors.
d) D3 receptors.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) D2 receptors.

Explanation: Typical antipsychotics strongly block Dopamine D2 receptors in the striatum, causing Extrapyramidal Side Effects (EPS). Atypical antipsychotics have a lower affinity for D2 receptors (or block them transiently) and also block Serotonin (5-HT2A) receptors, resulting in fewer EPS.

20.
A nursing intervention for a client diagnosed with conversion disorder which has resulted to the paralysis of lower extremity is:-
a) Be empathetic with them for suffering paralysis.
b) Give positive reinforcement for any improvement.
c) Advice patient to rest for many hours.
d) Advice the client to apply for sick leave from the employer.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Give positive reinforcement for any improvement.

Explanation: The goal is to avoid reinforcing the "sick role" (secondary gain). Focusing on the symptom (a) or encouraging rest (c) reinforces the disability. The nurse should minimize attention to the physical symptom while positively reinforcing any functional improvement or independence.

PART II: SHORT ANSWER QUESTIONS

40 Marks
1.
State six (6) gains achieved by the mentee during the mentorship process. (6 marks)
Show Answer
  1. Professional Growth: Acquisition of new skills and knowledge.
  2. Confidence Building: Increased self-confidence in professional abilities.
  3. Career Advancement: Guidance on career path and opportunities for networking.
  4. Psychosocial Support: Emotional support and encouragement in a new role.
  5. Role Modelling: Learning professional behaviors and ethics from the mentor.
  6. Feedback: Receiving constructive criticism to improve performance.
  7. Integration: Smoother transition into the workplace culture.
2.
Outline five (5) characteristics of problem-based learning (PBL). (5 marks)
Show Answer
  1. Student-Centered: Learners take responsibility for their own learning; the teacher acts as a facilitator.
  2. Problem-Driven: Learning begins with a problem, query, or puzzle that the learner wants to solve.
  3. Small Groups: Students typically work in small collaborative groups.
  4. Self-Directed Learning: Students identify what they need to learn to solve the problem and seek out resources.
  5. Integration of Knowledge: It integrates multiple disciplines/subjects rather than teaching them in isolation.
  6. Authentic Problems: Problems are often drawn from real-world practice.
3.
Outline the four (4) elements of product model of a curriculum development according to Ralph Tyler (1949). (4 marks)
Show Answer

The Tyler Model (Product Model) consists of four fundamental questions/elements:

  1. Educational Purposes (Objectives): What educational purposes should the school seek to attain?
  2. Educational Experiences (Content/Learning Activities): What educational experiences can be provided that are likely to attain these purposes?
  3. Organization of Experiences: How can these educational experiences be effectively organized?
  4. Evaluation: How can we determine whether these purposes are being attained?
4.
Explain four (4) strategies to enhance effectiveness of meetings. (6 marks)
Show Answer
  1. Have a Clear Agenda: Circulate an agenda beforehand with specific topics and time allocations to keep the meeting focused.
  2. Start and End on Time: Respect participants' time to maintain engagement and discipline.
  3. Effective Chairing/Facilitation: The leader should manage the discussion, ensure everyone participates, prevent dominance by a few, and keep the discussion on track.
  4. Actionable Minutes: Record decisions made, tasks assigned, persons responsible, and deadlines (Action items) rather than just a transcript of the discussion.
  5. Limit Attendance: Invite only necessary participants who can contribute to the decision-making.
5.
Role Transition Question

a) Define role transition. (1 mark)

Role transition is the process of changing from one role to another (e.g., student to staff nurse), involving the acquisition of new values, behaviors, and knowledge required for the new role.

b) Explain five (5) strategies that promote role transition. (5 marks)

  1. Preceptorship/Mentorship Programs: Assigning experienced nurses to guide the new graduate.
  2. Orientation Programs: Structured introduction to the organization's policies, culture, and specific unit routines.
  3. Clinical Internships/Residencies: Extended periods of supervised practice to build confidence and competence.
  4. Simulation Training: Providing safe environments to practice skills before performing them on patients.
  5. Supportive Environment: Encouraging open communication, debriefing sessions, and a non-punitive culture for asking questions.
  6. Strengthening Clinical Placements: Ensuring students have adequate hands-on experience during training.
6.
Explain two (2) main characteristics of pure experimental studies. (4 marks)
Show Answer
  1. Manipulation: The researcher actively intervenes or manipulates the independent variable (e.g., giving a drug) to observe its effect on the dependent variable.
  2. Control: The use of a control group (which does not receive the intervention) to compare with the experimental group, allowing the researcher to attribute changes to the intervention.
  3. Randomization: Subjects are randomly assigned to either the experimental or control group to ensure the groups are comparable and reduce selection bias. (Note: This is the third key element).
7.
State the five stages of grief according to Elizabeth Kubler-Ross. (5 marks)
Show Answer
  1. Denial: "No, not me." Refusal to believe the diagnosis or loss.
  2. Anger: "Why me?" Frustration and looking for someone to blame.
  3. Bargaining: "Yes, me, but..." Negotiating for more time or a cure (often with God).
  4. Depression: "Yes, me." Sadness, withdrawal, and mourning the loss.
  5. Acceptance: "I am ready." Coming to terms with the reality of the situation and finding peace.

PART III: ESSAY/LONG ANSWER QUESTIONS

40 Marks
1.
Mr X, 30 year old teacher is admitted into a psychiatric unit suffering from severe undifferentiated schizophrenia.

a) Explain the biochemical theory of causation of schizophrenia. (2 marks)

The biochemical theory, specifically the Dopamine Hypothesis, suggests that schizophrenia is caused by an excess of dopamine activity in certain areas of the brain (e.g., mesolimbic pathway). This excess dopamine transmission is thought to be responsible for the positive symptoms like hallucinations and delusions. Other neurotransmitters like serotonin, glutamate, and GABA are also implicated.

b) State six (6) main nursing diagnoses you will make for Mr X's condition. (6 marks)

  1. Disturbed Thought Processes related to biochemical imbalance as evidenced by delusions/disorganized thinking.
  2. Disturbed Sensory Perception (Auditory/Visual) related to biochemical imbalance as evidenced by hallucinations.
  3. Risk for Violence: Self-directed or Other-directed related to command hallucinations or paranoid delusions.
  4. Impaired Social Interaction related to withdrawal, flat affect, and poor communication skills.
  5. Self-Care Deficit (Hygiene/Grooming) related to lack of motivation (avolition) and disorganized behavior.
  6. Ineffective Coping related to inadequate support system and cognitive impairment.
  7. Interrupted Family Processes related to the stress of illness on family dynamics.

c) Describe four psychosocial treatment modalities of Mr X, giving a rationale for each. (12 marks)

  1. Individual Psychotherapy (Supportive Therapy):
    • Rationale: To build a therapeutic relationship, improve reality testing, and help the patient develop coping strategies for stress and symptoms.
  2. Social Skills Training:
    • Rationale: Schizophrenia often impairs social functioning. This training uses role-play and modeling to teach daily living skills, communication, and appropriate social behavior to facilitate reintegration into the community.
  3. Family Therapy/Psychoeducation:
    • Rationale: High "Expressed Emotion" (criticism, hostility) in families is linked to relapse. Educating the family about the illness, medication compliance, and stress reduction creates a supportive environment and reduces relapse rates.
  4. Group Therapy:
    • Rationale: Provides a safe setting to practice social skills, receive feedback from peers, reduce isolation, and realize that others share similar experiences (universality).
  5. Vocational Rehabilitation:
    • Rationale: To assess work skills and provide training/support to help Mr. X return to work (as a teacher or other role), enhancing self-esteem and financial independence.
2.
Quantitative research and qualitative research are two scientific approaches for establishing truth about a certain phenomenon.

a) State five (5) aspects that a researcher has to disclose to the subjects to achieve informed consent. (5 marks)

  1. Purpose of the Study: Why the research is being conducted.
  2. Procedures: What exactly will happen to the participant (interventions, tests, time commitment).
  3. Risks and Discomforts: Any potential physical, psychological, or social harm.
  4. Benefits: Potential benefits to the participant or to society/science.
  5. Voluntary Participation: The right to refuse to participate or withdraw at any time without penalty.
  6. Confidentiality/Anonymity: How their data will be protected and who will have access to it.
  7. Contact Information: Whom to contact for questions or in case of injury.

b) Describe the quantitative research process. (15 marks)

The quantitative research process is a linear, systematic series of steps:

  1. Formulating the Research Problem: Identifying a gap in knowledge and defining the specific research question.
  2. Literature Review: Reviewing existing studies to understand what is known and refine the problem.
  3. Formulating Hypotheses/Objectives: Stating the expected relationship between variables (predictions) to be tested.
  4. Research Design: Selecting the overall plan (e.g., experimental, descriptive, correlational) to answer the question.
  5. Defining the Population and Sample: Identifying the target group and selecting a representative sample using probability sampling methods.
  6. Instrumentation (Data Collection Tools): Developing or selecting valid and reliable tools (e.g., questionnaires, checklists) to measure variables numerically.
  7. Pilot Study: Testing the instrument on a small sample to ensure it works.
  8. Data Collection: Gathering the data from the study subjects in a standardized manner.
  9. Data Analysis: Using statistical software (e.g., SPSS) to perform descriptive (mean, mode) and inferential statistics (t-tests, chi-square) to test hypotheses.
  10. Interpretation of Results: Discussing the findings in relation to the hypothesis and existing literature.
  11. Dissemination: Communicating findings through reports, publications, or presentations.
