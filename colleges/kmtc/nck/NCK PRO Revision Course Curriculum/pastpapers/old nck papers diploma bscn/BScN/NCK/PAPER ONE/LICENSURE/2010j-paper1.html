Part I: Multiple Choice Questions

1.
The celiac artery has three primary branches, namely;
a) Left inferior phrenic, right gastric and left gastro-epiploic arteries.
b) Left gastric, splenic and common hepatic arteries.
c) Common hepatic, right gastric and left gastro-epiploic arteries.
d) Gastroduodenal, cystic and right gastro-epiploic arteries
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Left gastric, splenic and common hepatic arteries.

Explanation: The celiac trunk (celiac artery) is the first major branch of the abdominal aorta and it divides into three major branches: the Left Gastric Artery, the Splenic Artery, and the Common Hepatic Artery. These supply the foregut structures (stomach, liver, spleen, pancreas, duodenum).

2.
While planning a meal for a patient suffering from atherosclerosis, the nurse should include:
a) 30% of daily calorie intake with 10% saturated fats
b) 20% of daily calorie intake and 400mg of daily cholesterol intake.
c) 50% of daily calorie intake with 5% polysaturated fats
d) 10% of daily calorie intake with 10% monounsaturated fats
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) 30% of daily calorie intake with 10% saturated fats

Explanation: Standard guidelines for heart-healthy diets (like the AHA Step 1 diet) recommend that total fat intake should be no more than 30% of daily calories, with saturated fat limited to 8-10% (or less, <7% for therapeutic lifestyle changes). Option (a) reflects this standard balance. High cholesterol intake (option b) and very low calorie percentages (option d) are not standard recommendations.

3.
On assessing a 15 month old infant, the nurse would expect him to:
a) Follow one-step command with gesture, use two words to communicate.
b) Use two word combination, point to five body parts when named.
c) Follow one-step command without gesture, use four words to communicate
d) Use seven words to communicate, point to one body part when named
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Follow one-step command with gesture, use two words to communicate.

Explanation: By 15 months, a toddler can typically walk alone, say 3-6 words, and follow simple one-step commands (often accompanied by a gesture).
Developmental Milestones:
- 15 months: 3-6 words, 1-step command.
- 18 months: ~10-20 words, points to 1 body part.
- 24 months: 2-word combinations, points to 5-6 body parts.

4.
Aspartate aminotransferase (AST):
a) Decreases below 35u/ml with hepatocellular injury
b) Decreases to 2u/ml with increase of alkaline phosphates
c) Rises above 13u/ml with billiary obstruction
d) Rises above 40 u/ml with hepatocellular injury
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Rises above 40 u/ml with hepatocellular injury

Explanation: Normal AST levels are typically 8-40 U/L. AST is an enzyme released into the blood when liver cells (hepatocytes) are damaged. Therefore, in hepatocellular injury (like hepatitis or cirrhosis), levels rise significantly above the normal upper limit of 40 U/L. Decreasing levels (a, b) are not indicative of injury.

5.
Clinical features of hydatidiform mole include;
a) Positive pregnancy test of urine diluted to 1:100 after 4 weeks of amenorrhea, ultrasound shows the fetus
b) Amenorrhea followed by vomiting, breast changes.
c) Positive pregnancy test (diluted 1:100), vaginal bleeding, "snowstorm" pattern on ultrasound
d) Normal hCG levels, fetal heart tones present
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Positive pregnancy test (diluted 1:100), vaginal bleeding, "snowstorm" pattern on ultrasound

Explanation: Note: Option C is reconstructed based on clinical facts as the provided source text for Option A and B was incomplete/contradictory.
Hydatidiform mole is characterized by excessively high levels of hCG (causing a positive test even when urine is diluted 1:100), vaginal bleeding (often "prune juice" color), hyperemesis gravidarum, and a characteristic "snowstorm" or "bunch of grapes" appearance on ultrasound with no fetus present (in complete mole).
