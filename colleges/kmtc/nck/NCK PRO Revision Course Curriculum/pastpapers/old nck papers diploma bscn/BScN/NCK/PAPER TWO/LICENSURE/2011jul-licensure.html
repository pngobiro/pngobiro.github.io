Part I: Multiple Choice Questions

20 Marks
1.
Persistence of moro reflex beyond the age of 6 months is usually suggestive of:
a) Immaturity
b) Brain damage
c) Mental retardation
d) Brachial plexus palsy
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Brain damage

Explanation: The Moro reflex normally disappears by 3-6 months. Its persistence beyond 6 months indicates a delay in neurological maturation or an underlying neurological abnormality, such as cerebral palsy (brain damage).

2.
Babies born with intrauterine growth retardation are usually prone to;
a) Congenital abnormalities, hyperglycemia
b) Neonatal hyperglcemia, asphyxia
c) Nenatal asphyxia, birth defects
d) Neonatal tetanus, asphyxia
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Neonatal hyperglcemia, asphyxia (Note: "Hyperglcemia" is a likely typo for Hypoglycemia)

Explanation: IUGR infants have limited glycogen stores and are highly prone to Hypoglycemia. They are also prone to Perinatal Asphyxia due to poor placental reserve. While they may have congenital anomalies as a cause, hypoglycemia and asphyxia are the immediate complications they are prone to.

3.
Mrs. Ug, presents in maternity ward with pulsating prolapsed umbilical cord. The recommended nursing position would be;
a) Knee-chest position, exaggerated Sim's position
b) Fowler's position, McRoberts manouvre position
c) Sim's position, trendelenburg position
d) Prone position, knee-chest position
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Knee-chest position, exaggerated Sim's position

Explanation: The goal is to relieve pressure of the presenting part on the cord. Knee-chest position (or Trendelenburg) uses gravity to shift the fetus away from the pelvis.

4.
Lochia serosa is composed of:
a) Decidua, vernix, leucocytes
b) Leucocytess, cervical mucous, decidua
c) Cervical mucous, cholestrin crystals, lanugo
d) Leucocytes, cervical mucous, erythrocytes
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Leucocytes, cervical mucous, erythrocytes

Explanation: Lochia serosa (days 4-10) is pinkish-brown and contains serous exudate, erythrocytes (giving the color), leukocytes, cervical mucus, and microorganisms.

5.
During second stage of labour, the diameter that distends the vaginal orifice in face presentation is:
a) Occipito frontal
b) Submentovertical
c) Submento-bregmatic
d) Mentovertical
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Submento-bregmatic

Explanation: In a fully extended face presentation, the submento-bregmatic diameter (9.5 cm) is the engaging diameter and the one that distends the vaginal orifice/vulva if the chin is anterior.

6.
The right time for diagnosing HIV infection using antibody tests among children born to HIV positive mother is at or after;
a) 12 months of age
b) 16 months of age
c) 18 months of age
d) 6 months of age
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) 18 months of age

Explanation: Maternal HIV antibodies can persist in the infant's circulation for up to 18 months. Therefore, antibody tests (ELISA/Rapid) are only diagnostic after this period. Before 18 months, DNA-PCR is required for diagnosis.

7.
During management of severe eclampsia;
a) Anticonvulsant therapy should be given once daily
b) Ketamine is the drug of choice in performing a caesarean delivery
c) Nifedipine can be used to lower the blood pressure
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Nifedipine can be used to lower the blood pressure

Explanation: Rapid-acting oral Nifedipine is a common choice for acute management of severe hypertension in eclampsia. Anticonvulsants (Magnesium Sulphate) are given continuously/frequently, not once daily. Ketamine is generally avoided as it raises BP.

8.
Benefits of breastfeeding to the infant include:
a) Increased cognitive development, less chances of developing diabetes type 2, less risk of extreme obesity
b) Lowered risk of infection, less likely to become overweight, protection against malignancies
d) Strengthens bonding, protection against malignancies, decreased risk of cardiovascular diseases
e) Reduced risk of metabolic syndrome, lowered risk of developing necrotizing enterocolitis, protection against diabetes type 1
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Lowered risk of infection, less likely to become overweight, protection against malignancies

Explanation: All options list valid benefits, but Lowered risk of infection (gastrointestinal, respiratory, ear infections) is the most distinct and immediate benefit due to maternal antibodies (IgA).

9.
The common age of onset for gonoccocal ophthalmia neonatorum is:
a) Between 21-28 days after birth
b) Between 0-7 days after birth
c) Between 7-14 days after birth
d) At 28 days of age
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Between 0-7 days after birth

Explanation: Gonococcal conjunctivitis typically presents early, within the first 2-5 days of life. Chlamydial conjunctivitis presents later (5-14 days).

10.
Perinatal mortality refers to foetal and infants deaths including still births occurring from 28 weeks of gestation to within the first:
a) One week of life per 1000 live births
b) Two weeks of life per 1000 live births
c) Four weeks of life per 1000 live births
d) Six weeks of life per 1000 live births
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) One week of life per 1000 live births

Explanation: The Perinatal Mortality Rate includes stillbirths (after 28 weeks) and early neonatal deaths (deaths within the first 7 days or 1 week of life).

11.
The normal acidity found in the vaginal fluid usually results from;
a) Acidic cervical secretions
b) Emission of acid from vaginal walls
c) Action of lactobacilli on vaginal glycogen
d) Consumption of acidic fruits and vegetables
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Action of lactobacilli on vaginal glycogen

Explanation: Döderlein's bacilli (lactobacilli) metabolize glycogen in the vaginal epithelium into lactic acid, maintaining an acidic pH which protects against infection.

12.
The hormone responsible for relaxing the pelvic girdle, softening the cervix and suppressing uterine contractions towards the end of pregnancy is:
a) Oxytocin
b) Progesterone
c) Oestrogrn
d) Relaxin
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Relaxin

Explanation: Relaxin causes relaxation of pelvic ligaments (symphysis pubis) and cervical softening (ripening) in preparation for labour.

13.
The most common indication of a retained succenturate placenta lobe is:
a) A knot on thee insertion site of the umbilical cord
b) Centrally inserted umbilical cord into the placental membranes
c) A hole in the placental membranes with blood vessels running into it
d) Umbilical vessels running through the membranes throughout the placenta
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) A hole in the placental membranes with blood vessels running into it

Explanation: A succenturiate lobe is an accessory lobe connected by vessels. If it is retained, inspection of the delivered placenta/membranes will reveal a hole in the membranes with torn vessels that lead to the missing lobe.

14.
The anterior posterior fontanelles normally close by the age of:
a) 6weeks and 18 weeks respectively
b) 18 weeks and 6 weeks respectively
c) 18 months and 6 weeks respectively
d) 6 months and 18 months respectively
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) 18 months and 6 weeks respectively

Explanation: The Posterior fontanelle closes early (by 6-8 weeks). The Anterior fontanelle (Bregma) stays open longer for brain growth, closing by 18 months.

17.
When performing Leopold's maneuver, the purpose of the first maneuver is to determine:
a) Fetal part lying in the fundus and presentation
b) Location of fetal back and position
c) Engagement of the presenting part
d) The degree of flexion of fetal head
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Fetal part lying in the fundus and presentation

Explanation: The First Maneuver (Fundal Grip) determines which fetal pole (head or breech) occupies the fundus.

18.
The most important benefit of vestibular stimulation in the normal newborn is improvement of:
a) Sucking capability
b) Tactile sensitivity
c) Motor co-ordination
d) Attention span
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Motor co-ordination

Explanation: Vestibular stimulation (rocking, handling) helps in the development of balance, muscle tone, and motor coordination.

19.
For question 19 & 20 indicate the answer whether the statements are TRUE or FALSE concerning tetralogy of fallot.
a) There is obstruction of pulmonary outflow
b) The patient manifests with right ventricular hypertrophy and ventricular septal defect
Show Answer

a) TRUE (Pulmonary stenosis is a key feature).

b) TRUE (The four components are Pulmonary Stenosis, VSD, Overriding Aorta, and Right Ventricular Hypertrophy).

20.
Concerning Tetralogy of Fallot:
a) It is associated with a higher incidence of major non-heart congenital defects
b) Infants with this condition show obvious signs of cyanosis at birth
Show Answer

a) TRUE (Associated with syndromes like DiGeorge, Down's, cleft palate, etc.).

b) FALSE (Cyanosis is often not present immediately at birth "Pink Tet" but develops later as the ductus closes or stenosis increases, leading to "Tet spells").
