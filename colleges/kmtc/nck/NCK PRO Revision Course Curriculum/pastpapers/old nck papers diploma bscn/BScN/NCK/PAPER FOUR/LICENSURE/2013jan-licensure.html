PART I: MULTIPLE CHOICE QUESTIONS

20 Marks
1.
Paranoid personality disorder is usually characterized by:
a) Social anxiety, inability to make close friendship
b) Suspiciousness, social anxiety
c) Suspiciousness, sensitivity
d) Self dramatization, inability to make close friendship
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Suspiciousness, sensitivity

Explanation: Paranoid personality disorder is characterized by a pervasive distrust and suspiciousness of others such that their motives are interpreted as malevolent. They are often hypersensitive to criticism.

2.
When developing educational objectives the cognitive domain should focus on:
a) Attitudes, knowledge, skills
b) Values, feelings, knowledge
c) Skill, performance, attitude
d) Knowledge, concepts, facts
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Knowledge, concepts, facts

Explanation: The cognitive domain (Bloom's Taxonomy) deals with mental processes, including knowledge recall, comprehension, application, analysis, synthesis, and evaluation. Attitudes belong to the Affective domain, and Skills belong to the Psychomotor domain.

3.
Transformational leadership aims at:
a) Maintaining equilibrium or the status quo
b) Performing work according to policy
c) Inspiring others to achieve the organizational vision
d) Performing tasks at ones best to improve efficiency
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Inspiring others to achieve the organizational vision

Explanation: Transformational leaders inspire and motivate followers to exceed expectations and achieve a shared vision, often facilitating change. Maintaining status quo is characteristic of Transactional leadership.

4.
A null hypothesis:
a) Always states that a real relationship exists between two variables
b) Always states that no real relationship exists between two variables
c) Is suitable where previous findings conflict
d) Specifies the nature of the relationship between unknown variables
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Always states that no real relationship exists between two variables

Explanation: The null hypothesis ($H_0$) assumes there is no significant difference or relationship between variables, and any observed difference is due to chance.

5.
The stages of grieving process according to Elizabeth Kubler Ross are:
a) Shock, disbelief, awareness, restitution, recovery
b) Denial, anger, bargaining, depression, acceptance
c) Shock, denial, anger, depression, acceptance
d) Denial, anger, bargaining, restitution, recovery
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Denial, anger, bargaining, depression, acceptance

Explanation: The classic "DABDA" model by Kubler-Ross outlines the five stages of grief.

6.
The purpose of organizing in management is to:
a) Assign duties and co-ordinate the efforts of individuals
b) Spread responsibilities for final results to the top management
c) Distribute the human resource equally in the organization
d) Ensure that work is organized at all levels to ensure control
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Assign duties and co-ordinate the efforts of individuals

Explanation: Organizing involves structuring resources, assigning tasks (division of labor), and coordinating efforts to achieve organizational goals efficiently.

7.
In teaching, the skill of stimulus variation is achieved by:
a) Clarity, fluency
b) Paragraphing, repetition of major points
c) Use of gestures, variation of speech pattern
d) Use of gestures, avoiding negative expression
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Use of gestures, variation of speech pattern

Explanation: Stimulus variation refers to changing the mode of presentation to maintain student attention. This includes movement, gestures, changing speech patterns (tone, volume, speed), and switching sensory channels.

8.
The autonomic nervous system side effects of tricyclic anti-depressant drugs are:
a) Muscle twitching, fine tremor, peripheral neuropathy
b) Agranulocytosis, jaundice, epileptic seizures
c) Constipation, increased sweating, difficulty in micturition
d) Tachycardia, epileptic fits, muscle twitching
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Constipation, increased sweating, difficulty in micturition

Explanation: Tricyclic antidepressants (TCAs) have strong anticholinergic properties which affect the autonomic nervous system. Common side effects include dry mouth, blurred vision, constipation, urinary retention (difficulty in micturition), and tachycardia.

9.
Capital budgets in an organization:
a) Consist of salary and personnel expenditure
b) Represents income expected for the budget period
c) Is the statement of expected revenue for a period of one year
d) Aims at meeting the term goals of an organization
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Aims at meeting the term goals of an organization

Explanation: Capital budgets are used for long-term investments like purchasing major equipment, buildings, or infrastructure ("term goals"). Salaries (a) are operating budgets. Revenue (b/c) is the revenue budget.

10.
In total quality management, benchmarking refers to:
a) Measurement of the organization performance against the best in the industry
b) Quest for excellence in creating the best services that meet customer needs
c) Maintaining excellence in order to maintain a competitive advantage
d) Measurement of performance against set standard
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Measurement of the organization performance against the best in the industry

Explanation: Benchmarking is the process of comparing one's business processes and performance metrics to industry bests or best practices from other companies.

11.
The disadvantage of simulation as an instructional method include:
a) Allows the student to make their first serious mistake in a simulated situation rather than real one
b) Simulation techniques cannot model all dimensions of a real situation
c) Simulations provide a responsive environment since feedback is given
d) There is a link between training situation and real situation
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Simulation techniques cannot model all dimensions of a real situation

Explanation: While simulation is effective, it is often impossible to fully recreate the complexity, pressure, and nuances of real clinical environments.

12.
The accuracy and consistency of measurement, giving essentially the same results under similar conditions are:
a) Reliability
b) Predictability
c) Generalizability
d) Validity
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Reliability

Explanation: Reliability refers to the consistency or repeatability of a measure. Validity (d) refers to accuracy (measuring what it is supposed to measure).

13.
Anti-anxiety medication produce a calming effect by:
a) Inhibiting the production of monoamine oxidase enzyme
b) Depressing the sub-cortical levels of the central nervous system
c) Decreasing levels of norepinephrine and serotonin in the brain
d) Decreasing the levels of dopamine in the brain
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Depressing the sub-cortical levels of the central nervous system

Explanation: Anxiolytics (like Benzodiazepines) generally work by enhancing GABA (inhibitory neurotransmitter), leading to depression of the CNS, particularly the limbic system (sub-cortical) which controls emotion.

14.
A sample error can be defined as:
a) Any statistic data computed on a sample and has a sampling distribution
b) The discrepancy between the characteristics of the population and characteristics of the sample
c) A mathematical abstraction based on the chance of probability
d) A sample technique that allows the researcher to differentiate information
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) The discrepancy between the characteristics of the population and characteristics of the sample

Explanation: Sampling error is the difference between the sample statistic (e.g., sample mean) and the true population parameter (e.g., population mean) caused by chance in the selection of sample units.

15.
One of Mc Gregor theory X assumption states that:
a) An average person can learn responsibility
b) Human beings wants to avoid responsibility and need to be directed
c) Human beings like to exercise control of their work
d) The ability to make innovation is widely spread throughout population
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Human beings wants to avoid responsibility and need to be directed

Explanation: Theory X assumes employees are inherently lazy, dislike work, avoid responsibility, and need strict supervision (direction). Theory Y assumes the opposite (a, c, d).

16.
In conflict resolution accommodation refers to:
a) A strategy where both parties work to achieve satisfaction for all
b) A strategy where individuals neglect their own concerns in favor of the other persons concern
c) A strategy where either party fail to confront the issue
d) A strategy where both parties takes a middle position
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) A strategy where individuals neglect their own concerns in favor of the other persons concern

Explanation: Accommodation (smoothing) involves self-sacrifice and yielding to the other party's wishes to maintain harmony.

17.
The measure of variability as used in descriptive statistics are:
a) Mean, mode, variance
b) Standard deviation, range, variance
c) Mean, mode, median
d) Median, range, variance
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Standard deviation, range, variance

Explanation: Measures of variability (dispersion) describe the spread of data. Mean, Mode, and Median are measures of central tendency.

18.
The symptoms of catatonic schizophrenia include:
a) Flat affect, auditory hallucinations, suspicion
b) Incongruous giggling, facial grimaces, delusions of persecution
c) Mutism, negativism, waxy flexibility
d) Delusions of grandeur, argumentative, suspicious
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Mutism, negativism, waxy flexibility

Explanation: Catatonic schizophrenia is characterized by profound changes in motor activity, including immobility (stupor), waxy flexibility, mutism, and negativism (resisting instructions).

19.
In research problem statement:
a) Clearly indicates the researcher anticipated explanation or opinion regarding the study
b) Focuses on the phenomenon that the researcher desires to describe, predict, control or explain
c) Provides the basis for establishing hypothesis and observation for the study
d) Involves locating, regarding and evaluating reports and observations to identify the study purpose
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Focuses on the phenomenon that the researcher desires to describe, predict, control or explain

Explanation: The problem statement articulates the specific issue, gap, or phenomenon that needs to be addressed by the research.

20.
In an organization, policies are:
a) The decisions that govern action
b) The processes that influence allocation of scarce resources
c) The rules/procedures that govern an action
d) Step by step ways of carrying out the processes in an organization
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) The rules/procedures that govern an action

Explanation: Policies are general guidelines or rules used to guide decision-making and action within an organization. (Note: "Step by step ways" describes procedures).

PART II: SHORT ANSWER QUESTIONS

40 Marks
1.
State six (6) disadvantages of using lecture as a method of teaching (6 Marks)
Show Answer
  1. Passive Learning: Students are passive listeners rather than active participants.
  2. Limited Feedback: It is difficult for the teacher to gauge student understanding in real-time.
  3. Short Attention Span: Students may lose concentration after 15-20 minutes.
  4. One-Way Communication: Does not encourage discussion or critical thinking.
  5. Ignores Individual Differences: Assumes all students learn at the same pace.
  6. Poor for Skills Teaching: Not suitable for teaching psychomotor skills or changing attitudes.
2.
Explain the first three (3) psychosexual development stages in children according to Sigmund Freud (6 Marks)
Show Answer
  1. Oral Stage (Birth to 18 months): Pleasure centers on the mouth (sucking, biting, chewing). Conflict involves weaning.
  2. Anal Stage (18 months to 3 years): Pleasure focuses on bowel and bladder elimination; coping with demands for control (toilet training).
  3. Phallic Stage (3 to 6 years): Pleasure zone is the genitals; coping with incestuous sexual feelings (Oedipus/Electra complex).
3.
Explain three (3) factors/threats that can affect the internal validity of an experimental study. (6 Marks)
Show Answer
  1. History: Specific events occurring between the first and second measurement in addition to the experimental variable.
  2. Maturation: Processes within the subjects operating as a function of the passage of time (e.g., growing older, hungrier, more tired).
  3. Testing: The effects of taking a test on the scores of a second testing.
  4. Instrumentation: Changes in the calibration of a measuring instrument or changes in the observers/scorers.
  5. Selection Bias: Biases resulting in differential selection of respondents for the comparison groups.
  6. Attrition (Mortality): Loss of subjects from the comparison groups.
4.
State five (5) factors that influence curriculum development for health workers (5 Marks)
Show Answer
  1. Societal Health Needs: Disease patterns and community health demands.
  2. Government Policy: Ministry of Health guidelines and regulations.
  3. Professional Standards: Nursing Council of Kenya requirements.
  4. Available Resources: Financial, infrastructure, and human resources.
  5. Learner Characteristics: Entry behavior and educational background.
  6. Advances in Knowledge/Technology: New medical discoveries and treatments.
5.
Research Instruments

a) State four (4) purposes for pre-testing a research instrument/tool (4 Marks)

  • To identify ambiguous or unclear questions.
  • To estimate the time required to complete the tool.
  • To detect flaws in the layout or instructions.
  • To determine if the data collected will be relevant to the study objectives.

b) Differentiate between structured and unstructured interview (2 Marks)

  • Structured Interview: Uses a pre-determined set of questions asked in the same order for every respondent. Response categories are often fixed.
  • Unstructured Interview: Flexible and conversational; the interviewer has a general topic but allows the respondent to guide the flow, using open-ended questions.
6.
State five behavioral indicators of sexual abuse in children (5 Marks)
Show Answer
  1. Sexual knowledge or behavior inappropriate for the child's age.
  2. Sudden refusal to go to school or be with specific adults.
  3. Regressive behavior (e.g., bedwetting, thumb sucking).
  4. Sleep disturbances or nightmares.
  5. Withdrawal, depression, or excessive secrecy.
  6. Running away from home.
7.
Evaluation in Teaching

a) Explain the term “evaluation” as used in teaching and learning (2 Marks)

Evaluation is the systematic process of collecting, analyzing, and interpreting information to determine the extent to which instructional objectives are being achieved and to make decisions about the effectiveness of teaching and learning.

b) State four (4) purposes for evaluating learners (4 Marks)

  • To grade, rank, or certify students.
  • To provide feedback to students on their progress.
  • To motivate students to learn.
  • To diagnose learning difficulties.
  • To evaluate the effectiveness of teaching methods.

PART III: ESSAY/LONG ANSWER QUESTIONS

40 Marks
1.
You have been deployed as the Nursing Director of hospital X and you are expected to implement various changes for effective management.

a) Explain four (4) factors that can cause resistance to change (8 Marks)

  1. Fear of the Unknown: Staff may feel anxious about how the change will affect their jobs or routines.
  2. Lack of Trust: If there is a history of poor management or broken promises, staff may distrust the motives behind the change.
  3. Threat to Job Security/Status: Fear that the change will result in layoffs, demotions, or loss of authority.
  4. Poor Timing: Introducing change when staff are already overwhelmed or during a crisis.
  5. Lack of Communication: Failure to explain the "why" and "how" of the change leads to rumors and resistance.

b) Describe as a nurse director how you can overcome resistance to change (12 Marks)

  1. Education and Communication: Openly discuss the reasons for change, the benefits, and the process. Hold meetings and Q&A sessions.
  2. Participation and Involvement: Involve staff in the planning and implementation process. People support what they help create.
  3. Support and Facilitation: Provide training, resources, and emotional support to help staff adapt to new roles or technologies.
  4. Negotiation and Agreement: Offer incentives or trade-offs to key resistors to gain their compliance.
  5. Manipulation and Co-optation: (Use cautiously) Giving a key resistor a desirable role in the change process to win them over.
  6. Explicit and Implicit Coercion: (Last resort) Using authority to force compliance (e.g., "accept it or leave"), though this can damage morale.
  7. Building Trust: consistently acting with integrity to build a reservoir of goodwill.
2.
Patient X, a fifty five (55) year old male has been admitted In your ward and is scheduled for Electro-Convulsive therapy (ECT).

a) State five (5) contra-indications for ECT. (5 Marks)

  1. Increased Intracranial Pressure (Absolute Contraindication): e.g., Brain tumor, hematoma.
  2. Recent Myocardial Infarction: Within the last 3 months.
  3. Severe Hypertension: Uncontrolled high blood pressure.
  4. Retinal Detachment: Risk of further damage.
  5. Cerebrovascular Accident (Stroke): Recent history.
  6. Pheochromocytoma: Risk of hypertensive crisis.
