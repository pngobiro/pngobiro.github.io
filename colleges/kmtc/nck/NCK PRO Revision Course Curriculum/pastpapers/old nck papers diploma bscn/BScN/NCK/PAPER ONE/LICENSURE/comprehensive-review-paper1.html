Questions 1 - 25

1.
A patient with common bile duct obstruction related to cancer of the pancreas is likely to present with:
a) Brown faeces, dark coloured urine
b) Scleral icterus, malena stool
c) Dark coloured urine, scleral icterus
d) Jaundice, brown faeces
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Dark coloured urine, scleral icterus

Explanation: In common bile duct obstruction (obstructive jaundice), bilirubin cannot enter the intestine.
- Dark urine: Conjugated bilirubin builds up in the blood and is excreted by the kidneys.
- Scleral icterus: Deposition of bilirubin in the sclera (jaundice).
- Stools: Would be pale/clay-colored (acholic), not brown, because stercobilin is absent.

2.
Monochorionic twinning is always characterized by the following:
a) One placenta, one chorion
b) One placenta, one amnion
c) One chorion, one amnion
d) Two amnions, two chorions
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) One placenta, one chorion

Explanation: "Chorion" generally corresponds to the placentation. Monochorionic twins share a single placenta and a single outer membrane (chorion). They may have one or two amniotic sacs (monoamniotic or diamniotic), but they always share the chorion/placenta.

3.
Following hemorrhoidectomy the nurse should:
a) Ensure the patient sits on a soft cushion for the first three days.
b) Advise the patient to only request for a bland diet in menu till bowel action normalizes.
c) Ensure the patient has a sitz bath after every bowel action.
d) Remove the anal pack 8 hours after the surgery to allow for passing of flatus.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Ensure the patient has a sitz bath after every bowel action.

Explanation: Sitz baths promote hygiene, reduce pain/spasm, and increase circulation to the area to promote healing, especially after bowel movements.

4.
Indicate whether the following statements are true or false:
a) The left kidney is slightly lower than the right kidney.
Show Answer

Answer: False

Explanation: The right kidney is typically slightly lower than the left kidney because of the space occupied by the liver on the right side.

5.
Events of foetal development that happen during 8-12 weeks gestation include:
a) Fetal heart heard on auscultation, vernix caseosa appears
b) Lanugo appears, swallowing begins
c) Fetus responds to sound, eyelids close
d) Finger nails can be seen, gender determined
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Finger nails can be seen, gender determined (external genitalia differentiation)

Explanation: Between 8-12 weeks, major organogenesis concludes. External genitalia differentiate (detectable by 12 weeks), and nail beds form. Fetal heart tones can be heard by Doppler (10-12 weeks). Vernix/Lanugo appear later (around 20 weeks).

6.
The diagonal conjugate is measured anteroposteriorly from:
a) Lower border of symphysis to sacropromontory.
b) Upper border of symphysis to sacropromontory.
c) From the symphysis to coccyx.
d) From anterior of symphysis to the sacropromontory.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Lower border of symphysis to sacropromontory.

Explanation: The diagonal conjugate is a clinical measurement of the pelvic inlet, measured during vaginal examination from the lower border of the symphysis pubis to the sacral promontory. It is usually ~12.5 cm.

Following hemorrhoidectomy the nurse should:

8.
A late manifestation of compartment syndrome is:
a) Pallor.
b) Paresthesia.
c) Pulselessness.
d) Pain.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Pulselessness.

Explanation: The 6 Ps of compartment syndrome are Pain, Paresthesia, Pallor, Paralysis, Poikilothermia, and Pulselessness. Pain is the earliest sign. Pulselessness is a very late and ominous sign, indicating total arterial occlusion.

9.
The following are characteristics of the chorion membrane:
a) Tears to the edge of placenta, tough, smooth.
b) Tears to cord insertion, tough, smooth.
c) Tears to cord insertion, opaque, thick.
d) Opaque, thick, tears to the edge of placenta.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Opaque, thick, tears to the edge of placenta.

Explanation: The chorion is the outer membrane. It is opaque, thick, friable, and attached to the edge of the placenta. The amnion is the inner membrane, which is smooth, tough, and translucent, and can be peeled back to the cord insertion.

10.
The personality disorder associated with being insensitive to others and abusive behavior without remorse is:
a) Narcissistic
b) Paranoid
c) Histrionic
d) Antisocial
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Antisocial

Explanation: Antisocial Personality Disorder is characterized by a pervasive pattern of disregard for and violation of the rights of others, lack of remorse, deceitfulness, and impulsivity.

11.
The clinical features for kidney rejection post transplantation includes:
a) Anaemia, anuria, hypotension.
b) Hypertension, weight gain, oliguria.
c) Tenderness on the area over the transplanted kidney, weight gain, Hypokalemia.
d) Hyperuricemia, hyperactivity and polyuria
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Hypertension, weight gain, oliguria.

Explanation: Signs of acute rejection include fever, hypertension, graft tenderness, oliguria (decreased urine output), weight gain (fluid retention), and rising creatinine.

12.
On abdominal examination during labour, the sinciput is felt and occiput just felt. The descent is:
a) 4/5.
b) 3/5.
c) 2/5.
d) 1/5.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) 3/5 (or potentially 2/5 depending on specific guidelines).

Explanation: Using the rule of fifths per abdomen:
- If sinciput and occiput are both easily felt, the head is likely 4/5 or 5/5 above brim.
- If sinciput is felt but occiput is only "just felt" (meaning most of the occiput has descended), this usually corresponds to 3/5 or 2/5 palpable above the brim (engaged).
Standard NCK Interpretation: If occiput is just felt, it means the widest diameter has likely passed or is passing, often noted as 2/5 or 3/5.

13.
A classification of ringworms that infect the finger nails is:
a) Tinea corporis.
b) Tinea unguium.
c) Tinea cruris.
d) Tinea pedis.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Tinea unguium.

Explanation:
- Tinea unguium (Onychomycosis): Nails.
- Tinea corporis: Body (ringworm).
- Tinea cruris: Groin (jock itch).
- Tinea pedis: Feet (athlete's foot).

14.
When estimating the size of burns, the palm of the patient's hand represents total body surface area of:
a) 3%.
b) 1%.
c) 4.5%.
d) 9%.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) 1%.

Explanation: In the "Rule of Palms", the patient's palm (including fingers) represents approximately 1% of their Total Body Surface Area (TBSA). This is useful for estimating irregular or small burns.

15.
The prophylactic drugs given to a term new born at birth are:
a) 3% tetracycline eye ointment, vit. K 0.5mg i.m. stat.
b) Gentamycin eye drops, vit K. i.m. stat.
c) 1% tetracycline eye ointment, vit K, 1 mg i.m. stat.
d) Gentamycin eye drop, vit. K 1 mg i.m. stat.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) 1% tetracycline eye ointment, vit K, 1 mg i.m. stat.

Explanation: Standard newborn care in Kenya involves:
- Eye care: Tetracycline 1% ointment (prevention of Ophthalmia Neonatorum).
- Vitamin K: 1 mg IM for term infants (0.5 mg for preterms) to prevent hemorrhagic disease of the newborn.

16.
The process of immersing used dressing instruments in dilute hibitane solution is known as:
a) Chemical sterilization.
b) Disinfection.
c) Decontamination.
d) Cold sterilization.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Decontamination.

Explanation: Immersion in a disinfectant solution (like 0.5% chlorine or hibitane) immediately after use is termed decontamination. This makes items safer to handle before cleaning and sterilization.

17.
Specific defence mechanisms are achieved by:
a) Phagocytosis.
b) Chemotaxis.
c) Inflammatory responses.
d) Cell-mediated immunity.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Cell-mediated immunity.

Explanation:
- Non-specific (Innate): Phagocytosis, inflammation, barriers.
- Specific (Adaptive): Humoral (Antibody-mediated) and Cell-mediated immunity (T-cells target specific antigens).

18.
The cycle in which bile salts and other substances excreted by the liver are absorbed by the intestinal mucosa and returned to the liver via the portal circulation is:
a) Extra corporeal circulation.
b) Enterohepatic circulation.
c) Collateral circulation.
d) Mesenteric circulation.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Enterohepatic circulation.

Explanation: Enterohepatic circulation refers to the circulation of biliary acids, bilirubin, drugs, or other substances from the liver to the bile, followed by entry into the small intestine, absorption by the enterocyte and transport back to the liver.

19.
When a midwife closes the curtains, he/she minimizes heat loss by:
a) Conduction
b) Evaporation
c) Convection
d) Radiation.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Convection (or Radiation).

Explanation: Closing curtains/windows stops drafts (air currents), which minimizes heat loss by convection. It can also reduce heat loss to cold windows (radiation), but preventing drafts is the primary convective measure.

20.
Gram negative bacilli include:
a) Escherichia coli, salmonella, shigella.
b) Salmonella, staphylococcus, streptococcus.
c) Shigella, neisseria gonorhoea, streptococci.
d) Clostrichia, esherichia coli, staphylococcus.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Escherichia coli, salmonella, shigella.

Explanation:
- Gram Negative Bacilli (Rods): E. coli, Salmonella, Shigella, Pseudomonas.
- Gram Positive Cocci: Staphylococcus, Streptococcus.
- Gram Negative Cocci: Neisseria.

21.
Management of impetigo involves:
a) Use of systemic antibiotics.
b) Administering systemic corticosteroids.
c) Use of topical antifungal creams.
d) Treatment with opioid analgesics
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Use of systemic antibiotics (for widespread) or Topical Antibiotics.

Explanation: Impetigo is a bacterial infection (Staph/Strep). Treatment is antibiotics (Topical Mupirocin for localized, Systemic for widespread). Corticosteroids are contraindicated as they suppress immunity. Antifungals are for fungal infections.

Lochia Rubra:
a) Contains blood, pieces of membranes, has meconium.
b) Occurs on 5th – 9th day, is paler, contains cervical mucus.
c) Creamish in colour, occurs from 10th – 15th day, contains cervical mucus.
d) Occurs 1st - 4th day, contains blood, has shreds of decidua.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Occurs 1st - 4th day, contains blood, has shreds of decidua.

Explanation:
- Lochia Rubra (Red): Days 1-4. Blood, decidua, vernix.
- Lochia Serosa (Pink/Brown): Days 5-9. Old blood, serum, leukocytes.
- Lochia Alba (White/Yellow): Days 10-28. Mucus, leukocytes.

23.
Transurethral incision of the prostate is indicated in management of:
a) Cancer of the prostate.
b) Initial stages of benign prostatic hypertrophy.
c) Painful benign prostatic hypertrophy.
d) Benign prostatic hypertrophy causing complete urinary obstruction.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Benign prostatic hypertrophy causing complete urinary obstruction (or severe symptoms).

Explanation: Surgical intervention (like TUIP/TURP) is indicated when BPH causes complications like retention, renal failure, recurrent UTIs, or when medical therapy fails. Option (b) initial stages are treated medically.

24.
Reed-Sternberg cell is a pathologic hallmark essential in diagnosis of:
a) Hodgkin's disease.
b) Acute myeloid leukemia.
c) Chronic lymphocytic leukemia.
d) Non Hodgkin's disease.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Hodgkin's disease.

Explanation: Reed-Sternberg cells (giant cells with "owl's eye" appearance) are the definitive histological characteristic of Hodgkin's Lymphoma.

25.
Hormonal family planning methods which can be given to a mother at six weeks post partum include:
a) Implants, progesterone only injectable, progesterone only pills.
b) Progesterone only pills, combined oral contraceptives, implants.
c) Progesterone only pills, condoms, intra-uterine device.
d) Progesterone only injectable, intra-uterine device, combined oral pills
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Implants, progesterone only injectable, progesterone only pills.

Explanation: Breastfeeding mothers should avoid estrogen-containing contraceptives (COCs) as they can suppress lactation. Progestin-only methods (POPs, Depo-Provera, Implants) are safe. IUDs are non-hormonal (mostly) or progestin-only, but Option A lists purely hormonal progestin-only methods suitable for lactation.

27.
When plotting findings on a partograph, obstructed labour would be indicated by:
a) Poor cervical dilatation, foetal distress, clear liquor.
b) Slow descent, mild uterine contractions, early rupture of membranes.
c) Fetal distress, clear liquor, mild uterine contractions.
d) Slow descent, poor cervical dilatation, early rupture of membranes.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Slow descent, poor cervical dilatation, early rupture of membranes (often associated with moulding/caput).

Explanation: Obstructed labour is characterized by the failure of the presenting part to descend despite strong uterine contractions. On a partograph, this presents as the cervical dilatation line crossing the "Alert" line towards the "Action" line (poor dilatation) and arrest of descent. Early rupture of membranes is common.

28.
During pregnancy physiological haemodilution reaches its peak at:
a) 10th – 12th week.
b) 30th – 32nd week.
c) 36th – 40th week.
d) 32nd – 34th week.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) 32nd – 34th week (ranges vary, typically 30-34 weeks).

Explanation: Plasma volume increases by ~50% while red blood cell mass increases by only ~20-30%. This disparity is greatest around 32-34 weeks, resulting in the peak of "physiological anemia of pregnancy" (haemodilution).

29.
The primary reason for administering digoxin (Lanoxin) to a patient is to:
a) Relax the walls of the heart’s arteries.
b) Improve the strength of the heart beat.
c) Prevent irregularities in ventricular contractions.
d) Decrease inflammation of the heart wall.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Improve the strength of the heart beat.

Explanation: Digoxin is a positive inotrope, meaning it increases the force of myocardial contraction (improving stroke volume), and a negative chronotrope (decreases heart rate).

31.
A baby born of a mother on Anti-TB drugs initial phase should:
a) Get BCG immediately.
b) Get Isoniazid 5 mg/Kg twice daily for 3 months.
c) Get Isoniazid 5 mg/Kg once daily for 6 months.
d) Get BCG after three weeks.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Get Isoniazid 5 mg/Kg once daily for 6 months.

Explanation: According to Kenyan and WHO guidelines for TB, an infant born to a mother with active TB (sputum positive) should receive Isoniazid Preventive Therapy (IPT) for 6 months to prevent infection. BCG is usually deferred until after the course of IPT is complete and the baby is confirmed TB negative, though practices vary.

32.
Caput succedaneum is:
a) Collection of blood under the periosteum.
b) A swelling under the scalp (subcutaneous edema).
c) Oedema of the Periosteum.
d) Congenital abnormality.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) A swelling under the scalp.

Explanation:
- Caput Succedaneum: Edema of the scalp (subcutaneous tissue) that crosses suture lines.
- Cephalohematoma: Collection of blood under the periosteum (Option A) that does not cross suture lines.

33.
The priority nursing diagnosis for a patient with heart failure and pulmonary oedema is:
a) Risk for infection related to stasis of alveolar secretions
b) Impaired skin integrity related to pressure
c) Activity intolerance related to pump failure
d) Constipation related to immobility
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Activity intolerance related to pump failure (or Impaired Gas Exchange if listed).

Explanation: Among the options provided, Activity Intolerance is the most direct consequence of heart failure (pump failure) affecting the patient's immediate functional status. If "Impaired Gas Exchange" were an option, it would be the physiological priority for pulmonary edema.

34.
The sensitivity part of a culture & sensitivity test is for the purpose of:
a) Identifying the causative organism for the infection diagnosed
b) Identifying the most effective antibiotics against the causative organism
c) Identifying the antibiotics that the patient is resistant to
d) Testing the concentration of antibiotic required to cure an infection
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Identifying the most effective antibiotics against the causative organism

Explanation:
- Culture: Identifies the organism (Option A).
- Sensitivity: Determines which antibiotics effectively kill or inhibit the organism.

35.

Show Answer

Answer: True. It can lead to blindness within hours if not treated.

b) In spinal injury there is complete anesthesia below the level of injury.

36.
The primary rationales for tube feeding for a client post total laryngectomy due to cancer of larynx is to:
a) Meet dietary needs and prevent fistula formation
b) Maintain an open airway and prevent aspiration
c) Meet fluid & dietary needs and prevent aspiration
d) Prevent fistula formation and reduce aspiration
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Meet dietary needs and prevent fistula formation

Explanation: After total laryngectomy, the pharyngeal suture line needs time to heal. Oral feeding immediately could stress the suture line, leading to a pharyngocutaneous fistula. Tube feeding allows for nutrition while resting the surgical site.

37.
When a blood pressure (BP) reading is abnormal upon initial assessment, it is best to check the BP:
a) On the other arm
b) On both arms sitting and standing
c) With the patient standing
d) After a 5 min wait
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) On the other arm (or d - recheck after rest).

Explanation: Standard practice for an abnormal initial reading is to re-measure to confirm accuracy. Checking the contralateral arm helps rule out vascular abnormalities (like subclavian stenosis) and validates the finding.

38.
The following technique should be avoided when changing a hemiparalysis client’s position in bed:
a) Sliding the client to move her up in bed
b) Rolling the client onto her side
c) Lifting the client when moving her up in bed
d) Having the client lift herself off the bed using a trapeze
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Sliding the client to move her up in bed

Explanation: Sliding causes friction and shearing forces, which can damage the skin, especially in immobile or hemiplegic patients with compromised sensation/circulation. Patients should be lifted (using a draw sheet or assistance) to move them up.

39.
Neurovascular assessment of the lower limb post-cast application involves observation of:
a) Color, sensation and pulses
b) Level of consciousness, limb symmetry and muscular strength
c) Capillary refill, sensation and muscle power
d) Color, warmth and muscle power
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Color, sensation and pulses (also c/d cover aspects)

Explanation: The "5 Ps" of neurovascular assessment are: Pain, Pulse, Pallor (Color), Paresthesia (Sensation), and Paralysis (Movement). Option A covers circulation (Color/Pulse) and sensation.

40.
The risk for macrovascular complications in diabetes mellitus may be reduced by:
a) Lowering BP, avoiding nephrotoxic substances & reducing strenuous exercises.
b) Weight reduction, reducing fat/triglyceride levels, quitting smoking.
c) Foot care, early diagnosis of retinal detachment & daily U/Es checkups.
d) High fibre diet, low fat diet & prompt treatment of systemic infections
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Weight reduction, reducing fat/triglyceride levels, quitting smoking.

Explanation: Macrovascular complications (stroke, heart attack, PVD) are driven by atherosclerosis. Risk factors include obesity, dyslipidemia, and smoking. Managing these reduces macrovascular risk. Microvascular complications (retinopathy, nephropathy) are more tightly linked to glycemic control.

41.
Foods that may predispose a client to megaloblastic anemia if taken exclusively include:
a) Meat, eggs and beans
b) Beans, Peas and rice
c) Capsicum, Sorghum and Millet
d) Meat, Maize, Sorghum
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Capsicum, Sorghum and Millet (or Goat's Milk).

Explanation: Megaloblastic anemia is caused by Vitamin B12 or Folate deficiency.
- Meat/Eggs contain B12.
- Beans/Peas contain Folate.
- A diet consisting exclusively of starches (Maize, Sorghum, Millet) without animal products (B12) or green leafy vegetables/legumes (Folate) leads to deficiency. Goat's milk is notoriously low in folate.

42.
Nursing interventions for a patient with multiple myeloma include:
a) Providing low calorie diet in small frequent amounts
b) Providing plenty of oral fluids
c) Catheterization to monitor urine output
d) Encouraging weight bearing exercises
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Providing plenty of oral fluids (and d is also correct).

Explanation:
- Fluids (Priority): Hypercalcemia and Bence-Jones proteins cause renal failure/stones. 3-4L of fluid daily helps dilute urine and prevent this.
- Ambulation: Weight bearing helps keep calcium in the bones (treating hypercalcemia/osteoporosis).
Both are correct, but hydration is often the immediate nursing priority for renal protection.

43.
In positioning a casted leg, the nurse should:
a) Keep the leg in a flat position always
b) Elevate the leg for 3 hours then put it flat for 1 hour
c) Keep the leg flat for 3 hours then elevate it for 1 hour
d) Elevate the leg on pillow continuously for 24 to 48 hours.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Elevate the leg on pillow continuously for 24 to 48 hours.

Explanation: Elevation above heart level promotes venous return and reduces edema during the initial inflammatory phase (first 24-48 hours) after injury/casting.

44.
Drugs used in management of Meniere’s disease include:
a) Antiemetics, diuretics, glucocorticoids
b) Glucocorticoids, diuretics, antihistamines
c) Antihistamines, diuretics, antiemetics
d) Glucocorticoids, Antihistamines, antiemetics
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Antihistamines, diuretics, antiemetics

Explanation:
- Diuretics: To reduce endolymphatic fluid pressure.

45.
Normal Cerebral spinal fluid (CSF):
a) Contain red blood cells and white blood cells
b) Tests negative for glucose and protein
c) Appears clear and turbid (Source text error: Likely meant "Clear")
d) Appears slightly cloudy and turbid
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Appears clear (assuming 'turbid' is a distractor typo in source)

Explanation: Normal CSF is crystal clear ("gin clear"). It contains:
- No RBCs.
- Very few WBCs (0-5).
- Glucose (approx 2/3 of blood glucose).
- Protein (15-45 mg/dL).
Therefore, it tests positive for glucose/protein.

46.
Insertion of urinary catheter is facilitated by requesting the patient to:
a) Bear down
b) Breathe deeply.
c) Turn to the side.
d) Hold the labia or shaft of penis
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Bear down (in females).

Explanation: Asking a female patient to bear down gently helps relax the external sphincter and can make the urethral meatus more visible (it "winks"). Deep breathing (b) is also used to relax the patient during insertion.

47.
The type of seizures which may only affect a finger or hand is:
a) Petit mal seizure
b) Complex partial seizure
c) Absent seizure
d) Simple partial seizure
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Simple partial seizure (Focal Motor Seizure).

Explanation: Simple partial seizures affect a localized area of the brain and cause localized motor symptoms (like twitching of a finger/hand - Jacksonian March) without loss of consciousness.

48.
Microorganisms that have both bacterial and viral characteristics include:
a) Mycoplasma, spirochetes
b) Rickettsia, chlamydia
c) Plasmodium, mycoplasma
d) Spirochetes, chlamydia
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Rickettsia, chlamydia

Explanation: Rickettsia and Chlamydia are obligate intracellular parasites (like viruses, they need a host cell to replicate) but they have cell walls and contain both DNA and RNA (like bacteria) and are susceptible to antibiotics.

49.
Impetigo contagiosa is:
a) Primarily the result of allergies
b) A fungal infection
c) Best treated with benzyl benzoate
d) Caused by beta hemolytic streptococcus
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Caused by beta hemolytic streptococcus (or Staph aureus).

Explanation: Impetigo is a highly contagious bacterial skin infection caused primarily by Staphylococcus aureus or Group A Beta-hemolytic Streptococcus.

50.
Buck’s traction is:
a) A form of skin traction and is often used to relieve muscle spasms.
b) A form of skeletal traction for compound fractures.
c) Only used in correction of hip and femur fractures.
d) Always contra-indicated in obese patients
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) A form of skin traction and is often used to relieve muscle spasms.

Explanation: Buck's traction is a longitudinal skin traction applied to the lower leg. It is commonly used pre-operatively for hip fractures to reduce muscle spasms and maintain alignment.
