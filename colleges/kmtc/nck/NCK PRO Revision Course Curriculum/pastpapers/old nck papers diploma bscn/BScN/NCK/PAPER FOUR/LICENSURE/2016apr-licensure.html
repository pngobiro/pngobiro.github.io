PART I: MULTIPLE CHOICE QUESTIONS

20 Marks
1.
A client in an in-patient psychiatric unit has been admitted involuntarily. The nurse is about to administer the client's anti-anxiety medication when the client strikes the nurse and threatens to kill her. The most appropriate nursing action is for the nurse to:-
a) Decide not to administer the medication.
b) Initiate the ordered, forced medication protocol.
c) Initiate legal action to get the client declared incompetent.
d) Teach the client the pros and cons of medication compliance.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Initiate the ordered, forced medication protocol.

Explanation: The client is an immediate danger to the nurse (striking and threatening to kill). In an emergency situation where a patient is dangerous to self or others, forced medication (chemical restraint) is clinically and legally justifiable to ensure safety, even for involuntary patients.

2.
Benzodiazepines are used in:-
a) Long-term treatment of post-traumatic stress disorders, convulsive disorder and alcohol withdrawal.
b) Short-term treatment of generalized anxiety disorder, alcohol withdrawal, pre-operative sedation.
c) Short-term treatment of obsessive compulsive disorder, skeletal spasms, essential hypertension.
d) Long-term treatment of panic disorder, alcohol dependency and bipolar affective disorder manic episode.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Short-term treatment of generalized anxiety disorder, alcohol withdrawal, pre-operative sedation.

Explanation: Benzodiazepines are indicated for short-term relief of anxiety, alcohol withdrawal, and pre-op sedation. They are generally avoided for long-term treatment (option a/d) due to risk of dependence.

3.
Post-traumatic stress disorder (PTSD) is influenced by:-
a) Unsatisfactory parent/child relationship.
b) Excess of the neurotransmitter serotonin.
c) Distorted, negative cognitions.
d) Severity of stressor and lack of support systems.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Severity of stressor and lack of support systems.

Explanation: The development of PTSD is directly linked to the severity, duration, and proximity of the traumatic event (stressor), as well as the individual's lack of social support systems post-trauma.

4.
The ultimate goal of therapy for a client suffering from multiple personality disorder and presenting with suicidal ideations after taking an overdose of diazepam tablets is:-
a) Integration of the personalities into one.
b) For the client to have the ability to switch from one personality to another voluntarily.
c) For the client to select which personality he wants to be his dominant self.
d) For the client to recognize that the various personalities exist.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Integration of the personalities into one.

Explanation: The primary long-term therapeutic goal for Dissociative Identity Disorder (formerly Multiple Personality Disorder) is integration (fusion) of the fragmented identities into a cohesive whole.

5.
With an overdose of barbiturates, death would most likely occur from:-
a) Kidney failure.
b) Cardiac arrest.
c) Respiratory depression.
d) Cerebral haemorrhage.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Respiratory depression.

Explanation: Barbiturates are central nervous system depressants. In overdose, they suppress the respiratory center in the brainstem, leading to respiratory arrest and death.

6.
One of the strategies used for creating an appropriate environment when admitting a psychiatric emergency client is:-
a) Afford privacy, but not at the risk of compromising personal safety.
b) Interview the client at close range.
c) Make sure that there is minimal lighting in the admission room to avoid extreme stimulus.
d) Be relaxed and depend on help from colleagues.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Afford privacy, but not at the risk of compromising personal safety.

Explanation: Safety is paramount. While privacy is important for dignity and rapport, it should never compromise the physical safety of the nurse or staff. Doors should remain open or accessible, and staff should be within reach.

7.
For offenders with a history of alcohol misuse and violent offending, therapeutic approaches in prison can include:-
a) An assessment by clinical officer in-charge and detoxification.
b) An assessment by a specialist substance abuse service and detoxification.
c) Detoxification and severe restrictions in alcohol consumption.
d) Detoxification and stiffer jail term to serve as a warning for future offenders.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) An assessment by a specialist substance abuse service and detoxification.

Explanation: Effective management involves specialized assessment and treatment (detoxification and rehabilitation) tailored to substance abuse issues, rather than just medical officer assessment or punitive measures alone.

8.
Teachers who choose to assess the effects of their teaching in order to improve on their practice are referred to as-----------practitioners:-
a) Deducive.
b) Inductive.
c) Intuitive.
d) Reflective.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Reflective.

Explanation: A reflective practitioner is one who engages in self-analysis and evaluation of their teaching methods and outcomes to improve their future professional practice.

9.
A bias that may affect the validity of the standardized test scores for a test that presents multiple choice questions and uses a gridded answer sheet is likely to occur if:-
a) Three students use a geometric pattern to fill out their sheet.
b) A teacher gives the entire class an extra ten minutes to complete their work because three students with learning disabilities need more time.
c) A teacher selects a test that has questions that match the skills and concepts taught in that class.
d) Students taking the test took a different form of the same test the previous year.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) A teacher gives the entire class an extra ten minutes to complete their work because three students with learning disabilities need more time.

Explanation: Standardized tests rely on uniform administration conditions (timing, instructions). Giving the entire class extra time violates standard administration procedures and biases the scores, inflating them relative to the norm group.

10.
Freud's model of personality is described by:-
a) Formal operations.
b) Id, ego, super ego.
c) Consciousness, pre-consciousness, reality.
d) Id, ego, identity.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Id, ego, super ego.

Explanation: Sigmund Freud's structural model of personality consists of three parts: the Id (instincts), the Ego (reality), and the Superego (morality).

11.
T and S have been dating for two years. S breaks up with T because he cheated on her with B. S tells her friend K about T, but she tells her without showing any emotion. S is using-----------as defense mechanism:-
a) Repression.
b) Denial.
c) Projection.
d) Isolation.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Isolation.

Explanation: Isolation of affect (or simply Isolation) is a defense mechanism where the individual separates the memory of a traumatic event from its associated emotion, allowing them to discuss it calmly without feeling the pain.

Identify at least five (5) requirements for community psychiatric service for long term care. (5 marks)
Show Answer
  1. Accessible Facilities: Services must be physically close to where people live.
  2. Multidisciplinary Team: Presence of psychiatrists, nurses, social workers, and occupational therapists.
  3. Emergency/Crisis Services: Availability of 24-hour response for acute episodes.

Substance abuse/dependency is a problem that has the potential for impairment in an individual's social occupation, psychologic or physiologic functioning. With reference to nurses, identify five (5) clues that can help identify substance impairment in nurses. (5 marks)
Show Answer
  1. Attendance Issues: Frequent absenteeism, lateness, or disappearing from the unit (long breaks).
  2. Changes in Behavior/Mood: Irritability, mood swings, lethargy, or social isolation from colleagues.
  3. Documentation Discrepancies: Errors in narcotic counts, excessive wastage of drugs, or vague entries.

4.
Question on Pain

i) State the two broad categories or types of pain. (2 marks)

  • Acute Pain
  • Chronic Pain

ii) Under each of the above identify two sub-types. (4 marks)

  • Acute Pain:
    • Somatic pain (skin, muscle, bone).
    • Visceral pain (internal organs).
  • Chronic Pain:
    • Chronic malignant pain (cancer pain).
    • Chronic non-malignant pain (e.g., arthritis, lower back pain).
    • Neuropathic pain (phantom limb pain).

iii) What is the relevance of distinguishing between the types of the pain? (2 marks)

Distinguishing types allows for appropriate management. Acute pain often serves as a warning sign and resolves with healing (requires analgesics/treatment of cause), whereas chronic pain serves no protective purpose and requires a multidisciplinary approach focusing on function and quality of life rather than just "cure." Neuropathic pain responds differently to medications (e.g., antidepressants) compared to nociceptive pain.

Show Answer

  • Retains full power and authority.
  • Communication is downward (one-way).
  • Expects strict compliance and obedience.

PART III: ESSAY/LONG ANSWER QUESTIONS

40 Marks
1.
A female patient aged thirty is being admitted in medical ward with a chief complaint of nausea. She lies down on examination couch. When the nurse removes the patients blouse, she notices that the patient has bruises on her arms and pelvis. Some are bluish in color and others are various shades of green and yellow. The nurse also notices small scars on the body. The nurse strongly suspects that the patient is being abused.The nurse tries to talk to the patient about her bruises and scars but she refuses to say how she received them.

a) Identify five (5) indications of physical abuse. (5 marks)

  1. Bruises in various stages of healing: The presence of colors ranging from bluish (fresh) to green and yellow (older) indicates injuries occurring at different times, a hallmark of ongoing abuse.
  2. Defensive Injuries: Bruises on the arms often indicate the victim raising hands to protect themselves.
  3. Central Body Injuries: Injuries to the pelvis, trunk, or head are less likely to be accidental than injuries to extremities like shins.
  4. Inconsistency between history and injury: The patient's refusal to explain or offering a vague explanation ("I fell") that doesn't match the severity of injuries.
  5. Old Scars: Evidence of past trauma suggests a history of chronic abuse.
  6. Behavioral Signs: Fearfulness, poor eye contact, anxiety, or passivity during the examination.

b) Nursing Management

i) State three (3) nursing diagnoses relevant to the presented case. (3 marks)

  • Risk for Trauma/Injury related to abusive relationship/physical violence.
  • Fear related to threat of physical harm and powerlessness.
  • Ineffective Coping related to cycle of violence and personal vulnerability.
  • (Alternative: Acute Pain related to physical injuries).

ii) State two (2) objectives to guide the management of the patient. (2 marks)

  • The patient will experience immediate physical safety and treatment of injuries.
  • The patient will identify at least one support system or safe shelter to go to upon discharge.

iii) Describe the nursing intervention of a patient being physically abused. (5 marks)

  1. Ensure Privacy and Safety: Interview the patient alone, away from any accompanying persons (who might be the abuser). Ensure the environment is safe.
  2. Therapeutic Communication: Establish rapport. Use direct but non-threatening questions (e.g., "Do you feel safe at home?"). Validate her feelings and state clearly, "No one deserves to be hit."
  3. Physical Assessment and Treatment: Treat the physical injuries (nausea, bruises).
  4. Documentation: Meticulously document the injuries using a body map (size, color, location) and use the patient's own words. This is crucial for legal evidence.
  5. Safety Planning: Assist the patient in developing an escape plan (packing a bag, identifying emergency contacts/shelters). Provide referrals to social workers or legal aid.

c) Describe the evaluation of the nursing actions to assist the victim of violence on a short term basis. (5 marks)

Evaluation involves determining if the immediate goals were met:

  • Safety: Is the patient physically safe right now? Has she agreed to go to a safe place (shelter/relative) rather than returning to the abusive environment?
  • Disclosure: Was the patient able to disclose the abuse and discuss her feelings?
  • Plan: Can the patient verbalize a specific safety plan (e.g., "I have the hotline number" or "I am going to my sister's")?
  • Resources: Did the patient accept referrals to counseling, legal, or social services?
  • Physical Status: Have the physical injuries been treated and pain managed?
2.
Management Theory Question

i) Explain McGregor's theory X and theory Y. (10 marks)

Douglas McGregor proposed two distinct views of human beings affecting management style:

  • Theory X (Authoritarian Management):
    • Assumes the average employee implies an inherent dislike for work and will avoid it if possible.
    • People must be coerced, controlled, directed, or threatened with punishment to get them to put forth adequate effort.
    • The average human prefers to be directed, wishes to avoid responsibility, has relatively little ambition, and wants security above all.
    • Management Style: Strict supervision, centralized control, autocratic leadership.
  • Theory Y (Participative Management):
    • Assumes that physical and mental effort in work is as natural as play or rest.
    • External control and the threat of punishment are not the only means for bringing about effort toward organizational objectives. People will exercise self-direction and self-control if they are committed to the objectives.
    • Commitment to objectives is a function of the rewards associated with their achievement (e.g., self-actualization).
    • The average human being learns, under proper conditions, not only to accept but to seek responsibility.
    • The capacity to exercise a high degree of imagination, ingenuity, and creativity in solving organizational problems is widely distributed in the population.
    • Management Style: Delegation, participation, praise, and constructive feedback.

ii) Examine its relevance to participative management focusing on nursing profession in Kenya. (10 marks)

Theory Y is the foundation of Participative Management, which is highly relevant to the nursing profession in Kenya for the following reasons:

  1. Professional Autonomy: Nurses in Kenya are trained professionals (Diploma/Degree level). Theory Y recognizes their intellectual capacity and need for autonomy, moving away from the colonial "handmaiden" (Theory X) approach.
  2. Improved Motivation and Retention: Nursing shortages are common in Kenya. Participative management (involving nurses in decision-making, scheduling, and policy formation) increases job satisfaction and reduces staff turnover.
  3. Quality of Care: Frontline nurses understand patient needs best. When managers utilize Theory Y (encouraging creativity and problem-solving), nurses can innovate better ways to deliver care with limited resources.
  4. Teamwork: Modern healthcare requires multidisciplinary teams. Theory Y fosters a collaborative environment where communication is open, unlike the rigid hierarchy of Theory X.
  5. Shared Governance: Many Kenyan hospitals are moving towards shared governance models. This relies on the Theory Y assumption that nurses want to take responsibility for the standard of care in their units.
  6. Ownership of Goals: When nurses participate in setting unit goals (e.g., reducing infection rates), they are more committed to achieving them (internal motivation) than when targets are simply dictated from above.
