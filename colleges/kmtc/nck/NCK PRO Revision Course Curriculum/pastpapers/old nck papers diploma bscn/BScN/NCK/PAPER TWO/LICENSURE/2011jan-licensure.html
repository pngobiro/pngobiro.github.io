PAPER TWO

EXAMINATION FOR ADMISSION TO THE REGISTER OF BACHELOR OF SCIENCE IN NURSING (BScN)

Part I: Multiple Choice Questions

20 Marks
1.
Fetal causes of early pregnancy loss include:-
a) Structural abnormalities of the genital tract
b) Bicornulate uterus and fibroids
c) Chromosomal abnormalities of the conceptus
d) Infections such as rubella, listeria and Chlamydia
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Chromosomal abnormalities of the conceptus

Explanation: Chromosomal abnormalities (such as autosomal trisomies) are responsible for 50-70% of first-trimester spontaneous abortions. Options A and B are maternal anatomical causes. Option D represents infectious causes.

2.
The outcome of labor is not dependent on:-
a) Effectiveness of uterine contractions
b) Maternal size
c) The “give” of the pelvic joints
d) The degree of moulding of the fetal head
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Maternal size

Explanation: The outcome of labor is determined by the "3 Ps": Powers (uterine contractions - A), Passage (pelvis/joint give - C), and Passenger (fetal head moulding - D). While extreme short stature can be a risk factor, general maternal size is not a direct determinant compared to the other physiological factors.

3.
In monitoring fetal well being using the fetal kick chart, you will tell the client to notify you if she counts:-
a) More than 10 movements in 3 hours
b) Less than 10 movements in 3 hours
c) More than 10 movements per hour
d) Less than 10 movements per hour
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Less than 10 movements in 3 hours

Explanation: A common guideline (Cardiff count) is to feel at least 10 movements within a set period (often 12 hours, or simpler protocols say 2 hours). Feeling less than 10 movements in a prolonged period (like 3 hours) is a sign of reduced fetal activity and warrants further assessment (NST).

4.
During discharge, an important advice for the breastfeeding mother is to:-
a) Routinely avoid onions, cabbage and chocolate
b) Keep her calcium intake the same as during pregnancy
c) Increase her calories over her pregnancy requirements
d) Return to her pre-pregnancy intake for proteins but not calories
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Increase her calories over her pregnancy requirements

Explanation: Breastfeeding requires approximately 500 kcal/day above pre-pregnancy needs, which is higher than the additional ~300 kcal/day required during pregnancy. Nutritional needs (protein, calcium) generally remain high.

5.
The part of the uterus in which a contraction begins is:-
a) Cornua
b) Fundus
c) Isthmus
d) The body or corpus
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Cornua

Explanation: The physiological pacemaker of the uterus is located at the cornua (near the tubal ostia). Contractions originate here and sweep downwards over the fundus and body.

6.
The follicle stimulating hormone and luteinizing hormone are secreted by the:-
a) Ovaries and testes
b) Posterior pituitary gland
c) Anterior pituitary gland
d) Hypothalamus
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Anterior pituitary gland

Explanation: FSH and LH (gonadotropins) are produced by the gonadotroph cells of the Anterior Pituitary.

7.
Uterine contractions are controlled by the:-
a) Central nervous system
b) Sympathetic nervous system
c) Peripheral nervous system
d) Autonomic nervous system
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Autonomic nervous system

Explanation: The uterus receives innervation from the Autonomic Nervous System (Sympathetic T11-L2 and Parasympathetic S2-S4). While hormonal control (Oxytocin/Prostaglandins) is the primary driver, the ANS modulates contractions (e.g., fear/adrenaline can inhibit labor).

8.
The volume of amniotic fluid after 20 weeks gestation is:-
a) 500 to 1000 ml
b) 350 to 500 ml
c) 200 to 500 ml
d) 1000 to 1500 ml
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) 500 to 1000 ml

Explanation: Amniotic fluid volume increases steadily, reaching a peak of approximately 800-1000 ml at 34-36 weeks. A range of 500-1000ml is considered normal in the second half of pregnancy.

9.
The ectoderm differentiates to form the:-
a) Cardiovascular system
b) Peripheral nervous system
c) Respiratory tract
d) Connective tissues
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Peripheral nervous system

Explanation: The Ectoderm forms the nervous system (CNS and PNS) and the skin/epidermis. The Mesoderm forms cardiovascular, connective tissue, and muscles. The Endoderm forms the respiratory and digestive tracts.

10.
The hormone that maintains endometrium and stimulates maternal metabolism and development of breast alveoli during pregnancy is:-
a) Human chorionic gonadotrophin
b) Human placental lactogen
c) Oestrogen
d) Progesterone
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Progesterone

Explanation: Progesterone maintains the decidua (endometrium), relaxes smooth muscle (preventing labor), increases maternal metabolism (along with HPL), and stimulates alveolar growth in the breasts.

11.
The anteroposterior diameter of the pelvic outlet measures:-
a) 12 cm
b) 13 cm
c) 11 cm
d) 10 cm
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) 13 cm

Explanation: The anatomical AP diameter of the outlet is ~11.5 cm. During labor, the coccyx tilts backwards at the sacrococcygeal joint, increasing this diameter to 13 cm, making it the largest diameter at the outlet to facilitate head extension.

12.
During pregnancy, the glomerular filtration rate:-
a) Only changes when pathological conditions are present
b) Is directly influenced by the woman’s posture
c) Increases significantly above pre-pregnant levels
d) Makes the most dramatic changes near birth
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Increases significantly above pre-pregnant levels

Explanation: GFR increases by about 50% early in pregnancy and remains elevated, causing lower serum creatinine and urea levels.

13.
Signs of cardiac decompensation in a pregnant woman with cardiac disease are likely to appear at:-
a) 28-32 weeks gestation
b) 36-40 weeks gestation
c) 12-16 weeks gestation
d) 20-24 weeks gestation
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) 28-32 weeks gestation

Explanation: This gestation corresponds to the peak increase in cardiac output and blood volume, placing maximum stress on the heart.

14.
The mechanism of labor that allows the fetal head to present itself to fit the widest anteroposterior diameter of the pelvic cavity is:-
a) Flexion
b) Internal rotation
c) Descent
d) Extension
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Internal rotation

Explanation: The pelvic inlet is widest transversely, but the outlet is widest anteroposteriorly. The fetus must perform internal rotation of the head to align its long diameter (AP) with the outlet's long diameter (AP).

15.
Closure of the newborns foramen ovale occurs when:-
a) Blood flows from the pulmonary artery to the aorta
b) PO2 is increased causing constriction to occur
c) The umbilical cord is severed
d) Left atrial pressure exceeds right atrial pressure
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Left atrial pressure exceeds right atrial pressure

Explanation: At birth, pulmonary vascular resistance falls (lungs open), increasing return to the left atrium. Systemic resistance rises (cord clamp). This causes Left Atrial Pressure > Right Atrial Pressure, functionally closing the valve of the foramen ovale.

16.
The newborn with post maturity syndrome is at high risk for cold stress due to:-
a) Absence of vernix
b) Decreased subcutaneous fat
c) Parchment like skin
d) Extended posture
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Decreased subcutaneous fat

Explanation: Post-term infants often have used up their energy stores in utero due to aging placenta, resulting in decreased subcutaneous fat, which reduces insulation and predisposes them to hypothermia (cold stress).

17.
Presence of Meconium in the newborn’s lungs:-
a) Leads to respiratory alkalosis
b) Prevents air leaks
c) Traps inspired air in the alveoli
d) Prevents air from entering the airway
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) Traps inspired air in the alveoli

Explanation: Meconium aspiration causes a "ball-valve" obstruction where air can enter during inspiration (airways widen) but is trapped during expiration (airways narrow), leading to hyperinflation and air trapping.

18.
Symptoms of superficial thrombophlebitis include:-
a) Local redness and tenderness in a portion of the vein
b) Edema of the ankle and lower leg
c) Pain in the affected leg and foot
d) Positive Homan’s sign
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Local redness and tenderness in a portion of the vein

Explanation: Superficial thrombophlebitis typically presents with a palpable, cord-like vein that is red, warm, and tender. General leg edema and Homan's sign are more indicative of DVT.

19.
True or False Statements:
a) In the management of shoulder dystocia, Wood’s Maneuver is executed by exerting pressure on the anterior fetal shoulder to achieve rotation - FALSE
b) Zavanelli Maneuver is an obstetric maneuver that involves pushing back the delivered fetal head into the birth canal in anticipation of performing a cesarean section in case of shoulder dystocia - TRUE
Show Answer

a) False: Wood's Corkscrew maneuver involves pressure on the posterior aspect of the anterior shoulder (or anterior of posterior) to rotate the fetus. Suprapubic pressure (Rubin I) is applied anteriorly.

b) True: The Zavanelli maneuver is a desperate, last-resort measure involving cephalic replacement followed by immediate C-section.

20.
Direct causes of placenta abruption include:-
a) Abnormally short umbilical cord, sudden loss of uterine volume
b) Defective vascularization of the deciduas, previous uterine surgery
c) Injury to the abdomen from fall or accident, multiple pregnancy
d) Advanced maternal age, injury to the abdomen from fall or accident
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Abnormally short umbilical cord, sudden loss of uterine volume

Explanation: Direct mechanical causes include sudden uterine decompression (e.g., rapid polyhydramnios drainage or delivery of first twin), short umbilical cord (traction pulls placenta off), and trauma. Option (a) lists two direct mechanical causes. Option (c) lists trauma (direct) and multiple pregnancy (risk factor). Option (a) is the strongest pair of "direct" causes.

Part II: Short Answer Questions

40 Marks
1.
State the five leading causes of maternal mortality according to WHO indicating their percentages. (5 marks)
Show Answer
  1. Haemorrhage (mostly PPH): ~25-27%
  2. Hypertensive Disorders (Eclampsia): ~14%
  3. Sepsis/Infections: ~11%
  4. Unsafe Abortion: ~8-10%
  5. Obstructed Labour: ~9%
  6. (Embolism/Other direct causes: ~3-5%)
2.
Describe the process of fertilization. (4 marks)
Show Answer
  1. Capacitation: Physiological conditioning of sperm in the female tract to enable fertilization.
  2. Acrosome Reaction: Release of enzymes (hyaluronidase/acrosin) to penetrate the corona radiata and zona pellucida.
  3. Penetration: Sperm enters the perivitelline space and fuses with the oocyte plasma membrane.
  4. Cortical Reaction: Oocyte releases granules to harden zona pellucida, blocking polyspermy.
  5. Meiosis II Completion: Secondary oocyte finishes division to become mature ovum.
  6. Amphimixis: Fusion of male and female pronuclei to form a Zygote.
3.
Outline five factors that reduce accuracy of fundal height measurement in estimating duration of pregnancy. (5 marks)
Show Answer
  1. Maternal Obesity: Difficulty in palpating the fundus.
  2. Polyhydramnios: Increases fundal height excessively.
  3. Multiple Gestation (Twins): Increases fundal height.
  4. Uterine Fibroids: Adds to uterine bulk/height.
  5. Full Bladder: Displaces uterus upwards.
  6. Transverse/Oblique Lie: Reduces fundal height.
  7. IUGR: Reduces fundal height.
4.
State two conditions that must be met in order to establish a diagnosis of active phase labor. (2 marks)
Show Answer
  1. Cervical Dilatation: At least 4 cm (or 5-6cm per newer guidelines, but 4cm was standard).
  2. Uterine Contractions: Regular, painful contractions of adequate strength (e.g., 3 in 10 minutes).
5.
Identify five factors that can cause deviations in labor. (5 marks)
Show Answer

Based on the "5 Ps":

  1. Powers: Inefficient contractions (Hypotonic/Hypertonic) or inadequate maternal effort.
  2. Passage: Cephalopelvic Disproportion (CPD), Contracted pelvis.
  3. Passenger: Malposition (OP), Malpresentation (Breech), Macrosomia.
  4. Psyche: Extreme maternal anxiety, fear, or exhaustion.
  5. Position: Maternal position affecting descent (e.g., prolonged supine).
6.
a) List four causes of post-partum haemorrhage. (2 marks)
b) Explain the management of post-partum haemorrhage focusing on the above four causes. (6 marks)
Show Answer

a) Causes (The 4 Ts):

  1. Tone: Uterine Atony (70% of cases).
  2. Trauma: Lacerations of genital tract, Uterine Rupture.
  3. Tissue: Retained Placenta or clots.
  4. Thrombin: Coagulation disorders (DIC).

b) Management:

  • Atony: "Rub up" contraction (Fundal massage). Administer Oxytocics (Oxytocin, Ergometrine, Misoprostol). Empty bladder. Bimanual compression.
  • Trauma: Inspect cervix and vagina with good lighting. Suture tears immediately. Surgical repair for rupture.
  • Tissue: Manual Removal of Placenta (MROP) if retained. Exploration of uterus to remove fragments/clots under anaesthesia.
  • Thrombin: Replace blood products (FFP, Platelets, Cryoprecipitate). Manage shock and underlying cause.
