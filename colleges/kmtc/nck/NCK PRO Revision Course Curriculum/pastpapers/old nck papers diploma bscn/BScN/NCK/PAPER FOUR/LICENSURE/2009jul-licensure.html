PART I: MULTIPLE CHOICE QUESTIONS

20 Marks
1.
The most appropriate sequence in problem solving would be:
a) Identify the problem, identify possible solutions, select the best solution, take action to solve the problem, evaluate the implementation.
b) Identify the possible solution, select the best solution, identify the problem , take action to solve the problem , evaluate the implementation.
c) Identify the problem, take action to solve the problem , identify the possible solution , select the best solution, evaluate the implementation.
d) Take action to solve the problem, evaluate the implementation, select the best solution, identify the problem, identify possible solution.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Identify the problem, identify possible solutions, select the best solution, take action to solve the problem, evaluate the implementation.

Explanation: This follows the logical steps of the decision-making process: Definition (Assessment), Analysis (Alternatives), Selection (Planning), Implementation, and Evaluation.

2.
A manager who is involved in structuring activities into operational groups engages in a role of:-
a) Job designing.
b) Staffing.
c) Scheduling.
d) Organizing.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: d) Organizing.

Explanation: Organizing involves grouping activities and resources (structuring) to facilitate the accomplishment of objectives. It includes creating departments, teams, or operational groups.

3.
Lateral thinking in a problem solving process involves:-
a) Creativity on the use of a series of dimensions examined in relation to each other including the possible elements in each dimension.
b) A logical deduction in which an indisputable solution in arrived at by analyzing the existing factual information.
c) The use of various techniques associated with challenging the preconceptions and rejecting “yes/No” thinking.
d) Suspension or judgement on other peoples “ ideas to allow everyone to talk freely leading to generation of more and new ideas regardless of whether they make sense or not.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: c) The use of various techniques associated with challenging the preconceptions and rejecting “yes/No” thinking.

Explanation: Lateral thinking (coined by Edward de Bono) involves solving problems through an indirect and creative approach, using reasoning that is not immediately obvious. It is about changing concepts and perception, often challenging assumptions (preconceptions) rather than standard vertical logic (option b). Option (d) describes brainstorming.

4.
A budgetary process where information is gathered for re- justification of all expenditure is-
a) Performance budget.
b) Zero –based budget.
c) Operating budget.
d) Supplementary budget.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Zero –based budget.

Explanation: In zero-based budgeting (ZBB), the budget starts from "zero" each period. Managers must justify every expense and activity from scratch, rather than just adjusting the previous year's budget.

5.
When a nurse manager’s power comes into conflict with other powers the conflict is referred to as:-
a) Power conflict.
b) Power crisis.
c) Power engagement.
d) Confrontational power.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Power conflict.

Explanation: This refers to a struggle between two or more parties where the source of conflict is the distribution or exercise of power and authority.

6.
According to Carl Roger’s theory of student centered learning:-
a) Learning occurs when the learner perceives relevance related to his/her purposes.
b) Students must be supervised to learn.
c) People learn for self – enhancement rather than simply for utility.
d) There are categorized domains of learning.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: a) Learning occurs when the learner perceives relevance related to his/her purposes.

Explanation: Rogers emphasized "significant learning," which happens when the subject matter is perceived by the student as having relevance for their own purposes and maintenance or enhancement of the self.

7.
A core factor which would influence the curriculum developemt process is:-
a) Natural calamities.
b) Political events.
c) Student unrests.
d) Teacher characteristics.
Show Answer

Correct Answer: b) Political events.

Explanation: Political events and government policies are fundamental external factors that dictate national health priorities, funding, and legal requirements for nursing education, thus heavily influencing curriculum development.
