02

ğŸ“ Rules of Bed Making

  • Never flip or shake linen (prevents spread of microorganisms).
  • Never cover a patient's face with linen.
  • Always miter all corners for a neat and secure fit.
  • Ensure the bed is well-locked before starting.
  • Bottom sheet: Right side faces up.
  • Top sheet: Bottom side faces down (right side towards patient).
  • Linen should never touch the ground.
03

ğŸ”„ Making an Occupied Bed

Definition: The procedure used to prepare or change linen on the patient's bed when the patient is still in it.

Purpose: To promote patient comfort and minimize wrinkles and skin irritation while conserving patient energy.

Requirements:

  • 2 large sheets
  • Draw sheet & Mackintosh
  • Blanket & Bed cover
  • Pillow cases
  • Portable linen hamper
  • 2 stools or a chair for stripped linen
04

âš™ï¸ Implementation Steps

Steps Rationale
1. Wash hands and don clean gloves. Minimizes transfer of microorganisms.
2. Loosen linen from head to foot of the bed. Promotes efficiency.
3. Raise side rail nearest to the patient. Promotes patient safety.
4. Assist patient to turn on their side facing away from the clean side. Allows placement of clean linen and ensures comfort.
5. Fan-fold soiled linen towards the center of the bed. Leaves half the bed free to be changed.
6. Place clean bottom sheet and draw sheet; vertically fan-fold half. Facilitates changing without contamination.
7. Tuck and secure the sheets under the mattress. Ensures the sheet is smooth and free from wrinkles.

Check the patient for comfort and safety. Ensure the bed is neat and completely free of wrinkles to prevent skin breakdown.
