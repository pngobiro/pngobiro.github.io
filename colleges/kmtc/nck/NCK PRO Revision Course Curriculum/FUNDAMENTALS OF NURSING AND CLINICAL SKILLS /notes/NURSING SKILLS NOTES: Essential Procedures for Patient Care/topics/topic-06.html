Mouth Care

üìö Personal Hygiene
‚è±Ô∏è 15 min read
01

üîç Definition & Purpose

Definition: The practice of maintaining oral hygiene by cleaning the teeth, gums, tongue, and mucus membranes of the mouth.

Purpose:

  • To prevent oral infections (e.g., stomatitis, thrush).
  • To maintain intact mucous membranes.
  • To stimulate appetite and provide comfort.
  • To prevent halitosis (bad breath).
02

üì¶ Requirements

  • Cleaning Agents

    Toothbrush (soft bristles), Toothpaste, Mouthwash or Normal Saline.

  • Tools

    Kidney dish (receiver), Cup of water, padded tongue depressor (for unconscious patients), suction catheter (optional).

  • Protection

    Towel, Gloves, Lip balm/Vaseline.

03

üìê Brushing Technique

For effective cleaning, the toothbrush should be angled towards the gum line.

‚öôÔ∏è Implementation

For Conscious Patients

Step Rationale
1. Sit patient upright (High Fowler's). Prevents choking and aspiration.
2. Place towel under chin. Protects clothing.
3. Brush all surfaces of teeth and tongue. Removes plaque and bacteria.
4. Assist patient to rinse and spit into kidney dish. Clears debris.

For Unconscious Patients

‚ö†Ô∏è Aspiration Risk

Always position unconscious patients in a lateral (side-lying) position with the head turned to the side to allow fluids to drain out of the mouth, preventing pneumonia.

  • Position patient on side.
  • Place kidney dish under the chin.
  • Use a padded tongue depressor to keep mouth open gently.
  • Clean with swabs or soft brush, using minimal fluid.
  • Apply suction if necessary to remove pooling secretions.
  • Apply lip balm to prevent cracking.
