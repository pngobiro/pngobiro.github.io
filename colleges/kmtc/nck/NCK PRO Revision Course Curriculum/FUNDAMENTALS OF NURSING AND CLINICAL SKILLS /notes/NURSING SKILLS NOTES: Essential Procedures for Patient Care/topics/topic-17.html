Removing Urinary Catheter

üîç Definition & Purpose

Definition: The safe withdrawal of a urinary catheter from the bladder once its therapeutic purpose has been achieved.

Purpose: To remove the catheter safely and transition the patient back to normal bladder functioning.

02

üì¢ Indications & Assessment

Indications: Achievement of normal bladder function, or the need to change the catheter.

Assessment Steps:

  • Verify the physician's order to avoid premature removal.
  • Gather and confirm the availability of all required equipment.
  • Assess patient comfort and the appropriateness of the environment.
03

‚öôÔ∏è Removal Procedure

Steps Rationale
1. Wash hands and wear sterile gloves. Reduces transfer of microorganisms.
2. Remove adhesive tape and withdraw all water from the balloon using a syringe. Ensures the catheter can be removed without injuring the urethra.
3. Gently withdraw the catheter and place it in a receiver. Prevents urethral injury.
4. Inspect the catheter for completeness. Ensures no parts remain inside the patient.
5. Clean and dry the perineal area. Promotes hygiene and comfort.
04

üíß Post-Removal Instructions

  • Instruct the patient to inform staff when they first pass urine.
  • Encourage increased fluid intake to help flush the urinary system.
  • Monitor for signs of urinary retention or discomfort.
  • Measure and record fluid intake and output for 24 hours.
