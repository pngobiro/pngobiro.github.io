üîç Definition & Purpose

Definition: The treatment of areas considered at risk of developing ulcers (pressure sores) resulting from prolonged contact pressure with the bed.

Primary Purposes:

  • To maintain blood circulation.
  • To relieve pressure on bony prominences.
  • To prevent skin breakage and ulcer formation.
02

üì¢ Indications

Pressure area care is essential for all bed-ridden patients, particularly those who are:

  • Incontinent
  • Paralyzed
  • Obese or Emaciated
  • Unconscious
  • Unable to turn independently
  • Suffering from impaired peripheral circulation
03

üì¶ Requirements

A clean trolley with:

  • Top Shelf: Warm and cold water (37¬∞C), washing basin, gallipot, soap, spatula, Zinc oxide or Vaseline, cotton balls, gloves, flannels, and a turning chart.
  • Bottom Shelf: Clean linen, bath towels, clean gown, draw sheet, and bed sheets.
  • Additional: Air freshener, bucket for dirty water, and receivers for dirty linen/swabs.
04

‚öôÔ∏è Implementation Steps

Steps Rationale
1. Wheel trolley to bedside and explain procedure. Promotes efficiency and cooperation.
2. Mix water to 37¬∞C. Prevents injury and enhances comfort.
3. Soap hands and massage pressure points before turning. Avoids friction and stimulates circulation.
4. Rinse off soap using flannels and pat dry. Prevents chapping and maintains skin integrity.
5. Apply water repellent cream (Zinc oxide/Vaseline). Acts as a barrier against moisture.
6. Repeat for all pressure points and turn the client. Ensures comprehensive care and relief from pressure.
05

üìù Documentation

Proper record-keeping must include:

  • Patient's risk score and skin condition observations.
  • Interventions taken and turning intervals.
  • Description of position and pressure relieving devices used.
  • Any need for additional consultation for high-risk clients.
