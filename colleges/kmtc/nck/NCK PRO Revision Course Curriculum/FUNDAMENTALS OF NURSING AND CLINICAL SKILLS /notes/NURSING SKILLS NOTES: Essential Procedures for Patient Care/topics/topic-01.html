üîç Definition & Purpose

Hand washing is a basic technique by which vigorous rubbing together of all surfaces of hands lathered in soap is followed by rinsing under a stream of water. It involves removal of soil, dirt and pathogens by means of friction with soap or detergent and running water.

üéØ Primary Purpose

To remove or reduce dirt, resident and transient microorganisms from hands and prevent transfer of microorganisms from the environment, thereby preventing and controlling cross infection.

02

üì¢ Indications

Hand washing should be performed:

  • Routinely before and after performing any procedure.
  • After contact with a source of microorganisms including blood, body fluids, mucous membrane and contaminated inanimate objects.
  • After removing gloves.
  • Before leaving the ward or health facility.
  • When hands are visibly soiled.
  • After using the washroom.
  • Before eating.
  • After encountering patients with diarrhea.

üì¶ Requirements

05

‚ú® 9 Handwashing Steps

  • Wet Your Hands

    Remove rings or bracelets before you begin because they trap germs.

  • Apply Soap

    Take hands out of the water to add a quarter-sized portion of soap.

  • Rub Palms Together

    Create a rich lather for 8 seconds.

  • Rub Backs of Hands

    Use your right palm to massage the back of your left hand. Switch hands after 2 seconds.

  • Interlace Fingers

    Rub palms together. Interlace fingers and clean the underside of your fingers by rubbing them along your knuckles.

  • Scrub Thumbs

    Clasp right hand over the left thumb and rotate for 3 seconds. Repeat on the right thumb.

  • Rub Palms With Fingertips

    Rotate your right hand fingers clockwise and then counterclockwise on the left palm. Switch hands after 2 seconds.

  • Rinse

    Wash soap away until the water runs clear and you no longer see suds. Turn the faucet off with an elbow or paper towel.

  • Dry

    Use a paper towel or hand dryer to dry your hands ‚Äî not your scrubs!
