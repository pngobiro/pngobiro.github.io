üîç Definition & Purpose

Bladder Irrigation/Washout: The process of instilling a sterile solution into the urinary bladder to provide cleansing or administer medication.

Primary Purposes:

  • To prevent or relieve blockage of the urinary catheter (e.g., by blood clots or debris).
  • To instill medication for local treatment of the bladder.
02

üì¢ Indications

  • Patients with long-term indwelling catheters.
  • Post-bladder surgery or prostatectomy.
  • Bladder infection or inflammation.
  • Suspected blockage of the urinary catheter.
03

üìã Clinical Assessment

  • Order Verification: Confirm type and amount of solution to be used.
  • Allergy Check: Verify if the patient is allergic to antiseptic or irrigating fluids.
  • Distension Check: Palpate the abdomen for bladder distension to avoid over-filling.
04

Method Technique
Continuous Irrigation Slowly open the roller clamp and adjust the drip rate to provide a constant flush of debris/clots.
Intermittent Irrigation Clamp and release the catheter to allow for controlled exchange of fluids and uptake of medication.
üí° Aseptic Note

Always cleanse the irrigation port of the catheter with the recommended antiseptic solution before connecting tubing to decrease contamination risks.

05

üìù Monitoring & Record Keeping

Evaluation: Note patient comfort, the color of the drained urine (clear vs. clots), and reassess for bladder distension.

Documentation: Record the date, time, method, amount of solution, appearance of drained fluid, infusion rate, and total urine output.
