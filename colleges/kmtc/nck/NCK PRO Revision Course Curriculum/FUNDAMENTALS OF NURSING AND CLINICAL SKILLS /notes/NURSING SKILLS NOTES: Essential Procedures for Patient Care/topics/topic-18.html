Oxygen Administration

01

üîç Definition & Purpose

Definition: Giving of oxygen through the airway to correct hypoxia in the body and prevent tissue damage.

Purpose: To maintain or achieve arterial oxygen saturation at or above 90%.

02

üì¢ Indications

  • Airway obstruction
  • Severe infections (e.g., septicemia, lung disease)
  • Heart failure (inefficient cardiac suction)
  • Shock causing stagnation of blood flow
  • Compromised respiratory system
  • Hypoxia and hypoxemia
  • During labor
03

‚öôÔ∏è Implementation Procedure

Steps Rationale
1. Position the oxygen delivery device and secure it. Ensures patient comfort and delivery efficiency.
2. Open the cylinder and start flow; close flow meter when not in use. Prevents wastage and prepares delivery.
3. Regulate flow rate as prescribed. Delivers the correct therapeutic amount.
4. Connect patient to a pulse oximeter. To continuously monitor oxygen saturation (SpO2).
5. Encourage normal breathing. Ensures efficiency and reduces patient anxiety.
04

‚ö†Ô∏è Critical Safety Measures

  • No Smoking: Remove all fire hazards and post "No Smoking" signs.
  • Cylinder Check: Always have a spare cylinder ready; label empty cylinders.
  • Color Coding: Oxygen cylinders are identifiable by their black and white color.
  • Infant Care: Avoid prolonged high-flow oxygen in infants to prevent retrolental fibroplasia.
05

üìù Evaluation & Documentation

Evaluation: Assess breathing patterns, vital signs, and SpO2 levels to determine if further oxygen is needed.

Documentation: Record patient condition before/after, vital signs, SpO2, flow rate, and method of delivery.
