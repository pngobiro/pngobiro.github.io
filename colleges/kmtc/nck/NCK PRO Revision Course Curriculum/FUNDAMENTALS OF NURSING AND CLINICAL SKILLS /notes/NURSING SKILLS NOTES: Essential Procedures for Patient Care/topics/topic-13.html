Gastrostomy Tube Feeding

üîç Definition & Purpose

Definition: Administration of semi-solid food or fluids directly into the stomach through the abdominal wall using a surgically or endoscopically placed tube (Gastrostomy Tube).

Purpose: To maintain adequate nutrition in patients who cannot ingest food orally due to upper GI obstructions or other conditions.

02

üì¢ Indications

Common conditions requiring gastrostomy feeding include:

  • Esophageal tumors or obstructions
  • Severe candidiasis of the upper GI tract
  • Burns of the upper gastro-intestinal tract
  • Severe esophagitis
03

üìã Assessment Criteria

  • Verify the order for type, amount, and flow rate of feeds.
  • Assess tube placement and patency.
  • Evaluate patient tolerance to previous feeds (check for vomiting/distention).
  • Inspect the abdomen for distension and tenderness.
  • Ensure the patient's privacy and obtain informed consent.
04

‚öôÔ∏è Implementation Procedure

Steps Rationale
1. Elevate the head of the bed to 30 degrees. Prevents reflux and aids gravitational flow.
2. Aspirate stomach contents (if > 50ml, return and report). Checks digestion and tube placement.
3. Flush tubing with 15-20 ml of water. Ensures tube patency.
4. Administer feed slowly by gravity. Prevents sudden distention, nausea, or vomiting.
5. Flush with 30 ml of water after feeding. Clears sticky feeds to prevent occlusion.
6. Maintain elevated position for 30-60 mins post-feed. Minimizes the risk of reflux.
7. Inspect and clean the site around the tube. Prevents infection and maintains skin integrity.
05

üìù Evaluation & Documentation

Evaluation: Monitor for abdominal pain, nausea, and weight changes. Perform daily site care to prevent microorganism growth.

Documentation: Record formula type, amount, time, residual volume, and any signs of intolerance.
