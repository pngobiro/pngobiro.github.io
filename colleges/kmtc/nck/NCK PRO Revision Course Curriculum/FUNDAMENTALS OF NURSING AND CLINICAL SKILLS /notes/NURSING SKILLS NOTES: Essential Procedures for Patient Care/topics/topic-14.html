Urinary Catheterization

üîç Definition & Purpose

Definition: The process of inserting a catheter through the urethra into the urinary bladder for the withdrawal of urine or instillation of drugs.

Primary Purposes:

  • Control urinary incontinence.
  • Prevent urinary retention.
  • Allow for continuous, accurate monitoring of urinary output.
  • Allow for bladder irrigation.
02

üì¢ Indications & Assessment

Indications: Sterile specimen collection, pre/post abdominal surgery, post-prostatectomy, and during certain radiological procedures.

Key Assessments:

  • History of allergies to antiseptics or rubber (latex).
  • Patient's understanding of the procedure.
  • Anatomical landmarks for insertion.
  • Age of the patient (determines catheter size).
03

üë© Female Catheterization

  • Positioning: Place the patient in a dorsal recumbent position.
  • Sterile Prep: Perform surgical scrub and drape the perineal area.
  • Cleaning: Gently spread the labia and swab the mucosa starting from furthest point downward.
  • Insertion: Lubricate the catheter and insert into the urethral orifice (4-5 cm) until urine flows.
  • Secure: Inflate the balloon and secure the catheter to the thigh.
04

üë® Male Catheterization

  • Positioning: Position the patient supine with only the genitals exposed.
  • Cleaning: Hold the penis at a 90-degree angle. Pull back the foreskin (if uncircumcised) and clean the glans penis.
  • Insertion: Gently insert the lubricated catheter approximately 20 cm or until urine begins to drain.
  • Inflation: Inflate the retention balloon and check placement by pulling slightly.
  • Final Step: Replace the foreskin over the glans to prevent edema.
05

üìù Documentation

Note the date, size/type of catheter, amount of water instilled in the balloon, characteristic of urine, and any unusual discomfort reported by the patient.
