Removing Nasal Gastric Tube

üîç Definition & Purpose

Definition: Withdrawing a nasogastric tube (NGT) from the patient's gastrointestinal tract.

Purpose: To allow for the resumption of normal gastrointestinal functions once the reason for insertion (e.g., decompression, feeding) has been resolved.

üìç Indication

Removal is indicated when recovery from the initial condition requiring the NGT is achieved.

02

üìã Clinical Assessment

  • Verify Order: Check the patient's chart to confirm the order for tube removal to avoid nursing errors.
  • Level of Consciousness: Determine the patient's ability to cooperate with instructions.
  • Nasal Inspection: Check nostrils for irritation or dryness to establish a baseline.
  • Positioning: Place the patient in Fowler's position to ease removal and prevent aspiration in case of vomiting.
03

‚öôÔ∏è Removal Procedure

Steps Rationale
1. Disconnect suction or feeding pump. Prevents spillage of gastric secretions and protects esophageal tissue.
2. Inject 30-50cc of air into the tube. Clears the tube of gastric drainage that could irritate mucosa or be aspirated.
3. Remove securing tape and ask the patient to take a deep breath and hold it. Facilitates smooth removal and closes the glottis.
4. Pull the tube out slowly but evenly (within 3-6 seconds). Minimizes patient discomfort.
5. Provide oral hygiene and clear the nares. Promotes patient comfort and hygiene.
6. Monitor bowel sounds and flatulence. Indicates resumption of functional bowel activity.
04

üìù Documentation

Records should include:

  • Date and time of removal.
  • Patient's response to the procedure.
  • Any signs of irritation around the nares.
  • Patient complaints of nose or throat pain.
  • Amount of intake or drainage recorded prior to removal.
