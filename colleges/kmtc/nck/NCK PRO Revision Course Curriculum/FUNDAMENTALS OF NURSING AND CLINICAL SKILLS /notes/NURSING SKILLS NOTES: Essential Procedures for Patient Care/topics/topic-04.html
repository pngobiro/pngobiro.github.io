üîç Definition & Purpose

Definition: A bath given to a patient who is in bed and unable to bathe himself/herself.

Purpose: To prevent microorganisms from spreading on the skin, stimulate circulation, promote range of motion, make the patient comfortable, and allow for a thorough skin assessment.

02

üì¢ Indications & Assessment

Indications: Unconscious patients and critically ill patients who cannot perform their own hygiene.

Pre-procedure Assessment:

  • Condition of the patient: Determine assistance required and contraindicated positions.
  • Patient understanding: Determine concerns and education needs regarding hygiene.
03

üì¶ Equipment Requirements

  • Top Shelf (Cleaning)
    • 2 washing basins
    • Jugs for hot and cold water
    • Soap in a dish & 2 flannels
    • Clean gloves & Moisturizer
  • Bottom Shelf (Linen)
    • Bath towel & Mackintosh
    • Clean linen & gown/pajamas
    • Hair comb
    • Linen hamper for dirty items

    ‚öôÔ∏è Implementation Steps

    Step Rationale
    1. Gather all required equipment and wheel to bedside. Facilitates performance of the procedure.
    2. Wash, dry hands and don gloves. Infection prevention and control.
    3. Screen the patient and remove extra pillows. Maintains privacy and ensures patient comfort.
    4. Clean face with plain water (furthest eye first). Prevents cross-contamination.
    5. Clean neck, ears, hands, chest, and abdomen. Removes dirt from areas prone to soiling.
    6. Clean lower limbs, soak feet, and change water. Maintains hygiene and ensures fresh water for delicate areas.
    7. Clean genitalia and change water again. Removes dirt and ensures comfort.
    8. Turn patient and clean back, buttocks, and thighs. Completes the cleaning process.
    9. Apply massage oil and dress the patient. Moistens the skin and promotes dignity.
    üí° Clinical Tip

    Always pay special attention to skin folds (under breasts, umbilicus, axilla) in obese patients to prevent irritation and infection.
