Wound Dressing

01

üîç Definition & Purpose

Definition: The process by which a wound is cleaned and a sterile dressing is applied.

Purpose: To render the wound free of microorganisms and enhance the healing process.

02

üì¢ Indications & Assessment

Indications: Surgical incisions, septic wounds, decubitus ulcers, and removal of stitches/staples/drains.

Assessment Criteria:

  • Extent of skin impairment and characteristics of drainage (amount, color).
  • History of allergies to dressing solutions or materials.
  • Need for analgesia (pain relief) prior to the procedure.
  • Wound condition, surrounding tissues, and odor.
03

üì¶ Requirements

A sterile dressing pack containing:

  • 2 pairs of dressing forceps & 2 pairs of dissecting forceps.
  • Gallipots, cotton wool swabs, and gauze swabs.
  • Kidney dish and hand towel.
  • Sterile gloves.
  • Antiseptic solutions and topical drugs as required.
  • Adhesive tape or bandages.
04

‚öôÔ∏è Implementation Steps

Steps Rationale
1. Disinfect the trolley with spirit and allow to dry. Ensures a sterile working field.
2. Loosen the old dressing carefully. Facilitates subsequent removal without trauma.
3. Perform surgical hand scrub and don sterile gloves. Allows handling of sterile supplies without contamination.
4. Clean the wound from inside outward. Minimizes transfer of microorganisms to the clean area.
5. Use swabs only once and discard. Prevents cross-contamination.
6. Secure the new dressing with strapping. Ensures the dressing remains in place.
05

üìù Documentation

Always record: Characteristics of the wound, type/amount of dressing used, solutions applied, patient tolerance, and any detected abnormalities.
