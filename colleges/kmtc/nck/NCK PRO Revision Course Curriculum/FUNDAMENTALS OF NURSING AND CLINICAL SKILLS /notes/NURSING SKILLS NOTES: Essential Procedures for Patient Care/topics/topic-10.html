Nasal Gastric Tube Insertion

üîç Definition & Purpose

Definition: Introduction of a tube through the nose and esophagus to the stomach (NGT).

Primary Purposes:

  • To provide nourishment (feeding).
  • To empty stomach contents (decompression).
  • For diagnostic procedures.
  • For administration of medication.
02

üì¢ Indications

  • Intestinal obstruction
  • Poisoning
  • Gastro-esophageal reflux
  • Unconsciousness
  • Post-GI operations
  • Gastric distention
  • Severe malnutrition
03

üìè Measuring Tube Length

Correct measurement is critical to ensure the tube reaches the stomach and not the lungs.

üìç The Measurement Rule

Measure the distance from the tip of the nose to the ear lobe, then down to the xiphisternum. Mark this point with tape on the tube.

04

‚öôÔ∏è Insertion Procedure

Steps Rationale
1. Position patient with head above stomach (sitting or semi-Fowler's). Prevents aspiration and protects the airway.
2. Clean nares and lubricate the tip of the tube. Clears passage and prevents trauma to mucosa.
3. Tilt head backward and insert into the clearest nare. Facilitates smooth entry.
4. Request patient to swallow as the tube passes the pharynx. Swallowing closes the glottis, guiding the tube into the esophagus.
5. Advance until the marked tape reaches the external nare. Ensures the tube has reached the stomach.
6. Check position immediately (Aspirate content or use air-bubbling test). Confirm the tube is in the stomach and not the trachea.
üõë CRITICAL SAFETY

If the patient shows signs of distress (gasping, cyanosis), remove the tube immediately as it may be in the trachea.

05

‚úÖ Confirming Placement

  • PH Test: Aspirate 2ml of stomach content. It should turn blue litmus paper pink (confirming acidity).
  • Auscultation: Introduce 5ml of air and listen for bubbling sounds over the epigastrium using a stethoscope.
