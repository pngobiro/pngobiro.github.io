Bed Pan

üìö Patient Elimination

01

üîç Definition & Purpose

Definition: A device used for the elimination of urine and feces by a patient who is confined to bed.

Purpose: To assist patients with elimination needs while maintaining comfort and hygiene.

02

üì¶ Types & Requirements

  • Regular Bed Pan

    Has high sides and a curved rim. Used for patients who can lift their hips.

  • Fracture Pan

    Has a flat end. Used for patients with back injuries, casts, or inability to lift hips.

    • Equipment Needed:

    • Clean Bed Pan (with cover)
    • Toilet paper
    • Wash basin, soap, and towel
    • Gloves
    • Screen/Curtains for privacy

    ‚öôÔ∏è Implementation

    Step Rationale
    1. Ensure privacy and wash hands. Promotes dignity and prevents infection.
    2. Warm the bed pan (if metal) with warm water. Cold pans can cause muscle tension and reflex inhibition of voiding.
    3. Elevate head of bed (Semi-Fowler's). Mimics normal toileting position and aids elimination.
    4. Instruct patient to flex knees and lift hips. Allows space to slide the pan under.
    5. Slide pan under buttocks (open rim toward feet). Correct placement collects excreta.
    6. Leave patient alone if safe (call bell within reach). Privacy encourages relaxation.
    7. Remove pan carefully, holding it steady. Prevents spillage.
    8. Assist with cleaning/wiping and hand washing. Maintains hygiene.
    üí° Immobile Patients

    For patients who cannot lift their hips, roll them onto their side, place the pan against the buttocks, and then roll them back onto the pan.
