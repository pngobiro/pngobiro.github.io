Positions Used in Nursing

01

üîç Definition

The process of placing the client/patient in the bed in the most comfortable position that prevents musculoskeletal injuries and allows comfort and for therapeutic interventions.

02

üì¢ Indications

Proper positioning is especially critical for:

  • Paralyzed patients
  • Unconscious patients
  • Patients undergoing specialized diagnostic and therapeutic procedures
  • Post-operative patients
03

üìã Pre-positioning Assessment

Assessment Rationale
Client's understanding of position change To determine concerns, education needs, and acceptance.
Condition of the skin To identify precautions that may need to be considered.
General condition of the patient To determine assistance required and contraindicated positions.
Neuromuscular status For muscle powers warranting specific positions.
Environment appropriateness For the comfort of both client and nurse.
04

üßò Common Nursing Positions

  • Supine Position

    Patient lies on the back facing up. Use a pillow under the neck to prevent hyperflexion and extra pillows at the pelvis for alignment.

  • Lateral Position (Left/Right)

    Patient lies on the side with head supported by a low pillow. Flex the top leg forward and place a pillow between the legs for comfort and to prevent friction.

  • Prone Position

    Patient lies on the abdomen with the head turned to either side. Allows drainage of oral secretions. Place pillows under the chest and pelvis.

  • Sims Position

    Extreme lateral position where the patient lies partially on the abdomen. Forward hand and leg are extended flat on the bed.

  • Fowler's Position

    Sitting up position in bed with the head of the bed adjusted to an angle of 45 degrees. Promotes comfort and respiratory function.

  • Semi-Fowler's Position

    Similar to Fowler's but the bed is tilted at a lower angle of 15 to 30 degrees.

  • Trendelenburg Position

    The bed is tilted so the head is lower than the feet. Often used to promote venous return or during certain procedures.

  • Knee-Chest Position

    Patient kneels with chest and elbows resting on the bed. Thighs are straight and lower limbs are flat.
