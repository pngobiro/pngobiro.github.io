üëÑ Oral, Sublingual & Buccal

  • Oral Route: Swallowing medications for absorption in the GIT. Economic and safe.
  • Sublingual Route: Placed under the tongue. Bypasses first-pass metabolism and gastric destruction.
  • Buccal Route: Placed between the cheek and gum for local or systemic absorption.
‚ö†Ô∏è Safety Assessment

Always check for allergies and assess the patient's ability to swallow. Never give oral meds to patients with altered consciousness or dysfunctional gag reflexes.

‚úÖ The Five Rights of Medication

To ensure patient safety and prevent medication errors, always verify:

  • Right Patient
  • Right Drug
  • Right Dose
  • Right Route
  • Right Time
04

üõ°Ô∏è Critical Safety Protocols

  • IM Aspiration: Always withdraw the plunger slightly to ensure no blood vessel was punctured before injecting (except for vaccines per some protocols).
  • Needle Safety: Immediately discard needles and syringes in sharps containers. Never recap needles.
  • ID Bleb: A successful intradermal injection should produce a small 'bleb' or wheal (like a mosquito bite).
  • Digoxin Rule: Do not administer if the pulse rate is <60/min.
