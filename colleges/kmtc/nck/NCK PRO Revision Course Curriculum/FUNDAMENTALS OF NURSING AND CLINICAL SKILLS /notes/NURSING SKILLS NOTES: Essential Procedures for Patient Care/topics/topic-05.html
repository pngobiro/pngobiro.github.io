Wash Hair in Bed

üìö Personal Hygiene
‚è±Ô∏è 15 min read
01

üîç Definition & Purpose

Definition: The procedure of cleaning the hair and scalp of a patient who is confined to bed and unable to perform self-care.

Purpose:

  • To maintain hygiene and cleanliness of the hair and scalp.
  • To stimulate blood circulation to the scalp.
  • To promote patient comfort and a sense of well-being.
  • To assess the condition of the scalp (e.g., for dandruff, lesions, or lice).
02

üì¶ Requirements

  • Water System

    Jug of warm water (approx 40-43¬∞C), Bucket for waste water.

  • Protection

    Mackintosh (rubber sheet), Towels (2), Washcloths, Absorbent pads.

  • Cleaning Agents

    Shampoo, Conditioner (optional).

  • Tools

    Hair wash trough (Kelly pad) or improvised plastic trough, Comb/Brush, Hair dryer (optional).

    ‚öôÔ∏è Implementation

    Step Rationale
    1. Explain procedure and wash hands. Reduces anxiety and prevents infection.
    2. Position patient diagonally or with head at edge of bed. Facilitates drainage of water into the bucket.
    3. Place mackintosh and towel under head/shoulders. Protects bed linen from getting wet.
    4. Place trough under head, directing output to bucket. Channels dirty water away from the patient.
    5. Protect eyes with a washcloth. Prevents shampoo from irritating the eyes.
    6. Wet hair, apply shampoo, and massage scalp. Cleanses hair and stimulates circulation.
    7. Rinse thoroughly with warm water. Removes all soap residue which can cause itching.
    8. Remove trough, wrap hair in dry towel. Prevents chilling.
    9. Dry hair and style. Restores comfort and dignity.
