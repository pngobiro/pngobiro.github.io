<p>Family planning is the process of child spacing so that the couple has the number of children they want at the time desired, that is, when wanted, expected and welcomed.</p>
<p>Refer to unit one for further details and clarification.</p>
<div data-testid="stHeadingWithActionElements" class="st-emotion-cache-1p9ibxm e1icttdg2"><h3 level="3" id="benefits-of-family-planning">Benefits of Family Planning<span data-testid="stHeaderActionElements" class="st-emotion-cache-gi0tri e1icttdg3"><a href="#benefits-of-family-planning" class="st-emotion-cache-yinll1 e1icttdg1"><svg xmlns="http://www.w3.org/2000/svg" width="16" height="16" viewBox="0 0 24 24" fill="none" stroke="currentColor" stroke-width="2" stroke-linecap="round" stroke-linejoin="round"><path d="M15 7h3a5 5 0 0 1 5 5 5 5 0 0 1-5 5h-3m-6 0H6a5 5 0 0 1-5-5 5 5 0 0 1 5-5h3"></path><line x1="8" y1="12" x2="16" y2="12"></line></svg></a></span></h3></div>
<p>Family planning enables couples and individuals to decide freely and responsibly on the number and spacing of their children, as well as on the most appropriate time of having them.</p>
<p>Family planning helps everyone. The health benefits of FP play a major role in protecting and promoting the quality of lives, not only of the infants but of their siblings and the family as a whole.</p>
<div data-testid="stHeadingWithActionElements" class="st-emotion-cache-1p9ibxm e1icttdg2"><h3 level="3" id="can-you-think-of-three-benefits-of-family-planning">Can you think of three benefits of family planning?<span data-testid="stHeaderActionElements" class="st-emotion-cache-gi0tri e1icttdg3"><a href="#can-you-think-of-three-benefits-of-family-planning" class="st-emotion-cache-yinll1 e1icttdg1"><svg xmlns="http://www.w3.org/2000/svg" width="16" height="16" viewBox="0 0 24 24" fill="none" stroke="currentColor" stroke-width="2" stroke-linecap="round" stroke-linejoin="round"><path d="M15 7h3a5 5 0 0 1 5 5 5 5 0 0 1-5 5h-3m-6 0H6a5 5 0 0 1-5-5 5 5 0 0 1 5-5h3"></path><line x1="8" y1="12" x2="16" y2="12"></line></svg></a></span></h3></div>
<p>There are several benefits of family planning.</p>
<div data-testid="stHeadingWithActionElements" class="st-emotion-cache-1p9ibxm e1icttdg2"><h3 level="3" id="medical-benefits">Medical Benefits<span data-testid="stHeaderActionElements" class="st-emotion-cache-gi0tri e1icttdg3"><a href="#medical-benefits" class="st-emotion-cache-yinll1 e1icttdg1"><svg xmlns="http://www.w3.org/2000/svg" width="16" height="16" viewBox="0 0 24 24" fill="none" stroke="currentColor" stroke-width="2" stroke-linecap="round" stroke-linejoin="round"><path d="M15 7h3a5 5 0 0 1 5 5 5 5 0 0 1-5 5h-3m-6 0H6a5 5 0 0 1-5-5 5 5 0 0 1 5-5h3"></path><line x1="8" y1="12" x2="16" y2="12"></line></svg></a></span></h3></div>
<p>Medical benefits differ depending on the target population. Some of the benefits of family planning for women are shown below.</p>
<ul>
<li>Their health is better as they are protected against unwanted pregnancy, hence death related to high risk pregnancy and abortion is reduced.</li>
<li>Some methods, such as the hormonal method, have additional (non-contraceptive) benefits, including the reduction of anaemia and cancer of the genital tract.</li>
<li>Barrier methods minimise the transmission of STIs and HIV/AIDS if used properly.</li>
<li>Mothers become healthier as they avoid the extremes of maternal age and carry their pregnancies within the optimal child bearing age bracket of 19 to 35 years, before and after which the mothers are at high risk of developing complications related to pregnancy.</li>
<li>There is a decrease in the risks of abortion as the baby is welcome and the</li>
</ul>

<p>mother well prepared physically and psychologically for the pregnancy.</p>
<ul>
<li>There are reduced fertility and mortality rates.</li>
<li>There are improved health facilities and death related to pregnancy and childbirth is reduced.</li>
</ul>
<p>There are also medical advantages to the children.</p>
<ul>
<li>Children are healthier, since the optimum spacing between births is 27 to 38 months, giving the mother the time to nurse and care for the infant.</li>
<li>The death related to infections and malnutrition to the under fives is reduced.</li>
</ul>
<div data-testid="stHeadingWithActionElements" class="st-emotion-cache-1p9ibxm e1icttdg2"><h2 level="2" id="social-benefits">Social Benefits<span data-testid="stHeaderActionElements" class="st-emotion-cache-gi0tri e1icttdg3"><a href="#social-benefits" class="st-emotion-cache-yinll1 e1icttdg1"><svg xmlns="http://www.w3.org/2000/svg" width="16" height="16" viewBox="0 0 24 24" fill="none" stroke="currentColor" stroke-width="2" stroke-linecap="round" stroke-linejoin="round"><path d="M15 7h3a5 5 0 0 1 5 5 5 5 0 0 1-5 5h-3m-6 0H6a5 5 0 0 1-5-5 5 5 0 0 1 5-5h3"></path><line x1="8" y1="12" x2="16" y2="12"></line></svg></a></span></h2></div>
<p>The social benefits related to family planning are numerous.</p>
<ul>
<li>There is an improved relationship with the spouse and family due to less stress and better social life.</li>
<li>Socially healthy families are productive and economically more stable as they participate in community development and nation building.</li>

<div data-testid="stHeadingWithActionElements" class="st-emotion-cache-1p9ibxm e1icttdg2"><h2 level="2" id="do-you-remember-the-definition-of-counselling-as-covered-in-module-one">Do you remember the definition of counselling as covered in module one?<span data-testid="stHeaderActionElements" class="st-emotion-cache-gi0tri e1icttdg3"><a href="#do-you-remember-the-definition-of-counselling-as-covered-in-module-one" class="st-emotion-cache-yinll1 e1icttdg1"><svg xmlns="http://www.w3.org/2000/svg" width="16" height="16" viewBox="0 0 24 24" fill="none" stroke="currentColor" stroke-width="2" stroke-linecap="round" stroke-linejoin="round"><path d="M15 7h3a5 5 0 0 1 5 5 5 5 0 0 1-5 5h-3m-6 0H6a5 5 0 0 1-5-5 5 5 0 0 1 5-5h3"></path><line x1="8" y1="12" x2="16" y2="12"></line></svg></a></span></h2></div>
<p>Counselling is the act of working with a patient to help them clarify personal goals and find ways of overcoming their problems with the aim of assisting the individual to change behaviours that are interfering with attainment of basic needs.</p>
<p>For the purpose of FP, counselling is viewed as the</p>
<p>art whereby the service provider guides/assists a</p>
<p>patient to make an informed choice in matters regarding their RH needs, taking into account all the surrounding circumstances and the patient's personal needs at the time.</p>
<p>Counselling is a vital part of RH care. It helps patients to:</p>
<ul>
<li>Arrive at an informed choice of RH options</li>
<li>Select a contraceptive method with which they are satisfied</li>
<li>Use the chosen method safely and effectively</li>
</ul>
<p>Counselling in family planning is an important tool when serving patients. It is important to remember that they have:</p>
<ul>
<li>The right to decide whether or not to practise FP</li>
<li>The freedom to choose which method to use</li>
<li>The right of privacy and confidentiality</li>
<li>The right to refuse any type of examination</li>
</ul>
<p>While many contraceptive methods are highly effective, method failure can occur. In case of method failure, the patient should be counselled, informed about available options and referred for appropriate services.</p>

<div data-testid="stHeadingWithActionElements" class="st-emotion-cache-1p9ibxm e1icttdg2"><h2 level="2" id="patient-assessment">Patient Assessment<span data-testid="stHeaderActionElements" class="st-emotion-cache-gi0tri e1icttdg3"><a href="#patient-assessment" class="st-emotion-cache-yinll1 e1icttdg1"><svg xmlns="http://www.w3.org/2000/svg" width="16" height="16" viewBox="0 0 24 24" fill="none" stroke="currentColor" stroke-width="2" stroke-linecap="round" stroke-linejoin="round"><path d="M15 7h3a5 5 0 0 1 5 5 5 5 0 0 1-5 5h-3m-6 0H6a5 5 0 0 1-5-5 5 5 0 0 1 5-5h3"></path><line x1="8" y1="12" x2="16" y2="12"></line></svg></a></span></h2></div>
<p>The patient assessment will help you to give an appropriate method of contraception to an individual patient. In many ways counselling forms a part of patient assessment since both go together as you assist the patient to make an informed choice about the most appropriate method.</p>
<p>The main objective of carrying out a patient assessment is to determine:</p>
<ul>
<li>That the patient is not pregnant</li>
<li>Whether any conditions requiring additional care for a particular method exist</li>
<li>Whether there are any special problems that require further assessment, treatment or regular follow up or referral</li>
<li>Whether there are need for HIV/AIDs services and, if so, to provide the services or arrange the appropriate referral</li>
</ul>
<p>To achieve the above objective, you have to be careful with the questions you ask and remain attentive to the patient's concerns. Furthermore, you need to clarify any points that you feel the patient does not understand clearly.</p>
<p>How can you be reasonably sure that a female patient is not pregnant?</p>
<p>You can only be reasonably sure a patient is not pregnant when she has no signs and symptoms of pregnancy and exhibits the following characteristics:</p>
<ul>
<li>Has had no intercourse since her last menses</li>
<li>Is within seven days after the start of her menses (days one to seven)</li>
<li>Is within four weeks postpartum (for non-breast feeding women)</li>
<li>Is within seven days post abortion</li>
<li>Is fully breast feeding less than six months post partum and has had no menstrual bleeding</li>
<li>Has been correctly and consistently using another reliable contraceptive method</li>
</ul>
<p>Pelvic examination is seldom necessary except to rule out pregnancy of more than six weeks, measured from the Last Menstrual Period (LMP).</p>
<p>A pregnancy test is not essential, except in cases where:</p>
<ul>
<li>It is difficult to confirm pregnancy (that is, six weeks or less after the LMP)</li>
<li>The results of pelvic examination are unequivocal (for example, with an overweight patient sizing the uterus may be difficult)</li>
</ul>

<p>General Physical Examination</p>
<p>What do you think is the purpose of a physical examination?</p>
<p>The main purpose of a physical examination is:</p>
<ul>
<li>To assess the general health status of a patient</li>
<li>To aid in making a diagnosis</li>
</ul>
<p>A physical examination should be performed either as part of a routine medical examination or in case of sickness. You are expected to carry out a systematic head to toe examination, noting:</p>
<ul>
<li>Health status</li>
<li>Nutritional status</li>
<li>Behaviour</li>
<li>Mental state</li>
<li>Speech</li>

<p>Breast Examination</p>
<p>This is a very important examination for a family planner.</p>
<p>See if you can answer the questions below.</p>

<p>Why should you perform a breast examination?</p>
<p>The main reason for performing a breast examination is to enable you to detect abnormalities (such as cancer of the breast) and take appropriate action.</p>
<p>When should a breast examination be performed?</p>
<p>From the age of 20 years all females should do Self Breast Examination (SBE) immediately after their menstrual period. If the patient is on the pill, she should perform her SBE on the day she starts a new packet. Those in menopause should do a SBE on the first day of each calendar month.</p>
<p>How do you perform a breast examination?</p>
<p>This is the method whereby the patient inspects, palpates and expresses both breasts. The axilla is also palpated for any nodules.</p>
<p>Inspection</p>
<p>Ask the patient to undress and sit or stand relaxed in front of a mirror with good lighting. With hands at the sides, on the waist and on the head respectively, check on the size, shape, discoloration and retraction (dimpling) of the nipple. Turn from side to side and look underneath, push the hands inward and</p>

<div data-testid="stHeadingWithActionElements" class="st-emotion-cache-1p9ibxm e1icttdg2"><h2 level="2" id="introduction">Introduction<span data-testid="stHeaderActionElements" class="st-emotion-cache-gi0tri e1icttdg3"><a href="#introduction" class="st-emotion-cache-yinll1 e1icttdg1"><svg xmlns="http://www.w3.org/2000/svg" width="16" height="16" viewBox="0 0 24 24" fill="none" stroke="currentColor" stroke-width="2" stroke-linecap="round" stroke-linejoin="round"><path d="M15 7h3a5 5 0 0 1 5 5 5 5 0 0 1-5 5h-3m-6 0H6a5 5 0 0 1-5-5 5 5 0 0 1 5-5h3"></path><line x1="8" y1="12" x2="16" y2="12"></line></svg></a></span></h2></div>
<p>Natural family planning is an approach to the understanding and respecting of human sexuality with its inherent procreative capacity and its consequent responsibility. Any teaching of the natural methods of family planning is, therefore, within the context of strengthening the family bonds and promoting responsible parenthood and its pro-life.</p>
<p>The natural methods are based on simple observance of nature in regard to human fertility. The methods do not interfere with the body functions in any way. The natural family planning methods require the cooperation of both the husband and wife and the study and discussion of their combined fertility brings them closer together. The sacrifice and control they demand generate respect of love and life. Because this is a natural method it overcomes all barriers, such as religious, social or ethnic. NFP goes hand in hand with LAM. As the mother breastfeeds, ovulation is delayed for as long as two years thus reinforcing the relationship and baby care.</p>

<div data-testid="stHeadingWithActionElements" class="st-emotion-cache-1p9ibxm e1icttdg2"><h1 level="1" id="natural-family-planning-fertility-awareness-based-method">Natural Family Planning (Fertility Awareness Based Method)<span data-testid="stHeaderActionElements" class="st-emotion-cache-gi0tri e1icttdg3"><a href="#natural-family-planning-fertility-awareness-based-method" class="st-emotion-cache-yinll1 e1icttdg1"><svg xmlns="http://www.w3.org/2000/svg" width="16" height="16" viewBox="0 0 24 24" fill="none" stroke="currentColor" stroke-width="2" stroke-linecap="round" stroke-linejoin="round"><path d="M15 7h3a5 5 0 0 1 5 5 5 5 0 0 1-5 5h-3m-6 0H6a5 5 0 0 1-5-5 5 5 0 0 1 5-5h3"></path><line x1="8" y1="12" x2="16" y2="12"></line></svg></a></span></h1></div>
<p>Natural Family Planning (NFP) is the oldest method of contraception. In our societies most tribes practiced some form of FP, such as when a mother was breast feeding it was a taboo to have sex as it was believed the baby would become sick and even die. The woman would abstain from sex for two to four years after childbirth. On some occasions she would even go back to her family home. Many communities also sanctioned polygamy, which allowed the man to have his sexual desires fulfilled while the mother was breast feeding.</p>
<p>As communities started changing, monogamy was embraced and the couple stayed in one house. As a result, new methods of FP came into existence to complement the NFP method.</p>
<p>Natural family planning refers to a way of life whereby a couple, if properly instructed, can choose to avoid or achieve pregnancy by applying the appropriate sexual behaviour during the fertile and infertile phases of the menstrual cycle without the use of chemicals or mechanical devices. NFP is also referred to as periodic abstinence, rhythm or fertility awareness method.</p>
<p>The NFP method seems to work best when couples are clear about whether they want to have a child or not. The method appeals to couples who feel that fertility control is a joint responsibility and that neither partner should be required to bear the brunt of the health burden.</p>
<p>NFP educates couples on their reproductive processes and human sexuality as they work in harmony to control or regulate their fertility. There are several methods of NFP.</p>
