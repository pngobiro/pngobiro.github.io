# Content Integration Report: Anatomy & Physiology
**Date:** January 18, 2026
**Project:** BScN Degree Notes Integration
**Scope:** Integration of "Anatomy & Physiology Notes and Practice Questions" (13 Modules) into the existing structure.

## Executive Summary
A comprehensive scan of all existing HTML files (Papers 1, 2, 3, and 4) reveals that **pure Anatomy & Physiology (A&P) is currently not represented as a standalone subject.** While Unit 3 (Adult Nursing) is organized by body systems, its content focuses almost exclusively on pathophysiology, medical management, and nursing interventions.

## 1. Extent of Difference
The "Extent of Difference" between the new content and existing content is **high**.

| Feature | New A&P Content | Existing Adult Nursing Content |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Primary Focus** | Normal structure and function of the human body. | Abnormal states (diseases) and nursing care. |
| **Depth** | Detailed anatomical parts and physiological processes. | Symptoms, diagnosis, and treatment protocols. |
| **Target** | Pre-clinical/Foundational science. | Clinical practice and management. |

## 3. Integration Strategy
Since the content is significant (13 modules), I recommend one of the following approaches:

### Option A: Dedicated A&P Unit (Recommended)
Create a new unit `BScN_Paper1/Unit_0_Anatomy_and_Physiology/` with 13 topics. This serves as a "First Year Foundation" reference that students can visit before diving into clinical units.

### Option B: Integrated "A&P Review" Sections
Insert the new content at the top of each corresponding Unit 3 topic (e.g., `topic-04-digestive-system.html` would start with "Anatomy & Physiology Review of the GI System" before the diseases section).

## 4. Practice Questions
The "Anatomy and Physiology Questions and Answers with Rationales" should be converted into interactive quizzes for each module.
