Examinations range from plain x-rays to special procedures using contrast media (bronchography, pulmonary angiography) and advanced imaging like MRI and ultrasonography.

‚ö†Ô∏è

Nursing Considerations

  • Pregnancy: Inquire about possibility; x-rays generally contraindicated.
  • MRI Safety: Contraindicated for patients with pacemakers or metallic implants.
  • Contrast Media: Watch for allergic reactions.
  • Patient Education: Inform patient about preparation, procedure, and post-procedure care.

‚öôÔ∏è Dialysis

Dialysis is the process of artificially removing excess fluids, urea, and other wastes. It is indicated when kidneys fail.

üìã

Indications for Dialysis

  • 1

    Rising creatinine levels of 100 micromol/L in 24 hours.

  • 2

    Hyperkalaemia > 7mmol/L with fluid overload.

  • 3

    Symptoms of uraemia (pericarditis, GI bleeding, mental changes).

  • 4

    Acute poisoning with mental changes.

Peritoneal Dialysis (PD)

Sterile dialysing fluid is introduced into the peritoneal cavity. Excess fluid/solutes move via diffusion and osmosis into the fluid, which is then drained.

Central Venous Pressure (CVP)

Measures the pressure in large central veins (jugular, subclavian, vena cava). Normal CVP is 3 - 8 cm H‚ÇÇO or 2 - 5 mm Hg.

  • Low CVP: Indicates hypovolemia (needs fluid).
  • High CVP: Indicates fluid overload or heart failure.

Tracheotomy

Artificial opening in the trachea to relieve upper airway obstruction.

Nursing Management

  • Humidification: Artificial humidification is required as the nasopharynx is bypassed.
  • Suctioning: Regular suctioning to remove secretions.
  • Emergency Tray: Keep spare tubes and tracheal dilators at bedside.
