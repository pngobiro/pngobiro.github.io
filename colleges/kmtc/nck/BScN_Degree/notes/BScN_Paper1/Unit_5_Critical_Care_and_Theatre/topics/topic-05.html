üìú History of Theatre Nursing

Theatre nursing has developed alongside the history of surgery. In the past, operations were performed at home without trained personnel or anaesthesia. The major historical challenges were infection, bleeding, and pain.

Aims of a Theatre Nurse

  • To prepare conscientiously by study to adapt to the changing world of medicine.
  • To allay the fears of the patient.
  • To integrate patient care during their period in theatre.
  • To become highly skilled in theatre techniques and impart knowledge to others.

The custody and security of the patient before, during, and after operation is vested in the theatre team. The law grants patients specific rights, most notably Informed Consent.

‚ö†Ô∏è

Informed Consent Rules

Patients must be given information regarding the type of operation, necessity, and effects (good and bad) before signing.

  • Age Limit: In Kenya, those below 18 years are minors; consent is signed by parents/guardians.
  • Mental Capacity: Consent for the mentally ill is sought from parents/guardians/relatives.
  • Timing: Consent must be obtained before pre-medication drugs are administered, as these may affect reasoning.

Preoperative Care Requirements

To meet legal and safety standards, the following must be ensured before the patient enters theatre:

  • Nil by Oral: Fasting usually starts 6 hours before operation.
  • Identification: Verify patient name, IP Number, and match with files.
  • Site Preparation: Shaving and cleaning of the incision site.
  • Lab Works: Full Haemogram, Urea, Electrolytes within acceptable ranges.
  • Instrument Count: Items used (swabs, blades, forceps) must be counted before and after to prevent loss inside the patient.

üè• Theatre Layout & Equipment

Physical Layout

The theatre unit should be built in a central place, ideally near the ICU and surgical wards, but separated from main hospital traffic by closed doors.

  • Zones: Changing rooms, anaesthetic room, operating room, sluice room, sterile supply.
  • Materials: Walls, floors, and roofs made of labour-saving materials for hygiene. Stainless steel fittings.
  • Safety: Trolleys fitted with non-conductive rubber. Sockets placed >1 meter above floor level to prevent spark risks.

Surgical Needles

Round Bodied: Round and smooth, make a puncture. Used for delicate tissues/organs.
Cutting: Sharp edge, cut a crack. Used for strong tissues (skin, tendon).
Atraumatic: Suture and needle joined together (swaged), minimizing tissue damage.
