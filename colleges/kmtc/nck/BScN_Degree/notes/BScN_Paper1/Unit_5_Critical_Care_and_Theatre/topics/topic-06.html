üßº Preparation & Scrubbing

Preparation of the Operating Room

To minimize micro-organisms, the theatre must be cleaned thoroughly every morning. This involves high dusting, cleaning equipment with disinfectant, and ensuring the floor is scrubbed and mopped. The operating table must be positioned under the lights and checked for functionality.

Preparation of the Nurse

Staff must shower and change into a sterile theatre suit, boots, and cap. High standards of personal hygiene are mandatory. Staff with respiratory infections or gastroenteritis should not enter the operating room.

Scrubbing Durations

  • Gamophen soap: 5 minutes
  • Hibiscrub: 2 minutes
  • Ordinary soap: 10 to 15 minutes
‚ñ∂ Step-by-Step Scrubbing Procedure
  1. 1 Wet hands and arms to the elbow.
  2. 2 Perform a "social wash" for one minute.
  3. 3 Pick up a sterile brush using the elbow dispenser.
  4. 4 Scrub fingernails, fingers, palm, and back of the hand (starting with the left hand).
  5. 5 Scrub for 1.5 minutes, rinse, and repeat for the other hand.
  6. 6 Rinse hands and arms thoroughly in one direction from fingertips to elbows.
  7. 7 Close taps using elbows and keep hands upright.

üß§ Gowning & Gloving

Gowning

Pick up the gown by the neck-band, let it unfold, and slide hands into armholes without touching the outside. A runner nurse ties the back.

Gloving Technique

Strict aseptic technique is required to ensure the sterile exterior of the glove never touches the skin.

  1. 1 Pick the first glove by the folded part (inside) with the left hand and slip in the right hand.
  2. 2 Using the gloved right hand, slip fingers beneath the folded area (outside) of the remaining glove.
  3. 3 Slip the left hand in and unroll the cuffs over the gown sleeves.

üõèÔ∏è Patient Preparation & Positioning

Skin Preparation & Draping

The skin is sponged vigorously with strong disinfectant (Centrimide/Hibitine) and swabbed with iodine or alcohol. Sterile towels (drapes) are then placed to create a sterile field around the incision site.

Common Surgical Positions

Trendelenburg

Supine with head lowered and knees flexed. Used for pelvic operations.

Kidney Position

Lateral with the bridge of the table raised to elevate the loins.

Lithotomy

Supine with legs raised in stirrups. Used for perineum operations.

Laminectomy

Prone position with head supported on a horseshoe rest.

üíâ Anaesthesia

Local Anaesthesia

Induces analgesia in a specific region without loss of consciousness. Duration: 45 mins to 3 hours.

Method Description
InfiltrationInjected on/around the affected area.
Nerve BlockInjected into the nerve supplying the area.
SpinalIntroduced into the spine (lumbar puncture); paralyses area below puncture.
EpiduralInjected into the dura mater space; for abdomen and below.

General Anaesthesia

Causes total loss of consciousness. Agents include volatile inhalations (Ether, Halothane) and intravenous agents (Thiopentone, Ketamine).

üíä

Pre-medication

Administered to prepare the patient for anaesthesia:

  • Atropine: Reduces respiratory secretions and prevents bradycardia.
  • Pethidine/Morphine: Provides analgesia.
  • Valium: Relieves anxiety (night before).
