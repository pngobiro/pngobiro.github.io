üìã Preoperative Care

Fear and anxiety predominate the preoperative period of the patient, hence the care of a patient who is to undergo any operation does not start in the theatre but in the ward or outpatient department. Right from the ward, you need to reassure the patient and handle them with confidence so that their fears can be allayed.

The Receiving Area

This is where the ward nurse identifies the patient to the theatre nurse, discusses and hands over the patient's notes, and formally hands over the patient.

Theatre Reception Checklist

  • Identification: Check identification bands, name on notes, and IP Number. All must correspond.
  • Consent: Confirm the form is correct, signed, and relevant to the operation.
  • Pre-medication: Check what was given, tick and sign, noting the time on the checklist.
  • Physical Check: Transfer patient to theatre trolley and verify preparation (shaving, etc.).
  • Resources: Check for X-rays and blood from the blood bank (ward nurse responsibility).

üë• Theatre Nurse Roles

1. Anaesthetic Room Nurse

Responsible for assisting the anaesthetist during induction, intubations, and operation. This nurse is in charge of the anaesthetic room.

‚ñ∂ Duties During Induction & Operation
  • Help put in IV cannulas and administer oxygen by mask.
  • Hand over the airway and strapping for endotracheal tube; inflate and clip the tube.
  • Fix arms and secure them by strapping to the arm board.
  • Observe the patient (colour, sweating, restlessness) and report to anaesthetist.
  • Assist in removing the intubation tube after operation.
  • Clear trolleys and tidy the room for the next patient.

2. Scrub-Up Nurse

Ensures sterility by cleaning thoroughly and wearing sterile gown/gloves. Responsible for the sterile field around the operating table.

  • Arrange sterile instruments before the operation.
  • Prepare ligatures for different stages.
  • Count all equipment (swabs, instruments) before cavity closure to prevent loss.
  • Pass cavity mops and instruments to the surgeon.

3. Circulating (Runner) Nurse

Ensures the welfare of the scrub team and patient, plus maintaining sterility in the room.

  • Assist in positioning the patient.
  • Watch the scrub nurse and supply required items.
  • Ensure extra equipment (diathermy, suction) is working.
  • Check records and count used swabs to confirm correctness.
  • Record tourniquet time, bandages, IV fluids, and drugs used.

4. Administrative & Support Roles

Nurse Administrator: Overall administration, ensuring safety, adequate staffing (24hrs), orientation of new staff, equipment availability, and discipline.

Theatre Attendant: Cleans the sluice room, washes instruments after operation, cleans mackintoshes, and packs instruments for sterilisation.

üõå Postoperative Care

Recovery Room Nurse Duties

Observe the patient every 15 minutes (vital signs). Monitor IV infusions and fluid balance (input/output). A decrease in urine output calls for urgent action.

General Principles

Airway & Circulation

Place patient in recovery position (left-lateral). Maintain infusions. Calculate fluid requirements based on body weight.

Wound & Infection

Monitor for bleeding. Check for signs of infection (redness, tenderness, fever) after 24 hours.

GI Motility

Assess return of bowel sounds and flatus. Keep 'Nil by Oral' until return (3-4 days for laparotomy).

Ambulation

Encourage early movement to prevent Deep Venous Thrombosis (DVT) and pulmonary embolism.

Fluid Maintenance Calculations

Calculate maintenance requirement + fluid loss (operation loss + normal loss + insensible loss).

Adult Maintenance:

$$ 35\text{ml} \text{ per kg body weight in 24 hours} $$

Insensible Loss: approx. $0.5\text{ml} \text{ per kg per hour}$
