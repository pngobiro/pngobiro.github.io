ü©∏ Anaemias

Anaemia can be defined as a reduction of the oxygen carrying power of the blood, either due to reduction of red blood cell volume or haemoglobin concentration.

Types of Anaemia

‚ñ∂ 1. Iron Deficiency Anaemia

Most common in infants fed only milk after 4-5 months when liver iron reserves are depleted. Can be caused by insufficient dietary iron, malabsorption, or maternal anaemia. RBCs become microcytic and hypochromic.

‚ñ∂ 2. Haemorrhagic Anaemia

Caused by acute or chronic blood loss due to injuries, accidents, or epistaxis.

‚ñ∂ 3. Aplastic/Hypoplastic Anaemia

Bone marrow failure depressing red cells, white cells, and platelets. Causes include toxic drugs (chloramphenicol), radiation, or infection.

‚ñ∂ 4. Haemolytic Anaemia

Excessive/rapid destruction of RBCs. Causes include malaria, sickle cell, septicemia, or incompatible transfusion. Clinical features include jaundice and skin irritation.

Nursing Management of Anaemia

  • Bed rest during initial treatment.
  • High protein diet rich in vitamins.
  • Oxygen administration as needed.
  • Blood transfusion if indicated by Hb levels.
  • Supplements: Folic acid, Vitamin B complex.

üß¨ Sickle Cell Anaemia

Sickle cell anaemia is a congenital genetic disease characterised by abnormal haemoglobin (HbS). Sickle red blood cells have a short lifespan (approx. 20 days) compared to normal cells (120 days).

üîç Click to zoom

Pathophysiology

Blockage of small vessels by sickle cells causes vascular obstruction, leading to tissue necrosis, severe pain (crisis), and organ damage.

Clinical Features

  • Painful large joints of extremities
  • Growth failure and delayed sexual maturity
  • Cardiomegaly and hepatosplenomegaly
  • Susceptibility to pneumonia and infections
‚ö°

Management of Crisis

Objectives: Minimise energy expenditure, promote hydration, control pain, treat infection.

  • Admit to hospital on complete bed rest.
  • Administer oxygen and analgesics (Pethidine/Paracetamol).
  • Hydration: Plenty of oral/IV fluids to dilute blood.
  • Prophylaxis: Antibiotics (Penicillin V), Folic acid, Antimalarials.

üéóÔ∏è Leukaemia & Lymphoma

Leukaemia

Malignant condition of blood-forming cells with abnormal increase in white blood cells (leucocytosis). Common in children aged 4-8 years.

Types:

  • Myeloid: Affects granular/polymorph nuclear leucocytes.
  • Monocytic: Affects monocytes (uncommon).
  • Lymphocytic: Affects lymphocytes (most common in children).

Management: Cytotoxic drugs (Chlorambucil, Methotrexate), Steroids (Prednisone), Radiotherapy.

Burkitt's Tumour (Lymphoma)

A form of lymphoblastic beta cell lymphoma predominantly found in tropical Africa. Highly malignant. Often presents with swollen lymph glands in the neck/jaw, loose teeth, or protruding eyeball.

Treatment: Cytotoxic drugs (Cyclophosphamide, Methotrexate).

Dehydration

Excessive fluid loss from the body caused by diarrhoea, vomiting, excessive sweating, or polyuria.

Oedema

Abnormally large amount of fluid in intercellular tissue space. Can be localised (injury, inflammation) or generalised (Nephrotic syndrome, Cardiac failure, Kwashiorkor).

üõ°Ô∏è Immunodeficiency & HIV

Human Immunodeficiency Virus (HIV)

HIV depletes T-helper (T4) cells, resulting in a compromised immune system. Transmission in children is mainly vertical (mother-to-child), via breast milk, or blood products.

Clinical Features in Children

  • Repeated/prolonged diarrhoea
  • Oral thrush and Seborrhoeic dermatitis
  • Failure to thrive
  • Generalised lymphadenopathy
  • Repeated infections (Pneumonia, Otitis media, TB reactivation)
‚ñ∂ Antiretroviral (ARV) Therapy Classes
  1. Nucleoside Reverse Transcriptase Inhibitors (NRTIs): AZT, Lamivudine (3TC). Prevent RNA to DNA conversion.
  2. Non-Nucleoside Reverse Transcriptase Inhibitors (NNRTIs): Nevirapine, Efavirenz. Act directly against the conversion enzyme.
  3. Protease Inhibitors (PIs): Lopinavir, Ritonavir. Interfere with viral assembly.

Management Goal: Slow viral growth, prevent opportunistic infections (PCP prophylaxis), and provide nutritional support.
