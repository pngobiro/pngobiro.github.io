Eczematous Dermatitis

Inflammation of the epidermis and dermis. Can be acute or chronic.

‚ñ∂ 1. Infantile Eczema

Associated with allergies (asthma/hay fever history). Affects babies ~3 months old (boys > girls). Rash starts on face/neck, moves to joints (wrists, elbows, knees). The rash weeps and forms crusts.

‚ñ∂ 2. Primary Contact Eczema

Caused by irritants (soap, pollen, drugs). Rash starts on forehead/cheeks (mouth area clear). Blisters (vesicles) form, weep, and crust.

Management: Potassium permanganate baths, Antihistamines (Phenergan), Steroid creams (Hydrocortisone), Avoid irritants.

Bacterial Infections

Impetigo Contagiosa

Highly contagious infection caused by Staphylococcus aureus or Streptococcus. Characterised by pustules that leak serum/pus and form thick crusts.

Nursing Alert

Isolation is essential due to contagious nature. Remove crusts with warm water/starch poultice. Administer antibiotics (Cloxacillin/Erythromycin).

Parasitic Infestations

Condition Cause Key Features Management
Scabies Itch Mite (Sarcoptes scabiei) Severe itching at night. Burrows between fingers/toes. Benzyl Benzoate 25%. Treat whole family. Hygiene.
Jiggers Sand flea (Tunga penetrans) Burrows into skin (toes). Itching/inflammation. Removal with sterile needle. Tetanus prophylaxis.
Ringworm (Tinea) Fungal infection Circular patches, alopecia (scalp). Whitfield's cream, shave hair, hygiene.

üëÇ Disorders of the Ear

Otitis Media

Inflammation of the middle ear. Very common in young children because their eustachian tubes are wider, shorter, and more horizontal.

Clinical Features

  • Severe pain (child pulls ear/rolls head).
  • High fever (39-40¬∞C).
  • Bulging, opaque eardrum.
  • Possible convulsions/vomiting.

Management: Analgesics, Antibiotics (Amoxyl/Ampicillin), Myringotomy (incision of drum) if bulging severely.

Mastoiditis

Inflammation of mastoid air cells (complication of otitis media). Pain extends to mastoid process (behind ear), which becomes red/swollen. Pinna is pushed forward.

Treatment: IV Antibiotics. Surgical: Cortical or Radical Mastoidectomy.

Conjunctivitis

Inflammation of the conjunctiva.

Type Cause/Features Management
Ophthalmia Neonatorum Gonococcal (from birth canal). Swollen sticky eyelids, purulent discharge in newborn. Penicillin IM daily. Prophylaxis for all newborns.
Bacterial Purulent discharge, sticky eyelids. Tetracycline eye ointment. Systemic antibiotics.
Viral Lacrimation (tears), no pus. Self-limiting. Prevent secondary infection.
Allergic Itching, rubbing, lacrimation. Remove allergen. Zinc sulphate drops, Antihistamines.
Chemical Irritants (soap, acid). Irrigate immediately with water/saline for 30 mins.
