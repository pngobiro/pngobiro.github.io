Section Objectives

By the end of this section you will be able to:

  • Define the nursing process
  • Describe the rationale of using the nursing process
  • List the steps of the nursing process

The nursing process is a technique which scientifically attempts to solve problems created by the patient's illness and to meet the health care and nursing needs of patients.

01

Assessment

Assessment starts with your very first encounter with the patient. It involves a systematic collection of data conducted on a patient to determine their health status.

1. The Nursing History

Taken to determine the patient's state of illness. The primary skill used here is interviewing.

Interviewing Skills include:

  • Listening and questioning
  • Observing and interpreting
  • Synthesising
  • Incorporating what is learnt into a plan of care

2. The Physical Examination

Designed to determine the patient's assets which may serve to compensate for their limitations. The basic tools are the human senses of vision, hearing, touch and smell.

Fundamental Techniques:

Inspection

Using "seeing eyes" to observe age, appearance, body structure, posture, etc.

Palpation

Feeling with hands for solid masses, rigidity, or tenderness.

Auscultation

Listening to body sounds (lungs, heart, bowel) using a stethoscope.

Percussion

Tapping body parts to determine underlying structures.

Nursing Diagnosis

After assessment, you analyze data to identify Nursing Diagnoses. These are the patient's actual or potential problems that are resolvable by nursing actions. Note that a nursing diagnosis is different from a medical diagnosis.

02

Planning

This phase involves setting priorities, identifying specific interventions, and documenting the care plan.

Setting Priorities

Maslow's hierarchy of needs provides a useful framework. High priority is given to physical needs (like breathing) before higher needs.

Establishing Goals

  • 1 Immediate Goals: Address existing problems.
  • 2 Intermediate Goals: Address potential problems likely to occur in the near future.
  • 3 Long Term Goals: Require longer periods (prevention, rehabilitation).

03

Implementation

Implementation refers to carrying out the proposed plan of care. You assume responsibility for the nursing team and coordinate activities.

Nursing Interventions Include:

  • Hygienic care
  • Promotion of physical and psychological comfort
  • Support of respiratory and elimination functions
  • Facilitation of ingestion of food, fluids and nutrients
  • Environmental management and health teaching
  • Promotion of a therapeutic relationship

Delegation: You may delegate actions but must know the capabilities of team members and provide necessary information. Documentation is key to ensure continuity.

04

Evaluation

Evaluation is the final component. It is directed towards determining the patient's response to the nursing intervention and the extent to which the goals have been achieved.

The Cycle Continues

The nursing care plan is subject to change as the patient's problems change or as priorities shift. Continuous updating of the care plan is of paramount importance.
