The Nursing Profession

Bailliere's Nurses dictionary defines a profession as a "calling, vocation requiring specialised knowledge, methods and skills, as well as preparation in an institution of higher learning in the scholarly, scientific and historical principles underlying such methods and skills".

The nursing profession is distinguished by its philosophy of care, full time commitment to human well being, particular blend of knowledge and skills, valuable service to the community and the regulation of its practice.

Personal Grooming

Personal grooming is very important to nurses. Remember, every patient has a mental picture or image of how their nurse should be. The nurse's appearance provides security and hope to a patient.

üëî

Grooming Standards

  • Uniform: Should not be tight or very loose. Must be clean to prevent cross infection and well pressed.
  • Dress/Skirt: Below the knee cap to give allowance for bending. Material should not be 'see-through'.
  • Hair: Female nurses should tie back long hair neatly. Male nurses should have their beards and moustaches neatly shaven.
  • Shoes: Low-heeled and soft to avoid excessive noise.
  • Identification: It is advisable to wear an identification tag at all times showing full names and qualifications.

Nursing Ethics

Collin's dictionary defines ethics as: Moral principles, Code of behaviour, and Study of morals.

International Council of Nurses (ICN) Code of Ethics

The ICN Code of Ethics states that the fundamental responsibility of the nurse is fourfold:

  1. To promote health
  2. To prevent illness
  3. To restore health
  4. To alleviate suffering

Relationships in Ethics

‚ñ∂ Nurses and People

The nurse's primary responsibility is to those people who require nursing care. The nurse, in providing care, promotes an environment in which the values, customs and spiritual beliefs of the individual are respected. The nurse holds in confidence personal information and uses judgement in sharing this information.

‚ñ∂ Nurses and Practice

The nurse carries personal responsibility for nurse practice and for maintaining competence by continual learning. The nurse maintains the highest standards of nursing care possible within the reality of a specific situation.

‚ñ∂ Nurses and Co-workers

The nurse sustains a cooperative relationship with co-workers in nursing and other fields. The nurse takes appropriate action to safeguard the individual when their care is endangered by a co-worker or any other person.

‚ñ∂ Nurses and the Profession

The nurse plays the major role in determining and implementing desirable standards of nursing practice and nursing education. The nurse is active in developing a core of professional knowledge.

03

Professional Organisations

Nurses in Kenya are members of professional organisations at national, regional and global levels.

National Nurses Association of Kenya (NNAK)

NNAK is a professional association for nurses, registered by the Registrar of Societies as a welfare association. Membership is open to all nurses registered or enrolled by the Nursing Council of Kenya.

Functions of NNAK:

  • Promoting nursing and maintaining the honour, interest and practice of all aspects of the profession.
  • Promoting and maintaining high standards of nursing education.
  • Stimulating and encouraging nursing research.
  • Acting as a local representative body of the nursing profession.
  • Supporting a high standard of nursing ethics, conduct and practice.

East Central Southern Africa College of Nursing (ECSACON)

ECSACON is a professional agency of the Commonwealth Regional Health Community (CRHC). Its main objective is to promote and reinforce professional excellence through the development of programmes.

Benefits of Membership: Knowledge exchange, networking, reading materials, and participation in conferences and workshops.

International Council of Nurses (ICN)

Founded in 1899, ICN is a federation of nurses' associations in 122 countries. It is operated by nurses for nurses.

  • Goals: To influence health and nursing globally and strengthen national nurses associations.
  • Philosophy: Entails commitment to caring, advocating on behalf of patients, and helping people help themselves.
04

The Nursing Council of Kenya

The Nursing Council of Kenya (NCK) was established by the Nurses Act, Chapter 257 of the laws of Kenya. It is a corporate body with power to sue and be sued.

Functions of the Nursing Council

  • Establishing and improving standards of professional nursing and health care.
  • Prescribing and regulating syllabuses of instruction and courses of training.
  • Prescribing and conducting examinations for registration or enrolment.
  • Taking disciplinary measures to maintain proper standards of conduct.
  • Advising the Minister on matters concerning all aspects of nursing.

Standing Committees

Committee Function
Education Designing programmes, scrutiny of curriculum, monitoring students, dealing with examinations.
Standards and Ethics Initiation and maintenance of standards, coordinating council visits to health institutions.
Registration and Licensing Deals with registration, enrolment, and licensing of nurses (monthly meetings).
Investigations Investigates cases of professional misconduct, negligence, malpractice, and impropriety.
Finance Deals with all financial issues of the Council.
Disciplinary Deals with disciplinary cases recommended by the investigations committee.

Legal Aspects of Nursing in Kenya

Professional Offences

NEGLIGENCE
You will be charged with this offence if you do not provide the expected care to a patient or client in the field you were trained in.
MISCONDUCT
Includes stealing drugs or hospital property, forgery or fraud, coming on duty while drunk, fighting while on duty or use of abusive language.
MALPRACTICE
If you provide substandard care or perform procedures that are out of your scope of practice.
IMPROPRIETY
Conducting yourself unprofessionally on or off duty (e.g., fighting in a bar), discrediting or shaming the nursing profession.

Other Relevant Laws

  • Public Health Act (Cap 242): Describes health delivery services.
  • Pharmacy and Poisons Act (Cap 244): Related to drug prescription and administration.
  • Mental Health Act (Cap 248): Laws related to interaction with mental health patients.
  • Children and Young Persons Act: Recognizes rights of children.

Nursing Models

A model can be useful for nursing practice, education, management and research.

  • Orem (1971): Self-care model, depicting nursing as assisting individuals to their optimal level of self-care.
  • Roy (1970): Centred her model on the concept of adaptation.
  • Rogers (1970): Focused on the concept of environment and the nursing of a client interacting with the environment.
07

Professional Nursing Practice

Clinical nursing practice has three major roles:

Provider

Meeting nursing and healthcare needs of individuals using the nursing process. Involves leadership components like deciding, relating, influencing and facilitating.

Advocate

Anticipating and meeting requirements of a patient who is unable to meet their own needs. Communicating needs to other health professionals.

Researcher

Generating knowledge in a systematic way. Nurses must be continually aware of studies related to their clinical practice and share findings.
