üéØ Objectives & Overview

Pharmacology is the science of drugs, including their preparation, use, and effects on living tissues. This section covers the legal frameworks, drug standards, classifications, interactions, and the principles of the Essential Drugs Kit.

Learning Objectives

  • Explain therapeutic doses and calculation of drugs.
  • Describe drug interactions, assimilation, resistance, contraindications, and side effects.
  • Understand the legal framework governing drug use in Kenya.
  • Describe the principles of the Essential Drugs Kit.

‚öñÔ∏è Legal Framework

Pharmacy and Poisons Act (Cap 244)

Regulates the sale and distribution of poisons and drugs. Relevant schedules for nursing include:

Schedule Requirements
Schedule 1 (S.1) Must be stored in a locked cupboard. Supplied only on prescription by a registered practitioner or written order by the nurse in charge.
Schedule 4 (S.4A) Requires full prescription details (Doctor/Patient name, drug, quantity, dose, signature). Records kept for 2 years.

Narcotic Drugs Act (1994)

üîí

Controlled Drugs Rules

  • Must be stored in a double-locked cupboard (locked container within a locked cupboard).
  • Keys must be kept by the person in charge.
  • Must be ordered in a special DDA book.
  • Every dose administered must be witnessed and signed for.
  • Empty ampoules must be saved for verification.

Drug Properties

‚ñ∂ Key Standards
  • Purity: Concentration of extraneous substances.
  • Potency: Strength of the drug (measured by assay).
  • Bioavailability: Degree to which drug is absorbed and transported to site of action.
  • Efficacy: Effectiveness in treatment.
  • Safety/Toxicity: Incidence and severity of adverse reactions.

‚ö° Effects & Interactions

Types of Drug Effects

  • Therapeutic Effect: The desired beneficial action (e.g., pain relief).
  • Side Effects: Physiological effects not related to the desired therapeutic effect.
  • Toxic Effects: Excessive drug effect, often an exaggeration of therapeutic effect (e.g., coma from sedatives).
  • Idiosyncratic: Genetically determined, unexpected response (sensitivity or insensitivity).
  • Allergic Reaction: Immunologic response (requires previous exposure). e.g., Anaphylaxis.

Pharmacokinetics (ADME)

‚ñ∂ Absorption

Passage from site of application into circulation. Affected by pH, food, motility. IV drugs bypass this stage.

‚ñ∂ Distribution

Transport to tissues. Depends on permeability barriers, pH, and perfusion.

‚ñ∂ Metabolism

Breakdown of substance by enzymes. Primarily occurs in the liver.

‚ñ∂ Excretion

Elimination of drug. Primarily via the kidneys. Also lungs, skin, milk.

üì¶ Essential Drugs Kit

The Kenya Essential Drug Kit concept ensures drugs are accessible to all health facilities. Kits are distributed to Hospitals, Health Centers, and Dispensaries.

‚ö†Ô∏è

Management Principles

  • Use First-In, First-Out (FIFO) principle.
  • Use drugs that are about to expire first.
  • Return expired drugs to the pharmacy.
  • Redistribute unused common drugs.
