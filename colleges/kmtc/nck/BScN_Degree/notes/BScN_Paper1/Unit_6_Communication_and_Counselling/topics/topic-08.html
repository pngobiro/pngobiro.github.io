üéóÔ∏è HIV/AIDS Counselling

Counselling HIV/AIDS patients involves helping them cope with social stigma, rejection, and emotional distress (anger, fear, guilt). The GATHER approach is applied here:

  • G

    Greet the patient.

  • A

    Ask the patient about themselves.

  • T

    Tell the patient about HIV/AIDS.

  • H

    Help the patient accept the condition.

  • E

    Explain coping mechanisms.

  • R

    Return for follow-up visits.

Pre-Test Counselling

Necessary to provide accurate information, assess coping ability, and ensure informed consent.

‚ö†Ô∏è

Transmission Facts

Patients must understand HIV is transmitted via sexual intercourse, blood transfusion, unsterilized needles, traditional circumcision, and handling body fluids without protection.

Managing Complications

Patients should be informed about opportunistic infections:

‚ñ∂ Diarrhoea

Manage with plenty of fluids (boiled water, weak tea, soup) to prevent dehydration. Avoid fats, oils, and milk. Treated with Metronidazole/Imodium.

‚ñ∂ Skin Conditions

Rashes, sores, and itching (pruritic papules). Keep skin clean and dry. Use calamine for itching. Treat fungal infections with Nystatin/Nizoral.

‚ñ∂ Medications

Antiretrovirals: Zidovudine (AZT), Didanosine.
Antifungals: Nizoral, Nystatin.
For Herpes: Acyclovir.

Preparing for Death

For terminally ill patients, discuss feelings about dying, unfinished business, wills, debts, and funeral wishes. This helps the patient die with dignity and peace.

üõë Counselling Rape Victims

Rape victims are often bitter, frightened, and emotional. The counsellor must listen attentively and allow emotional expression.

Emotional Reactions

  • Shame and self-hatred.
  • Fear of pregnancy, STIs, and HIV/AIDS.
  • Physical pain from injuries.
  • Fear of parental/societal reaction.

Medical Examination Process

A medical exam is crucial for evidence and health. Explain the process gently:

ü©∫

Examination Steps

  1. Body Exam: Check for bruises (arms, back, buttocks).
  2. Clothing: Check for stains, tears, dust.
  3. Genitalia: Check for swelling, tears, bleeding. Hymen status.
  4. Samples: Vaginal fluids for seminal fluid check. Foreign hairs.
  5. Prevention: Refer to VCT for HIV status. Prophylactic ARVs. Antibiotics for STI prevention.

‚ôø Disabilities & Chronic Illness

People with disabilities or chronic illnesses often feel rejected or dependent. Counselling aims to help them accept the condition and coping mechanisms.

Four Basic Principles

1
Treat as a Human Being: Use language that avoids blame. They are people first, patients second.
2
Consider Social Impact: Illness affects the whole family. Family counselling is vital.
3
Consider Practical/Emotional Impact: Address dependency issues and loss of productivity.
4
Confront Reality: Help the patient accept the reality of their condition to find realistic coping mechanisms.
05

üë™ Family Planning Counselling

Using the GATHER methodology specifically for reproductive health services.

G Greet Offer a seat, ensure privacy, assure confidentiality. Establish rapport.
A Ask Ask about family, knowledge of FP methods, partner support, and preferred methods.
T Tell Explain available methods, advantages/disadvantages. Demonstrate use.
H Help Help patient make an informed choice suitable for them and their partner.
E Explain Demonstrate exactly how to use the selected method (e.g., pill schedule, condom use).
R Return Plan follow-up for supplies or problems. Encourage returning if issues arise.
