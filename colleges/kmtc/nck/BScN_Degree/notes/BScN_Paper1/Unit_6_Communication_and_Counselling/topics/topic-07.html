üõãÔ∏è Psychoanalytic Approach

Based on Sigmund Freud's theory, the human mind is divided into three components. The aim is to understand repressed experiences contributing to current problems.

Fig. 7.1: Freud's Structure of Personality

Id Unconscious Seeks immediate gratification (pleasure principle). No morality or logic.
Ego Preconscious Mediates between Id and reality. Represents reasoning and common sense.
Super Ego Conscious Represents morality and perfection. Contains demands of the Id via moral influence.

Howard's Derivations

Useful insights derived from Freud's theory include:

  • Human beings are not always conscious of what they are doing or why.
  • Self-awareness can be painful, leading to avoidance and defensiveness.
  • Guilt and shame keep facets of personality buried.
  • Unfinished business from the past disrupts the present.
  • Repressed thoughts threaten us, causing uncertainty.
03

üë§ Person-Centred Approach

Based on Maslow's theory of self-actualisation. It assumes people are motivated to exploit their capabilities and can regulate themselves given the right conditions. Psychological problems arise from failure to actualise.

Core Concepts

Self-Concept
The way an individual perceives the self and interacts with life.
Congruence
When self-conceptions match the reality of experience.
Incongruence
When self-conceptions differ from reality, often based on "conditions of worth" (e.g., being what parents want rather than what the self wants).
Real-Ideal Self
Comparing how I am (Real) versus how I would most like to be (Ideal).

The Three Core Conditions

The counsellor provides a conducive climate for growth through:

  1. Unconditional Positive Regard
  2. Respect
  3. Empathy
04

‚öôÔ∏è Behavioural Theory

Also known as "Behaviour Therapy". Based on the view that humans are born as a "blank slate" and behavior is learned. Therefore, maladaptive behavior can be unlearned.

This theory deals with the here and now. The past cannot be changed, but the future can be modified.

Objectives of Behavioural Therapy

  • Alter Maladaptive Behaviour: E.g., increasing socially assertive responses.
  • Optimize Decision Making: Listing possible courses of action.
  • Prevention: Preventing problems from occurring (e.g., teaching partner selection skills).
üìã

Assessment

Psychologists use questionnaires and checklists to identify what stimuli cause anxiety or what activities are rewarding. This helps identify reinforcers to be used in treatment.

05

üé® The Eclectic (Multimodal) Approach

Developed by Arnold Lazarus. Based on the belief that no single theory works for all patients. It borrows concepts and techniques from different schools of thought tailored to the individual.

The BASIC ID Modalities

Human personality consists of seven modalities:

B
Behaviour: Action-oriented, "doers".
A
Affect: Emotions and their expression.
S
Sensation: Value attached to sensory experiences (food, music, pain).
I
Imagery: Thinking in pictures, visualization.
C
Cognition: Analytical, planning, reasoning.
I
Interpersonal: Social interaction and relationships.
D
Drugs/Biology: Physical health, habits (exercise, sleep), and substance use.
