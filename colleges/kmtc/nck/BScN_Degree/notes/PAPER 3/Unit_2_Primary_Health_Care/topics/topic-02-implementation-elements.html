In this section, you will cover the activities that are relevant to the implementation of PHC elements at the seven levels of health care provision.

01

üìä The Seven Levels of Health Care

The health care provision system in Kenya is organized into seven distinct levels:

  1. Family Level - The basic unit of social organization.
  2. Community Level - Village and surrounding areas.
  3. Locational Level - Often consolidated with community level.
  4. Divisional Level - Administrative division.
  5. District Level - Focal point for PHC implementation.
  6. Provincial Level - Coordination and technical support.
  7. National Level - Policy making and resource allocation.
02

üè† Family Level Implementation

The family is the nucleus and main focus of each essential element of PHC. Here is how elements are implemented at this level:

‚ñ∂ Health Education & Nutrition
  • Education: Most effective in the home environment. Families provide basic education in language, beliefs, and customs.
  • Nutrition: Families are taught good food and nutrition practices, including appropriate methods of growing and storing food.
‚ñ∂ Water, Sanitation & Hygiene
  • Use the 'three pots' system for water.
  • Protect springs and wells.
  • Construct and use latrines.
  • Practice personal hygiene (hand washing).
‚ñ∂ MCH, Family Planning & Immunisation
  • Encourage good traditional practices and discourage harmful ones during pregnancy/labour.
  • Educate on antenatal care and family planning.
  • Families must take children for immunisation and understand the "Road to Health" card.
‚ñ∂ Disease Control (Malaria, HIV/TB) & Common Conditions
  • Malaria: Use treated nets, clear bushes/stagnant water, manage fever, seek early treatment.
  • Common Conditions: Recognise signs/symptoms, ensure prescribed treatment is taken, understand causes/prevention.
  • HIV/AIDS/TB: Encourage single partners, talk openly to children, provide home-based care, advocate condom use.
03

üèòÔ∏è Community Level Implementation

This level includes Location and Divisional levels. Activities focus on support, training, and mobilization.

  • Education

    Community seeks information from health providers and accepts to change negative habits/customs.

  • Water & Sanitation

    Protect water sources, promote VIP latrines, identifying leaders for village health committees.

  • MCH/FP & Immunisation

    Train/supervise Traditional Birth Attendants (TBAs). Ensure vaccine supply/cold chain. Integrated services.

  • Essential Drugs

    Technical supervision of drug use by CHWs. Training on rational drug use.

  • Disease Control

    Training/supervision of CHWs, constant drug supply, record keeping, referral system.

04

üè¢ District Level Health System

The district health system is the focal point for PHC implementation, transferring management from the central MOH to local levels.

Key Coordinator: The District Health Management Team (DHMT) coordinates PHC at the district level.

Key Issues for a Functioning District System:

  • Coordination: Highest district authority coordinates comprehensive services.
  • Health Management Teams: Decision making shared among MOH, district offices, facilities, and community.
  • Community Participation: Involving community leaders in decision making and resource mobilization.
  • Resources: Adequate staffing and supplies, especially at health centres.
  • Health Information: Monitoring indicators for decision making.

DHMT Responsibilities:

  • Ensure food security and nutrition surveillance.
  • Evaluate safe water coverage and organise training.
  • Train health workers in MCH/FP and monitor activities.
  • Distribute supplies and maintain cold chain for immunisation.
  • Manage outbreaks and provide referral backup.
  • Integrate Community Based Rehabilitation (CBR).
