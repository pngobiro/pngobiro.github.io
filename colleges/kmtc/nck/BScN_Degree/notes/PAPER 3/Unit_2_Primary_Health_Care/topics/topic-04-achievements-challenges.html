Achievements, Challenges and Way Forward

üìö Overview of achievements, challenges, and future strategies

In this final section, we review the progress made in implementing PHC in Kenya, the significant challenges that persist, and the strategies for future improvement.

01

üèÜ Achievements of PHC

While difficult to attribute all health changes solely to PHC, notable achievements include:

  • Preventive Focus

    Shift from curative to preventive programmes reduced mortality and morbidity.

  • Key Programmes

    Introduction of KEPI (Immunisation), Environmental Health, Nutrition, MCH/FP, and Disease Control.

  • Disease Reduction

    Decrease in childhood diseases (polio, measles, tetanus) due to expanded immunisation.

  • Equity

    Promoted more equitable distribution of health resources and reduced the gap in access to care.

Other Success Features

  • Focus on community level care and local workers.
  • Integration of traditional methods (e.g., TBAs).
  • Emphasis on health education and hygiene.
  • Decentralisation of planning to identify major local health problems.
02

‚ö†Ô∏è Challenges Faced

Despite progress, implementation gaps remain. Major challenges include:

‚ñ∂ Morbidity & Mortality / Disease Burden
  • Preventable diseases (diarrhoea, malaria) still cause high under-five mortality (36%).
  • One in nine children dies before first birthday.
  • HIV/AIDS/TB burden remains high.
‚ñ∂ Resource Allocation & Curative Services

Curative services consume ~70% of the health budget, mostly in urban tertiary facilities, undermining equity.

‚ñ∂ Infrastructure (Water & Sanitation)

55% of Kenyans lack access to safe water and sanitation, increasing risk of communicable diseases.

‚ñ∂ Operational Challenges
  • Guidelines: Lack of clear referral procedures.
  • Sustainability: High dependence on donor support (e.g., for KEPI).
  • Integration: Misunderstanding PHC as just "primary level" care rather than an integrated approach.
  • Other: Drug shortages, staff salary delays, poor supervision.
03

üöÄ The Way Forward

The government is implementing the National Health Sector Strategic Plan (NHSSP) to address these constraints through decentralisation and reform. At the facility level, you can improve PHC implementation by:

  • Rational Resource Use

    Using drugs, time, and funds effectively (e.g., correct diagnosis and prescribing).

  • Continuous Learning

    Updating knowledge and skills on new diseases (e.g., drug-resistant malaria) and policies.

  • Advocacy

    Advocating for policy change and good governance.

  • Surveillance

    Effective disease surveillance and timely reporting.
