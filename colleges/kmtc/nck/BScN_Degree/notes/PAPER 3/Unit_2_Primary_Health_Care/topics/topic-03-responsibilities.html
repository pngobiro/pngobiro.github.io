Responsibilities of Key Implementers

üìö Roles of government, community, and NGOs in PHC implementation
‚è±Ô∏è 15 min read
üìù Quiz Available

Implementation of PHC requires coordinated efforts from various levels of government, the community, and non-governmental organizations. Each plays a distinct role in ensuring the success of health care delivery.

01

üèõÔ∏è National Level Responsibilities

The government at the national level ensures a consistent policy and strategy base for PHC activities throughout the country. Responsibilities include:

  • Policy & Coordination: Provide overall coordination and ensure collaboration among government sectors (intersectoral approach).
  • Resource Mobilization: Avail resources from other sectors, NGOs, and international sources.
  • Technical Support: Provide technical and financial support for PHC activities.
  • Guidelines: Establish guidelines for implementation and training.
  • Monitoring: Maintain a database on PHC development and review activities.
02

üó∫Ô∏è Provincial & District Levels

Provincial Level

Provides technical support in planning and management of PHC at the district level. The Provincial Health Management Team is responsible for all PHC activities.

District Level

The government identifies existing health problems, resources, and prepares coordinated plans. Key bodies include:

  • District Development Committee (DDC):

    Chaired by District Commissioner. Responsible for planning and coordination of all development work.

  • District Health Committee:

    Sub-committee of DDC. Discusses and plans PHC activities, advises DDC.

  • District Health Management Team (DHMT):

    Responsible for planning and implementation of all health services. Provides technical advice to DDC.

03

üèòÔ∏è Divisional & Community Levels

Divisional Level

Implemented through the Division Development Committee, often delegated to a PHC Coordinating Team.

DDC Composition: District Officer, Divisional heads, Chiefs, Political leaders, Religious leaders, Self-help group leaders.

Community Level

Village Health Development Committee Responsibilities:

  • Identify health problems and set priorities.
  • Identify community resources.
  • Select and supervise Community Health Workers (CHWs).
  • Provide communication channel to division level.
  • Initiate income generating activities.

Households

Recognized as key units. Responsibilities include recognizing ill health in time, decision making, and administering simple, safe medication.

04

ü§ù Non-Governmental Organisations (NGOs)

NGOs have been actively involved in developing Community Based Health Care (CBHC) projects since the mid 1970s. They collaborate with the Ministry of Health to formulate guidelines and implement projects.

Active NGOs in Kenya include:

  • AMREF (African Medical and Research Foundation)
  • Aga Khan Health Services
  • CHAK (Christian Health Association of Kenya)
  • Kenya Red Cross Society
  • Action Aid - Kenya
  • Catholic Relief Services
  • Family Planning Association of Kenya
  • Institute of Cultural Affairs
  • NCCK (National Christian Churches of Kenya)
  • The Undugu Society
  • World Vision
