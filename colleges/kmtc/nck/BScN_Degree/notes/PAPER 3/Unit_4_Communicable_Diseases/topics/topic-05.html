Faecal-oral Diseases

üìö Gastrointestinal Infections
‚è±Ô∏è 40 min read
01

Introduction

Faecal-oral diseases are transmitted when pathogens excreted in stools enter the mouth of a susceptible person via contaminated water, food, flies, or fingers (The 4-F Connection).

02

Enteric Fevers (Typhoid)

Cause: Salmonella typhi. Humans are the only reservoir.

Stages of Typhoid:

  • Week 1: Gradual fever (step-ladder), headache, malaise, nosebleeds.
  • Week 2: High fever, bradycardia (slow pulse relative to fever), "pea-soup" diarrhoea, mental confusion.
  • Week 3: Complications! Intestinal perforation, haemorrhage, toxaemia.
  • Week 4: Convalescence (recovery) or death.

Diagnosis: Blood culture (Week 1), Widal test (Week 2+), Stool culture.

Treatment: Ciprofloxacin or Norfloxacin. Fluid replacement.

03

Cholera

Cause: Vibrio cholerae (El Tor strain in EA). Characterised by massive watery diarrhoea ("Rice-water stool").

Clinical Phases:

  • Evacuation: Sudden profuse painless diarrhoea and vomiting.
  • Collapse: Dehydration, cold skin, weak pulse, shock.
  • Recovery: With prompt rehydration.
üíß

Critical Management

REHYDRATION IS LIFE-SAVING. Use IV fluids for shock and ORS for maintenance. Tetracycline speeds recovery.

04

Dysentery & Protozoa

1. Bacillary Dysentery (Shigellosis)

Signs: Blood and mucus in stool, severe abdominal cramps (colic), tenesmus (urge to defecate).

Management: Ciprofloxacin, Rehydration.

2. Amoebiasis

Cause: Entamoeba histolytica. Can cause "bottle-shaped" ulcers in the colon or liver abscess.

Treatment: Metronidazole (Flagyl).

3. Giardiasis

Signs: Offensive, frothy, yellowish diarrhoea; bloating. Causes malabsorption.

Treatment: Metronidazole or Tinidazole.

05

Viral Hepatitis

  • Hepatitis A & E: Faecal-oral transmission (Infectious Hepatitis). Self-limiting.
  • Hepatitis B, C, D: Blood/body fluid transmission (Serum Hepatitis). Risk of Cirrhosis and Liver Cancer.

Symptoms: Pre-icteric (fever, malaise) followed by Icteric (Jaundice, dark urine, pale stools).

Prevention: Safe water/sanitation (A/E); Screening blood, vaccines, sterile needles (B/C/D).

06

The 4-F Connection

Prevention relies on breaking the connection between faeces and the mouth:

  • Safe water (boil or chlorinate).
  • Pit latrines (proper disposal).
  • Hand washing with soap.
  • Food hygiene (protect from flies).
  • Food handler screening.
