Introduction

Vector-borne diseases are transmitted by invertebrates (vectors), mostly insects like mosquitoes, flies, and ticks. The organism usually undergoes development inside the vector (extrinsic incubation).

02

Malaria

Cause: Plasmodium falciparum (98% in Kenya), P. malariae, P. ovale, P. vivax.

Vector: Female Anopheles mosquito.

Life Cycle

  • Asexual (Human): Sporozoites injected -> Liver (Schizonts) -> Merozoites -> Red Blood Cells (Trophozoites) -> Bursting (Fever).
  • Sexual (Mosquito): Gametocytes ingested -> Ookinete -> Oocyst -> Sporozoites in salivary glands.

Clinical Stages

  • Cold Stage: Shivering, 15-60 mins.
  • Hot Stage: High fever (40-41¬∞C), headache, vomiting, 2-6 hours.
  • Sweating Stage: Fever drops, profuse sweating, 2-4 hours.

Management

Uncomplicated: AL (Artemether-Lumefantrine) is standard. Paracetamol for fever.

Severe/Complicated: Parenteral Artesunate or Quinine.

Prevention: Insecticide-treated nets (ITNs), indoor residual spraying, clearing breeding sites.

03

Filariasis (Elephantiasis)

Cause: Wuchereria bancrofti. Affects lymphatic system.

Stages

  • Acute: Fever, lymphangitis (inflamed vessels).
  • Chronic: Lymphoedema, Elephantiasis, Hydrocele.

Treatment: Diethylcarbamazine (DEC).

04

Yellow Fever

Vector: Aedes aegypti.

Clinical: Fever, jaundice (yellow eyes), bleeding tendencies (haemorrhagic), renal failure.

Prevention: Yellow Fever Vaccine (mandatory for travelers), vector control.

05

Trypanosomiasis (Sleeping Sickness)

Vector: Tsetse fly (Glossina).

Forms:

  • T.b. rhodesiense (East Africa): Acute, rapid. Reservoir: Antelope.
  • T.b. gambiense (West/Central Africa): Chronic.

Signs: Chancre at bite site, Winterbottom's sign (swollen neck nodes), sleeping during day/restless at night (CNS stage).

06

Schistosomiasis (Bilharzia)

Transmission: Fresh water with specific snails. Cercariae penetrate skin.

Types:

  • S. mansoni: Intestinal (bloody stool). Vector: Biomphalaria.
  • S. haematobium: Urinary (blood in urine). Vector: Bulinus.

Treatment: Praziquantel.

07

Plague

Cause: Yersinia pestis. Vector: Rat flea (Xenopsylla cheopis).

Types: Bubonic (swollen lymph nodes/buboes), Pneumonic (lungs, airborne), Septicaemic.

Management: Streptomycin, Tetracycline. Isolation is mandatory.

08

Leishmaniasis (Kala Azar)

Vector: Sandfly (Phlebotomus).

Types:

  • Visceral (Kala Azar): Fever, weight loss, enlarged spleen/liver. High mortality if untreated.
  • Cutaneous: Skin ulcers.

Treatment: Sodium stibogluconate.
