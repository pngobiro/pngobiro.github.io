Overview

Contact diseases are transmitted through direct skin-to-skin contact or indirectly via contaminated objects (fomites) like clothing or bedding. They often occur in clusters (schools, households).

Risk Factors

  • Close personal contact
  • Overcrowding (slums, institutions)
  • Poor personal hygiene
  • Water scarcity
02

Scabies

Cause: Sarcoptes scabiei (itch mite). The female mite burrows into the skin to lay eggs.

Clinical Picture: Intense itching (worse at night), rash in skin folds (fingers, elbows, buttocks). Secondary bacterial infection is common due to scratching.

Management

  • Treat everyone: Treat the patient and all family members simultaneously.
  • Hygiene: Warm bath and scrub.
  • Medication: Apply 10% Benzyl Benzoate Emulsion (BBE) from neck to toe. Leave for 24 hours.
  • Repeat: Repeat treatment after 4-7 days to kill newly hatched larvae.
  • Laundry: Wash all clothes and bedding in hot water.
03

Dermatomycosis (Fungal Infections)

1. Ringworm (Tinea)

Named by body part affected:

  • Tinea capitis: Scalp (common in children).
  • Tinea corporis: Body (ring-shaped lesions).
  • Tinea pedis: Feet (Athlete's foot).

Treatment: Whitefield's ointment, keep dry and clean.

2. Candidiasis (Yeast)

Manifests as Oral Thrush, Vulvo-vaginitis, or Intertrigo (in skin folds).

Treatment: Gentian Violet paint (GV), Nystatin.

04

Trachoma

Cause: Chlamydia trachomatis. A major cause of blindness in water-scarce areas.

Transmission: Flies, fingers, and direct contact with eye discharge.

Stages of Trachoma

Stage Description
1. Early Follicles inside upper eyelid.
2. Pannus Blood vessels grow into the cornea.
3. Scarring White scars form on conjunctiva.
4. Complications Entropion: Eyelid turns inward.
Trichiasis: Eyelashes rub cornea -> Blindness.

Prevention Strategy: SAFE

  • Surgery: For entropion.
  • Antibiotics: Tetracycline eye ointment (3% for 6 months).
  • Facial Cleanliness: Washing faces to remove discharge.
  • Environmental Change: Water supply, latrines, fly control.
