Control Methods

The goal of control is to tip the balance against the disease agent. This is achieved by:

  1. Attacking the Source: Targeting the infected person, animal, or reservoir.
  2. Interrupting Transmission: Blocking the route the organism travels.
  3. Protecting the Host: Increasing resistance of the susceptible person.
02

Specific Strategies

1. Attacking the Source

  • Treatment: Curing infected persons (and carriers) to stop them from shedding the agent.
  • Isolation: Separating infected persons (e.g., Ebola, Marburg).
  • Notification: Informing health authorities to monitor and respond.
  • Animal Control: Treating sick cattle, immunizing dogs (rabies), or destroying reservoirs (rats for plague).

2. Interrupting Transmission

Breaking the chain of spread through environmental and hygiene measures:

  • Personal hygiene and sanitation.
  • Safe water supply.
  • Vector control (mosquitoes, flies).
  • Food hygiene and inspection.
  • Sterilisation of medical equipment (HIV, Hepatitis B).

3. Protecting the Host

Making the susceptible person resistant or less accessible to the agent.

  • Immunisation: Vaccines (KEPI) to build immunity.
  • Chemoprophylaxis: Preventive drugs (e.g., for malaria, cholera contacts).
  • Barriers: Bed nets, gloves, shoes.
  • General Health: Nutrition, housing, ventilation.
03

Application Levels

Level Responsibilities
Individual / Village Hygiene, nutrition, latrine use, bed nets, abstinence/condoms, completing immunisation.
Health Centre Vector control, water protection, food inspection, health education (IEC), notification.
District / National Mass campaigns, statistics, research, emergency response, staff training.

Notifiable Diseases

You must report these immediately: Plague, Cholera, Yellow Fever, Polio, Measles, Rabies, Anthrax, Tuberculosis, Typhoid.
