Helminthic Diseases

üìö Intestinal Worms
‚è±Ô∏è 30 min read
01

Introduction

Helminthic diseases are caused by parasitic worms. They are common in low-income areas due to poor human waste disposal. They are divided into Nematodes (cylindrical) and Flatworms (segmented).

02

Nematodes (Round-shaped)

1. Threadworm (Pinworm)

Cause: Enterobius vermicularis. Common in school children.

Signs: Intense perianal itching (at night), restlessness.

Cycle: Autoinfection (anus-to-mouth via fingers).

2. Roundworm (Ascariasis)

Cause: Ascaris lumbricoides. Largest intestinal worm.

Cycle: Soil-transmitted. Eggs ingested via contaminated food/hands -> Lungs -> Throat -> Swallowed -> Gut.

Complications: Intestinal obstruction, malnutrition.

3. Hookworm

Cause: Necator americanus. Sucks blood from intestinal walls.

Transmission: Larvae in moist soil penetrate bare skin (feet).

Hallmark: Severe Iron Deficiency Anaemia (pallor, weakness).

03

Flatworms (Segmented)

1. Tapeworm (Taeniasis)

  • Beef Tapeworm (T. saginata): Ingestion of raw/undercooked beef with cysts.
  • Pork Tapeworm (T. solium): Dangerous! Larvae can migrate to brain (Epilepsy) or skeletal muscles.

Treatment: Niclosamide (chewed) or Praziquantel.

2. Hydatid Disease

Cause: Echinococcus granulosis (dog tapeworm).

Transmission: Accidental ingestion of eggs from dog faeces. Forms large cysts in liver or lungs.

Management: Albendazole, Surgery, or PAIR procedure.

04

General Prevention

  • Proper latrine use (Stop soil contamination).
  • Hand washing before food and after toilet.
  • Thorough cooking of meat (beef, pork).
  • Meat inspection at slaughterhouses.
  • Wearing shoes (prevent hookworm).
  • Deworming domestic dogs (prevent hydatid).
