Introduction

Zoonotic diseases are infections transmitted between vertebrate animals and humans. Humans are often the final (accidental) host.

Common Transmission Routes

  • Ingestion: Consuming infected meat or unpasteurised milk (Anthrax, Brucellosis).
  • Bites: Saliva from an infected animal (Rabies).
  • Direct Contact: Handling skins, hides, or carcasses (Cutaneous Anthrax).
  • Vectors: Ticks, fleas, or flies (Plague, Trypanosomiasis).
02

Anthrax

Cause: Bacillus anthracis (spore-forming bacilli). Primarily a disease of herbivores.

Forms in Humans:

  • Cutaneous (Skin): Most common. "Malignant pustule" with a black necrotic centre. Usually painless.
  • Gastrointestinal: From eating meat of dead animals. High mortality.
  • Inhalational (Lungs): From handling wool/hides. High mortality.

Management: Penicillin. Ciprofloxacin is also effective.

Prevention: Inspecting meat, vaccinating cattle, proper carcass disposal (burn or bury deep with lime).

03

Rabies

Cause: Rabies virus (Lyssavirus). Transmitted via saliva of a rabid animal (dog, cat, jackal).

üíÄ

100% Fatality

Once clinical signs appear, death is inevitable. Immediate post-bite prophylaxis is the only hope.

Clinical Phases

  • Prodromal: Pain at bite site, irritability, headache.
  • Furious Rabies: Hydrophobia (fear of water due to pharyngeal spasms), aerophobia, convulsions, mania.
  • Paralytic Rabies: Coma and death.

Post-Bite Management

  1. Flush wound immediately with soap and plenty of running water for 15 mins.
  2. Disinfect with iodine/spirit. DO NOT STITCH the wound immediately.
  3. Administer Rabies Vaccine (HDCV) on days 0, 3, 7, 14, 28.
  4. Give Tetanus Toxoid.
04

Brucellosis (Malta Fever)

Cause: Brucella species (from cattle, goats, pigs).

Transmission: Drinking unpasteurised milk or contact with animal tissues/fluids.

Signs: Undulant (wavy) fever, night sweats, joint pains, weight loss. Often chronic if untreated.

Diagnosis: Agglutination test (Widal-like) or bone marrow culture.

Treatment: Doxycycline and Rifampicin for 6 weeks.
