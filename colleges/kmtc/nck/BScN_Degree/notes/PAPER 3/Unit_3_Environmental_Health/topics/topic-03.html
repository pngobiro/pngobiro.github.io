Introduction & Sources

Food hygiene aims to prevent contamination at all stages: production, collection, storage, sale, preparation, and consumption.

Sources of Food

Sources include rearing, farming, and slaughter. All sources must comply with regulations (e.g., Meat Control Act) to ensure safety. Contamination can occur from excreta, dirty fingers, flies, pesticides, or infected animals.

02

Food Storage

  • Dry Foods (Cereals)

    Store in dry, airy conditions in improved granaries.

  • Bagged Foods

    Store on raised shelves at least 18 inches off the ground to allow cleaning and pest inspection.

  • Perishable Foods

    Meat, milk, fish should be refrigerated to inhibit bacterial growth.

03

Food Preservation

Preservation prolongs shelf-life, ensures safety, and conserves food for scarcity.

Principles

  1. Heat Treatment: Destroy organisms (e.g., Cooking, Pasteurization).
  2. Cold Treatment: Inhibit micro-organisms (e.g., Freezing).

Methods

  • Cooking: Improves palatability and kills microbes, but overcooking can destroy nutrients (e.g., Vitamin C).
  • Blanching: Immersion in boiling water/steam (70-100¬∞C) for 2-10 mins. Inactivates enzymes before freezing/drying.
  • Pasteurisation: Heating below 100¬∞C. Kills pathogens in milk while maintaining quality.
  • Sterilisation: Heating above 100¬∞C to kill all spores.
  • Canning: Heating then sealing in sterile containers.
  • Freezing: Deep freezing at 0-4¬∞C retains texture and nutrients.
  • Salting/Sugaring: Dehydrates cells via osmosis (e.g., jams, ham).
  • Smoking/Drying: Removes moisture, preventing bacterial growth (e.g., fish, meat, grains).
04

Food Safety & Hygiene

Personal Hygiene for Food Handlers

  • Health: No respiratory infections, cuts, or diarrhea.
  • Clothing: Clean, washable outer garments/aprons.
  • Head Covering: Caps/nets to cover hair.
  • Habits: Daily baths, short clean nails, washing hands before handling food.

Regulations (Public Health Act Cap 242)

Part X (10): Protects foodstuffs, regulates storage buildings, prohibits sleeping in kitchens.

Part XI (11): Deals with milk, meat, and other foods. Authorizes inspection, seizure, and disposal of unwholesome food.

Inspections cover:

  • Meat: Slaughterhouses and butcheries.
  • Milk: Dairy shops, equipment cleanliness, healthy cows (TB-free).
  • Markets & Hotels: Hygiene standards, licenses, medical exams for handlers.
