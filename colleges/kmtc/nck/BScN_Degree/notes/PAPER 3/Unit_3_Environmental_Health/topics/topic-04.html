01

Introduction

Good housing provides shelter and protection from environmental hazards. Poor housing conditions are directly linked to specific health risks.

Housing Condition Health Risk
Overcrowding / Poor Ventilation Airborne droplet infections (TB, Influenza)
Unscreened Windows Malaria (Mosquito entry)
Cooking fires on floor Accidents, burns, respiratory issues
Earth walls / Dirty floors Breeding of flies, bedbugs, fleas
02

Types of Housing

  • Permanent Houses

    Stone foundation, cemented floor, plastered walls, iron/tile roof. Easy to clean but expensive.

  • Semi-Permanent Houses

    Cemented floor, iron sheet/timber walls, iron roof. Common in rural areas. Needs termite protection.

  • Temporary Houses

    Earth floor, mud/grass/cardboard walls & roof. Poor privacy, fire hazard, health risk. Should be discouraged.

03

Criteria for Adequate Housing

1. Biological Criteria

Minimizes disease transmission via good water supply, food storage, washing facilities, and refuse disposal.

2. Physical Criteria

Safe from accidents (burns, falls) and air pollution.

3. Social Criteria

Provides privacy for adults and a suitable setting for raising children.

Characteristics of a Good House

  • Lighting: Natural (sunlight kills microbes) and artificial (for night).
  • Ventilation: Continuous air movement without draughts.
  • Room Separation: Separate rooms for adults/children, animals, and food.
  • Structure: Cemented floors, plastered walls, rodent-proof.
04

Building Sites

Factors for a suitable site:

  • Dry, sunny, exposed to free air.
  • Hygienic surroundings.
  • Away from noise (factories, traffic).
  • High ground (prevents stagnation/mosquitoes).
05

Community Involvement

Improving housing requires behavior change. Steps for sensitisation:

  1. Meet Health Team: Plan and involve government officers (Housing Act).
  2. Sensitise at Facility: Share messages with patients/clients.
  3. Meet Leaders: Chiefs, opinion leaders, women/youth groups.
  4. Identify Improvements: Use local resources (manpower, materials).
  5. Evaluate: Check progress after ~6 months via surveys/reports.
