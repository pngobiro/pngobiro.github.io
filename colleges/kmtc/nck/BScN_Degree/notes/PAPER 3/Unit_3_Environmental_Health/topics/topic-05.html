01

Introduction

Vector: An organism (usually an insect) that transmits a pathogen from a source to a host.
Pest: An organism that adversely affects human health or the economy (e.g., rats, weevils).

Transmission Modes

  • Mechanical: Carries pathogen on body/limbs (e.g., Housefly).
  • Biological: Pathogen develops inside the vector (e.g., Malaria in Mosquito).
  • Contamination: Infected faeces rubbed into skin/wounds (e.g., Louse-borne relapsing fever).
  • Ingestion: Host eats the vector (e.g., Guinea worm).

03

Control Methods

Personal Hygiene

Hand washing, short nails, daily baths. Wearing shoes prevents jiggers/hookworms.

Environmental Hygiene

  • Drainage: Remove stagnant water (mosquitoes). Fill holes/ditches.
  • Clear Vegetation: Around buildings and water banks.
  • Waste Disposal: Proper disposal of refuse and excreta.
  • Housing: Cemented floors (fleas), plastered walls (bedbugs), rat-proofing, screening windows.

Chemical Control

Use of Insecticides (sprays, powders), Rodenticides (rat poison), and Molluscides (snails). Always follow agricultural guidelines for safe use.

04

Community Involvement

To ensure sustainability:

  1. Identify Resources: Work with agricultural officers and local chemists.
  2. Education: Use Chief's barazas to demonstrate safe use of agro-chemicals.
  3. Cooperation: Utilize co-operative societies for distribution.
