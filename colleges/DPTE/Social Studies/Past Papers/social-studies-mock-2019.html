Question 1

(a) What is dual citizenship? (2 marks)

(b) List five roles of citizens in a democratic society. (5 marks)

(c) Give four functions of a speaker of the county assembly. (4 marks)

(d) State four grounds in which a county governor can be impeached by members of the county assembly. (4 marks)

(e) Identify five independent commissions in Kenya. (4 marks)

Answer

(a) Dual citizenship is a status whereby a person who is a citizen of a country by birth acquires the citizenship of another country, holding both simultaneously.

(b) Five roles of citizens in a democratic society are:

  • Participating in elections by voting.
  • Vying for elective positions.
  • Paying taxes to fund government services.
  • Abiding by the law to maintain order.
  • Involvement in community development activities.

(c) Four functions of a speaker of the county assembly include:

  • Presiding over all house proceedings.
  • Chairing parliamentary debates.
  • Receiving parliamentary visitors to the assembly.
  • Attending national functions on behalf of the assembly.
  • Representing the county assembly in international forums.

(d) Four grounds for impeachment of a county governor are:

  • Gross misconduct.
  • Misappropriation of funds or corruption.
  • Incapacitation due to physical or mental illness.
  • Violation of the constitution.

(e) Five independent commissions in Kenya include:

  • Judicial Service Commission (JSC)
  • Teachers Service Commission (TSC)
  • Parliamentary Service Commission (PSC)
  • National Police Service Commission (NPSC)
  • National Land Commission (NLC)
  • Kenya National Human Rights and Equality Commission (KNEC)
  • Independent Electoral and Boundaries Commission (IEBC)
  • Commission on Revenue Allocation (CRA)
  • Salaries and Remuneration Commission (SRC)

Question 2

You have decided to use a resource person to teach the topic “Forestry in Kenya" to a standard five class.

(a) What preparations would you make for the lesson? (5 marks)

(b) State five qualities that you would look for when identifying a suitable resource person. (5 marks)

(c) State five follow-up activities you would carry out after the resource person's visit. (5 marks)

(d) Give five problems facing forests in Kenya. (5 marks)

Answer

(a) Preparations for the lesson would include:

  • Inform the school administration and seek permission.
  • Contact the resource person in advance to confirm availability.
  • Guide the resource person on the specific topics to be covered.
  • Liaise with the administration regarding any expenses for the resource person.
  • Inform the learners about the upcoming visit to create anticipation.
  • Prepare learners by advising them to read on the topic and formulate questions.
  • Avail necessary teaching and learning resources for the session.

(b) Five qualities to look for in a resource person are:

  • Knowledgeable in the subject area (Forestry).
  • Good communication skills to engage young learners.
  • Presentable and acts as a positive role model.
  • Morally upright and respectable.
  • Non-controversial and objective in their presentation.

(c) Five follow-up activities after the visit:

  • Conduct a class discussion to review what was learned.
  • Ask learners to draw pictures related to forestry.
  • Hold a question and answer session to clarify doubts.
  • Have learners write reports summarizing the visit.
  • Engage learners in role-playing activities related to conservation.

(d) Five problems facing forests in Kenya:

  • Over-exploitation for timber and charcoal.
  • Frequent forest fires, often caused by human activity.
  • Excisions/Degazettement of forest reserves for settlement or agriculture.
  • Pollution from industrial and agricultural activities.
  • Encroachment by communities for farming and settlement.

Question 3

You are planning to teach the topic 'the interaction of people in our county' using discussion method in class IV.

(a) State two objectives you intend to achieve by the end of the topic. (2 marks)

(b) During group discussion you realized that some learners had communication disorders. How would you assist them as a teacher during the lesson? (6 marks)

(c) State four advantages of using discussion method in teaching and learning of social studies. (4 marks)

(d) Explain four ways how people interact in our county to your class. (8 marks)

Answer

(a) By the end of the lesson, the pupil should be able to:

  • State/list the different ethnic or social groups found in our county.
  • Name activities that favour positive interaction among the people in the county.

(b) To assist learners with communication disorders, a teacher should:

  • Avoid lengthy explanations and use simple language.
  • Give clear, concise instructions and use visual aids/illustrations.
  • Give them enough time to speak without interruption.
  • Speak clearly and calmly to them.
  • Involve them in discussions with topics that will provoke them to talk and share their views.
  • Praise their efforts to build their confidence.

(c) Four advantages of the discussion method are:

  • It arouses the interest and curiosity of the learners.
  • It helps to mold positive attitudes and values in learners.
  • It boosts communication skills and confidence.
  • It improves the teacher-pupil relationship.

(d) People in a county interact in various ways, including:

  • Trade: People meet in markets and business centers to buy and sell goods.
  • Sports & Games: Sporting events and recreational activities bring people from different backgrounds together.
  • Ceremonies: Events like marriages and burials are communal activities that foster interaction.
  • Education: Children from various communities interact in schools, and parents meet during school events.
  • Health Centres: People meet at hospitals and clinics when seeking medical services.
  • Agricultural Shows: These events provide a platform for farmers and traders to interact and share ideas.

Question 4

You intend to take class eight pupils to a sugar factory in Mumias to find out how sugar is processed.

(a) State five activities you would ask pupils to carry out in preparation for the field study. (5 marks)

(b) Apart from tape recording, identify other four methods that learners would use to record data. (4 marks)

(c) While in the factory, a pupil may ask you the conditions necessary for growing sugar cane. What answers would you give? (5 marks)

(d) State three reasons why the teacher should carry out follow up activities after the study. (3 marks)

(e) Give three problems facing sugar cane growing in Kenya. (3 marks)

Answer

(a) Five preparatory activities for pupils:

  1. Discuss the topic of study with other members of the class to define objectives.
  2. Read ahead for background information on the topic of study.
  3. Study the maps of the area to be visited.
  4. Participate in the preparation of a questionnaire.
  5. Assemble the material and equipment required for the field study (e.g., notebooks, pens).

(b) Four other methods of data recording:

  1. Drawing sketches and maps.
  2. Filling in a questionnaire.
  3. Note-taking.
  4. Taking photographs or videos.
  5. Collecting samples (if permissible).

(c) Conditions necessary for growing sugar cane:

  • High temperatures, typically between 21°C - 27°C.
  • High and well-distributed rainfall (1200-1500mm annually).
  • A dry and sunny period during harvesting to increase sugar accumulation.
  • Fertile and well-drained soils.
  • Undulating or flat land to allow for machinery use.
  • Altitude between sea level and 1600m.

(d) Three reasons for follow-up activities:

  1. Helps learners to reflect on and consolidate the gains of the field study.
  2. It reinforces the concepts learned during the visit.
  3. It helps to correct any errors or misconceptions that may have occurred.

(e) Three problems facing sugar cane growing in Kenya:

  1. Low production capacity due to use of outdated technology.
  2. Competition from cheap imported sugar from COMESA and other countries.
  3. Pests (e.g., termites) and diseases (e.g., sugarcane mosaic) that lower yields.
  4. Mismanagement of some sugar factories leading to delayed payments and closures.

Question 1

Note: The questions below refer to a Map of Eastern Africa which was part of the original exam paper but is not available in this document. The answers are provided based on the original map.

(a) (i) Name the inland fishing lake marked P. (1 mark)

(a) (ii) Which mineral is mined in the area marked Q? (1 mark)

(a) (iii) Identify the community that migrated to their current settlement area through the route marked R. (1 mark)

(a) (iv) Name two Cushitic communities that settled in the area marked S during the pre-colonial period. (2 marks)

(b) State four effects of the rotation of the earth. (4 marks)

(c) It is 9.00 p.m. at Ruwenzori which is 30°E. What is the time at Accra (0°)? Show your calculations. (5 marks)

(d) Explain three effects of relief and drainage on the economic activities of the people of Africa. (6 marks)

Answer

(a) (i) Lake P is Malawi.

(a) (ii) The mineral mined in area Q is Salt.

(a) (iii) The community is the Western Bantu.

(a) (iv) Two Cushitic communities are Somali and Rendille (also Burji, Orma, Borana, Gabra).

(b) Four effects of the Earth's rotation:

  • It causes day and night.
  • It causes the daily rise and fall of tides in oceans.
  • It causes the deflection of winds and ocean currents (Coriolis effect).
  • It creates a time difference of one hour for every 15° of longitude.

(c) Time calculation:

Find the difference in longitude:
30°E - 0° = 30°

Convert longitude difference to time:
1° = 4 minutes
30° = 30 x 4 = 120 minutes

Convert minutes to hours:
120 minutes / 60 = 2 hours

Determine if time is earlier or later:
Accra (0°) is to the west of Ruwenzori (30°E), so its time is behind. We subtract the time difference.

Calculate the final time:
9:00 p.m. - 2 hours = 7:00 p.m.

(d) Three effects of relief and drainage on economic activities:

  • Mountainous areas with high rainfall (windward sides) support crop farming, while leeward sides with low rainfall are suitable for livestock rearing.
  • Rivers and waterfalls are harnessed for Hydro-Electric Power (HEP) generation.
  • Scenic relief features like mountains, valleys, and lakes attract tourists, generating income.
  • Mountain forests provide timber and other forest products (lumbering).
  • Steep slopes make the construction of roads and railways expensive, hindering transport and communication.

Question 2

(a) (i) Differentiate between weather and climate. (4 marks)

(a) (ii) Other than Humidity and air pressure, give three elements of weather. (3 marks)

(b) (i) Differentiate between Humidity and Air pressure. (2 marks)

(b) (ii) Name the scientific instruments used to measure Humidity and Air pressure. (2 marks)

(c) Apart from Mediterranean type of climate, name three other climatic regions of Africa North of the equator. (3 marks)

Answer

(a) (i)
Weather: Refers to the day-to-day atmospheric conditions of a place at a particular time.
Climate: Refers to the average weather conditions of a place recorded over a long period of time, usually 30-35 years.

(a) (ii) Three other elements of weather are:

  • Precipitation (rainfall)
  • Wind (speed and direction)
  • Temperature
  • Sunshine
  • Cloud cover

(b) (i)
Humidity: The amount of water vapour present in the atmosphere.
Air Pressure: The weight exerted by the atmosphere on the surface of the earth.

(b) (ii)
Humidity: Hygrometer.
Air Pressure: Barometer.

(c) Three other climatic regions of Africa north of the equator:

  • Equatorial climate
  • Savannah (Tropical) climate
  • Tropical desert climate
  • Mountain climate

Question 3

(a) (i) Give two methods that were used by the European powers to acquire colonies in Africa. (2 marks)

(a) (ii) Identify four social factors that led to the scramble for Africa by the Europeans. (4 marks)

(b) State five grievances of the people of Ghana against their British colonizers. (5 marks)

(c) Outline five challenges faced by the Mau Mau freedom fighters during the struggle for independence in Kenya. (5 marks)

Answer

(a) (i) Two methods used to acquire colonies:

  • Military conquests.
  • Signing of treaties (often deceptive).
  • Using divide and rule tactics.

(a) (ii) Four social factors for the scramble for Africa:

  • The desire to spread Christianity through missionary work.
  • The need to find land to settle surplus population from Europe.
  • The belief in the need to spread Western education and "civilize" Africans.
  • The introduction of Western medicine to combat tropical diseases.

(b) Five grievances of the people of Ghana:

  • Widespread unemployment among the local population.
  • Discrimination in trade, favouring European merchants.
  • Racial discrimination in employment and provision of social services.
  • Poor working conditions and low wages for Africans.
  • Unfavourable land tenure system that led to land alienation.

(c) Five challenges faced by Mau Mau fighters:

  • Inferior weapons compared to the well-equipped European forces.
  • Harsh weather conditions (cold, rainy) and difficult terrain (forests, mountains).
  • Betrayal by collaborating chiefs and sympathisers of the white man.
  • Poor coordination among the many Mau Mau generals.
  • Inadequate food and supplies.

Question 4

(a) (i) Mention three main trading blocks in Africa. (3 marks)

(a) (ii) Identify three different business opportunities found in Kenya. (3 marks)

(b) Explain the importance of reducing industrial pollution in Kenya. (6 marks)

(c) Give four problems caused by the construction of the Volta river scheme in Ghana. (4 marks)

(d) State four problems experienced by the Volta river scheme in Ghana. (4 marks)

Answer

(a) (i) Three main trading blocks in Africa:

  • The East African Community (EAC)
  • The Common Market for Eastern and Southern Africa (COMESA)
  • Southern Africa Development Community (SADC)
  • Economic Community of West Africa (ECOWAS)

(a) (ii) Three business opportunities in Kenya:

  • Farming / Agribusiness
  • Manufacturing and processing
  • Transport and logistics
  • Tourism and hospitality

(b) Importance of reducing industrial pollution:

  • Ensures clean air, which reduces respiratory health problems.
  • Allows wasteland to be reclaimed for economic use like agriculture or settlement.
  • Protects aquatic life by ensuring clean water in rivers and lakes.
  • Reduces the negative effects of greenhouse gases and acid rain.
  • Leads to a more conducive and healthy living environment.

(c) Four problems caused by the construction of the Volta River scheme:

  • Over 80,000 people were displaced from their homes.
  • Disruption of road transport between the East and the West of Ghana.
  • Increase in water-borne diseases like bilharzia and malaria.
  • Destruction of natural habitats and ecosystems.

(d) Four problems experienced by the Volta River scheme:

  • Inadequate capital for maintenance and expansion.
  • Inadequate skilled personnel to manage the facility.
  • Siltation of the dam, reducing its capacity.
  • Frequent droughts affecting water levels in the Volta River.

Question 5

(a) (i) What is a forest? (2 marks)

(a) (ii) Define forestry. (2 marks)

(a) (iii) Identify two types of forests found in Kenya. (2 marks)

(b) State four social benefits of forests. (4 marks)

(c) State four ways of conserving forests in Kenya. (4 marks)

(d) Explain three problems facing forests in Kenya. (6 marks)

Answer

(a) (i) A forest is an extensive tree cover occupying a large area of land.

(a) (ii) Forestry is the art and science of planting, tending, managing, and exploiting forests and their products.

(a) (iii) Two types of forests in Kenya are Natural forests and Planted (exotic) forests.

(b) Four social benefits of forests:

  • Provide aesthetic value and scenic beauty.
  • Act as recreation sites for tourism and leisure.
  • Contain religious centres like shrines and sacred sites.
  • Source of medicinal plants and herbs.

(c) Four ways of conserving forests:

  • Afforestation (planting trees where none existed) and Re-afforestation (replanting cleared areas).
  • Agro-forestry (integrating trees with crops and livestock).
  • Use of alternative sources of fuel to reduce reliance on charcoal and firewood.
  • Establishing tree nurseries to provide seedlings.

(d) Three major problems facing forests in Kenya:

  • Human encroachment on forest: People clear forest land for settlement and agriculture, leading to deforestation.
  • Forest fires: Accidental or intentional fires destroy large areas of forest cover.
  • Indiscriminate tree felling: Illegal logging for timber, poles, and charcoal production depletes forest resources faster than they can be replenished.

Question 6

(a) State three basic rights of a child. (3 marks)

(b) (i) Other than children, give any two categories of persons needing special protection under the Kenyan constitution. (2 marks)

(b) (ii) Identify four efforts being made by the Kenyan government in protection of the best interest of the child. (4 marks)

(c) State five problems faced by the United Nations in the attempt to promote world peace. (5 marks)

(d) Explain three ways in which the United Nations has contributed in the maintenance of peace in the world. (6 marks)

Answer

(a) Three basic rights of a child:

  • Right to life.
  • Right to adequate food, proper clothing, and good shelter.
  • Right to good medical care.
  • Right to parental love and care.

(b) (i) Two categories of persons needing special protection:

  • Youth
  • Aged / elderly persons
  • Minority and marginalized groups
  • People with disabilities

(b) (ii) Four government efforts to protect children's interests:

  • Provision of free and compulsory basic education.
  • Conducting awareness campaigns on the rights of children.
  • Enacting legislation (e.g., The Children's Act) to protect children.
  • Providing free medical services for mothers and children (e.g., pre- and post-natal care).

(c) Five problems faced by the United Nations:

  • Inadequate funds to carry out its missions.
  • Regional conflicts and civil strife that are difficult to resolve.
  • Conflicting national interests of member states.
  • Lack of a standing army, relying on contributions from member states.
  • The Veto power held by permanent Security Council members, which can delay decision-making.

(d) Three ways the UN contributes to world peace:

  • Provision of peacekeeping missions in war-torn countries to enforce ceasefires and protect civilians.
  • Facilitating mediation forums and diplomatic negotiations to resolve conflicts peacefully.
  • Provision of relief food and humanitarian aid to populations affected by war and natural disasters.
  • Combating natural epidemics like drought and disease through its specialized agencies like the WHO.
