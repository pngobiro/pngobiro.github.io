Quiz: Topic 5 - Educational Administration

Test your knowledge on the concepts from Topic 5.

1. Which administrative record is specifically designed to contain remarks from school inspectors and a history of important events during the school year?

  • A) The Cash Book
  • B) The Admission Register
  • C) The Log Book
  • D) The Pupil's Attendance Register

Answer: C) The Log Book.

Explanation: The text describes the Log Book as being for "remarks of the school inspector or other important officers" and to "contain a complete record of the important events that occur during the session."

2. According to the text, what is a primary role of a school principal as an instructional leader?

  • A) To personally handle all student discipline cases.
  • B) To create a shared vision and clear goals for their schools.
  • C) To manage the school's payroll exclusively.
  • D) To teach a full schedule of classes.

Answer: B) To create a shared vision and clear goals for their schools.

Explanation: The text lists the first role of a school principal as "(1) To create a shared vision and clear goals for their schools and ensure continuous progress toward achieving the goals".

3. True or False: The TSC Code of Conduct states that a teacher's personal interests should always come before their official duties.

Answer: False.

Explanation: The Code of Conduct clearly states that "A teacher's assigned duties shall take precedence over all his or her other activities" and that a teacher "shall avoid being in a position in which his or her personal interests conflict with his or her duties."

4. A teacher leader who serves as a role model and advises new teachers about instruction, curriculum, and school procedures is fulfilling the role of a ________.

Answer: Mentor.

Explanation: The text defines the "Mentor" role for teacher leaders as serving as a role model and acclimating/advising new teachers on various aspects of the school and profession.

5. What is a key benefit of establishing consistent classroom routines and procedures?

  • A) It allows teachers to take longer breaks.
  • B) It eliminates the need for lesson planning.
  • C) It reduces classroom distractions and enhances behavior management.
  • D) It ensures all students get the same grade.

Answer: C) It reduces classroom distractions and enhances behavior management.

Explanation: The text lists several benefits of routines, including that they "Enhances behavior management" and "Reduces classroom distractions" by creating a predictable and streamlined environment.
