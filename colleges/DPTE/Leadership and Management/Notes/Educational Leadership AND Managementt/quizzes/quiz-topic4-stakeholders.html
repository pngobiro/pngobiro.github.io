Quiz: Topic 4 - Stakeholders in Administration of Basic Education

Test your knowledge on the concepts from Topic 4.

1. What are the dual purposes of the Kenya National Union of Teachers (KNUT) as described in the text?

  • A) To manage teacher salaries and run for political office.
  • B) To serve as both a trade union and a professional organisation.
  • C) To only provide legal representation and organize strikes.
  • D) To set the national curriculum and manage school infrastructure.

Answer: B) To serve as both a trade union and a professional organisation.

Explanation: The text states, "The KNUT was founded as both a trade union and professional organisation, to unite all teachers of Kenya and look after their welfare and professional interests."

2. Who are considered the primary stakeholders in education because the entire system is created for their learning?

  • A) Teachers
  • B) Parents
  • C) Students
  • D) School Administrators

Answer: C) Students.

Explanation: The text clearly states, "Students are the primary stakeholders in education. The primary purpose of why the school was created is for student learning."

3. True or False: According to the text, the business community's only stake in education is providing financial donations.

Answer: False.

Explanation: The text explains that the business community also has a stake in "ensuring that the schools are preparing students for the workforce" and often partners with schools to provide internships and jobs.

4. Fill in the blank: Teacher unions negotiate __________, benefits, and working conditions on behalf of their members.

Answer: salaries.

Explanation: The text explicitly states that unions "negotiate salaries, benefits, and working conditions on behalf of their members."

5. What is a key legal and financial responsibility of a school board?

  • A) To directly teach classes when there is a teacher shortage.
  • B) To ensure schools meet educational standards and use taxpayer money wisely.
  • C) To select the textbooks for every individual classroom.
  • D) To organize parent-teacher conferences.

Answer: B) To ensure schools meet educational standards and use taxpayer money wisely.

Explanation: The text lists a school board's responsibilities as having a "legal responsibility to ensure schools... are meeting educational standards" and a "financial responsibility to use taxpayer money wisely".
