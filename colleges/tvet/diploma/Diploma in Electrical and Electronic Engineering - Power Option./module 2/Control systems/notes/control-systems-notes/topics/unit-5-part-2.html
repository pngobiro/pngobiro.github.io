Unit-5: Time Domain Analysis of Control Systems

üìà Response of 1st order system when the input is unit step -

$$ \frac{C(s)}{R(s)}=\frac{1}{(S T+1)} $$
$$ C(s)=\frac{1}{(S T+1)} \cdot R(s) $$

For Unit Step,

$$ R(S)=\frac{1}{S} $$
$$ C(s)=\frac{1}{(S T+1)} \cdot \frac{1}{s} $$
$$ C(s)=\frac{1}{S}-\frac{1}{(S T+1)} $$

Now, the partial fraction of above equation will be:

$$ C(s)=\frac{1}{S}-\frac{T}{(S T+1)} = \frac{1}{S}-\frac{1}{(S+\frac{1}{T})} $$

Taking the inverse Laplace of above equation is:

$$ c(t) = \mathcal{L}^{-1}\{C(s)\} = \mathcal{L}^{-1}\left\{\frac{1}{S}\right\} - \mathcal{L}^{-1}\left\{\frac{1}{S+\frac{1}{T}}\right\} = 1 - e^{-t/T} $$

At $t=T$:

$$ 1-e^{-T/T} $$
$$ 1-e^{-1}=1-0.368=0.632 $$

Where T is known as time constant of the system and it is defined as the time required for the signal to attain 63.2 % of final or steady state value. Time constant means how fast the system reaches the final value. As smaller the time constant, as faster is the system response. If time constant is larger, system goes to move slowly.

2Ô∏è‚É£ Time response of second order system

$$ \frac{C(s)}{R(s)}=\frac{G(S)}{1+G(S) H(S)} $$
$$ G(S)=\frac{\omega_{n}^{2}}{s\left(s+2 \xi \omega_{n}\right)}, \quad H(S)=1 $$
$$ \frac{C(S)}{R(S)}=\frac{\omega_{n}^{2} / s\left(s+2 \xi \omega_{n}\right)}{1+\frac{\omega_{n}^{2}}{s\left(s+2 \xi \omega_{n}\right)}} $$
$$ \frac{C(S)}{R(S)}=\frac{\omega_{n}^{2}}{s^{2}+2 \xi \omega_{n} s+\omega_{n}^{2}} \quad \ldots \ldots . . . . . e q .1 $$

In the above transfer function, the power of 's' is two in the denominator. That is why the above transfer function is of a second order, and the system is said to be the second order system.

Time response of second order system with unit step

From equation 1

$$ C(S)=\frac{\omega_{n}^{2}}{S^{2}+2 \xi \omega_{n} S+\omega_{n}^{2}} \cdot R(S) $$

For unit step the input is

$$ R(S)=\frac{1}{s} $$
$$ C(S)=\frac{1}{s} \cdot \frac{\omega_{n}^{2}}{s^{2}+2 \xi \omega_{n} s+\omega_{n}^{2}} $$
$$ C(S)=\frac{1}{s} \cdot \frac{\omega_{n}^{2}}{\left(s^{2}+2 \xi \omega_{n} s+\xi^{2} \omega_{n}^{2}+\omega_{n}^{2}-\xi^{2} \omega_{n}^{2}\right)} $$
$$ =\frac{1}{s} \cdot \frac{\omega_{n}^{2}}{\left(s+\xi \omega_{n}\right)^{2}+\omega_{n}^{2}\left(1-\xi^{2}\right)} $$
$$ =\frac{\left(s+\xi \omega_{n}\right)^{2}+\omega_{n}^{2}\left(1-\xi^{2}\right)-\left(s+\xi \omega_{n}\right)^{2}+\omega_{n}^{2} \xi^{2}}{s\left\{\left(s+\xi \omega_{n}\right)^{2}+\omega_{n}^{2}\left(1-\xi^{2}\right)\right\}} $$
$$ =\frac{\left(s+\xi \omega_{n}\right)^{2}+\omega_{n}^{2}\left(1-\xi^{2}\right)-s^{2}-2 s \xi \omega_{n}-\omega_{n}^{2} \xi^{2}+\omega_{n}^{2} \xi^{2}}{s\left\{\left(s+\xi \omega_{n}\right)^{2}+\omega_{n}^{2}\left(1-\xi^{2}\right)\right\}} $$
$$ =\frac{\left(s+\xi \omega_{n}\right)^{2}+\omega_{n}^{2}\left(1-\xi^{2}\right)-s\left(s+2 \xi \omega_{n}\right)}{s\left\{\left(s+\xi \omega_{n}\right)^{2}+\omega_{n}^{2}\left(1-\xi^{2}\right)\right\}} $$
$$ =\frac{1}{s}-\frac{s+2 \xi \omega_{n}}{\left(s+\xi \omega_{n}\right)^{2}+\omega_{n}^{2}\left(1-\xi^{2}\right)} $$

Now putting, $\omega_{d}=\omega_{n} \sqrt{1-\xi^{2}}$

$$ =\frac{1}{s}-\frac{s+2 \xi \omega_{n}}{\left(s+\xi \omega_{n}\right)^{2}+\omega_{d}^{2}} $$
$$ =\frac{1}{s}-\frac{s+\xi \omega_{n}}{\left(s+\xi \omega_{n}\right)^{2}+\omega_{d}^{2}}-\frac{\xi \omega_{n}}{\left(s+\xi \omega_{n}\right)^{2}+\omega_{d}^{2}} $$
$$ =\frac{1}{s}-\frac{s+\xi \omega_{n}}{\left(s+\xi \omega_{n}\right)^{2}+\omega_{d}^{2}}-\frac{\xi \omega_{n}}{\omega_{d}} \cdot \frac{\omega_{d}}{\left(s+\xi \omega_{n}\right)^{2}+\omega_{d}^{2}} $$

Now taking the inverse Laplace of above equation

$$ \mathcal{L}^{-1}[C(s)] = \mathcal{L}^{-1}\left[\frac{1}{s}\right] - \mathcal{L}^{-1}\left[\frac{s+\xi \omega_{n}}{\left(s+\xi \omega_{n}\right)^{2}+\omega_{d}^{2}}\right] - \mathcal{L}^{-1}\left[\frac{\xi \omega_{n}}{\omega_{d}} \cdot \frac{\omega_{d}}{\left(s+\xi \omega_{n}\right)^{2}+\omega_{d}^{2}}\right] $$
$$ \therefore c(t)=1-e^{-\xi \omega_{n} t} \cdot \cos \omega_{d} t-\frac{\xi \omega_{n}}{\omega_{d}} \cdot e^{-\xi \omega_{n} t} \cdot \sin \omega_{d} t $$

$\because \mathcal{L}^{-1}\left[\frac{1}{s}\right]=1, \mathcal{L}^{-1}\left[\frac{s+\alpha}{(s+\alpha)^{2}+\omega^{2}}\right]=e^{-\alpha t} \cos \omega t, $

$\mathcal{L}^{-1}\left[\frac{\omega}{(s+\alpha)^{2}+\omega^{2}}\right]=e^{-\alpha t} \sin \omega t$

This equation can also be written as

$$ c(t)=1-e^{-\xi \omega_{n} t}\left(\cos \omega_{d} t+\frac{\xi}{\sqrt{1-\xi^{2}}} \cdot \sin \omega_{d} t\right) $$
$$ =1-\frac{e^{-\xi \omega_{n} t}}{\sqrt{1-\xi^{2}}}\left(\sqrt{1-\xi^{2}} \cos \omega_{d} t+\xi \cdot \sin \omega_{d} t\right) $$

[Let, $\xi=\cos \emptyset$, hence , $\sqrt{1-\xi^{2}}=\sin \emptyset$]

$$ \therefore \quad c(t)=1-\frac{e^{-\xi \omega_{n} t}}{\sqrt{1-\xi^{2}}}\left(\sin \emptyset \cos \omega_{d} t+\cos \emptyset \sin \omega_{d} t\right) $$
$$ =1-\frac{e^{-\xi \omega_{n} t}}{\sqrt{1-\xi^{2}}} \sin \left(\omega_{d} t+\emptyset\right) $$

The error signal is given by $e(t)=r(t)-c(t)$, and hence

$$ e(t)=\frac{e^{-\xi \omega_{n} t}}{\sqrt{1-\xi^{2}}} \sin \left(\omega_{d} t+\emptyset\right) $$

üìù Transient response specification of second order system

The performance of the control system are expressed in terms of transient response to a unit step input because it is easy to generate initial condition basically are zero.

Following are the common transient response characteristics:

  1. 1. Delay Time.
  2. 2. Rise Time.
  3. 3. Peak Time.
  4. 4. Maximum Peak.
  5. 5. Settling Time.
  6. 6. Steady State error.

Delay Time

The time required for the response to reach 50% of the final value in the first time is called the delay time.

Rise Time

The time required for response to rising from 10% to 90% of final value, for an overdamped system and 0 to 100% for an underdamped system is called the rise time of the system.

Peak Time

The time required for the response to reach the 1st peak of the time response or 1st peak overshoot is called the Peak time.

Maximum overshoot

The difference between the peak of 1st time and steady output is called the maximum overshoot. It is defined by

$$ \text{Maximum percent overshoot} =\frac{C\left(t_{p}\right)-C(\infty)}{C(\infty)} $$
$$ \% M_{P}=e^{-\frac{\pi \xi}{\sqrt{1-\xi^{2}}}} \times 100 $$

Settling Time ($t_s$)

The time that is required for the response to reach and stay within the specified range (2% to 5%) of its final value is called the settling time.

$$ t_{s}=\frac{4}{\xi \omega_{n}} $$

Steady State Error ($e_{ss}$)

The difference between actual output and desired output as time't' tends to infinity is called the steady state error of the system.

$$ e_{s s}=\lim _{t \rightarrow \infty}[r(t)-C(t)] $$
