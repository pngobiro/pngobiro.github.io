Since there are 8,760 hours in one year, you can divide 3,647 by 8,760 to get 0.41 failures per year, or about 2 failures every five years.

As an example, suppose 10 units were tested under specified operating conditions where the test time is 600 hours, and assume that failed units are not repaired. Failures occur as follows:
- Unit 1 failed after 75 hours
- Unit 2 failed after 125 hours
- Unit 3 failed after 130 hours
- Unit 4 failed after 325 hours
- Unit 5 failed after 525 hours

Five units successfully completed the test cycle. The failure rate ($\boldsymbol{\lambda}$), in number of failures per hour, can be expressed as

$$\lambda=\frac{5}{75+125+130+325+525+5(600)}=\frac{5}{4180}=0.001196$$
