Failure Rate for Repairable and Non-repairable systems EXAMPLES

$$\text { MTBF } \quad \theta=\text { Total time / Total Number of failures }$$
$$\text { Average Failure Rate } \lambda=1/\theta \rightarrow \lambda \theta=1$$

Example:

300 cars have accumulated 45000 hours, 10 failures are observed. What is the MTBF? What is the failure rate?

Note: considering Car as repairable system, Use MTBF
MTBF $=45000/10=4500$ hours.
Average Failure rate $\lambda=10/45000 \geqslant 0.00022$ pei hour.

Five oil pumps were tested with failure hours of 45, 33, 62, 94 and 105. What is the MTTF and failure rate?

Note: considering pumps as non repairable systems, Use MTTF.
MTTF $=(45+33+62+94+105) / 5=67.8$ hours
Failure rate $\lambda=5/(45+33+62+94+105)=0.0147$ per hour.
Note that MTTF is a reciprocak of failure rate.
