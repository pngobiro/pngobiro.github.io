Example 2.3 : In a Kelvin's double bridge, there is error due to mismatch between the ratios of outer and inner arm resistances. The bridge uses,

$$\begin{aligned} \text { Standard resistance } & =100.03 \mu \Omega \\ \text { Inner ratio arms } & =100.31 \Omega \text { and } 200 \Omega \\ \text { Outer ratio arms } & =100.24 \Omega \text { and } 200 \Omega \end{aligned}$$

The resistance of the connecting leads from standard to unknown resistance is $700 \mu \Omega$. Calculate the unknown resistance under this condition.

Solution :

From the given data,
$\mathrm{R}_{3}=100.03 \mu \Omega, \mathrm{R}_{2}=100.24 \Omega, \mathrm{R}_{1}=200 \Omega$
$\mathrm{b}=100.31 \Omega, \mathrm{a}=200 \Omega, \mathrm{R}_{\mathrm{y}}=700 \mu \Omega$

Thus unknown resistance is,

$$ \begin{aligned} R_{x} & =\frac{R_{1} R_{3}}{R_{2}}+\frac{b R_{y}}{\left[R_{y}+a+b\right]}\left\{\frac{R_{1}}{R_{2}}-\frac{a}{b}\right\}\\ & =\frac{200 \times 100.03 \times 10^{-6}}{100.24}+\frac{100.31 \times 700 \times 10^{-6}}{\left[700 \times 10^{-6}+200+100.31\right]}\left\{\frac{200}{100.24}-\frac{200}{100.31}\right\}\\ & =1.9958 \times 10^{-4}+\left(2.3381 \times 10^{-4}\right)\left(1.3923 \times 10^{-3}\right)\\ & =1.999 \times 10^{-4} \Omega=199.905 \mu \Omega \end{aligned} $$
