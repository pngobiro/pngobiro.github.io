The basic circuit of an ac bridge is exactly the same as the Wheaistone bridge circuit except that impedances are used instead of resistances, and the supply is an $a c$-sgurce. Also, the null detector must be an $a c$ instrument.

PRINCIPLE

When the specific circuit conditions apply, the detector current becomes zero, which is known as null or balance condition. Since zero current, it means that there is no voltage difference across the detector,

Voltage at point $\boldsymbol{b}$ and $\boldsymbol{c}$ are equal. $\square$ $I_{1} Z_{1}=I_{2} Z_{2}$
The same thing at point $\boldsymbol{d}$.
$I_{1} Z_{3}=I_{2} Z_{4}$
From two above equation yield general bridge equation;

$$\frac{\mathrm{Z}_{1}}{\mathrm{Z}_{2}}=\frac{\mathrm{Z}_{3}}{\mathrm{Z}_{4}}$$

Therefore, Z1.Z4 = Z2.Z3

For AC-bridges, the null-condition is only valid if $V a$ and $V b$ are equal in amplitude and also same in-phase. In the polar form the impedance $Z$ can be written as

$$Z=Z \angle \theta$$

where $Z$ represents the impedance and $\theta$ represents the phase angle of complex impedance $Z$.Hence, the bridge arm impedances in polar form can be expressed as

$$\begin{aligned} Z_{1}&=Z_{1} \angle \theta_{1}\\ Z_{2}&=Z_{2} \angle \theta_{2}\\ Z_{3}&=Z_{3} \angle \theta_{3}\\ Z_{4}&=Z_{4} \angle \theta_{4} \end{aligned}$$

where $Z_{1}, Z_{2}, Z_{3}$ and $Z_{4}$ are the magnitudes and $\theta_{1}, \theta_{2}, \theta_{3}$, and $\theta_{4}$ are the phase angles. Hence, the balance equation in polar form representation will be

$$\left(Z_{1} \angle \theta_{1}\right) \cdot\left(Z_{4} \angle \theta_{4}\right)=\left(Z_{2} \angle \theta_{2}\right)\left(Z_{3} \angle \theta_{3}\right)$$

Since, in complex number multiplication the magnitudes are multiplied and the phase angles are added, the balance equation can be written as

$$\begin{aligned} Z_{1} Z_{4}(\angle \theta_{1}+\theta_{4})&=Z_{2} Z_{3}(\angle \theta_{2}+\theta_{3})\\ \text{i.e. } \Rightarrow Z_{1} Z_{4}&=Z_{2} Z_{3}\\ \text{and } \quad \angle \theta_{1}+\angle \theta_{4}&=\angle \theta_{2}+\angle \theta_{3} \end{aligned}$$
