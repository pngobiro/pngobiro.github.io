Electrostatic Dimensions

🔌 DIMENSIONS IN ELECTROSTATIC SYSTEM

Let us derive the dimensions of some practical units in electrostatic system of units interms of L, M, T and $\varepsilon$.

1. Charge (Q) :

According to coulomb's law force between two charges $\mathrm{Q}_{1}$ and $\mathrm{Q}_{2}$ is given by,

$$\mathrm{F}=\frac{\mathrm{Q}_{1} \mathrm{Q}_{2}}{\varepsilon \mathrm{d}^{2}}$$

where

$$\mathrm{d}=\text { Distance between the two charges }$$
$$\begin{aligned} \therefore & {\left[\mathrm{MLT}^{-2}\right] } & =\frac{\left[\mathrm{Q}^{2}\right]}{[\varepsilon][\mathrm{L}]^{2}} \\ & & {[\mathrm{Q}]^{2}} & =\left[\mathrm{ML}^{3} \mathrm{~T}^{-2} \varepsilon\right] \\ & \therefore & {[\mathrm{Q}] } & =\left[\varepsilon^{\frac{1}{2}} \mathrm{M}^{\frac{1}{2}} \mathrm{~L}^{\frac{3}{2}} \mathrm{~T}^{-1}\right] \end{aligned}$$

where $\varepsilon$ is the permitivity of a free space.

And vacuum is assume to be unity in such systems

2. Current (I) :

It is the rate of flow of charge.

$$\begin{aligned} \therefore & \mathrm{I}=\frac{\mathrm{Q}}{\mathrm{t}} \\ \therefore & {[\mathrm{I}]=\frac{\left[\varepsilon^{\frac{1}{2}} \mathrm{M}^{\frac{1}{2}} \mathrm{~L}^{\frac{3}{2}} \mathrm{~T}^{-1}\right]}{[\mathrm{T}] \mathrm{S}^{\frac{0}{0}}}=\left[\varepsilon^{\frac{1}{2}} \mathrm{M}^{\frac{1}{2}} \mathrm{~L}^{\frac{3}{2}} \mathrm{~T}^{-2}\right]} \end{aligned}$$

3. Potential difference or e.m.f. (E) :

It is the work done per unit charge.

$$\begin{aligned} E & =\frac{W}{Q} \\ \therefore \quad[E] & =\frac{M L^{2} T^{-2}}{\left[\varepsilon^{\frac{1}{2}} M^{\frac{1}{2}} L^{\frac{3}{2}} \mathrm{~T}^{-1}\right]}=\left[\varepsilon^{-\frac{1}{2}} \mathrm{M}^{\frac{1}{2}} \mathrm{~L}^{\frac{1}{2}} \mathrm{~T}^{-1}\right] \end{aligned}$$

4. Capacitance (C) :

It is the ratio of charge and the e.m.f.

$$\begin{aligned} C & =\frac{Q}{E} \\ \therefore & {[C] } \end{aligned}$$

5. Resistance ( R ) :

It is ratio of e.m.f. and the current.

$$\begin{aligned} R & =\frac{E}{I} \\ \therefore \quad[R] & =\frac{\left[\varepsilon^{-\frac{1}{2}} \mathrm{M}^{\frac{1}{2}} \mathrm{~L}^{\frac{1}{2}} \mathrm{~T}^{-1}\right]}{\left[\varepsilon^{\frac{1}{2}} \mathrm{M}^{\frac{1}{2}} \mathrm{~L}^{\frac{3}{2}} \mathrm{~T}^{-2}\right]}=\left[\varepsilon^{-1} \mathrm{~L}^{-1} \mathrm{~T}\right] \end{aligned}$$

6. Inductance (L) :

It is the ratio of e.m.f. and the corresponding rate of change of current.

$$\begin{aligned} & \mathrm{L}=\frac{\mathrm{E}}{\mathrm{dI} / \mathrm{dt}} \\ \therefore \quad \mathrm{L} & =\frac{[\mathrm{E}]}{\left\{\frac{[\mathrm{I}]}{[\mathrm{T}]}\right\}}=\frac{\left[\varepsilon^{-\frac{1}{2}} \mathrm{M}^{\frac{1}{2}} \mathrm{~L}^{\frac{1}{2}} \mathrm{~T}^{-1}\right]}{\left[\varepsilon^{\frac{1}{2}} \mathrm{M}^{\frac{1}{2}} \mathrm{~L}^{\frac{3}{2}} \mathrm{~T}^{-2}\right]} \\ & {[\mathrm{T}] } \\ & =[\varepsilon^{-1} \mathrm{~L}^{-1} \mathrm{~T}^{2}] \end{aligned}$$
