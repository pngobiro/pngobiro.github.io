Dimensional Equations and Formulas of Physical Quantities

The dimension of a physical quantity is defined as the power to which the fundamental quantities are raised to express the physical quantity. The dimension of mass, dength and time are represented as [ $\mathbf{M} \mathbf{3}$, [L] and [T] respectively. For example:

$$\text{Velocity} = \frac{\text{displacement}}{\text{time}} = \frac{[\mathrm{L}]}{[\mathrm{T}]}=[\mathrm{L}][\mathrm{T}-1]=[\mathrm{MOL} \mathrm{~T}-1]$$

We say that dimension of velocity are, zero in mass, 1 in length and -1 in time.

An advantage of the SI is the ease with which dimensional calculations can be performed. A derived unit is recognized by its dimensions, which can be defined as the complete algebraic formula for the derived unit. If we define the dimensions of length, mass, and time as [L], [M], and [T], respectively, then physical quantities may be expressed as $[\mathrm{L}]^{\mathrm{X}}[\mathrm{M}]^{\mathrm{y}}[\mathrm{T}]^{\mathrm{Z}}$. For instance, the dimension of acceleration is $[\mathrm{L}][\mathrm{T}]^{-2}$ and that of force is $[\mathrm{L}][\mathrm{M}][\mathrm{T}]^{-2}$. In the mks system of units, the systematic unit of acceleration is therefore $1 \mathrm{~m} / \mathrm{s}^{2}$ and that of force is $1 \mathrm{kgm} / \mathrm{s}^{2}$.

Dimensional Equations

The equation obtained by replacing each quantity in the mathematical equation by respective dimensions is called dimensional equations.

Advantages of Dimensional equations

  • ‚Ä¢ In conversion from one system of units to another system of units
  • ‚Ä¢ In derivation of equations for physical quantities
  • ‚Ä¢ In checking the accuracy of an equation

Uses of Dimensional Equations:

  • ‚Ä¢ i. To check the correctness of a physical relation.
  • ‚Ä¢ ii. To derive the relation between various physical quantities.
  • ‚Ä¢ iii. To convert value of physical quantity from one system of unit to another system.
  • ‚Ä¢ iv. To find the dimension of constants in a given relation.

Limitation of Dimensional analysis

  • ‚Ä¢ i. It does not give information about the dimensional constant.
  • ‚Ä¢ ii. if a quantity depends on more than three factors having dimension, the formula cannot be derived.
  • ‚Ä¢ iii. We cannot derive the formulae containing trigonometric function, exponential functions, logarithmic function, etc.
  • ‚Ä¢ iv. The exact form of relation cannot be developed when there are more than one part in any relation.
  • ‚Ä¢ v. It gives no information whether a physical quanfity is scalar or vector.
