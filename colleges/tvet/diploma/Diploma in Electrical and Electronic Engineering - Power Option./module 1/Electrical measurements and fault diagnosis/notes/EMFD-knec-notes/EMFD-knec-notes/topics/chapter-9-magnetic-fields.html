Magnetic field, field strength, and flux density

Magnetism $\square$ Force of attraction or repulsion that acts between magnets and other magnetic materials
Flux lines $\square$ Show direction and intensity of this field at all points
Flux, $\square$ : Total number of lines
The number of magnetic lines of forces set up in a magnetic circuit is called Magnetic Flux.
Symbol is Phi, ( $\Phi$ ) with the unit of flux being the Weber, ( Wb ).
But the number of lines of force within a given unit area is called the "Flux Density" and since flux ( $\Phi$ ) is measured in ( Wb ) and area ( A ) in metres squared, ( $\mathrm{m}^{2}$ ), flux density is therefore measured in Webers/Metre ${ }^{2}$ or ( $\mathrm{Wb} / \mathrm{m}^{2}$ ) and is given the symbol B .

Magnetic Flux Density

$$\text { Magnetic Flux Density, (tesla) }=\frac{\text { Magnetic Flux, (weber) }}{\text { Area, }(\mathrm{m} 2)}$$

The symbol for magnetic flux density is B and the unit of magnetic flux density is the Tesla, T .

$$B=\frac{\Phi}{A} \quad \text { in Teslas }$$

Magnetism Example No1

The amount of flux present in a round magnetic bar was measured at 0.013 webers. If the material has a diameter of 12 cm , calculate the flux density.

The cross sectional area of the magnetic material in m 2 is given as:

$$\begin{aligned} \text { Diameter }&=12 \mathrm{~cm}\\ \therefore \text { Area }&=\pi \mathrm{r}^{2}\\ \mathrm{~A}&=3.142 \times 0.06^{2}=0.0113 \mathrm{~m}^{2} \end{aligned}$$

The magnetic flux is given as 0.013 webers, therefore the flux density can be calculated as:

$$\mathrm{B}=\frac{\Phi}{\mathrm{A}}=\frac{0.013}{0.0113}=1.15 \mathrm{~T}$$

So the flux density is calculated as 1.15 Teslas.

Difference Between Magnetic Field & Magnetic Flux

The most significant difference between the magnetic field and the magnetic flux is that the magnetic field is the region around the magnet where the moving charge experiences a force, whereas the magnetic flux shows the quantity or strength of magnetic lines produced by the magnet.
