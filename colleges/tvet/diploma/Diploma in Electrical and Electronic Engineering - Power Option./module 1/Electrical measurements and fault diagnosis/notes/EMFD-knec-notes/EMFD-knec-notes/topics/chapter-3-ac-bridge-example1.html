Example

The impedances of the AC bridge in Figure below are given as follows

$$ \begin{array}{l} Z_{1}=200 \angle 30^{\circ} \Omega \\ Z_{2}=150 \angle 0^{0} \Omega \\ Z_{3}=250 \angle-40^{\circ} \Omega \\ Z_{x}=Z_{4}=\text { unknown } \end{array} $$

Determine the constants of the unknown arm.

$$\mathrm{Z}_{1} \mathrm{Z}_{\mathrm{x}}=\mathrm{Z}_{2} \mathrm{Z}_{3}, \quad \mathrm{Z}_{\mathrm{x}}=\left(\mathrm{Z}_{2} \mathrm{Z}_{3} / \mathrm{Z}_{1}\right), \quad=[(150 * 250) / \mathbf{2 0 0}]=187.5 \Omega$$

The second condition for balance requires that the sums of the phase angles of opposite arms be equal,

$$\theta_{1}+\theta_{\mathrm{x}}=\theta_{2}+\theta_{3}$$
$$\theta_{\mathrm{x}}=\theta_{2}+\theta_{3}-\theta_{1}=0+\left(-40^{\circ}\right)-30^{\circ}=-70^{\circ}$$

The unknown impedance $\mathrm{Z}_{\mathrm{x}}$, can be written as,
