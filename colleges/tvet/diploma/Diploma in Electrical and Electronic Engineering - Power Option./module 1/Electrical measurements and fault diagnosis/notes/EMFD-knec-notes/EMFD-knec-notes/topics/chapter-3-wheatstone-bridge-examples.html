Example 1

For the Wheatstone bridge circuit below If $R 1=1 \mathrm{~W}, R 2=2 \mathrm{~W}$, and $R x=3 \mathrm{~W}$, to what value should $R 3$ be adjusted so as to achieve a balanced condition?

$$\frac{R_{3}}{R_{1}}=\frac{R_{X}}{R_{2}}, \quad R_{3}=\frac{R_{1} R_{x}}{R_{2}}=\frac{1 \times 3}{2}=1.5 \Omega .$$

Example 2

A Wheatstone bridge shown below has a ratio arm of 1/100 (R2/R1). At first balance, R3 is adjusted to 1000.3 $\Omega$. The value of Rx is then changed by the emperature change, the new value of R3 to achieve the balance condition again is $1002.1 \Omega$. Find the erhange of Rx due to ${}^{\circ}$ the temperature change.

SOLUTION

At first balance: $\quad R_{x}$ old $=R_{3} \frac{R_{2}}{R_{1}}=1000.3 \times \frac{1}{100}=10.003 \Omega$
After the temperature change: $R_{x}$ new $=R_{3} \frac{R_{2}}{R_{1}}=1002.1 \times \frac{3}{100}=10.021 \Omega$
Therefore, the change of $\mathrm{R}_{\mathrm{x}}$ due to the temperature change is $0.018 \Omega$
