Maxwell's bridge is used to measure the value of medium inductance. The circuit diagram of Maxwell's bridge is shown in the below figure.

Let, $Z_{1}, Z_{2}, Z_{3}$ and $Z_{4}$ are the impedances 80 arms $\mathrm{DA}, \mathrm{AB}, \mathrm{CD}$ and $B C$ respectively. The values of these impedances will be:

$$\begin{aligned} Z_{1} &= \frac{R_{1}\left(\frac{1}{j \omega C_{1}}\right)}{R_{1}+\frac{1}{j \omega C_{1}}} \\ \Rightarrow Z_{1} &= \frac{R_{1}}{1+j \omega R_{1} C_{1}} \\ Z_{2} &= R_{2} \\ Z_{3} &= R_{3} \\ Z_{4} &= R_{4}+j \omega L_{4} \end{aligned}$$

Substitute these impedance values in the following balancing condition of AC bridge.

$$\begin{aligned} Z_{4}&=\frac{Z_{2} Z_{3}}{Z_{1}}\\ R_{4}+j \omega L_{4}&=\frac{R_{2} R_{3}}{\left(\frac{R_{1}}{1+j \omega R_{1} C_{1}}\right)}\\ \Rightarrow R_{4}+j \omega L_{4}&=\frac{R_{2} R_{3}\left(1+j \omega R_{1} C_{1}\right)}{R_{1}}\\ \Rightarrow R_{4}+j \omega L_{4}&=\frac{R_{2} R_{3}}{R_{1}}+\frac{j \omega R_{1} C_{1} R_{2} R_{3}}{R_{1}}\\ \Rightarrow R_{4}+j \omega L_{4}&=\frac{R_{2} R_{3}}{R_{1}}+j \omega C_{1} R_{2} R_{3} \end{aligned}$$

By comparing the respective real and imaginary terms of above equation, we will get

$$\begin{aligned} R_{4}&=\frac{R_{2} R_{3}}{R_{1}} \quad \text { Equation 1 }\\ L_{4}&=C_{1} R_{2} R_{3} \quad \text { Equation } 22 \end{aligned}$$
