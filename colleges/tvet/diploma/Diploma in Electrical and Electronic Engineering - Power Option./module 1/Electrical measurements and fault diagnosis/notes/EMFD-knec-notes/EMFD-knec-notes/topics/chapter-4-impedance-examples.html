Find the rms current flowing in an AC capacitive circuit when a $4 \mu \mathrm{F}$ capacitor is connected across a 880 V , 60 Hz supply.

$$\mathrm{X}_{\mathrm{C}}=\frac{1}{2 \pi f \mathrm{C}}=\frac{1}{2 \pi \times 60 \times\left(4 \times 10^{-6}\right)}=663 \Omega$$
$$\mathrm{I}_{\mathrm{RMS}}=\frac{\mathrm{V}_{\mathrm{RMS}}}{\mathrm{X}_{\mathrm{C}}}=\frac{880 \mathrm{~V}}{663 \Omega}=1.33 \mathrm{Amperes}$$

When a parallel plate capacitor was connected to a 60 Hz AC supply, it was found to have a reactance of 390 ohms. Calculate the value of the capacitor in micro-farads.

$$X_{C}=\frac{1}{2 \pi f C} \quad \therefore C=\frac{1}{2 \pi f X_{C}}$$
$$C=\frac{1}{2 \pi \times 60 \times 390}=6.8 \mathrm{uF}$$
