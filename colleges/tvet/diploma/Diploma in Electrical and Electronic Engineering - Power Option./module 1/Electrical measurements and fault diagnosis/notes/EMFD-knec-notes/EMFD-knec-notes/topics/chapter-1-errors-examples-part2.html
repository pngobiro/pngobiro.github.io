Example 1.5 : A 0-50 V voltmeter is specified to be accurate within $\pm 1 \%$ of full scale. Calculate the limiting error when the instrument reading is 15 V .

Solution :

The limiting error at full scale is,

$$\begin{aligned} \delta \mathrm{A} &= \pm 1 \% \text { of } 50\\ &= \pm \frac{1}{100} \times 50\\ &= \pm 0.5 \mathrm{~V} \end{aligned}$$

For a reading of 15 V , it is

$$\% e=\frac{0.5}{15} \times 100=3.33 \%$$

Thus as reading is less than full scale, the limiting error is more.

Example 1.10: A 0-100 V voltmeter has 200 scale divisions which can be read to $1 / 2$ division. Determine the resolution of the meter in volt.

Example 1.12 : Three resistances are specified as :

$R_{1}=200 \Omega \pm 5 \%, \quad R_{2}=100 \Omega \pm 5 \%, R_{3}=50 \Omega \pm 5 \%$

Determine the magnitude of the resultant resistance and the limiting error in percentage and in ohms if the resistances are connected in series.

Solution :

The resistances are in series,

$$\begin{aligned} \therefore \quad \mathrm{R}_{\mathrm{T}}&=\mathrm{R}_{1}+\mathrm{R}_{2}+\mathrm{R}_{3}=200+100+50\\ &=350 \Omega \end{aligned}$$
$$\begin{aligned} \mathrm{e}_{1}&=\mathrm{e}_{2}=\mathrm{e}_{3}=5 \%\\ \mathrm{a}_{1}&=\mathrm{R}_{1}=200 \Omega\\ \mathrm{a}_{2}&=\mathrm{R}_{2}=100 \Omega\\ \mathrm{a}_{3}&=\mathrm{R}_{3}=50 \Omega\\ \therefore \quad \% \mathrm{e}_{\mathrm{T}}&=\left[\frac{\mathrm{R}_{1}}{\mathrm{R}_{\mathrm{T}}} \cdot \mathrm{e}_{1}+\frac{\mathrm{R}_{2}}{\mathrm{R}_{\mathrm{T}}} \cdot \mathrm{e}_{2}+\frac{\mathrm{R}_{3}}{\mathrm{R}_{\mathrm{T}}} \cdot \mathrm{e}_{3}\right]=\left[\frac{200}{350} \cdot 5 \%+\frac{100}{350} \cdot 5 \%+\frac{50}{350} \cdot 5 \%\right]\\ &=5 \% \times\left[\frac{350}{350}\right]=5 \% \end{aligned}$$

Thus the limiting error in percentage is,

$$\begin{aligned} \mathrm{e}_{1}&=\mathrm{e}_{2}=\mathrm{e}_{3}=5 \%\\ \mathrm{a}_{1}&=\mathrm{R}_{1}=200 \Omega\\ \mathrm{a}_{2}&=\mathrm{R}_{2}=100 \Omega\\ \mathrm{a}_{3}&=\mathrm{R}_{3}=50 \Omega\\ \therefore \quad \% \mathrm{e}_{\mathrm{T}}&=\left[\frac{\mathrm{R}_{1}}{\mathrm{R}_{\mathrm{T}}} \cdot \mathrm{e}_{1}+\frac{\mathrm{R}_{2}}{\mathrm{R}_{\mathrm{T}}} \cdot \mathrm{e}_{2}+\frac{\mathrm{R}_{3}}{\mathrm{R}_{\mathrm{T}}} \cdot \mathrm{e}_{3}\right]=\left[\frac{200}{350} \cdot 5 \%+\frac{100}{350} \cdot 5 \%+\frac{50}{350} \cdot 5 \%\right]\\ &=5 \% \times\left[\frac{350}{350}\right]=5 \% \end{aligned}$$

Thus the limiting error in percentage is,

$$\mathrm{e}_{\mathrm{T}}= \pm \mathbf{5 \%}$$

Now $\quad \% \mathrm{e}_{\mathrm{T}}=\frac{\delta \mathrm{R}_{\mathrm{T}}}{\mathrm{R}_{\mathrm{T}}} \times 100$

$$\begin{aligned} \therefore \quad 5 &=\frac{\delta \mathrm{R}_{\mathrm{T}}}{350} \times 100\\ \therefore \quad \quad \delta \mathrm{R}_{\mathrm{T}}&=\frac{5 \times 350}{100}= \pm 17.5 \Omega \end{aligned}$$

Thus $\pm 17.5 \Omega$ is the limiting error in ohms.
