Hall effect

The Hall effect is an occurrence of a very small voltage that is generated on opposite sides of a thin currentcarrying conductor or semiconductor (the Hall element) that is in a magnetic field.

The Hall effect is widely employed by various sensors for directly measuring position or motion and can be used indirectly for other measurements.
