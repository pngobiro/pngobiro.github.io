Worked Examples (Magnetic)

Example

What is the magnetizing force of a coil having 4000 turns and 10 mA current flowing through it?
Solution
We have following formula to calculate ampere - turns (magnetizing force):
$\mathrm{F}=A-t$

So, putting values of current and turns into formula,
$\mathrm{F}=4000 * 0.01=40 A-t$
Thus, if a large amount of magnetizing force is needed and only a small amount of current is available, it is necessary to use a great number of turns on the coil.

Examples

1. A current of 5 A is passed through a 1000-turn coil wound on a circular magnetic circuit of radius 120 mm . Calculate (a) the magnetomotive force, and (b) the magnetic field strength.

(a) Magnetomotive force, m.m.f. $=\mathrm{NI}=(1000)(5)=5000 \mathrm{~A}$
(b) Length of magnetic field, $\mathrm{l}=2 \pi \mathrm{r}=2 \pi(0.120) \mathrm{m}$

$$\text { Hence, magnetic field strength, } \mathbf{H}=\frac{\mathrm{NI}}{\mathrm{l}}=\frac{100 \times 2^{6}}{0.5}=400 \mathrm{~AT} / \mathrm{m} \text { or } \mathrm{A} / \mathrm{m}$$
