Mechanical science - Assignment 4

üìù Assignment 4

This assignment covers the material contained in chapters 11 to 14.

The marks for each question are shown in brackets at the end of each question.

  1. 1. A train is travelling at $90\mathrm{~km}/ \mathrm{h}$ and has wheels of diameter 1600 mm . (a) Find the angular velocity of the wheels in both $\mathrm{rad}/ \mathrm{s}$ and $\mathrm{rev}/ \mathrm{min}$. (b) If the speed remains constant for 2 km , determine the number of revolutions made by a wheel, assuming no slipping occurs. (4)
  2. 2. The speed of a shaft increases uniformly from 200 revolutions per minute to 700 revolutions per minute in 12 s . Find the angular acceleration, correct to 3 significant figures. (4)
  3. 3. The shaft of an electric motor, initially at rest, accelerates uniformly for 0.3 s at $20\mathrm{~rad}/ \mathrm{s}^{2}$. Determine the angle (in radians) turned through by the shaft in this time. (3)
  4. 4. Determine the momentum of a lorry of mass 10 tonnes moving at a velocity of $81\mathrm{~km}/ \mathrm{h}$. (3)
  5. 5. A ball of mass 50 g is moving with a velocity of $4\mathrm{~m}/ \mathrm{s}$ when it strikes a stationary ball of mass 25 g . The velocity of the 50 g ball after impact is $2.5\mathrm{~m}/ \mathrm{s}$ in the same direction as before impact. Determine the velocity of the 25 g ball after impact. (4)
  6. 6. A force of 24 N acts on a body of mass 6 kg for 150 ms . Determine the change in velocity. (3)
  7. 7. The hammer of a piledriver of mass 800 kg falls a distance of 1.0 m on to a pile. The blow takes place in 20 ms and the hammer does not rebound. Determine (a) the velocity of impact (b) the momentum lost by the hammer (c) the average applied force exerted on the pile by the hammer. (8)
  8. 8. Determine the mass of the moving head of a machine tool if it requires a force of 1.5 N to bring it to rest in 0.75 s from a cutting speed of $25\mathrm{~m}/ \mathrm{min}$. (3)
  9. 9. Find the weight of an object of mass 2.5 kg at a point on the earth's surface where the gravitational field is $9.8\mathrm{~N}/ \mathrm{kg}$. (2)
  10. 10. A van of mass 1200 kg travels round a bend of radius 120 m , at $54\mathrm{~km}/ \mathrm{h}$. Determine the centripetal force acting on the vehicle. (3)
  11. 11. A spring, initially in a relaxed state, is extended by 80 mm . Determine the work done by using a work diagram if the spring requires a force of 0.7 N per mm of stretch. (4)
  12. 12. Water is pumped vertically upwards through a distance of 40.0 m and the work done is 176.58 kJ . Determine the number of litres of water pumped. (1 litre of water has a mass of 1 kg ). (3)
  13. 13. 3 kJ of energy are supplied to a machine used for lifting a mass. The force required is 1 kN . If the machine has an efficiency of $60 \%$, to what height will it lift the mass? (3)
  14. 14. When exerting a steady pull of 450 N , a lorry travels at $80\mathrm{~km}/ \mathrm{h}$. Calculate (a) the work done in 15 minutes and (b) the power required. (5)
  15. 15. An electric motor provides power to a winding machine. The input power to the motor is 4.0 kW and the overall efficiency is $75 \%$. Calculate (a) the output power of the machine, (b) the rate at which it can raise a 509.7 kg load vertically upwards (5)
  16. 16. A tank of mass 4800 kg is climbing an incline at $12^{\circ}$ to the horizontal. Determine the increase in potential energy of the tank as it moves a distance of 40 m up the incline. (3)
  17. 17. A car of mass 500 kg reduces speed from $108\mathrm{~km}/ \mathrm{h}$ to $36\mathrm{~km}/ \mathrm{h}$ in 20 s . Determine the braking power required to give this change of speed. (4)
