Mechanical science - Assignment 7

üìù Assignment 7

This assignment covers the material contained in chapters 21 and 24.

The marks for each question are shown in brackets at the end of each question.

When required take the density of water to be $1000\mathrm{~kg}/ \mathrm{m}^{3}$ and gravitational acceleration as $9.81\mathrm{~m}/ \mathrm{s}^{2}$.

  1. 1. A circular piston exerts a pressure of 150 kPa on a fluid when the force applied to the piston is 0.5 kN . Calculate the diameter of the piston, correct to the nearest millimetre. (3)
  2. 2. A tank contains water to a depth of 500 mm . Determine the water pressure (a) at a depth of 300 mm , and (b) at the base of the tank. (4)
  3. 3. When the atmospheric pressure is 101 kPa , calculate the absolute pressure, to the nearest kilopascal, at a point on a submarine which is 50 m below the seawater surface. Assume that the density of seawater is $1030\mathrm{~kg}/ \mathrm{m}^{3}$. (5)
  4. 4. A body weighs 2.85 N in air and 2.35 N when completely immersed in water. Determine (a) the volume of the body, (b) the density of the body, and (c) the relative density of the body. (6)
  5. 5. A submarine dives to a depth of 700 m . What is the gauge pressure on its surface if the density of seawater is $1020\mathrm{~kg}/ \mathrm{m}^{3}$. (3)
  6. 6. State the most appropriate fluid flow measuring device for the following applications: (a) A high accuracy, permanent installation, in an oil pipeline. (b) For high velocity chemical flow, without suffering wear. (c) To detect leakage in water mains. (d) To measure petrol in petrol pumps. (e) To measure the speed of a viscous liquid. (5)
  7. 7. A storage tank contains water to a depth of 7 m above an outlet pipe, as shown in Figure 22.12 on page 254. The system is in equilibrium until a valve in the outlet pipe is opened. Determine the initial mass rate of flow at the exit of the outlet pipe, assuming that losses at the pipe entry $=0.3v^{2}$, and losses at the valve $=0.2v^{2}$. The pipe diameter is 0.05 m and the water density, $\rho$, is $1000\mathrm{~kg}/ \mathrm{m}^{3}$. (6)
  8. 8. Determine the wind pressure acting on a slender building due to a gale of $150\mathrm{~km}/ \mathrm{h}$ that acts perpendicularly to the building. Take the density of air as $1.23\mathrm{~kg}/ \mathrm{m}^{3}$. (3)
  9. 9. Some gas occupies a volume of $2.0\mathrm{~m}^{3}$ in a cylinder at a pressure of 200 kPa . A piston, sliding in the cylinder, compresses the gas isothermally until the volume is $0.80\mathrm{~m}^{3}$. If the area of the piston is $240\mathrm{~cm}^{2}$, calculate the force on the piston when the gas is compressed. (4)
  10. 10. Gas at a temperature of $180^{\circ}\mathrm{C}$ has its volume reduced by a quarter in an isobaric process. Determine the final temperature of the gas. (3)
  11. 11. Some air at a pressure of 3 bar and at a temperature of $60^{\circ}\mathrm{C}$ occupies a volume of $0.08\mathrm{~m}^{3}$. Calculate the mass of the air, correct to the nearest gram, assuming the characteristic gas constant for air is $287\mathrm{~J}/(\mathrm{kg} \mathrm{~K})$. (3)
  12. 12. A compressed air cylinder has a volume of $1.0\mathrm{~m}^{3}$ and contains air at a temperature of $24^{\circ}\mathrm{C}$ and a pressure of 1.2 MPa . Air is released from the cylinder until the pressure falls to 400 kPa and the temperature is $18^{\circ}\mathrm{C}$. Calculate (a) the mass of air released from the container, and (b) the volume it would occupy at S.T.P. (Assume the characteristic gas constant for air to be $287\mathrm{~J}/(\mathrm{kg} \mathrm{~K})$). (7)
  13. 13. A platinum resistance thermometer has a resistance of $24\mathrm{~\Omega}$ at $0^{\circ}\mathrm{C}$. When measuring the temperature of an annealing process a resistance value of $68\mathrm{~\Omega}$ is recorded. To what temperature does this correspond? Take the temperature coefficient of resistance of platinum as $0.0038/^{\circ}\mathrm{C}$ (3)
  14. 14. State which temperature measuring device would be most suitable to measure the following: (a) materials in a high-temperature furnace in the range $1800^{\circ}\mathrm{C}$ to $3000^{\circ}\mathrm{C}$. (b) the air in a factory in the range $0^{\circ}\mathrm{C}$ to $35^{\circ}\mathrm{C}$. (c) an inexpensive method for food processing applications in the range $-20^{\circ}\mathrm{C}$ to $-80^{\circ}\mathrm{C}$. (d) boiler flue gas in the range $15^{\circ}\mathrm{C}$ to $250^{\circ}\mathrm{C}$. (4)
