CHAPTER 9
WATER HARDNESS.

üéØ 9.1 Introduction

Water which form lather easily with soap is said to be soft while water which do not form lather easily with soap is said to be hard. Hardness of water is caused by the presence of $\mathrm{Ca}^{2+}$ and $\mathrm{Mg}^{2+}$ ions.Ca2+ and Mg2+ ions react with soap to form an insoluble grey /white suspension/precipitate called Scum/curd. $\mathrm{Ca}^{2+}$ and $\mathrm{Mg}^{2+}$ ions in water come from the water sources passing through rocks containing soluble salts of $\mathrm{Ca}^{2+}$ and $\mathrm{Mg}^{2+}$ e.g. Limestone or gypsum.

üéØ 9.2 Types of water hardness.

Temporary hardness of water
Permanent hardness of water

üéØ 9.3 Temporary hardness of water

Temporary hardness of water is caused by the presence of dissolved calcium hydrogen carbonate $/ \mathrm{Ca}\left(\mathrm{HCO}_{3}\right)_{2}$ and magnesium hydrogen carbonate $/ \mathrm{Mg}\left(\mathrm{HCO}_{3}\right)_{2}$.

When rain water dissolve carbon(IV) oxide from the air it forms weak carbonic(IV) acid i.e. $\mathrm{CO}_{2(\mathrm{~g})}+\mathrm{H}_{2} \mathrm{O}_{(\mathrm{l})} \rightarrow \mathrm{H}_{2} \mathrm{CO}_{3(\mathrm{aq})}$

When carbonic (IV) acid passes through limestone/dolomite rocks it reacts to form soluble salts i.e.

In limestone areas; $\mathrm{H}_{2} \mathrm{CO}_{3(\mathrm{aq})}+\mathrm{CaCO}_{3(\mathrm{~s})} \rightarrow \mathrm{Ca}\left(\mathrm{HCO}_{3}\right)_{2}(\mathrm{aq})$
In dolomite areas; $\mathrm{H}_{2} \mathrm{CO}_{3(\mathrm{aq})}+\mathrm{MgCO}_{3(\mathrm{~s})} \rightarrow \mathrm{Mg}\left(\mathrm{HCO}_{3}\right)_{2}$ (aq)

(b) Permanent hardness of water

Permanent hardness of water is caused by the presence of dissolved calcium sulphate(VI)/CaSO $_4$ and magnesium sulphate(VI)/MgSO $_4$ Permanent hardness of water is caused by water dissolving $\mathbf{C a S O}_{\mathbf{4}}$ and $\mathbf{M g S O}_{\mathbf{4}}$ from ground rocks.

Hardness of water can be removed by the following methods

üéØ 9.3.1 Removing temporary hardness of water

(i) Boiling/heating.

Boiling decomposes insoluble calcium hydrogen carbonate/ $\mathrm{Ca}\left(\mathrm{HCO}_{3}\right) 2$ and magnesium hydrogen carbonate $/ \mathrm{Mg}\left(\mathrm{HCO}_{3}\right) 2$ to insoluble $\mathrm{CaCO}_{3}$ and $\mathrm{MgCO}_{3}$ that precipitate away. i.e

Chemical equation

$\mathrm{Ca}\left(\mathrm{HCO}_{3}\right) 2_{(\mathrm{aq})} \rightarrow \mathrm{CaCO}_{3(\mathrm{~s})}+\mathrm{CO}_{2(\mathrm{~g})}+\mathrm{H}_{2} \mathrm{O}_{(\mathrm{l})}$
$\mathrm{Mg}\left(\mathrm{HCO}_{3}\right) 2_{(\mathrm{aq})} \rightarrow \mathrm{MgCO}_{3(\mathrm{~s})}+\mathrm{CO}_{2(\mathrm{~g})}+\mathrm{H}_{2} \mathrm{O}_{(\mathrm{l})}$

(ii) Adding sodium carbonate (IV) /Washing soda.

Since boiling is expensive on a large scale , a calculated amount of sodium carbonate decahydrate / $\mathbf{N a}_{\mathbf{2}} \mathbf{C O}_{\mathbf{3}} . \mathbf{1 0} \mathbf{H}_{\mathbf{2}} \mathbf{O}$ precipitates insoluble $\mathrm{Ca} 2+_{(\mathrm{aq})}$ and $\mathrm{Mg} 2+_{(\mathrm{aq})}$ ions as carbonates to remove both temporary and permanent hardness of water . This a double decomposition reaction where two soluble salts form an insoluble and soluble salt. i.e.

(i) With temporary hard water

Chemical equation

$\mathrm{Na}_{2} \mathrm{CO}_{3}(\mathrm{aq})+\mathrm{Ca}\left(\mathrm{HCO}_{3}\right)_{2}(\mathrm{aq}) \rightarrow \mathrm{NaHCO}_{3(\mathrm{aq})}+\mathrm{CaCO}_{3}(\mathrm{~s})$
$\mathrm{Na}_{2} \mathrm{CO}_{3}{ }_{(\mathrm{aq})}+\mathrm{Mg}\left(\mathrm{HCO}_{3}\right)_{2}{ }_{(\mathrm{aq})} \rightarrow \mathrm{NaHCO}_{3(\mathrm{aq})}+\mathrm{MgCO}_{3}{ }_{(\mathrm{s})}$

Ionic equation

$\mathrm{CO}_{3}{ }^{2-}{ }_{(\mathrm{aq})}+\mathrm{Ca}^{2+}{ }_{(\mathrm{aq})} \rightarrow \mathrm{CaCO}_{3}{ }_{(\mathrm{s})}$
$\mathrm{CO}_{3}{ }^{2-}{ }_{(\mathrm{aq})}+\mathrm{Mg}^{2+}{ }_{(\mathrm{aq})} \rightarrow \mathrm{MgCO}_{3}{ }_{(\mathrm{s})}$

(ii) With permanent hard water

Chemical equation

$\mathrm{Na}_{2} \mathrm{CO}_{3}{ }_{(\mathrm{aq})}+\mathrm{MgSO}_{4}{ }_{(\mathrm{aq})} \rightarrow \mathrm{Na}_{2} \mathrm{SO}_{4}{ }_{(\mathrm{aq})}+\mathrm{MgCO}_{3}{ }_{(\mathrm{s})}$
$\mathrm{Na}_{2} \mathrm{CO}_{3}{ }_{(\mathrm{aq})}+\mathrm{CaSO}_{4(\mathrm{aq})} \rightarrow \mathrm{Na}_{2} \mathrm{SO}_{4(\mathrm{aq})}+\mathrm{MgCO}_{3(\mathrm{~s})}$

Ionic equation

$\mathrm{CO}_{3}{ }^{2-}{ }_{(\mathrm{aq})}+\mathrm{Ca}^{2+}{ }_{(\mathrm{aq})} \rightarrow \mathrm{CaCO}_{3}{ }_{(\mathrm{s})}$
$\mathrm{CO}_{3}{ }^{2-}{ }_{(\mathrm{aq})}+\mathrm{Mg}^{2+}{ }_{(\mathrm{aq})} \rightarrow \mathrm{MgCO}_{3}{ }_{(\mathrm{s})}$

(iii) Adding calcium (II)hydroxide/Lime water

  • - Lime water/calcium hydroxide removes only temporary hardness of water from by precipitating insoluble calcium carbonate (IV).

Chemical equation

$\mathrm{Ca}(\mathrm{OH})_{2(\mathrm{aq})}+\mathrm{Ca}\left(\mathrm{HCO}_{3}\right)_{2(\mathrm{aq})} \rightarrow 2 \mathrm{H}_{2} \mathrm{O}_{(\mathrm{l})}+2 \mathrm{CaCO}_{3(\mathrm{~s})}$

  • - Excess of Lime water/calcium hydroxide should not be used because it dissolves again to form soluble calcium hydrogen carbonate(IV) causing the hardness again.

(iv) Adding aqueous ammonia

  • - Aqueous ammonia removes temporary hardness of water by precipitating insoluble calcium carbonate (IV) and magnesium carbonate (IV)

Chemical equation

$2 \mathrm{NH}_{3(\mathrm{aq})}+\mathrm{Ca}\left(\mathrm{HCO}_{32(\mathrm{aq})} \rightarrow\left(\mathrm{NH}_{4}\right)_{2} \mathrm{CO}_{3(\mathrm{aq})}+\mathrm{CaCO}_{3}(\mathrm{~s})$
$2 \mathrm{NH}_{3(\mathrm{aq})}+\mathrm{Mg}\left(\mathrm{HCO}_{3}\right)_{2(\mathrm{aq})} \rightarrow\left(\mathrm{NH}_{4}\right)_{2} \mathrm{CO}_{3(\mathrm{aq})}+\mathrm{MgCO}_{3}(\mathrm{~s})$

(v) Use of ion-exchange permutit

  • - This method involves packing a chamber with a resin made of insoluble complex of sodium salt called sodium permutit.
  • - The sodium permutit releases sodium ions that are exchanged with $\mathrm{Mg} 2+$ and $\mathrm{Ca} 2+$ ions in hard water making the water to be soft. i.e.

$\mathrm{Na}_{2} \mathrm{X}_{(\mathrm{aq})}+\mathrm{Ca}^{2+}{ }_{(\mathrm{aq})} \rightarrow \mathrm{Na}^{+}{ }_{(\mathrm{aq})}+\mathrm{CaX}_{(\mathrm{s})}$
$\mathrm{Na}_{2} \mathrm{X}_{(\mathrm{aq})}+\mathrm{Mg}^{2+}{ }_{(\mathrm{aq})} \rightarrow \mathrm{Na}^{+}{ }_{(\mathrm{aq})}+\mathrm{MgX}_{(\mathrm{s})}$

  • - Hard water containing Mg and $\mathrm{Ca}^{2+}$
  • - $\mathrm{Na}^{+}$ ions replace $\mathrm{Mg}^{2+}$ and $\mathrm{Ca}^{2+}$ to make the water soft.
  • - When all the $\mathrm{Na}^{+}$ ions in the resin is fully exchanged with $\mathrm{Ca}^{2+}$ and $\mathrm{Ng}^{2+}$ ions in the permutit column, it is said to be exhausted.
  • - Brine /concentrated sodium chloride solution is passed through the permutit column to regenerated/recharge the column again.

(vi) Deionization /demineralization

  • - This is an advanced ion exchange method of producing deionized water.
  • - Deionized water is extremely pure water made only of hydrogen and oxygen only without any dissolved substances.
  • - Deionization involve using the resins that remove all the cations by using:
  • - A cation exchanger which remove /absorb all the cations present in water and leave only $\mathbf{H}^{+}$ ions.
  • - An anion exchanger which remove /absorb all the anions present in water and leave only $\mathbf{O H}^{-}$ ions.

The $\mathrm{H}^{+}{ }_{(\mathrm{aq})}$ and $\mathrm{OH}^{-}{ }_{(\mathrm{aq})}$ neutralize each other to form pure water.

Chemical equation

$\mathrm{H}^{+}{ }_{(\mathrm{aq})}+\mathrm{OH}^{-}{ }_{(\mathrm{aq})} \rightarrow \mathrm{H}_{2} \mathrm{O}_{(\mathrm{l})}$

When exhausted the cation exchanger is regenerated by adding $\mathrm{H}^{+}{ }_{\text {(aq) }}$ from sulphuric (VI)acid/hydrochloric acid.

When exhausted the anion exchanger is regenerated by adding $\mathrm{OH}^{-}{ }_{(\mathrm{aq})}$ from sodium hydroxide.

üéØ 9.3.2 Advantages of hard water

Hard water has the following advantages:

  • - $\mathrm{Ca}^{2+}{ }_{(\mathrm{aq})}$ in hard water are useful in bone and teeth formation.
  • - Is good for brewing beer.
  • - Contains minerals that cause it to have better /sweet taste.
  • - Animals like snails and coral polyps use calcium to make their shells and coral reefs respectively.
  • - Processing mineral water.

üéØ 9.3.3 Disadvantages of hard water

  • - Waste a lot of soap during washing before lather is formed.
  • - Causes stains/blemishes/marks on clothes/garments.
  • - Causes fur on electric appliances like kettle ,boilers and pipes form decomposition of carbonates on heating. This reduces their efficiency hence more/higher cost of power/electricity.
