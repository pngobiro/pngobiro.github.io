Revision Test 6 : Quadratics, logarithms and exponentials

üìã Revision Test 6

This assignment covers the material contained in Chapters 14-16. The marks available are shown in brackets at the end of each question.

  1. 1. Solve the following equations by factorization.
    (a) $x^{2}-9=0$
    (b) $x^{2}+12x+36=0$
    (c) $x^{2}+3x-4=0$
    (d) $3z^{2}-z-4=0$(8)
  2. 2. Solve the following equations, correct to 3 decimal places.
    (a) $5x^{2}+7x-3=0$
    (b) $3a^{2}+4a-5=0$(8)
  3. 3. Solve the equation $3x^{2}-x-4=0$ by completing the square. (6)
  4. 4. Determine the quadratic equation in $x$ whose roots are 1 and -3 . (3)
  5. 5. The bending moment $M$ at a point in a beam is given by $M=\frac{3x(20-x)}{2}$ where $x$ metres is the distance from the point of support. Determine the value of $x$ when the bending moment is 50 Nm .(5)
  6. 6. The current $i$ flowing through an electronic device is given by $i=(0.005v^{2}+0.014v)$ amperes where $v$ is the voltage. Calculate the values of $v$ when $i=3 \times 10^{-3}$.(5)
  7. 7. Evaluate the following, correct to 4 significant figures.
    (a) $3.2\ln 4.92-5\lg 17.9$
    (b) $\frac{5(1-e^{-2.65})}{e^{1.73}}$(6)
  8. 8. Solve the following equations.
    (a) $\lg x=4$
    (b) $\ln x=2$
    (c) $\log_{2} x=5$
    (d) $5^{x}=2$
    (e) $3^{2t-1}=7^{t+2}$(f) $3e^{2x}=4.2$(12)
  9. 9. Evaluate $\log_{16}\left(\frac{1}{8}\right)$(3)
  10. 10. Write the following as the logarithm of a single number.
    (a) $3\log 2+2\log 5-\frac{1}{2}\log 16$
    (b) $3\log 3+\frac{1}{4}\log 16-\frac{1}{3}\log 27$(6)
  11. 11. Solve the equation
    $\log (x^{2}+8)-\log (2x)=\log 3$.(5)
  12. 12. Evaluate the following, each correct to 3 decimal places.
    (a) $\ln 462.9$(b) $\ln 0.0753$(c) $\frac{\ln 3.68-\ln 2.91}{4.63}$(6)
  13. 13. Expand $xe^{3x}$ to six terms.(5)
  14. 14. Evaluate $v$ given that $v=E\left(1-e^{-\frac{t}{CR}}\right)$ volts when $E=100\mathrm{~V}, C=15\mu\mathrm{F},\quad R=50\mathrm{~k}\Omega$ and $t=1.5\mathrm{~s}$. Also, determine the time when the voltage is 60 V .(8)
  15. 15. Plot a graph of $y=\frac{1}{2}e^{-1.2x}$ over the range $x=-2$ to $x=+1$ and hence determine, correct to 1 decimal place,
    (a) the value of $y$ when $x=-0.75$, and
    (b) the value of $x$ when $y=4.0$(9)
