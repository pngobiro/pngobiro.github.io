Decimals

üéØ 3.1 Introduction

The decimal system of numbers is based on the digits 0 to 9.

There are a number of everyday occurrences in which we use decimal numbers. For example, a radio is, say, tuned to 107.5 MHz FM; 107.5 is an example of a decimal number.

In a shop, a pair of trainers cost, say, ¬£57.95; 57.95 is another example of a decimal number. 57.95 is a decimal fraction, where a decimal point separates the integer, i.e. 57, from the fractional part, i.e. 0.95

$$57.95 \text{ actually means } (5 \times 10)+(7 \times 1) + \left(9 \times \frac{1}{10}\right)+\left(5 \times \frac{1}{100}\right)$$

üîÑ 3.2 Converting decimals to fractions and vice-versa

Converting decimals to fractions and vice-versa is demonstrated below with worked examples.

üí° Problem 1. Convert 0.375 to a proper fraction in its simplest form

  • ‚Ä¢ 0.375 may be written as $\frac{0.375 \times 1000}{1000}$ i.e. $0.375=\frac{375}{1000}$
  • ‚Ä¢ Dividing both numerator and denominator by 5 gives $\frac{375}{1000}=\frac{75}{200}$
  • ‚Ä¢ Dividing both numerator and denominator by 5 again gives $\frac{75}{200}=\frac{15}{40}$
  • ‚Ä¢ Dividing both numerator and denominator by 5 again gives $\frac{15}{40}=\frac{\mathbf{3}}{\mathbf{8}}$

Since both 3 and 8 are only divisible by 1, we cannot 'cancel' any further, so $\frac{3}{8}$ is the 'simplest form' of the fraction.

Hence, the decimal fraction $0.375=\frac{3}{8}$ as a proper fraction.

üí° Problem 2. Convert 3.4375 to a mixed number

  • ‚Ä¢ 0.4375 may be written as $\frac{0.4375 \times 10000}{10000}$ i.e. $0.4375=\frac{4375}{10000}$
  • ‚Ä¢ Dividing both numerator and denominator by 25 gives $\frac{4375}{10000}=\frac{175}{400}$
  • ‚Ä¢ Dividing both numerator and denominator by 5 gives $\frac{175}{400}=\frac{35}{80}$
  • ‚Ä¢ Dividing both numerator and denominator by 5 again gives $\frac{35}{80}=\frac{7}{16}$

Since both 5 and 16 are only divisible by 1, we cannot 'cancel' any further, so $\frac{7}{16}$ is the 'lowest form' of the fraction.

  • ‚Ä¢ Hence, $0.4375=\frac{7}{16}$

Thus, the decimal fraction $3.4375=3 \frac{7}{16}$ as a mixed number.

üí° Problem 3. Express $\frac{7}{8}$ as a decimal fraction

To convert a proper fraction to a decimal fraction, the numerator is divided by the denominator.

  • ‚Ä¢ 8 into 60 goes 7, remainder 4. Place the 7 above the next zero and carry the 4 remainder to the next digit on the right, making it 40.
  • ‚Ä¢ 8 into 40 goes 5, remainder 0. Place 5 above the next zero.

üî¢ 3.3 Significant figures and decimal places

A number which can be expressed exactly as a decimal fraction is called a terminating decimal.

For example,

$$3 \frac{3}{16}=3.1825 \text{ is a terminating decimal}$$

A number which cannot be expressed exactly as a decimal fraction is called a non-terminating decimal. For example,

$$1 \frac{5}{7}=1.7142857 \ldots \text{ is a non-terminating decimal}$$

The answer to a non-terminating decimal may be expressed in two ways, depending on the accuracy required:

  • ‚Ä¢ correct to a number of significant figures, or
  • ‚Ä¢ correct to a number of decimal places i.e. the number of figures after the decimal point.

The last digit in the answer is unaltered if the next digit on the right is in the group of numbers 0, 1, 2, 3 or 4.

For example,

$$1.714285 \ldots = \mathbf{1} .7 \mathbf{14} \text{ correct to } 4 \text{ significant figures}$$ $$= \mathbf{1.714} \text{ correct to } 3 \text{ decimal places}$$

since the next digit on the right in this example is 2. The last digit in the answer is increased by 1 if the next digit on the right is in the group of numbers 5, 6, 7, 8 or 9. For example,

$$1.7142857 \ldots = \mathbf{1.7143} \text{ correct to } 5 \text{ significant figures}$$ $$= \mathbf{1.7143} \text{ correct to } 4 \text{ decimal places}$$

since the next digit on the right in this example is 8.

(a) $15.36815=\mathbf{15.37}$ correct to 2 decimal places.

üí° Problem 6. Express 0.004369 correct to
(a) 4 decimal places, (b) 3 significant figures

(a) $0.004369=\mathbf{0.0044}$ correct to 4 decimal places.

(b) $0.004369=\mathbf{0.00437}$ correct to 3 significant figures.

Note that the zeros to the right of the decimal point do not count as significant figures.

When multiplying decimal fractions:

  • ‚Ä¢ the numbers are multiplied as if they were integers, and
  • ‚Ä¢ the position of the decimal point in the answer is such that there are as many digits to the right of it as the sum of the digits to the right of the decimal points of the two numbers being multiplied together.

üí° Problem 12. Express $7 \frac{2}{3}$ as a decimal fraction, correct to 4 significant figures

Dividing 2 by 3 gives $\frac{2}{3}=0.666666 \ldots$ and $7 \frac{2}{3}=7.666666 \ldots$

Hence, $7 \frac{\mathbf{2}}{\mathbf{3}}=7.667$ correct to 4 significant figures.

Note that $7.6666 \ldots$ is called 7.6 recurring and is written as $\mathbf{7.6}$
