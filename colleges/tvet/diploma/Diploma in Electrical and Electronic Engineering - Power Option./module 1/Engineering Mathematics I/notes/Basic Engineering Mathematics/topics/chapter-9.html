Basic algebra

üéØ 9.1 Introduction

$3x-4$ is an example of an algebraic expression.

$3x-4=2$ is an example of an algebraic equation (i.e. it contains an '=' sign).

An equation is simply a statement that two expressions are equal.

Hence, $A=\pi r^{2}$ (where A is the area of a circle of radius $r$)

$F=\frac{9}{5} C+32$ (which relates Fahrenheit and Celsius temperatures)

and $y=3x+2$ (which is the equation of a straight line graph)

are all examples of equations.

If you can cope with evaluating formulae then you will be able to cope with algebra.

‚ûï 9.2 Basic operations

Algebra merely uses letters to represent numbers.

If, say, $a, b, c$ and $d$ represent any four numbers then in algebra:

  • (a) $a+a+a+\boldsymbol{a}=4 \boldsymbol{a}$. For example, if $a=2$, then $2+2+2+2=4 \times 2=8$.
  • (b) 5b means $5 \times \boldsymbol{b}$. For example, if $b=4$, then $5b=5 \times 4=20$.
  • (c) $2a+3b+a-2b = 2a+a+3b-2b = 3a+b$
    Only similar terms can be combined in algebra. The $2a$ and the +a can be combined to give $3a$ and the $3b$ and -2b can be combined to give $1b$, which is written as $b$.
    In addition, with terms separated by + and - signs, the order in which they are written does not matter. In this example, $2a+3b+a-2b$ is the same as $2a+a+3b-2b$, which is the same as $3b+a+ 2a-2b$, and so on. (Note that the first term, i.e. 2a, means +2a.)
  • (d) $4abc d = 4 \times a \times b \times c \times d$
    For example, if $a=3, b=-2, c=1$ and $d=-5$, then $4abcd=4 \times 3 \times -2 \times 1 \times -5=120$. (Note that $-\times-=$+)
  • (e) $(\boldsymbol{a})(\boldsymbol{c})(\boldsymbol{d})$ means $\boldsymbol{a} \times \boldsymbol{c} \times \boldsymbol{d}$
    Brackets are often used instead of multiplication signs. For example, (2)(5)(3) means $2 \times 5 \times 3=$ 30.
  • (f) $\boldsymbol{a b}=\boldsymbol{b a}$
    If $a=2$ and $b=3$ then $2 \times 3$ is exactly the same as $3 \times 2$, i.e. 6.
  • (g) $\boldsymbol{b}^{\mathbf{2}}=\boldsymbol{b} \times \boldsymbol{b}$. For example, if $b=3$, then $3^{2}=3 \times 3=9$.
  • (h) $\boldsymbol{a}^{\mathbf{3}}=\boldsymbol{a} \times \boldsymbol{a} \times \boldsymbol{a}$ For example, if $a=2$, then $2^{3}=2 \times 2 \times 2=8$.

Here are some worked examples to help get a feel for basic operations in this introduction to algebra.

9.2.1 Addition and subtraction

üí° Problem 1. Find the sum of $4x, 3x,-2x$ and $-x$

$$4x+3x+-2x+-x = 4x+3x-2x-x$$

(Note that $+\times-=-$)

$$=4x$$

üí° Problem 2. Find the sum of $5x, 3y, z,-3x,-4y$ and $6z$

$$5x+3y+z+-3x+-4y+6z$$ $$= 5x+3y+z-3x-4y+6z$$ $$= 5x-3x+3y-4y+z+6z$$ $$= 2x-y+7z$$

Note that the order can be changed when terms are separated by + and - signs. Only similar terms can be combined.

üí° Problem 3. Simplify $4x^{2}-x-2y+5x+3y$

$$4x^{2}-x-2y+5x+3y = 4x^{2}+5x-x+3y-2y$$ $$= 4x^{2}+\mathbf{4x}+\boldsymbol{y}$$

üí° Problem 4. Simplify $3xy-7x+4xy+2x$

$$3xy-7x+4xy+2x = 3xy+4xy+2x-7x$$ $$= \mathbf{7xy} \mathbf{-5x}$$

9.2.2 Multiplication and division

üí° Problem 5. Simplify $bc \times abc$

$$bc \times abc = a \times b \times b \times c \times c$$ $$= a \times b^{2} \times c^{2}$$ $$= \boldsymbol{a} \boldsymbol{b}^{\mathbf{2}} \boldsymbol{c}^{\mathbf{2}}$$

üí° Problem 6. Simplify $-2p \times -3p$

$-\times-=+ \text{ hence, } -2p \times -3p=\mathbf{6} \boldsymbol{p}^{\mathbf{2}}$

üí° Problem 7. Simplify $ab \times b^{2}c \times a$

$$ab \times b^{2}c \times a = a \times a \times b \times b \times b \times c$$ $$= a^{2} \times b^{3} \times c$$ $$= \boldsymbol{a}^{\mathbf{2}} \boldsymbol{b}^{\mathbf{3}} \boldsymbol{c}$$

üí° Problem 8. Evaluate $3ab+4bc-abc$ when $a=3, b=2$ and $c=5$

$$3ab+4bc-abc = 3 \times a \times b+4 \times b \times c-a \times b \times c$$ $$= 3 \times 3 \times 2+4 \times 2 \times 5-3 \times 2 \times 5$$ $$= 18+40-30$$ $$= \mathbf{28}$$

üí° Problem 9. Determine the value of $5pq^{2}r^{3}$, given that $p=2, q=\frac{2}{5}$ and $r=2 \frac{1}{2}$

$$5pq^{2}r^{3} = 5 \times p \times q^{2} \times r^{3}$$ $$= 5 \times 2 \times \left(\frac{2}{5}\right)^{2} \times \left(2 \frac{1}{2}\right)^{3}$$
$$= 5 \times 2 \times \left(\frac{2}{5}\right)^{2} \times \left(\frac{5}{2}\right)^{3} \quad \text{since } 2 \frac{1}{2}=\frac{5}{2}$$ $$= \frac{5}{1} \times \frac{2}{1} \times \frac{2}{5} \times \frac{2}{5} \times \frac{5}{2} \times \frac{5}{2} \times \frac{5}{2}$$ $$= \frac{1}{1} \times \frac{1}{1} \times \frac{1}{1} \times \frac{1}{1} \times \frac{1}{1} \times \frac{5}{1} \times \frac{5}{1} \quad \text{by cancelling}$$ $$= 5 \times 5$$ $$= 25$$

üí° Problem 10. Multiply $2a+3b$ by $a+b$

Each term in the first expression is multiplied by $a$, then each term in the first expression is multiplied by $b$ and the two results are added. The usual layout is shown below.


 Multiplying by $a$ gives           2a + 3b
                                  a + b
                                 -------
                                  2a2 + 3ab
 Multiplying by $b$ gives                   2ab + 3b2
 Adding gives                    -----------
                                  2a2 + 5ab + 3b2
                                 -----------

Thus, $(2a+3b)(a+b)=2a^{2}+5ab+3b^{2}$

                                  3x - 2y2 + 4xy
                                  2x - 5y
                                 -------------------
 Multiplying by $2x$ $\rightarrow$   6x2 - 4xy2 + 8x2y
 Multiplying by -5y $\rightarrow$         -20xy2             -15xy + 10y3

üí° Problem 12. Simplify $2x \div 8xy$

$2x \div 8xy$ means $\frac{2x}{8xy}$

$$\frac{2x}{8xy} = \frac{2 \times x}{8 \times x \times y}$$ $$= \frac{1 \times 1}{4 \times 1 \times y} \quad \text{by cancelling}$$ $$= \frac{1}{4y}$$

üí° Problem 13. Simplify $\frac{9a^{2}bc}{3ac}$

$$\frac{9a^{2}bc}{3ac} = \frac{9 \times a \times a \times b \times c}{3 \times a \times c}$$ $$= 3 \times a \times b$$ $$= \mathbf{3ab}$$

üí° Problem 14. Divide $2x^{2}+x-3$ by $x-1$

  • ‚Ä¢ $2x^{2}+x-3$ is called the dividend and $x-1$ the divisor. The usual layout is shown below with the dividend and divisor both arranged in descending powers of the symbols.

         2x + 3
        _______
x - 1 | 2x2 + x - 3
        2x2 - 2x
        -------
             3x - 3
             3x - 3
             ------
                  ?
  • ‚Ä¢ Dividing the first term of the dividend by the first term of the divisor, i.e. $\frac{2x^{2}}{x}$ gives $2x$, which is put above the first term of the dividend as shown.
  • ‚Ä¢ The divisor is then multiplied by $2x$, i.e. $2x(x-1)=2x^{2}-2x$, which is placed under the dividend as shown. Subtracting gives $3x-3$.
  • ‚Ä¢ The process is then repeated, i.e. the first term of the divisor, $x$, is divided into $3x$, giving +3 , which is placed above the dividend as shown.
  • ‚Ä¢ Then $3(x-1)=3x-3$, which is placed under the $3x-3$. The remainder, on subtraction, is zero, which completes the process.

Thus, $(2x^{2}+x-3) \div (x-1)=(2x+3)$.

(A check can be made on this answer by multiplying $(2x+3)$ by $(x-1)$, which equals $2x^{2}+x-3$.)

üí° Problem 15. Simplify $\frac{x^{3}+y^{3}}{x+y}$


        x2 - xy + y2
      ___________
x + y | x3 + 0 + 0 + y3
        x3 + x2y
        ---------
             -x2y + 0
             -x2y - xy2
             ----------
                  xy2 + y3
                  xy2 + y3
                  ---------
                        .
  • (i) x into x3 goes x2. Put x2 above x3.
  • (ii) x2(x+y) = x3 + x2y
  • (iii) Subtract.
  • (iv) x into -x2y goes -xy. Put -xy above the dividend.
  • (v) -xy(x+y) = -x2y - xy2
  • (vi) Subtract.
  • (vii) x into xy2 goes y2. Put y2 above the dividend.
  • (viii) y2(x+y) = xy2 + y3
  • (ix) Subtract.

Thus, $\frac{x^{3}+y^{3}}{x+y}=x^{2}-xy+y^{2}$.

The zeros shown in the dividend are not normally shown, but are included to clarify the subtraction process and to keep similar terms in their respective columns.

üìê 9.3 Laws of indices

The laws of indices with numbers were covered in Chapter 7; the laws of indices in algebraic terms are as follows:

  • (1) $\boldsymbol{a}^{\boldsymbol{m}} \times \boldsymbol{a}^{\boldsymbol{n}}=\boldsymbol{a}^{\boldsymbol{m+n}}$ For example, $a^{3} \times a^{4}=a^{3+4}=a^{7}$
  • (2) $\frac{\boldsymbol{a}^{\boldsymbol{m}}}{\boldsymbol{a}^{\boldsymbol{n}}}=\boldsymbol{a}^{\boldsymbol{m}-\boldsymbol{n}} \quad$ For example, $\frac{c^{5}}{c^{2}}=c^{5-2}=c^{3}$
  • (3) $\quad\left(\boldsymbol{a}^{\boldsymbol{m}}\right)^{\boldsymbol{n}}=\boldsymbol{a}^{\boldsymbol{m} \boldsymbol{n}} \quad$ For example, $\left(d^{2}\right)^{3}=d^{2\times3}=d^{6}$
  • (4) $\quad \boldsymbol{a}^{\frac{\boldsymbol{m}}{n}}=\sqrt[n]{\boldsymbol{a}^{\boldsymbol{m}}} \quad$ For example, $x^{\frac{4}{3}}=\sqrt[3]{x^{4}}$
  • (5) $\quad \boldsymbol{a}^{-\boldsymbol{n}}=\frac{\mathbf{1}}{\boldsymbol{a}^{\boldsymbol{n}}} \quad$ For example, $3^{-2}=\frac{1}{3^{2}}=\frac{1}{9}$
  • (6) $\quad \boldsymbol{a}^{\mathbf{0}}=\mathbf{1} \quad$ For example, $17^{0}=1$

Here are some worked examples to demonstrate these laws of indices.

üí° Problem 17. Simplify $a^{2}b^{3}c \times ab^{2}c^{5}$

$$a^{2}b^{3}c \times ab^{2}c^{5} = a^{2} \times b^{3} \times c \times a \times b^{2} \times c^{5}$$ $$= a^{2} \times b^{3} \times c^{1} \times a^{1} \times b^{2} \times c^{5}$$

Grouping together like terms gives

$$a^{2} \times a^{1} \times b^{3} \times b^{2} \times c^{1} \times c^{5}$$

Using law (1) of indices gives

$$a^{2+1} \times b^{3+2} \times c^{1+5} = a^{3} \times b^{5} \times c^{6}$$ $$\text{i.e. } \quad a^{2}b^{3}c \times a b^{2}c^{5} = \mathbf{a}^{\mathbf{3}} \boldsymbol{b}^{\mathbf{5}} \boldsymbol{c}^{\mathbf{6}}$$

üí° Problem 18. Simplify $a^{\frac{1}{3}}b^{\frac{3}{2}}c^{-2} \times a^{\frac{1}{6}}b^{\frac{1}{2}}c$

Using law (1) of indices,

$$a^{\frac{1}{3}}b^{\frac{3}{2}}c^{-2} \times a^{\frac{1}{6}}b^{\frac{1}{2}}c = a^{\frac{1}{3}+\frac{1}{6}} \times b^{\frac{3}{2}+\frac{1}{2}} \times c^{-2+1}$$ $$= \boldsymbol{a}^{\mathbf{1/2}} \boldsymbol{b}^{\mathbf{2}} \boldsymbol{c}^{\mathbf{-1}}$$

üí° Problem 19. Simplify $\frac{x^{5}y^{2}z}{x^{2}yz^{3}}$

$$\frac{x^{5}y^{2}z}{x^{2}yz^{3}} = \frac{x^{5} \times y^{2} \times z}{x^{2} \times y \times z^{3}}$$ $$= \frac{x^{5}}{x^{2}} \times \frac{y^{2}}{y^{1}} \times \frac{z}{z^{3}}$$ $$= x^{5-2} \times y^{2-1} \times z^{1-3} \quad \text{by law (2) of indices}$$ $$= x^{3} \times y^{1} \times z^{-2}$$ $$= \boldsymbol{x}^{\mathbf{3}}\boldsymbol{y}\boldsymbol{z}^{\mathbf{-2}} \text{ or } \frac{\boldsymbol{x}^{\mathbf{3}}\boldsymbol{y}}{\boldsymbol{z}^{\mathbf{2}}}$$

üí° Problem 20. Simplify $\frac{a^{3}b^{2}c^{4}}{abc^{-2}}$ and evaluate when $a=3, b=\frac{1}{4}$ and $c=2$

Using law (2) of indices,

$$\frac{a^{3}}{a}=a^{3-1}=a^{2}, \frac{b^{2}}{b}=b^{2-1}=b \text{ and}$$ $$\frac{c^{4}}{c^{-2}}=c^{4--2}=c^{6}$$

Thus, $\frac{a^{3}b^{2}c^{4}}{abc^{-2}}=\boldsymbol{a}^{\mathbf{2}} \boldsymbol{b} \boldsymbol{c}^{\mathbf{6}}$

When $a=3, b=\frac{1}{4}$ and $c=2$,

$a^{2}bc^{6}=(3)^{2}\left(\frac{1}{4}\right)(2)^{6}=(9)\left(\frac{1}{4}\right)(64)=\mathbf{144}$

üí° Problem 21. Simplify $(p^{3})^{2}(q^{2})^{4}$

Using law (3) of indices gives

$$(p^{3})^{2}(q^{2})^{4} = p^{3 \times 2} \times q^{2 \times 4}$$ $$= \boldsymbol{p}^{\mathbf{6}} \boldsymbol{q}^{\mathbf{8}}$$

üí° Problem 22. Simplify $\frac{(mn^{2})^{3}}{(m^{1/2}n^{1/4})^{4}}$

The brackets indicate that each letter in the bracket must be raised to the power outside. Using law (3) of indices gives

$$\frac{(mn^{2})^{3}}{(m^{1/2}n^{1/4})^{4}} = \frac{m^{1\times 3}n^{2\times 3}}{m^{(1/2)\times 4}n^{(1/4)\times 4}}=\frac{m^{3}n^{6}}{m^{2}n^{1}}$$

Using law (2) of indices gives

$$\frac{m^{3}n^{6}}{m^{2}n^{1}} = m^{3-2}n^{6-1}=\boldsymbol{m} \boldsymbol{n}^{\mathbf{5}}$$

üí° Problem 23. Simplify $(a^{3}b)(a^{-4}b^{-2})$, expressing the answer with positive indices only

Using law (1) of indices gives $a^{3+-4}b^{1+-2}=a^{-1}b^{-1}$

Using law (5) of indices gives $a^{-1}b^{-1}=\frac{1}{a^{+1}b^{+1}}=\frac{\mathbf{1}}{\boldsymbol{a} \boldsymbol{b}}$

üí° Problem 24. Simplify $\frac{d^{2}e^{2}f^{1/2}}{(d^{3/2}ef^{5/2})^{2}}$ expressing the answer with positive indices only

Using law (3) of indices gives

$$\frac{d^{2}e^{2}f^{1/2}}{(d^{3/2}ef^{5/2})^{2}} = \frac{d^{2}e^{2}f^{1/2}}{d^{3}e^{2}f^{5}}$$

Using law (2) of indices gives

$$d^{2-3}e^{2-2}f^{\frac{1}{2}-5} = d^{-1}e^{0}f^{-\frac{9}{2}}$$ $$= d^{-1}f^{-\frac{9}{2}} \quad \text{since } e^{0}=1 \text{ from law (6) of indices}$$ $$= \frac{1}{\boldsymbol{d} \boldsymbol{f}^{9/2}}$$

üí° Problem 25. Simplify $\frac{p^{1/2}q^{2}r^{2/3}}{p^{1/4}q^{1/2}r^{1/6}}$ and evaluate when $p=16, q=9$ and $r=4$, taking positive roots only

Using law (2) of indices gives $p^{\frac{1}{2}-\frac{1}{4}}q^{2-\frac{1}{2}}r^{\frac{2}{3}-\frac{1}{6}}$

$$p^{\frac{1}{2}-\frac{1}{4}}q^{2-\frac{1}{2}}r^{\frac{2}{3}-\frac{1}{6}} = p^{\frac{1}{4}}q^{\frac{3}{2}}r^{\frac{1}{2}}$$

When $p=16, q=9$ and $r=4$,

$$p^{\frac{1}{4}}q^{\frac{3}{2}}r^{\frac{1}{2}} = 16^{\frac{1}{4}} 9^{\frac{3}{2}} 4^{\frac{1}{2}}$$

$=(\sqrt[4]{16})(\sqrt{9^{3}})(\sqrt{4})$ from law (4) of indices

$=(2)(3^{3})(2)=\mathbf{108}$

üí° Problem 26. Simplify $\frac{x^{2}y^{3}+xy^{2}}{xy}$

Algebraic expressions of the form $\frac{a+b}{c}$ can be split into $\frac{a}{c}+\frac{b}{c}$. Thus,

$$\frac{x^{2}y^{3}+xy^{2}}{xy} = \frac{x^{2}y^{3}}{xy}+\frac{xy^{2}}{xy} = x^{2-1}y^{3-1}+x^{1-1}y^{2-1}$$ $$= \boldsymbol{x} \boldsymbol{y}^{\mathbf{2}}+\boldsymbol{y}$$

(since $x^{0}=1$, from law (6) of indices).

üí° Problem 27. Simplify $\frac{x^{2}y}{xy^{2}-xy}$

The highest common factor (HCF) of each of the three terms comprising the numerator and denominator is $xy$. Dividing each term by $xy$ gives

$$\frac{x^{2}y}{xy^{2}-xy} = \frac{\frac{x^{2}y}{xy}}{\frac{xy^{2}}{xy}-\frac{xy}{xy}}=\frac{\boldsymbol{x}}{\boldsymbol{y}-\mathbf{1}}$$

üí° Problem 28. Simplify $\frac{a^{2}b}{ab^{2}-a^{1/2}b^{3}}$

The HCF of each of the three terms is $a^{1/2}b$. Dividing each term by $a^{1/2}b$ gives

$$\frac{a^{2}b}{ab^{2}-a^{1/2}b^{3}} = \frac{\frac{a^{2}b}{a^{1/2}b}}{\frac{ab^{2}}{a^{1/2}b}-\frac{a^{1/2}b^{3}}{a^{1/2}b}}=\frac{\boldsymbol{a}^{\mathbf{3} / \mathbf{2}}}{\boldsymbol{a}^{\mathbf{1/2}} \boldsymbol{b}-\boldsymbol{b}^{\mathbf{2}}}$$

üí° Problem 29. Simplify $(a^{3} \sqrt{b} \sqrt{c^{5}})(\sqrt{a} \sqrt[3]{b^{2}} c^{3})$ and evaluate when $a=\frac{1}{4}, b=6$ and $c=1$

Using law (4) of indices, the expression can be written as

$$(a^{3} \sqrt{b} \sqrt{c^{5}})(\sqrt{a} \sqrt[3]{b^{2}} c^{3}) = (a^{3}b^{\frac{1}{2}}c^{\frac{5}{2}})(a^{\frac{1}{2}}b^{\frac{2}{3}}c^{3})$$

Using law (1) of indices gives

$$(a^{3}b^{\frac{1}{2}}c^{\frac{5}{2}})(a^{\frac{1}{2}}b^{\frac{2}{3}}c^{3}) = a^{3+\frac{1}{2}}b^{\frac{1}{2}+\frac{2}{3}}c^{\frac{5}{2}+3}$$ $$= a^{\frac{7}{2}}b^{\frac{7}{6}}c^{\frac{11}{2}}$$

It is usual to express the answer in the same form as the question. Hence,

$$a^{\frac{7}{2}}b^{\frac{7}{6}}c^{\frac{11}{2}}=\sqrt{\boldsymbol{a}^{\mathbf{7}}} \sqrt[6]{\boldsymbol{b}^{7}} \sqrt{\boldsymbol{c}^{\mathbf{11}}}$$

When $a=\frac{1}{4}, b=64$ and $c=1$,

$$\sqrt{a^{7}} \sqrt[6]{b^{7}} \sqrt{c^{11}} = \sqrt{\left(\frac{1}{4}\right)^{7}}\left(\sqrt[6]{64^{7}}\right)\left(\sqrt{1^{11}}\right)$$ $$= \left(\frac{1}{2}\right)^{7}(2)^{7}(1)=\mathbf{1}$$

üí° Problem 30. Simplify $\frac{(x^{2}y^{1/2})(\sqrt{x} \sqrt[3]{y^{2}})}{(x^{5}y^{3})^{1/2}}$

Using laws (3) and (4) of indices gives

$$\frac{(x^{2}y^{1/2})(\sqrt{x} \sqrt[3]{y^{2}})}{(x^{5}y^{3})^{1/2}} = \frac{(x^{2}y^{1/2})(x^{1/2}y^{2/3})}{x^{5/2}y^{3/2}}$$

from laws (5) and (6) of indices.
