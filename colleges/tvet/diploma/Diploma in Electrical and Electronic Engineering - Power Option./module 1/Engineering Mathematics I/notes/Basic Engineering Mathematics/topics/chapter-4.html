Using a calculator

üéØ 4.1 Introduction

In engineering, calculations often need to be performed. For simple numbers it is useful to be able to use mental arithmetic. However, when numbers are larger an electronic calculator needs to be used.

There are several calculators on the market, many of which will be satisfactory for our needs. It is essential to have a scientific notation calculator which will have all the necessary functions needed and more.

This chapter assumes you have a CASIO fx-83ES calculator, or similar, as shown in Figure 4.1.

Besides straightforward addition, subtraction, multiplication and division, which you will already be able to do, we will check that you can use squares, cubes, powers, reciprocals, roots, fractions and trigonometric functions (the latter in preparation for Chapter 21). There are several other functions on the calculator which we do not need to concern ourselves with at this level.

‚ûï 4.4 Evaluation of formulae

The statement $\boldsymbol{y}=\boldsymbol{m x}+\boldsymbol{c}$ is called a formula for $y$ in terms of $m, x$ and $c$.

$y, m, x$ and $c$ are called symbols.

When given values of $m, x$ and $c$ we can evaluate $y$.

There are a large number of formulae used in engineering and in this section we will insert numbers in place of symbols to evaluate engineering quantities.

Just four examples of important formulae are:

  • 1. A straight line graph is of the form $y=m x+c$ (see Chapter 17).
  • 2. Ohm's law states that $V=I \times R$.
  • 3. Velocity is expressed as $v=u+a t$.
  • 4. Force is expressed as $F=m \times a$.

Here are some practical examples. Check with your calculator that you agree with the working and answers.

üí° Problem 20. In an electrical circuit the voltage $V$ is given by Ohm's law, i.e. $V=I R$. Find, correct to 4 significant figures, the voltage when $I=5.36 \mathrm{~A}$ and $R=14.76 \Omega$

$$V=I R=(5.36)(14.76) = 79.0896$$

Hence, voltage $V=79.11 \mathrm{~V}$, correct to 4 significant figures.

üí° Problem 21. The surface area $A$ of a hollow cone is given by $A=\pi r l$. Determine, correct to 1 decimal place, the surface area when $r=3.0 \mathrm{~cm}$ and $l=8.5 \mathrm{~cm}$

$$A=\pi r l=\pi(3.0)(8.5) \mathrm{cm}^{2}$$

Hence, surface area $A=80.1 \mathrm{~cm}^{2}$, correct to 1 decimal place.

üí° Problem 22. Velocity $v$ is given by $v=u+a t$. If $u=9.54 \mathrm{~m} / \mathrm{s}, a=3.67 \mathrm{~m} / \mathrm{s}^{2}$ and $t=7.82 \mathrm{~s}$, find $v$, correct to 3 significant figures

$$v = u+at = 9.54 + 3.67 \times 7.82$$ $$= 9.54 + 28.6994 = 38.2394$$

Hence, velocity $v=38.2 \mathrm{~m} / \mathrm{s}$, correct to 3 significant figures.

üí° Problem 23. The area, $A$, of a circle is given by $A=\pi r^{2}$. Determine the area correct to 2 decimal places, given radius $r=5.23 \mathrm{~m}$

$$A=\pi r^{2}=\pi(5.23)^{2}=\pi(27.3529)$$

Hence, area, $A=\mathbf{85.93} \mathrm{m}^{2}$, correct to 2 decimal places.

üí° Problem 24. Density $=\frac{\text{mass}}{\text{volume}}$. Find the density when the mass is 6.45 kg and the volume is $300 \times 10^{-6} \mathrm{~cm}^{3}$

Density $=\frac{\text{mass}}{\text{volume}} = \frac{6.45 \mathrm{~kg}}{300 \times 10^{-6} \mathrm{~m}^{3}}=\mathbf{21500} \mathbf{k g} / \mathbf{m}^{\mathbf{3}}$

üí° Problem 25. The power, $P$ watts, dissipated in an electrical circuit is given by the formula $P=\frac{V^{2}}{R}$. Evaluate the power, correct to 4 significant figures, given that $V=230 V$ and $R=35.63 \Omega$

$$P=\frac{V^{2}}{R}=\frac{(230)^{2}}{35.63}=\frac{52900}{35.63}=1484.70390 \ldots$$

Press ENG and $1.48470390 \ldots \times 10^{3}$ appears on the screen.

Hence, power, $P=1485 \mathrm{~W}$ or 1.485 kW correct to 4 significant figures.

üí° Problem 26. The volume $V \mathrm{~cm}^{3}$ of a right circular cone is given by $V=\frac{1}{3} \pi r^{2} h$. Given that radius $r=2.45 \mathrm{~cm}$ and height $h=18.7 \mathrm{~cm}$, find the volume, correct to 4 significant figures

$$V = \frac{1}{3} \pi r^{2} h = \frac{1}{3} \pi (2.45)^{2} (18.7)$$ $$= \frac{1}{3} \times \pi \times 2.45^{2} \times 18.7$$ $$= 117.544521 \ldots$$

Hence, volume, $V=\mathbf{1 17.5} \mathrm{cm}^{\mathbf{3}}$, correct to $\mathbf{4}$ significant figures.

üí° Problem 27. Force $F$ newtons is given by the formula $F=\frac{G m_{1} m_{2}}{d^{2}}$, where $m_{1}$ and $m_{2}$ are masses, $d$ their distance apart and $G$ is a constant. Find the value of the force given that $G=6.67 \times 10^{-11}, m_{1}=7.36, m_{2}=15.5$ and $d=22.6$. Express the answer in standard form, correct to 3 significant figures

$$F=\frac{G m_{1} m_{2}}{d^{2}} = \frac{(6.67 \times 10^{-11})(7.36)(15.5)}{(22.6)^{2}}$$ $$= \frac{(6.67)(7.36)(15.5)}{(10^{11})(510.76)} = \frac{1.490}{10^{11}}$$

Hence, force $\boldsymbol{F}=\mathbf{1.49\times10^{-11}}$ newtons, correct to 3 significant figures.

üí° Problem 28. The time of swing, $t$ seconds, of a simple pendulum is given by $t=2 \pi \sqrt{\frac{T}{g}}$
Determine the time, correct to 3 decimal places, given that $l=12.9$ and $g=9.81$

$$t=2 \pi \sqrt{\frac{l}{g}}=(2 \pi) \sqrt{\frac{12.9}{9.81}} = 7.20510343 \ldots$$

Hence, time $t=7.205$ seconds, correct to 3 decimal places.

üí° Problem 29. Resistance, $R \Omega$, varies with temperature according to the formula $R=R_{0}(1+\alpha t)$. Evaluate $R$, correct to 3 significant figures, given $R_{0}=14.59, \alpha=0.0043$ and $t=80$

$$R=R_{0}(1+\alpha t) = 14.59[1+(0.0043)(80)]$$ $$= 14.59(1+0.344)$$ $$= 14.59(1.344)$$

Hence, resistance, $\boldsymbol{R}=\mathbf{1 9.6} \Omega$, correct to 3 significant figures.

üí° Problem 30. The current, $I$ amperes, in an a.c. circuit is given by $I=\frac{V}{\sqrt{\left(R^{2}+X^{2}\right)}}$. Evaluate the current, correct to 2 decimal places, when $V=250 \mathrm{~V}, R=25.0 \Omega$ and $X=18.0 \Omega$.

$$I=\frac{V}{\sqrt{\left(R^{2}+X^{2}\right)}}=\frac{250}{\sqrt{\left(25.0^{2}+18.0^{2}\right)}}=8.11534341 \ldots$$

Hence, current, $I=8.12 A$, correct to 2 decimal places.
