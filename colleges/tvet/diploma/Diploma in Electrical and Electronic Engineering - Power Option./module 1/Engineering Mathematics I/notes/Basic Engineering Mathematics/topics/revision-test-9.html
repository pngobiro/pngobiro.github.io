Revision Test 9 : Trigonometric waveforms and practical trigonometry

This assignment covers the material contained in Chapters 22-24. The marks available are shown in brackets at the end of each question.

  1. 1. A sine wave is given by $y=8 \sin 4x$. State its peak value and its period, in degrees. (2)
  2. 2. A periodic function is given by $y=15 \tan 2x$. State its period in degrees. (2)
  3. 3. The frequency of a sine wave is 800 Hz . Calculate the periodic time in milliseconds. (2)
  4. 4. Calculate the frequency of a sine wave that has a periodic time of 40 $\mu$s. (2)
  5. 5. Calculate the periodic time for a sine wave having a frequency of 20 kHz . (2)
  6. 6. An alternating current completes 12 cycles in 16 ms . What is its frequency? (2)
  7. 7. A sinusoidal voltage is given by $e=150 \sin (500 \pi t-0.25)$ volts. Determine the
    (a) amplitude,
    (b) frequency,
    (c) periodic time,
    (d) phase angle (stating whether it is leading or lagging $150 \sin 500 \pi t$).(8)
  8. 8. Determine the acute angles in degrees, degrees and minutes, and radians.
    (a) $\sin^{-1} 0.4721$
    (b) $\cos^{-1} 0.8457$
    (c) $\tan^{-1} 1.3472$(9)
  9. 9. Sketch the following curves, labelling relevant points.
    (a) $y=4 \cos (\theta+45^{\circ})$
    (b) $y=5 \sin (2t-60^{\circ})$(8)
  10. 10. The current in an alternating current circuit at any time $t$ seconds is given by
    $$i=120 \sin (100 \pi t+0.274) \text{ amperes}$$
    Determine
    (a) the amplitude, frequency, periodic time and phase angle (with reference to $120 \sin 100 \pi t$),
    (b) the value of current when $t=0$,
    (c) the value of current when $t=6 \mathrm{~ms}$.
    Sketch one cycle of the oscillation.(11)
  11. 11. A triangular plot of land $A B C$ is shown in Figure RT9.1. Solve the triangle and determine its area.
    Figure RT9.1(9)
  12. 12. A car is travelling 20 m above sea level. It then travels 500 m up a steady slope of $17^{\circ}$. Determine, correct to the nearest metre, how high the car is now above sea level.(3)
  13. 13. Figure RT9.2 shows a roof truss $PQR$ with rafter $PQ=3 \mathrm{~m}$. Calculate the length of
    (a) the roof rise $P P^{\prime}$,
    (b) rafter $P R$,
    (c) the roof span $Q R$.
    Find also (d) the cross-sectional area of the roof truss.
    Figure RT9.2(10)
  14. 14. Solve triangle $A B C$ given $b=10 \mathrm{~cm}, c=15 \mathrm{~cm}$ and $\angle A=60^{\circ}$.(9)
  15. 15. Change the following Cartesian co-ordinates into polar co-ordinates, correct to 2 decimal places, in both degrees and in radians.
    (a) $(-2.3, 5.4)$
    (b) $(7.6, -9.2)$(6)
  16. 16. Change the following polar co-ordinates into Cartesian co-ordinates, correct to 3 decimal places.
    (a) $(6.5, 132^{\circ})$
    (b) (3, 3 rad)(4)
