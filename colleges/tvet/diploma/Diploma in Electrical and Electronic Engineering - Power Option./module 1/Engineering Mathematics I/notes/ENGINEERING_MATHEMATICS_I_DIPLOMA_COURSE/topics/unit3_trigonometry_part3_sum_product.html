⇄3.3 SUM AND PRODUCT FORMULAE

We know that

\(\sin (A+B)=\sin A \cos B+\cos A \sin B \quad (1)\)
\(\sin (A-B)=\sin A \cos B-\cos A \sin B \quad (2)\)
\(\cos (A+B)=\cos A \cos B-\sin A \sin B \quad (3)\)
\(\cos (A-B)=\cos A \cos B+\sin A \sin B \quad (4)\)

Adding (1) and (2), we get

\(\sin (A+B)+\sin (A-B)=2 \sin A \cos B \quad (5)\)

Subtracting (2) from (1), we get

\(\sin (A+B)-\sin (A-B)=2 \cos A \sin B \quad (6)\)

Adding (3) and (4), we get

\(\cos (A+B)+\cos (A-B)=2 \cos A \cos B \quad (7)\)

Subtracting (4) from (3), we get

\(\cos (A+B)-\cos (A-B)=-2 \sin A \sin B \quad (8)\)

If we put,
\(A+B=C \quad (i)\)
\(A-B=D \quad (ii)\)
then
(i) + (ii) \(\Rightarrow 2 \mathrm{~A}=\mathrm{C}+\mathrm{D} \Rightarrow \mathrm{A}=\frac{\mathrm{C}+\mathrm{D}}{2}\)
(i) - (ii) \(\Rightarrow 2 \mathrm{~B}=\mathrm{C}-\mathrm{D} \Rightarrow \mathrm{B}=\frac{\mathrm{C}-\mathrm{D}}{2}\)

Then the results (5), (6), (7) and (8) will become

\(\sin C+\sin D=2 \sin \left(\frac{C+D}{2}\right) \cos \left(\frac{C-D}{2}\right) \quad (9)\)
\(\sin C-\sin D=2 \cos \left(\frac{C+D}{2}\right) \sin \left(\frac{C-D}{2}\right) \quad (10)\)
\(\cos C+\cos D=2 \cos \left(\frac{C+D}{2}\right) \cos \left(\frac{C-D}{2}\right) \quad (11)\)
\(\cos C-\cos D=-2 \sin \left(\frac{C+D}{2}\right) \sin \left(\frac{C-D}{2}\right) \quad (12)\)

The above results are known as sum and product formulae (because their LHS is sum & difference and RHS is product). These formulae can be remembered by the short way

S + S = 2SCwhere
S = sin
C = cos
S - S = 2CS
C + C = 2CC
C - C = -2SS

3.3 WORKED EXAMPLES

PART - A

1. Express the following as sum or difference:

(i) \(2 \sin 2 \mathrm{~A} \cos \mathrm{A}\)
(ii) \(2 \cos 5 \mathrm{~A} \sin 3 \mathrm{~A}\)
(iii) \(2 \cos 3 \mathrm{~A} \cos 2 \mathrm{~A}\)
(iv) \(2 \sin 3 \mathrm{~A} \sin \mathrm{A}\)

Solution:

(i) \(2 \sin 2 A \cos A \quad (2 \mathrm{SC}=\mathrm{S}+\mathrm{S})\)
\(=\sin (2 \mathrm{~A}+\mathrm{A})+\sin (2 \mathrm{~A}-\mathrm{A})\)
\(=\sin 3 \mathrm{~A}+\sin \mathrm{A}\)

(ii) \(2 \cos 5 A \sin 3 A \quad (2 \mathrm{CS}=\mathrm{S}-\mathrm{S})\)
\(=\sin (5 \mathrm{~A}+3 \mathrm{~A})-\sin (5 \mathrm{~A}-3 \mathrm{~A})\)
\(=\sin 8 \mathrm{~A}-\sin 2 \mathrm{~A}\)

(iii) \(2 \cos 3 A \cos 2 A \quad (2 \mathrm{CC}=\mathrm{C}+\mathrm{C})\)
\(=\cos (3 \mathrm{~A}+2 \mathrm{~A})+\cos (3 \mathrm{~A}-2 \mathrm{~A})\)
\(=\cos 5 \mathrm{~A}+\cos \mathrm{A}\)

(iv) \(2 \sin 3 A \sin A \quad (\mathrm{C}-\mathrm{C}=-2 \mathrm{SS})\)
\(=-[ - (2 \sin 3 \mathrm{~A} \sin \mathrm{A}) ]\)
\(=-[\cos (3 \mathrm{~A}+\mathrm{A})-\cos (3 \mathrm{~A}-\mathrm{A})]\)
\(=-[\cos 4 \mathrm{~A}-\cos 2 \mathrm{~A}]=\cos 2 \mathrm{~A}-\cos 4 \mathrm{~A}\)

2. Express the following as product

i) \(\sin 4 A+\sin 2 A\)
(ii) \(\sin 5 A-\sin 3 A\)
iii) \(\cos 7 A+\cos 3 A\)
iv) \(\cos 4 A-\cos 2 A\)

Solution:

i) \(\sin 4 A+\sin 2 A \quad (\mathrm{S}+\mathrm{S}=2 \mathrm{SC})\)
\(=2 \sin \left(\frac{4 \mathrm{~A}+2 \mathrm{~A}}{2}\right) \cos \left(\frac{4 \mathrm{~A}-2 \mathrm{~A}}{2}\right)\)
\(=2 \sin \left(\frac{6 \mathrm{~A}}{2}\right) \cos \left(\frac{2 \mathrm{~A}}{2}\right)\)
\(=2 \sin 3 \mathrm{~A} \cos \mathrm{A}\)

ii) \(\sin 5 \mathrm{~A}-\sin 3 \mathrm{~A} \quad (\mathrm{S}-\mathrm{S}=2 \mathrm{CS})\)
\(=2 \cos \left(\frac{5 \mathrm{~A}+3 \mathrm{~A}}{2}\right) \sin \left(\frac{5 \mathrm{~A}-3 \mathrm{~A}}{2}\right)\)
\(=2 \cos \left(\frac{8 \mathrm{~A}}{2}\right) \sin \left(\frac{2 \mathrm{~A}}{2}\right)=2 \cos 4 \mathrm{~A} \sin \mathrm{A}\)

iii) \(\cos 7 \mathrm{~A}+\cos 3 \mathrm{~A} \quad (\mathrm{C}+\mathrm{C}=2 \mathrm{CC})\)
\(=2 \cos \left(\frac{7 \mathrm{~A}+3 \mathrm{~A}}{2}\right) \cos \left(\frac{7 \mathrm{~A}-3 \mathrm{~A}}{2}\right)\)
\(=2 \cos \left(\frac{10 \mathrm{~A}}{2}\right) \cos \left(\frac{4 \mathrm{~A}}{2}\right)=2 \cos 5 \mathrm{~A} \cos 2 \mathrm{~A}\)

iv) \(\cos 4 \mathrm{~A}-\cos 2 \mathrm{~A} \quad (\mathrm{C}-\mathrm{C}=-2 \mathrm{SS})\)
\(=-2 \sin \left(\frac{4 \mathrm{~A}+2 \mathrm{~A}}{2}\right) \sin \left(\frac{4 \mathrm{~A}-2 \mathrm{~A}}{2}\right)\)
\(=-2 \sin \left(\frac{6 \mathrm{~A}}{2}\right) \sin \left(\frac{2 \mathrm{~A}}{2}\right)\)
\( =-2 \sin (3 \mathrm{~A}) \sin (\mathrm{A})\)

3. Write \(\sin 5 A-\sin 7 A\) in product form.

Solution:

\(\sin 5 A-\sin 7 A \quad (\mathrm{S}-\mathrm{S}=2 \mathrm{CS})\)
\(=2 \cos \left(\frac{5 \mathrm{~A}+7 \mathrm{~A}}{2}\right) \sin \left(\frac{5 \mathrm{~A}-7 \mathrm{~A}}{2}\right)\)
\(=2 \cos \left(\frac{12 \mathrm{~A}}{2}\right) \sin \left(\frac{-2 \mathrm{~A}}{2}\right)\)
\(=2 \cos (6 \mathrm{~A}) \sin (-\mathrm{A})\)
\(=2 \cos 6 A(-\sin A) \quad (\because \sin(-\theta)=-\sin \theta)\)
\(=-2 \cos 6 \mathrm{~A} \sin \mathrm{A}\)

4. Write the product \(2 \cos 4 \mathrm{~A} \cos 6 \mathrm{~A}\) as sum or difference of trigonometric function.

Solution:

\(2 \cos 4 A \cos 6 A \quad (\mathrm{C}+\mathrm{C}=2 \mathrm{CC})\)
\(=\cos (4 A+6 A)+\cos (4 A-6 A)\)
\(=\cos 10 A+\cos (-2 A)\)
\(=\cos 10 A+\cos 2 A \quad (\because \cos(-\theta)=\cos \theta)\)

PART -B

1. Prove that \(\sin 50^{\circ}-\sin 70^{\circ}+\sin 10^{\circ}=0\)

Solution:

LHS \(=\sin 50^{\circ}+\sin 10^{\circ}-\sin 70^{\circ} \quad (\mathrm{S}+\mathrm{S}=2 \mathrm{SC})\)
\(=2 \sin \left(\frac{50^{\circ}+10^{\circ}}{2}\right) \cos \left(\frac{50^{\circ}-10^{\circ}}{2}\right)-\sin 70^{\circ}\)
\(=2 \sin \left(\frac{60^{\circ}}{2}\right) \cos \left(\frac{40^{\circ}}{2}\right)-\sin 70^{\circ}\)
\(=2 \sin \left(30^{\circ}\right) \cos \left(20^{\circ}\right)-\sin (90^{\circ}-20^{\circ})\)
\(=2 \times \frac{1}{2} \cos 20^{\circ}-\cos 20^{\circ}\) (change of ratio for \(90^{\circ}\))
\(=\cos 20^{\circ}-\cos 20^{\circ}\)
\(=0=\mathrm{RHS}\)

2. Prove that \(\cos 20^{\circ}+\cos 100^{\circ}+\cos 140^{\circ}=0\)

Solution:

LHS \(=\cos 140^{\circ}+\cos 100^{\circ}+\cos 20^{\circ} \quad (\mathrm{C}+\mathrm{C}=2 \mathrm{CC})\)
\(=2 \cos \left(\frac{140^{\circ}+100^{\circ}}{2}\right) \cos \left(\frac{140^{\circ}-100^{\circ}}{2}\right)+\cos 20^{\circ}\)
\(=2 \cos \left(\frac{240^{\circ}}{2}\right) \cos \left(\frac{40^{\circ}}{2}\right)+\cos 20^{\circ}\)
\(=2 \cos 120^{\circ} \cos 20^{\circ}+\cos 20^{\circ}\)
\( \text{Since } \cos 120^{\circ}=\cos \left(180^{\circ}-60^{\circ}\right) = -\cos 60^{\circ} = -\frac{1}{2} \)
\(=2 \times -\frac{1}{2} \cos 20^{\circ}+\cos 20^{\circ}\)
\( = -\cos 20^{\circ}+\cos 20^{\circ} \)
\( =0 = \text{RHS} \)

3. Prove that \(\cos A+\cos \left(120^{\circ}+A\right)+\cos \left(120^{\circ}-A\right)=0\).

Solution:

LHS \(=\cos \left(120^{\circ}+A\right)+\cos \left(120^{\circ}-A\right)+\cos A \quad (\mathrm{C}+\mathrm{C}=2 \mathrm{CC})\)
\(=2 \cos \left[\frac{120^{\circ}+A+120^{\circ}-A}{2}\right] \cos \left[\frac{120^{\circ}+A-\left(120^{\circ}-A\right)}{2}\right]+\cos A\)
\(=2 \cos \left[\frac{240^{\circ}}{2}\right] \cos \left[\frac{120^{\circ}+A-120^{\circ}+A}{2}\right]+\cos A\)
\(=2 \cos \left(120^{\circ}\right) \cos \left(\frac{2 A}{2}\right)+\cos A\)
\(=2 \times -\frac{1}{2} \cos A+\cos A\)
\( = -\cos A+\cos A \)
\(=0=\mathrm{RHS}\)

4. Prove that \(\frac{\sin 2 \mathrm{~A}-\sin 2 \mathrm{~B}}{\cos 2 \mathrm{~A}+\cos 2 \mathrm{~B}}=\tan (\mathrm{A}-\mathrm{B})\).

Solution:

LHS \(=\frac{\sin 2 A-\sin 2 B}{\cos 2 A+\cos 2 B} \quad (\mathrm{S}-\mathrm{S}=2 \mathrm{CS} \quad \mathrm{C}+\mathrm{C}=2 \mathrm{CC})\)
\(=\frac{2 \cos \left(\frac{2 A+2 B}{2}\right) \sin \left(\frac{2 A-2 B}{2}\right)}{2 \cos \left(\frac{2 A+2 B}{2}\right) \cos \left(\frac{2 A-2 B}{2}\right)}\)
\(=\frac{2 \cos (A+B) \sin (A-B)}{2 \cos (A+B) \cos (A-B)}\)
\(=\frac{\sin(A-B)}{\cos(A-B)}\)
\(=\tan (\mathrm{A}-\mathrm{B})=\) RHS

PART - C

1. Prove that \((\cos \alpha+\cos \beta)^{2}+(\sin \alpha+\sin \beta)^{2}=4 \cos ^{2}\left(\frac{\alpha-\beta}{2}\right)\).

Solution:

LHS \( =(\cos \alpha+\cos \beta)^{2}+(\sin \alpha+\sin \beta)^{2} \)
Using Formulae: \(\mathrm{C}+\mathrm{C}=2 \mathrm{CC} \quad \mathrm{S}+\mathrm{S}=2 \mathrm{SC}\)
\(=\left[2 \cos \left(\frac{\alpha+\beta}{2}\right) \cos \left(\frac{\alpha-\beta}{2}\right)\right]^{2}+\left[2 \sin \left(\frac{\alpha+\beta}{2}\right) \cos \left(\frac{\alpha-\beta}{2}\right)\right]^{2}\)
\(=4 \cos ^{2}\left(\frac{\alpha+\beta}{2}\right) \cos ^{2}\left(\frac{\alpha-\beta}{2}\right)+4 \sin ^{2}\left(\frac{\alpha+\beta}{2}\right) \cos ^{2}\left(\frac{\alpha-\beta}{2}\right)\)
\(4 \cos ^{2}\left(\frac{\alpha-\beta}{2}\right)\) is common for both the terms
\(=4 \cos ^{2}\left(\frac{\alpha-\beta}{2}\right)\left[\cos ^{2}\left(\frac{\alpha+\beta}{2}\right)+\sin ^{2}\left(\frac{\alpha+\beta}{2}\right)\right]\)
\(=4 \cos ^{2}\left(\frac{\alpha-\beta}{2}\right) \times 1 \quad\left[\because \cos ^{2} \theta+\sin ^{2} \theta=1\right]\)
\(=4 \cos ^{2}\left(\frac{\alpha-\beta}{2}\right)=\) RHS

2. If \(\sin x+\sin y=a\) and \(\cos x+\cos y=b\). Prove that \(\tan ^{2}\left(\frac{x-y}{2}\right)=\frac{4-a^{2}-b^{2}}{a^{2}+b^{2}}\).

Solution:

Given \(a=\sin x+\sin y \quad b=\cos x+\cos y\)
\(a^{2}+b^{2} =[\sin x+\sin y]^{2}+[\cos x+\cos y]^{2}\)
Using Formula \(\mathrm{S}+\mathrm{S}=2 \mathrm{SC} \quad \mathrm{C}+\mathrm{C}=2 \mathrm{CC}\)
\(=\left[2 \sin \left(\frac{x+y}{2}\right) \cos \left(\frac{x-y}{2}\right)\right]^{2}+\left[2 \cos \left(\frac{x+y}{2}\right) \cos \left(\frac{x-y}{2}\right)\right]^{2}\)
\(=4 \sin ^{2}\left(\frac{x+y}{2}\right) \cos ^{2}\left(\frac{x-y}{2}\right)+4 \cos ^{2}\left(\frac{x+y}{2}\right) \cos ^{2}\left(\frac{x-y}{2}\right)\)
\(4 \cos ^{2}\left(\frac{x-y}{2}\right)\) is common to both terms
\(=4 \cos ^{2}\left(\frac{x-y}{2}\right)\left[\sin ^{2}\left(\frac{x+y}{2}\right)+\cos ^{2}\left(\frac{x+y}{2}\right)\right]\)
\(=4 \cos ^{2}\left(\frac{x-y}{2}\right) \times 1 \quad \because \sin ^{2} \theta+\cos ^{2} \theta=1\)
\(a^{2}+b^{2} =4 \cos ^{2}\left(\frac{x-y}{2}\right)\)

RHS \(=\frac{4-a^{2}-b^{2}}{\left(a^{2}+b^{2}\right)}=\frac{4-\left(a^{2}+b^{2}\right)}{\left(a^{2}+b^{2}\right)}\)
\(=\frac{4-4 \cos ^{2}\left(\frac{x-y}{2}\right)}{4 \cos ^{2}\left(\frac{x-y}{2}\right)}=\frac{4\left[1-\cos ^{2}\left(\frac{x-y}{2}\right)\right]}{4 \cos ^{2}\left(\frac{x-y}{2}\right)}\)
\(=\frac{\sin ^{2}\left(\frac{x-y}{2}\right)}{\cos ^{2}\left(\frac{x-y}{2}\right)} \quad \because 1-\cos ^{2} \theta=\sin ^{2} \theta\)
\(=\tan ^{2}\left(\frac{x-y}{2}\right)=\) RHS

3. If \(\sin x+\sin y=a\) and \(\cos x+\cos y=b\) prove that \(\sin (x+y)=\frac{2 a b}{a^{2}+b^{2}}\)

Solution:

\(a^{2}+b^{2} =4 \cos ^{2}\left(\frac{x-y}{2}\right) \quad\) [Refer Example 2]

\(2 a b =2(\sin x+\sin y)(\cos x+\cos y)\)
Using \(\mathrm{S}+\mathrm{S}=2 \mathrm{SC} \quad \mathrm{C}+\mathrm{C}=2 \mathrm{CC}\)
\(=2\left[2 \sin \left(\frac{x+y}{2}\right) \cos \left(\frac{x-y}{2}\right)\right] \cdot \left[2 \cos \left(\frac{x+y}{2}\right) \cos \left(\frac{x-y}{2}\right)\right]\)
\(=8 \sin \left(\frac{x+y}{2}\right) \cos \left(\frac{x+y}{2}\right) \cos ^{2}\left(\frac{x-y}{2}\right)\)

RHS \(=\frac{2 a b}{a^{2}+b^{2}}=\frac{8 \sin \left(\frac{x+y}{2}\right) \cos \left(\frac{x+y}{2}\right) \cos ^{2}\left(\frac{x-y}{2}\right)}{4 \cos ^{2}\left(\frac{x-y}{2}\right)}\)
\(=2 \sin \left(\frac{x+y}{2}\right) \cos \left(\frac{x+y}{2}\right)\)
Using \(2 \sin \theta \cos \theta=\sin 2 \theta\) where \(\theta=\frac{x+y}{2}\)
\(=\sin \left[2 \times \frac{(x+y)}{2}\right]\)
\(=\sin (x+y)=\mathrm{LHS}\)

4. Prove that \(\frac{\sin A+\sin 2 A+\sin 3 A}{\cos A+\cos 2 A+\cos 3 A}=\tan 2 A\).

Solution:

LHS \(=\frac{(\sin 3 A+\sin A)+\sin 2 A}{(\cos 3 A+\cos A)+\cos 2 A}\) (Using \(\mathrm{S}+\mathrm{S}=2 \mathrm{SC}\) and \(\mathrm{C}+\mathrm{C}=2 \mathrm{CC}\))
\(=\frac{2 \sin \left(\frac{3 A+A}{2}\right) \cos \left(\frac{3 A-A}{2}\right)+\sin 2 A}{2 \cos \left(\frac{3 A+A}{2}\right) \cos \left(\frac{3 A-A}{2}\right)+\cos 2 A}\)
\(=\frac{2 \sin \left(\frac{4 A}{2}\right) \cos \left(\frac{2 A}{2}\right)+\sin 2 A}{2 \cos \left(\frac{4 A}{2}\right) \cos \left(\frac{2 A}{2}\right)+\cos 2 A}\)
\(=\frac{2 \sin (2 A) \cos A+\sin 2 A}{2 \cos 2 A \cos A+\cos 2 A}\)
\(=\frac{\sin 2 A[2 \cos A+1]}{\cos 2 A[2 \cos A+1]}\) (Factoring out \(\sin 2A\) and \(\cos 2A\))
\(=\frac{\sin 2 A}{\cos 2 A}\)
\(=\tan 2 A=\mathrm{RHS}\)
