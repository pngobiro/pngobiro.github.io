üå±2.3 ROOTS OF COMPLEX NUMBERS

Definition:

A number \(\omega\) is called the \(n^{th}\) root of a complex number z , if \(\omega^{n}=z\) and we write \(\omega=z^{\frac{1}{n}}\).

Working rule to find the \(n^{th}\) roots of a complex numbers:

  1. Step (I): Write the given complex number in Polar form.
  2. Step (II): Add " \(2k \pi\) " to the argument.
  3. Step (III): Apply Demoivre's theorem
  4. Step (IV): Put k=0, 1, ....... upto (n-1).

Illustration:

Let \(z=r(\cos \theta+i \sin \theta)\)
\(\Rightarrow z =r[\cos (2 k \pi+\theta)+i \sin (2 \mathrm{k} \pi+\theta)] \quad\) where \(k \in I\)
\(\therefore z^{\frac{1}{n}} =\{r[\cos (2 k \pi+\theta)+i \sin (2 \mathrm{k} \pi+\theta)]\}^{\frac{1}{n}}\)
\(=r^{\frac{1}{n}}[\cos (2 \mathrm{k} \pi+\theta)+i \sin (2 \mathrm{k} \pi+\theta)]^{\frac{1}{n}}\)
\(=r^{\frac{1}{n}}\left[\cos \left(\frac{2 \mathrm{k} \pi+\theta}{n}\right)+\mathrm{i} \sin \left(\frac{2 \mathrm{k} \pi+\theta}{n}\right)\right]\) where \(K=0,1,2, \ldots \ldots ., n-1\).
Only these values of k will give 'n' different values of \(z^{\frac{1}{n}}\) provided \(z \neq 0\).

To find the \(n^{th}\) roots of unity

\(1=\cos 0+i \sin 0=\cos 2 \mathrm{k} \pi+\mathrm{i} \sin 2 \mathrm{k} \pi\)
\(\therefore \mathrm{n}^{\text {th }}\) roots of unity \(=1^{1 / n}=(\cos 2 \mathrm{k} \pi+\mathrm{i} \sin 2 \mathrm{k} \pi)^{1 / n}\)
\(=\cos \left(\frac{2 \mathrm{k} \pi}{\mathrm{n}}\right)+\mathrm{i} \sin \left(\frac{2 \mathrm{k} \pi}{\mathrm{n}}\right)\) where \(k=0,1,2, \ldots \ldots \ldots \ldots \ldots, n-1\)

The roots are,
for \(k=0 ; \quad R_{1}=\cos 0+i \sin 0=1+i 0=1=e^{i 0}\)
\(k=1 ; \quad R_{2}=\cos \frac{2 \pi}{n}+i \sin \frac{2 \pi}{n}=e^{i \frac{2 \pi}{n}}=\omega\) (say)
\(k=2 ; \quad R_{3}=\cos \frac{4 \pi}{n}+i \sin \frac{4 \pi}{n}=e^{i \frac{4 \pi}{n}}=\left[e^{i^{\frac{2 \pi}{n}}}\right]^{2}=\omega^{2}\)
...
\(k=n-1 ; R_{n}=\cos \frac{2(n-1) \pi}{n}+i \sin \frac{2(n-1) \pi}{n}=e^{i \frac{2(n-1) \pi}{n}}=\omega^{n-1}\)

\(\therefore\) The \(n^{th}\) roots of unity are
\(e^{i 0}, e^{i \frac{2 \pi}{n}}, e^{i \frac{4 \pi}{n}}, \ldots \ldots \ldots . ., e^{i \frac{2(n-1) \pi}{n}}\)
i.e., \(1, \omega, \omega^{2}, \ldots \ldots \ldots . ., \omega^{n-1}\).

üí°Result

If \(\omega\) is \(n^{th}\) roots of unity then

  1. (i) \(\omega^{n}=1\)
  2. (ii) Sum of the roots is zero.
    i.e \(1+\omega+\omega^{2}+\ldots \ldots \ldots+\omega^{n-1}=0\)
  3. (iii) The roots are in G.P with common ratio \(\omega\).
  4. (iv) The arguments are in A.P with common difference \(\frac{2 \pi}{n}\).
  5. (v) The product of the roots is \((-1)^{n-1}\).

To find cube roots of unity

Let \(x=(1)^{1 / 3}\)
\( =(\cos 0+i \sin 0)^{1 / 3} \)
\( =(\cos 2 \mathrm{k} \pi+\mathrm{i} \sin 2 \mathrm{k} \pi)^{1 / 3} \)
\( =\cos \left(\frac{2 \mathrm{k} \pi}{3}\right)+\mathrm{i} \sin \left(\frac{2 \mathrm{k} \pi}{3}\right) \) where \( k=0,1,2 \)

\(\therefore\) The roots are
for \(k=0 ; \quad R_{1}=\cos 0+i \sin 0=1+i 0=1\)
\(k=1 ; \quad R_{2}=\cos \frac{2 \pi}{3}+i \sin \frac{2 \pi}{3}=-\frac{1}{2}+i \frac{\sqrt{3}}{2}\)
\(k=2 ; \quad R_{3}=\cos \frac{4 \pi}{3}+i \sin \frac{4 \pi}{3}=-\frac{1}{2}-i \frac{\sqrt{3}}{2}\)

The cube roots of unity are \(1,-\frac{1}{2}+i \frac{\sqrt{3}}{2},-\frac{1}{2}-i \frac{\sqrt{3}}{2}\).

üí°Result

If we denote the second root \(R_{2}=\cos \frac{2 \pi}{3}+\mathrm{i} \sin \frac{2 \pi}{3}\) by \(\omega\) then the other root, \(R_{3}=\cos \frac{4 \pi}{3}+\mathrm{i} \sin \frac{4 \pi}{3}\) becomes \(\omega^{2}\)
Thus, \(R_{2}=\cos \frac{2 \pi}{3}+\mathrm{i} \sin \frac{2 \pi}{3}=-\frac{1}{2}+\mathrm{i} \frac{\sqrt{3}}{2}=\omega\)
\(R_{3}=\cos \frac{4 \pi}{3}+\mathrm{i} \sin \frac{4 \pi}{3}=-\frac{1}{2}-\mathrm{i} \frac{\sqrt{3}}{2}=\omega^{2}\)
\(\therefore\) The cube roots of unit are \(1, \omega, \omega^{2}\)

üí°Note

If \(\omega\) is cube roots of unity then (i) \(\omega^{3}=1\), (ii) \(1+\omega+\omega^{2}=0\)

Fourth roots of unity

Let \(x=(1)^{1 / 4}\)
\( =(\cos 0+i \sin 0)^{1 / 4} \)
\( =(\cos 2 \mathrm{k} \pi+\mathrm{i} \sin 2 \mathrm{k} \pi)^{1 / 4} \)
\( =\cos \left(\frac{2 \mathrm{k} \pi}{4}\right)+\mathrm{i} \sin \left(\frac{2 \mathrm{k} \pi}{4}\right) \) where \( k=0,1,2,3 \)

\(\therefore\) The roots are,
for \(k=0 ; \quad R_{1}=\cos 0+i \sin 0=1+i 0=1\)
\(k=1 ; \quad R_{2}=\cos \frac{\pi}{2}+\mathrm{i} \sin \frac{\pi}{2}=0+\mathrm{i}(1)=\mathrm{i}=\omega\) (say)
\(k=2 ; \quad R_{3}=\cos \pi+\mathrm{i} \sin \pi=-1+\mathrm{i}(0)=-1=\omega^{2}\)
\(k=3 ; \quad R_{4}=\cos \frac{3 \pi}{2}+\mathrm{i} \sin \frac{3 \pi}{2}=0+\mathrm{i}(-1)=-\mathrm{i}=\omega^{3}\)

The fourth roots of unity are \(1, i,-1,-\mathrm{i}\) (i.e.) \(1, \omega, \omega^{2}, \omega^{3}\).

üí°Note

  1. (i) The sum of the fourth roots of unity is zero. i.e \(1+\omega+\omega^{2}+\omega^{3}=0\) and \(\omega^{4}=1\).
  2. (ii) The value of \(\omega\) used in cube roots of untiy and in fourth roots of unity are different.

Sixth roots of unity

Let \(x=(1)^{1 / 6}\)
\( =(\cos 0+i \sin 0)^{1 / 6} \)
\( =(\cos 2 \mathrm{k} \pi+\mathrm{i} \sin 2 \mathrm{k} \pi)^{1 / 6} \)
\( =\cos \left(\frac{2 \mathrm{k} \pi}{6}\right)+\mathrm{i} \sin \left(\frac{2 \mathrm{k} \pi}{6}\right) \) where \( k=0,1,2,3,4,5 \)

\(\therefore\) The six roots are
for \(k=0 ; \quad R_{1}=\cos 0+i \sin 0=e^{i 0}=1\)
\(k=1 ; \quad R_{2}=\cos \frac{2 \pi}{6}+i \sin \frac{2 \pi}{6}=e^{i \frac{2 \pi}{6}}=\omega\)
\(k=2 ; \quad R_{3}=\cos \frac{4 \pi}{6}+i \sin \frac{4 \pi}{6}=e^{i \frac{4 \pi}{6}}=\omega^{2}\)
\(k=3 ; \quad R_{4}=\cos \frac{6 \pi}{6}+i \sin \frac{6 \pi}{6}=e^{i \frac{6 \pi}{6}}=\omega^{3}\)
\(k=4 ; \quad R_{5}=\cos \frac{8 \pi}{6}+i \sin \frac{8 \pi}{6}=e^{i \frac{8 \pi}{6}}=\omega^{4}\)
\(k=5 ; \quad R_{6}=\cos \frac{10 \pi}{6}+i \sin \frac{10 \pi}{6}=e^{i \frac{10 \pi}{6}}=\omega^{5}\)

\(\therefore\) The sixth roots of unity are \(e^{i 0}, e^{i \frac{2 \pi}{6}}, e^{i \frac{4 \pi}{6}}, e^{i \frac{6 \pi}{6}}, e^{i \frac{8 \pi}{6}}, e^{i \frac{10 \pi}{6}}\)
i.e, \(1, \omega, \omega^{2}, \omega^{3}, \omega^{4}, \omega^{5}\).

üí°Note

The sum of the sixth roots of unity is zero. i.e \(1+\omega+\omega^{2}+\omega^{3}+\omega^{4}+\omega^{5}=0\) and \(\omega^{6}=1\)

Note: \(1=\cos 0+i \sin 0\)
\(-1=\cos \pi+i \sin \pi\)
\(i=\cos \frac{\pi}{2}+i \sin \frac{\pi}{2}\)
\(-i=\cos \frac{3 \pi}{2}+i \sin \frac{3 \pi}{2}\)

WORKED EXAMPLES

PART - A

1. If \(\omega\) is a cube roots of unity, find the value of \(\omega^{4}+\omega^{5}+\omega^{6}\).

Solution:

If \(\omega\) is cube roots of unity then \(\omega^{3}=1\).
\(\therefore \omega^{4}+\omega^{5}+\omega^{6} =\omega^{3} \cdot \omega+\omega^{3} \cdot \omega^{2}+\omega^{3} \cdot \omega^{3}\)
\( =(1) \omega+(1) \omega^{2}+(1)(1)\)
\( =\omega+\omega^{2}+1\)
\( =1+\omega+\omega^{2}=0\)

2. Simplify: \((1+\omega)\left(1+\omega^{2}\right)\) where \(\omega\) is cube roots of unity.

Solution:

\((1+\omega)\left(1+\omega^{2}\right) =1+\omega^{2}+\omega+\omega^{3}\)
\( =\left[1+\omega+\omega^{2}\right]+\omega^{3}\)
\( =0+1\)
\( =1\)

3. Solve: \(x^{2}-1=0\)

Solution:

Given: \(x^{2}-1=0\)
\(x^{2} =1\)
\(x =(1)^{1 / 2}=[\cos 0+\mathrm{i} \sin 0]^{1 / 2}\)
\( =[\cos 2 \mathrm{k} \pi+\mathrm{i} \sin 2 \mathrm{k} \pi]^{1 / 2}\)
\( =\cos \left(\frac{2 \mathrm{k} \pi}{2}\right)+\mathrm{i} \sin \left(\frac{2 \mathrm{k} \pi}{2}\right) \) where \( k=0,1 \)

(Roots are \(x=1\) for k=0 and \(x=-1\) for k=1)

4. Find the value of \((i)^{1 / 3}\).

Solution:

Let \(x=(i)^{1 / 3}\)
\( =\left[\cos \frac{\pi}{2}+\mathrm{i} \sin \frac{\pi}{2}\right]^{1 / 3} \)
\( =\left[\cos \left(2 \mathrm{k} \pi+\frac{\pi}{2}\right)+\mathrm{i} \sin \left(2 \mathrm{k} \pi+\frac{\pi}{2}\right)\right]^{1 / 3} \)
\( =\cos \frac{1}{3}\left(2 \mathrm{k} \pi+\frac{\pi}{2}\right)+\mathrm{i} \sin \frac{1}{3}\left(2 \mathrm{k} \pi+\frac{\pi}{2}\right) \quad\) where \( k=0,1,2 \).

5. Find the value of \((-1)^{1 / 3}\).

Solution:

Let \(x=(-1)^{1 / 3}\)
\( =(\cos \pi+\mathrm{i} \sin \pi)^{1 / 3} \)
\( =[\cos (2 \mathrm{k} \pi+\pi)+\mathrm{i} \sin (2 \mathrm{k} \pi+\pi)]^{1 / 3} \)
\( =\cos \frac{1}{3}(2 \mathrm{k} \pi+\pi)+\operatorname{isin} \frac{1}{3}(2 \mathrm{k} \pi+\pi) \) where \( k=0,1,2 \)

6. Find the value of \(\left(\frac{-1+i \sqrt{3}}{2}\right)^{3}\).

Solution:

We have \(\frac{-1+i \sqrt{3}}{2}=\frac{-1}{2}+i \frac{\sqrt{3}}{2}=\cos 120^{\circ}+i \sin 120^{\circ}\)
\(\left(\frac{-1+\mathrm{i} \sqrt{3}}{2}\right)^{3} =\left(\cos 120^{\circ}+\mathrm{i} \sin 120^{\circ}\right)^{3}\)
\(=\cos 360^{\circ}+\mathrm{i} \sin 360^{\circ}\)
\(=1+\mathrm{i}(0)\)
\(=1\)

2. Find all the values of \((i)^{2 / 3}\).

Solution:

Let \(x=(i)^{2 / 3}\)
\(=\left[\left(\cos \frac{\pi}{2}+i \sin \frac{\pi}{2}\right)\right]^{2 / 3}\)
\(=\left[\left(\cos \frac{\pi}{2}+i \sin \frac{\pi}{2}\right)^{2}\right]^{1 / 3}\)

\(=[\cos (2 \mathrm{k} \pi+\pi)+\mathrm{i} \sin (2 \mathrm{k} \pi+\pi)]^{1 / 3}\)
\(=\cos \left(\frac{2 \mathrm{k} \pi+\pi}{3}\right)+\mathrm{i} \sin \left(\frac{2 \mathrm{k} \pi+\pi}{3}\right) \quad\) where \( k=0,1,2 \)

when \(k=0 ; \quad x=\cos \frac{\pi}{3}+i \sin \frac{\pi}{3}\)

3. Solve: \(x^{2}+16=0\)

Solution:

Given: \(x^{2}+16=0\)
\(x^{2} =-16=16 \times-1\)
\(x =(16)^{1 / 2}(-1)^{1 / 2}\)
\(=4[\cos \pi+\mathrm{i} \sin \pi]^{1 / 2}\)
\(=4[\cos (2 \mathrm{k} \pi+\pi)+\mathrm{i} \sin (2 \mathrm{k} \pi+\pi)]^{1 / 2}\)
\(=4\left[\cos \left(\frac{2 \mathrm{k} \pi+\pi}{2}\right)+\mathrm{i} \sin \left(\frac{2 \mathrm{k} \pi+\pi}{2}\right)\right]\) where \( k=0,1 \)

when \(k=0 ; \quad x=4\left[\cos \frac{\pi}{2}+\mathrm{i} \sin \frac{\pi}{2}\right]\) (= 4(0+i) = 4i)
\(k=1 ; \quad x=4\left[\cos \frac{3 \pi}{2}+\mathrm{i} \sin \frac{3 \pi}{2}\right]\) (= 4(0-i) = -4i)

4. If \(\omega\) is the cube roots of unity then prove that \(\left(1-\omega+\omega^{2}\right)^{5}+\left(1+\omega-\omega^{2}\right)^{5}=32\).

Solution:

If \(\omega\) is the cube roots of unity then \(\omega^{3}=1\) and \(1+\omega+\omega^{2}=0\).
LHS : \(\left(1-\omega+\omega^{2}\right)^{5}+\left(1+\omega-\omega^{2}\right)^{5}\)
\(=\left(1+\omega^{2}-\omega\right)^{5}+\left(1+\omega-\omega^{2}\right)^{5}\)
\( =((-\omega)-\omega)^{5}+((-\omega^2)-\omega^{2})^{5}\) (since \(1+\omega^2=-\omega\) and \(1+\omega=-\omega^2\))
\( =(-2 \omega)^{5}+\left(-2 \omega^{2}\right)^{5}\)
\( =(-2)^{5} \omega^{5}+(-2)^{5}\left(\omega^{2}\right)^{5}\)
\( =-32 \omega^{3}\omega^2-32 \omega^{10} \)
\( =-32 (1) \omega^{2}-32 (\omega^3)^3 \omega \)
\( =-32 \omega^{2}-32 (1)^3 \omega = -32(\omega^2 + \omega)\)
\( =-32(-1)=32=\text { RHS }\) (since \(1+\omega+\omega^2=0 \implies \omega+\omega^2 = -1\))

4. Find all the values of \(\left(\frac{1}{2}+i \frac{\sqrt{3}}{2}\right)^{3 / 4}\) and also prove that the product of the four values is 1.

Solution:

Let \(a+i b=\frac{1}{2}+i \frac{\sqrt{3}}{2}=r(\cos \theta+i \sin \theta)\) .....(1)
Here \(a=\frac{1}{2} \quad \& \quad b=\frac{\sqrt{3}}{2}\)
Modulus: \(r=\sqrt{a^{2}+b^{2}}=\sqrt{\left(\frac{1}{2}\right)^{2}+\left(\frac{\sqrt{3}}{2}\right)^{2}}=\sqrt{\frac{1}{4}+\frac{3}{4}}=\sqrt{1}=1\)
Arugument: \(\theta=\tan ^{-1}\left(\frac{b}{a}\right)=\tan ^{-1}\left[\frac{\sqrt{3} / 2}{1 / 2}\right]=\tan ^{-1}(\sqrt{3})=\frac{\pi}{3}\)
\(\therefore\) (1) becomes,
\(\frac{1}{2}+i \frac{\sqrt{3}}{2}=1\left(\cos \frac{\pi}{3}+i \sin \frac{\pi}{3}\right)\)
\(\Rightarrow\left(\frac{1}{2}+i \frac{\sqrt{3}}{2}\right)^{3 / 4}=\left(\cos \frac{\pi}{3}+i \sin \frac{\pi}{3}\right)^{3 / 4}\)
\(=\left[\left(\cos \frac{\pi}{3}+i \sin \frac{\pi}{3}\right)^{3}\right]^{\frac{1}{4}}\)
\(=\left[\cos 3\left(\frac{\pi}{3}\right)+i \sin 3\left(\frac{\pi}{3}\right)\right]^{1 / 4}\)
\(=[\cos \pi+\mathrm{i} \sin \pi]^{1 / 4}\)
\(=[\cos (2 \mathrm{k} \pi+\pi)+\mathrm{i} \sin (2 \mathrm{k} \pi+\pi)]^{1 / 4}\)
\(=\cos \left(\frac{2 \mathrm{k} \pi+\pi}{4}\right)+\mathrm{i} \sin \left(\frac{2 \mathrm{k} \pi+\pi}{4}\right) \quad\) where \( k=0,1,2,3 \)

\(\therefore\) The values are,
when \(k=0 ; R_{1}=\cos \frac{\pi}{4}+i \sin \frac{\pi}{4}\)
\(k=1 ; R_{2}=\cos \frac{3 \pi}{4}+i \sin \frac{3 \pi}{4}\)
\(k=2 ; R_{3}=\cos \frac{5 \pi}{4}+i \sin \frac{5 \pi}{4}\)
\(k=3 ; R_{4}=\cos \frac{7 \pi}{4}+i \sin \frac{7 \pi}{4}\)

Product of the four values
\(R_{1} \times R_{2} \times R_{3} \times R_{4}\)
\(=\left(\cos \frac{\pi}{4}+\mathrm{i} \sin \frac{\pi}{4}\right)\left(\cos \frac{3 \pi}{4}+\mathrm{i} \sin \frac{3 \pi}{4}\right)\left(\cos \frac{5 \pi}{4}+\mathrm{i} \sin \frac{5 \pi}{4}\right)\left(\cos \frac{7 \pi}{4}+\mathrm{i} \sin \frac{7 \pi}{4}\right)\)
\(=\cos \left(\frac{\pi}{4}+\frac{3 \pi}{4}+\frac{5 \pi}{4}+\frac{7 \pi}{4}\right)+i \sin \left(\frac{\pi}{4}+\frac{3 \pi}{4}+\frac{5 \pi}{4}+\frac{7 \pi}{4}\right)\)
\(=\cos \frac{16 \pi}{4}+i \sin \frac{16 \pi}{4}\)
\(=\cos 4 \pi+i \sin 4 \pi\)
\(=1+i(0)=1\)

Hence, the product of the four values is 1.
