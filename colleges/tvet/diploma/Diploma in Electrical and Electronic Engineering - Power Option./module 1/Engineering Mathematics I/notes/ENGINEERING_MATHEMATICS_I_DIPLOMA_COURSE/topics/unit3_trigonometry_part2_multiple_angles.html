üìê3.2 MULTIPLE AND SUB-MULTIPLE ANGLES

Trigonometric ratios of multiple angles of 2A in terms to that of A.

1. Prove that \(\sin 2 \mathrm{~A}=2 \sin \mathrm{A} \cos \mathrm{A}\)

Proof:

We know that
\(\sin (A+B)=\sin A \cos B+\cos A \sin B\)
Put \(B=A\)
\(\sin (A+A)=\sin A \cos A+\cos A \sin A\)
\(\sin 2 A=\sin A \cos A+\sin A \cos A\)

\(\sin 2 \mathrm{~A}=2 \sin \mathrm{A} \cos \mathrm{A}\)
(Formula for \(\sin 2A\))

2. Prove that \(\cos 2 \mathrm{~A}=\cos ^{2} \mathrm{~A}-\sin ^{2} \mathrm{~A}=2 \cos ^{2} \mathrm{~A}-1=1-2 \sin ^{2} \mathrm{~A}\).

Proof:

We know that
\(\cos (A+B)=\cos A \cos B-\sin A \sin B\)
Put B = A
\(\cos (A+A)=\cos A \cos (A)-\sin A \sin (A)\)

\(\cos 2 A=\cos ^{2} A-\sin ^{2} A\)
(Formula for \(\cos 2A\) (i))
\(=\cos ^{2} A-\left(1-\cos ^{2} A\right)\)
\(=\cos ^{2} A-1+\cos ^{2} A\)
\(\cos 2 A=2 \cos ^{2} A-1\)
(Formula for \(\cos 2A\) (ii))
\(=2\left(1-\sin ^{2} A\right)-1\)
\(=2-2 \sin ^{2} A-1\)
\(\cos 2 A=1-2 \sin ^{2} A\)
(Formula for \(\cos 2A\) (iii))

3. Prove that \(\tan 2 \mathrm{~A}=\frac{2 \tan \mathrm{A}}{1-\tan ^{2} \mathrm{~A}}\).

Solution:

We know that
\(\tan (A+B)=\frac{\tan A+\tan B}{1-\tan A \tan B}\)
Put \(B=A\)
\(\tan (A+A)=\frac{\tan A+\tan A}{1-\tan A \tan A}\)

\(\tan 2 A=\frac{2 \tan A}{1-\tan ^{2} A}\)
(Formula for \(\tan 2A\))

4. Prove that
a) \(\sin ^{2} A=\frac{1-\cos 2 A}{2}\)
b) \(\cos ^{2} A=\frac{1+\cos 2 A}{2}\)
c) \(\tan ^{2} A=\frac{1-\cos 2 A}{1+\cos 2 A}\)

Solution:

(a) RHS \(=\frac{1-\cos 2 A}{2}=\frac{1-\left(1-2 \sin ^{2} A\right)}{2}\) using formula for \(\cos 2 \mathrm{~A}-(\mathrm{iii})\)
\(=\frac{1-1+2 \sin ^{2} \mathrm{~A}}{2}=\frac{2 \sin ^{2} \mathrm{~A}}{2}\)
\(=\sin ^{2} \mathrm{~A}=\mathrm{LHS}\)

(b) RHS \(=\frac{1+\cos 2 \mathrm{~A}}{2}=\frac{1+\left(2 \cos ^{2} \mathrm{~A}-1\right)}{2}\) using formula for \(\cos 2 \mathrm{~A}-(\mathrm{ii})\)
\(=\frac{2 \cos ^{2} \mathrm{~A}}{2}=\cos ^{2} \mathrm{~A}=\mathrm{LHS}\)

(c) RHS \(=\frac{1-\cos 2 A}{1+\cos 2 A}=\frac{1-\left(1-2 \sin ^{2} A\right)}{1+\left(2 \cos ^{2} A-1\right)}\) using Formula (iii) & (ii)
\(=\frac{1-1+2 \sin ^{2} \mathrm{~A}}{1+2 \cos ^{2} \mathrm{~A}-1}=\frac{2 \sin ^{2} \mathrm{~A}}{2 \cos ^{2} \mathrm{~A}}\)
\(=\frac{\sin ^{2} \mathrm{~A}}{\cos ^{2} \mathrm{~A}}=\tan ^{2} \mathrm{~A}=\mathrm{LHS}\)

To express \(\sin 2 \mathrm{~A}\) and \(\cos 2 \mathrm{~A}\) in terms of \(\tan \mathrm{A}\)

Prove that
(a) \(\sin 2 \mathrm{~A}=\frac{2 \tan \mathrm{A}}{1+\tan ^{2} \mathrm{~A}}\)
(b) \(\cos 2 \mathrm{~A}=\frac{1-\tan ^{2} \mathrm{~A}}{1+\tan ^{2} \mathrm{~A}}\)

Solution:

(a) RHS \(=\frac{2 \tan \mathrm{A}}{1+\tan ^{2} \mathrm{~A}}=\frac{\frac{2 \sin \mathrm{A}}{\cos \mathrm{A}}}{1+\frac{\sin ^{2} \mathrm{~A}}{\cos ^{2} \mathrm{~A}}}\)
\(=\frac{\frac{2 \sin \mathrm{A}}{\cos \mathrm{A}}}{\frac{\cos ^{2} \mathrm{~A}+\sin ^{2} \mathrm{~A}}{\cos ^{2} \mathrm{~A}}}=\frac{\frac{2 \sin \mathrm{A}}{\cos \mathrm{A}}}{\frac{1}{\cos ^{2} \mathrm{~A}}}\)
\(=\frac{2 \sin \mathrm{A}}{\cos \mathrm{A}} \times \frac{\cos ^{2} \mathrm{~A}}{1}\) cancelling \(\cos A\)
\(=2 \sin A \cos A = \sin 2A = LHS\)

(b) RHS \(=\frac{1-\tan^2 A}{1+\tan^2 A} = \frac{1 - \frac{\sin^2 A}{\cos^2 A}}{1 + \frac{\sin^2 A}{\cos^2 A}} = \frac{\frac{\cos^2 A - \sin^2 A}{\cos^2 A}}{\frac{\cos^2 A + \sin^2 A}{\cos^2 A}} = \frac{\cos^2 A - \sin^2 A}{1} = \cos 2A = LHS\)

SUB-MULTIPLE ANGLES

If A is any angle, then A/2 is called sub-multiple angle.

Prove that
a) \(\sin A=2 \sin (A / 2) \cos (A / 2)\)
b) \(\cos A=\cos ^{2}(A / 2)-\sin ^{2}(A / 2)\)
\(=2 \cos ^{2}(A / 2)-1\)
\(=1-2 \sin ^{2}(A / 2)\)

Proof:

a) We know that \(\sin 2 A=2 \sin A \cos A\)
Divide the angles on both sides by 2
\(\sin \left(\frac{2 A}{2}\right) =2 \sin (A / 2) \cos (A / 2)\)
\(\sin A =2 \sin (A / 2) \cos (A / 2)\)

(b) We know that
\(\cos 2 A =\cos ^{2} A-\sin ^{2} A\)
\(=2 \cos ^{2} A-1\)
\(=1-2 \sin ^{2} A\)
Divide the angles on both sides by 2
\(\cos A =\cos ^{2}\left(\frac{A}{2}\right)-\sin ^{2}\left(\frac{A}{2}\right)\) .....(i)
\(=2 \cos ^{2}\left(\frac{A}{2}\right)-1\) .....(ii)
\(=1-2 \sin ^{2}\left(\frac{A}{2}\right)\) .....(iii)

Prove that
(a) \(\sin ^{2}\left(\frac{A}{2}\right)=\frac{1-\cos A}{2}\)
(b) \(\cos ^{2}\left(\frac{A}{2}\right)=\frac{1+\cos A}{2}\)
(c) \(\tan ^{2}\left(\frac{A}{2}\right)=\frac{1-\cos A}{1+\cos A}\)

Proof:

(a) RHS \(=\frac{1-\cos A}{2}=\frac{1-\left[1-2 \sin ^{2}\left(\frac{A}{2}\right)\right]}{2}\)
\(=\frac{1-1+2 \sin ^{2}\left(\frac{A}{2}\right)}{2}=\frac{2 \sin ^{2}\left(\frac{A}{2}\right)}{2} \quad\) using Formula (iii)
\(=\sin ^{2}\left(\frac{A}{2}\right)=\) LHS

(b) RHS \(=\frac{1+\cos A}{2}=\frac{1+2 \cos ^{2}\left(\frac{A}{2}\right)-1}{2}\) using Formula (ii)
\(=\frac{2 \cos ^{2}\left(\frac{A}{2}\right)}{2}=\cos ^{2}\left(\frac{A}{2}\right)=\) LHS

(c) RHS \(=\frac{1-\cos \mathrm{A}}{1+\cos \mathrm{A}}=\frac{1-\left[1-2 \sin ^{2}\left(\frac{\mathrm{A}}{2}\right)\right]}{1+2 \cos ^{2}\left(\frac{\mathrm{A}}{2}\right)-1} \quad\) using Formula (iii) & (ii)
\(=\frac{1-1+2 \sin ^{2}\left(\frac{\mathrm{A}}{2}\right)}{2 \cos ^{2}\left(\frac{\mathrm{A}}{2}\right)}=\frac{2 \sin ^{2}\left(\frac{\mathrm{A}}{2}\right)}{2 \cos ^{2}\left(\frac{\mathrm{A}}{2}\right)}\)
\(=\tan ^{2}\left(\frac{A}{2}\right)=\) LHS

To express \(\sin A, \cos A, \tan A\) in terms of \(\tan \left(\frac{A}{2}\right)\).

Prove that
a) \(\sin A=\frac{2 \tan \left(\frac{A}{2}\right)}{1+\tan ^{2}\left(\frac{A}{2}\right)}\)
b) \(\cos A=\frac{1-\tan ^{2}\left(\frac{A}{2}\right)}{1+\tan ^{2}\left(\frac{A}{2}\right)}\)
c) \(\tan A=\frac{2 \tan \left(\frac{A}{2}\right)}{1-\tan ^{2}\left(\frac{A}{2}\right)}\)

Proof:

We have already proved that
\(\sin (2 A)=\frac{2 \tan A}{1+\tan ^{2} A}\)
\(\cos (2 A)=\frac{1-\tan ^{2} A}{1+\tan ^{2} A}\)
\(\tan (2 A)=\frac{2 \tan A}{1-\tan ^{2} A}\)

Divide the angles on both sides by 2 on the above three results.
Then we get,

a) \(\sin A=\frac{2 \tan \left(\frac{A}{2}\right)}{1+\tan ^{2}\left(\frac{A}{2}\right)}\)
b) \(\cos A=\frac{1-\tan ^{2}\left(\frac{A}{2}\right)}{1+\tan ^{2}\left(\frac{A}{2}\right)}\)
c) \(\tan A=\frac{2 \tan \left(\frac{A}{2}\right)}{1-\tan ^{2}\left(\frac{A}{2}\right)}\)

Multiple angles of 3A

Prove that \(\sin 3 A=3 \sin A-4 \sin ^{3} A\)

Proof:

We know that \(\sin (A+B)=\sin A \cos B+\cos A \sin B\)
Put \(B=2A\)
\(\sin (A+2 A) =\sin A \cos (2 A)+\cos A \sin (2A)\)
\(\sin 3 A =\sin A\left(1-2 \sin ^{2} A\right)+\cos A(2 \sin A \cos A)\)
\( =\sin A-2 \sin ^{3} A+2 \sin A \cos ^{2} A \)
\( =\sin A-2 \sin ^{3} A+2 \sin A\left(1-\sin ^{2} A\right) \)
\( =\sin A-2 \sin ^{3} A+2 \sin A-2 \sin ^{3} A \)

\(\sin 3 A =3 \sin A-4 \sin ^{3} A\)
(Formula)

Prove that \(\cos 3 A=4 \cos ^{3} A-3 \cos A\)

Proof:

We know that \(\cos (A+B)=\cos A \cos B-\sin A \sin B\)
Put \(B=2A\)
\(\cos (A+2 A) =\cos A \cos 2 A-\sin A \sin 2 A\)
\(\cos 3 A =\cos A\left(2 \cos ^{2} A-1\right)-\sin A(2 \sin A \cos A)\)
\( =2 \cos ^{3} A-\cos A-2 \cos A \sin ^{2} A \)
\( =2 \cos ^{3} A-\cos A-2 \cos A\left(1-\cos ^{2} A\right) \)
\( =2 \cos ^{3} A-\cos A-2 \cos A+2 \cos ^{3} A \)

\(\cos 3A =4 \cos ^{3} A-3 \cos A\)
(Formula)

Prove that \(\tan 3 A=\frac{3 \tan A-\tan ^{3} A}{1-3 \tan ^{2} A}\).

Solution:

We know that \(\tan (A+B) =\frac{\tan A+\tan B}{1-\tan A \tan B}\)
Put \(B =2 A\)
\(\tan (A+2 A) =\frac{\tan A+\tan 2 A}{1-\tan A \cdot \tan 2 A}\)
\(=\frac{\tan A+\frac{2 \tan A}{1-\tan ^{2} A}}{1-\tan A \times \frac{2 \tan A}{1-\tan ^{2} A}}\)
\(=\frac{\frac{\tan A\left(1-\tan ^{2} A\right)+2 \tan A}{1-\tan ^{2} A}}{\frac{1-\tan ^{2} A-2 \tan ^{2} A}{1-\tan ^{2} A}}\)
\(=\frac{\tan A-\tan ^{3} A+2 \tan A}{1-3 \tan ^{2} A}\)

\(\tan 3A =\frac{3 \tan A-\tan ^{3} A}{1-3 \tan ^{2} A}\)
(Formula)

3.2 WORKED EXAMPLES

PART - A

1. Find the value of \(2 \sin 15^{\circ} \cos 15^{\circ}\).

Solution:

We know that \(\sin 2 A=2 \sin A \cos A\)
Put \(A=15^{\circ}\)
\(\sin \left(2 \times 15^{\circ}\right)=2 \sin 15^{\circ} \cos 15^{\circ}\)
\(\sin 30^{\circ}=2 \sin 15^{\circ} \cos 15^{\circ}\)
\(2 \sin 15^{\circ} \cos 15^{\circ}=\sin 30^{\circ}=\frac{1}{2}\)

2. Find the value of \(2 \sin 75^{\circ} \cos 75^{\circ}\).

Solution:

\(2 \sin 75^{\circ} \cos 75^{\circ} =\sin (2 \times \mathrm{A})\) (where A=75¬∞)
\(=\sin \left(2 \times 75^{\circ}\right)\)
\(=\sin 150^{\circ}=\sin (180-30)\)
\( =+\sin 30^{\circ}=\frac{1}{2}\)

3. Find the value of \(\cos ^{2} 15^{\circ}-\sin ^{2} 15^{\circ}\).

Solution:

We know that \(\cos ^{2} A-\sin ^{2} A=\cos 2 A\)
Put \(A=15^{\circ}\)
\(\cos ^{2} 15^{\circ}-\sin ^{2} 15^{\circ}=\cos \left(2 \times 15^{\circ}\right)\)
\(=\cos 30^{\circ}=\frac{\sqrt{3}}{2}\)

4. Find the value of \(1-2 \sin ^{2} 22 \frac{1}{2}^{\circ}\).

Solution:

\(1-2 \sin ^{2} \mathrm{~A}=\cos 2 \mathrm{~A}\)
\(1-2 \sin ^{2} 22 \frac{1}{2}^{\circ}=\cos \left(2 \times 22 \frac{1}{2}^{\circ}\right)=\cos 45^{\circ}=\frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}\)

5. Prove that \(\frac{2 \tan 15^{\circ}}{1+\tan ^{2} 15^{\circ}}=\frac{1}{2}\)

Solution:

We know that \(\frac{2 \tan \mathrm{A}}{1+\tan ^{2} \mathrm{~A}} =\sin 2 \mathrm{~A}\)
Put \(A =15^{\circ}\)
\(\frac{2 \tan 15^{\circ}}{1+\tan ^{2} 15^{\circ}} =\sin 2\left(15^{\circ}\right)\)
\(=\sin 30^{\circ}=\frac{1}{2}\)

6. Find the value of \(\frac{1-\tan ^{2} 15^{\circ}}{1+\tan ^{2} 15^{\circ}}\).

Solution:

We know that \(\frac{1-\tan ^{2} \mathrm{~A}}{1+\tan ^{2} \mathrm{~A}}=\cos 2 \mathrm{~A}\)
\(\frac{1-\tan ^{2} 15^{\circ}}{1+\tan ^{2} 15^{\circ}}=\cos (2 \times 15)=\cos 30=\frac{\sqrt{3}}{2}\)

7. Prove that \(\frac{\sin 2 A}{1+\cos 2 A}=\tan A\).

Solution:

LHS \(=\frac{\sin 2 A}{1+\cos 2 A}\)
\(=\frac{2 \sin A \cos A}{1+2 \cos ^{2} A-1} \quad\) (Using \(\sin 2A\) and \(\cos 2A\) formulae)
\(=\frac{2 \sin A \cos A}{2 \cos ^{2} A}\)
\(=\frac{\sin A}{\cos A} \quad\) (cancelling \(2 \cos A\))
\(=\tan A=\) LHS

8. Prove that \(\frac{\sin \mathrm{A}}{1-\cos \mathrm{A}}=\cot \left(\frac{\mathrm{A}}{2}\right)\).

Solution:

LHS \(=\frac{\sin A}{1-\cos A}\)
Using formulae: \(\sin A=2 \sin \mathrm{A} / 2 \cos \mathrm{A} / 2\) and \(\cos A=1-2 \sin ^{2} \mathrm{~A} / 2\)
\(=\frac{2 \sin \left(\frac{\mathrm{A}}{2}\right) \cos \left(\frac{\mathrm{A}}{2}\right)}{1-\left(1-2 \sin ^{2} \frac{\mathrm{A}}{2}\right)}\)
\(=\frac{2 \sin \left(\frac{\mathrm{A}}{2}\right) \cos \left(\frac{\mathrm{A}}{2}\right)}{1-1+2 \sin ^{2}\left(\frac{\mathrm{A}}{2}\right)}\)
\(=\frac{2 \sin \left(\frac{\mathrm{A}}{2}\right) \cos \left(\frac{\mathrm{A}}{2}\right)}{2 \sin ^{2}\left(\frac{\mathrm{A}}{2}\right)}\)
\(=\frac{\cos \left(\frac{\mathrm{A}}{2}\right)}{\sin \left(\frac{\mathrm{A}}{2}\right)}=\cot \left(\frac{\mathrm{A}}{2}\right)\) = RHS

9. Prove that \(\left(\sin \frac{\mathrm{A}}{2}-\cos \frac{\mathrm{A}}{2}\right)^{2}=1-\sin \mathrm{A}\).

Solution:

LHS \(=\left(\sin \frac{\mathrm{A}}{2}-\cos \frac{\mathrm{A}}{2}\right)^{2}\) (Using \((a-b)^{2}=a^{2}+b^{2}-2 a b\))
\(=\sin ^{2}\left(\frac{\mathrm{A}}{2}\right)+\cos ^{2}\left(\frac{\mathrm{A}}{2}\right)-2 \sin \left(\frac{\mathrm{A}}{2}\right) \cos \left(\frac{\mathrm{A}}{2}\right)\)
\(=1-\sin A\) (Using \(\sin^2 \theta + \cos^2 \theta = 1\) and \(2\sin(A/2)\cos(A/2) = \sin A\))
\(= RHS\)

10. Find the value of \(3 \sin 20^{\circ}-4 \sin ^{3} 20^{\circ}\).

Solution:

We know that \(\sin 3 A=3 \sin A-4 \sin ^{3} A\)
Put \(A=20\)
\(\sin \left(3 \times 20^{\circ}\right) =3 \sin 20^{\circ}-4 \sin ^{3} 20^{\circ}\)
\(3 \sin 20^{\circ}-4 \sin ^{3} 20^{\circ} =\sin \left(3 \times 20^{\circ}\right)\)
\(=\sin 60^{\circ}=\frac{\sqrt{3}}{2}\)

11. Find that value of \(4 \cos ^{3} 10^{\circ}-3 \cos 10^{\circ}\).

Solution:

We know that \(4 \cos^3 A - 3 \cos A =\cos 3 A\)
\(4 \cos ^{3} 10^{\circ}-3 \cos 10^{\circ} =\cos \left(3 \times 10^{\circ}\right)\)
\(=\cos 30^{\circ}=\frac{\sqrt{3}}{2}\)

12. Find the value of \(3 \sin 40^{\circ}-4 \sin ^{3} 40^{\circ}\).

Solution:

We know that \(3 \sin A-4 \sin^3 A =\sin 3 A\)
\(3 \sin 40^{\circ}-4 \sin ^{3} 40^{\circ} =\sin \left(3 \times 40^{\circ}\right)\)
\(=\sin 120^{\circ}\)
\(=\sin \left(180^{\circ}-60^{\circ}\right)\)
\(=\sin 60^{\circ}=\frac{\sqrt{3}}{2}\)

PART - B

1. Prove that \(\tan \mathrm{A}+\cot \mathrm{A}=2 \operatorname{cosec} 2 \mathrm{~A}\).

Solution:

LHS \(=\tan A+\cot A\)
\(=\frac{\sin \mathrm{A}}{\cos \mathrm{A}}+\frac{\cos \mathrm{A}}{\sin \mathrm{A}} =\frac{\sin ^{2} \mathrm{~A}+\cos ^{2} \mathrm{~A}}{\sin \mathrm{A} \cos \mathrm{A}}\)
\(=\frac{1}{\sin \mathrm{A} \cos \mathrm{A}}=\frac{2}{2 \sin \mathrm{A} \cos \mathrm{A}}\) (Multiply and Divide by 2)
\(=\frac{2}{\sin 2 \mathrm{~A}} \quad \because \sin 2 \mathrm{~A}=2 \sin \mathrm{A} \cos \mathrm{A}\)
\(=2 \operatorname{cosec} 2 \mathrm{~A} \quad \because \frac{1}{\sin \theta}=\operatorname{cosec} \theta\)
\(= RHS\)

2. Prove that \(\frac{\sin A+\sin 2 A}{1+\cos A+\cos 2 A}=\tan A\).

Solution:

LHS \(=\frac{\sin \mathrm{A}+\sin 2 \mathrm{~A}}{1+\cos \mathrm{A}+\cos 2 \mathrm{~A}}\)
Using Formula \(\sin 2A = 2\sin A \cos A\) and \(\cos 2A = 2\cos^2 A - 1\)
\(=\frac{\sin A+2 \sin A \cos A}{1+\cos A+2 \cos ^{2} A -1}\)
\(=\frac{\sin \mathrm{A}[1+2 \cos \mathrm{~A}]}{\cos \mathrm{A}[1+2 \cos \mathrm{~A}]}\) (\(\sin A\) common in Nr, \(\cos A\) common in Dr)
\(=\frac{\sin \mathrm{A}}{\cos \mathrm{A}}\) (cancelling \(1+2 \cos A\))
\(=\tan A=\) RHS

3. Prove that \(\cos ^{4} A-\sin ^{4} A=\cos 2 A\).

Solution:

LHS \(=\left(\cos ^{2} A\right)^{2}-\left(\sin ^{2} A\right)^{2}\) (Using \(a^{2}-b^{2}=(a+b)(a-b)\))
\(=\left(\cos ^{2} A+\sin ^{2} A\right)\left(\cos ^{2} A-\sin ^{2} A\right)\)
\(=1(\cos 2 A)=\cos 2 A=\) RHS

PART - C

1. Prove that \(\frac{1-\cos 2 A+\sin 2 A}{1+\cos 2 A+\sin 2 A}=\tan A\).

Solution:

LHS \(=\frac{1-\cos 2 \mathrm{~A}+\sin 2 \mathrm{~A}}{1+\cos 2 \mathrm{~A}+\sin 2 \mathrm{~A}}\)
Using Formulae \(\sin 2A = 2\sin A \cos A\), \(\cos 2A = 1-2\sin^2 A\) (for Nr), \(\cos 2A = 2\cos^2 A - 1\) (for Dr)
\(=\frac{1-\left(1-2 \sin ^{2} \mathrm{~A}\right)+2 \sin \mathrm{A} \cos \mathrm{A}}{1+2 \cos ^{2} \mathrm{~A}-1+2 \sin \mathrm{A} \cos \mathrm{A}}\)
\(=\frac{1-1+2 \sin ^{2} \mathrm{~A}+2 \sin \mathrm{A} \cos \mathrm{A}}{2 \cos ^{2} \mathrm{~A}+2 \sin \mathrm{A} \cos \mathrm{A}}\)
\(=\frac{2 \sin \mathrm{A}(\sin \mathrm{A}+\cos \mathrm{A})}{2 \cos \mathrm{A}(\cos \mathrm{A}+\sin \mathrm{A})}\) (\(2\sin A\) common in Nr, \(2\cos A\) common in Dr)
\(=\frac{\sin \mathrm{A}}{\cos \mathrm{A}}=\tan \mathrm{A}=\mathrm{RHS}\)

2. Prove that \(\frac{1-\cos A+\sin A}{1+\cos A+\sin A}=\tan \left(\frac{A}{2}\right)\).

Solution:

LHS \(=\frac{1-\cos \mathrm{A}+\sin \mathrm{A}}{1+\cos \mathrm{A}+\sin \mathrm{A}}\)
Using sub-multiple angle formulae: \(\sin A=2 \sin (A / 2) \cos (A / 2)\), \(\cos A=1-2 \sin ^{2}(A / 2)\), \(\cos A =2 \cos ^{2}(A / 2)-1\)
\(=\frac{1-\left[1-2 \sin ^{2}(\mathrm{~A} / 2)\right]+2 \sin (\mathrm{A} / 2) \cos (\mathrm{A} / 2)}{1+\left[2 \cos ^{2}(\mathrm{~A} / 2)-1\right]+2 \sin (\mathrm{A} / 2) \cos (\mathrm{A} / 2)}\)
\(=\frac{1-1+2 \sin ^{2}\left(\frac{\mathrm{A}}{2}\right)+2 \sin \left(\frac{\mathrm{A}}{2}\right) \cos \left(\frac{\mathrm{A}}{2}\right)}{1+2 \cos ^{2}\left(\frac{\mathrm{A}}{2}\right)-1+2 \sin \left(\frac{\mathrm{A}}{2}\right) \cos \left(\frac{\mathrm{A}}{2}\right)}\)
\(=\frac{2 \sin (\mathrm{A} / 2)[\sin (\mathrm{A} / 2)+\cos (\mathrm{A} / 2)]}{2 \cos (\mathrm{A} / 2)[\cos (\mathrm{A} / 2)+\sin (\mathrm{A} / 2)]}\) (\(2\sin(A/2)\) common in Nr, \(2\cos(A/2)\) common in Dr)
\(=\frac{\sin (A / 2)}{\cos (A / 2)}=\tan \left(\frac{A}{2}\right)=RHS\)

3. If \(\tan A=\frac{1}{3}\) and \(\tan B=\frac{1}{7}\) show that \(2 A+B=\frac{\pi}{4}\).

Solution:

Given \(\tan A=\frac{1}{3} \quad \tan B=\frac{1}{7}\)
Formula for \(\tan 2 A =\frac{2 \tan A}{1-\tan ^{2} A}\)
\(=\frac{2\left(\frac{1}{3}\right)}{1-\left(\frac{1}{3}\right)^{2}}\)
\(=\frac{\frac{2}{3}}{1-\frac{1}{9}}=\frac{\frac{2}{3}}{\frac{9-1}{9}}\)
\(=\frac{\frac{2}{3}}{\frac{8}{9}}=\frac{2}{3} \times \frac{9}{8} = \frac{3}{4}\)
\(\tan 2 A =\frac{3}{4}\) .....(1)

\(\tan (2 A+B) =\frac{\tan 2 A+\tan B}{1-\tan 2 A \tan B}\)
\(=\frac{\frac{3}{4}+\frac{1}{7}}{1-\frac{3}{4} \times \frac{1}{7}}\) using (1)
\(=\frac{\frac{21+4}{28}}{1-\frac{3}{28}} = \frac{\frac{25}{28}}{\frac{28-3}{28}}=\frac{\frac{25}{28}}{\frac{25}{28}}\)
\(=\frac{25}{28} \times \frac{28}{25}=1\)

\(\tan (2 A+B)=1=\tan 45^{\circ}\)
\(2 A+B=45^{\circ}=\frac{180^{\circ}}{4}=\frac{\pi}{4}\)

4. If \(\sin \theta=\frac{3}{5}\) find the value of \(\sin 3 \theta\).

Solution:

Given: \(\sin \theta=\frac{3}{5}\)
We know that \(\sin 3 \theta =3 \sin \theta-4 \sin ^{3} \theta\)
\(=3\left(\frac{3}{5}\right)-4\left(\frac{3}{5}\right)^{3}\)
\(=\frac{9}{5}-4\left(\frac{27}{125}\right)=\frac{9}{5}-\frac{108}{125}\)
\(=\frac{9 \times 25 - 108}{125} = \frac{225-108}{125}=\frac{117}{125}\)

5. If \(\cos A=\frac{1}{3}\) find the value of \(\cos 3 A\).

Solution:

Given : \(\cos A=\frac{1}{3}\)
\(\cos 3 A =4 \cos ^{3} A-3 \cos A\)
\(=4\left(\frac{1}{3}\right)^{3}-3\left(\frac{1}{3}\right)\)
\(=4\left(\frac{1}{27}\right)-1=\frac{4}{27}-1\)
\(=\frac{4-27}{27}=\frac{-23}{27}\)

6. Prove that \(\frac{\sin 3 A}{1+2 \cos 2 A}=\sin A\).

Solution:

LHS \(=\frac{\sin 3 \mathrm{~A}}{1+2 \cos 2 \mathrm{~A}}\)
Using Formulae \(\sin 3A = 3\sin A - 4\sin^3 A\) and \(\cos 2A = 1-2\sin^2 A\)
\(=\frac{3 \sin \mathrm{A}-4 \sin ^{3} \mathrm{~A}}{1+2\left(1-2 \sin ^{2} \mathrm{~A}\right)}\)
\(=\frac{\sin \mathrm{A}\left(3-4 \sin ^{2} \mathrm{~A}\right)}{1+2-4 \sin ^{2} \mathrm{~A}}\) (\(\sin A\) is common in Nr)
\(=\frac{\sin \mathrm{A}\left(3-4 \sin ^{2} \mathrm{~A}\right)}{\left(3-4 \sin ^{2} \mathrm{~A}\right)}\)
\(=\sin A=\) RHS

7. Prove that \(4 \sin A \sin (60+A) \sin (60-A)=\sin 3 A\).

Solution:

LHS \(=4 \sin A \sin (60+A) \sin (60-A)\)
\(=4 \sin A\left[\sin ^{2} 60-\sin ^{2} A\right]\) (Using Formula: \(\sin (A+B) \sin (A-B)=\sin ^{2} A-\sin ^{2} B\))
\( =4 \sin \mathrm{A}\left[\left(\frac{\sqrt{3}}{2}\right)^2-\sin ^{2} \mathrm{~A}\right] \)
\(=4 \sin A\left[\frac{3}{4}-\sin ^{2} A\right]\)
\(=4 \sin A\left[\frac{3-4 \sin ^{2} A}{4}\right]\)
\(=\sin A\left[3-4 \sin ^{2} A\right]\)
\(=3 \sin A-4 \sin ^{3} A = \sin 3A =\) RHS

8. Prove that \(\frac{\sin 3 A}{\sin A}-\frac{\cos 3 A}{\cos A}=2\)

Solution:

LHS \(=\frac{\sin 3 A}{\sin A}-\frac{\cos 3 A}{\cos A}\)
\(=\frac{\left(3 \sin A-4 \sin ^{3} A\right)}{\sin A}-\frac{\left(4 \cos ^{3} A-3 \cos A\right)}{\cos A}\)
\(=\frac{\sin A\left[3-4 \sin ^{2} A\right]}{\sin A}-\frac{\cos A\left[4 \cos ^{2} A-3\right]}{\cos A}\)
\(=\left[3-4 \sin ^{2} A\right]-\left[4 \cos ^{2} A-3\right]\)
\(=3-4 \sin ^{2} A-4 \cos ^{2} A+3\)
\(=6-4\left(\sin ^{2} A+\cos ^{2} A\right)\)
\(=6-4(1) \quad \because \sin ^{2} \mathrm{~A}+\cos ^{2} \mathrm{~A}=1\)
\(=6-4=2=\mathrm{RHS}\)

9. Prove that \(\frac{\cos ^{3} A-\cos 3 A}{\cos A}+\frac{\sin ^{3} A+\sin 3 A}{\sin A}=3\).

Solution:

LHS \(=\frac{\cos ^{3} A-\cos 3 A}{\cos A}+\frac{\sin ^{3} A+\sin 3 A}{\sin A}\)
Using Formulae \(\sin 3 \mathrm{~A}=3 \sin \mathrm{A}-4 \sin ^{3} \mathrm{~A}\), \(\cos 3 \mathrm{~A}=4 \cos ^{3} \mathrm{~A}-3 \cos \mathrm{A}\)
\(=\frac{\cos ^{3} A-(4 \cos^3 A - 3 \cos A)}{\cos A}+\frac{\sin ^{3} A+3 \sin A-4 \sin ^{3} A}{\sin A}\)
\(=\frac{-3\cos^3 A + 3 \cos A}{\cos A} + \frac{3\sin A - 3\sin^3 A}{\sin A}\)
\(=\frac{\cos A(-3\cos^2 A + 3)}{\cos A} + \frac{\sin A(3 - 3\sin^2 A)}{\sin A}\)
\(=3-3 \cos ^{2} \mathrm{~A}+3-3 \sin ^{2} \mathrm{~A}\)
\(=6-3\left(\cos ^{2} \mathrm{~A}+\sin ^{2} \mathrm{~A}\right)\)
\(=6-3(1)=6-3=3=\mathrm{RHS}\)

10. Prove that \(\cos 20^{\circ} \cos 40^{\circ} \cos 80^{\circ}=\frac{1}{8}\).

Solution:

LHS \(=\cos 20^{\circ} \cos 40^{\circ} \cos 80^{\circ}\)
\(=\cos 80^{\circ} \cos 40^{\circ} \cos 20^{\circ}\)
\(=\cos \left(60^{\circ}+20^{\circ}\right) \cos \left(60^{\circ}-20^{\circ}\right) \cos 20^{\circ}\)
\(=\left[\cos ^{2} 60^{\circ}-\sin ^{2} 20^{\circ}\right] \cos 20^{\circ}\) (Using Formula: \(\cos (A+B) \cos (A-B) = \cos^2 A - \sin^2 B\))
\(=\left[\left(\frac{1}{2}\right)^2 - \sin^2 20^{\circ}\right] \cos 20^{\circ}\)
\(=\left[\frac{1}{4} - (1-\cos^2 20^{\circ})\right] \cos 20^{\circ}\) (Cannot simplify further easily this way)

Alternative approach using \(4\cos A \cos(60-A)\cos(60+A) = \cos 3A\) is not directly applicable. Let's use product-to-sum:

LHS \( = \frac{1}{2} [2 \cos 20^\circ \cos 40^\circ] \cos 80^\circ \)
\( = \frac{1}{2} [\cos(60^\circ) + \cos(-20^\circ)] \cos 80^\circ \)
\( = \frac{1}{2} [\frac{1}{2} + \cos 20^\circ] \cos 80^\circ \)
\( = \frac{1}{4} \cos 80^\circ + \frac{1}{2} \cos 20^\circ \cos 80^\circ \)
\( = \frac{1}{4} \cos 80^\circ + \frac{1}{2} \times \frac{1}{2} [2 \cos 20^\circ \cos 80^\circ] \)
\( = \frac{1}{4} \cos 80^\circ + \frac{1}{4} [\cos(100^\circ) + \cos(-60^\circ)] \)
\( = \frac{1}{4} \cos 80^\circ + \frac{1}{4} [\cos(180^\circ - 80^\circ) + \cos 60^\circ] \)
\( = \frac{1}{4} \cos 80^\circ + \frac{1}{4} [-\cos 80^\circ + \frac{1}{2}] \)
\( = \frac{1}{4} \cos 80^\circ - \frac{1}{4} \cos 80^\circ + \frac{1}{4} \times \frac{1}{2} \)
\( = \frac{1}{8} = \text{RHS}\)
