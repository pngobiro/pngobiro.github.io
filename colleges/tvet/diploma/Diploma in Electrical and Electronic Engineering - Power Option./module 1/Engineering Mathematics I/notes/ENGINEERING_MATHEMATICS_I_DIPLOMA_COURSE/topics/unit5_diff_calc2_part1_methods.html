üîó5.1 DIFFERENTIATION METHODS

Function of Funtions Rule:

If 'y' is a function of 'u' and 'u' is a function of 'x' then the derivative of 'y' w.r.t 'x' is equal to the product of the derivative of 'y' w.r.t 'u' and the derivative of 'u' w.r.t 'x'.

i.e. \(\frac{d y}{d x}=\frac{d y}{d u} \cdot \frac{d u}{d x}\)

It is called function of function rule. This rule can be extended which is known as 'Chain rule'.

Chain rule:

If 'y' is a function of 'u' and 'u' is a function of 'v' and 'v' is a function of 'x' then.

\(\frac{d y}{d x}=\frac{d y}{d u} \cdot \frac{d u}{d v} \frac{d v}{d x}\)

Worked Examples (Chain Rule)

PART -A

Find \(\frac{d y}{d x}\) if

  1. \(y=(2 x+5)^{3}\)
  2. \(y=\sqrt{\sin x}\)
  3. \(y=\cos ^{4} x\)
  4. \(y=e^{\tan x}\)
  5. \(y=\log (\sec x)\)
  6. \(y=\sin m x\)
  7. \(y=\sec \sqrt{x}\)
  8. \(y=\cos (2-3 x)\)

Solution:

1) \(y=(2 x+5)^{3}\)
Let \(y=u^{3}\) where \(u=2 x+5\)
\(\frac{d y}{d u}=3 u^{2} \quad | \quad \frac{d u}{d x}=2\)
\(\frac{d y}{d x}=\frac{d y}{d u} \cdot \frac{d u}{d x} = \left(3 u^{2}\right)(2) = 6 u^2 = 6(2 x+5)^{2}\)

2) \(y=\sqrt{\sin x}\)
Let \(y=\sqrt{u}\) where \(u=\sin x\)
\(\frac{d y}{d u}=\frac{1}{2 \sqrt{u}} \quad | \quad \frac{d u}{d x}=\cos x\)
\(\frac{d y}{d x}=\left(\frac{1}{2 \sqrt{u}}\right)(\cos x) = \frac{\cos x}{2 \sqrt{\sin x}}\)

3) \(y=\cos ^{4} x\)
Let \(y=u^{4}\) where \(u=\cos x\)
\(\frac{d y}{d u}=4 u^{3} \quad | \quad \frac{d u}{d x}=-\sin x\)
\(\frac{d y}{d x}=4 u^{3}(-\sin x) = -4 \cos^3 x \sin x\)

4) \(y=e^{\tan x}\)
Let \(y=e^{u}\) where \(u=\tan x\)
\(\frac{d y}{d u}=e^{u} \quad | \quad \frac{d u}{d x}=\sec ^{2} x\)
\(\frac{d y}{d x}=\left(e^{u}\right)\left(\sec ^{2} x\right) = e^{\tan x} \sec ^{2} x\)

5) \(y=\log (\sec x)\)
Let \(y=\log u\) where \(u=\sec x\)
\(\frac{d y}{d u}=\frac{1}{u} \quad | \quad \frac{d u}{d x}=\sec x \tan x\)
\(\frac{d y}{d x}=\left(\frac{1}{u}\right)(\sec x \tan x) = \frac{1}{\sec x}(\sec x \tan x) = \tan x\)

6) \(y=\sin m x\)
Let \(y=\sin u\) where \(u=m x\)
\(\frac{d y}{d u}=\cos u \quad | \quad \frac{d u}{d x}=m\)
\(\frac{d y}{d x} =(\cos u)(m) = m \cos m x\)

7) \(y=\cos (2-3 x)\)
Let \(y=\cos u\) where \(u=2-3 x\)
\(\frac{d y}{d u}=-\sin u \quad | \quad \frac{d u}{d x}=0-3(1) =-3\)
\(\frac{d y}{d x}=(-\sin u)(-3) = 3 \sin (2-3 x)\)

8) \(y=\sec \sqrt{x}\)
Let \(y=\sec u\) where \(u=\sqrt{x}\)
\(\frac{d y}{d u}=\sec u \tan u \quad | \quad \frac{d u}{d x}=\frac{1}{2 \sqrt{x}}\)
\(\frac{d y}{d x} =(\sec u \tan u)\left(\frac{1}{2 \sqrt{x}}\right) = \frac{1}{2 \sqrt{x}} \sec \sqrt{x} \tan \sqrt{x}\)

üí°Note:

  1. 1. \(\frac{d}{d x}\left(e^{m x}\right)=m e^{m x}\)
  2. 2. \(\frac{d}{d x}(\sin m x)=m \cos m x\)
  3. 3. \(\frac{d}{d x}(\cos m x)=-m \sin m x\)
  4. 4. \(\frac{d}{d x}(\tan m x)=m \sec ^{2} m x\)
  5. 5. \(\frac{d}{d x}(\cot m x)=-m \operatorname{cosec}^{2} m x\)
  6. 6. \(\frac{d}{d x}(\sec m x)=m \sec m x \tan m x\)
  7. 7. \(\frac{d}{d x}(\operatorname{cosec} m x)=-m \operatorname{cosec} m x \cot m x\)

PART - B

Differentiate the following w.r.t 'x'.

  1. \(\left(2 x^{2}-3 x+1\right)^{3}\)
  2. \(\cos \left(e^{5 x}\right)\)
  3. \(e^{\sin ^{2} x}\)
  4. \(\log (\sec x+\tan x)\)
  5. \(y=\operatorname{cosec}^{3}(5 x+1)\)

Solution:

1) Let \(y=\left(2 x^{2}-3 x+1\right)^{3}\)
Let \(y=u^{3}\) where \(u=2 x^{2}-3 x+1\)
\(\frac{d y}{d u}=3 u^{2} \quad | \quad \frac{d u}{d x}=4 x-3\)
\(\frac{d y}{d x}=\frac{dy}{du}\frac{du}{dx} = \left(3 u^{2}\right)(4 x-3) = 3\left(2 x^{2}-3 x+1\right)^{2}(4 x-3)\)

2) Let \(y=\cos \left(e^{5 x}\right)\)
Let \(y=\cos u\) where \(u=e^{5 x}\)
\(\frac{d y}{d u}=-\sin u \quad | \quad \frac{d u}{d x}=5 e^{5 x}\)
\(\frac{d y}{d x}=\frac{dy}{du}\frac{du}{dx}=(-\sin u)\left(5 e^{5 x}\right) = -5 e^{5 x} \sin \left(e^{5 x}\right)\)

3) Let \(y=e^{\sin ^{2} x}\)
Let \(y=e^{u}\) where \(u=\sin ^{2} x\)
\(\frac{d y}{d u}=e^{u} \quad | \quad\) Let \(u=v^{2}\) where \(v=\sin x\)
\(\frac{d u}{d v}=2 v \quad | \quad \frac{d v}{d x}=\cos x\)
\(\frac{d y}{d x}=\frac{d y}{d u} \cdot \frac{d u}{d v} \cdot \frac{d v}{d x}\)
\(=\left(e^{u}\right)(2 v)(\cos x)\)
\( = e^{\sin^2 x} (2 \sin x) (\cos x) = e^{\sin^2 x} \sin(2x) \)

4) Let \(y=\log (\sec x+\tan x)\)
Let \(y=\log u\) where \(u=\sec x+\tan x\)
\(\frac{d y}{d u}=\frac{1}{u} \quad | \quad \frac{d u}{d x}=\sec x \tan x+\sec ^{2} x = \sec x(\tan x+\sec x)\)
\(\frac{d y}{d x}=\frac{dy}{du}\frac{du}{dx} = \left(\frac{1}{u}\right)[\sec x(\tan x+\sec x)]\)
\(=\frac{1}{(\sec x+\tan x)} \cdot \sec x(\tan x+\sec x)\)
\(=\sec x\)

5) Let \(y=\operatorname{cosec}^{3}(5 x+1)\)
Let \(y=u^{3}\) where \(u=\operatorname{cosec}(5 x+1)\)
\(\frac{d y}{d u}=3 u^{2} \quad | \quad\) Let \(u=\operatorname{cosec} v\) where \(v=5 x+1\)
\(\frac{d u}{d v}=-\operatorname{cosec} v \cot v \quad | \quad \frac{d v}{d x}=5\)
\(\frac{d y}{d x}=\frac{dy}{du}\frac{du}{dv}\frac{dv}{dx} = \left(3 u^{2}\right)(-\operatorname{cosec} v \cot v)(5)\)
\(=-15 \operatorname{cosec}^{2}(5 x+1) \cdot \operatorname{cosec}(5 x+1) \cot (5 x+1)\)
\( =-15 \operatorname{cosec}^{3}(5 x+1) \cot (5 x+1) \)

Differentitate the following w.r.t x.

  1. \(y=\sin \left(e^{x} \log x\right)\)
  2. \(y=\left(x^{2}+5\right) e^{-2 x} \sin x\)
  3. \(y=(x \cos x)^{3}\)
  4. \(y=\log \left(\frac{1-\cos x}{1+\cos x}\right)\)
  5. \(y=\sqrt{\frac{1-x^{3}}{1+x^{3}}}\)
  6. \(y=e^{4 x}+\sin \left(x^{2}+5\right)\)
  7. \(y=x^{3} e^{-5 x} \log (\sec x)\)

1) \(y=\sin \left(e^{x} \log x\right)\)
Let \(y=\sin u\) where \(u=e^{x} \log x\)
\(\frac{d y}{d u}=\cos u \quad | \quad \frac{d u}{d x}=e^{x} \frac{1}{x}+\log x \times e^{x} = e^{x}\left(\frac{1}{x}+\log x\right)\) (using product rule)
\(\frac{d y}{d x}=\frac{dy}{du}\frac{du}{dx}=(\cos u)\left[e^{x}\left(\frac{1}{x}+\log x\right)\right] = \cos \left(e^{x} \log x\right)\left[e^{x}\left(\frac{1}{x}+\log x\right)\right]\)

2) \(y=\left(x^{2}+5\right) e^{-2 x} \sin x\)
Let \(u=x^{2}+5 \implies \frac{du}{dx}=2x\)
Let \(v=e^{-2 x} \implies \frac{dv}{dx}=e^{-2 x}(-2) = -2e^{-2x}\)
Let \(w=\sin x \implies \frac{dw}{dx}=\cos x\)
\(\frac{d y}{d x}=u v \frac{d w}{d x}+v w \frac{d u}{d x}+u w \frac{d v}{d x}\)
\(=\left(x^{2}+5\right) e^{-2 x} \cos x+e^{-2 x} \sin x(2 x)+\left(x^{2}+5\right) \sin x\left(-2 e^{-2 x}\right)\)

3) \(y=(x \cos x)^{3}\)
Let \(y=u^{3}\) where \(u=x \cos x\)
\(\frac{d y}{d u}=3 u^{2} \quad | \quad \frac{d u}{d x}=x(-\sin x)+\cos x(1) = -x \sin x+\cos x\) (using product rule)
\(\frac{d y}{d x}=\frac{dy}{du}\frac{du}{dx} = \left(3 u^{2}\right)(-x \sin x+\cos x) = 3(x \cos x)^{2}(-x \sin x+\cos x)\)

4) \(y=\log \left(\frac{1-\cos x}{1+\cos x}\right)\)
Note: \(\frac{1-\cos x}{1+\cos x} = \frac{2\sin^2(x/2)}{2\cos^2(x/2)} = \tan^2(x/2)\). So \(y = \log(\tan^2(x/2)) = 2 \log(\tan(x/2))\).
Let \(y=2 \log u\) where \(u = \tan(x/2)\).
\(\frac{dy}{du} = \frac{2}{u} \quad | \quad \frac{du}{dx} = \sec^2(x/2) \cdot \frac{1}{2}\)
\(\frac{dy}{dx} = \frac{dy}{du} \frac{du}{dx} = \frac{2}{\tan(x/2)} \cdot \frac{1}{2} \sec^2(x/2) = \frac{\cos(x/2)}{\sin(x/2)} \cdot \frac{1}{\cos^2(x/2)} = \frac{1}{\sin(x/2)\cos(x/2)} = \frac{2}{2\sin(x/2)\cos(x/2)} = \frac{2}{\sin x} = 2 \operatorname{cosec} x\)

\(= \left(\frac{1+\cos x}{1-\cos x}\right)\left[\frac{2 \sin x}{(1+\cos x)^{2}}\right]\)
\(=\frac{2 \sin x}{(1-\cos x)(1+\cos x)}=\frac{2 \sin x}{1-\cos ^{2} x}\)
\(=\frac{2 \sin x}{\sin ^{2} x}\)
\(=\frac{2}{\sin x} = 2 \operatorname{cosec} x\)

5.1.2 DIFFERENTIATION OF INVERSE TRIGONOMETRIC FUNCTIONS

If \(x=\sin y\) then we get a unique real value of x to the given angle y. But if x is given y has many values.

We can express y interms of x as \(y=\sin ^{-1} x\) "Inverse sine of x". The Quantities \(\sin ^{-1} x, \cos ^{-1} x, \tan ^{-1} x, \cot ^{-1} x, \sec ^{-1} x, \operatorname{cosec}^{-1} x\) are called Inverse Trigonometric Functions.

Differential co-efficient of \(\sin ^{-1} x\):

Let \(y=\sin ^{-1} x\)
\(\Rightarrow \sin y=x\)
Differentiating w.r.t x
\(\cos y \cdot \frac{d y}{d x} =1\)
\(\frac{d y}{d x} =\frac{1}{\cos y}\)
\(=\frac{1}{\sqrt{1-\sin ^{2} y}}\)
i.e., \(\frac{d}{d x}\left(\sin ^{-1} x\right)=\frac{1}{\sqrt{1-x^{2}}}\)

Similarly we get

  1. (i) \(\frac{d}{d x}\left(\cos ^{-1} x\right)=\frac{-1}{\sqrt{1-x^{2}}}\)
  2. (ii) \(\frac{d}{d x}\left(\tan ^{-1} x\right)=\frac{1}{1+x^{2}}\)
  3. (iii) \(\frac{d}{d x}\left(\cot ^{-1} x\right)=\frac{-1}{1+x^{2}}\)
  4. (iv) \(\frac{d}{d x}\left(\sec ^{-1} x\right)=\frac{1}{x \sqrt{x^{2}-1}}\)
  5. (v) \(\frac{d}{d x}\left(\operatorname{cosec}^{-1} x\right)=\frac{-1}{x \sqrt{x^{2}-1}}\)

Worked Examples (Inverse Trig Diff)

PART - A

Find \(\frac{d y}{d x}\) if

(i) \(y=\sin ^{-1} \sqrt{x}\)
(ii) \(y=\cos ^{-1}(2 x)\)
(iii) \(y=\tan ^{-1}(\log x)\)

Solution:

(i) \(y=\sin ^{-1} \sqrt{x}\)
Let \(y=\sin ^{-1} u\) where \(u=\sqrt{x}\)
\(\frac{d y}{d u}=\frac{1}{\sqrt{1-u^{2}}} \quad | \quad \frac{d u}{d x}=\frac{1}{2 \sqrt{x}}\)
\(\frac{d y}{d x}=\frac{dy}{du}\frac{du}{dx} = \left(\frac{1}{\sqrt{1-u^{2}}}\right)\left(\frac{1}{2 \sqrt{x}}\right)\)
\(=\frac{1}{\sqrt{1-(\sqrt{x})^{2}}} \frac{1}{2 \sqrt{x}}\)
\(=\frac{1}{\sqrt{1-x}} \cdot \frac{1}{2 \sqrt{x}}\)
\(=\frac{1}{2 \sqrt{x(1-x)}}\)

(ii) \(y=\cos ^{-1}(2 x)\)
Let \(y=\cos ^{-1} u\) where \(u=2 x\)
\(\frac{d y}{d u}=\frac{-1}{\sqrt{1-u^{2}}} \quad | \quad \frac{d u}{d x}=2\)
\(\frac{d y}{d x}=\frac{dy}{du}\frac{du}{dx} = \frac{-1}{\sqrt{1-u^{2}}} \cdot 2\)
\(=\frac{-1}{\sqrt{1-(2 x)^{2}}} \cdot 2\)
\(\frac{d y}{d x}=\frac{-2}{\sqrt{1-4 x^{2}}}\)

(iii) \(y=\tan ^{-1}(\log x)\)
Let \(y=\tan ^{-1} u\) where \(u=\log x\)
\(\frac{d y}{d u}=\frac{1}{1+u^{2}} \quad | \quad \frac{d u}{d x}=\frac{1}{x}\)
\(\frac{d y}{d x}=\frac{dy}{du}\frac{du}{dx} = \left(\frac{1}{1+u^{2}}\right)\left(\frac{1}{x}\right)\)
\(=\frac{1}{x\left[1+(\log x)^{2}\right]}\)

PART - B

Find \(\frac{d y}{d x}\) if

(i) \(y=\sin ^{-1}(\cos x)\)
(ii) \(y=\cos ^{-1}(2 x+3)\)
(iii) \(y=\cos ^{-1}\left(2 x^{2}-1\right)\)
(iv) \(y=\sin ^{-1}\left(3 x-4 x^{3}\right)\)

Solution:

(i) \(y=\sin ^{-1}(\cos x)\)
Let \(y =\sin ^{-1} u\) where \(u=\cos x\)
\(\frac{d y}{d u}=\frac{1}{\sqrt{1-u^{2}}} \quad | \quad \frac{d u}{d x}=-\sin x\)
\(\frac{d y}{d x}=\frac{dy}{du}\frac{du}{dx} = \left(\frac{1}{\sqrt{1-u^{2}}}\right)(-\sin x)\)
\(=\frac{1}{\sqrt{1-\cos ^{2} x}}(-\sin x)\)
\(=\frac{-\sin x}{\sqrt{\sin ^{2} x}}= \frac{-\sin x}{|\sin x|} = -1\) (Assuming \(\sin x > 0\))

(ii) \(y=\cos ^{-1}(2 x+3)\)
\(\frac{d y}{d x} =\frac{-1}{\sqrt{1-(2 x+3)^{2}}} \cdot \frac{d}{d x}(2 x+3)\)
\(=\frac{-1}{\sqrt{1-(2 x+3)^{2}}} \times 2\)
\(=\frac{-2}{\sqrt{1-(2 x+3)^{2}}}\)

(iii) \(y=\cos ^{-1}\left(2 x^{2}-1\right)\)
Method 1: Chain Rule
\(\frac{d y}{d x} =\frac{-1}{\sqrt{1-\left(2 x^{2}-1\right)^{2}}} \cdot \frac{d}{d x}\left(2 x^{2}-1\right)\)
\(=\frac{-1}{\sqrt{1-\left(4x^4 - 4x^2 + 1\right)}} \cdot 4 x\)
\(=\frac{-4 x}{\sqrt{1-4 x^{4}+4 x^{2}-1}}=\frac{-4 x}{\sqrt{4 x^{2}-4 x^{4}}}=\frac{-4 x}{2 |x| \sqrt{1-x^{2}}} = \frac{-2 \text{sgn}(x)}{\sqrt{1-x^{2}}}\) (for \(x \neq 0\))

Method 2: Substitution (ALITER)
Let \(y=\cos ^{-1}\left(2 x^{2}-1\right)\)
Put \(x=\cos \theta \implies \theta=\cos^{-1}x\)
\(y =\cos ^{-1}\left(2 \cos ^{2} \theta-1\right)\)
\(=\cos ^{-1}(\cos 2 \theta)\)
\(y =2 \theta\)
\(y =2 \cos ^{-1} x\)
\(\frac{d y}{d x} =2 \times \frac{-1}{\sqrt{1-x^{2}}}=\frac{-2}{\sqrt{1-x^{2}}}\) (This result is valid for the principal value range of \(\cos^{-1}\))

(iv) \(y=\sin ^{-1}\left(3 x-4 x^{3}\right)\)
Method 1: Chain Rule
\(\frac{d y}{d x}=\frac{1}{\sqrt{1-\left(3 x-4 x^{3}\right)^{2}}} \cdot \frac{d}{dx}(3x-4x^3) = \frac{1}{\sqrt{1-\left(3 x-4 x^{3}\right)^{2}}} \cdot \left(3-12 x^{2}\right)\)

Method 2: Substitution (ALITER)
Put \(x=\sin \theta \implies \theta=\sin^{-1}x\)
\(y =\sin ^{-1}\left(3 \sin \theta-4 \sin ^{3} \theta\right)\)
\(=\sin ^{-1}(\sin 3 \theta)\)
\(y =3 \theta\)
\(y =3 \sin ^{-1} x\)
\(\frac{d y}{d x} =\frac{3}{\sqrt{1-x^{2}}}\) (Valid for principal value range)

PART - C

Find \(\frac{dy}{dx}\) if

(i) \(y=\cos ^{-1}\left(\frac{1-x^{2}}{1+x^{2}}\right)\)
(ii) \(y=\sin ^{-1}\left(\frac{2 x}{1+x^{2}}\right)\)
(iii) \(y=\tan ^{-1}\left(\frac{2 x}{1-x^{2}}\right)\)
(iv) \(y=\left(1+x^{2}\right) \tan ^{-1} x\)

Solution:

(i) \(y=\cos ^{-1}\left(\frac{1-x^{2}}{1+x^{2}}\right)\)
Let \(x=\tan \theta \Rightarrow \theta=\tan ^{-1} x\)
\(y =\cos ^{-1}\left(\frac{1-\tan ^{2} \theta}{1+\tan ^{2} \theta}\right)\)
\(=\cos ^{-1}(\cos 2 \theta)\)
\(=2 \theta\)
\(y =2 \tan ^{-1} x\)
\(\frac{d y}{d x} =2 \frac{1}{1+x^{2}}\)

(ii) \(y=\sin ^{-1}\left(\frac{2 x}{1+x^{2}}\right)\)
Let \(x=\tan \theta \Rightarrow \theta=\tan ^{-1} x\)
\(y =\sin ^{-1}\left(\frac{2 \tan \theta}{1+\tan ^{2} \theta}\right)\)
\(=\sin ^{-1}(\sin 2 \theta)\)
\(=2 \theta\)
\(y =2 \tan ^{-1} x\)
\(\frac{d y}{d x} =2 \cdot \frac{1}{1+x^{2}}\)

(iii) \(y=\tan ^{-1}\left(\frac{2 x}{1-x^{2}}\right)\)
Let \(x=\tan \theta \Rightarrow \theta=\tan ^{-1} x\)
\(y =\tan ^{-1}\left(\frac{2 \tan \theta}{1-\tan ^{2} \theta}\right)\)
\(=\tan ^{-1}(\tan 2 \theta)\)
\(y =2 \theta\)
\(y =2 \tan ^{-1} x\)
\(\frac{d y}{d x} =2 \cdot \frac{1}{1+x^{2}}\)

(iv) \(y=\left(1+x^{2}\right) \tan ^{-1} x\)
\(\frac{d y}{d x} =\left(1+x^{2}\right) \frac{d}{dx}(\tan^{-1}x) + \tan ^{-1} x \frac{d}{dx}(1+x^2)\) (using product rule)
\( = \left(1+x^{2}\right) \frac{1}{1+x^{2}}+\tan ^{-1} x(0+2 x) \)
\( =1+2 x \tan ^{-1} x\)

5.1.3 IMPLICIT FUNCTION

If the variables x and y are connected by a relation \(f(x, y)=0\) then it is called an implicit function. i.e If the dependent variable (y) cannot be expressed explicitly in terms of the independent variable x.

Example: (i) \(x^{2}+y^{2}-4 x+y+12=0\)
(ii) \(\frac{x^{2}}{a^{2}}+\frac{y^{2}}{b^{2}}=1 \quad\) are implicit function

Worked Examples (Implicit Diff)

PART -B

1. Find \(\frac{d y}{d x}\) if

(i) \(x^{2}+y^{2}+2 y=0\)
(ii) \(x^{2}+y^{2}=a^{2}\)
(iii) \(x y=c^{2}\)
(iv) \(\sqrt{y}+\sqrt{x}=\sqrt{a}\)

Solution:

(i) \(x^{2}+y^{2}+2 y=0\)
Differentiate w.r.t x
\(2 x+2 y \frac{d y}{d x}+2 \frac{d y}{d x}=0\)
\(x+y \frac{d y}{d x}+\frac{d y}{d x}=0\)
\((y+1) \frac{d y}{d x}=-x\)
\(\frac{d y}{d x}=\frac{-x}{1+y}\)

(ii) \(x^{2}+y^{2}=a^{2}\)
Differentiate w.r.t x
\(2 x+2 y \frac{d y}{d x}=0\)
\(x+y \frac{d y}{d x}=0\)
\(y \frac{d y}{d x}=-x\)
\(\frac{d y}{d x}=\frac{-x}{y}\)

(iii) \(x y=c^{2}\)
Differentiate w.r.t x
\(x \cdot \frac{d y}{d x}+y(1)=0\) (using product rule)
\(x \frac{d y}{d x}=-y\)
\(\frac{d y}{d x}=\frac{-y}{x}\)

(iv) \(\sqrt{y}+\sqrt{x}=\sqrt{a}\)
Differentiate w.r.t x
\(\frac{1}{2 \sqrt{x}}+\frac{1}{2 \sqrt{y}} \cdot \frac{d y}{d x}=0\)
Multiply by 2
\(\frac{1}{\sqrt{x}}+\frac{1}{\sqrt{y}} \cdot \frac{d y}{d x}=0\)
\(\frac{1}{\sqrt{y}} \frac{d y}{d x}=\frac{-1}{\sqrt{x}}\)
\(\frac{d y}{d x}=\frac{-\sqrt{y}}{\sqrt{x}}\)

PART - C

1. Find \(\frac{d y}{d x}\) if

(i) \(\cos x+\sin y=c\)
(ii) \(x^{2} \sin y=c\)
(iii) \(y=a+x e^{y}\)

Solution:

(i) \(\cos x+\sin y=c\)
Differentiate w.r.t x
\(-\sin x+\cos y \cdot \frac{d y}{d x}=0\)
\(\cos y \cdot \frac{d y}{d x}=\sin x\)
\(\frac{d y}{d x}=\frac{\sin x}{\cos y}\)

(ii) \(x^{2} \sin y=c\)
Differentiate w.r.t x
\(x^{2} (\cos y \cdot \frac{d y}{d x})+\sin y(2 x)=0\) (using product rule)
\(x^{2} \cos y \cdot \frac{d y}{d x}=-2 x \sin y\)
\(\frac{d y}{d x}=\frac{-2 x \sin y}{x^{2} \cos y}\)
\(\frac{d y}{d x}=\frac{-2}{x} \tan y\)

(iii) \(y=a+x e^{y}\)
Differentiate w.r.t x
\(\frac{d y}{d x}=0+x (e^{y} \cdot \frac{d y}{d x})+e^{y}(1)\) (using product rule)
\(\frac{d y}{d x}=x e^{y} \frac{d y}{d x}+e^{y}\)
\(\frac{d y}{d x}-x e^{y} \frac{d y}{d x}=e^{y}\)
\(\frac{d y}{d x}\left(1-x e^{y}\right)=e^{y}\)
\(\frac{d y}{d x}=\frac{e^{y}}{1-x e^{y}}\)

2. Find \(\frac{d y}{d x}\) if

(i) \(x^{3}+y^{3}=3 a x y\)
(ii) \(x^{2}+y^{2}-4 x+6 y-5=0\)
(iii) \(x^{2}+y^{2}=x y\)
(iv) \(a x^{2}+2 h x y+b y^{2}+2 g x+2 f y+c=0\)

Solution:

(i) \(x^{3}+y^{3}=3 a x y\)
Differentiate w.r.t x
\(3 x^{2}+3 y^{2} \frac{d y}{d x}=3 a\left[x \frac{d y}{d x}+y(1)\right]\) (using product rule)
Divide by 3
\(x^{2}+y^{2} \frac{d y}{d x}=a\left[x \frac{d y}{d x}+y\right]\)
\(x^{2}+y^{2} \frac{d y}{d x}=a x \frac{d y}{d x}+a y\)
\(y^{2} \frac{d y}{d x}-a x \frac{d y}{d x}=a y-x^{2}\)
\(\left(y^{2}-a x\right) \frac{d y}{d x}=a y-x^{2}\)
\(\frac{d y}{d x}=\frac{a y-x^{2}}{y^{2}-a x}\)

(ii) \(x^{2}+y^{2}-4 x+6 y-5=0\)
Differentiating w.r.t x
\(2 x+2 y \frac{d y}{d x}-4+6 \frac{d y}{d x}=0\)
\(2 y \frac{d y}{d x}+6 \frac{d y}{d x}=-2 x+4\)
\(\frac{d y}{d x}(2 y+6)=-2 x+4\)
\(\frac{d y}{d x}=\frac{-2 x+4}{2 y+6}=\frac{2(-x+2)}{2(y+3)}\)
\(=\frac{-x+2}{y+3}\)

(iii) \(x^{2}+y^{2}=x y\)
Differentiating w.r.t x ,
\(2 x+2 y \frac{d y}{d x}=x \frac{d y}{d x}+y(1)\) (uv rule)
\(2 y \frac{d y}{d x}-x \frac{d y}{d x}=y-2 x\)
\(\frac{d y}{d x}(2 y-x)=y-2 x\)
\(\frac{d y}{d x}=\frac{y-2 x}{2 y-x}\)

(iv) \(a x^{2}+2 h x y+b y^{2}+2 g x+2 f y+c=0\)
Differentiate w.r.t x
\(2 a x+2 h\left[x \cdot \frac{d y}{d x}+y(1)\right]+b(2 y) \cdot \frac{d y}{d x}+2 g(1)+2 f \cdot \frac{d y}{d x}+0=0\)
Divide by 2
\(a x+h x \frac{d y}{d x}+h y+b y \frac{d y}{d x}+g+f \frac{d y}{d x}=0\)
\(\frac{d y}{d x}(h x+b y+f)=-a x-h y-g\)
\(\frac{d y}{d x}=-\frac{a x+h y+g}{h x+b y+f}\)
