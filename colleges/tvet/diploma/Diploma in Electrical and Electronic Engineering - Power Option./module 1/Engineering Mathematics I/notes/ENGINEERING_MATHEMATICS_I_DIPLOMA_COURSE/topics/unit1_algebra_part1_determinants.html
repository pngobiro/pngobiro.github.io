1.1 DETERMINANTS

Definition: Determinant is a square arrangement of numbers (real or complex) within two vertical lines.

Example: \(\left|\begin{array}{ll}a_{1} & b_{1} \\ a_{2} & b_{2}\end{array}\right|\)

Order:

\(A=\left|\begin{array}{ll}a_{1} & b_{1} \\ a_{2} & b_{2}\end{array}\right|\), consisting of two rows and two columns is called a determinant of second order. The value of the determinant is \(\Delta=a_{1} b_{2}-a_{2} b_{1}\).

Example: Let \(A=\left|\begin{array}{cc}2 & -5 \\ 1 & 3\end{array}\right|\)
\(|A|=(2)(3)-(1)(-5)\)
\(|A|=6+5\)
i.e \(\Delta=11\)

Determinant of third order.
The expression \(\left|\begin{array}{lll}a_{1} & b_{1} & c_{1} \\ a_{2} & b_{2} & c_{2} \\ a_{3} & b_{3} & c_{3}\end{array}\right|\) consisting of three rows and three columns is called a determinant of third order.

The value of the determinant is obtained by expanding the determinant through any row or column with proper sign attached starting from \(a_{1}\), the first row first column elements, we will have positive and negative sign alternately.

Example: \(\left|\begin{array}{ccc}+ & - & + \\ 1 & -1 & 3 \\ - & + & - \\ 0 & 4 & 2 \\ + & - & + \\ 11 & 5 & -3\end{array}\right|\) expand thorugh first row.
\(=+1\left|\begin{array}{cc}4 & 2 \\ 5 & -3\end{array}\right|-(-1)\left|\begin{array}{cc}0 & 2 \\ 11 & -3\end{array}\right|+3\left|\begin{array}{cc}0 & 4 \\ 11 & 5\end{array}\right|\)
\(=1(-12-10)+1(0-22)+3(0-44)\)
\(=-22-22-132\)
\(=-176\)

Minor of an element:

Minor of an element is a determinant obtained by deleting the row and column in which that element occurs.

Example:
\(\left|\begin{array}{ccc}1 & -1 & 3 \\ 0 & 4 & 2 \\ 11 & 5 & -3\end{array}\right|\)
Minor of \(-1=\left|\begin{array}{cc}0 & 2 \\ 11 & -3\end{array}\right|=0-22=-22\)
Minor of \(0=\left|\begin{array}{cc}-1 & 3 \\ 5 & -3\end{array}\right|=3-15=-12\)

Co-factor of an element:

Minor of an element with proper sign attached is called co-factor of that element.

Example:
\(\left|\begin{array}{ccc}+ & - & + \\ 3 & -2 & 1 \\ 2 & 0 & -3 \\ 4 & 5 & 11\end{array}\right|\)
\(\text{Co-factor of } -2 = -\left|\begin{array}{ll}2 & -3 \\ 4 & 11\end{array}\right|=-[(22)+12] = -34\)
\(\text{Co-factor of } 0 = +\left|\begin{array}{cc}3 & 1 \\ 4 & 11\end{array}\right| = +(33-4) = +29\)

üí°Note

The sign for the element \(a_{ij}\) is \((-1)^{i+j}\)

Properties of determinant:

Property (1):

The value of the determinant is unaltered by changing rows into columns and vice versa.
(i.e) \(\left|\begin{array}{lll}a_{1} & b_{1} & c_{1} \\ a_{2} & b_{2} & c_{2} \\ a_{3} & b_{3} & c_{3}\end{array}\right|=\left|\begin{array}{lll}a_{1} & a_{2} & a_{3} \\ b_{1} & b_{2} & b_{3} \\ c_{1} & c_{2} & c_{3}\end{array}\right|\)

Property (2):

If any two rows or columns of a determinant are interchanged then the value of the determinant is changed in its sign only.
(i.e) \(\left|\begin{array}{lll}a_{1} & b_{1} & c_{1} \\ a_{2} & b_{2} & c_{2} \\ a_{3} & b_{3} & c_{3}\end{array}\right|=-\left|\begin{array}{lll}a_{2} & b_{2} & c_{2} \\ a_{1} & b_{1} & c_{1} \\ a_{3} & b_{3} & c_{3}\end{array}\right|\)
By notation \(R_{1} \leftrightarrow R_{2}\)
[Generally the rows and columns are denoted by \(R_{1}, R_{2} \ldots\ldots\). and \(C_{1}, C_{2} \ldots\ldots\). respectively]

Property (3):

If any two rows or columns of a determinant are identical or same, then the value of the determinant is zero.
(i.e) \(\left|\begin{array}{lll}a_{1} & b_{1} & c_{1} \\ a_{1} & b_{1} & c_{1} \\ a_{3} & b_{3} & c_{3}\end{array}\right|=0 \quad R_{1} \equiv R_{2}\)

Property (4):

If each element of a row or column of a determinant is multiplied by any constant \(k \neq 0\), then the value of the determinant is multiplied by same constant k .

Property (5):

If each element of a row or column is expressed as the sum of two elements then the determinant can be expressed as the sum of two determinant of the same order.
i.e. \(\left|\begin{array}{ccc}a_{1}+d_{1} & b_{1}+d_{2} & c_{1}+d_{3} \\ a_{2} & b_{2} & c_{2} \\ a_{3} & b_{3} & c_{3}\end{array}\right| = \left|\begin{array}{lll}a_{1} & b_{1} & c_{1} \\ a_{2} & b_{2} & c_{2} \\ a_{3} & b_{3} & c_{3}\end{array}\right|+\left|\begin{array}{lll}d_{1} & d_{2} & d_{3} \\ a_{2} & b_{2} & c_{2} \\ a_{3} & b_{3} & c_{3}\end{array}\right|\)

Property (6):

Any determinant is unaltered when each elements of any row or column is added by the equimultiples of any parallel row or column.
i.e. \(\left|\begin{array}{ccc}a_{1}+k a_{2} & b_{1}+k b_{2} & c_{1}+k c_{2} \\ a_{2} & b_{2} & c_{2} \\ a_{3} & b_{3} & c_{3}\end{array}\right| = \left|\begin{array}{ccc}a_{1} & b_{1} & c_{1} \\ a_{2} & b_{2} & c_{2} \\ a_{3} & b_{3} & c_{3}\end{array}\right|+\left|\begin{array}{ccc}k a_{2} & k b_{2} & k c_{2} \\ a_{2} & b_{2} & c_{2} \\ a_{3} & b_{3} & c_{3}\end{array}\right| = \left|\begin{array}{ccc}a_{1} & b_{1} & c_{1} \\ a_{2} & b_{2} & c_{2} \\ a_{3} & b_{3} & c_{3}\end{array}\right|+k(0)\)

Property (7):

In a given determinant if two rows or column are identical for \(a=b\), then \((a-b)\) is a factor of the determinant.
i.e \(\left|\begin{array}{ccc}1 & 1 & 1 \\ a & b & c \\ a^{2} & b^{2} & c^{2}\end{array}\right|\) for \(a=b\)
\(= \left|\begin{array}{ccc}1 & 1 & 1 \\ b & b & c \\ b^{2} & b^{2} & c^{2}\end{array}\right| = 0 \quad c_{1} \equiv c_{2}\)
\(\therefore(a-b)\) is a factor.

Solution of simultaneous equations using Cramer's rule:

Consider the linear equations
\(a_{1} x+b_{1} y=c_{1}\)
\(a_{2} x+b_{2} y=c_{2}\)
let \(\Delta=\left|\begin{array}{ll}a_{1} & b_{1} \\ a_{2} & b_{2}\end{array}\right| ; \Delta x=\left|\begin{array}{ll}c_{1} & b_{1} \\ c_{2} & b_{2}\end{array}\right|\) and
\(\Delta y=\left|\begin{array}{ll}a_{1} & c_{1} \\ a_{2} & c_{2}\end{array}\right|\), then \(x=\frac{\Delta x}{\Delta} ; y=\frac{\Delta y}{\Delta}\)

Provided \(\Delta \neq 0\). \(x, y\) are unique solutions of the given equations. This method of solving the line equations is called Cramer's rule.

Similarly for a set of three simultaneous linear equations in \(x, y\) and \(z\).
\(a_{1} x+b_{1} y+c_{1} z=d_{1}\)
\(a_{2} x+b_{2} y+c_{2} z=d_{2}\)
\(a_{3} x+b_{3} y+c_{3} z=d_{3}\), the solution of the system of equations by Cramer's rule is given by
\(x=\frac{\Delta x}{\Delta} ; y=\frac{\Delta y}{\Delta} ;\) and \(z=\frac{\Delta z}{\Delta}\)
provided \(\Delta \neq 0\). Where \(\Delta, \Delta x, \Delta y\) and \(\Delta z\) are the determinents formed in the same way as defined above.

WORKED EXAMPLES

PART - A

1. Solve \(\left|\begin{array}{ll}x & 4 \\ 9 & x\end{array}\right|=0\).

Solution:

\(\left|\begin{array}{ll}x & 4 \\ 9 & x\end{array}\right|=0\) By expanding we have
\(x^{2}-36=0\)
i.e \(x^{2}=36\)
\(\therefore x= \pm 6\)

2. Solve \(\left|\begin{array}{cc}x & 2 \\ x & 3x\end{array}\right|=0\)

Solution:

\(\left|\begin{array}{cc}x & 2 \\ x & 3 x\end{array}\right|=0\)
expand \(3 x^{2}-2 x=0\)
\(x(3 x-2)=0\)
\(x=0 ; \quad 3 x-2=0 \quad 3 x=2\)
\(x=0\) or \(x=\frac{2}{3}\)

3. Find the co-factor of 3 in the determinant \(\left|\begin{array}{ccc}1 & 2 & 0 \\ -1 & 3 & 4 \\ 5 & 6 & 7\end{array}\right|\).

Solution:

\(\text{Cofactor of } 3 =A_{22}=(-1)^{2+2}\left|\begin{array}{ll}1 & 0 \\ 5 & 7\end{array}\right|\)
\(=(-1)^{4}[7-0]\)
\(=+7\)

4. Write down the minor 3 in the determinant \(\left|\begin{array}{ccc}1 & -2 & -1 \\ 3 & 4 & -4 \\ 5 & 0 & 2\end{array}\right|=0\).

Solution:

The minor of \(3=\left|\begin{array}{cc}-2 & -1 \\ 0 & 2\end{array}\right|=-4-0 = -4\)

PART - B

1. Find the value of 'm' when \(\left|\begin{array}{ccc}m & 2 & 1 \\ 3 & 4 & 2 \\ -7 & 3 & 0\end{array}\right|=0\).

Solution:

Given \(\left|\begin{array}{ccc}m & 2 & 1 \\ 3 & 4 & 2 \\ -7 & 3 & 0\end{array}\right|=0\)
expand along \(R_{1}\), we have
\(m(0-6)-2(0+14)+1(9+28)=0\)
\(-6 m-28+37=0\)
\(-6 m+9=0\)
\(-6 m=-9\)
\(6 m=9\)
\(m=\frac{9}{6}=\frac{3}{2}\)

2. Using Cramer's rule solve \(2 x-3 y=5 ; x-4 y=8\).

Solution:

\(2 x-3 y=5\)
\(x-4 y=8\).
where \(\Delta=\left|\begin{array}{ll}2 & -3 \\ 1 & -4\end{array}\right| = -8+3=-5\)
\(\Delta x=\left|\begin{array}{ll}5 & -3 \\ 8 & -4\end{array}\right|=-20+24=4\)
\(\Delta y=\left|\begin{array}{ll}2 & 5 \\ 1 & 8\end{array}\right|=16-5=11\)
By Cramer's rule
\(x=\frac{\Delta x}{\Delta}=\frac{4}{-5} ; \mathrm{y}=\frac{\Delta \mathrm{y}}{\Delta}=\frac{11}{-5}=\frac{-11}{5}\)

3. Find the values of 'x' when \(\left|\begin{array}{ccc}x & 6 & 3 \\ 1 & x & 1 \\ -2 & 4 & x\end{array}\right|=0\).

Solution:

\(\left|\begin{array}{ccc}x & 6 & 3 \\ 1 & x & 1 \\ -2 & 4 & x\end{array}\right|=0\)
\(x\left(x^{2}-4\right)-6(x+2)+3(4+2 x)=0\)
\(x^{3}-4 x-6 x-12+12+6 x=0\)
\(x^{3}-4 x=0\)
\(x\left(x^{2}-4\right)=0\)
\(x=0, \quad x^{2}-4=0 \quad x^{2}=4\)
\(x=0, \quad x= \pm 2\)

PART - C

1. Using Cramer's rule. Solve the following simultaneous equations,
\(x+y+z=2\)
\(2 x-y-2 z=-1\)
\(x-2 y-z=1\)

Solution:

Let \(\Delta =\left|\begin{array}{ccc}1 & 1 & 1 \\ 2 & -1 & -2 \\ 1 & -2 & -1\end{array}\right|=1(1-4)-1(-2+2)+1(-4+1)\)
\(=1(-3)-1(0)+1(-3)\)
\(=-3-3\)
\(\Delta =-6 \neq 0\)

\(\Delta x =\left|\begin{array}{ccc}2 & 1 & 1 \\ -1 & -1 & -2 \\ 1 & -2 & -1\end{array}\right|=2(1-4)-1(1+2)+1(2+1)\)
\(=2(-3)-1(3)+1(3)\)
\(=-6-3+3\)
\(\Delta x =-6\)

\(\Delta y =\left|\begin{array}{ccc}1 & 2 & 1 \\ 2 & -1 & -2 \\ 1 & 1 & -1\end{array}\right|=1(1+2)-2(-2+2)+1(2+1)\)
\(=1(3)-2(0)+1(3)\)
\(=3+3\)
\(\Delta y =6\)

\(\Delta z =\left|\begin{array}{ccc}1 & 1 & 2 \\ 2 & -1 & -1 \\ 1 & -2 & 1\end{array}\right|=1(-1-2)-1(2+1)+2(-4+1)\)
\(=1(-3)-1(3)+2(-3)\)
\(=-3-3-6\)
\(\Delta z =-12\)

\(x= \frac{\Delta x}{\Delta}=\frac{-6}{-6}=1\)
\(y =\frac{\Delta y}{\Delta}=\frac{6}{-6}=-1 ; \quad \mathrm{z}=\frac{\Delta z}{\Delta}=\frac{-12}{-6}=2\)
