\section*{INSTRUCTIONS TO CANDIDATES}

You should have the following for this examination:
Answer booklet;
Mathematical table/Scientific calculator;
Drawing instruments.
This paper consists of EIGHT questions.
Answer FIVE questions in the answer booklet provided.
All questions carry equal marks.
Maximum marks for each part of a question are as indicated.
Candidutes should answer the questions in English.
1. (a) Simplify the expressions:
(i) $\frac{\left(1-x^{3}\right)^{-\frac{1}{2}}-x\left(1-x^{3}\right)^{\frac{1}{2}}}{\left(1-x^{3}\right)}$.
(ii) $\frac{\log 1024-\frac{1}{2} \log 256+3 \log 16}{\log 64-\frac{1}{2} \log 16+\log 4}$.
(b) Solve the equations:
(i) $\quad \log _{2} 4^{2 x}=\log _{3} 27^{(x-1)}$;
(ii) $16 \log _{2} x+4 \log _{4} x+2 \log _{16} x=37$;
(iii) $\quad \log _{y} 27=3+\frac{1}{\log _{8} y}$.
2. (a) Given that the complex numbers $z_{1}=3+4 j, z_{2}=6-7 j$ and $z_{3}=1+j$, express $z=z_{1}+\frac{z_{2} z_{3}}{z_{2}+z_{3}}$ in the form $a+j b$.
(b) Use De Moivre's theorem to prove that $\sin 3 A=3 \sin A-4 \sin ^{3} A$.
(c) Given that $z=1+j$ is a root of the equation $z^{4}-6 z^{3}+15 z^{2}-18 z+10=0$, determine the other roots.
3. (a) Given that $P \cosh 2 x+Q \sin h 2 x=4 e^{2 x}-3 e^{-2 x}$, determine the values of P and Q .
(b) Prove the identities:
(i) $\quad \cosh \frac{x}{2}=\sqrt{\frac{\cosh x}{2}+1}$;
(ii) $\tanh \frac{x}{2}=\sqrt{\frac{\cosh }{\cosh } \frac{-1}{+1}}$.
(c) Solve the equation $4 \cosh 3 x-5 \sin h 3 x=3$ correct to 3 decimal places.
4. (a) Find the derivative of $y=\sin 2 x$ from first principles.
(b) By using implicit differentiation, determine the equation of the normal to the curve $x^{2}+y^{2}+3 x y-8 x-3 y+6=0$ at the point $(1,1)$.
(c) Determine the turning points of the function:
$$f(x)=\frac{x^{3}}{3}+\frac{x^{2}}{2}-6 x+7 \text { and state their nature. }$$
5. (a) Determine the fourth term in the binomial expansion of $(x+3 y)^{12}$ and find its value when $x=\frac{1}{2}$ and $y=\frac{1}{3}$.
(b) Determine the first four terms in the binomial expansion of $\left(9-\frac{x}{3}\right)^{\frac{1}{2}}$ and state its range of validity.
(c) Show that for small values:
(i) of $\mathrm{x}, \sqrt{\frac{1-\frac{x}{4}}{1+\frac{x}{4}}}=1-\frac{x}{4}+\frac{x^{2}}{32}$ approximately;
(ii) By setting $x=\frac{1}{16}$, determine $\sqrt{\frac{63}{65}}$ approximately correct to 4 decimal places.
6. (a) Use factorization method to solve the equation $\frac{2}{(x-2)}+\frac{3}{(x-3)}=4$.
(b) The roots of the equation $a x^{2}+b x+c=0$ are $(\alpha-2)$ and $(\alpha+2)$ where $\mathrm{a}, \mathrm{b}, \mathrm{c}$ and $\alpha$ are constants. Prove that $b^{2}=4 a(c+4 a)$.
(c) The currents in a d.c circuit satisfy the simultaneous equations:
$2 I_{1}+I_{2}-3 I_{3}=-5$
$I_{1}-2 I_{2}+4 I_{3}=9$
$3 I_{1}+I_{2}+2 I_{3}=7$
Use the method of elimination to solve for $I_{1}, I_{2}$ and $I_{3}$.
7. (a) Solve $2 \sin ^{2} \theta+3 \sin \theta=2$ for $0<\theta<360^{\circ}$.
(b) Prove the identities:
(i) $\frac{\cos A+\sin A}{\cos A-\sin A}=\sec 2 A+\tan 2 A$;
(ii) $\sin 2 \phi+\sin \phi+\sin 3 \phi=2 \sin 2 \phi(1+2 \cos \phi)$;
(iii) $\frac{\cos 2 A+\sin 2 A-1}{\cos 2 A-\sin 2 A+1}=\tan A$.
(c) Given that $\sin A=\frac{3}{5}$ and $\cos B=\frac{12}{13}$. A is obtuse and B is acute. Determine:
(i) $\operatorname{Sin}(A+B)$;
(ii) $\quad \tan (\mathrm{A}-\mathrm{B})$.
8. (a) Given that $z=f(x, y)=\cos (a x+b y)$, Show that $\frac{\partial^{2} z}{\partial x^{2}}+\frac{\partial^{2} z}{\partial y^{2}}=-\left(a^{2}+b^{2}\right) z$ where a and b are constants.
(b) Evaluate the integrals:
(i) $\int \frac{2 x+4}{(x-1)(x-2)} d x$;
(ii) $\int \cos ^{2} \frac{\theta}{2} d \theta$;
(iii) $\int \frac{\left(e^{3 z-1}-4\right)}{2 e^{x}} d x$.
(c) Using calculus, determine the root mean square value of the voltage function $V=50 \sin (10 t)$ volts in the interval $t=0$ to $t=\pi$ correct to 2 decimal places.
