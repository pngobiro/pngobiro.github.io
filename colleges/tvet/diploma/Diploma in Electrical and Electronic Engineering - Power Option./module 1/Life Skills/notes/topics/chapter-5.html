Coping with Emotions

üìö Definition of Emotions

Emotions refer to any strong feelings such as fear, anger sorrow, joy and love. They are strong feelings that one has when something has happened or something has been done to you that you don't like. Emotions are motivated by psychological arousal and pleasantness or unpleasantness.

Emotions can be good or bad. Common negative emotions include anger, fear, guilt, jealousy, shame, bitterness

Common positive emotions include: love, interest, curiosity, happiness, etc

üò° Anger

It is an emotion related to perception of having been offended or wronged and it is a tendency to undo that wrong doing by retaliation. It is normal for people to express anger. Anger is healthy when the person experiencing it is able to control the emotions. We ordinarily express our anger through words. However when people are unable to control their anger they express it physically e.g. by hitting, slapping, punching, physical rage etc

Causes of Anger:

  • A memory can make you angry
  • You may become angry when you have a problem that frustrates you and worries you.
  • Your anger can be directed at a person or situation

Effects of Anger

  • When you are angry, your heart beats faster, you experience muscle tension, grinding of the teeth, ringing in the hear, excessive sweating, severe head ache etc
  • With anger one may make risky decisions
  • People normally express anger in an aggressive manner e.g. by cursing, swearing, fighting etc
  • Destructive behavior. With anger people do stupid things due to judgment mage while angry
  • Anger can cause depression
  • Anger can lead to drug and alcohol addiction

Anger Management Techniques

  • Relaxation, deep breathing and repeating calming words to calm you down, reduce the intensity of your anger. This will reduce your angry feelings so that you can take better hold of your emotions.
  • making conscious efforts not to curse or swear when angry
  • Counseling will help you understand your anger and how to deal with anger issues.
  • Exercise it is important to learn safe and effective ways to release your anger i.e. sleeping or going away from the situation.
  • if you feel out of control walk away from the situation temporarily until you cool down
  • changing the way we react to triggering situations it is the inappropriate reactions to anger that are wrong
  • Practicing forgiveness, anger often results in resentment. This can be resolved through forgiveness.

üòî Shyness

It is a form of fear whereby sometimes one shrinks from familiarity and contact with other people.

Effects of Shyness

  • Persistent shyness produces timidity resulting in underachievement.
  • can lead to generalized fear of strange things and this may hinder creativity
  • socially shy children tend to be overlooked and neglected by the group leading to poor adjustments
  • people who are shy find it difficult to assume leadership roles

Shyness Management Techniques

  • replace negative thoughts with positive action
  • set realistic and meaningful goals for yourself
  • rehearsing a situation before hand
  • acting confidently

üò¢ Grief

It is a natural response to a loss. it also refers to an emotional suffering you feel when something or someone you love is taken away. the death of loved one cause the most intense grief.

Common Symptoms of Grief

  • Shock and disbelief- you may trouble believing that the loss really happened or even deny the truth
  • Sadness- you may feeling of emptiness, despair or loneliness
  • Guilt- you may regret or feel guilty for not doing something to prevent the death or even if there was nothing you would have done
  • Anger- you may be angry at yourself, God the doctors or even the person who died for abandoning you.
  • Fear- you may feel anxious or insecure. You may have to face life without the person who died.

Coping with Grief

  • accepting the help of friends and family members
  • you can go to church and pray
  • counseling- man experienced therapist can help a person work through intense emotions and overcome obstacles to grieving
  • acknowledge the pain trying to avoid feelings of sadness and loss only prolonged the grieving process and unresolved grief can lead to complications such as depressions, substance abuse and health problems
  • look after you physical health

üòä Other Emotions

Curiosity-This is a pleasant emotional state. It provides motivation to explore things

Love- it is considered an emotion of strong affection and personal attachment

Happiness- it is a state of mind or feeling characterized by contentment, satisfaction pleasure or joy.

Interest- it is a feeling or emotion that causes attention to focus on an object event or process.

üß† Emotional Intelligence

Refers to skills, capabilities and competencies that influence a person's ability to succeed copying with environmental demands and is composed of 5 dimensions

  • Self awareness- ability to be aware of who you are
  • Self management - ability to manage one s own emotions and impulses
  • Self motivation-ability to persist in the face of setbacks and failures
  • Empathy- ability to sense how others feel
  • Social skills- ability to handle the emotions of others

How to Control Negative Emotions

  • Seeking counseling
  • Praying
  • Relaxation
  • Exercising
  • Being confident
  • Accepting that things happen naturally identifying problematic emotions
  • Talking to friends
  • Delaying your reactions

üíñ Values Associated with Emotional Intelligence

  • Humility- allow to consult and seek help when dealing with problematic emotions
  • Tolerance- you will not easily get emotional about other people's opinions e.g. becoming angry
  • Respect- you will not insult/ Harm or show any form of disrespect to yourself and others because of your emotions, you will learn to control your emotions
