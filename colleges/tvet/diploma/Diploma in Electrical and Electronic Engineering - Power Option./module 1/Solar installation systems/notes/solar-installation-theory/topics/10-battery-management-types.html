There are two categories of battery types. One is type of structure, the other is depth of discharge.

TYPE OF MAINTENANCE

  • Refill type
  • ‚Ä¢ Refill type batteries need top up of distilled water to maintain electrolyte level. This type of battery is easily available so that often used for SHS. However, most of the failure of SHS is caused by this type of batteries. Electrolyte is easily dried up or tap water is added to the electrolyte.
  • Maintenance free type
  • ‚Ä¢ Maintenance free type batteries do not require top up of distilled water. This has been designed to convert produced gas into water to recycle and there is a large reservoir of electrolyte. If overcharged, however, it produces too much gas resulting in the damage of battery.

TYPE OF CYCLE (DEPTH OF DISCHARGE)

  • Automotive type
  • ‚Ä¢ This type is very common and always available easily. This type is designed to withdraw large amount of current in seconds to start an engine. Therefore, this type of battery is not suitable for continuous discharge. Maximum depth of discharge is approximately $50 \%$.
  • Deep cycle type
  • ‚Ä¢ This type is designed for continuous discharging. Good for SHS use. Maximum depth of discharge is approximately $80 \%$.

TYPE OF ELECTROLYTE

  • Flooded type
  • ‚Ä¢ This type uses liquid electrolyte.
  • Gel type
  • ‚Ä¢ This type uses gel electrolyte. The battery is completely sealed.

üìè CAPACITY

RATINGS

Battery capacity is normally shown as Ampere-hour (Ah). This means available current for certain hours is obtained by dividing current. For example, 100Ah means 5A is discharged for 20 hours. (100Ah / 5A = 20h)

DEPTH OF DISCHARGE (DOD)

A battery's capacity cannot be all used. If a battery has capacity of 100 Ah , it stores 100 Ah . However, this battery cannot deliver 100Ah. The amount of power available for discharge depends on the battery type. This is called the depth of discharge.

Automotive battery can discharge $50 \%$. Deep cycle battery can discharge $80 \%$.

AMPERE-HOUR EFFICIENCY

When 50Ah battery is fully charged, it is expected that the same amount of 50Ah can be discharged within depth of discharge. If efficiency is $100 \%$, it is possible. Unfortunately, there are no 100\% efficient batteries. Energy is consumed or lost as a result of gas formation and heat generation during charging.

Efficiency is around $80 \%$ to $90 \%$ depending on quality of battery, type of battery, life stage and charge/discharge rate.

LIFETIME

The lifetime of a battery is a constrain on a SHS. The lifetime of a battery is shorter than other components of SHS, therefore, users must save money to replace a battery every 3 to 5 years. There are several factors that determine the lifetime of a battery and includes.

  • 1. Cycling ability
  • 2. Temperature
  • 3. Quality of battery
  • 4. Performance of charge controller
