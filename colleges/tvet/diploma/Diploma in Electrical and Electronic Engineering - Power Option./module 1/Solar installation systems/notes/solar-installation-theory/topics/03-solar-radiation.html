Solar Radiation Principles, Types, Irradiance & Insolation

☀️ PRINCIPLES OF SOLAR RADIATION

This is how sunlight reaches the earth. radiation is composed of millions of high energy particles known as photons. Each unit of solar radiation carries a fixed amount of solar energy.

Solar radiation often called the solar resource, is a general term for the electromagnetic radiation emitted by the sun.

Sunshine reaches the earth as a type of energy called radiation. Radiation is composed of millions of high-energy particles called photons. Each unit of solar radiation, or photon, carries a fixed amount of energy. Depending on the amount of energy that it carries, solar radiation falls into different categories including infrared (i.e. heat), visible (radiation that we can see) and ultraviolet (very high energy radiation).

Solar energy arrives at the edge of the Earth's atmosphere at a constant rate of about 1350 watts per square metre (W/m2): this is called the 'solar constant'. However, not all this energy reaches the Earth's surface. The atmosphere absorbs and reflects much of it, and by the time it reaches the Earth's surface, it is reduced to a maximum of about $1000 \mathrm{~W} / \mathrm{m} 2$.

↔️ TYPES OF SOLAR RADIATION

  • 1. Direct Radiation
  • • The portion of the light that appears to come straight from the Sun, normally called 'sunshine', is known as direct solar radiation. It can be focused to generate very high temperatures or can be used without such concentration in active solar heating systems.
  • 2. Indirect Radiation (diffuse radiation)
  • • it is radiation that is reflected by the atmosphere or scattered by clouds, smog, dust, smoke thereby reducing the amount that reaches the ground.

측정 IRRADIANCE VS. INSOLATION

Solar irradiance refers to the solar radiation actually striking a surface, or the power received per unit area from the sun. This is measured in watts per square meter ($\mathrm{W/m^2}$) or kilowatts per square meter ($\mathrm{kW} / \mathrm{m} 2$). If a solar module is facing the sun directly (i.e. if the module is perpendicular to the sun's rays) irradiance will be much higher than if the module is at a large angle to the sun.

Insolation (a short way of saying incident solar radiation) is a measure of the solar energy received on a specified area over a specified period of time.
