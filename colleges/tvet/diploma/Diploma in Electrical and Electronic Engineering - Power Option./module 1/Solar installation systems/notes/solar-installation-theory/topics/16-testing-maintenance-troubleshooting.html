Testing, Maintenance & Troubleshooting

‚úÖ TESTING AND FINAL CONNECTIONS

Before making final connections, carry out the following electrical tests on the system. These electrical pre-checks will ensure safety, efficiency, and proper operation. No electrical cables or system should be energized before it has been tested.

  • 1. Solar PV system tests
  • 2. Electrical tests

SOLAR PV SYSTEM TESTS

  • 1. Array: check the module/array's open-circuit voltage. Verify correct polarity/color-coding of the cables coming into the building (with a multimeter set on the DC volt scale), and verify it short-circuit current (with the multimeter set on the 0-10 DC amp scale).
  • 2. Array-battery voltage drop: check the voltage drop between the module(s) and the battery Full sun and a substantial current flowing into the batteries are required for this test. A voltage drops of more than 2 percent between the array and battery is unacceptable (some types of charge controllers reduce the voltage so consider this).
  • 3. Battery: check the battery voltage, DC polarity, and fuse with a multimeter.

ELECTRICAL TESTS

  • 1. Verification of polarity
  • 2. Test of the effectiveness of earthing
  • 3. Insulation resistance
  • 4. Test of ring circuit continuity

FINAL CONNECTIONS

After conducting all necessary wiring tests and a comprehensive visual inspection of the installation the final connection sequence can be carried out. The order presented below is only a suggested sequence.

  • 1. Connect the wires from the battery to the charge controller-
  • 2. Connect the load and check load polarity
  • 3. Connect the solar cell module(s)- First, turn all the lamps and sockets off using the main switches in the controller (if there are none, turn them off at the room switches). The two main cables ( + and - ) coming from the PV array will then be either connected to the charge controller (in a small one- or two-module system) or to an isolator/disconnect which feeds into the charge controller.
  • 4. Final visual inspection- Check to see that all the lights and sockets are in working order (plug an appliance into each socket to test it). Using a voltmeter, check for voltage drop at the farthest point from the batteries. Check the LED readings on the charge controller. The system wiring is complete, and it can now be safely commissioned and used. Remember, batteries should be fully charged before the system is utilized.

üîß MAINTENANCE OF PV SYSTEMS

A properly installed solar electric system requires relatively little maintenance. The work involved in maintaining a solar electric system is much less than that needed to maintain a diesel- or petrol-powered generator. The best maintenance practice is to make regular inspections of the equipment (especially batteries and modules), to make sure things are kept clean, and to make sure all electrical contacts are tight.

Important PV system information includes:

  • ‚Ä¢ Circuit diagrams and maps showing the location of batteries, loads, wire runs, junction boxes, and buried cables.
  • ‚Ä¢ The system completion, inspection, and test certificate.
  • ‚Ä¢ System design worksheets, plans, and specifications.
  • ‚Ä¢ Manuals, warranties, and manufacturers' specifications for system components.
  • ‚Ä¢ The system logbook. This contains records of battery state of charge and history, installation
