Self-Employment vs. Salaried (Paid) Employment

üíº Paid Employment Characteristics

  1. Under control of another person (employer)
  2. Supply your labor only
  3. Cannot sub-contract the work
  4. Mutuality of obligation to offer work and perform work
  5. Do not supply equipment/materials for the job
  6. Receive fixed hourly/weekly/monthly wages
  7. Entitled to sick pay/holiday pay etc.
  8. Employer provides insurance cover
  9. Work set number of hours per week
  10. Employer deducts tax from wages under PAYE

üöÄ Self-Employment Characteristics

  1. Own your own business
  2. Are exposed to financial risk
  3. Can subcontract the work
  4. No mutuality of obligation
  5. Supply necessary equipment for the job
  6. Cost and agree a price for the job
  7. Not entitled to paid leave
  8. Provide your own insurance cover
  9. Control your own hours in fulfilling job
  10. You are registered for Self-Assessment and are required to file your own returns

‚úÖ Advantages of Self-Employment

  1. Being your own boss - you'll be in control of all decisions affecting your working life. You'll decide on your business plan, quality assurance procedures, pricing and marketing strategies. You'll have job security and learn new skills.

  2. Work enjoyment - you'll be doing work that you enjoy, picking who you work with, and dealing directly with customers or clients.

  3. Flexibility - you can decide your own hours, working conditions, and business location. Working from home can reduce startup and operating costs.

  4. Higher earning potential - you can potentially make more than working for someone else, and keep all the proceeds.

  5. Control over business growth - you decide when to spend money to help your business grow.

  6. Family employment - you can distribute income to family members by hiring them as employees.

  7. Job satisfaction - engaging in business that suits your interests and skills.

  8. Improved living standards for you and your dependents.

‚ö†Ô∏è Disadvantages of Self-Employment

  1. Must be willing to make sacrifices for the job
  2. Long working hours with less time for family or leisure
  3. Last to get paid when cash flow is tight
  4. Substantial financial risk and need for cash backup
  5. No employer benefits (vacation, sick leave, health insurance)
  6. Lower social status in some contexts
  7. Can lead to specialization and boredom
  8. No assurance of income
  9. Limited skills in business management

‚úÖ Advantages of Paid Employment

  1. Job security
  2. Income stability
  3. Predictable work life
  4. Various allowances (house, medical, commuter)
  5. Socialization among employees
  6. Room for growth through promotions
  7. Training facilities through seminars and workshops
  8. Sponsorship opportunities for further studies

‚ö†Ô∏è Disadvantages of Paid Employment

  1. Limited monetary rewards for ideas and effort
  2. Creating wealth for others while receiving fixed wages
  3. Financial limitations and dependency
  4. No opportunity to supplement current earnings
  5. Limited recognition of abilities
  6. Limited leisure opportunities
  7. Lack of independence - following orders from superiors
  8. Difficulty implementing personal ideas
  9. May not receive challenging responsibilities
