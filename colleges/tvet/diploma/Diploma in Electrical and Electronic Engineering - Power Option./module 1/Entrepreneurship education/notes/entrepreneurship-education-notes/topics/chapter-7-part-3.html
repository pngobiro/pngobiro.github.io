Business Resources and Sources of Finance

Resources for a Business

A resource refers to anything that can be used to achieve an objective. These resources include:

1. Human Resource

Human resource (personnel) refers to the employees working in an organization. Employees will only be useful if they have the necessary knowledge and skills to successfully carry out the assigned tasks. Management must match the correct people with the correct job activities to ensure business success.

2. Financial Resource

Money is required to start and operate a business. A business with adequate finances that are properly allocated to various activities and monitored is likely to do better than one lacking such aspects.

3. Physical Resources

These include tangible facilities which belong to the business such as buildings, machinery, furniture and stock. Availability of such facilities enables the business to operate.

4. Technology

This refers to skills and methods used in production. Use of modern technology enhances production of goods and services.

Sources of Finance

1. Owner's Capital

Often the only source available for sole traders starting in business. While partnerships have more people involved, this capital is often quickly turned into fixed assets. Business owners could invest more money if there's a cash flow shortfall, but they may have already invested all their capital or be unwilling to risk further investment.

2. Ploughed Back Profits

Firms make profit by selling a product for more than it costs to produce. This is the most basic source of funds for any company and hopefully the method that brings in the most money.

3. Borrowings

Companies can borrow money privately through bank loans or publicly through debt issues. The drawback is the interest that must be paid to the lender.

4. Issue of Shares

A company can generate money by selling part of itself in the form of shares to investors (equity funding). While investors don't require interest payments, profits must be divided among all shareholders.

5. Overdraft

A form of bank loan where a business can withdraw more money than it has in its account. While often a cheap way of borrowing money, the bank:

  • Charges interest on the overdrawn amount
  • Only allows overdrafts for credit-worthy businesses
  • Can demand repayment at any time

6. Bank Loan

A fixed amount borrowed for a fixed period. Features include:

  • Regular repayments of interest and capital
  • May require security against assets
  • Usually for capital investment rather than short-term needs

7. Leasing

Allows business use of an asset through monthly payments without ownership. Benefits include:

  • No large initial capital outlay
  • Predictable monthly expenses
  • Access to needed equipment without purchase
