Types of Entrepreneurs

Categories of Entrepreneurs

1. Craft Entrepreneurs

Exploits and utilizes personal skills to start a business without thinking of its growth or the expansion objectives. Characteristics include:

  • No expanding even after a long time
  • Not business expansion oriented
  • Skills can be technical skills, professional skills etc.

2. Opportunistic Entrepreneurs

A person who starts a business, acts as a manager and with a view to expand the business to maximum. Characteristics include:

  • Might not have the skill to profession but has the opportunity to start and direct others
  • Sees beyond and has abilities to initiate and venture into business that will expand and grow
  • Innovative and able to delegate activities to others
  • Ready and able to scan the environment

3. Co-operative Entrepreneurs

Collaborates with other co-operative entrepreneurs to start and complete projects where each co-operative entrepreneur brings different skills and talents to the collaboration.

4. Creative Entrepreneurs

A creative artist who values their product above all else and puts Intellectual Property (IP) first. Creative Entrepreneurs are dedicated to the artistic and creative expression that is unique to them.

5. Lifestyle Entrepreneurs

Values their lifestyle first and builds their businesses so that they have a rewarding and sustainable lifestyle founded on and driven by their personal interests and talents.

6. Social Entrepreneurs

Values social change first and is driven to improve and transform their society, their environment, and economic conditions. Key aspects include:

  • Operating from a triple bottom line perspective of people, planet, and profit
  • Profit is often reinvested into the enterprise rather than being distributed to shareholders
  • Recognize social problems and take action using entrepreneurial principles
  • Focus on sustainable social change

7. Bottom-Line Entrepreneurs

Takes the initiative and launches a new enterprise that takes advantage of market opportunities with the goals of building capital and profits.

8. Innovative Entrepreneurs

Introduces new goods, inaugurates new method of production, discovers new market and reorganizes the enterprise. These entrepreneurs can work only when a certain level of development is already achieved and people look forward to change and improvement.

9. Imitative Entrepreneurs

Creatively imitate the innovative technical achievement made by another firm. Suitable for underdeveloped countries as it is hard for them to bear the high cost of innovation.

10. Fabian Entrepreneurs

Characterized by very great caution and skepticism to experiment any change in their enterprises. They:

  • Usually do not take any new challenge
  • Imitate only when it becomes perfectly clear that failure to do so would result in a loss of position
