Limited Liability Companies (Joint Stock Companies)

Definition and Features

A company is an association of persons registered under the companies act who contribute capital in order to carry out business with a view of making a profit. The act of registering a company is referred to as incorporation. Incorporation creates an organization that is separate and distinct from the person forming it.

A company is a legal entity that has the status of an "artificial person". It therefore has most of the rights and obligations of a human being. A company can:

  • Own property
  • Enter into contracts in its own name
  • Borrow money
  • Hire and fire employees
  • Sue and be sued on its own right
  • Form subordinate agencies
  • Disseminate information

Key Features

  • Artificial person with same rights as natural person
  • Members have limited liabilities
  • Perpetual life independent of owners
  • Created for particular purposes

Formation Process

People who wish to form a company are referred to as promoters. The promoters must submit the following documents to the registrar of companies:

1. Memorandum of Association

This document defines the relationship between the company and outsiders. It contains:

  • Name Clause: Company name ending with "Limited" (Ltd) or "PLC" for public companies
  • Objects Clause: Activities the company will engage in
  • Situation Clause: Registered office location
  • Capital Clause: Amount of capital and division into shares
  • Declaration Clause: Signed statement by promoters

2. Articles of Association

This document governs the internal operations of the company. It contains:

  • Rights of shareholders
  • Meeting procedures
  • Election rules for officials
  • Account preparation rules
  • Directors' powers and duties
  • Capital alteration methods

Additional Required Documents

  • List of directors with details
  • Declaration of registration requirements compliance
  • Directors' consent statement
  • Statement of share capital

Sources of Capital

1. Shares

The main source of capital is through sale of shares. Types of shares include:

Ordinary Shares

  • Have voting rights
  • No fixed rate of dividends
  • Claim dividends after preference shares
  • Last to be paid during liquidation

Preference Shares

  • Fixed rate of dividends
  • Prior claim to dividends
  • No voting rights
  • Can be redeemable or irredeemable
  • Can be cumulative or non-cumulative

2. Debentures

Loans from the public to a company with fixed interest rates, can be secured or unsecured.

3. Other Sources

  • Loans from banks and financial institutions
  • Profits ploughed back
  • Bank overdraft
  • Leasing and renting of property
  • Credit purchases
  • Hire purchase arrangements

Private Limited Company

Characteristics

  • 2-50 shareholders (excluding employees)
  • No public advertisement of shares
  • Restricted transfer of shares
  • Can be managed by one or two directors
  • Can start business immediately after incorporation
  • No authorized minimum share capital
  • Separate legal entity
  • Limited liability protection
