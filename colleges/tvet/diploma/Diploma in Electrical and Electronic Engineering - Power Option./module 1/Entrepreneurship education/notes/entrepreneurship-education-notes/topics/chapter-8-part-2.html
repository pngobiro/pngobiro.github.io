Business Plan Components - Part 1

1. Purpose

This is a short 2-3 paragraph section explaining why the plan is being written. For a plan to be used in application for funds, it should clearly state:

General Information

  • The types of funds being sought
  • Whether loan or grant
  • The reason for the funds

For Loan Applications

  • Amount required
  • How the funds are to be used
  • Duration of loan required
  • Proposed repayment pattern
  • Security available

This section helps readers understand the rest of the business plan by providing clear context and objectives.

2. Executive Summary

This is a short section summarizing the key points about the plan. It explains in brief the whole plan and should be written after the completion of the whole plan.

While this appears at the beginning of the plan, it should be written last to ensure it accurately reflects all aspects of the business plan.

3. Business Description

For Existing Businesses

  • Business form and ownership
  • When started
  • Past performance, success and failures
  • Reason for the required assistance

For New Start-up Businesses

  • Mission of the new venture
  • Reasons for going into business
  • Why you think you will be successful
  • Development work completed to date
  • Products or services description (including patent, copyright or trademark status)
  • Location and reasons for choosing it
  • Building details (new/renovation needs/costs)
  • Property status (leased/owned and terms)
  • Required office equipment
  • Equipment acquisition method (purchase/lease)
  • Relevant experience for business success

4. Marketing Plan

The marketing plan is based on the market data received in the market research activities. It describes the marketing conditions and strategies proposed for positioning the products and services.

Key Components

  • Description of goods and services being produced or to be produced and their uses
  • Present market situations and their products
  • Target market and expected market share
  • Advantages of your products against competing goods or services
  • Proposed price and distribution channels
  • Expected future market growth

The marketing plan should demonstrate a clear understanding of your market and how you plan to compete effectively within it.
