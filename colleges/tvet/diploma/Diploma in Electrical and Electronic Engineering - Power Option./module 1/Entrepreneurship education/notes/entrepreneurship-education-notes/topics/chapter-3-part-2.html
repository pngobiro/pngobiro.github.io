Cultural Factors in Entrepreneurship

Cultural Habits that Promote Entrepreneurial Development

Money Orientation

Money oriented people know the value of money and have the intention of making it. The money oriented people use the need of money as a motivating factor pushing them to being entrepreneurs.

Future Orientation

A society that has foresight to know about the future business environment is likely to have more entrepreneurs. This is because they are likely to visualize key changes that are likely to create opportunity.

Time Consciousness

Knowledge that time exists and its importance. Knowing the right time to start an entrepreneurial activity. Utilization of time. The correct timing of the market conditions.

Trust and Honesty

Through trust consumer demand is gained on the products and services available. Entrepreneurs should reciprocate this by ensuring honesty by providing the expected standards.

Hard Work

Willingness to work hard distinguishes between successful and unsuccessful persons.

Cultural Factors Inhibiting Entrepreneurial Development

Religion

Religious beliefs may deter entrepreneurial investments in items such as night clubs and pubs.

Language

Establishing businesses in areas where language barrier may allow poor communication or fear of invasion.

Personal Relationships

Married people may avoid getting involved in business activities since no time is spared for the family.

Attitude Towards Innovation

Especially in cultures which oppose innovation due to fear of change.

Networks

Poor networking and ability to meet people limit new:

  • Opportunities
  • New knowledge
  • New information

Technology

Lack of technical skills and knowledge may:

  • Slow growth and development of entrepreneurship
  • Lock one out of being competitive

Managing Inhibiting Factors

  1. Working in related business to gather the necessary skills required before one starts their own business
  2. Setting policies that ensure that entrepreneurship training is established in the school syllabus
  3. Young people to be encouraged to read articles from newspaper, watch television and business contacts to enable them choose products in demand with a bright future
  4. Young youths as well as aspiring adult entrepreneurs should be encouraged to get better and faster access to:
    • Knowledge
    • Information on business
    • Competition
    • Internet etc.
  5. Aspiring entrepreneurs to seek guidance in selection of machines and other facilities
