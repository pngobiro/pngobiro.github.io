Business Plan Components - Part 2

5. Management Plan

The management plan gives details on:

  • The owner/manager and other key people in the business
  • Their qualifications, skills and past experience
  • Their role ability to successfully carry out the proposed business
  • Any training required
  • Their salaries and benefits

6. Production/Operation Plan

This section details the manufacturing process and operations, including:

Key Components

  • Manufacturing process details
  • Items to be subcontracted
  • Costs and time frames
  • Physical plant layout
  • Technology requirements
  • Equipment needs
  • Raw materials
  • Manufacturing costs

Key Questions to Address

  • Will the venture handle all manufacturing operations?
  • Who will be the subcontractors?
  • What is the basis for selecting subcontractors?
  • What will be the production process layout?
  • What raw materials are needed and which are critical?
  • Who are the suppliers and what are the costs?
  • What are the future capital equipment needs?

7. Financial Plan

The financial plan includes projections of important financial data that determines the productivity of the venture and financial investments required.

Key Components

  • Estimated costs:
    • Purchase of fixed assets
    • Working capital needs
    • Stock and raw materials
    • Running costs, wages, power, transport
  • Funding sources:
    • Owner's contribution
    • Required loans/grants
  • Projected financial statements
  • Balance sheet details

8. Expected Performance

This section shows the expected future performance of the business in terms of:

  • Projected profits
  • Expected increase or decrease in business assets

This section should be prepared with the help of an accountant or consultant.

9. Action Plan

Details the actions for implementing the business proposal, showing:

  • What is to be done
  • When it will be done
  • Who will do it
  • How long it will take

10. Appendices

Supporting documents to include:

  • Projected financial statements (income, cash flow, balance sheets)
  • Past performance records
  • Property documentation (plot maps, title deeds, allocation letters)
  • CVs and certificates

Common Factors Leading to Business Plan Failure

  • Unreasonable goals set by the entrepreneur
  • Lack of commitment to the new enterprise
  • Inexperience and reliance on trial and error
  • No proper SWOT analysis
  • Poor customer orientation
  • Poor handling of finance matters
  • Unreasonable time schedule
  • No proper control for implementation
