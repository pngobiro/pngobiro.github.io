Forms of Business Ownership: Partnership

Introduction to Partnership

This is a relationship between persons who engage in a business with an aim of making profits/an association of two or more persons who run a business as co-owners. The owners are called Partners.

It is owned by a minimum of 2 and a maximum of 20 except for partnerships who provide professional services e.g. medicine and law which have a maximum of 50 persons.

Characteristics of Partnership

  • Capital is contributed by the partners themselves
  • Partnership has limited life - may end anytime due to death, bankruptcy or withdrawal of partners
  • Each partner acts as an agent of the firm with authority to enter into contracts
  • Partners are co-owners of a business, having an interest or claim in the business
  • Responsibility, profit and losses are shared on an agreed basis
  • All partners have equal right to participate in management

Types of Partnerships

A. According to Type/Liability of Partners

1. General/Ordinary Partnership

All members have unlimited liability which means in case a partnership is unable to pay its debts, the personal properties of the partners will be sold off to pay the debts.

2. Limited Partnerships

Members have limited liabilities where liability or responsibility is restricted to the capital contributed. However, there must be one partner whose liabilities are unlimited.

B. According to Period/Duration of Operation

1. Temporary Partnership

These are partnerships formed to carry out a specific task for a specific time after which the business automatically dissolves.

2. Permanent Partnerships

These are partnerships formed to operate indefinitely. They are also called a partnership at will.

C. According to Activity

1. Trading Partnerships

Partnership whose main activity is processing, manufacturing, construction or purchase and sale of goods.

2. Non-trading Partnerships

Partnership whose main activity is to offer services such as legal, medical or accounting services to members of the public.

Types of Partners

A. According to Role

  • Active Partner: Also known as acting partner, plays an active part in day-to-day running of the business
  • Sleeping/Dormant Partner: Does not participate in management but invests capital

B. According to Liability

  • General Partner: Has unlimited liabilities
  • Limited Partner: Has limited liabilities

C. According to Age

  • Major Partner: 18 years and above, responsible for all business debts
  • Minor Partner: Below 18 years, admitted with consent of other partners

D. According to Capital Contribution

  • Nominal/Quasi Partner: Does not contribute capital but allows use of name
  • Real Partner: Contributes capital to the business

E. Other Types

  • Secret Partner: Actively participates but not disclosed to public
  • Retiring Partner: Leaving the partnership
  • Incoming Partner: Being admitted to existing partnership

Formation

People who want to form a partnership must come together and agree on how the proposed business will be run to avoid future misunderstanding. The agreement can be oral or written (partnership deed).

Contents of Partnership Deed

  • Name, location and address of the business
  • Name, address and occupation of the partners
  • The purpose of the business
  • Capital to be contributed by each partner
  • Rate of interest on capital
  • Drawings by partners and rate of interest on drawings
  • Salaries and commissions to partners
  • Rate of interests on loans from partners to the business
  • Procedures of dissolving the partnership
  • Profit and loss sharing ratio
  • How to admit a new partner
  • What to do when a partner retires, dies or is expelled
  • The rights to inspect books of accounts
  • Who has the authority to act on behalf of other partners
