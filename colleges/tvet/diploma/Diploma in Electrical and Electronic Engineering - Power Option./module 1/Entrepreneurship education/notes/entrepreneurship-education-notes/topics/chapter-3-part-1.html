Entrepreneurial Culture

Understanding Culture

Definition of Culture

Culture is defined as a set of values, perceptions, wants and behavior learned by a member of a society from family and other institutions.

Culture is a tool of learned behavior patterns of living. It is a powerful human tool for survival, constantly changing and easily lost.

Weber argues that "Protestantism encourages a culture which emphasizes individualism, achievement motivation, legislation of entrepreneurial vocations, rationality and self-reliance."

Hosted defines culture as a collective programming of the mind which distinguishes the member of one group or category of people from another.

Entrepreneurial Culture Concept

Definition and Importance

Entrepreneurial culture refers to the way of embracing the concept of finding new opportunities in business and gathering the necessary resources to fill the opportunity.

Many governments around the world want to promote entrepreneurship because they have recognized its importance. In other words, entrepreneurial culture is a way of people embracing life by participating in activities that enable them to create new business enterprises.

Developing Entrepreneurial Culture

A country can develop the entrepreneurial culture by forming policies that constitute the following:

  • Integration of entrepreneurship training in the overall education system to tap on youths
  • Exposure of entrepreneurship those look potential to actual business practices and activities through the networks and business contacts of role models
  • Creation of a conducive and enabling environment that permits new business to emerge and flourish

The creation of entrepreneurial culture has to come from deep social convictions based on strong values and systems of the locals. It should be created in a way that it welcomes entrepreneurship and respects the investor while also reflecting the core values.

What Constitutes Entrepreneurial Culture?

  • Growth in concentration of firm's networks and linkages
  • Growth in intermediary organizations to which some tasks are delegated and different forms of entrepreneurship
  • High levels of education skills and learning
