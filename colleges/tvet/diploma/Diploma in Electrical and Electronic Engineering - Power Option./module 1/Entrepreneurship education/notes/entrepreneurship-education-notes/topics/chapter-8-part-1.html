Understanding Business Plans

What is a Business Plan?

A business plan is a written document that highlights the objectives of the business and steps to be followed in order to achieve these objectives. It indicates where the business is, where it wants to move to, how and when.

Need for a Business Plan

  • Avoiding mistakes

    In the process of drawing a plan, mistakes that would take place in the business are identified and corrected in the plan, helping to avoid their occurrence in the business.

  • Identifying strengths and weaknesses

    Helps in identifying strengths or weaknesses and where weaknesses are detected, remedial actions may be taken early enough.

  • Requirement by financiers

    Financial institutions such as banks may require a business plan before they can accept to finance the activities of the business.

  • Allocation of resources

    Helps to determine the resources required and plan on how and where to use them, ensuring resources are neither underutilized nor used for the wrong purpose.

  • Facilitates business evaluation

    Helps an entrepreneur to assess the progress of the business and any deviation from the intended plan can be corrected in good time.

  • Understanding competition

    Helps the entrepreneur to be fully aware of the market, understand important trends, and know competitors' strengths and weaknesses.

  • Motivating factor

    When communicated to all employees, it makes them aware of the direction to be taken by the business, motivating them to work towards that direction.

  • Adaptability

    A well-drawn business plan should give room to accommodate any changes that might occur in the future.

  • Tool for control

    Planning involves setting of standards against which performances can be assessed. In case of deviation, corrective measures can be taken.

Components of a Business Plan

A comprehensive business plan includes the following components:

  1. Purpose
  2. Executive summary
  3. Business description
  4. Marketing plan
  5. Management plan
  6. Production/operation plan
  7. Financial plan
  8. Expected performance
  9. Action plan
  10. Appendices
