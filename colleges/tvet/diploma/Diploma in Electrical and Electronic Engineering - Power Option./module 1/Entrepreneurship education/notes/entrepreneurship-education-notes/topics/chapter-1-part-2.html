Contribution of Entrepreneurship Towards National Development

  1. 1 Creates employment:
    • • Producers, warehousing, transport, insurance etc
    • • Which are basically levels of employment
  2. 2 Facilitates use of local resources through which:
    • • Local and international transactions are established
    • • Production of more goods and services
  3. 3 Rural development which in effect:
    • • Promotes change to the formal sector from informal sector
    • • Equitable development
    • • Reduced rural-urban migrations
  4. 4 Development of technology through:
    • • Establishment of research institutions
    • • Education systems
  5. 5 Government revenue:
    • • Through taxes
    • • From domestic borrowing (TBs)
  6. 6 Facilitating community development through:
    • • Establishment of small businesses
    • • Participation in community development projects
  7. 7 Providing a positive role model and facilitating:
    • • Competition between domestic entrepreneurship and imported
    • • Stimulating development of entrepreneurship
  8. 8 Reducing dependence on imported goods and services
  9. 9 Stimulates competition through:
    • • Quality production methods are adopted
    • • Quality products are produced
    • • Variety goods and services are produced
  10. 10 Facilitated development of the financial sector through:
    • • Capital accumulation is possible through savings
    • • Loaning is facilitated
    • • Development of the capital market

Other Benefits

  • • Improvement of standard of living
  • • Development of infrastructure by the government in where there are productive businesses
  • • Increased consumer choice because a variety of goods and services are provided
  • • Stability of prices
  • • Reduced domination of certain sectors by foreigners
