Business Opportunities and Evaluation

Sources of Business Ideas

1. Newspapers

Local newspapers like the Daily Nation, East African etc., especially in the business and advertising sections have a lot of information about commercial opportunities as well as personal services.

2. Shows and Exhibitions

Visiting shows and exhibitions organized by manufacturers and distributors and asking questions from the sales persons. Entrepreneurs can also get business ideas from products displayed in such shows.

3. Magazines and Journals

Reading magazines and journals with business information may equip an entrepreneur with new business ideas.

4. Hobbies

These are activities pursued for pleasure but they can also serve as a source of business ideas e.g. photography.

5. Vocational Training and Experience

A business idea may be developed from one's own area of training or experience e.g. a teacher may use ideas from their training to start a private school.

6. Surveys and Market Research

This involves conducting an investigation to gather information from consumers on what products they require.

7. Recycling/Using Waste Products

Some waste products could be converted into useful products e.g. scrap metal for making jikos, old tyres for making sandals etc.

Characteristics of a Good Business Idea

  • Easy to manage and involve minimal risk
  • Does not require excessive capital investments
  • Offers good returns on capital
  • Has scope for growth, expansion and diversification
  • Compatible with owner's goals and interest
  • Not against expectations of the society
  • Has a short gestation period
  • Has a readily available market
  • Easy to exit when necessary

Business Opportunities

A good business plan is not necessarily a business opportunity. A business idea becomes a business opportunity if it is viable i.e. it can be developed into a successful/profitable business enterprise.

A business opportunity is a favorable chance that an entrepreneur accepts for investment. It exists where there is a gap to be filled in the needs of the market. Examples of such gaps include:

  • In availability of products - Where goods and services needed by consumers are not available at all
  • Poor quality products - Opportunity exists if one offers better quality goods and services
  • Insufficient quantities - Where supplied goods are not enough to meet demand
  • Unaffordable prices - Opportunity exists where one would charge affordable prices
  • Poor services - Where customers are not served well

Evaluating Business Opportunities

Evaluation means assessing whether the identified opportunity is viable or not. This helps in arriving at the best decision concerning the business idea to implement. Evaluation should be done carefully, systematically and without emotions.

Personal Considerations

  • Objectives alignment with entrepreneur's goals
  • Required skills availability
  • Other commitments and their impact
  • Interest level in the business

Business Considerations

  • Market availability
  • Technology requirements
  • Raw materials and resource availability
  • Government policy compliance
  • Capital requirements
  • Expected profitability
  • Break-even period
  • Growth potential
  • Environmental impact
  • Security considerations
  • Competition level
  • Associated risks
