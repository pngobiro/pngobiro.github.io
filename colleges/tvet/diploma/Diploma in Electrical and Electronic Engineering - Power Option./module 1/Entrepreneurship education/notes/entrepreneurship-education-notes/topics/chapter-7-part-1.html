Starting a Small Business: Key Considerations

Factors to Consider

1. Capital

Entrepreneurs have to invest a certain amount of personal money for starting their business. They should know the various sources of capital available to them.

2. Business Opportunity

An entrepreneur should not start a business similar to existing ones without determining whether the market can accommodate all of them.

3. Entrepreneurial Skills and Knowledge

An entrepreneur should know their competencies, attitudes, and skills that will benefit the business.

4. Competitors

A person wishing to start a small business should know their competitors and the quality of products, so that they can make their products even better.

5. Legal Requirements

It's important for an entrepreneur to know the legal requirements of starting a business because these may prohibit or restrict consumption of certain commodities.

6. Business Premises

The location of a business is a key factor to consider. Important considerations include:

  • Transport facilities
  • Availability of energy power
  • Nearness to raw materials
  • Expansion ability in future
  • Availability of auxiliary services like banking

Procedures for Starting a Small Business

  1. Identification of a business idea
  2. Development of a business plan
  3. Location of business demand evaluation
  4. Registration of the business
  5. Choice of the business organization
  6. Business name selection
  7. Obtaining trading licenses/permits
  8. Start-up and management of the business

Important Note

All entrepreneurs are business people – though not all business people are entrepreneurs. Entrepreneurs tend to be more innovative than ordinary business people and end up developing business plans.
