Myths Associated with Entrepreneurship

Common Misconceptions

1. Entrepreneurs don't have a personal life

Most people think that entrepreneurs work throughout and this means that you will not have time for your family, friends and leisure activities.

Since entrepreneurs are their own bosses, they can schedule their own hours and stick to that time because a good entrepreneur manages his time well.

2. Entrepreneurs take lots of risks

Entrepreneurs take some risks and they are not gamblers, they take calculated risks.

3. Entrepreneurs are only motivated by money

Financial gain is not the only motivation to small business owners but achieving a lifelong dream is the main motivation for entrepreneurs.

4. Entrepreneurs raise money from venture capitalists

This is not true because not all entrepreneurs raise money from venture capitalists but instead personal loans, friends and family are other sources that outweigh venture capitalist.

5. Entrepreneurs have great ideas

This makes some people not attempt to start their own business because they do not have a unique idea.

You don't need a new idea to be an entrepreneur because you can use an existing idea to start a business.

6. Anyone can be an entrepreneur

This is not true because not everyone has the personality or resources to do this either.

7. Entrepreneurs have formal training and education

One does not need to study business or entrepreneurship to have a successful startup company. Though education helps, it's not completely necessary.

8. Entrepreneurs are young

This is not true because entrepreneurship has no limited age.

9. Entrepreneurs don't quit until they succeed

It's not easy to start and run your own business. Therefore entrepreneurs should accept failure as a potential reality.

10. Entrepreneurship is genetic

No one is born an entrepreneur.
