Public Limited Companies and Stock Exchange

Public Limited Companies

Characteristics

  • Minimum of 7 shareholders with no set maximum
  • Cannot start business before certificate of trading is issued
  • Managed by a board of directors
  • Shares and debentures are freely transferable
  • Advertises shares to the public
  • Must publish year-end accounts and balance sheets
  • Must have authorized minimum share capital
  • Has separate legal entity
  • Limited liability protection
  • Perpetual existence

Advantages

  • Wide range of capital sources through shares and debentures
  • Limited liability protection for shareholders
  • Access to specialized management expertise
  • Wide choice of business opportunities
  • Free transferability of shares
  • Perpetual continuity
  • Benefits from economies of scale
  • Employee motivation through share ownership
  • Shared risk among many shareholders
  • Safeguards against fraud through public accounts

Disadvantages

  • High formation costs
  • Many legal restrictions
  • Shareholders alienated from management
  • Lack of business secrecy
  • Potential conflicts of interest
  • Slow decision-making process
  • May suffer from diseconomies of scale
  • Double taxation on profits and dividends
  • Inflexible to market changes

Dissolution of Companies

Companies may be dissolved under the following circumstances:

  • Failure to commence business within one year of registration
  • Insolvency - inability to pay debts
  • Ultra-vires - acting contrary to objectives
  • Amalgamation with other companies
  • Court order following creditor complaints
  • Shareholders' decision in general meeting
  • Accomplishment of purpose or expiry of operation period

Stock Exchange Market

Key Definitions

Stock
A group of shares in a public limited company, formed when all authorized shares are issued and fully paid
Stock Exchange Market
A market where stocks from quoted companies are bought and sold
Quoted Company
A company registered as a member of the stock exchange market
Securities
Refers to shares or documents supporting share ownership
Initial Public Offer (IPO)
When a company floats new shares for public subscription
Secondary Market
Market dealing in second-hand shares transfer between parties

Roles of Stock Exchange

  • Facilitates buying of shares - provides conducive environment for investors
  • Facilitates selling of shares - creates market for share sales
  • Safeguards investors' interests through monitoring company performance
  • Provides timely, accurate and reliable market information
