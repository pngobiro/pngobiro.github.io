Question 1 ( marks)

(a) (i) Explain the term "ergonomics" as used in ICT.

(ii) Mr. Mop wishes to buy a computer to be used by his physically challenged son in vision.

Describe two features to be considered in modification for the computer to be suitable for his son.

(b) (i) Describe two types of bus found in a computer system

(ii) List two advantages of using laser technology in DVDs.

(c) Differentiate between primary and secondary storage found in a computer memory,

(d) (i) Distinguish between a leased line and dial-up line;

(ii) Outline two advantages of using leasing line for internet connection.

Answer

(a) (i) Ergonomics in ICT: Ergonomics, in the context of ICT (Information and Communication Technology), is the study of designing and arranging workplaces, products, and systems so that they fit the people who use them. It focuses on improving efficiency, productivity, comfort, and safety by considering the physical and cognitive capabilities and limitations of users.

(a) (ii) Features for Mr. Mop's son's computer:

  • Screen Magnification Software: This software enlarges the content displayed on the screen, making it easier for individuals with low vision to see. The magnification level can be adjusted to suit the user's needs.
  • Screen Reader Software: This software converts text displayed on the screen into speech, allowing the user to hear the content instead of reading it. This is particularly useful for individuals who are blind or have severe visual impairments.

(b) (i) Types of bus in a computer system:

  • Data Bus: This bus carries data between the CPU, memory, and peripheral devices. The width of the data bus (e.g., 32-bit or 64-bit) determines the amount of data that can be transferred at once.
  • Address Bus: This bus carries the memory addresses that the CPU wants to access. The width of the address bus determines the amount of memory that the CPU can address.

(b) (ii) Advantages of laser technology in DVDs:

  • Higher Storage Capacity: Laser technology allows for smaller pits and lands on the DVD surface, which means more data can be stored on a DVD compared to a CD.
  • Improved Data Density: The smaller laser beam used in DVDs enables higher data density, resulting in more information being packed into the same physical space.

(c) Primary vs. Secondary Storage:

  • Primary Storage (Main Memory): This is the computer's main memory, such as RAM (Random Access Memory). It is volatile, meaning data is lost when the power is turned off. It provides fast access to data that the CPU is actively using.
  • Secondary Storage: This is non-volatile storage, such as hard drives, SSDs (Solid State Drives), and USB flash drives. It provides long-term storage for data and programs, even when the power is turned off. Access to data in secondary storage is slower than in primary storage.

(d) (i) Leased Line vs. Dial-up Line:

  • Leased Line: A leased line is a dedicated, fixed-bandwidth connection between two points. It provides a constant, reliable connection and is typically used by businesses that require high-speed internet access.
  • Dial-up Line: A dial-up line uses a modem to connect to the internet over a telephone line. It is a slower, less reliable connection compared to a leased line.

(d) (ii) Advantages of using leasing line for internet connection:

  • Reliability: Leased lines provide a dedicated connection, ensuring consistent and reliable internet access.
  • High Bandwidth: Leased lines offer higher bandwidth compared to dial-up or other shared connections, allowing for faster data transfer speeds.

Question 2 ( marks)

(a) Explain the following terms as used in internet technology:

(i) URL;

(ii) HTML.

(b) (i) Explain two advantages of Optical Mark Readers as compared to Optical Character Readers.

(ii) Describe two disadvantages of OMR as compared to OCR.

(c) An airline intends to introduce a database management system to allow for on-line ticket booking:

(i) State two advantages of using DBMS.

(ii) List two limitations of the above system.

(d) Table 1 shows the daily collection from sales in Kolen Keegan Supermarket using a word processor.

Table 1

Item description Quantity (kg) Unit cost (Kshs) Total cost (Kshs)
Sugar 5 100
Maize meal 4 100
Milk 4 50
Daily total costs

(1) Using the given data write a formula that would calculate:

(i) Total cost of each commodity;

(ii) The daily total cost for all the items.

(II) Describe the most effective method you would use to present the data for maize meal.

Answer

(a) (i) URL: A URL (Uniform Resource Locator) is a web address that specifies the location of a resource on the internet. It is a standardized naming convention for addressing documents accessible over the Internet.

(a) (ii) HTML: HTML (HyperText Markup Language) is the standard markup language for creating web pages. It provides the structure and content of a web page, using tags to define elements such as headings, paragraphs, lists, and links.

(b) (i) Advantages of OMR over OCR:

  • Speed: OMR (Optical Mark Recognition) is generally faster than OCR (Optical Character Recognition) because it only needs to detect the presence or absence of a mark, rather than recognizing complex characters.
  • Accuracy: OMR is more accurate than OCR for reading marked data because it is less susceptible to errors caused by variations in handwriting or print quality.

(b) (ii) Disadvantages of OMR compared to OCR:

  • Limited Data Input: OMR can only read pre-defined marks, limiting the type of data that can be input. OCR can read a wider range of characters and text.
  • Inflexibility: OMR is less flexible than OCR because it requires specific forms with marked areas. OCR can read text from various sources, including scanned documents and images.

(c) (i) Advantages of using DBMS for online ticket booking:

  • Data Integrity: A DBMS (Database Management System) ensures data integrity by enforcing constraints and rules, preventing inconsistent or invalid data from being stored.
  • Data Security: A DBMS provides security features such as user authentication and access control, protecting sensitive data from unauthorized access.

(c) (ii) Limitations of the above system:

  • Cost: Implementing and maintaining a DBMS can be expensive, requiring specialized hardware, software, and personnel.
  • Complexity: A DBMS can be complex to design and administer, requiring expertise in database management and system administration.

(d) (1) (i) Total cost of each commodity:

The formula to calculate the total cost of each commodity is: Quantity (kg) * Unit cost (Kshs)

In a word processor table, you would enter the following formulas:

  • Sugar: =B2*C2
  • Maize meal: =B3*C3
  • Milk: =B4*C4

(d) (1) (ii) The daily total cost for all the items:

The formula to calculate the daily total cost for all items is the sum of the total cost of each commodity.

In a word processor table, you would enter the following formula:

=SUM(D2:D4)

(d) (II) Most effective method to present the data for maize meal:

The most effective method to present the data for maize meal would be a pie chart or a bar graph showing the quantity of maize meal sold compared to other commodities. This would visually highlight the contribution of maize meal to the total sales.

Question 3 ( marks)

(a) Define the following terms:

(i) Application software.

(ii) System software.

(b) Explain the following text editors

(i) Line editors.

(ii) Screen editors.

(c) (i) Distinguish between object linking and object embedding as used in DTP.

(ii) Describe three characteristics of Desk Top Publishing.

(d) A teacher intends to create a presentation for his class to teach first year students in impact of 'HIV-Aids'

(i) Design a slide that would be used to introduce the subject;

(ii) Explain the most appropriate techniques he would use to deliver a ten-slide presentation.

Answer

(a) (i) Application software: Application software is a type of computer program designed to perform specific tasks for users. Examples include word processors, spreadsheets, web browsers, and games.

(a) (ii) System software: System software is a type of computer program designed to manage and control the computer's hardware and software resources. Examples include operating systems, device drivers, and utility programs.

(b) (i) Line editors: Line editors are text editors that allow users to edit text one line at a time. They were commonly used in the early days of computing when computer terminals had limited display capabilities.

(b) (ii) Screen editors: Screen editors are text editors that allow users to edit text on the entire screen at once. They provide a more visual and interactive editing experience compared to line editors.

(c) (i) Object linking vs. object embedding in DTP:

  • Object linking: Object linking creates a connection between a document and an external object (e.g., an image or a chart). When the external object is modified, the changes are automatically reflected in the document.
  • Object embedding: Object embedding inserts a copy of an external object into a document. The object becomes part of the document, and changes to the original object are not reflected in the document.

(c) (ii) Three characteristics of Desk Top Publishing:

  • WYSIWYG (What You See Is What You Get): DTP software provides a visual interface that allows users to see exactly how the document will look when printed.
  • Typography Control: DTP software offers advanced typography controls, allowing users to fine-tune the appearance of text.
  • Layout and Design Tools: DTP software provides tools for creating complex layouts and designs, including the ability to manipulate images, text, and graphics.

(d) (i) Slide design for introducing 'HIV-Aids':

The slide should include a title such as "Understanding HIV/AIDS" or "The Impact of HIV/AIDS". It should also include a brief overview of the topic and a visually appealing image or graphic related to HIV/AIDS awareness.

(d) (ii) Techniques for delivering a ten-slide presentation:

  • Use Visual Aids: Incorporate images, charts, and graphs to illustrate key points and keep the audience engaged.
  • Keep Text Concise: Use bullet points and short sentences to convey information clearly and avoid overwhelming the audience with text.
  • Practice and Rehearse: Practice the presentation thoroughly to ensure a smooth delivery and maintain a confident demeanor.

Question 4 ( marks)

(a) (i) Distinguish between physical and logical backups.

(ii) (I) Outline two advantages of physical backups.

(II) Suggest two limitations of physical backups.

(b) Juma works as a technician with Bidco Industries. The company wishes to introduce multi media computer system managing their operations.

Describe three components that the system is likely to have.

(c) Thomas Owen discovered that his hard disk had low access speed.

Suggest three techniques that he would use to improve the system performance.

(d) Distinguish between a single and multi-user operating system.

Answer

(a) (i) Physical vs. Logical Backups:

  • Physical Backup: A physical backup involves copying the entire contents of a storage device (e.g., a hard drive) to another storage device. It creates an exact replica of the original data, including the operating system, applications, and data files.
  • Logical Backup: A logical backup involves copying specific files and folders that are deemed important. It allows users to select which data to back up, providing more flexibility and control.

(a) (ii) (I) Advantages of physical backups:

  • Complete Recovery: Physical backups allow for a complete system recovery in case of a disaster, as they include all data and system files.
  • Simplicity: Physical backups are relatively simple to perform, as they involve copying the entire contents of a storage device.

(a) (ii) (II) Limitations of physical backups:

  • Large Storage Space: Physical backups require a large amount of storage space, as they involve copying the entire contents of a storage device.
  • Time-Consuming: Physical backups can be time-consuming, especially for large storage devices.

(b) Components of a multimedia computer system for Bidco Industries:

  • High-Performance Processor: A fast processor is needed to handle the processing of multimedia content, such as audio and video.
  • Large Amount of RAM: A large amount of RAM (Random Access Memory) is needed to store and process multimedia data efficiently.
  • Graphics Card: A dedicated graphics card is needed to handle the display of high-resolution images and videos.

(c) Techniques to improve hard disk access speed:

  • Defragmentation: Defragmenting the hard disk can improve access speed by reorganizing files and reducing fragmentation.
  • Disk Cleanup: Removing unnecessary files and programs can free up disk space and improve access speed.
  • Upgrade to SSD: Upgrading to a Solid State Drive (SSD) can significantly improve access speed compared to a traditional hard disk drive.

(d) Single vs. Multi-user Operating System:

  • Single-user Operating System: A single-user operating system is designed to be used by only one user at a time. Examples include DOS and early versions of Windows.
  • Multi-user Operating System: A multi-user operating system is designed to be used by multiple users simultaneously. Examples include Linux, Unix, and Windows Server.

Question 6 ( marks)

(a) Identify six factors to consider in choosing the most appropriate channel of communication.

(b) Explain two differences between formal and informal meetings.

(c) State three reasons why effective internal communication should be encouraged in an organization.

(d) Identify four methods of upward communication commonly used in organizations.

(e) State two uses of each of the following in a business letter;

(i) Date

(ii) Reference number

(f) Distinguish between customer service and customer care.

(g) State three benefits of effective communication in an organization.

Answer

(a) (i) Information Privacy: Information privacy refers to the right of individuals to control how their personal information is collected, used, and disclosed.

(a) (ii) Four threats to Information Privacy:

  • Hacking: Unauthorized access to computer systems and networks can lead to the theft of personal information.
  • Phishing: Deceptive emails or websites can trick individuals into revealing their personal information.
  • Malware: Malicious software can be used to collect personal information without the user's knowledge or consent.
  • Data Breaches: Accidental or intentional disclosure of personal information by organizations can compromise individuals' privacy.

(a) (iii) Measures to minimize the threats in a(ii):

  • Strong Passwords: Using strong, unique passwords can help prevent unauthorized access to accounts and systems.
  • Antivirus Software: Installing and regularly updating antivirus software can help protect against malware.
  • Firewalls: Using firewalls can help prevent unauthorized access to computer systems and networks.
  • Data Encryption: Encrypting sensitive data can help protect it from unauthorized access, even if it is stolen.

(b) Many-to-many relationship in DBMS: A many-to-many relationship occurs when multiple records in one table are related to multiple records in another table. For example, a student can enroll in many courses, and a course can have many students. This relationship is typically implemented using a junction table.

(c) (i) Negative effects of IT on the physical environment:

  • Electronic Waste: The disposal of electronic devices (e-waste) can lead to pollution and environmental damage.
  • Energy Consumption: The production and use of IT equipment consume significant amounts of energy, contributing to greenhouse gas emissions.

(c) (ii) Positive effects of IT on the physical environment:

  • Reduced Paper Consumption: IT enables paperless communication and storage, reducing the need for paper and saving trees.
  • Remote Work: IT facilitates remote work, reducing the need for commuting and decreasing traffic congestion and air pollution.

Question 7 ( marks)

(a) State five uses of internet on business.

(b) The management of the organization you work for intends to open a new branch. As the communications Manager, write an appropriate letter to your customer to inform them of the new development.

Answer

(a) Identify six factors to consider in choosing the most appropriate channel of communication:

  • Audience: Consider the characteristics and preferences of the intended audience.
  • Message Complexity: Choose a channel that is appropriate for the complexity of the message.
  • Urgency: Select a channel that allows for timely delivery of the message.
  • Cost: Consider the cost associated with each communication channel.
  • Confidentiality: Choose a secure channel for sensitive information.
  • Feedback: Select a channel that allows for feedback and interaction.

(b) Explain two differences between formal and informal meetings:

  • Structure: Formal meetings follow a structured agenda and have established rules of procedure, while informal meetings are more relaxed and unstructured.
  • Purpose: Formal meetings are typically held for decision-making or information dissemination, while informal meetings are often used for brainstorming or team building.

(c) State three reasons why effective internal communication should be encouraged in an organization:

  • Improved Employee Morale: Effective internal communication can improve employee morale by fostering a sense of belonging and keeping employees informed.
  • Increased Productivity: Effective internal communication can increase productivity by ensuring that employees have the information they need to perform their jobs effectively.
  • Better Decision-Making: Effective internal communication can lead to better decision-making by providing managers with access to a wider range of perspectives and information.

(d) Identify four methods of upward communication commonly used in organizations:

  • Suggestion Boxes: Employees can submit suggestions and feedback anonymously through suggestion boxes.
  • Employee Surveys: Organizations can conduct employee surveys to gather feedback on various aspects of the workplace.
  • Open-Door Policy: Managers can adopt an open-door policy, encouraging employees to approach them with questions or concerns.
  • Town Hall Meetings: Organizations can hold town hall meetings to provide employees with an opportunity to ask questions and share feedback with senior management.

(e) (i) Date in a business letter:

  • Indicates when the letter was written: The date provides a reference point for when the letter was sent.
  • Helps with record-keeping: The date helps with organizing and tracking correspondence.

(e) (ii) Reference number in a business letter:

  • Identifies the specific letter: The reference number provides a unique identifier for the letter.
  • Facilitates tracking and retrieval: The reference number makes it easier to track and retrieve the letter from filing systems.

(f) Customer service vs. customer care:

  • Customer service: Customer service is the assistance and advice provided by a company to those people who buy or use its products or services.
  • Customer care: Customer care is the proactive actions taken by a company to ensure customer satisfaction and build long-term relationships.

(g) Three benefits of effective communication in an organization:

  • Improved Coordination: Effective communication improves coordination between different departments and teams.
  • Enhanced Collaboration: Effective communication enhances collaboration and teamwork.
  • Increased Customer Satisfaction: Effective communication increases customer satisfaction by providing clear and timely information.
