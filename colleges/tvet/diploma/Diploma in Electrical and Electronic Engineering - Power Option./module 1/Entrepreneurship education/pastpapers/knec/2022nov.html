SECTION A: INFORMATION COMMUNICATION TECHNOLOGY (40 marks)

Answer any FOUR questions from this section.

Question 1

(a) Explain two advantages of using flashdisks for storing data.

(b) Describe four functions of Information Communication Technology (ICT) in a manufacturing organization.

Answer

(a)

Two advantages of using flashdisks for storing data:

  • Portability - Small size makes them easy to carry and transport
  • Durability - No moving parts making them resistant to physical damage

(b)

Four functions of ICT in a manufacturing organization:

  • Inventory management and tracking
  • Production planning and scheduling
  • Quality control and monitoring
  • Supply chain management and coordination

Question 2

(a) Distinguish between system software and application software.

(b) Describe three physical threats to an organization's data.

Answer

(a)

System software manages and controls computer hardware resources and provides platform for running application programs, while application software performs specific tasks and functions for users.

(b)

Three physical threats to an organization's data:

  • Fire damage - Can destroy storage devices and hardware containing data
  • Water damage - From floods or plumbing issues can damage storage media
  • Theft of hardware - Physical theft of devices containing sensitive data

Question 3

(a) Describe two ways of connecting to the internet.

(b) A student is in the process of creating a table using a word processing application. Explain the circumstance under which the student may use each of the following features:

(i) text direction;

(ii) merge cells;

(iii) shading.

Answer

(a)

Two ways of connecting to the internet:

  • Wired connection - Using Ethernet cables to connect directly to a router or modem
  • Wireless connection - Using Wi-Fi to connect to a wireless network

(b)

(i) Text direction: Used when creating tables with vertical headers or when working with languages that read from right to left

(ii) Merge cells: Used when creating headings that span multiple columns or when combining cells to create a larger cell for content

(iii) Shading: Used to highlight specific cells or rows for emphasis or to improve readability of the table

Question 6 (Compulsory)

(a) Outline four characteristics of effective communication.

(b) Describe three psychological barriers to effective communication.

(c) You are a supervisor at XWZ company, you have been tasked to address a group of striking workers. Explain two challenges that you are likely to face while using this form of communication.

(d) A fire has destroyed a section of the workshop where you are in charge. Assuming investigations are complete, outline the items to be included in the structure of the report for this accident.

Answer

(a)

Four characteristics of effective communication:

  • Clarity - Message is clear and easily understood
  • Completeness - All necessary information is provided
  • Conciseness - Message is brief but comprehensive
  • Timeliness - Information is delivered at the appropriate time

(b)

Three psychological barriers to effective communication:

  • Emotional state - Strong emotions can interfere with message interpretation
  • Preconceptions and bias - Existing beliefs affecting message reception
  • Attention span - Limited ability to focus on lengthy messages

(c)

Two challenges when addressing striking workers:

  • Hostile audience - Workers may be unreceptive or antagonistic
  • Emotional atmosphere - High tensions can make rational communication difficult

(d)

Structure of accident report should include:

  1. Title and date of incident
  2. Executive summary
  3. Description of incident
  4. Cause analysis
  5. Extent of damage
  6. Actions taken
  7. Recommendations
  8. Supporting documents (photos, witness statements)

Question 7

(a) Highlight four challenges that a supervisor may experience while conducting a meeting in an organization.

(b) Describe each of the following types of interviews:

(i) Panel interview;

(ii) Group interview;

(iii) Informal interview.

Answer

(a)

Four challenges in conducting a meeting:

  • Time management - Keeping discussions within allocated time
  • Participant engagement - Maintaining interest and participation
  • Conflict management - Handling disagreements constructively
  • Focus deviation - Keeping discussions on track and relevant

(b)

(i) Panel interview: Multiple interviewers question a single candidate simultaneously, each assessing different aspects of the candidate's suitability.

(ii) Group interview: Multiple candidates are interviewed together, often used to assess interpersonal skills and group dynamics.

(iii) Informal interview: Casual, unstructured conversation used to get to know a candidate in a more relaxed setting.

Question 8

(a) List four categories of organizational customers.

(b) Explain three etiquette procedures that should be observed when participating in a group discussion.

Answer

(a)

Four categories of organizational customers:

  • Internal customers (employees, departments)
  • Business-to-business (B2B) customers
  • Government and public institutions
  • Non-profit organizations

(b)

Three etiquette procedures for group discussion:

  • Active listening - Pay attention to others and avoid interrupting
  • Turn-taking - Wait for your turn to speak and respect speaking order
  • Constructive contribution - Keep comments relevant and respectful

SECTION C: ENTREPRENEURSHIP EDUCATION (30 marks)

Answer any THREE questions from this section.

Question 9

(a) Outline four elements contained in the operation and production section of a business plan.

(b) Explain three ways in which Information Communication Technologies (ICTs) support the marketing activities of a small business.

Answer

(a)

Four elements in operation and production section:

  • Production process description
  • Equipment and machinery requirements
  • Quality control procedures
  • Production capacity and scheduling

(b)

Three ways ICTs support marketing activities:

  • Social media marketing - Reaching customers through platforms like Facebook and Instagram
  • Email marketing - Direct communication with customers through newsletters and promotions
  • Website presence - Online showcase of products and services

Question 10

(a) Explain the ways in which each of the following cultural factors hinder the growth of entrepreneurship in a country:

(i) Religion;

(ii) Upbringing practices.

(b) Explain three roles performed by an entrepreneur in a business.

Answer

(a)

(i) Religion may hinder entrepreneurship through:

  • Restrictions on certain business activities
  • Conservative attitudes towards profit-making

(ii) Upbringing practices may hinder entrepreneurship through:

  • Discouraging risk-taking behavior
  • Emphasizing traditional career paths over entrepreneurship

(b)

Three roles of an entrepreneur:

  • Risk-taker - Making business decisions under uncertainty
  • Innovator - Introducing new products or processes
  • Resource manager - Coordinating various business resources

Question 11

(a) Explain three ways through which an entrepreneur can evaluate a business opportunity.

(b) Outline four challenges of managing a partnership business.

Answer

(a)

Three ways to evaluate a business opportunity:

  • Market research - Analyzing demand and competition
  • Financial analysis - Assessing costs, revenue potential, and profitability
  • Feasibility study - Evaluating technical and operational viability

(b)

Four challenges of managing a partnership business:

  • Decision-making conflicts between partners
  • Profit sharing disagreements
  • Unequal commitment and effort from partners
  • Shared liability for business debts

Question 12

(a) Explain three ways through which an entrepreneur may motivate employees in a business.

(b) Outline four reasons that make enterprises social responsibility (ESR) useful to a small business.

Answer

(a)

Three ways to motivate employees:

  • Performance-based rewards and recognition
  • Professional development opportunities
  • Positive work environment and culture

(b)

Four reasons ESR is useful to small business:

  • Enhanced community relations and support
  • Improved brand image and reputation
  • Increased customer loyalty
  • Better employee satisfaction and retention
