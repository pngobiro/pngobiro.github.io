Question 1 ( marks)

(a) Explain the way in which each of the following categories of input devices enter data in a computer:

(i) pointing;

(ii) scanning.

(b) Explain the function of each of the following features in spreadsheet applications:

(i) wrap text;

(ii) merge cells;

(iii) sort.

Answer

(a)

(i) Pointing devices: These devices allow users to interact with the computer by pointing at objects on the screen. They translate physical movements into corresponding actions within the digital environment.

(ii) Scanning devices: These devices capture data from physical documents or objects and convert it into a digital format that a computer can understand.

(b)

(i) Wrap text: This feature automatically adjusts the text within a cell to fit its width by wrapping it onto multiple lines.

(ii) Merge cells: This feature combines two or more adjacent cells into a single, larger cell.

(iii) Sort: This feature arranges data in a specific order, such as ascending or descending, based on the values in one or more columns.

Question 2 ( marks)

(a) Outline four features of Wide Area Networks (WANs).

(b) Explain the circumstance under which each of the following database objects may be used:

(i) report;

(ii) query;

(iii) form.

Answer

(a)

Four features of Wide Area Networks (WANs):

  • Cover large geographical areas
  • Use various communication channels
  • Connect multiple smaller networks
  • Provide remote access to resources

(b)

(i) Report: Used to present data in a structured and formatted manner for analysis and decision-making.

(ii) Query: Used to retrieve specific data from a database based on defined criteria.

(iii) Form: Used to provide a user-friendly interface for entering, modifying, and viewing data in a database.

Question 6 ( marks)

(a) Outline three indicators of a poorly written essay.

(b) State two ways in which a receiver may exhibit etiquette during a telephone conversation.

(c) Some managers prefer to use written communication when conveying information to employees. State three reasons that may account for this preference.

(d) Distinguish between lateral communication and diagonal communication.

(e) Highlight three benefits that an engineering firm may derive from effective interpersonal communication.

(f) Outline three ways in which information communication technology (ICT) may promote effective business communication.

(g) State two strategies that may be adopted in an organization to enhance the loyalty of a new customer.

Answer

(a)

Three indicators of a poorly written essay:

  1. Lack of clarity
  2. Poor organization
  3. Grammatical errors

(b)

Two ways in which a receiver may exhibit etiquette during a telephone conversation:

  • Answering promptly
  • Speaking clearly

(c)

Some managers prefer to use written communication when conveying information to employees. State three reasons that may account for this preference:

  1. Provides a record
  2. Ensures consistency
  3. Allows for review

(d)

Lateral communication: This is communication between individuals or departments at the same level within an organization.

Diagonal communication: This is communication between individuals or departments at different levels and in different departments within an organization.

(e)

Highlight three benefits that an engineering firm may derive from effective interpersonal communication:

  • Improved teamwork
  • Enhanced problem-solving
  • Increased productivity

(f)

Outline three ways in which information communication technology (ICT) may promote effective business communication:

  • Faster communication
  • Wider reach
  • Reduced costs

(g)

State two strategies that may be adopted in an organization to enhance the loyalty of a new customer:

  • Provide excellent customer service
  • Offer loyalty programs

Question 9 ( marks)

(a) Outline four functions served by the business description section of a business plan.

(b) Distinguish between limited liability and unlimited liability as used in business ownership.

(c) State two ways in which an entrepreneur may benefit from agency banking services.

Answer

(a)

Four functions served by the business description section of a business plan:

  • Provides an overview of the business
  • Describes the products or services offered
  • Identifies the target market
  • Outlines the competitive advantages

(b)

Limited liability: The business owner's personal assets are protected from business debts.

Unlimited liability: The business owner is personally liable for all business debts.

(c)

Two ways in which an entrepreneur may benefit from agency banking services:

  • Increased access to banking services
  • Reduced transaction costs

Question 12 ( marks)

(a) Explain two purposes served by carrying out a pre-feasibility study of a business opportunity.

(i) suppliers;

(ii) employees.

Answer

(a)

Two purposes served by carrying out a pre-feasibility study of a business opportunity:

  1. To assess the viability of the business idea
  2. To identify potential risks and challenges

(b)

(i) Suppliers:

  • Pay them on time
  • Treat them fairly
  • Establish long-term relationships

(ii) Employees:

  • Provide a safe working environment
  • Offer fair wages and benefits
  • Promote employee development
