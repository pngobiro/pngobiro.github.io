  • This paper consists of EIGHT questions in THREE sections; $A, B$ and $C$.
  • Answer FOUR questions from section A, question SIX (Compulsory) and ONE other question from section $\boldsymbol{B}$ and THREE questions from section $\boldsymbol{C}$ in the answer booklet provided.
  • Maximum marks for each part of a question are as indicated.
  • Candidates should answer the questions in English.

SECTION A: ICT (40 marks)

Answer FOUR questions from this section.

1. (a) Describe each of the following types of computers:

(i) multi-user computer system;

(ii) workstation;

(iii) laptop.

(b) List four secondary storage media of a computer system.

(c) State two reasons that make some employees to lose their jobs when their employer installs a computerized system.

Answers:

(a)

(i) Multi-user computer system - Allows more than one user at a time

(ii) Workstation - Used in research and industries, investigation facility

(iii) Laptop - Micro-computer designed to be used on the lap

(b) Secondary storage media:

  • Hard disk drives
  • USB flash drives
  • CD/DVD
  • External hard drives

(c) Reasons for job loss:

  • Automation of manual tasks
  • Reduced need for human labor

2. (a) Explain each of the following types of computer software:

(i) system software;

(ii) application software;

(iii) utility program.

(b) State four factors that should be considered when selecting an operating system for a personal computer.

Answers:

(a)

(i) System software - Programs that control and manage the computer hardware and provide platform for running application software

(ii) Application software - Programs designed to perform specific tasks for users

(iii) Utility program - Software tools that help maintain, analyze, configure, optimize or maintain a computer

(b) Factors to consider when selecting an OS:

  • Hardware compatibility
  • Software compatibility
  • User interface
  • Cost

3. (a) State four threats to information stored in a computer that may arise due to connectivity to the internet.

(b) Explain three benefits of using electronic mail instead of ordinary paper mail to send a job application letter.

Answers:

(a) Internet threats:

  • Viruses and malware
  • Hacking attempts
  • Data theft
  • Phishing attacks

(b) Benefits of electronic mail:

  • Instant delivery
  • Cost-effective (no printing or postage costs)
  • Ability to track receipt and reading status

4. (a) State the word processing formula that should be used in a table to compute each of the values:

(i) total in a column;

(ii) total in a row;

(iii) mean score in a row of scores.

(b) State three formatting features that may be applied to a numeric entry in a cell of a spreadsheet.

(c) Distinguish between relative cell references and absolute cell references as used in spreadsheets.

Answers:

(a) Word processing formulas:

(i) =SUM(A1:B2) - for column total

(ii) =SUM(A1,B1) - for row total

(iii) =AVERAGE(A1:D1) - for mean score

(b) Formatting features for numeric entries:

  • Number format (decimal places)
  • Currency symbol
  • Thousand separator

(c) Cell references:

Relative: Changes when copied (e.g., A1)

Absolute: Stays fixed when copied (e.g., $A$1)

5. (a) Outline the steps that should be followed when creating a database.

(b) List four views in a presentation program.

(c) State three formatting features that may be applied to a graphic created using a desktop publishing program.

Answers:

(a) Steps in creating a database:

  1. Determine the purpose
  2. Identify required tables
  3. Define fields for each table
  4. Define relationships between tables
  5. Create forms and reports

(b) Presentation program views:

  • Normal view
  • Slide sorter view
  • Slide show view
  • Notes view

(c) Graphic formatting features:

  • Resizing
  • Rotation
  • Color adjustment

SECTION B: COMMUNICATION SKILLS (30 marks)

Answer question SIX (compulsory) and ONE other question from this section.

6. (a) Business organisations are increasingly using electronic communication. Explain three reasons that may account for this increase.

(b) It has been observed that majority of the employees of Peace Limited spend a lot of their time speaking on their cell phones. As the foreman, write a memorandum to the employees requesting them to stop this practice.

(c) (i) Explain the phrase "Annual General Meeting (AGM)".

(c) (ii) Outline four items of agenda that may be discussed in an annual general meeting of a publicly listed company.

Answers:

(a) Reasons for increased electronic communication:

  • Speed and efficiency
  • Cost effectiveness
  • Wide reach and accessibility

(b) Memorandum:

INTERNAL MEMO

TO: All Employees
FROM: The Foreman
DATE: [Current Date]
SUBJECT: Excessive Cell Phone Usage During Work Hours

It has been observed that many employees are spending excessive time on personal phone calls during work hours. This practice is affecting productivity and must stop immediately.

Please limit personal calls to break times only. Emergency calls should be brief and taken away from the work area.

Your cooperation in this matter is appreciated.

[Signature]
The Foreman

(c) (i) Annual General Meeting (AGM):

A mandatory yearly gathering of a company's shareholders and directors where they review financial performance, make important decisions, and discuss company matters.

(c) (ii) AGM agenda items:

  • Presentation of annual financial statements
  • Election/re-election of directors
  • Appointment of auditors
  • Declaration of dividends

7. (a) State four guidelines that should be followed when using tables to provide information in a report.

(b) Explain three measures that the management of an organisation may take to encourage upward communication.

Answers:

(a) Guidelines for tables in reports:

  • Clear table titles and headings
  • Consistent formatting
  • Appropriate spacing and alignment
  • Logical arrangement of data

(b) Measures to encourage upward communication:

  • Implement suggestion boxes
  • Regular feedback meetings
  • Open door policy

8. (a) Distinguish between a conference and a panel discussion.

(b) (i) Explain the term etiquette as used in communication.

(b) (ii) Outline four ways in which an interviewee may demonstrate etiquette during an interview.

Answers:

(a) Difference between conference and panel discussion:

Conference: Large formal meeting where many participants gather to discuss various topics, often with multiple sessions and speakers.

Panel Discussion: Specific format where selected experts discuss a topic in front of an audience, usually moderated.

(b) (i) Etiquette in communication:

The proper and polite way of communicating and behaving in professional settings.

(b) (ii) Interview etiquette:

  • Appreciating for the opportunity accorded
  • Using respectable and courteous language throughout
  • Excusing oneself before speaking
  • Not interrupting during consultation

SECTION C: ENTREPRENEURSHIP EDUCATION (30 marks)

Answer THREE questions from this section.

9. (a) Despite persuasion from friends, Danton is determined to quit salaried employment for self employment. Explain three reasons that may account for this determination.

(b) Explain the way in which each of the following cultural factors may enhance entrepreneurial development:

(i) time consciousness;

(ii) work ethics.

Answers:

(a) Reasons for choosing self-employment:

  • Desire for independence and autonomy
  • Higher income potential
  • Opportunity to pursue personal passion

(b) Cultural factors:

(i) Time consciousness:

  • Helps in meeting deadlines
  • Improves customer satisfaction
  • Enhances business reputation

(ii) Work ethics:

  • Builds trust with stakeholders
  • Ensures quality service delivery
  • Promotes business sustainability

10. (a) Outline four guidelines that the management of an enterprise should follow to implement effective social responsibility.

(b) Explain three factors that may complicate the management of a family business.

Answers:

(a) Social responsibility guidelines:

  • Identify community needs
  • Allocate adequate resources
  • Involve stakeholders in decision making
  • Monitor and evaluate impact

(b) Family business complications:

  • Mixing family and business relationships
  • Succession planning challenges
  • Emotional decision making

11. (a) Distinguish between social entrepreneur and a political entrepreneur.

(b) Outline six activities that may be included in the induction programme for new employees in a manufacturing firm.

Answers:

(a) Entrepreneur types:

Social entrepreneur: Focuses on creating solutions for social problems and community development

Political entrepreneur: Uses political influence and connections for business advantage

(b) Induction program activities:

  • Company overview and policies
  • Safety procedures training
  • Department tour
  • Introduction to team members
  • Job-specific training
  • HR documentation completion

12. (a) Outline four limitations of using a focus group discussion to generate a business idea.

(b) Jacob is in the process of writing a business plan. Outline six types of information that he should include in the business description section of the plan.

Answers:

(a) Focus group limitations:

  • Limited representation of target market
  • Potential for groupthink
  • Cost and time intensive
  • Difficulty in managing group dynamics

(b) Business plan description information:

  • Company name and location
  • Business structure and ownership
  • Products or services offered
  • Target market description
  • Business objectives and goals
  • Competitive advantage
