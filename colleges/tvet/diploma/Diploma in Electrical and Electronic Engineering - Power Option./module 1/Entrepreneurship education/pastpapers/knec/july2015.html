SECTION A: INFORMATION COMMUNICATION TECHNOLOGY

Answer FOUR questions in this section.

Question 1

(a) Explain the function of the following devices on a computer:

(i) keyboard;

(ii) monitor.

(b) Outline two characteristics of magnetic disks.

(c) With the aid of a sketch explain the Universal Serial Bus (USB) port.

(d) Distinguish between the following utility software programs:

(i) linker;

(ii) loader

Answer

(a)

(i) Keyboard: An input device that allows users to enter text and commands into the computer.

(ii) Monitor: An output device that displays visual information, such as text, images, and videos, to the user.

(b) Two characteristics of magnetic disks:

  • They are non-volatile, meaning they retain data even when the power is off.
  • They allow for direct access to data, meaning data can be accessed in any order.

(c) Universal Serial Bus (USB) port:

A USB port is a standard interface for connecting peripheral devices to a computer. It allows for data transfer and power supply. A sketch would show a rectangular port with pins inside.

(d)

(i) Linker: A program that combines object code files into a single executable file.

(ii) Loader: A program that loads an executable file into memory and starts its execution.

Question 2

(a) Explain the following computer terms as used in computer crime:

(i) hacking;

(ii) piracy.

(b) Jaribu Supermarkets have networked their computers in their chain of business enterprises. Explain three characteristics these computers are likely to exhibit if they are infected with a virus.

(c) Essolom Enterprises specialise in reorganisation and restructuring of their clients' filing systems. Advise them on the suitability of the following file organisation methods:

(i) sequential;

(ii) random.

(d) Describe the following in Database Management Systems:

(i) query;

(ii) table.

Answer

(a)

(i) Hacking: Unauthorized access to or control over computer systems or networks.

(ii) Piracy: The illegal copying, distribution, or use of software or other copyrighted materials.

(b) Characteristics of virus-infected computers in Jaribu Supermarkets:

  • Slow performance: The computers may run slower than usual.
  • Data loss or corruption: Files may be deleted or become corrupted.
  • Unexpected errors: The computers may display unusual error messages or crash frequently.

(c)

(i) Sequential file organization: Suitable for processing large volumes of data in a specific order, but inefficient for random access.

(ii) Random file organization: Suitable for accessing data quickly in any order, but requires more storage space and is more complex to implement.

(d)

(i) Query: A request for information from a database.

(ii) Table: A collection of related data organized in rows and columns.

Question 3

(a) Differentiate between the following terminologies:

(i) Electronic Funds Transfer (EFT);

(ii) Internet banking.

(b) Explain two advantages of using robots in the industry in this information technology age.

(c) Mount Kenya Institute has been spending a lot of money when responding to regular mails from students making enquiries on their programs. As a consultant, outline three benefits that the Institute could enjoy if it used electronic mails instead of regular mails.

(d) Joel Magana, an ICT specialist has decided to interlink his hospitals with the clinics of City Hall. Identify two suitable devices to enable him carry out the connection.

Answer

(a)

(i) Electronic Funds Transfer (EFT): The electronic transfer of money from one bank account to another.

(ii) Internet banking: A service that allows customers to conduct financial transactions online.

(b) Two advantages of using robots in the industry:

  • Increased efficiency: Robots can perform tasks faster and more accurately than humans.
  • Reduced costs: Robots can work continuously without breaks, reducing labor costs.

(c) Three benefits of using electronic mails instead of regular mails:

  • Faster delivery: Electronic mails are delivered instantly.
  • Reduced costs: Electronic mails eliminate postage costs.
  • Improved communication: Electronic mails allow for easy sharing of information and attachments.

(d) Two suitable devices to enable Joel Magana to interlink his hospitals with the clinics of City Hall:

  • Routers: To direct data traffic between the networks.
  • VPN (Virtual Private Network): To create a secure connection between the networks.

Question 4

(a) Define the term 'topology' as used in computer networking.

(b) KIM traders have linked their businesses using Ring Topology. As a consultant, explain two possible causes of failure of the system.

(c) With the aid of diagrams, explain the differences between Analogue data and Digital data signals.

(d) Describe the following terminologies for data transmission:

(i) address bus;

(ii) data bus;

(iii) control bus.

Answer

(a) Topology: The arrangement of nodes and connections in a computer network.

(b) Two possible causes of failure of the Ring Topology:

  • Cable break: A break in the cable can disrupt the entire network.
  • Node failure: If one node fails, it can prevent data from circulating around the ring.

(c) With the aid of diagrams, explain the differences between Analogue data and Digital data signals:

Analogue data signals are continuous waveforms, while digital data signals are discrete values represented by binary digits (0s and 1s).

(d)

(i) Address bus: A set of wires used to specify the memory location or I/O port being accessed by the CPU.

(ii) Data bus: A set of wires used to transfer data between the CPU, memory, and I/O devices.

(iii) Control bus: A set of wires used to send control signals between the CPU, memory, and I/O devices.

Question 5

(a) Explain the term 'spreadsheet' as used in computer software.

(b) Explain the techniques for copying data from one location to another in a spreadsheet program.

(c) Makini Groceries would like to audit its inventory. The management is in the process of purchasing software packages to do this. As a specialist advise them on three considerations they should make before they embark on any spending.

(d) Distinguish between the following terminologies for computer networks:

(i) multi-user;

(ii) multi-tasking

Answer

(a) Spreadsheet: A computer software program that arranges data in rows and columns, allowing for calculations, analysis, and organization.

(b) Techniques for copying data from one location to another in a spreadsheet program:

  • Copy and Paste: Select the data, copy it to the clipboard, and then paste it to the desired location.
  • Drag and Drop: Select the data and drag it to the new location.
  • Fill Handle: Use the fill handle to copy data to adjacent cells.

(c) Three considerations Makini Groceries should make before purchasing software packages:

  • Compatibility: Ensure the software is compatible with their existing systems.
  • Features: Choose software that meets their specific needs for inventory auditing.
  • Cost: Consider the total cost of ownership, including licensing, training, and maintenance.

(d)

(i) Multi-user: A system that allows multiple users to access and use the computer resources simultaneously.

(ii) Multi-tasking: A system that allows a single user to perform multiple tasks concurrently.

SECTION B: COMMUNICATION SKILLS

Answer question 6 and ONE other question in this section.

Question 6

(a) Outline three components of a communication situation.

(b) State two consequences of sending incomplete information to a recipient.

(c) State three benefits of effective internal communication in an organisation.

(d) Explain two ways in which the arrangement of a room may positively influence communication during a meeting.

(e) State two limitations of not having an organisation chart in an organisation.

(f) Outline three principles that may be followed in designing effective notices.

(g) The management of Imani Engineering Works Limited conducts appraisal interviews annually. Explain the importance of such interviews for the organisation.

(h) State three essentials of effective communication.

Answer

(a) Three components of a communication situation:

  • Sender: The person or entity initiating the communication.
  • Message: The information being conveyed.
  • Receiver: The person or entity receiving the message.

(b) Two consequences of sending incomplete information to a recipient:

  • Misunderstanding: The recipient may misinterpret the message.
  • Delayed action: The recipient may be unable to take appropriate action.

(c) Three benefits of effective internal communication in an organisation:

  • Improved employee morale: Employees feel valued and informed.
  • Increased productivity: Employees understand their roles and responsibilities.
  • Better decision-making: Management has access to accurate and timely information.

(d) Two ways in which the arrangement of a room may positively influence communication during a meeting:

  • Seating arrangement: A circular seating arrangement can promote open discussion.
  • Lighting and acoustics: Good lighting and acoustics can improve audibility and reduce distractions.

(e) Two limitations of not having an organisation chart in an organisation:

  • Lack of clarity: Employees may not understand their roles and responsibilities.
  • Poor communication: Communication channels may be unclear.

(f) Three principles that may be followed in designing effective notices:

  • Clarity: Use clear and concise language.
  • Visibility: Place notices in prominent locations.
  • Relevance: Ensure notices are relevant to the target audience.

(g) The importance of appraisal interviews for the organisation:

Appraisal interviews provide an opportunity for management to assess employee performance, provide feedback, and identify areas for improvement.

(h) Three essentials of effective communication:

  • Clarity: The message should be clear and easy to understand.
  • Accuracy: The message should be accurate and reliable.
  • Timeliness: The message should be delivered in a timely manner.

Question 8

(a) Explain the meaning of the following types of customers who may visit businesses:

(i) need-based customers;

(ii) loyal customers.

(b) State six situations that may make a manager opt to use oral means of communication.

Answer

(a) Types of customers:

(i) Need-based customers: Customers who visit businesses specifically when they have a particular need or requirement to fulfill.

(ii) Loyal customers: Regular customers who consistently choose to do business with a particular company due to satisfaction with products or services.

(b) Six situations when a manager may opt for oral communication:

  • Urgent matters requiring immediate attention
  • Sensitive or confidential information
  • Complex issues requiring discussion and clarification
  • Team meetings and briefings
  • Providing immediate feedback
  • Building relationships with staff

SECTION C: ENTREPRENEURSHIP EDUCATION

Answer any THREE questions from this section.

Question 9

(a) (i) Explain the meaning of the term 'window of opportunity' in entrepreneurship.

(ii) State four considerations that an entrepreneur should make when evaluating the viability of a business opportunity.

(b) Outline four guidelines that the management of an enterprise may follow in order to be effective in social responsibility activities.

Answer

(a)

(i) Window of opportunity: A period of time during which a unique business opportunity exists and can be exploited for maximum benefit. It represents a favorable set of circumstances that may be limited in duration.

(ii) Four considerations when evaluating business opportunity viability:

  • Market demand and potential
  • Financial feasibility and required investment
  • Competition and market entry barriers
  • Available resources and capabilities

(b) Four guidelines for effective social responsibility activities:

  • Align activities with company values and objectives
  • Engage with and understand community needs
  • Measure and evaluate impact of activities
  • Ensure sustainable and long-term commitment

Question 10

(a) State four benefits that an individual may derive from self employment.

(b) Outline six reasons that make business planning an important aspect in entrepreneurship.

Answer

(a) Four benefits of self employment:

  • Independence and autonomy in decision making
  • Potential for higher income and profits
  • Flexible working hours and schedule
  • Personal satisfaction from building own business

(b) Six reasons why business planning is important in entrepreneurship:

  • Provides clear direction and goals
  • Helps secure funding and investment
  • Identifies potential risks and challenges
  • Aids in resource allocation and management
  • Serves as a benchmark for performance measurement
  • Facilitates strategic decision making

Question 11

(a) Highlight four attributes that a successful entrepreneur should possess.

(b) Small and medium size enterprises have been on the rise in Kenya. State six contributions of these enterprises to the economic development of the country.

Answer

(a) Four attributes of a successful entrepreneur:

  • Innovation and creativity
  • Risk-taking ability
  • Leadership and management skills
  • Persistence and determination

(b) Six contributions of small and medium enterprises to Kenya's economic development:

  • Job creation and employment opportunities
  • Income generation and poverty reduction
  • Innovation and technological advancement
  • Economic diversification
  • Export revenue generation
  • Skills development and training

Question 12

(a) Outline four characteristics of the recovery stage of the business life cycle.

(b) Explain three benefits that may accrue to an organisation that out-sources services.

Answer

(a) Four characteristics of the recovery stage of the business life cycle:

  • Gradual increase in sales and revenue
  • Stabilization of cash flow
  • Renewed market confidence
  • Implementation of restructured business strategies

(b) Three benefits of outsourcing services:

  • Cost reduction: Lower operational and labor costs
  • Access to specialized expertise and technology
  • Focus on core business functions
