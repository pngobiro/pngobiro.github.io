SECTION A: ICT (40 marks)

Answer any TWO questions from this section.

Question 1

(a) Explain three fields in an e-mail software that may be filled when sending an e-mail to multiple recipients. (6 marks)

(b)

(i) State four types of hard copies that can be generated from a presentation program.

(ii) Explain two ways that may be used to deliver a presentation to an audience. (4 marks)

(c) When creating a database, state the most suitable data type for each of the following fields:

(i) the number of children in a family; (1 mark)

(ii) a line of company address; (1 mark)

(iii) gender of a person; (1 mark)

(iv) a person's birthday. (1 mark)

(d) State four examples of point-and-draw input devices. (2 marks)

Answer

(a) Email Fields for Multiple Recipients:

  1. To:
    • Primary recipients of the email
    • Main addressees who need to take action
    • Can contain multiple email addresses
  2. CC (Carbon Copy):
    • Secondary recipients who need to be informed
    • For keeping others in the loop
    • Recipients can see who else received the email
  3. BCC (Blind Carbon Copy):
    • Hidden recipients from other recipients
    • Maintains privacy of email addresses
    • Recipients cannot see other BCC recipients

(b)(i) Hard Copies from Presentation Program:

  • Handouts with multiple slides per page
  • Speaker notes with slides
  • Outline view printouts
  • Individual full-page slides

(b)(ii) Presentation Delivery Methods:

  1. Live Presentation:
    • Direct presentation to audience
    • Using projector or large display
    • Interactive with audience
  2. Self-Running Presentation:
    • Automated slideshow
    • Timed transitions
    • Kiosk-style presentation

(c) Database Field Data Types:

  • (i) Number of children: Number (Integer)
  • (ii) Company address line: Text
  • (iii) Gender: Text (Single character) or Yes/No
  • (iv) Birthday: Date/Time

(d) Point-and-Draw Input Devices:

  • Mouse
  • Graphics tablet/digitizer
  • Light pen
  • Touchscreen
  • Trackball
  • Touchpad

Question 3

(a) Figure 3 shows an extract of a document created using a word processor. Identify six formats that have been applied in the document.

(b) Distinguish between system software and application software, illustrating your answer with a commercial example in each case.

(c) Explain two reasons for having a backing storage as an essential component of a computer system.

(d)

(i) Outline the outcome when each of the following keys on a computer keyboard are pressed when using a word processor:

(I) backspace; (1 mark)

(II) tab key; (1 mark)

(III) enter key.

(ii) Distinguish between impact and non impact printers.

Answer

(a) Document Formats:

  1. Bold Text: Used for emphasis
  2. Italic Text: Used for specific words
  3. Underlined Text: Used for headings
  4. Bulleted List: Used for listing items
  5. Different Font Sizes: Used for headings and body text
  6. Text Alignment: Justified or left-aligned

(b) System vs. Application Software:

Feature System Software Application Software
Purpose Manages hardware and system resources Performs specific user tasks
Function Provides platform for applications Directly interacts with user
Examples Operating systems (Windows, Linux) Word processors (MS Word), Browsers (Chrome)

(c) Reasons for Backing Storage:

1. Data Persistence:

  • Stores data permanently
  • Retains data when power is off
  • Provides long-term storage

2. Large Storage Capacity:

  • Stores large amounts of data
  • Accommodates operating system, applications, and user files
  • Provides ample storage space

(d)(i) Keyboard Key Outcomes:

(I) Backspace:

  • Deletes character to the left of cursor

(II) Tab Key:

  • Inserts a tab space
  • Moves cursor to next tab stop

(III) Enter Key:

  • Creates a new paragraph
  • Moves cursor to next line

(d)(ii) Printer Types:

Feature Impact Printers Non-Impact Printers
Mechanism Strikes ribbon to form characters Uses ink or toner
Noise Noisier operation Quieter operation
Speed Slower printing speed Faster printing speed
Examples Dot matrix, daisy wheel Inkjet, laser

Question 4

(a) Identify six aspects of non-verbal communication which may influence communication.

(b) State four indicators of effective communication in an organisation.

(c) Outline five measures that a chair of a meeting may take to ensure time management during a meeting.

(d) The position of Communication Assistant at Rimox Bakers Limited has fallen vacant. Draft a press advertisement inviting suitably qualified persons to apply.

Answer

(a) Aspects of Non-Verbal Communication:

  • Facial Expressions:
    • Convey emotions and attitudes
    • Influence message interpretation
  • Body Language:
    • Posture, gestures, and movements
    • Indicate confidence, interest, or disinterest
  • Eye Contact:
    • Shows engagement and sincerity
    • Maintains attention and connection
  • Tone of Voice:
    • Conveys emotion and emphasis
    • Affects message reception
  • Personal Space:
    • Proximity and physical distance
    • Influences comfort and trust
  • Appearance:
    • Clothing and grooming
    • Impacts credibility and professionalism

(b) Indicators of Effective Communication:

  • Clear Understanding:
    • Message is accurately interpreted
    • No ambiguity or confusion
  • Timely Feedback:
    • Prompt responses and reactions
    • Active engagement from receiver
  • Mutual Respect:
    • Open and honest dialogue
    • Active listening and empathy
  • Achieved Objectives:
    • Desired outcomes are met
    • Goals are accomplished

(c) Time Management Measures for Meeting Chair:

  1. Prepare Agenda:
    • Distribute agenda in advance
    • Allocate time for each item
  2. Start on Time:
    • Begin meeting promptly
    • Set clear start and end times
  3. Stick to Agenda:
    • Keep discussions focused
    • Avoid digressions
  4. Manage Discussions:
    • Limit speaking time
    • Encourage concise contributions
  5. Summarize and Conclude:
    • Recap key points
    • Assign action items
    • End meeting on time

SECTION C: ENTREPRENEURSHIP (40 marks)

Answer any TWO questions from this section.

Question 6

(a) Despite persuasion by friends, Hamisi has been reluctant to quit salaried employment for self employment. Outline six reasons that may have led to this reluctance.

(b) Explain two reasons that makes it necessary for entrepreneurs to possess each of the following characteristics:

(i) good human relations;

(ii) high optimism;

(iii) independence.

(c) Explain four reasons that may be attributed to the slow growth of entrepreneurship in developing countries.

Answer

(a) Reasons for Reluctance to Self-Employment:

  1. Financial Security:
    • Regular salary in employment
    • Guaranteed monthly income
    • Employee benefits and insurance
  2. Risk Aversion:
    • Fear of business failure
    • Uncertainty of income
    • Market volatility concerns
  3. Capital Requirements:
    • Lack of startup funds
    • Limited access to financing
    • High initial investment needs
  4. Skills Gap:
    • Limited business management experience
    • Insufficient entrepreneurial skills
    • Need for additional training
  5. Family Responsibilities:
    • Financial obligations
    • Family dependents
    • Need for stable income
  6. Market Competition:
    • Saturated market conditions
    • Established competitors
    • Entry barriers

(b) Entrepreneurial Characteristics:

(i) Good Human Relations:

  1. Customer Relations:
    • Building customer loyalty
    • Understanding customer needs
  2. Team Management:
    • Effective employee coordination
    • Building productive relationships

(ii) High Optimism:

  1. Overcoming Challenges:
    • Persistence through difficulties
    • Maintaining motivation
  2. Opportunity Recognition:
    • Seeing potential in challenges
    • Positive market outlook

(iii) Independence:

  1. Decision Making:
    • Quick response to opportunities
    • Autonomous business choices
  2. Innovation:
    • Implementing unique ideas
    • Creative problem-solving

(c) Slow Growth of Entrepreneurship in Developing Countries:

  1. Limited Access to Capital:
    • Insufficient financial institutions
    • High interest rates
    • Strict lending requirements
  2. Inadequate Infrastructure:
    • Poor transportation networks
    • Unreliable power supply
    • Limited technology access
  3. Regulatory Challenges:
    • Complex business regulations
    • Bureaucratic procedures
    • High compliance costs
  4. Limited Skills and Education:
    • Insufficient entrepreneurial training
    • Limited business education
    • Lack of mentorship programs

Question 7

(a) State four reasons that make a partnership deed an important document in a partnership.

(b) Explain the meaning of each of the following terms as used in entrepreneurship:

(i) business opportunity;

(ii) enterprise management.

(c) Explain six strategies that women entrepreneurs may adopt to develop entrepreneurship.

Answer

(a) Importance of Partnership Deed:

  1. Defines Rights and Duties:
    • Clearly outlines partner responsibilities
    • Specifies profit/loss sharing ratios
    • Avoids future misunderstandings
  2. Legal Evidence:
    • Serves as proof of partnership existence
    • Used in legal disputes
    • Establishes terms of agreement
  3. Governs Operations:
    • Sets rules for business conduct
    • Guides decision-making processes
    • Provides framework for management
  4. Resolves Conflicts:
    • Provides mechanism for dispute resolution
    • Clarifies procedures for dissolution
    • Reduces potential conflicts

(b) Entrepreneurship Terms:

(i) Business Opportunity:

  • A favorable circumstance or situation
  • Potential to start or grow a business
  • Involves meeting market needs
  • Requires assessment of viability

(ii) Enterprise Management:

  • Process of planning, organizing, leading, and controlling
  • Managing resources effectively
  • Achieving business objectives
  • Involves strategic decision-making

(c) Strategies for Women Entrepreneurs:

  1. Networking:
    • Building connections with other entrepreneurs
    • Joining business associations
    • Seeking mentorship opportunities
  2. Access to Finance:
    • Exploring women-focused funding options
    • Improving financial literacy
    • Developing strong business plans
  3. Skills Development:
    • Attending training programs
    • Acquiring technical and management skills
    • Continuous learning
  4. Technology Adoption:
    • Leveraging digital tools
    • Utilizing e-commerce platforms
    • Improving online presence
  5. Advocacy and Policy Influence:
    • Participating in policy discussions
    • Advocating for supportive regulations
    • Promoting gender equality in business
  6. Work-Life Balance:
    • Developing strategies for managing responsibilities
    • Seeking support systems
    • Prioritizing well-being

Question 8

(a) Distinguish between partnership and co-ownership.

(b) Outline four indicators of a business plan which is not effective.

(c) Explain six reasons that make insurance an important business support service.

Answer

(a) Partnership vs. Co-ownership:

Feature Partnership Co-ownership
Basis Agreement (contractual) Status (may arise by law)
Purpose Carrying on a business for profit Joint holding of property
Agency Partners are agents of the firm Co-owners are not agents of others
Transfer of Interest Requires consent of all partners Interest can be transferred freely
Number of Members Usually limited (e.g., 2-20) No maximum limit
*(Note: This question (8a) is identical to question 9a in the July 2016 paper)*

(b) Indicators of an Ineffective Business Plan:

  1. Unclear Objectives:
    • Vague or undefined goals
    • Lack of measurable targets
    • No clear direction for the business
  2. Inadequate Market Analysis:
    • Poor understanding of target market
    • Insufficient competitor research
    • Unrealistic market size estimation
  3. Unrealistic Financial Projections:
    • Overly optimistic revenue forecasts
    • Underestimated costs and expenses
    • Lack of sound financial assumptions
  4. Lack of Actionable Strategy:
    • No clear steps for implementation
    • Missing marketing or operational plans
    • Absence of timelines and milestones

(c) Importance of Insurance as a Business Support Service:

  1. Risk Transfer:
    • Transfers potential financial losses to insurer
    • Protects against unforeseen events (fire, theft, etc.)
    • Reduces financial uncertainty
  2. Business Continuity:
    • Provides funds for recovery after a loss
    • Helps business resume operations quickly
    • Minimizes disruption
  3. Legal Compliance:
    • Meets legal requirements (e.g., workers' compensation)
    • Avoids penalties and fines
    • Ensures adherence to regulations
  4. Enhanced Credibility:
    • Shows responsibility to stakeholders (customers, lenders)
    • Improves business reputation
    • Can be a requirement for contracts
  5. Employee Protection:
    • Provides benefits like health or life insurance
    • Attracts and retains employees
    • Covers liability for employee injuries
  6. Asset Protection:
    • Covers damage or loss of physical assets
    • Protects property, equipment, and inventory
    • Safeguards business investments
