Question 1 (20 marks)

(a) Outline five differences between a leader and a manager. (10 marks)

(b) Highlight five conclusions of the Hawthorne experiments conducted by Elton Mayo. (10 marks)

Answer

(a) Differences between a Leader and a Manager:
Feature Leader Manager
Focus People, vision, inspiration Tasks, structure, control
Approach Innovates, develops Administers, maintains
Horizon Long-term perspective Short-term perspective
Question Asks "what" and "why" Asks "how" and "when"
Influence Inspires trust, motivates Relies on authority, directs
(b) Conclusions of the Hawthorne Experiments:
  1. Social Factors: Social and psychological factors significantly impact productivity, often more than physical conditions.
  2. Group Influence: Workplace culture and group norms strongly influence individual behavior and output.
  3. Supervision: Worker attitude towards management and supervision affects performance. Participative and employee-centered leadership is effective.
  4. Communication: Open communication between workers and management is crucial for understanding and morale.
  5. Recognition (Hawthorne Effect): Workers' productivity increased when they felt observed and valued, regardless of changes in physical conditions.

Question 2 (20 marks)

(a) Outline five benefits of strategic planning to an organization. (10 marks)

(b) Explain five principles that can help in ensuring effective delegation. (10 marks)

Answer

(a) Benefits of Strategic Planning:
  1. Provides Direction: Sets clear goals and objectives, guiding organizational efforts.
  2. Resource Allocation: Helps prioritize and allocate resources effectively towards strategic goals.
  3. Proactive Approach: Enables organizations to anticipate and respond to future changes and challenges.
  4. Improved Decision Making: Provides a framework for making informed decisions aligned with long-term objectives.
  5. Enhanced Performance: Focuses efforts on key priorities, leading to improved organizational performance and competitiveness.
(b) Principles for Effective Delegation:
  1. Principle of Clarity: Clearly define the task, expected results, authority level, and deadlines.
  2. Principle of Competence: Delegate tasks to employees who possess the necessary skills and knowledge.
  3. Principle of Authority and Responsibility: Grant sufficient authority to match the assigned responsibility.
  4. Principle of Unity of Command: Ensure employees report to only one supervisor for the delegated task to avoid confusion.
  5. Principle of Feedback and Support: Provide necessary resources, support, and regular feedback without micromanaging.

Question 3 (20 marks)

(a) There are certain financial incentives that an organization may give to its employees in order to enhance their productivity. Explain five such incentives. (10 marks)

(b) Outline five objectives of budgetary control in an organization. (10 marks)

Answer

(a) Financial Incentives to Enhance Productivity:
  1. Performance Bonuses: Lump-sum payments tied to achieving specific individual or team performance targets.
  2. Profit Sharing: Distributing a portion of the company's profits among employees, linking their rewards to organizational success.
  3. Stock Options: Offering employees the right to purchase company stock at a predetermined price, aligning employee interests with shareholder value.
  4. Commission: Paying employees a percentage of the sales revenue they generate, directly rewarding sales performance.
  5. Piece-Rate Pay: Paying employees based on the number of units they produce, directly linking pay to output quantity.
(b) Objectives of Budgetary Control:
  1. Planning: Formalizes future plans and translates them into financial terms.
  2. Coordination: Integrates the plans and activities of different departments towards common goals.
  3. Control: Monitors actual performance against budgeted targets, identifying variances for corrective action.
  4. Motivation: Provides clear targets and benchmarks, potentially motivating employees to achieve them.
  5. Communication: Communicates management's expectations and plans throughout the organization.

Question 4 (20 marks)

(a) Explain five factors that may limit the freedom of a manager when recruiting employees. (10 marks)

(b) Outline five phases of the disaster management cycle. (10 marks)

Answer

(a) Factors Limiting Manager's Freedom in Recruitment:
  1. Legal Constraints: Labor laws, anti-discrimination regulations, and employment standards restrict hiring practices.
  2. Organizational Policies: Internal policies regarding qualifications, diversity targets, internal promotions, and hiring procedures limit choices.
  3. Budgetary Constraints: Available budget for salaries, benefits, and recruitment costs restricts the pool of candidates.
  4. Labor Market Conditions: Availability of candidates with required skills and experience in the relevant labor market.
  5. Union Agreements: Collective bargaining agreements may dictate hiring procedures, seniority rules, or specific requirements.
(b) Phases of the Disaster Management Cycle:
  1. Mitigation: Actions taken to prevent or reduce the impact of disasters (e.g., building codes, land-use planning).
  2. Preparedness: Planning, training, and educational activities for events that cannot be mitigated (e.g., emergency drills, warning systems).
  3. Response: Actions taken immediately before, during, or directly after a disaster to save lives and meet basic human needs (e.g., search and rescue, emergency shelter).
  4. Recovery: Actions taken to restore community functions and infrastructure after a disaster (e.g., rebuilding homes, restoring services, economic recovery).
  5. Development (sometimes included): Integrating disaster risk reduction into long-term development planning to build resilience.

Question 5 (20 marks)

(a) Organizations find it necessary to continuously monitor the environment in which they operate. Explain five reasons for this necessity. (10 marks)

(b) Outline five advantages of the group decision making technique. (10 marks)

Answer

(a) Reasons for Monitoring the Operating Environment:
  1. Identify Opportunities: Spot emerging trends, new markets, or technological advancements that can be leveraged.
  2. Detect Threats: Recognize potential risks such as new competitors, changing regulations, or economic downturns.
  3. Adaptation: Understand changes in customer needs, market dynamics, and competitive landscape to adapt strategies accordingly.
  4. Strategic Planning: Provide essential information for formulating realistic and effective long-term plans.
  5. Resource Allocation: Make informed decisions about where to allocate resources based on environmental analysis.
(b) Advantages of Group Decision Making:
  1. More Information & Knowledge: Access to a wider range of information, expertise, and perspectives.
  2. Increased Acceptance: Decisions made collectively are often better understood and accepted by those involved in implementation.
  3. Higher Quality Decisions: Diverse viewpoints can lead to more thorough analysis and creative solutions.
  4. Enhanced Communication: Promotes discussion and understanding among group members.
  5. Development Opportunity: Provides a learning experience for group members, enhancing their decision-making skills.

Question 6 (20 marks)

(a) Explain five ways in which a manager may ensure efficient stock control in an organization. (10 marks)

(b) Organizations usually carry out interviews during employee selection. Explain five reasons why this is done. (10 marks)

Answer

(a) Ensuring Efficient Stock Control:
  1. Accurate Record Keeping: Maintain precise records of stock levels, movements, and values (e.g., using inventory management software).
  2. Regular Stocktaking: Conduct periodic physical counts (e.g., cycle counting, annual stocktake) to verify records and identify discrepancies.
  3. Implementing Control Systems: Use methods like ABC analysis (prioritizing high-value items), Just-In-Time (JIT), or Economic Order Quantity (EOQ) to optimize stock levels.
  4. Secure Storage: Ensure proper storage conditions to prevent damage, spoilage, or theft, with controlled access.
  5. Supplier Management: Build reliable relationships with suppliers to ensure timely deliveries and quality materials, reducing the need for excessive buffer stock.
(b) Reasons for Using Interviews in Employee Selection:
  1. Assess Communication Skills: Evaluate the candidate's verbal communication, listening skills, and ability to articulate thoughts.
  2. Evaluate Personality & Fit: Gauge personality traits, attitude, motivation, and cultural fit with the organization.
  3. Clarify Resume Information: Ask specific questions to elaborate on experience, skills, and qualifications listed in the resume/application.
  4. Assess Problem-Solving Skills: Use behavioral or situational questions to understand how candidates handle work-related challenges.
  5. Provide Job Information: Allows the candidate to ask questions and provides an opportunity to sell the job and organization to the candidate.

Question 7 (20 marks)

(a) Outline five benefits that may accrue to an organization from establishing an effective co-ordination function. (10 marks)

(b) Zamala Enterprises is in the process of developing a mission statement. Explain five ways in which this statement would be useful to the enterprise. (10 marks)

Answer

(a) Benefits of Effective Co-ordination:
  1. Synergy: Achieves greater results through combined efforts than individual actions (2+2=5 effect).
  2. Resource Optimization: Ensures efficient use of resources by avoiding duplication and conflict.
  3. Goal Achievement: Aligns departmental activities towards common organizational objectives.
  4. Improved Relationships: Fosters better communication and collaboration between departments and individuals.
  5. Unity of Action: Ensures all parts of the organization work harmoniously towards the same goals.
(b) Usefulness of a Mission Statement to Zamala Enterprises:
  1. Provides Direction: Clearly states the purpose and overall goal of the enterprise, guiding strategic decisions.
  2. Guides Resource Allocation: Helps prioritize activities and allocate resources effectively towards achieving the mission.
  3. Motivates Employees: Inspires employees by providing a sense of purpose and shared identity.
  4. Communicates to Stakeholders: Informs customers, investors, and the public about the enterprise's values and purpose.
  5. Basis for Goal Setting: Serves as a foundation for setting specific, measurable, achievable, relevant, and time-bound (SMART) objectives.
