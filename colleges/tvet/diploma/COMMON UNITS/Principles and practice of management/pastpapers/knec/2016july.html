Question 1 (20 marks)

(a) The management of Foka Limited has adopted the bureaucratic principles of management as advocated by Max Weber. Outline five ways in which the company is likely to benefit from this adoption. (10 marks)

(b) Explain five precautions that should be taken when using tests in employee selection to ensure that they achieve the intended purpose. (10 marks)

Answer

(a) Benefits of Bureaucratic Principles (Weber):
  1. Efficiency & Speed: Specialization and clear procedures can enhance speed and efficiency for routine tasks.
  2. Consistency & Predictability: Standardized rules ensure tasks are performed uniformly, leading to predictable outcomes and fair treatment.
  3. Rationality: Decisions are based on rules and logic, reducing subjectivity, bias, and emotional influence.
  4. Clear Authority & Accountability: The hierarchical structure clearly defines who is responsible for what and who reports to whom.
  5. Technical Competence: Emphasis on qualifications and expertise for positions ensures competence in roles.
(b) Precautions When Using Selection Tests:
  1. Validity: Ensure the test accurately measures what it claims to measure and is relevant to job performance (e.g., content validity, criterion-related validity).
  2. Reliability: Ensure the test yields consistent results over time and across different administrations.
  3. Standardization: Administer and score the test under uniform conditions for all applicants to ensure fairness.
  4. Job Relevance: Use tests that assess skills, knowledge, or abilities directly required for successful job performance.
  5. Non-Discrimination: Ensure the test does not unfairly discriminate against any protected group and complies with equal employment opportunity laws. Use multiple selection methods, not just tests.

Question 2 (20 marks)

(a) Organizations that adopt Management by Objectives (MBO) are likely to encounter certain challenges. Explain five such challenges. (10 marks)

(b) Highlight five criticisms that can be raised against the use of Herzbergâ€™s Two Factor theory in the motivation of employees in an organization. (10 marks)

Answer

(a) Challenges of Management by Objectives (MBO):
  1. Time-Consuming: The process of setting mutually agreed-upon objectives can be lengthy.
  2. Overemphasis on Quantifiable Goals: May neglect important qualitative aspects of performance or long-term objectives.
  3. Potential for Conflict: Disagreements between managers and subordinates during goal setting or appraisal.
  4. Rigidity/Inflexibility: Objectives set for a period might become unsuitable if the environment changes rapidly.
  5. Increased Paperwork: Can generate significant documentation and administrative overhead.
  6. "Tug of War": Subordinates might try to set easily achievable goals, while managers push for challenging ones.
(b) Criticisms of Herzberg's Two-Factor Theory:
  1. Methodology Issues: The critical incident technique used is prone to bias (people attribute satisfaction to themselves and dissatisfaction to external factors).
  2. Oversimplification: The strict separation of factors into 'hygiene' and 'motivators' may not reflect reality; some factors can act as both.
  3. Ignores Individual Differences: Assumes all employees react similarly to hygiene factors and motivators, neglecting individual preferences and values.
  4. Job Satisfaction vs. Productivity Link: The theory primarily links factors to job satisfaction, but the link between satisfaction and actual productivity is not always direct or strong.
  5. Limited Sample: Original research was primarily based on accountants and engineers, potentially limiting generalizability to other occupations.

Question 3 (20 marks)

(a) Give five reasons that make planning an important function of management. (10 marks)

(b) Explain five challenges that an organization may face from heavily relying on casual workers as a source of labour supply. (10 marks)

Answer

(a) Importance of Planning Function:
  1. Provides Direction: Sets objectives and defines the course of action, ensuring everyone works towards common goals.
  2. Reduces Uncertainty & Risk: Forces managers to anticipate future events, identify potential problems, and develop contingency plans.
  3. Facilitates Control: Establishes standards and benchmarks against which actual performance can be measured and evaluated.
  4. Encourages Innovation & Creativity: The planning process often involves thinking about new ways to achieve objectives.
  5. Optimizes Resource Utilization: Helps allocate resources (time, money, personnel) efficiently and effectively towards priorities, minimizing waste.
(b) Challenges of Relying Heavily on Casual Workers:
  1. Lower Commitment & Loyalty: Casual workers often have less attachment to the organization, potentially leading to higher turnover and lower motivation.
  2. Reduced Productivity/Quality: May lack specific skills, training, or familiarity with organizational processes compared to permanent staff, impacting output quality.
  3. Higher Training & Supervision Costs: Frequent turnover necessitates ongoing recruitment, onboarding, and potentially more intensive supervision.
  4. Workforce Instability & Scheduling Difficulties: Difficulty in maintaining consistent staffing levels, team cohesion, and predictable scheduling.
  5. Potential Legal & Compliance Risks: Risk of misclassification, violating labor laws regarding benefits, pay, or working conditions for casual staff.

Question 4 (20 marks)

(a) Explain six circumstances under which a manager may find it necessary to use the autocratic style of leadership in an organization. (12 marks)

(b) Outline four ways through which the management of an organization can fulfill the self actualization needs of employees. (8 marks)

Answer

(a) Circumstances for Using Autocratic Leadership:
  1. Crisis Situations: When quick, decisive action is needed in emergencies.
  2. Unskilled/Inexperienced Workforce: When employees lack the skills or knowledge to work independently and require clear direction.
  3. Routine/Simple Tasks: For straightforward tasks where procedures are well-defined and employee input is minimal.
  4. Time Pressure: When deadlines are tight and there is limited time for consultation or participation.
  5. Safety-Critical Operations: In situations where strict adherence to procedures is essential for safety.
  6. When Leader Has Unique Expertise: If the leader possesses significantly more knowledge or expertise than the team regarding the specific task.
(b) Fulfilling Self-Actualization Needs (Maslow):
  1. Provide Challenging Work: Assign tasks that utilize employees' full potential, creativity, and problem-solving skills.
  2. Offer Opportunities for Growth & Development: Support training, skill enhancement, and career advancement opportunities.
  3. Grant Autonomy & Empowerment: Allow employees control over their work methods and decision-making within their roles.
  4. Encourage Creativity & Innovation: Create an environment where employees feel safe to propose new ideas and experiment.

Question 5 (20 marks)

(a) The management of Maji Mazuri Limited reviews all the organizationâ€™s budgets after every six months. Explain six benefits that may accrue to the organization from this practice. (12 marks)

(b) Explain four leadership behaviours that are associated with the Path Goal Theory of leadership. (8 marks)

Answer

(a) Benefits of Regular Budget Reviews (e.g., semi-annually):
  1. Improved Accuracy & Realism: Allows budgets to be updated based on actual performance and revised forecasts, making them more relevant.
  2. Enhanced Control & Timely Action: Provides more frequent opportunities to compare actuals vs. budget, identify variances, and take corrective actions sooner.
  3. Increased Flexibility & Adaptability: Enables the organization to respond more quickly to changing market conditions, opportunities, or internal circumstances by adjusting plans and resource allocations.
  4. Better Resource Allocation: Facilitates the reallocation of funds from underperforming areas to more promising ones based on current performance data.
  5. Increased Managerial Accountability & Focus: Regular reviews keep managers focused on achieving budget targets and justify deviations.
  6. Facilitates Learning & Improvement: Provides insights into forecasting accuracy, operational efficiency, and cost drivers, improving future budgeting processes.
(b) Leadership Behaviors in Path-Goal Theory:
  1. Directive Leadership: The leader provides specific guidance, clarifies expectations, sets performance standards, and schedules work. (Effective when tasks are ambiguous or subordinates are inexperienced).
  2. Supportive Leadership: The leader is friendly, approachable, and shows concern for subordinates' needs and well-being. (Effective for stressful, frustrating, or dissatisfying tasks).
  3. Participative Leadership: The leader consults with subordinates, solicits their suggestions, and considers their input before making decisions. (Effective when subordinates are knowledgeable and desire involvement).
  4. Achievement-Oriented Leadership: The leader sets challenging goals, expects high performance, and expresses confidence in subordinates' abilities. (Effective for complex tasks where subordinates are achievement-motivated).

Question 6 (20 marks)

(a) Explain six roles that supervisors are expected to play inorder to ensure proper coordination of activities in their departments. (12 marks)

(b) Highlight four limitations of using written reports as a tool of control in an organization. (8 marks)

Answer

(a) Supervisor Roles in Coordination:
  1. Clarifying Roles & Tasks: Ensuring each team member understands their specific responsibilities and how they fit into the department's goals.
  2. Facilitating Communication: Acting as a communication link between team members, other departments, and upper management.
  3. Scheduling & Workflow Management: Organizing work schedules and processes to ensure smooth workflow and timely completion of tasks.
  4. Resource Allocation: Distributing necessary resources (tools, information, support) effectively within the team.
  5. Conflict Resolution: Addressing and resolving conflicts within the team or between the team and other units.
  6. Monitoring Progress: Tracking team activities and progress towards goals, ensuring alignment with overall plans.
(b) Limitations of Written Reports as Control Tools:
  1. Time Lag: Reports take time to compile, distribute, and read, meaning information may be outdated by the time action is taken.
  2. Lack of Nuance/Context: Written reports may not capture the full context or nuances of a situation that face-to-face communication can provide.
  3. Potential for Misinterpretation: Written language can be ambiguous or misinterpreted without the opportunity for immediate clarification.
  4. Information Overload: Excessive detail or poorly structured reports can overwhelm managers and obscure critical information.
  5. Cost & Effort: Preparing detailed written reports can be time-consuming and costly.

Question 7 (20 marks)

(a) Outline four ways in which an organization may benefit from engaging in corporate social responsibility activities. (8 marks)

(b) There are certain challenges that are associated with committee decision making in organizations. Explain six such challenges. (12 marks)

Answer

(a) Benefits of Corporate Social Responsibility (CSR):
  1. Enhanced Reputation & Brand Image: Positive public perception, increased trust, and stronger brand loyalty.
  2. Improved Employee Morale & Retention: Attracts and retains talent as employees value working for socially conscious companies.
  3. Increased Customer Loyalty: Consumers often prefer brands that demonstrate ethical and social responsibility.
  4. Risk Management & Mitigation: Proactive engagement can reduce risks related to environmental, social, or governance issues and enhance stakeholder relations.
  5. Attracting Investment: Growing interest from socially responsible investors (SRI) can improve access to capital.
(b) Challenges of Committee Decision Making:
  1. Time-Consuming: Scheduling meetings and reaching consensus among multiple members can significantly delay decisions.
  2. Costly: The cumulative time spent by members, especially managers, represents a significant organizational cost.
  3. Compromise Decisions: May lead to watered-down or suboptimal solutions ("lowest common denominator") to achieve agreement.
  4. Diffusion of Responsibility: No single individual feels fully accountable, potentially leading to riskier or less carefully considered choices.
  5. Groupthink: Pressure for conformity can suppress critical evaluation of alternatives and lead to poor decisions.
  6. Domination by Few: More vocal, senior, or influential members might unduly influence the outcome, hindering diverse input.
