Entrepreneurship KNEC Diploma Notes

Duration: 20 hours Level: Diploma

Course Overview

This course covers the KNEC Diploma curriculum for Entrepreneurship. It is designed to provide a comprehensive understanding of entrepreneurial concepts, skills, and practices.

  • Understand the fundamental concepts of entrepreneurship.
  • Identify and evaluate business opportunities.
  • Develop entrepreneurial skills and competencies.
  • Learn to start and manage a small business.
  • Understand the role of entrepreneurship in national development.
  • Appreciate ethical considerations and social responsibilities in business.

Course Modules

  1. Introduction to Entrepreneurship

    Basic terms and concepts of entrepreneurship, and its importance to national development.

    1 hour

  2. Evolution of Entrepreneurship

    The historical development of entrepreneurship, including myths and theories.

    1.5 hours

  3. The Entrepreneur

    Types, qualities, and roles of entrepreneurs.

    1.5 hours

  4. Creativity and Innovation

    The importance of creativity and innovation in business, including processes and barriers.

    1 hour

  5. Entrepreneurial Culture

    The concept of entrepreneurial culture and its impact on business development.

    1 hour

  6. Entrepreneurial Opportunities

    Identifying and evaluating business opportunities.

    1.5 hours

  7. Entrepreneurial Motivation

    Factors that motivate entrepreneurs to start and run businesses.

    1 hour

  8. Entrepreneurial Competencies

    Essential skills and competencies required for successful entrepreneurship.

    1.5 hours

  9. Starting a Small Business

    Procedures, factors to consider, and support services for starting a small business.

    2 hours

  10. Business Enterprise Management

    Functions of management, including planning, organizing, and controlling.

    2 hours

  11. Financial Management

    Sources of finance, recording transactions, and preparing financial statements.

    2 hours

  12. Marketing

    Marketing concepts, components, and methods of gathering market information.

    1.5 hours

  13. Enterprise Social Responsibility

    The role of businesses in contributing to society, including ethical considerations.

    1.5 hour

  14. Information Communication Technology (ICT)

    The importance of technology and how it is implemented in the business environment.

    1.5 hours

Prerequisites

  • Basic literacy and numeracy skills.
  • An interest in starting and managing a business.
