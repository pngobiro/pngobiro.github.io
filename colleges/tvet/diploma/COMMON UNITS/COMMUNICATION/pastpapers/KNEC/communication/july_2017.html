July 2017

COMMUNICATION

Exam Code: 2902/104

Duration: 3 hours

Period: JULY 2017

1. (3 marks)

List three items that must be contained in the agenda of an Annual General Meeting.

Answer

Minutes of the previous meeting.

Matters arising from the minutes.

Chairman's report.

2. (3 marks)

State three features of appraisal interviews.

Answer

It is a formal discussion between a manager and an employee.

It focuses on the employee's performance over a specific period.

It aims to provide feedback and identify areas for improvement.

3. (3 marks)

Outline three advantages of written reports.

Answer

Provide a permanent record of information.

Allow for detailed and complex information to be conveyed.

Enable wide distribution and can be referred to at any time.

4. (3 marks)

List three functions of external communication.

Answer

Building and maintaining the organization's image and reputation.

Marketing and promoting products or services to customers.

Managing relationships with stakeholders such as suppliers and the public.

5. (4 marks)

Outline four factors that may cause delays in the transmission of information.

Answer

Technical failures in communication systems or equipment.

Poor network connectivity or bandwidth limitations.

Inefficient communication processes or procedures.

Lack of clarity in communication, leading to misunderstandings and re-communication.

6. (4 marks)

List four categories of information that are included in a curriculum vitae.

Answer

Personal details including name, contact information, and address.

Educational background, listing qualifications and institutions attended.

Work experience, detailing previous roles, responsibilities, and achievements.

Skills and competencies relevant to the job or field of application.

7. (3 marks)

State three measures that one should take to ensure effective telephone communication.

Answer

Speak clearly and at a moderate pace.

Listen attentively and avoid interrupting.

Summarize key points and confirm understanding.

8. (3 marks)

List three challenges that one may experience when using visual communication to pass information.

Answer

Misinterpretation of visual cues due to cultural differences or individual perceptions.

Visual overload or complexity making it difficult to understand the intended message.

Technical limitations in displaying or accessing visual media.

9. (3 marks)

State three uses of non-verbal cues in oral communication.

Answer

To emphasize or reinforce verbal messages.

To convey emotions and attitudes.

To regulate the flow of conversation.

10. (3 marks)

Outline three benefits of using videoconferencing in an organization.

Answer

Reduces travel costs and time for meetings.

Facilitates face-to-face communication across geographical distances.

Enhances collaboration and teamwork among remote teams.

11. (16 marks)

As the secretary of Peston Association, write the minutes of a monthly meeting which discussed the following agenda items among others:

(a) security;

(b) tree planting;

(c) improving infrastructure.

12. (12 marks)

(a) Umoja Holdings Limited, a supermarket chain, is intending to close down two of its branches in a month's time. Write a circular letter to all stakeholders informing them about the closure.

(b) Ms. Rema, a student representative at Masomo University, will give a speech on their graduation day. Describe four non-verbal cues that she may use to make her speech effective.

13. (a) (2 marks)

The graph below show the approximate number of local tourists who visited City National Park over a period of six years.

(i) In which year did the park experience the greatest increase in the number of local tourists?

Answer

The park experienced the greatest increase in the number of local tourists in the year 2013.

13. (a) (ii) (2 marks)

What is the difference in the number of tourists received in 2012 and 2015?

Answer

The approximate number of tourists in 2012 is 45 and in 2015 is 60. The difference is 60 - 45 = 15. Therefore, the difference in the number of tourists received in 2012 and 2015 is 15.

13. (a) (iii) (2 marks)

What is the total number of local tourists received for the last four years?

Answer

The last four years are 2012, 2013, 2014, and 2015. The approximate number of tourists are 45, 70, 65, and 60 respectively. The total number of tourists is 45 + 70 + 65 + 60 = 240. Therefore, the total number of local tourists received for the last four years is approximately 240.

13. (a) (iv) (2 marks)

Outline one factor which may have led to the decline in the number of tourists between 2014 and 2015.

Answer

One factor that may have led to the decline in the number of tourists between 2014 and 2015 could be negative publicity or safety concerns regarding the park, which deterred potential visitors.

13. (b) (8 marks)

Describe four challenges which an organization may face due to poor internal communication.

Answer

Decreased Productivity: Poor internal communication leads to misunderstandings and errors, causing delays, rework, and ultimately reduced productivity.

Low Morale: Lack of clear and open communication can result in employees feeling undervalued, uninformed, and disconnected, leading to decreased morale and job satisfaction.

Conflict and Misunderstandings: Ineffective communication fosters confusion, rumors, and conflicts among employees and departments, hindering teamwork and collaboration.

Missed Opportunities: Poor information flow can result in missed deadlines, lost opportunities, and an inability to respond effectively to market changes or customer needs.

Passage for Question 14

The world is fast changing and there is stiff competition among businesses. This calls for innovation in order to survive. Every organization must, therefore, structure itself in such a way that it produces goods and services that meet clients' requirements, Chief Executive Officers must devise ways on how to cope with imminent challenges in order to remain relevant and profitable.

Some of the CEOs have tried to emulate other organizations which have made progress in innovation. This strategy has proved to be a challenge when CEOs fail to realize that some of the factors that make organizations to stand out may vary based on geographical regions, industry and size. For instance, what may be working for a technological organization in the United States of America might not work for a manufacturing organization in Africa.

In order to cope with changing requirements and competition, organizations have to be flexible. Organization flexibility can be described as the ability of an organization to not only survive difficult conditions but also thrive in the long term. This is achieved by identifying challenges and opportunities and finding solutions that lead to increased productivity. A flexible organization is characterised by four major qualities: ability to innovate rapidly and tailor goods and services to clients' requirements; ability to share information with suppliers and customers; ability to integrate ideas, experiences, knowledge and skills through employees, customers and suppliers; ability to reduce risks and have enough capital to take up new opportunities or face challenges.

There are certain strategies that an organization should adopt in order to remain relevant and profitable. Key among them is maintaining focus on its goals through proper structures, infrastructure, good organizational culture and effective management. This involves being ahead in innovation, mastering changing trends and being conversant with the clients' demands and needs and ensuring that every single employee lives and breathes the organization's values and beliefs.

Adaptability is another way through which an organization can remain relevant. This entails being able to deal with changes in legislature, market trends, customer demands, workforce needs and technological advancement. In this regard, the organization should be able to respond to demands brought about by globalization and keep up with changing employee needs.

Further, an organization should make decisions promptly and with certainty when faced with changing external conditions. Such decisions should be accompanied by prompt and detailed execution. To achieve timely decision-making, an organization must invest in data collection and analysis through its research and development department. Innovation, another strategy that an organization requires in order to remain relevant, demands that it keeps up with changes in technology. Most organizations, however, are limited by how fast they accept and adapt to changes. Limitations occur due to the high cost of installing new technology which sometimes tends to become obsolete very fast. In addition, organizations may be reluctant to incur the high costs of new technology due to the feeling that the technology already in place is expensive and has not yet given a return on investment. Further, organizations fear the cost of retraining staff on the use of the new technology they are required to adopt.

Besides, an organization must be able to deal with challenges and maintain focus on its core activities. This may be difficult since employees may be overwhelmed by the day-to-day operations of the organization. Therefore, efforts must be made by management to cultivate a culture that rewards the fast thinkers who tackle obstacles and innovators who move the organization forward.

An organization can also remain relevant through partnering with clients and other stakeholders. This involves sharing and creating mutually beneficial relationships that focus on continued improvement of goods and services. In this approach, every stakeholder must be treated as a business partner with insights on new processes and products. In other words, the organization should have "customer is right" attitude so as to design products and services as per the customer's demands.

If these strategies are adopted, business organizations will be competitive and relevant in a sustainable manner.

Adapted from MANAGEMENT MAGAZINE September 2015.

14. (a) (6 marks)

Explain the meaning of the following words and phrase as used in the passage:

(i) stiff;

(ii) vary;

(iii) thrive;

(iv) take up;

(v) entails;

(vi) cultivate.

Answer

Stiff: In the context of competition, 'stiff' means intense and challenging, indicating a high level of rivalry among businesses.

Vary: 'Vary' means to differ or change. In the passage, it refers to factors that make organizations stand out, which may differ based on various aspects like geographical regions.

Thrive: 'Thrive' means to prosper or flourish. It describes an organization's ability to not just survive but also succeed and grow in the long term.

Take up: 'Take up' means to embrace or adopt. In the passage, it refers to taking up new opportunities or facing challenges.

Entails: 'Entails' means to involve or require as a necessary part or consequence. Adaptability 'entails' being able to deal with changes.

Cultivate: 'Cultivate' means to develop or nurture. Management needs to 'cultivate' a culture that rewards innovators.

14. (b) (12 marks)

In about 120 words, and according to the passage, explain the strategies through which an organization can remain relevant and profitable.

Answer

According to the passage, organizations can remain relevant and profitable through several key strategies. Firstly, flexibility is crucial, enabling them to adapt to changing conditions and thrive long-term by innovating and tailoring services (Paragraph 3). Secondly, maintaining focus on goals with proper structure, culture, and effective management is essential. This includes innovation, trend awareness, and aligning with client needs (Paragraph 4). Adaptability to changes like legislation and market trends is also vital (Paragraph 5). Prompt decision-making, backed by data analysis, and embracing technological innovation are important strategies (Paragraph 6). Finally, focusing on core activities and cultivating a culture that values innovation and partnering with stakeholders are highlighted as key for sustained relevance and profitability (Paragraphs 7 & 8).
