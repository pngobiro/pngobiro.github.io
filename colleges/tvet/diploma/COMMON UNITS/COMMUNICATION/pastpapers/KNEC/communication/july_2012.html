July 2012

DIPLOMA IN COMMUNICATION STUDIES

Exam Code: COMMUNICATION MODULE I

Duration: 3 hours

Period: July 2012

1. (3 marks)

Outline three benefits that may accrue to a supervisor who listens to his subordinates.

Answer

Improved employee morale and motivation as subordinates feel valued and respected when their opinions are heard.

Enhanced problem-solving and decision-making due to a wider range of perspectives and insights from subordinates.

Stronger team cohesion and trust, fostering a more collaborative and productive work environment.

2. (3 marks)

State three reasons that make it necessary for workers to communicate with each other in an organization.

Answer

To coordinate tasks and activities, ensuring smooth workflow and efficient operations across different departments and teams.

To share information and knowledge, enabling informed decision-making and reducing duplication of effort.

To build relationships and rapport, fostering a positive and supportive work environment that enhances collaboration and teamwork.

3. (4 marks)

Outline four qualities of a good questionnaire.

Answer

Clarity: Questions should be phrased in simple, unambiguous language, easily understood by all respondents, avoiding jargon or technical terms.

Relevance: Questions must be directly related to the research objectives, ensuring that the data collected is pertinent and contributes to the study's goals.

Brevity: The questionnaire should be concise and to the point, respecting respondents' time and encouraging higher completion rates, avoiding unnecessary length or repetitive questions.

Objectivity: Questions should be neutral and unbiased, avoiding leading or loaded language that could influence responses, ensuring data reflects genuine opinions and attitudes.

4. (3 marks)

Highlight three roles played by members in a meeting.

Answer

Negotiating: Members participate in discussions to find mutually acceptable solutions and compromises on agenda items.

Proposing: Members contribute by suggesting new ideas, solutions, or courses of action relevant to the meeting objectives.

Affirming or opposing points: Members critically evaluate proposals and express their agreement or disagreement, contributing to balanced decision-making.

5. (3 marks)

Outline three reasons why job recruitment interviews are carried out in organizations.

Answer

To select the qualified person: Interviews allow employers to assess candidates' skills, experience, and qualifications to determine the best fit for the job requirements.

To get a good person with the qualities: Interviews help evaluate candidates' personality, attitude, and soft skills, ensuring they align with the organizational culture and team dynamics.

To determine the candidates you want: Interviews provide an opportunity to gauge candidates' motivation, career aspirations, and overall suitability for long-term contribution to the organization.

6. (3 marks)

State three advantages of face-to-face communication.

Answer

Saves time: Face-to-face interaction allows for immediate feedback and clarification, resolving issues and making decisions more quickly.

Immediate feedback: Non-verbal cues and instant responses enable communicators to gauge understanding and adjust their message in real-time.

Platform for interruption: Face-to-face settings facilitate natural interruptions for questions, clarifications, and deeper engagement in discussions.

7. (3 marks)

List three types of non-verbal communication.

Answer

Body language: This includes gestures, posture, facial expressions, and eye contact, which convey emotions and attitudes without words.

Space: Proxemics, or the use of personal space, communicates relationships and comfort levels between individuals.

Time: Chronemics, or the use of time, can convey messages about priorities, urgency, and respect in communication.

8. (3 marks)

Give three advantages of using the fax in an office.

Answer

Its accurate: Fax transmissions provide a reliable and verifiable record of sent documents, ensuring accuracy in business communications.

Faster transfer: Faxing offers a quick method for transmitting documents compared to traditional mail, facilitating timely information exchange.

Faster transfer: Faxing provides a reasonably fast method for transmitting documents, especially over long distances, compared to postal mail.

9. (4 marks)

Highlight four reasons for the increased use of mobile phones in the office.

Answer

Saves money: Mobile phones can reduce communication costs compared to traditional landlines, especially with bundled plans and data services.

It has immediate feedback: Mobile phones enable instant voice communication and text messaging, facilitating quick responses and real-time interaction.

Confidentiality of the message: Mobile communication can be made secure through encryption and private networks, ensuring confidential business exchanges.

It is easier to make for clarification of the message: Mobile phones facilitate easy follow-up calls or messages for immediate clarification and detailed discussions.

10. (3 marks)

State three qualities of a good report.

Answer

Should be clear: A good report is written in straightforward language, easily understood by the intended audience, avoiding ambiguity.

Should be brief: The report is concise and focused, presenting information efficiently without unnecessary detail or redundancy.

Should be interesting: While maintaining formality, a good report is engaging and well-structured, capturing the reader's attention and facilitating comprehension.

12. (a) (12 marks)

You are one of the students graduating at Miwanaisha Technical College. You have been asked to give a speech during the graduation ceremony on "The Benefits of Being Self-employed." in about 250 words, write the speech.

Answer

Esteemed members of the board, faculty, proud parents, and my fellow graduates, today marks not an end, but a commencement – the beginning of our journey into the vast world of opportunities. As we celebrate this milestone at Miwanaisha Technical College, I am honored to speak on a topic close to the heart of innovation and progress: "The Benefits of Being Self-Employed."

Self-employment, the path of entrepreneurship, offers unparalleled autonomy. It empowers us to be our own captains, charting our course and making decisions aligned with our vision. We gain control over our time, our projects, and our professional destiny. This freedom fosters creativity and allows us to pursue passions that ignite our entrepreneurial spirit.

Moreover, self-employment cultivates resilience and resourcefulness. It demands we wear multiple hats, learning diverse skills from marketing to finance, becoming versatile problem-solvers. This hands-on experience provides a practical education beyond textbooks, preparing us for the dynamic challenges of the modern economy.

The financial rewards of self-employment are directly proportional to our efforts and ingenuity. Unlike fixed salaries, our income potential is limitless, driven by our innovation and market demand. This prospect motivates us to strive for excellence and continuously adapt to evolving market needs.

Furthermore, self-employment contributes to societal growth by creating jobs and driving economic innovation. As entrepreneurs, we are not just job seekers but job creators, fostering prosperity and contributing to the nation's development. In conclusion, embracing self-employment is embracing freedom, resilience, and limitless potential, a path ripe with opportunity for us, the graduating class of Miwanaisha Technical College, to shape our futures and contribute meaningfully to the world.

12. (b) (6 marks)

Explain four factors that could make an interview process unsuccessful.

Answer

Poor communication skills: Inability to articulate thoughts clearly, listen attentively, or answer questions directly can significantly hinder an interviewee's chances of success.

Lack of preparation: Insufficient research about the company, role, or common interview questions can lead to vague answers and a perception of disinterest.

Negative attitude or body language: Projecting disinterest, arrogance, or nervousness through non-verbal cues can create a poor impression, overshadowing qualifications.

Mismatch of skills and experience: If a candidate's qualifications do not align with the job requirements, or if they fail to demonstrate relevant skills during the interview, the process will likely be unsuccessful.

13. (b) (10 marks)

Explain five consequences of poor communication in an organization.

Answer

Reduced productivity: Misunderstandings and unclear instructions lead to errors, rework, and delays, significantly hindering overall efficiency and output.

Decreased morale: Poor communication fosters confusion, frustration, and mistrust among employees, negatively impacting job satisfaction and team spirit.

Increased conflicts: Lack of clear communication channels and misinterpretations can escalate into interpersonal conflicts and departmental silos, disrupting workflow and collaboration.

Missed opportunities: Ineffective communication can cause organizations to miss critical market signals, innovative ideas, or customer feedback, leading to strategic disadvantages.

Poor management: Ineffective communication from leadership can result in a lack of direction, inconsistent policies, and failure to implement organizational goals effectively, undermining overall management effectiveness.

Passage

The Economic growth rate in Kenya for the year 2011-2012 could range between five and six per cent if no major shocks occur. However, the private sector has predicted that the successful completion of infrastructure and energy projects could see the economy grow at seven per cent during this period. According to the World Bank's Report on the Economic Prospects for Kenya, the seven per cent growth projected by the private sector is likely to depend on a number of factors.

To begin with are infrastructure projects that are likely to bring change in the economy. These include the construction of road and rail systems, Information Communication Technology (ICT) and the construction of geothermal energy plants. Transport and communication, energy and the cost of fuel are major implements of doing business. The infrastructure projects are an effort to address the three economic impediments and their successful completion could be a turning point for the Kenyan economy.

The successful construction of a number of roads is likely to speed up growth in the agriculture, horticulture and tourism sectors. This may significantly improve the economic performance from the predicted six per cent to seven per cent. The completion of a number of roads already under construction could make the country more attractive to investors and the cost of doing business is likely to go down. Some of the roads under construction include the Namanga-Athi River Road, Mombasa Road, Forest Road and Thika Road. Kenya's economic growth will also depend on investment in the geothermal energy projects earmarked for completion by 2015.

Another key driver for change in the country's economy is the ICT sector. It has been the main driver of Kenya's economic growth over the last decade. The sector has been growing on average by 20 per cent annually and has propelled the combined transport and communication sector into the economy's second largest after agriculture. Over the last three decades Kenya has experienced only two short episodes when economic growth exceeded five per cent and was sustained for at least three consecutive years: 1986 to 1988 and 2004 to 2007. The World Bank Report on Economic Prospects for Kenya indicates that since 2000, Kenya's economy grew at an average of four per cent. Without ICT, growth in 2010 would have been about three percent which is similar to the population growth rate. This would have meant that the income per capita would have stagnated.

While the public sector investments in infrastructure will help stimulate growth, the timely implementation of the constitution reforms would boost business confidence. Much of what is expected to be achieved will depend on how the country implements the constitution and how the country behaves politically in relation to the 2012 elections. The Kenya Association of Manufacturers (KAM) and the Federation of Kenya Employers (FKE) say that the implementation of the constitution will in essence reduce the country's political risk, while at the same time reassuring foreign and domestic investors that Kenya is a safe investment haven. Putting in place a new constitution cannot be entirely seen as a panacea to eradicating the country's political risk associated with each election year. However, this will assure investors thus reducing the capital flight and the slow-down of foreign direct investment experienced during general elections. Another factor that is expected to have a major impact on the country's economic growth, although negatively, is an increase in oil prices. Indeed, it has been indicated that this year might once again witness a return of runaway crude oil prices. This would be driven by increased global demand, a push for higher prices by oil producing nations, the return of speculators and increased tension in the Middle East. Several analysts have already predicted that oil prices could surpass the $100 per barrel mark. Although the government has so far introduced price controls in the local market to contain the uncontrolled increment of local prices by oil marketers, a surge in global prices will definitely not spare the country.

The East African Community (EAC) economic bloc might welcome a new member state, Southern Sudan, when the area separates from the control of the Khartoum government after the referendum. Southern Sudanese leaders have already expressed interest to join the EAC bloc. Admission of an additional state will translate to increased investment opportunities for Kenya which is the strongest economy in the bloc. In addition, this year could see the signing of a comprehensive protocol that will define trade between the EAC and the European Union. Hence, the long running negotiations on the Economic Partnership Agreements could finally come to a close.

Adapted from: The Financial Journal, The Standard, Tuesday, 11 January, 2011.

14. (a) (10 marks)

In about 150 words and according to the passage, explain the factors that are likely to influence Kenya's economic growth rate during the period under review.

Answer

According to the passage, Kenya's economic growth rate for 2011-2012 is influenced by several key factors. Infrastructure projects, including roads, rail, ICT, and geothermal energy plants, are crucial for driving economic change by addressing economic impediments and improving business operations (Paragraph 2). Successful completion of road construction is expected to boost agriculture, horticulture, and tourism, potentially increasing economic performance from six to seven percent (Paragraph 3). The ICT sector is also highlighted as a major driver, having propelled economic growth over the past decade (Paragraph 4). Furthermore, the implementation of constitutional reforms is expected to boost business confidence and reduce political risk, attracting both foreign and domestic investment (Paragraph 5). Conversely, rising global oil prices are identified as a negative factor that could hinder economic growth, despite government price controls (Paragraph 5). Lastly, the potential admission of Southern Sudan into the EAC and trade agreements with the EU could create increased investment opportunities, positively influencing Kenya’s growth as the strongest economy in the bloc (Paragraph 6). These factors collectively shape Kenya's economic outlook for the period.

14. (b) (5 marks)

Give the meaning of the following words and phrase as used in the passage.

Answer

**(i) shocks:**

In the context of the passage, "shocks" refers to unexpected and disruptive events that could negatively impact Kenya's economic growth rate. These are unforeseen occurrences that can destabilize economic projections.

**(ii) impediments:**

"Impediments" means obstacles or hindrances that slow down or prevent economic progress. The passage refers to infrastructure projects addressing "economic impediments," indicating that these projects aim to remove barriers to economic development.

**(iii) propelled:**

"Propelled" means driven forward or advanced significantly. The ICT sector is described as having "propelled" the transport and communication sector, signifying that ICT has been a major force in advancing these sectors.

**(iv) surpass:**

"Surpass" means to exceed or go beyond. Analysts predict that oil prices could "surpass" $100 per barrel, indicating they expect prices to rise above this level.

**(v) come to a close:**

"Come to a close" means to reach an end or conclusion. The passage mentions that long-running negotiations on Economic Partnership Agreements could "finally come to a close," indicating a potential resolution to these discussions.
