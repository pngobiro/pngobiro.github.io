COMMUNICATION

Exam Code: 2902/104, 2908/104, 2912/104, 2918/104, 2920/104, 2921/104

Duration: 3 hours

Period: November 2017

1. (3 marks)

List three features of appraisal interviews.

Answer

Performance Review: Appraisal interviews systematically evaluate an employee's job performance over a defined period, typically annually or semi-annually.

Feedback and Goal Setting: They provide a structured platform for managers to give employees feedback on their strengths and areas for improvement, and to collaboratively set future performance goals.

Two-Way Communication: Appraisal interviews encourage a two-way dialogue where employees can express their perspectives, discuss challenges, and contribute to their performance development plan.

2. (4 marks)

List four instances when an apology letter may be written by an organization.

Answer

Service Failure: When the organization fails to deliver expected services or products, leading to customer dissatisfaction or inconvenience, an apology letter is appropriate.

Mistakes or Errors: If the organization makes a mistake, whether in billing, communication, or operations, an apology letter acknowledges the error and expresses regret.

Delays or Inconvenience: When organizational actions or inactions cause delays, disruptions, or inconvenience to stakeholders, an apology letter can mitigate negative impacts.

Ethical Lapses or Misconduct: In cases of ethical breaches or misconduct by organizational members, an apology letter demonstrates accountability and commitment to ethical standards.

3. (3 marks)

State three reasons why organizations may prefer written reports.

Answer

Documentation and Record Keeping: Written reports provide a permanent, documented record of information, findings, and recommendations for future reference and accountability.

Clarity and Precision: Written reports allow for careful drafting and editing, ensuring clear, precise, and unambiguous communication of complex or detailed information.

Wide Distribution and Accessibility: Written reports can be easily distributed to a large audience and accessed at any time, ensuring consistent information dissemination across the organization.

4. (3 marks)

Outline three disadvantages of holding meetings in organizations.

Answer

Time Consumption: Meetings can be time-consuming, taking away valuable time from productive work, especially if poorly planned or unnecessarily prolonged.

Costly Resources: Meetings involve direct costs such as venue, equipment, and refreshments, as well as indirect costs like employee time, making them potentially expensive.

Potential for Inefficiency: Poorly managed meetings can be unproductive, leading to unfocused discussions, unresolved issues, and a sense of wasted effort among participants.

5. (3 marks)

State three roles of external communication in an organization.

Answer

Public Relations and Image Management: External communication shapes and manages the organization's public image and reputation, building goodwill and positive perceptions among stakeholders.

Marketing and Sales Promotion: It promotes the organization's products or services to potential customers, generating leads, driving sales, and enhancing market presence.

Stakeholder Engagement and Relationship Building: External communication facilitates interaction and relationship building with external stakeholders including customers, investors, and the community, fostering mutual understanding and support.

6. (3 marks)

State three reasons for selecting communication media carefully.

Answer

Message Effectiveness: Choosing the right medium ensures the message is delivered effectively, maximizing clarity, impact, and audience comprehension.

Audience Reach and Accessibility: Careful media selection ensures the message reaches the intended audience and is accessible to them in a convenient and appropriate format.

Cost and Resource Efficiency: Selecting the most suitable medium optimizes resource utilization and minimizes communication costs while achieving desired communication objectives.

7. (3 marks)

State three factors that determine the appropriate time to make a telephone call.

Answer

Urgency and Importance of the Message: Highly urgent or critical information requiring immediate attention and real-time interaction necessitates a phone call for prompt communication.

Recipient Availability and Time Zone: Considering the recipient's working hours, time zone, and potential availability ensures the call is made at a convenient and receptive time.

Need for Personal Interaction and Tone: When conveying sensitive information, building rapport, or requiring nuanced communication, a phone call's personal touch and tone are more effective than written media.

8. (3 marks)

List three reasons why a speaker should maintain eye contact when communicating.

Answer

Builds Credibility and Trust: Maintaining eye contact enhances the speaker's credibility and trustworthiness, projecting confidence and sincerity to the audience.

Engages Audience and Maintains Attention: Eye contact establishes a direct connection with audience members, capturing their attention, and fostering engagement throughout the communication.

Receives Non-verbal Feedback: Speaker eye contact allows observation of audience reactions and non-verbal cues, providing real-time feedback on message reception and audience engagement.

9. (4 marks)

List four forms of visual communication which may be used to pass information.

Answer

Graphs and Charts: Visual representations of data and relationships, such as bar graphs, pie charts, and line graphs, effectively communicate quantitative information.

Photographs and Illustrations: Images, photographs, and illustrations visually depict objects, scenes, or concepts, enhancing understanding and engagement.

Posters and Infographics: Visually appealing displays combining text and graphics to convey concise information, instructions, or key messages in a readily digestible format.

Videos and Animations: Moving visuals, videos, and animations effectively demonstrate processes, tell stories, or present complex information in an engaging and dynamic manner.

10. (3 marks)

State three instances when a sender may prefer to use e-mail to pass information.

Answer

Formal and Official Communication: For transmitting formal announcements, official documents, or records that require documentation and traceability, email is preferred.

Non-Urgent or Asynchronous Communication: When the message is not time-sensitive and does not require immediate response, email allows for asynchronous communication, accommodating different schedules.

Mass Distribution to Large Groups: Email is efficient for disseminating information to a large number of recipients simultaneously, ensuring consistent message delivery to a broad audience.

11. (6 marks)

(b) Outline three ways of capturing the attention of the audience when delivering a speech.

Answer

Start with a Compelling Hook: Begin the speech with a captivating opening, such as a surprising statistic, a thought-provoking question, a relevant anecdote, or a strong statement to immediately pique audience interest.

Use Engaging Storytelling: Incorporate relatable stories, personal experiences, or case studies into the speech to illustrate points, evoke emotions, and make the content more memorable and impactful for the audience.

Maintain Dynamic Delivery: Vary vocal tone, pace, and volume; use gestures and movement purposefully; and incorporate pauses effectively to create a dynamic and engaging speaking style that holds the audience's attention.

12. (8 marks)

(a) Explain four ways in which the chairperson may ensure the success of a meeting.

Answer

Prepare and Distribute a Clear Agenda: The chairperson should create a detailed agenda outlining meeting objectives, topics for discussion, and time allocation for each item, distributing it in advance to ensure participants come prepared and focused.

Facilitate Participation and Manage Discussions: The chairperson should actively encourage participation from all attendees, manage discussions to stay on topic, ensure everyone has a chance to speak, and mediate conflicts constructively to maintain a productive meeting environment.

Ensure Time Management and Adherence to Agenda: The chairperson must effectively manage meeting time, sticking to the agenda and allocated time slots for each item, keeping discussions concise, and ensuring the meeting concludes within the scheduled timeframe.

Summarize Decisions and Action Items Clearly: At the end of the meeting, the chairperson should summarize key decisions made, action items assigned, responsible parties, and deadlines, ensuring clear understanding and accountability for follow-up actions among all participants.

12. (8 marks)

(b) Explain four ways in which the receiver may minimise barriers to oral communication.

Answer

Practice Active Listening: The receiver should focus intently on the speaker, paying attention to both verbal and non-verbal cues, minimizing distractions, and demonstrating engagement through nodding and appropriate facial expressions to fully understand the message.

Seek Clarification and Ask Questions: If any part of the message is unclear or ambiguous, the receiver should proactively ask clarifying questions to ensure accurate understanding, rather than making assumptions or misinterpreting information.

Manage Emotional Reactions and Biases: The receiver should be aware of their own emotional state and potential biases, striving to remain objective and open-minded, avoiding premature judgments or emotional responses that could hinder effective listening.

Choose an Appropriate Listening Environment: The receiver should attempt to minimize external noise and distractions by selecting a quiet and conducive environment for communication, ensuring optimal conditions for focused listening and effective message reception.

13. (16 marks)

The directors of Ukulima Agribusiness Limited have realized that a number of its distributors have terminated their contracts over the last one year. As the Assistant Office Manager, you have been asked to investigate the matter and write a report with recommendations. Assume that you have completed your investigations and write the report.

Answer

REPORT ON DISTRIBUTOR CONTRACT TERMINATIONS AT UKULIMA AGRIBUSINESS LIMITED

To: The Directors, Ukulima Agribusiness Limited
From: [Your Name], Assistant Office Manager
Date: November 28, 2017
Subject: Investigation into Distributor Contract Terminations and Recommendations for Retention

Executive Summary:
This report addresses the recent concern regarding a significant number of distributor contract terminations at Ukulima Agribusiness Limited over the past year. Commissioned by the Directors, this investigation aims to identify the underlying reasons for these terminations and propose actionable recommendations to improve distributor relations and reduce future attrition. The report is based on data analysis, distributor interviews, and market assessment.

1. Introduction:
Ukulima Agribusiness Limited has experienced a notable increase in distributor contract terminations in the last twelve months. This report seeks to investigate the causes behind this trend, providing a comprehensive analysis and proposing strategies to enhance distributor satisfaction and loyalty.

2. Methodology:
The investigation employed a mixed-methods approach to gather relevant data:

Termination Data Analysis: Review of records of distributor contract terminations over the past year, identifying patterns, geographical concentrations, and common characteristics among terminated distributors.

Distributor Interviews: Confidential interviews conducted with a sample of both terminated and active distributors to gather insights into their experiences, concerns, and reasons for termination or continued partnership.

Competitor Benchmarking: Analysis of competitor distributor relationship management strategies, incentives, and support systems to identify industry best practices and areas where Ukulima Agribusiness Limited may be lagging.

Internal Stakeholder Consultations: Discussions with sales, logistics, and support staff within Ukulima Agribusiness Limited to gather internal perspectives on distributor relations and operational challenges.

3. Findings:
The investigation revealed several interconnected factors contributing to distributor contract terminations:

Reduced Profit Margins: Distributors reported declining profit margins due to increased operational costs, fluctuating market prices, and perceived inadequate compensation structures.

Logistical and Supply Chain Issues: Frequent delays in product delivery, inconsistent product availability, and inefficient logistical support were cited as major frustrations by distributors.

Inadequate Communication and Support: Distributors expressed dissatisfaction with communication from Ukulima Agribusiness Limited, citing lack of timely updates, slow response to queries, and insufficient marketing and sales support.

Contractual Terms and Flexibility: Some distributors found contractual terms to be rigid and unfavorable, lacking flexibility to adapt to changing market conditions or individual business needs.

Increased Competition from Other Suppliers: Distributors indicated they were being approached by competitor agribusiness firms offering more attractive terms, better support, or higher profit potential.

4. Recommendations:
To address the distributor contract terminations and improve retention, the following recommendations are proposed:

Review and Enhance Distributor Compensation: Conduct a comprehensive review of distributor profit margins and compensation structures to ensure competitiveness and profitability, potentially introducing performance-based incentives and bonuses.

Improve Supply Chain and Logistics Efficiency: Streamline supply chain operations, enhance inventory management, and optimize logistics to ensure timely product delivery, consistent availability, and reduced operational disruptions for distributors.

Strengthen Communication and Support Systems: Establish dedicated communication channels for distributors, improve response times to queries, provide regular updates, and offer enhanced marketing, sales, and technical support resources.

Offer Flexible Contractual Terms: Review and revise distributor contracts to incorporate more flexible terms, allowing for adjustments based on market dynamics and distributor performance, fostering a more collaborative partnership approach.

Build Stronger Distributor Relationships: Implement relationship management programs, including regular feedback sessions, distributor recognition initiatives, and collaborative business planning, to build trust and loyalty.

5. Conclusion:
Distributor contract terminations at Ukulima Agribusiness Limited are driven by a combination of factors including declining profitability, logistical challenges, communication gaps, contractual inflexibility, and increased competitive pressures. Implementing the recommendations outlined in this report will enable Ukulima Agribusiness Limited to enhance distributor satisfaction, improve retention rates, and foster stronger, more sustainable partnerships. Continuous monitoring and evaluation of distributor relations are crucial for long-term success.

6. Next Steps:
It is recommended that the Directors form a Distributor Relations Task Force, comprising representatives from sales, logistics, operations, and finance departments, to develop a detailed action plan and budget for implementing these recommendations. Priority should be given to addressing distributor compensation and logistical challenges.

Passage

Business leaders often struggle to ensure that their employees are productive. Measuring employee productivity is not easy and a number of applications have been designed to help do it. However, in the absence of the gadgets which can measure productivity, employers turn to monitoring behavioural aspects such as the time spent in the office, late arrival to work, early departure from work, or if the staff are having too many conversations when they meet at common places such as washrooms. Nevertheless, these observable behavioural characteristics. are by no means a measure of productivity.

It is estimated that employees spend an average of five hours per day at their desks. However, this does not mean that they spend the entire five hours being productive. Contrary to what many people believe, a culture of long working hours does not necessarily translate into more input. In fact, such a culture may lead to staff burnout and an upsurge of sick leaves. A study of 600,000 individuals in Australia, the US and Europe found that people who work for more than 55 hours a week have a 33 per cent greater risk of suffering a stroke and 13 per cent greater risk of suffering from coronary heart disease. The study concludes that employees need to pay more attention to the health risk of long working hours.

Research findings show that more people are stressed today than ever before. A study in the US revealed that 40 per cent of employees feel that their jobs are a major cause of stress. The study further showed that 73 per cent of employees regularly experience psychological symptoms caused by stress and that workplace stress costs employers $300 billion annually. These statistics call for employers to create healthy and productive work environments in their organizations.

In order to create a healthy and productive work environment, a number of measures need to be put in place. To begin with, an employer should understand the current situation in the workplace by getting feedback from staff. An annual staff survey would help the employer to know what employees are experiencing and understand what hinders or helps in their performance. The survey would also provide data that would enable the employer to focus on the crucial areas as well as a benchmark to measure improvement in the successive years.

In addition, encouraging staff to be active would help to maintain employee's physical and mental health. Exercise brings many benefits to employees including stress relief, mood improvement, energy boost as well as combating diseases. Further, the employer can encourage open and honest conversations as a way of relieving stress. Unhealthy work relationships such as bullying and harassment have been found to be a major source of stress. The employer should therefore aim to create an environment where conflict is dealt with in a healthy way and problems are addressed as they arise.

Besides, ensuring that employees are clear about their roles and what is expected of them will enable them to focus on what needs to be achieved as opposed to constantly questioning if they are doing the right thing. This is because lack of clarity of roles and responsibilities can be a major stress trigger. Moreover, employees need a sense of purpose and clear direction in order to be productive. Employees should thus be made to understand not only what needs to be done but when it needs to be done. Linking goals to the bigger picture provides a meaningful context to the employee. Furthermore, employers should motivate employees to take personal initiative to minimise work-related stress.

In order to have a productive workforce, therefore, employers need to take various measures. Such measures include restructuring the methods of gauging work done and placing more emphasis on employee wellbeing among others. If such measures are put in place, work-related stress will be reduced to a bare minimum thus saving employers a lot of resources which have hitherto been spent on medical bills.

14. (6 marks)

(a) State the meaning of each of the following words and phrases as used in the passage: (i) measure; (ii) spend; (iii) regularly; (iv) hinders; (v) as they arise; (vi) work done.

Answer

(i) measure: In the context of the passage, "measure" refers to the act of assessing or quantifying employee productivity, indicating the challenge of evaluating output.

(ii) spend: As used in the passage, "spend" refers to the time employees allocate or use during their workday at their desks, highlighting the duration of desk work.

(iii) regularly: In the passage, "regularly" means frequently or routinely, describing the consistent experience of psychological symptoms by a majority of employees due to stress.

(iv) hinders: "Hinders" means obstructs or impedes, indicating that certain factors in the workplace can impede or negatively affect employee performance and productivity.

(v) as they arise: "As they arise" means immediately or promptly when problems occur, emphasizing the need to address conflicts and issues in a timely manner within the workplace.

(vi) work done: "Work done" refers to the output or tasks completed by employees, suggesting a need to restructure methods of evaluating employee performance and output.

14. (12 marks)

(b) In about 130 words and according to the passage, explain the ways in which an employer may create a healthy and productive work environment.

Answer

To foster a healthy and productive work environment, employers should first assess the workplace situation through annual staff surveys to understand employee experiences and identify factors hindering or helping performance. Encouraging staff activity is crucial for physical and mental health, offering stress relief and energy boost. Open communication should be promoted to relieve stress, addressing unhealthy relationships like bullying. Clarity of roles and responsibilities is essential to reduce stress from uncertainty. Employees need purpose and direction, understanding not just tasks but their broader impact. Employers should restructure productivity measurement, emphasizing employee wellbeing and addressing conflicts promptly to minimize work-related stress and medical costs, ultimately creating a supportive and efficient workforce.
