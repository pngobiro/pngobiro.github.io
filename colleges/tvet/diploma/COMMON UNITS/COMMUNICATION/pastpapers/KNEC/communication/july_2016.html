July 2016

COMMUNICATION

Exam Code: 2902/104

Duration: 3 hours

Period: July 2016

1. (3 marks)

State three challenges a sender may encounter when encoding a message.

Answer

Lack of clarity: The sender may struggle to express their ideas clearly and concisely, leading to a message that is ambiguous or confusing.

Choice of medium: Selecting an inappropriate communication channel can hinder effective encoding, for example, using written communication for a highly emotional message.

Cultural differences: The sender's cultural background may influence their encoding process in ways that are not understood or misinterpreted by receivers from different cultures.

2. (3 marks)

List three guidelines to follow when designing a questionnaire for report writing.

Answer

Clear and concise questions: Ensure questions are easy to understand and avoid jargon or ambiguous language.

Logical flow and structure: Organize questions in a logical sequence, grouping related questions together and starting with easy questions.

Appropriate question types: Use a mix of question types (e.g., open-ended, multiple-choice, rating scales) suitable for the information needed and the respondents.

3. (4 marks)

State four possible reasons why an interviewee may fail to get a job despite having the right academic qualifications.

Answer

Poor communication skills: Inability to articulate thoughts clearly, listen effectively, or engage in professional conversation can negatively impact the interviewer's perception.

Lack of relevant experience: Even with qualifications, absence of practical experience or demonstrable skills related to the job can be a disadvantage.

Inadequate personality or cultural fit: The interviewee's personality or work style may not align with the company culture or the team dynamics.

Weak interview performance: Poor body language, lack of enthusiasm, insufficient preparation, or negative attitude during the interview can lead to rejection.

4. (4 marks)

Outline four roles that the chair of a meeting must play to ensure the success of the meeting.

Answer

Setting the agenda and objectives: The chair must define the meeting's purpose and create a structured agenda to guide discussions and ensure focus.

Facilitating discussion: The chair should encourage participation from all members, manage contributions, and keep the discussion on topic and within the allocated time.

Maintaining order and decorum: The chair is responsible for ensuring respectful communication, managing conflicts, and adhering to meeting rules and procedures.

Summarizing decisions and action points: The chair must clearly summarize key decisions, agreed actions, and responsibilities at the end of the meeting to ensure clarity and follow-up.

5. (3 marks)

State three purposes of writing reports in an organization.

Answer

Information dissemination: Reports serve to communicate findings, analysis, or progress on projects to relevant stakeholders within the organization.

Decision-making support: Reports provide data and insights that assist managers in making informed decisions and strategic planning.

Record keeping and accountability: Reports document activities, outcomes, and recommendations for future reference and to track responsibilities and performance.

6. (3 marks)

List three advantages of using visual aids in communication.

Answer

Enhanced understanding: Visual aids can simplify complex information, making it easier for the audience to grasp and remember key points.

Increased engagement: Visuals capture attention and maintain audience interest, making communication more dynamic and less monotonous.

Improved retention: Visual elements reinforce verbal messages, aiding memory and recall of information by the audience.

7. (3 marks)

State three barriers to effective oral communication.

Answer

Noise and distractions: External or internal noise, such as physical sounds or psychological distractions, can interfere with the clear reception of the message.

Language differences: Variations in language proficiency, accents, or use of jargon can create misunderstandings between speakers and listeners.

Non-verbal misinterpretations: Incongruence between verbal and non-verbal cues, or misinterpretation of body language, can lead to confusion and ineffective communication.

8. (3 marks)

State three ways in which technology is used to transmit information in organizations.

Answer

Email communication: For formal and informal correspondence, sharing documents, and disseminating information widely within and outside the organization.

Intranet and company portals: For internal communication, sharing company news, policies, resources, and facilitating employee collaboration.

Video conferencing and online meeting tools: For virtual meetings, presentations, training sessions, and real-time collaboration across different locations.

9. (3 marks)

Identify three instances when an employee may need to communicate with the manager.

Answer

Seeking clarification or guidance: When an employee needs direction on tasks, understanding of instructions, or resolution of work-related issues.

Reporting progress or problems: To update the manager on project status, achievements, challenges, or potential risks.

Requesting resources or support: When an employee requires tools, information, approvals, or assistance to perform their duties effectively.

10. (3 marks)

State three ways of ensuring that employees read notices.

Answer

Strategic placement: Posting notices in high-traffic areas where employees frequently pass, such as break rooms, entrances, or near time clocks.

Eye-catching design: Using clear headings, concise language, and visual elements to make notices more noticeable and easier to read.

Digital distribution and reminders: Sending notices via email or company intranet with read receipts and follow-up reminders to ensure visibility and acknowledgement.

- buying land for resale;

- recruiting new members;

- revising the monthly contributions.

As the secretary of the group, write the minutes.

11. (b) (6 marks)

Explain three reasons why interpersonal communication should be encouraged in an organization.

Answer

Enhanced team collaboration: Interpersonal communication fosters better understanding, trust, and rapport among team members, leading to more effective teamwork and cooperation.

Improved employee morale and satisfaction: Open and supportive interpersonal communication creates a positive work environment, boosting employee morale, job satisfaction, and loyalty.

Effective conflict resolution: Strong interpersonal communication skills enable employees to address disagreements constructively, resolve conflicts amicably, and maintain positive working relationships.

As the Welfare Officer, you have been asked to investigate the matter and write a report giving your recommendations. Assume that you have completed the investigations and write the report.

Passage for Question 14

A high number of matatu crews still use cash despite the expiry of the 1 December 2014 deadline for public transport to use cashless methods in payment of fare. Apparently, the matatu owners are unmoved by the stipulated Ksh. 100,000 fine against those who fail to change to the new system.

Opinion is still divided among the public on the new method of payment with some embracing while others seem reluctant to adopt it. The world's financial markets, however, are going cashless and Kenya has no choice but to follow suit. Experts say that digital payments will bring a lot of benefits. Since Kenya leads in the use of mobile money services in the region, going cashless in all aspects of the economy is inevitable.

The CEO and founder of Tangazoletu Limited, a firm that automates business processes and provides mobile financial products, says that the journey to digital payments has just started. "As much as cash has been the universal language, Kenyans need to be prepared to adopt a cashless system that is beneficial to the consumers, corporates and the government who are basically the key drivers of the economy. We are very innovative, and as long as we take gradual steps, the move to a cashless system is possible," he said.

A research conducted by Financial Sector Deepening (FSD) Kenya in mid-2012 showed that electronic payments cost between 50 and 65 per cent less than paper payments. This would translate to over Ksh. 90 billion annually in savings for Kenya. The figure is arrived at using the Kenya National Bureau of Statistics' Gross Domestic Product (GDP) figures for 2013 revised in September 2014 from Ksh. 3.8 trillion to Ksh. 4.76 trillion.

The figures underline the fact that cash payments are expensive. Even withdrawing cash in banks has a cost involved. The FSD survey showed that on average, people spend between 20 and 80 minutes to carry out banking services involving cash in Kenya's urban areas. Rural folks spend even more time travelling to carry out banking transactions.

Proponents of a cashless system point out various problems encountered when dealing with cash. First and foremost, carrying cash is dangerous as one is vulnerable to theft. People handling cash are left counting more losses than those with cards or mobile money in case of a robbery. In addition, transporting large surns of money is always a tense affair despite the armed escort. It is also costly because the transporters have to be paid. The money further requires to be insured against risks such as robberies.

Another problem is that cash is susceptible to fire and con men unlike a card which, even if destroyed or lost, the money remains safe somewhere. Besides, one can fall prey to con artists who peddle fake currencies. A cash system makes it hard for the tax man to curb tax evasion unlike a cashless system which is easier to trace.

FSD also found out that most Kenyans easily engage in impulse buying when they have cash. Electronic payments instil some level of discipline because the act of having to withdraw cash forces one to think about what one needs to buy. Further, it is harder for consumers to control and track their expenditure when using cash as opposed to dealing with a cashless system.

According to Central Bank estimates as at August 2014, more than 26 million Kenyans have adopted mobile money in purchasing, transfer and banking. The Communication Authority of Kenya (CAK) puts the number of mobile subscribers at 31.8 million, 80 per cent of whom use mobile money services. The figure is likely to rise as Equity Bank has rolled out its own Mobile Virtual Network Operator (MVNO) platform. Under the system, the bank's customers will use their handsets to transfer cash and buy goods using the money in their accounts. Globally, the financial market has shifted tremendously in the past three decades with the use of the cashless system rapidly increasing. People have turned to easier ways of transacting business especially with the introduction of mobile money.

Adapted from: 'Daily Nation', Tuesday December 9, 2014.

14. (a) (12 marks)

In about 120 words, and according to the passage, explain the various problems encountered when dealing with cash.

Answer

According to the passage, dealing with cash presents several problems. Firstly, it is dangerous due to vulnerability to theft, making handlers susceptible to robbery and financial losses (Paragraph 6). Secondly, transporting large amounts of cash is risky and costly, requiring armed escorts and insurance (Paragraph 6). Cash is also susceptible to fire and counterfeiting, unlike digital money (Paragraph 7). Furthermore, cash transactions complicate tax collection and facilitate tax evasion (Paragraph 7). Impulse buying is more prevalent with cash, as electronic payments encourage more conscious spending habits (Paragraph 8). Finally, tracking and controlling expenditure is harder with cash compared to cashless systems (Paragraph 8).

14. (b) (6 marks)

State the meaning of the following words and phrases as used in the passage:

(i) follow suit;

(ii) beneficial;

(iii) underline;

(iv) are left counting;

(v) curb;

(vi) shifted.

Answer

Follow suit: In the passage, 'follow suit' means to imitate or do the same as others, indicating Kenya's need to adopt cashless systems like global markets.

Beneficial: 'Beneficial' means advantageous or favorable, describing the cashless system as advantageous to consumers, corporates, and the government.

Underline: 'Underline' means to emphasize or highlight, indicating that the figures emphasize the expensiveness of cash payments.

Are left counting: 'Are left counting' means experiencing or facing, referring to people handling cash facing more losses in case of robbery.

Curb: 'Curb' means to restrain or control, indicating that a cash system makes it hard to control tax evasion.

Shifted: 'Shifted' means changed or moved, describing the financial market's significant change towards cashless systems.
