November 2016

COMMUNICATION

Exam Code: 2902/104

Duration: 3 hours

Period: November 2016

1. (3 marks)

List three types of minutes that may be written in a meeting.

Answer

Resolution minutes: These record the motions and resolutions passed during the meeting.

Narrative minutes: These provide a detailed account of the discussions and proceedings of the meeting.

Action minutes: These focus on the tasks assigned and decisions made, outlining who is responsible for what and by when.

2. (4 marks)

State four factors which may cause inaccurate interpretation of a message.

Answer

Cultural differences: Varying cultural backgrounds can lead to different understandings of message context and non-verbal cues.

Language barriers: Use of jargon, complex language, or translation issues can obscure the intended meaning of the message.

Emotional state: The receiver's emotional state (e.g., stress, anger) can filter the message and distort its interpretation.

Noise and distractions: Physical or psychological noise can interfere with the clear reception and processing of the message, leading to misinterpretation.

3. (3 marks)

List three roles of inter-departmental communication in an organization.

Answer

Coordination of tasks: Facilitates the smooth flow of information and resources between departments to ensure tasks are completed efficiently and effectively.

Problem-solving: Enables departments to share information and collaborate on solutions to organizational challenges and issues.

Strategic alignment: Ensures that all departments are aware of the organization's overall goals and work together to achieve them, promoting a unified direction.

4. (3 marks)

State three instances when electronic mail may be used to pass information.

Answer

Disseminating routine updates and announcements to a large group of employees or stakeholders.

Sharing documents and files for review or collaboration, especially when recipients are geographically dispersed.

Confirming appointments, sending reminders, and scheduling meetings efficiently.

5. (3 marks)

Outline three reasons for conducting exit interviews.

Answer

Gathering feedback: To obtain insights into employee experiences, reasons for leaving, and suggestions for improvement within the organization.

Identifying trends: To analyze patterns in employee turnover and pinpoint systemic issues that may be contributing to employee dissatisfaction.

Improving retention: To use the feedback to implement changes and strategies aimed at enhancing employee satisfaction and reducing future turnover rates.

6. (3 marks)

State three advantages of using several data collection methods when writing a report.

Answer

Triangulation of data: Using multiple methods increases the reliability and validity of findings by cross-verifying information from different sources.

Comprehensive understanding: Different methods can capture diverse aspects of the issue, providing a richer and more holistic view of the subject matter.

Reduced bias: Employing a variety of methods minimizes the limitations and biases inherent in any single data collection technique, leading to more balanced and objective reports.

7. (4 marks)

List four uses of visual aids when delivering a speech.

Answer

Enhancing audience engagement: Visual aids capture attention and maintain interest, making the speech more dynamic and less monotonous.

Clarifying complex information: Charts, graphs, and diagrams can simplify intricate data or concepts, improving audience understanding.

Improving retention: Visual elements reinforce verbal messages, aiding memory and recall of key points by the audience.

Adding visual appeal: Well-designed visuals make presentations more professional and aesthetically pleasing, enhancing the speaker's credibility and message impact.

8. (3 marks)

State three reasons why it is important for the listener to maintain eye contact with the speaker.

Answer

Demonstrates attentiveness: Eye contact signals to the speaker that the listener is engaged, focused, and paying attention to the message.

Builds rapport and trust: Maintaining eye contact fosters a connection between the speaker and listener, enhancing trust and credibility.

Provides feedback to the speaker: Listener's eye contact offers non-verbal cues that inform the speaker about audience reception and understanding, enabling them to adjust their delivery accordingly.

9. (3 marks)

Outline three instances when a map may be used in company reports.

Answer

Sales performance reports: Maps can visually represent sales data across different regions, highlighting high-performing areas and areas needing improvement.

Logistics and distribution reports: Maps can illustrate supply chains, distribution networks, and delivery routes, aiding in optimizing logistics operations.

Market research reports: Maps can display demographic data, market penetration, and competitor locations, providing geographical context to market analysis.

10. (3 marks)

List three ways in which information communication technology increases effectiveness in communication.

Answer

Increased speed and reach: ICT tools like email, instant messaging, and video conferencing enable rapid and widespread dissemination of information, overcoming geographical barriers.

Enhanced collaboration: Platforms for online collaboration, document sharing, and project management facilitate teamwork and communication across teams and departments.

Improved accessibility and storage: Digital communication methods provide easy access to information anytime, anywhere, and offer efficient storage and retrieval of communication records.

13. (b) (6 marks)

Describe four organizational barriers that may hinder effective flow of information.

Answer

Hierarchical levels: Multiple layers of management can slow down information flow, distort messages as they pass through different levels, and create communication silos.

Lack of trust and openness: A culture of secrecy or fear of feedback can discourage employees from sharing information openly, leading to incomplete or inaccurate communication.

Geographical distance: Physical separation between departments or branches can impede regular and informal communication, creating misunderstandings and delays.

Information overload: Excessive amounts of information can overwhelm employees, making it difficult to prioritize, process, and respond effectively to critical messages, resulting in communication breakdown.

Passage for Question 14

Fraud is the act of deceiving a person or an organization with the intention of getting financial or personal gain. It has become a very common occurrence in the world today. According to a survey conducted on online payments, millions of people are defrauded each year. Online payments are fast gaining acceptance due to speedy processing of credit cards, easy access of payment portals and immediate notifications of transactions among others. Online payments occur throughout and the fund transfer happens on immediate basis.

With increased access to the Internet in the world today, more people than before are using computers. These computers can be generally classified into two categories: private and public. Private computers are those that are used at home and in offices. Públic computers are found in cyber cafes, coffee shops and other public places. In addition, wireless networks are also everywhere; many of them free or available for a small fee. This availability of computers and networks makes it very easy to purchase online, check mail, chat with friends or even balance cheque books from anywhere. Using public computers to make online transactions can pose dangers. Thus caution is required since it is possible for someone to access vital information illegally. This is because one may not know what software or hardware has been installed in the public computers. Some software, for example, can capture one's username and password or other account information.

Regardless of the dangers posed by use of computers for online activities, various measures could be instituted to ensure fraud-free online transactions. One such measure is shunning public places and networks when making any online transactions This is because there are chances that someone may see personal information displayed on the screen Worse still, an intruder can grab sensitive information sent over the wireless network.

Furthermore, there is need to desist from the use of public computers as such computers may have programs that can copy keystrokes as well as other software that can capture one's sensitive information, Individuals should therefore, strive to make internet transactions using private computers and networks.

Another measure to combat fraud is the use of credit cards instead of debit cards. This is because debit transactions are riskier than credit transactions. In debit transactions, criminals can easily drain one's bank account making it possible for them to spend the money quickly and thus thwarting any efforts to unravel the theft in time-This is unlike in a credit card theft which is less disastrous as one'd credit card company can help to dig into the matter. In addition, if one has reservations about giving out one's credit card number online, then it is advisable to use third party services such as M-Pesa thus reducing chances of such online theft.

On the other hand it is necessary that one's computers are securely updated with antivirus, antispyware and firewall software programs since this could also serve as deterrents to online fraud Moreover, keeping accurate records which reflect details of online transactions can be of value in combating online viee. Such records will serve as evidence of one's purchases if problems occur. Though it may be a requirement at times that one uses personal data such as date of birth and ID number, it should be noted that most legitimate websites will not ask one to give out personal information. By intercepting such information, criminals can have enough data to apply for new credit cards in the victim's name consequently being able to defraud by carrying out online transactions without being detected. Therefore, one should always be cautious not to reveal such crucial details when carrying out online transactions.

14. (a) (6 marks)

State the meaning of the following words and phrases as used in the passage:

(i) classified;

(ii) instituted;

(iii) desist from;

(iv) combat;

(v) of value;

(vi) cautious.

Answer

Classified: In the passage, 'classified' means categorized or divided into groups, referring to the categorization of computers into private and public.

Instituted: 'Instituted' means established or introduced, referring to measures that could be established to ensure fraud-free online transactions.

Desist from: 'Desist from' means to abstain or refrain from, advising individuals to refrain from using public computers for online transactions.

Combat: 'Combat' means to counteract or fight against, referring to measures to counteract fraud.

Of value: 'Of value' means important or useful, highlighting the importance of keeping accurate records of online transactions.

Cautious: 'Cautious' means careful and prudent, advising individuals to be careful about revealing crucial details online.

14. (b) (12 marks)

In about 150 words, and according to the passage, explain the various measures which could be instituted to ensure fraud-free online transactions.

Answer

According to the passage, various measures can be instituted to ensure fraud-free online transactions. Firstly, individuals should avoid using public computers and networks for sensitive transactions due to potential security risks from unknown software and hardware (Paragraph 3 & 4). Secondly, using credit cards instead of debit cards is recommended, as credit card fraud is less impactful due to company intervention (Paragraph 5). Thirdly, employing third-party payment services like M-Pesa can reduce the risk of online theft (Paragraph 5). Furthermore, it is crucial to keep computer security software updated, including antivirus and firewalls, to deter fraud (Paragraph 6). Lastly, maintaining accurate records of online transactions is valuable for evidence in case of problems and being cautious not to reveal personal data online is essential (Paragraph 6).
