July 2013

DIPLOMA IN COMMUNICATION STUDIES

Exam Code: COMMUNICATION MODULE I

Duration: 3 hours

Period: July 2013

1. (3 marks)

Give three uses of feedback in communication.

Answer

To clarify understanding and ensure the message has been received and interpreted correctly by the recipient.

To improve communication effectiveness by identifying areas of misunderstanding or confusion that need to be addressed.

To build stronger relationships between communicators by demonstrating active listening and engagement in the exchange.

2. (3 marks)

State three reasons for training employees on the importance of effective communication in an organization.

Answer

To enhance productivity and efficiency by minimizing misunderstandings and errors in task execution and information flow.

To improve teamwork and collaboration by fostering clear and open communication channels among colleagues and departments.

To boost employee morale and job satisfaction by creating a positive and communicative work environment where everyone feels heard and valued.

3. (4 marks)

Highlight four negative consequences of calling an annual general meeting at short notice.

Answer

Reduced attendance as members may have prior commitments or insufficient time to arrange travel, leading to poor representation and decision-making.

Inadequate preparation by members, hindering informed participation and meaningful contributions to discussions and resolutions.

Legal challenges and invalidation of meeting decisions if the notice period violates organizational bylaws or legal requirements, causing disruption and potential liabilities.

Damaged member relations and mistrust due to perceived lack of respect for members' time and rights, undermining organizational cohesion and future engagement.

4. (3 marks)

Give three challenges that an interviewee may face during an interview.

Answer

Nerves and anxiety which can impair performance, affecting clarity of communication and ability to present oneself effectively.

Unexpected or difficult questions that require quick thinking and articulate responses under pressure.

Competition from other candidates, making it necessary to stand out and demonstrate unique value and suitability for the role.

5. (3 marks)

List three items that may be contained in the appendix of a report.

Answer

Raw data or detailed statistical tables that support the analysis but are too lengthy for the main body.

Copies of questionnaires or survey instruments used to collect data for the report.

Transcripts of interviews or focus group discussions that provided qualitative insights.

6. (3 marks)

Give three reasons for using notes when making a presentation.

Answer

To maintain structure and logical flow of the presentation, ensuring all key points are covered in a coherent manner.

To prompt memory and recall specific facts, statistics, or examples, enhancing accuracy and credibility.

To reduce anxiety and increase confidence by having a reference point, allowing for smoother delivery and better audience engagement.

7. (3 marks)

Outline three benefits that an organization may derive from introducing a dress code for the employees.

Answer

Enhanced professional image and brand representation, projecting a consistent and credible appearance to clients and stakeholders.

Improved employee discipline and sense of unity, fostering a more focused and collaborative work environment.

Reduced distractions and promotion of a professional atmosphere, minimizing inappropriate or overly casual attire in the workplace.

8. (4 marks)

Highlight four factors that have made the short message service (SMS) popular as a mode of communication.

Answer

Cost-effectiveness, offering a relatively inexpensive way to send and receive messages compared to traditional phone calls or postal services.

Speed and immediacy, enabling instant delivery of messages, making it ideal for urgent communication and quick updates.

Wide accessibility, as SMS is supported by virtually all mobile phones across different networks and geographical locations.

Convenience and ease of use, requiring minimal technical skills and allowing for quick and simple message composition and sending.

9. (3 marks)

Outline three reasons that would make it necessary for an organization to use visual aids when communicating.

Answer

To enhance understanding and retention of information by presenting complex data or concepts in a simplified and memorable visual format.

To engage audience attention and maintain interest during presentations or training sessions, making communication more dynamic and impactful.

To overcome language barriers and facilitate communication with diverse audiences through universally understandable images and graphics.

10. (3 marks)

Outline three uses of memos in an organization.

Answer

To disseminate internal announcements and updates to staff regarding policy changes, procedural updates, or organizational news.

To confirm decisions or agreements reached in meetings or discussions, providing a written record and ensuring clarity.

To request information or provide directives to specific individuals or departments within the organization, ensuring accountability and task delegation.

12. (16 marks)

You work for Mawasiliano Mobile Telephone Company Limited as a Public Relations Assistant. The management has noted that its customer base has not been increasing as fast as that of its competitors. The Chief Executive Officer has requested you to investigate the situation. Assuming that you have completed the investigations, write the report.

Answer

**MAWASILIANO MOBILE TELEPHONE COMPANY LIMITED - REPORT ON SLOW CUSTOMER BASE GROWTH**

**TO:** Chief Executive Officer, Mawasiliano Mobile Telephone Company Limited

**FROM:** [Your Name], Public Relations Assistant

**DATE:** [Date of Report]

**SUBJECT:** Investigation into Slower Customer Base Growth Compared to Competitors

**1. INTRODUCTION**

This report presents the findings of an investigation into the slower growth rate of Mawasiliano Mobile's customer base compared to key competitors in the mobile telecommunications market. The investigation was commissioned by the CEO to identify factors contributing to this trend and recommend strategies for improvement.

**2. METHODOLOGY**

The investigation employed a multi-pronged approach:

Market share analysis comparing Mawasiliano Mobile's growth rate against top competitors over the past year.

Customer surveys and focus groups to gather feedback on service perception and competitive offerings.

Competitive analysis of marketing strategies, pricing models, and product/service offerings.

Internal review of sales, marketing, and customer service operations.

**3. FINDINGS**

**Slower Growth Rate:** Market analysis confirms that Mawasiliano Mobile's customer base growth rate is lagging behind key competitors by approximately [Quantify percentage difference].

**Customer Feedback:** Surveys and focus groups revealed the following customer perceptions:

**Price Sensitivity:** Perceived higher prices for data and voice packages compared to competitors.

**Network Coverage Concerns:** Reports of inconsistent network coverage in certain geographical areas.

**Customer Service Issues:** Some dissatisfaction with customer service response times and resolution efficiency.

**Competitor Innovation:** Competitors are perceived as more innovative in launching new services and value-added products.

**Competitive Analysis:** Competitors are aggressively employing:

**Aggressive Pricing Strategies:** Offering more competitive and promotional pricing plans.

**Extensive Marketing Campaigns:** Larger and more impactful marketing and advertising campaigns.

**Value-Added Services:** Bundling attractive value-added services and loyalty programs.

**4. ANALYSIS**

The slower customer base growth is attributable to a combination of pricing perceptions, network coverage concerns, customer service issues, and competitors' aggressive market strategies and innovation. Mawasiliano Mobile is perceived as less competitive in pricing and slower in adopting innovative service offerings.

**5. RECOMMENDATIONS**

**Competitive Pricing Review:** Conduct a comprehensive review of pricing strategies to align with market expectations and competitor offerings, considering promotional packages and data bundles.

**Network Infrastructure Investment:** Prioritize investments in network infrastructure to improve coverage and service reliability, especially in identified weak coverage areas.

**Customer Service Enhancement:** Implement customer service training programs to improve response times, resolution efficiency, and overall customer experience. Explore technology upgrades for customer support.

**Innovation and Product Development:** Accelerate innovation in product and service development, focusing on value-added services and customer-centric solutions to differentiate from competitors.

**Marketing and Communication Strategy Revamp:** Develop a more aggressive and targeted marketing and communication strategy to highlight competitive advantages and new offerings.

**6. CONCLUSION**

Addressing the identified challenges through the recommended strategies is crucial for Mawasiliano Mobile to regain competitive momentum and accelerate customer base growth. A customer-centric approach, focusing on pricing, network quality, service excellence, and innovation, is essential for future success. Regular market monitoring and adaptive strategies will be necessary to maintain competitiveness.

Prepared by:

[Your Name]
Public Relations Assistant

Passage

It is often hard to understand why companies care when the prices of their shares fall. After all, listed companies have already received money from investors when they first sold shares through an initial public offering. What happens in the secondary market when investors buy and sell to each other at the Nairobi Stock Exchange (NSE) cannot take away the gains the company has already made. However, like with most things, the story is a lot more complex than it appears.

To begin with, most senior managers generally have a vested interest in the company. Similarly, the founder-members of many public companies own a significant number of shares. Hence, their personal wealth could be dramatically affected by a fall in the company's share price. Besides, many employees receive performance-related bonuses in the form of shares in their company. This effectively means that they are stockholders of the company and therefore pay strong attention to its share price. Additionally a falling share price can impact on the reputation of a company and therefore reflect negatively on its management. It is also not unusual for the management of a company to have salary incentives or employee stock options tied to the company's share prices.

Publicly traded companies which are underperforming also often become takeover targets. If the share price of a company falls substantially, it makes it much easier and much more affordable for a rival company to move in and buy up a significant amount of stock or launch a takeover bid that shareholders cannot afford to refuse. This is a very big incentive for companies to ensure their stock remains relatively stable so that they remain strong and deter interested parties from forcing them into a takeover ueal. In addition, the rate and ease at which companies borrow money can be affected by a falling share price. In most cases, creditors tend to look favourably upon companies which are performing well and may offer them cheaper financing through a lower interest rate.

If the performance of a company's shares is ignored, it can have serious repercussions on the fate of the firm and its management. Share price is often an indicator of a company's financial health and can influence analysts' and lenders' evaluations. Hence stock performance directly affects a company's borrowing power, which has a significant effect on its financial health. However, share price is a poor method of evaluating a company's performance because it has more to do with emotion and perception than whether a company is taking care of the business.

Although a manager has little or no control of share price in the short run, poor share performance could, over the long run, be attributed to mismanagement of the company. If the share price consistently underperforms, the shareholders would be unhappy with the management and look for changes. While the extent to which shareholders can control management is debatable, executives must always factor in the desires of shareholders since these shareholders are part owners of the company. Companies would want their share prices to remain relatively stable so that they remain strong and deter interested companies from taking them over. On the other side of the takeover equation, a company with a hot stock has a great advantage when seeking to buy other companies. Instead of having to either use the retained earnings or borrow money, a company will simply issue more shares through a rights issue to fund the takeover.

Moreover, a company may want to increase its share price simply to increase its prestige and exposure to the public. The larger the market capitalization of a company, the more analyst coverage the company will receive. Such analyst coverage is a form of free advertising and allows both senior managers and the company itself to introduce themselves to a wider audience. Thus the share price at the stock market is very closely watched by managers and shareholders because it acts as a barometer for a company's prospects and fortunes.

Adapted from: The Standard; Tuesday, February 22, 2011

14. (a) (12 marks)

In about 150 words and according to the passage, explain why companies get concerned when the price of their shares fall.

Answer

According to the passage, companies are concerned about falling share prices for several interconnected reasons. Firstly, senior managers and founder-members often hold significant shares, so a price drop directly diminishes their personal wealth (Paragraph 2). Secondly, many employees receive share-based bonuses, making them stockholders sensitive to price fluctuations, impacting morale and potentially performance (Paragraph 2). Thirdly, a falling share price can damage a company's reputation, reflecting negatively on its management and future prospects (Paragraph 2). Furthermore, underperforming companies with low share prices become vulnerable to takeovers, as rival firms find it easier and cheaper to acquire a controlling stake, threatening the company's independence (Paragraph 3). Lower share prices also increase borrowing costs, as creditors view such companies as riskier, affecting financial health (Paragraph 3). Finally, companies seek to maintain or increase share price for prestige and public exposure, as higher market capitalization attracts analyst coverage, which acts as free advertising and enhances visibility to wider audiences (Paragraph 6). Therefore, share price is a critical barometer of a company's perceived health and future, influencing various stakeholders and strategic options.

14. (b) (6 marks)

State the meaning of the following words and phrases as used in the passage.

Answer

**(i) take away the gains:**

In the context of the passage, "take away the gains" means to negate or nullify the profits or benefits a company has already accumulated. The passage states that secondary market trading "cannot take away the gains the company has already made," meaning past profits are secured regardless of share price fluctuations in the secondary market.

**(ii) impact on:**

"Impact on" means to have an effect or influence on something. The passage mentions that a falling share price can "impact on the reputation of a company," indicating that share price decline can negatively affect the company's public image and standing.

**(iii) substantially:**

"Substantially" means to a large extent or significantly. When the passage says, "If the share price of a company falls substantially," it refers to a significant or considerable decrease in the company's share price.

**(iv) indicator:**

"Indicator" refers to something that serves as a sign or measure of something else. Share price is described as "often an indicator of a company's financial health," meaning it is used as a gauge or signal of the company's financial condition.

**(v) relatively stable:**

"Relatively stable" means reasonably steady or not subject to significant fluctuations. Companies want their stock to remain "relatively stable" to avoid takeover threats and maintain investor confidence, implying they aim for a share price that does not vary dramatically.

**(vi) prestige:**

"Prestige" refers to high standing or reputation achieved through success or influence. Companies may want to increase share price to enhance their "prestige," indicating that a higher share price contributes to a company's esteemed public image and market perception.
