November 2021

COMMUNICATION

Exam Code: 2902/104, 2908/104, 2912/104, 2918/104, 2920/104, 2921/104

Duration: 3 hours

Period: November 2021

1. (4 marks)

List four ways in which effective communication helps to create a conducive environment in an organization.

Answer

Builds Trust and Transparency: Open and honest communication fosters trust among employees and management, creating a transparent environment where information is shared freely.

Enhances Collaboration and Teamwork: Clear communication ensures that team members understand their roles and responsibilities, promoting effective collaboration and synergy.

Reduces Conflicts and Misunderstandings: Effective communication minimizes ambiguities and misunderstandings, thereby preventing potential conflicts and promoting harmonious relationships.

Improves Employee Morale and Engagement: When employees feel heard and informed, their morale and engagement levels increase, leading to a more positive and productive work environment.

2. (3 marks)

State three measures that a manager may take to encourage feedback from external publics.

Answer

Implement Feedback Channels: Establish various channels such as online surveys, feedback forms on websites, and dedicated email addresses to facilitate easy submission of feedback.

Actively Solicit Feedback: Regularly request feedback through direct inquiries during customer interactions, focus groups, or by conducting periodic stakeholder surveys.

Demonstrate Responsiveness: Show that feedback is valued by acknowledging receipt, acting upon suggestions, and communicating changes made based on external public input.

3. (3 marks)

List three guidelines that should be followed when writing circular letters.

Answer

Clarity and Conciseness: Ensure the language is clear, simple, and to the point, avoiding jargon or ambiguity to convey the message effectively and quickly.

Professional Tone: Maintain a formal and professional tone throughout the letter, reflecting the organization's image and ensuring respectful communication.

Proper Formatting: Use a standard business letter format, including a clear subject line, date, recipient details, and a professional closing to enhance readability and organization.

4. (4 marks)

State four reasons why a meeting may lack quorum.

Answer

Poor Scheduling: Meetings scheduled at inconvenient times, conflicting with participants' schedules or deadlines, can lead to low attendance and lack of quorum.

Insufficient Notice: Inadequate advance notice provided to attendees may result in them having prior commitments, making it difficult to achieve quorum.

Lack of Perceived Importance: If attendees do not perceive the meeting's agenda or purpose as important or relevant to their roles, they may prioritize other tasks, affecting quorum.

Communication Failure: Ineffective communication of meeting details, including time, venue, and agenda, can lead to oversights and reduced attendance, resulting in a lack of quorum.

5. (2 marks)

List two ways in which an employee can prepare for a promotion interview.

Answer

Review Job Description: Thoroughly understand the requirements and expectations of the higher position to align responses and demonstrate suitability during the interview.

Prepare Examples of Achievements: Reflect on past accomplishments and prepare specific examples that showcase skills, leadership qualities, and contributions relevant to the promotion role.

6. (3 marks)

List three limitations of using observation to gather information when writing a report.

Answer

Subjectivity and Bias: Observations can be influenced by the observer's personal biases, perceptions, and interpretations, leading to subjective and potentially skewed data.

Limited Scope: Observation may only capture surface-level information and behaviors, failing to uncover underlying motivations, attitudes, or complex issues that require deeper investigation.

Time and Resource Intensive: Comprehensive observation can be time-consuming and resource-intensive, especially for large-scale studies or when detailed, continuous monitoring is required.

7. (3 marks)

Outline three ways of creating a positive first impression when making a speech.

Answer

Professional Appearance: Dress professionally and appropriately for the audience and occasion to convey competence and respect.

Confident Posture and Body Language: Maintain an upright posture, make eye contact, and use open and welcoming body language to project confidence and credibility.

Engaging Opening: Start with a compelling opening line, anecdote, or question to immediately capture the audience's attention and create interest in the topic.

8. (4 marks)

List four factors that influence ambience as a non-verbal cue.

Answer

Lighting: The type and intensity of lighting can significantly affect mood and perception, with bright light often associated with energy and dim light with relaxation.

Color: Colors evoke different emotions and associations; for instance, warm colors like red and yellow can create a stimulating atmosphere, while cool colors like blue and green promote calmness.

Sound and Music: Background noise or music can shape the ambience, with soft music creating a soothing environment and loud noises leading to stress or distraction.

Temperature and Air Quality: Comfortable temperature and fresh air contribute to a pleasant ambience, whereas extreme temperatures or poor air quality can cause discomfort and negative perceptions.

9. (3 marks)

State three disadvantages of using visual aids to pass information.

Answer

Technical Issues and Failures: Reliance on technology for visual aids can be problematic due to equipment malfunctions, software glitches, or power outages, disrupting presentations.

Over-Reliance and Distraction: Overuse or poorly designed visuals can distract the audience from the main message, reducing engagement with the spoken content.

Accessibility Limitations: Visual aids may not be accessible to all audience members, particularly those with visual impairments, without proper accommodations.

10. (3 marks)

Outline three measures that may be taken to increase efficiency when using ICT in communication.

Answer

Provide Training and Support: Ensure all users are adequately trained on ICT tools and provide ongoing technical support to maximize their effective and efficient use.

Standardize Communication Protocols: Establish clear guidelines and protocols for ICT usage, including preferred channels for different types of communication and response time expectations.

Regularly Update and Maintain Systems: Keep ICT systems and software updated to ensure optimal performance, security, and compatibility, reducing downtime and enhancing efficiency.

11. (10 marks)

(a) Different organizations emphasize certain areas of assessment in job selection interviews. Outline five such areas.

Answer

Technical Skills and Knowledge: Organizations assess the candidate's specific technical abilities, expertise, and knowledge relevant to the job role to ensure they possess the necessary competencies to perform effectively.

Problem-Solving and Analytical Skills: Employers evaluate the candidate's capacity to analyze complex issues, think critically, and develop effective solutions, often using situational questions or case studies.

Cultural Fit and Values Alignment: Organizations assess whether the candidate's personality, work style, and values align with the company culture and mission, ensuring team cohesion and organizational harmony.

Communication and Interpersonal Skills: Employers evaluate the candidate's verbal and non-verbal communication skills, ability to articulate ideas clearly, and interpersonal skills for effective collaboration and relationship building.

Leadership Potential and Managerial Competencies: For managerial roles, organizations assess leadership qualities, decision-making skills, strategic thinking, and potential to lead and motivate teams effectively.

11. (6 marks)

(b) Explain three reasons why it is important to evaluate a message during the listening process.

Answer

Ensure Accurate Understanding: Evaluating a message critically during listening helps to verify comprehension and identify any ambiguities or misunderstandings, ensuring the message is correctly interpreted as intended by the sender.

Filter Out Bias and Emotional Content: Evaluation allows listeners to separate facts from opinions, biases, or emotional appeals within a message, enabling a more objective and rational assessment of the information being conveyed.

Enhance Decision-Making: By critically evaluating messages, listeners can make informed judgments based on reliable and verified information, leading to better decision-making and problem-solving outcomes in professional and personal contexts.

12. (6 marks)

(a) Outline three benefits that an organization may derive from using modern technology to share information.

Answer

Increased Efficiency and Speed: Modern technology facilitates rapid and instantaneous information sharing across different locations and departments, significantly enhancing operational efficiency and accelerating decision-making processes.

Improved Collaboration and Accessibility: Technology enables seamless collaboration among team members, regardless of geographical location, and ensures that information is readily accessible to authorized personnel anytime, anywhere.

Reduced Costs and Enhanced Storage: Digital information sharing reduces the need for paper-based communication, lowering printing, distribution, and storage costs while offering vast digital storage capabilities for easy retrieval and management of data.

13. (16 marks)

The management of Bryson Limited has noted low attendance of its online meetings. As the Assistant Office Manager, you have been requested to investigate the matter and write a report. Assume that you have completed the investigations and write the report.

Answer

REPORT ON LOW ATTENDANCE AT ONLINE MEETINGS – BRYSON LIMITED

To: The Management, Bryson Limited
From: [Your Name], Assistant Office Manager
Date: November 28, 2021
Subject: Investigation into Low Attendance at Online Meetings and Recommendations for Improvement

Executive Summary:
This report addresses the issue of low attendance at Bryson Limited’s online meetings. Following management’s concern, an investigation was conducted to identify the reasons for poor participation. The report outlines the findings of this investigation and proposes actionable recommendations to enhance attendance and engagement in online meetings.

1. Introduction:
Bryson Limited has observed a consistent pattern of low attendance at its scheduled online meetings. This report aims to investigate the underlying causes of this issue and recommend strategies to improve employee participation and meeting effectiveness.

2. Methodology:
The investigation employed a multi-faceted approach to gather comprehensive data:

Attendance Data Review: Analysis of attendance records for online meetings over the past month to identify trends and patterns in participation rates across different departments and teams.

Employee Surveys: Anonymous surveys distributed to all employees to gather feedback on their experiences with online meetings, challenges faced, and suggestions for improvement.

Focus Group Discussions: Conducted focus group sessions with representatives from various departments to facilitate open discussions and gather qualitative insights into the reasons for low attendance.

Technology and Infrastructure Assessment: Review of the current ICT infrastructure and online meeting platforms to identify any technical issues or limitations affecting accessibility and user experience.

3. Findings:
The investigation revealed several key factors contributing to low attendance at online meetings:

Meeting Schedule Conflicts: Many employees reported that meeting times often clash with their workload or other commitments, making it difficult to attend regularly.

Lack of Perceived Value: Some employees perceive online meetings as unproductive, time-wasting, or lacking clear objectives, leading to decreased motivation to attend.

Technical Difficulties: Technical issues such as poor internet connectivity, platform incompatibility, or lack of familiarity with meeting tools deter some employees from joining or actively participating.

Communication Gaps: Insufficient pre-meeting communication regarding agenda, objectives, and expected outcomes may reduce employee interest and preparedness to attend.

Meeting Fatigue: Employees express feeling overwhelmed by the frequency and duration of online meetings, contributing to meeting fatigue and decreased engagement.

4. Recommendations:
To address the identified challenges and improve online meeting attendance, the following recommendations are proposed:

Optimize Meeting Schedules: Conduct a review of meeting schedules to identify and resolve time conflicts. Consider varying meeting times or using scheduling tools to accommodate different time zones and workloads.

Enhance Meeting Purpose and Relevance: Clearly communicate meeting agendas, objectives, and expected outcomes in advance. Ensure meetings are focused, productive, and perceived as valuable by attendees.

Improve Technology and Support: Upgrade ICT infrastructure and online meeting platforms to ensure reliability and user-friendliness. Provide technical support and training to employees to enhance their proficiency with meeting tools.

Promote Meeting Engagement Strategies: Implement interactive elements in online meetings, such as polls, Q&A sessions, and breakout rooms, to increase participant engagement and active contribution.

Reduce Meeting Frequency and Duration: Evaluate the necessity of each meeting and consolidate agendas where possible. Aim for shorter, more focused meetings to minimize meeting fatigue and maximize efficiency.

5. Conclusion:
Low attendance at Bryson Limited’s online meetings is a multifaceted issue stemming from scheduling conflicts, perceived lack of value, technical challenges, communication gaps, and meeting fatigue. By implementing the recommendations outlined in this report, Bryson Limited can significantly improve online meeting attendance, enhance employee engagement, and optimize meeting effectiveness. Regular monitoring and feedback collection will be essential to ensure sustained improvement.

6. Next Steps:
It is recommended that management reviews this report and establishes a task force comprising representatives from IT, HR, and departmental teams to develop and implement a detailed action plan based on these recommendations. Employee training on online meeting best practices and technology should be prioritized.

Passage

Micro and small businesses are now reviewing their strategies as they seek to change their fortunes following the adverse effects of the COVID-19 pandemic. Many businesses have seriously needed financial bailout to stay afloat. However, a new report on a number of enterprises reveals that, while money is crucial for their survival, business strategy and adoption of new innovative models such as going online are more important.

The Kenyan SME Performance Report by WYLDE International, a professional service firm that specialises in entrepreneurship surveys, reveals that nearly a half of Small and Medium Enterprises (SMEs) recorded a decline in performance between September and October 2020, This was mainly due to economic setbacks caused by measures taken to contain the spread of the COVID-19 pandemic. The adverse effects of the pandemic have compelled many businesses to change tack. This is because they realized that finances are just one of the many aspects that make a healthy and thriving business.

While the funding of SMEs remained critical after COVID-19 restrictions were eased, the report says its significance has now lessened as businesses seek other alternatives. The report states that only 60 percent of SMEs believe that funding or grants are important, which is lower than the 80 percent who believed the same in March-April 2020. Non-funding needs such as review of business strategy went up from 23 percent to 65 percent in the same period. Business development and managerial skills training was 46 percent while tax relief waivers and discounts took 45 percent. The WYLDE Report discovered that SMEs require strategy development for their businesses to grow more than they need financing.

The report cited a number of challenges. One is inadequate capital at different stages of running a business. About 58 percent of SMEs rate capital related problems as the most pressing challenge. In addition, high operational expenses are an issue. The problem is compounded by a myriad of factors such as the high cost of energy, labour and taxation among others. Additionally, the effect of lockdown also reduces operating hours that translates to lower sales and profits. In 2020, the government imposed strict lockdown measures that included a ban on local and international flights, restricted movement in and out of some counties as well as curfews to contain the spread of COVID-19.These measures saw thousands of firms close down leading to loss of thousands of jobs. Some of these restrictions have, however, been lifted while others have been eased. The turmoil caused by the pandemic did not just hit the SME sector but also big firms. The National Bureau of Statistics shows that the number of people in employment fell to 15.87 million between April and June 2020 compared to 17.59 million in the same period in 2019.

The report, whose sample was made up of 132 SMEs in Nairobi, revealed that businesses with less than five staff increased during the survey period as more SMEs resumed offering their services. The report also revealed that businesses had adapted their workforce to meet the growing market demand brought about by the slowly recovering economy. For employers, however, employee health and safety remained a huge concern followed by the need for staff retention.

To help the sector recover, traders are recommending removal of curfew to allow more hours of operating their businesses. They also want the government to educate them on the implication of the new taxes and their impact on businesses. Traders are further requesting for business support services by organizations and financiers to create stimulus funds for hard hit sectors such as tourism and hospitality. They also feel that they should be helped to develop digital business models to operate without geographical and time limits. Capacity-building programmes that focus on helping SMEs should be put in place. Traders also recommend leveraging on digital tools to remain visible, taking advantage of stimulus packages and adopting intentional growth strategies.

Adapted from: Business Daily, 23 February 2021

14. (5 marks)

(a) State the meaning of each of the following words and phrase as used in the passage: (i) stay afloat; (ii) compelled; (iii) cited; (iv) turmoil; (v) intentional.

Answer

(i) stay afloat: In the passage, "stay afloat" means to survive financially or to continue operating despite financial difficulties, especially during the pandemic.

(ii) compelled: As used in the passage, "compelled" means forced or obliged, indicating that businesses were forced to change their strategies due to the pandemic's impact.

(iii) cited: In the passage, "cited" means mentioned or referenced, indicating that the report mentioned several challenges faced by SMEs.

(iv) turmoil: "Turmoil" refers to a state of great disturbance, confusion, or uncertainty, describing the chaotic impact of the pandemic on the SME sector.

(v) intentional: "Intentional" means deliberate or planned, suggesting that growth strategies should be deliberately designed and not accidental.

14. (3 marks)

(b) Highlight the challenges facing SMEs as cited in the WYLDE Report.

Answer

According to the WYLDE Report cited in the passage, the main challenges facing SMEs include inadequate capital at different business stages and high operational expenses. These capital-related problems are considered the most pressing challenges for SMEs.

14. (10 marks)

(c) In about 110 words and according to the passage, write a summary on the recommendations that traders are making to help the SME sector recover.

Answer

Traders are recommending several measures to aid SME sector recovery. They suggest removing curfews to extend business operating hours and want government education on new taxes and their business impact. They request business support services from organizations and financiers to establish stimulus funds for severely affected sectors like tourism and hospitality. Additionally, traders emphasize the need for help in developing digital business models for operation without geographical and time restrictions. Capacity-building programmes focused on SMEs are crucial, along with leveraging digital tools, utilizing stimulus packages, and adopting planned growth strategies to ensure SME sector revitalization.
