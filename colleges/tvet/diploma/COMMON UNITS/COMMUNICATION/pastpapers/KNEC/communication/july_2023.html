July 2023

COMMUNICATION

Exam Code: 2902/104, 2908/104, 2912/104, 2918/104, 2920/104, 2921/104

Duration: 3 hours

Period: July 2023

1. (3 marks)

State three measures that a speaker may take to prevent forgetfulness when passing information orally.

Answer

Use memory aids such as acronyms, rhymes, or visual imagery to enhance recall during the presentation.

Prepare comprehensive speaking notes or outlines that include key points and supporting details, ensuring structured delivery.

Practice and rehearse the oral presentation multiple times to internalize the information and improve confidence in recall.

2. (4 marks)

State four aspects of an organization that a candidate should be aware of when preparing for a job interview.

Answer

Organizational Culture: Understand the company's values, work environment, and employee interaction styles to align expectations.

Company Mission and Goals: Research the organization's purpose, strategic objectives, and future plans to demonstrate interest and fit.

Industry Position and Market: Analyze the company's standing in its sector, competitors, and market trends to show industry awareness.

Products and Services: Gain knowledge about the organization's offerings, target customers, and unique selling propositions to discuss relevance.

3. (4 marks)

List four factors that may contribute to the lack of quorum in a meeting.

Answer

Short Notice: Insufficient time provided to members to arrange their schedules and attend the meeting.

Conflicting Schedules: Meeting time clashes with other important commitments or deadlines for members.

Lack of Motivation: Members perceive the meeting as unimportant, irrelevant, or a waste of time, reducing attendance.

Poor Communication: Inadequate reminders or unclear meeting purpose leading to oversight or lack of prioritization by members.

4. (3 marks)

List three factors that may lead to lack of feedback from juniors in the communication process.

Answer

Fear of Reprisal: Juniors may hesitate to provide feedback due to concerns about negative consequences or backlash from superiors.

Hierarchical Barriers: Formal organizational structures can inhibit open communication, making juniors feel their input is not valued or expected.

Lack of Trust: If juniors do not trust their seniors or the feedback process, they are less likely to offer honest or critical input.

5. (3 marks)

State three functions of external communication.

Answer

Public Relations: To manage and enhance the organization's image and reputation among external stakeholders.

Marketing and Sales: To promote products or services to customers and generate sales leads.

Stakeholder Engagement: To communicate with investors, government agencies, and the general public, ensuring transparency and building relationships.

6. (3 marks)

List three reasons why an organization may write a circular letter to its customers.

Answer

Mass Announcements: To disseminate important information to a large customer base efficiently, such as policy changes or service updates.

Promotional Campaigns: To announce special offers, new product launches, or seasonal sales events to all customers simultaneously.

Customer Relations: To maintain regular communication, build rapport, and keep customers informed about the organization's activities and offerings.

7. (3 marks)

List three functions of non-verbal communication.

Answer

Complementing Verbal Messages: Non-verbal cues can reinforce or emphasize spoken words, enhancing understanding and impact.

Regulating Interaction: Non-verbal signals like eye contact and body posture manage the flow of conversation and indicate turn-taking.

Expressing Emotions: Facial expressions, gestures, and tone of voice are primary channels for conveying feelings and attitudes.

8. (3 marks)

State three ways of making pie charts effective as visual aids.

Answer

Limit Slices: Use a maximum of five to seven slices to prevent visual clutter and ensure each segment is easily distinguishable.

Clear Labeling: Label each slice directly on the chart or use a concise legend with clear, contrasting colors for each category.

Strategic Color Use: Employ contrasting colors for adjacent slices and use color intensity to represent data magnitude effectively.

9. (3 marks)

List three factors that may contribute to high uptake of technology in an organization.

Answer

Perceived Usefulness: Employees recognize the technology's ability to improve efficiency, productivity, or job performance.

Ease of Use: The technology is user-friendly, intuitive, and requires minimal training, encouraging adoption across different skill levels.

Organizational Support: Management provides adequate resources, training, and technical assistance, fostering a supportive environment for technology integration.

10. (3 marks)

List three guidelines that should be followed when writing formal reports.

Answer

Objective Language: Maintain a neutral, unbiased tone, avoiding personal opinions or emotional expressions, and focusing on factual information.

Structured Format: Follow a clear and logical structure with headings, subheadings, and numbered or bulleted lists for readability and organization.

Accuracy and Clarity: Ensure all information is factually correct, data is verified, and language is precise and unambiguous, minimizing misinterpretations.

11. (8 marks)

(a) Explain four strategies that a sender may adopt to minimize technical barriers when communicating.

Answer

Use Simple Language: The sender should avoid jargon, acronyms, and overly complex terms that the receiver may not understand. Clarity and simplicity in language ensure the message is easily digestible and reduces chances of misinterpretation due to technical vocabulary.

Choose Appropriate Channels: Select communication channels that are accessible and familiar to the receiver. For instance, if communicating with a team unfamiliar with advanced video conferencing, opting for email or a phone call can minimize technical difficulties and ensure message delivery.

Provide Technical Support: Offer support or guidance to the receiver in using the communication technology. This could include providing user manuals, tutorials, or offering a point of contact for technical assistance. By proactively addressing potential technical issues, the sender facilitates smoother communication.

Test Technology in Advance: Before sending critical communications, the sender should test the technology to be used, ensuring it functions correctly and is compatible with the receiver's systems. This preemptive measure helps identify and resolve technical glitches, preventing disruptions during actual communication.

11. (8 marks)

(b) Benta Limited will be conducting its annual general meeting and 1,000 shareholders are invited. Explain four challenges that may be experienced when conducting the meeting.

Answer

Logistical Complexity: Managing a meeting for 1,000 shareholders presents significant logistical challenges. This includes securing a venue large enough to accommodate everyone, managing seating arrangements, and ensuring smooth entry and exit processes. Coordination of refreshments, registration, and materials distribution for such a large group requires meticulous planning and execution.

Maintaining Order and Engagement: With a large number of attendees, maintaining order during the meeting can be difficult. Ensuring that all shareholders have an opportunity to speak, ask questions, and participate constructively while adhering to the meeting agenda requires skilled facilitation and potentially strict time management for each speaker. Engagement can also be challenging as individual shareholders may feel lost in the crowd.

Technical Issues with Presentations and Voting: If presentations are involved, ensuring that audio-visual equipment is functional and visible to all 1,000 attendees is crucial. Furthermore, if electronic voting systems are used, they must be robust enough to handle a large volume of votes simultaneously without technical failures. Ensuring every shareholder can participate in voting without technical glitches is essential for a fair and effective AGM.

Communication Barriers and Misunderstandings: Communicating effectively to a large and diverse group of shareholders can be challenging. Ensuring that all announcements are heard clearly, questions are understood correctly, and responses are disseminated widely is crucial. Misunderstandings can easily arise due to the scale of the meeting, requiring clear, concise, and possibly multilingual communication strategies to cater to all attendees.

12. (6 marks)

(a) Ms Karat prefers using oral communication to disseminate information to employees. Explain three reasons for such preference.

Answer

Immediate Feedback: Oral communication allows for instant feedback and clarification. Ms. Karat can gauge employee understanding in real-time through questions and responses, enabling her to address confusions or concerns immediately. This interactive nature ensures that the message is accurately received and understood by the employees during the communication process itself.

Personal Touch and Rapport Building: Oral communication fosters a personal connection and helps in building rapport. Ms. Karat’s direct interaction with employees allows her to convey enthusiasm, empathy, and build trust more effectively than written communication. This personal touch can enhance employee morale and make communication more impactful and persuasive.

Efficiency for Complex or Sensitive Information: For complex or sensitive information, oral communication is often more efficient. Ms. Karat can use tone of voice, emphasis, and non-verbal cues to convey nuances and contextual understanding that might be lost or misinterpreted in written form. This richness of communication channels can make it easier to navigate sensitive topics and ensure the intended message is accurately conveyed and empathetically received.

Passage

The government of Kenya has banned the importation of second-hand trucks. The ban is a move to promote local vehicle assembly and create more jobs. It also aims to cut the number of old vehicles on the country's roads in the wake of the war against harmful emissions.

Data from the Kenya National Bureau of Statistics shows that local motor vehicle assembly firms produced 6,535 buses, trucks, and prime movers in 2021. As the ban takes effect, the local assembly numbers are going to increase since more motor vehicle dealers will opt to invest in assembly plants in the country. With the COVID-19 pandemic challenges reducing, the motor industry appears to be back on the upswing. The demand for light commercial vehicles is expected to increase gradually as business logistics and e-commerce are growing fast. This is because the country is also witnessing a rise in small and medium enterprises (SMEs), and entrepreneurs running online businesses require quick means of transport. Moreover, the rapid urbanisation that the country is undergoing has created new retail and e-commerce platforms that require efficient logistics, thus leading to the growth of the light commercial vehicles market.

Light commercial trucks come in all manner of shapes and sizes with varying interior features. A truck is basically designed to transport cargo or freight. There is huge diversity in the types, mechanism, power and configuration of the trucks. Industry records show that trucks within the light commercial segment are the best sellers in Kenya's motor industry. This is mainly due to their large carrying capacity and ability to operate in tough terrain. This makes their demand high among retailers, wholesalers, distributors, manufacturers and sand harvesters among others.

Buying a brand new commercial truck is a significant investment for any business, big or small. These vehicles are not only a means to transport cargo, but also represent a brand on the road. Therefore, before buying a truck, there are certain factors to consider. For example, not all truck types are created equal or will fit business needs. Hence, finding one that fits specific needs is essential. One should also consider the company's needs. A truck may be needed to transport bread, avocados, newspapers, cabbages or even potatoes. Based on the need, one should engage motor salespeople to establish the light commercial vehicles best suiting the firm. The

engagement entails asking all the essential questions to arrive at the right decision. In addition, one should consider the budget. Commercial trucks differ in price, so knowing the amount of money budgeted for them is critical before making the choice. One must decide how much money one is willing to spend on a single unit. One may also wish to consider fuel economy. Recently, the face of transport in Kenya has been changing as trucks with low fuel consumption dominate the market.

There is growing preference for locally assembled vehicles that allow owners to have the bodies customised. This is because trucks are valuable business tools that should meet the needs of a business. Besides, one should consider the fuel type the vehicle uses. Trucks use petrol or diesel, hence one should ask the dealers for advice on type of fuel. The final decision should be pegged on whether the business operations will be mainly in rural or urban areas.

A further factor to consider before making a purchase is the robustness of the commercial vehicle. This entails analysing how it copes with hauling cargo in rough terrain and how powerful the engine is. It is also worthwhile to consider how affordable it is to run and maintain the truck. This is because all businesses seek to keep expenses low.

With the right commercial truck, the business will run efficiently and be able to scale up. After all, the more efficient the business is, the more profits one is likely to make.

13. (16 marks)

The management of Watalii Game Park has noted that the number of tourists visiting the park has been declining for the last few years. As the Assistant Administrator, you have been asked to investigate the matter and write a report. Assume you have completed the investigations and write the report.

Answer

REPORT ON DECLINING TOURIST NUMBERS AT WATALII GAME PARK

To: The Management, Watalii Game Park
From: [Your Name], Assistant Administrator
Date: October 26, 2023
Subject: Investigation into Declining Tourist Numbers and Recommendations for Improvement

Executive Summary:
This report investigates the recent decline in tourist numbers at Watalii Game Park over the past few years. Through comprehensive data analysis, stakeholder interviews, and on-site assessments, several key factors contributing to this decline have been identified. The report outlines these factors and provides actionable recommendations aimed at revitalizing tourist interest and reversing the current downward trend.

1. Introduction:
Watalii Game Park has experienced a noticeable decrease in tourist arrivals over the last few years. Concerned by this trend, management commissioned this investigation to identify the underlying causes and propose effective strategies to restore and enhance tourist visitation.

2. Findings:
Our investigation revealed several interconnected factors contributing to the decline in tourist numbers:

Deterioration of Park Infrastructure: Park infrastructure, including roads, signage, and visitor facilities, has degraded over time due to insufficient maintenance. Poor road conditions limit accessibility to certain areas, and dilapidated facilities detract from the overall visitor experience.

Increased Competition: The emergence of new game parks and enhanced marketing efforts by existing competitors have intensified competition within the tourism sector. Watalii Game Park’s marketing and promotional activities have not kept pace with these developments, resulting in reduced visibility and appeal to potential tourists.

Negative Online Reviews and Word-of-Mouth: Online travel platforms and social media reviews indicate growing dissatisfaction among recent visitors. Recurring complaints about poor infrastructure, reduced wildlife sightings (attributed to potential poaching or animal migration), and inconsistent service quality are negatively impacting the park's reputation and deterring new visitors.

Economic Factors and Travel Trends: Global and local economic downturns have affected discretionary spending on tourism. Furthermore, evolving travel preferences towards more eco-friendly and community-based tourism models may not be fully addressed by Watalii Game Park’s current offerings.

3. Recommendations:
To address the identified challenges and revitalize tourism at Watalii Game Park, the following recommendations are proposed:

Infrastructure Upgrade and Maintenance: Implement a phased infrastructure improvement plan focusing on road repairs, facility renovation (visitor centers, restrooms, and accommodation), and enhanced signage. Establish a proactive maintenance schedule to prevent future deterioration.

Enhanced Marketing and Promotion: Develop a comprehensive marketing strategy that leverages digital platforms, social media, and targeted advertising campaigns to increase park visibility. Highlight unique selling points, such as specific wildlife species, scenic landscapes, or unique park activities. Collaborate with travel agencies and tour operators to create attractive packages.

Improve Visitor Experience and Service Quality: Invest in staff training to enhance customer service skills and park ranger expertise. Introduce value-added services such as guided tours, wildlife photography workshops, and cultural experiences. Ensure consistent service quality across all visitor touchpoints.

Wildlife Conservation and Anti-Poaching Measures: Strengthen wildlife protection efforts by increasing ranger patrols, implementing advanced monitoring technologies, and engaging with local communities in conservation initiatives. Successful conservation will lead to more wildlife sightings, a key tourist attraction.

Diversify Tourism Offerings: Explore opportunities to diversify tourism products, such as eco-tourism initiatives, nature trails, bird watching tours, and partnerships with local communities to offer cultural tourism experiences. Align park offerings with current sustainable tourism trends.

4. Conclusion:
The decline in tourist numbers at Watalii Game Park is a multifaceted issue requiring a strategic and concerted effort to address. By implementing the recommendations outlined in this report, Watalii Game Park can enhance its infrastructure, improve its market positioning, elevate visitor experiences, and ensure the long-term sustainability of its tourism sector. Regular monitoring and evaluation of these interventions will be crucial to track progress and adapt strategies as needed.

5. Next Steps:
It is recommended that management reviews this report and initiates a task force to develop a detailed action plan for implementing the proposed recommendations. Prioritization of actions, resource allocation, and setting clear timelines will be essential for successful execution.

14. (3 marks)

(a) State the meaning of each of the following words as used in the passage: (i) harmful; (ii) varying; (iii) essential.

Answer

(i) harmful: In the context of the passage, "harmful" refers to the negative impact of old vehicles on the environment due to their emissions, suggesting they are detrimental to air quality and public health.

(ii) varying: As used in the passage, "varying" indicates that light commercial trucks come in different shapes and sizes, implying a range of options and specifications available in terms of design and features.

(iii) essential: In the passage, "essential" emphasizes that finding a truck that fits specific business needs is critical, highlighting that this factor is fundamentally important and indispensable for business operations.

14. (12 marks)

(c) In about 130 words, and according to the passage, write a summary on the factors that are considered before buying a commercial truck.

Answer

Before purchasing a commercial truck, several factors are crucial. Firstly, assess business needs to determine the appropriate truck type for specific cargo, considering that not all types are equally suited for every business. Company needs dictate the truck’s purpose—transporting bread, newspapers, or heavier goods—influencing the selection. Budget is a key consideration, as prices vary, necessitating careful financial planning. Fuel economy is vital for managing operational costs, especially with fuel price fluctuations. Robustness is essential, evaluating the truck's ability to handle cargo in rough terrain. Customization options are important for aligning the truck's body with business requirements. Finally, consider the fuel type—petrol or diesel—based on operational location, rural or urban, and seek dealer advice to make an informed decision. These factors ensure the truck aligns with business needs and operational efficiency.
