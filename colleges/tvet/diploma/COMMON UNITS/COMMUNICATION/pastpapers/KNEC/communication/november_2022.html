COMMUNICATION

Exam Code: 2902/104, 2908/104, 2912/104, 2918/104, 2920/104, 2921/104

Duration: 3 hours

Period: November 2022

1. (3 marks)

State three factors to consider when choosing a channel of communication.

Answer

Audience: Consider the recipient's characteristics, preferences, and accessibility to different channels to ensure effective message reception.

Message Complexity: Complex or sensitive messages may require richer channels like face-to-face or video conferencing to allow for immediate clarification and feedback.

Cost and Time: Evaluate the cost associated with each channel and the urgency of the communication to select the most efficient and economical option.

2. (4 marks)

List four ways in which a sender may ensure clarity when communicating orally.

Answer

Use Simple Language: Avoid jargon, technical terms, and complex sentence structures that may confuse the listener; opt for straightforward and easily understandable vocabulary.

Structure the Message: Organize thoughts logically with a clear introduction, main points, and conclusion; this systematic approach aids listener comprehension and retention.

Provide Examples and Illustrations: Use concrete examples, analogies, or visual aids to clarify abstract concepts and make the message more relatable and easier to grasp.

Seek Feedback: Encourage questions and actively solicit feedback from the listener to confirm understanding and address any ambiguities or points of confusion immediately.

3. (3 marks)

State three features of an effective poster.

Answer

Visual Appeal: Utilize striking images, graphics, and a balanced layout to capture attention and make the poster visually engaging and memorable.

Concise Text: Employ brief, impactful text with clear headings and bullet points to convey the message quickly and efficiently; avoid lengthy paragraphs.

Legibility: Ensure font sizes are large enough to be easily read from a distance, with contrasting colors between text and background for enhanced readability.

4. (3 marks)

State three factors a secretary should consider when preparing for a meeting.

Answer

Agenda Preparation: Develop a detailed agenda outlining meeting objectives, topics to be discussed, and time allocation to ensure focused and productive discussions.

Logistics Arrangement: Organize venue booking, seating, equipment setup (projector, audio), and necessary materials to facilitate a smooth and comfortable meeting environment.

Attendee Communication: Send out timely meeting invitations with agenda and relevant pre-reading materials to ensure participants are well-informed and prepared.

5. (3 marks)

List three reasons for conducting exit interviews in an organization.

Answer

Identify Improvement Areas: Gather feedback on employee experiences to pinpoint organizational weaknesses, such as management issues or ineffective policies, needing improvement.

Reduce Future Turnover: Understand reasons behind employee departures to implement corrective actions and strategies aimed at enhancing retention and reducing future employee attrition.

Gain Insight on Workplace Culture: Obtain honest perspectives on the organization's culture, work environment, and employee relations to ensure a positive and productive workplace.

6. (3 marks)

List three circumstances under which a report may be presented orally.

Answer

Urgency and Immediacy: When the report's findings require immediate attention and action, oral presentation ensures swift communication and discussion of critical information.

Confidentiality Concerns: Sensitive or confidential information may be better conveyed orally to a restricted audience to maintain discretion and control information dissemination.

Interactive Discussion Required: If the report is expected to generate discussion, questions, and collaborative problem-solving, oral presentation facilitates real-time interaction and feedback.

7. (4 marks)

State four reasons why an audience should maintain eye contact with a presenter during speech delivery.

Answer

Show Respect and Attentiveness: Maintaining eye contact signals engagement and respect for the speaker, demonstrating that the audience is actively listening and valuing the presentation.

Enhance Comprehension: Eye contact facilitates better focus on the speaker's verbal and non-verbal cues, aiding in understanding the message and nuances being conveyed.

Build Rapport: Direct eye contact establishes a personal connection between the audience and presenter, fostering a more engaging and persuasive communication environment.

Provide Non-verbal Feedback: Audience eye contact provides the speaker with visual cues regarding audience reception, allowing them to adjust their delivery and ensure effective communication.

8. (3 marks)

State three ways in which a receiver should answer an official telephone call in the office.

Answer

Prompt and Polite Greeting: Answer the call promptly, ideally within three rings, and use a professional greeting including the company name and your name, spoken clearly and politely.

Active Listening and Clarification: Pay close attention to the caller, avoid interruptions, and ask clarifying questions to ensure accurate understanding of their needs and message.

Professional Demeanor and Closing: Maintain a courteous and helpful tone throughout the conversation, and conclude with a polite closing, confirming any next steps or actions to be taken.

9. (3 marks)

List three ways of encouraging upward communication in an organization.

Answer

Open-Door Policy: Implement an open-door policy where managers are readily accessible to employees for discussions, feedback, and concerns, fostering a culture of approachability.

Regular Feedback Mechanisms: Establish channels like suggestion boxes, employee surveys, and feedback forms to provide structured and anonymous ways for employees to voice opinions and suggestions.

Team Meetings and Forums: Conduct regular team meetings and open forums that encourage dialogue, idea sharing, and allow employees to raise issues and provide input to management directly.

10. (3 marks)

State three reasons why information communication technology is used to communicate in organizations.

Answer

Enhance Speed and Efficiency: ICT tools like email, instant messaging, and video conferencing enable rapid information exchange, accelerating communication processes and decision-making.

Improve Accessibility and Reach: ICT facilitates communication across geographical barriers, connecting remote teams and stakeholders instantly, regardless of location or time zones.

Cost Reduction: Utilizing ICT for communication can significantly reduce costs associated with traditional methods like paper documents, postage, and travel for face-to-face meetings.

11. (16 marks)

You work as the Sales Assistant at Mazuri Company Limited. It has been noted that the sales of building materials have declined significantly over the last three months. You have been asked by the Chief Executive Officer to investigate the matter and write a report. Assume that you have completed the investigations and write the report.

Answer

SALES DECLINE INVESTIGATION REPORT – BUILDING MATERIALS AT MAZURI COMPANY LIMITED

To: Chief Executive Officer, Mazuri Company Limited
From: [Your Name], Sales Assistant
Date: November 28, 2022
Subject: Investigation into Significant Sales Decline of Building Materials Over the Last Three Months

Executive Summary:
This report presents findings from an investigation into the significant decline in sales of building materials at Mazuri Company Limited over the past three months. The investigation was initiated following concerns raised by the Chief Executive Officer regarding this downturn. The report identifies key factors contributing to the sales slump and recommends actionable strategies to address these issues and revitalize sales performance.

1. Introduction:
Mazuri Company Limited has experienced a considerable decrease in building material sales over the last quarter. This report aims to analyze the causes of this decline, providing a comprehensive overview of contributing factors and proposing remedial actions to restore sales growth.

2. Methodology:
The investigation employed a mixed-methods approach, including:

Sales Data Analysis: Review of sales records for the past six months, comparing recent performance against historical trends and identifying specific product categories experiencing the most significant decline.

Customer Feedback Collection: Surveys and interviews conducted with key clients and distributors to gather insights into their purchasing behavior, satisfaction levels, and perceptions of Mazuri Company's offerings.

Competitor Analysis: Examination of competitor pricing strategies, promotional activities, and market positioning to understand the competitive landscape and identify potential areas of disadvantage for Mazuri Company.

Internal Stakeholder Interviews: Discussions with sales team members, marketing personnel, and production managers to gather internal perspectives on operational challenges and market dynamics.

3. Findings:
The investigation revealed the following key factors contributing to the sales decline:

Increased Market Competition: Emergence of new competitors offering similar building materials at lower prices has intensified market rivalry, eroding Mazuri Company’s market share.

Economic Downturn: A general economic slowdown has reduced construction activities, leading to decreased demand for building materials across the sector.

Pricing Issues: Mazuri Company’s pricing strategy appears less competitive compared to rivals, particularly for bulk orders, making it less attractive to large-scale construction projects.

Marketing and Promotion Shortfalls: Recent marketing efforts have been less impactful, failing to effectively communicate value propositions or reach the target customer base amidst increased competitor noise.

Customer Service Concerns: Feedback indicates some dissatisfaction with customer service responsiveness and order fulfillment efficiency, negatively impacting customer loyalty and repeat business.

4. Recommendations:
To address the identified issues and reverse the sales decline, the following recommendations are proposed:

Competitive Pricing Review: Conduct a comprehensive pricing analysis to align prices competitively with market standards, potentially introducing volume discounts or loyalty programs.

Enhance Marketing Strategies: Develop a targeted marketing campaign highlighting product quality, durability, and unique selling points, utilizing digital marketing, trade shows, and direct client engagement.

Improve Customer Service: Invest in customer service training to enhance responsiveness, order processing efficiency, and after-sales support, aiming to exceed customer expectations.

Product Diversification: Explore opportunities to diversify product offerings, potentially introducing eco-friendly or specialized building materials to cater to evolving market demands.

Strengthen Distributor Relationships: Enhance communication and support for distributors, offering incentives and collaborative marketing initiatives to boost channel sales performance.

5. Conclusion:
The sales decline at Mazuri Company Limited is attributed to a combination of increased competition, economic pressures, pricing issues, marketing shortfalls, and customer service concerns. Implementing the recommended strategies will enable Mazuri Company Limited to regain competitiveness, enhance customer satisfaction, and restore sales growth in the building materials sector. Regular monitoring and evaluation of these actions are crucial for sustained improvement.

6. Next Steps:
It is recommended that management reviews this report and forms a task force comprising sales, marketing, production, and customer service departments to develop and execute a detailed action plan based on these recommendations. Immediate implementation of pricing adjustments and marketing enhancements should be prioritized.

12. (6 marks)

(b) Explain four reasons for conducting job selection interviews.

Answer

Assess Candidate Skills and Competencies: Interviews provide a platform to evaluate a candidate's technical skills, problem-solving abilities, and relevant experience through targeted questions and practical scenarios, ensuring alignment with job requirements.

Evaluate Cultural Fit: Interviews allow employers to gauge a candidate's personality, values, and work style to determine their compatibility with the organization's culture and team dynamics, fostering a harmonious work environment.

Verify Information and Credentials: Interviews offer an opportunity to validate details provided in resumes and applications, clarifying qualifications, experience, and career history to ensure accuracy and authenticity.

Gauge Communication and Interpersonal Skills: Interviews enable direct observation of a candidate's communication proficiency, interpersonal abilities, and professionalism, critical for roles requiring teamwork, client interaction, or leadership.

13. (10 marks)

(a) Outline five uses of minutes of a meeting.

Answer

Record of Decisions: Minutes serve as an official record of decisions made during a meeting, providing clarity and accountability on agreed actions, resolutions, and responsibilities.

Action Tracking: Minutes document action items assigned to individuals, including deadlines, enabling follow-up and monitoring of progress towards meeting objectives and commitments.

Communication and Information Dissemination: Minutes inform absentees and stakeholders about meeting discussions, key points, and outcomes, ensuring everyone is aligned and updated on relevant information.

Historical Reference: Minutes act as a historical archive of meeting proceedings, offering a reference point for past discussions, decisions, and organizational memory for future planning and review.

Legal and Compliance Purposes: In certain contexts, minutes serve as legal documentation of meetings, demonstrating due diligence, compliance with regulations, and providing evidence of governance processes.

13. (6 marks)

(b) Explain three reasons why one should be diplomatic when communicating.

Answer

Maintain Positive Relationships: Diplomacy in communication helps preserve harmonious relationships by avoiding confrontational or offensive language, fostering mutual respect and goodwill among parties.

Prevent Misunderstandings and Conflicts: Diplomatic language is carefully chosen to be clear, tactful, and considerate, minimizing the chances of misinterpretations, hurt feelings, and escalation of disagreements into conflicts.

Achieve Desired Outcomes: Diplomatic communication is strategic and persuasive, enabling individuals to convey their message effectively while being mindful of others' perspectives, increasing the likelihood of achieving favorable outcomes and cooperation.

Passage

The Kenyan real estate market has grown rapidly over the past two decades. This is demonstrated by its contribution to the country's gross domestic product (GDP) which grew from 10.5% in 2000 to 13.8% in 2016. The growth was driven by factors such as improved roads, utility connections, upgrade of key airports and rapid urbanization. These factors have therefore led to development in the different real estate sectors.

One of the segments in the Kenyan real estate that has realized rapid growth is the office sector. This has been in line with the improving economy as firms expand their operations. This expansion has been brought about by the clientele changing their preferences to demand for world-class serviced offices. As the dynamics of office space design and demands continue to unfold, more developers are offering semi-fitted offices by providing facilities such as partitions and kitchen cabinets.

In addition, the retail business sector has also seen the continued growth of two key concepts, that is, the mall concept and the industrial parks concept. These concepts have developed to meet the changing tastes of the growing middle class. International and local developers have quickly grabbed the opportunity to tap into the ready market with the mall concept. This has made Kenya become the second largest provider of mall space in Africa. The most notable developments are the Two Rivers Mall, Garden City and The Hub.

On the other hand, the industrial sector has been receiving a gradual change that has led to the growth of modern industrial parks. These parks include Tatu City Industrial Park, Infinity Industrial Park and Tilisi. The growth of the parks has been due to a changing clientele who prefer high-quality products. The parks allow for modern retailing, distribution and manufacturing practices of high-quality products. The modern parks are also built in such a way that they allow for a concept known as Live, Work and Play. To achieve this, the new industrial parks are now moving to areas within Nairobi's periphery such as Kiambu and Machakos counties where they are easily accessible and are still near key airports and railway terminals.

Another real estate sector that has seen rapid growth is the residential sector which has recorded high demand due to the nationwide housing deficit. Currently, the nationwide housing deficit stands at 200,000 units annually and an accumulated deficit of over 2 million units. As a result, gated community estates are increasing with locations such as Kiambu and Machakos counties becoming favourites. In these estates, developers are increasingly applying low-cost housing construction methods such as alternative building technologies that are known to reduce construction costs by as much as 50%.

Dual branding is another concept in the real estate sector. This concept, which involves having serviced apartments and a hotel in the same building, is gaining popularity among investors. This is because the hospitality sector largely depends on tourism which has been experiencing an increase in the number of visitors. Meetings, incentives, conferences and exhibitions (MICE) have played a key role in increasing international tourists who are the backbone of the hospitality industry.

Infrastructural development has seen the value of land go up. Investors are increasingly buying land intending to sell in the future with the hope that value will have increased. Moreover, to attract buyers, developers are also adopting agribusiness as value addition to plots intended for sale and offering returns on a seasonal basis to clients.

Therefore, with the continued interest in the Kenyan property market by both local and international players, there is need to adopt measures to address issues that are hindering the growth of the sector. Such measures include continuous investment in infrastructure and improvement of the legal environment among others. If these measures are put in place, the Kenyan real estate will continue to improve and eventually cater for the housing deficit in the country.

14. (5 marks)

(a) State the meaning of each of the following words and phrase as used in the passage: (i) upgrade; (ii) notable; (iii) led to; (iv) favourites; (v) hindering.

Answer

(i) upgrade: In the context of the passage, "upgrade" refers to the improvement or modernization of key airports, indicating an enhancement of infrastructure.

(ii) notable: As used in the passage, "notable" means significant or prominent, highlighting the most remarkable or important real estate developments.

(iii) led to: In the passage, "led to" signifies resulted in or caused, indicating that certain factors have caused development in real estate sectors.

(iv) favourites: "Favourites" refers to locations that are preferred or most popular, indicating that Kiambu and Machakos counties are increasingly becoming preferred for gated community estates.

(v) hindering: "Hindering" means impeding or obstructing, suggesting that certain issues are preventing or slowing down the growth of the real estate sector.
