July 2021

COMMUNICATION

Exam Code: 2902/104, 2908/104, 2912/104, 2918/104, 2920/104, 2921/104

Duration: 3 hours

Period: July 2021

1. (4 marks)

State four situations that may prompt managers to convene meetings in an organization.

Answer

Problem Solving: When critical issues or challenges arise that require collective brainstorming and collaborative solutions from various team members or departments.

Decision Making: To make important organizational decisions that necessitate input, discussion, and agreement from key stakeholders or decision-makers within the company.

Information Sharing: To disseminate crucial information, updates, or policy changes to a group of employees efficiently and ensure consistent understanding across the organization.

Planning and Strategy Development: When formulating new strategies, projects, or initiatives that require collective planning, goal setting, and task allocation among relevant personnel.

2. (3 marks)

State three ways in which an interviewee should conduct himself when responding to questions during an interview.

Answer

Be Honest and Authentic: Provide truthful answers and represent oneself genuinely, avoiding exaggeration or fabrication of skills and experiences.

Maintain Eye Contact and Positive Body Language: Establish and maintain eye contact with the interviewer and use confident, open body language to demonstrate engagement and sincerity.

Answer Clearly and Concisely: Respond to questions directly and articulate answers in a clear, concise manner, avoiding rambling or overly lengthy responses, while ensuring all points are comprehensively addressed.

3. (4 marks)

List four barriers to effective oral communication.

Answer

Language Differences: Variations in language proficiency, accents, and dialects between speakers and listeners can lead to misunderstandings and misinterpretations.

Noise and Distractions: Environmental noise, interruptions, or distractions in the communication setting can impede the listener's ability to focus and accurately receive the message.

Poor Listening Skills: Lack of active listening, inattentiveness, or tendency to interrupt can prevent the receiver from fully understanding the speaker's intended message.

Emotional Barriers: Emotional states such as anger, stress, or anxiety in either the speaker or listener can distort the message and hinder effective communication.

4. (2 marks)

State two messages that may be relayed through a hug.

Answer

Affection and Love: A hug can convey feelings of love, care, and affection towards another person, expressing emotional warmth and closeness.

Support and Comfort: Hugs often communicate empathy, support, and comfort, offering reassurance and solidarity during times of distress or need.

5. (3 marks)

List three qualities of a good report.

Answer

Accuracy and Objectivity: A good report should present factually correct information, data, and analysis, maintaining an objective and unbiased perspective throughout.

Clarity and Conciseness: The report should be written in clear, straightforward language, avoiding jargon and ambiguity, while being concise and to the point, focusing on essential information.

Well-Structured and Organized: A quality report follows a logical structure with clear headings, subheadings, and sections, making it easy to read, navigate, and understand the information presented.

6. (4 marks)

State four advantages that an organization may get by communicating to its customers through WhatsApp.

Answer

Direct and Personal Communication: WhatsApp allows for direct, one-on-one communication with customers, enabling personalized interactions, addressing individual queries, and building stronger customer relationships.

Real-Time Interaction and Instant Feedback: The platform facilitates real-time conversations, enabling instant responses to customer inquiries, quick resolution of issues, and immediate feedback collection.

Cost-Effective Communication Channel: WhatsApp is a cost-efficient communication tool, reducing expenses associated with traditional methods like phone calls or postal mail, especially for mass communication and customer support.

Multimedia Sharing Capabilities: Businesses can easily share multimedia content such as images, videos, and documents via WhatsApp, enhancing communication richness and engagement with customers.

7. (3 marks)

List three uses of personal information in a résumé.

Answer

Identification and Contact: Personal information such as name, phone number, and email address enables employers to identify the applicant and establish contact for further communication.

Professional Summary Context: A brief personal summary or career objective provides context about the applicant's professional goals, skills, and what they seek in a job, helping employers quickly assess suitability.

Optional Demographic Information: In some regions or for certain applications, optional demographic information might be included for diversity and equal opportunity monitoring, though this is not universally required or recommended.

8. (3 marks)

State three advantages of using visual aids when communicating.

Answer

Enhance Understanding and Retention: Visual aids simplify complex information, making it easier for audiences to understand and remember key points, improving comprehension and recall.

Increase Engagement and Interest: Visuals capture attention, stimulate interest, and make presentations more dynamic and engaging, holding the audience's focus effectively.

Support Diverse Learning Styles: Visual aids cater to visual learners, complementing auditory information and accommodating different learning preferences within an audience, ensuring broader message reception.

9. (3 marks)

List three reasons why the receiver of a message may fail to give feedback.

Answer

Lack of Understanding or Clarity: If the message is unclear, ambiguous, or not easily understood by the receiver, they may be unable to formulate relevant or meaningful feedback.

Fear of Negative Consequences: Receivers may hesitate to provide feedback due to concerns about potential negative repercussions, such as criticism or retaliation from the sender or superiors.

Perceived Lack of Value or Impact: If receivers believe their feedback is not valued, will be ignored, or won't make a difference, they may be demotivated to invest time and effort in providing it.

10. (3 marks)

State three roles of internal communication in an organization.

Answer

Employee Engagement and Motivation: Internal communication keeps employees informed, involved, and connected, boosting morale, engagement, and commitment to organizational goals.

Alignment and Cohesion: It ensures that all employees are aligned with the organization's mission, values, and strategic objectives, fostering a sense of unity and shared purpose.

Change Management and Culture Building: Internal communication plays a crucial role in managing organizational change, facilitating smooth transitions, and shaping a positive and productive organizational culture.

11. (16 marks)

Student enrolment at Sololo Technical College has been declining over the past two years. As the Assistant Administrator, you have been requested by the Board to investigate the matter and write a report making recommendations. Assume you have completed your investigation and write the report.

Answer

REPORT ON DECLINING STUDENT ENROLMENT AT SOLOLO TECHNICAL COLLEGE

To: The Board of Governors, Sololo Technical College
From: [Your Name], Assistant Administrator
Date: July 27, 2021
Subject: Investigation into Declining Student Enrolment and Recommendations for Improvement

Executive Summary:
This report addresses the concerning trend of declining student enrolment at Sololo Technical College over the past two years. Conducted at the request of the Board, this investigation seeks to identify the root causes of this decline and propose actionable recommendations to reverse the trend and enhance enrolment figures. The report is based on data analysis, stakeholder interviews, and market research.

1. Introduction:
Sololo Technical College has experienced a consistent decrease in student enrolment over the last two academic years. This report presents findings from an investigation into this issue, aiming to pinpoint contributing factors and suggest strategies for enrolment recovery and growth.

2. Methodology:
The investigation employed a mixed-methods approach, including:

Enrolment Data Analysis: Review of enrolment statistics for the past five years, identifying trends, patterns, and specific program areas experiencing the most significant declines.

Stakeholder Interviews: Interviews conducted with current students, prospective students, faculty members, and administrative staff to gather diverse perspectives on enrolment challenges and potential solutions.

Market Research: Analysis of competitor institutions, industry trends, and demographic changes in the region to understand the external factors influencing enrolment.

Review of College Resources and Programs: Assessment of current academic programs, facilities, marketing strategies, and student support services to identify areas for improvement.

3. Findings:
The investigation revealed several interconnected factors contributing to the decline in student enrolment:

Increased Competition: Emergence of new technical colleges and vocational training centers in neighboring regions offering similar programs, intensifying competition for prospective students.

Economic Hardship: Economic downturn in the region has reduced the ability of families to afford technical education, impacting enrolment rates, particularly for fee-based programs.

Limited Program Relevance and Awareness: Some programs offered may not fully align with current market demands, and there is a lack of awareness among prospective students about the college’s offerings and career prospects.

Inadequate Marketing and Outreach: Current marketing and recruitment strategies are insufficient to effectively reach and attract prospective students, especially in the digital age.

Facility and Resource Constraints: Perceived inadequacy of college facilities, outdated equipment, or limited student support services may deter potential applicants.

4. Recommendations:
To address the enrolment decline and enhance student intake, the following recommendations are proposed:

Program Modernization and Diversification: Review and update existing programs to align with current industry needs and introduce new, in-demand technical courses to attract a broader range of students.

Enhanced Marketing and Recruitment: Develop a comprehensive marketing strategy leveraging digital platforms, social media, and targeted advertising campaigns to increase college visibility and appeal. Implement proactive outreach programs to secondary schools and community events.

Financial Aid and Scholarships: Explore options to offer more financial aid, scholarships, and payment plans to make technical education more accessible and affordable for students from diverse economic backgrounds.

Infrastructure Improvement: Invest in upgrading college facilities, modernizing equipment, and enhancing student support services to create a more attractive and conducive learning environment.

Strengthen Community and Industry Partnerships: Forge stronger relationships with local industries and community organizations to offer internships, apprenticeships, and job placement assistance, demonstrating clear career pathways for graduates.

5. Conclusion:
The declining student enrolment at Sololo Technical College is a complex issue influenced by increased competition, economic factors, program relevance, marketing effectiveness, and resource limitations. Implementing the recommendations outlined in this report will enable Sololo Technical College to enhance its attractiveness, competitiveness, and relevance in the technical education sector, thereby reversing the enrolment decline and ensuring sustainable growth. Continuous monitoring and evaluation of these strategies will be crucial for long-term success.

6. Next Steps:
It is recommended that the Board of Governors reviews this report and establishes an Enrolment Revitalization Committee tasked with developing a detailed action plan and budget for implementing these recommendations. Immediate focus should be placed on enhancing marketing efforts and modernizing key programs.

12. (6 marks)

(b) Outline three challenges that may be faced when conducting an online meeting.

Answer

Technical Difficulties: Participants may encounter technical issues such as poor internet connectivity, software glitches, or hardware problems, disrupting meeting flow and participation.

Reduced Engagement and Interaction: Online meetings can sometimes lead to lower levels of engagement and interaction compared to face-to-face meetings, making it harder to maintain participant attention and foster collaborative discussions.

Communication Barriers: Non-verbal cues can be less apparent in online settings, and language or cultural differences can be amplified, leading to misunderstandings or misinterpretations during communication.

13. (8 marks)

Explain four organizational barriers that may lead to ineffective communication.

Answer

Hierarchical Structure: Rigid organizational hierarchies with multiple layers can impede the flow of communication, leading to delays, distortions, and filtered information as messages travel up and down the chain of command.

Information Overload: Excessive information disseminated within an organization can overwhelm employees, making it difficult to process, prioritize, and effectively respond to critical messages, leading to communication breakdown.

Lack of Clear Communication Channels: Absence of well-defined communication channels and protocols can result in confusion about how and where to send or receive information, leading to miscommunication and inefficiencies.

Poor Communication Culture: An organizational culture that does not value open communication, feedback, or active listening, or that tolerates poor communication practices, can create significant barriers to effective information exchange and understanding.

13. (8 marks)

(b) Describe the following types of interviews that may be conducted in an organization: (i) Appraisal; (ii) Selection; (iii) Promotion; (iv) Exit.

Answer

Appraisal Interview: This type of interview is conducted to evaluate an employee's job performance over a specific period. It involves reviewing achievements, identifying areas for improvement, setting future goals, and providing feedback for professional development and growth.

Selection Interview: Selection interviews are used during the recruitment process to assess candidates for job openings. They aim to evaluate skills, experience, cultural fit, and suitability for a specific role, helping employers choose the best candidate from a pool of applicants.

Promotion Interview: Promotion interviews are conducted when an employee is being considered for a higher position within the organization. These interviews assess the employee's readiness for increased responsibilities, leadership potential, and suitability for the promoted role.

Exit Interview: Exit interviews are conducted with employees who are leaving the organization. The purpose is to gather feedback on their experiences, reasons for leaving, and suggestions for improvement, helping the organization understand employee satisfaction and identify areas for retention enhancement.

Passage

Climate change is no longer a hidden phenomenon. It is happening globally and there is enough scientific evidence to this effect. The shifting rainfall patterns, including the increased intensity of the El Nino and La Nina phenomena, and the rising temperatures that bring about erratic occurrences of tropical storms and drought, are evidence of changing weather patterns. The changes portend damaging after-effects, yet they are partly driven by human activity.

Kenya is extremely vulnerable to changes of climate because its economy is largely dependent on rainfall. For example, crop production and livestock keeping rely heavily on rain. Given that agriculture supports about 75 per cent of Kenya's population, erratic weather poses a threat to food security which is already a major problem in the country. Besides, agricultural produce forms the bulk of Kenya's exports. Bad weather conditions have drastically reduced agricultural exports resulting in lower Gross Domestic Product (GDP) and less foreign exchange.

In addition, Kenya is heavily reliant on hydro-electric power, a sustainable source of renewable energy. Long spells of drought greatly affect hydro-electric power generation. Further, climate change has affected tourism negatively. Tourism, a major foreign exchange earner, is a highly sensitive industry since climate change affects a wide range of environmental resources. For example, changes in rainfall patterns cause grass levels to either increase or decrease, thus causing migration or death of wildlife. This has led to a reduction in wildlife population and extinction of some species, thus affecting the number of tourists visiting Kenya.

The predicted global impact of climate change is believed to be more intense in tropical areas, where Kenya lies, than in temperate zones. For instance, it is expected that global food production needs to increase by a minimum of 50 to 70 per cent by 2050 because the

population is projected to rise to 9.6 billion from the present 7 billion. On the other hand, lenient predictions forecast that global crop yields will decline by about 15 per cent per decade if appropriate climate adaptation is not put in place. These effects foretell a distressing future if efforts are not made to make agriculture climate-smart. While increased agricultural productivity is vital to addressing the predictions of global food production shortfalls, the same agriculture contributes to emission of the greenhouse gases responsible for climate change. In fact, it is estimated that agriculture contributes about 30 per cent of global greenhouse gas emissions. This calls for an urgent need to aggressively adopt Climate Smart Agriculture (CSA).

CSA is aimed at achieving what is termed as triple_wins. These include sustainably increasing agricultural productivity and incomes since agriculture is the backbone of the Kenyan economy. It also includes adapting agricultural systems, building resilience to climate change and reducing greenhouse emissions. CSA has given rise to the Kenyan Climate Smart Agriculture Project (KCSAP); a government initiative supported by the World Bank that is being implemented in 24 counties.

KCSAP performs several functions. One such function is to strengthen climate-smart agricultural research and seed systems. This supports the development, validation and adoption of seeds to boost the agricultural sector. KCSAP is also concerned with scaling up climate- smart agricultural practices by financing interventions that promote and facilitate improved agricultural technologies, innovations and management practices. This is aimed at achieving increased productivity, enhanced resilience and reduced greenhouse gas emissions.

KCSAP further supports agro-weather, market climate and advisory services by financing the development of agro-weather forecasting and marketing information systems. Besides, it finances activities related to national and county project coordination and management such as annual work plans and budgets. In addition, it has an emergency contingency response which involves financing recovery efforts from natural and man-made disasters such as desert locust control.

Although Kenya has faced considerable challenges in responding to climate change, some farmers have made significant adjustments in farming practices. It is, therefore, hoped that individual and government initiatives such as KCSAP will cushion farmers, and the nation at large, from food insecurity. More importantly, it is anticipated that the agricultural sector will be boosted as the backbone of the economy.

Adapted from: "Daily Nation" Saturday, February 29, 2020.

14. (5 marks)

(a) State the meaning of each of the following words and phrase as used in the passage: (i) erratic; (ii) drastically; (iii) a wide range of; (iv) backbone; (v) man-made.

Answer

(i) erratic: In the context of the passage, "erratic" means unpredictable or irregular, describing weather patterns that are inconsistent and unreliable.

(ii) drastically: As used in the passage, "drastically" means severely or extremely, indicating a significant and intense reduction in agricultural exports.

(iii) a wide range of: In the passage, "a wide range of" refers to a large variety of environmental resources that are affected by climate change, highlighting the extensive impact.

(iv) backbone: "Backbone" means the main support or essential foundation, emphasizing that agriculture is the fundamental basis of the Kenyan economy.

(v) man-made: "Man-made" means caused or created by human activity, indicating that climate change is partly caused by human actions.

14. (5 marks)

(b) Highlight, according to the passage, why Kenya is extremely vulnerable to changes in climate.

Answer

According to the passage, Kenya is extremely vulnerable to climate change primarily because its economy is heavily dependent on rainfall. Agriculture, which supports 75% of the population and forms the bulk of exports, relies on consistent rainfall, making the country susceptible to erratic weather patterns and droughts caused by climate change.

14. (8 marks)

(c) In about 110 words, and according to the passage, write a summary on the functions of KCSAP.

Answer

The Kenyan Climate Smart Agriculture Project (KCSAP) aims to achieve "triple-wins": enhancing agricultural productivity and incomes, adapting farming systems to climate change, and reducing greenhouse emissions. KCSAP strengthens climate-smart agricultural research and seed systems, supporting seed development and adoption. It scales up climate-smart practices by funding interventions that promote improved agricultural technologies and management. KCSAP finances agro-weather, market climate, and advisory services, and supports national and county project coordination. It also provides emergency contingency funds for natural and man-made disasters, aiming to boost the agricultural sector as the backbone of Kenya's economy and ensure food security.
