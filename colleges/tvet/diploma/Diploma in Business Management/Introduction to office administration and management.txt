<p>All kinds of institution –social, political, religious .etc. are formed by groups of individual <strong>to </strong>pursue common goals and objectives. The attainment of these commonly defined objectives shall be fulfillment of these&nbsp; groups.</p>
<p>A business enterprise for example has the objectives of maximizing its profits through increased output and sales and to create an image in the society in general as a good employer and a good producer of quality products. It also contributes to the economic growth</p>
<p>If the activities of various individuals in the enterprise are not coordinated the results will be chaos. Each man in the enterprise shall be pushing his own &nbsp;activity&nbsp; with no collective objective in the mind, but if there is an agency that directs the activities of various individuals in an organization the results will be&nbsp; synthesis and cohesion. <p>Management can therefore be defined as principally a task of planning, coordinating, and motivating the efforts of others towards the specific objectives. In brief, management is the task of unifying efforts &nbsp;efficiently to achieve well defined objectives. The resources available to an organization remain idle till they are directed by the dynamic human effort. Management therefore aims at seeing that the job gets &nbsp;done efficiently.</p>

<p>Administration is a higher level activity while management is a lower level function Administration is determinative function concerned with the determination of objectives and policies while management is an executive function involving the implementation of policies and direction of efforts for the achievement of objectives.</p>
<p>At every level of management an individual management performs both types of function. Every manager spends a part of his time in administration and remaining time on management.</p>
<p>Managers at a higher level spends more time on administration function i.e. determination of objectives and policies, while managers at lower level devote greater time on management function. As one descends the organization hierarchy the emphasis is on execution of plans increases. For the purpose of this course office management and office administration will be considered synonymous.</p><div class="code-block code-block-1" style="margin: 8px 0; clear: both;">

<p><strong>AN ORGANISATION</strong></p>
<p>Organization is an element of management An organization occurs when two or more people work together and coordinate their activities to achieve common goals. It refers to structure of relationships among individuals. It can also be defined as a social unit that is structured and managed to meet a need or to achieve common objectives and collective goals. <p><strong>Organizational structures</strong></p>
<p>An organization structure is the basic framework within which the decision making behavior occurs It shows the different posts in organization and the responsibilities attached to these different posts</p>
<p><u>Various types of organizational structure</u></p>
<ol>
<li>Line organization (military)</li>
</ol>
<p>The authority move from top levels to lower levels. There is direct vertical relationship connecting the positions at each level with those above and below</p>
<p><em>Advantages</em></p>
<ul>
<li>Simple and is readily understood but the staff</li>
<li>Responsibility for work at different levels in clear</li>
<li>Lines of communication both upward and downward are indicated</li>
<li>Chain of command are shown</li>
<li>Economic and effective</li>
<li>Discipline can easily be maintained</li>
</ul>
<p><em>Disadvantages</em></p>
<ul>
<li>Works on a dictatorial basis</li>
<li>The organization is rigid and in inflexible</li>
<li>Each department tends to become an empire of its own</li>
<li>Where there is no overall planning, there are possibilities of lack of coordination of work at different departments</li>
</ul>
<ol start="2">
<li>Staff organization(functional organization)</li>
</ol>
<p>Various departments are established and each department is required to perform some specific duties e.g. production, marketing, finance etc.</p><div class="code-block code-block-1" style="margin: 8px 0; clear: both;">

<p><u>Advantages</u></p>
<ul>
<li>There is specialization by each department and responsibilities is also fixed</li>

<li>3<strong>. Line and staff organization </strong></li>

<p>Other service departments e.g. accounts, sales etc. provide service to all departments</p>

<p style="text-align: center;"><strong>PRINCIPLES OF ORGANISATION</strong></p>
<p><strong><em>1,Span of control</em></strong>-refers to the number of employees who report to a manager or number of subordinates that one manager can supervise directly. It is also called span of&nbsp; management ,span of supervision etc.</p>
<p>Span of control will depend on the following factors</p>
<ul>
<li>Ability of both subordinates and manager</li>
<li>Level of superior hierarchy</li>
<li>Faith in subordinates</li>
<li>Nature and type of work</li>
<li>Physical dispersion of subordinates</li>
<li>Need for communication</li>
<li>Types of organization and management</li>
</ul>

<p><strong>Barriers to delegation</strong></p>
<p>Managers may be reluctant to delegate because of several reasons</p>
<ul>
<li>Negative personal attitudes</li>
<li>Un receptiveness of other peoples view</li>
<li>Unwillingness to let go</li>
<li>Unwillingness to let others make mistakes</li>
<li>Perceived threat</li>
<li>A feeling of inadequacy</li>
</ul>
<p>Subordinates may require delegation because</p>
<ul>
<li>Lack of training</li>
<li>Lack of incentives</li>
</ul>
<p><u>Guidance for effective delegation</u></p>
<ul>
<li>Grant proper amount of authority</li>
<li>Define the results expected</li>
<li>Consider the capabilities of the subordinates</li>
<li>Make sure the authority is clearly stated</li>
<li>A supportive climate</li>
<li>Free communication</li>
<li>Develop a willingness to delegate</li>
<li>Follow unity of command and chain of command</li>
</ul>
<p><em>Advantages of delegation</em></p>
<ul>
<li>Improves morale</li>
<li>Decisions are likely to be better</li>
<li>Enables superior to perform higher levels of work</li>
<li>Enhance prompt action</li>
<li>Tool of development of subordinates</li>
</ul>
<p><em>Disadvantages of delegation</em></p>
<ul>
<li>May make serious mistakes</li>
<li>Mayn’t put in maximum efforts as the supervisor would do</li>
<li>If you assign responsibility without authority delegated work will not be properly done</li>
<li>If you delegate work to someone who avoids decisions he will not make mistakes but the work will suffer</li>
</ul>

<p>3,<strong> DECENTRALISATION OF AUTHORITY</strong></p>
<p>Decentralization is the systematic effort to delegate to the lowest level &nbsp;authority except that what can only be exercised at the central point .Decentralization is the opposite of centralization which is the &nbsp;systematic and consistent reservation of authority in a few hands at top. Decentralization implies a greater power to persons and places away from the center. It also means a lot of important decisions will be made at the lower levels without being subjected to prior approval of higher authority.</p><div class="code-block code-block-1" style="margin: 8px 0; clear: both;">

<p><strong>Centralization</strong></p>
<p>This is an arrangement whereby certain services common to all department like typing, filling supply of stationery etc are provided from a central place</p>
<p><strong>Departmentalization</strong></p>
<p>The opposite of centralization is decentralization or departmentalization whereby each department provides for its services</p>
<p>Both&nbsp; these arrangements have their respective advantages and disadvantages</p>
<p><em>Advantages of centralization</em></p>
<ul>
<li>This arrangement helps to follow the principle of specialization</li>
<li>Its economical to the organization</li>
<li>Reduces the number of furniture and equipment as well</li>
<li>Reduces the duplication of work and activities</li>
<li>There is an even and fair distribution of work in a centralised office</li>
<li>This arrangement make easy to follow staff leave rotation policy</li>
<li>A broader experience of the work of the office is given to one place which in turn&nbsp; means more facilities for staff training and development</li>
</ul>
<p><strong><em>Advantages of departmentalization</em></strong></p>
<ul>
<li>Departmental office services speed up work as there is less volume of work and documents</li>
<li>Its suitable for certain tasks of confidential nature like accounting personal work</li>
<li>Departmentalization reduces need for messengers and collection services between departments</li>
<li>Its offers personal contact between workers and executives</li>
<li>It reduces dissatisfaction of staff at the segregation of sexes that results from the creation of central services department of this type</li>
<li>This arrangement helps the workers to learn more in addition to their routine work</li>
<li>Sometimes lack of coordination and cooperation departments can result in delays, Departmentalization system avoids such delays</li>
</ul>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<ol>
<li>The advantages of centralization are disadvantages of departmentalization and vice versa.</li>
</ol>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p>Typing pool.</p>
<p><em>Advantages of centralization of typing pool</em></p>
<ul>
<li>Make economical use of typist labor as the typing pool supervisor spreads work evenly and fairly equal among the typists.</li>
<li>Sickness and holiday difficulties are easily overcome as reallocation can easily be done.</li>
<li>Training of new typist is easily done as all typist jointly contribute toward the training of s new member</li>
<li>Interruption to work is avoided as telephone and all other queries are dealt with by supervisor.</li>
<li>Noise especially emanating from manual typewriters is confined to one place.</li>
<li>Less time is wasted compared to departmental typing.</li>
<li>Higher standard of work can be achieved as there tends to be competition among typists.</li>
</ul>
<p><em>Disadvantages of centralization</em></p>
<ul>
<li>Lack of personal contact between the typist and the executive which to a large extent is supposed to create a good working relationship.</li>
<li>Increased need for messengers to collect scripts from the various department to the typing pool and back.</li>
<li>No specialization for particular work</li>
<li>Don’t give secretaries a chance of upward mobility.</li>
<li>It provides an atmosphere of gossip leading to waste of valuable time</li>
<li>Tendency by the supervisor to over work certain while giving less work to others.</li>
</ul>
<p>CENTRALISED &nbsp;FILING</p>
<p><em>Advantages</em></p>
<ul>
<li>Economy of space can be achieved.</li>
<li>A uniform system of filing can be established throughout the organizations</li>
<li>Files movement is efficiently controlled. This enables an efficient follow up system.</li>
<li>It avoids duplication where each department needs a copy a certain document</li>
<li>Suitable qualified staff are appointed or trained for the purpose</li>
<li>Less costly as few staff are appointed or handle for the purpose</li>
</ul>
<p><em>Disadvantages of filing</em></p>
<ul>
<li>Retrieval procedure may be longer than if it was localized</li>
<li>Filing staff may not have specialized departmental knowledge which is helpful when filling some certain documents.</li>
<li>It is desirable for confidential documents such as personnel records ,salary, legal documents etc.</li>
</ul>

<p>The office building should not only be located at a suitable site, it also should be of the proper size and shape. Its design and arrangement should be such as to adequately serve the particular requirement of the enterprise</p>
<p><u>Owned office</u></p>
<p>Fully owned office has the following</p>
<p><em>Advantages</em></p>
<ul>
<li>Can be planned and designed to the exact requirements of the organization</li>
<li>Can be constructed in a suitable location to desire all the accidental advantages</li>
<li>The surplus space can be rented out</li>
<li>An attractive office building will enhance the prestige of the business with its staff, customers and the public</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>Buying an existing building</strong></p>
<ul>
<li>It saves time spent on construction or if its available at a bargain price</li>
<li>It may also be the only option in a specific location where all building space has already been occupied</li>
<li>But the plan and design of such buildings may not fit the exact requirements’</li>
<li>And the location may not be a suitable one</li>
<li>It’s of course possible to adapt or alter the design to suit the requirement but it will mean additional cost</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>Renting or leasing</strong></p>
<ul>
<li>This may be convenient</li>
<li>Arrangement for medium and small offices and newly started firms</li>
<li>Suitable where the requirements of space is limited or the business isn’t stable or permanent character</li>
<li>The cost of owning office accommodation is beyond the capacity of the firm</li>
</ul>
<p><em>Disadvantages</em></p>
<ul>
<li>Its rarely possible to get the accommodation exactly suited to the requirements</li>
<li>To get it in the most suited location</li>
<li>Leased rented space can’t be adopted or altered suit the requirements or even possible to sub-lease surplus space</li>
</ul>

<p><u>Other aspects</u></p>
<ol>
<li>Size-</li>
<li>–The size of the office building or the amount of the office space must be adequate not only for the present requirement but also for possible future expansion. The guiding principle regarding size is that it should provide adequate space for the staff and equipment to allow the most efficient performance of both.</li>
</ol>
<ul>
<li>Shape large</li>
</ul>
<ol>
<li>A square or a rectangular shape is better than n a narrow one as the latter will obviously necessitate much working back and forth by the office staff and may not allow the most profitable utilization of&nbsp; the space.</li>
<li>Lighting and/ventilation</li>
<li>Good lighting and ventilation is essential for ensuring a comfortable environment and for enhancing the morale and efficiency of the staff. The office should have adequate number of large windows, skylights etc. to allow entry of maximum light and free circulation of natural air.</li>
</ol>
<ul>
<li>Staff and customer convenience</li>
<li>–Careful attention should be given to arrangement for convenience and amenities of the staff and customers. The arrangement of the rooms should be such that customers can have easy access to the department requiring frequent visits from them eg cash department, sales department etc. Adequate provision of lifts and elevators(if necessary) cloak rooms, washrooms water fountains ,canteens for the convenience of the staff.</li>
</ul>
<ol>
<li>Cost of office accommodation</li>
<li>The cost factor will be ultimately determining the final location and selection of accommodation for the office. The cost of the office not only depends on the location of the office building or space but also depends on factors like size of space, internal arrangement etc. The aim should to strike a balance between the requirement and the capacity to bear the cost.</li>
</ol>

<p><strong>Cost of office accommodation</strong></p>
<p>The cost factor will dictate the final selection of accommodation for the office</p>

<p><strong>Location of departments</strong></p>
<ul>
<li>Departments dealing with public like reception and mail office must be close to the entrance</li>
<li>Those requiring some concentration and much thinking like planning or M.D’s office should be kept a bit far to allow them to work in quieter atmosphere</li>
<li>The department with heavy machinery and equipment should be kept on ground floor</li>
<li>Those which serve other department should be kept centrally eg typing pool</li>
<li>Department should be located in such that toilets, wash basins and other facilities are within the reach of all</li>
<li>Some departments require natural light e.g. drawing department. They should be located accordingly</li>
<li>Some departments have very close relationship regarding their activities, and thus should be close to each other e.g. sales and credit</li>
</ul>

<p><strong>Temperature and humidity</strong></p>
<p>The temperature and humidity of the office rooms should be maintained at the proper level as too hot and cold humid atmosphere induces physical discomfort and affects the efficiency of the clerks</p>
<p>Noise affects the efficiency of office staff to a great extent .Its important to control noise that may be external or internal</p>
<p>External noise may be caused by traffic movements on the street, noise from neighboring premises, factories etc.</p>
<p><strong>Causes of internal noise</strong></p>
<ul>
<li>Machines movements</li>
<li>Movement and conversation of clerks, visitors</li>
<li>Creaking doors</li>
<li>Calling bells, telephones</li>
<li>Shifting of furniture from one place to another</li>
</ul>
<p><strong><u>Measures to prevent internal noise</u></strong></p><div class="code-block code-block-1" style="margin: 8px 0; clear: both;">

<ul>
<li>Carpets or rubber mats spread on the floor will reduce the sounds caused by the movements of the clerks and other people</li>
<li>Felt pads should be placed below office machines which create noise in operation</li>
<li>Calling bells can be replaced with buzzers</li>
<li>Telephone may be kept in sound -proof booths to reduce the sound</li>
<li>Clerks must be instructed to maintain calmness</li>
<li>Workers must be engaged fully during the office hours so that they don’t waste time over idle gossip</li>
<li>Banging or creaking doors should be fitted with rubber or felt steps to reduce sound</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>Measures to prevent external noise&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; </strong></p>
<ul>
<li>Choosing the site of office away from noisy area</li>
<li>Affixing double glazing windows and doors</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>Office safety</strong></p>
<p>Accidents can do happen to office employees, serious accidents may cause permanent disability and at least prolonged hospitalization</p>
<p><strong><u>Nature of accidents</u></strong></p>
<p>Accidents in offices may be caused by</p>
<ul>
<li>Exposed electric wire in loose connection</li>
<li>Slippery floors or loose carpeting</li>
<li>Open drawers of desks or filing cabinets</li>
<li>Tilting backwards in reclining chairs</li>
<li>Sharp edges of office equipment, broken glass etc.</li>
<li>Unguarded moving parts of office machines</li>
<li>Pencils, sharpeners, pins etc.</li>
<li>Running or rapid walking along stairways or through doorways</li>
<li>Reading while walking along passages</li>
<li>Inadequate lighting or worn out tread on stairways</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>Causes of accidents</strong></p>
<ol>
<li>Mechanical causes</li>
<li>Physiological causes</li>
<li>Psychological causes</li>
</ol>
<p><strong>Measures for ensuring safety</strong></p>
<ul>
<li>Provision of safe working place</li>
<li>Safeguarding hazardous machines and equipment</li>
<li>Provision of safety clothing</li>
<li>Maintenance of orderliness and cleanliness</li>
<li>Provision of first aid service</li>
<li>Provision of fire lighting equipment</li>
<li>Safety education and training of employees</li>
<li>Promoting safety consciousness</li>
</ul>

<p>In large organizations, office security is a major problem for office managers</p>
<p>Everyday risks pilferage or theft of cash and materials by staff or public have to be faced by almost all organization</p>
<p>To protect confidentiality of office secrets as well as the assets and employees against such risks, an adequate security arrangement for the office is essential</p><div class="code-block code-block-1" style="margin: 8px 0; clear: both;">

<p><strong>Causes of security hazards</strong></p>
<ul>
<li>No check on unauthorized and undesirable visitors</li>
<li>Unguarded entrances and exits of the office building and compound</li>
<li>Leaving windows and skylight opens</li>
<li>Access of all and sundry to confidential documents of all kinds</li>
<li>Copying of confidential documents by unauthorized persons</li>
<li>No checking of visitors brief-cases</li>
<li>Access to cash and safes by too many people and keeping of too much cash on the premises</li>
<li>No control over keys of safes and doors</li>
<li>No check on taking of files and documents out of the office by staff</li>
<li>Staff talking freely of confidential matters inside and outside the office</li>
<li>Cars and good vehicle left unguarded in places where these can be tampered with</li>
<li>No system of checking in and out of the staff and outsiders leaving open chances of pilferage of stationery, office equipment etc.</li>
<li>No security vetting of newly recruited staff</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>Security measures likely to be effective</strong></p>
<p><strong>Safeguarding the premises</strong></p>
<p>Closing all doors, windows and sky lights</p>
<p>A fool proof burglar alarm system should be installed to alert security staff</p>
<p>Compound should be regularly patrolled by security staff</p>
<p><strong>Regulating the entry of visitors</strong></p>
<ul>
<li>Entry of all visitors should be checked</li>
<li>There should preferably be only entrance of visitors</li>
<li>Preferably each visitor should be provided with a pass</li>
<li>Unless well known to the office a visitor should be allowed to visit the department he has business with</li>
<li>Brief cases of visitors should be checked at the entry point</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>Security regulations for the staff</strong></p>
<p>Each member of the staff should be vetted for security during recruitment</p>
<ul>
<li>Security regulation for the staff should be trained carefully in consultation with the representatives</li>
<li>Each employee should be issued with an identity card with photograph and signature of the person</li>
<li>Each employee irrespective of seniority should be asked to identify himself</li>
<li>Regulation should provide for checking of briefcases and package of staff at the same time of entry and exist</li>
<li>Protecting confidentiality of mail and communication systems</li>
</ul>

<p>Consists of a large hall, or room wherein the entire clerical staff seat and work</p>

<ul>

<li>The supervisors feel easy to watch the office</li>
<li>Its possible to reduce the number of supervisors</li>
<li>The layout of the office can be altered or changed easily</li>
<li>There is more economy in arrangement of light</li>
<li>It reduces the movement of staff</li>
<li>Better working conditions possible</li>
<li>Decoration ,cleaning and maintenance can be done inexpensively</li>

<p><em>Disadvantages</em></p>

<li>The office will appear to be a crowded place</li>
<li>Top executives may not feel comfortable in an open office</li>
<li>A big hall may not be efficiently supervised</li>
</ul>

<p style="text-align: center;"><strong>CHAPTER SIX</strong></p>
<p style="text-align: center;"><strong>RECORD MANAGEMENT AND FILLING</strong></p>
<p><strong>Outline the purposes maintaining business records</strong>.</p>
<ul>
<li>To measure the progress of the business overtime.</li>
<li>Proper study of the position of the of the firm.</li>
<li>Comparison of businesses.</li>
<li>In case of dispute</li>
<li>Past records, events, progress etc. are very necessary to decide policies and plans.</li>
<li>Certain records are kept for a number of years from the legal point of view.</li>
<li>Records are good evidence in court of law in case of suits.</li>
<li>For general use.</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>Give a classification of a business records</strong></p>
<ul>
<li>Personal records</li>
<li>Correspondence records.</li>
<li>Accounting records..</li>
<li>Other business records.</li>
<li>Legal records</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>Outline the principles of record management</strong>.</p>
<ul>
<li>Verification-Records can be verified whenever needed</li>
<li>Classification-Records must be classified according to their use.</li>
<li>Records must be maintained for some justified reasons.</li>
<li>Information-The require information must be a available whenever needed.</li>
<li>Record system must be elastic in capacity so that expansion or contraction of records is possible,</li>
<li>Reasonable cost. The cost of record management must be&nbsp; a reasonable one.</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>What is filling?</strong></p>
<p>Is a process of classifying and arranging the records so that they can obtain without delay.</p>
<p><u>What are the advantages (importance) of filling</u></p>
<ol>
<li>Serves the purpose of ready reference.</li>
<li>They saves time and increase efficiency</li>
</ol>
<ul>
<li>Safeguard the documents against loss.</li>
</ul>
<ol>
<li>Files serve as reliable basis for future planning and action.</li>
<li>Past records are good evidence in case of dispute.</li>
<li>A proper control is facilitated</li>
</ol>
<p><strong>&nbsp;</strong></p>
<p><strong>What are the characteristics of a good filing system</strong></p>
<ol>
<li>Compactness-Should take up too much space especially floor space for filing cabinet</li>
<li>Accessibility</li>
<li>Simplicity</li>
<li>Simple</li>
<li>Elasticity</li>
<li>Cross-reference</li>
</ol>
<p><strong>Give the procedure of keeping documents</strong></p>
<ol>
<li>They are first classified under headings like orders invoices correspondences etc.</li>
<li>After this, they are filed in respective filed on the basis of classification system followed by the organization.</li>
<li>The documents are kept for a specific period say , 5 years based on the retention policy of the organization.</li>
<li>After this period these documents re destroyed.</li>
</ol>

<p><u>Centralized filling</u></p>
<ul>
<li>Leads to development of specialist filing staff.</li>
<li>All files are controlled in one room.</li>
<li>More supervision and control files is possible.</li>
<li>It ensures that all correspondence about the same subjects are filed together.</li>
<li>A Uniform system of filing can be established throughout the organization.</li>
</ul>
<p><u>Departmental filling</u></p>
<ul>
<li>Files are quickly made available as they are not too many.</li>
<li>As every department keeps its files within, they are readily available.</li>
<li>The filing system is not so large and therefore easier to handle.</li>
<li>It doesn’t need a specialist staff for filing and this reduces costs.</li>
<li>Its more suitable for confidential documents as they aren’t open to every member or the filing department.</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>Explain various filing classification, giving their advantages and disadvantages</strong></p>
<ol>
<li><strong><u>Alphabetical</u></strong><strong>.</strong></li>
</ol>
<p>-Documents are filed according to the first letter of either name of the sender or subject.</p>
<p>-Similar to words in English dictionary or telephone directory.</p>
<p><em>Advantages</em></p>
<ul>
<li>Convenience of grouping paper by name of the company etc.</li>
<li>Direct filing with no need for index i.e. the first letter will tell you where the document is located</li>
<li>Simple and easy to understand even explaining to new staff</li>
<li>Its possible to open one file for miscellaneous papers which can’t make up their own files</li>
</ul>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p><em>Disadvantages </em></p>
<ul>
<li>In large systems it takes longer time to find papers</li>
<li>Congestion under common names</li>
<li>For large organizations ,papers may be reasonably be filed under different headings</li>
<li>It’s difficult to forecast space requirement for different letters of the alphabet.</li>
<li>There are possibilities of a document being filed under a different name due to differences in spelling etc.</li>
</ul>
<ol start="2">
<li><strong>Numerical</strong></li>
</ol>
<p>Correspondence are arranged according to numbers rather than letters</p>
<p>This system is useful for filling orders or other items kept in numerical sequence</p>
<p>An index is necessary for locating the correct file</p>
<p><em>Advantages</em></p>
<ul>
<li>They have unlimited possibilities for expansion</li>

<p>O&amp;M refers to the systematic attempt on the part of an enterprise to improve and maintain office at a high level of efficiency.</p>

<p><u>Objectives of O&amp;M</u></p>
<ul>
<li>Eliminate waste of time, human energy, space, equipment, stores and stationery</li>
<li>Improvement in output and quality of work</li>
<li>Improvement in office services</li>
<li>Making the staff efficiency conscious</li>
</ul>
<p><u>Functions of organization and methods</u></p>
<ul>
<li>Defining precisely objectives of different functions and operations</li>
<li>Drawing up a plan of work indicating the general and specific purposes of the tasks involved, nature of the information to be procured and the sources of information to be tapped</li>
<li>Making a thorough examination of the existing procedures</li>
<li>Making detailed study of the sources of information</li>
<li>Examination of all relevant facts gathered in the course of investigation and study and suggesting new or modified procedures if found necessary</li>
<li>Once the suggestions of the O&amp;M department are accepted helping the administration and staff in implementing the suggestions</li>
</ul>
<p><u>Outline the principles which guide O&amp;M</u></p>
<ul>
<li>All unnecessary operations should be eliminated</li>
<li>all operations which can, with advantages, be combined should be so combined</li>
<li>all operations should be in their correct sequence to avoid back tracking</li>
<li>all necessary operations should be as simple as possible</li>
<li>the shorter the office work cycle the higher will be the overall productivity</li>
<li>the work flow should not only be fast but also have an even tempo</li>
</ul>
