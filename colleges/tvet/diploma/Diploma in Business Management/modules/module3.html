Office Organization

Methods of Providing Office Services

An office provides various services to the whole organization. These services may be provided either centrally or departmentally.

Centralization

An arrangement whereby certain services common to all departments (like typing, filing, supply of stationery) are provided from a central place.

Advantages

  • Follows the principle of specialization
  • Economical to the organization
  • Reduces furniture and equipment needs
  • Reduces duplication of work
  • Even and fair distribution of work
  • Easier staff leave rotation
  • Better facilities for staff training

Departmentalization (Decentralization)

Each department provides its own services independently.

Advantages

  • Speeds up work due to less volume
  • Suitable for confidential tasks
  • Reduces need for messengers
  • Offers personal contact between workers and executives
  • Helps workers learn beyond routine work
  • Avoids delays from lack of coordination

Specific Centralized Services

Typing Pool

Advantages

  • Economical use of typist labor
  • Easy management of staff absence
  • Efficient training of new typists
  • Minimized work interruptions
  • Confined noise to one area
  • Higher work standards through competition

Disadvantages

  • Lack of personal contact with executives
  • Increased need for messengers
  • Limited specialization
  • Reduced career advancement opportunities

Centralized Filing

Advantages

  • Economy of space
  • Uniform filing system
  • Efficient file movement control
  • Avoids duplication
  • Specialized staff handling
  • Cost-effective

Disadvantages

  • Longer retrieval time
  • Lack of specialized departmental knowledge
  • Not suitable for confidential documents

Factors Influencing Degree of Decentralization

  • Nature of growth of the enterprise
  • Outlook of top management
  • Size and dispersal of operations
  • Extent of dispersion
  • Nature of functions
  • Availability of able management

Key Points Summary

  • Both centralization and decentralization have their place in office organization
  • Choice depends on organization's specific needs and circumstances
  • Some services may be better centralized while others work better decentralized
  • Balance is needed between efficiency and personalization of services
  • Regular evaluation of service provision methods ensures continued effectiveness
