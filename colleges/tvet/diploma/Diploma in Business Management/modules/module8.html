Filing and Storage of Records

Records Management

Purposes of Maintaining Business Records

  • To measure business progress over time
  • To study the position of the firm
  • For business comparison
  • For dispute resolution
  • To guide policy and planning decisions
  • For legal compliance
  • As evidence in court
  • For general reference

Principles of Record Management

  • Verification - Records can be verified when needed
  • Classification - Records must be classified by use
  • Justification - Records must have valid reasons for maintenance
  • Accessibility - Required information must be readily available
  • Flexibility - System must allow for expansion or contraction
  • Cost-effectiveness - Management costs must be reasonable

Filing Systems

What is Filing?

Filing is the process of classifying and arranging records so they can be accessed without delay.

Importance of Filing

  • Serves as ready reference
  • Saves time and increases efficiency
  • Safeguards documents against loss
  • Provides reliable basis for planning
  • Serves as evidence in disputes
  • Facilitates proper control

Characteristics of a Good Filing System

  • Compactness - Efficient use of space
  • Accessibility - Easy retrieval
  • Simplicity - Easy to understand and use
  • Elasticity - Ability to expand
  • Cross-reference capabilities

Filing Classification Methods

1. Alphabetical Filing

Documents are filed according to the first letter of sender's name or subject.

Advantages

  • Convenient grouping by name
  • Direct filing without index
  • Simple and easy to understand
  • Allows miscellaneous files

Disadvantages

  • Time-consuming in large systems
  • Congestion under common names
  • Difficult to forecast space requirements
  • Possible filing errors due to spelling variations

2. Numerical Filing

Correspondence arranged by assigned numbers rather than letters.

Advantages

  • Unlimited expansion possibilities
  • Highly accurate system
  • Simple cross-referencing
  • Reference numbers for correspondence

Disadvantages

  • Requires two operations (indexing and filing)
  • Indirect filing and finding
  • Separate index needed
  • Training time for new employees

3. Geographical Filing

Files divided by location (countries, regions, etc.)

Advantages

  • Suitable for organizations with multiple branches
  • Easy reference when location is known
  • Direct access for filing

Disadvantages

  • Requires geographical knowledge
  • Location must be known in addition to name
  • Index needed for occasional reference

Filing Equipment and Maintenance

Types of Filing Equipment

  • Filing cabinets
  • Box files
  • Lever arch files
  • Concertina files
  • Ring binders

File Maintenance

  • Regular file reviews
  • Proper handling procedures
  • Systematic disposal of obsolete records
  • Regular cleaning and organization
  • Proper labeling and indexing

Key Points Summary

  • Effective filing systems are crucial for office efficiency
  • Choice of filing method depends on organizational needs
  • Proper equipment and maintenance ensure longevity
  • Regular system review keeps filing effective
  • Security and accessibility must be balanced
