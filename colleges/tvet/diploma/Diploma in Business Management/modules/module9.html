Reprography Services

Photocopying

Photocopying is a process of obtaining copies of documents directly from the original with photographic detail. It's most suitable when few copies are required or when exact photographic reproduction is necessary.

Advantages

  • Exact copies without loss of detail
  • Most economical for few copies
  • Some machines can produce color copies
  • Large documents can be reduced in size
  • Can be operated by junior staff
  • Quicker than duplicating (no master preparation)

Disadvantages

  • Expensive for large quantities
  • Copies may fade over time
  • Some machines don't handle colors

Duplicating Methods

Duplicating involves preparing a master copy from which multiple copies are produced. There are two basic methods:

1. Stencil Duplicating

Uses a thin fibrous sheet covered with chemical coating through which ink cannot pass. Stencils are easy to type on but not to write on.

Advantages

  • Economical for large quantities
  • Errors can be corrected with fluid
  • Clear, permanent copies
  • Electronic stencil option available
  • Stencils can be reused if stored properly

Disadvantages

  • Expensive for few copies
  • Not suitable for color production
  • Time-consuming master preparation
  • Not suitable for promotional materials

2. Spirit Duplicating

Uses a master with special carbon on its reverse side. The process involves master preparation and copy production.

Advantages

  • Quick and easy master preparation
  • Can duplicate colors simultaneously
  • Suitable for diagrams and charts

Disadvantages

  • Difficult to correct mistakes
  • Limited number of copies (200-300)
  • Image becomes weaker with use
  • Lower quality than stencil duplicating

Printing

A specialized duplicating method used for large quantities (e.g., 50,000 copies) where high-quality printing is important. Uses metal or paper plates typed with special typewriters.

Advantages

  • Best reproduction quality
  • Economical for large quantities
  • High speed (up to 5,000 copies per hour)
  • Can use any paper type
  • Plates can be stored and reused
  • Multiple colors and typefaces available

Disadvantages

  • Expensive for small quantities
  • Requires specialized operators
  • Separate runs needed for different colors
  • Requires additional office space

Choosing the Right Method

Factors to Consider

  • Number of copies required
  • Frequency of demand
  • Number of colors needed
  • Urgency of copies
  • Intended recipients
  • Quality requirements
  • Available budget
  • Storage considerations

Modern Reprographic Technologies

  • Digital printing
  • Multi-function devices
  • Network printing
  • Cloud-based solutions
  • Environmental considerations

Key Points Summary

  • Different methods suit different needs and quantities
  • Consider quality, cost, and time requirements
  • Modern technology offers new possibilities
  • Environmental impact should be considered
  • Choose method based on specific requirements
