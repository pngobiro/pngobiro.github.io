Office Furniture and Stationery

Office Stationery

Office stationery refers to all writing materials used in the office including papers, envelopes, rubber, ribbon, stencil, correcting fluid, stamp pads, etc.

Stationery Control Guidelines

  • Centralized bulk purchasing to secure quantity discounts
  • Planned issuing system to eliminate wasteful consumption
  • Proper supervision of stationery use
  • Minimal stock maintenance
  • Quality paper for important customer documents
  • Exploration of stationery reuse possibilities
  • Proper storage to prevent deterioration
  • Setting appropriate stock levels

Control Process Steps

  • Purchasing of stationery
  • Receipt of stationery
  • Bin card maintenance
  • Issue management
  • Stock verification

Office Furniture

Office furniture consists of tables, chairs, desks and tools, storage and filing equipment like filing racks and cabinets, cupboards, lockers, safes, etc.

Types of Furniture

1. Executive Furniture

  • Designed according to executive preferences
  • Adds to organizational and personal prestige
  • Higher quality and aesthetic appeal

2. Built-in Furniture

  • Space-saving design
  • Reduced expenditure
  • Easier cleaning and maintenance
  • Integrated with building structure

3. General Furniture

  • Designed for clerical staff
  • Size depends on work requirements
  • Functional and practical design

Basic Principles in Selecting Furniture

  • Suitability for purpose
  • Comfort in design
  • Durability of materials
  • Appropriate weight
  • Efficient use of space
  • Cost-effectiveness
  • Hygienic considerations
  • Aesthetic appearance

Furniture Purchase Guidelines

  • Purchase only required furniture within reasonable cost limits
  • Consider multi-purpose functionality
  • Ensure economical use of space
  • Focus on user comfort and convenience
  • Verify adequate work surface area
  • Check for smooth drawer operation
  • Ensure rounded edges for safety
  • Select adjustable chairs for ergonomics
  • Consider worker positioning for productivity
  • Verify adaptability and durability

Maintenance and Care

  • Regular cleaning and maintenance schedules
  • Prompt repairs of damaged furniture
  • Proper handling of furniture to prevent damage
  • Regular inventory checks
  • Proper storage of unused items

Key Points Summary

  • Effective stationery control reduces waste and costs
  • Furniture selection impacts workplace efficiency
  • Different types of furniture serve different purposes
  • Regular maintenance extends furniture life
  • Proper storage ensures longevity of supplies
