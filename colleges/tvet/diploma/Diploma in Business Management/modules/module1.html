Introduction to Office Administration and Management

Definition of Management

"The emergence of management as an essential distinct and leading institution is a pivotal event in social history, purely if ever has a new leading group emerged as fast as has management since the turn of 20th century." - Peter W. Drucker

Management can be defined as principally a task of planning, coordinating, and motivating the efforts of others towards specific objectives. All kinds of institutions – social, political, religious, etc. are formed by groups of individuals to pursue common goals and objectives.

Management vs Administration

There has been considerable debate about the distinction between management and administration. Key differences include:

Administration

  • Higher-level activity
  • Determinative function
  • Concerned with objective and policy determination

Management

  • Lower-level function
  • Executive function
  • Involves policy implementation

At every level, managers perform both types of functions, with the proportion varying by organizational level. Higher-level managers spend more time on administration, while lower-level managers focus more on management functions.

Office Management

"Office management is the branch of management concerned with the services of obtaining, recording and analyzing information for planning and communicating by means of which the management safeguards assets, promotes affairs and achieves objectives." - Institute of Administrative Management England

Office management means planning, organizing, coordinating and controlling the activities of the office. It involves:

  • Planning and coordinating office activities
  • Managing information flow
  • Supervising office personnel
  • Maintaining office resources
  • Implementing office systems and procedures

Business Enterprise Objectives

A business enterprise typically has objectives such as:

  • Maximizing profits through increased output and sales
  • Creating a positive image in society as a good employer
  • Producing quality products
  • Contributing to economic growth

These objectives require coordinated efforts across all levels of the organization to achieve success.

Key Points Summary

  • Management is essential for coordinating group efforts toward objectives
  • Administration and management serve different but complementary functions
  • Every manager performs both administrative and management duties
  • Office management focuses on organizing and controlling office activities
  • Clear objectives and coordination are crucial for organizational success
