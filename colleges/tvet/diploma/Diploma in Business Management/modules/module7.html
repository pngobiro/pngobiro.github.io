Handling Office Correspondence

Introduction to Office Correspondence

Office correspondence, also known as mail, involves the process of receiving and sending letters in any organization. Mail handling can be divided into three categories:

  • Incoming mail
  • Outgoing mail
  • Interdepartmental mail

Incoming Mail Procedures

Collecting Mail

  • Usually collected by messengers
  • Fixed collection times (typically morning and afternoon)
  • Use of locked briefcase for security
  • Delivery to mail office for processing

Opening Mail

  • Registered mail requires immediate attention and signature
  • Opening should be done by at least two clerks with supervisor present
  • Private, personal, or confidential letters remain sealed
  • Check for enclosures mentioned in letters
  • Double-check envelopes before discarding

Recording Incoming Mail

  • Maintain records for important correspondence
  • Always record items containing cash or checks
  • Log registered and forwarding mail
  • Sort into categories: urgent, important, and routine

Distribution Process

  • Quick distribution after receipt
  • Forward personal/confidential letters unopened
  • Sort mail by department and priority
  • Make copies of urgent letters requiring multiple attention
  • Maintain proper tracking system

Outgoing Mail Procedures

Mail Office Delivery

  • Establish clear responsibility for mail delivery
  • Set fixed timing for mail delivery
  • Designate specific out-trays
  • Send unsealed except confidential items
  • Mark special handling requirements clearly

Preparing Mail for Dispatch

  • Check all letters received in mail office
  • Match letters with correct envelopes
  • Fold letters properly
  • Consider using window envelopes
  • Weigh for correct postage
  • Apply stamps or use franking machine

Recording Outgoing Mail

  • Record addressee details
  • Note nature of document
  • Track postage costs
  • Maintain dispatch register
  • Get receipts for registered items

Special Mail Handling

  • Registered mail requires proper documentation
  • Express mail needs immediate attention
  • Confidential correspondence requires special care
  • International mail needs customs consideration
  • Bulk mailings require special preparation

Key Points Summary

  • Systematic mail handling ensures efficient communication
  • Security measures protect confidential information
  • Proper recording prevents loss of important documents
  • Timely distribution is crucial for office efficiency
  • Special handling procedures protect important correspondence
