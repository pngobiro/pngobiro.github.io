2.1 Fundamentals of Mathematics

Specific Objectives

At the end of this topic, the trainee should be able to:

  • Form and solve algebraic equations
  • Solve linear equations and inequalities
  • Work with relations and functions
  • Solve linear simultaneous equations

Algebraic Equations

What is Algebra?

Algebra is a branch of mathematics in which letters are used to represent numbers. This allows us to work with general expressions rather than specific numbers.

Basic Algebraic Concepts

  • Variables: Letters used to represent unknown values (e.g., x, y, z)
  • Constants: Fixed numerical values (e.g., 2, 5, -3)
  • Terms: Parts of an expression separated by + or - signs
  • Coefficients: Numbers that multiply variables (e.g., in 3x, 3 is the coefficient)

Example 1: Algebraic Expression

In the expression 3x + 2y - 5:

  • 3x is a term where 3 is the coefficient and x is the variable
  • 2y is a term where 2 is the coefficient and y is the variable
  • -5 is a constant term

Solving Basic Equations

To solve an equation:

  1. Keep the equation balanced (what you do to one side, do to the other)
  2. Isolate the variable on one side
  3. Simplify both sides

Example 2: Solving an Equation

Solve: 2x + 3 = 11

Step 1: Subtract 3 from both sides

2x + 3 - 3 = 11 - 3 2x = 8

Step 2: Divide both sides by 2

2x ÷ 2 = 8 ÷ 2 x = 4

Linear Equations and Inequalities

Linear Equations

A linear equation is an equation where each term is either a constant or the product of a constant and a variable to the first power. The graph of a linear equation forms a straight line.

Example 3: Linear Equation

The equation 3x - 4 = 8 is linear because:

  • The variable x is only to the first power
  • All terms are either constants or products of constants and x

Inequalities

An inequality compares expressions using symbols: >, <, ≥, ≤, or ≠

Key Rules:

  • When multiplying or dividing by a negative number, reverse the inequality sign
  • The solution may be a range of values rather than a single value

Example 4: Solving an Inequality

Solve: 2x + 5 > 13

Step 1: Subtract 5 from both sides

2x + 5 - 5 > 13 - 5 2x > 8

Step 2: Divide both sides by 2

2x ÷ 2 > 8 ÷ 2 x > 4

The solution is all numbers greater than 4, written as: x > 4

Practice Problems

  1. Solve: 5x - 3 = 12
  2. Solve: -2x + 7 ≤ 1
  3. Solve: 3x + 4 > 2x - 2

Linear Simultaneous Equations

Simultaneous Equations

Simultaneous equations are a set of equations with two or more unknowns that must be satisfied at the same time. In linear simultaneous equations, all variables are to the first power.

Methods of Solving

  1. Substitution Method: Express one variable in terms of another and substitute
  2. Elimination Method: Add or subtract equations to eliminate one variable

Example 5: Solving by Substitution

Solve:

x + 2y = 7 x - y = 1

Step 1: Express x in terms of y from the second equation

x - y = 1 x = y + 1

Step 2: Substitute this expression into the first equation

(y + 1) + 2y = 7 y + 1 + 2y = 7 3y + 1 = 7

Step 3: Solve for y

3y = 6 y = 2

Step 4: Find x by substituting y = 2

x = y + 1 x = 2 + 1 x = 3

Solution: x = 3, y = 2

Example 6: Solving by Elimination

Solve:

2x + y = 5 x + y = 3

Step 1: Subtract the second equation from the first

2x + y = 5 -(x + y = 3) x = 2

Step 2: Substitute x = 2 into either equation

2(2) + y = 5 4 + y = 5 y = 1

Solution: x = 2, y = 1

Practice Problems

  1. Solve:
    3x + 2y = 12
    x - y = 1
  2. Solve:
    2x + 3y = 8
    4x - y = 5

Key Points

  • •

    Algebra uses letters (variables) to represent unknown values in mathematical expressions.

  • •

    Linear equations contain variables only to the first power and form straight lines when graphed.

  • •

    When solving inequalities with negative numbers, remember to reverse the inequality sign.

  • •

    Simultaneous equations can be solved using either substitution or elimination methods.

  • •

    Always verify solutions by checking them in all equations or inequalities.
