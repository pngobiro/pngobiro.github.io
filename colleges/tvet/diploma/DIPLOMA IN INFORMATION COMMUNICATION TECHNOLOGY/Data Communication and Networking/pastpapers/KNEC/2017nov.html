    Questions

    (a) (i) Outline three advantages of hybrid topology networks.
    (ii) Distinguish between hubs and bridges as used in networking.
    (b) (i) Outline three factors that should be considered when selecting a transmission media, other than cost.
    (ii) Network administrators use several documents when troubleshooting a network. Describe two documents commonly used.
    (c) Network design is a very important phase in networking. Explain three objectives to be attained by the phase.

    Answer

    Sample answers for this question are provided below. Replace with official or custom content as needed.

    • (a) Hybrid topology network advantages:
      1. Combines benefits of multiple topologies (e.g., star, bus, ring).
      2. Scalable and flexible for network expansion.
      3. Fault isolation is easier; a failure in one segment does not affect the entire network.
    • (b) Hubs vs. Bridges:
      • Hub: Broadcasts data to all ports; operates at OSI Layer 1.
      • Bridge: Filters traffic by MAC address; operates at OSI Layer 2.
    • (c) Transmission media selection factors (other than cost):
      1. Bandwidth capacity.
      2. Distance and attenuation.
      3. Susceptibility to electromagnetic interference (EMI).
    • (d) Network troubleshooting documents:
      • Network topology diagrams.
      • Configuration documentation (IP addresses, device settings).
    • (e) Network design objectives:
      1. Reliability and availability.
      2. Scalability for future growth.
      3. Security of data and resources.
