Question 1

(a) Explain each of the following terms as used in the Internet:

(i) webpage;

(ii) hyperlink.

(b) Peter intends to acquire communication software for his company's computer systems. Outline four factors other than cost that he should consider while carrying out the task.

(c) Mondi Company Ltd. would like to implement encryption techniques in their network. Explain two types of encryption techniques that the company could consider using.

(d) (i) Explain two methods of acquiring computer equipment.

(ii) Token passing is a classic media access methods used in local area networks. Explain two of its strengths that could be influencing its popularity.

Answer

(a) Explain each of the following terms as used in the Internet:

(i) Webpage:

A webpage is a document, typically written in HTML, that is accessible through the Internet using a web browser. It can contain text, images, videos, and other multimedia elements.

(ii) Hyperlink:

A hyperlink is a reference or link in a webpage that allows users to navigate to another location within the same document or to an entirely different resource on the web. It enables seamless navigation and access to related content.

(b) Peter intends to acquire communication software for his company's computer systems. Outline four factors other than cost that he should consider while carrying out the task:

  • Compatibility with existing systems: The software should be compatible with the company's current hardware and software infrastructure to ensure smooth integration and avoid conflicts.
  • Security features: The software should have robust security features to protect sensitive data and prevent unauthorized access.
  • Scalability: The software should be scalable to accommodate the company's future growth and changing needs.
  • Ease of use: The software should be user-friendly and easy to learn to minimize training costs and maximize productivity.

(c) Mondi Company Ltd. would like to implement encryption techniques in their network. Explain two types of encryption techniques that the company could consider using:

  • Symmetric encryption: This technique uses the same key for both encryption and decryption. It is faster but requires a secure way to share the key. Examples include AES and DES.
  • Asymmetric encryption: This technique uses two different keys: a public key for encryption and a private key for decryption. It is more secure but slower than symmetric encryption. Examples include RSA and ECC.

(d) (i) Explain two methods of acquiring computer equipment:

  • Purchasing: This involves buying the equipment outright, giving the company full ownership and control.
  • Leasing: This involves renting the equipment for a specific period, which can be more cost-effective in the short term and allows for easier upgrades.

(ii) Token passing is a classic media access method used in local area networks. Explain two of its strengths that could be influencing its popularity:

  • High reliability: Token passing ensures that each device gets a fair chance to transmit data, preventing collisions and ensuring reliable communication.
  • Fairness: Token passing provides equal access to the network for all devices, preventing any single device from monopolizing the bandwidth.

Question 2

(a) (i) Outline three benefits of a client-server network model.

(ii) Distinguish between a repeater and a transceiver as used in computer networks.

(b) (i) Outline three examples of network transmission impairments.

(ii) Vicky was required to use divide-and-conquer troubleshooting technique to solve a problem in a client's network. Explain two challenges that she could have realized while using the technique.

(c) In order to carry out a network design, one is required to conduct user needs analysis. Explain three significances of this stage.

Answer

(a) (i) Outline three benefits of a client-server network model:

  • Centralized management: A client-server model allows for centralized management of resources and security policies, making it easier to administer and maintain the network.
  • Improved security: With a central server, security measures can be implemented and enforced more effectively, protecting sensitive data from unauthorized access.
  • Resource sharing: Clients can easily share resources such as files, printers, and applications stored on the server, improving efficiency and collaboration.

(ii) Distinguish between a repeater and a transceiver as used in computer networks:

A repeater is a device that amplifies and regenerates signals to extend the distance over which they can be transmitted. It operates at the physical layer of the OSI model.

A transceiver, on the other hand, is a device that both transmits and receives signals. It combines the functions of a transmitter and a receiver in a single unit and is used to connect devices to a network.

(b) (i) Outline three examples of network transmission impairments:

  • Attenuation: This is the weakening of a signal as it travels over a distance.
  • Distortion: This is the alteration of the signal's waveform, which can lead to errors in data transmission.
  • Noise: This is unwanted electrical or electromagnetic energy that interferes with the signal, making it difficult to distinguish the data.

(ii) Vicky was required to use divide-and-conquer troubleshooting technique to solve a problem in a client's network. Explain two challenges that she could have realized while using the technique:

  • Time-consuming: The divide-and-conquer method can be time-consuming, especially in complex networks where the problem could be in any of several places.
  • Requires expertise: This method requires a good understanding of the network and its components to effectively divide the problem and isolate the cause.

(c) In order to carry out a network design, one is required to conduct user needs analysis. Explain three significances of this stage:

  • Understanding user requirements: This stage helps in understanding the specific needs and expectations of the users, ensuring that the network is designed to meet those requirements.
  • Identifying potential issues: By analyzing user needs, potential issues and bottlenecks can be identified early on, allowing for proactive solutions.
  • Ensuring user satisfaction: A well-designed network that meets user needs leads to increased user satisfaction and productivity.

Question 3

(a) Describe two types of fiber optic cables.

(b) (i) Outline two typical characteristics of a local area network.

(ii) Figure 1 shows a frame relay protocol frame. Explain the function of each of the fields labelled (i) and (ii).

(c) Several organizations are cautious about using public cloud computing. Outline four issues that could be influencing its adoption.

(d) Data encapsulation is a critical process in the transport layer of OSI. Explain three uses of this process.

Answer

(a) Describe two types of fiber optic cables:

  • Single-mode fiber: This type of fiber optic cable has a small core and allows only one mode of light to propagate. It is used for long-distance communication due to its low attenuation and high bandwidth.
  • Multi-mode fiber: This type of fiber optic cable has a larger core and allows multiple modes of light to propagate. It is used for shorter distances and local area networks.

(b) (i) Outline two typical characteristics of a local area network:

  • High data transfer rates: LANs typically offer high data transfer rates, allowing for fast communication between devices.
  • Limited geographical area: LANs are confined to a limited geographical area, such as a building or a campus.

(ii) Figure 1 shows a frame relay protocol frame. Explain the function of each of the fields labelled (i) and (ii):

Assuming (i) is DLCI and (ii) is CRC:

  • DLCI (Data Link Connection Identifier): This field identifies the virtual circuit used for communication between two devices in a frame relay network.
  • CRC (Cyclic Redundancy Check): This field is used for error detection. It contains a checksum value calculated from the data in the frame, which is used to verify the integrity of the data during transmission.

(c) Several organizations are cautious about using public cloud computing. Outline four issues that could be influencing its adoption:

  • Security concerns: Organizations are concerned about the security of their data stored in the cloud, as it is potentially vulnerable to unauthorized access and cyberattacks.
  • Privacy issues: Organizations are concerned about the privacy of their data stored in the cloud, as they may not have full control over how it is accessed and used.
  • Compliance requirements: Organizations may be subject to regulatory compliance requirements that restrict the storage and processing of data in the cloud.
  • Vendor lock-in: Organizations may be concerned about becoming too dependent on a single cloud provider, making it difficult to switch to another provider in the future.

(d) Data encapsulation is a critical process in the transport layer of OSI. Explain three uses of this process:

  • Adding header information: Encapsulation adds header information to the data, which includes source and destination addresses, protocol information, and other control information.
  • Adding trailer information: Encapsulation adds trailer information to the data, which includes error detection codes and other control information.
  • Ensuring data integrity: Encapsulation helps ensure the integrity of the data during transmission by adding error detection codes that can be used to detect and correct errors.

Question 4

(a) (i) Outline two types of multiplexing techniques.

(ii) Distinguish between broadcast and loopback IP addresses.

(b) Ruth intends to use unguided transmission medium for her company's network. Explain two limitations of this medium.

(c) Quality of service majorly depends on the bandwidth of a network. Outline four factors that are considered when determining the bandwidth.

(d) IP is key in accessing the Internet. Explain three limitations characteristic of IP.

Answer

(a) (i) Outline two types of multiplexing techniques:

  • Frequency Division Multiplexing (FDM): This technique divides the available bandwidth into multiple frequency channels, each of which is used to transmit a separate signal.
  • Time Division Multiplexing (TDM): This technique divides the available time into multiple time slots, each of which is used to transmit a portion of a signal.

(ii) Distinguish between broadcast and loopback IP addresses:

  • Broadcast IP address: This is a special IP address that is used to send packets to all devices on a network. It typically has the form of x.x.x.255, where x.x.x represents the network address.
  • Loopback IP address: This is a special IP address (127.0.0.1) that is used for testing network interfaces on a local machine. Packets sent to this address are looped back to the sender without being transmitted over the network.

(b) Ruth intends to use unguided transmission medium for her company's network. Explain two limitations of this medium:

  • Susceptibility to interference: Unguided transmission media, such as radio waves and microwaves, are susceptible to interference from other electromagnetic sources, which can degrade the quality of the signal.
  • Limited range: Unguided transmission media have a limited range, as the signal strength decreases with distance. This limits the distance over which data can be transmitted reliably.

(c) Quality of service majorly depends on the bandwidth of a network. Outline four factors that are considered when determining the bandwidth:

  • Number of users: The number of users on the network affects the bandwidth requirements, as more users require more bandwidth to maintain acceptable performance.
  • Network infrastructure: The capacity of the network infrastructure, such as routers and switches, affects the available bandwidth.
  • Types of applications used: Different applications have different bandwidth requirements. For example, video conferencing requires more bandwidth than email.
  • Network protocols: The protocols used on the network can affect the bandwidth efficiency. Some protocols are more efficient than others.

(d) IP is key in accessing the Internet. Explain three limitations characteristic of IP:

  • Unreliable delivery: IP does not guarantee reliable delivery of packets. Packets may be lost, duplicated, or delivered out of order.
  • No inherent security: IP does not provide any inherent security mechanisms. Data transmitted over IP is vulnerable to eavesdropping and tampering.
  • Limited address space (IPv4): The IPv4 address space is limited to 4.3 billion addresses, which is not enough to accommodate the growing number of devices connected to the Internet.

Question 5

(a) Differentiate between star and hybrid network topologies.

(b) Explain three functions of the session layer as used in OSI.

(c) With the aid of a diagram, describe the TCP/IP layer model.

(d) A network design should be properly laid out to achieve network availability. Outline four guidelines that should be followed to achieve this objective.

Answer

(a) Differentiate between star and hybrid network topologies:

  • Star topology: In a star topology, all devices connect to a central hub or switch. This central device acts as a single point of failure, but it also simplifies network management and troubleshooting.
  • Hybrid topology: A hybrid topology is a combination of two or more different network topologies, such as star, bus, and ring. Hybrid topologies are often used in large networks to provide flexibility and scalability.

(b) Explain three functions of the session layer as used in OSI:

  • Session establishment: The session layer is responsible for establishing a connection between two applications. This includes negotiating the connection parameters and authenticating the users.
  • Session termination: The session layer is responsible for terminating a connection between two applications. This includes releasing the resources used by the connection and notifying the applications that the connection has been closed.
  • Session management: The session layer is responsible for managing the ongoing communication between two applications. This includes synchronizing the data flow, handling errors, and re-establishing the connection if it is lost.

(c) With the aid of a diagram, describe the TCP/IP layer model:

(Diagram description)

  • Application Layer: This layer provides network services to applications, such as email, web browsing, and file transfer.
  • Transport Layer: This layer provides reliable and unreliable data transport between applications. TCP and UDP are the two main protocols used at this layer.
  • Internet Layer: This layer is responsible for routing packets between networks. IP is the main protocol used at this layer.
  • Network Access Layer: This layer is responsible for providing access to the physical network. Ethernet and Wi-Fi are the two main technologies used at this layer.

(d) A network design should be properly laid out to achieve network availability. Outline four guidelines that should be followed to achieve this objective:

  • Redundancy: Implement redundant network components, such as routers, switches, and power supplies, to ensure that the network can continue to operate even if one component fails.
  • Proper cabling: Use high-quality cabling and ensure that it is properly installed and maintained to minimize the risk of cable failures.
  • Regular maintenance: Perform regular maintenance on network equipment, such as updating software and firmware, to prevent problems and ensure optimal performance.
  • Disaster recovery plan: Develop a disaster recovery plan that outlines the steps to be taken to restore the network in the event of a disaster, such as a fire or flood.

Question 6

(a) (i) Outline four tools that could be used when troubleshooting a network.

(ii) Figure 2 shows typical tool bar of a browser program. State the function of each of the tools labelled (i), (ii), (iii) and (iv).

(b) (i) Define the term channel as applied in data transmission.

(ii) Nancy configured a domain naming server (DNS) for her company's network. Explain two roles that the server would provide.

(c) (i) Outline two roles of a hub in a computer network

(ii) Explain two circumstances under which a network administrator could disable SSID (Service Set Identifier) of a wireless access point.

Answer

(a) (i) Outline four tools that could be used when troubleshooting a network:

  • Ping: This tool is used to test the reachability of a device on the network by sending ICMP echo requests and waiting for a response.
  • Traceroute: This tool is used to trace the path that packets take from the source to the destination, identifying any routers or devices along the way.
  • Nslookup: This tool is used to query DNS servers to resolve domain names to IP addresses and vice versa.
  • Wireshark: This tool is a network protocol analyzer that captures and analyzes network traffic, allowing you to inspect the contents of packets and identify network problems.

(ii) Figure 2 shows typical tool bar of a browser program. State the function of each of the tools labelled (i), (ii), (iii) and (iv):

  • (i) Back: This tool navigates to the previous page in the browser's history.
  • (ii) Forward: This tool navigates to the next page in the browser's history.
  • (iii) Refresh: This tool reloads the current page.
  • (iv) Home: This tool navigates to the browser's homepage.

(b) (i) Define the term channel as applied in data transmission:

A channel is a medium through which signals travel from a sender to a receiver. It can be a physical medium, such as a wire or fiber optic cable, or a wireless medium, such as radio waves or microwaves.

(ii) Nancy configured a domain naming server (DNS) for her company's network. Explain two roles that the server would provide:

  • Translating domain names to IP addresses: DNS servers translate human-readable domain names, such as www.example.com, to IP addresses, such as 192.168.1.1, which are used to locate devices on the network.
  • Caching DNS records: DNS servers cache DNS records to speed up the resolution process. When a DNS server receives a request for a domain name, it first checks its cache to see if it has a recent record for that domain name. If it does, it returns the cached record to the client, avoiding the need to query other DNS servers.

(c) (i) Outline two roles of a hub in a computer network:

  • Connecting devices: A hub connects multiple devices to a network, allowing them to communicate with each other.
  • Repeating signals: A hub repeats signals received on one port to all other ports, ensuring that all devices on the network receive the signal.

(ii) Explain two circumstances under which a network administrator could disable SSID (Service Set Identifier) of a wireless access point:

  • Hiding the network: Disabling the SSID hides the network from casual users who are not actively searching for it, making it more difficult for unauthorized users to connect.
  • Preventing unauthorized access: Disabling the SSID can help prevent unauthorized access to the network, as users must know the SSID to connect.

Question 7

(a) Explain each of the following terms as used in the Internet:

(i) bookmark;

(ii) plug-in;

(iii) search engine.

(b) Keith intends to propose physical security measure for a network. Outline four physical measures that he could have identified.

(c) Differentiate between malware and ransom ware as applied in network security.

(d) Zawadi was required to select a proprietary distance vector routing protocol that would provide extremely quick convergence times with minimal network traffic.

(i) Identify the most appropriate routing protocol that she could prefer to use justifying your answer.

(ii) Explain two drawbacks of the protocol identified in (i).

Answer

(a) Explain each of the following terms as used in the Internet:

(i) Bookmark:

A bookmark is a saved shortcut to a webpage that allows users to quickly access the page without having to remember the URL or search for it again.

(ii) Plug-in:

A plug-in is a software component that adds specific features or functionality to a web browser. Plug-ins are used to display multimedia content, run interactive applications, and perform other tasks that are not natively supported by the browser.

(iii) Search engine:

A search engine is a tool for finding information on the Internet. Users enter keywords or phrases into the search engine, and the search engine returns a list of webpages that are relevant to the search query.

(b) Keith intends to propose physical security measure for a network. Outline four physical measures that he could have identified:

  • Security cameras: Security cameras can be used to monitor the network premises and deter unauthorized access.
  • Access control systems: Access control systems, such as keycard readers and biometric scanners, can be used to restrict access to the network premises to authorized personnel only.
  • Locks: Locks can be used to secure network equipment and prevent theft or tampering.
  • Fences: Fences can be used to create a physical barrier around the network premises, making it more difficult for unauthorized users to access the network.

(c) Differentiate between malware and ransom ware as applied in network security:

  • Malware: This is a general term for malicious software that is designed to harm or disrupt computer systems. Malware can include viruses, worms, Trojan horses, spyware, and other types of malicious code.
  • Ransomware: This is a type of malware that encrypts the victim's files and demands a ransom payment in exchange for the decryption key. Ransomware attacks can be very disruptive and costly for organizations.

(d) Zawadi was required to select a proprietary distance vector routing protocol that would provide extremely quick convergence times with minimal network traffic.

(i) Identify the most appropriate routing protocol that she could prefer to use justifying your answer:

Enhanced Interior Gateway Routing Protocol (EIGRP) is a proprietary distance vector routing protocol that provides extremely quick convergence times with minimal network traffic. EIGRP uses a sophisticated algorithm called the Diffusing Update Algorithm (DUAL) to ensure that routing updates are propagated quickly and efficiently.

(ii) Explain two drawbacks of the protocol identified in (i):

  • Proprietary: EIGRP is a proprietary protocol, which means that it is only supported by Cisco devices. This can limit the flexibility of the network and make it more difficult to interoperate with devices from other vendors.
  • Complexity: EIGRP is a complex protocol, which can make it difficult to configure and troubleshoot. Network administrators need to have a good understanding of the protocol to effectively manage EIGRP networks.

Question 8

(a) Outline two attributes of 10 base T Ethernet standard.

(b) For each of the following scenarios, state the appropriate transmission method:

(i) waikie-talkie communication;

(ii) cell phone conversation;

(iii) lecturer in a class lesson;

(iv) market selling/buying session.

(c) Miriam identified several layers of an Ethernet frame during a job appraisal. Outline the first four layers of an Ethernet frame that she could have identified.

(d) (i) Distinguish between instant messaging and podcasting as applied in Internet services.

(ii) Mitambo Technical Institute installed Wireless Internet in the student's hostels. Explain three benefits that the students will realize from the installation.

Answer

(a) Outline two attributes of 10 base T Ethernet standard:

  • Baseband transmission: 10Base-T uses baseband transmission, meaning that the entire bandwidth of the cable is used to transmit a single signal.
  • Uses twisted pair cable: 10Base-T uses unshielded twisted pair (UTP) cable, which is a common and inexpensive type of cable.

(b) For each of the following scenarios, state the appropriate transmission method:

  • (i) Walkie-talkie communication: Half-duplex
  • (ii) Cell phone conversation: Full-duplex
  • (iii) Lecturer in a class lesson: Simplex
  • (iv) Market selling/buying session: Half-duplex

(c) Miriam identified several layers of an Ethernet frame during a job appraisal. Outline the first four layers of an Ethernet frame that she could have identified:

  • Preamble: This is a 7-byte field that is used to synchronize the receiver with the incoming signal.
  • Destination MAC Address: This is a 6-byte field that specifies the MAC address of the destination device.
  • Source MAC Address: This is a 6-byte field that specifies the MAC address of the source device.
  • EtherType: This is a 2-byte field that specifies the protocol being carried in the data portion of the frame.

(d) (i) Distinguish between instant messaging and podcasting as applied in Internet services:

  • Instant messaging: This is a real-time text-based communication service that allows users to exchange messages with each other instantly.
  • Podcasting: This is a method of distributing audio or video files over the Internet, typically using RSS feeds. Users can subscribe to podcasts and automatically receive new episodes as they are released.

(ii) Mitambo Technical Institute installed Wireless Internet in the student's hostels. Explain three benefits that the students will realize from the installation:

  • Access to online resources: Students can access online resources, such as e-books, research papers, and online courses, from the comfort of their hostels.
  • Improved communication: Students can communicate with each other and with their instructors more easily using email, instant messaging, and social media.
  • Enhanced learning experience: Students can use the Internet to enhance their learning experience by accessing online tutorials, participating in online discussions, and collaborating on projects.
