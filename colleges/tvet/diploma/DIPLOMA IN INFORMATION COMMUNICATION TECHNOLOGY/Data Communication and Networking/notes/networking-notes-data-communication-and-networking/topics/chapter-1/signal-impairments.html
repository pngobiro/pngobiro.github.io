Signal Impairments

During transmission, signals can be impaired in three main ways: attenuation, distortion, and noise. These impairments can affect the quality and reliability of data communication.

📉 Attenuation

Attenuation is the loss of energy as the signal propagates through the medium. The signal becomes weaker as it travels further from the source.

Signal Attenuation
Original Signal
Attenuated Signal

Causes

  • Resistance in transmission medium
  • Signal absorption by material
  • Distance from signal source

Solutions

  • Use of amplifiers or repeaters
  • Better transmission media
  • Signal regeneration

↭ Distortion

Distortion occurs when the signal changes its form or shape. It can happen when different frequency components of a signal travel at different speeds.

Signal Distortion

Causes

  • Varying signal propagation speeds
  • Non-linear characteristics of medium
  • Bandwidth limitations

Solutions

  • Use of equalizers
  • Modulation techniques
  • Bandwidth management

⚡ Noise

Noise is unwanted electrical or electromagnetic energy that degrades the quality of signals and data. Various factors can cause noise during transmission.

Signal with Noise
⚠️ Types of Noise
  • Thermal noise: Caused by temperature-induced electron movement
  • Induced noise: Caused by electromagnetic interference
  • Crosstalk: Caused by undesired coupling between signal paths
  • Impulse noise: Caused by spikes in power systems

Causes

  • Thermal agitation of electrons
  • Electromagnetic interference
  • Cross-talk between channels
  • External disturbances

Solutions

  • Shielding
  • Filtering
  • Error detection and correction
  • Signal processing techniques
📝

Key Points to Remember

  • 🔑

    Attenuation weakens the signal and reduces its amplitude over distance.

  • 🔑

    Distortion changes the shape of the signal due to varying delays of different frequencies.

  • 🔑

    Noise adds unwanted components to the signal from various sources.

  • 🔑

    Each impairment type requires specific solutions and mitigation techniques.
