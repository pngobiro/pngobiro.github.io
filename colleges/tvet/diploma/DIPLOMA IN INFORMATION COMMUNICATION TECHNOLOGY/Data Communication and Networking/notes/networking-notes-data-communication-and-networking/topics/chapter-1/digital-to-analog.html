Digital-to-Analog Conversion

Digital-to-analog conversion is the process of changing digital data to analog signals. This conversion is necessary when we need to send digital data over an analog transmission medium. There are several modulation techniques used for this conversion.

üìä Amplitude Shift Keying (ASK)

In Amplitude Shift Keying, the amplitude of the carrier signal is varied to represent binary 1 and 0. The frequency and phase remain constant.

ASK Modulation

Advantages

  • Simple to implement
  • Bandwidth efficient
  • Cost-effective

Disadvantages

  • Sensitive to noise
  • Not suitable for high-speed transmission
  • Limited range

üîÑ Frequency Shift Keying (FSK)

Frequency Shift Keying uses two different frequencies to represent binary values. The amplitude and phase remain constant.

FSK Modulation

Advantages

  • Better noise immunity than ASK
  • More reliable detection
  • Less affected by signal strength variations

Disadvantages

  • Requires more bandwidth
  • More complex implementation
  • Less bandwidth efficient

‚≠ï Phase Shift Keying (PSK)

Phase Shift Keying alters the phase of the carrier signal to represent different binary values. The amplitude and frequency remain constant.

PSK Modulation

Advantages

  • Better noise immunity than ASK
  • More bandwidth efficient than FSK
  • Good for high-speed transmission

Disadvantages

  • More complex to implement
  • Requires more complex receiver
  • Sensitive to phase distortion

Characteristic ASK FSK PSK

Key Points to Remember

  • üîë

    ASK modifies amplitude, FSK modifies frequency, and PSK modifies phase to encode digital data.

  • üîë

    Each modulation technique has specific advantages and disadvantages for different applications.

  • üîë

    Choice of modulation technique depends on requirements for bandwidth, noise immunity, and cost.
