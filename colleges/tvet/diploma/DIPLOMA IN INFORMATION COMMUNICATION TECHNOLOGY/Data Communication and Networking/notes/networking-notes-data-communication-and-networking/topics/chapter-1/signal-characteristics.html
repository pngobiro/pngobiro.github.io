Signal Characteristics

A periodic signal can be characterized by several fundamental properties. The main characteristics are amplitude, period, frequency, and phase. Understanding these characteristics is crucial for signal analysis and processing.

📊 Amplitude

Amplitude represents the signal strength or magnitude of the variation in the signal. For a sine wave, it is measured as the height from the time axis to the peak (or trough).

📏 Measurement
  • Peak amplitude: Maximum value of signal
  • Peak-to-peak amplitude: Difference between maximum and minimum
  • Average amplitude: Mean of all values over one period

🔄 Frequency

Frequency refers to the number of cycles completed in one second. It is measured in Hertz (Hz). One Hertz means one cycle per second.

f = 1/T

where T is the period in seconds.

⭕ Phase

Phase describes the position of a signal relative to time zero or to another signal. It is measured in radians or degrees.

🔄 Phase Values
  • 0° or 0 rad: Signal starts at the middle point, moving upward
  • 90° or π/2 rad: Signal starts at the peak
  • 180° or π rad: Signal starts at the middle point, moving downward
  • 270° or 3π/2 rad: Signal starts at the trough

📝

Key Points to Remember

  • 🔑

    Amplitude determines the strength or power of a signal.

  • 🔑

    Frequency is inversely proportional to the period of a signal.

  • 🔑

    Phase shift can affect signal synchronization and interpretation.

  • 🔑

    These characteristics are fundamental to signal analysis and processing.
