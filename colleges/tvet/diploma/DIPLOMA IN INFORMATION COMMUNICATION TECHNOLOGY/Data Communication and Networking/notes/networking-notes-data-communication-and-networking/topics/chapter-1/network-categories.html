Network Categories

🏢 Local Area Networks (LAN)

Local area networks, generally called LANs, are privately-owned networks within a single building or campus of up to a few kilometers in size. They are widely used to connect personal computers and workstations in company offices and factories to share resources and exchange information.

Characteristics of LANs

  • 📏
    Size: Restricted in size, which means transmission time is bounded and known in advance
  • 🔌
    Technology: Often uses a transmission technology consisting of a single cable to which all machines are attached
  • ⚡
    Speed: Traditional LANs run at speeds of 10 Mbps to 100 Mbps, with newer ones operating at up to 10 Gbps
  • ⏱️
    Latency: Low delay (microseconds or nanoseconds) and very few errors

🌆 Metropolitan Area Network (MAN)

A metropolitan area network, or MAN, covers a city. The best-known example of a MAN is the cable television network available in many cities.

Evolution and Applications

  • 📺
    Origins: Evolved from early community antenna systems in areas with poor over-the-air TV reception
  • 📡
    Modern Development: Now includes high-speed wireless Internet access (IEEE 802.16)
  • 📍
    Coverage: Typically spans an entire city or metropolitan area
Metropolitan Area Network (MAN) Structure

🌎 Wide Area Network (WAN)

A wide area network, or WAN, spans a large geographical area, often a country or continent. It contains a collection of machines intended for running user (i.e., application) programs. These machines are called hosts.

Key Components

  • 💻
    Hosts: End-user machines that run applications and are owned by customers
  • 🔄
    Communication Subnet: Handles message transmission, typically owned by telephone companies or Internet service providers
ℹ️ WAN Structure

Most WANs consist of two main components:

  • Transmission lines: Move bits between machines
  • Switching elements: Specialized computers that connect multiple transmission lines

Store-and-Forward Operation

When packets are sent from one router to another via intermediate routers:

  • 📦
    Each packet is received in its entirety before being forwarded
  • ⏳
    Packets are stored until the required output line becomes free
  • 🔄
    This approach is called store-and-forward or packet-switched networking
Wide Area Network (WAN) Structure

Satellite Networks in WANs

  • 🛰️
    Operation: Each router has an antenna for sending and receiving signals
  • 📡
    Coverage: All routers can hear the satellite's output and sometimes each other's upward transmissions
  • 🔊
    Advantage: Particularly useful when broadcast capability is important
📝

Key Points to Remember

  • 🔑

    LANs operate at high speeds within limited geographic areas and are typically privately owned.

  • 🔑

    MANs cover city-wide areas and often support both traditional cable TV and modern data services.

  • 🔑

    WANs connect multiple LANs across large geographical areas using communication subnets.

  • 🔑

    Store-and-forward packet switching is fundamental to most WAN operations.
