Periodic Signals

A periodic signal completes a pattern within a measurable time frame, called a period, and repeats that pattern over identical subsequent periods. The completion of one full pattern is called a cycle.

〰️ Sine Wave

The sine wave is the most fundamental form of a periodic analog signal. A sine wave can be represented by three parameters: amplitude, frequency, and phase.

s(t) = A sin(2πft + φ)

Where:

  • A
    A is the peak amplitude
  • f
    f is the frequency in hertz (Hz)
  • φ
    φ is the phase in radians

🔀 Composite Signals

A composite signal is a combination of multiple sine waves with different frequencies, amplitudes, and phases. According to Fourier analysis, any composite signal is a combination of simple sine waves with different frequencies, amplitudes, and phases.

⏹️ Square Wave

A square wave alternates between two fixed voltage levels for equal periods. It can be created by adding odd harmonics to the fundamental frequency sine wave with specific amplitudes.

Square Wave
ℹ️ Important Note

A perfect square wave requires an infinite number of odd harmonics. In practice, we use a limited number of harmonics, which results in some distortion of the wave shape.

📐 Triangle Wave

A triangle wave is a periodic waveform whose shape is a triangle – that is, it is composed of straight lines moving between minimum and maximum values.

Triangle Wave
📝

Key Points to Remember

  • 🔑

    Periodic signals repeat their pattern over fixed time intervals.

  • 🔑

    Any periodic signal can be decomposed into sine waves using Fourier analysis.

  • 🔑

    Common periodic waveforms include sine waves, square waves, and triangle waves.

  • 🔑

    The period of a signal is the time required for one complete cycle.
