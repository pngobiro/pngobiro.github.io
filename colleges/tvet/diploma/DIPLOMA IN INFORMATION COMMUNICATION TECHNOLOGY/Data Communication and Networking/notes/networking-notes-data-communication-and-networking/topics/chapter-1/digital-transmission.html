Digital Transmission

Digital transmission involves sending digital data between two points. Various line coding schemes are used to represent digital data as digital signals. Each scheme has its own advantages and characteristics.

üìä Non-Return to Zero (NRZ)

In NRZ encoding, the signal level remains constant throughout the bit interval. Two voltage levels represent the two binary values.

NRZ Encoding

Advantages

  • Simple implementation
  • Efficient bandwidth usage
  • No signal transitions for consecutive identical bits

Disadvantages

  • No error detection capability
  • DC component present
  • Synchronization issues with long strings of same bit

üîÑ Return to Zero (RZ)

RZ encoding returns to zero level in the middle of each bit period. This provides better synchronization but requires more bandwidth.

RZ Encoding

Advantages

  • Built-in synchronization
  • No DC component
  • Easy clock recovery

Disadvantages

  • Requires twice the bandwidth of NRZ
  • More complex implementation
  • Inefficient use of bandwidth

‚ö° Manchester Encoding

Manchester encoding combines the clock signal with data by having a transition in the middle of each bit period. The direction of transition determines the bit value.

Manchester Encoding
üí° Encoding Rule
  • Bit 1: Transition from high to low in middle of bit
  • Bit 0: Transition from low to high in middle of bit
  • Always has a transition in middle of bit period

Advantages

  • Self-clocking
  • No DC component
  • Error detection capability

Disadvantages

  • Requires twice the bandwidth of NRZ
  • More complex implementation
  • Higher signal frequency components
