DIPLOMA IN INFORMATION COMMUNICATION TECHNOLOGY

SYSTEMS ANALYSIS AND DESIGN

INSTRUCTIONS TO CANDIDATES

Answer any FIVE of the following EIGHT questions in the answer booklet provided.

ALL questions carry equal marks.

Candidates should answer the questions in English.

Question 1 (20 marks)

(a) (i) Outline two typical qualities of a systems analyst in organizations. (2 marks)

(ii) Distinguish between scientific and deterministic approaches as used in systems thinking. (4 marks)

(b) Explain three limitations of hard system thinking as used in development. (6 marks)

(c) (i) Outline four elements of a system design. (4 marks)

(ii) Alice intends to create system documentation for her company’s information system. Explain two uses of the documentation. (4 marks)

Answer

(a)(i) Qualities of Systems Analyst:

  • Analytical & Problem-Solving Skills
  • Communication & Interpersonal Skills
  • Technical Knowledge
  • Business Acumen

(a)(ii) Scientific vs Deterministic Approaches:

Scientific ApproachDeterministic Approach
Uses observation, hypothesis testing, and empirical evidence. Acknowledges uncertainty.Assumes predictable outcomes based solely on inputs and defined rules. No element of chance.

(b) Limitations of Hard System Thinking:

  • Difficulty with ill-defined, complex social problems.
  • Neglects human factors, perspectives, and politics.
  • Can oversimplify reality to fit quantitative models.

(c)(i) Elements of System Design:

  • Output Design
  • Input Design
  • Process Design
  • Database/File Design
  • Control Design
  • Interface Design

(c)(ii) Uses of System Documentation:

  • System Understanding & Communication
  • Maintenance & Enhancement Support
  • Training Resource
  • Audit & Compliance

Question 2 (20 marks)

(a) Outline two negative impacts of emerging trends in systems analysis and design. (2 marks)

(b) Sammy intends to use object-oriented methodology for a proposed information system in his company. Explain three typical features that he could realize while using the methodology. (6 marks)

(c) Daisy was required to identify possible causes of project slippage. Outline four threats that she could have identified. (4 marks)

(d) (i) Lazarus intends to use payback method to evaluate a proposed project in his company. Explain two limitations of the method. (4 marks)

(ii) Risk management requires several strategies in organizations. Explain two strategies that could be used. (4 marks)

Answer

(a) Negative Impacts of Emerging Trends:

  • Increased Complexity: Integrating new technologies can make systems more complex.
  • Rapid Obsolescence: Investments in new trends can quickly become outdated.
  • Security Risks: New technologies often introduce new security vulnerabilities.
  • Skill Gaps: Difficulty in finding or training staff with expertise in new trends.

(b) Features of Object-Oriented Methodology:

  • Encapsulation: Bundling data and methods that operate on the data within objects, hiding internal details.
  • Inheritance: Allowing new classes (subclasses) to inherit properties and methods from existing classes (superclasses), promoting code reuse.
  • Polymorphism: Allowing objects of different classes to respond to the same message (method call) in different ways.
  • Modularity: Systems are built from discrete objects/classes.

(c) Causes of Project Slippage (Threats):

  • Scope Creep: Uncontrolled expansion of project requirements.
  • Poor Estimation: Underestimating the time or resources needed.
  • Resource Unavailability: Lack of required personnel, budget, or tools when needed.
  • Unforeseen Technical Problems: Unexpected difficulties during development or integration.
  • Lack of Stakeholder Communication/Commitment.

(d)(i) Limitations of Payback Method:

  • Ignores Time Value of Money: Treats all cash flows equally regardless of when they occur.
  • Ignores Cash Flows After Payback Period: Does not consider profitability beyond the point where the initial investment is recovered.

(d)(ii) Risk Management Strategies:

  • Risk Avoidance: Changing the project plan to eliminate the risk.
  • Risk Mitigation: Taking steps to reduce the likelihood or impact of the risk.
  • Risk Transfer: Shifting the risk to a third party (e.g., insurance).
  • Risk Acceptance: Acknowledging the risk and deciding not to take action.

Question 3 (20 marks)

(a) (i) Outline two advantages of using Jackson Systems Development (JSD). (2 marks)

(ii) Outline three factors that should be considered when selecting systems development methodology. (3 marks)

(b) (i) Outline two typical components of an information system. (2 marks)

(ii) Differentiate between test data and test case as applied in system testing. (4 marks)

(c) Miriam has decided to use program flow charts to design an IS. Outline three disadvantages of this tool. (3 marks)

(d) A team of system developers were allocated several independent modules to design and develop. Assume you are hired as a system analyst in the company.

(i) Identify the most appropriate type of testing they could apply. (2 marks)

(ii) Explain two strengths of the testing identified in (i). (4 marks)

Answer

(a)(i) Advantages of JSD:

  • Focus on Real World: Models the system based on real-world entities and their time-ordered actions, leading to stable models.
  • Good for Data-Driven Systems: Particularly effective for systems where the processing is driven by the structure of data streams.
  • Provides Clear Structure: Uses specific diagramming techniques (structure diagrams, network diagrams) for clear representation.

(a)(ii) Factors for Selecting Methodology:

  • Project Size & Complexity
  • Clarity & Stability of Requirements
  • Technical Environment
  • Team Skills
  • Time & Budget Constraints

(b)(i) Components of Information System:

  • Hardware
  • Software
  • Data
  • People
  • Processes

(b)(ii) Test Data vs Test Case:

Test DataTest Case
The actual values used as input for testing.A set of conditions or variables under which a tester will determine whether a system meets requirements or works correctly. Includes inputs, execution conditions, and expected results.
Example: Inputting "123", "ABC", "-5".Example: "Test login with valid username/password. Expected result: Successful login." (Requires test data like "user1", "password123").

(c) Disadvantages of Program Flowcharts:

  • Difficult to Modify: Altering logic often requires redrawing significant portions.
  • Complex for Large Programs: Can become very large, cluttered, and hard to follow for complex logic.
  • Focus on Low-Level Logic: Emphasizes procedural steps rather than higher-level structure or data.

(d)(i) Appropriate Testing Type:

  • Unit Testing. Justification: Since modules were developed independently, unit testing focuses on verifying the functionality of each individual module in isolation before they are combined.

(d)(ii) Strengths of Unit Testing:

  • Early Defect Detection: Finds bugs within individual modules early in the development cycle, making them easier and cheaper to fix.
  • Facilitates Integration: Ensures individual modules work correctly before attempting to integrate them, simplifying integration testing.
  • Improves Code Quality: Encourages developers to write modular and testable code.

Question 4 (20 marks)

(a) (i) Explain the term feedback as applied in system control. (2 marks)

(ii) Outline two characteristics of a deterministic system. (2 marks)

(b) (i) Outline two file processing methods. (2 marks)

(ii) Differentiate between probability and non probability sampling as applied in data collection. (4 marks)

(c) Rose intends to carry out file conversion for a new information system. Explain two methods she could use to accomplish the task. (4 marks)

(d) Master-bits Systems Ltd. intends to use on job training for its client. Explain three advantages of the training method. (6 marks)

Answer

(a)(i) Feedback:

  • Information about the output or state of a system that is returned to the system as input, often used for control or modification purposes to help maintain a desired state or achieve a goal.

(a)(ii) Characteristics of Deterministic System:

  • Predictable Output: Outcome is fully determined by its initial state and inputs.
  • No Randomness: Behavior does not involve chance.

(b)(i) File Processing Methods:

  • Batch Processing: Transactions are collected over a period and processed together in a single run.
  • Online/Real-time Processing: Transactions are processed individually as they occur.

(b)(ii) Probability vs Non-Probability Sampling:

Probability SamplingNon-Probability Sampling
Every member of the population has a known, non-zero chance of being selected.Selection is not based on random chance; some members have zero chance or an unknown chance of being selected.
Allows for statistical inference about the population. Results are generalizable.Does not allow for reliable statistical inference. Results may not be generalizable.
Examples: Simple random, stratified, systematic sampling.Examples: Convenience, judgment, quota, snowball sampling.

(c) File Conversion Methods:

  • Direct Conversion: Data is moved directly from the old format/system to the new one, often requiring custom programs or scripts. Can be complex if formats differ significantly.
  • Bridge Program: A temporary program is created to read data from the old file format and write it to the new file format. Used during the transition period.
  • Manual Re-entry: Data is manually keyed into the new system from old records. Suitable only for very small amounts of data due to time, cost, and error potential.

(d) Advantages of On-the-Job Training (OJT):

  • Contextual Learning: Training occurs in the actual work environment using the live system, making it highly relevant.
  • Cost-Effective: Often less expensive than off-site training as it doesn't require separate facilities or travel.
  • Immediate Application: Trainees can immediately apply what they learn to their tasks.
  • Minimal Disruption (if planned well): Can sometimes be integrated into the normal workflow.

Question 5 (20 marks)

(a) Differentiate between economical and technical feasibility studies. (4 marks)

(b) With the aid of a diagram, describe ELH for a typical library system. (6 marks)

(c) Collins intends to formulate a user guide for his client’s proposed information system. Outline four guidelines that he should use. (4 marks)

(d) A structured walkthrough is a meeting for a group of peers to review and discuss the technical aspects of system development. Explain three objectives of such meetings in organizations. (6 marks)

Answer

(a) Economical vs Technical Feasibility:

Economical FeasibilityTechnical Feasibility
Assesses the cost-effectiveness of a proposed system. "Do the benefits outweigh the costs?"Assesses whether the proposed system can be built with existing technology and expertise. "Can we build it?"
Considers development costs, operational costs, tangible/intangible benefits, ROI, payback period.Considers hardware availability, software compatibility, required technical skills, integration challenges.

(b) ELH for Library Book Entity (Typical Example):

Description: A book is acquired (ordered, received, catalogued). Then, it repeatedly circulates (Loan sequence: either issued or reserved, followed by Return). Finally, it is disposed of (either withdrawn or sold). Asterisk (*) denotes iteration, Circle (o) denotes selection.

(c) User Guide Formulation Guidelines:

  • Target Audience Focus: Write clearly for the intended users, avoiding overly technical jargon.
  • Task-Oriented Structure: Organize content around tasks users need to perform.
  • Include Visuals: Use screenshots, diagrams, and examples to illustrate steps.
  • Provide Navigation Aids: Include a table of contents, index, and search function (if online).
  • Keep it Concise and Accurate: Be precise and ensure information reflects the current system.

(d) Objectives of Structured Walkthroughs:

  • Defect Detection: To find errors, omissions, or inconsistencies in the work product (code, design, document) early.
  • Improve Quality: To enhance the overall quality, clarity, and maintainability of the work product through peer review.
  • Knowledge Sharing: To educate team members about different parts of the system or specific techniques used.
  • Enforce Standards: To ensure adherence to project or organizational coding/design standards.

Question 6 (20 marks)

(a) Explain the term license key as applied in system acquisition. (2 marks)

(b) Explain two disadvantages of questionnaires. (4 marks)

(c) (i) With the aid of examples in each case, explain two methods of system acquisition. (4 marks)

(ii) Differentiate between a project scope and a project cycle as applied in project management. (4 marks)

(d) Patrick was required to carry out training of users for a new information system in an organization.

(i) Explain the most appropriate category of users that should be the last to be trained as per organizational structure. (2 marks)

(ii) Explain two challenges that are likely to be experienced during the training. (4 marks)

Answer

(a) License Key:

  • A unique code or sequence of characters provided by a software vendor that activates the software and verifies that the user has a legitimate right (license) to use it, often controlling the level of functionality or number of users allowed.

(b) Disadvantages of Questionnaires:

  • Low Response Rate: Not everyone contacted may return the questionnaire.
  • Inflexibility: Cannot probe deeper or clarify ambiguous answers like in an interview.
  • Misinterpretation: Respondents might misunderstand questions, leading to inaccurate data.
  • Lack of Depth: May not capture complex opinions or detailed qualitative insights effectively.

(c)(i) Methods of System Acquisition:

  • In-house Development: Building the system using the organization's own IT staff. Example: A bank developing its own unique mobile banking application.
  • Purchasing COTS (Commercial Off-The-Shelf) Software: Buying a pre-existing software package from a vendor. Example: A small business purchasing Microsoft Office or QuickBooks accounting software.
  • Outsourcing: Hiring an external company to develop the system. Example: A hospital contracting a specialized firm to build a custom patient record system.

(c)(ii) Project Scope vs Project Cycle:

Project ScopeProject Cycle (Life Cycle)
Defines the boundaries of the project – *what* work is included and excluded, and the specific deliverables.Refers to the sequence of phases a project goes through from initiation to closure (e.g., initiation, planning, execution, monitoring & control, closure).
Focuses on the "what" (features, functions, deliverables).Focuses on the "how" and "when" (stages, process flow).

(d)(i) Last User Category to Train:

  • Top Management/Executives. Justification: They typically need less detailed operational training and more high-level overview/reporting training, which can often occur after the system is stable and operational staff are proficient. Their schedules are often harder to coordinate.

(d)(ii) Challenges During Training:

  • User Resistance: Some users may be reluctant to learn a new system or change their work habits.
  • Varying Skill Levels: Trainees may have different levels of computer literacy or learning speeds, making it hard to pace the training effectively for everyone.
  • Scheduling Conflicts: Finding time for users to attend training without disrupting essential operations.
  • Lack of Adequate Resources: Insufficient trainers, training materials, or suitable training environments.

(b) Explain each of the following terms as applied in project management:

(ii) deliverable. (2 marks)

(c) Baraka was required to install an information system in a new company.

(i) Identify the most appropriate changeover strategy that he could use justifying your answer. (2 marks)

(ii) Explain two advantages of the changeover strategy identified in (i). (4 marks)

(d) Read the following narrative to answer the question that follows. Makutano Dairy Farmers Sacco intends to develop an information system that will automate its operations. Farmers are required to fill registration form and return them to the clerk’s office. The clerical officer then keys in, verifies and then stores farmer’s details in verified details files. The verified farmers are issued with registration numbers and passwords that are then stored in credentials file. During the milk delivery, farmers provide the registration number to the clerical attendant to enable the system retrieves his/her files. After weighing the milk, the farmer file is updated by clerical officer and a transaction receipt issued to that farmer, transaction receipt details are then stored in transaction file. At the end of the month, the accountant of the Sacco is required to process farmer's payment by subtracting stores credit from the farmer’s income. The accountant is then required to debit farmers bank account with the net balance and then update all records for that month.

Draw a level 1 dataflow diagram to model the narrative into a system. (8 marks)

Answer

(a) Indicators for Urgent System Review:

  • Frequent System Crashes/Failures: Significant increase in downtime or errors impacting operations.
  • Major Security Breach: Discovery of a significant vulnerability or data compromise.
  • Inability to Meet Critical Business Needs: System fails to support essential, time-sensitive business functions.
  • Significant Performance Degradation: System becomes unacceptably slow, hindering productivity.

(b) Project Management Terms:

  • (i) Milestone: A significant point or event in a project schedule, indicating the completion of a major phase, deliverable, or objective. It has zero duration.
  • (ii) Deliverable: A tangible or intangible product, result, or capability produced as an outcome of a project or project task (e.g., a requirements document, a software module, a final report).

(c)(i) Changeover Strategy for New Company:

  • Direct Changeover (or potentially Pilot if the "new company" has distinct departments starting up). Justification: Since there is no existing system to run in parallel or phase out, a direct cutover is often the most practical approach for a completely new installation. If departments start sequentially, a pilot might be feasible.

(c)(ii) Advantages of Direct Changeover (in this context):

  • Simplicity: Avoids the complexity of running two systems simultaneously or building temporary interfaces.
  • Cost-Effective: Generally the least expensive method as it avoids parallel operations.
  • Fast Implementation: The switch happens at a single point in time.

Question 8 (20 marks)

(a) Explain each of the following terms as used in system design:

(i) modularity; (2 marks)

(ii) cohesion. (2 marks)

(b) A group of students were carrying out an assignment on characteristics of an information system. Outline four characteristics that they are likely to have listed. (4 marks)

(c) (i) Explain the term selection bias as used in feasibility study. (2 marks)

(ii) With the aid of a diagram, describe a closed-loop system. (4 marks)

(d) Jemo was required to review an information system in order for it to accommodate a new technology.

(i) Identify the most appropriate type of maintenance that he could have used justifying your answer. (2 marks)

(ii) Explain two challenges he could have experience while carrying out the maintenance identified in (i). (4 marks)

Answer

(a) System Design Terms:

  • (i) Modularity: Designing a system as a collection of independent, interchangeable components (modules), each responsible for a specific function. Promotes reusability and maintainability.
  • (ii) Cohesion: The degree to which the elements within a single module belong together and focus on a single task. High cohesion is desirable (module does one thing well).

(b) Characteristics of an Information System:

  • Components (Hardware, Software, Data, People, Processes)
  • Purpose/Goal-Oriented
  • Input, Processing, Output
  • Feedback Mechanism
  • Boundary and Environment
  • Interrelated Components

(c)(i) Selection Bias (Feasibility Study):

  • Occurs when the sample or data chosen for the study is not representative of the overall situation, leading to skewed or inaccurate conclusions about feasibility.

(c)(ii) Closed-Loop System:

  • A system that uses feedback from its own output to control or modify its actions, aiming to maintain a desired state.
+---------+      +-----------+      +--------+
| Input   |----->| Processor |----->| Output |
+---------+      +-----------+      +--------+
    ^                 |                 |
    | Control Signal  |                 | Feedback
    +-----------------| Sensor/Monitor  |<----------+
                      +-----------------+

Description: Input affects the Processor, which creates Output. A Sensor measures the Output and sends Feedback. The Processor compares Feedback to a reference/goal and adjusts its actions via a Control Signal.

(d)(i) Type of Maintenance:

  • Adaptive Maintenance. Justification: This involves modifying the system to cope with changes in the technical environment, such as accommodating new technology.

(d)(ii) Challenges of Adaptive Maintenance:

  • Integration Issues: Ensuring the new technology works correctly with existing parts of the system.
  • Testing Complexity: Requires thorough testing to ensure changes haven't introduced new problems (regression testing).
  • Learning Curve: Staff may need training on the new technology.
  • Cost & Time: Can be expensive and time-consuming to implement and test changes related to new technology.
