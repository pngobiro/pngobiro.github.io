Question 1 (15 marks)

(a) State the meaning of each of the following terms as used in Artificial Intelligence:

(i) environment;

(ii) forward chaining;

(iii) heuristics;

(iv) knowledge engineering;

(v) percepts. (5 marks)

(b) Explain a reason why each of the following entities may require accounting information:

(i) investors;

(ii) lenders;

(iii) creditors. (6 marks)

(c) Distinguish between a polyline and a spline as used in Computer Aided Design (CAD) programs. (4 marks)

Answer

(a) AI Terms:

(i) Environment:

  • The external world or context in which an AI agent operates
  • Includes all factors that affect and interact with the agent

(ii) Forward chaining:

  • A reasoning method that starts with known facts
  • Applies rules to reach new conclusions
  • Data-driven inference process

(iii) Heuristics:

  • Problem-solving methods using rules of thumb
  • Practical techniques for finding solutions quickly
  • May not guarantee optimal solutions

(iv) Knowledge engineering:

  • Process of building knowledge-based systems
  • Involves capturing and structuring expert knowledge
  • Converting human expertise into computer-usable form

(v) Percepts:

  • Input received by an AI agent from its environment
  • Sensory information or data
  • Used for decision making and response

(b) Need for accounting information:

(i) Investors:

  • Evaluate investment potential
  • Assess company's financial health
  • Make informed investment decisions

(ii) Lenders:

  • Assess creditworthiness
  • Evaluate ability to repay loans
  • Determine risk levels

(iii) Creditors:

  • Evaluate payment capability
  • Assess short-term liquidity
  • Make credit term decisions

(c) Polyline vs. Spline:

Polyline Spline
Series of connected straight lines Smooth curved line
Angular connections between segments Continuous curvature
Used for straight-edged shapes Used for flowing, organic shapes
Linear segments between points Mathematical curve through points

Question 2 (15 marks)

(a) Explain the effect of enabling annotative properties to a drawing in a CAD program. (2 marks)

(b) State whether each of the following items is recorded on the debit or credit side of the trial balance: (3 marks)

Carriage inwards, Carriage outwards, Sales, Manufacturing Expenses, Sales returns, Discount received

(c) Distinguish between the latitude and the longitude of a point as used in Geographic Information System (GIS). (4 marks)

(d) Programmers at Gilko are faced with technological challenges of integrating natural language processing in their proposed internet search engine system. Explain three challenges that they may be experiencing. (6 marks)

Answer

(a) Annotative properties in CAD:

  • Annotations scale automatically with viewport changes
  • Maintains consistent text and dimension sizes across different views
  • Improves drawing readability at different scales

(b) Trial balance entries:

Item Side
Carriage inwards Debit
Carriage outwards Debit
Sales Credit
Manufacturing Expenses Debit
Sales returns Debit
Discount received Credit

(c) Latitude vs. Longitude:

Latitude Longitude
Measures north-south position Measures east-west position
Parallel lines to equator Meridian lines from pole to pole
Range: -90° to +90° Range: -180° to +180°
0° at equator 0° at prime meridian

(d) NLP integration challenges:

  1. Language ambiguity
    • Multiple word meanings
    • Context-dependent interpretation
    • Idiomatic expressions
  2. Processing complexity
    • Computational resource requirements
    • Real-time response needs
    • Large data processing
  3. Language variations
    • Different dialects and accents
    • Grammar irregularities
    • Multilingual support

Question 3 (15 marks)

(a) Outline five advantages of using double entry system in accounting procedures. (5 marks)

(b) Outline a way in which each of the following intelligent agents perceive their environment:

(i) a human agent;

(ii) a robotic agent;

(iii) a software agent. (3 marks)

(c) Outline one role of each of the following personnel in GIS applications:

(i) Database managers;

(ii) Remote sensing analysts;

(iii) Spatial analysts;

(iv) Land surveyors. (4 marks)

(d) Paul intends to raise capital from potential investors to expand his business. State three financial statements that these investors may require in order to assess the viability of the business. (3 marks)

Answer

(a) Advantages of double entry system:

  1. Error detection
    • Trial balance verification
    • Easy identification of mistakes
  2. Complete records
    • Captures both aspects of transactions
    • Comprehensive financial history
  3. Financial position clarity
    • Clear view of assets and liabilities
    • Accurate profit determination
  4. Fraud prevention
    • Built-in verification system
    • Audit trail availability
  5. Decision making support
    • Detailed financial information
    • Performance analysis capability

(b) Agent environment perception:

(i) Human agent:

  • Through five senses (sight, hearing, touch, smell, taste)
  • Cognitive processing of sensory input

(ii) Robotic agent:

  • Through sensors (cameras, proximity sensors, touch sensors)
  • Digital signal processing

(iii) Software agent:

  • Through data inputs and API calls
  • Digital information processing

(c) GIS personnel roles:

(i) Database managers:

  • Maintain spatial databases
  • Ensure data integrity and security

(ii) Remote sensing analysts:

  • Interpret satellite/aerial imagery
  • Extract geographic information

(iii) Spatial analysts:

  • Perform geographic analysis
  • Create spatial models

(iv) Land surveyors:

  • Collect field data
  • Measure and map land features

(d) Required financial statements:

  1. Balance Sheet
    • Shows financial position
    • Assets and liabilities
  2. Income Statement
    • Shows profitability
    • Revenue and expenses
  3. Cash Flow Statement
    • Shows cash movements
    • Operating, investing, and financing activities

Question 6 (15 marks)

(a) Explain each of the following terms as used in Artificial Intelligence:

(i) Artificial Neural Networks;

(ii) Expert Systems. (4 marks)

(b) Explain a reason why businesses use each of the following records in financial accounting:

(i) Petty cash;

(ii) Sales journal. (4 marks)

(c) XYZ company has been advised to computerise its book-keeping and accounting procedures. Outline four benefits that would be gained by the company from heeding the advice. (4 marks)

(d) Wendi intends to purchase a CAD program for use in her organisation to design drawings and objects. State three such programs she may choose from. (3 marks)

Answer

(a) AI terms:

(i) Artificial Neural Networks:

  • Computing systems inspired by biological neural networks
  • Process information through interconnected nodes
  • Learn and improve through training with data
  • Used for pattern recognition and prediction

(ii) Expert Systems:

  • AI programs that emulate decision-making of human experts
  • Use knowledge base and inference engine
  • Provide expert-level solutions in specific domains
  • Support decision-making processes

(b) Financial records usage:

(i) Petty cash:

  • Manage small, frequent expenses
  • Avoid processing minor transactions through main accounts
  • Maintain control over small expenditures

(ii) Sales journal:

  • Record all credit sales transactions
  • Provide detailed sales history
  • Facilitate customer account management

(c) Benefits of computerized accounting:

  1. Increased accuracy
    • Reduced human error
    • Automatic calculations
  2. Time efficiency
    • Faster processing
    • Automated reporting
  3. Better organization
    • Systematic record keeping
    • Easy retrieval of information
  4. Real-time reporting
    • Instant financial status
    • Quick decision making

(d) CAD programs:

  1. AutoCAD
    • Industry standard
    • Comprehensive features
  2. SolidWorks
    • 3D modeling capabilities
    • Engineering focus
  3. SketchUp
    • User-friendly interface
    • Architectural design focus
