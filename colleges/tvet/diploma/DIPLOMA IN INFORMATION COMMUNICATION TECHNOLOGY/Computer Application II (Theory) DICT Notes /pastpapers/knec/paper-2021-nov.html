Question 1 (15 marks)

(a) Outline three advantages of using Geographic Information Systems to manage disasters in a region. (3 marks)

(b) Differentiate between best-first search and breadth-first search as used in artificial intelligence. (4 marks)

(c) Kalili Warehouses Ltd. has incurred the following expenses for the year 2020:

(i) Construction of an extension to the current warehouse;

(ii) Repainting the exterior of the current warehouse;

(iii) Replacing the main entrance door to the warehouse after the old one was damaged by a delivery truck;

(iv) Transport for materials for constructing the warehouse extension;

Identify the type of expenditure incurred by the business in each case. (4 marks)

(d) A user intends to remove some of the quick access toggle commands on a Computer-Aided Design interface. Outline four such commands that the user is likely to remove. (4 marks)

Answer

(a) Advantages of GIS in disaster management:

  1. Real-time monitoring and early warning
    • Tracks disaster development
    • Enables timely evacuation
  2. Resource allocation optimization
    • Identifies areas needing immediate attention
    • Helps in planning emergency response
  3. Risk assessment and mapping
    • Creates vulnerability maps
    • Helps in prevention planning

(b) Best-first search vs. Breadth-first search:

Best-first Search Breadth-first Search
Uses heuristic evaluation Systematic level-by-level search
Explores most promising nodes first Explores all nodes at current level
Can be more efficient Guarantees optimal solution
May not find optimal solution More memory intensive

(c) Types of expenditure:

(i) Construction of extension: Capital expenditure

  • Creates a new asset
  • Long-term benefit

(ii) Repainting exterior: Revenue expenditure

  • Maintenance cost
  • Regular upkeep

(iii) Replacing entrance door: Revenue expenditure

  • Repair and replacement
  • Maintains existing asset

(iv) Transport for extension materials: Capital expenditure

  • Part of extension cost
  • Direct cost of new asset

(d) Quick access toggle commands that could be removed:

  1. Grid display toggle
  2. Snap mode toggle
  3. Ortho mode toggle
  4. Object snap toggle

Question 2 (15 marks)

(a) Explain the meaning of the term robot as used in artificial intelligence. (2 marks)

(b) Distinguish between the explode and offset commands as used in Computer-Aided Design. (4 marks)

(c) The following drawings show projections of a solid block in third angle projection. Sketch the block in isometric view taking X as the lower most position. (6 marks)

(d) Outline three reasons for integrating database management systems in Geographic Information System. (3 marks)

Answer

(a) Robot in AI:

  • A programmable machine that can sense, process information, and interact with its environment
  • Uses AI algorithms to make decisions and perform tasks autonomously

(b) Explode vs. Offset commands:

Explode command:

  • Breaks complex objects into component parts
  • Separates compound objects into basic elements
  • Allows editing of individual components

Offset command:

  • Creates parallel copies of objects
  • Maintains specified distance from original
  • Used for creating parallel lines or shapes

Question 6 (15 marks)

(a) Outline four types of precision errors associated with information from a Geographic Information System. (4 marks)

(b) A Computer-Aided Design user has a drawing which is to be modified. Outline six commands in the program that he is likely to use. (3 marks)

(c) Outline three factors to consider when selecting a search algorithm to be used in artificial intelligence. (3 marks)

(d) With the aid of an example, distinguish between accounts payable and accounts receivable as used in accounting. (5 marks)

Answer

(a) GIS precision errors:

  1. Source data errors
    • Inaccurate measurements
    • Data collection errors
  2. Processing errors
    • Data transformation mistakes
    • Calculation errors
  3. Scale errors
    • Resolution mismatch
    • Generalization issues
  4. Classification errors
    • Incorrect categorization
    • Attribute assignment mistakes

(b) CAD modification commands:

  1. MOVE
  2. COPY
  3. STRETCH
  4. ROTATE
  5. SCALE
  6. TRIM

(c) Search algorithm selection factors:

  1. Problem complexity
    • Size of search space
    • Number of variables
  2. Performance requirements
    • Speed constraints
    • Memory limitations
  3. Solution requirements
    • Optimal vs satisfactory solution
    • Completeness requirements

(d) Accounts payable vs. receivable:

Accounts Payable Accounts Receivable
Money owed to suppliers Money owed by customers
Example: Bills to be paid Example: Customer credit sales
Liability account Asset account

Example:

  • Accounts Payable: When a business buys inventory worth $1000 on credit
  • Accounts Receivable: When a business sells goods worth $1000 on credit
