Question 1 (15 marks)

(a) Outline the function of each of the following commands as used in a Computer-Aided Design (CAD) program:

(i) erase;

(ii) regen;

(iii) join. (3 marks)

(b) A company was tasked to develop a Geographical Information System (GIS) to manage information about schools in the country. Outline four data attributes that this system is likely to use. (4 marks)

(c) Outline four reasons that may necessitate a business to use computer systems in recording financial transactions. (4 marks)

(d) Outline two characteristics of job categories that are harder to replace with robotic technologies, giving two examples in each case. (4 marks)

Answer

(a) CAD commands:

(i) erase: Removes selected objects or elements from the drawing. It allows users to delete unwanted geometry, text, or other CAD entities.

(ii) regen: Regenerates or refreshes the display of the drawing. This command recalculates all geometry and updates the screen, particularly useful after complex operations or when display issues occur.

(iii) join: Combines multiple objects into a single entity. It's used to connect lines, arcs, or polylines that share endpoints into one continuous object.

(b) GIS data attributes for schools:

  1. Geographic location (latitude and longitude coordinates)
  2. School demographics (student population, staff count)
  3. Infrastructure details (number of classrooms, facilities)
  4. Academic performance metrics (exam results, completion rates)

(c) Reasons for using computer systems in financial transactions:

  1. Accuracy - Reduces human error in calculations and data entry
  2. Speed - Processes transactions faster than manual methods
  3. Security - Provides better control and audit trails
  4. Real-time reporting - Enables immediate access to financial data and reports

(d) Job categories harder to replace with robotic technologies:

1. Creative jobs requiring original thinking and innovation

  • Examples: Art directors, Marketing strategists

2. Jobs requiring complex human interaction and emotional intelligence

  • Examples: Psychologists, Teachers

Question 2 (15 marks)

(a) State six source documents used to record financial transactions in accounting. (3 marks)

(b) A city management ordered that all commuter vehicles in the city be self-driven. Explain two benefits that these type of vehicles are likely to bring. (4 marks)

(c) Distinguish between absolute and relative coordinates systems as used in a CAD program. (4 marks)

(d) Distinguish between reference map and thematic maps as used in GIS. (4 marks)

Answer

(a) Source documents in accounting:

  1. Invoices
  2. Receipts
  3. Purchase orders
  4. Bank statements
  5. Credit notes
  6. Debit notes

(b) Benefits of self-driven commuter vehicles:

  1. Enhanced safety
    • Eliminates human error in driving
    • Reduces accidents caused by fatigue or distractions
  2. Improved traffic management
    • Better coordination between vehicles
    • Optimized route selection and traffic flow

(c) Absolute vs. Relative coordinates in CAD:

Absolute coordinates:

  • Uses fixed points relative to the origin (0,0)
  • Specifies exact X,Y positions in the drawing space

Relative coordinates:

  • Measures distances from the last point entered
  • Uses displacement values rather than fixed positions

(d) Reference maps vs. Thematic maps in GIS:

Reference maps:

  • Show locations of natural and human-made features
  • Focus on accurate geographical representation

Thematic maps:

  • Display specific themes or subjects
  • Emphasize spatial patterns of specific phenomena

Question 3 (15 marks)

(a) Outline three reasons that make relational database management system (DBMS) suitable for storing data in GIS. (3 marks)

(b) With the aid of an example, describe the Polar Coordinates system as used in a CAD program. (4 marks)

(c) Explain three ethical concerns that may arise as a result of introduction of robots to support operations in the society. (4 marks)

(d) Explain two rules of double entry in accounting. (4 marks)

Answer

(a) Reasons for using RDBMS in GIS:

  1. Efficient data organization through tables and relationships
  2. Support for complex spatial queries and data analysis
  3. Data integrity and consistency through constraints and normalization

(b) Polar Coordinates system:

Polar coordinates specify points using:

  • Distance from origin (radius)
  • Angle from reference direction (usually horizontal)

Example: Point (5, 45Â°) means:

  • 5 units from the origin
  • 45 degrees counterclockwise from horizontal

(c) Ethical concerns with robots in society:

  1. Job displacement and unemployment
    • Workers losing jobs to automation
  2. Privacy and surveillance concerns
    • Data collection by robotic systems
  3. Safety and liability issues
    • Responsibility for robot-caused accidents

(d) Rules of double entry accounting:

  1. Every transaction affects at least two accounts
    • One debit entry and one credit entry
  2. Total debits must equal total credits
    • Maintains accounting equation balance

Question 4 (15 marks)

(a) Outline the mouse operation that would be carried out to achieve each of the following view options in a CAD program:

(i) zoom in;

(ii) zoom extents;

(iii) pan. (3 marks)

(b) Outline three advantages of raster data models in Geographical Information System (GIS). (3 marks)

(c) Describe two features of a stores ledger as used in accounting. (4 marks)

(d) Figure 1 shows components of an expert system used in artificial intelligence. Use it to answer the questions that follow:

(i) Identify the component labelled 1. (1 mark)

(ii) Explain the function of each of following components in the system:

- knowledge base;

- user interface. (4 marks)

Answer

(a) CAD mouse operations:

(i) zoom in: Scroll mouse wheel forward or click and drag a window while holding Ctrl+left mouse button

(ii) zoom extents: Double-click mouse wheel or press Home key

(iii) pan: Hold down mouse wheel and drag or hold Shift+right mouse button and drag

(b) Advantages of raster data models in GIS:

  1. Simple data structure and easy to understand
  2. Efficient for representing continuous data (elevation, temperature)
  3. Good for satellite imagery and aerial photographs

(c) Features of stores ledger:

  1. Records stock movements
    • Tracks incoming and outgoing inventory
    • Shows current stock levels
  2. Contains item details
    • Item descriptions and codes
    • Unit costs and quantities

(d) Expert system components:

(i) Component 1: Inference Engine

(ii) Component functions:

Knowledge base:

  • Stores domain-specific knowledge and rules
  • Contains expert knowledge in structured format

User interface:

  • Facilitates interaction between user and system
  • Handles input and displays results in user-friendly format

Question 5 (15 marks)

(a) Outline three ways that GIS could be used in public health industry. (3 marks)

(b) Explain each of the following terms as used in artificial intelligence:

(i) Chatbots;

(ii) neural networks. (4 marks)

(c) Explain a circumstance that necessitates the use of each of the following commands in a CAD program:

(i) blocks;

(ii) splines. (4 marks)

(d) State two differences between a cash book and a bank statement as used in accounting. (4 marks)

Answer

(a) GIS applications in public health:

  1. Disease surveillance and mapping
    • Tracking outbreak patterns
    • Identifying high-risk areas
  2. Healthcare facility planning
    • Optimal location analysis
    • Service coverage assessment
  3. Environmental health monitoring
    • Pollution impact analysis
    • Health risk assessment

(b) AI terms:

(i) Chatbots:

  • Computer programs that simulate human conversation
  • Use natural language processing to understand and respond to user inputs

(ii) Neural networks:

  • Computing systems inspired by biological neural networks
  • Learn from examples to perform tasks without explicit programming

(c) CAD commands usage:

(i) blocks:

  • When creating repetitive design elements
  • For standardized components in drawings

(ii) splines:

  • When creating smooth curves
  • For organic shapes and complex contours

(d) Cash book vs. Bank statement differences:

Cash Book Bank Statement
Maintained by business Prepared by bank
Records transactions when they occur Records transactions when processed by bank

(i) withdrawal of cash from the bank for personal use;

(ii) purchase of a motorcycle on credit-general;

(iii) payment of a creditor by cheque. (3 marks)

(b) Distinguish between a multileader and multiline text as used in a CAD program. (4 marks)

(c) With the aid of an illustration, explain the following terms as used in Artificial Intelligence:

(i) agent;

(ii) environment;

(iii) percepts. (8 marks)

Answer

(a) Ledgers for transactions:

(i) Drawings ledger/Private ledger

(ii) Asset ledger and Creditors ledger

(iii) Creditors ledger and Bank ledger

(b) Multileader vs. Multiline text in CAD:

Multileader:

  • Used for annotations with leader lines
  • Points to specific parts of drawing

Multiline text:

  • Creates paragraphs of formatted text
  • Supports text formatting and word wrapping
