Question 1 (15 marks)

(a) Outline four components of an Expert System. (4 marks)

(b) Paul an architectural engineer intends to draw a floor plan of a house. State how he would use each of the following CAD commands:

(i) mirror; (2 marks)

(ii) Explode. (2 marks)

(c) Define the term Geographical Information System. (1 mark)

(d) Figure 1 shows an object drawn in an isometric view. Sketch the front, end and plan elevation in the first angle orthographic projection. Take the front elevation to be the view indicated by arrow labelled A. (6 marks)

Answer

(a) Expert System components:

  1. Knowledge base
    • Stores domain knowledge
    • Contains rules and facts
  2. Inference engine
    • Processes knowledge
    • Makes decisions
  3. User interface
    • Input/output mechanism
    • User interaction
  4. Explanation facility
    • Justifies decisions
    • Shows reasoning process

(b) CAD commands usage:

(i) Mirror command:

  • Creates symmetrical copies
  • Flips objects across axis
  • Used for identical opposite sides

(ii) Explode command:

  • Breaks compound objects
  • Separates elements
  • Allows individual editing

(c) GIS definition:

  • Computer system for capturing, storing, analyzing and displaying geographic data
  • Integrates spatial and attribute data
  • Supports spatial decision making

(d) Orthographic projections:

[Note: A proper drawing would show:

  • Front elevation
  • End elevation
  • Plan view
  • Proper alignment
  • Hidden lines
  • Correct proportions

Question 2 (15 marks)

(a)(i) Define the term accounting. (1 mark)

(ii) Outline four objectives of accounting in an organization. (4 marks)

(b)(i) With the aid of a diagram describe spatial data layer as used in GIS. (4 marks)

(ii) Outline two parameters that determine the position of a geographic object in a spatial space. (2 marks)

(c) Tumaini Youth Group started their business on 1st May 2014. Calculate their capital based on the following transactions:

  • Bought a van for Ksh. 450,000
  • Bought a business stall for Ksh. 200,000
  • Purchased stock worth Ksh. 1,500,000 (owing Ksh. 104,000)
  • Borrowed Ksh. 150,000 from BKB bank
  • Cash in hand Ksh. 40,000
  • Cash at bank Ksh. 11,000

Answer

(a)(i) Accounting definition:

  • Process of recording, classifying, summarizing and interpreting financial transactions
  • Systematic method of tracking business activities

(ii) Accounting objectives:

  1. Financial reporting
    • Accurate records
    • Compliance requirements
  2. Decision making
    • Information for management
    • Performance evaluation
  3. Control
    • Asset protection
    • Fraud prevention
  4. Planning
    • Budgeting
    • Future projections

(b) GIS spatial data:

(i) Spatial data layers:

                [Base Map Layer]
                      ↓
                [Roads Layer]
                      ↓
                [Buildings Layer]
                      ↓
                [Vegetation Layer]
  • Thematic organization
  • Layer hierarchy
  • Independent manipulation

(ii) Geographic position parameters:

  1. Coordinates
    • Latitude/Longitude
    • Grid references
  2. Elevation
    • Height above sea level
    • Terrain position

(c) Capital calculation:

                Assets:
                Van                450,000
                Business stall     200,000
                Stock            1,500,000
                Cash in hand       40,000
                Cash at bank       11,000
                Total Assets    2,201,000

                Liabilities:
                Stock owing       104,000
                Bank loan         150,000
                Total Liabilities 254,000

                Capital = Assets - Liabilities
                       = 2,201,000 - 254,000
                       = Ksh. 1,947,000
