Double Entry System

üéØ Learning Objectives

  • Define double entry system.
  • Explain the rules of double entry.
  • Apply double entry rules to business transactions.

üìöDouble Entry System

This is a system of book-keeping which recognizes the dual aspect of every transaction. It means that every transaction has got two effects, a debit and a credit, and for every debit entry there must be a corresponding credit entry and vice versa.

For every transaction, at least two accounts are affected. One account is debited and another account is credited. It is on this principle that the whole system of accounting is based.

üìöRules of Double Entry

The rules are based on the classification of accounts into:

  • Personal accounts
  • Impersonal accounts

Impersonal accounts are further classified into:

  • Real accounts
  • Nominal accounts

üìöClassification of Accounts

  1. Personal Accounts

    2. These are accounts of persons, firms, companies, debtors and creditors. The rule is:

    • Debit the receiver
    • Credit the giver

    Examples of personal accounts are:

    • Debtors accounts
    • Creditors accounts
    • Capital account
    • Drawings account
    • Bank account
    • Loan accounts
    • Outstanding expenses accounts
    • Prepaid expenses accounts
    • Accrued income accounts
    • Unearned income accounts

    Personal accounts can be further classified into:

    • Natural personal accounts e.g. Kamau‚Äôs account, Atieno‚Äôs account
    • Artificial personal accounts e.g. company accounts, firms accounts, bank accounts
    • Representative personal accounts e.g. prepaid rent account, outstanding salaries account

    The common feature of all personal accounts is that they relate to persons.

  2. Impersonal Accounts

    3. These are accounts which are not personal accounts. They are further classified into:

    1. Real Accounts

      2. These are accounts of tangible and intangible assets. The rule is:

      • Debit what comes into the business
      • Credit what goes out of the business

      Examples of real accounts are:

      • Land and buildings account
      • Motor vehicles account
      • Furniture and fittings account
      • Plant and machinery account
      • Stock account
      • Cash account
      • Goodwill account
      • Patents account
      • Copyrights account
      • Trademarks account
      • Investments account

      Real accounts relate to assets and properties.

    2. Nominal Accounts

      3. These are accounts of expenses, losses, incomes and gains. The rule is:

      • Debit all expenses and losses
      • Credit all incomes and gains

      Examples of nominal accounts are:

      • Sales account
      • Purchases account
      • Rent account
      • Salaries and wages account
      • Discount allowed account
      • Discount received account
      • Commission received account
      • Carriage inwards account
      • Carriage outwards account
      • Depreciation account
      • Bad debts account
      • Interest account

      Nominal accounts relate to incomes and expenses.
