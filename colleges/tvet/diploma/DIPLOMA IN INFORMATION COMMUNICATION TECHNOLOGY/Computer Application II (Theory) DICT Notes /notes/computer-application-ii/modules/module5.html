Module 5

Books of Original Entry

üéØ Learning Objectives

  • Define books of original entry.
  • Explain the various books of original entry.
  • Prepare the books of original entry.

üìöBooks of Original Entry

These are books in which transactions are recorded for the first time from the source documents. They are also called subsidiary books or day books. The source documents are documents which provide evidence that a transaction has taken place and they provide the details of the transaction. Examples of source documents are receipts, invoices, credit notes, debit notes, pay-in slips, cheques, etc.

The books of original entry include:

  1. Sales journal
  2. Purchases journal
  3. Returns inwards journal
  4. Returns outwards journal
  5. Cash book
  6. Petty cash book
  7. Journal proper

üìöTypes of Books of Original Entry

  1. Sales Journal

    2. This is a book used to record credit sales only. Cash sales are recorded in the cash book. The source document for sales journal is the sales invoice (copy).

  2. Purchases Journal

    3. This is a book used to record credit purchases only. Cash purchases are recorded in the cash book. The source document for purchases journal is the purchase invoice.

  3. Returns Inwards Journal

    4. This journal is used to record goods returned by customers. The source document is credit note.

  4. Returns Outwards Journal

    5. This journal is used to record goods returned to suppliers. The source document is debit note.

  5. Cash Book

    6. This book is used to record all cash and bank transactions. It is like a bank and cash account in one book. It has two sides, the debit side and the credit side. The debit side is used to record cash and bank receipts while the credit side is used to record cash and bank payments. The source documents are receipts, invoices, pay-in slips, cheques, etc.

  6. Petty Cash Book

    7. This book is used to record small cash payments e.g. postage, stationery, fares, etc. The source document is petty cash voucher.

  7. Journal Proper

    8. This book is used to record transactions which cannot be recorded in any of the above books. Examples of transactions recorded in the journal proper are:

    • Credit transactions for assets e.g. credit purchase of furniture will be recorded in journal proper and not purchases journal because purchases journal is for goods bought for resale.
    • Opening entries
    • Closing entries
    • Adjusting entries
    • Transfer entries
    • Rectification entries
    • Writing off bad debts
    • Depreciation of fixed assets
