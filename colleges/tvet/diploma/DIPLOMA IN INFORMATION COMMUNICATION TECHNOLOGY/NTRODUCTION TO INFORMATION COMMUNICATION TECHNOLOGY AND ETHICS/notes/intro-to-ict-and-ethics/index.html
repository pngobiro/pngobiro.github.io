Intro to ICT and Ethics

Duration: 15 hours Level: Diploma

Course Overview

This course provides an introduction to Information and Communication Technology and Ethics, covering fundamental concepts, hardware, software, networking, data processing, security, ergonomics, ethical and professional issues, conflict management, social responsibility, and emerging trends in ICT.

Course Modules

  1. Module 1: Information Communication Technology

    Introduction to ICT concepts, history, advantages, disadvantages, and classifications.

    60 minutes

  2. Module 2: Computer Hardware

    Detailed study of computer hardware components and their functions.

    90 minutes

  3. Module 3: Serial, Parallel Transmission & Software Introduction

    Understanding data transmission methods and introduction to computer software.

    60 minutes

  4. Module 4: Basic Computer Networking

    Fundamentals of computer networking, types, components, and topologies.

    90 minutes

  5. Module 5: Data Processing

    Exploring data processing cycles, modes, hierarchy, and file organization.

    60 minutes

  6. Module 6: Data Security

    Understanding data security threats, hazards, and securing methods.

    75 minutes

  7. Module 7: Computer Safety and Ergonomics

    Focus on computer safety, ergonomics, and related health issues.

    60 minutes

  8. Module 8: Ethics and Professional Issues in ICT

    Ethical considerations and professional conduct in the ICT field.

    60 minutes

  9. Module 9: Conflict Management

    Understanding and managing conflicts in professional environments.

    45 minutes

  10. Module 10: Social Responsibility in ICT

    Exploring social responsibilities and environmental concerns in ICT.

    45 minutes

  11. Module 11: Emerging Trends and Issues in ICT

    Overview of emerging trends and challenges in the ICT landscape.

    60 minutes

Prerequisites

  • Basic computer literacy.
  • No prior ICT knowledge required.
