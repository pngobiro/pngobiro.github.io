INTRODUCTION TO INFORMATION COMMUNICATION TECHNOLOGY AND ETHICS

Exam Code: 2920/101

Duration: 3 hours

Period: March/April 2024

1. (a) (4 marks)

Outline four advantages of using a hard disk for data storage.

Answer

Large Storage Capacity: Hard disks offer substantial storage capacities, typically ranging from hundreds of gigabytes to several terabytes, making them suitable for storing large volumes of data, including operating systems, applications, and user files.

Cost-Effectiveness: Compared to other storage technologies like SSDs (Solid State Drives), hard disks provide a lower cost per gigabyte, making them an economical option for mass storage solutions.

Durability: Hard disks are relatively durable and can withstand typical operating conditions, providing reliable long-term data storage when handled properly.

Data Access Speed: While slower than SSDs, modern hard disks still offer reasonable data access speeds suitable for most general computing tasks and applications.

1. (c) (4 marks)

Differentiate between policy and guideline as used in Information Communication Technology data protection.

Answer

Policy:

In ICT data protection, a policy is a high-level, formal document that outlines an organization's overarching stance and mandatory requirements regarding data protection. It defines the principles, objectives, and governance framework for data protection, setting the mandatory rules and expectations for data handling across the organization. Policies are authoritative and non-negotiable, establishing the 'what' and 'why' of data protection.

Guideline:

Guidelines, in contrast, are lower-level, advisory documents that provide detailed practical steps and recommended best practices on how to implement and comply with data protection policies. Guidelines offer specific instructions, procedures, and tips on the 'how-to' aspects of data protection, aiding employees in practically applying the policy requirements in their daily tasks. Guidelines are more flexible and descriptive, offering support and direction for policy implementation.

Key Differences:

Policies are mandatory, high-level rules defining 'what' and 'why', while guidelines are advisory, detailed instructions on 'how-to' implement policies. Policies set the overall data protection framework, and guidelines provide practical assistance for compliance. Policies are authoritative and less flexible, whereas guidelines are supportive and more adaptable to specific situations.

Answer

Database Design and Implementation: Database Administrators (DBAs) design and implement database systems based on organizational requirements. This includes creating database schemas, defining data structures, and setting up database environments to ensure efficient data storage and retrieval.

Database Security and Access Control: DBAs are responsible for implementing and managing database security measures. They establish access controls, user permissions, and security protocols to protect sensitive data from unauthorized access, breaches, and cyber threats.

They analyze database performance metrics, identify bottlenecks, and implement tuning strategies to enhance query speeds, system responsiveness, and overall database efficiency.

They schedule regular backups, test recovery processes, and ensure data can be effectively restored in case of system failures, disasters, or data breaches, ensuring business continuity.

2. (c) (4 marks)

Differentiate between single-mode and multi-mode fibre optic cables.

Answer

Single-Mode Fiber Optic Cables:

Single-mode fiber optic cables are designed to transmit a single ray or mode of light through a very narrow glass core (around 9 microns in diameter). They are optimized for long-distance data transmission with minimal signal loss and high bandwidth capacity. Single-mode fibers use laser light sources and are typically more expensive due to their precision manufacturing.

Multi-Mode Fiber Optic Cables:

Multi-mode fiber optic cables have a larger core diameter (typically 50 or 62.5 microns) that allows multiple modes or rays of light to travel simultaneously. They are used for shorter-distance data transmission, such as within buildings or campuses. Multi-mode fibers use LED light sources and are generally less expensive than single-mode fibers but have lower bandwidth and higher signal attenuation over long distances.

Key Differences:

The primary difference is the core diameter and mode of light transmission. Single-mode fibers have a narrow core for a single light mode and long-distance, high-bandwidth transmission, while multi-mode fibers have a larger core for multiple light modes and shorter-distance applications. Single-mode is for long distances and higher cost, multi-mode for shorter distances and lower cost.

These laws promote data security best practices, enhance accountability for data protection, and empower individuals with rights over their personal information.

Computer Misuse Act and Amendments: Strengthening or enacting a Computer Misuse Act that specifically addresses unauthorized access, modification, or disruption of computer systems and data is essential. Regularly updating and amending such legislation to cover emerging cyber threats and technologies ensures legal frameworks remain relevant and effective in combating evolving cyber attacks.

3. (c) (4 marks)

Differentiate between leasing and outsourcing methods of software acquisition.

Answer

Leasing Software:

Leasing software, also known as software subscription, involves acquiring software access rights for a specific period through a recurring fee, typically monthly or annually. The software vendor retains ownership, and the lessee gains the right to use the software and receive updates and support during the lease term. Leasing often includes cloud-based software solutions and Software-as-a-Service (SaaS) models.

Outsourcing Software Acquisition:

Outsourcing software acquisition involves contracting a third-party company to develop, customize, or manage software solutions for an organization. The organization delegates software-related tasks, such as development, maintenance, and support, to an external provider, focusing on their core business functions while relying on external expertise for software needs. Outsourcing can include custom software development or managed software services.

Key Differences:

Leasing is about renting software usage rights, whereas outsourcing is about delegating software-related work to an external provider. Leasing focuses on access and usage, outsourcing on development and management. Leasing typically involves standardized software, while outsourcing can include custom solutions. Ownership remains with the vendor in leasing, while outsourcing can involve custom software ownership agreements.

7. (a) Outline four impacts of Very Large-Scale Integrated Circuits in the fourth-generation computers.

Answer

Miniaturization and Size Reduction: Very Large-Scale Integrated (VLSI) circuits enabled significant miniaturization and size reduction of computers. VLSI technology allowed thousands of transistors to be integrated onto a single chip, leading to smaller, more compact computers compared to previous generations.

Increased Processing Power and Speed: VLSI circuits dramatically increased the processing power and speed of computers. Microprocessors based on VLSI technology could perform complex calculations and execute instructions much faster than earlier generations, enhancing computing performance significantly.

Reduced Cost and Affordability: VLSI technology led to a substantial reduction in the cost of computer production. Mass production of microprocessors became more economical, making computers more affordable and accessible to a wider range of users, including individuals and small businesses.

Enhanced Reliability and Energy Efficiency: VLSI circuits improved computer reliability and energy efficiency. Integrated circuits were more reliable and generated less heat compared to discrete components, leading to more stable and energy-efficient computers. Lower power consumption and increased reliability contributed to the widespread adoption of computers.

8. (c) (i) (4 marks)

Differentiate between sector and track as used in a computer hard disk.

Answer

Track:

In a computer hard disk, a track is a concentric circular path on the surface of a disk platter where data is recorded magnetically. Tracks are formatted during disk preparation and serve as the fundamental unit for data organization on the disk surface. Data is stored sequentially along each track, and hard disks typically have thousands of tracks on each platter surface.

Sector:

A sector is a subdivision of a track on a hard disk platter. Each track is divided into multiple sectors, which are the smallest addressable units of storage on a hard disk. Sectors are used to store fixed blocks of data, typically 512 bytes or 4 kilobytes in size. Sectors provide the basic storage units for reading and writing data on the disk, and file systems organize data storage at the sector level.

Key Differences:

Tracks are concentric circles on the disk surface, while sectors are subdivisions of tracks. Tracks are pathways for data recording, and sectors are the smallest storage units within tracks. Tracks are higher-level organizational units, and sectors are the basic units for data addressing and storage. Sectors are contained within tracks, and tracks are concentric paths across the disk platter.
