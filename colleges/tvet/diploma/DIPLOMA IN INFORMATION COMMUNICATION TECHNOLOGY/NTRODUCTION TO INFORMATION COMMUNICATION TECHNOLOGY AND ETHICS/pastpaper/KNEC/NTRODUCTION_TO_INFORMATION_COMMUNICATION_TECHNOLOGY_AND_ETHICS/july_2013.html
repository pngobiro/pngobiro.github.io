INTRODUCTION TO ICT AND ETHICS

Exam Code: 2920/101

Duration: 3 hours

Period: July 2013

1. (a) (4 marks)

Outline four disadvantages of a microfilm output as used in data processing.

Answer

Microfilm output presents several disadvantages in data processing environments.

Access Limitations: Microfilm requires specialized equipment such as readers and printers, making direct human readability and access cumbersome compared to digital formats.

Update Inflexibility: Updating microfilm is a complex and often impractical process. Once data is recorded, modifications or additions are difficult to implement, leading to version control challenges.

Storage and Handling: While compact, microfilm requires specific environmental conditions for long-term preservation. Improper storage can lead to degradation, and physical handling can cause scratches or damage.

Retrieval Inefficiency: Searching for specific information within microfilm is a sequential process, which is significantly slower and less efficient than the random access capabilities of digital storage.

1. (b) (i) (2 marks)

Define the term ethics as used in Information Communication Technology.

Answer

Ethics in Information Communication Technology refers to the moral principles and standards that govern the conduct of individuals and organizations in their use and application of ICT. It encompasses responsible, just, and fair practices in the digital domain.

1. (b) (ii) (2 marks)

Outline two ways that could be used to enforce ethics in Information Communication Technology industry.

Answer

Legislation and Regulation: Governments can enact laws and regulations that set ethical standards for ICT practices, such as data protection laws and cybersecurity regulations. Enforcement mechanisms and penalties can deter unethical behavior.

Professional Codes of Conduct: ICT professional organizations can develop and promote codes of conduct that outline ethical responsibilities for their members. These codes, combined with disciplinary actions for violations, can encourage ethical behavior within the industry.

This enhanced accessibility improves productivity and enables real-time collaboration among teams, regardless of geographical location.

1. (d) (i) (4 marks)

Distinguish between personal responsibility and corporate social responsibility as used in ICT and ethics.

Answer

Personal Responsibility:

Personal responsibility in ICT and ethics refers to the ethical obligations and accountability of individual ICT users or professionals. It emphasizes the moral duty of individuals to act ethically in their use of technology, including respecting intellectual property, protecting privacy, and using technology responsibly.

Corporate Social Responsibility:

Corporate social responsibility (CSR) in ICT and ethics pertains to the broader ethical obligations of organizations operating in the ICT sector. It extends beyond legal compliance and focuses on a company's commitment to ethical and sustainable practices. CSR includes environmental sustainability, ethical sourcing of materials, fair labor practices, and contributing positively to the community through technology.

Key Differences: Personal responsibility is focused on individual conduct, while corporate social responsibility addresses organizational-level ethics and societal impact. CSR encompasses personal responsibility but extends to broader organizational policies and actions that affect stakeholders and society.

2. (a) (i) (2 marks)

Define each of the following terms as used in computer networks: extranet;

Answer

An extranet is a controlled private network that allows access to authorized external users, such as business partners, vendors, or customers. It extends the intranet beyond the organization's boundaries, enabling secure collaboration and information sharing with selected external entities.

2. (a) (ii) (2 marks)

Define each of the following terms as used in computer networks: intranet.

Answer

An intranet is a private network that is contained within an organization. It is designed to be accessible only to the organization's members, employees, or others with authorization. Intranets typically use internet protocols and technologies to share information and computing resources within the organization.

2. (b) (4 marks)

Distinguish between peer-to-peer and client-server as used in computer networks.

Answer

Peer-to-Peer Network:

In a peer-to-peer (P2P) network, computers act as both clients and servers. There is no central server; instead, each computer (peer) can directly share resources and communicate with other computers on the network. P2P networks are characterized by decentralized control and ease of setup, often used for file sharing and small networks.

Client-Server Network:

In a client-server network, there are dedicated servers that provide resources and services, and clients that request and utilize these services. Servers are centralized and manage network resources, security, and access. Client-server networks are designed for scalability, security, and centralized administration, commonly used in enterprise environments and the internet.

Key Differences: Peer-to-peer networks are decentralized with no dedicated servers, while client-server networks are centralized with dedicated servers. P2P is simpler to set up and less secure, whereas client-server is more complex but offers better security and scalability.

Stress negatively impacts employee output by diminishing productivity and efficiency. High stress levels can also result in burnout and absenteeism, further reducing organizational productivity.

2. (c) (ii) (3 marks)

Answer

Emotions significantly influence employee output, both positively and negatively. Negative emotions such as anger or sadness can decrease focus and motivation, leading to reduced productivity and poor quality work. For example, an employee feeling undervalued and demotivated may produce lower quality code or provide substandard customer service. Conversely, positive emotions like enthusiasm and job satisfaction can enhance creativity, collaboration, and overall output, leading to increased efficiency and innovation.

2. (d) (6 marks)

Kilimu Company intends to put in place measures to reduce strain on its computer users' eyes. Explain three such measures that the company could put in place.

Answer

Ergonomic Workstation Setup: Implementing ergonomic principles in workstation design is crucial. This includes providing adjustable chairs, monitor stands to position screens at eye level, and ensuring proper keyboard and mouse placement. Ergonomic setups reduce physical strain and contribute to better posture, minimizing eye strain and fatigue.

Regular Breaks and the 20-20-20 Rule: Encourage employees to take frequent short breaks from screen work. The 20-20-20 rule is a practical guideline: every 20 minutes, users should look at something 20 feet away for 20 seconds. This exercise helps relax eye muscles and reduces eye strain caused by prolonged focus on screens.

Optimize Display Settings: Adjusting display settings can significantly reduce eye strain. This includes reducing screen brightness to match ambient light levels, increasing text size for easier readability, and using blue light filters or software to minimize blue light emission, which is known to cause eye fatigue and disrupt sleep patterns.

3. (a) (2 marks)

Outline two characteristics of the second generation computers.

Answer

Transistor Technology: Second-generation computers used transistors instead of vacuum tubes. Transistors were smaller, more reliable, consumed less power, and generated less heat, leading to computers that were smaller, faster, and more energy-efficient than their predecessors.

Programming Languages: The development of higher-level programming languages like FORTRAN and COBOL marked the second generation. These languages made programming more accessible and efficient compared to machine code or assembly language, significantly improving software development productivity.

4. (a) (i) (2 marks)

Explain the term data bus as used in Information Communication Technology.

Answer

A data bus is a system within a computer or computing device that transmits data between components. It is a set of parallel wires that facilitate the transfer of digital information, enabling communication between the CPU, memory, and peripheral devices. The data bus width determines the amount of data that can be transferred simultaneously.

4. (a) (ii) (4 marks)

With the aid of an example, describe the term procrastination as used in time management.

Answer

Procrastination in time management refers to the act of delaying or postponing tasks that should be done, often in favor of less important or more pleasurable activities. It is a common behavior that can significantly hinder productivity and lead to missed deadlines.

Example: Consider a computer programmer who is assigned to complete a coding project by the end of the week. Instead of starting the project immediately and breaking it down into manageable tasks, the programmer spends the first few days checking social media, playing games, and attending to non-urgent emails. As the deadline approaches, the programmer faces increased pressure and stress to complete the project in a rush, likely resulting in lower quality work and potential errors due to the last-minute effort. This scenario exemplifies procrastination, where delaying important work leads to negative consequences on time management and project outcomes.

4. (c) (4 marks)

Distinguish between super computer and main frame computer as used in Information Communication Technology.

Answer

Supercomputer:

Supercomputers are the most powerful type of computers, designed for extremely high-speed processing and complex calculations. They are characterized by their ability to perform trillions of operations per second and are used for computationally intensive tasks such as scientific research, weather forecasting, and complex simulations. Supercomputers are built for maximum processing power and speed.

Mainframe Computer:

Mainframe computers are large, powerful computers designed for processing vast amounts of data and handling high volumes of transactions. They are known for their reliability, security, and scalability, making them suitable for large organizations requiring robust systems for data processing, financial transactions, and managing critical applications. Mainframes prioritize reliability, data handling capacity, and input/output operations.

Key Differences: Supercomputers are optimized for speed and computational power for complex calculations, while mainframe computers are optimized for reliability and handling massive data and transactions. Supercomputers are used for scientific and research purposes, whereas mainframes are used for business data processing in large organizations.

5. (a) (i) (2 marks)

Explain the contribution of each of the following persons to the development of computer technology: Charles Babbage;

Answer

Charles Babbage is credited with conceptualizing the first general-purpose computer, the Analytical Engine, in the 19th century. Although never fully built in his lifetime, his designs included key components of modern computers such as an arithmetic logic unit, control flow, and memory. Babbage's work laid the theoretical foundation for digital computers.

5. (a) (ii) (2 marks)

Explain the contribution of each of the following persons to the development of computer technology: Blaise Pascal.

Answer

Blaise Pascal invented the Pascaline in the 17th century, one of the earliest mechanical calculators. The Pascaline could perform addition and subtraction directly and multiplication and division through repeated addition or subtraction. Pascal's invention demonstrated the feasibility of mechanical computation and was a significant step in the history of computing devices.

MANs are larger than Local Area Networks (LANs) but smaller than Wide Area Networks (WANs). They are typically used to connect multiple LANs within a city or metropolitan region, enabling communication and resource sharing across geographically dispersed locations within the same urban area.

6. (b) (4 marks)

Differentiate between open architecture and closed architecture as used in computers.

Answer

Open Architecture:

Open architecture in computers refers to a system design that allows for easy expansion and modification by users. It typically involves standardized components and interfaces, enabling users to add or replace hardware components, such as expansion cards, memory modules, and storage devices, without significant restrictions. Open architecture promotes flexibility, customization, and interoperability.

Closed Architecture:

Closed architecture, conversely, is a system design that limits user modification and expansion. Typically, in closed architecture systems, hardware and software are tightly integrated and proprietary. Upgrades or modifications are restricted to components specifically designed and approved by the manufacturer. Closed architecture prioritizes system integration and control by the vendor but limits user flexibility and customization.

Key Differences: Open architecture is characterized by flexibility and user expandability using standardized components, while closed architecture is characterized by limited expandability and proprietary components controlled by the manufacturer. Open architecture favors customization and interoperability, whereas closed architecture emphasizes vendor control and system integration.

6. (c) (6 marks)

Joy prefers a laser printer to a dot matrix printer for her personal use. Explain three reasons that may have influenced to her preference.

Answer

Superior Print Quality: Laser printers offer significantly higher print quality compared to dot matrix printers. Laser printers produce sharp, clear, and professional-looking text and graphics with high resolution. This superior quality is essential for personal documents that require a polished appearance, such as reports, presentations, or photographs, making laser printers preferable for users who value print clarity and detail.

Faster Printing Speed: Laser printers are considerably faster than dot matrix printers, especially for text and graphics-intensive documents. Laser printers can print multiple pages per minute, making them efficient for handling larger print jobs quickly. For personal use, especially in a home office or for frequent printing tasks, the faster speed of laser printers saves time and improves productivity.

Quieter Operation: Laser printers operate much more quietly than dot matrix printers. Dot matrix printers use impact printing, which involves striking a ribbon against the paper, resulting in a noisy operation. Laser printers use a non-impact printing process, making them significantly quieter and more suitable for personal use environments where noise levels are a concern, such as home offices or shared living spaces.

Preventive conflict management techniques are proactive measures taken to minimize the likelihood of conflicts arising in the first place. In ICT, this includes establishing clear communication channels, defining roles and responsibilities, setting project expectations, and fostering a collaborative team environment. The focus is on creating a work environment that reduces potential triggers for conflict.

Key Differences: Preventive techniques are proactive and aim to avoid conflicts, while reactive techniques are employed after conflicts occur to resolve them. Prevention focuses on creating a conflict-resistant environment, whereas reaction focuses on addressing and resolving existing conflicts.

8. (b) (4 marks)

Distinguish between silent and automatic software installation methods as used in Information Communication Technology.

Answer

Silent Software Installation:

Silent software installation is a method of installing software without displaying any prompts or user interfaces to the user during the installation process. It typically runs in the background, requiring no user interaction. Silent installations are often used by system administrators for deploying software across multiple computers efficiently. The installation process is hidden from the end-user.

Automatic Software Installation:

Automatic software installation refers to software installations that proceed with minimal user intervention but may still display some prompts or interfaces. It automates many steps of the installation process but may require initial user authorization or confirmation. Automatic installations aim to simplify the process for end-users while still providing some level of visibility or control.

Key Differences: Silent installation is completely hidden from the user with no UI, while automatic installation automates the process but may still have some UI prompts. Silent is used for background deployment, and automatic aims for simplified user-initiated installation.

Navigating social interactions and maintaining healthy relationships are important but often stressful aspects of student life.

Financial Concerns and Future Uncertainty: Financial worries, such as tuition fees, living expenses, and student loans, are common stressors. Additionally, uncertainty about future career prospects after graduation can create anxiety and stress. Concerns about job market competitiveness and making successful transitions after college contribute to student stress related to their future and financial stability.
