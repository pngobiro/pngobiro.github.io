November 2018

INTRODUCTION TO INFORMATION COMMUNICATION TECHNOLOGY AND ETHICS

Exam Code: 2920/101

Duration: 3 hours

Period: November 2018

1 (a) (i) (2 marks)

Explain the term "byte" as used in computers.

Answer

A byte is a unit of digital information that consists of 8 bits. It is commonly used to represent a single character, such as a letter, number, or symbol, in computer systems.

1 (a) (ii) (2 marks)

Explain the term "nibble" as used in computers.

Answer

A nibble is a unit of digital information that consists of 4 bits. It is half of a byte and is often used in computing to represent a single hexadecimal digit.

1 (b) (4 marks)

JK Company Ltd. intends to hire a consultant to address work conflicts among its staff. Assume that you are hired as a consultant, outline four strategies that you could recommend to address the conflict.

Answer

Implement conflict resolution training programs to equip employees with skills to manage and resolve disputes effectively.

Establish clear communication channels to ensure that employees can express their concerns and grievances openly.

Introduce a mediation process where a neutral third party can help resolve conflicts between employees.

Develop and enforce a company policy that outlines acceptable behavior and procedures for handling conflicts.

1 (c) (i) (2 marks)

State four file formats supported by flatbed scanners.

Answer

JPEG (Joint Photographic Experts Group)

PDF (Portable Document Format)

PNG (Portable Network Graphics)

TIFF (Tagged Image File Format)

1 (c) (ii) (4 marks)

Distinguish between self-esteem and self-motivation as applied in life skills.

Answer

Self-esteem: Refers to an individual's overall sense of self-worth or personal value. It is how much a person appreciates and likes themselves, regardless of the circumstances.

Self-motivation: Refers to the ability to initiate and persist in pursuing goals without external influence. It involves setting personal goals and taking action to achieve them, driven by internal desires and ambitions.

1 (d) (i) (2 marks)

Define the term "tender" as used in procurement of IT hardware and software.

Answer

A tender is a formal, structured process where organizations invite suppliers to submit bids for the supply of goods or services, such as IT hardware and software. It is a competitive process aimed at obtaining the best value for money.

1 (d) (ii) (4 marks)

Spamming is one of the unethical ICT behaviours in organizations. Explain two strategies that could be used to manage it.

Answer

Implement email filtering software to automatically detect and block spam emails before they reach users' inboxes.

Educate employees on how to recognize and report spam emails, and enforce strict policies against the use of company email for spamming activities.

2 (a) (4 marks)

State four factors that should be considered when selecting the main memory for a computer.

Answer

Capacity: The amount of data the memory can store, typically measured in gigabytes (GB).

Speed: The rate at which data can be read from or written to the memory, measured in MHz or GHz.

Compatibility: Ensure the memory is compatible with the computer's motherboard and processor.

Type: The specific type of memory required, such as DDR4 or DDR5, depending on the system's specifications.

2 (b) (i) (4 marks)

DEF Company Ltd. intends to use data encryption to safeguard its data during transmission. Describe two encryption techniques it could use.

Answer

Symmetric Encryption: This technique uses a single key for both encryption and decryption. It is fast and efficient but requires secure key management to prevent unauthorized access.

Asymmetric Encryption: This technique uses a pair of keys, a public key for encryption and a private key for decryption. It is more secure than symmetric encryption but is slower due to the complexity of the key management process.

2 (b) (ii) (4 marks)

Polly was required to replace the toner of a printer for her boss. Outline four precautions that she should ensure in order to complete the task.

Answer

Ensure the printer is turned off and unplugged before starting the replacement process to avoid electrical hazards.

Wear gloves to prevent toner from coming into contact with skin, as it can be difficult to wash off and may cause irritation.

Follow the manufacturer's instructions carefully to avoid damaging the printer or the new toner cartridge.

Dispose of the old toner cartridge properly, following environmental regulations for hazardous waste.

2 (c) (i) (4 marks)

Token ring topology is widely used in corporate computer networks. Explain two of its advantages that could be influencing this trend.

Answer

Efficient Data Transmission: Token ring topology ensures that data is transmitted in an orderly manner, reducing the chances of data collisions and improving network efficiency.

Equal Access: Each device in the network has an equal opportunity to transmit data, as the token is passed sequentially, ensuring fair access to network resources.

2 (c) (ii) (4 marks)

Explain two circumstances under which an organization would install a hybrid network topology.

Answer

Scalability: A hybrid topology allows organizations to scale their network by combining different topologies, such as star and ring, to accommodate growth and changing needs.

Fault Tolerance: A hybrid topology can provide redundancy and fault tolerance by combining the strengths of different topologies, ensuring that the network remains operational even if one part fails.

3 (a) (i) (3 marks)

Outline three factors that should be considered when selecting a power supply, other than cost.

Answer

Wattage: Ensure the power supply provides sufficient wattage to support all components in the system.

Efficiency: Look for a power supply with a high efficiency rating (e.g., 80 Plus certification) to reduce energy consumption and heat output.

Connectors: Verify that the power supply has the necessary connectors for all components, such as the motherboard, GPU, and storage devices.

3 (a) (ii) (4 marks)

Distinguish between volatile and non-volatile memory.

Answer

Volatile Memory: This type of memory requires power to maintain stored data. When the power is turned off, the data is lost. Examples include RAM (Random Access Memory).

Non-Volatile Memory: This type of memory retains data even when the power is turned off. Examples include ROM (Read-Only Memory), flash memory, and hard drives.

3 (b) (6 marks)

Explain three negative impacts of social engineering in society.

Answer

Identity Theft: Social engineering can lead to the unauthorized access of personal information, resulting in identity theft and financial loss for individuals.

Data Breaches: Organizations may suffer data breaches due to social engineering attacks, leading to the exposure of sensitive information and loss of customer trust.

Financial Fraud: Social engineering can be used to manipulate individuals into transferring money or providing access to financial accounts, resulting in significant financial losses.

3 (c) (3 marks)

Outline three circumstances under which computers would be disposed.

Answer

Obsolete Technology: When the computer's hardware or software is no longer supported or compatible with current technology.

Irreparable Damage: When the computer has suffered physical or technical damage that is too costly or impossible to repair.

Upgrade: When the organization or individual upgrades to newer, more efficient systems, rendering the old computers unnecessary.

3 (d) (4 marks)

Operating systems come with several disk utility software. Outline four examples of such software.

Answer

Disk Defragmenter: Rearranges fragmented data on a disk to improve performance.

Disk Cleanup: Removes unnecessary files to free up disk space.

Disk Check: Scans and repairs file system errors on a disk.

Partition Manager: Allows users to create, resize, or delete disk partitions.

4 (a) (i) (2 marks)

Explain the term "optical" as used in disk storage technologies.

Answer

Optical storage technology uses lasers to read and write data on optical discs, such as CDs, DVDs, and Blu-ray discs. The data is stored in the form of pits and lands on the disc's surface, which are interpreted by the laser.

4 (a) (ii) (2 marks)

Explain the term "magnetic" as used in disk storage technologies.

Answer

Magnetic storage technology uses magnetic fields to store data on disks, such as hard drives. Data is represented by the orientation of magnetic particles on the disk's surface, which can be read and written by a magnetic head.

4 (b) (4 marks)

Differentiate between a system-user and a super-user.

Answer

System-User: A system-user is a regular user with limited permissions, typically restricted to accessing and modifying only their own files and applications. They cannot make system-wide changes or access sensitive system files.

Super-User: A super-user, often referred to as an administrator or root user, has full access to the system. They can modify system settings, install software, and access all files, including those of other users.

4 (c) (4 marks)

Computers use ports to meet input and output needs. Describe two types of these ports that could be found in computers.

Answer

USB Port: A Universal Serial Bus (USB) port is used to connect peripheral devices such as keyboards, mice, printers, and external storage devices. It supports data transfer and power supply to connected devices.

HDMI Port: A High-Definition Multimedia Interface (HDMI) port is used to transmit audio and video signals from a computer to a display device, such as a monitor or television.

4 (d) (i) (4 marks)

Anna was experiencing change management issues when rolling out a social responsibility program in her company. Explain two strategies that she could use to mitigate this challenge.

Answer

Stakeholder Engagement: Involve key stakeholders early in the planning process to gain their support and address any concerns they may have about the change.

Clear Communication: Provide clear and consistent communication about the goals, benefits, and steps of the social responsibility program to ensure all employees understand and are aligned with the change.

4 (d) (ii) (4 marks)

Off-the-shelf software is preferred for small/medium-sized businesses. Explain two reasons that could inform such a decision.

Answer

Cost-Effective: Off-the-shelf software is generally less expensive than custom-developed software, making it more accessible for small and medium-sized businesses with limited budgets.

Quick Deployment: Off-the-shelf software is ready to use immediately after purchase, reducing the time and resources needed for development and implementation.

5 (a) (3 marks)

Outline three possible causes of power failure in computers.

Answer

Power Surges: Sudden increases in voltage can damage the power supply unit (PSU) or other components, leading to power failure.

Overheating: Excessive heat can cause the computer's power supply or other components to fail, resulting in a shutdown.

Faulty Power Supply: A defective or aging power supply unit may fail to provide consistent power to the computer, causing it to shut down unexpectedly.

5 (b) (6 marks)

Explain three positive aspects of computer hacking in society.

Answer

Security Improvements: Ethical hacking helps organizations identify vulnerabilities in their systems, leading to improved security measures and protection against malicious attacks.

Innovation: Hacking can lead to the development of new technologies and solutions, as hackers often push the boundaries of what is possible with existing systems.

Awareness: High-profile hacking incidents raise public awareness about the importance of cybersecurity, encouraging individuals and organizations to take proactive steps to protect their data.

5 (c) (i) (2 marks)

Outline two qualities expected of a leader in a computer-based organization.

Answer

Technical Expertise: A leader should have a strong understanding of the technology and systems used within the organization to make informed decisions.

Communication Skills: Effective communication is essential for a leader to convey goals, provide guidance, and foster collaboration among team members.

5 (c) (ii) (3 marks)

Charles intends to buy a computer monitor. Outline three types of monitors that he could select.

Answer

LCD (Liquid Crystal Display): Known for its thin profile and energy efficiency, LCD monitors are widely used for general computing tasks.

LED (Light Emitting Diode): LED monitors offer better brightness and color accuracy compared to LCDs, making them suitable for multimedia and gaming.

OLED (Organic Light Emitting Diode): OLED monitors provide superior contrast and color reproduction, making them ideal for high-end graphics and video editing.

5 (d) (6 marks)

Operating systems perform critical functions in a computer system. Explain three such functions.

Answer

Process Management: The operating system manages the execution of processes, ensuring that each process gets the necessary resources and that processes do not interfere with each other.

Memory Management: The operating system allocates and deallocates memory space for processes, ensuring efficient use of the computer's RAM.

File Management: The operating system manages files on the computer, including creating, deleting, and organizing files, as well as controlling access to them.

6 (a) (4 marks)

Explain two categories of software licensing options.

Answer

Proprietary Software: This type of software is owned by an individual or company, and users must purchase a license to use it. The source code is not available, and users are restricted from modifying or redistributing the software.

Open-Source Software: This type of software is distributed with its source code, allowing users to modify and redistribute it freely. Open-source licenses, such as the GNU General Public License (GPL), often require that any modifications also be made available under the same license.

6 (b) (i) (1 mark)

Identify the appropriate type of network based on geographical coverage: connect an infinite number of networks.

Answer

The appropriate type of network is the Internet.

6 (b) (ii) (1 mark)

Identify the appropriate type of network based on geographical coverage: connect computers within a building.

Answer

The appropriate type of network is a Local Area Network (LAN).

6 (b) (iii) (1 mark)

Identify the appropriate type of network based on geographical coverage: connect different offices of a county government in a town.

Answer

The appropriate type of network is a Metropolitan Area Network (MAN).

6 (b) (iv) (1 mark)

Identify the appropriate type of network based on geographical coverage: connect upcountry branches.

Answer

The appropriate type of network is a Wide Area Network (WAN).

6 (c) (4 marks)

Passwords are synonymous with computer systems. Outline four guidelines that should be adhered to by computer users to benefit from this security feature.

Answer

Use Strong Passwords: Create passwords that are at least 12 characters long and include a mix of uppercase and lowercase letters, numbers, and special characters.

Avoid Common Words: Do not use easily guessable words or phrases, such as "password" or "123456," as they are vulnerable to brute-force attacks.

Change Passwords Regularly: Update passwords periodically to reduce the risk of unauthorized access, especially after a security breach.

Do Not Share Passwords: Keep passwords confidential and avoid sharing them with others, even if they are trusted individuals.

6 (d) (i) (4 marks)

With the aid of an example in each case, describe two types of printers that could be used in business.

Answer

Laser Printer: A laser printer uses a laser beam to produce high-quality text and graphics on paper. It is commonly used in offices for printing documents and reports. Example: HP LaserJet Pro.

Inkjet Printer: An inkjet printer sprays tiny droplets of ink onto paper to create images and text. It is often used for printing high-quality photos and color documents. Example: Canon PIXMA.

6 (d) (ii) (4 marks)

Sam was nominated to chair an ICT meeting in his company. Outline four life skills that he could apply to realize the success of the meeting.

Answer

Effective Communication: Clearly articulate the meeting's objectives and ensure all participants understand the agenda and their roles.

Time Management: Keep the meeting on track by adhering to the scheduled time and ensuring that discussions remain focused on the agenda.

Conflict Resolution: Address any disagreements or conflicts that arise during the meeting in a calm and constructive manner to maintain a positive atmosphere.

Decision-Making: Facilitate the decision-making process by gathering input from all participants and guiding the group toward a consensus.

7 (a) (4 marks)

Differentiate between Control Unit (CU) and Arithmetic & Logic Unit (ALU) as used in computers.

Answer

Control Unit (CU): The CU is responsible for directing the operations of the computer's processor. It fetches instructions from memory, decodes them, and coordinates the execution of these instructions by sending control signals to other parts of the CPU.

Arithmetic & Logic Unit (ALU): The ALU performs arithmetic operations (such as addition and subtraction) and logical operations (such as AND, OR, and NOT) on data. It is the part of the CPU that carries out the actual computation.

For the following function, identify the appropriate category of keyboard keys that is responsible: enter texts and numbers in a text editor program.

Answer

The appropriate category of keyboard keys is the Alphanumeric Keys.

7 (b) (ii) (1 mark)

For the following function, identify the appropriate category of keyboard keys that is responsible: modify the normal action of another key.

Answer

The appropriate category of keyboard keys is the Modifier Keys.

7 (b) (iii) (1 mark)

For the following function, identify the appropriate category of keyboard keys that is responsible: move the cursor to different positions on the screen.

Answer

The appropriate category of keyboard keys is the Navigation Keys.

7 (b) (iv) (1 mark)

For the following function, identify the appropriate category of keyboard keys that is responsible: act as a shortcut to perform a specific task in a computer.

Answer

The appropriate category of keyboard keys is the Function Keys.

7 (c) (6 marks)

Most governments are striving to implement e-government to ease doing business. Explain three hindrances to this noble activity in sub-Saharan Africa.

Answer

Limited Infrastructure: Many sub-Saharan African countries lack the necessary technological infrastructure, such as reliable internet connectivity and electricity, to support e-government initiatives.

Digital Literacy: A significant portion of the population lacks the digital skills required to effectively use e-government services, limiting their adoption and effectiveness.

Cybersecurity Concerns: The risk of cyberattacks and data breaches poses a significant challenge to the implementation of e-government, as governments must ensure the security and privacy of citizens' data.

7 (d) (6 marks)

Morris intends to build a computer network for his company. Explain three possible benefits that are likely to be realized by the staff.

Answer

Improved Communication: A computer network enables seamless communication among staff through email, instant messaging, and shared documents, enhancing collaboration and productivity.

Resource Sharing: Staff can share resources such as printers, scanners, and storage devices, reducing costs and improving efficiency.

Centralized Data Management: A network allows for centralized storage and management of data, making it easier for staff to access and update information, leading to better decision-making and workflow.

List four examples of pointing devices used in computers.

Mouse

8 (a) (ii) (4 marks)

Differentiate between competing and withdrawal conflict management techniques.

Answer

Competing: This technique involves asserting one's own needs and desires over those of others, often leading to a win-lose outcome. It is used when quick, decisive action is needed, but it can damage relationships if overused.

Withdrawal: This technique involves avoiding or withdrawing from the conflict altogether. It is used when the issue is trivial or when more time is needed to cool down, but it can lead to unresolved issues if not addressed later.

8 (b) (4 marks)

Outline four attributes that could be used to attest to the safeness of software.

Answer

Regular Updates: The software should receive regular updates to patch vulnerabilities and improve security.

Encryption: The software should use encryption to protect sensitive data from unauthorized access.

User Authentication: The software should require strong user authentication, such as passwords or biometric verification, to ensure only authorized users can access it.

Compliance with Standards: The software should comply with industry security standards, such as ISO/IEC 27001, to ensure it meets recognized security benchmarks.

8 (c) (4 marks)

Ahead Company Ltd. used pirated software in its computer systems. Explain two negative impacts that could be experienced by the company while using the software.

Answer

Legal Consequences: The company could face legal action, fines, or penalties for using pirated software, as it violates copyright laws and software licensing agreements.

Security Risks: Pirated software often lacks official updates and patches, making it more vulnerable to malware, viruses, and other security threats, which could compromise the company's data and systems.

8 (d) (i) (3 marks)

Agnes was required to propose computer laboratory rules for her company. Outline three example rules that she could have proposed.

Answer

No Food or Drinks: Prohibit eating and drinking in the computer lab to prevent spills and damage to equipment.

Authorized Access Only: Restrict access to the lab to authorized personnel only, ensuring that only trained individuals use the equipment.

Regular Maintenance: Implement a schedule for regular maintenance and cleaning of the lab equipment to ensure optimal performance and longevity.

8 (d) (ii) (3 marks)

Software as a Service (SaaS) is slowly getting adopted by small business enterprises. Outline three factors that could be affecting its fast adoption.

Answer

Cost Concerns: Small businesses may be hesitant to adopt SaaS due to the recurring subscription costs, which can be higher than the one-time cost of traditional software.

Data Security: Concerns about the security and privacy of data stored in the cloud may deter small businesses from adopting SaaS, especially if they handle sensitive information.

Internet Dependency: SaaS requires a reliable internet connection, which may not be available in all areas, limiting its adoption in regions with poor connectivity.
