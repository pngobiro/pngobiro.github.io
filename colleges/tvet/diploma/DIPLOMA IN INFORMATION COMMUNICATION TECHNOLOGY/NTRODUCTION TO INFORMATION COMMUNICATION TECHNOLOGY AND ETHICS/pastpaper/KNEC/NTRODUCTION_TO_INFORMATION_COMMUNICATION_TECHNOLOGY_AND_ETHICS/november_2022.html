Question 1

(a) Outline four uses of computers in the mining industry. (4 marks)

Sample Answer:

  1. Geological Data Analysis: Analyzing geological data to identify potential mining sites.
  2. Mine Planning and Design: Simulating and designing mine layouts for optimal extraction.
  3. Equipment Monitoring and Control: Monitoring the performance and controlling heavy machinery used in mining.
  4. Safety and Environmental Monitoring: Tracking environmental conditions and ensuring safety protocols.
(b) Explain three characteristics of mini computers. (6 marks)

Sample Answer:

  1. Mid-Range Processing Power: Mini computers offer more processing power than microcomputers but less than mainframes, suitable for small to medium-sized organizations.
  2. Multi-User Support: They can support multiple users simultaneously, often used as servers in a network.
  3. Larger Size and Cost: Physically larger and more expensive than microcomputers, requiring a dedicated space.
(c) Elimu Institute intends to acquire computers by leasing. Explain three challenges the institute is likely to encounter. (6 marks)

Sample Answer:

  1. Higher Long-Term Cost: Leasing may be more expensive in the long run compared to purchasing.
  2. Dependence on Lessor: Reliance on the leasing company for maintenance and support, which may cause delays.
  3. Limited Customization: Restrictions on modifying or upgrading the leased computers.
(d) With the aid of a diagram, describe mesh computer network topology. (4 marks)

Sample Answer:

In a mesh topology, each device is connected to every other device in the network. This creates redundant paths for data transmission. If one link fails, data can be rerouted through other connections.

Question 2

(a) Outline four advantages of a wireless computer mouse. (4 marks)

Sample Answer:

  1. Increased Mobility: Freedom to move the mouse without being restricted by a cable.
  2. Reduced Clutter: No cable to tangle or clutter the workspace.
  3. Ergonomics: Can be used in a more comfortable position, reducing strain.
  4. Convenience: Easy to connect and use with various devices.
(b) A student has been experiencing stress due to low academic grades. Explain three ways the student could manage this stress. (6 marks)

Sample Answer:

  1. Time Management: Create a study schedule and prioritize tasks to improve academic performance.
  2. Seek Help: Talk to a teacher, counselor, or tutor for academic support and guidance.
  3. Practice Relaxation Techniques: Engage in activities like exercise, meditation, or deep breathing to reduce stress levels.
(c) Differentiate between copyright and patent as used in information communication technology legislation. (4 marks)

Sample Answer:

Copyright: Protects the expression of an idea (e.g., software code, website content). It prevents unauthorized copying, distribution, and modification.

Patent: Protects inventions (e.g., a new algorithm, a hardware design). It grants the inventor exclusive rights to use, sell, and manufacture the invention for a certain period.

(d) Ziptech Company disposed their old computers in an open field. Explain three hazards this disposal could have on the environment. (6 marks)

Sample Answer:

  1. Soil Contamination: Heavy metals (lead, mercury) and other toxic substances from the computers can leach into the soil.
  2. Water Pollution: Rainwater can carry these contaminants into nearby water sources.
  3. Air Pollution: Burning of e-waste releases harmful chemicals into the air.

Question 3

(a) Outline four causes of conflicts among employees in an organisation. (4 marks)

Sample Answer:

  1. Communication Breakdown: Lack of clear communication or misinterpretations.
  2. Personality Clashes: Differences in personalities and work styles.
  3. Resource Competition: Competition for limited resources (budget, equipment, promotions).
  4. Unclear Roles and Responsibilities: Ambiguity about job duties and responsibilities.
(b) A computer technician preferred to use a coaxial cable in a computer network. Explain three reasons for this preference. (6 marks)

Sample Answer:

  1. High Bandwidth: Coaxial cables can transmit large amounts of data at high speeds.
  2. Resistance to Interference: They are shielded, making them less susceptible to electromagnetic interference.
  3. Durability: Coaxial cables are relatively robust and can withstand some physical stress.
(c) Anita an ICT student has been experiencing low self-esteem. Explain three possible causes that could have led to this. (6 marks)

Sample Answer:

  1. Academic Difficulties: Struggling with coursework or exams can lead to feelings of inadequacy.
  2. Social Isolation: Lack of social connections or feeling excluded from peer groups.
  3. Cyberbullying: Experiencing harassment or bullying online.

Question 4

(a) Outline four factors a company would consider before engaging in corporate social responsibility. (4 marks)

Sample Answer:

  1. Cost Implications: The financial resources required to implement and sustain CSR initiatives.
  2. Stakeholder Expectations: The expectations of customers, employees, investors, and the community regarding the company's social and environmental impact.
  3. Alignment with Business Goals: How CSR activities align with the company's overall mission and objectives.
  4. Potential Benefits: The positive outcomes of CSR, such as improved brand reputation and customer loyalty.
(b) Differentiate between collaborative and accommodative conflict management approaches. (4 marks)

Sample Answer:

Collaborative: A win-win approach where both parties work together to find a solution that satisfies everyone's needs and concerns.

Accommodative: One party prioritizes the needs and concerns of the other party, often yielding their own position to maintain harmony.

(c) Explain three circumstances that could necessitate the use of a light pen as a computer input device. (6 marks)

Sample Answer:

  1. Graphic Design and CAD: Precise drawing and editing on screen, especially in applications like Computer-Aided Design.
  2. Interactive Presentations: Directly highlighting or annotating content on a projected screen during presentations.
  3. Form Filling on Touch Screens: Selecting options or writing directly on touch-sensitive screens for data entry.
(d) Goldy Company hired a system designer. Explain three roles that this computer personnel would play in the company (6 marks)

Sample Answer:

  1. Analyzing User Requirements: Understanding the needs of users and translating them into technical specifications for new systems.
  2. Designing System Architecture: Creating the overall structure and components of the system, including hardware and software.
  3. Developing Prototypes: Building preliminary versions of the system to test and refine the design.

Question 5

(a) Outline four disadvantages of a magnetic tape as a computer storage device. (4 marks)

Sample Answer:

  1. Slow Access Time: Data is accessed sequentially, making it slow to retrieve specific files.
  2. Data Degradation: Tapes can degrade over time, leading to data loss.
  3. Sensitivity to Environment: Tapes are sensitive to temperature, humidity, and magnetic fields.
  4. Specialized Hardware: Requires a tape drive, which is not a standard component in modern computers.
(b) Describe three features of second generation computers. (6 marks)

Sample Answer:

  1. Use of Transistors: Replaced vacuum tubes, making computers smaller, faster, and more reliable.
  2. Magnetic Core Memory: Used magnetic cores for main memory, improving speed and capacity.
  3. High-Level Programming Languages: Development of languages like FORTRAN and COBOL made programming easier.
(c) Paul has been selling pirated software. Explain three reasons that could have led to this unethical behaviour. (6 marks)

Sample Answer:

  1. Financial Gain: Selling pirated software can be profitable due to low production costs.
  2. Lack of Awareness: Ignorance of copyright laws and the consequences of software piracy.
  3. Accessibility: Easy access to pirated software through the internet or informal markets.
(d) Differentiate between defragmenter and compressor utilities software. (4 marks)

Sample Answer:

Defragmenter: Reorganizes fragmented files on a hard drive to improve access speed and overall system performance.

Compressor: Reduces the size of files to save storage space and facilitate faster transfer over networks.

Question 6

(a) Outline four circumstances that could lead to the use of outsourcing method for software acquisition. (4 marks)

Sample Answer:

  1. Lack of In-House Expertise: When the organization lacks the necessary skills or resources to develop the software internally.
  2. Cost Reduction: Outsourcing to a company in a region with lower labor costs can be more economical.
  3. Focus on Core Competencies: Allows the organization to focus on its primary business activities.
  4. Faster Development Time: Outsourcing can speed up the development process due to the availability of specialized resources.
(b) Describe three characteristics of optical storage devices. (6 marks)

Sample Answer:

  1. Data Storage Using Lasers: Data is written and read using laser technology.
  2. Portability: Optical discs (CDs, DVDs, Blu-ray) are portable and easy to transport.
  3. Relatively Low Cost Per GB: Optical media is generally inexpensive compared to other storage options.
(c) Joyce, a programme uses an ergonomic seat in her office. Explain three benefits of using this seat. (6 marks)

Sample Answer:

  1. Improved Posture: Supports the natural curvature of the spine, reducing back pain.
  2. Increased Comfort: Provides better cushioning and support, reducing discomfort during long work hours.
  3. Reduced Risk of Musculoskeletal Disorders: Helps prevent conditions like carpal tunnel syndrome and back injuries.
(d) Differentiate between install and configure as used in computer software. (4 marks)

Sample Answer:

Install: The process of placing the software files onto the computer's storage system (e.g., hard drive) so it can be run.

Configure: The process of customizing the software settings after installation to meet specific user preferences or system requirements.

Question 7

(a) Outline four negative impacts of information communication technology to a college student. (4 marks)

Sample Answer:

  1. Distraction: Social media and online entertainment can divert attention from studies.
  2. Cyberbullying: Online harassment and bullying can negatively impact mental health.
  3. Information Overload: Difficulty in managing and processing the vast amount of information available online.
  4. Reduced Physical Activity: Excessive use of technology can lead to a sedentary lifestyle.
(b) Differentiate between of-the-shelf and bespoke software. (4 marks)

Sample Answer:

Off-the-shelf: Pre-built software designed for a general audience with standard features and functionalities.

Bespoke: Custom-made software developed specifically for a particular organization or user, tailored to their unique requirements.

(c) Freetech Company has implemented user authentication measures for data security. Explain three challenges the company is likely to encounter. (6 marks)

Sample Answer:

  1. User Resistance: Employees may find the authentication process inconvenient or time-consuming.
  2. System Complexity: Managing and maintaining complex authentication systems can be challenging.
  3. Cost of Implementation: Implementing and maintaining strong authentication measures can be expensive.
(d) Helen, an ICT student possesses good interpersonal skills. Explain three such skills. (6 marks)

Sample Answer:

  1. Communication: Effectively conveying information and ideas, both verbally and non-verbally.
  2. Active Listening: Paying attention to and understanding what others are saying.
  3. Empathy: Understanding and sharing the feelings of others.

Question 8

(a) Explain three advantages of a non-impact printer. (6 marks)

Sample Answer:

  1. Quiet Operation: Non-impact printers (like inkjet and laser printers) are much quieter than impact printers.
  2. High Print Quality: They produce higher-quality images and text, especially for graphics.
  3. Faster Print Speed: Generally faster than impact printers, particularly for complex documents.
(b) Differentiate between intranet and extranet as used in computer networks. (4 marks)

Sample Answer:

Intranet: A private network accessible only to members of an organization (e.g., employees). It's used for internal communication and resource sharing.

Extranet: A private network that extends access to authorized external users (e.g., suppliers, customers) in addition to internal users. It allows controlled collaboration and information sharing with external parties.

(c) The following are examples of systems controls: employee rotation, password, CCTV, locks, biometric and motivation. Classify each of these examples to its respective type of control. (6 marks)

Sample Answer:

  • Employee Rotation: Preventive Control (specifically, administrative)
  • Password: Preventive Control (specifically, logical/technical)
  • CCTV: Detective Control (specifically, physical)
  • Locks: Preventive Control (specifically, physical)
  • Biometric: Preventive Control (specifically, logical/technical)
  • Motivation: Preventive Control (specifically, administrative)
(d) Gaztech Company has put in place measures to reduce unethical behaviour in its information communication technology department. Explain two such measures. (4 marks)

Sample Answer:

  1. Code of Ethics: Establishing a clear code of conduct outlining expected ethical behavior for all IT personnel.
  2. Regular Training: Providing training on ethics, security awareness, and compliance with relevant regulations.
