1. (a) (4 marks)

Outline four challenges that an organization could encounter while undertaking social responsibility in ICT.

Answer

Organizations undertaking social responsibility in ICT may face several challenges:

Cost of Implementation: Implementing socially responsible ICT initiatives can be expensive. This involves financial investments in new technologies, infrastructure upgrades, employee training, and ongoing maintenance to ensure ethical and sustainable practices are embedded within ICT operations.

Measuring Impact and ROI: Quantifying the social and environmental impact of ICT initiatives is complex. It is difficult to establish clear metrics and collect data to demonstrate the return on investment (ROI) for social responsibility efforts, making it challenging to justify these investments to stakeholders.

Stakeholder Engagement and Conflicting Interests: Engaging with diverse stakeholders, including employees, customers, communities, and regulatory bodies, is crucial for effective social responsibility. However, balancing potentially conflicting interests and expectations among these groups can be a significant challenge.

Keeping Pace with Evolving Ethical Standards and Regulations: The field of ICT ethics and social responsibility is constantly evolving due to rapid technological advancements and changing societal norms. Organizations must continuously monitor and adapt to new ethical standards, legal requirements, and best practices to maintain responsible ICT operations.

1. (b) (4 marks)

Differentiate between serial port and parallel port as used in computers.

Answer

Serial Port:

A serial port transmits data one bit at a time, sequentially, over a single wire pair. It is commonly used for devices that do not require high-speed data transfer, such as modems, mice, and older printers. Serial ports are characterized by their simplicity and ability to transmit data over longer distances reliably.

Parallel Port:

A parallel port transmits multiple bits of data simultaneously over multiple wires (typically 8 bits or a byte at a time). Historically used for printers and scanners, parallel ports offer faster data transfer rates than serial ports for short distances. However, they are more complex and prone to data corruption over longer distances due to synchronization issues between the parallel data streams.

Key Differences:

The primary difference lies in the method of data transmission: serial ports transmit data bit by bit sequentially, while parallel ports transmit data in multiple bits simultaneously. Serial ports are simpler, slower, and better for long distances, whereas parallel ports are faster for short distances but more complex and less reliable over long distances. Parallel ports are largely obsolete in modern computers, replaced by faster serial technologies like USB.

2. (a) (2 marks)

Explain the term computer ergonomics.

Answer

Computer ergonomics is the science of designing and arranging workplaces, products, and systems so that they fit the people who use them. In the context of computing, it focuses on optimizing the interaction between humans and computers to enhance user comfort, efficiency, and safety, while minimizing the risk of musculoskeletal disorders and other health problems associated with prolonged computer use. It encompasses factors like workstation setup, chair adjustments, monitor positioning, and input device design.

2. (b) (ii) (4 marks)

Explain two information security goals.

Answer

Confidentiality: Confidentiality ensures that sensitive information is accessible only to authorized individuals, entities, or processes. It is about preventing unauthorized disclosure of data. Mechanisms to achieve confidentiality include encryption, access control lists, and security clearances. Protecting customer data, trade secrets, and personal information are key aspects of confidentiality.

Integrity: Integrity guarantees the accuracy and completeness of data throughout its lifecycle. It ensures that information is not altered or corrupted in an unauthorized manner, whether during storage, transmission, or processing. Integrity is maintained through checksums, version control, access controls, and intrusion detection systems. Ensuring data reliability for decision-making and preventing fraudulent activities are primary objectives of integrity.

4. (a) (i) (4 marks)

4. (a) (i) Outline four approaches to effective time management.

Answer

Prioritization using Eisenhower Matrix: This approach involves categorizing tasks into four quadrants based on urgency and importance: Urgent/Important, Not Urgent/Important, Urgent/Not Important, and Not Urgent/Not Important. By focusing on Urgent/Important and Not Urgent/Important tasks, and minimizing time spent on Urgent/Not Important and Not Urgent/Not Important tasks, individuals can prioritize effectively and allocate time to high-value activities.

Time Blocking: Time blocking is a technique where individuals schedule specific blocks of time for particular tasks or activities in their calendar. By allocating dedicated time slots for focused work, meetings, emails, or breaks, individuals can structure their day, prevent multitasking, and improve concentration on single tasks, leading to better time utilization.

Pomodoro Technique: The Pomodoro Technique is a time management method that uses focused work intervals (typically 25 minutes) followed by short breaks (5 minutes). After every four Pomodoros, a longer break (15-20 minutes) is taken. This cycle enhances concentration, reduces mental fatigue, and promotes consistent productivity throughout the day.

Delegation: Effective delegation involves assigning tasks to others who are capable of handling them, freeing up time for higher-priority or strategic activities. By identifying tasks that can be delegated and entrusting them to competent team members, individuals can optimize their workload, reduce personal overload, and focus on responsibilities that require their unique skills and expertise.

Answer

Self-awareness:

Self-awareness is the conscious knowledge of one's own character, feelings, motives, and desires. It involves understanding your strengths, weaknesses, values, beliefs, emotions, and how you impact others. Self-awareness is about introspection and recognizing your internal state and its influence on your behavior and interactions.

Self-esteem:

Self-esteem is the subjective evaluation of one's own worth. It reflects how much you appreciate and like yourself, regardless of external circumstances. Self-esteem is about your overall sense of self-value, confidence in your abilities, and belief in your right to be happy and successful.

Key Differences:

Self-awareness is about knowing yourself, while self-esteem is about valuing yourself. You can be self-aware (understand your strengths and weaknesses) without necessarily having high self-esteem (liking yourself). Self-awareness is a prerequisite for personal growth and can contribute to building self-esteem, but they are distinct concepts. Self-awareness is more about understanding, while self-esteem is about evaluation and worth.

4. (d) (i) (4 marks)

(d) (i) Distinguish between synchronous and asynchronous data transmission.

Answer

Synchronous Data Transmission:

Synchronous data transmission is a method where data is transmitted in continuous blocks or frames, with the sender and receiver clocks synchronized. Data bits are transmitted at a constant rate, and timing signals are used to ensure that the receiver knows when to expect each bit. Start and stop bits are not required for each character, as synchronization is maintained throughout the transmission. This method is efficient for transmitting large volumes of data at high speeds.

Asynchronous Data Transmission:

Asynchronous data transmission is a method where data is transmitted character by character, with each character framed by start and stop bits. There is no continuous clock synchronization between the sender and receiver. The start bit signals the beginning of a character, and the stop bit indicates the end. This method is simpler to implement and suitable for transmitting data at irregular intervals, such as keyboard input or low-speed communications.

Key Differences:

The primary distinction lies in synchronization and data framing. Synchronous transmission relies on continuous clock synchronization and transmits data in blocks without start/stop bits per character, offering higher speed and efficiency for large data volumes. Asynchronous transmission uses start and stop bits for each character, does not require continuous synchronization, is simpler, and is better suited for intermittent data transmission at lower speeds.

5. (a) (ii) (4 marks)

(ii) Distinguish between disk scan and disk clean up as used in computers.

Answer

Disk Scan:

Disk scan, often referred to as disk error checking or scanning, is a utility that examines a computer's hard drive or storage device for file system errors and physical defects. It scans the disk's file system structure, metadata, and surface for inconsistencies, bad sectors, and other issues that can lead to data corruption, system instability, or data loss. Disk scanning tools like Windows Check Disk (chkdsk) attempt to repair file system errors and mark bad sectors to prevent data from being stored in those areas.

Disk Clean Up:

Disk clean up is a utility that helps users free up disk space by identifying and removing temporary files, system files, and other unnecessary data that accumulate over time. It scans the hard drive for files that are safe to delete, such as temporary internet files, downloaded program files, recycle bin contents, and system cache files. Disk cleanup utilities like Windows Disk Cleanup allow users to select file categories for deletion, helping to reclaim storage space and improve system performance.

Key Differences:

Disk scan is focused on detecting and repairing disk errors and physical problems to maintain data integrity and system stability, while disk cleanup is aimed at freeing up disk space by removing unnecessary files to improve system performance and storage capacity. Disk scan is for disk health and data reliability, whereas disk cleanup is for space management and performance optimization.

6. (b) (i) (4 marks)

(b) (i) Differentiate between CRT and LCD monitors as used computers.

Answer

CRT Monitors (Cathode Ray Tube):

CRT monitors are older display technology that uses a vacuum tube to project images onto a phosphor-coated screen. They use electron beams to scan across the screen and illuminate phosphors, creating visible pixels. CRT monitors are known for their deep blacks, wide viewing angles, and fast response times, but they are bulky, heavy, and consume significant power.

LCD Monitors (Liquid Crystal Display):

LCD monitors are modern flat-panel displays that use liquid crystals to modulate light and create images. They consist of a backlight, polarizing filters, and liquid crystal cells arranged in pixels. LCD monitors are lightweight, slim, energy-efficient, and produce less heat compared to CRTs. However, early LCDs had limitations in viewing angles and response times, although modern LCDs have significantly improved in these areas.

Key Differences:

The fundamental difference is in display technology: CRT uses electron beams and phosphors, while LCD uses liquid crystals and backlighting. CRT monitors are bulky, power-hungry, but offer deep blacks and fast response. LCD monitors are slim, energy-efficient, but historically had narrower viewing angles and slower response, though modern LCDs have largely overcome these limitations and are now the dominant display technology due to their advantages in size, weight, and power consumption.

6. (c) (ii) (3 marks)

(ii) Describe three types of optical disks.

Answer

Compact Disc Read-Only Memory (CD-ROM): CD-ROMs are optical disks that store data that is permanently written during manufacturing and cannot be altered or erased by the user. They typically have a storage capacity of around 700MB and are primarily used for distributing software, music, and multimedia content. CD-ROM drives use lasers to read data from the disk surface.

Digital Versatile Disc (DVD): DVDs are optical disks that offer significantly higher storage capacity compared to CDs, typically ranging from 4.7GB to 17GB. DVDs are used for storing high-definition video, large software applications, and data backups. DVD technology uses a narrower laser beam and more densely packed data pits than CDs, enabling higher data density.

Blu-ray Disc (BD): Blu-ray Discs are high-capacity optical disks designed for storing high-definition video and large amounts of data, offering storage capacities starting from 25GB for single-layer discs and up to 50GB for dual-layer discs, with even higher capacities available in newer formats. Blu-ray technology uses a blue laser (shorter wavelength than red lasers used in CDs and DVDs), allowing for even smaller data pits and tracks, thus greatly increasing storage capacity and enabling the storage of ultra-high-definition (4K) video content and massive datasets.

7. (a) (i) (4 marks)

7.. (a) (i) Citing an example in each case, distinguish between volatile and non volatile memory.

Answer

Volatile Memory:

Volatile memory is a type of computer memory that requires power to maintain the stored information. When power is turned off, the data stored in volatile memory is lost. Random Access Memory (RAM) is a primary example of volatile memory. RAM is used as the main memory in computers to store data and instructions that the CPU is actively using. For instance, when you are working on a document in a word processor, the text you are typing is stored in RAM. If there is a power outage or the computer is turned off without saving, this unsaved data in RAM will be lost.

Non-Volatile Memory:

Non-volatile memory is a type of computer memory that retains stored information even when power is removed. Data in non-volatile memory is preserved across power cycles. Examples of non-volatile memory include Hard Disk Drives (HDDs), Solid State Drives (SSDs), and Read-Only Memory (ROM). For example, the operating system, applications, and files stored on an SSD remain intact even when the computer is powered off. ROM, another non-volatile memory, stores firmware or boot instructions that are essential for starting up the computer and are retained permanently.

Key Differences:

The fundamental difference is data retention with and without power. Volatile memory loses data when power is off (e.g., RAM), while non-volatile memory retains data even without power (e.g., SSD, ROM). Volatile memory is typically faster and used for active data processing, whereas non-volatile memory is used for long-term data storage. Volatility is the key distinguishing characteristic between these memory types.

7. (a) (ii) (2 marks)

(ii) Outline four functions of a control unit in a computer system.

Answer

Instruction Fetching: The control unit fetches instructions from the main memory (RAM). It retrieves the next instruction to be executed based on the program counter, which holds the address of the next instruction in sequence. Fetching instructions is the first step in the instruction execution cycle.

Instruction Decoding: Once an instruction is fetched, the control unit decodes it to understand the operation to be performed and the operands involved. Decoding involves interpreting the instruction's opcode and operand fields to determine what actions the computer needs to take and which data to use.

Instruction Execution Control: The control unit generates control signals to direct other components of the CPU, such as the arithmetic logic unit (ALU), registers, and memory, to perform the operations specified by the decoded instruction. It manages the sequence of operations needed to execute each instruction, ensuring proper coordination between CPU components.

Data Transfer Management: The control unit manages the flow of data between different components of the computer system, including memory, registers, and input/output devices. It generates control signals to initiate data transfers, specify data sources and destinations, and synchronize data movement within the system. This function ensures that data is available to the CPU when needed and stored correctly after processing.

7. (b) (4 marks)

(b) A college with a computer laboratory consisting of twenty computers would like to employ hardware techniques to reduce computer virus infection. Outline four such techniques.

Answer

BIOS-Level Write Protection: Implement BIOS-level write protection on the computer hard drives. Some BIOS settings allow administrators to enable write protection, making the hard drive read-only during normal operation. This prevents viruses from writing to the disk and making persistent changes, reducing the risk of infection. However, this method needs careful management as it can also restrict legitimate software installations and updates.

Hardware Firewalls: Deploy a hardware firewall at the network perimeter of the computer laboratory. A hardware firewall acts as a security gateway, inspecting network traffic and blocking unauthorized access and malicious traffic before it reaches individual computers. Configuring the firewall to filter incoming and outgoing traffic based on predefined rules and protocols can significantly reduce the risk of network-borne virus infections.

Trusted Platform Module (TPM): Utilize Trusted Platform Modules (TPMs) in the computers. TPM is a hardware security module that provides hardware-based security functions, such as secure boot and cryptographic key storage. Secure boot ensures that only authorized software and operating systems are loaded during startup, preventing boot-level malware infections. TPM can also store encryption keys securely, enhancing data protection and integrity.

Network Segmentation and VLANs: Segment the computer laboratory network using Virtual LANs (VLANs). VLANs divide a physical network into multiple logical networks, isolating different groups of computers. By placing the lab computers in a separate VLAN from the college's main network, you can contain virus outbreaks within the lab network, limiting the spread of infections to other parts of the college's IT infrastructure. Network segmentation enhances security and reduces the impact of potential breaches.
