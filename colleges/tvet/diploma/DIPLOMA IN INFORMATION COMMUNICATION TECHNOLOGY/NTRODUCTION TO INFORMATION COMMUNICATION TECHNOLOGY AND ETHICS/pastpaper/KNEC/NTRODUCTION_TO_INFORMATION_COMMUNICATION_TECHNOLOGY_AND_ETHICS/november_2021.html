INTRODUCTION TO INFORMATION COMMUNICATION TECHNOLOGY AND ETHICS

Subject name/Module title: INTRODUCTION TO INFORMATION COMMUNICATION TECHNOLOGY AND ETHICS

Exam duration: 3 hours
Exam date/period: November 2021
Module number: MODULE I

INSTRUCTIONS TO CANDIDATES

This paper consists of EIGHT questions.

Answer any FIVE of the EIGHT questions in the answer booklet provided.

Candidates should answer the questions in English.

1.

(a) Outline four advantages of the outsourcing method of acquiring computer services. (4 marks)
  1. Cost Savings: Outsourcing can reduce costs related to infrastructure, personnel, and technology.
  2. Focus on Core Competencies: It allows companies to concentrate on their main business activities by delegating IT functions to specialists.
  3. Access to Expertise: Outsourcing provides access to specialized skills and technologies that may not be available in-house.
  4. Increased Efficiency: Specialized outsourcing vendors can often deliver services more efficiently due to their focus and economies of scale.
(b) Describe three benefits of flash disks as storage devices. (6 marks)
  1. Portability: Flash disks are small and lightweight, making them highly portable and easy to carry around for data transfer between computers.
  2. Durability: They are more durable than traditional hard drives as they have no moving parts, making them resistant to shocks and physical damage.
  3. Fast Data Transfer Speeds: Flash disks, especially USB 3.0 and above, offer fast read and write speeds, enabling quick transfer of large files.
(c) Distinguish between defragmenter and debugger as used in computers. (4 marks)

A defragmenter is a utility that reorganizes fragmented files on a hard drive to improve access speed and system performance. It arranges file pieces contiguously. On the other hand, a debugger is a programming tool used to test and debug software programs. It helps programmers identify and fix errors (bugs) in the code by allowing them to step through the program execution, inspect variables, and set breakpoints.

(d) Sofia, a secretary at a technical institute, has low self-esteem. Explain three ways she could deal with this. (6 marks)
  1. Seek Professional Counseling: Therapy can help Sofia understand the root causes of her low self-esteem and develop coping mechanisms and strategies to build confidence.
  2. Focus on Strengths and Achievements: Sofia should make a conscious effort to recognize her accomplishments and skills. Keeping a journal of successes, no matter how small, can boost her self-perception.
  3. Practice Self-Compassion and Positive Self-Talk: Sofia should treat herself with kindness and understanding, especially during setbacks. Replacing negative self-talk with positive affirmations can gradually improve her self-image.

2.

(a) Outline three positive effects of Information Communication Technology to a college student. (3 marks)
  1. Enhanced Learning Resources: ICT provides access to a vast amount of online educational materials, e-libraries, and digital learning platforms, expanding learning opportunities beyond traditional textbooks.
  2. Improved Communication and Collaboration: ICT tools facilitate better communication with lecturers and peers through email, online forums, and collaborative platforms, enhancing group projects and discussions.
  3. Development of Digital Literacy Skills: Regular use of ICT in education equips students with essential digital skills that are highly valuable in the modern job market and for lifelong learning.
(b) Figure 1 shows a computer input device. Use it to answer the questions that follow.

(i) Identify the device shown in the Figure 1. (1 mark)

Mouse

(ii) Explain two advantages of the device identified in (i). (4 marks)
  1. User-Friendly Interface: A mouse provides a graphical and intuitive way to interact with a computer, making it easier for users to navigate and control applications compared to command-line interfaces.
  2. Increased Efficiency: Using a mouse for tasks like selecting text, dragging and dropping files, and navigating menus is generally faster and more efficient than using keyboard shortcuts alone.
(c) Explain three possible reasons that lead to unethical behaviour in Information Communication Technology. (6 marks)
  1. Lack of Awareness and Training: Insufficient understanding of ethical principles and guidelines in ICT can lead to unintentional unethical behavior. Lack of proper training on data privacy, intellectual property, and cyber security contributes to this.
  2. Pressure to Perform and Meet Targets: In competitive environments, individuals may feel pressured to compromise ethical standards to achieve targets, such as cutting corners in software development or data handling to meet deadlines.
  3. Ease of Anonymity and Reduced Accountability: The digital environment can provide anonymity, making it easier for individuals to engage in unethical activities without fear of immediate consequences or detection. This reduced accountability can encourage unethical actions.
(d) A programmer is developing a computer program for an organisation using a high-level language. Describe three characteristics of such languages. (6 marks)
  1. Readability and Understandability: High-level languages are designed to be closer to human language, making them easier to read, write, and understand compared to low-level languages. This improves programmer productivity.
  2. Machine Independence: Programs written in high-level languages are generally portable across different computer architectures. They are not tied to a specific hardware, as they need to be compiled or interpreted into machine code for each specific system.
  3. Abstraction: High-level languages provide a higher level of abstraction, hiding the complex details of computer hardware and machine code. This allows programmers to focus on the problem-solving logic rather than low-level implementation details.

3.

(a) Outline four advantages of a mesh computer network topology. (4 marks)
  1. High Reliability and Fault Tolerance: In a mesh topology, multiple paths exist between nodes. If one path fails, data can be rerouted through another path, ensuring network continuity.
  2. Enhanced Privacy and Security: Data can be transmitted through various paths, making it harder to intercept, thus potentially increasing security and privacy.
  3. Decentralized Communication: Mesh networks don't rely on a central node. This decentralization reduces the risk of a single point of failure bringing down the entire network.
  4. Scalability: It's relatively easy to add new nodes to a mesh network without disrupting the existing network infrastructure, making it scalable.
(b) Differentiate between minicomputer and microcomputer. (4 marks)

A minicomputer is a mid-range computer that is more powerful and capable than a microcomputer but less powerful than a mainframe computer. Minicomputers typically support multiple users simultaneously and are used in small to medium-sized organizations for tasks like server applications and data processing. A microcomputer, also known as a personal computer (PC), is a small, relatively inexpensive computer designed for individual use. Microcomputers are used for a wide range of applications, including word processing, internet browsing, and entertainment.

(c) George has noticed that his computer has been infected by a virus. Explain three symptoms this computer could have exhibited. (6 marks)
  1. Slow Performance: A virus can consume system resources, leading to a noticeable slowdown in computer speed, applications taking longer to load, and overall sluggishness.
  2. Frequent Crashes and Freezes: Viruses can cause system instability, resulting in frequent computer crashes, blue screens of death, and applications freezing or becoming unresponsive.
  3. Unusual Error Messages or Pop-ups: A virus might display strange error messages, unusual pop-up windows, or alerts that are not typical for the operating system or installed software.
(d) Plains Technical Institute computers have been attacked through the internet. Explain three cyber-crimes that could have been committed. (6 marks)
  1. Hacking (Unauthorized Access): Cybercriminals could have gained unauthorized access to the institute's computer systems to steal sensitive data, modify system settings, or disrupt operations.
  2. Malware Attack (Virus/Ransomware): The attack could involve the introduction of malware such as viruses or ransomware, which could corrupt or encrypt data, causing data loss or demanding a ransom for data recovery.
  3. Denial-of-Service (DoS) Attack: Attackers might have launched a DoS attack to overwhelm the institute's network resources, making their online services and internet access unavailable to legitimate users.

4.

(a) Outline four types of Read Only Memory (ROM). (4 marks)
  1. Mask ROM (MROM): Data is physically encoded into the circuit at the time of manufacture. It's the oldest type of ROM and cannot be altered after production.
  2. Programmable ROM (PROM): PROM can be programmed once by the user. It is initially blank, and users can write data using a special device called a PROM programmer. Once programmed, it cannot be erased or reprogrammed.
  3. Erasable Programmable ROM (EPROM): EPROM can be erased and reprogrammed, but the process is relatively slow and requires exposure to ultraviolet (UV) light to erase the data before reprogramming.
  4. Electrically Erasable Programmable ROM (EEPROM): EEPROM can be erased and reprogrammed electrically, without needing to be removed from the circuit. It allows byte-level erasure and reprogramming, making it more flexible than EPROM. Flash memory is a type of EEPROM.
(b) Distinguish between compromising and collaborating conflict management techniques. (4 marks)

Compromising is a conflict management technique where both parties involved in a conflict give up something to reach a mutually acceptable solution. It aims for a "middle ground" where neither party fully gets what they want, but both gain something. Collaborating is a conflict management technique where parties work together to find a solution that fully satisfies the concerns of both. It seeks a "win-win" outcome by integrating ideas and finding a creative solution that addresses everyone's needs.

(c) Delight Company intends to hire a network administrator. Explain three skills that this computer personnel should possess. (6 marks)
  1. Networking Knowledge: A network administrator must have a strong understanding of networking concepts, protocols (like TCP/IP, DNS, DHCP), network devices (routers, switches, firewalls), and network topologies to effectively manage and troubleshoot network infrastructure.
  2. Operating System Proficiency: They should be proficient in managing server operating systems (like Windows Server, Linux) and client operating systems. This includes user administration, system configuration, security settings, and performance monitoring.
  3. Problem-Solving and Troubleshooting Skills: Network administrators need excellent analytical and problem-solving skills to diagnose and resolve network issues quickly. This includes identifying network bottlenecks, troubleshooting connectivity problems, and restoring network services efficiently.
(d) Explain three circumstances under which a company could use telecommuting. (6 marks)
  1. Cost Reduction: Companies can reduce operational costs by allowing employees to telecommute. This can lead to savings on office space, utilities, and other office-related expenses.
  2. Improved Employee Morale and Productivity: Telecommuting can enhance employee job satisfaction and work-life balance, leading to increased morale and potentially higher productivity due to reduced commute stress and a more comfortable work environment.
  3. Access to a Wider Talent Pool: Telecommuting enables companies to hire talent from a broader geographical area, as physical location becomes less of a constraint. This allows access to specialized skills and expertise that may not be available locally.

5.

(a) Outline four ways a student could manage stress. (4 marks)
  1. Time Management and Organization: Planning and organizing study schedules, breaking down large tasks into smaller manageable parts, and prioritizing activities can reduce feelings of being overwhelmed and improve time management skills.
  2. Regular Exercise and Healthy Diet: Physical activity and a balanced diet are crucial for stress management. Exercise releases endorphins, which have mood-boosting effects, and proper nutrition supports overall well-being.
  3. Sufficient Sleep: Getting enough sleep is essential for both physical and mental health. Sleep deprivation can exacerbate stress, so maintaining a regular sleep schedule is important.
  4. Relaxation Techniques: Practicing relaxation techniques such as meditation, deep breathing exercises, or mindfulness can help calm the mind and reduce stress levels.
(b) Describe each of the following terms as used in Information Communication Technology:
(i) Information privacy; (2 marks)

Information privacy refers to the right of individuals to control how their personal information is collected, used, and disclosed. It encompasses the ability to determine what personal data is shared, with whom, and for what purpose, ensuring confidentiality and protection from unauthorized access or misuse.

(ii) Copyright; (2 marks)

Copyright is a legal right granted to the creator of original works of authorship, including literary, artistic, musical, and software works. It provides exclusive rights to control the reproduction, distribution, adaptation, public display, and performance of their work, protecting their intellectual property from unauthorized use.

(iii) Data protection. (2 marks)

Data protection involves a set of rules, policies, and procedures aimed at safeguarding personal data from unauthorized access, processing, loss, or damage. It includes measures to ensure data confidentiality, integrity, and availability, complying with legal and regulatory frameworks to protect individuals' privacy rights.

(c) Distinguish between dumb and intelligent terminals. (4 marks)

A dumb terminal is a computer terminal that relies entirely on the host computer for processing. It has minimal processing capabilities of its own and primarily serves as an input/output device. An intelligent terminal, also known as a smart terminal or thin client, has its own processing capabilities. While it can still connect to a host computer or server, it can perform some processing tasks locally, reducing the load on the central server and improving response times.

(d) Jucktech Technical Institute has been undertaking unsuccessful social responsibility programmes. Explain three possible causes of this failure. (6 marks)
  1. Poor Planning and Lack of Clear Objectives: Social responsibility programmes may fail if they are not properly planned with clear, measurable objectives and realistic timelines. Vague goals and inadequate preparation can lead to ineffective implementation and lack of impact.
  2. Insufficient Stakeholder Engagement: Failure to involve and engage with relevant stakeholders, including the community, beneficiaries, and employees, can result in programmes that do not address actual needs or lack community support and participation.
  3. Inadequate Resources and Funding: Social responsibility initiatives require sufficient resources, including financial, human, and material resources. Underfunding or lack of dedicated resources can hinder the effective execution and sustainability of these programmes.

(a) Outline four benefits that a student could accrue from good time management. (4 marks)
  1. Reduced Stress: Effective time management helps students feel more in control of their workload, reducing stress and anxiety associated with deadlines and academic pressure.
  2. Improved Academic Performance: By managing time effectively, students can allocate sufficient time for studying, assignments, and revision, leading to better understanding of concepts and improved grades.
  3. Increased Productivity: Good time management enables students to accomplish more in less time by focusing on important tasks, minimizing procrastination, and utilizing study time efficiently.
  4. Better Work-Life Balance: Time management allows students to balance academic responsibilities with personal life, social activities, and hobbies, promoting overall well-being and preventing burnout.
(b) Amani Counselling Clinic has been handling many domestic conflict issues among its workers. Explain three possible causes of such conflicts. (6 marks)
  1. Work-Life Imbalance: Employees struggling to balance work responsibilities with family and personal life may experience stress and conflict at home, which can spill over into domestic conflicts.
  2. Financial Stress: Financial difficulties, such as job insecurity, wage stagnation, or unexpected expenses, can create tension and conflict within families, contributing to domestic disputes.
  3. Communication Breakdown: Poor communication patterns, misunderstandings, and lack of effective communication skills within families can escalate minor disagreements into larger conflicts and domestic issues.

(d) Julius installed an application software in his computer. Describe two classes of this software that he could have installed. (4 marks)
  1. Productivity Software: This class includes applications designed to enhance productivity, such as word processors (e.g., Microsoft Word), spreadsheets (e.g., Microsoft Excel), presentation software (e.g., Microsoft PowerPoint), and database management systems (e.g., Microsoft Access). These tools help users create documents, manage data, and perform various office tasks efficiently.
  2. Utility Software: Utility software is designed to support and optimize the computer's infrastructure and system performance. Examples include antivirus software, disk defragmentation tools, system monitoring tools, file compression utilities (e.g., WinZip), and backup software. These applications help maintain system security, improve performance, and manage computer resources.

7.

(a) Outline four types of people skills that should be possessed by an Information Communication Technology graduate. (4 marks)
  1. Communication Skills: ICT professionals need strong verbal and written communication skills to effectively explain technical concepts to non-technical users, collaborate with team members, and document processes and procedures.
  2. Teamwork and Collaboration: ICT projects often involve working in teams. Graduates should be able to collaborate effectively, share knowledge, and contribute to team goals in diverse and multidisciplinary environments.
  3. Problem-Solving Skills: People skills in problem-solving involve the ability to understand user needs, analyze issues from different perspectives, and work collaboratively to find effective solutions that meet both technical and user requirements.
  4. Customer Service Orientation: Many ICT roles involve direct interaction with users or clients. People skills related to customer service include patience, empathy, active listening, and the ability to provide support and resolve user issues in a friendly and helpful manner.
(b) Explain three circumstances that could necessitate the use of a batch method in data processing. (6 marks)
  1. Large Volume of Data: When processing a very large amount of data that accumulates over time, batch processing is efficient. It allows for processing data in bulk during off-peak hours, reducing system load during peak times.
  2. Non-Time-Critical Processing: For tasks where immediate results are not required and there is no need for real-time interaction, batch processing is suitable. Examples include payroll processing, end-of-month financial reports, and generating bulk mailing lists.
  3. Cost-Effectiveness: Batch processing can be more cost-effective for certain tasks as it can be scheduled to run during off-peak hours when computing resources may be cheaper or more readily available. It also reduces the need for continuous system monitoring and immediate response systems.
(c) Differentiate between logical and physical access controls as used in data security. (4 marks)

Logical access controls are security measures that control access to computer systems, networks, data, and applications. They are software-based and include measures like passwords, usernames, access control lists (ACLs), encryption, and authentication protocols. Physical access controls are security measures that restrict physical access to facilities and resources, such as data centers, server rooms, and offices. They include measures like security guards, locks, biometric scanners, security badges, and surveillance cameras.

(d) Jacob, an ICT manager in a company, advised the management to acquire a mainframe computer for networking. Explain three reasons that could have led to this advice. (6 marks)
  1. High Processing Power and Performance: Mainframe computers are designed to handle extremely large volumes of data and transactions with high speed and reliability. For a company with extensive data processing needs and a large number of users, a mainframe can provide the necessary processing power to ensure smooth and efficient operations.
  2. Scalability and Reliability: Mainframes are highly scalable, allowing them to handle increasing workloads and user demands as the company grows. They are also renowned for their exceptional reliability and uptime, crucial for businesses that require continuous operation and minimal downtime for critical applications.
  3. Enhanced Security and Centralized Management: Mainframes offer robust security features and centralized management capabilities. They provide a secure environment for sensitive data and enable centralized control over system resources, user access, and security policies, simplifying administration and enhancing overall security posture.

8.

(a) Explain three characteristics of real-time data processing method. (6 marks)
  1. Immediate Processing: Real-time processing requires data to be processed immediately as it is generated or received, with minimal delay. This is crucial for applications where timely responses are critical, such as air traffic control systems or online transaction processing.
  2. Continuous Data Flow: Real-time systems typically handle a continuous stream of input data and produce outputs in a continuous and ongoing manner. They are designed to operate without interruption and maintain a constant flow of data processing.
  3. High Speed and Responsiveness: Real-time processing systems are characterized by their high speed and responsiveness. They must be able to process data and generate results quickly enough to meet strict time constraints and ensure timely responses in dynamic environments.
(b) Explain two circumstances that could lead to upgrading of a computer hard disk. (4 marks)
  1. Insufficient Storage Capacity: When the existing hard disk is consistently running out of storage space due to increasing data volume, software installations, or multimedia files, an upgrade to a larger capacity hard disk becomes necessary to accommodate the growing storage needs.
  2. Performance Degradation: If the computer's performance is slowing down due to a slow or failing hard disk, upgrading to a faster hard disk, such as an SSD (Solid State Drive), can significantly improve data access speeds, boot times, and overall system responsiveness.
(c) An ICT technician prefers to use a fibre optic cable in a computer network. Explain three reasons for this preference. (6 marks)
  1. Higher Bandwidth and Speed: Fibre optic cables offer significantly higher bandwidth and data transfer speeds compared to copper cables. They can transmit data at much faster rates, making them ideal for high-speed network applications and large data transfers.
  2. Greater Distance and Signal Integrity: Fibre optic cables can transmit data over much longer distances without signal degradation compared to copper cables. They maintain signal integrity over long distances, ensuring reliable data transmission even in geographically dispersed networks.
  3. Immunity to Electromagnetic Interference (EMI): Fibre optic cables are immune to electromagnetic interference and radio frequency interference (RFI) because they transmit data as light pulses rather than electrical signals. This makes them more reliable in environments with electrical noise and reduces the risk of data corruption.
(d) A company used ICT legislation and guidelines in developing its ICT policy. Outline four sources of these legislations and guidelines. (4 marks)
  1. National ICT Acts and Laws: Legislation passed by the national government related to information and communication technology, such as cybercrime laws, data protection acts, and electronic transaction laws, form a primary source of guidelines for ICT policies.
  2. International Standards and Frameworks: Global standards and frameworks like ISO 27001 for information security management, GDPR (General Data Protection Regulation), and other international cybersecurity and privacy standards provide benchmarks and best practices for ICT policy development.
  3. Industry-Specific Regulations: Certain industries, such as healthcare, finance, and education, may have specific regulatory requirements and guidelines related to ICT and data handling. These industry-specific regulations must be considered in ICT policy formulation.
  4. Organizational Policies and Codes of Conduct: Internal organizational policies, ethical codes of conduct, and corporate governance guidelines also influence the development of ICT policies. These internal documents reflect the company's values, ethical standards, and operational principles related to technology use.
