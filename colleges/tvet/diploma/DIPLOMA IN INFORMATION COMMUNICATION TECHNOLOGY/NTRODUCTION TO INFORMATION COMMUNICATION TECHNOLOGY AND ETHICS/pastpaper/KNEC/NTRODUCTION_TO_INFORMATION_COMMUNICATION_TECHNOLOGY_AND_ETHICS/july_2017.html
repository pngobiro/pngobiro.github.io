1. (a) (3 marks)

Outline three precautions that should be observed while replacing a processor in a computer.

Answer

Replacing a processor requires careful handling to prevent damage and ensure proper installation.

Power Off and Disconnect: Ensure the computer is completely powered off and disconnected from the power source. This prevents electrical shock and damage to components during the replacement process.

Static Discharge Prevention: Ground yourself and use an anti-static wrist strap to prevent electrostatic discharge (ESD). ESD can damage sensitive processor components. Handle the processor by its edges and avoid touching the pins.

Proper Handling and Alignment: Handle the processor with care and align it correctly with the CPU socket on the motherboard. Gently place the processor into the socket without forcing it, ensuring it is properly seated before locking the retention mechanism.

1. (b) (6 marks)

Explain three negative impacts of computer hacking in organizations.

Answer

Computer hacking can have severe negative impacts on organizations, affecting their operations, finances, and reputation.

Financial Losses: Hacking incidents can lead to significant financial losses for organizations. These losses can arise from theft of funds through fraudulent transactions, business disruption causing loss of revenue, costs associated with data recovery and system restoration, and fines for regulatory non-compliance due to data breaches. Financial repercussions can be substantial and long-lasting.

Loss of confidential data can lead to legal liabilities, loss of customer trust, competitive disadvantage from intellectual property theft, and potential regulatory penalties. Data breaches can severely damage an organization's reputation and long-term viability.

Reputational Damage and Loss of Customer Trust: Successful hacking incidents and data breaches can severely damage an organization's reputation and erode customer trust. Public disclosure of security vulnerabilities and data compromises can lead to loss of customer confidence, negative media coverage, and long-term damage to brand image. Restoring customer trust after a hacking incident is a challenging and time-consuming process, impacting customer loyalty and future business prospects.

1. (c) (ii) (3 marks)

Magnetic disks are known for their reliability. Outline three measures that could be adopted to promote their reliability.

Answer

While magnetic disks are generally reliable, certain measures can further enhance their operational lifespan and data integrity.

Regular Defragmentation: Perform regular disk defragmentation to optimize file organization on magnetic disks. Defragmentation reduces file fragmentation, minimizing drive head movement and wear, thus improving access times and extending drive lifespan.

Proper Environmental Control: Maintain optimal environmental conditions for magnetic disks. Avoid extreme temperatures, humidity, and physical shocks, as these can negatively impact drive mechanics and data integrity. Proper cooling and stable operating environments enhance drive reliability.

Scheduled Disk Health Monitoring and Error Checking: Implement scheduled disk health monitoring and error checking using disk utilities. Regular checks can detect and address potential issues like bad sectors or drive degradation early on, preventing data loss and ensuring continued drive reliability.

1. (d) (6 marks)

Operating systems perform critical functions in a computer system. Explain three such functions.

Answer

Operating systems (OS) are fundamental software components that manage computer hardware and software resources, providing essential services.

Resource Management: The OS manages computer hardware resources, including the CPU, memory, storage devices, and peripherals. It allocates these resources efficiently among different applications and processes, ensuring fair and optimized utilization. Resource management by the OS is crucial for system performance and stability, preventing resource conflicts and maximizing system throughput.

User Interface Provision: The OS provides a user interface (UI) that allows users to interact with the computer system. This UI can be a graphical user interface (GUI) or a command-line interface (CLI), enabling users to execute commands, launch applications, manage files, and control system settings. The user interface is essential for user accessibility and interaction with the computer system.

Process Management and Multitasking: The OS manages processes, which are instances of running programs. It handles process creation, scheduling, execution, and termination. Modern operating systems support multitasking, allowing multiple processes to run concurrently, giving users the ability to work with multiple applications simultaneously. Process management and multitasking enhance system responsiveness and user productivity.

2. (a) (ii) (4 marks)

Differentiate between avoiding and accommodating conflict management techniques.

Answer

Avoiding and accommodating are contrasting conflict management techniques that differ in their approach and outcomes.

Avoiding: Avoiding conflict management involves withdrawing from or ignoring the conflict situation. Individuals using this technique neither pursue their own concerns nor those of the other party. This approach is characterized by inaction, passivity, and a desire to evade the conflict. While it may temporarily reduce tension, it often leaves the underlying issues unresolved and can lead to resentment or escalation later.

Accommodating: Accommodating conflict management involves prioritizing the other party's concerns over one's own. Individuals using this technique are willing to yield or concede to the other party's demands, often to maintain harmony or avoid confrontation. This approach is characterized by selflessness, yielding, and a desire to please. While it can resolve the immediate conflict, it may lead to unmet needs and feelings of being undervalued if used consistently.

These tests simulate real-world attacks to uncover vulnerabilities that could be exploited by malicious actors, allowing for proactive security improvements.

User feedback and incident reports provide valuable insights into real-world software safety concerns, enabling timely responses and corrective actions to address identified problems.

3. (a) (i) (3 marks)

Outline three types of computer mouse technologies.

Answer

Computer mice have evolved through different technologies to track movement and user input.

Mechanical Mouse: Mechanical mice use a rubber or metal ball on their underside to detect movement. As the ball rolls, it rotates internal rollers that translate the motion into cursor movement on the screen. Mechanical mice are less common now due to their susceptibility to dirt and wear, requiring frequent cleaning.

Optical Mouse: Optical mice use an LED or laser and a sensor to track movement. They illuminate the surface beneath the mouse and capture images, detecting changes in the images to determine mouse motion. Optical mice are more precise and require less maintenance than mechanical mice, working on most surfaces without a mouse pad.

Laser Mouse: Laser mice are a type of optical mouse that uses a laser diode as the light source instead of an LED. Laser technology provides higher precision and sensitivity compared to traditional optical mice, enabling accurate tracking on a wider variety of surfaces, including glossy or reflective surfaces where optical mice may struggle.

3. (a) (ii) (4 marks)

Distinguish between a micro and a mini computer.

Answer

Microcomputers and minicomputers represent different eras and scales of computing power and application.

Microcomputer: Microcomputers are small, relatively inexpensive computers that utilize a microprocessor as their central processing unit (CPU). They are designed for individual use or small-scale applications. Examples include personal computers (PCs), laptops, and smartphones. Microcomputers are characterized by their compact size, affordability, and suitability for personal productivity, home use, and small business tasks.

Minicomputer: Minicomputers are mid-range computers, larger and more powerful than microcomputers but smaller and less powerful than mainframe computers. They are designed to support multiple users simultaneously and handle more demanding tasks than microcomputers. Minicomputers were historically used in departmental settings, small to medium-sized businesses, and for tasks such as server applications and industrial process control. They bridge the gap between personal computing and large-scale enterprise computing.

Connector availability and compatibility are critical for system build and expansion.

Reliability and Brand Reputation: Consider the reliability and brand reputation of the PSU manufacturer. Opt for reputable brands known for producing high-quality, reliable PSUs. Reviews and certifications from reputable sources can provide insights into PSU reliability and build quality. A reliable PSU minimizes the risk of power failures and protects system components from damage due to power fluctuations or instability.

5. (c) (ii) (4 marks)

Explain two circumstances under which an organization would install a ring topology network.

Answer

Ring topology networks, while less common today, might be considered under specific circumstances where their characteristics offer advantages.

Token Ring Networks for Deterministic Data Transmission: In scenarios requiring deterministic data transmission and predictable network access, ring topology, particularly Token Ring, can be advantageous. Token Ring uses a token-passing mechanism to regulate network access, ensuring that only the device holding the token can transmit data, preventing collisions and providing guaranteed bandwidth allocation. Deterministic data transmission is beneficial in time-sensitive applications or industrial control systems.

Fault Tolerance in Specific Configurations: In certain configurations, ring topologies can offer fault tolerance. Dual-ring topologies, for example, can maintain network connectivity even if a single link or node fails, as data can be routed in the opposite direction around the ring. Fault tolerance may be a consideration in critical applications where network uptime is paramount, although other topologies like mesh or redundant star are more commonly used for high availability today.

6. (a) (4 marks)

Differentiate between serial and ports as used in computers.

Answer

Serial ports and ports, in the context of computers, represent different approaches to data communication and connectivity.

Serial Ports: Serial ports transmit data one bit at a time sequentially over a single communication line. They were historically used for connecting peripherals like modems and mice. Serial ports are characterized by slower data transfer rates compared to parallel ports or modern interfaces, and typically use connectors like DB9 or DB25. Serial communication is suitable for long-distance communication and devices requiring lower bandwidth.

Ports (General Computer Ports): In a broader sense, "ports" on a computer refer to various types of interfaces used to connect peripherals and external devices. This includes a wide range of interfaces such as USB ports, Ethernet ports, HDMI ports, audio ports, etc. These ports facilitate data communication, video output, audio input/output, and network connectivity. Modern computers utilize a variety of ports to support diverse peripherals and communication standards, offering faster data transfer and versatile connectivity options compared to solely relying on serial ports.

6. (d) (6 marks)

Wireless Internet has several vulnerabilities that can be easily exploited by malicious users. Explain three possible vulnerabilities.

Answer

Wireless Internet (Wi-Fi) networks have inherent vulnerabilities that malicious users can exploit if not properly secured.

Eavesdropping and Packet Sniffing: Wireless signals are broadcast through the air, making them susceptible to eavesdropping and packet sniffing. Attackers can use readily available tools to intercept wireless signals and capture data packets transmitted over the network, potentially gaining access to sensitive information, login credentials, or unencrypted communications. Eavesdropping poses a significant privacy and security risk in wireless networks.

Unauthorized Access and Network Intrusion: Weak or improperly configured wireless security protocols can allow unauthorized users to gain access to the wireless network. Attackers can exploit vulnerabilities in Wi-Fi security protocols (e.g., WEP, weak WPA) or use brute-force attacks to crack passwords and gain network access. Once inside the network, attackers can launch further attacks, access shared resources, or intercept network traffic.

Rogue Access Points and Man-in-the-Middle Attacks: Attackers can set up rogue access points that mimic legitimate Wi-Fi networks to lure unsuspecting users to connect to their malicious access point. Once connected, users' traffic can be intercepted and manipulated in man-in-the-middle attacks. Rogue access points and MITM attacks can be used to steal credentials, inject malware, or redirect users to fraudulent websites, compromising user security and data.

7. (c) (4 marks)

Chipset is a key component in a computer motherboard. Explain two functions that are provided by this component.

Answer

The chipset is a crucial component on a computer motherboard, facilitating communication and control between various hardware components.

Northbridge Functionality (Memory and PCI Express Controller): In traditional chipset architectures, the northbridge component of the chipset manages high-speed communication between the CPU, RAM (memory), and PCI Express (PCIe) slots for graphics cards. It controls memory access, data transfer rates between CPU and memory, and provides the interface for high-bandwidth peripherals like GPUs. Northbridge ensures efficient data flow between critical performance components.

Southbridge Functionality (I/O Controller): The southbridge component of the chipset handles slower input/output (I/O) functions and communication with peripherals. It manages interfaces for storage devices (SATA, IDE), USB ports, audio, Ethernet, BIOS, and other onboard peripherals. Southbridge controls communication between the CPU and a wide range of I/O devices, enabling peripheral connectivity and system functionality.
