INTRODUCTION TO INFORMATION COMMUNICATION TECHNOLOGY AND ETHICS

Exam Code: 2920/101

Duration: 3 hours

Period: July 2021

1 (a) (4 marks)

Outline four advantages of using coaxial cable in computer networking.

Answer

1. High bandwidth: Coaxial cables can support high data transmission rates, making them suitable for broadband communication.

2. Noise immunity: The shielding in coaxial cables reduces electromagnetic interference, ensuring reliable data transmission.

3. Long-distance transmission: Coaxial cables can transmit data over longer distances without significant signal loss compared to twisted pair cables.

4. Durability: Coaxial cables are robust and can withstand physical stress, making them suitable for both indoor and outdoor use.

1 (b) (6 marks)

Explain three uses of information communication technology in the manufacturing sector.

Answer

1. Automation: ICT enables the automation of production processes, reducing human error and increasing efficiency.

2. Supply chain management: ICT systems facilitate real-time tracking of inventory, orders, and shipments, improving supply chain efficiency.

3. Quality control: ICT tools such as sensors and monitoring systems help in maintaining product quality by detecting defects early in the production process.

1 (c) (4 marks)

Distinguish between control bus and address bus as used in computers.

Answer

Control Bus: The control bus is used to transmit control signals between the CPU and other components. It manages the operations of the system by sending signals like read, write, and interrupt requests.

Address Bus: The address bus is used to carry memory addresses from the CPU to the memory or I/O devices. It specifies the location of data to be read or written.

Key Differences: The control bus handles control signals, while the address bus carries memory addresses. The control bus is bidirectional, whereas the address bus is unidirectional.

1 (d) (6 marks)

John, a manager at Citiview Company, has noticed conflicts amongst his employees. Explain three types of conflicts that he could have noticed.

Answer

1. Task conflict: Disagreements among employees about the content and goals of the work, such as differing opinions on how to complete a project.

2. Relationship conflict: Personal incompatibilities or clashes between employees, often resulting from personality differences or interpersonal issues.

3. Process conflict: Disputes about how work should be done, including disagreements over resource allocation, responsibilities, and task delegation.

2 (a) (ii) (4 marks)

Outline four features of a third generation computer.

Answer

1. Integrated circuits: Third-generation computers used integrated circuits (ICs) instead of transistors, which increased processing speed and reduced size.

2. Smaller size: The use of ICs made computers more compact and portable compared to previous generations.

3. Higher reliability: Integrated circuits were more reliable and produced less heat than transistors, leading to fewer hardware failures.

4. Improved performance: Third-generation computers had faster processing speeds and could handle more complex tasks due to advancements in IC technology.

Housekeeping Software: Merge, sort, anti-virus, defragmenter.

2 (c) (i) (2 marks)

Describe the factor considered when selecting computer software: authenticity.

Answer

Authenticity refers to the genuineness of the software. It ensures that the software is legally obtained, free from malware, and comes from a reputable source. Authentic software is more reliable and secure.

2 (c) (ii) (2 marks)

Describe the factor considered when selecting computer software: portability.

Answer

Portability refers to the ability of software to run on different platforms or operating systems without requiring significant modifications. Portable software is flexible and can be used across various devices.

2 (d) (6 marks)

Kwetu Technical Institute intends to undertake a social responsibility exercise in its neighbourhood. Explain three factors the institution should consider when choosing an activity to engage in.

Answer

1. Community needs: The activity should address a pressing need or issue within the community, such as education, health, or environmental conservation.

2. Resource availability: The institution should consider the resources it has, including manpower, funds, and materials, to ensure the activity is feasible.

3. Impact: The chosen activity should have a significant positive impact on the community, providing long-term benefits rather than short-term solutions.

3 (a) (4 marks)

Outline four advantages of a linear network topology.

Answer

1. Simplicity: Linear topology is easy to design and implement, making it suitable for small networks.

2. Cost-effective: It requires less cabling and fewer networking devices, reducing installation and maintenance costs.

3. Easy to expand: New devices can be added to the network without significant disruption to existing connections.

4. Fault isolation: Issues in one part of the network do not necessarily affect the entire network, making troubleshooting easier.

3 (b) (4 marks)

Differentiate between conflict resolution and conflict management.

Answer

Conflict Resolution: This refers to the process of resolving a conflict by addressing the root cause and finding a solution that satisfies all parties involved. It aims to eliminate the conflict entirely.

Conflict Management: This involves managing and controlling conflicts to prevent them from escalating. It focuses on maintaining a productive environment rather than completely resolving the conflict.

Key Differences: Conflict resolution seeks to eliminate the conflict, while conflict management aims to control and minimize its impact.

3 (c) (6 marks)

Joan uses a computer regularly. Explain three measures she could put in place to ensure that her computer monitor is ergonomically safe.

Answer

1. Proper positioning: The monitor should be placed at eye level, about an arm's length away, to reduce strain on the neck and eyes.

2. Adjustable settings: The brightness, contrast, and font size should be adjusted to comfortable levels to prevent eye fatigue.

3. Regular breaks: Joan should take regular breaks to rest her eyes and avoid prolonged exposure to the screen, following the 20-20-20 rule (every 20 minutes, look at something 20 feet away for 20 seconds).

3 (d) (6 marks)

Elimu Technical Institute held a talk on methods of stress management. Explain three methods that could have been mentioned.

Answer

1. Time management: Prioritizing tasks and setting realistic goals can help reduce stress by preventing last-minute rushes and overwhelming workloads.

2. Physical activity: Regular exercise, such as walking or yoga, can help reduce stress by releasing endorphins and improving overall well-being.

3. Relaxation techniques: Practices such as deep breathing, meditation, and mindfulness can help calm the mind and reduce stress levels.

4 (a) (4 marks)

Outline four roles of a computer programmer.

Answer

1. Writing code: Programmers write and test code to create software applications based on specifications.

2. Debugging: They identify and fix errors in the code to ensure the software runs smoothly.

3. Maintenance: Programmers update and maintain existing software to improve performance and fix bugs.

4. Collaboration: They work with other developers, designers, and stakeholders to ensure the software meets user needs and business requirements.

4 (b) (4 marks)

Distinguish between leasing and outsourcing system acquisition methods.

Answer

Leasing: Leasing involves renting hardware or software for a specific period. The lessee pays regular fees but does not own the equipment. At the end of the lease, the lessee may have the option to purchase the equipment.

Outsourcing: Outsourcing involves contracting a third-party provider to handle specific IT functions or services. The organization pays for the services but does not manage the resources directly.

Key Differences: Leasing is about renting equipment, while outsourcing involves delegating tasks or services to an external provider.

Answer

2. Sensitivity issues: Microphones may be too sensitive or not sensitive enough, leading to distorted or faint audio recordings.

3. Compatibility: The microphone may not be compatible with the computer or software being used, requiring additional drivers or configurations.

3. Integration: Tailor-made software can be seamlessly integrated with existing systems and workflows, ensuring smooth operations.

5 (c) (4 marks)

Distinguish between avoiding and competing as used in conflict management.

Answer

Avoiding: This strategy involves ignoring or withdrawing from the conflict to prevent escalation. It is used when the issue is trivial or when more information is needed.

Competing: This approach involves asserting one's own needs or desires over others, often leading to a win-lose outcome. It is used when quick, decisive action is needed.

Key Differences: Avoiding seeks to sidestep the conflict, while competing involves confronting it directly to achieve one's own goals.

5 (d) (6 marks)

One of the considerations made when acquiring a computer is the processor. Explain three specifications that one could use in this consideration.

Answer

1. Clock speed: The clock speed, measured in GHz, indicates how many cycles per second the processor can execute. A higher clock speed generally means better performance.

2. Number of cores: Processors with multiple cores can handle more tasks simultaneously, improving multitasking and overall performance.

3. Cache size: The cache is a small amount of high-speed memory on the processor. A larger cache allows the processor to access frequently used data more quickly, enhancing performance.

7 (a) (4 marks)

Outline four advantages of using a Magnetic Ink Character Reader (MICR) as an input device.

Answer

1. High accuracy: MICR technology is highly accurate in reading characters, reducing errors in data entry.

2. Fraud prevention: MICR-encoded documents are difficult to forge, enhancing security in financial transactions.

3. Speed: MICR devices can process documents quickly, making them suitable for high-volume environments like banks.

4. Durability: MICR-encoded documents are resistant to wear and tear, ensuring long-term readability.

7 (c) (4 marks)

Differentiate between confidentiality and privacy as used in data security.

Answer

Confidentiality: Confidentiality refers to the protection of sensitive information from unauthorized access. It ensures that only authorized individuals can access the data.

Privacy: Privacy refers to the right of individuals to control how their personal information is collected, used, and shared. It focuses on protecting personal data from misuse.

Key Differences: Confidentiality is about protecting data from unauthorized access, while privacy is about protecting individuals' rights to control their personal information.

7 (d) (6 marks)

James was advised to use a strong password in his computer. Explain three characteristics that this password should possess.

Answer

1. Length: A strong password should be at least 12 characters long to make it difficult to guess or crack.

2. Complexity: The password should include a mix of uppercase and lowercase letters, numbers, and special characters to increase its strength.

3. Unpredictability: The password should not contain easily guessable information, such as common words, names, or dates.

8 (b) (4 marks)

Differentiate between analogue and digital computers.

Answer

Analogue Computers: Analogue computers process continuous data and are used for tasks that require real-time processing, such as measuring temperature or pressure. They are less precise but faster for certain applications.

Digital Computers: Digital computers process discrete data and are used for a wide range of tasks, including calculations, data storage, and communication. They are more precise and versatile than analogue computers.

Key Differences: Analogue computers handle continuous data, while digital computers process discrete data. Digital computers are more precise and versatile, whereas analogue computers are faster for specific real-time applications.
