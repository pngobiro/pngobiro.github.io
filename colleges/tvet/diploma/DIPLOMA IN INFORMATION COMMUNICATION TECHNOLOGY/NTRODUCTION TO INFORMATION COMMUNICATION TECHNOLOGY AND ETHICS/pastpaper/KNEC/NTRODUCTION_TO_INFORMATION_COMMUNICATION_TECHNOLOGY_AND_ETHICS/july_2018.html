INTRODUCTION TO ICT AND ETHICS

Exam Code: 2920/101

Duration: 3 hours

Period: July 2018

1. (a) (4 marks)

Outline four limitations of touch screen as an output device.

Answer

Touch screens, while versatile input and output devices, have certain limitations when used primarily as output devices. These include:

Limited Tactile Feedback: Touch screens lack physical buttons or keys, providing no tactile feedback to the user. This can make it difficult for users to interact with the output without looking directly at the screen, and can be less efficient for tasks requiring precise or repeated interactions.

Fingerprint Smudges and Obstruction: As output devices, touch screens are prone to fingerprint smudges, which can reduce screen clarity and visibility of the displayed information. Furthermore, the user's fingers or stylus can obstruct part of the screen, making it difficult to view the entire output, especially during interaction.

Durability and Cleaning: Touch screens, being exposed surfaces, are more susceptible to scratches and damage compared to output devices that are protected behind a screen. They also require frequent cleaning to remove smudges and maintain optimal visibility, adding to maintenance overhead.

Cost and Complexity: Touch screen technology is generally more expensive to implement than traditional display-only output devices. Integrating touch functionality adds complexity in terms of hardware and software, potentially increasing the overall cost of the system.

1. (b) (4 marks)

Describe each of the following ethical issues in regard to computer technology: (i) patent rights; (ii) trademark.

Answer

Ethical issues surrounding patent rights and trademarks in computer technology are crucial for innovation and fair competition.

(i) Patent Rights: Patent rights in computer technology ethically pertain to the exclusive rights granted to inventors for their inventions, typically software algorithms, hardware designs, or technological processes. The ethical issue arises in balancing the inventor's right to profit from their creation and society's need for access and further innovation. Overly broad patents or aggressive patent enforcement can stifle competition and hinder technological progress. Ethically, the patent system should incentivize genuine innovation without creating monopolies that impede broader societal benefit or fair use.

(ii) Trademark: Trademarks in computer technology relate to brand names, logos, and symbols used to identify and distinguish goods and services. Ethically, trademarks are intended to protect consumers from confusion and ensure fair market practices. The ethical concerns arise when trademarks are used to unfairly compete, create confusion through similar branding, or when companies engage in cybersquatting or domain name disputes. Ethical trademark practices involve respecting established brands, avoiding deceptive similarity, and using trademarks to build genuine brand reputation rather than to mislead consumers or unfairly impede competitors.

1. (c) (6 marks)

Computer technology has had positive impact to the society with respect to how people communicate. Explain three such impacts.

Answer

Computer technology has revolutionized communication, yielding several positive societal impacts.

Increased Speed and Efficiency: Computer technology, particularly the internet and mobile communication devices, has dramatically increased the speed and efficiency of communication. Information can be transmitted instantly across geographical barriers, enabling real-time conversations and rapid dissemination of news and ideas. This has enhanced productivity in business, facilitated quicker responses in emergencies, and fostered more dynamic social interactions.

Enhanced Accessibility and Inclusivity: Communication technologies have become more accessible and inclusive due to computerization. Lower communication costs, diverse communication platforms (email, social media, video conferencing), and features like translation tools have broken down barriers related to geography, socioeconomic status, and disability. This has enabled broader participation in social, political, and economic spheres, fostering global communities and connecting individuals across diverse backgrounds.

richer and Multimedia Communication: Computer technology supports richer and more versatile forms of communication beyond traditional text or voice. The ability to share images, videos, audio, and interactive content enhances expression and understanding. Video conferencing, for instance, allows for non-verbal cues and visual demonstrations, improving clarity and personal connection in remote communication. This multimedia capability enriches personal relationships, facilitates more effective online education, and improves collaborative projects across distances.

1. (d) (6 marks)

Explain three ways in which one would improve low personal self esteem at the place of work.

Answer

Improving low personal self-esteem in the workplace requires a multifaceted approach focusing on individual empowerment and positive workplace culture.

Seek and Accept Constructive Feedback: Individuals with low self-esteem often fear criticism, but proactively seeking and accepting constructive feedback is crucial for professional growth and building confidence. By viewing feedback as an opportunity to learn and improve rather than as personal attacks, individuals can develop competence and a sense of mastery, gradually enhancing their self-esteem. Focus on specific, actionable points for improvement and celebrate progress.

Set Achievable Goals and Celebrate Successes: Low self-esteem can stem from feeling overwhelmed or inadequate. Setting small, achievable goals and breaking down larger tasks into manageable steps can foster a sense of accomplishment. Celebrating even minor successes reinforces positive self-perception and builds momentum. Acknowledging personal achievements, no matter how small they seem, helps to counter negative self-talk and cultivates a more positive self-image.

Develop and Utilize Strengths: Focusing on personal strengths and actively utilizing them in the workplace can significantly boost self-esteem. Identifying areas of competence and seeking opportunities to apply these skills not only contributes to professional effectiveness but also enhances self-belief. Taking on tasks that align with strengths leads to greater success and positive recognition, further reinforcing self-worth and confidence in one's abilities.

2. (a) (8 marks)

Outline four examples of negative impact that each of the following information accessed through the use of ICT would have on the society: (i) pornography; (ii) slander.

Answer

Access to information through ICT can have negative societal impacts, particularly with content like pornography and slander.

(i) Pornography:

Sexual Exploitation and Abuse: Widespread access to pornography can contribute to the sexual exploitation and abuse of individuals, especially children and vulnerable populations. The demand for pornography fuels trafficking and coercion, causing significant harm to victims.

Distorted Perceptions of Sexuality and Relationships: Pornography often presents unrealistic and harmful portrayals of sex and relationships, leading to distorted expectations and potential dissatisfaction in real-life interactions. It can normalize objectification, violence, and unhealthy power dynamics in sexual relationships.

Mental and Emotional Health Issues: Exposure to pornography, particularly compulsive consumption, can be linked to mental and emotional health problems, including anxiety, depression, and addiction. It can also negatively impact self-esteem, body image, and relationship satisfaction.

Social Degradation and Moral Decay: The normalization and widespread consumption of pornography can contribute to a decline in societal moral standards and erode traditional values related to sexuality, relationships, and respect for others. It can foster a culture of disrespect and contribute to the breakdown of healthy social norms.

(ii) Slander:

Reputational Damage and Social Stigma: Slanderous content disseminated through ICT platforms (social media, online forums) can rapidly and widely damage an individual's reputation. False and defamatory statements can lead to social stigma, loss of social standing, and difficulty in personal and professional relationships.

Emotional Distress and Psychological Harm: Being the target of online slander can cause significant emotional distress, anxiety, and psychological harm. The public nature and permanence of online content can amplify the impact of slander, leading to feelings of shame, isolation, and vulnerability.

Erosion of Trust and Social Cohesion: The prevalence of online slander can erode trust in online information and in social interactions. It can create a climate of fear and suspicion, undermining social cohesion and making individuals hesitant to engage openly in online spaces.

Incitement of Violence and Discrimination: Slanderous statements, particularly those targeting specific groups or individuals based on protected characteristics (race, religion, gender), can incite hatred, discrimination, and even violence. Online platforms can become breeding grounds for harmful ideologies and contribute to real-world harm.

2. (b) (6 marks)

Describe how each of the following are used to threaten the privacy of personal data through computer technology: (i) cookies; (ii) electronic profile; (iii) spyware;

Answer

Cookies, electronic profiles, and spyware are computer technologies that can significantly threaten the privacy of personal data.

(i) Cookies: Cookies are small text files that websites store on a user's computer. While some cookies are essential for website functionality (e.g., remembering login details), others, particularly tracking cookies, are used to monitor browsing activity across multiple websites. These tracking cookies collect data about user preferences, browsing habits, and online behavior without explicit consent, creating detailed profiles that can be used for targeted advertising, price discrimination, or even sold to third parties, thus violating user privacy.

(ii) Electronic Profile: Electronic profiles are comprehensive collections of personal data aggregated from various online and offline sources. These profiles can include browsing history, purchase records, social media activity, location data, and demographic information. Data brokers and companies create and utilize these profiles to categorize individuals and make inferences about their interests, beliefs, and behaviors. This aggregation of data, often without user knowledge or consent, creates detailed digital dossiers that can be used for surveillance, manipulation, and discriminatory practices, posing a significant threat to individual privacy.

(iii) Spyware: Spyware is malicious software secretly installed on a user's computer or device without their knowledge or consent. Once installed, spyware can monitor user activity, capture keystrokes (including passwords and financial information), collect browsing history, access emails and messages, and even activate cameras and microphones. This data is then transmitted to a third party, often for malicious purposes like identity theft, financial fraud, or corporate espionage. Spyware represents a severe privacy threat as it operates covertly, collecting highly sensitive personal information without the user's awareness or ability to control it.

2. (c) (6 marks)

Biometric devices have been used to control access to highly secured data control rooms. Explain three ways in which this has been achieved.

Answer

Biometric devices enhance security in data control rooms by providing robust and reliable methods for access control.

Fingerprint Scanning: Fingerprint scanners are widely used biometric devices for access control. They operate by capturing and analyzing the unique patterns of ridges and valleys on a person's fingertip. In data control rooms, fingerprint scanners are integrated into door access systems. Only authorized personnel whose fingerprints are registered in the system database are granted entry. This method is highly effective because fingerprints are unique to each individual and difficult to forge, ensuring only pre-approved individuals can physically access sensitive areas.

Iris Recognition: Iris recognition technology analyzes the unique patterns in the iris, the colored part of the eye. Iris patterns are highly complex and stable over time, making them an exceptionally reliable biometric identifier. Iris scanners used for data control room access capture a high-resolution image of the iris and compare it to stored templates. This method offers a high level of security due to the uniqueness and difficulty in replicating iris patterns. It provides a non-contact and hygienic access control solution, suitable for sensitive environments.

Facial Recognition: Facial recognition systems identify individuals by analyzing and comparing facial features from an image or video feed to a database of enrolled faces. In data control rooms, facial recognition can be used as a primary or secondary authentication method. Cameras at entry points capture facial images of individuals seeking access, and the system verifies their identity against authorized personnel. Facial recognition offers a convenient and non-intrusive access control method, especially when combined with other biometric or traditional security measures for multi-factor authentication.

3. (a) (4 marks)

Outline two differences between a worm and a virus as used in computer security.

Answer

Computer worms and viruses are both types of malware, but they differ in their propagation and operational mechanisms.

Propagation Mechanism: A primary difference is in how they spread. Computer viruses require a host program to attach themselves to and rely on user action (like opening an infected file or program) to propagate. In contrast, computer worms are self-replicating and can spread independently across networks without needing a host program or user intervention. Worms exploit network vulnerabilities to automatically propagate to other systems, making them capable of rapid and widespread dissemination.

Operational Impact: While both can be destructive, their typical operational impacts differ. Viruses often focus on corrupting or deleting files on the infected host system and may remain dormant until a specific condition is met or a trigger event occurs. Worms, on the other hand, primarily focus on replicating and spreading, often consuming network bandwidth and system resources in the process. This network congestion and resource depletion can lead to system slowdowns, denial-of-service conditions, and can be exploited to carry secondary payloads like viruses or backdoors.

3. (c) (4 marks)

A national examination system requires the use of direct input devices to capture data due to the large volume of data involved. Describe two types of input devices that may be suitable for this system.

Answer

For large-scale data capture in a national examination system, direct input devices are essential for efficiency and accuracy.

Optical Mark Recognition (OMR): OMR devices are highly suitable for processing large volumes of data from standardized answer sheets used in multiple-choice exams. OMR scanners read pre-defined marks made in specific positions on paper. Candidates fill in bubbles or mark boxes corresponding to their answers, and OMR devices quickly and accurately capture this data. OMR is efficient, cost-effective for large-scale assessments, and minimizes manual data entry errors.

Optical Character Recognition (OCR) with Intelligent Character Recognition (ICR): While OCR is generally used for converting scanned text images to machine-readable text, ICR is an advanced form capable of recognizing handwritten characters. In examination systems, ICR can be used to process handwritten responses in answer booklets or forms. Combined OCR/ICR systems can automate the extraction of textual data from scanned documents, reducing manual transcription for questions requiring short written answers or candidate details. This enhances efficiency and reduces errors compared to manual data entry from handwritten materials.

4. (a) (4 marks)

Outline four advantages of digital cameras over analogue cameras.

Answer

Digital cameras offer several advantages over traditional analogue (film) cameras, transforming photography and image capture.

Instant Image Preview and Review: Digital cameras allow for immediate viewing of captured images on a built-in screen. Users can instantly review photos, check composition and exposure, and retake shots if necessary. This instant feedback significantly improves the learning curve for photographers and allows for immediate correction of errors, saving time and resources compared to waiting for film development with analogue cameras.

No Film or Development Costs: Digital cameras eliminate the need for photographic film and chemical development processes. Images are stored electronically, reducing ongoing expenses associated with purchasing film, processing chemicals, and developing prints. This cost-effectiveness makes digital photography more accessible and economical, particularly for high-volume shooting or experimentation.

Easy Image Storage, Sharing, and Manipulation: Digital images are easily stored on memory cards or computer hard drives, offering vast storage capacity and convenient organization. Digital photos can be readily shared electronically via email, social media, or cloud services, facilitating rapid dissemination. Furthermore, digital images can be easily edited and enhanced using software, offering extensive post-processing capabilities unavailable with analogue film.

Versatility and Multifunctionality: Digital cameras are often multifunctional devices, capable of capturing still images and video. Many digital cameras incorporate features like built-in image editing, GPS tagging, and wireless connectivity. This versatility expands their application beyond still photography, making them useful for various multimedia and communication purposes, unlike the more specialized nature of analogue cameras.

4. (b) (6 marks)

Many Internet service providers have continued to replace coaxial cables with fiber optic cables for Internet connectivity. Explain three reasons for this trend.

Answer

The shift from coaxial cables to fiber optic cables by Internet Service Providers (ISPs) is driven by significant technological and performance advantages.

Higher Bandwidth and Data Capacity: Fiber optic cables offer significantly higher bandwidth and data-carrying capacity compared to coaxial cables. Fiber optics transmit data as pulses of light through thin glass or plastic fibers, enabling much faster data transfer rates. This superior bandwidth is crucial for supporting increasing internet speeds and data-intensive applications like streaming high-definition video, online gaming, and cloud computing, which coaxial cables struggle to deliver effectively.

Greater Transmission Distance and Signal Integrity: Fiber optic cables can transmit data over much longer distances with minimal signal degradation compared to coaxial cables. Coaxial cables suffer from signal attenuation over distance, requiring signal boosters at regular intervals. Fiber optic signals, however, can travel tens of kilometers without significant loss, reducing the need for repeaters and simplifying network infrastructure. This results in clearer signals, reduced latency, and more reliable high-speed internet service, especially over long distances.

Improved Security and Reduced Interference: Fiber optic cables offer enhanced security and are less susceptible to electromagnetic interference and signal tapping compared to coaxial cables. Data transmission in fiber optics is inherently more secure as it is difficult to intercept light signals without physically breaking the fiber, which is easily detectable. Furthermore, fiber optic cables are immune to electromagnetic interference, ensuring signal clarity and stability even in electrically noisy environments. These security and interference advantages make fiber optics a more robust and reliable choice for internet infrastructure.

4. (c) (4 marks)

Explain two types of random access memory of a computer system.

Answer

Random Access Memory (RAM) is essential for computer operation, providing fast, temporary storage for actively used data. Two primary types of RAM are:

Static RAM (SRAM): SRAM utilizes flip-flops to store each bit of data. It is characterized by its speed and low power consumption in idle mode, making it significantly faster than DRAM. SRAM does not require constant refreshing to retain data, which contributes to its speed. However, SRAM is more expensive and less dense than DRAM, meaning it stores fewer bits per unit area. SRAM is typically used for CPU caches where speed is paramount, despite the higher cost and lower density.

Dynamic RAM (DRAM): DRAM uses capacitors to store each bit of data. Unlike SRAM, DRAM requires periodic refreshing (recharging the capacitors) to maintain data integrity, as capacitors leak charge over time. DRAM is slower than SRAM but is significantly cheaper and offers higher density, allowing for larger amounts of memory in a smaller physical space and at a lower cost. DRAM is the primary type of RAM used in main system memory due to its balance of performance, cost, and capacity.

Information technology has lately been introduced in many manufacturing plants. Explain three benefits of using this technology to the manufacturing industry.

Answer

The integration of Information Technology (IT) into manufacturing plants, often termed Industry 4.0 or smart manufacturing, brings substantial benefits.

Increased Automation and Efficiency: IT enables greater automation of manufacturing processes through technologies like robotics, computer-aided design (CAD), computer-aided manufacturing (CAM), and the Internet of Things (IoT). Automation reduces manual labor, minimizes errors, and optimizes production workflows, leading to significantly increased efficiency, faster production cycles, and higher output rates. Automated systems can operate continuously, 24/7, further enhancing productivity and reducing operational costs.

Improved Quality Control and Precision: IT facilitates advanced quality control and precision in manufacturing. Sensors, machine vision systems, and data analytics tools enable real-time monitoring of production processes and product quality. Automated inspection systems can detect defects early in the production cycle, allowing for immediate corrective actions and reducing waste. Precise control over manufacturing parameters through computerized systems ensures consistent product quality and adherence to stringent specifications, minimizing errors and improving product reliability.

Enhanced Supply Chain Management and Responsiveness: IT systems integrate manufacturing plants with the broader supply chain, improving visibility and responsiveness. Enterprise Resource Planning (ERP) systems, supply chain management (SCM) software, and real-time data analytics enable manufacturers to optimize inventory levels, predict demand fluctuations, and coordinate effectively with suppliers and distributors. Enhanced supply chain visibility and responsiveness reduce lead times, minimize inventory holding costs, and allow manufacturers to adapt quickly to changing market demands and customer requirements, improving overall agility and competitiveness.

Joan has two choices of desktop publishing packages from which to buy one for the company. Explain four factors she should consider assuming the cost is the same for both packages.

When selecting a desktop publishing (DTP) package, even with cost parity, several factors become critical for organizational suitability.

Feature Set and Functionality: Evaluate the features and functionality offered by each DTP package in relation to the company's specific publishing needs. Consider aspects like text handling capabilities (typography, text flow, advanced formatting), image editing and manipulation tools, layout and page design options (master pages, grids, templates), support for different document types (brochures, newsletters, reports), and output formats (PDF, print-ready files). Compatibility and Integration: Check the compatibility of each DTP package with the company's existing IT infrastructure and software ecosystem. Ensure compatibility with operating systems used within the organization, file format compatibility with other software (word processors, spreadsheet applications, image editing software), and integration capabilities with other business systems (e.g., content management systems, databases). Seamless integration reduces workflow disruptions, simplifies data exchange, and ensures smooth operation within the company's overall IT environment.

Support and Training Resources: Evaluate the availability and quality of support and training resources for each DTP package. Consider the vendor's reputation for customer support, the availability of online documentation, tutorials, user forums, and formal training programs. Reliable support and comprehensive training resources are crucial for addressing user issues, maximizing software utilization, and ensuring long-term success with the chosen DTP package. Accessible support minimizes downtime and empowers employees to effectively troubleshoot problems and enhance their skills over time.

5. (c) (6 marks)

Computers use utility software to enhance the operation of the operating system. Explain three examples of such software.

Answer

Utility software plays a crucial role in augmenting the functionality and efficiency of operating systems.

Antivirus Software: Antivirus software is a critical utility designed to protect the computer system from malware threats, including viruses, worms, Trojans, spyware, and ransomware. Justification: Antivirus programs scan files, system memory, and network traffic for malicious code, detect and remove malware, and provide real-time protection against new threats. By safeguarding the system from malware infections, antivirus software ensures system stability, data integrity, and prevents performance degradation caused by malware activities, thereby enhancing the overall operation of the operating system.

Disk Defragmentation Software: Disk defragmentation utilities optimize the organization of files on hard disk drives (HDDs). Over time, files become fragmented, meaning parts of files are scattered across different physical locations on the disk. Justification: Defragmentation software rearranges fragmented files to store them in contiguous blocks, reducing disk access time and improving file read/write speeds. By optimizing disk performance, defragmentation utilities enhance system responsiveness, application loading times, and overall system efficiency, contributing to smoother operating system performance.

System Backup and Recovery Software: Backup and recovery utilities are essential for data protection and system resilience. These utilities create copies of system files, applications, and user data, enabling system restoration in case of data loss, system failure, or hardware malfunctions. Justification: Backup software allows for scheduled backups to local drives, external storage, or cloud services. Recovery utilities enable restoring the system to a previous working state, minimizing data loss and downtime in the event of system errors or disasters. By providing data protection and system recovery capabilities, these utilities enhance system reliability and ensure business continuity, supporting consistent operating system operation.

6. (a) (6 marks)

Explain three situations where the monitor may not be an appropriate output device.

Answer

While monitors are versatile output devices, certain situations necessitate alternative output methods.

Situations Requiring Physical Hard Copies: In scenarios where physical, tangible output is required, a monitor is unsuitable. Justification: For tasks like printing documents, creating physical reports, generating receipts, or producing photographic prints, a printer is the appropriate output device. Monitors display information visually on screen but cannot produce physical copies needed for archival purposes, physical distribution, or situations where digital displays are impractical or insufficient.

Environments with Limited Visual Accessibility or Privacy Concerns: In situations where visual display is not feasible or desirable, monitors are not appropriate. Justification: For visually impaired users, audio output devices like speakers or headphones, coupled with screen readers, provide accessible information delivery. In environments requiring privacy, such as secure testing or confidential data output, monitors may compromise data security. In such cases, alternative output methods like encrypted data storage or secure print solutions are necessary to maintain confidentiality and accessibility.

Mobile or Embedded Systems with Size or Power Constraints: For mobile devices, embedded systems, or applications with strict size or power limitations, traditional monitors may be impractical or inefficient. Justification: Small, portable devices like smartphones, sensors, or IoT devices may utilize alternative output methods due to size, weight, or power consumption constraints. These systems might rely on auditory outputs, small LCD displays with limited information, or data transmission to remote monitoring systems instead of full-sized monitors to conserve space, power, and enhance portability.

6. (b) (6 marks)

Many companies face the challenge of electronic waste due to computerization. Explain three factors that contribute to the production of such waste.

Answer

Electronic waste (e-waste) is a growing environmental concern, significantly exacerbated by increasing computerization.

Rapid Technological Obsolescence: The fast pace of technological advancements in the IT industry leads to rapid obsolescence of electronic devices. Justification: Newer, faster, and more feature-rich computers and peripherals are continually released, making older equipment seem outdated and less desirable, even if still functional. This cycle of planned or perceived obsolescence encourages frequent upgrades and premature disposal of older electronics, contributing significantly to e-waste generation. Consumer demand for the latest technology and marketing strategies promoting upgrades further fuel this trend.

Short Lifespan of Electronic Devices: Many electronic devices, particularly consumer electronics, are designed for relatively short lifespans. Justification: Built-in obsolescence, limited repairability, and the use of lower-quality components in some devices contribute to shorter product lifecycles. Devices fail or become inefficient relatively quickly, necessitating replacement. This shorter lifespan, coupled with high consumption rates, accelerates the volume of electronics reaching end-of-life and becoming e-waste. The lack of durable design and repair options exacerbates this issue.

Lack of Effective Recycling Infrastructure and Consumer Awareness: Inadequate e-waste recycling infrastructure and low consumer awareness about responsible disposal practices contribute to e-waste accumulation. Justification: Many regions lack sufficient collection, processing, and recycling facilities for electronic waste. Consumers may not be aware of proper disposal methods or lack convenient options for recycling electronics. Consequently, a large proportion of e-waste ends up in landfills or informal recycling streams, leading to environmental pollution and loss of valuable resources. Insufficient regulations, inadequate recycling facilities, and lack of public awareness hinder effective e-waste management and contribute to the problem.

6. (c) (4 marks)

With the aid of four examples, describe the term microcomputer as use in computer systems.

Answer

A microcomputer, in computer systems, refers to a small, relatively inexpensive computer with a microprocessor as its central processing unit (CPU).

Personal Computers (PCs): Desktop and laptop computers used in homes, offices, and schools are prime examples of microcomputers. PCs are designed for individual use and cater to a wide range of applications, from word processing and web browsing to gaming and software development. Their affordability and versatility make them ubiquitous microcomputer systems.

Smartphones: Modern smartphones are powerful microcomputers in mobile form. They integrate microprocessors, memory, storage, and various input/output interfaces, enabling them to perform complex tasks like running applications, accessing the internet, capturing photos and videos, and more. Smartphones exemplify the miniaturization and mobility of microcomputer technology.

Tablets: Tablet computers, like iPads and Android tablets, are another category of microcomputers. They bridge the gap between laptops and smartphones, offering larger screens than phones but retaining portability. Their robust mechanical design makes them more resilient to dust, humidity, and temperature variations, suitable for industrial settings, warehouses, or environments where laser or inkjet printers might be less reliable. Their durability and low running costs make them practical for specific niche applications.

7. (d) (4 marks)

Call center staff spend most of their time using computers for data entry. (i) Explain two health-related problems to the staff that would be caused by each of the following devices: (I) key board; (II) CRT visual display unit.

Answer

Prolonged computer use for data entry in call centers can lead to health issues related to input devices and display technology.

(i) Key Board:

(I) Repetitive Strain Injury (RSI): Prolonged and repetitive typing on a keyboard can lead to Repetitive Strain Injuries, such as carpal tunnel syndrome and tendonitis. Justification: RSI results from overuse and strain on muscles, tendons, and nerves in the hands, wrists, and arms due to repetitive keyboard movements and awkward postures. This can cause pain, numbness, tingling, and reduced hand function, impacting work performance and long-term health.

(II) Musculoskeletal Disorders (MSDs): Poor keyboard ergonomics and prolonged static postures while typing can contribute to Musculoskeletal Disorders affecting the back, neck, and shoulders. Justification: Incorrect keyboard placement, lack of wrist support, and prolonged sitting in fixed positions can strain back and neck muscles, leading to pain, stiffness, and discomfort. MSDs from keyboard use can result in chronic pain, reduced mobility, and decreased work productivity.

(ii) CRT Visual Display Unit:

(I) Eye Strain and Fatigue: Prolonged viewing of CRT (Cathode Ray Tube) displays can cause eye strain and fatigue. Justification: CRT screens emit flicker and radiation, and prolonged focus on these displays can strain eye muscles, leading to headaches, blurred vision, dry eyes, and fatigue. Eye strain reduces visual comfort and can negatively impact concentration and work efficiency.

(II) Posture-related Discomfort (Neck and Back Strain): Improper positioning of CRT monitors and prolonged static postures can contribute to neck and back strain. Justification: CRT monitors are often bulky and less adjustable than modern LCD screens, leading to awkward monitor placement. Users may adopt uncomfortable neck and back postures to view the screen properly, resulting in muscle strain, pain, and discomfort over time. Poor monitor ergonomics with CRTs can exacerbate musculoskeletal issues.

7. (d) (ii) (2 marks)

(ii) Suggest one solution for each of the devices that would minimize the associated health risks in (i).

Answer

Mitigating health risks associated with keyboard and CRT monitor use requires ergonomic adjustments and work practice modifications.

(i) Key Board:

Solution: Ergonomic Keyboards and Work Breaks: Using ergonomic keyboards designed to promote natural wrist and hand postures can reduce strain. Regular short breaks from typing, incorporating stretching exercises, and ensuring proper keyboard placement (height, angle, and distance) can minimize the risk of RSI and MSDs. Ergonomic keyboards and work breaks promote healthier typing habits and reduce physical stress.

(ii) CRT Visual Display Unit:

Solution: Replace CRT with LCD Monitors and Ergonomic Monitor Setup: Replacing CRT monitors with modern LCD (Liquid Crystal Display) monitors is a primary solution. LCDs eliminate flicker and emit less radiation, reducing eye strain. Additionally, ergonomic monitor setup, including adjustable monitor stands to position the screen at eye level and proper viewing distance, minimizes neck and back strain. Switching to LCDs and optimizing monitor ergonomics significantly reduces health risks associated with prolonged display viewing.

A commercial bank has offices in several floors of a building and other branches within the same city. The company intends to install a computer network to link all their offices. With the aid of a diagram, describe the most appropriate type of network for the company.

Answer

For a commercial bank with offices in a multi-story building and branches within a city, a combination of network topologies is most appropriate to efficiently link all locations.

Network Type: Combination of LAN and MAN.

Diagram: (Unfortunately, I am unable to create visual diagrams directly in this text-based format. However, I can describe the network structure to be visualized.)

Description:

Local Area Network (LAN) within each office building and branch: Within each building (headquarters and branches), a Local Area Network (LAN) should be implemented. Ethernet cabling or Wi-Fi would be used for connectivity within each location. Metropolitan Area Network (MAN) connecting buildings and branches across the city: To connect the headquarters building and the geographically dispersed branches within the city, a Metropolitan Area Network (MAN) is required. Technologies like fiber optic cables or leased lines would be used to create the MAN infrastructure, providing high-speed and reliable connectivity across the metropolitan area. A central router or network infrastructure at the headquarters would manage the MAN and facilitate communication between all locations.

Justification: This hybrid LAN/MAN approach is most suitable because:

Scalability and Manageability: LANs within each location offer localized control and management, while the MAN provides city-wide connectivity, allowing for scalable network expansion as the bank grows.

Cost-Effectiveness: LAN infrastructure within buildings is cost-effective and easy to deploy. Utilizing a MAN for inter-branch connectivity optimizes bandwidth and cost for wider area communication compared to extending LANs across the city.

Performance and Reliability: Fiber optic MAN connections provide high bandwidth and reliability for inter-branch data transfer, essential for banking operations, while LANs ensure fast local network performance within each office.
