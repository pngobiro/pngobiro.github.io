2920/101
DIPLOMA IN INFORMATION COMMUNICATION TECHNOLOGY
INTRODUCTION TO INFORMATION COMMUNICATION TECHNOLOGY AND ETHICS

March/April 2023

Time: 3 hours

Instructions to Candidates:

  • This paper consists of EIGHT questions.
  • Answer any FIVE questions in the answer booklet provided.
  • Candidates should answer the questions in English.

Question 1

(a) Outline four advantages of outsourcing acquisition method. (4 marks)

Sample Answer:

  • Cost reduction through economies of scale
  • Access to specialized expertise and technologies
  • Allows focus on core business functions
  • Reduced infrastructure investment needs

(b) Explain two characteristics of the third-generation computers. (4 marks)

Sample Answer:

  • Used integrated circuits (ICs) which made them smaller and more reliable than previous generations
  • Introduced keyboard and monitor as standard input/output devices

(c) A computer laboratory has been secured using facial recognition. Explain three limitations of this system. (6 marks)

Sample Answer:

  • Can be affected by changes in lighting conditions, leading to false rejections
  • May have difficulty recognizing faces with masks, glasses, or other facial coverings
  • System performance can degrade over time as facial features change

(d) (i) With the aid of a diagram, describe peer to peer computer network. (4 marks)

Sample Answer:

A peer-to-peer network is a decentralized network where each computer acts as both client and server, sharing resources directly without a central server.

(d) (ii) Outline two characteristics of the network in d(i). (2 marks)

Sample Answer:

  • No central server required - all computers have equal status
  • Direct resource sharing between computers

Question 2

(a) Outline four benefits of self-awareness to a college student. (4 marks)

Sample Answer:

  • Better understanding of personal strengths and weaknesses
  • Improved emotional intelligence and relationships
  • Enhanced decision-making abilities
  • Better stress management and coping skills

(b) Shelyn, a Module II student, prefers using an impact printer. Explain three reasons that could have led to this preference. (6 marks)

Sample Answer:

  • Ability to create multiple copies simultaneously using carbon paper
  • Lower cost of operation compared to non-impact printers
  • Durability and reliability in harsh environments

(c) Distinguish between intra-group and inter-group conflict. (4 marks)

Sample Answer:

Intra-group conflict occurs between members within the same group, while inter-group conflict occurs between different groups within an organization.

(d) Explain three circumstances that could make an organisation fail in its corporate social responsibility. (6 marks)

Sample Answer:

  • Insufficient financial resources to support CSR initiatives
  • Lack of commitment from top management
  • Poor communication and implementation of CSR policies

Question 3

(a) Explain two benefits of copyright. (4 marks)

Sample Answer:

  • Protects creators' rights and ensures they receive compensation for their work
  • Encourages innovation and creativity by providing legal protection for intellectual property

(b) An organisation intends to develop its ICT legislation. Explain three sources of these legislation. (6 marks)

Sample Answer:

  • Government regulations and national ICT policies
  • International standards and best practices
  • Industry-specific regulatory requirements

(c) Differentiate between data bus and address bus as used in computers. (4 marks)

Sample Answer:

Data bus carries actual data between CPU and other components, while address bus carries memory addresses to specify locations for reading or writing data.

(d) Heztech Technical institute has launched a virtual learning programme for its students.

(i) Explain two challenges of this programme to the students. (4 marks)

Sample Answer:

  • Limited access to reliable internet connectivity
  • Lack of direct face-to-face interaction with instructors

(ii) Outline two opportunities that this programme could create. (2 marks)

Sample Answer:

  • Flexible learning schedule for working students
  • Access to education for geographically distant students

Question 4

(a) Outline four advantages of a light pen as an input device. (4 marks)

Sample Answer:

  • Direct and intuitive pointing device
  • High precision for drawing and selecting
  • Natural hand-eye coordination
  • Good for graphics and design work

(b) Dan suffers from low self-esteem. Explain three symptoms that he could be exhibiting. (6 marks)

Sample Answer:

  • Difficulty accepting compliments and positive feedback
  • Self-critical thoughts and negative self-talk
  • Avoiding social interactions and new challenges

(c) Distinguish between twisted pair and fibre optic cables as used in networking. (4 marks)

Sample Answer:

Twisted pair cables use copper wires and transmit electrical signals, while fiber optic cables use glass/plastic fibers and transmit light signals. Fiber optic offers higher bandwidth and longer transmission distances.

(d) Mark uses pirated software in his laptop. Explain three reasons that could have led to this use. (6 marks)

Sample Answer:

  • High cost of original software licenses
  • Easy accessibility of pirated versions
  • Lack of awareness about legal implications

Question 5

(a) (i) Define repetitive strain injury as used in ergonomics. (2 marks)

Sample Answer:

Repetitive strain injury is a condition caused by prolonged repetitive movements, often resulting in pain and damage to muscles, tendons, and nerves.

(ii) Outline two symptoms exhibited by a computer user suffering from repetitive strain injury. (2 marks)

Sample Answer:

  • Pain and tingling in wrists and fingers
  • Numbness and weakness in affected areas

(b) Explain three benefits of time management to a student. (6 marks)

Sample Answer:

  • Improved academic performance through better study planning
  • Reduced stress and anxiety levels
  • Better work-life balance

(c) Differentiate between ink jet and laser printers. (4 marks)

Sample Answer:

Ink jet printers spray tiny droplets of liquid ink onto paper, while laser printers use toner powder and heat to fuse the image onto paper. Laser printers are typically faster and more cost-effective for high-volume printing.

(d) Employees at Geztech Company have work related conflicts among themselves. Explain three possible causes of these conflicts. (6 marks)

Sample Answer:

  • Poor communication between team members
  • Unclear roles and responsibilities
  • Competition for limited resources

Question 6

(a) Explain two features of system software. (4 marks)

Sample Answer:

  • Manages hardware resources and provides platform for application software
  • Performs basic system operations like file management and memory allocation

(b) Distinguish between withdrawing and smoothing conflict resolution techniques. (4 marks)

Sample Answer:

Withdrawing involves removing oneself from the conflict situation, while smoothing involves emphasizing areas of agreement and downplaying disagreements to maintain harmony.

(c) Reztech Company intends to acquire a mainframe computer. Explain three benefits the company will accrue from this computer. (6 marks)

Sample Answer:

  • High processing power for large-scale operations
  • Ability to handle multiple tasks simultaneously
  • Enhanced data security and reliability

(d) An organisation has a responsibility of protecting the confidentiality of its data. Explain three safeguarding measures that could be put in place to achieve this. (6 marks)

Sample Answer:

  • Implementation of strong encryption protocols
  • Regular security audits and updates
  • Access control and user authentication systems

Question 7

(a) Outline three uses of computers in an engineering firm. (3 marks)

Sample Answer:

  • Computer-Aided Design (CAD) for drafting and modeling
  • Project management and scheduling
  • Structural analysis and simulation

(b) Explain each of the following terms as used in ICT:

(i) compiler;

Sample Answer:

A program that translates source code written in a high-level programming language into machine code.

(ii) defragmenter;

Sample Answer:

A utility program that reorganizes fragmented files on a disk to improve system performance.

(iii) disk partition. (6 marks)

Sample Answer:

A logical division of a hard disk drive that can be managed separately from other segments.

(c) Distinguish between laptop and tablet computers. (4 marks)

Sample Answer:

Laptops have a physical keyboard and clamshell design, running full desktop operating systems, while tablets are touchscreen devices with virtual keyboards, typically running mobile operating systems.

(d) Figure 2 shows a computer storage device. Use it to answer the questions that follow.

(i) Identify this device. (1 mark)

Sample Answer:

Hard Disk Drive (HDD)

(ii) Explain three characteristics of the device identified in (i) (6 marks)

Sample Answer:

  • Non-volatile storage that retains data when powered off
  • Uses magnetic platters for data storage
  • Higher storage capacity compared to solid-state drives

Question 8

(a) Outline four duties of a network administrator. (4 marks)

Sample Answer:

  • Managing network security and access controls
  • Monitoring network performance and troubleshooting issues
  • Installing and configuring network hardware and software
  • Managing user accounts and access permissions

(b) Explain three circumstances that necessitates information privacy in an organisation. (6 marks)

Sample Answer:

  • Protection of sensitive customer data and personal information
  • Safeguarding proprietary business information and trade secrets
  • Compliance with data protection regulations and laws

(c) The following are computer keyboard keys:

Home, Tab, Enter, End, Insert, Ctrl, Alt, Page Up.

Classify each of these keys as either navigation or special. (4 marks)

Sample Answer:

Navigation keys: Home, Tab, End, Page Up

Special keys: Enter, Insert, Ctrl, Alt

(d) An organisation uses an in-house software. Explain three disadvantages of this software. (6 marks)

Sample Answer:

  • High development and maintenance costs
  • Time-consuming development process
  • Limited support and documentation compared to commercial software
