July 2019

INTRODUCTION TO INFORMATION COMMUNICATION TECHNOLOGY AND ETHICS

Exam Code: 2920/101

Duration: 3 hours

Period: July 2019

1 (a) (i) (2 marks)

State two equipment other than computers, that are likely to be found in the ICT department of a typical organisation.

Answer

1. Printers

2. Routers

1 (a) (ii) (2 marks)

Students use snap-chat to communicate. Outline two challenges they are likely to face.

Answer

1. Privacy concerns: Snapchat messages can be screenshotted or saved, leading to potential privacy breaches.

2. Distraction: The app can be a source of distraction, affecting students' focus on academic tasks.

1 (b) (4 marks)

Kisima Company intends to network all its computers. Explain two disadvantages of this move to the company.

Answer

1. Security risks: Networking computers increases the risk of cyber-attacks and unauthorized access to sensitive data.

2. Cost: Setting up and maintaining a network can be expensive, requiring investment in hardware, software, and IT support.

1 (c) (i) (4 marks)

Jacky had the following devices in the computer laboratory: Keyboard, printer, plotter, speaker, scanner, mouse, trackball, monitor. Classify them as either input or output devices.

Answer

Input Devices: Keyboard, mouse, trackball, scanner.

Output Devices: Printer, plotter, speaker, monitor.

1 (c) (ii) (2 marks)

Describe the function of a plotter.

Answer

A plotter is an output device used to produce high-quality graphics and designs, typically used for large-scale printing such as architectural blueprints or engineering drawings.

1 (d) (6 marks)

A lecturer at Masomo Bora Technical is preparing a lesson on the topic emotions. Explain three positive ways of expressing emotions that could be included in the lesson.

Answer

1. Verbal communication: Expressing emotions through words in a calm and constructive manner.

2. Writing: Journaling or writing about emotions can help individuals process and understand their feelings.

3. Physical activity: Engaging in exercise or sports can help release pent-up emotions in a healthy way.

2 (a) (6 marks)

Explain three uses of Information Communication Technology in a college.

Answer

1. Online learning: ICT facilitates e-learning platforms, allowing students to access course materials and attend virtual classes.

2. Administrative tasks: ICT systems streamline administrative processes such as student registration, grading, and attendance tracking.

3. Research: ICT provides access to online databases, journals, and other resources, enhancing the quality of academic research.

2 (b) (i) (4 marks)

With the aid of a diagram, describe a bus topology.

Answer

A bus topology is a network configuration where all devices are connected to a single central cable, known as the bus. Data is transmitted along the bus, and each device checks if the data is intended for it. If not, the data passes along the bus to the next device.

2 (b) (ii) (2 marks)

State two advantages of the topology described in (i).

Answer

1. Cost-effective: Bus topology requires less cabling and is cheaper to install compared to other topologies.

2. Easy to expand: New devices can be added to the network without significant disruption to existing connections.

2 (c) (6 marks)

The management of Sweet Land Company intends to use a preventive approach to manage conflicts among its employees. Explain three methods they could use to achieve this.

Answer

1. Clear communication: Establishing open and transparent communication channels to prevent misunderstandings.

2. Training: Providing conflict resolution training to employees to equip them with skills to handle disputes effectively.

3. Policies: Implementing clear policies and procedures for addressing conflicts, ensuring that employees know how to report and resolve issues.

2 (d) (2 marks)

On a particular day, a secretary performed the following activities in the office: Opening and reading office email messages, calling friends with office phone, hosting a relative in the office, printing internal Memo for meetings. Classify each of the activities as either ethical or unethical.

Answer

Ethical: Opening and reading office email messages, printing internal Memo for meetings.

Unethical: Calling friends with office phone, hosting a relative in the office.

3 (a) (4 marks)

Outline four physical security measures that a company could implement in their ICT department.

Answer

1. Access control: Implementing key cards or biometric systems to restrict access to the ICT department.

2. Surveillance: Installing CCTV cameras to monitor and record activities in the ICT department.

3. Locking devices: Using locks on server rooms and cabinets to prevent unauthorized access to hardware.

4. Security personnel: Employing security guards to monitor and protect the ICT department.

3 (b) (6 marks)

Explain three challenges an organisation could face when engaging in social responsibility.

Answer

1. Cost: Implementing social responsibility initiatives can be expensive, requiring significant financial investment.

2. Resource allocation: Allocating resources to social responsibility projects may divert resources from core business activities.

3. Measuring impact: It can be challenging to measure the impact of social responsibility initiatives, making it difficult to assess their effectiveness.

3 (c) (6 marks)

Sadie company is contemplating replacing the off-the-shelf with tailor-made software. Explain three benefits that the company would realize from this move.

Answer

1. Customization: Tailor-made software can be designed to meet the specific needs of the company, improving efficiency and productivity.

2. Scalability: Custom software can be scaled to accommodate the company's growth and changing requirements.

3. Integration: Tailor-made software can be seamlessly integrated with existing systems, ensuring smooth operations.

3 (d) (4 marks)

Outline four reasons that could lead to unethical behaviour in Information Communication and Technology.

Answer

1. Lack of awareness: Employees may not be aware of ethical guidelines and policies.

2. Pressure: High work pressure or tight deadlines may lead to unethical shortcuts.

3. Personal gain: Employees may engage in unethical behavior for personal financial gain or career advancement.

4. Poor supervision: Lack of oversight and accountability can lead to unethical practices.

4 (a) (i) (2 marks)

State two roles of a programmer.

Answer

1. Writing code: Programmers write and test code to create software applications based on specifications.

2. Debugging: They identify and fix errors in the code to ensure the software runs smoothly.

4 (a) (ii) (2 marks)

State two roles of a database administrator.

Answer

1. Data management: Database administrators manage and organize data to ensure its accuracy and accessibility.

2. Security: They implement security measures to protect data from unauthorized access and breaches.

4 (b) (6 marks)

Goldy Company Limited developed an ICT policy. Explain three benefits the company would accrue from this.

Answer

1. Improved security: An ICT policy outlines security measures, reducing the risk of data breaches and cyber-attacks.

2. Compliance: The policy ensures that the company complies with legal and regulatory requirements related to ICT.

3. Efficiency: Clear guidelines and procedures improve the efficiency of ICT operations, reducing downtime and errors.

4 (c) (i) (4 marks)

Explain the function of the Prt Scr key on a computer keyboard.

Answer

The Prt Scr (Print Screen) key captures an image of the entire screen and copies it to the clipboard. This image can then be pasted into an image editor or document for further use.

4 (c) (ii) (4 marks)

Explain the function of the Home key on a computer keyboard.

Answer

The Home key moves the cursor to the beginning of the current line or document, depending on the context. It is used to quickly navigate to the start of a line or page.

4 (d) (6 marks)

With the aid of a block diagram, describe the functional components of a computer system.

Answer

A computer system consists of the following functional components:

1. Input devices: These devices allow users to input data into the computer (e.g., keyboard, mouse).

2. Central Processing Unit (CPU): The CPU processes data and executes instructions.

3. Memory: This includes RAM and ROM, which store data and instructions temporarily or permanently.

4. Output devices: These devices display or output processed data (e.g., monitor, printer).

5. Storage devices: These devices store data for long-term use (e.g., hard drive, SSD).

5 (a) (i) (2 marks)

Kiboko Company Limited uses an impact printer for printing. Outline two challenges the company would face by using such a printer.

Answer

1. Noise: Impact printers are noisy, which can be disruptive in a quiet office environment.

2. Speed: Impact printers are slower compared to non-impact printers, leading to longer printing times.

5 (a) (ii) (4 marks)

Distinguish between a router and a bridge as used in computing.

Answer

Router: A router connects multiple networks and directs data packets between them. It operates at the network layer (Layer 3) of the OSI model and uses IP addresses to route data.

Bridge: A bridge connects two network segments and filters data traffic based on MAC addresses. It operates at the data link layer (Layer 2) of the OSI model.

Key Differences: Routers connect different networks and use IP addresses, while bridges connect network segments and use MAC addresses.

5 (b) (i) (2 marks)

Explain the importance of self-awareness to a college student.

Answer

Self-awareness helps students understand their strengths, weaknesses, and emotions, enabling them to make better decisions, manage stress, and improve interpersonal relationships.

5 (b) (ii) (4 marks)

Bilal Company Limited has trained its employees on resolving family conflicts. Explain two benefits of this training to the company.

Answer

1. Improved productivity: Employees who can resolve family conflicts are likely to be more focused and productive at work.

2. Reduced absenteeism: Effective conflict resolution can reduce stress-related absenteeism, leading to better attendance and performance.

5 (c) (4 marks)

Distinguish between leasing and outsourcing methods of software acquisition.

Answer

Leasing: Leasing involves renting software for a specific period. The lessee pays regular fees but does not own the software. At the end of the lease, the lessee may have the option to purchase the software.

Outsourcing: Outsourcing involves contracting a third-party provider to handle specific IT functions or services. The organization pays for the services but does not manage the software directly.

Key Differences: Leasing is about renting software, while outsourcing involves delegating tasks or services to an external provider.

5 (d) (4 marks)

The management of Zackies Ltd. uses information communication and technology legislations in their company. Explain two sources of such legislations.

Answer

1. Government regulations: Laws and regulations enacted by the government to govern the use of ICT, such as data protection and cybersecurity laws.

2. Industry standards: Guidelines and best practices established by industry bodies to ensure compliance and ethical use of ICT.

6 (a) (2 marks)

State four characteristics of fourth-generation computers.

Answer

1. Use of microprocessors: Fourth-generation computers use microprocessors, which integrate the CPU onto a single chip.

2. Increased speed: These computers have faster processing speeds due to advancements in microprocessor technology.

3. Smaller size: Fourth-generation computers are more compact and portable compared to earlier generations.

4. User-friendly: They are designed to be more user-friendly, with graphical user interfaces (GUIs) and easier-to-use software.

6 (b) (i) (2 marks)

Faida Co. Ltd. has been experiencing a lot of conflict between workmates. Outline two causes of such conflicts.

Answer

1. Miscommunication: Lack of clear communication can lead to misunderstandings and conflicts.

2. Competition: Employees may compete for resources, recognition, or promotions, leading to conflicts.

6 (b) (ii) (6 marks)

Distinguish between magnetic and optical storage devices, giving an example in each case.

Answer

Magnetic Storage Devices: These devices use magnetic fields to store data. An example is a hard disk drive (HDD).

Optical Storage Devices: These devices use laser technology to read and write data. An example is a DVD.

Key Differences: Magnetic storage devices use magnetic fields, while optical storage devices use lasers. Magnetic devices are generally faster, while optical devices are more durable and portable.

6 (c) (6 marks)

The management of Fre-ex Company has a life skill training programme for its staff. Explain three benefits the company would realize from this training.

Answer

1. Improved communication: Life skills training enhances employees' communication skills, leading to better teamwork and collaboration.

2. Stress management: Employees learn techniques to manage stress, leading to improved mental health and productivity.

3. Conflict resolution: Training in conflict resolution helps employees handle disputes effectively, reducing workplace conflicts.

6 (d) (4 marks)

Explain two circumstances that would make a student prefer to use an LCD monitor to a CRT monitor.

Answer

1. Space-saving: LCD monitors are thinner and take up less space compared to bulky CRT monitors.

2. Energy efficiency: LCD monitors consume less power, making them more energy-efficient and cost-effective in the long run.

7 (a) (4 marks)

Outline four characteristics of a supercomputer.

Answer

1. High processing speed: Supercomputers can perform trillions of calculations per second, making them extremely fast.

2. Large memory capacity: They have vast amounts of RAM and storage to handle complex computations and large datasets.

3. Parallel processing: Supercomputers use multiple processors to perform tasks simultaneously, increasing efficiency.

4. Specialized applications: They are used for specialized tasks such as weather forecasting, scientific research, and simulations.

7 (b) (i) (2 marks)

Explain the term "approach-approach" conflict resolution.

Answer

Approach-approach conflict occurs when an individual must choose between two desirable options. The conflict arises because both options are attractive, making it difficult to decide.

7 (b) (ii) (2 marks)

Explain the term "approach-avoid" conflict resolution.

Answer

Approach-avoid conflict occurs when an individual is faced with a single option that has both positive and negative aspects. The conflict arises because the option is simultaneously attractive and unattractive.

7 (c) (i) (2 marks)

Describe the term ergonomics as used in Information Communication Technology.

Answer

Ergonomics in ICT refers to the design of workspaces and equipment to fit the user's needs, ensuring comfort, efficiency, and safety while using technology.

7 (c) (ii) (4 marks)

Differentiate between micro and mini computers.

Answer

Microcomputers: Microcomputers are small, single-user computers designed for personal use. Examples include desktops, laptops, and tablets.

Mini Computers: Mini computers are larger than microcomputers and are designed for multiple users. They are used in small to medium-sized businesses for tasks such as data processing and server management.

Key Differences: Microcomputers are for personal use, while mini computers are for multiple users. Mini computers are more powerful and have greater storage capacity than microcomputers.

7 (d) (i) (4 marks)

Galnic Company has opted to use electronic money transfer to pay its suppliers instead of cash. Explain two benefits the Company would realize from this move.

Answer

1. Speed: Electronic money transfers are faster than traditional cash payments, ensuring timely payments to suppliers.

2. Security: Electronic transfers reduce the risk of theft or loss associated with carrying large amounts of cash.

7 (d) (ii) (2 marks)

Outline two challenges the company is likely to face.

Answer

1. Technical issues: Electronic money transfers may be affected by technical glitches or system failures.

2. Fraud risk: There is a risk of fraudulent transactions or unauthorized access to the company's financial accounts.

8 (a) (4 marks)

Outline four unethical behaviors that are common to computer technicians while executing their duties.

Answer

1. Data theft: Unauthorized access and theft of sensitive data.

2. Sabotage: Deliberately damaging or disrupting computer systems.

3. Overcharging: Charging clients for unnecessary repairs or services.

4. Misuse of resources: Using company resources for personal gain.

8 (b) (6 marks)

Peshà, an ICT Manager, intends to buy software for her organization. Explain three factors she should consider.

Answer

1. Compatibility: The software should be compatible with existing systems and hardware.

2. Cost: The total cost of ownership, including purchase, installation, and maintenance, should be within the budget.

3. Support: The availability of technical support and updates from the software vendor is crucial for long-term use.

8 (c) (4 marks)

Titus, a computer user, has been experiencing back pain as a result of using a computer. Outline four precautions that he could take to resolve this.

Answer

1. Ergonomic chair: Use an ergonomic chair that supports the lower back and promotes good posture.

2. Proper desk height: Ensure the desk is at the correct height to prevent slouching or straining.

3. Regular breaks: Take regular breaks to stretch and move around, reducing the risk of back pain.

4. Monitor position: Position the monitor at eye level to avoid neck strain and maintain a neutral spine position.

8 (d) (i) (2 marks)

Zawadi Company is planning to clean up the neighboring town as part of its social responsibility. Explain one importance of this exercise to the company.

Answer

1. Enhanced reputation: Engaging in social responsibility activities improves the company's public image and builds trust with the community.

8 (d) (ii) (4 marks)

Differentiate between distress and eustress as types of stress.

Answer

Distress: Distress is negative stress that can lead to anxiety, depression, and other health issues. It occurs when an individual feels overwhelmed and unable to cope with demands.

Eustress: Eustress is positive stress that motivates and energizes individuals. It occurs when challenges are perceived as opportunities for growth and achievement.

Key Differences: Distress is harmful and debilitating, while eustress is beneficial and motivating.
