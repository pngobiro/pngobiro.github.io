1. (a) (ii) (6 marks)

Annete a computer teacher noticed that a student in her class had a problem of low self esteem. Explain three causes of this problem.

Answer

Negative Feedback and Criticism: Consistent negative feedback or excessive criticism, especially in academic settings, can significantly erode a student's self-esteem. If a student frequently receives criticism without balanced positive reinforcement, they may internalize these negative messages and develop a belief in their inadequacy, leading to low self-esteem.

Social Comparison and Peer Pressure: Students often compare themselves to their peers, particularly in academic performance and social acceptance. If a student perceives themselves as falling short compared to their classmates, or if they experience negative peer pressure and social exclusion, it can lead to feelings of inadequacy and lower their self-esteem. Social dynamics in the classroom can significantly impact a student's self-perception.

Lack of Success and Achievements: A consistent lack of perceived success or achievements in academic tasks can contribute to low self-esteem. If a student struggles to meet academic expectations, faces repeated failures, or lacks opportunities to experience success, they may develop a diminished sense of competence and self-worth. Experiencing academic setbacks without adequate support to overcome them can be particularly damaging to self-esteem.

1. (b) (4 marks)

With the aid of a diagram, outline the parts of a computer floppy disk.

Answer

Parts of a Computer Floppy Disk:

A floppy disk, although largely obsolete, consists of several key parts:

Protective Case (Jacket): The outer square plastic casing protects the delicate magnetic disk inside from dust, scratches, and damage.

Magnetic Disk (Mylar Disk): A thin, circular piece of flexible plastic coated with magnetic material where data is stored. This is the core component that holds the magnetic patterns representing data.

Metal Hub: A small metal ring at the center of the disk that allows the drive motor to grip and rotate the disk.

Read/Write Notch (Write-Protect Notch): A small notch on the side of the disk. When uncovered, it allows data to be written to the disk; when covered, it write-protects the disk, preventing accidental data erasure or overwriting.

Head Access Window: An oval opening covered by a sliding metal shutter that allows the read/write head of the floppy drive to access the magnetic disk surface when in use.

Liner (Inner Lining): A soft fabric lining inside the jacket that helps to clean the disk surface and reduce friction as the disk rotates.

2. (a) (ii) (6 marks)

Differentiate between super computers and mainframe computers, citing an area of application in each case.

Answer

Supercomputers:

Supercomputers are the most powerful type of computers, designed for extremely high-speed processing and massive computational tasks. They are characterized by their ability to perform trillions of calculations per second (teraflops and petaflops). Supercomputers utilize parallel processing architectures with thousands of processors working in tandem to solve complex problems.

Area of Application: Scientific Research and Complex Simulations. Supercomputers are primarily used in scientific research, engineering, and areas requiring intensive computation, such as weather forecasting, climate modeling, nuclear research, drug discovery, and complex simulations like aerodynamic testing and quantum mechanics calculations. They are essential for pushing the boundaries of scientific and engineering knowledge.

Mainframe Computers:

Mainframe computers are large, powerful computers designed for processing very large volumes of data and handling thousands of transactions concurrently. While powerful, they are generally less computationally intensive than supercomputers but excel in reliability, security, and input/output operations for large-scale data processing and transaction management.

Area of Application: Enterprise Data Processing and Transaction Management. Mainframes are widely used by large organizations like banks, insurance companies, and government agencies for critical applications such as processing financial transactions, managing customer databases, handling airline reservations, and managing large-scale data warehousing. They are crucial for ensuring reliability and security in handling massive daily business operations.

Differentiation:

The primary differentiation lies in their processing focus and application. Supercomputers are optimized for maximum computational speed and are used for complex calculations and simulations in research and engineering. Mainframe computers are optimized for high-volume data processing, reliability, and transaction management in large business and governmental operations. Supercomputers prioritize raw processing power, while mainframes prioritize reliability and transaction throughput.

3. (a) (ii) (4 marks)

Differentiate between laser jet printer and a fax machine as used in ICT.

Answer

Laser Jet Printer:

A laser jet printer is an output device that produces high-quality printed documents by using a laser beam to fuse toner onto paper. It is primarily used for printing documents, text, and graphics from a computer. Laser jet printers are known for their speed, efficiency, and high print quality, especially for text-based documents.

Fax Machine:

A fax machine is a communication device used to transmit and receive documents over telephone lines. It scans a document, converts it into electrical signals, and transmits these signals over phone lines to another fax machine, which then prints a replica of the original document. Fax machines are used for transmitting hard copies of documents between locations via telephone networks.

Differentiation:

The key distinction lies in their primary function and technology. A laser jet printer is an output device for printing documents from a computer, using laser and toner technology. A fax machine is a communication device for transmitting and receiving documents over phone lines, using scanning and telecommunication technology. Laser jet printers produce documents locally, while fax machines transmit documents remotely.

3. (b) (6 marks)

Explain three measures that an organization should put in place to avoid unauthorized access to data.

Answer

Implement Strong Password Policies and Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA): Organizations should enforce strong password policies requiring complex passwords that are regularly changed. Implementing multi-factor authentication adds an extra layer of security by requiring users to provide multiple verification factors (e.g., password and a code from a mobile device) before granting access. These measures significantly reduce the risk of unauthorized access due to compromised or weak credentials.

Employ Access Control Lists (ACLs) and Role-Based Access Control (RBAC): Organizations should use Access Control Lists and Role-Based Access Control to manage and restrict data access. ACLs define permissions for specific files or resources, while RBAC assigns access rights based on job roles and responsibilities. By implementing these controls, organizations ensure that users only have access to the data necessary for their job functions, minimizing the potential for unauthorized data exposure or breaches.

Regular Security Audits and Monitoring: Conducting regular security audits and implementing continuous monitoring systems are crucial for detecting and preventing unauthorized access. Security audits assess vulnerabilities and compliance with security policies, while monitoring systems track user activities and system logs for suspicious behavior or unauthorized access attempts. Proactive monitoring and auditing enable timely detection and response to potential security threats and policy violations, enhancing overall data security.

3. (c) (4 marks)

Anna, a computer user is experiencing back and neck ache regularly as a result of using a computer for long hours. Identify four precautions that she should take to solve her problem.

Answer

Use an Ergonomic Chair: Anna should use an ergonomic chair that provides good lumbar support, adjustable height, and armrests. A proper chair promotes good posture and reduces strain on the back and neck during prolonged sitting.

Adjust Monitor Height and Position: Anna should adjust her monitor so that the top of the screen is at or slightly below eye level and position it directly in front of her. Correct monitor placement reduces neck strain by preventing her from tilting her head up or down for extended periods.

Take Regular Breaks and Stretch: Anna should take frequent breaks from computer work, ideally every 20-30 minutes, to stand up, walk around, and perform stretching exercises for her neck, back, and shoulders. Regular breaks and stretches help relieve muscle tension and improve circulation.

Maintain Proper Posture: Anna should consciously maintain good posture while sitting at her computer. This includes keeping her back straight, shoulders relaxed, feet flat on the floor or on a footrest, and avoiding slouching. Correct posture minimizes strain on the spine and neck muscles.

4. (d) (i) (2 marks)

Distinguish between each of the following computer devices: dot matrix and inkjet printer;

Answer

Dot Matrix Printer:

A dot matrix printer is an impact printer that creates characters and images by striking an ink-soaked ribbon against the paper using a matrix of pins. It is known for its low printing cost and ability to print on multi-part forms but produces lower print quality and is noisy.

Inkjet Printer:

An inkjet printer is a non-impact printer that creates images by spraying tiny droplets of ink onto paper. It is known for producing high-quality color and monochrome prints, being relatively quiet, and being versatile for various printing tasks in homes and offices.

Distinction:

The primary distinction lies in the printing mechanism. Dot matrix printers use impact printing with pins and a ribbon, while inkjet printers use non-impact printing by spraying ink droplets. Inkjet printers generally offer superior print quality and are quieter, but dot matrix printers are more durable and cost-effective for certain applications like multi-part forms.

4. (d) (ii) (2 marks)

Distinguish between each of the following computer devices: voice output and computer output on microfiche;

Answer

Voice Output (Audio Output):

Voice output, or audio output, is a form of computer output that presents information through sound. It uses speakers or headphones to convert digital audio signals into audible sound waves, allowing users to hear information, music, speech, or sound effects. Voice output is transient and intangible.

Computer Output on Microfiche:

Computer Output on Microfiche (COM) is a technology that records computer output directly onto microfilm or microfiche, which are small sheets of film containing miniaturized images of documents. Microfiche is a physical, archival form of output used for long-term storage of large volumes of documents in a compact format. It requires special equipment to read and view.

Distinction:

The key distinction is the output medium and form. Voice output is auditory and transient, using sound to convey information, while computer output on microfiche is visual and physical, using microfilm for archival storage of document images. Voice output is dynamic and for immediate consumption, whereas microfiche is static and for long-term preservation.

4. (d) (iii) (2 marks)

Distinguish between each of the following computer devices: zip disk and compact disk.

Answer

Zip Disk:

A Zip disk was a type of removable magnetic disk storage medium introduced by Iomega. It had a higher storage capacity than floppy disks (typically 100MB, 250MB, or 750MB) and required a dedicated Zip drive to read and write data. Zip disks were used for data backup, file transfer, and software distribution, offering more capacity than floppy disks but less than CDs or DVDs.

Compact Disk (CD):

A Compact Disk (CD) is an optical disc storage medium that stores digital data which is read using laser technology. CDs are widely used for audio CDs, software distribution (CD-ROM), and data storage (CD-R, CD-RW). CDs typically have a storage capacity of around 700MB and are read by CD-ROM drives.

Distinction:

The key distinction lies in the storage technology and capacity. Zip disks are magnetic storage media with higher capacity than floppy disks but lower than CDs, requiring a Zip drive. Compact Disks are optical storage media with a standard 700MB capacity, read by CD-ROM drives. CDs became more popular due to their larger storage capacity, lower cost, and wider compatibility compared to Zip disks.

6. (b) (4 marks)

Differentiate between RAM-and ROM as used in computers.

Answer

RAM (Random Access Memory):

RAM is volatile memory used for temporary storage of data and instructions that the CPU is currently processing. Data in RAM is lost when the computer is powered off. RAM is read-write memory, meaning data can be both written to and read from RAM. It is crucial for the computer's active operations, providing fast access to data needed for running applications and the operating system.

ROM (Read-Only Memory):

ROM is non-volatile memory used for permanent storage of data that is typically written during manufacturing and is not meant to be altered by the user. Data in ROM is retained even when power is off. ROM is primarily read-only, meaning data can be read from ROM but not easily written to or changed. It stores essential firmware, like BIOS, needed for booting up the computer.

Differentiation:

The key distinction lies in volatility and data modification. RAM is volatile, temporary, and read-write, used for active processing. ROM is non-volatile, permanent, and read-only (or mostly read-only), used for storing fixed, essential instructions. RAM is for working memory, while ROM is for permanent boot instructions.

Present User Interface: The VDU presents the graphical user interface (GUI) of the operating system and applications, allowing users to navigate, interact with software, and view program outputs through visual elements like windows, icons, and menus.

8. (d) (6 marks)

Nicholson bought a computer software package on a DVD which he intends to use in his computer. Explain three benefits of installing the software on the computer hard disk instead of running the software directly from the DVD.

Answer

Faster Software Loading and Execution: Installing software on the hard disk allows for significantly faster loading and execution times compared to running it directly from a DVD. Hard disks, especially Solid State Drives (SSDs), offer much quicker data access speeds than DVD-ROM drives. Software installed on the hard disk can be accessed and run almost instantaneously, enhancing performance and user experience.

Improved Software Responsiveness and Performance: Software runs more smoothly and responsively when installed on the hard disk. The faster data access reduces delays and lag associated with accessing data from a DVD, leading to improved software performance, smoother operation, and enhanced user interaction. Applications installed on the hard disk can utilize system resources more efficiently, resulting in better overall performance.

Reduced Wear and Tear on DVD-ROM Drive and DVD Media: Running software directly from a DVD requires constant reading from the DVD-ROM drive and continuous spinning of the DVD media. This constant activity can lead to increased wear and tear on both the DVD-ROM drive and the DVD disc itself, potentially shortening their lifespan. Installing software on the hard disk eliminates the need to continuously use the DVD-ROM drive and DVD, reducing hardware wear and extending the longevity of both the drive and media.
