1. (b) (4 marks)

Sawa Company Ltd. are experiencing internal hacking of their computer system. Outline four possible root causes of the problem.

Answer

Internal hacking at Sawa Company Ltd. suggests vulnerabilities within their system. Possible root causes include:

Weak Password Policies: Inadequate password policies, such as allowing employees to use weak or easily guessable passwords, can be a significant root cause. Weak passwords make it easier for internal individuals to gain unauthorized access to accounts and systems through password cracking or guessing.

Insider Threats (Malicious or Negligent Employees): Disgruntled or negligent employees with internal access can intentionally or unintentionally cause hacking incidents. Malicious insiders may seek to steal data or disrupt systems, while negligent employees may inadvertently compromise security through poor practices or lack of awareness.

Insufficient Access Controls: Lack of proper access controls, such as inadequate user permissions or failure to implement role-based access control, can allow employees excessive access to sensitive data and systems beyond their job requirements. Overly broad access permissions increase the risk of unauthorized data access and internal hacking.

Vulnerabilities in Internal Network Security: Weaknesses in the company's internal network security, such as unpatched systems, misconfigured firewalls, or lack of intrusion detection systems, can create opportunities for internal hacking. Internal network vulnerabilities can be exploited by individuals within the network to gain unauthorized access to systems and data.

1. (c) (ii) (4 marks)

Answer

(ii) Distinguish between stress and fatigue.

Stress: Stress is a state of mental or emotional strain or tension resulting from adverse or demanding circumstances. It is a psychological and physiological response to stressors, which can be external pressures or internal demands. Stress is characterized by feelings of being overwhelmed, anxious, or pressured, and can manifest in physical symptoms like headaches, muscle tension, and increased heart rate. Stress is often triggered by specific events or ongoing pressures.

Fatigue: Fatigue is a state of extreme tiredness, typically resulting from mental or physical exertion or illness. It is characterized by a feeling of weariness, lack of energy, and reduced ability to concentrate or perform tasks effectively. Fatigue can be physical, mental, or emotional and is often a consequence of prolonged activity, lack of sleep, or underlying health conditions. Fatigue is primarily a state of reduced energy and capacity for activity.

Answer

(c) (i) With the aid of diagrams in each case, explain two categories of computer networks based on how information is shared.

1. Client-Server Network:

Diagram: (A diagram showing a central Server connected to multiple Clients. Arrows depict requests from Clients to the Server and responses from the Server back to Clients.)

Explanation: In a client-server network, information sharing is centralized. A dedicated server stores and manages resources, data, and applications. Client computers request services or data from the server, and the server responds to these requests. Information is primarily shared from the server to clients, with clients relying on the server for resources. This model is efficient for managing shared resources and ensuring data consistency and security.

2. Peer-to-Peer Network:

Diagram: (A diagram showing multiple computers (Peers) directly connected to each other without a central server. Arrows indicate data sharing directly between peers.)

Explanation: In a peer-to-peer (P2P) network, information sharing is decentralized. Each computer (peer) can act as both a client and a server, sharing resources directly with other peers. Information is shared directly between connected computers without relying on a central server. P2P networks are suitable for small groups or direct file sharing, but lack centralized management and security.

Answer

(a) Differentiate between microcomputer and minicomputer.

Microcomputer: Microcomputers are small, relatively inexpensive computers that use a microprocessor as their central processing unit (CPU). They are designed for individual use or small-scale applications. Examples include personal computers (PCs), laptops, and workstations. Microcomputers are characterized by their affordability, compact size, and suitability for personal productivity and general-purpose computing tasks.

They were designed to support multiple users simultaneously and handle more demanding tasks than microcomputers. Minicomputers were historically used in departmental settings, small to medium-sized businesses, and for tasks like server applications and industrial process control, bridging the gap between personal and large-scale computing.

5. (a) (ii) (4 marks)

(ii) Distinguish between RAM and ROM as used in computers.

Answer

(ii) Distinguish between RAM and ROM as used in computers.

RAM (Random Access Memory): RAM is volatile memory used for temporary data storage that the CPU can access quickly for active processes. RAM is read-write memory, meaning data can be both written to and read from RAM. RAM is essential for running applications and operating systems, providing fast access to data currently in use. Data in RAM is lost when the computer is powered off.

ROM (Read Only Memory): ROM is non-volatile memory used for permanent or semi-permanent data storage that is typically read-only during normal operation. ROM primarily stores firmware, such as the BIOS or UEFI, which contains essential instructions for booting the computer and initializing hardware. Data in ROM is retained even when power is off, making it suitable for storing critical system startup instructions and firmware.

Functional Components of a System Unit:
