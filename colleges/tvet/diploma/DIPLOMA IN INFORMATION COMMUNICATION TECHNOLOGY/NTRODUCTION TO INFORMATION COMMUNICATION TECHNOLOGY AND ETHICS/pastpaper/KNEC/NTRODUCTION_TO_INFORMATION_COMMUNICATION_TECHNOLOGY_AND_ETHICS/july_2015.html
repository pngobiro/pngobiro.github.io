INTRODUCTION TO INFORMATION COMMUNICATION TECHNOLOGY AND ETHICS

Exam Code: 2920/101

Duration: 3 hours

Period: July 2015

1. (a) (4 marks)

Outline four advantages of secondary storage media over primary storage media in computer systems.

Answer

Non-volatility: Secondary storage retains data even when power is turned off, unlike primary storage (RAM) which is volatile.

Higher Capacity: Secondary storage media like hard drives and SSDs offer much larger storage capacities compared to primary storage, allowing for the storage of vast amounts of data.

Lower Cost per Bit: Secondary storage is significantly cheaper per unit of storage compared to primary storage, making it economical for bulk data storage.

Portability: Many secondary storage devices, such as USB drives and external hard drives, are portable, allowing for easy data transfer and backup across different systems.

1. (b) (6 marks)

With the aid of a diagram in each case, explain the difference between COM and VGA computer ports.

Answer

COM Port (Serial Port):

A COM port, or serial port, is an older type of port used for serial communication. Diagrammatically, a COM port is represented by a D-sub connector, typically with 9 pins (DB9) or 25 pins (DB25). In a diagram, it would show a trapezoidal shape with pins arranged in two or more rows. It transmits data one bit at a time, sequentially, making it slower than parallel ports but suitable for long-distance communication and simpler devices.

VGA Port (Video Graphics Array):

A VGA port is a standard port for video output from a computer to a monitor or projector. Diagrammatically, a VGA port is also represented by a D-sub connector, specifically a 15-pin connector arranged in three rows (DB15). In a diagram, it would show a similar trapezoidal shape but with 15 pins in a denser arrangement. It transmits analog video signals, using separate pins for red, green, blue color components, as well as synchronization signals.

Key Differences:

The primary difference lies in their function and communication method. COM ports are for serial data communication, typically for peripherals like modems or older mice, transmitting data bit by bit. VGA ports are specifically for video output, transmitting analog video signals to display devices. Visually, they can be distinguished by the number of pins and their internal wiring, although both use D-sub connectors. Modern computers are phasing out COM ports in favor of USB and other faster interfaces, while VGA is being replaced by digital video interfaces like HDMI and DisplayPort.

1. (c) (4 marks)

Distinguish between device driver and storage drive as used in computer software.

Answer

Device Driver:

A device driver is a software program that enables the operating system to interact with a hardware device. It acts as a translator between the operating system and the hardware. Without a device driver, the operating system would not be able to recognize and properly utilize the hardware, such as a printer, graphics card, or network adapter. Device drivers are specific to the operating system and the hardware they support.

Storage Drive:

A storage drive is a hardware component used for storing digital data. Examples include Hard Disk Drives (HDDs), Solid State Drives (SSDs), and USB flash drives. Storage drives physically store files, applications, and the operating system itself. They are accessed and managed by the operating system through file systems. Storage drives are characterized by their capacity, speed, and type of storage technology.

Distinction:

The key distinction is that a device driver is software that facilitates communication between the operating system and hardware, whereas a storage drive is the physical hardware itself responsible for data storage. Device drivers are essential for the operating system to utilize storage drives (and other hardware) effectively.

1. (d) (6 marks)

Paul attended a seminar that focused on the measures computer users could adopt in order to conserve the environment. Explain three of the measures that could have been discussed.

Answer

Power Management: Computer users can enable power-saving settings on their devices. This includes setting monitors to automatically turn off after a period of inactivity, utilizing sleep or hibernate modes for computers during breaks, and unplugging devices and chargers when not in use to reduce standby power consumption. These practices collectively minimize energy wastage and lower carbon footprint associated with electricity usage.

Responsible Disposal and Recycling of E-waste: Users should properly dispose of old or obsolete computer equipment and peripherals through certified e-waste recycling programs. E-waste contains hazardous materials that can harm the environment if improperly discarded in landfills. Recycling recovers valuable materials, reduces the need for new resource extraction, and prevents environmental contamination. Users should avoid throwing electronics in regular trash and seek out designated collection points or recycling facilities.

Promoting Paperless Practices: Computer users can reduce paper consumption by adopting digital workflows. This involves reading documents on-screen instead of printing, using digital note-taking and collaboration tools, and storing documents electronically rather than in hard copy. Reducing paper usage conserves forests, decreases the energy and resources needed for paper production, and minimizes waste generation. Encouraging digital communication and document management helps in moving towards a more sustainable, paper-light environment.

2. (b) (6 marks)

Sigils high school intends to introduce Internet facilities for students' academic work. Explain three negative impacts that the students could be exposed to.

Answer

Exposure to Inappropriate Content: Unfiltered internet access can expose students to harmful or inappropriate content, including pornography, violence, and hate speech. This can negatively impact their moral development, psychological well-being, and distract them from academic pursuits. Schools need to implement robust content filtering and monitoring systems to mitigate this risk, but complete prevention is challenging.

This can have severe emotional and psychological consequences for victims, leading to anxiety, depression, and decreased academic performance. Schools need to educate students about responsible online behavior, implement reporting mechanisms for cyberbullying, and have policies to address such incidents.

Distraction and Reduced Focus on Studies: The internet offers numerous distractions, including social media, online games, and entertainment websites. Students may spend excessive time on these non-academic activities, leading to decreased focus on their studies, procrastination, and lower grades. Balancing internet access for academic purposes with measures to prevent overuse and distraction is crucial. Schools might consider time limits, website blocking during study hours, and promoting digital literacy to encourage responsible internet use.

3. (c) (4 marks)

Distinguish between open and limited tender methods of computer hardware acquisition.

Answer

Open Tender:

Open tender, also known as public tender, is a procurement method where the invitation to bid for the supply of goods or services is advertised publicly and is open to all eligible suppliers. This method aims to maximize competition, ensure transparency, and achieve the best possible value for money. Any vendor meeting the specified criteria can submit a bid. It is commonly used for government and public sector procurements to ensure fairness and accountability.

Limited Tender:

Limited tender, also known as restricted tender or selective tender, is a procurement method where the invitation to bid is sent only to a select list of pre-qualified or known suppliers. This method is used when there are a limited number of suppliers capable of providing the required goods or services, or when time is of the essence, or for reasons of confidentiality or security. It is less transparent than open tendering but can be more efficient in specific situations.

Distinction:

The key distinction is that open tender is public and open to all eligible bidders, promoting wide competition, whereas limited tender is restricted to a select group of suppliers, often used for specialized needs or when efficiency and speed are prioritized over broad competition.

3. (a) (4 marks)

Answer

Requirement Analysis: A systems analyst gathers and analyzes the information needs of an organization. This involves understanding the business processes, identifying problems, and determining the specific requirements for an information system to address those needs.

4. (c) (4 marks)

Distinguish between Wi-Fi and bluetooth as used in computer networks.

Answer

Wi-Fi (Wireless Fidelity):

Wi-Fi is a wireless networking technology that allows devices to connect to the internet or a local network wirelessly. It is based on the IEEE 802.11 standards and operates over longer ranges, typically tens to hundreds of meters. Wi-Fi is designed for higher bandwidth and is primarily used for networking computers, smartphones, and other devices to access the internet or share resources within a network.

Bluetooth:

Bluetooth is a wireless technology standard used for exchanging data over short distances (typically up to 10 meters) using short-wavelength UHF radio waves. It is primarily designed for creating personal area networks (PANs) to connect devices like headphones, keyboards, mice, and smartphones for data exchange or control. Bluetooth is characterized by its low power consumption and simplicity in pairing devices.

Distinction:

The key distinction lies in their range, bandwidth, and primary use cases. Wi-Fi is for longer range, higher bandwidth networking and internet access, while Bluetooth is for short-range, lower bandwidth connections between personal devices, often emphasizing low power consumption and ease of pairing.

4. (a) (4 marks)

Outline four measures that should be put in place to enhance data security in a computer system.

Answer

Implement Strong Access Controls: Employ robust user authentication methods such as strong passwords, multi-factor authentication, and biometric logins. Restrict user access based on the principle of least privilege, ensuring users only have access to the data and resources necessary for their roles. Regularly review and update access permissions to prevent unauthorized data access.

Utilize Data Encryption: Encrypt sensitive data both in transit and at rest. Use encryption protocols like TLS/SSL for network communication and encrypt hard drives and databases to protect data even if physical storage is compromised. Encryption makes data unreadable to unauthorized individuals, safeguarding confidentiality.

Regular Data Backups and Disaster Recovery: Implement a consistent data backup schedule to create copies of critical data. Store backups securely and ideally offsite to protect against data loss from hardware failures, natural disasters, or cyberattacks. Develop and regularly test a disaster recovery plan to ensure quick data restoration and business continuity in case of data loss incidents.

Install and Maintain Firewalls and Intrusion Detection Systems: Deploy firewalls to monitor and control network traffic, blocking unauthorized access and malicious intrusions. Implement intrusion detection systems (IDS) to actively monitor network and system activities for suspicious behavior and potential security breaches. Regularly update firewall rules and IDS signatures to protect against new threats.

5. (c) (6 marks)

Zippy prefers to install a country wide wireless communication network over the wired media. Explain three circumstances that could have led to her preference.

Answer

Geographical Coverage and Scalability: For a country-wide network, wireless communication offers superior geographical coverage and scalability compared to wired media. Deploying physical cables across an entire country is extremely expensive, time-consuming, and faces significant logistical challenges, especially in remote or difficult terrains. Wireless networks, using technologies like cellular or satellite, can cover vast areas and easily scale to accommodate more users and locations without the need for extensive physical infrastructure. This makes wireless a more practical and cost-effective option for broad geographical reach.

Mobility and Flexibility Requirements: Wireless networks inherently support mobility and flexibility, which are crucial for nationwide communication. Users can connect from various locations across the country without being tethered to physical cables. This is essential for mobile workforces, field operations, and public access. Wired networks lack this inherent mobility, restricting connectivity to fixed locations. If Zippy's needs involve supporting a mobile user base or providing communication across diverse and changing locations, wireless is the more suitable choice.

Rapid Deployment and Reduced Initial Infrastructure Costs: Setting up a wireless network, especially utilizing existing cellular or satellite infrastructure, can be significantly faster and less expensive in initial infrastructure costs compared to laying down a wired network across a country. Wired networks require extensive civil works, trenching, and cable installation, which are time-consuming and capital-intensive. Wireless deployment can leverage existing towers and infrastructure, reducing the initial investment and speeding up the network setup process. This rapid deployment capability and lower initial cost can be a major factor in choosing wireless for a country-wide network.

6. (c) (4 marks)

Distinguish between broadcast and point-to-point as used in computer networks.

Answer

Broadcast Communication:

In broadcast communication, data is transmitted from a single sender to all devices on the network simultaneously. When a device sends a broadcast message, every other device on the network receives and processes it. This is commonly used in local area networks (LANs), such as Ethernet networks, for tasks like address resolution (ARP) where a device needs to find the MAC address associated with an IP address and sends a broadcast request to all devices on the network.

Point-to-Point Communication:

In point-to-point communication, data is transmitted directly between two specific devices on a network. There is a dedicated communication channel established between the sender and receiver. This type of communication is used for one-to-one data transfer, such as when a client requests a webpage from a web server or when transferring a file between two computers. Examples include direct cable connections or virtual private network (VPN) tunnels.

Distinction:

The key distinction is in the communication scope. Broadcast is one-to-all, where a single transmission reaches every device on the network, while point-to-point is one-to-one, involving a direct and dedicated link between two specific devices for data exchange.

8. (c) (6 marks)

Computer users experience different physical injuries during work. Explain three of these injuries with a possible remedy for each.

Answer

Carpal Tunnel Syndrome (CTS): CTS is a condition caused by compression of the median nerve in the wrist, often due to repetitive hand and wrist movements like typing. Symptoms include pain, numbness, tingling, and weakness in the hand and fingers. Remedy: Ergonomic keyboard and mouse setup, wrist rests, frequent breaks to stretch and rest hands, and in severe cases, medical treatment like wrist splints or surgery.

Computer Vision Syndrome (CVS) or Eye Strain: CVS is a group of eye and vision problems resulting from prolonged computer, tablet, e-reader, and cell phone use. Symptoms include eyestrain, headaches, blurred vision, dry eyes, and neck/shoulder pain. Remedy: Follow the 20-20-20 rule (every 20 minutes, look at something 20 feet away for 20 seconds), adjust screen brightness and contrast, use proper lighting to reduce glare, and ensure correct monitor distance and height. Regular eye exams are also important.

Back Pain (Lower Back Pain): Prolonged sitting in poor posture while using computers can lead to back pain, particularly in the lower back. Poor chair ergonomics, lack of lumbar support, and extended periods of sitting contribute to muscle strain and discomfort. Remedy: Use an ergonomic chair with good lumbar support, adjust chair height to ensure feet are flat on the floor or use a footrest, maintain good posture while sitting, take regular breaks to stand, stretch, and walk around, and ensure the monitor is at eye level to prevent neck and back strain.

Gova intends to purchase computers for his company. Explain three factors that he should consider.

Answer

Performance Requirements and Intended Use: Gova should first assess the performance needs of the computers based on their intended use within the company. This involves considering the types of tasks employees will perform, such as word processing, data analysis, graphic design, software development, or running specialized applications. Performance factors like processor speed, RAM capacity, storage type (SSD or HDD), and graphics capabilities should be aligned with these requirements to ensure the computers can handle the workload efficiently. Over-specifying or under-specifying can lead to unnecessary costs or performance bottlenecks.

Budget and Total Cost of Ownership (TCO): Gova needs to establish a budget for purchasing computers and consider the total cost of ownership beyond the initial purchase price. TCO includes factors like software licensing costs, maintenance, support, energy consumption, and potential upgrade costs over the computer's lifecycle. Balancing upfront costs with long-term operational expenses is crucial. Exploring options like leasing or purchasing refurbished computers might be considered to manage budget constraints, while ensuring the chosen option meets performance and reliability needs.

Compatibility, Scalability, and Manageability: Gova should ensure that the new computers are compatible with the company's existing IT infrastructure, software applications, and peripherals. Compatibility issues can lead to integration problems and increased IT support costs. Scalability is also important; the chosen computers should be able to accommodate future growth and changing business needs. Furthermore, consider manageability features, such as remote management capabilities, centralized software deployment, and ease of maintenance, which can simplify IT administration and reduce ongoing support overhead, especially for a larger number of computers.

8. (a) (2 marks)

Explain the term corporate social responsibility as used in computer ethics.

Answer

Corporate Social Responsibility (CSR) in computer ethics refers to a company's commitment to operate in an ethical and sustainable manner in relation to its use and impact of information and communication technologies. It involves considering the broader societal and environmental consequences of their ICT practices beyond legal and profit obligations, encompassing areas like data privacy, digital inclusion, environmental sustainability, and responsible innovation.

8. (b) (i) (4 marks)

Explain two factors that are usually considered when accessing data on a hard disk.

Answer

Seek Time: Seek time is the time it takes for the hard drive's read/write head to move across the platters to the correct track where the requested data is located. A lower seek time results in faster data access. Factors affecting seek time include the physical distance the head needs to travel and the drive's actuator arm speed. Seek time is a significant factor in overall hard drive performance, especially for random data access patterns.

Latency (Rotational Latency): Latency, or rotational latency, is the time it takes for the desired sector on the hard disk platter to rotate under the read/write head once the head is positioned on the correct track. It depends on the rotational speed of the disk (measured in RPM - Revolutions Per Minute). Higher RPM drives have lower latency and thus faster data access. Average latency is typically half of the time for a full rotation. Latency is another key factor contributing to the overall data access speed of a hard disk.

8. (b) (ii) (4 marks)

With the aid of an example in each case, distinguish between general and special purpose software.

Answer

General Purpose Software:

General purpose software is designed to perform a wide variety of tasks and is applicable to many different users and situations. It is versatile and can be used for broad categories of tasks.

Example: Microsoft Word is a general-purpose word processing software. It can be used by students, professionals, writers, and anyone needing to create, edit, and format text documents for various purposes like writing essays, reports, letters, or books.

Special Purpose Software:

Special purpose software is designed to perform a specific task or set of tasks and is tailored for a particular industry, profession, or application. It is not intended for general use but rather for specialized needs.

Example: AutoCAD is special-purpose Computer-Aided Design (CAD) software. It is specifically designed for architects, engineers, and designers to create precise 2D and 3D drawings and models for building design, mechanical engineering, and other specialized design applications. It is not intended for general document creation or spreadsheet tasks.

Distinction:

The main distinction lies in their scope of application. General purpose software is versatile and broadly applicable, while special purpose software is narrowly focused and designed for specific, often specialized, tasks or industries.
