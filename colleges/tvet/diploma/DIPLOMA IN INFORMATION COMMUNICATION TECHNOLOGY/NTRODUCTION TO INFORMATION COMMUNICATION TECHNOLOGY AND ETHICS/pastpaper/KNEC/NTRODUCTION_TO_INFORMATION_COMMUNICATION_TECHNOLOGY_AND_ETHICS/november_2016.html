1. (a) (4 marks)

Outline four characteristics of third generation computers.

Answer

Third generation computers, utilizing integrated circuits, exhibited notable characteristics.

Smaller Size and Compactness: Integrated circuits replaced transistors, leading to significantly smaller and more compact computers compared to second-generation machines. Miniaturization was a key feature.

Increased Reliability: Integrated circuits improved reliability as they contained many components in a single chip, reducing interconnections and failure points, leading to more stable operation.

Lower Power Consumption and Heat Generation: Integrated circuits consumed less power and generated less heat than transistors, contributing to energy efficiency and reducing the need for extensive cooling systems.

Faster Processing Speed: Processing speeds increased in third-generation computers due to the faster switching speeds of integrated circuits, enabling more complex computations and improved performance.

1. (c) (i) (4 marks)

Explain two benefits of installing a UPS in a company's computer system.

Answer

Installing an Uninterruptible Power Supply (UPS) in a company's computer system provides critical benefits for power protection and business continuity.

Protection Against Power Outages and Data Loss: A UPS provides backup power during electrical outages, allowing computer systems to continue running for a limited time. This prevents abrupt system shutdowns and data loss that can occur during power failures, safeguarding critical data and ensuring business operations can continue uninterrupted during brief outages.

Protection Against Power Surges and Voltage Fluctuations: UPS units also protect computer equipment from power surges, voltage spikes, and fluctuations. These power anomalies can damage sensitive electronic components and shorten hardware lifespan. UPS systems regulate power flow, providing clean and stable power to connected devices, protecting hardware investment and enhancing system reliability.

1. (d) (i) (4 marks)

A programmer developed a program using an assembly language. (i) Describe two characteristics that this program should exhibit.

Answer

A program developed using assembly language typically exhibits specific characteristics due to the nature of assembly programming.

Machine-Specific and Hardware-Dependent: Assembly language programs are machine-specific and directly correspond to the architecture of a particular processor or computer hardware. They are not portable across different hardware platforms without significant rewriting, as assembly code is closely tied to the instruction set and registers of the target processor.

High Performance and Efficiency: Assembly language programs, when written effectively, can achieve high performance and efficiency in terms of execution speed and resource utilization. Because assembly language provides direct control over hardware and low-level operations, programmers can optimize code for specific tasks, achieving maximum performance for critical sections of code or resource-constrained environments.

2. (a) (4 marks)

Outline four roles of a system designers in system development.

Answer

System designers play crucial roles in the system development process, shaping the architecture and functionality of the system.

Requirement Analysis and Definition: System designers analyze and interpret user requirements, translating them into detailed system specifications and design documents. They define the system's functional and non-functional requirements, ensuring a clear understanding of what the system needs to achieve and how it should operate.

System Architecture and Design: Designers create the overall architecture and design of the system, specifying components, modules, interfaces, and data flow. They decide on the system's structure, technologies to be used, and how different parts will interact to meet the defined requirements. Architectural design is crucial for system scalability, performance, and maintainability.

Database Design and Data Modeling: System designers are responsible for designing the database schema and data models for the system. They define data structures, relationships, and storage mechanisms to ensure efficient data management, integrity, and accessibility. Effective database design is essential for data-centric applications.

Interface Design and User Experience: Designers contribute to the user interface (UI) and user experience (UX) design of the system. They specify UI layouts, navigation, and interaction patterns to ensure the system is user-friendly, intuitive, and meets user needs effectively. Good interface design enhances usability and user satisfaction.

2. (b) (4 marks)

Distinguish between tailor made and off-the-shelf software acquisition methods.

Answer

Tailor-made and off-the-shelf software acquisition methods represent contrasting approaches to obtaining software solutions, differing in customization and development.

Tailor-made Software (Custom Software): Tailor-made software, also known as custom software, is developed specifically for a particular organization or user to meet their unique and specific requirements. It is designed and built from scratch, addressing the precise needs and workflows of the client. Tailor-made software offers high customization but typically involves higher development costs, longer timelines, and is exclusive to the commissioning organization.

Off-the-shelf Software (Packaged Software): Off-the-shelf software is pre-developed, ready-made software that is available for purchase or licensing to the general public. It is designed to meet common needs and is not customized for a specific user. Off-the-shelf software is generally more cost-effective, readily available, and quicker to implement but offers limited customization options and may not fully align with unique organizational requirements.

4. (b) (6 marks)

With the aid of a diagram in each case, describe the following networks: (i) peer-to-peer; (ii) client-server.

Answer

(i) Peer-to-Peer Network:

Diagram: (A simple diagram showing several computers connected directly to each other, forming a mesh-like structure without a central server. Arrows indicating direct connections between each computer.)

Description: In a peer-to-peer (P2P) network, computers (peers) are directly connected to each other without relying on a central server. Client-server networks offer centralized management, security, and resource sharing, suitable for larger organizations and applications requiring centralized control and service delivery.

7. (a) (4 marks)

Explain two functions of the control unit of a computer system.

Answer

The control unit (CU) is a key component of the CPU, responsible for coordinating and controlling computer operations. Two primary functions are:

Instruction Fetch and Decode: The control unit fetches instructions from memory, retrieves them from the instruction queue or memory address specified by the program counter. It then decodes these instructions to understand the operation to be performed and the operands involved. Instruction fetch and decode are the initial steps in the instruction execution cycle, managed by the CU.

Instruction Execution Control: The control unit generates control signals to direct other components of the CPU and computer system to execute the decoded instructions. It coordinates the activities of the arithmetic logic unit (ALU), registers, memory, and input/output devices to carry out the operations specified by each instruction. Instruction execution control ensures the proper sequencing and coordination of operations to execute programs correctly.

7. (c) (4 marks)

Differentiate between a magnetic strip reader and a chip reader input devices.

Answer

Magnetic strip readers and chip readers are both input devices for reading card-based data, but they utilize different technologies and data storage methods.

Magnetic Strip Reader: A magnetic strip reader reads data encoded on a magnetic stripe embedded in cards, such as credit cards, debit cards, and ID cards. It works by swiping the card through the reader, which magnetically reads the encoded data from the stripe. Magnetic stripe readers are commonly used for point-of-sale (POS) systems, access control, and identification purposes. They are relatively simple and cost-effective but offer lower data storage capacity and security compared to chip readers.

Chip Reader (Smart Card Reader): A chip reader, also known as a smart card reader, reads data from integrated circuit chips embedded in cards, such as EMV chip cards and smart ID cards. It requires inserting the card into the reader or tapping a contactless card. Chip readers communicate with the chip on the card to read and process data, enabling more complex transactions and enhanced security features like encryption and authentication. Chip readers are used for secure payments, identification, and access control applications where higher security and data processing capabilities are required.

Data Privacy and Protection: Training should cover principles of data privacy, data protection regulations (e.g., GDPR, CCPA), and ethical handling of personal and sensitive information. Employees should learn about data confidentiality, data security measures, responsible data collection, and data usage practices. 8. (c) (6 marks)

Brighters Company Limited is in the process of developing an ICT policy for its Information system. Describe three issues that would be necessary to be included in this policy.

Answer

Brighters Company Limited's ICT policy should address critical issues to guide responsible and secure use of its information systems. Essential issues to include are:

Acceptable Use Policy (AUP): The ICT policy must include an Acceptable Use Policy (AUP) that defines permissible and prohibited uses of company ICT resources, including internet, email, software, and hardware. AUP should outline guidelines on appropriate online behavior, data usage, software usage, and consequences of policy violations. A clear AUP sets expectations for responsible ICT usage by employees.

Data Security and Privacy: The policy must address data security and privacy measures to protect organizational and personal data. It should outline data security protocols, access control policies, data encryption practices, data breach response procedures, and compliance with data protection regulations. Data security and privacy are paramount, requiring comprehensive policy guidelines.

Password Management and Authentication: The ICT policy should include password management and authentication guidelines to ensure secure access to systems and data. It should specify password complexity requirements, password change frequency, secure authentication methods (e.g., MFA), and rules for handling user credentials. Strong password management and authentication protocols are essential for access control and preventing unauthorized system access.

8. (d) (6 marks)

Goldie IT Company intends to reach out to the society as part of their Corporate Social Responsibility. Explain three challenges that the company is likely to encounter.

Answer

Goldie IT Company's Corporate Social Responsibility (CSR) initiative to reach out to society is commendable but may face certain challenges during implementation.

Resource Constraints and Budget Limitations: CSR initiatives require financial resources, staff time, and logistical support. Goldie IT Company may face resource constraints and budget limitations in allocating sufficient funds and resources to effectively implement and sustain their CSR program. Balancing CSR activities with core business objectives and financial sustainability can be a challenge.

Measuring Impact and Demonstrating ROI: Quantifying the impact of CSR initiatives and demonstrating a clear Return on Investment (ROI) can be challenging. Measuring social impact, community benefits, or long-term outcomes of CSR programs may be complex and difficult to assess quantitatively. Demonstrating tangible results and communicating the value of CSR efforts to stakeholders can be a challenge for the company.

Community Engagement and Stakeholder Alignment: Effectively engaging with the community and aligning CSR initiatives with community needs and stakeholder expectations can be challenging. Understanding community priorities, building trust, and ensuring CSR programs are relevant and impactful to the target community requires careful planning and stakeholder engagement. Misaligned CSR efforts or ineffective community engagement can lead to limited impact and stakeholder dissatisfaction.
