There are Four methods to solve a system of equations:

  1. 1.Graphing
  2. 2.Substitution
  3. 3.Elimination or addition method
  4. 4.Matrices

For example

$$6 x+3 y=12$$
$$5 x+y=7$$

üìä Method 1 - Graphing

To solve by graphing, the two lines must be graphed on the same rectangular co-ordinate system. To graph each line, you need to find at least two points on each line. Set up an $x$ and $y$ charts on each. The points on which the two graphs intersect is the solution.

üîÑ Method 2 - Substitution

To solve by substitution, select one equation (it does not matter which of the equation) and solve for one of the variables (it does not matter which of the variables). Then substitute that expression into the other equation.

For $6 x+3 y=12$ I am going to solve for $Y$ (why?, because I want to)

$$6 x+3 y=12$$

$(6 x+3 y)-6 x=12-6 x$ therefore $3 y=-6 x+12$

$3 y / 3=-(6 x+12) / 3$ hence $y=-2 x+4$

Since I solved for y , I am going to use this to substitute into the y variable of the second equation

For $5 \mathrm{x}+\mathrm{y}=7$

$5 x+(-2 x+4)=7$ now solve for $X$

$5 x-2 x+4=7 \Rightarrow 3 x+4=7 \Rightarrow 3 x=3 \Rightarrow x=1$

Now that we know what $x$ is, use this to solve for $y$ by using one of the original equations (it does no matter which of the equations)

I'll use $6 x+3 y=12$ (why?, because I want to)

$6 x+3 y=12 \Rightarrow 6(1)+3 y=12 \Rightarrow 6+3 y=12 \Rightarrow 3 y=12-6 \Rightarrow 3 y=6$ therefore $y=6 / 3=2$

Therefore, our answer is $(1,2)$ which is our point of intersection.

‚ûï Method 3 - Elimination or Addition method

The objective of elimination method is to add the equations such that like terms add up to zero. If no like terms add up to zero, the multiplication is used to alter the system of equations such that like terms do add up to zero.

If the equation $6 x+3 y=12$ were added now, no like terms world add up to zero

$$5 x+y=7$$
$$11 x+4 y=19$$

Therefore multiplication is required to alter the equation. To do this legally, remember that whatever you do on one side of the equation you must do the same on the other side of the equation.

To eliminate a variable, do the following

  1. 1.Decide which variable to eliminate(it does not matter which of the equation)
$$\begin{aligned} & 6 x+3 y=12 \quad \text { I'll pick the } X \text { term (why?, because I want to) } \\ & 5 x+y=7 \end{aligned}$$
  1. 2.Looking at the variable from each equation, find the least common multiple of the coefficients ( X and Y )
$$\begin{aligned} & 6 x+3 y=12 \\ & 5 x+y=7 \end{aligned}$$

The least common multiple of 5 and 6 is 30

  1. 3.Now multiply each equation by the number which will make the co-efficient on the $x$ variable 30
$$\begin{aligned} & 5[6 x+3 y=12] \\ & 6[5 x+y=7] \end{aligned} \quad \begin{aligned} & 30 x+15 y=60 \\ & 30 x+6 y=42 \end{aligned}$$

Notice that if this equations were added, the X terms would still not add up to zero, therefore one of the factors, 5 or 6 , which was used to multiply with the equations must be negative, I'll chose 6 to be negative (why?, because I want to)

  1. 4.Now substitute this value into one of the original equations(it does not matter which of the equation) to solve for $X$

$6 x+3 y=12$ (why? because I want to)

$6 x+3(2)=12 \Rightarrow 6 x+6=12 \Rightarrow 6 x=6 \Rightarrow x=1$

Therefore our answer is $(1,2)$ which happens to be the point of intersection.

Example 1

EQUATIONS

  • ‚Ä¢$x+y+z=6$

  • ‚Ä¢$2 x+5 y-z=27$

A Matrix is an array of numbers, right?

$$\left[\begin{array}{ccc} 6 & 4 & 24 \\ 1 & -9 & 8 \end{array}\right]$$

A Matrix

Well, think about the equations:

$$\begin{aligned} x+y+z & =6 \\ 2 y+5 z & =-4 \\ 2 x+5 y-z & =27 \end{aligned}$$

They could be turned into a table of numbers like this:

111=6
025=-4
25-1=27

We could even separate the numbers before and after the " $=$ " into:

1116
025and-4
25-127

Now it looks like we have 2 Matrices.

In fact we have a third one, which is [ x y z], and the way that matrices are, we need to set it up like this:

And we know that $\mathrm{x}+\mathrm{y}+\mathrm{z}=6$, etc, so we can write the system of equations like this:

$$\left[\begin{array}{rrr} 1 & 1 & 1 \\ 0 & 2 & 5 \\ 2 & 5 & -1 \end{array}\right]\left[\begin{array}{l} x \\ y \\ z \end{array}\right]=\left[\begin{array}{r} 6 \\ -4 \\ 27 \end{array}\right]$$

Pretty neat, hey?

The Matrix Solution

We can call the matrices " A ", " X " and " B " and the equation becomes:

$$\mathrm{AX}=\mathrm{B}$$

Where A is the $3 x 3$ matrix of $x, y$ and $z$ coefficients X is $x, y$ and $z$, and B is $6,-4$ and 27

Then (as shown on the Inverse of a Matrix page) the solution is this:

$$\mathrm{X}=\mathrm{A}^{-1} \mathrm{~B}$$

(Assuming we can calculate the Inverse Matrix $\mathrm{A}^{-1}$ )

Then multiply $\mathrm{A}^{-1}$ by B (you can use the Matrix Calculator again):

$$\left[\begin{array}{l} x \\ y \\ z \end{array}\right]=\frac{1}{-21}\left[\begin{array}{rrr} -27 & 6 & 3 \\ 10 & -3 & -5 \\ -4 & -3 & 2 \end{array}\right]\left[\begin{array}{r} 6 \\ -4 \\ 27 \end{array}\right]=\frac{1}{-21}\left[\begin{array}{r} -105 \\ -63 \\ 42 \end{array}\right]=\left[\begin{array}{r} 5 \\ 3 \\ -2 \end{array}\right]$$

And we are done! The solution is:

$$x=5, y=3 \text { and } z=-2$$

üìñ Definition of Matrix

A matrix is a collection of numbers arranged into a fixed number of rows and columns. Usually the numbers are real numbers. In general, matrices can contain complex numbers but we won't see those here. Here is an example of a matrix with three rows and three columns:

The top row is row 1 . The leftmost column is column 1. This matrix is a $3 \times 3$ matrix because it has three rows and three columns. In describing matrices, the format is:

Rows X columns

Each number that makes up a matrix is called an element of the matrix. The elements in a matrix have specific locations.

The upper left corner of the matrix is row 1 column 1 . In the above matrix the element at row 1 col 1 is the value 1 . The element at row 2 column 3 is the value 4.6 .

üìè Matrix Dimensions

The numbers of rows and columns of a matrix are called its dimensions. Here is a matrix with three rows and two columns:

$$\left(\begin{array}{ll} 1 & 4 \\ 2 & 5 \\ 3 & 6 \end{array}\right)_{3 \times 2}$$

Sometimes the dimensions are written off to the side of the matrix, as in the above matrix. But this is just a little reminder and not actually part of the matrix. Here is a matrix with different dimensions. It has two rows and three columns. This is a different "data type" than the previous matrix

$$\left[\begin{array}{lll} 1 & -2 & 3 \\ 0 & 8 & 4.6 \\ 4 & -1 & 0 \end{array}\right] \quad 2 \times 3$$
