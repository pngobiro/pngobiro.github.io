CHAPTER 8: MEASURES OF CENTRAL TENDENCY

üéØ Introduction to central tendency

A central tendency (or, more commonly, a measure of central tendency) is a central value or a typical value for a probability distribution. It is occasionally called an average or just the center of the distribution. The most common measures of central tendency are the arithmetic mean, the median and the mode.

‚ú® The significance property of measures of central tendency

  • ‚Ä¢It represents a single score around which the center of the entire distribution tends to be located.
  • ‚Ä¢It conveys us the shape and nature of the distribution of data.
  • ‚Ä¢It condenses the data to a single value.
  • ‚Ä¢It enhances the comparison between data.

üìä Types and measures of central tendency

The most commonly used measures of central tendency are:

  • ‚Ä¢Mean
  • ‚Ä¢Median
  • ‚Ä¢Mode
MeasureDescription
Mean:Average.
The sum of a set of data divided by the number of data.
(Do not round your answer unless directed to do so.)
Median:The middle value, or the mean of the middle two values, when the data is arranged in numerical order. Think of a "median" being in the middle of a highway.
Mode:The value ( number) that appears the most.
It is possible to have more than one mode, and it is possible to have no mode. If there is no mode-write "no mode", do not write zero (0).

Variance and Standard Deviation

The formulae for the variance and standard deviation are given below. $\mu$ means the mean of the data.

Variance = $\sigma^{2}=\frac{\Sigma\left(\mathrm{X}_{\mathrm{r}}-\mu\right)^{2}}{n}$

The standard deviation, $\sigma$, is the square root of the variance.

What the formula means:

  1. (1)$x_{r}-\mu$ means take each value in turn and subtract the mean from each value.
  2. (2)$\left(x_{r}-\mu\right)^{2}$ means square each of the results obtained from step (1). This is to get rid of any minus signs.
  3. (3)$\Sigma\left(\mathrm{x}_{\mathrm{r}}-\mu\right)^{2}$ means add up all of the results obtained from step (2).
  4. (4)Divide step (3) by $n$, which is the number of numbers
  5. (5)For the standard deviation, square root the answer to step (4).

Example

Find the variance and standard deviation of the following numbers: $1, 3, 5, 5, 6, 7, 9, 10$.

The mean = $46 / 8=5.75$

Grouped Data

There are many ways of writing the formula for the standard deviation. The one above is for a basic list of numbers. The formula for the variance when the data is grouped is as follows. The standard deviation can be found by taking the square root of this value.

$$\begin{aligned} & \text { Mean }=\frac{\sum f \mathrm{x}}{\sum \mathrm{f}} \\ & \text { Variance, } \sigma^{2}=\frac{\sum \mathrm{fx}^{2}}{\sum \mathrm{f}}-\left(\frac{\sum \mathrm{fx}}{\Sigma \mathrm{f}}\right)^{2} \end{aligned}$$
