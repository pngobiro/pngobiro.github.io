Question 1 (20 marks)

(a) Explain each of the following measurement scales as used in the classification of statistical data:

(i) nominal scale; (2 marks)

(ii) ordinal scale; (2 marks)

(iii) interval scale; (2 marks)

(iv) ratio scale. (2 marks)

(b) Given two polynomial functions: $y=16x-x^2-13$, and $y=2x+11$:

(i) Determine by calculation the coordinates of their points of intersection; (6 marks)

(ii) Determine the surface area enclosed by the two lines. (4 marks)

Answer

Disclaimer: Answers are AI-generated and may contain errors. Verify independently.

(a) Measurement Scales:

(i) Nominal Scale: Classifies data into distinct categories without any order or ranking. Examples: Gender (Male, Female), Colors (Red, Blue, Green).

(ii) Ordinal Scale: Classifies data into categories that have a meaningful order or rank, but the intervals between ranks are not necessarily equal. Examples: Education Level (High School, Bachelor's, Master's), Satisfaction Rating (Poor, Fair, Good, Excellent).

(iii) Interval Scale: Data has a meaningful order, and the intervals between values are equal and meaningful. However, there is no true zero point. Examples: Temperature in Celsius or Fahrenheit, Calendar Years.

(iv) Ratio Scale: Data has a meaningful order, equal intervals, and a true zero point, indicating the absence of the quantity being measured. Ratios between values are meaningful. Examples: Height, Weight, Age, Income.

(b) Polynomial Functions Intersection and Area:

(i) Points of Intersection: Set the equations equal to find common x-values:

$16x - x^2 - 13 = 2x + 11$

$0 = x^2 - 14x + 24$

Factor the quadratic equation:

$0 = (x - 2)(x - 12)$

The intersection occurs at $x = 2$ and $x = 12$.

Find the corresponding y-values using $y = 2x + 11$:

If $x = 2$, $y = 2(2) + 11 = 4 + 11 = 15$. Point: (2, 15)

If $x = 12$, $y = 2(12) + 11 = 24 + 11 = 35$. Point: (12, 35)

The points of intersection are (2, 15) and (12, 35).

(ii) Area Enclosed: The area between the curves $y_1 = 16x - x^2 - 13$ (parabola opening downwards) and $y_2 = 2x + 11$ (line) from $x=2$ to $x=12$ is found by integrating the difference between the upper curve ($y_1$) and the lower curve ($y_2$).

Area $= \int_{2}^{12} (y_1 - y_2) dx$

Area $= \int_{2}^{12} ((16x - x^2 - 13) - (2x + 11)) dx$

Area $= \int_{2}^{12} (-x^2 + 14x - 24) dx$

Integrate term by term:

Area $= \left[ -\frac{x^3}{3} + \frac{14x^2}{2} - 24x \right]_{2}^{12}$

Area $= \left[ -\frac{x^3}{3} + 7x^2 - 24x \right]_{2}^{12}$

Evaluate at the limits:

At $x=12$: $-\frac{12^3}{3} + 7(12^2) - 24(12) = -\frac{1728}{3} + 7(144) - 288 = -576 + 1008 - 288 = 144$

At $x=2$: $-\frac{2^3}{3} + 7(2^2) - 24(2) = -\frac{8}{3} + 7(4) - 48 = -\frac{8}{3} + 28 - 48 = -20 - \frac{8}{3} = -\frac{60+8}{3} = -\frac{68}{3}$

Area $= (144) - (-\frac{68}{3}) = 144 + \frac{68}{3} = \frac{432 + 68}{3} = \frac{500}{3}$

The enclosed area is $\frac{500}{3}$ square units (approximately 166.67 square units).

(a) Interpolation vs. Extrapolation:

Interpolation: Estimating a value *within* the range of known data points. Example: Given temperature at 1 PM and 3 PM, estimating the temperature at 2 PM.

Extrapolation: Estimating a value *outside* the range of known data points by extending the observed trend. Example: Given temperature from 1 PM to 3 PM, estimating the temperature at 4 PM.

(b) Grouped Data Estimates:

When data is grouped into classes, individual data point values are lost. Calculations (like mean, median, standard deviation) rely on assumptions about the distribution within each class (e.g., using class midpoints). These assumptions introduce estimation errors, so the calculated measures are approximations, not exact values.

A parity bit is an extra bit added to a string of binary code to detect errors during data transmission. It ensures that the total number of 1s in the string (including the parity bit) is either always even (even parity) or always odd (odd parity). If the receiving end detects the wrong parity, it indicates a transmission error.

Question 5 (20 marks)

(a) Define each of the following terms as used in numerical analysis:

(i) absolute error; (2 marks)

(ii) relative error. (2 marks)

(b) Explain three properties of the standard deviation as a measure of dispersion. (6 marks)

(c) Given two matrices A and B such that:

$A=\left[\begin{array}{ll}5 & 7 \\ 4 & 6\end{array}\right]$.

$B=\left[\begin{array}{ll}6 & 4 \\ 5 & 3\end{array}\right]$,

Show that:

(i) $AB \neq BA$ (3 marks)

(ii) $\det(A) \times \det(B) = \det(AB)$ (3 marks)

(iii) $A^{-1}B^{-1} \neq (AB)^{-1}$ (4 marks)

Answer

Disclaimer: Answers are AI-generated and may contain errors. Verify independently.

(a) Numerical Analysis Terms:

(i) Absolute Error: The absolute difference between the true value and the approximate value. Formula: $|True Value - Approximate Value|$. It measures the magnitude of the error.

(ii) Relative Error: The absolute error divided by the magnitude of the true value (or sometimes the approximate value if the true value is unknown). Formula: $\frac{|True Value - Approximate Value|}{|True Value|}$. It expresses the error as a fraction of the true value, indicating its significance relative to the value's size.

(b) Properties of Standard Deviation:

  1. Non-negativity: Standard deviation is always zero or positive. It is zero only when all data points are identical.
  2. Sensitivity to Outliers: Standard deviation is sensitive to extreme values (outliers) because it involves squared differences from the mean. A single large outlier can significantly increase the standard deviation.
  3. Units: Standard deviation has the same units as the original data, making it directly interpretable in the context of the data's scale.

(c) Matrix Operations:

$A=\left[\begin{array}{ll}5 & 7 \\ 4 & 6\end{array}\right]$, $B=\left[\begin{array}{ll}6 & 4 \\ 5 & 3\end{array}\right]$

(i) Show $AB \neq BA$:

$AB = \left[\begin{array}{ll}5 & 7 \\ 4 & 6\end{array}\right] \left[\begin{array}{ll}6 & 4 \\ 5 & 3\end{array}\right] = \left[\begin{array}{ll}(5)(6)+(7)(5) & (5)(4)+(7)(3) \\ (4)(6)+(6)(5) & (4)(4)+(6)(3)\end{array}\right] = \left[\begin{array}{ll}30+35 & 20+21 \\ 24+30 & 16+18\end{array}\right] = \left[\begin{array}{ll}65 & 41 \\ 54 & 34\end{array}\right]$

$BA = \left[\begin{array}{ll}6 & 4 \\ 5 & 3\end{array}\right] \left[\begin{array}{ll}5 & 7 \\ 4 & 6\end{array}\right] = \left[\begin{array}{ll}(6)(5)+(4)(4) & (6)(7)+(4)(6) \\ (5)(5)+(3)(4) & (5)(7)+(3)(6)\end{array}\right] = \left[\begin{array}{ll}30+16 & 42+24 \\ 25+12 & 35+18\end{array}\right] = \left[\begin{array}{ll}46 & 66 \\ 37 & 53\end{array}\right]$

Since $\left[\begin{array}{ll}65 & 41 \\ 54 & 34\end{array}\right] \neq \left[\begin{array}{ll}46 & 66 \\ 37 & 53\end{array}\right]$, $AB \neq BA$.

(ii) Show $\det(A) \times \det(B) = \det(AB)$:

$\det(A) = (5)(6) - (7)(4) = 30 - 28 = 2$

$\det(B) = (6)(3) - (4)(5) = 18 - 20 = -2$

$\det(A) \times \det(B) = (2) \times (-2) = -4$

$\det(AB) = \det\left(\left[\begin{array}{ll}65 & 41 \\ 54 & 34\end{array}\right]\right) = (65)(34) - (41)(54) = 2210 - 2214 = -4$

Thus, $\det(A) \times \det(B) = \det(AB) = -4$.

(iii) Show $A^{-1}B^{-1} \neq (AB)^{-1}$:

$A^{-1} = \frac{1}{\det(A)} \left[\begin{array}{rr}6 & -7 \\ -4 & 5\end{array}\right] = \frac{1}{2} \left[\begin{array}{rr}6 & -7 \\ -4 & 5\end{array}\right] = \left[\begin{array}{rr}3 & -3.5 \\ -2 & 2.5\end{array}\right]$

$B^{-1} = \frac{1}{\det(B)} \left[\begin{array}{rr}3 & -4 \\ -5 & 6\end{array}\right] = \frac{1}{-2} \left[\begin{array}{rr}3 & -4 \\ -5 & 6\end{array}\right] = \left[\begin{array}{rr}-1.5 & 2 \\ 2.5 & -3\end{array}\right]$

$A^{-1}B^{-1} = \left[\begin{array}{rr}3 & -3.5 \\ -2 & 2.5\end{array}\right] \left[\begin{array}{rr}-1.5 & 2 \\ 2.5 & -3\end{array}\right] = \left[\begin{array}{ll}(3)(-1.5)+(-3.5)(2.5) & (3)(2)+(-3.5)(-3) \\ (-2)(-1.5)+(2.5)(2.5) & (-2)(2)+(2.5)(-3)\end{array}\right]$

$= \left[\begin{array}{ll}-4.5 - 8.75 & 6 + 10.5 \\ 3 + 6.25 & -4 - 7.5\end{array}\right] = \left[\begin{array}{rr}-13.25 & 16.5 \\ 9.25 & -11.5\end{array}\right]$

$(AB)^{-1} = \frac{1}{\det(AB)} \left[\begin{array}{rr}34 & -41 \\ -54 & 65\end{array}\right] = \frac{1}{-4} \left[\begin{array}{rr}34 & -41 \\ -54 & 65\end{array}\right] = \left[\begin{array}{rr}-8.5 & 10.25 \\ 13.5 & -16.25\end{array}\right]$

(a) Measures of Central Tendency as Equilibrium Points:

(i) Arithmetic Mean: It's the balancing point of the distribution. The sum of deviations of all data points from the mean is always zero ($\sum(x_i - \bar{x}) = 0$). It acts like the center of gravity for the data.

(ii) Median: It's the point that divides the distribution into two equal halves in terms of frequency. 50% of the data points fall below the median, and 50% fall above it. It represents the positional center.

(b) Diagonal vs. Non-singular Matrix:

Diagonal Matrix: A square matrix where all elements outside the main diagonal are zero. Elements on the main diagonal can be zero or non-zero. Example: $\begin{bmatrix} 2 & 0 \\ 0 & -5 \end{bmatrix}$.

Non-singular Matrix: A square matrix whose determinant is non-zero. This means the matrix has an inverse, and its rows/columns are linearly independent. A diagonal matrix can be singular (if a diagonal element is 0) or non-singular (if all diagonal elements are non-zero).

(c) Matrix Method for Simultaneous Equations:

$$\begin{aligned} 3x+4y &= 52 \\ 5x+8y &= 96 \end{aligned}$$

Matrix form: $\begin{bmatrix} 3 & 4 \\ 5 & 8 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} x \\ y \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 52 \\ 96 \end{bmatrix}$

Let $A = \begin{bmatrix} 3 & 4 \\ 5 & 8 \end{bmatrix}$.

$\det(A) = (3)(8) - (4)(5) = 24 - 20 = 4$.

$A^{-1} = \frac{1}{\det(A)} \begin{bmatrix} 8 & -4 \\ -5 & 3 \end{bmatrix} = \frac{1}{4} \begin{bmatrix} 8 & -4 \\ -5 & 3 \end{bmatrix}$.

Solution: $\begin{bmatrix} x \\ y \end{bmatrix} = A^{-1} \begin{bmatrix} 52 \\ 96 \end{bmatrix} = \frac{1}{4} \begin{bmatrix} 8 & -4 \\ -5 & 3 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} 52 \\ 96 \end{bmatrix}$

$\begin{bmatrix} x \\ y \end{bmatrix} = \frac{1}{4} \begin{bmatrix} (8)(52) + (-4)(96) \\ (-5)(52) + (3)(96) \end{bmatrix} = \frac{1}{4} \begin{bmatrix} 416 - 384 \\ -260 + 288 \end{bmatrix} = \frac{1}{4} \begin{bmatrix} 32 \\ 28 \end{bmatrix}$

$\begin{bmatrix} x \\ y \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 32/4 \\ 28/4 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 8 \\ 7 \end{bmatrix}$

Therefore, $x=8$ and $y=7$.

Question 8 (20 marks)

(a) (i) Outline four disadvantages of the arithmetic mean as a measure of central tendency. (4 marks)

(ii) One of the properties of the arithmetic mean is that it is dependent on origin. Explain this property using a suitable illustration. (3 marks)

(b) A curve is defined by the quadratic function $y=12x-x^2-20$.

(i) Determine the roots of the equation using the factorisation method; (3 marks)

(ii) By using calculus techniques, determine the coordinates of the turning point of the curve. (3 marks)

(c) General observation on a certain road indicates that there are 3 potholes for every 240metre distance along the road. A random length of 900 metres was selected from a section of the road.

(i) Model this problem as a Poisson probability distribution; (2 marks)

(ii) Determine the probability of getting exactly 10 potholes on the selected length. (2 marks)

(iii) Determine the probability of getting between 4 and 6 potholes inclusive on the selected length of the road. (3 marks)

Answer

Disclaimer: Answers are AI-generated and may contain errors. Verify independently.

(a) Arithmetic Mean:

(i) Disadvantages:

  • Affected by extreme values (outliers).
  • Cannot be calculated for open-ended class intervals.
  • May not correspond to an actual data value.
  • Cannot be determined graphically (unlike median or mode).

(ii) Dependence on Origin: This property means that if a constant value (change of origin) is added to or subtracted from every observation in a dataset, the arithmetic mean also increases or decreases by the same constant value.

Illustration: Consider data set {2, 4, 6}. Mean = (2+4+6)/3 = 4.

Change origin by adding 5 to each value: {7, 9, 11}.

New Mean = (7+9+11)/3 = 27/3 = 9.

The new mean (9) is the original mean (4) plus the constant (5). New Mean = Original Mean + Constant.

(b) Quadratic Function $y=12x-x^2-20$:

(i) Roots by Factorisation:

Set $y=0$: $12x - x^2 - 20 = 0$

Multiply by -1: $x^2 - 12x + 20 = 0$

Find two numbers that multiply to 20 and add to -12: -10 and -2.

Factor: $(x - 10)(x - 2) = 0$

The roots are $x = 10$ and $x = 2$.

(ii) Turning Point by Calculus:

Find the first derivative and set it to zero:

$y = -x^2 + 12x - 20$

$\frac{dy}{dx} = -2x + 12$

Set $\frac{dy}{dx} = 0$: $-2x + 12 = 0 \implies 2x = 12 \implies x = 6$.

Find the corresponding y-value:

$y = 12(6) - (6)^2 - 20 = 72 - 36 - 20 = 16$.

Find the second derivative to determine nature of turning point:

$\frac{d^2y}{dx^2} = -2$. Since it's negative, the turning point is a maximum.

The coordinates of the turning point (maximum) are (6, 16).

(c) Pothole Poisson Distribution:

(i) Model:

Rate = 3 potholes per 240 metres.

Average rate ($\lambda$) for 900 metres = $\frac{3 \text{ potholes}}{240 \text{ m}} \times 900 \text{ m} = \frac{3 \times 900}{240} = \frac{2700}{240} = 11.25$ potholes.

Let X be the number of potholes in 900m. X follows a Poisson distribution with $\lambda = 11.25$.

$P(X=k) = \frac{e^{-\lambda}\lambda^k}{k!} = \frac{e^{-11.25}(11.25)^k}{k!}$

(ii) Probability of Exactly 10 Potholes:

$P(X=10) = \frac{e^{-11.25}(11.25)^{10}}{10!}$

$e^{-11.25} \approx 0.000012976$

$(11.25)^{10} \approx 3.1058 \times 10^{10}$

$10! = 3,628,800$

$P(X=10) \approx \frac{(0.000012976) \times (3.1058 \times 10^{10})}{3,628,800} \approx \frac{402988}{3628800} \approx 0.11105$

(iii) Probability of Between 4 and 6 Potholes Inclusive:

$P(4 \le X \le 6) = P(X=4) + P(X=5) + P(X=6)$

$P(X=4) = \frac{e^{-11.25}(11.25)^4}{4!} \approx \frac{0.000012976 \times 16018.066}{24} \approx \frac{0.2078}{24} \approx 0.00866$

$P(X=5) = \frac{e^{-11.25}(11.25)^5}{5!} \approx \frac{0.000012976 \times 180203.24}{120} \approx \frac{2.3384}{120} \approx 0.01949$

$P(X=6) = \frac{e^{-11.25}(11.25)^6}{6!} \approx \frac{0.000012976 \times 2027286.5}{720} \approx \frac{26.306}{720} \approx 0.03654$

$P(4 \le X \le 6) \approx 0.00866 + 0.01949 + 0.03654 \approx 0.06469$
