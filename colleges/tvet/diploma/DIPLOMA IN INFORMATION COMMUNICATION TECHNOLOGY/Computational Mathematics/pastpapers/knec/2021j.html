Question 1 (20 marks)

(a) Explain each of the following terms as used in statistics:

(i) population; (2 marks)

(ii) sample. (2 marks)

(b) Rama, Shem and Mogan purchased cookies of different brands P, Q and R. Rama purchased 10 packets of P, 7 packets of Q and 3 packets of R. Shem purchased 4 packets of P, 8 packets of Q and 10 packets of R. Mogan purchased 4 packets of P, 7 packets of Q and 8 packets of R. The cost of each brand is Kshs 40, 50 and 60 respectively.

(i) Represent the information in matrices notation. (2 marks)

(ii) Use the matrix method to determine the amount of money spent by each person. (6 marks)

(c) Use the cofactor method to determine the inverse of matrix $D=\left[\begin{array}{lll}3 & 1 & 6 \\ 2 & 0 & 4 \\ 5 & 7 & 2\end{array}\right]$. (8 marks)

(a) Statistical Terms:

(i) Population: The entire group of individuals, items, or data points that share one or more characteristics relevant to a statistical study. It's the complete set from which a sample is drawn.

(ii) Sample: A subset or portion of the population selected for study. It is used to make inferences or draw conclusions about the entire population.

(b) Cookie Purchases:

(i) Matrix Notation:

$S = \begin{bmatrix} 10 & 7 & 3 \\ 4 & 8 & 10 \\ 4 & 7 & 8 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} 40 \\ 50 \\ 60 \end{bmatrix}$

$S = \begin{bmatrix} (10)(40) + (7)(50) + (3)(60) \\ (4)(40) + (8)(50) + (10)(60) \\ (4)(40) + (7)(50) + (8)(60) \end{bmatrix}$

Amount spent: Rama = Ksh 930, Shem = Ksh 1160, Mogan = Ksh 990.

Question 2 (20 marks)

(a) (i) Outline three features of symbolic model. (3 marks)

(ii) A certain shop has all their commodities on sale with a 15% discount offer.

(I) Formulate a mathematical model that could be used to determine the sale price of each commodity in the shop. (2 marks)

(II) Using the model formulated in (I), determine the sale price of a commodity whose original price is Ksh 500. (2 marks)

(b) The following statements describes various operations:

(i) Picking a team of 3 student leaders from a group of 10 students;

(ii) Picking a chairman, secretary and treasurer from a group of 10 students;

(iii) Choosing 3 desserts from a menu of 10;

(iv) Listing your 3 favourite desserts, in order, from a menu of 10;

State whether each of the statement requires a permutation or a combination operation. (4 marks)

(c) A 4-digit PIN is formed using the digits 0, 3, 5, 7, 8 and 9 such that the PIN is divisible by 10 and the digits are not repeated. Determine the total number of possible PINs that would be formed. (4 marks)

(d) Distinguish between absolute error and relative error as used in measurement. (5 marks)

Answer

Disclaimer: Answers are AI-generated and may contain errors. Verify independently.

(a) Symbolic Model:

(i) Features:

  • Uses symbols (letters, numbers, mathematical operators) to represent components and relationships of a system.
  • Abstract representation, focusing on structure and logic rather than physical appearance.
  • Allows for manipulation and analysis using mathematical or logical rules.

(ii) Discount Model:

(I) Let $P$ be the original price and $S$ be the sale price. Discount = 15% = 0.15.

Sale Price = Original Price - Discount Amount

$S = P - (0.15 \times P)$

$S = P(1 - 0.15)$

$S = 0.85P$

The mathematical model is $S = 0.85P$.

(II) Original Price $P = 500$.

Sale Price $S = 0.85 \times 500 = 425$.

The sale price is Ksh 425.

(b) Permutation vs. Combination:

(i) Picking a team of 3 from 10: **Combination** (Order doesn't matter in a team).

(ii) Picking chairman, secretary, treasurer from 10: **Permutation** (Order matters, specific roles).

(iii) Choosing 3 desserts from 10: **Combination** (Order of choosing desserts doesn't matter).

(iv) Listing 3 favourite desserts in order from 10: **Permutation** (Order is specified).

(c) 4-Digit PIN:

Digits available: {0, 3, 5, 7, 8, 9} (6 digits).

PIN must be 4 digits, divisible by 10, no repeated digits.

Divisible by 10 means the last digit (4th digit) **must be 0**.

_ _ _ 0

Now we need to choose the first 3 digits from the remaining 5 digits {3, 5, 7, 8, 9} without repetition.

This is a permutation problem because the order of the first three digits matters.

Number of choices for 1st digit = 5

Number of choices for 2nd digit = 4 (one less, as no repetition)

Number of choices for 3rd digit = 3 (two less)

Number of choices for 4th digit = 1 (must be 0)

Total possible PINs = $5 \times 4 \times 3 \times 1 = 60$.

Alternatively, using permutation formula: $P(n, k) = \frac{n!}{(n-k)!}$. We need to arrange 3 digits from the remaining 5: $P(5, 3) = \frac{5!}{(5-3)!} = \frac{5!}{2!} = \frac{120}{2} = 60$.

There are 60 possible PINs.

(d) Absolute vs. Relative Error:

Absolute Error: The magnitude of the difference between the measured/approximate value and the true/actual value. It indicates the size of the error in the same units as the measurement. Formula: $|True Value - Approximate Value|$.

Relative Error: The absolute error expressed as a fraction (or percentage) of the true value. It indicates the significance of the error relative to the size of the quantity being measured. Formula: $\frac{|True Value - Approximate Value|}{|True Value|}$. It is often dimensionless or expressed as a percentage.

(a) Characteristics of Skewness:

(i) Positive Skewness (Right-Skewed):

  • The tail on the right side of the distribution is longer or fatter than the left side.
  • The bulk of the data values (including the median and mode) are concentrated on the left side.
  • The relationship between central tendency measures is typically: Mean > Median > Mode.

(ii) Negative Skewness (Left-Skewed):

  • The tail on the left side of the distribution is longer or fatter than the right side.
  • The bulk of the data values (including the median and mode) are concentrated on the right side.
  • The relationship between central tendency measures is typically: Mode > Median > Mean.

(b) Absolute vs. Relative Measures of Dispersion:

  1. Units: Absolute measures (e.g., range, standard deviation) are expressed in the same units as the original data. Relative measures (e.g., coefficient of variation, coefficient of range) are unitless ratios or percentages.
  2. Comparison: Absolute measures are suitable for comparing variability within a single dataset or datasets with the same units and similar means. Relative measures are better for comparing variability between datasets with different units or significantly different means.
  3. Interpretation: Absolute measures indicate the actual amount of spread in the data's units. Relative measures indicate the spread relative to the average (central tendency), providing a standardized measure of variability.

(a) Probability Terms:

(i) Random Experiment: An action or process whose outcome cannot be predicted with certainty before it occurs, but the set of all possible outcomes is known.

(ii) Sample Space (S): The set of all possible distinct outcomes of a random experiment.

(iii) Outcome: A single possible result of a random experiment.

(iv) Sample Point: An individual outcome within the sample space; an element of the sample space.

(b) Discrete vs. Continuous Random Variable:

Discrete Random Variable: A variable whose possible values can only be a finite number of distinct values or a countably infinite sequence of values (often integers). There are gaps between possible values. Example: The number of heads when flipping a coin 3 times (can be 0, 1, 2, or 3).

Continuous Random Variable: A variable whose possible values can take on any value within a given range or interval. There are no gaps between possible values. Example: The height of a student (can be 1.65m, 1.651m, 1.6512m, etc., within a range).

(c) Odd vs. Even Parity:

Parity is used for error checking in data transmission.

Odd Parity: An extra bit (parity bit) is added to a block of data such that the total number of '1' bits in the block (including the parity bit) is always odd. If the count of 1s in the data is even, the parity bit is set to 1; if odd, it's set to 0.

Even Parity: An extra bit (parity bit) is added such that the total number of '1' bits in the block (including the parity bit) is always even. If the count of 1s in the data is odd, the parity bit is set to 1; if even, it's set to 0.
