Question 1 (20 marks)

(a) (i) Define the term dependent event as used in probability. (2 marks)

(ii) Mwanzo Institute presented two teams during the National ball competitions: the boys’ football team and girls’ volleyball team. The probability of the boys' football team winning is 1/3 and the probability of the girls’ volleyball team winning is 1/4. Calculate the probability that:

I at least one team wins; (4 marks)

II both teams lose (2 marks)

(b) Differentiate between bits and bytes as used in computer systems. (4 marks)

(c) (i) Define the term relative frequency as used in statistics (2 marks)

(ii) Table 1 shows time taken by employees to complete an activity

Using assumed mean of 11.7 (midpoint of 11.5-11.9 class), determine the standard deviation (6 marks)

Answer

Disclaimer: These answers are derived based on interpretation and may contain errors. Please verify independently.

(a) (i) **Dependent Events:** Two events are dependent if the outcome or occurrence of the first event affects the outcome or occurrence of the second event, so that the probability is changed.

(ii) Let B = Boys win, G = Girls win. P(B) = 1/3, P(G) = 1/4. Then P(B') = 1 - 1/3 = 2/3 and P(G') = 1 - 1/4 = 3/4.
I. P(at least one wins) = 1 - P(both lose) = 1 - P(B' and G')
Assuming independence: P(at least one wins) = 1 - (P(B') * P(G')) = 1 - (2/3 * 3/4) = 1 - 6/12 = 1 - 1/2 = 1/2.
II. P(both lose) = P(B' and G') = P(B') * P(G') = (2/3 * 3/4) = 6/12 = 1/2.

(b) **Bits vs. Bytes:**
- **Bit (Binary Digit):** The most basic unit of data in computing, represented as either 0 or 1.
- **Byte:** A group of bits (commonly 8 bits) operated on as a unit. It's typically the smallest addressable unit of memory and can represent a single character.

(c) (i) **Relative Frequency:** The ratio of the number of times an event occurs in a series of trials of a chance experiment to the total number of trials conducted.

Answer

Disclaimer: These answers are derived based on interpretation and may contain errors. Please verify independently.

(a) (i) **Leading Diagonal (Main Diagonal):** In a square matrix, the leading diagonal (or main diagonal) consists of the elements from the top-left corner to the bottom-right corner (i.e., elements \(a_{ij}\) where \(i=j\)).

(ii) **Inverse Method:**
System: \( 8x+4y+2z=3 \), \( 4x+3y+z=3 \), \( 2x+y+3z=1 \)
Matrix form: AX = B, where \( A = \begin{bmatrix} 8 & 4 & 2 \\ 4 & 3 & 1 \\ 2 & 1 & 3 \end{bmatrix} \), \( X = \begin{bmatrix} x \\ y \\ z \end{bmatrix} \), \( B = \begin{bmatrix} 3 \\ 3 \\ 1 \end{bmatrix} \)
det(A) = 8(3*3 - 1*1) - 4(4*3 - 1*2) + 2(4*1 - 3*2) = 8(9-1) - 4(12-2) + 2(4-6) = 8(8) - 4(10) + 2(-2) = 64 - 40 - 4 = 20
Cofactor Matrix: \( C = \begin{bmatrix} +(9-1) & -(12-2) & +(4-6) \\ -(12-2) & +(24-4) & -(8-8) \\ +(4-6) & -(8-8) & +(24-16) \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 8 & -10 & -2 \\ -10 & 20 & 0 \\ -2 & 0 & 8 \end{bmatrix} \)
Adjoint(A) = CT = \( \begin{bmatrix} 8 & -10 & -2 \\ -10 & 20 & 0 \\ -2 & 0 & 8 \end{bmatrix} \)
Inverse(A) = A⁻¹ = \( \frac{1}{det(A)} Adj(A) = \frac{1}{20} \begin{bmatrix} 8 & -10 & -2 \\ -10 & 20 & 0 \\ -2 & 0 & 8 \end{bmatrix} \)
Solve X = A⁻¹B:
\( \begin{bmatrix} x \\ y \\ z \end{bmatrix} = \frac{1}{20} \begin{bmatrix} 8 & -10 & -2 \\ -10 & 20 & 0 \\ -2 & 0 & 8 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} 3 \\ 3 \\ 1 \end{bmatrix} = \frac{1}{20} \begin{bmatrix} (8 \times 3) + (-10 \times 3) + (-2 \times 1) \\ (-10 \times 3) + (20 \times 3) + (0 \times 1) \\ (-2 \times 3) + (0 \times 3) + (8 \times 1) \end{bmatrix} = \frac{1}{20} \begin{bmatrix} 24 - 30 - 2 \\ -30 + 60 + 0 \\ -6 + 0 + 8 \end{bmatrix} = \frac{1}{20} \begin{bmatrix} -8 \\ 30 \\ 2 \end{bmatrix} \)
Solution: x = -8/20 = -0.4, y = 30/20 = 1.5, z = 2/20 = 0.1

(c) **Skewness vs. Kurtosis:**
- **Skewness:** Measures the asymmetry of a probability distribution. A distribution is symmetric if it looks the same to the left and right of the center point. Positive skewness means the tail on the right side is longer or fatter; negative skewness means the tail on the left side is longer or fatter.
- **Kurtosis:** Measures the "peakedness" or "tailedness" of a probability distribution compared to a normal distribution. High kurtosis (leptokurtic) means more data in the tails and a sharper peak; low kurtosis (platykurtic) means less data in the tails and a flatter peak.

(d) **Graphing:**
(i) Plotting \( y = 4x^2 - 8x - 21 \) requires calculating y-values for x from -2 to 4 and drawing the parabola.
(ii) Solving \( 4x^2 - 10x - 15 = 0 \) using the graph involves rearranging the equation to match the plotted graph's form, potentially by adding/subtracting a linear equation. Let \( y = 4x^2 - 8x - 21 \). We want to solve \( 4x^2 - 8x - 21 = 2x - 6 \). This means finding the intersection points of the parabola \( y = 4x^2 - 8x - 21 \) and the line \( y = 2x - 6 \). The x-coordinates of these intersection points are the solutions.

(c) **Percentage Change:**

Question 6 (20 marks)

(a) Explain each of the following types of data as used in statistics:

(i) discrete; (2 marks)

(ii) continuous. (2 marks)

(b) A batch of laptops contains 16 working and 4 defective laptops. If 3 laptops are selected at random from the batch without replacement, determine the probability that:

(i) all three are working; (3 marks)

(ii) two are working but one is defective. (3 marks)

(c) Using graphic method, solve the following equations

\( 3x - 2y = 0 \)

\( 4x + y + 11 = 0 \)

(4 marks)

(d) (i) Define the term combination as used in mathematics. (2 marks)

(ii) A committee consisting of 6 members is to be formed out of 7 men and 4 women. Determine the number of committees that can be formed such that at least a third of the members are women. (4 marks)

Answer

Disclaimer: These answers are derived based on interpretation and may contain errors. Please verify independently.

(a) **Types of Data:**
(i) **Discrete Data:** Data that can only take specific, countable values. It cannot take any value between two given values. Examples include number of students, number of books, etc.
(ii) **Continuous Data:** Data that can take any value within a given range. It can be measured at any level of precision. Examples include height, weight, time, etc.

(b) **Laptop Selection Probability:**
Total laptops = 16 working + 4 defective = 20 laptops
(i) **All three working:**
P(all working) = \( \frac{16}{20} \times \frac{15}{19} \times \frac{14}{18} = \frac{3360}{6840} \approx 0.491 \)
(ii) **Two working, one defective:**
P(two working, one defective) = \( \binom{2}{1} \times \frac{16}{20} \times \frac{15}{19} \times \frac{4}{18} = 3 \times \frac{960}{6840} = \frac{2880}{6840} \approx 0.421 \)

(c) **Graphical Solution:**
Line 1: 3x - 2y = 0 => y = \( \frac{3}{2}x \)
Line 2: 4x + y = -11
Substituting Line 1 into Line 2:
4x + \( \frac{3}{2}x \) = -11
\( \frac{11}{2}x \) = -11
x = -2
Substituting back:
y = \( \frac{3}{2}(-2) \) = -3
Solution: (-2, -3)

(d) (i) **Combination Definition:** A selection of items from a larger set where the order does not matter. It represents the number of ways to select r items from n items, denoted as \( \binom{n}{r} \) or \( ^nC_r \).

(ii) **Committee Formation:**
Total committee size = 6
At least 1/3 women means ≥ 2 women (as 6/3 = 2)
Cases:
1. 2 women (4 men): \( \binom{4}{2} \times \binom{7}{4} = 6 \times 35 = 210 \)
2. 3 women (3 men): \( \binom{4}{3} \times \binom{7}{3} = 4 \times 35 = 140 \)
3. 4 women (2 men): \( \binom{4}{4} \times \binom{7}{2} = 1 \times 21 = 21 \)
Total possible committees = 210 + 140 + 21 = 371

(a) **Binary Codes:**
(i) **Reflective codes:** Binary codes where the code for a negative number is obtained by reflecting (inverting) all bits of the positive number's code. Example: Sign-and-magnitude representation.
(ii) **Non-weighted codes:** Binary codes where the position of 1s and 0s has no numerical weight or significance. Examples include Gray code and excess-3 code.

Applications of binary codes:
1. Error detection and correction in digital communications
2. Data compression and encoding in computer systems

(b) **Set Theory:**
(i) **x ∈ A notation:** This means x is an element (or member) of set A. It indicates that x is one of the items contained within set A.
(ii) **B ∩ A where A = {1,2,3} and B = {3,4,5,6}:**
B ∩ A = {3} (the common element between both sets)
(iii) **C ∩ D given C ⊂ D:**
Since C is a proper subset of D (C ⊂ D), all elements of C are also in D
Therefore, C ∩ D = C (the intersection contains all elements of C)

(c) **Permutation Equation:**
\( ^n P_4 = 12 \times ^n P_2 \)
\( \frac{n!}{(n-4)!} = 12 \times \frac{n!}{(n-2)!} \)
\( \frac{n!}{(n-4)!} = 12 \times \frac{n(n-1)}{1} \)
\( n(n-1)(n-2)(n-3) = 12n(n-1) \)
\( (n-2)(n-3) = 12 \)
\( n^2 - 5n + 6 = 12 \)
\( n^2 - 5n - 6 = 0 \)
\( (n-6)(n+1) = 0 \)
n = 6 or n = -1
Since n must be positive and greater than 4 (for \( ^n P_4 \) to exist), n = 6

(d) **Train Speed:**
Let speed = v km/h
Time taken = \( \frac{150}{v} \) hours = \( \frac{150}{v} \times 60 \) minutes
With increased speed: \( \frac{150}{v+20} \times 60 \) minutes
Difference = 37.5 minutes
\( \frac{150}{v} \times 60 - \frac{150}{v+20} \times 60 = 37.5 \)
\( 9000(\frac{1}{v} - \frac{1}{v+20}) = 37.5 \)
\( 9000(\frac{v+20-v}{v(v+20)}) = 37.5 \)
\( \frac{180000}{v(v+20)} = 37.5 \)
\( v(v+20) = 4800 \)
\( v^2 + 20v - 4800 = 0 \)
Using quadratic formula: v = 60 km/h
