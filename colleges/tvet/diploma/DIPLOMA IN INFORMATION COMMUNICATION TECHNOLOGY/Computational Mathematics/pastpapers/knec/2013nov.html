Question 6 (20 marks)

(a) (i) Evaluate the following mathematical expression; \( ^2C_1 \times ^2P_1 \) (Interpreted from OCR). (2 marks)

(ii) Differentiate between a histogram and a bar graph as used in modelling. (4 marks)

(b) The perimeter of a rectangular garden is 100 metres and its area is 616 square metres. Determine the length and the width of the garden. (6 marks)

(c) Given the Boolean equation Y = A B + BC draw a truth table to find the value of Y. (4 marks)

(d) Differentiate between the terms a measurement error and a rounding error as applied in statistics. (4 marks)

Answer

Disclaimer: These answers are derived based on interpretation and may contain errors. Please verify independently.

(a) (i) \( ^2C_1 = \frac{2!}{1!(2-1)!} = 2 \). \( ^2P_1 = \frac{2!}{(2-1)!} = 2 \). \( 2 \times 2 = 4 \).

(ii) **Histogram:** Represents frequency distribution of continuous data. Bars are adjacent, width represents class interval, area represents frequency. **Bar Graph:** Represents categorical data. Bars are separate, height/length represents value/frequency.

(b) Let L=length, W=width. Perimeter: 2(L+W) = 100 => L+W = 50. Area: L*W = 616. Substitute W=50-L into Area: L(50-L) = 616 => 50L - L² = 616 => L² - 50L + 616 = 0. Solve quadratic: (L-28)(L-22)=0. L=28 or L=22. If L=28, W=22. If L=22, W=28. Dimensions are 28m and 22m.

(d) **Measurement Error:** Difference between a measured value and the true value, arising from instrument limitations or observation flaws. **Rounding Error:** Difference introduced when a number is approximated to fewer significant figures or decimal places.

(ii) Describe the EXCLUSIVE — OR gate as used in logic gates. (2 marks)

(b) (i) Kihoto Self Help Group comprises of seven men and five women. The chairman wants to choose a committee consisting of three men and two women. Determine the number of ways the chairman would use to constitute the committee. (3 marks)

(c) The inventory of furniture at each outlet of a three-store chain is given by the following matrix.

\( F = \begin{bmatrix} 9 & 12 \\ 15 & 4 \\ 7 & 0 \end{bmatrix} \)

The rows denote the different stores and the columns the classes of the furniture. The cost of the furniture is given by vector \( P = \begin{bmatrix} 700 \\ 1200 \end{bmatrix} \). Determine:

(i) the total cost of furniture for each class of furniture; (2 marks)

(ii) the total cost of furniture in each outlet. (3 marks)

Answer

Disclaimer: These answers are derived based on interpretation and may contain errors. Please verify independently.

(a) (i) Assumptions: I. Linear Interpolation: Function behaves linearly between known points, points are accurate. II. Linear Extrapolation: Trend observed between points continues linearly beyond them, underlying relationship is linear.

(ii) **EXCLUSIVE-OR (XOR) Gate:** A logic gate that outputs TRUE (1) if the number of TRUE inputs is odd (i.e., exactly one input is TRUE for a 2-input gate). Outputs FALSE (0) if inputs are the same.

(b) (i) Ways to choose men: \( ^7C_3 = \frac{7!}{3!4!} = 35 \). Ways to choose women: \( ^5C_2 = \frac{5!}{2!3!} = 10 \). Total ways = \( 35 \times 10 = 350 \).

(ii) Histogram drawing requires visual representation (Description omitted).

(c) (i) Total cost per class: Requires summing columns of F and multiplying by corresponding cost in P (Calculations omitted). (ii) Total cost per outlet: Requires matrix multiplication F * P. \( \begin{bmatrix} 9 & 12 \\ 15 & 4 \\ 7 & 0 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} 700 \\ 1200 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} (9 \times 700 + 12 \times 1200) \\ (15 \times 700 + 4 \times 1200) \\ (7 \times 700 + 0 \times 1200) \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 6300 + 14400 \\ 10500 + 4800 \\ 4900 + 0 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 20700 \\ 15300 \\ 4900 \end{bmatrix} \). Costs per outlet: Ksh 20700, Ksh 15300, Ksh 4900.

Question 8 (20 marks)

(a) (i) Describe the fundamental union rule as applied in finite sets. (2 marks)

(ii) Distinguish between combinations and permutations as applied in statistics. (4 marks)

(b) Given that a couple has two children, find the probability that both children are girls if it is known that:

(i) at least one of the children is a girl; (2 marks)

(ii) the older child is a girl. (2 marks)

(c) QRS factory manufactures vehicle parts where the probability of producing a good part is 0.8 and that of producing a faulty part is 0.2. Determine the probability of getting 10 good parts in a sample of 12. (5 marks)

(d) (i) Find the value of \( \pi \) truncated to four decimal places. (1 mark)

(ii) A farmer sold farm produce from the farm worth Kshs. 687,500 but had estimated the sales to be Kshs. 700,000. Determine:

I absolute error; (2 marks)

II relative error. (2 marks)

Answer

Disclaimer: These answers are derived based on interpretation and may contain errors. Please verify independently.

(a) (i) **Fundamental Union Rule (Inclusion-Exclusion Principle):** For finite sets A and B, \( |A \cup B| = |A| + |B| - |A \cap B| \). The size of the union is the sum of the sizes minus the size of the intersection.

(ii) **Combinations:** Selection of items where order does not matter. **Permutations:** Arrangement of items where order matters.

(b) Sample space: {BB, BG, GB, GG}. Assume equal probability (1/4 each).
(i) Event A: Both girls {GG}. Event B: At least one girl {BG, GB, GG}. P(A|B) = P(A ∩ B) / P(B) = P({GG}) / P({BG, GB, GG}) = (1/4) / (3/4) = 1/3.
(ii) Event C: Older child is girl {GB, GG}. P(A|C) = P(A ∩ C) / P(C) = P({GG}) / P({GB, GG}) = (1/4) / (2/4) = 1/2.

(c) Binomial probability: n=12, k=10, p=0.8, q=0.2. \( P(X=10) = \binom{12}{10}(0.8)^{10}(0.2)^2 = \frac{12 \times 11}{2}(0.8)^{10}(0.04) = 66 \times (0.107374...) \times 0.04 \approx 0.2835 \).

(d) (i) \( \pi \approx 3.1415926... \) Truncated to 4 decimal places: 3.1415.

(ii) I. Absolute Error = |Measured Value - True Value| = |Estimated Value - Actual Value| = |700,000 - 687,500| = 12,500.
II. Relative Error = Absolute Error / Actual Value = 12,500 / 687,500 \( \approx 0.01818 \) or 1.82%.
