MANAGEMENT INFORMATION SYSTEMS

INSTRUCTIONS TO CANDIDATES

Answer any FIVE of the EIGHT questions in the answer booklet provided.

Candidates should answer the questions in English.

Question 1

(a) Outline three benefits of using network diagrams in project management. (3 marks)

(b) Peter, an ICT manager at Masomo Institute is the custodian of personal data for all the employees. Explain three ethical obligations that he has with regard to this data. (6 marks)

(c) A multinational organisation intends to implement a new Information System. Describe three levels of planning that would be necessary for successful implementation. (6 marks)

(d) Uwezo institute is in the process of acquiring a library Management Information System.

  • (i) Outline two activities that should have been undertaken at the preparation stage. (2 marks)
  • (ii) Outline three steps that the institute followed at vendor choosing stage. (3 marks)

Answer

(a) Benefits of Network Diagrams in Project Management:
  1. Visual Representation: Provides a clear graphical view of project activities, their sequence, and dependencies, making the project plan easier to understand.
  2. Identification of Critical Path: Helps determine the sequence of activities that dictates the shortest possible project duration, allowing managers to focus on critical tasks.
  3. Resource Scheduling: Facilitates the planning and allocation of resources by showing when activities need to start and finish.
  4. Progress Monitoring: Allows managers to track progress against the plan and identify potential delays early.
(b) Ethical Obligations of ICT Manager (Peter) Regarding Employee Data:
  1. Confidentiality: Ensuring that personal employee data is protected from unauthorized access, disclosure, or use. Access should be restricted to those with a legitimate need-to-know.
  2. Accuracy & Integrity: Taking reasonable steps to ensure the personal data held is accurate, complete, and up-to-date, and implementing measures to prevent unauthorized modification.
  3. Purpose Limitation & Security: Ensuring data is used only for the legitimate purposes for which it was collected (e.g., payroll, HR administration) and implementing appropriate technical and organizational security measures (e.g., encryption, access controls, secure storage) to protect it.
(c) Levels of Planning for IS Implementation:
  1. Strategic Planning: Long-term planning (3-5+ years) focusing on aligning the new IS with the overall business strategy, defining high-level goals for the system, identifying required resources, and assessing major risks and opportunities. It sets the overall direction.
  2. Tactical Planning: Medium-term planning (1-3 years) focusing on translating strategic goals into specific projects and departmental plans. This involves defining project scope, objectives, timelines, resource allocation for different phases, and key milestones for the IS implementation.
  3. Operational Planning: Short-term planning (daily, weekly, monthly) focusing on the detailed execution of specific tasks within the implementation project. This includes scheduling specific activities, assigning tasks to team members, managing day-to-day resources, and monitoring immediate progress.
(d) Library MIS Acquisition (Uwezo Institute):
  • (i) Preparation Stage Activities:
    1. Needs Assessment/Requirements Gathering: Identifying the specific problems the current system has (or the needs a new system must fulfill), defining functional and non-functional requirements for the new library MIS.
    2. Feasibility Study: Assessing the technical, economic, operational, and schedule feasibility of acquiring and implementing a new system.
  • (ii) Vendor Choosing Stage Steps:
    1. Request for Proposals (RFP)/Vendor Shortlisting: Sending out RFPs detailing requirements to potential vendors and shortlisting those that meet key criteria based on their proposals.
    2. Vendor Demonstrations & Evaluation: Inviting shortlisted vendors to demonstrate their systems and evaluating them against predefined criteria (functionality, cost, support, vendor reputation).
    3. Reference Checks & Due Diligence: Contacting existing clients of the top vendors to verify claims and assess satisfaction levels.
    4. Negotiation & Contract Award: Negotiating contract terms, pricing, and service level agreements (SLAs) with the chosen vendor.
    *(Only three steps were required)*

(d) Happyland Company Ltd operates its business globally. Describe two systems that could enable the company transact electronically. (4 marks)

Answer

(a) Circumstances Leading to Adaptive Maintenance:
  1. Changes in External Environment: Modifications required due to new government regulations, industry standards, or legal requirements (e.g., changes in tax laws affecting financial systems).
  2. Changes in Technology Platform: Updates needed to make the system compatible with new hardware, operating systems, database versions, or network protocols.
  3. Changes in Business Processes or Requirements: Adjustments required because the organization has changed its business strategy, structure, or operational workflows, and the system needs to support these new processes.
(b) (i) Information Systems for Top Management:
  • Executive Support Systems (ESS) / Executive Information Systems (EIS)
  • Decision Support Systems (DSS)
  • Management Information Systems (MIS) (providing summarized reports)
  • Business Intelligence (BI) Systems / Data Warehouses
(b) (ii) How IS Leads to Fewer Management Levels:
  1. Direct Access to Information: IS allows senior managers to directly access operational data and performance reports, reducing the need for middle managers to primarily act as information conduits and summarizers.
  2. Empowerment & Decentralized Decision-Making: IS can provide lower-level employees and teams with the information and analytical tools needed to make decisions previously handled by middle management, enabling flatter organizational structures.
(c) Critical Path Determination:

Forward Pass (Calculate ES and EF):

  • A: ES=0, EF=0+7=7
  • B: ES=EF(A)=7, EF=7+10=17
  • C: ES=EF(A)=7, EF=7+15=22
  • D: ES=max(EF(B), EF(C))=max(17, 22)=22, EF=22+5=27

Project Duration = 27 weeks.

Backward Pass (Calculate LF and LS):

  • D: LF=27, LS=27-5=22
  • C: LF=LS(D)=22, LS=22-15=7
  • B: LF=LS(D)=22, LS=22-10=12
  • A: LF=min(LS(B), LS(C))=min(12, 7)=7, LS=7-7=0

Slack Calculation (LF-EF or LS-ES):

  • A: Slack = 7-7 = 0
  • B: Slack = 22-17 = 5
  • C: Slack = 22-22 = 0
  • D: Slack = 27-27 = 0

Critical Path: Activities with zero slack.

Critical Path is A -> C -> D (Duration = 27 weeks).

(d) Systems for Global Electronic Transactions:
  1. E-commerce Platform: A website/application enabling global customers to browse products, place orders, and make payments online, facilitating international B2C or B2B sales.
  2. Electronic Data Interchange (EDI): Allows standardized electronic exchange of business documents (POs, invoices) with global suppliers or partners, automating B2B transactions across borders.
  3. Global Payment Systems / EFT: Utilizing international banking networks (like SWIFT) and online payment gateways to facilitate secure electronic fund transfers for international sales and purchases.

Question 3

(a) Outline two application areas of information society in a developing economy. (2 marks)

(b) (i) Explain two circumstances that would lead an organization to use an Electronic Fund Transfer system. (4 marks)

(b) (ii) Ujuzi Institute intends to streamline its decision-making through the use of information system. Describe three types of decisions that could be streamlined. (6 marks)

(c) Tegemeo Industries has adopted the use of robots in their production processes. Explain two advantages that the organisation would accrue from this move. (4 marks)

(d) Differentiate between intranet and extranet as used in Management Information Systems. (4 marks)

Answer

(a) Application Areas of Information Society in Developing Economy:
  1. E-Government: Providing government services online (e.g., applications, information access, tax filing) to improve efficiency, transparency, and citizen access.
  2. E-Learning/Education: Using ICT for distance learning, accessing educational resources online, and improving educational administration.
  3. E-Health: Utilizing ICT for telemedicine, health information systems, managing patient records, and disseminating public health information.
  4. E-Commerce/Business: Enabling businesses to reach wider markets, conduct online transactions, improve supply chain management, and access market information.
  5. Agriculture: Providing farmers access to market prices, weather information, pest control advice, and financial services via mobile phones or internet kiosks.
*(Note: Only two areas were required)*
(b) (i) Circumstances for Using Electronic Fund Transfer (EFT):
  1. Payroll Processing: Paying employee salaries directly into their bank accounts efficiently and securely, especially for large numbers of employees.
  2. Supplier Payments/B2B Transactions: Making regular payments to suppliers or receiving payments from business customers electronically, reducing paperwork and speeding up transactions.
  3. Customer Payments (Direct Debit/Credit): Collecting recurring payments from customers (e.g., subscriptions, loan repayments) or processing online customer payments via debit/credit cards.
*(Note: Only two circumstances were required)*
(b) (ii) Types of Decisions Streamlined by IS (Ujuzi Institute):
  1. Structured Decisions: Routine, repetitive decisions with established procedures that IS can automate or provide clear rules for. Example: Automatically flagging students with overdue library books based on borrowing records and loan periods.
  2. Semi-Structured Decisions: Decisions requiring some human judgment but also benefiting from data analysis provided by IS. Example: An MIS report showing enrollment trends by course could help decide which courses to promote more heavily, but the final decision involves managerial judgment.
  3. Operational Decisions: Day-to-day decisions related to smooth operations. Example: A scheduling system automatically assigning classrooms based on course size and availability, streamlining the manual allocation process.
(c) Advantages of Using Robots in Production (Tegemeo Industries):
  1. Increased Consistency & Quality: Robots perform repetitive tasks with high precision and consistency, reducing errors and improving product quality compared to manual labor.
  2. Increased Productivity & Efficiency: Robots can operate continuously (24/7) without fatigue, breaks, or variations in speed, leading to higher output and efficiency.
  3. Operation in Hazardous Environments: Robots can perform tasks in environments unsafe or uncomfortable for humans (e.g., high heat, toxic substances, heavy lifting).
*(Note: Only two advantages were required)*
(d) Intranet vs. Extranet:
  • Intranet: A private network contained within an organization, using internet protocols (like TCP/IP and HTTP) to securely share internal information, resources, and applications among employees. Access is restricted to authorized internal users.
  • Extranet: A private network that uses internet technology to securely share parts of a business's information or operations with external stakeholders like suppliers, vendors, partners, or customers. It extends the intranet to authorized external users, often requiring logins and passwords.

Question 4

(a) Outline four factors that could be considered to ascertain effectiveness of information systems maintenance. (4 marks)

(b) Many organisations in Kenya install a Transaction Information System. Explain two reasons for this. (4 marks)

(c) (i) State two categories of information system resources. (2 marks)

(c) (ii) Distinguish between efficiency and effectiveness as used in information systems resource utilisation. (4 marks)

(d) Kazana Institute has been overseeing a project that stalled. Explain three reasons that could have led to this. (6 marks)

Answer

(a) Factors to Ascertain Effectiveness of IS Maintenance:
  1. System Uptime/Availability: Measuring the percentage of time the system is operational and available to users as expected.
  2. User Satisfaction: Gathering feedback from users regarding system performance, usability, and the responsiveness of the maintenance team.
  3. Number & Severity of Errors/Bugs: Tracking the frequency and impact of system errors reported after maintenance activities.
  4. Maintenance Backlog & Response Time: Monitoring the number of pending maintenance requests and the time taken to resolve reported issues.
  5. Cost of Maintenance: Tracking the resources (time, money) spent on maintenance activities relative to the system's value or budget.
*(Note: Only four factors were required)*
(b) Reasons for Installing Transaction Information Systems (TIS/TPS) in Kenya:
  1. Efficiency in Handling High Volumes: To automate and speed up the processing of large numbers of routine transactions common in businesses (e.g., sales, inventory updates, payments), reducing manual effort and costs.
  2. Improved Accuracy & Data Capture: To reduce errors inherent in manual data entry and capture transaction data accurately at the source for operational control and record-keeping.
  3. Foundation for Other Systems: TPS provides the essential, up-to-date data required by higher-level systems like MIS and DSS for reporting and analysis.
*(Note: Only two reasons were required)*
(c) (i) Categories of Information System Resources:
  • People Resources: End users, IS specialists (programmers, analysts, administrators).
  • Hardware Resources: Physical computer equipment (servers, workstations, peripherals), network devices.
  • Software Resources: System software (OS), application software, utility programs.
  • Data Resources: Databases, data files, data warehouses.
  • Network Resources: Communication media, network infrastructure, internet access.
*(Note: Only two categories were required)*
(c) (ii) Efficiency vs. Effectiveness in IS Resource Utilization:
  • Efficiency: Refers to how well resources are used to produce output with minimal waste. In IS, it relates to processing speed, resource consumption (CPU, memory), throughput, and cost per transaction. "Doing things right."
  • Effectiveness: Refers to how well the information system helps the organization achieve its goals and objectives. It relates to the quality of information, support for decision-making, user satisfaction, and contribution to business value. "Doing the right things."
(d) Reasons for Project Stalling (Kazana Institute):
  1. Lack of Clear Goals/Scope Creep: The project objectives may have been poorly defined initially, or the scope may have expanded uncontrollably without proper management, leading to confusion and delays.
  2. Insufficient Resources/Funding: The project may have run out of budget, lacked necessary personnel with the right skills, or faced shortages of required equipment or technology.
  3. Poor Project Management/Leadership: Inadequate planning, scheduling, monitoring, communication, or lack of decisive leadership could lead to loss of direction and momentum.
  4. Technical Difficulties/Unforeseen Problems: Unexpected technical challenges, integration issues, or complexities may have arisen that the team was unable to resolve effectively.
  5. Lack of Stakeholder Support/User Resistance: Key stakeholders or end-users may not have supported the project, leading to resistance, lack of cooperation, or withdrawal of support.
*(Note: Only three reasons were required)*

Question 5

(a) Outline four benefits realised by an organisation that outsource an information system. (4 marks)

(b) A certain training company intends to acquire a system through User Application Development approach.

  • (i) Outline four situations that make this approach applicable. (4 marks)
  • (ii) Outline two limitations of this approach. (2 marks)

(c) Differentiate between confidentiality and integrity security goals. (4 marks)

(d) Fanaka College has an ICT project that is in progress. Describe three factors that are likely to affect resource allocation to this project. (6 marks)

Answer

(a) Benefits of Outsourcing an Information System:
  1. Cost Savings: Potential reduction in IT costs due to vendor's economies of scale, lower labor costs, or reduced need for in-house expertise and infrastructure investment.
  2. Access to Expertise: Gain access to specialized skills, technology, and best practices that may not be available internally.
  3. Focus on Core Business: Allows the organization to concentrate its resources and management attention on its primary business activities rather than IT management.
  4. Scalability & Flexibility: Easier to scale IT resources up or down based on business needs by adjusting the outsourcing contract.
(b) User Application Development (End-User Development):
  • (i) Applicable Situations:
    1. Simple applications with limited scope and complexity.
    2. Applications needed quickly where formal development processes would take too long.
    3. Situations where user requirements are difficult to formalize or change frequently.
    4. Availability of user-friendly development tools (e.g., spreadsheet macros, database query tools, 4GLs).
    5. One-off or ad-hoc analysis/reporting needs specific to a user or small group.
    *(Note: Only four situations were required)*
  • (ii) Limitations:
    1. Lack of Documentation & Standards: User-developed applications often lack proper documentation and may not adhere to organizational standards, making them difficult to maintain or integrate.
    2. Data Integrity & Security Risks: May lead to "private" databases, data redundancy, inconsistency, and potential security vulnerabilities if not properly managed.
    3. Limited Scope & Scalability: Often unsuitable for large, complex, or mission-critical applications requiring robustness and scalability.
    *(Note: Only two limitations were required)*
(c) Confidentiality vs. Integrity Security Goals:
  • Confidentiality: Ensures that information is not disclosed to unauthorized individuals, entities, or processes. It's about protecting data privacy and secrecy (preventing unauthorized reading).
  • Integrity: Ensures the accuracy and completeness of information and processing methods. It's about preventing unauthorized modification or deletion of data, ensuring data is trustworthy and reliable.
(d) Factors Affecting Resource Allocation to ICT Project (Fanaka College):
  1. Strategic Importance: Projects closely aligned with the college's strategic goals and offering significant potential benefits (e.g., improved student services, competitive advantage) are likely to receive higher resource priority.
  2. Budget Availability: The overall ICT budget and competing demands from other projects or operational needs will constrain the resources available for this specific project.
  3. Project Risk & Feasibility: Higher-risk projects or those with uncertain technical feasibility might receive fewer resources initially until risks are mitigated or feasibility is proven.
  4. Availability of Skilled Personnel: The availability of internal staff or external consultants with the required technical and project management skills will influence the pace and scale of resource allocation.
  5. Return on Investment (ROI) / Cost-Benefit Analysis: Projects demonstrating a clear and high potential return on investment or significant cost savings are more likely to secure necessary funding and resources.
*(Note: Only three factors were required)*

Question 7

(a) (i) State two uses of project management software. (2 marks)

(a) (ii) Outline two inputs that a user may enter into project management software for a given project. (2 marks)

(b) Explain each of the following environmental factors of an information system during planning:

  • (i) Economic; (2 marks)
  • (ii) Technical; (2 marks)
  • (iii) Social. (2 marks)

(c) Distinguish between ready-made and tailor-made information systems acquisition methods. (4 marks)

(d) The management of a certain company developed an ICT security policy document. Explain three components of this document. (6 marks)

Answer

(a) (i) Uses of Project Management Software:
  1. Planning & Scheduling: Defining tasks, setting durations, establishing dependencies, creating timelines (Gantt charts, network diagrams), and identifying the critical path.
  2. Resource Allocation & Management: Assigning resources (personnel, equipment, budget) to tasks and tracking their utilization.
  3. Tracking & Monitoring Progress: Monitoring task completion, comparing actual progress against the baseline plan, and identifying variances.
  4. Communication & Collaboration: Facilitating communication among team members and stakeholders through shared task lists, reports, and notifications.
*(Note: Only two uses were required)*
(a) (ii) Inputs for Project Management Software:
  1. Task List: A breakdown of all the activities or tasks required to complete the project (Work Breakdown Structure - WBS).
  2. Task Durations: Estimated time required to complete each individual task.
  3. Task Dependencies: The relationships between tasks (e.g., Task B cannot start until Task A is finished).
  4. Resource Information: Details about available resources (people, equipment, materials) and their costs/availability.
*(Note: Only two inputs were required)*
(b) Environmental Factors in IS Planning:
  • (i) Economic: Availability of capital, cost of technology, inflation rates, market competition, and overall economic health influence budget constraints, investment decisions, and the potential ROI of IS projects.
  • (ii) Technical: The pace of technological change, availability of new hardware/software, existing IT infrastructure, and availability of skilled IT personnel impact technology choices, feasibility, and implementation strategies.
  • (iii) Social: Customer expectations, employee skills and attitudes towards technology, data privacy concerns, and societal trends (e.g., mobile usage, social media) influence system requirements, user interface design, and adoption strategies.
(c) Ready-Made vs. Tailor-Made IS Acquisition:
  • Ready-Made (Off-the-Shelf): Acquiring pre-existing software packages developed for a general market. They are typically cheaper and faster to implement but may require business processes to adapt to the software and might not meet all specific organizational needs.
  • Tailor-Made (Custom Development): Developing a unique system specifically designed to meet the organization's exact requirements. This offers a perfect fit but is generally more expensive, time-consuming, and carries higher development risks.
(d) Components of an ICT Security Policy Document:
  1. Acceptable Use Policy (AUP): Defines how employees are permitted to use company ICT resources (hardware, software, network, internet), outlining prohibited activities.
  2. Access Control Policy: Specifies rules for granting access to information systems and data based on user roles and responsibilities (e.g., password complexity, user authentication methods, authorization levels).
  3. Data Security & Privacy Policy: Outlines procedures for handling sensitive data, including storage, transmission, backup, disposal, and compliance with data protection regulations.
  4. Incident Response Plan: Defines procedures for detecting, responding to, and recovering from security breaches or incidents.
*(Note: Only three components were required)*

Question 8

(a) Explain one limitation of Gantt charts as a project management tool. (2 marks)

(b) (i) Outline two approaches to information system classification. (2 marks)

(b) (ii) Explain three roles played by the ICT department in Management Information System of an organisation. (6 marks)

(c) Differentiate between perfective and preventative information system maintenances. (4 marks)

(d) Futuregen Company Ltd acknowledges that personnel are core to a secure information system. Explain three mechanisms that the company’s personnel department may use to enhance this. (6 marks)

Answer

(a) Limitation of Gantt Charts:

One key limitation is that Gantt charts do not clearly show task dependencies or the critical path. While they visually represent the schedule and duration of tasks, it's difficult to see how a delay in one task impacts others without additional information or a network diagram view.

(b) (i) Approaches to Information System Classification:
  1. By Organizational Level Supported: Classifying systems based on the management level they primarily serve (Operational, Knowledge, Management, Strategic).
  2. By Functional Area: Classifying systems based on the specific business function they support (e.g., Sales & Marketing IS, Manufacturing & Production IS, Finance & Accounting IS, Human Resources IS).
  3. By Type of Support Provided: Classifying based on the nature of support (e.g., TPS, MIS, DSS, ESS, OAS, KWS).
*(Note: Only two approaches were required)*
(b) (ii) Roles of ICT Department in MIS:
  1. System Development & Maintenance: Designing, developing, implementing, and maintaining the hardware, software, and network infrastructure that constitute the MIS.
  2. Data Management & Security: Managing the organization's data resources, ensuring data integrity, availability, and implementing security measures to protect information assets.
  3. User Support & Training: Providing technical support to MIS users, troubleshooting problems, and conducting training to ensure effective system utilization.
  4. Strategic Planning & Consultation: Advising management on leveraging technology, planning future IS investments, and ensuring alignment with business goals.
*(Note: Only three roles were required)*
(c) Perfective vs. Preventative Maintenance:
  • Perfective Maintenance: Focuses on improving the existing system's performance, efficiency, usability, or maintainability, even if it's currently functioning without errors. It aims to make the system better. Example: Optimizing code for speed.
  • Preventative Maintenance: Involves activities undertaken to reduce the likelihood of future system failures or problems. It aims to prevent issues before they occur. Example: Regularly updating software patches, performing hardware diagnostics, or restructuring code to improve understandability and reduce future errors.
(d) Personnel Department Mechanisms to Enhance IS Security (Futuregen):
  1. Background Checks & Screening: Implementing thorough background checks during the hiring process for personnel who will have access to sensitive systems and data, verifying credentials and identifying potential risks.
  2. Security Awareness Training: Conducting regular training programs for all employees on security policies, procedures, common threats (e.g., phishing, social engineering), and their role in maintaining security.
  3. Clear Policies & Enforcement: Developing and clearly communicating ICT security policies (e.g., Acceptable Use Policy, password policies) and consistently enforcing disciplinary actions for violations.
  4. Role-Based Access Control & Separation of Duties: Working with IT to ensure access rights are granted based on the principle of least privilege (only necessary access for the job role) and implementing separation of duties for critical functions.
*(Note: Only three mechanisms were required)*
