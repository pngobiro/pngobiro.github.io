MODULE III

MANAGEMENT INFORMATION SYSTEMS

INSTRUCTIONS TO THE CANDIDATE

This paper consists of EIGHT questions.

Answer any FIVE questions in the answer booklet provided.

Candidates should answer the questions in English.

Question 1

(a) Outline four components of an information resource. (4 marks)

(b) Rubbia bank has installed a transaction processing system for its operations. Explain three benefits of this system to the bank. (6 marks)

(c) Rancho Company require an accounting information system and have opted to use their ICT staff developed it. Explain three benefits of this move to the company. (6 marks)

(d) Distinguish between copyright and Intellectual property as applied in computer ethics. (4 marks)

Answer

(a) Components of an Information Resource:

Information resources encompass more than just data; they include:

  1. Data/Information: The raw facts, processed data, and knowledge itself.
  2. Technology: Hardware, software, and networks used to capture, store, process, and transmit data/information.
  3. People: Users, IS professionals, and managers who create, manage, and utilize the information and technology.
  4. Processes/Procedures: The rules, methods, and workflows governing how information is handled and used.
(b) Benefits of TPS to Rubbia Bank:
  1. Efficiency & Speed: Automates high-volume routine transactions like deposits, withdrawals, and transfers, processing them much faster and more efficiently than manual methods.
  2. Accuracy & Consistency: Reduces human error in data entry and calculations, ensuring consistency in processing transactions according to predefined rules.
  3. Real-time Data Availability: Captures transaction data immediately, providing up-to-date account balances and transaction histories for both customers and bank staff.
  4. Audit Trail: Creates a detailed electronic record of all transactions, facilitating auditing, reconciliation, and compliance requirements.
*(Note: Only three benefits were required)*
(c) Benefits of In-house Development (Rancho Company):
  1. Customization & Specific Fit: The system can be tailored precisely to Rancho Company's unique accounting procedures, reporting requirements, and existing workflows, potentially offering a better fit than off-the-shelf software.
  2. Control over Development & Features: The company retains full control over the development process, features included, and future modifications or enhancements.
  3. Potential Competitive Advantage: A unique, custom-built system might incorporate proprietary processes or features that provide a competitive edge, which wouldn't be available in standard packages.
  4. Internal Skill Development: Developing the system in-house builds valuable technical skills and system knowledge within the ICT staff.
*(Note: Only three benefits were required)*
(d) Copyright vs. Intellectual Property:
  • Copyright: A specific legal right granted to the creator of original works of authorship (e.g., software code, literature, music, artistic works), giving them exclusive rights to control its reproduction, distribution, adaptation, and public display/performance for a certain period. It protects the *expression* of an idea, not the idea itself.
  • Intellectual Property (IP): A broader category of intangible creations of the human intellect that are protected by law. Copyright is one type of IP. Other types include patents (for inventions), trademarks (for brand identity), and trade secrets (for confidential business information). IP grants creators exclusive rights over the use of their creations.

Question 2

(a) Outline two benefits of distributed systems. (2 marks)

(b) Freshmans Ltd intends to implement new government legislation to their existing information system.

  • (i) Describe a suitable type of system maintenance that would be performed on the system to incorporate this legislation. (2 marks)
  • (ii) Explain two other types of system maintenance. (4 marks)

(c) A certain company has established that its information is being electronically eavesdropped through their telephone lines. Explain three measures that the company could put in place to minimize this crime. (6 marks)

(d) Cantata motor assembly plant intends to use product differentiation strategy to gain an advantage over its competitors. Explain three ways in which information systems could assist the firm use this strategy. (6 marks)

Answer

(a) Benefits of Distributed Systems:
  1. Improved Reliability/Availability: Failure of one node does not necessarily bring down the entire system; other nodes can continue operating.
  2. Scalability: Easier to add more nodes (computers) to the system as processing or storage needs grow.
  3. Performance: Tasks can be processed in parallel across multiple machines, potentially improving overall performance and throughput.
  4. Resource Sharing: Allows geographically dispersed users to share data and resources.
*(Note: Only two benefits were required)*
(b) System Maintenance (Freshmans Ltd):
  • (i) Suitable Type: Adaptive Maintenance. This type involves modifying the system to cope with changes in the external environment, such as new government legislation.
  • (ii) Other Types:
    1. Corrective Maintenance: Fixing errors or bugs discovered in the system after implementation (e.g., fixing a calculation error).
    2. Perfective Maintenance: Enhancing the system's performance, usability, or efficiency even if it's functioning correctly (e.g., optimizing code, improving UI).
    3. Preventive Maintenance: Activities undertaken to reduce the likelihood of future failures (e.g., applying patches, code restructuring).
    *(Note: Only two other types were required)*
(c) Measures to Minimize Electronic Eavesdropping (Telephone Lines):
  1. Encryption: Implementing end-to-end encryption for voice calls (using VoIP with secure protocols like SRTP/TLS) or encrypting data transmitted over lines if they carry digital information.
  2. Secure Physical Access: Restricting physical access to telephone lines, junction boxes, and communication closets to prevent unauthorized tapping.
  3. Regular Line Sweeping/Monitoring: Periodically checking telephone lines for unauthorized taps or listening devices, possibly using specialized equipment or services.
  4. Use of Secure Communication Channels: Utilizing alternative secure channels like encrypted VPNs for data transmission or secure messaging apps instead of potentially vulnerable traditional phone lines for sensitive communication.
*(Note: Only three measures were required)*
(d) IS Assisting Product Differentiation Strategy (Cantata):
  1. Customer Relationship Management (CRM) Systems: Collecting detailed customer data to understand preferences and tailor products, services, or marketing messages to specific customer segments, creating perceived uniqueness.
  2. Computer-Aided Design/Manufacturing (CAD/CAM): Using advanced design and manufacturing systems to create unique product features, improve quality, or offer customization options not easily replicated by competitors.
  3. Supply Chain Management (SCM) Systems: Optimizing the supply chain to offer faster delivery times, better product availability, or unique service guarantees that differentiate the company.
  4. Marketing Information Systems: Analyzing market trends and competitor activities to identify opportunities for unique product positioning, branding, or feature development.
*(Note: Only three ways were required)*

Question 3

(a) Explain how management information systems facilitate the execution of each of the following organizational tasks:

  • (i) coordination;
  • (ii) planning;
  • (iii) control. (6 marks)

(b) Explain three ways in which decision support systems are applied in an organization. (6 marks)

(c) The management of Jolene Company has incorporated a social interaction platform in its Management Information System. Explain two reasons for this move. (4 marks)

(d) Jabali Company intends to secure its Management Information System. Explain two physical measures that the company could put in place. (4 marks)

Answer

(a) How MIS Facilitates Organizational Tasks:
  • (i) Coordination: MIS facilitates coordination by providing shared databases, communication platforms (email, messaging), and integrated systems (like ERP) that allow different departments and individuals to access common information, share updates in real-time, and align their activities.
  • (ii) Planning: MIS provides historical data, forecasting tools, and analytical capabilities (e.g., reports, dashboards) that help managers analyze trends, predict future conditions, set objectives, and allocate resources more effectively during the planning process.
  • (iii) Control: MIS supports control by generating performance reports that compare actual results against planned targets (budgets, schedules, KPIs). This allows managers to identify deviations, investigate causes, and take timely corrective actions.
(b) Applications of Decision Support Systems (DSS):
  1. Financial Planning & Analysis: Using financial models to analyze investment opportunities, forecast revenue/expenses, perform sensitivity analysis ("what-if"), and support budgeting decisions.
  2. Marketing & Sales Analysis: Analyzing customer data to segment markets, evaluate marketing campaign effectiveness, predict sales trends, or optimize pricing strategies.
  3. Production & Operations Management: Using models for production scheduling, inventory control optimization, logistics planning, or quality control analysis.
  4. Human Resource Management: Analyzing workforce data for manpower planning, evaluating compensation plans, or predicting turnover rates.
*(Note: Only three ways were required)*
(c) Reasons for Incorporating Social Interaction Platform (Jolene Company):
  1. Improved Internal Communication & Collaboration: To facilitate faster information sharing, knowledge exchange, and collaboration among employees, breaking down departmental silos and fostering a sense of community.
  2. Enhanced Employee Engagement & Morale: To provide a platform for informal communication, social connection, recognition, and feedback, potentially boosting morale and employee engagement.
  3. Knowledge Management: To capture and share tacit knowledge and expertise that might not be documented in formal systems, creating a searchable repository of internal know-how.
*(Note: Only two reasons were required)*
(d) Physical Security Measures for MIS (Jabali Company):
  1. Access Control to Facilities: Implementing measures like locks, security guards, access cards, or biometric scanners to restrict physical access to data centers, server rooms, and other areas housing critical IT infrastructure.
  2. Environmental Controls: Installing systems to protect hardware from environmental threats, such as fire suppression systems, temperature and humidity controls (HVAC), and uninterruptible power supplies (UPS)/backup generators.
  3. Hardware Security: Physically securing servers and network equipment in locked racks or rooms, using cable locks for laptops, and implementing controls over removable media.
*(Note: Only two measures were required)*

Answer

(a) Cyber Warfare:

Cyber warfare refers to the use of computer technology and internet-based attacks by a nation-state or its proxies to disrupt, damage, or destroy the critical infrastructure, computer systems, or information assets of another nation or organization for political or military objectives. It involves actions like espionage, sabotage, propaganda, and denial-of-service attacks targeting government, military, or civilian infrastructure.

(b) Information System Types:
  • (i) Group Decision Support System (GDSS): An interactive computer-based system designed to facilitate group decision-making by supporting brainstorming, idea evaluation, consensus building, and communication among group members, often in a meeting room setting (using tools like electronic brainstorming, voting tools, shared screens).
  • (ii) Executive Information System (EIS) / Executive Support System (ESS): A system specifically designed for senior executives, providing easy access to high-level, summarized internal and external information relevant to strategic goals. Features include graphical dashboards, drill-down capabilities, and access to key performance indicators (KPIs).
  • (iii) Enterprise Resource Planning (ERP) System: An integrated suite of software modules that manages core business processes across different functional areas (e.g., finance, HR, manufacturing, supply chain) using a central database, providing a unified view of organizational data and operations.
(c) Attributes of Information as a Resource:
  1. Shareable: Unlike physical resources, information can be used by multiple people simultaneously without being depleted.
  2. Expandable: The value and amount of information can grow through use, analysis, and combination with other information.
  3. Transportable: Information can be moved instantly across vast distances electronically.
  4. Compressible: Large amounts of information can often be summarized or compressed into more concise forms.
  5. Valuable: Accurate, timely, and relevant information has significant value for decision-making, operations, and strategy.

Question 5

(a) Describe each of the following approaches to organizational development:

  • (i) socio-technical;
  • (ii) behavioral. (4 marks)

(b) Distinguish between real-time and batch modes of data processing. (4 marks)

(c) Most organizations align their information system functions to their business strategic plan. Explain three benefits of this alignment. (6 marks)

(d) Managers are often faced with tasks of decision making for their organizations. Outline the sequence of this decision making process. (6 marks)

Answer

(a) Approaches to Organizational Development:
  • (i) Socio-technical Approach: Focuses on the interaction between the social system (people, tasks, structure) and the technical system (technology, tools). It aims to jointly optimize both systems to improve overall organizational performance and employee well-being, recognizing their interdependence.
  • (ii) Behavioral Approach: Focuses on changing the attitudes, behaviors, and interpersonal skills of individuals and groups within the organization. It uses techniques like sensitivity training, team building, and survey feedback to improve communication, collaboration, and problem-solving.
(b) Real-time vs. Batch Processing:
  • Real-time Processing: Data is processed immediately as a transaction occurs, providing instant updates to system records. This allows for immediate feedback and response. Example: Airline reservation systems, ATM transactions.
  • Batch Processing: Data is collected over a period and processed together in a group or "batch". Updates to records are delayed until the batch is processed. Example: Processing payroll checks at the end of the month, generating monthly billing statements.
(c) Benefits of Aligning IS Functions with Business Strategic Plan:
  1. Ensures IS Investments Support Business Goals: Guarantees that IT resources and projects are directed towards achieving key business objectives, maximizing strategic value.
  2. Improved Resource Allocation & Prioritization: Helps prioritize IS projects and allocate resources based on their contribution to strategic goals, ensuring focus on high-impact initiatives.
  3. Competitive Advantage: Enables the development of information systems that support unique business strategies and provide a competitive edge in the marketplace.
  4. Better Integration & Coordination: Promotes better integration of systems across functional areas in support of overall business processes and strategies.
*(Note: Only three benefits were required)*
(d) Sequence of the Decision-Making Process (e.g., Simon's Model):
  1. Intelligence Phase: Recognizing and defining the problem or opportunity. Involves gathering information, identifying symptoms, and understanding the context. What is the problem?
  2. Design Phase: Developing and analyzing potential alternative solutions or courses of action. What are the possible solutions?
  3. Choice Phase: Evaluating the alternatives based on defined criteria (e.g., cost, benefit, risk) and selecting the most suitable option. Which solution is best?
  4. Implementation Phase: Putting the chosen solution into action and allocating necessary resources. (Often added to Simon's original model).
  5. Monitoring/Review Phase: Evaluating the outcome of the decision and the effectiveness of the chosen solution, making adjustments as needed. (Also often added).
*(Note: Sequence based on Simon's model primarily includes Intelligence, Design, Choice)*

Question 6

(a) Explain three ways in which cultural barriers could pose challenges to developing global information systems. (6 marks)

(b) Distinguish between information system literacy and computer literacy. (4 marks)

(c) Teamwork is a strategy being embraced by managers to improve their organizations performance. State four communication technology tools that would be used to facilitate this strategy. (4 marks)

(d) Most companies have embraced cloud computing technology in their operations. Explain three possible risks that could arise from the use of this technology. (6 marks)

Answer

(a) Cultural Barriers in Global IS Development:
  1. Language Differences: Variations in language require translation of interfaces, documentation, and support materials, increasing complexity and potential for miscommunication. Different interpretations of icons or symbols can also cause issues.
  2. Different Work Practices & Norms: Variations in work schedules, communication styles, decision-making processes, and attitudes towards hierarchy across cultures can make standardizing processes through a global IS challenging.
  3. Legal & Regulatory Differences: Diverse laws regarding data privacy, security standards, and cross-border data flows in different countries complicate system design and compliance.
  4. User Interface Design Preferences: Cultural differences in aesthetics, color meanings, layout preferences, and date/time/currency formats need to be considered for user acceptance.
*(Note: Only three ways were required)*
(b) Information System Literacy vs. Computer Literacy:
  • Computer Literacy: Focuses on knowledge of information technology, primarily how computer hardware and software work, and how to use them (e.g., using a word processor, spreadsheet, or operating system).
  • Information System Literacy: A broader concept that includes computer literacy but also encompasses understanding the management and organizational dimensions of systems. It involves knowing how information systems function within an organization, how they support business processes and decision-making, and their strategic implications.
(c) Communication Technology Tools for Teamwork:
  1. Email: For formal communication, sending documents, and reaching multiple team members.
  2. Instant Messaging (IM) / Chat Platforms (e.g., Slack, Microsoft Teams): For quick questions, real-time conversations, and informal coordination.
  3. Video Conferencing (e.g., Zoom, Google Meet): For virtual meetings, face-to-face discussions, and screen sharing, especially for remote or distributed teams.
  4. Collaboration Platforms / Groupware (e.g., SharePoint, Google Workspace, Asana): For shared document editing, task management, project tracking, and maintaining a central repository for team information.
(d) Risks of Cloud Computing:
  1. Security Vulnerabilities: Entrusting sensitive data to a third-party provider introduces risks related to unauthorized access, data breaches, or shared infrastructure vulnerabilities if the provider's security measures are inadequate.
  2. Vendor Lock-in & Dependency: Migrating data and applications away from a specific cloud provider can be difficult and costly, creating dependency on the vendor's pricing, policies, and viability.
  3. Compliance & Legal Risks: Ensuring the cloud provider complies with relevant industry regulations and data residency laws (especially for cross-border data storage) can be complex.
  4. Downtime & Availability Issues: Dependence on the provider's infrastructure means that outages or performance issues on their end can disrupt the organization's access to its systems and data.
*(Note: Only three risks were required)*

Question 7

(a) Outline four factors that could influence the choice of an information system acquisition method. (4 marks)

(b) Explain the following types of decisions, stating the level of management where each could be applied:

  • (i) unstructured;
  • (ii) structured;
  • (iii) semi structure. (6 marks)

(c) Digital library materials have become popular among producers and publishers globally. Outline four reasons for the popularity of these materials. (4 marks)

(d) Arbiter Hospital has installed an expert system to assist in its operations. Explain three ways in which the system could be used by the hospital. (6 marks)

Answer

(a) Factors Influencing IS Acquisition Method Choice:
  1. Cost: The budget available for acquisition, implementation, and ongoing maintenance (custom development is usually more expensive upfront than off-the-shelf).
  2. Time Availability: How quickly the system is needed (off-the-shelf is faster to deploy than custom development).
  3. Uniqueness of Business Needs: The extent to which organizational requirements are specific or unique (favors custom development) versus standard (favors off-the-shelf).
  4. Availability of Suitable Packages: Whether appropriate ready-made software exists in the market that meets the core requirements.
  5. In-house Technical Expertise: The availability of internal staff with the skills needed for custom development or for customizing/integrating an off-the-shelf package.
*(Note: Only four factors were required)*
(b) Types of Decisions and Management Levels:
  • (i) Unstructured Decisions: Non-routine, complex decisions requiring significant judgment, evaluation, and insight, where there is no predefined procedure. Typically made by **Top/Strategic Management**. Example: Deciding on entering a new international market.
  • (ii) Structured Decisions: Repetitive, routine decisions with clear procedures and rules for making them. Often programmable. Typically made by **Operational/Lower Management**. Example: Deciding whether to reorder inventory when stock reaches a certain level.
  • (iii) Semi-structured Decisions: Decisions with some defined procedures but also requiring human judgment and analysis. Often supported by DSS. Typically made by **Middle/Tactical Management**. Example: Developing a marketing budget based on sales forecasts and performance data.
(c) Reasons for Popularity of Digital Library Materials:
  1. Accessibility: Can be accessed remotely 24/7 from anywhere with an internet connection, unlike physical materials limited by library hours and location.
  2. Searchability: Digital text allows for quick searching for specific keywords or phrases within documents, improving research efficiency.
  3. Space Saving: Large volumes of digital materials can be stored electronically, requiring significantly less physical space than print collections.
  4. Portability: Users can carry vast amounts of digital material on portable devices (laptops, tablets, e-readers).
  5. Multimedia & Interactivity: Digital materials can incorporate multimedia elements (audio, video) and interactive features not possible with print.
*(Note: Only four reasons were required)*
(d) Uses of Expert System in Arbiter Hospital:
  1. Diagnosis Support: Assisting doctors by suggesting possible diagnoses based on patient symptoms and medical history entered into the system, leveraging a knowledge base of medical expertise.
  2. Treatment Recommendations: Providing recommendations for treatment plans based on the diagnosed condition, patient data, and established medical protocols.
  3. Medical Training: Serving as a training tool for junior doctors or medical students by presenting case scenarios and expert reasoning.
  4. Interpreting Medical Images/Data: Assisting radiologists or lab technicians in analyzing complex images (X-rays, MRIs) or lab results by identifying patterns or anomalies based on expert rules.
*(Note: Only three ways were required)*

Question 8

(a) (i) Outline four components of an information system plan. (4 marks)

(a) (ii) Explain one reason why an organization may choose not to connect its Management Information System to the Internet. (2 marks)

(b) Distinguish between value chain and value system as applied in Management Information Systems. (4 marks)

(c) Kombi Company has noted that some of its employees are resistant the newly installed information system. Explain three possible causes for this resistance. (6 marks)

(d) Consider the following issues at work places:

  • - Use of assigned access privileges to make changes to an MIS for personal gain.
  • - A company that installs copyrighted software.
  • - Requesting for a fee to award a job to an unqualified applicant.
  • - An employer who fails to pay employees their dues.

Classify each of them as either moral or legal issues. (4 marks)

Answer

(a) (i) Components of an Information System Plan:
  1. Alignment with Business Strategy: How the IS plan supports overall organizational goals and strategies.
  2. IS Architecture: The blueprint for the organization's data, applications, and technology infrastructure.
  3. Project Portfolio: A list of prioritized IS projects to be undertaken, including scope, objectives, and timelines.
  4. Resource Allocation Plan: Details on budget, staffing, hardware/software acquisition, and other resources needed for the plan.
  5. Implementation Schedule: A timeline outlining when different projects or phases will be executed.
*(Note: Only four components were required)*
(a) (ii) Reason Not to Connect MIS to Internet:

Security Concerns: Connecting an internal MIS, especially one containing sensitive financial or operational data, directly to the public internet significantly increases its exposure to external threats like hacking, malware, denial-of-service attacks, and data breaches. Keeping it isolated ("air-gapped") or strictly limiting external connections enhances security.

(b) Value Chain vs. Value System:
  • Value Chain (Porter): A model that views an organization as a series of primary and support activities that add value to its products or services. Primary activities include inbound logistics, operations, outbound logistics, marketing & sales, and service. Support activities include infrastructure, HR, technology development, and procurement. MIS can support and optimize activities within this internal chain.
  • Value System: A larger concept encompassing the value chains of an organization, its suppliers, distributors, and customers. It recognizes that value creation involves multiple organizations working together. MIS can be used to coordinate and optimize activities across this entire system (e.g., through SCM or CRM systems linking different entities).
(c) Causes for Employee Resistance to New IS (Kombi Company):
  1. Fear of Job Loss/Change: Employees may fear that the new system will automate their tasks, leading to redundancy, or significantly change their job roles and required skills.
  2. Lack of Perceived Benefit/Need: If employees don't understand why the change is necessary or how the new system will benefit them or the organization, they are less likely to embrace it.
  3. Inadequate Training/Support: Insufficient training or lack of readily available support can lead to frustration, errors, and a negative perception of the new system.
  4. Disruption of Routines/Comfort Zone: People are often comfortable with existing routines, and a new system forces them to learn new processes, which can be perceived as inconvenient or difficult.
  5. Poor Communication/Lack of Involvement: If the implementation process lacks clear communication or fails to involve users, it can lead to rumors, mistrust, and resistance.
*(Note: Only three causes were required)*
(d) Classification of Workplace Issues:
  • Use of assigned access privileges for personal gain: **Moral & potentially Legal** (Depending on specific actions and company policy/laws regarding fraud or data misuse).
  • Installing copyrighted software without license: **Legal & Moral** (Violates copyright law and ethical principles).
  • Requesting a fee for a job award: **Moral & Legal** (Bribery/corruption, likely illegal and unethical).
  • Employer failing to pay dues: **Legal & Moral** (Violates labor laws/contracts and ethical obligations).

Note: Many actions have both moral and legal dimensions. The classification highlights the primary nature.
