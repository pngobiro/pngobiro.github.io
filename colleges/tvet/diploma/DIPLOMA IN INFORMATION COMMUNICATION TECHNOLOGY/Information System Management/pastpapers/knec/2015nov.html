Question 1 (20 marks)

(a) Outline four features of an executive information system (EIS). (4 marks)

(b) Define each of the following terms as applied in management information systems:

  • (i) interaction; (2 marks)
  • (ii) interdependence. (2 marks)

(c) The management of KPNG Company has purchased a transaction processing system for its operations. Explain three functions of the system to the company. (6 marks)

(d) Explain three circumstances under which an organization would adopt the use of expert systems in its operations. (6 marks)

Answer

(a) Features of an Executive Information System (EIS):

  • Graphical User Interface (GUI): Presents information visually through charts, graphs, and dashboards for easy comprehension by senior executives.
  • Drill-Down Capabilities: Allows executives to explore summarized data in greater detail by clicking on specific elements to see underlying figures or reports.
  • Access to External Data: Integrates external data sources (e.g., market trends, competitor information, economic news) alongside internal data for a broader strategic perspective.
  • Ease of Use: Designed for executives who may not be technically savvy, focusing on intuitive navigation and minimal training requirements.

(b) Terms in MIS:

  • (i) Interaction: Refers to the way different components or subsystems within an information system communicate and work together, exchanging data or signals to perform tasks.
  • (ii) Interdependence: Describes the mutual reliance between different components or subsystems. Changes in one part of the system often affect other parts, highlighting the need for coordination.

(c) Functions of a Transaction Processing System (TPS) for KPNG Company:

  • Data Capture: Recording routine business transactions as they occur (e.g., sales orders, payments, inventory updates, payroll entries).
  • Transaction Processing: Performing calculations, updating records, and generating documents related to the transaction (e.g., calculating totals, updating stock levels, printing receipts).
  • Database Maintenance: Ensuring the organization's operational database is kept up-to-date with the details of all processed transactions.

(d) Circumstances for Adopting Expert Systems:

  • Scarcity of Human Expertise: When specialized human knowledge is rare, expensive, or difficult to retain (e.g., diagnosing rare diseases, complex equipment troubleshooting).
  • Need for Consistency: When decisions need to be made consistently according to complex rules or regulations, reducing variability between human decision-makers.
  • High-Volume, Repetitive Tasks: When expert knowledge needs to be applied to a large number of similar cases quickly and efficiently (e.g., credit scoring, insurance underwriting).

Answer

(a) Terms in Project Management:

  • (i) Project Planning Document (PPD): A comprehensive document outlining the project's scope, objectives, deliverables, schedule, resources, budget, risks, assumptions, and management approach. It serves as a roadmap for the project execution and control.
  • (ii) Total Quality Management (TQM): A management philosophy focused on continuous improvement of processes, products, and services involving all members of an organization to meet or exceed customer expectations. It emphasizes quality in all aspects of the business.

(b) Factors Contributing to MIS Modification Recommendation (ABX Company):

  • Changing Business Needs: The company's strategies, processes, or market environment may have changed, requiring the MIS to be updated to provide new functionalities or support different workflows.
  • Technological Obsolescence: The existing MIS might be running on outdated hardware or software that is no longer supported, inefficient, or incompatible with newer technologies.
  • Performance Issues: The current system might be slow, unreliable, difficult to use, or unable to handle the growing volume of data or transactions, necessitating modifications for improvement.

(c) Trojan Horse vs. Worm:

  • Trojan Horse: Malicious software disguised as legitimate or desirable software. It relies on users executing it, after which it performs harmful actions (e.g., stealing data, installing backdoors) without the user's knowledge. It does not self-replicate.
  • Worm: A standalone malware program that replicates itself to spread to other computers, often exploiting security vulnerabilities in networks. Unlike viruses, worms do not need to attach themselves to existing programs or require user interaction to spread.

(d) Components of a Decision Support System (DSS):

  2. Model Management System (MBMS): Stores and manages various analytical models (statistical, financial, optimization, simulation) that users can apply to the data.
  3. Dialogue Management System (User Interface): Allows the user to interact with the DSS, input data/parameters, select models, and view results (often graphically).

Question 3 (20 marks)

(a) Explain each of the following modes of data processing as applied in management information systems:

  • (i) real-time transaction; (2 marks)
  • (ii) batch. (2 marks)

(b) Differentiate between efficiency and effectiveness as used in management information systems. (4 marks)

(c) Project budgets could be implemented using the top-down approach in an organization.

  • (i) Explain two advantages of this approach. (4 marks)
  • (ii) Outline two disadvantages of the approach. (2 marks)

(d) Explain three strategies that project managers could use to manage risks. (6 marks)

Answer

(a) Modes of Data Processing:

  • (i) Real-time Transaction Processing: Data is processed immediately as each transaction occurs. The system's records are updated instantaneously, reflecting the current state (e.g., airline reservation systems, ATM withdrawals).
  • (ii) Batch Processing: Transactions are collected over a period (e.g., end of day/week) and processed together in a single run (batch). Suitable for non-critical tasks where immediate updates are not required (e.g., payroll processing, generating monthly bills).

(b) Efficiency vs. Effectiveness in MIS:

  • Efficiency: Refers to how well the system uses resources (time, cost, personnel) to perform its tasks. An efficient system does things right, minimizing waste and maximizing output with given inputs (e.g., processing transactions quickly, using minimal storage).
  • Effectiveness: Refers to how well the system achieves its intended goals or meets user requirements. An effective system does the right things, providing accurate, relevant, and timely information that supports business objectives and decision-making.

(c) Top-Down Budgeting Approach:

  • (i) Advantages:
    1. Strategic Alignment: Ensures the overall project budget aligns with the organization's strategic goals and financial constraints as set by senior management.
    2. Faster Process: Can be quicker to establish an overall budget figure compared to building it up from detailed task estimates (bottom-up).
  • (ii) Disadvantages:
    1. Less Accurate/Realistic: Budgets set by top management without detailed input from those performing the work may be unrealistic or insufficient for actual project needs.
    2. Reduced Buy-in: Team members may feel less ownership or commitment to a budget imposed from above without their input.

(d) Strategies for Managing Project Risks:

  • Risk Avoidance: Changing the project plan to eliminate the risk or protect the project objectives from its impact (e.g., changing scope, using a proven technology instead of a new one).
  • Risk Mitigation: Taking steps to reduce the probability or impact of the risk occurring (e.g., implementing redundancy, conducting more testing, improving communication).
  • Risk Transfer: Shifting the risk or its consequences to a third party (e.g., buying insurance, outsourcing a high-risk component, using fixed-price contracts).

Question 4 (20 marks)

(a) Explain each of the following terms as used in organizational change:

  • (i) rationalization; (2 marks)
  • (ii) business process re-engineering. (2 marks)

(b) Differentiate between a hacker and a cracker as used in computer security. (4 marks)

(c) Jabil Company has incorporated an information system for its decision making process. Explain three ways that the system would help improve the decision making process. (6 marks)

(d) Explain three circumstances that would cause an organization to incorporate an information system in the production section. (6 marks)

Answer

(a) Terms in Organizational Change:

  • (i) Rationalization (of Procedures): Streamlining standard operating procedures by removing obvious bottlenecks or inefficiencies, often as a precursor to automation. Less radical than BPR.
  • (ii) Business Process Re-engineering (BPR): Fundamental rethinking and radical redesign of business processes to achieve dramatic improvements in performance (cost, quality, speed, service). Often involves leveraging IT to enable entirely new ways of working.

(b) Hacker vs. Cracker:

  • Hacker: Originally referred to someone skilled in computer systems, often exploring system capabilities, sometimes pushing boundaries. While sometimes associated with unauthorized access, the term isn't inherently malicious and can include ethical hackers who test security.
  • Cracker: An individual who breaks into computer systems with malicious intent, such as stealing data, causing damage, or disrupting services. This term specifically denotes criminal activity.

(c) How IS Improves Decision Making (Jabil Company):

  • Faster Access to Information: Provides quick access to relevant internal and external data, reducing the time needed to gather information for decision-making.
  • Improved Analysis Capabilities: Offers tools (reporting, modeling, visualization) to analyze data, identify trends, evaluate alternatives, and understand potential outcomes more effectively.
  • Enhanced Information Quality: Can improve the accuracy, consistency, and completeness of information compared to manual methods, leading to more reliable decisions.

(d) Circumstances for Incorporating IS in Production:

  • Need for Improved Efficiency/Productivity: Automating tasks, optimizing machine utilization, or improving workflow through systems like Manufacturing Execution Systems (MES) or robotics.
  • Requirement for Better Quality Control: Implementing systems for automated inspection, statistical process control (SPC), or tracking materials/products to ensure adherence to quality standards.
  • Complex Scheduling/Coordination Needs: Managing complex production schedules, coordinating material flow, or synchronizing different stages of production, especially in make-to-order or just-in-time (JIT) environments.

(b) Jade intends to write a term paper on ways in which system users could get involved in unethical use of information. Explain three such ways. (6 marks)

Answer

(a) Functions of an Office Automation System (OAS):

  • Document Management: Creating, editing, storing, retrieving, and sharing documents (e.g., word processing, spreadsheets, presentations).
  • Communication: Facilitating communication through email, instant messaging, calendaring, and scheduling.
  • Data Management: Simple database functions for managing lists, contacts, or basic records.
  • Task Management: Tools for managing personal or team tasks and deadlines.

(b) Unethical Use of Information by System Users (for Jade's paper):

  • Unauthorized Access/Disclosure: Accessing confidential employee records, customer data, or company financial information without permission and potentially sharing it inappropriately.
  • Personal Use of Resources: Using company computers and networks for excessive personal web browsing, online shopping, social media, or running a personal business, consuming resources and time.
  • Spreading Misinformation/Gossip: Using internal communication systems (email, chat) to spread rumors, gossip, or false information about colleagues or the company.

(c) Decision Making Model (MIS):

(Requires diagram, similar to Q8d in 2014n paper)

A common model used in MIS context is Herbert Simon's model, consisting of four stages:

  1. Intelligence: Recognizing and defining the problem or opportunity. Gathering data about the situation. What is the problem?
  2. Design: Developing and analyzing potential solutions or alternative courses of action. What are the alternatives?
  3. Choice: Selecting the best alternative based on defined criteria and analysis. Which alternative is best?
  4. Implementation: Putting the chosen solution into practice and monitoring its effectiveness. Is the solution working?

(d) Factors to Consider for Acquiring HR Management System (Reki Company):

  • Functionality and Modules: Does the system cover the required HR functions (e.g., recruitment, payroll, benefits administration, performance management, training records, compliance reporting)? Are the modules integrated?
  • Scalability and Flexibility: Can the system accommodate future growth in employee numbers and adapt to changing organizational structures or HR policies?
  • Ease of Use and Training: Is the system user-friendly for both HR staff and employees (if self-service features are included)? What level of training is required, and is it provided by the vendor?

Question 6 (20 marks)

(a) Explain the following terms as used in management information systems:

  • (i) computer aided design; (2 marks)
  • (ii) computer aided manufacture. (2 marks)

(b) Companies could acquire their information system through leasing method.

  • (i) Outline three advantages of this method. (3 marks)
  • (ii) Outline three limitations of the method. (3 marks)

(c) Explain three challenges experienced in regulating global information society. (6 marks)

(d) The analyst of Zippra Company has advised the company to acquire a security management system for its operations. Explain two functions of this software to the company. (4 marks)

Answer

(a) Terms in MIS:

  • (i) Computer-Aided Design (CAD): Software used by engineers, architects, and designers to create precise 2D and 3D drawings, models, and technical illustrations digitally. Improves design quality and speeds up the design process.
  • (ii) Computer-Aided Manufacturing (CAM): Software used to control machine tools and related machinery in the manufacturing process. Uses CAD models to generate toolpaths and instructions for automated production.

(b) Leasing Information Systems:

  • (i) Advantages:
    1. Lower Upfront Costs: Avoids large capital expenditure required for purchasing, making it easier to acquire systems with limited initial budget.
    2. Predictable Expenses: Lease payments are regular, predictable operating expenses, simplifying budgeting.
    3. Easier Upgrades: Often easier to upgrade to newer technology at the end of the lease term compared to disposing of owned, depreciated assets.
  • (ii) Limitations:
    1. Higher Total Cost: Over the long term, leasing can be more expensive than purchasing due to interest/financing charges included in payments.
    2. No Ownership/Equity: The company does not own the asset at the end of the lease (unless it's a lease-to-own agreement) and builds no equity.
    3. Contractual Obligations: Locked into lease payments for the term, even if the system is no longer needed or becomes obsolete.

(c) Challenges in Regulating Global Information Society:

  • Jurisdictional Issues: Differing laws and regulations across countries make it difficult to enforce rules consistently for activities (like data privacy, cybercrime, content control) that cross borders easily via the internet.
  • Rapid Technological Change: Laws and regulations often lag behind the rapid pace of technological development, making it hard to address new issues effectively.
  • Cultural Differences: Varying cultural norms and values regarding issues like free speech, privacy, and censorship complicate efforts to create universally accepted regulations.

(d) Functions of Security Management System (Zippra Company):

  • Access Control and Authentication: Managing user identities, verifying credentials (passwords, MFA), and enforcing permissions to ensure only authorized users access specific system resources.
  • Monitoring and Intrusion Detection/Prevention: Monitoring system logs and network traffic for suspicious activities, generating alerts, and potentially blocking malicious actions or unauthorized access attempts in real-time.

Question 7 (20 marks)

(a) Define each of the following types of maintenance as used in management information systems:

  • (i) corrective; (2 marks)
  • (ii) preventive. (2 marks)

(b) Planning is considered as an important function of an MIS in management. Explain three importance of this function to an organization. (6 marks)

(c) A certain company intends to use a matrix model for the management of its projects. Explain two advantages they are likely to achieve from this move. (4 marks)

(d) Most colleges have adopted the use of wireless fidelity (Wi-Fi) in their operations. Explain three reasons for this trend. (6 marks)

Answer

(a) Types of Maintenance in MIS:

  • (i) Corrective Maintenance: Fixing errors, bugs, or defects discovered in the system after it has been implemented. It's reactive, addressing problems as they arise.
  • (ii) Preventive Maintenance: Performing activities on a regular schedule to reduce the likelihood of future system failures (e.g., hardware checks, data backups, software patching). It's proactive.

(b) Importance of Planning Function of MIS:

  • Resource Allocation: Helps managers allocate resources (budget, personnel, time) effectively based on data-driven forecasts and analysis of needs versus availability.
  • Goal Setting and Alignment: Provides information to help set realistic organizational goals and ensures that departmental or individual plans align with overall strategic objectives.
  • Performance Measurement Baseline: Establishes the targets and metrics against which future performance will be measured, enabling effective control and evaluation.

(c) Advantages of Matrix Model for Project Management:

  • Efficient Resource Utilization: Allows specialized personnel (e.g., engineers, programmers) to be shared across multiple projects as needed, rather than being dedicated to a single project, improving overall resource efficiency.
  • Enhanced Communication and Information Flow: Facilitates communication and knowledge sharing between different functional departments as team members from various areas work together on the project.

(d) Reasons for Wi-Fi Adoption in Colleges:

  • Flexibility and Mobility: Allows students, faculty, and staff to access network resources and the internet from various locations across campus (classrooms, libraries, dorms, common areas) using laptops, tablets, and smartphones.
  • Support for Modern Learning Tools: Enables the use of online learning platforms, digital textbooks, collaborative tools, and streaming educational content that require internet connectivity.
  • Reduced Cabling Costs/Infrastructure Complexity: Eliminates the need for extensive wired network infrastructure in every room, potentially reducing installation and maintenance costs, especially in older buildings.

(a) Differences Between Strategic and Tactical Plans:

  • Time Horizon: Strategic plans focus on the long term (typically 3-5 years or more), while tactical plans focus on the shorter term (usually 1 year or less) to implement parts of the strategic plan.
  • Scope: Strategic plans are broad, covering the entire organization and its overall direction, while tactical plans are narrower, focusing on specific departments, functions, or projects.
  • Level of Detail: Strategic plans are high-level, outlining major goals and objectives, while tactical plans are more detailed, specifying actions, resources, and timelines required to achieve specific targets.

(b) Factors Causing Organizations to Fail Objectives:

  • Poor Planning/Unclear Objectives: Objectives that are vague, unrealistic, not communicated effectively, or lack clear action plans are difficult to achieve.
  • Inadequate Resources: Lack of sufficient funding, personnel, skills, technology, or time needed to execute the plans required to meet objectives.
  • Failure to Adapt to External Changes: Not recognizing or responding effectively to changes in the market, competition, technology, regulations, or economic conditions that impact the organization's ability to achieve its goals.
