Question 1 (20 marks)

(a) Outline three functions of a Management Information Systems in an organization. (3 marks)

(b) Explain three challenges that an organization using a modern Management Information Systems face when dealing with ethical issues. (6 marks)

(c) A school Information Technology project is at the planning stage. Explain three activities that would be carried out at this stage. (6 marks)

(d) A certain organization decided to outsource some of its information systemâ€™s services.

  • (i) Outline two possible reasons for this approach. (2 marks)
  • (ii) Outline three benefits that the organization would derive from this approach. (3 marks)

Answer

(a) Functions of MIS:

  • Data Collection & Storage: Gathering internal and external data relevant to organizational operations and storing it in an organized manner.
  • Information Processing & Analysis: Transforming raw data into meaningful information through sorting, summarizing, calculating, and analyzing to support managerial tasks.
  • Information Dissemination & Reporting: Providing reports, dashboards, and alerts to managers at different levels to support decision-making, planning, and control.

(b) Ethical Challenges with Modern MIS:

  • Data Privacy Violations: The ease of collecting, storing, and analyzing vast amounts of personal data (employee, customer) increases the risk of misuse or unauthorized access, violating individual privacy rights.
  • Security Breaches: Modern interconnected systems are vulnerable to hacking and breaches, potentially exposing sensitive organizational or personal data, leading to financial loss and reputational damage.
  • Algorithmic Bias/Discrimination: Decision-making algorithms within MIS (e.g., hiring, credit scoring) can inadvertently perpetuate or even amplify existing societal biases if not carefully designed and audited, leading to unfair or discriminatory outcomes.

(c) Activities in IT Project Planning Stage (School):

  • Define Scope and Objectives: Clearly outlining what the project aims to achieve (e.g., implement a new student management system, upgrade network infrastructure), its boundaries, and specific, measurable goals.
  • Identify Requirements: Gathering detailed functional and non-functional requirements from stakeholders (teachers, administrators, students, parents) through interviews, surveys, or workshops.
  • Develop Schedule and Budget: Creating a timeline with key milestones and estimating the resources (personnel, hardware, software, funding) required for the project.

(d) Outsourcing IS Services:

  • (i) Reasons for Outsourcing:
    1. Cost Reduction: To leverage economies of scale or lower labor costs offered by specialized external providers, reducing internal IT expenses.
    2. Lack of Internal Expertise: When the organization lacks the necessary skills or personnel to manage specific complex IT functions or technologies effectively.
  • (ii) Benefits of Outsourcing:
    1. Access to Expertise/Technology: Gaining access to specialized skills, advanced technologies, and best practices without significant internal investment.
    2. Focus on Core Business: Allowing the organization to concentrate its resources and management attention on its primary activities rather than IT operations.
    3. Improved Service Levels (Potentially): Service Level Agreements (SLAs) with the provider can guarantee specific levels of performance, availability, and support.

Question 2 (20 marks)

(a) An Insurance Company intends to undertake an Information System project. Outline four challenges that a manager is likely to face while planning for it. (4 marks)

(b) (i) Outline four areas in which an Enterprise Resource Planning System could be used in a government organization. (4 marks)

(ii) Outline four goals of a transaction processing system to an organization. (4 marks)

(c) Explain two goals of optimal information technology resources utilizations. (4 marks)

(d) A light industry uses Management Information Systems in its tactical level of operations. Explain two functional tasks of this Management Information Systems in each of following areas:

  • (i) Production; (2 marks)
  • (ii) Accounting. (2 marks)

Answer

(a) Challenges in Planning IS Project (Insurance Company):

  • Defining Clear Requirements: Accurately capturing the complex and often evolving needs of different departments (underwriting, claims, finance) and translating them into specific system requirements.
  • Estimating Costs and Resources: Accurately predicting the time, budget, and personnel needed for development, especially with uncertainties in scope or technology.
  • Securing Stakeholder Buy-in: Gaining agreement and ongoing support from various stakeholders (management, users, IT staff) who may have different priorities or perspectives.
  • Managing Risks: Identifying and planning for potential risks such as technological challenges, budget overruns, resistance to change, or regulatory compliance issues specific to the insurance industry.

(b) ERP and TPS:

  • (i) ERP Areas in Government Organization:
    1. Financial Management: Budgeting, accounting, procurement, grant management.
    2. Human Resources: Payroll, personnel administration, recruitment, benefits management.
    3. Supply Chain/Logistics: Managing procurement, inventory, and distribution of public resources or assets.
    4. Asset Management: Tracking and managing government-owned assets (buildings, vehicles, equipment).
  • (ii) Goals of Transaction Processing System (TPS):
    1. Process Data Efficiently: Handle large volumes of routine transactions quickly and cost-effectively.
    2. Maintain Data Integrity: Ensure accuracy, consistency, and reliability of transaction data through validation and error checking.
    3. Produce Timely Documents/Reports: Generate necessary outputs like receipts, invoices, or operational reports promptly.
    4. Update Organizational Databases: Keep operational databases current with the details of all processed transactions.

(c) Goals of Optimal IT Resource Utilization:

  • Maximize Return on Investment (ROI): Ensuring that investments in IT hardware, software, and personnel deliver tangible business value and contribute effectively to organizational goals.
  • Minimize Waste/Cost Efficiency: Utilizing IT resources (processing power, storage, network bandwidth, licenses) efficiently to avoid unnecessary expenditure and reduce operational costs.

(d) Tactical MIS Tasks (Light Industry):

  • (i) Production:
    1. Production Scheduling: Developing weekly or monthly production schedules based on demand forecasts and resource availability.
    2. Inventory Management: Monitoring inventory levels, determining reorder points, and analyzing stock turnover rates.
  • (ii) Accounting:
    1. Budget Analysis: Comparing actual departmental spending against budgeted amounts and analyzing variances.
    2. Cost Accounting: Analyzing production costs, calculating product profitability, and identifying areas for cost reduction.

Question 3 (20 marks)

(a) (i) Outline two factors that affect decision making in an organization. (2 marks)

(ii) Anewty Ventures Ltd intends to hire a consultant to assist in establishment of effective decision-making process. Outline the decision-making process cycle that the consultant would use in order to achieve the goal. (7 marks)

(b) Explain two reasons that have led to slow growth of information societies in developing nations. (4 marks)

(c) Many organizations are opting to use mobile phones for their business processes. Outline three challenges that they would face from this approach. (3 marks)

(d) Distinguish between executive information system and decision support system as used in Management Information Systems. (4 marks)

Answer

(a) Decision Making:

  • (i) Factors Affecting Decision Making:
    1. Information Availability/Quality: Decisions are limited by the amount, accuracy, and relevance of information available to the decision-maker.
    2. Cognitive Biases/Personal Judgment: Individual biases, experiences, risk tolerance, and intuition can significantly influence how information is interpreted and which alternatives are chosen.
  • (ii) Decision-Making Process Cycle (Consultant for Anewty Ventures): (Similar to Simon's Model)
    1. Intelligence: Identify and clearly define the specific problem or opportunity requiring a decision within Anewty Ventures. Gather relevant data.
    2. Design: Brainstorm and develop potential alternative solutions or courses of action. Analyze the feasibility, costs, and benefits of each alternative.
    3. Choice: Evaluate the alternatives against predefined criteria (e.g., cost, effectiveness, alignment with goals) and select the most suitable option.
    4. Implementation: Develop an action plan, allocate resources, and put the chosen decision into practice.
    5. Monitoring/Review: Track the results of the implemented decision, measure outcomes against objectives, and gather feedback to make adjustments or inform future decisions.

(b) Reasons for Slow Growth of Information Societies (Developing Nations):

  • Lack of Infrastructure: Insufficient or unreliable access to electricity, telecommunications networks (especially broadband internet), and affordable computing devices hinders widespread adoption and use of information technologies.
  • Low Digital Literacy/Skills Gap: A significant portion of the population may lack the basic digital skills and education needed to effectively use computers, the internet, and information systems, limiting participation.

(c) Challenges of Using Mobile Phones for Business Processes:

  • Security Risks: Mobile devices are often more vulnerable to theft, loss, malware, and insecure Wi-Fi connections, increasing the risk of data breaches or unauthorized access to company systems.
  • Integration Issues: Integrating mobile applications and data seamlessly with existing backend enterprise systems can be complex and costly.
  • Device Management/Support: Managing a diverse range of employee-owned or company-issued devices (BYOD/COPE), ensuring compatibility, providing support, and enforcing policies can be challenging.

(d) Executive Information System (EIS) vs. Decision Support System (DSS):

  • Target User: EIS are specifically designed for top-level executives, while DSS are typically used by middle managers or business analysts.
  • Decision Type: EIS primarily supports unstructured, strategic decisions requiring high-level summaries and external context, whereas DSS supports semi-structured decisions requiring more detailed analysis and modeling.
  • Data Focus: EIS emphasizes graphical displays, KPIs, and access to external data, while DSS often involves more detailed internal data and allows users to manipulate data and models interactively.

Question 4 (20 marks)

(a) Explain two circumstances that could necessitate maintenance of information system. (4 marks)

(b) Many organizations keep on acquiring new information system for their operations. Explain three reasons for this. (6 marks)

(c) Apexet Company Ltd has an IS project that is in progress. Explain two circumstances that would make the project to halt before its scheduled completion time. (4 marks)

(d) With the aid of a diagram, describe three distinctive levels of management in an organisation indicating in each case an appropriate information system. (6 marks)

Answer

(a) Circumstances Necessitating IS Maintenance:

  • System Errors/Bugs: Corrective maintenance is needed when users discover errors, the system produces incorrect output, or it fails to function as intended.
  • Changes in Environment: Adaptive maintenance is required when there are changes in the business environment (e.g., new regulations, business processes) or technical environment (e.g., operating system upgrades, new hardware) that the system must accommodate.

(b) Reasons for Acquiring New Information Systems:

  • Technological Obsolescence: Existing systems become outdated, unsupported, inefficient, or incompatible with newer technologies, hindering performance or integration capabilities.
  • Changing Business Needs/Strategy: New business strategies, expansion into new markets, mergers/acquisitions, or changes in business processes may require functionalities not supported by the current system.
  • Competitive Pressure: Organizations may need to adopt new systems to match or exceed the capabilities of competitors, improve customer service, or gain a competitive edge through innovation.

(c) Circumstances Halting an IS Project (Apexet Company):

  • Budget Depletion/Overrun: If the project significantly exceeds its allocated budget and further funding is unavailable or deemed unjustified, management may decide to halt it.
  • Change in Strategic Priorities: The organization's strategic goals may shift, making the project no longer relevant or a high priority compared to other initiatives, leading to its cancellation or indefinite postponement.

Question 5 (20 marks)

(a) Differentiate between choosing process and monitoring process during acquisition of an information system. (4 marks)

(b) The support of top management is necessary for success of an information system in organization. Explain three roles that they play during the planning phase. (6 marks)

(c) An information system designer in an institution has been tasked to develop a secure internet connection. Outline four mechanisms the designer would put in place to ensure security of data. (4 marks)

(d) Explain three circumstances that would lead an organization to automate its processes. (6 marks)

Answer

(a) Choosing vs. Monitoring Process (IS Acquisition):

  • Choosing Process: Involves evaluating different system alternatives (e.g., different vendors, build vs. buy options) against defined criteria (functionality, cost, technical requirements) and selecting the best fit for the organization's needs. Focus is on selection.
  • Monitoring Process: Involves tracking the performance of the chosen system and the acquisition/implementation project against the plan. This includes monitoring vendor performance, project milestones, budget adherence, and ensuring the system meets requirements after implementation. Focus is on control and evaluation.

(b) Top Management Roles in IS Planning Phase:

  • Setting Strategic Direction: Ensuring the IS plan aligns with the overall business strategy and providing a clear vision for how technology will support organizational goals.
  • Allocating Resources: Approving the necessary budget and committing personnel resources required for successful IS planning and subsequent implementation.
  • Championing the Initiative: Demonstrating visible support for the IS planning process, communicating its importance, and overcoming potential organizational resistance.

(c) Mechanisms for Secure Internet Connection (Data Security):

  • Firewall Implementation: Installing and configuring firewalls to control incoming and outgoing network traffic based on predefined security rules, blocking unauthorized access attempts.
  • Data Encryption: Using encryption protocols (like TLS/SSL for web traffic, VPNs for remote access) to protect data transmitted over the internet from eavesdropping. Encrypting sensitive data stored on servers.
  • Intrusion Detection/Prevention Systems (IDPS): Deploying systems to monitor network traffic for suspicious patterns or known attack signatures and alerting administrators or automatically blocking malicious activity.
  • Strong Authentication: Implementing robust methods (e.g., multi-factor authentication) to verify the identity of users accessing internal resources via the internet connection.

(d) Circumstances Leading to Process Automation:

  • High Volume of Repetitive Tasks: When processes involve numerous manual, repetitive steps that are time-consuming and prone to human error (e.g., data entry, invoice processing).
  • Need for Increased Speed/Efficiency: When faster processing times are required to meet customer demands, improve turnaround times, or gain a competitive advantage.
  • Requirement for High Accuracy/Consistency: When tasks require high precision and consistency that is difficult for humans to maintain reliably (e.g., complex calculations, quality control checks).

Answer

(a) Factors Influencing IS Maintenance Cost:

  • System Complexity and Age: Older, more complex systems, or those built with outdated technology, are often harder and more expensive to maintain due to lack of documentation, scarcity of skilled personnel, and integration challenges.
  • Frequency and Type of Maintenance: The amount of corrective, adaptive, perfective, and preventive maintenance required directly impacts cost. Systems requiring frequent changes or fixing many bugs will be more costly to maintain.

(b) Critical Success Factor (CSF) vs. Downsizing (MIS Planning):

  • Critical Success Factor (CSF): A planning methodology focusing on identifying the few key areas where an organization must perform well to achieve its strategic goals. MIS planning then prioritizes systems that support these CSFs.
  • Downsizing (in IT context): Refers to migrating applications from larger computers (mainframes, minicomputers) to smaller, networked platforms (like client-server systems or PCs), often aiming to reduce hardware and maintenance costs. It's an implementation strategy, not primarily a planning methodology like CSF.

  • Empowered Employees/Changed Roles: Jobs may become more multi-dimensional, employees often work in teams and are given more decision-making authority within the redesigned processes.

Question 7 (20 marks)

(a) Explain one purpose of a project management software in each of the following activities:

  • (i) monitoring; (2 marks)
  • (ii) reporting. (2 marks)

(b) A Government institution is planning to set up a new department to oversee its resources. Explain two categories of resources that the new department would consider. (4 marks)

(c) Explain three threats to a business that may result from computer crimes. (6 marks)

(d) A manager in an entertainment industry intends to use data driven decision support system to design a new product. Describe three characteristics that this system should posses. (6 marks)

Answer

(a) Purpose of Project Management Software:

  • (i) Monitoring: To track actual progress of tasks, resource usage, and costs against the planned schedule and budget, allowing managers to identify variances and potential issues early.
  • (ii) Reporting: To generate various reports (e.g., Gantt charts, milestone reports, budget summaries, resource allocation reports) for stakeholders, providing visibility into project status and performance.

(b) Categories of Resources for New Government Department:

  • Human Resources: The personnel required to staff the department, including their skills, roles, responsibilities, and associated costs (salaries, benefits, training).
  • Financial Resources: The budget allocated to the department for operational expenses, salaries, procurement of assets, and project funding.

(c) Threats to Business from Computer Crimes:

  • Financial Loss: Direct theft of funds, costs associated with system downtime and recovery, reputational damage leading to lost customers, and potential fines or legal fees resulting from data breaches.
  • Data Breaches/Loss of Confidential Information: Theft or unauthorized disclosure of sensitive customer data, employee records, intellectual property, or trade secrets, leading to loss of competitive advantage and legal liability.
  • Operational Disruption: Malware (like ransomware) or denial-of-service (DoS) attacks can halt critical business operations, preventing access to systems and data, and disrupting service delivery.

(d) Characteristics of Data-Driven DSS for New Product Design (Entertainment):

  • Access to Diverse Data Sources: Ability to integrate and analyze various data types relevant to entertainment products, such as customer demographics, viewing habits, social media trends, competitor product performance, and market research data.
  • Advanced Analytical/Modeling Capabilities: Tools for segmentation, predictive modeling, trend analysis, and "what-if" scenarios to help identify target audiences, predict product success, and optimize design features based on data insights.
  • Interactive Visualization Tools: User-friendly interface with dashboards and visualization tools (charts, graphs) to help managers easily explore data, understand complex relationships, and present findings related to potential product designs.

Question 8 (20 marks)

(a) Explain the importance of each of the following qualities of a good management information system:

  • (i) Completeness; (2 marks)
  • (ii) Economical; (2 marks)
  • (iii) Timeliness. (2 marks)

(b) Outline two benefits of using a Gantt chart as a project management tool. (2 marks)

(c) Gaze Company has embraced the use of Management Information System for competitive advantage. Explain three impacts that this approach may have caused to the company. (6 marks)

(d) Some personnel in an organization could be a threat to a stable Management Information System. Explain three approaches that should apply in order to avert these threats. (6 marks)

Answer

(a) Importance of MIS Qualities:

  • (i) Completeness: Information should include all the necessary data required for a specific decision or task. Incomplete information can lead to misunderstandings or flawed conclusions.
  • (ii) Economical: The value derived from the information should justify the cost of collecting, processing, and disseminating it. Information systems should be cost-effective.
  • (iii) Timeliness: Information must be available when it is needed for decision-making. Outdated information loses its value and can lead to missed opportunities or poor responses.

(b) Benefits of Using Gantt Charts:

  • Visual Scheduling: Provides a clear, graphical overview of project tasks, their durations, and planned start/end dates against a timeline.
  • Progress Tracking: Allows easy comparison of actual progress against the planned schedule, helping to identify delays and monitor overall project status.

(c) Impacts of Using MIS for Competitive Advantage (Gaze Company):

  • Improved Efficiency/Lower Costs: Automating processes and optimizing resource allocation through MIS can lead to lower operational costs, potentially allowing for more competitive pricing or higher profit margins.
  • Enhanced Customer Service: Using MIS (like CRM systems) to understand customer needs better, personalize offerings, or provide faster service can increase customer loyalty and differentiation.
  • Faster Innovation/Product Development: Utilizing MIS for market analysis, R&D collaboration, or streamlining design processes can help the company bring new products/services to market faster than competitors.

(d) Approaches to Avert Threats from Personnel:

  • Access Controls and Monitoring: Implementing strong authentication, authorization based on the principle of least privilege, and monitoring user activity logs to detect and deter unauthorized actions or misuse.
  • Training and Awareness Programs: Educating employees about security policies, ethical use of information systems, common threats (like phishing), and the consequences of misuse.
  • Clear Policies and Enforcement: Establishing clear Acceptable Use Policies (AUPs) regarding system usage and consistently enforcing consequences for violations.
