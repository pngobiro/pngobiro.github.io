Question 1 (20 marks)

(a) Explain each of the following types of systems as used in management information systems:

  • (i) knowledge management system; (2 marks)
  • (ii) supply chain management system. (2 marks)

(b) Susan intends to purchase a transaction process management system for her company. Explain three characteristics that she should consider. (6 marks)

(c) Distinguish between technical and behavioral approaches to management information systems. (4 marks)

(d) Zilpa, an ICT expert at JTL. Company intends to write a report on the negative impacts of information systems to its users. Explain three impacts that she could mention. (6 marks)

Answer

(a) Types of Systems:

  • (i) Knowledge Management System (KMS): A system designed to support the creation, capture, storage, retrieval, sharing, and utilization of organizational knowledge and expertise. It helps organizations leverage their collective intelligence, improve decision-making, and foster innovation. Examples include document repositories and expert directories.
  • (ii) Supply Chain Management (SCM) System: A system managing the flow of goods, data, and finances from raw material procurement to final product delivery. It aims to optimize efficiency, reduce costs, and enhance collaboration among supply chain partners.

(b) Characteristics of a Transaction Processing System (TPS) for Susan:

  • Reliability and Consistency: The system must accurately record every transaction and maintain data integrity (ACID properties). Errors can be very costly.
  • High Performance/Throughput: It needs to process a large volume of transactions quickly and efficiently to avoid delays, especially in customer-facing scenarios.
  • Security: Robust measures are essential to protect sensitive financial and personal data from unauthorized access, fraud, and breaches.

(c) Technical vs. Behavioral Approaches:

  • Technical Approach: Focuses on the technology, mathematical models, and physical capabilities (hardware, software, networks). Emphasizes optimization using computer science and operations research.
  • Behavioral Approach: Concentrates on human and organizational aspects – implementation challenges, user adoption, training, ergonomics, social/ethical impacts. Draws from sociology, psychology, etc.

(d) Negative Impacts of Information Systems for Zilpa's Report:

  • Job Displacement/Deskilling: Automation can replace human roles or reduce the skill level required for certain tasks, potentially leading to unemployment or reduced job satisfaction.
  • Health Issues: Prolonged use can cause physical problems like Repetitive Strain Injury (RSI), Computer Vision Syndrome (eyestrain), and contribute to stress or burnout.
  • Privacy Concerns: The collection and storage of vast amounts of data increase the risk of privacy violations through breaches, surveillance, or misuse of personal information.

Question 2 (20 marks)

(a) Outline four advantages of automation to organizations that embrace it. (4 marks)

(b) Jupiter, a distribution company intends to install a Global Positioning System in all its vehicles. Explain three advantages that the company would derive from this system. (6 marks)

(c) Distinguish between office automation and executive information systems as used in management information systems. (4 marks)

(d) ABX Company prefers to outsource some of its management information system functions. Explain three benefits that may have contributed to their preference. (6 marks)

Answer

(a) Advantages of Automation:

  • Increased Efficiency/Productivity: Automated systems can perform tasks faster and more consistently than humans, often 24/7, leading to higher output.
  • Reduced Costs: Lower labor costs, fewer errors (reducing rework/waste), and optimized resource usage contribute to cost savings.
  • Improved Quality/Consistency: Automation minimizes human error, leading to more uniform product quality and service delivery.
  • Enhanced Safety: Automating dangerous or repetitive tasks can reduce workplace accidents and injuries.

(b) Advantages of GPS for Jupiter Distribution Company:

  • Improved Routing and Efficiency: Real-time tracking allows for optimized routes, reducing fuel consumption, mileage, and delivery times. Dispatchers can redirect vehicles based on traffic or new pickups.
  • Enhanced Security and Asset Tracking: The company can monitor vehicle locations to prevent theft, unauthorized use, and recover stolen vehicles more easily.
  • Better Customer Service: Accurate location data enables better Estimated Time of Arrival (ETA) predictions for customers and proof of delivery confirmation.

(c) Office Automation vs. Executive Information Systems:

  • Office Automation Systems (OAS): Focus on improving the productivity of data workers and clerical staff by automating routine office tasks like word processing, document management, scheduling, and communication (e.g., Microsoft Office Suite).
  • Executive Information Systems (EIS) / Executive Support Systems (ESS): High-level systems designed for senior management. They provide summarized internal and external data, often graphically, to support strategic, non-routine decision-making. Focus on ease of use, visualization, and access to key performance indicators (KPIs).

(d) Benefits of Outsourcing MIS Functions for ABX Company:

  • Cost Savings: Accessing specialized skills and infrastructure without the high cost of hiring, training, and maintaining an in-house IT team and equipment. Economies of scale from the provider.
  • Access to Expertise: Gaining access to specialized skills, advanced technologies, and best practices that might be unavailable or too expensive to develop internally.
  • Focus on Core Business: Allows the company to concentrate its resources and management attention on its primary business activities rather than managing complex IT functions.

Question 3 (20 marks)

(a) Outline four characteristics of information as a resource in an organization. (4 marks)

(b) Management information systems decisions are categorized into three. Explain each of this by stating the level in management where each is applied. (6 marks)

(c) Explain each of the following types of systems maintenance:

  • (i) corrective; (2 marks)
  • (ii) adaptive. (2 marks)

(d) Gabby, an information system manager at Globe Company advised the company to connect its Management Information System to the Internet. Explain three advantages that the company would gain from this connection. (6 marks)

Answer

(a) Characteristics of Information as a Resource:

  • Shareable: Unlike physical resources, information can be used by multiple people simultaneously without being depleted.
  • Expandable: Using information often generates more information, leading to growth in knowledge.
  • Transportable: Information can be moved quickly and easily across vast distances electronically.
  • Value is Contextual/Time-Dependent: The usefulness and value of information often depend on the situation and its timeliness. Old information may lose value.

(b) MIS Decision Categories and Management Levels:

  • Strategic Decisions: Long-term, unstructured decisions defining organizational objectives and goals. Made by Top-Level Management (e.g., CEOs, Board). Require summarized internal/external data, forecasts.
  • Tactical Decisions (Managerial Control): Medium-term decisions on resource allocation and performance monitoring to achieve strategic goals. Made by Middle-Level Management (e.g., Department Heads). Require semi-structured, periodic reports.
  • Operational Decisions (Operational Control): Short-term, structured decisions related to day-to-day activities and transactions. Made by Lower-Level Management/Supervisors. Require detailed, real-time transaction data.

(c) Types of Systems Maintenance:

  • (i) Corrective Maintenance: Fixing errors or bugs discovered in the system after implementation. Reactive process to address system failures or incorrect outputs.
  • (ii) Adaptive Maintenance: Modifying the system to cope with changes in the organizational or technical environment (e.g., new regulations, operating system upgrades, new business requirements).

(d) Advantages of Connecting MIS to the Internet for Globe Company:

  • Wider Accessibility: Authorized users (employees, partners, customers) can access the MIS from anywhere with an internet connection, improving flexibility and collaboration.
  • Integration with External Systems/Data: Enables connection to supplier systems, customer portals, market data feeds, cloud services, etc., enhancing functionality and data richness.
  • Improved Communication/Collaboration: Facilitates real-time data sharing and communication across different locations or departments via web-based interfaces or integrated tools.

Question 4 (20 marks)

(a) Explain two advantages of using Gantt charts in project management. (4 marks)

(b) Govi, the systems manager at JZX Company decided to use the direct procurement method during a procurement process. Explain three circumstances that may have influenced his decision. (6 marks)

(c) Differentiate between leading and monitoring as used project management. (4 marks)

(d) The management of Junta Company just installed an MIS in different departments. Some department staff are alleging that the system is not good but the real situation is that they did not want to use it. Explain three factors that the company should put in place to ensure that the staff embrace the system. (6 marks)

Answer

(a) Advantages of Gantt Charts:

  • Visual Clarity: Provides a clear, easy-to-understand visual representation of the project schedule, showing tasks, durations, start/end dates, and dependencies over time.
  • Progress Tracking: Allows project managers and stakeholders to easily track progress against the planned schedule, identify potential delays, and monitor task completion.

(b) Circumstances Influencing Govi's Use of Direct Procurement:

  • Urgency: The goods, works, or services are needed urgently, and following lengthy tendering processes would be impractical or detrimental (e.g., emergency repairs).
  • Single Source Availability: The required item is only available from one specific supplier (e.g., proprietary technology, unique expertise, specific spare parts).
  • Small Value Procurement: The value of the procurement is below a certain threshold defined by regulations, making open tendering inefficient or uneconomical.

(c) Leading vs. Monitoring in Project Management:

  • Leading: Involves influencing, motivating, and directing the project team to achieve project objectives. Focuses on people management, communication, conflict resolution, and building team cohesion. It's about guiding the team's efforts.
  • Monitoring: Involves tracking project performance against the plan, measuring progress, identifying variances, and assessing risks. Focuses on data collection, analysis, reporting, and control. It's about observing and measuring the project's status.

(d) Factors for MIS Acceptance at Junta Company:

  • User Involvement and Training: Involve staff early in the selection/implementation process and provide comprehensive, ongoing training to ensure they understand how to use the system effectively and see its benefits.
  • Management Support and Communication: Demonstrate strong commitment from top management, clearly communicate the reasons for the new system, its benefits (for both the company and employees), and address concerns openly.
  • Change Management Strategy: Implement a structured approach to manage the transition, addressing resistance, providing support, celebrating small wins, and potentially appointing system champions within departments.

Question 5 (20 marks)

(a) Outline four causes of project failure. (4 marks)

(b) (i) Explain the term data privacy as used in computer ethics. (2 marks)

(ii) Describe the term enterprise resource planning as used in management information systems. (2 marks)

(c) Marko Company has integrated the use of cloud computing in its operations. Explain three advantages that the company is likely to gain from this move. (6 marks)

(d) June, the IT manager of a hospital has advised the management to install an expert system for disease diagnosis. Explain three reasons that could have led to her advice. (6 marks)

Answer

(a) Causes of Project Failure:

  • Poor Planning/Scope Creep: Inadequate initial planning, unclear objectives, or uncontrolled changes to the project scope leading to budget overruns and missed deadlines.
  • Lack of Resources: Insufficient funding, personnel, or necessary tools/technology to complete the project successfully.
  • Poor Communication: Lack of clear and consistent communication among stakeholders, project team members, and management leading to misunderstandings and errors.
  • Inadequate Risk Management: Failure to identify, assess, and plan for potential risks, leaving the project vulnerable to unforeseen problems.

(b) Definitions:

  • (i) Data Privacy (Computer Ethics): Refers to the rights of individuals to control the collection, storage, use, and disclosure of their personal information held by organizations or processed by computer systems. It involves issues like consent, confidentiality, and security of personal data.
  • (ii) Enterprise Resource Planning (ERP) (MIS): Integrated software systems that manage core business processes (like finance, HR, manufacturing, supply chain, services, procurement) within a single, unified system using a common database. Aims to streamline processes and improve data visibility across the organization.

(c) Advantages of Cloud Computing for Marko Company:

  • Scalability and Flexibility: Easily scale computing resources (storage, processing power) up or down based on demand, paying only for what is used, without large upfront hardware investments.
  • Cost Savings: Reduced capital expenditure on hardware and infrastructure, lower maintenance costs, and potentially lower energy consumption. Pay-as-you-go models can be more predictable.
  • Accessibility and Collaboration: Access applications and data from anywhere with an internet connection, facilitating remote work and collaboration among employees.

(d) Reasons for June Recommending an Expert System for Disease Diagnosis:

  • Consistency and Accuracy: Expert systems apply diagnostic rules consistently, reducing variability between physicians and potentially improving diagnostic accuracy by considering a vast knowledge base.
  • Knowledge Capture and Retention: Captures the specialized knowledge of experienced doctors, making it available to less experienced staff and preserving expertise even if experts leave.
  • Decision Support/Second Opinion: Can act as a valuable tool to assist doctors by suggesting possible diagnoses, relevant tests, or treatment options, serving as a 'second opinion'.

Question 6 (20 marks)

(a) Define each of the following terms as used in management information systems:

  • (i) re-engineering; (2 marks)
  • (ii) rationalization. (2 marks)

(b) Jane, a systems administrator at Lazebo Company intends to put measures in place to prevent unauthorized access to the company’s information system. Explain three measures that she could enforce. (6 marks)

(c) Karri has been contracted to design a decision support system for Motif Company. Explain three components that he should consider. (6 marks)

(d) Distinguish between responsibility and accountability as used in management information systems. (4 marks)

Answer

(a) Definitions in MIS:

  • (i) Re-engineering (Business Process Re-engineering - BPR): The fundamental rethinking and radical redesign of business processes to achieve dramatic improvements in critical, contemporary measures of performance, such as cost, quality, service, and speed. Often involves leveraging information technology.
  • (ii) Rationalization (of Procedures): The streamlining of standard operating procedures, eliminating obvious bottlenecks or redundancies, so that automation can make operating procedures more efficient. Less radical than re-engineering.

(b) Measures to Prevent Unauthorized Access (Jane at Lazebo):

  • Authentication Controls: Implementing strong user identification methods like passwords, multi-factor authentication (MFA), biometrics, or smart cards to verify user identity before granting access.
  • Authorization Controls (Access Control Lists/Role-Based Access): Defining and enforcing permissions that specify which users or groups can access specific resources (files, applications, data) and what actions they can perform (read, write, execute, delete). Principle of least privilege.
  • Network Security Measures: Using firewalls to control network traffic, intrusion detection/prevention systems (IDPS) to monitor for malicious activity, and virtual private networks (VPNs) for secure remote access.

(c) Components of a Decision Support System (DSS) for Karri:

  • Database (or Data Warehouse): Stores and manages the relevant internal and external data needed for the decision-making process. May include historical data, transactional data, market data, etc.
  • Model Base: Contains various quantitative models (statistical, optimization, simulation, financial models) that users can apply to the data to analyze alternatives and generate insights.
  • User Interface (Dialogue Management): Allows the user to interact with the DSS, input data and parameters, select models, and view results in an understandable format (often graphical).

(d) Responsibility vs. Accountability in MIS:

  • Responsibility: Refers to the duty or obligation assigned to an individual to perform specific tasks or functions related to the information system (e.g., data entry, report generation, system maintenance). It's about *doing* the job.
  • Accountability: Refers to the answerability for the outcomes or results of those tasks or functions. It means being held liable for whether the assigned duties were performed correctly and achieved the desired results. It often resides higher up the management chain than responsibility.

Question 7 (20 marks)

(a) Define each of the following terms as used in project management:

  • (i) optimistic time; (2 marks)
  • (ii) most likely time. (2 marks)

(b) Explain two group decision-making techniques as used in management. (4 marks)

(c) Manile Company intends to use the open tendering method to procure its management information system. Outline three requirements of this method that the company should fulfill. (6 marks)

(d) Jake prefers not to procure the tailored software for his company. Outline three reasons for his preference. (6 marks)

Answer

(a) Definitions in Project Management (PERT/CPM):

  • (i) Optimistic Time (to): The minimum possible time required to accomplish a task, assuming everything proceeds exceptionally well with no delays.
  • (ii) Most Likely Time (tm): The most realistic or probable time required to accomplish a task, based on normal conditions and historical experience.

(b) Group Decision-Making Techniques:

  • Brainstorming: A technique to generate a large number of ideas quickly. Participants suggest ideas freely without criticism, which are then evaluated later. Encourages creativity and broad participation.
  • Nominal Group Technique (NGT): A structured method where individuals first generate ideas silently, then share them one by one in a round-robin fashion. Ideas are discussed for clarification, and then members rank them privately. Reduces dominance by individuals and encourages equal participation.

(c) Requirements for Open Tendering (Manile Company):

  • Public Advertisement: The tender opportunity must be advertised widely and publicly (e.g., newspapers, government gazettes, online portals) to allow any interested and qualified supplier to participate.
  • Clear and Non-Discriminatory Specifications: The tender documents must provide clear, precise, and objective technical specifications and evaluation criteria that do not unfairly favor or discriminate against any potential bidder.
  • Fair and Transparent Evaluation Process: A pre-defined, objective evaluation process must be followed consistently for all bids received, ensuring fairness and transparency in selecting the winning bidder based on the published criteria.

(d) Reasons for Jake Not Procuring Tailored Software:

  • High Cost: Custom-developed (tailored) software is typically much more expensive than off-the-shelf packages due to the significant development effort involved.
  • Long Development Time: Building software from scratch takes considerable time, which might not align with the company's immediate needs or project timelines.
  • Higher Risk: Custom development carries inherent risks, including potential bugs, delays, budget overruns, and the possibility that the final product may not fully meet requirements despite the effort. Off-the-shelf software is often proven and tested.

Question 8 (20 marks)

(a) Outline four characteristics the information systems planning model. (4 marks)

(b) Distinguish between direct end user and indirect end user as used in computer operations. (4 marks)

(c) Rima, an advisor at JXT Company intends to set up mechanisms that could be used to prevent repetitive strain injury to its employees. Explain three such mechanisms. (6 marks)

(d) Jeremy had the following characteristics about the levels of management:

  • (i) short range goals, plans and tactics.
  • (ii) day to day plans and supervisory task.
  • (iii) long range goals , plans and strategies.

Identify each of the levels and state at least one information requirement of each level. (6 marks)

Answer

(a) Characteristics of Information Systems Planning Model:

  • Strategic Alignment: Ensures that the IS plan directly supports the overall business strategy and objectives of the organization.
  • Resource Allocation Focus: Guides the allocation of limited resources (budget, personnel, technology) to the most critical IS projects and initiatives.
  • Architectural Definition: Defines the desired future state of the organization's IT infrastructure, applications, and data architecture.
  • Long-Term Perspective: Looks beyond immediate needs to anticipate future technological trends and business requirements over a multi-year horizon.

(b) Direct vs. Indirect End User:

  • Direct End User: An individual who directly interacts with the computer system by inputting data or commands and receiving output (e.g., a data entry clerk using an accounting system, a programmer using a development tool).
  • Indirect End User: An individual who uses the output or reports generated by a computer system but does not operate the system themselves (e.g., a manager reading a sales summary report, a customer receiving an automated bill).

(c) Mechanisms to Prevent Repetitive Strain Injury (RSI) at JXT Company:

  • Ergonomic Workstation Design: Providing adjustable chairs, desks, monitor stands, keyboards, and mice to allow employees to maintain neutral body postures and minimize strain.
  • Regular Breaks and Stretching: Encouraging employees to take short, frequent breaks away from their computers and perform recommended stretching exercises for hands, wrists, neck, and shoulders.
  • Training and Awareness Programs: Educating employees on proper posture, typing techniques, workstation setup, and the early signs of RSI to promote preventative habits.

(d) Management Levels and Information Requirements:

  • (iii) Long range goals, plans and strategies: Corresponds to the Strategic Level (Top Management). Information Requirement Example: Summarized internal performance data, external market trends, competitor analysis, economic forecasts.
  • (i) Short range goals, plans and tactics: Corresponds to the Tactical Level (Middle Management). Information Requirement Example: Departmental performance reports, budget variance analysis, resource allocation data, sales forecasts.
  • (ii) Day to day plans and supervisory task: Corresponds to the Operational Level (Lower Management/Supervisors). Information Requirement Example: Detailed transaction data, daily/weekly production schedules, inventory levels, employee attendance records.
