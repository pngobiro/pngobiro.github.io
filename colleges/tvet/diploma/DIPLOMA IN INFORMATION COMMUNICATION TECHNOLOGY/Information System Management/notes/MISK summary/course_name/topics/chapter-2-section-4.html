Types of Processing Systems

‚ö° Transaction Processing Modes

üì¶

Batch Processing

Business transactions are collected as they occur and are accumulated over a period of time and prepared for input as a single unit or batch. This is normally processed periodically e.g. monthly, examples include the payroll and the general ledger.

‚ö°

Interactive/Real-Time Processing

The transactions data are processed immediately without delay e.g. inventory systems, ticketing systems, reservation systems and ATMs. TPS can also be based on a client/server and internet technologies. Innovations such as online transactions processing (OLTP) can serve many people by allowing suppliers to enter the TPS and look at the inventory level.

üîÑ Types of TPS (Functional TPS)

  • üìù Order Processing: Necessary to ensure that customer orders are filled in a timely manner and that sufficient finished goods are always available. Sales people in many organizations will enter orders from client sites using portable wireless computers.
  • üì¶ Inventory/Stock Control: A manufacturing firm will have several inventories such as raw materials, work in progress (WIP) and finished goods. These could also contain maintenance parts. The inventory TPS monitors stock going out or coming in to the organization via its various warehouses and plants.
  • üí∞ Accounts Receivable/Sales Ledger: This manages the cash flow of the company by keeping track of the money owned on charges for the goods sold. The system updates records and raises an invoice/statement regularly.
  • üí≥ Accounts Payable/Purchase Ledger: Keeps track of money owed to company by various creditors or suppliers.
  • üíµ Payroll: This monitors the salaries and has the primary output as checks and pay slips to be distributed to the employees and provides a payroll register which is a summary of all the transactions.
  • üë• Human Resources: This system gives the information about the employees for personnel planning and government reporting and also the management of the company to benefit from other programmes.
  • üìä General Ledger: This is a record of the monetary transactions e.g. payment to suppliers, receipts from customers and payments to employees. It will give financial statements such as the trading and profit and loss account, the trial balance and the balance sheet.

üß† Knowledge Work and Office Systems

üí°

Knowledge Work Systems (KWS)

KWS are the systems that support the knowledge workers. They assist the knowledge workers in the creation and integration of new knowledge in the organization. The knowledge workers are the people who have the responsibility of creating or finding new sources of information. This includes engineers, doctors, scientists and programmers.

  • üîÑ Processing involves modeling and simulations
  • üìä Outputs are models and graphics for professional and technical staff
üíª

Office Automation Systems (OAS)

Office Automation systems are computer systems that include word processors, emails and scheduling systems, designed to increase the productivity of the data workers in the office. Data workers have less formal qualifications and tend to process information that is already available. This includes secretariat, accounts, filling clerks or managers whose jobs include the use, manipulation or dissemination of information.

  • üìù Input: Documents and schedules
  • üîÑ Processing: Delegation, scheduling and communication
  • üìä Output: Clerical schedules and mails for clerical workers
üîë

Key Points to Remember

  • üìå

    TPS can operate in batch or real-time processing modes depending on business needs.

  • üìå

    Different functional TPS serve specific business needs like order processing, inventory control, and accounting.

  • üìå

    KWS support creation and integration of new knowledge in organizations.

  • üìå

    OAS focus on increasing productivity of data workers through automation of office tasks.
