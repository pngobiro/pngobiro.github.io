What is a Management Information System (MIS)?

üìö Introduction

In this unit we are going to discuss the meaning and some important aspects of management information system. According to the Systems Approach of Management, an organization is a sum of the interrelated parts or sub systems. The management is responsible to make relations amongst these Sub-Systems into a total system. For this purpose Management Information System (MIS) becomes an unavoidable part of any organization. Management Information System is a systematized cyclic pattern of communication.

üí° System Concept

üìå

What is a System?

This is an organized grouping of components having certain interrelationships and working collectively to achieve a set of objectives. The system approach is a method or framework which helps us to analyze and explore the operations and interactions which exist in the systems around us.

System Characteristics

  • ‚û°Ô∏è Central objective - Among the interacting components there has to be goals or objectives focused by all.
  • ‚û°Ô∏è Interaction - This is the manner in which each component functions with other components of the system.
  • ‚û°Ô∏è Interdependence - This means that parts of the organization or system depend on one another.
  • ‚û°Ô∏è Integration - This is concerned with how a system is tied together.

üîÑ Elements of a System

  1. INPUT

    These are components that are required to be processed so as to give the output. This includes raw materials, energy and human labor.

  2. OUTPUT

    This is the product of processing e.g. goods or a service.

  3. PROCESSING

    This is the transformation or conversion procedure of input into output. i.e. production department or factory.

  4. CONTROL

    This element guides the system. In an organization this is the decision making body that controls the pattern of activities, governs input, processing and output e.g the management of a company.

  5. FEEDBACK

    Control in a dynamic system is achieved by feedback which is the measure of output against the standard.

  6. ENVIRONMENT

    This is the source of external elements that interact with the system or exert pressure e.g. customers, suppliers, government policies and competition.

  7. BOUNDARY

    These are the limits that identity its components, processes and interrelationships when it interfaces with another system e.g. in a company the invoicing function could be undertaken by the sales department whereas in another it's undertaken by the Account department.

üìù Meaning and Definition of MIS

MIS consists of three words, that is Management, Information and System. On the basis of these three words MIS can be defined as a system, which supplies information to Management. Actually, MIS is a process of providing necessary information to management. It helps planning, controlling and in taking different managerial decisions.

üí° Key Point:

MIS is the structured, formal, systematic part of communication system. MIS is an assemblage of personnel and facilities, organized into an integrated system by which relevant, adequate and timely information is supplied to the executives.

Expert Definitions

  • üìå According to Kennevans: MIS is an organised method of providing past, present and projection information relating to internal operations and external intelligence.
  • üìå According to Gordon B. Davis: MIS is an integrated man/machine system for providing information to support the operations management and decision making functions of an organization.
üìö Comprehensive Definition of MIS

A system to convert data from internal and external sources into information and to communicate that information, in an appropriate form, to managers at all levels in all functions to enable them to make timely and effective decisions for planning, directing and controlling the activities for which they are responsible.

‚ú® Characteristics of MIS

  • ‚úÖ Management oriented: Under MIS, necessary information is provided to each manager at the right time, in right form and a relevant one, which is required by the management for effective managerial decision.
  • ‚úÖ Future oriented: MIS is designed and developed keeping in view the future position of the business. It provides useful information based on projections for future action.
  • ‚úÖ Integrated: MIS takes a comprehensive view of interlocking sub-systems within the company, considering all aspects of production, marketing, accounting, financing, management etc.
  • ‚úÖ Common-data based: MIS deals with common data available in the business to provide information for effective managerial decisions.
  • ‚úÖ Long term planning: MIS is prepared for long term planning, avoiding outdated data and considering present situation and future trends.
  • ‚úÖ Control database: MIS is based on centralized data and information to supply data in a way that management can take important decisions.

Key Points to Remember

  • üìå

    MIS is a structured, formal, systematic part of communication system that provides information to management.

  • üìå

    A system is an organized grouping of components working collectively to achieve objectives.

  • üìå

    System elements include input, processing, output, control, feedback, environment, and boundary.

  • üìå

    MIS characteristics include being management oriented, future oriented, integrated, and based on centralized data.
