Information and Management

üí° Understanding Information

üìö Information Defined

Information is knowledge and understanding that is usable by the recipient. It reduces uncertainty and has surprise value. It must tell the recipient something not already known and which could not be predicted. If a message or report does not have these attributes, as far as the recipient is concerned, it contains merely data not information.

üí≠ Drucker's Perspective:

"The manager will never be able to get all the facts he should have. Most decisions have to be based on incomplete knowledge - either because the information is not available or it would cost too much in time and money to get it. There is nothing more treacherous or alas, more common, than the attempt to make precise decisions on the basis of coarse and incomplete information."

- Drucker, Management: Tasks, Responsibilities, Practices, 1993

  • üìà Increases knowledge

üîÑ Information Classifications

Information has many characteristics and can be classified in numerous ways including:

  1. By Source

    Internal, external, primary, secondary, Government

  2. By Nature

    Quantitative, qualitative, formal, informal

  3. By Level

    Strategic, tactical, operational

  4. By Time

    Historical, present, future

  5. By Frequency

    Continuous (real time), hourly, daily, monthly, annually

  6. By Use

    Planning, control, decision making

  7. By Form

    Written, aural, visual, sensory

  8. By Occurrence

    At planned intervals, occasional, on demand

  9. By Type

    Detailed, summarized, aggregated, abstracted

üë• Role of Information in Management

üíº

Management Perspective

A worthwhile extension to the well-known adage that 'management get things done through people' would be that, 'management get things done through people, by using relevant and timely information'.

üí° Important Note:

Although all managers need information they do not all need the same type of information. The type of information required is dependent on many factors including: the level of management, the task in hand, confidentiality, urgency, etc.

Key Points to Remember

  • üìå

    Information must reduce uncertainty and provide value to the recipient.

  • üìå

    Information can be classified in multiple ways including source, nature, level, and frequency.

  • üìå

    Different management levels require different types of information.

  • üìå

    Relevant information increases knowledge, reduces uncertainty, and is usable for its intended purpose.
