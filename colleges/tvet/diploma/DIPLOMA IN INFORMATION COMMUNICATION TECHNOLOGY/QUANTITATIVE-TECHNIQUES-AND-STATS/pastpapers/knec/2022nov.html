(b) Probability Calculations:

Let A = event computer A fails, P(A) = 0.15

Let B = event computer B fails, P(B) = 0.25

Let A' = event computer A works, P(A') = 1 - 0.15 = 0.85

Let B' = event computer B works, P(B') = 1 - 0.25 = 0.75

Assume independence.

  1. Probability both computers are working: \[ P(A' \cap B') = P(A') \times P(B') = 0.85 \times 0.75 = 0.6375 \] Probability = 0.6375
  2. Probability both computers are not working (failing): \[ P(A \cap B) = P(A) \times P(B) = 0.15 \times 0.25 = 0.0375 \] Probability = 0.0375
  3. Probability only one computer is working: \[ P(\text{only one working}) = P(A' \cap B) + P(A \cap B') \] \[ = (P(A') \times P(B)) + (P(A) \times P(B')) \] \[ = (0.85 \times 0.25) + (0.15 \times 0.75) \] \[ = 0.2125 + 0.1125 = 0.3250 \] Probability = 0.3250
  4. Probability at least one computer is working: \[ P(\text{at least one working}) = 1 - P(\text{both not working}) \] \[ = 1 - P(A \cap B) = 1 - 0.0375 = 0.9625 \] Probability = 0.9625

(a) Advantages of Simple Random Sampling:
  1. Unbiased Selection: Every member of the population has an equal and independent chance of being selected, minimizing selection bias.
  2. Simplicity: It's a straightforward method to understand and implement, especially with smaller populations or when a complete population list is available.
  3. Representativeness: If the sample size is large enough, it's likely to produce a sample that is representative of the population's characteristics.
  4. Statistical Inference: Allows for easy calculation of sampling error and enables researchers to make statistical inferences about the population based on the sample data.
  5. Foundation for Other Methods: Serves as the basis for more complex probability sampling techniques.

Question 4

(a) Explain the meaning of each of the following terms used in probability theory:

  1. mutually exclusive events;
  2. independent events;
  3. collectively exhaustive events;
  4. complimentary events.

(8 marks)

(b) The following data shows the profits made by a company for a period of 10 years.

YearSales (Ksh. millions)
200540
200655
200760
200860
200970
201075
201190
2012110
2013105
2014130
  1. Using the method of semi-averages, determine the trendline equation.
  2. Using the result in (i) above, determine the profit the company will make in the year 2015.

(12 marks)

Answer

(a) Meaning of Probability Terms:
  1. Mutually Exclusive Events: Events that cannot occur at the same time. If one event happens, the other(s) cannot. The occurrence of one precludes the occurrence of the others. Mathematically, \( P(A \cap B) = 0 \).
  2. Independent Events: The occurrence or non-occurrence of one event does not affect the probability of the other event occurring. Mathematically, \( P(A \cap B) = P(A) \times P(B) \).
  3. Collectively Exhaustive Events: A set of events where at least one of the events must occur when an experiment is performed. The union of these events covers the entire sample space. Mathematically, \( P(A \cup B \cup ...) = 1 \).
  4. Complementary Events: Two events are complementary if they are mutually exclusive and collectively exhaustive. One event is the complement (or negation) of the other. If A is an event, its complement is A'. Mathematically, \( P(A) + P(A') = 1 \).
(b) Method of Semi-Averages:

Step 1: Divide Data and Calculate Averages

Data has 10 years (2005-2014). Divide into two halves of 5 years each.

  • Half 1 (2005-2009): Profits = 40, 55, 60, 60, 70.
    • Average Profit \( \bar{Y}_1 = \frac{40 + 55 + 60 + 60 + 70}{5} = \frac{285}{5} = 57 \)
    • Mid-Point Year = 2007
  • Half 2 (2010-2014): Profits = 75, 90, 110, 105, 130.
    • Average Profit \( \bar{Y}_2 = \frac{75 + 90 + 110 + 105 + 130}{5} = \frac{510}{5} = 102 \)
    • Mid-Point Year = 2012

Step 2: Assign Time Variable (X) and Determine Points

Let X = Year number, with 2005 = 1.

  • Point 1: Mid-Year 2007 corresponds to X = 3. So, \( (\bar{X}_1, \bar{Y}_1) = (3, 57) \).
  • Point 2: Mid-Year 2012 corresponds to X = 8. So, \( (\bar{X}_2, \bar{Y}_2) = (8, 102) \).

Step 3: Calculate Trendline Equation (Y = a + bX)

  • Slope (b): \[ b = \frac{\bar{Y}_2 - \bar{Y}_1}{\bar{X}_2 - \bar{X}_1} = \frac{102 - 57}{8 - 3} = \frac{45}{5} = 9 \]
  • Intercept (a): Using Point 1 \( (3, 57) \): \[ Y = a + bX \] \[ 57 = a + 9(3) \] \[ 57 = a + 27 \] \[ a = 57 - 27 = 30 \]
  1. Trendline Equation: \( Y = 30 + 9X \)
    (Where Y = Profit in Ksh. millions, X = Year number with 2005=1)
  2. Profit Prediction for 2015:
    The year 2015 corresponds to X = 11 (2015 - 2005 + 1). \[ Y_{2015} = 30 + 9(11) = 30 + 99 = 129 \] Predicted profit for 2015 = Ksh. 129 million.

Answer

(a) Factors Determining Sample Size:
  1. Population Size (N): The total number of individuals or items in the group being studied. While less critical for very large populations, it's a factor in formulas for smaller ones.
  2. Desired Level of Precision (Margin of Error, e): The maximum acceptable difference between the sample estimate and the true population parameter. A smaller margin of error (higher precision) requires a larger sample.
  3. Desired Confidence Level: The probability that the sample estimate will fall within a certain range (defined by the margin of error) of the true population value (e.g., 95%, 99%). Higher confidence requires a larger sample.
  4. Population Variability (Standard Deviation, σ): The degree of diversity or heterogeneity within the population regarding the characteristic being measured. Higher variability requires a larger sample size.
  5. Available Resources (Time and Budget): Practical constraints often dictate the maximum feasible sample size. Cost per sample unit and total available funds/time are major considerations.

From Mean: \( \bar{x} = \frac{\sum fm}{N} \implies 1.46 = \frac{(0 \times 46) + (1 \times x) + (2 \times y) + (3 \times 25) + (4 \times 10) + (5 \times 1)}{200} \)

\( 1.46 = \frac{x + 2y + 75 + 40 + 5}{200} \implies 292 = x + 2y + 120 \implies x + 2y = 172 \) (Eq 2)

(a) Distinction between Financial Terms:
  1. Perpetuity vs. Annuity:
    • An Annuity involves a series of equal payments made at equal intervals for a finite duration.
    • A Perpetuity involves a series of equal payments made at equal intervals that continues indefinitely.
  2. Discounting vs. Compounding:
    • Compounding calculates the future value of money, considering the earning of interest on previously earned interest over time.
    • Discounting calculates the present value of future money, determining its worth today by applying a discount rate.
