Question 1

(a) Explain five advantages of using interview as a method of data collection. (10 marks)

(b) A company advertised for a position of a Managing Director. The number of applicants for the position were 50, out of which 10 were shortlisted for interview.

Determine the probability that an applicant selected at random will be:

  1. shortlisted for interview;
  2. shortlisted for interview and employed as the Managing Director;
  3. employed as the Managing Director, given that he has been shortlisted for interview;
  4. shortlisted for interview and not employed as the Managing Director;
  5. not shortlisted for interview.

(10 marks)

Answer

(a) Advantages of Interview Method:
  1. Higher Response Rate: Face-to-face interaction often encourages participation compared to methods like questionnaires.
  2. Clarification: The interviewer can clarify ambiguous questions, and the respondent can ask for clarification, leading to more accurate data.
  3. In-depth Information: Allows for probing questions to gather detailed insights, opinions, and feelings that might be missed in written surveys.
  4. Observation: The interviewer can observe non-verbal cues (body language, tone) which can add context to the verbal responses.
  5. Flexibility: The interviewer can adapt the questions based on the respondent's answers, exploring unexpected but relevant topics.

(Note: Only five advantages were required)

(b) Probability Calculations:
  • Total applicants (N) = 50
  • Shortlisted applicants (S) = 10
  • Employed applicants (E) = 1
  1. Probability of being shortlisted: \[ P(S) = \frac{\text{Number Shortlisted}}{\text{Total Applicants}} = \frac{10}{50} = \frac{1}{5} = 0.2 \]
  2. Probability of being shortlisted AND employed: \[ P(S \cap E) = \frac{\text{Number Employed}}{\text{Total Applicants}} = \frac{1}{50} = 0.02 \] (Assumes the employed person must be from the shortlist)
  3. Probability of being employed GIVEN shortlisted: \[ P(E | S) = \frac{\text{Number Employed}}{\text{Number Shortlisted}} = \frac{1}{10} = 0.1 \]
  4. Probability of being shortlisted AND NOT employed: \[ P(S \cap E') = P(S) - P(S \cap E) = \frac{10}{50} - \frac{1}{50} = \frac{9}{50} = 0.18 \]
  5. Probability of NOT being shortlisted: \[ P(S') = 1 - P(S) = 1 - \frac{10}{50} = \frac{40}{50} = \frac{4}{5} = 0.8 \]

(8 marks)

  1. Calculate Spearman’s rank correlation coefficient for each pair of judges.
  2. Based on the results in (i) above, determine the pair of judges that were most consistent in their ranking.

(12 marks)

Answer

(a) Differentiation of Terms:
  1. Perpetuity vs. Annuity:
    • An Annuity is a series of equal payments made at equal intervals for a finite period.
    • A Perpetuity is a series of equal payments made at equal intervals that continues indefinitely.
  2. Ordinary Annuity vs. Annuity Due:
    • An Ordinary Annuity has payments made at the end of each period.
    • An Annuity Due has payments made at the beginning of each period.
(b) Spearman's Rank Correlation Coefficient:

The formula is: \( \rho = 1 - \frac{6 \sum d^2}{n(n^2 - 1)} \), where \(d\) is the difference between ranks for each employee, and \(n = 10\).

Step 2: Calculate ρ for each pair.

\( n = 10 \), so \( n(n^2 - 1) = 10(100 - 1) = 990 \).

  1. Judges X and Y: \[ \rho_{XY} = 1 - \frac{6 \times \sum d_{XY}^2}{n(n^2 - 1)} = 1 - \frac{6 \times 174}{990} = 1 - \frac{1044}{990} \approx 1 - 1.055 = -0.055 \]
  2. Judges X and Z: \[ \rho_{XZ} = 1 - \frac{6 \times \sum d_{XZ}^2}{n(n^2 - 1)} = 1 - \frac{6 \times 60}{990} = 1 - \frac{360}{990} \approx 1 - 0.364 = 0.636 \]

Conclusion:

The pair of judges with the most consistent ranking is **X and Z**, as their correlation coefficient (\( \rho_{XZ} \approx 0.636 \)) has the highest absolute value, indicating the strongest agreement in their rankings.

(a) Reasons for Classification of Data:
  1. Simplification: Reduces large volumes of raw data into a more compact, understandable, and manageable form.
  2. Facilitates Comparison: Grouping data into classes allows for meaningful comparisons between different groups or datasets.

(b) The following information shows the contribution to the national income in millions of shillings by three sectors of an economy:

YearMININGAGRICULTUREFISHING
2015150550300

Answer

(a) Reasons Sampling is Preferred to Census:
  1. Reduced Cost: Sampling is significantly cheaper due to fewer personnel, less travel, and lower processing costs.
  2. Greater Speed: Data collection and analysis are much faster, allowing for timely results.
  3. Increased Accuracy/Quality: Resources can be focused on better training, data collection techniques, and thorough checks, potentially leading to higher quality data.
  4. Feasibility: A census is often impossible for infinite or very large populations; sampling is the only practical option.
  5. Destructive Testing: When testing destroys the item (e.g., bulb lifespan), sampling is essential to avoid destroying the entire population.

  1. interest;
  2. principal;
  3. discounting;
  4. compounding;
  5. sinking fund.

(10 marks)

  1. Using three-period moving averages, determine the trend values of the sales.

(10 marks)

Answer

(a) Meaning of Terms in Financial Mathematics:
  1. Interest: The cost of borrowing money or the return earned on lending money, usually expressed as a percentage rate over a period.
  2. Principal: The initial amount of money borrowed, lent, or invested, upon which interest is calculated.
  3. Discounting: The process of determining the present value (PV) of a future sum of money or series of payments by applying a discount rate.
  4. Compounding: The process where interest earned is added to the principal, and subsequent interest is calculated on the new, higher principal ("interest earning interest").
  5. Sinking Fund: A fund created by setting aside money regularly (periodic payments) to accumulate a specific sum by a target date, often to repay debt or replace an asset.
