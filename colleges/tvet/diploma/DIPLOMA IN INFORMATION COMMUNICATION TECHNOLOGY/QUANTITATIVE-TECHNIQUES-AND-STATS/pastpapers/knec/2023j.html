Question 1 (20 marks)

(a) Explain five advantages of observation as a method of data collection. (10 marks)

(b) The following is the distribution of the ages of transport vans in a company:

Age Number of vans
0-2 10
2-4 6
4-6 18
6-8 10
8-10 14
10-12 2

Determine the:

(i) mean;

(ii) median;

(iii) mode.

(10 marks)

Answer

(a) Advantages of observation as a method of data collection:

  1. Direct and firsthand information
    • Data collected is based on actual behavior
    • Not influenced by personal bias or opinions
  2. Detailed and accurate data
    • Captures both verbal and non-verbal behaviors
    • Records actions in their natural setting
  3. Natural behavior observation
    • Subjects act normally in their environment
    • More reliable than self-reported data
  4. Real-time data collection
    • Immediate recording of events
    • No reliance on memory or recall
  5. Scientific objectivity
    • Systematic recording of observations
    • Minimizes subjective interpretations

(b) Statistical calculations:

Working:

Age (x) Frequency (f) Mid-point (m) fm
0-2 10 1 10
2-4 6 3 18
4-6 18 5 90
6-8 10 7 70
8-10 14 9 126
10-12 2 11 22
Total Σf = 60 Σfm = 336

(i) Mean

Mean = Σfm ÷ Σf = 336 ÷ 60 = 5.6 years

(ii) Median

N/2 = 60/2 = 30 (lies in 4-6 class)

Median = L + ((N/2 - F) × c) ÷ f

Where:

  • L = Lower boundary of median class = 4
  • F = Cumulative frequency before median class = 16
  • c = Class interval = 2
  • f = Frequency of median class = 18

Median = 4 + ((30 - 16) × 2) ÷ 18 = 5.56 years

(iii) Mode

Mode class is 4-6 years (highest frequency = 18)

Mode = L + ((d1) ÷ (d1 + d2)) × c

Where:

  • L = Lower boundary of modal class = 4
  • d1 = Difference between modal frequency and frequency before = 18 - 6 = 12
  • d2 = Difference between modal frequency and frequency after = 18 - 10 = 8
  • c = Class interval = 2

Mode = 4 + ((12) ÷ (12 + 8)) × 2 = 5.2 years

Question 2 (20 marks)

(a) Explain five disadvantages of the simple random sampling method of data collection. (10 marks)

(b) The following data relates to two factories; X and Y, operating in the same industry.

Factory Average monthly wages (ksh) Standard deviation wages Number of workers
X 15,000 1,000 120
Y 13,500 1,500 100

Determine the:

(i) combined mean of all the workers in the two factories.

(ii) coefficient of variation of the monthly salaries for each factory.

(iii) Using the results in (ii) above, comment on the variability in the monthly wages in the two factories.

(10 marks)

Answer

(a) Disadvantages of simple random sampling:

  1. Sampling frame requirement
    • Needs complete list of population
    • May be difficult or costly to obtain
  2. Large sample size needed
    • Requires bigger samples for accuracy
    • Increases cost and time
  3. Geographical spread
    • Units may be widely dispersed
    • Increases data collection costs
  4. Non-representation of subgroups
    • May miss important minority groups
    • Can lead to biased results
  5. Administrative complexity
    • Requires proper randomization
    • Need for trained personnel

(b) Statistical calculations:

(i) Combined mean:

Combined mean = (N₁X₁ + N₂X₂) ÷ (N₁ + N₂)

Where:

  • N₁ = 120 workers (Factory X)
  • X₁ = 15,000 (Factory X mean)
  • N₂ = 100 workers (Factory Y)
  • X₂ = 13,500 (Factory Y mean)

Combined mean = (120 × 15,000 + 100 × 13,500) ÷ (120 + 100)

= (1,800,000 + 1,350,000) ÷ 220

= 3,150,000 ÷ 220

= 14,318.18 ksh

(ii) Coefficient of variation:

CV = (Standard deviation ÷ Mean) × 100%

Factory X:

CV = (1,000 ÷ 15,000) × 100%

= 6.67%

Factory Y:

CV = (1,500 ÷ 13,500) × 100%

= 11.11%

(iii) Comparison of variability:

  • Factory X has lower CV (6.67%) indicating more consistent wages
  • Factory Y has higher CV (11.11%) showing greater wage variation
  • Factory Y's wages are approximately 1.67 times more variable than Factory X
  • This suggests Factory X has more standardized wage structure

Question 4 (20 marks)

(a) Explain four limitations of using questionnaires in the collection of data. (8 marks)

(b) A box contains bag A and bag B. Bag A contains 5 apples and 6 oranges, while bag B contains 5 apples and 4 oranges. A bag is selected at random and two fruits are selected at random one at a time with replacement.

(i) Present the information above in a tree diagram.

(ii) Determine the probability of selecting:

(I) two apples;

(II) two oranges;

(III) an orange and an apple.

(12 marks)

Answer

(a) Limitations of questionnaires:

  1. Low response rate
    • Many recipients fail to respond
    • Can lead to biased results
  2. Lack of clarification
    • No opportunity to explain ambiguous questions
    • Respondents may misinterpret questions
  3. Response validity
    • Cannot verify who actually completed the questionnaire
    • Answers may not reflect true opinions
  4. Limited depth of information
    • Fixed format restricts detailed responses
    • Cannot capture non-verbal cues

(b) Probability calculations:

(i) Tree diagram:

                   ┌── A(5/11) ── A(5/11) ── AA: (5/11)(5/11)
                   │         └── O(6/11) ── AO: (5/11)(6/11)
Bag A(1/2) ───────┤
                   │         ┌── A(5/11) ── OA: (6/11)(5/11)
                   └── O(6/11) ── O(6/11) ── OO: (6/11)(6/11)
                   
                   ┌── A(5/9) ── A(5/9) ── AA: (5/9)(5/9)
                   │         └── O(4/9) ── AO: (5/9)(4/9)
Bag B(1/2) ───────┤
                   │         ┌── A(5/9) ── OA: (4/9)(5/9)
                   └── O(4/9) ── O(4/9) ── OO: (4/9)(4/9)

(ii) Probability calculations:

(I) P(two apples):

From Bag A: (1/2)(5/11)(5/11) = 25/242

From Bag B: (1/2)(5/9)(5/9) = 25/162

Total = 25/242 + 25/162 = (25/242) + (37.5/242) = 62.5/242 ≈ 0.258 or 25.8%

(II) P(two oranges):

From Bag A: (1/2)(6/11)(6/11) = 36/242

From Bag B: (1/2)(4/9)(4/9) = 16/162

Total = 36/242 + 16/162 = (36/242) + (24/242) = 60/242 ≈ 0.248 or 24.8%

(III) P(one orange and one apple):

From Bag A:

  • Orange then Apple: (1/2)(6/11)(5/11) = 30/242
  • Apple then Orange: (1/2)(5/11)(6/11) = 30/242

From Bag B:

  • Orange then Apple: (1/2)(4/9)(5/9) = 20/162
  • Apple then Orange: (1/2)(5/9)(4/9) = 20/162

Total = (60/242) + (40/162) = (60/242) + (60/242) = 120/242 ≈ 0.496 or 49.6%

Question 5 (20 marks)

(a) Explain five uses of regression analysis in business decision making. (10 marks)

(b) The following are the quarterly sales made by a retailer for four consecutive years in thousands of shillings.

Year Quarterly Sales (ksh 000's)
I II III IV
2018 26 44 116 46
2019 32 56 122 50
2020 34 58 122 52
2021 36 60 130 58

Determine the seasonal variation indices. (10 marks)

Answer

(a) Uses of regression analysis in business decision making:

  1. Sales forecasting
    • Predict future sales based on historical data
    • Account for various influencing factors
  2. Market trend analysis
    • Identify patterns in market behavior
    • Determine relationships between variables
  3. Cost estimation
    • Project future costs based on activity levels
    • Budget planning and control
  4. Performance analysis
    • Evaluate impact of business decisions
    • Measure effectiveness of strategies
  5. Risk assessment
    • Analyze relationship between risk factors
    • Quantify potential business risks

(b) Seasonal Variation Indices:

Step 1: Calculate quarterly averages

Quarter 2018 2019 2020 2021 Average
I 26 32 34 36 32
II 44 56 58 60 54.5
III 116 122 122 130 122.5
IV 46 50 52 58 51.5
Overall Mean 65.125

Step 2: Calculate Seasonal Variation Indices

Seasonal Index = (Quarter Average ÷ Overall Mean) × 100

Quarter I:

(32 ÷ 65.125) × 100 = 49.13%

Quarter II:

(54.5 ÷ 65.125) × 100 = 83.69%

Quarter III:

(122.5 ÷ 65.125) × 100 = 188.10%

Quarter IV:

(51.5 ÷ 65.125) × 100 = 79.08%

  • Quarter III shows the highest seasonal peak (188.10%)
  • Quarter I has the lowest seasonal activity (49.13%)
  • Quarters II and IV show moderate seasonal activity
  • The pattern suggests strong seasonal variation with peak sales in Q3

Question 6 (20 marks)

(a) Outline five advantages of the arithmetic mean as a measure of central tendency. (10 marks)

(b) A newly employed graduate intends to buy a car at a cost of Ksh 1,500,000, 5 years from now.

(i) Determine the lump sum amount that he needs to deposit at the beginning of the 5 year period in an account paying compound interest at the rate of 13% per annum to achieve his objective.

(ii) Determine the compound rate of interest per-annum that he should negotiate if he deposits Ksh 1,200,000 in an account at the beginning of the 5-year period to achieve his objective.

(10 marks)

Answer

(a) Advantages of arithmetic mean:

  1. Simple to calculate
    • Basic mathematical operations
    • Easy to understand and explain
  2. Uses all values
    • Considers every data point
    • Representative of entire dataset
  3. Suitable for further analysis
    • Used in statistical formulas
    • Basis for advanced calculations
  4. Algebraic properties
    • Can handle positive and negative values
    • Useful in mathematical manipulations
  5. Unique value
    • Only one possible result
    • No ambiguity in interpretation

(b) Compound Interest Calculations:

(i) Lump sum needed:

Using formula: FV = PV(1 + r)ⁿ

Where:

  • FV = 1,500,000 (Future Value)
  • r = 13% = 0.13 (Interest rate)
  • n = 5 years
  • Need to find PV (Present Value)

1,500,000 = PV(1 + 0.13)⁵

1,500,000 = PV(1.842461)

PV = 1,500,000 ÷ 1.842461

PV = 814,150.94 ksh

(ii) Required interest rate:

Using same formula with:

  • PV = 1,200,000
  • FV = 1,500,000
  • n = 5 years
  • Need to find r

1,500,000 = 1,200,000(1 + r)⁵

1.25 = (1 + r)⁵

⁵√1.25 = 1 + r

r = ⁵√1.25 - 1

r = 1.0456 - 1

r = 0.0456 or 4.56% per annum

Question 7 (20 marks)

(a) Outline four properties of a normal distribution curve. (8 marks)

(b) The following is the distribution of marks scored by students in an examination:

Marks Number of students
40 - 50 10
50 - 60 15
60 - 70 35
70 - 80 25
80 - 90 10
90 - 100 5

Determine the:

(i) interquartile deviation;

(ii) quartile deviation;

(iii) coefficient of quartile deviation.

(12 marks)

Answer

(a) Properties of a normal distribution curve:

  1. Bell-shaped and symmetric
    • Perfect symmetry about the mean
    • Both halves are mirror images
  2. Mean, median and mode are equal
    • All central measures coincide
    • Located at the peak of the curve
  3. Infinite tails
    • Extends infinitely in both directions
    • Never touches the horizontal axis
  4. Total area equals 1
    • Area under curve is 100%
    • Probability distribution property

(b) Quartile calculations:

Step 1: Find cumulative frequency

Class f cf
40 - 50 10 10
50 - 60 15 25
60 - 70 35 60
70 - 80 25 85
80 - 90 10 95
90 - 100 5 100

Step 2: Find quartiles

Total frequency (N) = 100 students

Q₁ position = N/4 = 25 (lies in 50-60 class)

Q₃ position = 3N/4 = 75 (lies in 70-80 class)

Q₁ = L + ((n/4 - F) × c)/f

Where:

  • L = 50 (lower boundary)
  • F = 10 (previous cf)
  • c = 10 (class interval)
  • f = 15 (frequency)

Q₁ = 50 + ((25 - 10) × 10)/15 = 60

Q₃ = L + ((3n/4 - F) × c)/f

Where:

  • L = 70 (lower boundary)
  • F = 60 (previous cf)
  • c = 10 (class interval)
  • f = 25 (frequency)

Q₃ = 70 + ((75 - 60) × 10)/25 = 76

(i) Interquartile deviation = Q₃ - Q₁

= 76 - 60 = 16 marks

(ii) Quartile deviation = (Q₃ - Q₁)/2

= 16/2 = 8 marks

(iii) Coefficient of quartile deviation = (Q₃ - Q₁)/(Q₃ + Q₁)

= (76 - 60)/(76 + 60)

= 16/136

= 0.118 or 11.8%
