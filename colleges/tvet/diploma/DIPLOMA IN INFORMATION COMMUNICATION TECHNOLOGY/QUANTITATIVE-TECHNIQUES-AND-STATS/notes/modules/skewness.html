Understanding Skewness

Skewness is a concept commonly used in statistical decision making. It refers to the degree to which a given frequency curve deviates from the normal distribution.

Types of Skewness

  1. Positive Skewness
  2. Negative Skewness

1. Positive Skewness

This is the tendency of a given frequency curve leaning towards the left, with the long tail extending to the right.

Characteristics:

  • The mean is usually bigger than the mode and median
  • The median always occurs between the mode and mean
  • There are more observations below the mean than above the mean

Note: This frequency distribution is characteristic of age distributions in developing countries.

2. Negative Skewness

This is an asymmetrical curve in which the long tail extends to the left.

Characteristics:

  • The mode is usually bigger than the mean and median
  • The median usually occurs between the mean and mode

Note: This frequency curve is characteristic of age distributions in developed countries.

Key Points

  • Skewness measures the asymmetry of a probability distribution
  • Positive skewness has a tail extending to the right
  • Negative skewness has a tail extending to the left
  • The relationship between mean, median, and mode differs based on the type of skewness
