Measures of Skewness

Measures of skewness are numerical values which assist in evaluating the degree of deviation of a frequency distribution from the normal distribution.

Commonly Used Measures of Skewness

1. Coefficient of Skewness (First Pearson's Coefficient)

Coefficient of Skewness = 3 × (mean - median) / Standard deviation

2. Second Pearson's Coefficient of Skewness

Coefficient of Skewness = (mean - mode) / Standard deviation

Note: These two coefficients above are known as Pearsonian measures of skewness.

3. Quartile Coefficient of Skewness

Coefficient = (Q₃ + Q₁ - 2Q₂) / (Q₃ - Q₁)

Where:

  • Q₁ = First quartile
  • Q₂ = Second quartile (median)
  • Q₃ = Third quartile

Important Notes on Pearsonian Coefficients

  • The coefficients usually range between -3 and +3
  • These are extreme values indicating high degrees of skewness
  • Negative values indicate negative skewness
  • Positive values indicate positive skewness

Key Points

  • Three main measures of skewness: Pearson's First and Second Coefficients, and Quartile Coefficient
  • Pearsonian coefficients range from -3 to +3
  • The sign of the coefficient indicates the direction of skewness
  • Larger absolute values indicate greater deviation from normal distribution

Example Solution

1. Calculating Arithmetic Mean

Arithmetic mean = Assumed mean + (c × Σfu/Σf)

Where:

  • Assumed mean = 63
  • c = 5
  • Σfu = 428
  • Σf = 610

Arithmetic mean = 63 + (428 × 5)/610 = 66.51

Note: You can also calculate arithmetic mean directly using: Σ(f·x)/Σf where x is the midpoint

2. Calculating Standard Deviation

Standard deviation = c × √[(Σfu²/Σf) - (Σfu/Σf)²]

Where:

  • c = 5
  • Σfu² = 3086
  • Σfu = 428
  • Σf = 610

Standard deviation = 10.68

3. Calculating Median

Position of median (m) = (n + 1)/2 = (610 + 1)/2 = 305.5

Median = L + [(m - F)/f] × c

Where:

  • L (lower class boundary) = 60.5
  • m = 305.5
  • F (cumulative frequency before median class) = 191
  • f (frequency of median class) = 120
  • c = 5

Median = 60.5 + [(305.5 - 191)/120] × 5 = 65.27

4. Calculating Pearsonian Coefficient of Skewness

Coefficient = 3 × (mean - median)/standard deviation

= 3 × (66.51 - 65.27)/10.68

= 0.348

Interpretation

The coefficient of skewness (0.348) indicates that the frequency distribution of the loans is positively skewed. Since the coefficient is positive but relatively small (much less than 1), this suggests a slight positive skewness in the distribution.

Example 2: Quartile Coefficient of Skewness

1. Calculating Quartiles

Position of Q₁ = (n + 1)/4 = (610 + 1)/4 = 152.75

Q₁ = 55.5 + [(152.75 - 94)/97] × 5 = 58.53

Position of Q₂ = 2(n + 1)/4 = 305.5

Q₂ = 60.5 + [(305.5 - 191)/120] × 5 = 65.27

Position of Q₃ = 3(n + 1)/4 = 458.25

Q₃ = 70.55 + [(458.25 - 403)/83] × 5 = 73.83

2. Calculating Quartile Coefficient of Skewness

Coefficient = (Q₃ + Q₁ - 2Q₂)/(Q₃ + Q₁)

= (73.83 + 58.53 - 2(65.27))/(73.83 + 58.53)

= 0.013

Interpretation

The quartile coefficient of skewness (0.013) also indicates a very slight positive skewness, consistent with the Pearsonian coefficient. The small value suggests that the degree of deviation from the normal distribution is minimal.
