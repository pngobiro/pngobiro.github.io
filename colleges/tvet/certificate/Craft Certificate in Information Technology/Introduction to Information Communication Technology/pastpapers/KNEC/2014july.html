Question 1 (4 marks)

Describe two wireless communication media that could be used in a computer network.

Answer

Two wireless communication media:

  • Wi-Fi: A wireless networking technology that uses radio waves to provide high-speed internet and network connections.
  • Bluetooth: A wireless technology standard used for exchanging data between devices over short distances using short-wavelength radio waves.

Question 2 (4 marks)

Differentiate between impact and non-impact printers.

Answer

Impact printers: Use a physical impact to transfer ink onto the paper, like dot-matrix printers.

Non-impact printers: Use technologies like inkjet or laser to transfer ink onto the paper without physical impact.

Question 3 (4 marks)

State the function of each of the following computer keyboard keys:

(a) Page Up;

(b) Esc;

(c) Num lock;

(d) Home.

Answer

(a) Page Up: Moves the cursor or scrolls the page upwards.

(b) Esc: Used to exit a program or cancel an operation.

(c) Num lock: Toggles the numeric keypad between numeric entry and cursor control.

(d) Home: Moves the cursor to the beginning of a line or document.

Question 4 (4 marks)

Distinguish between data and information giving an example in each case.

Answer

Data: Raw, unorganized facts that need processing. Example: 25, 160, 7.

Information: Processed, organized, structured or presented data that is meaningful and useful. Example: Ages of students are 25, 16 and 7.

Question 5 (4 marks)

Outline four functions of an operating system in a computer.

Answer

Four functions of an operating system:

  • Resource management (managing hardware and software resources)
  • Process management (managing the execution of applications)
  • File management (organizing and storing files)
  • Security (protecting the system from unauthorized access)

Question 6 (4 marks)

Explain two circumstances that may lead to uninstalling software from a computer.

Answer

Two circumstances for uninstalling software:

  • The software is no longer needed or used.
  • The software is causing problems or conflicts with other software.

Question 8 (4 marks)

Classify each of the following as either secondary or primary storage devices.

Readonly Memory, Floppy disk, Magnetic disk, Zip disk, Random access memory

Answer

Primary storage devices: Readonly Memory, Random access memory

Secondary storage devices: Floppy disk, Magnetic disk, Zip disk

Question 9 (4 marks)

Draw the communication lines in each of the following diagrams to make the topology indicated:

(a) Star Topology

(b) Bus Topology

(c) Ring Topology

(d) Mesh Topology

Answer

(a) Star Topology: Connect each device to the central node.

(b) Bus Topology: Connect all devices to a single cable in a linear fashion.

(c) Ring Topology: Connect each device to form a closed loop.

(d) Mesh Topology: Connect each device to every other device in the network.

Outline four ways in which local area networks are used in organisation.

Answer

Four ways local area networks are used in organizations:

  • File sharing
  • Printer sharing
  • Centralized data storage
  • Communication and collaboration

Question 11 (15 marks)

(a) Explain two challenges that the introduction of computers may pose to an organisation.

(b) Jane has acquired a computer and she would like to use it to process and store valuable data.

  (i) Explain two procedures she would take to protect the computer from getting viruses.

  (ii) Other than viruses, outline three other possible forms of threats on her files (3 marks)

(c) Many people nowadays use their computers to do on-line shopping. Outline two challenges of this form of shopping.

Answer

(a) Two challenges of introducing computers:

  • Cost of implementation and maintenance.
  • Need for training and adaptation by employees.

(b) (i) Two procedures to protect from viruses:

  • Install and regularly update antivirus software.
  • Avoid downloading files from untrusted sources.

(ii) Three other possible forms of threats:

  • Malware
  • Data theft
  • Hardware failure

(c) Two challenges of online shopping:

  • Security risks (e.g., credit card theft).
  • Lack of physical inspection of goods before purchase.

Question 15 (15 marks)

(a) State three differences between a local area network and a wide area network. (6 marks)

(b) In order to enforce ethical issues relating to personal data, the govemment should introduce data protection act.

  (i) Outline three principles that the data protection act may contain. (3 marks)

  (ii) Outline three ways through which an employee in an organisation might break laws governing data protection.

(c) With the aid of a labelled diagram, outline a Client-Server network as used in data communication.

Answer

(a) Differences between LAN and WAN:

  • Geographical coverage: LAN covers a small area (building/campus), WAN covers large areas (cities/countries)
  • Speed: LANs typically have higher data transfer speeds than WANs
  • Cost: LANs are less expensive to set up and maintain compared to WANs

(b) (i) Three principles of data protection act:

  • Personal data must be processed fairly and lawfully
  • Data should only be collected for specified and legitimate purposes
  • Data should be accurate and kept up to date

(ii) Three ways employees might break data protection laws:

  • Sharing confidential information without authorization
  • Using personal data for purposes other than intended
  • Failing to protect data from unauthorized access
