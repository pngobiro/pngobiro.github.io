SECTION A (40 Marks)

Answer ALL the questions in this section.

Question 1

(a) Define the term hardware as used in Information Communication Technology. (2 marks)

(b) Explain one use of a computer in the security industry.

Answer

(a) Hardware definition:

The physical components of a computer system that you can see and touch, including both internal parts (like CPU, motherboard) and external devices (like keyboard, monitor).

(b) Computer use in security:

Surveillance and monitoring through CCTV systems - computers can record, store, and analyze security camera footage in real-time.

Question 2

(a) The second-generation computers were faster than the first-generation computers. Explain the technology that brought this difference.

(b) Describe a computer bus.

Answer

(a) Technology difference:

The use of transistors instead of vacuum tubes. Transistors were smaller, more reliable, generated less heat, and could switch states much faster than vacuum tubes.

(b) Computer bus:

A set of electrical connections (pathways) that allows data and control signals to travel between different components of a computer system. It acts as a communication highway for data transfer.

Question 3

The following are components of a computer system:

Memory stick, joystick, optical mark reader, printer, microfilm, plotter, digital scanner, camera.

Classify each one of them as either input, output or storage device.

Answer

Input devices:

  • Joystick
  • Optical mark reader
  • Digital scanner
  • Camera

Output devices:

  • Printer
  • Plotter

Storage devices:

  • Memory stick
  • Microfilm

Question 4

Jane, an ICT graduate, would like to buy a new output device. Outline four factors that she would consider.

Answer

  • Cost of the device and ongoing maintenance
  • Resolution/output quality
  • Speed/performance
  • Compatibility with existing hardware and software

Question 5

Differentiate between linker and loader system utilities.

Answer

Linker:

  • Combines object files and libraries into an executable program
  • Resolves references between different program modules

Loader:

  • Loads the executable program into memory for execution
  • Allocates memory space and prepares program for running

Question 6

(a) Explain the term ergonomics as used in Information Communication Technology.

(b) John has been experiencing wrist pain due to long hours of computer use. Outline two precautions he could have taken to avoid this pain.

Answer

(a) Ergonomics:

The science of designing equipment and workplaces to maximize productivity by reducing operator fatigue and discomfort. In ICT, it involves designing computer workstations and equipment to fit the user's physical needs.

(b) Precautions:

  • Use an ergonomic wrist rest and maintain proper wrist position while typing
  • Take regular breaks and perform wrist exercises

Question 7

(a) Outline two important reasons for blowing a computer.

(b) Maisha Computer College has located its computer room on the first floor of a building while the classes are on the ground floor. Outline the most probable reason for this decision.

Answer

(a) Reasons for blowing computer:

  • Remove dust that can cause overheating
  • Prevent dust accumulation on sensitive components

(b) Computer room location reason:

To protect equipment from potential flooding and water damage, as ground floor locations are more susceptible to these risks.

Question 8

Describe two network topologies used in a Local Area Network.

Answer

1. Star Topology:

  • All devices connect to a central hub/switch
  • Easy to manage and troubleshoot

2. Bus Topology:

  • All devices connect to a single cable (backbone)
  • Simple and cost-effective for small networks

Question 9

A cashier in a microfinance institution records deposits and withdrawals in his computer. These records are later compiled and used to update files in the manager's computer.

(a) Identify the type of file used by the cashier.

(b) Describe the type of file in the manager's computer.

Answer

(a) Cashier's file type:

Transaction file - contains daily transactions and temporary records

(b) Manager's file type:

Master file - contains permanent records that are updated periodically with transaction data

Question 10

Distinguish between piracy and plagiarism as used in Information Communication Technology.

Answer

Piracy:

  • Unauthorized copying, distribution, or use of software
  • Violation of software licensing agreements

Plagiarism:

  • Using someone else's work or ideas without proper attribution
  • Presenting others' content as your own

Answer any FOUR questions from this section.

Question 11

(a) Outline three advantages of computer networks.

(b) Distinguish between intellectual property right and copyright as used in Information Communication Technology.

(c) A newly established technical institution connected their computers to the internet. Explain four crimes that the institution could be exposed to.

Answer

(a) Advantages of computer networks:

  • Resource sharing (printers, storage, etc.)
  • Improved communication between users
  • Centralized data management and backup

(b) Intellectual property right vs Copyright:

Intellectual property right:

  • Broader protection for creative works, inventions, and ideas
  • Includes patents, trademarks, and trade secrets

Copyright:

  • Specific protection for original works (software, documentation)
  • Focuses on preventing unauthorized copying and distribution

(c) Potential internet crimes:

  • Hacking attempts and unauthorized access
  • Malware and virus attacks
  • Phishing attempts
  • Data theft or breaches

Question 12

(a) Outline three stages in the data processing cycle.

(b) Peter, an ICT expert, installed an operating system in a new computer.

(i) Explain two ways through which this software could be classified. (4 marks)

(ii) Outline three functions of this software in a computer.

(c) A company uses a networked printer in its offices. The printer starts printing when it authenticates the user through a biometric security system.

(i) Identify the most appropriate security device to use.

(ii) Outline three advantages of the device identified in (i).

Answer

(a) Data processing cycle stages:

  • Input - collecting and entering data
  • Processing - manipulating and transforming data
  • Output - presenting processed information

(b) (i) Operating system classification:

  • By user interface (GUI vs Command-line)
  • By number of users (Single-user vs Multi-user)

(ii) Operating system functions:

  • Memory management
  • Process management
  • File system management

(c) (i) Appropriate security device:

Fingerprint scanner

(ii) Advantages of fingerprint scanner:

  • Unique identification for each user
  • Cannot be forgotten or lost like passwords
  • Quick and convenient authentication

Question 13

(a) Outline three file access methods used in a computer.

(b) A technician intends to set up a computer network. Describe two types of cables he could use.

(c) (i) Explain the term write protection with respect to diskettes.

(ii) A student preferred to use a CD-RW to store information during a practical lesson. Explain three reasons that could have led to this preference.

Answer

(a) File access methods:

  • Sequential access
  • Random access
  • Indexed sequential access

(b) Network cable types:

1. Twisted Pair Cable:

  • Available in shielded (STP) and unshielded (UTP) versions
  • Common in office networks

2. Coaxial Cable:

  • Better shielding against interference
  • Suitable for longer distances

(c) (i) Write protection:

A physical or logical mechanism that prevents data from being modified or deleted on a storage device.

(ii) Reasons for CD-RW preference:

  • Ability to rewrite data multiple times
  • More durable than diskettes
  • Larger storage capacity

Question 14

(a) (i) Explain the term anti-virus software as used in Information Communication Technology.

(ii) State three examples of anti-virus software available in the market.

(b) Steve, an ICT expert, recommended the use of a page printer for document printing. Explain three reasons that could have led to this recommendation.

(c) Distinguish between mechanical and electronic data processing methods.

Answer

(a) (i) Anti-virus software:

Programs designed to detect, prevent, and remove malicious software (malware) from computer systems.

(ii) Examples of anti-virus software:

  • Norton Antivirus
  • McAfee
  • Kaspersky

(b) Reasons for page printer recommendation:

  • Higher print quality
  • Faster printing speed for multiple copies
  • Lower cost per page for large volumes

(c) Mechanical vs Electronic data processing:

Mechanical processing:

  • Uses mechanical devices like punch cards
  • Slower and more labor-intensive

Electronic processing:

  • Uses electronic computers
  • Faster and more accurate

Question 15

(a) Outline two uses of computers in a hospital other than data storage.

(b) (i) Tom, an ICT student, has access to the internet. Explain two possible negative impacts this access could have on him.

(ii) A computer network in an organization consists of a central computer that controls the other computers. Outline four disadvantages of such a network.

(c) Figure 1 shows two computer connectors. Use it to answer the questions that follow:

(i) Identify the connectors labelled (i) and (ii).

(ii) For each of the identified connectors, state the hardware device(s) that it connects.

Answer

(a) Uses of computers in hospitals:

  • Patient monitoring and diagnostic systems
  • Medical imaging processing (X-rays, MRI, etc.)

(b) (i) Negative impacts of internet access:

  • Addiction to social media and online activities
  • Exposure to inappropriate content

(ii) Disadvantages of centralized network:

  • Single point of failure
  • Network congestion at central computer
  • Higher cost of central computer
  • Performance bottlenecks

(c) (i) Connector identification:

Connector (i): USB connector

Connector (ii): VGA connector

(ii) Connected devices:

USB connector:

  • Keyboards, mice, printers, flash drives

VGA connector:

  • Monitors and projectors
