Question 1

(a) Define the term firmware as used in computer systems.

(b) Outline two reasons why computer users generate backup files.

Answer

Two justifications for preferring an LCD (Liquid Crystal Display) monitor over a CRT (Cathode Ray Tube) monitor:

  1. Size, Weight, and Space Savings:
    • LCD monitors are significantly thinner and lighter than bulky CRT monitors.
    • They occupy much less desk space, making them ideal for smaller workspaces or multi-monitor setups.
    • Easier to move, mount, and adjust.
  2. Image Quality and Eye Strain Reduction:
    • LCDs typically offer sharper text and images with no screen flicker, which reduces eye strain during prolonged use compared to the flickering inherent in CRT refresh rates.
    • They produce less glare and generally have better brightness and contrast ratios under typical office lighting conditions.
    • Consume significantly less power and generate less heat compared to CRTs.

Question 2

(a) State two features of the first generation computers.

(b) Joan discovered that her computer could not boot even though the power connectivity was right. Explain what could have been the cause of this.

Answer

(a) Advantages of Magnetic Ink Character Reader (MICR): (2 marks)

  1. High Security & Accuracy: MICR characters use special magnetic ink and unique fonts that are difficult to forge. The magnetic reading process is highly accurate and less prone to errors compared to optical scanning, crucial for financial documents like cheques.
  2. Speed and Efficiency: MICR readers can process documents very quickly, automating tasks like cheque sorting and clearing in the banking industry, leading to significant efficiency gains.

(b) Ways to Minimize Software Piracy:

  1. Licensing and Activation Keys: Implementing strict software licensing agreements and requiring unique activation keys or online activation to use the software legally. This makes it harder to install unauthorized copies.
  2. Digital Rights Management (DRM) and Copy Protection: Employing technological measures (DRM) that restrict copying, printing, or sharing of the software or its output. Physical copy protection methods can also be used for distributed media.
  3. Legal Action and Education: Pursuing legal action against individuals or organizations involved in large-scale piracy and educating users about the legal and ethical implications of using pirated software.
  4. Affordable Pricing and Subscription Models: Offering software at more accessible price points or through subscription models (SaaS) can reduce the incentive for users to seek pirated versions.

Question 3

Differentiate between real-time and batch data processing modes.

Answer

(a) Functions of a Flatbed Scanner in an Office:

  1. Digitizing Documents: Converting physical paper documents (letters, reports, invoices, photos) into digital image files (e.g., PDF, JPG, TIFF). This allows for electronic storage, sharing, and archiving, reducing physical clutter.
  2. Optical Character Recognition (OCR): Using OCR software in conjunction with the scanner to convert scanned images of text documents into editable and searchable digital text files. This enables easy searching, copying, and editing of document content.
  3. Creating Digital Copies: Making digital duplicates of important documents for backup purposes or for electronic distribution via email or shared drives.

(b) Assembly Language:

Assembly language is a low-level programming language that uses mnemonics (short abbreviations or symbols) to represent the basic machine code instructions that a computer's processor can understand and execute.

  • It provides a more human-readable representation of machine code compared to raw binary.
  • Each assembly instruction typically corresponds directly to one machine instruction.
  • It is specific to a particular computer architecture (CPU family).
  • Requires an assembler program to translate the mnemonics into executable machine code.
  • Offers fine-grained control over hardware but is more complex and less portable than high-level languages.

Question 4

Outline two advantages of each of the following data transmission media:

(i) coaxial cables;

(ii) twisted pair cables.

Answer

(a) Features of First Generation Computers (approx. 1940s-1956):

  1. Vacuum Tube Technology: Used vacuum tubes as the primary electronic component for circuitry and logic, which were bulky, generated significant heat, and were prone to failure.
  2. Large Size and High Power Consumption: These computers were enormous, often occupying entire rooms, and consumed large amounts of electricity, generating substantial heat requiring extensive cooling systems.
  3. Machine Language Programming: Programming was done directly in machine language (binary code), making it extremely difficult and time-consuming.
  4. Limited Memory and Slow Speed: Used magnetic drums or delay lines for memory, offering very limited storage capacity and slow processing speeds (measured in milliseconds).

(b) Possible Causes for Boot Failure (Despite Power):

If a computer receives power but fails to boot (start the operating system), potential causes include:

  • Hardware Failure:
    • Faulty Power Supply Unit (PSU) not delivering correct voltages.
    • Failed motherboard component (e.g., capacitor, chipset).
    • Defective RAM module(s).
    • Failed CPU.
    • Problem with the graphics card preventing display output.
    • Loose or disconnected internal cables.
  • BIOS/UEFI Issues:
    • Corrupted BIOS/UEFI firmware.
    • Incorrect BIOS/UEFI settings (e.g., wrong boot order, disabled components).
    • Failed CMOS battery causing settings loss.
  • Boot Drive Problems:
    • Hard drive or SSD failure (where the OS is stored).
    • Corrupted operating system boot files (e.g., Master Boot Record - MBR).
    • Incorrect boot drive selected in BIOS/UEFI.

Question 5

(a) James intends to buy a flatbed scanner for office use. Outline two functions that the scanner could be used for.

(b) Explain the term assembly language as used in computer systems.

Answer

(a) Firmware Definition:

Firmware is a specific class of computer software that provides low-level control for a device's specific hardware. It is held in non-volatile memory devices such as ROM, EPROM, or flash memory.

  • It acts as a permanent software program embedded into a hardware device.
  • Examples include the BIOS/UEFI on a computer motherboard, or the control software on a router or printer.
  • It bridges the gap between hardware and higher-level software like the operating system.

(b) Reasons for Backup Files:

  1. Data Recovery: To restore data in case of hardware failure (e.g., hard drive crash), accidental deletion, software corruption, or malware infection. Backups provide a safety net to recover lost information.
  2. Disaster Recovery/Business Continuity: To ensure business operations can continue after a major disaster (e.g., fire, flood, cyberattack) by restoring systems and data from backups stored off-site.

Question 6

(a) Describe direct file organization method as used in computer data storage.

(b) Explain the term data encryption as used in computer security.

Answer

Difference between Real-Time and Batch Data Processing Modes:

Feature Real-Time Processing Batch Processing
Processing Timing Data is processed immediately or almost immediately (within milliseconds or seconds) as it is generated or received. Data is collected over a period (e.g., hours, days) and processed together in large groups or "batches".
Data Input Input data arrives continuously and is processed individually or in very small groups. Input data is accumulated and processed collectively at scheduled times or when a certain volume is reached.
System Response Provides immediate feedback or results to the user or system. There is a delay between data input and the availability of results, as processing happens later.
Resource Usage Requires continuous availability of processing resources. Can be resource-intensive. Can be scheduled during off-peak hours to optimize resource usage. Efficient for large volumes.
Examples Airline reservation systems, online banking transactions, industrial process control, live stock market updates. Payroll processing, generating monthly bank statements, large-scale data analysis, billing systems.
Complexity Often more complex to design and manage due to concurrency and timing requirements. Generally simpler to manage, but requires scheduling and handling large data volumes.

Question 7

(a) Outline two advantages of Magnetic Ink Character Reader (MICR) as used in ICT. (2 marks)

(b) State two ways of minimizing piracy of computer software.

Answer

Advantages of data transmission media:

  1. Coaxial Cables:
    • Better Shielding/Less Interference: Compared to unshielded twisted pair (UTP), the metallic shield in coaxial cable provides better protection against electromagnetic interference (EMI) and radio frequency interference (RFI), leading to a cleaner signal over longer distances than UTP.
    • Higher Bandwidth than UTP: Coaxial cables generally support higher bandwidth and data rates than standard twisted pair cables, making them suitable for applications like cable television and early Ethernet networks (though modern twisted pair categories can match or exceed some coax types).
  2. Twisted Pair Cables (UTP/STP):
    • Lower Cost and Ease of Installation: Twisted pair cabling, especially UTP, is typically the most inexpensive cabling option and is relatively easy to install, terminate, and manage using standard connectors (like RJ45).
    • Flexibility and Widespread Use: It is highly flexible, widely available, and forms the basis of most modern Ethernet LANs. Different categories (Cat 5e, Cat 6, Cat 6a, etc.) offer varying levels of performance to suit different needs.

Question 8

Explain two methods that a computer operator would apply to improve ergonomics on a computer being used in an office environment.

Answer

(a) Direct File Organization Method:

Direct file organization (also known as random access or hashed file organization) allows records to be accessed directly without reading through preceding records. Key characteristics include:

  • Address Calculation: A record's physical storage address (e.g., block number) is determined directly from its key field using a mathematical formula or hashing algorithm.
  • Fast Access: Enables very fast retrieval and updating of specific records because the system can calculate the address and go directly to that location.
  • Non-Sequential Storage: Records are not necessarily stored in sequential order based on their keys; their location depends on the hashing algorithm's output.
  • Collision Handling: Requires mechanisms to handle situations where different keys hash to the same address (collisions), such as using overflow areas or chaining.
  • Use Cases: Suitable for applications requiring rapid access to individual records, like online transaction processing (OLTP) systems or real-time databases.

(b) Data Encryption:

Data encryption is the process of converting plaintext data into a coded format (ciphertext) using an algorithm (cipher) and a secret key. The primary purpose is to protect the confidentiality of data.

  • Confidentiality: Makes data unreadable to unauthorized parties who do not possess the correct decryption key.
  • Process: An encryption algorithm mathematically transforms the data, and only someone with the corresponding decryption key can reverse the process to recover the original plaintext.
  • Types: Can be symmetric (same key for encryption and decryption) or asymmetric (different public/private keys).
  • Application: Used to secure data at rest (stored on disks) and data in transit (transmitted over networks) to prevent eavesdropping or unauthorized access.

Question 9

Jerry prefers an LCD monitor to a CRT monitor. Explain two justifications for his preference.

Answer

Two methods to improve ergonomics for a computer operator in an office:

  1. Adjusting Workstation Setup:
    • Chair Adjustment: Ensure the chair height allows feet to be flat on the floor, knees at roughly 90 degrees, and provides adequate lumbar (lower back) support. Armrests should support forearms parallel to the floor.
    • Monitor Positioning: Place the monitor directly in front, about an arm's length away. The top of the screen should be at or slightly below eye level to maintain a neutral neck posture. Reduce glare by adjusting screen angle or using anti-glare filters.
    • Keyboard and Mouse Placement: Position the keyboard and mouse close together and directly in front, allowing elbows to be bent at approximately 90 degrees and wrists to remain straight while typing or using the mouse. Use wrist rests if needed.
  2. Implementing Proper Work Habits:
    • Taking Regular Breaks: Encourage frequent short breaks (e.g., 5 minutes every hour) to stand up, stretch, and walk around. This reduces muscle fatigue and eye strain. Use the 20-20-20 rule for eyes (look at something 20 feet away for 20 seconds every 20 minutes).
    • Varying Tasks: Alternate between computer tasks and non-computer tasks (if possible) throughout the day to avoid prolonged static postures and repetitive movements.
    • Maintaining Good Posture: Be mindful of sitting upright, keeping shoulders relaxed, and avoiding slouching or leaning forward excessively.

Question 10

Explain the function of each of the following computer keyboard keys:

(i) Print Screen;

(ii) Esc.

Answer

Function of keyboard keys:

  1. Print Screen (PrtScn / PrtSc):
    • Primary Function: Captures an image of the entire screen (or the active window, often in combination with the Alt key) and copies it to the computer's clipboard.
    • Usage: The captured image can then be pasted into an image editing program (like Paint) or a document (like Word) for saving, editing, or sharing.
    • Variations: On some systems or with specific software, it might directly save the screenshot as a file or send it to a printer, but the clipboard function is the most common default behavior.
  2. Esc (Escape):
    • Primary Function: Used to cancel or abort the current operation or command, close a dialog box, menu, or window, or exit a specific mode (like full-screen view).
    • Usage: Acts as a general "stop" or "back out" key in many applications and operating systems. For example, stopping a webpage from loading, closing a pop-up window, or deselecting an item.
    • Context-Dependent: Its exact function can vary depending on the active application or context.

Question 11

(a) Describe computer output to microfilm (COM) as used in computer systems.

(b) Explain three factors to be considered when purchasing computer hardware, other than cost.

(c) Dan intends to install utility software in his home computer for domestic use. Explain three utilities he could install.

Answer

(a) Computer Output to Microfilm (COM):

COM is a process for copying data from electronic media on computers directly onto microfilm (microfiche or roll microfilm). It involves:

  • Data Transfer: Transferring digital data (usually reports or large archives) from computer storage (like magnetic tape or disk) to a COM recorder.
  • Image Creation: The COM recorder projects the data onto a small screen (often a CRT) and photographs it onto microfilm.
  • Microfilm Output: The result is a compact, human-readable archive on microfilm, which requires a microfilm reader to view.
  • Purpose: Primarily used for long-term archival storage of large volumes of data before digital storage became cheap and prevalent. It offered space savings and longevity compared to paper archives.

(b) Factors for Purchasing Computer Hardware (Other than Cost):

  1. Performance and Specifications: Does the hardware meet the required performance levels for the intended tasks? Consider processor speed, RAM capacity, storage speed/capacity, graphics capabilities, network speed, etc. Ensure specifications match the software requirements.
  2. Compatibility and Integration: Will the new hardware work seamlessly with existing systems, software, operating systems, and peripherals? Check for driver availability and compatibility with network infrastructure.
  3. Reliability, Support, and Warranty: Consider the manufacturer's reputation for reliability, the length and terms of the warranty offered, and the availability and quality of technical support and repair services.
  4. Scalability and Future-Proofing: Does the hardware allow for future upgrades (e.g., adding more RAM or storage)? Will it likely meet the organization's needs for a reasonable period, or will it become obsolete quickly?

(c) Utility Software Dan Could Install:

  1. Antivirus/Anti-malware Software: To protect the computer from viruses, spyware, ransomware, and other malicious software threats encountered online or through external media. (e.g., Windows Defender, Avast, Malwarebytes).
  2. Backup Software: To create regular backups of important personal files (documents, photos, etc.) to an external drive or cloud storage, protecting against data loss due to hardware failure or accidental deletion. (e.g., Windows Backup, Macrium Reflect Free, Cloud services like Google Drive/OneDrive backup).
  3. Disk Cleanup/Optimization Utility: To remove temporary files, clear caches, uninstall unwanted programs, and potentially defragment the hard drive (less needed for SSDs) to maintain system performance and free up disk space. (e.g., Windows Disk Cleanup, CCleaner).

Question 12

(a) With the aid of an example in each case, outline three functions of spreadsheet computer software.

(b) A computer consulting company intends to educate the public on various forms of computer crimes. Explain three forms of such crimes.

(c) With the aid of a block diagram, explain the functional components of a computer system.

Answer

(a) Functions of Spreadsheet Software:

  1. Data Organization and Storage: Allows users to enter, organize, and store data in a structured grid format of rows and columns (cells).
    • Example: Creating a list of student names, IDs, and grades in separate columns.
  2. Calculations and Analysis: Performs mathematical, statistical, logical, and financial calculations using built-in formulas and functions on the data entered.
    • Example: Using the `SUM` function to calculate total sales, or `AVERAGE` to find the average score of students.
  3. Data Visualization (Charting): Creates various types of charts and graphs (bar charts, pie charts, line graphs) based on the data to visually represent trends and patterns.
    • Example: Creating a bar chart to compare monthly sales figures.

(b) Forms of Computer Crimes:

  1. Unauthorized Access (Hacking): Gaining access to computer systems, networks, or data without permission. This can involve exploiting vulnerabilities or using stolen credentials.
  2. Malware Distribution: Creating and spreading malicious software like viruses, worms, trojans, ransomware, or spyware to damage systems, steal information, or disrupt operations.
  3. Fraud and Financial Crimes: Using computers and networks to commit financial fraud, such as phishing (stealing credentials), identity theft, credit card fraud, or online scams.
  4. Data Theft/Espionage: Stealing sensitive corporate or personal data, including intellectual property, trade secrets, customer information, or government secrets.
  5. Cyberstalking/Harassment: Using electronic means to stalk, harass, or threaten individuals.

Functional Components of a Computer System

Explanation:

  • Input Devices: Allow users to enter data and commands (e.g., keyboard, mouse).
  • CPU (Central Processing Unit): The "brain" containing the Control Unit (directs operations), ALU (performs calculations/logic), and Registers (temporary storage).
  • Primary Memory (RAM): Temporarily stores data and instructions currently being used by the CPU. Volatile.
  • Output Devices: Display or present processed information to the user (e.g., monitor, printer).
  • Secondary Storage: Provides long-term, non-volatile storage for data and programs (e.g., hard drive, SSD).

Question 13

(a) With the aid of a diagram, describe star network topology.

(b) A secondary school intends to buy computers for its management staff. Explain two uses that the computers could be put into. (4 marks)

(c) Explain each of the following file management functions of an operating system:

(i) file dumping;

(ii) file loading;

(iii) file deletion.

Answer

(a) Star Network Topology:

        PC1          PC2
         |            |
         |            |
PC3 ----[HUB/SWITCH]---- PC4
         |            |
         |            |
        PC5          PC6

Star Network Topology

Description:

  • In a star topology, all network devices (computers, printers, servers) are connected individually to a central device, such as a hub or, more commonly today, a switch.
  • Each device has a dedicated point-to-point link to the central device.
  • All communication between devices must pass through the central hub/switch.
  • It is the most common topology used in modern Ethernet LANs.

(b) Uses of Computers for Secondary School Management Staff: (4 marks)

  1. Student Information Management: Maintaining a database of student records, including enrollment details, attendance, grades, disciplinary actions, and contact information. This allows for efficient tracking and reporting.
  2. Financial Management and Administration: Handling school finances, including fee collection, budgeting, payroll for staff, generating financial reports, and managing school resources and inventory.
  3. Communication and Scheduling: Facilitating communication with parents, staff, and students via email or school portals. Managing school calendars, timetables, room bookings, and event scheduling.

(c) File Management Functions of an Operating System:

  1. File Dumping (Backup): Creating a copy of files or entire file systems onto a separate storage medium (like tape, external drive, or cloud storage). This is done for archival purposes or to allow recovery in case the original data is lost or damaged.
  2. File Loading (Opening/Retrieving): Transferring a file (or parts of it) from secondary storage (like a hard drive) into primary memory (RAM) so that it can be accessed, viewed, or processed by the CPU or an application program.
  3. File Deletion: Removing a file entry from the file system's directory structure and marking the storage space occupied by the file as available for reuse. The actual data might not be immediately erased but becomes inaccessible through normal means.

Question 14

(a) Explain the difference between desktop and palmtop computers.

(b)

(i) Distinguish between indexed sequential and sequential file organization methods.

(ii) A company has supplied the following software to a college for use. novel, Ms PowerPoint, unix, Ms Access, Windows for Workgroup, Classify the above software either as; the operating system, presentation software or database management system.

(c)

(i) Explain the relationship between each of the following data storage units:

I. byte and bit;

II. kilobyte and gigabyte;

(ii) Outline two functions of the primary storage of a computer system.

Answer

(a) Difference between Desktop and Palmtop Computers:

Feature Desktop Computer Palmtop Computer (PDA/Handheld)
Size & Portability Larger, designed for stationary use on a desk. Separate components (monitor, tower, keyboard, mouse). Not easily portable. Very small, designed to be held in one hand or fit in a pocket. Integrated screen and input (stylus/small keyboard). Highly portable.
Processing Power & Capabilities Generally more powerful processors, more RAM, larger storage capacity. Suitable for demanding tasks. Less powerful processors, limited RAM and storage. Designed for specific tasks like scheduling, contacts, notes (though modern smartphones blur this line).
Input Method Full-size keyboard and mouse. Small physical keyboard, stylus on a touchscreen, or virtual keyboard.
Power Source Requires constant connection to mains power. Runs on rechargeable batteries.
Primary Use General computing, office work, gaming, multimedia creation. Personal information management, basic communication, specific mobile tasks (less common now due to smartphones).

(b) File Organization and Software Classification:

  1. Indexed Sequential vs. Sequential File Organization:
    • Sequential: Records are stored one after another in a predetermined order (e.g., by key field). To access a specific record, you must read through all preceding records. Efficient for batch processing where most records are processed.
    • Indexed Sequential (ISAM): Records are stored sequentially, but an index is also maintained. The index allows direct access to the approximate location of a record, after which sequential search may be needed. Supports both sequential and direct access efficiently.
  2. Software Classification:
    • Operating System: Novel (NetWare - a network OS), Unix, Windows for Workgroup
    • Presentation Software: Ms PowerPoint
    • Database Management System: Ms Access

(c) Data Storage Units and Primary Storage Functions:

  1. Relationship between Storage Units:
    • I. Byte and Bit: A bit is the smallest unit of data (0 or 1). A byte is typically composed of 8 bits and is the standard unit for representing a character (like a letter or number).
    • II. Kilobyte (KB) and Gigabyte (GB): These are units of digital information storage capacity. 1 Kilobyte (KB) is approximately 1,024 bytes. 1 Gigabyte (GB) is approximately 1,024 Megabytes (MB), or 1,024 * 1,024 Kilobytes, or roughly one billion bytes. (Note: Sometimes decimal prefixes (1000) are used instead of binary (1024)).
  2. Functions of Primary Storage (RAM):
    • Holding Active Programs: Stores the instructions of the operating system and application programs currently being executed by the CPU.
    • Holding Data Being Processed: Stores the data that the active programs are currently working on, allowing the CPU quick access for reading and writing during calculations.
    • Holding Intermediate Results: Temporarily stores the results of calculations performed by the ALU before they are transferred to output devices or secondary storage.

Question 15

(a)

(i) The use of ICT technology for money transfer has changed the way in which Kenyans do business. Explain two benefits to business people for using this service.

(ii) Explain one circumstance under which a wireless mouse would be of necessity as an input device.

(b) A computer laboratory requires some standard measures to be followed to ensure safety and security. Outline four such measures.

(c) A large retail shop has installed a computer network for its operations. Explain three benefits that the shop will gain with this move.

Answer

(a) ICT Money Transfer and Wireless Mouse:

  1. Benefits of ICT Money Transfer for Business People:
    • Convenience and Speed: Allows for quick and easy transfer of funds anytime, anywhere using mobile phones or computers, eliminating the need to visit a bank branch. Payments can be made or received instantly.
    • Reduced Costs and Increased Reach: Often lower transaction fees compared to traditional banking methods. Enables businesses to easily transact with customers and suppliers across wider geographical areas, including rural locations.
    • Improved Record Keeping: Digital transactions provide an automatic electronic record, simplifying accounting and financial tracking for businesses.
  2. Circumstance for Wireless Mouse Necessity:
    • Presentations or Teaching: When presenting from a laptop connected to a projector or large screen, a wireless mouse allows the presenter to control the computer (e.g., advance slides, point) while moving freely around the room, away from the laptop itself.
    • Cluttered Desk or Ergonomics: In situations where desk space is limited or cable management is difficult, a wireless mouse eliminates cable clutter and offers more freedom of movement for better ergonomics.
    • Using a Computer from a Distance: Controlling a media center PC connected to a TV from a couch, or operating a computer in a location where being tethered by a cable is impractical.

(b) Safety and Security Measures for a Computer Laboratory:

  1. Physical Security: Control access to the lab using locks, key cards, or security personnel. Install surveillance cameras to monitor activity. Secure equipment physically using cable locks.
  2. Electrical Safety: Ensure proper wiring, grounding, and use of surge protectors or Uninterruptible Power Supplies (UPS) to protect against power fluctuations and surges. Avoid overloading circuits. Keep liquids away from equipment.
  3. Environmental Control: Maintain appropriate temperature and humidity levels to prevent equipment overheating or damage. Ensure adequate ventilation and dust control.
  4. Data Security and User Policies: Implement strong password policies, user authentication, and access controls. Install and regularly update antivirus and firewall software. Establish clear rules for acceptable use, prohibiting installation of unauthorized software or access to inappropriate websites. Regular data backups.

(c) Benefits of Networking for a Large Retail Shop:

  1. Centralized Inventory Management: Real-time tracking of stock levels across all Point of Sale (POS) terminals and potentially warehouses. This allows for automated reordering, reduces stockouts, and minimizes overstocking.
  2. Efficient Sales Processing and Reporting: POS terminals connected to a central server allow for faster checkouts, integrated payment processing, and centralized collection of sales data for analysis, reporting, and identifying trends.
  3. Improved Communication and Coordination: Enables communication between different departments (e.g., sales floor, stock room, management) and potentially between multiple store locations. Facilitates sharing of information like promotions, price updates, and staff schedules.
  4. Enhanced Customer Relationship Management (CRM): Allows for collecting customer data (with permission) through loyalty programs linked to POS transactions, enabling targeted marketing and personalized offers.
