  • Candidates should answer the questions in English.

SECTION A (40 marks)

Answer ALL the questions in this section.

Question 1

Outline two characteristics of the following types of computer monitors:

(i) cathode-ray tube;

(ii) flat-panel display.

Answer

Cathode-ray tube (CRT):

  • Bulky and heavy construction
  • High power consumption

Flat-panel display:

  • Thin and lightweight design
  • Lower power consumption

Question 2

Describe each of the following types of computers:

(i) analogue;

(ii) hybrid.

Answer

Analogue computers:

  • Process continuous data
  • Measure physical quantities like temperature, pressure, voltage

Hybrid computers:

  • Combine features of both analogue and digital computers
  • Can process both continuous and discrete data

Question 3

Explain two circumstances that could lead an organisation to secure their electronic data.

Answer

  • Threat of cyber attacks and data breaches
  • Legal requirements and compliance with data protection regulations

Question 4

A college intends to adopt computerised data processing in its operations. Describe two modes of processing that the college could consider.

Answer

1. Batch Processing:

  • Data is collected and processed in groups
  • Suitable for periodic tasks like payroll processing

2. Real-time Processing:

  • Data is processed immediately as it is received
  • Suitable for student registration and attendance tracking

Question 5

Tiffany, a student, has been using a flash disk to store her project work. Describe two benefits that she is likely to realise from using this device.

Answer

  • Portability - easily carried and used on different computers
  • Quick and easy data transfer with plug-and-play functionality

Question 6

Dan, a computer user, has been experiencing wrist pains. Explain two precautions he could take to prevent such pains.

Answer

  • Use an ergonomic wrist rest and maintain proper wrist position
  • Take regular breaks and perform wrist exercises

Question 7

Describe each of the following factors to consider when selecting computer hardware:

(i) compatibility;

(ii) expandability;

Answer

Compatibility:

  • Ability to work with existing hardware and software
  • Support for required operating systems and applications

Expandability:

  • Ability to upgrade or add components in the future
  • Available slots and ports for expansion

Question 8

Distinguish between optical mark reader (OMR) and optical character reader (OCR).

Answer

Optical Mark Reader (OMR):

  • Reads marks or shaded areas on forms
  • Used for multiple-choice tests and surveys

Optical Character Reader (OCR):

  • Recognizes and reads printed or handwritten text
  • Converts text images into editable text

Question 9

Diana created a master file on her new computer. Describe two types of files she could have used to create this file.

Answer

  • Transaction files - containing daily updates and changes
  • Reference files - containing static or rarely changed data

Question 10

Outline four methods that can be used to secure data in a computer system.

Answer

  • Data encryption
  • Password protection
  • Regular data backup
  • Access control mechanisms

SECTION B (60 marks)

Answer any FOUR questions from this section.

Question 11

(a) Outline three features of a mainframe computer.

(b) Mary copyrighted a new book she has published for sale. Explain three benefits she is likely to realise from copyrighting.

(c) Cloud Company has adopted the use of green computing technology. Explain three features of this technology.

Answer

(a) Features of mainframe computer:

  • Large processing capacity
  • Ability to handle multiple tasks simultaneously
  • High reliability and uptime

(b) Benefits of copyright:

  • Legal protection against unauthorized copying
  • Control over distribution and sales
  • Ability to earn royalties from the work

(c) Features of green computing:

  • Energy-efficient hardware and operations
  • Reduced electronic waste through recycling
  • Use of environmentally friendly materials

Question 12

(a) Explain three negative effects of information communication technology to a college student.

(b) Distinguish between \(CD-RW\) and \(CD-R\) as used in computers.

(c) Figure 1 shows a computer network configuration. Use it to answer the questions that follow:

(i) Identify the type of computer network in Figure 1.

(ii) Outline four benefits of this network to an organisation.

Answer

(a) Negative effects of ICT to students:

  • Addiction to social media and online activities
  • Health issues from prolonged computer use
  • Distractions from studies

(b) CD-RW vs CD-R:

CD-RW (Rewritable):

  • Can be written to multiple times
  • More expensive than CD-R
  • Data can be erased and rewritten

CD-R (Recordable):

  • Can only be written to once
  • Less expensive than CD-RW
  • Data cannot be erased once written

(c) (i) Network type:

Star topology network

(ii) Benefits of star network:

  • Easy to install and configure new devices
  • Fault isolation - failure of one node doesn't affect others
  • Centralized control and management
  • High performance due to direct connections

Question 13

(a) Sisi Company uses random file access method. Explain three advantages of this method.

(b) Explain two uses of computers for defence.

(c) Harry bought a wireless mouse for use in his office computer.

(i) describe this device.

(ii) outline three disadvantages of this device.

Answer

(a) Advantages of random file access:

  • Quick and direct access to specific records
  • No need to search through entire file
  • Efficient for real-time applications

(b) Uses of computers in defence:

  • Military surveillance and monitoring systems
  • Weapon systems control and simulation

(c) (i) Wireless mouse description:

A pointing device that connects to the computer without physical cables, using radio frequency or Bluetooth technology.

(ii) Disadvantages of wireless mouse:

  • Requires battery replacement or charging
  • Possible interference from other wireless devices
  • Generally more expensive than wired mouse

Question 14

(a) Outline four application areas of computers in agriculture.

(b) Bei Bora supermarket uses a point of sale terminal (POS) to serve its customers. Describe two keying in devices that the supermarket could use.

(c) Explain two reasons for the preference of a star topology configuration.

(d) A technician installed a network operating system to an institute's computers. Outline three features of this software.

Answer

(a) Computer applications in agriculture:

  • Weather monitoring and forecasting
  • Irrigation control systems
  • Crop yield prediction
  • Farm management and record keeping

(b) POS keying devices:

  • Barcode scanner - for reading product codes
  • Numeric keypad - for entering quantities and prices

(c) Reasons for star topology preference:

  • Easy troubleshooting and maintenance
  • High reliability due to independent connections

(d) Network operating system features:

  • User authentication and access control
  • File and printer sharing capabilities
  • Network security management

Question 15

(a) Outline three types of utility programs used in computers.

(b) John used shielded twisted pair cable in a computer network. Explain three advantages of using this cable.

(c) Pauline would like to buy a user friendly software for her company. Describe three characteristics of such software.

Answer

(a) Types of utility programs:

  • Antivirus software
  • Disk defragmentation tools
  • Backup utilities

(b) Advantages of shielded twisted pair cable:

  • Better protection against electromagnetic interference
  • Reduced signal loss over longer distances
  • Higher data transmission speeds

(c) Characteristics of user-friendly software:

  • Intuitive interface and easy navigation
  • Clear error messages and help documentation
  • Consistent design and layout throughout
