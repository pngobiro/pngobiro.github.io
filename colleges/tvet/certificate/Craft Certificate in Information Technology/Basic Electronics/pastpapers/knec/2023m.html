Question 1 (4 marks)

Outline four disadvantages of an Integrated Circuit (IC).

Answer

Disclaimer: Answers are AI-generated and may contain errors. Verify independently.

Four disadvantages of Integrated Circuits (ICs):

  • Limited Power Handling: Individual ICs generally cannot handle very high power levels compared to discrete components or electromechanical devices.
  • Inductors and Large Capacitors Difficult to Fabricate: It's difficult or impractical to fabricate high-value inductors or large capacitors directly onto an IC chip. These often need to be external components.
  • Repair is Impossible: If a single component within an IC fails, the entire chip usually needs to be replaced; individual components cannot be repaired.
  • Susceptibility to Damage: ICs can be sensitive to electrostatic discharge (ESD), overheating, and radiation, requiring careful handling and protective measures.

Question 3 (4 marks)

Using one's complement, calculate $1110000_{1}-10101111_{2}$.

Answer

Disclaimer: Answers are AI-generated and may contain errors. Verify independently.

Calculate $1110000_2 - 10101111_2$ using one's complement.

  1. Pad the minuend ($1110000_2$) to match the length of the subtrahend ($10101111_2$, 8 bits): $01110000_2$.
  2. Find the one's complement of the subtrahend ($10101111_2$): $01010000_2$.
  3. Add the minuend ($01110000_2$) to the one's complement of the subtrahend ($01010000_2$): 
       01110000
+  01010000
-----------
   11000000
  4. There is no end-around carry. This indicates the result is negative. The result ($11000000_2$) is the one's complement of the magnitude.
  5. Find the magnitude by taking the one's complement of the result: $1's \text{ complement of } 11000000_2 = 00111111_2$.

Magnitude: $00111111_2 = 32 + 16 + 8 + 4 + 2 + 1 = 63_{10}$.

Result: $-00111111_2$ (or $-63_{10}$)

Check (Decimal): $01110000_2 = 112_{10}$. $10101111_2 = 175_{10}$. $112 - 175 = -63$. Correct.

Question 5 (4 marks)

Explain two advantages of Cache memory in computers.

Answer

Disclaimer: Answers are AI-generated and may contain errors. Verify independently.

Two advantages of Cache Memory:

  1. Reduced Average Memory Access Time: By storing frequently used data and instructions in a smaller, faster memory closer to the CPU, cache significantly reduces the average time the CPU spends waiting for data from the slower main memory (RAM).
  2. Increased System Performance/Throughput: Reducing memory access latency allows the CPU to process instructions faster and more continuously, leading to higher overall system performance and throughput (more tasks completed per unit time).

Question 9 (4 marks)

Explain two disadvantages of using hexadecimal number systems.

Answer

Disclaimer: Answers are AI-generated and may contain errors. Verify independently.

Two disadvantages of using hexadecimal:

  1. Less Intuitive for Humans: While more compact than binary, hexadecimal is still less intuitive for most people than the familiar decimal system, especially for arithmetic operations or quickly grasping the magnitude of a number.
  2. Requires Conversion: Humans typically think and input data in decimal. Using hexadecimal requires conversion to/from decimal or binary, which can be error-prone if done manually and adds a layer of abstraction.

Question 10 (4 marks)

Figure 1 represents a closed electric circuit of capacitors $\mathrm{C}_{1}(0.6 \mu \mathrm{~F}), \mathrm{C}_{2}(0.3 \mu \mathrm{~F})$ and $\mathrm{C}_{3}(0.9 \mu \mathrm{~F})$ and a charge of 10 Coulomb. Use it to answer the question that follows.

Determine the voltage in the circuit.

Answer

Disclaimer: Answers are AI-generated and may contain errors. Verify independently.

The circuit shows three capacitors ($C_1=0.6 \mu F, C_2=0.3 \mu F, C_3=0.9 \mu F$) connected in series. The total charge (Q) is given as 10 Coulombs.

1. Calculate the equivalent capacitance ($C_{eq}$) for capacitors in series:

$$ \frac{1}{C_{eq}} = \frac{1}{C_1} + \frac{1}{C_2} + \frac{1}{C_3} $$ $$ \frac{1}{C_{eq}} = \frac{1}{0.6 \mu F} + \frac{1}{0.3 \mu F} + \frac{1}{0.9 \mu F} $$

To add these, find a common denominator (e.g., 1.8):

$$ \frac{1}{C_{eq}} = \frac{3}{1.8 \mu F} + \frac{6}{1.8 \mu F} + \frac{2}{1.8 \mu F} = \frac{11}{1.8 \mu F} $$ $$ C_{eq} = \frac{1.8}{11} \mu F \approx 0.1636 \mu F $$

2. Calculate the total voltage (V) using the formula $Q = CV$:

$$ V = \frac{Q}{C_{eq}} $$ $$ V = \frac{10 \, C}{0.1636 \times 10^{-6} \, F} \approx 61.1 \times 10^6 \, V $$

Result: The voltage in the circuit is approximately $\mathbf{61.1 \, MV}$ (Megavolts).

Note: A charge of 10 Coulombs on capacitors with microfarad values results in an extremely high voltage, suggesting a potential issue in the question's premise or values.

(a) (i) Disadvantages of Integrated Circuits (ICs):

  • Limited Power Handling: Cannot handle very high power/voltage compared to discrete components.
  • Difficult to Fabricate Inductors/Large Capacitors: High-value inductors and large capacitors are impractical to integrate directly onto the chip.
  • Non-Repairable: If a component within the IC fails, the entire chip must be replaced.

(b) (ii) Battery Terms:

  • (I) Float Charging: A method of charging a secondary (rechargeable) battery where it is continuously connected to a charger that maintains the battery at a full charge level by applying a low, constant voltage.
