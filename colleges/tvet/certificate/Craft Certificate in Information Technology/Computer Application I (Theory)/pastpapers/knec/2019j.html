Question 1 (X marks)

(a) Outline two advantages of using a mouse over a keyboard as an input device. (2 marks)

(b) Outline the effect of over-using each of the following in a presentation program:

  1. Slide Transitions; (1 mark)
  2. Standard Clipart;
  3. Sound Effects.

Answer

Disclaimer: Answers are AI-generated and may contain errors. Verify independently.

(a) Advantages of Mouse over Keyboard:

  • Direct Manipulation: Allows users to directly point at, click on, and drag graphical objects (icons, windows, files), which is often more intuitive for spatial tasks than typing commands.
  • Ease of Selection: Selecting text, files, or graphical elements is often faster and easier by clicking and dragging with a mouse compared to using keyboard combinations.

(b) Effects of Over-using Presentation Features:

  1. Slide Transitions: Can be distracting, appear unprofessional, slow down the presentation flow, and take focus away from the content.
  2. Standard Clipart: Can make the presentation look dated, generic, unprofessional, and may not align well with the specific message or branding.
  3. Sound Effects: Often perceived as annoying, distracting, unprofessional, and can detract significantly from the speaker's message.

Question 2 (X marks)

Explain two reasons for an organisation opting to use e-mails as a mode of communication.

Answer

Disclaimer: Answers are AI-generated and may contain errors. Verify independently.

Reasons for using e-mails:

  • Cost-Effectiveness: Sending emails is significantly cheaper than traditional mail (postage) or long-distance phone calls, especially for international communication.
  • Speed and Efficiency: Emails are delivered almost instantly, allowing for rapid communication and quicker turnaround times compared to postal mail.
  • Record Keeping: Emails provide an automatic written record of communication that can be easily stored, searched, and retrieved for future reference.
  • Asynchronous Communication: Allows sender and receiver to communicate without needing to be available at the exact same time.

Question 4 (X marks)

(a) A student bought an optical mouse for her laptop. Outline two mouse clicking techniques applicable to this kind of mouse.

(b) With an aid of a diagram, describe a hierarchical database model.

Answer

Disclaimer: Answers are AI-generated and may contain errors. Verify independently.

(a) Optical Mouse Clicking Techniques:

  • Single-Click (Left Button): Used to select an item, place the cursor, or activate a button/menu.
  • Double-Click (Left Button): Used typically to open a file/folder or run a program/shortcut.
  • Right-Click: Opens a context-sensitive menu with options relevant to the item clicked on.
  • Click-and-Drag (Left Button): Used to move items (like icons or windows) or select multiple items/text.
  • Scroll Wheel Click (Middle Button, if present): Often enables auto-scrolling or can be customized for other functions.

(b) Hierarchical Database Model:

Organizes data in a tree-like structure where records are linked in parent-child relationships. Each child record has only one parent, but a parent can have multiple children. Data access typically follows the defined paths from parent to child.

Question 5 (X marks)

Explain each of the following terms as used in computer software:

(a) off-the self;

(b) in-house.

Answer

Disclaimer: Answers are AI-generated and may contain errors. Verify independently.

Computer Software Terms:

(a) Off-the-shelf: Refers to ready-made software applications that are commercially available for purchase or license by the general public or businesses. They are designed to meet the needs of a broad audience (e.g., Microsoft Word, Adobe Photoshop).

(b) In-house: Refers to custom software developed specifically by or for an organization for its own internal use. It is tailored to the organization's unique processes and requirements and is not typically sold commercially.

Question 6 (X marks)

Aun was using a word processing program to create a document. Explain two approaches that she may use to navigate through the document.

Answer

Disclaimer: Answers are AI-generated and may contain errors. Verify independently.

Approaches to navigate through a word processing document:

  • Using Scroll Bars: Clicking and dragging the vertical or horizontal scroll bars to move up/down or left/right through the document pages.
  • Using Keyboard Keys: Employing keys like Page Up, Page Down, Home, End, Arrow Keys, and combinations with Ctrl (e.g., Ctrl+Home to go to the beginning, Ctrl+End to go to the end) to move the cursor and view different parts of the document.
  • Using the Find/Search Feature: Searching for specific text to jump directly to its occurrences within the document.
  • Using the Go To Feature: Navigating directly to a specific page number, section, line, bookmark, or other item.
  • Using Navigation Pane/Document Map: Clicking on headings or pages in a dedicated navigation pane to jump to that section.

Question 7 (4 marks)

The following are cell references used in a certain worksheet. State the type of reference used in each case:

(a) B5; (1 mark)

(b) $G$6; (1 mark)

(c) K$31; (1 mark)

(d) $A7. (1 mark)

Answer

Disclaimer: Answers are AI-generated and may contain errors. Verify independently.

Cell Reference Types:

(a) B5: Relative Reference (both column B and row 5 will adjust when copied).

(b) $G$6: Absolute Reference (both column G and row 6 remain fixed when copied).

(c) K$31: Mixed Reference (column K is relative, row 31 is absolute).

(d) $A7: Mixed Reference (column A is absolute, row 7 is relative).

Question 8 (X marks)

(a) A student created a form in a database. Outline two reasons for this.

(b) A student has opted to use a graphical user interface operating system. Outline two characteristic of this user interface.

Answer

Disclaimer: Answers are AI-generated and may contain errors. Verify independently.

(a) Reasons for Creating a Database Form:

  • Improved User Interface: Forms provide a more visually organized and user-friendly way to enter, edit, and view data compared to the raw table (datasheet) view.
  • Enhanced Data Entry Control: Forms allow for the inclusion of validation rules, input masks, dropdown lists, and other controls to guide users and ensure data accuracy and consistency during input.
  • Simplified Data Access: Forms can be designed to show data from related tables together, making it easier for users to see connected information in one place.

(b) Characteristics of Graphical User Interface (GUI):

  • Visual Elements: Uses graphical elements like icons, windows, buttons, menus instead of text commands.
  • Direct Manipulation: Users interact with objects directly using a pointing device (like a mouse) through actions like clicking, dragging, dropping.
  • WIMP (Windows, Icons, Menus, Pointer): These are the standard components that define most GUIs.
  • User-Friendly/Intuitive: Generally considered easier to learn and use, especially for non-technical users, compared to command-line interfaces.

Question 9 (X marks)

Differentiate between an entity and an attribute as used in databases.

Answer

Disclaimer: Answers are AI-generated and may contain errors. Verify independently.

Entity vs. Attribute in Databases:

  • Entity: Represents a distinct real-world object, person, place, concept, or event about which data is stored. In a relational database, an entity typically corresponds to a table (e.g., Student, Product, Order).
  • Attribute: Represents a property, characteristic, or piece of information describing an entity. In a relational database, an attribute typically corresponds to a column or field within a table (e.g., StudentName, ProductPrice, OrderDate are attributes of their respective entities).

Question 10 (4 marks)

(a) John would like to use views to organise a presentation he had created. State four types that he is likely to use. (2 marks)

(b) A student while using a word processor interacted with a thesaurus feature. Outline two functions of this feature.

Answer

Disclaimer: Answers are AI-generated and may contain errors. Verify independently.

(a) Presentation Views:

  • Normal View: The main editing view, showing the current slide large, an outline/thumbnail pane, and a notes pane.
  • Slide Sorter View: Shows thumbnails of all slides, allowing easy rearrangement and application of transitions/timings.
  • Notes Page View: Shows the slide and its associated speaker notes, primarily for printing or reviewing notes.
  • Reading View: Displays the presentation in a window with simple controls, similar to a slide show but not full screen.
  • Slide Show View: Displays the presentation full screen as it would appear to the audience.

(b) Thesaurus Feature Functions:

  • Finding Synonyms: Provides alternative words with similar meanings for a selected word, helping to vary vocabulary or find a more precise term.
  • Finding Antonyms (Sometimes): Some thesaurus tools also offer antonyms (words with opposite meanings).
