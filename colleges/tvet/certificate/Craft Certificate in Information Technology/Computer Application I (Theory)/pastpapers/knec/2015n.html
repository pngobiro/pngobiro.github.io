Question 1 (X marks)

State four text wrap options that one may apply on graphics inserted on a word processing document.

Answer

Disclaimer: Answers are AI-generated and may contain errors. Verify independently.

Four text wrap options for graphics in word processing:

  • In Line with Text: Treats the graphic like a large character within the text line.
  • Square: Text wraps around the rectangular bounding box of the graphic.
  • Tight: Text wraps closely around the actual contours of the graphic (if irregular).
  • Behind Text: The graphic is placed on a layer behind the text, allowing text to flow over it.
  • In Front of Text: The graphic is placed on a layer above the text, obscuring any text underneath it.

Question 3 (X marks)

Distinguish between a form and a report as used in databases.

Answer

Disclaimer: Answers are AI-generated and may contain errors. Verify independently.

Form vs. Report in Databases:

  • Form: Primarily used for interactive data entry, viewing, and editing of records, usually one record at a time. Forms provide a user-friendly interface to interact directly with table data.
  • Report: Designed for presenting summarized and formatted data retrieved from tables or queries, typically for printing or viewing. Reports are generally read-only and focus on data presentation rather than data modification.

Question 4 (X marks)

Outline four characteristics of a relational database.

Answer

Disclaimer: Answers are AI-generated and may contain errors. Verify independently.

Four characteristics of a relational database:

  • Data is stored in tables: Information is organized into relations (tables) consisting of rows and columns.
  • Unique Primary Keys: Each row (tuple) in a table is uniquely identified by a primary key.
  • Relationships via Foreign Keys: Tables are linked together using foreign keys, which reference primary keys in other tables, establishing relationships.
  • Data Integrity: Enforces rules (like entity integrity, referential integrity) to ensure data accuracy and consistency.
  • Structured Query Language (SQL): Typically uses SQL for data definition, manipulation, and querying.

Question 7 (X marks)

Marion, a computer laboratory technician opted to install software with graphical user interface. Describe two components of the software that will make the user to efficiently interact with it.

Answer

Disclaimer: Answers are AI-generated and may contain errors. Verify independently.

Two GUI components for efficient user interaction:

  • Menus (e.g., File, Edit, View): Organize commands and options logically, allowing users to easily find and execute actions without needing to memorize commands. Drop-down or context menus provide relevant options quickly.
  • Icons and Buttons: Provide visual representations of actions, files, or applications. Clicking an icon or button offers a direct and intuitive way to trigger functions or open items, making interaction faster than typing commands.
  • Windows: Allow multiple applications or documents to be viewed and managed simultaneously, enabling multitasking and easy information transfer between tasks.

Question 8 (X marks)

A company prefers to use a system that is managed and controlled by a computer rather than a human being. Explain two reasons for this preference.

Answer

Disclaimer: Answers are AI-generated and may contain errors. Verify independently.

Reasons for preferring computer-controlled systems:

  • Consistency and Accuracy: Computers perform repetitive tasks with high precision and consistency, reducing errors often associated with human fatigue or variability.
  • Speed and Efficiency: Automated systems can often operate much faster than humans, processing large amounts of data or performing actions rapidly, leading to increased productivity and efficiency.
  • Availability: Computer systems can operate 24/7 without breaks, fatigue, or shift changes, ensuring continuous operation where needed.

Question 9 (X marks)

Explain two benefits of using blue tooth technology in data communication.

Answer

Disclaimer: Answers are AI-generated and may contain errors. Verify independently.

Benefits of using Bluetooth technology:

  • Wireless Convenience: Eliminates the need for cables for short-range communication between devices (e.g., connecting headsets, keyboards, mice, speakers, or transferring files between phones/laptops), offering greater flexibility and reducing clutter.
  • Low Power Consumption: Bluetooth (especially Bluetooth Low Energy - BLE) is designed to use minimal power, making it ideal for battery-operated devices like wearables, wireless earbuds, and sensors.
