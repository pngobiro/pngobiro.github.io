Question 1 (4 marks)

1. Convert each of the decimal numbers to an 8-bit two's complement binary number:

(i) \(47_{10}\);

(ii) \(-72_{10}\).

Answer

(i) \(47_{10}\):

To convert 47 to an 8-bit two's complement binary number:

1. Convert 47 to binary: \(47_{10} = 00101111_{2}\)

Since 47 is positive, the two's complement representation is the same as the binary representation.

Therefore, the 8-bit two's complement representation of 47 is 00101111.

(ii) \(-72_{10}\):

To convert -72 to an 8-bit two's complement binary number:

1. Convert 72 to binary: \(72_{10} = 01001000_{2}\)

2. Invert the bits: 10110111

3. Add 1: 10111000

Therefore, the 8-bit two's complement representation of -72 is 10111000.

Question 2 (4 marks)

2. Differentiate between \(B C D\) and \(E B C D I C\) computer coding systems.

Answer

BCD (Binary Coded Decimal):

- Represents each decimal digit with 4 bits.

- Can represent numbers 0-9.

- Simpler to implement but less efficient for storing large numbers.

EBCDIC (Extended Binary Coded Decimal Interchange Code):

- Represents characters with 8 bits.

- Can represent 256 different characters.

- Used primarily in IBM mainframe systems.

Difference: BCD is used to represent decimal digits, while EBCDIC is used to represent a wider range of characters.

Question 3 (4 marks)

3. Describe each of the following types of statistical data:

(i) discrete data;

(ii) continuous data.

Answer

(i) Discrete data:

- Data that can only take specific, distinct values.

- These values are often integers.

- Examples: Number of students in a class, number of cars in a parking lot.

(ii) Continuous data:

- Data that can take any value within a given range.

- Can include fractions and decimals.

- Examples: Height of students, temperature of a room.

Question 4 (4 marks)

4. Use the binomial theorem to expand the expression \((5 x+2 b)^{3}\) in the descending powers of \(x\).

Answer

Using the binomial theorem, the expansion of \((5x + 2b)^3\) is:

\((5x + 2b)^3 = (5x)^3 + 3(5x)^2(2b) + 3(5x)(2b)^2 + (2b)^3\)

\(= 125x^3 + 3(25x^2)(2b) + 3(5x)(4b^2) + 8b^3\)

\(= 125x^3 + 150x^2b + 60xb^2 + 8b^3\)

Therefore, \((5 x+2 b)^{3} = 125x^3 + 150x^2b + 60xb^2 + 8b^3\)

Question 5 (4 marks)

5. Given that matrix \(\mathbf{W}=\left[\begin{array}{rr}3 & -4 \\ -2 & 5\end{array}\right]\), determine \((2 \mathbf{W})^{-1}\).

Answer

First, calculate 2W:

\(2\mathbf{W} = 2\left[\begin{array}{rr}3 & -4 \\ -2 & 5\end{array}\right] = \left[\begin{array}{rr}6 & -8 \\ -4 & 10\end{array}\right]\)

Next, find the inverse of 2W:

The determinant of 2W is: \((6 \times 10) - (-8 \times -4) = 60 - 32 = 28\)

The inverse of 2W is:

\((2\mathbf{W})^{-1} = \frac{1}{28}\left[\begin{array}{rr}10 & 8 \\ 4 & 6\end{array}\right] = \left[\begin{array}{rr}10/28 & 8/28 \\ 4/28 & 6/28\end{array}\right] = \left[\begin{array}{rr}5/14 & 2/7 \\ 1/7 & 3/14\end{array}\right]\)

Therefore, \((2 \mathbf{W})^{-1} = \left[\begin{array}{rr}5/14 & 2/7 \\ 1/7 & 3/14\end{array}\right]\)

Question 6 (4 marks)

6. Use the matrix method to solve the following pair of simultaneous equations:

\(\begin{array}{r} x+2 y=-1 \\ 3 x-5 y=19 \end{array}\)

Answer

The matrix representation of the equations is:

\(\left[\begin{array}{rr}1 & 2 \\ 3 & -5\end{array}\right]\left[\begin{array}{l}x \\ y\end{array}\right] = \left[\begin{array}{r}-1 \\ 19\end{array}\right]\)

Let \(A = \left[\begin{array}{rr}1 & 2 \\ 3 & -5\end{array}\right]\), \(X = \left[\begin{array}{l}x \\ y\end{array}\right]\), and \(B = \left[\begin{array}{r}-1 \\ 19\end{array}\right]\)

Then \(AX = B\), so \(X = A^{-1}B\)

The determinant of A is: \((1 \times -5) - (2 \times 3) = -5 - 6 = -11\)

The inverse of A is:

\(A^{-1} = \frac{1}{-11}\left[\begin{array}{rr}-5 & -2 \\ -3 & 1\end{array}\right] = \left[\begin{array}{rr}5/11 & 2/11 \\ 3/11 & -1/11\end{array}\right]\)

Now, calculate X:

\(X = \left[\begin{array}{rr}5/11 & 2/11 \\ 3/11 & -1/11\end{array}\right]\left[\begin{array}{r}-1 \\ 19\end{array}\right] = \left[\begin{array}{r}(5/11 \times -1) + (2/11 \times 19) \\ (3/11 \times -1) + (-1/11 \times 19)\end{array}\right] = \left[\begin{array}{r}-5/11 + 38/11 \\ -3/11 - 19/11\end{array}\right] = \left[\begin{array}{r}33/11 \\ -22/11\end{array}\right] = \left[\begin{array}{r}3 \\ -2\end{array}\right]\)

Therefore, \(x = 3, y = -2\)

Question 7 (4 marks)

7. Determine the value of \(x\) in each of the following inequalities:

(i) \(-2(x+3)<10\);

(ii) \(-4 x(x-3) \leq 16\).

Answer

(i) \(-2(x+3)<10\):

Expand the inequality: \(-2x - 6 < 10\)

Add 6 to both sides: \(-2x < 16\)

Divide by -2 (and reverse the inequality): \(x > -8\)

Therefore, \(x > -8\)

(ii) \(-4 x(x-3) \leq 16\):

Expand the inequality: \(-4x^2 + 12x \leq 16\)

Rearrange: \(-4x^2 + 12x - 16 \leq 0\)

Divide by -4: \(x^2 - 3x + 4 \geq 0\)

To find the discriminant: \(b^2 - 4ac = (-3)^2 - 4(1)(4) = 9 - 16 = -7\)

Since the discriminant is negative, the quadratic has no real roots. Also, since the coefficient of \(x^2\) is positive, the parabola opens upwards. Therefore, the inequality is always true.

Therefore, No real solutions

Question 8 (4 marks)

8. Outline three methods of collecting statistical data.

Answer

Three methods of collecting statistical data are:

- Surveys: Collecting data through questionnaires or interviews. This method is useful for gathering information from a large group of people.

- Experiments: Collecting data through controlled experiments. This method is useful for determining cause-and-effect relationships.

- Observation: Collecting data through direct observation of events or phenomena. This method is useful for studying behavior in natural settings.

Question 9 (4 marks)

9. Describe two ways in which statistical data can be classified.

Answer

Two ways in which statistical data can be classified are:

- Qualitative vs. Quantitative: Qualitative data is descriptive and non-numerical (e.g., colors, opinions), while quantitative data is numerical (e.g., height, weight).

- Primary vs. Secondary: Primary data is collected directly from the original source (e.g., through surveys or experiments), while secondary data is collected from existing sources (e.g., government publications, research articles).

Question 10 (4 marks)

10. Explain two assumptions in Poisson probability distribution.

Answer

Two assumptions in Poisson probability distribution are:

- Events occur independently: The occurrence of one event does not affect the probability of another event occurring. In other words, events are random and not influenced by previous events.

- The average rate of events is constant: The average rate at which events occur is constant over time or space. This means that the probability of an event occurring is the same regardless of when or where it occurs.

Question 11 (15 marks)

(a) Describe each of the following types of matrices:

(i) column matrix;

(ii) scalar matrix.

(b) Given the following matrices, \(\mathbf{X}=\left[\begin{array}{rc}2 & -1 \\ 3 & 0 \\ -5 & 2\end{array}\right], \mathbf{Y}=\left[\begin{array}{rrr}4 & 4 & -3 \\ 0 & -1 & -2\end{array}\right]\) and \(\mathbf{Z}=\left[\begin{array}{rr}1 & 6 \\ 1 & -2 \\ 0 & -3\end{array}\right]\)

Determine each of the following matrix operations:

(i) \((\mathbf{X Y}) \mathbf{Z}\);

(ii) \(\quad \mathbf{X}^{\mathrm{T}} \mathbf{Z}\).

(c) Convert each of the following numbers to their equivalent number systems indicated:

(i) \(63150_{8}\) to binary;

(ii) \(153_{8}\) to hexadecimal.

Answer

(a) (i) A column matrix is a matrix with only one column. It has dimensions \(m \times 1\), where m is the number of rows.

(ii) A scalar matrix is a diagonal matrix in which all the diagonal elements are equal. It is a square matrix where all non-diagonal elements are zero and all diagonal elements are the same scalar value.

(b) (i) \((\mathbf{X Y}) \mathbf{Z}\):

First, calculate \(\mathbf{X Y}\):

\(\mathbf{X Y} = \left[\begin{array}{rc}2 & -1 \\ 3 & 0 \\ -5 & 2\end{array}\right] \left[\begin{array}{rrr}4 & 4 & -3 \\ 0 & -1 & -2\end{array}\right] = \left[\begin{array}{rrr}8 & 9 & -4 \\ 12 & 12 & -9 \\ -20 & -22 & 11\end{array}\right]\)

Then, calculate \((\mathbf{X Y}) \mathbf{Z}\):

\((\mathbf{X Y}) \mathbf{Z} = \left[\begin{array}{rrr}8 & 9 & -4 \\ 12 & 12 & -9 \\ -20 & -22 & 11\end{array}\right] \left[\begin{array}{rr}1 & 6 \\ 1 & -2 \\ 0 & -3\end{array}\right] = \left[\begin{array}{rr}17 & 14 \\ 24 & 21 \\ -42 & -40\end{array}\right]\)

(ii) \(\mathbf{X}^{\mathrm{T}} \mathbf{Z}\):

First, find the transpose of \(\mathbf{X}\):

\(\mathbf{X}^{\mathrm{T}} = \left[\begin{array}{rrr}2 & 3 & -5 \\ -1 & 0 & 2\end{array}\right]\)

Then, calculate \(\mathbf{X}^{\mathrm{T}} \mathbf{Z}\):

\(\mathbf{X}^{\mathrm{T}} \mathbf{Z} = \left[\begin{array}{rrr}2 & 3 & -5 \\ -1 & 0 & 2\end{array}\right] \left[\begin{array}{rr}1 & 6 \\ 1 & -2 \\ 0 & -3\end{array}\right] = \left[\begin{array}{rr}5 & 23 \\ -1 & 0\end{array}\right]\)

(c) (i) \(63150_{8}\) to binary:

Convert each octal digit to binary:

\(6_{8} = 110_{2}\), \(3_{8} = 011_{2}\), \(1_{8} = 001_{2}\), \(5_{8} = 101_{2}\), \(0_{8} = 000_{2}\)

Combine the binary digits: \(110\ 011\ 001\ 101\ 000\)

Therefore, \(63150_{8} = 11001100110101000_{2}\)

(ii) \(153_{8}\) to hexadecimal:

Convert octal to binary: \(153_{8} = 001\ 101\ 011_{2}\)

Group the binary digits into groups of 4 from right to left: \(0011\ 0101\ 0011\)

Add leading zeros to the leftmost group if necessary: \(0011\ 0101\ 0011\)

Convert each group of 4 to hexadecimal: \(3_{16}\ 5_{16}\ 3_{16}\)

Therefore, \(153_{8} = 353_{16}\)

Question 12 (15 marks)

(a) Table 1 shows the probability of selling a specified number of cars by a certain car dealer in a certain month. Use it to answer the question that follows.

Table 1

\(\begin{tabular}{|l|l|l|l|l|l|l|l|l|} \hline Number of cars & 8 & 9 & 10 & 11 & 12 & 13 & 14 & 15 \\ \hline Probability (x) & 0.10 & 0.15 & 0.15 & 0.25 & 0.20 & 0.10 & 0.0 & 0.05 \\ \hline \end{tabular}\)

Determine the number of cars the car dealer expects to sell during the period. (2 marks)

(b) Use the substitution method to solve the following pair of simultaneous equations:

\(\begin{aligned} & 3 x-y=11 \\ & 3 x-2 y=4 \end{aligned}\)

(c). Using graphical method, solve the following pair of simultaneous equations:

(Use the range \(-4 \leq x \leq 3\))

\(\begin{aligned} & y=2 x^{2}+x-5 \\ & y=2 x+1 \end{aligned}\)

(d) A team comprising of 7 men and 6 women and a committee of 5 persons is to be formed from a group. Determine the number of ways in which a committee of 3 men and 2 women could be formed.

Answer

(a) The expected number of cars is calculated as the sum of (number of cars * probability):

\(E(x) = (8 \times 0.10) + (9 \times 0.15) + (10 \times 0.15) + (11 \times 0.25) + (12 \times 0.20) + (13 \times 0.10) + (14 \times 0.0) + (15 \times 0.05)\)

\(E(x) = 0.8 + 1.35 + 1.5 + 2.75 + 2.4 + 1.3 + 0 + 0.75 = 10.85\)

Therefore, the car dealer expects to sell approximately 10.85 cars during the period.

(b) Using the substitution method to solve the following pair of simultaneous equations:

\(\begin{aligned} & 3 x-y=11 \\ & 3 x-2 y=4 \end{aligned}\)

From the first equation, express y in terms of x: \(y = 3x - 11\)

Substitute this into the second equation: \(3x - 2(3x - 11) = 4\)

\(3x - 6x + 22 = 4\)

\(-3x = -18\)

\(x = 6\)

Substitute x = 6 into the first equation: \(3(6) - y = 11\)

\(18 - y = 11\)

\(y = 7\)

Therefore, \(x = 6, y = 7\)

(c) Using graphical method, solve the following pair of simultaneous equations:

\(\begin{aligned} & y=2 x^{2}+x-5 \\ & y=2 x+1 \end{aligned}\)

Equate the two equations: \(2x^2 + x - 5 = 2x + 1\)

\(2x^2 - x - 6 = 0\)

Factorize the quadratic equation: \((2x + 3)(x - 2) = 0\)

Solve for x: \(x = -3/2\) or \(x = 2\)

Substitute x = -3/2 into \(y = 2x + 1\): \(y = 2(-3/2) + 1 = -3 + 1 = -2\)

Substitute x = 2 into \(y = 2x + 1\): \(y = 2(2) + 1 = 4 + 1 = 5\)

Therefore, \(x = -1.5, y = -2\) and \(x = 2, y = 5\)

(d) A team comprising of 7 men and 6 women and a committee of 5 persons is to be formed from a group. Determine the number of ways in which a committee of 3 men and 2 women could be formed.

Number of ways to choose 3 men from 7: \(\binom{7}{3} = \frac{7!}{3!(7-3)!} = \frac{7!}{3!4!} = \frac{7 \times 6 \times 5}{3 \times 2 \times 1} = 35\)

Number of ways to choose 2 women from 6: \(\binom{6}{2} = \frac{6!}{2!(6-2)!} = \frac{6!}{2!4!} = \frac{6 \times 5}{2 \times 1} = 15\)

Total number of ways to form the committee: \(35 \times 15 = 525\)

Therefore, the number of ways in which a committee of 3 men and 2 women could be formed is 525.

Question 13 (15 marks)

(a) Outline three properties of a binomial probability distribution.

(b) Using Pascal's triangle, expand the expression \((x-3 y)^{4}\) in ascending powers of \(y\).

(c) Table 2 shows distribution of overtime hours worked by 100 employees of a company. Use it to answer the questions that follow.

Table 2

\(\begin{tabular}{|l|c|c|c|c|c|c|} \hline Overtime hours & 10-15 & 15-20 & 20-25 & 25-30 & 30-35 & 35-40 \\ \hline Number of employees & 11 & 20 & 35 & 20 & 8 & 6 \\ \hline \end{tabular}\)

Calculate each of the following measures about the overtime hours:

(i) the median;

(ii) the standard deviation.

Answer

(a) Three properties of a binomial probability distribution are:

- The experiment consists of n repeated trials: The binomial distribution is based on a fixed number of trials, where each trial is independent of the others.

- Each trial has only two possible outcomes: success or failure: Each trial can result in only one of two outcomes, typically labeled as success or failure.

- The probability of success, denoted by p, is the same for each trial: The probability of success remains constant across all trials.

(b) Using Pascal's triangle, expand the expression \((x-3 y)^{4}\) in ascending powers of \(y\).

The coefficients from Pascal's triangle for n=4 are 1, 4, 6, 4, 1.

\((x-3y)^4 = 1(x)^4(-3y)^0 + 4(x)^3(-3y)^1 + 6(x)^2(-3y)^2 + 4(x)^1(-3y)^3 + 1(x)^0(-3y)^4\)

\(= x^4 + 4x^3(-3y) + 6x^2(9y^2) + 4x(-27y^3) + 1(81y^4)\)

\(= x^4 - 12x^3y + 54x^2y^2 - 108xy^3 + 81y^4\)

(c) Calculate the median and standard deviation for the overtime hours:

First, calculate the cumulative frequency:

\(\begin{tabular}{|l|c|c|c|} \hline Overtime hours & Frequency (f) & Midpoint (x) & Cumulative Frequency (cf) \\ \hline 10-15 & 11 & 12.5 & 11 \\ 15-20 & 20 & 17.5 & 31 \\ 20-25 & 35 & 22.5 & 66 \\ 25-30 & 20 & 27.5 & 86 \\ 30-35 & 8 & 32.5 & 94 \\ 35-40 & 6 & 37.5 & 100 \\ \hline Total & 100 & & \\ \hline \end{tabular}\)

(i) Median:

Median position = \(N/2 = 100/2 = 50\)

The median class is 20-25 (since 50 falls within the cumulative frequency of 66).

Median = \(L + \frac{(N/2 - cf_b)}{f_m} \times w\)

Where L = lower class boundary of median class = 20

\(cf_b\) = cumulative frequency before median class = 31

\(f_m\) = frequency of median class = 35

w = class width = 5

Median = \(20 + \frac{(50 - 31)}{35} \times 5 = 20 + \frac{19}{35} \times 5 = 20 + \frac{95}{35} = 20 + 2.714 = 22.714\)

Median \(\approx 22.71\)

(ii) Standard Deviation:

Calculate \(fx\) and \(fx^2\):

\(\begin{tabular}{|l|c|c|c|c|} \hline Overtime hours & f & x & fx & fx^2 \\ \hline 10-15 & 11 & 12.5 & 137.5 & 1718.75 \\ 15-20 & 20 & 17.5 & 350.0 & 6125.00 \\ 20-25 & 35 & 22.5 & 787.5 & 17718.75 \\ 25-30 & 20 & 27.5 & 550.0 & 15125.00 \\ 30-35 & 8 & 32.5 & 260.0 & 8450.00 \\ 35-40 & 6 & 37.5 & 225.0 & 8437.50 \\ \hline Total & 100 & & 2310.0 & 57575.00 \\ \hline \end{tabular}\)

Mean (\(\bar{x}\)) = \(\frac{\sum fx}{N} = \frac{2310}{100} = 23.1\)

Standard Deviation (\(\sigma\)) = \(\sqrt{\frac{\sum fx^2}{N} - (\bar{x})^2}\)

\(\sigma = \sqrt{\frac{57575}{100} - (23.1)^2} = \sqrt{575.75 - 533.61} = \sqrt{42.14} \approx 6.49\)

Standard Deviation \(\approx 6.49\)

(i) 2 men and 2 women should be chosen;

(ii) The probability that the committee consists of 2 men and 2 women.

(ii) Total number of ways to choose 4 members from 7 staff: \(\binom{7}{4} = \frac{7!}{4!3!} = 35\)

Probability = \(\frac{\text{Number of ways to choose 2 men and 2 women}}{\text{Total number of ways to choose 4 members}} = \frac{18}{35}\)

(b) Venn diagram representations:

\(X \cap U = \{11, 12, 13, 14\}\)

\(\left[\begin{array}{ccc}3 & -1 & 2 \\ -3 & 1 & 5 \\ 0 & 3 & 7\end{array}\right] \left[\begin{array}{ccc}8 / 63 & -13 / 63 & 1 / 9 \\ -1 / 3 & -1 / 3 & 1 / 3 \\ 1 / 7 & 1 / 7 & 0\end{array}\right]\)

Calculating the product (details omitted for brevity) results in the identity matrix \(\left[\begin{array}{ccc}1 & 0 & 0 \\ 0 & 1 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & 1\end{array}\right]\).

Therefore, the given matrix is indeed the inverse of L.
