Question 1 (4 marks)

State three properties of a binomial probability distribution.

Answer

Properties of binomial probability distribution:

  1. Fixed number of trials (n)
  2. Each trial has only two possible outcomes (success/failure)
  3. The probability of success (p) remains constant for all trials
  4. The trials are independent of each other

Question 2 (4 marks)

A farmer intends to plant potatoes on a 40 acre piece of land, maize on a 25 acre piece and wheat on a 30 acre piece. The cost of seeds for an acre of potatoes, maize and wheat is Ksh 400, Ksh 300 and Ksh 500 respectively.

(i) Using a row vector and column vector for acreage and cost respectively, represent this information.

(ii) Determine the total cost of seeds for the three pieces of land.

Answer

(i) Vector representation:

Acreage (row vector): [40 25 30]

Cost (column vector): \[\begin{bmatrix}400 \\ 300 \\ 500\end{bmatrix}\]

(ii) Total cost calculation:

Using matrix multiplication:

[40 25 30] \[\begin{bmatrix}400 \\ 300 \\ 500\end{bmatrix}\]

= (40 × 400) + (25 × 300) + (30 × 500)

= 16,000 + 7,500 + 15,000

= Ksh 38,500

Question 14 (15 marks)

(a) Explain each of the terms as used in statistics:

(i) mutually exclusive events;

(ii) independent events.

(b) Convert each of the following numbers to their respective equivalents:

(i) 253₈ to hexadecimal;

(ii) 1808₁₀ to octal.

(c) Given the following matrices: \[X=\begin{bmatrix}3 & -2 \\ 4 & 1\end{bmatrix}\] \[Y=\begin{bmatrix}1 & 5 \\ 0 & -7\end{bmatrix}\]

Show that XY ≠ YX

(d) A line passes through the points (6,8) and (12,4). Determine the equation of the line.

Answer

(a) Statistical terms:

(i) Mutually exclusive events:

  • Events that cannot occur simultaneously
  • P(A ∩ B) = 0
  • If one occurs, the other cannot

(ii) Independent events:

  • Occurrence of one does not affect probability of other
  • P(A ∩ B) = P(A) × P(B)
  • Events have no influence on each other

(b) Number conversions:

(i) Converting 253₈ to hexadecimal:

  1. Convert to binary:
    • 2₈ = 010₂
    • 5₈ = 101₂
    • 3₈ = 011₂
    • Combined: 010101011₂
  2. Group into fours: 0101 0101 1₂
  3. Convert to hex: 55₁₆

(ii) Converting 1808₁₀ to octal:

  1. Divide by 8 repeatedly:
    • 1808 ÷ 8 = 226 remainder 0
    • 226 ÷ 8 = 28 remainder 2
    • 28 ÷ 8 = 3 remainder 4
    • 3 ÷ 8 = 0 remainder 3
  2. Read remainders bottom-up: 3420₈

(c) Matrix multiplication:

XY = \[\begin{bmatrix}3 & -2 \\ 4 & 1\end{bmatrix}\begin{bmatrix}1 & 5 \\ 0 & -7\end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix}3 & 15 \\ 4 & 13\end{bmatrix}\]

YX = \[\begin{bmatrix}1 & 5 \\ 0 & -7\end{bmatrix}\begin{bmatrix}3 & -2 \\ 4 & 1\end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix}23 & 3 \\ -28 & -7\end{bmatrix}\]

Since results are different, XY ≠ YX

(d) Line equation:

  1. Using point-slope form:
    • slope = (y₂-y₁)/(x₂-x₁)
    • m = (4-8)/(12-6) = -4/6 = -2/3
  2. y - y₁ = m(x - x₁)
  3. y - 8 = (-2/3)(x - 6)
  4. y = -2/3x + 12

Question 15 (15 marks)

(a) State the binomial theorem using mathematical notation.

(b) Using a Venn diagram identify each of the following set operations:

(i) X ∩ Y;

(ii) X ∪ Y;

(iii) Xᶜ.

(c) Kipande Company Ltd manufactures bulbs in two regions, Western and Eastern. The Eastern region manufactures 60% of the bulbs of which 4% are defective and 5% Western region bulbs are defective.

(i) Using a tree diagram, represent this information.

(ii) Determine the probability that a bulb chosen at random is defective.

Answer

(a) Binomial Theorem:

(x + y)ⁿ = \[\sum_{r=0}^n \binom{n}{r} x^{n-r} y^r\]

where \[\binom{n}{r} = \frac{n!}{r!(n-r)!}\]

(b) Set Operations:

(i) X ∩ Y: Intersection - elements common to both sets

(ii) X ∪ Y: Union - elements in either set

(iii) Xᶜ: Complement - elements not in set X

(c) Probability solution:

(i) Tree diagram shows:

  • Eastern Region (0.60)
    • Defective (0.04)
    • Non-defective (0.96)
  • Western Region (0.40)
    • Defective (0.05)
    • Non-defective (0.95)

(ii) Total probability of defective bulb:

P(defective) = (0.60 × 0.04) + (0.40 × 0.05)

= 0.024 + 0.020

= 0.044 or 4.4%

(c) Given n(U)=169, n(A)=81, and n(B)=66, determine n(A ∩ B) if n(A ∪ B)=137.

Answer

(a) Matrix terms:

Example: \[I=\begin{bmatrix}1 & 0 \\ 0 & 1\end{bmatrix}\]

(ii) Singular matrix: A square matrix whose determinant equals zero. Has no inverse.

(iii) Orthogonal matrix: A square matrix whose transpose equals its inverse.

  • Accompaniments: 4

137 = 81 + 66 - n(A ∩ B)

137 = 147 - n(A ∩ B)

n(A ∩ B) = 10

Question 12 (15 marks)

(a) Convert the number 46₁₀ to its excess-3 code equivalent.

(b) Construct a graph to represent the linear inequality 2x + y ≤ 6

(c) Explain each of the following measures as used in statistics:

  1. arithmetic mean
  2. standard deviation
  3. quartiles

(d) Given that function g(x)=x², determine each of the following:

  1. g(2x)-1
  2. g(-5)

Answer

(a) Converting to excess-3:

  1. Convert each digit to BCD:
    • 4: 0100
    • 6: 0110
  2. Add 3 to each digit:
    • 4+3=7: 0111
    • 6+3=9: 1001

Therefore, 46₁₀ = 0111 1001 (excess-3)

(b) Linear inequality:

  1. Plot points: (0,6) and (3,0)
  2. Draw solid line
  3. Shade region below line where 2x + y ≤ 6

(c) Statistical measures:

  1. Arithmetic mean:
    • Sum of all values divided by count
    • Formula: \[\bar{x}=\frac{\sum x_i}{n}\]
    • Represents central tendency
  2. Standard deviation:
    • Measure of spread/dispersion
    • Square root of variance
    • Formula: \[\sigma=\sqrt{\frac{\sum(x-\bar{x})^2}{n}}\]
  3. Quartiles:
    • Divide data into four equal parts
    • Q1 (25th percentile)
    • Q2 (median, 50th percentile)
    • Q3 (75th percentile)

(d) Function evaluation:

(i) g(2x)-1 = (2x)² - 1 = 4x² - 1

(ii) g(-5) = (-5)² = 25

Question 6 (4 marks)

With the aid of a sketch, explain the term kurtosis as used in measures of dispersion.

Answer

Kurtosis measures the "peakedness" of a frequency distribution. There are three types:

  1. Mesokurtic (Normal Distribution)
    • Standard bell-shaped curve
    • Kurtosis coefficient = 3
    • Used as reference for other distributions
  2. Leptokurtic
    • More peaked than normal
    • Kurtosis coefficient > 3
    • Higher, sharper peak
    • Heavy tails
  3. Platykurtic
    • Flatter than normal
    • Kurtosis coefficient < 3
    • Lower, broader peak
    • Light tails

Question 7 (4 marks)

Differentiate between discrete and continuous data types as used in statistics.

Answer

Discrete Data:

  • Can only take specific, separated values
  • Usually counted in whole numbers
  • No values possible between two consecutive points
  • Examples: Number of students, number of cars

Continuous Data:

  • Can take any value within a range
  • Can be measured to any degree of precision
  • Infinite number of possible values between any two points
  • Examples: Height, weight, time

Question 4 (4 marks)

Using matrix method, solve the following set of simultaneous equations:

\[\begin{aligned} 5x - 2y &= 2 \\ 4x + y &= 4 \end{aligned}\]

Answer

Matrix Solution:

Writing in matrix form:

\[\begin{bmatrix}5 & -2 \\ 4 & 1\end{bmatrix}\begin{bmatrix}x \\ y\end{bmatrix}=\begin{bmatrix}2 \\ 4\end{bmatrix}\]

Using inverse matrix method:

  1. Find determinant:
    • |A| = 5(1) - (-2)(4) = 13
  2. Find inverse:
    • \[A^{-1}=\frac{1}{13}\begin{bmatrix}1 & 2 \\ -4 & 5\end{bmatrix}\]
  3. Multiply by solution vector:

    \[\begin{bmatrix}x \\ y\end{bmatrix}=\frac{1}{13}\begin{bmatrix}1 & 2 \\ -4 & 5\end{bmatrix}\begin{bmatrix}2 \\ 4\end{bmatrix}\]

Therefore: x = 1, y = 0

Question 5 (4 marks)

(a) Outline two properties of arithmetic mean using words.

(b) A mathematics teacher intends to make a multiple-choice test. She wants to give each student the same questions, but have each student's questions appear in a different order. If there are twenty-seven students in the class, determine the least number of questions the quiz could contain.

Answer

(a) Properties of arithmetic mean:

  1. Sum of deviations from the mean is zero
  2. Sum of squared deviations from the mean is minimum
  3. Mean is affected by extreme values (outliers)
  4. Mean lies between the minimum and maximum values

(b) Multiple-choice test:

Using permutations concept:

  • Number of students = 27
  • Need number of questions (n) where n! ≥ 27
  • Check factorials:
    • 3! = 6 (too small)
    • 4! = 24 (too small)
    • 5! = 120 (sufficient)

Therefore, minimum number of questions needed = 5
