Question 3

Solve the following simultaneous equation using matrices:

$$ \begin{array}{r} 8 x+6 y=36 \\ 10 x-4 y=22 \end{array} $$

Answer

The matrix equation is:

$$ \begin{bmatrix} 8 & 6 \\ 10 & -4 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} x \\ y \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 36 \\ 22 \end{bmatrix} $$

Let $A = \begin{bmatrix} 8 & 6 \\ 10 & -4 \end{bmatrix}$, $X = \begin{bmatrix} x \\ y \end{bmatrix}$, and $B = \begin{bmatrix} 36 \\ 22 \end{bmatrix}$

Then $AX = B$, so $X = A^{-1}B$

$A^{-1} = \frac{1}{(8 \times -4) - (6 \times 10)} \begin{bmatrix} -4 & -6 \\ -10 & 8 \end{bmatrix} = \frac{1}{-32 - 60} \begin{bmatrix} -4 & -6 \\ -10 & 8 \end{bmatrix} = \frac{1}{-92} \begin{bmatrix} -4 & -6 \\ -10 & 8 \end{bmatrix}$

$X = \frac{1}{-92} \begin{bmatrix} -4 & -6 \\ -10 & 8 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} 36 \\ 22 \end{bmatrix} = \frac{1}{-92} \begin{bmatrix} (-4 \times 36) + (-6 \times 22) \\ (-10 \times 36) + (8 \times 22) \end{bmatrix} = \frac{1}{-92} \begin{bmatrix} -144 - 132 \\ -360 + 176 \end{bmatrix} = \frac{1}{-92} \begin{bmatrix} -276 \\ -184 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 3 \\ 2 \end{bmatrix}$

Therefore, $x = 3$ and $y = 2$

Question 4

(a) Failure of students in an exam ( $x$ ) is represented by a binomial distribution $x \sim \mathrm{~B}(40,0.3)$. Determine the probability that exactly 15 students will fail.

(b) Outline two advantages of secondary data.

Answer

(a) Using the binomial distribution formula: $P(X=k) = {n \choose k} * p^k * (1-p)^{(n-k)}$

Where $n = 40$, $k = 15$, and $p = 0.3$

$P(X=15) = {40 \choose 15} * (0.3)^{15} * (0.7)^{25}$

$P(X=15) = 1.268 * 10^{11} * 1.434 * 10^{-8} * 1.215 * 10^{-3}$

$P(X=15) = 0.220$

(b) Two advantages of secondary data are:

- It is readily available.

- It is less expensive to obtain than primary data.

Question 5

Solve for $n$ in each of the following equations:

(a) ${ }^{n} P_{2}=110$

(b) ${ }^{n} P_{4}=30 \times{ }^{n} P_{2}$

Answer

(a) ${ }^{n} P_{2}=110$

$\frac{n!}{(n-2)!} = 110$

$n(n-1) = 110$

$n^2 - n - 110 = 0$

$(n - 11)(n + 10) = 0$

$n = 11$ or $n = -10$ (invalid since n must be positive)

Therefore, $n = 11$

(b) ${ }^{n} P_{4}=30 \times{ }^{n} P_{2}$

$\frac{n!}{(n-4)!} = 30 \times \frac{n!}{(n-2)!}$

$\frac{1}{(n-4)!} = 30 \times \frac{1}{(n-2)!}$

$(n-2)! = 30(n-4)!$

$(n-2)(n-3)(n-4)! = 30(n-4)!$

$(n-2)(n-3) = 30$

$n^2 - 5n + 6 = 30$

$n^2 - 5n - 24 = 0$

$(n - 8)(n + 3) = 0$

$n = 8$ or $n = -3$ (invalid since n must be positive)

Therefore, $n = 8$

Question 6

(a) The ages of 6 students in a class are:

$$17,15,18,21,14,19$$

Determine the median age.

(b) Given the following matrix:

$$B=\left(\begin{array}{ll} 3 & 4 \\ 0 & 2 \end{array}\right)$$

Determine $B^{-1}$.

Answer

(a) To find the median age, first sort the ages:

$$14, 15, 17, 18, 19, 21$$

Since there are 6 students (an even number), the median is the average of the middle two ages:

Median = $\frac{17 + 18}{2} = \frac{35}{2} = 17.5$

(b) To find the inverse of matrix B:

$B=\left(\begin{array}{ll} 3 & 4 \\ 0 & 2 \end{array}\right)$

det(B) = (3 * 2) - (4 * 0) = 6

$B^{-1} = \frac{1}{6} \left(\begin{array}{ll} 2 & -4 \\ 0 & 3 \end{array}\right) = \left(\begin{array}{ll} 1/3 & -2/3 \\ 0 & 1/2 \end{array}\right)$

Question 7

(b) Solve the following inequality:

$$\cdot \frac{5 x+5}{-10} \leq 2 x-1$$

Question 8

In a class of 100 students, 45 study history, 53 study English and 15 study both subjects.

Using a Venn diagram determine the number of students who study neither history nor English.

Answer

Let H be the set of students who study history, and E be the set of students who study English.

n(H) = 45

n(E) = 53

n(H ∩ E) = 15

n(H ∪ E) = n(H) + n(E) - n(H ∩ E) = 45 + 53 - 15 = 83

Number of students who study neither history nor English = Total students - n(H ∪ E) = 100 - 83 = 17

determine $\left(A^{T}\right)^{-1}$

Question 10

One tenth of items in a production line are defective. In a random sample of 20 items, determine the probability that:

(a) exactly 3 items are defective;

(b) at least 1 item is defective.

Answer

Let $p = 0.1$ (probability of a defective item)

$q = 1 - p = 0.9$ (probability of a non-defective item)

$n = 20$ (sample size)

(a) Probability of exactly 3 defective items:

$P(X=3) = {20 \choose 3} * (0.1)^3 * (0.9)^{17} = 1140 * 0.001 * 0.1667 = 0.1899 \approx 0.190$

(b) Probability of at least 1 defective item:

$P(X \geq 1) = 1 - P(X=0) = 1 - {20 \choose 0} * (0.1)^0 * (0.9)^{20} = 1 - 1 * 1 * 0.1216 = 1 - 0.1216 = 0.8784$

Question 11

(a) In a particular cyber cafe, the probability of one of the computers failing to operate is 0.15 . If 5 computers are selected at random, determine the probability that:

(i) 2 computers will fail to operate;

(ii) less than 3 computers will fail to operate;

(iii) 4 computers will operate;

(iv) at least 1 of the computers will fail to operate.

(b) Explain the meaning of each of the following terms as used in set theory:

(i) universal set;

(ii) subset;

(iii) empty.

Answer

(a) Let p = 0.15 (probability of a computer failing)

q = 1 - p = 0.85 (probability of a computer operating)

n = 5 (number of computers)

(i) P(X=2) = {5 \choose 2} * (0.15)^2 * (0.85)^3 = 10 * 0.0225 * 0.614125 = 0.138

(ii) P(X < 3) = P(X=0) + P(X=1) + P(X=2)

P(X=0) = {5 \choose 0} * (0.15)^0 * (0.85)^5 = 0.4437

P(X=1) = {5 \choose 1} * (0.15)^1 * (0.85)^4 = 5 * 0.15 * 0.522 = 0.3915

P(X < 3) = 0.4437 + 0.3915 + 0.138 = 0.9732

(iii) P(4 computers operate) = P(X=1) = 0.3915

(iv) P(at least 1 fails) = 1 - P(X=0) = 1 - 0.4437 = 0.5563

(b) (i) Universal set: A set that contains all the elements under consideration.

(ii) Subset: A set whose elements are all contained in another set.

(iii) Empty set: A set that contains no elements.

Question 13

(a) A survey of 210 job applicants was carried out to determine whether they were competent in three foreign languages: French, Spanish and Japanese. The following were the results of the survey:

- 10 were competent in all the three languages;

- 18 were competent in both Japanese and French;

- 22 were competent in both Japanese and Spanish;

- 48 were competent in both Spanish and French;

- 104 were competent in French;

- 126 were competent in Spanish;

- 50 were competent in Japanese.

(i) Present the information above in a Venn diagram.

(ii) Determine the number of applicants that were competent in:

(I) Spanish but not French;

(II) Japanese but not French;

(III) neither French not Spanish;

(IV) French or Spanish;

(V) both French and Spanish but not Japanese.

(b) Explain three properties of a normal distribution curve.

Answer

(a) (i) (A Venn diagram would be drawn with three overlapping circles representing French, Spanish, and Japanese. The numbers would be filled in based on the given information.)

(ii) (I) Spanish but not French: 126 - (48 - 10) - (22 - 10) - 10 = 126 - 38 - 12 - 10 = 66

(II) Japanese but not French: 50 - (18 - 10) - (22 - 10) - 10 = 50 - 8 - 12 - 10 = 20

(III) neither French nor Spanish: 210 - (104 + 126 - 48) = 210 - 182 = 28

(IV) French or Spanish: 104 + 126 - 48 = 182

(V) both French and Spanish but not Japanese: 48 - 10 = 38

(b) Three properties of a normal distribution curve:

- It is bell-shaped and symmetrical about the mean.

- The mean, median, and mode are equal and located at the center.

- The total area under the curve is equal to 1.

Question 14

(a) The daily wages of factory employees is normally distributed with a mean of Ksh 600 and a standard deviation of Ksh 100. Determine the probability that an employee selected at random has a daily wage of:

(i) above Ksh 800;

(ii) below Ksh 700;

(iii) between Ksh 500 and Ksh 650;

(iv) below Ksh 500.

(b) (i) Convert +52 to binary using 8 bits machine.

(ii) Determine the I's compliment of -7 .

(iii) (I) Add +9 and +4 for 5 bits machine.

(II) Determine the equivalent of the results in (I) above.

Answer

(a) Using z-scores:

(i) For Ksh 800:

z = (800 - 600)/100 = 2

P(X > 800) = P(Z > 2) = 0.0228

(ii) For Ksh 700:

z = (700 - 600)/100 = 1

P(X < 700) = P(Z < 1) = 0.8413

(iii) For Ksh 500 and 650:

z₁ = (500 - 600)/100 = -1

z₂ = (650 - 600)/100 = 0.5

P(500 < X < 650) = P(-1 < Z < 0.5) = 0.6915 - 0.1587 = 0.5328

(iv) For Ksh 500:

z = (500 - 600)/100 = -1

P(X < 500) = P(Z < -1) = 0.1587

(b) (i) +52 to binary:

52 = 32 + 16 + 4 = 00110100 (8 bits)

(ii) -7 to 1's complement:

7 in binary: 00000111

1's complement: 11111000

(iii) (I) Adding in binary:

9 in binary: 01001

4 in binary: 00100

Sum: 01101

(II) 01101 = 1×2⁴ + 1×2³ + 0×2² + 1×2¹ + 1×2⁰ = 16 + 8 + 0 + 2 + 1 = 13

Question 15

(a) A shop sold three types of products; $\mathrm{A}, \mathrm{B}$ and C on a certain day as follows:

2 units of A, 3 units of B and one unit of C for Ksh 490.

.3 units of A, 4 units of B and 2 units of C for Ksh 700.

1 unit of A, 2 units of B and 1 unit of C for Ksh 330 .

(i) Formulate simultaneous equations to represent the information above.

(ii) Determine the:

(I) price per unit of each of the three products;

(II) total amount to be paid for 4 units of A and 2 units of B .

(b) Explain three challenges that an organization may encounter from the use of computers in its operations.

Answer

(a) (i) Let A, B, and C be the prices per unit of each product.

The simultaneous equations are:

2A + 3B + C = 490 ... (1)

3A + 4B + 2C = 700 ... (2)

A + 2B + C = 330 ... (3)

(ii) (I) Solving the equations:

From (3): A = 330 - 2B - C ... (4)

Substituting (4) in (1):

2(330 - 2B - C) + 3B + C = 490

660 - 4B - 2C + 3B + C = 490

660 - B - C = 490

-B - C = -170 ... (5)

Substituting (4) in (2):

3(330 - 2B - C) + 4B + 2C = 700

990 - 6B - 3C + 4B + 2C = 700

990 - 2B - C = 700

-2B - C = -290 ... (6)

From (5) and (6):

B = 120

C = 50

From (4):

A = 330 - 2(120) - 50 = 40

Therefore: A = Ksh 40, B = Ksh 120, C = Ksh 50

(II) Total amount for 4 units of A and 2 units of B:

= 4(40) + 2(120)

= 160 + 240

= Ksh 400

(b) Three challenges that an organization may encounter from using computers:

1. High initial cost - Organizations need substantial investment for:

- Hardware and software purchases

- Infrastructure setup

- Maintenance expenses

2. Need for skilled personnel:

- Training costs for existing staff

- Hiring specialized IT professionals

- Ongoing technical support requirements

3. Security and reliability risks:

- Vulnerability to cyber attacks

- Risk of data loss or corruption

- System downtime affecting operations

- Need for backup systems and security measures
