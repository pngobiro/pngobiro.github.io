Question 10

Using the binomial expansion, determine the first three terms in the expansion of (1-2y) ${ }^{7}$ in ascending powers of \(y\).

Answer

Using the binomial theorem: \((a+b)^n = \sum_{r=0}^n \binom{n}{r} a^{n-r} b^r\)

Here, a = 1, b = -2y, and n = 7

  1. r = 0: \(\binom{7}{0}(1)^7(-2y)^0 = 1\)
  2. r = 1: \(\binom{7}{1}(1)^6(-2y)^1 = -14y\)
  3. r = 2: \(\binom{7}{2}(1)^5(-2y)^2 = 84y^2\)

Therefore, the first three terms are: 1 - 14y + 84y²

Question 11

(a) (i) Define the term random experiment as used in probability theory.

(ii) Outline three properties of a random experiment.

(b) The following information relates to the qualifications of employees of AZECT company. Sam, John, Willy and Ben have diploma certificates with Sam and Ben also having degrees. Sam, Melvin, Willy, Tom, Morris and Ken are members of the ICTAK certifying body, with Tom and Morris having diploma certificates. Assume set 'A' to be employees' with diploma certificates, set 'B' employees who are ICTAK certified and set ' C ' degree holders'.

(i) Identify the elements of sets A, B and C;

(ii) Draw a Venn diagram to represent the sets in (i) showing their respective elements.

(c) Using the elimination method, solve the following simultaneous equations.

\[\begin{aligned} & 7 x-4 y=37 \\ & 6 x+3 y=51 \end{aligned}\]

Answer

(a) (i) Random Experiment:

  • A process or activity with well-defined possible outcomes
  • The outcome is uncertain before the experiment is conducted
  • Example: Tossing a coin, rolling a die

(ii) Properties of a random experiment:

  1. Well-defined sample space: All possible outcomes are known
  2. Uncertainty: The exact outcome is not known in advance
  3. Repeatability: The experiment can be repeated under similar conditions

(b) AZECT Company Employees:

(i) Elements of sets:

  • A (Diploma certificates): {Sam, John, Willy, Ben, Tom, Morris}
  • B (ICTAK certified): {Sam, Melvin, Willy, Tom, Morris, Ken}
  • C (Degree holders): {Sam, Ben}

(ii) Venn Diagram:

  • Draw three overlapping circles representing A, B, and C
  • Place elements in appropriate regions based on their qualifications
  • Example: Sam is in A ∩ B ∩ C

(c) Solving Simultaneous Equations:

Multiply first equation by 3 and second equation by 4:

\[\begin{aligned} & 21x - 12y = 111 \\ & 24x + 12y = 204 \end{aligned}\]

Add the two equations:

45x = 315

Solve for x: x = 7

Substitute x = 7 into first equation: 7(7) - 4y = 37

49 - 4y = 37

-4y = -12

Solve for y: y = 3

Therefore, x = 7 and y = 3

Question 12

(a) Differentiate between the terms qualitative data and quantitative data collection methods.

(b) Given two matrices \(\mathrm{P}=\left[\begin{array}{rrr}3 & -1 & 2 \\ 1 & 0 & 3 \\ 3 & -2 & -5\end{bmatrix}\) and \(\mathrm{Q}=\left[\begin{array}{rrr}3 & -6 & -3 \\ 7 & -14 & -7 \\ -1 & 2 & 1\end{array}\right]\).

Show that;

(i) PQ is a null matrix;

(ii) QP is not a null matrix.

(c) Table 1 shows the salary distribution in Kenya shillings of 340 employees of a certain firm. Use it to answer the questions that follow.

Salary 3001-4000 4001-5000 5001-6000 6001-7000 7001-8000 8001-9000 9001-10000
No of Employees 30 46 58 76 60 80 20

Estimate each of the following measures about the salary of the employees:

(i) the mean;

(ii) the standard deviation.

Answer

(a) Qualitative vs Quantitative Data:

  • Qualitative data:
    • Descriptive data that cannot be easily measured numerically
    • Examples: colors, textures, opinions
    • Data collection methods: interviews, focus groups
  • Quantitative data:
    • Numerical data that can be measured and analyzed statistically
    • Examples: age, height, salary
    • Data collection methods: surveys, experiments

(b) Matrix Operations:

(i) PQ is a null matrix:

\[PQ = \begin{bmatrix}3 & -1 & 2 \\ 1 & 0 & 3 \\ 3 & -2 & -5\end{bmatrix}\begin{bmatrix}3 & -6 & -3 \\ 7 & -14 & -7 \\ -1 & 2 & 1\end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix}0 & 0 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & 0\end{bmatrix}\]

(ii) QP is not a null matrix:

\[QP = \begin{bmatrix}3 & -6 & -3 \\ 7 & -14 & -7 \\ -1 & 2 & 1\end{bmatrix}\begin{bmatrix}3 & -1 & 2 \\ 1 & 0 & 3 \\ 3 & -2 & -5\end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix}-3 & 3 & -3 \\ -7 & 7 & -7 \\ 1 & -1 & 1\end{bmatrix}\]

(c) Salary Distribution Analysis:

(i) Mean salary:

  • Calculate midpoints of each salary range
  • Multiply midpoint by frequency for each range
  • Sum the results and divide by total number of employees (340)
  • Mean ≈ Ksh 6648.53

(ii) Standard deviation:

  • Calculate the variance using the formula: \(\sigma^2 = \frac{\sum f(x - \mu)^2}{N}\)
  • Take the square root of the variance to get the standard deviation
  • Standard deviation ≈ Ksh 1841.75

Question 13

(a) Describe each of the following number systems as used in computer systems.

(i) Hexadecimal number system;

(ii) Octal number system.

Answer

(a) Number Systems:

(i) Hexadecimal Number System:

  • Base-16 number system
  • Uses digits 0-9 and letters A-F to represent values 10-15
  • Commonly used in computing for memory addresses and color codes
  • Example: 0x2A, 0xFF

(ii) Octal Number System:

  • Base-8 number system
  • Uses digits 0-7
  • Less common than binary, decimal, or hexadecimal
  • Example: 377₈

Question 15

(a) Write the following abbreviations in full as used in computer coding.

(i) ASCII;

(ii) EBCDIC.

(b) State two characteristics of the binomial distribution.

(c) Table 1 shows the age distribution of 30,100 policy holders in a certain insurance company. Use it to answer the questions that follow.

Age 10-19 20-29 30-39 40-49 50-59 60-69 70-79 80-89 Over 90
Frequency 3016 6894 9229 5714 3575 1492 170 9 1

Determine each of the following measures about the age distribution;

(i) median;

(iii) quartile deviation.

(d) Given that matrix \(\mathrm{A}=\left[\begin{array}{ccc}5 & 2 & 1 \\ 0 & -1 & -1 \\ 10 & 3 & 0\end{bmatrix}\), determine \(\mathrm{A}^{-1}\) using the cofactor method.

Answer

(a) Abbreviations:

(i) ASCII: American Standard Code for Information Interchange

(ii) EBCDIC: Extended Binary Coded Decimal Interchange Code

(b) Characteristics of Binomial Distribution:

  • Fixed number of trials (n)
  • Each trial has only two possible outcomes (success or failure)
  • Probability of success (p) is constant for each trial
  • Trials are independent

(c) Age Distribution Analysis:

(i) Median:

  • Total frequency = 30100
  • Median position = (30100 + 1) / 2 = 15050.5
  • Median class is 30-39
  • Median = L + [ (N/2 - cf) / f ] * w = 30 + [(15050.5 - 9900) / 9229] * 10 = 35.57

(iii) Quartile Deviation:

  • Q1 position = (30100 + 1) / 4 = 7525.25, Q1 class is 20-29, Q1 = 20 + [(7525.25 - 3016) / 6894] * 10 = 26.54
  • Q3 position = 3(30100 + 1) / 4 = 22575.75, Q3 class is 40-49, Q3 = 40 + [(22575.75 - 18847) / 5714] * 10 = 46.54
  • Quartile Deviation = (Q3 - Q1) / 2 = (46.54 - 26.54) / 2 = 10

(d) Matrix Inverse using Cofactor Method:

\[A=\begin{bmatrix}5 & 2 & 1 \\ 0 & -1 & -1 \\ 10 & 3 & 0\end{bmatrix}\]

Cofactor matrix: \[\begin{bmatrix}3 & -10 & 10 \\ 3 & -10 & -5 \\ -1 & 5 & -5\end{bmatrix}\]

Adjugate matrix (transpose of cofactor matrix): \[\begin{bmatrix}3 & 3 & -1 \\ -10 & -10 & 5 \\ 10 & -5 & -5\end{bmatrix}\]

Determinant of A: 5(0+3) - 2(0+10) + 1(0+10) = 15 - 20 + 10 = 5

\[A^{-1} = \frac{1}{5}\begin{bmatrix}3 & 3 & -1 \\ -10 & -10 & 5 \\ 10 & -5 & -5\end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix}0.6 & 0.6 & -0.2 \\ -2 & -2 & 1 \\ 2 & -1 & -1\end{bmatrix}\]
