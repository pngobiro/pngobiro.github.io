Question 1 (4 marks)

Differentiate between primary data and secondary data as used in statistics.

Answer

Primary Data:

  • Data collected directly by the researcher for a specific purpose
  • First-hand information gathered through surveys, interviews, experiments, or direct observations

Secondary Data:

  • Data that has been previously collected by someone else for some other purpose
  • Information obtained from existing sources like books, journals, government records, or previously conducted research

Question 4 (4 marks)

Explain each of the following terms as used in statistics:

(a) arithmetic mean;

(b) harmonic mean.

Answer

(a) Arithmetic Mean:

  • The sum of all values in a dataset divided by the number of values
  • Represented by the formula: \[\bar{x} = \frac{\sum_{i=1}^{n} x_i}{n}\]
  • Most commonly used measure of central tendency

(b) Harmonic Mean:

  • The reciprocal of the arithmetic mean of reciprocals of values in a dataset
  • Represented by the formula: \[H = \frac{n}{\sum_{i=1}^{n} \frac{1}{x_i}}\]
  • Particularly useful for averaging rates and speeds

Question 5 (4 marks)

Explain two properties of standard deviation as a measure of dispersion.

Answer

Properties of Standard Deviation:

  1. Non-negative value:
    • Standard deviation is always zero or positive
    • It equals zero only when all values in the dataset are identical
  2. Units of measurement:
    • Standard deviation is expressed in the same units as the original data
    • This makes it directly interpretable in context of the data
  3. Sensitivity to outliers:
    • More sensitive to extreme values than other measures of dispersion
    • Squares deviations from the mean, giving more weight to larger differences
  4. Independence of sample size:
    • Formula accounts for sample size through division
    • Allows comparison between datasets of different sizes

Question 6 (4 marks)

Describe each of the following computer coding systems:

(a) American Standard Code for Information Interchange;

(b) Binary Coded Decimal.

Answer

(a) American Standard Code for Information Interchange (ASCII):

  • A character encoding standard for electronic communication
  • Uses 7 or 8 bits to represent each character
  • Includes 128 characters in standard ASCII (0-127):
    • Control characters (0-31)
    • Printable characters (32-127) including letters, numbers, and symbols
  • Extended ASCII uses 8 bits allowing for 256 characters

(b) Binary Coded Decimal (BCD):

  • A system where each decimal digit is represented by its own 4-bit binary code
  • Each decimal digit (0-9) is encoded separately:
    • 0 = 0000
    • 1 = 0001
    • ...
    • 9 = 1001
  • Codes 1010 to 1111 are not used
  • Useful in financial calculations where exact decimal representation is required

Question 7 (4 marks)

(a) State the binomial theorem as used in mathematics.

(b) Expand the following expression using the binomial theorem:

\((x+2y)^4\)

Answer

(a) Binomial Theorem:

For any positive integer n, the expansion of \((x+y)^n\) is given by:

\[(x+y)^n = \sum_{r=0}^n \binom{n}{r} x^{n-r} y^r\]

where \(\binom{n}{r}\) is the binomial coefficient.

(b) Expansion of \((x+2y)^4\):

Using the formula with x and 2y:

  • \(\binom{4}{0}x^4(2y)^0 = x^4\)
  • \(\binom{4}{1}x^3(2y)^1 = 4x^3(2y) = 8x^3y\)
  • \(\binom{4}{2}x^2(2y)^2 = 6x^2(4y^2) = 24x^2y^2\)
  • \(\binom{4}{3}x^1(2y)^3 = 4x(8y^3) = 32xy^3\)
  • \(\binom{4}{4}x^0(2y)^4 = 16y^4\)

Therefore:

\[(x+2y)^4 = x^4 + 8x^3y + 24x^2y^2 + 32xy^3 + 16y^4\]

Question 9 (4 marks)

The probability that Angeline does her homework is \(\frac{3}{4}\). The probability that her teacher checks the homework is \(\frac{5}{6}\). Use a probability tree to determine the probability that Angeline does not do her homework and the teacher does not check.

Answer

Solution using probability tree:

Given:

  • P(does homework) = \(\frac{3}{4}\)
  • P(doesn't do homework) = \(\frac{1}{4}\)
  • P(teacher checks) = \(\frac{5}{6}\)
  • P(teacher doesn't check) = \(\frac{1}{6}\)

To find P(doesn't do homework AND teacher doesn't check):

\[P(\text{no homework and no check}) = \frac{1}{4} × \frac{1}{6} = \frac{1}{24}\]

Therefore, the probability is \(\frac{1}{24}\) or approximately 0.042 or 4.2%

Question 11 (15 marks)

(a) (i) Define each of the following terms as used in matrices:

  • Unity Matrix
  • Singular Matrix

(ii) Using One's complement, evaluate the following: 1430-610

(b) Using Cramer's rule, solve the following simultaneous equations:

\[\begin{aligned} x+y+z &= 2 \\ x+2y+z &= 3 \\ x+y+2z &= 1 \end{aligned}\]

(c) John is twice as old as his son Peter. 10 years ago, John was three times as old as Peter. Determine their present ages.

(4 marks)

(d) A line passes through points (4,7) and (-2,1), determine the equation of the line.

(2 marks)

Answer

(a) (i) Matrix Definitions:

  • Unity Matrix: A square matrix with 1's on the main diagonal and 0's elsewhere. Also called identity matrix.
  • Singular Matrix: A square matrix whose determinant is zero, meaning it has no inverse.

(ii) One's complement subtraction:

  1. 1430 in binary: 0101 1000 0110
  2. 610 in binary: 0010 0110 0010
  3. One's complement of 610: 1101 1001 1101
  4. Add: 0101 1000 0110 + 1101 1001 1101 = 0011 0001 1100
  5. Result = 820

(b) Using Cramer's rule:

For system AX = B, x = D₁/D, y = D₂/D, z = D₃/D where:

D = |A| = determinant of coefficient matrix

D₁, D₂, D₃ = determinants with respective column replaced by constants

\[D = \begin{vmatrix} 1 & 1 & 1 \\ 1 & 2 & 1 \\ 1 & 1 & 2 \end{vmatrix} = 2\]

\[D_1 = \begin{vmatrix} 2 & 1 & 1 \\ 3 & 2 & 1 \\ 1 & 1 & 2 \end{vmatrix} = 2\]

Therefore: x = 1, y = 1, z = 0

(c) Ages problem:

Let p = Peter's current age

Then: John's age = 2p

10 years ago: 2p - 10 = 3(p - 10)

2p - 10 = 3p - 30

-p = -20

p = 20

Therefore:

  • Peter's current age = 20 years
  • John's current age = 40 years

(d) Line equation:

Using point-slope form: \(y - y_1 = m(x - x_1)\)

Slope m = \(\frac{7-1}{4-(-2)} = \frac{6}{6} = 1\)

Using point (4,7): y - 7 = 1(x - 4)

Therefore: y = x + 3

(a) (i) Evaluating the expression:

Given: x=3, y=-5, a=-4, b=-7

\[\sqrt{\frac{6(-4)^2}{3}+\frac{2(-7)^2}{-5}}\]

\[\sqrt{\frac{6(16)}{3}+\frac{2(49)}{-5}}\]

\[\sqrt{32-19.6}\]

\[\sqrt{12.4} \approx 3.52\]

(a) (ii) Matrix solution:

Writing in matrix form:

\[\begin{bmatrix}3 & 1 \\ 1 & 2\end{bmatrix}\begin{bmatrix}x \\ y\end{bmatrix}=\begin{bmatrix}3 \\ 7\end{bmatrix}\]

Using inverse matrix method:

\[\begin{bmatrix}x \\ y\end{bmatrix}=\frac{1}{5}\begin{bmatrix}2 & -1 \\ -1 & 3\end{bmatrix}\begin{bmatrix}3 \\ 7\end{bmatrix}\]

AB:

\[\begin{bmatrix}1 & 2 \\ 1 & 3 \\ 1 & 1\end{bmatrix}\begin{bmatrix}1 & 1 & 2 \\ 2 & 0 & 2\end{bmatrix}=\begin{bmatrix}5 & 1 & 6 \\ 7 & 1 & 8 \\ 3 & 1 & 4\end{bmatrix}\]

\[P(\text{all black})=\frac{C(8,3)}{C(12,3)}\]
