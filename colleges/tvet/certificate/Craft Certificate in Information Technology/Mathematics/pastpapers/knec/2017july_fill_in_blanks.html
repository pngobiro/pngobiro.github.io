Question 9

A researcher carried out a survey about car and motorbike ownership on 500 university students. It was found out that 123 students own motorbikes, 91 own cars while 29 own both.

(i) Draw a Venn diagram to represent this information.

(ii) Determine the percentage of students who own a motorbike or a car.

Answer

(i) Venn Diagram:

Draw two overlapping circles, one for motorbike owners (M) and one for car owners (C).

M = 123, C = 91, M ∩ C = 29

M only = 123 - 29 = 94

C only = 91 - 29 = 62

(ii) Percentage of students who own a motorbike or a car:

M ∪ C = M + C - M ∩ C = 123 + 91 - 29 = 185

Percentage = (185 / 500) * 100 = [ANSWER_20]%

(b) To show AB = I, multiply the matrices A and B:

\[\mathbf{A B}=\left[\begin{array}{rrr}7 & -8 & 5 \\ -4 & 5 & -3 \\ 1 & -1 & 1\end{array}\right]\left[\begin{array}{rrr}2 & 2 & -1 \\ 1 & 2 & 1 \\ -1 & -1 & 3\end{array}\right] = \left[\begin{array}{rrr}1 & 0 & 0 \\ 0 & 1 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & 1\end{array}\right] = \mathbf{I}\]
