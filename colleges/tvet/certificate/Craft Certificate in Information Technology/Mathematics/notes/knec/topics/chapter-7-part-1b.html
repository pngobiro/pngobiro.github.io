7.2.1 Types of Multiplication with Unit Vectors

  • Pre-Multiplication with Row Vector.
  • Post-Multiplication with a Column Vector.

Multiplication of a Matrix with a Row Vector

(Pre-Multiplication of a Matrix with a unit vector)

$$ \vec{u} \cdot A = \vec{r} $$

To sum up all the people "Vertically" under the respective category (i.e. from A to F) multiply by a row vector [1 1 1 1].

$$ \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 1 & 1 & 1 \end{bmatrix} \cdot \begin{bmatrix} 0 & 2 & 5 & 16 & 0 & 0 \\ 2 & 12 & 15 & 27 & 5 & 1 \\ 0 & 0 & 24 & 15 & 3 & 2 \\ 0 & 0 & 10 & 9 & 2 & 1 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 2 & 14 & 54 & 67 & 10 & 4 \end{bmatrix} $$

Result: Total staff in each category (A to F) for Andover.

Question: Can the Andover matrix be Pre-Multiply by a column vector?

Answer: No, matrix dimensions (1x4) and (4x6) are compatible for pre-multiplication, but a column vector (e.g., 4x1) cannot pre-multiply a 4x6 matrix.

Multiplication with Column Vector

(Post-multiplication of matrix A with a unit vector.)

To sum up all elements "horizontally" across (i.e., total staff in each grade), the matrix is Post-Multiplied by a column vector.

$$ \begin{bmatrix} 0 & 2 & 5 & 16 & 0 & 0 \\ 2 & 12 & 15 & 27 & 5 & 1 \\ 0 & 0 & 24 & 15 & 3 & 2 \\ 0 & 0 & 10 & 9 & 2 & 1 \end{bmatrix} \cdot \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ 1 \\ 1 \\ 1 \\ 1 \\ 1 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 23 \\ 62 \\ 44 \\ 22 \end{bmatrix} $$

Result: Total staff in each grade (1 to 4) for Andover.

Question: Can you Post-Multiply by a row-vector?

Answer: No, matrix dimensions (4x6) and (e.g., 1x6) are not compatible for post-multiplication.

Calculating the Total Wage Bill

The total wage bill for grade 2 and 3 in category C to E, is obtained by Pre-Multiply by the appropriate row vector and Post-Multiply by the appropriate column vector.

(Note: the one's and zero's in the row and column vector select the desired elements.)

Wage Matrix (W):

$$ W = \begin{bmatrix} 3 & 5 & 8 & 10 & 12 & 15 \\ 4 & 6 & 10 & 12 & 15 & 18 \\ 5 & 8 & 12 & 15 & 18 & 20 \\ 6 & 10 & 15 & 18 & 20 & 24 \end{bmatrix} $$

Total Staff Matrix (T = A+B):

$$ T = \begin{bmatrix} 0 & 5 & 23 & 16 & 0 & 1 \\ 3 & 16 & 27 & 45 & 7 & 2 \\ 0 & 0 & 40 & 29 & 8 & 3 \\ 0 & 0 & 18 & 15 & 3 & 1 \end{bmatrix} $$

To get total wage bill, we need element-wise multiplication (Hadamard product) of T and W, then sum selected elements.

Let TW be the element-wise product T * W.

Select Grades 2 & 3, Categories C, D, E:

$$ \begin{bmatrix} 0 & 1 & 1 & 0 \end{bmatrix} \cdot TW \cdot \begin{bmatrix} 0 \\ 0 \\ 1 \\ 1 \\ 1 \\ 0 \end{bmatrix} $$

Question: Can you understand the meaning of the multiplication above?

Answer: The row vector selects grades 2 and 3. The column vector selects categories C, D, and E. The matrix multiplication sums the wage bill for the selected staff.

Increasing/Decreasing by Matrix Method

To increase the wages by 5%, 6%, 4%, 8% for the respective grade (1, 2, 3, 4), set up the diagonal matrix 'I' below and pre-multiply the Wage matrix W.

$$ I = \begin{bmatrix} 1.05 & 0 & 0 & 0 \\ 0 & 1.06 & 0 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & 1.04 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & 0 & 1.08 \end{bmatrix} $$

New Wage Matrix W_new = I * W

Question: Can you understand the logic of matrix I?

Answer: Matrix I is a diagonal matrix where each diagonal element represents the multiplier (1 + percentage increase) for the corresponding grade's wages.
