üí° 6.1 Introduction

In addition to our 'ordinary' Boolean algebra, De Morgan's Laws is one tool used for simplifying complex expressions in Boolean algebra or complex switching circuits.

üìú 6.2 The Laws of Boolean Algebra

Commutative law

$$ A \cdot B = B \cdot A $$
$$ A + B = B + A $$

Associative law

$$ A \cdot (B \cdot C) = (A \cdot B) \cdot C = A \cdot B \cdot C $$
$$ A + (B + C) = (A + B) + C = A + B + C $$

Distributive law

$$ A \cdot (B + C) = A \cdot B + A \cdot C $$
$$ (A + B) \cdot (A + C) = A + B \cdot C $$

Boolean rules (Identities)

$A \cdot 0 = 0$$A + 0 = A$
$A \cdot 1 = A$$A + 1 = 1$
$A \cdot A = A$$A + A = A$
$A \cdot \overline{A} = 0$$A + \overline{A} = 1$
$\overline{\overline{A}} = A$ (Double Negation)

Properties of absorption

$$ A + (A \cdot B) = A $$
$$ A \cdot (A + B) = A $$
$$ A + (\overline{A} \cdot B) = A + B $$
$$ A \cdot (\overline{A} + B) = A \cdot B $$

‚ú® 6.3 Simplification of Expressions

The rules given may be used to simplify Boolean expression, just as the rules of normal algebra may be used to simplify expression. Consider the expression

$$ (A+B)(A+\overline{B})(\overline{A}+C) $$

The first two terms $(A+B)(A+\overline{B})$ reduce to A (using distributive law $X+YZ = (X+Y)(X+Z)$ in reverse, or by expansion as shown in Ch 5).

The expression becomes $A(\overline{A}+C)$.

Applying distributive law: $A \cdot \overline{A} + A \cdot C$.

Since $A \cdot \overline{A} = 0$, the expression simplifies to $0 + A \cdot C = A \cdot C$.

Another expression that may be simplified is $A B C + A \overline{B} C + A B \overline{C}$.

Factor out A: $A(B C + \overline{B} C + B \overline{C})$.

Factor C from first two terms inside parenthesis: $A(C(B + \overline{B}) + B \overline{C})$.

Since $B + \overline{B} = 1$: $A(C \cdot 1 + B \overline{C}) = A(C + B \overline{C})$.

Using absorption rule $X + \overline{X} Y = X + Y$: $C + B \overline{C} = C + B$.

The expression simplifies to $A(B+C)$ or $A B + A C$.

¬¨ 6.4 De Morgan's Laws

The following two rules constitute De Morgan's Laws:

$$ \overline{A+B} = \overline{A} \cdot \overline{B} $$
$$ \overline{A \cdot B} = \overline{A} + \overline{B} $$

The complement of any Boolean expression, or part of any expression, may be found by means of this law. Using these rules, two steps are used to form a complement:

  1. Change all AND (.) operators to OR (+) and all OR (+) operators to AND (.).
  2. Complement each individual variable (and constant).

Example: Complement of $A+B \cdot C$

$\overline{A+(B \cdot C)} = \overline{A} \cdot \overline{(B \cdot C)} = \overline{A} \cdot (\overline{B} + \overline{C})$

Example: Complement of $A \overline{B} + C \overline{D}$

$\overline{(A \cdot \overline{B}) + (C \cdot \overline{D})} = \overline{(A \cdot \overline{B})} \cdot \overline{(C \cdot \overline{D})} = (\overline{A} + \overline{\overline{B}}) \cdot (\overline{C} + \overline{\overline{D}}) = (\overline{A} + B) \cdot (\overline{C} + D)$

Example: Simplify $\overline{\overline{A}+\overline{B} \cdot C+C \cdot B}$

= $\overline{\overline{A}} \cdot \overline{(\overline{B} \cdot C)} \cdot \overline{(C \cdot B)}$ (De Morgan's Law)

= $A \cdot (B + \overline{C}) \cdot (\overline{C} + \overline{B})$ (Double Negation and De Morgan's Law)

= $A \cdot (B\overline{C} + B\overline{B} + \overline{C}\overline{C} + \overline{C}\overline{B})$ (Distributive Law)

= $A \cdot (B\overline{C} + 0 + \overline{C} + \overline{B}\overline{C})$ (Identity Laws)

= $A \cdot (\overline{C}(B+1+\overline{B}))$ (Factoring)

= $A \cdot (\overline{C}(1))$ (Identity Law)

= $A \cdot \overline{C}$

üìå Points to Remember (Boolean Algebra II)

Boolean algebra and De Morgan's laws are two tools to simplify complicated logic expressions or logic circuits.
