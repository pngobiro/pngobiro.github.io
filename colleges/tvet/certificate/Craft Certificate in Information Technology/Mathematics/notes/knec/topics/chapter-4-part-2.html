‚öôÔ∏è 4.6 Properties

Commutative Property

$$ \begin{aligned} & A \cap B = B \cap A \\ & A \cup B = B \cup A \end{aligned} $$

Associative Property

$$ \begin{aligned} & (A \cap B) \cap C = A \cap (B \cap C) \\ & (A \cup B) \cup C = A \cup (B \cup C) \end{aligned} $$

Distribution Property

$$ \begin{aligned} & A \cap (B \cup C) = (A \cap B) \cup (A \cap C) \\ & A \cup (B \cap C) = (A \cup B) \cap (A \cup C) \end{aligned} $$

Example: If $A = \{1, 3, 5, 7\}, B = \{1, 2, 3, 4\}$ and $C = \{3, 4, 7\}$ then

  1. $A \cap (B \cup C) = \{1, 3, 5, 7\} \cap \{1, 2, 3, 4, 7\} = \{1, 3, 7\}$
  2. $(A \cap B) \cup (A \cap C) = \{1, 3\} \cup \{3, 7\} = \{1, 3, 7\}$

üîß 4.7 Applications

Use of Venn diagrams in Solving Problems

Example:

In a group of 40 boys, 27 like to play basketball, 25 like tennis while 17 like both. Deduce the number of boys who play neither game.

Solution:

Let B represent the set of boys who play basketball and T represents those playing tennis. We are told that 17 like both, that means $n(B \cap T) = 17$.

Let the number of boys who play neither game be x.

Figure 4-10

We are given n(U) = 40

Number only B = n(B) - n(B ‚à© T) = 27 - 17 = 10

Number only T = n(T) - n(B ‚à© T) = 25 - 17 = 8

Total = (Only B) + (Only T) + (Both) + (Neither)

40 = 10 + 8 + 17 + x

40 = 35 + x

x = 40 - 35

x = 5

Therefore, 5 boys play neither game.
