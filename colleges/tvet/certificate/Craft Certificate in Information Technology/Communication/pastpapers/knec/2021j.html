Question 11 (16 marks)

(a) Outline four ways in which a communicator can develop effective listening skills. (8 marks)

(b) The Welfare Committee of Sakwa Motorcycle Riders Association intends to hold a special meeting in two weeks' time to discuss ways of improving the welfare of its members. Write the notice to convene the meeting. (8 marks)

Answer

Disclaimer: Answers are AI-generated and may contain errors. Verify independently.

(a) Developing Effective Listening Skills:

  • Pay Attention: Focus fully on the speaker, minimize distractions (physical and mental).
  • Show You Are Listening: Use non-verbal cues like nodding, maintaining eye contact, and leaning slightly forward.
  • Provide Feedback: Paraphrase the speaker's points ("So, what you're saying is...") to confirm understanding.
  • Defer Judgment: Avoid interrupting or forming counter-arguments while the speaker is still talking.
  • Ask Clarifying Questions: Seek more information if something is unclear, rather than making assumptions.

Question 13 (18 marks)

(a) Outline the steps that are followed when writing a business report. (8 marks)

(b) Explain five guidelines that an interviewer should follow in order to make an interview session effective. (10 marks)

Answer

Disclaimer: Answers are AI-generated and may contain errors. Verify independently.

(a) Steps in Writing a Business Report:

  1. Define the Purpose and Scope: Clearly understand why the report is needed and what it should cover. Identify the target audience.
  2. Gather Information: Collect relevant data through research, interviews, surveys, observations, etc.
  3. Analyze the Information: Interpret the collected data, identify patterns, trends, and key findings.
  4. Organize the Content: Create a logical structure (e.g., Introduction, Findings, Conclusion, Recommendations) and outline the main points for each section.
  5. Draft the Report: Write the first version of the report, focusing on presenting information clearly and logically.
  6. Revise and Edit: Review the draft for clarity, accuracy, completeness, conciseness, and correctness (grammar, spelling, punctuation). Ensure appropriate tone and format.
  7. Finalize and Distribute: Prepare the final version, including any necessary visuals (charts, tables), and distribute it through the appropriate channel.

(Note: Only eight marks allocated, so fewer steps might suffice depending on detail expected).

(b) Guidelines for an Effective Interview Session (Interviewer):

  • Prepare Thoroughly: Review the job description and candidate's application; prepare relevant, structured questions.
  • Create Rapport: Start by putting the candidate at ease with introductions and brief small talk to create a comfortable atmosphere.
  • Ask Open-Ended Questions: Use questions that encourage detailed responses rather than simple yes/no answers (e.g., "Tell me about a time when...").
  • Listen Actively: Pay full attention to the candidate's answers, using verbal and non-verbal cues to show engagement. Avoid interrupting unnecessarily.
  • Control the Interview Flow: Keep the interview on track, manage time effectively, and ensure all key areas are covered.
  • Provide Information & Answer Questions: Give the candidate adequate information about the role and company, and allow them time to ask questions.
  • Evaluate Objectively: Take notes and assess the candidate based on pre-defined criteria related to the job requirements, avoiding personal bias.

(Note: Only five were required)
