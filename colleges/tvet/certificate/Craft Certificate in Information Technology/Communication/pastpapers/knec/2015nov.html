Question 11 (18 marks)

(a) Explain four ways by which a sender can minimize barriers to effective communication. (8 marks)

(b) Explain five factors that may contribute to the failure of a meeting. (10 marks)

Answer

Disclaimer: Answers are AI-generated and may contain errors. Verify independently.

(a) Ways a sender can minimize communication barriers:

  • Use Clear and Simple Language: Avoid jargon, ambiguity, and overly complex sentences. Tailor language to the receiver.
  • Choose the Appropriate Channel: Select the medium (email, face-to-face, memo) that best suits the message, audience, and urgency.
  • Seek Feedback: Actively ask questions and encourage the receiver to paraphrase to ensure understanding.
  • Be Mindful of Non-Verbal Cues: Ensure body language, tone, and facial expressions align with the verbal message (in oral communication).
  • Listen Actively: Pay attention to the receiver's verbal and non-verbal feedback during interaction.

(Note: Only four were required)

(b) Factors contributing to meeting failure:

  • Lack of Clear Purpose/Agenda: Attendees are unsure why they are there or what needs to be achieved.
  • Poor Facilitation/Chairing: The meeting is not kept on track, discussions wander, or conflicts aren't managed.
  • Dominating Participants: One or a few individuals monopolize the conversation, preventing others from contributing.
  • Lack of Preparation: Attendees (or the chair) come unprepared without reading materials or considering the topics.
  • No Clear Decisions or Action Items: The meeting ends without clear outcomes, assigned responsibilities, or deadlines.
  • Wrong Attendees: Key decision-makers are absent, or irrelevant people are included, wasting time.

(Note: Only five were required)

Question 12 (16 marks)

Customers of Bidii Supermarket have complained that goods expire on the shelves before being bought. As the Assistant Operations Manager, you have been requested to investigate the matter. Assume that you have completed the investigations and write the report giving recommendations.

Answer

Disclaimer: Answers are AI-generated and may contain errors. Verify independently.

MEMORANDUM

TO: The Operations Manager, Bidii Supermarket

FROM: [Your Name], Assistant Operations Manager

DATE: [Current Date]

SUBJECT: INVESTIGATION INTO CUSTOMER COMPLAINTS REGARDING EXPIRED GOODS

1. Introduction
This report details the findings of an investigation into recent customer complaints concerning goods expiring on the shelves at Bidii Supermarket. The investigation aimed to identify the root causes and propose recommendations.

2. Methodology
The investigation involved:

  • Reviewing customer complaint logs for the past [Time Period, e.g., 3 months].
  • Conducting spot checks on shelves across various departments (Grocery, Dairy, Bakery, etc.).
  • Interviewing shelf-stocking staff and department supervisors.
  • Analyzing stock rotation procedures and inventory records.

3. Findings
The investigation revealed the following contributing factors:

  • Inconsistent Stock Rotation: Staff are not consistently implementing the First-In, First-Out (FIFO) principle, leading to older stock remaining at the back.
  • Inadequate Expiry Date Checks: Regular checks for near-expiry dates are infrequent or not systematically performed across all sections.
  • Overstocking: Some slow-moving items are overstocked, increasing the likelihood of expiry before sale.
  • Staff Training Gaps: Some staff members appeared unclear on the correct procedures for stock rotation and expiry date management.
  • Poor Visibility: In some shelf arrangements, expiry dates are difficult to see without removing the product.

4. Conclusion
The issue of goods expiring on shelves stems primarily from procedural inconsistencies in stock management and inadequate staff adherence to existing protocols, compounded by some overstocking issues.

5. Recommendations
To address this issue and prevent recurrence, the following actions are recommended:

  1. Mandatory FIFO Training: Conduct refresher training for all shelf-stocking staff emphasizing the strict implementation of FIFO.
  2. Implement Systematic Date Checks: Introduce a daily checklist system for supervisors to verify expiry date checks in their sections. Utilize color-coded tags for items nearing expiry.
  3. Review Ordering Procedures: Analyze sales data to adjust order quantities for slow-moving items and reduce overstocking.
  4. Improve Shelf Management: Ensure products are arranged to make expiry dates clearly visible.
  5. Regular Audits: Conduct random internal audits to monitor compliance with stock rotation and date-checking procedures.

Implementing these recommendations should significantly reduce the incidence of expired goods on shelves and improve customer satisfaction.

Regards,

[Your Name]
Assistant Operations Manager
