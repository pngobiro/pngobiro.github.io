Question 11 (18 marks)

(a) You have been invited to give a speech to the students of Daima High School. Explain five non-verbal cues that you will use to enhance your presentation. (10 marks)

(b) Describe four methods that are used to carry out upward communication. (8 marks)

Answer

Disclaimer: Answers are AI-generated and may contain errors. Verify independently.

(a) Five non-verbal cues to enhance presentation:

  1. Eye Contact: Maintain appropriate eye contact with different sections of the audience to engage them and show confidence.
  2. Gestures: Use natural hand movements to emphasize points and add visual interest to the presentation.
  3. Facial Expressions: Show enthusiasm and interest through appropriate facial expressions that match the content.
  4. Posture: Maintain an upright, confident posture while standing or moving on stage.
  5. Movement: Use purposeful movement across the stage to maintain audience attention and emphasize transitions.

(b) Methods for upward communication:

  1. Reports: Regular written reports submitted to superiors about work progress, challenges, and achievements.
  2. Suggestion Systems: Formal channels for employees to submit ideas and recommendations for improvement.
  3. Performance Reviews: Regular meetings where employees can discuss their work, goals, and concerns with supervisors.
  4. Staff Meetings: Regular gatherings where employees can share updates and concerns with management.

Question 14 (18 marks)

Read the passage below and then answer the questions that follow.

With entrepreneurship gaining popularity, competition has become stiff and only the very best entrepreneurs will thrive. This is because business nowadays can be tricky as people change their preferences and tastes every now and then. Moreover, technology is fast changing the way of doing business. For instance, Internet and product delivery to anywhere and at any time has changed the rules of competition.

Getting customers to come again and again is the most important endeavour of every business. No business can be successful if it is continually dealing with new customers. Repeat customers are the source of profit, and this allows a business to grow and prosper. While there have been signs of growth being recorded in various sectors of the economy, many businesses continue to experience poor performance and reduced profits. As a result of these tough economic conditions, customer loyalty has gained popularity among businesses trying to maintain their profitability.

To gain customer loyalty, there are several principles that an entrepreneur should follow. Practicing effective communication is one such principle. It pays to reach out to repeat buyers through various means such as e-mail, newsletter, monthly fliers, a reminder card or holiday greeting card. Such customers should always be informed of any change in business processes, products or services. Offering good customer service is also an important principle. Entrepreneurs should always go an extra mile to meet customer needs by training employees to ensure customer satisfaction. This is because customers remember being treated well and knowing that they are valued.

In addition, an entrepreneur can give customers incentives such as free offers, coupons, low interest rates and so on. This gives customers a reason to return to the business. Moreover, a business should be reliable to attract customer loyalty. For instance, an entrepreneur should let customers know when something goes wrong and compensate them for the inconvenience. Being flexible is a further principle that an entrepreneur should apply when solving customers' problems or handling complaints. Excuses such as "that's our policy" will only lead to loss of more business.

Besides, customers should get value for their money. Value for money is the amount that a customer will get from a service or product in relation to the cost. A good entrepreneur should give a free trial period for a product or service to gain brand loyalty. This principle ensures that the customers will return not only to buy the same product but also to try other products and services offered by the entrepreneur. Furthermore, an entrepreneur should have product awareness. This is by understanding the products the business is dealing in.

For these principles to be followed effectively, employees - who are major stakeholders - should be involved in every business activity. Employees play an important role building customer loyalty and in ensuring that the efforts by a business to maintain loyal customers are fruitful. Training the employee's ability to provide superior service to customers should, therefore, be seen as a long-term value addition initiative.

Besides, effort spent in selecting and training employees can lead to increased employee satisfaction and competence. This will result in customer satisfaction which in turn will create customer loyalty hence improved sales and higher profit margins.

Adapted from: "KASNEB Newsline" Issue No. 3 July-September 2012

(a) State the meaning of the following words and phrases as used in the passage: (6 marks)

  1. thrive
  2. tough
  3. go an extra mile
  4. fruitful
  5. in turn

(b) In about 120 words, and according to the passage, explain the principles that an entrepreneur should follow to maintain customer loyalty. (12 marks)

Answer

Disclaimer: Answers are AI-generated and may contain errors. Verify independently.

(a) Meanings of words and phrases:

  1. thrive: To grow, develop, and be successful
  2. tough: Difficult, challenging, or hard to deal with
  3. go an extra mile: To make an additional or special effort beyond what is expected
  4. fruitful: Producing good or helpful results; successful
  5. in turn: As a consequence; one after another

(b) Principles for maintaining customer loyalty (120 words):

Entrepreneurs should follow several key principles to maintain customer loyalty. Effective communication is essential, involving regular contact through various channels like emails, newsletters, and greeting cards to keep customers informed of changes. Good customer service is crucial, requiring well-trained employees who can satisfy customer needs. Businesses should offer incentives such as free offers and coupons to encourage repeat visits. Reliability is important, including being transparent about problems and offering compensation when necessary. Flexibility in handling customer issues is vital, avoiding rigid policy enforcement. Providing value for money and offering free trial periods helps build brand loyalty. Product awareness is also essential, requiring thorough understanding of the business offerings. Employee involvement and training are crucial for successfully implementing these principles.
