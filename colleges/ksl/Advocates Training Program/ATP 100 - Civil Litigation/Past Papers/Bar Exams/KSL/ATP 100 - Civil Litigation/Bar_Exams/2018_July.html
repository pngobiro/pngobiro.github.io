Compulsory Q1 Monday 16th July, 2018 3 Hours

ATP 100: Civil Litigation

Council of Legal Education - Examination for Admission to the Roll of Advocates

Instructions to Candidates

  • This paper contains Four (4) printed pages including the cover page, with a total of Six questions.
  • Candidates MUST answer FIVE questions.
  • Question ONE is compulsory and carries 20 marks.
  • All other questions carry 10 marks each.
  • Answers MUST be supported by relevant case law and statutory provisions where required.

Question One (Compulsory)

20 Marks
1

You have recently been retained as a pupil at the law firm of Xavier and Co. Advocates, Y Plaza, P.O. Box 24 Ubungo and in your daily tasks in the firm, you take down the following notes:

Dan wants a case lodged in court. He was involved in an accident where he broke his left leg. He complains that he suffered loss as a result of the accident. The driver, Derrick was blamed for the accident of motor vehicle Registration Number KBS 149A, a bus in which Dan was a fare paying passenger. The driver was blamed for driving the motor vehicle dangerously, reckless and/or negligently. The motor vehicle was also found to be in an unroadworthy condition.

The accident occurred on 20th November 2004 in Vimax District in the Republic of Kez. The vehicle belonged to Mary who resides within Vimax District. Derrick is still working for Mary. Her office is located at Pom Plaza. The address is P.O. Box 124 Ubungo.

There are several documents in the file related to the case. A discharge summary sheet from the hospital showing nature of treatment, medical receipts amounting to Kshs. 70,000/-, transport receipts for the cab company totaling Kshs. 30,000/-. There are also receipts amounting to Kshs. 20,000 as evidence of money paid to a house help who assisted Dan during the time of recovery, Dan has already been referred to a medical expert who charged Kshs. 5,000/- for a medical opinion. The receipt is available in the file for that. The medical report is also ready. Jayden who was a passenger in the bus is ready to testify in support of Dan's case. All witness statements for Dan, Jayden and the Doctor are ready. A demand letter is also in place but there has been no response thereto.

Draft the appropriate pleadings seeking the necessary remedies in court and state documents, if any, that should be filed together with the pleadings. (20 marks)

Question Two

10 Marks
2

You are a legal officer employed by Granto People Company Limited and the Legal Manager has instructed you to write a brief.

A former director of the Company Mr. Peter James is aggrieved by a ruling of the Employment and Labour Relations Court which the Judge declined to review and set aside a judgement delivered against the company.

In the judgement, the court held that Mr. Peter James who was not a party to the suit had sexually harassed Mary Amber. Accordingly, the court awarded her Kshs. 7 million as general damages against Granto People Company Limited.

Mr. Peter James has appealed the decision to the Court of Appeal alleging that he was not given an opportunity to be heard.

Give a brief on joinder of parties, review of judgements by Employment and Labour Courts and chances of success. (10 marks)

Question Three

10 Marks
3

Juma Jomo of P.O. Box 1314 Kericho has approached you with the following complaint:

"That in 1987, he was enlisted in the Kenya Army as member number 59030 and posted to the Military Corps, Lanet Department. He rendered service with dedication and rose through the ranks to the position of Senior Sergeant, earning a salary of Kshs. 59,884.

In the month of July 2017, he was charged with the offence of neglect of duty and two counts of conduct to prejudice of good order and discipline contrary to section 65 and 121 of the Kenya Defence Forces Act respectively. He was arrested on 5th August 2017 and confined in the barracks up to 9th September 2017 when his trial commenced. He was not allowed to instruct anybody to represent him. He was eventually found guilty as charged and was summarily dismissed from the force without any benefits. His dismissal was not subjected to the automatic administrative review provided for by Section 159 of the Act.

Juma contends that his right to fair labour practices, fair administrative action and to a fair hearing have been violated. He has also taken issue with Section 244(2) of the Act which provides for the withholding, reduction of the amount or suspension of benefits payable to any member who has been dismissed under any circumstances.

The Service Commander, Kenya Army of P.O. Box 30178, Nairobi did not respond to his request for "automatic review."

He has requested you to study the pleadings seeking redress for the alleged violation of his rights.

Draft appropriate pleadings indicating the nature or reliefs you will be seeking. (10 marks)

Question Four

10 Marks
4

Peter has sued Jane in a suit. Peter's claim against Jane is of Kshs. 1 million which arose out of supply of goods. Jane has failed to settle the said amount. Peter is concerned that when the case is concluded judgement will be given in his favour. He has attached all relevant documents. Jane already has lawyers who have also filed the relevant papers in court. A hearing date has been fixed two months away.

While in the course of business, Peter gets credible information that Jane has planned to move her transport trucks to the neighbouring country in an attempt to escape justice in the present suit. Peter is also informed that Jane has no other assets but the trucks. Jane has also been heard stating that she will not settle the money claimed in the suit. Peter is advised that if he does not move with speed, he might not be able to recover the money even if judgment is entered in his favour. Peter's lawyers have filed the relevant application in court affirming all facts stated above.

As a legal researcher to the sitting magistrate:

(a) Comment on the relevant Order being sought by Peter and the rationale behind the orders sought. (3 marks)

(b) Advise on the decision likely to be given by court and reasons for the decision. (7 marks)

Question Five

10 Marks
5

People purporting to be auctioneers broke into Helen Chloe offices and carried away several items under the pretext of instructions from Anne Parker the decree holder. Helen Chloe informs you that there was no proclamation notice served on her and she is not aware of the existence of Civil Suit Number 700 of 2017 wherein Ann Parker had sought payment of a debt. In the court records Ann Parker had applied for an interlocutory judgement which was granted.

Helen Chloe confides in you that she owes Ann Parker Kshs. 1,000,000/- and she has kept her bargain by diligently paying Kshs. 200,000/- as agreed upon on a quarterly basis and she expects to pay off the debt as agreed in two years' time.

Give her advice and the process to be taken. (10 marks)

Question Six

10 Marks
6

Jamy has a judgment in his favour. The defendant is supposed to pay Kshs. 15,000,000. This is according to the judgement that that was rendered by the court. However, the amount that were proved cumulatively in the trial was Kshs. 17,000,000. Kendo the defendant has no problem in settling the amount of Kshs. 17,000,000. Both parties agree that Judges are also human and should be given a second chance to correct the Judgement.

As an advocate representing Jamy you concur with their sentiments:

(a) Advise Jamy on the appropriate legal mechanism to be initiated and the steps to be undertaken. (7 marks)

(b) Suppose it was Suzy, personal assistant to Jamy who raised the issues above. Explain whether it would be proper for Suzy to initiate legal proceedings to have the Judgement corrected on behalf of Jamy who is still within the jurisdiction of the court. (3 marks)
