Question Two

10 Marks
2

There is an application that has been filed by the plaintiff for an order to compel the defendant to produce for inspection by the plaintiff. This is for the purpose of proving that the defendant obtained a valid title of LR.No.1/2019 Umoja before transferring the same to the Plaintiff failure to which the defence filed be dismissed and judgement be entered for plaintiff against defendant.

The reasons for the application is that the defendant failed to respond to a notice to produce documents for inspection whilst the documents are essential for determination of crucial issues in dispute between parties.

The plaintiff argued that the defendant did not have good title to the suit property and did not therefore pass a good title to the plaintiff. There is a consent to transfer that had purportedly been issued and according to the plaintiff the consent had irregularly been issued since the suit property had already been declared a national monument. Therefore, it was necessary for the plaintiff to inspect any document the defendant could be holding.

The notice served upon the defendant requiring production of documents described the documents to be:

"the documents to prove the valid title of LR No.1/2019 acquired by the Defendant."

(a) As an advocate retained by the defendant/respondent, write a brief showing your argument before court requiring the notice for inspection to be dismissed. (6 marks)

(b) What are the processes required to have been done by the applicant for an inspection order to be given by the court? (4 marks)

Question Three

10 Marks
3

As an associate in the firm of Y & Co. Advocates which has been retained by Jane listed as a plaintiff, the following are case notes you have made from a file you are handling:

  1. It is a divorce case. A plaint has already been filed. The plaintiffs listed are namely, Jane, the wife and Jane's mother, Elizabeth. The defendants are listed as Joseph, the husband and Mary the one who is allegedly 'destroying' the marriage.
  2. There is also a verifying affidavit which was filed together with the plaint.
  3. The plaint contained a brief narration of romantic escapades the defendant had with his lover.
  4. There is a paragraph in the plaint that contains a text message that details an arrangement of one of the escapades.
  5. The affidavit of service shows that the plaint had been served on the watchman of the residence of the defendant. The defendant had nonetheless entered appearance.
  6. The plaint was amended long after the defence had been filed thus after 2 months, there being no pleading filed after the defence. You do not see any court attendance for the period the pleadings were amended.

After the filing of the plaint in Court you learn that you have made fatal mistakes in the process of drafting which would eventually compromise your client's suit.

You therefore decide to take corrective measures to cure the anomalies and file the amended plaint before close of pleadings.

Proceed and amend the plaint. (10 marks)

...On 2nd November 2017, the court issued a Notice of Stay of execution and commanded James and Mukijo Enterprises "not to take any steps as regards the attached/proclaimed property if any and to keep it in safe custody. On 4th November 2017, Peter, through his advocate filed a notice of intention to proceed. Simon never filed an application seeking to lift the attachment until 20th November 2017.

On 25th November 2017, he executed a transfer in favour of John and on the same day, the Chief Land Registrar entered the name of John as the proprietor in the Register and issued him with a provisional title.

Simon being dissatisfied with the sale, filed a Notice of Motion under Articles 19, 20, 21, 22, 25, 40, 47, 50(1), 165(3) and (4), 259 and 260 of the Constitution of Kenya 2010, Section 1A, 1B and 3A of the Civil Procedure Act Cap 63 and the inherent powers of the Court seeking to set aside the sale and several Constitutional declarations for alleged violations of his fundamental rights and freedoms. He named Peter, John, Thomas, Innocent and the Chief Land Registrar as respondents.

John has instructed you to act for him in the application but wants you to give him a legal opinion before you take any steps.

Write a concise legal opinion on the legal issues raised and the merits or otherwise of the application. (10 marks)
