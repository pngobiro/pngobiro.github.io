üö´ Consequences of Non-Attendance

On the day fixed for the hearing, parties must attend either in person or by their advocates. Order 12 prescribes specific consequences depending on who fails to appear.

Scenario Court's Action (Order 12)
Neither Party Attends The court may dismiss the suit (Rule 1).
Only Plaintiff Attends
  • If notice duly served: Proceed ex parte.
  • If not served: Order second notice.
  • If served late: Postpone hearing (Rule 2).
Only Defendant Attends The suit shall be dismissed unless the defendant admits the claim (or part of it), in which case judgment is entered on the admission (Rule 3).

Partial Attendance

  • Some Plaintiffs Absent: The court may proceed with the suit or make orders as it deems just (Rule 4).
  • Some Defendants Absent: The court may proceed and give judgment against the absent defendants (Rule 5).

Can a Fresh Suit be Filed?

‚ö†Ô∏è

The Rule 3 Trap

Where a suit is dismissed under Rule 3 (only defendant attends), the plaintiff is barred from bringing a fresh suit in respect of the same cause of action (Rule 6). Their only remedy is to apply to set aside the dismissal.

However, if dismissed under Rule 1 (neither attends), a fresh suit may be brought subject to limitation laws.

Setting Aside Judgment or Dismissal (Rule 7)

Where judgment has been entered ex parte or a suit dismissed for non-attendance, the aggrieved party may apply to the court to set aside or vary the order.

  • Application: Usually by Notice of Motion.
  • Grounds: Must show "sufficient cause" for non-attendance (e.g., illness, mistake of counsel, lack of notice).
  • Terms: The court may set aside on terms it thinks just (e.g., payment of costs "thrown away").
