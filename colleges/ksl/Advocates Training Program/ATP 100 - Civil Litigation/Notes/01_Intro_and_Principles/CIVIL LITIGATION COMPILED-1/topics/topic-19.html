Execution signifies the enforcement of the judgment or orders of a court. The Decree Holder (DH) selects the appropriate mode of execution against the Judgment Debtor (JD), subject to the court's discretion.

Statutory Modes (Section 38)

  • Delivery of property specifically decreed.
  • Attachment and sale (or sale without attachment) of property.
  • Attachment of debts (Garnishee proceedings).
  • Arrest and detention in prison.
  • Appointment of a receiver.

Which Court Executes?

Generally, the court that passed the decree executes it. However, it may be transferred to another court if:

  • The JD resides/works within the other court's jurisdiction.
  • The JD has no property within the local limits of the original court.
  • The decree directs the sale of immovable property situated elsewhere.
02

üìù Application for Execution

The court cannot execute a decree on its own motion; there must be a formal application by the Decree Holder.

Formal Requirements (Order 22 Rule 6)

  • Written Application: Must be in tabular form (Form 14 Appendix A) containing details like suit number, date of decree, amount due, and mode of execution required.
  • Oral Application: Permitted at the time of passing the decree for immediate arrest if the JD is within court precincts.

Notice to Show Cause (Rule 18)

Execution generally issues without notice, BUT a Notice to Show Cause is mandatory if:

Circumstance Reason
> 1 Year Delay Application is made more than one year after the decree.
Legal Representatives Execution is sought against the legal representative of a party.
Salary Attachment To establish if the JD is still employed.
Arrest Before committing a JD to civil jail (Section 38/Rule 31).

Case Law: Madhaji v Alibhai [1960] EA 167 - Failure to issue notice where required renders subsequent orders a nullity.

03

üè∑Ô∏è Attachment & Sale of Property

Immovable Property

Attachment is effected by registering a Prohibitory Order against the title. This prohibits the JD from transferring or charging the property.

  • Sale Procedure: Must be by public auction conducted by an appointed officer (auctioneer).
  • Notice Period: Sale cannot take place until 30 days after notification.

Movable Property

Executed by seizure and delivery (for specific movables) or attachment and sale.

  • Agricultural Produce: Attached by affixing warrant in the field.
  • Salary: Attachment limited to one-third of the salary.
  • Notice Period: Sale cannot take place until 15 days after notification (unless perishable).
üö´

Prohibited Participation

The Decree Holder must not bid or participate in the auction without express permission of the court. If they do, the sale may be set aside.

04

‚õìÔ∏è Arrest & Detention (Section 38)

Execution by detention in prison is a measure of last resort. The court must be satisfied, for reasons recorded in writing, that:

  1. 1 The JD is likely to abscond or leave the local limits to obstruct execution.
  2. 2 The JD has dishonestly transferred or concealed property in bad faith.
  3. 3 The JD has the means to pay but refuses or neglects to do so.
  4. 4 The decree is for a sum for which the JD was bound in a fiduciary capacity to account.
