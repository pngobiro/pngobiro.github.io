üéØ Standing to Sue (Locus Standi)

A "party" is a person who, on the record of the court, has commenced a proceeding or has been served with summons or added by court order. The determination of whether a person is capable of suing or being sued is procedural and governed by the lex fori (law of the forum).

Definition: Locus Standi

Legal standing is the status required of a person to enable them to invoke the jurisdiction of the courts. It prevents the court's time from being wasted by "busy bodies."

The "Aggrieved Person" Rule

  • Civil Matters: Generally, a person must be "aggrieved" to have locus.
  • Constitutional/Public Interest: Article 22(2) allows any person to sue on behalf of others in respect of public interest litigation or constitutional interpretation.
02

üë∂ Persons Under Disability

Minors (Infants)

A minor (under 18) is under a legal disability. They cannot sue or be sued directly.

Role Representation Requirement
Minor as Plaintiff Sues by Next Friend Written authority must be filed with plaint.
Minor as Defendant Defends by Guardian ad litem If no guardian appointed, plaintiff must apply for one (Order 32).

Title of Action Example:

Pleading Title
Serah Jerotich (a minor) by Cecilia Tarus (her mother and next friend)
                                    VERSUS
Jonas Mambo (a minor) by Mwadzile Mambo (his father and guardian ad litem)

Attaining Majority

When a minor plaintiff comes of age, the next friend must cease proceedings. The former minor must file a notice to either adopt or repudiate the proceedings.

Mentally Incompetent Persons

If a party becomes mentally incompetent during proceedings:

  • Proceedings are stayed (not discontinued).
  • Proceedings may be taken to have the party declared insane or to appoint a representative.
  • Recovery: If the party recovers, they should apply to discharge the next friend/guardian ad litem.
03

üåç Special Parties

Aliens

  • Alien Friend: Can sue/be sued generally like a subject.
  • Alien Enemy: Generally cannot sue (status of an outlaw) but may be made a defendant. Courts will not assist proceedings that enrich an alien enemy.

Foreign States & Diplomats

Generally immune from suit under International Law, unless privilege is waived. Includes foreign sovereigns, diplomats, and international organizations (UN, AU).

Exception: Immunity is not absolute. No immunity for ordinary commercial transactions (Thai-Europe Tapioca Service Ltd v. Government of Pakistan).

The Government

The Attorney General (AG) is the principal legal adviser and represents the Government (Art. 156 Constitution; S.12 Government Proceedings Act).

Note: In constitutional interpretation or public interest cases, the AG must be joined as a party.

04

üè¢ Corporate & Representative Bodies

Companies & Statutory Persons

A body corporate sues/is sued in its corporate name. To sue, it must authorize the action via a special resolution.

  • Liquidation: Liquidator sues in the name of the company.
  • Receivership: Receiver for debenture holders may sue in company name.

Partnerships (Order 30 Rule 1)

May sue or be sued in the firm's name. A judgment is enforceable against firm property and any partner individually served.

üí°

Drafting Tip

State the names of partners followed by "trading as [Firm Name]". E.g., "John Doe and Jane Doe trading as Doe Associates."

Representative Parties

Administrators/Executors: Sue on behalf of the estate. Must have grant of letters of administration/probate.

Class Actions (Order 1 Rule 8): One or more persons may sue/defend on behalf of all persons having the same interest in the suit. Requires notice to other parties (personal service or advertisement).
