üö´ Consequences of Non-Appearance

If a defendant fails to enter an appearance within the time stipulated in the summons, the plaintiff may proceed to obtain judgment in default. The procedure depends on the nature of the claim.

‚ö†Ô∏è

Prerequisites

Before entering judgment, the plaintiff MUST file an Affidavit of Service proving that the summons was duly served (Order 10 Rule 2).

Types of Claims & Judgments

Nature of Claim Type of Judgment Next Step
Liquidated Amount
(Specific sum, e.g., debt)		 Final Judgment Execution for sum + interest + costs.
Unliquidated Damages
(e.g., General damages in tort)		 Interlocutory Judgment Set down for Formal Proof (Assessment of Damages).
Detention of Goods Interlocutory Judgment Assess value of goods/damages.

Liquidated vs. Unliquidated Demand

  • Liquidated: A specific sum capable of arithmetic calculation (e.g., Kshs 500,000 for goods sold).
  • Unliquidated: Requires judicial assessment (e.g., "damages for pain and suffering").
02

‚öôÔ∏è Procedure & Special Cases

Request for Judgment

For liquidated claims, the plaintiff applies via a Request for Judgment (Form No. 13 Appendix A). The court (Deputy Registrar) enters judgment for the sum claimed not exceeding the plaint amount.

Multiple Defendants

If some defendants appear and others do not:

  • Judgment can be entered against the defaulters.
  • Execution can issue against defaulters without prejudice to the right to proceed against those who appeared.

Judgment Against the Government (Order 10 Rule 8)

üèõÔ∏è

Special Rule

No judgment in default of appearance or pleading may be entered against the Government without leave of the court.

Application for leave must be served not less than 7 days before the hearing.

Persons Under Disability

If no appearance is entered for a minor or person of unsound mind, the plaintiff must first apply to the court to appoint a guardian ad litem before proceeding (Order 10 Rule 1).

03

üîÑ Setting Aside Judgment

A default judgment is not a judgment on the merits. To prevent injustice, the court has discretion to set it aside (Order 10 Rule 11).

Regular vs. Irregular Judgment

Type Description Court's Approach
Regular Obtained in strict compliance with rules (proper service, timelines expired). Set aside at court's discretion; usually requires a defence on merit.
Irregular Obtained in breach of rules (e.g., entered prematurely, no service). Set aside ex debito justitiae (as of right), usually without terms.

Case Law: Southern Credit Banking Corporation v. Jonah Stephen Nganga (2006) eKLR.

Criteria for Setting Aside (Regular Judgments)

The applicant must generally show:

  1. Reasonable Excuse: Why they failed to appear (e.g., mistake by advocate, illness, ignorance of procedure).
  2. Defence on Merit: A triable issue that constitutes a valid defence.
üí°

Delay Factors

The application should be made promptly. If delayed, reasons must be given. However, the court may still set aside if:

  • No prejudice is caused that costs can't cure.
  • Allowing the judgment to stand would be oppressive.
