01

üöÄ Disposal of Suits by Summary Procedure

Trial, as a rule, should precede judgment. However, under Order 36, a plaintiff may seek summary judgment without going into a full trial. This procedure is intended to enable a plaintiff with a liquidated claim, to which there is clearly no good defence, to obtain a quick judgment without being delayed by the defendant's tactics.

‚ÑπÔ∏è

Scope of Order 36

Applicable to actions begun by an endorsed plaint for:

  • Debt or liquidated claim.
  • Recovery of land (with/without rent/mesne profits) where term has expired/determined.

Not applicable to: Libel, slander, malicious prosecution, allegations of fraud, probate actions, or actions against the Government.

Conditions for Application

  1. An endorsed plaint must have been served on the defendant.
  2. The plaint must be supported by an affidavit verifying the facts and stating the deponent's belief that there is no defence.
  3. Summons must be served not less than 10 clear days before the return day.
02

‚öôÔ∏è The Application Procedure

The application is made by way of Notice of Motion supported by an affidavit (Form 24 Appendix A). The affidavit must be sworn by the applicant or a person who can swear positively to the facts.

Timing

  • Application must be made after appearance has been entered but before defence is filed.
  • Notice of not less than 7 days must be given to the defendant.

The Court's Options

Scenario Outcome
No Triable Issue Judgment for the plaintiff.
Technical Defect Application dismissed (e.g., outside Order 36 scope).
Triable Issue Found Unconditional leave to defend granted.
Shadowy Defence Conditional leave granted (e.g., deposit security).
03

üõ°Ô∏è The Defendant's Response

The defendant must show, by affidavit or oral evidence, that there is a bona fide triable issue of fact or law. They do not need to show a "good" defence on the merits, just that there is a dispute worthy of trial.

Grounds for Opposition

  • Preliminary technicality.
  • Clear defence exists.
  • Serious issue of fact to be tried.
  • Arguable point of law.
  • Raising a prima facie set-off or counterclaim.

Types of Leave to Defend

Conditional vs. Unconditional

  • ‚û§

    Unconditional Leave: Granted when the defendant raises a triable issue. They must not be shut out.

  • ‚û§

    Conditional Leave: Granted when the defence is "shadowy" or has little substance. The court may require security for costs or payment into court.

04

üè¶ The "Cheque Rule"

Where goods or services are paid by a cheque which is subsequently dishonoured, the payee is entitled to summary judgment. This is a rule of commercial convenience; cheques are regarded as equivalent to cash.

üö´

No Set-Off

The defendant is precluded from setting off damages (e.g., for defective goods) against the claim on the cheque. They must pay first and counterclaim later.

Allowed Defences in Cheque Actions

The only possible defences allowed are:

  • Fraud
  • Invalidity
  • Illegality
  • Duress
  • Total failure of consideration
