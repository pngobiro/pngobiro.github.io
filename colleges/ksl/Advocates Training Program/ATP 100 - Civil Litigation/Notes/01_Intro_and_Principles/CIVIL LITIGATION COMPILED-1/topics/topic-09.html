üë• Third Party Proceedings (Order 1 Rule 15)

Third party proceedings arise where a defendant claims against a person not already a party to the suit that they are entitled to contribution or indemnity.

üéØ

Objects of the Procedure

  • ‚û§

    Prevent multiplicity of actions and save expenses.

  • ‚û§

    Prevent the same issue being heard twice with potentially different results.

  • ‚û§

    Have the issue between defendant and third party bound by the decision in the main action.

  • ‚û§

    Decide the issue between defendant and third party as soon as possible after the main action.

Scope of Proceedings

A defendant may claim against a third party when:

  • They are entitled to contribution or indemnity.
  • They are entitled to a relief or remedy related to the original suit.
  • A question connected to the subject matter is substantially the same and should be determined together.
‚ö†Ô∏è

Important Limitation

The third party claim must not be independent from the original action. There must be a connection of fact or subject matter. However, the cause of action need not be similar (e.g., main suit in tort, third party claim in contract).

Procedure

  1. Application: Apply via ex parte chamber summons within 14 days after the close of pleadings for leave to issue a third party notice.
  2. Service: Serve a copy of the notice (Form No. 1 Appendix A) and the plaint upon the third party.
  3. Appearance: Third party must enter appearance within the specified time to dispute liability.

Default of Appearance (Rule 17)

If the third party fails to enter an appearance:

  • They are deemed to admit the validity of the decree obtained against the defendant.
  • They are deemed to admit their own liability to contribute or indemnify.

Note: The Government cannot be in default without leave of the court.

‚öñÔ∏è Interpleader Proceedings (Order 34)

An interpleader proceeding is filed by a person holding property that is being adversely claimed by two or more people. The applicant seeks the court's determination on who the lawful owner is.

Conditions for Application

The applicant must satisfy the court by affidavit that:

Condition Explanation
1. Neutrality The applicant claims no interest in the subject matter other than for charges or costs.
2. No Collusion There is no collusion between the applicant and any of the claimants.
3. Willingness The applicant is willing to pay or transfer the subject matter into court or dispose of it as directed.

Procedure

  • No Pending Suit: Application is made by way of Originating Summons.
  • Pending Suit: If the applicant is already a defendant in a suit concerning the property, application is by Chamber Summons.

Possible Court Orders

Upon hearing the application, the court may:

  1. Declare a claimant barred against the applicant if they fail to appear.
  2. Make a claimant a defendant in a suit in place of the applicant.
  3. Order an issue between claimants to be stated and tried.
  4. Summarily dispose of the matter if the value is small or facts are undisputed.
