An injunction is an order of the court directing a party to do or refrain from doing a specified act. An interlocutory (temporary) injunction is granted pending the final determination of the suit to preserve the status quo.

üí°

The Giella v. Cassman Brown Principles

To succeed in an application for an interlocutory injunction, the applicant must satisfy three conditions:

  1. 1 Establish a prima facie case with a probability of success.
  2. 2 Show that they will suffer irreparable injury that cannot be adequately compensated by damages.
  3. 3 If in doubt, the court will decide on the balance of convenience.

Grounds for Grant (Order 40 Rule 1)

  • Property in dispute is in danger of being wasted, damaged, or alienated.
  • Property is in danger of being wrongfully sold in execution of a decree.
  • Defendant threatens to remove/dispose of property to defraud creditors.
02

‚ö° Interim Injunctions (Ex Parte)

An interim injunction is granted ex parte (without hearing the other side) under a certificate of urgency. It restrains the defendant only until a named day or further order.

Duties of the Applicant

  • Full Disclosure: Must disclose all material facts, including those adverse to the application. Material non-disclosure is a ground for discharge.
  • Promptness: Delay defeats equity (Vigilantibus non dormientibus jura subvenient).
  • Clean Hands: No element of fraud.
‚è±Ô∏è

The 14-Day Rule (Order 40 Rule 4)

An ex parte injunction may be granted only once for not more than 14 days. It shall not be extended thereafter except once by consent or by court order for a period not exceeding 14 days.

03

üîê Mareva & Anton Piller Orders

Mareva Injunction ("Freezing Order")

Designed to prevent a defendant from disposing of assets to frustrate a potential judgment. It operates in personam and can bind third parties (e.g., banks) with notice.

Requirement Explanation
Good Arguable Case Plaintiff must show a strong case on the merits.
Assets in Jurisdiction Defendant must appear to have assets within the court's reach.
Risk of Dissipation Real risk that assets will be removed or dissipated.

Anton Piller Order ("Search Order")

A mandatory injunction ordering a defendant to allow an independent attorney to enter premises to search for and seize evidence (documents, property) relevant to the claim.

  • Element of Surprise: Sought ex parte to prevent destruction of evidence.
  • Requirements: Extremely strong prima facie case, serious actual/potential harm, and clear evidence of incriminating material.

‚õìÔ∏è Enforcement & Discharge

Penal Notice

An order must be served with a Penal Notice warning the party that disobedience will result in contempt proceedings. For corporations, this applies to directors.

Consequences of Breach (Rule 3)

üö´

Sanctions

  • Attachment: Property may be attached for up to 1 year (and sold if breach persists).
  • Imprisonment: Detention in prison for a term not exceeding 6 months.

Discharge of Injunction

An injunction may be discharged on application by the dissatisfied party on grounds such as:

  • Material non-disclosure on ex parte application.
  • Material change of circumstances.
  • Failure to prosecute the substantive claim diligently.
  • Oppression or interference with third-party rights.
