üìÑ Introduction to Pleadings

Legal proceedings commence when a plaintiff makes a complaint or demand before a court in due form. Pleadings are written statements served on each party that set out, in summary form, the nature of the case and the material facts supporting the claim.

üéØ

Objects of Pleadings

  • ‚û§

    To ascertain the real dispute or issue between the parties.

  • ‚û§

    To narrow down the area of conflict.

  • ‚û§

    To preclude one party from taking the other by surprise.

  • ‚û§

    To prevent miscarriage of justice.

Formal Requirements (Order 2)

  • Structure: Divided into paragraphs numbered consecutively; each allegation in a separate paragraph.
  • Figures: Dates, sums, and numbers expressed in figures (Order 2 Rule 2(2)).
  • Material Facts Only: Brief summary of material facts relied upon, NOT the evidence to prove them (Order 2 Rule 3(1)).

‚úçÔ∏è Drafting the Plaint

The plaint identifies the parties, describes the factual basis for the lawsuit, makes a demand for relief, and establishes jurisdiction.

1. The Caption (Heading)

Identifies the court, parties, and case track.

Standard Plaint Header 
REPUBLIC OF KENYA
IN THE HIGH COURT AT NAIROBI
ENVIRONMENT AND LAND DIVISION
E.L.C. NO. ________ OF 2026

JOHN WHITE HEAD ................................. PLAINTIFF
-VERSUS-
KEY NORTH EAR ................................... DEFENDANT

PLAINT (FAST TRACK)

Case Tracks (Order 3 Rule 1)

  • Small Claims: Simple claim, 2 parties, value ‚â§ Kshs 49,999.
  • Fast Track: Undisputed facts/legal issues, few parties, likely concluded within 180 days.
  • Multi-Track: Complex facts/law, several parties, likely concluded within 240 days.

2. The Body

Must contain:

  1. Description of Parties: Including addresses for service.
  2. Cause of Action: Facts constituting the cause of action and when it arose.
  3. Jurisdiction: Facts showing the court has jurisdiction.
  4. Pending Suits: Averment that no other suit is pending or previously determined on the same subject matter (Sub judice / Res judicata).
  5. Notice of Intention to Sue: Statement that demand was made and not complied with.

3. The Prayer

Specific statement of relief sought (damages, specific performance, injunction, etc.). General damages and costs need not be explicitly asked for but it is practice to state specific relief.

4. Subscription & Verification

  • Signature of the plaintiff or advocate.
  • Verifying Affidavit: Sworn statement by the plaintiff confirming contents are true (Order 4 Rule 1(3)).

üîé Specific Pleadings & Particulars

Matters to be Specifically Pleaded (Order 2 Rule 4)

A party must plead specifically any matter which, if not pleaded, might take the opposing party by surprise or which raises issues of fact not arising out of preceding pleadings. Examples include:

Fraud
Limitation
Illegality
Performance

Particulars (Order 2 Rule 10)

Every pleading must contain particulars of any misrepresentation, fraud, breach of trust, willful default, or undue influence. If a party alleges a condition of the mind (malice, fraudulent intention), particulars must be given.

üí°

Request for Particulars

Before filing a defence, a defendant may request further and better particulars if the plaint is vague. This is done via notice (Form No. 2 Appendix A).

04

‚ö° Originating Summons (O.S.)

Used where the Civil Procedure Rules or specific statutes expressly provide. It is intended for a simpler, shorter, and speedier process where facts are generally not in dispute.

Key Features

  • Evidence is by way of Affidavit (no oral witnesses usually).
  • Questions for decision are raised directly in the summons.
  • Listed for directions within 30 days of filing.

Common Uses (Order 37)

Rule Subject Matter
Rule 3 Agreements for sale/purchase of immovable property (validity not in dispute).
Rule 4 Mortgages (Sale, foreclosure, redemption, reconveyance).
Rule 5 Caveats (Extension/Removal).
Rule 6 Extension of time under Limitation of Actions Act.
Rule 7 Adverse Possession claims.

üìë Other Applications

Notice of Motion (Order 51)

All applications to court are generally made by Notice of Motion heard in open court unless ordered otherwise.

  • Format: Concise statement of claim/relief. Evidence by affidavit.
  • Notice: Generally requires notice to the other party. Can be ex parte if delay would cause serious prejudice.
  • Uses: Summary Judgment, Stay of Proceedings, Injunctions, Judicial Review.

Chamber Summons

Used for applications heard in chambers (private), though in Kenya the distinction between chambers and open court business is often blurred.
