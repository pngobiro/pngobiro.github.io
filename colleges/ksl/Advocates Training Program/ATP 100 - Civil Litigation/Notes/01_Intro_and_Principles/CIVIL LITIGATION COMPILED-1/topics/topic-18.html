Every judgment takes effect immediately upon pronouncement. However, the court has the inherent power to stay execution if justice requires it. A stay prevents the decree holder from enforcing the judgment.

‚ö†Ô∏è

Notice of Appeal ‚â† Stay

The mere filing of a Notice of Appeal or an appeal itself does not operate as a stay. A specific order from the court is required.

Conditions for Granting Stay

The applicant must satisfy the court of the following conditions under Order 42 Rule 6(2):

  1. 1 Substantial Loss: The applicant will suffer substantial loss if the stay is not granted (rendering the appeal nugatory).
  2. 2 No Delay: The application must be made without unreasonable delay.
  3. 3 Security: The applicant must furnish such security as the court orders for the due performance of the decree.

Procedure

  • Informal Application: Can be made orally to the judge immediately after judgment is delivered.
  • Formal Application: By Notice of Motion (Order 51).
  • Appellate Court: If the High Court refuses a stay, the applicant can apply to the Court of Appeal (Rule 5(2)(b) Court of Appeal Rules).

Automatic Stays

Certain events act as an automatic stay:

  • Order for winding up a company.
  • Garnishee order absolute against a Judgment Debtor (JD).
  • Order for payment by installments.
02

üí∏ Payment in Installments

Under Order 22 Rule 12, the court may order the amount decreed to be paid by installments, with or without interest.

Key Rules

  • Application: By Judgment Debtor (JD) with sufficient cause shown.
  • Effect: The total debt ceases to be due at once; only the accrued installments are due. Execution cannot issue for the whole amount as long as installments are paid.
  • Consent: An order for installments after judgment generally requires the consent of the Decree Holder (DH), though the court has discretion on application.
‚ÑπÔ∏è

Rescission of Order

A JD cannot apply to rescind the order or reduce installments. Only the Decree Holder can move the court to rescind the order if they discover the JD has means to pay the debt at once.

03

ü§ö Objector Proceedings (Order 22 Rule 51)

When property is attached in execution, a third party (Objector) may claim that the property belongs to them and not the Judgment Debtor. These proceedings investigate such claims.

Procedure

  1. 1 Notice: Objector files a Notice of Objection.
  2. 2 Application: Files a Chamber Summons supported by affidavit within 7 days.
  3. 3 Stay: Court may stay execution for up to 14 days pending the hearing.
  4. 4 Creditor's Reply: Attaching creditor must intimate within 7 days if they intend to proceed. If not, attachment is raised.

What the Court Investigates

üîç

Focus: Possession vs. Title

  • ‚û§

    The primary question is who was in possession of the property at the time of attachment.

  • ‚û§

    If the Objector was in possession on their own account (not in trust for the JD), the court must release the property.

  • ‚û§

    Questions of legal title are generally not relevant unless they determine the nature of possession.

Outcome: If successful, the property is released. If failed, attachment proceeds. If complex questions of title arise, a separate suit (Rule 65) may be required.
