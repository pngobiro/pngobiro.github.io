üìä Pre-Trial Case Management

Pre-trial procedure is a conference between opposing counsel, under court supervision, to crystallize issues, eliminate non-controversial matters, and stipulate facts. Order 11 aims to further the expeditious disposal of cases.

Application

Order 11 applies to all suits except Small Claims (as defined under Order 3 Rule 1) or suits specifically exempted by the court.

The Case Management Timeline

Step Timing Action Required
1. Pre-Trial Questionnaire Within 10 days after close of pleadings Parties complete, file, and serve Appendix B.
2. Case Conference Within 30 days after close of pleadings Court convenes conference; Case Conference Order (Appendix C) made.
3. Settlement Conference Within 60 days (Fast Track) or 90 days (Multi-Track) of Case Conference Explore settlement; Exchange Briefs 7 days prior.
4. Trial Conference At least 30 days before hearing Plan trial time, witnesses; Sign Trial Conference Memorandum (Appendix E).

üó£Ô∏è The Three Conferences

‚ñ∂ 1. Case Conference (Rule 3)

The court deals with preliminary issues to ensure the trial proceeds without interruption. Agenda items include:

  • Identify contested and uncontested issues.
  • Consider consolidation of suits or identifying a test suit.
  • Make referral orders for Alternative Dispute Resolution (ADR).
  • Give orders for discovery, interrogatories, or commissions.
‚ñ∂ 2. Settlement Conference (Rule 5)

Convened to explore avenues for settlement or narrowing issues.

Settlement Conference Brief: Exchanged 7 days prior, containing:

  • Summary of facts (issues & admissions).
  • Summary of law to be relied upon.
  • Final list of witnesses and expert reports.
‚ñ∂ 3. Trial Conference (Rule 7)

Held 30 days before trial to:

  • Plan trial time.
  • Deal with admissions and amend pleadings.
  • Allow adduction of affidavit evidence.

Parties sign a Trial Conference Memorandum (Appendix E) which binds them.

Sanctions (Rule 7(2))

Willful failure to comply is a violation of the overriding objective (Sections 1A & 1B). The court may order costs against the defaulting party.

03

üîé Discovery

Discovery allows parties to obtain evidence from each other before trial. There are two main types:

1. Discovery of Facts (Interrogatories)

Questions administered in writing to the opposing party. Requires leave of court.

  • Purpose: To obtain admissions or impeach the opponent's case.
  • Allowed: If relevant to matters in issue and assists administration of justice.
  • Disallowed: If confidential, scandalous, irrelevant, or "fishing" expeditions.

2. Discovery of Documents

Disclosure on oath of all material documents in possession or power. Generally voluntary and automatic (parties agree on a discovery plan).

Legal Privilege (Section 134 Evidence Act)

Advocates are under a duty to keep client affairs confidential. Privileged information is exempt from discovery.

üö´

Exception to Privilege

If a client communicates with an advocate in furtherance of illegal acts, the advocate is under a duty to disclose such facts. Privilege does not cover illegality.
