üìë Types of Interim Orders

Interlocutory reliefs or interim orders are granted pending the determination of the main suit to preserve the status quo or ensure that the final judgment will not be rendered nugatory.

02

üëÆ Commissions, Arrest & Attachment

Order for Commission

Applied for via Chamber Summons to:

  • Examine witnesses who cannot attend court.
  • Make local investigations.
  • Examine accounts.
  • Hold scientific investigations.

Arrest Before Judgment (Order 39)

Generally, a creditor must obtain a decree before execution. However, arrest before judgment may be ordered if the defendant:

  • Is planning to leave the jurisdiction with intent to abscond liability.
  • Is about to leave Kenya under circumstances affording reasonable probability that the plaintiff will be obstructed in executing a decree.

Attachment Before Judgment (Order 39)

Where the defendant is disposing of their property or removing it from the court's jurisdiction to defeat the realization of a decree, the court may order attachment. This power is exercised cautiously as it interferes with property rights before judgment.

03

üëî Appointment of Receivers (Order 41)

The appointment of a receiver is an equitable relief, often drastic, as it takes away rights from both parties temporarily. A receiver is an impartial person appointed to collect and receive the rents, issues, and profits of land or personal estate.

‚ö†Ô∏è

Professional Indemnity

One is allowed to select a receiver with professional indemnity insurance. This ensures that if the receiver occasions loss through negligence or mismanagement, the parties can claim from the insurance.

04

üí∞ Security for Costs (Order 26)

This is money paid into court to ensure an unsuccessful plaintiff can satisfy an award of costs made against them. It protects the defendant from frivolous suits by plaintiffs who have no assets within the jurisdiction.

Grounds for Application

A defendant may apply for security for costs if:

Ground Description
Foreign Resident Plaintiff is ordinarily resident out of jurisdiction and has no substantial property within.
Nominal Plaintiff Plaintiff sues for benefit of another and cannot pay costs.
Address Issues Plaintiff's address is not stated, incorrectly stated, or changed to evade consequences.
Limited Company Reason to believe company assets will be insufficient to pay costs.

Case Law: Gatirau Peter Munya v Dickson Mwenda Kithinji & 2 others [2014] eKLR; John Lokitare Lodinyo v Mark Lomunokol & 2 others [2013] eKLR.

üö´

Consequence of Default

If the plaintiff fails to furnish security within the time fixed by the court, the suit shall be dismissed.
