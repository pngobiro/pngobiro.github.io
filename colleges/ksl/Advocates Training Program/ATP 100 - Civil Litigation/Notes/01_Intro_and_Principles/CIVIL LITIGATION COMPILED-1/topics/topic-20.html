üè¶ Garnishee Proceedings (Order 23)

Attachment of debts is a process enabling a Decree Holder (DH) to reach money due to the Judgment Debtor (JD) that is in the hands of a third person. This third person is called the Garnishee.

‚ÑπÔ∏è

The "Debt Due" Rule

To support a garnishee, there must be a debt due or accruing due (in praesenti). It is not sufficient to show a contingent liability or a debt that "may or may not" become due depending on a condition.

Procedure (Two Stages)

  1. 1 Stage 1: Order Nisi (Temporary)
    Application is made ex parte supported by an affidavit stating the debt exists and the garnishee is within jurisdiction. The court issues an Order Nisi attaching the debt.
  2. 2 Stage 2: Order Absolute (Final)
    The garnishee appears to "show cause" why they should not pay. If they admit the debt or fail to appear, the court makes the order Absolute, compelling payment to the DH.

Effect of the Order

  • Equitable Charge: Service of the Order Nisi creates an equitable charge. The garnishee cannot pay the debt to the JD without risking paying it twice.
  • Discharge: Payment made by the garnishee in compliance with the order is a valid discharge of their liability to the JD.
02

üèõÔ∏è Execution Against Government

Execution against the Government is restricted by the Government Proceedings Act and Order 29. Standard execution methods like attachment of property or arrest are NOT available against the Government.

üö´

Prohibited Orders (Order 29 Rule 2)

No order against the Government may issue under:

  • Order 14 Rule 4 (Impounding documents)
  • Order 22 (Execution/Attachment)
  • Order 23 (Garnishee/Attachment of debts)
  • Order 40 (Injunctions - Subject to recent constitutional interpretation)
  • Order 41 (Appointment of Receiver)

The Certificate Procedure

To enforce a money judgment against the Government:

  1. 1 Application: Apply to the Registrar for a Certificate of Satisfaction/Order against the Government (Form 22/23 Appendix A). This can be done ex parte.
  2. 2 Service: Serve the Certificate on the Attorney General and the Accounting Officer of the relevant ministry.
  3. 3 Payment: The AG writes to the accounts office instructing payment.
03

üî• Mandamus & Contempt

If the Certificate of Order is served but the Government official refuses to pay, the Decree Holder's remedy is Judicial Review seeking an order of Mandamus.

Mandamus Procedure

  • Leave: Apply for leave to seek Mandamus.
  • Motion: File Notice of Motion within 21 days of granting leave.
  • Service: Serve Attorney General and Accounting Officer (at least 8 clear days before hearing).
‚õìÔ∏è

Contempt of Court

If an order of Mandamus is granted and the officer is still obstinate, the court can invoke contempt proceedings. The officer may be committed to civil jail for non-compliance.

See: Republic v Permanent Secretary... ex-parte Nassir Mwandihi [2014] eKLR.
