üìÖ Issue & Validity of Summons

Upon filing a suit, summons shall issue to the defendant to appear and answer. The summons is prepared by the plaintiff (or advocate), filed with a copy of the plaint, and signed/sealed by the court within 30 days of filing.

Duration & Renewal (Order 5 Rule 2)

The 12-Month Rule

Summons shall be valid initially for 12 months from the date of issue. If not served within this time, the court may extend validity if it deems it just.

  • Extension Application: Must be made by filing an affidavit of service indicating attempts made.
  • Dismissal: If no application for extension is made, the court may dismiss the suit upon expiry of 24 months from the date of issue.
  • Enlargement of Time: The court has discretion to enlarge time (Order 50 Rule 6) even after expiry, but will generally not do so if the claim would be time-barred by limitations statutes.

üì¨ Modes of Service

Service is the formal delivery of legal documents. The general rule is personal service where practicable.

General Rules (Rules 6-11)

  • Personal: Delivering a duplicate to the defendant, who signs an acknowledgement on the original.
  • Agent: Service on an agent empowered to accept service (e.g., an advocate with instructions).
  • Adult Resident: Service on an adult member of the family residing with the defendant (Note: Service on a wife who refuses to sign or when the husband is known to be abroad may not be effective).

Service on Entities

Entity Method
Corporations Secretary, Director, or Principal Officer. Alternatively: leave at registered office, registered post to postal address.
Government Leave at AG's office or prepaid registered post to AG. Personal service is NOT required.

Affidavit of Service (Rule 15)

The serving officer must swear an affidavit (Form No. 4) stating time, manner, and the name/address of the person identifying the recipient. Failure to identify the witness renders the affidavit incurably defective.

03

üì¢ Substituted Service

Used when the defendant cannot be found or refuses service despite due diligence (Rule 14).

Methods of Substituted Service

  • Affixing a copy on the court-house notice board.
  • Affixing a copy on a conspicuous part of the defendant's last known residence or business.
  • Advertisement: In a newspaper (Order 5 Rule 17(4)).
Advertisement Format (Form 5 Appendix A) 
SUBSTITUTED SERVICE BY ADVERTISEMENT
To: Jameson Walker
P.O. Box 53, 00700 Nairobi

TAKE NOTICE that a plaint has been filed in the Milimani High Court at Nairobi... in which you are named as defendant. Service of summons has been ordered by this advertisement...

And further take notice that unless you enter an appearance within 21 days the case will be heard in your absence.
04

üåç Service Out of Jurisdiction

Service out of Kenya (Rule 21) requires leave of the court. It is allowed in specific circumstances, such as:

  • Subject matter is immovable property in Kenya.
  • Relief is sought against a person domiciled/resident in Kenya.
  • Contract made in Kenya or governed by Kenyan law.
  • Tort committed in Kenya.
  • Injunction sought for acts within Kenya.

Procedures for Service Abroad

Status of Defendant Document Served Procedure
Commonwealth Citizen Summons Sent via Ministry of Foreign Affairs to defendant's country.
Non-Commonwealth Notice of Summons Notice (not the summons itself) sent via diplomatic channels.

Case Law: Justus Nyaribo v Clerk to Nyamira County Assembly [2013] eKLR - It is an absurdity if a person duly appears in court and then seeks an order declaring that summons was not duly served. Appearance cures defective service.
