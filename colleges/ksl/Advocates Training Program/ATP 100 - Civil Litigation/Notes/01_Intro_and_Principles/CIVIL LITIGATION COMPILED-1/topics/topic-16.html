üìú Order of Proceedings

At the hearing, parties may appear in person or by an advocate/recognized agent. The trial typically begins with opening statements.

The Right to Begin

The general rule is that the plaintiff has the right to begin.

Exceptions

The defendant may have the right to begin if:

  • A preliminary objection has been filed.
  • The defendant admits the facts alleged but contends on a point of law.
  • Based on additional facts, the plaintiff is not entitled to the reliefs sought.
  • The court otherwise orders.

Sequence of Events

  1. 1 Plaintiff's Case: Plaintiff calls witnesses one by one. Each witness undergoes Examination-in-chief ‚ûî Cross-examination ‚ûî Re-examination. Plaintiff makes a final speech to close their case.
  2. 2 Defendant's Case: Defendant opens their case (if electing to give evidence). Witnesses are called and examined similarly.
  3. 3 Closing Submissions: Defendant makes a closing speech first, followed by the plaintiff's closing speech (oral or written).
  4. 4 Judgment: The court fixes a date for pronouncement of judgment.

üîç Evidence at Trial

Admissibility of evidence is governed by the Evidence Act (Cap. 80) and the Civil Procedure Act.

Key Principles

  • Relevancy (Section 5): Evidence is restricted to facts in issue and relevant facts.
  • Burden of Proof: "He who alleges must prove." The person adducing evidence must lay a foundation for it.
  • Admissions: Not conclusive proof but may operate as an estoppel.

Types of Evidence

Type Description
Oral Evidence Must be direct. Used to prove all facts except contents of documents.
Documentary Evidence Primary: The document itself (General rule).
Secondary: Copies (Allowed only where primary is unobtainable).

Under Section 48 of the Evidence Act, the court may seek the opinion of persons specially skilled when forming an opinion on:

  • Foreign law
  • Science or art
  • Identity/authenticity of handwriting or fingerprints

Procedure

Under Order 11 Rule 5(2), parties exchange a Settlement Conference Brief containing expert reports. If there is a dispute on reliability, the court may settle for a neutral expert.

‚öñÔ∏è

Customary Law (Case Law)

In Nyaribo Nyankomba v. Mary Bonarere Munge [2010] eKLR, it was held that experts versed in particular customs must be summoned to testify. In the absence of such expert testimony, a claim based on customary law remains unproved.

04

ü§ù Settlement

Parties can settle disputes out of court at any time. The Kenyan justice system encourages this to deliver justice quickly and reduce costs.

Recording Settlement

Where an agreement is reached:

  1. A consent is filed reflecting the agreement.
  2. The court records a Consent Judgment to close the matter.

Confidentiality of Settlements

üîí

Public Record Rule

Once a matter is in court, a settlement becomes part of the court record and generally cannot remain confidential. However, parties may agree to mark the matter as "Settled" without filing the details of the agreement, adopting the consent as a court order to keep specific terms private.
