Jurisdiction is Everything

Without jurisdiction, the court must "down tools" once it concludes it is lacking. (Milkah Nanyokia Masungo v. Robert Wekesa Mwembe & 2 Others [2013] eKLR).

The plaint in any action must contain allegations showing that the lawsuit is being filed in the proper court. Jurisdiction is generally categorized into two main types:

Type Definition Key Factor
Territorial Jurisdiction Refers to the court's power over events and persons within specific geographical bounds. Location of defendant / cause of action
Pecuniary Jurisdiction Refers to the maximum monetary value of cases a court can entertain. Value of the subject matter

The "Lowest Court" Rule (Section 11)

Generally, a suit shall be instituted in the court of the lowest grade competent to try it.

  • If this rule is not followed, the court may return the claim for presentation in the proper court.
  • Exception: Where a case involves an important point of law, it may be instituted in a court other than that of the lowest grade.

üó∫Ô∏è Determining Venue

The Civil Procedure Act (Cap 21) provides specific rules for where a suit should be instituted based on the nature of the claim.

1. Immovable Property (Section 12)

Suits involving land or immovable property must be instituted in the court within whose local limits the property is situated.

üè°

Multiple Jurisdictions

If the property is situated within the jurisdiction of different courts, the suit may be instituted in any court within whose local limits any portion of the property is situated.

2. General Suits (Section 15)

For personal injuries, breaches of contracts, or statutory obligations, the defendant must be within the jurisdiction. The suit may be filed where:

  1. The defendant actually and voluntarily resides, carries on business, or works for gain.
  2. The cause of action, wholly or in part, arises.

3. Multiple Defendants

Where there is more than one defendant residing in different jurisdictions:

  • The suit may be filed where any of the defendants resides or works.
  • Condition: Either leave of the court is given, OR the defendants who do not reside there acquiesce to the jurisdiction.
