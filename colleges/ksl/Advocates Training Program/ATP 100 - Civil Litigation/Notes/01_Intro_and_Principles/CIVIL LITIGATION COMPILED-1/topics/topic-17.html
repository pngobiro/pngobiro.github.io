Judgment & Decree

A Judgment is the final decision of the court on the facts of the case at the end of the entire procedure. It differs from an Order, which is usually an interlocutory decision.

Pronouncement (Order 21 Rule 1)

Judgment must be pronounced in open court either at once or within 60 days from the conclusion of the trial.

‚è±Ô∏è

The 60-Day Rule

If judgment is not given within 60 days, reasons must be forwarded to the Chief Justice and a date for judgment immediately fixed.

Form and Contents

A judgment in a defended suit must contain:

  • A concise statement of the case.
  • The points for determination (issues).
  • The decision thereon.
  • The reasons for such decision.
üö´

Delegation Prohibited

The award and calculation of damages is a judicial function. It cannot be delegated to a Deputy Registrar. A judgment must be complete and conclusive when pronounced (Kenya Revenue Authority v. Menginya Salim Murgini).

02

üè∑Ô∏è Special Types of Judgments

Ex Parte Judgment

Obtained by default (e.g., failure to file defence). It is not a judgment on merit and may be set aside by the court if the party shows sufficient cause.

Consent Judgment

Based on the agreement of parties. It acts as a contract and creates an estoppel. It is very difficult to set aside unless one can prove grounds that would rescind a contract (e.g., fraud, mistake, lack of authority).

  • Authority: A counsel with general instructions is deemed to have apparent authority to compromise the suit.
  • Restriction: Representatives (e.g., next friend of a minor) cannot consent to judgment without court approval.
03

üìú The Decree

A decree is the formal expression of adjudication which conclusively determines the rights of the parties. It is the technical translation of the judgment capable of execution.

Procedure for Extraction (Rule 8)

  1. 1 Drafting: Successful party prepares the draft decree.
  2. 2 Approval: Draft is sent to the other party for approval.
  3. 3 Signing: If approved (or if no objection within 7 days), the Registrar signs and seals it.
  4. 4 Settlement: If parties disagree, the decree is listed for "Settlement of Terms" before the judge.

Contents (Rule 7)

  • Suit number, names, and descriptions of parties.
  • Particulars of the claim.
  • Specification of relief granted.
  • Order on costs.
  • Date: Must bear the date the judgment was delivered.
04

üèóÔ∏è Special Forms of Decrees

Subject Matter Requirement
Immovable Property Must contain a description sufficient to identify it (boundaries, survey numbers).
Movable Property Must state the amount of money to be paid as an alternative if delivery cannot be had.
Payment of Money Court may order interest (Section 26) and/or payment by installments.
Recovery of Land May include possession, rent, or mesne profits (profits accrued while wrongfully held).

Preliminary Decree

Issued when further inquiries are needed before a final decree can be passed. Common in:

  • Administration Suits: Taking accounts of an estate.
  • Partnership Dissolution: Declaring shares and fixing dissolution date.
  • Partition: Declaring rights before physical division.
