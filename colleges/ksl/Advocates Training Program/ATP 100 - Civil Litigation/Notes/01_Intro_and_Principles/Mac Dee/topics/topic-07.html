üèõÔ∏è The Trial Process

1. Attendance and Hearing Dates

Parties must take a hearing date by invitation to the registry. If taken ex parte, a Hearing Notice must be served on the other party to prevent adjournments.

Consequences of Non-Attendance (Order 12):

Scenario Consequence
Neither Party Appears Suit dismissed.
Only Plaintiff Appears If notice proved: Ex parte hearing. If not proved: Second summons or adjournment.
Only Defendant Appears If liability denied: Suit dismissed. If admitted: Judgment on admission.

2. Order of Proceedings (Order 18)

Right to Begin: The Plaintiff generally has the right to begin. However, the Defendant begins if they admit the facts but contend the Plaintiff is not entitled to relief on points of law.

  • 1
    Opening Statement: Brief outline of the case and witnesses to be called. Not evidence.
  • 2
    Examination of Witnesses:
    • Examination-in-Chief: By the calling party. No leading questions allowed.
    • Cross-Examination: By the opposing party. Leading questions allowed. Tests credibility and facts.
    • Re-Examination: By the calling party. Clarifies points from cross-exam. No new matters.
  • 3
    Closing Speech/Submissions: Reconciling facts, law, and precedents to support prayers.
‚ö†Ô∏è

Hostile Witnesses

If a party's own witness gives evidence adverse to their case, they can apply to the court to declare the witness hostile. If granted, the party can cross-examine their own witness to impeach credibility.

02

üìú Judgment and Decree (Order 21)

Judgment

The final decision of the court on the facts. Must be pronounced in open court, dated, and signed. Once read, the court becomes functus officio (cannot alter it except for clerical errors under s.99).

Contents of a Judgment:

  1. Concise statement of the case.
  2. Points/Issues for determination.
  3. Decision on each issue with reasons.
  4. Final remedy/order.

Decree

The technical translation of the judgment capable of execution. It must align perfectly with the judgment.

  • Drafting: Usually prepared by the successful party and approved by the other party before being signed and sealed by the Registrar.
  • Contents: Suit number, names of parties, particulars of claim, relief granted, costs orders.
03

‚öñÔ∏è Execution of Decrees (Order 22)

Execution signifies the enforcement of the judgment. The court cannot execute on its own motion; the Decree Holder (DH) must apply for execution.

Modes of Execution (Section 38 CPA)

  • Delivery of Property: For specific movable or immovable property.
  • Attachment and Sale: Seizing property (via Prohibitory Order for land/shares or physical seizure for movables) and selling by public auction.
  • Attachment of Debts (Garnishee): Intercepting money owed to the Judgment Debtor by a third party (Garnishee).
  • Arrest and Detention: Committing the Judgment Debtor to civil jail (requires notice to show cause).
  • Appointment of Receiver: To manage income-generating property.
üì¢

Notice to Show Cause

Required before execution if:

  • More than 1 year has passed since the decree.
  • Execution is against a legal representative.
  • Execution is by attachment of salary.
  • Execution is by arrest and detention.

Garnishee Proceedings (Order 23)

A process to attach debts owed to the Judgment Debtor by a third party (Garnishee, e.g., a bank).

Two-Stage Process:

  1. Order Nisi: Temporary freeze. Garnishee is called to show cause why they shouldn't pay the DH.
  2. Order Absolute: If Garnishee admits debt or fails to appear, the order is made absolute, compelling payment to the DH.
04

üèõÔ∏è Execution Against Government

General Rule: No execution orders (attachment, arrest, injunction) can be issued against the Government (Section 21, Government Proceedings Act).

The Procedure: Satisfaction Orders

  1. 1
    Apply for a Certificate of Order against the Government (Form 22) after 21 days from the date of the order.
  2. 2
    Serve the Certificate on the Attorney General and the Accounting Officer of the relevant ministry.
  3. 3
    The Accounting Officer is legally obligated to pay.
  4. 4
    If they fail to pay, the remedy is Judicial Review (Mandamus) to compel the officer to perform their statutory duty.
Comparison of Enforcement Procedures
Subject vs Subject Subject vs Government
Attachment & Sale of Property Certificate of Order (Satisfaction)
Arrest & Detention Mandamus (to compel payment)
