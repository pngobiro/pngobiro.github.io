üå¨Ô∏è The "Oxygen Principle"

The Overriding Objective, often referred to as the "Double O" or "Oxygen Principle", is the foundational guide which courts must follow when exercising their civil jurisdiction. It fundamentally shifts the focus of litigation from technical compliance to substantive justice.

Giving effect to the overriding objective is the primary goal in any exercise of power under the Civil Procedure Act or under any other Act, rule, or common law principle.

üìú

Section 1A, Civil Procedure Act

(1) The overriding objective of this Act and the rules made hereunder is to facilitate the just, expeditious, proportionate and affordable resolution of the civil disputes governed by the Act.

(2) The Court shall, in the exercise of its powers under this Act or the interpretation of any of its provisions, seek to give effect to the overriding objective specified in subsection (1).

(3) A party to civil proceedings or an advocate for such a party is under a duty to assist the Court to further the overriding objective of the Act and, to that effect, to participate in the processes of the Court and to comply with the directions and orders of the Court.

This principle is similarly mirrored in Sections 3A and 3B of the Appellate Jurisdiction Act (Cap 9).

02

üèõÔ∏è Duty of the Court (Section 1B)

For the purpose of furthering the overriding objective, the Court is mandated to handle all matters presented before it with specific aims. Section 1B outlines these duties:

  • Just Determination: The fair resolution of the proceedings.
  • Efficient Disposal: Timely handling of the business of the Court.
  • Resource Management: Efficient use of available judicial and administrative resources.
  • Timely & Affordable: Disposal of proceedings at a cost affordable by the respective parties.
  • Technology: The use of suitable technology.
03

üìñ Constitutional Foundation

The Overriding Objective is enshrined in the Constitution of Kenya, 2010, specifically under Article 159.

üí°

Article 159(2)

In exercising judicial authority, the courts and tribunals shall be guided by the following principles:

  • (a) Justice shall be done to all, irrespective of status.
  • (b) Justice shall not be delayed.
  • (c) Alternative forms of dispute resolution (ADR) shall be promoted.
  • (d) Justice shall be administered without undue regard to procedural technicalities.
  • (e) The purpose and principles of this Constitution shall be protected and promoted.

Section 100: General Power to Amend

Even before the codification of the Oxygen Principle, courts had general powers to amend defects to ensure justice. Section 100 provides:

"The court may at any time, and on such terms as to costs or otherwise as it may think fit, amend any defect or error in any proceeding in a suit; and all necessary amendments shall be made for the purpose of determining the real question or issue raised by or depending on the proceeding."

‚öñÔ∏è Judicial Application & Case Law

Kantaria vs. Kantaria

The Court of Appeal discussed the "Oxygen Principle" regarding an application to strike out a notice of appeal for failure to serve all parties.

üë®‚Äç‚öñÔ∏è

Key Holding

The purpose of the principle is "To enable the court to take case management principles to the centre of the court process... so as to conduct the proceedings in a manner that makes attainment of justice fair, quick and cheap."

However, the court noted that the principle "is not a cure for all ills" and must be judicially ascertained. In this case, the court struck out the appeal because it is essential to have all parties before the court.

Hunker Trading Co Ltd Vs Elf Oil Kenya Ltd

The applicant sought a stay of execution after failing to comply with a High Court condition to deposit Ksh 5M. The Court of Appeal held:

  • The applicant, having failed to comply with the High Court order, was in breach of Section 1A(3) (duty to assist the court).
  • Abusing the court process by seeking to argue the same points without compliance violates the Oxygen Principle.
  • Waste of court resources is an abuse of process.

Application to Legal Practitioners

Under Section 1A(3), advocates have a duty to assist the Court to further the overriding objective. This includes:

  • Not deceiving the court.
  • Not withholding required information/documents.
  • Not abusing court processes or wasting time.
  • Not using dishonest tactics to hinder an opponent.
‚ö†Ô∏è

Conflict of Duties

A legal practitioner's primary duty is to the court. Where there is a conflict between the duty to the client and the overriding objective, the duty to the court prevails.
