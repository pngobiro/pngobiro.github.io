‚öñÔ∏è Constitutional Litigation

This refers to civil litigation with the aim of having a constitutional question resolved or enforcing the Bill of Rights.

Legal Basis

  • Article 23(1): Authority of the High Court to hear and determine applications for redress of a denial, violation or infringement of, or threat to, a right or fundamental freedom in the Bill of Rights.
  • Article 165(3)(b): Jurisdiction of the High Court to determine the question whether a right or fundamental freedom in the Bill of Rights has been denied, violated, infringed or threatened.
  • Procedure Rules: The Constitution of Kenya (Protection of Rights and Fundamental Freedoms) Practice and Procedure Rules, 2013 (Mutunga Rules).

Modes of Commencement

  1. 1
    Originating Petition: Filed at the beginning of the suit to enforce rights.
  2. 2
    Interlocutory Application: Filed in the course of other proceedings to determine a constitutional issue (other proceedings are usually stayed).

Important Requirement

The petition must be supported by an affidavit setting out the facts. This affidavit must be sworn by the Petitioner and NEVER by the advocate.

Contents of a Petition

The petition must explicitly state:

  • The Petitioner's name and description.
  • The designation of the Respondent(s).
  • The facts relied upon.
  • The specific constitutional provisions violated (Articles).
  • The nature of the injury caused or likely to be caused.
  • The relief sought.

Remedies Available (Article 23(3))

  • Declaration of rights.
  • Injunction.
  • Conservatory Order.
  • Declaration of invalidity of any law.
  • Order for compensation.
  • Order for Judicial Review.

üó≥Ô∏è Election Petitions

Guided by the Elections Act No. 24 of 2011 and the Elections (Parliamentary and County Elections) Petitions Rules, 2017.

Jurisdiction

Type of Election Court
Presidential Election Supreme Court (Exclusive jurisdiction)
Parliamentary (MP/Senate) & Governor High Court
Member of County Assembly (MCA) Resident Magistrate's Court

Grounds for Petition

  • Substantial non-compliance with election laws and regulations.
  • Commission of an election offence (e.g., bribery, undue influence, impersonation).
  • Lack of majority of lawful votes.
  • Irregularities affecting the result.

Parties

  • Petitioner: A person who voted, had a right to vote, or was a candidate at the election.
  • Respondent: The person whose election is complained of, and the Electoral Commission (IEBC) / Returning Officer.

Contents of an Election Petition

IN THE HIGH COURT OF KENYA AT NAIROBI
ELECTION PETITION NO. ... OF 20...

IN THE MATTER OF THE ELECTIONS ACT
AND
IN THE MATTER OF THE ELECTION FOR [CONSTITUENCY]

BETWEEN
[PETITIONER] ............................ PETITIONER
AND
[WINNING CANDIDATE] ..................... 1ST RESPONDENT
[RETURNING OFFICER] ..................... 2ND RESPONDENT
[IEBC] .................................. 3RD RESPONDENT

PETITION

The Humble Petition of [Name] showeth that:
1. The Petitioner was a candidate...
2. The election was held on...
3. The 1st Respondent was declared winner...
4. GROUNDS:
   (a) Bribery...
   (b) Violence...
   (c) Tallying errors...

WHEREFORE the Petitioner prays that:
(a) It be determined that the 1st Respondent was not duly elected.
(b) The election be declared null and void.
(c) Scrutiny and recount be ordered.
(d) Costs.

Dated this ... day of ... 20...
[Signed Petitioner]

Timelines

An election petition must be filed within 28 days after the date of publication of the results in the Gazette.

It must be heard and determined within 6 months of filing.
