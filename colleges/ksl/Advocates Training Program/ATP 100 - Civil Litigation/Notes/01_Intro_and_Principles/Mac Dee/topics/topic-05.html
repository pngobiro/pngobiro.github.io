Commencement of Suits & Pleadings

01

üë• Parties to a Suit (Order 1)

A "party" is a person who has commenced a proceeding, is served with summons, or has been added by court order. The Plaintiff is dominus litus (master of the suit) and chooses who to sue.

  • Joinder: Plaintiffs or Defendants may be joined in one suit if the right to relief arises out of the same act/transaction and a common question of law or fact would arise (Order 1, r 1 & 3).
  • Misjoinder/Non-joinder: A suit cannot be defeated solely by reason of misjoinder or non-joinder of parties (Order 1, r 9). The court can strike out wrong parties or join necessary parties (Order 1, r 10).
  • Representative Suits: Where numerous persons have the same interest in one suit, one or more may sue or be sued on behalf of all (Order 1, r 8). Requires court permission and notice to all concerned.

Capacity and Special Parties

Party Type Requirements
Minors / Mentally Incompetent Sue by a Next Friend; Defend by a Guardian ad litem (Order 32). Authority must be filed.
Government Sued through the Attorney General (Government Proceedings Act). Requires notice.
Corporations Sue/Sued in corporate name. Requires board resolution.
Partnerships Can sue/be sued in the Firm Name (Order 30).
Foreign Sovereigns/Diplomats Generally enjoy immunity unless waived or acting in a private commercial capacity.
02

üöÄ Modes of Commencement

1. The Plaint (Order 4)

The general mode of commencing a suit involving substantial disputes of fact.

  • Content: Court name, Suit No., Parties' descriptions, Cause of Action facts, Jurisdiction facts, Reliefs sought (Prayers), Pecuniary value.
  • Requirements: Divided into numbered paragraphs; Dates/Sums in figures.
  • Accompaniments: Verifying Affidavit (Order 4 r 1(2)), List of Witnesses, List of Documents, Witness Statements.
Structure of a Plaint 
REPUBLIC OF KENYA
IN THE HIGH COURT AT NAIROBI
CIVIL SUIT NO. [Year] OF [Year]

AB ........................................... PLAINTIFF
VERSUS
XY ........................................... DEFENDANT

PLAINT

1. The Plaintiff is... (Description & Address)
2. The Defendant is... (Description & Address)
3. On or about [Date], the Plaintiff... (Facts constituting cause of action)
4. (Particulars of Negligence/Fraud/Breach etc.)
5. The Plaintiff claims... (Special Damages)
6. The Court has jurisdiction...
7. Despite demand...

REASONS WHEREFORE the Plaintiff prays for judgment for:
(a) General Damages
(b) Special Damages of Kshs...
(c) Costs and Interest

Dated at Nairobi this ... day of ... 20...
[Advocate's Signature]

2. Originating Summons (Order 37)

A simplified procedure used where the facts are generally undisputed or the issues are simple/legal interpretation.

  • Evidence: By Affidavit (no witnesses usually).
  • Common Uses: Land disputes (Caveats, Adverse Possession), Mortgage foreclosure, Administration of Estates, advocate-client costs.
  • Process: Filed in court -> Registrar lists for directions within 30 days.

3. Petition

Used where specifically prescribed by statute.

  • Constitutional Petitions: Alleged violation of Bill of Rights.
  • Election Petitions: Challenging validity of elections.
  • Matrimonial Causes: Divorce.
  • Company Winding Up.

4. Notice of Motion (Order 51)

Primarily for interlocutory applications but can commence proceedings where statute provides (e.g., Judicial Review leave, Habeas Corpus).

‚úçÔ∏è Rules of Pleadings (Order 2)

Pleadings are written statements of parties served on each other, setting out material facts to narrow issues.

General Rules:

  • Material Facts Only: Plead facts, not evidence, and not law (Order 2 r 3).
  • Specific Pleading: Fraud, Libel, Misrepresentation, etc., must be specifically pleaded with particulars.
  • Signature: Must be signed by the advocate or the party (Order 2 r 16).
  • Deemed Admission: Allegations not specifically denied (traversed) are deemed admitted (except damages) (Order 2 r 11).
‚ö†Ô∏è

Affidavits are NOT Pleadings

An affidavit is evidence on oath. While it supports applications, it is technically not a "pleading" under the definition in the Civil Procedure Act.

Responding to Pleadings (Defence)

A defendant has 14 days after appearance to file a defence (Order 7 r 1).

Types of Responses:

  1. 1
    Traverse/Denial: "The Defendant denies paragraph 3..." (Must be specific, not general).
  2. 2
    Confession and Avoidance: Admit facts but add new facts to avoid legal consequence (e.g., "I hit him, but it was self-defence").
  3. 3
    Objection in Point of Law: E.g., Suit is time-barred.
  4. 4
    Set-off: Defendant claims Plaintiff owes them money (liquidated sum).
  5. 5
    Counter-claim: A cross-action by Defendant against Plaintiff (treated as a separate suit).
04

üì® Issue & Service of Summons (Order 5)

Once a suit is filed, summons must be signed/sealed within 30 days and collected for service. Validity: 12 months (renewable).

Modes of Service:

  1. 1
    Personal Service: Best practice. Delivered to defendant or authorized agent.
  2. 2
    Substituted Service: If defendant cannot be found after "reasonable diligence". Requires court order. Methods: Advertisement, affixing on door.
  3. 3
    Service on Corporations: Secretary, Director, Principal Officer, or Registered Post to registered office.
  4. 4
    Service on Government: Attorney General (State Law Office).
  5. 5
    Service out of Jurisdiction: Requires leave of court (Order 5 r 21). E.g., Subject matter is land in Kenya, Tort committed in Kenya.

Return of Service: The process server must file an Affidavit of Service detailing time, manner, and identification of the person served.

05

‚öñÔ∏è Appearance & Default (Order 10)

Entering Appearance

The Defendant files a Memorandum of Appearance within the time stipulated in the summons.

  • Unconditional Appearance: Admits jurisdiction, intends to defend.
  • Conditional Appearance: Objects to jurisdiction or regularity of service.

Default Judgment

If Defendant fails to appear or file defence:

Claim Type Consequence
Liquidated Demand (Specific sum) Final Judgment entered immediately for the sum + interest + costs.
Unliquidated Demand (Damages) Interlocutory Judgment entered on liability. Suit set down for formal proof/assessment of damages.

Note: Default judgment against the Government requires leave of court.

06

‚úèÔ∏è Amendment of Pleadings (Order 8)

Pleadings can be amended to ensure the real issues are determined (Cropper v Smith).

  • Without Leave: Once before pleadings are closed.
  • With Leave: After pleadings close. Application by Chamber Summons.
  • Principles: Allowed if good faith, no injustice to other party (compensatable by costs), and necessary to determine real questions.
  • Format: Deleted text struck through in Red, new text Underlined in Red.
07

üîó Third Party & Interpleader

Third Party Proceedings (Order 1 r 15)

Used when a Defendant claims contribution or indemnity from a person not party to the suit.

  • Procedure: Defendant applies for leave to issue a Third Party Notice (ex parte Chamber Summons).
  • Effect: Third party becomes a party to the suit with same rights to defend.

Interpleader Proceedings (Order 34)

Used when a person (Applicant) is in possession of property/money claimed by two or more adverse parties (Claimants) and claims no interest in it (except costs).

  • Objective: Applicant seeks court's direction on who to pay/deliver to and indemnification.
  • Commencement: By Originating Summons (if no pending suit) or Notice of Motion (in pending suit per s.58 CPA).
