Judicial Review Procedure

üìö Order 53 & Prerogative Orders
‚è±Ô∏è 60 min read
üìù Quiz Available
01

üèõÔ∏è Nature & Scope

Judicial Review (JR) is the process by which the High Court exercises jurisdiction over proceedings and decisions of tribunals, inferior courts, and other bodies performing public functions. It is not an appeal on merits but a review of the decision-making process.

Legal Basis:

  • Article 23(3)(f) of the Constitution
  • Sections 8 & 9 of the Law Reform Act (Cap 26)
  • Order 53 of the Civil Procedure Rules

Grounds for Judicial Review

  • Ultra Vires: Acts beyond the scope of power or authority granted by law (Substantive or Procedural).
  • Breach of Natural Justice: Failure to be heard (audi alteram partem) or bias (nemo judex in causa sua).
  • Abuse of Power: Using power for improper purposes, fettering discretion, or failing to exercise power.
  • Unreasonableness / Irrationality: Decisions so unreasonable that no reasonable authority could have made them (Wednesbury unreasonableness).
  • Legitimate Expectation: Where a public body has made a promise or established a practice that an individual relied upon.
02

üõ°Ô∏è Prerogative Orders

Order Function
Certiorari A quashing order. It brings a decision of an inferior court/tribunal/body to the High Court to be quashed (cancelled).
Prohibition Restrains a public authority or inferior tribunal from continuing proceedings in excess of jurisdiction or contravention of law.
Mandamus "We command". Compels a public authority to perform a peremptory statutory duty they have wrongfully failed to do.

Injunctions in JR

Strictly speaking, injunctions are private law remedies. Judicial Review deals with public law. However, stay of proceedings (operating like an injunction) can be granted at the leave stage.

03

üìú The Two-Stage Procedure

Judicial Review is unique because it requires permission (leave) from the court before the substantive case can be filed. This filters out frivolous applications.

Stage 1: Application for Leave

Mode: Ex Parte Chamber Summons.

Requirements for Leave:

  1. 1
    Prima Facie Case: The applicant must show an arguable case, not just mere dissatisfaction. (Hirji Transport Services v R; Mirugi Kariuki v AG).
  2. 2
    Locus Standi: The applicant must have "sufficient interest" in the matter. Not just a busybody.
  3. 3
    Time Limitation:
    • Certiorari: Must be applied for within 6 months of the decision complained of (Order 53 r 2).
    • Mandamus/Prohibition: No specific limit in rules, but unreasonable delay (laches) can lead to dismissal.
‚è∏Ô∏è

Stay of Proceedings

Under Order 53 Rule 1(4), when granting leave, the judge can direct that the grant of leave operates as a stay of the proceedings in question until the substantive motion is determined.

Stage 2: Substantive Motion

If leave is granted, the actual judicial review process begins.

  • Mode: Notice of Motion.
  • Timeline: Must be filed within 21 days of the grant of leave.
  • Service: Must be served on all persons directly affected. There must be at least 8 clear days between service and the hearing date.
  • Documents: Copies of the Statement and Affidavits used in the leave application must be attached. No new grounds can be raised without amendment.
04

üìÑ Necessary Documents

1. The Statutory Statement (Order 53 r 1(2))

Sets out:

  • Name and description of the applicant.
  • The relief sought (Certiorari, Mandamus, etc.).
  • The grounds on which the relief is sought.

2. Verifying Affidavit

Verifies the facts relied upon in the statement. Must be sworn by the applicant. Failure to file this renders the application incompetent.

3. Chamber Summons (Ex Parte)

The formal application document for the Leave Stage.

4. Notice of Motion

The document commencing the Substantive Stage (Inter Partes).
