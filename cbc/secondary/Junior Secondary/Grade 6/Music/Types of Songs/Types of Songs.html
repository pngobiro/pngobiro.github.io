Types of Songs

Western Art Songs

Western art songs are European or western folk songs that have been arranged for solo or choral performance. They are based on the western/European style of performance, written in verses, and often tell stories or describe the beauty of nature.

When performing western art songs, it is important to bring out the correct mood through facial expressions, gestures, observing performance directions, and maintaining the appropriate tempo or speed.

Here is an example of a western art song:

African Art Songs

African art songs are African folk songs or African songs that have been rearranged for solo or choral performances. They can be accompanied by African or western musical instruments and are performed in an African style, using African rhythms and languages.

African art songs are based on African traditions.

Patriotic Songs

Patriotic songs are sung in praise of a country and its leaders. They emphasize patriotism, peace, unity, love, pride in our country, and social cohesion among citizens.

The words in patriotic songs may talk about a nation's history, people, resources, sceneries, and values.

Examples of Patriotic Songs

  • Kenya, Kenya Taifa Leo
  • Tunajivunia kenya nchi yetu,
  • Tushangilie kenya.

Religious / Sacred Songs

Religious or sacred songs are performed by different religious groups (e.g., Christianity, Islam, and Hinduism) specifically for the purpose of worship, praise, and prayer to a divine being.

Topical Songs

Topical songs address issues affecting society. They convey special messages on pertinent and contemporary issues and offer advice on how society can address various concerns.

Some of the issues that affect society and can be covered in topical songs include:

  • Safety
  • Personal hygiene
  • Health and nutrition
  • Importance of education
  • Environmental conservation
  • Drought/famine
  • Disease
  • Water and sanitation
  • Children's rights

Examples of Topical Songs

  • Corona ni hatari
  • Wash your hands
  • Njoo twende shule
