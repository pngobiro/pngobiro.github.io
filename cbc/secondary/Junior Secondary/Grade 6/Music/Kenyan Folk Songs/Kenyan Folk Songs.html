Kenyan Folk Songs

Types of Kenyan Folk Songs

Folk songs are traditional songs performed during different occasions by different communities. They are performed by common people of a region in a local language. There are different types of folk songs:

  • Naming folk songs
  • Worship folk songs
  • Marriage folk songs
  • Initiation folk songs
  • Work folk songs
  • Funeral folk songs
  • Solo folk songs

A. Naming Folk Songs

  • Are performed to celebrate the birth of a child.
  • They welcome a new member to the family and society at large.
  • Performed as a way of thanksgiving to God for a new life.
  • Are sung to congratulate the parents for bringing a new life to the world.
  • They educate the parents on how to take care of the newborns.
  • Example: "Mwana wa Mberi" from the Luhya community

B. Worship Folk Songs

  • Are performed during religious ceremonies in different places of worship.
  • They are used to educate believers about God, praise God, entertain the worshippers, and encourage and give hope to the worshippers.
  • Example: "Roho Maler"

Diction in Folk Songs

Diction refers to the proper pronunciation of words when singing. To get good diction, you should:

  • Pay attention to the vowels and consonants in the words
  • Pronounce all the endings of the words in the folk songs
  • Have good breath control during singing

Features of Folk Songs

Folk songs have the following features:

  • Mood
  • Climax
  • Structure
  • Vocal ornamentation
  • Message

Importance of Performing Folk Songs from Diverse Communities in Kenya

Performing folk songs from diverse communities in Kenya is important for the following reasons:

  • It helps in cultural preservation
  • Promotes national unity
  • It is a source of employment
  • Attracts tourists
  • Folk songs entertain the audience
  • Educate people on moral and values
