Eastern African Vegetation

Introduction

Vegetation refers to the plant life that grows in a particular region. It encompasses trees, bushes, grass, and shrubs. Natural vegetation grows without human intervention, while planted vegetation is cultivated by humans.

Types of Vegetation in Eastern Africa

  • Tropical Rainforest
  • Savannah Woodland
  • Savannah Grassland
  • Mountain
  • Swamp
  • Desert
  • Semi-desert
  • Mangrove

Characteristics of Main Vegetation Types

Tropical Rainforest Vegetation

Found in the equatorial climatic region, tropical rainforests are characterized by:

  • Tall trees forming canopies
  • Evergreen vegetation
  • Straight tree trunks with broad leaves and buttress roots
  • Dense and thick forests
  • Predominantly hardwood trees (e.g., oak, teak, mahogany)

Savannah Woodland Vegetation

Savannah woodlands are dominated by trees and characterized by:

  • Medium-height trees with wide spacing (scattered)
  • Deciduous trees
  • Tall grass growing between trees
  • Umbrella-shaped treetops
  • Common trees include acacia and baobab
  • Deep-rooted trees with thick bark

Savannah Grassland Vegetation

Savannah grasslands are dominated by grass with few scattered trees and characterized by:

  • Drought-resistant vegetation
  • Grass growing up to 2 meters tall (e.g., elephant grass)
  • Scattered trees with thick bark and thorny leaves
  • Main trees include baobab, acacia, cacti, and euphorbia

Swamp Vegetation

Found in swampy regions, along rivers, and shores of small lakes, swamp vegetation thrives in poorly drained areas. Main vegetation includes:

  • Papyrus reeds
  • Water lilies

Mangrove Vegetation

Growing in muddy, salty waters along the shores of the Indian Ocean, mangrove vegetation is characterized by:

  • Trees with breathing roots (aerial roots)
  • Predominantly hardwood, evergreen, and medium-height trees

Mountain Vegetation

Mountain vegetation, also called alpine or montane vegetation, grows on mountain slopes and varies with altitude. It is mainly influenced by altitude and ranges from:

  • Bare rocks
  • Health and moorland
  • Bamboo
  • Rainforest
  • Savannah

Semi-desert Vegetation

Found in areas with rainfall between 250mm and 500mm, semi-desert vegetation is characterized by:

  • Short, scanty, thorny bushes
  • Short, tough, and scattered patches of grass
  • Drought-resistant plants with modified thorns (e.g., cactus, baobab, euphorbia)

Desert Vegetation

Found in areas with rainfall less than 250mm, desert vegetation is characterized by:

  • Stunted plants with thorny or needle-like leaves (e.g., cactus)
  • Large sections of bare ground
  • Plants with thick, fleshy stems and deep roots
  • Spiky, scanty grass
