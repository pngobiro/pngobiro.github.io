Effects of Migration & Population Distribution in Eastern Africa

Effects of the Migration and Settlements of Selected Language Groups in Eastern Africa

  • Displacement of smaller communities (e.g., Agikuyu, Abagusii, Bantu)
  • Conflicts between communities (e.g., Abagusii & Luo, Galla & Bantu)
  • Population increase
  • Intermarriage between communities
  • Adoption of economic practices (e.g., Bantu learning cattle keeping)
  • Introduction of new trade items
  • Development of new languages through borrowed words
  • Borrowing of cultural practices (e.g., circumcision, age-set systems)

Unity of Language Groups in Eastern Africa

  • Intermarriage
  • Trade activities
  • Education for all
  • Cultural interaction
  • Living peacefully

Population Distribution in Eastern Africa

Population distribution is uneven, with areas that are:

  • Sparsely populated
  • Medium/moderately populated
  • Densely populated

Factors Influencing Population Distribution:

  • Climate (rainfall and temperature)
  • Soils
  • Relief
  • Drainage
  • Economic activities
  • Vegetation
  • Government policy
  • Pests and diseases
  • Political factors (security)

Population Density in Eastern Africa

Densely Populated Areas:

  • Highlands
  • Lake basin
  • Major towns and cities
  • Coastal areas
  • Rwanda
  • Mining areas

Sparsely Populated Areas:

  • North Eastern Uganda
  • Central and Northern Tanzania
  • Parts of Ethiopia, Eritrea, Kenya, Somalia, Sudan
  • Mountain tops
  • Flood plains

Effects of High Population Density

Negative Effects:

  • Food shortage
  • Low living standards and slums
  • Inadequate social amenities
  • Land fragmentation

Positive Effects:

  • Attracts investments
  • Increases tax collection
  • Big markets for products
  • Cheap labor
