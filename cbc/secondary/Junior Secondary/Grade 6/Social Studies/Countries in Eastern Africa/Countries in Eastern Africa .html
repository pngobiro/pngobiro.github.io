Countries in Eastern Africa

Eastern Africa is the region located in the eastern region of Africa.

It is made up of eleven independent countries, namely:

  • Sudan
  • Ethiopia
  • Tanzania
  • Somalia
  • South Sudan
  • Kenya
  • Uganda
  • Eritrea
  • Burundi
  • Rwanda
  • Djibouti

NB:

  • Sudan is the largest country in eastern Africa.
  • South Sudan became independent in July 2011.
  • Djibouti is the smallest country in eastern Africa.
  • Countries without a coastline are called landlocked, e.g., Burundi, Uganda, Rwanda, Ethiopia, and South Sudan.

Position Of Countries Of Eastern Africa

Using a compass, you can locate the position of a country in relation to its neighbors.

Kenya lies to the:

  • Northeast of Tanzania
  • South of Ethiopia
  • Southeast of South Sudan
  • East of Uganda
  • West of Somalia

The immediate neighbors of Kenya are Tanzania, Uganda, South Sudan, Ethiopia, and Somalia.

Locating Places On A Map Using Latitudes And Longitudes

Eastern Africa lies within latitudes 22°N and 12°S and between longitudes 22°E and 51°E/52°E.

Latitudes

Latitudes are imaginary lines that run from west to east. They are also called parallels.

The main line of latitude is the equator at 0°.

The equator divides the earth into two equal halves called hemispheres.

Characteristics Of Latitudes

  • They are parallel.
  • They are of different lengths.
  • They affect climate.
  • They are measured in degrees from the equator.
  • There are 180 lines of latitudes in total.

Latitudes lines are:

  • Equator at 0°
  • The Tropic of Cancer at 23½°N
  • The Arctic Circle at 66½°N
  • The Tropic of Capricorn at 23½°S
  • The Antarctic Circle at 66½°S

Longitudes

Longitudes are imaginary lines that run from the North Pole to the South Pole of the earth. They are also called meridians or horizontals.

The main line of longitude is the Greenwich meridian at 0°.

The Greenwich meridian is also called the prime meridian.

The prime meridian passes through the Greenwich town in London and Accra in Ghana.

Characteristics Of Longitudes

  • They run from north to south.
  • They are not parallel.
  • They meet at the poles.
  • They affect time.
  • They are of the same length.
  • They are measured in degrees east or west of the prime meridian.
  • There are 360° lines of longitudes in total.
