Pictorial Composition

Montage Pictorial Composition

Montage is a picture composition that is created by cutting and joining images that relate to each other.

The cut-out pictures used to create a montage are glued on a surface known as the base or mounting surface. The base used should be flat, such as:

  • Manila paper
  • Cardboard
  • Hardboard
  • Plywood
  • Or any other flat surface

Characteristics of a Montage

  • In a montage, pictures are arranged in a manner that they can overlap each other. Overlapping means one picture is glued on top of the other.
  • Pictures used to make a montage can be sourced from old newspapers, old magazines, calendars, or downloaded and printed images from the internet.
  • The pictures used are usually related so as to tell the same story or communicate a particular message.
  • Cut-out text can also be used to help a montage communicate even better.

Spacing and Balancing Forms in a Montage Composition

Arranging cut-out images on the base before pasting them will help you achieve balance when creating a montage.

You can choose to arrange the picture cut-outs side by side or overlap them.

In montage, overlapping involves putting one picture cut-out on top of another in a way that allows the picture at the back to be partly visible.

When overlapping, let the small picture cut-outs be glued on top of the big picture cut-outs.

Ensure proper use of space by using more than half of the mounting surface. Good use of space helps create balance and a center of interest for the montage.

When balancing objects, arrange them in a way that they communicate well with each other to tell a story or send a message.

Creating a Montage Composition

  1. Collect the suggested tools and materials: base/adhesive/razor/scissors/pictures.
  2. Pick the subject matter for your montage.
  3. Cut out the pictures you need for your montage.
  4. Arrange the picture cut-outs on the base.
  5. Mark the position of each picture to avoid disarranging when you will be pasting.
  6. Apply glue to the back side of each picture cut-out and then paste it onto the base.
  7. Add more details to your montage by making cut-outs of other things like grass.
  8. You can trim parts of the cut-outs that are outside the mounting surface.
  9. Mount your work.

Appreciating Each Other's Work

The teacher can appreciate pupils' work by:

  • Displaying good pieces of work
  • Showing personal enthusiasm
  • Evident interest in what learners are doing
  • Discussing and judging good work
  • Personal enthusiasm by other learners
