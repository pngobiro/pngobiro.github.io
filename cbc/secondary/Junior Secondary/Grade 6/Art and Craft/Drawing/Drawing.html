Introduction

Picture making involves the application of various methods such as painting, drawing, collage, and montage.

Drawing is the process of making pictures using lines and shades.

Stippling technique involves using dots to create value and make an object look solid while on a drawing surface.

Creating Light And Dark Effect Using The Stippling Techniques

The stippling technique uses dots to create value and make an object look solid while on a drawing surface.

In stippling, areas that are light have a small number of dots, and areas that are dark have many dots.

Tonal value is the darkness and lightness of the color of an object.

When stippling, the number of dots should gradually change from one area to another. Gradually means that the dots should not change suddenly but slowly from one step to the next.

Creating A Dark Effect On Two Overlapped Forms By Stippling

When two or more objects are placed or drawn in a way that they appear to be covering or blocking each other's parts, the objects are said to be overlapping.

When overlapping objects:

  • The large objects should be placed at the back â€“ background, while the small objects in front - foreground.
  • The objects in front should be drawn at a lower position than the ones at the back.
  • Objects closer to you are the ones in front, and those far from you are the ones at the back.
  • Objects in the middle are said to be at the middle ground.
  • The objects that overlap are always separated by light and dark effect.

Pot/gourd/calabash

Imaginative Composition

Imaginative drawing is a drawing made from an idea that is in your mind, not what you see.

Creation of textural effect by stippling technique

In the stippling technique, dots can be used to show the surface quality of an object.

Texture is the quality of a surface. Texture is used to show how an object would feel when touched.

The surface with many dots is considered to be rough, while the surface with few dots is considered to be smooth.

Creating Visual Balance When Drawing Several Forms

Balance is the arrangement of objects in a space within a picture composition.

Balance makes an artwork more attractive. It makes the artwork more stable and more comfortable to look at.

When balancing objects, we should place them at the center of our space and make them large enough to occupy at least more than half of our space.

We can balance objects or forms using:

  • Colors
  • Lines
  • Shapes
  • Texture
  • Tonal value

There are three ways of creating balance:

  1. Placing objects at the center of the drawing space, so that the left part and the right part of the composition look equal.
  2. Arranging forms in a way that the left and the right side of your composition are not equal.
  3. Arranging forms in an imaginative manner.

Drawing An Imaginative Composition Of A Twig With Two Leaves And A Fruit

  1. Collect the suggested tools and materials: pencil/pen/rubber/picture of a real twig with two leaves and a fruit.
  2. Place a picture of a real twig with two leaves and a fruit on a flat surface.
  3. Use a ruler to draw the drawing space you will use in your drawing book. Draw an outline shape of the objects. Use dotted lines to show where the objects are overlapping.
  4. Use a rubber to erase parts or lines which have been overlapped. Reduce the darkness of your outline using a rubber.
  5. Choose the direction you want to have more light and the direction you want less light.
  6. Apply more dots on the dark areas and fewer dots on the light areas. Let the dots fade gradually.
  7. Identify the sharp edges, corners, and holes on the twig and fill them with more dots to create texture.
