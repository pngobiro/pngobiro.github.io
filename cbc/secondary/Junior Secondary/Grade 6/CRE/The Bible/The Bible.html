The Bible

The Bible As The Inspired Word Of God

According to 2 Timothy 3:16-17, the Bible is a collection of Holy Books and is considered the inspired word of God.

Why the Bible is the inspired word of God

The Bible contains the word of God, which is used for:

  • Teaching the truth
  • Rebuking wrongs
  • Correcting faults
  • Giving instructions for right living

The Bible is divided into two testaments: the Old Testament and the New Testament. The Old Testament has 39 books, while the New Testament has 27 books, making a total of 66 books. The Bible was written by people who were inspired by God.

The Authors Who Were Inspired To Write The Old And The New Testaments

The Bible was written by different authors who were inspired by the Holy Spirit.

Old Testament Authors

The first five books of the Old Testament, known as the Pentateuch, were written by Moses:

  • Genesis (meaning "origin" or "beginning")
  • Exodus (meaning "coming out" or "departure")
  • Leviticus (explaining rules and regulations for priests)
  • Numbers (meaning "census")
  • Deuteronomy

Reasons That Lead To Translation Of The Bible Into Local Languages

The Old Testament was originally written in Hebrew, before Jesus Christ came to exist physically. It was later translated into Greek and other languages.

The New Testament was originally written in Greek, after Christ.

The Bible was translated into local languages to help missionaries spread the word of God easily.

Advantages Of Translating The Bible Into Various Local Languages

  • Bible translation enabled missionaries to communicate the word of God in languages that local people could understand.
  • The translation of the Bible enabled missionaries to learn local languages.
  • The gospel reached more people because the language of communication was familiar.
  • More Africans learned to read and write, allowing them to read the Bible for themselves.
  • Education was promoted by the translation work of the Bible.
