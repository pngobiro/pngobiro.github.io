Light Energy: Reflection

Reflection of Light

Reflection of light occurs when light falls on a surface and the light rays bounce back.

Reflection of Light on Different Materials

  • Reflection of light takes place when light falls on a shiny, smooth opaque surface.
  • Examples of opaque materials that cause reflection include mirrors and smooth shiny surfaces.

Regular Reflection

If a ray of light falls on a smooth, shiny opaque surface, the reflection will occur in one direction. This type of reflection is called regular reflection.

Irregular Reflection

If a ray of light falls on a rough, shiny opaque surface, the reflection will occur in different directions. This type of reflection is called irregular reflection.

Application of Reflection of Light in Daily Life

  1. A microscope uses a mirror to reflect light to the specimen under the microscope.
  2. Car mirrors enable the driver to see things behind the cars.
  3. Reflection enables one to check how they look.
  4. Reflection facilitates the dentist checking in your mouth.
  5. Reflection is used when making a periscope. A periscope is used to observe objects around corners and by submarines to observe vessels in the sea.
