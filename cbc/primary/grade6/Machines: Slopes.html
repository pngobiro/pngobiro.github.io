Machines: Slopes

Understanding Inclined Planes

Any flat surface can be referred to as a plane. An inclined plane, like a slope, is an example of a plane. It makes work easier by reducing the effort required to perform a certain task.

Examples of Inclined Planes

  • Ramps
  • Ladders
  • Staircases
  • Roads winding uphill

Examples of Inclined Planes

1. Staircase

A staircase makes it easier for a person to move to another part of a building or a room.

2. Ladder

Ladders are used in construction sites to move to a higher level, reducing the effort when climbing.

3. Ramp

Ramps are flat planes used when moving from a lower point to an upper part. They are necessary in most buildings because they facilitate easy movement for physically challenged people to access the upper parts.

Important Tips

  • Machines make work easier.
  • A ladder is a simple machine that makes work easier.
  • A ladder belongs to a group of machines called slopes.
