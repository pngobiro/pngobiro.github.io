Reproductive System & Adolescence

Functions of Parts of the Reproductive System

The reproductive system is responsible for creating new life. It's made up of different parts in both males and females, and each part has a special job to do.

Male Reproductive Parts

  • Penis

    The penis delivers sperm to the female's reproductive system. It also releases urine from the body.

  • Urethra

    This tube runs inside the penis and carries both sperm and urine out of the body.

  • Testes

    These two small organs produce sperm, the male reproductive cells.

  • Sperm duct

    This tube carries sperm from the testes to the urethra.

  • Cowper's Glands

    These glands produce a fluid that helps sperm swim and survive.

When sperm mixes with this fluid, it's called semen.

Female Reproductive Parts

  • Vagina

    The vagina is also called the birth canal. It receives sperm from the penis and is the passageway for a baby to be born.

  • Ovaries

    These two organs produce eggs, the female reproductive cells. They also produce hormones that control the menstrual cycle.

  • Fallopian tubes

    These tubes carry eggs from the ovaries to the uterus. This is where fertilization happens if an egg meets a sperm.

  • Uterus

    The uterus, also called the womb, is where a baby grows and develops if an egg is fertilized.

  • Cervix

    This is a strong muscle that connects the vagina to the uterus.

Both the male and female reproductive systems are essential for creating new life. Each part plays a vital role in the process of reproduction.

Physical Changes During Adolescence

Adolescence is a time when your body goes through many changes as you transition from a child to an adult. These changes can be exciting, but they can also be confusing or even a little scary. Here are some of the physical changes you might experience:

Changes in Girls

  • Breasts start to develop and grow.
  • Hips become wider.
  • You start your period (menstruation).
  • You might get pimples or acne.
  • Hair grows under your arms and in your pubic area.
  • You grow taller and gain weight.

Changes in Boys

  • Your voice gets deeper.
  • Hair grows on your face, chest, under your arms, and in your pubic area.
  • Your shoulders become broader.
  • You might get pimples or acne.
  • You grow taller and gain weight.
  • You might experience wet dreams (nocturnal emissions).

Remember, everyone goes through these changes at their own pace. If you have any questions or concerns, don't hesitate to talk to a trusted adult like your parent, doctor, or school nurse.
