Composition of Air

What is Air Made of?

Air is a mixture of gases. The major gases found in the air include:

  • Nitrogen: 78%
  • Oxygen: 21%
  • Carbon dioxide: 0.03%
  • Inert gases: 0.97%

Uses of Oxygen

  • Oxygen is used in burning.
  • Oxygen is used in germination.
  • Oxygen is used in breathing.

Uses of Carbon Dioxide

  1. Photosynthesis:

    • This is the process by which green plants make their own food.
    • Green plants use carbon dioxide to make food during the day and release oxygen to the atmosphere.
    • Therefore, they clean air.
  2. Preservation of soft drinks:
    • Carbon dioxide is added to soft drinks to preserve them, allowing them to last longer.
  3. Making fire extinguishers:
    • Carbon dioxide does not support burning, so it is used to put out fires.

Uses of Nitrogen

  1. In leguminous plants:
    • Leguminous plants such as beans, peas, and clovers use nitrogen from the air in the soil to make proteins.
  2. In food preservation/packaging:
    • Nitrogen is used in food packaging containers to keep off oxygen, helping to extend the shelf life of food.
  3. In light bulbs:
    • Nitrogen is used to prevent the filament from burning in light bulbs.
  4. In making fertilizers:
    • Nitrogen is one of the materials used in making fertilizers that are used by plants.

Uses of Inert Gases

  1. Making electric tubes and bulbs.
  2. Making advertisement lights.
